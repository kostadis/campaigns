# Derrick

Source document detailing the rescue of merchant survivor Derrick from the Moathouse. Documents his captivity as a food source for the ogre Lubash, his emotional breakdown while recounting victims, and the unexplained significance of his purple robe. Records his joint reward promise with fellow survivor Florian and his current status under the Duke’s care. Key narrative catalyst for themes of survival, dehumanization, and hidden identity.

Derrick was a traveling merchant captured and held in the Moathouse, where he was kept as a living commodity—intended as sustenance for the ogre Lubash. His survival was marked by psychological trauma, including vivid recollections of other prisoners who did not survive, which led to an emotional collapse during his debriefing with the party. The purple robe he wore during rescue has since become a symbolic narrative clue, its origin and meaning remaining unexplained despite investigation.

He shares a formal promise of reward with fellow survivor Florian, and both are currently under the protective custody of the Duke, recovering from their ordeal. His story has become central to the party’s understanding of the Moathouse’s horrors and the broader system of prisoner-as-commodity exploitation in the region.
