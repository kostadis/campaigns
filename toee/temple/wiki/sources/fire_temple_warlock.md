# Fire Temple Warlock

An unnamed servant of Alrrem the All-Consuming, the Fire Temple Warlock was a devoted zealot and direct handler of the Fire Temple’s engineered chimera. He played a significant role in the story as a field agent of the Fire Temple, elevating the faction from a background cult to an active, organized military-logistical entity.

He appeared abruptly during the party’s long rest, bursting through the southern door with the chimera restrained by a ritual control chain. His declaration — “Alrrem has sent his champion to break their bones and leave nothing but meat for the earth” — exemplified [[theatrical-zealotry-as-tactical-weakness]], prioritizing ideological performance over tactical adaptability.

His authority over the chimera collapsed the moment Zephyr seized the control chain, revealing the ritual nature of his command. He was subsequently engulfed by the chimera’s breath attack alongside his bugbear ally, left severely burned and incoherent before being silenced by Zinnia.

Recovered from his body were:
- A wax-sealed ledger bearing the crest of the [[crimson-trader]], confirming operational ties between the Fire Temple and the maritime logistics network.
- A heavy gold amulet inscribed with the eight-pointed star of Alrrem.
- A potion of fire resistance — unused despite the fire-based threat, suggesting ritual constraint, divine prohibition, or external interference.
- A masterwork whip of alchemically treated red leather, likely a symbol of rank or a conduit for divine energy.

His death marks the first direct confrontation between the party and a field agent of the Fire Temple. The ledger is the critical link confirming the [[deniable-logistics-network]] extends to theological operatives. His failure under pressure reinforces the concept of [[expendable-asset-tactics]] — the Fire Temple deploys zealots as disposable instruments, not elite champions.
