# Supply-Line Control

**Supply-Line Control** refers to the strategic management and manipulation of material flows — food, weapons, beasts, forbidden artifacts, and other critical resources — to sustain proxies, exert influence, destabilize rivals, and conceal forbidden knowledge. In Nulb’s lawless and contested regions, control over these supply lines is not merely economic but deeply political, theological, and militarized.

Zelt the Rat exemplifies the *criminal* model: he dominates smuggling routes and territorial corridors to extract profit through extortion, smuggling, and brute-force control. The [[crimson-reapers]] operate similarly, leveraging violence and territorial dominance to monopolize movement across Nulb’s underworld. Their power is visible, predatory, and transactional.

In contrast, institutional actors like the [[fire-temple]] and the [[water-temple-temple-of-elemental-evil]] wield supply-line control as a silent, deniable instrument of power. The Fire Temple employs the [[deniable-logistics-network]], using civilian contractors such as Captain Varsik to move sacred yet forbidden objects — like the chimera — under the guise of legitimate commerce. This represents **Religious Institutional Logistics**, where sacred authority co-opts commercial infrastructure to bypass legal and moral constraints.

Belsornig of the Water Temple elevates this to a new level: he does not command armies — he commands supply chains. He ensures Snej’s bandits at the [[abbey-of-allitur]] are fed and armed. He channels resources through Vesrek to enable the chimera’s transport. He does not fight directly — he makes others fight, and ensures they have what they need to do so. His power lies not in temples or rituals, but in the ability to sustain and direct violence without ever appearing in it. This is the essence of **institutional warfare via proxy networks**.

This form of control transcends mere profit or territory. It is a tool of [[indirect-warfare-via-proxy-networks]] and [[asymmetric-pressure-campaign]], turning logistics into strategy and scarcity into leverage. Even mundane maritime trade, as embodied by the [[crimson-trader]], becomes a vector for necromantic expansion and ideological infiltration.

**Contrast:**  
- *Criminal:* Zelt the Rat controls supply to profit  
- *Institutional:* Belsornig controls supply to destroy  

Supply-line control is no longer about movement — it is about control over access to power. Whether through food, weapons, living experiments, or forbidden knowledge, those who control the line control the fate of nations, cults, and empires.
