# Asymmetric Pressure Campaign

An **Asymmetric Pressure Campaign** is a strategic approach that avoids direct confrontation in favor of sustained, low-intensity pressure across multiple fronts — economic, logistical, psychological, and social — to erode an enemy’s capacity, morale, and alliances over time.

Belsornig of the Water Temple is the archetype of this strategy. Rather than attacking the party or rival temples head-on, he:
- Funds bandits (Snej) to harass travelers and destabilize regions
- Uses the chimera to bankrupt or implicate the Fire Temple
- Leverages the Abbey of Allitur as a node of disruption
- Maintains plausible deniability through layered intermediaries

His goal is not victory in battle, but the slow collapse of institutional cohesion. He exploits the Fire Temple’s reliance on visible logistics, the Earth Temple’s predictability, and the party’s reliance on direct engagement.

This contrasts sharply with the Fire Temple’s overt, high-casualty operations and the Earth Temple’s brute-force dominance. Belsornig’s power lies in patience, fragmentation, and the illusion of chaos.

The campaign is “asymmetric” because the attacker (Water Temple) is weaker in direct force but stronger in structure, information, and endurance. The enemy is not defeated — they are exhausted, confused, and isolated.

This model is now the dominant threat in the Temple of Evil — and the most difficult to counter.
