# Pragmatism
Pragmatism is the quality of being practical, which involves setting aside personal or ideological conflicts to achieve a greater goal. It refers to a practical approach to problems or situations.

## Examples of Pragmatism
* Jerry showed pragmatism by setting aside his conflicts with Warda to face a greater threat.
* Warda demonstrated pragmatism by putting aside her ideological differences with [[the-party]] to address a common threat, showcasing her practical approach to achieving her objectives.
* Dick Rentsch's decision to tolerate Wat's divided loyalties is an example of pragmatism, as he recognizes the need for cooperation until they secure complete power.

Both of these instances, along with Dick Rentsch's approach, highlight the importance of pragmatism in overcoming immediate challenges and working towards a common objective, despite underlying differences.
