# Existential-threat-prioritization

A governance strategy that deliberately allocates resources, attention, and authority toward large-scale, low-probability, high-impact threats — such as giants, ancient curses, or ecological collapse — while deprioritizing immediate, visible dangers like banditry or petty crime. 

Headgnome Glennddarc exemplifies this model in Sheernobb, dismissing Captain Gnonsbim’s bandit suppression campaign as “electioneering” and redirecting the party toward the giants, which he identifies as the true existential threat. Unlike political theater, which seeks public validation, existential-threat-prioritization operates in silence, often without public acknowledgment or visible action. It assumes that survival depends on addressing threats that are not yet manifest — or that the population is too distracted to perceive.

This concept contrasts with [[concepts/political-theater-in-law-enforcement]], revealing a fractured leadership structure in Sheernobb: one layer (Glennddarc) protects through foresight and secrecy; another (Gnonsbim) governs through spectacle and perception. The strategy may be wise, cynical, or desperate — but its effectiveness hinges on whether the threat is real, and whether those in power are willing to act before it’s too late.
