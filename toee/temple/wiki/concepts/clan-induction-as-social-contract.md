# Clan induction as social contract

A formal, ritualized process by which outsiders are adopted into the Rhennee clan, transforming them from temporary allies into kin with binding obligations and privileges.

In the case of the party, induction followed the successful rescue of Serena — not as a reward, but as a sacred act of trust. It conferred:  
- Protection from Rhennee networks and allies  
- Access to hidden routes, safe houses, and intelligence  
- Moral and social legitimacy within Nulb’s nomadic underworld  

In return, the party became bound to defend the clan, uphold its secrets, and honor its taboos — particularly those concerning spiritual practices and elder safety.

This concept extends [[transactional-leverage-in-underworld-economies]] by introducing non-monetary leverage: loyalty, kinship, and ritual obligation replace gold or threats as the currency of power. Induction is not transactional — it is existential. Once accepted, the party cannot walk away without violating a sacred covenant.

Eryk’s induction of the party may be genuine gratitude — or a calculated move to bind them to a hidden Rhennee agenda. The ambiguity makes this ritual one of the most potent and dangerous social tools in Nulb.
