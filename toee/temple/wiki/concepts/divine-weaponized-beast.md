# Divine-weaponized-beast

A supernatural creature — typically a chimera, golem, or hybrid construct — bred, bound, or animated under direct divine command to serve as a weapon of war. Unlike conventional war beasts, divine-weaponized-beasts are not merely trained or magically enhanced; they are ritually anchored to a deity’s will, often through soul-binding, blood oaths, or sacred chains.

The chimera under the Fire Temple Warlock’s control is a canonical example: its aggression, resilience, and fire-based breath attack align with Alrrem’s theology of purification through consumption. The control chain is not a physical restraint but a ritual conduit — its severance immediately breaks the beast’s obedience, proving the link is metaphysical, not behavioral.

This concept extends [[necromancy]] beyond corpse reanimation into the realm of divine engineering — where life is not stolen, but consecrated. The Fire Temple’s use of the chimera suggests a scalable model: multiple beasts, multiple handlers, multiple deployment points, all under centralized theological authority.

The existence of divine-weaponized-beasts implies that the gods of this world do not merely bless warriors — they forge weapons with living flesh.
