# Magical Item Recovery from Deceased Fey

The practice of acquiring potent magical artifacts from slain fey or other supernatural predators. While rewarding, this practice carries significant moral, spiritual, and magical risks.

The Hat of Wizardry and *The Hag's Guide to 101 Recipes Of Children* recovered from Aunt Emma exemplify this dynamic. Both items are powerful, yet tainted by the hag’s essence. The hat may amplify illusion magic; the book may be sentient or cursed. Their acquisition follows the same risk-reward calculus as [[custom-magical-rewards-as-patronage]], but without institutional oversight or accountability.

Unlike rewards granted by patrons (e.g., Gnonsbim’s promised weapons), items from fey are not earned through service—they are taken from the dead. This blurs the line between scavenging and desecration.

**Risks**:  
- Residual curses or possession  
- Attraction of other fey seeking retribution  
- Psychological corruption from contact with predatory magic  

**Ethical tension**: Is the use of such items a necessary survival tool, or a descent into the very monstrosity one seeks to destroy?

**Note**: This concept should be referenced whenever the party acquires magic from undead, fey, or demonic sources.
