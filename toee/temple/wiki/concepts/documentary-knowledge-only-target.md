# Documentary-Knowledge-Only Target

A **Documentary-Knowledge-Only Target** is a figure whose existence, role, and influence are known exclusively through written, recorded, or indirect evidence — never through direct encounter. Their power derives from their invisibility; their threat from their unverifiable reach. Their location, motivations, and current status remain unknown, making them immune to conventional countermeasures.

This archetype is defined by the absence of the target — not as a myth, but as a real, operational presence confirmed only by fragments: ledgers, confessions, manifests, third-party testimony, or forensic analysis. The target is real, but intangible. Countermeasures are impossible without a face, a location, or a voice. The party must act on fragments — and risk misinterpreting the whole.

## Core Examples

- **Captain Varsik:** Known through cargo manifests and recovered ledgers, yet never seen by the party. His ship, role, and fate are documented, but his person remains absent.
- **Belsornig:** The definitive example — his name appears in the ledger of a dead warlock, is confirmed by an orc cook, and is identified by a rival faction leader. He has never been seen, spoken to, or intercepted.
- **Vesrek:** Known only through ledger entries and secondhand testimony, with no corroborating physical evidence.
- **Hartsch:** Known through intelligence analysis and indirect alliances, never through direct contact or communication.
- **Urot:** Referenced in intercepted communications and ritual fragments, but no physical trace remains.

## Narrative and Structural Function

Documentary-only targets serve as:
- **Catalysts for deeper investigation** — compelling the party to trace networks rather than pursue individuals
- **Indicators of hidden systems** — revealing the architecture of institutional secrecy (e.g., the [[deniable-logistics-network]], the Fire Temple)
- **Narrative placeholders for unresolved arcs** — sustaining mystery and tension over long-term campaigns
- **Archaeological puzzles** — transforming intelligence gathering into the excavation of ghosts, where evidence is the only corpse

Their power lies not in who they are, but in what their absence reveals about the structure of power around them. They are the ultimate defense for institutional secrecy: to be known, but never seen — to shape events without ever being present.

This concept transforms intelligence from pursuit into archaeology — digging through ruins of evidence to reconstruct a ghost.
