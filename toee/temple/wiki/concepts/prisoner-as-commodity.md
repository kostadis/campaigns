# Prisoner-as-Commodity

The treatment of sentient beings—particularly captives—as expendable resources to be consumed, traded, or used for magical gain. This is not mere violence; it is institutionalized dehumanization, where human life is reduced to utility: biological fuel, financial collateral, or ritual ingredient.

This logic manifests across Nulb’s underworld in multiple, overlapping forms:

- **In the Moathouse**, captives like Derrick and Florian are held for literal consumption by the ogre Lubash—reduced to primal, biological sustenance. This reflects a collapse of moral boundaries where survival for the captor necessitates the literal ingestion of the captive.
  
- **In the Iron Lash brothel**, Serena is held as collateral for financial leverage, illustrating how captivity serves transactional purposes within underworld economies (cf. [[transactional-leverage-in-underworld-economies]]).

- **In the case of Aunt Emma**, children are not merely captured—they are harvested, prepared, and meticulously recorded in *The Hag’s Guide to 101 Recipes Of Children*. This cookbook elevates cannibalism into a codified, possibly coven-based ritual economy, blending necromancy and culinary tradition. Her actions mirror the [[deniable-logistics-network]]: each step is compartmentalized, each victim anonymized, each meal a calculated act of power.

**Contrast**:  
- **Lubash**: Primal, biological consumption — survival through ingestion.  
- **Serena (Iron Lash)**: Transactional captivity — value as collateral.  
- **Aunt Emma**: Ritualized, magical consumption — value as sacred ingredient.  

**Implication**: In Nulb’s fractured world, humanity is not just threatened—it is categorized by its utility. The concept of the Prisoner-as-Commodity extends beyond economic or political leverage into visceral, ontological erasure. It contrasts sharply with [[public-execution-as-political-theater]], where death is spectacle; here, death is sustenance, currency, or spell component.

This framework underscores the primal brutality of the Moathouse and its alignment with deeper, non-humanized forces in the region—forces that treat personhood as obsolete. The party must now consider: who else is being harvested? And who is keeping score?
