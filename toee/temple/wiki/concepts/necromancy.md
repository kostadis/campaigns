# Necromancy

Necromancy in this setting is not limited to raising the dead — it encompasses the experimental manipulation of life, death, and hybrid biological forms, evolving into a sophisticated art of biological engineering and divine binding. In Nulb and its surrounding regions, necromancy has transcended traditional occult practices to become a systematic, institutionalized discipline rooted in ritual, theology, and state-sanctioned warfare.

The Fire Temple is actively engaged in the creation and transport of chimeric entities, suggesting a bureaucratic, industrialized approach to forbidden biology. These chimeras are not merely reanimated corpses, but fused organisms composed of multiple species, bound not by spells alone, but by ritual chains tied to a deity’s will. This process — termed “theological necromancy” — treats death not as an end, but as a sacrament: souls of the constituent creatures are anchored, tortured, and subsumed into a single controlled consciousness, creating weaponized beings that serve as divine instruments.

Evidence of this practice includes the unused potion of fire resistance and the wax-sealed ledger, both pointing to a system where death is ritualized, commodified, and weaponized. The [[deep-temple]] is suspected as the origin of this practice — a hidden sanctum where the boundaries between flesh, spirit, and divine command dissolve, serving as the core laboratory for these experiments.

The transport of the chimera via Captain Varsik and the *Crimson Trader* confirms that necromantic research requires a sophisticated logistical infrastructure. This includes [[deniable-logistics-network]] operations, where civilian contractors are used to move experimental beings without implicating the Temple directly. The chimera’s movement implies a network of hidden labs, collection points, and delivery terminals — possibly linked to the [[deep-temple]] and integrated with [[supply-line-control]] systems.

The party’s investigation must now consider not just the creatures themselves, but the entire supply chain, ritual framework, and theological doctrine that sustains them. Necromancy is no longer a fringe occult practice, but a core component of a state-sanctioned, covert biological and divine program rooted in the [[fire-temple]], with implications extending to the [[control-chain-of-alrrem]] and the [[divine-weaponized-beast]] doctrine.
