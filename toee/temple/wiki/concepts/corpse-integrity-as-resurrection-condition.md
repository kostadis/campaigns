# Corpse-integrity-as-resurrection-condition

A magical prerequisite in the world’s cosmology: resurrection rituals require the body to remain physically intact and undamaged. Thalsor’s threat to mutilate Calmer’s corpse was not mere cruelty — it was a precise, knowledge-based attempt to permanently erase the possibility of his return. This concept transforms death from a final state into a conditional one, where the body becomes a tactical asset.

The party’s recovery of Calmer’s intact corpse underscores the strategic importance of corpse preservation. It also introduces narrative tension: if resurrection fails, Calmer’s death becomes permanent, and the Church’s investment in him is lost. This mechanic parallels the use of [[prisoner-as-commodity]] — both treat living beings as resources whose value is contingent on physical state. The condition creates a new layer of risk in combat: not just death, but desecration.
