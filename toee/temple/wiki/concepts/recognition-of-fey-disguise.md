# Recognition of Fey Disguise

The ability to perceive the true form of fey entities that use illusion to mask their nature. This is not merely a magical sense but a learned, often traumatic, form of awareness developed through exposure to fey deception.

Zinnia demonstrated this skill when she immediately identified Aunt Emma as a hag, preventing the party from entering the Gingerbread House under false pretenses. Her recognition triggered Zephyr’s decisive action and saved the party from certain consumption.

This skill stands in stark contrast to Calmer’s [[pious-social-dysfunction]]—where faith blinded him to danger—and highlights the difference between institutional loyalty and adaptive survival. Recognition of fey disguise is not taught by the Church of St. Cuthbert; it is earned through near-death experience or innate sensitivity.

**Importance**:  
- Critical for survival in fey-influenced zones like the Gnarley Forest  
- A rare, non-magical form of defense against supernatural predators  
- Makes Zinnia an indispensable party asset  

**Training**: No formal method is known. May be linked to bloodline, trauma, or exposure to fey magic.  
**Note**: This concept should be tracked as a potential party-wide competency to develop.
