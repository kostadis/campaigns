# Unused-potion-as-narrative-irony

A narrative device in which a character possesses a resource perfectly suited to counter an imminent threat — yet fails to use it — creating dramatic irony and implying deeper constraints on their agency.

The Fire Temple Warlock carried a potion of fire resistance, yet never activated it during the chimera’s fire breath attack — despite being engulfed and burned. This is not negligence; it is symbolic. Possible explanations include:
- Ritual prohibition: Alrrem demands suffering as proof of devotion.
- Divine interference: The potion was magically suppressed or cursed.
- External control: The potion was withheld by a superior as a test or punishment.
- Identity deception: The potion was not his — planted to mislead.

This moment transforms the potion from a mundane item into a clue: it suggests the warlock was not fully autonomous. His actions were constrained by doctrine, patronage, or divine will — reinforcing the concept of [[expendable-asset-tactics]] and the idea that even his survival was not his to command.

In a world where magic is abundant, the unused potion is the most telling artifact.
