# Clerical-assignment-as-influence-tool

The practice of assigning clerics to adventuring parties not as healers or spiritual guides, but as agents of institutional oversight. Terjon’s assignment of Calmer to the party exemplifies this tactic: Calmer’s presence was not to support the party’s mission, but to report on its actions, ensure doctrinal alignment, and maintain the Church’s influence over its operations.

This strategy transforms religious personnel into political instruments. Calmer’s loyalty to Terjon over the party, his insistence on including Terjon in council meetings, and his failure to adapt to the party’s evolving pragmatism all reveal his function as a surveillance asset. His death may trigger institutional retribution — or replacement — making this assignment a long-term political gambit. The tactic mirrors [[political-theater-in-law-enforcement]] but operates through spiritual authority rather than public spectacle.
