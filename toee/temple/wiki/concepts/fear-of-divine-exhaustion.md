# Fear of divine exhaustion

A deeply held cultural taboo within the Rhennee clan: the belief that spiritual practices — particularly divination or communion with higher powers — can drain the life force of the elderly or frail, leading to death.

Eryk Velkori actively opposed his mother Vethra’s attempts at divination, not out of disrespect, but out of terror that the ritual would kill her. This fear suggests the Rhennee possess firsthand knowledge of magic’s lethal cost — possibly from witnessing past rituals at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon or from inherited trauma tied to ancestral practices.

This taboo creates a paradox: a society immersed in a world of magic, yet terrified of its most potent forms. It implies that spiritual power is not a tool but a debt — one that must be paid in life. The persistence of the fire at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon may be a manifestation of this debt — a ritual that consumes its practitioners over time.

The fear of divine exhaustion may be the key to understanding why the Rhennee remain neutral, why they avoid direct confrontation, and why they are so protective of their elders — not as symbols of wisdom, but as living warnings of what happens when magic is misused.
