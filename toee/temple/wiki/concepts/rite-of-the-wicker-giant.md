# Rite of the Wicker Giant

A secretive druidic ritual taught by [[elazar]] at the [[hall-of-the-scarlet-moon]], the Rite of the Wicker Giant has an unknown true purpose and nature, though its effects are unmistakable and deeply unsettling. Described by Eryk Velkori as a festive gathering, the rite is marked by an unnatural, persistent fire — the [[persistent-ritual-fire]] — that burns continuously despite environmental conditions, suggesting the ritual is not merely ceremonial but actively sustained by magical or necromantic means.

The term “wicker giant” implies a colossal, elemental construct woven from wood and flame, possibly serving as a vessel, guardian, or avatar. The ritual involves sustained fire, symbolic transformation, and possibly the summoning or binding of this entity. Its purposes may include:

- Summoning a protective or destructive entity to defend or threaten a region  
- Binding elemental forces to a location (e.g., the Hall of the Scarlet Moon)  
- Transforming participants into something beyond human — possibly through sacrifice or spiritual fusion  

Verigo is actively preparing for the rite, indicating its imminent performance. The ritual’s secrecy and the presence of multiple unknown groups at the Hall suggest it is not a local tradition, but part of a wider, hidden network of occult practice.

Its connection to the giants identified by Headgnome Glennddarc is highly suspect; the “wicker giant” may be a manifestation, precursor, or symbolic representation of the [[existential-threat-prioritization]] looming over Sheernobb. The Rhennee clan’s fear of spiritual exhaustion may stem from prior exposure to this rite — suggesting that its use carries a lethal cost to practitioners or the land, potentially tied to [[necromancy]] or the draining of ambient life force.

The ritual’s true origins, full mechanics, and ultimate goal remain shrouded in mystery, reinforcing its status as one of the most enigmatic and dangerous ceremonies in the region.
