# Control Chain of Alrrem

A ritual artifact used by the Fire Temple to command its engineered chimera, the Control Chain of Alrrem is not a mere physical restraint but a conduit of divine authority. Crafted from braided hellfire-iron and inscribed with inverted prayers to Alrrem, the chain binds the chimera’s will to the handler’s through a metaphysical tether — severing it severs the command link.

The warlock’s immediate collapse after Zephyr seized the chain confirms its function: control is not learned, but granted. The chain is a sacrament — its possession is a sign of divine favor, and its loss is a sign of divine abandonment. The chain’s design suggests standardized production, indicating institutional training and uniformity across Fire Temple handlers.

This concept extends [[necromancy]] into the domain of ritual technology — where binding is not a spell but a sacramental act. It also connects to [[custom-magical-rewards-as-patronage]], as the chain may be issued by high priests as a mark of rank, not merely a tool.

The chain’s vulnerability to seizure suggests a critical weakness: the system is centralized, and its agents are dependent on external conduits of power. This makes the Fire Temple’s operatives predictable — and exploitable.
