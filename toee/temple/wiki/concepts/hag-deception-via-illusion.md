# Hag Deception via Illusion

A core predatory tactic employed by Green Hags and other fey predators: the use of illusion magic to assume the form of a harmless, often maternal or elderly human figure. This disguise is designed to evoke trust, comfort, and lowered vigilance—particularly in isolated or vulnerable settings.

In the case of Aunt Emma, she appeared as a kindly woman offering sweets and directions, luring the party into her gingerbread house. The illusion was so convincing that even seasoned travelers might have been deceived—had Zinnia not possessed the ability to perceive fey true forms.

This tactic is distinct from [[concepts/transactional-leverage-in-underworld-economies]] or [[concepts/pious-social-dysfunction]] in that it is not about economic or ideological manipulation, but biological and psychological entrapment. The hag’s goal is not negotiation, but consumption.

**Countermeasures**:  
- [[concepts/recognition-of-fey-disguise]]  
- Use of detect magic or true seeing  
- Avoidance of unsolicited hospitality in wild zones  

**Significance**: This deception is the primary vector through which hags sustain their predatory cycles. Recognizing it is a matter of survival.
