# Inter-Temple Rivalry (Elemental Evil)

The Inter-Temple Rivalry refers to the covert, systemic conflict between the three major factions within the Temple of Elemental Evil: the Water Temple, the Earth Temple, and the Fire Temple. Each faction pursues its own agenda, not merely to expand power, but to undermine the others — turning internal competition into a form of institutional warfare.

The Water Temple, led by Belsornig, employs [[indirect-warfare-via-proxy-networks]] and [[asymmetric-pressure-campaign]] to destabilize rivals. Its strategy is to avoid direct confrontation, instead using proxies (e.g., Snej’s bandits) and logistical traps (e.g., the chimera scheme) to weaken the Earth and Fire Temples. The chimera was not meant to kill the party — it was meant to bankrupt the Fire Temple or implicate the Earth Temple in its failure.

The Earth Temple, represented by Hartsch, favors direct confrontation and brute-force dominance. The Fire Temple, previously assumed to be the primary antagonist, is revealed to be a target — its overt logistics (e.g., the Crimson Trader) make it vulnerable to sabotage.

This rivalry transforms the Temple of Evil from a monolithic evil force into a fractured, self-consuming hierarchy. The party’s mission must now account for not just external threats, but the internal power plays that make the Temple of Evil more dangerous than any single faction.

**Key Insight:** The greatest threat is not the one that strikes — but the one that lets others strike for it.
