# Profit-driven neutrality

Profit-driven neutrality is a behavioral strategy in which actors in lawless environments deliberately avoid ideological, political, or moral alignment — operating solely on the basis of economic incentive. Unlike figures such as Madame Selentis, who use terror to enforce political order, or Zephyr, who pursues occult truths, profit-driven neutrals like Elvanna care only for value extraction. Their power lies in their reliability as intermediaries, not in their loyalty to any cause.

Elvanna embodies this concept: she holds Serena not out of vengeance or political messaging, but because the Rhennee’s actions created a debt she could monetize. She expresses no hostility toward the party, nor does she offer aid — only a transaction. Her sardonic remark after being paid — that she might have asked for more — reveals her detachment and her focus on maximizing return, not asserting dominance.

This neutrality makes her a trusted node in Nulb’s underground network. Those who need information, access, or temporary containment know they can deal with her without fear of betrayal — so long as they pay. Her lack of alignment is not weakness; it is her strength, allowing her to serve clients across factions without becoming a target.

Profit-driven neutrality complements *transactional leverage* and contrasts sharply with *public-execution-as-political-theater*, revealing a spectrum of control mechanisms in Nulb — from spectacle to silence, from terror to trade.
