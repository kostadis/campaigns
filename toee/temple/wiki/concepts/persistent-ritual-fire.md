# Persistent ritual fire

An unnatural, continuous flame that burns at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon regardless of weather, season, or environmental conditions. Unlike ceremonial fires, this one does not flicker, diminish, or require fuel — it simply endures.

Eryk Velkori noted its strangeness: while others treat it as part of a festive gathering, he perceives it as a sign of hidden, sustained ritual activity. The fire’s persistence defies natural explanation and suggests magical or necromantic binding — possibly anchoring a spirit, warding a location, or serving as a beacon to distant entities.

Its connection to the rite of the wicker giant implies it is not decorative but functional: a conduit, a prison, or a signal. It may be tied to the giants identified by Headgnome Glennddarc — either as a warning, a summoning mechanism, or a containment field.

The fire’s endurance may also be a symptom of spiritual exhaustion — a ritual that consumes life force to remain lit, echoing the Rhennee’s fear of divine exhaustion. If so, the fire is not a symbol of celebration, but of sacrifice — and its continued burning may be a sign that something is being fed.

Its existence transforms the Hall of the Scarlet Moon from a mere site of ritual into a living, breathing anomaly — and one of the most dangerous unknowns in Nulb.
