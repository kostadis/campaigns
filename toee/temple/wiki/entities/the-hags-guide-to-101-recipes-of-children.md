# "The Hag's Guide to 101 Recipes Of Children"

A leather-bound, blood-stained cookbook recovered from the Gingerbread House after the death of Aunt Emma. The pages are written in a flowing, archaic script with illustrations of children being prepared for consumption—roasted, stewed, pickled, and preserved. Each recipe includes notes on optimal age, seasoning, and magical enhancements (e.g., “Add a drop of fear-sweat for flavor,” “Use bones in necromantic brews”).

The book is not merely a record—it is a ritual artifact. Pages shift when unobserved. Names of children appear and vanish. When read aloud, it emits a low, childlike humming. Zinnia believes it may be sentient or bound to a coven’s collective will.

This artifact confirms the hag’s habitual predation on children and provides direct evidence of [[poisoned-hospitality-as-predatory-ritual]] and [[prisoner-as-commodity]] taken to its most extreme form. It may also be a spellbook, a conduit for necromantic rites, or a key to locating other hags.

**Status**: Held in the party’s secure archive.  
**Warning**: Do not read aloud. Do not store near living children. Do not attempt to cook from it.  
**Research priority**: High. Is this a single hag’s journal, or a shared coven text? Are there other copies?
