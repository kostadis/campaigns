# Headgnome Glennddarc

Headgnome Glennddarc is the elderly and irascible Headgnome of [[sheernobb]], serving as both a village leader and a critical internal bureaucrat within its municipal government. He is a municipal insider and internal critic who possesses deep, often clandestine knowledge of local affairs, power structures, and personal relationships — particularly those involving [[captain-gnonsbim]] and [[pasmarie-clee]].

## Character Profile

Glennddarc is characterized by his sharp intellect, deep skepticism of official narratives, and little patience for misdirected priorities. His primary concern as a leader is the protection of [[sheernobb]] from giants — a tangible, existential threat — which contrasts sharply with the performative governance he observes in his own administration. He is a voice of realism in a system built on spectacle, and his irascibility stems from frustration at the gap between genuine need and political theater.

## Role and Influence

As an informant and internal critic, Glennddarc routinely provides private intelligence that contradicts official accounts. He has privately characterized [[captain-gnonsbim]]’s anti-bandit campaign as “political theater,” exposing it not as a genuine law enforcement effort but as a calculated performance designed to maintain appearances and consolidate influence. His insights reveal a broader, unspoken culture of performative governance within Sheernobb’s hierarchy.

His access to confidential information — including Gnonsbim’s romantic interest in [[pasmarie-clee]] — underscores his position as a well-connected insider with visibility into the private lives of those in power. This knowledge allows him to act as a narrative counterweight, subtly guiding [[the-party]]’s actions by directing their resources toward addressing Pasmarie’s situation, thereby undermining the official narrative while advancing a more authentic form of justice.

Glennddarc’s existence implies that Sheernobb’s governance contains internal dissenters who understand the mechanisms of power but remain constrained by bureaucratic inertia. He does not openly rebel — he operates through whispers, leaks, and strategic redirection, making him a vital, if quiet, force in shaping the village’s hidden truths.
