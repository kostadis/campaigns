# Sequoia

Sequoia is a pragmatic, physically dominant member of the party and its de facto leader, known for methodical strategy, stealth, and results-driven decision-making. They consistently oppose [[Calmer]]’s leadership claims and institutional interference, embodying leadership-by-competence in stark contrast to Calmer’s leadership-by-assertion. Sequoia physically pushed Calmer aside during the Voss incident, shut down his attempt to include [[Terjon]] in village council meetings, and repeatedly intervened to prevent Calmer’s tactical blunders.

Sequoia was instrumental in the party’s survival during the Moathouse excursions, often rescuing Calmer from the consequences of his own actions. After Calmer’s death at the hands of [[Thalsor]], Sequoia infiltrated the Black Earth Compound in gaseous form to recover Calmer’s intact corpse — a critical act that preserved the possibility of resurrection. His actions reflect a cold, effective pragmatism: he does not mourn Calmer’s death, but he ensures its consequences are managed. Sequoia embodies the party’s shift away from institutional deference toward self-reliant, results-driven leadership.

Sequoia is also known to be knowledgeable about gnomish illusionary traditions, having explained the animation of [[Keenleene]]'s statue in [[Sheernobb]] as an example of this tradition. As a member of the village council, Sequoia has consistently resisted attempts to expand institutional influence within the party’s operations, reinforcing their role as a stabilizing, non-doctrinal authority.
