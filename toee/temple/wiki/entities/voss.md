# Voss

Voss is a low-ranking bandit grunt and traumatized merchant prisoner held in the Moathouse. He was captured by the party during the Moathouse incursion and is a man brought low by circumstance, demonstrating fear and emotional fragility under pressure. His presence highlights the failure of institutional piety in contexts of trauma.

Calmer attempted to “comfort” him by delivering a doctrinal lecture on St. Cuthbert’s enemies — an act of [[pious-social-dysfunction]] that worsened Voss’s psychological state. Unlike interrogation techniques designed to extract information, Calmer’s approach was performative and self-referential, revealing more about his own need for validation than any genuine pastoral intent. Voss’s silence after the encounter underscores the irrelevance — even harm — of uninvited spiritual intervention in environments of terror.
