# Gurdy the Bard

Gurdy the Bard is a member of the party whose inclusion Calmer vehemently opposed. Their conflict came to a head at the Honey Haven Tavern, where Calmer, visibly drunk on mead, objected to Gurdy’s presence — likely due to ideological or aesthetic dissonance. Gurdy represents a counterpoint to Calmer’s rigid orthodoxy: artistic, improvisational, and socially fluid. Calmer’s resistance to Gurdy reflects his inability to tolerate deviation from his own moral and structural worldview. Gurdy’s role in the party remains ambiguous, but his existence challenges Calmer’s belief in hierarchical, doctrinal purity.
