# Brother Eldin

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Monk of the Beory monastery near Gallow's Corner; defector from the Earth Cult faction within the order
- **Faction:** Abbey of Beory (followers of Beory, goddess of nature)
- **First Appearance:** At the Beory monastery, where he and Sister Alina led the party through the "Path of Righteousness" — a structured series of moral dilemmas evaluating the party's character. Calmer attempted to deceive an elderly monk (possibly Eldin himself) by claiming to be lost in the woods. The party also engaged him indirectly through his stone guardian in the ancient library, which posed a riddle about nature's eternal cycles before granting passage deeper into the monastery.
- *Also known as: Brother Eldrin, Brother Eldren, Eldin, Eldrin, Eldren.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protect the Abbey of Beory and preserve its sanctity; stop the Earth Cult's activities — particularly the Titan-summoning ritual; see powerful artifacts of good returned to their rightful custodians
- Earnest, principled, and cautious — speaks with measured authority on matters of faith and artifact lore, though he lacks the power to enforce his counsel
- Quick to reward those who aid him, but equally quick to warn against dangerous courses of action
- Willingness to defect and risk himself as a diversion suggests a quietly conflicted character — someone who can be pushed to act when the truth becomes undeniable

## History with the Party
1. **Path of Righteousness:** Co-led the party (alongside Sister Alina) through the monastery's structured moral evaluation. The party's conduct during these dilemmas was observed and assessed.
2. **Stone Guardian Riddle:** Party first engaged with him indirectly through his stone guardian in the ancient library, which tested them with a riddle about nature's eternal cycles before granting passage deeper into the monastery.
3. **Interrogation & Revelation:** During an interrogation, the Earth Cult's true nature was revealed. This appears to have been a turning point — Eldin broke from the monastery's compromised faction.
4. **Defection & Diversion:** Formed a tactical alliance with the party. Agreed to act as an internal distraction to enable a split-force infiltration, specifically to allow Zinnia and the GM's character to confront Sister Alina directly.
5. **Post-Tharok Survival:** Following the party's destruction of the ritual cavern beneath the abbey, Eldin was found alive but wounded in the main abbey. Expressed relief at Tharok's defeat and declared the abbey safe.
6. **Reward:** Paid the party 70 gp, 400 sp, and 400 cp each; provided a Scroll of Web and a Potion of Greater Healing.
7. **Chest of Rao warning:** Upon seeing the party carrying the Chest of Rao, he explained its nature — an artifact of great good created by high priests of Rao — and warned it would attract both evil forces seeking to exploit it as a magical battery and the forces of the Archclericy of Veluna seeking to reclaim it. He urged them to return it. The party ignored his counsel and kept the chest, intending to use it as leverage against Temple agents in Hommlet.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Abbey of Beory, in the mountains near Gallow's Corner
- **Active Plans:** Recovering from his wounds; abbey operations resuming now that Tharok is gone
- **What the Party Knows:** Eldin is a knowledgeable monk with ties to divine lore; he is disillusioned with the cult, defected to help them, survived the cavern collapse, and is aware they have the Chest of Rao and rejected his advice to return it
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether Eldin will take further action — contacting the Archclericy of Veluna, warning others, or simply remaining passive — is unknown; the depth of his prior knowledge of the cult's activities

## Relationships
- **Sister Alina:** Former colleague and co-leader of the Path of Righteousness; now on opposing sides — Eldin actively enabled the party's move against her
- **The Party:** Cautious ally turned grateful benefactor; views them as heroes who restored peace to the Abbey; rewarded them generously; now holds concern or potential disapproval over their custody of the Chest of Rao
- **Calmer:** Minor prior tension — Calmer attempted deception on an elderly monk at the monastery (possibly Eldin himself)
- **Zinnia:** Eldin's distraction was specifically designed to enable Zinnia's confrontation with Sister Alina
- **Tharok:** Enemy/threat — now eliminated
- **Archclericy of Veluna:** Aware of their interest in the Chest of Rao; suggested they would actively pursue its return

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase (Trust/Alliance):** Defected following the interrogation; volunteered to serve as a diversion at personal risk to himself.
