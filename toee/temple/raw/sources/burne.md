# Burne

## Identity
- **Role:** Wizard, co-leader of the Hommlet fortification project, representative of Verbobonc
- **Faction:** Viscounty of Verbobonc
- **Partner:** Rufus (romantic relationship and professional co-leader)
- **First Appearance:** The party visited the tower; Rufus called Burne down mid-conversation, interrupting his experiments. Burne was horrified at the suggestion of a Temple resurgence, calling it impossible.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintain Verbobonc's political standing and the Viscount's legitimacy; manage the Temple resurgence narrative carefully to avoid panic or embarrassment.
- **Political over altruistic:** Burne's responses consistently prioritize optics and institutional stability over direct action. He is quick to reframe troubling evidence in ways that protect existing power structures.
- He can be dismissive at first impression but grows thoughtful when presented with concrete information. He is politically astute — he deduced that Zinnia was behind Sequoia's embedded infiltration plan before being told.

## History with the Party

1. **First meeting at the tower:** Called down by Rufus mid-experiment. Reacted with disbelief and horror at the suggestion of a Temple resurgence.
2. **Inn of the Welcome Wench meeting:** Initially dismissed the eye-and-tear stone ("This is it?"), then grew more engaged as Rufus revealed the copied blueprints. Instructed the party to keep the investigation quiet.
3. **Village council meeting (Planting 20, 579):** Downplayed the evidence publicly, arguing it was locally significant but not worth alerting the arch-cleric. Offered an alternative theory — that the apparent cultists might be agents of another power trying to destabilize the Viscounty by faking a Temple resurgence. Praised the party's work and framed their deputization as validated. Sequoia privately suspected him of covering his own failings.
4. **Morning after deaths of Rannos and Gremag:** Burne and Rufus approached the party together, confirmed Elmo had sent rangers after the missing chest, and urged the party to deal with the Moathouse.
5. **Later council meeting:** Present when the party presented proof of the Temple's resurgence and Lareth's death.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Rufus's tower, Hommlet
- **Active Concerns:** The Chest of Rao's reappearance and its political implications; managing the narrative around the Temple's return

**What the party knows:** Burne is politically cautious, skeptical of rushing to conclusions, and keen to control how information about the Temple spreads. He has confirmed and supported their actions while consistently shaping events to protect Verbobonc's interests.

**What remains hidden:** Whether his alternative theory about outside agents was genuine analysis or deliberate misdirection. Sequoia suspects he may be covering his own failings, but this is unconfirmed.

## Relationships
- **Rufus:** Romantic partner and co-leader; they present a united front but Rufus appears to take the lead on field matters
- **The Party:** Treats them as useful assets; praises their work publicly while managing what they report upward
- **Sequoia:** Specifically praised her embedded plan and credited Zinnia for it — suggests he watches the party closely
- **Elmo:** Coordinating with him on ranger deployment regarding the chest
