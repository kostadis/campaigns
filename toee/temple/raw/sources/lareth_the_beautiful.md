# Lareth the Beautiful

## Identity
- **Role:** Commander of the Fire Temple faction operating from the Moathouse dungeon
- **Faction:** Fire Temple (elemental cult network)
- **First Appearance:** Brief appearance at the moathouse early on; formally encountered Planting 20, 579 in a barracks area when the party, disguised in fire temple robes, bluffed their way in with a guard escort
- *Also known as: Lareth, Larith, Larith the Beautiful.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Consolidating power within the elemental cult hierarchy by eliminating rival factions (particularly the Earth Temple / Black Earth cult) and expanding the Fire Temple's influence
- Calculating and composed — assessed the party as "professionals" and chose to let them operate rather than interfere; quietly manipulative, keeping the party at arm's length while steering them toward his ends
- Described as a stunning man who exudes evil — his physical beauty is at odds with his sinister nature
- Suspicious; accepted the party's fabricated defection story on the surface while simultaneously manipulating them for his own ends
- Perceptive enough to eventually see through the party's deceptions despite initial success

## History with the Party
1. **Moathouse first contact:** Lareth appeared briefly, dismissed his sergeant's concern about the party, and made direct eye contact with Zephyr — signaling awareness of their private deal and his intent to keep it secret. No open confrontation occurred.
2. **Planting 20, 579 — Defection bluff:** The party, disguised as fire temple operatives, approached Lareth claiming to have killed Snej of the Water Temple and wishing to defect. Lareth accepted the story and declared their loyalty proven. Rather than granting reinforcements for the abbey, he sent them on a retrieval mission: recover a chest stolen by bandits in the Kron Hills near Sheernob, whom he dismissed as poseurs pretending to be Earth elemental cultists. He warned them to tell no one.
3. **Unknowing service:** Party completed the retrieval, not yet realizing Lareth had used them to eliminate Black Earth cult competition. Zinnia later pieced together that the "bandit" mission was calculated to consolidate Lareth's power by removing elemental rivals. The chest was in fact the Viscounty's stolen diplomatic gift intended for the Kingdom of Celene.
4. **Final confrontation:** Party returned and tried to deceive him again — claiming the Deep Temple had taken the chest and that Rannos and Gremag had been acting on his behalf, hoping to provoke internal infighting. Despite a successful group persuasion check, Lareth rolled high and saw through the lie. He accused them of treachery. When Zephyr mocked him ("The only person here that's backstabbing is you"), he called his guards to arms.
5. **Death:** In combat, Lareth proved formidable — summoning Mirror Image, severely wounding Calmer with Inflict Wounds, and paralyzing both Zephyr and Zinnia with Hold Person. He was reduced to a single hit point before Sequoia, unaffected by the paralysis, landed the killing blow.
6. **Frame-up afterward:** The party scattered Lareth's Fire Temple insignia and robes (recovered from his body and barracks operation) throughout a prison chamber as false evidence, framing the Fire Temple for a prisoners' escape.

## Current Status
- **Status:** **Dead** — slain by Sequoia during the confrontation at the Moathouse
- **Loot recovered:** Mace of Smiting, magical Plate Mail, Ring of Free Action, silver service set (2,000 GP), fire opal necklace (3,500 GP), gems (800 GP)
- **What the party knows:** Lareth was the Fire Temple commander, manipulated them into destroying Earth Temple rivals, and ultimately saw through their deceptions
- **What remains hidden:** The full extent of Fire Temple operations above Lareth's level; who, if anyone, he reported to

## Relationships
- **Fire Temple:** Commanded its Moathouse operation; his death leaves the faction leaderless at this location
- **Black Earth Cult / Earth Temple:** Rivals he covertly used the party to destroy
- **Rannos & Gremag:** The party implicated them in their final deception attempt; whether this had any downstream consequence is unresolved
- **Zephyr:** Had a private arrangement of unknown terms; Lareth considered it sensitive enough to explicitly promise silence
- **Sequoia:** Disclosed enough of Lareth's plan to relay it to Rufus and Burne; ultimately delivered the killing blow
- **Rufus and Burne:** Informed of Lareth's scheme via Sequoia
- **His Sergeant:** Subordinate; Lareth overruled his concern about the party
- **The Party:** Used them as unwitting pawns, grew suspicious of them, and ultimately met his end at their hands
