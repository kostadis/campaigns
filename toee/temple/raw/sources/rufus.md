# Rufus of Hommlet

## Identity
- **Role:** Military commander of the Hommlet area; representative of Verbobonc tasked with constructing a castle
- **Faction:** Viscounty of Verbobonc
- **Partner:** Burne (wizard; the two operate jointly from their tower in Hommlet)
- **First Appearance:** The party's first visit to Burne and Rufus's tower, where Rufus attempted to compel their cooperation through deputization

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Secure the region militarily, root out Temple resurgence, protect Verbobonc's political interests, and complete the castle without sabotage
- Rufus is pragmatic and politically cautious — he frames threats in terms of what can be officially acted upon, not what the party suspects
- He leads with persuasion but shifts quickly to authority and veiled threats when he doesn't get voluntary compliance
- He keeps a tight grip on information flow and insists sensitive matters be handled quietly, suggesting a preference for controlled outcomes over transparency

## History with the Party

**First Meeting (Tower, early sessions)**
Rufus attempted to get Sequoia to voluntarily share intelligence exclusively with him. When Sequoia declined, Rufus formally deputized the entire party, stating deputies were required to report to him first and threatening arrest or worse if they investigated the Moathouse without accepting his terms.

**The Stolen Blueprints**
When the party presented the mysterious stone at the Inn, Rufus initially dismissed it, then grew thoughtful. He disclosed that the castle blueprints had gone missing and returned with charcoal dust — evidence of copying. He had relocated the plans and believed a saboteur was embedded among the construction crew. He tasked the party with quietly identifying the thief.

**Village Council Meeting**
Rufus acknowledged the party's evidence of bandit activity but publicly stated there was no conclusive proof of Temple resurgence. He characterized the threat as local rather than national in scope, explicitly arguing it did not warrant involving the arch-cleric of Verbobonc. This framing minimized the party's findings at the official level.

**Planting 20, 579 — Return from the Moathouse**
Rufus met with the party on their return. He was visibly excited at mention of the cult's leader and asked directly whether the party had killed him. When Sequoia revealed the leader was named Lareth and that the party intended to use him as an asset, Rufus accepted this without objection. He then provided critical intelligence about the stolen Chest of Rao: it was the winning entry from the Viscounty's annual art competition, meant as a diplomatic gift to the Kingdom of Celene. The delivery mission was attacked and the chest disappeared. The official position holds that the elves received it — meaning the chest's reappearance would embarrass the Viscount. Rufus did not give direct orders but made clear that recovering the chest and capturing those responsible would be of great value.

**Final Council Meeting**
Following the deaths of Rannos and Gremag, Rufus joined Burne in urging the party to act against the Moathouse threat. He was present at the final council meeting that officially confirmed the Temple's resurgence.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Rufus's tower, Hommlet
- **Active Concerns:**
  - Saboteur among the castle construction workers (blueprints were copied)
  - Recovery of the Chest of Rao and those who stole it
  - Managing the political fallout of confirmed Temple activity
- **What the Party Knows:** Rufus is a Verbobonc agent with real authority; he's politically cautious and prefers quiet resolution; the chest is a diplomatic liability for the Viscount
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent of Verbobonc's intelligence on the Temple; whether Rufus has other agents or contacts operating in the area; his personal stake, if any, beyond official duty

## Relationships
- **Burne:** Partner and co-commander in Hommlet; they share political goals and present a unified front
- **The Viscount of Verbobonc:** Rufus reports upward to the Viscounty; he is protective of the Viscount's reputation (see: the chest situation)
- **Sequoia:** Initial friction — Rufus tried to personally recruit her and was rebuffed, escalating to formal coercion; relationship is functional but carries that early tension
- **The Party (general):** Official deputies of Rufus; he treats them as useful assets and expects information to flow to him first
