# Thorne

## Identity
- **Role:** Mercenary man-at-arms and freelance cleanup operative; nominally hired through Rannos Davl
- **Faction:** None confirmed — explicitly no loyalty to the Temple of Elemental Evil
- **Appearance:** Late 30s, weathered face, short brown hair, piercing green eyes hidden under a leather cap, studded leather armor
- **First Appearance:** Planting 18 — appeared outside the Inn of the Welcome Wench as the party departed for the moathouse; accompanied them to the ruins and set up camp outside
- *Also known as: The Duke, Duke.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core drive:** Money. Cynical and pragmatic with no ideological attachment to the cult or any employer
- Affable and unhurried — mocked Calmer's stretching routine, offered to cook frog legs, volunteered for guard duty without being asked
- Underneath the easygoing exterior is a man who knows how to survive: immediately provided actionable, ruthless advice (dismember bodies, remove tongues) when his life depended on it
- Loyalty is transactional. He will work for whoever pays him

## History with the Party

**Moathouse Expeditions (Planting 18 onward)**
Met the party outside the Inn of the Welcome Wench on Planting 18, accompanied them to the moathouse, and set up camp outside the drawbridge on both visits, refusing to enter. Kept spirits light — mocked Calmer's warm-up routine, offered to cook giant frog legs after the first frog encounter (earning a glare from Zinia). When the party emerged with rescued prisoners, he immediately volunteered to guard and care for them.

**Rannos Davl's Shop — Ambush & Interrogation**
The party lured Thorne out from the shop's back area and overwhelmed him in a surprise attack. Zephyr landed multiple hits, Zinnia followed up, and Sequoia delivered the killing blow that knocked him unconscious. Under interrogation he:
- Confirmed he was a hired hand with no cult loyalty
- Identified Rannos and Gremag as agents of the **Deep Temple** (overarching leadership of the Temple of Elemental Evil)
- Warned the party of severe retribution from the cult
- Advised dismembering bodies and removing tongues to defeat *Speak with Dead*
- Offered his cleanup services on retainer: **20 gp/month + 100 gp/incident**

**Used as Leverage** — His unconscious body was used by the party as a prop/threat when they entered Rannos's inner office.

**Misdirection Campaign**
Once retained, Thorne directed split-party cover ops — sent **Zinnia and Calmer** to plant bloodied clothes outside town (simulating a struggle/flight), and sent **Zephyr and Sequoia** to the Inn of the Welcome Wench to spread a cover story that Rannos and Gremag had left urgently with a magical chest.

**Retainer Agreement** — Party agreed to terms, paid **100 gp** for the first cleanup job, and released him.

**Post-Lareth Operation** — Accepted a reduced fee of **50 gp** to clean up after the Lareth operation. Continued to confirm his mercenary, non-ideological nature.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Moathouse exterior camp / at large following the retainer agreement
- **Active arrangement:** On informal retainer with the party (20 gp/month + per-incident fees)
- **What the party knows:** He is a capable, cynical hired sword with no cult allegiance, useful cleanup skills, and inside knowledge of Deep Temple personnel and methods. He talks freely when it benefits him.
- **What remains hidden:** Unknown whether he has any other employers or contacts the party is unaware of; his full background and how he came to know Deep Temple tradecraft are unexplained

## Relationships
- **Rannos Davl / Gremag:** Former employers; Thorne holds no loyalty to them and gave them up without hesitation
- **Deep Temple / Temple of Elemental Evil:** No stated allegiance; his inside knowledge of their personnel and methods is unexplained
- **The Party (general):** Cautious working relationship built on mutual self-interest; on friendly terms
- **Sequoia:** Delivered the blow that knocked him out — Thorne has shown no apparent grudge
- **Calmer:** Target of Thorne's light mockery (the stretching incident)
- **Zinia:** Recipient of a glare over the frog legs offer; minor friction
