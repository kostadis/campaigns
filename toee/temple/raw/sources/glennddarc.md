# Glennddarc

## Identity
- **Role:** Headgnome of Sheernobb
- **Faction:** Village leadership of Sheernobb
- **First Appearance:** Planting 21, 579 — encountered at the Moot Hall in Sheernobb
- *Also known as: Headgnome Glennddarc, Headgnome.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** Protecting Sheernobb from giants, which he views as the primary existential threat to the village
- **Traits:** Elderly and irascible, Glennddarc has little patience for what he sees as misdirected priorities. He is politically shrewd, dismissing Captain Gnonsbim's bandit concerns as electioneering rather than genuine security interest. He falls asleep mid-conversation, suggesting age, indifference, or both.

## History with the Party
- **Planting 21, 579:** The party met Glennddarc at the Moot Hall, apparently rousing him from sleep. He was visibly annoyed that they were focused on bandits rather than giants. He deflected their bandit inquiries by redirecting them to Pasmarie Clee's beehives for information, implied Gnonsbim's concerns were politically (and romantically) motivated, and offered greater rewards for addressing the giant threat. He fell back asleep before the party could respond.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Moot Hall, Sheernobb
- **Active Plans:** Seeking outside help to deal with giants threatening the village; willing to pay for it
- **Party Knows:** He deprioritizes bandits, considers giants the real threat, and is directing resources accordingly
- **Hidden from Party:** His specific information about the giants (if any) was never shared; he fell asleep before the conversation concluded

## Relationships
- **Captain Gnonsbim:** Dismissive of him; believes his bandit focus is electioneering and implies he is romantically interested in Pasmarie Clee
- **Pasmarie Clee:** Referenced as a local with knowledge of bandit activity near her beehives; Glennddarc directed the party to her
- **The Party:** Mildly antagonistic opening — he found them frustrating but offered incentive to redirect their efforts
