## Romag

Romag is the leader of the Earth Temple who met with the party after they survived the chimera attack. He received the news of the Fire Temple's assassination attempt with thunderous fury, expressing outrage over both the audacity of the attack and the theft of prisoners. After the party explained that the Fire Temple had financially overextended itself through the chimera purchase, Romag declared that the moment to strike had arrived and ordered the party to deal with Alrrem, the weakened leader of the Fire Temple. His preferred leadership style is passive and erosive — positioning himself as a chess king who sends others to fight his battles rather than wielding destructive, direct force. This style is a point of quiet contempt for his sub-general Hartsch. Romag is currently unaware that the party is plotting his assassination with Hartsch's encouragement. Last known location: Romag's Chambers within the Earth Temple.

## Hartsch

Hartsch is Romag's sub-general and speaker within the Earth Temple, and the party met with him privately in their quarters following the report to Romag. He provided detailed tactical intelligence on the temple factions: he revealed that Alrrem's power had been declining since the Battle of Emridy Meadows a decade prior, that the loss of the Fire Temple's werewolves combined with the ruinous cost of the chimera had left Alrrem nearly broken, and that the Air Temple under Kelno was a farcical remnant with no meaningful power or territory. He identified Vesrek as an ally of Belsornig who arranged the chimera purchase as a calculated Water Temple scheme — a win-win designed to either eliminate the party or bankrupt the Fire Temple. Hartsch expressed a strong philosophical preference for the destructive, earthquake-like exercise of power over Romag's slow-erosion approach, framing this in terms of the earth's true vengeance. When Zinnia used deception to probe his ambitions, Hartsch responded with thinly veiled intent, stating that if a position of leadership were to "suddenly open itself," he would absolutely consider himself worthy of it — a clear signal that he wants the party to assassinate Romag and install him in command. The party interpreted this as an actionable alliance and agreed privately to move forward with a plan to kill Romag, elevate Hartsch, and engineer a three-way war between the Earth, Fire, and Water temples to destroy all factions from within. Last known location: the party's private quarters within the Earth Temple.

## Alrrem

Alrrem is the prophet and leader of the Fire Temple, described as currently weakened and near breaking. He was originally a dominant figure who led the combined temple forces at the catastrophic Battle of Emridy Meadows a decade ago. Since that defeat, his power has steadily eroded — he lost his werewolves in a prior engagement with the party, and the enormous financial cost of purchasing the chimera through Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader has left him nearly spent. It was in Alrrem's name that the Fire Temple warlock declared war on the party, proclaiming him "the all-consuming" and announcing the chimera as his champion sent to break their bones. Romag, upon hearing of the failed assassination attempt, ordered the party to deal with Alrrem directly, declaring the moment to strike had arrived. Hartsch confirmed Alrrem's weakened state, reinforcing that he is now a viable target. Alrrem did not appear in person during this session. Current status: alive but severely weakened; targeted for elimination by the party on Romag's orders.

## Belsornig

Belsornig is the prophet and head of the Water Temple, identified by Hartsch as a cunning political manipulator operating behind the scenes. He did not appear in person but was implicated in the chimera incident through documentary evidence found on the slain warlock. The ledger recovered from the warlock's remains named Vesrek — identified by Hartsch as Belsornig's ally — as the party who arranged the chimera purchase through Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader. Hartsch described the scheme as a calculated win-win for the Water Temple: either the party is killed, weakening the Earth Temple, or the Fire Temple bankrupts itself paying for the chimera. Belsornig is characterized as a formidable rival whose elimination Hartsch identified as the true prize beyond the weakened Fire Temple. Current status: not present; operating as an unseen antagonist. Last known affiliation: Water Temple.

## Kelno

Kelno is the prophet of the Air Temple, mentioned only in Hartsch's political briefing during the private meeting. Hartsch dismissed him and his faction outright as a joke — a farcical remnant reduced to a handful of rooms with no meaningful power or forces remaining. Kelno was not given further detail and did not appear in person. Current status: alive but irrelevant as a faction threat according to Hartsch.

## Vesrek

Vesrek is identified in the ledger recovered from the slain Fire Temple warlock as the individual who arranged the chimera purchase from Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader on behalf of — or in coordination with — the Water Temple. Hartsch confirmed that Vesrek is an ally of Belsornig, the Water Temple prophet, and framed the arrangement as a deliberate manipulation designed to benefit the Water Temple regardless of outcome. Vesrek did not appear in person during this session. Current status: alive; role confirmed as Water Temple operative and arranger of the chimera transaction.

## Captain Varsik

Captain Varsik is named in the ledger found on the slain Fire Temple warlock as the seller of the chimera. He is the captain of the Crimson Trader, a vessel whose crest — a crimson ship — appeared as a wax seal on the bill of sale document. The ledger confirmed the chimera was purchased through him and paid in full by the Fire Temple, with the transaction arranged by Vesrek. Varsik did not appear in person. Current status: unknown; last known association is the Crimson Trader vessel.
