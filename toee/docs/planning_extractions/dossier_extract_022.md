## Romag

Romag woke the party after eight hours of rest to deliver urgent news about Lieutenant Krell's defection to the gnoll and bugbear territories. He outlined a trap: Earth Temple forces would guard the entrance and exit while the party approached from behind through gnoll-infested corridors. Guards overheard by the party described Romag as a new and inexperienced leader who micromanages and lacks the cleverness of his predecessor, who was promoted to the deep temple.

After the party successfully subdued Krell and brought him back alive as ordered, Romag was immensely pleased. He declared that all must witness the consequences of betrayal and ordered the party to attend a ritual sacrifice in the great chamber. Atop a twenty-foot pyramid of hard-packed dirt, Romag chanted to the elder elemental earth god, shoved dirt into Krell's mouth, and commanded four massive earth elementals to rise from the ground and tear Krell apart before the cheering crowd.

Following the ritual, Romag was overheard with his lieutenant Hartsch lamenting that the defeat had cost valuable resources and weakened plans to overwhelm the Fire Temple. He acknowledged Hartsch's suggestion to use the party as weapons against the Fire or Air Temple. When the party complained that Commander Joradin had restricted their movement, Romag dismissed Joradin as a new recruit brought in by Dick Rentsch who didn't yet understand how things worked. Romag then granted the party formal territory over the gnoll and bugbear areas they had cleared, provided written permission for them to explore freely, and warned them sternly not to betray his trust. He cautioned that holding territory made them targets, as advancement in the temple often came by stepping over rivals.

Romag's last known location is within the Earth Temple, where he disappeared through a secret door after the ritual. His authority over the Earth Temple appears genuine but fragile, relying on massed low-level troops rather than powerful monsters, and he is visibly eager to prove himself.

## Lieutenant Krell

Krell was revealed as a traitor who had fled to gnoll and bugbear territories to plot rebellion against the Earth Temple. During the party's assault, his voice was heard encouraging the gnolls with the words "I told you they would try to kill you. The Fire Temple protects its own," confirming he had defected to the Fire Temple under Alremm. He wielded a peculiar hand crossbow contraption of his own devising—described as a pistol—and used it to target Calmer directly, firing twice and knocking the cleric unconscious with devastating shots.

When surrounded and desperate, Krell attempted to negotiate, arguing that the Earth Temple used its followers as cannon fodder while the Fire Temple valued power and glory, and offering to join the party to destroy the Earth Temple and be hailed as heroes. The party rejected his offer. Sequioa delivered the final non-lethal blow that knocked Krell unconscious, and the party brought him bloodied and restrained before Romag.

Krell screamed that he had been tricked as Romag shoved dirt into his mouth atop the ritual pyramid. He was then killed by four earth elementals at Romag's command, his body torn apart before the assembled crowd of Earth Temple worshippers. His death was noted by temple guards as creating opportunities for promotion. His quarters contained platinum pieces hidden in a brazier, gold, a three-dragon ante set, flasks of oil, and a secret door leading deeper into the complex.

## Hartsch

Hartsch is Romag's lieutenant, observed in conversation with Romag following the ritual execution of Krell. He assessed the costs of the defeat frankly, noting that it had consumed valuable resources and weakened the Earth Temple's plans to overwhelm the Fire Temple. Hartsch proposed that the party—the new allies Romag had acquired—could be used as weapons against the Fire or Air Temple. His last known location is within the Earth Temple alongside Romag.

---

## Commander Joradin

Joradin arrived and confronted the party while they were exploring beyond their sanctioned area. He acknowledged their status as new Earth Temple recruits but asserted his authority over that section of the temple and ordered them to return to their quarters. When the party brought this confrontation to Romag, Romag dismissed Joradin as a new recruit brought in by Dick Rentsch who did not yet understand how things worked in the temple. Joradin's last known location is the section of the Earth Temple he claims authority over.

---

## Dick Rentsch

Dick Rentsch is mentioned only indirectly by Romag as the person who brought Commander Joradin into the Earth Temple. Rentsch does not appear directly in this session but is established as a figure with enough standing to introduce new personnel into the temple hierarchy.

---

## Alremm

Alremm is referenced by Krell as the leader of the Fire Temple. Krell invoked Alremm's name and the Fire Temple's values of power and glory when attempting to recruit the party to his cause, contrasting it with the Earth Temple's practice of using followers as cannon fodder. Alremm does not appear directly in this session.
