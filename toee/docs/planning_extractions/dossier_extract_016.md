## Thorne (the Duke)

Thorne, also known as "the Duke," was a captured mercenary whom the party consulted after slaying Rannos and Gremag. He immediately identified the two dead merchants as agents of the Deep Temple, the overarching leadership of the Temple of Elemental Evil, and warned the party of severe retribution from that organization.

Thorne revealed practical knowledge of cleanup tradecraft, advising the party to dismember the bodies and remove the tongues to prevent the use of *Speak with Dead* against them. He personally executed the cleanup and directed the party on how to stage misdirection: he sent Zinnia and Calmer to abandon the victims' bloodied clothes outside town to simulate a struggle, while Zephyr and Sequoia were instructed to return to the Inn of the Welcome Wench and spread a cover story about Rannos and Gremag urgently leaving town with a magical chest.

Thorne offered his services to the party on a retainer basis, asking 20 gold pieces per month plus 100 gold pieces per incident. The party agreed and paid him 100 gold pieces for this first job, after which he was untied. His behavior throughout confirmed a cynical, money-driven character with no loyalty to the cult.

Last known status: Freed by the party and operating as a paid retainer.

## Ostler Goodingoat

Ostler Goodingoat, the tavern keeper at the Inn of the Welcome Wench, questioned the party about the sudden and unexplained departure of the merchants Rannos and Gremag. The party successfully bluffed him with the cover story about the magical chest, and he accepted the explanation without further suspicion.

Last known status: Tending the Inn of the Welcome Wench, unaware of the true fate of Rannos and Gremag.

## Elmo

Elmo was a member of Hommlet's town committee who had previously presented himself as a drunken buffoon. Zinnia's natural 20 Insight roll confirmed the party's suspicions that his inebriation was entirely feigned and that he was in fact an agent working for Verbobonc.

When Zephyr directly exposed his act, Elmo dropped the pretense immediately and agreed to speak with the party as equals in a private room. He confirmed his role as a Verbobonc operative and expressed dismay upon hearing that the chest had supposedly left town with Rannos and Gremag. He rushed off to dispatch rangers to pursue the missing chest.

Before leaving, Elmo gave the party an urgent mission: return to the Moathouse immediately to investigate and destroy whatever evil presence remained there. He explained that the cultists potentially now possessing the chest might enact unknown rituals, and emphasized the political sensitivity of the situation — Verbobonc could not send troops directly without risking conflict with the gnomes of the Kron Hills, making the party's independent action necessary.

Last known status: Left the Inn of the Welcome Wench to contact rangers regarding the missing chest; whereabouts otherwise unknown.

## Burne

Burne approached the party the following morning alongside Rufus, confirming that Elmo had already sent rangers after the missing chest. Burne and Rufus jointly urged the party to deal with the Moathouse, reinforcing the same directive Elmo had given the night before.

Last known status: In Hommlet, having spoken with the party the morning after the killings.

## Rufus

Rufus approached the party the following morning alongside Burne, confirming Elmo had dispatched rangers after the missing chest and adding his voice to the call for the party to address the Moathouse threat.

Last known status: In Hommlet, having spoken with the party the morning after the killings.

## Lareth the Beautiful

Lareth the Beautiful was encountered at his headquarters within the Moathouse, reached through a secret entrance where the party first killed two gnoll guards and delivered their severed heads as a dramatic opening gesture to Lareth's remaining guard.

Lareth greeted the party with predatory suspicion, having already heard rumors of the deaths at the Earth Temple. When the party attempted to lie — claiming the Deep Temple had taken the chest and that Rannos and Gremag had been acting on Lareth's behalf — they were executing a calculated strategy to provoke infighting within the Temple's ranks. Despite the party succeeding on their group persuasion check, Lareth rolled a high check of his own and saw through the deception entirely.

Lareth reacted with fury, accusing the party of treachery and blaming them for failing his mission while attempting to backstab him. When Zephyr mocked him directly — "The only person here that's backstabbing is you" — Lareth's response was immediate and decisive: he called his guards to arms and initiated combat.

Lareth's defining characteristic in this encounter was extreme paranoia, which ultimately overrode even a successful persuasion attempt by the party.

Last known status: Initiating combat with the party at the Moathouse; fight unresolved at session end.
