## NPC Activity

**Black Jay**
- Described by a tavern patron as a mysterious, forlorn herdsman operating as an informant for local bandits.
- Relationship to the Temple of Elemental Evil is unknown to locals; Zinia notes this as significant intelligence.

**Y'dey (Head Cleric of Hommlet)**
- Has mysteriously disappeared. Current whereabouts unknown.
- A tavern patron suspects Terjon played a role in her disappearance.

**Terjon (Priest of St. Cuthbert)**
- Now acting head of the Temple of St. Cuthbert in Y'dey's absence.
- Described by locals as closed-mouthed about Y'dey's disappearance.
- Met with Zinia; recognized him as a recent convert and spoke privately with him.
- Agreed to release Calmer to join the party.
- Issued a directive identical to those of Jaroo and Rufus: all information discovered must be shared with him and him alone, framing it as a matter of Oerth's safety.

**Jaroo (Druid, Keeper of Beory's Grove)**
- Met with the party at his grove in Hommlet.
- Revealed he was sent to Hommlet to observe events following the defeat of the Temple of Elemental Evil.
- Expressed open disgust toward Zephyr (a Tiefling), consistent with druidic views on unnatural beings.
- Provided historical context on the Moathouse: formerly the Temple's advance outpost, used for raids and as a staging ground for westward expansion, ended at the Battle of Emiry Meadows.
- Drew a direct parallel between the party's discovered outpost network and the Temple's original ring-of-fortresses strategy.
- Directed the party to investigate the Moathouse.
- Issued an information control directive: report findings to him and him alone; warned that Temple agents may be present in Hommlet and that Burne and Rufus would use intelligence to suppress local freedoms.

**Rufus (Military Commander, Verbobonc Representative)**
- Met with the party at the partially constructed tower.
- Received the party's report on potential Temple resurgence; expressed skepticism alongside Burne.
- Attempted to recruit the party as voluntary informants, framing it as a matter of critical secrecy.
- When voluntary compliance was not forthcoming, formally deputized the party under his military authority.
- Issued an explicit ultimatum: share all information with him first or face arrest or worse if they approach the Moathouse.
- Appears to be in a romantic relationship with Burne.

**Burne (Mage, Verbobonc Representative)**
- Present at the tower meeting; arrived mid-briefing.
- Reacted with horror to the suggestion of a Temple resurgence, calling it impossible.
- Appears to be in a romantic relationship with Rufus.
- No independent directives issued to the party.

## Faction Movements

**Temple of Elemental Evil (Suspected Resurgence)**
- No direct agents appeared, but Jaroo identified the party's discovered outpost network as structurally identical to the Temple's pre-defeat ring-of-fortresses expansion pattern, suggesting active replication of original strategy.
- The Moathouse is identified as the likely focal point of renewed activity.

**Church of St. Cuthbert (Hommlet Chapter)**
- Terjon has consolidated control of the local church following Y'dey's disappearance.
- Actively seeking to control information flow regarding the Temple threat.
- Released Calmer to accompany the party, contingent on Terjon receiving exclusive intelligence.

**Verbobonc (Crown/Church Representatives — Burne and Rufus)**
- Formally extended military authority over the party via deputization.
- Actively positioning to be the primary recipients of any intelligence on the Temple resurgence.
- Castle construction ("Burne's Folly") ongoing; interpreted by locals and Jaroo as a mechanism for absorbing Hommlet into Verbobonc's political and religious sphere.

**Druidic Circle / Church of Beory (Jaroo)**
- Actively monitoring Hommlet and the surrounding region for signs of Temple resurgence.
- In direct ideological opposition to Verbobonc's expansion and St. Cuthbert's proselytizing mission.
- Seeking independent intelligence on the Moathouse, separate from Verbobonc or the Church of St. Cuthbert.

**Local Bandit Network**
- Maintains an informant (Black Jay) embedded among local herdsmen.
- Locals appear unaware of any connection between the bandits and the Temple.

## Revealed Information

- **Black Jay's role:** A local herdsman named Black Jay is acting as a bandit informant. Locals do not know of any Temple connection.
- **Y'dey's disappearance:** The head cleric has vanished under unclear circumstances; Terjon, who now holds her position, is suspected by at least one local to be involved.
- **Moathouse history:** The Moathouse was the Temple of Elemental Evil's forward outpost and command post for regional subjugation prior to the Battle of Emiry Meadows. Its current state and occupants are unknown.
- **Temple expansion pattern:** The Temple historically used rings of outposts to extend its reach outward in growing circles. The current outpost activity mirrors this exact pattern.
- **Three-way information control conflict:** Jaroo, Terjon, and Rufus have each independently demanded exclusive first-access to any intelligence the party uncovers — each for different ideological or political reasons. The party is aware of the conflict and has committed to none.
- **Verbobonc's regional agenda:** Local sentiment and Jaroo both interpret the castle construction as a Verbobonc strategy to bring Hommlet under St. Cuthbert's ecclesiastical authority, threatening the region's existing religious plurality (particularly Beory worship).
