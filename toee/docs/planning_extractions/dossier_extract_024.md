## Romag

Romag met with the party following their clearance of a dungeon section, expressing pleasure at their success and inviting them to sit for an ideological training session. He explained the foundational doctrine of the Temple of Elemental Evil: the universe is defined by constant chaos between elements, with earth destined to reduce all things to dirt, and the temple exists to hasten that inevitable reduction to wasteland. When the party asked why the temple was not called simply the Temple of Earth, Romag responded with visible frustration, explaining that the Earth Temple alone could not overcome all opposition, leading to an alliance of all four elemental temples—earth, fire, water, and air—with the understanding that their internal differences would be settled only after shared victory.

Romag recounted the defeat ten years prior at the Battle of Emory Meadows, where temple forces were routed by the Archbishop of Veluna, the Kingdom of Furyondy, and the Circle of Eight—including wizards Bigby, Tenser, and Mordenkainen—who subsequently locked the temple itself, cutting off access to its central driving power. He described the present situation as one of plotting and searching for the key to unlock the temple's heart.

After judging the party sufficiently loyal based on their cleared territory, ritual attendance, and general conduct, Romag shared brandy with them and revealed that the key is an orb—the only object capable of forcing the chaotic elements to submit to earth's stability. He described it as the shackle that binds storm and flood, capable of imposing the discipline of stone upon fire and water. He stated that possession of the orb would grant him the mandate of the temple, allow him to supersede the bureaucratic authority of the greater temple, flood the dungeon with earth elementals and gorgons, and crush all opposing temples. He also confirmed the orb serves as the key to a vault opening the deep places within the temple, but refused to reveal what the vault contained, stating that information had to be earned through further demonstrated loyalty.

Romag tasked the party with recruiting the mercenary company called the Broken Blades, citing intelligence from Dick Rentsch that they were currently taking bids in Nulb. He expressed dissatisfaction with gnolls and bugbears as unreliable and unruly, preferring disciplined soldiers loyal to gold. He noted urgency because the Water Temple was competing for the same recruits.

Upon the party's successful return with the Broken Blades, Romag was extremely pleased. He assigned the newly cleared territory to the mercenaries under the party's command, though he claimed a portion of the territory for himself. That night he hosted a celebratory drinking party in the cleared banquet hall—still reeking of cadavers and dead creatures—attended by leaders from various parts of the temple. He proposed a toast to Sequoia, Zephyr, Zinnia, and Calmer, specifically singling out Calmer's conversion from a cleric of St. Cuthbert to a follower of the elder elemental god as a notable achievement for the Earth Temple's growing power.

Romag is currently positioned as the party's direct superior within the Earth Temple hierarchy, progressively sharing deeper secrets as their loyalty is demonstrated, and is actively scheming to locate the orb and dominate the other elemental temples.

## Dick Rentsch

Dick Rentsch had previously sent word to Romag about the Broken Blades mercenary company being recruited in Nulb. When the party arrived at the Watersteide Hostel, he met with them and took them to a private back room to discuss business. He explained the three methods by which the Broken Blades' captain Dren Halveth could be recruited: outbidding the Water Temple, defeating him in a fist fight, or completing a task for him. He arranged the meeting between the party and Dren Halveth.

When Calmer questioned Dick about keeping Wat the barkeep on his payroll despite Wat's apparent loyalty to the Water Temple, Dick responded that until complete power was secured, everyone had to work together, indicating a pragmatic, politically cautious approach to operating in Nulb's factional environment.

Dick Rentsch's last known location is the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb, where he operates as a fixer or intermediary with connections to the Earth Temple and knowledge of mercenary activity in the region.

## Dren Halveth

Dren Halveth is the captain of the Broken Blades, described as a half-orc with broad shoulders, extensive scarring, and one ruined white eye. He met with the party at the Watersteide Hostel with his crew positioned behind him in a relaxed but ready posture, fingers near weapons. He interrogated the party on who sent them, their business in Nulb, and which gods they prayed to.

Dren revealed that a shipment of weapons belonging to his company had been stolen by the Crimson Reapers, who were occupying the room next door at the hostel. He tasked the party with retrieving the weapons without drawing broader temple attention, using this as his condition for considering recruitment.

When the party returned and explained that the Crimson Reapers had given him until midnight to join the temple or face death, Dren expressed skepticism, citing the Water Temple's large monetary offer and characterizing the Earth Temple as an organization that used recruits as cannon fodder. He was persuaded by Sequoia's argument that the Water Temple relied on propaganda while the Earth Temple demonstrated real commitment by personally recruiting them and offering shared territory. He agreed to join the Earth Temple in exchange for one hundred gold pieces to help establish their new operation.

As the party departed, Wat the barkeep approached Dren and whispered something to him, but Dren shoved him away, suggesting he had committed to the Earth Temple arrangement. Dren Halveth and the Broken Blades are now stationed in the newly cleared dungeon territory, reporting to the party under Romag's authority.

## Wat

Wat is the barkeep at the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb. Zinnia observed that he appeared very unhappy with the outcome of the party's recruitment of the Broken Blades, confirming suspicion that he works for or is loyal to the Water Temple. As the party and Broken Blades departed, Wat approached Dren Halveth and whispered something to him, though Dren shoved him away. Dick Rentsch acknowledged keeping Wat on staff despite his divided loyalties, framing it as a necessary compromise until greater power is consolidated. Wat's last known location is the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb.

## The Crimson Reaper (unnamed spokesperson)

A tattooed and scarred member of the Crimson Reapers answered the door when Calmer knocked. He became hostile when asked about the stolen weapons chest. Upon learning Calmer worked for the Earth Temple, he clarified that the chest was temple property that Dren Halveth had stolen, and issued an ultimatum: Dren had until midnight to join the temple or he and his crew would be killed. His phrasing—referring to "the temple" as a singular entity distinct from the four elemental temples—indicated the Crimson Reapers serve a deeper organization operating behind all four elemental temples rather than any one of them directly. He is last known to have been in the room adjacent to Dren Halveth's at the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb.
