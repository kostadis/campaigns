## Prefect Terjon

Terjon appeared at the Temple of St. Cuthbert when Calmer and the group arrived to present their findings from the moathouse. Calmer excitedly showed him the marked stone as proof of elemental evil activity. Terjon examined the stone — rubbing it between his fingers and weighing it — then dismissed it with contempt, calling it a "painted rock" and criticizing the group for losing their bandit prisoner rather than bringing him in for questioning. He told them to find actionable information rather than trinkets.

When Zinia asked for the stone back, Terjon surprised the group by saying he wanted to keep it — a detail Zephyr found suspicious. When Zephyr challenged him on why he would want to keep something he called worthless, Terjon visibly winced as if in physical discomfort, then threw the rock to Zephyr and muttered that Zephyr was right, it was worthless. His initial desire to retain the stone and his pained reaction to Zephyr's question suggest he may have recognized its significance despite his dismissive words.

Terjon is a member of the village council. Calmer later excused himself from the meeting with Burne and Rufus, saying he needed to return to the Temple for prayers, suggesting an ongoing relationship with Terjon's institution.

Last known location: Temple of St. Cuthbert, Hommlet.

---

## Burne

Burne appeared at the Inn of the Welcome Wench with Rufus, where they had summoned the party via militiamen. He examined the marked stone that Zephyr handed over and reacted with initial skepticism ("This is it?"), but quickly became thoughtful alongside Rufus upon reflection.

Burne is one of the leaders overseeing construction of the keep outside Hommlet. He told the group to keep their investigation quiet, though Zephyr privately noted that half the village already seemed to know their business. He is a member of the village council. His blueprints for the keep went missing and were later returned with charcoal dust on them, indicating someone copied them. He moved the plans to a more private location but remained worried.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet, before departing with guards.

---

## Rufus

Rufus appeared alongside Burne at the Inn of the Welcome Wench. He asked directly what the group had found and, upon seeing the stone, shared sensitive intelligence about the copied blueprints. He revealed that he had discovered the keep's blueprints missing, found them returned later with charcoal dust traces, and concluded a saboteur — likely a worker in the encampments — had copied them for the bandits. He connected this theft to the stone the group recovered, which convinced him the threat was serious. He tasked the group with finding the thief.

Rufus is a council member and co-leader of the keep construction project alongside Burne.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet, before departing with guards.

---

## Jaroo

Jaroo is the druid of Hommlet. The group visited him after their meeting with Burne and Rufus, presenting him with the stone and the bandit's confession. Like the other town leaders, Jaroo was disappointed with the evidence and told them they needed to go deeper into the moathouse to find more proof.

As Zephyr left the grove, Jaroo called after him asking who else they had spoken to. When Zinia told him the truth, Jaroo was pleased and rewarded the group with the blessing of Beory and a healing potion. He is a member of the village council. Ostler had already noted to the group that Jaroo would want to be informed of whatever they had discussed with the other leaders.

Last known location: His grove, Hommlet.

---

## Elder Kenter

Elder Kenter is the village elder of Hommlet, residing in a well-defended compound with an eight-foot stone wall and heavy gate, shared with his four sons, their wives, and grandchildren. He received the trio after his second son Hawthorne reported their movements through the village.

Kenter demonstrated awareness of all local political dynamics, naming the full council membership: Jaroo, Rufus, Burne, Ostler, Captain Zeb Fletcher, and Mytch the Miller. He expressed concern that the council had chosen not to present the party to the full council so all members could learn simultaneously. He framed the bandit threat as a danger to village safety and stability, praised Burne and Rufus's leadership, and emphasized unified leadership as the village's greatest strength. He concluded by instructing the group to come to him first going forward.

His wife Valsenora brought refreshments to the meeting. Kenter's manner was described as grandfatherly, and he was noted to have eyes showing uncommon wisdom.

Last known location: His compound, Hommlet.

---

## Ostler

Ostler is the innkeeper of the Inn of the Welcome Wench and a member of the village council. He approached the trio after their meeting with Burne and Rufus to make note that Jaroo would expect to be consulted as well, subtly pressuring Zinia to go speak with the druid. He enforced the inn's policy of a minimum three-night stay and warned that goods left behind would be sold after three days.

Later, Ostler hired the trio covertly, bringing them into the kitchen to explain his job: to prove that Furnok was stealing from the inn's guests. He offered free rooms for three nights as payment. When Zinia obtained the loaded dice as evidence, Ostler confronted Furnok directly, escalating the situation until Elmo intervened.

Ostler also provided travel advice when the group decided to return Airgid, explaining the fastest route west through the Kron Hills and the Greenway Valley past gnomish mining towns.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet.

---

## Furnok

Furnok, referred to as "Furnok the Fabulous," was present at the Inn of the Welcome Wench and suspected by Ostler of cheating the inn's guests. Zephyr's pickpocket attempt against him failed, prompting Zephyr to tip over Furnok's table as a distraction. Zinia grabbed Furnok's dice in the chaos and confirmed they were loaded — the proof Ostler needed. When Ostler confronted Furnok with the evidence, Furnok rejected the accusations and the confrontation began to escalate before Elmo intervened and told him to leave or face Captain Fletcher.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet, before being ejected.

---

## Elmo

Elmo is a burly, muscular man who was drinking at another table in the Inn of the Welcome Wench during the confrontation with Furnok. He intervened by threatening to call Captain Fletcher if Furnok did not leave, which ended the confrontation. Sequoia identified him as Elmo and noted he smelled strongly of alcohol, describing him as a drunk.

Despite his apparent state, Elmo is an adventurer and ranger by profession. When Zephyr mentioned there was a monster in the Gnarley Forest that would require a tracker, Elmo immediately volunteered himself for the job.

Elmo is the son of Captain Zeb Fletcher, who is a council member. His brother is Mytch the Miller, also a council member.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet.

---

## Spugnoir

Spugnoir is a wizard present at the Inn of the Welcome Wench. When Sequoia suggested speaking with him, Spugnoir deflected the rumor about a secret passage under the inn ("The only thing under this inn is stinky food") but immediately pivoted to reveal that he knows the location of a lost wizard's laboratory under Emiry Meadows. He offered to join the group as their fourth member in exchange for the first pick of any arcane scrolls found.

Sequoia accepted, noting that Spugnoir seemed strangely familiar. Spugnoir proposed departing the following day due to the late hour.

The following morning (Planting 18), Spugnoir was at the inn waiting alongside Calmer, having expected to go adventuring the previous day. He discovered the party had left without him to return Airgid. When Zephyr chose Calmer over him for the moathouse expedition due to the need for a healer, Spugnoir displayed the look of someone accustomed to being passed over, smiled, and accepted the decision.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet.

---

## Calmer

Calmer is a cleric affiliated with the Temple of St. Cuthbert who travels with the group. He led the initial presentation to Prefect Terjon, excitedly laying out the evidence they had gathered. He was shocked by Terjon's dismissive response.

During the meeting with Burne and Rufus at the inn, Calmer excused himself to return to the Temple for prayers, asking if the group would meet again the next day at the same time. On Planting 18, he was waiting at the Inn of the Welcome Wench alongside Spugnoir, having been left behind the previous day when the group departed to return Airgid without him.

Zephyr chose Calmer over Spugnoir for the moathouse expedition on the grounds that a healer was needed. Inside the moathouse courtyard, Calmer suggested finishing the full exploration of the ruins to avoid leaving threats behind, a suggestion Sequoia agreed with. He expressed visible nervousness when departing from the Duke's camp ("Calmer gulps").

Last known location: Moathouse ruins, actively exploring with the party.

---

## Airgid

Airgid is a young silver dragon the group had been sheltering, at increasing expense to their budget, with growing concern the dragon might feed on horses. On Planting 17, the group undertook a journey west to return Airgid to its home near the Lotrimil mountains, traveling south and then north through the Kron Hills and the Greenway Valley past gnomish mining towns.

Shortly before reaching the town of Tulvar, after a full day of travel, Airgid suddenly recognized the area and cried out "HOME!" The dragon took flight westward and called back to the party that her mother would reward them, expressing confident certainty and gratitude before disappearing.

Last known location: Flying west toward the Lotrimil mountains after departing near Tulvar.

---

## Mickey

Mickey is a young man who approached the trio at the Inn of the Welcome Wench on the evening of Planting 16. He identified himself as the son of Virgil Tamen, the Braumeister. He had observed cloaked figures lurking behind the brewery around midnight and, worried they might be spies connected to rumors that the old temple was not as dead as believed, asked the group to investigate. He was visibly nervous and stumbled over his words before the trio wordlessly agreed to look into it. He left relieved.

The investigation revealed the figures were two teenagers meeting in secret, not spies. Mickey is not seen again after making his request.

Last known location: Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet.

---

## Jira

Jira is a teenage girl who was one of the two "cloaked spies" Mickey had reported behind the brewery at midnight. When Zephyr put his sword to her throat, she shrieked, and when confronted, she explained the situation: her father Jakub is a member of the old faith and disapproves of her relationship with Marek, whose father Tomlyn is a tailor and member of the Church of St. Cuthbert. The midnight meetings were secret romantic rendezvous, not espionage.

As she and Marek left, Jira pleaded with Zinia to help them by speaking to their parents, appealing to the group's status as heroes.

Last known location: Behind the brewery, Hommlet, departing after being confronted.

---

## Marek

Marek is the teenage boy accompanying Jira at the midnight brewery meeting. He began to cry when Zephyr confronted them. His father is Tomlyn, the tailor, a member of the Church of St. Cuthbert, which is the source of the conflict with Jira's father Jakub of the old faith.

Last known location: Behind the brewery, Hommlet, departing after being confronted.

---

## The Duke

The Duke appeared outside the Inn of the Welcome Wench on Planting 18 when the group set out for the moathouse. He set up camp outside the moathouse ruins and said his farewells to the party as they entered, expressing hope that he would see them again.

Last known location: Camp outside the moathouse ruins.
