## Romag

The leader of the Earth Temple, encountered in his office after the party cleared the harpies, ghouls, and ghasts from the adjacent territory. He was visibly pleased when the party reported their success in subjugating creatures that had long refused to submit to his authority. He complained that ever since his predecessor departed to work for the Deep Temple, the undead in the area had stopped cooperating with him.

Romag delivered a lengthy, passionate tirade about the power of earth to grind all things to dust, framing the party's territorial expansion in religious terms. When the party asked about payment, he corrected their expectations: the arrangement flows upward, not downward—the party is expected to bring treasure to the temple and pay him a portion, not receive compensation themselves.

When the party requested official titles and higher ranks within the Earth Temple hierarchy, Romag agreed in principle but stated that advancement requires a demonstration of loyalty. He instructed his subordinate Harch to prepare a ceremony. After overhearing Zephyr warning Calmer to be ready to die, Romag clarified that the party members are too valuable to sacrifice—prisoners would be used for the ritual instead, their bodies to be ripped apart as an offering to the earthen god. He postponed the ceremony until the following day and instructed the party to rest.

His plan is complicated by the fact that the prisoners the party previously freed are now missing, and the party intends to blame the Fire Temple for their disappearance.

Last known location: his office within the Earth Temple.

## Harch

A subordinate of Romag within the Earth Temple hierarchy. Romag instructed Harch to prepare the prisoners for the loyalty ritual ceremony, in which the party is expected to be active participants. No further details about Harch's appearance, role, or disposition were revealed.

Last known location: the Earth Temple, presumably preparing for the ritual.
