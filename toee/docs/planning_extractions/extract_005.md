## NPC Activity

**Calmer** (Cleric of St. Cuthbert)
- Arrived at the Green Dragon Inn for hire, ordered boiled eggs, milk, and bread
- Expressed over-eager, zealous demeanor during initial meeting with Zephyr
- Attempted to "lead from the front" on the drawbridge, triggering its collapse
- Fell into the moat water along with Sequoia; sustained injuries from falling debris
- Was rescued from the water by Zinia, who patched his wounds
- Entered the moathouse courtyard after the bandit engagement, made a pun ("time to call to calm down")
- Attempted to comfort the captive bandit Voss by describing Church of St. Cuthbert punishments — counterproductively increasing the prisoner's panic
- Was physically pushed aside by Sequoia during the interrogation

**Thorne / "Duke"** (Man-at-Arms, sent by Ranos Davl)
- Arrived at the Green Dragon Inn and identified himself as hired muscle sent by Ranos Davl
- Established camp just past the moathouse drawbridge, declined to enter
- Offered to cook giant frog legs after the bridge encounter
- Taunted the party about their early difficulties ("So you guys going to die before you even make it through the door")

**Voss** (Captured Bandit)
- Last surviving bandit of the moathouse gang, knocked unconscious by Zinia and interrogated
- Revealed the moathouse was used as a hideout because the Temple of Elemental Evil was believed dead and the location abandoned by adventurers
- Confirmed the bandit gang had a connection to the Temple, receiving a flat river rock inscribed with an eye and a tear as a membership token
- Described the token as coming from "the big boss" through a chain of intermediaries — indicating he was low-ranking and had no direct knowledge of Temple leadership or plans
- Broke down crying upon confrontation with the Temple symbol, aware that cult membership carries a death sentence

## Faction Movements

**Temple of Elemental Evil**
- Operatives (the bandit gang) were using the Moathouse as a forward hideout, exploiting the assumption that the Temple was defunct
- The gang's leader is now dead; three bandits escaped south through an enlarged arrow slit
- A token system using flat river rocks inscribed with an eye and a tear is in use to mark cult membership across hierarchical layers

**Church of St. Cuthbert**
- Deployed Calmer as a field operative accompanying the party, representing the Church's active interest in the Moathouse investigation

**Ranos Davl** (Faction associate, off-screen)
- Provided Thorne/Duke as contracted muscle for the party's operation, confirming an ongoing support relationship with Zephyr

## Revealed Information

- The Temple of Elemental Evil is not fully dead; it has reconstituted enough to place agents in the Moathouse and maintain a hierarchical command structure with at least three tiers (big boss → intermediate boss → foot soldiers)
- Cult membership is marked by a flat river rock inscribed with an eye and a tear, distributed downward through the chain of command
- The Moathouse was deliberately chosen as a hideout because conventional wisdom held it was cleared out and no longer of interest — suggesting Temple operatives have knowledge of regional adventuring patterns
- The bandit gang's leader is confirmed dead; Voss has no actionable intelligence on upper Temple leadership
