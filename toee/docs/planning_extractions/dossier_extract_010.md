## Grindin Well-Read

A gnome bookshop owner seated behind a desk-sized for a human, perched on a massive cushion-covered chair in Grindin Books on the northern branch of Sheernobb's main road. The shop is a small cottage crammed with hundreds of books, maps, and scrolls on solid oak shelves, with additional stacks on tables and trolleys. Grindin reads behind the counter in a plush reclining chair under green-tinted candle bulbs when not serving customers.

When Zephyr asked about bandits, Grindin was visibly annoyed at the intrusion and focused not on stolen merchandise but on the theft of his invitation to an unspecified conference: "My invitation to the conference was stolen! I didn't have the opportunity to reject the invitation!" His proposed solution to bandit attacks was enchanted wards and protective charms distributed to caravans, which Zinnia dismissed as ignorant. Despite his unhelpfulness on the bandit question, Grindin confirmed that Palsmarie Clee had information on the bandits. He also sold Zephyr a map of the local area, which later proved useful when Pasmarie Clee marked the location of the raid on it. His stock included a book titled "Map of the Elemental Nexus," noted by Zinnia as potentially worth purchasing later.

## Keenleene

The founder of Sheernobb, commemorated by an ancient grey stone statue at the base of the steps leading to the Moot Hall. The statue bears an inscription identifying her as "Keenleene, Founder of Sheernobb." When Sequoia approached, the stone figure animated through what Sequoia identified as gnome illusionism: Keenleene's gem-like eyes sparkled, her braided gemstone-adorned hair shimmered, and she laughed heartily before playing a lively tune on pipes and winking at onlookers. Sequoia explained the display to his companions as an example of gnomes' love of illusion and performance. Current status: a commemorative statue with an enchanted illusory animation.

## Glennddarc

The Headgnome of Sheernobb and elder of the Kron Hills clan village, encountered by Sequoia in the Moot Hall. He is an elder gnome with brown wrinkled skin and long white wild hair, seated in a large elegantly engraved oak chair with his bare feet crossed over one arm. He keeps a weasel as a companion, which woke him from sleep by jumping into his lap, then nuzzled his ear and bared its belly while he stroked it and watched the visitors.

When Sequoia mentioned bandits, Glennddarc was openly dismissive, stating the town's real problem was giants, not bandits. He was also contemptuous of Gnonsbim, suggesting the Captain of the Guard was pursuing the bandit issue only because he was running for re-election and wanted to appear a man of action, and further speculating that Gnonsbim had "gone sweet on" Pasmarie Clee and was pursuing the bandit investigation to impress her. He directed Sequoia to Pasmarie's beehives as the source of the bandit complaints, while making clear that the greater reward and the genuine civic need lay in dealing with giants. He fell back asleep before Sequoia could respond further. Last known location: the Moot Hall of Sheernobb, asleep in his chair.

## Pasmarie Clee

A petite gnome woman and keeper of Sheernobb's beehives, encountered at her farm a quarter mile southeast of Sheernobb. Her face is kind and weathered, framed by unruly curls, her apron smeared with honeycomb wax and pollen. She was aware of the party's arrival, having been told by Gnonsbim to expect them.

She explained that a shipment of her honey bound for the merchants of Verbobonc had been ambushed on the trade road just north of the village. The surviving guards reported the attackers spoke of the "Black Earth" and carried weapons of stone, identifying them as something more than ordinary bandits. She expressed concern not just for the monetary loss but for Sheernobb's reputation with Verbobonc merchants. She offered enchanted honey as a reward in addition to her gratitude, acknowledging she had little gold. She marked the location of the last raid on Zephyr's map when he showed it to her, placing it a few miles to the northeast. She described herself as better at tending bees than facing armed fanatics and declined to pursue the matter herself. Last known location: her beehive farm southeast of Sheernobb.

## Quinna Poul

A half-orc cook at the tavern in Sheernobb, encountered by Zephyr on the morning of Planting 22. She marked a location on Zephyr's map where he could find the wild boar, describing it as "a tricky little bugger." When Zephyr and Zinnia successfully returned with the boar, she was delighted and announced plans to throw "the most fantastic feast on the 30th of this month," also promising the party a dinner the following day of a quality they had never experienced. Last known location: the tavern kitchen in Sheernobb.

## Aunt Emma

A green hag encountered in a fertile glade in the Gnarley Forest while the party was searching for the wild boar on Planting 22. She initially appeared as an old woman in a brown ankle-length dress, rough green woolen shirt, and leather belt, approaching the party with offers of sweets and directing them to her gingerbread house — a single-room dwelling with gingerbread walls, white frosting window trim, and sugared plum roof decorations. Inside she had a circular table, chairs, and a wood oven producing a plum pie that was in fact poisonous.

Zinnia identified her as a hag before Zephyr registered the deception. When Zephyr drew his sword, her illusion of human appearance dropped, revealing green skin, clawed hands, and sharp teeth. She first threatened to eat the party, then when she perceived she would not survive the fight, pleaded for her life in exchange for revealing her treasure. Zephyr refused and killed her. Her possessions included a Hat of Wizardry and a book titled "The Hag's Guide to 101 Recipes Of Children." The gingerbread on the door frame was confirmed by Zinnia to be genuine. Current status: dead.

## Glennon Clee

A gnome encountered at the tavern on the evening of Planting 22, who approached Zinnia to ask whether his stolen barrels had been found. When Zinnia said no, Glennon asked if they would look for them and accepted Zinnia's affirmative. He subsequently showed Zinnia the location where the barrels were stolen, providing a trail that Zinnia assessed as easy to follow. His relationship to Pasmarie Clee is not specified but the shared surname suggests a family connection. Last known location: near the tavern in Sheernobb.

## The Dryad

An unnamed dryad encountered in a forest glade near a solitary tree approximately an hour from where Glennon Clee's barrels were stolen. Described as a humanoid with branches extruding from its arms and leaves growing on its head, the creature was found crying while staring at one of the stolen barrels. It had taken the barrel because it had been told that humans drink when they are "dumped," and it was suffering from unrequited love — its beloved did not reciprocate its feelings. When Zephyr approached and explained that drinking from a barrel requires opening it, that stealing is wrong, and that the barrel was not the dryad's to take, the dryad accepted the reasoning and expressed that it had not truly wanted to steal, only acted out of emotional distress. The barrels were recovered. Last known location: the forest glade near the solitary tree in the Gnarley Forest.
