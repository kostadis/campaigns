## Calmer

Calmer arrived at the Inn of the Welcome Wench on Planting 16 for breakfast with Zephyr, presenting himself in polished armor with his holy symbol — a cudgel — dangling from his wrist. He is short at 5'2", with copper-red hair, hazel eyes, and a flattish nose, and carries the over-eager look of a religious zealot. He ordered boiled eggs, milk, and bread while waiting.

On the journey to the Moathouse, Calmer admitted insecurity about this being his first adventure and initially deferred to Zephyr and Zinia. However, when the party prepared to cross the drawbridge after dispatching the first wave of giant frogs, he announced his intention to lead, declaring it was what great clerics of St. Cuthbert do. He marched onto the rotting drawbridge, and when a second wave of frogs landed on it, the combined weight caused the bridge to shatter. Calmer fell into the water and was injured by falling debris before being pulled out and healed by Zinia.

After recovering at the Duke's campsite, Calmer refused to quit when Thrune mocked the group, insisting they had come to do a job. Once inside the moathouse courtyard, he volunteered to guard the rear rather than advance — his fear apparent to Sequoia. After the ambush on the bandits concluded, Calmer walked in and made a pun on his own name ("I think it's important that we call to calm down"), visibly delighted at his own joke.

When the captured bandit Voss broke down in tears upon learning cult membership was punishable by death, Calmer's attempt to calm him consisted of explaining what the Church of St. Cuthbert does to its enemies — worsening the situation until Sequoia pushed him aside. Calmer is associated with St. Cuthbert and appears to be on his first significant field adventure.

## Thorne (Duke)

Thorne, who goes by "Duke" because his name sounds like a throne, arrived at the Inn of the Welcome Wench on Planting 16, identifying Zephyr by his Tiefling appearance and confirming he had been sent by Ranos Davl as a hired man-at-arms. He is a rugged man in his late 30s with a weathered face marked by scars, short brown hair, and piercing green eyes partially hidden under a weather-beaten leather cap, wearing studded leather armor.

On the journey to the Moathouse, Duke set up camp just past the drawbridge, wishing the group luck rather than entering with them. After the bridge collapse and frog fight, he taunted the group — "So you guys going to die before you even make it through the door?" — and offered to cook the giant frog legs, claiming they taste like chicken. He also asked whether the party wanted to take their horses with them and mockingly asked if they were calling it quits after Calmer's injury.

Duke is currently at the camp just past the Moathouse drawbridge, watching the horses and guarding the party's rear.

## Voss

Voss is a captured bandit found inside the Moathouse on Planting 16. He was knocked unconscious by Zinia during the ambush on the bandit group — whose leader and several members were killed by Zephyr, Zinia, and Sequoia — and was the sole survivor taken for interrogation.

Upon waking and seeing Zephyr, Calmer, and Sequoia standing over him with bloodied weapons, Voss broke down crying. Under questioning, he explained his path to banditry: he had a property to sell, accumulated gambling debts and a drinking problem, and things went downhill from there. He said the Moathouse was an attractive hideout because the Temple was considered dead and adventurers had already picked the place clean, meaning no one came around.

When Sequoia produced a flat river rock inscribed with an eye and a tear — found among the bandit leader's belongings — Voss became hysterically frightened. He explained that the symbol was given down through the chain of command from "the big boss" to his boss to his group, and that it signified belonging. He did not know more about its meaning or origin, confirming he was too low in the hierarchy to have useful intelligence about the cult.

Sequoia showed Voss mercy, telling him he did not need to hand him over to the authorities and only needed information about the symbol. Voss's current status after the interrogation is not specified, but he was last seen alive in the Moathouse with the party.
