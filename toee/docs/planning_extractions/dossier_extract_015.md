## Tharok

High Cultist Tharok led the earth temple faction's ritual in the vast chamber deep beneath the abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner. His physical appearance was distinctive — skin mottled like cracked stone and eyes burning with fervent intensity — marking him as deeply touched by earth elemental power. Three robed cultists knelt around him in reverence as he conducted his ritual over the Chest of Rao, which rested upon a cracked altar surrounded by concentric rings of golden runes.

When the party emerged from the secret passageway, Tharok declared them defilers of his sacred ritual and threatened that the earth itself would swallow them whole. He demonstrated formidable combat ability, striking down Jerry the Verbabonc soldier with devastating elemental attacks and causing the entire chamber to quake violently, knocking several party members to the ground and disrupting Calmer's blessing. He hurled Zephyr backward with a devastating blow even while bloodied.

When Sequioa knocked the Chest of Rao from the altar, the ritual circle was broken and the swirling magical energies began to dissipate, weakening Tharok's position. Despite this disruption, he continued fighting until Calmer landed a Guiding Bolt dealing 9 damage, granting Zinnia advantage. Zinnia then struck with a flurry of attacks totaling 14 damage, finally killing him. His death triggered a violent collapse of the ritual cavern. Brother Eldin confirmed the Abbey of Beory was safe upon learning of his defeat.

## Brother Eldin

Brother Eldin was found alive but wounded in the main abbey after the party fled the collapsing ritual cavern. He expressed relief upon learning Tharok had been vanquished and declared the Abbey of Beory safe once more. He rewarded the party for their service with 70 gold pieces, 400 silver pieces, and 400 copper pieces each, as well as a Scroll of Web and a Potion of Greater Healing.

Upon seeing the party carrying the Chest of Rao, Brother Eldin provided crucial lore about the artifact. He explained that Rao is the patron of peace and harmony and that the chest is an artifact of great good, constructed by the high priests of Rao. He warned that possessing it was extremely dangerous, as it would draw both evil forces seeking to use it as a powerful magical battery and the forces of good from the Archclericy of Veluna seeking to reclaim it. He urged the party to return the chest rather than risk the peril it would attract. The party did not follow his counsel, choosing instead to use the chest as leverage against Temple agents in Hommlet. His last known location was the Abbey of Beory.

## Jerry

Jerry was a soldier of Verbabonc found imprisoned in the monastery alongside Warda, the two engaged in heated political arguments even while captive. He represented Verbabonc's interests and agreed to help fight the cultists after the party explained the threat. He provided crucial intelligence about a secret passageway to the deeper temple, knowledge passed down from his grandmother who had once worshipped Beory in these halls. During the battle against Tharok, Jerry charged bravely toward the high cultist but was struck down by Tharok's devastating elemental attacks, his body crumpling under the fury. He did not survive the encounter.

## Warda

Warda was a gnome nationalist found imprisoned alongside Jerry in the monastery torture chamber. Despite her ideological opposition to what she viewed as Verbabonc's imperial expansion, she agreed to join the fight against the cultists after the party explained the dire threat. She remained engaged in combat even after witnessing Jerry's death, her hatred for imperial forces redirected toward the cultist threat. In the continued battle she opened the final round against Tharok, landing a strike after two misses. Tharok retaliated with a devastating blow dealing 29 damage to her, though she remained fighting. Her fate after the cavern's collapse is not recorded. Her last known location was the ritual chamber beneath the monastery.

## Rannos

Rannos was a trader in Hommlet operating as an agent of the Temple of Elemental Evil alongside his partner Gremag. The party sent him a note requesting a meeting behind the carpenter's field, but Rannos, described as suspicious and paranoid, refused that location and instead invited the party to meet at his establishment after dark. During the confrontation in his inner office, he immediately dismissed the unconscious Thorne as useless and demanded the Chest of Rao. When the party attempted to negotiate for the Temple to leave the region, he and Gremag mocked them. After Gremag initiated combat and was ultimately killed by Calmer, the party turned their full attention on Rannos. Sequioa struck him for a massive 26 points of damage. The session ended with Rannos severely wounded but not confirmed dead, with the local Temple operation in Hommlet effectively annihilated around him.

## Gremag

Gremag served as Rannos's bodyguard and fellow Temple of Elemental Evil operative in Hommlet. During the confrontation in Rannos's inner office, he immediately initiated combat upon the party's failed negotiation attempt, attacking Zinnia. In the first round he received severe blows from Zinnia and Zephyr before retaliating with two swift and brutal strikes — first severely injuring Zinnia, then knocking her unconscious with the second hit. Sequioa dealt him a further 25 points of damage. In the second round, Calmer landed a critical blow that instantly killed him. His death marked the decisive turning point of the confrontation.

## Thorne

Thorne, also referred to as Duke, was a man-at-arms hired by Rannos to serve as muscle for the Temple's Hommlet operation. The party lured him out from the shop's back area under cover of night and launched a surprise attack. Zephyr landed multiple hits, Zinnia followed, and Sequioa delivered the final strike, knocking Thorne unconscious. When interrogated, he confirmed he was a simple hired hand with no deep Temple loyalty, stating he would rather be left for dead than face the cult's wrath. The party subsequently used his unconscious body as leverage when entering Rannos's inner office.
