## Lubash

Lubash is an ogre who served as a guardian in the lower level of the moathouse, stationed in a room to the east of the main storage area. He was used by the dungeon's occupants as a disposal mechanism for prisoners: captives were held in a barred southern room and fed to Lubash one per day. At least six prisoners were subjected to this fate before the party arrived — Martin, Damien, Eltograph the Elf, and others named only in grief by the surviving merchant Derrick. Lubash announced himself by shouting "I am Lubash!" and declared the party "food" before attacking with his club. He was killed by the party in the doorway of his room. His nest contained a magical cloak of elvenkind, raising the question of which elf among his victims it belonged to.

## Derrick

Derrick is a merchant originally from Norwell, found imprisoned in the barred southern room of the moathouse dungeon alongside Florian. He was wearing a distinctive purple robe, now soiled with dirt, blood, and excrement. He and Florian were captured by brigands on the road to Hommlet and brought to the moathouse to serve as food for Lubash. Derrick recounted the names of the prisoners taken before them — Martin, Damien, and Eltograph the Elf among them — before breaking down in tears. He had no actionable intelligence about the dungeon's operators but promised a reward if the party returned him safely to Hommlet. He was left in the care of the Duke while the party continued exploring.

## Florian

Florian is a merchant from Norwell, held prisoner alongside Derrick in the moathouse dungeon under the same circumstances. He was rescued by the party and left with the Duke outside the moathouse. He offered no information beyond what Derrick provided and promised a reward upon return to Hommlet.

## Jarran

Jarran is a gnome spy sent by the merchants of Lotrimil to investigate the brigand attacks in the region around Hommlet. He was captured and imprisoned in the same pantry room as Derrick and Florian, waiting to be fed to Lubash. Upon rescue, he identified himself and his mission, expressed a desire to return to his assignment, and offered to join the party if they continued exploring. Zinnia declined his company, citing distrust of other spies. Before departing, Jarran gave the party a ring. His current whereabouts are unknown after being escorted outside the moathouse.

## Jaroo

Jaroo is the druid of Hommlet, described as grey-bearded and meeting the party in his grove. The party chose to report to him first, with Sequoia arguing he was the only trustworthy figure in town. Jaroo listened carefully to their report but pushed back on the interpretation, arguing that the evidence showed brigands masquerading as temple members rather than a genuine resurgence of the Temple of Elemental Evil. He cautioned that the information was cause for concern but not yet grounds for concrete action, and stated it must be presented to the elders. When Sequoia expressed reluctance to present, Jaroo agreed to present on their behalf and appeared flattered by the request. He arranged a meeting at the Village Elder's house for the following morning. At that meeting, he framed the narrative around brigand activity rather than temple resurgence. Ostler indicated approval of the party's decision to go to Jaroo before the "interloping outsiders." Jaroo also invited the party to participate in end-of-month festival planning, including decorating and the location committee.

## Spugnoir

Spugnoir is a magic user staying at the Inn of the Welcome Wench who had previously expressed interest in a tower near the Battle of Emiry Meadows. He appeared noticeably pleased when the party returned without Calmer. He asked about the tower and was told to wait until the next day. At the village elder's meeting he was not invited and expressed mild disappointment at not being included in the moathouse excursion. He accompanied the party to the tower, staying as far back as possible during entry. Once the tower was cleared of a smoke mephit, a dust mephit, and two cultists, he became extremely excited about the discoveries: two potions of gaseous form, a potion of healing, a magical dagger, a scroll of fog, silent image, gust of wind, and misty step, and a spellbook containing feather fall, fog cloud, mage armor, magic missile, protection from good and evil, silent image, Tenser's floating disk, unseen servant, gust of wind, misty step, rope trick, fly, gaseous form, magic circle, and sleet storm. He declared these additions would make him much more useful to the party. During the journey he shared lore about the Circle of Eight — Mordenkainen, Bigby, Ottiluke, Leomund, and others — explaining they appeared at the Battle of Emiry Meadows when an artifact was in use, and afterward bound some evil presence within the Temple of Elemental Evil. He characterized the Circle as neutral arbiters of balance. He was not taken along on the return to the moathouse on Planting 19.

## Burne

Burne is one of the two powerful figures — alongside Rufus — stationed in Hommlet, present at the village elder's meeting on Planting 19. He was dismissive of the temple threat interpretation and took a cautious stance. He commended the party for their work and expressed pleasure that the decision to deputize them had produced good intelligence. He added nothing beyond Rufus's dismissal of the information as locally but not nationally significant.

## Rufus

Rufus is the captain of the militia in Hommlet, present at the village elder's meeting. He and Burne together dismissed the idea that the information warranted alerting the arch-cleric of Verbobonc. He acknowledged strong evidence of brigand activity but stated there was no conclusive evidence of the Temple of Elemental Evil's re-emergence, characterizing the information as of local rather than national importance.

## Ostler

Ostler is the innkeeper of the Inn of the Welcome Wench. He informed Zinnia that her room was paid for through the 19th and whispered approvingly that the party had gone to Jaroo before approaching the "interloping outsiders," suggesting awareness of and alignment with Jaroo's faction in village politics. He was present at the village elder's meeting on Planting 19.

## Hestort Flintshine

Hestort Flintshine, known as Tort, is a thin man approximately five feet tall whom Zephyr identified as the person who had copied maps — likely of the moathouse — and passed them to the Duke. Zephyr spooked him at the workers' encampment near the fortress, causing him to flee toward the river, where his own bandit allies shot him. The party saved his life with a healing potion, and under Zephyr's intimidation — including a fear-inducing cantrip or ability — Tort revealed that he worked with the Duke, who paid him to obtain the plans. He claimed ignorance of the Duke's ultimate employer, saying only that the Duke was working with "some shady people" and that the person behind it was someone in the village. He could not or would not identify who. He declared he was leaving and would never be seen again. His current whereabouts are unknown.

## Lareth the Beautiful

Lareth the Beautiful is the commander of the fire temple cultists operating in the deep level beneath the moathouse. He is described as a stunning man who exudes evil. He appeared from behind a northern door in the barracks area when the party, disguised in fire cult robes, presented themselves to his sergeant. He accepted Zephyr's bluff that the party had defected from the water temple after eliminating the water temple operative Snej, and acknowledged their claimed loyalty. However, before sending them with the sergeant to secure the abbey, he pivoted and assigned the party a different task: retrieving a chest — described as a particular piece of art he values — stolen by bandits masquerading as earth elemental cultists. He directed them to search the Kron Hills near Sheernob and promised them the abbey in exchange for its return. His motivations include consolidating territory by eliminating rival factions and recovering valued possessions. He is aware of the factional dynamics between the water and fire temples. He remains in the moathouse dungeon as of the end of the session.

## Erin Darkmoon

Erin Darkmoon is a bandit leader, previously encountered at a watchtower, who provided the party with the name of Lareth the Beautiful and the information that he ran the moathouse operation. She gave this intelligence in exchange for a promise that the party would kill him. She does not appear in this session directly but her prior intelligence was instrumental in the party's infiltration of the deep level.
