## NPC Activity

**Prefect Terjon**
- Examined the marked stone brought by Calmer; dismissed it as a "painted rock" and chastised the party for not preserving the bandit prisoner for questioning.
- Initially attempted to retain the stone when Zinia requested it; released it after Zephyr challenged him.
- Remained at the Temple of St. Cuthbert.

**Burne and Rufus**
- Met with the party at the Inn of the Welcome Wench, summoned via militia escort.
- Examined the stone; initially dismissive, then grew concerned.
- Rufus revealed that the blueprints for the keep had been secretly copied (evidenced by charcoal dust); plans have since been relocated to a more secure location.
- Concluded the blueprint thief is likely a worker in the encampments and connected to the bandits.
- Commissioned the party to identify the saboteur; instructed them to keep the matter quiet.
- Departed with their guards after the meeting.

**Jaroo (Druid)**
- Received the stone and bandit confession; dismissed the evidence as insufficient.
- Directed the party to go deeper into the moathouse for more substantial proof.
- Awarded the party the blessing of Beory and a healing potion.
- Inquired who else the party had spoken to upon their departure.

**Elder Kenter**
- Revealed that his son Hawthorne had surveilled the party's movements through the village, tracking their visits to Jaroo, Terjon, Burne, and Rufus.
- Named the full composition of the village council: Jaroo, Rufus, Burne, Ostler, Captain Zeb Fletcher, and Mytch the Miller.
- Expressed dissatisfaction that council members met the party individually rather than collectively.
- Instructed the party to report to him first going forward.
- Hosted the party with wine and refreshments; his wife Valsenora served.

**Ostler (Innkeeper)**
- Informed the party of the three-night minimum stay policy and the sale of abandoned goods.
- Privately commissioned the party to gather evidence against Furnok for stealing from inn guests.
- Offered free rooms for three nights as payment.
- Confronted Furnok with the loaded dice evidence.

**Furnok the Fabulous**
- Operating as a gambler at the Inn of the Welcome Wench.
- Found to be using loaded dice; denied all accusations when confronted by Ostler.
- Was pressured to leave by Elmo under threat of involving Captain Fletcher.
- Last seen departing the inn under duress.

**Elmo**
- Present at the Inn of the Welcome Wench; intervened to expel Furnok, citing Captain Fletcher's authority.
- Identified as a ranger and adventurer; son of Captain Zeb Fletcher.
- Observed to be heavily intoxicated.
- Expressed willingness to assist the party in tracking a monster in the Gnarley Forest.

**Spugnoir**
- Dismissed the rumor of a secret passage under the inn.
- Claimed knowledge of a lost wizard's laboratory beneath Emiry Meadows; proposed a treasure-splitting arrangement with first claim on arcane scrolls.
- Offered to join the party as a fourth member.
- Was passed over when the party departed for the dragon errand; showed disappointment but accepted the decision.

**Calmer**
- Excused himself from the meeting with Burne and Rufus to return to the temple for prayers.
- Rejoined the party for the moathouse expedition on Planting 18.

**Mickey (son of Virgil Tamen, the Braumeister)**
- Approached the party at the inn to report two cloaked figures lurking behind the brewery around midnight.
- Expressed concern they might be temple spies.
- Departed relieved after the party agreed to investigate.

**Jira and Marek**
- Identified as the "cloaked figures" at the brewery; revealed to be teenagers conducting a secret courtship.
- Jira's father Jakub is a member of the Old Faith; Marek's father Tomlyn is a tailor and member of the Church of St. Cuthbert — the religious divide prevents their relationship.
- Jira appealed to the party to intercede with their parents.
- Last seen departing the brewery.

**Airgid (Silver Dragon)**
- Accompanied the party westward toward the Lotrimil Mountains via the Greenway Valley.
- Recognized her home territory near Tulvar and departed independently, promising her mother would reward the party.

**Old Faith / Church of St. Cuthbert**
- Passive factional tension noted at the local level: the Jira/Marek situation reflects active social friction between Old Faith adherents (Jakub) and St. Cuthbert followers (Tomlyn the tailor).

- **Council composition disclosed**: Elder Kenter named all six council members — Jaroo, Rufus, Burne, Ostler, Captain Zeb Fletcher, and Mytch the Miller.
- **Hawthorne's surveillance role**: Elder Kenter's second son Hawthorne routinely monitors unusual activity in the village and reports to his father.
- **Moathouse trophy room**: Contains preserved humanoid heads (dwarf, human, elf), suggesting the former occupants were enemies of the Temple.
- **Loaded dice**: Furnok confirmed to be cheating inn patrons using weighted dice.
- **Jira/Marek situation**: The "cloaked figures" at the brewery are not temple spies but star-crossed teenagers from religiously divided families (Old Faith vs. St. Cuthbert).
- **Airgid's origin**: The silver dragon's home territory is near the Lotrimil Mountains, west through the Kron Hills via Greenway Valley.
