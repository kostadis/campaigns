## NPC Activity

**Romag**
- Directed the party to explore and clear areas south and east of the Earth Temple, continuing his expansion plans.
- Revealed (implicitly) that he uses prisoners either as food for his gnolls or as sacrifices to the Earth Temple.
- Revealed (implicitly) that he feeds rivals and enemies to the Otyugh as a living disposal system—a rusted Fire Temple breastplate was found in the creature's remains.
- Not present during session events; unaware as of session end that his prisoners have escaped or that his Otyugh has been killed.

**Dren (and Broken Blades mercenaries)**
- Settled into newly claimed territory within the Earth Temple adjacent to the explored areas.
- Present as a potential witness to the prisoner escape; the party stationed a guard to manage this risk.
- No direct interaction with party beyond their presence being a complicating factor.

**Jorin Pike Kale**
- River sailor, found imprisoned by Rentsch's men as punishment for making a pass at a woman named Dalia.
- Freed by the party along with six other prisoners.
- Thanked the party profusely and in gratitude revealed Captain Tolubb's pirate ship schedule at the docks in Nulb.

**Master Aldous Glimmerfield and Mistress Lenora Glimmerfield**
- Textile merchants from Divers, captured by raiders along with their household staff.
- Freed by the party.
- Promised a reward of one hundred silver pieces upon rescue.

**Kalen, Brask, Horgus, and Davine**
- Four farm boys turned mercenaries who surrendered quickly when captured.
- Freed by the party along with the Glimmerfields and other prisoners.

**Captain Tolubb**
- Not present, but referenced. Previously betrayed the party by directing them into a Fire Temple ambush.
- Jorin Pike Kale revealed Tolubb's pirate ship schedule at the docks in Nulb.

**Dick Rentsch**
- Not present, but referenced as the one who ordered Jorin Pike Kale's imprisonment.

**The Otyugh**
- Inhabited a refuse-filled chamber within Romag's territory, sustained by garbage generated by Romag's crew.
- Used by Romag as a disposal mechanism for enemies, evidenced by a rusted Fire Temple breastplate found in the refuse.
- Attacked the party, grappling and poisoning Zinnia and grappling Zephyr; stunned both Zephyr and Sequioa with tentacle slams.
- Killed by the party.

**Harpies (three, unnamed)**
- Appeared in a chambered trap—a gate dropped behind the party upon entry, sealing them in.
- At least one harpy's song has already charmed Sequioa.
- Two additional harpies capable of charming remaining party members remain active.
- Status: combat ongoing at session end, party trapped in sealed chamber.

## Faction Movements

**Earth Temple (Romag's faction)**
- Romag's prisoner stock has been entirely freed by the party; the escape was staged to implicate the Fire Temple using Larith the Beautiful's insignia and robes.
- Romag's Otyugh, a key tool for disposing of enemies and rivals, has been killed without his knowledge.
- Large portions of Romag's claimed dungeon territory were found to be empty and unmaintained, suggesting insufficient resources or poor organizational capacity.
- Romag remains unaware of both losses as of session end.

**Fire Temple**
- Framed for the prisoner escape by the party, who planted Fire Temple insignia and robes (previously taken from Larith the Beautiful) throughout the prison chamber.
- A Fire Temple operative's breastplate was found in the Otyugh's refuse, confirming at least one Fire Temple member was fed to the creature by Romag.

**Romag / Earth Temple Instability**
- The party has killed Romag's Otyugh and freed all of his prisoners while operating under his banner—both actions risk severe consequences when discovered. Score increase warranted for compounding deceptions that, if revealed, could collapse the party's position within the Earth Temple entirely.

## Revealed Information

- **Romag feeds prisoners to gnolls or sacrifices them to the Earth Temple.** Confirmed by the prisoners themselves.
- **Romag uses the Otyugh to dispose of enemies**, including Fire Temple rivals. Evidenced by the Fire Temple breastplate found in the refuse.
- **Romag's territorial control is weaker than presented**—multiple armories and chambers were found empty, unused, and cobwebbed, indicating he lacks the manpower or resources to hold his claimed domain effectively.
- **Captain Tolubb's pirate ship schedule at the docks in Nulb** was revealed by Jorin Pike Kale in gratitude for his rescue.
- **Dick Rentsch imprisons people as personal punishment**, not solely for temple purposes—Jorin Pike Kale was jailed for a social offense rather than any strategic reason.
- **A passage from Romag's level descends to the second dungeon level**, connecting near the chamber housing the eldritch creature previously encountered near the Air Temple representatives' meeting point.
