## Dick Rentsch

Dick Rentsch served as the party's contact at the Waterside Hostel in Nulb, operating out of a back room where he arranged a meeting between the party and Romag, High Priest of the Earth Temple. He led the party out of Nulb along a rutted, disguised road toward the Temple of Elemental Evil, navigating carefully around the glowing runes on the main bronze doors and bringing them instead through a side entrance. Inside the temple, he guided them through the vestibule, the altar chamber, and down into the bone-filled corridor, warning them not to disturb the dead. When gnoll skeletons animated during the passage, he urged the party to run quickly before more could rise.

At the guarded area beyond the corridor, Rentsch identified the party to four Earth Temple guards as the new recruits he had promised to Romag. He brought them through the barracks and lounge to Romag's chamber, presented the party for their meeting, and provided them with a map showing the locations of gnolls within the temple. He demonstrated clear familiarity and comfort within the temple, describing it as his "happy place" and revealing his ideological alignment with the Earth Temple's cause—predicting it would soon be filled with supplicants slaughtering all who opposed them. He expressed mild contempt for the party's caution, sighing at their request for rope and remarking he had hoped for better recruits.

Despite facilitating the meeting with Romag and framing the mission as controlled infiltration, Rentsch deposited the party directly into the Air Temple's territory—the octagonal chamber and pit where a wraith immediately attacked them. Whether this was a deliberate test, a betrayal, or negligent omission of critical information remained unclear at the session's end. His true loyalties and motivations beyond service to the Earth Temple are unconfirmed.

## Romag

Romag is the High Priest of the Earth Temple, encountered in his lavishly decorated private chamber deep beneath the Temple of Elemental Evil. He greeted the party warmly when Dick Rentsch presented them, expressing pleasure that Rentsch had finally brought him something worthy of his time. He received the Chest of Rao from the party directly.

Romag revealed significant information about the temple's structure: the surface temple is merely a facade, with the true power concentrated in descending levels reaching into what he described as the very pit of evil. The first two levels are divided among the Earth, Fire, Water, and Air temples, which are in constant conflict with one another. Below these lie deeper levels containing deeper secrets. He laughed at the party's ignorance of the temple's scale.

His mission for the party was to destroy the other elemental temples—particularly targeting gnolls associated with rival factions—so the Earth Temple could seize ultimate power over the complex. He promised that successful completion of this mission would earn the party the right to wear the badge of Earth. His immediate rivalries include the Fire Temple and its head, Alremm, whom the Earth Temple guards expressed eagerness to kill. Romag's last known location is his chamber in the Earth Temple's controlled section of the dungeon.

## Alremm

Alremm was identified by the Earth Temple guards as the head of the Fire Temple. The guards stated that once they killed enough gnolls, Alremm would be theirs, indicating active hostility between the Fire and Earth temples. Alremm did not appear in person during this session and is known only through this secondhand reference. Current whereabouts are within the Fire Temple's territory in the dungeon complex.

## Spugnoir

Spugnoir was referenced by the party as a source of prior information. He had previously told them the story of the Circle of Eight—powerful wizards including Mordenkainen and Bigby—who had come to the Temple of Elemental Evil and performed mighty magic to seal the main entrance with glowing runes. Spugnoir did not appear in this session and was recalled only when the party observed the sealed bronze doors bearing those runes.
