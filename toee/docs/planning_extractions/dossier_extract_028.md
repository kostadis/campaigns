## Alrrem

Leader of the Fire Temple, referred to as "the all-consuming." Alrrem orchestrated the Chimera attack against the party, sending a Fire Temple warlock to unleash the beast and proclaiming it a champion sent to destroy them. He was not present in person during this attack but was the force behind it. Later, when the party slipped a note under the Fire Temple's door claiming to have information about a betrayal, Alrrem eventually responded through the closed door. The party expressed admiration for his power and revealed that Belsornig of the Water Temple had orchestrated the Chimera incident — using the Fire Temple as a pawn to either eliminate the party or drain Alrrem's resources. Alrrem fell into a rage, screamed Belsornig's name, and immediately ordered his troops to attack the Water Temple. This triggered the three-way temple war the party had engineered. Last known status: leading Fire Temple forces in an active assault on the Water Temple on the second level, while his bugbears simultaneously hold a barricade against the Earth Temple's advance up the stairway.

## Hartsch

Sub-general of the Earth Temple under High Priest Romag. During a private meeting with the party, Hartsch laid out the political landscape of the temple factions: Alrrem's Fire Temple had been waning since a catastrophic defeat a decade prior, the Air Temple was a powerless remnant, and the Water Temple was the true rival. When Zinnia pressed him on his feelings about Romag's passive, erosion-based philosophy of leadership, Hartsch made no effort to conceal his ambitions, stating openly that if a position of leadership were to open itself, he would absolutely consider himself worthy of it. The party recognized this as an invitation to remove Romag on his behalf. During the assassination, Hartsch instantly understood what was happening when Zephyr slammed the chamber doors shut — he placed his weapons on the ground and stepped aside without hesitation, allowing the party to kill Romag unimpeded. Immediately after Romag's death, Hartsch plunged his hands into Romag's body, covered them in blood, seized Romag's sacred vestments, and marched into the main chamber of the Earth Temple to declare himself Supreme Prophet. He announced that the era of passive resignation was over and that the destructive power of earth would be unleashed upon their enemies. The gathered cultists erupted in cheers. Hartsch formally introduced the party to his troops as the Obsidian Edge — his personal vanguard, described as the sharpest stone in the deep earth. He subsequently performed rituals, summoned earth elementals, and delivered speeches whipping his cultists into a war frenzy. When the party informed him that the Fire Temple was distracted by its assault on the Water Temple, Hartsch ordered a full-scale charge. When his army stalled at the Fire Temple barricade on the stairway, he turned to the Obsidian Edge and asked them to do what his army could not. Last known status: leading the Earth Temple's assault force, currently bottlenecked at the barricade on the stairway to the second level.

## Romag

High Priest of the Earth Temple, advocate of a slow, erosive philosophy of power — believing dust and gradual erosion to be the ultimate expression of earth's strength. When the party brought him evidence implicating the Fire Temple in the Chimera attack, he received the news with thunderous fury and ordered the party to strike the weakened Fire Temple. He was unaware that Hartsch harbored ambitions against him or that the party had any intention of removing him. The assassination took place in his private sleeping chamber, a room decorated with earthen motifs, dirt-covered floors, a small altar, and a desk with personal notes. He was caught entirely off guard when Zephyr's Thaumaturgy slammed the chamber doors shut, sealing him in with the party and Hartsch. Zinnia rushed him with a flurry of precise strikes, Sequioa followed with additional blows, and Calmer finished him with a necrotic Toll the Dead resonance that caused him to wither and collapse. His vestments were immediately seized by Hartsch. Current status: dead, killed in his own chambers during the party's coup.

## Belsornig

Prophet of the Water Temple. Belsornig did not appear in person this session but was identified as the orchestrator of the Chimera attack through the wax-sealed ledger found on the dead Fire Temple warlock's remains. His name appeared in the document as an ally of the Water Temple who arranged the Chimera's purchase — a calculated win-win scheme designed to either eliminate the party or bankrupt the Fire Temple in the process. The party used this information as a weapon, revealing Belsornig's role to Alrrem through the Fire Temple's door and triggering an immediate Fire Temple assault on the Water Temple. Last known status: leading or present within the Water Temple, now under active attack from Fire Temple forces on the second level.

## Captain Vesrek

Captain of the Crimson Traitor, a vessel identified by the crimson ship crest on the wax-sealed ledger found on the dead Fire Temple warlock. The ledger was a bill of sale documenting Vesrek's transaction — the sale of the Chimera to the Fire Temple, apparently arranged through Belsornig of the Water Temple. Vesrek did not appear in person this session. Current status and whereabouts unknown; last referenced as the seller in a document linking the Chimera's acquisition to the Water Temple's scheming.

## Kelno

Prophet of the Air Temple. Kelno did not appear in person this session. He was referenced by Hartsch during the political briefing as the leader of a faction dismissed as a powerless remnant. Later, when the party passed through the Air Temple and encountered the wraith haunting its corridors, Calmer noted with grim amusement that Kelno is so ignorant he apparently believes the wraith to be an air elemental — a detail that serves as a fitting symbol of the Air Temple's irrelevance and Kelno's incompetence. Current status and whereabouts unknown; last referenced as nominally in control of a faction too weak to be considered a meaningful threat.

## Dren

Leader of the Crimson Guard faction, operating within the Earth Temple's sphere. Dren was enlisted by the party to arrange a morning meeting with Romag, ensuring the high priest would be present and off-guard for the assassination. Visibly impressed by the party's victory over the Chimera, Dren agreed without hesitation and returned with confirmation that Romag was looking forward to the meeting. Later, as Hartsch's forces mobilized for war, the party encountered Dren preparing his men for battle. After a brief exchange, the party sent him and his Crimson Guard into the fray, with Dren rallying his men with promises of plunder and blood. Last known status: leading the Crimson Guard into the three-way temple war as part of the Earth Temple's assault force.
