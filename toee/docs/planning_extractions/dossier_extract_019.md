## Captain Tolub

Tolub betrayed the party by sending them into a trap at the fire temple rather than connecting them with Romag of the Earth Temple as he had promised. Following the party's escape, he spread word throughout Nulb that he had personally disposed of them, framing them as meddlers who didn't belong. Skole at the Boatman's Tavern revealed that Tolub works for the fire prophet, not the Earth Temple, explaining the nature of his deception. He is not a neutral fixer but an agent of a specific faction within the Temple of Elemental Evil. Last known whereabouts: Nulb, where he believes the party to be dead.

## Albreth

A fire priest at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon who, along with Ingulf, unleashed devastating magical flames on the party during the trap Tolub sent them into. Nearly killed Calmer before the party escaped using magical dust. Last known status: alive at the fire temple.

## Ingulf

A fire priest at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon who participated alongside Albreth in the magical flame assault on the party. Part of the trap engineered by Tolub. Last known status: alive at the fire temple.

## Skole

The bartender at the Boatman's Tavern in Nulb. Was visibly shocked to see the party alive, having heard Tolub's claims that he had disposed of them. Took the party into his back room and confided that he despises both Tolub and Lodriss, the tavern's actual owner, and wishes to be free of both. Provided the party with critical intelligence: that Dick Rentsch at the Waterside Hostel is the proper contact for reaching the Earth Temple, not Tolub. Skole appears sympathetic to the party and is positioned as a potential ally, motivated by his hatred of his superiors.

## Lodriss

The actual owner of the Boatman's Tavern in Nulb, despised by Skole. Not directly present in this session but referenced as one of the people Skole wishes to be free of. No further details revealed.

## Elfar Twell

A man found hanging dead from the Gallows Table in Nulb, executed on the orders of Madame Selentis for the crime of crossing her. His fate served as a public demonstration of Selentis's power and the ruthless nature of authority in Nulb. Current status: dead.

## Madame Selentis

A powerful figure in Nulb who ordered the execution of Elfar Twell for crossing her. Her ability to publicly hang someone at Gallows Table signals significant authority within the town's power structure. Did not appear directly but her influence was evident.

## Magda

Runs Magda's Trove, a merchant stall in Nulb selling strange magical curiosities including cursed ink. Encountered briefly as the party passed through town. No further details revealed.

## Garik

Operates an armory stall in Nulb selling rusted weapons. Encountered briefly as the party passed through town. His shop reflects the desperation and low quality of goods available in Nulb.

## Dick Rentsch

The gruff proprietor of the Waterside Hostel in Nulb, identified by Skole as the correct contact for reaching the Earth Temple. Initially suspicious and guarded when the party arrived, warning them he ran a tight operation that didn't tolerate trouble. His demeanor shifted to excitement when the party indicated they had something of interest for the Earth Temple. Took them to a private back office for conversation. Set the party a test of loyalty and capability: ambush and kill a group of gnolls and their handlers being recruited for the fire temple that night. When the party succeeded, they earned his trust and gained entry to the Earth Temple's network. The bandit chief present in his tavern during their meeting suggests the Waterside Hostel functions as neutral ground among factions.

## Romag

Named as the prophet of the Earth Temple, one of four cult leaders within the Temple of Elemental Evil structure. Not directly present but identified as the faction the party is seeking to contact. His subordinate Dick Rentsch operates in Nulb as a recruiter and gatekeeper.

## Belsornig

Named as the prophet of the Water Temple, one of four cult leaders within the Temple of Elemental Evil. Referenced only in the party's intelligence-gathering about the temple's structure. Not present.

## Wat

The bartender at the Waterside Hostel. Approached the party while they waited for Dick Rentsch and attempted to extract information about their business. The party deflected him. He seemed relieved by their non-answers and mentioned someone named Dala before warning the party not to drink the beer and serving them gin instead. His warning about the beer and mention of Dala suggest he may know more than he lets on or is cautiously trying to help.

## Dala

Referenced by Wat the bartender at the Waterside Hostel when he warned the party and served them gin. No further context provided. Identity and significance unknown.
