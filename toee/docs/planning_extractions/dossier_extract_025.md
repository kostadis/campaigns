## Romag

Romag appeared at the session's outset, pleased with the party's prior successes within the Earth Temple. He suggested they continue expansion by exploring and clearing areas to the south and east, framing it as a means to solidify his power base. His motivations are clearly self-serving: he uses prisoners either as food for his gnolls or as sacrifices to the Earth Temple, and the discovery of a Fire Temple breastplate in the Otyugh's refuse pile revealed that he also feeds rivals to the creature as a living disposal system. His pet Otyugh, now dead at the party's hands, served this purpose. Romag is unaware that the party freed his prisoners and staged the escape to look like Fire Temple involvement. He has also left large sections of the dungeon empty and unused, suggesting poor management or insufficient resources. Last known location: within the Earth Temple, unaware of the party's actions during this session.

## Dren

Dren and his Broken Blades mercenaries were noted as having settled into their newly claimed territory within the Earth Temple that morning. He was a potential complication during the prisoner rescue, as his crew occupied adjacent territory and might have overheard the commotion. The party stationed Calmer at the door as a precaution. Dren took no direct action this session and did not interact with the party. Last known location: his claimed territory within the Earth Temple, adjacent to the prison chamber.

## Jorin Pike Kale

Jorin Pike Kale was one of seven prisoners found chained in a filthy prison chamber behind a locked and padlocked door. He identified himself as a river sailor who had been imprisoned by Dick Rentsch's men as punishment for making a pass at a woman named Dalia. Upon being freed by the party, he thanked them profusely and, in gratitude, volunteered valuable intelligence about Captain Tolubb's pirate ship schedule at the docks in Nulb—information the party noted as potentially useful given Tolubb's prior betrayal in directing them into a Fire Temple ambush. Last known status: freed and guided to an escape route via a stairway discovered by Zephyr.

## Dick Rentsch

Dick Rentsch was mentioned by Jorin Pike Kale as the man whose agents imprisoned Jorin as punishment for making a pass at a woman named Dalia. Rentsch did not appear in person this session. No location or further details provided.

## Dalia

Dalia was referenced by Jorin Pike Kale as the woman whose attention he had sought, which led to his imprisonment by Dick Rentsch's men. She did not appear in person this session and no further details were provided.

## Master Aldous Glimmerfield

Aldous Glimmerfield was among the seven prisoners freed from the Earth Temple prison chamber. He and his wife Lenora were identified as textile merchants from Divers who had been captured by raiders. He was chained to the wall along with the other male prisoners. Upon rescue, the Glimmerfields promised the party a reward of one hundred silver pieces. Last known status: freed and guided to escape via the stairway.

## Mistress Lenora Glimmerfield

Lenora Glimmerfield was among the seven prisoners freed from the Earth Temple prison chamber, identified alongside her husband Aldous as a textile merchant from Divers captured by raiders. She was one of two female prisoners and was not described as chained. Together with her husband, she promised the party a reward of one hundred silver pieces upon their rescue. Last known status: freed and guided to escape via the stairway.

## Captain Tolubb

Captain Tolubb was not present this session but was referenced by Jorin Pike Kale, who provided intelligence about Tolubb's pirate ship schedule at the docks in Nulb. The party noted this information as significant given that Tolubb had previously betrayed them by directing them into a Fire Temple ambush. Last known location: operating out of Nulb's docks on a schedule now known to the party.

## Larith the Beautiful

Larith the Beautiful was not present this session but was referenced in connection with Fire Temple insignia and robes the party had previously collected from her. These materials were used to frame the Fire Temple for the prisoners' escape, with the party scattering them throughout the prison chamber. No new information about Larith was revealed.
