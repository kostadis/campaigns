## Thalsor

Thalsor was a druid associated with the Black Earth cultists (earth elemental faction) whose compound the party raided on Planting 23, 579. During the initial assault on Planting 23, he proved devastatingly effective in combat, knocking out Sequoia and Zinnia, then knocking out Calmer when Calmer attempted to heal them, and finally killing Calmer outright just as Zephyr moved to retrieve the body. He held Calmer's corpse hostage overnight, screaming threats of mutilation throughout the night to pressure the party into surrendering and revealing who sent them, explicitly stating he would mutilate the body to prevent resurrection if they did not comply. He also demanded to know who had sent them, suggesting awareness that they were operating on behalf of someone else. Zinnia identified him as a threat requiring elimination specifically because allowing him to live would expose the party's identities to the cult and connect them back to the village elders.

On Planting 24, Thalsor stood in his yard continuing his threats when the party executed their counterattack. Sequoia used a potion of gaseous form to slip past him undetected and retrieve Calmer's body. The subsequent surprise attack from Zinnia and Zephyr struck him down quickly. He commanded a golem and a mud mephit as assets; without Calmer's spells the party could not destroy the golem and focused fire on Thalsor instead. He was killed in this second engagement. His compound contained notes from Romag to Thalsor and an unsent note from Thalsor confirming that the lost chest was located in a monastery in the mountains. Zephyr and Zinnia concluded after his death that Thalsor and his Black Earth cultists were rival cultists to Lareth's faction, and that Lareth had manipulated the party into eliminating his competition.

## Pasmarie Clee

Pasmarie Clee was located in Sheernobb and had previously hired the party to recover stolen honey. The party returned her honey after defeating the Black Earth cultists and received their reward. She is related to Robert Deakin, the tavern keeper in Ostverk, who mentioned possessing honey mead from the Honey Tavern Inn connected to the Clee family. No further details about her motivations or current status are provided beyond the completion of this transaction.

## Terjon

Terjon is a cleric at the Temple of St. Cuthbert in Hommlet. To fund Calmer's resurrection following his death at Thalsor's hands, Zinnia offered Terjon a magical trident as a tithe. Zinnia noted that this arrangement chafed, particularly given Terjon's prior dismissiveness toward the party. Jaroo separately warned the party that Terjon and followers of St. Cuthbert might withhold healing magic from non-followers, suggesting Terjon uses religious affiliation as a condition of his services. Terjon accepted the trident and presumably performed or facilitated Calmer's revival. His current location is the Temple of St. Cuthbert in Hommlet.

## Jaroo

Jaroo met with the party in Hommlet following their return from the Black Earth cultist compound. He reiterated the importance of securing the lost chest and warned that followers of St. Cuthbert such as Terjon might refuse healing magic to those outside their faith. No further details about his motivations or whereabouts beyond Hommlet are provided.

## Burne

Burne met with the party in Hommlet following their return. He confirmed interest in the lost chest alongside Rufus, though Zinnia assessed their priorities as more political than purely good. No further details are provided about his current status or location beyond Hommlet.

## Rufus

Rufus met with the party in Hommlet alongside Burne. He confirmed interest in the lost chest, with Zinnia noting their motivations appeared politically driven. No further details are provided about his current status or location beyond Hommlet.

## Robert Deakin

Robert Deakin is a gnome who owns and operates "The Cheap Forester" tavern in Ostverk. He refused to speak with the party until they purchased food. He shared several pieces of intelligence: he confirmed that many groups were searching for the lost chest; he argued that rumors of a powerful evil force were lies spread to obscure the reality that they were merely bandits, claiming authority on this point because he fought in the Battle of Emiry Meadows and believed the Temple was truly destroyed; he theorized that the chest's true purpose was to secure a treaty with the Kingdom of Celene, and that the Viscounty of Verbobonc intended to use this alliance to wage war on the gnomes and seize their gem mines, expressing satisfaction that the alliance had not formed; he noted that the elves at Caer Ostverk and Lord Denelu were willing to pay well for information on the chest's location. He is related to the Clee family of Sheernobb and carried honey mead from the Honey Tavern Inn. His last known location is his tavern in Ostverk.

## Rithelm Drake

Rithelm Drake is a human soldier encountered at "The Cheap Forester" tavern in Ostverk, described as a massive man of average height with a square face, rough pink skin, and green eyes. He initially demanded a drink from the party but softened when he realized they were adventurers, ultimately buying them a drink himself. He expressed regret that he had not become an adventurer, having instead settled into family life with a wife and three children. When the party asked about a monastery, he was initially confused but recalled an abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner, providing the party with a direct confirmation of their primary lead. His last known location is Ostverk.

## Gonnathrad Kevanarial

Gonnathrad Kevanarial is a High Elf Diplomat encountered at "The Cheap Forester" tavern in Ostverk, described as a skinny man with long wavy black hair, grey eyes, and a fancy red tunic. He became excessively excited at the prospect of acting as a quest giver, offering to write a five-page backstory for the party and expressing strong desire to participate in what he perceived as an adventure narrative. His official purpose in Ostverk was to find adventurers to hunt bandits. The party manipulated his enthusiasm by hinting at their knowledge of the monastery and chest without revealing specifics, which prompted him to issue them a formal letter of permit officially hiring them to pursue the chest near the monastery at Gallow's Corner, with a promised reward of 100 gold pieces upon presentation of the letter and the relevant information to Caer Ostverk. He appeared genuinely confused about why the party was focused on an abandoned monastery but allowed his romanticized excitement to override his professional judgment. His last known location is Ostverk.

## Lord Denelu

Lord Denelu was mentioned by Robert Deakin as one of the parties willing to pay well for information regarding the location of the lost chest, alongside the elves at Caer Ostverk. He does not appear directly in the session. No further details about his identity, location, or motivations are provided beyond this passing reference.
