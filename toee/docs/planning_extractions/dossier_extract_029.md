## Calmer

Calmer appeared in two significant encounters during this session.

In the scalding corridor battle against Fire Temple bugbears, Calmer contributed a decisive moment when he channeled divine power in the form of guiding radiance, blowing the barricade doors open with finality. This broke the tactical stalemate and allowed Earth Temple forces to pour through the gap, fracturing the Fire Temple defensive line exactly as the party had intended.

In the Air Temple corridor, Calmer confronted a wraith and attempted Channel Divinity twice in succession. Both attempts failed — the wraith made its saving throws on each — leaving the divine light dissipating harmlessly in the cold air. The wraith then announced that one of the party would die, and struck Calmer across the back as he sprinted away, dealing sixteen cold damage. Additional fire bolt damage of eight hit him during the retreat. As the party cleared the threshold, the wraith laughed hysterically at Calmer's failed attempts. Calmer's internal response was characterized by a sense of deep injustice — he reflected that he had already survived being killed and raised once in this temple and found the wraith's resistance profoundly unfair.

Later, when the party encountered four ghouls in their territory — raised from recently-dead Earth Temple members — Calmer's Turn Undead succeeded against all three remaining ghouls after Zephyr killed the first. The release of divine power was dramatically disinhibited: Calmer shouted affirmations repeatedly and broke into cursing mid-sentence, releasing what appeared to be accumulated frustration from the two earlier failures. He was observed by Zephyr with a note of concern — the intensity of the reaction prompted Zephyr to silently question whether the response was entirely Calmer's own, given the months spent inside a temple where evil is practiced earnestly and Calmer has been repeatedly subjected to its ceremonies and pressures. Calmer's current status is alive and traveling with the party toward the Deep Temple level.

## Zephyr

Zephyr appeared throughout the session in multiple engagements and strategic moments.

In the scalding corridor, Zephyr put an arrow into the steam from the edge of the corridor before the main engagement, maintaining distance and acting with efficiency Zinnia credited as sensible. He received some damage from the bugbear counterattack, though less than Zinnia.

During the wraith encounter, Zephyr fired his pistol at the wraith and landed a hit for five damage after the creature's resistance halved it — the only wound inflicted on the wraith before the party fled. He broke for the secret passage ahead of the others.

In the ghoul ambush, Zephyr killed the lead ghoul before it could act with a single dagger strike dealing twenty-seven damage. He proposed pursuing whoever had raised the ghouls, noting that the creatures had been recently-dead Earth Temple members, indicating active necromantic activity in the dungeon during the engineered war.

In the Hall of Trophies, Zephyr proposed finding a single elemental to question about the Deep Temple's location, only for the plan to collapse when no one in the party spoke Primordial. He adjusted without much ceremony and supported the decision to exit via the steam cover.

In the prisoner chamber, Zephyr led the interrogation of the Air Temple cultist prisoner. His first persuasion attempt was flat and landed poorly. He then tried a harder approach, using his physical presence and implied threat — described as bravado and borrowed menace — to press for information. Calmer's presence at his shoulder shifted the dynamic, and a second persuasion attempt succeeded. The prisoner revealed the location of the secret passage to the Deep Temple, which he had learned directly from Kelno. Zephyr filed this information carefully. When an Earth Temple runner arrived with a summons from Hartsch, Zephyr received it without showing impatience and led the party upstairs, internally registering the constraint of the alliance as a form of leash even while maintaining the appearance of unhurried choice. He was privately focused on the tension between being summoned by Hartsch and finally possessing the information about the Deep Temple route.

## Sequoia

Sequoia appeared in several encounters during the session.

In the scalding corridor, Sequoia received some damage from the bugbear counterattack and landed the finishing blow on the second bugbear after the barricade fell, described as having a satisfying weight to it.

During the ghoul ambush, Sequoia attempted to set a trap or obstruction at the door as the ghouls approached but rolled a 1, causing the mechanism to trigger too early and failing to slow the creatures. After Calmer's Turn Undead routed the remaining three ghouls, Sequoia put an arrow into the back of the last fleeing ghoul before it escaped the corridor, finishing the engagement.

On the second level, Sequoia read the environmental evidence carefully — broken bottles, shoved armor, blood trails without bodies — and assessed that something structural had broken open in the temple war rather than a localized skirmish. He observed Kelno's goblins and ghouls moving through a hidden door, hauling looted coin and weapons with purposeful efficiency rather than fleeing. He registered that Kelno had been underestimated, having quietly positioned himself to profit from the war while the other factions fought. He internally noted this as a failure of attention on the party's part.

In the prisoner chamber, Sequoia picked the lead prisoner's shackles after Zinnia assessed the man as truthful. He rolled twenty-seven on the check and opened the lock cleanly. He was aware of the remaining four prisoners still chained and prepared to continue the work of freeing them. He observed the interrogation of the freed prisoner and noted the man's decision-making process — the pause before he agreed to share the Deep Temple route — as genuine rather than performed.

## Kelno

Kelno did not appear directly but was identified as the organizing intelligence behind activity observed on the second level. Sequoia recognized his badge on goblins moving through a hidden door, hauling bags of looted coin while ghouls dragged looted weapons alongside them. The operation was orderly and purposeful — not a retreat from the temple war but a deliberate exploitation of it. Kelno had apparently assessed that letting the other three temples destroy each other was more advantageous than direct participation, and had positioned his people to collect resources from the chaos.

The Air Temple prisoner freed by the party confirmed that his knowledge of the secret passage to the Deep Temple had come directly from Kelno, indicating the prisoner had been close enough to Kelno to receive sensitive navigational information. This places the prisoner as a former associate or subordinate of Kelno's rather than a generic cultist.

Kelno's current whereabouts are unknown. He is not confirmed to be present on the second level personally. His faction appears to be operating with coordination and restraint while the other temples fight, which Sequoia assessed as a significant and previously underestimated threat.

## Hartsch

Hartsch did not appear directly but sent a runner to summon the Obsidian Edge to an urgent war council upstairs. The summons was characterized as having the texture of immediacy. Zephyr received it and complied, leading the party away from the Deep Temple entrance they had just located. Zephyr privately registered the summons as an expression of the constraint embedded in the alliance — the sense of belonging to someone else's chain of command. Hartsch's current location is upstairs in the temple where the war council is convening.
