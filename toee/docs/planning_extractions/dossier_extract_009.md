## Lareth

Lareth, the leader of the moathouse, appeared briefly at the start of the session. He told his sergeant that the party did not need his help, describing them as "professionals." He then met Zephyr's gaze directly and warned that he would not tell anyone about their deal, suggesting he is aware of the arrangement's sensitivity and is calculating how to use the party to his advantage. His plan, as relayed by Sequoia to Rufus and Burne, is to use the party as agents to eliminate rival cultists — specifically, he wants them to recover a stolen chest that he claims belongs to a rival group. He believes the party works for him. Last known location is the moathouse.

## Rufus

Rufus appeared at his tower in Hommlet, dressed in a white blouse, green velvet pantaloons, and a red sash, seated at the main table after a meal. He questioned the party about evidence of the cult and raised the possibility that the cultists were not genuine but rather agents of chaos or rival governments seeking to destabilize Verbobonc by making it appear the Temple of Elemental Evil had returned. When Sequoia mentioned the stolen chest Lareth wanted recovered, Rufus immediately connected it to the Viscounty's diplomatic art initiative — a program where crafted art is left near the border of the Kingdom of Celene as a goodwill gesture. He revealed that a chest selected from the most recent annual competition went missing after the transport mission was attacked, and that the official position is that the elves accepted it. He expressed that recovering the chest and identifying who stole it would be of great assistance, though its reappearance would embarrass the Viscount. He appears to be covering for intelligence failures regarding the cult's return.

## Burne

Burne appeared alongside Rufus at the tower in Hommlet, wearing a leisure suit and seated at the table after a meal. He suggested that the cultists could be agents of chaos or rival governments rather than genuine Temple of Elemental Evil adherents, framing the situation as a political destabilization effort against the Viscounty. When Sequoia explained that Lareth believed the party worked for him and intended to use them to destroy rivals, Burne called the plan brilliant and credited the masked one with devising it. He pointed a fork at Zinnia and noted that finding the chest and those who stole it would be of great assistance, while acknowledging the official position that the elves accepted the chest must be maintained. He appears politically motivated and concerned with managing the Viscount's reputation.

## Gurdy

Gurdy is a well-traveled gnome bard who approached the party's table at the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb. He performed a song in exchange for coin paid by Zephyr, then sat uninvited at the table, had Mari refill his drink at Zephyr's expense, and provided information about the bandit situation — noting the bandits have become more organized than before and that locals want protection but no one wants to actually confront them. He suggested the party visit the moot hall for more information and offered his services as a bard to inspire them against the bandits, which Calmer vehemently rejected. Zephyr expressed interest in working with him. Last seen at the Honey Haven Inn.

## Mari Clee

Mari Clee is the gnome innkeeper and bartender of the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb. She was humming and shaking her hips behind the bar when the party arrived, pulling a hedgehog out of a mug when she turned to greet them. She quoted room and meal prices and, upon seeing Zephyr treat his companions poorly by ordering himself chicken and a private room while insisting the others take the cheapest options, devised a mischievous plan to have everything billed to Zephyr as the supposed leader, letting Calmer order the expensive moonbeam pie and Zinnia order fine Honey Mead without challenge. She also drew Zephyr's coin for Gurdy's refill. She runs the inn with evident care and a strong sense of hospitality. Her husband or co-owner also offered the party free lodging for a week plus a small keg of enchanted mead in exchange for finding who stole barrels of his mead, with tracks leading into the forest.

## Quinna Pool

Quinna Pool is a half-orc cook at the Honey Haven Inn, wearing an apron and smelling of kitchen fire. She approached the party after learning they were adventurers and hired them to hunt a rare gnarled boar that roams the gnarly forest, describing it as notoriously elusive and dangerous. She needs the meat to prepare a celebratory meal for the Harvest Festival. In exchange, she offered to cook them a special meal that would bless them and make them stronger. She reacted with squealing delight when Zephyr agreed to the quest. Last seen at the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb.

## Captain Gnonsbim

Captain Gnonsbim is the captain of the guard in Sheernobb. He came to the Honey Haven Inn after hearing the party were adventurers and hired them to deal with the organized bandits affecting trade, explaining he lacks the personnel to send a patrol without exposing the village. He offered 25 gold for the camp location, 10 gold per dead bandit, and 25 gold for the leader, with an additional reward of custom-forged magical weaponry if the party complies with one additional condition: when they return with evidence, they must let him give a speech and publicly agree that the success was due to his leadership, as an election is approaching. He also paid for a round of mead for the party and directed them to speak with Pasmarie Clee, who has been pressing him about the bandit problem. Last seen departing the Honey Haven Inn after making the arrangement.

## Headgnome Glennddarc

Headgnome Glennddarc was mentioned by a gnome guard at the entrance to Sheernobb as the person in charge of the settlement, available to speak with at the Sheernobb Moothall, described as the largest mound in the village. Glennddarc did not appear directly in the session.

## Knarfallan

Knarfallan is a gnomish adventurer previously assisted by the party. He gave Zephyr a ring, which a gnome guard at Sheernobb's entrance recognized on Zephyr's pinky finger. Recognition of the ring immediately changed the guard's demeanor from cautious to friendly, granting the party smoother entry into the village. Knarfallan did not appear directly in the session.
