- Prisoner held in the ogre's pantry room; originally from Norwell

**Florian**
- Prisoner held alongside Derrick; originally from Norwell

**Jarran (Gnome Spy)**

**Burne**

**Ostler Gundigoot**

## Faction Movements

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Fire Temple**
- Maintains an active armed presence in the moathouse dungeon, including a barracks with guards and a sergeant under Lareth's command
- Has been using the moathouse as a staging and supply point: stockpiles discovered include 50 shields, 12 suits of leather armor, barrels of unspoiled salted meat, and black fire temple robes — sufficient to equip a significant force
- Has been maintaining and actively using the moathouse's lower level infrastructure (oiled hinges, new locks, recently repaired fountain, fresh bedrolls)
- Uses the torture chamber to process prisoners, leaving remains for zombies
- Lost Lubash the ogre (killed by party)
- Lost the tower near Emiry Meadows and its personnel (killed by party)
- Lareth is seeking recovery of a stolen chest from poseur earth-cult bandits near Sheernob in the Kron Hills

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Water Temple**
- Previously had an operative named Snej operating in the region (referenced as now eliminated)
- Had recruited or attempted to recruit agents; the party used knowledge of Snej's death to bluff Lareth
- Appears to be in active rivalry with the Fire Temple

**Lolth Cult (Unnamed)**
- Small cell operating out of the tower near Emiry Meadows
- Maintained a laboratory, alchemy setup, forge, summoning circle, and living quarters in the tower
- All known members killed by the party
- Cell included human worshippers of Lolth, notable given Lolth's reported absence/imprisonment
- Resources lost: potions, magical dagger, scroll, spellbook, and all personnel

**Brigands (Bandit Network – Temple-Affiliated or Aligned)**
- Actively capturing travelers on roads near Hommlet and supplying them as food to Lubash
- Confirmed victims include Derrick, Florian, Martin, Damien, Eltograph the Elf, and others
- Have an operative (the Duke) working within Hommlet or its immediate vicinity
- Connected to the bandit cell at the river north of the workers' encampment (now eliminated except for the escaped captain)
- Used Flintshine to obtain plans, likely of the moathouse

**Merchants of Lotrimil**
- Dispatched Jarran as a spy to investigate brigand activity in the region
- Jarran was captured before completing his mission; now freed and returning to Hommlet
- No further operational activity noted this session

**Village of Hommlet – Elder Council**
- Met formally to hear the party's report
- Consensus: brigand threat is real and local; no evidence of temple re-emergence sufficient to escalate to arch-cleric of Verbobonc
- Burne and Rufus characterized as cautious and dismissive; Ostler aligned with Jaroo's faction
- Internal political tension noted: Calmer attempted to raise the question of Prefect involvement and was shut down

**Circle of Eight**
- No direct activity; historical role revealed by Spugnoir
- Previously intervened at the Battle of Emiry Meadows to bind an evil presence within the Temple of Elemental Evil
- Characterized as neutral, intervening only when cosmic balance is threatened

## Threat Arc Events

**Temple of Elemental Evil Re-emergence**
- Discovery of active weapons and supply stockpiles (50 shields, 12 suits of leather armor, unspoiled meat, fire temple robes) in the moathouse lower level — evidence of ongoing logistical preparation, not mere historical remnant
- Discovery of a secret shaft leading to deeper dungeon levels, with new iron rungs, confirming recent construction and active use
- Party makes direct contact with Lareth the Beautiful, confirming a named commander is actively operating out of the moathouse dungeon
- Fire temple guards, a sergeant, and barracks confirmed in the deep level
- Black fire temple robes discovered in quantity, confirming ideological organization, not just opportunistic banditry

**Lolth Cult Activity**
- Discovery and elimination of a functioning Lolth cult cell operating near Emiry Meadows
- Human worshippers of Lolth confirmed active in the region despite Lolth's reported absence or imprisonment
- Summoning circle with permanent markings found in the tower, suggesting organized ritual activity

**Undead Presence / Necromantic Activity**
- Thirteen or more zombies found imprisoned in moathouse dungeon cells, apparently created from torture victims left in the dungeon
- Multiple ghouls discovered in the crypt below the dungeon, including at least four in the main hall with more driven off by Calmer's turning
- Crypt accessed via a secret shaft originating in the torture chamber, suggesting intentional necromantic use of the space

## Revealed Information

- **Lareth the Beautiful** is the named commander of fire temple forces in the moathouse dungeon; he is aware of and hostile to the water temple faction
- **Snej** was a water temple operative whose death the party is using as cover; Lareth accepted this as proof of loyalty
- **The Duke** is working with unidentified "shady people" and paid Flintshine to obtain plans, likely of the moathouse; his employer is described only as "someone in the village"
- The bandit network has a cell operating from the river north of the workers' encampment; this cell was willing to kill its own informant (Flintshine) to prevent capture
- **Eltograph the Elf** was one of the prisoners fed to Lubash; the cloak of elvenkind found in Lubash's nest is likely Eltograph's
- The **Circle of Eight** (Mordenkainen, Bigby, Otiluke, Leomund, and others) previously intervened at the Temple of Elemental Evil after the Battle of Emiry Meadows, casting binding spells on something within; their involvement implies whatever was bound was of multiverse-scale threat
- The tower near Emiry Meadows contained a functional summoning circle with a magic circle formula inscribed permanently — its original purpose and any completed summonings are unknown
- **Lareth** is seeking a stolen chest taken by bandits posing as earth elemental cultists; these bandits are believed to be in the Kron Hills near Sheernob
- The **abbey** referenced by Lareth is a location of strategic interest to the fire temple; Lareth offered it to the party as a reward, implying it is either already under partial fire temple control or is a target for seizure
- Hommlet's elder council is internally divided: Jaroo and Ostler represent one faction skeptical of Burne and Rufus, whom Ostler called "interloping outsiders"
