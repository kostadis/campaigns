## Characters

### Calmer
**Class/Level:** Cleric (War Domain) 6 | **Species:** Human | **Player:** Kostadis/Kostadis Roussos/kostadis1

Calmer is a deeply devout cleric of St. Cuthbert operating under profound cover stress—publicly posing as a converted follower of the elder elemental god while privately maintaining his true faith. He is growing harder and more decisive in violence (executed a gnoll prisoner unilaterally, delivered killing blows on Rannos, Gremag, Romag, and the Chimera) while simultaneously showing visible moral distress at the rituals he is expected to endorse. His teammates are quietly monitoring whether his Earth Temple cover identity is beginning to affect his true loyalties.

**Arc Score Tracks:**

| Track | Current Value | Key Triggers (Up) | Key Triggers (Down) |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Faith Under Pressure | High | Resisting deception traps; turning undead effectively; protecting party | Near-invoking St. Cuthbert aloud in the temple; visible moral collapse during rituals |
| Tactical Courage | High | Delivering killing blows under pressure; pre-casting before dangerous encounters; healing mid-combat | Being knocked unconscious; failed Channel Divinity attempts |
| Cover Integrity | Moderate | Convincing Earth Temple guards; being publicly recognized by Romag | Visible nausea at rituals; confronting Zephyr in front of NPCs |

**Notable Relationships:**
- *Zephyr:* Strained. Calmer has directly confronted Zephyr about being dismissed and about the risk of sacrifice; Zephyr watches him for identity drift.
- *Zinnia:* Positive. Zinnia saved him from drowning early on; Calmer expressed gratitude after being revived by Sequoia. Zinnia provides stoic reassurance.
- *Sequoia:* Positive. Sequoia revived him with a healing potion; Calmer expressed explicit gratitude.
- *Prefect Terjon:* Obligation—extracted a promise to report exclusively to him; raised Calmer from the dead in exchange for a magical trident.
- *Romag (deceased):* Publicly declared himself a converted cleric of St. Cuthbert to this man; now has killed him.
- *Hartsch:* Nominal superior within the Earth Temple cover.

## Party Dynamics

**Cohesion:** The party functions as a highly effective operational unit. They coordinate well in combat and deception, and have successfully run multi-layered infiltrations across numerous factions.

**Primary Tension — Zephyr vs. Calmer:** Zephyr's dismissiveness toward Calmer has produced a direct confrontation ("I have opinions that matter"). Calmer defied Zephyr's orders during the Sheernobb session and later confronted him privately. Zephyr is now monitoring Calmer for signs of identity drift, which adds a layer of surveillance to an already strained dynamic. This is the most active interpersonal fault line in the party.

**Secondary Tension — Sequoia's Unilateralism:** Sequoia has accepted multiple quests and commitments without consulting the group, and secretly redistributed treasure against Zephyr's share. If either is discovered, it could fracture the party's financial trust or create obligation conflicts.

**Shared Goals:** All four characters are invested in dismantling the Temple of Elemental Evil from within and maintaining their covers long enough to reach the Deep Temple. The Chest of Rao's fate (given to Romag against Brother Eldin's explicit advice, now in Hartsch's possession) is a shared unresolved moral weight—particularly for Calmer.

**Calmer as a Watch Point:** Multiple session extracts flag that party members are observing whether Calmer's cover identity is affecting his real loyalties. His increasingly violent choices (unilateral executions, killing blows on human enemies) and his near-invocation of St. Cuthbert inside the temple are noted. The GM should treat his psychological state as actively developing rather than stable.

**The Wraith Threat:** The wraith's declaration ("one of you is going to die today") is unresolved and sits in the path to the party's primary objective. It has failed twice against Calmer's Channel Divinity. This is a known, named threat that the party cannot avoid indefinitely.
