Smigmal’s Background
Smigmal hails from the war-torn Bandit Kingdoms,
where survival meant mastering cunning and violence.
Her lethal skills caught the attention of Temple agents,
leading to her swift rise through the ranks by
eliminating threats and proving herself to Supreme
Commander Hedrack and Senshock.
Mercenary Roots: Raised among cutthroats and
raiders, she learned to fight, deceive, and survive.
Temple Recruitment: Started as a scout, later placed
in command of the outer defenses.
Elven Disguise: Uses a magical hat to pose as an
elven diplomat, gathering intelligence in Nulb and
Hommlet.
Role in the Temple
As leader of the Temple’s surface defenses, Smigmal
commands raiding parties and ensures no enemy
infiltrates the ruined tower stronghold.
Lead Defensive Operations: Orchestrates raids and
ambushes against Temple enemies.
Espionage and Sabotage: Works with Falrinth to spy
on enemies and rivals..
Mercenary Control: Manages Temple-hired forces,
ensuring loyalty through fear.
“Obedience comes not from loyalty, but from fear. And fear
is a currency I deal in generously.” - Smigmal
Relationships
Falrinth (Lover and Wizard Ally)
Smigmal’s closest ally and lover, Falrinth, uses her
position to gather intelligence and eliminate rivals. His
quasit familiar provides her with a steady flow of spying
reports from Nulb, the Temple, and beyond.
However, Falrinth’s ambitions outweigh his loyalty.
He sees Smigmal as a pawn in his quest for power,
hoping to seize control after Hedrack’s fall. Smigmal,
aware of his grandiose dreams, knows he will abandon
the Temple if it collapses—and she plans accordingly.
Hedrack (Supreme Commander)
Hedrack tolerates Smigmal due to her effectiveness, but
distrusts her closeness to Falrinth, whose loyalty to
Lolth makes him a wildcard. He keeps a watchful eye on
her, using her to maintain order but never fully trusting
her.
Deggum (Commander of Security)
As her direct superior, Deggum relies on Smigmal for
defense reports and coordination. Though she respects
his tactical mind, she sees him as a roadblock to her
ambitions and remains wary of his authority.
“Barkinar may wear the armor, but it’s his boots that tell
the truth—always pointed toward the exit. He’s waiting,
watching, but when the time comes, he’ll run.”
Smigmal, like many in the temple, pays lip service to the
Elder Elemental God but does not hold true faith. She
sees the cult’s chaos as a useful distraction for her own
agendas but has no desire to awaken the ancient power.
“Hedrack plays the king, Senshock schemes for the
throne, and Barkinar waits like a vulture. I simply watch
and sharpen my knives.” - Smigmal

Lucius Graeme, Temple Agent
Courier (a.k.a. Aldren)
Lucius Graeme, a Oeridian/Suel operative, commands
attention—not through stature, but through an aura of
quiet menace. His portly frame, draped in simple gray
garments, conceals a calculating and dangerous mind. A
small gray hat often shadows his scarred face, marked
by an old injury from falling debris. His golden,
asymmetrical eyes seem to uncover secrets no one
wishes to reveal, and his smooth, unsettling voice has a
way of disarming or unnerving listeners.
Personality
Once a vain and cruel nobleman, Lucius’s fall from
grace left him with a bitter hatred for order and
morality. Now, he is a manipulative schemer, wielding
intelligence and deception like a blade. Beneath his
calm exterior simmers a barely controlled rage, which
he unleashes without mercy when provoked.
Master Manipulator: Feigns weakness, allowing
enemies to underestimate him.
Chaos as Power: Believes disorder breeds
opportunity, shaping the world in his image.
Devotion to Elemental Forces: Sees the primordial
powers of the elemental planes as neither good nor
evil—only tools for the strong.
“Chaos is the truest form of control—those who embrace it will
shape the world to their will.” - Aldren
The Art of Deception
Lucius delights in playing the fool, allowing others to
underestimate him while he quietly gathers intelligence
and consolidates power. He thrives in chaos, seeing it as
the ultimate tool of control. To him, the elemental
planes are not moral forces, but raw engines of
destruction and renewal, waiting to be harnessed by
those strong enough to command them.
“Civilization is a fragile mask over the face of the
elemental chaos. It’s a shame when it breaks… or a
delight.“ - Lucius Graeme

Meeting Aldren (Lucius)
Aldren, draped in a well-worn cloak and clutching a
weathered merchant’s satchel, approaches the adventurers
with a practiced, disarming smile. His smooth, slightly
unnerving voice carries a friendly yet probing tone as he
speaks: “Ah, travelers! A fine day to find company on these
lonely roads, wouldn’t you agree? Allow me to introduce myself
—just a humble merchant peddling wares and tales alike. The
name’s Aldren. Been wandering these hills for years, trading
with the gnomes and passing adventurers like yourselves. Always
an adventure out here, isn’t it?”
He adjusts his satchel, letting the faint glimmer of trinkets
and oddities peek out, an inviting distraction as his golden
eyes linger on each party member, assessing them carefully.
“Tell me, friends, what brings you to the Kron Hills? It’s not
often I see a group like yours—armed and armored, yet with the
air of purpose about you. Are you hunting treasure, seeking
glory, or perhaps delivering some noble cause? The hills have no
shortage of mysteries, I assure you.“
Aldren leans forward conspiratorially, his tone dropping
slightly, as if sharing a secret.
“These parts have their dangers, mind you. Not just the usual
bandits or wild beasts. Strange things—whispers of old powers
stirring, unnatural storms, and… well, I hear tales of the
Greenway Valley being less safe for travelers these days. Makes a
man curious about what might draw someone like you to these
parts. And perhaps… how I might be of assistance?”
His voice softens further, laced with subtle charm and
intrigue.
“After all, we’re all travelers here. A bit of information for a
bit of coin—or a friendly exchange of stories. Who knows?
Maybe our paths cross for a reason, eh?”
Aldren smiles warmly, his golden eyes glinting faintly with
a mix of feigned innocence and calculated intent.

power by inheriting the foundation laid by his
predecessor, Barkinar. A pragmatic leader and skilled
manipulator, he focuses on defense and fortification
rather than expansion, though his position is constantly
challenged by internal dissent and external rivalries.
Prophet of Earth
Romag is an imposing figure of Oeridian/Flan descent,
hardened by years of surviving the Temple’s brutal
hierarchy. His earthen-toned robes, adorned with
gemstones infused with elemental power, reflect his
devotion to the Black Earth.
Stout, weathered physique, muscular from years of
physical endurance.
Brown and gray robes, symbolizing the resilience of

Wields a stone-etched warhammer, imbued with

Defensive Focus: Strengthens temple defenses

History of Romag
Origins and Early Life
Romag’s rise to power was not one of glory or divine
providence, but of betrayal, endurance, and revenge.
Born into a minor Oeridian noble house in Verbobonc,
he spent his youth struggling under his father’s failing
landholdings. Dissatisfied with his fate, Romag
abandoned his family’s burdens and sought power in
the priesthood, drawn to the forbidden teachings of the
Elder Elemental God, which promised strength through
elemental might.
His journey led him to a band of like-minded outcasts:
Dick Rentsch, a cunning manipulator.
Dala, Dick’s ruthless lover.
Senshock, an ambitious wizard.
Together, they delved into lost ruins, searching for
wealth and power. Though Romag served as their
spiritual guide, his influence was overshadowed by
Senshock’s magic and Dick’s scheming.
“You cannot bargain with stone. You cannot plead with earth.
You will break before I do.” -Romag
Betrayal in the Dwarven Stronghold
The group’s most infamous expedition led them into a
forgotten dwarven stronghold deep within the Lortmil
Mountains. Hoping to unearth wealth and arcane
secrets, they ventured too far and encountered the
fortress’s ancient guardians—dwarven constructs and
stone-clad sentinels. As the battle turned against them,
Senshock made a ruthless decision—he sacrificed
Romag, abandoning him to the enemy while ensuring
his own escape alongside Dick and Dala.
Captured by the dwarves, Romag endured years of
imprisonment—harsh interrogations, isolation, and the
gnawing torment of betrayal. Yet within the stone walls
of his prison, he deepened his connection to the power
of earth, drawing strength from the very stone around
him, biding his time for an opportunity to break free.
“I was buried once, left to rot beneath stone and soil. Now, I am
the mountain that will crush them all.” -Romag
Meanwhile, Senshock returned to the Temple of
Elemental Evil, leveraging their expedition’s treasures
to secure a high-ranking position within the Greater
Temple, while Dick and Dala settled in Nulb, using their
ill-gotten wealth to establish the Waterside Hostel, a
hub for illicit dealings and information gathering.
“Senshock thinks himself untouchable, but even the tallest
towers crumble when their foundations crack.”
Escape and the Path to Revenge
After years of brutal captivity, Romag finally saw his
chance for freedom during a skirmish within the
dwarven stronghold. Using his deepened connection to
earth magic and his understanding of subterranean
passages, he orchestrated a daring escape, emerging
into the wilderness as a changed man—hardened,
vengeful, and more determined than ever.
His sole purpose became revenge—not only against
those who betrayed him but against all who doubted his
strength. Romag made his way back to civilization,
following whispers of his former companions’
whereabouts until he arrived at Nulb, where he found
Dick Rentsch and Dala running the Waterside Hostel.
A Reckoning in Nulb
Fueled by rage and betrayal, Romag confronted Dick
and Dala, nearly killing them both in a violent outburst.
However, the cunning duo turned his fury toward
Senshock, painting him as the true traitor who
orchestrated Romag’s downfall. They pledged their
loyalty to Romag, convincing him that Senshock’s
power was the true prize and that revenge would be
best served from within the Temple itself.
Seeing opportunity in their offer, Romag spared them,
forging an uneasy alliance. With Dick and Dala’s
knowledge of the Temple’s politics and Nulb’s
underworld, Romag carefully reintegrated himself into
the cult, using his old ties to Senshock to feign
forgiveness and renewed loyalty.
Climbing the Ranks
Senshock, sensing an opportunity to control Romag,
arranged for him to become High Priest of the Earth
Temple after Barkinar’s promotion to the Greater
Temple. However, despite his new title, Romag found
himself leading the weakest of the four elemental
factions.
Fire Temple Leader Alrrem viewed him as a cowardly
fool, unworthy of his station. Water Temple High Priest
Belsornig sought to manipulate him, using his faction as
a pawn in his own power plays. Aware of his precarious
position, Romag leveraged Dick and Dala’s information
network in Nulb to spy on his rivals. Slowly, he fed
critical intelligence to Senshock and Barkinar, gaining
favor within the Greater Temple while secretly hoarding
knowledge for his own purposes.
Romag also expanded the Earth Temple’s influence,
quietly recruiting miners, mercenaries, and enforcers
under the pretense of temple fortifications. While others
saw the Earth Temple as stagnant, Romag was laying
the groundwork for his own power play.
The Long Game – A Path to Revenge
Yet, Romag’s true goal remained unfulfilled—the
downfall of Senshock. Knowing that open confrontation
was suicidal, he patiently played the long game, feigning
loyalty and submission, waiting for the perfect moment
to strike.

Political Relationships
Romag sees himself as the stabilizing force within the
Temple of Elemental Evil, believing Elemental Earth
represents the temple’s true foundation of power. He
views the other elemental factions as reckless and
undisciplined, favoring patience and fortification to
slowly undermine them. Though outwardly loyal to
Supreme Commander Hedrack and the Greater
Temple, Romag harbors ambitions of expanding his
influence, though his cautious nature prevents him from
making bold moves like his rivals.
“Nulb is a swamp of fools and thieves, but even mud can serve a
purpose if you shape it well.” - Romag
Influence in Nulb and Rivalry with Madame Selentis
Romag exercises subtle yet significant influence in Nulb
through the manipulative dealings of Dick Rentsch,
proprietor of the Waterside Hostel. Dick Rentsch,
alongside his associate Dala, provides Romag a
constant influx of recruits, spies, and intelligence on
Nulb’s shifting underworld. Yet, Romag’s grip over Nulb
faces continuous challenge from Madame Selentis
(Lady Nysera), whose position as Hedrack’s logistics
overseer in Nulb threatens Romag’s activities.
Romag resents Madame Selentis’s growing influence,
viewing her efforts to centralize power and command
Nulb’s illicit networks as dangerously counterproductive
to his own careful plans. Though their rivalry remains
largely subtle and cloaked in deniability, Romag
constantly seeks ways to subvert her authority without
provoking direct retaliation from Hedrack or Lucius.
Key Political Alliances
Dick Rentsch and Dala: Essential informants and
recruiters, though Romag trusts neither. Dick and
Dala’s constant pursuit of self-interest and coin
makes them unreliable; Romag constantly seeks
leverage over them.
Senshock: A dangerous rival whom Romag pretends
to serve while plotting his downfall.
Barkinar: Once a mentor, now an obstacle to
Romag’s ambitions within the Temple.
Madame Selentis (Nysera Krivaltis) (Major Rival):
Romag’s primary adversary within Nulb; their
ongoing power struggle shapes local Temple politics.

Cold, calculating, and brutally pragmatic, Gefreid

errors from his handpicked bandits.
“This cottage is just a hunting lodge, nothing more. You’d best
