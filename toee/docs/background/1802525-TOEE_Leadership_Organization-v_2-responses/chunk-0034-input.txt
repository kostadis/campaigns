Ash and Saltwater
Nulb is a cauldron where the Temple of Elemental Evil
stirs its schemes, and few know this better than those
who run its taverns. Wat, the sullen bruiser behind the
bar at the Waterside Hostel, answers to Alrrem of the
Fire Temple, while Skole, former river smuggler and
fixer at the Boatman’s Tavern, is loyal to Canon
Belsornig of the Water Temple.
But Nulb isn’t big enough for both of them—and
Lodriss, the tavern’s mistress, isn’t interested in letting
her lover Captain Tolub’s temple allies burn down the
village in a pissing contest. So when the adventurers
stumble into the Boatman’s Tavern on a windless night,
they find more than ale and rumors—they find the flame
and tide colliding.
The adventurers stumble into this tension by
accident… or design.
Objectives
Overhear the Argument between Wat and Skole.
Choose a Faction to support, or pit them against
each other.
Inform Lodriss, or manipulate her to influence Tolub
and the pirates.
Report to Selentis, Romag, or Belsornig—each of
whom may act on the fallout.
Setting: The Boatman’s Tavern
The adventurers arrive at Boatman’s Tavern on a night
thick with mist. The river’s breath coils under the
warped door, and most patrons keep to themselves—
except two men hissing barbed threats across a
shadowed corner table.
A successful DC 14 Stealth or DC 16 Perception
check allows the party to overhear the exchange.
Failure means the argument ends abruptly. If spotted,
Skole offers silence. Wat just narrows his eyes and
memorizes faces.
Approach Options:
1. Espionage: Learn the Factional Rift
If the party eavesdrops, they learn:
Skole is smuggling enchanted items and Temple
coin to Tolub’s pirates at the behest of Belsornig.
Wat believes Captain Tolub is edging the Fire
Temple out of Nulb’s logistics network.
Both are terrified of Madame Selentis, who monitors
all smuggling across the bridge from her manor.
2. Manipulate Lodriss
Lodriss may not wear a Temple emblem, but her silence
is power. If the adventurers inform her of the Water vs.
Fire feud:
She may side with Skole, using Tolub to pressure
Selentis or even Alrrem directly.
Alternatively, she may have Skole removed and
replace him with a pirate loyal only to her.
The Argument
As the adventurers approach the Boatman’s Tavern,
they see Wat leaning over Skole’s table, his fingers
gripping the wooden surface with quiet menace. Skole,
clearly agitated, wipes sweat from his brow and shakes
his head vehemently.
Dialogue Snippet (Overheard)
Wat (gritted teeth): “Skole, you’re going to do what I damn
well tell you. The Fire Temple needs eyes here, and I need
someone I can trust. You’d be wise to listen before I lose my
patience.”
Skole (frustrated whisper): “I told you, Wat! Lodriss will gut
me if she finds out! You don’t cross her unless you’ve got a death
wish. And Tolub? He’ll keelhaul me before I can even pack a
bag!”
Wat (snarling): “Lodriss won’t know, you fool. We just need a
few whispers—when the big players are in town, when
shipments are coming through. No one’s asking you to sell your
soul. Just your ears.”
Skole (angrily shaking his head): “And what happens when
she finds out? She will. And then my corpse is feeding the fish in
the Imeryd’s Run.”
At this point, if the adventurers are sneaky (a DC 14
Stealth check) they can listen in further without being
detected. If they fail, Wat and Skole will notice them,
and the conversation will abruptly stop. Skole will feign
ignorance, while Wat will eye the party suspiciously,
wary of their intentions.
Report to Selentis
If Madame Selentis is informed of Wat and Skole’s
disobedience:
She may have them replaced, placing her own loyal
thugs at both taverns.
Or she may play one against the other, ensuring they
funnel Temple loot more directly through her
logistics network.
Reward: Reputation increase with the Greater
Temple or potential assignment for sabotage.
4. Feed False Information to Skole
Trick Skole into thinking Wat plans to report him to
Belsornig. This could:
Force Skole to act rashly and lose Lodriss’
protection.
Cause Belsornig’s pirates to lash out preemptively
against Wat or the Fire Temple.
5. Report to Romag or Alrrem
Alrrem may order Wat to retaliate, potentially triggering
street-level violence in Nulb. Romag, seeing an
opportunity, might use the party to destabilize both
Water and Fire factions.
