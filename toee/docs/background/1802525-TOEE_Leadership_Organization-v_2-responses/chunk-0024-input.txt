Falrinth’s Shadow - Waterside
Falrinth, the rogue wizard known for his dark
allegiances and deeper secrets, occasionally emerges
from his subterranean lair beneath the Temple of
Elemental Evil to conduct clandestine meetings in the
shadows of Nulb. His visits unsettle the delicate balance
of power, drawing attention from both friend and foe
alike. His latest journey takes him to the Waterside
Hostel, owned by Dick Rentsch, an informant for
Romag’s Earth Temple. This time, Falrinth is not
merely gathering information—he seeks something
more dangerous: an alliance to secure his grip on the
Orb of Golden Death and subtly undermine Supreme
Commander Hedrack.
The Setup
The adventurers find themselves in Nulb at a crucial
moment. Rumors float through the grimy streets that
Falrinth himself has been seen slipping through the
shadows toward the Waterside Hostel. Dick Rentsch,
the hostel’s proprietor, is known to keep close ties with
Romag and the Earth Temple—an unlikely ally for
Falrinth, who owes fealty only to Lolth and himself. But
alliances in Nulb shift faster than quicksand, rivals can
find common ground when darker interests align.
Falrinth’s Purpose:
Negotiating Alliances: Falrinth aims to quietly form a
temporary alliance with Romag through Dick
Rentsch, using their mutual distrust of Hedrack and
Senshock to find common ground.
Gathering Intelligence: He seeks information about
Madame Selentis and her growing influence in Nulb
—especially her ties with Lucius Graeme and
Hedrack’s broader plansHedrack.
Protecting the Orb: Falrinth is wary of Hedrack’s
growing suspicion about his possession of the Orb of
Golden Death. He needs allies and information to
safeguard his power.
Companions
Falrinth is shadowed by two hardened brigands from
Smigmal Redhand’s personal enforcers. They’re under
strict orders to guard him and report his every move
back to Smigmal, whose loyalty remains a volatile
mixture of love and mistrust.
Encounter at the Waterside
The hostel’s dim interior stinks of river mud and stale
ale. Falrinth sits cloaked in shadows at a secluded table,
his quasit familiar Kriitch invisible and lurking nearby.
Dick Rentsch moves between tables, eyes flicking
nervously toward the wizard as he quietly briefs his
agents on Romag’s behalf.
Falrinth’s Behavior:
Watches newcomers warily, seeking potential
threats or opportunities.
Listens attentively for any whispers concerning
Lucius Graeme, Madame Selentis, or Supreme
Commander Hedrack.
Engages Dick in brief, intense conversations that
always conclude abruptly.
Player Interaction
Insight (DC 17): Adventurers notice Falrinth’s wary
demeanor, his fingers subtly weaving arcane
patterns beneath the table.
Arcana (DC 15): Characters sense magical
protections woven around Falrinth, as well as the
unseen presence of his quasit familiar.
Perception (DC 18): Detect the nervous behavior of
Dick Rentsch and his men, revealing their
discomfort in dealing with Falrinth openly.
Falrinth’s Tactics
Non-engagement: Prefers to avoid direct conflict
unless cornered or openly provoked. He will gather
information and retreat quickly back to his lair
beneath the Temple.
Combat and Escape: If confronted, Falrinth deploys
illusions, confusion, and misdirection. Kriitch, his
quasit, attacks from stealth to distract and confuse
while his brigands engage physically, allowing
Falrinth an opportunity to slip away.
Key Outcomes:
Intel on Hedrack and Orb: Information overheard or
forcibly extracted can reveal Falrinth’s possession of
the Orb of Golden Death and his deeper connections
to Lolth and Smigmal Redhand.
Insight on Madame Selentis: Information gathered
exposes Madame Selentis’s critical role as
Hedrack’s hidden strategist in Nulb, potentially
weakening Hedrack’s position if leveraged correctly.
Alliance Disruption: Falrinth’s intentions to align
temporarily with Romag through Dick Rentsch can
disrupt the delicate power balances of the temples.
Intelligence Gained:
Confirmation of Falrinth’s dangerous dual loyalties—his
true fealty to Lolth, and his willingness to undermine
Hedrack’s authority within the Temple. Awareness of
the growing tension between temple factions,
particularly Romag’s Earth Temple and Hedrack.
