Greater Temple Supply Staging
Expanded Greater Temple Supply Operations Gefreid
Patris plays a crucial role in the logistical network of the
Temple of Elemental Evil, acting as a central hub for
supplies, prisoners, and stolen goods moving between
the Moathouse, Nulb, and the Temple itself. His
management of the Hunting Cottage positions him as
an indispensable facilitator of the Temple’s covert
operations.
Coordination with Gattas Void at the Moathouse
Gefreid maintains regular and discreet contact with
Lieutenant Gattas Void, second-in-command to Lareth
at the Moathouse High Road Bandit Camp. Gattas
coordinates frequent raids against merchant caravans,
isolated farms, and travelers throughout the Viscounty
of Verbobonc. Captured livestock, valuable loot, and
abducted prisoners are transferred by heavily guarded
wagons to Gefreid’s custody at the Hunting Cottage.
Upon receiving shipments, Gefreid personally
inspects the captured goods and evaluates prisoners.
Valuable captives suitable for ritual sacrifice or forced
labor are transferred directly into the tunnel network
toward the Greater Temple. Supplies and lesser
prisoners are redirected to Madame Selentis’s
operatives in Nulb for secondary distribution.
Gefreid’s Bandits (Greater Temple)
Gefreid commands a disciplined squad of hardened
Temple-aligned brigands provided by Commander
Deggum. These human bandits maintain the secrecy
and efficiency of operations, enforcing Feldrin’s orders.
Bandit Personnel Roster
Morvik Blackthorn (Lieutenant) – Second-incommand; cautious, organized, and fiercely loyal to
Gefreid. Responsible for day-to-day discipline.
Jarus Vrell – Silent tracker and scout; known for
detecting threats long before they reach the cottage
perimeter.
Dieter “Redknife” – Former pirate turned enforcer;
infamous for his intimidation skills and handling
prisoner transfers.
Gavrik “Ironfoot” – Manages barn operations; tough,
burly, and methodical. Oversees packing, repacking,
and prisoner containment.
Elric the Younger – Youngest bandit; ambitious and
watchful. Reports on any irregularities among his
peers to Gefreid directly.
Ralf “The Hound” – Bandit tasked with resetting
perimeter snares each morning; brutal and quick to
violence if triggered.
Quillen – In charge of courier horses and supplywagon handling; meticulous with shipment logs and
inventory checks.
Brannoc Grimtooth – Prisoner overseer; cruel and
unfeeling, enjoys authority over captives awaiting
transport.
Vulnerability of the Tunnel
Though well-hidden, the supply tunnel is a single point
of failure in the Greater Temple’s operations.
Adventurers who discover and sabotage the tunnel
could cut off the Temple’s lifeline. (T1-4 TOEE module
page 38, 4. ESCAPE TUNNEL)
Logistical Operations
Under Gefreid’s careful oversight, the Hunting Cottage
serves multiple critical roles:
Command Post: Coordination of logistical
operations, prisoner sorting, and intelligence
exchange.
Supply Depot: Receiving shipments of stolen goods,
prisoners, and supplies from Madame Selentis’s
bandits, Vesrek’s pirates, and Gattas Void’s raiders.
Prisoner Handling: Slaves and prisoners destined
for sacrifice or labor in the Temple are temporarily
held and sorted in reinforced barn cages.
Political Relationships
Commander Feldrin: Gefreid answers directly to
Feldrin, the Temple supply commander.
Lucius Graeme: Regularly inspects operations and
delivers orders. Gefreid respects Lucius’s position
but finds him insufferably arrogant.
Madame Selentis (Nysera Krivaltis): Exchanges
shipments through intermediaries. Distrustful of her
ambitions but acknowledges her ruthless efficiency.
Smigmal Redhand and The Crimson Reapers:
Occasionally delivers shipments directly,
maintaining tension and wary cooperation.
Gattas Void (Lareth’s Lieutenant): Supplies regular
deliveries of prisoners and loot from the Moathouse.
Their relationship is strictly professional and
transactional.
