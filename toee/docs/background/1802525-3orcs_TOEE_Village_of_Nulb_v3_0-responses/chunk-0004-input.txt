Madame Selentis: Quest Web
1. Power Struggle in Nulb (Against
Selentis)
Hook: One of Selentis’s rivals—Skole (Boatman’s floor
boss), Dick Rentsch (Waterside), or Lodriss (market
mistress)—quietly hires the party to prove Selentis is the
Temple’s civilian handler.
What’s really going on: Selentis runs the Old Mayor’s
Manor as a covert hub for sorting prisoners,
contraband, and orders flowing from the river and
wagons to the Hunting Cottage and its tunnel into the
Temple. Evidence exists—if you dare the manor cellars.
Lift a ledger or sealed order from Selentis’s
command room.
Shadow a night transfer from the Boatman’s docks
to the manor, then cottage (map times, faces, crates).
2. Mission of Blood (Pro-Selentis)
Brief: Selentis wants Captain Tolub (and thus
Belsornig) checked without open war. She orders a
“lame the wolf, don’t kill it” operation. Wreck a pirate
rendezvous at the Boatman’s (plant false “Earth
Temple” sigils to frame Romag).
Knife-work option: remove Grud Squinteye, Tolub’s
brutal mate, to fracture crew discipline. Why it
matters. Tolub is Belsornig’s river fist; Selentis is his
land counterweight, and the tavern is their neutral
chessboard.
Twist: Belsornig’s go-betweens offer the party a
better purse to expose Selentis’s informants.
“You look like you’ve got more sense than the usual sellswords,
there’s power shifting in this filth-ridden town… and some of us
ain’t keen on being beneath it.” Madame Selentis
3. The Rise of the “Baroness” (ProSelentis… or Against)
Aim: Secure leverage over slaver-captain Vesrek.
Run: Board the Crimson Trader, copy manifests
tying him to Temple shipments and Nulb cellars (the
manor stores Vesrek paperwork).
Fork: Turn the evidence over to Selentis to polish
her “public” legitimacy—or to Verbobonc agents to
put a bounty on her head next.
4) The Constable’s Last Badge
Hook: Constable Edrick Malvaar drinks to forget he’s a
puppet. Selentis keeps him pliant with coin, fear, and
staged “wins.” PCs can break him… or use him.
Redemption: extract blackmail ledgers from the
manor; free him to act as your lawful screen.
Leash: fabricate proof he’s skimming Temple coin.
Fallout: If exposed clumsily, Lucius replaces Edrick.
5. The Silent Road (Pro-/Anti-Selentis)
Mission: Intercept a midnight convoy: Boatman’s →
Manor → Hunting Cottage → Tunnel.
Decide whether to seize, poison, or ghost-escort the
load to learn the schedule deeper in.
Inside the cottage. Gefreid’s logs, bugbear timings,
rune-stones, and the worked cave to the Temple—
this is the artery.
6. Break the Chain (Anti-Temple)
Job: Sabotage the Hunting Cottage tunnel node to stall
the uprising without revealing how you knew.
Details: Goblin whistles, bugbear fire-teams, carrion
crawler contingencies, even a planned cave-in
protocol—Gefreid runs it like a ledger.
Success: Weeks of supply shock; Selentis scrambles
to patch the route, giving PCs time to move on the
manor.
7. Parley in the Grand Hall (Social)
Scene: Selentis invites the party to “negotiate” under
crossbows in the ruined ballroom—her velvet glove over
iron. Choices.
Trade names and routes for immunity.
Offer to take a contract against her rivals (Tolub,
Rentsch, Skole) earn temporary safe-harbor in Nulb.
Hidden Clock. If talks stall, a “house fire” starts—
stirges loose, dire hounds unchained, and a polite
bow from the balcony.
Rewards & Levers (use as needed)
Documents & Ledgers. Shipment schedules, slave
manifests, pirate cuts—real power at your table is
paper and signatures.
Pardons/Passes. Temporary writs from the
constable or stamped “market scrip” that buys them
five minutes in the Boatman’s back rooms.
Allies on a String. A frightened clerk, a bandit
sergeant tired of bugbear bullying, a barmaid
informant.
Failure States (Escalation Ladder)
Quiet Knife. A messenger vanishes; prices go up;
doors shut, lost corporation.
Smigmal’s Shadow. Assassin dog the party’s trail.
Feldrin’s Teeth. Bugbear “inspectors” appear in
town to “audit” the problem.
Invitation Withdrawn. The Grand Hall parley
becomes a trap.
