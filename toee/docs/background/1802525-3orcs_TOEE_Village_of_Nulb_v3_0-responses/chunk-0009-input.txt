1. Waterside Hostel of Nulb
seedy stronghold of secrets on the bank of
Imeryds Run. The Waterside Hostel is a
cracked jewel in Nulb’s gutter crown—a
tangle of tavern, inn, and haven for outlaws
that clings to the riverbank like a leech.
Stone foundation. Timber bones. Smokestained rafters. Its rooms creak, the ale is
sour, if something bites you in the night, pray it’s a rat.
Owner: Dick Rentsch (covert operative of the Earth
Temple under Romag)
Function: Inn, tavern, smuggling waystation
Atmosphere: Perpetual tinderbox—kegs of menace
“Rooms are cheap. Lives cheaper.” —Local saying
scratched into the outhouse door.
Physical Features
Two stories: field-stone foundation, patched timber
walls; lean-to sheds and a rickety gallery facing the
river. The roofline sags under too many winters.
Tavern Room: Low ceiling, smoke-thick air, lantern
light the color of old butter. Sound stacks under the
beams; whispers carry farther than you think.
Semi-Private Rooms (B–D): Cramped bunk rooms
with warped doors and rusted latches. Mildew,
sweat, and old blood in the boards. “Privacy” is
something you rent by the hour, lose by the minute.
Hostile Hospitality
Dick Rentsch: The gruff publican mask hides a
patient recruiter and sorter for the Earth Temple. He
weighs every guest for utility: coin, leverage, or
sacrifice. He smiles with his mouth, listens with his
eyes, and remembers everything.
Wat (barkeep): Quiet, sharp, and loyal—to Alrrem’s
Fire Temple. The bar is a cold war line: Earth vs.
Fire under the same roof. Each man tolerates the
other, Hedrack demands no schisms in Nulb.
Serving Staff
Dala & Pearl (cutpurse wenches; level-3 thieves):
pour ale, lift purses, start rumors.
Two manservants (level-2 fighters): sleep in the loft
above the gallery with chain, shields, light
crossbows, and orders to crack heads fast.
Hostel Structure (for DMs)
The Waterside was a decent inn but The Temple’s rise
rotted it into a predator den. Dick and Wat run a
laundering and staging house.
At night, the place transforms into a boiling pot of
violence and plotting. Brawls are common, secrets are
whispered over spilled ale, and death is always just one
drink away.

Political Ties, Infamy, and Survival
Madame Selentis (Nysera Krivaltis): The spider over
Nulb’s web. Her Temple guards shadow convoys, lift
captives, and collect what’s owed. The Waterside is
useful, so she lets Dick run it—so long as it stays
useful.
Romag (Earth Temple): This is his surface foothold:
screening recruits, moving coin, and holding “special
guests” for pickup. Dick answers to Romag when it’s
profitable, and to Selentis when it’s wise.
Alrrem (Fire Temple): Present only as whispers and
Wat’s eyes. Fire uses the bar to watch Earth, not to
claim the ground.
Belsornig (Water Temple): Keeps his hands off the
Waterside; his crews drink and deal across the
bridge at the Boatman’s Tavern.
Neutral Ground (with teeth): Every faction uses the
Waterside. None dare torch their own table. Fights
end fast unless someone important gives orders.
Infested & Infamous: The rooms itch. The beds bite.
And if you snore too loud, you might not wake up.
Dagger Behind the Ale: It’s well-known that staying
at the Waterside Hostel is a gamble—but in Nulb,
sometimes that’s the best you can hope for.
Nightfall Rule: When the sun drops, the Waterside
boils—fights, deals, and whispered heresies. Blood is
bad for business, but “accidents” happen. The only
law here is sold to the highest bidder—and Selentis
buys in bulk.
