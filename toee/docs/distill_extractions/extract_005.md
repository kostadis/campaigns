## World Events

- Zephyr assembled the party at an inn on Planting 16, 579, meeting Calmer and Thorne for the first time.
- The party departed the inn and traveled east on the road toward Nulb before turning north onto the overgrown track leading to the Moathouse.
- The party arrived at the Moathouse after a slow journey through boggy, overgrown terrain.
- Thorne set up camp just past the drawbridge and declined to enter the Moathouse.
- Four giant frogs emerged from the pool near the bridge and attacked; all were killed by the party.
- Calmer declared he would lead and marched onto the drawbridge.
- Four more giant frogs jumped onto the drawbridge, and the combined weight caused the rotting bridge to shatter.
- Zinia and Zephyr remained on the bridge; Sequoia and Calmer fell into the moat and were injured by falling debris; two frogs were also injured.
- A frog attacked Calmer and was severely wounded; Zinia jumped into the water and killed one injured frog; Zephyr killed another frog; the last frog was killed by an arrow through the eye.
- Zinia pulled Calmer from the water and treated his wound.
- The party entered the Moathouse courtyard and great hall; Zephyr identified signs of recent human and pack animal traffic.
- Sequoia tracked foot traffic to a barricaded door in the northeast of the great hall.
- Zephyr used an illusion to cause noise outside the door, prompting the bandits inside to open it.
- The party executed a coordinated stealth assault: Zephyr entered first and killed the bandit leader; Zinia entered second and killed another bandit; Sequoia entered and killed one more.
- The surviving bandits panicked; three escaped south through an enlarged arrow slit.
- Sequoia killed one more bandit and blocked the exit; Zinia knocked out the last bandit (Voss) with a flurry of blows.
- Calmer arrived in the chamber after combat concluded.
- Sequoia woke Voss and interrogated him; Voss confirmed Temple affiliation and described the cult's token system.
- Sequoia discovered a flat river rock inscribed with an eye and a tear among the bandit leader's possessions.
- Three bandits successfully escaped the Moathouse to the south.

## Threads & Mysteries

- Three bandits escaped south through an enlarged arrow slit in the Moathouse; their destination and whether they will report to Temple superiors is unknown.
- The Temple cult appears to be active again despite being considered "dead" by Voss and other locals; the identity and location of the "big boss" and intermediate leaders are unknown.
- The flat river rock inscribed with an eye and a tear is an unexplained cult token; its full significance, origin, and who issues them has not been established.
- Voss described a three-tier hierarchy (big boss → boss → bandit gang) but knows nothing beyond his immediate superior; the upper levels of this structure are unidentified.
- The purpose of the pack animal traffic noted by Zephyr inside the Moathouse has not been explained; something is being transported to or from the site.
- The interior of the Moathouse has not been fully explored; passageways lead east and south from the great hall, and the door to the bandit chamber was only one of several leads.
- Ranos Davl's identity, faction, and motives for providing Thorne to Zephyr have not been explained.
- Calmer's competence and readiness for adventuring is in question following his near-death on the first day.
