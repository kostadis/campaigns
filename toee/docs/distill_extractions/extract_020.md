## World Events

- The party interrogated a bound gnoll prisoner; Zephyr slapped it during questioning but received only threats in return.
- Calmer executed the gnoll after the interrogation failed.
- The party searched the gnoll bodies and recovered a scroll with ancient markings, three ornate gold buttons, and identified the dead as servants of the Fire Temple via their symbols.
- The party returned to the Waterside Hostel in Nulb and met with Dick Rentsch in the back room.
- Dick Rentsch confirmed he had arranged a meeting with Romag of the Earth Temple.
- Calmer barricaded the door with a heavy chest and stationed Zephyr at the window while they waited.
- Dick Rentsch led the party from Nulb along a rutted, weed-choked road toward the Temple of Elemental Evil.
- A party member noticed that recent heavy multi-creature traffic on the road had been deliberately disguised.
- The party arrived at the Temple of Elemental Evil and observed its exterior, including sealed main bronze doors bearing glowing silvery runes placed by the Circle of Eight.
- Dick Rentsch led the party through a side entrance into the temple's vestibule.
- Dick Rentsch led the party to an inner altar chamber containing a pinkish-white marble altar with a human-shaped depression stained with old blood, and a twenty-foot circular pit surrounded by broken crystal.
- Dick Rentsch described the temple as his "happy place" and explained the Earth Temple's plan to destroy rival factions and fill the temple with supplicants.
- The party descended into the pit via a narrow spiraling ledge and steep steps, using rope at Calmer's insistence.
- At the bottom, Dick Rentsch knocked a pattern on a wall, opening a secret door into a corridor approximately one hundred feet long and twenty feet wide, entirely covered in human and humanoid bones from an ancient battle.
- Several gnoll skeletons animated and attacked the party in the bone corridor.
- Sequioa destroyed the first skeleton; Sequioa and Zephyr were wounded by skeleton attacks; Zinnia struck down another skeleton; Calmer turned undead, destroying one skeleton outright and damaging others; the party finished off the remaining skeletons.
- Dick Rentsch urged the party to move quickly before more skeletons could animate; the party ran through the rest of the corridor.
- The party was questioned by four Earth Temple guards and passed through as new recruits brought by Dick Rentsch.
- The party passed through a barracks area and a lounge decorated with scenes of drinking and debauchery.
- The party was brought before Romag in his chamber; the Chest of Rao was presented to him.
- Romag explained the temple's multi-level structure: the first two levels divided among four warring elemental temples, with deeper levels below containing greater secrets and power.
- Romag tasked the party with destroying the other three temples so the Earth Temple could seize ultimate power; he promised them the badge of Earth upon success.
- Dick Rentsch provided the party with a map showing gnoll locations within the temple.
- The party descended again and ran through the bone corridor without further incident.
- The party arrived in a large octagonal room fifteen feet below the normal dungeon level, identified as the Air Temple's power source area.
- The party dropped into the central pit of the octagonal room.
- A wraith materialized and attacked the party; Zephyr struck it with a dagger; the wraith drained Zephyr's life essence, reducing her maximum vitality.
- Calmer used divine power, causing the wraith to recoil in fear and take holy damage; the frightened wraith retreated.
- Calmer expressed suspicion that Dick Rentsch had withheld information about what awaited them in the temple.

## Locations

**Nulb**
- A settlement containing the Waterside Hostel; the party's base of operations prior to entering the temple
- Current state: Not described as disturbed; party departed from here

**Waterside Hostel**
- Located in Nulb; contains a back room used for private meetings
- Recent events: Party met Dick Rentsch here; Calmer barricaded the door with a chest; Zephyr watched the window; Dick Rentsch later led the party out from here

**Road from Nulb to the Temple of Elemental Evil**
- A rutted, weed-choked road with rank vegetation (thistles, burrs, thorns, nettles); apparently little-used but showing recent signs of heavy multi-creature traffic that had been deliberately disguised

**Temple of Elemental Evil (exterior)**
- A massive structure larger than the party anticipated; northeast corner features a ruined tower jutting from shattered walls
- Described as leprous gray, with obscene arched buttresses covered in climbing vegetation; every surface carved with leering faces and writhing forms depicting obscenities
- Surrounded by dead trees, unnaturally colored and twisted vegetation, white skulls and bones, and strange fungus
- A pervading sense of fear hangs over the area; sounds are distorted; movements flicker at the edges of vision; wind moans through hundreds of apertures like wailing souls; ravens present among the ruins
- Main entrance: two pairs of massive bronze doors, 23 feet high and 20 feet wide, sealed with huge iron chains with all cracks sealed; graven with glowing silvery runes placed by the Circle of Eight; the party avoided these
- Current state: Largely exterior ruins intact; main doors sealed by the Circle of Eight

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Side Entrance / Vestibule**
- Accessed via a side door; floor covered in reddish-brown slate-like tiles; plastered walls painted with scenes of killing, torture, enslavement, robbery, and unspeakable acts; dim light through stained glass windows casting revolting colors; pinkish mineral pillars with warm-colored veins supporting arches decorated with vile reliefs

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Altar Chamber**
- Contains a central altar of pinkish-white marble carved with a human-shaped depression stained dark with old blood; a circular pit twenty feet in diameter to the north of the altar, surrounded by broken crystal vessels

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Bone Corridor**
- Accessed via secret door opened by a knock pattern on a wall at the base of the altar pit's descent
- Approximately 100 feet long and 20 feet wide; entirely covered in a jumble of human and humanoid skeletons, many in sundered armor or clutching broken weapons, remnants of an ancient battle
- Gnoll skeletons animate and attack intruders; multiple skeletons encountered and destroyed by the party
- Current state: Several skeletons destroyed; others potentially capable of animating

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Earth Temple (guard area, barracks, lounge, Romag's chamber)**
- A guarded torch-lit hall with four guards; adjacent barracks area and lounge decorated with scenes of drinking and debauchery; Romag's chamber is well-furnished and lavishly decorated
- Current state: Occupied and controlled by the Earth Temple

**Temple of Elemental Evil – Octagonal Room (Air Temple power source)**
- Located fifteen feet below the normal dungeon floor; large octagonal room with polished gray stone floor shot through with glittering mica; swirling glowing mist partially obscures the floor; ceiling, walls, and floor emit an eerie milky radiance
- Features: a great pierced bronze square at center; a circular shaft above a pit of equal diameter; an alabaster block south of the pit bearing two knives and a crystal bowl; crystal braziers suspended from silver chains emitting sickly sweet perfume smoke; bronze doors to the north sealed with iron chains and warning runes identical to those on the main entrance
- Current state: Controlled by the Air Temple; a wraith guards it; the party is currently present after dropping into the pit; wraith has retreated after being repelled by Calmer

**Hommlet**
- Referenced as the location where Spugnoir told the party about the Circle of Eight sealing the temple; not visited in this session

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Dick Rentsch's true intentions:** Whether Dick Rentsch deliberately deposited the party into Air Temple territory (the octagonal room and its pit) as a test of their abilities, an act of betrayal, or simply negligence is unresolved. Calmer suspects Dick withheld information.
- **The wraith:** The wraith retreated after being repelled by Calmer but was not destroyed; it may return or summon reinforcements.
- **Zephyr's drained vitality:** Zephyr's maximum vitality has been permanently reduced until restored by extended rest or powerful healing magic; access to such rest or healing within the temple is uncertain.
- **The Chest of Rao:** The party delivered it to Romag; its nature, contents, and significance have not been explained.
- **The scroll with ancient markings:** Recovered from the gnoll bodies; its contents and purpose are unknown.
- **The three ornate gold buttons:** Recovered from the gnoll bodies; their significance is unknown.
- **The badge of Earth:** Romag promised this upon completion of the mission; its powers or significance have not been described.
- **The map provided by Dick Rentsch:** Shows gnoll locations within the temple; the party has not yet acted on this information.
- **The deeper temple levels:** Romag stated that below the first two levels (divided among the four elemental temples) lie deeper levels with deeper secrets; these have not been explored or described.
- **The ancient battle in the bone corridor:** A great battle clearly took place here, leaving hundreds of human and humanoid dead; the circumstances, participants, and date of this battle are unknown.
- **The sealed bronze doors in the octagonal room:** Sealed with the same iron chains and warning runes as the main entrance; what lies beyond them is unknown.
- **The conflict between elemental temples:** The Earth, Fire, Water, and Air temples are in constant conflict; the outcomes of this conflict and its effect on the party's mission are unresolved. The Fire Temple's gnolls are a current target.
- **Alremm and the Fire Temple:** The Earth Temple guards stated that killing enough gnolls would deliver Alremm to them; Alremm's capabilities and resources are unknown.
- **The Circle of Eight's sealing magic:** The runes were placed by Mordenkainen, Bigby, and others; the extent and durability of this sealing, and whether it can be broken, is unaddressed.
- **Signs of disguised traffic on the road:** Someone is working to conceal heavy movement of creatures toward the temple; who is responsible and why is unknown.
- **The strange fungus on bones outside the temple:** None of the party had encountered it before; its nature and significance are unexplained.
