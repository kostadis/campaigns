## World Events

- The party met with Lareth in the moathouse and struck a deal: they would act as his agents to eliminate rivals in exchange for an unspecified arrangement; Lareth believes the party works for him.
- The party withheld key information from the Duke upon returning to his campsite, omitting the secret doors, the active torture chamber, and their deal with Lareth.

- The party travelled to Sheernobb on Planting 21, 579.
- The party was stopped at the village entrance by gnome guards; Zephyr's ring from Knarfallan secured them entry.
- The party took rooms and meals at the Honey Haven Inn; Zephyr was manipulated into paying for the group's food and drink by Mari Clee and the party members.
- Gurdy the bard shared information about increasingly organised bandit attacks and offered to join the party.
- Calmer strongly objected to Gurdy joining; Zephyr expressed openness to the idea.
- The inn's other owner (unnamed, male, squeaky-voiced) hired the party to recover stolen barrels of mead, with tracks leading into the forest; reward: one week's free lodging and a small keg of enchanted mead.
- Quinna Pool hired the party to hunt a rare gnarled boar in the gnarly forest for the Harvest Festival; reward: a special strength-blessing meal.
- Captain Gnonsbim hired the party to locate and neutralise the bandit camp; payment: 25gp for camp location, 10gp per dead bandit, 25gp for the leader, plus a custom-forged magical weapon if the party publicly credits his leadership.
- Gnonsbim instructed the party to speak with Pasmarie Clee.
- Calmer became noticeably drunk despite Zephyr's objection.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Lareth's true allegiance and calculations**: Lareth is described as calculating; it is unknown what he is actually planning or who he ultimately serves beyond the Fire Temple.
- **The missing chest**: A chest selected in the Viscounty art competition was stolen when its delivery mission to Celene was attacked. Its current whereabouts are unknown. Lareth has separately tasked the party with recovering a chest stolen by a rival group — whether these are the same chest is unconfirmed but strongly implied.
- **The rival cultists**: Lareth described a rival group of cultists who stole the chest. Their identity, faction, and location are unknown.
- **The Duke's trustworthiness**: The Duke is described as "very untrustworthy"; his true allegiances and motivations have not been revealed.
- **Are the moathouse cultists genuine Temple agents or political poseurs?**: Rufus and Burne suggested they may be agents of another government seeking to destabilise the Viscounty. This question is unresolved.
- **Knarfallan's ring**: The ring grants the party immediate credibility with Sheernobb guards; the full extent of its significance and Knarfallan's relationship with the village is unexplored.
- **Pasmarie Clee**: Captain Gnonsbim directed the party to speak with her regarding the bandits; she has not yet been met.
- **The organised bandits**: Bandits near Sheernobb are described as newly more organised than before. Their leadership, origin, and connection (if any) to the cult or the stolen chest are unknown.
- **Stolen mead barrels**: Tracks lead into the forest; the thieves have not been identified.
- **The gnarled boar**: A rare and dangerous creature in the gnarly forest; not yet hunted.
- **Celene diplomatic situation**: It is unknown whether the elves have ever actually accepted any of the Viscounty's art gifts, or whether they are simply destroying all of them. Zinnia suspects the Viscounty misunderstands why art goes missing.
- **Gurdy's potential role**: Whether Gurdy will join the party as a bard remains unresolved following Calmer's objection and Zephyr's openness to the idea.
- **The ogre from the moathouse**: An ogre claimed to serve an all-powerful cult leader; the identity of this leader has not been established.
