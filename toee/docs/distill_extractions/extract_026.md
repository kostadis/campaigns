## Threads & Mysteries

- **The Missing Prisoners:** The party previously freed prisoners who were to be used in Romag's loyalty ritual. Romag does not yet know they are missing. When he discovers this, it will likely create a crisis. The party's contingency plan is to blame the Fire Temple for the disappearance; it is unresolved whether this deception will succeed.
- **The Loyalty Ritual:** Romag has scheduled a ceremony for the following day involving the ripping apart of prisoners' bodies as an offering to the earthen god. The party is expected to actively participate. Calmer is morally opposed. The missing prisoners mean the ritual cannot proceed as planned, forcing a confrontation or improvisation.
- **Romag's Predecessor and the Deep Temple:** Romag's predecessor left to work for the Deep Temple, after which the local undead stopped following Romag. The nature of the Deep Temple, its relationship to the Earth Temple, and the predecessor's current role and motivations are entirely unresolved.
- **The Ghasts' True Loyalty:** The two ghasts declared Earth Temple allegiance purely for self-preservation. Their actual intentions and whether they will remain nominally cooperative or cause problems is unresolved.
- **Elven Chain Mail:** The party recovered elven chain mail from the harpy nesting room and intends to sell it. Its origin (who it previously belonged to, how it ended up in the harpy nesting area) is unexplained.
- **Sequioa's Secret Skimming:** Sequioa has secretly given Zephyr a smaller share of the treasure while giving himself, Calmer, and Zinnia more. Calmer and Zinnia are aware of receiving more. Whether Zephyr ever discovers this, and how it affects party dynamics, is unresolved.
- **Party Advancement Within the Earth Temple:** Romag agreed in principle to grant official titles and higher ranks, pending the loyalty ritual. The disruption caused by the missing prisoners may derail or complicate this advancement.
- **Fire Temple as Scapegoat:** The party intends to blame the Fire Temple for the missing prisoners. Whether this deception is believable to Romag, and what consequences it may have for relations between the Earth Temple and Fire Temple, is unresolved.
- **Calmer's Breaking Point:** Calmer has expressed growing moral fatigue with the party's decisions and the Earth Temple situation. Whether he will refuse to participate in or sabotage the planned ritual is an open question.
