# Hartsch

## Identity
- **Role:** Sub-general and speaker of the Earth Temple; lieutenant and strategic advisor to Romag
- **Faction:** Earth Temple (Temple of Elemental Evil)
- **First Appearance:** Chapter 18 — observed by the party in conversation with Romag following Krell's sacrifice ritual; referred to briefly as "Harch" in Chapter 22 when Romag instructed him to prepare prisoners for the loyalty ritual; formally identified as Hartsch in Chapter 23 during a private meeting with the party in their quarters
- *Also known as: Harch; "the Earth Pretender" (epithet for the Earth Temple's aspirant to the throne).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** To replace Romag as leader of the Earth Temple. Believes in aggressive, destructive leadership — an "earthquake" style — over Romag's passive approach.
- Tactically minded and opportunistic — quickly identified the party as a potential weapon to deploy against rival temples.
- Politically shrewd and willing to speak candidly when away from Romag's ears, suggesting he has been biding his time and looking for the right opportunity.
- Frames his ambition in theological terms ("vengeance power of the earth"), giving his coup-minded intentions an air of doctrinal conviction rather than naked self-interest.

## History with the Party
- **Chapter 18:** Witnessed by the party during a post-ritual conversation with Romag. Proposed using the party as an asset against either the Fire Temple or the Air Temple, framing them as a tool rather than genuine allies.
- **Chapter 22:** Briefly encountered as "Harch," tasked by Romag with preparing prisoners for the loyalty ritual ceremony. No significant direct interaction recorded.
- **Chapter 23:** Met privately with the party in their quarters, away from Romag. Delivered extensive tactical intelligence on the temple's political landscape:
  - **Fire Temple:** Nearly broken. Alremm's power has been waning since the Battle of Emridy Meadows (~10 years prior). Loss of his werewolves and the ruinous cost of the chimera have left it crippled.
  - **Air Temple:** Effectively irrelevant. Prophet Kelno leads a farcical remnant reduced to a handful of rooms with very little power remaining.
  - **Water Temple:** The true rival. Belsornig leads a formidable operation. His ally Vesrek arranged the chimera purchase as a deliberate scheme to drain both the Fire and Earth temples simultaneously — a calculated win-win against his rivals.
  - When Zinnia probed his ambitions through deception, Hartsch thinly veiled his desire for Romag's position: *"I would always, if a position were to suddenly have opened itself, I would absolutely like to think that my faith and my leadership qualities would be considered."*
- The party read this as an invitation and agreed to assassinate Romag, with the implicit understanding that Hartsch would take command of the Earth Temple afterward.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The party's private quarters within the Earth Temple (end of Chapter 23).
- **Active Plans:** Awaiting — or anticipating — the party's move against Romag. Has signaled willingness to assume leadership the moment a vacancy opens.
- **What the Party Knows:** That Hartsch wants Romag gone and considers himself the rightful successor. He has been cooperative and forthcoming with intelligence.
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether Hartsch has his own agenda beyond simply replacing Romag — his ultimate loyalty to the party's interests (versus using them as tools) is unconfirmed. His full capabilities and resources within the temple are unknown.

## Relationships
- **Romag:** Direct superior. Hartsch serves as sub-general and speaker but privately views Romag's leadership as insufficiently aggressive. Wants him removed.
- **Belsornig (Water Temple):** Identified as the Earth Temple's most dangerous rival. No personal relationship noted.
- **Vesrek:** Known to Hartsch as Belsornig's ally and architect of the chimera scheme targeting both Fire and Earth temples.
- **Alremm (Fire Temple):** Assessed by Hartsch as a spent force, no longer a serious threat.
- **Kelno (Air Temple):** Dismissed contemptuously as leading a near-nonexistent faction.
- **The Party:** Currently an asset and potential kingmakers. Hartsch has invested trust in them by sharing sensitive intelligence privately.
- **Zinnia (specifically):** She conducted the deception probe that drew out his ambitions; Hartsch responded to her directly and openly.
