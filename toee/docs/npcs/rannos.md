# Rannos

## Identity
- **Role:** Merchant/trader in Hommlet; covert agent of the Deep Temple of Elemental Evil
- **Faction:** Temple of Elemental Evil (alongside partner Gremag)
- **First Appearance:** Responded to a party-sent note requesting a meeting; made contact by inviting them to his establishment after dark

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** Served the Temple's interests in Hommlet; sought to acquire the Chest of Rao on the Temple's behalf
- Suspicious and calculating — refused the party's proposed meeting location in favor of his own controlled territory
- Contemptuous of the party; he and Gremag mocked their attempt to negotiate a peaceful withdrawal from the region

## History with the Party
1. Party sent a note requesting a meeting; Rannos responded with suspicion and countered with his own terms (his establishment, after dark)
2. During the meeting, the party attempted to negotiate for the Temple to abandon the region; Rannos and Gremag mocked them and demanded the Chest of Rao
3. With Gremag already dead, Rannos was severely wounded by Sequoia (26 damage), then finished by Calmer's Spiritual Weapon when he had 2 hp remaining
4. Thorne dismembered the body to prevent *Speak with Dead*
5. Bloodied clothes were left outside town; the party bluffed Ostler Goodingoot with a cover story that both traders had urgently departed after securing a magical chest

## Current Status
- **Status:** Deceased; body dismembered
- **Cover Story (Party-constructed):** Rannos and Gremag left Hommlet in a hurry after obtaining a magical chest
- **What the Party Knows:** He was a Temple agent working with Gremag; he wanted the Chest of Rao
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether the Temple is aware of his death or will investigate his disappearance

## Relationships
- **Gremag:** Co-conspirator and fellow Temple agent; also killed by the party in the same encounter
- **Ostler Goodingoot:** Local contact who accepted the party's cover story about Rannos and Gremag's departure
- **The Party:** Killed by Sequoia (killing blow by Calmer); body disposed of by Thorne
