# Varek Solain

> **Name note:** The module (area 206) does not name this NPC. "Varek Solain" is a proposed name — redirect if the table has already established something else.

## Identity

- **Role:** Commander of the Fire Temple's ground forces; now retained under party authority
- **Faction:** Former Fire Temple (military arm); currently subordinate to the Obsidian Edge / new Upper Temple administration
- **First Appearance:** Area 206 (Audience Chamber), Fire Temple, Dungeon Level Two — found alone and exhausted on the red-and-gilt throne. He knew the Earth Temple had won the war. When the party entered, he expected to die. Zephyr made him an offer instead: subordinate to the Earth Temple, keep the old job. He accepted. He was pleased.
- **Module chassis:** Gladiator (MM)

## Look

Heavyset, mid-forties, with the kind of build that comes from thirty years of actual fighting rather than training for it. A dark red cloak, faded and frayed at the hem. An eight-pointed fire amulet on a gold chain — the garnet catches the torchlight and he doesn't bother to tuck it away. The robe of entanglement hangs coiled at his belt like a coiled whip. He moves slowly when he doesn't have to move fast, and very fast when he does.

**Anchor:** The throne. The huge wooden seat lacquered red and gilt, with gold skulls on the armrests and above the back. He was found sitting in it. He has not moved it. When the party arrived, he was too tired to stand up, and that is approximately how he feels about most things now.

## Tic

Answers every question with its tactical implication first, before giving the actual answer. "Where are the remaining bugbears?" gets "Patrol rotation is down to three routes. South passage is uncovered from the second bell onward. They're on the east side."

## Personality & Motivations

- **Core goal:** Keep his remaining forces alive and functioning. He outlasted the clerics, the prophets, and the war itself; he would like to continue doing that.
- Not a fanatic. Never was. Served the Fire Temple because it paid reliably, promoted on merit, and the alternatives in this part of the world were worse.
- Pragmatic to the point of dryness — but the offer surprised him. He walked into that meeting expecting a sword. He got a job. That lands differently than a routine change of management.
- Quietly contemptuous of the clerical leadership — Alrrem died trying to summon a fire elemental "they were going to use to kill us all," and Varek is not surprised. He had filed a report about that summoning chamber three months ago recommending it be sealed. No one read it.
- The pleasure he felt at Zephyr's offer is real and he knows it. He is not going to perform gratitude, but he is genuinely glad to still be alive and still be doing his job. That is not nothing, and he doesn't pretend it is.
- Respects demonstrated competence above rank. Zephyr walked in expecting him to be a problem worth solving, not a body. That is exactly the kind of judgment Varek respects.

## What he wants from any interaction

Clarity on what's expected of him and how much of his authority over the remaining forces he actually retains.

## What he would never do

Throw his remaining men into a fight they can't survive to satisfy someone else's ambition. He watched Alrrem do that. He knows what it looks like.

## Voice

- *"The troll in 203 is technically still on contract. I don't know who's paying it now. Neither does it."*
- *"I don't have opinions about the greater temple's politics. I have patrol routes and a headcount. Tell me which of those you need."*
- *"The clerics told me what the goal was. You tell me what the goal is. I'll tell you what it costs."*
- *"I thought you were here to kill me. I'm glad you weren't."* — said once, to Zephyr, quietly, after the deal was struck. He will not say it again.

**Never says:** Anything ideological about elemental fire, the Elder Elemental God, or chaos. He has worked here for years and he treats the theology the way a prison guard treats the prison's founding philosophy — something other people care about.

**Body:** Stays seated when he doesn't need to stand. Keeps his right hand near the Flametongue but doesn't touch the hilt unless he's already decided to draw it. When giving information, he looks at a point above whoever he's talking to, like he's reading from a mental ledger.

## History with the Party

1. **Ch. 28, Area 206:** Found alone and exhausted on the throne. He knew the Earth Temple had won; he knew what that meant for a surviving Fire Temple commander. When the party entered he did not reach for the Flametongue. Zephyr made him the offer — subordinate to the Earth Temple, keep the old job. He said yes. He was pleased, and he let it show, briefly, before his face went back to the ledger.
2. **Ch. 28, introductions:** The party introduced him to Maeris Dorn — the surviving curate, now installed as head of the Fire Temple and therefore his clerical superior. They also introduced him to the salamander now working for the Obsidian Edge. He met both without visible reaction. He has known Maeris by reputation; he is reassessing her now that she outranks him. The salamander he is filing under "forces I do not control but share a corridor with."

## Current Status

- **Location:** Area 206, Audience Chamber; occasionally the surrounding corridors doing his own patrol assessment
- **Active:** Taking stock of what's left — headcount, patrol routes, the troll situation, the treasury (Belsornig was pulling it before he died; Varek knows the south route was involved)
- **What the party knows:** He accepted the offer; he's staying
- **What remains hidden:** Whether his acceptance was permanent or situational; the full extent of what he knows about the treasury route; his read on the party's actual competence

## Relationships

- **The Obsidian Edge:** New employers. He is watching them the way he watched every cleric he's worked for — but Zephyr's offer bought something the clerics never managed: his actual goodwill, not just his compliance. He is not going to perform loyalty. He is going to do the job.
- **Zephyr specifically:** The one who made the offer. Varek will deal with Zephyr as his primary contact before he extends that to the rest of the party. He doesn't know yet whether the others share Zephyr's judgment.
- **Maeris Dorn (Fire Temple head cleric):** His new clerical superior. He knew her by reputation as Alrrem's curate. He did not expect her to be the one left standing, and he is quietly recalibrating. He will work with her. Whether he respects her yet is an open question.
- **The salamander (Obsidian Edge retainer):** Met during introductions. He has no framework for a salamander as a colleague rather than a threat. He is not going to say that out loud.
- **Dren Halveth:** Same tier of problem. Two military commanders serving the same new authority. Varek doesn't know Dren yet; the introduction will be important.
- **Remaining bugbears:** He knows their names. They know his. This is not nothing.
- **The troll (area 203):** Technically still on contract. Nobody has told it otherwise. He is leaving this for someone else to resolve.
- **Barkinar:** Knows the Greater Temple exists; knows it just promoted the party. Has no illusions about what happens to him if he becomes inconvenient to them.

## Notes for the GM

- **The Flametongue is a lever.** The party may want it. He may offer it as a gesture of loyalty, or he may not — it's the most valuable thing he owns and the only weapon in the temple calibrated for fire creatures. If the salamander fight is still active when they meet him properly, he may already have assessed the situation.
- **The treasury thread.** He knows Belsornig was pulling the Water Temple treasury out the south route. He doesn't know if it made it. This is usable.
- **Dren meeting Varek.** Two pragmatic military commanders, both now nominally under the same authority, both with a slightly skeptical read on the clerics who died above them. They will either form a professional alliance immediately or resent each other's territory. The party is the variable.
- **His report about the summoning chamber.** He filed it. No one read it. He still has the copy. If the party ever investigates how Alrrem died, this comes out.
- **Promote to strategy doc** if Varek becomes a recurring figure in the temple administration arc.
