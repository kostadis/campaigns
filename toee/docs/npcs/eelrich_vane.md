# Brother Eelrich Vane

**Role:** Last surviving under-priest of the Water Temple; the party's recruit-or-kill choice for puppet head-cleric of the Water cult
**Location:** Area 214 ("The Lounge"), adjacent to the Hall of Verdigris (213), Water Temple, Dungeon Level 2
**Faction:** Water Temple (Temple of Elemental Evil) — leaderless since Belsornig's death; serves the water-evil archomental (Olhydra) the way the rank-and-file unknowingly serve Zuggtmoy
**First seen:** Not yet — staged for the Water Temple session following the 213 juggernaut/gargoyle fight

## Look
A soft, damp man in cowled green robes with a gold circle stitched front and back, plate mail glinting underneath — and he is never quite *dry*. Water beads at his hairline and his cuffs no matter the room; he leaves faint wet handprints on everything he touches, and on his right hand he wears the cult's **gold ring shaped as a ten-armed sea-monster, a peridot held in its tentacles** (750 gp), which he twists, slow and constant, whenever he is calculating — which is always.

## Tic
He **renegotiates everything** — every answer he gives is really a counter-offer, and the moment a blade is pointed at him he is already pitching his own value: *listing* what he knows, what he can run, what dies with him if they're hasty.

## What they want from any interaction
To still be breathing and useful at the end of it — and, if he can manage it, to have made himself indispensable to whoever now holds the whip.

## What they would never do
Die for a cause. Belsornig is dead, the temple is sacked, and Eelrich Vane was never a believer — he was a survivor in vestments. He will not fight to the last; he flips, flees, or buys his way out.

## Voice
- *"Before you decide — and you should decide slowly, you strike me as people who decide too fast — let me tell you what's in my head, because it is worth more to you alive than my blood is worth on that nice floor."*
- *"Belsornig is dead. I wept the regulation amount. Now: the treasury under the fourth basin doesn't overflow because it drains to a sump only I can find, and the trolls he was buying — that contract isn't signed yet. Kill me and you go looking for both. Keep me and I hand them to you by supper."*
- *(if pressed on loyalty, smiling, twisting the ring)* "Loyalty. Yes. I'm extremely loyal. To the *current* arrangement. You'll find that's the most reliable kind."

## What the party knows about them
- [KNOWN on sight] A water-cult priest in the canon green-and-gold robes, plate beneath, the kraken-and-peridot ring. [KNOWN] He's alive and alone — the only Water priest left after the Fire Temple's assault gutted the cult in the war the party engineered.
- [SUSPECTED, fast] He'll flip. He makes it obvious. The *question* the party has to sit with is whether that's a feature or the trap.
- [GM-ONLY] He genuinely holds operational gold: the Water Temple treasury and its hidden sump-drain (area 213's fourth basin), the **half-finished troll-chieftain contract** Belsornig was negotiating (areas 218–219), the water-cult spy habits, and a working read on which Nulb contacts were Belsornig's. This is the real value of recruiting over killing.
- [GM-ONLY] He survived precisely *because* he is the kind of man who sells the room he's standing in. He has no idea about Hedrack's coup, Lucius, or the Deep Temple — but he is exactly the sort of asset Lucius would later turn, or that would quietly sell the party's secrets to the highest bidder if one appears.

## Notes for the GM

- **The contrast with the Fire Cleric (June 14) is the point.** That one capitulated with a shrug and was harmless comic relief. Vane capitulates *smoothly and immediately* and is **not** harmless — he's Belsornig's ethos in miniature (cunning, fear-trained, manipulative) wearing a smaller hat. Recruiting him literally re-seats the Water Temple's manipulator archetype inside the party's new regime. That is the "you are going in too deep" lever Zinnia already flagged — let her or Calmer feel it.
- **The choice has real teeth, not a fake one.**
  - **Recruit:** he becomes the puppet head-cleric, hands over the treasury/sump, the troll contract, and the spy network — a genuine intelligence windfall — but you've planted a snake. Future hook: he's the obvious seam for a Deep Temple / Lucius / Scarlet-Brotherhood agent to pull, or a slow betrayal of his own if the wind shifts. He never stops weathervaning.
  - **Kill:** clean and safe, and it loses you the Water Temple's operational knowledge — the sump-drain, the unsigned troll deal (which then defaults badly, per `war-in-the-temple.md` Flashpoint 1), and the soft Nulb intel. Calmer's player may *want* this on principle; let that be a party-tension beat.
- **The second under-priest is the fork made flesh (canon: area 214 quarters *two* under-priests).** Play the other one as the foil: a true fanatic who attacks on sight, calls the alarm to areas 215/220–225, and dies fighting — the "kill" path embodied — while Vane is already raising his hands behind him. The party can save Vane *from* his own zealot colleague, which buys his loyalty cheaply and stages the choice cleanly. (Or, if you want the recruit harder to get, reverse it: the zealot is the one who almost talks Vane back into fighting.)
- **If the players try to verify his loyalty (Insight/Zone of Truth/etc.):** he tells the truth, because the truth is *"I will serve you exactly as long as serving you is the safest thing I can do,"* and he says it to your face. There's nothing to catch — that openness is itself the unsettling part. He is not lying about being a man who would.
- **If they install him and later need leverage:** he fears (a) being handed back to anything water-elemental that remembers him as a deserter, and (b) the Fire Temple remnants who slaughtered his brethren — a grievance the party can point him at, the way Vurakhal can be pointed at Water. Note the mirror: the party now holds a fire-creature who hates Water (Vurakhal) and may hold a water-priest who hates Fire (Vane). Putting them in the same room is a scene.

## Combat capability (if it goes the other way)

Use the canon chassis: **Priest (MM p348), CR 2** — AC 13 (chain shirt + shield; canon says plate beneath robes, bump to **AC 18** if you want him to survive a round), HP ~27, two war-hammer attacks, *Divine Eminence*. Reflavor the spell list to water per canon area-214:
- **1st level:** *Command*, *Cure Wounds* (reflavor: *Tidemend* — a coil of cold seawater knits the wound), *Sanctuary*, *Protection from Evil and Good* (as *Saltward*)
- **2nd level:** *Hold Person* (as *Undertow* — the target's limbs go leaden, as if dragged down), *Spiritual Weapon* (a striking jet of pressured brine)
- He is **CR 2 against a level-8 party — i.e., not a threat.** That's intentional: the drama here is the *social* choice, not the fight. If steel is drawn he casts *Sanctuary* and *Undertow*/*Hold Person* to stall, screams for areas 215/220–225, and breaks for the seaweed-draped south alcove (213) and its bronze doors at the first opening. He does not die in place. If cornered with no exit, he drops his hammers and resumes negotiating mid-sentence.
- For a full reflavored block, run `/gm-statblock` reflavor mode on *Priest* → "water under-priest of Olhydra." Worth doing only if you expect a real fight; otherwise the line above is enough to run him.

## Open decisions (for the user)
- **Does he become recurring?** If the party installs him and you want him to matter, promote this into a `gm-strategy-doc` (the snake-in-the-regime, with an actual play-of-his-own). If he's a one-scene flip, this dossier is enough.
- **The peridot kraken ring** (750 gp) — loot if killed; if recruited, does he keep it as a badge of office, or do the party take it as a trophy/leash? Small but characterful.
- **Which way the second under-priest breaks** — zealot-attacks-and-dies (cleaner stage for the choice) vs. zealot-nearly-pulls-Vane-back-into-the-fight (makes the recruit harder-won). Your call by table mood.
