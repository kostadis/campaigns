# Romag

## Identity
- **Role:** High Priest and leader of the Earth Temple within the Temple of Elemental Evil
- **Faction:** Earth Temple (Temple of Elemental Evil)
- **First Appearance:** Introduced formally by Dick Rentsch, who brought the party to Romag's lavishly decorated chambers as their official contact within the Earth Temple hierarchy

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Seize ultimate power within the Temple of Elemental Evil by destroying rival elemental temples; recover the orb that unlocks the temple's heart and the vault within; rebuild Earth Temple strength following costly internal setbacks
- Romag is ambitious but inexperienced—widely characterized as a new leader overcompensating through micromanagement and grandiose ritual displays rather than genuine tactical cleverness
- He prefers to operate as a "chess king," sending others to fight while he directs from safety, and relies on masses of low-level troops rather than powerful assets
- He presents warmth and approval freely when pleased, but responds to betrayal with spectacular, public violence, as demonstrated by Krell's ritual sacrifice

## History with the Party
**Early Contact:** Romag received the party via Dick Rentsch, accepted the Chest of Rao from them, and outlined the temple's true scope—four elemental factions in constant internal conflict, with deeper secrets below. He tasked the party with destroying rival temples so the Earth Temple could claim dominance, promising the badge of Earth in return.

**Werewolf Test & Badges:** When the party survived a werewolf trial (arranged by Alrrem rather than gnolls as expected), Romag awarded them Earth Temple badges and confronted Dick Rentsch over the substitution.

**Krell Affair:** Romag dispatched Lieutenant Krell on a raid against Fire Temple territory; the party exposed Krell as a saboteur. Romag claimed he had known all along and ordered Krell brought back alive. He then conducted a public sacrifice—forcing dirt into Krell's mouth and commanding four earth elementals to tear the traitor apart as a warning. He was privately overheard with Hartsch acknowledging the rebellion had weakened Earth Temple resources and plans.

**Ideological Indoctrination:** Romag briefed the party on the temple's history—the defeat at the Battle of Emory Meadows ten years prior by the Archbishop of Veluna, Furyondy, and the Circle of Eight (Bigby, Tenser, Mordenkainen)—and revealed the existence of a powerful orb described as *the shackle that binds storm and flood*, which can supersede the temple bureaucracy and open a sealed vault. He declined to reveal the vault's contents, stating that information must be earned.

**Broken Blades Recruitment:** Romag sent the party to Nulb to recruit the mercenary company the Broken Blades before the Water Temple could. Upon their successful return with Dren Halveth, he hosted a celebratory drinking party, singling out Calmer's conversion from St. Cuthbert worship as a point of ideological pride.

**Territorial Expansion:** Romag directed the party to clear dungeon territory to the south and east. When they succeeded against ghouls, harpies, and ghasts, he complained that the undead had refused his authority since his predecessor was promoted to the Deep Temple, and praised the party for bringing the ghasts under control.

**Rank & Ceremony:** When the party pressed for titles and payment, Romag clarified they owed the temple a cut of recovered treasure rather than the reverse. He agreed to advance their ranks but demanded a loyalty ritual first, instructing Harch to prepare prisoners—unaware the party had already freed those prisoners.

**Chimera Attack (Ch. 23):** Romag received news of a Fire Temple chimera attack and prisoner theft with fury, declaring it the moment to strike Alrrem while the Fire Temple was weakened. He ordered the party to move against Alrrem directly.

**Assassination Plot:** Following a private meeting with Hartsch, the party resolved to assassinate Romag and install Hartsch as his replacement.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** His chambers within the Earth Temple
- **Active Plans:** Directing the party to strike against the weakened Fire Temple leader Alrrem; advancing the Earth Temple's internal power position; loyalty ceremony for the party (postponed, unknowingly reliant on prisoners already freed)
- **What the Party Knows:** His leadership is characterized as weak, inexperienced, and micromanaging; Hartsch views him as expendable; he is unaware the prisoners are gone, unaware his Otyugh is dead, and unaware the party is plotting against him
- **What Remains Hidden from the Party:** The full contents of the vault the orb unlocks; what the "Deep Temple" is and what Romag's predecessor does there

## Relationships
- **Dick Rentsch:** Original intermediary who introduced the party to Romag; Romag holds him accountable for party performance
- **Hartsch (Lieutenant):** Romag's subordinate and the party's secret co-conspirator; privately disparages Romag's leadership style as slow and passive
- **Harch:** Subordinate instructed to prepare the loyalty ceremony
- **Alrrem (Fire Temple):** Primary rival and current military target; Romag views the chimera attack as justification to move against him now
- **The Party:** Valued as elite operatives—too useful to sacrifice; Romag has invested ideological energy in their conversion and advancement, unaware they are planning his death
- **Calmer (party):** Specifically highlighted by Romag as a trophy conversion from St. Cuthbert worship

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase:** Awarded Earth Temple badges after party survived the werewolf test; praised the party publicly after the Broken Blades recruitment; expressed strong approval following territorial clearance
- **Decrease:** Exposed as oblivious to large portions of his own territory; privately acknowledged by Hartsch and himself as weakened after the Krell rebellion; party has resolved to assassinate him and replace him with Hartsch
