# Maeris Dorn

> **Name note:** The module (area 205, curate) does not name this NPC. "Maeris Dorn" is a proposed name — redirect if the table has already established something else. Pronoun: she/her (per module text).

## Identity

- **Role:** Curate of the Fire Temple; now installed as its new head cleric under party authority
- **Faction:** Fire Temple (formerly under Alrrem); now subordinate to the Obsidian Edge / Greater Temple administration
- **First Appearance:** Area 205b (Alrrem's chamber complex), Fire Temple, Dungeon Level Two — caught mid-flight, arms full of portable valuables, when the party surprised her. She was about to attack. Zephyr and the party grappled her. Barkinar announced the party's authority. She looked at her three options — fight and die, flee and be hunted, subordinate and live — and took the third. She is now the ranking cleric of the Fire Temple.
- **Module chassis:** Cult Fanatic (MM), Dexterity 16

## Look

Lean, mid-thirties, with the precise economy of movement that comes from being the least physically imposing person in a room for most of her career. Dark hair, close-cropped on one side from an old burn scar she doesn't explain. She is now wearing Alrrem's ceremonial robe — red silk with golden skulls on back and sleeves, the eight-pointed fire symbol set in garnets across the front. It is too big for her. She has not taken it in. She wears it as-is.

**Anchor:** The robe. She was in the process of stuffing it into a bag when the party grabbed her. It is worth 3,000 gp. She chose not to run with it after all. She chose to wear it instead.

## Tic

Does the math out loud, briefly, before committing to anything. Not deliberation — a single sentence, clipped, almost to herself. *"Three options. The first kills me immediately. The second, eventually. I'll take the third."* She said this to no one in particular in the moment she decided to submit, and everyone heard it.

## Personality & Motivations

- **Core goal:** Survive, and now that survival looks more interesting than expected, figure out what head of the Fire Temple actually means when the Fire Temple's previous leadership died trying to summon a fire elemental.
- Not a fanatic in the devotional sense. She took orders from the Fire Temple because the Fire Temple offered advancement, structure, and access to real magical training. The theology was the price of admission.
- Smart in a way she has mostly had to hide. Alrrem tolerated competent subordinates up to a point, and she had learned where that point was.
- The decision to submit was made in the same register she decides everything: quickly, without performance, with full awareness of the odds. She is not grateful to the party for sparing her. She is aware that they did, and she is acting accordingly.
- She now holds a title she was not supposed to reach for another decade. She knows this changes her position in every room she enters. She is still working out what to do with that.

## What she wants from any interaction

To understand what the party actually expects from her so she can either deliver it or negotiate something workable.

## What she would never do

Martyr herself for the Elder Elemental God. She never believed in that sufficiently to die for it, and she is not going to start now.

## Voice

- *"I wasn't running. I was consolidating portable assets."*
- *"You need a cleric. I need to not be dead. This is not complicated."*
- *"Alrrem believed fire was an end. I think it's a tool. We'll see which philosophy survives longer."*
- *"Three options. The first kills me immediately. The second, eventually. I'll take the third."* — said once, to herself, in the moment of decision. The party heard it. She has not repeated it.

**Never says:** Anything expressing genuine devotion to the Elder Elemental God, Imix, or elemental fire as a cosmic principle. She can perform it if required. She will not mean it.

**Body:** Keeps her hands visible when talking to the party — a habit from being grappled once and not wanting it to happen again. When she is actually thinking, she goes very still. When she is performing competence, she moves more.

## History with the Party

1. **Ch. 28, Area 205b:** Caught mid-flight with portable valuables from Alrrem's chambers. Grappled before she could attack. Barkinar announced the party's authority over the Upper Temple. She assessed her options aloud ("Three options...") and chose subordination. Installed as the new head cleric of the Fire Temple — a position she was not Alrrem's intended successor for, and which is now hers because she is the only Fire Temple cleric still alive.
2. **Ch. 28, introductions:** The party introduced her to Varek Solain as her military commander — and to the salamander now working for the Obsidian Edge. She filed both carefully. Varek she knows as competent and pragmatic; learning he also flipped tells her the party is running a consistent offer, not improvising. The salamander she watched more carefully than she showed. A fire creature serving an Earth Temple-aligned authority is theologically strange, and she noted it.

## Current Status

- **Location:** Area 205 (Alrrem's chamber complex); she has not yet moved to a different space
- **Active:** Taking stock of the Fire Temple's clerical situation, which consists almost entirely of herself
- **What the party knows:** She submitted; she is the new Fire Temple head; she made the decision rationally and quickly
- **What remains hidden:** The full extent of what she knows about the Fire Temple's internal operations, treasury, and Alrrem's private correspondence; whether she was in contact with the Greater Temple independent of Alrrem; how far her actual loyalty extends once she has established herself

## Relationships

- **The Obsidian Edge:** New authority. She is watching Calmer specifically — he is the party's designated cleric, which makes him either her peer or her superior depending on how the party structures things, and neither of them knows yet which it is.
- **Calmer specifically:** Two clerics, two different gods, in the same temple hierarchy. She has no reason to trust his faith and every reason to study it. She will not challenge him directly. She will notice everything.
- **Varek Solain (Commander, area 206):** They have worked in the same building for some time. She knows him as competent and not ideological. She does not know yet that he also accepted the party's offer. That introduction will be significant.
- **Barkinar:** Heard him announce the party's authority. That announcement is the load-bearing reason she submitted rather than fled. She understands Greater Temple power when she sees it.
- **Alrrem (deceased):** Served under him. Did not mourn him. Did take his robe.

## Notes for the GM

- **The robe is the scene.** She is wearing a dead man's vestments because she decided, in real time, that wearing them was more useful than selling them. That is her character in one image.
- **The Calmer dynamic.** The party now has two clerics in its orbit — one (Calmer) who is secretly St. Cuthbert under an Earth Temple cover, and one (Maeris) who is the nominal head of the Fire Temple. If Calmer's cover ever slips, Maeris is the one most likely to catch it. She is trained to read devotional behavior.
- **Alrrem's scroll tube.** It contains *dispel magic*, *flame strike*, and *tongues*. If she had it in her bag, the party may not know. If it's still in the room, she knows where it is. Either way it's a real asset and she knows its value.
- **Her knowledge of the treasury.** She was grabbing portable valuables. She knows where the non-portable ones are. The Fire Temple's financial situation is something she has operational knowledge of that the party does not.
- **Promote to strategy doc** if she becomes a significant figure in the temple administration arc or if the Calmer/Maeris dynamic develops into something load-bearing.
