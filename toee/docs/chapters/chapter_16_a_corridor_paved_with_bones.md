# Chapter 16 A Corridor Paved with Bones

## **Summary**

The party stood over their captured gnoll, bound and snarling at their feet. Calmer, the cleric of St. Cuthbert, burned with righteous fury at the sight of the creature—a spawn of demon powers born by bursting from the bodies of hyenas. When Zephyr attempted to politely question the prisoner, Calmer suggested a more forceful approach. Zephyr slapped the gnoll and demanded information, but the creature only hissed and threatened to rip them to shreds. With the interrogation clearly failing, Calmer swiftly ended the gnoll’s life, his hatred for these demonic beings overriding any further attempts at diplomacy.

Searching the bodies, the party discovered several items of interest: a scroll bearing ancient markings, three ornate gold buttons, and symbols marking the dead as servants of the Temple of Fire. With their grim task complete, they made their way back to Nulb and the Waterside Hostel, where their contact Dick Rentsch awaited them. In the back room, Dick Rentsch confirmed that he had arranged a meeting with Romag, a powerful figure within the temple they sought. Calmer, ever cautious and weary of being killed yet again, insisted on taking precautions while they waited. He moved a heavy chest to barricade the door and positioned Zephyr to watch the window, muttering about his growing exhaustion with making deals with evil powers that wanted them all dead.

When Dick Rentsch returned, he led them out of Nulb along a rutted, weed-choked road toward their destination. The path was spotted with rank vegetation—thistles, burrs, thorns, and nettles—and though it appeared little-used, one of the party members noticed signs that many creatures of various sizes had recently traveled this way, with someone working hard to disguise the heavy traffic. As they approached, the landscape grew increasingly disturbing. Dead trees stood like skeletons, and twisted, unnaturally colored vegetation surrounded them. Skulls and bones gleamed white among the weeds, some covered in strange fungus none of them had ever seen before.

The Temple of Elemental Evil loomed before them, a massive structure far larger than they had imagined. A ruined tower jutted from the northeast corner of shattered walls, and the temple itself stood like a leprous gray monument to darkness. Its arched buttresses were obscene, covered in climbing vegetation, and every surface was carved with leering faces and writhing forms depicting unspeakable obscenities. A pervading sense of fear hung over the entire area like a tangible cloud of vileness. Sounds seemed distorted, and movements flickered at the edges of their vision. The wind moaned through hundreds of apertures in the temple walls, creating sounds like the wails of doomed souls, while ravens croaked and flapped among the ruins.

The main entrance featured two pairs of massive bronze doors, twenty-three feet high and twenty feet wide, held fast by huge iron chains with all cracks sealed. Graven upon these doors were glowing runes that burned with silvery radiance, making their eyes water to look upon. Dick Rentsch carefully avoided these runes, leading them instead to a side entrance. The party recalled a story from Spugnoir in Hommlet about the Circle of Eight—powerful wizards including the legendary Mordenkainen and Bigby—who had come here and performed mighty magic to seal this place.

Dick Rentsch pulled them inside through the side door into a vestibule. The floor was covered in reddish-brown slate-like tiles, and the plastered walls were painted with horrifying scenes of killing, torture, enslavement, robbery, and unspeakable acts. Dim light filtered through stained glass windows, casting revolting colors across the floor. Pinkish mineral pillars shot through with warm-colored veins supported arches decorated with vile reliefs. Dick Rentsch led them deeper into the temple, to a chamber containing a central altar of pinkish-white marble carved with a disturbing human-shaped depression stained dark with old blood. North of the altar yawned a circular pit twenty feet in diameter, surrounded by shards of broken crystal vessels.

Dick Rentsch seemed disturbingly at ease here, describing it as his happy place. When asked why the temple was so empty, he laughed and explained that once the Earth Temple destroyed the other false prophets, this place would be filled with supplicants who would kill and slaughter and bury alive all who opposed them. He then led them down into the pit itself, where a narrow ledge spiraled down the northern face to steep steps below. As the party prepared to descend, Calmer asked if they could use rope for safety. Dick Rentsch sighed in exasperation, muttering that he thought they were getting better recruits this time, but allowed them to tie themselves together before beginning the long descent.

At the bottom, Dick Rentsch knocked on a wall in a particular pattern, and a secret door opened to reveal a corridor at least one hundred feet long and twenty feet wide. The entire passage was covered in bones—a terrible jumble of human and humanoid skeletons, many still wearing sundered armor or clutching broken weapons, testifying to a great battle that had taken place here. Dick Rentsch warned them to be careful not to disturb the dead as he picked his way through the grim remains. As they followed him eastward, several gnoll skeletons suddenly animated and rose from the bone pile to attack.

The party fought desperately against the undead creatures. Sequioa struck down the first skeleton with a powerful blow, while the remaining skeletons attacked, wounding both Sequioa and Zephyr. Zephyr retreated and struck back, while Zinnia delivered a devastating blow to another skeleton. Calmer raised his holy symbol and called upon the power of St. Cuthbert, destroying one skeleton outright and damaging others with divine radiance. The party quickly finished off the remaining undead, and Dick Rentsch urged them to move quickly before more skeletons could animate. They ran through the rest of the corridor, emerging into a guarded area.

Four guards stationed in a torch-lit hall questioned Dick Rentsch, who explained that the party were the new recruits he had promised to Romag. The guards expressed their loyalty to the Earth Temple and their hatred for the Fire Temple, boasting that once they killed enough gnolls, Alremm—the head of the Fire Temple—would be theirs. The party passed through a barracks area and a lounge decorated with scenes of drinking and debauchery, finally arriving at a well-furnished chamber where two commanders stood guard. Dick Rentsch asked if he needed to confiscate their weapons or if they were smart enough not to try anything stupid. The party assured him of their cooperation, though there was some lighthearted banter about cutting off Zephyr’s tiefling horns.

They were brought before Romag, the High Priest of the Earth Temple, who sat in his lavishly decorated chamber. Romag greeted them warmly, pleased that Dick Rentsch had finally brought him something worthy of his time. He asked for the Chest of Rao, and the party presented it to him. When the party seemed uncertain about the temple’s scope, Romag laughed heartily at their ignorance. He explained that the surface temple was merely the beginning, and that the true power lay in the deeper levels descending into the very pit of evil itself. The first two levels were divided among the Earth, Fire, Water, and Air temples, all locked in constant conflict. Below that lay the deeper temple and deeper secrets. Romag’s mission for them was clear: destroy the other temples so the Earth Temple could seize ultimate power. Dick Rentsch provided them with a map showing where the gnolls could be found, explaining that they would need to navigate through territory controlled by multiple hostile factions. Romag promised that once they proved themselves capable of operating within the temple by completing this mission, they would earn the right to wear the badge of Earth.

The party descended once more, running quickly past the skeleton-filled corridor to avoid another encounter. They arrived in a large octagonal room fifteen feet below the normal dungeon floor, illuminated by an eerie milky radiance emanating from the ceiling, walls, and floor. The polished gray stone floor, shot through with glittering mica, was partially obscured by swirling, glowing mist. In the center stood a great pierced bronze square, and a circular shaft opened above a pit of the same diameter. South of the pit sat an alabaster block bearing two knives and a crystal bowl, while crystal braziers suspended from silver chains emitted sickly sweet perfume smoke. Bronze doors to the north were sealed with iron chains and warning runes identical to those on the main entrance above.

The party dropped into the pit, and immediately a wraith materialized before them—a terrifying undead spirit of malevolent power. Zephyr struck first, landing blows with her dagger, but the wraith retaliated with devastating force. Its spectral touch drained Zephyr’s very life essence, not only wounding him but reducing his maximum vitality in a way that could only be restored through extended rest or powerful healing magic. Calmer called upon St. Cuthbert’s divine power once more, causing the wraith to recoil in fear and suffer holy damage. As the frightened wraith retreated, Calmer moved away, stating grimly that he was beginning to think Dick hadn’t told them everything about what awaited them here.

The party regrouped, realizing with growing concern that they were standing in what was clearly the Air Temple’s power source. Dick Rentsch had not simply sent them down a hallway—he had deposited them directly into enemy territory belonging to a rival elemental faction. Whether this was a test of their abilities or something more sinister remained unclear, but one thing was certain: they were now deep within the Temple of Elemental Evil, surrounded by ancient evil and warring factions, with only their wits and weapons to see them through the trials ahead.
