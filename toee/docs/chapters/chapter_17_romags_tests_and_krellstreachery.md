# Chapter 17 Romag’s Tests and Krell’sTreachery

## **Summary**

The party stood in the eerie octagonal room deep beneath the Temple of Elemental Evil, having just escaped a terrifying wraith that had drained Zephyr’s very life essence. The chamber glowed with an unnatural milky red light, swirling mist obscuring the polished gray floor shot through with glittering mica. Calmer muttered that Dick Rentsch hadn’t told them everything about what awaited them in these depths, and the party realized with growing concern that they stood in the Air Temple’s power source—deposited directly into enemy territory.

Following the map Dick Rentsch had provided, the party made their way through a processional hallway painted with scenes of gross evil, death, and destruction. Bronze doors lined the southern walls, their polished surfaces carved with vile scenes and runes. The party understood this was where clerics would drag their sacrificial captives during religious ceremonies, leading them toward the various elemental temples that warred for dominance in this cursed place.

When they reached the designated door and knocked, they entered a room that felt entirely different from the rest of the temple. The floor was white marble, the walls and vaulted ceiling sheathed in pearly alabaster, creating a right feel—a place of safety in a storm of evil. The room was bare and clean, and as they looked around, a sheet of cloudy crystal substance set into the northern portion of the east wall began to brighten, its radiance lighting the chamber. Two man-like creatures with wings of snowy feathers and radiant faces appeared amidst glowing, cloud-tinted billows, robed in purest white with a pale blue sky visible between them.

The brighter of the two radiant figures peered at the party and spoke in dulcet tones, asking what fair and just folk were doing in this foul place of pain and base wickedness. When the party explained they were on a mission to find gnolls and defeat the Temple of Elemental Evil, the figures seemed delighted. The brighter figure then made an unusual request: the party should place every weapon, holy symbol, magic item, and silvered item before the crystal window, then step out and count to seventy-seven while the figures sent a ray of just white through the room to bless their equipment, doubling its efficacy for seven hours.

The party hesitated, their instincts warning them something was amiss. Zephyr offered only his short sword, Zinnia gave a single dagger, and Sequioa reluctantly parted with one dagger as well. When the radiant figures pressed them to surrender more weapons, insisting they surely wouldn’t refuse such a divine blessing, the party stood firm in their refusal. Disappointed, the angelic figures declared they would reserve their blessing for someone else and vanished, leaving the party to wonder what trap they had narrowly avoided.

As they exited through the eastern door, Zinnia’s keen eyes spotted a secret entrance. Opening it discreetly, they discovered a bare room permeated by a feral odor, with nesting heaps of cloth and pillows, bones and litter covering the floor, and rust-red cloaks hanging on pegs. Inside were two werewolves who snarled at the intruders, their eyes gleaming with predatory intelligence. One of them laughed darkly, saying this could have been much easier if they had just dropped all their weapons and come in unarmed—they supposed they would have to do it the hard way for the Fire Temple.

The battle was fierce and desperate. The party quickly realized their weapons were ineffective against the lycanthropic creatures, their blades seeming to pass through flesh without causing proper harm. Calmer called upon his divine power, channeling energy to temporarily enchant Sequioa’s short sword and Zinnia’s spear with magical properties. Zephyr’s dagger already possessed such enchantment, and Calmer wielded his mace of smiting. The werewolves fought with savage ferocity, their claws and fangs tearing into the party, but Calmer’s radiant guardians materialized around him, burning the creatures with holy light. Zinnia landed a devastating blow with his enchanted spear, and together the party brought down both werewolves, standing victorious but battered.

Calmer declared there had better be some magical items in the lair after such a difficult fight. Searching through the room, they discovered a silver service worth a considerable sum, which they divided among themselves. More importantly, they found a magical dagger and short sword, which they distributed to those who still lacked enchanted weapons. But it was Zinnia who made the most crucial discovery—a loose flagstone that concealed a hidden treasure pile. Beneath it lay silver pieces, holy symbols of Saint Cuthbert and Rao, and most critically, five silver daggers and nineteen silver-tipped arrows. The irony was not lost on them: the very equipment they had needed to fight the werewolves had been hidden in the werewolves’ own lair.

The party decided to return to Romag and report what they had found. They carefully navigated back through the dangerous corridors, sneaking past the wraith in the octagonal room and moving silently through the bone-covered hallway where skeletal warriors lay waiting to animate. When they finally reached Romag’s chamber, they found Dick Rentsch sitting beside the high priest. Romag was pleased to see them alive, as Dick Rentsch had apparently wagered they would not survive. When the party explained they had encountered werewolves instead of gnolls, Romag turned sharply to Dick Rentsch, who admitted that Alremm had hired werewolves in the secret chamber as a test of the new recruits.

Romag expressed his approval that the party had not fallen for the angelic trap, recognizing their wisdom and caution. He handed them badges of the Earth Temple, declaring they had proven their worth and would have a place among his followers. That night, the party rested in the bunk rooms with the other guards, where Calmer boldly claimed to be a former cleric of Saint Cuthbert who had converted to the Earth Temple’s cause. His declaration swayed the other guards, who saw the conversion of a holy man as proof of the Earth Temple’s righteousness.

The next morning, Lieutenant Krell appeared with a mission from Romag. The Fire Temple had seized a portion of their level, and the party was to raid them, killing as many as they could without getting themselves killed. He explained that Earth Temple gnolls occupied one area while Fire Temple gnolls held another, and the rivals periodically clashed in brutal combat. The party, having shown more subtlety than the typical gnoll, was tasked with tipping the balance in the Earth Temple’s favor.

The party moved stealthily to the designated location and entered a chamber that had once been a plush guest chamber but was now dirty and cluttered. They surprised the creature, making quick work of the creature. but as they examined its body, a terrible realization dawned on them—the ogre was a member of the Earth Temple, not the Fire Temple. Someone had set them up, and the most likely culprit was Lieutenant Krell himself.

Furious at the betrayal, the party returned to Romag to explain what had happened. When they arrived, Lieutenant Krell was conspicuously absent. Romag listened to their account, and his rage became palpable as he declared he had known the lieutenant was a spy and had kept him under watch. With fury burning in his eyes, Romag commanded the party to bring the traitor to him alive so he could sacrifice him in the Temple of Earth as a warning to all who would dare betray them. The party now faced a new mission: hunting down a cunning spy who had nearly gotten them killed, knowing that the treacherous politics of the Temple of Elemental Evil made trust a dangerous commodity.
