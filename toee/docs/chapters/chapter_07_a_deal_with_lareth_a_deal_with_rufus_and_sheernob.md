# Chapter 07 A deal with Lareth, a deal with Rufus, and Sheernob

### Planting 20, 579 Midday

#### Zephyr

As Zephyr watches Lareth intently, the leader of the moathouse turns to his sergeant, “I don’t think they need your help. They are professionals, after all.” 

Zephyr wonders if Lareth has seen through their disguise. 

Lareth meets Zephyr’s gaze, “I wouldn’t tell anyone about our deal.” 

*This man is calculating something, but I do not know what it is.*

A few minutes later, Zephyr stands at the campsite where the very untrustworthy Duke sits. 

In his gruff voice, he shouts, “You’re alive?” 

It’s Sequoia who shouts what Zephyr is thinking, “Sorry.” 

“Any evidence of the cult?” 

#### Sequoia

Sequoia shrugs, “None.” 

The Duke stares at him, intently, “So what were you doing in the moathouse?” 

“Looking for evidence?” Sequoia deadpans. 

*He’s not convinced.* 

“We went into the moat house. Made it to the lower level. We found an abandoned torture chamber with some zombies—an ogre who claimed to work for some all-powerful cult leader. We found weapons. That’s it. We thought there might be some secret passageway but found nothing.” 

*There is no need for him to know we found the secret doors and that the torture chamber is not unused.* 

A few hours later, the group arrives at Hommlet. Sequoia sees Rufus’ folly in the distance and sighs \- “Let’s report to the local chief of police.” 

Inside the tower, past the guard, the halfling rogue is once again in the presence of the two agents of Verbobonc. Burne is wearing a leisure suit, and Rufus is wearing a white blouse, green velvet pantaloons, and a red sash. They are both sitting around the main table, obviously full.

Rufus begins, “So any evidence of the cult?” 

“They were operating as members of the fire temple.” 

“Members of the cult?” 

“Yes.” 

“Are you sure they aren’t poseurs?” 

Sequoia is surprised, “Who would pretend to be members of the temple of elemental evil?”

“Bandits who would like to destabilize the government of Verbobonc. Agents of chaos,” mutters Captain Burne. 

*I think someone is trying to cover their ass.*

“My dear Sequoia, the events of the Temple of Elemental Evil destabilized the entire region. As a result, the Viscounty has expanded its military presence in the area. Some would like to pretend that the Viscounty has not stabilized matters. To do so, they would like to pretend that the agents of chaos have returned. If they were to return without our knowing, it would encourage those who see the Viscount as an imperialist instead of an agent of progress and safety to reject us. I don’t doubt that you found evidence of cultists. But whether they are cultists of a reanimated temple or agents of some other power or government is unknown.” 

*Definitely trying to cover his ass.* 

Burne sighs, “Did you find the leader?” 

“Yes.” 

Rufus is excited, “Did you kill him?” 

Sequoia pauses. It's *probably not the correct answer to say we didn’t. But if we didn’t, why? I know—these guys are convinced this is just some agent of some other government. I’ll pretend that I* *agree. “*As you said, Burne, we don’t know who this leader worked for. His name is Lareth. He thinks we work for him. He seemed excited to use us as an agent to eliminate some of his rivals. I thought that would be a good plan. We eliminate some other, as you say, poseurs and learn more.” 

Burne looks at Sequoia and says, “Brilliant. I admire your deviousness. I imagine it was the masked one who devised this plan. Destroying Lareth’s enemies will weaken our enemies as well. Excellent\! What was this mission that he sent you on?” 

“Someone stole some chest, and he wants us to get it back. He claims it’s from a rival group of cultists.” 

Rufus stops, “A chest? That is fascinating.” There is a pause. “The chest must be the gift. Every so often, the Viscounty has an art festival, and the purpose of the art festival is to craft some piece of art as a gift to the Kingdom of Celene. The purpose is to convince the Kingdom of Celene to begin diplomatic negotiations. The art is taken to the border and left there. ” 

#### Zinnia 

The Masked One stares at Rufus and remembers something he had been told. The Viscounty creates this art, which is then dropped near the borders. The elves apparently will destroy any art they think is hideous, whereas any art they believe has potential they will leave behind. 

Rufus continues, “Although we don’t know, we believe the Kingdom has accepted the gift if the artwork is gone in a week. If it is still there, the art is believed to have been rejected. We hope the elves will see our efforts over time and choose to meet with a delegation. This has been going on for some time, and now the competition is viewed as an opportunity for the rich, powerful, and talented to showcase their talent.”.  		 

Zinnia knows that the Kingdom of Celene believes the right place for any non-elf is outside the Kingdom of Celene. 

*These idiots probably select art that elves find most awful because they misunderstand entirely why the art that goes missing.* 

Rufus stares at him. Zinnia stares back. 

“A few months ago, the annual competition occurred, and a chest was selected. There was a mission to take the chest to Celene. The mission was attacked, and the chest went missing.” 

Burne picks up a fork, stares at it, points it at Zinnia, and says, “If you were to find the chest and the folks who stole it, that would be of great assistance. The official position, of course, is that the Elves accepted the Chest. But the chest reappearing would embarrass the Viscount.” 

### Planting 21, 579 Morning

#### Zephyr  

The journey to Sheernobb, where the bandits were last seen, is uneventful. 

As he approaches the verdant hills of Sheernobb, the landscape changes, giving way to a charming village nestled within the lush greenery of the Kron Hills. The road narrows and winds through the dense forest, guiding him toward the heart of the gnome settlement. The air is filled with the earthy scent of the forest, mingled with the faint aroma of roasting potatoes and fresh ale.

Just as Zephyr begins to take in the quaint beauty of the village, he is halted by a group of gnome guards. They are small in stature but carry an air of authority, their eyes sharp and watchful.

“What business do you have in Sheernobb?” one of the guards demands, stepping forward. His tone is not hostile, but it carries the weight of someone who has asked this question many times.

Zephyr explains that it’s a bit of tourism \- “We were here a few weeks ago to return a dragon? You might recall. And we helped a gnomish adventurer, Knarfallan. We are also adventurers and wondered if there would be jobs we could do.” 

The guard looks at Zephyr quizzically. The gnome then notices a ring on his pinky that Knarfallan gave him. 

Once that ring is noticed, the guard’s demeanor changes instantly and is much friendlier. 

“Very well, you may enter,” he says, gesturing with a gloved hand towards the village. “But be warned, we keep a close eye on all newcomers, even friends of Knarfallan. For accommodations, we recommend the Honey Haven Tavern. It’s suited for humans and will be a comfortable place for you to stay.”

The guard looks at the Tiefling more carefully, “Or whatever you are.” 

*This again.* 

“I am a human. A victim of a curse that I have been unable to rid myself of.” 

The guard seems comfortable with the answer. 

“If you have any questions or need to speak with someone in charge, you can find Headgnome Glennddarc at the Sheernobb Moothall. It’s the largest mound in the village; you can’t miss it.”  
With that, the guards step aside, allowing Zephyr to continue his journey into Sheernobb. As he proceeds, the bustling activity of the village begins to unfold before him. Tiny burrow homes dot the hillsides, their doors intricately carved and inviting. Gnomes go about their daily routines, casting curious glances his way.

#### Sequoia  

“Let’s go to the tavern.” 

Green awnings and flowering ivy tendrils adorn the outside of the squat little inn, and warm laughter burbles out through an open window. The brightly colored sign above the doorway proudly proclaims it to be The Honey Haven Inn and depicts a hand-painted bee perched on the edge of a flower. The wood panels of the exterior are weather-worn but clean—an indication of the love and care with which this place is maintained.

The gnomes of this settlement have created an inn that is perfect for people of diminutive sensibilities, though it is pretty cramped for larger humanoids. 

*Good thing I am small.* 

The benches, tables, desks, and bar have all been shaped out of Elm wood, with horsehair cushions fastened to the seat—the smell of roasting potatoes and fresh ale wafts toward Sequoia when he pushes through the door. Logs crackle in the oversized fireplace, lighting up the room with several dozen small glass globes hanging from chains, and close examination reveals that the light comes from numerous fireflies somehow coaxed within, as there are holes from which they could escape if they had a mind to do so. Bright woven tapestries hang along the walls and add a touch of color to the wooden interior. The barkeep stands behind a counter to the left of the entrance, and the doorway to the kitchen opens behind them. A pair of double doors lead to the patio outback. Several dour gnomes sit here and there, drinking unknown concoctions from steaming mugs.

A little figure at the low bar has her back turned, humming an unfamiliar tune. She shakes her hips to the beat of her music, each twitch jingling a large ring of keys on her belt. The gnome turns and gives Sequoia a surprised smile. She pulls a hedgehog out of the mug she’s holding and sets the critter down, sending it on its way with a little pat.

“Oh aye yip\! Travelers\! Will you be needing cool refreshing drinks?\! A warm, cozy bed to rest your weary bones for the night?\!

It’s Zephyr who answers first, “Both.”

“A private room costs eight silver; a shared bunk costs five copper.” 

Sequoia rolls his eyes when Zephyr explains that he will get a private room, but the rest of his crew will just get a bunk.

A few minutes later, they are sitting at a table, and the owner,  Mari Clee, gives them a menu, “So what will it be?” 

Sequoia is very annoyed when Zephyr explains that he will have chicken while his crew must eat the cheapest. 

Sequoia notices that Mari seems taken aback. He guesses her sense of hospitality is offended. But she smiles, and then he sees a mischievous look in her eyes as she hands the other menus to the group.

Sitting next to Sequoia, Calmer says, “I will have the moonbeam pie.” 

Zephyr whistles at the expense. 

But Mari has a plan, “I assume the leader, here, will pay for everything?” 

Sequoia chokes down a laugh but laughs out loud when Zinnia mutters, “He is, after all, the boss.” 

“And what will you drink.” 

Zephyr thunders, “We do not need to drink.”

And Zinnia ignores him, “I’ll have some of the fine Honey Meed.” 

Sequoia admires the spunk of Mari, who just ignores Zephyr and says, “An excellent choice. We are very famous for our meed. Good choice and I am sure your leader will be delighted to pay.” 

Another gnome walks up to their table as they sit down to eat. 

He is a well-traveled gnome and looks the part of the bard. 

“My name is Gurdy. And I would be happy to play a song for you. If you had some coin to spare.” 

#### Zephyr 

Zephyr stares at the bard and realizes he is not leaving this inn without paying through the nose. “I like music, " he says, tossing some coin.

The bard plays a song, and when he is done, he grabs a chair and sits at the table. 

“What are you here for?” 

“Looking for problems that need solving with the pointy end of a sword.” 

“Adventurers? Good thing you met Gurdy.” He pauses, “But I am a bit parched. Mari, can you fill it up?” 

Mari takes some more coin from Zephyr, who sighs, and Gurdy explains \- “We have had bandit attacks forever. But something is different now. They are more organized. Everyone wants to protect their town and farms, and no one wants to find the bandits and deal with them. But you folks seem to want to do just that.” 

Gurdy drinks a swig and then smiles, “I would go to the moot hall and see what I could learn there. And if you need a Bard to inspire you and take those bandits down, I could be convinced.” 

Zephyr notices how Calmer turns pale and then tries not to laugh when the cleric shouts, “I am sorry, there is no room in this quartet. You are not welcome.

Zephyr can’t resist, “I have never worked with a bard.” 

As Gurdy gets up to leave, Calmer gets in Zephyr’s face, “I thought I was part of the team\!” 

*Oh god, this again.* 

“You are part of the team, but we need a backup plan, and he’s part of the backup team.” 

The argument is getting heated when the other inn owner approaches and says in a squeaky voice, “Gurdy says you are adventurers?”

Before Zephyr can answer, he says, “Someone stole some barrels of my mead. Some tracks led to the forest. Find out what happened, and you get free lodging at the Honey Haven Tavern for a week and a small keg of my enchanted meed.” 

Zephyr ponders his answer carefully, but Sequoia has already accepted the quest. 

*Great, now everyone in this town will want us to run some fetch quest.* 

On cue, the cook, a half-orc named Quinna Pool, wearing an apron and smelling of the kitchen fire, approaches. “So you are adventurers. And you have pointy sticks, and you are for hire?” Zephyr wants to say no, but Sequoia says yes. 

Quinna smiles and says, “It turns out that I want to prepare a celebratory meal for the Harvest Festival. I need the meat of a rare gnarled boar that roams the gnarly forest. It's notoriously elusive and dangerous, and nobody has gotten it for me.” 

Seeing that even Sequoia wants to hear the reward, “I’ll make you a special meal that will bless you and make you stronger than ever\!” 

Zephyr nods. 

Quinna squeals in delight, “ I hope to see you soon and help our small town have its festival. It will be so wonderful. It'll be so tasty, and I promise my meal will be fantastic, the likes of which you have never had.” 

And just as Zephyr is about to get up, another gnome walks in. It’s someone who looks like the captain of the guard. 

“The name is Captain Gnonsbim. I was told you are adventurers.” He doesn’t wait for an answer. “There are some bandits that are causing a mess and affecting trade. If I send a patrol to find them, I risk exposing the village. I don’t have the gnome power necessary to do what needs to be done. And that’s where you come in. Find me this bandit. And I know you want to be paid, so here’s the deal \-  I’ll give you 25 for the camp location, 10 for every dead bandit, and 25 for the leader.” 

Zephyr nods. 

“Great. And one more thing, when you bring back the evidence, you need to let me give a speech, nod, and agree that the success was because of my leadership. There’s an election coming up. If you do all that, I suppose I could get you a custom-forged bit of magical weaponry.”

Zephyr rolls his eyes, “Okay.” 

The captain shouts, “Mari, give them some more mead. I’ll pay for it..” He then turns to Zephyr and says, “You should talk to Pasmarie Clee. She’s been bugging me about this.” 

And with that, the captain goes. 

Calmer is about to drink another glass full of mead, but Zephyr notices that the cleric is already a bit drunk and shouts, “That’s enough.” Calmer ignores him and drinks it anyway.
