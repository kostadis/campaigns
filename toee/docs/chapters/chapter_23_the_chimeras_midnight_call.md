# Chapter 23 The Chimera's Midnight Call

Date: Mar 11th, 2026

## Summary

The party's attempt at a long rest was shattered in the dead of night when Zinnia's sharp senses caught the unmistakable sound of dragging chains and a haunting, animalistic chorus echoing from beyond their southern door — the guttural roar of a lion twisted together with the frantic, deranged bleating of a giant goat. He roused his companions just in time, for moments later the door exploded inward as a monstrous Chimera came crashing through, its three heads snarling with demonic fury. Behind it, a Fire Temple warlock gripped the beast's chain and bellowed a declaration of war, proclaiming that Alrrem, the all-consuming, had sent his champion to break their bones and leave nothing but meat for the earth. The creature was a horrifying amalgamation — the hindquarters of a great goat, the powerful forequarters of a lion, and the leathery wings of a dragon, each head driven by its own savage instinct to hunt, hoard, and destroy.

The battle that followed was desperate and brutal. Sequioa charged the beast head-on but was quickly overwhelmed, battered into unconsciousness by the combined fury of the Chimera's attacks and the warlock's crackling magical blasts. Calmer called upon divine power to mend Sequioa's wounds and bring him back to his feet, even as the warlock continued to goad the beast forward. It was Zephyr who turned the tide, slipping past the rampaging monster and ripping the control chain clean from the warlock's hands, leaving the man screaming in terror at what he had unleashed. The Chimera, now free of its handler, thrashed wildly — its chain whipping through the air as the warlock scrambled desperately to reclaim it.

With the beast unbound, chaos reigned. The Chimera unleashed a devastating breath of fire upon both the warlock and a nearby bugbear who had tried to intervene, scorching them badly. Zinnia darted in to deliver a series of precise strikes before retreating to safety, while Zephyr managed to snatch up the fallen chain once more in a bold attempt to dominate the creature. The warlock, badly burned and barely standing, screamed that they were all mad — but Zinnia silenced him permanently with a flurry of devastating blows, leaving the Fire Temple's champion dead on the stone floor. The Chimera, however, was far from finished, and it turned its full, unhinged fury upon those who remained.

Zephyr bore the brunt of the beast's wrath, suffering a savage onslaught of bites and claws that left the rogue crumpled and unconscious. Calmer, muttering something about having only walking band-aids to offer, called upon his divine gifts once more to pull Zephyr back from the edge of death. Sequioa, having recovered and hidden himself behind a chest, loosed a precise shot that left the Chimera teetering on the very brink of collapse — but the enraged beast charged him before he could finish it, knocking him unconscious with a series of brutal strikes. It was Calmer who ended it, invoking the tolling resonance of necrotic power that rang out like a funeral bell, dropping the Chimera dead upon the cold stone floor.

In the quiet aftermath, the party searched the warlock's charred remains and uncovered a trove of revealing items. A wax-sealed ledger bore the crest of a crimson ship — a bill of sale from Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader, detailing the purchase of the Chimera paid in full by the Fire Temple. More damning still, the name Vesrek  appeared in the document — an ally of Belsornig, the prophet of the Water Temple — suggesting the Water Temple had orchestrated the whole affair as a calculated win-win, either eliminating the party or bankrupting the Fire Temple in the process. The warlock also carried a heavy gold amulet bearing the eight-pointed symbol of the Fire Temple, a potion of fire resistance he had never managed to reach for in the chaos, and a masterwork whip of alchemically treated red leather. The heavy iron collar and chains used to control the beast were stamped with the marks of Nulb's River Pirates, revealing that someone in Nulb had been paid to deliver the monster to the temple.

The party brought their findings to Romag, who received the news of the chimera attack with thunderous fury, declaring that the moment to strike against Alrrem had arrived and ordering the party to deal with the Fire Temple's weakened leader. But it was a private meeting with Hartsch, Romag's sub-general, that proved far more illuminating. Hartsch laid out the political landscape of the temples in stark detail — Alrrem's power had been waning since the catastrophic defeat at the Battle of Emridy Meadows a decade prior, and the loss of his werewolves, followed by the ruinous cost of the chimera, had left him nearly broken. The Air Temple under the prophet Kelno was dismissed outright as a farcical remnant, reduced to a handful of rooms and no meaningful power. The Water Temple, however, remained a formidable rival, and Hartsch made clear that its elimination would be the true prize.

It was during this meeting that the party's ambitions crystallized into a bold and dangerous plan. Zinnia, using carefully chosen words and a disarming manner, drew out Hartsch's true feelings about Romag's passive, erosion-like style of leadership — and Hartsch made no effort to hide his belief in the destructive, earthquake-like power of the earth, nor his desire to wield it. When Zinnia pressed further, Hartsch replied with thinly veiled intent: if a position of leadership were to suddenly open itself, he would absolutely consider himself worthy of it. The party recognized the signal for what it was — Hartsch wanted Romag removed, and he wanted them to do it. With a plan taking shape to assassinate Romag, install Hartsch as the Earth Temple's new leader, and ultimately engineer a three-way war between the Earth, Fire, and Water temples to destroy all three factions from within, the party quietly agreed that the time for subtlety was nearly over.

## NPCs

### Fire Temple Warlock

A servant of Alrrem who led a Chimera on a chain to assassinate the party. He used magical blasts and commanded the beast until Zephyr disarmed him of the chain. He was ultimately slain by Zinnia.

### Chimera

A horrific multi-headed monstrosity with the features of a lion, a goat, and a dragon, imported by the Fire Temple at great expense to destroy the party. It went on a mindless rampage after being freed from its chains and was ultimately slain by Calmer's necrotic magic.

### Bugbear

A guard or observer who attempted to intervene in the chaos by pointing a weapon at the Chimera, but was caught in the beast's devastating breath attack.

### Calmer

A cleric and party member who healed fallen companions and ultimately slew the Chimera. His companions grew concerned that he may be genuinely embracing his Earth Temple cover identity rather than simply acting.

### Romag

The leader of the Earth Temple who prefers to lead from behind, viewing himself as a chess king who sends others to fight his battles. He ordered the party to deal with Alrrem after hearing of the chimera attack.

### Hartsch

Romag's sub-general and speaker who provided the party with tactical information and maps. He revealed the political landscape of the temples and subtly signaled his desire for the party to remove Romag from power so he could take command.

### Alrrem

The leader of the Fire Temple, currently weakened after losing his werewolves and his expensive chimera asset. He was originally the leader of the temples in the great battle of Emridy Meadows and has been struggling to hold power since.

### Belsornig

The Prophet and head of the Water Temple, suspected by Hartsch of being a cunning manipulator who arranged the chimera purchase through his ally Vesrek  as a win-win scheme against both the Fire and Earth temples.

### Kelno

The Prophet of the Air Temple, dismissed by Hartsch as a joke with very little remaining power or territory, reduced to a few rooms within the temple.

### Vesrek

An ally of Belsornig, the Water Temple prophet, who is identified in the ledger as the one who arranged the purchase of the chimera for the Fire Temple through Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader.

## Locations

### Party's Quarters

The room where the party attempted to take a long rest, featuring a southern door and a secret door. The Chimera smashed through the door here, leading to a desperate struggle, and the room later served as a private meeting place with Hartsch.

### Romag's Chambers

The command center for the Earth Temple where Romag and Hartsch coordinate their forces. The party reported the chimera attack here and received orders to deal with Alrrem.

## Items

### Chimera Chain

A heavy iron collar and dragging chains used by the Fire Temple warlock to control and goad the Chimera into battle. Zephyr managed to rip the chain from the warlock's hands during the fight. The chains were later found to be stamped with the marks of Nulb's River Pirates, revealing that someone in Nulb was paid to deliver the monster.

### Warlock's Ledger

A document found on the Fire Temple warlock, containing a heavy wax-sealed receipt bearing the crest of a crimson ship. It is a bill of sale from Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader detailing the purchase of the chimera, paid in full by the Fire Temple. It also revealed a connection to Vesrek , an ally of the Water Temple's prophet Belsornig.

### Fire Temple Amulet

A heavy gold amulet and chain depicting the eight-pointed symbol of the Fire Temple, found on the warlock. Its craftsmanship and gold weight make it worth 100 gold pieces.

### Potion of Fire Resistance

A magical elixir found on the warlock, intended to protect the user from heat and flames. The warlock had it on his person but panicked and never reached for it during the chaos of the chimera's rampage.

### Masterwork Whip

A high-quality whip woven with alchemically treated red leather, found among the warlock's belongings. While not magical, it is of fine craftsmanship, features a ten-foot reach, and can slow an enemy's movement.

## Spells

### Eldritch Blast

Beams of crackling energy fired by the Fire Temple warlock at Sequioa, dealing significant damage early in the fight.

### Healing Word

A quick divine incantation used by Calmer on two separate occasions during the battle — first to restore Sequioa to consciousness, and later to bring Zephyr back from the brink of death.

### Toll the Dead

A necrotic spell cast by Calmer that manifests as the sound of a melancholy tolling bell. It was used multiple times during the fight and ultimately delivered the final, fatal blow to the wounded Chimera.
