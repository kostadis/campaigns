# Chapter 06 Repast for an Ogre

## Date: Planting 18, 579 

### Calmer

I will boldly lead the group down into the lower level of the moathouse. 

There is no doubt that some evil powers are here. And I will defeat them\! 

But first, a brief rest. It’s essential to rest when facing monsters. Back in Verbobonc, one of the trainers argued that rest, not activity, builds strength. Rest allows the muscles to learn the lessons of what has happened. 

The Duke is, predictably, less than friendly. He is a bit full of himself and mocks me as I stretch. 

It’s essential to engage the muscles after a workout.

And now we are standing at the threshold of the lower levels of the moathouse. A staircase is next to the great hall, where we faced brigands who may or may not have been aligned with the Temple of Elemental Evil. 

I insist that I must lead. My experience and those innate attributes of wisdom and perception make me the right person to be in front. 

Zephyr walks with me. Zinia and Sequoia are behind.

And it’s a good thing I was in front because I noticed, just in time, the blob of green slime hanging over the arch at the bottom of the stairwell. 

### Zephyr

Calmer brags about how he can see everything. And then a blob of green slime lands on him. And he starts squealing. 

Calmer, he should be Louder.

Using some wood I happened to be carrying on me, I removed the slime from his armor. Unfortunately, the slime ate through it. 

Calmer is now whimpering at the fact that his beautiful, shiny armor has been destroyed. 

“Grow up,” I tell him. And then we walk through the archway. 

Looking around, this place seems as abandoned as the upper layers. There are a few torn sacks, broken barrels, and shattered weapons racks. This was once a storage place and armory for the fortress. 

And there’s no treasure.

All I can see are heaps of worthless rubbles and broken containers at the western end of the room. 

“Junk,” I whisper. 

Calmer has finally calmed down. 

### Sequoia 

If the west has nothing but junk, to the east, we must go. 

And there is a door.

Beyond the door is a littered storeroom. 

That’s interesting. The door hinges are greased. For a place that should be abandoned, that’s odd. 

I then overhear a bang. I pull my swords, ready to strike, and realize it’s the cleric. 

I want to kill him. If there is anything past the door to the east, it heard us. 

“I am Lubash\!”

Standing at the now-open eastern door is an ogre. 

“You are not members of the temple\! You are food\!” 

And it swings it’s club. 

And boy do Ogre clubs hurt. 

### Zinnia

This amateur-hour infiltration of the temple has got to stop. We have a cleric who can’t keep his feet on the ground, and instead of using some kind of disguise, we just march around like the kind of intruders anyone who sees here will attack first and speak with the dead later. 

But first, we need to kill this Ogre.   
   
My fists pummel the creature. Zephyr and Squoia’s blades cut him to size, but not before Calmer almost dies again. 

With the dead carcass lying in the doorway, I enter carefully into the room beyond. 

That’s interesting. 

This place looks lived in. And there in the corner is where the Ogre lived. 

I look inside the Ogre’s nest and discover a magical cloak of elvenkind. 

“Now that’s even more interesting. Who is the elf that lost this?” 

An Ogre needs a lot of food. And so someone has been providing it with food. 

And why is that door to the south so heavily barred? 

Calmer, who has recovered from being attacked by an ogre, and I clear the door. 

I carefully open it with Zephyr and Sequoia by my side. We tell Calmer to stand back. I would tell him to stand all-the-way back to Hommlet, but we need a healer. 

“Who are you?” says a tired and frightened voice. 

 “Who are you?” I ask. 

The mystery of the Ogre’s food is unraveled. And it’s a pretty grisly story. 

Two of the prisoners are merchants. They are wearing the traveling clothes of the rich, now covered in dirt, blood, and excrement. 

One of them, wearing a strange purple robe, says, “My name is Derrick, and this is Florian. We’re originally from Norwell. Brigands captured us along the road to Hommlet. Those captured alive were brought here as a repast for the Ogre. There were six of us. Every day, the Ogre would come in and take one of us. First, there was Martin, Damien, Eltograph the Elf, and…” 

Damn it, he’s crying. 

“I can help?” shouts Calmer. And I think, the last time he helped, he started berating someone about their life choices. 

“No, I think I can handle this.” 

The merchants make it clear they have no other helpful information but promise a reward if brought back to Hommlet. 

As for the other prisoner, he’s way more interesting. He’s a gnome and a spy. 

“My name is Jarran. I was sent out here by the merchants of Lotrimil to investigate the brigands attacking this area. I didn’t expect to get caught and put in this pantry room. I have a mission. And I need to get back to it. Although, if you need another sword and promise to keep exploring, I could stick around.” 

I don’t trust other spies. 

“No, I don’t think that’s necessary. Let’s get you all outside.” 

### The Duke

They’re still alive. And they are bringing a bunch of people. This is interesting. And these people look like they were trapped in the moathouse. That’s very interesting. 

“Don’t you worry, team. I’ll make sure to guard them. When you come back, for good, we can all take them back to Hommlet.”

### Sequoia

“Let’s check that hallway to the south we ignored last time, guys.”

The hallway is 50 feet long and runs north-south. Three pillars are 20 feet apart, and a passageway leads east. 

Five doors are on the western wall. They look like they haven’t been opened in a long time. Something like prison doors designed to keep people in. Well, those doors are so rotten that they won’t keep anyone in anymore. 

Okay, let’s be careful here. 

“Zombies\!” 

Everyone says they are saying “Brains\!” but that’s not true. They aren’t saying anything. But what happens is the sound that escapes from their decaying vocal cords sounds like “nsazsa,” and everyone thinks they are saying Brains\!” 

I wonder if Calmer has any power over the undead\! And yes, he does\! 

They are turned. We have about a minute to check out what’s here. 

And that’s when I hear it.

“Brains\!”

Remember those doors that won’t keep anyone inside? Well, they aren’t keeping those zombies inside the cell. 

Okay, we can handle this. Two zombies shouldn’t be a problem. 

Except, what exactly is in the other cells?

I can worry about that later. 

“Brains\!”

There were more zombies in that cell.

That’s okay. We can handle it. 

And then I hear the sound again. 

“Brains\!” 

MORE ZOMBIES? 

That’s when Zephyr shouts, or is it Zinia? Honestly, I can’t tell. 

“The other three doors also have zombies\!” 

“Time to get out of here,” I shout. Zephyr’s like, “We can take them\!” 

I look at him and think, the next time, there will be 13 zombies to kill…

But he’s not that foolish. And runs for the archway and the stairs that go up. 

A few minutes later, we’re standing in front of the Duke, who is giving out food and talking to the merchants. The gnome is still sitting there and hands us a ring.
