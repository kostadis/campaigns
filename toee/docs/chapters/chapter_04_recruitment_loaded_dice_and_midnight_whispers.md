# Chapter 04 Recruitment, Loaded Dice, and Midnight Whispers

### Date: Planting 16, 579 

The stone's strange markings suggest elemental evil, and after a confession from one of the bandits, the group decides to return to Hommlett. 

A few hours later, the heroes can see “The Tower” in the distance and wonder where to go with their collected information. Everyone in town wants to be told first, 

Calmer insists, “We must go to the Prefect and inform him of what has happened\!” 

Sequoia, Zinia, and Zephyr agree. Of all the factions, at least this one had skin in the game. 

As they trudge through the village, the locals notice them and quietly discuss why the adventurers are back. 

Once inside the temple of St. Cuthbert, Calmer excitedly explains to Prefect Terjon the proof that he and his companions have found.

The Prefect examines the stone that Calmer has produced, rubs it between his fingers, weighs it, and finally says in disgust, “You bring me a painted rock. You had a prisoner I could have questioned; now, all I have is your word. Go find me some actionable information, not this trinket and your words.” 

Calmer is shocked.

Zinia asks for the rock, but Terjon surprisingly says, “I would like to keep this worthless rock.” 

Zephyr is intrigued by Terjon wanting to hold onto a worthless rock. He decides that he doesn’t wish Terjon to keep it. He smiles sweetly, “If this rock is so worthless, why keep it?” 

Terjon winces as if something painful has moved in his stomach, and then he tosses the rock to Zephyr. Terjon mutters as it flies through the air, “You’re right. It is worthless.” 

Zephyr, the stone in his fist, exits the temple. As his eyes adjust to the sunlight, he notices two militiamen. They are local militia members and have orders to bring the four to Burne and Refus, who are currently drinking at the Inn of the Welcome Wench. 

The tiefling smiles, and soon, the entire group follows the militiamen. 

At the Inn, in the main hall, Burne and Refus are sitting at the table, drinking. 

For some reason, Calmer bows out and says he must return to the Temple to complete some prayers. Before he leaves, he asks, “Tomorrow at the same time?”   
The group sits at the table with the two men. Once comfortable, Rufus asks, “What did you find?”

Zephyr hands over the rock.

Two scrutinize the rock, and finally, Burne mutters, “This is it?” 

Zephyr is not surprised and shares that the stone was found on the body of a bandit. 

Burne and Rufus’ reaction is a surprise. They suddenly become thoughtful, and Rufus whispers, “We may have a job for you.  I was recently examining the blueprints for the keep and found them missing. My architect had no idea where they went. When I rechecked them later, they had returned, but there were traces of charcoal dust on them. I suspect that someone has copied our blueprints. I have moved the plans into a more private part of the tower, but I guess it's a saboteur.  Until you brought me this rock, I was not convinced this was worth worrying about. But now, now I am concerned. The thief must have worked with the bandits. I am convinced it is one of the workers in the encampments. You must go and find the thief.” 

Burne stretches and says, “And keep this quiet. We don’t want to tell anyone.” 

Zephyr thinks, “Other than half the village.” And then he says, “Of course.” 

The two men then thank Zephyr and leave with their guards. 

As Zephyr, Zinia, and Sequoia enjoy their drink, Ostler, the innkeeper, sits at the table \- “I see Burne and Rufus like you.” 

“Not sure I would call it like.” 

“I also noticed that you didn’t talk to the Druid. Does the Druid not like you?” 

“Yes, he does,” insists Zephyr. 

“I’ll believe it when he says so, which will happen after you talk to him about whatever you talked to Burne, Rufus, and Terjon about.” 

Zinia gets the point and gets up, heading to the door. Ostler grabs him by the arm, “So that you know; adventurers have a minimum three-night stay at the Inn. And as a matter of policy, any goods left behind are sold after three days.” 

Like all the other town leaders, he is disappointed when Jaroo is presented with the stone and confession.

“You must go deeper into the moathouse and find more evidence,” he admonishes. 

Zephyr sighs and heads out of the grove. As he leaves, Jaroo shouts, “Who else have you spoken to?” 

Zinia realizes this town has no secrets, and he tells the truth. 

Jaroo is pleased and offers them the blessing of Beory and a healing potion. 

“We should go talk to the village elder,” mutters Zinia. As an expert on local politics, he understands the importance of getting local leaders on board. 

![][image3]

And so Zinia leads them to a place with an eight-foot-high stone wall and a heavy gate. It is the residence of some well-to-do folk and easily defensible in times of trouble.

They open the gate and step inside. Looking around, Zinia quickly discovers that the compound has five families: the village elder and his wife, the four sons and their wives. A gaggle of children of different ages is running around. 

He also notes that there are some servants here as well.

For his part, Sequoia notices how two sons carefully groom the horses. He thinks that they must be very proud of their possessions. 

Just then, one of the wives approaches the trio \- “The elder is waiting for you.” 

Past the central doorway, there is a large seating area with several comfortable chairs, a divan, several colorful rugs, and a small table. Standing in the middle of the room is an older man with white hair who looks reasonably fit and whose eyes show uncommon wisdom. 

“My name is Kenter, although folks call me Elder Kenter. Please sit.” 

As the trio sits, one of his daughters brings out some glasses and a carafe of wine and pours. 

When the Elder gets his glass, he coughs and toasts, “The Blessings of Beory.” 

After taking a sip, he turns to the trio and says in a grandfatherly voice, “It is good that you came here. My second son, Hawthorne, saw you come from the moathouse. He followed you and saw you visited Jaroo, Terjon, Burne, and Refus.” 

Sequoia is tempted to protest, but Kenter continues, “Don’t worry, whenever anyone does something out of the ordinary, Hawthorne’s natural curiosity is to learn. And it’s good that he did. The people you spoke to are on the council. And, unfortunately, they chose not to have you present to the council, where all could learn simultaneously.” 

There is a slight pause, and he continues: “The council members are Jaroo, Rufus, Burne, and Ostler, whom you have already met, as well as Captain Zeb Fletcher, father of Elmo and Mytch the Miller.” 

The trio mutely stares, so he finishes, " The bandit activity surge is alarming. It's not just an economic issue; it's a threat to the safety and stability of our village. We must address it with utmost urgency. Burne and Rufus have shown remarkable leadership and strategic acumen. Their efforts in organizing defenses and training our militia have been crucial in our response to the bandit threat. Our unified leadership is our greatest strength. By working together, Burne, Rufus, Captain Fletcher, and I can coordinate our efforts and ensure a comprehensive response to the bandit menace.” 

Sequoia drinks. 

The Elder concludes, “Therefore, going forward, I expect you all to come here first.” 

They all nod and leave politely after sampling some of the treats that Kenter’s wife, Valsenora, brings to the table. 

“Well, that was interesting,” mutters Zinia. 

From there, the trio heads to the Inn of the Welcome Wench, where Ostler, obviously informed of the visit to Jaroo and the blessing, greets the trio at the door.   
From there, he ushers them into the kitchen, explaining that his job offer is to prove that Furnoc is a crook who steals from the Inn’s guests. 

To sweeten the pot, he offers free rooms for three nights. 

Zephyr smiles, “No problem.” 

Unfortunately, it is a problem, as his attempt at pick-pocketing Furnok the Fabulous fails. Seeing his opportunity lost, he creates a distraction for Sequoia or Zenia to try their luck. So he falls on the table that Furnok is sitting on, causing the table to tip over. 

Zinia moves quickly and grabs the dice. As he picks them up, he realizes they are loaded—“The proof I needed\!” 

Ostler confronts Furnok, who rejects the accusations. Things start to escalate when a burly, muscular man sitting at another table shouts, “Get out of here, Furnok, or I’ll have to call the Captain, and he won’t be as kindly as Ostler is.” 

Sequoia realizes that this is Elmo. He also smells the stench of booze on him: “A drunk.” 

And yet, Sequoia and the rest of the trio find themselves sitting around Elmo’s table. They quickly learn that Elmo is an adventurer and a ranger by profession. 

Zephyr smiles, “There is a monster in the Gnarley forest, and we will need someone to help us find it.” 

Elmo mutters, “I am your person\!” 

While this witty dialogue is going on with Elmo, Zinia carefully listens to the conversations and talks amongst the patrons. Overhearing one pair, he learns  that a farmer's daughter, Lysia, is itching to explore the ruined boathouse, but she needs some equipment and a few competent companions.” The person hearing the tale turns to his companion and replies, “ I hope she can swing a sword better than she can plow a field.” 

From another person, he hears, “ There is a secret passage below the inn that leads to an extensive set of four lost chambers of a long-dead wizard.” 

Zephyr is tempted to sneak around and see what’s happening inside the kitchen until Sequoia suggests they talk to Spugnoir. 

“You must be joking\! The only thing under this inn is stinky food\!” Spugnoir pauses. “But I know the location of a lost wizard laboratory under Emiry Meadows. I am offering to split the treasures as long as I get the first crack at any arcane scrolls we find.” 

The trio stares at him, confused. 

“I would like to go adventuring with you and complete your quartet. If you would have me?” 

Sequoia laughs, “Somehow, you are very familiar.” He then pauses, “Sure.” 

“Tomorrow then, as it is quite late\!” 

After dinner, the trio discusses their plans for the next day when a young man walks up to them and whispers, “Rumor has it that you are the right people to go to if there is a problem.” 

“Yes?” says Sequoia

“My name’s Mickey,” the boy says. The Trio stares at him, confused; the boy realizes they don’t know him and says, “I am the son of Virgil Tamen, the Braumeister.” Still, there is silence. The boy then plunges ahead, “ I've seen a couple of cloaked people lurking behind the brewery at around midnight.

Yeah, I, I'd like, could, could you guys look into it? 'cause I'm worried, I mean, I, I, you know, I, the rumors around the village is going that the temple isn't as dead as everybody thought. And so maybe, those are, uh, spies.” 

The Trio stares at each other and then wordlessly agrees to look into this potential threat. 

Mickey smiles and leaves, relieved. 

Towards midnight, Zephyr, Zinia, and Sequoia climb outside their room window and drop to the ground. The village is eerily quiet, with none of the noise of the day. In particular, Sequoia notices how much noise the castle construction makes. 

The village has no lights, making the journey to the brewery much easier. Zinia and Sequoia take the position to watch anyone arriving or leaving, and Zephyr plans to sneak up on the spies. 

At around midnight, Mickey’s spies show up. Zephyr quickly advances on them, his sword in his right hand, and puts the blade to the throat of the smaller of the two—the spy shrieks. 

Before he can do anything, the other spy starts to cry. 

Zinia and Sequoia come rushing out of their hiding spots; weapons bared and somewhat confused. 

Zephyr uses all his tiefling fierceness to force the two squealing teenagers to be quiet. And then he asks, “What’s going on?”   
The girl introduces herself as Jira, and the boy as Marek. They explain that Jakub is a member of the old faith and won’t let Jira be with Marek, whose father is Tomlyn, the tailor and a member of the Church of St. Cuthbert. 

Zinia snarls and tells them to go away. 

As they leave, Jira pleads with Zinia to help them and talk to their parents \- “You’re heroes, and they’ll listen\!” 

Sequoia struggles to sleep as he thinks about Zephyr looking confused and the two teenagers squealing. 

### Date: Planting 17, 579 

The following day, the group decides to return the silver dragon to its home. The dragon Airgid has been straining the budget over the last few days, and the group has increasingly been worried that it might decide to feed on some horses.

The dragon explains that its home is near the Lotrimil mountains. Ostler explains that that’s to the west through the Kron Hills. He also tells them that it’s a pretty safe journey. 

“The fastest way is to head south and turn north into the Greenway Valley. A reasonably well-traveled road passes small gnomish mining towns.” 

The journey takes them past the moat house, and while the place calls to them for a moment, they have another job right now. 

A little before they arrive at Tulvar, after a day of hard traveling, the dragon suddenly shouts, “HOME\!” 

Airgid takes off and starts to fly west. As she leaves, she shouts, “Don’t worry, my mommy will reward you\! I KNOW SHE WILL\! THANK YOU\!” 

Session 5

### Date: Planting 18, 579 

The heroes are in Hommlet at the Inn of the Welcome Wench, and Spugnoir and Calmer stare at the heroes. Both had expected to go on an adventure the previous day and discovered that the heroes had left without them. 

Zephyr looks at them and turns to Spugnoir, “It’s Calmer. We need a healer for the Moathouse.” 

Spugnoir has that crushed look of someone who is too used to not being picked for the team. He smiles, “Very well.” 

Outside of the Inn is the Duke. 

“So we heading out?” 

A little bit later, they are once again at the moathouse. 

The Duke sets up camp.

“I hope I see you again.” 

Calmer gulps.

Inside the courtyard, Calmer asks, “What do we want to check out?” 

Zinia points to the tower that they ignored last time.

The two rogues peer inside. The upper portion of the tower has collapsed, and the interior is dark. Looking at the floor, Sequoia and Zephyr notice a faint gleam of coins. 

As they reach for the coin, a giant wolf spider attempts to turn them into its next meal. But it is overmatched by the two rogues and the monk. 

They find an ivory box about the size of a large book among the treasures. 

With his allies following, Sequoia crosses the courtyard, enters the great hall, and turns west into a passageway with two doors on the south and one to the north. The passageway ends at the door. 

The first door to Sequoia’s left opens into a trophy room. Only a few mangy pelts, stuffed heads, and shattered antlers remain. Zinia, who enters the room, looks more carefully and realizes that some of the trophies are the heads of dwarves, humans, and elves \- “Enemies of the temple,” he mutters..” 

The next door on the southern wall opens into the domicile of the majordomo of the castle. Zephyr sighs; it’s stripped of anything valuable. 

From there, Zephyr opens the westernmost door, where the hallway ends. Much like the other parts of the castle, this room is partially collapsed. The remains of cots and plain wooden chests convince the rogue that this was once a barracks room for castle guards—undoubtedly the human sort.

Zephyr notices an intact chest at the south end of the room. As he approaches it, he notices a giant lizard just in time. The lizard was using the chest as a lure. This time, the poor creature died. 

Sequoia leads the group past the last door in the hallway and into a room with a stairway leading down. 

It also has a swarm of rats. Rats that are quickly killed. 

Calmer turns to the group, “I suggest we finish exploring the ruins of the moathouse. Don’t want any nasty surprises to follow us.” 

Sequoia agrees. They cross the wrecked great hall and head down the southern passageway, which is 70 feet long and has three doors. The hallway runs north-south. 

The first door the rogue opens reveals an old conference room chamber.  He sniffs and shuts the door, “Nothing to be found.”

Sequoia mutters, “And for door number two,” as he opens the next door on the western wall. And in this chamber, he finds what must have been a bed chamber for some lowly official.   
Across the hallway, he sees another door and excitedly opens it. He looks inside, and although the room is in shambles and things are missing, whatever remains still appears valuable. He enters carefully, and as he picks up the treasures, he realizes they are worthless. 

He looks around and mutters, “A salon for meetings?” 

Leaving the salon and reentering the hallway, he walks into the large chamber at the end of the hallway. Much like other parts of the moathouse, this room is empty of anything valuable. Unlike other rooms, this room has a giant poisonous snake that attempts to strike. It fails. 

Soon, like the other nuisance monsters the group has faced, it is dead.
