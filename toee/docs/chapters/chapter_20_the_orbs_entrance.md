# Chapter 20 The Orb's Entrance

## Summary

The battle was over. The party stood victorious amid the corpses of giants and over twenty ghouls and ghasts, having successfully cleared an entire section of the dungeon. They had claimed new territory for the temple, positioning themselves as formidable agents within the enemy stronghold. However, they understood that simply holding ground was not enough—they would need to negotiate with Romag to officially claim this territory and recruit monsters to populate and defend their new domain.

The party approached Romag to report their success and lay claim to the cleared area. Romag, pleased with their accomplishment, invited them to sit and began an ideological training session. He explained that the Temple of Elemental Evil was founded on the principle that the true nature of the universe is constant chaos between the elements, with earth destined to reduce all things to dirt. The temple attracted those who embraced this inevitability rather than feared it, seeking to bring about the blessed moment when all would be reduced to a wasteland of earth.

When the party questioned why it was called the Temple of Elemental Evil rather than simply the Temple of Earth, Romag gritted his teeth in frustration. He explained that when the temple was founded, it was decided that the Earth Temple alone could not fight against all who would oppose it. The four elemental temples—earth, fire, water, and air—aligned to unify their ambitions and destroy their enemies, planning to settle their differences only after achieving victory.

Romag then revealed what had happened ten years ago. The forces of the temple had united and marched out to conquer, but they were defeated at the Battle of Emory Meadows by the Archbishop of Veluna and the Kingdom of Furyondy. The Circle of Eight, including powerful wizards like Bigby, Tenser, and Mordenkainen, had shown up and locked the temple itself, blocking access to the central driving power that gave them their necessary energy. Now they sat and plotted, searching for the key to unlocking the heart of the temple.

The party asked Romag about this key, and after considering their loyalty—having cleared territory, attended rituals, and proven themselves—he pulled out some brandy and shared it with them. He revealed that the key was an orb, the only object capable of forcing the chaotic elements to submit to the stability of earth. It was the shackle that binds the storm and flood, the tool to impose the discipline of stone upon the reckless factions of fire and water. Whoever held this orb would hold the mandate of the temple and have the power to supersede the bureaucratic authority of the greater temple.

Romag explained that with the orb, he could flood the dungeon with earth elementals and gorgons, crushing all opposing temples and ruling supreme. The orb was also the key to the vault that opened the deep places within the temple. When asked what was in the vault, Romag stated that such information needed to be earned—he did not yet trust them enough to reveal everything. The party understood they needed to continue proving themselves to learn more about the temple's deepest secrets.

To earn more trust, Romag tasked them with recruiting people to man the newly captured areas. Dick Rentsch had sent word of a group of professional mercenaries called the Broken Blades currently being recruited in Nulb. Romag was tired of relying on unreliable gnolls and unruly bugbears; he wanted disciplined, brutal soldiers loyal to gold. However, the Water Temple was also trying to recruit them, making it urgent for the party to secure the mercenaries first.

The party traveled to Nulb, arriving discreetly at the Watersteide Hostel, a run-down establishment with a sign reading "Rooms are cheap, lives cheaper." Inside the smoke-filled tavern room, they met with Dick Rentsch, who took them to his back room to discuss business privately. He explained that the captain of the Broken Blades, Dren Halveth, was currently taking bids and could be recruited in three ways: outbidding the Water Temple, defeating him in a fist fight, or completing a task for them. The party chose to pursue the option of completing a task.

Dick arranged a meeting with Dren Halveth, a half-orc with broad shoulders, scars, and one ruined white eye. His crew hung back behind him, relaxed but dangerous, with fingers drifting near weapons. Dren demanded to know who sent them, what they wanted in Nulb, and which gods they prayed to. The party explained they had gained territory in the temple for the Earth Temple. Dren revealed that a shipment of weapons belonging to his company had been stolen by the Crimson Reapers, who occupied the room next door. He tasked the party with retrieving the weapons without bringing the wrath of the temple down on everyone.

The party decided to try negotiating with the Crimson Reapers rather than stealing the chest. Calmer was sent to knock on their door while the rest of the party hid nearby. A tattooed and scarred Crimson Reaper opened the door and became angry when Calmer asked about the chest. When Calmer revealed he worked for the Earth Temple, the Reaper explained that the chest was temple property that Dren had stolen. He stated that Dren had until midnight to join the temple or he and his crew would be dead. The party realized the Crimson Reapers worked for a deeper organization behind the four elemental temples, not one of the four temples directly.

Returning to Dren, the party explained the situation: he must either join the temple or be killed by midnight. Dren expressed skepticism, noting the Water Temple was offering a large sum of money and claiming the Earth Temple just used recruits as cannon fodder. Sequoia countered brilliantly, arguing that the Water Temple relied on propaganda and lies while the Earth Temple showed real commitment by coming to recruit them personally. The party emphasized they had just claimed new territory and were willing to share it with the Broken Blades. Impressed by these arguments, Dren agreed to join the Earth Temple in exchange for one hundred gold pieces to help set up their new business.

As the party prepared to leave the hostel with the Broken Blades, Zinnia noticed that Wat, the barkeep, looked very unhappy about the outcome, confirming their suspicion that he worked for the Water Temple. When Calmer questioned Dick Rentsch about keeping Wat on his payroll despite his apparent loyalty to other factions, Dick responded that until they had secured complete power, everyone had to work together. As they departed, Wat approached Dren and whispered something to him, but Dren shoved him away.

The party successfully brought the Broken Blades back to the temple, where Romag was extremely pleased with their success. He announced that the new territory would be given to the mercenaries, who would report to the party, though he claimed another portion of the cleared territory for himself. That night, Romag hosted a celebratory drinking party in the newly cleaned banquet hall, which still carried the stench of cadavers and dead creatures mixed with food. Leaders from various areas of the temple attended the celebration. Romag proposed a toast to Sequoia, Zephyr, Zinnia, and Calmer for their contributions to increasing the Earth Temple's power, specifically highlighting Calmer's conversion from a cleric of St. Cuthbert to a follower of the elder elemental god. The party had successfully positioned themselves deeper within the temple's hierarchy, continuing their dangerous mission of espionage and sabotage from within the enemy stronghold.
