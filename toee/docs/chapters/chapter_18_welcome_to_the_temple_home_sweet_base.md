# Chapter 18 Welcome to the Temple: Home Sweet Base

## Summary

Romag woke the party after eight hours of rest with urgent news. The traitor had been discovered—Lieutenant Krell had fled to the gnoll and bugbear territories, plotting rebellion against the Earth Temple. Romag outlined a plan to trap the traitor: Earth Temple forces would guard the entrance and exit while the party approached from behind through the dangerous gnoll-infested area. As the party prepared for their mission, they overheard rumors about Romag being a new and inexperienced leader trying to prove himself, known for micromanaging and lacking the cleverness of his predecessor, who had been promoted to the deep temple. The Earth Temple's strategy relied on overwhelming enemies with masses of low-level troops rather than the powerful monsters employed by the Fire, Water, and Air Temples.

The party devised a clever strategy to convince the Earth Temple guards to mount a frontal assault on the gnolls as a distraction. Their persuasion succeeded easily, as the human guards harbored deep fears of being replaced by gnolls and becoming their food. However, when the guards advanced into the gnoll territory, disaster struck. Screams of "It's a trap\!" echoed through the corridors as the gnolls sprang their ambush, catching the guards in nets and slaughtering them mercilessly. Zinnia and Zephyr crept forward to investigate and witnessed a horrifying scene of gnolls feeding on the fallen guards.

The party engaged the remaining gnolls in swift combat, with Calmer casting powerful magic that summoned spectral guardians to devastate the clustered enemies. As they fought, they heard Lieutenant Krell's voice encouraging the gnolls, revealing his betrayal: "I told you they would try to kill you. The Fire Temple protects its own." The party pressed their advantage, cutting through the gnolls with precision strikes. When Krell emerged, wielding a peculiar hand crossbow contraption—a pistol of his own devising—he targeted Calmer directly, firing twice and knocking the cleric unconscious with devastating shots. Sequioa quickly revived Calmer with a healing potion, and the grateful cleric thanked him for his loyalty.

Surrounded and desperate, Krell attempted to negotiate with the party. He argued that the Earth Temple used its followers as mere cannon fodder, while the Fire Temple under Alremm valued power and glory, offering to join forces to destroy the Earth Temple and be hailed as heroes. The party rejected his offer, choosing to remain loyal to Romag. They attacked Krell carefully, using non-lethal strikes to subdue him rather than kill him, as Romag had demanded he be brought back alive. After a brief but intense fight, Sequioa delivered the final blow that knocked Krell unconscious.

The party searched Krell's quarters and the surrounding rooms, discovering considerable treasure including platinum pieces hidden in a brazier, gold, a three-dragon ante set, and useful supplies like flasks of oil. They also found a secret door leading deeper into the complex. With their prisoner secured, they brought the bloodied and unconscious Krell before Romag, who was immensely pleased with their success. He declared that everyone must witness the consequences of betrayal and ordered the party to attend a ritual sacrifice in the great chamber.

The party was led through ornate corridors of polished brown marble veined with black and inlaid with yellow triangular patterns, lit by burning bronze crescents. They entered a vast ritual chamber with rough-hewn sandstone walls and a floor of dark brown earth, dimly illuminated by phosphorescent lichen. At the center stood a twenty-foot pyramid of hard-packed dirt topped with a stone column bearing carved triangles and bronze knives. Romag stood atop the pyramid and began chanting to the elder elemental earth god, shoving dirt into Krell's mouth as the traitor screamed about being tricked. Four massive earth elementals rose from the ground at Romag's command and slowly approached the altar. As the crowd chanted and cheered, the elementals tore Krell's body apart in a gruesome display of the Earth Temple's brutal justice before descending back into the earth.

The party carefully blended in with the cheering crowd, feigning enthusiasm for the horrific spectacle to avoid suspicion. After the ritual concluded, they followed Romag and his lieutenant Hartsch, overhearing them discuss how the defeat had cost valuable resources and weakened their plans to overwhelm the Fire Temple. Hartsch suggested they could use their new allies—the party—as weapons against the Fire or Air Temple. The party noticed Romag disappearing through a secret door and decided to explore the area further.

Their exploration led them to a charred library filled with the burned remains of books on evil philosophy, double-dealing, self-advancement, and treachery. Among the remnants, they found references to the sacred earth triangle and warnings about trials of earthy terrors awaiting the foolhardy in the depths below. They discovered another secret door before continuing their investigation. Eventually, they encountered four guards who challenged them, but the party identified themselves as new Earth Temple recruits. The guards mentioned rumors about Krell's death creating opportunities for promotion, revealing the cutthroat nature of advancement within the temple.

Commander Joradin arrived and questioned the party's presence in his area. Though he acknowledged their status as new recruits, he asserted his authority over that section of the temple and ordered them to return to their quarters. The party, unwilling to accept this restriction, returned to Romag to question why Joradin could deny them access. Romag, still basking in what he considered a great victory despite the heavy losses, explained that Joradin was a new recruit brought in by Dick Rentsch who didn't yet understand how things worked in the temple.

Realizing the party needed proper accommodations, Romag granted them the territory they had cleared of gnolls and bugbears as their own base within the Earth Temple. He provided them with written permission to explore freely and warned them sternly not to betray his trust. He explained that the Earth Temple's territory extended throughout specific areas of the complex, and the party now controlled a significant portion of it. However, he also cautioned that having territory made them targets, as promotion within the temple often came by stepping over the bodies of rivals. The party had successfully infiltrated the Temple of Elemental Evil, earned Romag's trust, and established their own base of operations deep within the enemy stronghold, positioning themselves perfectly to continue their dangerous mission of espionage and sabotage.
