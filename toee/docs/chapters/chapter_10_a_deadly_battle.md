### **Zinnia's Perspective**

After our recent skirmish with the Black Earth cultists, we secured the stolen honey from Pasmarie Clee and, crucially, found **notes indicating the lost chest was located in a monastery within the mountains**. Our return to Hommlet was punctuated by the pressing need to share this new information, though the various factions in town, with their conflicting demands, made such a task fraught.

The first stop was to Pasmarie Clee in Sheernobb, where we returned her honey and received our reward. Back in Hommlet, the immediate priority was Calmer, who had fallen in the line of duty. To revive him, I made the difficult decision to **give up a magical trident, offering it as a tithe to Terjon at the Temple of St. Cuthbert**. It was a practical move, though it still chafed, especially considering Terjon's prior dismissiveness. We also made sure to visit Jaroo, who reiterated the importance of securing the chest, noting that the Terjon (implied to be followers of St. Cuthbert like Terjon) might withhold healing magic from non-followers. Our subsequent visit to Burne and Rufus confirmed their interest in the chest, though their priorities, as usual, seemed more political than purely good.

With our immediate obligations in Hommlet fulfilled and a new lead in hand, we headed to Ostverk. Upon arrival, our first objective, as always for a spy, was to gauge the local sentiment and gather intelligence. We made our way to "The Cheap Forester," a local tavern. The tavern keeper, Robert Deakin, a gnome, insisted we purchase food before he’d talk. While consuming our mandatory meal, I keenly observed the rumors circulating:

* The local chatter confirmed that **a powerful chest had indeed been lost to bandits, and many were searching for it**. This corroborated Lareth's story and our own findings.  
* Deakin, however, was adamant that **rumors of a "big evil power" were lies, insisting they were merely bandits**. He claimed to know because he had fought in the Battle of Emiry Meadows, implying the Temple was truly destroyed.  
* He unveiled a fascinating theory: the chest's true purpose was to secure a treaty with the Kingdom of Celene. The Viscounty (Verbobonc) intended to use this alliance to **wage war on the gnomes and seize their gem mines**. Deakin, being a gnome, was clearly displeased by this, and surprisingly, was glad the alliance hadn't formed. This insight into the region's geopolitical undercurrents was invaluable.  
* It was also rumored that the elves at Caer Ostverk and Lord Denelu were willing to pay well for information on the chest's location.  
* Finally, Deakin mentioned he was related to the Clee family (our honey purveyors in Sheernobb) and even had honey mead from the Honey Tavern Inn.

The layers of conspiracy and conflicting interests in this small corner of the world continued to surprise me, even for a seasoned spy.

### **Sequoia's Perspective**

The journey to Ostverk was uneventful, a welcome change after the chaos of the Moathouse and the Kron Hills. Our primary objective was to follow the lead we’d uncovered regarding the lost chest's location in a monastery. The information from the notes of Romag to Thalsor, confirmed by an unsent note from Thalsor, was our best hope.

In Ostverk, we settled into "The Cheap Forester" tavern, a place that lived up to its name in terms of ambiance but thankfully proved rich in information. The tavern keeper, Robert Deakin, shared his theories on the lost chest and the broader political landscape, highlighting how many groups were searching for it.

During our time there, we encountered two notable individuals:

* **Rithelm Drake, a human soldier**, who presented as a massive man of average height with a square face and rough, pink skin. He initially demanded a drink but, upon realizing we were adventurers, softened his stance and bought us one. He confessed that he could have been an adventurer himself, but family life had taken precedence. When questioned about the monastery, he was initially confused but eventually recalled **an abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner**. This was a direct hit on our primary objective.  
* **Gonnathrad Kevanarial, a High Elf Diplomat**, entered the scene, a skinny man with long, wavy black hair and grey eyes, dressed in a fancy red tunic. It quickly became apparent that **Gonnathrad was one of those individuals who had consumed far too many adventure stories from taverns and now desperately wanted to be a part of one himself**. He became overly excited at the prospect of being a "quest giver", even offering to write a five-page backstory for us, replete with intricate details for our adventure. He almost divulged information about the monastery before recalling his actual duty to find adventurers to hunt bandits.

We recognized an opportunity here. Despite his eagerness, Gonnathrad was a diplomat, and his official endorsement could be useful. We obliquely hinted at our knowledge of the monastery and the chest, carefully withholding specifics, which only spurred his enthusiasm. He pressed us for more, but we remained firm. Seizing the moment, he **issued us a letter of permit, officially hiring us to pursue a lead regarding the chest near a monastery by Gallow's Corner**. This letter, he assured us, would secure us **100 gold pieces** upon presentation to Caer Ostverk along with the information. It was clear he was still confused about why we were so focused on an abandoned monastery, but his romanticized view of adventure overshadowed his professional curiosity. As always, gathering information and manipulating circumstances to our advantage was proving more effective than direct confrontation.

### **Zephyr's Perspective**

After collecting the honey reward in Sheernobb and enduring the religious zeal of Calmer in Hommlet, where Zinnia gave up a perfectly good magical trident, our current objective was clear: follow the trail to the lost chest. The notes we found with the Black Earth cultists explicitly mentioned a monastery in the mountains as its location. With that, we set off for Ostverk.

The journey there was quiet, which was fine by me; less trouble, less attention drawn to my, well, *unique* appearance. We found ourselves in "The Cheap Forester" tavern in Ostverk, where a gnome named Robert Deakin owned the place. He was cagey at first, demanding we buy food before talking. I didn't mind; a good meal is always welcome. We picked up on the local rumors about the chest, which seemed to confirm everyone was looking for it. Deakin, though, had his own theories, arguing that the talk of "big evil powers" was just a cover for bandits, and he knew it because he'd fought in the Battle of Emiry Meadows.

While at the tavern, we met a few interesting characters:

* **Rithelm Drake**, a human soldier, was a burly man with a square face and green eyes. He was demanding a drink when we first saw him. When we started asking about the monastery, he seemed confused, but then, surprisingly, he remembered **an abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner**. Once he realized we were adventurers, he offered to buy us a drink, lamenting that he'd once considered being an adventurer himself before settling down with a wife and three kids. I just nodded; people always had a story, and usually, it involved them not doing what they really wanted.  
* Then there was **Gonnathrad Kevanarial**, a skinny High Elf Diplomat dressed in a fancy red tunic. This guy was a real piece of work. He got ridiculously excited when he thought he could be a "quest giver". He seemed to have read too many adventure stories, wanting to be part of our "narrative" and even offering to write a five-page backstory for us. We gave him just enough information to keep him hooked without revealing too much, and in his eagerness, **he actually gave us a letter of permit**. This letter confirmed he'd hired us to pursue the chest near the monastery at Gallow's Corner, and it promised **100 gold pieces** as a reward when presented to Caer Ostverk. He was so caught up in the "adventure story" of it all, he didn't even seem to care *why* we wanted to go to an abandoned monastery.

Once we had that letter and the confirmed location, we made our exit. I, for one, was eager to **leave Ostverk as quickly as possible**. In a town full of people with agendas, the less attention drawn to us, the better, especially with my appearance. We had the lead, and it was time to follow it.
