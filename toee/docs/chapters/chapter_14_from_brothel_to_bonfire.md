# Chapter 14 From Brothel to Bonfire

## **Summary**

The party arrived in the village of Nulb, a place notorious for its loose morals and unsavory reputation. After meeting with local guards at the Drowned Cottage, who warned them about the town’s dangerous nature, they explained they were seeking work for those willing to commit crimes and misdemeanors. As they explored the settlement, passing landmarks like the boatwright, Waterstead Hostel, Nulb Stables, and Gallows Square, they noticed signposts from Constable Eldrin Malavar warning citizens about a condemned temple south of town and declaring the Pilgrim’s Trail off-limits to all.

At the Boatman’s Tavern, they met Skole, the owner and bartender, along with Ketta, a serving wench. Their investigation led them to the Rhennee encampment, where they encountered Eryk Velkori, the caravan leader, who was consumed by grief over his missing sister, Serena. The usually vibrant camp was unnaturally still, the air thick with ash and river rot, as Eryk clutched a bottle of rum and wept openly. He explained that Serena had vanished after a night at the Boatman’s Tavern, and he believed she had been taken by the Zelrads, the Reapers, or Nisera’s Talons through a place called the Iron Lash. When Sequioa jokingly asked about a map to hell, Eryk responded that he was already living in one, his tragedy serving as the very path to damnation.

Eryk provided crucial information about Nulb’s criminal underworld. He revealed that the Crimson Reapers, led by a man named Zelt the Rat, sought to dominate the town’s criminal activities and funnel supplies through the area. More ominously, he spoke of Madame Selentis, also known as Nisera, who was the true power in Nulb—a member of a disgraced noble family from Verbobonc who now controlled everything like a spider in a web. Eryk warned that if Madame Selentis ever took notice of them, things would have gone very badly indeed. When asked about the Iron Lash, he laughed heartily and revealed it was simply the name of the local brothel.

Eryk’s daughter Tala then approached the party, inviting them to meet with her mother, Vethra Velkori, for a divination. The elderly Rhennee woman sat cross-legged in the shadows of her wagon, surrounded by bones, blackened cards, and a copper bowl that smelled of clove and blood. Her clouded blue eyes seemed to look through them rather than at them as she offered to read their fates. The party chose smoke for the first reading, and Vethra burned a lock of braided black hair, revealing visions of a crooked house with shifting shadows, a tavern with too many secrets called the Waterside, and a man with a red sash who dealt in whispers and iron.

For the second reading, the party chose bone, and Vethra tossed wolf knuckles and pigeon vertebrae into a shallow bowl. The bones clicked and scattered, forming a pattern that made her voice darken as she declared that Serena might be on her knees but was not broken, not with Rhennee blood in her veins. Despite her son Eryk’s protests that the divination would make her sick or even kill her, Vethra insisted on drawing cards from her faded Tarokka deck. She revealed The Chain, The Betrayer, and The Flooded Gate, then drew a fourth card with a hiss of breath—a crimson-painted barge sailing low on the Velverdyva, heavy with souls that no longer sang. Serena had been taken there, bound and traded like meat. Exhausted, Vethra collapsed back, and the party felt the weight of occult promises settling upon them as Eryk declared they had indeed found their map to hell.

The party returned to the Boatman’s Tavern in the late evening, where they observed various groups including a gentleman with guards, a ship captain with river pirates, and an old woman who feigned disinterest while carefully watching everything. They approached the serving wenches and spoke with Ketta, who nervously revealed there had been a fight involving Serena and the Rhennee, but she pointed fearfully toward the pirate captain, clearly too terrified to say more. The party then approached Captain Tolub, a boisterous and intimidating figure who made an awful joke that his crew laughed at with exaggerated enthusiasm. After some negotiation and charm from the party, Tolub agreed to buy them drinks and asked about their business in Nulb, questioning why they would help a man like Eryk Velkori.

When the party explained they might look like bandits but still had heart, Tolub laughed heartily at their sentimentality. He revealed that Serena had been taken to the Iron Lash as payment for something, and when the party asked where the brothel was located, he mockingly informed them it was upstairs in the very same building. The party ascended the stairs to find a low-ceiling, smoky lounge guarded by a massive man with an iron cudgel. They demanded to speak with the boss and were directed to Elvanna’s chambers, where they met a gorgeous woman with orange-red hair who spoke with a posh accent. Elvanna confirmed she held Serena as compensation for damages caused by the Rhennee at the Boatman’s Tavern and demanded five hundred gold pieces for her release.

The party paid the sum without negotiation, which surprised Elvanna, who remarked that she might have asked for more if she had known they were that desperate. Serena was brought from the pits, appearing dazed but defiant, and as she left, Elvanna mockingly wished to see her again, to which Serena retorted with a wish for Elvanna to go to hell. The party escorted Serena back to the Rhennee camp as quickly as possible, where she explained she had been used as leverage for a debt she didn’t know about and that her father was being coerced into ambushes along the forest road. Upon their arrival, Eryk’s eyes lit up with joy, and the entire camp erupted in music and dancing, the previous gloom completely dispelled.

Eryk declared the party family and invited them to spend the night celebrating with them. After a long night of drinking and dancing, they woke to find all their belongings intact, confirming Eryk’s honesty and his formal welcome of them as members of the Rhennee clan. He offered them protection and friendship in Nulb, warning that in this town, friends were worth a lot, but they shouldn’t trust all their friends. The party also discovered they had been credited with five hundred gold pieces for Serena’s rescue, the same amount they had paid for her release.

Seeking to advance their mission, the party returned to find Captain Tolub at his boat. When they expressed their desire to contact the higher powers or prophets of the area, specifically mentioning Romag, Tolub directed them to the Hall of the Scarlet Moon, a small encampment a day’s hike northeast of Nulb in the Kron Hills. Before departing, the party consulted Eryk Velkori, who described the Hall as a place where a druid named Elazar taught the rite of the wicker giant to restore nature’s balance. Eryk mentioned that several groups had camped there in festive anticipation, though he noted that many people seemed to be burning fire all day, which struck him as unusual.

The party traveled south through Hommlet, stopping at the tavern where Ostler Goodingoot, the owner and council member, directed them to speak with Jaroo, a local druid. Jaroo expressed deep suspicion about the Circle of the Scarlet Moon, finding them too showy, flashy, and ecstatic for true druids, suggesting their practices were more religious than natural. Despite these warnings, the party continued past Shernobb into the Kron Hills, where they arrived at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon. They observed a square tower ringed by ruined walls, a massive bonfire shaped like a fiery giant, and seven smaller bonfires with tents clustered around them.

The party attempted to approach stealthily and first visited Camp Number One, where they met four friendly human druids named Verigo, Mahoon, Iniri, and Faria. These druids were excited about the upcoming rite of the wicker giant and offered the party food, drink, and brandy, inviting them to stay the night. Deciding to scout further, the party moved to Camp Number Two, where they encountered two robed men named Albrith and Ingulf, who identified themselves as circuits of the Scarlet Moon. When the party asked whether they were related to the Temple of Elemental, the robed figures adopted threatening poses and revealed their allegiance by stating “fire, water, earth, or air,” confirming their connection to elemental evil.

The party immediately realized they had been set up by Captain Tolub, who had sent them to the Fire Temple instead of the Earth Temple. As they attempted to flee, the fire priests unleashed devastating magical attacks, hurling explosive bursts of flame that severely wounded the party and knocked Calmer unconscious. Zephyr quickly used a magical dust to render the entire party invisible, and Sequioa grabbed the fallen Calmer while the others scattered in different directions. To create a diversion, Zephyr cut the rope holding one of the leashed bears, unleashing the enraged animal upon the priests. Unable to see their invisible quarry and distracted by the rampaging bear, the fire clerics could only watch in fury as the party escaped into the hazy wilderness, having learned a harsh lesson about the dangers of direct confrontation and the value of stealth and cunning in their quest.
