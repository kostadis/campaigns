# Chapter 09 The Black Earth Cult?

### Planting 22, 579 

#### Zinnia

With the barrel over my shoulder I arrive at the Honey Tavern Inn. It’s after the dinner rush and only those who want to socialize or drink are left. 

I drop the barrel down, and smile. Glannon looks at the barrel. 

“You found it?” 

“Yes.” 

He rolls the barrel behind the bar, and takes out a keg, “Here’s my fine keg of mead. I know that mead can give you an advantage when you need to deploy that extraordinary charisma of yours.” 

I look at him, and try to figure out if he is mocking me. 

He realizes his mistake, “No, I didn’t mean it that way. The magic in the mead will help when you need to interact with people and persuade them. Drink a mug, and you’ll turn the heads of those you are attracted to, convince a merchant to cut a better deal, and in general be more persuasive. I know of one bard who drinks some before a performance and gets better tips.” 

I thank him. 

###  Planting 23, 579 

#### Sequioa

Quinna Poul comes rushing out of the kitchen as I saunter into the first floor dining area. 

I can’t help but notice that she’s both smiling and worried. 

“So \- remember that feast, well some of the ingredients were animated and they rushed into the village. Could I bother you to go get them?” 

I muse “Bouncing around the village, causing chaos? Sounded a bit ridiculous, but hey, more adventure, right?” 

So, we split up to round them up. I figured the broccoli was my best bet. It took off like a shot towards the apothecary, that place with all the magical potions and stuff. Honestly, I wasn't quite sure what to do. Part of me thought, "Let it go, it's just a broccoli." But Quinna needed it, and we kinda owed her after that feast.

It’s just bouncin, bouncin, bouncin on down the road, and it goes into the apothecary shop. I’m thinking now that what am I going to do. Firebolt it? No, then the whole place might catch fire. So I ask for another idea and they say “shoot it”. I roll, and I can’t remember if I hit, but yes, I do and it just kind of collapses. I think I hear a couple of voices, but no, just the owner looking bewildered. I pick up the “broccoli” now just a very dark green something and go back to the honey tavern.

It's funny, you know? One minute we’re facing down bandits and cultists, the next we’re chasing rogue vegetables. It’s just the life, I guess. Never a dull moment.

#### Zephyr

“Bouncing potatoes,” I shout to Seqoia and run after them. 

The potatoes stare at me as if taunting me. I grab one, and the other two pause as if angry. 

So I take my knife and stab the one in my hand. 

The other two potatoes are stunned. I wonder if animated potatoes can cry, or feel anything, and decide that’s not my problem. Taking advantage of horror, I grab them and stuff them into a bag. 

Terrified, they don’t move around in the bag. 

And a few hours later, the potato is in my belly. And I feel blessed. The honey-glazed forest stew feels like a blessing indeed. 

“You served me well, potato. And you were certainly more filling than that rat I caught in that prison.” 

As I put the last bit of food in my mouth, Palsmarie Clee coughs. The beekeeper druid of Sheernobb is very quiet. 

Has she walked into the Inn, or was she always there? 

“Let me show you on the map where the caravan was. They didn’t take the standard road, the fools. Go there, follow the bandits, and get the honey.” 

“So who did this?” 

“I don’t know. Gnonsbim says they are bandits. I don’t think so. The guards who survived said that the attackers chanted ‘Black Earth’ and carried weapons of stone. I’d have you talk to them, but they have all since left our town. ” 

This might be the clue and evidence we need. It certainly is who Lareth wants us to kill, and they might have this box that Lareth wants. 

I look at Zinnia, and although he is inscrutable, even his eyes reveal a momentary flash of pleasure. 

#### Sequoia

The wagon is precisely where she said. The site is littered with overturned wagons, broken barrels, and scattered honey jars. 

The ground is churned up.

Calmer shouts, “Magic was used here, or something very big. This isn’t just a bunch of brigands.” 

Our cleric heals and states the obvious. 

So I started looking around and soon found many valuable clues. Large footprints lead away from the road. 

Looking at the footprints, I wonder what could be so big and cause so much harm; whatever it is, it isn’t something good. 

There’s something that’s bugging me about this fight. So I take another look, and yes, there it is. I shout, “Magic.” Calmer rushes up to me, and I point at faintly glowing runes etched into the rocks. 

Zinnia looks at me, and I nod, “This is not bandits. This must be the cultists we are looking for.” 

The trail into the Kron Hills is easy to follow. Although along the way, we almost get crushed by falling rocks. I am worried that the stones were not an accident, but we find nothing. 

“Sometimes a rock is a falling rock,” I say. 

Unless you are following this trail, it ends at a well-hidden encampment. It’s built into a shallow cave on a rocky outcrop in the Kron Hills. There is a guarded gate with makeshift stone barricades. The guardian are wearing stone-studded leather armor and carrying stone weapons.  

I whisper, “These are the guys.” 

Inside the walls is a temple to some god, a weird stone statue, as well the barrels that we are looking for. 

#### Zephyr

Right, okay. The frontal assault won’t work. So our plan is to send Calmer in while we climb over the wall and attack from the rear.  We've just gotten over the wall, me, Zenia, and Zephyr. Meanwhile, Calmer tries to bluff these bandits–or whatever they are–about being lost, but it’s backfiring something fierce. This Thalsor of the Stone fellow, some kind of druid, starts shouting about the earth mother and how we're trampling on sacred ground. He’s not buying what Calmer’s selling.

“So, these are the meddling fools who dare tread upon the sacred ground of the Earth Mother,” Thalsor’s voice rumbles, low and gravelly, like a landslide in slow motion. I watch as he steps forward, his hands tightening around a stone staff adorned with jagged crystals.

“You think yourselves heroes?” I hear the words laced with disdain. “You, who scurry above the earth like witless insects, blind to the power beneath your feet? The very stones you trample upon are older, stronger, and far wiser than any of you could ever hope to be.”

Thalsor raises his staff, and the runes on the walls flare with an ominous light. “You call yourselves adventurers, but all I see are fools and thieves, unworthy of the honey you dare to steal back. That honey now belongs to the Black Earth Temple, a tribute to her enduring might.”

I think, “How the heck did he know that?” 

My musings are interrupted by his laughter that echoes through the cavern, harsh and unyielding. “Come then, insects. Let me show you the futility of your struggle. Let me show you what it means to face the unbreakable will of stone\!

Then it gets properly nasty. This druid, Thalsor, whatever his name is, rumbles something about stone, and suddenly two muddy creatures appear right beside Calmer. Mud men. Not something you see every day. Things get chaotic real quick. We gain the initiative – thankfully, I get an advantage because of my rogue training, and I come in right behind Sequoia.

Sequoia just goes to town on one of the bandits—absolute flurry of attacks. I’m next up and decide to help Sequoia, slashing at the bandit, he’s already wounded. Got the first one down and move onto the next, landing a solid hit. Zinnia follows, just laying into the bandits with some serious force. Flurry of blows, he calls it, multiple attacks, and the bandits are dropping like flies.

Then, something weird happens. When these mud men go down, they kind of explode, make it so you can’t move. I’m quick enough to dodge the worst of it, but still, it’s a nasty trick. Sequoia’s already switched to attacking this Thalsor fellow directly, landing some tough blows. I chip in where I can, finishing off the bandits when I get the chance.

But then Thalsor he brings out something else. He taps this stone thing and – bam\! – it comes alive. Stone Golem, they call it. Big, tough, and resistant to everything but magical attacks. Definitely not something you want to get into a fistfight with. Calmer hits it with some kind of holy spell, deals a ton of damage, but it’s still standing.

Thalsor isn't happy at all, though. He starts ranting again about the honey and the earth, then suddenly shifts gears and tries to negotiate. Weird guy. Offers to trade, anything but giving up the honey. We try to intimidate him – Calmer leads the charge with that – but the druid's not having any of it. Instead, he conjures up a storm of sharp stones. I manage to dodge most of it, but it still stings like hell.

Sequoia’s still hacking away at Thalsor, getting some good hits in. I’m helping out, switching targets as needed. Then, Thalsor brings out another hail of stones. We dodge again, but it’s wearing us down. It's all a bit frantic. Gotta stay out of the stone golem’s way, keep hitting Thalsor, avoid getting hit by the flying rocks. We're still in the thick of it – Thalsor's barely alive, and the golem’s still a threat. Things are far from over.
