# Chapter 11 Tharok's Fury Unleashed

## **Summary**

The session began with the dungeon master providing crucial context about the political landscape and ongoing conflicts. The party learned about the tensions between Verbabonc and the gnomes of the Kron Hills, where territorial disputes over valuable gem mines had created a powder keg of conflict. Verbabonc’s construction of a strategic castle in Hommlet served as force projection, much like a medieval aircraft carrier, allowing them to control the valley roads and threaten gnomish territory. The gnomes viewed this expansion as imperialism, while Verbabonc claimed it was necessary protection against the Temple of Elemental Evil.

The party discovered that the various elemental temple factions operated like competing mob families, each controlling different territories and fighting for dominance. Lareth controlled the fire temple territory and had been using the party to eliminate earth temple gangs, expanding his own power base. However, all these factions ultimately answered to the Temple of Elemental Evil, the overarching criminal organization pulling the strings. The Chest of Rao served as the prize everyone sought \- a powerful magical battery capable of fueling devastating rituals according to the laws of magical thermodynamics.

Continuing their exploration of the abandoned monastery, the party encountered Brother Eldrin’s stone guardian in an ancient library. The guardian presented them with a riddle about the eternal cycles of nature, testing their understanding of Beory’s old faith. Zephyr successfully identified the answer as the water cycle, demonstrating wisdom that honored the old ways. The guardian declared their worthiness and became inanimate, opening the passage deeper into the monastery complex.

Moving stealthily through the stone corridors, the party overheard cultists excitedly discussing Tharok’s impending ritual and wishing they could witness the momentous event. Sequioa and Zephyr eliminated the guards silently, finding a key among their possessions. The cultists wore brown robes marked with triangle symbols, indicating their allegiance to the earth temple faction.

The key unlocked a prison chamber where two captives were being held for torture. Jerry, a soldier of Verbabonc, and Warda, a gnome nationalist, were engaged in heated political arguments even while imprisoned. Despite their ideological differences \- Jerry representing Verbabonc’s interests and Warda opposing what she saw as imperial expansion \- both agreed to help fight the cultists after the party explained the dire threat. Jerry revealed crucial information about a secret passageway to the deeper temple, knowledge passed down from his grandmother who had once worshipped Beory in these halls.

Armed with scimitars taken from the fallen cultists, the group followed the secret passage to the heart of the monastery. They emerged into a vast ritual chamber that took their breath away \- ancient stone carved with intricate elemental motifs, towering pillars like colossal trees, and a massive cracked altar at the center. The Chest of Rao rested upon the altar, radiating both protective magic and creeping corruption, while arcane symbols floated above it in eerie greenish-gold light.

High Cultist Tharok stood before the altar, his imposing form marked by skin mottled like cracked stone and eyes burning with fervent intensity. Three robed cultists knelt around him in reverence, their whispered invocations weaving into the elemental hum that filled the air. The floor bore concentric rings of golden runes representing elemental power, and the very air vibrated with raw magical energy that made the chamber feel alive.

When Tharok spotted the intruders, he declared them defilers of his sacred ritual and threatened that the earth itself would swallow them whole. Battle erupted as the party moved to stop the ritual before it could be completed. Zinnia, Sequioa, and Zephyr quickly dispatched the three cultist guards with swift, decisive strikes, while Calmer blessed the party with divine magic to aid their efforts.

Jerry charged bravely toward the high cultist but was struck down by Tharok’s devastating attacks, his body crumpling under the elemental fury. Tharok’s earth magic caused the entire chamber to quake violently, dust and stones cascading from the ceiling as tremors knocked several party members to the ground. The earthquake disrupted Calmer’s blessing as he struggled to maintain his footing on the shifting stone.

Sequioa recognized that disrupting the ritual itself might weaken their enemy and rushed to the altar, knocking the Chest of Rao from its resting place. The magical energies swirling through the chamber immediately began to dissipate as the ritual circle was broken. Zephyr struck with deadly precision, landing a critical blow against the high cultist, while Warda continued fighting despite witnessing Jerry’s death, her hatred for imperial forces now focused on the cultist threat.

The battle raged on as Calmer called upon divine power and Zinnia pressed the attack with relentless strikes. Tharok retaliated with earth-shaking force, hurling Zephyr backward with a devastating blow while his elemental magic continued to make the chamber tremble. Though bloodied and battered, the high cultist remained standing as the session concluded, the outcome of this crucial confrontation still hanging in the balance.

**📝 Session Summary: Triumph and Treachery in Hommlet**  
The session began in a state of high tension and immediate action within the Earth Temple's ritual chamber, located deep beneath the abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner. High Cultist **Tharok**, his skin mottled like cracked stone, was reeling from having his vital ritual disrupted, but still posed a lethal threat. The gnome nationalist **Warda** opened the round, landing a single strike against the cultist after two initial misses. **Zephyr** followed, landing a strong blow bolstered by a Sneak Attack. The enraged **Tharok** lashed out, striking **Warda** with a devastating blow that dealt 29 damage, though his subsequent attacks missed their mark.

The party pressed the advantage, determined to end the fight before **Tharok** could regain control. **Calmer** targeted the high cultist with a *Guiding Bolt*, dealing 9 damage and granting **Zinnia** advantage on her next strike. **Zinnia** capitalized on the opportunity, launching a flurry of attacks that struck true, dealing a total of 14 points of damage and finally felling the formidable **High Cultist Tharok**.

With **Tharok** dead, the entire cavern began to violently collapse, forcing the party to make a swift exit. After a quick debate, they decided to secure the **Chest of Rao**—the powerful artifact radiating both protective and corruptive energy—and fled through the secret passageway.

### **The Abbey's Reward and the Lore of Rao**

The party arrived safely back in the main abbey, where the damage seemed miraculously contained to the ritual cavern. They found **Brother Eldin**, who was alive but wounded. **Brother Eldin** was relieved to learn that **Tharok** had been vanquished and declared the Abbey of Beory safe once more.

He offered the adventurers a reward for their service:

* **Wealth:** 70 gold pieces, 400 silver pieces, and 400 copper pieces each.  
* **Magic Items:** A **Scroll of Web** and a **Potion of Greater Healing**.

Seeing the party carrying the magical relic, **Brother Eldin** delivered crucial lore regarding the **Chest of Rao**. He explained that **Rao** is the patron of peace and harmony, and the chest is an artifact of **great good** built by the high priests of Rao. He warned that the chest was extremely dangerous to possess, as it would attract both those who would claim it for evil—using it as a powerful magical battery—and the forces of good who would reclaim it for the Archclericy of Veluna. He urged them to return it rather than risk the peril it would draw.

### **Setting the Trap in Hommlet**

Despite the warnings, the party chose to use the **Chest of Rao** as powerful leverage in their ongoing conflict with the Temple's agents in **Hommlet**. They planned to eliminate the traders **Rannos** and **Gremag**, who they knew were working for the Temple of Elemental Evil.

The party sent a note to the agents, requesting a meeting behind the carpenter's field. **Rannos**, suspicious and paranoid, refused the meeting spot but invited them to meet at his establishment after dark. The party retreated to the Inn of the Welcome Wench to wait and plan, where **Calmer** purchased an expensive gnomish mead.

Under the cover of night, the party moved to the shop's back area and lured out their first target, **Thorne (Duke)**, the man-at-arms hired by **Rannos**. They instantly launched a surprise attack, quickly overwhelming the mercenary. **Zephyr** landed multiple hits, followed by **Zinnia**. **Sequoia** delivered the final strike, **knocking Thorne unconscious**. Interrogating the mercenary, they confirmed he was a simple hired hand who would rather be left for dead than face the wrath of the cult.

### **The Decisive Confrontation**

Armed with the unconscious **Thorne**, the party entered the inner office to confront **Rannos** and his bodyguard, **Gremag**. **Rannos** immediately dismissed **Thorne** as useless and demanded the **Chest of Rao**. When the party attempted to negotiate for the Temple to leave the region, **Rannos** and **Gremag** mocked them, and **Gremag** immediately initiated combat, attacking **Zinnia**.

The first round was devastating for the party:

* **Zinnia** and **Zephyr** landed severe blows on **Gremag**, wounding him deeply.  
* **Gremag** retaliated with two swift and brutal strikes, first severely injuring **Zinnia** and then **knocking her unconscious** with the second hit.  
* **Sequoia** continued the assault, dealing a total of 25 points of damage.

In the second round, the tide turned decisively. While **Zinnia** lay dying, **Calmer** finally landed a critical blow, hitting **Gremag** and instantly **killing** the Temple operative. With **Gremag** dead and **Zinnia** downed, the party immediately focused their remaining strength on **Rannos**. **Zephyr** moved to secure a position, granting **Sequoia** the advantage he needed. **Sequoia** struck, wounding **Rannos** for a massive 26 points of damage.

The session concluded with the local leadership of the Temple operation in **Hommlet** annihilated: **Gremag** dead and **Rannos** severely wounded, leaving the **Chest of Rao** in the hands of the victorious adventurers.
