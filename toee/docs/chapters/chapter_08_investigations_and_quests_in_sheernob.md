# Chapter 08 Investigations, and quests in Sheernob. 

### Planting 21, 579 Early Afternoon

#### Zephyr 

Well, that was expensive\!

After having his pocket cleanly picked by Mari, Zephyr was looking for an opportunity to get out. 

“Let’s go explore the village and see if we can learn anything\!” 

The rest of the group stares at him. He watches as Calmer drinks the last of his mead and then tells him in as disrespectful a tone Calmer can muster: “As you wish, boss\!” 

Zephyr thinks about what to do next. The village of Sheernobb is a small town. The dominant building is what the locals call the Moot Hall, where folks go to meet. The road ends at the moot hall and splits into two branches, one going south and another north around the moot hall. The two branches converge after the moot hall on the road going out of town. The two branches of the moot hall form a circle around which most stores are located. The Tavern is on the south branch, just after the road coming into town ends. 

Zephyr remembers how a bookstore was on the northern branch. 

“To the bookstore.” 

Seeing an opportunity to explore the town more, rather than go directly to the bookstore, he decided to explore the rest of the village by continuing on the southern branch and following the road to the bookstore.  

The journey is short, and he notes the stores: “Djinibac’s Apothecary, a Tabac Shop, Fiver Woodcarver, Peony’s Mill, and would you look at that a pond, Henker’s Shop, Naglo Crystal Divine Shop, and there is Grindin Books.” 

Nestled within a small cottage, Grindin Books is instantly recognizable by the cluttered display of books and maps in its windows and a studded oak door bearing the welcoming sign, “Welcome to the Grindin Books.”   
The quaint exterior belies the wealth of knowledge contained within.

This dimly-lit bookshop has been constructed to the owner's specifications, and the owner wants to read books when not helping customers. Behind the counter, a plush chair with a clever reclining mechanism lets them put their feet on an equally plush stool. Over the counter, two glass bulbs, tinted green to reduce eye strain, hold burning candles, perfectly positioned for comfortable reading. And there are books beyond counting, books crammed in every available shelf, and books stacked on tables and trolleys. Hundreds of books, maps, and scrolls fill solid oak shelves. Behind a desk-sized for a human, perched on a massive cushion-covered chair, sits the gnome Grindin Well-Read.

#### Sequoia 

The halfling leaves the bookstore and urges the group to go to the Moot Hall. “The mayor may have more information and a bigger reward.” 

And so they walk across the road to the Moot Hall. Just before the entrance is a statue. Sequoia wants to look at it and approaches. At the statue's base, he reads a sign \- “Keenleene, Founder of Sheernobb\! 

As he looks at the ancient grey stone statue, he hears the sound of laughter. Before his eyes, the stone figure of Keenleene transforms, coming to life with lifelike mischief. His massive gem-like eyes sparkle brightly, and his bulky nose twitches with glee. The braided hair adorned with gemstones seems to shimmer as if alive.

Keenleene begins to laugh heartily, a sound that resonates with pure joy. He picks up his pipes and starts playing a lively tune, his fingers moving deftly over the instrument. As he plays, he winks at Sequoia and the audience, his expression filled with mirth and warmth. He twirls and hops, inviting all who witness his performance to join in the merriment. The enchantment brings an undeniable sense of happiness and amusement, captivating.

Sequoia shrugs and tells his friends, “Gnomes are master illusionists. And they love to put on shows like this.” 

The Moothall is in the center of Sheernobb on the largest hill. It boasts wide stone steps leading to grand stone doors carved with runes. These doors open into the Feasting Hall, the village’s largest building, used for special gatherings. Wide stone steps lead to a set of large stone doors with runes carved over the door \- Ni te pharkalu, quo lun verra lu,  with a helpful translation in common: “If you see it, it’s not the right it.”

He wonders for a minute, and it reminds Gnomes that what you see may be an illusion. He pushes the door open and enters a small hallway, entering a large chamber. The chamber's center is below Sequoia, surrounded by three tiers of broad steps, each with benches on which to sit. The ceiling height allows everyone to walk erect. The woodwork within the hall is impressive, from the intricately carved beams, supports, and posts to the benches and floor. An elder gnome is seated in a large (by gnome standards) elegantly engraved oak chair with brown wrinkled skin and long white wild hair. The wizened gnome seems to have nodded off and is sound asleep with his bare feet crossed at the ankle over one of the arms of the chair. A weasel jumps out from seemingly nowhere into the elder gnome's lap, waking him in a grouchy yelp. The weasel darts quickly to nuzzle the elder's ear, then flips on its back, baring its soft underbelly. Then, with a bemused smile, the gnome gently strokes the weasel's fur while watching Squoial.

“Welcome. I am Glennddarc, Headgnome of this village. You are in the moot hall of Sheernobb, one of my clan villages in this part of the Kron Hills. Why are you here?” 

“We’re looking for information on some bandits?”   
Glennddarc is annoyed. “Bandits? We have problems with giants, and you are interested in Bandits?” 

Sequoia is taken aback, “Well, we did talk to Gnonsbim, the Captain of the Guard, and he asked us to investigate them.” 

Glennddarc looks more annoyed, “Yes, Gnonsbim\! He’s running for re-election and wants to show he’s a man of action. You should go to Pasmarie’s beehives if it's hunting bandits. She’s been making the biggest ruckus.” The headgnome snickers,  “If you ask me, Gnonsbim has gone sweet on her. If you want to help a young gnome looking for a wife, listen to Gnonsbim. When you want to help the town, I need help with some giants. The rewards will be greater.” 

Sequoia doesn’t have a chance to protest, as the headgnome is sleeping again. 

“Let’s go.” 

###  Planting 21, 579 Late Afternoon 

#### Zephyr 

 A quarter mile southeast of Sheernobb, Zephyr and his fellow travelers arrive at Pasmarie’s Beehives. Verdant fields and wildflowers surround the farm. The hives are neatly arranged in rows, with the buzzing of bees creating a harmonious backdrop. The area is well-maintained, with paths leading to each hive and small benches for visitors to observe the bees at work.

Standing in the doorway of a small building is a petite gnome woman with a kind, weathered face framed by unruly curls. Her apron is smeared with honeycomb wax and pollen, and her  
hands, though small, show the strength of someone who’s worked the land for years.

“The adventurers Gnonsbim spoke of\!” she says, melodic voice blending urgency and hope. She steps closer, her oversized boots clicking on the dirt. “Gnonsbim said you’re the sort to lend a hand when trouble stirs.”  

She sets her hands on her hips.

“I’m Pasmarie Clee, keeper of Sheernobb’s finest beehives—or what’s left of them. You see, a shipment of my honey, bound for the merchants of Verbobonc, has been stolen.”

Her voice trembles slightly, but she steadies herself, determination glinting in her eyes. “The caravan was ambushed on the trade road just north of the village. The guards who survived said the attackers weren’t bandits—no, they spoke of the ‘Black Earth’ and carried weapons of stone. Whoever they are, they’ve taken my honey and vanished into the Kron Hills.”

She leans forward, her voice dropping to a whisper.“This honey isn’t just about trade. It carries Sheernobb’s name and our reputation. If we lose the trust of the merchants, our village will  
Suffer.”

Straightening, she clasps her hands together. “Will you help me? Follow the trail, find these raiders, and bring back what they’ve stolen. I’d go myself, but… I’m better at tending  
bees than facing armed fanatics.”

Her lips quirk into a faint smile as she added, “I can’t offer much gold, but you’ll have my gratitude and a share of my finest enchanted honey—a taste of Sheernobb’s magic that might save your life in a pinch. What do you say?” 

Zephyr stares at her momentarily and thinks, “Reward is added to the other reward. And we get to find the chest. This is starting to be a very lucrative operation\!”  

“Of course. Can you tell us where the last raid was?” 

She sighed and explained that it had happened a few miles to the north east.

Zephyr shows her the map he purchased, and she marks it. 

“We’ll find those bandits and save the honor of your bees\!”
