## Romag

Romag is the high priest and leader of the Earth Temple within the Temple of Elemental Evil. He first appeared when the party returned to his chamber after surviving the werewolf test, where he sat alongside Dick Rentsch. He was pleased the party survived, as Dick Rentsch had wagered they would not. When informed the party had faced werewolves rather than gnolls, Romag confronted Dick Rentsch, who admitted that Alremm had arranged the werewolves as a test of new recruits. Romag expressed approval that the party had not fallen for the angelic disarmament trap and awarded them badges of the Earth Temple.

Romag dispatched Lieutenant Krell the following morning with a mission to raid the Fire Temple's seized territory. When the party returned having killed an Earth Temple ogre rather than a Fire Temple enemy—revealing Krell as a saboteur—Romag claimed he had already known Krell was a spy and had kept him under watch. He ordered the party to bring Krell back alive for sacrifice.

After Krell was captured and delivered, Romag conducted a public ritual sacrifice in the great earth chamber, chanting to the elder elemental earth god, shoving dirt into Krell's mouth as the traitor screamed, and commanding four massive earth elementals to tear Krell's body apart as a warning to all who would betray him. Following the ritual, Romag was overheard discussing with his lieutenant Hartsch how the defeat against Krell's rebellion had cost valuable resources and weakened plans against the Fire Temple, and suggesting the party could be used as weapons against the Fire or Air Temple.

Romag later held an ideological training session with the party, explaining that the Temple of Elemental Evil united four elemental temples to fight their enemies before settling their differences, and that ten years ago they were defeated at the Battle of Emory Meadows by the Archbishop of Veluna, the Kingdom of Furyondy, and the Circle of Eight—including Bigby, Tenser, and Mordenkainen—who locked the temple and blocked access to its central power source. He revealed that the key to unlocking the temple's heart is an orb described as the shackle that binds storm and flood, capable of forcing chaotic elements to submit to earth's stability. Whoever holds it holds the mandate of the temple and can supersede the bureaucratic authority of the greater temple. He stated that the orb also opens a vault deep within the temple but declined to reveal what the vault contains, saying such information must be earned.

Romag tasked the party with recruiting the mercenary company the Broken Blades from Nulb before the Water Temple could secure them, expressing frustration with unreliable gnolls and bugbears. When the party successfully returned with Dren Halveth and the Broken Blades, Romag was extremely pleased and hosted a celebratory drinking party in the cleared banquet hall, toasting the party by name and specifically highlighting Calmer's conversion from a cleric of St. Cuthbert as proof of the Earth Temple's righteousness.

Romag subsequently directed the party to expand southward and eastward, clearing additional dungeon territory. When they returned reporting successful clearance of ghouls, harpies, and ghasts, he complained that the undead had refused to acknowledge his authority ever since his predecessor was promoted to work for the Deep Temple. He praised the party for bringing the ghasts under temple control and delivered an ideological speech about earth crushing all things to dust.

When the party asked about payment for their services, Romag clarified that the arrangement required them to bring treasure to the temple and pay him a portion, not receive payment themselves. When they pressed for official titles and higher ranks, he agreed but stated advancement required a demonstration of loyalty, instructing his subordinate Harch to prepare a ceremony. He clarified that the party was too valuable to sacrifice—prisoners would be used for the ritual instead—but postponed the ceremony until the following day, unaware that the prisoners had already been freed by the party.

Romag is characterized as a new and inexperienced leader trying to prove himself, known for micromanaging and lacking the cleverness of his predecessor. His temple's strategy relies on overwhelming enemies with masses of low-level troops rather than powerful monsters. He appeared unaware of significant portions of his own territory, having left large dungeon sections empty and unused. He also maintained an Otyugh as a living disposal system for rivals, though the party killed it without his knowledge. His last known location is his chamber within the Earth Temple.

## Lieutenant Krell

Lieutenant Krell was an officer within the Earth Temple who appeared in Chapter 17 delivering a mission from Romag to raid Fire Temple territory. He directed the party to attack what he claimed were Fire Temple forces, but the target turned out to be an Earth Temple ogre, revealing his sabotage. When the party returned to report, Krell was conspicuously absent.

Romag revealed he had suspected Krell as a spy and kept him under watch. In Chapter 18, Romag reported that Krell had fled to gnoll and bugbear territories and was plotting rebellion against the Earth Temple. During the party's mission to capture him, they heard Krell's voice encouraging gnolls against the party's allied guards, declaring "I told you they would try to kill you. The Fire Temple protects its own," confirming his allegiance to the Fire Temple.

Krell emerged during the battle wielding a hand crossbow contraption described as a pistol of his own devising, firing twice at Calmer and knocking the cleric unconscious with devastating shots. Surrounded and desperate, he attempted to negotiate with the party, arguing that the Earth Temple used followers as cannon fodder while the Fire Temple under Alremm valued power and glory, and proposing they join forces to destroy the Earth Temple and be hailed as heroes. The party rejected his offer.

The party subdued Krell with non-lethal strikes per Romag's orders. His quarters contained platinum hidden in a brazier, gold, a three-dragon ante set, flasks of oil, and a secret door leading deeper into the complex. Krell was delivered to Romag bloodied and unconscious. At the ritual sacrifice, he screamed about being tricked before Romag commanded four earth elementals to tear his body apart. Krell is deceased.

## Dick Rentsch

Dick Rentsch was referenced in Chapter 17 as having provided the party with a map of the dungeon and having failed to tell them everything about the dangers they would face. He was found sitting beside Romag when the party returned after surviving the werewolf encounter, and Romag noted that Dick Rentsch had wagered the party would not survive. When confronted about the werewolves, Dick Rentsch admitted that Alremm had hired werewolves to occupy the secret chamber as a test of new recruits.

In Chapter 20, Dick Rentsch sent word to Romag about the Broken Blades mercenaries being recruited in Nulb, identifying an opportunity to secure disciplined soldiers. When the party traveled to Nulb, they met Dick Rentsch at the Watersteide Hostel, where he took them to his back room and explained that the mercenary captain Dren Halveth could be recruited by outbidding the Water Temple, defeating him in a fist fight, or completing a task. He arranged the party's meeting with Dren.

When the party questioned Dick Rentsch about keeping Wat the barkeep on his payroll despite Wat's apparent loyalty to the Water Temple, Dick responded that until they had secured complete power, everyone had to work together. He is characterized as a pragmatic operator who maintains relationships across factional lines. Romag described Joradin as a new recruit brought in by Dick Rentsch, suggesting he actively recruits personnel for the Earth Temple. His last known location is the back room of the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb.

## Dren Halveth

Dren Halveth is the captain of the Broken Blades, a professional mercenary company. He appeared in Chapter 20 at the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb, described as a half-orc with broad shoulders, scars, and one ruined white eye. His crew hung behind him, relaxed but dangerous with fingers drifting near weapons. He questioned the party about who sent them, what they wanted in Nulb, and which gods they prayed to.

Dren revealed that a shipment of weapons belonging to his company had been stolen by the Crimson Reapers, who occupied the adjacent room, and tasked the party with retrieving the weapons without bringing the wrath of the temple down on everyone. When the party returned with information that the Crimson Reapers worked for an organization behind all four elemental temples and had issued an ultimatum—join the temple or die by midnight—Dren expressed skepticism about the Earth Temple specifically, noting the Water Temple was offering significant money and claiming the Earth Temple used recruits as cannon fodder.

Dren was persuaded to join the Earth Temple by Sequioa's argument that the Water Temple relied on propaganda while the Earth Temple demonstrated real commitment by recruiting personally, combined with the party's offer to share their newly claimed territory. He agreed to join in exchange for one hundred gold pieces to help establish his new operation. As the party departed the hostel, Wat whispered something to him, but Dren shoved the barkeep away, suggesting he was not swayed by whatever the Water Temple agent communicated. Dren and the Broken Blades successfully returned to the Earth Temple with the party, where Romag assigned them the newly cleared territory under the party's nominal command. His last known location is the former gnoll and bugbear quarters within the Earth Temple.

## Alremm

Alremm is referenced as a figure associated with the Fire Temple. In Chapter 17, Dick Rentsch revealed that Alremm had hired werewolves to occupy the secret chamber as a test of new recruits. In Chapter 18, Krell invoked Alremm's name during his attempted negotiation, stating the Fire Temple under Alremm valued power and glory as opposed to the Earth Temple's use of followers as cannon fodder. Alremm does not appear directly in any of these chapters but is established as a significant authority within the Fire Temple and an opponent of the Earth Temple. Current whereabouts unknown.

## Hartsch

Hartsch is Romag's lieutenant within the Earth Temple, referenced in Chapter 18. He was observed in conversation with Romag after Krell's sacrifice ritual, during which he suggested that their new allies—the party—could be used as weapons against the Fire or Air Temple. He appears to serve as Romag's strategic advisor. His last known location is within the Earth Temple.

## Joradin

Commander Joradin appeared in Chapter 18 during the party's post-ritual exploration of the Earth Temple complex. He arrived and questioned the party's presence in his section of the temple, acknowledging their status as new recruits but asserting his authority over that territory and ordering them to return to their quarters. When the party complained to Romag, the high priest explained that Joradin was a new recruit brought in by Dick Rentsch who did not yet understand how things worked in the temple. His last known location is his claimed section of the Earth Temple complex.

## Jorin Pike Kale

Jorin Pike Kale appeared in Chapter 21 as one of seven prisoners discovered in a locked prison chamber within the Earth Temple's territory. He was a river sailor who had been imprisoned by Dick Rentsch's men as punishment for making a pass at a woman named Dalia. Upon being freed by the party, he expressed profound gratitude and shared actionable intelligence about Captain Tolubb's pirate ship schedule at the docks in Nulb, framing it as useful information given that Tolubb had previously betrayed the party by directing them into a Fire Temple ambush. His current whereabouts are unknown following his escape, which the party staged to appear as the work of the Fire Temple.

## Aldous Glimmerfield

Master Aldous Glimmerfield appeared in Chapter 21 as one of the seven prisoners held in the Earth Temple's prison chamber. He was identified as a textile merchant from Divers who had been captured by raiders. He was freed by the party as part of the staged Fire Temple escape. He and his wife Mistress Lenora Glimmerfield promised a reward of one hundred silver pieces for their rescue. His current whereabouts are unknown following his escape.

Mistress Lenora Glimmerfield appeared in Chapter 21 as one of the seven prisoners in the Earth Temple's prison chamber, identified as a textile merchant from Divers and wife of Master Aldous Glimmerfield. ## Wat

Wat is the barkeep at the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb, appearing in Chapter 20. When the party successfully recruited Dren Halveth and the Broken Blades for the Earth Temple, Zinnia observed that Wat looked very unhappy about the outcome, confirming suspicion that he worked for the Water Temple. As the party departed with the mercenaries, Wat approached Dren and whispered something to him, though Dren shoved him away. When Calmer questioned Dick Rentsch about keeping Wat employed despite his apparent divided loyalties, Dick Rentsch explained that until they secured complete power, everyone had to work together. His last known location is the Watersteide Hostel in Nulb.

## Harch

Harch appeared briefly in Chapter 22 as a subordinate of Romag within the Earth Temple. Romag instructed Harch to prepare a ceremony for the party's advancement in rank and official title within the temple hierarchy, a ritual that would involve prisoner sacrifice. He was referenced by name only in this context, with no further details about his role or appearance provided. His last known location is within the Earth Temple.
