## Romag

The leader of the Earth Temple, operating from his office/chambers within the temple complex. During the party's report of their territorial expansion (Chapter 22), Romag was found sitting and looking very pleased. He confirmed the party had dealt with the ghasts, ghouls, and harpies that had refused to bow to him, then delivered a five-minute tirade about the power of the earth crushing everything to dust. When the party asked about payment, he clarified that the arrangement works in reverse — the party brings treasure to the temple and pays him a portion. He agreed to grant the party official titles and higher ranks but stipulated they must first demonstrate loyalty through a ritual. He instructed Harch to prepare prisoners for a ceremony involving ripping bodies apart, though he postponed it until the following day. He also overheard Zephyr suggesting Calmer might die in the ritual and clarified that the party members are too valuable to kill — prisoners would be used instead.

In Chapter 23, Romag received news of the chimera attack with thunderous fury, expressing outrage over the Fire Temple's audacity and the theft of prisoners. He declared the moment to strike against Alrrem had arrived and ordered the party to deal with the Fire Temple's weakened leader. His leadership style was later characterized by Hartsch as slow and erosion-like — preferring to send others to fight while he operates as a chess king from behind. The party, having met privately with Hartsch, resolved to assassinate Romag and install Hartsch in his place. Last known location: his chambers within the Earth Temple.

## Harch / Hartsch

Referenced in Chapter 22 as "Harch," an individual instructed by Romag to prepare prisoners for the loyalty ritual ceremony. In Chapter 23 he is identified more fully as "Hartsch," Romag's sub-general and speaker. During the private meeting with the party in their quarters — away from Romag's ears — Hartsch provided detailed tactical intelligence about the temple's political landscape. He revealed that Alrrem's power had been waning since the Battle of Emridy Meadows a decade prior, and that the loss of his werewolves followed by the ruinous cost of the chimera had left the Fire Temple nearly broken. He dismissed the Air Temple under prophet Kelno as a farcical remnant with very little remaining power, reduced to a handful of rooms. He identified the Water Temple under Belsornig as the true formidable rival. He also revealed that Vesrek, an ally of Belsornig, had arranged the chimera purchase as a calculated win-win scheme against both the Fire and Earth temples.

When Zinnia used deception to probe Hartsch's ambitions, Hartsch openly expressed belief in the "vengeance power of the earth" and a preference for a destructive, earthquake-like leadership style over Romag's passive approach. He responded to Zinnia's suggestion with thinly veiled intent: "I would always, if a position were to suddenly have opened itself, I would absolutely like to think that my faith and my leadership qualities would be considered." The party interpreted this as a signal that Hartsch wants Romag removed and would take command if given the opportunity. The party subsequently agreed to proceed with assassinating Romag and installing Hartsch as Earth Temple leader. Last known location: the party's private quarters within the Earth Temple.

## Alrrem

The prophet and leader of the Fire Temple, not personally encountered but discussed extensively. He was originally a dominant figure in the great Battle of Emridy Meadows a decade prior and has been struggling to hold power since that catastrophic defeat. He has recently suffered two major setbacks: the loss of his werewolves and the financially ruinous purchase of the chimera. Hartsch described him as nearly broken as a result. He dispatched the Fire Temple warlock and the chimera to assassinate the party, which Romag interpreted as an act of war. Romag subsequently ordered the party to deal with Alrrem directly, describing the moment as ideal given Alrrem's weakened state. Current status: alive but weakened, location within the Fire Temple.

---

## Belsornig

The prophet and head of the Water Temple, not personally encountered. Identified by Hartsch as a cunning manipulator who arranged the chimera purchase through his ally Vesrek and Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader. Hartsch characterized the scheme as a win-win: either the chimera eliminates the party (damaging the Earth Temple) or the Fire Temple bankrupts itself paying for the beast. The Water Temple under Belsornig was described by Hartsch as a formidable rival and the true prize to eliminate. His name also appears in the ledger recovered from the warlock's body. Current status: alive, location within the Water Temple.

---

## Kelno

The prophet of the Air Temple, mentioned only by Hartsch during the private briefing. Hartsch dismissed Kelno and the Air Temple outright as a joke with very little remaining power or territory, reduced to a handful of rooms. Not encountered in person. Current status: alive, confined to minimal Air Temple territory.

---

## Vesrek

An ally of Belsornig, the Water Temple prophet, identified in the ledger recovered from the Fire Temple warlock's body. Vesrek arranged the purchase of the chimera for the Fire Temple through Captain Varsik of the Crimson Trader, connecting the Water Temple to the chimera attack as a manipulative scheme against both the Earth and Fire temples. Not encountered in person. Current status and location unknown.

---

## Fire Temple Warlock

A servant of Alrrem who led the chimera on a chain through the party's southern door during their attempted long rest, smashing the door inward and declaring that Alrrem the all-consuming had sent his champion to break their bones and leave nothing but meat for the earth. He used Eldritch Blast against Sequioa and commanded the chimera until Zephyr ripped the control chain from his hands, leaving him screaming in terror. A bugbear attempted to intervene in the chaos and was caught alongside the warlock in the chimera's devastating breath attack, badly burning both. The warlock, badly burned and barely standing, screamed that they were all mad before Zinnia silenced him permanently with a flurry of devastating blows. On his body the party found: a wax-sealed ledger bearing the crest of the Crimson Trader, a heavy gold amulet bearing the eight-pointed symbol of the Fire Temple worth 100 gold pieces, a potion of fire resistance he never reached for during the chaos, and a masterwork whip of alchemically treated red leather. Current status: dead.

## Captain Varsik

Not encountered in person. Named in the ledger recovered from the warlock as the captain of the Crimson Trader, the vessel through which the chimera was purchased and delivered on behalf of the Fire Temple, with Vesrek arranging the transaction. Current status and location unknown.
