## Pasmarie Clee
Pasmarie Clee was a resident of Sheernobb from whom the party had previously recovered stolen honey. The party returned her honey following their skirmish with the Black Earth cultists and received their agreed reward in exchange.

## Terjon
Terjon was a cleric at the Temple of St. Cuthbert in Hommlet. Zinnia offered him a magical trident as a tithe in exchange for resurrection magic to revive Calmer. Despite Terjon's prior dismissiveness toward the party, the tithe was accepted and Calmer was revived. Terjon's temple had a policy of withholding healing magic from non-followers.

## Jaroo
Jaroo was a druid in Hommlet who met with the party on multiple occasions. He reiterated the importance of securing the Chest of Rao and warned that followers of St. Cuthbert like Terjon might withhold healing from non-believers. After the party cleared the Moathouse and killed Lareth, Jaroo convened with the local council and asserted that the party's next vital step was to travel to Nulb to infiltrate the Temple's operations there, identify who was truly in charge, and disrupt their machinations. He later expressed deep suspicion about the Circle of the Scarlet Moon, finding them too showy and ecstatic for true druids and suggesting their practices were more religious than natural.

## Burne
Burne was a wizard operating alongside Rufus in Hommlet. The party confirmed Burne's interest in the Chest of Rao during an early visit, though his priorities appeared more political than altruistic. Following the deaths of Rannos and Gremag, Burne and Rufus approached the party the morning after, confirming that Elmo had sent rangers after the supposedly missing chest and urging the party to deal with the Moathouse. Burne was present at the council meeting where the party presented proof of the Temple's resurgence and Lareth's death.

## Rufus
Rufus operated alongside Burne in Hommlet and shared similar political interests regarding the Chest of Rao. He joined Burne in urging the party to address the Moathouse threat after the deaths of Rannos and Gremag and was present at the final council meeting confirming the Temple's resurgence.

## Robert Deakin
Robert Deakin was a gnome who owned and operated "The Cheap Forester" tavern in Ostverk. He insisted the party purchase food before he would speak with them. He shared several pieces of intelligence: corroborating that a powerful chest had been lost to bandits with many groups searching for it; dismissing rumors of a "big evil power" as lies, insisting they were merely bandits based on his experience at the Battle of Emiry Meadows; and theorizing that the chest's true purpose was to secure a treaty with the Kingdom of Celene, which Verbobonc intended to use to wage war on the gnomes and seize their gem mines. As a gnome, Deakin was pleased the alliance had not formed. He also noted that the elves at Caer Ostverk and Lord Denelu would pay well for information on the chest, and revealed he was related to the Clee family of Sheernobb.

## Rithelm Drake
Rithelm Drake was a human soldier encountered at "The Cheap Forester" tavern in Ostverk. He was a large man with a square face and rough pink skin, described elsewhere as having green eyes. He initially demanded a drink from the party but softened upon realizing they were adventurers and bought them one instead. When questioned about a monastery, he was initially confused but recalled an abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner, providing the party's primary lead. He lamented that he had once considered being an adventurer but had settled down with a wife and three children.

## Gonnathrad Kevanarial
Gonnathrad Kevanarial was a High Elf Diplomat encountered at "The Cheap Forester" in Ostverk. He was a skinny man with long wavy black hair, grey eyes, and a fancy red tunic. He became overexcited at the prospect of acting as a quest giver, apparently having consumed too many adventure stories, and offered to write a five-page backstory for the party. He nearly divulged information about the monastery before remembering his actual duty to find adventurers to hunt bandits. The party carefully withheld specifics about the monastery while feeding his enthusiasm, and he issued them an official letter of permit hiring them to pursue a lead regarding the chest near a monastery by Gallow's Corner, promising 100 gold pieces upon presentation to Caer Ostverk. He remained confused about why they were focused on an abandoned monastery but his romanticized view of adventure overrode his professional curiosity.

## Brother Eldin (Brother Eldrin)
Brother Eldin was a resident of the Abbey of Beory located in the mountains near Gallow's Corner. His stone guardian in the ancient library tested the party with a riddle about the eternal cycles of nature before granting them passage deeper into the monastery. Brother Eldin himself was found alive but wounded after the collapse of the ritual cavern. He was relieved to learn Tharok had been killed and declared the Abbey of Beory safe once more. He rewarded the party with 70 gold pieces, 400 silver pieces, 400 copper pieces each, a Scroll of Web, and a Potion of Greater Healing. He delivered crucial lore about the Chest of Rao, explaining it was an artifact of great good built by the high priests of Rao, patron of peace and harmony, and warned that possessing it would attract both evil forces seeking to use it as a magical battery and good forces from the Archclericy of Veluna seeking to reclaim it. He urged the party to return the chest rather than risk the danger it would draw.

## Tharok
Tharok was the High Cultist of the Earth Temple operating within the abandoned monastery near Gallow's Corner. His skin was mottled like cracked stone and his eyes burned with fervent intensity. He led three robed cultists in a ritual centered on the Chest of Rao within a vast underground chamber carved with elemental motifs. When the party interrupted his ritual, he declared them defilers and threatened that the earth would swallow them whole. He used earth magic to cause violent earthquakes throughout the chamber, knocking party members to the ground and disrupting Calmer's blessing. He struck down Jerry with devastating elemental attacks and hurled Zephyr backward with a powerful blow. He used his remaining strength to paralyze both Zephyr and Zinnia with Hold Person. Despite being reduced to a single hit point after Sequoia knocked the Chest of Rao from the altar and the party pressed the attack, he was ultimately felled when Zinnia struck him for 14 points of damage after being granted advantage from Calmer's Guiding Bolt. His death triggered the collapse of the ritual cavern.

## Jerry
Jerry was a soldier of Verbobonc found imprisoned in the monastery's torture chamber alongside Warda. Despite being a prisoner, he and Warda engaged in heated political arguments reflecting their opposing views on Verbobonc's expansion. After the party explained the threat posed by Tharok's ritual, he agreed to help fight. He provided crucial information about a secret passageway to the deeper temple, knowledge passed down from his grandmother who had once worshipped Beory in the monastery's halls. He charged bravely against Tharok during the battle but was struck down by the high cultist's devastating attacks, his body crumpling under the elemental fury.

## Warda
Warda was a gnome nationalist found imprisoned alongside Jerry in the monastery's torture chamber. She and Jerry argued constantly about Verbobonc's imperial expansion despite sharing a cell. After the party explained Tharok's threat, she agreed to assist in the fight. She opened the combat against Tharok, landing a single strike after two misses, and continued fighting even after witnessing Jerry's death, her hatred for imperial forces now redirected toward the cultist threat. She was struck by Tharok for 29 damage at one point but survived the encounter.

## Rannos
Rannos was a trader in Hommlet operating as an agent of the Deep Temple of Elemental Evil alongside Gremag. He responded to the party's note requesting a meeting with suspicion, refusing their suggested location behind the carpenter's field and instead inviting them to his establishment after dark. When the party entered his inner office and attempted to negotiate for the Temple to leave the region, Rannos and Gremag mocked them. He demanded the Chest of Rao. With Gremag dead and the party focused on him, Rannos was severely wounded by Sequoia for 26 points of damage at the end of one session, then dispatched by Calmer's Spiritual Weapon when he had only 2 hit points remaining in the following session. His body was subsequently dismembered by Thorne to prevent use of Speak with Dead, and his bloodied clothes were abandoned outside town to simulate a departure. Ostler Goodingoot was successfully bluffed with a story that Rannos and Gremag had urgently left town after securing a magical chest.

## Gremag
Gremag was Rannos's bodyguard and fellow Temple of Elemental Evil operative in Hommlet. He initiated combat immediately when negotiations broke down, attacking Zinnia without warning. In the first round he delivered two swift and brutal strikes, severely injuring Zinnia and then knocking her unconscious. He was ultimately killed by Calmer's critical blow while Zinnia lay dying. His body was dismembered by Thorne alongside Rannos's to prevent Speak with Dead.

## Thorne (Duke)
Thorne, known as "the Duke," was a mercenary man-at-arms hired by Rannos. The party lured him out from the shop's back area and quickly overwhelmed him in a surprise attack, with Sequoia delivering the final strike to knock him unconscious. Under interrogation he confirmed he was a simple hired hand with no deep loyalty to the cult. He identified Rannos and Gremag as agents of the Deep Temple and warned of severe retribution. He advised the party to dismember the bodies and remove tongues to prevent Speak with Dead. He revealed his cleaning skills and offered his services on retainer for 20 gold pieces per month plus 100 gold pieces per incident. The party agreed to pay him 100 gold pieces for the first cleanup, and he was untied and released. He later accepted a 50 gold piece fee to clean up after the Lareth operation and confirmed his cynical, money-driven nature and lack of loyalty to the cult.

## Elmo
Elmo was a member of Hommlet's town committee who habitually performed the role of a drunken buffoon. Zinnia's natural 20 Insight roll confirmed the party's suspicions that he was feigning inebriation and working as an agent for Verbobonc. When Zephyr exposed his subterfuge, Elmo immediately dropped the act and agreed to speak privately with the party as equals. He was dismayed to learn the chest had supposedly left with Rannos and Gremag and rushed off to contact the rangers to pursue it. Before departing he gave the party an urgent task: return to the Moathouse immediately to investigate and destroy whatever evil presence remained there. He emphasized the political sensitivity, noting that Verbobonc could not send troops without risking conflict with the gnomes of the Kron Hills.

## Ostler Goodingoot (Osler Goodingut)
Ostler Goodingoot was the tavern keeper of the Inn of the Welcome Wench in Hommlet and a member of the local council. He questioned the party about the sudden departure of Rannos and Gremag and was successfully bluffed with the story that the merchants had urgently left town after securing a magical chest. He directed the party to speak with Jaroo when they passed through Hommlet en route to the Hall of the Scarlet Moon. He was present at the council meeting where the party presented proof of the Temple's resurgence.

## Lareth the Beautiful
Lareth the Beautiful was the leader of the Fire Temple faction operating from the Moathouse. The party had previously been used by him to eliminate Earth Temple gangs, unknowingly expanding his power base. When the party confronted him, they attempted to lie by claiming the Deep Temple had taken the chest and that Rannos and Gremag had been acting on Lareth's behalf, hoping to cause infighting within the Temple. Despite the party succeeding on their group persuasion check, Lareth rolled a high check and saw through their deception. He accused them of treachery and, when Zephyr mocked him with "The only person here that's backstabbing is you," immediately called his guards to arms and initiated combat. During the battle he summoned a Mirror Image of himself, severely wounded Calmer with Inflict Wounds, and used Hold Person to paralyze both Zephyr and Zinnia. He was reduced to a single hit point before Sequoia, who had not been paralyzed, struck the killing blow. His body and quarters yielded a Mace of Smiting, magical Plate Mail, a Ring of Free Action, a silver service set worth 2,000 GP, a fire opal necklace worth 3,500 GP, and gems worth 800 GP.

## Eryk Velkori
Eryk Velkori was the leader of a Rhennee caravan encamped near Nulb. When the party first encountered him, he was consumed by grief over his missing sister Serena, clutching a bottle of rum and weeping openly while the camp sat unnaturally still with the air thick with ash and river rot. He explained that Serena had vanished after a night at the Boatman's Tavern and believed she had been taken through a place called the Iron Lash by the Zelrads, the Reapers, or Nisera's Talons. When Sequoia jokingly asked about a map to hell, he responded that he was already living in one. He provided crucial intelligence about Nulb's criminal underworld, naming the Crimson Reapers under Zelt the Rat and identifying Madame Selentis as the true power in Nulb. He warned that if Madame Selentis took notice of the party, things would go very badly. He protested his mother Vethra's divination attempts, fearing they would make her sick or kill her. After the party rescued Serena and returned her to the camp, Eryk's eyes lit up with joy and the entire camp erupted in music and dancing. He declared the party family, invited them to spend the night celebrating, and formally welcomed them as members of the Rhennee clan, offering protection and friendship in Nulb while warning that not all friends could be trusted. The party discovered they had been credited 500 gold pieces for Serena's rescue. Eryk later described the Hall of the Scarlet Moon as a place where a druid named Elazar taught the rite of the wicker giant, noting that many groups had camped there in festive anticipation though he found it odd that people seemed to be burning fire all day.

## Vethra Velkori
Vethra Velkori was Eryk's elderly mother and the Rhennee clan's diviner. She sat cross-legged in the shadows of her wagon surrounded by bones, blackened cards, and a copper bowl smelling of clove and blood. Her clouded blue eyes seemed to look through people rather than at them. When the party chose smoke for the first reading, she burned a lock of braided black hair and revealed visions of a crooked house with shifting shadows, a tavern with too many secrets called the Waterside, and a man with a red sash who dealt in whispers and iron. For the second bone reading she tossed wolf knuckles and pigeon vertebrae into a shallow bowl, declaring that Serena might be on her knees but was not broken. Despite Eryk's protests that the divination would make her sick or kill her, she drew from her faded Tarokka deck, revealing The Chain, The Betrayer, and The Flooded Gate, then drew a fourth card with a hiss of breath depicting a crimson-painted barge sailing low on the Velverdyva, heavy with souls that no longer sang—confirming Serena had been taken there, bound and traded. She then collapsed back from exhaustion.

## Serena
Serena was Eryk Velkori's sister, taken from the Boatman's Tavern as payment for an unspecified debt. She was held in the pits of the Iron Lash brothel by Elvanna. The party paid 500 gold pieces for her release. When she was brought out she appeared dazed but defiant, and as she left she retorted to Elvanna's mockery with a wish for her to go to hell. Once back at the Rhennee camp she explained she had been used as leverage for a debt she didn't know about and that her father Eryk was being coerced into ambushes along the forest road.

## Ketta
Ketta was a serving wench at the Boatman's Tavern in Nulb. When questioned by the party she nervously revealed there had been a fight involving Serena and the Rhennee, but she was visibly terrified and pointed fearfully toward Captain Tolub rather than providing further details.

## Skole
Skole was the owner and bartender of the Boatman's Tavern in Nulb. He was visibly shocked when the party returned alive after Tolub had told everyone he had disposed of them. Taking the party into his back room, he confided that he despised both Tolub and Lodriss, the tavern's actual owner, and wished to be free of them both. Crucially, he redirected the party to Dick Rentsch at the Waterside Hostel as the correct contact for the Earth Temple, revealing that Tolub worked for the fire prophet rather than the earth faction.

## Captain Tolub
Captain Tolub was a boisterous and intimidating ship captain with river pirate connections who frequented the Boatman's Tavern in Nulb. He had told everyone after the party left that he had disposed of them, claiming they were meddlers who didn't belong. He initially made an awful joke that his crew laughed at with exaggerated enthusiasm. After some negotiation and charm from the party he agreed to buy them drinks. He confirmed that Serena had been taken to the Iron Lash as payment for something and mockingly revealed the brothel was located upstairs in the same building. When the party later sought to contact the Earth Temple, Tolub directed them to the Hall of the Scarlet Moon—a deliberate act of betrayal, as he worked for the fire prophet and sent them into a fire temple trap rather than to Romag of the Earth Temple. Skole confirmed Tolub's treachery after the party survived.

## Elvanna
Elvanna was the madam running the Iron Lash brothel located above the Boatman's Tavern in Nulb. She was a gorgeous woman with orange-red hair who spoke with a posh accent. She held Serena as compensation for damages caused by the Rhennee at the Boatman's Tavern and demanded 500 gold pieces for her release. When the party paid without negotiation she remarked she might have asked for more had she known they were that desperate. After Serena was released and retorted with a wish for Elvanna to go to hell, Elvanna mockingly wished to see her again.

## Madame Selentis (Nisera)
Madame Selentis, also known as Nisera, was described by Eryk Velkori as the true power in Nulb—a member of a disgraced noble family from Verbobonc who now controlled everything like a spider in a web. Eryk warned the party explicitly that if she ever took notice of them, things would go very badly. A dead man named Elfar Twell was observed hanging from the gallows in Gallows Square, executed on her orders for the crime of crossing her, illustrating the reach of her power. She did not appear directly in these sessions.

## Zelt the Rat
Zelt the Rat was named by Eryk Velkori as the leader of the Crimson Reapers, a criminal organization seeking to dominate Nulb's criminal activities and funnel supplies through the area. He did not appear directly in these sessions.

## Dick Rentsch
Dick Rentsch was a gruff proprietor at the Waterside Hostel in Nulb and a contact for the Earth Temple. He initially sized up the party with suspicion and warned he ran a tight operation that didn't tolerate trouble, but his demeanor shifted to excitement when they mentioned having something of interest for the Earth Temple. He assigned the party a test mission: ambush and kill a group of gnolls and their handlers being recruited for the fire temple that night. After the party completed this successfully, he led them out of Nulb along a weed-choked road to the Temple of Elemental Evil, describing the main entrance's sealed bronze doors and guiding them through a side entrance. Inside he appeared disturbingly at ease, describing the temple as his happy place and explaining that once the Earth Temple destroyed the other false prophets the place would be filled with supplicants. He led the party down a pit into a skeleton-filled corridor, warning them not to disturb the dead, and guided them past guards who confirmed their status as new recruits for Romag. He asked whether he needed to confiscate their weapons or if they were smart enough not to try anything stupid. He brought them before Romag and later provided them with a map showing gnoll locations for their mission. Whether his act of depositing them in the Air Temple's territory was a deliberate test or something more sinister remained unclear to the party.

## Wat
Wat was the bartender at the Waterside Hostel in Nulb. While the party waited for Dick Rentsch he attempted to extract information about their business. When the party deflected, Wat seemed relieved, mentioned someone named Dala, and warned them not to drink the beer, serving them gin instead.

## Albrith (Albreth)
Albrith was one of two robed men encountered at Camp Number Two of the Hall of the Scarlet Moon who identified themselves as circuits of the Scarlet Moon. When the party asked whether they were connected to the Temple of Elemental Evil, Albrith and his companion adopted threatening poses and declared their allegiance by stating "fire, water, earth, or air," confirming their connection to elemental evil. He and Ingulf then unleashed devastating magical fire attacks that severely wounded the party and knocked Calmer unconscious before the party used magical dust to turn invisible and flee.

## Ingulf
Ingulf was Albrith's companion at Camp Number Two of the Hall of the Scarlet Moon, also identified as a circuit of the Scarlet Moon and a fire temple operative. He joined Albrith in the threatening declaration of elemental allegiance and participated in the magical fire assault on the party. Unable to see the invisible party and distracted by a rampaging bear Zephyr had unleashed by cutting its leash, Ingulf could only watch in fury as the party escaped.

## Romag
Romag was the High Priest of the Earth Temple, operating in lavishly decorated chambers deep within the Temple of Elemental Evil. He was brought before the party by Dick Rentsch as their formal contact within the Earth Temple hierarchy. He greeted them warmly and was pleased Dick Rentsch had finally brought him something worthy of his time. He requested and received the Chest of Rao from the party. He laughed heartily at the party's ignorance of the temple's true scope, explaining that the surface was merely the beginning—the true power lay in deeper levels descending into the pit of evil itself, with the first two levels divided among Earth, Fire, Water, and Air temples all in constant conflict, and deeper secrets below. His mission for the party was to destroy the other temples so the Earth Temple could seize ultimate power, promising that success would earn them the badge of Earth.

## Elazar
Elazar was a druid described by Eryk Velkori as teaching the rite of the wicker giant at the Hall of the Scarlet Moon to restore nature's balance. He did not appear directly in these sessions. Jaroo found the Circle of the Scarlet Moon's practices suspiciously ecstatic and more religious than natural.

## Spugnoir
Spugnoir was a person in Hommlet who had told the party a story about the Circle of Eight—powerful wizards including Mordenkainen and Bigby—who had come to the Temple of Elemental Evil and performed mighty magic to seal its main bronze doors. He did not appear directly in these sessions.
