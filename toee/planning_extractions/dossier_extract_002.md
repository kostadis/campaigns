## Hestort Flintshine (Tort)

Hestort Flintshine, a thin, five-foot-tall man who goes by "Tort," was found by the party after they defeated the bandits who had targeted him on the evening of Planting 18, 579. Zephyr stood over him and questioned him. Under magical intimidation from Zephyr that left him urinating himself, Tort revealed that he works for "the Duke" (the same Duke the traders had previously offered to help the party manage their belongings), who had paid him to acquire some plans. He claimed to work for Terjon, though the party doubted this. He knew the Duke was working with shady people but did not know the Duke's employer, only that it was "someone in the village." He could not identify who was ultimately in charge. After breaking down in tears during questioning, Tort declared he was leaving and would never be seen again. Last known status: fled.

## Erin Darkmoon

Erin Darkmoon was the brigand leader from the watchtower, referenced by Zinnia in the midday session of Planting 20, 579. Prior to the events described, she had been captured or interrogated by the party and provided them with intelligence about the moathouse's operations — specifically that a man named Lareth the Beautiful ran the moathouse. She gave this information in exchange for the party's promise to kill Lareth. Last known status: not present in current scenes; referenced as a past source.

## Snej

Snej is referenced by Zinnia as a deceased operative who worked for the water temple, mortal enemies of the fire temple. His identity and affiliation were used by the party as cover during their infiltration of Lareth's operation — Zinnia and Zephyr told Lareth they had eliminated Snej as a demonstration of loyalty. Snej is also mentioned as the source of information the party used to understand why they were at the moathouse. Last known status: dead prior to these events.

## Lareth the Beautiful

Lareth the Beautiful is the leader of the moathouse dungeon operation, revealed on Planting 20, 579. He appeared from behind a door in a barracks area when Zephyr and the party, disguised in fire temple robes, bluffed their way in with a guard escort. Lareth is described as a stunning man who exudes evil. He accepted the party's fabricated story — that they had eliminated Snej of the water temple and wished to defect to the fire temple — and declared their loyalty proven. Rather than immediately granting them reinforcements to seize the abbey, Lareth pivoted with what appeared to be a calculating gleam in his eye and sent them on a retrieval mission: a chest he described as a piece of art he is fond of, stolen by bandits in the Kron Hills near Sheernob whom he characterized as poseurs pretending to be earth elemental cultists. He warned the party not to tell anyone about their deal. Zephyr noted afterward that Lareth may have seen through their disguise, or at minimum was calculating something unknown. Zinnia later realized that Lareth had actually sent them to eliminate his elemental rivals — the Black Earth cult — thereby removing competition and consolidating his own power. The chest he sought was in fact the Viscounty's stolen diplomatic gift intended for the Kingdom of Celene. Last known status: alive at the moathouse.

## Rufus

Rufus, an agent of Verbobonc stationed in Hommlet alongside Burne, met with the party on Planting 20, 579 upon their return from the moathouse. He is described as wearing a white blouse, green velvet pantaloons, and a red sash. He questioned the party about evidence of the cult and became excited when Sequoia mentioned finding the leader, asking if they had killed him. When Sequoia revealed the leader's name was Lareth and that the party intended to use him as an asset rather than kill him, Rufus accepted this. He provided crucial information about the stolen chest: it was the entry selected from the Viscounty's annual art competition, intended as a diplomatic gift left at the border of the Kingdom of Celene. A mission to deliver the chest was attacked and the chest went missing. He noted the official position is that the elves accepted the chest, meaning its reappearance would embarrass the Viscount. He did not instruct the party directly but indicated that recovering the chest and those who stole it would be of great assistance. Last known status: at Rufus's tower in Hommlet.

## Burne

Burne, the other Verbobonc agent in Hommlet, was present at the same meeting on Planting 20, 579. He is described as wearing a leisure suit. Burne argued that the cultists the party found might not be genuine temple remnants but rather agents of another power seeking to destabilize the Viscounty by making it appear the Temple of Elemental Evil had returned — which would undermine the Viscount's legitimacy. Sequoia privately judged him to be covering his own failings. Burne praised Sequoia's plan to remain embedded with Lareth as brilliant and deduced it was likely Zinnia's idea. He confirmed the party's course of action and noted the political stakes of the chest's reappearance. Last known status: at Rufus's tower in Hommlet.

## Glennddarc

Glennddarc is the Headgnome of Sheernobb, encountered at the Moot Hall on Planting 21, 579. He is an elderly gnome with brown wrinkled skin and long white wild hair, accompanied by a weasel that woke him from sleep during the party's visit. He was visibly annoyed that the party was focused on bandits rather than the giants he considered the real threat to his village. He dismissed Captain Gnonsbim's bandit concerns as electioneering, suggesting Gnonsbim had romantic feelings for Pasmarie Clee. He directed the party toward Pasmarie's beehives for bandit information and offered greater rewards for dealing with the giants. He fell back asleep before the party could respond. Last known status: at the Moot Hall in Sheernobb.

## Captain Gnonsbim

Captain Gnonsbim is the captain of the guard in Sheernobb, encountered at the Honey Haven Tavern on Planting 21, 579. He approached the party directly, confirmed they were adventurers, and commissioned them to locate the bandit camp without waiting for their response. His offered rewards: 25 gold for the camp location, 10 per dead bandit, and 25 for the leader. He also requested a political favor — that the party allow him to give a speech claiming credit for the success when they return evidence, as he is running for re-election. In exchange he promised custom-forged magical weaponry. He paid for a round of mead for the party, told them to speak with Pasmarie Clee, and departed. Headgnome Glennddarc later confirmed privately that Gnonsbim's focus on bandits is electoral posturing and that he is believed to be romantically interested in Pasmarie. Last known status: in Sheernobb.

## Mari Clee

Mari Clee is one of the owners of the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb, encountered on Planting 21, 579. She is described as a small gnome figure who was humming and shaking her hips behind the bar, and who pulled a hedgehog out of a mug upon noticing the party. She welcomed them enthusiastically and managed room and meal pricing, skillfully leveraging Zephyr's stinginess toward his companions by confirming to the group that the leader would be paying for everything. She consistently charged Zephyr for items throughout the visit. Last known status: at the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb.

## Gurdy

Gurdy is a well-traveled gnome bard encountered at the Honey Haven Inn on Planting 21, 579. He approached the party's table asking for coin to play a song, which Zephyr paid. After performing, he sat uninvited, had Mari pour him a drink at Zephyr's expense, and provided local intelligence: that bandit attacks on Sheernobb had become more organized than usual. He suggested visiting the Moot Hall and offered his services as a bard to accompany the party. Calmer strongly objected to adding Gurdy to the group. Zephyr expressed interest in working with a bard to antagonize Calmer. Last known status: at the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb.

## Glennon Clee

Glennon Clee is an inn owner in Sheernobb, presumably related to Mari Clee, who had barrels of enchanted mead stolen in the bandit raids. He commissioned the party to recover the barrels on Planting 21, 579, offering free lodging for a week and a small keg of enchanted mead as reward. When Zinnia returned on the evening of Planting 22 with the recovered barrel, Glennon confirmed the reward: the enchanted mead grants enhanced persuasion and charisma when consumed, useful for negotiations, performance, and social interactions. Last known status: at the Honey Haven Inn in Sheernobb.

## Quinna Poul

Quinna Poul is the half-orc cook at the Honey Haven Inn, encountered on Planting 21, 579. She wears an apron and smells of the kitchen fire. She commissioned the party to hunt a rare and dangerous gnarled boar in the Gnarley Forest, promising a special enchanted meal that would bless and strengthen them. On Planting 22, she marked the boar's location on Zephyr's map. The party successfully returned with the boar. She announced a celebratory feast for the Harvest Festival on the 30th of the month and promised the party a special dinner the following day. On Planting 23, she rushed out to inform the party that some of the feast's ingredients had been magically animated and scattered through the village, requesting the party retrieve them. She confirmed this was the broccoli and the potatoes, which the party recovered. Last known status: at the Honey Haven Inn kitchen in Sheernobb.

## Pasmarie Clee

Pasmarie Clee is a gnome beekeeper and druid of Sheernobb, encountered at her beehive farm a quarter mile southeast of Sheernobb on Planting 21, 579. She is petite with a kind weathered face, unruly curls, and an apron stained with honeycomb wax and pollen. She reported that a shipment of honey bound for Verbobonc had been ambushed on the trade road north of the village. The surviving guards described the attackers chanting "Black Earth" and carrying stone weapons. She suspected they were not ordinary bandits. She offered enchanted honey as a reward. On Planting 22, she appeared at the Honey Haven Inn and marked the caravan attack site on Zephyr's map, confirming the guards had since left town. Captain Gnonsbim directed the party to her, and Headgnome Glennddarc suggested Gnonsbim had romantic feelings for her. Last known status: at her beehive farm outside Sheernobb.

## Grindin Well-Read

Grindin Well-Read is the gnome owner of Grindin Books on the northern branch of Sheernobb's main road, encountered on Planting 21, 579. He is described as sitting on a massive cushioned chair behind a large desk, surrounded by hundreds of books, maps, and scrolls. He was annoyed by the party's interruption and initially unhelpful regarding bandits, instead complaining that his invitation to an unspecified conference had been stolen. He suggested enchanted protective wards for caravans as a solution. He eventually confirmed that Pasmarie Clee had useful bandit information. Zephyr purchased a map of the local area from him. Zinnia also noted a book titled "Map of the Elemental Nexus" that could prove valuable for future research on the elemental planes. Last known status: at Grindin Books in Sheernobb.

## Aunt Emma (Green Hag)

Aunt Emma appeared in the Gnarley Forest on Planting 22, 579, posing as a kindly old woman in a brown dress and green woolen shirt, offering sweets to travelers. She lured the party toward a gingerbread house — a single-room dwelling with a wood oven producing poisonous plum pie. Zinnia identified her as a hag; Zephyr initially misunderstood but quickly recognized the threat and drew his sword. Her illusion dropped, revealing her true form as a green hag with green skin, clawed hands, and fanged mouth. She threatened to eat the party, then when she realized she would not survive, offered to reveal her treasure's location in exchange for her life. Zephyr killed her regardless. The party recovered a hat of Wizardry and a book titled "The Hag's Guide to 101 Recipes Of Children" from her dwelling. Last known status: dead.

## Thalsor of the Stone

Thalsor is a druid of the Black Earth cult who led the fortified encampment in the Kron Hills where the stolen honey and Lareth's chest were held. He was encountered on Planting 23, 579. His compound featured stone barricades, guards in stone-studded leather armor with stone weapons, a temple to an earth deity with a stone statue, and a stone golem under his command. He wielded a stone staff adorned with jagged crystals and could summon mud mephits and stone golems, as well as conjure hailstorms of sharp stones. When the party's initial assault nearly killed Sequoia and Zinnia and ultimately killed Calmer, Thalsor offered to negotiate — he would return Calmer's body undamaged if the party revealed who sent them. He threatened to mutilate the corpse to prevent resurrection. Overnight he continued shouting threats. The following morning, Planting 24, while Thalsor was distracted guarding against an expected frontal assault and making threats, Sequoia used a potion of gaseous form to retrieve Calmer's body. The party then focused fire on Thalsor and killed him quickly, leaving only a mud mephit which was also dispatched. The stone golem became immobile after his death. Zinnia and Zephyr subsequently realized Thalsor's Black Earth cult was a rival elemental faction that Lareth had deliberately sent them to destroy, thereby consolidating fire temple power. Last known status: dead.

## Calmer

Calmer is a cleric of St. Cuthbert who had been traveling with the party, encountered again at the campsite outside the moathouse on Planting 19, 579. He turned the zombies in the moathouse dungeon with a declaration invoking St. Cuthbert's power, enabling the party to kill them systematically. He was increasingly used by Zephyr as a disposable asset — notably sent into the pool room alone to retrieve treasure while a giant crayfish attacked him, with Zephyr and Sequoia waiting until the treasure was secured before helping. He grew visibly drunk on mead at the Honey Haven Inn and objected strongly to Gurdy the bard joining the party. In the battle at the Black Earth compound, Calmer healed fallen party members during the chaotic initial assault but was ultimately killed by Thalsor. Thalsor threatened to mutilate the body to prevent resurrection; the party recovered the intact corpse on Planting 24 through Sequoia's gaseous infiltration, meaning resurrection remains possible. Last known status: dead, body recovered and intact.
