## Jaroo

Jaroo is the keeper of the druid grove in Hommlet, a follower of Beory, and was sent to the village to observe events following the defeat of the Temple of Elemental Evil. He met with the party twice during these sessions.

In the first meeting, Jaroo showed visible disgust toward Zephyr upon seeing the Tiefling, believing that Tieflings are outside the natural order and have no place on the prime material plane. He spoke with Sequoia instead, explaining the history of the Moathouse as a former outpost of the Temple of Elemental Evil that served as an advance base for raids and subjugation, with the intent to spread the Temple's power in ever-growing rings. He connected the current patterns of bandit activity to the original Temple's methods and urged the party to investigate the Moathouse. He instructed them to report their findings to him and him alone, warning that agents of the Temple may be present in Hommlet and that Burne and Rufus would use any information to restrict local freedoms.

He distrusts Burne and Rufus, believing their castle construction is not about defense but about extending the Archbishop of Verbobonc's power and absorbing the faithful of Beory into the Church of St. Cuthbert. He similarly distrusts Terjon's goals.

When the party returned with the stone and bandit confession, Jaroo was disappointed with the evidence and told them they must go deeper into the Moathouse to find more proof. He asked who else they had spoken to, and when Zinia told him the truth, Jaroo was pleased and offered them the blessing of Beory and a healing potion.

At the village council meeting on Planting 19, Jaroo presented the party's findings but framed the narrative as brigands masquerading as Temple agents rather than actual Temple resurgence. He supported the party's decision to come to him first. After the meeting, he invited the party with apparent amusement to help with end-of-month festival decorating and the location committee, prompting Sequoia and Zinia to flee.

## Terjon

Terjon is the high priest and Prefect of the Temple of St. Cuthbert in Hommlet. He met with Zinia privately in session 2, recognizing Zinia as a recent convert. He agreed to allow Calmer to join the adventuring party but made one specific request: that whatever information the party gathered be shared with him and him alone, citing that the safety of the people of Oerth might hang in the balance.

Local gossip suggests that Y'dey, the head cleric, mysteriously disappeared and that Terjon played a role in her disappearance, as he is now in charge.

When the party returned from their first Moathouse excursion with the eye-and-tear stone, Terjon dismissed the evidence contemptuously, calling it a painted rock and complaining that they had released a prisoner he could have interrogated. He declared the stone worthless and said he wanted to keep it, but when Zephyr challenged him on why he would keep a worthless object, Terjon winced as though in physical pain and tossed the stone back. He muttered that Zephyr was right, it was worthless.

Zinia chose not to accompany Calmer to report to Terjon after the second Moathouse excursion, unwilling to endure Terjon's drone. Calmer reported alone.

At the village council meeting, Terjon was absent. Calmer attempted to insist that the Prefect be included in future council meetings, but was shut down by Sequoia. Burne and Rufus were dismissive of any claim of Temple resurgence and framed the findings as local bandit activity not worthy of disturbing the arch-cleric of Verbobonc.

## Burne

Burne is a young mage with a slight build, long brown hair, green eyes, and an aquiline nose. He wears a scuffed mage robe and is in a relationship with Rufus. He is one of the two leaders of the fortification project in Hommlet and a representative of Verbobonc.

When the party first visited the tower, Rufus called him down mid-conversation and apologized for interrupting his experiments. Burne was horrified when told there might be a resurgence of the Temple of Elemental Evil, declaring it impossible.

When the party brought the eye-and-tear stone to him and Rufus at the Inn of the Welcome Wench, Burne initially dismissed it with "This is it?" but then became thoughtful. He listened as Rufus revealed the matter of the copied blueprints. At the end of that conversation, Burne told the party to keep the investigation quiet.

At the village council meeting, Burne expressed dismissiveness about the evidence, stating there was no conclusive proof of Temple resurgence and framing the findings as locally significant but not worth disturbing the arch-cleric. He commended the party for their work and framed their actions as validation of the decision to deputize them.

Last known location: Hommlet, the tower or the Inn of the Welcome Wench.

## Rufus

Rufus is the military commander of the Hommlet area and Burne's partner. He is powerfully built, of average height, with blue eyes, a delicate nose, and golden blond hair. He is a representative of Verbobonc tasked with constructing a castle.

When the party first visited the tower, Rufus attempted to get Sequoia to voluntarily agree to share information exclusively with him. When Sequoia did not bite, Rufus hardened and formally deputized the party, stating that as deputies they were required to share information with him first, and threatening arrest or worse if they explored the Moathouse without accepting his offer.

When the party presented the stone at the Inn, Rufus initially dismissed it but then grew thoughtful. He revealed that the blueprints for the keep had gone missing and reappeared with charcoal dust on them, suggesting someone had copied them. He had moved the plans to a more private location but believed a saboteur was at work among the construction workers. He asked the party to investigate and find the thief, insisting the matter be kept quiet.

At the village council meeting, Rufus stated the party had brought strong evidence of bandit activity but that there was no conclusive evidence of Temple resurgence. He framed it as local, not national, importance and not worthy of involving the arch-cleric of Verbobonc.

Last known location: Hommlet.

## Ostler Goodingoat

Ostler is the innkeeper of the Inn of the Welcome Wench and a member of the village council. He is observant of the party's movements and politically aware.

He greeted the party at the door after their visit to Jaroo's grove, having clearly been informed of the blessing. He ushered them into the kitchen and offered them the job of proving that Furnok was stealing from the inn's guests, offering three free nights as payment. When Zephyr's pickpocketing attempt on Furnok failed and the resulting distraction allowed Zinia to recover loaded dice, Ostler confronted Furnok directly over the evidence.

He stopped Zinia on the way to his room to remind him that rooms were paid through the 19th and conspiratorially expressed pleasure that the party had gone to Jaroo before the outsiders Burne and Rufus. He was clearly monitoring whom the party reported to and in what order.

Last known location: The Inn of the Welcome Wench.

## Furnok

Furnok, described as a fabulous rogue, was present at the Inn of the Welcome Wench. He was the subject of Ostler's suspicions about stealing from guests. When Zephyr failed to pickpocket him directly, Zephyr created a distraction by tipping over Furnok's table, allowing Zinia to recover the dice. The dice were loaded, providing the proof Ostler needed. Furnok rejected Ostler's accusations when confronted. The situation escalated until Elmo, seated at another table, threatened to call the Captain if Furnok did not leave.

Last known location: The Inn of the Welcome Wench, where he was expelled or pressured to leave.

## Elmo

Elmo is an adventurer and ranger, and the son of Captain Zeb Fletcher. He was present at the Inn of the Welcome Wench during the Furnok confrontation, seated at another table. He smelled strongly of alcohol. When the Furnok situation escalated, he intervened with a threat to call the Captain, pressuring Furnok to leave. Sequoia identified him as a drunk.

When Zephyr mentioned needing someone to help find a monster in the Gnarley Forest, Elmo enthusiastically volunteered. He was receptive to the party and presented himself as available for hire.

Last known location: The Inn of the Welcome Wench.

## Spugnoir Balkof

Spugnoir is a magic user staying at the Inn of the Welcome Wench. He first appeared in session 1. During these sessions he twice expected to join the party on an adventure and was passed over or left behind, including when the party departed to return the silver dragon Airgid without him.

When approached about rumors of a secret passage under the Inn, he dismissed the idea but offered instead to lead the party to a lost wizard's laboratory under Emiry Meadows, requesting to split treasures and receive first claim on any arcane scrolls. He asked to join as a fourth companion, and Sequoia agreed.

He became very talkative during the march to Emiry Meadows, sharing lore about the Circle of Eight — Mordenkainen, Bigby, Otiluke, Leomund, and others — explaining that they had appeared at the Battle of Emiry Meadows when some artifact was being used, then traveled to the Temple of Elemental Evil and cast powerful binding spells. He noted that the Circle of Eight operates on balance rather than good or evil, and that their appearance indicated whatever was present threatened that balance. His personal goal is to meet them.

At the laboratory, he was visibly excited by the finds: two potions of gaseous form, a potion of healing, a magical dagger, and most importantly a spellbook containing feather fall, fog cloud, mage armor, magic missile, protection from good and evil, silent image, Tenser's floating disk, unseen servant, gust of wind, misty step, rope trick, fly, gaseous form, magic circle, and sleet storm, as well as a scroll of fog, silent image, gust of wind, and misty step. He declared these would make him much more useful to the party.

He appeared again the morning of Planting 19, eager to depart, and was told to wait while the party attended the council meeting. After the meeting, he joined the party.

Last known location: Hommlet / traveling with the party.

## Elder Kenter

Elder Kenter is the village elder of Hommlet, an older man with white hair who appears reasonably fit and has eyes showing uncommon wisdom. He lives in a well-defended compound with an eight-foot stone wall housing himself, his wife Valsenora, and his four sons with their families and servants.

He received the party after his second son Hawthorne had followed them and reported that they had visited Jaroo, Terjon, Burne, and Rufus all in one day. Kenter was not angry but pointed out that those individuals were all council members and that they had chosen not to present to the council collectively. He identified the full council membership: Jaroo, Rufus, Burne, Ostler, Captain Zeb Fletcher, and Mytch the Miller.

He praised the unified leadership of Hommlet and characterized the bandit activity as a serious threat requiring urgent coordinated response. He concluded by declaring that going forward, the party was expected to come to him first.

He and his wife Valsenora served food and wine. He toasted with the blessing of Beory.

Last known location: His compound in Hommlet.

## Calmer

Calmer is a cleric of St. Cuthbert assigned to join the party at Terjon's approval. He is short at 5'2", with copper-red hair, hazel eyes, a flat nose, and the over-eager look of a religious zealot. His armor was destroyed by green slime during the lower level exploration of the Moathouse.

During the first Moathouse excursion he insisted on leading but fell into the moat with Sequoia when the drawbridge collapsed under the combined weight of the party and four giant frogs. A frog attacked and nearly killed him. Zinia pulled him from the water and patched his wound.

On the second excursion he again insisted on leading but almost immediately had green slime drop on his armor from the arch at the bottom of the stairwell, destroying it. He wept over the loss of his armor. In the lower level he made a loud noise that alerted Lubash the ogre. He nearly died again in the ogre fight. He successfully turned the zombies in the prison corridor, buying the party approximately one minute before more zombies broke through rotting cell doors.

He helped free the prisoners and tended to their distress, though his method of calming the terrified merchant Voss was to lecture him on what the Church of St. Cuthbert does to its enemies, requiring Sequoia to push him aside.

He was excluded from the stakeout operation due to his tendency to make noise and want to talk to people. He reported to Terjon alone after the second excursion at Zinia's direction.

At the council meeting he attempted to insist that Terjon be included in future meetings and was shut down by Sequoia. He continued the habit of announcing himself and his intentions at inopportune moments, and made a pun on his own name ("I think it's important that we call to calm down") with evident glee.

He asked the party to confirm meeting again the next day at the same time before departing the Inn.

Last known location: Hommlet, the Temple of St. Cuthbert or the Inn of the Welcome Wench.

## Lubash

Lubash was an ogre dwelling in the lower level of the Moathouse, stationed in a room near the prisoner pantry. He announced himself by name and declared the party were not temple members and therefore food before attacking with his club. He dealt significant damage before being killed by the combined efforts of Zinia, Zephyr, and Sequoia. His nest contained a magical cloak of elvenkind, which raised questions about which elf had lost it and who had been supplying Lubash with food and prisoners. He had consumed four of the six merchants captured by brigands: Martin, Damien, Eltograph the Elf, and one unnamed prisoner.

Status: Dead.

## Derrick

Derrick was one of two surviving merchant prisoners held in the pantry room of the Moathouse lower level as food for Lubash. He wore a strange purple robe over traveling clothes covered in dirt, blood, and excrement. He was originally from Narwell and was captured by brigands on the road to Hommlet along with five others. He named the victims taken by Lubash before breaking down crying. He and his companion Florian promised a reward if returned safely to Hommlet.

Last known location: The Moathouse exterior campsite in the Duke's care, awaiting return to Hommlet.

---

## Florian

Florian was the second surviving merchant prisoner held alongside Derrick. He was also from Narwell, captured on the road to Hommlet. He provided no additional information beyond what Derrick shared.

Last known location: The Moathouse exterior campsite, awaiting return to Hommlet.

## Jarran

Jarran is a gnome spy sent by the merchants of Lotrimil to investigate the brigands attacking the region. He was captured and placed in the Moathouse pantry alongside the two merchants. He was direct about his identity and mission upon rescue, asking to join the party if they intended to continue exploring. Zinia distrusted him as a fellow spy and declined the offer. Before parting, Jarran gave the party a ring as a promise of future help.

Last known location: Released outside the Moathouse, intending to return to his mission.

## Thorne (Duke)

Thorne, who goes by Duke because his name sounds like a throne, is a man-at-arms hired through Rannos Davl. He is in his late 30s with a weathered face, short brown hair, and piercing green eyes hidden under a leather cap. He wears studded leather armor.

He set up camp outside the Moathouse drawbridge both times the party visited, declining to enter and wishing the party luck. He mocked Calmer's stretching routine during the rest before the lower level descent. When the party emerged with prisoners, he immediately volunteered to guard them and keep them comfortable until the party returned.

His most notable contribution was offering to cook giant frog legs after the first frog fight, claiming they taste like chicken, which drew a glare from Zinia.

Last known location: The Moathouse exterior camp, guarding the rescued prisoners.

## Voss

Voss was a bandit captured alive during the first Moathouse incursion. He was found in the room where the party killed the bandit leader and several others. Under questioning he wept and explained he had become a bandit due to a property he had to sell, gambling debts, and drinking. He confirmed the Moathouse was used because it was considered abandoned after the Temple's defeat. When Sequoia produced the flat river rock with an eye and tear symbol, Voss became hysterically panicked, revealing that the symbol was given by the big boss to a middleman boss and then to his group as a sign of belonging. Zephyr concluded he was too low on the hierarchy to know anything more useful.

Last known location: The Moathouse ground floor, fate unrecorded after interrogation.

## Mickey

Mickey is the son of Virgil Tamen, the Braumeister of Hommlet. He approached the party at the Inn of the Welcome Wench and asked them to investigate two cloaked figures he had seen lurking behind the brewery at around midnight, fearing they were temple spies given the rumors circulating in the village. He was visibly nervous and relieved when the party agreed. The figures turned out to be two teenagers named Jira and Marek meeting in secret due to their parents' religious differences.

Last known location: The Inn of the Welcome Wench.

---

## Jira

Jira is a teenage girl who was one of the two cloaked figures Mickey reported lurking behind the brewery at midnight. She was meeting in secret with Marek because her father Jakub is a member of the old faith and forbids the relationship. When Zephyr put a blade to her throat she shrieked. When Zinia dismissed them both, she pleaded with him to intervene with their parents, appealing to the party's status as heroes.

Last known location: Hommlet, released from the confrontation at the brewery.

---

## Marek

Marek is a teenage boy meeting secretly with Jira behind the brewery. His father is Tomlyn the tailor, a member of the Church of St. Cuthbert, making the relationship with Jira religiously unacceptable to both families. He began crying when confronted by the party.

Last known location: Hommlet, released from the confrontation.

## Airgid

Airgid is a silver dragon that had been traveling with or sheltered by the party at some expense, straining their budget and raising concerns that she might feed on horses. She explained that her home was near the Lotrimil mountains. On Planting 17 the party escorted her toward Tulvar via the Greenway Valley road through the Kron Hills. Just before reaching Tulvar she recognized her home territory, shouted it, took flight west, and departed, calling back that her mother would reward the party and thanking them.

Status: Returned home. Mother's reward pending.
