## Beat 1 — Hartsch's Fool's Reckoning

Hartsch is a fool of a general who panicked, made a stupid decision (sending the Broken Blades to die at Water Temple after the party laid out a competent two-pronged plan), and has now realized he fucked up.

He is oscillating between two cope strategies:

- **Murder.** Kill the party, blame the slaughter on "Fire Temple sleeper agents in our own vanguard." Politically coherent — silences the witnesses and rewrites the disaster as a counterintelligence win.
- **Grovel.** Confess, weep, offer the Chest of Rao, beg them to salvage it. Emotionally coherent.

He hasn't committed. Loyalists are positioned for an ambush; a confession speech is in his head. The mode he picks is decided by the party's first thirty seconds in the room.

**Flip mechanic — Hartsch reads what the party brings:**

- Party reads as *threatening* → he reads weakness in himself → picks **grovel**
- Party reads as *sympathetic / forgiving* → he reads weakness in *them* → picks **murder**

Whichever mode the party walks in expecting, give them the other.

**Levers the party already holds:**
- The Broken Blades are loyal to the party now (Zinnia's rally + Dren grabbing the party). Dren is at their back when they walk in. Hartsch sees this.
- The Chest of Rao is in Hartsch's possession. Both his modes route it back to the party — grovel hands it over, murder forces them to take it.

**Outcome regardless of mode:** the Earth Temple as a coherent faction ends this scene. The Obsidian Edge no longer has an Earth Temple cover; the party becomes free agents.

## Greater Temple Gate — Unlock Condition

**Two prophets dead + remaining two sufficiently weakened.**

**Prophet roster (current, not cumulative — Romag does not count):**
- Earth: Hartsch
- Fire: Alrrem
- Water: Belsornig
- Air: Kelno

**Why current roster:** makes Beat 1's outcome materially matter for the gate, preserves the weight of three more prophet kills, prevents the party from coasting on a kill made before they understood the rules.

**What "sufficiently weakened" looks like per prophet:**

| Prophet | Threshold sketch |
|---------|-----------------|
| Alrrem (Fire) | Loss of Varek Redflame, loss of Flametongue, loss of fire-elemental ritual capacity, driven from the Fire Temple proper. Killing Varek alone is probably enough. |
| Belsornig (Water) | Treasury looted, ledger captured, Vesrek dead/exposed, troll bid lost, salon evacuated. Capturing his evacuation in DA-B is sufficient. |
| Kelno (Air) | Bribery treasury looted, ledger seized, network of bugbears/gnolls/goblins scattered. Trivially weakened — his power is purely economic. |
| Hartsch (Earth, if alive after Beat 1) | Already weakened by definition. Public dependency on the party qualifies. |

GM call at the table on whether the threshold has crossed. The condition rewards the party's actual style (manipulation, sabotage, infiltration) over pure assassination.

## Decisive Actions

### Decisive Action A — Alrrem's Pyre / Last March (Fire's end)

Alrrem is broken on two fronts and now hears that the Broken Blades raid hit Water — meaning Earth has effectively allied with him without saying so. He's a hard prophet to weaken without killing because the Pyre ritual gives him a power spike. **Must be killed or interrupted in motion.**

Two flavor options:

- **The Pyre.** Locks himself in Fire Temple (Area 212) to summon a fire elemental that will burn Level 2 to ash. Party uses the new Air Temple flank route to drop on him before he completes. Time-pressure encounter.
- **The Last March.** With Earth gone, Alrrem rebrands — the war was won, he is the lawful inheritor, he marches on Water (and on the party in their now-uncovered territory). Open-corridor encounter. Broken Blades are an asset.

### Decisive Action B — Belsornig's Vault (Water's end or weakening)

Was: intercept his evacuation under Fire pressure.
Now: the Broken Blades raid that Hartsch ordered already hit him and got chewed up. Belsornig has stabilized — thinks he's survived the worst. Evacuation is now deliberate, less frantic.

- The Broken Blades survivors (party-loyal) know the route his guards used to push them back. **Sequoia and Dren can coordinate the intercept without the party physically present.**
- Belsornig may have more retainers around him because he stopped fleeing.

Decisive Action B is now **optional-as-kill, mandatory-as-weakening.** Party can intercept the evacuation, take the ledger, and let him live — degraded enough for the gate. Or take the kill.

### Decisive Action C — Kelno's Throne (Air's end or weakening, NEW)

Doc never gave Kelno a decisive action. He's the apex of every faction the party hasn't personally killed yet. Confrontation happens in Area 225 or in the Air Temple corridor approach. The wraith was his primary defensive moat — gone. He'll be unprepared for how fast the party can reach him.

Two paths:

- **Stealth kill** (see contingency below) — likely play given party composition
- **Loot the throne** — take his treasury and ledger, scatter his network, let him sit in an empty Air Temple as a husk. Qualifies as weakening.

## Kelno Stealth-Kill Contingency

Three-quarters of the party is stealth-optimized (Zephyr Assassin, Sequoia Halfling Rogue, Zinnia Monk). They have Cloak of Elvenkind + 3× Dust of Disappearance + Amulet of Proof against Detection. Wraith is dead. Calmer is down — which removes their only reason to fight Kelno loud. **Grade: medium-likely, not unlikely.**

### What collapses immediately on the kill

- **Air Temple ceases to exist.** Goblin spies scatter, gnoll scouts go feral, bugbears flee or sell their loyalty to whoever offers next.
- **Bribery network goes dark.** No more bugbear defections from Water. Belsornig wins the Troll Auction by default — gnoll bid disappears with Kelno.
- **Suffocating Fog beat dies.** Kelno was casting it.
- **Air Temple becomes the party's third controlled territory.** They effectively own the upper level.

### What's in Kelno's quarters — GM decision

Pick the load-out before the session:

- **Bribery treasury** (always — gold, gems, advance-payment chests)
- **Bribery ledger** (recommended). Names of corrupted bugbears, gnolls, and possibly low-level Water Temple defectors. Intelligence asset.
- **Deep Temple route in written form** (journal/sketch map). Confirms the prisoner's intel; adds detail.
- **The Orb** (campaign-ending if placed here — see Barkinar branches)
- **A pointer to the Orb, not the Orb itself** (recommended hedge). Sigil, partial map, or letter from a Greater Temple agent to Kelno about what he was meant to deliver.

**Recommended load-out: bribery treasury + ledger + a pointer to the Orb.** Rewards the stealth kill as an intel windfall without bypassing the climactic arc.

### Tactical note

If the party recons with Calmer left behind to recover, they cannot afford to be discovered — no Turn Undead, no party heavy healing. Give Kelno 2–3 bugbear enforcers on patrol routes rather than as room guards — patrol routes can be read, watched, and timed. Rewards the build.

## Barkinar Timing — three branches

Barkinar walks out the Greater Temple door when the unlock threshold crosses. He is the Deep Temple's enforcer arriving to judge the survivors. Not an upstairs officer from Level 3.

- **Early branch:** Beat 1 kills Hartsch, party stealth-kills Kelno, walks past two visibly broken temples to the door. Gate opens with Alrrem and Belsornig still alive. **Barkinar arrives into an unfinished war.** Chaotic — Fire and Water cultists may still be fighting in the corridors while the party stands at the door.
- **Standard branch:** Party works through the beats, hits threshold cleanly. Gate opens onto a relatively quiet upper level. Barkinar arrives into cleanup.
- **Slow branch:** Party tries to weaken without killing. The gate doesn't open. The Deep Temple waits. Party has to commit to at least two kills.

The early branch is the most interesting failure mode — the party "wins" but the upper level is still on fire.
