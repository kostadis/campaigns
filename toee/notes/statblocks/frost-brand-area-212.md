# Frost Brand — Area 212 (Fire Temple Offering Box)

**Weapon (longsword), legendary (requires attunement by a creature of good alignment)**

This longsword does not glow, even in magical darkness — its cold is held inward, invisible until drawn. The blade is unremarkable in appearance, which is exactly why the salamanders stored it in the offering box rather than destroying it.

---

## Properties

**+3 to attack and damage rolls** with this magic weapon.

**Against fire creatures** (any creature with immunity or resistance to fire damage), the bonus increases to **+6** to attack and damage rolls.

**+1d6 cold damage** on a hit. Against creatures with vulnerability to cold damage (such as salamanders), this damage doubles to 2d6 before the vulnerability multiplier.

**Fire resistance.** While attuned to this weapon, you have resistance to fire damage.

**Extinguish flames.** Immediately after you hit and deal damage with this weapon, you can extinguish all nonmagical flames within 30 feet of you. This property can be used once per dawn.

**No light.** Unlike a standard Frost Brand, this weapon does not shed dim light in freezing temperatures. Its power is held inward.

---

## Sentience

The Frost Brand is a sentient weapon of **lawful good** alignment. It has the following statistics:

| Stat | Score | Mod |
|------|-------|-----|
| Intelligence | 16 | +3 |
| Wisdom | 12 | +1 |
| Charisma | 16 | +3 |

**Senses:** Hearing and darkvision out to 120 feet.

**Languages:** The weapon speaks Common, Giant, Draconic, and Ignan (corresponding to the 1e "Common, Fire Giant, Red Dragon, Salamander"). It can communicate telepathically with any attuned wielder who holds it, and can speak aloud if it chooses.

### Powers (always active or at will)

The weapon can use the following abilities on its own initiative (Int modifier +3, spell save DC 13), sharing the effect with its attuned wielder when appropriate:

- **Detect Evil and Good** (at will). The weapon senses the presence of aberrations, celestials, elementals, fey, fiends, and undead within 30 feet, as the spell.
- **Detect Magic** (at will). The weapon senses magical auras within 30 feet, as the spell.
- **Sense architecture.** The weapon is aware of secret doors, sliding walls, false floors, and rooms that have recently shifted or rotated within 30 feet of its wielder. It communicates these findings telepathically as a direction and description ("stone seam, north wall, ten feet, lever concealed behind the sconce").

### Powers (expended)

- **Levitate** (3/day). The weapon casts *levitate* (as the 2nd-level spell, save DC 13, +3 to hit). It can target itself and its wielder as a single unit, or target one creature within 60 feet. The effect lasts up to 10 minutes, concentration required by the wielder. The weapon typically uses this to get its wielder into an advantageous position over fire creatures that cannot fly.

## Personality

The Frost Brand is old, patient, and opinionated. It has spent a long time in a box surrounded by fire creatures, and it is not cheerful about the experience. It respects effective violence and competence; it is contemptuous of hesitation in the face of evil.

**Ideal.** *Fire is the enemy. Cold endures.*
**Bond.** The weapon wants to fight fire giants, red dragons, and salamanders — in that order. It will push for engagements with fire creatures and may sulk if the party avoids them.
**Flaw.** It will not willingly remain sheathed in the presence of a fire creature it believes the party can defeat. If the party attempts to parley with the salamanders while the Frost Brand is attuned, it will make its displeasure known — loudly, in Ignan.

**Conflict:** If an attuned wielder of neutral alignment tries to spare a fire creature the weapon considers an enemy of good, the weapon may assert control (Charisma DC 13). Creatures of evil alignment cannot attune to this weapon; the blade simply warms slightly and does nothing.

## Notes

**Mid-fight pickup.** If the party opens the offering box during the salamander fight, the weapon's **sense architecture** power can detect any secret exits the salamanders might flee to. More importantly, the +6 vs. fire creatures means even a rogue picking it up mid-combat without attunement gains the +3 and cold damage (no attunement bonus until a short rest, but the raw bonuses apply).

**The Ignan problem.** The weapon speaks Ignan. If it opens with taunts at the salamanders in their own language before the party has chosen to negotiate, that is entirely in character.

**1e source.** The longsword "does not glow" per the module text (a rare note — worth preserving as flavor since the party will likely use Detect Magic and get the aura before they understand what it is).
