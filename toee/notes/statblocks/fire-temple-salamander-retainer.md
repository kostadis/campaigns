# The Surviving Salamander — Bound Retainer (post-conquest, area 212)

The last of the three Temple of Fire salamanders, surrendered when the second of its kin
died. A masterless conscript who served whoever held the altar; the party now hold it.
Use this if the party accept its service instead of killing it or letting it flee.

**Name:** *Vurakhal* (Ignan; roughly "ember-that-was-spared") — confirmed at table.

---

## Loyalty: how a bound salamander actually obeys

A salamander is **lawful evil and transactional**, not loyal. It does not love the party and
never will. It obeys because (a) it has nowhere to go — its plane-binding was Alrrem's, and
Alrrem is ash, leaving it stranded on the Material until something re-binds or dismisses it,
and (b) the party are demonstrably stronger than the things that killed its kin. It serves
the way a mercenary serves a paymaster who could also kill it.

**Loyalty score (track it):** start at **+1 (grudging).** Range −3 (will betray first chance)
to +5 (genuinely invested). It moves:
- **+1** each time the party treat it as a being, not a tool (let it rest in the pit, ask its
  counsel, use its name, don't make it stand in cold water).
- **+1** if the party kill or humiliate something it hates (giants, anyone from the Water
  Temple — Belsornig's people ran the raids that broke its kin).
- **−1** each time it's ordered to do something self-destructive (charge a wraith, stand in a
  river, guard an empty room for days).
- **−2** if ordered onto cold/water terrain, into combat it can't win, or if a party member
  openly talks about dismissing/killing it.

At **+3 or higher** it volunteers information and warns of danger. At **−1 or lower** it starts
slow-walking orders and looking for the door. At **−3** it acts on the betrayal options below.

## What it will do

- **Guard the Fire Temple altar and approaches.** This is its native job; it does it well and
  willingly. A CR-5 sentry that's immune to fire and lives in a fire pit is a genuinely strong
  static defense for the dungeon the party now own.
- **Fight the party's enemies** — especially Water Temple remnants and giants. Eager here.
- **Talk.** It knows the Fire Temple's layout, Alrrem's hidden caches, the rough geography of
  the **fire node**, and which of the lesser-temple survivors fled where. A useful intel asset.
- **Carry / haul / labor** around heat and stone. Won't complain about a forge, a furnace, a
  collapsed tunnel.

## What it will NOT do

- **Enter water or deep cold.** Cold is its *vulnerability* (double damage); it will refuse a
  river crossing or a frozen node outright, and loyalty drops if pushed.
- **Leave the Material Plane's "fire" for long.** It can't go far from a heat source comfortably;
  a long overland march through wet country degrades it (treat as exhaustion-ish, GM's call).
- **Die for the party.** It is not a fanatic — that's the whole reason it surrendered. Order it
  into a clearly losing fight and it flees instead, every time. Don't expect a last stand.
- **Obey a command that re-enslaves it harder than it already is** (e.g. a magical re-binding
  ritual). It will comply with a *dismissal* gratefully — but a forced re-bind triggers betrayal
  calculus.

## How it betrays them (if loyalty craters)

A salamander betrays by *abandonment and information*, not a dramatic backstab — it's too
weak to win a straight fight and knows it.
1. **It walks off post.** Abandons the altar guard at the worst moment, leaving a hole in the
   defense it was supposed to plug.
2. **It sells what it knows.** The fire node geography, Alrrem's caches, the party's routines
   and weaknesses — to whoever's left to buy (a Greater Temple agent, a surviving leader).
3. **Only if cornered with an ally** will it fight them — and then it leads with the tail-grapple
   on the squishiest PC, same as any salamander.
   **Tell:** before it turns, it stops using its name for itself and goes cold and formal. A
   perceptive party (passive Insight) gets one warning scene.

## The St. Cuthbert problem (table texture, not mechanics)

Calmer is a cleric of St. Cuthbert running an Earth-Temple cover. Keeping a bound creature of
elemental *evil* as a household servant is exactly the kind of compromise that's been eroding
his cover-vs-faith line all campaign. Worth a beat: does he tolerate Vurakhal, try to convert/
redeem it (futile but characterful), or insist it be dismissed? This is a *party* decision with
a real internal-friction cost — which is what makes the retainer interesting rather than just
free muscle.

## Dismissal

If the party ever want it gone cleanly: a salamander stranded by its summoner's death can be
sent home by *dismissal*, *banishment*, or simply by destroying the fire pit that anchors it to
this site (it fades back to the Plane of Fire over a few days without a bound flame to inhabit).
It will not resist a genuine dismissal — going home intact is a better outcome than most of its
kin got.

## Source / chassis

MM **Salamander** (CR 5), unchanged stats. This doc is the *retainer overlay* — loyalty terms,
command boundaries, betrayal conditions. Behavior derived from the module's area-212 note that
these salamanders are demoralized veterans who "flee if damaged and always negotiate if cornered,"
extended forward to a post-conquest world where the party hold the altar and Alrrem is dead.
