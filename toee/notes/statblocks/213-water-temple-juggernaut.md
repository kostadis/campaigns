# The Verdigris Juggernaut — Area 213 (Hall of Verdigris, Water Temple)

5e conversion of the T1-4 juggernaut. **The serpentine marine idol in the south alcove IS the
juggernaut** ("The idol is actually a juggernaut" — T1-4 area 213). It sits inert and
indistinguishable from a cult statue until the altar is defiled or its treasure disturbed, then
animates and rolls. Per the June 14 session, the idol *already began to rotate* when the four
gargoyles woke — so this thing is winding up to speed in the middle of a fight the party is already
a couple of kills into.

Tuned **brutal / near-canon** (GM call, 2026-06-22): the roll-over can drop a level-8 PC in one
pass. Sized as the heavy centerpiece of the in-progress 4-gargoyle battle, party of ~4 at level 8.

---

**The Verdigris Juggernaut** — Huge animated stone-and-bronze crushing engine; the Water Temple's idol-guardian

**AC** 18 (natural armor) | **HP** 184 (16d12 + 80) | **Speed** 40 ft. (see *Slow to Start*)

**STR** 21 (+5) **DEX** 6 (−2) **CON** 20 (+5) **INT** 5 (−3) **WIS** 11 (+0) **CHA** 1 (−5)

**Damage Vulnerabilities** thunder *(rigid stone — resonance shatters it; the party's lever)*
**Damage Immunities** poison, psychic
**Condition Immunities** charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone *(it rolls — it cannot be toppled)*
**Senses** tremorsense 60 ft., blindsight 30 ft. (blind beyond), passive Perception 9
**Languages** understands the cant of the Water Temple but cannot speak
**CR** 9 (5,000 XP) | **Proficiency** +4

***Idol Disguise.*** While motionless, the juggernaut is indistinguishable from an ordinary
serpentine-stone cult idol. A creature must make a DC 17 Intelligence (Investigation) check —
or have it pointed out by *detect magic* (it reads as a faint conjuration/transmutation aura) —
to discern it is a creature before it animates. It typically gets a surprise round on the turn it
first moves.

***Slow to Start (Ponderous Engine).*** The juggernaut is brutally hard to get rolling. On the
first round it animates, its speed is **10 ft.** Its speed increases by **10 ft.** at the start of
each of its turns thereafter, to its maximum of 40 ft. *(Round 1: 10 ft. Round 2: 20 ft. Round 3:
30 ft. Round 4+: 40 ft.)* This windup is the party's window — early on it is almost stationary.

***Cannot Turn Sharply.*** Wheels and runners do not pivot. The juggernaut can change its facing by
at most 90 degrees per 30 feet it travels, and it cannot reverse direction or move back the way it
came unless it spends its entire turn coming to a complete stop (no other movement that turn). A
clever party can make it overshoot, ram a wall, or trundle uselessly past them.

***Siege Monster.*** The juggernaut deals double damage to objects and structures. *(It will happily
demolish the altar, the plinths, the bronze fountain — terrain becomes rubble around it.)*

***Immutable Form.*** Immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.

**Actions**

***Multiattack.*** The juggernaut makes three Pseudopod attacks. *(Canon allows up to six growths;
at the table, three is the calibrated number. If you want a horror beat, let it sprout a fourth
eel-head against a clustered party — adds ~12 average damage.)*

***Pseudopod.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 12 (2d6 + 5)
bludgeoning damage (a stone ram or pincer) or 12 (2d6 + 5) piercing damage (a slim toothy eel-head),
juggernaut's choice. *(These are the "slim toothy heads, pincers, rams, and so forth" of canon —
reach 10 ft., lashing in any direction.)*

***Roll-Over (Trample).*** *In place of its Multiattack,* the juggernaut moves up to its current
speed in a straight line (subject to *Cannot Turn Sharply*), passing through the space of any Large
or smaller creature in its path; those spaces are difficult terrain. The first time it enters a
creature's space during this move, that creature makes a **DC 17 Dexterity saving throw**, taking
**55 (10d10) bludgeoning damage** and falling **prone** on a failure, or half damage and no prone
on a success. A creature can be affected by Roll-Over only once per turn.
> *Optional canon rider (crushing blow vs. gear):* a creature that fails the save by 5 or more also
> has one nonmagical, non-attuned item it wears or carries (DM's choice — a shield, a helm, a
> weapon) bent or shattered. Skip this if it bogs the table; it's pure 1e flavor.

**Reactions**

***None.*** The juggernaut is too ponderous to seize fleeting openings — it takes no reactions and
makes no opportunity attacks. *This is deliberate and load-bearing:* nimble PCs can Disengage,
slip past, and reposition around it freely. Its menace is the lane it threatens, not the squares
beside it.

## Tactics — how the engine actually fights

**Opening (rounds 1–2):** It is barely moving (10–20 ft.) and *cannot line anyone up* yet, so it
flails — three Pseudopods at whoever is in reach (10 ft.), favoring the squishiest body it can
touch. This is the party's chance to spread out and deny it a straight lane. A smart party scatters
*now*, while it's slow.

**Mid-fight (rounds 3+):** Once it's up to 30–40 ft., it commits to **Roll-Over** down whatever
corridor holds the most PCs — the wide 40'×90' hall gives it room to build a lane along the
long (E–W) axis. It will happily trample its own gargoyles' positions; it does not care. After a
roll-over it is pointed the wrong way (*Cannot Turn Sharply*) and spends the next turn either
grinding to a halt to reverse or arcing wide — that lag is when the party piles on.

**The kite:** Against a party that understands *Cannot Turn Sharply* + *no reactions*, the
juggernaut is beatable by movement: bait the lane, sidestep the charge (Dex save / just stepping out
of the straight line), and chew it down while it laboriously comes about. Reward that play — it's
the intended counter and it feels great.

**It does not flee, does not negotiate, does not stop.** It is a guardian engine with animal
intelligence and one directive: crush whatever defiled the altar. It "dies" when reduced to 0 (it
collapses into a heap of verdigris-streaked rubble and bronze shards). It will keep rolling through
unconscious PCs if they're in a lane.

**Thunder is the cheat code.** It's vulnerable to thunder (rigid stone). Calmer's *Shatter*, a
thunderwave, Frostbrand's wielder shouting for the right spell — anything thunder doubles up and
melts that 184 HP fast. If the party has no thunder, the fight is a slugfest against magic weapons
(the party's enchanted blades bite full; Zephyr's assassin burst and Zinnia's flurries are the DPR
that gets there in 3–4 rounds).

**Synergy with the gargoyles (already in play):** the gargoyles are mobile flyers harassing from the
plinths; the juggernaut is the ground threat that punishes anyone who clusters to deal with them.
The deadly combination is a roll-over through a party that has bunched up to focus-fire a gargoyle.
Telegraph the windup ("the idol grinds a quarter-turn toward you") so the trample feels earned, not
ambush-cheap.

## Treasure

The juggernaut carries nothing — it *is* the idol. On its destruction:
- The **altar basin** (area 213) still holds its offerings: coins, a sprinkling of gems —
  canon names **3 red garnets and 6 tourmalines worth ~100 gp each** (~900 gp) — and many
  seashells, in standing water.
- Buried in the rubble of the idol's core, *if you want a node hook:* a fist-sized **bound water-
  focus** (a verdigris bronze sphere weeping cold seawater that never empties) — a thematic
  breadcrumb toward the Water Node / the Orb's elemental gems. Optional.

## CR calibration

Intended **CR 9 (5,000 XP)**, sized for ~4 PCs at level 8. Expected outcome: **deadly** as the
escalation of the already-running 4-gargoyle fight — a focused roll-over can drop a level-8 PC
outright (55 avg, DC 17 Dex save for half), exactly the "brutal / near-canon" lethality the GM
asked for. The two pressure-release valves that keep it fair: (1) *Slow to Start* gives the party a
1–2 round head start while it's nearly immobile, and (2) *Cannot Turn Sharply* + *no reactions*
make it kiteable by anyone who reads the movement rules. Thunder vulnerability is the fast-kill
lever if the party finds it. If it plays *too* hard, drop Multiattack to two Pseudopods and the
trample to 8d10; if too soft, restore the canon sixth growth and let it Roll-Over and Pseudopod in
the same turn (move + action).

## Source / chassis

Math chassis: **Earth Elemental (MM p124)** — the conversion explicitly endorsed by
`docs/background/adventure-t14-5e.json` area 213 ("use earth elemental as base, with modified
slam/trample attacks"). Tuned **up** from CR 5 to CR 9 for a level-8 party: HP raised (126 → 184),
proficiency/attacks bumped to CR 9, Earth Glide dropped (a wheeled idol does not glide through
stone), and the two signature juggernaut mechanics rebuilt from T1-4 Appendix B —
**Roll-Over/Trample** (1e: 10d10, save vs. death ray for half → 5e: 10d10, DC 17 Dex save for half)
and the **multi-pseudopod growths** (1e: up to six attacks, 2d6 each, 6 ft. reach → 5e: Multiattack
of reach-10 Pseudopods, 2d6+5). Canon movement quirks (*Slow to Start*, *Cannot Turn Sharply*)
preserved verbatim-in-spirit. Thunder vulnerability is an added party lever, not canon.
