# Area 212 — Temple of Fire: Salamander Encounter (Hard, party of 4 @ L8)

**Concept:** Three battle-weary fire salamanders, full MM CR-5 chassis, guarding the
fire-pit altar. They are *not* fanatics — recent giant and human raids have ground them
down. They flee when hurt and will deal if cornered. The fight is Hard only because they
break; run them to the death and it becomes Deadly.

**Encounter framing (the set piece is intact):** When the party fails to make the major
sacrifice, the fire pit still erupts with **16 apparent salamanders** that "grow to full
size in four rounds." Only **3 are real** — the rest are mirror images (down from the
module's 4 real, to land the fight on Hard). The four-round grow-in is a free turn-1
warning: the party can flee, sacrifice, or position before the real ones can move or attack.

---

## Salamander (×3, real)

**AC** 15 (natural armor) | **HP** 90 (12d10 + 24) | **Speed** 30 ft.
**STR** 18 (+4) **DEX** 14 (+2) **CON** 15 (+2) **INT** 11 (+0) **WIS** 10 (+0) **CHA** 12 (+1)
**Damage Vulnerabilities** cold
**Damage Immunities** fire
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 10
**Languages** Ignan
**CR** 5 (1,800 XP)

***Heated Body.*** A creature that touches the salamander or hits it with a melee attack
while within 5 ft. of it takes 7 (2d6) fire damage.

***Heated Weapons.*** Any metal melee weapon the salamander wields deals an extra 3 (1d6)
fire damage on a hit (included below). The module's "spear of red-hot bronze" *is* this
weapon.

**Actions**

***Multiattack.*** The salamander makes two attacks: one with its spear and one with its tail.

***Spear.*** *Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft.,
one target. *Hit:* 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage, or 13 (2d8 + 4) piercing damage if used
in two hands to make a melee attack, plus 3 (1d6) fire damage.

***Tail.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 11 (2d6 + 4)
bludgeoning damage plus 7 (2d6) fire damage, and the target is **grappled** (escape DC 14).
Until this grapple ends, the target is **restrained**, the salamander can automatically hit
the target with its tail, and the salamander can't make tail attacks against other targets.

**Reactions**

None in the canon block. (Heated Body covers melee retaliation as a passive — no reaction
to track.)

## How they fight

The grapple is the whole danger, not the fire. A salamander leads with its **tail**, locks
one PC (escape DC 14), and from then on auto-hits that PC for ~18/round while keeping its
spear free for a second body. Against the squishy rogues (Sequoia, Zephyr) or the cleric, a
locked grapple snowballs fast — the restrained target has disadvantage to hit and is easy
to crit.

- **Opening:** the four-round grow-in is theirs to lose. Smart salamanders spread out so a
  single fireball... wouldn't matter (they're fire-immune), but a single melee PC can't tie
  up all three. Each tries to grapple a *different* PC.
- **Pivot:** once one is below ~18 HP (the module's 80%-damaged flee threshold — round it to
  "bloodied-and-then-some"), it disengages, drops its grapple, and backs toward the fire pit
  (it can stand in the flames; the party can't). If two are hurt, all three lose nerve.
- **Break / negotiate:** if cornered with no path to the pit, a salamander **talks** (Ignan;
  the party needs a speaker or magic). It will trade: "the cold-blade in the offering box,
  take it and we are even" — i.e. it gives up the Temple of Fire's own loot to be left alone.
  This is the module's "always negotiate if cornered" written as a concrete offer.

**The pressure valve (read this before the table):** these salamanders fleeing at ~80%
damage is what makes the encounter *Hard* and not *Deadly*. Three full CR-5 salamanders run
to the death is an 10,800-adjusted-XP grind. The flee/deal behavior caps the real damage the
party eats. If you want it *harder*, have them fight two more rounds before breaking; if a
PC goes down early and you want to pull the punch, trigger the flee sooner — it's diegetically
justified (they're demoralized veterans, not zealots).

## Counters the party already holds

- **Zephyr (tiefling):** resistance to fire — halves the 10ish fire/round and ignores
  Heated Body's 7. Put him in melee; he's the safest grappler-breaker.
- **Ring of Free Action (party):** wearer auto-escapes / can't be grappled by the tail. This
  is the single best answer to the whole encounter — whoever wears it is immune to the lock.
- **Potions of Fire Resistance (×2 in party inventory):** same fire trim as tiefling for two
  rounds-worth of fights; modest, since most damage is physical.
- **Cold damage = vulnerability (double).** If anyone has a cold source, it doubles. See
  treasure — the offering box hands the party the perfect anti-salamander weapon mid-fight.

## Treasure (offering box, area 212)

Per the module, the iron box at the altar holds:

- **2 × Potion of Fire Resistance**
- **Ring of Fire Resistance** (the room's reward for the fire damage it throws at you)
- **A longsword that does not glow** — the module's **Frost Brand** (described in the legacy
  text as "+3/+6 vs. fire creatures," Lawful Good, Int 16 / Ego 17, speaks Common, Fire Giant,
  Red Dragon, Salamander; powers: *detect evil*, *detect magic*, *detect shifting walls/rooms*,
  and *levitate*).
  - **5e adjudication note:** in pure 5e the *Frost Brand* (DMG) deals +1d6 **cold** on a hit
    and grants the wielder fire resistance. Either way it is *the* anti-salamander weapon —
    cold is the salamanders' vulnerability, so its cold rider **doubles** against them. If the
    party pries the box open mid-fight, that's a genuine swing. The intelligent-weapon
    personality (LG, wants to fight fire creatures) is a fun pull-toward-the-fight if a PC
    attunes on the spot.

## CR calibration

Intended difficulty **Hard**, sized for **4 PCs at level 8** (War Cleric, Assassin Rogue,
Monk, Rogue per docs/party.md — note docs list them at L6; built to L8 per request).

- **Raw:** 3 × 1,800 = 5,400 XP. **Adjusted** (×2 for 3–6 monsters) = **10,800**.
- L8 party-of-4 thresholds: Hard 8,400 / Deadly 11,200. So 10,800 sits **just under Deadly
  on paper.**
- **Why it plays as Hard for *this* party:** (1) the flee-at-80%/negotiate behavior ends the
  fight before the full XP-grind lands; (2) tiefling fire resistance + Ring of Free Action
  neuter the two scariest vectors (Heated Body and the tail-lock). Net: a tense Hard, with a
  Deadly ceiling if the dice and the GM both push.

## Source / chassis

MM **Salamander** (CR 5), used at full power — **no stat changes**. The "scaling" is purely
encounter-level: 3 real instead of 4, plus the module's own demoralized-veteran behavior
(area 212 DM notes: "80% likely to flee if damaged, and will always negotiate if cornered")
as the difficulty governor. Environmental hazards of the room (cauldron 2d6 fire on defilement,
magic-mouth sacrifice demand) are module features, not added lair actions.
