# Hidden Allies in Nulb — GM-Only

The party has been hunting the Temple of Elemental Evil for almost the entire campaign without realizing that a parallel covert investigation has been running alongside them. Three good-aligned operators are embedded in Nulb itself, watching the Temple's resurgence and waiting for the right moment to surface.

**This resolves multiple open campaign threads simultaneously.**

---

## Y'dey — The Missing Cleric Is Alive

**Per party canon (`docs/npcs/y_dey.md`):** the party knows Y'dey is the missing Head Cleric of Hommlet's Church of St. Cuthbert. They learned of her disappearance through tavern gossip in the early sessions. A patron suggested Terjon — her subordinate, now acting head — was suspiciously tight-lipped and may have been involved. The party has never resolved this thread.

**Per leadership canon:**

> **Canoness Y'dey** — An influential cleric of St. Cuthbert, operates under the guise of **Mother Screng in the sinister village of Nulb.**
>
> *Primary Goal:* Sent by the Church of St. Cuthbert, Y'dey in Nulb under the guise of Mother Screng.
>
> *Secondary Goals:* Y'dey harbors doubts about the true origins of the Temple of Elemental Evil. Her mission involves uncovering the forces behind it and preventing their resurgence.

**She did not disappear. She went undercover.** Terjon's tight-lipped behavior is *because he knows the operation and is protecting it.* The local suspicion of Terjon is wrong — he is the Hommlet end of Y'dey's communication channel. He cannot tell anyone the truth.

### Where she is

The **Herb Shop** in Nulb. Per the Nulb chapter: *"Herb Shop — Mother Screng's establishment where herbalism and potions are the stock of trade."*

She has been there for some time. She sells herbal remedies. She listens. She watches. She has a "daughter" named **Hruda** (actually Murfles — see below).

### What she knows / suspects

- The Temple is genuinely resurgent. The Battle of Emridy Meadows did not fully end it.
- There is "something deeper" behind the elemental cults — she does not yet know about Zuggtmoy's deception or Hedrack's coup, but she suspects the cult is not what it presents itself as.
- She has been gathering intelligence on Nulb's Temple operations through the herb-shop traffic. Most cultists need herbalist services eventually.
- She may have observed Madame Selentis's people directly. She does not know Selentis's real identity.
- She knows of Otis the smith as an ally.

### What she does NOT know

- Hedrack's plan (no information channel reaches that high)
- Zuggtmoy's Grand Deception (would be a campaign-defining revelation for her)
- Falrinth's existence specifically
- That the party are operating undercover

### Her mission report channel

Likely through Veluna and Furyondy via covert courier. Possibly through Verbobonc (Burne/Rufus would not be the channel — she would route around them to avoid Temple compromise). Recommend: she reports up to a **named Veluna cleric** the party has never heard of, perhaps via Otis the smith or via Murfles.

### Why she hasn't surfaced

She is undercover. Coming out as Y'dey ends the operation. She only reveals herself if:
- She is convinced the surfacer is trustworthy AND knowledgeable
- The operation is about to collapse anyway
- An emergency requires her cleric-level capabilities

**Calmer is a St. Cuthbert cleric.** He is the natural unlock. If the party visits the herb shop and Calmer interacts directly, she will *recognize* him as church-aligned. She will probe carefully — possibly through subtle religious-tradition references that only a real Cuthbertine would catch. If Calmer responds appropriately, she has the opening she has been waiting for.

**Counter-pressure:** Calmer is currently under Earth Temple cover. Visibly worshipping St. Cuthbert in Nulb is suicide. The encounter needs to be handled with extreme discretion. **This is exactly the kind of scene the party plays well** — a high-stakes private reveal that risks Calmer's cover but unlocks a major ally.

## Murfles — "Hruda" the Bard

**Per canon:** *"Murfles, posing as Hruda — a sharp-tongued daughter of Mother Screng — is an undercover agent of Good stationed in Nulb. Despite her rough, unappealing demeanor, Murfles is a highly capable adventurer, bard, and an ally of Otis and Canoness Y'dey."*

**Primary goal:** Protect and assist Y'dey.
**Secondary:** Strike covertly on behalf of Veluna and Furyondy.

### Her role

- Plays a sullen, plain "daughter" working the herb shop — protective camouflage for Y'dey
- Actual bard, fully geared for covert ops (charm/persuasion, languages, magical inspiration, possibly some combat capability)
- Field agent for actions Y'dey can't take in her Mother Screng identity (Y'dey doesn't leave the shop; Murfles can move around Nulb)
- Likely the source of the actual courier-route intel — she carries the messages out

### Why she matters to the party

If the party flips Mother Screng / Y'dey, **Murfles is the operational arm.** She can:
- Gather intel for the party from sources the party can't approach as Earth Temple agents
- Run interference at the Boatman's, the docks, etc.
- Carry messages to Veluna/Furyondy/Verbobonc on the party's behalf
- Identify Temple agents the party hasn't spotted

She is also a **bard** — a fellow infiltrator. Personality-wise she should mesh well with Zinnia (the party's primary social operator) and Sequoia (the other halfling-or-small rogue depending on her race). Possibly *too* well with Zephyr — bards and rogue assassins have either great rapport or instant friction.

## Otis the Master Smith

**Per canon:** *"Otis is a humble blacksmith of Nulb by trade, but in truth, he is one of the Viscounty's most loyal rangers and a covert agent of the Gnarley Rangers. Scarred by war and tempered by decades of hardship, Otis walks a careful line between his quiet life and his sacred duty to monitor the growing evil around the village of Nulb."*

*Primary Goal:* Monitor and report the resurgence of the Temple of Elemental Evil to the Gnarley Rangers and trusted allies in Hommlet and Verbobonc.

*Secondary Goals:* Train and evaluate potential adventurers who might aid in the coming storm.

### His role

- **Gnarley Rangers covert agent.** The Rangers have been watching the Gnarley Forest for the Temple's regional movements. Otis is their listening post in Nulb itself.
- Blacksmith cover: legitimate trade, lets him see who needs weapons, repairs, special orders.
- "Trains and evaluates potential adventurers" — he is on the lookout for people he can recruit/equip for anti-Temple operations.
- Probably has equipment caches and possibly a fallback safehouse outside Nulb in the Gnarley Forest.

### Why he matters to the party

Otis is the **military/martial-skill ally** to complement Y'dey's clerical/intelligence work and Murfles's bardic infiltration. He can:
- Identify, repair, or upgrade Temple-loot weapons the party has accumulated (the party has *a lot* of Temple gear they don't fully understand — the TZGY scarab being only the most pressing example)
- Provide safe travel through the Gnarley to Hommlet/Verbobonc via Gnarley Ranger paths
- Connect the party to the broader Gnarley Rangers network — actual organized military force opposed to the Temple
- Recognize bandit insignia, ranger sign, Temple symbology

### The Gnarley Rangers connection

This is a bigger thread than it looks. The Gnarley Rangers are the regional para-military force watching the forest. Elmo (Verbobonc agent, son of Captain Zeb Fletcher) is known to the party — Elmo's "rangers" sent on the false chest trail were likely Gnarley Rangers (or affiliated). **Otis is the senior Ranger asset in Nulb.** Connecting the party to him also reconnects them to Elmo's circle, which has been off-screen since the false-trail dispatch.

### How Otis would meet the party

He has probably already noticed them. They are too capable and too active in Nulb to escape a careful smith's attention. He has been *evaluating them* from a distance.

He won't approach openly. Possible entry points:
- The party brings him weapons to repair (natural smith-customer interaction). He drops a careful question. Their response tells him a lot.
- He sends an intermediary — possibly Murfles, who could engineer a "chance" meeting in the herb shop.
- The party investigates Bandit/Temple gear they recovered, asks around Nulb for a smith competent enough to identify it. He becomes the obvious referral.

## The Reveal — Suggested Structure

This is a multi-scene reveal, not a single setpiece.

### Beat 1 — Otis notices the party first
He has the most ambient visibility. He has been watching. During the Phase 3 cleanup or the early days of Phase 4, the party visits his smithy for legitimate trade reasons. He drops a coded line — *"You know, smith to traveler — there's a kind of work I do for people who care about more than coin. Should you ever find yourselves with questions of conscience, my door is also open after sundown."*

Not an alliance pitch yet. A door cracking. The party may or may not pull on it.

### Beat 2 — Murfles engineers a meeting
If the party pulls on Otis's line, or if they ever stop at the herb shop (for healing supplies, poisons, hangover cures — natural visits), Murfles establishes contact. She is "Hruda" — surly, plain, asking too many specific questions about what the party needs. She tests them with the *kind* of questions a real bard would ask. Her actual goal: confirm to Y'dey that the party is worth surfacing for.

### Beat 3 — Y'dey reveals herself
**Calmer is the key.** If the party brings Calmer into the herb shop, Mother Screng will probe Calmer's St. Cuthbert orthodoxy directly. She may use the Brilliant Acclamation (a Cuthbertine call-and-response) or a Cuthbertine saint's day reference. If Calmer responds correctly, she shifts gears.

The reveal: she lets the disguise slip just for him. *"You are Calmer of the Earth Temple, they say. I am Mother Screng of the herb shop, they say. Both true. Both also incomplete."* The Hommlet reveal lands: Y'dey isn't missing. She is on assignment. She has been *here* the entire time.

### Beat 4 — The full alliance
If the party reciprocates with their actual mission (covertly destroying the Temple from within), the alliance forms. Each side has something the other doesn't:

**What the party brings to the alliance:**
- Inside-Temple access (Earth Temple cover, Greater Temple cultivation)
- Killings of multiple senior Temple leaders (Romag, Hartsch, Alrrem, Belsornig, Lareth)
- Knowledge of Hedrack's existence and the supply chain
- The TZGY scarab (Senshock's control device)
- Possibly Falrinth contact if they have it

**What Y'dey's network brings:**
- Veluna/Furyondy/Verbobonc government contacts (real escalation channels above Burne/Rufus)
- Gnarley Rangers as a deployable military force
- Mother Screng as a healing/clerical capability for emergencies (Calmer is no longer alone)
- Murfles as a second infiltrator
- Safe houses outside Nulb
- Y'dey may be able to *raise the dead* if a party member dies (this is the kind of high-leverage capability Calmer needs but cannot openly access)

## What This Reveal Solves

1. **Y'dey's disappearance** — open since the early sessions. Closed.
2. **Terjon's suspicious behavior** — closed. He's not a villain; he's the Hommlet end of a covert op.
3. **Calmer's isolation** — Calmer has been the only Cuthbertine in the party, with no orthodox support and a forced cover identity that visibly distresses him. Y'dey is the senior Cuthbert authority who can validate his choices, hear his confession, and reabsorb him into the church when this is over.
4. **The party's lack of escalation channels** — every faction contact they have (Jaroo, Burne, Rufus, Terjon, Elder Kenter) is local. Y'dey's network gives them Veluna/Furyondy reach.
5. **A clean post-campaign landing pad** — when the Hedrack arc resolves, the party needs a place to go. The Veluna/Furyondy church connection is that landing.

---

## Why Save This for the Right Moment

Don't let the party stumble into Y'dey by accident. The reveal is *valuable narrative real estate.* Suggested triggers:

- The party has just done something morally heavy (executed someone questionably, made the Chest of Rao decision, killed Hartsch by inaction). They need a moment of grace.
- Calmer's Cover Integrity stress is at a breaking point — he needs his own faith back in the room.
- Phase 4 has begun and the party is starting to suspect they're being used by Lucius. They need to know they have a fallback.

Y'dey's reveal is the campaign's first genuine *good news.* Use it accordingly.

## Side Note — Terjon's Silence

If the party ever asks Y'dey directly: *"Why did Terjon not tell us?"*

Y'dey's answer (paraphrase): *"Because he didn't know what kind of cleric you would become. Calmer was a young man hauled into ugly work very fast. Terjon protected the operation. If I had been compromised and Calmer had been a different kind of cleric, that conversation would have ended differently for both of us. Now you have grown into people I can speak to. Do not blame Terjon. Blame the Temple."*

This redeems Terjon as a character. The party has been mildly suspicious of him for most of the campaign; this resolves it cleanly without making him a hero — he's just a careful operator doing what the operation required.
