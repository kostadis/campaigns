# Furnishing the Household

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the session that opens mid-gargoyle-fight in
the Water Temple and ends with the party's first quiet morning as management, when a friendly
man named Lucius arrives to give them everything they want.

Companion docs: `lucius_network.md` (Lucius's supply chain + the magic-item menu — read it before
the centerpiece scene), `phase3_endgame.md` (Barkinar's List in full), the `_TEMP_*` grounding
docs in `summaries/20260625/` (current state).

---

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

By end of session: the Water Temple is theirs (the fight is won, the treasury is at least
counted, the trolls are at least addressed); the party has taken a long rest; and **Lucius has
installed himself as their go-to resource** — they leave the table thinking *"finally, we have a
guy."* That's the landing. Barkinar's other chores are bonus, sequenced at the party's pace.

> **GM-only:** The real win is invisible. Lucius arming and supplying them is the first knot in
> Hedrack's leash (`hedrack_strategy.md`). If the party ends the night richer, better-equipped,
> and grateful — *and never feels handled* — the session did its job.

## The Default Trajectory (what happens if the party does nothing)

- **The gargoyle fight** finishes regardless — they're already winning. (One dead, two slowed and
  bloodied, Calmer's Spirit Guardians up.) Passivity just means a slower mop-up.
- **The Water Temple treasury** sits in its vault, uncounted. Barkinar's check-in later asks why.
- **The trolls (Areas 218-219)** stay on an unpaid contract Belsornig signed. Left alone, they get
  restless — and a restless troll warband in your basement is a Barkinar-check-in problem, or worse,
  a problem that comes looking for back pay.
- **The Chest of Rao** stays wherever Hartsch left it; the decision just defers.
- **The necromancer** keeps working. Every day unaddressed, more Earth Temple dead get up.
- **Lucius comes anyway, after the rest.** He doesn't need them to *do* anything to earn the visit —
  the visit is the play. The only thing that changes the Lucius scene is how flush and how trusting
  they are when he knocks.

## The Clever Play (what the optimal party would do)

1. **Win the fight fast, claim the room, pocket the altar treasure.** Don't over-explore Water on
   bloodied resources.
2. **Count the treasury and triage the trolls before resting** — both are cheap to address and both
   are things Barkinar will grade. Paying the trolls out of Belsornig's own coffer is the elegant
   move (cheap, sets them up as employers, avoids a CR-5 brawl on tired legs).
3. **Rest.** They've earned it and the next beat needs them fresh.
4. **Receive Lucius warmly but not stupidly** — take the goods, give nothing real, notice (if anyone
   makes the Insight) that the nice man is *very* good at this. They can't lose this scene by
   accepting gifts; they can only bank a quiet question for later.

## Session Timeline (the gate)

**Finish the fight → (optional, player-driven) chores → LONG REST → Lucius.**

Lucius does **not** appear until after the long rest. Everything between the fight and the rest is
the party's to fill or skip. Hold the line on the gate — if they try to summon a quartermaster
before sleeping, "Barkinar's man will come by in the morning, boss."

## Scene 1 — The Gargoyles (cold open, the one fixed scene)

### Setup
- **Where:** the Water Temple grand hall (azurite-and-malachite, the sea-glow, the bronze
  fish-head basins, the serpentine idol, the round bronze altar full of coins/gems/seashells).
- **State as of 6/14 end:** Zephyr disturbed the altar relief; the idol rotated; four stone
  gargoyles animated. **One already destroyed** by Calmer's 4th-level Spirit Guardians; **two
  more wounded and slowed** (took 21 and 20, failed the save); a fourth in play. Initiative had
  Zephyr & Sequoia at 27, Zinnia at a miserable 5, Calmer mid-pack. Calmer's Spirit Guardians
  aura is still up and grinding them.
- **Reference:** `notes/statblocks/213-water-temple-juggernaut.md` for the room's heavier guardian
  if you want to escalate; the gargoyles themselves are standard (AC 15, ~52 HP, CR 2 each).

### Approach
This is a **victory-lap mop-up**, not a crisis. Two slowed gargoyles and one fresh against a
rested-enough level-8 party with Spirit Guardians ticking is one, maybe two rounds. Let it be
*cool*: Zephyr's sneak attacks, Sequoia's blade, Zinnia finally rolling above a 5, the aura
scouring the stone. Calmer wanted *"every last one of them dead, dead, dead"* — give him the
carnage he asked for.

> **Milestone:** Securing the Water Temple is a **level-up — the party hits 9th** (your
> one-level-per-temple-cleared rule). Decide whether that lands the moment the last gargoyle
> falls, or once they've actually cleared/claimed the deeper Water chambers. Either way, the
> Lucius scene then plays to a freshly-9th-level party — worth a beat of table celebration before
> the quiet morning.

### Branch — if they want the idol / altar intact
The serpentine idol and the basins are *fixtures*, not loot you can carry — but the altar basin
holds coins, a sprinkling of gems, and seashells (real, modest treasure). The drapery behind the
idol and the side doors lead deeper into Water Temple proper (Areas 214, 216, 220-222 — leaderless
but inhabited; save for if they push on).

### Branch — if a player does something reckless on low HP
Someone diving into the deeper Water chambers bloodied is how a mop-up becomes a real fight (the
Underpriests, water elementals, and the trolls are all still down there). Let the room *imply*
that — the open doors, a far-off splash — so the smart move (claim this hall, rest before pushing)
feels like their idea.

### NPC notes
- **The gargoyles** — mindless guardians; no interiority, no parley. Pure cold open.

## Scene 2 — Before the Rest (modular, player-driven, optional)

Whatever the party chooses to do before bedding down. Pre-staged so you can drop any of these in,
in any order, on demand. They may do all, some, or none. Don't push — offer the hooks and follow.

### 2a — The Water Temple Treasury (audit + tithe)
- Belsornig's vault (in/adjacent to Area 215) plus the altar basin. Substantial gold/gems,
  Belsornig's personal effects, the contents of his **trapped iron chest** (the small key the
  party took when they killed him opens it — if they kept it).
- **The political wrinkle:** Barkinar expects a *tithe*. The party can underreport — but his people
  will look. First quiet test of whether the party skims their own bosses. (No punishment tonight;
  it goes in the profile.)
- **Failure is material:** a botched check on the trapped chest is a poison-needle or a glyph — the
  cost is HP or a lost item, not death. Sequoia's the obvious opener.

### 2b — The Troll Contract (Areas 218-219)
- Belsornig recruited trolls before he died; they expect payment. **Troll Chieftain ~CR 5.**
- **Three clean options:** **pay them** from Belsornig's treasury (cheap; sets the party up as
  employers; Dren will grumble — *"trolls eat people, boss"*); **break the contract by force**
  (expensive in HP, satisfying, and they regenerate — bring fire/acid); or **hire them onward** as
  the party's own muscle (Dren objects louder; but a troll warband is a real asset for the Nodes).
- **Voice — the Chieftain** (slow, transactional, not stupid): *"The fish-man promised gold by the
  dark of the moon. Fish-man is dead. You are not fish-man. So. You pay the dead man's debt, or you
  are the next thing we are owed."*

### 2c — The Chest of Rao (the moral beat)
- The Chest is in Earth Temple holdings (Hartsch's quarters / the Earth vault). Barkinar named it
  as something the Greater Temple "has interest in." Brother Eldin, months ago, begged them to
  return it to the Archclericy of Veluna. They handed it to Romag against that advice.
- **The choice:** hand it over (compliance), hide it (leverage — "we know you have it"), or
  substitute a convincing fake (trickery). **There is no clean option** — that's the point.
- **Voice — Calmer's conscience:** this is the one that should needle *Calmer* specifically, given
  how deep into "Prophet Calmer" he's gone. Zinnia's "you're going in too deep" warning has teeth
  here.

> **GM-only:** Hedrack does not actually need the Chest (`phase3_endgame.md`). *Whatever* they do
> serves him — hand over = compliance logged; hide = leverage acquired; fake = useful trickery
> noted. Don't telegraph it. Let them agonize, and let the choice feel weighty even though the
> house wins either way.

### 2d — The Necromancer (flush-out)
- Someone has been raising freshly-dead Earth Temple members as ghouls (the party fought a batch
  during the war). With Hartsch dead and Dren commanding, the party's authority is enough to start
  flushing them out — interrogate Dren's troops, sweep the morgue-corridors, set a watch on the
  corpse-piles the war left.
- **What a sweep turns up tonight:** a fresh kill that *shouldn't* be a ghoul yet but is; drag-marks
  toward an unexplored door; an Earth Temple soldier who "saw someone in the dark who wasn't one of
  ours." Enough to confirm the necromancer is real, local, and active — not enough to corner them
  yet.

> **GM-only:** Identity is still open (`_TEMP_planning.md`). Strong candidates to braid in later:
> a Falrinth/Lolth operation, or a Greater Temple necromancer Barkinar "forgot" to mention. Keep it
> a loose menace this session; don't resolve it before the rest.

### 2e — Dren's Formalization & Kelno Follow-up (short political beats)
- **Dren:** Calmer already declared him Supreme Commander of the Earth Temple forces in-fiction
  (6/14). This is the *ceremony* — a swearing-in in front of the right witnesses so Barkinar's
  report has a name to point at. Cheap, warm, no combat. Good place for a character beat.
- **Kelno:** the party's conditional #2. Barkinar clocked him standing beside them. A check-in
  scene if the party wants reassurance — Kelno is reliable *for now* (betrayal isn't profitable
  while they're his ticket), and he'll happily prove useful (goblin scouts, the Air Temple
  treasury, intel) to stay indispensable.

## Scene 3 — The Long Rest (the gate)

The party beds down (Hartsch's comfortable chambers, most likely — Calmer's prize). The world ticks
while they sleep. Use these as morning texture:

- **The necromancer** worked overnight (if 2d went unaddressed: another body's missing; if it was
  addressed: a half-finished raising abandoned mid-ritual — they got close enough to spook someone).
- **The trolls** settle or stew depending on 2b.
- **Barkinar's Blackthorn Ogre** stands its honest watch by the door all night, blinking slowly.
- **The Temple breathes** — distant settling of the leaderless Fire and Water remnants; a goblin
  scuttles past on Kelno's errand; somewhere far below, the Greater Temple is quiet.

> **Sequencing note:** If you intend to keep Falrinth's "golden eyes, stall him" warning (Open
> Decision in `lucius_network.md`), the recommendation is to **NOT** fire it tonight — let Lucius
> arrive un-warned. Falrinth's contact lands *after* this session, so the party meets the nice man
> first and gets told to fear him second.

## Scene 4 — Lucius, the Friendly Quartermaster (centerpiece, post-rest)

The first thing the morning brings. **Run the whole scene off `lucius_network.md`** — that doc has
his intel picture, his voice, the supply chain, and the magic-item menu. Summary here; detail there.

### Setup
- **When:** immediately after the long rest. Unhurried, announced.
- **The herald:** Dren, uneasy — *"Boss, there's a man downstairs says Barkinar sent him to 'see to
  the household.' Soft-looking. Gray. Smiles too much. I had Krael pat him down; he laughed about
  it and let us. I don't trust harmless."*
- **Who he is (GM-only):** Lucius Graeme, alias "Aldren," Hedrack's golden-eyed courier and the
  most dangerous man the party will meet this month — playing the least dangerous man in the
  building. This session the menace is **entirely holstered.** He is here to *give*, not to take.

### Approach — the gift and the open hand
He comes with a gift (wine / potions / a returned-and-identified item — Open Decision 5 in
`lucius_network.md`), fills their cups, undersells himself, and says the magic words: *"Tell me what
you're short of, and I'll fix it."* Then he delivers — from the temple's own confiscated vaults
(now), from Nulb's black market (days), or "by inquiry" for the rare ask (a future favor).

> **Lucius (lift verbatim):** *"Barkinar asked me to make sure the new management has everything it
> needs, and between us I think he undersold the job. Four prophets in a day! Sit, sit. I brought
> you something, and then you're going to tell me what you're short of, and I'm going to fix it.
> That's the whole of my talent — I carry things from clever people to clever people."*

### The menu
See `lucius_network.md` → **The Menu & The Price.** Level-8/9-appropriate, sourced and gated. **Get
the GM's sign-off on the actual item list before play** — it's the one real power-level decision in
the session. Hard limits: nothing that stacks reliable escape + invisibility + free action; the
build-defining stuff is "let me make some inquiries" (a future obligation), never a freebie.

### Branch — the party is eager (most likely)
They make a shopping list. Lucius is delighted, produces what he can on the spot, promises the rest,
and leaves them visibly better-armed and feeling, for once, *supported.* The leash is on and they're
grinning. **This is the intended outcome** — don't undercut it.

### Branch — the party is cautious ("what's the catch?")
He doesn't flinch — that's what makes him frightening. *"The catch? You're suspicious of a gift. Good.
Stay that way; it'll keep you alive down here. There's no catch this morning. There may be a day I
ask a favor of clever friends. This isn't it. Today I'm just the man with the warehouse."* Honest,
disarming, and true. Caution costs them nothing and gains them nothing — let them feel clever for
asking, then take the goods anyway.

### Branch — the party tests or probes him
Insight is allowed and *should* occasionally land a flicker — he's human, the fool-act has a seam.
A success gives them a *thread, not a confession*: "he's sharper than he's pretending, and he's
working very hard to be liked." He recovers smoothly and is warmer than ever. He will not be
rattled, cornered, or flipped in a gift-giving scene — anyone trying just spends goodwill. If they
get aggressive, he deflects with a joke and leaves goods on the table on his way out: *"I'll leave
these. Send word through Dren when you've thought of a list. No hurry."*

### NPC notes
- **Lucius "Aldren" Graeme** — *wants:* to make himself indispensable and to read these unknowns
  while giving nothing away; *worry (hidden):* he didn't choose them and doesn't yet know what they
  are; *voice tic:* fills cups, undersells himself, turns every compliment back on them; *never (this
  session):* probes the Prince, names Senshock as a target, raises his voice, or lets the warmth drop.
- **Dren** — the honest counterweight; his distrust of "harmless" is the party's permission to stay
  a little wary without breaking the warm scene.

## Anticipated Player Questions

- **"Can't we just take whatever we want from the vaults ourselves? We rule the place."** → Yes —
  but they don't know where anything is or what it does, and the Greater Temple's stores aren't
  inventoried *to them.* That's exactly the gap Lucius fills. Let them try; let it be slow and partial
  without him. (Leaves the door open for a player to *insist* on self-sufficiency — fine, it just
  means less free gear and a colder relationship with the most useful man in the dungeon.)
- **"What's the catch with this guy?"** → There's no catch *they can find tonight.* See the cautious
  branch. The honest answer Lucius gives is also the true one for this session.
- **"Who's Senshock? Falrinth? Barkinar mentioned them."** → Lucius deflects warmly and gives nothing
  load-bearing. *"You'll meet Senshock. Careful man. More wine?"* Don't spend those reveals here.
- **"Do we have to pay the trolls / hand over the Chest / tithe the treasury?"** → No hard
  enforcement tonight. Everything is graded at Barkinar's *check-in*, not now. The pressure is
  reputational and deferred — which is scarier than a deadline if you play it right.

## Stage Directions

- **Win the gargoyle fight quickly and let it feel good.** It's a victory lap. Don't grind it.
- **Hold the rest-gate.** Lucius is a morning beat. Whatever the party does before sleeping, he comes
  *after.*
- **Let the party feel served, and do not tip the leash.** The Lucius scene fails if they leave
  suspicious-and-empty-handed *or* if the GM winks at the camera. Warm, generous, clean. The dread is
  ours, not theirs — yet.
- **When someone makes the Insight on Lucius, give them the flicker and move on.** A thread, not a
  confession. Then have him be even warmer. The contrast is the unease.
- **Don't fill Dren's silences.** His "I don't trust harmless" should sit in the air for a beat.
