# Phase 3 — The Engineered Collapse

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the bridge session (Kelno's quarters) and the endgame sessions that follow.

---

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

The Greater Temple door stays sealed until the lesser-temple war "runs its course." A course is run when the party is alive and at least **two of the four other faction leaders are dead**. That's it. Anything that produces that state opens the door.

The four leaders: **Hartsch** (Earth, party's puppet), **Alrrem** (Fire), **Belsornig** (Water), **Kelno** (Air).

## The Default Trajectory (what happens if the party does nothing)

This is the baseline. Lock it in your head — every party action either accelerates, redirects, or wastes against this current.

- **Hartsch dies.** Belsornig has already dispatched assassins in retaliation for the Broken Blades raid. Without intervention, they reach him.
- **Alrrem dies.** He's bankrupt, unhinged, and committed to either a mass-suicide pyre ritual or a suicidal frontal charge. Earth Temple forces (or his own ritual) finish him.
- **Belsornig survives.** Cunning. Retreats with treasury when his line breaks. Lives to threaten the party later.
- **Kelno survives.** Has spent the war scavenging and bribing. Hasn't risked anything.

Default outcome: door opens with two living elemental leaders still in play. The party walks into the Greater Temple with Belsornig and Kelno at their back. Bad.

## The Clever Play (which is also what Kelno wants)

The party's optimal move is to kill the survivors, not the doomed:

1. **Kill Belsornig.** Removes the assassin operator and the cunning long-term threat.
2. **Either kill or recruit Kelno.** Both endings work. Kelno has done the math: with Hartsch, Alrrem, and Belsornig dead, the upper temple is the party's. He wants to be #2 in that arrangement.
3. **Let the default trajectory finish Hartsch and Alrrem.** No party action required. They die on their own.

End state: all four other leaders dead OR three dead + Kelno as a subordinate. Either way, the party owns levels 1-2. Door opens. Greater Temple negotiates with the new bosses.

## Bridge Session — Kelno's Quarters

The party is at the cleared Air Temple secret door. Calmer is being dragged off by Dren's mercs. Sequoia, Zephyr, Zinnia decide on recon.

### Setup
- **Time:** half a day, in-fiction. The war advances in the background (see "Background Ticks" below).
- **PCs:** likely three (Sequoia, Zephyr, Zinnia). If Calmer comes along, his Turn Undead is unnecessary here — the wraith is dead, no undead in Air Temple proper.
- **Air Temple footprint:** four canon areas on Level Two. **Area 209/209a** (the Processional Corridor + the secret corridor with the gargoyle fountain — Kelno's eastern escape route), **Area 225** (Grim Chamber — Kelno's residence), **Area 226** (Kitchen — bugbear/goblin barracks), **Area 227** (Hall of Triumphal Evil — bone-littered ruin, no live defenders). The party's entry point (the secret door they exposed in chapter 26) opens onto the Air Temple corridor system somewhere in or near 209.

### Approach — Kitchen (226) first

The kitchen is the only meaningful outer defense and it's the tactical choice scene before Kelno. **Canonically 6 bugbears + 4 goblins**, but Kelno has been running a bribery and scavenging operation across the level for the entire war — most of his force is out:
- 2-3 bugbears remain (the loyalist core; the bribed ones are dead or defected, including the one the party already killed)
- 1-2 goblins remain as servants (most are out scavenging the war zone for Air Temple loot)
- The room is even dirtier and emptier than the canon description suggests — long trestles half-bare, sausages strung from the arches, heaps of rags and skins in the corners

**Three party options, all viable:**

1. **Stealth past.** Sequoia and Zephyr can thread the kitchen via the rags-and-skins corners and the pantry NE. DC 14 Stealth (the bugbears are bored and not actively watching). Success = arrive at Kelno's door undetected — *and* he notices anyway, because his goblin spy in the kitchen rafters has been signalling him for hours. This is the cleanest path; it also tells Kelno the party are exactly the operators he hoped they were.

2. **Talk past.** Claim Kelno-business — "we are here under arrangement, your master expects us." Deception DC 14 against bored bugbears who don't actually know whether Kelno is expecting anyone. Likely succeeds. The party loses no resources but they have *announced themselves* — Kelno is now waiting with his pitch fully prepared, which slightly degrades their leverage.

3. **Fight loud.** 2-3 bugbears + 1-2 goblins is a fast encounter for the party at their current level. **But the moment combat starts, Kelno triggers Darkness 15' radius and flees** east through the drapery → 209a secret corridor → either corridor 209 or down the staircase to Level Three (canon escape pattern). The party loses the negotiation entirely. They get the loot in his room but not the man — and Kelno becomes a Falrinth-equivalent loose end on Level Three, holding grudges, knowing their faces.

The party should be **rewarded for sneaking or talking** and punished for the loud assault. Make this clear in the framing: the kitchen's emptiness is itself a clue that this is meant to be slipped through, not stormed.

### Kelno's Chamber (Area 225) — what the party sees

Match the canon for atmosphere. Read or paraphrase:

> *"The room is entirely gray — carpeting, plaster, wall hangings, furnishings, all the same dull ashen shade. Lamps of grayish metal on a small table and a wall shelf throw a dim, watery light. A pair of upholstered chairs by the table. Pewter dishes. A plain crock half-filled with mead. The shelves hold some foodstuffs, a few scrolls and books, and — eye-catching against the gray — **several human and demi-human skulls**, polished and arranged carefully, each labeled in a thin spidery hand. A narrow bed in the northeast corner with a small stand beside it. A heavy drapery covers the eastern wall."*

The skulls are the tell. Up close: each is labeled with a name and a date. **Three are recent.** One bears the name of an Air Temple apprentice "killed by Fire," another "killed by Water," a third "Marad — gone to Lolth." Kelno is keeping count. The labels suggest a man who memorializes his losses while planning his payback.

The wardrobe holds the **ceremonial cassock of silver cloth** (crystal beads and jet, worth 1,500 gp). The drapery conceals the eastern escape corridor — gargoyle fountain, arms pile (11 suits of bugbear armor + assorted weapons, 50 gp each to the right buyer), and a **locked chest trapped with a poison needle** containing only 11 gp (the canon's joke — the real treasure is the silver cassock, not the chest).

### Is Kelno waiting?

**Yes. He is in one of the upholstered chairs by the table.** He has been there since the goblin spy in the kitchen rafters signaled him — possibly longer. The mead in his crock has stopped steaming. He has prepared a second pewter cup for whoever shows up first.

When the party enters, he raises his hand — palm open, no weapon — and gestures to the other chair.

> *"Forgive the small theatrics. I have only one chair to offer, but if your captain takes it I think we can manage a conversation. I have been expecting you for two days."*

He does not stand. He does not draw his mace. His hand is nowhere near the dagger of venom at his belt. He is making it as physically clear as possible that this is a *meeting*, not a fight.

**What's on the table beside the mead:**
- An open ledger (his bribery records — defection lists, who got paid what, who he still trusts)
- An intercepted Belsornig dispatch (proof that Belsornig's assassins are en route to Hartsch)
- A folded map showing the Greater Temple entrance route
- A small lacquered box (contains the *dagger of venom* — he intends to offer it as the close of his pitch, "for the Belsornig you are about to make")

### What's in Kelno's quarters
- **The Air Temple treasury** — coin and items scavenged from the war (substantial; Kelno has been busy). Stored in the arms cache east, plus in the trapped chest (most of the haul moved deeper for safety; what's here is operational reserve)
- **Kelno's ledger** — bribery records, defection lists, predictions about who survives
- **A marked map of the Greater Temple entrance** — corroborates the route the party already has from the freed prisoner
- **Correspondence** — at least one intercepted Belsornig dispatch (proof Belsornig's assassins are en route to Hartsch)
- **The ceremonial silver cassock** (1,500 gp; if the party takes it, *the Air Temple itself loses its high-cleric vestment* — meaningful if anyone tries to install a successor)
- **The skulls** (no mechanical value; pure character texture)
- **Kelno himself** — in his chair, waiting

### Kelno's behavior when discovered

He does not fight. He raises his hands. He smiles. He talks.

**The pitch (use as a speech draft, paraphrase at table):**

> "Before you do something dramatic — let me show you something. *(taps the ledger)* Belsornig's assassins reached Hartsch's antechamber an hour ago. By tonight your puppet is meat. Alrrem is on his pyre as we speak; he won't last the morning. That leaves me, and Belsornig, and — most interestingly — you.
>
> Here is the part I think you have not let yourselves think yet. When this war ends and the great door opens, *somebody* is going to be standing on top of the upper temple. The whoever-it-is becomes Greater Temple's new partner up here. That is going to be you. I have known it for a week.
>
> So. You can kill me. I have the worst guards in the dungeon and you have me. But then you walk into the Greater Temple alone, with no Air Temple, no goblin eyes in the dark, no bribery network. *Or* you walk out of here with me as your #2. I run Air Temple under you. My goblins are your goblins. My informants are your informants. You get the throne, I get to keep my office. We split nothing because there is nothing to split — you are obviously the senior partner.
>
> Belsornig is the problem. Not me. Belsornig is going to kill Hartsch within the day. Alrrem is already dead and doesn't know it. Kill Belsornig. Let me help you do it. After, we talk to whoever opens that door together."

### The party's choice

**Option A — Take the deal.** Kelno becomes a (treacherous, useful) lieutenant. He provides:
- Goblin scouts and runners across both levels
- His bribery network turned against Belsornig's bugbears
- Intel on the Greater Temple voice (he can name a candidate — see "Behind the Door" below)
- A second front against Belsornig if the party wants it

The trust question is real. Kelno will betray them the moment betrayal is profitable. But during Phase 3, betrayal is *not* profitable for him — the party is his ticket to the new regime.

**Option B — Kill him.** Clean. No loose end. Take the ledger, the treasury, the map, the dispatch. Phase 3 plays out the same but without the goblin asset and the smug villain in the room when the door opens.

**Option C — Leave him alive but uncommitted.** Worst option. He'll go to Belsornig with what he knows about the party. The doc should NOT signal this as viable — Kelno himself will note that this is unstable and one of the other two options must be chosen.

### Closing the bridge session
However the party decides on Kelno, they exit with:
- The Belsornig assassins-en-route-to-Hartsch dispatch
- The map confirming Greater Temple route
- The understanding that Belsornig is the priority target
- (If A) Kelno as ally
- (If A or B) The treasury

They emerge into a temple where the war has noticeably worsened.

## Post-Bridge Session — The Execution

### Cold open

Party emerges from the Air Temple passage. Sounds of distant combat. A runner sprints past, doesn't even register them. **Dren intercepts them** at the Earth Temple boundary.

**Dren's read (line draft):**

> "Captain to captain — Hartsch is finished. Belsornig's people are at his door. He's screaming for you. I'd say save him, but… you didn't put me in charge of three companies of mercenaries because I tell you what to do. You tell me. Alrrem's on his pyre. Belsornig's pulling his treasury out the south route — slow, heavy, vulnerable. Your call."

Hartsch's note arrives via a panicked runner mid-conversation: *"They are at the door. Where are you. I gave you everything."*

The party makes the call. The expectation is **let Hartsch die, hit Belsornig.** If they hesitate, Kelno (if allied) prompts; if not allied, Zinnia's read on Dren's tone is enough.

### Scene: Belsornig's Salon — The Assassination

**Location:** Area 215, Belsornig's private Salon inside the Water Temple. He's in his chambers, *planning* an evacuation, not executing one. Maps spread, treasury still in its vault, two Underpriests reporting from the front. The party reaches him via stealth route (rafters, side passage, or — if Kelno allied — a goblin-scouted approach the Air Temple has used to spy on Water for weeks).

**Setup:** Pure setpiece for Sequoia and Zephyr. Zinnia provides the read on the guard rotation. The bulk of the Water Temple's forces are elsewhere — fighting Fire in the steam corridors, defending the outer perimeter against Hartsch's leaderless remnants. Belsornig is comparatively unguarded *in* his sanctum because his army is *out* fighting.

**The kill:** Belsornig has the Rod of Smiting and high AC, but he's reading a dispatch, hands occupied. The cinematic moment: Sequoia from above, Zephyr from below, Belsornig dies between them before he can raise the rod. Underpriests die or flee deeper into Water Temple proper.

**What they take from the sanctum (not the whole temple):**
- Rod of Smiting (on his person)
- The ledgers and intercepted correspondence (in his desk — Vesrek/Chimera originals, his bribery network, his read on Greater Temple)
- A small key (opens his personal trapped chest — leave the chest in place as a return-trip puzzle)

**What stays behind for later exploration:**
- The Water Temple treasury (Area 215 vault and adjacent rooms) — fully intact
- Water Temple's remaining Underpriests, gargoyles, water elementals, and the Troll Chieftain (if recruited by Belsornig in his last days)
- The deeper Water Temple chambers (Areas 213, 214, 216, 220-222) — leaderless but inhabited; cultists in disarray, not abandonment
- Belsornig's trapped chest, his spell components, his shrine

**Cover impact:** Witnesses are Underpriests who don't survive or who flee deeper. Belsornig's death is attributed to Earth Temple by anyone who hears about it. Party cover holds. **Water Temple becomes a leaderless dungeon-crawl site, not an empty room.**

### Discovered Tableau: Hartsch

The party returns to the Earth Temple. Hartsch is dead in his office. Belsornig's assassins (two of them) are also dead — Dren's Broken Blades found them in the corridor and finished them. Hartsch's body has multiple stab wounds and a slit throat. The forged orders book is open on his desk.

**Dren's line:** "We got the assassins. Too late for him. Orders, boss?"

The party is now the de facto power on the upper temple. Dren says "boss" without irony.

### Distant Spectacle: Alrrem's End

Smoke and roaring from the Fire Temple corridor (Area 212). The ritual misfires. The party hears it from the next level: a wet crack of fire that consumes itself, then silence.

When anyone is brave enough to look — and most aren't, the Fire Temple is still full of zealots who don't know their leader is dead — they find Alrrem charred dead at the center of a glassed-over ritual circle, Varek Redflame dead beside him with the Flametongue still in his grip. No fire elemental was summoned. The ritual ate itself.

**Crucially, Earth Temple forces do NOT push in to loot.** Hartsch is dead. Their command structure is gone. The remaining Earth Temple soldiers fall back to defensive positions in their own territory. **Fire Temple is left leaderless but populated** — zealots, fire snakes, the wounded Salamander (if alive), Underpriests, Varek's body and Flametongue, treasure rooms, the brass doors of Area 212 — all still in place.

The party watches Alrrem's death without lifting a finger (Kelno's goblin scouts if allied, or just hearing the corridor go quiet if not). They will be the ones to walk into Fire Temple proper *later*, on their own terms, when they're ready to clear it as a dungeon. **The war killed the leader. It did not strip the rooms.**

### The Door

Once Belsornig is confirmed dead, the Hall of Trophies door — the one that has been opening and closing on its own — opens fully and stays open. Behind it, a corridor leading down. A voice from below:

> "Well. That was efficient. Come down. Bring your puppet master if he is alive. We should talk."

(If Kelno is allied, he glances at the party: "That would be Barkinar. He's the muscle. Be careful — but he respects function over loyalty. We are functional.")

### Barkinar Arrives — The Cultivation Begins

> **GM note:** Barkinar is the front for Hedrack's long game. See `hedrack_strategy.md` for the actual mission Hedrack is running. The party should NOT realize Barkinar is screening them for a much larger purpose. He plays this as a competent mid-level manager onboarding promising new hires — warm, professional, slightly intimidating, with hints of bigger work ahead.

Barkinar (Level 7 Cleric of the Greater Temple) doesn't wait for the party to descend. He comes UP, with a detachment of Blackthorn Ogres and Bugbears at his back. He walks the upper levels surveying the damage. He nods at Dren — professional acknowledgment. He turns to Kelno (if allied) and says, "Hello, Kelno. Useful as ever. Good." Then he turns to the party with what is, for him, genuine interest.

**Barkinar's opening (line draft — warmer than the previous version; he LIKES them):**

> "Romag. Hartsch. Alrrem. Belsornig. Four problems I had on a list this morning, and four problems gone by sundown. You will forgive me if I'm impressed — that is not the sort of efficiency the Greater Temple is used to seeing from the upper levels.
>
> Some practical matters. The upper temple is yours now. There are leaderless cultists in the Fire Temple, trolls on contract in the Water Temple, treasury unaccounted for, mercenaries who need a chain of command. There is also a chest — the one you delivered to Romag — that the Greater Temple has interest in. None of this is urgent in the sense that it will burn you alive tomorrow. All of it is urgent in the sense that the upper temple should look organized when I come back through. Shall we say a week?
>
> One other thing. There are people downstairs you will want to be careful of, and one or two you might want to actually meet. **Senshock** — our Lord Wizard — has opinions about new arrivals. He is ambitious and paranoid; he is also useful. Be civil and let him underestimate you. **Falrinth** is a wizard who has been hiding from the Greater Temple for reasons of his own. We can talk about Falrinth later. For now, organize your house. We'll be in touch."

He doesn't wait for a response. He turns, says something quiet to one of the Blackthorn Ogres (which then stays behind, watching, "as an honor guard until you are properly equipped with a household"), and walks back through the door he came from. The door does not close behind him.

**What this scene establishes (for the party):**
- They've been promoted, not hired. The tone is "welcome to management," not "you work for me now."
- They have a week before Barkinar's check-in
- The Blackthorn Ogre is visible surveillance — Barkinar is being honest about that
- Two names are now in the air: **Senshock** and **Falrinth**. Both are deliberate seeds. The party will start hearing more about both.
- The door stays open, not closed. They can descend whenever they're ready — but Barkinar's expectation is they handle the upper level first.

**What this scene establishes (for the GM):**
- Barkinar has just begun the cultivation arc. Everything from this point forward serves Hedrack's long game (see `hedrack_strategy.md`).
- His warmth is real — he genuinely admires competence — AND it is also instrumental. Both at once. Critically, **Barkinar himself does not know about Hedrack's coup.** He believes Hedrack is loyal to Iuz and that the party is being cultivated for legitimate Iuz-aligned operations. His honesty is the structural load-bearing element of the whole deception. See `hedrack_strategy.md` → "The Tier System."
- The Chest of Rao is now in play but as a *test*, not a deliverable. Hedrack benefits whether the party hands it over or hides it.
- The Senshock and Falrinth name-drops are bait the party will eventually take. Plant them now; let them grow.
- **Senshock is NOT Iuz's failsafe.** He is an independent power-seeker who wants the Orb for himself. Barkinar's casual framing (*"ambitious and paranoid... let him underestimate you"*) is accurate but incomplete. Senshock will eventually be the target of Arc A — which is sold to the party as eliminating a renegade but actually serves to remove a rival to Hedrack.

End of Phase 3 proper. The "Barkinar's List" section below is what they have to deal with — and it is also their *audition* for the deeper game.

## Post-War State of the Temples (preserved for future plunder)

The war kills the *leaders*, not the *territories*. By design, the Fire and Water temples remain populated, treasured, and explorable after the door opens. The party's "reward" for winning the war isn't an empty dungeon — it's a leaderless one they can dismantle at their pace.

### Fire Temple (Areas 204-208, 212) — post-Alrrem
- Alrrem and Varek Redflame dead in Area 212 (ritual circle); Flametongue recoverable from Varek
- The brass doors of Area 212 still closed; the ritual chamber is a tomb until someone opens it
- Remaining forces: zealot cultists (leaderless, defensive), fire snakes still roaming, the Salamander if still alive, possibly the surviving Fire Temple warlock cohort if any (GM call)
- Treasure: Area 212 altar treasures, Alrrem's personal effects, the temple's reserve coffers (Alrrem was bankrupt for ops, not for sacred artifacts)
- Dynamic: no organized command. The party can negotiate a surrender, slaughter the leaderless, or pick the territory apart room by room.

### Water Temple (Areas 213-216, 220-222) — post-Belsornig
- Belsornig dead in Area 215, his sanctum stripped of *only what the party carried out* (Rod of Smiting, ledgers, small key)
- Treasury vault still intact in/adjacent to 215
- Belsornig's trapped iron chest still in his sanctum — opens with the small key the party took
- Remaining forces: Underpriests (multiple, scattered), Water Temple gargoyles, water elementals, Troll Chieftain and trolls if Belsornig recruited them (likely yes, given default trajectory)
- The deep chambers (220-222) intact and inhabited
- Dynamic: cunning leaderless force. The trolls may try to leverage their contract for back pay; Underpriests may split into competing successor factions; gargoyles still hold the perimeter.

### Air Temple — depends on Kelno
- **If Kelno allied (took the deal in his chamber):** Air Temple is *the party's* territory. Goblin scouts and gnoll bribers report to them via Kelno. Air Temple altar and treasury are accessible. Not adversarial. Kelno hands over the ceremonial cassock as a gesture; the dagger of venom comes with his pitch.
- **If Kelno killed cleanly in negotiation (party chose execution):** Air Temple is hollowed out — goblins scatter, bribery network collapses. Party keeps the cassock, ledger, map, dispatch, dagger of venom, and trapped chest loot. Physical rooms still have altar treasures and bugbear gear caches. Light content.
- **If Kelno escaped (party assaulted loudly, he triggered Darkness and fled):** **Worst outcome.** Kelno survives on Level Three as a Falrinth-equivalent loose end — he knows the party's faces, holds grudges, will hire assassins. His goblin network may regroup under his command and continue to operate at the party's expense. Phase 3 still closes (Belsornig dies, door opens), but the party walks into Phase 4 with an extra living enemy who has every reason to expose them.

### Earth Temple — under party control via Dren
- Hartsch dead; Dren and the Broken Blades occupy the territory under party direction
- Romag's chambers, the gnoll/bugbear territory, the prison chamber, the harpy hall — all party-administered
- Earth elementals and remaining Earth Temple forces follow Dren's orders
- Not content to plunder; this is the party's home base on the upper levels

### Barkinar's List — What the Party Owes Within One Week

Barkinar's expectations translate into a concrete to-do list. Each item is a dungeon-crawl mission or a political/negotiation scene with content attached. The party can sequence these however they want.

> **GM note:** Every item on this list is *also* a screening test for Hedrack's cultivation arc (see `hedrack_strategy.md`). Sloppiness here doesn't fail the party out — Hedrack wants them to succeed and will extend rope as needed. But how they handle each item tells Hedrack what kind of asset they are, what they value, and where they can be leveraged. Track the party's choices; they become Hedrack's profile of them.

1. **Pacify the Fire Temple (Areas 204-208, 212).** Leaderless cultists, fire snakes, possibly a wounded Salamander. The party chooses the method: storm the temple, negotiate surrender, install a puppet from among the surviving Underpriests, or clear it room by room. Recoverable: Varek's Flametongue, Alrrem's personal effects, the temple's sacred treasury.

2. **Resolve the Troll Contract (Water Temple, Areas 218-219).** Belsornig recruited the trolls before dying. They expect payment. The party can: pay them out of Belsornig's treasury (cheap, sets a precedent), break the contract by force (expensive in HP, satisfying), or hire them onto the party's side (Dren will object — trolls eat people). The Troll Chieftain is a CR 5 problem either way.

3. **Audit and Secure the Water Temple Treasury (Area 215 vault + Belsornig's trapped chest).** Tithe to the Greater Temple is expected. The party can underreport but Barkinar's people will look. Recoverable: significant gold/gems, Belsornig's personal magic items, the contents of the trapped chest (GM-pick — Rod of Smiting is already taken).

4. **Formalize Dren's Command of the Earth Temple Forces.** Hartsch is dead. Dren is acting. The party needs to install him formally so the Broken Blades and the remaining Earth Temple soldiers operate under a clear chain of command — and so Barkinar's report has someone to point at. This is a political scene (a swearing-in, a proclamation, a quiet word in front of the right witnesses) more than a combat one.

5. **Recover the Chest of Rao.** The Chest was delivered to Romag → inherited by Hartsch → now somewhere in the Earth Temple's holdings (Hartsch's quarters, the Earth Temple vault, or — if Belsornig's assassins took anything — possibly in Water Temple's loot now). Barkinar mentioned wanting it. Returning it surrenders a powerful artifact to the actual enemy. **Withholding or substituting it is the party's first major moral dilemma as upper-temple management** — they'll need to forge an account of its loss or quietly hide it. This is the scene Brother Eldin warned them about months ago.

  > **GM note:** Hedrack doesn't actually need the Chest. Whatever the party does with it serves him: hand it over = compliance recorded; hide it = leverage acquired ("we know you have it"); substitute it = useful trickery noted for future use. Don't telegraph this. Let the party agonize.

6. **Deal with Kelno (if not yet decided).** Barkinar saw him standing next to the party. If Kelno is allied, he's now part of the regime — but his loyalty is conditional. If the party kept him uncommitted, Barkinar's smile when greeting him is a warning the party needs to act on.

7. **Handle the Necromancer.** The unidentified necromancer who raised Earth Temple ghouls earlier is *still operating somewhere*. With Hartsch dead and Dren in command, the party's authority should be enough to flush them out — but the necromancer is still a free agent in their territory.

### Sequencing and Pacing

The party doesn't have to do these in order. But the week is finite. Expect them to prioritize:
- **Day 1-2:** Dren's formalization (cheap, fast, no combat) + Fire Temple recon
- **Day 2-4:** Fire Temple pacification (the big dungeon crawl)
- **Day 4-5:** Troll resolution + treasury audit
- **Day 5-6:** Chest of Rao crisis (the moral one)
- **Day 6-7:** Cleanup (necromancer, Kelno follow-up) and prep for Barkinar's check-in

If they try to skip Fire Temple or the Troll contract, Barkinar's check-in surfaces it — *"You left a leaderless cult in your basement. Why?"* — and they lose standing with the Greater Temple. Recovery is possible but costly.

### The Falrinth Contact — Mid-Week Wildcard

> **GM-only:** See `falrinth_strategy.md` for the full picture. Short version: Falrinth is a chaotic evil power-hungry wizard. He has the gemless Orb of Golden Death. He **serves Lolth** (Lolth's mission: keep the Orb gemless to prevent the Elder Elemental God's resurgence). Falrinth personally **chafes** under Lolth's leash and would love to restore the Orb for his own ascension. He **does NOT know about Hedrack's specific Zuggtmoy coup** — he assumes standard Temple ambition. He approaches the party transactionally, not heroically. The party killed Lareth (his Lolth ally) and Falrinth knows it.

Sometime during the cleanup week — recommend Day 3 or 4, after the party have settled into their new role but before they've met Lucius Graeme — Falrinth makes first contact.

**Recommended method:** an illusory image of a hooded man appears in the party's quarters late at night. He addresses one PC by name (Zinnia is the right read — most perceptive). The image weighs them silently before speaking. **Tone is oily, transactional, slightly amused.** Not warm. Not threatening. Evaluating.

**Falrinth's first-contact draft (paraphrase at table):**

> "Forgive the theatrics. I cannot be seen sending a runner, and you cannot afford to be seen receiving one. My name will mean nothing to you yet — soon enough, the man called Lucius Graeme will come to you with a request that involves me. Before you accept what he tells you, I would like you to consider a few small things.
>
> The Orb that the Temple is so concerned about — yes, I have it. I am not interested in giving it to Hedrack, to Senshock, or to any of the holy gentlemen who would like to see me dead. I am also not interested in giving it to you, but that is a separate conversation.
>
> What I *am* interested in is not having you turned against me by a flattering man with golden eyes. The trinket your cleric carries — the scarab with the four letters — you should be more careful with it. Lucius will recognize it. Senshock will recognize it. Both of them will use it against you in different ways. That is the kind of thing I notice and they have not. Yet.
>
> Lareth was a friend. I am told you handled him with discretion, which I appreciate more than you might expect. But you should know that there are other friends, and not all of us would extend the same patience. So when Lucius comes to you, do not commit. Stall. Find your way back to this room, and signal — a candle in the west window will reach me — and we will continue the conversation.
>
> Or do not. Report me to Barkinar; he will believe you. I will simply not be where you tell him I am. *But you will have made your choice."*

The image fades.

### What the Party Should Take Away (and what they probably won't, immediately)

This contact is a planted seed, not a recruitment pitch. Critical things this delivers:

1. **The TZGY scarab is identified as a known dangerous artifact.** This closes the silent thread of Calmer's mystery item — see `tzgy_scarab_urgent.md`. The party now knows it's a *credential of some kind*, that Lucius and Senshock will recognize it, and that they need to handle it carefully.
2. **Lucius Graeme is named** — golden eyes, "flattering man," coming for them. When Lucius later introduces himself (through Madame Selentis — see "After the Week" below), the party will recognize the signature.
3. **Lareth was someone's friend.** Falrinth's acknowledgment that the party killed Lareth — and his appreciation that they did it discreetly rather than vengeance for it — is a startling tell. The party will not immediately work out what it means (Lareth was Lolth's agent), but it should unsettle them.
4. **A contact channel** is offered (candle in the west window).
5. **A test:** do they report Falrinth to Barkinar, or do they wait?

Likely party reactions:
- **Distrust** — *"Who was that? Some Water Temple remnant? A Fire cultist? He admitted he's evil."*
- **Recognition** — *"Wait, the scarab? That thing Calmer's been carrying? It's important?"*
- **Caution** — *"Don't commit. Stall. That's reasonable advice regardless of who he is."*
- **Memory** — *"He knew we killed Lareth. Nobody knew that. Only Thorne and us."*

Don't push them toward a conclusion. Let the seed sit. The point is that **when Lucius Graeme arrives with the Senshock pitch, the party already has a voice in their heads telling them to stall.**

If the party reports the contact to Barkinar:
- Barkinar takes it seriously. Reports to Hedrack. Hedrack plays it cool — *"A clever attempt at division. They'll try again. Tell me if they do."*
- This *accelerates* Hedrack's timeline. Lucius approaches the party sooner.
- Confirms to Hedrack that the party are upward-reporters — useful information for him.
- **Does NOT compromise Falrinth.** He prepared for this exact outcome. Wherever Barkinar's people look, Falrinth is not there.

If the party keeps the contact secret:
- They are sitting on intelligence that could change the campaign. They don't know it yet.
- Falrinth will reach out again — more substantively — during Arc B when Lucius asks them to find him.

Either way, the seed is planted. Falrinth has identified himself as a player. The party know there is a wizard with the Orb, who Lucius will ask them to hunt, who appreciates discretion, and who recognizes their scarab. That is plenty.

### After the Week — Barkinar's Check-In

Barkinar returns. He walks the upper levels again, this time slower, asking specific questions. He nods at things he approves of. He raises an eyebrow at things he doesn't. He does not visibly punish anything — but he is *building a profile* (genuinely, in service of what he believes is Iuz's interest).

Then he says something like:

> "Well done. There is a man you should meet — Lucius Graeme. He handles certain matters for the Greater Temple that fall outside my particular expertise but are very much within yours. He prefers to do business away from the Temple itself; he will reach out to you through channels you already know. When you next have reason to be in Nulb, expect a message through Madame Selentis. She knows you are coming."

This is the canonical Hedrack→Lucius→Selentis chain. Per the Nulb supplement: Lucius personally extracted Selentis on Hedrack's orders 10 years ago and has run her ever since (see `nulb_network.md` for the full network).

**Why Selentis is the introduction vector:**
- The party already *knows* of Madame Selentis (warned about her by Eryk Velkori; saw the Elfar Twell execution as her work)
- She is feared, named, but unmet — the perfect intermediary because the party will *show up* if she summons them
- Routing through her gives Lucius plausible "neutral" ground — the party doesn't see Lucius at the Greater Temple's heart
- It also lets Selentis size up the party for her own files

**Likely first-contact flow:**
1. After the Barkinar check-in, the party has reason to go to Nulb (Phase 3 cleanup loose ends, the Erin Darkmoon obligation, the Tolub problem, anything)
2. They arrive in Nulb. A discreet runner — possibly Skole (party-friendly already; would be coerced into delivering the message) — pulls them aside. *"Madame Selentis would speak with you. The Old Mayor's Manor. After dark."*
3. They arrive at the Manor. Sir Torvel Hawkes is at the door. Selentis is in the war room — measured, gracious, dangerous. She does not actually have business with them. She is the courtesy.
4. "Aldren," a portly merchant in gray garments with golden asymmetrical eyes, is already there. He smiles. *"Ah — the famous Obsidian Edge. I was hoping our paths would cross. Madame Selentis has been kind enough to host."*
5. Lucius takes over from there. Selentis withdraws but does not leave the room. The party is now in a deeply controlled space — Hedrack's two senior outside-Temple operatives in one room with them.

If the party recognizes Lucius from Falrinth's warning (golden eyes), the test begins immediately: do they show the recognition? Do they stall as Falrinth said? Do they walk into the pitch?

### The Deep Irony

They set out to destroy the Temple. They've just become its most effective administrators. They are about to be promoted again — into work that will feel, when they first hear it, like the heroic culmination of their entire campaign.

The fantasy of "we're the clever spies" is intact for now. It will be hollowed out, slowly, over the coming sessions, as missions framed as "destroy the Temple's evil" turn out to have been something else entirely. When they finally realize it — and how deeply they've been used — that realization is the inflection point of the campaign.

> **GM-only:** See `hedrack_strategy.md` for the full Phase 4+ corridor. The corrected picture: **Hedrack is double-crossing Iuz.** Per canon (Greater Temple section), *"Iuz aims to subjugate Zuggtmoy"* while *"Hedrack... is Iuz's enforcer, keeping the elemental cults in check while secretly orchestrating Zuggtmoy's release."* The campaign overlay: Zuggtmoy has promised him godhood. The party are his "Divine Proxies" — irreplaceable agents he needs ALIVE to execute three manipulation arcs:
> - **Arc A** — assassinate **Senshock** (frame: dangerous renegade wizard; actual: blind Iuz's informant and remove a rival Orb-seeker)
> - **Arc B** — find, recover, and restore the **Orb of Golden Death** (frame: gather components to destroy it — *"only when whole can it be shattered"*; actual: power up Hedrack's phylactery; sub-mission B1 hits Falrinth, B2 sends the party into the Elemental Nodes for the Gems, B3 delivers the restored Orb to Hedrack's "consecration site")
> - **Arc C** — mount a "final battle" assault on the Greater Temple as cover for Hedrack's ascension ritual in Zuggtmoy's sanctuary
>
> Pitches are delivered by **Lucius Graeme** (Tier 1 — knows the coup, communes with Zuggtmoy directly), NOT by Barkinar (Tier 2 — Iuz operative who genuinely believes Hedrack is loyal). **Deggum is also Tier 1.** Senshock is Tier 2 (independent power-seeker; Iuz's likely informant whether he knows it or not). See `hedrack_strategy.md` → "The Tier System" for the full breakdown.
>
> The party is in *more* danger now than before — but Hedrack is also their most invested protector until the ritual completes. The realization should land during or after Arc C. Possible early reveals via Falrinth's continued contact, Barkinar noticing patterns, or the party's own investigation.

## Behind the Door — Who the Voice Is

Two options to pick from. Either works; choose for tone.

**Option 1 — Commander Barkinar (the war-in-the-temple doc default).** Level 7 Cleric, Greater Temple muscle. Direct. Wants to see what the party can do under interrogation. Tactical respect. Functions as "the general inspecting his new lieutenants."

**Option 2 — Hedrack (module canon).** Senior Greater Temple priest. More political, more dangerous. Treats the party as potential equals or potential threats — not subordinates. Higher-stakes opening scene.

Recommend Barkinar for the door-opening scene with Hedrack as the next-session reveal. Barkinar is the right scale for "first contact" — Hedrack is the next escalation.

## Failure Modes (for your reference, not the players')

- **Party tries to save Hartsch:** Hartsch lives, Belsornig still active, party splits attention, may run out of time/HP before reaching Belsornig. Door condition may not trigger if only Alrrem dies. War continues, party is stuck inside while the dungeon ecology collapses around them (ghoul tide etc.).

Recovery requires reconnecting with Dren and the Broken Blades — Dren remains loyal regardless of Earth Temple turning.
- **Kelno alliance betrayed mid-Phase 3:** Kelno wouldn't do this — Phase 3 is when betraying the party costs him everything. He betrays *after* the door opens, not during. So during execution he's reliable.

## What the Players Should Feel

The fantasy is: *we're the smart ones. We saw the play three moves before anyone else. We let our enemies kill each other and we picked off the survivors. We walk through the door as the new bosses.*

Every NPC line above is engineered to make that fantasy land. The intel is handed over. The plan is obvious. The execution is the cool part. Belsornig's death is a cinematic moment, not a deduction puzzle. The door opens because they were efficient.

Don't make them figure it out. Make them feel like they already had.
