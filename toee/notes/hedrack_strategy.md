# Hedrack's Strategy — GM-Only Long Game

Authoritative for this campaign. The party does NOT know any of this. Surface only through filtered NPC dialogue, observable consequences, and the slow accumulation of contradictions.

This doc is **layered**. Each layer is what a different observer believes Hedrack is doing.

---

## Layer 1 — What Iuz Believes

Iuz's view of his Supreme Commander and Mighty Priest:
- Loyal lieutenant.
- Managing Zuggtmoy's imprisonment as Iuz wishes (per canon: *"Iuz aims to subjugate Zuggtmoy"* — Iuz wants her contained, not freed).
- Preparing Temple forces for the invasion of Furyondy and Veluna.
- Working toward Iuz's ultimate strategic goal: a portal linking the Elemental Nodes to Iuz's capital, Dorakaa, to unleash his armies on the Flanaess.
- Monitoring rivals (Senshock as a useful internal foil; Falrinth as a rogue to be tracked).

This is the official story. Barkinar believes it. Most of the Greater Temple believes it. **The party will be told this version** through Barkinar, then through Lucius Graeme (in a more refined form).

## Layer 2 — Canon: What Hedrack Is Actually Doing

Per the Leadership canon ("Hedrack's Role" in the Greater Temple section): *"Hedrack, Supreme Commander of the Greater Temple, is Iuz's enforcer, keeping the elemental cults in check while **secretly orchestrating Zuggtmoy's release.**"*

He is **betraying Iuz.** Freeing Zuggtmoy directly contradicts Iuz's stated will. This is treason if Iuz finds out.

## Layer 3 — Homebrew: Why He Would Do It

Zuggtmoy has promised him **godhood, or at minimum superior demon status**, in exchange for freeing her *with all her powers intact, in his presence, at the right moment*.

This explains Layer 2. Hedrack is not just freeing Zuggtmoy — he is *defecting* to her and using Iuz's authority structure as the launch platform for his own ascension.

Three conditions for his ascension:
1. **The Orb of Golden Death must be located, retrieved, and fully restored.** Hedrack knows it exists, suspects Falrinth has it, but cannot investigate openly (Senshock would notice). The party must do the finding.
2. **The four Elemental Power Gems must be recovered from the Nodes** and fused into the Orb. The Nodes are too dangerous for Hedrack to risk Greater Temple troops. The party must do the recovering.
3. **Iuz must be blind** to all of this until the ritual completes. Senshock (Iuz's likely informant whether or not he knows it) must be neutralized. Barkinar (sincere Iuz operative) must be kept in the dark.

After ascension, Hedrack is a demigod — Iuz's response becomes a different problem.

## The Tier System

Critical: who knows what.

**Hedrack** — the architect.

**Lucius Graeme** — Temple Agent Courier, alias "Aldren." He is a Zuggtmoy cleric posing as Hedrack's spy master. He delivers the manipulative pitches to the party; he runs the Verbobonc/Nulb/Hommlet networks; he holds the Selentis leash; he is the operational hand of the coup.

- Cloak of the Forsaken Veil — Pass Without Trace 1/day.
- Mace of the Withering — exhaustion saves, necrotic vs frightened.
- Approaches the party as a friendly merchant (golden asymmetrical eyes are the tell).

**Deggum** — Commander of the Temple Guards. Half-elf, Cleric 5 / Wizard 4, Chaotic Evil. **Knows Zuggtmoy is the actual power behind EEG worship**: per canon, *"Despite his public devotion to the Elder Elemental God, Deggum knows the truth—Zuggtmoy, not the Elder Elemental Eye, holds the Temple's reins."* Wears the Black Scarab of Zuggtmoy.

- Was personally **recruited by Lareth the Beautiful** — which is a giant flag, since Lareth was a Lolth agent. Deggum may be a hidden Lolth sleeper inside Hedrack's security command. Treat as ambiguous; play him as loyal to Hedrack until/unless something forces a reveal.
- Brokered the Blackthorn Tribe alliance (Chieftain Garghuk, ogres). The Black Scarab is his authority over them.

### Tier 2 — Deceived (serves the cover story, would oppose if they knew)

**Barkinar** — Commander of the Greater Temple. Iuz operative (initiated personally by Hedrack two years ago). Has forsaken the Elder Elemental Eye and pledged to Iuz, while maintaining EEG cover. Believes Hedrack is loyal to Iuz and that the party is being cultivated for legitimate Iuz-aligned operations. **Positions himself as Hedrack's successor.**

- Genuinely impressed by competence — his warmth toward the party is real.
- His entire career depends on Iuz's continued patronage. If Hedrack's coup is exposed, Barkinar must turn on Hedrack or be implicated.
- **Sleeper ally for the party** post-reveal: senior Greater Temple cleric with direct reporting channels to Iuz and Greater Temple troops at his back. Keep him alive through the cultivation phase.

**Senshock** — Lord Wizard of the Greater Temple. Independent power-seeker, Verbobonc-born wizard recruited by Zuggtmoy years ago. **NOT Iuz's failsafe** — he is his own man. Wants the Orb to overthrow Hedrack and rule the Temple himself. Paranoid, arrogant, prone to overcomplicated schemes.

- Knows the Orb exists. Believes Falrinth might have it. Hunts it independently.
- Sees Barkinar, Lucius, and Deggum as Hedrack-tools to be exploited or used against him.
- TZGY scarab is his (see `tzgy_scarab_urgent.md`).
- His rivalry with Hedrack is the only reason Hedrack hasn't trusted him with the coup. If Senshock learned the truth he would either expose it to Iuz (to ruin Hedrack) or try to seize the Orb-restoration mission himself (more likely).

## The Five-Faction Landscape

The party are operating inside a five-way collision. Most of them are invisible from where the party stands. They will surface gradually.

### 1. Hedrack's Coup (Zuggtmoy-aligned)
Goal: ascension. Tier 1 above. The party are being cultivated as Divine Proxies — irreplaceable agents.

### 2. Iuz's Actual Interests
Goal: subjugate Zuggtmoy, build the Dorakaa portal via the Nodes, conquer Furyondy/Veluna/Verbobonc. Served by Senshock (probably unwittingly — Senshock thinks he serves himself) and Barkinar (knowingly, sincerely). Iuz himself is not onstage; he is the offstage god whose attention is the worst possible outcome for Hedrack.

### 3. Zuggtmoy (Demon Queen of Fungi)
The actual recipient of EEG worship. The elemental priests the party killed (Romag, Alrrem, Belsornig) were unknowingly serving her, believing they served Rentaq the Elder Elemental God. So is Kelno. So is the whole rank-and-file EEG cult. She has promised Hedrack his ascension. She is in her prison, conscious, scheming. She is also a Demon Queen with no loyalty to her bargains — Hedrack's "deal" is only as good as his control over the moment of liberation.

### 4. Lolth (Demon Queen of Spiders)
**Holds the key to the Elder Elemental God's prison.** Her interest is preventing the EEG's full resurgence — direct theological/political warfare with Zuggtmoy and Iuz both. Her tactics per canon: *"Sowing discord through misinformation, eroding Iuz's grip on the Temple, and fueling internal rivalries."* Her agents in the Temple:
- **Falrinth** (alive, has the gemless Orb — see `falrinth_strategy.md`)
- **Smigmal Redhand** (Falrinth's lover/partner, surface ops, infiltrating Hommlet)
- **Lareth the Beautiful** (DEAD — the party killed Lolth's other key Nexus operative in chapter 13 without knowing it)
- Possibly **Deggum** (recruited by Lareth — sleeper potential)
- **Kriitch** (Falrinth's quasit familiar — *gift from Lolth*)

**Lolth has reason to resent the party for killing Lareth.** Falrinth knows this. Smigmal would know. Their first contact with the party is therefore *cautious and conditional*, not friendly.

### 5. The Actual Cult of the Elder Elemental God
Serves **Rentaq** — the actual Elder Elemental God, shattered by Beory the Oerth Mother and scattered as the Elder Elemental Eyes (artifact shards). The cult is trying to find shards to resurrect him. **They consider Zuggtmoy, Iuz, AND Lolth to be enemies/obstacles.** Most cultists don't realize Zuggtmoy has usurped their worship.

### Bonus Faction — The Scarlet Brotherhood
Seeded in Nulb. **Elvanna at the Iron Lash brothel is their agent.** The party met her when they ransomed Serena. Separate operator from everyone above. Depth and goals unknown. Future thread.

## What Hedrack Cares About (priority order)

1. **Receive Zuggtmoy's gift** at the moment of her liberation. Godhood / superior demon status.
2. **Zuggtmoy freed with all powers intact**, in his presence, at the right moment.
3. **Senshock neutralized** before the Orb is restored — Iuz must stay blind.
4. **The Orb fully restored** (gems recovered, fused) and delivered to him for the final ritual.
5. **Plausible deniability with Iuz** maintained until ascension is complete.
6. **The party kept alive** through the three manipulation arcs — they are irreplaceable until the work is done.
7. **The party eliminated** after the ascension — no witnesses, no loose ends.

Items 6 and 7 are in sequential tension only. Until the ritual completes, Hedrack is the party's most invested protector. After, he is their executioner.

## What Hedrack Does NOT Care About

- The Chest of Rao (he'd accept it as a token; if the party hides it, it becomes leverage; otherwise irrelevant)
- The elemental temple leaders (all dead, all replaceable, all unwitting Zuggtmoy-servants)
- Day-to-day upper-temple operations (Barkinar handles those, post Phase-3 cleanup)
- Iuz's broader strategic interests (about to betray Iuz)
- Barkinar/Deggum/Senshock as individuals — will sacrifice any of them if the ritual requires
- The fate of Lolth, the Scarlet Brotherhood, the actual EEG cult — they are obstacles to manage, not goals

## The Three Manipulation Arcs

These are how the party gets weaponized. Each arc is pitched by **Lucius Graeme**, NOT by Barkinar — Barkinar genuinely doesn't know.

### Arc A — The Assassination of Senshock

**Why Hedrack needs it.** Senshock is the Lord Wizard. He's paranoid, he's hunting the same Orb, he can counterspell the liberation ritual, and he might be reporting to Iuz (Hedrack assumes worst case). He must die before the Orb is restored.

**The pitch (Lucius, not Barkinar):** Senshock is the *real* mad architect of the Temple's recent troubles. He has been summoning demons in his quarters. He is plotting against the Greater Temple's stability. He has agents the party should know about. *"We cannot move against him openly; he is too well-placed; but you, with your demonstrated talents for quiet action..."* Lucius will feed manufactured evidence — letters, witnesses, victims.

**What the party will be told:** Senshock is a dangerous internal renegade.

**What is actually happening:** Iuz's potential informant is being silently removed.

**The trap Falrinth tries to spring (if his Phase 3 contact landed):** *"Senshock is not your enemy. Whatever they tell you."* — Falrinth wouldn't know this in canon (he serves Lolth, not Iuz), but the **Lolth network's mission is to disrupt Hedrack's plans**, so Falrinth's contact serves to make the party hesitate on Arc A regardless of his motives. If the party hesitates on Senshock, the whole timeline slips for Hedrack.

**Risk.** If Senshock survives or escapes, Hedrack disavows the party as rogue actors. The Blackthorn Ogre "honor guard" upstairs becomes the cleanup crew.

### Arc B — The Restoration of the Orb (under false pitch)

**Why Hedrack needs it.** The Orb is somewhere in the temple. Falrinth probably has it. The four Gems are in the Nodes. The party are the only available asset that can do both the *finding* and the *surviving* — Hedrack cannot risk his own forces without alerting Iuz.

**The pitch (Lucius):** Three sub-missions, all framed as a heroic culmination.

- **B1 — Find the Orb.** Track Falrinth. *"Hedrack believes the Orb is here in the temple. He cannot tell you more without compromising his sources. The wizard Falrinth has been hiding — he is the most likely holder. Find him; recover it."*
- **B2 — Recover the four Gems from the Nodes.** *"The Nodes are perilous testing grounds full of trapped elementals and dangerous fanatics. Only adventurers of your caliber can survive them. We cannot send Temple forces there. They would not return."*
- **B3 — Restore and Deliver.** *"Fuse the Gems into the recovered Orb. Bring the restored artifact to a designated consecration site deep in the Greater Temple — where it can be unmade. There is an ancient prophecy: the Orb can only be destroyed when it is whole. **Only when whole can it be shattered.**"*

**What the party will be told:** they are gathering the components needed to **destroy** the Temple's most dangerous artifact, and Hedrack is risking everything to help them do it.

**What is actually happening:** they are doing Hedrack's investigation FOR him (he doesn't know where the Orb is), surviving content Hedrack can't risk his own forces in (the Nodes), powering up his phylactery, and delivering it to his ascension chamber.

**The lie is elegant** because it inverts what the party expects. They believe they are *destroying* the artifact. They are *concentrating* it.

**Counter-warning from Falrinth:** Falrinth will refuse to surrender the Orb. He fights for it (per his Tactical Notes — uses Kriitch for scouting, dimension door for escape, dies hard). If captured he warns the party about the Orb's true effect. If killed, his journal (per canon "Falrinth's Journal" quest) misleads the party into thinking he was a Zuggtmoy-liberator — useful Lolth-side misdirection that *doesn't* expose Hedrack but *does* make the party more suspicious of the Orb itself.

### Arc C — The False Final Battle

**Why Hedrack needs it.** To complete the liberation ritual, Hedrack must be in Zuggtmoy's sanctuary (Dungeon Level 3 / lower) for an extended period, undisturbed by Greater Temple rank-and-file or by Iuz's spies. He needs a massive distraction.

**The pitch:** *"The consecration site where the restored Orb can be shattered is in the Greater Temple's deepest chambers. The Temple's defenses must be breached. The cultists must be drawn into combat. We will mount a final assault on the Greater Temple's main gates. Hedrack — through his unique position — will lower the inner defenses to let you through."*

**What the party will be told:** this is the climactic raid. Storm the Temple. Destroy the Orb. End the evil.

**What is actually happening:** they are creating cover for Hedrack to slip into Zuggtmoy's sanctuary and present her with the restored Orb to claim his divine reward.

**During this arc** the party may finally realize what they've been doing. The discovery moment is the climax of the manipulation phase. What they do with the realization is the campaign's endgame.

## When and How the Party Should Realize

NOT during Phase 3 (cultivation). NOT during Arc A (Senshock killed cleanly). NOT during Arc B1 (Orb retrieved). The realization should land during or after **Arc B2** (the Nodes) or during **Arc C** (the false final battle).

Reveal vectors, in increasing impact:

- **Falrinth's first-contact warning** (Phase 3 cleanup week) plants doubt.
- **Senshock's quarters or final moments** reveal he was hunting Hedrack's coup. Documents, last words, scrying records.
- **Falrinth captured or interrogated** during Arc B1 — he knows about Lolth's containment mission and the Orb's true effect. He can be flipped to say enough.
- **A Lucius slip** — Insight check during a meeting catches a tell. Lucius is human, he occasionally underestimates the party.
- **Barkinar realizes** independently — he is loyal to Iuz, sees a pattern, asks the party what they were told to do. This is the moment Barkinar becomes the sleeper ally.
- **Zuggtmoy at the moment of liberation** — direct contact with the demon queen reveals everything in the worst way possible.
- **Witnessing the ascension ritual** — the party arrives at the "consecration site" and finds Hedrack mid-ritual with the Orb on a Zuggtmoy altar, not a destruction circle.

## Endgame Moves When the Realization Lands

Don't pre-pick. Let the players choose. Available paths:

1. **Stop Hedrack.** Heroic. The Orb is in the room; Zuggtmoy is half-freed; Hedrack is mid-ritual. They have to kill him before he ascends, possibly while fighting whatever Zuggtmoy has become. Iuz's incoming retaliation is the post-credits problem.

2. **Flip the deal.** Kill Hedrack, present the Orb to Zuggtmoy themselves. She would promise them anything. They become the new gods. They betray everything they thought they stood for. (For 15-year-olds, this is a delicious dark-fantasy option even if they don't take it.)

3. **Tell Barkinar.** Probably the most viable path for an organized rebellion from inside the Temple. He becomes the senior cleric backing them with Greater Temple troops. Strike against Hedrack happens with proper coordination.

4. **Warn Iuz directly.** Skip Barkinar. Find a way to alert Iuz. Greater evil to stop greater evil. Iuz's gratitude is its own poisoned gift.

5. **Side with Zuggtmoy directly.** She would gladly take their service over Hedrack's if they prove useful. Demon Queens have no loyalty to their bargains.

6. **Side with Lolth via Falrinth.** If they kept Falrinth alive and have a working relationship, Lolth's containment mission becomes their cause. They help her seal the EEG prison permanently. Lolth's gratitude is also poisoned.

7. **Walk away.** Take the Orb, destroy it themselves (somehow — research, Veluna, the Circle of Eight), let the Temple collapse without ascension. Hardest path; requires they actually know how to destroy it. The Cult of EEG would consider this their victory; everyone else would consider it a problem.

The Barkinar path is the most *accessible* for a young party — clear ally, immediate action, righteous footing.
