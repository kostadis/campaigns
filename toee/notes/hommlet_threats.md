# Hommlet Threats — GM-Only

The party's home base is more compromised than they realize. Four hostile operators are embedded in or around Hommlet — none confronted, several known by name only.

This doc covers the Temple of Elemental Evil's Hommlet penetration. For Hommlet's hidden allies and good-aligned operators, see `nulb_allies.md` (Y'dey, Murfles, Otis — all displaced to Nulb).

We could use your local knowledge to set things right."*

## 1. Smigmal Redhand — "Lady Eryndel"

**Canon role:** Falrinth's lover/partner (see `falrinth_strategy.md`), commands the outer Temple defenses from the ruined Tower above the Temple. **Wears a Hat of Disguise** that lets her pass as a refined elven noblewoman: **"Lady Eryndel."**

**What she's been doing in Hommlet (per the canon *Web of Deception* sub-quest):**
- Spreading misinformation among Hommlet's political class
- Framing innocent locals as Temple spies
- Targeting Hommlet figures who oppose the Temple's regional influence for assassination
- Manipulating the village's leadership to keep factions divided

**She is operating among the party's NPC web right now.** Possible interactions:
- She may have met one of the party socially as "Lady Eryndel" without anyone realizing
- She is likely on speaking terms with Burne, Rufus, or other Hommlet leaders the party knows
- She has been listening to gossip about the party's recent escalations

**Stats:** Half-orc Fighter/Assassin. Six feet tall. Leather armor +1, shield +2, +1 shortsword (poisoned: DC 13 CON, 25 poison damage on fail, half on success). Ring of spell storing (5 invisibility charges). Two earrings worth 400 gp each, jeweled belt 3,200 gp, ~7 gp + 12 ep in purse. Knows most creatures react favorably to bloodstones; accumulating them.

**Lolth alignment:** She serves Lolth via Falrinth. The Lolth network in Hommlet runs through her. If the party investigates "Lady Eryndel" and exposes her, the Lolth-side of the campaign opens earlier than expected.

**Recommended play:** introduce "Lady Eryndel" as a name in passing during the Phase 3 cleanup week. *"There's an elven noblewoman who's been asking about your group at Ostler's. Lady Eryndel? Refined. Polite. Curious."* Don't push it. Let it linger. The reveal of "Lady Eryndel = Smigmal" lands much later, possibly during Arc B when the party pursues Falrinth.

If the party kills "Lady Eryndel" in Hommlet without realizing what she is, they cripple the Lolth network without knowing it — and Falrinth becomes much more isolated.

### Three Smigmal-quest variants

Per canon (*Smigmal — The Assassin's Mark*):

1. **The Assassin's Mark:** Smigmal targets the party as assassin. Failed attempts leave cryptic warnings. Track her back to the Tower.
2. **Web of Deception:** Expose her "Lady Eryndel" identity before she manipulates Hommlet further. Political intrigue.
3. **Broken Trust:** Falrinth has betrayed her. She seeks revenge. Party can exploit the rift. (Only viable if Falrinth contact happened and Falrinth has reason to remove her — possibly because she's becoming a liability with the party knowing about her.)

Pick the one that fits your party's actions. If they kill Falrinth, Smigmal becomes (3). If they ally with Falrinth, Smigmal stays at his side. If they go directly to the Tower, Smigmal becomes (1).

## 2. Zert — Lucius Graeme's Personal Enforcer

**Canon role (Gambler's Veil quest):** *"Zert pretends to be a wandering mercenary but is Lucius' personal enforcer. If the adventurers dig too deeply into Lucius' dealings, Zert will be dispatched to silence them."*

**Where he is:** **The Inn of the Welcome Wench, Hommlet.** The party's home base. Where they stay between operations. Where Ostler runs the show.

**This closes the planning.md open thread** that listed Zert as "Not yet addressed in any session. Status: open." Canon answer: he's Lucius's wet-work man, *at the inn*, waiting to be dispatched if the party crosses Lucius.

**The party has likely seen him without registering him.** Wandering mercenaries pass through the Welcome Wench constantly. Zert is one of those faces, drinking quietly, occasionally engaging the party in small talk. Sharp Insight check (DC 18) over multiple sessions would catch that he is always around when the party is around, never quite where they aren't.

**When does Zert fire?**
- If the party investigates Lucius/Aldren (Gambler's Veil quest)
- If the party investigates the Hommlet supply chain (Chains of Command quest, when the trail loops back to Lucius)
- If Lucius decides the party has become a liability
- If Lucius is killed, Zert may go rogue and try to avenge him

**Stats:** Treat as a Veteran (CR 3) or Assassin (CR 8) depending on how hard you want the fight. Stat block is GM choice; the character beat is "the guy who has always been in the corner finally stands up."

### Pre-emptive option

If the party becomes suspicious of Aldren *during* the Phase 4 cultivation and starts asking questions in Hommlet, Zert escalates to active surveillance. They notice him following them. The unmasking of Zert is a tight setpiece — they corner him in an alley, he reveals only what he must, and the trail leads back to Aldren.

## 3. Joseph Millar — Doomwatch Keep Saboteur

**Canon role (Gambler's Veil quest):** *"A laborer deliberately stalling construction on Verbobonc's fortifications. His orders come directly from Lucius, funneled through encrypted notes. If caught, Joseph will beg for his life and offer information on Lucius' greater plan."*

**Where he is:** **Doomwatch Keep** — a fortification under construction somewhere in Verbobonc's territory. This is a *new* location for the party — Doomwatch Keep hasn't appeared in their notes. Likely a Verbobonc border keep that the Viscount is building to monitor the Gnarley Forest / Kron Hills approach.

**What he does:** Sabotages construction. Stalls timelines. Falsifies material orders. Plants weaknesses in the masonry. The keep will be defensively useless when it's "completed" if Joseph isn't stopped.

**Why the party should care:**
- Doomwatch Keep is meant to defend against the very Temple threat the party knows is real
- A weak Doomwatch Keep means the Temple's invasion plans (Furyondy/Veluna) get a foothold faster
- Burne and Rufus's tower project is parallel work — Joseph may be connected to the **blueprint thief** in Burne's tower construction (open planning.md thread)

**Recommended play:** **Joseph is the missing piece in the Burne/Rufus saboteur investigation.** The blueprints stolen from Burne's tower were used by Joseph (or his network) to identify weaknesses in standard Verbobonc fortification design that he applies to Doomwatch sabotage. **Solving one solves both.**

If the party investigates the Burne/Rufus saboteur thread seriously, the trail leads to Joseph at Doomwatch, then back through Joseph's coded orders to Lucius. This is a perfect entry point to expose Lucius's Hommlet network *before* Lucius escalates Phase 4.

**On capture:** Joseph is a coward. He talks. He names Lucius (as "Aldren"). He provides location/method intel for delivering messages. He may know about Zert. **He will probably not know about Smigmal** — Smigmal operates separately, through different channels.

## 4. Black Jay — The Herdsman

**Already known to the party** but unaddressed. Per their NPC dossier: "He is a herdsman working as a bandit informant. His connection to the Temple of Elemental Evil is unknown to Hommlet's common folk."

**Canon role:** Hommlet bandit informant. Reports on caravan movements, militia patrols, adventurer comings and goings. Almost certainly feeds Lucius via the Crimson Reapers' channel (Lucius has documented contact with the Reapers).

**Why he matters now:**
- The three Moathouse bandits who escaped south after the party's takedown may have contacted Black Jay
- If so, Black Jay knows the party is the force that destroyed Lareth's operation
- That intel goes up through bandit network to Lucius
- Lucius, on receiving it, *already knows* the party are killers but pretends not to during Phase 4 cultivation

**This makes Black Jay a structural information leak.** Eliminating him before he reports = preserving the party's cover. Eliminating him after = closing the barn door.

**Recommended play:** mention the three Moathouse bandits' southward flight in conjunction with Black Jay's continued presence. Time pressure. The party already has the intel — they just need to act on it.

**Capture vs. kill:** Black Jay alive is a source. He'd know:
- The chain of command above him (who he reports to, drops, frequency)
- Who else is in the local bandit network
- What Lucius's couriers look like

Killed cleanly with no trace, Black Jay's *absence* tells the bandit network something happened. Replacement informants take weeks. The party gets breathing room.

## How These Threats Connect to Phase 3 / Phase 4

### During Phase 3 cleanup (current)
- All four are operating in the background
- The party isn't actively investigating any of them
- They are not direct threats to the party right now — they are reporting on the party

### Once Lucius makes contact (start of Phase 4)
- Zert escalates to active surveillance the moment Lucius decides the party is being cultivated
- Joseph keeps sabotaging unrelated to the party until the party finds him
- Black Jay continues reporting until silenced
- Smigmal/Lady Eryndel may openly try to maneuver against the party's Hommlet political relationships if Lolth wants the party off-balance

### When the party realizes Lucius is dirty
- This whole network is suddenly the most actionable thing they can hit
- Tearing it down cripples Hedrack's *intelligence apparatus*, not just supply
- The Chains of Betrayal canon quest is built around exactly this arc — exposing Lucius via his Verbobonc operations (Lord Bridgeforth, Lakash Quallad, the anti-gnome propaganda) eventually loops back to the Hommlet operators

### When Lucius is dead
- Zert may try to avenge him (immediate threat)
- Joseph collapses (no orders, no funds, runs)
- Black Jay flips loyalty to whoever pays next (cheap to suborn, or kill)
- Smigmal continues operating independently for Falrinth/Lolth — she was never Lucius's, only adjacent

## Doomwatch Keep — Add to World Map

This is a new location surfaced by canon. Recommend placing it:
- Verbobonc's border with the Gnarley Forest, west or north of Hommlet
- A day or two's ride from the village
- Under construction by Verbobonc engineers
- Garrisoned by a small Verbobonc detail
- Strategic mission: monitor the Gnarley approach to the Viscounty, react to bandit and Temple movements

This gives the party a *Verbobonc-side* target they can visit, which opens up direct contact with the Viscounty's military structure (relevant later when the party may need to escalate the Temple threat above Burne/Rufus to the actual Viscount).
