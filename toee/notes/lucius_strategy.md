# Lucius Graeme ("Aldren") — Strategy Doc

GM-only working doc. As of Planting 579 CY (the party's first cleanup week as upper-temple rulers). Lucius has **not yet met the party** — this dossier covers his first contact and the Thrommel interrogation. For the coup he serves, see `notes/hedrack_strategy.md`; for the rival demon-thread, `notes/falrinth_strategy.md`; for the session this feeds, `notes/the-sleeping-prince.md`.

## The Frame (Lucius from inside his own head)

He was a lord once. Vain, cruel, comfortable — and then order betrayed him (a fall, a ruin, a scar across the face from collapsing debris that he tells four different lies about). What he learned in the fall is the only thing he now believes: **civilization is a thin mask over elemental chaos, and the man who embraces the chaos is the only man who is ever truly in control.** Order is a lie the weak tell each other. Morality is a leash. He slipped his.

So now he is the most useful man in the Temple and looks like the least. He keeps his frame portly and soft, his clothes plain gray, his hat low, his manner faintly buffoonish — because **a man others underestimate hears everything.** He delights in it. He plays the fool the way a card-sharp plays the drunk. Behind it sits a calculating engine and, lower still, a banked rage that he unleashes without mercy on the rare occasions someone earns it.

He serves Zuggtmoy — *truly* serves her, hears her directly, which is more than most of the holy men above him can say. He runs Hedrack's whisper-web because the coup is the fastest road to the chaos he worships: a Temple that conquers Furyondy and Veluna, an Orb made whole, the mask of civilization torn off the whole Flanaess. He lets Senshock think Lucius is *his* informant; he lets Hedrack think Lucius is *his* courier. Both are half-right. Lucius is chaos's man, and chaos is patient.

And now four prophets are dead in a single day and Barkinar has handed the upper temple to a band of clever, ruthless unknowns. Lucius did not choose them. That bothers him more than he shows. He needs to know exactly what they are — and above all whether they have stumbled anywhere near **the sleeper in the iron coffin**, because a waking prince is the one thing that brings the kingdoms down on the Temple before the work is done.

## Their Current Position

- **Hedrack's spymaster and courier** — runs the Temple's external networks across **Verbobonc, Nulb, and Hommlet**: Lakash (Verbobonc merchant, launders Temple funds), Bridgeforth (a noble steering court rumor), the Hommlet saboteur ring. **Zert** at the Inn of the Welcome Wench is his personal enforcer.
- **Holds the Selentis leash** — Madame Selentis / "Lady Nysera Krivaltis" is his protégé and logistics chief.
- **Tier-1 in the coup.** He, not Barkinar, delivers the three manipulation arcs to the party (assassinate Senshock → "destroy" the Orb → the false final battle). Barkinar genuinely doesn't know the coup exists; Lucius does, and runs the party-facing lies.
- **Senshock's "informant."** Senshock believes he controls Lucius by feeding him crafted intelligence. Lucius lets him believe it. (This is a live double-game and a future party lever — see Contingencies.)
- **Undetectable.** His *Amulet of the Shrouded One* hides his alignment and location from *detect evil and good*, *scrying*, *commune*, *divination*. The party **cannot** simply magic-sense that he's lying or evil. They have to *read* him — which is the whole game.

## Their Secret (the lever)

**Lucius's true allegiance is to Zuggtmoy directly — not to Hedrack — and he would betray the coup itself the instant chaos (or the demon's whim) pointed elsewhere.** Hedrack thinks Lucius is his loyal operational hand. He isn't. He is the demon queen's man, embedded in a mortal's scheme that happens to serve her *for now*. If Hedrack's plan and Zuggtmoy's interest ever diverge, Lucius is already on the other side.

The party-usable edge inside that secret: **Lucius is the seam between Hedrack and Senshock.** He feeds Senshock crafted lies (as Senshock's "informant") and feeds the party crafted lies (as Hedrack's courier). A party that catches *one* of those threads can pull the other. Unmasking Lucius — canon literally has an "Unmasking of Lucius Graeme" — collapses Hedrack's information control. That's why his cover is worth so much to him, and why he plays the fool so hard.

## The Optimal Play (For Him)

This is what Lucius does this week if you run him at his full cunning:

1. **Get in the room early, as a friend.** He arranges the welcome meeting himself, framed as routine post-war housekeeping — "Barkinar asked me to make sure the new management has everything it needs." Warm, generous, self-deprecating. He brings a gift (wine, a useful map, a roster of the surviving cultists). He is *helpful.*
2. **Map them before he uses them.** First meeting is reconnaissance disguised as welcome. He probes — lightly — for three things: (a) how controllable they are, (b) whether they've made *outside* contact (Nulb, Hommlet — his network already flagged it), and (c) **whether they know about the Prince.** He never asks directly. He floats bait and watches faces.
3. **The Thrommel probe (the load-bearing beat).** He raises "the usual ghost stories — sleeping kings, buried treasures" *himself,* lightly, as something to laugh off, and watches each PC's eyes when he says it. An innocent party laughs along. A guilty one flickers. (See the welcome-meeting script in `the-sleeping-prince.md`, Scene 3.) **Him raising it is the tell** a sharp player can catch on Insight — an innocent handler wouldn't bring up the one thing he most needs buried.
4. **Begin seeding Arc A (Senshock).** Even on day one he starts painting Senshock as the paranoid mad architect they'll eventually be asked to remove — planting the frame weeks before the ask.
5. **Keep Senshock fed.** Report a sanitized version of the party to Senshock to maintain that cover, while reporting the real read to Hedrack.
6. **Contain, don't confront.** If anything alarms him, his move is never a drawn blade. It's a quiet tightening: reassign their access, accelerate a mission to occupy them, whisper to Barkinar, or let Selentis's people watch the roads. Violence is a last resort he outsources (Zert, Selentis's knight Torvel, Smigmal-placed captain Krael) — Lucius is never personally in the fight if he can help it.

## The Optimal Play (Against Him)

What the party's best path actually is, so you can give it weight when they stumble onto it:

1. **Don't show your hand.** The single highest-value move against Lucius in the welcome meeting is to *reveal nothing* about the Prince and let him relax. A relaxed Lucius stops watching them closely — that's the opening for everything else.
2. **Notice he raised it.** Catching that *Lucius* introduced the "sleeping king" story is the thread. It confirms there IS something below worth this much misdirection — Falrinth's "ask what he's so afraid you'll find" pays off in the moment.
3. **Pull the Senshock seam.** Lucius is Senshock's "informant." If the party ever talks to Senshock honestly (dangerous, but possible), or captures one of Lucius's couriers, the contradiction between what Lucius tells Senshock and what he tells the party is the crack that unmasks him.
4. **Use his vanity.** He plays the fool to be underestimated — a party that *visibly* underestimates him back (lets him think they're dimmer/greedier than they are) feeds him exactly the false read he wants, and buys them room. Beating Lucius means out-fooling the fool.
5. **They can't scry him.** Note for the party's planning: divination won't work on Lucius (the Amulet). They have to beat him with human-level tradecraft — surveillance, captured intermediaries, contradictions — not magic. Make that limitation explicit if they try to *detect evil* him and it comes back clean (which will itself read as suspicious to a clever player).

## Negotiation Stances

- **Concedes (cheaply, to keep cover):** information that costs him nothing, useful gifts, flattery, small true favors, throwing a *lesser* asset under the cart (he'll sacrifice Zert or a Hommlet spy without blinking to protect the larger game).
- **Trades:** access and missions for the party's compliance and their *unwitting* service to the coup. He will happily make them rich and empowered — as long as they're aimed where he wants.
- **Never gives up:** the secret of the coup; the truth about the Orb's "destruction"; the existence/location of the Prince; his direct line to Zuggtmoy; his own cover. He would rather flee and have the party hunt a ghost than confirm any of these.
- **The line he won't cross even cornered:** he will not fight to the death for Hedrack. His loyalty is to chaos/Zuggtmoy and to *himself*. Cornered, he runs, lies, or flips — he does not martyr himself for another man's coup.

## Contingencies

- **If the party reveals they know about the Prince (the big one):** Lucius's warmth does **not** drop — that's what makes him frightening. He files it, finishes the meeting graciously, and *then* acts: within a day their deep-level access quietly tightens, a time-consuming mission is proposed to occupy them, and he begins arranging for the Prince to be moved/re-hidden or, worst case, "martyred" so the leverage can't be turned against the Temple. He reports them to Hedrack as a liability. **This isn't a fail-state — it's a harder game.** The party now races a closing net.
- **If the party stalls / plays dumb:** he relaxes, the secret stays buried, their access holds, and they keep their window to reach area 334. Best outcome for them.
- **If they try to flip or threaten him in the first meeting:** he deflects with a joke, leaves, and notes them as a problem. He is *not* killable or flippable in a welcome meeting — trying tips the coup's hand and accelerates the Barkinar clock. Hold any real Lucius confrontation for the unmasking, mid-to-late Hedrack arc.
- **If they catch a slip (Insight win):** give it to them — Lucius is human and occasionally underestimates them (his one flaw is the vanity of the fool-act). A slip is a thread, not a confession. He recovers smoothly and is *more* careful afterward.
- **If Senshock dies (Arc A completes):** Lucius loses his "informant" cover but also loses a watcher — net win for the coup, and he consolidates. If Senshock dies *messily* and leaves records, those records are a primary vector for unmasking Lucius later.
- **If Falrinth's probe reached the party first** (it does, in `the-sleeping-prince.md`): the party arrives at the welcome meeting already primed with the name "golden eyes" and the warning to stall. Lucius doesn't know Falrinth tipped them. If the party are *too* poised — stall too perfectly — Lucius's instinct pricks: someone coached them. That's a thread he'll pull.
- **If cornered with no exit (late game):** *Pass Without Trace*, *Nondetection*, and a lifetime of escape routes. He vanishes into the Verbobonc/Nulb network he built. Canon "Lucius' Last Gambit" — he'll spend every asset and ally to escape or to take the party down with him before he dies. He does not surrender meekly; he gambles.

## Voice samples

- *(warm, expansive, a host filling cups)* "Forgive the fuss — I'm only a courier, I carry things from clever people to clever people. And you four are the cleverest thing to happen to this miserable rock in a year. Sit. Drink. Tell me what you need and I'll see it carried."
- *(the probe, light as air, eyes steady)* "Been down to Nulb, I hear. Restocking — war's thirsty work. Did some herb-wife fill your heads with the usual? Sleeping kings, buried gold under the altar?" *(a soft laugh)* "People do love a bedtime tale. Nothing below but rock and the faithful's work."
- *(his creed, when he thinks no one important is listening)* "Civilization is a fragile mask over the face of the elemental chaos. It's a shame when it breaks. Or a delight."
- *(rare — the rage off the leash, when genuinely provoked)* "You think the soft voice is the whole of me. Everyone does. It's the last thing they think."
- *(the line he'd say of himself, smiling)* "Chaos is the truest form of control. Those who embrace it shape the world. The rest are shaped."

## What he would never say

He would never name the Prince, the Orb's true fate, or the coup — those are the secrets the whole performance protects. He would never raise his voice in negotiation (the rage is for *after* the mask, not during it). He would never admit he hears Zuggtmoy directly, or that his loyalty runs past Hedrack to the demon. He would never be caught *seeming* to care about being underestimated — the fool-act only works if it looks like the truth. And he would never make a threat he intends to carry out personally; his menace is always something that will happen *to* you, arranged by hands that aren't his.

## Open decisions (for you)

1. **Does Lucius *know* the party went to Mother Screng specifically?** His network flagged "Nulb." Whether he knows it was the herb shop (and thus brushes Y'dey's operation) is a big dial — if he does, Y'dey's cover is in danger and the stakes spike. Recommend: he knows they were *in Nulb*, not *who they saw* — yet.
2. **How hard does the welcome meeting probe?** A single light pass (recommended — preserves the slow burn and lets the party feel clever for stalling) vs. a sustained interrogation (higher tension, higher risk the quest closes early). Your call by table mood.
3. **Is Lucius's Zuggtmoy-over-Hedrack secret in play this arc, or held?** Recommend held — surface it only when the party is deep enough in the coup to use it. This session, he's just the golden-eyed handler.
4. **Does he have a dossier?** If he's going to recur as the party's primary handler, promote the Frame + Voice into `docs/npcs/lucius_graeme.md` so future prep has it.
