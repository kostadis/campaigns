# TZGY Scarab — Urgent Note

**The party has been carrying Senshock's personal control device for months without knowing.**

## What they have

Per `docs/party.md` (Calmer) and `docs/campaign_state.md`: "Small black scarab inscribed with glyphs **TZGY** (significance unknown)" — looted from Rannos/Gremag's quarters in Hommlet (chapter 12).

## What it actually is

Per Leadership Organization canon (Senshock entry): *"a distinctive scarab pendant marked 'TZGY' grants him control over the Temple's hidden mechanisms."* The TZGY scarab is **Senshock's** — Lord Wizard of the Greater Temple, master of arcane defenses, third-rank Greater Temple leadership.

The glyphs spell **Z(uggt)moy** — TZGY is a four-letter contraction. The scarab is a Zuggtmoy-cult control sigil. Compare to Deggum's "Black Scarab of Zuggtmoy" which controls hidden temple mechanisms via the same craft tradition. The TZGY scarab is the matched piece for senior leadership.

## What it does (GM rulings — pick what you want)

The canon is vague. Suggested specifics for play:

1. **Recognition.** Any TOEE cultist of rank seeing the scarab assumes the bearer is a senior Greater Temple operator. Lower-rank cultists defer automatically. Mid-rank cultists ask whose authority is being delegated. Senior Greater Temple operators (Barkinar, Deggum, Lucius) recognize it as Senshock's personal device.
2. **Temple mechanisms.** Doors, secret passages, and security wards keyed to senior Greater Temple staff respond to the scarab — *if* the bearer knows the right gesture or rune to invoke. The party will discover this by accident or by being shown.
3. **Direct communication.** Senshock can sense when the scarab is in use and where (this is the dangerous part). Wearing it openly inside the temple is a beacon.

## How Rannos and Gremag had it

Best read: Senshock gave one of the Hommlet operatives the scarab as a credential — proof they spoke with his authority — and they never returned it before the party killed them. The party scooped it up with the rest of the contents. It's been in Calmer's inventory since.

Senshock has been *aware it was missing* but has not had the leverage to recover it (Rannos/Gremag are "missing" per the cover story, not confirmed dead). He will absolutely recognize it the moment he sees it on a party member.

## Why this matters NOW

The party is about to be cultivated into the Greater Temple's inner circle (Phase 4). They will meet Senshock. They will meet Lucius Graeme who knows what the scarab is. **The scarab is the first hard-to-explain item that ties the party to the assassination of Senshock-trusted operatives.**

Three plausible scenarios when it surfaces:

### Scenario A: Senshock sees it on a PC
Senshock recognizes it immediately. Reads it as: *the party killed my operatives and looted them.* Senshock is paranoid by nature. He doesn't share this with Hedrack — he files it for later use. The party may not realize he's noticed.

### Scenario B: Lucius Graeme sees it on a PC during a cultivation meeting
Lucius recognizes it. Lucius is Tier 1 (knows Hedrack's coup plan, communes with Zuggtmoy). He files it as leverage *against* Hedrack — if he can prove the party killed Senshock's operatives, he can frame them as Senshock's enemies or Hedrack's enemies depending on what serves Hedrack's plan that week. He doesn't tell Senshock either.

### Scenario C: The party realizes what they have first
If they pull on the thread (research the glyphs, ask Spugnoir, ask anyone who knows Temple cult symbology), they may figure it out before someone exploits them. At that point they choose: hide it deeper, plant it on an enemy to frame them, use it to bluff their way through Senshock-controlled mechanisms, or destroy it.

## Recommended GM play

Plant a clue *now* — before the party gets to a Senshock encounter — so the realization can be the party's, not the GM's. Options:

- **Falrinth knows.** If Falrinth's first contact happens (per `phase3_endgame.md`), he can mention "the trinket your cleric carries — you should be more careful with it. Senshock will recognize it the moment you stand in the same room." Falrinth wouldn't necessarily explain further — it's leverage he holds over the party.
- **Lucius drops a tell.** During a Lucius/Aldren meeting, his golden eyes linger on Calmer's neck (or wherever the scarab is). He says nothing. Insight DC 16 catches that he noticed something. The party then has to figure out what.
- **Spugnoir, asked about Temple iconography, recognizes the TZGY pattern** as a Greater Temple senior-leadership credential and warns the party not to wear it visibly. (Spugnoir is the obvious safe consultant since the party already trusts him.)

The scarab should become a recurring stress point. *It is too valuable to throw away, too dangerous to wear, too distinctive to fence.* That's exactly the kind of item this party will agonize over — perfect for them.

## Action item for next session

Decide which scenario above will fire first and seed it. Recommend **Falrinth knows** during the Phase 3 cleanup-week first-contact scene — it makes him more credible as a player who has been watching them, and it gives the party a concrete reason to take his contact seriously beyond his words.
