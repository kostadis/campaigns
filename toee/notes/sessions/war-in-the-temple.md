# \#\# This is a sequence of events to run a multi-front war in the temple of elemental evil levels 1 and 2

#  Opening 

**Phase I: The Earthen Tomb (The Assassination)**  
Romag operates from his Sleeping Apartment (Area 137), a defensible position guarded by bugbears and human fighters in the adjacent rooms.

* The Setup: Hartsch, acting as Romag’s speaker, arranges a private audience for the party under the guise of finalizing the battle plan against Alrrem (the Fire Temple). Hartsch uses his authority to dismiss the immediate guards or ensure the heavy door separating Romag’s room from the outer guards remains unbarred.  
* The Kill: Romag is no fool. Mechanically, he is a 5th Edition Cult Fanatic (AC 16 with a *\+1 chain shirt*). The moment the party draws steel, Romag will not fight to the death. His primary tactic is to cast *Darkness*, slip into his concealed northwest alcove, grab his treasure from his trapped iron chest, and flee through his secret passage.  
* The Tactical Challenge: The party must kill him in the first round or two before he escapes into the darkness. If he screams, the guards in Area 136 (the Guard Leader) and Area 135 will rush in.

**Phase II: The Puppet Prophet (The Coronation)**  
With Romag dead, Hartsch must immediately assume control before the Earth Temple forces splinter.

* The Scapegoat: The party still possesses the Fire Temple warlock's red leather whip and the golden fire amulet from the Chimera attack. They must plant this evidence on Romag’s corpse or in his room.  
* The Proclamation: Hartsch dons Romag’s vestments (worth 750 gp) and summons the Earth Temple guards and the newly recruited Broken Blades. He declares that Fire Temple assassins infiltrated the chamber and murdered Romag, but that he and the party fought them off.  
* The Motivation: Hartsch (an Acolyte) is mechanically weak but politically ambitious. He uses his "destructive, earthquake-like" philosophy to whip the troops into a frenzy, promising them plunder from the Fire Temple instead of Romag's boring guard duty.

**Phase III: The Spark of Chaos (The Three-Way War)**  
Hartsch now commands the Earth Temple's numbers, but a direct assault on the Fire Temple is what Belsornig (Water Temple) wants. The party must widen the conflict to drag Water into the bloodshed.

* The Extortion Play (Targeting Fire): The party sends a secret missive to Alrrem (Fire). They include a copy of the black-market ledger proving that Vesrek, an ally of Belsornig, sold the Fire Temple the Chimera. The message reveals that the Water Temple overcharged Alrrem and purposely sold him an uncontrollable beast to bankrupt him.  
* The First Strike (Targeting Water): While Alrrem’s rage shifts toward Belsornig, Hartsch orders the Earth Temple forces (including the Broken Blades) to launch a devastating raid on the Water Temple’s outposts on Dungeon Level 2\.  
* The Result: Alrrem (Fire) attacks Belsornig (Water) for the Chimera betrayal. Belsornig (Water) is simultaneously assaulted by Hartsch (Earth). The dungeon dissolves into a chaotic, multi-front war.  
* 

**Events: Once the war starts, these events can take place as the GM sees fit**  

**1\. The Environmental Collapse (The Dungeon is Tearing Itself Apart)**  
The conflict shouldn't just be NPCs hitting each other with swords; it should be an elemental cataclysm. The dungeon infrastructure cannot handle a full-scale magical war.

* **Earth vs. Fire (Structural Failure):** As Alrrem’s warlocks hurl *Fireballs* and Hartsch’s clerics cast *Earth Tremor*, the structural integrity of the transitional stairs and corridors begins to fail. Ceilings cave in, crushing combatants indiscriminately. The air is choked with thick, abrasive stone dust and the stench of sulfur.  
* **Fire vs. Water (The Boiling Fog):** When the Fire Temple breaches the Water Temple’s perimeter, Belsornig’s clerics will unleash floods of water against Alrrem's flames. This creates zones of **scalding steam**. Treat these areas as heavily obscured (like *Fog Cloud*) but any creature entering or starting their turn in the steam takes 1d4 fire/boiling damage. The party must navigate blind, listening to the screams of dying cultists boiling to death in the fog.

**2\. The Scavenger Frenzy (A Fourth, Uncontrolled Faction)**  
The sheer volume of freshly slain bodies will trigger an ecological nightmare. Nature—and the undead—abhors a vacuum.

* **The Ghoul Tide:** The ghouls and ghasts from Level 1 (Areas 115–118) and the crypts, driven mad by the scent of hundreds of bleeding soldiers, abandon their territories and flood the warzone.  
* **The Horror Element:** The party should stumble upon skirmishes where both Fire and Earth cultists have stopped fighting each other because they are being eaten alive by swarms of starving ghouls. The undead do not care about faction politics. This shows the party that their actions have broken the natural order of the dungeon.

**3\. The Broken Blades' Regret (The Human Cost)**  
The party recruited Captain Dren Halveth and the Broken Blades, effectively making them their responsibility. To make the players feel the weight of the war, show them the terror of their own troops.

* During **Skirmish 3 (The Broken Blades' Dilemma)**, Dren shouldn't just be fighting; he should be panicking. Have him covered in blood, screaming at the party: *"You didn't say it would be a slaughterhouse\! Romag's replacement is a madman\! We're being fed to a meat grinder\!"*  
* Seeing hardened mercenaries break down in fear of the elemental horrors (like Salamanders or Juggernauts) highlights that this is no longer a standard dungeon crawl—it is a massacre.

**4\. The Panic of the Innocent**  
The Temple relies heavily on slaves and prisoners for labor and sacrifice.

* As the party navigates the skirmishes, have them witness groups of chained prisoners desperately trying to flee the crossfire, only to be crushed by falling rubble, immolated by stray fire spells, or slaughtered by panicking bugbears who are killing anyone in their path. The party's "clever political maneuver" is racking up a massive civilian body count.

#  War

**Random generator** 

**Collateral Damage Table". At the top of every combat round during the war phase, roll 1d6.**

* **1-2: A stray elemental spell (Firebolt/Ice Storm) blasts through the corridor.**  
* **3-4: A section of the ceiling collapses (Dex save or bludgeoning damage).**  
* **5: A thick cloud of choking, toxic smoke/steam rolls in, blinding everyone.**  
* **6: 1d4 starving Ghouls drop from the shadows, attacking the most wounded creature (PC or NPC). This mechanic ensures the party feels that the environment itself is a lethal, unpredictable enemy.**

**Phase I: The Fog of War (Skirmishes)**  
Following Romag's assassination and the delivery of the forged black-market ledger (implicating Water in Fire's bankruptcy), confusion reigns. The factions do not immediately commit their full armies; they send probing attacks to test defenses.  
**Skirmish 1: The Stairwell Barricade (Earth vs. Fire)**

* **The Scene:** The transitional stairs between Level 1 and Level 2 are blocked. Fire Temple Bugbears have dragged heavy wooden pews and stone rubble to create a makeshift barricade.  
* **The Combatants:** 3 Earth Temple **Ogres** (CR 2\) are blindly hurling shattered masonry at the barricade, while 4 Fire Temple **Bugbears** (CR 1\) and 1 **Cult Fanatic** (CR 2\) return fire from heavy cover with light crossbows and *Sacred Flame*.  
* **Party Intervention:** The party arrives behind the Ogres. The Earth forces are taking heavy losses. The party can use precise magic or rogue stealth to bypass the barricade and break the Fire Temple's line, allowing Earth to flood into Level 2\.

**Skirmish 2: The Steam Corridors (Fire vs. Water)**

* **The Scene:** Alrrem, enraged by the forged ledger, sends his elemental pets to burn out Belsornig's outer defenses (Area 213/214). The clash of fire and water magic has filled the corridors with scalding steam, heavily obscuring vision.  
* **The Combatants:** 2 **Fire Snakes** (CR 1\) and 1 wounded **Salamander** (CR 5\) are actively trying to boil away a defensive line of 4 Water Temple **Gargoyles** (CR 2).  
* **Party Intervention:** The party stumbles into the blind melee. They can use the heavy obscurement to assassinate the Salamander (crippling Fire's heavy assets) or shatter the Gargoyles (stripping Water of its physical shields).

**Phase II: The War of Attrition**  
As the skirmishes escalate, the rank-and-file begin to panic. Mercenaries realize they are being used as cannon fodder, and the dungeon's ecology reacts to the bloodshed.  
**Skirmish 3: The Broken Blades' Dilemma**

* **The Scene:** Hartsch, lacking Romag's tactical patience, ordered the newly recruited Broken Blades (Captain Dren Halveth) to push too far into Water Temple territory. They are currently pinned down in an intersection by Belsornig’s elite guards.  
* **The Combatants:** Dren Halveth and 3 surviving **Veterans** (CR 3\) are backed into a corner, holding off a coordinated assault by Water Temple **Bugbears** (CR 1\) and a **Bugbear Chief** (CR 3).  
* **Party Intervention:** Dren spots the party and shouts for aid. If the party abandons them to weaken Hartsch's Earth Temple, they lose their mercenary asset. If they save Dren, the Broken Blades pledge ultimate loyalty to the party directly, rather than to Hartsch.

**Phase III: The Decisive Actions**  
After the skirmishes have depleted the factions' resources, the leaders are forced into the open. The party must choose which decisive battles to trigger to officially eliminate a rival temple.  
**Decisive Action A: Alrrem’s Pyre (The Death of Fire)**

* **The Setup:** Bankrupt, lacking his werewolves, and stripped of his Chimera, Prefect Alrrem is broken. He barricades himself in the Fire Temple (Area 212\) with his remaining zealots, intending to perform a mass suicide ritual to summon a fire elemental that will burn Level 2 to ash.  
* **The Strike:** The party breaches the brass doors. Alrrem (Level 5 Cleric) and his commander Varek Redflame (Veteran with a *Flametongue* sword) fight a desperate last stand amidst the glowing coals of the fire pit. The party must kill Alrrem before he completes the sacrifice.

**Decisive Action B: Belsornig’s Vault (The Crippling of Water)**

* **The Setup:** Canon Belsornig is too smart to fight on the front lines. While his forces clash with Earth, he attempts to quietly evacuate his treasury and ledgers from his Salon (Area 215\) and retreat to the deeper levels.  
* **The Strike:** The party intercepts Belsornig's retreat. Belsornig (Cleric, AC 17, wields a *Rod of Smiting*) is guarded by his remaining Underpriests. If the party kills him and secures his ledgers, the Water Temple fractures into leaderless infighting and ceases to be a strategic threat.

**Phase III: The Flashpoints**  
Flashpoint 1: The Troll Chieftain's Allegiance (Decides Water's Fate)  
**The Setup:** Canon Belsornig is the most cunning of the four high priests, ruling through an intricate network of spies and manipulation. Realizing he needs heavy shock troops to break the Earth Temple's momentum, he has sent his elite emissaries to Areas 218-219 to secure the loyalty of the Troll Chieftain. **The Scene:** The party finds the Troll Chieftain (CR 5\) and his bodyguards surrounded by Water Temple clerics offering chests of gold, fresh meat, and magic items to enter the war. **The Agency:**

* If the party slaughters the Water clerics and kills the Chieftain, the Water Temple loses its heavy infantry and its momentum shatters.  
* If the party outbids Belsornig or intimidates the Troll into joining Hartsch (Earth), the Water Temple is immediately crushed by its own intended mercenaries.  
* *Consequence of ignoring:* Belsornig secures the trolls. This triggers a massive defection, as the dungeon's bugbear mercenaries—fearing the trolls—will abandon their posts and shift the balance of power entirely.

Flashpoint 2: Alrrem’s Desperate Charge (Decides Fire's Fate)  
**The Setup:** Prefect Alrrem is desperate, ambitious, and fully aware his temple's power is waning. Driven completely unhinged by the party's forged evidence regarding his bankruptcy, he abandons defensive positioning and leads a suicidal vanguard directly into the Earth Temple's line. **The Scene:** The party finds Alrrem (Level 5 Cleric) and his elite enforcer, Commander Varek Redflame (Veteran with a *Flametongue* sword), pinned down in a fiery, smoke-filled hallway, fighting desperately against Hartsch’s Earth Temple ogres. **The Agency:**

* If the party flanks and assassinates Alrrem and Varek here, the Fire Temple is utterly eradicated and ceases to exist as a faction.  
* If the party *helps* Alrrem break the Earth Temple line (betraying their puppet Hartsch), they allow Fire to survive and deal a crippling blow to Earth, keeping the dungeon in a state of weak, divided chaos.  
* *Consequence of ignoring:* Alrrem's reckless ambition burns out. He inflicts massive casualties on the Earth Temple but is ultimately killed, leaving Earth victorious but severely depleted.

Flashpoint 3: The Puppet's Panic (Decides Earth's Fate)  
**The Setup:** Hartsch is ambitious but lacks Romag's careful, defensive tactical mind. Drunk on his new power, he has pushed his Earth Temple forces too far into Level 2, leaving his flanks exposed to Belsornig's spy network. **The Scene:** A breathless Broken Blade mercenary finds the party. Water Temple assassins have bypassed the front lines and cornered Hartsch in his newly claimed command post on Level 1\. **The Agency:**

* If the party rushes back to save Hartsch, the Earth Temple survives the war and becomes the dominant power on the upper levels, with the party as the true powers behind the throne.  
* *Consequence of ignoring:* Hartsch is assassinated. The Earth Temple fractures back into leaderless banditry without Romag's foundation. The party loses their "safe" base of operations but successfully wipes out the Earth faction's leadership in the process.

# The kelno wild card

While the war is going on, Kelno is being ignroed as irrelevant. Here’s what he is doing 

While Earth, Fire, and Water tear each other apart in the corridors of the upper dungeon, Kelno, the High Priest of the Air Temple, will not sit idly by. Because he harbors an intense hatred for both the Fire and Water Temples—whose clerics murdered his former apprentices—he will view this three-way war not as a threat, but as a divine gift.  
Kelno is a bitter, self-serving manipulator who avoids direct combat, thriving instead on treachery and opportunism. Here is how he will react and exploit the chaos the party has unleashed:  
**Phase I: The Vulture's Feast**  
Kelno knows his Air Temple is currently the weakest faction, reduced to a handful of rooms. As Alrrem and Belsornig deplete their forces, Kelno will deploy his goblin spies and gnoll scouts into the warzone strictly to scavenge. They will strip armor, weapons, and coin from the fallen of all three warring factions, dragging the spoils back to Area 225 to replenish Kelno's exhausted treasury.  
**Phase II: The Bugbear Exodus**  
This is Kelno's masterstroke. He has already spent all his funds secretly bribing the bugbear mercenaries currently employed by the Water Temple. These bugbears are terrified of the trolls that Belsornig (Water) is trying to recruit.

* **The Trigger:** As soon as the war kicks off—and especially if the party resolves the Troll Chieftain Flashpoint in Water's favor—Kelno will send the signal.  
* **The Action:** The bugbears in Areas 223-226 will defect en masse, abandoning Belsornig's defensive lines right as Hartsch (Earth) and Alrrem (Fire) attack. This sudden betrayal will cause the Water Temple's flank to collapse completely, allowing Kelno to swell his own ranks without swinging a single mace.

**Phase III: The Suffocating Fog**  
Kelno experiments with weather manipulation and uses spells to disrupt his enemies. Instead of marching an army, he will use magic like *Silence 15' radius* or *Darkness 15' radius* to trap fleeing, wounded survivors of the three-way war, allowing his bugbear enforcers to quietly assassinate them in the dark and claim their gear.

# AFTERMATH  The dust has settled, the steam has cleared, and the corridors of Levels 1 and 2 are choked with the dead. You do not need to randomly roll to determine which rooms are empty; the outcome is directly dictated by the party’s choices during the Flashpoints, transitioning the players from "dungeon delvers" to "dungeon managers."

Here is how you decide the state of the dungeon and give the party agency in the aftermath.  
**Phase 1: The Direct Consequences of the Flashpoints**  
The rooms are emptied based on which factions the party allowed to be annihilated.

* **If Fire was destroyed (Alrrem killed):** Areas 204 through 208 and the Fire Temple (Area 212\) are completely empty of organized resistance. The remaining fire snakes or elemental pets will act as wild, feral hazards rather than coordinated guards.  
* **If Water was destroyed (Belsornig killed):** Areas 213 through 216 and 220 through 222 are vacated. The surviving Water Cultists either fled the dungeon or surrendered to become slaves of the Earth Temple.  
* **The Recruitment Halt:** The most critical mechanical change you must enforce is the dungeon's recruitment rule. As dictated by the module, humanoids normally replenish at a rate of one per day, larger humanoids one every three days, and monsters one per week. However, once a faction leader (Alrrem or Belsornig) is eliminated, **neither they nor their forces can be repaired or replaced**. The party has permanently crippled those sectors.

**Phase 2: The Scavenger Vacuum**  
Nature abhors a vacuum, and a dungeon full of hundreds of fresh corpses is a feast. The empty rooms do not stay empty for long.

* Because the party orchestrated a mass slaughter, the undead ecosystem reacts. The ghouls and ghasts from the unaligned territories (Areas 115-118, or Level 2's crypts) will migrate into the newly emptied Fire and Water territories to feed.  
* You tell the players: *"You have won the war, but the stench of death is drawing scavengers from the deep. If you do not secure the empty territories, you will be fighting a ghoul infestation by tomorrow."*

**Phase 3: The Party's Agency (Playing "Dungeon Boss")**  
To give the party ultimate agency over the aftermath, you hand them the map. Because they are the true power behind the surviving puppet (Hartsch), Hartsch comes to them to ask how to distribute the surviving Earth Temple forces and the Broken Blades.  
Give the players a specific pool of surviving assets (e.g., 15 Earth Guards, 4 Ogres, the Broken Blades, and perhaps the Troll Chieftain if they recruited him). **Ask the players to assign these troops to specific rooms or chokepoints.**

* Do they abandon Level 1 entirely to fortify the newly conquered Level 2?  
* Do they set up a toll-gate at the Level 1/Level 2 stairs?  
* Do they order the Ogres to haul the bodies to the Otyugh's garbage room (Area 108\) to prevent a ghoul outbreak?

By forcing them to deploy their own troops, the players literally redesign the dungeon map for the rest of the campaign.

#  War is Over  **The General's Inspection.** Just as the party finishes redeploying their troops and looting the empty Fire/Water vaults, the heavy bronze doors leading to Level 3 open. **Commander Barkinar** (Level 7 Cleric) marches up with a detachment of elite Blackthorn Ogres and Bugbears. He demands that Hartsch and his "new lieutenants" (the party) explain the carnage and prove they are strong enough to hold the territory they just stole, forcing the party into a high-stakes diplomatic standoff with the dungeon's true military commander.
