# Handouts — Kelno's Chamber

Five player-facing handouts for the Kelno encounter (Bridge Session between Phase 2 and Phase 3). Each file contains the handout content (for the players) plus GM notes on tone, what to highlight, and how to use it.

| # | File | What it is | When to surface |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `01_belsornig_dispatch.md` | Intercepted military dispatch ordering Hartsch's assassination | On the table when the party enters — Kelno will gesture to it during his pitch |
| 2 | `02_kelno_ledger.md` | One page from Kelno's bribery ledger — payments, defections, margin notes | On the table, open — visible from the moment they enter |
| 3 | `03_kelno_map.md` | Hand-drawn annotated map of the route to the Greater Temple | Folded on the table; Kelno offers it as part of the pitch |
| 4 | `04_kelno_unsent_letter.md` | A letter Kelno wrote to a dead apprentice; never sent | **Only if the party searches the shelf.** Kelno does not offer this. |
| 5 | `05_skull_labels.md` | The 23 labels on the skulls displayed on the shelf | Read aloud as PCs approach the shelf and read the tags |

## Reading order at the table

If the party arrives and Kelno makes his pitch:
1. **He gestures to the dispatch first** — the time-pressure piece. (Handout 1)
2. **He taps the ledger** — proof he's been ahead of them. (Handout 2)
3. **If they hesitate, he unfolds the map** — *"and this is what you have been working toward without quite knowing it."* (Handout 3)
4. **The skulls are environmental** — they are read whenever a PC gets close to the shelf. (Handout 5)
5. **The unsent letter is private** — only found by searching, and only meaningful afterward. (Handout 4)

## Tone notes

All five handouts use the same dry, exact, monastic voice — Kelno keeps records the way a quiet bookkeeper would, regardless of the subject. The skull labels, the ledger margins, the unsent letter, the map annotations — all the same hand, all the same restraint. This is intentional. It tells the players who Kelno is even before he speaks.

The single break in tone is the **"MARAD — gone to Lolth"** label, which is the only one in capital letters across all five handouts. If anyone notices this in play, it's the most important seed in the handout pack.

## What the handouts together accomplish

- **Validate Kelno's pitch** with documentary evidence (dispatch, ledger)
- **Hand the party the strategic picture** (map, ledger margin notes)
- **Plant Phase 4 seeds** (Patris, Joseph, Dala, Lucius, Lolth via Marad)
- **Give Kelno depth as a person** (skulls, letter) — making the kill-or-spare choice carry weight
- **Reveal the antipathy ward** on the descent past the Greater Temple door — load-bearing for Phase 4 alignment management

If you only print one: the map (handout 3). The annotations contain the most actionable intelligence.

If you only read aloud one: the unsent letter (handout 4), but *only after* the party has made their decision about Kelno's fate. Use it as the closing beat.
