# HANDOUT — The Labels on Kelno's Skulls

> **GM context:** Twenty-three skulls on the shelves in Kelno's Grim Chamber. Polished. Arranged in rows of about eight. Each labeled in the same thin spidery hand as his ledger — small parchment tags tied through the eye sockets with gray thread.
>
> Most are old — apprentices and acolytes dead over the years of internal Temple violence. Three are recent (the three the party should notice first).
>
> Read aloud as the party gets close enough to read the tags. Pace it slowly — let them register what they're looking at.

---

---

> **What the party should notice:**
>
> - **23 skulls.** Twenty-three apprentices, named, counted, dated. Kelno has been the Air Temple prefect since 575 (per the canon — Ashrem his predecessor was sacrificed by Alrrem of Fire around then) and the count goes back further. *He inherited the count.*
>
> - **"Tannet — killed by my hand, 571 (he asked)"** — the only one Kelno killed himself. The parenthetical is the tell. Mercy killing, by request. Tannet was Kelno's first apprentice. Kelno has not killed an apprentice since.
>
> - **"Iven — killed in the nodes, 576"** and **"Galen — killed in the nodes, 577"** — two of his apprentices died *in the Elemental Nodes.* This is the only direct mention so far that Air Temple operatives have entered the Nodes and not come back. Foreshadowing for Arc B2.
>
> - **"Mira-the-second — killed by Fire, 577 (named for her aunt)"** — generational. The first Mira (row one) died in 570. The second was named for her, lived seven years, died the same way. Kelno tracks the cycles.
>
> - **The three recent:** Galt and Iren both died on Planting 14 — the same day — Belsornig's wave and Alrrem's torch. The same day. This was a coordinated Fire-and-Water strike on the Air Temple two and a half weeks ago. The two days before the three-way war began. Kelno *survived* this strike with no apprentices left to defend.
>
> - **"MARAD — gone to Lolth, 579 Plant 6"** — the only skull labeled in capital letters. The only label using *"gone to"* instead of *"killed by"*. Marad is also the one the unsent letter (handout 04) is addressed to. Something about Marad is different from the others.
>   - **GM-only interpretation:** Marad was Kelno's most senior apprentice. He was approached by Lolth's network (via Falrinth or Smigmal) and recruited away. Marad accepted — and was killed shortly after (the Lolth network does not keep witnesses to a recruitment that failed to bear fruit, or perhaps killed him for backing out). Either way, Marad chose Lolth and Marad died for it. Kelno blames Lolth specifically. **This is why Kelno will, if asked, sell out the Lolth network the moment he is asked.** He hates Lolth more than Fire or Water.

> **GM use:**
>
> - Have one PC do the close reading. Zinnia is the obvious choice (Operational Acuity score is high). Read the row labels in groups, let them ask questions, let them count.
> - The Marad label is the single most important seed. If the party ever encounters Lolth-side material later, the recognition should be theirs: *"the skull with capital letters — Marad — that was Lolth."*
> - If the party shows compassion at the shelf — a comment, a quiet moment, anyone touching one of the tags — Kelno notices and his next line softens. Possible Kelno line: *"You may name yours, too, when this is done. I will keep the count if you do not want to."*
> - **Do not let the party take the skulls.** Kelno would consider this an offense beyond all measure. The skulls stay on the shelf even if Kelno dies — they are a memorial, not loot.
