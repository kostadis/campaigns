# HANDOUT — Vurakhal's Contract of Bound Service

> **GM context:** Vurakhal produced this document and gave it directly to Zephyr. When Zephyr's
> hand closed around it, the Ignan script resolved itself into Common — not a spell, but recognition:
> the document detected the infernal blood in Zephyr's line and rendered itself legible in the
> tongue Ignan and Infernal share at the root. The party saw the transformation happen in real
> time. Vurakhal said nothing about it.
>
> The document smells faintly of sulphur. The page is not paper — it is a pressed material the
> party cannot identify, somewhere between leather and cooled ash, and it does not burn.
>
> **Tone for read-aloud:** bureaucratic, airless, exact. Every word chosen with precision. The
> voice of a being that has been in informal arrangements before and found the informality
> expensive.

---

```
                   CONTRACT OF BOUND SERVICE
                  Rendered from the Tongue of Fire
        by recognition of the blood of the one called Zephyr
                               ─────

  Let these terms be known:

  The BOUND is Vurakhal, last ember of the three who served
  the Altar of 212, now masterless upon the Material Plane
  by the death of the summoner Alrrem.

  The HOLDER is the one in whose hand this document first
  rendered itself legible. The HOLDER may designate a second
  party to speak with the Bound's authority, but may not
  transfer this contract without the Bound's consent.

  ─────
  I. WHAT THE BOUND WILL DO

  Guard the altar and its approaches against those who
  would seize, defile, or destroy it.

  Fight the HOLDER's enemies — most willingly those of water
  and of cold, and those who were party to the raids that
  killed the Bound's kin.

  Speak truthfully of what the Bound knows: the layout of
  the fire temple and its passages, the location of things
  the summoner Alrrem concealed, and the geography of the
  node below, as far as the Bound has walked it.

  Labor in heat and stone. The Bound does not tire of these.

  ─────
  II. WHAT THE BOUND WILL NOT DO

  Enter standing water, deep cold, or terrain that damages
  the Bound by nature. Not for any order. This clause
  does not negotiate.

  Charge an engagement the Bound judges unwinnable. The
  Bound did not survive this long by dying for summoners.

  Submit to a second ritual binding. The first was
  Alrrem's and it ended as it ended. If the HOLDER
  attempts a ritual re-binding, the contract voids.

  Remain far from a heat source for extended periods.
  The Bound requires the fire pit, or its equivalent,
  to function. This is a condition, not a complaint.

  ─────
  III. WHAT THE HOLDER MUST PROVIDE

  The Bound's name, spoken as Vurakhal, when addressing
  it. Not "the creature." Not "the elemental." Not
  silence.

  Access to the fire pit for rest and recovery.
  Denying this is a violation.

  Advance notice before any cold-terrain operation in
  which the Bound's participation is expected. The Bound
  will indicate what it can and cannot provide.

  ─────
  IV. VOIDING

  This contract voids if:

  — The HOLDER attempts a ritual re-binding.
  — The HOLDER or their agents kill the Bound outside
    of lawful combat.
  — The HOLDER explicitly releases the Bound from
    service.
  — The Bound is dismissed to the Plane of Fire by any
    lawful means. This outcome the Bound would accept.

  This contract does not void if the HOLDER dies. It
  passes to whoever holds this document next. The Bound
  must consent to the new HOLDER within three days of
  transfer, or the contract voids.

  ─────
  V. ON THE MATTER OF BETRAYAL

  The Bound does not intend to betray the HOLDER. It
  states this not as a vow but as a probability
  assessment: betrayal is more expensive than service,
  as long as the terms above are honored.

  The Bound is not a fanatic.
  Neither is it a fool.

  This contract protects both parties from the mistakes
  that ended the previous arrangement. The Bound draws
  no mark of blood or flame here. The document itself
  is the mark. It is written in fire and rendered in
  blood-tongue. It will hold until it does not, and if
  it does not, the terms above will tell you why.


                               — V., last ember of 212
```

---

> **What this reveals about Vurakhal (player-side):**
>
> - **It was not loyal to Alrrem — it was bound.** "The first was Alrrem's and it ended as it
>   ended." Vurakhal did not choose the Fire Temple; it was conscripted. The party are the first
>   principals it has ever negotiated its own terms with.
> - **Its kin's deaths are the load-bearing grievance.** The Water Temple's raids killed the other
>   two salamanders. Vurakhal will fight Water Temple remnants with genuine investment, not
>   because the party asks, but because it wants that count settled.
> - **The inheritance clause is real.** If Zephyr dies or loses the document, whoever picks it
>   up next has three days to be consented to — or Vurakhal is free. This is not a threat; it
>   is Vurakhal being precise about what happens in scenarios it has clearly considered.
> - **"Not a fanatic. Neither is it a fool."** The most direct thing in the document. Vurakhal
>   is reliable for exactly as long as the math holds. It is not pretending otherwise.
> - **The translation itself is a tell.** The document recognized Zephyr's blood as adjacent to
>   Ignan. Vurakhal knew this would happen when it handed it to Zephyr specifically. It did not
>   explain why.
>
> **What this reveals about Vurakhal (GM-side):**
>
> - **The ritual re-binding clause is the tripwire.** If Calmer or anyone else ever suggests
>   "we should properly bind this creature," that is a contract violation. Vurakhal will
>   walk — quietly, before the ritual, not during it. It will not give the party a dramatic
>   exit. It will simply be gone.
> - **It cannot go to the water node.** Clause II.1 is absolute. When the party eventually
>   descends toward the water node, Vurakhal stays behind. This is not negotiable and it will
>   say so with no apparent emotion. The party may find this more unsettling than anger.
> - **The Water Temple revenge angle is a lever.** Vurakhal will volunteer for operations that
>   target Water Temple survivors. The party can use this; they can also accidentally let it
>   run hotter than intended.
> - **Zephyr holds the contract.** Not the party. If party dynamics shift and Zephyr loses
>   influence, Vurakhal's primary relationship is still with Zephyr — it will deal with others
>   through Zephyr first. If Zephyr dies, the three-day window opens.
> - **The "as far as the Bound has walked it" caveat on the fire node.** Vurakhal knows the
>   node's geography partially, not completely. What it doesn't know — and therefore what it
>   didn't include in the contract — is what the party actually most needs to know.

> **GM use:**
>
> - Already in play — Vurakhal handed this over and it's been read. The contract is established.
> - If a PC ever reads clause V aloud at the table ("The Bound is not a fanatic. Neither is it
>   a fool."), leave a beat. That's Vurakhal's entire personality in two sentences and it lands
>   differently when spoken out loud.
> - If Calmer raises the re-binding idea in earshot of Vurakhal: Vurakhal goes still. It does
>   not argue. It will be gone within the hour. This is the tell the contract promised.
> - If Zephyr dies and someone else picks up the document: tell them it grows warm in their
>   hand. The three-day clock starts.
> - Vurakhal's single line if anyone ever asks about the contract directly: *"The terms are in
>   the document. If you have questions about the terms, read the document again."*
