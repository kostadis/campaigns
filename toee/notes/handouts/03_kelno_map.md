# HANDOUT — Kelno's Annotated Map

> **GM context:** Folded once, on Kelno's table beside the dispatch and ledger. Drawn in graphite on thick parchment, then heavily annotated in red and black ink — different inks, different sessions. Some lines are scratched out and re-drawn.
>
> The map shows **two different routes down to Dungeon Level Three.** Kelno's annotations distinguish them carefully and explicitly warn against the wrong one.
>
> If you have a real handout to print, sketch the map by hand on parchment paper. The annotations are the load-bearing part — they can also be read aloud.
>
> **Tone for read-aloud:** Kelno's annotations have the same dry exactness as his ledger. Distances are precise. Doubts are recorded honestly.

   ▸ TWO ROUTES. ONE OF THEM WILL KILL YOU. The other is what you
     came to find. I label them carefully because I have done both

---

> **What the party should notice:**
>
> - **Two distinct routes down to Level Three.** Route A — the door they already found, which opens when the war ends — leads through a plain (no-ward) counter-clockwise stair to Area 314. Route B — the antipathy stair from Area 227 — is a separate and *forbidden* path that ends at the cold-iron bronze doors at Area 340.
>
> - **"The Great Door... is barred from inside. It is not warded. It is a barricade, not an enchantment."** This is the critical correction the players need. The door the wraith was guarding is being **physically held shut from the other side by Barkinar's people** until the war is settled. It is not a ward they have to break or test against. When Belsornig dies and the war resolves, the barricade lifts. They walk through.
>
> - **"The voice from beyond it has spoken to me twice. The second time it asked my name. I did not give it. You may; I think it will be glad of yours."** Kelno offers the party the opening pitch with Barkinar — "give your name and you will be received." This is Kelno's gift to them.
>
> - **The antipathy route is a separate problem for a separate session.** Mark it clearly: NOT NOW. The cold-iron bronze doors at 340 require the *pillars* (the module's elemental pillars — referenced in canon area 7349). Kelno doesn't know where they are. The party shouldn't either, yet. **Powerful magic only.**
>
> - **"This is one of the four sets of doors that bind the demoness below to the dungeon."** The first explicit mention to the party that there are warded gates *binding* something — they will not yet know about Zuggtmoy by name, but the word "demoness" lands. The four gates are canon (entrance + 210 + 145 + 340). Each pair sundered = Zuggtmoy gains some freedom. **The party should NOT be tempted to sunder them.** Kelno's annotation is doing the work of warning them off.
>
> - **"Belsornig knows about the prisoner who told you... Either it does not threaten him or he wants the door opened."** Belsornig has a plan for the post-war moment that does not involve fighting the party at the door. **Reinforces** the Belsornig dispatch ("They are not to be killed yet"). The party will not work out *why* yet — but they should feel the cold shape of it.

> **GM use:**
>
> - The map gives the party operational confidence — they now have a *drawn* route to Level 3 via the Great Door, with a faster sub-route via Kelno's drapery (225 → 209a → fountain → stair).
> - The antipathy route is a *future* problem. Plant it now as a clearly labeled "do not attempt." It becomes relevant much later — possibly when Hedrack tries to access the deeper gates as part of his ritual, or when the party realizes they need to break a gate themselves.
> - The "voice asks names" line is Kelno setting the party up to *succeed* with Barkinar. He has cheated for them. Acknowledge it gracefully.
> - Save the **four gates / pillars / demoness** lore as cold setup. It pays off when Hedrack's plan finally surfaces (the gates bind Zuggtmoy; sundering them frees her; the pillars are how) — see `hedrack_strategy.md` and module area 7349.
