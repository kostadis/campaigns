# HANDOUT — Belsornig's Intercepted Dispatch

> **GM context:** Found on Kelno's table, opened. Sealing wax broken — Kelno's goblin runners intercepted the courier two days ago, copied the message, then let the original continue (the actual assassins still got their orders; the party reads what Kelno's people read). This is the smoking gun proving Hartsch's death is in motion and that the party is out of time on the puppet.
>
> **Tone for read-aloud:** terse military hand. Belsornig writes the way he fights — efficient, precise, no wasted words.

---

```
       ⚛ BY THE CANON OF THE CRUSHING WAVE ⚛

To Telgar, by my own seal.

The Earth pretender names himself "Supreme Prophet"
and sends mercenaries against my outposts.
His authority is paper. End him.

Two men through the north service hall while the
guard is at the long table. Wait until he is alone
in his chamber. Door bar should be unset; he barred
it once and was reminded not to insult his speakers.
Be brief. Take nothing.

I expect confirmation by the third bell.

If the Obsidian Edge appear in your hall, withdraw
to the steam corridors and report. Do not engage.
They are not to be killed yet.

                                          — B.
```

---

> **What the party should notice:**
> - **"The Earth pretender names himself Supreme Prophet"** — Belsornig knows about Hartsch's coronation; he's been getting real-time intel
> - **"Two men through the north service hall"** — specific operational detail; this is happening
> - **"Door bar should be unset"** — someone inside the Earth Temple is helping. A traitor in Hartsch's household.
> - **"I expect confirmation by the third bell"** — there is a clock. Hartsch dies tonight if nothing changes.
> - **"If the Obsidian Edge appear in your hall, withdraw... They are not to be killed yet."** — Belsornig is aware of the party by their Earth Temple alias, considers them too dangerous to engage casually, and is *holding* on them for some later play. He has a *plan* for them.

> **GM use of the dispatch:**
> - Confirms Kelno's pitch ("Belsornig's assassins reached Hartsch's antechamber an hour ago") with documentary proof
> - Hardens the choice — letting Hartsch die is no longer abstract; the bar will be unset, the assassins are walking the north service hall
> - Plants two follow-up threads: (1) who is the traitor inside Hartsch's household? (2) what was Belsornig saving the party *for*?
