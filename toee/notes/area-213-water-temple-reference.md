# Area 213 — Hall of Verdigris (Water Temple, canonical reference)

Authoritative module description for the Water Temple's main hall. Use for handouts, image
prompts, and read-aloud. Verbatim read-boxed text below; do not paraphrase it into "facts"
without checking against the source.

**Orientation:** the party enters from the **NORTH** — the north wall is the door, near its
**east end**. The altar sits roughly center; the fountain is on the **west** wall; the
serpentine idol is in the alcove to the **south**.

**Shape:** the hall is a **wide, shallow rectangle — 90' wide (E–W) × 40' deep (N–S)**. The
party looks across the *wide* axis, NOT down a long nave. Facing south from the door, **east is
on their left, west on their right.**

**GM's table layout (from the door, facing south):**
- ~20' to the **left (east)** — a verdigris bronze gargoyle plinth (NE corner).
- ~40' **straight ahead** against the far south wall — the serpentine marine-monster idol, in
  its seaweed-draped alcove.
- ~50' to the **right (west)**, deeper in — the round bronze altar; the fish-head fountain basin
  is on the far west wall; two more gargoyle plinths stand in the western corners.

## Read-boxed description (verbatim)

> This huge hall, 40' x 90', has an arched ceiling some 30 feet overhead. The many buttresses
> and arches form a tracery of shadowy dimness; the ceiling's exact height is not discernible.
> The floor and walls are covered in slabs of polished stone, apparently azurite-malachite from
> the swirls of blue intermingled with the deep green. Some magic has evidently been placed
> here, for a soft cloudy greenish luminosity seeps from the walls and floor, seemingly floating
> in the air, making the whole chamber appear as if deep underwater. This impression is enhanced
> by the bronze doors, fountain, and other work; all are covered with verdigris. Even the damp
> air seems to smell of the sea. Several weird things in this place come to your immediate
> attention.
>
> In the middle of the west wall is a great sheet of bronze, a bas relief of an underwater
> vista—seaweed, shells, and various forms of marine life. The head of a fish-like thing
> projects from this, a most hideous visage. Its ghastly maw emits a stream of water, which
> falls into a tiered series of four basins. The fourth and largest never overflows, so it must
> have a drain system somewhere.
>
> Near the mid-portion of the hall, some 40 feet distant from the east wall and 30 feet from the
> north wall, is a bronze altar with many marine creatures sculpted in bas relief upon its sides.
> It is about four feet high and round, with a ten-foot diameter. Its top is concave, and a
> shallow basin apparently about two feet deep is filled with water. The bottom of the basin is
> filled with coins, a sprinkling of gems, and many small seashells.
>
> In the mouth of a 20' wide alcove south of the altar basin stands an idol or statue of
> serpentine stone, expertly sculpted to depict a writhing, amorphous agglomeration of fish,
> eels, octopi, and other marine monsters. This horrid thing is about eight feet high and nearly
> as broad, the whole forming a globular mass standing about four feet from the altar basin. The
> alcove to its rear is ten feet deep, the back wall of which is covered by a drapery apparently
> fashioned from seaweed and water!
>
> Each corner of the hall is filled by a triangular plinth, filling it and extending about 20
> feet upwards. Atop each of these columns is a gargoyle-like statue made of bronze and coated
> with verdigris.

## Key features at a glance

- **Dimensions:** 40' × 90' hall, arched ceiling ~30' up, indiscernible exact height; shadowy
  buttress/arch tracery.
- **Surfaces:** polished **azurite-malachite** — blue swirled with deep green.
- **The illusion:** soft cloudy **greenish luminosity** seeps from walls/floor, floating in the
  air → the dry chamber **looks deep underwater**. Damp, sea-smelling air.
- **Verdigris everywhere:** bronze doors, fountain, fittings all green-corroded.
- **West wall — fountain:** great bronze bas-relief underwater vista; a **hideous fish-head**
  spouts water into a **tiered cascade of four basins** (fourth/largest never overflows — hidden
  drain).
- **Center — altar:** round **bronze altar**, 10' diameter, 4' high, marine bas-relief sides;
  concave top with a ~2'-deep **water basin** full of **coins, gems, seashells**. (~40' from
  east wall, ~30' from north wall.)
- **South alcove — idol:** 8'-tall **serpentine-stone idol**, a writhing globular mass of fish,
  eels, octopi and marine monsters, ~4' from the altar; alcove 10' deep behind it; back wall
  hung with a **seaweed-and-water drapery**.
- **Corners:** four triangular plinths, ~20' tall, each topped with a **verdigris bronze
  gargoyle** statue.

>
> Drowned, sacred and sinister atmosphere of an evil water cult — silent, dripping, a flooded temple
> that holds no water. Cinematic ultra-wide panoramic composition from the east doorway looking
> across the broad hall, ethereal underwater lighting, palette of teal, deep green, verdigris bronze
> and shadowed blue, volumetric green god-rays, faint silt-like motes suspended in the air, painterly
> high-detail fantasy concept art. 21:9 ultrawide (or 16:9).

**Prompt notes:**
- The shape lever is **"twice as wide as it is deep" + a wide aspect ratio (21:9)** stated together
  — that's what forces the rectangle. Image models default to square-ish rooms otherwise.
- Composition uses **left/right** (not east/west) because the model places objects by screen
  position. Party faces south, so east = left, west = right.
- If the render floods with real water, add "the room is completely dry; the underwater effect is
  glowing green light only."
- The fish-head fountain and the serpentine idol each reward a separate close-up prompt for handout
  art.
