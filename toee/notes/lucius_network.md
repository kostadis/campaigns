# Lucius Graeme — The Friendly Resource (Network & Supply Map)

GM-only operational map. Covers Lucius's **first contact with the party run as a pure
resource play** — the helpful man who can get them what they want. This is the *outside*
of Lucius (his intel picture, his supply chain, how he books the leash as a favor). For the
*inside* — his creed, his secret allegiance to Zuggtmoy, the Thrommel probe, the manipulation
arcs — see `lucius_strategy.md`. For the coup he serves and why the generosity exists at all,
see `hedrack_strategy.md`. For the Nulb half of his supply chain, see `nulb_network.md`.

> **The one-line GM frame:** This session, Lucius is not interrogating them and not steering
> them anywhere. He is *furnishing* them. Every probe, every Senshock seed, every Prince
> question is **holstered**. The whole point of the visit is that the party walks away thinking
> *"finally, someone useful,"* and the leash is the fact that they now have a guy. The warmth is
> real and the warmth is the weapon. Both at once.

---

## The Frame — What This Visit Is

Lucius arrives as the Greater Temple's quartermaster-of-favors, sent (truthfully) by Barkinar
to make sure the new management has what it needs. Recall Barkinar's exit line when he installed
them: he left a Blackthorn Ogre behind *"as an honor guard until you are properly equipped with
a household."* **Lucius is the equipping.** That is the literal pretext and it holds up because
it is genuinely his job.

He plays it as the least threatening man in the dungeon: portly, soft, plain gray clothes, low
gray hat over the scarred face, faintly buffoonish, a host filling cups. He brings a gift. He
asks what they need. He gets it for them. He does not ask for anything back — *this time* — and
that is what makes it work. A party that has spent the whole campaign being used, lied to, and
under-supplied suddenly has a friendly man with a warehouse. Of course they take the deal. There
is no deal to take. That's the deal.

> **GM-only:** The leash goes on here, invisibly. Hedrack's whole strategy (per
> `hedrack_strategy.md`) is to keep the party *alive, empowered, and pointed*. Empowering them
> is step one. Lucius makes them rich and well-armed because a well-armed asset survives the
> Nodes, kills Senshock, and recovers the Orb. The generosity is an investment in tools he
> intends to use. Do not let the party feel the hook. Let them feel served.

## What Lucius KNOWS About the Party (his intel picture)

His network is the best in the Temple, and he has had two weeks and a war to watch. Walk in
assuming he knows roughly this much:

- **The headline.** Four prophets dead in a single day (Romag, Hartsch, Alrrem, Belsornig), and
  Barkinar handed these four unknowns the Upper Temple. He did *not* choose them — which, per
  `lucius_strategy.md`, privately unsettles him more than he shows. He wants to *map* them. But
  this session that mapping is passive: he watches, he files, he does not push.
- **They were in Nulb.** His network flagged it (Selentis's people, the Waterside traffic). He
  knows they were *in Nulb*; recommend he does **not** yet know *who they saw there* (this keeps
  Y'dey / Mother Screng's cover safe for now — see `nulb_allies.md`). If the party ever sat at
  the Waterside Hostel, they may have brushed a washed-up gray-clad gambler named **"Aldren"**
  without knowing it (his Nulb alias, per `nulb_network.md`) — a delicious quiet callback if it
  fits their history.
- **They want things.** Coin, gear, magic. This is the lever he's leading with, and it's an easy
  read — they've been hauling unidentified Temple loot for months (the TZGY scarab, the werewolf
  silver, the warlock's whip, Lareth's plate that fits no one).
- **The scarab.** Calmer carries the **TZGY scarab** — Senshock's personal control device (see
  `tzgy_scarab_urgent.md`). Lucius would recognize it on sight. How he plays that recognition is
  an **Open Decision** below; the recommended read is that he clocks it silently and gives nothing
  away.
- **The Lareth question.** Whether Lucius knows the party killed Lareth (a Lolth agent) runs
  through Deggum/Smigmal channels. **Open Decision** below; recommend he does not surface it.

**What he does NOT know:** anything about the sleeping Prince (Thrommel) — the one thing he most
needs and most fears; and the party's true allegiance (he assumes they're ruthless opportunists,
which is close enough that it doesn't alarm him).

## What the Party Knows About Lucius (the asymmetry)

**Essentially nothing — and that's the point of running him early.** In the original sequencing
(`phase3_endgame.md`), Falrinth's late-night contact primed them first: *"a flattering man with
golden eyes is coming; do not commit; stall."* Pulling Lucius forward means **that warning has not
happened.** The party gets:

- No "golden eyes" tell to match.
- No reason to suspect the friendly quartermaster is anything but a friendly quartermaster.
- No instinct to stall.

So the asymmetry is total: **he reads them; they do not read him.** A sharp player might still
get a flicker on Insight (he's human, his fool-act has a seam), but there is no external cue
telling them to look. This is the cleanest read Lucius will ever get of the party, which is
exactly why it's worth giving him — and exactly why Falrinth's priming should move to *after*
this session (Open Decision 3) so the manipulation arc still has its warning shot later.

## How He Approaches — the friendly-resource script

Arrival: after the party's long rest, unhurried, announced (a runner, or Dren ushering him in —
"Boss, there's a man from downstairs says Barkinar sent him; seems harmless; I don't trust
harmless"). He comes with a gift and an open hand.

Voice (lift or paraphrase — warm, expansive, self-deprecating):

> *"Forgive the intrusion on your first quiet morning. I'm Aldren — well, Lucius, Aldren when
> I'm working, it's a long story and a dull one. Barkinar asked me to make sure the new
> management has everything it needs, and between us I think he undersold the job. Four prophets
> in a day! The man could barely say it without smiling, and he never smiles. Sit, sit. I brought
> you something to take the edge off, and then you're going to tell me what you're short of, and
> I'm going to fix it. That's the whole of my talent — I carry things from clever people to clever
> people, and you four are the cleverest thing to happen to this rock in a year."*

The gift (pick one, GM): a cask of genuinely good wine; a *Potion of Superior Healing* each "for
the household"; or — the warmest read — he returns something *they* lost or never identified (he's
had one of their unidentified items quietly appraised and hands back the answer plus the item,
"a courtesy"). The gift costs him nothing and buys enormous goodwill.

Then the open offer: *"Tell me what you need."*

## The Supply Chain — How He Actually Delivers Magic Items

"I can get you anything" has to be *true* at the table or it's hollow. Lucius can deliver because
he sits on top of three real pipes:

### Pipe 1 — Temple-Confiscated Gear (he brokers access)
The party nominally *rules* the Upper Temple, but they don't know where anything is or what it is.
Lucius does. Four dead prophets' personal effects, the Water Temple treasury they're auditing right
now, the Greater Temple's quartermaster stores — all of it is inventory he can *unlock* for them.
He's not giving them his things; he's giving them *their own conquered things, sorted and
identified*, which feels like a gift and is really just information advantage. Canonically floating
in the Temple leadership's holdings (sourceable): *Ring of Protection*, *Ring of Invisibility*,
*Ring of Free Action*, *Ring of Feather Falling*, *Ring of Fire Resistance*, *Boots of Levitation*,
*Hat of Disguise*, *Staff of Striking*, *Dagger of Venom*, *Potion of Speed*, *Potion of Superior
Healing*, *Spell Scroll of Teleport*.

### Pipe 2 — The Nulb Funnel (the black market)
Anything not in the Temple, he buys up the river. Per `nulb_network.md`: Selentis's people →
the Broker (Lorveth) runs discreet auctions and laundering upstairs at the Boatman's → smuggling
in via the supply chain. This is how he sources *specific requests* ("a wand of that, a cloak of
this") on a few days' delay. It also quietly tightens the party's dependence on **Madame
Selentis's** logistics — a future thread, not a present one.

### Pipe 3 — His Own Courier Network (special orders & favors)
For the genuinely rare ask, Lucius "knows a man." This is the slowest pipe (a session or more of
in-fiction lead time) and the one that most explicitly turns a purchase into an *obligation* — the
groundwork for asking a favor back later. He won't lean on that this session; he's just
establishing that the channel exists.

## The Menu & The Price

**The apparent price:** nothing. Or "favors between friends." Or a fraction of the treasury they're
already counting. He is conspicuously, disarmingly cheap.

**The real price (GM-only):** dependency and direction. Every item makes them more capable of the
work Hedrack needs (the Nodes, Senshock, the Orb) and more accustomed to Lucius being the answer
to "where do we get X." He is buying the right to be their first phone call.

**A level-8/9-appropriate starting menu** (curate to taste — this is the GM-approval scope decision,
Open Decision 4). Keep it uncommon/rare; nothing legendary, nothing that trivializes the Nodes later:

| The party is short on… | What Lucius produces | Pipe | Lead time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Survivability | *Ring of Protection*, *Potion of Superior Healing* ×N | 1 | now |
| Mobility / infiltration | *Boots of Levitation*, *Hat of Disguise*, *Ring of Feather Falling* | 1 | now |
| A sword for Sequoia / Zephyr | *Dagger of Venom*, or a +1 weapon from the prophets' effects | 1 | now |
| Burst / escape | *Potion of Speed*, *Spell Scroll of Teleport* | 1 | now |
| Anti-caster | *Staff of Striking* | 1 | now |
| A *specific* named item they ask for | "Let me make some inquiries." | 2 / 3 | days |

> **GM caution (the only real scope risk):** What the party walks away holding changes the
> difficulty of everything downstream. Two hard limits worth keeping: (1) nothing that hands them
> reliable long-range escape *and* invisibility *and* free action all at once — that breaks the
> Nodes and the Senshock hit; (2) the genuinely build-defining stuff (a second *Ring of Free
> Action*, *Mirror of Mental Prowess*, anything Hedrack/Senshock-tier) is **Pipe 3 / "inquiries"**,
> i.e. a future-session obligation, never a freebie this morning. Approve the actual list before play.

## Connections to Other Nodes

- **Madame Selentis / Nulb** (`nulb_network.md`) — Pipe 2 runs through her. Sourcing through Lucius
  quietly routes the party into Selentis's logistics. The canonical introduction chain
  (Hedrack → Lucius → Selentis) still exists; running Lucius early just means he leads with goods
  instead of with Selentis's vouch.
- **Falrinth** (`falrinth_strategy.md`) — the rival who is *supposed* to warn the party about Lucius
  ("golden eyes, stall him"). Pulled-early Lucius means Falrinth's warning lands *after* this
  session and reads to the party as *"wait — the nice quartermaster?"* — which is a stronger beat,
  not a weaker one. See Open Decision 3.
- **Senshock** (`hedrack_strategy.md`, `tzgy_scarab_urgent.md`) — the TZGY scarab Calmer carries is
  Senshock's. Lucius recognizing it is a live future lever; how he plays it this session is Open
  Decision 1.
- **Dren Halveth** — the obvious, trusted delivery vector. Lucius can route gifts and goods through
  Dren, which both reassures the party (their man handled it) and gently tests Dren's loyalty.

## Negotiation Stances (the friendly subset)

- **Concedes freely (it's the whole pitch):** goods, identification, appraisal, small true favors,
  genuinely useful gifts, flattery, the appearance of total transparency.
- **Trades, lightly:** access and supply for goodwill and the standing invitation to be their
  fixer. He is *not* asking them to do anything yet. The ask comes later, off the credit he's
  building now.
- **Deflects, smoothly (never refuses — he *redirects*):** any question that gets near the Prince,
  the Orb, his real allegiance, or the coup. He doesn't stonewall; he laughs, charms, and changes
  the subject so the party doesn't notice a door was closed. ("Senshock? Oh, you'll meet him.
  Careful man. Brilliant. Doesn't like new people. More wine?")
- **Never, even friendly:** raises his voice; makes a threat; lets the fool-act slip on purpose;
  admits the warmth is strategic.

## Open Decisions (for you)

1. **The TZGY scarab.** Lucius would recognize Senshock's control device on Calmer. This session,
   does he **(a)** clock it silently, give nothing away, and file it *(recommended — preserves the
   future lever and keeps the visit clean)*; **(b)** genuinely not notice (simpler, but wastes a
   beat); or **(c)** gently flag it — *"that's an unusual little thing your cleric's carrying; I'd
   keep it out of sight downstairs"* — which is friendly, useful, and plants that the scarab matters
   without explaining why?
2. **Does Lucius know about Lareth?** Channels exist via Deggum/Smigmal. Recommend he does **not**
   surface it this session under any circumstances — it's a Falrinth-thread reveal, not a
   quartermaster-visit one.
3. **Falrinth's priming contact.** Now that Lucius arrives early and clean, move Falrinth's
   "golden eyes / stall him" contact to *after* this session (re-arming the manipulation arc with
   its warning shot intact), or cut it? **Recommend: move it later** — the party meeting the nice
   man *first* and being warned *second* is the better story.
4. **The magic-item menu.** The concrete list above is a starting point. Approve / trim / extend it
   before play — this is the one genuine power-level scope decision in the whole visit.
5. **The gift.** Wine (cheapest, warmest-comic), potions (useful), or returning-an-identified-item
   (most disarming, most work). Your call by table mood.
