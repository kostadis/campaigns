# Nulb Network — GM Reference

The full operational map of Nulb as Hedrack's logistical heart and the surface of multiple competing intrigues. The party has been operating in Nulb since chapter 13 but has seen only fragments. This doc gives you the whole picture for cross-referencing.

See also: `hedrack_strategy.md` for the campaign-level five-faction landscape, `falrinth_strategy.md` for the Lolth thread, `nulb_allies.md` for the hidden good-aligned operators.

## The Selentis Operation

### Madame Selentis — Real Name Lady Nysera Krivaltis

The de facto power broker of Nulb. Officially: a charismatic widow/criminal matron running the village's underworld from the Old Mayor's Manor. Actually: **Hedrack's chief Nulb logistics overseer**, installed and protected by Lucius Graeme.

**Origin canon:**
- Daughter of **Lord Eldris Krivaltis** of Verbobonc, executed in 569 CY for *aiding the Temple of Elemental Evil.* Family stripped of land and title.
- **Lucius Graeme personally extracted her on Hedrack's orders** — forged her death certificate, manufactured her false identity as Madame Selentis (a "minor noble widow from the Kron Hills with trade ties to Dyvers"), and installed her in Nulb.
- Has served Hedrack ever since. Her true name is a Temple Secret by Hedrack's direct decree.

**What she runs:**
- All non-temple logistics in Nulb: smuggling, prisoner sorting, contraband, supply routing.
- The Nulb Funnel: river drops (Tolub/Vesrek) → Boatman's intake → Old Mayor's Manor cellars → night carts → Hunting Cottage → tunnel into the Greater Temple.
- A bandit force of ~28-34 regulars + 6 veterans (squad leaders) + 1 lieutenant. Bandit Captain stat block for the lieutenant. Provided by Smigmal Redhand.
- **Cover:** "estate stewards collecting late taxes for a dead mayor." No Temple insignia in public.
- **Rule of operation:** *"Keep Nulb dirty, busy, and small. Make every problem look like pirates and bandits — not priests."*

**Personality:** Spider-like, patient, indirect. Operates through others. Public executions used sparingly but ruthlessly (Elfar Twell hanged at Gallows Table on her orders — the party knows this).

**Quote canon:** *"Order is a story we tell the frightened. I sell the ending."*

**What the party currently knows:** Per their docs, she "runs Nulb, comes from disgraced Verbobonc nobility, and is extremely dangerous." Warned about her by Eryk Velkori. Saw the consequences via Elfar Twell. **Has not met her in person.** This is the gap to exploit when Lucius needs to introduce himself credibly.

### Sir Torvel Hawkes — Selentis's Bodyguard

Loyal knight. Scarred veteran of the Battle of Emridy Meadows. Abandoned his noble path to serve Selentis after the fall of House Krivaltis. Motivation: redeem Nysera's honor through devotion and military strength.

He is the *human face* of Selentis's loyalty network. If the party ever meets her, Hawkes will be the silent armored figure beside her.

### Captain Krael Vortash — Greater Temple Captain in Nulb

Smigmal-placed Greater Temple Captain assigned to assist Selentis. Effectively Smigmal's military liaison in Nulb. Coordinates with Selentis's regulars on bigger operations.

### The Manor — Old Mayor's Manor

Selentis's command post. Sagging portico, boarded windows, garden gone to nettles. Two lanterns lit together only when a convoy is incoming. Notable features:
- War Room: river charts, rota boards, wax-sealed tallies from Boatman's Market and Waterside.
- Cellars: lock-pens for short-term prisoner holding, counterfeit tools, dye vats, **trapdoor to a rat-run leading to the next cottage** (escape route).
- Roof raven cage for signals.
- Cracked bell: three short peals = scatter to sites "1-4".
- Skeleton crew of 6 during daylight; 2-3 fireteams of 6-8 at night.

## The Supply Chain — Hedrack's True Operation in Nulb

This is the spine of Temple logistics. Disrupting any link causes turbulence. Disrupting all links — per the canonical *Chains of Command* quest — collapses Temple supply for weeks.

```
Pirates (Tolub, Vesrek) — river intake
    ↓
Boatman's Tavern cellars (Lodriss/Skole) — first sort
    ↓
The Broker (upstairs at Boatman's) — auctions/laundering
    ↓
Old Mayor's Manor (Selentis) — central sort, prisoner pens, manifest control
    ↓
Wainwright Depot (Ivar Maddok) — overland transport prep
    ↓
Hunting Cottage (Gefreid Patris) — final waypoint, sorting and storage
    ↓
Secret tunnel — Central Oval Cavern beneath Temple
    ↓
Greater Temple lower levels (under Commander Feldrin)
```

### Commander Feldrin — Logistics Master

Half-Oeridian, half-Flan rogue. Thief level 7, Neutral Evil. CR 4. Commands brigands by *leverage, not strength*. Wields +2 Rapier, has Cloak of Protection +1, studded leather +1, sneak attack 4d6.

- Resides in Area 243a on the Temple's second dungeon level (the party was on this level recently — Feldrin was nearby during the elemental war but uninvolved).
- Wealthy quarters reflect his importance.
- Trusted by Hedrack for sensitive missions (e.g., emissary to gnoll king Korvash Bonegnaw of the Jagged Fangs in the Lortmils).
- **Lt. Brunk** is his half-orc second-in-command — resentful, ambitious, wants Feldrin's job. Exploitable per the *Pact of the Howling Moon* quest.

### Gefreid Patris — Hunting Cottage Overseer

Runs the relay node where bandit loot and prisoners are inspected and redirected. Lives at the Hunting Cottage in the Gnarley Forest. Heavily guarded by Temple bandits, traps, dire hounds, bugbear/goblin guards in the tunnel, giant rats near the secret well entrance.

### Gattas Void — Moathouse Bandit Commander

Coordinated bandit raids out of the Moathouse during Lareth's tenure. Worked closely with Gefreid. Loyal to Lareth — **and now Lareth is dead by the party's hand.** Gattas does not know the party killed Lareth (cover story holds), but his world is destabilized. **Currently unaccounted-for in the campaign state** — likely a free agent looking for new patronage.

## The Tavern War — Surface Visible to the Party

The party has been operating in this triangle and knows several pieces. They have it *almost* right.

### Waterside Hostel — Earth Temple front (with Fire Temple infiltration)

**Owner: Dick Rentsch** — outwardly genial publican, secretly **Earth Temple agent** under Romag's network. With Romag dead (party kill), Rentsch's command-line is severed. He'll likely shift allegiance to whoever survives.

**Barkeep: Wat** — *canonically a Fire Temple agent* under Prefect Alrrem. With Alrrem dead (per the Phase 2 war), Wat's principal is also gone. Wat tried to interfere with the Broken Blades recruitment and was rebuffed by Dren.

**The party's grounding docs list Wat as Water Temple.** This is an in-character misread — they have him wrong. Worth noting; not worth correcting unless the party investigates further. (Skole correctly told them Tolub is Fire Temple; the implication that Wat is Tolub's man may have garbled into "Wat is Water." Canon: Wat → Fire, via Alrrem; Skole → Water-coerced via Tolub.)

**Wat's situation post-Phase-2:** His patron (Alrrem) is dead. He is a Fire Temple agent without a Fire Temple. He may seek to flip — to Earth (now party-controlled via Dren?), to Selentis directly, or out of Nulb.

**Serving staff: Dala and Pearl** — level-3 thieves who lift purses and feed gossip. **Dala** is a Rentsch lover/informant and also sells secrets to Smigmal and Lucius (per the *Whispered Rite* quest). She is a node in multiple networks.

### Boatman's Tavern — Water Temple front (technically) but really Lodriss's

**Hidden owner: Lodriss** — the actual power. Pirate Tolub's lover. Maintains her independence by playing Water Temple ties and pirate alliances against each other. Has not appeared to the party in person.

**Floor boss: Skole** — coerced into Water Temple collaboration through Tolub's leverage on Lodriss. Hates both. **Already established as a party ally** (chapter — when the party returned alive after Tolub announced their deaths, Skole pulled them privately to a back room and revealed Tolub works for Fire Temple, redirecting them to Dick Rentsch).

Skole is the most useful Nulb informant the party currently has. Push him gently — he's afraid of Lodriss and Tolub but already chose the party once.

**Above the Boatman's: The Iron Lash brothel (Elvanna)** — see Scarlet Brotherhood below.

**Upstairs: The Broker (Lorveth)** — slave factor. Runs discreet auctions for Selentis's supply chain. Coordinates with Elvanna (brothel) and the pirate captains. Keeps the ledgers. Launders coin.

### Captain Tolub — Iron Dagger

Pirate captain, Lodriss's lover, **Fire Temple agent** (was Alrrem's man). Lieutenant: **Grud Squinteye** (ruthless enforcer). Master of the Iron Dagger ship.

**Active enemy of the party.** Tolub publicly claimed to have killed them. With Alrrem dead, his principal is gone. He is now:
- A pirate captain without a Temple patron
- Lodriss's lover (her interest in him may shift)
- Still hostile to the party
- Holding leverage over Skole

His ship schedule is known (Jorin Pike Kale provided it). The party has all the intel needed to ambush him.

### Captain Marad Vesrek — Crimson Trader

Slaver captain. Slave Lord aspirations. Allied with Belsornig (now dead). Runs the Crimson Trader slaver ship along the Velverdyva. **Named in the warlock's ledger the party carries** — proven Chimera transaction with Fire Temple, working for the Water Temple side.

Vethra Velkori's divination ("crimson barge on the Velverdyva heavy with souls") = this ship.

**Active threat.** Belsornig dead means his Temple-side patronage is gone. Vesrek is now a free-agent slaver — possibly looking for new Temple alignment (Hedrack via Selentis?), possibly trying to leverage his slaver income into political independence.

### Constable Edrick Malvaar

A puppet. Once a proud soldier of Verbobonc's military, now a broken drunk maintained as a symbolic figure by Selentis. She keeps him pliant with coin, fear, and staged "wins." The party has encountered him — he declared a condemned temple south of Nulb off-limits.

**Use:** Selentis breaks him in front of the party if she needs a demonstration of control. Or — the party could break him as a quest, freeing him as a "lawful screen" they can leverage.

## Lucius Graeme's Nulb Operation

Lucius is *physically present in Nulb* periodically. He uses the alias "Aldren" — a washed-up gambler/merchant at the Waterside Hostel. Per the *Gambler's Veil* canon quest:
- He never loses too much at dice. Lets others win just enough to avoid suspicion.
- Subtly steers conversations toward Verbobonc defenses, Hommlet activity, adventurer movements.
- Dick Rentsch shows him *unusual deference* — Rentsch knows Lucius outranks him.
- His "gambling companions" are seasoned bandits doubling as guards.
- A coded courier slips messages to him.

**He receives party intel through Madame Selentis.** When he wants to meet the party, the introduction will run: Selentis sends a message via Skole (or another known Nulb contact) → party comes to the Old Mayor's Manor or another agreed location → Lucius is "the merchant Aldren who happens to be there."

**The Selentis-credentialed introduction is what makes Lucius safe to listen to.** Madame Selentis is a feared known quantity. If she vouches for "this gentleman has business proposals for capable operators," the party will at least show up. Once there, Lucius does the rest.

## The Scarlet Brotherhood Thread

Per canon: **Elvanna at the Iron Lash brothel** is a Scarlet Brotherhood agent. *"True Allegiance: The Scarlet Brotherhood seeded Elvanna in Nulb's brothel years ago."*

**The party has met her.** They paid her 500 gp to ransom Serena Velkori. They thought she was just a transactional brothel madam.

**Implications:**
- The Scarlet Brotherhood has been quietly observing Nulb operations for years
- Elvanna sees the party as "easy marks" but useful contacts (per her dossier)
- The Scarlet Brotherhood's depth and goals are unknown — separate operator from Iuz/Zuggtmoy/Lolth
- The party paying 500 gp without negotiation marked them as "naive but well-funded" in her assessment

**Don't surface this thread yet.** Save for a moment when the party needs another wrinkle, or when they're in Verbobonc/anywhere a Scarlet Brotherhood presence makes sense. The Iron Lash is a Scarlet Brotherhood listening post in Hedrack's Nulb. Both factions know the other is there. Neither acts because it would burn each other's cover.

Given the canon supply chain, the most likely coercer is **Selentis or Feldrin via local bandit pressure** — the Rhennee caravans are useful for moving Temple supplies disguised as legitimate Rhennee trade.

  - Smoke (crooked house / Waterside tavern / man with red sash) → **Lucius Graeme at the Waterside**

  - Tarokka (The Chain / The Betrayer / The Flooded Gate / crimson barge on the Velverdyva heavy with souls) → The Chain = the supply chain itself, The Betrayer = Hedrack, The Flooded Gate = the Water Temple, the crimson barge = Vesrek's Crimson Trader

The Rhennee may know more than they've said. Eryk's coercion is a thread the party can pull to investigate Selentis's network from a friendly angle.

## Operational Implications

### What's available to the party in Nulb right now

1. **Skole** — already an ally, can be pushed further. Knows Tolub's movements.
2. **Tolub** — assassinable, isolated since Alrrem's death. Ship schedule known. Free hit if the party wants it.
3. **Selentis** — feared but unmet. The next major Nulb encounter will go through her.
4. **Lucius Graeme via Selentis** — will introduce himself once Phase 3 cleanup is done. The credential is "Madame Selentis sent me."
5. **The Iron Lash / Elvanna** — known, transactional, unknown deeper. Scarlet Brotherhood thread.
6. **Eryk Velkori's coercion thread** — open investigation; likely leads to Selentis network.

### What's still hidden

- That Selentis is Hedrack's chief
- That Lucius runs Selentis
- That Wat is Fire Temple (party thinks Water)
- That Lodriss is the Boatman's true owner (party knows OF her but not her power)
- That Vesrek's slaver ship is also a Temple supply node
- That the Hunting Cottage / Gefreid Patris exists at all
- That Feldrin commands Temple logistics from the second dungeon level
- That Elvanna is Scarlet Brotherhood
- That Y'dey is alive in Nulb as Mother Screng (see `nulb_allies.md`)
- That Otis the smith is a Gnarley Rangers agent (see `nulb_allies.md`)

### Quest hooks for sessions 28+

- **Chains of Command:** dismantle the supply chain step by step (Boatman's → Manor → Wainwright → Hunting Cottage → Feldrin). Major arc.
- **The Will of the Earth quest is now MOOT** — the party has already moved past its activation criteria (Lareth killed openly per the canon's "ways the party failed"). Romag is dead anyway.
- **Wat's Proposal** is also moot — Alrrem is dead, Wat has no Fire Temple to recruit for. Wat may make a personal appeal to the party for safe passage / employment.
- **Ash and Saltwater** can fire: Wat vs Skole vs Lodriss vs Tolub tensions now play differently since Fire/Water principals are dead. Lodriss may consolidate; Skole may finally act.
- **The Eryk coercion thread** is the cleanest way to expose Selentis to the party from a sympathetic angle.
