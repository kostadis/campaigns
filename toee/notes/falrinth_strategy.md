# Falrinth and the Lolth Network — GM-Only

Authoritative for this campaign. The party doesn't know any of this. Lolth's network is one of the five factions in play (see `hedrack_strategy.md` for the full landscape). This doc covers Falrinth, Smigmal, the dead Lareth thread, and the Kriitch detail — everything that orbits Lolth's containment mission inside the Temple.

---

## Lolth's Actual Goal

Per canon: *"Lolth, the Demon Queen of Spiders, spins a web of deceit within its ranks. The Temple stands upon an ancient site sacred to the Elder Elemental God, and Lolth is determined to prevent its full resurgence. **Holding the key to the Elder Elemental God's prison, she is a crucial force in deciding its fate.**"*

Lolth's interest is **theological containment.** She literally holds the key to the EEG's prison. If the EEG is resurrected — or if Zuggtmoy (who has hijacked the EEG cult's worship) is freed with full powers — the Flanaess shifts in a way that disrupts every other demonic power's plans, including Lolth's. Her tactic per canon: *"Sowing discord through misinformation, eroding Iuz's grip on the Temple, and fueling internal rivalries."*

In this campaign, this rolls up into a broader Lolth strategic context (user homebrew layer): Lolth is preparing an **interplanar invasion of Oerth from the Demonweb Pits.** The Temple resurgence is *useful* to her as a local crisis that absorbs Verbobonc's attention and keeps the gaze of the Flanaess pointed at the Temple, not at her own invasion preparation elsewhere. The Orb's restoration would scale the threat up to coalition-response levels and risk pulling attention away from the diversion. Her preferred outcome: the Temple stays a contained, ugly local mess. The Orb stays gemless. Everyone keeps fighting each other over a thing that never activates.

**Lolth's view of the party:** complicated. They killed her agent Lareth in chapter 13 — an unforgivable strike against her Nexus operation. But they have also been the most effective force against the Temple's elemental factions, which serves her containment mission. **She'd prefer to control them rather than kill them**, if Falrinth can manage it.

## Falrinth — Chaotic Evil, Leashed

**Module canon (T1-4):** Falrinth is an 8th-level magic-user living in Area 337 on Dungeon Level 3 (south room, adjacent to area 338 the hidden study). Functions as 9th-level while Kriitch is within one mile. Power-mad ambitions: *"plans to use Smigmal as a tool to achieve control of the remaining Temple forces, so he can become ruler of a domain of death and destruction equalling, even surpassing, that of Iuz."* Kriitch is canonically *"a gift from the demoness Lolth, who is happy to have catspaws in Zuggtmoy's territory (for obvious reasons)."*

**Leadership supplement canon:** *"Though he outwardly serves Lolth... his true allegiance is to power itself."* / *"He schemes to advance his own goals, betraying even Lolth if it means seizing greater control."* / Secretly possesses the gemless Orb of Golden Death.

**Internal tension — the engine of his character.** Three forces pull on Falrinth in different directions:

1. **Lolth's mission:** keep the Orb gemless, prevent EEG/Zuggtmoy resurgence, sow discord.
2. **Personal ambition:** restore the Orb himself and use its power for his own ascension to demigod status — *"surpassing Iuz."*
3. **Pragmatic survival:** Hedrack is closing in on him. Senshock hunts him. Smigmal is his only true ally. He needs allies or he needs to escape.

The leash is currently holding because Lolth would obliterate him for restoring the Orb without her sanction. He chafes against it constantly. **Every interaction with the party is filtered through whether he can use them to slip the leash.**

### What Falrinth knows
- The Orb's true nature and power.
- The four Elemental Gems are needed; he has placed at least one (Earth Gem, per his journal) before Lolth pulled him back.
- Hedrack is hunting the Orb. Falrinth assumes standard Temple ambition — he does *not* know about the personal-godhood deal with Zuggtmoy.
- Senshock is also hunting the Orb (rival).
- The party has been working as Hedrack's Earth Temple agents. Now upper-temple managers. About to be sent against him.
- The party killed Lareth. He knows. Lolth knows.

### What Falrinth does NOT know
- Hedrack's actual Zuggtmoy ascension plan (the Layer 3 secret).
- That Lucius Graeme and Deggum are Tier 1 in the coup.
- The party's Hommlet/Verbobonc political web in detail.

### Falrinth's read of the party
- Capable. Ruthlessly so — they took down four elemental priests including Romag and Lareth.
- Operating under cover. Not committed to the Temple's ideology.
- Killers of one of his closest allies (Lareth).
- Possibly useful as agents who could disrupt Hedrack's Orb mission *without* understanding they were doing Lolth's work.
- **If they're already loyal Hedrack tools** when he contacts them, he's exposed and dead. So his first contact is a careful probe, not a recruitment pitch.

### Falrinth's first contact with the party (Phase 3 cleanup week)

Method: an illusory image of a hooded man appears in the party's quarters late at night. He addresses one PC by name — Zinnia is the right read (most perceptive). The image weighs them silently before speaking.

**Tone:** transactional, slightly oily, neither warm nor threatening. He is *evaluating* them, not befriending them.

**Pitch draft (paraphrase at table):**

> "Forgive the theatrics. I cannot be seen sending a runner, and you cannot afford to be seen receiving one. My name will mean nothing to you yet — soon enough, the man called Lucius Graeme will come to you with a request that involves me. Before you accept what he tells you, I would like you to consider a few small things.
>
> The Orb that the Temple is so concerned about — yes, I have it. I am not interested in giving it to Hedrack, to Senshock, or to any of the holy gentlemen who would like to see me dead. I am also not interested in giving it to you, but that is a separate conversation.
>
> What I *am* interested in is not having you turned against me by a flattering man with golden eyes. The trinket your cleric carries — the scarab with the four letters — you should be more careful with it. Lucius will recognize it. Senshock will recognize it. Both of them will use it against you in different ways. That is the kind of thing I notice and they have not. Yet.
>
> Lareth was a friend. I am told you handled him with discretion, which I appreciate more than you might expect. But you should know that there are other friends, and not all of us would extend the same patience. So when Lucius comes to you, do not commit. Stall. Find your way back to this room, and signal — a candle in the west window will reach me — and we will continue the conversation.
>
> Or do not. Report me to Barkinar; he will believe you. I will simply not be where you tell him I am. *But you will have made your choice."*

The image fades.

**Key things this scene delivers:**
- The TZGY scarab is identified as a known dangerous artifact (closes that thread for the party — see `tzgy_scarab_urgent.md`)
- A name dropped: Lucius Graeme, with golden eyes, who is coming for them
- An offer of contact channel (candle in the window)
- An implicit acknowledgment that the party killed Lareth — and Falrinth chose to *appreciate it* rather than avenge it, which is a startling tell
- A *test*: do they report him to Barkinar, or do they wait?

**What the party should NOT get from this contact:**
- Hedrack's coup is not mentioned — Falrinth doesn't know.
- "Senshock is not your enemy" is not the line — Falrinth doesn't actually know Iuz politics. He knows Senshock is *also* a player and that the party shouldn't be set against him for Lucius's benefit.
- No alliance offer. Just a probe.

### What Falrinth wants from the party (if they pull the thread)

Working from least to most: information, neutrality, alliance.

- **Information first.** He wants to know what Lucius asks them to do. The party becomes his eyes inside Hedrack's network without realizing they're feeding Lolth.
- **Neutrality second.** If they refuse to do Lucius's bidding on the Orb mission, that itself serves Lolth — the Orb stays gemless.
- **Alliance third.** If they prove trustworthy and effective, he is willing to hand them the gemless Orb on the condition they help him destroy it (the Lolth-canonical path) or, if his personal ambition wins out, on the condition they help him restore it on *his* terms instead of Hedrack's. The latter would mean betraying Lolth — Falrinth would do it; would the party?

### How combat goes if Falrinth is cornered

Use his module canon tactics: Kriitch in giant centipede form scouts from a hidden aperture. Falrinth pre-casts Minor Globe of Invulnerability + Mirror Image, then leads with Hold Person. Backup: darkness, invisibility, dimension door to either the corridor east of area 313 or the secret exit to the Tower/outside. If Kriitch dies, Falrinth drops to 4th level and flees immediately.

**He does not fight to the death** in his lair. If he loses Kriitch, he runs. If captured alive, he negotiates — he has the Orb's location as his ace.

If killed, his journal (per the "Falrinth's Journal" canon quest) becomes a misleading artifact: it documents him pursuing Zuggtmoy's liberation via Orb restoration, *which is false in this campaign* — that journal was either a cover story for other Lolth-skeptical Temple agents who might find it, or it documents his personal ambition before Lolth's leash pulled him back. The party finding it will misread him.

## Smigmal Redhand — Falrinth's Partner

**Module canon:** Half-orc Fighter/Assassin. Lives in Area 336 (the north room adjacent to Falrinth's). *"She is the leman of Falrinth"* — i.e., his lover. Sees him as her tool toward her own power. Will fight to protect him *if such action appears worthwhile,* but will not die for him. Wears leather armor +1, shield +2, +1 shortsword (or a non-glowing normal shortsword for assassinations). Sword is poison-coated. Carries a *ring of spell storing* (5 invisibility spells). Has a hidden compartment chest with 30 gems and jewelry.

**Leadership supplement canon:** Six feet tall, lean, piercing yellow eyes betraying orc blood. Uses a **Hat of Disguise** to pass as an elven noblewoman. Currently masquerading as **"Lady Eryndel"** infiltrating Hommlet (and possibly Verbobonc, Gnarley Forest). Brings intelligence to Falrinth via Kriitch. Loyalty is primarily to Falrinth, secondarily to her own power. Tense respect with Hedrack (who tolerates her).

### Smigmal's operational role
- Commander of the outer defenses (the ruined Tower above the Temple) — leads brigands, mercenaries, raiding parties.
- Surface intelligence: she's in Hommlet *now*, posing as a noblewoman, gathering intel on the party and others.
- Assassination capability: poisoned blade, magical shortsword, ring of invisibility.
- Lolth-network surface arm (Falrinth being the lair-bound mage).

### What Smigmal might do during the campaign
- **The party encounters "Lady Eryndel" socially in Hommlet** before they realize what she is. She may have already brushed past them at the Welcome Wench or at a faction meeting.
- **Smigmal-the-assassin attempts on the party**, depending on whether Falrinth concludes they are a threat rather than a tool. Each attempt leaves a cryptic warning (per the "Smigmal — Assassin's Mark" canon quest pattern).
- **A Falrinth-Smigmal split** is canonically possible. If Falrinth betrays her (per "Broken Trust" sub-quest), she becomes a wounded, vengeful party-side asset.

Don't put her in early combat with the party. Hold her for a reveal moment — *"Lady Eryndel" steps out of the smoke and her face is half-elf, half-orc."*

## Kriitch — The Quasit Familiar

Falrinth's familiar, gifted by Lolth. Per canon: while Kriitch is within 10 ft of Falrinth, each has 25% Magic Resistance and regenerates 1 hp/round. Beyond that, Kriitch's senses are telepathically available to Falrinth at up to 1 mile range, so Falrinth functions as 9th-level while Kriitch is in his region. Kriitch usually scouts in **giant centipede form** through a small aperture in the wall of Area 337.

**Kriitch is Lolth's eyes on Falrinth.** This is the canonical leash. If Falrinth tried to betray Lolth, Kriitch would know — and Lolth would know within hours. Falrinth is fully aware of this.

**Tactical implication for the party:** if they need to negotiate with Falrinth in a way that *Lolth* shouldn't see, Kriitch has to be neutralized or distanced first. This is hard. It also creates an opening — if the party offers Falrinth a path to *escape Lolth's leash*, Kriitch is the obstacle they could help him remove. That is the moment Falrinth might genuinely defect.

## Lareth the Beautiful — The Killed Lolth Agent

Per Leadership canon: *"Lareth the Beautiful (Moathouse Leader): Hedrack sees Lareth as a valuable but dangerously ambitious servant of the Elder Elemental God. While he supports Lareth's efforts at the Moathouse, he remains cautious of his growing influence, **unaware that Lareth is, in truth, an agent of Lolth.**"*

And: *"Falrinth and Lareth: A Dangerous Partnership. Both serve Lolth, working to manipulate the elemental nodes, possibly undermining Zuggtmoy and the Elder Elemental God. Their shared mission, as dictated by Lolth, is to gain control of the elemental nodes (Nexus), though the full scope of their plan remains hidden."*

**The party killed Lareth in chapter 13.** They thought they were eliminating the new Fire Temple commander of the Moathouse. They were actually executing one of Lolth's two senior Nexus operatives. The other (Falrinth) is now alone in his Lolth-side work, holding the gemless Orb without his partner.

**This is why Falrinth's first contact is so cautious.** The party are murderers of his ally. He is choosing to approach them anyway because they are also the most effective anti-Temple force in the region. The math works for him — but barely.

**A Falrinth-Lareth-related reveal could land later.** If the party finds Lareth's personal effects, his journals, or his correspondence with Falrinth (preserved at the Moathouse or in the Lolth network's safe-houses), they may realize they killed the wrong target. Hedrack's cover for them on Lareth's death actually serves them well here — nobody knows the party did it except Thorne.

## Implications for Play

### How Falrinth integrates into the Hedrack arcs

- **Arc A (Senshock).** Falrinth has no direct stake but benefits from chaos. If he's in contact with the party, he may feed them real information about Senshock that *complicates* the Lucius pitch — "Yes, Senshock is dangerous, but he's not who Lucius says he is. He hunts the Orb for himself, not for Iuz."
- **Arc B1 (Find the Orb).** This is the head-on collision. Lucius tells the party to find and recover the Orb from Falrinth. Falrinth's already established contact (if he made it) is the party's leverage to *not* simply kill him. The negotiation scene with Falrinth is the centerpiece of Arc B1.
- **Arc B2 (Recover the Gems).** Falrinth has already been in the Nodes (per his canon journal). He has scouting intel. If allied, he provides it. If not, the Nodes are harder.
- **Arc B3 (Restore and Deliver).** Falrinth would object violently to actually fusing the Gems into the Orb. This is the point where Lolth's interest screams loudest. If the party has heard from Falrinth at all, they will know that fusing the gems is the bad outcome. That doubt may be the trigger for the Layer 3 realization about Hedrack.
- **Arc C (False Final Battle).** If Falrinth survives and is in any kind of alliance, he is a wizard-tier asset for the storm. If he's dead, his Lolth replacement (if any) may surface.

### How the party should feel about Falrinth

He is **not** the cool helpful ally. He is **not** a redemption arc. He is a chaotic evil opportunist working for a Demon Queen who happens to have an interest aligned with the party's actual goal. They should always feel like they're handling a snake — useful, but it could bite.

For 15-year-old players: the texture should be *"this guy is gross but he's right about Lucius."* The fun is in working with someone obviously evil because the alternative is worse. That's a fantasy they will eat.

### When to introduce Lolth as a named force

Not yet. Falrinth's first contact does NOT mention Lolth. The Lolth thread should surface gradually:
- First indirect (Falrinth's behavior, Kriitch, the Lareth connection if they discover it)
- Then named (a captured Lolth-aligned agent confesses, or the party identifies a drow sigil somewhere)
- Then directly threatening (Lolth herself takes interest, sends agents, or Falrinth confesses his leash openly)

The Demonweb invasion / Verbobonc-distraction layer should remain GM-only until the party has dealt with the Hedrack arc. Lolth is a future-campaign hook, not a current one.
