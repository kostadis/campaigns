# Area 212 — The Temple of Fire (canonical reference)

Authoritative module description for the Fire Temple chamber. Use this for handouts, image
prompts, and read-aloud. Verbatim read-boxed text below; do not paraphrase it into "facts"
without checking against the source.

## Read-boxed description (verbatim)

> There can be no doubt that this huge hall of red granite, lit by scores of flambeaux and
> pervaded by the odor of heated metal and sulfur, is the Fire Temple. The great doors of
> beaten brass reflect the leaping flames. Tapestries adorn the walls of the north (wider)
> section, depicting scenes of fire in evil form. Despite the many torches and flaming
> cressets, the air seems to carry motes of rust-colored luminescence, and the streaks of
> blue and mica flakes in the polished granite walls appear to leap and dance as if flames,
> caught within the rock.
>
> A brass tube, 20' long and 2' in diameter, is suspended from the ceiling by chains of the
> same metal. Just to the north of it is a shallow fire pit; and to either side of the tube
> are low stands of brass, upon which are many small blocks of charcoal. To either hand, east
> and west beyond lines of pillars, are great copper cauldrons, flanking the main area of the
> Temple. Before each such vessel is an octagonal brass table; on each table are 16 bowls and
> a copper dipper.

## Key features at a glance

- **Material:** huge hall of polished **red granite**, streaked with blue and mica flakes that
  appear to dance like flames trapped in the stone.
- **Light/air:** scores of flambeaux + flaming cressets; **rust-colored motes** drifting in the
  air; odor of **heated metal and sulfur**.
- **Doors:** great **beaten-brass** doors, reflecting the flames.
- **Tapestries:** on the **north (wider) wall**, depicting **fire in evil form**.
- **Central fixture:** a **brass tube, 20' long × 2' diameter**, suspended horizontally from the
  ceiling on **brass chains**.
- **Fire pit:** **shallow**, just **north** of the brass tube. (This is the pit tied to the
  salamander grow-in / sacrifice mechanic — see statblocks.)
- **Charcoal stands:** low **brass** stands to either side of the tube, holding many small
  blocks of **charcoal**.
- **Cauldrons:** great **copper cauldrons** east and west, **beyond lines of pillars**; each
  fronted by an **octagonal brass table** holding **16 bowls and a copper dipper**.

> interior, lit by scores of flaming torches and brass cressets. The air glows faintly with
> drifting rust-colored motes of luminescence, thick with the haze of heated metal and sulfur.
> The polished red granite walls are shot through with streaks of blue and glittering flecks of
> mica that seem to flicker and dance like flames caught and frozen inside the stone. At the far
> end, great doors of beaten brass reflect the leaping firelight. The wider north wall is hung
> with woven tapestries depicting fire in evil, demonic forms.
>
> Dominating the center of the hall, a long horizontal brass tube, twenty feet long and two feet
> across, hangs suspended from the high ceiling on heavy brass chains. Just north of it lies a
> shallow fire pit glowing with low flame. To either side of the tube stand low brass racks
> holding many small black blocks of charcoal. Running east and west, two lines of tall stone
> pillars divide the hall; beyond them, flanking the main floor, sit great copper cauldrons, and
> before each cauldron an octagonal brass table set with rows of small bowls and a copper dipper.
>
> Oppressive, sacred, and menacing atmosphere of an evil fire cult. Cinematic wide-angle
> composition, dramatic low-key lighting dominated by torch and firelight, deep shadows, a rich
> red-orange-and-brass color palette with cold blue glints in the stone, floating embers and
> heat shimmer, volumetric light, painterly high-detail fantasy concept art. 16:9.

**Prompt notes:** the suspended brass tube is the detail most likely to render wrong — if it
does, give it its own opening sentence + "like a great brass pipe hanging horizontally
overhead." The blue/mica-in-red-granite effect is what makes this room *this* room; keep it.
Trim the cauldrons/tables first if length is a problem.
