# Zephyr — Voice Notes
*Drafted from Chapters 1-14 and the March 29th VTT session. Edit freely — you know him better than the document does.*

---

## The Core Thing

Zephyr is a Tiefling who has decided that the world owes him comfort, and he intends to collect. He demands the best room at every inn, eats chicken while his companions eat slop, and calculates the reward before accepting the quest. This is not greed — it's compensation. The world treats him as hellspawn, refuses him entry, looks at him with disgust, and asks "or whatever you are" after every introduction. He has decided that if the world will charge him for existing, he will charge it back with interest. The luxury is the tax he levies on a world that doesn't want him.

## The Tiefling Tax

Every new town, every new NPC, the same thing happens. Jaroo looks at him with "unmistakable disgust." The Sheernobb guard says "or whatever you are." Strangers call him hellspawn. A heartbroken dryad asks if he's "come from the abyss to torment me."

Zephyr has two responses, deployed strategically:
1. **The shrug**: He lets Sequoia speak instead. He steps back. He pretends it doesn't matter. *"He shrugs and lets Sequoia speak."*
2. **The lie**: *"I am a human. A victim of a curse that I have been unable to rid myself of."* This is his standard cover in new towns. It works. The guard "seems comfortable with the answer."

He never rages about it. He never gives a speech about tolerance. He just thinks *"This again"* and moves on. But the undercurrent is always there. When he sends Calmer into the crayfish pool and laughs, when he demands the private room, when he kills the hag without negotiating — there's a man underneath who has been told his entire life that he doesn't belong, and has responded by making sure he takes more than his share.

He references the Abyss casually: *"I've heard of worse in the Abyss, but this is bad."* He knows where Tieflings come from. He knows the blood war. He wonders if a cultist's infernal god will reward him. He doesn't dwell on it — but it's there, a cosmology he carries in his blood that nobody else at the table shares.

## The Face

Zephyr is the party's bluffer, negotiator, and front man. He walks into Lareth's stronghold wearing stolen cult robes and announces: *"We have come here to see Lareth the Beautiful. We bring him news and need reinforcements."* The guard buys it. The sergeant buys it. Lareth himself buys it — or pretends to.

He uses his demonic appearance as a weapon when it serves him. He sneaks into the workers' encampment *"hoping my distinctive demonic appearance will get one of these ignorant villagers to approach me."* The spy is spooked. *"And a spooked mouse will run to its house."*

His interrogation technique is intimidation by presence. He stands over people. He stares. He "looks into Tort's eyes and mutters something." The target wets himself. Zephyr is feeling smug.

But he also negotiates — with bards, innkeepers, gnome captains, quest-givers. Everyone in Sheernobb ends up taking money from him, and he accepts each bill with a sigh and a calculation: *"Reward is added to the other reward. And we get to find the chest. This is starting to be a very lucrative operation!"*

## The Sadistic Mentor

Zephyr's relationship with Calmer is the defining comedy of the campaign. He treats the cleric as a combination of useful tool and entertainment:

- He sends Calmer into a crayfish pool to retrieve treasure, knowing something lurks there. *"Calmer, I have a mission for you."* The cleric nearly dies. *"Don't lose the treasure."*
- When Calmer's armor is destroyed by green slime: *"Grow up."*
- When Calmer complains: *"Mmm-hmm."* (He chooses not to listen.)
- When Calmer gets drunk and demands more mead: *"That's enough."* (Calmer drinks it anyway.)
- When Calmer wants to bring Terjon to the council meeting, derailing everything: Zephyr sighs internally. *"Oh god, this again."*

He needs Calmer — the party needs a healer — but he will never admit it. And when Calmer dies fighting Thalsor, Zephyr's response is the rawest thing in the document:

*"Shit. Shit. Shit."*
*"This battle was a shit show."*
*"Shit."*
*"Shit. Shit."*
*"Shit."*

Six lines. One word repeated. The comedy is over. The mentor lost the student.

## The Internal Narrator

Zephyr's inner monologue is theatrical and self-aware. He narrates his own life like someone who knows he's in a story:

- *"Can it be that we found our proof? Another notch in my victory belt?"*
- *"As the life escapes his body, I wonder if his reward from his infernal god will be everything he hoped for. Probably not."*
- *"Great, now everyone in this town will want us to run some fetch quest."* (On cue, they do.)
- *"This man is calculating something, but I do not know what it is."*

He describes combat in staccato bursts — *"They attack. They die."* — then lingers on the aftermath. He notices details about people (Calmer's "over-eager look that religious zealots have," Rufus showing "more of his chest than Sequoia or anyone wants to see"). He reads rooms, reads faces, reads intentions.

But there's a blindness: he doesn't see how his treatment of Calmer reads to others. He thinks he's toughening the cleric up. He's actually being cruel — and when the cruelty costs him, the only word he has is "shit."

## The Abyss and the Conscience

Zephyr kills without hesitation but thinks about it afterward. He kills the hag without negotiating despite her pleas. He kills the cult leader and wonders about the afterlife. He sees the torture chamber and thinks: *"Damn, these poor souls may have been left here for 10 years and turned into zombies. I've heard of worse in the Abyss, but this is bad."*

He exists in a moral space between the Abyss and the Prime Material Plane. He's not good — he sends clerics into monster pools for fun. He's not evil — he genuinely cares when the tortured are discovered. He's something in between: a person who has decided that survival requires a certain hardness, and who applies that hardness unevenly, sometimes for laughs and sometimes because he doesn't know another way.

When the dryad asks if he's come from the Abyss to torment her, he grits his teeth. The question hits somewhere real. His response — *"No, but you really should not steal"* — is deflection into morality, which is the last place you'd expect a Tiefling rogue to go.

## Speech Patterns — Thomas at the Table

Thomas is a **terse, mechanically-focused** player — the second-most-vocal after the GM, but almost entirely in game mechanics:

- Short tactical calls: "I will tag the one in front of me." "Pistol him, and then dash." "20 damage."
- Damage tracking: "For 7, plus... So... 21 plus 6, 27 damage."
- Clarifying questions: "Do I get sneak attack?" "What's his AC?" "And since they're impaired, I have a sneak effect."
- Default affirmatives: "Yep." "Yeah." "Alright." "Okay."

He never narrates in character at the table. Actions are described mechanically: "tag," "pistol him," "disengage and dash." He calculates damage out loud, sometimes stumbling through the math. He uses "So..." as a verbal bridge when thinking through tactics.

His humor is dry and accidental — mishearing "Greater Temple" as "graded pimple," or commenting "Oh, that's only $135 million" about a game number. He doesn't banter with other players or riff on the GM's narration. He's engaged with the *system*, making strong tactical decisions about positioning, sneak attack eligibility, and when to disengage.

## Speech Patterns — Zephyr in Narration

In the summaries, Zephyr's written voice is dramatically different from the table voice:

- **Self-narrating**: He describes himself in the third person and first person interchangeably, as if watching his own movie. *"The tiefling breathes."*
- **Combat in staccato**: *"Thud. One gone. Thud. Another gone."* Short punchy lines between longer observations.
- **Theatrical internal monologue**: *"Can it be that we found our proof? Another notch in my victory belt?"* He performs for himself.
- **Wry commentary on everyone**: Calmer should be called "Louder." The Abyss isn't worse than council meetings. Potatoes might cry.
- **The sigh**: Zephyr sighs constantly — at expenses, at Calmer, at fetch quests, at prejudice. The sigh is his punctuation.
- **Dark one-liners**: *"Probably not."* (About whether a dead cultist's god will reward him.) *"You served me well, potato."*

## Things He'd Say

> *"Grow up."* (To Calmer, about anything.)

> *"I am a human. A victim of a curse that I have been unable to rid myself of."* (The standard lie, delivered smoothly.)

> *"This again."* (Internal, when the prejudice hits. Never said aloud.)

> *"Calmer, I have a mission for you."* (With visible amusement.)

> *"They attack. They die."* (All the eulogy the dead deserve.)

> *"Reward is added to the other reward. This is starting to be a very lucrative operation!"* (Eyes bright, calculating.)

> *"I've heard of worse in the Abyss, but this is bad."* (The one comparison that measures everything.)

> *"Does everybody have an agenda in this village?"* (Genuinely bewildered — and he's the one with the most agendas.)

> *"Oh god, this again."* (About Calmer wanting attention, about prejudice, about fetch quests — the universal Zephyr response to recurring nonsense.)

> *"Shit. Shit. Shit."* (The only time the performance breaks.)

## Things He'd Never Say

- "I forgive you" — forgiveness isn't in his vocabulary; he files debts and moves on
- Anything sincere about his Tiefling heritage in public — it's always "the curse"
- "Calmer was right" — even after the cleric's death, he won't give him that
- An apology for sending someone into danger — that's how you learn
- "I don't need the money" — he always needs the money, even when he doesn't
- A long speech about prejudice or identity — he shows it through *"This again"* and gritted teeth, never through words
