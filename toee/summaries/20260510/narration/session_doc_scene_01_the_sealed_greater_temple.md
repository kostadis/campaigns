## Zephyr — The Sealed Greater Temple

The goblins were the first thing we saw when we pushed into the Air Temple.

Moving fast toward Kelno's territory, arms piled with everything they'd stripped from the dead. Equipment, robes, the kind of things you take when you're not sure there will be another opportunity. They kept their eyes down. We kept ours forward. Nobody said anything.

*This again.* The war I'd helped arrange, moving around me in looting parties.

The Water Temple door had burn marks on it — the Fire Temple had tried to force an entry and failed, or stopped, or been stopped. Hard to say which. The bodies along the passage didn't clarify matters. Looted already, by the time we got there. Everything useful gone.

The wraith was at the entrance to the Greater Temple when we arrived. Feasting. Not hunting — feasting, which is a distinction that mattered to my continued good health. He looked up from whatever unfortunate corpse had his attention and called out with the warmth of something that finds everything amusing.

"Your turn will come! Don't worry, my little pets! Your turn will come."

The last time I'd stood this close to him, I'd come away with something missing — not a wound exactly, more like a portion of myself that the cold reached into and found worth keeping. That feeling has not entirely left.

Sequoia said, "Lucky me."

I agreed with the sentiment.

Calmer, naturally, saw an opportunity.

"I can kill him! I can kill him, I can so totally kill him!"

"It's okay," I said.

I meant: *the math does not work.* Calmer apparently heard the first word and not the rest, because he subsided, still looking at the wraith with that hungry ecclesiastical expression he gets when he's found something worth smiting. I appreciate the energy. I have learned not to rely on it as a tactical plan.

We pressed on toward the Greater Temple entrance. I had, in point of fact, never been through.

"Have you gone through the Greater Temple?" I asked.

No one had.

"Oh, we could go there."

Someone agreed. We moved.

The door didn't cooperate. Something held it from the other side — braced, or barricaded, or committed to not opening. "Or dash through," I said. Calmer began hammering at it instead, which achieved the same noise with considerably less tactical elegance, and the wraith found this very funny.

I turned back to face him.

The wraith had been here the whole time. Watching everything we'd done in this temple — the dead we'd made, the war we'd triggered, the bodies lining every passage we'd walked through. Information was information, and the wraith was currently the only entity in the room willing to talk.

"And Gary Race," I said, "can you tell us what's happening?"

He looked at me with the amusement of something that has eaten recently and is deciding whether to eat again.

"I could tell you," he said, "or I could kill you—"

He paused. His attention drifted to the bodies around him — the ones we'd arranged, directly or otherwise.

"But you provided me so much tasty food! Food!"

Then: "I want to thank you about something."

"Yes," I said.

I let the calculation run. The wraith was grateful. Wraiths, as a rule, are not grateful — they are hungry and patient and eventually inevitable — but this one was grateful, and that was leverage. I could feel the conversation tilting, the wraith no longer performing menace but something closer to collegial warmth. Peer to peer. Which meant we were not, at this precise moment, each other's prey.

"With all the bodies you've created," he announced, with the satisfaction of a man presenting a ledger that has resolved favorably, "I now have minions."

The specters materialized one by one. He counted them out. One, two, three. Four, five. Mostly Earth Temple forces — I recognized the remnants of robes, the insignia, the general silhouette of people we had, in one way or another, contributed to killing. Six, seven.

Seven specters. Raised from bodies we were responsible for.

The ledger does not forgive debts. It only moves them around.

"Why don't you go fight the other temples?" I said.

He did not answer. He was busy being pleased with himself.

Then the voice.

Cold. From behind the door Calmer had been hammering. Everything went still.

"The Greater Temple is sealed until the battle is over. No one gets in, no one gets out."

The wraith laughed harder.

"Where are you?" I asked the door.

The wraith answered instead.

"You see, what you don't understand, you poor little fools, you poor little delightful little fools — this happens every so often, and when it does, the Greater Temple seals the lesser temples and waits for the battle to end. You're going to be trapped in here like the rest of us, and maybe you'll be like one of my servants."

He said "my servants" with obvious satisfaction. He was counting on it.

I let the geometry of it settle. We were inside. The door was sealed. The wraith had seven specters and a month's supply of Earth Temple dead to expand the collection. The voice on the other side had decided the war was not yet over.

"Who is… what is the Greater Temple?" I asked.

"The Greater Temple," the wraith said, with the tone of something explaining things it finds obvious, "is the deep temple. It's the people who are really in charge."

The people who are really in charge.

I had spent weeks operating under the assumption that Romag was in charge. Then Hartsch. Then, briefly, us. The ledger kept updating.

"I'd like to meet them," I said.

The voice from behind the door answered.

"Survive the fight, and maybe you'll get a chance."

Terms. Don't die in the war we started, and there would be an introduction. I've worked with worse opening offers.

I looked at the seven specters. I looked at the wraith. I looked at the door.

"Why don't we camp out next door?" I said.

Nobody moved toward camping.

The seven specters were, as it turned out, a relevant consideration. The wraith had them and was not going to stop having them. The door was sealed. We were, by the wraith's own enthusiastic assessment, excellent raw material for future servants.

"Alright," I said. "We'll get out and come back later."

The wraith shouted after us as we moved.

"I can't wait till you come back here! I will be waiting!"

He meant it warmly. The absolute confidence of something that has already decided the outcome and is merely permitting time to catch up.

The tiefling kept walking.

*Unresolved. Return when the ledger is more favorable.*
