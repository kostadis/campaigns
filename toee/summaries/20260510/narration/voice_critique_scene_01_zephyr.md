## Flags

### [1] Voice spec drift — internal monologue word choice

> I meant: *the accounting runs against us.*

**Why:** The examples file carries the same beat with the line "*the math does not work.*" "The math" is more Zephyr — direct, punchy, shortest word that works. "The accounting runs against us" pre-signals the ledger metaphor that arrives two paragraphs later ("The ledger does not forgive debts. It only moves them around."), which weakens both appearances. Zephyr's internal voice says the shortest true thing, then lingers on the aftermath.
**Suggested rewrite:** `I meant: *the math does not work.*`

### [2] Mild convergence — ledger metaphor density in the sign-off

> *Unresolved. Return when the ledger is more favorable.*

**Why:** The scene earns the ledger frame ("The ledger does not forgive debts. It only moves them around.") but the closing sign-off runs it a third time with more words. Zephyr's terse sign-offs elsewhere go shortest: "They attack. They die." / "*Shit.*" "When the ledger is more favorable" is four words where his voice usually stops at two.
**Suggested rewrite:** `*Unresolved. Return with a better ledger.*` — or simply `*Unresolved.*`

Strong section; the wraith negotiation is essentially the reference version of this voice (it appears in political_maneuvering.md nearly verbatim). Two subtle flags: a single word swap on the internal monologue and tightening the sign-off. Spot-edit territory, not a re-run.
