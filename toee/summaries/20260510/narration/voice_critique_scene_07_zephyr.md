## Flags

### [1] Generic prose — stock simile

> Sequoia said, flat as a slab

**Why:** "Flat as a slab" is a stock simile for deadpan delivery and adds nothing to Zephyr's economic register. The sentence already earns the observation: "I will admit the line was clean enough that I let it stand" is doing the real work. The simile between "said" and the observation is generic filler that Zephyr's voice — which values precision and compression — would cut.
**Suggested rewrite:** `Sequoia said — no setup, no emphasis — and I will admit the line was clean enough that I let it stand.`

### [2] Voice spec conflict — wrong pronoun for Zinnia (two occurrences)

> We needed her for the kind of thing waiting in there.

> "We'll have to go without her."

**Why:** The Zinnia voice spec uses "he/him" throughout. Same error appears in scene 05 (Calmer's section: "she flowed into another"). This is a factual consistency error that needs to be corrected in both scenes before assembly — not a judgment call, and both fixes should land in the same edit pass so the pronoun is consistent across the document.
**Suggested rewrite:** `We needed him` / `go without him`

## Verdict

The Tiefling Tax sequence in this scene is Zephyr at his best in the document: "*This again.*" / "Different mouths, same bill." / "Come and get it." / "That's the going rate when you let me set the terms." / "The gnoll didn't so much die as stop." All land cleanly. Two fixes: the stock simile and the Zinnia pronoun. No re-run.
