## Flags

### [1] Lucky-strike framing undermines front-liner confidence

> The first cut found exactly where it needed to go — clean, the way it goes maybe once in a long while, when the body knows the work before the mind catches up.

**Why:** The updated spec's central addition is The Strike: Sequoia goes in first, hunts the clean window (Vex → Advantage → Sneak Attack), and expects to hit clean when he's set up correctly. "The way it goes maybe once in a long while" frames what is actually confident execution as a rare lucky moment. He caught a target who never clocked him coming — that's not luck, that's position. The hedge is closer to Calmer's surprised-it-worked register than Sequoia's front-liner identity. When the setup is right, he knows the hit is coming.
**Suggested rewrite:** `The first cut found exactly where it needed to go. Clean. He never even turned.`

## Verdict

The scene is the strongest Sequoia prose in the document: "He dies." / "One does not simply take bribes to defect." / "*That's odd. That's not good.*" are all placed correctly and in exactly the right register. One flag: the kill-beat hedges what should be matter-of-fact confidence. Single sentence edit.
