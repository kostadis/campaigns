## Flags

### [1] Self-aggrandizing claim

> The difference is I'm right when I do it, and I clean up after myself, and I don't get good men killed to feel like I'd done something.

**Why:** The spec lists "Anything self-aggrandizing — he leads by doing, not by claiming to lead" under Things He'd Never Say. This sentence claims general rightness directly, and immediately contradicts the line two sentences earlier in the same narration: "Being half-right is still wrong." His "No Illusions" section is about honesty, not about positioning himself above others. He recognized himself in Hartsch — that recognition is the beat. The claim of superiority cancels it.
**Suggested rewrite:** Cut. The recognition stands without the justification: `The difference I tell myself is the consequence. I don't know if it's true.` Or cut entirely and let the recognition sentence close the paragraph.

### [2] Pronoun error for Zinnia

> I watched her face go still the way it does when the lie underneath finishes coming up.

**Why:** Zinnia is he/him. Factual error in Sequoia's POV that will confuse readers. Not a voice issue but it needs fixing before finalization.
**Suggested rewrite:** `I watched his face go still the way it does when the lie underneath finishes coming up.`

## Verdict

The Hartsch self-recognition passage is the best prose in this scene — "*I couldn't wait* is a sentence I have lived inside of" is clean, personal, and exactly right for a character who has done the same thing. Two spot edits: cut the self-aggrandizing claim that follows it, fix the Zinnia pronoun. No re-run needed.
