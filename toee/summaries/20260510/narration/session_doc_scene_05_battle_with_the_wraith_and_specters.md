I had laid the ground before I crossed the threshold. The Spiritual Weapon hung at my shoulder, a thing of light and judgment. And the chamber itself — I filled it. Spirit Guardians, golden, the radiance of a power I have not said aloud in this place in longer than I want to count, pooling across the brown marble and its veins of black, waiting for something dead to step into it. To any wretched, hollow thing that should not exist in the world, that aura is fire. To me it is the warmest thing I have felt down here.

So I charged in. Boldly. I want that recorded.

And the Wraith — the one that had laughed at me twice, that had stood between us and the way down for too long — turned its empty face and said, almost delighted, "Oh, you're back!"

"Uh, yeah," Zephyr said.

Then the room moved.

Sequoia hit a specter and it came apart — gone before it understood it was dying. Zephyr stepped into a second one, his blade finding the seam before the thing had so much as twitched, and it unraveled. He drifted to a third and clipped it, a glancing thing, nothing fatal, but the count was already dropping in our favor and I let myself believe, for one breath, that this would be clean.

It was not going to be clean.

The Wraith flew. It crossed into my golden field and the radiance bit into it — I felt the spell strain and pull, saw it stagger and slow in the light. Good. *Good.* And then it reached me anyway.

I have been hurt before. This was not that. This was cold pouring in where warmth should be — not a wound, an absence, something reaching past the plate and the prayers and taking. Not heat applied. Heat *removed.* I felt the floor of myself drop out and knew, the way you know a true thing instantly, that I was at the very bottom of what a man can hold and still stand. And worse — I knew this particular cold does not mend. No light I carry would fill that back in. I was going to walk the rest of this with the door open.

Something else came after the cold, some second clawing thing trying to follow the first into me. And it didn't. I held. Clean, complete, the kind of holding that surprises even the one doing it — I shut the door in its face and it did not get through.

The specters swarmed then, three of them grasping at me, and missed, every one, and paid the toll for being in my light — the gold searing them as they reached. One lost its nerve and tried to flee the aura. *No.* I caught it on the way out, my reaction, the old draconic gift flaring in my chest — a strength it could not meet, a fist of force that flung it back broken into the radiance it was running from. Zinnia finished it before it could try again.

I was on three hit points. I knew it. I want that recorded too, because of what I did next.

I did not heal. I did not run. I am a warrior of St. Cuthbert and we do not run from the dead — and besides, there was nothing left in me that could undo what the Wraith had taken. So I reached for the thing I had reached for twice before, in two other rooms, on two other nights, and had it die in my hands both times. The creature had *laughed.* Turn Undead. The third time.

I called it. The light went out of me in a single blast of judgment, and the room of dead things flinched as one — and they could not hold. The Wraith could not hold. Specter after specter could not hold. They came apart in the radiance, all of them but one, scattered like the lie they were.

The Wraith was dead. The thing that had mocked me. *Dead.*

I could not contain it. I did not try.

"It worked! It worked!" The words came out of me without permission, without dignity, four sessions of it failing in my hands and now this. "Who's the man? Who is the man?"

"That's you," Zephyr said.

Zinnia put down the last surviving specter — missed the first strike, took it on the second, and when it still clung to four points of stubbornness she flowed into another and it was simply over.

The passage was clear. No more cold thing standing guard over the way down, no more creature that murders your friends and stands them back up as itself. Two roads to the second level now, and one of them open. A real victory — the strategic kind, the kind Hartsch's war wanted.

I stood in the cooling gold with the door still open in my chest, unable to be healed past the bottom of myself, and I was, against all sense, grinning.
