## ISSUE 3: Kelno Identified as "Air Temple" Figure — Contradicts Water Temple Attribution
**Location:** Summary, NPCs (Kelno), Locations (Water Temple), Scenes (The Defector's End)
**Issue:** The recap is internally contradictory about Kelno. The Summary calls him "Kelno of the Water Temple," the Locations section (Water Temple) describes him as "a figure from the Water Temple," and the NPC entry says he is "from the Air Temple." The Scenes section correctly notes the bugbear named Kelno but the coins were Air Temple — meaning the bugbear's claim about Kelno may be unreliable or Kelno is being used as cover. The NPC section's flat assertion that Kelno is "from the Air Temple" goes further than the evidence supports and contradicts the Water Temple references elsewhere.
**Evidence:** Party Reference Document does not mention Kelno at all, so no external grounding exists. The in-session evidence is only: (1) a panicked bugbear named Kelno, (2) coins with Air Temple insignia. Kelno's actual faction is unconfirmed.
**Suggested Fix:** Remove the claim that Kelno is "from the Air Temple" from the NPC entry. The NPC entry should read that Kelno is a figure *named by the bugbear* as a Water Temple contact, but that the Air Temple coins suggest the bugbear's information (or Kelno's stated affiliation) may be false or that Air Temple gnolls were operating under his name as cover. Flag this as **unresolved** for future sessions.

## ISSUE 4: Wraith "Raised Specters from Earth Temple Soldiers" — Contradicted Later
**Location:** Summary, Scenes (The Sealed Greater Temple), NPCs (Erith / The Wraith)
**Issue:** The Summary and Scenes state the seven specters were "raised from the bodies of fallen Earth Temple soldiers." The Memorable Moments section quotes the Wraith saying he "feasted on Water Temple dead" and had "been hoping for Earth Temple blood" — which implies the Water Temple dead were his prior feast, not the source of the Earth Temple specters. The Scenes section does say the specters are "mostly raised from Earth Temple forces," which is softer but still potentially contradicted by the Wraith's own words about preferring Earth Temple blood.
**Evidence:** The Wraith's own dialogue in the Scenes section (passage negotiation): "Feasted on them enough. I was hoping for some Earth Temple blood." This implies the Wraith's recent feast was Water Temple dead, yet the specters are attributed to Earth Temple corpses.
**Suggested Fix:** Clarify that the specters were raised from whatever bodies were available in the area (likely a mix), or reconcile by noting the Wraith feasted on Water Temple dead in a prior area while the specters in this choke point were raised from Earth Temple soldiers killed nearby. Flag as a minor ambiguity worth clarifying with the DM.

## ISSUE 5: Zephyr's Sneak Attack Described with Assassin's "Attack Before Enemies Have Acted" — Class Feature Misattributed
**Location:** Scenes (Battle with the Wraith and Specters)
**Issue:** The recap states Zephyr leveraged "his assassin's training to attack before enemies have acted." This describes the Assassin's Assassinate feature, which grants advantage and automatic critical hits against creatures that have not yet taken a turn — but this feature applies on the *first round of combat* to creatures that haven't acted, not simply to acting before enemies on initiative. The recap's phrasing implies Zephyr attacked a specter "before it had acted" as if this is a persistent ability he can trigger at will, which misrepresents how Assassinate works. More importantly, the damage reported (14 sneak + 9 weapon = 23, "total with other dice: 33") is described as a sneak attack, not a critical hit — if Assassinate applied, it would be a critical (doubled dice), which should significantly change the damage calculation.
**Evidence:** Party Reference Document lists Zephyr as "Tiefling Rogue (Assassin)" but does not detail his level or exact feature list. Standard 5e Assassin: Assassinate applies advantage + auto-crit on surprised creatures or creatures that haven't taken a turn yet in round 1.
**Suggested Fix:** Either (a) confirm whether this was Round 1 and whether the specter was surprised (making it a legitimate auto-crit, in which case the damage math should reflect doubled dice), or (b) remove the "assassin's training" justification and describe it simply as Zephyr winning initiative and attacking first. Clarify with DM whether a critical was actually rolled and whether the damage figure is correct.

## ISSUE 7: Hartsch Sends Broken Blades to "Water Temple" — But Plan Was Air Temple
**Location:** Summary, Scenes (War Council and Hartsch's Blunder), NPCs (Hartsch)
**Issue:** The party's agreed plan was a two-pronged attack on the **Air Temple** (identified as the weakest faction). When Hartsch goes rogue, the recap says he "sent the Broken Blades to attack" — but the Scenes section, when the party finds Dren, places the mercenaries in what appears to be the Air Temple area (they are told to wait "outside the Air Temple" after the rescue). However, the Locations section under "Water Temple" describes it as "the target of Hartsch's ill-fated raid," and the Failed Raid scene is titled "rescuing the Broken Blades from the Water Temple." This directly contradicts the agreed plan, which targeted the Air Temple, and is never explained. Did Hartsch send them to the Water Temple (deviating from the plan entirely) or the Air Temple (following the target but not the timing)?
**Evidence:** Scenes (Return to Earth Temple): plan targets Air Temple; party/Hartsch agree. Scenes title: "The Failed Raid and Rescuing the Broken Blades — the party abandons their Air Temple assault to rescue the Broken Blades from the Water Temple." Locations (Water Temple): "The target of Hartsch's ill-fated raid."
**Suggested Fix:** Clarify in the Summary and War Council scene which temple Hartsch actually sent the Broken Blades to attack. If it was the Water Temple (contradicting the agreed plan), this should be noted as a significant additional betrayal — he not only acted without authorization but also changed the target. If it was the Air Temple, correct all references in Locations (Water Temple) and the scene title.

## ISSUE 8: Calmer's Cover Described as "Earth Temple Cleric" in One Place, "St. Cuthbert Cleric" in Another
**Location:** Summary, Scenes (The Failed Raid and Rescuing the Broken Blades)
**Issue:** The Summary states Calmer worried that "using his full divine power in front of the mercenaries might expose his cover as a St. Cuthbert cleric." The Scenes section quotes him directly: "It would be better if they weren't there and saw as I called on St. Cuthbert's Power." This is accurate — his real identity is St. Cuthbert cleric, his cover is Earth Temple cleric. However, the NPC section on Hartsch and the party document both confirm the cover is as an Earth Temple cleric (converted follower of the elder elemental god), not simply generically "not St. Cuthbert." The Summary's phrasing ("expose his cover as a St. Cuthbert cleric") is slightly backwards — it would expose that he *is* a St. Cuthbert cleric, thereby *breaking* his cover as an Earth Temple cleric.
**Evidence:** Party Reference Document (Calmer): "publicly posing as a converted follower of the elder elemental god while privately maintaining his true faith."
**Suggested Fix:** Rephrase to: "using his full divine power in front of the mercenaries might expose him as a cleric of St. Cuthbert, blowing his cover as a convert to the elder elemental god." Minor phrasing issue but worth correcting to avoid future confusion.

## ISSUE 9: Zinnia's Insight Check Result Described as "Low at 9" But Called "Enough"
**Location:** Scenes (War Council and Hartsch's Blunder)
**Issue:** The recap states "Zinnia rolls an insight check (result: 9, low but enough for the DM to reveal a critical detail)" — that Hartsch falsely attributed the orders to the party. This is noted in two places: the Summary says the check "revealed the sickening detail," while the Scenes section qualifies it as "low but enough." These framings are contradictory: if 9 is genuinely low, it is unusual for a DM to reveal a critical plot detail on a low roll. This ambiguity could mislead future editors or the DM reviewing session notes into thinking a DC 9 insight check reliably detects deliberate deception.
**Evidence:** No campaign document sets insight DCs. This is an internal consistency issue within the recap.
**Suggested Fix:** Clarify the framing. Either note that the DM chose to reveal this detail regardless of the roll (perhaps it was a freebie clue), or adjust the description to remove the implication that DC 9 is a meaningful threshold for detecting deliberate NPC deception. The Memorable Moments section omits the roll qualifier entirely (just "Zinnia's insight check") — align all references to use the same framing.

## ISSUE 11: Sequoia Uses "Sneak Attack" on the Bugbear Without Stated Conditions
**Location:** Scenes (The Defector's End)
**Issue:** The recap states Sequoia "attacks without rolling for initiative, dealing 22 points of damage in the first exchange" and finishes with "one extra attack remaining." Rogues in 5e get one sneak attack per turn and do not have an "extra attack" feature — the Extra Attack feature belongs to fighters, rangers, and some other classes, not rogues. If Sequoia dealt 22 damage across two attacks, this should be described as his attack action and his sneak attack, not an "extra attack."
**Evidence:** Party Reference Document lists Sequoia as "Halfling Rogue" with no multiclass noted. Rogues do not have Extra Attack.
**Suggested Fix:** Rephrase "with one extra attack remaining, Sequoia finishes him" to something like "Sequoia's attack, boosted by sneak attack damage, finishes him" — or confirm whether Sequoia has a feature (e.g., Two-Weapon Fighting, or a bonus action attack from a specific subclass feature) that accounts for a second attack. Clarify with player/DM.
