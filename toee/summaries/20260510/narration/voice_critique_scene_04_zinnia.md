## Flags

### [1] Zephyr's expenditure frame in Zinnia's POV

> We were spending other people's blood and the price was fair.

**Why:** "Spending blood / the price was fair" is the Zephyr expenditure frame from his revised spec (The Expenditure section). Zinnia reads what Zephyr just did as information — what was traded, what the wraith now holds — not as a transaction he endorses in Zephyr's economic vocabulary. He would note it in operational terms: what was given, what it bought, what the wraith now knows about them.
**Suggested rewrite:** `Zephyr gave up Water Temple dead in exchange for passage. The wraith got what it wanted. We got through. I noted what it now knew about us.`

### [2] Over-elaborated perception beat

> The exact word, the exact weight — I could see it land.

**Why:** Zinnia's spec says his perception reads arrive as finished observations, terse and direct. "The exact weight" as a complement to "the exact word" is the kind of elaboration that draws attention to the prose. His acting background makes "the exact word" defensible (he knows what precision of expression looks like), but "the exact weight" pushes into literary pairing. The beat ends stronger at "the exact word."
**Suggested rewrite:** `The exact word — I could see it land.`

### [3] Literary purple in the rallying-speech aftermath

> Not loyalty to an order. Loyalty to the one person who came when the order had eaten them alive.

**Why:** "When the order had eaten them alive" is a worked literary image — the kind that requires the reader to pause and process. Zinnia's spec shows his terse Perception voice naming things plainly when they matter most. The preceding line ("Not loyalty to an order") is clean; the clause following it should match that register.
**Suggested rewrite:** `Not loyalty to an order. Loyalty to the person who showed up when the order left them there.`

### [4] Zephyr's economic vocabulary in Zinnia's read of Zephyr

> He never stops levying.

**Why:** "Levying" is Zephyr's own frame — the Tiefling Tax, the economic register of the revised Zephyr spec. Zinnia would read what Zephyr is doing in operational terms (Zephyr is recruiting, converting liabilities into assets), not in Zephyr's own vocabulary. Using Zephyr's word to describe Zephyr is a subtle frame bleed; Zinnia sees what Zephyr does, but names it in his own language.
**Suggested rewrite:** `He was already recruiting. He never leaves a room without adding to the count.`

## Verdict

The scene's strongest passages are the wraith passage read (*It remembers we came past. It remembers we paid in someone else's people.*), the Hartsch-name-as-something-to-file beat, and "I don't rally people. I observe, I wait, I move" — all accurate Zinnia. The four flags are subtle frame-bleed from Zephyr's economic vocabulary and two moments where terse perception tips into literary elaboration. Spot edits.
