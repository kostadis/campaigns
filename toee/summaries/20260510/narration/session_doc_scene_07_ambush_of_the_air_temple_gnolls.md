The bugbear was still settling into the floor when the secret door coughed up its replacements.

Three gnolls, hunched and reeking, spilling out into the corridor with the indignation of creditors. *"We paid good money for that!"* one of them snarled, and I understood the whole situation before the second sentence arrived. *"Now we will get our money, and get food!"* The bugbear had been a purchased defection. We had killed their investment. And now they wanted a refund and a meal, in that order, and they had come to collect both from the people standing over the corpse.

Air Temple gnolls. Kelno's recruitment drive, apparently, running door to door. *This again.* Everyone in this temple was buying everyone else, and the loyalty lasted exactly as long as the coin was warm.

"Didn't know you guys liked eating while dead, but okay," Sequoia said, flat as a slab, and I will admit the line was clean enough that I let it stand. He has been getting funnier the deeper into this place we go. It's the only thing in here that's improving.

Then Dren stepped up with his two mercenaries and put his hands on Calmer.

The cleric did not take it gracefully. *"Unhand me, you creatures!"* — bellowed, full-throated, the over-eager zealot's outrage at being denied a fight he had no business standing in. But the man had nothing left in him. Necrotic cold from the wraith still sat in his chest, and whatever well he draws his healing from was scraped dry; he could not patch himself past the thin margin he was running on. So Dren dragged him back up the corridor, all protest and no leverage, and the math of the skirmish rearranged itself around his absence.

Three of us now. No healer. Whatever finished these gnolls had to come out of me and Sequoia, and the body knew it before the thought did.

We moved first. We always do — these things never see us coming until we're already inside their reach, and the gnolls were too busy salivating to set themselves.

One of them turned his yellow eyes on me. *"Tiefling flesh!"* it howled, delighted, like a man discovering a delicacy he'd thought extinct. *"Haven't had tiefling flesh in ages!"*

There it was. The world charging me for existing again, except this one wanted to do it with its teeth.

"Come and get it," I said.

I didn't flinch. I never do, not for this. A dryad once asked if I'd crawled out of the Abyss to torment her; a gnome guard once asked *or whatever you are*; and now a starving Air Temple dog wanted me on a spit. Different mouths, same bill. The threat gets solved the same way every time.

I went in on the first one. The opening swing didn't land the way I wanted — sometimes the blade just doesn't fall true — but the second found the gap, and the dagger went where daggers go. The strike from concealment is the only round that matters. Thud. Steel through the soft place under the ribs, the full weight of the ambush behind it, and the thing folded most of the way to dead in a single beat. Zinnia drifted in and finished what I'd left twitching. One gone.

The survivor got his one good moment then. Caught me across the side, hard enough to register, not hard enough to mean anything. *Not lethal.* The other one lunged and missed entirely. Two dogs, one scratch between them. That's the going rate when you let me set the terms.

Sequoia took the second. Stepped in, tagged it clean, opened it up — the thing had nothing left to lose and lost it anyway.

Which left the last one for me.

Sequoia slid in beside it to give me the angle, and I took the angle. Dagger high, dagger low, and then the third strike from the blind side with everything stacked behind it. Thud. Thirty pounds of pressure in a six-inch line. The gnoll didn't so much die as stop. They attack. They die. There is no longer eulogy than they earn.

And then it was quiet in that ugly brown-and-black corridor, and three of us were standing, and the secret door hung open on whatever the gnolls had crawled out of.

"This leads to the water temple?" I said, already counting what might be behind it.

Air Temple, came the correction. Of course. A whole new route, and we owned the doorway now.

"Well, I think we can go in." But Sequoia was right and I knew it before he said it — no Calmer, no healer, three people and a door into someone else's stronghold. We needed her for the kind of thing waiting in there.

"We'll have to go without her." Then, looking at the dark on the far side of it, I corrected myself. Not an assault. Not yet. "Recon mission."

We have ridiculous stealth. A door is just a door when you're the thing nobody hears coming.
