## Calmer — Cleric of St. Cuthbert (War Domain) 8 · Human · *Kostadis*

Publicly **"Prophet Calmer,"** the Greater Temple's designated chief cleric of the Upper Temple. The
session arc is now openly about **identity drift**: he took Hartsch's chambers, insists on his title
("please refer to me furthermore as the Prophet Calmer"), taunts foes, and wants to plunder. He had his
campaign high point when his Turn Undead finally destroyed the wraith Erith after two prior failures
("It worked! Who's the man?"). **Zinnia has formally warned him he's going too deep** — he deferred to
her judgment, but the pull is strong.

- **Watch:** the moment cover-evil becomes real-evil; near-invocations of St. Cuthbert that break cover.
- **Debt:** still owes Prefect Terjon (resurrection + exclusive reporting) — increasingly incompatible
  with his current role.
- **Items:** Mace of Smiting; +1 Plate Mail; concealed holy symbol; TZGY scarab; Fire Shield (frost
  variant), Spirit Guardians, Toll the Dead, Sacred Flame, Gift of the Gem Dragon in his kit.

## Party Dynamics

- **Central fault line — Calmer's drift vs. Zinnia's brake.** This has matured from "is his cover
  affecting him?" into an explicit, on-screen confrontation. Zinnia is winning the argument, barely.
- **Zephyr vs. the leash.** Old Zephyr-vs-Calmer friction has eased into a working relationship; the new
  tension is Zephyr resenting that they now have *superiors* (Barkinar) at all.
- **Sequoia's quiet self-interest** persists (treasure, side-deals) but Frostbrand — a literal good-aligned
  conscience strapped to his hip — is a new and ironic counterweight.
- **Shared goal, inverted method:** still dismantle the Temple of Elemental Evil — but now from the throne
  rather than the shadows. Sequoia's doctrine: bankrupt it, nobody gets paid, and climb to the Greater
  Temple from the inside.
