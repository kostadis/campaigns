## Where the next session opens

The party is **mid-fight in the Water Temple** (Level 2) against animated stone gargoyles — one down,
two slowed by Spirit Guardians. Pick up here or just after. The Water Temple is the last of the four
sub-temples to bring to heel, and holds an unaudited treasury plus "trolls on contract" the party
hasn't seen yet.

## The frame that changed everything

The party **won.** They didn't infiltrate-and-destroy — they infiltrated, decapitated all four
elemental temples, and got promoted to run the Upper Temple for the Greater Temple. The dramatic
engine is no longer "will their cover hold among enemies." It's now:

1. **The slow rot of cover from the inside.** They have to *administer* an evil temple convincingly.
   Calmer is the pressure point — he likes it too much.
2. **The Greater Temple is the real boss now, and they answer to it.** Barkinar, Senshock, Falrinth,
   and Levels 3–4 are the back half of the campaign. The party is embedded one floor below the apex.
3. **The one-week clock.** Loot now; look organized by check-in; don't get flayed.

## Active Plots

### 1. The Water Temple — finish the job (Immediate)
Gargoyle guardians animated from disturbing the altar. After the fight: audit/secure the treasury
(Barkinar's task), and surface the **troll contract** (trolls on retainer somewhere in Water Temple
territory — unresolved, potentially dangerous). This is the live scene.

### 2. Calmer's Identity Drift (URGENT — character arc, now acute)
Calmer has gone from "stressed cleric maintaining cover" to *enjoying* being Prophet Calmer: took the
nicest chambers, demands his title, taunts enemies ("I want every last one of them dead, dead, dead!"),
wants to "plunder." **Zinnia openly warned him: "You are going in too deep. You're not a real cleric
for the Earth Temple."** He conceded — for now. This is the party's central internal fault line. Watch
for: him making a genuinely evil call, or invoking St. Cuthbert in a way that breaks cover, or refusing
to leave the role when the time comes.

### 3. The Greater Temple — the apex (High Priority, looming)
Barkinar rules from Levels 3–4 with ogres + a hill giant. **Senshock** (paranoid wizard, "let him
underestimate you") and **Falrinth** (wizard hiding from the Greater Temple) are seeded. The Orb,
elemental nodes, Zuggtmoy, Prince Thrommel, the bronze gates — all still ahead and untouched. The
party is now positioned *inside* the structure, which is a far stronger approach vector than a frontal
assault would have been.

### 4. The Chest of Rao (High Priority, multi-claim)
Greater Temple (Barkinar) wants it recovered/accounted for. Brother Eldin wanted it returned to the
Archclericy of Veluna. Party holds it. Whatever they do creates an enemy — hand it up and they arm the
Greater Temple; divert it and Barkinar notices. A genuine dilemma to force.

### 5. The Unidentified Necromancer (High Priority, open)
Raised recently-dead Earth Temple members as ghouls during the war. Barkinar named finding him as a
task. Strong candidate to tie into Falrinth and/or the Greater Temple's deeper operations. Good
convergence point.

### 6. Loose threads inside the Upper Temple (Moderate)
- **Varek Solain** — submitted but resentful, carries a Flametongue; a betrayal-in-waiting.
- **Vorkal's contract** — does it bind only Zephyr or the whole party? He hints at Greater Temple intel.
- **Kelno** — transactional ally; will flip if it serves survival.
- **Maeris Dorn** — grief-compliant; shallow loyalty.
- **Sequoia's old short sword** — disposition unresolved after claiming Frostbrand.

### 7. Surface / Hommlet (Moderate, deferred)
The council (Jaroo, Terjon, Burne/Rufus, Kenter) is owed reports and has no idea how deep the party is.
**Terjon holds Calmer's resurrection debt + exclusive-reporting demand** — increasingly untenable.
**Y'dey's disappearance** (Terjon suspected) still unresolved. The longer the party stays "down," the
worse the surface reckoning gets.

### 8. Background / low-priority hooks

- **Calmer is the engine.** The most interesting pressure isn't external anymore — it's whether the
  party (and the player) lets Calmer's "Prophet" persona become real. Zinnia is the in-fiction brake.
  Give Calmer chances to make calls that are *good administration of an evil temple* and watch the line.
- **Vorkal + Frostbrand + the necromancer + Falrinth** likely braid together into the Greater Temple
  reveal. Frostbrand (Emridy Meadows trophy) and Vorkal both dangle "I know what's really going on."
- **The watching door from the war (Ch 25)** is plausibly retconned-explained: the Greater Temple had
  hidden forces on Level 2 the whole time. If you want, that observer was always Barkinar's people.
- **The one-week clock** is the soft timer that keeps the looting honest. When it runs out, the next
  Greater Temple check-in is the natural next big set-piece / pressure beat.
