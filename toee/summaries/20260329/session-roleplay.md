# Roleplay Highlights — Session — 2026-03-30

## Character Voices

### Zephyr
- Operates at a single register: blunt transactional efficiency. Every conversation is a negotiation with a goal, and the goal is always information, access, or leverage. He doesn't do small talk.
- When a plan collapses, he abandons it without ego and pivots immediately — "Alright. This plan is done. Britain." The comic non-sequitur at the end suggests he processes failure by moving on before the embarrassment can land.
- His satisfaction at engineering the four-way temple war is genuine but understated — more "yes, this is correct" than triumph.

> "You'll have your turn. Give me 2 seconds."
> "Why are you laughing?"
> "So… We've done a good job. Right."

---

### Calmer (voiced by GM)
- Calmer has passed from exhaustion into something stranger — a holy frenzy that reads as equal parts faith and breakdown. He's stopped calculating and started feeling, and what he feels is hatred.
- His self-aware aside about his own armor class ("but… not me, explains Calmer, whose armor class is 20") shows he's still lucid enough to know he's surviving on technicalities — which only makes the hysteria funnier and sadder.
- The repeated "yes, yes, yes, yes, yes" as he watches ghouls die isn't victory — it's release. He has been waiting for something he is allowed to destroy without guilt.

> "He's going to try one more time to kill this creature, because he hates it with such an abiding and pure passion."
> "Palmer's like, This is not fair!"
> "Die, you pieces of… This is how the power of… " *(voice rising past the point of finishing the sentence)*

---

### The Freed Prisoner (NPC)
- Speaks in the register of a man who has been terrified for a long time and is now buying himself moments by being useful — dry, self-deprecating, and aggressively reasonable once he realizes demanding more would be stupid.
- His line about the Deep Temple is the voice of universal dungeon common sense: nobody *wants* to go there. That you're asking means something has gone wrong with you.
- Invokes his mother as a character witness to his own practicality — a very specific kind of deflated dignity.

> "Because nobody wants to get to the Deep Temple. Everybody wants to not… not ever be near the Deep Temple, but… since you asked…"
> "I guess I would be pushing my luck if I insisted for more help, wouldn't I? My mother never raised… didn't raise me to be a fool."

---

### The Wraith (NPC)
- Theatrical and contemptuous. Doesn't threaten quietly — announces. Laughs at a fleeing cleric rather than simply pursuing him.
- The laughter at Calmer's retreat is the most character-defining moment: the wraith doesn't just want to kill, it wants to be *right* about you.

> "Well, one of you is going to die today."

## Standout Moments

- **Calmer's holy breakdown against the ghouls.** The cascade of "yes, yes, yes, yes, yes, yes" as he curses the undead marks a shift — not into competence, but into something rawer. The GM noted the party was "starting to realize Calmer's going a little bit on the nutso job side." This is the sound of a man whose moral exhaustion has curdled into religious fury. He's stopped repressing it.

- **Zephyr's pivot after the gargoyle plan collapses.** "Alright. This plan is done. Britain." The word "Britain" appears to mean nothing at all, which is exactly why it works — it's the sound of Zephyr's brain already elsewhere. No frustration. No ego. Just gone.

- **The freed prisoner's negotiation.** Having set his terms, received partial assistance, and then been stared down by Calmer's aura of barely-contained divine menace, the prisoner concludes — with genuine pragmatism — that his mother would not want him to push it. He gives up the secret door. It is the most sensible decision anyone has made in the dungeon all session.

- **Zephyr's summary of the campaign situation.** "So… We've done a good job. Right." Said with the satisfaction of a man checking a box. Four factions now at war, the Deep Temple access in hand, the party still alive and in cover. He's correct. It is a good job. The period at the end of "Right" does a lot of work.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**NPC Speech Patterns to Maintain:**

- *The Wraith:* Theatrical, contemptuous, announces rather than acts. Should always have a line ready before attacking — it wants an audience for its cruelty. Laughter is its tell.
- *Freed Prisoner (if he recurs):* Dry, self-deprecating, pragmatic. Survival-oriented. Will invoke family or personal history to humanize himself when under pressure. Not brave, but not stupid either.
- *Calmer (GM-voiced):* Currently alternating between clipped self-aware commentary and bursts of barely-coherent religious intensity. The lucidity makes the frenzy worse. Do not smooth him out — the tension between the two registers is the character right now.

**PC Emotional States for NPC Dialogue:**

- *Zephyr* is riding high on strategic success. NPCs who challenge his competence will get dismissal; NPCs who offer information or leverage will get his full attention. He is currently in the mood to go deeper into the dungeon — lean into that momentum.
- *Calmer* is in a fragile state that looks, from the outside, like zealotry. NPCs who treat him as a true convert to elemental evil will disturb him deeply. NPCs who treat him as a holy warrior — even mockingly — may reach something real. He is dangerous to himself and to the party's cover right now.
- *Zinnia and Sequoia* (less active this session) are watching the pieces fall into place. They should be addressed by NPCs as the calm, competent ones — the foil to Zephyr's momentum and Calmer's volatility.

**Promises, Threats, and Vows to Reference:**

- The freed prisoner was released in exchange for information about the Deep Temple's secret entrance. If he resurfaces, he owes the party nothing more — but the party made an implicit promise of safety by freeing him. Whether they honored that promise for the other prisoners is worth tracking.
- Zephyr's declaration — "So, perfect for us to go down to the lower temple" — functions as a vow of intent. Future NPCs aware of the party's movements should treat the Deep Temple as their next destination.
