# Chapter 25 Opening Battles

Date: Mar 29th, 2026

## Summary
The party emerged onto the second level of the temple to find a battlefield already drenched in chaos. Scalding steam billowed through the corridors, and the bodies of fallen warriors from both the Fire and Earth temples littered the ground. Through the haze, Fire Temple bugbears held a barricade against a stalled Earth Temple advance, and the Obsidian Edge saw their opportunity. Zephyr struck first, loosing a precise shot from the mist, and Zinnia followed close behind, cutting down an evil cultist at the front of the line before the bugbears even knew what had hit them.

The bugbears retaliated with savage fury, landing a devastating blow on Zinnia that left her reeling, and striking at Zephyr and Sequioa as well. But Calmer turned the tide in a single, thunderous moment — channeling divine light into a blast that smashed the barricaded double doors wide open, allowing Earth Temple forces to pour through. With the barricade broken and the mission accomplished, the party pressed their advantage. Zephyr cut down another bugbear with a flurry of precise strikes, and Sequioa delivered a crushing blow that finished off another, leaving the remaining enemies caught between two warring factions. Recognizing that the magical steam and escalating elemental violence made the battlefield far too dangerous to linger in, the party disengaged and retreated.

Their escape route led them through the Air Temple, where their least favorite resident was waiting — a malevolent wraith that haunted the corridors with cold menace. Calmer, driven by his deep religious hatred of the undead, attempted twice to repel the creature with the power of his faith, but the wraith resisted both times, laughing at him with hollow contempt. Zephyr managed to wound it with a shot before dashing toward a secret passage, and the rest of the party followed in a desperate sprint. As Calmer made his own furious retreat, the wraith struck him hard across the back, and its mocking laughter echoed behind them as they scrambled upstairs to safety.

The party retreated to their own territory to regroup, but the respite was short-lived. The acrid stench of decay crept through the corridors, and four ghouls lurched into view — their sunken faces eerily familiar, resembling recently dead members of the Earth Temple, clearly raised by dark necrotic magic. Sequioa attempted to set a trap at the door to slow their advance, but it was sprung too soon, and the ghouls shambled forward. Zephyr moved with lethal precision, driving a dagger into the nearest ghoul before it could react, dropping it instantly. Then Calmer, his holy bloodlust fully ignited, channeled the full force of his divine wrath and sent the remaining ghouls fleeing in terror, cursing them with furious religious fervor as Sequioa finished off the last wounded creature with a well-placed arrow.

After a brief rest to tend their wounds, the party descended once more into the temple's dimly lit corridors, pushing deeper while the sounds of battle raged around them. The stone floors were scattered with signs of hurried looting — broken bottles, discarded armor, and blood trails that led nowhere. Through the haze, they glimpsed goblins and ghouls dragging weapons and bags of coin through hidden passages, and the party realized with grim clarity that Kelno, the seemingly meek Air Temple cleric, had been quietly sending his goblins to scavenge the dead for his own purposes. All four temples were now entangled in the conflict, and the war had grown far more complicated than it first appeared.

Pressing further north, the party stumbled upon a massive, echoing hall that had once been the scene of great and terrible celebrations. The floor was strewn with bones and the wreckage of furniture, while the walls were hung with tapestries depicting scenes of raiding and violence, and grim trophies — mummified heads, torn battle standards, and a broken broadsword — decorated the stone. Broad steps of dull black stone descended from the north wall into shadow. Nearby, they witnessed fire snakes and a wounded salamander locked in brutal combat with water gargoyles amidst eruptions of scalding steam, and with no way to communicate with the elemental creatures, the party wisely chose to slip away rather than wade into another fight.

Moving northeast through the corridors, the party discovered something unexpected — a chamber full of chained human prisoners huddled against the wall, trembling in their shackles and pleading not to be abandoned. The prisoners appeared to have been gathered as sacrifices or subjects for dark rituals, and one among them spoke up with a desperate bargain: he knew the way to the Deep Temple, but he would only talk if they freed him first. Zinnia watched him carefully and determined he was telling the truth, and Sequioa made short work of the iron shackles. The freed man, a former member of the Air Temple, revealed that Kelno himself had known of a secret door hidden within the Air Temple that led to the lower levels — and after Calmer's imposing presence convinced him that pushing his luck further would be unwise, the prisoner pointed out the exact location of the passage. With the secret of the Deep Temple finally in hand, the Obsidian Edge prepared to descend into the darkness below, but before they could go down, a runner from the Earth Temple found them. They had to get back to Hartsch. Things were not going well.

## NPCs
### Fire Temple Bugbears
Warriors holding a barricade against the Earth Temple's advance on the second level. They fight with morning stars and maces, proving to be dangerous opponents in the steam-filled corridors, landing a critical hit on Zinnia.

### Evil Cultist
A member of the fire temple positioned at the front of the bugbear barricade who was quickly dispatched by Zinnia at the start of the battle.

### Wraith
A malevolent undead spirit that haunts the Air Temple. It resisted Calmer's attempts to turn it twice, struck Calmer with a powerful blow as he fled, and laughed at him hysterically as he ran away.

### Ghouls
Undead scavengers that appeared to be recently deceased members of the Earth Temple, likely raised by necrotic magic. They came to scavenge dead bodies and attacked the party in their territory before being repelled by Calmer's divine power.

### Calmer
A cleric of St. Cuthbert and party member who harbors an intense religious hatred for the undead. He smashed open the barricaded doors with a guiding bolt, repeatedly attempted to turn the wraith, and entered a state of bloodlust during the ghoul ambush, cursing the fleeing undead.

### Kelno
The Air Temple cleric who has been sending goblins to scavenge dead bodies from the ongoing temple wars. A freed prisoner reveals that Kelno knew of a secret door inside the Air Temple leading to the lower temple.

### Former Air Temple Cultist
A human prisoner and former member of the Air Temple who bargained his knowledge of the secret passage to the Greater Temple in exchange for his freedom. He was convinced to reveal the exact location of the secret door after Calmer's intimidating presence wore down his resistance.

## Locations
### Temple Second Level
A chaotic battlefield flooded with water and choked with zones of scalding steam. It serves as a violent front line between the Fire and Earth temples, with dead bodies from both factions littering the ground.

### Air Temple
A section of the temple complex where the party encounters a wraith. It also contains a secret door leading to the Greater Temple and the lower levels, as revealed by a freed prisoner.

### Party Territory
A relatively secure area where the party attempts to rest, though it is eventually breached by scavenging ghouls that have come to collect dead bodies.

### The Great Hall of Revels
A massive, echoing chamber filled with the wreckage of furniture and decorated with tapestries depicting scenes of raiding and violence, as well as gruesome trophies like mummified heads, torn battle standards, and a broken broadsword. Broad steps of dull black stone in the north wall lead to a depressing descending stair.

### Prisoners' Chamber
A grim room where human prisoners are kept in shackles, awaiting their fate as sacrifices or subjects for necrotic spells. The party discovers and frees one prisoner here who reveals the location of the secret door to the Deep Temple.
