## Session Summary

# Session — 2026-03-30

## Overview

The party completed the assassination of Romag and watched Hartsch seize control of the Earth Temple before descending into the dungeon's second level to participate in — and then escape from — the three-way temple war they had engineered. They broke a Fire Temple barricade, fled a murderous wraith twice, repelled a ghoul ambush in their own territory, and deduced that all four temple factions are now entangled in the conflict. Pushing deeper through the chaos, they discovered a chamber of chained prisoners, extracted a critical piece of intelligence — the location of a secret door inside the Air Temple leading to the Deep Temple — and were then recalled upstairs by a runner from Hartsch, who requires their presence at a war council before they can descend further.

---

## Session Events

**Pre-Session Recap / Session Open**
- The party killed Romag in his private chamber via secret passage. Zephyr magically slammed the doors shut. Zinnia, Sequoia, and Calmer killed him. Calmer nearly invoked St. Cuthbert aloud mid-combat but caught himself, channeling necrotic energy instead.
- Hartsch immediately seized the moment: covered his hands in Romag's blood, took his sacred vestments, declared himself Supreme Prophet of the Earth Temple, and named the party "the Obsidian Edge" — his personal vanguard — before the assembled temple.

**Engineering the Temple War**
- The party moved to the second level and slipped a note under the Fire Temple door. When Alremm responded through the door, the party revealed that Belsornig of the Water Temple had orchestrated the Chimera incident. Alremm screamed Belsornig's name and immediately ordered a full assault on the Water Temple.
- Informed that the Fire Temple was attacking the Water Temple, Hartsch ordered a full-scale Earth Temple assault. All three factions are now at open war.

**Barricade Battle (Second Level)**
- The Earth Temple advance stalled at a second-level stairway barricade — Fire Temple bugbears had dragged pews and stone rubble across it. Hartsch tasked the Obsidian Edge with breaking the stalemate.
- The party bypassed the route by cutting through the Air Temple. The wraith drifted toward them; they ran, and it turned back once they left its domain.
- On the second level, amid billowing magical steam, Zephyr opened with a bow shot, Zinnia killed a Fire Temple cultist in melee, and bugbears retaliated — critically wounding Zinnia (25 damage) and also striking Zephyr and Sequoia.
- Calmer used Guiding Bolt to blow the barricaded double doors open, allowing Earth Temple forces to charge through. Zephyr killed one bugbear; Sequoia killed another.
- With the barricade broken and the steam making the battlefield increasingly dangerous, the party disengaged.

**Wraith Encounter (Air Temple, First Pass)**
- Retreating back through the Air Temple, Calmer attempted Turn Undead on the wraith twice — the wraith made both DC 15 saves and resisted.
- Zephyr shot the wraith with his pistol (5 damage after resistance). The party dashed for the secret passage.
- The wraith struck Calmer for 16 damage as he fled and laughed hysterically at him. A stray fire bolt caught Calmer for an additional 8 damage as the party emerged upstairs.
- The wraith declared "one of you will die today." Calmer's deadpan response: "But not me."
- Zinnia (Investigation 24–25) noticed a door to the east opening and closing as if someone was watching — origin unresolved.

**Ghoul Ambush (Party Territory)**
- The party retreated to their own quarters. Four ghouls — appearing to be recently deceased Earth Temple members raised by necrotic magic — arrived to scavenge corpses.
- Sequoia attempted to set a trap at the door; the ghouls rolled a 1 and triggered it prematurely.
- Zephyr killed one instantly with a dagger using Assassin advantage and sneak attack (27 damage).
- Calmer successfully channeled Turn Undead — all remaining ghouls failed their saves, fled, and took 4d8 holy damage. Calmer entered a state of religious bloodlust, cursing the retreating undead.
- Sequoia finished off one fleeing ghoul in melee.

**Short Rest and Second Descent**
- The party took a short rest in their territory (Calmer recovered to approximately 23 HP).
- Descending again, they observed goblins dragging looted weapons and coins through a secret door — Zinnia (Insight 25) identified them as Kelno's. The party concluded all four temples are now involved in the conflict, with Kelno opportunistically scavenging the dead.

**The Great Hall of Revels**
- The party passed through a massive ruined hall: mummified heads, torn battle standards, battered shields, a broken broadsword, raiding tapestries, broad black stone steps descending north to the lower level (appeared inaccessible).
- Nearby: fire snakes, a wounded salamander, and four water gargoyles fighting amid scalding steam. No party member speaks Primordial; Zephyr briefly considered capturing one to interrogate about the Deep Temple but abandoned the plan. Party moved south.

**Prisoner Chamber and Intelligence Extraction**
- Moving northeast, the party found a chamber of chained human prisoners pleading not to be abandoned.
- One prisoner claimed knowledge of the Deep Temple in exchange for his freedom. Zinnia used Insight with advantage (roll: 24) and confirmed he was telling the truth. Sequoia picked his shackles (roll: 27).
- The freed prisoner — a former Air Temple cultist — revealed that Kelno knows of a secret door inside the Air Temple leading to the Lower/Greater Temple.
- Zephyr's initial intimidation attempt failed (roll: 4). Calmer's imposing presence granted advantage on a reroll (roll: 19), which succeeded. The prisoner revealed the exact location of the secret door on the party's map.
- The remaining four prisoners demanded to be freed as well. Their fate was not resolved on-screen.
- Before the party could act on the new intelligence, an Earth Temple runner found them: Hartsch has summoned the Obsidian Edge to a war council upstairs.

## Character Voices

### Zephyr
- Operates at a single register: blunt transactional efficiency. Every conversation is a negotiation with a goal, and the goal is always information, access, or leverage. He doesn't do small talk.
- When a plan collapses, he abandons it without ego and pivots immediately — "Alright. This plan is done. Britain." The comic non-sequitur at the end suggests he processes failure by moving on before the embarrassment can land.
- His satisfaction at engineering the four-way temple war is genuine but understated — more "yes, this is correct" than triumph.

> "You'll have your turn. Give me 2 seconds."
> "Why are you laughing?"
> "So… We've done a good job. Right."

---

### Calmer (voiced by GM)
- Calmer has passed from exhaustion into something stranger — a holy frenzy that reads as equal parts faith and breakdown. He's stopped calculating and started feeling, and what he feels is hatred.
- His self-aware aside about his own armor class ("but… not me, explains Calmer, whose armor class is 20") shows he's still lucid enough to know he's surviving on technicalities — which only makes the hysteria funnier and sadder.
- The repeated "yes, yes, yes, yes, yes" as he watches ghouls die isn't victory — it's release. He has been waiting for something he is allowed to destroy without guilt.

> "He's going to try one more time to kill this creature, because he hates it with such an abiding and pure passion."
> "Palmer's like, This is not fair!"
> "Die, you pieces of… This is how the power of… " *(voice rising past the point of finishing the sentence)*

---

### The Freed Prisoner (NPC)
- Speaks in the register of a man who has been terrified for a long time and is now buying himself moments by being useful — dry, self-deprecating, and aggressively reasonable once he realizes demanding more would be stupid.
- His line about the Deep Temple is the voice of universal dungeon common sense: nobody *wants* to go there. That you're asking means something has gone wrong with you.
- Invokes his mother as a character witness to his own practicality — a very specific kind of deflated dignity.

> "Because nobody wants to get to the Deep Temple. Everybody wants to not… not ever be near the Deep Temple, but… since you asked…"
> "I guess I would be pushing my luck if I insisted for more help, wouldn't I? My mother never raised… didn't raise me to be a fool."

---

### The Wraith (NPC)
- Theatrical and contemptuous. Doesn't threaten quietly — announces. Laughs at a fleeing cleric rather than simply pursuing him.
- The laughter at Calmer's retreat is the most character-defining moment: the wraith doesn't just want to kill, it wants to be *right* about you.

> "Well, one of you is going to die today."

## Standout Moments

- **Calmer's holy breakdown against the ghouls.** The cascade of "yes, yes, yes, yes, yes, yes" as he curses the undead marks a shift — not into competence, but into something rawer. The GM noted the party was "starting to realize Calmer's going a little bit on the nutso job side." This is the sound of a man whose moral exhaustion has curdled into religious fury. He's stopped repressing it.

- **Zephyr's pivot after the gargoyle plan collapses.** "Alright. This plan is done. Britain." The word "Britain" appears to mean nothing at all, which is exactly why it works — it's the sound of Zephyr's brain already elsewhere. No frustration. No ego. Just gone.

- **The freed prisoner's negotiation.** Having set his terms, received partial assistance, and then been stared down by Calmer's aura of barely-contained divine menace, the prisoner concludes — with genuine pragmatism — that his mother would not want him to push it. He gives up the secret door. It is the most sensible decision anyone has made in the dungeon all session.

- **Zephyr's summary of the campaign situation.** "So… We've done a good job. Right." Said with the satisfaction of a man checking a box. Four factions now at war, the Deep Temple access in hand, the party still alive and in cover. He's correct. It is a good job. The period at the end of "Right" does a lot of work.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**NPC Speech Patterns to Maintain:**

- *The Wraith:* Theatrical, contemptuous, announces rather than acts. Should always have a line ready before attacking — it wants an audience for its cruelty. Laughter is its tell.
- *Freed Prisoner (if he recurs):* Dry, self-deprecating, pragmatic. Survival-oriented. Will invoke family or personal history to humanize himself when under pressure. Not brave, but not stupid either.
- *Calmer (GM-voiced):* Currently alternating between clipped self-aware commentary and bursts of barely-coherent religious intensity. The lucidity makes the frenzy worse. Do not smooth him out — the tension between the two registers is the character right now.

**PC Emotional States for NPC Dialogue:**

- *Zephyr* is riding high on strategic success. NPCs who challenge his competence will get dismissal; NPCs who offer information or leverage will get his full attention. He is currently in the mood to go deeper into the dungeon — lean into that momentum.
- *Calmer* is in a fragile state that looks, from the outside, like zealotry. NPCs who treat him as a true convert to elemental evil will disturb him deeply. NPCs who treat him as a holy warrior — even mockingly — may reach something real. He is dangerous to himself and to the party's cover right now.
- *Zinnia and Sequoia* (less active this session) are watching the pieces fall into place. They should be addressed by NPCs as the calm, competent ones — the foil to Zephyr's momentum and Calmer's volatility.

**Promises, Threats, and Vows to Reference:**

- The freed prisoner was released in exchange for information about the Deep Temple's secret entrance. If he resurfaces, he owes the party nothing more — but the party made an implicit promise of safety by freeing him. Whether they honored that promise for the other prisoners is worth tracking.
- Zephyr's declaration — "So, perfect for us to go down to the lower temple" — functions as a vow of intent. Future NPCs aware of the party's movements should treat the Deep Temple as their next destination.
