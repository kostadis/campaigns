# Session — 2026-03-30

## Overview

The party completed the assassination of Romag and watched Hartsch seize control of the Earth Temple before descending into the dungeon's second level to participate in — and then escape from — the three-way temple war they had engineered. They broke a Fire Temple barricade, fled a murderous wraith twice, repelled a ghoul ambush in their own territory, and deduced that all four temple factions are now entangled in the conflict. Pushing deeper through the chaos, they discovered a chamber of chained prisoners, extracted a critical piece of intelligence — the location of a secret door inside the Air Temple leading to the Deep Temple — and were then recalled upstairs by a runner from Hartsch, who requires their presence at a war council before they can descend further.

---

## Session Events

**Pre-Session Recap / Session Open**
- The party killed Romag in his private chamber via secret passage. Zephyr magically slammed the doors shut. Zinnia, Sequoia, and Calmer killed him. Calmer nearly invoked St. Cuthbert aloud mid-combat but caught himself, channeling necrotic energy instead.
- Hartsch immediately seized the moment: covered his hands in Romag's blood, took his sacred vestments, declared himself Supreme Prophet of the Earth Temple, and named the party "the Obsidian Edge" — his personal vanguard — before the assembled temple.

**Engineering the Temple War**
- The party moved to the second level and slipped a note under the Fire Temple door. When Alremm responded through the door, the party revealed that Belsornig of the Water Temple had orchestrated the Chimera incident. Alremm screamed Belsornig's name and immediately ordered a full assault on the Water Temple.
- Informed that the Fire Temple was attacking the Water Temple, Hartsch ordered a full-scale Earth Temple assault. All three factions are now at open war.

**Barricade Battle (Second Level)**
- The Earth Temple advance stalled at a second-level stairway barricade — Fire Temple bugbears had dragged pews and stone rubble across it. Hartsch tasked the Obsidian Edge with breaking the stalemate.
- The party bypassed the route by cutting through the Air Temple. The wraith drifted toward them; they ran, and it turned back once they left its domain.
- On the second level, amid billowing magical steam, Zephyr opened with a bow shot, Zinnia killed a Fire Temple cultist in melee, and bugbears retaliated — critically wounding Zinnia (25 damage) and also striking Zephyr and Sequoia.
- Calmer used Guiding Bolt to blow the barricaded double doors open, allowing Earth Temple forces to charge through. Zephyr killed one bugbear; Sequoia killed another.
- With the barricade broken and the steam making the battlefield increasingly dangerous, the party disengaged.

**Wraith Encounter (Air Temple, First Pass)**
- Retreating back through the Air Temple, Calmer attempted Turn Undead on the wraith twice — the wraith made both DC 15 saves and resisted.
- Zephyr shot the wraith with his pistol (5 damage after resistance). The party dashed for the secret passage.
- The wraith struck Calmer for 16 damage as he fled and laughed hysterically at him. A stray fire bolt caught Calmer for an additional 8 damage as the party emerged upstairs.
- The wraith declared "one of you will die today." Calmer's deadpan response: "But not me."
- Zinnia (Investigation 24–25) noticed a door to the east opening and closing as if someone was watching — origin unresolved.

**Ghoul Ambush (Party Territory)**
- The party retreated to their own quarters. Four ghouls — appearing to be recently deceased Earth Temple members raised by necrotic magic — arrived to scavenge corpses.
- Sequoia attempted to set a trap at the door; the ghouls rolled a 1 and triggered it prematurely.
- Zephyr killed one instantly with a dagger using Assassin advantage and sneak attack (27 damage).
- Calmer successfully channeled Turn Undead — all remaining ghouls failed their saves, fled, and took 4d8 holy damage. Calmer entered a state of religious bloodlust, cursing the retreating undead.
- Sequoia finished off one fleeing ghoul in melee.

**Short Rest and Second Descent**
- The party took a short rest in their territory (Calmer recovered to approximately 23 HP).
- Descending again, they observed goblins dragging looted weapons and coins through a secret door — Zinnia (Insight 25) identified them as Kelno's. The party concluded all four temples are now involved in the conflict, with Kelno opportunistically scavenging the dead.

**The Great Hall of Revels**
- The party passed through a massive ruined hall: mummified heads, torn battle standards, battered shields, a broken broadsword, raiding tapestries, broad black stone steps descending north to the lower level (appeared inaccessible).
- Nearby: fire snakes, a wounded salamander, and four water gargoyles fighting amid scalding steam. No party member speaks Primordial; Zephyr briefly considered capturing one to interrogate about the Deep Temple but abandoned the plan. Party moved south.

**Prisoner Chamber and Intelligence Extraction**
- Moving northeast, the party found a chamber of chained human prisoners pleading not to be abandoned.
- One prisoner claimed knowledge of the Deep Temple in exchange for his freedom. Zinnia used Insight with advantage (roll: 24) and confirmed he was telling the truth. Sequoia picked his shackles (roll: 27).
- The freed prisoner — a former Air Temple cultist — revealed that Kelno knows of a secret door inside the Air Temple leading to the Lower/Greater Temple.
- Zephyr's initial intimidation attempt failed (roll: 4). Calmer's imposing presence granted advantage on a reroll (roll: 19), which succeeded. The prisoner revealed the exact location of the secret door on the party's map.
- The remaining four prisoners demanded to be freed as well. Their fate was not resolved on-screen.
- Before the party could act on the new intelligence, an Earth Temple runner found them: Hartsch has summoned the Obsidian Edge to a war council upstairs.

## NPC Interactions

- **Romag:** Killed in his sleeping chamber. Final expression was confusion turning to horror. No longer a factor.
- **Hartsch:** Required no persuasion. Understood the coup the moment Zephyr slammed the doors, laid down his weapons, and stepped aside. After Romag died, he seized leadership unprompted — covered himself in blood, declared himself Supreme Prophet, named the party the Obsidian Edge. Later ordered a full Earth Temple assault when told the Fire Temple was occupied. Has now sent a runner to summon the party to a war council. Hartsch is in command and actively using the Obsidian Edge as his instrument.
- **Alremm (Fire Temple):** Received the party's note and spoke through the door. When the party revealed Belsornig's role in the Chimera incident, he immediately screamed Belsornig's name and ordered an attack on the Water Temple. Confirmed as the trigger for the three-way war.
- **Dren Halveth / Broken Blades:** Had arranged Romag's morning meeting on the party's behalf. Later found preparing his Crimson Guards for the assault. Party sent Dren and his men into the battle.
- **Kelno (not encountered directly):** Deduced to be sending goblins to loot the dead during the battle. A freed prisoner revealed Kelno knows of a secret door in the Air Temple leading to the Deep Temple. Calmer noted with dark humor that Kelno apparently believes the wraith is an air elemental.
- **The Wraith:** Resisted Turn Undead twice. Struck Calmer for 16 damage during his retreat. Declared one of the party would die. Laughed hysterically as Calmer fled. Satisfied once the party left its domain and did not pursue past the Air Temple.
- **Former Air Temple Cultist (freed prisoner):** Bargained knowledge for freedom. Confirmed truthful by Zinnia's Insight. Revealed the secret door to the Greater Temple after Calmer's intimidating presence broke his resistance. Pointed out the exact location on the party's map.
- **Remaining Prisoners (4):** Demanded freedom after the first prisoner was freed. Fate unresolved.
