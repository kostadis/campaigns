## Flags

### [1] Generic fantasy prose — stock haunted-corridor opening

> The corridor air changed before I saw it. That's the only way I can describe it — a drop in temperature that had nothing to do with stone or depth, a wrongness that sat behind my eyes like a headache forming.

**Why:** "A drop in temperature that had nothing to do with stone or depth" is near-boilerplate haunted-dungeon writing. The headache simile is too mild and domestic for Calmer's visceral, theological hatred of undead — his spec says his relationship with undead "is not tactical. It is visceral." He doesn't get headaches around evil; he gets righteous fury. The opening em-dash is also narration-level and could convert to a comma.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I knew before I turned the corner. Not by sight or sound — by the particular wrongness that St. Cuthbert's training leaves in a cleric who's seen enough of what shouldn't exist. The air at the threshold was cold in the way that cold doesn't come from stone."

### [2] Voice spec conflict — Zephyr's vocabulary in Calmer's mouth

> Not fear exactly. Fear implies you're still calculating odds. This was older than that.

**Why:** "Calculating odds" is Zephyr's explicit cognitive mode — his spec describes him reading probabilities, people, and rooms. Calmer's spec says he "doesn't doubt himself, he doubts his advisors" — he operates through conviction, not probability. He would frame the distinction theologically, not probabilistically.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Not fear exactly. Fear still leaves you room to answer back. This was the other thing — older than training, older than faith — the kind that gets in before St. Cuthbert can."

### [3] Mechanical scan A — trailing em-dash

> the way it simply *drifts* while I channel power that has worked before and should work now —

**Why:** Trailing narration-level em-dash at sentence end. The incompletion is better rendered by the next paragraph's silence than by syntactic truncation.
**Suggested rewrite:** "...should work now." (period; let the failed attempt land in the following paragraph)

### [4] Mechanical scan A — em-dash pair

> Sixteen damage — cold, deep, the kind of cold that doesn't stop at skin — and then a stray fire bolt from somewhere in the chaos added eight more

**Why:** Em-dash pair around an appositive descriptor. Comma reconstruction: "Sixteen damage, cold and deep, the kind of cold that doesn't stop at skin, and then a stray fire bolt..."

### [5] Qualifier softening Calmer's absolute hatred

> an undead abomination stood untouched and apparently unbothered

**Why:** "Apparently" hedges the observation. Calmer's spec says his hatred of undead is absolute and visceral; he doesn't observe them with scientific caution. He knows the wraith was unbothered. The qualifier weakens the wound.
**Suggested rewrite:** "an undead abomination stood untouched and completely unbothered."

## Verdict

The scene's core engine — two failed turns, the formal name of St. Cuthbert vs. "dank dungeon corridor," *"But not me"*, the AC 20 aside — is excellent Calmer. The flags concentrate at the opening and in one vocabulary bleed. The opening paragraph is the highest priority: the temperature-drop + headache-simile + "calculating odds" cluster is three distinct problems in four sentences. Rewrite the opening; spot-edit the trailing em-dash and "apparently."
