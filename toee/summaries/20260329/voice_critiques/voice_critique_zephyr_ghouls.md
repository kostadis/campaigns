## Flags

### [1] Mechanical scan A — em-dash pair (long parenthetical)

> I've been paralyzed by ghouls before — we all have, at this point, the claws hit and your body just *stops*, which is a sensation I would describe as among my least favourite — so I have developed a strong professional interest in killing them before they get the opportunity.

**Why:** Em-dash pair around an extended parenthetical. The clause is long enough that the pair loses emphatic function; a parenthetical construction preserves the aside without the dashes.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I've been paralyzed by ghouls before (we all have, at this point — the claws hit and your body just *stops*, a sensation I would describe as among my least favourite), so I have a strong interest in killing them first."

### [2] Voice spec conflict — clinical vocabulary

> the word Zinnia used was *identity drift* which I refuse to engage with directly

**Why:** "Identity drift" is therapeutic/diagnostic vocabulary — the language of psychology and narrative theory. Zephyr's spec says he reads people through what they *want*, what lever moves them — not through clinical frameworks. He names things in behavioral or economic terms, not diagnoses. He wouldn't have this term in his vocabulary.
**Suggested rewrite:** "the concern Zinnia had put into words once, something about Calmer going native — which I am choosing not to look at directly."

### [3] Register bleed — "professional interest"

> so I have developed a strong professional interest in killing them before they get the opportunity

**Why:** "Professional interest" is Zinnia's spy-tradecraft register, not Zephyr's theatrical-ledger voice. Zephyr frames self-interest personally and economically, not professionally.
**Suggested rewrite:** "so I make it a rule to kill them before the paralysis has a chance to vote." or "so I have a very personal and practical reason to kill them first."

## Verdict

The scene's best moments — *"Oh god. This again."*, the creditor-defaulter simile for Turn Undead, the two-beat internal observation about Calmer's "yes yes yes yes" — are strong Zephyr. The main flag is "identity drift": one clinical term that breaks his vocabulary sharply enough to rewrite. "Professional interest" is a softer issue. Spot-edit both; no re-narration needed.
