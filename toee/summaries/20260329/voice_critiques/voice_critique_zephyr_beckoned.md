## Flags

### [1] Mechanical scan A — em-dash pair (emphatic repetition)

> They had it — finally had it — and now they had to go back up.

**Why:** Em-dash pair for emphatic repetition. Comma version preserves the rhythm without the dashes: "They had it, finally had it, and now they had to go back up."

### [2] Register-wrong vocabulary — military frame

> The particular indignity of belonging to someone else's chain of command while you were trying to build your own.

**Why:** "Chain of command" is military/institutional vocabulary. Zephyr's register is economic and relational — ledger, expenditure, debt, leash. The preceding sentence uses "leash," which is correct Zephyr. "Chain of command" is a register step away from how he thinks about hierarchy.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The particular indignity of being someone else's asset while you were trying to build your own balance sheet."

### [3] Cliché / on-the-nose simile

> The door's location sat behind his eyes like a lit coal.

**Why:** "Lit coal" as a closing image for burning urgency is a familiar simile. Zephyr's characteristic similes are economic and ledger-based at his best ("like a creditor who's finally located a defaulter"). A closing image in his vocabulary would lean on value and weight, not heat.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The door's location sat behind his eyes like money he hadn't spent yet." or "...like the one item on his ledger that actually mattered tonight."

## Verdict

The scene's self-accounting ("He'd leaned on the man harder than necessary, all bravado and borrowed threat") and the dry "Presumably" are strong Zephyr. The "chain of command" register-wrong and the closing simile are both worth revising — the ending especially, since "lit coal" is the last impression of the document and a ledger-vocabulary image would close in Zephyr's actual voice. Spot-edit the closing paragraph.
