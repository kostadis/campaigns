## Flags

### [1] Voice spec conflict — too literary for Sequoia's staccato mode

> There's a particular kind of threat that's worse when it doesn't attack you — it just watches you go, already knowing you'll be back.

**Why:** Sequoia's spec: "staccato internal monologue: 'My turn. That's odd. That's not good.' Short. Declarative. Each thought is a complete assessment." A general observation about the *nature* of threats is contemplative and literary — Calmer or Zephyr might make it. Sequoia observes facts, then acts. This sentence is three beats past where he'd stop.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The wraith didn't move as we cleared its territory. That was worse."

### [2] Mechanical scan A — em-dash (clause connector)

> There's a particular kind of threat that's worse when it doesn't attack you — it just watches you go, already knowing you'll be back.

**Why:** Narration-level em-dash connecting two independent clauses. Period serves the staccato rhythm better: "...doesn't attack you. It just watches you go, already knowing you'll be back."

### [3] Mechanical scan A — em-dash pair (emphatic repetition)

> Behind them, ghouls — ghouls — dragging looted weapons, threading the same passage with the same purposeful efficiency.

**Why:** Emphatic repetition via em-dash pair. The repetition is right for the moment, but the dashes enclose it in a way that reads as literary rather than staccato. Period break preserves the beat more cleanly in Sequoia's voice.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Behind them, ghouls. Ghouls. Dragging looted weapons, threading the same passage with the same purposeful efficiency."

### [4] Register-wrong vocabulary

> These aren't aesthetic details. They're a map of what happened and who survived it.

**Why:** Sequoia's spec: "practical vocabulary: no metaphors, no flourish." "Aesthetic details" is an art-criticism category he wouldn't reach for. He'd use a word for things that don't matter, not a word for their sensory quality.
**Suggested rewrite:** "These aren't decoration. They're a map of what happened and who survived it."

### [5] Register-wrong vocabulary

> That was a problem we hadn't designed for yet.

**Why:** "Designed for" implies architectural planning in advance. Sequoia's spec says he doesn't "design a four-step combo that depends on enemies standing where he needs them" — he reads and responds. "Designed for" reads as Zephyr's administrative vocabulary (Zephyr proposes elections and thinks about institutional structure).
**Suggested rewrite:** "That was a problem we hadn't accounted for yet." or "That was outside the plan."

## Verdict

The scene's best moves — "I was reading the floor before I was reading the room," "They were shopping in it," the Kelno read, and "I filed it under *both* and kept walking" — are excellent Sequoia: flat delivery, one-liner assessment, forward orientation. The flags concentrate in the wraith-clearing paragraph (too literary) and two vocabulary instances. Spot-edit; the Kelno sequence doesn't need to change.
