## Flags

### [1] Mechanical scan A — em-dash (appositive)

> I catalogued them the way I catalogue everything — automatically, without sentiment.

**Why:** Narration-level em-dash before an adverb pair. Comma serves better and avoids the emphatic weight the dash adds unnecessarily: "I catalogued them the way I catalogue everything, automatically, without sentiment."

### [2] Register-wrong vocabulary — academic economics

> Information asymmetry. The oldest problem.

**Why:** "Information asymmetry" is game-theory / economic vocabulary. Zinnia's register is spy tradecraft: "leverage," "cover," "operational security," "the mouse." The concept is accurate for Zinnia — he can't get the information he needs — but the label he'd reach for comes from tradecraft, not economics.
**Suggested rewrite:** "A language gap with nothing to translate through. No way in." or "Locked. No bridge, no leverage."

### [3] Tell-not-show / over-explained silence

> I did not say anything. There was nothing to say that the situation hadn't already said.

**Why:** Zinnia's spec: "observations stated as finished reads, not reasoned conclusions." He arrives at things; he doesn't explain his arrivals. The second sentence explains his silence — but Zinnia's narration doesn't justify its own silences. The silence is the narration. Everything else his assessment generated is already in the preceding paragraph.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I didn't say anything." (stop there; let the next paragraph pick up with action)

## Verdict

Strong scene overall. "I always notice doors," "Asset or threat, TBD," "Move south. The steam gives cover," and the observation about the self-opening door are textbook Zinnia: spy vocabulary, terse, arrived-at, perceptive before investigative. The main flag is "information asymmetry" — academic economics in a tradecraft voice, one phrase. The over-explained silence is a secondary spot-edit. Neither warrants re-narration.
