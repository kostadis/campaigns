## Flags

### [1] Mechanical scan A — em-dash (appositive pair)

> A single pulse of guiding radiance — the kind of divine light that doesn't ask permission — and the double doors at the barricade simply *stopped existing as an obstacle*.

**Why:** Narration-level em-dash pair around an appositive clause. The parenthetical interrupts the drive of the sentence.
**Suggested rewrite:** "A single pulse of guiding radiance, the kind of divine light that doesn't ask permission, and the double doors at the barricade simply *stopped existing as an obstacle*."

### [2] Mechanical scan B — register-wrong vocabulary

> I've taken hits that did more structural damage.

**Why:** "Structural" is engineering vocabulary. Zinnia's spec specifies a perceptual, not analytical, cognitive mode — his self-assessments arrive as finished reads, not clinical evaluations of damage type.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I've taken hits that cost me more." or "I've taken hits that did worse."

### [3] Register-wrong vocabulary / bureaucratic metaphor

> the recalibration that happens automatically when your body files a formal complaint

**Why:** Two issues in one clause. "Recalibration" is calibration-instrument vocabulary; "files a formal complaint" is HR/bureaucratic metaphor. Neither belongs to Zinnia's spy-tradecraft register or his body-knows-before-mind cognitive mode. His dry humor runs toward operational absurdity, not institutional absurdity.
**Suggested rewrite:** "the adjustment that happens when your body starts disagreeing with your decisions" or "the automatic correction when you've absorbed something you weren't supposed to."

## Verdict

Strong scene — the geometry vocabulary is confirmed Zinnia (the voice spec explicitly quotes him saying "That's geometry"), and the operational throughline ("the hinge of the barricade," "kill zone you personally created," "operational hygiene") is exactly right. The two register-wrong instances — "structural damage" and "files a formal complaint" — are isolated. Spot-edit only; no re-narration needed.
