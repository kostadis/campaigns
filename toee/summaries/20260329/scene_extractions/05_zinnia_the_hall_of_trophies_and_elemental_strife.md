**Entering the Great Hall**
The party passed through a massive ruined hall — the floor strewn with bones and the wreckage of furniture, the walls hung with tapestries depicting scenes of raiding and violence. Grim trophies decorated the stone: mummified heads, torn battle standards, battered shields, a broken broadsword. Broad steps of dull black stone descended from the north wall into shadow.
The hall had once been the scene of great and terrible celebrations. Now it was a ruin, and the silence of it pressed down.

---

**The Elemental Battle**
Nearby, fire snakes and a wounded salamander were locked in brutal combat with four water gargoyles, eruptions of scalding steam billowing between them. The creatures tore at each other without pause or reason Zinnia could discern.
There was no way in and no way to make them stop — only the violence itself, filling the chamber with heat and noise.

---

**Zephyr Attempts to Interrogate the Elementals**
Thomas (as Zephyr): "I wanna find one guy and ask him where the deep tempo is."
GM: "So, you notice that these are not humans… Does anybody here speak Primordial?"
Thomas (as Zephyr): "Speak goblin."
GM: "Alright, well, so you can't speak to the gargoyles. That's a problem."
Thomas (as Zephyr): "Alright. This plan is done. Britain."
No words could cross the gap between the party and the elemental creatures — not Common, not Goblin, nothing. Whatever knowledge they carried, it was locked away behind a language no one present could speak.

---

**Slipping Away Under Cover of Steam**
With no way to communicate and no reason to wade into a fight that wasn't theirs, the party moved south, using the billowing clouds of scalding steam as cover to slip away before any of the creatures turned their attention outward.
The heat clung to Zinnia's skin as she went, and the sounds of the battle faded behind them into the haze.
