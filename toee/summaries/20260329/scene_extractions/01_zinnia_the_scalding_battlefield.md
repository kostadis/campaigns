**Arriving on the Battlefield**
The party emerged onto the second level of the temple into a corridor thick with billowing magical steam. The air was scalding — bodies from both the Fire and Earth temples were already strewn across the stone floor. Through the haze, Fire Temple bugbears had dragged pews and stone rubble into a barricade across the stairway, holding back a stalled Earth Temple advance. The heat was punishing, the visibility poor, and the sounds of battle echoed from every direction.

**Zinnia Charges the Cultist**
While Zephyr opened with a precise bow shot from the mist, Zinnia didn't wait. She rushed forward into the scalding haze and drove into the Fire Temple cultist at the front of the line — a series of swift strikes, and the cultist went down before the bugbears even understood what had hit them. The kill was clean and fast, and for a moment, the chaos bent in the party's favor.

**The Bugbear's Counterstrike**
The bugbears retaliated with savage fury. One landed a devastating blow on Zinnia — a critical hit that dealt 25 damage and left her reeling, the impact jarring through her whole body. The same furious counterattack caught Zephyr and Sequoia as well, but Zinnia had taken the worst of it. The battlefield had answered her aggression.

**Calmer Breaks the Doors**
A single thunderous flash of divine light — Calmer channeled a bolt of guiding radiance directly into the barricaded double doors, and they smashed open. Earth Temple forces poured through the gap, crashing into the Fire Temple line. The stalemate was broken in an instant.

**The Barricade Falls**
With the doors shattered and the Earth Temple flooding through, the remaining bugbears were caught between two warring factions. Zephyr cut down one with a flurry of precise strikes. Sequoia delivered a crushing blow that finished another. The barricade that had held the advance was gone.

**Deciding to Retreat**
The magical steam wasn't dissipating — if anything, it was thickening as the elemental violence escalated. The battle was no longer theirs to control. With the mission accomplished and the environment growing increasingly lethal, the party made the call: disengage, and get out before the crossfire swallowed them.
