**Entering the Prisoner Chamber**
The party moved northeast through the corridors and found a chamber full of chained human prisoners huddled against the wall, trembling in their shackles.
GM as Prisoners: "Please, please don't leave us to die!"
The sight of living captives — clearly gathered as sacrifices or subjects for dark rituals — was a stark contrast to the battlefield carnage the party had been moving through.

**Zephyr Asks About the Deep Temple**
Thomas Kolivakis as Zephyr: "Can I ask them about the Deep Temple?"
One prisoner alone responded, immediately setting his terms before anyone could press further.
GM as Knowing Prisoner: "I know about the Deep Temple, but I'm only going to talk if you let… if you get me out of here."

**Zinnia Reads the Prisoner**
Zinnia watched the man carefully, studying him with a trained eye. His Insight roll came back at 24 — the prisoner was telling the truth.
With the information confirmed as genuine, the path forward was clear.

**Sequoia Picks the Shackles**
Sequoia stepped forward and worked the iron shackles. The lock gave way on a roll of 27 — clean and unhesitating.
The prisoner's chains fell free.

**The Other Prisoners Erupt**
GM as Remaining Prisoners: "What about us, what about us?"
Thomas Kolivakis as Zephyr: "You'll have your turn. Give me 2 seconds."
Sequoia had freed one man; four others were still chained to the wall, and their desperation filled the chamber.

**The Freed Prisoner Takes Stock**
Now free and surrounded by battle-worn operatives, the former Air Temple cultist sized up the situation with wary eyes.
GM as Freed Prisoner: "The Deep Temple? What do you want to know about the Deep Temple?"
Thomas Kolivakis as Zephyr: "How do you get there?"

**The Prisoner Finds the Question Absurd**
GM as Freed Prisoner: "Because nobody wants to get to the Deep Temple. Everybody wants to not… not ever be near the Deep Temple, but… since you asked…"
Thomas Kolivakis as Zephyr: "Why are you laughing?"
The man's dark humor faded when Calmer's presence made itself fully felt.

**Calmer Breaks the Prisoner's Resistance**
Zephyr's initial intimidation attempt had landed flat — a roll of 4, barely convincing. But Calmer's imposing presence granted a reroll, and the second attempt came back at 19.
GM as Freed Prisoner: "I guess I would be pushing my luck if I insisted for more help, wouldn't I? My mother never raised… didn't raise me to be a fool. Alright, so here's the secret door to the Greater Temple."
The prisoner pointed out the exact location on the party's map — a hidden passage inside the Air Temple, knowledge he had drawn from Kelno himself.
The Obsidian Edge finally had the route to the Deep Temple, but reaching it meant passing the wraith again — and somewhere behind them, four prisoners were still chained to the wall.
