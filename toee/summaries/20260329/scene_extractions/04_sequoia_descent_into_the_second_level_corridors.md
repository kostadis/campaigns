**Short Rest Before the Descent**
The party paused in their own territory to tend their wounds before pushing deeper into the temple. Calmer recovered ground after the brutal retreat through the Air Temple. The corridors beyond were still alive with the sounds of war — distant steel, shouting, the hiss of elemental steam — and the stone floors offered no comfort.
The calm before the next descent, brief and uneasy.

---

**Passing the Wraith Again**
Moving back through the Air Temple on their way to the lower corridors, the wraith drifted into view once more — the same cold presence that had already struck Calmer across the back and laughed at his fleeing form. It held its domain with the same hollow menace. The party did not stop to engage it. They pressed past the edge of its territory and kept moving, and the wraith turned back once they cleared its ground.
The creature's patience felt worse than its violence — it simply waited, knowing they would have to come back.

---

**The Stone Floors and the Signs of Looting**
Descending into the dimly lit second-level corridors, the atmosphere thickened. The air was cooler here, carrying the smell of blood and old stone. Broken bottles lay scattered across the floor, and discarded armor had been shoved against the walls. Blood trails led away into shadow and simply stopped. The sounds of the larger battle were muffled but present — a war happening in every direction at once.
The corridors felt like the aftermath of something still ongoing.

---

**Goblins and Ghouls in the Secret Passages**
Through the haze, movement caught the party's eye — goblins dragging bags of coin through a hidden door, alongside ghouls hauling looted weapons. They were moving with purpose, threading through passages the party had only just learned to notice. Zinnia read them clearly: these were Kelno's. The meek Air Temple cleric, who had barely registered as a threat, had been quietly sending his people into the chaos to strip the dead while the other temples bled each other out.
The realization landed with grim weight — all four temples were now in this, and Kelno had been playing his own game the entire time.

---

**Confirming the Four-Faction War**
Zephyr put it plainly:
Zephyr: "So… We've done a good job. Right."
GM: "So all four temples are now involved in that."
Zephyr: "So, perfect for us to go down to the lower temple."
Sequoia had watched the goblins vanish into the passage and understood what it meant. The engineered war had grown past what the party had designed — it was no longer three factions, and Kelno had not been idle. The Obsidian Edge had lit the fire, but other hands were already reaching into the flames.
