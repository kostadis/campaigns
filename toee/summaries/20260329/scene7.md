## Zephyr — Beckoned

The freed prisoner's directions were good. The door was real, the location confirmed, the path to the Deep Temple laid out in words Zephyr had turned over in his head until they sat flush and solid, like a key he could feel the weight of even when he wasn't touching it.

He'd had to work for those words. Not elegantly.

The interrogation hadn't been his finest hour — he'd leaned on the man harder than necessary, all bravado and borrowed threat, standing at the full height the horns gave him and hoping the prisoner would fill in the rest. The man had. Fear is efficient that way. Zephyr didn't find it satisfying exactly, but satisfying wasn't the point. The point was the information, and now he had it. Another notch in the victory belt, even if the notching had been sloppy.

So. The door existed. They knew where it was.

The moment he turned the location over in his mind — corridor junction, left passage, counting stones from the bracket — was the moment the runner found them.

An Earth Temple runner, collar damp with effort, mouth already open before he'd finished stopping. Hartsch wanted them. Hartsch wanted his Obsidian Edge assembled, upstairs, immediately. War council. The summons had the texture of *now*.

Zephyr looked at the runner, then at the corridor beyond him, then at nothing in particular.

He filed the directions away in the same place he kept everything he planned to collect later: the Rhennee debt, Captain Tolub's ship schedule, the exact weight of every favor he was owed. The Deep Temple wasn't going anywhere. Presumably.

The weight of it was the thing he didn't say aloud. They had it — finally had it — and now they had to go back up. Because Hartsch whistled and the Obsidian Edge came running, because that was the arrangement they had made, because Hartsch was useful and useful things required maintenance. The alliance was real. The leash attached to it was also real.

*This again.* 

Not prejudice this time. Something else. The particular indignity of belonging to someone else's chain of command while you were trying to build your own.

He let the runner see him receiving the message calmly. Unhurried. The kind of man who goes where he chooses, and who happens to be choosing this. He gestured for the others to follow and started walking.

The door's location sat behind his eyes like a lit coal.
