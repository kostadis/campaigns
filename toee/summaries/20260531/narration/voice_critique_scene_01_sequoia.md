## Flags

### [1] Generic prose — slight lyrical drift

> Somewhere below us the war we'd lit was still burning — fire in the southern corridors, water finding the low ground, the long groan of stone settling under a fight nobody could win.

**Why:** "water finding the low ground" anthropomorphizes water evocatively, and "the long groan of stone settling" is atmospheric description. The spec is explicit: no metaphors, no flourish. Sequoia notices things in terms of tactical relevance, not their sound or poetry. He also wouldn't naturally land on "groan of stone" — he'd notice a structural failure, not how it sounds.

**Suggested rewrite:** Somewhere below us the war we'd lit was still burning — fire in the southern corridors, water in the low passages, and the creak of something structural nobody had stopped yet. We had built that.

---

### [2] Slight self-dramatization

> We had built that. We had stood in this exact hall and decided who died first.

**Why:** "decided who died first" puts rhetorical weight on what Sequoia would state flat. The spec flags self-aggrandizement as something he never does; he leads by doing, not by claiming to lead. The first sentence ("We had built that.") is already the right Sequoia beat — clean, accurate, closed. The second sentence amplifies it into something closer to a speech.

**Suggested rewrite:** We had built that. (Cut the second sentence, or replace with: "We had built that, and now someone above us owned the rubble.")

---

## Verdict

The scene is tight and well-matched to Sequoia's voice. "I had not, until that exact moment, been an employee" and "We came to destroy this place. We got jobs." are exactly the right flat, ironic closings. Both flags are minor — the lyrical description being the stronger of the two. Spot-edit rather than re-narrate.
