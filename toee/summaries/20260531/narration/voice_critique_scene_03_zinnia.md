## Flags

### [1] Voice spec conflict — explicit reasoning chain where Zinnia arrives

> The rest came right behind it, the way these things do — not reasoning, just arrival. I have spent enough time inside the criminal mind, and we have all spent more than enough time now inside the demonic one. Where do you hide what you can't afford to lose, in a temple that worships flame? You put it where no one sane would reach. You stuff it in the fire.

**Why:** The spec is unambiguous: "What he does NOT do: reason through to a conclusion step by step. He arrives." The paragraph walks the reader through the logic explicitly — inside the criminal mind → inside the demonic mind → therefore hide where no one reaches → therefore in the fire. This is exactly the step-by-step process the spec forbids. In the examples, Zinnia's equivalent moment is *Gotcha.* (finding the blood trail) or *There must be a secret door somewhere here.* — arrived at, not reasoned to. The *Gotcha.* that follows this paragraph is the correct Zinnia beat; the reasoning chain before it is the wrong one.

**Suggested rewrite:** Collapse to: *Gotcha.* / The treasure chest was in the pit. — or at most add one observation-not-inference before the italicized line: "There was only one place in a temple that worships fire where you put something you can't afford to lose." Then *Gotcha.*

---

### [2] Self-conscious spec explanation

> The rest came right behind it, the way these things do — not reasoning, just arrival.

**Why:** The phrase "not reasoning, just arrival" describes Zinnia's cognitive mode rather than demonstrating it. In the per-character examples, Zinnia never explains how he knows — he simply knows. "I catalogued them the way I catalogue everything — automatically, without sentiment" (examples, Hall of Trophies) is the closest analog, and that phrase describes the *action* of cataloguing, not the epistemology of arriving. "Not reasoning, just arrival" paraphrases the voice spec in a way that reads as the narration explaining itself.

**Suggested rewrite:** Cut this phrase entirely, or replace with a sensory beat that enacts the arrival: "The rest came right behind it, on instinct, the way a read finishes before you know you started it."

---

### [3] Convergence with Zephyr — financial register

> The treasure chest was in the pit. I'd have wagered the whole take on it.

**Why:** "Wagered the whole take" is Zephyr's idiom — financial framing, gambling metaphor, ledger language. Zinnia's register is operational: he'd stake his cover, his position, his read. Not a wager. In the examples, Zinnia's confidence markers are *Gotcha* and "I filed it" and "Asset or threat, TBD" — operational, not financial.

**Suggested rewrite:** The treasure chest was in the pit. I'd have staked my cover on it.

---

## Verdict

The entry into the Fire Temple is Zinnia's strongest scene in this session — the room-reading is excellent, and "I stopped reading the room as inventory and started reading it as a problem" is exactly right. The core issue is the explicit reasoning chain in flag [1], which is a structural problem (not just a line fix) — if the narration is keeping the logical walkthrough, that paragraph needs to be revoiced as an atmospheric sense rather than a deduction. Flags [2] and [3] are single-line edits.
