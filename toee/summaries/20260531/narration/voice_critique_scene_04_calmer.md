## Flags

### [1] Generic atmospheric simile — wrong register

> a thin wall of fog, not deep, not large, drifting across the floor like the chamber had exhaled

**Why:** "Like the chamber had exhaled" is evocative atmospheric description. Calmer's spec is clear about his register: he's tactical and reasoning-based, not atmospheric. He notes things for their authority and consequence relevance ("Perhaps we wish to dump water on it?" — tactical hypothesis). Environmental atmosphere belongs to Zinnia's scene-reading mode. Here, Calmer waited for the chest to appear in the fog, and when nothing appeared, the narration's energy should be on his assessment, not the fog's quality.

**Suggested rewrite:** The water hit the coals and came back up as steam. I studied it. Nothing in the fog told me anything. The chest did not reveal itself.

---

### [2] Generic tactile simile — again off-register

> The fire pit sat in the center of the chamber, coals glowing under a heat that pressed against my plate like a hand.

**Why:** A second environmental simile in the same scene: "pressed against my plate like a hand" is a stock tactile description (heat as physical presence). Both similes in this scene are the kind of image any narrator might produce. Calmer's opening move is usually tactical or self-aggrandizing, not atmospheric. His spec: he reads situations in terms of whose call it is, what the reasoning was, who failed to brief him.

**Suggested rewrite:** The fire pit sat in the center of the chamber, coals glowing, the heat already inside my armor by the time I'd taken two steps. I assessed it. My expertise in the criminal and the demonic mind had already begun forming conclusions.

---

### [3] Tell-not-show — abstract tactical naming

> I felt the moment slip, the opening that should have been ours going to them instead.

**Why:** "I felt the moment slip" names the failure in abstract terms. Calmer's spec says he deflects blame ("Zephyr told me to do it") and pivots to pomposity ("Gentlemen, why didn't any of you tell me this was going to happen?"). The abstract "moment slipping" is generic narrator language for losing initiative; Calmer would frame this differently — as a failure of advisors or an unexpected tactical development. The spec: "brief in complaint, elaborate in reasoning."

**Suggested rewrite:** We were all caught flat-footed. Every one of us. "Gentlemen," I said, "why didn't any of you tell me this was going to happen?" Nobody had an answer, because nobody had one.

## Verdict

Scene 04 is the best Calmer scene in the session — the VTT-derived dialogue lands perfectly, the reasoning-about-the-fire-temple logic is exactly his voice, and "*But not me.*" as the closing beat is excellent. Both atmospheric simile flags ([1] and [2]) are the same recurring issue: the narration slips into sensory/atmospheric description when Calmer's register is tactical and self-referential. Either cut the similes or replace them with Calmer noting the tactical implications instead.
