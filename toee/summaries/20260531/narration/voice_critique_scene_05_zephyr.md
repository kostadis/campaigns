# Voice Critique — Zephyr, scene 05: The Fire Temple Skirmish

**Narration:** summaries/20260531/narration/session_doc_scene_05_the_fire_temple_skirmish.scrubbed.md
**Voice spec:** voice/zephyr_voice.md
**Per-char examples:** examples/political_maneuvering.md (Wraith Negotiation section)

---

## Flags

### [1] Factual error — pronoun

> She knows the rule. I watched her take it.

**Why:** Zinnia uses he/him pronouns throughout every campaign document — voice spec, gm-assist narratives, and all grounding docs. "She/her" is a clean error, not a voice issue, but it will propagate into assembled documents and attribution.

**Suggested rewrite:** He knows the rule. I watched him take it.

---

### [2] Mixed metaphor — incoherent sensory / temporal blend

> the corridor filled with the smell of a war we'd started two levels up arriving on schedule down here

**Why:** "Smell of a war" is a sensory frame; "arriving on schedule" is a financial/temporal frame. The two don't combine — wars don't arrive on schedule in a sensory sense, and schedules don't smell. In Zephyr's spec, combat descriptions are staccato ("Thud. One gone.") or financially framed ("every ounce of patience cashing out at once"). The mixed metaphor is reaching for evocative effect and landing on something muddy.

**Suggested rewrite:** Salamanders. Bodies of moving heat. The Fire Temple, finally collecting on the debt we owed it.

---

### [3] Sensory confusion

> And then we stopped, the survivors bloodied, the corridor stinking of cold and char.

**Why:** Cold does not have a smell. "Stinking of cold" is either a confused sensory image or a metaphor that doesn't land. "Char" has a smell; cold is the absence of heat, not a scent. This reads as generic atmosphere-setting rather than Zephyr's sharp physical observation.

**Suggested rewrite:** And then we stopped, the survivors bloodied, the corridor reeking of char and burned scale and the wet-iron smell of Calmer's frost still dissipating in the air.

---

### [4] Unclear referent — weak scene close

> "Yep, yep," I said. "Guess they're in this place."

**Why:** "They're in this place" has no clear referent in context. The preceding lines are about leadership and the water. The sentence reads as a VTT fragment preserved without its surrounding context. Zephyr's scene-closing voice per spec and examples is either a wry observation, a financial one-liner, or a dry internal summary ("The tiefling kept walking"). This closing deflates the scene into confusion rather than irony.

**Suggested rewrite:** Cut or replace. The scene's natural closing is "Amateur hour, boss. That's what happens when you promote a man above his qualifications." — end there, or follow it with a one-line Zephyr internal note: *I made a note to brief him before the next one.*

---

## Verdict

The combat narration in scene 05 is Zephyr at his best — "Are they made of fire, or only wearing it?" and "every ounce of patience cashing out at once" and "The tiefling breathes" are exactly the right register. The four flags are all fixable: [1] is a factual correction, [2] and [3] are prose clarity issues, and [4] is a weak close that either needs to be cut or replaced with a real Zephyr landing line.
