## Strongest Recurring Theme: Atmospheric Simile Drift

The most consistent issue across all five scenes is **atmospheric / sensory description slipping into scenes where it doesn't belong to the narrator's register**. This shows up differently per character:

- **Calmer (scene 04):** Two similes ("pressed against my plate like a hand," "like the chamber had exhaled") that are evocative but belong to Zinnia's room-reading mode, not Calmer's tactical-reasoning mode.
- **Zephyr (scene 05):** "stinking of cold" (sensory confusion) and the mixed metaphor opening.
- **Sequoia (scene 01):** "water finding the low ground, the long groan of stone settling" — lyrical image where Sequoia would give flat tactical notation.
- **Zinnia (scene 03):** The reasoning chain (flag [1]) is the more serious issue; the Zephyr-register intrusion ("wagered the whole take") is the simile variant.

This pattern suggests the model is defaulting to evocative sensory description when transitioning into or out of action beats, regardless of which character is narrating. The fix is consistent: replace atmospheric similes with the narrator's actual mode of perception — Calmer's authority-framing, Sequoia's flat notation, Zephyr's financial register, Zinnia's operational vocabulary.
