# A Bargain in the Air Temple

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party explores the Air Temple and encounters Kelno, a cunning survivor who offers a strategic alliance.
- The party decides to explore a southwest passageway in the Air Temple while Calmer recovers elsewhere.
- Zephyr leads the group through a curtained doorway into a spartan, gray-carpeted room where they find Kelno waiting for them, seated in a chair: "come, sit down, I've been waiting for you."
- The room is described in detail: gray carpeting, plaster, wall hangings, dimly lit by grayish metal lamps, with shelves holding foodstuffs, scrolls, books, and several human and demi-human skulls, and a narrow bed in the northeast corner.
- Kelno appears very old, very tired, and very cunning; he taps a piece of paper and lays out the situation: "assassins reached Hartsch's antechamber an hour ago. By tonight, your puppet is meat. Alrrem is on his pyre as we speak, he won't last until the morning. That leaves me and both Belsornig, and most interestingly, you."
- The party sits "silently and menacingly," which Kelno appreciates.
- Kelno explains the prize: "When this war ends, and the great doors open. Somebody is going to be standing on top of the upper temple, and whoever it is becomes the greater temple's new partner up here. That is going to be you."
- He frames the alliance bluntly: "So you can kill me. I have the worst guards in the dungeon... but then you walk into the greater temple alone with no air temple, no goblin eyes in the dark, no bribery network. Or, you walk out of here with me as your number two. I run the Air Temple. Under you. My goblins are your goblins. My informants are your informants. You get the throne. I get to keep my office. ... You are obviously the senior partner."
- When pressed on his loyalties, Kelno deflects with a smile: "Which temple? I, of course, swear loyalty to the power of air." Asked whether he is for chaos or order, he replies, "Yes, I am for chaos, but I'm also for living."
- Kelno reveals the unwritten rules: when two of the four prophets are killed, the Greater Temple intervenes and installs whoever stands on top as its new partner; the last competition was won by the former head of the Earth Temple.
- Zephyr concludes, "we must align ourselves with the victories," and Sequoia declares, "We are the victors."
- Kelno shows the party Belsornig's note (signed "by the cannon of the crushing wave"), detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and instructions for his agents to withdraw to the steam corridors and avoid the Obsidian Edge — "Do not engage. They are not to be killed." Nick (Sequoia) asks, "Are you trying to kill us?"
- George (Zinnia) urges caution: "We have to be suspicious of him."
- Seeing the choices, the party decides to let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig in the Water Temple; Kelno responds, "very well."
- The group searches Kelno's office under the pretext of a formality — "since you're a new partner, I just wanna know... Do you have anything hiding around here?" — finding a ledger of payments and the note revealing he had been anticipating their arrival for two weeks.
- The party instructs Kelno to post guards so no one enters the Water Temple while they conduct their business; he agrees.
