# Entry into the Water Temple

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party enters the atmospheric and eerie Water Temple, preparing for a stealthy assault.
- The party enters a massive 40-by-90-foot hall with a 30-foot arched ceiling and walls of polished azerite malachite that glow with a soft, cloudy greenish luminosity, making the chamber feel like the bottom of the sea.
- They observe a large bronze altar sculpted with marine creatures, its concave top filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells.
- A horrific serpentine idol depicting a writhing mass of fish, eels, and octopi stands in a 20-foot alcove near the altar, its rear wall covered by a drapery fashioned from seaweed.
- Four triangular plinths in the corners of the hall each bear a bronze gargoyle-like statue coated in verdigris.
- The party resolves to touch nothing, the DM noting it was "not the time and place to pull for treasure."
- The group successfully sneaks through the hall without alerting anyone (Zephyr rolling 22, Zinnia 19).
- The party reaches a door to the west (choosing it over the eastern door to avoid passing through more rooms), and attempts to smash it open to surprise the cultists within, but Zinnia's attempt fails twice (rolling 7 each time), alerting the guards inside.
