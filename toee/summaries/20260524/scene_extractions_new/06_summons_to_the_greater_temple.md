# Summons to the Greater Temple

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Following the collapse of the elemental prophets, the party is summoned to the depths of the Greater Temple to meet their new overseers.
- As the party prepares to climb the stairs in the Air Temple to return to their quarters, a large group of bugbears wearing the black of the Greater Temple arrive from multiple directions — the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door — surrounding the party and revealing the power of the Greater Temple.
- The bugbears gruffly announce that Barkinar wishes to speak to the new rulers of the Upper Temple; when Zephyr says, "Yes, we will go see him," a bugbear corrects him: "he will see you."
- The bugbears inquire about the whereabouts of the cleric; Zephyr says, "I will summon him," and assures them, "he's hurt, but he will be fine." A bugbear confirms, "The cleric is in charge, of course."
- Zephyr offers the bugbears meat — "Are you hungry? ... Would you like some meat?"
- A successful Insight check (26) reveals to the party that the Greater Temple had forces not just below but on this level as well, hidden in the unexplored portions of the map.
- A disembodied voice from the stairs leading down calls out: "That was efficient. Come down, bring your puppet master if he is alive. We should talk."
- Barkinar brings the injured Calmer down to join the party, assuming he is the new chief cleric of the Earth Temple.
- The party descends into a large, intimidating chamber guarded by a hill giant and ogre bodyguards, where Kelno is brought to face Barkinar, who smiles at him.
- Barkinar commends the party for eliminating Romag, Hartsch, Alrrem, and Belsornig.
- The party is officially recognized as the new rulers of the Upper Temple, inheriting control over leaderless cultists in the Fire Temple, trolls on contract in the Water Temple, an unaccounted treasury, and mercenaries who need a chain of command.
- The official mentions the Chest of Rao (previously delivered to Romag) that the Greater Temple has interest in recovering, warning that while none of it is urgent enough to "flay you alive tomorrow," the upper temple should "look organized" by the next check-in.
- The party is warned to be wary of the ambitious and paranoid Lord Wizard Senshock — "Be civil, and let him underestimate you."
- A wizard named Falrinth, who has been hiding from the Greater Temple for his own reasons, is mentioned as someone to be discussed later: "For now, organize your house. We'll be in touch."
- Barkinar departs, speaking quietly to one of his ogre bodyguards before walking away, giving the party one week to organize the upper temple before the next check-in.
- Out of character, the DM congratulates the party on the most surprising outcome of the adventure he has ever run, and awards everyone a level. The players muse about embracing their new roles, with Zephyr noting, "I might turn chaotic evil, we'll see."
