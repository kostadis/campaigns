# Chapter 27 Dungeon Boss!

Date: May 24th, 2026

## Summary
With Calmer badly wounded and carried off to safety by Captain Dren Halveth and his mercenaries, the remaining members of the party — Zephyr, Sequioa, and Zinnia — slipped through the secret door that had been exposed during their skirmish with the gnolls and descended into the Air Temple. The passage opened into a sparse, gray-carpeted room lit by dull metal lamps, its shelves lined with scrolls, foodstuffs, and the skulls of the dead. There, seated in one of two upholstered chairs beside a small table, was an old man — ancient even by the standards of evil cultists — who looked up at them with tired, cunning eyes and said simply, "Come, sit down. I've been waiting for you."

The man introduced himself as Kelno, the leader of the Air Temple, and he wasted no time laying out the situation with the precision of someone who had been rehearsing the conversation for weeks. He explained that the party's arrival and promotion of Hartsch had triggered a war between the prophets, a war that would only end when two of the four prophets were dead. Acknowledging Calmer as the new Prophet of Earth, Kelno pointed out that the party had to choose who to keep alive. He revealed that Belsornig had already dispatched assassins to Hartsch's antechamber, and that Alrrem was already dying. As the weakest of the prophets, Kelno offered no negotiation: he would clearly be their number two. He slid a note across the table, a message from Belsornig himself, detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and containing a chilling instruction: if the group known as the Obsidian Edge appeared, his agents were to withdraw and not engage. Kelno's offer was blunt and practical — kill Belsornig, and Kelno would serve as their subordinate, offering his goblins, his informants, and his network in exchange for his life and his office. As the party searched his quarters, they found a ledger of payments and, more tellingly, a note indicating that Kelno had set out a second chair and had been waiting for them for two weeks, having anticipated their arrival from the very moment they first appeared in the temple.

Seeing the choices in front of them, the party decided the best option was to let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig, agreeing to the deal not a moment too soon. Captain Dren arrived with urgent news: Hartsch was finished, Belsornig's forces were at his door, and Belsornig himself was pulling his treasury out through the south route — vulnerable, exposed, and distracted. The party ordered Dren to remain behind with his mercenaries to guard Calmer, and then they moved toward the Water Temple.

The Water Temple was a breathtaking and unsettling place. Its massive hall stretched forty feet wide and ninety feet long beneath a vaulted ceiling thirty feet overhead, its walls and floors covered in polished azerite malachite that swirled with deep blue and green. Some enchantment had been placed upon the stone, causing a soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity to seep from the walls and floor, making the entire chamber feel as though it lay at the bottom of the sea. At the center stood a great bronze altar sculpted with marine creatures, its concave top forming a shallow basin filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells. In an alcove nearby loomed a horrific serpentine idol — eight feet high and nearly as broad — depicting a writhing, amorphous mass of fish, eels, octopi, and other marine monsters, while four bronze gargoyle statues coated in verdigris watched from atop corner plinths. The air smelled of the sea, and the silence was heavy.

The party moved through the hall without a sound and reached a door to the west. Their attempt to smash it open failed, and the guards within were alerted. Rather than panic, the party improvised — they claimed to be messengers bearing news of the assassination of Alrrem, and the guards, believing them to be allies, opened the door. The moment it swung wide, the party struck. Sequioa and Zinnia cut down the first cult fanatic in a rapid, brutal exchange, while Zephyr delivered a devastating blow to the second, dropping him before he could raise a meaningful defense. The way to Belsornig's inner sanctum was open.

Moving with exceptional care, the party crept into the chamber where Belsornig stood, preoccupied and completely unaware that his guards were already dead. Kelno had previously entrusted the group with a Dagger of Venom he had been saving for exactly this purpose, and now the moment had come. The party fell upon Belsornig in a coordinated storm of strikes — Sequioa, Zinnia, and Zephyr working in lethal tandem — and before the Prophet of Water could mount any real resistance, Zephyr drove the poisoned blade home. As Belsornig fell, a deep gong resonated through the entire level. The party moved to get back to their quarters on the first level, heading to the Air Temple. As they were about to climb up the stairs, a group of bugbears dressed in black arrived from multiple directions — through the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door — announcing that the Greater Temple wished to speak to the victors. The sheer number of bugbears surrounding them made the party realize the true power of the Greater Temple in an instant. The fact that the bugbears arrived through the eastern and western doors reinforced the notion that the Greater Temple was on the second level, leading the party to conclude they had come from areas they had not yet explored.

The bugbears inquired about the whereabouts of the cleric, and the party assured them he was injured but would recover. Then a voice called out from the stairs leading down — cold, composed, and unmistakably powerful — commending the party's efficiency and inviting them to descend. Barkinar, the temple leader, brought the injured Calmer down to join them, and together the party entered a vast, heavily guarded chamber deep in the Greater Temple, where Barkinar stood flanked by ogre bodyguards and a hill giant. Kelno was brought to face Barkinar, who smiled at him. Barkinar assumed Calmer was the new chief cleric of the Earth Temple and addressed him as such, looking at them with something approaching genuine admiration and said, "Romag. Hartsch. Alrrem. Problems I had on a list yesterday morning, and four problems gone by sundown. You will forgive me if I'm impressed." He declared the upper temple theirs, outlined the work ahead — leaderless cultists, trolls on contract, a treasury to account for, mercenaries in need of a chain of command, and a chest previously delivered to Romag that the Greater Temple had a particular interest in recovering. He warned them to be civil toward the Lord Wizard Senshock, who was ambitious and paranoid but useful, and to let him underestimate them. A wizard named Falrinth, hiding from the Greater Temple for reasons of his own, was mentioned as a matter to be discussed later. Then, without waiting for a response, Barkinar turned, spoke quietly to one of his ogre bodyguards, and walked back down into the dark — leaving the party standing in the heart of the very temple they had come to destroy, now its rulers.

# Chapter 27: Dungeon Boss!

Date: May 24th, 2026

## Summary
With Calmer badly wounded and carried off to safety by Captain Dren Halveth and his mercenaries, the remaining members of the party — Zephyr, Sequoia, and Zinnia — slipped through the secret door that had been exposed during their skirmish with the gnolls and descended into the Air Temple. The passage opened into a sparse, gray-carpeted room lit by dull metal lamps of grayish metal, its shelves lined with scrolls, foodstuffs, and the skulls of the dead — both human and demi-human. There, seated in one of two upholstered chairs beside a small table set with pewter dishes and a plain crock half-filled with mead, was an old man — ancient even by the standards of evil cultists — who looked up at them with tired, cunning eyes and said simply, "Come, sit down. I've been waiting for you."

The man introduced himself as Kelno, the leader of the Air Temple, and he wasted no time laying out the situation with the precision of someone who had been rehearsing the conversation for weeks. He explained that the party's arrival and promotion of Hartsch had triggered a war between the prophets, a war that would only end when two of the four prophets were dead. Acknowledging Calmer as the new Prophet of Earth, Kelno pointed out that the party had to choose who to keep alive as their second. He revealed that Belsornig had already dispatched assassins to Hartsch's antechamber, and that Alrrem was already dying. As the weakest of the prophets — the one everyone else had always discounted in any war — Kelno offered no real negotiation: he would clearly be their number two. He slid a note across the table, a message from Belsornig himself, detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and containing a chilling instruction: if the group known as the Obsidian Edge appeared, his agents were to withdraw and not engage. Kelno's offer was blunt and practical — kill Belsornig, and Kelno would serve as their subordinate, offering his goblins, his informants, and his network in exchange for his life and his office. He made clear there was nothing to split, since the Air Temple's power was already theirs: "You are obviously the senior partner." As the party searched his quarters, they found a ledger of payments to various people throughout the temple complex, and, more tellingly, a note indicating that Kelno had set out a second chair and had been waiting for them for two weeks — having anticipated their arrival from the very moment they first appeared in the temple. The DM noted that the instant Kelno saw the party work their way through the power structure and deal with Romag, he realized they might be his solution to the problem of being the weakest prophet and almost certainly doomed to be killed.

The party asked Kelno directly about his loyalties. When Zephyr pressed him — "Are you for or against the temple?" — Kelno smiled and replied that the question depended on which temple, that he of course swore loyalty to the power of air and to the elemental forces, but above all: "I am for chaos, but I'm also for living." He warned the party that they had never been told the rules of the conflict: when the last two prophets were killed, the Greater Temple would intervene and install whoever was left standing as the new partner. That meant the party needed to choose their second carefully. He then pointed out that the Greater Temple did not want any single person to remain victorious unchallenged, which meant simply destroying everyone was not the endgame. The party asked who had won the last competition; Kelno confirmed it had been the former head of the Earth Temple. Seeing the logic, the party agreed with Kelno's assessment: "We are the victors." They accepted the deal — they would let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig — and Kelno was visibly pleased, observing, "I think this is the beginning of a wonderful relationship."

Captain Dren arrived shortly after, having deposited Calmer upstairs. He was clearly exhausted and not shy about it, noting that Calmer "won't shut up" and "whines a lot." His report was urgent: Hartsch was finished, Belsornig's forces were already at his door, and Belsornig himself was pulling his treasury out through the south route — low, healthy, vulnerable, and distracted. The party ordered Dren to remain behind with his mercenaries to guard Calmer, and then they moved toward the Water Temple.

The Water Temple was a breathtaking and unsettling place. Its massive hall stretched forty feet wide and ninety feet long beneath a vaulted ceiling thirty feet overhead — the exact height indiscernible in the dim light — its walls and floors covered in polished azerite malachite that swirled with deep blue and green. Some enchantment had been placed upon the stone, causing a soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity to seep from the walls and floor, making the entire chamber feel as though it lay at the bottom of the sea. The many buttresses and arches formed a tracery of shadowy dimness overhead. At the center stood a great bronze altar sculpted with marine creatures in bas-relief, its concave top forming a shallow basin filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells. In an alcove nearby loomed a horrific serpentine idol — eight feet high and nearly as broad — depicting a writhing, amorphous mass of fish, eels, octopi, and other marine monsters, its alcove backed by a drapery fashioned from seaweed and water. Four triangular plinths in the corners of the hall each bore a bronze gargoyle-like statue coated in verdigris. The air smelled of the sea, and the silence was heavy. The party wisely touched nothing and moved through without a sound.

They reached a door to the west. Their attempt to smash it open failed twice — Zinnia bouncing off the door both times despite Sequoia's help — and the guards within were alerted. Rather than panic, the party improvised. Zinnia claimed to have news of the assassination of Alrrem for Belsornig, and the guards, believing them to be allies, opened the door. As it swung wide, one of the cultists began to crow: "She's dead, then, with Alrrem dying, and those fools — all that remains is for us to defeat those pompous passes known as the Obsidian Edge." The moment the door was open, the party struck. The DM granted them surprise for the quality of the roleplay. When the guards saw who was actually standing there, they barely had time to register the party's announcement — "We are the Obsidian Edge" — before the fight was over. The cult fanatics had pre-cast Spiritual Weapon in anticipation of trouble, but it made no difference. Sequoia and Zinnia cut down the first cult fanatic in a rapid, brutal exchange, while Zephyr delivered a devastating critical blow to the second, and Sequoia finished him off. The way to Belsornig's inner sanctum was open.

Before departing the Air Temple, Kelno had pressed a Dagger of Venom into the party's hands — one he had been saving for exactly this purpose, telling them he had long wished to use it on Belsornig himself and asking if they would do him the honors. Moving with exceptional care through the inner chamber, the party crept up on Belsornig, who was preoccupied and completely unaware that his guards were dead. Stealth rolls of 26, 20, and 18 ensured he heard nothing. The party fell upon him in a coordinated storm of strikes — Sequoia, Zinnia, and Zephyr working in lethal tandem across two rounds — dealing a staggering amount of damage. When Belsornig was reduced to a single hit point, Zephyr stepped forward, took the poisoned blade, and drove it home. As Belsornig fell, a deep gong resonated through the entire level.

The party moved back through the Water Temple toward the Air Temple stairs. Before they could climb, bugbears dressed in black arrived from multiple directions simultaneously — through the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door — surrounding the party and making the true power of the Greater Temple unmistakably clear. Zephyr's Insight check of 26 confirmed what the display implied: the Greater Temple had not only had forces below, but had maintained a presence on the second level all along, in areas the party had never explored. The bugbears inquired immediately about the cleric; the party assured them he was injured but would recover and that they would summon him. The bugbears made clear that Barkinar wished to speak to the new rulers of the upper temple — and that he would see them, not the other way around.

A voice called out from the stairs leading down — cold, composed, and unmistakably powerful — commending the party's efficiency and inviting them to descend. Kelno was brought along by the bugbears to face Barkinar as well. Together the party entered a vast, heavily guarded chamber deep in the Greater Temple, where Barkinar stood flanked by ogre bodyguards and a hill giant. The atmosphere made one thing clear: a fight here would not be survivable. Barkinar greeted them with something approaching genuine admiration: "Romag. Hartsch. Alrrem. Belsornig. Problems I had on a list yesterday morning, and four problems gone by sundown. You will forgive me if I'm impressed." He declared the upper temple theirs and outlined the work ahead — leaderless cultists in the Fire Temple, trolls on contract in the Water Temple, a treasury to account for, mercenaries in need of a chain of command, and a chest previously delivered to Romag that the Greater Temple had a particular interest in recovering. He warned them that none of it was urgent in the sense it would kill them tomorrow — and he let the word "tomorrow" hang with unmistakable menace — but that the upper temple should look organized when he came back through those steps. He warned them to be civil toward the Lord Wizard Senshock, who was ambitious and paranoid but useful, and to let him underestimate them. He mentioned a wizard named Falrinth, hiding from the Greater Temple for reasons of his own, as a matter to be discussed later. Then, without waiting for a response, Barkinar turned, spoke quietly to one of his ogre bodyguards, and walked back down into the dark — leaving the party standing in the heart of the very temple they had come to destroy, now its rulers, with one week to organize their new domain before the next check-in. The DM noted this was the most surprising outcome he had ever seen from this adventure across five runs of it — the first time any group had actually managed to start the war and then outmaneuver every faction within it.

The DM reminds them that the Greater Temple wants the inter-temple war to end, and that the lesser temple cultists are all chaotic evil individuals motivated primarily by power — "pretty much all you have to do is let them keep fighting and periodically stab one in the back."
- A staircase leading down (likely toward the lower temple, also probably barricaded) is noted but the party opts to explore the southwest passageway first.

### A Bargain in the Air Temple
#### The party explores the Air Temple and encounters Kelno, a cunning survivor who offers a strategic alliance.
- Zephyr leads the group through a curtained doorway into a spartan, gray-carpeted room. The room features grayish metal lamps on a small table and wall shelf that dimly light the space, a pair of upholstered chairs by a table set with pewter dishes and a plain crock half-filled with mead, shelves holding foodstuffs, scrolls, books, and several human and demi-human skulls, and a narrow bed in the northeast corner with a small stand beside it.
- Kelno is waiting for them. He is described as very old even by the standards of evil cultists, very tired-looking, and very cunning. He greets them: "Come, sit down. I've been waiting for you."
- The party sits in silence and, as Nicholas put it, "menacingly." Kelno appreciates this.
- Kelno explains that the party's promotion of Hartsch triggered a war between the prophets that only ends when two of the four are dead, and acknowledges Calmer as the new Prophet of Earth.
- He lays out the party's choices bluntly: Hartsch (whose door Belsornig's assassins are already at), Alrrem (already on his pyre), himself (the weakest), or Belsornig. Those are the options for who lives as the party's second.
- Kelno warns the party they were never told the rules: when two prophets are eliminated, the Greater Temple opens its doors and installs whoever is left standing as the new partner. The Greater Temple does not want a single unchallenged victor.
- The party asks who won the last competition; Kelno confirms it was the former head of the Earth Temple.
- Kelno shows the party a note from Belsornig — signed "Elsnor, Nick" in the transcript — detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch: two men through the north service hall while the guard is at the long table, wait until he is alone, door bar unset. Most significantly, the note instructs Belsornig's agents: "If the Obsidian Edge appear in your hall, withdraw to the steam corridors, and report. Do not engage. They are not to be killed."
- Nicholas asks Kelno directly, "Are you trying to kill us?" — clarifying that Belsornig wrote the note, not Kelno, who is presenting it as intelligence.
- When Zephyr asks "Are you for or against the temple?", Kelno smiles and sidesteps: "What does that mean? Which temple?" He affirms loyalty to the power of air and to elemental forces, but adds: "I am for chaos, but I'm also for living."
- Kelno makes his offer explicit: kill Belsornig, and "you get the throne, I get to keep my office. We split nothing, because there is nothing to split. You are obviously the senior partner. My goblins are your goblins. My informants are your informants."
- The party accepts the deal. Thomas states: "We won't kill you for his news." Kelno responds: "I think this is the beginning of a wonderful relationship."
- The party searches the room. They find a ledger of payments on his desk. They also discover a note showing Kelno had set out the second chair and had been waiting for them for two weeks — from the moment they first appeared in the temple and began working their way up the power structure, Kelno identified them as his potential salvation.
- Before the party departs, Kelno hands them a Dagger of Venom, explaining he had been saving it for Belsornig and asking if they would do him the honor of using it.
- The party orders Kelno to post his remaining guards to prevent anyone from entering the Water Temple and interrupting their business.

### The Captain's Report
#### Captain Dren Halveth arrives with urgent news regarding the collapse of the other temple leaders.
- Captain Dren Halveth arrives after depositing the injured Calmer safely upstairs. He is clearly relieved to be rid of the cleric, describing him as someone who "whines a lot" and "won't shut up."
- Dren reports the tactical situation in rapid succession: "Hartsch is finished. Belsornig's people are at his door. He's screaming for you." He adds that Alrrem "is on his pyre" and that Belsornig is "pulling his treasury out the south route." His assessment: "Low, healthy, vulnerable. Your call."
- The party briefly considers whether to try to save Hartsch, then decides against it, recognizing that letting him and Alrrem die completes the conditions for the party to become the ruling faction.
- The party orders Dren to take his mercenaries, stay back, and guard Calmer. Dren accepts: "Okay, boss."
- Zephyr asks Dren to come along into the Water Temple; the DM cautions that Dren represents the only reliably loyal force remaining and that getting him killed would be a significant loss. The party concurs and leaves him behind.

### Entry into the Water Temple
#### The party enters the atmospheric and eerie Water Temple, preparing for a stealthy assault.
- The party enters a massive 40-by-90-foot hall with an arched ceiling approximately 30 feet overhead, the exact height indiscernible due to the dimness and tracery of buttresses and arches above.
- The walls and floors are covered in polished azerite malachite swirling with deep blue and green. Some magic causes a soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity to seep from the walls and floor, making the entire chamber feel as though it lies at the bottom of the sea.
- A great sheet of bronze is set into the middle of the west wall.
- Near the mid-portion of the hall stands a bronze altar sculpted with marine creatures in bas-relief, roughly 4 feet high and round with a 10-foot diameter. Its concave top forms a shallow basin approximately 2 feet deep, filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells.
- In the mouth of a 20-foot alcove south of the altar stands a serpentine stone idol, 8 feet high and nearly as broad, depicting a writhing, amorphous mass of fish, eels, octopi, and other marine monsters. The alcove's back wall is hung with a drapery apparently fashioned from seaweed and water.
- Four triangular plinths fill each corner of the hall, extending about 20 feet upward, each topped by a bronze gargoyle-like statue coated in verdigris.
- The air is damp and smells of the sea.
- The party successfully sneaks through the hall without alerting anyone (stealth rolls of 22 and 19 for Zephyr and Zinnia respectively; Sequoia rolls once connectivity is restored).
- The DM instructs the party not to touch anything — "not the time and place to pull for treasure."
- The party reaches a door to the west and briefly considers their options. Zephyr puts an ear to the door, hears nothing alarming, and the decision is made to smash it open for surprise.

### Infiltration of the Water Temple
#### The party uses a clever ruse to bypass guards and then launches a devastating ambush on the Prophet of Water.
- The attempt to smash the door open fails — Zinnia rolls a 7, even with Sequoia's assistance rolling another 7 — and the guards inside are alerted. They call out a muffled name and demand to know who is there.
- Rather than panic, the party bluffs. Zinnia claims to be a messenger with news of the assassination of Alrrem for Belsornig. The guards pause: "Yes, and what is it?" The party presses further, implying they have a letter and coins. The door opens.
- One of the cult fanatics, believing the party to be allies, immediately begins to gloat: "She's dead, then, with Alrrem dying, and those fools — all that remains is for us to defeat those pompous passes known as the Obsidian Edge." The DM grants surprise for the quality of the roleplay.
- The party announces themselves. When the guards see who has actually arrived, the DM notes they barely have time to react before initiative is rolled.
- The cult fanatics had pre-cast Spiritual Weapon in anticipation of combat — they were not entirely caught off-guard by the war around them — but it proves insufficient.
- Initiative order: Sequoia (24), Zinnia (21), Zephyr (16), Cult Fanatics (12).
- **Round 1 (Surprise round):** Sequoia attacks Cult Fanatic 1 for 13 + 9 = 22 points of damage across two attacks. Zinnia follows with 27 total points of damage, killing Cult Fanatic 1. Zephyr delivers an automatic critical strike (assassin ability on surprise) to Cult Fanatic 2 for 30 points of damage on the first strike and 8 on the second. Sequoia finishes the surprise round with 16 points of damage to Cult Fanatic 2, killing him.
- The way to Belsornig's inner sanctum is open.
- The party moves into the inner chamber with exceptional stealth: Sequoia rolls 26, Zephyr rolls 20, Zinnia rolls 18. Belsornig hears nothing.
- Belsornig's armor class is 16. The party goes in initiative order from the previous engagement.
- **Round 1 (Surprise):** Sequoia strikes for 18 + 8 = 26 total points of damage. Zinnia uses her bonus attack and deals 15 + 30 = 45 additional points of damage (approximately — the DM confirms this runs to around 46 total for the round from Zinnia's attacks). Zephyr delivers an automatic critical strike for a combined 38 total points of damage including sneak attack.
- **Round 2:** Sequoia misses. Zinnia deals 10 points of damage, reducing Belsornig to 1 hit point. Zinnia elects to leave him there rather than finish him, stepping back. Zephyr steps in, takes the Dagger of Venom from Sequoia, and drives it into Belsornig for 8 points of damage, killing him.
- The party had briefly debated keeping Belsornig alive; Nicholas overruled it: "No, let's just kill him."
- As Belsornig falls, a deep gong resonates through the entire second level.
- The party begins moving back toward the Air Temple stairs to return to their quarters on the first level.
- As they approach the stairs in the Air Temple, bugbears dressed in black arrive simultaneously from multiple directions: the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door.
- Zephyr rolls an Insight check of 26, confirming the DM's reveal: the Greater Temple had maintained forces on the second level all along, in areas the party had never explored.

### Summons to the Greater Temple
#### Following the collapse of the elemental prophets, the party is summoned to the depths of the Greater Temple to meet their new overseers.
- Bugbears dressed in the black of the Greater Temple arrive from three directions simultaneously, surrounding the party and making the balance of power unmistakable.
- The bugbears announce that "Barkinar wishes to speak to the new rulers of the Upper Temple."
- When the party responds "Yes, we will go see him," one bugbear corrects them flatly: "No. He will see you."
- The bugbears immediately ask where the cleric is. The party tells them he will be summoned and that he is injured but will be fine. One bugbear states, pointedly: "The cleric is in charge, of course."
- More bugbears continue to arrive, reinforcing just how thoroughly surrounded the party is.
- A voice calls from the stairs leading down — composed and unmistakably powerful: "That was efficient. Come down. Bring your puppet master if he is alive. We should talk."
- Kelno is brought along by the bugbear escort.
- The party descends into a large chamber that immediately conveys that a fight here would not be survivable — a hill giant is present, along with ogre bodyguards and multiple tables and doors suggesting a heavily staffed complex.
- Barkinar greets them with the note that Romag, Hartsch, Alrrem, and Belsornig were all problems on his list that morning, and all four are gone by sundown. He delivers this with admiration: "You will forgive me if I'm impressed. That is not the sort of efficiency the Greater Temple is used to seeing from the upper levels."
- Barkinar outlines the party's new responsibilities: leaderless cultists in the Fire Temple, trolls on contract in the Water Temple, a treasury unaccounted for, mercenaries who need a chain of command. He also specifically mentions the chest previously delivered to Romag as something the Greater Temple has significant interest in recovering.
- He warns about Lord Wizard Senshock: "ambitious and paranoid," but useful. The party is instructed to "be civil, and let him underestimate you."
- He mentions Falrinth, a wizard hiding from the Greater Temple for his own reasons, as a matter to be discussed later.
- Barkinar gives the party one week before the next check-in, with the clear implication that disorganization will not be tolerated — the word "tomorrow" is allowed to carry real menace.
- Without waiting for a response, Barkinar speaks quietly to one of his ogre bodyguards and walks back down into the dark, leaving the party as the new rulers of the upper temple.
- The DM congratulates the party: "This was definitely the most surprising of all of the outcomes I've ever done in this adventure. Nobody ever figures out how to start a war. You guys actually figured out how to start a war — and you also managed to outmaneuver everybody else who starts the war."
- The party levels up. The DM instructs everyone to update their characters over the week, or it will be handled at the start of the next session.
- The session ends with Thomas noting "Maybe we'll let the evil take over" and his player acknowledging Calmer might be on the path to turning chaotic evil.

## Locations

### Air Temple
A section of the temple complex accessible via a secret door previously exposed during the gnoll skirmish. Features stairs leading down to the lower levels (probably also barricaded) and a passageway to the southwest. Kelno's quarters within are characterized by gray carpeting, plaster wall hangings, Spartan furniture, grayish metal lamps, a small table with upholstered chairs, and shelves lined with skulls of human and demi-human origin, scrolls, books, and foodstuffs. A narrow bed occupies the northeast corner. The quarters also contained a ledger of payments and a note revealing Kelno had been preparing for the party's arrival for two weeks. It was from the Air Temple stairs that the party was intercepted by the Greater Temple's bugbear escort following Belsornig's death.

### Earth Temple
The area where the party previously sought refuge and where Hartsch's forces were stationed. The party used it as a base of operations. As of this session, Hartsch has been confirmed killed by Belsornig's assassins and the Earth Temple is effectively leaderless, its mercenary forces now answering to the party.

### Water Temple
A grand 40-by-90-foot hall with an arched ceiling approximately 30 feet overhead — exact height indiscernible — and walls and floors of polished azerite malachite swirling with deep blue and green that emit a soft, cloudy greenish luminosity giving the impression of standing at the bottom of the sea. The many buttresses and arches form a tracery of shadowy dimness. The air is damp and smells of the sea. The hall contains a great sheet of bronze set into the west wall, a large round bronze altar filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells, a horrific serpentine idol of amorphous marine monsters in an alcove backed by a seaweed-and-water drapery, and four bronze gargoyle statues atop corner plinths, all coated in verdigris. It served as the final stronghold of Belsornig.

### Belsornig's Inner Sanctum
The private chamber of the Prophet of Water, located past the main Water Temple hall through a westward door, where Belsornig was cornered and killed by the party. He was preoccupied and completely unaware of the party's presence until the assault began.

### Greater Temple
The deep, subterranean heart of the temple complex, characterized by large chambers, heavy guard presence including a hill giant and ogre bodyguards, and an atmosphere of oppressive, organized power. The party was summoned here after eliminating the upper temple prophets. The Greater Temple also maintained a covert presence on the second level — in areas the party had never explored — that only became apparent when the bugbears arrived from multiple directions following Belsornig's death. Barkinar conducts his business from here.
