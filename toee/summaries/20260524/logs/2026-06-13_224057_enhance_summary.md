# Chapter 27 Dungeon Boss!

Date: May 24th, 2026

## Summary
With Calmer badly wounded and carried off to safety by Captain Dren Halveth and his mercenaries, the remaining members of the party — Zephyr, Sequioa, and Zinnia — slipped through the secret door that had been exposed during their skirmish with the gnolls and descended into the Air Temple. The passage opened into a sparse, gray-carpeted room lit by dull metal lamps, its shelves lined with scrolls, foodstuffs, and the skulls of the dead. There, seated in one of two upholstered chairs beside a small table, was an old man — ancient even by the standards of evil cultists — who looked up at them with tired, cunning eyes and said simply, "Come, sit down. I've been waiting for you."

The man introduced himself as Kelno, the leader of the Air Temple, and he wasted no time laying out the situation with the precision of someone who had been rehearsing the conversation for weeks. He explained that the party's arrival and promotion of Hartsch had triggered a war between the prophets, a war that would only end when two of the four prophets were dead. Acknowledging Calmer as the new Prophet of Earth, Kelno pointed out that the party had to choose who to keep alive. He revealed that Belsornig had already dispatched assassins to Hartsch's antechamber, and that Alrrem was already dying. As the weakest of the prophets, Kelno offered no negotiation: he would clearly be their number two. He slid a note across the table, a message from Belsornig himself, detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and containing a chilling instruction: if the group known as the Obsidian Edge appeared, his agents were to withdraw and not engage. Kelno's offer was blunt and practical — kill Belsornig, and Kelno would serve as their subordinate, offering his goblins, his informants, and his network in exchange for his life and his office. As the party searched his quarters, they found a ledger of payments and, more tellingly, a note indicating that Kelno had set out a second chair and had been waiting for them for two weeks, having anticipated their arrival from the very moment they first appeared in the temple.

Seeing the choices in front of them, the party decided the best option was to let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig, agreeing to the deal not a moment too soon. Captain Dren arrived with urgent news: Hartsch was finished, Belsornig's forces were at his door, and Belsornig himself was pulling his treasury out through the south route — vulnerable, exposed, and distracted. The party ordered Dren to remain behind with his mercenaries to guard Calmer, and then they moved toward the Water Temple.

The Water Temple was a breathtaking and unsettling place. Its massive hall stretched forty feet wide and ninety feet long beneath a vaulted ceiling thirty feet overhead, its walls and floors covered in polished azerite malachite that swirled with deep blue and green. Some enchantment had been placed upon the stone, causing a soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity to seep from the walls and floor, making the entire chamber feel as though it lay at the bottom of the sea. At the center stood a great bronze altar sculpted with marine creatures, its concave top forming a shallow basin filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells. In an alcove nearby loomed a horrific serpentine idol — eight feet high and nearly as broad — depicting a writhing, amorphous mass of fish, eels, octopi, and other marine monsters, while four bronze gargoyle statues coated in verdigris watched from atop corner plinths. The air smelled of the sea, and the silence was heavy.

The party moved through the hall without a sound and reached a door to the west. Their attempt to smash it open failed, and the guards within were alerted. Rather than panic, the party improvised — they claimed to be messengers bearing news of the assassination of Alrrem, and the guards, believing them to be allies, opened the door. The moment it swung wide, the party struck. Sequioa and Zinnia cut down the first cult fanatic in a rapid, brutal exchange, while Zephyr delivered a devastating blow to the second, dropping him before he could raise a meaningful defense. The way to Belsornig's inner sanctum was open.

Moving with exceptional care, the party crept into the chamber where Belsornig stood, preoccupied and completely unaware that his guards were already dead. Kelno had previously entrusted the group with a Dagger of Venom he had been saving for exactly this purpose, and now the moment had come. The party fell upon Belsornig in a coordinated storm of strikes — Sequioa, Zinnia, and Zephyr working in lethal tandem — and before the Prophet of Water could mount any real resistance, Zephyr drove the poisoned blade home. As Belsornig fell, a deep gong resonated through the entire level. The party moved to get back to their quarters on the first level, heading to the Air Temple. As they were about to climb up the stairs, a group of bugbears dressed in black arrived from multiple directions — through the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door — announcing that the Greater Temple wished to speak to the victors. The sheer number of bugbears surrounding them made the party realize the true power of the Greater Temple in an instant. The fact that the bugbears arrived through the eastern and western doors reinforced the notion that the Greater Temple was on the second level, leading the party to conclude they had come from areas they had not yet explored.

The bugbears inquired about the whereabouts of the cleric, and the party assured them he was injured but would recover. Then a voice called out from the stairs leading down — cold, composed, and unmistakably powerful — commending the party's efficiency and inviting them to descend. Barkinar, the temple leader, brought the injured Calmer down to join them, and together the party entered a vast, heavily guarded chamber deep in the Greater Temple, where Barkinar stood flanked by ogre bodyguards and a hill giant. Kelno was brought to face Barkinar, who smiled at him. Barkinar assumed Calmer was the new chief cleric of the Earth Temple and addressed him as such, looking at them with something approaching genuine admiration and said, "Romag. Hartsch. Alrrem. Problems I had on a list yesterday morning, and four problems gone by sundown. You will forgive me if I'm impressed." He declared the upper temple theirs, outlined the work ahead — leaderless cultists, trolls on contract, a treasury to account for, mercenaries in need of a chain of command, and a chest previously delivered to Romag that the Greater Temple had a particular interest in recovering. He warned them to be civil toward the Lord Wizard Senshock, who was ambitious and paranoid but useful, and to let him underestimate them. A wizard named Falrinth, hiding from the Greater Temple for reasons of his own, was mentioned as a matter to be discussed later. Then, without waiting for a response, Barkinar turned, spoke quietly to one of his ogre bodyguards, and walked back down into the dark — leaving the party standing in the heart of the very temple they had come to destroy, now its rulers.

- Kelno names the real target: "Belsornig is the problem, not me. Belsornig is going to kill Hartsch within the day. [He's] already dead and doesn't know it. Kill Belsornig! Let me help you do it."
- Sequioa, suspicious, asks "Are you trying to kill us?"; George cautions "We have to be suspicious of him."
- Asked whether he is for or against the temple, Kelno smiles and answers slyly: "Which temple? I, of course, swear loyalty to the power of air." Pressed on chaos versus order, he says, "Yes, I am for chaos, but I'm also for living."
- Kelno explains that the party never understood the rules of the conflict — "You never asked what the rules were, did you? You didn't even know there were rules." — clarifying that the Greater Temple opens its doors and installs a new partner once two of the four prophets are dead, and that the last victor was the former head of the Earth Temple.
- The party reaches its conclusion: Zephyr — "Okay, so we must align ourselves with the victors." Sequioa — "We are the victors."
- Kelno shows the party a note from Belsornig (read aloud in full): the Earth Pretender "names himself Supreme Prophet, and sends mercenaries against my outposts. His authority is paper... Two men through the north service hall, while the guard is at the long table. Wait until he is alone in his chamber... Be brief, take nothing. I expect confirmation. If the obsidian edge appear in your hall, withdraw to the steam corridors, and report. Do not engage. They are not to be killed." It is signed by Belsornig.
- Seeing the choices in front of them, the party decides the best option is to let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig in the Water Temple, sealing the deal — Kelno responds, "I think this is the beginning of a wonderful relationship."
- As a "formality," Zephyr tells Kelno he wants to make sure there is nothing hidden, and the group searches the office, finding a ledger of payments to various people and the note revealing he had been anticipating their arrival for two weeks.
- The party instructs Kelno to post his few guards so no one enters the Water Temple and interrupts their business.

### The Captain's Report
#### Captain Dren Halveth arrives with urgent news regarding the collapse of the other temple leaders.
- Captain Dren Halveth arrives after depositing the injured Calmer safely upstairs, grumbling that "that guy whines a lot" and "he won't shut up"; Zephyr defends Calmer — "He's a worrier at heart."
- Dren reports tersely: "The captain? March is finished. Belsornig's people are at his door. He's screaming for you. I'd say save them, but you didn't put me in charge of mercenaries because I tell you what to do. You tell me."
- Dren adds: "Balern's [Alrrem's] on his pyre, Belsornig pulling his treasury out the south route," summarizing the target as "Low, healthy, vulnerable. Your call."
- The party decides to leave Dren and his mercenaries behind to guard Calmer while they press into the Water Temple; Dren accepts: "Okay, boss."

### Entry into the Water Temple
#### The party enters the atmospheric and eerie Water Temple, preparing for a stealthy assault.
- The party enters a massive 40-by-90-foot hall with a 30-foot arched ceiling and walls of polished azerite malachite that glow with a soft, cloudy greenish luminosity, making the chamber feel like the bottom of the sea; the DM describes buttresses and arches forming "a tracery of shadowy dimness," and notes "even the damp air seems to smell of the sea."
- A great sheet of bronze is set into the middle of the west wall.
- They observe a large bronze altar (about 4 feet high, 10-foot diameter, round) sculpted with marine creatures in bas-relief, its concave top forming a shallow basin filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells.
- A horrific serpentine idol depicting a writhing mass of fish, eels, and octopi stands in a 20-foot alcove south of the altar, the back wall draped with "seaweed and water."
- Four triangular plinths in the corners of the hall each bear a bronze gargoyle-like statue coated in verdigris.
- The party deliberately touches nothing; the DM remarks it is "not the time and place to pull for treasure."
- The group successfully sneaks through the hall without alerting anyone (Zephyr rolling a 22, Zinnia a 19).
- Per Kelno's informants, the targets are through a doorway; the party chooses the west door over the east to avoid passing through extra rooms, listening at the door before attempting to burst in.
- The party reaches a door to the west and attempts to smash it open to surprise the cultists within, but Zinnia rolls a 7 twice and bounces off the door, alerting the guards inside.

### Infiltration of the Water Temple
#### The party uses a clever ruse to bypass guards and then launches a devastating ambush on the Prophet of Water.
- After the failed break-in, the guards shout a muffled name and demand "You're not supposed to be here? Who is this?" Sequioa quips "Housekeeping!" before the party settles on a real bluff.
- The party claims to be messengers for Kelno, then pivots to claiming to bear news of Alrrem's assassination, "a letter [and] a coin to show it."
- Believing the party, the guards let slip their own intel: "She's dead, then with Alremm dying, and those fools... All that remains is for us to defeat those pompous [asses] known as the obsidian Edge." — then open the door.
- The DM rewards the role-play: "Given how well you role-played this, yes, you have surprise." Zephyr, as an assassin, scores an automatic critical on a surprise hit.
- Sequioa and Zinnia strike down the first cult fanatic with a rapid series of blows; Zinnia uses a bonus attack for 27 points of total damage to finish it.
- Zephyr delivers a lethal critical strike to the second cult fanatic, and Sequioa finishes him for 16 points, clearing the way to the inner sanctum.
- The DM notes the fanatics "weren't entirely stupid" — being in a war zone, they had pre-cast Spiritual Weapon.
- Kelno had previously given the party a Dagger of Venom, handing it over with the words: "I've been saving this to kill Belsornig for some time. If you wouldn't mind doing me the honors to do the deed?"
- Using exceptional stealth (Sequioa rolling a 26, Zephyr a 20), the party sneaks into the inner chamber where Belsornig is preoccupied and unaware.
- The party launches a coordinated assault on Belsornig (Armor Class 16), overwhelming him with a devastating flurry of automatic crits — Sequioa for 26, Zinnia for 30 more with her bonus, Zephyr for 38 total; the DM remarks, "You guys did a staggering amount of damage in one round."
- Zephyr briefly muses "Maybe we should keep him alive?" but Sequioa insists "No, let's just kill him."
- Zephyr delivers the killing blow to Belsornig using the Dagger of Venom.
- A loud gong resonates through the temple level as the Prophet falls.
- As the party is about to climb the stairs in the Air Temple to return to their quarters, a large group of bugbears wearing the black of the Greater Temple arrive and announce that the Greater Temple wishes to speak to the victors.

### Summons to the Greater Temple
#### Following the collapse of the elemental prophets, the party is summoned to the depths of the Greater Temple to meet their new overseers.
- As the party prepares to climb the stairs in the Air Temple to return to their quarters, a large group of bugbears wearing the black of the Greater Temple arrive from multiple directions — the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door — surrounding the party and revealing the power of the Greater Temple.
- The bugbears gruffly announce that "Barkinar... wishes to speak to the new rulers of the Upper Temple." Zephyr replies "Yes, we will go see him," and is corrected: "No — he will see you."
- The bugbears inquire about the whereabouts of the cleric; Zephyr says "I will summon him" and assures them "he's hurt, but he will be fine." A bugbear notes pointedly, "The cleric is in charge, of course."
- In a moment of dark humor, Zephyr offers the bugbears hospitality — "Are you hungry? Would you like some meat?"
- A Zinnia insight check (rolled 26) confirms the Greater Temple had forces not just below but on this very level, hidden in unexplored portions of the map.
- A disembodied voice from the stairs leading down calls out, commending the party — "That was efficient. Come down, bring your puppet master if he is alive. We should talk."
- Barkinar brings the injured Calmer down to join the party, assuming he is the new chief cleric of the Earth Temple.
- The party descends into a large, intimidating chamber guarded by a hill giant and ogre bodyguards, where Kelno is brought to face Barkinar, who smiles at him; the DM stresses this is a place where "if you were to have a fight, you would not survive."
- Barkinar commends the party for eliminating Romag, Hartsch, Alrrem, and Belsornig.
- The party is officially recognized as the new rulers of the Upper Temple, inheriting control over the remaining cultists, mercenaries, and trolls on contract: "The upper temple is yours now. There are leaderless cultists in the Fire Temple, trolls on contract in the Water Temple, treasury unaccounted for, mercenaries who need a chain of command."
- The official mentions a chest previously delivered to Romag that the Greater Temple has interest in recovering, with a veiled threat that the work is urgent "in the sense that the upper temple should look organized when I come back through."
- The party is warned to be wary of the ambitious and paranoid Lord Wizard Senshock — "Be civil, and let him underestimate you" — and told a wizard named Falrinth has been hiding from the Greater Temple for his own reasons.
- Barkinar says quietly to an ogre bodyguard and departs, giving the party one week to organize the upper temple before the next check-in ("Shall we say in a week?").
- Out of character, the DM congratulates the players, declares everyone gains a level, and notes the players have now become "the bosses of the parts of the evil temple," who must "worry about murder hobo adventurers showing up and killing you."

## Locations
### Air Temple
A section of the temple complex accessible via a secret door, featuring stairs leading down to the lower levels and a passageway to the southwest (and one to the northeast). Kelno's quarters within are characterized by gray carpeting, plaster walls, Spartan furniture, grayish-metal lamps, a table set with pewter dishes and a crock of mead, a narrow bed in the northeast corner, and shelves lined with human and demi-human skulls, scrolls, books, and foodstuffs.

### Earth Temple
The area where the party previously sought refuge and where Hartsch's forces were stationed. The party used it as a base of operations before pressing into the Water Temple.

### Water Temple
A grand 40-by-90-foot hall with 30-foot arched ceilings and walls of polished azerite malachite that emit a soft greenish glow, giving the impression of being deep underwater. The air smells of the sea. It contains a great sheet of bronze in the west wall, a large bronze altar filled with water, coins, and gems, a horrific serpentine idol, and four bronze gargoyle statues atop corner plinths. It served as the final stronghold of Belsornig.

### Belsornig's Inner Sanctum
The private chamber of the Prophet of Water, located past the main Water Temple hall (reached through the western door), where Belsornig was cornered and killed by the party.

### Greater Temple
The deep, subterranean heart of the temple complex, characterized by large chambers, tables, multiple doors, and a heavy guard presence including a hill giant and ogre bodyguards, with an atmosphere of oppressive power. The party was summoned here after eliminating the upper temple prophets — a place the DM noted no fight could be survived.

## Items
### Hartsch's Note
A message left in Hartsch's office explaining his decision to send the Broken Blade mercenaries to attack the Water Temple instead of following the original plan agreed upon with the party.

### Air Temple Coins
Currency stamped with the insignia of the Air Temple, recovered from a defecting bugbear who had been bribed by gnolls to abandon his post.

### Belsornig's Note
A strategic message from Belsornig shown to the party by Kelno, detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and containing instructions for his agents to withdraw to the steam corridors and not engage if the 'Obsidian Edge' appeared — explicitly ordering "They are not to be killed." Signed by Belsornig himself.

### Kelno's Ledger
A book found on Kelno's desk containing records of payments made to various individuals throughout the temple, revealing the extent of his financial network. Alongside it the party found a note reading "set out the second chair," proving he had been awaiting them for two weeks.

### Bronze Altar
A four-foot-high, ten-foot-diameter round altar in the Water Temple, sculpted with marine creatures in bas-relief. Its concave top forms a shallow basin filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells.

### Serpentine Idol
An eight-foot-high statue of serpentine stone in the Water Temple depicting a writhing, amorphous mass of fish, eels, octopi, and other marine monsters, standing near the bronze altar within a draped 10-foot-deep alcove.

### Dagger of Venom
A magical poisoned dagger provided by Kelno to the party, which he had been saving to kill Belsornig. Belsornig was already dead from the party's flurry of attacks when Zephyr struck with it, so its poison was never tested — but it delivered the symbolic killing blow.

### Romag's Chest
A chest previously delivered to the Prophet of Earth, Romag, that the Greater Temple official specifically mentioned as something the Greater Temple has a significant interest in recovering.

## Spells
### Divine Radiance
A blinding wave of holy energy invoked by Calmer through the power of St. Cuthbert, which successfully obliterated the Wraith and nearly all of its specters in a single burst. It was the third time Calmer had attempted this feat and the first time it had worked.

### Spiritual Weapon
A spectral weapon pre-cast by the Water Temple cult fanatics in anticipation of combat, demonstrating that even the guards had prepared for potential conflict in the war zone.

## NPCs
### Hartsch
The Prophet of Earth, reported by Kelno and Dren to be under imminent threat of assassination by Belsornig's forces. The party ultimately chose not to rescue him, allowing him to be killed.

### Dren Halveth
The leader of the Broken Blade mercenaries who remained loyal to the party — the DM noted "Dren currently represents the only person in this temple who might be loyal to you." He deposited the injured Calmer safely upstairs (complaining that Calmer "whines a lot" and "won't shut up"), delivered urgent tactical updates on the status of the other temple prophets, and was ordered to stand guard over Calmer rather than join the fighting.

### Alrrem
The Prophet of Fire, reported by Kelno to already be on his pyre and not expected to survive. He does not appear directly in this session.

### Kelno
An old, cunning, and tired-looking cultist who leads the Air Temple. He had been anticipating the party's arrival for two weeks, viewing them as a wildcard he could leverage for his own survival as the weakest of the four temples — facing certain death in the war. He explained that the party's promotion of Hartsch triggered a war that only ends when two prophets are dead, and that the party "never asked what the rules were." Acknowledging Calmer as the new Prophet of Earth, Kelno offered no negotiation: if the party killed Belsornig, he would serve as their clear number two — "My goblins are your goblins. My informants are your informants. You get the throne. I get to keep my office." He declared his loyalty was to "chaos, but also for living," sealed the deal with "I think this is the beginning of a wonderful relationship," and gifted the party his Dagger of Venom to use on Belsornig.

### Belsornig
The Prophet of Water (styling himself "the Cannon of the Crushing Wave") and the party's primary target this session. He had sent assassins to kill Hartsch and was attempting to move his treasury out the south route when the party ambushed and killed him in his inner sanctum, Zephyr delivering the final blow with the Dagger of Venom.

### Cult Fanatics
Two guards of the Water Temple who had pre-cast a spiritual weapon in anticipation of trouble. They were tricked into opening their door by the party's bluff — even revealing intel about Alrrem's death and the "Obsidian Edge" — before being swiftly and brutally eliminated.

### Senshock
The Lord Wizard of the Greater Temple, described by Barkinar as ambitious and paranoid but useful, with "opinions about new arrivals." The party was warned to be civil and let him underestimate them.

### Falrinth
A wizard mentioned by Barkinar as someone who has been hiding from the Greater Temple for his own mysterious reasons, to be discussed with the party at a later time.

### Barkinar
The leader of the Greater Temple, flanked by a hill giant and ogre bodyguards, who commended the party for eliminating the elemental prophets — "four problems gone by sundown." He assumed Calmer was the new chief cleric of the Earth Temple, smiled upon seeing Kelno, declared the upper temple the party's to run, and departed with a veiled threat that things should "look organized" by his return, giving them one week before the next check-in.
