# Chapter 27 Dungeon Boss!

Date: May 24th, 2026

## Summary
With Calmer badly wounded and carried off to safety by Captain Dren Halveth and his mercenaries, the remaining members of the party — Zephyr, Sequoia, and Zinnia — slipped through the secret door that had been exposed during their skirmish with the gnolls and descended into the Air Temple. The passage opened into a sparse, gray-carpeted room lit by dull metal lamps, its shelves lined with scrolls, foodstuffs, and the skulls of the dead. There, seated in one of two upholstered chairs beside a small table, was an old man — ancient even by the standards of evil cultists — who looked up at them with tired, cunning eyes and said simply, "come, sit down, I've been waiting for you."

The man introduced himself with the words "I am Kelno," the leader of the Air Temple, and he wasted no time laying out the situation with the precision of someone who had been rehearsing the conversation for weeks. He explained that the party's arrival and promotion of Hartsch had triggered a war between the prophets, a war that would only end when two of the four prophets were dead. Acknowledging Calmer as the new Prophet of Earth, Kelno pointed out that the party had to choose who to keep alive. He revealed that Belsornig had already dispatched assassins to Hartsch's antechamber an hour before ("By tonight, your puppet is meat"), and that Alrrem was already dying on his pyre ("he won't last until the morning"). As the weakest of the prophets, Kelno offered no negotiation: he would clearly be their number two. He slid a note across the table, a message from Belsornig himself, detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and containing a chilling instruction: if the group known as the Obsidian Edge appeared, his agents were to withdraw to the steam corridors and not engage — "They are not to be killed." Kelno's offer was blunt and practical — kill Belsornig, and Kelno would serve as their subordinate, offering his goblins, his informants, and his network in exchange for his life and his office. As the party searched his quarters, they found a ledger of payments and, more tellingly, a note reading "set out the second chair," indicating that Kelno had been waiting for them for two weeks, having anticipated their arrival from the very moment they first appeared in the temple.

Seeing the choices in front of them, the party decided the best option was to let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig, agreeing to the deal not a moment too soon. Captain Dren arrived with urgent news: Hartsch was finished, Belsornig's forces were at his door, and Belsornig himself was pulling his treasury out through the south route — vulnerable, exposed, and distracted. The party ordered Dren to remain behind with his mercenaries to guard Calmer, and then they moved toward the Water Temple.

The Water Temple was a breathtaking and unsettling place. Its massive hall stretched forty feet wide and ninety feet long beneath a vaulted ceiling thirty feet overhead, its walls and floors covered in polished azerite malachite that swirled with deep blue and green. Some enchantment had been placed upon the stone, causing a soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity to seep from the walls and floor, making the entire chamber feel as though it lay at the bottom of the sea. At the center stood a great bronze altar sculpted with marine creatures, its concave top forming a shallow basin filled with water, coins, gems, and seashells. In an alcove nearby loomed a horrific serpentine idol — eight feet high and nearly as broad — depicting a writhing, amorphous mass of fish, eels, octopi, and other marine monsters, while four bronze gargoyle statues coated in verdigris watched from atop corner plinths. The air smelled of the sea, and the silence was heavy.

The party moved through the hall without a sound and reached a door to the west. Their attempt to smash it open failed — Zinnia bounced off the door twice — and the guards within were alerted, shouting "You're not supposed to be here? Who is this?" Rather than panic, the party improvised — they claimed to be messengers bearing news of the assassination of Alrrem, and the guards, believing them to be allies, opened the door. The moment it swung wide, the party struck. Sequoia and Zinnia cut down the first cult fanatic in a rapid, brutal exchange (Zinnia delivering 27 points of damage with a bonus attack), while Zephyr delivered a devastating automatic critical to the second, dropping him before he could raise a meaningful defense. The way to Belsornig's inner sanctum was open.

Moving with exceptional care, the party crept into the chamber where Belsornig stood, preoccupied and completely unaware that his guards were already dead. Kelno had previously entrusted the group with a Dagger of Venom he had been saving for exactly this purpose, and now the moment had come. The party fell upon Belsornig in a coordinated storm of strikes — Sequoia, Zinnia, and Zephyr working in lethal tandem — and before the Prophet of Water could mount any real resistance, Zephyr drove the poisoned blade home. As Belsornig fell, a deep gong resonated through the entire level. The party moved to get back to their quarters on the first level, heading to the Air Temple. As they were about to climb up the stairs, a group of bugbears dressed in black arrived from multiple directions — through the previously barred secret door, the eastern door, and the western door — announcing that the Greater Temple wished to speak to the victors. The sheer number of bugbears surrounding them made the party realize the true power of the Greater Temple in an instant. The fact that the bugbears arrived through the eastern and western doors reinforced the notion that the Greater Temple was on the second level, leading the party to conclude they had come from areas they had not yet explored.

The bugbears inquired about the whereabouts of the cleric, and the party assured them he was injured but would recover. Then a voice called out from the stairs leading down — cold, composed, and unmistakably powerful — commending the party's efficiency and inviting them to descend. Barkinar, the temple leader, brought the injured Calmer down to join them, and together the party entered a vast, heavily guarded chamber deep in the Greater Temple, where Barkinar stood flanked by ogre bodyguards and a hill giant. Kelno was brought to face Barkinar, who smiled at him. Barkinar assumed Calmer was the new chief cleric of the Earth Temple and addressed him as such, looking at them with something approaching genuine admiration and said, "Romag. Hartsch. Alrrem. Problems I had on a list yesterday morning, and four problems gone by sundown. You will forgive me if I'm impressed." He declared the upper temple theirs, outlined the work ahead — leaderless cultists, trolls on contract, a treasury to account for, mercenaries in need of a chain of command, and a chest previously delivered to Romag that the Greater Temple had a particular interest in recovering. He warned them to be civil toward the Lord Wizard Senshock, who was ambitious and paranoid but useful, and to let him underestimate them. A wizard named Falrinth, hiding from the Greater Temple for reasons of his own, was mentioned as a matter to be discussed later. Then, without waiting for a response, Barkinar turned, spoke quietly to one of his ogre bodyguards, and walked back down into the dark — leaving the party standing in the heart of the very temple they had come to destroy, now its rulers. The DM noted this was the first time in five runnings of the adventure that a party had ever figured out how to start the war — and then climbed to the top of it.

## Scenes
### The Aftermath and the Secret Door
#### The party regroups after a series of brutal battles and prepares to enter the Air Temple through a newly discovered secret door.
- The party reflects on their recent victory over a Wraith and several specters that had been haunting the temple passages.
- Calmer, severely injured from the previous battle, is taken to safety by Captain Dren Halveth and his mercenaries — not dead, but "deeply injured," taken up so "he doesn't get himself killed."
- The group prepares to enter through the secret door leading into the Air Temple, which had been previously exposed during a skirmish with gnolls.
- Zephyr takes the lead as the party prepares to descend further into the temple complex.
- The party discusses the strategic situation, noting that the Greater Temple has barricaded itself while the lesser temples engage in a civil war.

### A Bargain in the Air Temple
#### The party explores the Air Temple and encounters Kelno, a cunning survivor who offers a strategic alliance.
- The party decides to explore a southwest passageway in the Air Temple while Calmer recovers elsewhere.
- Zephyr leads the group through a curtained doorway into a spartan, gray-carpeted room where they find Kelno waiting for them, seated in a chair: "come, sit down, I've been waiting for you."
- The room is described in detail: gray carpeting, plaster, wall hangings, dimly lit by grayish metal lamps, with shelves holding foodstuffs, scrolls, books, and several human and demi-human skulls, and a narrow bed in the northeast corner.
- Kelno appears very old, very tired, and very cunning; he taps a piece of paper and lays out the situation: "assassins reached Hartsch's antechamber an hour ago. By tonight, your puppet is meat. Aurim [Alrrem] is on his pyre as we speak, he won't last until the morning. That leaves me and both Belsornig, and most interestingly, you."
- The party sits "silently and menacingly," which Kelno appreciates.
- Kelno explains the prize: "When this war ends, and the great doors open. Somebody is going to be standing on top of the upper temple, and whoever it is becomes the greater temple's new partner up here. That is going to be you."
- He frames the alliance bluntly: "So you can kill me. I have the worst guards in the dungeon... but then you walk into the greater temple alone with no air temple, no goblin eyes in the dark, no bribery network. Or, you walk out of here with me as your number two. I run the Air Temple. Under you. My goblins are your goblins. My informants are your informants. You get the throne. I get to keep my office. ... You are obviously the senior partner."
- When pressed on his loyalties, Kelno deflects with a smile: "Which temple? I, of course, swear loyalty to the power of air." Asked whether he is for chaos or order, he replies, "Yes, I am for chaos, but I'm also for living."
- Kelno reveals the unwritten rules: when two of the four prophets are killed, the Greater Temple intervenes and installs whoever stands on top as its new partner; the last competition was won by the former head of the Earth Temple.
- Zephyr concludes, "we must align ourselves with the victories," and Sequoia declares, "We are the victors."
- Kelno shows the party Belsornig's note (signed "by the cannon of the crushing wave"), detailing the assassination plot against Hartsch and instructions for his agents to withdraw to the steam corridors and avoid the Obsidian Edge — "Do not engage. They are not to be killed." Nick (Sequoia) asks, "Are you trying to kill us?"
- George (Zinnia) urges caution: "We have to be suspicious of him."
- Seeing the choices, the party decides to let Hartsch and Alrrem die and kill Belsornig in the Water Temple; Kelno responds, "very well."
- The group searches Kelno's office under the pretext of a formality — "since you're a new partner, I just wanna know... Do you have anything hiding around here?" — finding a ledger of payments and the note revealing he had been anticipating their arrival for two weeks.
- The party instructs Kelno to post guards so no one enters the Water Temple while they conduct their business; he agrees.

## Locations
### Air Temple
A section of the temple complex accessible via a secret door, featuring stairs leading down to the lower levels and a passageway to the southwest. Kelno's quarters within are characterized by gray carpeting, plaster walls, Spartan furniture, grayish metal lamps, a small table holding pewter dishes and a crock half-filled with mead, a narrow bed in the northeast corner, and shelves lined with skulls, scrolls, books, and foodstuffs.

### Earth Temple
The area where the party previously sought refuge and where Hartsch's forces were stationed. The party used it as a base of operations before pressing into the Water Temple.

### Water Temple
A grand 40-by-90-foot hall with 30-foot arched ceilings and walls of polished azerite malachite that emit a soft greenish glow, giving the impression of being deep underwater. The air smells of the sea. It contains a large bronze altar filled with water, coins, and gems, a horrific serpentine idol in a seaweed-draped alcove, and four bronze gargoyle statues atop triangular corner plinths. It served as the final stronghold of Belsornig. The party noted the temple was lightly guarded at the time, making it as good a moment as any to strike.

### Belsornig's Inner Sanctum
The private chamber of the Prophet of Water, located past the main Water Temple hall, where Belsornig was cornered and killed by the party.

### Greater Temple
The deep, subterranean heart of the temple complex, characterized by large chambers, tables, multiple doors, and heavy guard presence including a hill giant and ogre bodyguards, with an atmosphere of oppressive power — a place where the party clearly would not survive a fight. The party was summoned here after eliminating the upper temple prophets.
