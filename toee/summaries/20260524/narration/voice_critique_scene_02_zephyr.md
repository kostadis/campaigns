## Flags

### [1] Convergence with another narrator's vocabulary

> She was right. She's always right about the seam in a thing.

**Why:** "Seam in a thing" is Sequoia's vocabulary — it appears in Scene 03 with the same image: "The whining was the seam." Zephyr reads people as leverage points, appetites, and things that can be turned; Sequoia reads them for the structural crack that gives way under pressure. These are related but distinct cognitive modes, and their image vocabularies should stay separate. Granting Zinnia credit with Sequoia's characteristic word is a convergence flag.

**Suggested rewrite:** "She was right. She has a way of finding the part of a thing that doesn't hold — the place where it was already going to give."

---

### [2] Generic/literary, not Zephyr's interiority

> He laid it out, and I felt the floor of the thing shift under me as he talked.

**Why:** "The floor of the thing" reaches for atmospheric effect and lands abstract. The political_maneuvering example shows how Zephyr actually renders the same perceptual moment: "I could feel the conversation tilting, the wraith no longer performing menace but something closer to collegial warmth." He names a *specific* social shift — what was performing, what it shifted toward. "Floor of the thing" names nothing specific and reads like the author's voice rather than Zephyr's theatrical but concrete interiority.

**Suggested rewrite:** "He laid it out, and I felt the terms shift — the weight on each side of the deal rearranging itself while he talked."

---

## Verdict

Scene 02 is otherwise very strong — the Kelno assessment ("Not loyalty — appetite, the clean honest hunger of a man who only wants to wake up tomorrow. I respect that. I trust it more than I'd ever trust a vow.") is the best political-voice writing in the session. Both flags are phrases to swap, not structural failures. The vocabulary convergence with Sequoia is the more important catch; watch for it across the full session doc.
