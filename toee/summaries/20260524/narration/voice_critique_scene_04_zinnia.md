# Voice Critique — Zinnia, scene 04: Entry into the Water Temple

**Per-char examples:** toee/examples/introspection_and_observation.md (Zinnia sections); toee/examples/stakeout_and_interrogation.md (Zinnia sections)

## Flags

### [1] Cliché simile

> Zephyr moved like water finding a crack.

**Why:** "Water finding a crack" is a stock simile for stealth — it could appear in any narrator's section. In a water temple, the image also blurs with the setting's literal water imagery, diluting both. Zinnia's movement descriptions in the examples are kinetic and geometric: "Through the steam... a sequence of controlled impacts, the kind of work where your body knows the geometry better than your mind does." He describes *how* motion works, not what it resembles. His reads of others are operational: what they're doing and what that means, not what they're like.

**Suggested rewrite:** "Zephyr moved the way he moves when it matters — no announcement, just presence, closing the distance before the room processed it."

---

### [2] Cliché simile at the scene's highest-stakes moment

> the surprise we'd carried across that whole drowned hall went out of the room like air from a wound.

**Why:** "Like air from a wound" is a generic deflation metaphor that could appear in any narrator's description of a plan falling apart. Zinnia registers operational reversals as facts, not similes. His interiority from the examples: "The mission was accomplished in the narrowest operational sense." "Time to not be here anymore." He names what happened and assesses it; he does not reach for a poetic image to render the emotional weight of the reversal. The sentence before this one — "Twice. The same flat, jarring nothing, twice." — is exactly right. The simile undercuts it.

**Suggested rewrite:** "The surprise was gone. We had carried it forty feet across that glowing floor, and spent it on a door."

---

## Verdict

Scene 04 has the most concentrated simile problem in the session — both flags occur at the two peak moments (the successful infiltration across the hall, then the failed door breach), and both replace what should be Zinnia's terse operational language with worked figurative prose. The room-catalogue opening is strong, the "Four watchers in the corners. A god in the alcove. Treasure in the font." trio is perfect Zinnia, and the door-failure sequence is otherwise well-rendered. Two spot edits at the flagged lines; no need to re-narrate the full scene.
