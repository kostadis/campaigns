## Flags

### [1] Generic prose

> A voice came up from the stairs leading down. Unhurried. It owned the air it traveled through.

**Why:** "Owned the air it traveled through" is a stock literary formulation for a commanding voice — it could appear in any narrator's section and signals authority without conveying anything Zinnia-specific. Zinnia reads voices operationally: what cadence tells him about the speaker's relationship to power, whether urgency is present, what the choice of words reveals. His examples read situational facts, not literary impressions: "The bugbear corrected him without heat." His assessment of Barkinar's voice would be an operational read, not an aesthetic one.

**Suggested rewrite:** "A voice came up from the stairs leading down. Unhurried. The voice of someone who had been in this room before and knew what happened in it."

---

### [2] Author's meta-commentary in Zinnia's voice — the scene's most important flag

> It is the cleanest irony I have ever stood inside.

**Why:** "Irony" is a literary category, not an operational assessment. Zinnia doesn't narrate his own experience as a writer observing a theme — he reads situations for leverage, for what they reveal, for what they cost. This sentence lands the thematic center of the whole document (we became what we came to burn) but routes it through the author's awareness of the reversal rather than through the character's. Zinnia would name the operational fact: we are now the structure we came to dismantle. He would not name the *literary quality* of being in that position.

**Suggested rewrite:** "We came here to take this temple apart. We had become it instead. I sat with that for a moment without moving my face."

---

## Verdict

The [2] flag is the strongest catch in the session — it falls at the scene's (and the session's) thematic summit, and substituting a literary term for an operational assessment at exactly that moment is the kind of voice drift that can make a reader feel the GM's hand more than the character's eyes. Everything else in the scene is strong: the three-approach anaphora, "*Gotcha.* Except this time the catch was on me," and "I filed that" after the Kelno-smile are all exactly Zinnia. Fix the [2] flag first; [1] is secondary. No need to re-narrate.
