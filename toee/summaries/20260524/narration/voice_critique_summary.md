## Strongest recurring theme: stock similes and figurative language at moment-of-maximum-stakes

Four of the six scenes have a cliché or generic simile appearing at exactly the moment the prose needs to be most character-specific:

- **Sc01** (Calmer): "like water on stone" for failed Channel Divinity
- **Sc04** (Zinnia): "like water finding a crack" for Zephyr's stealth; "like air from a wound" for the lost surprise
- **Sc02** (Zephyr): "floor of the thing" reaching for atmospheric effect and landing abstract

The pattern: the narration builds through specific, character-grounded language, then reaches for a simile when it needs to render *impact* — and the simile is borrowed from the general stock rather than drawn from the character's image vocabulary. The fix in each case is the same: name the operational or emotional fact directly, in the character's vocabulary, without the figurative detour.
