## Flags

### [1] Generic prose / not Sequoia's vocabulary register

> He's the one who likes adding the reward to the reward.

**Why:** "Adding the reward to the reward" is too clever and elaborated for Sequoia's flat accounting-mode narration. His vocabulary for tracking Zephyr's financial instincts would be blunt and practical — the spec and examples show staccato internal monologue: "My turn." "That's not good." "I know." When he characterizes another party member, he names a function, not a personality flourish. "Adding the reward to the reward" reads like the author finding a stylish turn of phrase, not Sequoia finding an accurate label.

**Suggested rewrite:** "I let him run it. He's better at the math when there's more than one prize in the room."

---

### [2] Over-literary for Sequoia's staccato mode

> A dead loyal man is a story you tell about how you wasted the only one you had.

**Why:** Sequoia makes declarative assessments, not aphorisms. The spec is explicit: "Short. Declarative. Each thought is a complete assessment." His examples are "That's not good." "My turn." "I need a cleric." This sentence builds a full narrative arc inside a clause ("a story you tell about how you wasted the only one you had") in a way that belongs to Zephyr's theatrical interiority, not Sequoia's blunt internal inventory. The observation itself is correct; the shape it takes is wrong.

**Suggested rewrite:** "A dead loyal man is a waste. We had one."

---

## Verdict

Scene 03 is otherwise very clean — "I'm not. I just talk less," "I noted it. A man who knew exactly what he was for. Rare, in this place," and the closing "That's not good" are all exactly on-spec. Both flags are about the interiority getting slightly literary at two points where Sequoia's voice would have cut harder and shorter. Spot edits only; do not re-narrate.
