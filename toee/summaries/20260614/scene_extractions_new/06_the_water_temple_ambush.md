# The Water Temple Ambush

*Zephyr's, Zinnia's, and Sequoia's lines reconstructed against their voice files. Calmer/GM lines are preserved verbatim (Calmer is also acting as DM this session). Goal: strip filler words and collapse false-start fragments — not to invent content. Unclear lines are flagged rather than guessed.*

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party travels to the Water Temple to plunder its riches but inadvertently triggers a magical trap, awakening stone guardians.
- After a short rest, the party returned to the Water Temple, a grand hall of polished azurite and malachite glowing with a "soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity" that smelled of the sea.
- The DM detailed the chamber: a great bronze bas-relief of an underwater vista with a hideous fish-head spewing water into four tiered basins, a round bronze altar (~10-foot diameter) filled with coins, gems, and seashells, an 8-foot serpentine stone idol of writhing marine monsters in a southern alcove, and four pillars topped with stone gargoyles.
- Zephyr chose to investigate the great bronze sheet, deciding to "see if it's real" by knocking on it — which triggered the idol to rotate and the gargoyles to animate. *(Zephyr asked "the altar?" and the DM confirmed something was happening; the idol and altar are separate objects ~40 ft apart, but both activated.)*
- The stone idol began to rotate and all four gargoyles sprang to life.
- Calmer refused to flee: "if we flee, we'll never get the treasure... This is what we're here for! I want to plunder!" and then "good! I want to kill them! I want them dead! I want every last one of them dead, dead, dead!"
- Zephyr egged him on — "If you're so excited, you should start killing" — and Calmer replied, "I was hoping you would ask," while needling Zephyr: "I poured water. At least I didn't put fire on the water temple, but you had to go poke everything with your stick."
- Initiative: Zephyr and Sequioa both rolled 27; Zinnia rolled a dismal 5, beaten even by Calmer.
- Zephyr struck a gargoyle (AC 15) for a sneak-attack hit, dealing roughly 26 damage (4d6 sneak attack plus base damage).
- Sequioa engaged another gargoyle, dealing 21 on his first attack before disengaging.
- Calmer cast 4th-level Spirit Guardians, the swirling spirits instantly destroying one gargoyle, dealing 21 to a second (halving its movement), and 20 to a third (also halving its movement) as he advanced through the hall.
- Zinnia earned an inspiration point for her in-character spy work warning Calmer not to go too deep; the session ended on the DM's praise.
- *Open thread: Hartsch's war council summons (received last session as urgent) was not answered — the party went to the Water Temple instead. Attendance was expected; deferral may have consequences.*
