## Summary
The battle against the fire salamanders raged on within the heart of the Fire Temple's central pit. One of the serpentine creatures coiled around Zephyr with terrifying speed, constricting him in place before driving its flaming spear into him with devastating force. Though Zephyr's quick instincts allowed him to deflect the worst of the physical blow, the searing heat that washed over him was inescapable. Meanwhile, the second salamander turned its fury on Sequioa, launching a pair of ranged strikes that both sailed wide. Zinnia charged across the chamber and delivered a killing blow to one of the wounded creatures before pivoting to strike another, while Sequioa carved into a salamander with a series of precise cuts before wisely pulling back from the front lines. Then, just as the party braced for another brutal exchange, the last remaining salamander did something wholly unexpected — it knelt, lowered its weapon, and waited.

Through careful observation and intuition, the party came to understand that the creature, a fire salamander named Vorkal, recognized them as the new masters of the Fire Temple and had no desire to die for a cause already lost. Zephyr communicated through gesture and expression that the party now ruled the temple, and Vorkal acknowledged this with a slow, grudging nod. The salamander then produced a magical contract, its text shifting into a readable language the moment it was touched, offering to transfer his loyalty to the party in exchange for continued service as the temple's guardian. Zephyr read the document carefully, striking out several clauses that would have claimed pieces of their souls, and the amended terms were accepted. Vorkal agreed to guard the Fire Temple against intruders and hinted that, should the business relationship prove fruitful, he possessed knowledge about the Greater Temple that he would be willing to share. Calmer voiced quiet concern about forging alliances with elemental evil creatures while maintaining their cover, and Zinnia — drawing on her deep experience as a spy — warned him plainly that he was going in too deep, a caution Calmer took to heart.

With the battle concluded and an unlikely ally secured, the party turned their attention to a trapped chest hidden within the fire pit itself. Sequioa carefully worked the lock, narrowly avoiding a blade that sliced through the air as the chest sprang open. Inside, they found two vials of fire-resistant potion, a ring imbued with similar protective magic, and a short sword that was, impossibly, cold to the touch — despite having been stored inside a fire pit. The party regarded the blade with deep suspicion, and rather than risk touching it directly, Sequioa conjured a spectral hand to lift it from the chest. The sword responded by letting out an audible cough.

What followed was one of the stranger conversations the party had experienced in their time beneath the Temple of Elemental Evil. The sword introduced itself as Frostbrand, a sentient weapon of considerable power and even more considerable opinions, and immediately complained about how deeply unpleasant it was to have been locked in a box. It declared that it could only be wielded by those of good alignment and surveyed the party with an almost imperious air before settling on Sequioa as a worthy bearer. Frostbrand then revealed that it had been held prisoner since at least the Battle of Emridy Meadows, with Vorkal serving as its jailor — a revelation that prompted the sword to immediately demand satisfaction from the salamander. The two exchanged heated words in the salamander tongue before a grudging peace was brokered, largely thanks to Zephyr's persuasive intervention. Zinnia took a moment to explain the party's deep undercover mission to the sword, which Frostbrand found genuinely admirable, and with that, Sequioa claimed the blade and the newly freed weapon declared, with great enthusiasm, that together they would sneak through the shadows and defeat the evil of the Temple of Elemental Evil.

The party pressed further into the Fire Temple through a secret door to the north, where they discovered a lavishly decorated chamber dominated by a great lacquered wooden throne. Seated in it was a tired, wounded man — Varek Solain, the former commander of the Fire Temple's forces. He recognized the party immediately as the Obsidian Edge, the interlopers who had killed Alrrem and dismantled everything he had served, and he drew his Flametongue with the grim resolve of a man who had nothing left to lose. Zephyr spoke calmly, assuring him that no harm was intended and that a new order had simply taken hold. Calmer formally declared himself the Prophet of the Earth Temple and informed the commander that he now answered to Dren Halveth, Supreme Commander of the Earth Temple Forces. The bewildered man, outnumbered and outmaneuvered, paid a small tribute of two gold coins for his life and muttered that he would not forget the encounter.

Deeper still, the party entered a chamber draped in scarlet and gold, its ceiling painted with scenes of demons and lewd revelry, and found a fire cleric — Maeris Dorn — in the middle of frantically looting the room before making her escape. She turned on them with fury, her grief over the destruction of her temple written plainly across her face. Rather than draw weapons, the party surprised her entirely by physically swarming her in a group grapple, holding her in place until she had no choice but to listen. Zephyr offered her a simple choice — leave the temple freely, or stay and serve. Calmer added that any who remained must acknowledge the supremacy of the Earth Temple over the Fire Temple. Maeris, after a long pause, admitted that she had never particularly liked Alrrem anyway, and accepted the terms. The party appointed her as the new head cleric of the Fire Temple and sent her off to organize the remaining troops and restore order.

With the Fire Temple firmly under their control, the party made their way to the Water Temple — a grand hall of polished blue and green stone that shimmered with a soft, eerie luminosity, as though the entire chamber lay fathoms beneath the sea. A hideous bronze fish-head relief poured a constant stream of water into a series of tiered basins along the western wall, and at the center of the hall stood a round bronze altar, its shallow basin filled with coins, gems, and seashells. A serpentine stone idol of writhing marine creatures loomed in an alcove to the south, and four stone gargoyles perched atop pillars throughout the chamber. When one of the party members moved to investigate the great bronze sheet on the wall, the idol began to rotate and the gargoyles shuddered to life, their stone eyes fixing on the intruders with predatory hunger. Calmer, rather than retreat, declared with relish that he wanted every last one of them dead, and the party surged into action. Zephyr struck with the speed and precision of a trained assassin, Sequioa delivered a series of powerful blows before pulling back to safety, and Calmer called upon a host of divine spirits that swirled around him like a storm, instantly destroying one gargoyle and leaving two others badly wounded and sluggish. As the spirits continued to batter the remaining creatures, the party regrouped, the Water Temple's treasures still waiting to be claimed.

- The DM noted this was a classic "Gygax House" treasure: "in the treasure of the monster you're trying to kill is the things you needed to kill the monster."
- The party recovered two potions of fire resistance, a ring of fire resistance, and a mysterious, cold magical sword from the chest.
- Calmer chose this moment to demand his new title, insisting the party refer to him as "the Prophet Calmer" / "the Prophet."

### The Discovery of Frostbrand
#### The party investigates the cold sword found in the fire pit and discovers it is a powerful sentient weapon with strong opinions.
- An Arcana check (Sequioa being the arcane Trickster with the best modifier, around +3) revealed only that the sword was unnervingly cold despite sitting in a fire pit.
- Deeply suspicious, Sequioa first asked for a glove, then flipped the entire chest over to dump the items onto the floor, then finally used Mage Hand to lift the sword without touching it ("I don't trust this. At all").
- The sword audibly coughed and revealed itself as Frostbrand, complaining about being stuck in a box.
- Frostbrand declared, "I can only be held by those of the good alignment," then surveyed the party: Calmer (lawful good), Zinnia (chaotic good), and Sequioa (neutral good) all qualified, but Zephyr (neutral) did not. It settled on Sequioa: "you, you are exactly what I was looking for."
- Frostbrand announced itself as "the great sword Frostbrand" and revealed it had been a trophy "since the Battle of Emridy Meadows," asking "Where's Alrrem? And who are you?"
- When Sequioa asked to leave the fire pit, Frostbrand began speaking the salamander tongue to Vorkal, recognized its former jailor, and demanded satisfaction: "he was my prisoner, I demand satisfaction!"
- Frostbrand clarified it had in fact been Vorkal's prisoner, trapped in the box and guarded by the salamander, before Zephyr brokered peace with persuasion (a 14 boosted to a 24 with help).
- The sword identified Zinnia as "one of St. Cuthbert's," and Calmer scrambled to keep the party's identities quiet; the sword had to be reined in for being "overly good."
- Zinnia explained the party's deep undercover mission, which Frostbrand found admirable: "we are deep undercover! Yes, is that the plan? We are sneaking around! ... Well, I like that! Yes, that is an excellent plan!"
- Sequioa claimed the sword, and Frostbrand enthusiastically declared, "Sequoia and I shall sneak around and kill creatures! ... We shall defeat the evil of the Temple of Elemental Evil, we shall acquire our vengeance!" — while Vorkal, "if it was possible for somebody to look more bored, is looking bored and rolls his eyes."

### Confronting the Fallen Commander
#### The party discovers the wounded former commander of the Fire Temple through a secret door and negotiates his submission.
- The party found a secret door to the north (opened by Zephyr) leading to a lavish 30-foot square chamber with a huge red-and-gilt lacquered wooden throne, where the wounded former commander sat.
- The commander recognized them: "oh, it's you. The obsidian edge, the interlopers who killed... Alrrem, destroyed my temple, massacred my forces. Have you come here to finish the job?"
- When Zephyr first answered "Yes," the commander drew his sword ("I won't go down without a fight!"), prompting Zephyr to backtrack: "No, I lied. It's okay. You can continue being a Fire Temple soldier."
- Calmer formally declared the new order: "the Obsidian Edge and the Chief Cleric is myself, [Calmer]," and asserted the commander would be subordinate to Dren Halveth, "supreme commander of the Earth Temple Forces."
- Zephyr demanded a tribute — "Two gold coins for sparing your life, please" — and when Calmer scoffed ("just two?"), explained "What I demand is not money, but respect."
- The commander handed over two gold pieces, confused, and the exchange of warnings closed the scene: Zephyr — "I will not forget this." / Commander — "Nor will I."

### Maeris Dorn's Surrender
#### The party discovers a surviving fire cleric attempting to flee with valuables and convinces her to serve their new regime.
- The party entered a 20-by-30 chamber strewn with rugs and tapestries, plush furniture, and a plastered ceiling "painted with scenes of demons and humans and lewd activities," catching the fire cleric grabbing as much as possible to flee.
- Zephyr opened with "Why are you fleeing?", which only enraged the cleric further, as the party had "killed everything that mattered to him."
- Rather than fight, the party executed a group grapple ("Let's all go hug him together to surprise him") — Zinnia landed a natural 20, then a 21 with advantage, and Sequioa joined to pin him in a "group hug."
- Zephyr made the pitch from within the grapple: "sometimes we lose things that are due to us, but... We gain a freedom that is more important. ... So, you could be our subordinate, or you could leave the temple."
- Calmer added the third option: "or you can die, let's not forget that option."
- Calmer demanded the cleric "acknowledge that the Earth Temple is now supreme, and that the Fire Temple is subordinate to Earth. You must acknowledge the power of earth over fire."
- The cleric, with a dagger at his throat, capitulated — "You know, I never really liked Alrrem" — and confirmed he was the last living fire cleric.
- The party appointed Maeris Dorn as the new head cleric of the Fire Temple, instructing her to find the captain of the fire guard, organize the troops, and restore order.

### The Water Temple Ambush
#### The party travels to the Water Temple to plunder its riches but inadvertently triggers a magical trap, awakening stone guardians.
- After a short rest, the party returned to the Water Temple, a grand hall of polished azurite and malachite glowing with a "soft, cloudy, greenish luminosity" that smelled of the sea.
- The DM detailed the chamber: a great bronze bas-relief of an underwater vista with a hideous fish-head spewing water into four tiered basins, a round bronze altar (~10-foot diameter) filled with coins, gems, and seashells, an 8-foot serpentine stone idol of writhing marine monsters in a southern alcove, and four pillars topped with stone gargoyles.
- Zephyr chose to investigate the great bronze sheet, deciding to "see if it's real" by knocking on it — which improperly disturbed the altar.
- The stone idol began to rotate and all four gargoyles sprang to life.
- Calmer refused to flee: "if we flee, we'll never get the treasure... This is what we're here for! I want to plunder!" and then "good! I want to kill them! I want them dead! I want every last one of them dead, dead, dead!"
- Zephyr egged him on — "If you're so excited, you should start killing" — and Calmer replied, "I was hoping you would ask," while needling Zephyr: "I poured water. At least I didn't put fire on the water temple, but you had to go poke everything with your stick."
- Initiative: Zephyr and Sequioa both rolled 27; Zinnia rolled a dismal 5, beaten even by Calmer.
- Zephyr struck a gargoyle (AC 15) for a sneak-attack hit, dealing roughly 26 damage (4d6 sneak attack plus base damage).
- Sequioa engaged another gargoyle, dealing 21 on his first attack before disengaging.
- Calmer cast 4th-level Spirit Guardians, the swirling spirits instantly destroying one gargoyle, dealing 21 to a second (slowing it), and 20 to a third (also slowing it) as he advanced through the hall.
- Zinnia earned an inspiration point for her in-character spy work warning Calmer not to go too deep; the session ended on the DM's praise.

## Locations
### Fire Temple
A massive hall of red granite lit by torches and flaming crescents, smelling of heated metal and sulfur. It contains a central fire pit where serpentine salamanders dwell and hidden treasure was recovered.

### Fire Temple Fire Pit
A dangerous pit of glowing coals within the Fire Temple where the party discovered a hidden trapped chest containing magical items and a sentient sword. The chest was protected by a blade trap that nearly struck Sequioa when he opened it.

### Fire Temple Commander's Chamber
A lavish 30-foot square room decorated in reds, oranges, and yellows, featuring tapestries of the triumph of elemental fire and a large red-and-gilt lacquered wooden throne with a gold skull on the back and armrests, where the wounded former commander was found.

### Fire Temple Chambers
A series of rooms decorated in bright scarlet, orange, and gold, featuring plush furniture, cushions, small tables, ewers and cups, dishes of nuts and sweetmeats, and a plastered ceiling painted with scenes of demons and lewd activities. A hooded scarlet robe lay under a heap of pillows. A fire cleric was caught here attempting to flee with valuables.

### Water Temple
A grand hall constructed from polished azurite and malachite, filled with a soft greenish luminosity and the scent of the sea. It contains a great bronze bas-relief with a hideous fish-head pouring water into four tiered basins (the largest of which never overflows), a round bronze altar ~4 feet high and ~10 feet in diameter filled with coins, gems, and seashells, an 8-foot serpentine stone idol of marine monsters in a 20-foot alcove, and four gargoyle-topped pillars. There is also a curtain behind the statue. The party's improper investigation of the altar triggered the guardians to animate.

## NPCs
### Vorkal
A fire salamander who served as a guardian of the Fire Temple and had served as custodian of the trapped chest containing Frostbrand. The sword named Vorkal its jailor, though whether he personally imprisoned it or inherited that guardianship from a previous era remains unresolved. After seeing the tide of battle turn, Vorkal surrendered and presented a magical contract to serve the party as a sentry and potential informant about the Greater Temple. Throughout the Frostbrand confrontation, he looked supremely bored, rolled his eyes, and traded curses with the sword in the salamander tongue.

### Frostbrand
A sentient magical short sword (which announced itself grandly as "the great sword Frostbrand") that is unnervingly cold to the touch despite being stored in a fire pit. It can only be wielded by those of good alignment, possesses a strong and verbose personality, and claims to have been imprisoned since at least the Battle of Emridy Meadows, where it was taken as a trophy. It speaks multiple languages, including the salamander tongue, and was claimed by Sequioa. It delights in the party's undercover mission and immediately declared its intent to sneak around and slay the evil of the Temple of Elemental Evil.

### Varek Solain
The former commander of the Fire Temple's forces, found wounded and defeated in his lavish chambers. He recognized the party as the 'Obsidian Edge' and initially prepared to fight with his Flametongue +1 longsword, but submitted to their authority after being outnumbered, paying a tribute of two gold coins. He carries the Flametongue openly — a flagged campaign lever whose disposition after his submission was not resolved this session. He parted with the ominous promise "Nor will I" forget the encounter.

### Maeris Dorn
A surviving member of the Fire Temple caught attempting to flee with valuables after the party's takeover, furious over the destruction of the temple and the death of everything that mattered to him. He was physically restrained in a surprise group grapple and co-opted into serving as the new head cleric of the Fire Temple under the party's authority, admitting he never liked Alrrem and confirming he was the last living fire cleric. The party sent him to find the captain of the fire guard and restore order.

### Dren Halveth
A loyal mercenary captain previously promoted by the party to lead the Earth Temple's forces. Calmer formally declared him Supreme Commander of the Earth Temple Forces when addressing the Fire Temple commander, placing the commander beneath him in the chain of command.

### Calmer
A cleric of St. Cuthbert and party member currently posing as the Prophet of the Earth Temple. He is authoritative and theatrical in asserting dominance over the other elemental factions, insisting he be addressed as "the Prophet Calmer," while also expressing concern about making alliances with elemental evil creatures. In the Water Temple, he eagerly called for the destruction of the gargoyle guardians ("I want every last one of them dead, dead, dead!") and deployed Spirit Guardians against them. He closed the session reflecting that "one of the most important things in power is to recognize when you have it."

## Items

A magical document presented by the fire salamander Vorkal. Its text shifts to a language the reader understands upon touch, outlining terms of service and loyalty — including some boilerplate clauses involving the signatory's soul, which Zephyr (a tiefling familiar with such contracts) struck out before agreeing. The contract was to be signed in blood; Zephyr cut himself to do so.

Two vials found within a trapped chest in the fire pit of the Fire Temple, designed to protect the imbiber from extreme heat. Added to the party's shared treasure.

### Ring of Fire Resistance
A magical ring discovered in the Fire Temple's trapped treasure chest that grants the wearer protection against fire.

### Frostbrand
A sentient magical short sword found inside a trapped chest within the fire pit. Despite its location, the blade is unnaturally cold to the touch. It can only be wielded by those of good alignment, speaks multiple languages including the salamander tongue, and has a verbose and opinionated personality. It was claimed by Sequioa and declared its intent to help defeat the evil of the Temple of Elemental Evil.

## Spells
### Toll the Dead
Calmer called upon this divine magic against the last fire salamander, creating the sound of a dolorous bell to inflict necrotic damage — though the salamander successfully made its Wisdom saving throw and resisted its effects.

### Mage Hand
Sequioa conjured a spectral floating hand to safely retrieve the freezing cold sword from the treasure chest without touching it directly, after first trying a glove and flipping the chest over — avoiding any potential harm from the mysterious blade.

### Spirit Guardians
Calmer summoned a host of protective spirits that swirled around him in the Water Temple, radiating divine power, cast at 4th level with a 15-foot radius. The spirits instantly destroyed one gargoyle outright, dealt 21 damage and slowed a second, and dealt 20 damage and slowed a third as Calmer moved through the hall.
