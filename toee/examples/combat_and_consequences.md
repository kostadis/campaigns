## Calmer — Confrontation with the Wraith

The corridor air changed before I saw it. That's the only way I can describe it — a drop in temperature that had nothing to do with stone or depth, a wrongness that sat behind my eyes like a headache forming. I turned the corner into the Air Temple passage and there it was, drifting in the dark ahead of us. Waiting. Patient in the way that things without hearts are patient.

My first instinct, if I'm being honest, was the same instinct I had the first time I ever came face to face with something that should not exist. Pure, animal want-to-be-elsewhere. Not fear exactly. Fear implies you're still calculating odds. This was older than that.

But I am a cleric of St. Cuthbert, and St. Cuthbert does not permit elsewhere as a destination when undead are in the corridor.

I gathered the light. I thrust it outward. Channel divinity — the real thing, the genuine article, the power that has cracked ghoul skulls and sent lesser dead screaming into whatever passes for oblivion on the other side. I aimed it at the wraith with all the conviction I possess, which is considerable.

The wraith looked at it. And made its save. The holy light dissolved into the cold air like breath in winter, leaving exactly nothing behind.

There is a particular quality to failure when it happens in front of an audience. The wraith didn't flinch. The corridor didn't tremble. The darkness simply remained exactly as dark as it had been, and the wraith remained exactly as present, and I stood there holding the afterglow of a divine power that had achieved nothing at all.

A lesser cleric might have retreated at that point. A wiser one, perhaps. But I have been killed once already in this gods-forsaken temple, revived at considerable cost to someone who claims not to care about me, and I did not survive all of that to be mocked by something that doesn't even have the decency to be alive when it defies me. I tried again.

The GM said it later, and he was right: there was an abiding and pure passion in that second attempt. He meant hatred, and he was correct about that too. I hate this thing. I hate what it is, what it represents, the cold contempt of its existence, the way it simply *drifts* while I channel power that has worked before and should work now —

The wraith made its save again.

Two attempts. Two failures. The divine light of St. Cuthbert, Cudgel of Ortho, the Arbiter Eternal, dispersed twice into the air of a dank dungeon corridor while an undead abomination stood untouched and apparently unbothered.

*This is not fair*, I thought, which is not a thought with a great deal of theological dignity, but it was the honest one.

Then the wraith turned its hollow gaze on all of us, considering the room, and it said, with what I can only describe as serene confidence: *"Well, one of you is going to die today."*

Zephyr raised his pistol. The shot caught the wraith across what might generously be called its torso — five damage after the creature's resistance halved it — the only wound anyone managed to land on the thing before he broke for the secret passage. I am told I should credit this. I do. Zephyr wounded the wraith and then ran, which is functionally what the rest of us were about to do as well, so perhaps the lesson is simply that even recklessness improves when pointed at a door.

The wraith was hurt. The wraith was not stopped.

The party began dashing. One by one, boots loud on stone, the Air Temple corridor filling with the very specific sound of adventurers not fighting a tactical retreat but simply *running*. Sequoia. Zinnia. Zephyr already gone. This is the correct response to a wraith that has resisted two consecutive attempts at divine power and announced its intention to kill someone. I knew that.

*But not me*, I explained, to no one in particular, or possibly to St. Cuthbert directly, and I dashed.

My armor class is 20. I want that on record. Plate mail, blessed and magical, the finest protection available to a cleric of my station and general level of being-placed-in-danger-repeatedly. I have good reason to believe I am harder to hit than most.

The wraith reached out as I sprinted and caught me across the back. Sixteen damage — cold, deep, the kind of cold that doesn't stop at skin — and then a stray fire bolt from somewhere in the chaos added eight more, because apparently the universe decided one insult was insufficient. I cleared the threshold. I made it through the passage. I was, technically, alive.

And behind me, echoing up the corridor as we scrambled upstairs, as the stone closed the distance between us and it, came the sound of the wraith laughing at me.

*Hysterically*, the GM said. It laughed at me *hysterically*.

Two failed turns. A divine power that worked on the chimera, that has worked before, that should by every measure of theological logic have worked today — and the wraith's only response to the entirety of my efforts was to chase me into a corridor and laugh.

I am going to need a moment before I speak about this further.

## Chapter 10 — A Deadly Battle

#### Zephyr

Shit. Shit. Shit.

This battle was a shit show.

Sequoia, and Zinna were knocked out. Calmer healed them. They tried to run. But they were attacked by Thalsor. Calmer tried to heal them again, but Thalsor knocked him out. I saved Sequoia and Zinnia. And then just as I was about to get Calmer's body, Thalsor killed him.

Shit.

Shit. Shit.

Shit.

#### Zinnia

The operation was a disaster. Our initial attack was successful, as we overwhelmed with speed and stealth the enemy. But we did not have proper tactical awareness of the enemies assets. In particular, the power of the druid and the golem.

Our plan was reckless and the price was the death of Calmer.

"I know you want your friend back. I want to know who sent you. Give me that information, and I will not mutilate his body."

The bastard will mutilate the body, making it impossible to raise him from the dead.

But more importantly - "If we let him live, our identities will be exposed to the cult. Someone will connect us to the village elders. Thalsor needs to die."

#### Sequoia

"We have to save Calmer."

Zephyr looks at me, "How?"

Sequoia stares at his wounds, "Tomorrow."

Zephyr nods.

## Sequoia — Early Afternoon, Planting 19, 579

*My turn.*
Sequoia squeezes through the opening and looks into the room beyond. It's a crypt. Many of the niches are empty. The niche the secret door opened has a coffin that is splintered. *That's odd.*
Examining the coffin more carefully, he notices gnawed and split bones.

*That's not good.*
He looks a bit more carefully into the crypt's main hall.

A man is wearing what was once fine clothing. Sequoia does a double-take. Perhaps this wretched thing was once human, but hunger has replaced humanity. Its withered body seems composed of little more than bruised skin stretched over sinew and bone, but its bulbous belly juts out and sloshes from side to side as it moves. Filth and gore stain its claw-like hands and wizened face. And eyes as black as pitch weep a tarry substance, which a black tongue—slithering from its fanged mouth—licks from its cracked lips.

*There are four of them. I need a cleric.*
"Calmer, get over here; we have ghouls."
