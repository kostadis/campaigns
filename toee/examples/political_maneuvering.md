# Style Example: Political Maneuvering and Dialogue

### Zinia

Zinia, always the master spy, decided that they needed information. The best way to get it was to talk to folks in a Tavern looking for someone to buy them a drink.

Looking around, he noticed that these were all common folk, farmers, traders, and passers-by.

It was midday, and these people had no business or chores.
Precisely the kind of people who would be most interested in telling him what they knew. One talked about a Black Jay. He was a mysterious and forlorn herdsman in league with local bandits. This mysterious man would tip the bandits off.
Zinia thinks, "Ah, so these folks don't know the relationship to the Temple. Good."

The following person, a stout woman of indeterminate middle age, mutters that Y'dey, the head cleric of the village, has mysteriously disappeared. The priest, Terjon, is awfully closed-mouthed about the situation. She best that he played no small role in her disappearance as he is in charge now.

Zinnia reflects, "Distrust of central authority, and prone to conspiratorial thinking, Good."

Finally, the last one reveals his take on the local gendarmerie: "Burne and Rufus are nothing but lackeys for the Crown of Verbobonc. Once they complete their castle ("Burne's Folly" is what I would call it), they will reveal their true motivations, and we will be under the thumb of Verbobonc's Tyrannical Rule."

Zinnia connects the dots and explains to his companions, "The locals see the threat of being assimilated by Verbobonc as bigger than the temple. It's probably related to their allegiance to the god Beory and Verbobonc's theocracy. Verbobonc is committed to St. Cuthbert, and that god is committed to proselytizing and eliminating through conversion anyone who doesn't at least acknowledge the importance of St. Cuthbert."

#### Sequoia

Inside the tower, past the guard, the halfling rogue is once again in the presence of the two agents of Verbobonc. Burne is wearing a leisure suit, and Rufus is wearing a white blouse, green velvet pantaloons, and a red sash. They are both sitting around the main table, obviously full.

Rufus begins, "So any evidence of the cult?"

"They were operating as members of the fire temple."

"Members of the cult?"

"Yes."

"Are you sure they aren't poseurs?"

Sequoia is surprised, "Who would pretend to be members of the temple of elemental evil?"

"Bandits who would like to destabilize the government of Verbobonc. Agents of chaos," mutters Captain Burne.
*I think someone is trying to cover their ass.*

"My dear Sequoia, the events of the Temple of Elemental Evil destabilized the entire region. As a result, the Viscounty has expanded its military presence in the area. Some would like to pretend that the Viscounty has not stabilized matters. To do so, they would like to pretend that the agents of chaos have returned. If they were to return without our knowing, it would encourage those who see the Viscount as an imperialist instead of an agent of progress and safety to reject us. I don't doubt that you found evidence of cultists. But whether they are cultists of a reanimated temple or agents of some other power or government is unknown."
*Definitely trying to cover his ass. *

Burne sighs, "Did you find the leader?"

"Yes."

Rufus is excited, "Did you kill him?"

Sequoia pauses. It's *probably not the correct answer to say we didn't. But if we didn't, why? I know—these guys are convinced this is just some agent of some other government. I'll pretend that I agree. *"As you said, Burne, we don't know who this leader worked for. His name is Lareth. He thinks we work for him. He seemed excited to use us as an agent to eliminate some of his rivals. I thought that would be a good plan. We eliminate some other, as you say, poseurs and learn more."

Burne looks at Sequoia and says, "Brilliant. I admire your deviousness. I imagine it was the masked one who devised this plan. Destroying Lareth's enemies will weaken our enemies as well. Excellent! What was this mission that he sent you on?"
