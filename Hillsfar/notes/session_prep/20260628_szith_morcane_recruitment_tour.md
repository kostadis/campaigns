# The Recruitment Tour — Five Doors, Five Liars

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the session after Ch17 (Jun 22). Party level 11: Akritas (ranger, NPC/Kostadis), Bramgrim (Life cleric, NPC/Dimos), **Daein** (fighter-bard, the face — *nephew*), **Felkur** (artillerist artificer, the Phantom Thief — *son, 16*), + Owlbear. DDEX3-15 *Szith Morcane Unbound*, Parts 2–3, reconciled to campaign canon.

**What I understood (catch me if I misread):** The drow are rallied (~20 + a cured, loyal Quil'bryn). Now the party has to turn a scatter of feuding Underdark factions into an army before they can crack the fire-giant outpost. The five levers are the Hidden, the Lodge of Faces, the Nerevenans (Kiaransalee revenancers), the slaves (~200), and Messanth the caged dragon. The session's spine is **negotiation**, which for *this* table means **manipulation** — two players who reflexively rebel against authority, handed five authority figures with agendas to outwit. The "why" is the arsenal: Szith Morcane forges the siege weapons shipped to Maerimydra; break it here or eat it on the walls.

See also: `notes/szith_morcane_maerimydra_theater_network.md` (the operational map, supply-chain pipe, every NPC's GM-only agenda) and `session_prep/20260622_szith_morcane_the_keystone_is_dead.md` (last session's rally, Alder, the outcome ladders).

## How to run this FOR THIS TABLE (read this first — it's the whole design)

Daein and Felkur don't want to be *given* a quest by a wise NPC. They want to **walk into a room with a powerful person who has something they need, and walk out having gotten it on their own terms** — ideally with the powerful person thinking *they* won. That instinct is not a problem to manage; it is the engine of the entire session. Every faction below is built as **a leader with a pressure point**, not a quest-giver with a task.

**The three doors out of every faction room** — always leave all three open:
1. **The straight deal** (boring, available, the NPCs Bramgrim/Akritas will push for).
2. **The con** — promise them what they want, mean none of it. (Daein + Felkur's love language. *Encourage it.* See "the bill comes due" below.)
3. **The domination** — find the leader's weakness and impose terms publicly, the way they braincapped Quil'bryn into proving his own sanity. Most satisfying for these two; give every leader an exposed nerve they can press.

**The con is a feature — let them over-promise.** The post-war claims *conflict by design*: Hamadh wants to rule Szith Morcane, Rilira wants her noble house restored, Velasta wants it as a death-goddess seat. If the party promises all three the same chair, **they are assembling a coalition that will civil-war the moment the giants are dead.** Don't warn them. Let the lies stack. The bill comes due at Maerimydra or after — that's a whole future session you're banking tonight. Reward the cleverness now; spring the collision later.

**Use the two NPCs as the conscience the players will rebel against.** Bramgrim (peace-without-proof skeptic, Life cleric) and Akritas (reluctant Enclave loyalist, still sore about the unread ledger) are your built-in friction. When the party wants to ally with a death cult or free a corrupted dragon, *Bramgrim objects* — and the players get to overrule the authority figure at their own table. That's the rebel beat in miniature. **Kostadis: step in only to stop Bramgrim/Akritas being dragged fully out of character** (Bramgrim will *protest* a Kiaransalee pact; he will not *bless* it).

**Rhythm — don't run five negotiations in a row.** Each door has a non-talk hook so the session breathes: the Hidden = a **heist/lock puzzle** (Felkur's spotlight), the Lodge = a **"separate the giant from the crystal" puzzle**, the Slaves = **a fight** (free a pen / spark the uprising — Daein & the Owlbear), the Nerevenans = **horror-roleplay** + a grim task, Messanth = **a search puzzle in Solom's chambers** + a setpiece dragon. Realistically **2–3 doors fit in a session**; drow + one is already enough to assault. Let them pick; pre-stage the lean.

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

The session lands if the party leaves with **at least one faction beyond the drow committed**, a **read on the arsenal clock** (they understand that the forge is shipping weapons to Maerimydra and that dawdling costs them at the finale), and **a target picked** (decapitate Dengor at the Forge vs. open assault). Bonus: they've planted at least one **conflicting promise** you can detonate later. The actual assault is next session's payoff.

---

## The Default Trajectory (if the party is passive / blows the tour)

- **The forge keeps running.** Every day they dither, another cart of siege weapons rolls up the tunnel to Maerimydra. What ships now, they fight on the walls later; what fires now, hits Hillsfar/Elventree.
- **The factions stay siloed and get picked off.** The Hidden keep hiding; Decha stays lost to the crystal; Velasta pursues her own vendetta; the slaves stay enslaved; Messanth festers further into shadow.
- **No coalition → the assault is the 11-fire-giant brawl** (drow alone = suicide). The giants finish the work and turn on the surface — the exact outcome everyone sent them to prevent.
- **Quil'bryn's credibility burns.** He staked his honor rallying the drow to *this* plan; a party that recruits no one makes him look like a fool in front of the people he just won back.

---

## The Clever Play (so you know when to nudge — NOT a rail)

1. **Read the clock first.** Scouting the Forge of Surtr (or lifting the shipment manifest) tells them exactly how much is left to ship. That number *is* the session timer. Smart play: move fast, take the highest-leverage one or two factions, don't try to bag all five.
2. **Pick for synergy, not completeness.** The **Hidden** (infiltration routes straight into the decapitation) + **Messanth** (eats the behir, opens the gate) is the engineer's dream combo. Or **Slaves** (numbers + uprising-on-signal) if they want the open-war option.
3. **Con the leaders, decapitate the giant.** Promise the post-war chair to whoever's in the room; aim the whole coalition at the **Forge of Surtr**, kill **Dengor**, shatter the azer-binding → production stops, the other giants flee to Maerimydra. Pull the keystone instead of fighting the wall.
4. **Weaponize the rivalries.** Hamadh vs. Rilira, Kâras (Vhaeraun) vs. Quil'bryn (Lolth), Korkan vs. Rilira — every faction hands the party a wedge. The optimal party plays them against each other rather than earnestly courting each.

## Scene 0 — Cold Open: Murssvayas Lays Out the Board (the "why")

### Setup
The rallied camp, the morning after. Murssvayas — hoarse, relieved the drow stayed, already dreading what comes next — pulls the party to a scratched-out map. **He delivers the local ground-truth, not the cosmos.** Keep Alder's Graz'zt-trap picture off him; let the *players* connect the Hillsfar dot themselves.

### The beat (lift these)
> *(the stake)* "They didn't burn Szith Morcane. That's what keeps me up. They kept the forges *lit*. Whatever Dengor's hammering out down there, it isn't horseshoes, and it isn't for anyone in the Underdark. It's meant for up. Your sky."
>
> *(the pipe — pay now or later)* "They don't stockpile what they forge. Every few days a cart goes up the tunnel toward Maerimydra and comes back empty. So you can break it here — in the dark, with the smiths still chained to it — or you can meet it later, on the queen's walls, pointed at you. I'd break it here."
>
> *(the handoff)* *(rubs his sideburns)* "Solom was the general. I'm a wizard who got handed a war. You've done more with less — everyone's heard about the giant in the bag. Twenty drow won't crack that outpost. Tell me who you want, and I'll point you at them."

**Let the players close the loop.** When Murssvayas says "meant for up," that's Akritas's cue (you, Kostadis): *"...siege engines. The Enclave warned us. That's the Hillsfar street-map we found in the mind flayer's lair — they're forging the things to aim at it."* The NPC gives the fact; the table supplies the dread. Don't pre-chew it.

### The menu (Murssvayas hands it over — these are his words, his read)
- **The Hidden** — "Thieves. Vhaeraun's people. They'd cut a giant's purse off before they'd cut its throat, but they know ways into that outpost no one else does. Don't bring our people — they worship the Masked Lord, and they *hate* Lolth. Go alone." *(Quil'bryn, a Lolthite, will bristle.)*
- **The Lodge of Faces** — "Stone giants. Carvers. Their elder, Decha, went strange — sits and stares at a crystal he carved and won't be moved. If you can wake him up, they hit like an avalanche." *(The galeb duhr you cheered up is your way in.)*
- **The Nerevenans** — *(lowers his voice)* "The dead ones. Kiaransalee's priestesses. Velasta wanted Solom gone and the city given to her goddess. Quil'bryn handed her half of that without meaning to. She'll deal. You won't like the price." *(Bramgrim's head comes up here.)*
- **The slaves** — "Two hundred of them, give or take, breaking ore for the forge. Arm them, give them a signal, and they're an avalanche of a different kind. There's a noblewoman down there, Rilira, thinks they're hers. And a hobgoblin, Korkan, who'd like her dead. Sort that out before you light the fuse."
- **(Messanth — Murssvayas does NOT volunteer this.)** It surfaces only if they search Solom's chambers (see Door 5).

### Stage direction
Keep Scene 0 to **five minutes.** It's a briefing, not a scene. The instant a player says "let's go talk to the thieves," you're out of it. The map and the manifest are the takeaways; everything else is doors.

## Door 1 — The Hidden *(recommended lean: Felkur's home turf — a HEIST PUZZLE)*

### The authority figure
**Hamadh** — leader of the Hidden, a drow thief who wants to **rule Szith Morcane** once the giants are gone. Smooth, never quite faces you head-on, treats every conversation as a negotiation he's already winning. **Pressure point:** his ambition is naked and his rival (Rilira) is recruitable too — he *needs* the party more than he lets on, because whoever delivers the city to him decides whether he gets the chair.
**Kâras** — his second, a **Vhaeraun zealot.** Fanatical, anti-Lolth, suspicious of outsiders. The wall the party has to get past to reach Hamadh's pragmatism.

### Why the rebels love this room
The Hidden don't respect deference — they respect *competence and nerve* (Vhaeraun is the god of ambition and self-reliance). You can't bow your way in; you have to *impress* them. **Felkur is literally a figure of thief legend** (the Phantom Thief of Mulmaster) — if the table plays that card, Kâras's hostility curdles into grudging professional respect. This is the one room where being a difficult, ambitious operator is the *password.*

### The puzzle hook (Felkur's spotlight)
The Hidden don't ally with people who *say* they're capable. **They set a test:** something the giants took — a cache, a key, a prisoner, a piece of the forge's shipment — sitting behind a lock/ward in a part of the outpost the Hidden can route them to but won't risk themselves. *"Bring it back and we'll believe you're worth dying beside."* This is a contained infiltration puzzle: a route, a guard pattern, a lock or a magical seal (Felkur's All-Purpose Tool / thieves' tools / a Faerzress-warded mechanism that's a *puzzle*, not a DC). Solve it clean and you've also **scouted the outpost for free** (feed the intel straight into the assault).

### The three doors
- **Straight deal:** pass the test, ally earnestly. Hamadh asks for the city after; the party agrees. (Bramgrim: fine with it. Akritas: notes they just promised a thief a throne.)
- **The con:** promise Hamadh he rules after — *and* promise Rilira her house back two doors later. Let it ride. (This is the trap they'll spring on themselves.)
- **The domination:** the party flips the test — instead of fetching what the Hidden ask, they steal something *from the Hidden* to prove the point, or they expose that Hamadh needs them more than they need him and dictate terms. Kâras will hate it; Hamadh will respect it.

### Friction
**Quil'bryn cannot come** (Lolthite; Kâras would knife the deal). Good beat: the party has to *leave their new drow asset behind* to make this one work — a small, real cost. **Akritas** quietly approves of the Hidden (operators, like her); **Bramgrim** distrusts anyone who worships a god of thieves.

### Success / Failure (material, comedic)
- **Win:** infiltration routes into the outpost (cuts the assault's risk hard), + the Hidden as a strike team. Felkur's legend grows.
- **Botch:** the Hidden don't attack — they just *vanish*, and now there's a crew of master thieves in the outpost who know the party's faces and owe them nothing. (And maybe Felkur's All-Purpose Tool goes missing in the night. Comedic, material, recoverable as a later beat.)

## Door 2 — The Lodge of Faces *(a "free the giant from the crystal" PUZZLE)*

### The authority figure
**Decha (module: Daitya)** — elder of the Lodge, a stone giant **lost to Graz'zt's madness of infatuation.** He carved a Faerzress crystal so perfect he can no longer look away from it; he sits, stares, and his people wait, leaderless and grieving. The same madness that took Quil'bryn — *a cure problem, not a fight.* **He is also the authority who exiled the galeb duhr for an "imperfect" face** — a petty tyrant of perfectionism even before the crystal.

### The way in
**The weeping galeb duhr** the party already cheered up (the one with the shattered nose Decha rejected). It's their guide and their emotional throughline: the rebels get to **champion the rejected outcast against the perfectionist elder** — and prove the elder wrong by saving him with the very "imperfect" creature he threw away.

### The puzzle hook
You cannot out-muscle a lodge of stone giants. The problem: **how do you separate a maddened giant from the object of his obsession without a fight you'll lose?** Open design — reward whatever the table invents:
- A **braincap mushroom** (they carry treated ones — but how do you make a giant *eat* it? Daein mimics the crystal's "voice" telling him to swallow? Felkur engineers a delivery?).
- A **swap** — Felkur fabricates a duplicate crystal; the con artists pull a shell game on a demigod's worth of infatuation.
- **The galeb duhr** — letting the rejected carver "finish" the imperfect face, breaking the spell of perfection that's the root of the madness. (Thematically the cleanest; let a player reach it.)
- Smashing the crystal works too — but it might **break Decha's mind** instead of freeing it (a Faerzress detonation — tie to the Crystal Cascade thread). Risk/reward.

### The three doors
- **Straight:** cure Decha, earn the Lodge's gratitude and their avalanche-tier muscle.
- **The con:** they don't actually have to cure him — they could promise the *galeb duhr* leadership of the Lodge, or promise the stone giants vengeance on Maerimydra they don't intend to deliver. Thinner here; the Lodge is the most earnest faction.
- **The domination:** expose Decha's madness to his own people (the braincap-Quil'bryn move at giant scale) — humiliate the perfectionist elder publicly, install the exile, flip the whole Lodge by toppling its tyrant.

### Friction
**Bramgrim** (Underdark/illusion lore, *True Seeing*) is genuinely useful here — let Dimos shine reading the crystal's enchantment. This is the faction where the NPC cleric is an *asset*, not an obstacle — a nice change of texture.

### Success / Failure
- **Win:** stone giants — the single best heavy-hitters vs. fire giants. The galeb duhr redeemed.
- **Botch:** maddened Decha + lodge turns hostile (a brutal telegraphed fight, or a fast retreat). Or worse-comedic: they cure him and he's *mortified*, immediately wants to re-carve everyone's faces "correctly," including the party's. A grateful giant with terrible boundaries.

## Door 3 — The Nerevenans / Revenancers *(HORROR ROLEPLAY + the Bramgrim wedge)*

### The authority figure
**Velasta T'sarran** — revenant priestess of **Kiaransalee** (drow goddess of vengeance and undeath). Cold, patient, unhurried — she speaks like someone for whom time is not a constraint. **She wanted Solom dead and the city given to her goddess; Quil'bryn handed her half of that for free**, so she's already half-satisfied and therefore *cheap* — which is exactly what should make the party nervous.
**Asala** — a revenancer who wants revenge on the giants for killing her lover. The grief that gives the cold cult a human handle.

### Why this is the party-friction faction
A **Life cleric allying with a death cult.** This is the room where **Bramgrim plants his feet.** He will argue, openly, in front of the players — and the rebels get to overrule the moral authority at their own table. *(Kostadis: Bramgrim protests, negotiates limits, refuses to perform any rite — but he doesn't walk out on the party. Step in only if someone tries to make him *bless* it.)* The T'sarran name ties to Maerimydra's **Undying Temple** — recruiting them imports a death-cult problem into the capital. That's a *feature*: it's the best slow-burn consequence in the campaign.

### The hook (grim task or horror beat, not a fight by default)
Velasta tests them with something distasteful rather than dangerous — desecrate a giant shrine, deliver a specific giant's skull, swear a vengeance-oath on Kiaransalee's name (the oath itself is the trap Bramgrim sees coming). Or a small undeath puzzle/setpiece: the revenancers are bound to an unfinished vengeance and the party can resolve or exploit it. Keep the register **eerie and transactional**, not combative.

### The three doors
- **Straight:** swear the oath, take the undead. (Bramgrim: "Absolutely not." Players: *grin*.)
- **The con:** promise Velasta the city-as-death-seat with zero intent — easy to *say*, and it conflicts directly with what they promised Hamadh and Rilira. The richest lie on the board.
- **The domination:** Velasta is already half-satisfied; a clever party realizes she *needs the win* to consolidate her power-grab and dictates terms downward — "you're getting your goddess's outpost regardless; you'll take *our* terms for it."

### Success / Failure
- **Win:** revenancer undead shock troops, fearless and expendable. + a Kiaransalee time-bomb under Maerimydra (good).
- **Botch / refuse:** Velasta is unbothered — she shrugs and waits; the party simply doesn't get the undead. But if they *insult* the goddess, the revenancers become a third hostile faction in the outpost during the assault. Bramgrim, notably, is *relieved* if they walk away.

## Door 4 — The Slaves *(the LIBERATION beat — a FIGHT, Daein & Owlbear spotlight)*

### The authority figures (two, in conflict)
**Rilira** — former head of a drow noble house, now in rags, **convinced the 200 slaves are *hers* to command** by birthright. Speaks like nobility who never noticed she lost. **The authority figure the rebels will most enjoy puncturing** — entitled, presumptuous, reclaiming power.
**Korkan** — a hobgoblin slave-boss who wants Rilira dead and the slaves led by strength, not blood. Brutal, pragmatic, honest about it.

### Why the rebels love this room
This is the **liberation fantasy** — freeing 200 enslaved people to rise against the giants is the purest anti-authority beat in the session. But the leadership is *contested*, and the party gets to decide: back the noble, back the strongman, or **reject both and arm the slaves directly** (the maximally rebellious option — give the power to no leader at all). Daein the face runs this room; the Owlbear is beloved by frightened people.

### The combat hook
The way in is **a fight:** the Scene-1 escapees lead the party to a **slave pen** with overseers (a few azer + a hell hound, or a low giant), and freeing it is the proof-of-good-faith that makes the rest of the slaves believe. Or stage the **uprising-spark itself** as the setpiece — a running fight to arm the pens before the giants notice. Daein's Belt of Frost Giant Strength, the Owlbear, Akritas's bow, Felkur's cannon all get to *open up* here after three rooms of talking. The table will be hungry for it by now — **slot this one third or fourth for pacing.**

### The puzzle layer (optional, Felkur)
200 unarmed people under the giants' noses is a *logistics* problem: how do you arm and coordinate them without tipping off the garrison? Smuggled forge-tools, a signal everyone can see, timing it to the assault. Felkur's wheelhouse if the table wants brains over brawn.

### The three doors
- **Straight:** pick Rilira *or* Korkan, free the slaves, gain the uprising-on-signal.
- **The con:** promise Rilira her house restored (conflicts with Hamadh's throne!) while privately backing Korkan, or vice versa. Play the claimants against each other.
- **The domination / the rebel special:** refuse to anoint either leader. Arm the slaves as a *leaderless* force loyal to the party directly — humiliating both would-be authorities. Messy, powerful, very on-brand.

### Friction
**Akritas** (Enclave — protects the innocent) is fully on board with liberation. **Bramgrim** too. This is the faction with the *least* NPC friction — let the players enjoy a clean heroic beat, then complicate it with the Rilira/Korkan succession knife-fight they now own.

### Success / Failure
- **Win:** numbers — the uprising that turns the assault from a raid into a war the giants lose. Lowest combat value per head, highest total mass.
- **Botch:** the uprising goes early or gets betrayed (Korkan or Rilira sells them out if scorned) → a slave massacre the party watches happen. Heavy, material, and a grudge that follows them.

## Door 5 — Messanth *(the SEARCH PUZZLE + the two-voiced dragon — the engineer's bonus)*

### The caged power
**Messanth** — a young **bronze-shadow dragon**, captured years ago by **Solom** and imprisoned in the Shadowfell, slowly corrupting. He wanted Solom dead; *Solom is dead*; so right now, briefly, **he can be reasoned with** in a way he won't stay. Freed, he hunts the **behir** and tears the outpost gate open — solving the party's single worst infiltration problem. Doubles as an **Order of the Gauntlet** recovery mission (route the ask to Akritas).

### Why it fits the rebels
Messanth is a **prisoner of the old authority (Solom)** — and freeing a caged thing the dead tyrant locked away is catnip. But he's half-shadow now: is he the dragon the Gauntlet wants back, or something the Shadowfell rewrote? The party gambles on a corrupted prisoner's word. Daein and Felkur will *love* the risk; Bramgrim and Akritas will *hate* it — perfect tension.

### The hook (two parts)
1. **Search puzzle:** Murssvayas doesn't volunteer Messanth — the party finds him by searching **Solom's chambers** (now accessible). Solom's wards, ciphered notes, the Shadowfell key — a contained puzzle that also drops Crystal-Cascade/Blink lore for the attentive. Felkur + Bramgrim's lore.
2. **The roleplay setpiece:** Messanth speaks in **two voices, bronze and shadow, slightly out of sync** — a memorable NPC to *play*. The negotiation is the scene: he'll honor a deal to fight the behir; his long-term loyalty is a future thread, deliberately left open.

### The three doors
- **Straight:** free him, deal honestly, he eats the behir.
- **The con:** promise him freedom-after-service and intend to re-cage him for the Gauntlet (very dark; let them consider it; Akritas's Gauntlet ask pulls this way).
- **The domination:** they hold the Shadowfell key — they negotiate from leverage, dictating the terms of his release rather than begging his help.

### Friction
**Akritas** gets the Gauntlet recovery ping (her coalition wants the dragon home) — a real pull on the NPC you run, Kostadis. **Bramgrim** distrusts the corrupted thing claiming alliance (direct echo of his Glor skepticism — "peace without proof"). Strong two-NPC scene.

### Success / Failure
- **Win:** the behir solved, the chasm cleared, the gate opened — the assault's hardest obstacle gone, plus a Gauntlet favor banked.
- **Botch:** a half-shadow dragon loose in the Underdark owing the party nothing — possibly *worse* than the behir. A magnificent future problem.

## Anticipated player questions

- **"Do we have to do all five?"** → No. Drow + one more is a viable assault. Going wide burns the arsenal clock (more carts reach Maerimydra). Pick for synergy.
- **"Can we just promise everyone everything?"** → *Yes, please.* (GM-only: the chairs they're promising overlap — Hamadh, Rilira, and Velasta all want to rule the same outpost. Let it ride. Bill comes due later.)
- **"What's the rush?"** → The forge. Show them the manifest/the carts. Every faction courted = days the weapons keep shipping. The clock is diegetic and visible, not a GM threat.
- **"Can we skip recruiting and just decapitate Dengor?"** → They can *try* — but 20 drow + the party vs. the full garrison (behir, azer, hell hounds, 11 giants) is a meat-grinder. The Hidden's routes or Messanth's behir-solution are what make decapitation survivable. Leave the door open; let them learn the cost by scouting.
- **"Where's Quil'bryn while we do all this?"** → With the drow — except he *can't* go to the Hidden (Lolth/Vhaeraun). Use him as a scout/voucher elsewhere; his outpost knowledge is real value.
- **"Can we turn the slaves on the Hidden / the revenancers on the slaves?"** → If they want to play factions against each other, *let them* — that's the optimal play. Just track who ends up owing whom; the post-war board is yours to collide later.

## Stage directions (timing / silence cues)

- **Scene 0 is five minutes.** The second a player picks a door, you're moving. Don't lecture the menu — Murssvayas hands it over and gets out of the way.
- **Let the players connect the Hillsfar dot, not Murssvayas.** Leave the beat of silence after "meant for up" and let Akritas (you) or a player close it. The realization should feel earned.
- **When the con-promises start stacking, keep a straight face and a private note.** Don't tip the collision with a knowing GM smile. The lies should feel like winning *right now.*
- **Pace the fight (Slaves) into the back half.** Three negotiation rooms then a liberation brawl is the right rhythm. If energy flags earlier, pull the Slaves forward.
- **Play the faction leaders as winners, not supplicants.** Hamadh, Velasta, Rilira each think *they're* the one using the party. The fun for the table is being underestimated and proving it.
- **When Bramgrim objects to the Nerevenans, don't rush past it.** Let the disagreement breathe at the table — it's a real beat, not an obstacle to clear.

## Open decisions (for you — I won't decide these)

1. **Which two doors to pre-stage deep?** I've written the **Hidden** (Felkur/puzzle) and the **Slaves** (Daein/combat) as the leans for this table's psychology. Say the word and I'll deepen the Lodge, the Nerevenans, or Messanth into a full beat with a stat-light encounter instead.
2. **The arsenal clock — soft or hard?** How many "ticks" (carts/days) before the last shipment leaves? Pick a number you'll show them via the manifest, so the cost of dawdling is concrete, not vibes.
3. **Do you want the conflicting-promises collision to fire THIS arc or next?** It can detonate during the assault (a faction defects at the worst moment) or be banked for the Maerimydra aftermath. Decide roughly when, so you can seed it.
4. **Messanth's true nature** — is he salvageable (the Gauntlet gets their dragon) or has the Shadowfell rewritten him (a future antagonist)? Decide before they free him, because his dialogue should hint at the answer.
5. **The Lodge cure method** — do you want to bless a specific solution (the galeb-duhr-finishes-the-face is the thematic winner) or run it fully open and reward whatever the table improvises?
6. **Kâras vs. Quil'bryn** — is the Lolth/Vhaeraun hostility a hard wall (Quil'bryn *cannot* be present) or a tense-but-workable thing a great Persuasion roll can bridge? Affects whether leaving Quil'bryn behind is a forced cost or a player choice.
