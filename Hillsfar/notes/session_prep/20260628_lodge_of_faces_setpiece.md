# Set-Piece — The Lodge of Faces: The Perfect Face

*Full staging for Door 2 of the recruitment tour. The cure puzzle, deepened. Companion to `20260628_szith_morcane_recruitment_tour.md` (Door 2) and `20260628_szith_morcane_encounters.md` (the fail-state stats). Reskin note: canon elder is **Decha (module: Daitya)**; the weeping guide is the **galeb duhr (module: Grek)** the party already cheered up.*

**The one-line trick:** the theme *is* the solution. The Lodge's whole religion says **a perfect face is a dead face** — and Decha carved a perfect one and got trapped staring into it. The cure is to make it *imperfect* again. And the one creature who can walk up to him unaffected is the "imperfect" outcast he exiled. **Perfectionism is the disease; the rejected flaw is the key.** For two players who rebel against authority, the answer is to overturn the tyrant *using his own faith against him* — and crown the outcast he threw away.

---

## The Gallery (set the scene before any mechanic)

> A cathedral of faces. Every surface of this cavern — walls, pillars, the ceiling lost in dark — is carved with the features of giants, gods, strangers. Hundreds of them, and not one is whole: this one missing an ear, that one with a split lip, another with one eye larger than the other, a cheek left rough and unfinished. They are beautiful *because* they are flawed — each carver's deliberate wound.
>
> At the center sits **Decha**, elder of the Lodge — a stone giant gone grey and still as the rock he shaped. He does not look up. He is staring into a **crystal the size of a war-shield**, set on a plinth before him, and in its facets is carved a single face: *flawless*. Perfect symmetry, perfect proportion, perfect and cold. He has not eaten in days. The other giants stand at the gallery's edge, helpless, grieving, waiting for an elder who will not rise.

**The wrong note is visible.** Anyone who looks (passive Perception 12) clocks that the crystal-face is the *only* perfect thing in a room that worships imperfection. That dissonance is the whole puzzle, on display.

---

## Reading the Riddle (how the party learns the cure)

Three ways in — give them whichever they reach for:

- **The galeb duhr just tells them** (it's their guide; it grew up in this faith). *"The Stone Father wears a thousand faces, and no two are whole. To carve a perfect face is to carve a tomb — the carver looks in, and sees nothing left to make better, and so he stops. Forever. Decha forgot the oldest teaching. He carved a god with no flaw, and now he cannot look away."*
- **Read the gallery** — DC 15 Insight, Religion, or History (Bramgrim's lore shines): the deliberate flaws aren't damage, they're *doctrine*. The crystal violates it.
- **Ask the grieving giants** — they'll confirm the teaching but are too reverent to "deface" their elder's masterwork themselves. *That taboo is why they're stuck and the party isn't.*

> **GM-only — the heresy the rebels can weaponize:** by the Lodge's *own* older teaching, the cracked-nosed galeb duhr Decha exiled has the **holier** face — imperfect, therefore alive. Decha is the apostate; the outcast is the truer carver. A player who realizes this can turn the Lodge against its own elder *with scripture*, not swords. Reward that hard — it's the perfect beat for this table.

---

## The Madness Mechanic (the obstacle to every solution)

Decha is **Stone Giant Dreamwalker** stats (VGM, CR 10 — full block in the encounter pack), but **he does not fight unless attacked or the crystal is threatened clumsily.** What matters here is one trait:

- **Dreamwalker's Charm** — any *enemy* starting its turn within 30 ft makes a **DC 13 Charisma save** or is **charmed** (drawn toward the crystal, wanting to look). The room itself pulls people in. Approaching Decha means walking into that pull.
- **The poetry:** the **galeb duhr is immune to the charmed condition** (elemental). *The one being who can cross the aura and stand at Decha's shoulder untouched is the exile he cast out for being imperfect.* Don't say it. Let them discover that the outcast walks where the heroes can't.

---

## The Four Solutions (all valid — rank for nudging, never gate)

### ① The Outcast Finishes the Face — *the thematic winner; steer here gently*
The galeb duhr (charm-immune) approaches and, with the party's blessing, **adds a single flaw** to the perfect crystal-face — a chip, a crack across the cheek, the same shattered nose Decha rejected it for. The spell of perfection breaks the instant the face becomes *alive* again.
- **No roll required** if they think of it — it's the *right* answer and should feel like one. (If you want a beat of tension: the galeb duhr hesitates — *"He threw me out for a broken face. Now you ask me to break his?"* — a DC 13 Persuasion or a kind word from a PC steadies it.)
- **The redemption lands twice:** the exile is proven the truer carver, and Decha wakes to find the creature he scorned is the one who saved him.

### ② Daein Speaks With the Stone Father's Voice — *the deceiver's play*
Daein (voice mimicry, the face) impersonates the Stone Father / the crystal itself, *commanding* Decha to eat the braincap or to look away.
- **DC 18 Deception or Performance.** **Advantage** if they've read the riddle and frame it in doctrine ("a perfect face is a tomb — *unmake it, my son*"). Success → Decha obeys, eats the treated braincap (*greater restoration*), and breaks free.
- On a fail, Decha stirs, *suspicious* — the charm aura intensifies (DC 15 now) as the crystal "defends" itself. Not yet a fight; a wobble.

### ③ Felkur's Shell Game — *the artificer's con*
Felkur fabricates a flawed duplicate crystal (All-Purpose Tool, **DC 18 tinker's/forgery check**, ~10 min or a montage) and **swaps it** for the real one under cover of a distraction.
- The swap: **DC 18 Sleight of Hand**, or *automatic* if the galeb duhr/another PC draws Decha's focus first. Remove the true crystal from his sight → the obsession has nothing to hold → he surfaces, disoriented.
- Lovely rebel flavor: they *steal* a demigod's worth of infatuation out from under him. Felkur keeps the real crystal (a live Faerzress object — Cascade-thread fuel, or a hazard).

### ④ Smash It — *fast, loud, risky*
- Landing a hit on the crystal: **DC 15 attack** (small, and Decha will guard it once threatened — this may *start the fight*). On a hit, it shatters: **15-ft burst, DC 15 Con, 27 (6d8) psychic** + a Crystal-Cascade sensory tell (free Cascade seed).
- **The gamble:** Decha makes a **DC 15 Wisdom save.** Success → he's freed, but *raw* and grieving. **Failure → his mind shatters with the crystal** (the bad ending below). Tell the players, before they swing, that smashing a thing someone has poured their soul into is a *risk*, not a guaranteed cure — let them choose with open eyes.

## Outcome Ladder

- **Cured cleanly (①/②/③):** the Lodge swings to the party — **stone-giant avalanche muscle** for the assault (the best anti-fire-giant heavies on the board). Decha wakes guilt-wracked and mortified, **publicly indebted to the outcast he exiled** (if ① — install the galeb duhr's honor on the spot; the rebels just toppled and replaced a tyrant *within his own faith*).
- **Smashed, Decha survives sane:** allies — but the Lodge quietly resents the surfacers who **defaced a sacred work** to do it. Bank as future friction, not a crisis.
- **Smashed, mind broken:** no Lodge alliance; Decha is a hollow, weeping ruin (or worse, hostile — the fight). The galeb duhr is **devastated** — you've broken your own guide's heart. A heavy, earned loss.
- **They start the fight (any clumsy threat to the crystal):** see the fail-state — Dreamwalker + 2 Stone Giants (~23,000 XP adj, deterrent-grade). The **charm→Petrifying Touch** combo can petrify a PC for the duration. Give them one round to feel the wall, then a clean exit. Losing here doesn't kill the tour — it just means the Lodge stays out.

## NPC notes
- **Decha** — wants (maddened): only to keep looking. Wants (freed): to undo what he did, and to atone to the one he cast out. Tic (freed): cannot meet the galeb duhr's eyes. *A tragic tyrant, not a villain.*
- **The galeb duhr (Grek)** — wants: to belong again; secretly, to be *proven right*. Tic: still touches its own shattered nose when nervous. Its arc completes here — let the player who cheered it up last session feel the payoff.
- **The grieving giants** — want their elder back but are too reverent to act. Once the party breaks the taboo *for* them, they follow gratefully. They will not lead; they wait to be told the old teaching is safe to believe again.

## Stage directions
- **Open on silence and the unblinking elder.** Let the players feel the wrongness of the perfect face before anyone explains it.
- **Don't hand them the riddle — let them notice the flaws are deliberate.** The "aha" that the whole room is imperfect *on purpose* is the puzzle clicking. If they stall, the galeb duhr offers the teaching.
- **When the face cracks and Decha comes back, give it a long beat.** First breath, the weeping, the slow turn toward the outcast. Don't fill the silence.
- **If they crown the galeb duhr:** that's a campaign moment — the rejected thing made elder by the people who came to use the Lodge and stayed to fix it. Let it breathe.

## Open decisions
1. **Bless ① as the "intended" answer or run fully open?** I'd let ② and ③ win just as cleanly — the table's joy is *their* method, not the right one. ① just gets the richest thematic payoff if they find it.
2. **What's the real crystal worth if Felkur keeps it?** A Cascade barometer, a one-use boon, or a slow hazard (it wants to be *looked at*)? Decide before he pockets it.
3. **Does curing Decha grant a tangible boon** vs. the fire giants (the Lodge knows the outpost's stonework / a hidden carved passage), or purely the stone-giant muscle? Either is fine; pick before play.
