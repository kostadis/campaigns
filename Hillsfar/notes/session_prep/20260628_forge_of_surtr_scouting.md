# Set-Piece — The Forge of Surtr: Scouting the Arsenal

*The scene that delivers the manifest handout and makes the clock real. Companion to `20260628_recruitment_cheatsheet.md` (the clock), `handouts/szith_morcane_forge_manifest.md` (the prop), and `20260628_szith_morcane_encounters.md` (patrols). Can run standalone or as the back half of Door 1 (the Hidden's heist target sits inside the forge complex).*

**The one-line premise:** the party needs to see what Dengor is building before they'll feel the urgency — and the forge is the only place that makes it concrete. This is a *stealth/scouting scene*, not a fight. The combat is in the slave pens (Door 4). The forge is what makes the clock diegetic.

---

## Entry: Three Ways In

**A. Through the Hidden (Door 1 first)**
Hamadh's test sends them to retrieve something from the forge complex — a cache, a key, a stolen piece of equipment. His people route them in via a **maintenance tunnel** behind the azer furnace-feeds: low, hot, invisible to the main garrison patrol. They emerge onto the forge floor. *If they've already done Door 1 clean, they know this route before the scouting beat — it's free.*

**B. Through the Slaves (advance intel)**
A freed slave or one of the Scene-1 escapees sketches the layout in the dirt. The forge has three formal entry points; the eastern one — the loading dock — is lightly guarded at shift change. *DC 13 Stealth or Pass Without Trace trivializes entry; without it, the dock guard is awake.*

**C. Cold infiltration**
No route intelligence: the party finds their own way. A DC 14 Perception or Investigation check on the tunnels leading toward the outpost spots the heat shimmer and soot trail that leads to the forge vents. Entry is harder (DC 16 Stealth without a route) but not impossible.

---

## The Forge Floor (what they see)

> *The heat hits before the light does. Then both at once: a cavern the size of a keep's great hall, ceilings lost in smoke, the floor a grid of forge-channels where molten rock runs controlled by azer who move around the heat like they were born from it — because they were. On the western wall, a row of finished frames, steel and darkwood, each one eight feet long and shaped like a siege bow crossed with a serpent's jaw. Three are crated and stacked near a loading dock at the far end. Four more are half-assembled on the floor, their binding-rings not yet set. The azer don't look up. They are not curious. They are sealed.*

**What the party can observe without rolling:**
- The scale: this is industrial, not a workshop. At least a dozen azer on the floor.
- The engines: identifiable as oversized ballistae or hellfire projectors to anyone who's seen a siege. The serpent-jaw design is unusual — fiend-forged.
- The crates: three finished, roped and stacked at the dock with destination slates chained to the rope.
- The unfinished four: still on the floor, binding-rings missing, awaiting final assembly.

**DC 12 Perception (passive counts):** a large iron-bound ledger-slate hangs on a post at the loading dock, beside the crates. Something is tallied in it. Bramgrim clocks it immediately — Dethek runes, his language.

**DC 15 Perception:** a side passage behind the eastern crates leads toward a deep chasm — and from somewhere beyond it, a sound. Not forge-noise. Something large, breathing wrong. *(The behir. Don't elaborate unless they investigate.)*

---

## The Loading Dock (where the manifest lives)

The dock is the most exposed part of the forge — but also the least watched, because the azer foremen who supervise it are sealed to their stations and don't patrol. **One hell hound** is chained to the dock post during the day shift. At shift change (~every 4 hours in Underdark time), it's walked to the barracks side for water — a 10-minute gap.

**Getting to the slate:**
- **With the hell hound present:** DC 16 Stealth to approach without triggering it. Alternatively, Felkur can fabricate a distraction (a thrown stone, a smell, a decoy item) — DC 14 Sleight of Hand buys 2 rounds.
- **At shift change:** automatic — no roll required. The gap is visible if they observe for a few minutes before committing.
- **Daein's runic key / telekinesis:** can lift the slate from 30 feet away. Auto-success. Clean.

**Reading it:**
- Bramgrim reads Dethek cold — hand Dimos the beat; let him read the numbers aloud.
- Anyone else: *Comprehend Languages*, or DC 12 Intelligence to parse the numerals even without the prose.
- Hand over **`handouts/szith_morcane_forge_manifest.md`** and say nothing. Let the math happen at the table.

**What the fine print reveals (GM only — let them find it):**
- "Awaiting the Bloodheart's seal" on the dispatch line: Dengor must sign every shipment. He is the bottleneck. One blade = zero more carts.
- "Ore short": the slaves' escapes are already starving the forge. Door 4 is already costing the giants something.
- "XI-score azer, sealed to the Anvil": the workforce is ritual-bound. Kill the binder (Dengor) and the binding breaks — the azer stop working, not because they flee, but because the compulsion ends.

## Dengor's Office (optional, high risk / high reward)

Past the forge floor, behind a fire-giant-sized iron door, is **Dengor's command post** — where his seal-stamp lives. The party doesn't need to go here (killing him breaks the bottleneck regardless), but if they want to:

- **Entry:** DC 18 Thieves' Tools to pick the fire-giant lock, or Felkur's All-Purpose Tool at DC 16.
- **Inside:** the seal-stamp (a fist-sized iron brand, his personal glyph), a rough map of the Maerimydra delivery route, and a set of **foreman's orders** in Giant naming the day's patrol rotation (advantage on Stealth for the rest of the forge if translated).
- **Risk:** Dengor is not here, but **his personal hell hound** is sleeping just inside the door (DC 14 Perception to hear it breathing before committing). Waking it brings the forge floor in two rounds.

*Don't push them toward this. It's a depth reward for a party that goes looking.*

## The Chasm (glimpse — seed Messanth's relevance)

If anyone investigates the eastern passage:

> *The passage opens onto a chasm — thirty feet wide, the bottom lost in dark. A chain-bridge spans it, thick links the size of a man's forearm, and on the far wall is a gate of worked iron, sized for a giant. Something lives in the chasm. You hear it before you see it: a slow scrape of many legs on stone, a sound like a millstone dragged across wet granite. A shape passes below, briefly, in the glow of the forge-bleed. It is enormous. It has too many legs. Its back catches the light like tarnished copper, and then it is gone into the dark.*

This is the behir. They do not fight it here. **This is why Messanth is on the table** — a party that sees this and then finds Solom's vault has all the context they need. Don't explain the connection; let them make it.

*If they try to fight it from the bridge: the behir can reach the bridge at initiative 20 (climb speed 50). This is not the fight. A missed round of action and a visible line of sight to how badly they are outmatched should move them.*

## Patrol Encounters (if stealth fails)

**The main floor patrol (if noise is made or movement is spotted):**
- **1 × Fire Giant** (patrol overseer) + **2 × Azer** (forge security). The giant is the threat; the azer are fast responders. *Budget: ~6,600 XP adj = Hard without the Owlbear, Medium with.* **The goal is not to fight this** — the goal is to run. They have everything they came for.
- A loud failure here doesn't kill the scouting beat: they still have the manifest if they grabbed it. The cost is the patrol is now on alert (disadvantage on future Stealth in the outpost this session) and the forge's security tightens before the assault.

**The dock guard (if they blow the hell hound):**
- **1 × Hell Hound** — see encounter pack (Door 1) for stats. Loud, fast, fire. The concern is noise, not the fight itself.

## What They Leave With

- **The manifest** — the clock is now a number, not a vibe. Let the arithmetic do the work.
- **Dengor as the target** — the "Bloodheart's seal" line plants the decapitation without you saying it. A clever party connects it to Quil'bryn's strategy on their own.
- **The behir problem, visible** — they've seen the thing guarding the gate. The assault's hardest chokepoint is now diegetically real.
- **Optional: patrol rotation** (if they went for Dengor's office) — makes the assault significantly less punishing.

---

## Stage Directions

- **Open on the heat and the scale.** The forge is impressive in a grim way — don't undersell the industrial horror of what's being made.
- **Don't narrate the manifest's numbers.** Hand the prop; let Bramgrim's player read it aloud. The moment of dread comes from a player's voice, not yours.
- **The behir sighting is one paragraph and then silence.** Describe it, let it pass into the dark, say nothing else. The players will fill the silence with the right emotion.
- **If they grab more than the manifest:** the patrol rotation from Dengor's office, a vial of azer-binding reagent from the reagent bench (a possible lever, a possible hazard) — have these ready as depth rewards for a thorough party without telegraphing them.

## Open Decisions

1. **When does this scene happen?** Best slotted *after* the cold open (they know the clock is a thing) and *before* the slave pen fight (so they arrive at the slaves with the manifest in hand — "we know what they're building and we need your help to stop it"). Can also happen as the back half of Door 1 if the Hidden's test takes them into the forge.
2. **Does the Hidden's heist target sit in the forge?** If yes, Door 1 and this scene collapse into one — Felkur steals from the forge, the party grabs the manifest in the same run. Elegant if you want the session tighter.
3. **Do they have the manifest before or after recruiting?** Having it first makes every recruitment pitch sharper ("look at this number — *that's* what you're fighting"). Having it mid-tour works too; the clock feel lands whenever it arrives.
