# The Keystone Is Dead

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the party's arrival at the Szith Morcane drow resistance (DDEX03-15 *Szith Morcane Unbound*, Parts 2–4, reconciled to campaign canon). Party level 11: Akritas (ranger / Emerald Enclave), Bramgrim (Life cleric, *True Seeing*), Daein (fighter-bard, the face), Felkur (artificer, the schemer), + Owlbear.

**What I understood from the docs (catch me if I misread):** Ten days have passed. Solom's purge ritual *completed successfully* at Sporedome — regional demonic taint is reduced and Brandyr (the captive fiendborne giant) is spent. The party never liked Solom; he treated them as "important but replaceable." So his death is not grief — it's an **operational disaster**, because the entire march-on-Maerimydra plan ran through him. The party will descend expecting to link up with a functioning ally and instead walk into a **leaderless resistance imploding into infighting**, and have to figure out how to get these people organized before anything can happen.

---

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

The session lands if the party converts a **fracturing, leaderless drow resistance + a scatter of disconnected potential allies** into *one organized force with an actionable plan to take Szith Morcane.* Concretely, four moves, in rough priority:

1. **Stop the bleeding.** Keep the ~20+ wavering, combat-capable drow from walking out the door with Quil'bryn (they carry all the outpost layout intel).
2. **Resolve the leadership vacuum.** Prop up Murssvayas, install someone better, or take de-facto command themselves.
3. **Line up at least one additional faction** beyond the drow (The Hidden, the Lodge of Faces stone giants, the Kiaransalee revenancers, the 200 slaves under Rilira, or Messanth the dragon).
4. **Produce a plan against Dengor** — ideally the decapitation strike, not the 11-fire-giant brawl.

If they leave the cave with the drow staying, one extra faction committed, and a target picked — that's a landed session. The actual assault is next session's payoff.

> **GM framing note:** This is a management problem dressed as a dungeon. The party isn't here to mourn a man they didn't like — they're here because their supply chain just lost its only architect and the project is now over budget and leaderless. Lean into that. Dry, exasperated, problem-solving energy is *correct* and on-tone. Modern register and management-speak are in bounds.

---

## The Default Trajectory (what happens if the party does nothing / fails)

- **The drow walk.** Quil'bryn leads 20+ of the most combat-worthy drow out to "found a new drow city served by slaves." Murssvayas is left with a rump of demoralized non-combatants. The resistance is no longer a fighting force.
- **No assault is possible.** Without the drow's numbers and layout intel, storming Szith Morcane is suicide. The fire giants finish their work and turn on **Hillsfar and the Dalelands** — the exact outcome the factions sent the party to prevent.
- **The optional allies stay siloed.** The Hidden keep hiding, the Lodge of Faces stays lost to Daitya's madness, the revenancers pursue their own vendetta, the slaves stay enslaved.
- **Messanth stays caged** in the Shadowfell, festering into a fully shadow-corrupted dragon. The Order of the Gauntlet's recovery mission rots.
- **Quil'bryn becomes a future problem** — a mad, hedonist drow warlord founding a mini-Maerimydra somewhere with slaves of his own.

---

## The Clever Play (what the optimal party would do — so you know when to nudge)

1. **Remember they are NOT starting from zero.** This is the single biggest reframe. The module assumes the party spends Part 1 recruiting the Sporedome myconids and the Ryxyg mind flayers. *They already have both.* Solom's myconids and **Glor's Ryxyg illithids are already in their pocket.** That's the credibility lever to rally a demoralized drow band: *"We already have the mind flayers and the fungus-people. We have an army. You are the missing piece, not the foundation."*
2. **Cure Quil'bryn, don't kill him.** Each PC carries a treated braincap (acts as *greater restoration*). DC 20 Persuasion to get Quil'bryn to eat it; advantage if they play to his hedonism (offer the wine, promise comfort *after* victory). A cured Quil'bryn = the chief defector flips to asset and his whole bloc stays. **Daein owns this scene.**
3. **Don't try to save everyone in four hours.** Pick the *one* highest-leverage second faction and commit. (Recommend **The Hidden** — drow thieves, natural fit for an infiltration party — or **Messanth**, who solves the behir.)
4. **Solve the behir before the assault.** A CR-11 behir lurks the chasm/bazaar. Recovering Messanth means *the dragon fights the behir for them*, ripping the gate open. And Messanth's grievance — he wanted Solom dead — is **already satisfied.** He's the easiest "monster" in the module to talk to right now.
5. **Decapitate, don't brawl.** Don't fight 11 fire giants. Wait in the Forge of Surtr and kill **Dengor**. Kill him + show proof → the other giants flee to comfier Maerimydra, and the ritual binding the ~100 azer to Faerûn **shatters**, sending them home. Pull the keystone; the arch falls. (The party are keystone-pullers by temperament — Veris, Brandyr, the Lexicon forgery were all this move.)

## Alder — the Harper Who Caged Lolth (recurring NPC; enters tomorrow)

> **Who she is:** A halfling rogue, 20th+ level, and one of the most senior Harpers alive — a *coordinator*, not a lone blade. Generations ago she was part of the company that **imprisoned Lolth** (your campaign's very first adventure). The **Blink** let Lolth back out — but Alder has already triaged that: Lolth is loose, yes, and *"she's not coming around the North anytime soon."* The real fire is the **demonic madness spreading through the Underdark** (Graz'zt's Rage of Demons). Alder's job is to make sure that madness doesn't get out and **destroy everything.** That's why she runs multiple fronts at once and can't stay to fight any single one. *(Family note: Alder is the character your son's mother played in the table's first adventure — a living piece of the campaign's origin. Treat her with that weight.)*

**Her function in this arc — the George Washington play.** Literally a commander-in-chief: she *dispatches* teams to fronts and watches; she doesn't win the battle herself. She is *not* here to fix the party's problems — she's here to **take their measure** and decide if they can be trusted with the Maerimydra front while she manages the rest of the war. Her power is presence and judgment, never a combat button. **Do not let her swing a blade in the resistance scene or the assault** — that's the whole point. She could end the Quil'bryn argument in four seconds and deliberately won't.

**What she knows (and gives the party) — the cascade's in-world voice.** Alder is the one who can tell them what's *really* happening: she **sent another company into Menzoberranzan** to put the demon lords in the ground, one by one ("the other part of the plan"). She names them with the weary sigh of a leader who scraped the barrel — *"that was the best I could find"*: **Celia the Druid, Mor the Barbarian, Spots the Tabaxi Paladin, Draco the Warlock, and Connor the Rogue.** The party will never meet them and don't need to. Their piece is here: **Graz'zt is the one demon lord who will slip that trap, and he'll run to the only place still loyal to him — Maerimydra.** The party is going to be standing in the door when he arrives. When the crystals start detonating (the Cascade), Alder is the voice that frames it: *that's the lords falling; Graz'zt's foundation is being kicked out from under him.*

**Voice — Washington gravitas, undercut by halfling dryness.** Economical. Doesn't raise her voice; the room orients on her anyway despite her size. Warm but appraising. Uses their own reputation back at them, then deflates it. Never wastes a word. Sample lines to lift:
- *(arrival, dry)* "I've read about you. The fire giant in the bag. The forged book. Don't preen — I read about a lot of people. Most of them are dead now."
- *(naming her other team, sighing)* "I've got a company in Menzoberranzan right now, killing demon lords in their sleep. Celia. Mor. The cat who thinks he's a paladin — Spots. A warlock named Draco, gods help me. And a rogue, Connor." *(beat)* "That was the best I could find. You understand the kind of year I'm having."
- *(the strategic picture)* "You won't meet them. You don't need to. Your job is the one who'll wriggle loose of all that — Graz'zt — and he'll run home to Maerimydra. You're going to be standing in the doorway."
- *(her actual mission — not Lolth)* "Lolth's out, yes. I helped put her under once; I'll lose sleep over her another night. She's not coming for the North any time soon. *This* — the madness, down here, eating its way up — this is what ends the world if no one's minding it. So I'm minding it. All of it. Everywhere at once. Which is why I can't stay and hold your hand."
- *(the eyes-on-you, declining to fix the resistance)* "I could settle that little argument over there in about four seconds. I'm not going to. Solom's dead, and these people have to learn to follow someone who isn't him. So do you. Go on. I'll watch."
- *(exit)* "You'll see me again. Try to be worth the trip."

**What she wants from the scene:** to find out if this party is a front she can *stop worrying about* — one team off her overloaded list — while she runs the rest of the war. Every choice they make tomorrow, she's grading. That grade is your lever for the rest of the campaign — earn her respect and the Harpers open up; disappoint her and she keeps a cold, busy distance.

## Scene 1 — The Descent (keep it short)

### Setup
- **When/where:** Ten days after the party left Sporedome for Hillsfar. They re-enter the Underdark via the Waydown and travel toward Szith Morcane. The factions can't teleport them in — too much Faerzress — so it's an overland trek.
- **Purpose of the scene:** Burn the travel time, confirm the ten-day gap, and hand them the patrol contact. Do not linger.

### Approach
Random-encounter terrain if you want texture (escaped slaves, slavers, warden patrols — Part 1 tables), but the tone says **don't pad**. One light encounter max. A pack of **escaped slaves** fleeing Szith Morcane is the best one — it foreshadows the occupation *and* the slave-uprising lever, and it can drop the first hint that "something happened to the drow leader."

The scene ends when a **small drow patrol** intercepts them. The drow are jumpy, thin, badly led. A familiar name (Murssvayas, Solom, or their own reputation) gets them escorted in. *(Foreshadow Alder here if you like:* the patrol leader mutters that "the small one — the halfling — got here two days ago and nobody knows how she got past us." Plant her before they see her.*)*

### Branch — if the party leans on reputation
The drow have heard of the surfacers who captured a fire giant and robbed the Spire. Good — that reputation is *capital* for the rally scene. Let them spend it.

### NPC notes
- **Drow patrol leader** — wants: to not die, to not be blamed for letting outsiders in. Tic: keeps glancing back toward the camp like he expects it to be gone when he returns. He already knows the camp is falling apart and is embarrassed by it.

## Scene 2 — The Resistance Is Imploding (the core scene)

### Setup
- **Where:** The resistance's forward staging cave, ~a day from Szith Morcane. (Canon stitch: Solom finished the ritual at Sporedome, then moved forward *with* the mobilizing refugees to stage the retaking — and was murdered here, days ago, by Quil'bryn. Sporedome remains the deep rear base.)
- **What the party walks into:** ~20 drow, packs shouldered, mid-argument, about to walk out. **Murssvayas** is hoarse, out of patience, losing. **Quil'bryn** — burly, smiling, stroking a scrap of fur clothing — is winning the room with promises of comfort. And off to one side, sitting on a crate with her boots up, watching the whole circus and doing *nothing about it:* **Alder** (see her dossier above). She's been here two days. She greets the party first — gives them the strategic picture and the why-she-can't-stay — then pointedly hands them the disaster and steps back to grade them.

### Alder's framing (run her *before* the persuasion mechanic)
She intercepts the party as they enter, sizes them up, and lays out the board: the Menzoberranzan company (named, with the sigh), the coming cascade, Graz'zt as the loose end they'll catch, and the madness-containment war as *her* mandate (not Lolth — she's triaged that). Then the handoff — she will NOT settle the drow argument for them (*"I'll watch."*). This does three jobs at once: it explains the whole campaign architecture in one scene, it raises the stakes on the persuasion roll (a legend is watching them work), and it gives you a reason a 20th-level NPC is in the room without trivializing it. **Once she's framed it, get her out of the mechanic** — she observes, she doesn't roll.

### The news (deliver it flat)
Let Murssvayas deliver it without ceremony, because the party won't pretend to mourn:

> **Murssvayas:** "Solom is dead. Quil'bryn put a blade in him eight days ago, in this cave, while half of them watched and did nothing. The ritual finished first — that much he gave us. And now—" *(gestures at the packing drow)* "—now they want to walk into the dark and start over somewhere the giants haven't found yet. As if there is such a place."

> **GM-only:** Quil'bryn isn't lying about wanting comfort — that's the horror of the madness. Graz'zt's hedonistic taint made him *mean it.* He killed Solom because Solom would never stop fighting, and Quil'bryn is tired. He is offering the exhausted exactly what they want. Play him warm and reasonable, never cartoonish. The cure (braincap) gives him back his horror at what he did.

### The mechanic (Part 3, run as written)
**Goal: 5 successes, DC 15 each**, to persuade the wavering drow to stay. Drow PCs get advantage (n/a here, but any *reasonable* angle a player invents is fair — honeyed lies, threats, appeals to vengeance, displaying their captured-fire-giant reputation, name-dropping the mind flayer and myconid allies they *already hold*). Track successes openly-ish; let the players feel the room tipping.

**The Quil'bryn side-play:** A separate DC 20 Persuasion to get him to eat a treated braincap. Success **counts as 2** toward the 5. Advantage if they pander to his hedonism. This is the optimal line — it converts the ringleader instead of fighting him.

**Outcome ladder (module):**
- **0 successes:** Quil'bryn (drow elite warrior) + 5 drow veterans *attack.* Combat, then survivors stay.
- **1:** Quil'bryn + 20 supporters + 10 more leave. Resistance gutted.
- **2:** Quil'bryn + 20 leave.
- **3–4:** Quil'bryn + half leave; 10 stay.
- **5:** Everyone stays, Quil'bryn included.

### Branch — they cure Quil'bryn
Best case. He stays, his bloc stays, and you've got a guilt-wracked, newly-sane drow elite warrior who *knows the outpost cold* and will throw himself at the front line to atone. Huge asset for the assault. Give him a beat of dawning horror.

### Branch — they kill Quil'bryn
Clean and fast and *very* on-brand for this party. The remaining drow stay (cowed/freed). Cost: you lose the cured-asset upside, and a few drow privately resent the outsiders executing one of their own in their own camp. Bank that as a small future friction, not a crisis.

### Branch — they botch it / get bored and let them leave
Don't force the rally. If they shrug and the drow walk, the assault just got much harder and they'll have to overcommit on a *non-drow* faction (Messanth + slaves + The Hidden) to compensate. That's a valid, harder session — follow it.

### NPC notes
- **Murssvayas Dhuurniv** — wants: someone, anyone, to take this weight off him; he never wanted to lead. Tic: rubs his thick sideburns when cornered. *Will gratefully cede tactical command to the party if they show competence.* He's your release valve for the leadership vacuum — the party can rule through him without having to be drow. (Owlbear's unexplained familiarity with him is still a live foreshadow — drop it if it's fun, don't resolve it.)
- **Quil'bryn** — wants: rest, comfort, slaves, wine, an end to fighting. Tic: strokes the fur scrap, talks like a man inviting you to a dinner party. The madness *is* the seductiveness.
- **Alder** — wants: to learn whether this party can be trusted to hold the Graz'zt door — one front she can stop worrying about while she runs the rest of the madness-containment war. Watches the persuasion play out and **delivers a verdict at the end** — your hook for the whole rest of the campaign:
  - *They rally the drow cleverly / cure Quil'bryn:* a short nod. *"Huh. You're better than your reviews."* The Harpers are now genuinely open to them.
  - *They butcher their way through it / botch it:* a flatter read. *"...Well. You're decisive. We'll see if that's enough."* She leaves less sure, keeps her distance.
  - Either way she exits on her Lolth-hunt with the eyes-on-you note. She returns when the cascade begins, and again at the Graz'zt finale.

## Scene 3 — Building the Coalition (the org-chart scene)

### Setup
The party now has a resistance that (probably) stays. The module says: in a 4-hour slot, **the drow + ONE more faction is sufficient.** Treat the available allies as a menu of org problems, each with a different "manager" to win over. Don't run all of them — let the party pick, and pre-stage the one they lean toward.

### The menu (each is a faction with a grievance, not a quest-giver)
- **The Hidden** *(recommended for this party)* — drow thieves/rogues under **Hamadh**, with **Kâras**, a Vhaeraun (god of thievery) zealot. Hamadh wants to *rule* Szith Morcane after the giants are gone — so he's an ally with a post-war agenda. Natural fit for an infiltration crew; gives the party stealth routes and inside knowledge. *Watch:* Hamadh is a rival to Rilira (the noble) and to Murssvayas for who runs the place after.
- **The Lodge of Faces** — stone giants who carve faces, led by **Daitya**, who is *lost to Graz'zt's madness of infatuation* after carving a Faerzress crystal. Reached via **Grek**, a galeb duhr the giants rejected (cracked face). Curing/snapping Daitya out of it (braincap? the myconids?) wins a heavy-hitter wrecking crew vs. the fire giants. High effort, high payoff.
- **The Revenancers** — Kiaransalee's undead, led by **Velasta T'sarran**, a revenant priestess, with **Asala** (wants revenge on the giants for killing her lover). *Velasta wanted Solom dead and Szith Morcane under Kiaransalee's priestesses.* **Quil'bryn just handed her half her wish.** Allying with her is a Faustian bargain — she'll want the outpost theocratized to a death-goddess after. Bramgrim will *hate* this. Great party-friction fuel.
- **The Slaves (~200)** under **Rilira** (former noble house head, wants her power back) — found in the Slave Dwellings; **Korkan** the hobgoblin slave-boss wants Rilira dead (internal rivalry to exploit or defuse). Lowest combat value per head, highest numbers, best "rise up at the signal" payoff. The escaped slaves in Scene 1 are the thread in.
- **Messanth / Bronze Shadows** — see Scene 4; doubles as the behir solution and the Order of the Gauntlet mission.

### Branch — they pick The Hidden
Pre-stage Hamadh as a transactional, post-war-ambitious ally. He gives infiltration routes (feeds straight into Scene 4) in exchange for a tacit "you get the city after." The party are professional liars; let them promise it and mean none of it.

### Branch — they go wide / greedy
If they try to bag two-plus factions, let them — but burn the clock visibly. The fire-giant work-completion is a soft clock; every faction courted is time the giants get closer to marching on Hillsfar. Don't hard-fail them, just make "we'll get to the assault next session" the natural consequence.

> **GM-only — the dark irony to keep in your pocket:** *Two* of the optional faction leaders (Velasta, Messanth) wanted Solom dead. Quil'bryn — a maddened wreck — accidentally did the one thing that makes both of them easier to recruit. The party's biggest setback (losing their patron) is, from the coalition's view, a recruiting tailwind. If a player notices, reward it. Don't say it out loud otherwise.

### NPC notes
- **Hamadh** — wants the throne of Szith Morcane. Tic: never quite faces you head-on. Ally you can't fully trust.
- **Daitya** — wants the thing she's infatuated with (the crystal/face she carved). Tragic, not evil. A cure target.
- **Velasta** — wants Kiaransalee ascendant and Szith Morcane as a death-cult seat. Cold, patient, already half-satisfied.
- **Rilira** — wants her house restored. Tic: speaks like she's still nobility, in rags. The "legitimate" post-war claimant vs. Hamadh.

## Scene 4 — Scouting the Outpost & the Behir (Part 2 — run light, planning-forward)

### Setup
Once they have a coalition, Murssvayas points them at the outpost. The party shifts into saboteur/heist mode. This is reconnaissance, not a fight — reward stealth and cleverness (their wheelhouse: Akritas's *Pass Without Trace*, Daein's illusions, Felkur's tools, Bramgrim's *True Seeing*).

### The layout (what scouting reveals)
- **The Chasm & the Web** — 600 ft drop to a black river. The dead spider's web is the only way across; frayed (DC 10 Acrobatics or you fall *1d6×10* ft and entangle). Fire destroys web. Combat on the web is brutal for anyone without a climb speed (disadvantage; fall on any hit, DC 15 Str save). **Flag this as a death-trap to the players so they plan around it.**
- **The Behir (CR 11)** — lurks the Bazaar Ruins, fed unruly slaves by the giants. 1-in-3 asleep at any time; otherwise crawls walls/ceilings in the dark. *The* obstacle to a quiet entry. **This is what Messanth is for.**
- **The Barracks** — ~4d6 azer + 1d6 hell hounds, dispersed. Don't fight them all; avoid or thin.
- **The Inverted Tower** — Dengor forced the wizards out but its wards keep *everyone* out, including him. Sealed. (Foreshadow: this was Solom's school. Something of his may still be in there.)
- **The Main Cavern (Commoner's Level)** — vast (700×1400 ft), five terraces, fungus farms + rothé pastures up front, slaves in the middle, Dengor's half-built palace + the giants in the back. A **third Underdark entrance** (the giants' original way in) exists — find it via the drow resistance. Two doubled azer/hell-hound patrols guard it from *behind*.
- **Where's Dengor?** When they enter the Main Cavern, roll **1d20**: **1–5** asleep in his Cave (3J), **6–15** in his Palace (3F), **16–20** in the Forge of Surtr (Area 4). This determines the decapitation setup.

### The two assault doctrines (let them choose; this is the plan they leave the session with)
1. **Decapitation (recommended).** Wait in/near the Forge of Surtr, take Dengor in isolation. Kill him + show proof → the other ~10 giants *flee* and the azer-binding ritual *shatters* (100 azer vanish). Murssvayas offers a covert way in: he and his wizards cast *gaseous form* to slip the party through the collapsed House Morcane tunnels into the forge. **This is the engineer's win.**
2. **Open war.** Coalition vs. the full garrison — needs the drow AND the Lodge stone giants AND the revenancers AND the slave uprising to have a prayer. This is the "if they rallied everyone" path; bigger, bloodier, next-session-sized.

### Branch — they recover Messanth first (Bronze Shadows)
Murssvayas (now leader) lets them search **Solom's chambers** for the means to reach Messanth. Messanth = young **bronze-shadow dragon**, captured years ago by Solom and imprisoned in the Shadowfell, slowly corrupting. He *wanted Solom dead* — and Solom is dead. He can be **reasoned with.** Freed, he engages the behir and tears the gate open (Area A / Bazaar), solving the party's hardest infiltration problem and clearing the chasm. Doubles as the **Order of the Gauntlet recovery mission** (route the ask to Akritas via a telepathic coalition briefing — Regilastadar, the ancient bronze, wants his progeny home).

> **GM-only:** Messanth is half-shadow now. Recovering him for the Gauntlet is morally gray — is he still the dragon they wanted back, or something the Shadowfell rewrote? Leave that ambiguous. He'll honor a deal to fight the behir/giants; his long-term loyalty is a future thread, not tonight's.

### NPC notes
- **Dengor Bloodheart** — fire giant forgepriest of Surtr; megalomaniac, power-thirsty, building himself a palace and consorting with demons. Wants: to be a *king*, not a frontier overseer. Tic: vanishes into his finished palace rear for hours (Graz'zt communion?). *The keystone.*
- **Messanth** — wants: out, and Solom dead (done). Tic: speaks in two voices, bronze and shadow, slightly out of sync. Reasonable *now* in a way he won't stay.

## Scene 5 — The Forge of Surtr (stub — likely next session)

- Then it's Dengor, isolated, on the party's terms. Save the actual fight for a fresh session with everyone's spell slots full.

## Anticipated player questions

- **"Solom's dead? Good riddance — who's in charge now?"** → Murssvayas, barely, and he doesn't want to be. The honest answer is *no one* — that's the disaster. The party can fix it by backing him, replacing him (Xaven? Rilira? Hamadh?), or running it themselves through a drow figurehead.
- **"Did the ritual at least work?"** → Yes. Taint reduced, region's fiendborne corruption weakened, Brandyr spent. It's the one thing that went right — and the dead man's last gift. *(Open decision: does it grant a tangible boon vs. the Maerimydra giants? See below.)*
- **"Where's Xaven?"** → GM decision (see Open). He's the surviving alchemist who understood Solom's work — the natural successor/brains. Strong org solution if you want one. Decide before play.
- **"Can we just take over the resistance ourselves?"** → They can take *tactical command* (Murssvayas will cede it gratefully). They can't be *figureheads* — the drow won't rally behind surfacers/outsiders as their leader. They need a drow face out front. That tension is the scene.
- **"Can we just kill Quil'bryn and skip the talking?"** → Yes (the 0-success / fight path, or any time). It works and it's fast. It costs the cured-asset upside and earns quiet resentment. Their call.
- **"Do we have to save all these factions?"** → No. Drow + one more is enough for a viable assault. Going wide burns the clock toward the giants marching on Hillsfar.
- **"Haven't we already *been* to Maerimydra?"** → Yes — and that's a real continuity question for you, not them (see Open decisions). In-fiction: this is the *staging base* to retake it properly, with an army instead of a smash-and-grab.

## Stage directions (timing / silence cues)

- **When Murssvayas says "Solom is dead," do NOT editorialize or cue emotion.** Deliver it flat and let the silence sit. The players didn't like him; let *their* (lack of) reaction set the temperature. If they're cold and tactical about it, that's correct — match them. The disaster is logistical; play it logistical.
- **Play Quil'bryn warm, never villainous.** He's the most reasonable-sounding person in the room. The horror is that the exhausted drow *agree with him.* If the party cures him, give him one beat of pure horror before anyone speaks — then move on.
- **The behir reveal is an "oh no," not a "roll initiative."** Let them spot it crawling the ceiling in the dark *before* it spots them. The dread is the point; it makes the Messanth solution feel earned.
- **Let the coalition follow, never lead.** Per the module: don't let allies outshine the PCs. NPCs execute the party's plan; they don't have the plan. If the players freeze, have Murssvayas ask *them* "what do you want us to do?" — not the reverse.

## The Crystal Cascade (cross-campaign thread — START SEEDING TOMORROW)

**The conceit.** This campaign and the OOTA campaign (your son's, now finished) are *simultaneous and causally linked.* In OOTA, that party ran Vizeran DeVir's ritual and banished the demon lords — **but Graz'zt foresaw the trap and slipped it** (confirmed: his fate was left open in the son's ending; this is exactly what DDEX3-16 assumes). He escaped to Maerimydra. So your party is the one who catches the loose end the other table left. Two games, two tables, one Dark Prince — and neither party ever learns the other finished their fight.

**The payoff lives in DDEX3-16, Part 6 (U29, the Greater Temple):** Graz'zt's power on the Material Plane is anchored by **Seven Symbols of Evil**, each binding one of the *other* seven OOTA demon lords. Breaking a symbol wounds him:

| Symbol (demon lord) | Effect on Graz'zt when it falls |
|---|---|
