# Set-Piece — Solom's Vault & the Two-Voiced Dragon

*Full staging for Door 5 of the recruitment tour. The search puzzle, deepened, + the dragon negotiation. Companion to `20260628_szith_morcane_recruitment_tour.md` (Door 5), the handout `handouts/solom_ciphered_notes.md` (the cipher prop), and `20260628_szith_morcane_encounters.md` (Messanth/behir stats).*

**The chain, one line:** get past Solom's ward → read his log → crack the cipher (shift back 7) → *"name him to the black mirror"* → speak **Messanth** to the obsidian glass → the Shadowfell cell opens. Then the real scene: a caged bronze dragon who **answers in two voices**, and a party that can't tell if they're freeing an ally or a future enemy. Murssvayas never volunteers any of this — it only exists if they *search*.

---

## Why they're in here at all

Once the leadership question settles, Murssvayas grants the party the run of **Solom's chambers** — "he kept things from all of us; maybe one of them helps." Soft hooks to send them: the behir problem (someone mutters Solom had a *weapon* he never used), the unsearched Inverted-Tower lore, or just a party that loots everything. The room is opt-in; the reward is the assault's hardest obstacle solved.

---

## STAGE 1 — The Ward (entry)

> A drow archmage's sanctum, untouched since he marched out to die. Cold-lamps, a bench of half-finished reagents, shelves of ciphered ledgers — and across the inner door, a **sigil burned into the stone, faintly pulsing.** It "watches."

**A recognition ward** — it knows Solom's own. Solves (any one):
- **Solom's Pendant** *(Bramgrim wears it — docs confirm)*. **The pendant warms as they near the door** (telegraph it). Present it → the sigil reads Solom's signet and dims. *The NPC cleric's keepsake is the key — reward the player who connects it.*
- **DC 18 Arcana** to unpick the weave (Felkur, Bramgrim).
- **Felkur's tools / a dispel** — DC 18.
- **The stolen runic key** (telekinesis) won't open it but can hold the trap's trigger-plate down while someone works — advantage.

**If forced (Glyph-of-Warding teeth):** the sigil flares — **15-ft burst, DC 16 Dex, 27 (6d8) psychic**, and a **shadow guardian** (use a Shadow / Specter, CR 1–3) peels off the wall to harry them for 1 minute. Costly, loud, survivable. The pendant skips all of it.

## STAGE 2 — The Log (the cipher)

Hand over **`handouts/solom_ciphered_notes.md`.** Full solution, the acrostic, and the hint ladder live in that file. The short version for the table:
- Plain text (Drow — read it via *Comprehend Languages* / Akritas / Bramgrim) reveals: **Messanth** (a young **bronze** dragon, now "sings in two voices" — Shadowfell-warped), caged "beyond the grey door," and the **seven-anchors / Cascade** lore Solom theorized but never saw bloom.
- The **ringed cipher block** resists translation — it's a **Caesar shift +7** (keyed to Solom's "Seven" obsession; the seven log entries acrostic to `SEVENTH`). Decoded: **"NAME HIM TO THE BLACK MIRROR."**
- **Let a player crack it.** Walk the hint ladder only if they stall. The payoff is someone reading the decoded line aloud, then turning to look at the mirror.

> **GM-only — the moral seed:** the log's last lines are Solom keeping a *good* dragon caged as "a card to play" — choosing "the realms lose a weapon" over loosing it ungoverned. Plant it. It's the question the whole door turns on: is Messanth a victim to free, or did Solom's cruelty + the Shadowfell already make him something else?

## STAGE 3 — The Black Mirror (the gate)

> Tall as a giant, an oval of obsidian glass in an iron frame, swallowing the lamplight. It shows no reflection. *The grey door.*

**Speak Messanth's name to it** (the decoded instruction). The black drains to a **grey twilight**: a cell of dead stone in a colorless waste — the **Shadowfell** — and in it, a coiled young dragon the dull green-black of tarnished bronze, eyes like two different lights. He turns his head toward the glass as if he's been waiting.

**Pacing choice — open it now or mark it for the assault:**
- **Now:** the negotiation runs this session; if they strike a deal, Messanth is ready to be loosed against the behir during the assault. Richer, longer.
- **Defer:** they've *found* the door and the method; the meeting/release is staged into the assault itself, when the behir actually blocks them. Tighter, keeps tonight moving.
*(Either way, finding and solving the vault is the win — don't gate the door behind the dragon also being resolved tonight.)*

## The Two-Voiced Dragon (the negotiation — the real scene)

**Messanth** = *Young Bronze Dragon (XMM, CR 8)* + half-shadow reskin (resist necrotic; hides in dim light; Lightning Breath can read as shadow/necrotic — full note in the encounter pack). **Lightning-immune, like the behir** — which is the point.

**Play him as two voices, slightly out of sync** — sometimes finishing each other's sentences, sometimes contradicting. The party should never be sure which one will hold the deal.

- **Bronze (what he was — warm, weary, honorable):**
  > *"Solom Ned'razak. You wear his pendant, dwarf, and yet here you stand bargaining instead of gloating. So he's dead. …Good. I'd thank you, but I've forgotten how the word tastes."*
  > *"There is a beast at the gate of that outpost — many legs, breath of storm. I have wanted to kill it for a long time, for no reason but that it is there and I am caged. Loose me at it. That, at least, I will do honestly."*
- **Shadow (what the grey place made — cold, transactional, hungry):**
  > *"He kept us in the dark to be used. Now you would do the same — 'fight our beast, dragon, and we'll discuss the rest.' …Say the rest. We are listening. We are always listening now."*
  > *"Promise us nothing you mean to keep and we will get along beautifully."*
- **The crack — lift this:**
  > *(both at once, the voices sliding apart)* "Ask your question, little ones. Ask which of us answers when you call. **He does not know either.**"

**What's honest vs. what's open:**
- **The behir fight is honest.** *Both* voices want out, and the behir is in the way. He'll duel it and tear the gate open — that part of any deal holds. (See encounter pack: freed Messanth vs. the behir is the trade that keeps the party from fighting CR 11 themselves.)
- **His long-term loyalty is the open thread.** Which voice is ascendant *after* the behir is the future you're seeding. Leave it unresolved tonight.

## The Akritas Pull (the Gauntlet ask — for you, Kostadis)

As they decode/approach, Akritas takes a **telepathic ping** — the Order of the Gauntlet (via the Enclave coalition): **Regilastadar**, an ancient bronze, has long sought a lost progeny taken in the Underdark. *"If the creature called Messanth still lives, the Gauntlet would see it home — and would owe the one who brings it."* This pulls the NPC *you* run toward **free-and-recover**, against Bramgrim's caution and the players' instinct to either con or weaponize the dragon. Good three-way table tension — let it play before you (as Akritas) settle.

---

## Outcome Ladder

- **Freed on clear terms (the deal):** behir solved, chasm cleared, the gate torn open for the assault — the party's single hardest obstacle gone — **plus a banked Gauntlet favor** if they honor the recovery. Bronze voice ascendant… for now.
- **Freed recklessly (no terms / "just go kill it"):** he fights the behir, owes them nothing, and slips into the dark. **Shadow voice ascendant** → high future-antagonist risk. A magnificent loose end.
- **The con — promise freedom, plan to re-cage him for the Gauntlet:** dark, and *very* on-brand. If the shadow voice catches the lie (Insight contest, or just because it expects betrayal), you've made a half-shadow dragon your enemy *and* failed the Gauntlet. High risk, high story.
- **Leave him caged:** the behir remains the worst problem in the assault. Valid — they just chose the harder door at Szith Morcane.

## NPC notes
- **Messanth** — wants (both voices): *out.* Wants (bronze): to be the dragon he was before the grey rewrote him. Wants (shadow): to never be caged or used again, by anyone, ever. Tic: the two voices fall in and out of sync with his mood — calm = unison, agitated = a discordant round. *Reasonable tonight in a way he will not stay.*
- **Solom (posthumous, via the log):** the architect who caged a good thing for a tool and told himself it was prudence. He's not here to answer for it. The party gets to decide whether to finish his cruelty or undo it.

## Stage directions
- **Make them earn the cipher.** The session's best beat in this room is a *player* decoding the line, not an NPC. Hint ladder, never hand-off.
- **The mirror reveal is quiet.** Black to grey, the dragon's slow turn toward the glass as if expected. Don't oversell it — the stillness is the dread.
- **Run the two voices straight, never campy.** The horror is that the *kind* voice and the *cold* voice are equally real and equally him. If a player asks "which do we trust?", the honest answer is the line above: *he doesn't know either.*
- **Let Akritas's Gauntlet pull collide with Bramgrim's caution out loud** before you resolve it. That argument *is* the scene's stakes — the dragon's just the object they're arguing over.

## Open decisions
1. **Open the mirror tonight, or at the assault?** (Pacing — see Stage 3. Defer if the tour's already full; open it if they're hungry for the dragon now.)
2. **Is Messanth salvageable** (bronze wins, the Gauntlet gets their dragon) **or rewritten** (shadow wins, future antagonist)? Decide the *lean* now so the two voices tilt consistently — even if you leave the players unsure.
3. **Does the black mirror come with them** as a portable Shadowfell-scrying tool (useful, dangerous) or stay fixed in Solom's vault? Affects whether this is a one-time gate or a recurring item.
4. **Gauntlet reward** for recovering Messanth — name it now (a boon, a contact, gear) so Akritas's pull has weight, or keep it as pure faction goodwill.
