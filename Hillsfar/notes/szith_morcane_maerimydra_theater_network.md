# Szith Morcane → Maerimydra Theater — GM Reference

GM-only operational map of the whole war theater the party is now standing in: the fire-giant occupation of **Szith Morcane** (the giants' forge outpost, DDEX3-15 *Szith Morcane Unbound*) and its parent capital **Maerimydra** (the destination, DDEX3-16 *Assault on Maerimydra*), both run as a single machine for the demon lord **Graz'zt**. The party's immediate job — tomorrow — is the **recruitment tour**: turn a scatter of feuding Underdark factions around Szith Morcane into one army, break the outpost, then march that army on Maerimydra.

**Why Szith Morcane matters (the load-bearing "why"):** Szith Morcane is the **arsenal**. Dengor's Forge of Surtr — worked by ~100 bound azer — is where the giants' siege weapons are *produced*; Maerimydra is where they're *deployed* (staged to fire on Hillsfar/Elventree). The two modules are one supply chain, not two errands. So the party's stake is **pay now or pay later**: break the forge here, in the dark, with the smiths still chained to it — or meet its output later on Maerimydra's walls, pointed at them, *and* let whatever ships out in the meantime reach the surface. This is the spine. The drow's homeland claim, the clear-the-flank logic, and the home-defense clock are all true and supporting, but *this* is the reason to lead with — it makes the waypoint matter to the Maerimydra assault the party already cares about, and it's their native love language (break the supply chain before it reaches the battlefield). It also finally gives the module's 100 azer + Forge of Surtr a strategic reason to exist, and the **off-switch is the decapitation the party already favors**: kill Dengor → the azer-binding shatters → the smiths vanish home → production stops.

This is the layered who-really-runs-what picture for cross-referencing at the table. It does **not** re-run the scene-by-scene prep — for that see `session_prep/20260622_szith_morcane_the_keystone_is_dead.md` (Scenes, mechanics, outcome ladders, Alder). Also: `docs/chapters/chapter_07_maerimydra_city_of_fire_and_lies.md` (the party's earlier Maerimydra raid), `docs/chapters/chapter_16_szith_morcane_s_shadow_rises.md`, `docs/planning.md` (NPC dossiers + faction states).

**Reconciliation note up front (canon vs module names):** the table renamed a few DDEX figures. Where they differ I pair them as **Canon (module: X)**. The Lodge elder is **Decha (module: Daitya)**; the party's "**Nerevenans**" are the module's **Revenancers** (Kiaransalee's undead under Velasta); the weeping galeb duhr is unnamed in play (module: **Grek**). The Szith Morcane forge-lord **Dengor Bloodheart** has not yet been named on-screen.

## The Chain of Command — the whole theater in one diagram

```
            GRAZ'ZT  (the Abyss / off-screen prime mover)
              │   anchors his Material-Plane power in the
              │   SEVEN SYMBOLS OF EVIL (Undying Temple, Maerimydra)
              ▼
   QUEEN HLEDH  — Castle Maerimydra (capital, the throne)
              │   fiendborne fire giant queen; runs the city, deploys
              │   the finished siege weapons, the slave economy
              ▼
   DENGOR BLOODHEART  — Szith Morcane (the FORGE / the arsenal)
              │   forgepriest of Surtr; PRODUCES the siege weapons,
              │   ships them up the tunnel to Maerimydra
              ▼
   THE GARRISON: ~10 more fire giants · ~100 bound azer · hell hounds
                 · 2 salamanders · 1 behir (CR 11) · drow/slave labor

   ── medium that holds it together ──────────────────────────────
   GRAZ'ZT'S MADNESS (Rage of Demons taint). It is not just an enemy;
   it is the glue and the solvent. It maddened Quil'bryn into killing
   Solom; it has Decha/Daitya infatuated with a carved crystal; it is
   what the braincap mushrooms cure. Every recruitment lever below is,
   underneath, "who has the madness done what to."
```

The strategic point for the GM: **this is a top-heavy occupation held up by two keystones.** Pull Dengor and Szith Morcane's garrison scatters (azer-binding shatters, the other giants flee to comfier Maerimydra). Pull Graz'zt's Seven Symbols and Hledh's whole regime loses its patron. The party are temperamental keystone-pullers (Veris, Brandyr, the Lexicon forgery were all this move) — the theater is *built* for that approach. Do not let it become an 11-fire-giant brawl by default.

## Layer 0 — What the party actually sees right now

A leaderless, fracturing drow resistance they just (probably) rallied; ~20 drow plus a cured-and-loyal **Quil'bryn**; a list of four possible allies a contact handed them ("the Hidden, the Lodge of Faces, the Nerevenans, escaped slaves"); a fire-giant-held outpost they have not yet scouted; and a vague sense that Maerimydra — which a few of them have *physically stood in before* — is the real prize. They do **not** yet see: that two of their recruitment targets secretly *wanted Solom dead* (so the disaster is a recruiting tailwind), that the outpost can be decapitated instead of stormed, or that Graz'zt is being quietly disassembled offscreen (the Crystal Cascade).

## The Occupation — Graz'zt's War Machine

### Graz'zt — "The Dark Prince" (off-screen prime mover)

Surface: a name on a six-fingered banner. Reality: the patron the entire theater answers to, and the party's *recurring personal antagonist* — he has already vowed vengeance on them jointly with the escaped Rogue Thought, and his Ch1 surface plot (Malkyn Grenefeld) was the party's first kill.

**Origin canon:**
- The Demon Lord of Six Hundred and Sixty-Six Layers, freed into the Underdark by **the Blink** — the Faerzress event that let the demon lords *in* (Solom's notes, Ch7). Same crystal-physics as the campaign's existing thread.
- Holds Maerimydra as the one place still loyal to him. Cut a pact with the fire giants: fiendish blood for service.

**What he runs:** Hledh's regime, the giant pact, the madness spreading up out of the Underdark, and a standing grudge against this specific party.

**Quote canon:** *"Let the others dance to Vizeran's strings. I'll stay lord of Toril."*

**What the party knows:** [KNOWN] he controlled Hillsfar (Ch1) and Maerimydra; [KNOWN] he and Rogue Thought swore revenge; [GM-ONLY] he is the loose end of a *second*, simultaneous cosmic war (the OOTA banishment) and is about to be disassembled by it — see the Crystal Cascade.

### Queen Hledh — Fiendborne Fire Giant Queen of Maerimydra (not yet met)

The throne of the theater. Rules from the twin-towered **Castle Maerimydra** on the eastern plateau. Pipyap's one-line warning to the party: *avoid her at all costs.*

**What she runs:** the capital, the **deployment** of the finished siege weapons aimed at Hillsfar and Elventree (Dengor *forges* them at Szith Morcane; she *fields* them from Maerimydra), the slave economy, and the giant garrison. Dengor reports up to her.

**Personality:** megalomaniac queen, secure in her castle, has not had to leave it. The party robbed her city under her nose (Ch9) and she never saw them.

**What the party knows:** [KNOWN] she's the ruler, she's in the eastern castle, she's the boss-level threat; [KNOWN] (their docs flag it) "not yet encountered." [GM-ONLY] DDEX3-16 makes *her* the published final-tier obstacle before Graz'zt — and almost nothing the party did in Maerimydra last time touched her.

### Dengor Bloodheart — Forge-Lord of Szith Morcane *(the keystone for tomorrow's arc)*

The frontier overseer, and the single most important name the party hasn't heard yet. Fire giant **forgepriest of Surtr**; megalomaniac who wants to be a *king*, not a frontier manager. Building himself a half-finished palace in the main cavern and communing with demons.

**What he runs:**
- The Szith Morcane garrison and the **Forge of Surtr — the arsenal** producing the siege weapons, then shipping them up to Maerimydra (Dengor *makes* the weapon; Hledh *fires* it). "The work, nearing completion" = his production line.
- ~100 **azer** bound to Faerûn by an ongoing ritual — the forge's workforce, labor not loyalists. The binding is his single point of failure: break it and the arsenal stops.
- Forced the drow wizards out of the **Inverted Tower**; its wards now lock *everyone* out, including him.

**Personality:** power-thirsty, demon-courting, vanishes into his finished palace rear for hours (Graz'zt communion). Tic: builds monuments to himself while the war is unfinished.

**Quote canon:** *"Surtr's fire built this. I'll wear the crown it forges."*

**What the party knows:** [SUSPECTED] there's a giant in charge they'll have to deal with; [GM-ONLY] his name, that he's the keystone, and the decapitation math — **kill Dengor + show proof → the other ~10 giants flee to Maerimydra and the azer-binding ritual shatters (100 azer vanish home).** This is the engineer's win and the party will reach for it if scouting reveals it.

## The Szith Morcane Garrison — the wall the army has to break

What scouting (Scene 4) reveals. Run light, planning-forward; reward stealth.

- **The Chasm & the Web** — 600-ft drop to a black river; the dead spider's frayed web is the only crossing (DC 10 Acrobatics or fall *1d6×10* ft + entangle; fire destroys it). **Flag as a death-trap so they plan around it.**
- **The Behir (CR 11)** — lurks the Bazaar Ruins, fed unruly slaves by the giants; 1-in-3 asleep, otherwise wall-crawling in the dark. *The* obstacle to a quiet entry. **This is what Messanth is for.**
- **The Barracks** — ~4d6 azer + 1d6 hell hounds, dispersed. Avoid or thin; don't fight all.
- **The Inverted Tower** — sealed by its own wards (was Solom's school; something of his may remain inside).
- **The Main Cavern (Commoner's Level)** — 700×1400 ft, five terraces: fungus farms + rothé pastures front, slaves middle, Dengor's half-built palace + the giants rear. A **third, hidden Underdark entrance** (the giants' original way in) exists — learnable from the drow.
- **Dengor's location** — when they enter, roll 1d20: **1–5** asleep in his Cave, **6–15** in his Palace, **16–20** in the Forge of Surtr. The Forge result is the clean decapitation setup.
- **The Forge of Surtr** — the arsenal floor: azer smiths + **2 salamanders** under duress, a **half-built siege engine**, and a **loading dock** where finished, crated weapons go up the tunnel to Maerimydra (a thief can lift the shipment manifest = the clock made legible). **Levers:** beat the forge azer and the salamanders can be *turned* on Dengor (flip his own guards); and this is the room where "kill Dengor → binding shatters → production stops" actually lands.

## The Supply Chain — the arsenal pipe (the load-bearing diagram)

Szith Morcane *produces*, Maerimydra *deploys*. This is the spine of "why the party is here" — break this pipe or eat its output twice (on the surface, and again on Maerimydra's walls).

```
   GRAZ'ZT  (blood pact: fiendish power)
       │
       ▼
   ── PRODUCTION ──────────────────────────────────────────────
   SZITH MORCANE — the FORGE / arsenal (Dengor)
       │
       ├── azer-binding ritual ──► ~100 azer smiths (the workforce, bound to Faerûn)
       ├── slave mining ─────────► ore out of the tunnels (the Scene-1 escapees
       │                            are a leak in THIS pipe)
       └── Forge of Surtr ───────► SIEGE WEAPONS = "the work," nearing completion
                                          │
                                          │  shipped up the tunnel, every few days,
                                          │  a cart goes out and comes back empty
                                          ▼
   ── DEPLOYMENT ──────────────────────────────────────────────
   MAERIMYDRA — capital, throne (Hledh)
       │   fields the finished weapons; stages the surface assault
       ▼
   aimed at HILLSFAR & ELVENTREE (surface)
   (street-map of Hillsfar already recovered from the Rogue Thought's
    lair — that's the targeting intel for the weapons forged here)
```

**The pay-now-or-pay-later mechanic (run it as a dial, NOT a gotcha):**
- **What they break at Szith Morcane, they don't face again.** Kill Dengor fast → the azer-binding shatters → the ~100 smiths vanish home → **production stops cold.** Decapitation and "break the arsenal" are the *same action*.
- **What's already shipped is already gone — up to Maerimydra's walls.** Run a soft clock: each faction the party courts is days the forge keeps running and the last carts keep rolling out. Whatever's left in the forge when they strike, they've stopped; whatever already left, they'll meet at the finale (more artillery on the approach) — and whatever it fires at the surface in the meantime, hits Hillsfar/Elventree.
- **Partial credit, always.** Don't hard-fail them for reasonable pacing. The consequence is *self-inflicted and visible*, never a surprise — see the foreshadow below.

**Where else it's vulnerable:** the slave labor (an uprising removes the workforce *and* gives the party numbers); the chain of command (decapitate the outpost and the capital loses its forge). Every recruitment lever in the next section is, functionally, the party prying a node off this diagram.

> **FORESHADOW — make the arsenal visible during scouting (Scene 4).** The pay-later cost only feels earned if the party *sees* it coming. When they recon the outpost, show them the pipe in physical form so they can count what's leaving:
> - A **half-assembled siege engine** on the Forge of Surtr floor — azer fitting fire-hardened plates, salamanders heating the metal.
> - Azer hauling **finished, crated weapons** toward a tunnel mouth marked (in Giant) for the capital — a loading dock, not a stockpile.
> - A **shipment tally / manifest** a thief can lift: "X delivered to Maerimydra, Y remaining." That number *is* the clock made legible — it tells the players exactly what reaching the finale late will cost them.
>
> Then **honor it at Maerimydra:** the exact weapons they watched leave are the ones on the walls. Chekhov's siege engine — fired by their own delay.

## The Coalition — Five Levers (the recruitment menu)

The module says **drow + ONE more faction = a viable assault** in a session. Treat each as a faction with a grievance and a post-war agenda, not a quest-giver. Don't run all five — let the party pick and pre-stage the lean. The thing nobody at the table sees yet: **two of these leaders secretly wanted Solom dead, so Quil'bryn's madness-murder accidentally made them *easier* to recruit.** (Reward a player who notices; otherwise keep it in your pocket.)

### 1. The Drow Resistance — *in hand* (Murssvayas / Quil'bryn)
~20 drow, the layout intel, the only faction already committed. **Murssvayas Dhuurniv** is the reluctant figurehead (will cede *tactical* command to the party gratefully; never wanted to lead — tic: rubs his sideburns when cornered). **Quil'bryn**, cured of the madness and guilt-wracked, is now a loyal drow-elite second who *knows the outpost cold* and will throw himself at the front to atone. **Post-war friction:** the drow won't rally behind surfacers as their *face* — they need a drow out front. That tension is the lever's cost.

> **Murssvayas is your mouthpiece for the "why" (the local ground-truth, not the cosmic frame).** He's the quartermaster who's watched the carts go out — give him the arsenal reveal; keep the Graz'zt-trap picture on Alder, and let the *party* connect the Hillsfar dot (the recovered street-map, the Enclave's warning). Liftable:
> - *(the stake)* "They didn't burn Szith Morcane — they kept the forges lit. Whatever Dengor is hammering out down there, it isn't horseshoes, and it isn't for anyone in the Underdark. It's meant for up. Your sky."
> - *(the pipe — the pay-now-or-later)* "They don't stockpile what they forge. Every few days a cart goes up the tunnel toward Maerimydra and comes back empty. So you can break it here, in the dark, with the smiths still chained to it — or meet it later, on the queen's walls, pointed at you. I'd break it here."
> - *(the handoff / cede of command)* *(rubs his sideburns)* "Solom was the general. I'm a wizard who got handed a war. You've done more with less — everyone's heard about the giant in the bag. Tell me what you need, and who you want, and I'll point you at them."

### 2. The Hidden — *recommended lean* (Hamadh / Kâras)
Drow thieves and rogues; natural fit for an infiltration party; gives stealth routes and inside knowledge of the outpost.
- **Real leader: Hamadh** — wants to *rule* Szith Morcane after the giants are gone. A transactional ally with a post-war throne agenda. Tic: never quite faces you head-on. Rival to Rilira (the noble claimant) and to Murssvayas for who runs the place after.
- **Kâras** — a zealot of **Vhaeraun** (drow god of thievery, the Masked Lord), deeply hostile to followers of **Lolth**. **This is why the party must approach the Hidden *alone*** — without their Lolthite drow allies in tow.
- **The play:** the party are professional liars; let them promise Hamadh the city and mean none of it.
- **What the party knows:** [KNOWN] the Hidden exist, are Vhaeraun-worshippers, are anti-Lolth, must be approached alone (Quil'bryn told them). [GM-ONLY] Hamadh's post-war ambition; the Hamadh-vs-Rilira rivalry.

### 3. The Lodge of Faces — high effort, high payoff (Decha / the galeb duhr)
Stone giants who carve faces; a heavy-hitter wrecking crew vs. the fire giants if recruited.
- **Decha (module: Daitya)** — the Lodge elder, **lost to Graz'zt's madness of infatuation** after carving a Faerzress crystal. Tragic, not evil. **A cure target** (braincap? the myconids the party already holds?). Snap him out of it and the Lodge swings.
- **The way in: the weeping galeb duhr (module: Grek)** — the sentient rock creature the party already met, exiled by Decha for an "imperfect" face (shattered nose). They cheered it up; it gave them directions. It is their credential and guide to the Lodge.
- **What the party knows:** [KNOWN] the Lodge exists, it's stone giants, Decha exiled the galeb duhr, they have directions. [SUSPECTED/GM-ONLY] that Decha is *maddened* (the same madness as Quil'bryn) and is therefore a *cure* problem, not a *fight* problem.

### 4. The Nerevenans — *the party's name for the Revenancers* (Velasta / Asala)
Kiaransalee's undead — a **Faustian** ally. Recruiting them means promising the outpost gets theocratized to a death-goddess after. **Bramgrim (Life cleric) will hate this** — excellent party-friction fuel.
- **Velasta T'sarran** — revenant priestess of **Kiaransalee** (drow goddess of vengeance and undeath). Cold, patient. **She wanted Solom dead and Szith Morcane under Kiaransalee's priestesses — Quil'bryn just handed her half her wish.** She is already half-satisfied and therefore *cheap* to recruit. (Note the surname: the **T'sarran** are the Kiaransalee bloodline tied to the **Undying Temple** in Maerimydra — this faction is a thread that runs all the way to the capital.)
- **Asala** — wants revenge on the giants for killing her lover. The emotional handle.
- **What the party knows:** [SUSPECTED] there's an undead/vengeance faction (the garbled "Nerevenans"). [GM-ONLY] that they're Kiaransalee revenancers, that Velasta wanted Solom dead, and the T'sarran → Undying-Temple connection to Maerimydra.

### 5. The Slaves (~200) — most numbers, lowest combat value (Rilira / Korkan)
Found in the Slave Dwellings; best "rise up at the signal" payoff. The escaped-slave band in Scene 1 is the thread in.
- **Rilira** — former noble house head; wants her power and house restored. The "legitimate" post-war claimant — **rival to Hamadh** for who rules after. Tic: speaks like nobility in rags.
- **Korkan** — hobgoblin slave-boss who wants Rilira dead. Internal rivalry to exploit or defuse.
- **What the party knows:** [KNOWN] there are escaped/enslaved laborers (met a band already). [GM-ONLY] Rilira/Korkan, the noble-claim politics, the uprising-on-signal option.

### 6. Messanth — the dragon / the behir solution (not a "faction," a lever)
A young **bronze-shadow dragon**, captured years ago by **Solom** and imprisoned in the Shadowfell, slowly corrupting. **He wanted Solom dead — and Solom is dead** — so he can be *reasoned with right now* in a way he won't stay. Freed, he fights the **behir** and tears the gate open, solving the party's single hardest infiltration problem and clearing the chasm. Doubles as the **Order of the Gauntlet** recovery mission (Regilastadar, the ancient bronze, wants his progeny home — route the ask to Akritas).
- **The way in:** Murssvayas (once leadership is settled) lets them search **Solom's chambers** for the means to reach him.
- **GM-only ambiguity:** he's half-shadow now. Is he still the dragon the Gauntlet wanted back, or something the Shadowfell rewrote? Leave it open. He'll honor a deal to fight the behir/giants; long-term loyalty is a future thread.

## The Dark Ironies — GM-only levers to keep in your pocket

- **The recruiting tailwind.** *Two* faction leaders — **Velasta** (wanted Solom dead, wanted the outpost for Kiaransalee) and **Messanth** (wanted Solom dead) — had their grievance *satisfied* by Quil'bryn's madness-murder. The party's worst setback (losing their patron Solom) is, from the coalition's view, the thing that makes half the menu cheap.
- **The party are not starting from zero.** The module assumes Part 1 is spent recruiting the **Sporedome myconids** and the **Ryxyg mind flayers (Glor)**. *The party already holds both.* That's the credibility lever for the whole rally: *"We already have the mind flayers and the fungus-people. You're the missing piece, not the foundation."*
- **The keystone everywhere.** Dengor (azer-binding), the salamanders (turnable), Decha (a cure not a fight), the Symbols (Graz'zt pre-broken). The entire theater rewards pulling one stone instead of fighting the wall. Nudge toward it only if they don't find it themselves.
- **Two keystones wanted Solom dead AND the city for a death-goddess** — recruiting Velasta hands Maerimydra's Undying Temple thread a foothold *before the party ever gets there.* If they take the Nerevenans, the Kiaransalee problem at Maerimydra gets bigger. Bramgrim's instinct to refuse is *correct* — bank the friction.

## Gaps the GM can exploit (credible NPC entrances)

- **Dengor has no name to the party yet.** First scout/prisoner/drow briefing that names "Dengor Bloodheart, forgepriest of Surtr" lands as a reveal — the boss gets a face before they fight him.
- **Pipyap's contract is still live.** The imp can re-enter at Maerimydra as a returning asset the instant the party arrives — they never formally closed him out.
- **Vraxen's betrayal journal** is unrecovered blackmail; a Hidden/Velasta contact could dangle it as a price.
- **Messanth's existence is unknown to the party** until they search Solom's chambers — the dragon-as-behir-solution is a reveal you control the timing of.
- **The Crystal Cascade is pure dramatic irony for two sessions** — the party reacts to detonations they can't yet explain. That confusion is the gap; the explanation is the U29 payoff.
- **Alder grades them.** Her verdict on tomorrow's rally is the hook for the rest of the campaign — earn her respect and the Harpers open; disappoint her and she keeps a cold, busy distance.
