# HANDOUT — The Forge Manifest ("Surtr's Tongues")

*The arsenal clock, made into a physical prop. Felkur (or any thief) lifts it during the Forge/scouting beat; it turns "the giants are building something" into a number the party can count down.*

---

## GM WRAPPER (read this first — not for players)

**What it is:** a delivery ledger kept by the azer forge-foreman, tallying the siege engines ("Surtr's Tongues" — fiend-forged hellfire ballistae) produced at Szith Morcane and shipped up the tunnel to Maerimydra. Scratched in **Dethek runes on a slate tally-board** chained to the loading-dock post.

**Who can read it:** It's in Giant, written in Dethek script. **Bramgrim (hill dwarf) reads Dethek cold** — give Dimos the translation beat; it's a clean moment for the NPC cleric to be the one who cracks the bad news. Otherwise: *Comprehend Languages*, Felkur's tools, or a DC 12 Intelligence check to puzzle out the numerals even without the words (the *tally* is legible even if the prose isn't).

**Where/when they find it:** on the Forge of Surtr loading dock (see the encounter pack, Door 5 area / the scouting beat). A thief lifts the slate or copies it; a careful party just *reads* it and leaves it (smarter — no missing-board alarm).

**What it reveals — player-side [the clock]:**
- The giants have built **20** of these engines. **14 are already at Maerimydra.** **6 remain here** — 3 crated on the dock, 3 unfinished on the floor.
- **A cart leaves every two days.** Next departure is imminent.
- Translation: *strike now and only 14 are on Maerimydra's walls; dawdle through the recruitment tour and the last 6 roll out behind them, one cart at a time.* The countdown is **~3 ticks** (each ≈ 2 days ≈ roughly one faction courted).

**What it reveals — GM-side [the levers, hidden in the fine print]:**
- **"Awaiting the Bloodheart's seal"** — every shipment needs Dengor's personal mark. *He is the bottleneck.* Kill him and nothing else ships, ever. (Reinforces decapitation without you saying it.)
- **"Fewer hands in the pits — ore short"** — the forge is already starved because slaves keep escaping. *The slave uprising starves the arsenal directly.* (Ties Door 4 to the clock.)
- **The smiths are tallied as bound stock, not workers** ("XI score azer, sealed to the Anvil") — confirms the azer are ritual-bound labor. Break the binding (kill Dengor) → the workforce vanishes → production stops cold.
- **Destination column reads "MAERIMYDRA — Q. Hledh, by the Queen's road."** Confirms the production→deployment pipe in the giants' own hand.

**Theatrical timing:** Don't narrate the numbers. Hand the slate (or read it) and let the table do the subtraction. The moment that lands is a *player* saying "wait — fourteen are already *there*? And one leaves every two days?" Let the dread arrive as arithmetic. Then shut up.

## THE PROP (player-facing text — read aloud or hand over)

> *(Scratched in heavy Dethek runes, the numerals gouged deeper where a thumb has dragged across them many times. Dwarven eyes read it without effort; the rest is harder.)*
>
> **— TALLY OF THE TONGUES OF SURTR —**
> *forged at the Anvil of Szith Morcane · for the Queen's road · by the Bloodheart's command*
>
> | Batch | Forged | Sent to Maerimydra | Standing here |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | First through Fourth | XVI | XVI | — |
> | Fifth | IIII | II | **II, crated, dock** |
> | Sixth | (in the fire) | — | **IIII, unfinished** |
> | **TALLY** | **XX intended** | **XIV gone** | **VI remain** |
>
> *Cart to the Queen's road departs every second day. Next: two days hence.*
> *Each load awaits the Bloodheart's seal — none rolls without his mark.*
>
> *Foreman's hand, below:*
> *"Fewer hands in the pits. Ore runs short. The Anvil-bound do not tire, but they cannot eat stone that is not dug. Tell the Bloodheart, or do not — he hears only the fire."*
> *"XI-score azer, sealed to the Anvil. They do not flee. We do."*
