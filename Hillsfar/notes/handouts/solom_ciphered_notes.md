# HANDOUT — Solom Ned'razak's Private Log

*A real, crack-at-the-table cipher prop. Found in Solom's chambers (see `20260628_messanth_vault_setpiece.md`). It names Messanth, reveals he's caged in the Shadowfell, drops Crystal-Cascade lore, and — in cipher — gives the release method. For a son + nephew who like puzzles, this is the centerpiece.*

---

## GM WRAPPER (not for players)

**What it is:** the last pages of the dead archwizard's working journal — arrogant, paranoid, his own hand. Most of it is plain (in Drow; *Comprehend Languages*, Akritas/Bramgrim, or a translation beat reads it). **One block is enciphered** — Solom's personal cipher — and it's the operational secret: how to release the thing he caged.

**Three things it reveals (plaintext):**
1. **Messanth exists, and what he is** — a young **bronze** dragon, caged years ago, now **half-rewritten by the Shadowfell** ("sings in two voices"). His *name* is here in plain text — the players need it for the release.
2. **He's imprisoned in the Shadowfell** — "beyond the grey door," reachable via a **black scrying mirror** in the room (the Shadowfell key — stage 3 of the vault).
3. **Cascade lore (free seed)** — Solom counted **seven** demon-lord "wakes" in the faerzress after the Blink, and theorized that **cutting their anchors would drag them back**. That's the Crystal Cascade, foreshadowed in an NPC's own research — exactly the thing the OOTA party is doing offscreen. He doesn't know it's already happening.

**The enciphered block (the release method):**
> Ciphertext on the prop: **`UHTL OPT AV AOL ISHJR TPYYVY`**
> Plaintext: **"NAME HIM TO THE BLACK MIRROR"**
> Cipher: **Caesar shift +7** (each letter advanced 7 — A→H, B→I, … T→A). To decode, shift back 7.

**Why 7 — the in-fiction key, with a hint ladder:**
- **Hint 0 (thematic, everywhere):** the plaintext is *obsessed* with **"Seven."** Seven princes, seven wakes, seven anchors. The number is screaming at them.
- **Hint 1 (the acrostic):** the **first letters of the seven log entries spell `SEVENTH`** (S-E-V-E-N-T-H). A puzzle-brain (Felkur's player) will find it.
- **Hint 2 (give it if stuck):** a margin scrawl — *"as ever, my own count"* — Solom always keyed his cipher to whatever number was eating him. Right now that's seven.
- **Hint 3 (hard rescue):** an Arcana/Investigation DC 15 — "the gibberish is a simple shift; you just need the number." Then let them brute-force (ROT is fast) or hand them 7.

**Putting it together:** decode → *"name him to the black mirror"* → they have the name (**Messanth**, from the plaintext) → speak it to the black mirror in the room → the Shadowfell cell opens / the path to him reveals (stage 3). **One clean chain: read → find the key (7) → decode → speak the name.**

**The moral-gray fuel (play it):** the last entries are Solom keeping a *good* dragon caged as "a card to play," explicitly choosing the realms-lose-a-weapon over letting it loose. That's the seed of "is Messanth salvageable, or did the Shadowfell — and Solom's cruelty — already make him a future enemy?" Don't answer it.

**Theatrical timing:** let them *work* the cipher — don't let an NPC solve it. The win is a player at the table going "wait, shift everything back seven —" and reading it aloud. If energy stalls, walk the hint ladder; don't skip to the answer.

## THE PROP (player-facing — hand this over once they're past the wards)

> *(Drow script, a fine cramped hand. The bulk you can read. One block is gibberish — letters in neat groups that spell nothing.)*
>
> **— S. Ned'razak. My hand, my eyes. Burn if found. —**
>
> • **S**even. Always seven. The Menzoberranzan fools tore the veil, and seven of the Abyss's princes spilled through — I have counted the wakes they leave in the faerzress, and there are seven, no more, no fewer.
> • **E**ach prince is *anchored* to this plane. Cut the anchors that hold them here, and they would be dragged back screaming into the dark. The fools do not grasp what they have built. I do.
> • **V**raxen suspects nothing of my work. Good. Let the fox count his coins in his tower.
> • **E**ight days the purge has run on the giant's blood, and the taint recedes. It works. Of course it works. It is *mine*.
> • **N**ever trust a thing that bargains. The demons offered me dominion — they offer *everyone* dominion. I declined. Chaos cannot be ruled, only outlived.
> • **T**he asset remains where I left it, beyond the grey door, in the place the light forgot. It hates me. Let it. A blade need not love the hand that draws it.
> • **H**eld against the day I have no other card. If I fall, it stays caged — better the realms lose a weapon than loose one ungoverned.
>
> *(margin, smaller:)* — the bronze whelp, **Messanth**, still sings in two voices now. The grey place has been… *editing* it. I no longer know which voice answers when I call. No matter. A cage does not care what it holds.
>
> *(the enciphered block, set apart, ringed twice in ink:)*
>
> ### `UHTL  OPT  AV  AOL  ISHJR  TPYYVY`
>
> *(beneath it, almost an afterthought:)* — as ever, my own count.
