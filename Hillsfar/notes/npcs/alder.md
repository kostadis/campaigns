# Alder — Senior Harper, the Halfling Who Caged Lolth

> **Status:** Staging (notes/, not canon). Promote to `docs/planning.md` / `docs/world_state.md` after she's played at the table. Introduced in the Szith Morcane arc — see `notes/session_prep/20260622_szith_morcane_the_keystone_is_dead.md`.

## Identity

- **Race/class:** Halfling rogue, 20th+ level.
- **Affiliation:** One of the most senior Harpers alive — a **coordinator/spymaster**, not a lone blade. She dispatches teams to fronts and watches; she does not fight the battles herself.
- **Family significance (real-world):** Alder is the character **the user's son's mother** played in the table's *very first* adventure. She is a living piece of the campaign's origin — handle with that weight.

## History

- Generations ago she was part of the company that **imprisoned Lolth** — that first adventure.
- The campaign's **"Blink"** (the Faerzress surge) let Lolth back out. Alder has **triaged this and moved on:** *"Lolth's out, yes… she's not coming for the North any time soon."*

## Current mission (NOT Lolth)

Make sure the **demonic madness spreading through the Underdark** (Graz'zt's Rage of Demons) doesn't get out and **destroy everything.** She runs *multiple fronts at once* and therefore cannot stay to fight any single one. This is why a 20th-level NPC can appear without trivializing the party's work.

## The two fronts she's coordinating

1. **Menzoberranzan front** — a company she sent to put the demon lords in the ground, one by one ("the other part of the plan"). She names them with the weary sigh of a leader who scraped the barrel — *"that was the best I could find":*
   - **Celia** — Druid
   - **Mor** — Barbarian
   - **Spots** — Tabaxi Paladin
   - **Draco** — Warlock
   - **Connor** — Rogue
2. **Maerimydra front (the party)** — because **Graz'zt is the one demon lord who slips the Menzoberranzan trap** and runs home to Maerimydra. The party is "standing in the doorway" when he arrives.

## Function at the table — the "George Washington" play

She is *not* here to fix the party's problems; she's here to **take their measure** and decide whether the Maerimydra front is a team she can stop worrying about. **Never let her swing a blade in the resistance scene or the assault** — presence and judgment only. She could end the Quil'bryn argument in four seconds and deliberately won't.

She is also the **in-world voice of the Crystal Cascade**: when Faerzress crystals detonate (offscreen demon-lord deaths from the Menzoberranzan front), Alder is who frames it — *that's the lords falling; Graz'zt's foundation is being kicked out from under him.*

## Voice — Washington gravitas, undercut by halfling dryness

Economical; never raises her voice; the room orients on her despite her size. Warm but appraising. Uses the party's reputation back at them, then deflates it. Liftable lines:

- *(arrival)* "I've read about you. The fire giant in the bag. The forged book. Don't preen — I read about a lot of people. Most of them are dead now."
- *(naming her other team, sighing)* "Celia. Mor. The cat who thinks he's a paladin — Spots. A warlock named Draco, gods help me. And a rogue, Connor. That was the best I could find. You understand the kind of year I'm having."
- *(her actual mission)* "Lolth's out, yes. I helped put her under once; I'll lose sleep over her another night. *This* — the madness, down here, eating its way up — this is what ends the world if no one's minding it. So I'm minding it. All of it. Everywhere at once. Which is why I can't stay and hold your hand."
- *(eyes-on-you, declining to fix the resistance)* "I could settle that little argument over there in about four seconds. I'm not going to. Solom's dead, and these people have to learn to follow someone who isn't him. So do you. Go on. I'll watch."
- *(exit)* "You'll see me again. Try to be worth the trip."

## Relationship lever

Her **verdict** on the party (delivered after the resistance scene) is the Harper-relationship dial for the rest of the campaign:
- *Clever rally / cured Quil'bryn:* a nod — *"Huh. You're better than your reviews."* Harpers open up.
- *Butchered or botched it:* *"...Well. You're decisive. We'll see if that's enough."* Cold, busy distance.

## Cross-references (existing campaign threads she ties together)

- **The Blink** (`world_state.md`) — the surge that freed Lolth; Alder's origin enemy, now downgraded by her own triage.
- **The Harpers / Emerald Enclave coalition** — she's the senior face behind Akritas's faction briefings.
- **The Crystal Cascade** — she's its narrator; the seven Symbols of Evil in Graz'zt's temple = the demon lords her Menzoberranzan team is killing.
- **Graz'zt** — already vowed vengeance on the party (Ch1 + Rogue Thought pact); Alder explains *why he'll come to them* in Maerimydra.
