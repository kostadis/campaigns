# Chapter 11 Some Folks Never Learn

Date: Apr 12th, 2026

## Summary
With chaos erupting in the dining hall below, the party slipped away from the three-way confrontation they had engineered between Vraxen the Arcanoloth, the fire giant Veperyd, and High Inquisitor Veris. Armed with the stolen key Daein had lifted from the obsidian table, they made for the lift access at the base of the spire. As they moved, Akritas noticed with a quiet unease that their imp guide had vanished entirely, leaving the party to navigate the tower alone. Felkur stepped forward to examine the lift's locking mechanism and, with a craftsman's contempt, mocked the ancient and notoriously unreliable Abyssal operating system powering the device before bypassing it so cleanly that no one below would notice it had been touched at all.

Beyond the lift, the party found themselves at the entrance to a long, fifty-foot corridor lined with floor tiles that seemed innocent enough at first glance. Felkur crouched low and began carefully probing the tiles, his tools dancing across the stone, when he suddenly recoiled — the second tile was rigged with a devastating magical glyph that would have detonated in his face had he touched it. Bramgrim called upon a powerful enchantment that stripped away all illusions and concealments, and through his newly sharpened sight, the pattern became clear: the traps were laid upon tiles corresponding to prime numbers. Stepping carefully around the dangerous tiles, the party crossed the corridor unharmed and arrived at the vault door.

Guarding the door was a stone golem, a hulking, unreasoning sentinel that could not be argued with, bribed, or deceived by ordinary means. Felkur studied the creature carefully and deduced that it was keyed to recognize the specific scent of Vraxen himself. The party could not fight it easily — its stony hide shrugged off most magic — but Felkur, ever the resourceful tinkerer, reached into his collection of gadgets and produced a device he had originally built to extract unpleasant flavors from his food. He turned it instead to extracting Vraxen's scent from the Owlbear, who had been bounding across the Arcanoloth's table not long before and had picked up the smell. The golem's blank eyes registered the familiar scent, and it stepped silently aside.

The vault door itself was a formidable thing of adamantine and mithril, but Felkur's practiced hands made short work of its locks, and the party stepped inside. The chamber was cool and precisely maintained, its walls lined with shelves bearing soul coins, infernal ledgers, and contracts written in the language of devils. At the center of the room, resting beneath a crystal dome on a stone pedestal, was the Lexicon of Oaths — a massive tome bound in devilhide, its pages made from processed souls that glowed with a soft, eerie light, automatically updating itself with every infernal contract struck across Maerimydra. Bramgrim confirmed through his enchanted sight that the room held no further traps, the vault's keeper apparently unwilling to risk destroying the precious documents within.

Felkur opened the Lexicon and searched for his own name, and what he found stopped him cold. Transaction 44781, dated to a winter night long past, recorded the sale of a priceless brooch belonging to the high priests of Maerimydra — sold by one Felkur Oldenwood, intoxicated, blood alcohol content noted at a remarkable 0.18%, for the sum of forty-five gold pieces to a fence named Limping Oric. The notes added, with bureaucratic indifference, that the seller had repeatedly mumbled about a bad trade and that he should have kept the shiny thing. In his drunken fiddling with the brooch, Felkur had accidentally activated its most powerful enchantment: a ward that made its bearer completely impervious to magical location. Because no one could find the brooch or trace who had taken it, Felkur had unknowingly become the Phantom Thief — a figure so mysterious that his theft had sparked paranoid conspiracy theories, multiple executions of high investigators, and years of fruitless searching across Maerimydra.

The party also uncovered something darker within the ledger's pages: Maram il Vass had sold Felkur's information and placed a contract on the entire party, compelling them to retrieve the Lexicon and deliver it to him. But as Felkur read the contract's precise wording with a craftsman's eye for detail, he found a loophole — if the Lexicon no longer contained the information needed to identify him, the contract was null and void. The solution was elegant and immediate. Felkur set to work altering the entry, carefully replacing his own name with that of High Inquisitor Veris, and then forged a new entry attributing the original theft to the Phantom Thief. He also added a new entry crediting the Phantom Thief with stealing the record itself, ensuring that even the forgery would become legend.

While Felkur worked, the rest of the party was not idle. They discovered Vraxen's personal journal, which detailed the Arcanoloth's secret plans to betray his Yugoloth superiors — dangerous knowledge in the right hands. A soul coin ledger recording every infernal transaction in Maerimydra sat on a nearby shelf, practically radiating blackmail potential. Felkur, apparently having learned nothing from the evening's events, also slid a large pure crystal — Faerzess Shard, a powerful enhancer of artificer abilities — quietly into his pack. The sound of the elevator beginning to move broke the moment of quiet looting, and the party heard the unmistakable grinding of stone and the approach of pursuit.

With the stone golem's fail-safe beginning to reactivate and enemies ascending toward them, the party searched the chamber for another way out. Bramgrim spotted a hidden door concealed within the vault's walls, and the adventurers slipped through it, climbing upward through the secret passage toward the very peak of the spire. They emerged into a vast circular chamber open to the poisonous clouds above, where a permanent teleportation circle pulsed with sickly green light — their only way out. Felkur immediately set to work recalibrating the circle using his tools and the arcane crystals the party had collected, a delicate and time-consuming process that demanded his complete and unwavering concentration.

The rest of the party moved to defend him. The Owlbear planted itself over the floor hatch, Akritas readied her bow toward the iron door leading to the staircase, and Daein positioned himself to provide support. Bramgrim cast a protective enchantment over Felkur to shield him from direct assault, and Daein used the magical mandolin to render Felkur invisible, allowing the gnome to slip behind one of the chamber's stone pillars and continue his work unseen. Felkur successfully completed the first critical stages of the recalibration, the crystals beginning to hum with redirected energy. Then, from below, came the sound of heavy stone footsteps and the furious voice of High Inquisitor Veris: "I will have his head. I will get my revenge."

The stone golem smashed the iron door from its hinges, and Veris stormed into the chamber, his face twisted with fury. "Did you think I would not find your forgery?" he snarled. "Blaming me? I will not be killed by the likes of you!" Daein attempted to shove the golem back through the ruined doorway, but the massive construct barely registered the effort. The Owlbear threw itself at the golem with raking magical claws, tearing into the stone with surprising ferocity. Bramgrim called upon spectral guardians that swirled through the chamber like a storm of vengeful spirits, tearing at both the golem and Veris as they advanced.

Felkur, invisible behind his pillar, deployed his eldritch cannon, which swiveled and fired a crackling blast into the stone golem's flank. Akritas marked the golem as her quarry and loosed a well-aimed shot that struck true. Veris, unwilling to be caught in the swirling chaos of the spirit guardians, teleported across the chamber to reposition himself, his eyes scanning the room for the gnome he knew had to be hiding somewhere. The battle hung in a precarious balance — Felkur needed only a few more precious minutes to finish his work on the portal, but those minutes would have to be bought with blood and steel.

The fight raged on with the portal's green light pulsing steadily behind them, each passing moment bringing the party closer to escape — or to disaster. Veris circled the chamber with cold fury, the stone golem continued its relentless advance, and Felkur worked in silence and shadow, his invisible hands threading arcane crystals through the teleportation circle's mechanisms with painstaking care. The fate of the party rested entirely on whether he could finish before everything fell apart around him.

## Locations
### Spire of Inevitable Reckoning
A formidable tower within the fire giant stronghold of Maerimydra. The party infiltrated it by posing as merchants, stole a key during a tense dinner, and used it to access the lift and vault within.

### The Glyph Corridor
A 50-foot long hallway within the Spire where the floor tiles are rigged with explosive glyphs of warding on prime-numbered tiles, requiring careful navigation.

### The Vault of the Spire
A 30-foot by 30-foot chamber kept at a constant 55 degrees Fahrenheit, lined with shelves of infernal contracts, soul coins, and infernal ledgers. The Lexicon of Oaths rested on a central pedestal under a crystal dome.

### Spire of Inevitable Reckoning Peak
A 60-foot diameter chamber at the very top of the spire, featuring a permanent teleportation circle glowing with sickly green energy. Flying off is not an option due to the surrounding poisonous clouds. The site of the final confrontation with Veris and the stone golem.

## NPCs
### Vraxen
An Arcanoloth who hosted the dinner at the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning. His personal scent was used to bypass the vault's stone golem guardian, and his personal journal detailing plans to betray his Yugoloth superiors was discovered in the vault.

### Veperyd
A fire giant who attended the dinner at the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning and was drawn into the three-way confrontation that allowed the party to escape.

### Veris
The High Inquisitor of Mulmaster, secretly hunting Felkur. His name was forged into the Lexicon of Oaths by Felkur to frame him for the theft of a priceless artifact. He pursues the party to the top of the spire, furious over the forgery, and teleports into the portal chamber to confront them.

### Owlbear
The party's sidekick who caused a distraction by jumping on the magical table during the dinner, inadvertently picking up Vraxen's scent. Later used to block the floor hatch in the portal chamber and engaged the stone golem in melee combat with magical claws.

### Stone Golem
A massive, magically immune guardian stationed before the vault door, programmed to only allow passage to those carrying Vraxen's scent. A second stone golem accompanied Veris to the portal chamber, smashing through the door and engaging the party in combat.

### Limping Oric
A fence based in Daggerford who purchased a stolen brooch from a drunken Felkur years ago, as recorded in the Lexicon of Oaths. Due to the brooch's magic, he could not remember who sold it to him.

## Items
### Obsidian Table Key
A key stolen by Daein from an obsidian table during the dinner at the Spire, used to grant access to the spire's lift and ultimately the vault.

### Thieves' Tools
A set of specialized tools used by Felkur to bypass the lift mechanism and unlock the vault door, with exceptional skill that left no trace of tampering.

### Scent Extractor
A specialized mechanism among Felkur's gadgets, originally designed to extract flavors to avoid spicy food. Repurposed to extract Vraxen's scent from the Owlbear's fur to fool the stone golem guardian.

### Lexicon of Oaths
A massive tome bound in devilhide with pages made from processed souls glowing with inner light. It automatically updates with all infernal contracts and served as the primary objective of the party's infiltration. Felkur altered its entries to replace his name with Veris's and add a new entry attributing the theft to the Phantom Thief.

### Priceless Brooch
A powerful artifact belonging to the high priests of Maerimydra. Felkur stole it while drunk and sold it to Limping Oric for 45 gold pieces. It possesses a special ability that makes the bearer impervious to being located, which Felkur accidentally activated, leading to him becoming known as the Phantom Thief.

### Soul Coins
Currency found within the vault, representing captured souls used in infernal transactions.

### Soulcoin Ledger
A record of all soulcoin transactions in Maerimydra, found by the party in the vault and noted as valuable blackmail material.

### Vraxen's Personal Journal
A private journal discovered in the vault detailing the Arcanoloth's plans to betray his Yugoloth superiors.

### Faerzess Shard
A large, pure crystal stolen from the vault by Felkur. It enhances an artificer's abilities, acting similarly to a wand of the mage with a limited number of charges per day, providing a bonus to artificer abilities.

### Blank Infernal Contracts
Blank contracts found in the vault that could be used to bind devils, taken by a party member.

### Arcane Crystals
Blue, white, and green crystals collected earlier and used by Felkur to recalibrate the teleportation circle, providing additional time in case concentration was lost.

### Magical Mandolin
An instrument used by Daein to cast Invisibility on Felkur, concealing him from Veris and the stone golem during the portal room battle.

### Eldritch Cannon
A magical device deployed by Felkur that fires blasts of energy at enemies while he maintains concentration on the portal recalibration.

## Spells
### True Seeing
Cast by Bramgrim to reveal the hidden magical glyphs on the floor tiles in the corridor, allowing the party to identify the pattern of the traps and navigate safely. Also used to confirm the vault chamber was free of traps.

### Glyph of Warding
Magical traps placed on the prime-numbered floor tiles of the corridor leading to the vault, designed to explode if stepped upon by unauthorized individuals.

### Sanctuary
Cast by Bramgrim on Felkur to prevent enemies from targeting him with direct melee attacks while he concentrated on recalibrating the teleportation circle.

### Invisibility
Cast by Daein via a magical mandolin to hide Felkur from Veris and the stone golem, allowing him to work on the portal from behind a pillar without being directly targeted.

### Spirit Guardians
Summoned by Bramgrim at fifth level to create a protective area of spectral spirits that damaged both the stone golem and Veris as they entered the portal chamber.

### Hunter's Mark
Used by Akritas to magically mark the stone golem as her quarry, dealing additional damage with her attacks against it.

### Teleport
Employed by Veris to instantly reposition himself within the portal chamber during combat, gaining a tactical advantage against the party.
