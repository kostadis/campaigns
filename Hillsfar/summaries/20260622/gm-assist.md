# Chapter 17 Berets, Braincaps, and Backup Plans

Date: Jun 22nd, 2026

## Summary
The party found themselves in Hillsfar, still catching their breath from past battles, when Charles — a former revolutionary leader who had fully embraced a dramatic French resistance aesthetic, complete with a beret and an affected accent — approached them with urgent news. He led them to a tavern he insisted on calling a café, where over strong drinks and fine food, he reminded them of their shared victories against Hillsfar's tyranny and the looming symbol of the six-fingered demon, Graz'zt. He warned that the fire giants of Szith Morcane were nearing the completion of some terrible work and had begun turning their eyes toward the surface world. The party was tasked with returning to Szith Morcane to rally the Drow resistance alongside the wizard Murssvayas Dhuurniv. Before the evening was over, Felkur proposed a bold business venture — a commercial network of teleportation circles he called "Stargate" — and Charles, dropping his revolutionary persona entirely, launched into an enthusiastic discussion of guild paperwork, tax benefits, and securing land through Istana Rao, promising to handle everything while the party was away.

The following day, the party made their way through The Waydown, a village serving as a gateway to the Underdark, where an Emerald Enclave contact helped them pass the guards without incident. As they descended into the eternal darkness below and began the long march toward Szith Morcane, a figure materialized from the shadows without warning — a halfling woman bearing the symbol of the Harpers on her chest. She introduced herself as Alder, and her blunt, world-weary manner made it immediately clear she was no ordinary operative. She explained that she was the head Harper overseeing operations across the Underdark, and that she had already dispatched a group of five adventurers — Celia, Mor, Spots, Draco, and Connor — toward Menzoberranzan to deal with the demon lord invasion. She recounted how a reckless spell cast by Gromph Baenre, the Archmage of Menzoberranzan, had accidentally torn open a passage between the Abyss and the material plane while attempting to free Lolth from her interdimensional prison, flooding the world with demon lords. Graz'zt, she warned, had a backup plan to flee to Maerimydra if the other adventurers succeeded in Menzoberranzan, and the party was her contingency — her backup plan to the backup plan. Before vanishing back into the darkness, she used her formidable power to teleport the entire group deep into the Underdark, sparing them days of travel, while Bramgrim managed to get her to sign his sword with a quill before she disappeared, muttering about the sorry state of the realms.

Now close to Szith Morcane, the party began searching for their supposed allies when the grinding of stone announced an unexpected encounter. A creature emerged from the cavern wall — a Galeb Duhr, a sentient rock being whose right half had been beautifully carved to resemble a stone giant's face, though its nose was shattered. The creature was weeping, lamenting that it was too ugly to ever again bask in the glory of the Stone Father, having been exiled from the Lodge of Faces by the Stone Giant elder Decha for being imperfect. The party offered words of encouragement, and the creature's spirits lifted enough that it gratefully provided them with directions to the Lodge of Faces before retreating back into the stone.

Pressing onward, the party stumbled upon a grim scene — a mixed group of Drow, Quaggoths, and Duergar picking over the bodies of fallen slavers. The group's leader was brusque and dismissive, ordering the party to move along, but when the Drow resistance was mentioned, his tone shifted. The escapees revealed they had been slaves in a forge, sent to search for ore in the tunnels, and had seized a sudden opportunity to break free. Before parting ways, they pointed the party toward the location of the Drow resistance camp near Szith Morcane.

When the party arrived at the resistance camp, they found it in shambles. Murssvayas Dhuurniv greeted them with barely concealed desperation, explaining that Solom Ned'razak — the resistance's leader — had been murdered eight days prior by a Drow warrior named  Quil'bryn , while half the assembled Drow had stood by and done nothing. The resistance was fracturing, with many Drow arguing it was better to flee and find somewhere the fire giants hadn't yet reached. Felkur climbed atop a table and delivered a passionate speech, urging the assembled Drow to stop running and unite against the giants before it was too late, and his words struck something deep in the crowd.

 Quil'bryn , the assassin, stepped forward to challenge the party's authority, insisting he was not mad and that his actions had been perfectly rational. Daein, unmoved by the posturing, issued a cold ultimatum — eat the braincap mushroom and prove his sanity in front of everyone, or face the consequences.  Quil'bryn , eager to demonstrate his clarity of mind to the watching Drow, accepted the challenge and consumed the mushroom. The transformation was immediate and devastating. His body language shifted, his voice changed, and the arrogant warrior was replaced by a horrified elite soldier who stared at his own hands and asked, barely above a whisper, whether it was true that Solom had died by his blade. Murssvayas confirmed it gently, and  Quil'bryn , shattered by the realization, drew his sword and pledged his allegiance to Felkur, rallying the remaining Drow to the cause.

With twenty Drow now committed to the fight,  Quil'bryn  made it clear that they would need far more bodies to have any hope of breaking through Szith Morcane and reaching Maerimydra. The party identified four potential groups of allies: the Hidden, the Lodge of Faces, the Nerevenans, and escaped slaves.  Quil'bryn  warned that the Hidden — followers of the Drow god Vhaeraun — harbored deep hostility toward followers of Lolth, meaning the party would need to approach them alone. He also outlined a strategy of using allied forces as a shield so that the Drow and the party could strike at the most critical moments, a plan that Daein diplomatically agreed not to share with the recruits. Meanwhile, Akritas, who had been barely concealing her distrust of the Drow throughout, revealed a hidden agenda — the Emerald Enclave's High Council wanted access to a powerful magical ledger belonging to an Arcanaloth, and she had been sitting on that information the entire time. After a heated debate about the risks, the party agreed to attempt the heist if the opportunity safely presented itself, with the understanding that if things went sideways, the ledger would have to wait. With their plans laid and their unlikely alliance forged, the party prepared to march toward Szith Morcane, recruit their army, and stage their assault on Maerimydra from Sporedome.

## Locations
### Hillsfar
A city recently liberated from tyranny and racism against non-humans, now transitioning under a new regime focused on openness and community. The party meets with Charles here to receive their new mission and discuss a potential teleportation guild business.

### The Waydown
A village serving as an entrance to the Underdark, guarded by soldiers who let the party pass after an Emerald Enclave contact vouches for them.

### The Underdark
A vast, subterranean world of eternal darkness where the party travels toward Szith Morcane, encountering a Galeb Duhr, escaped slaves, and the fractured Drow resistance.

### Lodge of Faces
A location where Stone Giants obsessed with stonework reside, led by an elder named Decha. The party receives directions here from the exiled Galeb Duhr and intends to recruit them as allies.

### Szith Morcane
A Drow outpost currently occupied by fire giants. The party arrives here to rally a resistance force and recruit allies for an assault on Maerimydra.

### Maerimydra
A major Drow city conquered by fire giants and currently influenced by the demon lord Graz'zt. It is the ultimate target of the party's campaign and the location of Graz'zt's backup escape plan.

### Sporedome
The designated staging ground where the party plans to organize their forces before the final assault on Maerimydra.

### Menzoberranzan
The famous Drow city mentioned by Alder as the site of a major conflict involving other adventuring parties and demon lords, where a counter-spell is being prepared to defeat the demon invasion.

## NPCs
### Charles
A former revolutionary Drow leader who has adopted a French resistance persona, complete with a beret and a dramatic accent. He acts as a liaison for the resistance, provides the party with their new mission to return to Szith Morcane, and enthusiastically pivots to business planning when Felkur proposes a teleportation guild.

### Alder
A high-level Harper rogue and legendary hero who previously helped imprison Lolth. Blunt and slightly cynical, she is currently managing multiple crises across the North. She intercepts the party in the Underdark to explain the threat of Graz'zt's backup plan, teleports them deeper into the Underdark, and reluctantly signs Bramgrim's sword before departing.

### Gromph Baenre
The Archmage of Menzoberranzan whose reckless spellcasting accidentally tore a hole between the Abyss and the material plane, freeing Lolth and allowing demon lords to invade.

### Murssvayas Dhuurniv
A Drow mage and resistance ally who is exasperated by the collapse of the resistance following Solom's death. He reunites with the party at the resistance camp and helps them navigate Drow politics.

###  Quil'bryn 
An elite Drow warrior who murdered Solom Ned'razak while afflicted by demonic madness. After being cured by a braincap mushroom administered by the party, he is horrified by his actions and pledges his sword to Felkur, becoming a calculating and loyal second-in-command.

### Solom Ned'razak
The former leader of the Drow resistance who was assassinated by  Quil'bryn  eight days before the party's arrival, throwing the resistance into chaos.

### Decha
The Stone Giant elder of the Lodge of Faces who exiled the Galeb Duhr for being imperfect.

### Galeb Duhr
A sentient rock creature with a partially carved stone giant face and a shattered nose. It is deeply insecure about its appearance after being exiled by the Stone Giant elder Decha for being 'imperfect.' It provides the party with directions to the Lodge of Faces after they cheer it up.

## Items
### Braincap Mushroom
A specialized fungus capable of pushing away the demonic madness of Graz'zt. It was used by the party to cure  Quil'bryn 's madness in front of the assembled Drow resistance, causing him to realize with horror that he had murdered Solom Ned'razak while under demonic influence. The party also previously used it to brew a special alcohol.

### Bramgrim's Sword
Bramgrim's personal weapon, which he presented to the legendary Harper Alder and asked her to sign with a quill as a souvenir. She reluctantly obliged before departing.

### The Ledger
A powerful magical record belonging to an Arcanaloth that the Emerald Enclave is eager to access for its sensitive information. Akritas revealed a secret agenda from the Emerald Enclave to obtain or view entries within it, prompting the party to agree to attempt a heist if the opportunity safely presents itself.

## Spells
### Teleportation
A powerful magical effect used by Alder to instantly transport the entire party deep into the Underdark, bypassing what would have been days of travel on foot and placing them close to Szith Morcane.
