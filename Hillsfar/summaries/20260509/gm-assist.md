# Chapter 12 Clash on Vraxen's Tower

Date: May 9th, 2026

## Summary
Atop the dizzying heights of Vraxen's Tower, the party pressed their assault against the cunning wizard Veris and his Stone Golem. Daein loosed arrows at Veris but failed to find his mark, while the Giant Owlbear tore into the Stone Golem with savage fury. Veris, desperate to regain control of the battlefield, conjured a massive cube of thick, sticky webbing that ensnared several of the party members, binding them in place and leaving them vulnerable to the Golem's crushing blows. To make matters worse, Veris then hurled a bolt of fire at the Stone Golem itself, burning away the webs that had trapped the construct and freeing it to pound Daein mercilessly. Bramgrim called upon his divine gifts to mend Daein's wounds and free himself from the clinging strands, while Felkur unleashed a torrent of flame from his cannon — scorching the webs and liberating his allies, though not without singeing Daein and the Owlbear in the process.

With the webbing reduced to ash, Akritas turned her fury on the Stone Golem, striking it again and again until the massive construct finally crumbled into rubble. Sensing that the tide had turned against him, Veris made a desperate gambit — he conjured an invisible wall of pure magical force around Felkur, declaring with cold menace that there was no escape. At that same moment, a Shield Guardian lumbered up the stairwell to reinforce the embattled wizard, and the sound of demonic footsteps echoed from below. Bramgrim shattered Veris's magical prison with a powerful dispelling, and Daein's arrow found Veris at last, slowing the wizard's retreat. Felkur, thinking ahead, maneuvered his cannon down the chute toward Vraxen's precious library far below, intending to detonate it as a diversion that would draw the archmage away from the battle entirely. The Owlbear seized the moment to hurl the Shield Guardian to the ground, and Akritas, with a final volley of magically-laced thorns, cut Veris down just before he could unleash a devastating burst of fire upon the party.

With Veris dead and the Stone Golem destroyed, the party turned their attention to the Shield Guardian, which clambered back to its feet and swung at Bramgrim — only to miss entirely. Bramgrim's protective spirits swarmed the construct as it moved, tearing into it with ghostly fury. The party scrambled to use one of Felkur's precious magical tokens to conjure a barrier across the stairwell entrance, hoping to buy themselves precious time against the wave of demons surging upward. But before the barrier could be raised, a Mezzoloth — a fearsome demon clad in chitinous armor — burst onto the tower top and immediately exhaled a billowing cloud of choking, poisonous gas that washed over the entire party. Most of the adventurers managed to resist the worst of the venom, but the cloud lingered, threatening to unravel Bramgrim's concentration and leave the party without their spiritual protectors just as the battle reached its most desperate hour.

## Locations
### Vraxen's Tower (Top Floor)
The summit of a high tower where the party is engaged in a desperate combat against Veris, a Stone Golem, a Shield Guardian, and demonic reinforcements. It features a chute leading down to a library and a stairwell from which reinforcements are emerging.

## NPCs
### Veris
A powerful magic-user and 'big bad' who utilized legendary actions, Web spells, firebolts, and a Wall of Force to control the battlefield. He was ultimately slain by Akritas's Hail of Thorns before he could unleash a fireball.

### Owlbear
The party's loyal sidekick who fought bravely against the Stone Golem despite being caught in magical webbing and scorched by Felkur's cannon, and later helped shove the Shield Guardian prone.

### Shield Guardian
A large magical construct that arrived as reinforcement for Veris, attempting to pummel Bramgrim but failing to land a hit before being knocked prone by the Owlbear and damaged by Spirit Guardians.

### Mezzoloth
A demonic entity that emerged from the stairwell as reinforcement and immediately cast Cloudkill, filling the tower top with a poisonous cloud to choke the adventurers.

## Items
### Wand of Web
A magical item used by Veris to conjure a massive 20-foot cube of sticky, flammable webbing that restrained the party and made the area difficult to traverse.

### Felkur's Cannon
An artificer's invention used by Felkur to fire a flamethrower blast that burned away the magical webs restraining the party. Felkur also moved it toward the library chute with the intention of detonating it as a diversion to draw Vraxen away from the tower.

### Special Orb
An item previously stolen by Felkur that grants a bonus to ability checks involving artificer abilities. The party debated whether it also affected spell save DC, ultimately determining it did not.

### Magical Token (Scroll of Wall of Force)
A consumable token belonging to Felkur that, when used, produced a Scroll of Wall of Force. The party used it to create a barrier to block incoming demonic reinforcements from the stairwell.

## Spells
### Web
Cast by Veris as a legendary action, this spell conjured a 20-foot cube of sticky, flammable webbing that restrained party members within it, imposed disadvantage on their attack rolls, and granted advantage to attackers targeting them.

### Firebolt
Used by Veris to target the Stone Golem and burn away the webs restraining it, and later used as a legendary action to deal heavy damage to Akritas.

### Spirit Guardians
A concentration spell maintained by Bramgrim throughout the battle, creating a protective aura of spirits that damaged the Stone Golem, Shield Guardian, and Mezzoloth as they moved through the area, and reduced the Stone Golem's speed on a failed saving throw.

### Freedom of Movement
Cast by Bramgrim on himself, allowing him to move freely through the magical webs without being restrained.

### Mass Healing Word
A bonus action spell used by Bramgrim to restore health to Daein during the heat of battle, returning 17 hit points.

### Wall of Force
Cast by Veris to create an invisible, physical barrier to isolate Felkur, declaring there was no escape. It was later successfully dispelled by Bramgrim. The party also used a token to obtain a scroll of this spell to block demonic reinforcements from the stairwell.

### Greater Invisibility
An active spell on Felkur that allowed him to remain hidden while utilizing his cannon. The GM ruled that Felkur could use his cannon and remain invisible, overriding the strict rules-as-written interpretation to keep the game engaging.

### Dispel Magic
Cast by Bramgrim at fifth level to automatically shatter Veris's Wall of Force, freeing the party to strike back at the wizard.

### Hail of Thorns
Cast by Akritas as a bonus action at third level, turning her arrow into a burst of magical thorns that rained down on Veris and ultimately killed him before he could unleash a fireball.

### Cloudkill
Cast by the Mezzoloth upon entering the battlefield, conjuring a billowing cloud of poisonous gas that forced the party to make Constitution saving throws or take heavy poison damage. The cloud persists and threatens the party's concentration spells each round.
