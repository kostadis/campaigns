# Chapter 14 Saving the world with brains.

Date: May 25th, 2026

## Summary
The party had settled into a rare and welcome stretch of rest deep within Sporedome, the luminous fungal expanse of the Underdark. Felkur put his artificer's mind to work constructing a specialized distillery, and together with Akritas, the two refined the unique hallucinogenic mushrooms of the region into potent concoctions. Daein, ever the entrepreneur, turned his attention toward future business ventures — soups, trade, and the dream of fabulous wealth. Bramgrim, meanwhile, communed with nature in his own particular fashion, which culminated in at least one memorable afternoon of him sprinting naked through the fungus forest, leaving the rest of the party deeply and permanently unsettled.

The peace was broken when a messenger from the Emerald Enclave arrived to summon Akritas on behalf of a coalition of powerful factions — the Emerald Enclave, the Harpers, the Lords' Alliance, and the Order of the Gauntlet. Akritas, swirling a mushroom-infused drink in her hand, tried to convince the others that attending the meeting was not only their duty but also in their financial interest. Daein was skeptical, loudly declaring his singular ambition to acquire what he called "FU money," while Bramgrim, his hair pointing in every conceivable direction from his recent communion with nature, argued that destiny itself might be calling. Eventually, the group agreed — if only to hear what apocalypse was being laid at their feet this time. Akritas also ruefully noted, mid-swirl, that they had missed a golden opportunity by never reading the ledger from their previous heist, a realization that landed with the full weight of hindsight.

The meeting took place inside a hollowed-out dead mushroom roughly the size of a small house, its interior furnished with little more than stools, a low table, and a desk. A cowled humanoid figure greeted them telepathically, introducing itself as Glor — and as the conversation deepened, the party came to understand they were speaking with a mind flayer, a representative of the Ryxyg illithid encampment located several miles below Sporedome. Glor claimed, to Bramgrim's immediate suspicion, that his community had chosen peaceful cohabitation with other Underdark species over the enslavement and conquest typical of his kind. Akritas, still nursing her drink, was not exactly moved to embrace him.

Glor laid out the stakes with unsettling calm. The fire giants of Maerimydra had constructed devastating arcane siege weapons and were preparing to unleash them on the surface world, beginning with Elventree and Hillsfar — a development that, as Akritas pointedly noted, would be very bad for business. Glor claimed to possess the knowledge of how to disable those war engines without the perpetrators ever being identified, and he was willing to share it — for a price. That price was the destruction of a rogue mind flayer, one who had severed its connection to the elder brain's collective consciousness and gone its own way, hiding in a lair deep within a region saturated with Faerzress, a magical energy that weakened psionic and arcane abilities alike. The party debated the ethics of hunting a creature simply for possessing free will, but Glor was unmoved, noting that a mind flayer without the restraint of the collective was a predator without a leash. When Daein delivered a cold and credible warning — that if the promised intelligence proved false, the Ryxyg colony would face consequences — Glor paused, reassessed the group before him, and for the first time took them seriously. A deal was struck, and Glor handed over a map.

Ten days of travel through the Underdark brought the party to the rogue's lair, the Faerzress growing so thick in the final stretch that a constant mental buzz settled over each of them. Felkur prepared specialized ice-infused ammunition for the confrontation ahead, and the group pushed through the entrance into a dimly lit chamber. What greeted them was grim: four bodies — three human men and a drow woman — were arranged around a table as though they had simply stopped mid-conversation. Closer inspection revealed four holes bored into the crown of each skull, and the brains had been cleanly removed through them. Whatever lived here was unmistakably a mind flayer.

The table itself was covered in intelligence of alarming scope. Spread across it were detailed maps of Hillsfar, Phlan — a city now encased in a dome of magical brambles by a green dragon — and two maps of Mulmaster, one of which bore red X marks and mocking annotations scrawled over sections labeled as destroyed. Felkur, drawing on his own history with Mulmaster, recognized the destruction as the work of elemental cults. Alongside the maps were drow rations, empty scroll tubes, playing cards, and crumpled parchments outlining mercantile agreements between an unnamed drow outpost and a group called the Red Blooms. The room also held a stone basin carved to resemble the underside of a human brain, and after some grim deliberation, the party concluded it served as the key to an arcanely sealed door — one that could only be opened by placing an actual brain inside it.

Two other doors led deeper into the lair. From behind one came the sound of guttural voices hurling insults, answered by a defiant voice retorting in Undercommon — a voice Daein recognized as Arrizz, a drow they had encountered in their past. The second door was noticeably colder than anything else in the lair, radiating a chill that gave the party pause. With Arrizz apparently imprisoned somewhere beyond the first door and something unknown lurking behind the second, the party stood at a crossroads, the rogue mind flayer's lair stretching out before them, full of secrets and danger yet to be uncovered.

## Locations
### Sporedome
A vibrant fungal environment in the Underdark filled with diverse and hallucinogenic mushrooms, where the party rested and established a temporary distillery.

### Ryxyg
An illithid encampment located several miles below Sporedome, known for engaging in peaceful trade with Myconids. Home community of the mind flayer Glor.

### Hollowed-out Mushroom Dwelling
A mostly stable, dead mushroom roughly the size of a small house used as a neutral meeting place. The interior contains basic furniture including stools, a low table, and a desk.

### Maerimydra
A drow city currently occupied by fire giants with demonic ties. Glor claims the giants have built devastating arcane siege weapons there intended for use against the surface world.

### The Rogue Thought's Lair
A dark, foreboding complex deep in the Underdark, heavily permeated by Faerzress. Features chambers with pools of glowing liquid, corpses with their brains removed, a brain-shaped basin lock, and doors of varying temperatures.

## NPCs
### Emerald Enclave Messenger
A representative of the Emerald Enclave who tracked down the party in Sporedome to deliver a mission briefing to Akritas on behalf of multiple factions.

### Glor
A mind flayer representative from the Ryxyg community who communicates telepathically and wears a cowl. He seeks the destruction of a 'rogue thought' — a mind flayer disconnected from the collective — and offers strategic intelligence on disabling Maerimydra's arcane siege weapons in exchange. He was initially dismissive of the party but grew to respect them after Daein's intimidating warning.

### Arrizz
A drow acquaintance of the party from their past adventures, currently held prisoner in the rogue mind flayer's lair. He is heard shouting defiant insults at his captors in Undercommon.

## Items
### Hallucinogenic Mushrooms
Unique Underdark fungi found in Sporedome. Felkur and Akritas used them as the primary ingredient in their distillery experiments, while Bramgrim consumed them during his nature communing — with notably uninhibited results.

### Mushroom Distillery
A specialized apparatus constructed by Felkur using his artificer skills to refine and distill the unique properties of Underdark mushrooms into potent substances.

### Map of Hillsfar
A highly detailed and large map found on the table in the rogue's lair, showing every street, building, and piece of infrastructure within the city of Hillsfar. Considered valuable in the right hands.

### Map of Phlan
A detailed map of the town of Phlan found in the rogue's lair, approximately a year or two old. It depicts 'The Thicket,' a dense dome of thick brambles and briars erected around the city by a green dragon that took the city by force.

### Maps of Mulmaster
Two maps of Mulmaster found in the rogue's lair. The newer map shows harbor sections marked with red X's and mocking annotations over destroyed areas. The older map details trade routes, guard rotations, and patrol schedules. Felkur identified the destruction as the work of elemental cults.

### Elemental Ice Bullets
Specialized ammunition prepared by Felkur the artificer before entering the rogue's lair, infused with ice energy to exploit potential vulnerabilities of the enemies within.

### Brain Basin
A stone basin found in the first chamber of the rogue's lair, carved to resemble the underside of a human brain. The party deduced it serves as a macabre biological lock for a nearby arcanely sealed door, requiring an actual brain to be placed inside to open it.

### Map to the Rogue's Lair
A map provided by Glor illustrating the path of least resistance through the Underdark to the rogue mind flayer's lair, with the journey estimated to take approximately ten days.

## Spells
### Arcane Lock
A powerful magical seal placed upon a door in the rogue's lair, rendering it immune to mechanical tampering. The party discovered it could not be bypassed by conventional means and instead required a biological key — a brain placed in the nearby basin — to open.
