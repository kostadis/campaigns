# Chapter 10 Scent of a Golem, Soul of a Contract

Date: Apr 5th, 2026

## Summary
With chaos erupting below them at the dinner table of the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning, the party slipped away from the three-way confrontation they had so carefully engineered between Vraxen, Veperyd, and the High Inquisitor Veris. They ascended the tower with the stolen key in hand, though Akritas noted with unease that their imp guide had vanished somewhere in the commotion. Felkur wasted no time at the lift access, his expert hands working the mechanism with such practiced ease that he couldn't help but mock the archaic "Abyssal 23" operating system controlling it—and his flawless work left no trace that anyone had passed through at all.

The upper corridor greeted them with an unsettling stillness, its floor covered in a series of tiles that seemed almost innocent at first glance. Felkur crouched low and began probing the floor carefully, his instincts screaming at him to stop just before his fingers grazed the second tile—a Glyph of Warding that would have detonated in his face. Bramgrim called upon a powerful enchantment that stripped away all magical concealment, and through his newly sharpened sight, the truth of the corridor was laid bare: every tile corresponding to a prime number was a trap waiting to be sprung. Stepping only on the safe tiles, the party picked their way through the corridor and arrived at the vault door without a scratch.

Standing before that door, however, was a stone golem—silent, immovable, and utterly indifferent to reason or argument. Felkur studied it carefully and came to a troubling conclusion: the construct had been keyed to recognize Vraxen by scent alone, and without that scent, it would not budge. The party exchanged glances, and then Felkur reached into his pack and produced a small, peculiar gadget. He explained, with complete sincerity, that he had always hated spicy food and had long carried a device for extracting unwanted flavors—and as it happened, the Owlbear had been bouncing on Vraxen's table not long ago and had picked up the arcanaloth's scent in the process. Felkur extracted it, applied it, and the stone golem stepped silently aside.

Beyond the golem lay a reinforced Mithril door with three distinct locking mechanisms, the first of which was a physical lock that Felkur dispatched with almost contemptuous ease. The door swung open to reveal a temperature-controlled chamber kept at a constant chill, its walls lined floor to ceiling with shelves bearing soul coins, infernal ledgers, and contracts written in the language of devils. At the center of the room, resting on a pedestal beneath a crystal dome, was the Lexicon of Oaths—a massive tome bound in devil hide, its pages made from processed souls that glowed with an eerie inner light, automatically updating itself with every infernal contract struck in Maerimydra. Bramgrim confirmed through his enchanted sight that the room held no further traps, the vault's keeper apparently unwilling to risk destroying the precious records within.

Felkur approached the Lexicon and began searching its pages, and what he found stopped him cold. There, recorded in meticulous infernal script, was Transaction 44781: on a night during the Twin Moons of Winter Deep, one Felkur Oldenwood—intoxicated, identification verified, blood alcohol content noted at a remarkable 0.18%—had sold a priceless artifact to a fence named Limping Oric for the sum of forty-five gold pieces. The notes added, almost cheerfully, that the seller had repeatedly mumbled about a bad trade and that he should have kept the shiny thing. The artifact in question was the Watcher's Eye Brooch belonging to the wizards of the Brotherhood of the Cloak, left carelessly on a workbench by a craftsman who had assumed no one but a powerful mage could activate it—and yet Felkur, in his drunken fiddling, had done exactly that, triggering an ability that made its bearer impossible to locate through any form of divination magic.

The consequences of that one drunken evening had rippled outward in ways Felkur had never imagined. Limping Oric, unable to remember who had sold him the brooch thanks to its magic, had passed it along unknowingly. The wizards of the Brotherhood of the Cloak, unable to find the thief through their most powerful scrying, had descended into paranoia, convinced the theft was part of a grand conspiracy against them. High investigators had been executed for their failure to produce answers, and the shadowy figure responsible had become known in Mulmaster as the Phantom Ghost—a legendary, untraceable ghost of a criminal. The party also realized, reading between the lines, that Maram il Vass had sold Felkur's information and placed a contract on the party to retrieve the very book they now held in their hands.

Felkur, ever resourceful, identified a loophole: the contract obligating them to deliver the Lexicon was only binding so long as the book contained the information needed to identify him. If it didn't, the contract was void. He set to work immediately, carefully altering his entry in the Lexicon and replacing his own name with that of Veris of Mulmaster, a city in Faerûn on the Moonsea—a satisfying piece of misdirection that would send any future investigator chasing entirely the wrong person. Felkur first pocketed the Shard of Crysalized Faerzess—a large, pure crystal sitting on the vault shelf that practically hummed with arcane potential—prompting Akritas to stare at him in disbelief and even the Owlbear to shake its head slowly, given how the last theft affected them. Seeing their reaction, Felkur had the clever idea of blaming the Phantom Ghost. He added a forged entry attributing the theft of the Shard to the Phantom Ghost, hoping that Vraxen and others would be convinced that the legendary thief had slipped through their fingers again.

Felkur located a hidden door in the vault wall, and the party slipped through it and climbed upward, emerging at the peak of the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning. The chamber was vast and open to the sky, though the sky above offered nothing but poisonous clouds that made flight a death sentence. At the center of the room pulsed a twenty-foot diameter teleportation circle, its silver inlay glowing with a sickly green light that connected to five predetermined locations deep in the Underdark. Felkur immediately set to work recalibrating it using the Fae crystals the party had collected, his fingers moving across the arcane mechanisms with focused precision as the others took up defensive positions around him.

The iron door leading to the staircase was the only other way into the chamber, and the party moved quickly to hold it. Daein and the Owlbear planted themselves against it while Akritas nocked an arrow and Bramgrim stood ready. They didn't have to wait long. The unmistakable grinding of stone on stone announced the stone golem's approach, and behind it came the furious voice of a visibly injured High Inquisitor Veris, bearing wounds from the three-way battle below and screaming that he would not be framed, that he would have Felkur's head, and that he would have his revenge. The golem threw itself against the door, and when brute force alone wasn't enough, Veris commanded it to tear the door from its hinges entirely—which it did, sending splinters of iron flying across the chamber.

Daein threw himself at the golem in an attempt to shove it back through the opening, but the construct was immovable as a mountain. The Owlbear, undeterred, launched itself at the golem with its enchanted claws, raking deep gouges into the stone. Bramgrim called upon a powerful ward, and spectral guardians erupted into being around him, swirling like a storm of divine fury that battered both the golem and Veris with relentless force. Felkur, rendered invisible by Daein's magic and sheltered behind a pillar, deployed his eldritch cannon and sent a blast of fire roaring into the golem while Akritas marked the construct with a hunter's mark and put an arrow into it with practiced precision.

Felkur, even while the battle raged around him, managed to install the shattered nodes and rewire the Fae crystals into the portal mechanism, stabilizing the recalibration and buying the party precious additional time should his concentration falter. Veris, growing increasingly desperate and furious, teleported past the frontline entirely and materialized inside the chamber, his eyes burning with rage as he closed the distance to the party. The teleportation circle continued to pulse and shift as Felkur worked, its green light flickering toward something new—a way out. The battle hung in the balance, with six more agonizing rounds standing between the party and their escape from the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning.

## Locations
### Maerimydra
A fire giant stronghold where the party is currently infiltrating a high-security spire.

### Spire of Inevitable Reckoning
A tower within Maerimydra hosted by the arcanaloth Vraxen. The party infiltrated it posing as merchants, stole a key at dinner, and worked their way up through trapped corridors, a stone golem, and a high-security vault to reach a teleportation circle at its peak.

### The Glyph-Warded Corridor
A 50-foot long hallway within the spire where the floor is covered in tiles, many of which are enchanted with Glyphs of Warding on tiles corresponding to prime numbers.

### The Vault of Oaths
A 30-by-30-foot temperature-controlled chamber kept at a constant 55 degrees Fahrenheit. Its walls are lined with shelves containing soul coins and infernal ledgers, with the Lexicon of Oaths resting on a central pedestal beneath a crystal dome.

### Spire of Inevitable Reckoning Peak
A 60-foot diameter chamber at the top of the tower, open to poisonous clouds above, featuring a 20-foot diameter permanent teleportation circle glowing with sickly green energy and a single iron door leading to the interior staircase.

## NPCs
### Vraxen
An arcanaloth and host of the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning. His scent is required to bypass the stone golem guarding the vault, and his personal journal—found in the vault—reveals plans to betray his Yugoloth superiors.

### Veperyd
A fire giant infused with demonic blood who attended the dinner at the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning and was drawn into the three-way confrontation engineered by the party.

### Veris
A High Inquisitor of Mulmaster who attended the dinner and was secretly hunting Felkur. Injured from the three-way battle below, he pursues the party to the top of the spire, furious over the forgery left in the Lexicon of Oaths, and eventually teleports into the portal chamber to confront them directly.

### Owlbear
The party's loyal sidekick who caused a commotion by jumping on the magical table during dinner, inadvertently picking up Vraxen's scent. Later used his bulk to help block the staircase door and engaged the stone golem in melee with his magical claws.

### Stone Golem
A mindless magical construct guarding the vault door that requires Vraxen's scent to allow passage. A second stone golem later accompanies Varexis to the portal chamber, smashing the iron door off its hinges to gain entry.

### Limping Oric
A fence from Daggerford who purchased a stolen artifact from a drunken Felkur for 45 gold pieces. Due to the brooch's magic, he could not remember who sold it to him, inadvertently creating the legend of the Phantom Ghost.

## Items
### Stolen Key
A key taken by Daein from the obsidian table during the dinner at the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning, used to access the lift and upper levels of the tower.

### Lexicon of Oaths
A massive tome bound in devil hide and stitched together, with pages made from processed souls that glow with an inner light. It automatically records and updates all infernal contracts in Maerimydra. The party's primary objective, it contained the record of Felkur's drunken sale of a priceless artifact—an entry Felkur subsequently altered.

### The Watcher's Eye Brooch
A priceless artifact belonging to the wizards of the Brotherhood of the Cloak that Felkur unknowingly stole while drunk and sold to Limping Oric for 45 gold. His drunken fiddling accidentally activated its special ability, making the wearer impervious to being located by divination magic—inadvertently creating the legend of the Phantom Ghost.

### Shard of crystallized Faerzess
A large, pure crystal found in the vault and pocketed by Felkur. It functions like a wand of the mage for an artificer's abilities, granting a bonus to any artificer ability used.

### Faerzess Crystals from Singed Veil
Specialized arcane crystals collected by the party prior to this session, used by Felkur to recalibrate the permanent teleportation circle at the spire's peak and buy additional time in case concentration is lost.

### Scent Extraction Mechanism
One of Felkur's personal gadgets, originally kept to extract unpleasant flavors from food. Used here to extract Vraxen's scent from the Owlbear so the party could fool the stone golem's security measure.

### Eldritch Cannon
A magical device deployed by Felkur during the portal chamber battle that fires blasts of fire at enemies while he maintains concentration on recalibrating the teleportation circle.

### Magical Mandolin
An instrument belonging to Daein used to channel magic, specifically employed to cast an invisibility spell on Felkur during the portal chamber battle.

## Spells
### True Seeing
Cast by Bramgrim to see through illusions and magical concealment. Used first to identify which tiles in the corridor were trapped with Glyphs of Warding, and maintained into the vault to confirm the room was free of additional traps and to help locate a secret door.

### Glyph of Warding
A magical trap placed on specific floor tiles in the corridor corresponding to prime numbers, designed to explode if stepped upon by unauthorized individuals. Detected by Felkur's investigation and later fully revealed by Bramgrim's True Seeing.

### Sanctuary
Cast by Bramgrim on Felkur to prevent enemies from targeting the gnome with direct melee attacks while he concentrated on recalibrating the teleportation circle.

### Invisibility
Cast by Daein using his magical mandolin to hide Felkur from sight, allowing the gnome to work on the portal's mechanisms from behind a pillar without being easily targeted.

### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Bramgrim at fifth level, summoning spectral guardians that swirled around both the stone golem and Varexis, forcing them to make Wisdom saving throws and dealing significant damage to the golem while Varexis resisted partially.

### Hunter's Mark
A mystical brand placed by Akritas on the stone golem to increase the damage of her strikes against it.
