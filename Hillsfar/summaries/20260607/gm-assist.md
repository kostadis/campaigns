# Chapter 15 Arrizz's Return and the Intellect Devourer

Date: Jun 7th, 2026

## Summary
The party stood before two sealed doors deep within the rogue mind flayer's lair, the silence of the Underdark pressing in around them. From behind one of the doors, a faint and haunted voice drifted through the stone — the voice of Arrizz, a drow they had crossed paths with before. His words were few but chilling: "I was weak once, never again." The party chose to open the left door first, and Daein eased it open with careful, practiced silence.

What greeted them on the other side was a grim tableau. Six Githyanki — five warriors clad in ornate, exotic armor and a formidable knight bearing a massive silvery greatsword — were gathered around Arrizz, who had been bound to a post. They poked and prodded him with equal parts morbid curiosity and revulsion, tormenting him over the vivid, colorful scars that still marked his face from a disease he had suffered long ago. Drawing on their knowledge of Githyanki lore, the party understood these creatures to be ancient enemies of mind flayers — arrogant, warlike, and deeply hostile to anyone they deemed beneath them, which was essentially everyone. It was clear that Arrizz would not survive much longer in their custody.

The party wasted no time. They surged into the room, and the battle erupted in a storm of steel and sorcery. Daein led the charge, cutting into the nearest warrior with swift, decisive strikes. Bramgrim called upon divine power, summoning a swirling host of spectral guardians that radiated outward and scorched the Githyanki with holy energy, slowing those who could not resist the sacred force. Akritas moved with hunter's precision, marking her targets and cutting them down one by one, while the Owlbear — too large to squeeze fully into the narrow hallway — struck at whatever foes it could reach from the doorway.

The Githyanki fought back fiercely. The knight used bursts of magical teleportation to reposition across the battlefield, delivering punishing blows against the Owlbear before slipping out of reach. Warriors attempted to break Bramgrim's concentration and disrupt the spectral storm surrounding him, but their strikes went wide. Daein, fueled by a surge of adrenaline, unleashed a flurry of additional attacks, landing a devastating critical blow that felled one warrior and wounded another. Bramgrim conjured a floating spectral weapon to harry the remaining enemies and called upon dark necrotic power to sap the strength of a wounded foe. When the tide turned decisively against them, the Githyanki Knight made his choice — he cast a powerful planar spell and vanished entirely from the material world, leaving behind only the echo of a threat that the party had made a permanent enemy.

With the warriors defeated and the knight fled, the party freed Arrizz from his bonds. He was not well. The demonic madness that had been seeping through the Underdark had taken hold of him, and his words were erratic and paranoid. The party fed him a piece of their magical mushroom, and after a disorienting moment, the old calculating gleam returned to his eyes. He explained, with characteristic dry wit, that after their last adventure together he had decided the surface world was simply not for him — too bright, too full of people smiling when they weren't happy, and far too hard on his eyes. He had returned to the Underdark, been captured by the Githyanki patrol, and had nearly ended up with his brain harvested as a key for one of the lair's sealed doors. After some negotiation, Arrizz agreed to wait nearby and travel with the party for a time before going his own way, on the condition that they owed him a beer.

Turning their attention northeast, the party approached the second sealed door, its surface thick with fungus and slick moss. Felkur led the investigation, detecting a faint, acrid odor hanging in the air — something like freshly cut grass in a humid field. The party pushed the door open to reveal a stale, oppressive chamber coated in moss and fungus, with several large orbs bobbing gently in the dim air. Another brain basin sat within, consistent with the grim mechanisms they had already encountered throughout the lair.

As the party searched the chamber, Felkur discovered a humanoid shape buried beneath a mound of debris and muck. They uncovered an unconscious Duergar, who stirred awake and introduced herself as Thera Grabelthum. She spun a tale of being an innocent traveler waylaid by a Githyanki patrol, but the party's instincts told them something was off. Under firm and pointed intimidation, Thera's story began to crack — she admitted she had not simply been passing through. She had been working as a spy for the Zhentarim Black Network, collecting intelligence on Sporedome and other Duergar operations. She refused to say more about her employers, citing a very reasonable fear of what they would do to her if she talked.

Before the party could press further, the chamber erupted into chaos. Two Githyanki warriors burst from the muck in a coordinated ambush, while Thera turned invisible and slipped away into the darkness. The warriors struck hard and fast, targeting the already-battered Owlbear. The party fought back, and Akritas cut down one of the warriors — only for something horrifying to happen. From the fallen warrior's skull, a grotesque creature emerged: a pulsating brain walking on four feline legs, an Intellect Devourer that had been lurking inside the Githyanki all along. It immediately turned its psychic power on the Owlbear, unleashing a devastating mental blast that left the great beast stunned and helpless in the middle of the fight.

The party scrambled to contain the threat. Akritas marked the creature and struck it hard, leaving it barely clinging to life. Bramgrim called upon a new power, summoning a towering spectral guardian that lashed out at the Intellect Devourer and reduced it to the very edge of destruction. The remaining Githyanki warrior, seeing the battle lost, used a burst of magical teleportation to slip beyond the party's reach. The session drew to a close with the Intellect Devourer barely alive, Thera invisible and somewhere in the darkness, the Owlbear stunned and vulnerable, and the party standing in a chamber full of unanswered questions — with the rogue mind flayer's deepest secrets still waiting behind the lair's sealed doors.

## Locations
### Rogue Mind Flayer's Lair
A dark, unsettling complex deep in the Underdark containing surgical remains, detailed maps of surface cities, stone brain basins that serve as door mechanisms, and narrow hallways that restrict large creatures like the Owlbear.

### Sporedome
A location in the Underdark where the party had a brief rest before being summoned to meet with the mind flayer Glor.

### The Humid Chamber
A stale, humid room within the rogue's lair covered in slick moss and fungus, with several large orbs bobbing in the air. It contained a brain basin, hidden Githyanki warriors, and the deceptive Duergar Thera Grabelthum buried beneath debris.

## NPCs
### Glor
A mind flayer representing the Ryxyg Illithid encampment who claims his community has chosen peaceful coexistence. He hired the party to hunt a rogue mind flayer and provided them with a map to the rogue's lair.

### Arrizz
A drow acquaintance from the party's past, found tied to a post and being tormented by Githyanki. He bears distinct facial scars from a past affliction and was suffering from magically induced madness when found. After being cured with a magical mushroom, he agreed to travel alongside the party for a time through the Underdark.

### Githyanki Knight
A formidable leader of the Githyanki patrol wearing ornate exotic armor and wielding a massive silvery greatsword. He used Misty Step to reposition during combat and ultimately escaped by casting Plane Shift, leaving behind a clear threat that the party had made a permanent enemy.

### Githyanki Warriors
Green-skinned humanoid soldiers, ancient enemies of Mind Flayers, who were tormenting Arrizz and later ambushed the party from within the fungal chamber. They are described as arrogant and hostile, viewing other races as subservient.

### Thera Grabelthum
A Duergar who initially claimed to be an innocent traveler captured by Githyanki, but was revealed under intimidation to be a spy working for the Zhentarim Black Network. She possesses the ability to turn invisible and attempted to flee during the ambush.

### Intellect Devourer
A horrific creature resembling a brain with four feline legs that burst from the skull of a slain Githyanki warrior. It used a powerful mind blast that successfully stunned the Owlbear during combat.

### Owlbear
The party's loyal sidekick who struggled to fit through the narrow hallways of the lair. He was heavily struck by the Githyanki Knight and later stunned by the Intellect Devourer's mind blast.

## Items
### Brain Basin
Stone basins shaped like the underside of a human brain, serving as macabre key mechanisms for sealed doors within the rogue mind flayer's lair. The party discovered multiple such basins and recognized that actual brains would be needed to open the corresponding doors.

### Magical Mushroom
A special fungal specimen carried by the party and used to cure magically induced madness. It successfully restored Arrizz's sanity and calculating demeanor, but appeared to have no effect when given to Thera Grabelthum.

### Silvery Greatsword
A massive, ornate blade carried by the Githyanki Knight, used to deliver powerful blows against the Owlbear during the skirmish in the lair.

### Glor's Map
A map provided by the mind flayer Glor to guide the party through the Underdark to the rogue mind flayer's hideout, referenced in the session recap.

## Spells
### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Bramgrim, this spell summoned spectral spirits that swirled around him in a fifteen-foot radius, searing several Githyanki warriors with holy energy and halving the speed of those who failed their saving throws.

### Spiritual Weapon
Summoned by Bramgrim as a bonus action, this floating spectral weapon was conjured to strike at enemies from a distance during the battle with the Githyanki.

### Toll the Dead
A dark cantrip used by Bramgrim to inflict necrotic damage on a wounded Githyanki warrior during the skirmish.

### Guardian of Faith
Cast by Bramgrim to summon a large spectral guardian that automatically struck out at enemies moving within its reach, dealing significant damage to the Intellect Devourer and reducing it to a single hit point.

### Hunter's Mark
Used by Akritas to magically mark Githyanki warriors and later the Intellect Devourer, allowing her to track targets and deal increased damage with her strikes.

### Misty Step
Used repeatedly by the Githyanki warriors and knight to instantly teleport short distances across the battlefield, allowing them to bypass the party's front line, reposition, and ultimately attempt to escape.

### Plane Shift
A powerful spell used by the Githyanki Knight to vanish entirely from the material plane and escape the party, leaving behind a threat of permanent enmity.

### Firebolt
Cast by Felkur against the Githyanki ambushers in the fungal chamber. Despite landing a hit, the damage dealt was unfortunately minimal.

### Mind Blast
A psychic ability used by the Intellect Devourer that successfully stunned the Owlbear, leaving the sidekick incapacitated for a round during the ambush in the fungal chamber.
