# Chapter 16 Szith Morcane's Shadow Rises

Date: Jun 14th, 2026

## Summary
Deep within the rogue Mind Flayer's lair, the party pressed onward through its twisted corridors, uncovering a moss-covered chamber where they found an unconscious Duergar named Thera Grabelthum. Under the pressure of the party's intimidation, her story of being an innocent traveler quickly unraveled, and she confessed to spying on Duergar operations for the Zhentarim Black Network. Before the party could press her further, two Githyanki warriors burst from the muck in a sudden ambush while Thera turned invisible and fled into the darkness. The fight took a horrifying turn when a slain warrior's skull cracked open to reveal an intellect devourer — a pulsating brain on legs — which unleashed a devastating psychic blast that left the Owlbear stunned and helpless. The party scrambled to contain the threat, and after a fierce struggle, the creature was destroyed, the remaining Githyanki warrior fled, and Thera was killed in the chaos.

With the ambush behind them and the Owlbear healed, the party turned their attention to the final magically sealed doors. They discovered a freezing chamber kept cold by towering columns of ice, at the center of which sat a massive block of opaque ice preserving suspended brains, small vials of yellowish liquid, and strange otherworldly symbols. A hidden copper plate emblazoned with alien markings lay tucked within the room, and a suspicious chest sat nearby — one the party wisely chose to leave alone. After a short rest to recover their strength, they placed the brains they had collected at each of the sealed doors, unlocking the path to the Mind Flayer's innermost sanctum.

The chamber beyond was unlike anything they had seen — its walls festooned with graven images of skulls and brains of every size, shimmering with the alien pulse of concentrated Faerzress. Three tall, decorative brain jars stood at the center of the room alongside a large pile of cushions, and the acoustics were so sharp that even the faintest whisper rang out clearly across the hard surfaces. There, sipping casually from one of his grisly jars, sat Rogue Thought — the mad Mind Flayer they had come to destroy. His voice slithered directly into their minds as he regarded them with cold amusement and declared that he had been waiting for them for a very long time. Flanking him were four Githyanki warriors, one of whom immediately screamed that their master wished the party dead.

The battle erupted immediately. The Githyanki warriors charged with their greatswords while Rogue Thought used the ambient Faerzress energy to lash out at Bramgrim, whom he had identified through his mind-reading as the greatest magical threat. Bramgrim responded by summoning a storm of spectral guardians that scorched the warriors with holy energy and slowed their advance, while Akritas loosed precise and punishing arrows from across the chamber. Two of the Githyanki warriors used their innate ability to teleport away from the spectral guardians, desperately trying to break Bramgrim's concentration, but both failed to land a decisive blow. The Owlbear charged through the far door and crashed into Rogue Thought himself, delivering a powerful strike that visibly staggered the ancient creature.

Then Felkur made his move. Carefully positioning himself, he unleashed a massive fireball that roared through the chamber and engulfed the Mind Flayer and nearly every Githyanki warrior in a single catastrophic explosion. The blast tore through the room, leaving most of the warriors badly wounded and Rogue Thought himself reeling. Daein surged forward and delivered a relentless series of blows that drove the Mind Flayer to the very edge of destruction. Sensing his imminent defeat, Rogue Thought did something unexpected — he severed his own tentacles, dropped them to the ground, and vanished in a flash of planar magic, escaping to another dimension entirely. His parting words echoed in their minds: this was not over, and both he and Graz'zt would have their revenge. Bramgrim finished off the last surviving Githyanki warrior with a burst of necrotic energy, and the chamber finally fell silent.

The party collected Rogue Thought's severed tentacles as proof of their victory and made their way back through the Underdark to Sporedome, with Arrizz traveling alongside them one last time. At the edge of the fungal city, the dry-witted drow paused and offered his farewell with characteristic pragmatism: he hoped never to see them again, but if he did, he was certain it would be because they were coming to save him. The Mind Flayer who had hired them received the tentacles without ceremony and rewarded the party with powerful magical gifts, promising to pass vital intelligence about Graz'zt's operations to the Order of the Gauntlet — the only faction the creature trusted enough to deal with directly.

Back on the surface, the party returned to Hillsfar and were promptly invited to a private dinner by Isthana, the city's powerful and influential criminal matriarch. Over fine food and three bottles of rare wine — a personal gift to Felkur — she listened with great interest as the party floated the idea of starting a business in the city. Her response was warm but unmistakably pointed: any business under her patronage would thrive, while any venture without her support would find the city a very difficult place to operate. The party understood the message clearly and left the dinner without signing anything, though the door remained open for future arrangements.

The evening ended with a telepathic message arriving for Akritas from the Emerald Enclave. The news was grave: fire giants controlling the Underdark city of Szith Morcane were nearing completion of their work and had turned their eyes toward the surface — toward the Dalelands and Hillsfar itself. The brain-capped mushrooms the party had secured were holding back the worst of Graz'zt's madness, but time was running out. The party was called to travel to Szith Morcane, link up with the Drow resistance led by the wizard Murssvayas Dhuurniv, and stop the giants before they could unleash Graz'zt's corruption upon the surface world. The next great battle was coming, and it would be fought in the dark heart of the Underdark.

## Locations
### Rogue Mind Flayer's Lair
A dark, dangerous complex filled with moss-covered chambers, magically sealed doors requiring brains to open, and freezing rooms used for preserving biological specimens.

### The Brain Fridge
A freezing chamber within the lair kept at a much cooler temperature by columns of ice, containing a large block of opaque ice used to preserve suspended brains, small vials of yellowish liquid, and a hidden copper plate with otherworldly symbols.

### Rogue Thought's Chamber
A large room decorated with graven images of skulls and brains of varying sizes, shimmering with the alien pulse of concentrated Faerzress. Three tall decorative brain jars stand at the center alongside a large pile of cushions, and the acoustics are so sharp that even the quietest whisper can be plainly heard.

### Sporedome
A fungal city in the Underdark where the party reported their success to the Mind Flayer who hired them and received their reward.

### Hillsfar
A major surface city where the party met with Isthana to discuss starting a business, and where Akritas received a new mission from the Emerald Enclave.

### Szith Morcane
A location mentioned in the Emerald Enclave's message as being near an abundance of Faerzress and currently controlled by fire giants who are nearing completion of their work and threatening to invade the surface.

## NPCs
### Arrizz
A drow previously encountered by the party who was found bound and tormented in the lair, suffering from demonic madness that was cured by a piece of magical mushroom. Dry-witted and pragmatic, he agreed to travel with the party briefly in exchange for a beer, then parted ways at Sporedome with a wry farewell.

### Thera Grabelthum
A Duergar found unconscious in a moss-covered chamber who initially claimed to be an innocent traveler. Under intimidation, she admitted to spying on Duergar operations for the Zhentarim Black Network. She turned invisible and fled during the ambush but was killed in the chaos.

### Githyanki Warriors
Arrogant and warlike warriors who used greatswords and teleportation to fight the party. Some served as guards in the lair, while others were found tormenting Arrizz. One of their skulls cracked open to reveal a hidden intellect devourer.

### Intellect Devourer
A horrifying, pulsating brain on legs that emerged from a slain Githyanki warrior's cracked skull. It unleashed a devastating psychic blast that left the Owlbear stunned and helpless before being destroyed by the party.

### Rogue Thought
A mad Mind Flayer found in his inner sanctum sipping from decorative brain jars. He was served by Githyanki warriors, appeared to have been blessed by Graz'zt, and communicated telepathically. When brought near death by the party, he severed his own tentacles and plane shifted away, vowing revenge.

### Owlbear
The party's loyal sidekick who was stunned by the intellect devourer's psychic blast, healed during a short rest, and then charged into the final chamber to deliver a powerful strike against Rogue Thought.

### Solom Ned'razak
A drow archmage referenced at the end of the session who declared that the time had come to move against Graz'zt in Maerimydra.

### Isthana
The powerful and influential head of the criminal element in Hillsfar who invited the party to a private dinner. She offered patronage and protection for any business they might start, making clear that ventures without her support would not fare well, and sought to co-opt the party as powerful assets for the city.

### Murssvayas Dhuurniv
A Drow wizard mentioned in the Emerald Enclave's message as having returned to his people and being ready to receive the party at Szith Morcane to lead the resistance against the fire giants.
