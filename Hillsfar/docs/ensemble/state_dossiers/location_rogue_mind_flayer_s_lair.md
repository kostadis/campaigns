### Rogue Mind Flayer's Lair

**Type:** Location (Underdark Complex)
**Current State:** An active, dark, and dangerous complex deep in the Underdark, heavily permeated by Faerzress. The interior is characterized by twisted corridors, moss-covered chambers, and narrow hallways specifically designed to restrict large creatures (e.g., Owlbears).

**Key Features & Mechanics:**
*   **Security:** Contains magically sealed doors requiring a brain to operate via stone basins carved to resemble the underside of a human brain.
*   **Atmosphere:** Features rooms with varying temperatures, including notably cold doors and specific freezing rooms used for preserving biological specimens.
*   **Hazards:** The complex is littered with surgical remains and four corpses with surgically removed brains.
*   **Intel:** Detailed maps of surface cities are stored within the lair.

**Recent Activity & Occupants:**
*   **Discovery:** The party recently uncovered a moss-covered chamber containing an unconscious Duergar named Thera Grabelthum.
*   **Sounds:** Previously, guttural voices shouting insults and a defiant voice responding in Undercommon were heard from behind a northwest door.
*   **Progress:** The party has pressed onward through the twisted corridors, standing before two sealed doors deep within the complex.

## Uncertainty
*   **Current Occupancy:** While Thera Grabelthum was found unconscious in a moss-covered chamber in Chapter 16, the current status of the "guttural voices" and the "defiant voice" heard in Chapter 14 is unknown; it is unclear if they are still present, dead, or have moved deeper into the complex.
*   **Specific Map Locations:** The facts confirm the *presence* of detailed maps of surface cities but do not specify which room they are currently located in or if they have been moved/destroyed.
*   **Status of Sealed Doors:** The party was "standing before" two sealed doors in Chapter 15 and "pressed onward" in Chapter 16; it is ambiguous whether these specific doors were opened, bypassed, or if the party moved past them to a new, unmentioned area.
