### Mezzaloth

**Current Status:** The specific Mezzaloth engaged in the tower defense is **dead**, slain by Daein. A second Mezzaloth arrived later, destroyed the Wall of Force, and **teleported away to safety** (status unknown, presumed alive).

**Location:**
*   **Dead Mezzaloth:** Killed on the tower top during the defense.
*   **Escaped Mezzaloth:** Location unknown; last seen teleporting away from the battlefield after destroying the Wall of Force.

**Allegiance/Faction:** Yugoloth (demonic cannon fodder).

**Current Possessions & Traits:**
*   **Armor:** Chitinous armor.
*   **Weapon:** Trident (used by the dead individual).
*   **Magic Item:** Wand of Disintegration (wielded by the escaped individual).
*   **Abilities:** Can cast *Cloudkill* (poisonous yellow-green fog), *Teleport*, and cast dispelling magic. Resistant to fire.
*   **Wounds:** The dead individual was staggered by Bramgrim's fire bolt before being cut down.

**Key Relationships & Actions:**
*   **Dead Individual:** Engaged Daein (missed with trident), attempted to break party concentration, cast *Cloudkill*, was staggered by Bramgrim's fire bolt, and was ultimately killed by Daein before escaping.
*   **Escaped Individual:** Arrived with a wand of disintegration, failed to dispel the Wall of Force with magic, succeeded in destroying the Wall of Force using the wand, and teleported away to safety.

**Defining Recent Actions:**
*   One Mezzaloth breached the tower, cast *Cloudkill*, and was killed by Daein after failing to hit him with a trident.
*   A second Mezzaloth arrived, utilized a wand of disintegration to shatter the party's Wall of Force, and escaped via teleportation.

## Uncertainty
*   **Entity Distinction:** The facts describe "a Mezzaloth" (singular) in Chapter 12 and early Chapter 13, then explicitly mention "A new Mezzaloth" and "a second Mezzaloth" in Chapter 13. It is clear there are at least two distinct entities, but the dossier must distinguish the dead one (trident user) from the escaped one (wand user). The facts do not confirm if the "new" Mezzaloth was the *only* other one present, or if more exist.
*   **Dead Individual's Escape:** One fact states the Mezzaloth "teleported away in desperation" while another states Daein cut it down "before it could escape." The "before it could escape" fact (Daein cutting it down) is the definitive outcome for the trident-wielding Mezzaloth, rendering the "teleported away" fact likely applicable to the *second* Mezzaloth or a misattributed description of the second entity's actions. The dossier assumes the teleportation applies to the wand-wielder, but the text "The Mezzaloth teleported away" appears in isolation without a clear subject switch in some lines, creating a slight ambiguity on which specific instance the "teleported away" quote refers to if not the wand user. However, the sequence "failed to dispel... subsequently teleporting" strongly links teleportation to the wand user.
*   **Poison Source:** One fact states "The Mezzaloth's poison caused Bramgrim's concentration... to break," while others state the fire bolt scattered the cloud. It is unclear if the poison *itself* broke concentration (via a save or effect) or if the scattering of the cloud (caused by the fire) was the primary resolution. The facts support both the cloud being cast and the concentration breaking, but the direct causal link ("poison caused... break") is stated as a fact, though the fire bolt scattering is also stated as the cause of the cloud dispersing. This is a minor narrative conflict on the *mechanism* of the cloud's end, not the state of the entity.
