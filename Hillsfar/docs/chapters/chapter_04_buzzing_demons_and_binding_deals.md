# Chapter 04 Buzzing Demons and Binding Deals

Date: Dec 30th, 2025

## Summary
The party stood in Maram il Vass's opulent office, ready to present their payment for the drow prisoner. Felkur had used his magical abilities to create two sets of armor, which would appear genuine for the next eight hours. When the Yugoloth—appearing as a handsome half-elf to most, but revealed as a seven-foot-tall demon with jaws and claws to Bramgrim's enhanced sight—questioned the origin of the armor, Felkur spun an elaborate tale. He claimed the sets came from bandit camps and a raided castle, carefully avoiding any mention of their true magical creation. When Maram il Vass mentioned rumors of Graz'zt's Chosen being killed in Hillsfar and suggested the armor might be from there, making it more valuable, Daein seized the opportunity and wove the suggestion into their story with such conviction that the Yugoloth was completely convinced. Impressed by their tale, Maram il Vass handed them a key and directed them to the hanging gardens below to retrieve their prisoner.

The party descended through a storage room filled with empty containers and down a long staircase that transformed from worked stone to rough cavern walls. They emerged into a vast underground chamber, one hundred feet tall and bathed in the eerie pale glow of luminescent fungus clinging to the ceiling. Heavy chains ran the length of the cavern, suspending numerous cages from floor to ceiling, most containing a single miserable occupant. An unsettling buzzing sound echoed throughout the space, and before they could fully take in the scene, they heard screaming and the sound of creatures rushing toward them. Two Chasme Demons appeared, their bodies covered in buzzing insects, their forms twisted and horrifying. Daein recognized them as intelligent taskmasters from the Abyss, creatures designed to punish deserters from the Blood War, and warned the party to plug their ears against the necrotic droning sound they produced.

Battle erupted in the cavern as Bramgrim called upon radiant energy to strike at the demons while summoning a spectral weapon to aid in the fight. Daein shouted for the drow prisoner while preparing to engage, and Felkur hurled bolts of flame at the creatures, though their demonic nature allowed them to shrug off much of the fire's damage. The Owlbear stood ready to strike, and Akritas marked one of the demons with her hunter's focus before loosing arrows at it. One demon flew toward Bramgrim, and the Owlbear lashed out with its claws, tearing into the creature's flesh. The demon attempted to strike back at Bramgrim but failed to penetrate his defenses, all while speaking in a disturbing polyphonic voice that buzzed and clicked, taunting him about finding the weakness in his stony exterior.

Bramgrim called forth spectral guardians that swirled around him in a protective aura, damaging the demons and slowing their movements. He directed his spectral weapon to strike at the chain holding one of the cages, and it shattered under the magical assault. The Owlbear heroically grabbed the falling chain and held it steady, preparing to lower the prisoner to safety. Daein charged forward with his longsword, striking one of the demons repeatedly and calling upon his reserves of strength for additional attacks. Akritas repositioned herself and continued her barrage of arrows, dealing devastating damage to the creatures. One demon fell, dissolving into dust, but the other continued its assault, telepathically threatening Akritas with promises of terror and pain. It lunged at her with its proboscis, but Daein's earlier strike had weakened it enough that the attack went wide.

As the Owlbear carefully lowered the prisoner's cage, Bramgrim's spectral guardians continued to burn at the remaining demon. The party made the tactical decision to retreat rather than continue the fight, with Bramgrim covering their escape while taking a vicious critical strike from the demon's proboscis that dealt massive damage to both his body and his life force. He disengaged and fled up the stairs with the others, the demon's mocking voice following them, commenting that Maram il Vass would need new guardians for his prisoners. As they reached the top of the stairs, they glimpsed three hags arriving to take over guard duty, muttering complaints about the quality of hired help. The party successfully escaped back to Maram il Vass's office with the freed drow, Xaven Tanor'this.

Back in the office, Maram il Vass observed their injuries with mild interest and inquired about the fate of his demon guards. When informed that one had been killed, he seemed unconcerned, noting that the hags could handle the situation. Then his attention turned to Felkur, and his demeanor shifted to one of calculated interest. He revealed that he had been searching for Felkur for some time, connected to an item the artificer had allegedly stolen. Maram il Vass explained that he had made a deal with a devil who sought Felkur, and unless a new arrangement could be made, he would be obligated to reveal Felkur's location. The Yugoloth proposed a solution: if Felkur would retrieve a specific magical item that Maram il Vass owed to another demon, he would keep Felkur's whereabouts secret.

Felkur, with encouragement from Daein, engaged in an intense negotiation with the master of contracts. He demanded to know the location and defenses of the item he would need to retrieve, and Maram il Vass, impressed by the artificer's boldness, agreed to include this information. Felkur then crafted a no-betrayal clause, ensuring that if Maram il Vass switched sides or revealed his location anyway, the Yugoloth would suffer a cosmic curse or forfeit his true name. The contract became airtight, binding both parties to their agreement. Finally, Felkur suggested sealing the deal with a magical binding ritual, demonstrating such knowledge of binding law that even Maram il Vass expressed genuine admiration. The contract was finalized with magical force, granting Felkur additional protection against fear while under its terms, and binding him to retrieve the mysterious item that Maram il Vass required.

## Locations
### Maram il Vass's Office
An opulent chamber within Maram il Vass's palace, filled with expensive incense, tapestries, exotic flowers, and containers holding unusual creatures. The party negotiates with Maram il Vass here and returns after retrieving the drow prisoner.

### Empty Storage Room
A room adjacent to Maram il Vass's office, containing shelves with empty bottles, vials, casks, boxes, crates, and barrels, and a staircase leading downwards to the Hanging Gardens.

### Hanging Gardens
A vast cavern beneath Maram il Vass's palace, 100 feet tall and lit by luminescent fungus that creates an eerie pale illumination. Heavy chains run the length of the cavern from floor to ceiling, suspending numerous cages, most occupied by a single prisoner. An unusual buzzing sound echoes throughout the area, and it is guarded by Chasme Demons.

### Hillsfar
A location mentioned by Maram il Vass as a place where Graz'zt's Chosen was rumored to have been killed, potentially making an item from there more valuable due to its association with Graz'zt's failure.

## Items
### Armor Sets
Two sets of armor magically created by Felkur using his abilities, lasting for eight hours. The party fabricated their origin, claiming they came from bandit camps and a raided castle, to increase their perceived value to Maram il Vass. They were presented as payment for the drow prisoner.

### Key
A key given to the party by Maram il Vass to access the Hanging Gardens where the drow prisoner is held. The party takes it after Maram il Vass unlocks the door and leaves.

### Chains
Large, heavy chains running the length of the Hanging Gardens cavern, from which numerous cages are suspended. One chain was broken by Bramgrim's Spiritual Weapon to free Xaven Tanor'this's cage, which the Owlbear then held to lower him safely.

### Magical Item
An item of 'no material consequence' that Maram il Vass desires and tasks Felkur with retrieving as part of a contract. Its exact nature and location are to be revealed, though Felkur negotiated to know the location and defenses of the item.

### Contract
A magically created, boilerplate document with numerous clauses and signatures, meticulously crafted by Maram il Vass. It was further refined by Felkur during negotiations to include a clause about the magical item's location and defenses, a 'no-betrayal' clause that would curse Maram il Vass if he broke the agreement, and a magical binding ritual. The contract grants Felkur a bonus on saving throws against fear while under its terms.

## Spells
### Toll the Dead
A cantrip cast by Bramgrim against a Chasme Demon, requiring a wisdom saving throw and dealing necrotic damage.

### Spiritual Weapon
A spell cast by Bramgrim as a bonus action, creating a floating, spectral weapon that can attack enemies. It was positioned near the Chasme Demons and later used to break the chain holding Xaven Tanor'this's cage.

### Firebolt
A cantrip cast by Felkur that hurls a bolt of fire at a target, dealing fire damage. It was used against the Chasme Demons, though they resisted half the fire damage.

### Hunter's Mark
A spell cast by Akritas that marks a target, allowing her to deal additional damage to it with her attacks.

### Spirit Guardians
A spell cast by Bramgrim that creates spectral guardians around him in a 15-foot radius, dealing radiant damage to enemies within its area. Creatures affected by it have their speed halved if they fail their saving throw. Bramgrim used it to damage the Chasme Demons during combat and maintained it during the retreat.

### Pass Without Trace
A spell mentioned by a player as a potential way to avoid detection, though it was not used on the way down into the cavern.

## NPCs
### Maram il Vass
A seven-foot-tall Yugoloth with demonic jaws and claws, though he appears as a handsome, chic half-elf to those without magical sight. He is a master of contracts and deals, famous for his meticulous and binding agreements. He holds the drow prisoner Xaven Tanor'this and negotiates with the party for his release. He reveals his interest in Felkur and proposes a new deal involving a magical item.

### Xaven Tanor'this
A drow prisoner held by Maram il Vass in the Hanging Gardens, acquired from fire giants. The party successfully retrieves him from a hanging cage in the cavern.

### Chasme Demons
Two intelligent, cruel demons that serve as taskmasters and interrogators in the Abyss, created to punish deserters from the Blood War. They have magic resistance, immunity to poison, resistance to fire, cold, and lightning, and advantage on saving throws against magic. Their droning sound can cause necrotic damage. They communicate telepathically with polyphonic, buzzing, clicking voices. One was defeated by the party, while the other remained to guard the prisoners.

### Owlbear
A loyal sidekick who actively participates in combat, attacking the Chasme Demons and heroically breaking the chain of a prisoner's cage to lower Xaven Tanor'this to safety.

### Hags
Three hags who arrived in the Hanging Gardens to take over guarding the prisoners after the party's escape, muttering about the difficulty of finding good hired help.
