# Chapter 13 The Phantom Lives

Date: May 10th, 2026

## Summary
The battle was already chaos when the Mezzaloth made its move. The cruel Yugoloth lashed out at Daein with its trident, driven not by strategy but by sheer malice, yet every strike sailed wide. Unwilling to linger within range of Bramgrim's protective spirits, the creature vanished in an instant and reappeared across the battlefield, leaving the party scrambling to adjust. Meanwhile, a suffocating cloud of poisonous yellow-green fog conjured by the Mezzaloth continued to choke the air, forcing everyone to fight through burning lungs and watering eyes.

The poison took its toll on Bramgrim, whose concentration finally broke under the relentless assault, causing his protective spirits to dissolve into nothing. He pushed through the pain, summoning a floating spectral weapon to keep fighting, and attempted to clear the toxic fog with a burst of dispelling magic — but the cloud held. It was Felkur who quietly bore the greatest burden of all, maintaining his concentration on the invisible Wall of Force that stood between the party and the full fury of the enemy forces massing outside. Bramgrim managed to heal the battered Owlbear, who had come dangerously close to the edge, while Daein used a moment's respite to catch his breath and recover his strength.

The tide began to turn when Bramgrim hurled a bolt of fire at the Mezzaloth. The creature, resistant to flame though it was, still staggered from the blow, and the impact was enough to scatter the poisonous cloud at last. The Mezzaloth, realizing it could no longer play the hero, attempted to shatter the Wall of Force with a burst of dispelling magic — and failed. It teleported away in desperation, but Daein was already moving, cutting the creature down before it could escape. Three Apprentice Arcanaloths had emerged in the chaos, screaming for reinforcements, and two enormous Fire Giants came hauling themselves over the wall, furious at the party's perceived insults to their master. The giants hurled themselves at Daein again and again, and again and again, they missed — every single strike falling short in a display so improbable it drew stunned silence from the party.

With one round left before their escape window closed, a second Mezzaloth arrived carrying a wand crackling with disintegrating energy. It pressed the wand against the Wall of Force, and the barrier that had protected the party for five brutal rounds simply ceased to exist. In the same breath, one of the Apprentice Arcanaloths unleashed a roaring fireball that engulfed the entire party — yet every single one of them endured it. Felkur, who had held his concentration through poison, fire, and the relentless pressure of battle, held it still. The party seized their moment and teleported away, leaving the Mezzaloth's corpse and a furious army behind them.

They materialized in Sporedome, where Solom Ned'razak and Xaven Tanor'this rushed out to greet them. Drow assistants carefully took custody of Brandyr, the captured Fire Giant, and moved the enormous creature to a nearby chamber to begin the ritual Solom had long prepared. Solom thanked the party and explained that the process would take ten days to complete, during which time they were given the option to return to Hillsfar, though they decided to stay in Sporedome instead. He assured them he would remain in contact through Murssvayas Dhuurniv, and reminded them gravely that while this had been a great victory, the battle was far from over.

Back in the safety of Sporedome, the party took stock of what they had accomplished and looked ahead to what was coming. Akritas refined her archery instincts, sharpening her ability to fire through cover and hold her ground even when enemies pressed close. Bramgrim deepened his divine connection, gaining a steadier hand for maintaining his sacred wards and adding a new radiant prayer to his arsenal. Felkur mused aloud about engineering a device that could reshape the very nature of his elemental attacks, turning fire into frost or crackling lightning at will. And when someone pointed out that Felkur was now, technically, a figure of legend — a phantom thief no one could name — he had only one question: could he sell the merchandise?

## Locations
### Sporedome
A safe haven where the party returns after their mission. It is inhabited by Drow and serves as a base for Solom Ned'razak's magical research. Solom Ned'razak and Xaven Tanor'this rushed out to greet the party upon their arrival.

## NPCs
### Mezzaloth
A cruel Yugoloth that uses a trident and teleportation to harass the party. It attempted to dispel the Wall of Force and attacked Daein out of pure malice before being slain by the party.

### Owlbear
The party's sidekick who was caught in the poisonous cloud and took damage, nearing a critical state during the skirmish before being healed by Bramgrim.

### Apprentice Arcanaloth
Yugoloth spellcasters who arrived as reinforcements, calling for aid and blasting the party with a fireball. Three of them appeared, each with 32 hit points and an armor class of 17.

### Fire Giant
Massive warriors who climbed the walls to attack the party in retaliation for the assault on their stronghold. Two appeared and attacked Daein repeatedly, but miraculously missed every single strike.

### Solom Ned'razak
A Drow Archmage who receives the party upon their return to Sporedome. He takes possession of the captured Fire Giant, Brandyr, for a ten-day ritual and instructs the party to return to Hillsfar while he remains in contact via Murssvayas Dhuurniv.

### Xaven Tanor'this
A Drow Alchemist who greets the party alongside Solom Ned'razak after their successful escape.

### Brandyr
A Fire Giant captured by the party and handed over to Solom Ned'razak's Drow assistants for experimentation and blood-borne rituals.

## Items
### Trident
The weapon wielded by the Mezzaloth, used in a failed attempt to strike Daein during combat.

### Oathbow
A powerful magical bow used by Akritas to strike enemies from a distance. During this session it was used against the Shield Guardian, though without a sworn enemy active it dealt reduced damage.

### Wand of Disintegration
A legendary magical artifact brought by a second Mezzaloth as reinforcement. It was pressed against the Wall of Force and used to instantly destroy the barrier, ending the party's protection.

## Spells
### Cloudkill
A sphere of poisonous yellow-green fog cast by the Mezzaloth that filled the battlefield, forcing all creatures within to make repeated constitution saving throws or take poison damage. The cloud persisted for several rounds and was eventually dissolved when Bramgrim struck the Mezzaloth with a firebolt.

### Spirit Guardians
Protective spirits summoned by Bramgrim that damaged the Mezzaloth before Bramgrim's concentration was broken by poison damage from the Cloudkill.

### Wall of Force
An invisible, indestructible barrier that protected the party for five rounds. The Mezzaloth attempted to dispel it and later a wand of disintegration was used to finally destroy it.

### Dispel Magic
Attempted by both the Mezzaloth and Bramgrim during the session. The Mezzaloth tried to use it against the Wall of Force but failed. Bramgrim attempted it to clear the Cloudkill but also failed his spellcasting check.

### Spiritual Weapon
A floating spectral weapon summoned by Bramgrim as a bonus action after his spirit guardians were lost, allowing him to continue contributing to combat.

### Fireball
A massive explosion of flame cast by an Apprentice Arcanaloth that engulfed the entire party, dealing sixteen points of fire damage to each member. All party members survived the blast.

### Word of Radiance
A divine cantrip newly selected by Bramgrim upon leveling up, which causes a burst of divine light to strike nearby foes.
