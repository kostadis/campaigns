# Chapter 02 The Howling Pits: A Strategic Retreat

Date: Dec 7th, 2025

## Summary
The party returned to the familiar sinkhole known as The Waydown, a half-day's journey from Hillsfar that served as their gateway to the Underdark. They had traveled this route so many times that they joked about becoming tour guides. This time, however, they found a partially built wall surrounding the entrance, guarded by two nervous soldiers in splint armor. The soldiers recognized them as the heroes who had saved Hillsfar from demonic possession and were visibly relieved that the party posed no threat. Their commander, Vuhm Yestral, had returned to the city with a larger contingent of the Red Plumes, leaving only these two guards who had no desire to venture into the darkness below.

Before descending, the party made preparations for the journey ahead. They enhanced their Owlbear companion with magical gloves that would make its attacks more powerful and effective in combat. Daein, the only member of the party without natural darkvision, finally purchased a pair of enchanted goggles that would allow him to see clearly in the perpetual darkness of the Underdark. With these preparations complete, they descended the hundred-foot drop to the swift-flowing underground river and began their two-day journey to the Howling Pits, guided by Murssvayas's accurate directions and map.

The journey through the Underdark was surprisingly uneventful, with the party encountering no problems along the way—an unusual occurrence that made them suspicious. They traversed rough tunnels and caves, surrounded by increasing patches of fungus and enveloped in darkness that seemed to choke out all light. As they approached the Howling Pits, they detected a faint piping sound echoing through the caves, growing louder as they descended. A strange breeze rose from the darkness, bringing with it an unearthly howling that resembled the sound of bones rattling in the wind.

Upon entering a large cave within the Howling Pits, the party was suddenly ambushed by two massive fire giants who leaped from behind stalactites, screaming with rage. These were no ordinary giants—one possessed leathery wings too small for flight, while the other had an extra eye, clear signs that they had been affected by the madness plaguing the giants. Felkur attempted to strike one with magic, but the attack proved remarkably ineffective. The giants retaliated with devastating force, their greatswords cleaving through the air with terrifying power.

Bramgrim positioned himself strategically and summoned radiant guardians to protect the party, creating an aura that slowed the giants' movements. Despite Daein's attempts to shield him from harm, one of the giants landed a critical blow that sent Bramgrim crashing to the ground, unconscious and bleeding. The party quickly realized they were outmatched and made the difficult decision to flee. Daein grabbed the fallen Bramgrim while Akritas helped Felkur, and they disengaged from combat. The giants laughed mockingly at their retreat before returning to their resting places in the cave.

After Bramgrim regained consciousness, confused and shaken by what had happened, the party discussed a new strategy. They acknowledged that their goal was not to kill every creature they encountered, but to navigate through the area and understand the true danger these maddened giants posed to the world above. Bramgrim suggested using stealth to bypass the giants rather than engaging them directly. Akritas wove a spell that would mask their movements, and despite a stumble from Felkur, the party managed to sneak past the giants successfully, with Akritas and Daein swiftly pulling their companions to safety when one giant briefly stirred.

The party then encountered a treacherous section filled with dangerously deep pits from which relentless winds howled. Daein used his magical abilities to grant himself flight and then transferred each party member across the hazardous terrain one by one. Despite the strong winds that threatened to knock them off course, everyone maintained their balance and safely crossed to the other side. They continued onward, navigating the increasingly dangerous tunnels of the Howling Pits.

They soon reached a wide cave bisected by a swift-flowing underground river. Strange gray and white fungi covered the banks in large patches. When Akritas attempted to identify the fungus, she failed, much to Bramgrim's amusement. Drawing on his knowledge as an Underdark creature, Bramgrim smugly explained that the fungi were Cave Creepers, which would drive anyone mad if touched. The party carefully discussed their options and decided to fly over the river, with Daein once again using his powers to transfer everyone across while meticulously avoiding contact with the dangerous fungi.

Further along their journey, the party entered a tunnel peppered with patches of yellow mold. As they proceeded, the wind suddenly picked up, threatening to spread the poisonous spores into the air. Akritas recognized the danger just in time, identifying the spores as toxic. The party decided to hold their breath and rush through the affected area. Most of them successfully resisted the poison, but Bramgrim, despite his natural resistance, suffered some effects before ultimately overcoming them. Once the wind carried the poison away, they were safe to continue.

The tunnel eventually narrowed to a tight passageway with high ceilings, and Bramgrim refused to lead the way, pointing out that it would be an ideal place for an ambush from above. Using his keen observation skills, Daein spotted bat-like creatures lurking in the shadows above. Akritas identified them as cloakers, mysterious and intelligent predators of the Underdark that descended from above to attack their prey. Once again, Akritas wove her spell of concealment, and the party carefully crept through the narrow passage. Despite another misstep from Felkur, the magical enhancement to their stealth proved sufficient, and they successfully evaded the cloakers without being detected.

As the party reached the edge of the cave and prepared to continue deeper into the Howling Pits, they reflected on their journey so far. They had demonstrated remarkable creativity and resourcefulness, using stealth and magical abilities to bypass dangerous encounters rather than fighting everything in their path. Though they had been forced to flee from the fire giants, they had learned valuable lessons about the true danger lurking in these depths. With each challenge overcome, they grew more confident in their ability to navigate the treacherous Howling Pits and complete their mission, whatever dangers still lay ahead in the darkness.

### Catapult
A spell cast by Felkur against one of the mutated fire giants during the ambush. Despite being aimed at a powerful enemy, the spell was remarkably ineffective, with Felkur rolling extremely poorly and dealing only four points of damage to the giant.

### Protective Field
A defensive reaction ability used by Daein to protect Bramgrim from the fire giant's devastating attacks. When activated, it reduces incoming damage by four points, though this proved insufficient against the giant's critical hit that knocked Bramgrim unconscious.

A powerful spell cast by Bramgrim at fifth level during the fire giant encounter. A stealth-enhancing spell cast by Akritas multiple times throughout the journey. The spell grants a plus ten bonus to Stealth checks for the entire party, allowing them to successfully sneak past the fire giants after their initial defeat and later evade the cloakers in the narrow tunnel. The spell's duration is one hour and requires concentration.

A spell cast by Daein using his magical mandolin, granting flight with a duration of one hour and requiring concentration. Daein used it on himself to scout ahead and navigate dangerous terrain. The mandolin can only cast the same spell once per day, so Daein relied on his own powers to transfer the rest of the party across obstacles like the deep pits and the underground river filled with Cave Creeper fungi.
