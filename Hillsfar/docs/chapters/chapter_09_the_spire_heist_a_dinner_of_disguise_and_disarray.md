# Chapter 09 The Spire Heist: A Dinner of Disguise and Disarray

Date: Mar 22nd, 2026

## Summary
The party moved through the demon-haunted city of Maerimydra with a singular, audacious goal: to infiltrate the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning and steal the Lexicon of Oaths before anyone else could reach it. Their first obstacle was the fire giant Brandyr, a towering sentinel standing watch over a ritual courtyard of polished obsidian, its ebony pillars each branded with the crimson six-fingered hand of the demon lord Graz'zt. Working in perfect coordination, Felkur unleashed a devastating enchantment that dropped the massive giant into a deep, helpless slumber. With Brandyr unconscious and defenseless, Bramgrim and Daein worked in tandem to shrink her enormous frame down to a fraction of her original size, and Akritas — cloaked in invisibility — swept in and shoved the shrunken giant into a portable hole fitted with a breathing apparatus. In moments, one of Maerimydra's most fearsome warriors had simply vanished.

The cambion guards stationed at the courtyard were left utterly bewildered, murmuring among themselves about the sudden disappearance of their giant commander. Sensing an opportunity, Daein conjured a flawless imitation of Graz'zt's terrible voice, letting it boom through the courtyard with divine displeasure directed squarely at the fire giants. The cambions — already resentful of the giants who had encroached on their demonic prestige — erupted in ecstatic reverence. Convinced that Graz'zt himself had reached down and smited the unworthy Brandyr, they abandoned their posts entirely and took to the air, spreading the glorious revelation to every demon in the city. The path to the Spire was clear, and the rumor they had seeded would ripple through Maerimydra like wildfire.

Before pressing forward, the party consulted their imp companion Pipyap, who — after some firm persuasion — revealed the layout of the Spire and the nature of the evening's gathering. Vraxen, the fox-faced Arcanaloth who served as Graz'zt's contract enforcer, was hosting a tense dinner with Veperyd, a smoke-weeping fire giant who ran the city's Colosseum, duels, and betting pool, and High Inquisitor Veris, a cold and calculating mage from Mulmaster. Most critically, Pipyap revealed that Vraxen kept a runic key on his person — the only means of accessing the upper vaults where the Lexicon of Oaths was hidden. It was also during this exchange that Felkur pieced together a chilling realization: Maram il Vass had engineered a double cross, and the Lexicon itself likely contained proof of Felkur's identity as the infamous Phantom Ghost thief of Mulmaster. They had to reach that book before Veris did.

The party approached the Spire's entrance with confidence, presenting themselves as traders from Hillsfar bearing critical information. The guards, impressed by their boldness, waved them through into the dining hall, where a magically distorted table made a fire giant, a demon, and a human mage all appear to be seated at eye level with one another. The air was thick with sulfur, expensive wine, and barely contained fury. Vraxen was already needling Veperyd about skimming from the betting pool, while Veperyd responded with barely restrained volcanic rage. And High Inquisitor Veris, cold-eyed and calculating, had immediately fixed his unsettling gaze on Felkur, identifying him as someone with the hands of a master smith and the eyes of a man who had seen the inside of a Mulmaster cell.

Before Veris could press his interrogation further, Daein and Bramgrim loudly redirected the conversation toward the Colosseum's finances, stoking the already smoldering tension between Vraxen and Veperyd. The distraction bought precious seconds, but the party needed more chaos. At a whispered command from Felkur, their owlbear companion — apparently concluding that since everyone at the table was pounding and roaring, this must simply be a pounding room — leapt onto the formal dinner table and began smashing everything in sight, bellowing at the top of its lungs. In the erupting pandemonium, Daein reached out with his telekinetic power and silently dragged the runic key across the obsidian table and into the party's possession. No one noticed.

With the key secured, the party needed only to push the situation past the point of no return. Daein conjured one final illusion — the voice of High Inquisitor Veris himself — insinuating that the mage had been conspiring with Veperyd to skim from the betting pool. The Arcanaloth's composure shattered instantly. Vraxen shrieked about treason and ordered his guards to flay the giant, while Veperyd roared back with a promise to repay the debt in blood. Veris, furious and exposed, reached for his staff as the entire room descended into a three-way brawl of demons, giants, and wizards. The party slipped away into the chaos and began their ascent toward the upper vaults of the Spire.

As they moved through the corridors of the Spire, the runic key in hand and the sounds of battle echoing below them, one of the party glanced around and noticed something unsettling: Pipyap was gone. The little imp, who had guided them, bargained with them, and eaten their food with such relish, had simply vanished somewhere in the chaos of the dinner. Whether he had fled for his own safety, been taken, or slipped away for reasons of his own, no one could say. The Lexicon of Oaths and the teleportation device that would carry them out of Maerimydra still waited somewhere above — and now they would have to face whatever lay ahead without their peculiar, food-obsessed guide.

## Locations
### Six-Fingered Courtyard
A ritual area on a plateau in Maerimydra featuring a polished obsidian floor surrounded by ebony pillars, each emblazoned with a crimson six-fingered hand — the mark of Graz'zt. This is where the party ambushed and kidnapped the fire giant Brandyr.

### Maerimydra
A city in the Underdark currently occupied by fire giants who have made a pact with the demon lord Graz'zt, who has infused them with demonic power.

### Spire of Inevitable Reckoning
A formidable tower in Maerimydra overseen by the Arcanaloth Vraxen. It features a dining hall with a magically distorted table that allows creatures of vastly different sizes to converse at eye level, and upper vaults accessible only by a runic key.

### Mulmaster
A surface city known for its powerful wizards, mentioned as the origin of High Inquisitor Veris and the location from which Felkur accidentally stole a potent divination artifact years ago.

## NPCs
### Brandyr
A fire giant who was the target of the party's kidnapping plot. She was magically put to sleep, shrunk, and stuffed into a portable hole during the ambush at the Six-Fingered Courtyard.

### Cambions
Half-demon, half-human guards stationed at the Six-Fingered Courtyard. They were easily manipulated by their devotion to Graz'zt and their existing resentment of the fire giants, and were tricked into believing Graz'zt had personally smited Brandyr.

### Pipyap
An imp who serves as a guide and informant for the party, obsessed with food and prone to making difficult deals for his information. He revealed the layout of the Spire and the existence of Vraxen's runic key, but mysteriously vanished during the chaos of the dinner.

### Vraxen
An Arcanaloth with a fox-like face who serves as the enforcer of Graz'zt's contracts in Maerimydra. He is obsessed with financial accuracy and suspects General Veperyd of embezzling from the Colosseum's tribute. He carries a runic key that grants access to the spire's upper vaults.

### Veperyd
A mutated fire giant whose skin weeps oily black smoke. He runs the Colosseum, the duels, and the betting pool in Maerimydra and is suspected by Vraxen of skimming from the betting pool.

### High Inquisitor Veris
A cold and calculating mage from Mulmaster who is hunting for the 'Phantom Ghost' thief. He attended the dinner at the Spire and immediately grew suspicious of Felkur, identifying him as a master smith with ties to Mulmaster.

## Items
### Magical Token
A specialized item used by Felkur to cast a high-potency sleep spell that bypassed the fire giant Brandyr's natural defenses. Using it left Felkur with four tokens remaining.

### Portable Hole
A magical extra-dimensional storage space equipped with a custom-built breathing apparatus, used by the party to hide the unconscious and shrunken fire giant Brandyr.

### Reduction Scroll
Magical scrolls used by the party to shrink the fire giant Brandyr's massive frame down to a manageable size. Two scrolls were used to reduce her by two size categories.

### Runic Key
A key carried by Vraxen that grants access to the upper vaults of the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning. It was dangling loose on the obsidian dining table during the chaos of the dinner and was snatched by the party using telekinesis.

### Seeming Scroll
A magical scroll held by the party as a backup plan to disguise themselves as servants if their infiltration of the Spire was compromised.

### The Lexicon of Oaths
A powerful book located within the upper vaults of the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning. It is desired by Maram il Vass and is believed to contain information that could expose Felkur's true identity as the Phantom Ghost thief of Mulmaster.

### Divination Artifact
A powerful item originally stolen from Mulmaster by Felkur, who did not know what it was. It grants perfect divination and protection from scrying. Felkur accidentally activated it and then sold it for 45 gold pieces, triggering years of investigation by Mulmaster's hierarchy.

## Spells
### Sleep
A powerful enchantment cast by Felkur using a magical token that successfully incapacitated the fire giant Brandyr, bypassing her physical defenses through a wisdom-based magical assault.

### Reduce
A transmutation spell cast twice — by Bramgrim and Daein — to shrink the fire giant Brandyr's size by two categories so she could fit inside the party's portable hole. Because Brandyr was incapacitated, no saving throw was required.

### Nature's Veil
A ranger ability used by Akritas to become invisible as a bonus action, allowing her to approach the incapacitated giant and the cambion guards undetected.

### Pass Without Trace
Cast by Akritas to provide a significant boost to the entire party's stealth, masking their movements and sounds as they retreated from the Six-Fingered Courtyard.

### Minor Illusion
Used twice during the session: first by Daein to mimic the terrifying voice of the demon lord Graz'zt to deceive the Cambion guards into abandoning their posts; and later to mimic the voice of High Inquisitor Veris during the dinner, tricking Vraxen into believing a conspiracy was afoot between Veris and General Veperyd.

### Telekinetic Movement
Used by Daein to silently pull the runic key across the obsidian dining table and into the party's possession while the hosts were distracted by the Owlbear's rampage.
