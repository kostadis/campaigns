# Chapter 03 The Devil's Bargain

Date: Dec 14th, 2025

## Summary
Exhausted and beaten from their harrowing journey through the Underdark, the party stumbled upon a defensible cave with a single entrance. Bramgrim suggested they rest there, though his companions were skeptical given his previous questionable decisions while under the influence of madness. Despite their paranoia about potential attacks, they set up an improvised alarm trap with string and metal at the entrance and positioned themselves strategically throughout the cave. After eight uneventful hours of rest, Bramgrim smugly reminded them that not everything in the Underdark was out to get them.

Their respite was short-lived. As they prepared to continue their journey, a ragged scream suddenly choked off echoed from the tunnels ahead. Moving cautiously through the howling winds, they discovered two filthy, traumatized commoners: a woman named Teegan protectively standing over a man named Drystan, who sat rocking and clutching his knees. Both bore visible lash marks and torn clothing, and dried blood pooled near Drystan from wounds in his ears. Teegan initially accused the party of working with their captor, Maram il Vass, but before Bramgrim could offer healing, two ugly Barlgura demons burst from behind, howling about recapturing "the pets."

The battle was swift and decisive. The demons attacked with tormenting bites and thrashing claws, but the party's coordinated assault quickly overwhelmed them. Spectral guardians swirled around Bramgrim as he channeled radiant energy, while Akritas's arrows found their marks with deadly precision. Despite one demon's attempt to torment Daein with horrifying psychic visions, the party cut through their foes, watching as the demons dissolved into dust and ochre upon defeat.

With the immediate threat eliminated, Teegan broke down and revealed their terrible story. She and Drystan had been enslaved by Maram il Vass for five years after being captured by orcs and sold into the Underdark. They had escaped only a day ago while being led out to feed on mushrooms, choosing to brave the maddening winds rather than endure further torment. Teegan confirmed that a Drow had indeed been brought to Maram's palace, though she didn't know his exact location. She warned them about Gabberforth, a deceptively polite deep gnome who guarded the palace, and described the horrors that had broken Drystan's mind so completely that he had driven a sharpened stick into both his ears to escape the torment.

The party's efforts were rewarded with newfound strength and abilities as they prepared for the final confrontation. They discussed their approach carefully, deciding to save their most powerful revelatory magic for when they faced Maram directly. After another day's travel, they emerged from the tunnels into a vast cavern dominated by an impressive structure of black and red marble, its domes lit by eerie blue, green, and purple fairy fire.

At the ornate brass doors carved with images of a half-elf's rise to power, they were met by Gabberforth and his shield guardian. The deep gnome demanded to know their business, but the party confidently stated they wished to speak with his master. Inside, the palace was filled with expensive incense and perfume, its halls lined with tapestries depicting wealth and power, vases of exotic flowers, and glass containers holding unusual creatures. They were led to a grand chamber where a rakishly handsome half-elf sat behind a desk of unusual purple wood.

The moment Bramgrim invoked his sight beyond sight, the illusion shattered. The charming half-elf was revealed as a seven-foot-tall Yugoloth, a demon of contracts and deals, with demonic jaws and claws. Maram il Vass spoke casually of the endless blood war and immediately demonstrated his knowledge by identifying Felkur and the troubles with those who believed he had stolen from them. When asked about the Drow, Maram confirmed he held a prisoner named Xaven Tanor'this, acquired from fire giants and treated with what he called "hospitality."

The negotiation began in earnest, though Maram's opening offer was shocking: he would trade Xaven Tanor'this for Felkur himself, citing the profitable exchange he could make with those hunting the party member. When the party vehemently refused, he suggested Bramgrim instead, but they pushed back, calling the trade unfair. Maram then revealed his true price: ten thousand gold pieces or two rare magical items. The party requested time to deliberate privately, and after careful negotiation over the terms of a contract ensuring their privacy, Maram reluctantly agreed to let them use his side office. As they prepared to enter, two more demons emerged from the back room, a reminder that they were deep in the devil's den, surrounded by forces far beyond their immediate perception.

## Locations
### Underdark
A vast, subterranean realm, described as less terrifying and more like a daily commute for the party, filled with hazards like bottomless pits, madness shrooms, and poison spores.

### Cave (Resting Spot)
A defensible cave with a single entrance, found by Bramgrim, where the party takes a long rest. It is part of an interconnected tunnel system and features a stalactite.

### Howling Pits
A section of the Underdark tunnels characterized by persistent, mournful winds, which the party has just traversed before reaching Maram il Vass's domain. It was previously thought to be empty but contained crazed giants, poisonous fungi, and bottomless pits.

### Den of the Whispered Word
An expansive cavern housing Maram il Vass's palace, a grand structure of black and red marble, lit by blue, green, and purple fairy fire. The central building stands 50 feet high, flanked by two smaller 30-foot buildings. The interior is filled with expensive incense and perfume, adorned with tapestries depicting a half-elf's rise to power, vases of flowers, and glass containers holding unusual creatures. It serves as an extra-planar marketplace.

### Entrance Foyer (Maram il Vass's Palace)
The initial entry area of Maram il Vass's palace, featuring two large tapestries depicting the half-elf's rise to power, and a hallway lined with tall vases of fresh flowers and glass containers holding small, unusual creatures such as fish, birds, and reptiles.

### Grand Chamber (Maram il Vass's Palace)
A wide, long hallway ending in an archway before a grand chamber. The floor is polished white marble streaked with red and black. A large desk of unusual purple wood sits in the middle, and two brass doors lead to southeast and southwest corners.

### Side Office (Maram il Vass's Palace)
An office within Maram il Vass's palace where the party is directed to discuss their negotiation privately, presumably without being spied upon.

## Items
### Alarm Trap (improvised)
An improvised trap made of string and a loud metal item, set up by the party at the entrance of the cave to alert them of any approaching threats during their long rest.

### Pointed Stick
A short length of sharpened wood that was the only weapon Drystan and Teegan possessed during their escape. Drystan, in his maddened state from torture and the howling winds, tragically shoved it into both of his ears, causing self-inflicted wounds.

### Brass Doors
Ornate doors of beaten brass, serving as the only entrance to Maram il Vass's palace. They are carved with self-adulating images of a half-elf in various stages of becoming rich and powerful.

### Tapestries
Two large tapestries in the entrance foyer of Maram il Vass's palace, continuing the theme of the front door's carvings, depicting the story of a self-made half-elf of extraordinary influence.

### Vases with Fresh Flowers
Tall vases lining the hallway in Maram il Vass's palace, filled with fresh flowers, contributing to the air of expensive incense and perfume.

### Glass Containers with Creatures
Glass containers filled with various small and unusual creatures such as fish, birds, reptiles, and other curiosities found in the hallway of Maram il Vass's palace.

### Desk of Purple Wood
A large desk made of an unusual purple wood, situated in the middle of the grand chamber where Maram il Vass conducts his business.

### Contract
A document presented by Maram il Vass to the party, initially a 'wrong contract', then a proposed contract for their privacy during negotiations, which the party scrutinizes for loopholes to ensure Maram won't spy on them or disrupt their deliberations.

## Spells
### Pass Without Trace
Cast by Daein to allow the party to move stealthily through the tunnels without leaving a trace, aiding their approach to investigate the source of the scream.

### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Bramgrim at fourth level during combat with the Barlgura demons, creating a protective aura of spectral guardians that damaged both demons with radiant energy and halved the speed of one of them.

### Phantasmal Killer
Cast by a Barlgura demon on Daein, inflicting psychic damage each round and imposing disadvantage on attack rolls due to a failed Wisdom saving throw.

### Hunter's Mark
Cast by Akritas on one of the Barlgura demons, marking the target to allow Akritas to deal additional damage with weapon attacks. It can be moved to a new target as a bonus action on subsequent rounds if the original target is defeated.

### Favorite Foe
Used by Akritas in conjunction with Hunter's Mark, this ability further enhanced her damage against the marked demon without requiring a bonus action to cast.

### Toll the Dead
Cast by Bramgrim on a Barlgura demon, dealing necrotic damage to the target if it fails a Wisdom saving throw.

### Volley
A new ability gained by Akritas upon leveling up to 11th level, allowing her to use her action to make a ranged attack against any number of creatures within 10 feet of a point she can see within her weapon's range.

### Silence
A third-level spell Akritas considers taking upon leveling up, which can prevent sound and verbal spellcasting within an area.

### Command
A spell Bramgrim adds to his repertoire upon leveling up, useful for social interactions and negotiations.

### True Seeing
A powerful 6th-level spell Bramgrim gains upon leveling up, allowing him to see through illusions, invisibility, and into the ethereal plane, revealing the true nature of things. It lasts for one hour and was used to reveal Maram il Vass's true form as a Yugoloth.

### Tongues
A spell Bramgrim learns upon leveling up, allowing him to understand and speak any language.

### Banishment
A spell Bramgrim already possesses, which can dismiss creatures, particularly useful against demons, potentially sending them back to their home plane permanently if they fail enough saving throws.

### Cure Wounds
A first-level healing spell discussed by Felkur as a potential candidate for his Spell Storing Item ability, providing 10 uses of healing.

### Enlarge/Reduce
A second-level spell discussed by Felkur as a potential candidate for his Spell Storing Item, suggested for getting through small spaces more efficiently.

### Levitate
A second-level spell discussed by Felkur as a potential candidate for his Spell Storing Item, useful for vertical movement.

## NPCs
### Owlbear
A loyal companion to the party who participates in combat. During the battle with the Barlgura demons, it was attacked and knocked prone but recovered using its second wind. It levels up with the party to 11th level.

### Teegan
A commoner enslaved by Maram il Vass for five years after being captured by orcs. She is protective of her brother Drystan and initially suspicious of the party, but after they defeat the demons, she shares vital information about Maram il Vass, the Drow prisoner named Xaven Tanor'this, and the palace's defenses. She refuses to follow the party further into the palace.

### Drystan
Teegan's brother, also enslaved by Maram il Vass. He is found in a state of mental breakdown, rocking and with self-inflicted wounds from shoving a pointed stick into both of his ears, having succumbed to torture and the maddening winds. He is unable to communicate.

### Barlgura
Two ugly-looking demons that ambushed the party, interested in recapturing Teegan and Drystan, whom they called 'the pets'. They are not particularly smart or lethal, but can climb and attack with tormenting bites and thrash attacks. They were defeated by the party and turned into dust and ochre.

### Maram il Vass
Initially appearing as a 'rakishly handsome half-elf', he is revealed by True Seeing to be a Yugoloth, a type of demon specializing in contracts and information. He is cunning, manipulative, and trades in information and power. He is the captor of the Drow named Xaven Tanor'this and attempts to negotiate for the Drow's release, first by trading Felkur, then for 10,000 gold pieces or two rare magical items. He speaks of the endless blood war and has knowledge of the party members' pasts.

### Gabberforth
A deep gnome who serves as a guard and majordomo for Maram il Vass. He is deceptively polite but protective of his master's patience and guards the entrance to the palace, accompanied by a shield guardian.

### Shield Guardian
A construct that stands sentry at the entrance to Maram il Vass's palace, accompanying Gabberforth.

### Xaven Tanor'this
A Drow held captive by Maram il Vass, brought to him by fire giants while unconscious and in bad shape. The party is seeking to rescue or retrieve him.
