# Chapter 07 Maerimydra: City of Fire and Lies

Date: Feb 8th, 2026

## Summary
The party stood victorious over the carnage at the Black Ember Outpost, having just completed a daring assault that began with tactical mischief and ended in swift brutality. 

The path forward, on the Path of the Shadows, was down a well. 

Before descending into the well, the party searched the storeroom and discovered a hidden chest containing potions of flying and fire giant strength, along with coral gemstones worth a considerable sum. They debated how to hide their tracks, ultimately deciding to stage an elaborate scene inspired by a play about pretending the dead were still alive. Daein's performance was so convincing that the bodies of the fire giant and ogres appeared to be merely resting, buying the party precious time before their absence would be discovered.

The party plunged into the dark, murky waters of the well, with Bramgrim and Felkur—both too short to comfortably navigate the five-foot depth—riding on the Owlbear's back. They swam through a tunnel for about forty feet before emerging into a large cave that served as Solom Ned'razak's private office. Among the papers and notes scattered about, they learned that the drow wizard had been working to subvert the power of Lolth's clerics in Maerimydra and had developed theories about a mysterious event called the Blink. This phenomenon had caused all the faerzress—a dimension-controlling substance that provides illumination and blocks teleportation—to flicker dramatically before returning to normal, after which demons began appearing throughout the Underdark.

Solom's notes revealed his frustration at not having conceived of the Blink himself, blaming powerful wizards and liches from Menzoberranzan for the catastrophe. He had initially attempted to negotiate with the demons but quickly realized they were interested only in chaos and destruction. Most intriguingly, the notes mentioned that Lolth's clerics, who had been mysteriously unable to commune with their goddess for some time, suddenly regained their powers after the Blink—because Lolth herself had used the event to escape her imprisonment and was now reestablishing her dominion over her followers.

The tunnel finally opened into Maerimydra itself, a city of fire and ash nestled in a titanic cavern six hundred feet tall. Thick smoke hung above the city like a shroud, and the heavy scent of charcoal filled the air from countless burning braziers. The former drow metropolis was now controlled by demonic forces aligned with the demon lord Graz'zt, its streets patrolled by fire giants, armored ogres, hellhounds, and demons of all varieties, with drow reduced to chains and slavery. The party observed the city's layout: huge buildings dominated the center, a western plateau bore the six-fingered symbol of Graz'zt above a courtyard, an eastern castle with towers stretching to the ceiling housed the Fire Giant Queen Hledh, and a massive coliseum loomed in the southern district.

Shortly after entering the city, the party encountered four ogres tormenting a small creature by its barbed tail—an imp desperately clutching a bloodstained satchel. The imp cried out for help, promising valuable information about the city in exchange for rescue. Though the party debated whether intervening would draw unwanted attention, they ultimately decided the potential intelligence was worth the risk. The battle was swift and decisive, with Daein, Akritas, the Owlbear, and Felkur's magic making short work of the ogres.

The rescued imp introduced himself as Pipyap and eagerly accepted food from the party, though he complained about the lack of salt in their provisions. After some careful negotiation—and rejecting Pipyap's initial contract that would have bound them to serve him—the party crafted a favorable agreement using their experience from a previous deal with an Ultraloth. Pipyap revealed he had been brought to the prime material plane by a magic user's mistake and had exploited loopholes in his contracts to avoid returning to the Nine Hells, where he had no desire to go.

Pipyap proved to be a wealth of information about Maerimydra's power structure and the party's objectives. He explained that Queen Hledh ruled from the eastern castle and should be avoided at all costs. He identified two fiendish giants crucial to the party's mission: Veperyd, who ran the Coliseum and its betting operations, and another who managed the six-fingered courtyard where demons and giants worshiped Graz'zt. Most importantly, Pipyap revealed that a wizard from Mulmaster had arrived for dinner at the residence of Vraxen, a Yugoloth who lived in the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning—the same tower that housed both the book the party needed to steal and a teleportation circle they could use to escape.

The party formulated their plan with Pipyap's guidance. They would first capture the fiendish giant from the courtyard, then enter the Spire of Inevitable Reckoning and provoke a conflict between Vraxen and Veperyd by revealing that Veperyd had been stealing from the betting pool. While chaos erupted, they would steal the book and escape through the teleportation circle before the Mulmaster wizard could acquire it at the dinner. Bramgrim recalled that Hillsfar had been controlled by Graz'zt, suggesting they could bluff their way into the tower by posing as merchants from that city or perhaps as staff. With their strategy set and their wounds tended, the party prepared to venture deeper into the demon-infested city, knowing they had only until nightfall to accomplish everything before the dinner began and their window of opportunity closed forever.

## Locations
### Black Ember Outpost
A fortress built into a cave wall in the Underdark, made of polished obsidian. It served as a base for fire giants, ogres, and hellhounds. The party previously cleared it out during their assault.

### Storeroom (Black Ember Outpost)
A room within the Black Ember Outpost where ogres were hauling water from a well. It contained a hidden chest with treasures and served as the entry point to the Path of Shadows.

### Well (Black Ember Outpost)
A well in the storeroom of the Black Ember Outpost, recognized as the entrance to the Path of Shadows. The party descended into it to reach Maerimydra.

### Path of Shadows
An underground passage of dark, murky, warm water about five feet deep, fed by a stream connecting to a larger lake of shadows. It features a five-foot-wide tunnel that the party swam through for about 40 feet, leading to Solom Ned'razak's private office and eventually Maerimydra.

### Solom Ned'razak's Office
A private office area within a large cave along the Path of Shadows, containing a lamp and papers detailing Solom's research and observations about the Blink event, the faerzress, demons, and Lolth's clerics.

### Maerimydra
A former drow metropolis now a city of fire and ash, nestled in a titanic cavern 600 feet tall at its highest point. It is controlled by demonic evil forces aligned with Graz'zt. The city features huge buildings, plateaus 100 feet high connected by 100-foot-wide grand stairs, a thick smoke layer above 250 feet, and is lit by numerous burning braziers creating a heavy charcoal scent. It is inhabited by fire giants, ogres, hellhounds, drow slaves, and demons.

### Six-Fingered Courtyard
A courtyard on a raised plateau in the western section of Maerimydra, surrounded by black spires bearing a prominent six-fingered symbol (of Graz'zt). Demons and giants gather here to worship a demon lord, and one of the fiendish giants manages it.

### Coliseum
A massive coliseum dominating the southern end of the residential district in Maerimydra, where great gladiator fights occur. Veperyd, a fiendish giant, is in charge of it and manages the betting pool.

### Eastern Castle
A castle on a plateau in the eastern side of Maerimydra, with two towers stretching from floor to ceiling. The Fire Giant Queen Hledh resides here.

### The Spire of Inevitable Reckoning
A tower in Maerimydra where Vraxen (the Yugoloth) resides. It contains the book the party seeks and a teleportation circle. A dinner event is scheduled to occur here.

## NPCs
### Owlbear
A loyal companion to the party who participated in combat against ogres at the Black Ember Outpost and in Maerimydra. Helped Bramgrim and Felkur stay afloat in the deep water by allowing them to ride on its back.

### Solom Ned'razak
A male Drow wizard whose private office and notes were discovered by the party in the Path of Shadows. His writings revealed his efforts to subvert Lolth's clerics in Maerimydra, his theories on the 'Blink' event in the faerzress, his observations on the appearance of demons, and his notes about Lolth's clerics regaining their powers. He blamed powerful wizards and liches from Menzoberranzan for the Blink and wished it had been his idea. He initially sought accommodation with demons but realized they only caused chaos and destruction.

### Pipyap
An imp (devil) with a barbed tail and a bloodstained satchel, rescued by the party from four ogres in Maerimydra. He is knowledgeable about the city's layout, inhabitants, and key figures. He made a contract with the party to provide information and assistance in exchange for his safety. He was brought to the prime material plane by a magic user's mistake and has a strong desire to avoid returning to the Nine Hells, having found loopholes in his previous contracts to remain free. He complained about the lack of salt in the party's food and enjoys being on the prime material plane.

### Hledh
The Fire Giant Queen who rules Maerimydra, residing in the eastern castle. Pipyap advises the party to stay away from her.

### Veperyd
A fiendish giant who runs the Coliseum in Maerimydra and manages the betting pool. Pipyap describes him as 'a bit of an idiot' who has been stealing money from Vraxen, making him a potential target for creating a distraction.

### Vraxen
A Yugoloth who resides in The Spire of Inevitable Reckoning. He possesses the book the party is seeking and has a business arrangement with Veperyd regarding the Coliseum's betting pool, from which Veperyd has been stealing. A dinner event is scheduled at his residence.

### Veris
A High Inquisitor of Mulmaster who has travelled to attend the dinner at Vraxen's residence in The Spire of Inevitable Reckoning, where he intends to acquire the book the party seeks. Secretly hunting Felkur, he is a rival buyer for the tome and sets the party's deadline — they must steal the book before the dinner concludes and he obtains it.

### Graz'zt
A demon lord who controls Maerimydra and Hillsfar. He made a deal with the giants to imbue them with fiendish blood and brought in Vraxen to ensure his plans are carried out. His symbol is a six-fingered hand, prominently displayed at the six-fingered courtyard where demons and giants worship him.

### Lolth
The Drow goddess whose clerics were unable to commune with her for some time. Her powers returned after 'the Blink,' an event she used to escape imprisonment. She is now reestablishing her power, organizing her clerics, and extracting revenge on those who stopped being faithful to her.
