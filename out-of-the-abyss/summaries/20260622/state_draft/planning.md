### Candlekeep — the murder cast (UPDATED Ch. 57)

**Bookwyrm (First Reader)** — **Now the prime remaining antagonist, not just the employer.** Alkrist told her post-midnight that "the problem was solved"; she stormed off. Suspected of removing/hiding Janussi's **heart in a magical chalice** (knew exactly how it would defeat divination/resurrection). Crucially, **she has read Kalan's methodology paper** — so she knows a lone *zone of truth* confession is not proof, and can use that to **discredit the party and Kalan**. Plays the "I deputized you" card; "not above suspicion" was always true.

**Kalan Strongbranch** — Gate Warden; **ally**. Removed from the case by Bookwyrm at Janussi's instigation (he's *sidelined*, not stripped of his post). Cast the *zone of truth* (concealed outside Alkrist's room). His thesis — *no single spell is proof; converge independent channels* — is both how the case was cracked and how it can be un-cracked. The party considered handing him the evidence to "clean house."

**Elian** — Kalan's clerk; **ally**. Sought out Thorin, delivered **The Threefold Proof** (the methodology memorandum) via the Ember Grapple, angled for monograph credit, and hinted "there is something rotten here." Believes Kalan's removal is tangled with the murder. Dossier: `docs/npcs/elian.md`; handout: `notes/sessions/handouts/threefold_proof.md`.

**Alkrist** — Bronze dragonborn ("Dragonborn TechBro"); **Bookwyrm's nephew**; germ-warfare researcher Janussi shut down. **Confessed** under *zone of truth*: he and A'lai planned the poisoning; he executed it. Tried "entrapment" then "mind control" defenses. Genuinely shocked about the heart.

**A'lai Aivenmore** — **Drow** (table call, overrides stale module-human/dragonborn prep). Named by Alkrist as the conspiracy's **"brains."** Was facing **exile**, not the succession. **Lever:** a drow with Underdark/Menzoberranzan connections — Daz wants what he knows; the party floated leveraging/blackmailing him.

**Sylvira Savikas** — Tiefling devil-scholar; Janussi's ex-lover; **framed/impersonated**. Points suspicion at Daral Yashenti, Kazryn Nyantani, Teles Ahvoste; claims **Bookwyrm** pushed hardest against Kalan's methodology.

**Daral Yashenti** — Bitter, drunken poet; **innocent of the murder** but poisoned himself licking the reclaimed gift book. Flattered the party for a *Lesser Restoration* (withheld pending cooperation). The book is now in party hands.

**Fembris Lancer** — Enthusiastic Great Reader; gave Glabbagool his notes; fetched Kalan for the interrogation. (Earlier table confusion with the halfling *Fheminor Scrivenbark* is resolved — Fembris is the one who appeared.)

## Faction States

### Candlekeep (UPDATED Ch. 57)
- **Stance:** Was neutral/transactional; the murder has made it a **political battlefield**. The party cracked the case but inside an institution where the acting head (Bookwyrm) is now their chief suspect and controls legitimacy.
- **The methodological fault line:** Kalan's Threefold Proof (championed by him, delivered by Elian) vs. the temptation to treat one spell as proof. Whoever controls the *standard of evidence* controls the outcome — and Bookwyrm read the paper first.
- **Open institutional questions:** Who convenes/validates a verdict? Does Kalan get reinstated to "clean house"? What is Bookwyrm's standing if implicated?

## Active Plots

*Ordered by urgency.*

### 1. The Candlekeep Murder — The Reckoning (NEW PRIMARY, Ch. 57)
- **What:** The conspiracy is exposed (Alkrist + A'lai; Bookwyrm complicit in cover-up, suspected of taking the heart). But it is **not proven** and **not closed.**
- **The trap:** The party believes Alkrist's *zone of truth* confession settles it. By **Kalan's own published standard** (The Threefold Proof), a single spell is not proof. **Bookwyrm has read that standard** and can weaponize the party's over-reliance on it — to exonerate herself and discredit both the party and Kalan. (`notes/threads/candlekeep_false_confidence_bookwyrm_lever.md`.)
- **Loose ends to force convergence:** the heart (chalice → Bookwyrm), the locket/key, the unproven impersonator, the polymorph timeline anomaly.
- **Decision points:** hand evidence to Kalan; pursue Bookwyrm; leverage A'lai for Daz's intel; demand reward ("a book per suspect").
- **Intersections:** A'lai ↔ Daz's identity hunt; Bookwyrm ↔ Kalan's reinstatement; the heart ↔ resurrection/speak-with-dead being deliberately blocked.

### Convergence Points to Watch (Ch. 57 additions)
- **Bookwyrm vs. the party's confidence.** The single most exploitable dramatic beat right now: the party "won" by ignoring the very paper Candlekeep's Gate Warden wrote. Let Bookwyrm wield it.
- **A'lai as a hinge.** He is both a murder conspirator and Daz's best lead on Underdark/Menzoberranzan intel — pressuring/sparing him couples the murder arc to Daz's identity arc.
- **The heart and the chalice.** Whoever took the heart wanted Janussi unrevivable and un-questionable. That is the deepest motive in the room and points past Alkrist.
