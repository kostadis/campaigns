# Interview with Sylvira

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Daz, alone, visits the ailing Sylvira to investigate the possibility that she is being framed for Janussi's murder.
- Sylvira replies to the interview summons saying she has "a bad case of the winter flu" and welcomes the investigators to her quarters instead.
- Daz enters Sylvira's quarters, finding her bedridden with a severe fever, wrapped in elegant lilac robes, her copper-red skin sheened with sweat, lines under her "magnetic golden eyes," and her blue-black hair "tangled and greasy." A mage hand opens the door for Daz. She instructs her familiar — "Jezebel! Organize the chamber's seats in front of me!" — and offers wine or coffee, which Daz declines.
- Sylvira is shocked to learn that someone has been impersonating her likeness around Candlekeep, initially suspecting Daz of a trick: "Are you here to accuse me of a crime?" A persuasion roll of 19 convinces her of his sincerity — "if that was a trick that the drow use in Menzoberranzan... it was a poor one, so I will assume it is not one."
- She points suspicion toward Daral Yashenti, "a drunk who is bitter, and having to sell off some of his worthless [poetry] to fund a new library," though she dismisses him as too much of a drunk to plan such a thing. She also names Kazryn Nyantani, Janussi's former lover whom she calls "that bitter old hag" and "a frumpy old boar," who resented Sylvira for replacing her.
- Sylvira explains that as a tiefling who researches the infernal planes, she is an easy and convenient target for a frame-up due to widespread prejudice — "people look at a tiefling and assume that a tiefling is an abyssal spawn."
- She reveals that Kalan Strongbranch had asked her to intercede with Janussi to save his job, but she privately agreed with Janussi's decision to fire him, calling Kalan "a clown, a boar" whose research was "third-rate."
- Sylvira describes her 11:30 PM end-of-year meeting with Janussi, which centered on a new library dedicated to the Great Wheel of Planes and ended in an argument over a private matter — though she insists she "never called him a genus traitor."
- Sylvira provides an alibi, stating she snuck out to visit her son in his magical sanctuary and returned to her chambers sometime between 12:30 and 1 a.m. (she had no precise watch), after which she saw no one. She categorically denies being the figure seen near the kitchen — "honey? I would never drink honey. What kind of nonsense is this?"
- She attributes her worsening illness to an abyssal plague contracted from her son, who was born in the abyss, and whom she had been trying to coax back into the magical sanctuary she built for his protection.
- Sylvira points further suspicion toward Teles Ahvoste, who she claims has harbored hostility toward Janussi for being overlooked for Keeper of Tomes nearly 20 years ago despite being "older, and generally considered more skilled."
- She also notes that it was Bookwyrm who pushed hardest against Kalan Strongbranch's investigative proposals when Janussi brought them up.
- Daz needed a second persuasion roll (16) to keep the distraught Sylvira focused on the investigation after she broke down recalling her last words with Janussi.
