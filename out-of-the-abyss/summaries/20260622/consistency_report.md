## ISSUE 7 — Kalan Strongbranch casts Zone of Truth; no established spellcasting ability; contradicts his own stated philosophy

**Location:** Scenes (Interrogation of Alkrist), Spells (Zone of Truth)

**Issue:** Kalan casts Zone of Truth — a 2nd-level divine/bardic spell — with no prior establishment of him as a spellcaster. More significantly, the recap explicitly states his core argument is that "magic makes evidence adversarial rather than reliable." Having him use magic to compel testimony undercuts a stated thematic pillar and will confuse players who received that paper two scenes earlier.

**Evidence:** `world_state.md`: Kalan is described solely as "Gate warden." `campaign_state.md` NPC table has no spellcasting notation. The Investigation Methodology Paper (Items section, same session) argues against magical evidence. No prior session has established Kalan as a spellcaster.

**Suggested fix:** Either establish Kalan's spellcasting class in his NPC entry (e.g., "retired paladin") to make this consistent, or clarify that a different party member or NPC cast the Zone of Truth and Kalan assisted through other means. If Kalan did cast it as a deliberate contradiction of his principles, note that irony explicitly so future sessions can play on it.
