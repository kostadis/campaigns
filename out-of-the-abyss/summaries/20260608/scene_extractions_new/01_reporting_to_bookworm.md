# Reporting to Bookwyrm

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party returns to the Great Chapter House to update Bookwyrm on their initial investigation of Janussi's room.
- Moon elf scribes move to halt the party at the entrance before Bookwyrm herself waves them through.
- The party had attempted to overhear the conference between Bookwyrm and Kei Tigersteel beforehand; nobody rolled above the perception threshold and they heard nothing.
- Bookwyrm is found in conference with Kei Tigersteel — described as a slender human-elf of around fifty years, bald scalp framed by black side whiskers chiseled into a goatee — who serves as the Lorekeeper of Candlekeep and priest of the Temple of Oghma.
- The party confirms they have finished examining the crime scene and the body, sharing only generalities: they have a couple of suspects they would like to pursue based on perceived motive, but the investigation is ongoing.
- Bookwyrm probes: "was there nobody that you wished to start your investigation with?" The party deflects, noting she should not read anything into their choices.
- The party inwardly debates whether to give Bookwyrm anything more, aware that she has the power to dismiss them and that she is not above suspicion.
- Bookwyrm instructs Kei Tigersteel to take Janussi's body to the Shrine of Oghma for purification and dismisses him.
- The party is led to Deneir's Sanctum, a shrine and library dedicated to the art of writing and study, to serve as their base of operations.
- Bookwyrm assigns Fembris Lancer to assist the party and Vooshadi Moonriver to guard the door; she also cautions them not to move through Candlekeep without one of the two attendants, noting the library is very easy to get lost in.
- The party notices Bookwyrm has a marked preference for them to speak with Sylvira Savikas, though she does not say so directly.
- The party debates whether to stay together or split up to cover more ground, ultimately deciding that splitting increases coverage even if each member must roll alone.
