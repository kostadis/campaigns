# Research in the Flora Theca

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Grygum visits the library's botanical section to identify the poison and research the stolen sapphires.
- Fembris Lancer guides Grygum to the Flora Theca — described as a subsection of the Pavilion Naturalis within the Great Library, dedicated to plants, herbs, and fungi.
- Grygum rolls a 17 on his investigation check.
- He discovers a tome titled *Fey-mous Flowers & Fungi* by Archibald Roots, Jr., son of the legendary gnome botanist Archibald Roots Sr. The book covers the botanist's life, explorations, and collection of botanical specimens.
- The tome identifies the poison as midnight tears: a highly toxic pale ochre dust made from the pollen of a plant usually found only in the Feywild. The book notes that when midnight tears is mixed with the sap of a certain Feywild tree it loses its toxic properties entirely and becomes a powerful pain relief solution; otherwise it is lethal. It can be delivered in food or liquid.
- Its defining property: the victim suffers no ill effect whatsoever until the stroke of midnight, at which point death follows swiftly.
- The party deduces that the poison was applied to the pages of a book and that Janussi ingested it by licking his fingers to turn pages — consistent with the black discoloration found on his fingers and lips (the ochre dust darkening on contact with saliva).
- Grygum also researches the significance of the stolen sapphires in spellcasting. The DM identifies the relevant spell as Drawmij's Instant Summons — a powerful wizard spell requiring an expensive sapphire as a component that allows an object to be hidden in an extradimensional space and later summoned directly to the caster's hand. The DM shares the spell text in the session Discord.
- Grygum immediately theorizes this as the mechanism for concealing or retrieving the stolen High Tower key; one player notes the spell would be ideal for the keys specifically.
- The question is raised whether the spell would function if an object was being worn on a lanyard; the DM indicates it would not apply to an object currently being worn.
