# Interrogating Daral Yashenti at the Hearth

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Daz and Thorin confront Daral Yashenti, a disgruntled and intoxicated scholar, at the Hearth tavern.
- Fembris Lancer is sent to summon Daral Yashenti; he returns with a two-word reply. Daral is not coming to the party.
- The party goes to the Hearth and finds Daral drunk, boisterous, and in conspicuously good spirits. Daz's drow instincts provide an immediate read: in the Underdark, attacking your superior and failing meant death for you and your bloodline; up here, people merely get fired. Daral may simply be relieved he is not facing worse consequences.
- Daral greets them: *"Oh, look, it's Faron's most famous detectives! Bookwyrm's Brownnosers!"* He then orders brandies at his own expense: "Barman, a brown of spice brandies for our beloved Brownnosers. My treat."
- Daral freely admits to his 11 AM end-of-year meeting with Janussi, during which Janussi rejected his proposal to preserve certain priceless works of poetry, intending instead to sell them to fund ritual materials for Sylvira — "his horned tart," in Daral's words, one of several racially tinged remarks about tieflings and lizardfolk that surface during the conversation.
- Daral describes Janussi as an "uneducated bookseller" and says of his death: "Haha, may the gods bless whoever bumped him off."
- Daral explains that he returned to Janussi's chambers that evening and found him: *"When I arrived at his chamber, the shameless hypocrite was embraced in one of the very books he'd have me sell!"* He took the book back; Janussi objected physically; *"In defending myself, I may have caught him lightly on the nose."*
- After the altercation, Daral repaired to the Hearth to calm his nerves. As he tells the story, he casually flips through the pages of that same book, licking his fingers to turn each page.
- The party asks where he obtained the book; Daral says it has been in his personal collection for years. The party notes the contradiction: he gave Janussi the book specifically so Janussi would read it, yet was enraged to find Janussi reading it. Daral's only reply: "It was a long day. I was drugged. I was upset. I don't know what I was thinking."
- Daral makes a disparaging remark about Bookwyrm with reptilian undertones: *"There's no telling what's going on in her lizard brain."* A party member stands up to defend lizardfolk; Daral quickly backtracks and calls it barroom chatter.
- Daral names his suspects: Teles Ahvoste ("thought he was first in line last time around, the deluded old git"), A'lai Aivenmore ("spoiling to rule the roost, the preying cock"), Fheminor Scrivenbark ("seething about the dissolution of her precious [interests] — don't be fooled by her stature, she has a steely nature"), and Bookwyrm herself.
- The party warns Daral that the book is likely laced with midnight tears poison, applied to the pages — and he has been ingesting it by licking his fingers. He drops the book immediately: *"You've got to do something!"*
- The party secures the book as evidence and resolves to keep Daral close, with Lesser Restoration and a cure poison available should he show symptoms before midnight.
- The party notes internally: if Daral dies at midnight, he almost certainly did not poison the book.
