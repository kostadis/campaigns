# A Secret Meeting with Kalan

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party encounters Kalan Strongbranch in the corridor, where he shares critical intelligence about the High Tower keys and his own investigation.
- Multiple party members are present; Kalan presses the small brass key specifically into Grygum's hand.
- Kalan sweeps the corridor left and right before speaking: *"Whatever Bookwyrm is telling you, listen for what she isn't. The High Tower has two keys. Janussi wore one, I held the other. I have given it now to someone who is not me. If I and I do not see you tomorrow, do not look for me at my post."*
- Zalthir alone pursues Kalan to ask him questions.
- Asked whether he fears being killed for the key: *"It is best that if I am killed, the key not be found."*
- Kalan shows Zalthir  his own now-empty locket to illustrate how both keys were carried; the Zalthir asks him to open it and verify its contents to confirm the key they have received is genuinely his.
- Zalthir confirms that both keys are required simultaneously to enter the High Tower — possession of one alone is insufficient. The High Tower itself is never unobserved even when the keys are elsewhere; entry would be noticed.
- Kalan shares his forensic conclusions: the keeper was poisoned by something he touched or held in his left hand that interacted with his saliva; the heart was hacked out after death, most likely to prevent resurrection.
- Kalan asserts that the murderer was a powerful magic user capable of bypassing both mundane and arcane locks — Knock spells of the highest order.
- Kalan assumes one person is responsible for both the murder and the rifling of the safe. Zalthir challenges this: four people came and went from the apartment, the evidence suggests one person knew exactly where the locket was while another searched the safe for it, so these may have been two separate actors. Kalan pauses: *"I don't know. Perhaps I was a little bit too hasty. Perhaps it was two different people."*
- Kalan voices his overarching suspicion: one of the Great Readers is the murderer, and either Bookwyrm is protecting them or was manipulated into removing him from the case to protect the killer.
- Kalan delivers his assessment of the party's involvement: *"I believe you're only being employed so you're likely to bungle the case, or else be silent if you do happen upon the right scent. You don't matter. You're outsiders."*
- Zalthir counters that they have already corrected one of his assumptions. He shares the detail that someone was impersonating Sylvira Savikas on the night of the murder — information Kalan did not have. He responds: "You are very good at this" — as close to a compliment as he gets. He adds that whoever set this up "will be very disappointed in you."
- Kalan and Zalthri  negotiate an information-sharing arrangement. Kalan proposes it in corporate terms: *"Why don't we all go off and do offline chat… come back and have a meeting where we go over all of our findings in a group setting where we'll examine everything one by one."* Zalthir asks if they can divide it into work streams; Kalan enthusiastically accepts and proposes five. The exchange ends with agreement to "merge all the things."
- Kalan provides his timeline: 8 AM meeting with Janussi, in which he pressed for more watchers and was rejected; 10:15 AM visit to Sylvira Savikas for advice on a cosmological question; then return to the Chamber of Lost Lore for private research. On the day of the murder he arrived at the crime scene shortly after 8 AM, ordered the chamber cordoned off, posted a watcher, declared a state of emergency, and doubled the guard at the High Tower Library.
- zalthir asks whether Kalan has spells for truth detection; he confirms he has capabilities in that area and would consider deploying them under appropriate circumstances.
- Kalan warns the party to be wary of shapeshifters and those who can change their face before departing.
