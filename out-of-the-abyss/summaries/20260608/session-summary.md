# Chapter 55 Questions, Answers and a Key

Date: Jun 8th, 2026

## Summary

The party returned to the Great Chapter House to report their findings to Bookwyrm, the First Speaker of Candlekeep. Moon elf scribes moved to block their entrance before Bookwyrm herself waved them through. They found her in conference with Kei Tigersteel — a slender human-elf of around fifty years, his bald scalp framed by sharp black side whiskers chiseled into a goatee — who served as the spiritual leader of the library and priest of the Temple of Oghma. The party had attempted to listen in on the conference beforehand but failed to exceed the perception threshold, hearing nothing. When Bookwyrm questioned them, they shared only the broadest strokes of their investigation, declining to name suspects and deflecting her pointed follow-up — "was there nobody that you wished to start your investigation with?" — by noting she should read nothing into their choices of whom to see next. Bookwyrm dismissed Kei Tigersteel with instructions to take Janussi's body to the Shrine of Oghma for purification. She then led the party to Deneir's Sanctum, a shrine dedicated to the art of writing, and offered it as their private office, assigning the eager acolyte Fembris Lancer to fetch suspects and the moon elf Vooshadi Moonriver to stand guard at the door. The party noted that Bookwyrm appeared to have a strong preference for them to speak to Sylvira Savikas, though she never said so directly.

Before the party could fully settle into their new headquarters, Kalan Strongbranch appeared in the corridor and pressed a small brass key into Grygum's hand with quiet urgency, his eyes sweeping left and right before he spoke: *"Whatever Bookwyrm is telling you, listen for what she isn't. The High Tower has two keys. Janussi wore one, I held the other. I have given it now to someone who is not me. If I and I do not see you tomorrow, do not look for me at my post."* He explained that Janussi had worn the second High Tower key in a locket around his neck, and that locket was now missing from the crime scene. He feared he was being targeted for the key he had held, and made clear that if he were killed, it was better the key not be found on him. Kalan also shared his forensic conclusions: the keeper had been poisoned by something he touched or held in his left hand that interacted with his saliva; the heart had been hacked out after death, most likely to prevent anyone from resurrecting the corpse; and the murderer was a powerful magic user capable of bypassing both mundane and arcane locks — Knock spells of the highest order, he said. When the party challenged his assumption that a single culprit had both murdered Janussi and rifled the safe — pointing out that one person clearly knew where the locket was while another clearly did not — Kalan paused and conceded: "Perhaps I was a little bit too hasty. Perhaps it was two different people." He suspected one of the Great Readers was the murderer and that Bookwyrm may have been manipulated into removing him from the case to protect the killer. His initial assessment of the party was bluntly unflattering: *"I believe you're only being employed so you're likely to bungle the case, or else be silent if you do happen upon the right scent. You don't matter. You're outsiders."* But when the party revealed that someone had been impersonating Sylvira Savikas on the night of the murder — a detail Kalan had not previously known — he conceded with as much grace as he could muster: "You are very good at this." He agreed to pool findings that evening, proposing the arrangement in strikingly corporate terms: *"Why don't we all go off and do offline chat… have offline meetings, and come back and have a meeting where we go over all of our findings in a group setting where we'll examine everything one by one."* Grygum asked if they could divide it into work streams; Kalan embraced this enthusiastically, proposing five; the exchange concluded with a mutual commitment to "merge all the things." Before departing to conduct his own parallel investigation, he warned the party to be wary of those who could change their face.

The party split their efforts. Grygum made his way to the Flora Theca, a botanical subsection of the Pavilion Naturalis, guided by Fembris. A roll of 17 on his investigation check surfaced a tome called *Fey-mous Flowers & Fungi* by Archibald Roots, Jr. Within its pages he identified the poison as midnight tears: a highly toxic pale ochre dust harvested from the pollen of a plant native only to the Feywild, deliverable through food or liquid, and bearing the lethal property that the victim suffers no ill effect whatsoever until the stroke of midnight, at which point death comes swiftly. This matched the timeline precisely — and pointed directly at the black discoloration on Janussi's fingers and lips, the ochre dust reacting with saliva. Grygum also researched the stolen sapphires and discovered they were prized components for Drawmij's Instant Summons, a powerful spell capable of hiding an object in an extradimensional space and later summoning it directly to the caster's hand. The party immediately theorized that the gems had been intended as the mechanism for concealing or retrieving the stolen High Tower key.

Meanwhile, Fembris was sent to summon Daral Yashenti; he returned with a two-word reply. Daral was not coming to them. When the party arrived at the Hearth, they found him drunk, boisterous, and in conspicuously good spirits — a mood that Daz's drow instincts explained immediately: in the Underdark, attacking your superior and failing meant death for you and your bloodline; up here, people merely got fired. Daral greeted the party with a mocking toast — *"Oh, look, it's Faron's most famous detectives! Bookwyrm's Brownnosers!"* — and ordered a round of spiced brandies at his own expense. He admitted freely to his 11 AM end-of-year meeting with Janussi, during which the Keeper had rejected his proposal to preserve certain priceless works of poetry, intending instead to sell them to fund ritual materials for Sylvira, whom Daral referred to as "his horned tart" — one of several racially tinged remarks that drew pushback from the party on behalf of tieflings and lizardfolk alike. Furious, Daral had returned to Janussi's chambers that evening, found him reading the very book of poetry Daral had gifted him, and taken it back: *"In defending myself, I may have caught him lightly on the nose."* When the party pointed out the contradiction — he had given Janussi the book specifically so Janussi would read it, yet was outraged to find him reading it — Daral's only explanation was "I was drugged. I was upset. I don't know what I was thinking." He then repaired to the Hearth, and in the telling of all of this, casually flipped through the pages of that same book, licking his fingers to turn each page. The theory crystallized in real time: midnight tears had been applied to the pages, and Janussi had ingested it exactly this way. Daral, doing the same thing in front of them, had potentially sealed his own fate. When the party warned him, he dropped the book in a panic: *"You've got to do something!"* They secured the book as evidence. The party resolved to keep Daral close until midnight, with Lesser Restoration and a cure poison effect available should he begin showing symptoms — and noted that if Daral died at midnight, that would strongly argue against his being the poisoner. In his rattled state, Daral offered up his suspicions freely: Teles Ahvoste had always believed himself first in line; A'lai Aivenmore was "spoiling to rule the roost, the preying cock"; Fheminor Scrivenbark was "seething about the dissolution of her precious [interests] — don't be fooled by her stature, she has a steely nature." As for Bookwyrm: *"There's no telling what's going on in her lizard brain"* — a remark that immediately produced an indignant response from a party member on behalf of lizardfolk everywhere. Daral quickly backpedaled, calling it barroom chatter.

While all of this unfolded, Glabbagool made his way to the Whispering Dome of Introspection, with Zalthir following. A shadow monk posed a question about the art of movement. Glabbagool went still for roughly sixty seconds. Then he answered: *"The body that mimics nothing is already shaped by what it watches."* The monk smiled and nodded. Glabbagool announced he had been waiting a very long time for this moment, flowed up onto Zalthir's arm, and declared a *"Glabbagool Zalthir combo action."* He had transformed from a wandering curiosity into a true companion — an eighth-level sidekick capable of teleporting up to sixty feet in darkness, performing a flurry of blows, forcing his acidic shadow into wounds, and executing a corrosive embrace. A former shadow monk watching from the side observed dryly that Zalthir would have understood exactly what the exchange meant if he had paid more attention in the relevant training sessions — which had apparently been the day Zalthir was asleep.

## Locations

### Great Chapter House
The central administrative hub of Candlekeep where the Council of the Great Readers meets and where Bookwyrm holds her conferences. Moon elf scribes stood at the entrance and briefly halted the party before Bookwyrm waved them through.

### Deneir's Sanctum
A shrine and library dedicated to the art of writing and study, provided to the party by Bookwyrm as a private office for their investigation. The room's name derives from the deity Deneir. Vooshadi Moonriver is posted outside as guard; Bookwyrm noted no one she does not want will be permitted to enter.

### Shrine of Oghma
A sacred place within Candlekeep where Janussi's body is sent for purification rites at Bookwyrm's instruction, under the oversight of Kei Tigersteel, priest of the Temple of Oghma.

### High Tower
A restricted area within Candlekeep housing powerful magical artifacts of the sort that every evil entity in the world would want access to, requiring both of two specific keys to enter simultaneously. It is currently under doubled guard following the murder. The DM notes that entry to the High Tower does not go unobserved — watchers are always posted regardless of where the keys are; walking in on a Monday would be noticed.

### Chamber of Lost Lore
A location within Candlekeep where Kalan Strongbranch conducts his private research and duties. He stated he returned here after visiting Sylvira Savikas on the morning before the murder and continued his private research until the alarm was raised.

### Flora Theca
A subsection of the Pavilion Naturalis within the Great Library, dedicated to the study of plants, herbs, and fungi. Grygum conducted his poison research here with a roll of 17, surfacing *Fey-mous Flowers & Fungi*.

### The Hearth
A social tavern within Candlekeep where Daral Yashenti was found drinking and reading the suspicious book. The party conducted their interrogation here after Daral sent back a two-word reply to Fembris's summons, refusing to come to the party.

### Whispering Dome of Introspection
A sacred space within Candlekeep where supplicants seek self-knowledge through silence and philosophical inquiry. Glabbagool underwent his transformation here; Zalthir was present to witness the exchange with the shadow monk.

## Items

### High Tower Key (Kalan's)
A small brass key, one of two required simultaneously to access the High Tower's vault of powerful magical artifacts. Kalan Strongbranch secretly pressed it into Grygum's hand for safekeeping, fearing he would be killed for possessing it. Both keys are needed at once; one alone opens nothing.

### Janussi's Locket
A personal item stolen from the victim's body during the murder. It contained the second High Tower key and was taken by someone who knew exactly where to look — in stark contrast to whoever separately rifled the safe, apparently unaware the key was already gone.

### Poison Sample
A substance recovered from Janussi's fingers and lips, serving as a primary piece of physical evidence. The black discoloration — the ochre midnight tears dust darkening on contact with saliva — was the key clue pointing toward identification of the poison.

### Fey-mous Flowers & Fungi
A botanical tome authored by Archibald Roots, Jr., son of the legendary gnome botanist Archibald Roots Sr., discovered by Grygum in the Flora Theca with a roll of 17. It provided the critical identification of midnight tears, its Feywild origin, its ochre appearance, its midnight-activation property, its delivery method, and the Feywild-tree antidote.

### Midnight Tears
A highly toxic pale ochre dust made from the pollen of a plant usually found only in the Feywild. Its defining property: the victim suffers no ill effect until the stroke of midnight, at which point death comes swiftly — making it a near-perfect murder weapon. It can be delivered in food or liquid. When mixed with the sap of a specific Feywild tree, midnight tears loses its toxicity entirely and becomes a powerful pain reliever. The party theorized it was applied to the pages of Daral Yashenti's book of poetry and ingested when Janussi licked his fingers to turn pages.

### Daral Yashenti's Book
A book of poetry that Daral Yashenti gifted to Janussi from his personal collection — intending it as a demonstration of the books' value, to make the point that they should not be sold — and later forcibly reclaimed during their altercation. It is the suspected delivery mechanism for the midnight tears poison: Janussi was found having read it while licking his fingers to turn pages, and Daral Yashenti was seen doing the same thing at the Hearth. The book is now in the party's custody as evidence.

### Sapphires
Expensive gems stolen from Janussi's chambers. Grygum's research revealed they are the required costly component for Drawmij's Instant Summons, a powerful wizard spell that hides an object in an extradimensional space and later summons it directly to the caster's hand. The party theorizes the stolen sapphires were intended for use in concealing or retrieving the stolen High Tower key.

A necromancy spell that Kalan reportedly attempted to cast on Janussi's corpse, but which failed because someone else had already cast it first, locking out the spell for a period of days. Kalan confirmed he can cast this spell and offered to assist the party with it should the need arise.

### Knock
A spell of the highest order that Kalan theorized was used by the murderer to circumvent both the mundane and arcane locks protecting Janussi's chambers. Kalan's phrasing — "of the highest order" — implies this was an exceptionally powerful casting, not a routine application.

### Lesser Restoration
Mentioned as a potential remedy to save Daral Yashenti if he begins showing symptoms of midnight tears poisoning before midnight. Both Grygum and Dawnbringer are noted as being capable of casting this or a cure poison effect.

### Drawmij's Instant Summons
A powerful wizard spell requiring an expensive sapphire as a component, identified during Grygum's library research. It allows an object to be hidden away in an extradimensional space and later summoned directly to the caster's hand. The DM shared the spell text in the session Discord. The party immediately theorized this as the mechanism for hiding or retrieving the stolen High Tower key. The DM noted the spell would not function for an object currently being worn or carried on a person; it would not apply to an object on a lanyard.

Bookwyrm dismissed him from the debrief and directed him to take Janussi's body to the Shrine of Oghma for purification.

### Fembris Lancer
An eager acolyte adjutant who guides the party through Candlekeep and summons suspects for questioning. When sent to fetch Daral Yashenti, he returned with a two-word reply — Daral was not coming to them. He successfully guided Grygum to the Flora Theca for the poison research.

### Vooshadi Moonriver
A moon elf scribe assigned by Bookwyrm to stand guard outside the party's temporary office in Deneir's Sanctum. Moon elf scribes of the same order initially moved to block the party at the entrance to Bookwyrm's conference before being waved through.

### Kalan Strongbranch
The Gate Warden of Candlekeep, officially removed from the murder investigation by Bookwyrm. He secretly pressed one of the two High Tower keys into Grygum's hand in the corridor, fearing for his life if found with it. He is philosophical and given to sweeping pronouncements about fate, order, and futility. He shared detailed forensic observations — poison entered via the left hand and interacted with saliva; the heart was removed post-mortem to prevent resurrection; the murderer used high-order Knock magic. When the party corrected his assumption that it was one culprit rather than two, he conceded without resentment. When told the party had already learned more than he had and corrected one of his assumptions, he acknowledged: "You are very good at this." He proposed a corporate-flavored collaboration framework and agreed to share findings later that evening. He suspects one of the Great Readers is the killer and that Bookwyrm may have been manipulated. He warned the party to watch for shapeshifters.

### Janussi
The deceased Keeper of Candlekeep whose murder is the central focus of the investigation. He wore one of the two High Tower keys in a locket around his neck, which was stolen. His heart was removed post-mortem, most likely to prevent resurrection. At his 8 AM meeting with Kalan he rejected a proposal for increased watchers; at his 11 AM meeting with Daral he rejected the proposal to preserve the poetry books. He was found that evening reading a book of poetry gifted by Daral Yashenti, licking his fingers as he turned pages — almost certainly the means by which he ingested the midnight tears poison. He died at or near midnight. Daral referred to him as "that philistine."

### Sylvira Savikas
Janussi's on-and-off girlfriend, a tiefling who was notably absent from the council meeting. Someone was reportedly seen impersonating her on the night of the crime — a detail Kalan had not previously known and which prompted him to revise his assessment of the investigation. Daral Yashenti referred to her as Janussi's "horned tart" and blamed the Keeper's plans to sell books on her influence. Kalan visited her chamber at 10:15 AM on the day before the murder for advice on a cosmological question.

### Fheminor Scrivenbark
A dowdy female halfling and Great Reader who expressed concern at the council meeting about whether Janussi had named a successor. Daral Yashenti described her as "seething about the dissolution of her precious [interests]" and warned the party: "Don't be fooled by her stature, she has a steely nature."

### Daral Yashenti
An arrogant and frequently intoxicated Great Reader who held deep resentment for Janussi's leadership, particularly his plans to sell priceless books. He admitted freely to gifting Janussi a book of poetry from his personal collection, returning to reclaim it that evening, and striking Janussi during the altercation: "In defending myself, I may have caught him lightly on the nose." He was found at the Hearth in conspicuously good spirits — mood explained by the drow party member as likely relief at not facing Underdark-style consequences for attacking his superior. He was actively reading the suspected murder weapon and licking his fingers to turn pages when the party arrived. When warned of the poison, he dropped the book in panic. He named multiple suspects while rattled: Teles Ahvoste, A'lai Aivenmore, Fheminor Scrivenbark, and Bookwyrm herself. His racial remarks about tieflings and lizardfolk drew pushback from the party. He is now both a person of interest and a potential victim — if he dies at midnight, he almost certainly did not poison the book.


### A'lai Aivenmore
A Great Reader described by Daral Yashenti as "spoiling to rule the roost, the preying cock" — openly hungry for power. He was the most vocal figure at the council meeting about restoring leadership to Candlekeep quickly, pushing for an immediate vote.

### Teles Ahvoste
An older Great Reader who Daral Yashenti claims believed himself first in line for the Keeper position during the last transition of power — "the deluded old git," in Daral's words. He was heard at the council meeting asking where the Gate Warden was with his report.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent grey ooze who visited the Whispering Dome of Introspection with Zalthir present. He posed a question about the art of movement to the shadow monk there, went still for roughly sixty seconds, and answered: *"The body that mimics nothing is already shaped by what it watches."* A monk's smile and nod confirmed the answer. He has now become an eighth-level sidekick bonded to Zalthir, capable of shadow teleportation (up to 60 feet in darkness), a flurry of blows, acidic strikes into wounds, and a corrosive embrace (2 Ki cost, requires a grappled target). He manifests several eyes on Zalthir's arm and is keeping watch over the drow captive in the bag of holding.
