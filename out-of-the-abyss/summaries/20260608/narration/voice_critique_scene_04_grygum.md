## Flags

### [1] Atmospheric simile (inanimate personification, not Grygum's mode)

> "he walked the way Candlekeep people walk — like the books are sleeping and might wake."

**Why:** Grygum notices *people's behavior*, not atmospheric metaphor. His spec says he reads people fast with quiet confidence, and his observation style is paratactic and functional. "The books are sleeping and might wake" is literary scene-setting; Grygum would observe what Fembris *does* — how he moves, what it costs him — not impute feelings to the shelves.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The librarian who took me down was named Fembris, and he walked like someone who believed noise had consequences." [Still observational, but of behavior rather than inanimate personification.]

### [2] Generic colloquial

> "You consume it, you're hunky-dory, and then you're dead at midnight."

**Why:** "Hunky-dory" is a generic positive-affect filler that doesn't match Grygum's precise deadpan register. His examples show drier phrasing with specific deadpan timing: "That worked better than I had any right to expect." "Kaboom! I didn't expect it to work." When he uses slang, it tends to apply policy or civic language to fantasy — not American idiom.
**Suggested rewrite:** "You consume it, you're fine — apparently fine — and then you're dead at midnight."

### [3] Inverted Stroud reference

> "the answer was sitting there waiting, the way the chessboard waits for the man who can't actually play chess."

**Why:** The chessboard wasn't waiting for Stroud (the non-player who rolled a natural 20) — it was waiting for Protanther, who had studied it for 400 years. Stroud's point is that the man who couldn't play still won. "The chessboard waits for the man who can't play" flips the story's meaning: it implies the board will defeat the unqualified, when the story actually says the unqualified sometimes win anyway. Grygum knows this story; he'd have the spin right.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I almost laughed. I had been bracing for a week of cross-referencing and instead the answer was sitting there waiting. Stroud couldn't play chess either, and he still rolled the winning move."

## Verdict

This is the cleanest narration in the set — the discovery structure, "Poison is just botany with intent," the Schwarzenegger time-zone riff, and the priestly training thought source (*"The purpose of a maxim is not to tell you what to do — it is to tell you what you are doing."*) followed immediately by "Bahamut may or may not have been watching me work it out. I took notes either way" are all strongly in character. The flags are genuinely spot-edits. The Stroud reference [3] is the most important fix: the story does real work in Grygum's voice and the inverted framing misfires its meaning.
