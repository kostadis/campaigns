## Flags

### [1] Over-articulated observation (Voice spec conflict)

> "He chose the orc. I don't think it was chance. The man who carries the weight is the man someone has decided to trust."

**Why:** Zalthir's inner voice is terse: "Filed it." "Not again." "I sense some racial animus." He observes and moves; he does not unpack the implication into a general aphorism. "The man who carries the weight is the man someone has decided to trust" reads as Grygum's philosophical-witness register, not Zalthir's declarative notation. The observation "He chose the orc. I don't think it was chance." already lands; the elaboration dilutes it.
**Suggested rewrite:** "He chose the orc. I don't think it was chance. Filed."

### [2] Generic idiom

> "I had one card and I played it plainly."

**Why:** Card-game language is generic and also intrudes from Daz's information-as-currency register. Zalthir doesn't name his tactical moves before making them — he acts, and the action is the argument. "I had one card" is announcing a thing Zalthir would just do.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I told him the one thing he didn't have." or simply remove the frame: drop directly into the thing told.

### [3] Simile too elaborate for terse narrator

> "He looked at me the way a man looks at someone explaining how he came to run the gate of Candlekeep."

**Why:** Zalthir's examples compress reactions to one short clause: "I sense some racial animus." "Filed it." An elaborate how-he-came-to simile is three beats too long for his register. He would name the look or skip to what he did next.
**Suggested rewrite:** "He looked at me like I'd said something self-evident." or just "He looked at me. Said nothing."

### [4] Italicized theatrical aside

> "He performed the moment — *an interview with Kalan Strongbranch* —"

**Why:** Italicized air-quotes signal stylized self-awareness — closer to Daz's social-theater framing than Zalthir's straight-faced notation. Zalthir would say what Kalan did, not comment on him doing it as a performance.
**Suggested rewrite:** "He staged it — reminded us twice that he'd been wanted off the case."

## Verdict

The core Zalthir voice is solid throughout — "True enough. Filed under things that are also true," the decisive questioning of Kalan's two-people-in-the-apartment logic, and the close ("I'm just saying, and let him go to his research") are all in spec. The main drift is toward over-articulation: Zalthir elaborates where the spec says he'd just mark and move. Flag [1] is the strongest miss — it's Grygum's register, not Zalthir's.
