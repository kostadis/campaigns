# Voice Critique — Daz, scene 01: Reporting to Bookwyrm

**Narration:** `summaries/20260608/narration/session_doc_scene_01_reporting_to_bookworm.scrubbed.md`
**Voice spec:** `voice/daz_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/daz.md`

## Flags

### [1] Cliché simile

> "Behind me, Grygum was worrying at the word like a loose tooth."

**Why:** Daz doesn't observe companions through body-sensation similes; his observations are categorical (who owes what, who has what leverage, what column do I put this in). "Like a loose tooth" is stock — it could appear in any narrator's prose.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Behind me, Grygum was running the word back and forth. Checking it against what we'd actually been told."

### [2] Generic ornate metaphor

> "the trouble is that my eye for the written page is a scalpel and my ears, through a closed door, are nothing special"

**Why:** Daz's self-description deploys bookkeeping vocabulary (audit, account, column, precision in destruction from the Sculpt Spells angle) — not surgical imagery. "Scalpel" is a competent figure for precision but it isn't his precision vocabulary. The examples show him framing skill as supply and reconnaissance, not as medical tools.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I had the right tools for reading a page. Through a closed door, those tools were the wrong shape entirely."

### [3] Wrong-idiom metaphor (maritime frame)

> "a hand on the rudder of an investigation she'd rather not have pointed at herself"

**Why:** Nautical "rudder" doesn't fit Daz's cognitive frame. He is a drow raised underground, trained in the Menzoberranzan House system; his spatial metaphors run to architecture, finance, and blast radius — not ships. Three other bookkeeping phrases appear in the same narration; "rudder" breaks the set.
**Suggested rewrite:** "a thumb on the scale of an investigation she'd rather not have tilted toward herself"

### [4] Bookkeeping vocab repeated without rotation (spec violation)

> "Both columns stayed open." [line ~56]  …followed by…  "Both ledgers stayed open on that one too." [line ~73]

**Why:** The voice spec explicitly flags this: *"rotate the bookkeeping vocabulary as you narrate — audit, tally, account, column, balance, debt, the running list — rather than letting one noun ('ledger') dominate a scene or the document."* Using both "columns" and "ledgers" for the identical rhetorical move within one scene is over-indexing without rotating.
**Suggested rewrite:** Keep "Both columns stayed open." Change the second instance to: "I left that account open too." or "Both debts stayed unresolved."

## Verdict

The Daz voice is largely on-target here — the Menzoberranzan training dictum ("the first rule of any House"), the two-column analytical mode, the dry deflection of Bookwyrm's probes, and the final "I could live with that" are all strongly in character. The four flags are spot-edits rather than systemic drift. The strongest single flag is [3] (maritime metaphor from a character whose entire spatial vocabulary is underground and financial). Flag [4] is a quick copy edit.
