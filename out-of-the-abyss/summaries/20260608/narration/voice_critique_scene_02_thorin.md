## Flags

### [1] Generic simile

> "Down here everyone lies as a reflex, like breathing."

**Why:** "Like breathing" is a stock comparison. The sentence that follows it — "You can't out-talk a culture that talks for a living" — is the stronger, more distinctive Thorin line and does the same work. Keeping both weakens the second. Thorin's voice uses physical concrete language; the reflex would be described as a thing in the room, not a body analogy.
**Suggested rewrite:** Drop the simile; start the sentence at the conclusion. "Down here everyone lies as a reflex. You can't out-talk a culture that talks for a living."

### [2] Wrong-idiom register (card game)

> "everybody's holding a card they won't show"

**Why:** Card-game metaphors belong to Daz's information-as-currency frame, not Thorin's terrain vocabulary. Thorin thinks in positions, leverage points, and who can be trusted to act predictably — ground and structure, not hands and bluffs. The voice spec is explicit: *"He uses physical, stone-weight language: things land, strike, hold or break."*
**Suggested rewrite:** "everybody's got a door they won't open" or "everybody's standing in a position they won't leave"

### [3] Stone-idiom over-engineered

> "You don't say the word until the stone proves it."

**Why:** While Thorin does use stone-weight language, this reads as deliberately placed "stone = dwarf" color. His examples show blunt declaratives rather than manufactured-idiom similes; compare "The heart is a fact" (five lines later in the same narration, which is excellent). "The stone proves it" is what a dwarf says in a fantasy novel; Thorin is who he is without announcing it.
**Suggested rewrite:** "You don't say the word until you have the fact in your hand." Or just: "You don't say the word. Not until you're sure."

### [4] Frontmatter typo (mechanical fix)

> `narrator: Throin`

Should be `narrator: Thorin`. See meta note above.

## Verdict

Strong Thorin narration overall — the giants-upbringing framing, the tactical terrain-read on Kalan's account of the corpse, "The heart is a fact," and the solo-conversation close ("One conversation, one truth, no committee") are all exactly right. The card-game idiom [2] is the most off-voice flag because it imports Daz's register. The frontmatter typo [4] is the most urgent mechanical fix.
