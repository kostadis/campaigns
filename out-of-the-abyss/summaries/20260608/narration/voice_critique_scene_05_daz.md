# Voice Critique — Daz, scene 05: Interrogating Daral Yashenti at the Hearth

**Narration:** `summaries/20260608/narration/session_doc_scene_05_interrogating_daral_yashenti_at_the_hearth.scrubbed.md`
**Voice spec:** `voice/daz_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/daz.md`

## Flags

### [1] Wrong vocabulary register

> "I ran it through the only grammar I have for these situations."

**Why:** Daz's analytical framework is expressed in bookkeeping vocabulary: audit, tally, column, account, debt, running list. The voice spec explicitly delineates this rotation set and names "grammar" as not belonging to it. "Grammar" is a linguistic/structural metaphor — it's a reasonable word for *framework* but it's not in Daz's cognitive lexicon.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I ran it through the only accounting I have for these situations." or "I ran it through the Menzoberranzan framework — the one that maps punishment to probability."

### [2] Generic simile

> "Fembris went off to fetch Daral Yashenti with the eagerness of a man who has finally been handed a task"

**Why:** "The eagerness of a man who has finally been handed a task" is a generic simile Daz wouldn't reach for. He observes people and files their behavior against tactical implications — he doesn't analogize them to unnamed hypothetical men. The original observation is in the scene extraction: Fembris "very excited." Daz would notice the behavioral fact and note its implication, not frame it as comparison.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Fembris went off with the particular efficiency of someone who'd been waiting for a task to belong to, and came back with two words."

### [3] Atypically warm Thorin observation

> "Thorin started gentle, which is his version of a knife."

**Why:** This is an attractive line but it reads as fond — warm interpersonal appreciation of Thorin's style. Daz's internal commentary on others is categorical, not affectionate; he files people's methods the way he files tactical data. He'd notice the technique's function, not name it as "his version of a knife" with the implied intimacy.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Thorin opened with the easy question — when did you last see him, nothing threatening. It's a pattern. It works every time." [Daz notes the pattern and its track record, not the warmth of the approach.]

## Verdict

The strongest Daz scene in the session — "It isn't anger. It's bookkeeping," "I have heard that register before," "I watched the finger go to the mouth and back to the paper," and the closing "The dead poisoner is the innocent one" are all excellent and exactly in voice. The three flags are minor; [1] is the most significant because it's a vocabulary-set violation. [3] is borderline — if the warmth toward Thorin is intentional character development, keep it; if tightening toward cold-Daz, revise.
