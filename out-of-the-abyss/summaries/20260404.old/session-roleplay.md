# Roleplay Highlights — Session — 2026-04-04

## Character Voices

### Zalthir (Gabe)
- Understated, deadpan delivery even in the most dramatic combat moments; lets his actions speak louder than his words. Describes grappling a drow priestess mid-air and dropping her 40 feet as "annoying her."
- Honors the spirit of game mechanics rather than rules-lawyering — voluntarily limits his own options when frightened, even when it costs him effectiveness. Expresses genuine frustration at being sidelined.
- Efficient kill confirmations delivered with zero fanfare; reserves emotional energy for moments of helplessness rather than triumph.

> "I'm just gonna try annoying her then."
> "I mean, that is the way it was going, but the spirit seems to be that I should do nothing to become closer to her, or have her closer to me. I am supposed to be trying to not be near her."
> "I shall punch the mage."

### Thorin (Joe)
- Stream-of-consciousness talker who stumbles into innuendo and pop culture references mid-sentence; his thought process is audibly visible. Alternates between tactical questioning and absurdist humor.
- Quietly competent in the moments that matter — obliterates the heart fungus in a single round, then downplays it with a shrug. His frustration at the sprawling battlefield reveals a character who wants to be in the thick of things at all times.
- Wrestles with moral choices aloud, even rolling dice to resolve his own internal debates.

> "I do what I can."
> "So, what happens if he kills her and we don't? Does that change anything? Does he get, like, first dibs on booty or something? And by booty, I mean, like... they used to be Lover's Booty, I mean, like... the goods."
> "And I'm just sitting here watching trade blows like a tennis match."

### Grygum (Ben Pfaff)
- Dry wit and gentle humor that never breaks his cleric's composure; delivers one-liners that land precisely because of their understated tone. References Indiana Jones and Christmas carols mid-combat.
- Genuinely caring — his first instinct when Ilvara's Insect Plague hits is concern for Glabbagool. Offers Revivify for fallen minions before being reminded they're the enemy's minions.
- Tactical pragmatism wrapped in pastoral warmth; navigates around the Jorlan-Ilvara lovers' quarrel with the delicacy of a marriage counselor who also has a Guiding Bolt prepared.

> "It's not resistant to swords."
> "This is turning into the 12 days of Christmas."
> "I'm amused by the Jorlan-Ilvara drama, but I think I'm still going to go ahead and attack them."

### Daz (Mike Hall)
- Confident under fire — dismisses incoming crossbow bolts with bravado before snapping Shield into place. His combat narration carries the swagger of someone who's earned his reputation.
- Refuses to be sidelined or protected, asserting agency even when mercenaries explicitly tell him to stay down. His defiance carries genuine heat.
- Smoothly maintains his cover with Asha Vandree even in the chaos of battle, improvising lies without missing a beat.

> "They look a little too smug, and too sure of themselves, I'm sure they're not a good shot, and they missed."
> "I am not gonna leave. Everyone here... to fight for me without me also participating. I don't give a damn if you get paid. The best way for you to get paid, then, is to continue the fight and prevent all of us from dying."
> "Fuck that lady. Let's kill her."

### Ilvara Mizzrym (GM/Kostadis)
- Even in her final battle, Ilvara is defined by spite and prophetic menace. Her legendary reaction punishes Zalthir's boldest move with magical fear — turning his greatest strength against him. She howls, mutters dark promises about Zuggtmoy's bride, and dies in an explosion of poisonous spores rather than quietly.

> "She mutters something about Zuggtmoy and the bride — she will come and cause all sorts of chaos and mayhem."
> "You are frightened. Which is very convenient, because you cannot now approach her."

### Kaelira & Nym Duskryn (GM/Kostadis)
- Kaelira is urgent, exasperated, professional — a mercenary who treats Daz's survival as a job and his heroism as an inconvenience. Nym is cutting and sardonic, dismissive of everyone around her, especially Jorlan.
- Together they represent a drow dynamic entirely different from Ilvara's fanaticism: transactional, sharp-tongued, competent, and deeply irritated by their client.

> **Kaelira:** "Come with me if you want to live, you idiot."
> **Kaelira:** "If you don't want to live, I'll still take you, but could you… it would be much easier if you'd just come."
> **Nym:** "Can I kill your brother?"

### Asha Vandree (GM/Kostadis)
- Asha remains a zealot even in the aftermath of battle — her first reaction to seeing unknown drow is to scream about traitors to Lolth, and her response to Ilvara's death is to drop to her knees and pray. She is unwavering, which makes her both useful and dangerous.

> "Who are you? You must be traitors! Traitors to Lolth!"
> "The evil! The undead!"

### House T'sarran Drow (GM/Kostadis)
- The T'sarran drow are a comedic counterpoint to the battle's intensity — bickering about tactics, blaming each other for poor spell preparation, and debating whether to walk through an insect plague. Their elite warrior's parting threat lands with the energy of someone saving face rather than promising vengeance.

> **Elite Warrior:** "And you don't have a Dispel Magic."
> **Mage:** "No, I don't have a Dispel..."

## Standout Moments

- **Zalthir's aerial assault on Ilvara** — grapples her mid-air, scores two critical hits during Flurry of Blows, drops her 40 feet, and then is punished by her legendary reaction with magical fear. The sequence perfectly captures Zalthir's combat mastery arc hitting its ceiling: he executes the most spectacular attack of the session, only to be rendered helpless by the one thing his fists can't solve. His quiet "Well, that didn't work" and later honest engagement with the frightened condition ("I am supposed to be trying to not be near her") show genuine philosophical growth over rules exploitation.

- **Daz refuses extraction** — When Kaelira physically grabs him and orders him to flee, Daz delivers the session's most character-defining speech: "I am not gonna leave. Everyone here... to fight for me without me also participating. I don't give a damn if you get paid." This is the culmination of his Leadership/Purpose arc — the detached intellectual who once manipulated from the shadows now refuses to let others fight in his place.

- **Thorin one-shots the heart fungus** — The Big Heart Fungus, which the GM spent considerable time preparing, is obliterated in a single round by Dawnbringer's radiant damage. Thorin's modest "I do what I can" perfectly captures his character: devastating when pointed at the right target, humble about it afterward.

- **Grygum kills Ilvara with Guiding Bolt** — The party's mortal enemy since Session 1 is destroyed not by the monk's aerial assault or the wizard's fireball, but by the cleric's steady, patient radiant bolt from range. Ilvara explodes in poisonous spores, and Grygum's first reaction is practical concern: "How big is the cloud? I'm glad I was standing back." The pragmatic wisdom arc in its purest form.

- **Daz smoothly covers for the Duskryn mercenaries** — When Asha sees the unknown drow and screams about traitors to Lolth, Daz improvises without hesitation: "No, these are friends of Lolth, and they're here to help." His deception mastery is so reflexive it's almost indistinguishable from honesty.

- **Thorin rolls a die to decide his own moral choice** — Unable to decide whether to stop Jorlan from chasing Ilvara, Thorin literally rolls for it: "Alright, so 8 says I do the sensible thing." This is Thorin's moral compass arc made mechanical — he wants to do the right thing but can't always tell what it is, so he trusts fate.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**Key NPC Speech Patterns to Maintain:**
- **Kaelira Duskryn:** Direct, exasperated professionalism. Uses insults as terms of endearment. Terminator references are canon to her characterization. She treats Daz's survival as a contractual obligation, not a personal concern.
- **Nym Duskryn:** Cutting, sardonic, dismissive. Refers to Jorlan as "the dumber one." Casually suggests killing allies. Has a transactional worldview and zero patience.
- **Asha Vandree:** Religious zealot energy — drops to her knees to pray mid-battlefield. Shouts accusations of betrayal. Still fully believes Daz is a Lolth convert. Her faith is genuine and loud.
- **House T'sarran Drow:** Bickering corporate middle-management energy. They blame each other, threaten to report to the matron mother, and retreat with hollow threats. Play them as competent fighters with incompetent coordination.

**PC Emotional States and Relationship Notes:**
- **Zalthir** was at his most vulnerable this session — the frightened condition forced him to sit out the climactic kill after executing the session's most impressive combat sequence. Future encounters should note that magical fear is the one thing that genuinely sidelines him, and he engages with it honestly rather than looking for loopholes.
- **Daz** has crossed a threshold — he will not be extracted or protected. NPCs who try to sideline him for his own safety should expect fierce resistance. His cover with Asha is intact but increasingly strained by the presence of the Duskryn mercenaries.
- **Thorin** spent much of this battle chasing the action across a sprawling map. He's most satisfied when he can engage directly and decisively. Give him targets he can reach.
- **Grygum** continues to be the party's emotional and tactical anchor. His concern for Glabbagool was immediate and genuine. NPCs should recognize him as the steady presence — the one who follows up, who asks about collateral damage, who offers healing even to former enemies' minions.

**Promises, Threats, and Vows to Reference:**
- **House T'sarran's parting threat:** "We will meet again. Enjoy your victory for the moment." — This retreat should have consequences; the matron mother will indeed hear about the failed ambush.
- **Ilvara's dying prophecy:** She muttered about "Zuggtmoy and the bride" causing chaos and mayhem before dying. This should seed future Zuggtmoy plot developments.
- **Daz's vow to fight alongside his allies:** His refusal to be extracted is now a defining character moment that NPCs who know him should reference or test.
- **Asha's intact deception:** She still believes Daz is a Lolth convert and the Duskryn drow are "friends of Lolth." This lie is now load-bearing and will eventually collapse.
- **Ilvara's promised magical items to Asha:** Per the existing deal with Asha Vandree, Ilvara's gear was promised as payment. With Ilvara dead and exploded into spores, the status of this payment needs resolution.
