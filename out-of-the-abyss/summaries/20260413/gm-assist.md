# Chapter 48  The Ember Grapple

Date: Apr 14th, 2026

## Summary
Deep beneath the surface world, in the lightless corridors of the Underdark, the Ember Vanguard paused to take stock of how far they had come. The trials they had endured had forged them into something harder and more capable than before, and each member of the group felt the weight of that growth settle into their bones. Thorin wrestled with a nagging vulnerability — his inability to strike at enemies from a distance had cost the party in the past, and he knew the creatures lurking in the dark ahead would not always close the gap for him. After much deliberation, he committed to mastering the art of the thrown javelin, training himself to hurl it with devastating precision across great distances, cutting through cover and slowing the retreat of fleeing foes.

Daz, ever the scholar of arcane possibility, turned his attention to the spellbook the party had recovered in an earlier foray, its pages brimming with knowledge he had not yet had time to absorb. He had long carried a gift for bending the fabric of space, slipping through shadows in an instant, but now he found a way to do so without drawing on his deepest reserves of power, preserving his strength for the battles that truly demanded it. The discovery opened new doors in his mind, and he filled the space left behind with spells drawn from the recovered tome, expanding his arsenal in ways that made him quietly confident about what lay ahead.

It was Zalthir, however, who stumbled upon the idea that sent a ripple of excitement through the group. Rummaging through the possibilities available to him, he realized that a length of rope — humble, mundane, utterly unglamorous — could become one of the most fearsome tools in his arsenal. He had always excelled at seizing enemies and holding them fast, but now he could bind them completely, leaving them helpless while his companions brought ruin down upon them. The moment the rest of the Ember Vanguard understood what this meant alongside Daz's ability to conjure a swirling storm of conjured blades over a fixed point, the strategy practically wrote itself — Zalthir would pin the enemy in place, and Daz would make them regret staying there. Someone in the group laughed and suggested they rename themselves the Ember Grapple, and no one entirely disagreed.

## Scenes
### The Level Eight Milestone
#### The party reaches a significant milestone in their journey through the Underdark, gaining new powers and specialized abilities.
- The party is informed they have reached level eight, triggering a period of reflection and growth.
- Thorin considers whether to address his lack of ranged capabilities or double down on his current specializations.
- The characters begin discussing the importance of specialization at higher levels, noting that damage output must scale significantly to keep pace with increasingly powerful foes.
- The group discusses the lack of ranged weapons in the party and how it might be a disadvantage in future encounters.

### Choosing Feats and Abilities
#### The party members deliberate over their feat selections, weighing specialized combat options against general ability score improvements.
- Daz considers the Warcaster feat to improve concentration on spells, and explores the Fey Touched feat to gain additional prepared spells and free castings.
- Daz notes that Dawnbringer deals radiant damage rather than slashing damage, which influences his tactical thinking.
- Daz selects the Fey Touched feat, which allows him to cast certain spells without expending spell slots and opens up additional spell slots for new spells from the found spellbook.
- Thorin considers the Speedy feat to increase his movement speed and mobility across difficult terrain, noting that closing distance quickly is critical given his limited ranged options.
- Zalthir discovers the Street Justice feat, which allows him to use rope to restrain enemies he has grappled, and becomes excited about its synergy with his grappling style.
- The group discusses the 'Gabe theory of micro-optimization,' debating whether to increase base ability scores or rely on magical items like potions of giant strength.
- Thorin, guided by an AI suggestion, settles on the Sharpshooter feat to improve his effectiveness with javelins at long range, with the DM allowing a house rule to apply the stat bonus to strength instead of dexterity.

### Tactical Synergies and Final Decisions
#### The party finalizes their level-up choices and discusses new combat synergies, particularly around grappling and area-of-effect spells.
- Daz and Zalthir coordinate a strategy to use the Cloud of Daggers spell on enemies held in place by a grapple, ensuring continuous damage.
- The group clarifies that a grapple does not place the grappler in the same five-foot space as the target, meaning area-of-effect spells can be placed on the grappled enemy without harming the grappler.
- The party discusses the limits of Cloud of Daggers, confirming that a creature can only take the damage once per turn.
- Zalthir works through the action economy of using rope in combat — using his bonus action to grapple and his action to bind the target with rope, consuming his full turn.
- The group jokingly considers renaming themselves the 'Ember Grapple' due to their newfound focus on restraining tactics.
- Thorin plans to acquire javelins at the next opportunity to take advantage of his new Sharpshooter training.
- The DM confirms the party will not receive a long rest at this time, as no combats are imminent.

## Locations
### Underdark
A vast subterranean realm where the party is currently traveling. The DM notes that while flying enemies are less common here, the party's lack of ranged weapons will still be tested.

## Items
### Dawnbringer
A magical weapon carried by one of the party members that deals radiant damage rather than standard slashing damage, a distinction that influenced tactical discussions during the level-up session.

### Spellbook
A spellbook found by the party in a previous session. Its contents opened up additional spell options for Daz, and the Fey Touched feat allowed him to free up prepared spell slots to learn more spells from it.

### Potions of Giant Strength
Magical elixirs discussed as an alternative to permanently increasing a character's strength score, cited as an example of the 'Gabe theory of micro-optimization' — using consumable items rather than investing feat slots in stat improvements.

### Javelins
Simple thrown weapons that Thorin plans to acquire and use for ranged combat. With his new Sharpshooter training, he can hurl them at long distances without disadvantage, ignore cover, and slow struck enemies' movement speed.

### Rope
A standard piece of adventuring gear that Zalthir intends to use in combat to bind and restrain opponents once they have been grappled, enabled by his newly selected Street Justice feat.

## Spells
### Misty Step
A teleportation spell that Daz already had access to but gains the ability to cast freely through the Fey Touched feat without expending a spell slot. This provides a reliable way to escape grapples or reposition during battle without provoking opportunity attacks.

### Cloud of Daggers
A spell that conjures a localized cube of spinning daggers. Daz plans to use this in tandem with Zalthir's grappling to trap enemies within the hazardous area, dealing continuous damage each turn. The party confirms a creature can only take the damage once per turn.

### Spiritual Guardians
A protective spell referenced during a discussion about damage mechanics and the importance of following established rules regarding how often a creature can be harmed by an effect in a single turn, used as an example of a spell that could be misused as an infinite damage source.
