## Standout Moments

- **Daz claims Fey Touched**, strategically freeing up prepared spell slots by converting previously-learned spells into always-available ones — a quiet optimization move that reflects the character's scholarly resourcefulness. Mike's excitement was palpable: *"That was pretty cool. I did the Fey Touch thing, and I had already chosen the two spells that the Fae Touch gives me for free, so I was able to delete those from my Learn spellbook and learn additional ones."*

- **Zalthir takes Street Justice**, completely abandoning his planned +2 DEX in a moment of tactical revelation. The feat enables him to restrain enemies with rope after grappling — giving allies advantage on attacks against his targets and locking down threats for Daz's area spells. Kostadis immediately recognized it would reshape his encounter design: *"I'm gonna be trying to figure out ways to circumvent it for the rest of this campaign."*

- **Thorin takes Sharpshooter (with a house-ruled STR bump)**, finally addressing his zero-range-capability problem with javelins — a weapon that fits his giant-raised fighting style. The Slow mastery on javelins also lets him reduce fleeing enemies' speed by 10 feet, solving the kiting problem that has plagued him. *"If something's trying to kite us, I can try and keep up with it."*

- **The Ember Grapple doctrine is born.** The session's defining tactical discovery: Zalthir grapples and restrains a target, Daz drops Cloud of Daggers (or Fireball) on the pinned enemy using Sculpt Spells to protect Zalthir, and Thorin closes distance with his new speed boost or softens targets with javelins from range. The party has evolved from a collection of survivors into a coordinated tactical unit with interlocking capabilities.

- **Joe's honest self-assessment** — *"Dude, I suck at this, I'm not Gabe"* — followed by his willingness to consult AI, ask the table for help, and ultimately land on a feat that elegantly addresses his character's biggest weakness. A moment of genuine table camaraderie.

## Voice Keeper Notes

- **Party tactical identity has shifted.** The "Ember Grapple" combo (Zalthir grapple/restrain → Daz area damage → Thorin javelin/melee cleanup) should be referenced by NPCs who witness or hear about their fights going forward. Their reputation should evolve from "dangerous escapees" to "terrifyingly coordinated."

- **Kostadis has openly declared he will design encounters to counter the grapple-and-burn combo.** Future enemies should occasionally have features that punish or circumvent grappling (freedom of movement, teleportation, auras that damage grapplers, swarm-type enemies that can't be meaningfully grappled).

- **Thorin now has ranged capability (javelins, 120 ft with Sharpshooter).** This changes his tactical role — he is no longer purely a close-range fighter. NPCs and enemies who previously exploited his lack of range should find that gap closed.

- **Daz has Fey Touched (Misty Step free cast).** This makes him significantly harder to pin down. Enemies who rely on grappling or cornering the wizard will need new approaches.

- **Zalthir's Street Justice feat** gives restrained condition on rope-tied targets — allies get advantage on attacks against them. This should be reflected in how combat flows: restrained targets should react with desperation, and intelligent enemies who've seen it should prioritize breaking Zalthir's concentration or targeting him first.

- **House rule established:** Sharpshooter feat's +1 ASI can be applied to Strength instead of Dexterity (granted to Thorin). This should be noted for consistency if similar requests arise.
