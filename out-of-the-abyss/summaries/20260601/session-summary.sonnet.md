# Chapter 54: The Case of the Missing Tickles

Date: Jun 1st, 2026

## Summary
The party gathered in the chamber of Janussi, the Keeper of the Tome, to investigate his murder. They found his body lying on its back, his rib cage smashed by what appeared to be an axe, and his heart grotesquely removed from his chest. The blood, however, was strangely localized around the wound — far too contained for a living man — leading the party to conclude that the heart had been torn out after death, almost certainly to prevent anyone from resurrecting him through magical means. As Grygum observed, whoever removed the heart was not in a rush and was not trying to kill anyone — they were methodically desecrating a corpse that was already still.

Daz conducted a meticulous examination of the body, uncovering clotted blood beneath the victim's nose and smeared ink stains along the right sleeve of his tunic. More troubling still was a dark, blackened substance found on the tip of Janussi's left forefinger, his lower lip, and his tongue — a residue distinctly darker than the midnight blue ink he used for his scholarly notes. The party theorized he had been licking his finger to turn pages while reading, inadvertently ingesting whatever poison had been applied to the book. Grygum further noted that Janussi's nose had been fractured and bruised before his death — bruising cannot occur after the heart stops — placing a violent altercation somewhere in the hours between dinner and the actual killing. Glabbagool, the party's intelligent grey ooze companion, was called upon to apply his Underdark experience with strange substances to identify the black residue, but even with the party's encouragement he was unable to determine its nature with certainty.

The chamber itself yielded a wealth of additional clues. A chipped piece of tooth was found a few feet from the body, suggesting Janussi had fallen face-first upon death before being rolled over by whoever removed his heart. Three roughly circular impact wounds had torn through the back of an armchair, scattering its horsehair stuffing — the unmistakable signature of magic missiles fired in anger. A wall-mounted safe had been left open, its contents partially disturbed: one sapphire and one scrap of parchment were missing from among the spell component pouches, yet an enormous fortune in diamonds, platinum, gold, a raven figurine of wondrous power, a potion of supreme healing, and a potion of water breathing had been left completely untouched. Beneath the victim's neck lay broken lead-chain links, the remnants of a locket that had been forcibly torn away. The windows and doors of the chamber showed no signs of damage or forced entry.

On the desk, the party found two sets of notes written in blue ink — one set annotating a comedic book called The Golden Ass, in which Janussi had been recording jokes he found funny, and another set of musings on unfamiliar spells from a volume called The Discoveries, which contained product placement linking to the module author's other works. The notes on The Discoveries were written after the notes on The Golden Ass, making the spell research the last thing Janussi worked on. The Golden Ass itself was nowhere to be found, though Janussi's notes confirmed it had been in his hands recently. Tucked in the wastebasket was a gift tag written in a cramped, sour hand: "To Janussi, read it and weep, you old snob, a proper Dead Winter gift, your loving rival." The heavy scent of cherry tobacco that filled the room — Janussi apparently loved the smell and kept his chamber perpetually fragrant with it — would have been more than sufficient to mask the smell of any poison, a point Zalthir was quick to raise.

Their assistant Fembris Lancer identified Daral Yashenti as Janussi's most bitter professional rival — the same man who had dismissively called the party "sell swords" upon their arrival. The party then descended to the apartment of Hollypocket, the elderly gnome housekeeper, who received them with herbal tea and a flood of carefully observed gossip. She recounted a day of explosive confrontations: Kalan Strongbranch had stormed out of Janussi's office with a face like thunder, nearly knocking the tray from her hands; Teles Ahvoste had screamed the word "hypocrite" before slamming the door around the ninth bell; and Daral Yashenti had visited twice — once around the eleventh bell while nursing a hangover, shouting that Janussi was "not fit to be Candlekeep's Keeper" and "nothing but a glorified book peddler," and again late at night, drunk and pounding on the door, before fleeing down the stairs in a panic. Most chillingly, Hollypocket described a violent confrontation with the "devil woman" — not an epithet but a professional description, as Sylvira Savikas is the tiefling scholar of demons and devils — who had screamed that Janussi was a traitor before nearly tearing the door from its hinges. And in the small hours of the morning, Hollypocket claimed to have seen Sylvira's face glowing red in the candlelight, moving downward through the tower — carrying a lantern, despite the fact that tieflings have no need of one in the dark. Hollypocket also revealed that Sylvira and Janussi had shared a long, explosive on-again, off-again romantic relationship, with breakups and hookups both equally dramatic.

The party then turned to an unlikely witness: Queenie, the tower's resident cat, a five-year-old female with strong opinions about tickling and the hygiene of two-legged creatures. Daz used the Stonespeaker Crystal to speak with her directly, having first earned the cat's grudging cooperation with a tickle. Speaking in feline terms of cycles and naps rather than hours and clocks, Queenie revealed that Sylvira had blasted an armchair with magic missiles during what appeared to be a furious lover's quarrel with Janussi earlier in the day. Queenie also confirmed that Daral had fled the chamber in a panic, clutching what she described as a "hypnotic block" — almost certainly the missing book, as Queenie noted it was the kind of thing that made two-leggers sit and stare without offering any tickles. Most unsettling of all, the cat noted that the figure she had seen leaving the tower in the night looked like Sylvira but did not smell like her, suggesting that whoever had descended those stairs had been wearing a magical disguise. The interrogation concluded when Queenie, offended by the general hygiene standards of the party, dismissed them and walked away.

With the interviews concluded, the shape of the mystery had grown clearer — and more sinister. The locket torn from Janussi's neck was believed to contain one of the two keys to the Hightower Library, the other being held by the gate warden Kalan Strongbranch — who had himself been seen storming out of a furious meeting with the victim. Fembris confirmed Janussi had always kept his key on his person. Someone had gone to extraordinary lengths to kill Janussi, steal the book and the key, and leave behind a fortune in treasure without a second glance — suggesting the motive was never wealth, but something far more specific and dangerous. The party now had a list of suspects, a trail of bitter rivalries, and the unsettling knowledge that someone in Candlekeep was not who they appeared to be. The next phase of the investigation would require direct interviews with the suspects themselves.

#### The party examines the remains of the Keeper of the Tome, Janussi, to uncover the circumstances of his death.

- Grygum notes the victim's rib cage was smashed by an axe-like weapon, though the blood splatter is strangely localized, suggesting the heart was removed after death when there was no longer blood pressure to send spurts across the room.
- The DM notes that whoever removed the heart "was not in a rush" and "wasn't trying to kill somebody, but was dealing with a corpse."
- Grygum observes the victim also has a fractured and bruised nose — an injury that stands out sharply because keepers in a library do not typically get into fights.
- The party rolls investigation checks. Ben rolls a 5, Gabe rolls a 2, but Daz rolls a 22, becoming the lead investigator for the body examination.

## Locations

### Candlekeep
The world's largest library and a fortress of knowledge where the murder investigation is taking place. The DM notes that Candlekeep emerged around the prophecies of the seer Alaundo, whose prophecies are repeated continuously until they are fulfilled — one of which, "on the longest night, the keeper falls," appears directly relevant to Janussi's death on the winter solstice.

### Janussi's Chamber
A stone tower room filled with bookshelves, a desk covered in research notes, a wall-embedded safe, and a silver pedestal for tobacco pipes. It is the site of the murder and the primary location of the party's investigation, filled with the scent of cherry tobacco — a scent Janussi deliberately cultivated, making periodic use of Cure Disease to manage the health consequences. The windows and doors show no signs of damage or forced entry. A fallen marble statue, an armchair riddled with magic missile impacts, and an open safe with a partially disturbed fortune round out the scene.

### Hollypocket's Apartment
A modest but spacious and comfortable apartment located directly below the Keeper of the Tome's chamber in Candlekeep, where the housekeeper Hollypocket lives and serves herbal tea to the party during their interview. Its position directly below Janussi's chamber meant Hollypocket could hear shouting matches, thuds, and footsteps on the stairs throughout the fateful evening.

### Hightower Library
A restricted area of Candlekeep that requires two special keys to enter. One of these keys was kept in a locket worn by Janussi and was stolen during the murder; the other is held by the gate warden Kalan Strongbranch. The party speculates, drawing on Thorin's "launch the nukes" analogy, that both keys may need to be used together — like a two-key nuclear launch system.

## Items

### Keeper of the Tome's Vestments
The blood-covered robes of the victim, featuring strangely localized bloodstains around the wound and smears of midnight blue ink on the right sleeve, consistent with recent note-taking.

### Blackened Residue
A mysterious dark substance found on the Keeper's left forefinger, the center of his lower lip, and the tip of his tongue. It is definitively darker than the blue ink used for his notes and is suspected to be a poison he ingested while licking his finger to turn pages. Glabbagool was unable to identify it. The party notes it can be researched in the library's collection of books on poisons.

A finely carved pipe resting on a silver pedestal in Janussi's chamber, used to smoke cherry-flavored tobacco. The DM confirms the heavy tobacco scent was a deliberate feature of Janussi's room — he kept it perpetually fragrant — and that this would have been more than sufficient to mask the smell of any poison. Janussi reportedly managed the health consequences with periodic Cure Disease spells.

A two-foot metal cube embedded in the tower wall, found open and undamaged — the lock was not visibly tampered with. It contained scattered sapphires, spell component pouches labeled with book names (including pouches labeled "Ring of Protection" and "of minute seeing"), and a large amount of untouched treasure: a raven figurine of wondrous power, a potion of supreme healing, a potion of water breathing, 5 large diamonds worth 5,000 GP each, a bag of 1,000 platinum pieces, and a bag of 1,000 gold pieces. One sapphire and one parchment were missing. Four additional closed pouches each containing a large sapphire and a book-named parchment remain inside.

### Marble Statue
A three-foot-tall statue of a hooded, bearded man holding a candle aloft in his right hand, found toppled on the floor near the armchairs. It was reportedly knocked over by A'lai Aivenmore when he discovered the body at dawn. Thorin's suggestion that the candle might be significant caused Tadric to spend the rest of the scene eyeing the statue anxiously.

### Broken Lead-Chain Links
Small metallic links found beneath the victim's neck, indicating a chain was snapped and a locket forcibly removed from the body. The DM confirms these are among the items definitively missing from the scene, along with the sapphire, the parchment, and the contents of the locket.

### The Discoveries
A spellbook found on Janussi's desk containing his musings and notes on unfamiliar spells and how they might be cast — the last thing Janussi was annotating before his death, written after the notes on The Golden Ass. The DM notes it contains in-world product placement linking to the module author's other published adventures.

### The Golden Ass
A comedic book that Janussi was reading and taking jokes from shortly before his death. It is currently missing from the scene. The gift tag in the wastebasket suggests it was given to him as a bitter Dead Winter gift by his "loving rival." Queenie the cat confirmed that Daral fled the chamber carrying what she described as a "hypnotic block" — a book-shaped object that made two-leggers sit and stare without tickling.

### Gift Tag
A scrap of parchment found in the wastebasket near the desk, written in what the DM describes as a "cramped, sour hand" — suggesting a resentful author. It reads verbatim: "To Janussi, read it and weep, you old snob, a proper Dead Winter gift, your loving rival." The party interprets this as confirmation the missing book was given as a pointed, bitter gift.

### Janussi's Locket
A piece of jewelry Janussi reportedly never removed, now missing and leaving only broken lead-chain links behind. It is believed to have contained one of the two keys to the Hightower Library. Both Hollypocket and Fembris confirm Janussi was never without it.

### Stonespeaker Crystal
A magical artifact carried by Daz that grants the ability to communicate with animals for up to 10 minutes, with two charges per long rest. It was used to interview Queenie the cat, to the surprise of the other party members who had been planning to purchase a potion of animal speaking.

### Lantern
A light source allegedly carried by the figure resembling Sylvira Savikas when spotted by Hollypocket moving downward through the tower in the small hours of the morning — suspicious because Sylvira is a tiefling with darkvision and would have no need for one. The party connects this detail immediately to Queenie's report that the figure smelled wrong.

## Spells

### Raise Dead
A resurrection spell referenced as the likely reason the killer removed Janussi's heart after death — the spell cannot function if a vital organ is missing. The DM notes that this is not an obscure countermeasure: "Anyone who knows anything about Raised Dead knows that you steal the heart." The act suggests the killer was knowledgeable about resurrection magic and specifically wanted to prevent Janussi from being questioned by the living.

### Locate Object
Used by the party to search the immediate area for the chipped piece of Janussi's front tooth, which was found a few feet from his face on the chamber floor. This confirmed that Janussi had fallen forward upon death before being rolled over by whoever removed his heart.

### Magic Missile
Identified by Daz as the cause of three roughly circular impact wounds on an armchair in Janussi's chamber — two of the impacts punched clean holes through the chair back, scattering horsehair stuffing. The cat later confirmed Sylvira Savikas cast this spell during a furious confrontation with Janussi earlier in the day. The DM notes the missiles were aimed at the chair, not the victim: "If it was him, he would have the signs of injury" — and magic missiles do not miss.

### Speak with Animals
Used by Daz via the Stonespeaker Crystal to communicate with Queenie the cat, allowing the party to gather crucial eyewitness testimony about the visitors to Janussi's chamber on the night of the murder. Daz rolls 26 on investigation to parse Queenie's feline frame of reference and translate her account into a usable timeline.

### Cure Disease
Referenced by the DM as the spell Janussi used periodically to manage the health consequences of keeping his chamber perpetually filled with tobacco smoke. Noted with some humor: "Cure Disease is a spell, you know, periodically has to get treatment for his lung cancer — not a big deal, kind of thing."

## NPCs

### Janussi
The deceased Keeper of the Tome and the murder victim. A scholarly man described by Hollypocket as "a little stiff, maybe, but such a nice man." He had a sense of humor — his notes from The Golden Ass recorded jokes he found funny — and enjoyed cherry tobacco to the point of filling his chamber with its scent. He was researching unfamiliar spells in The Discoveries and had a volatile, explosive on-again, off-again romantic relationship with Sylvira Savikas. He held one of the two keys to the Hightower Library in a locket he never removed from his person.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent grey ooze accompanying the party who was called upon to identify the mysterious black substance on the victim's fingers and tongue, on the grounds that Underdark oozes have encountered all manner of dangerous materials. Despite the party's encouragement and a rerolled intelligence check, he was unable to determine the residue's nature, though the party gained the impression it was connected to the cause of death.

### Tadric
A guard or attendant present at the crime scene who becomes increasingly anxious about the fallen marble statue after Thorin raises the possibility of it coming to life. He explains that the statue was knocked over by A'lai Aivenmore when the body was discovered at dawn. After Thorin's comment about the candle, Tadric cannot stop staring at the statue, prompting Thorin to reassure him — with the caveat "assuming you're not guilty."

### A'lai Aivenmore
A Great Reader identified as the one who discovered Janussi's body at dawn and reportedly knocked over the marble statue in shock during that discovery.

### Fembris Lancer
An acolyte assistant assigned to the party by the Bookwyrm, who provides local knowledge of Candlekeep's inhabitants and rivalries. The DM reminds the party that Fembris also functions as the Bookwyrm's informant — everything shared with him will be repeated. He identifies Daral Yashenti as Janussi's primary rival, confirms that Janussi held one of the two Hightower Library keys in his locket, and notes that the other key is held by Kalan Strongbranch. He is eager for information and attempts to extract details from the party as they leave the chamber.

### Daral Yashenti
A rival scholar and Great Reader with a long-standing, bitter animosity toward Janussi — described by Fembris as having been "at each other's throats for years." He is the same man who dismissively called the party "sell swords" on their arrival. He visited Janussi twice on the final evening: once around the eleventh bell while nursing a hangover, shouting that Janussi was "not fit to be Candlekeep's Keeper" and "nothing but a glorified book peddler"; and again late at night, drunk and pounding on the door, before fleeing the stairs in a panic. The gift tag's signature — "your loving rival" — points strongly toward him. Queenie confirms he fled the chamber carrying what she called a "hypnotic block," almost certainly the missing book, The Golden Ass.

### Hollypocket
An elderly gnome housekeeper responsible for Janussi's daily needs — morning tea and pastry, and emptying his chamber pot. She lives directly below Janussi's chamber and heard every shouting match and thud through the evening. She provides the party with a detailed, enthusiastic account of the day's confrontations while maintaining a pretense of disliking gossip. She confirms she was awake until 11pm listening for Janussi's usual sounds before going to sleep, then woke to a thud she believes was Janussi dying. She reports seeing Sylvira's face glowing red in the candlelight around 2 or 3am, moving downstairs carrying a lantern.

### Kalan Strongbranch
A Great Reader who was seen leaving Janussi's office with "a face like thunder" after a morning meeting, nearly knocking Hollypocket's tray from her hands. He also holds the second key to the Hightower Library, making him doubly significant to the investigation.

### Teles Ahvoste
A Great Reader who engaged in a shouting match with Janussi around the ninth bell, calling him a "HYPOCRITE" before storming out of the chamber.

### Sylvira Savikas
A tiefling scholar of demons and devils — her title of "devil woman" is a professional description, not an epithet — who had a long, volatile on-again, off-again romantic relationship with Janussi. She was heard screaming that Janussi was "a traitor" during a violent confrontation earlier in the day, and was seen by Hollypocket in the small hours of the morning carrying a lantern despite having darkvision as a tiefling. Queenie the cat confirmed that during the day Sylvira had blasted an armchair with magic missiles during what appeared to be a lover's quarrel with Janussi. Most critically, Queenie noted that a figure leaving the tower in the night looked like Sylvira but did not smell like her — suggesting either a shapeshifter or a magical disguise.

### Fheminor Scrivenbark
Described by Queenie as "the short-legged one." He visited Janussi's chamber in the evening around 9pm and left after 15 to 20 minutes, then returned drunk around 10:30pm, pounding on the door until admitted, arguing loudly, and then fleeing down the stairs in a panic — the last confirmed visitor before the midnight thud. Hollypocket also observed this second visit and noted she had never seen him run so fast.

### Queenie
A five-year-old female cat with "sleet goose hair" residing in the tower. She has strong opinions about tickling, two-legged hygiene ("I've met cleaner dogs in my time"), and the proper use of visits to her tower. She communicated through Daz via the Stonespeaker Crystal and provided crucial testimony: Sylvira's magic missile outburst during a daytime quarrel, Daral fleeing with a "hypnotic block," and a figure that looked like Sylvira but smelled wrong leaving the tower at night. She concluded the interview by dismissing the party and demanding they lick themselves properly before returning.
