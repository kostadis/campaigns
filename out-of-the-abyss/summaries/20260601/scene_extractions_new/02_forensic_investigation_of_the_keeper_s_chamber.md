# Forensic Investigation of the Keeper's Chamber

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party conducts a detailed forensic examination of the crime scene, uncovering clues about the victim's final moments and the nature of the attack.
- The party discovers a blackened substance on the victim's tongue and a recently chipped front tooth; the chipped piece is found a few feet from his face, suggesting he fell forward upon death.
- Medical examination reveals the victim's nose was fractured and bruised prior to death, indicating a struggle or assault earlier in the evening, as bruising does not occur after death.
- The party deduces that the victim fell face-first, chipped his tooth, and was then rolled over by whoever removed his heart.
- Investigators find broken lead-chain links and an impression on the victim's neck, suggesting a locket or necklace was forcibly removed.
- A search of the room reveals a silver pedestal with a finely carved ebony wood pipe and cherry-flavored tobacco, which may have been used to mask the scent of poison.
- The party examines a wall-mounted safety deposit box that was left open, containing scattered sapphires and spell component pouches, though a large amount of treasure — including diamonds, platinum, and gold — was left untouched.
- Daz identifies impact marks on an armchair as the result of magic missiles, with two holes punched through the chair back and horsehair stuffing scattered about.
- Tadric explains that a fallen three-foot marble statue of a hooded, bearded man holding a candle was likely knocked over by the Great Reader A'lai Aivenmore, who discovered the body at dawn.
- The party notes the statue’s placement near the armchairs and tea table, suggesting a struggle or disturbance occurred in that area.
- The party confirms the safe was undamaged and unlocked — no forced entry, implying the killer had access or knowledge of the combination.
- The party observes the presence of four closed pouches in the safe, each with a large sapphire and parchment, matching the names of spells — one sapphire and one parchment are missing from the open group.
- Joe Beda (as Thorin) asks if he can shake the candle on the statue, prompting Tadric to fixate on it nervously: "Try not to conspicuously look at the candle, but, like, stares at it."
