# The Housekeeper's Testimony

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party interviews Hollypocket, the gnome housekeeper, to gather information about the events leading up to Janussi's death.
- The party visits Hollypocket in her modest apartment below the Keeper's chamber, where she offers them herbal tea.
- Hollypocket recounts a series of explosive one-on-one meetings Janussi had with various Great Readers throughout the day, noting that at least half ended in shouting matches.
- She describes seeing Kalan Strongbranch leaving Janussi's office with a face like thunder, nearly knocking the tray from her hands.
- She recounts Teles Ahvoste visiting around the ninth bell, with the meeting descending into shouting, including Teles yelling "hypocrite!" before storming out.
- She describes Daral Yashenti visiting around the eleventh bell while hungover, shouting that Janussi was "not fit to be Candlekeep's Keeper" and calling him "nothing but a glorified book peddler."
- Daral returned later that night, drunk, pounding on Janussi's door until admitted, then having another loud argument before fleeing down the stairs.
- Hollypocket describes a violent confrontation between Janussi and Sylvira Savikas, whom she calls the "devil woman," during which Sylvira screamed that Janussi was a traitor and the two seemed to act violently before Sylvira stormed out.
- The party clarifies that "devil woman" is not an insult but a job title — Sylvira is the official Curator of the Infernal Fortress.
- The housekeeper reports hearing a loud thud from the chamber above shortly after the midnight bells, but did not investigate as Janussi did not like to be disturbed after eleven.
- Hollypocket claims to have seen a vision of Sylvira Savikas's face glowing red in the candlelight around two or three in the morning, going downward — notable because Sylvira is a tiefling with darkvision and would have no need for a lantern.
- She adds: "I was never sure but what the keeper saw in her, but he certainly incurred her wrath that morning."
- The party learns that Janussi always wore a locket on his person — "He carried it, he carried it all, all the time. He never took it off."
