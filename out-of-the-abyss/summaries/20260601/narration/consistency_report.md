## ISSUE 1: Spell Used to Find the Chipped Tooth

**Location:** Spells — *Locate Object*

**Issue:** The recap states *Locate Object* was used to search for the chipped piece of Janussi's front tooth. *Locate Object* is a concentration spell requiring the caster to have seen the object before or to describe it. A tooth fragment that the party did not know existed beforehand cannot be the target of *Locate Object*. More importantly, no character on the party sheet has *Locate Object* listed as a known or prepared spell. Grygum (Life Cleric 8) could prepare it, but it isn't noted. Daz (Evoker 8) does not have it in his documented spellbook.

**Evidence:** Party document lists all character sheets; *Locate Object* appears nowhere. The tooth was an unknown object, making the spell's use mechanically questionable regardless.

**Suggested Fix:** Remove the spell reference entirely. The tooth was found through a standard Investigation or Perception check during the physical search of the room. No spell was required or used.

## ISSUE 2: Stonespeaker Crystal — Speak with Animals Attribution

**Location:** Scenes — *Interrogating the Tower Cat* / Items — *Stonespeaker Crystal* / Spells — *Speak with Animals*

**Issue:** The recap repeatedly describes the Stonespeaker Crystal as granting *Speak with Animals*, and the Items section states it grants "Speak with Animals, Dead, and Plants." The scene and spell descriptions then conflate this with the *Speak with Animals* spell. These are distinct mechanics. More importantly, the recap says "Zalthir and the others spoke through him" — implying Daz channeled the effect and the rest of the party communicated through Daz as a relay. This is not how *Speak with Animals* or a Stonespeaker Crystal functions. The crystal user speaks directly; others can listen but do not "speak through" the attuned user in any standard mechanical sense.

**Evidence:** Party document: Stonespeaker Crystal is attuned to **Daz** and listed under his items. The description in the Items section says it grants Speak with Animals, Dead, and Plants — this is consistent.

**Suggested Fix:** Clarify in the recap that Daz communicated directly with Queenie via the crystal, and the party relayed questions *to Daz* who then asked them. Remove the phrase "Zalthir and the others spoke through him" or rephrase to "Zalthir and the others directed their questions through Daz." The Spells section entry for *Speak with Animals* should note this was a crystal effect, not a prepared spell.

## ISSUE 8: Raise Dead — Heart Removal as Prevention Method

**Location:** Spells — *Raise Dead* / Summary

**Issue:** The recap states the killer removed the heart "to prevent anyone from resurrecting him through magical means" and that *Raise Dead* "cannot function if a vital organ is missing." *Raise Dead* in 5e requires the body to be "relatively intact" but does not specify that a missing heart prevents casting — this is a DM ruling or in-world lore decision, not RAW. The recap presents it as established fact ("common knowledge among clerics and scholars") which is a claim that should be flagged as a campaign-specific ruling.

**Evidence:** 5e RAW does not explicitly bar *Raise Dead* due to a missing heart; this is a DM decision.

**Suggested Fix:** Add a note that this interpretation is a campaign-specific ruling ("the party confirms this is the case in this world, per their understanding") rather than presenting it as universal mechanical fact. This protects future session continuity if the DM's ruling is ever revisited.
