# Chapter 54 The case of the missing tickles.

Date: Jun 1st, 2026

## Summary
The party gathered in the chamber of Janussi, the Keeper of the Tome, to investigate his murder. They found his body lying on its back, his rib cage smashed by what appeared to be an axe, and his heart grotesquely removed from his chest. The blood, however, was strangely localized around the wound — far too contained for a living man — leading the party to conclude that the heart had been torn out after death, almost certainly to prevent anyone from resurrecting him through magical means.

Daz conducted a meticulous examination of the body, uncovering clotted blood beneath the victim's nose and smeared ink stains along the right sleeve of his tunic. More troubling still was a dark, blackened substance found on the tip of Janussi's left forefinger, his lower lip, and his tongue — a residue distinctly darker than the blue ink he used for his scholarly notes. The party theorized he had been licking his finger to turn pages while reading, inadvertently ingesting whatever poison had been applied to the book. Grygum further noted that Janussi's nose had been fractured and bruised before his death — bruising cannot occur after the heart stops — placing a violent altercation somewhere in the hours before the killing.

The chamber itself yielded a wealth of additional clues. A chipped piece of tooth was found a few feet from the body, suggesting Janussi had fallen face-first upon death before being rolled over by whoever removed his heart. Three roughly circular impact wounds had torn through the back of an armchair, scattering its horsehair stuffing — the unmistakable signature of magic missiles fired in anger. A wall-mounted safe had been left open, its contents partially disturbed: one sapphire and one scrap of parchment were missing from among the spell component pouches, yet an enormous fortune in diamonds, platinum, and gold had been left completely untouched. Beneath the victim's neck lay broken lead-chain links, the remnants of a locket that had been forcibly torn away.

On the desk, the party found two sets of notes written in blue ink — one set annotating a comedic book called *The Golden Ass*, and another set of musings on unfamiliar spells from a volume called *The Discoveries*. The *Golden Ass* itself was nowhere to be found, though Janussi's notes confirmed it had been in his hands recently. Tucked in the wastebasket was a gift tag written in a cramped, bitter hand: "To Janussi, read it and weep, you old snob, a proper Dead Winter gift, your loving rival." The heavy scent of cherry tobacco that filled the room, the party noted, would have been more than sufficient to mask the smell of any poison.

Their assistant Fembris Lancer identified Daral Yashenti as Janussi's most bitter professional rival — the same man who had dismissively called the party "sell swords" upon their arrival. The party then descended to the apartment of Hollypocket, the elderly gnome housekeeper, who received them with herbal tea and a flood of carefully observed gossip. She recounted a day of explosive confrontations: Kalan Strongbranch had stormed out of Janussi's office with a face like thunder; Teles Ahvoste had screamed the word "hypocrite" before slamming the door; and Daral Yashenti had visited twice — once while nursing a hangover, shouting that Janussi was nothing but a glorified book peddler, and again late at night, drunk and pounding on the door, before fleeing down the stairs in a panic. Most chillingly, Hollypocket described a violent confrontation with a woman she called the "devil woman" — the tiefling scholar Sylvira Savikas — who had screamed that Janussi was a traitor before nearly tearing the door from its hinges. And in the small hours of the morning, Hollypocket claimed to have seen Sylvira's face glowing red in the candlelight, moving downward through the tower — carrying a lantern, despite the fact that tieflings have no need of one in the dark.

The party then turned to an unlikely witness: Queenie, the tower's resident cat, a five-year-old female with strong opinions about tickling and the hygiene of two-legged creatures. Speaking with her through magical means, Daz used the Stonespeaker Crystal to communicate with Queenie while Zalthir and the others spoke through him, learning that Sylvira had blasted an armchair with magic missiles during what appeared to be a furious lover's quarrel with Janussi earlier in the day. Queenie also confirmed that Daral had fled the chamber in a panic, clutching what she described as a "hypnotic block" — almost certainly the missing book. Most unsettling of all, the cat noted that the figure she had seen leaving the tower in the night looked like Sylvira but did not smell like her, suggesting that whoever had descended those stairs had been wearing a magical disguise.

With the interviews concluded, the shape of the mystery had grown clearer — and more sinister. The locket torn from Janussi's neck was believed to contain one of the two keys to the Hightower Library, the other being held by the gate warden, Kalan. Someone had gone to extraordinary lengths to kill Janussi, steal the book and the key, and leave behind a fortune in treasure without a second glance — suggesting the motive was never wealth, but something far more specific and dangerous. The party now had a list of suspects, a trail of bitter rivalries, and the unsettling knowledge that someone in Candlekeep was not who they appeared to be.

## Locations
### Candlekeep
The world's largest library and a fortress of knowledge where the murder investigation is taking place.

### Janussi's Chamber
A stone tower room filled with bookshelves, a desk covered in research notes, a wall-embedded safe, and a silver pedestal for tobacco pipes. It is the site of the murder and the primary location of the party's investigation, filled with the scent of cherry tobacco. The room contains a toppled three-foot marble statue of a hooded, bearded man holding a candle aloft, and an armchair riddled with magic missile holes.

### Hollypocket's Apartment
A modest but spacious and comfortable apartment located directly below the Keeper of the Tome's chamber in Candlekeep, where the housekeeper Hollypocket lives and serves tea to the party during their interview. The apartment is tidy, warm, and smells faintly of herbs.

### Hightower Library
A restricted area of Candlekeep that requires two special keys to enter. One of these keys was kept in a locket worn by Janussi and was stolen during the murder; the other is held by the gate warden, Kalan. Access to this library is said to be tied to a prophecy: *“On the longest night, the keeper falls.”*

## Items
### Keeper of the Tome's Vestments
The blood-covered robes of the victim, featuring localized bloodstains and smears of midnight blue ink on the right sleeve, consistent with recent note-taking.

### Blackened Residue
A mysterious dark substance found on the Keeper's left forefinger, lower lip, and tongue. It is definitively darker than the blue ink used for his notes and is suspected to be a poison he ingested while licking his finger to turn pages. Its origin remains unknown.

### Ebony Wood Pipe
A finely carved pipe resting on a silver pedestal in Janussi's chamber, used to smoke cherry-flavored tobacco. The heavy tobacco scent in the room may have been used to mask the smell of poison. The DM confirms Janussi used a magical device to generate the smoke continuously, a habit tied to his lung condition.

### Safety Deposit Box
A two-foot metal cube embedded in the tower wall, found open. It contained scattered sapphires, spell component pouches labeled with book names (including *Ring of Protection* and *Minute Seeing*), and a large amount of untouched treasure including diamonds, platinum, and gold. One sapphire and one parchment were missing, suggesting the thief sought only specific components — likely for a single, powerful spell.

### Marble Statue
A three-foot-tall statue of a hooded, bearded man holding a candle aloft, found toppled on the floor near the armchairs. It was reportedly knocked over by A'lai Aivenmore when he discovered the body at dawn. The candle atop it remains unlit, but Thorin’s inquiry about shaking it triggers Tadric’s paranoid fixation on the statue coming to life.

### Broken Lead-Chain Links
Small metallic links found beneath the victim's neck, indicating a chain was snapped and a locket forcibly removed from the body. The locket’s design and contents are unknown, but it is believed to contain one of the two keys to the Hightower Library.

### The Discoveries
A spell book found on Janussi's desk containing his musings and notes on unfamiliar spells and how they might be cast. It was the last thing Janussi was annotating before his death. The book is a fictional product placement for an actual D&D module, with embedded links in the digital handout.

### The Golden Ass
A comedic book that Janussi was reading and taking notes on shortly before his death. It is currently missing from the scene, and the cat was seen watching Daral flee the chamber with a book matching this description. The notes suggest Janussi was recording jokes — but the party suspects hidden meaning or a cipher.

### Gift Tag
A scrap of parchment found in the wastebasket near the desk, written in a cramped, sour hand. It reads: *“To Janussi, read it and weep, you old snob, a proper Dead Winter gift, your loving rival.”* The handwriting matches no known scribe in Candlekeep, and the tone implies deep personal animosity.

### Janussi's Locket
A piece of jewelry Janussi reportedly never removed. It is now missing, leaving only broken chain links behind. It is believed to have contained one of the two keys to the Hightower Library. Queenie confirms it was “intimate” and always worn.

### Stonespeaker Crystal
A magical artifact that grants the ability to communicate with animals. It was used to interview Queenie the cat about the events of the murder. Daz reveals it has two charges per long rest — one of which was spent on the cat interview.

### Lantern
A light source allegedly carried by the figure resembling Sylvira Savikas when spotted by Hollypocket in the middle of the night — suspicious because Sylvira is a tiefling with darkvision and would have no need for one. The implication: whoever carried it was not Sylvira, but someone impersonating her.

## Spells
### Raise Dead
A resurrection spell referenced as the likely reason the killer removed Janussi's heart after death — the spell cannot function if a vital organ is missing, suggesting the killer was knowledgeable about resurrection magic and wanted to prevent the victim from being brought back.

### Locate Object
Used by the party to search for the chipped piece of Janussi's front tooth, which was found a few feet from his face on the chamber floor, helping the party deduce that he fell forward upon death.

### Magic Missile
Identified by Daz as the cause of three roughly circular impact wounds on an armchair in Janussi's chamber, with two holes punched through the chair back and horsehair stuffing scattered about. The cat later confirmed Sylvira Savikas cast this spell during a confrontation with Janussi.

### Speak with Animals
Used by Daz via the Stonespeaker Crystal, with Zalthir and the party speaking through him, to communicate with Queenie the cat, allowing the party to gather crucial eyewitness testimony about the visitors to Janussi's chamber on the night of the murder.

## NPCs
### Janussi
The deceased Keeper of the Tome and the murder victim. A scholarly man with a sense of humor who enjoyed cherry tobacco, was researching obscure spells, and had a volatile on-again, off-again romantic relationship with Sylvira Savikas. He held one of the two keys to the Hightower Library in a locket he never removed. His final written notes were on *The Discoveries*, but he had been laughing over jokes from *The Golden Ass* before his death.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent grey ooze accompanying the party who attempted to use his knowledge of Underdark substances to identify the mysterious blackening on the victim's tongue, but was unable to determine its nature. His failed Intelligence check (6) was met with DM praise for creative roleplaying.

### Tadric
A guard or attendant present at the crime scene who becomes increasingly fixated on the possibility of the fallen marble statue's candle coming to life after the party raises the concern. He stares at the candle with unnerving intensity throughout the rest of the session, muttering, *“Perhaps we should keep the candle down…”*

### A'lai Aivenmore
A Great Reader who discovered Janussi's body at dawn and reportedly knocked over a marble statue in shock. His presence explains the statue’s fall — but not the magic missiles, the locket’s theft, or the missing book.

### Fembris Lancer
An acolyte assistant assigned to the party who provides local knowledge of Candlekeep's inhabitants and their rivalries, including identifying Daral Yashenti as Janussi's primary rival. She is a green dragonborn, and the DM clarifies she is a “bookwyrm” (spelled with a W), not a “bookworm,” and repeats everything the party says verbatim.

### Daral Yashenti
A rival scholar and Great Reader with a long-standing animosity toward Janussi. He visited Janussi twice on the night of the murder — once while hungover and once while heavily drunk — shouting that Janussi was unfit for his position. He was seen by the cat fleeing the chamber with a book matching *The Golden Ass*. He also previously insulted the party by calling them "sell swords." The gift tag’s tone strongly implicates him.

### Hollypocket
An elderly gnome housekeeper responsible for Janussi's daily needs, including bringing his morning tea and emptying his chamber pot. She provides crucial testimony about the shouting matches and visitors Janussi received on his final day, and reports seeing a glowing vision of Sylvira's face in the night. She confirms the locket was never removed and that Janussi was alive until after 11 PM.

### Kalan Strongbranch
A Great Reader who was seen leaving Janussi's office with a face like thunder after a morning meeting. He also holds the second key to the Hightower Library. His anger may have been tied to the upcoming Deadwinter Day audits — but his alibi is strong.

### Teles Ahvoste
A Great Reader who engaged in a shouting match with Janussi around the ninth bell, calling him a hypocrite before storming out. His motive is unclear, but his outburst was public and emotional.

### Sylvira Savikas
A tiefling scholar of demons and devils who had a volatile, explosive on-again, off-again romantic relationship with Janussi. She was heard screaming that Janussi was a traitor and was allegedly seen in the tower late at night carrying a lantern — unusual because Sylvira is a tiefling with darkvision and would have no need for one. The cat noted that a figure resembling her left the tower but did not smell like her — suggesting a disguise, illusion, or shapeshifter.

### Fheminor Scrivenbark
Described by the cat as the "short-legged one," Fheminor Scrivenbark visited Janussi's chamber on the night of the murder, arriving around 9 p.m. and leaving after 15 to 20 minutes, then returning drunk around 10:30 p.m. Her second visit triggered the final argument before Daral’s exit.

### Queenie
A five-year-old female cat residing in the tower who provided cryptic but vital information about the night's events in exchange for tickles. She witnessed Sylvira's magic missile outburst, Daral fleeing with a book, and a figure that looked like Sylvira but did not smell like her leaving the tower. She dismisses the party with the line: *“You may now leave, and please give yourself a proper licking before returning to my tower.”* Her insights, though feline in tone, are precise and unerring.
