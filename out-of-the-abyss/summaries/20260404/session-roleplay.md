# Roleplay Highlights — Session — 2026-04-05

## Character Voices

### Zalthir (Gabe)
- Methodical and economy-of-words in combat; states actions with clinical precision, then delivers devastating results. Accepts setbacks (frightened condition) with deflated one-word acknowledgments. Dry humor surfaces when cornered.
- Maintains tactical self-awareness even while seeking loopholes — acknowledges the *spirit* of mechanical restrictions rather than just the letter.
> "Now that I'm holding her, I will punch her again, because why not?"
> "I mean, that is the way it was going, but the spirit seems to be that I should do nothing to become closer to her, or have her closer to me. I am supposed to be trying to not be near her."
> "I'm a monk."

### Thorin (Joe)
- Blunt, wry, and perpetually frustrated by situations where he can't just hit something. Watches the drow political drama with bemused detachment. Makes decisions by coin-flip and immediately regrets restraint. Pop-culture quips come naturally.
- Physical comedy of being the melee bruiser on a sprawling vertical battlefield, always one step behind the action.
> "Death to mushrooms."
> "And I'm just sitting here watching trade blows like a tennis match."
> "All I know is this map sucks, because I feel like I'm running around trying to get to things."
> "I do what I can."

### Grygum (Ben Pfaff)
- Dry, warm, and perpetually the adult in the room. Offers healing, consolation (to the GM), and tactical observation in equal measure. His humor is gentle and self-deprecating. Checks on everyone's status before committing to offense.
- Treats escalating chaos with the patience of someone who has seen too many Underdark surprises.
> "Death to some mushrooms, anyway."
> "It's tough being a minion."
> "This is turning into the 12 days of Christmas."
> "I'm amused by the Jorlan-Ilvara drama, but I think I'm still going to go ahead and attack them."

### Daz (Mike Hall)
- Defiant bravado that escalates under pressure — refuses to be sidelined, talks back to his own rescuers, and throws fireballs at darkness. Quick to assess his remaining hit points and calculate acceptable risk. Pride in his combat contributions outweighs self-preservation instincts.
> "Fuck that lady. Let's kill her."
> "I am not gonna leave. Everyone here... to fight for me without me also participating. I don't give a damn if you get paid. The best way for you to get paid, then, is to continue the fight and prevent all of us from dying."
> "They'll get to see for just a short period of time, while they're getting flamed up."

### Kaelira (NPC — Drow Elite Warrior, Vandree/Duskryn faction)
- Clipped, impatient, pragmatic. Treats Daz like a package to be delivered rather than a person. Exhausted competence — the tone of a professional mercenary surrounded by amateurs.
> "Come with me if you want to live, you idiot."
> "If you don't want to live, I'll still take you, but could you… it would be much easier if you'd just come."

### Nym Duskryn (NPC — Drow, Kaelira's associate)

## Standout Moments

- **Zalthir's ceiling assault on Ilvara:** Grappled her with Eldritch Tentacles, then rolled back-to-back critical hits during Flurry of Blows for 50+ damage — then dropped her 40 feet. The frightened condition she inflicted in response shut down his entire offensive strategy, but the sheer brutality of the initial assault set the tone for the battle.

- **Thorin destroys the Big Heart Fungus with Dawnbringer:** After learning the fungus was doubly vulnerable to radiant damage, Thorin carved through it in a single turn with the sentient sunblade. His understated "I do what I can" perfectly captured his straightforward approach to problem-solving.

- **Grygum's Guiding Bolt kills Ilvara:** The party's moral anchor delivers the killing blow on their longest-standing nemesis — and his first reaction to her exploding in poisonous spores is the deeply practical "Not ideal. How big is the cloud?"

- **Daz's defiant stand against Nym Duskryn:** Refusing to be treated as cargo by his own rescuers, Daz asserts his identity as a combatant and leader rather than a protected asset. His speech — "I don't give a damn if you get paid" — represents the fullest expression of his Leadership arc, choosing solidarity with the Ember Vanguard over self-preservation.

- **Thorin coin-flips restraint:** Letting Jorlan pass rather than taking the opportunity attack — deciding by die roll and immediately regretting it — captured the eternal tension between Thorin's desire to hit things and his growing discipline.

- **Daz smoothly lies to Asha Vandree** about the Vandree extraction team being "friends of Lolth," maintaining his cover as a Lolth-convert even in the chaos of multi-faction combat. The deception holds.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**Key NPC Speech Patterns to Maintain:**
- **Kaelira:** Curt, professional, treats her charges like difficult freight. Uses conditional threats delivered with tired efficiency. "Come with me if you want to live, you idiot" is her baseline register.
- **Nym Duskryn:** Aristocratic contempt, asks for permission to commit violence as casually as requesting a drink. "Drower-more-than-thou" condescension toward everyone, including Daz.
- **House T'sarran forces:** Bickering, blame-shifting, professionally incompetent. Their retreat threat ("We will meet again") should be remembered as hollow — the party witnessed their dysfunction firsthand.
- **Asha Vandree:** Zealous and emotionally volatile. Prays in beatitudes to Lolth. Quick to label unknowns as traitors. Currently in a state of religious fervor after witnessing Ilvara's destruction.

**PC Emotional States for Future NPC Dialogue:**
- **Daz** has firmly established himself as someone who will not be sidelined or protected — NPCs who try to shelter him will get pushback. Nym and Kaelira now know this about him. His Lolth-convert deception is active with Asha.
- **Thorin** is increasingly the odd man out in drow political maneuvering — his tennis-match metaphor reflects genuine alienation from the intrigue around him. NPCs should acknowledge his physical dominance (destroyed the Big Heart Fungus) while noting his political bewilderment.
- **Zalthir** was frightened by Ilvara's magic — a rare moment of vulnerability for the most combat-dominant PC. Future encounters could reference this (taunts about fear, or Zalthir's own determination never to be frightened again).
- **Grygum** is steady and dependable but increasingly concerned about party resources and healing — his "Does anybody need any serious healing?" check should be mirrored by NPCs who recognize him as the group's caretaker.

**Promises, Threats, and Vows to Reference:**
- **House T'sarran's retreat threat:** "We will meet again. Enjoy your victory for the moment." — This faction will return, and the party knows they are incompetent but backed by a matron mother.
- **Nym Duskryn's contract:** "We only get paid if you live" — Daz's survival is a paid contract. The identity of the patron paying for Daz's extraction remains unresolved.
- **Kaelira's assessment of Jorlan:** "He was always the dumber of the ones" — Jorlan's fate post-Ilvara is unresolved; his own family views him as expendable.
- **Daz's vow to fight alongside his allies:** His refusal to hide should be referenced if anyone again suggests he take a backseat role.
- **Asha Vandree's prayers to Lolth:** She is in full religious fervor; Daz's cover as a Lolth-convert will be tested in the aftermath.
