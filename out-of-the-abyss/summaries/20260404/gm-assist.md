# Chapter 47 A Spore-Filled Finale

Date: Apr 4th, 2026

## Summary
High above the massive heart fungus that dominated the cavern floor, Zalthir launched himself through the air toward Ilvara, the corrupted drow priestess at the center of it all. Using his eldritch tendrils to close the distance, he seized her in a crushing grip and rained blow after devastating blow upon her before releasing his hold entirely — sending her plummeting forty feet to the stone below. The fall was brutal, and though Ilvara struck the ground hard, she was far from finished. In retaliation, she reached into the darkest corners of her power and flooded Zalthir's mind with supernatural terror, leaving him frozen in place, unable to advance on her no matter how fiercely he willed himself forward.

While Zalthir wrestled with his fear, the enormous heart fungus lashed out at him with a thunderous strike, its massive limbs reaching all the way to the cavern ceiling. The blow was staggering, though Zalthir managed to deflect much of the physical force. Ilvara, meanwhile, conjured a swirling sphere of biting insects at the entrance of the cave, targeting Grygum, Daz, and Glabbagool. The swarm filled the air with a deafening, chittering roar, catching the grey ooze and the others in the maelstrom. Jorlan Duskryn, the scarred drow warrior who had once been Ilvara's lover before she cast him aside, saw his opportunity in the chaos and broke away from the party entirely, charging toward her with fury burning in his eyes.

Thorin, growing increasingly frustrated by his inability to find a target in a battle where enemies kept their distance, descended from the elevated walkways and plunged into the difficult terrain below, making straight for the heart fungus. His strikes with Dawnbringer, his sentient sun-blade, crackled with radiant energy, and the massive creature recoiled under the holy light before wilting entirely, collapsing in on itself and dying. The DM had clearly prepared something elaborate for the encounter, but the fungus never had a chance to show it. With the primary threat neutralized, the party turned their full attention to Ilvara and the growing chaos around her.

From the shadows at the edge of the cavern, five drow warriors emerged. However, the thick insect bloom blocked their line of sight, preventing them from seeing Daz or advancing further into the cavern. Before Daz could fully recover, a drow elite warrior stepped forward from the darkness and grabbed him by the arm, pulling him northeast through the stinging insect swarm and away from the worst of the fighting. The warrior made it clear that his mission was to keep Daz alive, and he was willing to take a face full of poisonous gas to do it. Asha Vandree, watching from nearby, immediately grew suspicious of the newcomers — until Daz smoothly convinced her that these drow were loyal servants of Lolth, here to assist.

A drow mage lurking in the magical darkness fired a bolt of lightning blindly into the cavern, the crackling energy streaking past everyone without finding a target. Daz, seizing the moment, unleashed a roaring fireball into the darkness at an empowered level. The explosion lit up the tunnel in a brilliant orange flash, incinerating the drow guards outright and leaving the mage barely standing. Zalthir, highly annoyed that his magically induced fear prevented him from rushing Ilvara to deliver his monk strikes, realized that while he couldn't get closer to her, he could advance in a different direction. He teleported across the battlefield, away from Ilvara, and took his frustration out on the drow of House T'sarran, finishing the wounded mage off with a single decisive strike.

Ilvara, increasingly desperate, reshaped the battlefield around her — conjuring a bloom of fungal growth to slow Jorlan Duskryn's advance and dropping her concentration on earlier spells to free her focus. She called upon the power of Zuggtmoy, muttering promises of chaos and ruin, before calling down a column of divine fire upon Daz and Nym Duskryn. Both were scorched, though they endured. Thorin, thinking tactically, turned his attention to the bridge beneath Ilvara's feet and struck it with enough force to send it crashing down. Ilvara and Jorlan Duskryn both leapt clear of the collapse, landing on the cavern floor below — shaken but unbroken. Nym Duskryn, apparently unimpressed with Jorlan Duskryn's continued survival, struck him with a bolt of lightning that sent him staggering.

Grygum channeled a concentrated beam of radiant holy light directly at Ilvara. The bolt struck true, and the effect was immediate and catastrophic. Ilvara, deeply vulnerable to radiant energy, did not simply fall — she detonated, her body erupting in a massive cloud of poisonous spores that billowed outward through the cavern. Zalthir and Thorin were caught in the toxic bloom and staggered, but both endured. The remaining drow elite warrior of House T'sarran surveyed the wreckage, offered a cold promise that they would meet again, and vanished into the tunnels. Asha Vandree, standing amid the settling spores and the silence that followed, dropped to her knees and began murmuring prayers and beatitudes to Lolth, her voice the only sound left in the ruined cavern.

## NPCs
### Ilvara
The primary antagonist and a high-ranking drow priestess, now partially corrupted with myconid-like hybrid parts. She is a formidable opponent who uses legendary actions to teleport, command drones, and magically frighten her enemies. She wields powerful clerical magic including Insect Plague and Flamestrike, and is obsessed with vengeance. She is particularly vulnerable to radiant damage, which ultimately leads to her destruction — she explodes into a cloud of poisonous spores.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent grey ooze (after the pudding king swapped bodies with him) and ally to the party. He narrowly survives Ilvara's magical insect swarm during the encounter, taking significant damage but remaining in the fight.

### Jorlan Duskryn
A scarred drow warrior and former lover of Ilvara. Driven by spite after being cast aside, he disengages from the party mid-battle to hunt down his former mistress himself, declaring his intent as he charges toward her.

### Asha Vandree
A drow combatant who has been tricked into believing Daz is a high-ranking servant of Lolth. She remains suspicious of the new drow reinforcements until Daz convinces her they are allies of Lolth. Following Ilvara's death, she begins chanting prayers and beatitudes to Lolth.

### Kaelen T'sarran
Kaelen T'sarran is a specialized drow combatant from House T'sarran who emerges from hiding to rescue Daz, working with Nym to protect him and claiming they only get paid if he lives. He leads Daz through the insect plague to safety, taking damage in the process. After Ilvara's death, he disengages and retreats, vowing the party will meet again.

### Zalith T'sarran
A drow spellcaster who blindly fires a lightning bolt into the fray from the darkness, frustrated by the lack of easy kills and the presence of magical hazards. He is ultimately killed by Daz's fireball.

### Nym Duskryn
A drow mage who works with Kaelira to protect Daz and assists the party. She asks Kaelira if she can kill her brother. She shows little mercy for Jorlan Duskryn, striking him with a lightning bolt, and navigates the chaotic battlefield by dashing to a safer position. She is also struck by Ilvara's Flamestrike.

## Locations
### The Fungal Cavern
A high-ceilinged underground chamber approximately 40 feet tall, dominated by a massive heart fungus and elevated walkways. The cavern features pools of difficult terrain, zones of magical darkness, blinding yellow circles, and a 30-foot radius protective sphere. The atmosphere grows thick with spores and the scent of ozone as the battle rages.

## Items
### Hand Crossbow
Small, concealable ranged weapons used by the Drow warriors to fire bolts at Daz from the darkness. Most shots were deflected or missed due to Daz's magical shield and the obscuring conditions on the battlefield.

### The Icon
A mysterious object present in the cavern used by the Demon Lord Zuggtmoy to communicate. The party is warned to stay away from it, as proximity to it is considered dangerous — particularly for those who have already had a deep personal encounter with its power.

## Spells
### Insect Plague
Cast by Ilvara, this spell filled a 20-foot radius sphere at the cave entrance with biting insects, targeting Grygum, Daz, and Glabbagool. It created a hazardous barrier that blocked the arriving group of five drow from seeing Daz and advancing.

### Shield
A reactive barrier of magical force cast by Daz as a reaction to deflect incoming crossbow bolts from the Drow warriors, boosting his armor class and causing subsequent attacks to miss.

### Lightning Bolt
Used by both the Drow Mage — who fired it blindly into the darkness and missed everyone — and by Nym Duskryn, who directed it at Jorlan Duskryn during the final stages of the battle.

### Fireball
Cast by Daz at an empowered level, it engulfed the area of magical darkness in flames, killing several Drow guards outright and dealing heavy damage to the Drow Mage, who was subsequently finished off by Zalthir.

### Guiding Bolt
A flash of radiant light cast by Grygum. An earlier attempt missed Ilvara, but a later cast struck her directly, dealing devastating radiant damage that exploited her vulnerability and led to her destruction.

### Flamestrike
A clerical spell used by Ilvara to call down a vertical column of divine fire upon Daz and Nym Duskryn, dealing significant damage to both targets.
