# Chapter 53 Candlekeep's Cold Case: The Grygumite Gambit

Date: May 18th, 2026

## Summary
Brother Vareth — whose narrower specialty in the puzzle of why a half-orc would convert to Bahamut had drawn him to Grygum in the first place — led Grygum through the West Inner Ward of Candlekeep to the Immortal Chambers — Candlekeep's theology and religion library, a striking chamber where each floor tile bore the symbol of a different deity and green rectangular roofs arched overhead. Along the way, Pizwog — an ancient tortle librarian — shuffled past at approximately one mile per hour and pressed into Grygum's hands what turned out to be one of his own dense academic research papers — a syncretic analysis of religions across the Sword Coast. Grygum tucked it into his satchel, promising to read it in due time. Once settled in Vareth's side office, the old scholar laid out the three great schools of thought consuming the intellectual world: the Stroudite school, which held that mortal choice was sovereign above all; the Bahamutian school, which argued that divine providence arranged all things; and the Draconic school, which claimed that ancient agency set the frame within which mortals merely operated.

Vareth made plain how serious these arguments had become. Libraries were arguing. Taverns were arguing. Chapter houses were arguing. The courts of two cities had asked for written opinions. The Stroud-Eye pamphlets had crossed the Sea of Swords. Three duels had broken out at Candlekeep itself over which polemic shelf received the larger window — three, in a library where nobody had dueled in two hundred years. Sister Yvenne had threatened to confiscate everyone's bookplates. What was being argued beneath the chess games and the partisan colors was, Vareth insisted, the question of what kind of universe mortals actually lived in.

Grygum listened carefully and then proposed something none of the existing schools had quite assembled — a theory of fixed points: moments in time where choice became constrained by the shape of the universe itself, while the vast space between those points remained wide open and free. The effect on Vareth was disproportionate. He erupted: "A fourth school of thought?" He spoke of philosophical quadrangles, of circles whose three points described a centre that nobody had yet named. He speculated that a god wishing to control the outcome of mortal lives would not need to compel a single hand — she would only need to shape the rules of the evidence, the form of polemic, the architecture of a chapter house, the quiet. And Stroud, on the other hand, said: the universe can be silent and indifferent, or it can be silent and conspiratorial. Grygum agreed: indifferent outside the fixed framework, and conspiratorial as one approached it. Building on what Vareth promptly labeled the "strong-weak hypothesis" — Grygum a weak Stroudian and strong Bahamutian around fixed points, a weak Bahamutian outside them — the old scholar could barely contain himself as he pressed a collection of Stroudite notes and papers into Grygum's hands and rushed off to begin his own research. He had a new angle on the question at the centre of the age, and the question now had Grygum's name on the back of it. Grygum promised to return a marked-up copy of the materials when he was done.

That evening, the party gathered for dinner at the Refectory, where the eight Great Readers sat at the high table. The atmosphere was uneasy — one reader was visibly drunk, another looked pale and unwell, and two were locked in hushed, intense conversation in a corner. As the meal wound down, a hooded member of the Avowed hurried past with something tucked beneath their robes, and from the high gallery above, the recited prophecies of Alaundo drifted down through the candlelit hall. One line caught the air like a cold draft: "On the longest night of winter's deepest cold, the keeper falls, the heart is sold."

The party retired to the House of Rest and the Hearth, but their sleep was cut short before dawn by frantic banging at the door. A breathless, boyish young half-elf acolyte — Fembris Lancer, the First Reader's adjutant — stood outside, wide-eyed and urgent, shouting that the Keeper of Tomes was dead and that the First Reader wanted them through the Emerald Door before the rest of the Avowed awoke. Glabbagool, the party's gelatinous companion, tucked himself into the bag of holding with eyeballs peering out as the group set off into the snow-blanketed dark of Candlekeep. Fembris led them through the grounds, pointing out the Astronomicon's curious blue stack of globes, the gleaming gold of the Whispering Dome under the snow, and the Drakonoikos — a building constructed from the scales and bones of an ancient dragon, housing lore on fire-breathing creatures and a dragon breath simulation chamber that had already claimed one life among the Avowed and now required the Keeper's personal permission to enter. Fembris, visibly out of shape by the time the party climbed the many high-ceilinged halls, tapestry-covered corridors, and austere staircases to the Chapter House, arrived with aching legs.

They entered the Chapter House inside the Exaltation bastion to find the Council of Twelve in full uproar. A tall, handsome human male with smooth dark skin and a braided beard — A'lai Aivenmore — shouted that they must have the vote and restore leadership to Candlekeep. A dowdy halfling Great Reader with a pince-nez insisted they first determine whether Janussi had named a successor. A slender female human with dark gray hair and heavy wrinkles cried that they must find the murderer. A red-cheeked wizard with a receding mane of white hair watched his colleagues with shrewd gray eyes. A sour, thin-lipped sage named Daral Yashenti demanded to know why Sylvira Savikas was not present — the party recalled she had looked unwell at dinner. And a formidably sized bronze dragonborn academic in bejeweled robes called for the Gatewarden to be dismissed with immediate effect. Grygum, seizing the moment, introduced himself as the founder of the Grygumite School — founder, in fact — briefly derailing the entire crisis into an academic tangent before the First Reader, a formidable green dragonborn known as Bookwyrm, looking shaken and weary, restored order with a sharp tap of her fingers on the table: "FOCUS, PEOPLE." Daral dismissed the party as mere sell-swords who looked like a bunch of cell swords and whom he did not know from Alaundo. The party defended their credentials by recounting their survival in the Underdark and their discovery of the ancient sentient blade Dawnbringer, and Dawnbringer herself weighed in, confidently explaining that the party must be the greatest discoverers of all, having found her imprisoned tomb after two thousand years.

Bookwyrm appointed the party as independent investigators, citing the need for impartiality given the suspicious relationships among the council members themselves, and requiring them to sign confidentiality papers before beginning their work. When asked about remuneration, the council was struck into stunned silence — it had not occurred to them. After some negotiation, with Thorin suggesting they receive carte blanche to go anywhere and read anything in the library (at which the council looked deeply uncomfortable), Bookwyrm agreed that upon a successful resolution, each member of the party would receive a copy of any book in the library, made at the House of the Binder. The Gatewarden, Kalan Strongbranch — a man with a neatly trimmed gray-white beard and piercing blue eyes — was then publicly stripped of the investigation in front of the full council, owing to his troubled relationship with the victim. Janussi had asked for Kalan's resignation just the day before the murder, concerned that the Gatewarden was losing his mental faculties and becoming negligent in his work. Kalan bowed curtly and stormed off; the party noted he had looked visibly pissed, then recovered himself, and Tadric would later note that he had looked strangely more determined after his own private inspection of the chamber.

Bookwyrm escorted the party up a spiraling staircase through the tower, passing a shrine of Oghma and a large apartment whose door was left ajar, before arriving at the Keeper's door. On the way, a gray cat called Queenie — Janussi's, by long-standing arrangement — sat motionless in the middle of the stairs, yellow eyes open and daring anyone to disturb her. Even Bookwyrm stepped carefully around the animal. When pressed, Bookwyrm admitted that nobody had ever been able to conclusively prove whether the cat was some kind of power that dwelt in Candlekeep, and nobody had been able to conclusively prove that it was not — and so it was left to sit wherever it wished. Fembris rolled his eyes and muttered that the Avowed had turned an ordinary cat sitting wherever it liked into an interstellar, interspatial, interdimensional horror — though Zalthir's quiet "Is it?" gave even Fembris momentary pause. Outside the chamber stood Tadric, a 23-year-old watcher with a bowl-cut haircut, who provided the party with a careful timeline: the body had been discovered at 7:30 in the morning by A'lai Aivenmore and Janussi's ancient gnome housekeeper Miss Hollypocket; the Gatewarden had arrived with Tadric around 8 a.m.; a distraught woman named Kazryn Nyantani had appeared weeping shortly after and viewed the body only from the doorway; a scholar named Fheminor Scrivenbark had arrived at 8:30 wanting to inspect the room but was turned away; Bookwyrm had arrived from the baths at 9; and the Gatewarden had returned at 11 to conduct his own inspection — seeming careful not to sully the crime scene — before leaving to report to the council, looking more determined on his way out than when he had arrived. Tadric admitted he had not been watching closely enough inside the room to know whether the Gatewarden had touched anything. Bookwyrm instructed the party to report back when done and to remove nothing from the room, then departed to attend to other business.

The party entered the Keeper's chambers to find a spacious circular room hazy with the lingering smell of tobacco smoke, its dozen lancet windows filling the space with pale morning light. Janussi — a human male of around seventy years — lay near the foot of an elegant mahogany desk, his white robes soaked in blood, his rib cage caved in, and a gaping hollow where his heart had been. The room bore signs of a struggle: a chair was toppled near the desk, a marble statue and its pedestal lay shattered on the floor, and the candles of the candelabra had burned entirely to their stubs and left a waxy mess below — something Tadric confirmed Miss Hollypocket would never have permitted, suggesting the murder had occurred many hours before the body was found. Near a safe on the far side of the room, blue gemstones and empty pouches were scattered across the stone floor, as though the thief had found them worthless compared to whatever they had actually come for. Fembris took one look at the body, sprinted to the nearest window, flung it open, and vomited outside the tower; fortunately, nothing disturbed the evidence.

The party examined the desk carefully, finding a spellbook called The Discoveries lying open at its center, with notes written in Celestial on a sheaf of papyrus beside it — musings on unfamiliar spells within the book and how they might be cast. A quill lay on the floor nearby, its nib wet with midnight blue ink, and a damp linen ink carrier sat in a wicker basket close by — the vessel for the spilled pot. Atop a large pile of notes at the back of the desk, more annotations in the same midnight blue ink had been made on a book called The Golden Ass, while beneath those lay older notes on The Discoveries written in a now-dry purple ink — suggesting Janussi had switched inks recently, perhaps within the last day or so. The tobacco smoke still lingered despite the candles having been long burned out, which the party flagged as a detail worth investigating. The blood around the wound was surprisingly localized — far less than one would expect from such a violent injury, raising the grim possibility that Janussi had already been dead before his chest was opened. Zalthir's examination confirmed that the rib cage had been smashed by what appeared to be an axe or similar weapon, using several precise blows all very close to one another, and that only the heart was missing — no other organs had been taken. Grygum immediately understood the theological implication: without the vital organs present, Raise Dead was impossible. The heart had not been taken as a trophy — it had been taken to ensure that no power in the world could ever bring Janussi back to speak of what he knew. The party began to wonder whether someone had already attempted Speak with Dead before their arrival, potentially blocking further attempts for ten days — and whether the Gatewarden's determined expression as he left the scene meant he had found something, or done something, that the party had yet to uncover.

### The Immortal Chambers and the Question of the Age
#### Brother Vareth leads Grygum to the Immortal Chambers — Candlekeep's theology library in the West Inner Ward — where they engage in a deep philosophical debate about the nature of the universe.
- Brother Vareth escorts Grygum to the West Inner Ward, arriving at the Immortal Chambers — described as a tile floor with one deity symbol per tile and green rectangular roofs. Grygum notes the risk of choosing which deity to step on, and tries to watch where Vareth steps so he can follow in the same path.
- Pizwog, an ancient tortle librarian, shuffles past at approximately one mile per hour and distributes what appears to be a religious tract but turns out to be one of his own dense research papers — a syncretic analysis of different religions across the Sword Coast. Grygum notes that Vareth looks irritated and annoyed but remains respectful during the approximately five-minute handoff. Grygum tucks it into his satchel and promises to read it in due time.
- Vareth leads Grygum to his side office and explains he has been keeping this corner of the library for forty-one years, and he knows what a fashion looks like — this is not a fashion. Libraries are arguing. Taverns are arguing. Chapter houses are arguing. The courts of two cities have asked for written opinions. Three duels have been fought at Candlekeep over which polemic shelf gets the larger window — three, in a library where nobody has dueled in two hundred years. Sister Yvenne threatens to confiscate everyone's bookplates.
- Vareth lays out the three dominant schools of thought regarding the Question of the Age: the Stroudite school (mortal choice is sovereign — Tiamat was denied her power because a dwarf chose, and the chain of choices that followed was a chain of free acts); the Bahamutian school (divine providence prevails — Stroud chose what he was fitted to choose, the chain was an arranged chain); and the Draconic school (ancient agency rules — Protanther honored Stroud's move because Protanther is honorable, not because Bahamut compelled him, and mortals operate in the frames the dragons set).
- Grygum proposes a fourth school of thought involving fixed points where choices are constrained, while remaining free in between, describing it as a grand compromise. He notes that it could have been Stroud at that fixed point — or it could have been another party of adventurers — but someone would have arrived there.
- Vareth is ecstatic, immediately labeling Grygum a "weak Stroudian and strong Bahamutian around fixed points, and a weak Bahamutian outside of fixed points" — and declaring this the "strong-weak hypothesis." He speculates about a philosophical quadrangle and the center of a circle described by the three existing schools. He theorizes that a god who wished to control the outcome of mortal lives would not need to compel a single hand — she would only need to shape the rules of the evidence, the form of polemic, the architecture of a chapter house.
- Vareth provides Grygum with a collection of Stroudite notes and papers to study, barely able to contain himself as he rushes off to begin his own research. Grygum promises to return a marked-up copy when he is done.
- Vareth calls the compromise closer to the Bahamutian school than the Stroudite, and classifies Grygum as a weak Stroudite — a strong Stroudite would hold that there are no fixed points at all.

- Atop the large pile of notes at the back of the desk, held down by a quartz weight, are more annotations in midnight blue ink on a book called The Golden Ass. Beneath those are older notes on The Discoveries in a different, now-dry purple ink — Janussi had switched inks recently. The blue ink represents the most recent work; the purple, an earlier layer.
- Thorin notes the tobacco smoke is lingering even though the candles burned out long ago — a detail flagged for follow-up.
- The party deduces from the burned-down candles that Janussi must have been murdered many hours before the body was found. Tadric confirms Miss Hollypocket would never have allowed the Keeper to spend the night with candles burning down to nothing like that.
- The party rolls Medicine and Investigation to examine the body. Zalthir rolls a 17 and confirms: the Keeper's rib cage was smashed by what appears to be an axe or similar weapon, using several precise blows all very close to one another. Only the heart is missing — no other organs. The blood is surprisingly localized around the wound, far less than one would expect from such a violent injury — suggesting Janussi may already have been dead before the chest was opened.
- Grygum immediately recognizes the implication: Raise Dead is impossible if the vital organs are not present. The heart was taken specifically to prevent resurrection.
- Thorin asks whether they can still Speak with Dead without the heart — they only need the brain and a mouth. Tadric reveals that someone already tried — and the dead were not speaking. The party considers that the spell may have already been cast (which would block further attempts for ten days), or that some other effect is preventing it.
- Thorin asks whether waiting ten days would allow them to try again. The answer is yes — though the corpse will be considerably more fragrant by then. Thorin asks whether there is any ice available to preserve the body.
- The session ends with the party mid-investigation, the crime scene rich with unanswered questions: the identity of the hooded Avowed seen at dinner; the significance of The Golden Ass and the ink change; who cast Speak with Dead and when; what Kalan found during his inspection; and what was taken from the safe.

## Locations
### Immortal Chambers (West Inner Ward)
The Immortal Chambers — Candlekeep's theology and religion library in the West Inner Ward — featuring a tile floor where each tile is adorned with a different deity's symbol, and green rectangular roofs. Curated by A'lai Aivenmore. Brother Vareth's side office is located within one of the library's side rooms, where he maintains his decades-long collection of polemics and research on the Question of the Age. Grygum noted the danger of choosing which deity tile to step on, and tried to follow in Vareth's exact footsteps.

### The Refectory
The dining hall of Candlekeep where the Great Readers and the Avowed gather for meals. The party observed the council members here — one visibly drunk, one unwell, two in hushed conversation — and heard the recited prophecies of Alaundo floating down from the high gallery, including the ominous verse about the keeper falling on the longest night of winter.

### House of Rest and the Hearth
The guest quarters outside Candlekeep's Outer Ward where the party stayed during their visit. Fembris Lancer banged on their door here before dawn to summon them to the investigation.

### Astronomicon
A curious blue stack of globes within Candlekeep, pointed out by Fembris during the morning tour. According to Fembris, everything Candlekeep knows about the stars can be found here.

### Whispering Dome
Part of the Hall of Momentous Deeds within Candlekeep. Its gold gleamed under the fresh snow as the party passed. According to Fembris, the words of past heroes can be heard there and visitors can be inspired.

### Drakonoikos
A building within Candlekeep constructed from the scales and bones of Fyrentennimar, an ancient red dragon, pointed out by Fembris during the morning tour. It houses lore on fire-breathing creatures. Fembris fumbled the dragon's name on first try before landing on Fyrentennimar, noting: "Anything you need to know about fire-breathing lizards, you'll find in there." It contains a dragon breath simulation chamber that has proven fatal to at least one member of the Avowed; the Keeper's personal permission is now required to use it.

### Chapter House
The executive meeting room of the Exaltation bastion, located under the western gables of its green slanted roof opposite Reader's Tower. Features a distinctive rose window that allows lateral light to stream in and a dodecagonal table with places for the Keeper of Tomes, the First Reader, the eight Great Readers, the Gate Warden, and the Lorekeeper — a council of twelve. The party was officially appointed as investigators here after signing confidentiality papers.

### House of the Binder
A facility within Candlekeep where books are copied and bound. Referenced as the source of the party's promised reward: a copy of any book in the library for each party member upon successful resolution of the investigation.

### Janussi's Chambers
A spacious circular chamber at the top of a spiraling staircase within a tower of Candlekeep, ringed by a dozen lancet windows that fill the room with natural light. Features an elegant mahogany desk in the center, a seven-armed bronze candelabra, a marble statue (now toppled), a safe, and a wooden staircase leading to a second floor. The staircase ascending to the chamber passes a reception chamber, a shrine of Oghma, and a large apartment with an ajar door. The chamber was heavy with tobacco smoke at the time of the party's arrival and showed clear signs of a violent struggle. The mysterious gray cat was found sleeping on the stairs below the chamber door.

## Items
### Syncretic Analysis Tract
A highly academic and structurally incoherent research paper on different religions across the Sword Coast, handed to Grygum by the ancient turtle librarian. It turned out to be one of the librarian's own research papers rather than the standard religious tract it resembled. Grygum tucked it into his satchel and promised to read it in due time.

A collection of marked-up papers and research material regarding the Stroudite school of thought, given to Grygum by Brother Vareth for study. Grygum promised to return a marked-up copy when he has reviewed them. Vareth noted these would probably seem like nonsense to a weak Stroudite such as Grygum.

### Dawnbringer
A sentient artifact found by the party in an ancient tomb, cited during the council meeting as proof of the party's investigative and discovery credentials when Daral challenged their qualifications. Dawnbringer herself spoke up during the meeting, arguing that the party must be the greatest discoverers of all, having found her imprisoned tomb after two thousand years.

### The Discoveries
A spellbook found lying open in the center of the Keeper of Tomes' mahogany desk. It was the subject of Janussi's most recent research, with accompanying notes written in Celestial on a sheaf of papyrus musing on the unfamiliar spells within and how they might be cast. Older notes on the same book, written in a dry purple ink, were also found beneath the more recent blue-ink annotations in the pile at the back of the desk.

### The Golden Ass
A book referenced in Janussi's notes, with annotations written in midnight blue ink found atop the pile of notes at the back of the desk. This book was the subject of Janussi's most recent annotation work — the blue ink being more freshly applied than the purple ink underneath. The nature of the book's contents was not immediately apparent from the notes. Notably, The Golden Ass itself was not found anywhere in the chamber.

### Midnight Blue Ink
The freshest ink present at the scene, found in a spilled pot and on a discarded quill near the Keeper's desk, and in the most recently written annotations on both The Discoveries notes and The Golden Ass notes. Contrasts with older, now-dry purple ink found in the same pile of notes, suggesting Janussi switched inks at some point within the last day or so.

### Blue Gemstones
Valuable stones found scattered on the floor in front of the safe in Janussi's chambers, along with several empty pouches. They appeared to have been left behind by the intruder, suggesting whatever was taken from the safe was considered far more valuable than the gemstones.

## Spells
### Speak with Dead
The spell grants a semblance of life and intelligence to a corpse, allowing it to answer questions. It requires the corpse to still have a mouth and fails if the spell has already been cast on the same corpse within the last ten days. The Gate Warden appeared to have already attempted this during his private inspection of the chamber — Tadric confirmed that someone had tried and the dead were not speaking. The party theorizes either the spell was cast and is now blocked for ten days, or some other effect is preventing communication. The party also notes that the removal of Janussi's heart was specifically intended to prevent resurrection magic (such as Raise Dead), which requires vital organs to be present. The party discusses waiting ten days to attempt the spell themselves, though the state of the corpse will deteriorate significantly by then — prompting Thorin to ask whether any ice is available.

## NPCs
### Brother Vareth
An elderly scholar who has kept his corner of the Theology Library at Candlekeep for forty-one years. His narrow specialty is the puzzle of why a half-orc would convert to Bahamut, given that orcs and dragons are traditional enemies who occupy similar ecological niches and that a sufficient mass of orcs can mathematically bring a dragon down. Stroud's chess victory three years ago dragged this niche question into the centre of the Question of the Age. Vareth laid out all three existing schools of thought in a prepared speech to Grygum, but was electrified by Grygum's proposal of a fourth: fixed points around which choices become constrained. He immediately labeled Grygum a "weak Stroudian and strong Bahamutian around fixed points," declared the concept a philosophical quadrangle and the "strong-weak hypothesis," and rushed off to begin his own research — barely pausing to hand Grygum the Stroudite materials and hear Grygum's promise to return them marked up.

### Pizwog
An ancient tortle librarian at Candlekeep — the resident librarian of the Immortal Chambers — extraordinarily slow-moving, who shuffles through the theology library at approximately one mile per hour and distributes what appear to be religious tracts but turn out to be his own academic papers. He handed Grygum one of his own papers — a syncretic analysis of different religions across the Sword Coast — during the approximately five-minute encounter. Vareth looked irritated by the interruption but maintained respectful patience.

### Sister Yvenne
A member of the library staff at Candlekeep, mentioned by Vareth as having threatened to confiscate everyone's bookplates due to the heated duels breaking out over library shelf space in the wake of the Question of the Age controversy. She has not yet acted on the threat, but Vareth expects it.

### Fembris Lancer
An enthusiastic, boyish, somewhat out-of-shape young half-elf acolyte, adjutant to the First Reader, who brings the party the news of the Keeper's death and serves as their guide and adjutant throughout the investigation. He led the morning tour of the Candlekeep grounds with great energy, pointing out the Astronomicon, the Whispering Dome, and the Drakonoikos. He arrived at the Chapter House with aching legs. He dismissed the Avowed's reverence for the mysterious gray cat as overblown superstition — until Zalthir's single quiet "Is it?" gave him pause. Upon entering the Keeper's chambers, he took one look at the body, sprinted to the nearest window, flung it open, and vomited outside the tower, thereafter remaining outside. When assigned as the party's adjutant and offering to help with "anything," he hastily qualified his offer upon noticing the composition of the party.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent gelatinous cube who accompanies the party. He hitched a ride in the bag of holding with eyeballs peering out as the party rushed to the crime scene before dawn, apparently excited to be on the cusp of something new.

### Bookwyrm
A green dragonborn and the First Reader of Candlekeep. She appeared shaken and weary following the murder but took firm charge of the chaotic council meeting, silencing the arguing council members with a sharp tap on the table and a repeated "FOCUS, PEOPLE!" She proposed delegating the investigation to the party to ensure impartiality, agreed to the copy-of-a-book reward after negotiation, publicly stripped Kalan Strongbranch of the investigation, personally escorted the party to the crime scene, and stepped carefully around the mysterious gray cat on the stairs. She deflected questions about what specific negligence Kalan had been accused of, and when asked where she herself was the night of the murder, she arrived at the crime scene door before answering. She instructed the party to remove nothing from the room and report back when done.

### A'lai Aivenmore
A tall, handsome human male Great Reader with smooth dark skin and a braided beard. He pushed loudly for an immediate vote to restore leadership to Candlekeep, prioritizing the resumption of normal business above all else. He was also one of the first two people to discover the body, alongside Miss Hollypocket, around 7:30 a.m. He was ultimately willing to support the party's appointment if Bookwyrm preferred it, reasoning that the faster the matter resolved, the sooner governance could be restored.

### Daral Yashenti
A sour, thin-lipped human sage Great Reader with greasy, slick-backed gray hair. He was the most vocal skeptic of the party's appointment, dismissing them contemptuously as people he did not know from Alaundo who looked like a bunch of sell-swords. He also asked why Sylvira Savikas was not present at the meeting.

### Janussi
The late Keeper of Tomes, a human male around seventy years old. Found murdered in his chambers with his rib cage caved in by several precise axe-like blows and his heart removed. He had been engaged in academic research involving Celestial translations of The Discoveries and annotations on The Golden Ass at the time of his death. He had asked for Gatewarden Kalan Strongbranch's resignation the morning before the murder, citing concerns about Kalan's mental faculties and negligence. He was found with his white robes heavily bloodied around the torso, vacant eyes fixed on the ceiling.

### Sylvira Savikas
The Great Reader who curates the Infernal Fortress (Candlekeep's library of the Abyss and the Nine Hells), absent from the council meeting due to a lingering illness. Daral called attention to her absence. The party had observed her looking unwell at dinner the previous evening. She was the reader who had appeared pale at the Refectory.

### Kalan Strongbranch
The Gatewarden of Candlekeep, a man with a neatly trimmed gray-white beard and piercing blue eyes. He was publicly and abruptly stripped of the murder investigation in front of the full council and the party by Bookwyrm, who cited his troubled relationship with the victim. Janussi had asked for his resignation the morning before the murder. He looked visibly pissed at the dismissal, then recovered himself, bowed curtly, and stormed off. During his private inspection of the crime scene at 11 a.m., Tadric noted he looked more determined when he came out than when he went in — and that Kalan had appeared careful not to sully the scene. The party believes he may have already attempted Speak with Dead, which would account for the spell's current failure, and that he may have discovered or done something the party has yet to uncover. Tadric personally defends Kalan as a great and honest man.

### Tadric
A 23-year-old watcher with a distinctive bowl-cut haircut and dark brown hair who was tasked with guarding the door to the Keeper's chambers. He provided the party with a careful and detailed timeline of everyone who visited the room before their arrival. He admitted he was not watching closely enough to track everything the Gatewarden did inside, as his job was crowd control outside, not observation within — and he confessed he does not really like blood and murder scenes. He spoke warmly of Kalan Strongbranch and seemed troubled by the Gatewarden's removal. When asked if he had done a good job, he reflected: "I don't think I did a good job."

### Miss Hollypocket
Janussi's ancient gnome housekeeper, one of the first two people to discover the murder alongside A'lai Aivenmore, at around 7:30 a.m. Tadric confirmed that she would never have permitted the Keeper to let his candles burn down to nothing — which the party used to deduce that the murder had taken place many hours before the body was found.

### Kazryn Nyantani
A Great Reader who arrived at the crime scene distraught and weeping shortly after 8 a.m., wanting to see the body of Janussi. She was permitted to view it only from the doorway. Her strong emotional reaction to the death and her early arrival make her a person of interest.

### Fheminor Scrivenbark
A scholar who arrived at the murder chamber at 8:30 a.m. appearing shocked, wanting to inspect the chamber, but was turned away as the order to admit no one had already been set.
