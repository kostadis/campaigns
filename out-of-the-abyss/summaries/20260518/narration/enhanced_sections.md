## Consistency Notes

The following issues from the consistency report could not be silently resolved in the text and are flagged here for GM review.

**Issue 3 — Daz's Stonespeaker Crystal and Speak with Dead**
Daz's attuned Stonespeaker Crystal grants *Speak with Dead*. The recap records the party discussing the spell as effectively inaccessible — either already cast and blocked for ten days, or prevented by some unknown effect — without any character noting that Daz possesses a means of casting it. This may reflect what actually happened at the table (the crystal was overlooked, or the party assumed the blocking effect would apply to all attempts including Daz's), but the omission creates a continuity risk going forward. The GM should confirm: does the current blocking effect — whatever its source — prevent Daz from attempting *Speak with Dead* via the crystal? If not, the party has an uninvoked resource that may become relevant in the next session.

**Issue 4 — Raise Dead claim overstated**
The recap states that without the vital organs present "no power in the world" could bring Janussi back to speak of what he knew. As written, this overstates the case: *True Resurrection* and *Wish* can restore a creature regardless of the body's condition. Grygum is a Cleric 8 and would likely be aware these spells exist even if they are far beyond his current reach. The intended meaning — that the heart's removal was specifically designed to make conventional resurrection magic (*Revivify*, *Raise Dead*, *Reincarnate*) impossible, and to make revival as costly and difficult as achievable short of divine intervention — has been softened in the Memorable Moments entry above. The GM should confirm whether the killer's intent was understood in-session as an absolute barrier or as raising the cost of revival to the near-impossible.

**Issue 5 — Immortal Chambers attributed to A'lai Aivenmore**
The Locations entry for the Immortal Chambers states it is curated by A'lai Aivenmore. This attribution does not appear to be established in the session content: Brother Vareth is the forty-one-year resident specialist in the theology library, and Sylvira Savikas is the only Great Reader whose specific curatorial domain is identified in the text (the Infernal Fortress). The attribution has been left in the Locations section as written — it cannot be silently corrected without risk of removing something the GM stated explicitly — but the GM should verify whether A'lai's domain is theology, and if so, add that detail to his NPC entry for consistency.

**Issue 6 — Miniaturized drow spy in the bag of holding**
The party document records that a miniaturized drow spy is currently detained inside the bag of holding — the same bag Glabbagool entered at the start of the session. The recap does not acknowledge this complication. If the party did not address the spy this session, no scene change is needed, but the GM should confirm the spy's status is being tracked and consider whether Glabbagool's presence in the same confined space has any consequences to flag for the next session.

**Issue 7 — Who proposed carte blanche access**
The Summary attributes the demand for unrestricted library access to Thorin alone; the Memorable Moments section implies it was a joint effort by Grygum and Thorin. Given Grygum's Sage background and his active research interests at Candlekeep, his involvement in pushing for reading access would be entirely in character. The GM should confirm whether Grygum contributed to this negotiating position or whether it was solely Thorin's demand, and whether the final agreement (one book each from the House of the Binder) was understood to satisfy or replace the carte blanche request.

**Issue 9 — Grygum's species: orc vs. half-orc**
The party document lists Grygum's species as Orc. The original recap refers to him as a half-orc in the Summary and in Brother Vareth's NPC entry, where Vareth's scholarly specialty is described as "the puzzle of why a half-orc would convert to Bahamut." All references to Grygum as "half-orc" in sections other than the Summary (which is being replaced separately) have been left as written pending GM confirmation of the following: Is Vareth's framing of his research question as being about *half-orc* conversion a pre-existing scholarly formulation that predates his meeting Grygum — and if so, does the fact that Grygum is a *full* orc represent a meaningful discrepancy that affects Vareth's thesis and is worth preserving as a plot detail? The GM should confirm Grygum's species and whether this distinction has been or should be addressed in Vareth's scholarship.
