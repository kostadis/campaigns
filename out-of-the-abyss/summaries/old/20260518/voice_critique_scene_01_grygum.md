# Voice Critique — Grygum, scene 01: The Immortal Chambers And The Question Of The Age

**Narration:** `summaries/20260518/gm-assist-doc.md` (section 1)
**Voice spec:** `voice/grygum_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/grygum.md`

## Flags

### [1] Workshop simile — wrong narrator's hand

> We are briefly interrupted by Pizwog, who is an ancient tortle and moves at the velocity of a very confident glacier.

**Why:** "Velocity of a very confident glacier" is a stylish-prose mashup of physics + personality that reads as authorial-voice rather than Ben/Grygum. Ben's deadpan operates by applying *policy/civics* vocabulary to fantasy nonsense ("the state of goblin equality and inclusion is rough at the moment"), not by inventing similes. Grygum at the table would name the slowness flatly and move.
**Suggested rewrite:** "We are briefly interrupted by Pizwog, who is an ancient tortle. He moves at the speed of an ancient tortle. I wait."

### [2] Generic-prose flourish

> His side office is the corner of a man who has been winning his argument with the furniture for four decades.

**Why:** Cute but invented. The voice spec is explicit that Grygum's narration is "biblical-formal" register dropping back to "plain conversational English" — not literary-fiction tidiness about furniture-as-character. Grygum would describe the office in objects, not metaphor.
**Suggested rewrite:** "His side office is small and full. He has been keeping this corner for forty-one years, he says. I believe him."

### [3] Workshop simile

> Vareth has a very specific kind of excitement that I associate with men who have been tending a fire alone in a room for years and have just heard someone knock.

**Why:** Same pattern — a long, decorative simile in a voice the spec characterizes as paratactic, short, ~8-word sentences. The image is fine for a literary narrator; it isn't a Grygum image. He'd name what the excitement *did* (academic implications, what got dismissed) rather than reach for the lonely-firekeeper picture.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Vareth is excited in the particular way of an academic who has just been handed a problem he can spend the next year on. He dismisses the Bahamutians. He waves off the dragons."

### [4] Generic register-creep in opener

> The West Inner Ward smells like old paper and a kind of ceremonial solemnity that I have mostly associated with spaces where people have decided collectively to take themselves very seriously.

**Why:** "Ceremonial solemnity" is generic religious-fiction vocabulary. The line is doing observational work, which is on-voice for Grygum, but the noun is the kind of word he would *deflate* rather than reach for — see "Living Serpent Head Scourge sounds… it sounds like a great museum piece." His preacher-cadence is reserved for divine invocation; describing a corridor he'd be plainer.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The West Inner Ward smells like old paper and the particular self-seriousness of people who have decided collectively to be impressive. Brother Vareth moves through it like he has been doing this for forty-one years, because he has."

## Verdict

Voice is broadly intact — "I file that thought for later," "I took notes either way," "I feel like I handled that competently" are pitch-perfect — but the model reaches for a literary simile every few paragraphs and loses Grygum's flat dryness whenever it does. Spot-edit the four offenders; do not re-run.
