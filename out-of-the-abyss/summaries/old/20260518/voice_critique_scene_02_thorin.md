# Voice Critique — Thorin, scene 02: Dinner at the Refectory

**Narration:** `summaries/20260518/gm-assist-doc.md` (section 2)
**Voice spec:** `voice/thorin_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/thorin.md`

## Flags

### [1] Workshopped simile chain — wrong register

> It landed the way cold drafts land — not dramatically, not with any ceremony, just suddenly there, already inside the room before you noticed the door was open.

**Why:** Thorin's spec is stone-weight, concrete: "things land, strike, hold or break." This is the opposite — a chain of negatives ("not dramatically, not with any ceremony") that gestures at a feeling rather than naming it. The cold-draft image is decorative; Thorin would say the prophecy hit and tell you what hit him about it.
**Suggested rewrite:** "It landed. Winter's deepest cold. I looked at Daz. I looked at Grygum. Neither of them had reacted, so either they hadn't heard it or they'd done what I was already doing: kept it."

### [2] Gesture-not-name (banned-words-adjacent)

> Whatever the ooze had been doing in there, or thinking, or absorbing through the particular process Zalthir had been patiently trying to formalize into something resembling monastic discipline, it had arrived at what could only be described as the cusp of something. I didn't know what the something was.

**Why:** "The cusp of something" / "what could only be described as" is the same family as the banned "the shape of X" — gesturing at a pattern without naming it. Thorin would name a thing or admit he doesn't know in one short sentence, not run three clauses around the not-knowing.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Whatever Glabbagool had been doing in the bag all day, he was done with it. He wanted out. I didn't know what *out* meant for an ooze. Neither did he."

### [3] Wrong-narrator vocabulary

> Most of the recited lines slid past me the way background noise does — present, ambient, categorized and discarded.

**Why:** "Categorized and discarded" is Daz's auditing vocabulary, not Thorin's. The spec is explicit: Thorin's nouns are *bridge, glyph, pit, rock, sword, scabbard, ground, position*. He doesn't categorize information; he hears it or he doesn't.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Most of the recited lines slid past me. Background noise. The kind you stop hearing in a tavern after the first round."

### [4] Generic emotional naming

> ...and sat down with the particular kind of tired that isn't about sleep.

**Why:** The "particular kind of X that isn't about Y" construction is the same hedge as flag 2 — gesturing at a feeling instead of naming it. Thorin's spec: *"I'm tired of this" means despair.* He'd say it shorter and flatter.
**Suggested rewrite:** "...and sat down. The fire was warm. That was a different kind of tired than I'd had in months."

### [5] Voice-bleed: "filed it"

> Neither of them had visibly reacted, which meant either they hadn't heard it or they were filing it the same way I was filing it: not immediately actionable, worth keeping.

**Why:** *Filing* is Grygum's and Daz's verb — Thorin's spec uses "I noted", "worth noting", "kept it." The next paragraph correctly closes on *"I kept it,"* which is the voice — the *filing* line a few sentences earlier breaks register.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Neither of them had visibly reacted. Either they hadn't heard it, or they'd already done what I was about to do. Kept it."

## Verdict

The giant-aphorism beat ("a wall is just a rock in the right place. A prophecy that hasn't happened yet is just a warning waiting for its moment") is exactly right, and "Yep. Been there." lands. But the scene reaches for literary similes (cold drafts) and Daz/Grygum bookkeeping verbs (categorized, filed) every time it slows down to register a feeling — spot-edit those five and the scene is solid.
