# Voice Critique — Zalthir, scene 04: The Tour of the Bastion

**Narration:** `summaries/20260518/gm-assist-doc.md` (section 4)
**Voice spec:** `voice/zalthir_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/zalthir.md`

## Flags

### [1] Generic poetic image

> In the early light the scales caught the sun and held it.

**Why:** Stock fantasy-sunlight sentence — the kind of line that could close any scene by any narrator. Zalthir's spec is "terse, observational, slightly removed from emotion"; when he registers visual beauty he marks *what he chose to mark* and stops. The verbatim chapter-1 example shows the register: *"The air changed first. It thinned in a way that had nothing to do with depth; it simply became something else. Something with weather in it. Then the light hit."* Notice the line that *isn't* a flourish.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The early light hit the scales. They held it." (Or cut entirely — Grygum's *"those scales really gleam in the sunrise"* is the line that's already doing the awe-work in dialogue.)

### [2] Slight literary lift — "reduced now to architecture"

> Fyrentennimar, Fembris finally produced the name after a pause and a visible effort, dragon of some age and significance, reduced now to architecture.

**Why:** Borderline. "Reduced now to architecture" *is* on-voice in spirit (Zalthir treats heritage as chassis-not-narrative), but the formal phrasing reads as a literary narrator polishing the image rather than a monk filing it. The chapter-1 example shows him doing the same observation flatter: *"He has little use for combs. ... Then he pockets the paper."* The reduction lands without being labeled a reduction.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Fyrentennimar, Fembris finally produced the name. A dragon of some age, now a building." (Or push further into Zalthir register: "A dragon, now a wall.")

## Verdict

Voice fidelity is the highest of any scene in the doc — the tactical-inventory cadence ("Exits: three visible archways, one service passage…"), the *"Brother Heslin — or possibly Brother Arrath"* uncertain-monk pattern, *"I had been watching the window,"* and *"Fembris's legs were done. Mine were not"* are all pitch-perfect. Two minor flourishes only; no re-narration needed.
