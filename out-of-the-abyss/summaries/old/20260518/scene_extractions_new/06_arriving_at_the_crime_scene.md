# Arriving at the Crime Scene

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Bookwyrm escorts the party to the Keeper's tower, where they gather initial details from the watcher on guard before entering the murder chamber.
- Bookwyrm marches the party to the tower. On the way, she reveals that Janussi had asked for Kalan's resignation the morning before the murder. When the party presses for more details on what Kalan had been negligent in, Bookwyrm deflects: "I don't want to prejudice your investigation." Thorin presses: "But your testimony is evidence. Maybe you are evidence, and we should examine you." Bookwyrm pauses: "Everyone should be examined. But I can assure you, I had nothing to do with it." Thorin asks where she was. Bookwyrm does not answer before the party arrives at the tower.
- The spiraling staircase rings the tower, passing a reception chamber on one floor, a shrine of Oghma on a second, and a large apartment on a third whose door is left ajar, before arriving at the Keeper's door.
- On the stairs, Queenie — Janussi's gray cat — sits motionless in the middle of the steps, yellow eyes open and daring anyone to disturb her. Bookwyrm steps gingerly around her.
- Thorin asks about the cat. Bookwyrm explains that nobody has been able to conclusively prove the cat is some kind of power that lives here — and nobody has been able to conclusively prove that it is not. So the cat stays where the cat wishes.
- Fembris rolls his eyes and mutters to the party that the Avowed have turned a cat sitting wherever it likes into an interstellar, interspatial, interdimensional horror — that's just what cats do. Zalthir delivers a quiet "Is it?" Fembris reconsiders: if Bookwyrm was scared of the cat, maybe he should be scared of the cat.
- The party arrives at the crime scene. A solemn-faced watcher named Tadric guards the door — short, human male, 23 years old, dark brown hair in a bowl cut. A moon-elf scribe had tried to stop the party on the way up; Fembris explained they had been summoned by the First Reader. Bookwyrm informs Tadric that Kalan has been relieved from the investigation and that the party will be taking over.
- Bookwyrm instructs the party to report back as soon as they are done and to remove nothing from the room, then departs for other business.
- Fembris is officially assigned as the party's adjutant. He is enthusiastically willing to help with anything — then, noticing the drow, the dragonborn, Thorin, and the half-orc, he hastily qualifies: anything legal, such as where the bathrooms are or where a particular room might be found.
- The party questions Tadric for a timeline. He provides: Janussi found dead by A'lai Aivenmore and Janussi's ancient gnome housekeeper Miss Hollypocket around 7:30 a.m.; the Gate Warden arrived with Tadric around 8 a.m., having spoken briefly with A'lai and Miss Hollypocket; Kazryn Nyantani arrived distraught and weeping shortly after 8 and viewed the body only from the doorway; Fheminor Scrivenbark arrived at 8:30 appearing shocked, wanting to inspect the chamber but was turned away; Bookwyrm arrived at 9 from the baths and was allowed a brief look before confirming the Gate Warden's order; the Gate Warden returned at 11 for a full inspection, seeming careful not to sully the crime scene, before leaving to make his report. Tadric notes the Gate Warden looked more determined when he came out. Tadric also admits he was not watching closely enough to track everything the Gate Warden did inside — his job was crowd control outside, not observation within. Tadric defends Kalan as a great, honest, stand-up guy and seems troubled by his removal from the case.
- The party asks whether the Gate Warden discovered something during his inspection. Tadric: "He definitely looked more determined after he walked out."
- The party learns the Gate Warden had previously attempted Speak with Dead — but the dead were not speaking.
