# Dinner at the Refectory

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party gathers for a meal at the Refectory where they observe the Great Readers and the atmosphere of Candlekeep.
- The party attends dinner at the Refectory, observing the eight Great Readers at the high table.
- The party rolls Insight rather than Perception to read the room; results range from 9 to 20.
- The party notices one reader is visibly drunk, another looks unwell, and two are locked in deep, hushed conversation in one corner.
- A hooded member of the Avowed is seen hurrying past with something hidden under their robes.
- The party hears the recited prophecies of Alaundo floating down from the high gallery. Vareth had previously explained that these prophecies are recited continuously, and that whenever one comes true it is removed — so only unfulfilled prophecies remain. Nobody knows what will happen when they run out.
- One prophecy catches the air like a cold draft: "On the longest night of winter's deepest cold, the keeper falls, the heart is sold."
- The party retires to the House of Rest and the Hearth after dinner.

## Verbatim moments

**[The Party Gathers at the Refectory]**
- The clock chimes and the party is taken to the refectory for dinner.
- All eight Great Readers are present at the high table, with meals being served.

**GM** — *asking for dice rolls to read the room*
> "You do roll a perception."

**Zalthir** — *clarifying who is present*
> "Which of us are there?"

**GM** — *explaining the scene*
> "All of you are now, but basically what… yes, basically what's happened is you've done parts of your research, now they've decided they need to go do more preparation, time has elapsed, it's dinner time, you know, they've got other meetings to attend to, group, you know, discussions to do, other paperwork, other students to work with."

**GM** — *correcting the roll type*
> "I meant to roll insight. Oh, very well."

**[Insight Rolls — Reading the Room]**
- Zalthir rolls an 11 ("a critical fill for me").
- Grygum rolls a 9.
- Daz rolls (after initially rolling Perception, then re-rolling Insight).
- Thorin re-rolls Insight, scoring 20.

**Zalthir** — *reporting results*
> "Not good. 11… a critical fill for me. Gurgan got a 9. Daz hasn't rolled yet."

**GM** — *clarifying the roll*
> "Insight, insight, rolled insight, sorry."

**Thorin** — *on his re-roll*
> "At 20s on the inside. Shitty rolls on perception."

**[What the Party Observes]**
- One Great Reader is visibly drunk.
- Another looks unwell.
- Two are in deep conversation in a corner of the room.

**GM** — *describing the refectory observations*
> "So, you saw all the great readers. One of them is drunk, visibly. And another one looks unwell. And two of them appear to be in deep conversation at one corner."

**[The Hooded Avowed]**
- As the party heads back from dinner, they notice a hooded Avowed hurrying past with something tucked under their robes.

**GM** — *describing the figure*
> "As you were going down, you noticed a… as you're… as you're heading back out to dinner, it's after dinner, you noticed a… a hooded avowed hurrying past with something tucked under their robe."

**[The Prophecies of Alaundo]**
- The party hears the recited prophecies of Alaundo floating down from the high gallery.
- GM explains the nature of the prophecies: fulfilled ones are removed, only unfulfilled remain.

**GM** — *explaining Alaundo's prophecies*
> "At some point in time, you probably asked what the prophecies are, and it turns out there was a guy named Alaundo who had prophecies that turned out remarkably accurately, and so the folks at Candlekeep keep reciting them."

**Grygum** — *asking about the prophecies*
> "Have they… are there some that have not…"

**GM** — *explaining the system*
> "Oh yeah, those are the ones that are not… the ones that have not yet come true. So apparently, whenever a prophecy comes true, it gets removed, and the prophecies shrink over time. Nobody knows what happens when you run out of prophecies."

**Grygum** — *pressing further*
> "Are there any that, you know, could no longer come true, or are they all vague enough that they still could?"

**GM** — *responding*
> "Who knows? That, that, that, as they say, is a question worthy of asking the avowed and the great readers themselves. And perhaps having a lot of patience."

**[The Ominous Prophecy]**
- One prophecy stands out during the recitation.

**GM** — *delivering the prophecy*
> "Once during the day, they catch, one that says, on the longest night of winter's deepest cold, the keeper falls, the heart is sold."

**GM** — *closing the scene*
