# Voice Critique — Daz, scene 06: Arriving at the Crime Scene

**Narration:** `summaries/20260518/gm-assist-doc.md` (section 6)
**Voice spec:** `voice/daz_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/daz.md`

## Flags

### [1] Convergence pattern — three "with the particular [noun] of someone who…" constructions in one scene

> ...tracking us with the particular economy of a creature that has decided it need not move for anyone.
> Bookwyrm stepped around her with the practiced delicacy of someone who had done this many times and preferred not to think about why.
> Tadric admitted, with the particular embarrassment of someone who had suddenly understood that his job was smaller than the job that needed doing, that his assignment had been crowd control.

**Why:** Three instances of the same workshop construction inside ~40 lines. The construction shows up for every narrator across the doc (see summary), but this scene concentrates it. Daz's spec is *measured, categorical* — he categorizes by noun ("Boyish. Very slightly delighted by his own urgency."), not by attaching a relative-clause portrait to the verb. The first instance feels like Daz; the third feels like the same machine producing the same shape.
**Suggested rewrite (one example):** "...the practiced delicacy of someone who had done this many times" → "She had clearly done this many times, and clearly preferred not to think about why."

### [2] Telling-not-showing — Bookwyrm's tell

> She paused — actually paused, mid-step — and something moved across her face before she smoothed it back.

**Why:** "Something moved across her face before she smoothed it back" is generic mystery-novel prose. Daz's auditing-eye would name *what* moved or note *what specifically* he caught — see the chapter-15 example: *"he is detached from reality"* (named, after observation). The two-em-dash *"actually paused, mid-step"* is doing the work; the second clause weakens it.
**Suggested rewrite:** "She paused — actually paused, mid-step — and the question stopped being courtesy and started being a problem to be managed."

### [3] Mild — adverbial pile-up on Fembris

> Fembris, who had been maintaining the politely suppressed energy of a man who desperately wanted to comment on something, finally broke.

**Why:** Another instance of the relative-clause portrait pattern, slightly less load-bearing here. The observation is Daz-shaped (notice-energy-before-action), but the sentence reaches for *politely suppressed energy of a man who desperately wanted* instead of cutting harder.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Fembris had been quietly bursting since the second landing. He finally broke."

## Verdict

The investigative-Daz beats are excellent — *"She delivered it like a gift. Information, freely offered, before anyone had thought to ask. I filed that immediately — not the content, the timing,"* *"That was a gap. Gaps were interesting,"* and the *Everybody that did it says that* held-back line are all canonical. The scene's only systemic problem is the relative-clause "with the particular X of someone who..." construction, which it leans on three times — that's a model tic, not a one-line fix.
