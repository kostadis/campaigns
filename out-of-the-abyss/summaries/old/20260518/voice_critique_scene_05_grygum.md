# Voice Critique — Grygum, scene 05: The Council of Twelve in Uproar

**Narration:** `summaries/20260518/gm-assist-doc.md` (section 5)
**Voice spec:** `voice/grygum_voice.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/grygum.md`

## Flags

### [1] Wrong-narrator vocabulary — "internal ledger"

> She at least had the right priority. I gave her a small point in my internal ledger.

**Why:** *Ledger* is Daz's noun (and Daz's spec includes an explicit warning to "rotate the bookkeeping vocabulary as you narrate ... rather than letting one noun ('ledger') dominate"). Grygum's bookkeeping is *notes* — "I took notes," "I file that," "I noted her for the column marked *capable*" (which appears five paragraphs later, correctly). The two verbs in the same scene contradict each other; *notes* is the Grygum one.
**Suggested rewrite:** "She at least had the right priority. I gave her a small note in the margin."

### [2] Workshop simile

> The council looked at us the way you look at someone who has just correctly identified which window you left unlocked.

**Why:** Stylish but invented — it's the line a clever third-person narrator would write, not a thing Grygum would *think* to himself mid-negotiation. Ben's deadpan is *applied vocabulary* (legal, policy, civics), not built similes. The very next paragraph closes correctly on *"I read the room the way I read a Bahamut text — not for what it says, but for what it is conspicuously not saying,"* which IS Grygum's image-vocabulary; the window-unlocked line should be cut so the Bahamut-text line lands cleanly.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The council looked at us like people who had just realized they were the ones being audited."

### [3] Mild — convergence pattern (see summary)

> The academics looked at one another with the particular expression of people who have never, in their professional lives, been asked to pay for something by the person they were asking.

**Why:** Funny observation, on-voice in spirit, but it's the *fifth* "with the particular [noun] of someone who..." construction in the doc — a model tic the user should be aware of (see summary). Not a re-narrate flag; flagged for pattern-tracking.
**Suggested rewrite:** Leave as-is; just be aware the construction recurs across narrators.

## Verdict

The strongest Grygum scene in the doc — *"Filing a motion in a burning building. I noted that,"* *"I am the head of the Grygumite School. Founder, in fact,"* *"The bow was correct and the eyes were not,"* and *"I did not write down what I thought her expression meant when he asked that. But I took notes"* are all canonical Grygum. The *ledger* slip is the only thing that genuinely breaks character; fix that one line and move on.
