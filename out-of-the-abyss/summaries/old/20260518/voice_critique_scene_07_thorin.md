# Voice Critique — Thorin, scene 07: Investigating the Keeper's Chambers

## Flags

### [1] Voice-bleed — "I filed it"

> That was worth noting. Determined is different from satisfied. Determined means he found something and hadn't decided what to do with it yet. I filed it.

**Why:** *Filed it* is Grygum's verb (and Daz's). Thorin's spec uses **I noted, worth noting, kept it, I clocked that** — see the very next paragraph in this same scene, *"I clocked that and kept moving,"* which is exactly the right register. The two appear two paragraphs apart and the *filed* one breaks. Cheap fix.
**Suggested rewrite:** "That was worth noting. Determined is different from satisfied. Determined means he found something and hadn't decided what to do with it yet. I noted it."

### [2] Slightly awkward observational construction

> He said it the way someone says something when they've just realized they probably should have done both.

**Why:** The "X did Y the way someone does Y when they've just realized Z" recursion is clunky. Thorin's spec is concrete and stone-weight; he'd describe what Tadric *did* — the look on his face, the pause — rather than spin a relative clause around the realization. The Joe-at-the-table verbatim shows this pattern: Joe names the action and the consequence, not the meta-realization.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Tadric heard himself say it. The realization landed on his face. He'd been doing crowd control. He should have been doing both."

## Verdict

The cleanest of the seven scenes — the desk inventory, the tobacco-smoke deduction, the *first thing on a Tuesday* line, the *Gotta interview that dude* / *Corpse will be a little stinky by then. Is there any ice around?* close-out are all canonical Joe-as-Thorin, hedge tics and all. One verb-bleed and one awkward sentence; spot-edit only.
