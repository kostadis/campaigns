**The Drow Emerge from the Shadows**
From the edges of the cavern, five drow warriors of House T'sarran materialized out of the darkness. The insect plague Ilvara had conjured blocked their line of sight deeper into the cavern, but they had been waiting — positioned and ready. A drow elite warrior, Kaelen T'sarran, stepped forward from the darkness, seized Daz by the arm, and pulled him northeast through the stinging insect swarm and away from the worst of the fighting. The warrior made it clear his mission was to keep Daz alive — he was willing to take a face full of poisonous insects to do it. Nym Duskryn worked alongside Kaelen to protect Daz from the chaos.
The sudden appearance of supposed allies who seemed more interested in extracting Daz than fighting Ilvara was bewildering — and immediately suspicious to those watching.

**Drow Crossbow Bolts Rain Down on Daz**
Mike Hall as Daz: "They look a little too smug, and too sure of themselves, I'm sure they're not a good shot, and they missed."
Mike Hall as Daz: "When the first one hits, I immediately cast Shield, because it's a reaction."
Three drow warriors fired crossbow bolts at Daz. His Shield spell flared to life, deflecting the bolts that would have found their mark. The T'sarran drow were not all allies — some had come to kill him.
A moment of instinct and arcane reflex that kept Daz standing when he should have gone down.

---

**Convincing Asha the Newcomers Are Lolth's Servants**
Asha Vandree grew immediately suspicious of the new drow arrivals — their House allegiance, their timing, their interest in Daz. But Daz, leveraging the deception he had already built with Asha — that he was a delusional Lolth-devoted mage — smoothly convinced her that these drow were loyal servants of Lolth, here to assist in the fight against Ilvara.
A lie layered atop a lie, holding together only because Asha wanted to believe it — and because Daz understood exactly which threads to pull.

---

**The Lightning Bolt Streaks Past**
A T'sarran drow mage, lurking in magical darkness deeper in the tunnel, fired a bolt of lightning blindly into the cavern. The crackling energy streaked past everyone — Daz included — without finding a target. The mage had guessed at where Daz stood and guessed wrong.
The near-miss lit up the darkness for a split second, revealing the mage's position to anyone paying attention.

---

**Daz's Fireball into the Darkness**
Seizing the moment — the flash of the lightning bolt betraying the mage's location — Daz unleashed an empowered fireball into the darkness at a heightened level. The explosion lit up the tunnel in a brilliant orange flash, incinerating the T'sarran drow guards outright and leaving the mage barely standing, scorched and staggering.
The tunnel rang with the roar of the blast and fell silent after. Daz had turned a blind shot into a massacre.

**Zalthir's Frustrated Redirect**
Gabe as Zalthir: "How do I not be frightened?"
Kostadis (GM): "You wait a turn... you wait till the end of your turn."
Zalthir, locked in place by Ilvara's supernatural fear, could not approach her no matter how fiercely he willed himself forward. But he realized that while he couldn't move toward Ilvara, he could move in a different direction entirely. He teleported across the battlefield, away from Ilvara and toward the House T'sarran drow, and took his frustration out on the wounded mage — finishing him off with a single decisive punch.
The fear hadn't broken Zalthir — it had just redirected him, and the T'sarran mage paid the price for being the nearest available target.
