## Zalthir — Ilvara's Desperate Stand

The frightened condition is, in theory, simple. You avoid the source of your fear and you wait for it to pass. Clean. Mechanical. Zalthir understood it immediately — and hated it with the particular hatred reserved for things that cannot be argued with.

He had opened this fight by *picking Ilvara up*. Used the Eldritch Claw Tattoo to wrap tentacles around her, hauled her forty feet into the air, hit her twice in the ribs during the ascent, and dropped her. She'd hit stone like something that had stopped mattering. The sequence had lasted perhaps four seconds. He had delivered it with the emotional register of someone pushing a chair under a table.

And then she looked at him. And something in that look — something Zuggtmoy-deep, something that had nothing to do with Ilvara Mizzrym's personal opinion of Zalthir and everything to do with the demon lord wearing her face — *landed*. The fear dropped into him like a stone into still water. No noise, just depth. And suddenly he could not go forward. Could not cross the distance. Every trained instinct read the direction toward Ilvara as simply, absolutely wrong.

He stood there and accepted it.

*I am supposed to be trying to not be near her. Right.*

Not a question. Just the rule, stated plainly, applied against his own interest the way you check a knot even when you know you tied it correctly. He didn't fight the condition. He didn't look for angles. The spirit of it was that he shouldn't close on her, and he knew the spirit of it, and so he didn't. The discipline is self-administered. Always has been.

It was still humiliating.

"How do I not be frightened?" He had asked, because he wanted to know the timeline. One turn. He just needed to wait one turn.

"I'm a monk."

This came out in response to someone noting — correctly, irritatingly — that his entire contribution to a fight was predicated on being in contact with the thing he was supposed to be destroying. The observation landed without malice. It was just the fact on the table. *Yeah. I mean, how do you slap?* No answer to that. A monk frozen by fear is a monk who has no tools. He had the Eldritch Claw Tattoo — fifteen feet of reach — but the distance between him and Ilvara was not fifteen feet. She was busy reshaping the battlefield around herself, throwing up fungal growth, buying room she knew she needed. Even injured. Even howling.

She was still muttering. Something about Zuggtmoy. Something about the bride, and chaos, and the mayhem to come. She said it in the tone of someone who genuinely believed it, which was the worst kind. Not a threat. A prophecy she'd already made her peace with.

He watched from across the cavern and could not move toward her and filed it away and waited.

One turn is a long time when you have nothing to do in it.

The T'sarran mage was still standing. Barely. Daz had already run an empowered fireball through him; the man was singed at the edges and blinking with the stunned expression of someone who had entered this fight expecting an easy kill and received instead a detailed education in why that assumption was wrong. He had fired a lightning bolt into the cavern when he arrived — a dramatic entrance, the kind with real commitment — and hit nothing. Just open air and ambition.

*I shall punch the mage.*

Zalthir said this without particular enthusiasm. He teleported across the battlefield — shadow-step, the distance closing in the space between one breath and the next — and hit the mage once, and that was enough. The man went down. Zalthir straightened.

The fear cleared at the end of his turn, the way it had been promised to. He noted this without celebration.

Ilvara was still on the far side of the cavern, still howling, her voice ragged with the particular desperation of someone who has run out of futures but not yet run out of throat. *Vengeance. Zuggtmoy. The bride.* All of it tumbling out between strikes and retreats. Even cornered, she filled the space with prophecy rather than offers. He could respect the discipline of that, abstractly. She wasn't going to beg.

The mage's body settled on the cavern floor. Behind Zalthir, someone — Grygum, probably — was already moving forward. Someone else was dealing with the insect swarm at the entrance. The fight was contracting toward its end the way fights do, the radius shrinking as each threat resolved.

*We barely knew you, man.* He didn't say this aloud. Just noted it, and turned toward where the remaining work was being done.
