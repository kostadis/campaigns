## Zalthir — Tactical Synergies and Final Decisions

The Darkness spell has always been the problem between Daz and me.

He throws fire. I throw shadow. For months we have been apologizing to each other in the aftermath — *your spell blocked my line of sight, my darkness ate your targeting* — working around it the way you work around a bad step in a tunnel you know well. You learn where to place your foot. You don't fix the tunnel.

This was different.

"If you hold one in place," Daz said, "the daggers keep finding them."

I had been thinking about it too. Cloud of Daggers: a rotating cage of blades, hungry in one fixed point in space. The problem has always been that enemies move. They step out of it, or we drag them out of it by accident, or the fight shifts and the spell becomes decoration. But a grapple — a proper hold, weight-to-weight, the kind that pins something to a specific square of floor — that solved it. The daggers would find them exactly where I left them.

We talked through the geometry of it. The question was whether I would catch myself in my own trap, whether holding something at arm's length meant I'd be eating Daz's spell alongside whatever I was holding. The answer was no. A grapple keeps the target where I put them, but I am not inside them — I am beside them, adjacent, occupying my own space while they occupy theirs. Daz could put his cloud directly on the grappled target and I would walk away without a scratch.

I said: there was a class on this. At the monastery. Something about battlefield geometry and the difference between control and proximity. I think it was Brother Heslin — or possibly that was the one who taught falling. I'm genuinely uncertain. Either way, I remember the light through the shuttered window and thinking the form was taking too long. I did not remember what they said. I am currently learning it from a Drow wizard in a tunnel.

The next thing to work through was the action economy.

Grapple is a quick thing — one motion, a grab, a test of weight and leverage, done in the space of a heartbeat. That leaves the hands. Rope is slower. Tying something takes focus, takes both hands, takes the better part of a turn spent crouching over a target that is probably trying to bite you. The grapple could happen in the spare moment before my main action. The binding would consume the action itself. The whole turn — both pieces of it — spent on one enemy, pinned to the floor, with Daz's daggers already spinning above them.

That was a full turn spent on setup. The next turn, I would be free — and whatever I had tied down would still be there, still bleeding, while I moved to the next problem.

It was not lost on me that "Zalthir ties things to the ground for Daz to cook" is not exactly the heroic narrative I had constructed for myself. I have killed an ettin with my hands. I dragged the Pudding King through his own throne room. And now here I am, discussing the correct knot.

Grygum made a sound. Thorin said something about "the Ember Grapple." Someone — possibly Thorin again, possibly Grygum, it is hard to tell when they are both making noise — found this very funny.

I did not correct it. The name was not wrong.

Thorin mentioned javelins. He had apparently acquired some facility with thrown weapons — a Sharpshooter's instinct, trained in, applied to range — and he wanted something with reach for the next engagement. Something to hold the gap while I was busy tying knots and Daz was busy making things into fire. Practical. I noted it and moved on.

There would be no long rest before whatever came next. I had run the tunnels in my head — the distance we had traveled, the pace, the absence of obvious threats immediately ahead. Whatever we were carrying into the next fight, we were carrying in. Nothing replenished itself on account of wishing.

I turned that over the way I turn most things over: once, completely, and then I put it down. The math was the math. What we had was what we had.

The Ember Grapple.

I let it settle. As names go, I have heard worse. The Ember Vanguard charges in with fire and flash and the announcement of itself. The Ember Grapple holds something still until there is nothing left to hold.

Both accurate. Possibly the same party described from two different positions in the room.
