# Session 2026-04-28

Date: Apr 28th, 2026

## Summary
The party emerged from the Underdark into the warm embrace of the surface world, and for a long moment, they simply breathed in the open air and let the sunlight wash over them. Glabbagool, the peculiar gelatinous ooze who had become an unlikely companion, gazed up at the sky with his googly eyes, visibly moved by the experience. Zalthir watched him quietly, contemplating the strange and winding path that had brought such an unlikely group together, reflecting on how he had — in some sense — offered to teach Glabbagool the ways of a monk. The DM noted that Glabbagool was very much looking forward to the day he would do the "run, run, slap, slap, slap" of a monk's combat style, though nobody had the heart to tell him yet that he moves extremely slowly and taking a single step would be a good start. Dawnbringer, the party's sentient sword, was practically radiant with joy, declaring it the best decision she had ever made to travel with them.

The celebration was bittersweet, however, as Eldeth prepared to part ways with the group. The dwarven warrior spoke with deep sincerity, telling Thorin that they were like brothers who had fought, bled, and faced abyssal horrors together. She explained that she needed to travel to Mithril Hall and Gauntlgrym first, to warn the lords there of the dangers lurking in the Underdark, and asked Thorin to wait before following her. Thorin was clear that he took her at her word — "I'm taking this seriously. I don't think you're just being socially nice here. I think you really do want me to" — and Eldeth responded by swearing on her honor that once she had explained the situation to the lords, Thorin would be embraced by her clan. Dawnbringer threw a small hissy fit at being described as "a really cool sword who's kind of annoying sometimes," which Thorin acknowledged with characteristic diplomacy. Spiderbait, the small and anxious goblin of the group, initially declared his excitement about exploring the surface world freely, confident that goblins were totally integrated into surface society and that nobody would attack him just for being a goblin. The party gently disabused him of this notion, with Grygum noting that the state of goblin equality and inclusion was "rough at the moment." Spiderbait quickly revised his plans and asked to travel with the party to Candlekeep, where he hoped to find quiet, innocuous work as a cook — though the DM noted he is, in fact, terrible at making food.

The journey west was peaceful at first, a welcome contrast to the relentless pursuit they had endured in the depths below. Dawnbringer reveled in every moment of sunlight and starlight, cheerfully narrating her delight. But on the third day, as dusk settled over the forest road, something shifted in the air. Most of the party felt it instinctively — the prickling certainty that they were not alone. Grygum, rolling a 9 on his Insight check, confidently concluded the presence was a deer. Thorin, Daz, and Zalthir, each rolling a 20, 16, and 16 respectively, correctly identified that someone had been following them for some time — and that the tracker was within 20 to 40 feet, coming from the northern area.

Daz moved first, vanishing from the road in a curl of smoke and reappearing high in the branches of a large tree to scout. He spotted a drow figure concealed in the brush below, but the spy's perception was sharp enough to notice him in return — a tense mutual awareness. Daz signaled the party with a prearranged bird call, impersonating a cardinal (a bird the party noted has no business being in these particular trees), and Zalthir acted without hesitation, teleporting directly onto the spy and delivering a swift, precise series of blows — grappling her, striking her a second time for a combined 25 damage, and forcing a failed DC 13 Constitution save that left her stunned. The rest of the party closed in quickly, with Daz climbing down from the tree to tie the spy up while delivering the dry observation that "it's very rude to follow people without just coming up and talking to them, so we'll talk to you now." Thorin loomed over the incapacitated figure, demanding to know who she worked for — though since the spy was still stunned, she simply stared at him blankly.

Once the spy recovered her senses, she was defiant, offering only her piwafwi as a trophy rather than her name, identifying herself as a member of House T'sarran, and declaring that her matron's vengeance would be swift and terrible. Thorin suggested an alternative fate worse than death: letting her go and telling her matron how pathetic she had been, which Grygum correctly noted was "almost the same as killing her." Then Grygum, reading her as a low-ranking agent stuck with a miserable above-ground assignment who was putting on a brave face, took a more disarming approach and casually offered her a job. The spy was visibly confused at the prospect of surviving the encounter at all. Then, without a word of explicit threat, he simply handed her the prayer beads of Bahamut and told her to put them on. The religious implications — the unknown rituals of a faith she associated with a demonic-adjacent perspective given her drow background — sent a visible wave of panic through her, and the defiance in her eyes flickered. Dawnbringer's blinding light held close to her face added to the pressure.

Under this combined intimidation, the spy began to talk. She revealed that her orders had been to track the party to their surface destination and observe who approached them — she had not been sent to engage at all. More importantly, Daz observed through a 27 Insight check that the spy was specifically and carefully trying not to make it obvious that she was only watching him, not the whole party. The spy's Matron Mother had not sent her after Daz himself — she had sent her after whoever had paid to have Daz protected. The mercenaries who had helped the party earlier had not known who employed them. House T'sarran believed this unknown and powerful benefactor would reveal themselves now that Daz had surfaced, and the spy's mission was to observe and report. Daz realized with some discomfort that he was being used as bait: "So basically, you're treating me as bait... you're watching and observing so that when something happens to me, you can go report back what happened to me." The spy confirmed this without apology: "We are looking for the one who paid to have you protected."

The spy proposed a mutual information-sharing arrangement, but Thorin was skeptical: "The minute she has the information she needs, she's off, and we get jack shit." The party briefly entertained the plan of temporarily killing Daz to draw out his protector — Grygum noted he had a Revivify spell, making it "just a death light" — which made Daz visibly uncomfortable. The spy ultimately suggested being escorted to Candlekeep and handed to the guards, implying she already knew how to escape. The party debated this, with Thorin noting she seemed "so confident she can escape" that bringing her into the stronghold was unwise.

After considerable debate, the party arrived at a creative and deeply undignified solution. They produced magical shrinking mushrooms from their supplies, reduced the spy to a tiny, squeaking, furious fraction of her former size, and nicknamed her Polly Pocket. She was placed inside the bag of holding — with holes poked in it so she could breathe "like a hamster" — with Glabbagool assigned as her personal guard. Grygum and Thorin conspicuously mentioned to each other, within the spy's earshot, that the shrunken prayer beads were in fact more potent at their reduced size. The DM noted the indignity was beginning to get to her, with the spy starting to wonder whether there were other fates she might prefer. Thorin offered one small mercy: at least the bag of holding provided shade from the sun. Glabbagool, for his part, took his new responsibility seriously, fixing one eye permanently on the indignant prisoner as the party continued west.

The great fortress-library of Candlekeep rose before them at last. The party learned that entry required the donation of a unique book not already held in its vast collection, and that donating more books would grant them more time with the Avowed — the scholars, sages, and monk-professors who staffed the library. They offered all five of their rare texts: the Velkynvelve Prisoner Journal, Princess Ebonir's spellbook, Ilvara's prayer book with Zuggtmoy marginalia, Asha Vandree's theological argument, and Plinky's Journal. Glabbagool, deeply confused about why anyone would not simply donate every book to be able to ask questions until the end of time, was nevertheless granted his own question to put to the scholars, as the party felt he deserved to feel like a full member of the group.

The First Reader received the Prisoner Journal with barely contained excitement, calling it a masterpiece and noting it as an excellent addition to her existing collection of drow prison journals — an apparently robust genre. Thorin suggested the party could simply be interviewed as primary sources, sparing her the reading. The party also presented their shrunken drow captive as a living exhibit, which prompted the tiny spy to declare "This is highly irregular, and House T'sarran will have its vengeance!" — to which Daz replied dryly, "Tiny, tiny vengeance." Glabbagool was assigned to keep her under control, and demonstrated his authority by suddenly manifesting a large number of eyes, making the situation deeply unsettling for the prisoner. The First Reader efficiently directed the party to their relevant scholars: Zalthir to learn about Jimjar, Thorin and Dawnbringer to Philemon (a specialist in the psychological care of sentient magical artifacts, with a noted track record of success), and noted that the avowed scholar Khell-Vire had specifically expressed interest in Grygum.

Before the party could fully settle in, a frantic elderly half-elf scholar came rushing in, practically quivering, and declared to the First Reader that he could not believe he had not been told about the arrival of a cleric of Bahamut. This was Vareth, whose previously obscure research into half-orc conversions to the worship of Bahamut had become the hottest field in academia following the legendary events of Stroud and Protanther. He grabbed Grygum's hand and practically dragged him toward his office, describing Grygum as "a real living half-orc cleric of Bahamut" and a "living data point," and revealing that he had managed to secure a record of the legendary chess game known as Stroud's Gambit. Grygum confirmed he was familiar with the game, which only intensified Vareth's excitement: "This is all novel news! I can't wait to take advantage of it!" The party reflected on their shared familiarity with Malfire's multi-volume history, which Daz has cited to the point of exhaustion throughout their travels — and Daz mused privately that he has some interest in eventually writing his own account, one designed to obscure certain truths so that the real story can remain larger than the published one.

## Locations

### The Overbright
The surface world above the Underdark, characterized by sunlight, stars, and open air. The party revels in their freedom here after months underground. Dawnbringer is particularly ecstatic. The surface is noted to be deeply uncomfortable for drow like the spy from House T'sarran, who found the above-ground posting miserable.

### Gauntlgrym and Mithril Hall
The ancestral dwarven strongholds where Eldeth intends to return to warn her people of the abyssal threats lurking in the Underdark. Eldeth specifically wants to speak to the lords there before Thorin arrives, so that his unusual background (dwarf raised by giants, traveling with a drow) does not derail the briefing.

### Forest Road
A dirt road through a forested area west of the Underdark exit, where the party attempted to set up camp at dusk and discovered they were being followed. The road runs through terrain the tracker used effectively for cover. Notably, it is not stone — rendering Thorin's Tremor Sense useless, much to his frustration.

### Candlekeep
A legendary, impregnable fortress-library on the Sword Coast. Entry is granted only in exchange for a unique book not already in its vast collection; donating more books earns more time with the Avowed — the scholars, sages, and monk-professors who staff it. The First Reader heads the institution. The DM describes it as "basically an impregnable fortress" and analogizes it to "the Alexandrian Library" in terms of its scope. The party notes that once inside, it will be practically impossible for House T'sarran to send agents after them.

Magical fungi carried by the party from the Underdark, used to reduce the drow spy to a tiny, squeaky, indignant fraction of her former size for transport. The party confirmed they also have a mushroom to return her to full size when needed.

### Bag of Holding
A magical satchel used to carry the shrunken drow captive, with Glabbagool assigned to guard her inside. The party poked holes in it to ensure she could breathe, and Thorin noted that it at least provided shade from the sun.

### Princess Ebonir's Spellbook
A valuable magical tome, available as a copy, donated by the party to Candlekeep as one of their entry offerings. Potentially useful for researching Daz's magical lineage.

### Velkynvelve Prisoner Journal
A detailed account of life within a drow prison, donated by the party to Candlekeep. The First Reader received it with barely contained excitement, calling it "a masterpiece of interesting value" and noting it would complement her existing collection of drow prison journals — apparently a robust genre. Thorin noted the party could serve as primary sources, saving the First Reader some reading.

### Ilvara's Prayer Book
A religious text featuring marginalia related to Zuggtmoy, the Demon Lady of Rot, donated by the party to Candlekeep. The DM noted this book may be particularly relevant to questions about Jimjar.

### Asha Vandree's Theological Argument
A book containing a theological argument connecting Lolth and Zuggtmoy, donated by the party to Candlekeep. The party noted that anything connected to Lolth would be relevant to drow-related questions.

### Plinky's Journal
A journal recovered from the Whirlstone Caverns, written by a figure involved with Demogorgon, detailing experiments in creating demonic monsters. Donated by the party to Candlekeep as one of their entry offerings. The DM identified Plinky as the figure from the Whirlstone Caverns who was writing about how "we're gonna go create a demonic monster."

### Stroud's Gambit
A record of a legendary chess game between Stroud and Protanther, currently held by the scholar Vareth at Candlekeep. He considers it a prized artifact of his research. Grygum's mention of the name by that specific title triggered Vareth's near-collapse of excitement: "This is all novel news!"

### Malfire's Book
A multi-volume historical text containing detailed accounts of past events in the Forgotten Realms, frequently cited by Daz during the party's travels — to the point where the DM notes that Zalthir has perfected his Shadow Step specifically as an escape mechanism whenever Daz begins citing it. Daz privately muses about writing a counter-account designed to obscure certain truths and keep the real story larger than what is publicly known.

## Spells

### Misty Step
Cast by Daz to teleport into the branches of a large tree to scout for the tracker, requiring him to see his landing spot clearly. He vanished in a curl of smoke. He rolled Stealth with advantage and got a 12 (rolling a 1 and an 11), which was enough to beat the spy's Perception roll of 3 — though she noticed his arrival near her position regardless.

### Stunning Strike
Used by Zalthir after teleporting directly to the drow spy's location. The spy failed a DC 13 Constitution save (rolling with a plus-2 modifier), leaving her stunned and unable to act, and giving the party time to close in, tie her up, and begin interrogation. The stun lasted until the start of Zalthir's next turn.

### Revivify
Mentioned by Grygum as a potential means to temporarily kill Daz in order to draw out his mysterious protector — "just a death light," in Thorin's words. The plan was discussed with some enthusiasm by everyone except Daz, and was ultimately not pursued, but remains on what Zalthir called "the maybe list."

## NPCs

### Eldeth
A dwarven warrior who fought alongside the party throughout the Underdark. She departs for Mithril Hall and Gauntlgrym to warn her clan and their lords of the dangers below. She speaks sincerely and at length to Thorin: "We are like brothers. We have fought together, we have bled together, we have faced the abyssal creatures together." She swears: "After I have spoken and explained to the lords of Gauntlgrym and Mithril Hall the dangers of the Underdark, I am certain they will want to speak to you, and I will make it a statement of purpose that you shall be embraced by our clan." She also gives Zalthir a knowing look about the political wisdom of preparing the lords before Thorin's unusual background becomes the main topic of conversation.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent gelatinous ooze with googly eyes — which, he explains, are affectations he adopted because he discovered "if you have these cute eyes, people take you more friendly." He is overjoyed to be on the surface and visibly moved by seeing the sun for the first time. He is fascinated and confused by the concept of a library and cannot understand why one would not simply donate every book and ask questions forever. He is granted his own question to put to the Candlekeep scholars. He serves as the party's guard over their shrunken drow captive, fixing one eye on her permanently, and demonstrates his authority when needed by manifesting a large number of eyes at once, which the DM describes as becoming "very creepy very quickly."

### Spiderbait
A small goblin who has never been on the surface before and is genuinely excited about it — until the party informs him that goblin-surface relations are not as harmonious as he had assumed. He asks to travel with the party to Candlekeep in hopes of finding safe, innocuous work as a cook. The DM notes he is, in fact, terrible at making food but is trying hard to stay attached to the group.

### Drow Spy
An agent of House T'sarran sent to the surface to track Daz and identify his mysterious protector. She is stealthy, defiant, and refuses to give her name, offering her piwafwi as a trophy of her failure. She identified herself as a member of House T'sarran. Under sustained pressure — Zalthir's Stunning Strike and grapple, Thorin's intimidation attempts, Grygum's disarming friendliness, the prayer beads, and Dawnbringer's light — she eventually reveals her mission: to observe and report on whoever paid for Daz's protection. She proposes a mutual information-sharing deal and then suggests being handed to Candlekeep's guards. She is ultimately subdued, reduced to tiny size with a shrinking mushroom (becoming "very squeaky and indignant"), nicknamed Polly Pocket, stored in a bag of holding, and assigned to Glabbagool's custody. The indignity is noted to be getting to her.

### Matron Mother of House T'sarran
The powerful ruler of House T'sarran, said to have held power for 200 years. She is orchestrating the surveillance of the party from afar, specifically seeking to identify the unknown entity that paid for Daz's protection. When Daz claims not to have heard of her, the spy finds this baffling: "You have not heard? Are you not drow?"

### The First Reader
The head of the Avowed at Candlekeep. She is an enthusiastic collector of rare texts, particularly those concerning drow imprisonment, and receives the Velkynvelve Prisoner Journal with barely contained excitement, calling it "a masterpiece of interesting value" that will "go great with my other editions in drow prison journals." She efficiently directs the party to the appropriate scholars for their questions.

### Vareth
An elderly, frantic, and officious half-elf scholar and member of the Avowed. He specializes in half-orc conversions to the worship of Bahamut — a field that was previously so obscure and ignored that colleagues had been ignoring him for years. Since the legendary events of Stroud and Protanther, his research has exploded: he is now receiving funding, conference invitations, and serious academic attention. He rushes in furious that the First Reader did not immediately summon him upon a cleric of Bahamut's arrival, grabs Grygum's hand, and practically drags him toward his office, describing him as "a real living half-orc cleric of Bahamut" and "a living data point." He has secured a record of the legendary chess game, and when Grygum identifies it by name as "Stroud's Gambit," Vareth declares: "This is all novel news! I can't wait to take advantage of it! The field of Bahamut half-orc conversions will benefit tremendously from your interview."

### Philemon
An Avowed monk at Candlekeep who specializes in "psychological preservation and historical cataloging of ancient sentient artifacts" and has a "tremendous track record of success in working through the interpersonal conflict issues of the sentient artifact." He is assigned to work with Thorin and Dawnbringer. Thorin is enthusiastic: "I'm all up for couples counseling with Dawnbringer."

### Khell-Vire
A scholar at Candlekeep who expressed a specific interest in studying Grygum, prompting the First Reader to note that it was "very excellent" that the party had arrived, as Khell-Vire wishes to learn about Grygum specifically — in parallel with Zalthir's own questions about Jimjar.

### Sister Yvenne
A scholar at Candlekeep designated to speak with the party regarding their deeper questions, mentioned by the First Reader when directing the party to their relevant contacts.
