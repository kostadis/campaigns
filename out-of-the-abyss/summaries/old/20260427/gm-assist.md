# Session 2026-04-28

Date: Apr 28th, 2026

## Summary
The party emerged from the Underdark into the warm embrace of the surface world, and for a long moment, they simply breathed in the open air and let the sunlight wash over them. Glabbagool, the peculiar gelatinous ooze who had become an unlikely companion, gazed up at the sky with his googly eyes, visibly moved by the experience. Zalthir watched him quietly, contemplating the strange and winding path that had brought such an unlikely group together. Dawnbringer, the party's sentient sword, was practically radiant with joy, declaring it the best decision she had ever made to travel with them.

The celebration was bittersweet, however, as Eldeth prepared to part ways with the group. The dwarven warrior clasped Thorin's hand and spoke with deep sincerity, telling him that they were like brothers who had fought, bled, and faced abyssal horrors together. She explained that she had to travel to Gauntlygrym first, to warn the lords of Gauntlgrym of the dangers lurking in the Underdark, but she swore on her honor that she would petition the lords to embrace Thorin as one of their own clan. Thorin, moved by her words, made clear he took her promise seriously and intended to hold her to it. Spiderbait, the small and anxious goblin of the group, decided that wandering the surface alone was far too dangerous for a goblin in the current political climate, and opted to tag along with the party toward Candlekeep, hoping to find quiet, innocuous work as a cook.

The journey west was peaceful at first, a welcome contrast to the relentless pursuit they had endured in the depths below. Dawnbringer reveled in every moment of sunlight and starlight, cheerfully narrating her delight to anyone who would listen. But on the third day, as dusk settled over the forest road, something shifted in the air. Most of the party felt it instinctively — the prickling certainty that they were not alone, that someone had been shadowing their steps for some time. Grygum, perhaps because he was hungry for overbrite food, convinced himself it was merely a deer.

Daz moved first, vanishing from the road in a curl of smoke and reappearing high in the branches of a large tree to scout the surrounding forest. He spotted a drow figure concealed in the brush below, but the spy was sharp and noticed him in turn. Daz signaled the party with a prearranged bird call, and Zalthir acted without hesitation, teleporting directly onto the spy and delivering a swift, precise series of blows that left the tracker stunned and grappled before she could react. The rest of the party closed in quickly, and Thorin loomed over the incapacitated figure, demanding to know who she worked for.

Once the spy recovered her senses, she was defiant, refusing to give her name and offering only her piwafwi as a trophy of her failure. She identified herself as a member of House T'sarran and declared that her matron's vengeance would be swift and terrible. Grygum, taking a more disarming approach, casually offered her a job, which left her visibly confused and uncertain about whether she was actually going to survive the encounter. Then, without a word of threat, he simply handed her the prayer beads of Bahamut and told her to put them on. The religious implications sent a visible wave of panic through her, and the defiance in her eyes flickered.

Under the combined pressure of Dawnbringer's blinding light held close to her face and the unsettling implications of the prayer beads, the spy began to talk. She revealed that her Matron Mother had not sent her after Daz himself — she had sent her after whoever had paid to have Daz protected. House T'sarran believed that this unknown and powerful benefactor would reveal themselves now that Daz had reached the surface, and the spy's mission had been to observe and report. Daz, piecing this together, realized with some discomfort that he was being used as bait in someone else's political game. The spy proposed a mutual information-sharing arrangement, but the party remained deeply skeptical of her motives and her ability to keep any such promise.

After considerable debate, the party arrived at a creative and deeply undignified solution. They produced magical shrinking mushrooms from their supplies, reduced the spy to a tiny, squeaking, furious fraction of her former size, and nicknamed her Polly Pocket. She was placed inside the bag of holding with Glabbagool assigned as her personal guard, and Grygum helpfully informed her that the shrunken prayer beads were, in fact, even more potent at their reduced size. Glabbagool took his new responsibility seriously, fixing one eye permanently on the indignant prisoner as the party continued their journey to Candlekeep.

The great fortress-library of Candlekeep rose before them at last, and the party learned that entry required the donation of a unique book not already held in its vast collection. They offered all five of their rare texts — including the Velkynvelve Prisoner Journal, Princess Ebonir's spellbook, Ilvara's prayer book, Asha Vandree's theological argument, and Plinky's Journal — and were welcomed inside with considerable enthusiasm. The First Reader received the Prisoner Journal with barely contained excitement, noting it as a masterpiece for her collection of drow prison journals, and efficiently directed the party to the scholars best suited to their questions. Philemon, a specialist in the psychological care of sentient magical artifacts, was assigned to work with Thorin and Dawnbringer. Then, before the party could fully settle in, a frantic elderly half-elf scholar named Vareth came rushing in, nearly beside himself with excitement at the sight of Grygum. His previously obscure research into half-orc conversions to the worship of Bahamut had become the hottest field in academia following the legendary events of Stroud and Protanther, and a living half-orc cleric of Bahamut had just walked through his door. He practically dragged Grygum toward his office, clutching a record of the legendary chess game known as Stroud's Gambit, and declared that the field of Bahamut half-orc conversions would benefit tremendously from the interview.

## Locations
### The Overbright
The surface world above the Underdark, characterized by sunlight, stars, and open air. The party revels in their freedom here after months underground, though it is deeply uncomfortable for drow like the spy from House T'sarran.

### Gauntlygrym
The ancestral home of Eldeth's dwarven clan, where she intends to return to warn her people of the abyssal threats lurking in the Underdark.

### Forest Road
A dirt road through a forested area west of the Underdark exit, where the party attempted to set up camp at dusk and discovered they were being followed.

### Candlekeep
A legendary, impregnable fortress-library on the Sword Coast. Entry is granted only in exchange for a unique book not already in its vast collection. It is staffed by the Avowed, a group of dedicated scholars and monks who specialize in a wide range of arcane and historical fields.

## Items
### Dawnbringer
A sentient, sun-loving sword carried by Thorin. She is ecstatic to be out of the Underdark and in the sunlight, commenting on Thorin's anger issues during the pursuit of the tracker. Her bright light was used to intimidate the drow captive. The party hopes to find a specialist at Candlekeep to help her work through her psychological issues.

### Piwafwi
A traditional drow cloak offered by the spy as a trophy of her defeat, rather than reveal her name or allegiance beyond her house.

### Prayer Beads
Religious artifacts held by Grygum and presented to the drow captive. Their religious implications caused significant distress to the captive, who feared the unknown rituals associated with them. They were shrunken along with the spy and implied to be even more potent at reduced size.

### Shrinking Mushrooms
Magical fungi carried by the party from the Underdark, used to reduce the drow spy to a tiny size for easier and more secure transport to Candlekeep.

### Bag of Holding
A magical satchel used to carry the shrunken drow captive, with Glabbagool assigned to guard her inside. The party poked holes in it to ensure the prisoner could breathe.

### Princess Ebonir's Spellbook
A valuable magical tome belonging to a drow princess, donated by the party to Candlekeep as one of their entry offerings, potentially useful for researching Daz's magical lineage.

### Velkynvelve Prisoner Journal
A detailed account of life within a drow prison, donated by the party to Candlekeep. The First Reader received it with great excitement, calling it a masterpiece and noting it would complement her collection of drow prison journals.

### Ilvara's Prayer Book
A religious text featuring marginalia related to Zuggtmoy, the Demon Lady of Rot, donated by the party to Candlekeep as one of their entry offerings.

### Asha Vandree's Theological Argument
A book containing a theological argument connecting Loth and Zuggtmoy, donated by the party to Candlekeep as one of their entry offerings.

### Plinky's Journal
A journal recovered from the Whirlstone Caverns detailing experiments involving Demogorgon and the creation of demonic monsters, donated by the party to Candlekeep as one of their entry offerings.

### Stroud's Gambit
A record of a legendary chess game between Stroud and Protanther, currently held by the scholar Vareth at Candlekeep. He considers it a prized artifact of his research into half-orc conversions to Bahamut.

### Malfire's Book
A multi-volume historical text containing detailed accounts of past events in the Forgotten Realms, frequently cited by Daz during the party's travels to the point of annoying his companions.

## Spells

Cast by Daz to teleport into the branches of a large tree to scout for the person following the party, requiring him to see his destination clearly before vanishing in a puff of smoke.

### Stunning Strike
Used by Zalthir after teleporting to the drow spy's location, delivering a powerful blow that left the spy dazed and unable to act, giving the party time to tie her up and begin their interrogation.

### Revivify
Mentioned by the party as a potential means to temporarily kill Daz in order to draw out his mysterious protector, though the spell was never actually cast.

## NPCs
### Eldeth
A dwarven warrior who fought alongside the party in the Underdark. She departs for Gauntlygrym to warn her clan of the dangers below, and sincerely promises to petition the lords of Gauntlgrym and Gauntlygrym to embrace Thorin as one of their own.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent gelatinous ooze with googly eyes who is overjoyed to be on the surface. He is fascinated by the concept of a library and serves as the party's guard over their shrunken drow captive, intimidating her by manifesting numerous eyes.

### Spiderbait
A small goblin who fears for his safety on the surface and chooses to accompany the party to Candlekeep in hopes of finding safe, innocuous work as a cook.

### Drow Spy
An agent of House T'sarran sent to the surface to track Daz and identify his mysterious protector. She is stealthy and defiant, refusing to give her name and offering only her piwafwi as a trophy. She is ultimately subdued, shrunken with magical mushrooms, and transported in a bag of holding under Glabbagool's watch.

### Matron Mother of House T'sarran
The powerful ruler of House T'sarran, said to have held power for 200 years. She is orchestrating the surveillance of the party from afar, specifically seeking to identify the unknown entity that paid for Daz's protection.

### The First Reader
The head of the Avowed at Candlekeep. She is an enthusiastic collector of rare texts, particularly those concerning Drow imprisonment, and efficiently directs the party to the appropriate scholars for their questions.

### Vareth
An elderly, frantic, and officious half-elf scholar and member of the Avowed. He specializes in the niche field of half-orc conversions to the worship of Bahamut. His previously obscure research has become highly sought-after following the legendary events of Stroud and Protanther, and he is thrilled to interview Grygum as a living subject.

### Philemon
An Avowed monk at Candlekeep who specializes in the psychological preservation and counseling of sentient magical artifacts, assigned to work with Thorin and Dawnbringer.

### Khell-Vire
A scholar at Candlekeep who expressed a specific interest in studying Grygum.

### Sister Yvenne
A scholar at Candlekeep designated to speak with the party regarding their deeper questions.
