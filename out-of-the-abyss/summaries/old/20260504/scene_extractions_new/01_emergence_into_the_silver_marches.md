# Emergence into the Silver Marches

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party exits the Underdark and begins their journey across the surface world, starting in the Silver Marches.
- The party emerges from the Blingdenstone surface exit into the Silver Marches during a mid-spring afternoon.
- Eldeth bids the party farewell as they begin their trek across the surface.
- The group begins traveling south through the region, observing the landscape of the surface world for the first time in a long while.
- The DM notes that the travel is structured as a series of short vignettes rather than a full retcon of the geography.

## Verbatim moments

**[GM]** — *explaining the session structure to players*
> "I thought it'd be fun, like, I went through the map and realized that, holy shit, like, where you guys would have come out with Blingdenstone was completely out of the ordinary, and I thought, you know, we could just completely retcon it, but then I got inspired over the weekend. I said, wouldn't it be fun if you guys… we actually did the travel log, we're gonna do it as a series of short vignettes."

**[The Party Emerges — Traveling South Through the Silver Marches]**
- GM describes the party heading south from Blingdenstone's surface exit through war-scarred land.
- The landscape shows signs of destruction from barbarian hordes that swept the region after the War of the Giants.
- The party observes burned hamlets, a broken standing stone, and a desecrated Tempus shrine.
- A refugee family — three generations, one ox, no grandfather — is spotted with a hand cart heading south, sharing the road with the party.

**[GM]** — *describing the landscape and prompting roleplay*
> "As you're walking through this area, you sort of see how this whole area has been destroyed and torn up by barbarian hordes that happened after the War of the Giants. You see burned hamlets, a broken standing stone. Tempus shrine… You also see a refugee family. With a hand cart heading south, three generations, one ox, no grandfather, they share the road with you."
