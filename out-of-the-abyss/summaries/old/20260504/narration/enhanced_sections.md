## Consistency Notes

The following issues from the consistency report could not be silently resolved in the text and are flagged for GM review before the next session.

**Issue 1 — Zalthir's Shadow Step lighting conditions.** The Spells section labels Zalthir's escape from the guard as "Teleportation." Shadow Step (Monk 8, Warrior of Shadow) requires both origin and destination to be in dim light or darkness. The recap does not establish that the street scene occurred in such conditions. The GM should confirm whether this was a ruling, whether the scene occurred at dusk or in a shadowed alley, or whether the spell label should be updated to reflect what actually happened mechanically.

**Issue 2 — Dustsight Spectacles listed under both Zalthir and Daz.** The party document assigns the Dustsight Spectacles to both characters — Zalthir's sheet lists them without qualification; Daz's sheet lists them as "(attuned)." A single item cannot be attuned by two characters simultaneously. The GM should determine which character holds the item, update the party document accordingly, and note the tactical implications (particularly for scenes involving magical darkness).

**Issue 3 — Mending used to repair a full roof.** The recap presents Daz's use of the Mending cantrip to repair the monastery's leaky roof as an elegant, instant resolution. Mending addresses breaks or tears no larger than one foot in any dimension. Repairing a structurally damaged roof would require either many castings over an extended period or a deliberate DM ruling. If this was a ruling, it should be noted as such to prevent the cantrip's scope from being treated as a precedent.

**Issue 4 — Thorin's AC of 22 requires verification.** Standard plate (18) plus a +1 Shield (1) yields AC 19. Reaching 22 requires an additional +3 from sources not documented in the recap or party sheet — possibly a Defense fighting style (+1), a magically enhanced plate armor, or another modifier. The GM should confirm and document the full AC breakdown explicitly.

**Issue 5 — Eldred's second voice compared to Eldeth's.** The Summary and the NPCs section both state that the second voice in Triboar bore an unsettling resemblance to Eldeth's — close enough to unsettle Grygum and Zalthir. The Scenes / Ruins of Triboar section does not include this detail, describing only a familiar quality of madness without the Eldeth comparison. If the Eldeth resemblance was a confirmed player observation (rather than intended foreshadowing to be revealed later), the Scenes section should be updated to match. If it was deliberate dramatic irony not yet surfaced in play, it should be flagged as GM-only information rather than stated as fact in the NPC entry.

**Issue 6 — "War of the Giants" vs. "War of the Dragons" — terminology inconsistency.** The recap uses both terms across different sections without clearly establishing their relationship. The War of the Giants appears to refer to the conflict that restructured the giant hierarchy (the Ordning) and triggered the Uthgardt raids; the War of the Dragons refers to the conflict documented by Milo Goodbarrel and debated by religious factions. Whether these are sequential, concurrent, or geographically distinct events is not established in the text. A brief clarifying note in either the Locations or Scenes section would prevent future terminology drift.

**Issue 7 — Glabbagool in the bag of holding: suffocation and the miniaturized drow spy.** The party document specifies that Glabbagool travels in the bag of holding, which also contains a miniaturized drow spy. The recap describes Glabbagool spending extended time inside the bag during the Waterdeep visit. Two questions remain open: (a) whether grey oozes require air (if not, suffocation is not an issue); and (b) what the current status of the drow spy is, given that the bag is being opened regularly to allow Glabbagool to peer out and receive cheese. The GM should confirm ooze respiration rules and note whether the spy's detention has been addressed, remains deliberately unresolved, or has become an unintentional continuity gap.

**Issue 8 — Drow spy in bag of holding: no mention in recap.** The detained miniaturized drow spy, listed as an active unresolved obligation in the party document, receives no acknowledgment across this session's recap despite the party spending days in multiple populated locations with the bag in active use. The GM should either confirm the spy remains contained and the matter was deliberately deferred, or flag it as a continuity gap to address at the start of next session.

**Issue 9 — Mass Healing Word resource expenditure.** The recap describes Grygum performing "a ceremonial mass healing word for the many injured refugees" in Triboar. Mass Healing Word affects up to six creatures per casting. If the intent was to heal a large crowd, multiple castings were required, consuming 3rd-level spell slots. The GM should confirm whether this was a single symbolic casting (six refugees, atmospheric crowd) or multiple castings, and note the spell slot expenditure accordingly.

**Issue 10 — Thorin/Dawnbringer relationship milestone.** The party document describes the Thorin–Dawnbringer bond as "developing and sometimes contentious." The recap presents the healing-of-orphans scene as a clear point of shared purpose and warmth, with Dawnbringer described as "very happy." If this represents a meaningful shift in the relationship, the party document should be updated to reflect the current dynamic before next session.

**Issue 11 — Dawnbringer's restorative power attributed to Grygum and Zalthir.** The recap states "Grygum and Zalthir — with Thorin's involvement — subsequently spend time in the city's poorer quarters using Dawnbringer's restorative power." Dawnbringer is attuned to Thorin; its Lesser Restoration can only be directed by him. The phrasing implies all three characters were channeling the sword's ability, which is mechanically inaccurate. The intended reading is likely that Thorin used Dawnbringer while Grygum and Zalthir participated through their own means. The GM may wish to clarify this in the session record.

**Issue 12 — Party document current location contradicts end-of-session position.** The party document lists the current location as Candlekeep. The recap ends with the party arriving in Daggerford, with Candlekeep still ahead. The party document should be updated to reflect Daggerford as the end-of-session position, with Candlekeep noted as the next destination rather than current location.

## Scenes
### Emergence into the Silver Marches
#### The party exits the Underdark and begins their journey across the surface world, starting in the Silver Marches.
- The party emerges from the Blingdenstone surface exit into the Silver Marches during a mid-spring afternoon.
- Eldeth bids the party farewell as they begin their trek across the surface.
- The group begins traveling south through the region, observing the landscape of the surface world for the first time in a long while.
- The DM notes that the travel is structured as a series of short vignettes rather than a full retcon of the geography.

### The Road Through the Silver Marches
#### The party travels south from Blingdenstone's surface exit, witnessing the devastation left by the War of the Giants.
- The party observes burned hamlets, a broken standing stone, and a defaced Tempus shrine along the road.
- Grygum performs a basic reverence at the defiled shrine — genuflecting, muttering a blessing, and continuing on — not wishing to see shrines left desecrated.
- The party encounters a refugee family heading south with their meager belongings — three generations, one ox, no grandfather.
- The party learns from a local at a wayhouse about the history of the Uthgardt raids that followed the War of the Giants: a group of adventurers stole religious artifacts from the Uthgardt barbarians, triggering a crusade against the civilized areas of the Silver Marches region.
- The DM explains the hierarchy of giants restored by the War of the Giants — the Ordning, running from Storm Giants at the top, through Cloud, Stone, Fire and Frost, down to Hill Giants at the bottom. The party had previously encountered a Cloud Giant castle.
- A local at a wayhouse notes an unusual quiet in the land — fewer bears this year, pixies not singing in the sown lands — which goes beyond what the war alone accounts for. Grygum, having recently spent time adjacent to the Fey, is immediately suspicious.

### A Detour to Mirabar
#### The party visits the dwarven mining city of Mirabar, finding it crowded with refugees and buzzing with philosophical debate.
- Smoke and red light from the forges are visible from miles out as the party approaches.
- Thorin explores the dwarven city, feeling like a fish out of water despite his heritage — intimidated by its scale and unfamiliar with what it means to be a dwarf in such a place. Eldeth had previously told him about Mirabar.
- The party observes a large refugee population sleeping in shifts at the Smith's Gate. The Smith's Quarter never sleeps — hammer rings all night from the inns above, with half the orders for armor for refugee militia.
- The city held during the Uthgardt raids; the outer farmsteads did not.
- Stroud is ubiquitous in the city: a bronze Stroud bust at the Smiths Guildhall, a tavern named The Knight Beneath the Knight, and a traveling Stroud School polemicist preaching mortal agency in the corner of the market.
- The polemicist immediately spots Grygum and attempts to recruit him: "Brother, have you heard the witness call? Are you walking the road for Stroud?" He identifies himself as of the Stroudite school, rejecting both the Bahamutian hypothesis and the idea that Protanther let events unfold, insisting on mortal agency as the decisive force.
- Grygum replies that he stands for "strength and luck," triggering a debate. He rolls a 6 on his persuasion attempt to win the preacher over; the preacher instead hands him a pamphlet titled *Stroud and the Human Agency Hypothesis: A Critique — Dragons and Gods Do Not Rule*. The pamphlet is well-formed and divided into paragraphs. Grygum keeps it for later, noting at least it is not a wall of text.
- Grygum gets the sense that there is some kind of factionalism in the Bahamutian religious culture surrounding the person of Stroud and the events around Protanther.
- The party visits an Order of the Gauntlet shrine featuring a skulled bronze frontispiece reproduction of Milo Goodbarrel's Account, Volume 1. Two half-orcs tend the shrine in shifts.
- On the back wall of the shrine hangs a large painting, nearly full height, titled *Malfire, the Vindicator of Nature*: a figure in weathered green at the edge of a forest, longbow lowered, an enormous gray wolf at his side, stars rising over his shoulder. The painting is by Milo Goodbarrel.
- Thorin considers upgrading his equipment; the DM notes that as a dwarf in a dwarven city, this is the place to do it, and suggests he research what armor would be appropriate for his build.

### The Ruins of Triboar
#### The party arrives in the war-torn town of Triboar, witnessing the aftermath of the barbarian raids and hearing rumors of a mysterious Kenku ranger.
- The party arrives in Triboar, a caravan economy town hot from the rebuild. One-third is still in ruins, with memorial stones in the central square.
- Past the memorial square, before an inn with recently laid flagstones, stands a cairn of silver fieldstones naming the Triboar dead: 243 names.
- The town is crowded for its size — caravans staging, traders, pilgrims, Concordant Order knights, the occasional Lord's Alliance courier, those carrying rebuild permits — the party blending into the flow.
- The party overhears every trade route rumor for the last 200 miles. Drovers, teamsters, and outriders speak of a shadow figure haunting the caravan trails north of Yartar: a Kenku hooded in black, hovering a foot off the ground, appearing at a distance and gone before anyone can approach. The image is described as consistent across all accounts.
- One drover provides context: the figure is the Ardragon, formerly a Zhentarim route-flyer who used to steal anything not nailed down — then something happened out east, and now he stops banditry, takes nothing, doesn't speak, mimics a hawk, and rides the next thermal.
- Another voice adds: "We don't pay him. We leave a coin on a rock, and he never picks it up."
- At the Mountain's Mouth inn, a half-orc named Kessler who keeps a small Bahamut shrine sees the party and his face lights up: "You're going south! You oughta know, there's a temple! The brother who keeps it stopped writing five years ago. I keep a small house down the road from him. I have not seen him in three years. The temple fire still burns. I don't know what that means. I would very much like to know." He asks them to carry a written note to the brother. Grygum immediately agrees, calling it an omen.
- Thorin buys a drink for a local man at the inn who is talking to himself in two voices — his own and a younger voice that answers questions the first voice never asks. Another patron sitting nearby watches the man with a strange expression.
- A neighbor at the bar explains: "That's Eldred. He ran courier, Triboar to Sundabar in the war, came back from his last run two summers past, and he was both of them. We feed him, and we don't ask."
- Grygum and Zalthir, both rolling above 14 on insight, notice that the second voice sounds like Eldred's but doesn't — something is his and not him, a quality of madness that is familiar. Grygum recognizes it as the same kind of madness the party witnessed throughout the Underdark, and realizes that whatever was festering below is bleeding through into the surface world.
- Grygum considers using magic to investigate further but decides instead to simply observe from the sidelines, noting that Eldred seems to be having quite a successful conversation with himself.
- Glabbagool is gifted an entire wheel of cheese by the innkeeper as a curiosity. The ooze takes the wheel, dissolves it entirely, and declares: "This is very good." He then pauses and asks with genuine curiosity whether dairy is bad for people. This is his first encounter with cheese. Throughout the stay, he continues pestering Zalthir about shadow monk training and periodically attempts to demonstrate walking — painful to watch, but enthusiastic.
- Grygum performs a ceremonial mass healing word for the many injured refugees in Triboar, drawing the attention of local Bahamutian clerics who immediately want to know his position on the only question of the age.
- Grygum's response — "Are we not all the playthings of fate?" — causes the Bahamutian faction to erupt with enthusiasm ("Exactly! That's exactly what we believe!"), while a third of the audience leaves frustrated and another third cries "Deist!" The remaining third clusters around him warmly and wants to continue the conversation.
- Grygum engages the enthusiastic faction further, noting that the absence of a gigantic chessboard makes one wonder whether the gods play games with mortals. One of them warns that this is "heretically close to the Protanther Doctrine," but Grygum deflects: "But the differences between Bahamut and Protanther are Legion."

### Arrival in Waterdeep
#### The party reaches the massive metropolis of Waterdeep, where they encounter statues of legendary figures and face the city's prejudices against oozes.
- The party enters Waterdeep, with Glabbagool forced to remain hidden inside a bag due to the city's intolerance of oozes. Thorin considers taking a stand in solidarity; the DM acknowledges the moral outrage but notes the practical consequences.
- Glabbagool peers out of his bag with two small eyeballs. He is allowed out occasionally to eat cheese, but must minimize that activity for the duration of the visit.
- They observe a larger-than-life bronze statue on Castle Road depicting the famous chess match between Stroud and Protanther, rendered in inlaid marble, with a small brass plaque reading: *This is the position. The move is yours.*
- Grygum admires it, noting the game has been studied for years, and muses: "If only Protanther had moved his Rook, all could have gone another way" — immediately discovering that any expressed opinion draws a crowd of passionate strangers eager to debate the question of the age.
- Thorin acquires mithril plate armor during the party's time in the city, bringing his armor class to 22.
- Grygum expresses interest in a javelin of lightning and wants to visit a Bahamut shrine or temple, as well as something adjacent to Bahamut for recruiting purposes.
- The party is told that Waterdeep is effectively a place where they can buy almost anything, but it is expensive — compared by Grygum to New Jersey.

### Literary Debates at Rishaal's Pageturners
#### Daz visits a bookstore in Waterdeep to acquire the latest volume of a famous historical account, leading to an encounter with passionate fans.
- Daz visits Rishaal's Pageturners to purchase Volume Three of Milo Goodbarrel's Account of the War of the Dragons. He previously had Volumes 1 and 2.
- Above the desk hangs the framed original painting from Milo's last book signing — the artwork that made Volume Three a hit.
- Rishaal explains: Milo delivered Volume 3 nine months ago; sales had declined when he came to town for the signing, but the painting brought them back. "Fat painting saved the print run! Milo finished it two days before the signing. We had it framed by boarding. People come here for the painting and left with a book."
- The painting depicts Stroud, Milo, Malfire, and Whistler — the Kenku hooded in black, hovering a foot off the ground, beak half-shadowed under the hood, one hand raised in mimicry as if quoting a line of metallic dragon speech to the others.
- Rishaal describes the "real-world fiction enthusiasts" as "exasperating, but they buy three copies of everything."
- A group of those fans is audible at the back of the shop, arguing animatedly about a passage in Volume 2 — specifically whether Whistler could have flown from Triboar to a given location in two days, citing the Weber Report's account of wind conditions over ten days. Daz engages with the debate.
- The fans are also split on Whistler's redemption arc: half believe Milo's account of his morally confused post-lycanthropy stage is too generous; the other half believe Malfire's "operational expenses, cost of living" framing is a philosophical breakthrough about the impossibility of pure heroism. Grygum observes that the book also has an unreliable narrator.
- The party discovers a sign on the wall: *Whistler, Kenku of the First Faction, fallen at Glaziel's lair, the year of the Alliance, redeemed in life and death. May his silence speak.* Some factions have decided it is convenient to believe Whistler died at the end of the war. Daz absorbs this in silence.

### Shadows and Shakedowns in Waterdeep
#### Zalthir seeks training in the shadow arts while the party encounters a prophetic street preacher.
- Zalthir seeks out shadow monk training in Waterdeep and learns that the best place to pursue it is Candlekeep — specifically, a master there named Khell-Vire, described as "the best of the best."
- A local monastery offers to provide a letter of introduction to Khell-Vire, but pauses and mentions that the monastery has a leaky roof — a transparent shakedown. Zalthir asks whether 140 gold worth of his time fixing the roof would do instead; the monks explain that donation money and operational money are different things.
- Zalthir rolls a persuasion check of 4 against the monastery's 18; the price lands at 140 gold pieces.
- Zalthir pulls Daz over to argue. Grygum notes that if it were Whistler, he would have stolen the roof tiles. Daz resolves the situation by using the Mending spell to repair the leaky roof outright, bypassing the monetary demand. The monks, somewhat sheepishly, provide the letter.
- In the Field Ward, the party encounters a ragged street preacher addressing any crowd that passes, chanting: "The kraken under the keep! The kraken under the keep! The chant will break, and the kraken will rise!"
- Thorin rolls a 16 on insight and gets the distinct impression the preacher is right about something he cannot name.
- The preacher spots Daz as a drow and breaks his chant: "You came up! You came up, you will go again. You will go down again. Do not—" before cycling back into the repeating chant.
- Thorin notices the preacher's broken, looping speech rhythm matches that of the dual-voiced man in Triboar. The pattern is becoming harder to ignore.
- Grygum notes that Candlekeep may be a place to seek answers about the kraken reference, since the word "keep" is suggestive.

### The Ooze Rights Confrontation
#### A routine city guard inspection in Waterdeep turns into a philosophical debate regarding the sentience of oozes and magical items.
- Over the course of several days, Glabbagool remains largely unnoticed in his bag — but eventually a city guard insists on inspecting it.
- Thorin takes an immediate stand for ooze rights, arguing that sentience is sentience and deserves respect regardless of form.
- Dawnbringer, the sentient sword, supports the argument directly: "One day they're going to ban oozes, the next day they'll ban sentient swords!"
- Thorin and Dawnbringer affirm they are on the same page: "What, one day they're going to ban oozes, the next day they'll ban sentient swords!"
- The guard, realizing he is dealing with what appears to be a group of very committed and possibly confused individuals, is bewildered. He asks whether Thorin needs to be taken to a cleric. Grygum notes he is a cleric. Dawnbringer offers to perform lesser restoration.
- While Thorin has the guard's full attention, Zalthir teleports away with the bag. The guard notices the bag is gone and tells Thorin he thinks he may want to walk away.
- Thorin finishes his coffee and walks away.
- The confrontation draws a crowd of mixed reactions — some nodding in agreement, some cautious — planting seeds of a new idea in the city.
- Grygum and Zalthir — with Thorin's involvement — subsequently spend time in the city's poorer quarters using Dawnbringer's restorative power to heal orphans and the sick. This was Thorin's idea: he wanted to use the Lesser Restoration to find orphans and heal them, as a way for him and Dawnbringer to bond over helping others. Dawnbringer is described as very happy about this.

### Onward to Daggerford
#### The party wraps up their time in Waterdeep and continues south toward Daggerford.
- The party hears persistent rumors and legends about the Ardragon and Whistler as they continue south.
- Grygum confirms his interest in purchasing a javelin of lightning, pasting a reference link for the DM. The DM confirms this is available.
- The party arrives at the settlement of Daggerford as they continue their journey south, with Candlekeep and its master Khell-Vire still ahead.
- The DM indicates that events in Daggerford will be continued in the next session, noting there is more to come there — including at least one overheard story that was pulled back before being revealed.
