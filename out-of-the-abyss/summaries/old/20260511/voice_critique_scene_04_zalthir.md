## Flags

### [1] Voice spec conflict — sentence length, missing terseness

> The spy, for her part, was furious. She turned to Kalan and announced that she was a prisoner and wished to be formally jailed by Candlekeep's guards, delivered with the kind of imperious certainty that I imagine comes naturally when you spend your life in a society that runs on hierarchy and has no framework for being three inches tall and thoroughly outmaneuvered.

**Why:** Zalthir's spec is "sentences are short and stacked. He notices the world before he notices himself in it." This is one sentence at 53 words, with a four-clause subordinate tail that *speculates* about the spy's social conditioning. Zalthir observes and files; he doesn't theorize the sociology behind a tantrum. The tail also reads as a different narrator's voice (Daz could write this; Zalthir wouldn't).
**Suggested rewrite:** *The spy was furious. She turned to Kalan and announced that she was a prisoner and wished to be formally jailed by Candlekeep's guards. She delivered this the way someone delivers a thing they have never had to argue for.*

### [2] Workshop simile — fortune-cookie ending

> Seeing it in person was different. It just sat there, enormous and settled, the way things that have been collecting knowledge for centuries tend to sit.

**Why:** The "the way things that have X tend to Y" construction is a workshopped formulation, not Zalthir's. His example file opens *"Velkynvelve was a Drow outpost carved into a cavern wall a hundred feet above the floor"* — concrete, no flourish. The spec line that matches this beat is *"The air changed first. It thinned in a way that had nothing to do with depth; it simply became something else."* — terse, observed, no rhetorical tail.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Seeing it in person was different. It sat there. Centuries do that to a building.*

### [3] Out-of-character meta-wink

> Bookwyrm — the First Reader, a dragonborn sage, and yes, I noticed the name — assigned us each to a handler without ceremony.

**Why:** Zalthir does not wink at the reader. His spec is explicit: "He never explains his reasoning unless someone's about to make a fatal mistake. He never raises his voice." The "and yes, I noticed" is a knowing aside to the audience — a Daz or Thorin move, not Zalthir's. His register would either silently note the name or not mention it at all.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Bookwyrm — the First Reader, a dragonborn sage — assigned us each to a handler without ceremony.* (Drop the aside entirely; the name does its own work.)

## Verdict

Strongest scene in the doc for voice fidelity — the *"I noticed. I didn't say anything yet"*, *"He said it plainly, which I appreciated"*, *"Keep at it, buddy"*, and the Brother-Ferrath-or-possibly-Brother-Aldas callout all hit the spec exactly. The three flags are the sentences where the narrator forgets to stay terse. Spot-edit.
