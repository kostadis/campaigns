## Flags

### [1] Convergence with Daz's vocabulary — "recalibrates"

> There was a pause — the kind where someone recalibrates something they thought they already had figured out.

**Why:** *Recalibrate* is in Daz's spec ("a recalibration event") and is one of his signature reframing words — the audit-register noun for an embarrassment Daz won't name as embarrassment. Putting it in Thorin's mouth borrows the wrong narrator's vocabulary. Thorin would name the pause concretely (per spec: *"stone-weight language, things land, strike, hold or break"*).
**Suggested rewrite:** *There was a pause. The kind where someone resets the question in their head before they say the next thing.*

### [2] Cliché simile — "the way a healer's eyes go straight to a wound"

> He came around from behind his desk when we entered, and his eyes went straight to the sword at my hip the way a healer's eyes go straight to a wound.

**Why:** Stock fantasy simile. The image is borrowed from a different writer's toolkit; Thorin's similes when they work (*"like I've just knocked over a shelf that he had carefully organized in his mind and the pieces are falling and he's watching them fall"* in his example file) are *load-bearing and specific to the situation*, not generic figures. A healer-and-wound simile doesn't tell us anything Thorin actually saw.
**Suggested rewrite:** *He came around from behind his desk when we entered. His eyes went to the sword before they went to me.*

### [3] Voice spec conflict — too-smooth explanatory prose

> He explained it the way scholars explain things: carefully, and with a slight wobble in the middle where the concept doesn't quite fit the words available.

**Why:** This is observation-about-other-people in a register Thorin's spec actively shapes him *away* from — *"He buries feelings in observations. He doesn't write about loyalty; he writes about the move he's making because of the loyalty."* Thorin would render the wobble by paraphrasing Philemon's actual stumble, not by characterizing the genre of scholarly speech in the abstract.
**Suggested rewrite:** *He explained it carefully. He hit a spot in the middle where the words he had didn't quite carry the thing he was reaching for, and he slowed down.*

## Verdict

The *"I'd been doing it since Velkynvelve. Old habit"* beat and *"I accepted this the way I accept most things I've already decided to do: by not arguing about it"* are dead-center Thorin and worth keeping intact. The flags are all places where the prose forgets that Thorin renders feeling through *what he does next*, not through smooth observation of other people. Spot-edit.
