## Flags

### [1] Cliché / generic-fantasy headache description

> My head had been complaining since we arrived at Candlekeep. Something about the proximity — to all this knowledge, or to something underneath it — that pressed against the inside of my skull with a dull, insistent weight.

**Why:** "Pressed against the inside of my skull with a dull, insistent weight" is reusable fantasy-headache stock prose — could appear in any character's narration. Daz's audit register handles his own body the way it handles everything else: precise, categorical, almost clinical. The voice spec is also explicit that "fear surfaces as cold logistics, never as a stated emotion" — Daz would describe the headache by what it interfered with, not by how it felt.
**Suggested rewrite:** *My head had been complaining since we arrived at Candlekeep. The proximity — to the knowledge, or to whatever sat underneath the knowledge — was producing a friction I had been mis-attributing to the sun all day.*

### [2] "Ledger" vocabulary drift — three uses, against spec

> Struck is a ledger entry someone paid to have removed from the column it belonged in.
>
> I have been running that ledger unconsciously since before I could name it.
>
> She said nothing. / Which is, in the ledger, an answer.

**Why:** The voice spec carries an explicit author's note: *"rotate the bookkeeping vocabulary as you narrate — audit, tally, account, column, balance, debt, the running list — rather than letting one noun ('ledger') dominate a scene or the document."* This scene uses *ledger* three times, and the same scene is doing the audit work the spec is most worried about. The closer (*"Which is, in the ledger, an answer"*) is the most load-bearing instance and worth keeping; rotate the other two.
**Suggested rewrite:** Keep the closer as-is. For the earlier two: *"Struck is what you do to a record when you want it gone but it won't cooperate with being gone. Struck is the column above the column — someone paid to move the entry out of one ledger and into another."* and *"I have been running that audit unconsciously since before I could name it."*

## Verdict

Strongest-voiced scene in the doc alongside Zalthir's prisoner-question scene. The three-paths-to-handle-the-information passage, the *Daz'issin* footnote, *"Yvenne's body had been disagreeing with her words for the last five minutes"*, and the final *"Which is, in the ledger, an answer"* are all dead-center Daz. The two flags are small: rotate the bookkeeping noun per author's note and replace one generic-headache sentence. Spot-edit, no re-narration needed.
