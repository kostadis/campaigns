# Chapter 52 Candlekeep: Trials, Truths, and Therapy

Date: May 11th, 2026

## Summary
The party arrived in Daggerford during their journey south toward Candlekeep, and the town greeted them with an unsettling quiet. The townsfolk moved carefully through the streets, offering no explanation for their wariness, and the group settled in for the night at the River Shining Tavern. Before they did, they passed through the village square and found a bronze cast of a severed forearm and hand mounted atop a stone pillar — a monument to Travis Houlin, a paladin of Bahamut who had died three years prior fighting the Cult of the Dragon. A Dwarven inscription on the base read "The move is yours," mirroring a phrase tied to the legendary figure Stroud back in Waterdeep's Castle Ward, and a smaller inscription on the back noted the monument had been witnessed and recorded by Milo Goodbarrel — head of his own publishing house and a member of the Order of the Gauntlet — who personally signed off on the commission in his capacity as a kind of artistic director for the Order's commemorative work. The local village priest arrived soon after, selling small pewter reproductions of Travis's hand cast to raise donations, and explained that the monument had been funded by the Metalworkers Guild — possibly with Stroud-school sponsorship — though he himself declared proudly that he was a "pure Bahamutian" and did not believe in the heresy of the Stroudites. When pressed about why a Dwarven Stroudite inscription had been allowed to remain, he conceded that since the casting had been a gift, the inscription could stay — after all, wasn't Travis trying to save everyone, regardless of what happened in that game of chess?

That evening at the River Shining Tavern, a weaver named Meredith of the Ford approached the party with desperation in her eyes. Her seven-year-old daughter Ellen had been silent for six days, drawing the same shape on every flat surface she could find, and speaking only in her sleep — saying that it was only the shape that held. The party attempted to examine the girl, failing to identify the cause medically, and Dawnbringer, Thorin's sentient sun-sword, pompously declared she would bring the light to bear before performing a lesser restoration. The cure was only partial: Ellen spoke briefly, uttering the haunting words "The wells are not wells anymore," before falling silent once more. The party concluded that this was no ordinary disease — something was twisting the girl's mind from within, causing auditory hallucinations that no conventional healing could reach. Grygum noted that what was happening felt interior rather than exterior, something that could not be slapped or grappled away, and the party recognized the madness of the Underdark as appearing to seep into the surface world.

The road south brought the party to Beregost, the last meaningful stop before Candlekeep, where they took shelter at the Burning Wizard Inn. The gnome innkeeper, Festroon, recounted the inn's grim namesake — a wizard burned alive during a dragon cult uprising decades past — and pointed to a permanent soot mark on the south wall, refusing to paint over it and reciting his grandfather's words: "The wall remembers." A traveling bard performed that evening, with her closing number for the season being a four-verse drinking song about a Kenku thief who had learned to steal no more, with the whole inn singing along on the chorus of "Black Hood." After Thorin pressed a silver piece into her hand at Grygum's request, she sang a winding love song about Stroud and the famous game of chess — winding it, as Grygum observed with gentle cynicism, into the usual claptrap about love, emotions, flowers, and drinking. Meanwhile, Daz noticed something deeply unsettling about a young human scholar nearby: the man was reciting a chant, and while the words were almost right, his breathing and cadence were wrong by a hair — the same eerie, syncopated pattern Daz had observed in Eldred, a man who had gone insane earlier in their adventure, and in a Field Ward preacher. He quietly took note of the chant, intending to bring it to the scholars of Candlekeep. Thorin sat near the sea that night, contemplating how strangely life moved and deciding simply that you just had to go with it, while Zalthir climbed to the inn's roof to meditate and found himself curiously — and perhaps not entirely disappointingly — at peace with nothing interrupting him.

The party arrived at Candlekeep the following morning, traveling along the Way of the Lion as the great fortress-library came into view from miles away along the cliffs above the Sea of Swords. As they drew closer, Daz noticed that the pressure behind his eyes — a familiar Underdark-linked ache he had been carrying for some time — had grown sharper since leaving Beregost, intensifying with each step toward the keep. Grygum confirmed that something tenuous seemed to connect Daz's headache to what had been afflicting Ellen, though she lacked the knowledge to name it. Inside, they were received by Bookwyrm, the First Reader — a dragonborn sage who had clearly heard every joke about her name and was entirely unbothered by it. She assigned each member of the party to a specific mentor within the library, but before she dismissed them, the Gate Warden Callan Strongbench was summoned to address the matter of the shrunken House T'sarran spy currently residing in the party's bag of holding. Callan explained that Candlekeep did not keep prisoners — they held them in comfortable, localized anti-magic bell tower cells and then exiled them at the gate with a warning rather than delivering them back to their house or executing them — and left the decision of what to do with her entirely to the party.

The miniature drow spy was furious, demanding to be restored to her proper size and handed over to the Candlekeep guards. Glabbagool, the sentient grey ooze who had been serving as her jailer, cheerfully explained that she kept trying to kill him, so he would simply expand to trap her whenever she ran, and then attempt to teach her meditation — and that they had, in his estimation, become great friends. When Thorin asked what exactly she provided in the way of entertainment, Glabbagool clarified that she kept attacking him, he would slap her around, she would try to escape, he would expand to catch her, she would scream at him, and he would try to explain the way of the shadow monk — and then she would scream about the meditation, and then they would talk. Zalthir confirmed that watching the prisoner was part of Glabbagool's pre-monk training, which Glabbagool took with great seriousness, solemnly asking Zalthir whether he would be more or less of a monk for watching her. Zalthir confirmed it was absolutely pre-monk training. The party ultimately decided to leave her in Glabbagool's many-eyed care, and Glabbagool fixed all six of his pseudo-eyes on her to demonstrate his commitment.

Zalthir was taken to the Echoing Hall in the north inner ward of the history library, where he met Brother Khell-Vire — a 200-year-old half-elf who had left the Garden of Shadow eighty years ago after refusing an order and now wore a faded shadow monk under-robe beneath the avowed gray of Candlekeep. On Khell-Vire's desk lay a cryptic note reading "Zalthir, the Underdark, until the Bone King sings," the meaning of which neither of them could explain. Khell-Vire challenged Zalthir with a cryptic monk-style question — "What were you sent toward?" rather than "Why were you sent here?" — before rephrasing it more gently when Zalthir's patience with the oblique approach showed its limits, asking whether the phrase meant anything to him through lived experience. It did not. Khell-Vire sent Zalthir to the Trial of the Broken Mirror, stating plainly: "I will not be there. This is your work, not mine."

A blind half-elf monk tested Zalthir's shadow stepping and martial forms, immediately criticizing him for skipping the third breath and letting his ki bleed into his fingertips like a poison — and then pausing to note that Zalthir had arrived at his destination three seconds faster than the grandmaster. Zalthir proceeded to fail every formal demonstration of skill that followed, rolling badly on acrobatics, athletics, and stealth alike, leading the monk to nearly dismiss him entirely and walk away in disappointment — at which point Zalthir landed a devastating strike on the departing monk that left him genuinely stunned, prompting the admission that "perhaps not all is lost." The monk set a final challenge: shadow stepping to intercept and smash a thrown brick mid-air. Zalthir succeeded, shattering the brick completely. The monk declared that while Zalthir's forms were wrong in every scroll — still skipping breaths, still not entering his ki in the hara — his combination of shadow stepping and striking was something special that redeemed everything else. Zalthir then demonstrated his shadow tentacles and eldritch tattoo, which the monk found less impressive, but agreed that combining those two elements into a single strike in an interesting way was the next frontier. A sage of xenobiology wandered over to ask whether a mindless digestive system could truly achieve enlightenment, and Zalthir defended Glabbagool's intelligence and progress with conviction, noting that he was by no means mindless, was already walking, and was already admirably tormenting the pocket drow. The party received a vial of resonant slime from the sage. Brother Khell-Vire ultimately acknowledged Zalthir's unconventional mastery and granted him access to a specialized shadow technique — offering three options, from which Zalthir chose the third.

Thorin was brought to Master Archivist Philemon, a clinical specialist in sentient artifacts who immediately began assessing Dawnbringer with the detached enthusiasm of a scholar examining a rare specimen — a framing Thorin immediately pushed back on, clarifying that this was his friend Dawnbringer, not a specimen. Philemon identified her as a late Netherese-era artifact of sun worship origin that had suffered profound arcane stress from millennia of darkness exposure, and noted with some surprise that the bond between Thorin and the blade was stronger than he had feared. When Philemon asked Dawnbringer directly about the wielder she had been found with — meaning the corpse in which she had been discovered — the sword faltered and went silent. Philemon gently reframed what the blade needed: not polishing (which, he realized midsentence, was not even physically possible for her), but the chance to remember what daylight felt like without needing to fight — and then, when she was ready, to grieve. He outlined a two-phase recovery process, beginning with the work of light and moving to the work of mourning, and Thorin found himself unexpectedly signed up for something that felt far more like therapy than archival research.

Daz was taken to the Hall of Divination, where spectral mage hands drifted through the air fetching tomes for researchers, and where Sister Yvenne — a meticulous and authoritative keeper of the public record — waited for him. She established that information in Candlekeep was exchanged reciprocally, and Daz told her everything: the amnesia, the jail, the Underdark, the mercenaries trying to protect him and the ones trying to kill him, and the headache that had been growing worse since he arrived. Yvenne explained the brutal politics of Drow noble houses — the matron mothers, the rule of intrigue, the law that betrayal was upward-rewarded if it strengthened Lolth's web, and crucially the rule that if even one member of a targeted house survived, the attacking house was forfeit and destroyed. She illustrated this with a story of a noble house that tried to wipe out a rival and left a "blithering idiot child" alive, only for the rest of the drow community to come and tear the attacking house to the ground, leaving a black puddle behind. Daz asked what happened to the surviving child. Yvenne did not know.

She then read him a list of houses struck from the record — the second seat, the fourth seat struck, the eleventh seat, and the twenty-third seat — and Daz, rolling an exceptional investigation check, noticed that the word "struck" was strange and out of place. It implied censorship rather than elimination. Yvenne admitted there was one house she could not speak of in that room, not because she refused, but because the public record was sealed and she was the public record: "If you want what is behind the seals, you will have to want it through other doors." When Daz asked for the full list of houses that had ever existed, she handed him a document — but the word "struck" appeared again, and she noted that the fourth seat held ritual archive privileges in Menzoberranzan, making a struck-fourth seat a very political and expensive erasure. Digging deeper into a two-hundred-year-old heraldry book, Daz discovered that an entire appendix had been mechanically removed with a razor at least fifty years ago — a cut, not natural wear. A surviving footnote referenced the name "Daz'issin," which Yvenne explained was a softening name that mothers used for boys they wanted to live, and that the houses which had used it most had not lasted. As Daz turned the implications over in his mind — the sealed records, the razored appendix, the mercenaries, the name — he began to sense that he was piecing together something far more dangerous than a forgotten past. Yvenne's careful silences and precise body language told him he was close to something she could not say aloud, and the session ended with Daz pushing gently: "But what if they weren't successfully all gone?" The truth of who he was, and why someone had gone to such lengths to erase it, waited somewhere behind a door that Candlekeep itself could not open for him.

- Grygum agrees to examine the girl; Daz offers to help, providing advantage on the medicine check — but both roll poorly (a 10 and a 7 respectively), leaving the diagnosis inconclusive beyond "she is not well."

- The party determines that the madness previously seen in the Underdark seems to be bleeding into the surface world, affecting people's ability to process what they hear rather than causing a conventional illness.
- Daz checks whether there is any connection between Ellen's condition and the growing pressure in his own head; Grygum rolls a medicine check (23) and confirms a tenuous connection, though she lacks the knowledge to name it precisely.

### Zalthir's Trial of the Broken Mirror
#### Zalthir meets with Brother Khell-Vire and undergoes a martial test against a blind monk to prove his unconventional skills.
- Zalthir is taken to the Echoing Hall in the north inner ward of the history library, to Calvier's lower gallery office where the Shadow Discipline Lineage Records are kept.
- Khell-Vire is a 200-year-old half-elf who left the Garden of Shadow 80 years ago after refusing an order. He wears a faded shadow monk under-robe beneath the avowed gray of Candlekeep.
- On Khell-Vire's desk, Zalthir sees a note reading: "Zalthir, the Underdark, until the Bone King sings." Neither Zalthir nor Khell-Vire can explain it.
- Khell-Vire opens with a cryptic monk question — "What were you sent toward? Not why were you sent here?" — which Zalthir finds immediately annoying in the familiar way of all those shadow monk classes he ignored. When Zalthir asks for clarification, Khell-Vire rephrases: "Does this phrase mean anything to you that you have lived through that resonates with it?" It does not. Khell-Vire tries: "Is it an entity? An event? A person?" Zalthir replies: "That is precisely what I am asking you."
- Khell-Vire declines to answer and instead states: "There are monks here in the inner ward who have read everything and fought nothing. They will want to test you. Let them. You will learn what you do not see, and they will learn what cannot be read. I will not be there. This is your work, not mine."
- A blind half-elf monk enters and immediately says: "Give me a standard shadow step."
- Zalthir shadow steps. The monk criticizes him at once: "You skip the third breath. You don't enter your ki in the hara. You let it bleed into your fingertips like a poison. It is technically wrong in every scroll." He then pauses: "And yet, you arrived at your destination three seconds faster than the grandmaster did. Show me what else you haven't learned."
- Zalthir asks whether he can punch the monk. The DM confirms he can. Zalthir rolls a 20 and a 13 on strikes. The sparring match is evenly balanced — Zalthir could take him if he committed — but the monk is also half-blind.
- The monk pivots to a formal skill challenge. Zalthir must demonstrate one of three techniques: the Weightless Shadow (acrobatics), the Efficient Strike (athletics), or the Eldritch Claw (stealth). Zalthir picks acrobatics. He rolls a 3. The monk stares at him as he falls. "You have learned nothing."
- The monk asks for the Efficient Strike (athletics). Zalthir rolls a 1. The monk sighs: "The corridor was narrow. The form was irrelevant. Did you not practice your forms while traveling?"
- Zalthir tries stealth for the Eldritch Claw demonstration. He rolls a 4, for an 11 total. The monk walks away.
- As the monk departs, Zalthir lands a strike on him from behind — rolling a 30 to hit. The monk stops. "Hmm. Perhaps not all is lost." He is genuinely impressed; no one has hit him that hard in a long time.
- The monk sets a final challenge: throw the brick, Zalthir must shadow step to intercept and smash it mid-air. Zalthir rolls 29 to hit. The brick is "smashed into a variety of pieces. Completely unprecedented form. Still skipping breaths. Still not taking maneuvers. But the results do speak for themselves."
- The monk asks whether Zalthir has any other insights from his schooling. Zalthir demonstrates his shadow tentacles and eldritch tattoo. The monk is not impressed but acknowledges that combining two-hit into something interesting is the next step.
- A sage of xenobiology approaches and asks whether "a mindless digestive system" can achieve enlightenment. Zalthir pushes back: Glabbagool is "by no means mindless" — he is a student, he is already walking, he is already tormenting the pocket drow. The xenobiology sage, apparently satisfied, provides a vial of Resonant Slime. Glabbagool is very pleased.
- Earlier in the session, Zalthir recalls that he once punched through the shadows in the Underdark to create a precise puncture — and proposes demonstrating a version of that maneuver: shadow stepping and striking simultaneously in a way that redeems all other failings. He rolls a 26 to hit on the demonstration. Khell-Vire raises an eyebrow and acknowledges that "there is something special about how you hit," and that the combination of shadow step and the hit "redeems your lack of skills in all other possible areas."
- Brother Khell-Vire grants Zalthir access to a specialized shadow technique — presenting three options, from which Zalthir selects option C.

### Therapy for a Sentient Blade
#### Thorin and Master Archivist Philemon begin a process of healing for the sentient sun-sword Dawnbringer.
- Master Archivist Philemon greets Thorin and Dawnbringer and immediately refers to the blade as "a remarkable specimen — late Netherese-era recovery, sudden worship origins, sustained darkness exposure of approximately 3,000 to 4,000 years."
- Thorin corrects him immediately: "What do you mean, specimen? This is my friend Dawnbringer." Philemon adjusts, noting with some surprise that the bond between Thorin and the blade is stronger than he originally feared.
- Thorin explains that Dawnbringer struggles with darkness — something they have been working through together.
- Philemon asks Dawnbringer directly about the wielder she was found with — meaning the corpse in which she was discovered. Dawnbringer falters and goes silent.
- Thorin remarks, somewhat flippantly, "The rebound relationship, I get it, okay" — then immediately walks it back: "No, no, it's okay, I'm being flippant. This is a serious topic."
- Philemon gently corrects himself mid-sentence: "Your sword does not need polishing." Then he realizes: "She can't be polished, actually." He reframes: "Your sword needs to remember what daylight feels like without needing to fight, and then when she's ready and you are willing, she needs to grieve. The first work is light. The second work is mourning. We will start with the first."
- Thorin and Dawnbringer are effectively signed up for a two-phase therapeutic process; Thorin notes, with characteristic lightness, that hopefully Dawnbringer "grows a plus one" out of it.

### The Sealed Records of Menzoberranzan
#### Daz consults with Sister Yvenne in the Hall of Divination to uncover his forgotten past and discovers that records of his potential lineage have been intentionally erased.
- Daz is taken to the Hall of Divination in the Arcanium, where spectral mage hands fetch tomes for researchers. He immediately notices tomes flying around him and wonders aloud whether any might help with his headache.
- He approaches Sister Yvenne and asks whether he can only get one question answered. She explains that information is exchanged reciprocally in Candlekeep — when you ask an Avowed, you also provide information. Daz, rather than negotiating, simply tells her everything: the amnesia, waking up in jail, the Underdark escape, the mercenaries trying to protect him and those trying to kill him, the headache growing worse as he approached Candlekeep.
- Yvenne is visibly surprised by the totality of his disclosure — "she's kind of impressed," the DM notes — but also visibly confused, as Daz is a drow and the honesty is unexpected.
- She explains drow noble house politics: each house is ruled by a matron mother; betrayal is upward-rewarded if it strengthens Lolth's web (the "rule of intrigue"); and critically, if you attempt to destroy a rival house and fail to eliminate every member, your own house is forfeit and destroyed by the wider drow community.
- She illustrates this with a specific story: a house tried to wipe out another house and left "some blithering child idiot alive," whereupon the rest of the drow community came and tore the attacking house to the ground, leaving a black puddle behind. Daz asks what happened to the surviving child. Yvenne does not know.
- Daz asks whether the surviving child would have inherited both their original house and the forfeited attacking house. The DM confirms that is how the law would work — the child's house was never made extinct, so the attacking house's forfeiture flows accordingly.
- Yvenne reads him a list of houses struck from the record: the second seat, the fourth seat struck, the eleventh seat, and the twenty-third seat.
- Daz rolls investigation — a natural 20. He notices the word "struck" is deeply strange in this context. It is not the language of elimination; it suggests political, paid censorship.
- Yvenne admits there is one house she cannot speak of in the room, because "the public record is sealed and I am today the public record." She tells him: "If you want what is behind the seals, you will have to want it through other doors."
- Daz asks for the full list of houses that have ever existed. Yvenne provides a document. The word "struck" appears again. She adds that the fourth seat holds ritual archive privileges in Menzoberranzan — making a struck-fourth seat "a very political, expensive erasure."
- Daz muses: "So somebody paid a lot of money to erase some stuff. I don't know my history. We have mercenaries following me around trying to keep me alive, and other mercenaries trying to kill me. So perhaps I am associated with some expensive striking."
- He is given the heraldry book Geography and Cities, Menzoberranzan, Pillars of Woe. As he examines it, he finds a footnote referencing "Daz'issin (lit. 'small shadow')" — and notices the appendix is entirely missing. The cut is mechanical: a razor, not natural wear, at least fifty years old.
- He asks Yvenne: "Who is this Daz'issin?" She replies: "It is a softening name. Mothers use it for boys they wanted to live." She notes there were not many uses of it in the past three centuries, and "the houses that used it, most of them, did not last."
- Daz asks whether Yvenne knows his name. She does. He asks whether someone might call him "Daz" but this was actually a name his mother used. She pivots: "Let us turn to your patron. Did you not come here to learn who protected you?" He confirms that yes, that too. She observes: "It is possible your name is related, but it is unlikely. The houses that had those names have all gone."
- Daz pushes back quietly: "But what if they weren't successfully all gone?" The DM ends the scene there, confirming to Mike that he has probably figured out more than Yvenne is willing to say aloud — and that her body language has been telling him precisely that.

## Locations
### Daggerford
A quiet town where the townsfolk move carefully without explanation — "a different quiet than the Silver Marches," as the DM notes. It features a stone pillar in the village square topped with a bronze cast of a severed forearm dedicated to Travis Houlin, a fallen paladin of Bahamut who died there three years prior fighting the Cult of the Dragon. The town was far enough south to avoid the post-giant-war Uthgardt barbarian raids that struck the northern regions.

### River Shining Tavern
An inn in Daggerford where the party stops for the night and encounters Meredith of the Ford and her afflicted daughter Ellen.

### Beregost
The last meaningful waystop before Candlekeep. It is close enough to the coast that Thorin — who grew up in the mountains and has spent months underground — sits near the sea that night for the first time and contemplates how strangely life moves.

### The Burning Wizard Inn
An inn in Beregost named after a wizard who was burned alive during an earlier dragon cult uprising. The gnome innkeeper Festroon tells the story to anyone who buys a second pint, always pointing to a permanent soot mark on the south wall: "That is where he fell. We scrub the floor every season. We do not paint over the wall. My grandfather said the wall remembers." The inn is lively enough for a traveling bard to perform a full evening set.

### Way of the Lion
The road running along the cliffs above the Sea of Swords leading toward Candlekeep, from which the keep is visible from miles out.

### Candlekeep
A massive fortress-library where the party arrives to seek knowledge and training. Entry requires the gift of a book. It features anti-magic bell tower cells used for prisoners, though the institution's policy is to exile rather than execute offenders. The gate warden inspects all arrivals, including the contents of bags of holding.

### The Athenaeum (Echoing Hall)
The history library within the north inner ward of Candlekeep — specifically Calvier's lower gallery office — where shadow discipline lineage records are kept. This is where Zalthir meets with Brother Khell-Vire and is assessed by the blind half-elf monk during the Trial of the Broken Mirror.

### Hall of Divination
A section of Candlekeep where spectral mage hands fetch tomes for researchers throughout the Arcanium. Sister Yvenne maintains the public records of history and heraldry here. Daz conducts his investigation into his past in this room, examining the heraldry book with the razored appendix.

## Items
### Bronze Cast Forearm Monument
A bronze sculpture of a severed forearm and hand mounted on a stone pillar in the Daggerford village square, dedicated to Travis Houlin, a paladin of Bahamut who died there three years prior fighting the Cult of the Dragon, which had been actively recruiting in the town. It bears a Dwarven inscription reading "The move is yours" — the same phrase found beneath the Castle Ward statue associated with Stroud in Waterdeep — and a smaller Common inscription on the back noting it was witnessed and recorded by Milo Goodbarrel — an independent publisher and member of the Order of the Gauntlet who signs off on commemorative commissions in the Order's name. The casting was funded by the Metalworkers Guild, possibly with Stroud-school sponsorship, though the local priest considers that incidental.

### Travis's Hand Medallion
A small pewter reproduction of the severed hand monument in Daggerford, sold by the local village priest for donations to the Order of the Gauntlet. Grygum purchased one for a gold piece.

### Ring of Protection
A magical ring acquired by Daz during a retconned shopping trip in Waterdeep (cost: 3,500 GP drawn from the party fund), providing a bonus to his armor class and all saving throws. The party discussed several alternatives including a Cloak of Displacement (too expensive for party funds) and Bracers of Defense (6,000 GP, also out of reach), before settling on the Ring of Protection for its dual benefit to both AC and saving throws.

### Drow Piwafwi
A drow-made stealth cloak held by Daz — granting advantage on Stealth checks while worn, with no attunement required. Its effectiveness is limited in direct daylight; the DM confirms it functions normally in darkness or at night. (The recap previously mislabeled this as a Cloak of Elvenkind.)

### Dawnbringer
A sentient sun-sword of late Netherese origin held by Thorin. She illuminates the darkness compulsively — the DM notes she "lights up everything, making it impossible to see anything" at night because she does not like the dark. Master Archivist Philemon identifies her as having suffered profound arcane stress from millennia of darkness exposure and notes she cannot be polished. A two-phase therapeutic recovery process is begun: the work of light first, then the work of mourning.

### Bag of Holding
A magical container used by the party to transport their gear, Glabbagool, and the shrunken House T'sarran drow prisoner. Its contents were inspected at Candlekeep's gate, which is how the spy's presence came to the attention of the Gate Warden.

### Note of the Bone King
A mysterious note found on Brother Khell-Vire's desk in the Athenaeum's Echoing Hall, reading: "Zalthir, the Underdark, until the Bone King sings." Its origin and meaning are unknown to both Zalthir and Khell-Vire.

### Vial of Resonant Slime
A biological sample provided to the party by a sage of xenobiology after Zalthir successfully defended Glabbagool's intelligence and progress during the martial trials. Glabbagool is very pleased that the sage has recognized his promise.

### Geography and Cities, Menzoberranzan, Pillars of Woe
A 200-year-old heraldry book found in Candlekeep's Hall of Divination, provided to Daz by Sister Yvenne. It contains a footnote referencing "Daz'issin (lit. 'small shadow')" and has had its entire appendix mechanically removed — cut with a razor rather than naturally worn away — at least fifty years ago, representing a deliberate and expensive censorship. The fourth seat's ritual archive privileges in Menzoberranzan make the struck records especially significant politically.

## Spells
### Lesser Restoration
Cast by Dawnbringer on the afflicted girl Ellen in Daggerford, after the party failed their medicine checks to diagnose the condition conventionally. Mechanically, the spell originated from Dawnbringer's own sentient-weapon properties and was channeled through Thorin as her attuned wielder — not from any spell slot of Thorin's (Fighter 8, Battle Master, no spellcasting). Dawnbringer performed it with pompous ceremony — "Let me bring the light to you." While it allowed Ellen to speak briefly — uttering "The wells are not wells anymore" — it could not fully cure the profound mental twisting she was experiencing, as the condition was determined to be something interior and beyond a standard disease or magical effect.

### Shadow Step
A teleportation ability used by Zalthir during his Trial of the Broken Mirror at Candlekeep. The blind half-elf monk immediately criticized Zalthir's form — "You skip the third breath. You don't enter your ki in the hara. You let it bleed into your fingertips like a poison. It is technically wrong in every scroll" — but then acknowledged Zalthir arrived at his destination three seconds faster than the grandmaster. Zalthir later combined shadow step with a precise strike to smash a thrown brick mid-air (rolling 29 to hit), which the monk declared redeemed his lack of traditional skill in every other area. This combination — shadow step plus precise strike — was previously demonstrated in the Underdark when Zalthir created a precise puncture, and forms the basis for the specialized technique Khell-Vire ultimately grants him.

## NPCs
### Meredith of the Ford
A weaver by trade in Daggerford who spots Grygum's holy symbol and approaches the party at the River Shining Tavern seeking help for her seven-year-old daughter Ellen, who has been silent for six days, draws the same shape on every flat surface, and says in her sleep that "it is only the shape that holds."

### Ellen
A seven-year-old girl suffering from a mysterious mental affliction that causes her to draw repetitive shapes and hear things that do not exist. After Dawnbringer's pompous lesser restoration, she briefly speaks the words "The wells are not wells anymore" before falling silent again. The party determines her condition is interior — not a disease, and not something that can be conventionally healed — and connects it to the Underdark madness seeping into the surface world.

### The Village Priest
A cleric in Daggerford who sells small pewter reproductions of Travis Houlin's hand cast to raise donations for the Order of the Gauntlet. He identifies himself proudly as a "pure Bahamutian" who does not believe in the heresy of the Stroudites. When pressed about the Dwarven Stroudite inscription on the monument, he concedes pragmatically that since it was a gift, the inscription could remain — "After all, wasn't Travis trying to save us all, regardless of what actually happened in that game of chess?"

### Festroon
An old gnome innkeeper at the Burning Wizard Inn in Beregost who recounts the story of the wizard burned alive during a dragon cult uprising to anyone who buys a second pint, always pointing to the permanent soot mark on the south wall and citing his grandfather's words: "The wall remembers."

### The Bard
A traveling performer at the Burning Wizard Inn whose closing number this season is a four-verse drumming drinking song about a Kenku thief who learned to steal no more, with the whole inn singing along on the chorus of "Black Hood." After receiving a silver piece from Thorin, she asks Grygum whether he is a Bahamutian or a Stroudite, then sings a love song about Stroud and the famous chess game that winds through flowers, emotions, and drinking. Grygum observes it is "the usual claptrap."

### The Human Scholar
A young man in Beregost whose strange, syncopated chanting and breathing pattern catches Daz's attention during the bard's performance. Daz rolls investigation (23) and recognizes the pattern: the same off-by-a-hair wrongness previously observed in Eldred, who went insane earlier in the adventure, and in the Field Ward Preacher. Daz records the chant for investigation at Candlekeep.

### Bookwyrm
The First Reader of Candlekeep, a dragonborn sage who has "heard them all" regarding her name and is entirely unbothered. She assigns the party to their respective mentors: Daz to Sister Yvenne, Zalthir to Brother Khell-Vire, Thorin to Master Archivist Philemon, and Grygum to Varith. She oversees the discussion about the shrunken House T'sarran spy, noting that the decision rests with the party, not the spy.

### Callan Strongbench
The Gate Warden of Candlekeep — summoned to the Arcanium to address the miniature drow spy discovered in the party's bag of holding during inspection. He explains Candlekeep's position with precision: "The bell tower has cells. Comfortable, localized anti-magic. They are very rarely used. Anyone we find guilty of a major offense is exiled rather than imprisoned. Which is to say, we hold prisoners. We do not keep them. If you give her to us, what you are giving us is a delay. We will not deliver to House T'sarran, and we will not execute her. Eventually, we will release her at our gate with a warning." He leaves the choice entirely to the party.

### House T'sarran Spy
A drow spy who was shrunken and kept as a prisoner in the party's bag of holding, watched over by Glabbagool. She is furious about her situation — she thinks it is "outrageous and unacceptable" — constantly tries to kill Glabbagool, runs for it whenever possible, and demands to be restored to her normal size and formally imprisoned by Candlekeep's guards rather than left in Glabbagool's custody. She has, despite herself, been subjected to Glabbagool's meditation lessons.

### Glabbagool
A sentient grey ooze (transformed from his original gelatinous-cube form in Blingdenstone) who has been acting as jailer for the miniature drow spy. He shapes fake eyes out of his pseudopods as an affectation — the recurring "six pseudo-eyes" are his own choice. He describes their daily routine with cheerful equanimity: she attacks him, he slaps her around, she tries to run, he expands to catch her, she screams, he explains the way of the shadow monk, she screams about the meditation, and then they talk. "We've become great friends!" He asks Zalthir seriously whether watching the prisoner will make him more or less of a monk, accepts Zalthir's confirmation that this is pre-monk training, and commits to the task with all six pseudo-eyes fixed on his charge. He is also very pleased when the xenobiology sage's interest in him is defended by Zalthir.

### Brother Khell-Vire
A 200-year-old half-elf and former monk of the Garden of Shadow who now serves at Candlekeep. He left the Garden eighty years ago after refusing an order, and wears a faded shadow monk under-robe beneath the avowed gray of Candlekeep. He has a note on his desk reading "Zalthir, the Underdark, until the Bone King sings" that he cannot explain. He opens with a cryptic monk-style question — "What were you sent toward?" — that Zalthir finds predictably annoying from long familiarity with the type, and sends him to the Trial of the Broken Mirror with the instruction: "I will not be there. This is your work, not mine." He ultimately acknowledges Zalthir's unconventional mastery and grants him access to one of three specialized shadow techniques.

### Master Kenshi (the blind monk)
A blind half-elf monk who tests Zalthir's combat abilities through the Trial of the Broken Mirror. He immediately identifies the flaws in Zalthir's shadow stepping — skipping the third breath, bleeding ki into the fingertips like a poison — but notes Zalthir arrived three seconds faster than the grandmaster. He is deeply unimpressed by every formal skill demonstration (acrobatics: 3, athletics: 1, stealth: 11), turns to walk away, and is genuinely stunned when Zalthir lands a powerful hit on him from behind — "Perhaps not all is lost." He sets the brick challenge, and when Zalthir smashes it with a 29, declares that while the forms are wrong in every scroll, the combination of shadow step and strike redeems everything else. He observes wryly that Zalthir is "beginning to remember, I think, all those shadow monks that drove you nuts."

### Master Archivist Philemon
A clinical specialist in sentient artifacts at Candlekeep who initially greets Dawnbringer as "a remarkable specimen" until Thorin corrects him. He identifies her as a late Netherese-era sun worship artifact suffering profound arcane stress from millennia of darkness, notes mid-sentence that she cannot actually be polished, and outlines a two-phase recovery process: the work of light (first) and the work of mourning (second). He prompts Dawnbringer about the wielder she was found with and she falters and goes silent. He is gentle and precise, reframing what seemed like an artifact assessment into something much closer to grief counseling.

### Sister Yvenne
An authoritative librarian at Candlekeep who serves as "the public record" — a phrase she uses with quiet weight when explaining why she cannot speak of a sealed house in that room. She provides Daz with information on drow noble house politics, the rule of intrigue, the matron mother system, and the law of forfeiture when a targeted house is not fully destroyed. She reads him a list of struck houses, explains that "Daz'issin" is a softening name mothers used for sons they wanted to live, provides the heraldry book with the razored appendix, and communicates through careful silences and precise body language that Daz is close to something she cannot say aloud. Her final explicit statement on the matter: "It is possible your name is related, but it is unlikely. The houses that had those names have all gone."
