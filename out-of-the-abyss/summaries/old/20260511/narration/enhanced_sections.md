## Memorable Moments

> "The wells are not wells anymore."
> — Ellen

*The only words spoken by the silent seven-year-old girl after Dawnbringer's lesser restoration — delivered after a pompous declaration of "Let me bring the light to you" — hinting at the Underdark madness spreading to the surface world.*

> "Let me bring the light to you."
> — Dawnbringer

*Dawnbringer takes on what the DM describes as "a very pompous voice" before casting lesser restoration on the afflicted child, only for the cure to prove incomplete.*

**Zalthir fails every skill demonstration — acrobatics, athletics, and stealth — during the Trial of the Broken Mirror, then blindsides the departing monk with a devastating strike that earns genuine respect.**

*After rolling a 3, a 1, and an 11 on formal tests, Zalthir's combat-optimized character saves face by landing a hit no one has managed in a long time — prompting the monk to say "Perhaps not all is lost."*

> "Although your skills are weak — clearly showing a lack of focus and training — there is something special about how you hit. The way of the shadow monk, though, is not about hitting. It is about discretely moving. And it is your combination of the shadow step and the hit that redeems your lack of skills in all other possible areas."
> — Master Kenshi (paraphrase, assembled from DM narration)

> "She needs to remember what daylight feels like without needing to fight, and then when she's ready and you are willing, she needs to grieve. The first work is light. The second work is mourning. We will start with the first."
> — Master Archivist Philemon

*A clinical artifact specialist reframes Dawnbringer's trauma in deeply emotional terms, turning what seemed like a weapon assessment into something closer to therapy — with Thorin noting afterward that he apparently signed them both up for it.*

> "There is one house I cannot speak of in this room, not because I will not, because the public record is sealed, and I am today the public record. If you want what is behind the seals, you will have to want it through other doors."
> — Sister Yvenne

*Yvenne reveals to Daz that someone powerful has had an entire Drow noble house erased from Candlekeep's records, hinting it may be connected to his identity.*

> "She keeps trying to kill me! And then I slap her around, and then she tries to run away, but there's nowhere to run away to, so I just sort of expand, and then she's trapped, and then she starts screaming at me, and then I try to explain to her how the way of the shadow monk is to be quiet, and that she should learn meditation, and then she screams at me about the meditation, and then we talk about things. We've become great friends!"
> — Glabbagool

*Glabbagool cheerfully describes his daily routine as jailer, which involves being attacked, expanding to trap the spy, and offering unsolicited monk wisdom — all of which Zalthir confirms is excellent pre-monk training.*

> "It is a softening name. Mothers use it for boys they wanted to live."
> — Sister Yvenne, on the name Daz'issin

*After Daz discovers his name referenced in a footnote of a heraldry book with its appendix razored out, Yvenne's quiet explanation of what the name means lands with considerable weight.*

**Daz discovers that an entire appendix has been razored out of a 200-year-old heraldry book — cut mechanically, not worn — and a surviving footnote references "Daz'issin," possibly his own true name.**

*Piecing together censored records and a softening name used by mothers for sons they hoped would survive, Daz begins to suspect his origins are far more dangerous than he knew, pushing back: "But what if they weren't successfully all gone?"*

**Thorin immediately corrects Master Archivist Philemon's clinical framing, insisting the blade being assessed is his friend — not a specimen.**

*Philemon refers to Dawnbringer as "a remarkable specimen" within his opening breath. Thorin cuts him off: "What do you mean, specimen? This is my friend Dawnbringer." Philemon adjusts, quietly noting the bond between wielder and blade is stronger than he had feared — and the entire session pivots from artifact analysis to something closer to grief counseling.*

**Thorin's flippant "rebound relationship" comment about Dawnbringer's previous wielder, immediately walked back with visible discomfort.**

*When Philemon asks Dawnbringer about the wielder she was found with and she goes silent, Thorin fills the silence with "The rebound relationship, I get it, okay" — then catches himself: "No, no, it's okay, I'm being flippant. This is a serious topic." The correction lands heavier than the joke.*

**Glabbagool solemnly asks Zalthir whether watching the prisoner will make him more or less of a monk — then fixes all six pseudo-eyes on her when told it is pre-monk training.**

*The question is asked with complete sincerity. Zalthir's confirmation that it is "absolutely pre-monk training" is met with total commitment: six pseudo-eyes lock onto the miniature drow spy simultaneously. The DM delivers it as a straight beat.*

> "I will not be there. This is your work, not mine."
> — Brother Khell-Vire

*Khell-Vire sends Zalthir to the Trial of the Broken Mirror without accompanying him, offering the instruction with the particular flatness of a teacher who has decided to stop protecting a student from their own potential.*

**The village priest of Daggerford declares himself "a pure Bahamutian" while selling pewter replicas of a dead paladin's severed hand outside a monument commissioned by possible Stroudite sponsors — and concedes the heretical inscription can stay because Travis was trying to save everyone anyway.**

*The moment crystallizes the theological mess the party has been navigating since Waterdeep: Stroudite iconography, Bahamutian martyrdom, and Metalworkers Guild money all tangled around a bronze forearm on a pillar, with the priest selling keepsakes to fund the Order of the Gauntlet and insisting none of it is a contradiction.*

> "Life takes you in weird directions and weird places. You just gotta go with it."
> — Thorin

*Sitting by the sea for what may be the first time after months underground, Thorin reflects aloud to Dawnbringer — who is lighting up everything around them because she does not like the dark — with the particular simplicity of someone who has stopped trying to make sense of things and found that restful.*
