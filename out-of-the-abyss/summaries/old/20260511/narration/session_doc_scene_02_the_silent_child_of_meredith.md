Meredith of the Ford had the look of a woman who had already been turned away. You develop an eye for it, in this work — the specific exhaustion of someone who has walked into every room that was supposed to help and left each one carrying the same weight back out. She spotted my holy symbol during dinner, which is the usual trigger. People see the dragon-and-scale emblem and they think: well, at least this one won't say no before I finish asking.

She didn't say no to anything, in her own telling. She said yes. Yes, she'd watched Ellen for six days. Yes, she'd noted the drawings — same thing, over and over, every flat surface in the house. Yes, she'd listened to her daughter talk in her sleep, because what else do you do. And yes, she'd been to the temples, plural, and the temples had no answer.

"My daughter Ellen is 7 years old and has been silent for 6 days. She draws the same shape on every flat surface. She talks in her sleep. She says it is only the shape that holds. Please, we have asked the temples. The temples have no answer. Will you look at her?"

I thought about Stool. I couldn't help it. A creature that communicated through spores and rapport rather than words, who nonetheless had more to say than most creatures I'd met with full use of their tongues. Silence isn't absence. Seven years old and drawing the same thing on every flat surface is not nothing — it's insistence. She was trying to say something. The problem was we didn't have the vocabulary for it yet.

"Yes," I said. "I… I will, I will examine her."

Daz came along, which I appreciated. He has a precise mind and a habit of noticing the things that other people decide not to notice. Between the two of us, we ought to have been able to produce something useful.

We did not produce something useful.

There is a particular feeling when a medicine examination goes badly — not dramatically badly, not the kind where someone collapses or the situation gets worse, just the quiet professional failure of looking at a patient and knowing that you are not seeing what you need to see. Daz's assistance helps, as a rule. The two minds catch more than one.

We still came up with essentially nothing.

"Yeah, I still only got a 10," I heard myself say. "Oops."

Which summarizes it. Ellen was — and this was the clinical conclusion — not well. I could have told Meredith that before I sat down. Something was wrong with her daughter. The daughter was drawing the same pattern on every flat surface and had not spoken in six days. We had established that something was wrong. We had failed to establish what.

I was still turning this over when Thorin mentioned Dawnbringer could help. Dawnbringer did not wait for a second invitation. The sword has opinions about when to make an entrance, and this was apparently an occasion.

"Let me bring the light to you."

Very pompous. Even for Dawnbringer. The lesser restoration settled over Ellen like a change in air pressure.

And then she spoke.

"The wells are not wells anymore."

Then silence.

I wrote it down. It is what I do. But I also just held it for a moment, the way you hold something before you decide what category it goes in — because there was something in her voice that the restoration hadn't fixed, something underneath the words that the words were pointing toward rather than containing. The wells are not wells anymore. It was the kind of sentence that means more than it says, and doesn't tell you what the extra meaning is.

The restoration had cleared enough for her to say one thing. The thing she chose to say was that.

We worked through it. Not a disease — that was the first solid conclusion. Something was disrupting how the world reached her. Not an infection, not a conventional illness, not something with a clear Underdark vector. I asked about fungal involvement specifically, because I still think about Zuggtmoy, and will probably continue thinking about Zuggtmoy for the rest of my life.

"Is there any evidence of any sort of fungal infection? No. No. Okay. So it's not limited to fungus."

Which was both a relief and the opposite of a relief, because fungus I understand. Fungus has a mechanism. This was something else.

"Oh," I said, as something slid into place. "There's all the people — hearing voices?"

The answer was yes. The madness from the Underdark — the deep, structural wrongness we'd felt building around the demon lords, the way Neverlight Grove had been singing and the Pudding King's court had been dissolving and everything below a certain depth had been quietly coming apart — was not, apparently, staying below. It was reaching up. It was reaching Ellen.

Zalthir articulated the mechanism: the input, the audio, the way the world was arriving at her rather than the world itself. And that was right. It wasn't a disease in the conventional sense. It was more like the world had started speaking a language she couldn't process, and the gap between what was arriving and what she could understand was where the drawings and the six days of silence lived.

"She's having hallucinations. Audio."

"And the hallucinations are not magic," came the reply. "They're something more profound than a lesser restoration can fix."

Right. Filed. That's the ceiling on what we can do tonight. Whatever was happening inside Ellen was interior in a way that made it untouchable through conventional means. Not an exterior effect. I said it aloud, completing the thought someone else started.

"Not something that can be slapped."

"Or grappled."

Zalthir produced an alarm from somewhere, which I took as professional commentary.

Then Daz mentioned the pressure in his own head — had been mentioning it, in various indirect ways, for a while now. I looked at him. I looked at Ellen. I thought about what we'd just established: that something from below was bleeding up, that it was affecting how people processed what reached them.

I sat with that question long enough to actually examine it properly.

"Does it seem related at all to how the other person was hearing voices?"

Twenty-three. My best of the evening, arriving too late to be medically useful and just in time to be philosophically concerning.

There was something there. Not the same thing, not a direct causal chain I could map and annotate and hand to Vareth as a neat theological puzzle. More like two things that had the same obscure author — a tenuous connection, the kind that suggests rather than demonstrates.

"There's some kind of tenuous connection there?"

"Yes. There's some kind of tenuous connection there."

I took notes. Ellen couldn't speak and Daz had a headache and somewhere below us the demon lords were apparently still broadcasting, still finding new listeners, still rewriting the language of wells and shapes and held things into something the surface world hadn't been built to receive.

Bahamut may or may not have had a contingency plan for this. I filed it under open questions and kept writing.
