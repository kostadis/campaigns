## Flags

### [1] Cliché simile — "the expression of a man who had asked for X and received Y"

> Grygum received this with the expression of a man who had asked for tea and received warm water.

**Why:** Workshop construction; reads as a magazine essay's sardonic-observation register, not Daz's audit register. Daz catalogues *categories* and *deviations from baseline* (per spec) — when he describes another character's reaction he tends to name the move, not draw a simile around it. Compare *"Grygum was walking toward me with the expression he wears when he has had what he calls a divine inspiration and what I call a plan that will require me to improvise"* (from the example file): same shape, but grounded in their known dynamic, not in a stock figure.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Grygum received this in the way he receives most things downgraded by translation: politely, and with the file already updated.*

### [2] Convergence with house style — "with the particular X of Y who Z" template (twice in this scene)

> Dawnbringer, in characteristic fashion, lit up everything around them with the particular enthusiasm of a sword that has strong opinions about darkness, which made it impossible to see anything.
>
> He came back down — later, considerably more settled — with the particular expression of someone who has been surprised by the value of a thing they previously dismissed.

**Why:** The "with the particular X of Y who Z" construction recurs across at least four scenes of this doc — it's becoming the narrator's house tic rather than a per-character voice. Daz's audit register is *categorical and precise*; he names the function, he doesn't describe the affect at one remove. The Dawnbringer sentence also editorializes about the sword's "enthusiasm" — anthropomorphizing in a way that's more Thorin's territory than Daz's.
**Suggested rewrite (first):** *Dawnbringer, predictably, lit up everything around them, which defeated the purpose of going outside to look at the sea.*
**Suggested rewrite (second):** *He came back down later, settled. The expression of someone closing an internal argument, not opening an external one.*

### [3] Generic prose — atmospheric scene-setting

> She had what every working bard has at a waystation like this: a closing number she'd been doing all season, calibrated to the room's energy by the second pint and tightened by the third.

**Why:** This is good *travel-writing*, not Daz auditing. Daz's bard-observation in voice would inventory what the bard is selling (the closing number, the silver-piece tier, the request-honoring pattern). This sentence reaches for warmth and cleverness in a way Daz's register usually files past.
**Suggested rewrite:** *She had a closing number she'd been doing all season. Audience knew it. Came in on the chorus.*

## Verdict

The "I will not call what I felt next a headache. I will call it the specific cognitive friction of almost-recognition" passage is a perfect spec-callout and the cataloguing of the inn (exits, the young scholar, the chant pattern) is on-voice. The Daz scenes in this doc are the strongest — the flags here are house-style polish creeping in, not character drift. Spot-edit.
