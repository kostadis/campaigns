# Arrival in Daggerford

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party arrives in the quiet town of Daggerford during a flashback sequence as they travel toward Candlekeep, discovering a monument to a fallen paladin.
- The DM establishes that the entire session takes place in a flashback: the party is traveling south from the Silver Marches toward Candlekeep, currently passing through Daggerford.
- The party enters Daggerford, noting an unusual, heavy silence among the townsfolk who move carefully without explanation — "a different quiet than the Silver Marches," as the DM notes.
- The group stops for the night at the River Shining Tavern.
- While passing through the village square, the party observes a bronze cast of a severed forearm and hand mounted on a stone pillar; Daz immediately goes to examine it.
- They read an inscription identifying the monument as a tribute to Travis Houlin, a paladin of Bahamut who died three years ago fighting the Cult of the Dragon in Daggerford itself, where a cult had been actively recruiting.
- A secondary inscription in Dwarven is discovered on the base, reading "The move is yours," which Daz recognizes as mirroring the inscription beneath the Castle Ward statue in Waterdeep associated with Stroud.
- A smaller inscription on the back of the pillar in Common notes the monument was witnessed and recorded by Milo Goodbarrel, head of his publisher and member of the Order of the Gauntlet — who apparently personally signed off on the Daggerford monument.
- The party notes that virtually every community in the North was affected by the War of the Dragon in some form — dragons, raiding cults, pillaging, or burning — and this monument is one more marker of that.
- Daggerford, being sufficiently south, was not affected by the post-giant-war Uthgardt barbarian hordes that struck the regions further north.
- The local village priest arrives selling small pewter reproductions of Travis's hand cast to raise donations for the Order of the Gauntlet, addressing the group with cheerful surprise at finding a half-orc cleric of Bahamut, a Dragonborn, a dwarf, and a drow all walking in together.
- The priest explains the monument was funded by the Metalworkers Guild, possibly with Stroud-school sponsorship — though he himself declares proudly "I am a pure Bahamutian" and does not believe in the heresy of the Stroudites.
- When Grygum asks why the Dwarven Stroudite inscription was allowed to remain, the priest concedes it was a gift: "After all, wasn't Travis trying to save us all, regardless of what actually happened in that game of chess?"
- Grygum donates a gold piece and receives a pewter medallion.
