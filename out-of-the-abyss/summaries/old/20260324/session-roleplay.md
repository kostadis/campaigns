# Roleplay Highlights — Session — 2026-03-29

## Character Voices

### Daz (Mike Hall)
- Smooth-talking drow con artist who thinks fast on his feet; shifts between theatrical mysticism and dry, transactional negotiation without missing a beat. Defaults to a wry, unimpressed register when offered intangible rewards.
- Skilled at managing chaos — covers for party members' blunders and keeps Glabbagool quiet mid-deception.
> "I am… you are not speaking to the wizard of this body. I… he… this body has communed with Lolth. And I… I am speaking through this body."
> "I already feel the gratitude of Lolth. I have that."
> "It's the kind of conviction *this* follower of Lolth shows."

### Asha Vandree (GM — Kostadis Roussos)
- Imperious, paranoid, and perpetually exasperated drow priestess. Speaks in clipped, condescending sentences directed through Daz as intermediary — refuses to address non-drow directly. Prone to long, bitter tangents about interpersonal grievances. Covers moments of embarrassment by retroactively framing them as tests.
- Oscillates between genuine tactical insight and petty disdain; deeply resentful of being stuck mediating Ilvara and Jorlan's messy breakup.
> "Aha, you are trying to test me, to see if I was one of those fake followers of Lolth, who are willing to believe every charlatan magic user that is somehow being possessed by the power of Lolth."
> "I thought you had strong allies, who had a tactical awareness and understanding how to use their magic for destructive purposes."
> "You would think that in the midst of the abyss with the demons on the breasts, everyone could get over their relationship issues, but no, no, no, no, no, Jorlan still is complaining that Ilvara… Ilvara this and Ilvara that."

### Zalthir (Gabe)
- Tactical-minded dragonborn who asks dangerously specific questions and maintains a dry, deadpan humor even when plans collapse around him. Quietly competent, unbothered by being called a "servant," and deeply concerned about spore infection.
> "That was on the list, sure."
> "I mean, I pointed at Dad. Like, look at him! He's already infected. I don't want to be infected."
> "I'm out of actions and bonus actions, so I guess I'll just kind of look on and be confused."

### Grygum (Ben Pfaff)
- Enthusiastic spellcaster who gets genuinely excited about deploying new abilities; balances earnest tactical thinking with self-deprecating humor and the occasional terrible pun. Willing to sympathize with anyone, even drow relationship drama.
> "Oh, man, these relationships are so complicated."
> "I mean, that thing is all heart."
> "That worked better than I had any right to expect."

### Thorin (Joe Beda / Mike Hall)
- Practical brawler who knows his range limitations and accepts support roles without ego. Asks good questions, defers to the casters' plans, and steps up when melee is needed.
> "Because it's better than nothing."
> "Yeah, my darts will only… are not great for range, so… This is… I gotta figure something out."
> "Alright, guys, should I attack one of the Shamblers, or Jorlan, or go for the Big Heart Fungus? What do you think is most effective here?"

### Glabbagool (GM — narrated)
- A gelatinous cube trying very hard to be stealthy and failing completely. Endearingly oblivious — opens his eyes while pretending to be a rock, talks while supposed to be invisible.
> "He starts talking and has to be reminded that Invisible Rock doesn't talk. Then he opens his eyes, and… eyes that will look left and right are not very invisible."

### Ilvara (GM — Kostadis Roussos)
- Encountered primarily through Asha's description and in combat. Furious, shrieking, and possessive of her Zuggtmoy artifact. Uses legendary actions with ruthless efficiency.
> "She screams at the top of her lungs, furious. At herself."

## Memorable Exchanges

**The Deception Spiral** — Daz attempts to convince Asha he's a vessel of Lolth, and she systematically dismantles every angle until landing on an interpretation that suits her.
> **Asha:** "You're a wizard. A wizard… a wizard who prays to Lolth?"
> **Daz:** "I am… you are not speaking to the wizard of this body. I… he… this body has communed with Lolth."
> **Asha:** "Aha, you are trying to test me, to see if I was one of those fake followers of Lolth…"
> **Asha:** "Oh, dear goodness, have you two been infected by the spores, possessed by the crazed magic of Zuggtmoy?"
> **Asha:** "Perhaps my prayers have been answered through this… confused vessel."
> **Daz:** "I really do believe that I have. Perhaps there are some spores within me, but I… it feels… I feel it. And by the way, I've convinced others to join me."

**Negotiating Payment** — Daz tries to extract something concrete from Asha, who offers divine gratitude.
> **Asha:** "The gratitude of Lolth."
> **Daz:** "I already feel the gratitude of Lolth. I have that."

**Conviction Check** — Asha questions Daz's commitment and gets an answer that's honest in the wrong way.
> **Asha:** "That is not the kind of conviction a follower of Lolth shows."
> **Daz:** "It's the kind of conviction *this* follower of Lolth shows."

**The Boss/Master Confusion** — Asha compliments Zalthir through Daz, and cultural mistranslation nearly blows their cover.
> **Asha:** "You have found a great servant, Daz."
> **Zalthir:** "It's a good boss, yeah."
> **Asha:** "Boss? I thought he was your master. Very well. Boss it is!"
> **Zalthir:** "Yeah, yeah, totally. That, yes. It's a term they use among Dragonborn."

**Spore Anxiety** — Zalthir presses Asha on the danger of destroying the heart fungus, and Asha is unimpressed.
> **Asha:** "Oh, no, no, oh, oh, oh, you think that you'll get infected by some spores and turned into Zuggtmoy demon spores and become mindless creatures?"
> **Zalthir:** "That was on the list, sure."
> **Asha:** "You worry about these things a lot."
> **Zalthir:** "Yes."
> **Grygum:** "We do, yeah."
> **Zalthir:** "I mean, I pointed at Dad. Like, look at him! He's already infected. I don't want to be infected."

**Asha's Relationship Rant** — Asha vents about being stuck between Ilvara and Jorlan's toxic breakup.
> **Asha:** "They used to have an affair, it used to be this whole thing, and then she broke up with him, and then he was bitter… and now I've had to be in the middle of these two for the last few months. Do you have any idea how aggravating that is?"
> **Grygum:** "Oh, man, these relationships are so complicated."
> **Asha:** "I agree! You would think that in the midst of the abyss with the demons on the breasts, everyone could get over their relationship issues, but no, no, no, no, no…"

**Taunting Jorlan into the Glyph** — Grygum baits Jorlan with schoolyard simplicity, and it works.
> **Grygum:** "Come get it, idiot."
> **GM:** "He runs towards the trap."
> **Grygum:** "Kaboom! I didn't expect it to work."

## Standout Moments

- **Daz's masterful cover for the party's tactical questions:** When Grygum naively suggests "blowing everything up" and Asha sneers at the party's intelligence, Daz rescues the moment with a perfectly drow-logical explanation about needing local expertise on spore side effects. Asha, caught off guard by his reasoning, pivots to: *"Well put, well put. This was just a test. Don't touch me."*

- **The double-cross pitch:** The GM reminds Daz that in drow culture, nothing builds trust like a well-executed double-cross — betray Asha to Ilvara to get close, then kill Ilvara anyway. Daz's instant response: *"Okay, I like that plan."* A perfect character moment for a drow who's internalized his culture's values even while working against its agents.

- **Glabbagool as "ooze familiar":** When Thorin proposes passing off the sentient gelatinous cube as Daz's familiar, the GM notes Asha is *even more impressed* with Daz's apparent magical power. An absurd improvisation that somehow deepened the con.

- **Zalthir stranded above the heart fungus:** After Ilvara melts out of his grapple via legendary action, Zalthir is left hovering with empty claws over a hostile fungus, delivering a perfectly deflated: *"I'm out of actions and bonus actions, so I guess I'll just kind of look on and be confused."*

- **Recognition of the spore walkers:** The GM's description of reanimated drow prison guards — former captors now reduced to fungal automatons — lands with genuine pathos. Gabe's quiet *"Those poor guards, they've been through quite a lot"* followed by the GM's grim reminder: *"That was Valen's fate."*

- **Grygum's Dust of Suleiman and Fireball combo:** After the combined assault deals massive damage to the heart fungus and Ilvara, Grygum's honest assessment: *"That worked better than I had any right to expect."*

## Voice Keeper Notes

- **Asha Vandree speech patterns:** Always addresses non-drow party members through Daz as intermediary. Uses condescending terms of address (*"my dear boy"*). Retroactively frames her mistakes as deliberate tests. Prone to digressive rants about Ilvara and Jorlan's relationship. Ends uncomfortable exchanges with *"Don't touch me."* Should continue treating Zalthir as Daz's "servant" and using the word "boss" with visible confusion.

- **Ilvara:** Primarily encountered in combat this session. Screams in fury, commands spore walkers, uses legendary actions aggressively. Her emotional attachment to the Zuggtmoy artifact is exploitable — she ignored threats while tending to it.

- **Daz's deception status with Asha:** Asha currently believes Daz is a spore-confused but genuinely powerful drow mage with an ooze familiar and dragonborn servants. This cover story is fragile and could unravel if Asha observes the party dynamic too closely. The party is also considering a future double-cross of Asha after Ilvara is dealt with.

- **Zalthir's spore anxiety:** Genuinely concerned about fungal infection; this fear should be referenced and potentially exploited by NPCs. He has already pointed at Daz as evidence of what spores can do to a person.

- **Grygum's sympathy:** Surprisingly empathetic toward everyone, including drow complaining about relationship drama. This openness could be leveraged by manipulative NPCs.

- **Thorin's range limitation:** Actively frustrated by his lack of ranged options; borrowing Grygum's crossbow as a stopgap. Future encounters should note he fights with Dawnbringer (has Sap property, imposes disadvantage on target's next attack).

- **Valen's fate:** Confirmed as having become a spore servant. This should carry emotional weight if any remaining spore walkers are encountered — the party recognized their former guards.

- **Promises/obligations in play:** The party has committed to killing Ilvara and destroying her Zuggtmoy artifact. Asha expects them to prevent the "bride heresy" from spreading. The party is privately considering betraying Asha afterward. Asha has promised to join the fight but positioned herself to arrive late — her trustworthiness remains questionable.
