## Events

- The party debated tactical approaches for attacking Ilvara and the Heart Fungus in the fungal cavern, weighing spell options including Glyph of Warding, Dust of Suleiman, Phantasmal Killer, and Fireball.
- Ben (Grygum) explained his available 4th-level spells: Glyph of Warding, Dust of Suleiman (20 damage/turn in a 20-foot sphere, 120-foot range, concentration up to 10 minutes), and Phantasmal Killer (4d10 psychic damage per failed Wisdom save, DC 15).
- Grygum can only cast one 4th-level spell, but can inscribe a Glyph of Warding at 3rd level, allowing him to use both Glyph and Dust of Suleiman.
- The party decided to use Dust of Suleiman on the big Heart Fungus (per Asha's tactical suggestion) rather than directly on Ilvara.
- Grygum inscribed a Glyph of Warding on a bridge on Jorlan's side of the cavern, set to trigger against anyone approaching from Jorlan's direction, creating a 20-foot radius explosion.
- Zalthir (Gabe) proposed grappling Ilvara and dropping her into the Dust of Suleiman's area of effect near the Heart Fungus.
- Thorin (Joe) was assigned perimeter defense, and Grygum offered to lend him his light crossbow for ranged capability.
- The party considered and rejected the idea of a double-cross on Asha Vandry (pretending to betray her to get closer to Ilvara, then killing Ilvara).
- The party agreed to kill Ilvara first and then decide whether to negotiate further with or deal with Asha afterward.
- The GM confirmed that a single fireball would not be enough to kill Ilvara.
- The GM confirmed that attacking the Heart Fungus would alert Ilvara and draw her into combat.

## NPC Interactions

### Asha Vandry
- Asha suggested using the Dust of Suleiman on the big Heart Fungus rather than directly on Ilvara, calling out the party's lack of tactical awareness. She was placated when Daz (Mike) explained they were unfamiliar with fungal spore mechanics.
- Asha revealed the Heart Fungus is a major weapon Ilvara uses to control the area and that it needs sustained damage to be destroyed.
- Asha clarified that the Heart Fungus's spores cause poisoning, pain, suffering, and breathing difficulties but do NOT cause the specific fungal infection/disease — that only comes from Ilvara's personal spores and her mushroom artifact. She hedged slightly: "I believe."
- Asha revealed that Ilvara can summon spore servants (corrupted drow) from the ground/muck/pools at will, but only so many at a time.
- Asha confirmed only she and Jorlan remain uninfected — herself through loyalty to Lolth, Jorlan through sheer bitterness toward Ilvara.
- Asha complained at length about being stuck mediating between Ilvara and Jorlan's failed romantic relationship for months.
- Asha promised to rush in and defend the party from other attackers (spore servants) once combat begins.
- Asha contemptuously refused to address Zalthir directly, instead speaking through Daz, reflecting drow gender hierarchy (males below priestesses).
- Asha was impressed when Glabbagool was presented as Daz's ooze familiar, further elevating her opinion of Daz.
- Asha referred to Zalthir as Daz's "servant" and was confused when Zalthir called Daz "boss" instead of "master."

### Glabbagool
- Glabbagool spoke up at one point ("I can assure you that they've been very—") before being cut off, nearly blowing his cover.
- The party successfully passed Glabbagool off as Daz's familiar to Asha, who was impressed by the concept of an ooze familiar.
- Joe (Thorin) confirmed Glabbagool would not be offended by being called a familiar.

### Jorlan
- Asha described Jorlan as a bitter ex-lover of Ilvara who is not yet infected by spores due to his hatred of her.
- Jorlan is NOT an ally — the party chose Asha over Jorlan. The Glyph of Warding was set specifically to target anyone approaching from Jorlan's side.

### Ilvara
- Confirmed to be corrupted by the "bride" heresy of Zuggtmoy.
- She can summon spore servants from the ground at will.
- The specific fungal disease originates only from Ilvara's personal spores and her mushroom artifact.
- Attacking the Heart Fungus will alert her and provoke a response.

## PC Actions & Decisions

- **Daz (Mike):** Led negotiations with Asha Vandry; successfully explained the party's caution regarding spores; presented Glabbagool as his familiar; planned to open with fireballs at range.
- **Grygum (Ben):** Chose to inscribe a 3rd-level Glyph of Warding on the bridge on Jorlan's side, triggered by anyone approaching from that direction. Reserved his 4th-level slot for Dust of Suleiman to be cast on the Heart Fungus at 120-foot range. Also identified Phantasmal Killer as a potential option (DC 15 Wisdom save, 4d10 psychic) but did not commit to it. Offered his light crossbow to Thorin.
- **Zalthir (Gabe):** Planned to grapple Ilvara and drop her into the Dust of Suleiman's area of effect on the Heart Fungus. Asked probing tactical questions about pool depth (3-4 feet, difficult terrain), spore servant locations, and fungal infection risks. Accepted the "servant/boss" dynamic with Daz for Asha's benefit.
- **Thorin (Joe):** Assigned to perimeter defense against spore servants. Acknowledged his lack of ranged options ("my darts are not great for range"). Accepted Grygum's offer of a light crossbow.

## Out-of-Character Notes

- The GM (Kostadis) noted that the 2024 5th Edition rules changed many spells to allow moving spell effects, but confirmed that Dust of Suleiman cannot be moved after placement per its specific wording.
- Ben initially misread Dust of Suleiman as movable, then corrected himself — it is stationary.
- Ben confirmed Glyph of Warding can be inscribed at 3rd level, preserving his single 4th-level slot for Dust of Suleiman.
- The GM clarified that pools in the cavern are 3-4 feet deep and count as difficult terrain.
- The GM suggested the double-cross tactic as an option (betraying Asha to gain Ilvara's trust, then killing Ilvara), but the party did not pursue it.
