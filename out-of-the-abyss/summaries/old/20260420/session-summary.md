# Session — 2026-04-24

## Overview
This session covered the final confrontation with Ilvara at a corrupted fungal outpost, followed by the conclusion of the party's long journey through the Underdark. The session opened mid-preparation, with Daz executing an elaborate deception to recruit Asha Vandree as a temporary ally against Ilvara, framing himself as a useful-if-delusional mage rather than a vessel of Lolth. Combat then erupted against Ilvara, Jorlan Duskryn, the Big Heart Fungus, and a wave of drow spore servants, with the session cutting off mid-fight. Per the GM reference summary (gm-assist.md), the battle was subsequently resolved: Ilvara was killed by Grygum's Guiding Bolt (her body erupting in poisonous spores), Jorlan was killed in the same engagement, Asha Vandree survived the fight only to be slain by Zalthir when she attempted to assert divine authority over the party, and Nym and Kaelira Duskryn revealed their contract to protect Daz had been issued in Menzoberranzan by an unknown benefactor targeting House Mizzrym and House T'sarran as threats — even as a burnt contract on the drow mage's body revealed that House T'sarran itself had hired the kidnapping, putting T'sarran on both sides of the Daz puzzle. The party looted the outpost extensively, uncovered disturbing evidence of Ilvara's Zuggtmoy corruption, persuaded Dawnbringer to cleanse the corrupted serpent-head scourge, and ultimately escaped the Underdark into the surface world—the Overbright—for the first time in months.

## Session Events

**Recruitment of Asha Vandree**
- Daz staged a two-phase deception: bat familiar (posed as a spider) recalled to hand, Dancing Lights used to simulate a Lolth-light effect absorbed into Daz's body, then familiar dismissed—intended to simulate a divine communion.
- Asha was skeptical; she suspected a test of faith or a charlatan. Multiple deception rolls (including an Inspiration reroll that still failed at 5) nearly collapsed the ruse.
- Daz pivoted strategy after a Religion 18 check reminded him that drow clerics distrust magic users claiming direct Lolth communion without priestly intermediaries. He reframed himself as a delusional mage who genuinely believes he communes with Lolth.
- An Insight 18 check confirmed Asha was looking for useful allies, not true believers. The adjusted approach succeeded.
- Asha revealed her goal: kill Ilvara (corrupted by Zuggtmoy, carrying a mushroom artifact from Neverlight Grove that spreads the infection) and destroy the artifact. She offered Ilvara's magic items as payment.
- Glabbagool was introduced to Asha as Daz's "ooze familiar"; she accepted this. Glabbagool was instructed to stay still and quiet.
- Asha confirmed she and Jorlan are the only uninfected members of Ilvara's party—she through faith, Jorlan through personal hatred of Ilvara. She also clarified the Big Heart Fungus does not transmit Zuggtmoy's spore infection; only Ilvara's mushroom artifact does.

**Pre-Combat Preparation**
- Party placed a Glyph of Warding (triggered) on the bridge on Jorlan's side of the cavern as a defensive trap.
- Zalthir placed magical Darkness behind the party's position.
- Tactics discussed: Glyph of Warding (placed), Dust of Suleiman on the heart fungus, Phantasmal Killer and Fireball as options against Ilvara. Noted that most high-impact options required 4th-level slots and only one was available.
- Thorin borrowed Grygum's light crossbow as a backup ranged option.

**Combat with Ilvara and the Big Heart Fungus**
- Zalthir teleported to Ilvara and struck for 16 damage, established a grapple, struck again for 15 (advantage from grapple), then attempted to fly-carry her into the Dust of Suleiman area—foiled by Ilvara's legendary action (fungal meld/teleport). She reappeared elsewhere and screamed in fury.
- The Big Heart Fungus released a poison spore cloud (20-foot radius, DC 15 CON save). Zalthir failed and was Poisoned; he flew 10 feet up to exit the area.
- Ilvara summoned four drow spore servants (former Velkynvelve prison guards, fungal not undead).
- Thorin called out to Jorlan, luring him into the Glyph of Warding; explosion dealt 21 damage. One spore servant destroyed outright; Jorlan and others variously damaged. Thorin attacked Jorlan with Dawnbringer—hit for 14 damage (natural 20), second attack missed (rolled 2). Sap maneuver applied: Jorlan has disadvantage on next attack.
- A spore servant directed by Ilvara's legendary action attacked Thorin—rolled natural 1 on first strike, hit for 5 on second.
- Grygum cast Dust of Suleiman centered on the Big Heart Fungus: 20 force damage, no save.
- Daz cast 4th-level Fireball (Sculpt Spells) centered between Ilvara and the Zuggtmoy icon. Ilvara saved (DC 15, rolled 19) for 12 damage. The Zuggtmoy icon also took 12 damage.
- Total damage dealt to Ilvara at cut-off: approximately 43 (Zalthir 31, Fireball 12). Session ended mid-combat.

**Resolution (per gm-assist.md reference summary)**
- Ilvara was killed by Grygum's Guiding Bolt. Her body erupted in a cloud of poisonous spores that caught Zalthir and Thorin in the blast. Combat concluded; Jorlan Duskryn fell in the same engagement.
- Asha Vandree survived the fight convinced she was divinely blessed by Lolth. She immediately attempted to assert command over the party, treating non-drow members with contempt.
- Thorin grappled Asha and covered her mouth. Grygum attempted a second Guiding Bolt—this one missed. Zalthir killed Asha with a sustained assault. Nym and Kaelira Duskryn offered sardonic applause.
- Grygum used fire breath to incinerate remaining fungal corruption on Ilvara's platform — corrupted matter bearing Zuggtmoy's influence, not a fragment of the demon lord herself, who remains active at Neverlight Grove. Beneath the ash: a leather-wrapped ceremonial bundle (incense cones, silver bowl, cloth bearing fused Lolth/Zuggtmoy sigils).
- Thorin found a fist-sized pearl-like node at the heart fungus's center, radiating necromantic energy (estimated value: 300 gp to right buyer).

**The Mercenary Revelation**
- Nym and Kaelira Duskryn moved to leave. Pressed, they revealed they were hired in Menzoberranzan to ensure Daz's survival, with their contract specifically naming Ilvara of House Mizzrym and forces of House T'sarran as the threats to neutralize. Contracting party unknown to them.
- Daz attempted to negotiate continued protection; the mercenaries declined—their contract was fulfilled and further service was beyond the party's means.
- Partially burned documents on the drow mage's body confirmed an extraction contract for Daz alive and unharmed; House T'sarran was the party behind that contract.
- **House T'sarran appears in both contracts in opposite roles.** The mercenaries' protection contract named T'sarran as a threat the Duskryn sisters were to neutralize; the burnt extraction contract revealed T'sarran as the house that had hired the kidnapping. Either an unknown patron paid Nym and Kaelira to defend Daz from the same house that had hired the operation that produced the drow mage, or T'sarran is internally divided. This contradiction is the central puzzle the party intends to resolve at Candlekeep.
- A signet ring bearing House T'sarran's seal (found on the drow mage) was identified by Daz as a forgery tool and proof of the house's involvement.
- Nym and Kaelira expressed bafflement that a "nobody" from Menzoberranzan commanded such a high protection price. Kaelira compared Daz to every orphan who believes they have secret noble lineage—then acknowledged the price paid suggested something more.
- Daz proposed that the party investigate together and split proceeds. Party agreed.

**Looting the Outpost**
- Payroll float: 480 gp and 1,200 sp (Ilvara had been carrying Velkynvelve's payroll since the original breakout).
- Prisoner belongings chest: Thorin's tooth (Ilvara had kept it as a trophy), a water-damaged prisoner journal, a flask of Overbright brandy.
- Jailer's ring of keys (the original Velkynvelve keys).
- Asha's body: holy symbol of Lolth, drow chain mail, obsidian prayer beads, sealed wax pouch (contains theological treatise arguing Lolth and Zuggtmoy are one entity—evidence of her corruption).
- Zalthir claimed the drow cloak of elvenkind from Asha's effects (grants stealth; disintegrates in sunlight).
- Ilvara's prayer book: marginalia in a second hand tracking her Zuggtmoy corruption in detail.
- Sealed orders from House Mizzrym: increasingly frantic messages to Ilvara that went unanswered.
- Letter from Neverlight Grove: written by Ilvara's agent before that agent became "fungus food"; references "the bride" and a meeting that never occurred.
- Drow mage's body: House T'sarran signet ring, obsidian wand (captive spider in amber tip; not magical, worth ~150 gp), partially burned extraction contract.
- Ilvara's body: prayer book with Zuggtmoy marginalia, living serpent-head scourge (corrupted; aerosolizes spores when used; Dawnbringer could potentially purify it).
- Ceremonial bundle (beneath heart fungus): incense cones, small silver bowl, fused Lolth/Zuggtmoy sigil cloth.
- Necromantic pearl node: from heart fungus center (300 gp value to appropriate buyer).
- Spore sacks: harvestable by an alchemist.
- Guard bodies: gold, short swords, long sword, steel whistle.
- Dawnbringer expressed strong objection to being used as a purification tool for the serpent-head scourge; was ultimately persuaded (the Vaultmaster Locking Scabbard was decisive). The cleansing was performed and completed: the scourge is now a clean magical weapon, free of the spore corruption that aerosolized on use.

**Emergence and Forward Planning**
- The party reached the surface—the Overbright—after months in the Underdark.
- Glabbagool experienced sunlight for the first time, peering from the bag of holding.
- Drow cloak of elvenkind (Zalthir's) disintegrates in sunlight (noted as a constraint).
- Party agreed to travel to Candlekeep to research House T'sarran, Jimjar, and the mystery of why Daz was worth such investment.
- Individual downtime intentions noted: Thorin considering finding a "sword whisperer" for Dawnbringer; Grygum planning Bahamut lore research.
- Party chose to invest recovered wealth in adventuring gear rather than business ventures.
