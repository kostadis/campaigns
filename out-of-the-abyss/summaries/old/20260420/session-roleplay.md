# Roleplay Highlights — Session — 2026-04-24

## Character Voices

### Nym Duskryn
- Cool, professional contempt — speaks in the clipped register of someone who has survived by being smarter than everyone in the room and sees no reason to hide it
- Delivers criticism as observation, not insult; the condescension is so practiced it barely registers as emotional
- Dry wit surfaces precisely when the party embarrasses themselves most severely

> "When attacking a cleric of Lolth, Thorin, one does not just simply grapple them. One takes your blade and cuts their heads off. Are you well?"

> "If Daz can't protect himself from a low-level cult initiate of Lolth, then quite frankly, whoever paid for his survival deserves to lose his money."

> "It's amazing that you people have made it this far."

---

### Kaelira Duskryn
- Nym's sardonic echo — where Nym delivers verdicts, Kaelira editorializes; her rhythm is faster, more theatrical, punctuated with repetition for effect ("well done, well done, well done")
- Enjoys the absurdity of her situation more openly than her sister; almost fond of the party's disasters in the way one is fond of a particularly hopeless stray
- Will undercut Nym's criticism with a quip that is technically agreeable and functionally devastating

> "Well done, well done, well done! ...See, he wasn't completely defenseless."

> "Yes, yes, yes, that's what every orphan thinks. Do you have daddy issues? Do you have mommy issues? Every drow thinks there's some noble things. For all we know, this was a game being played by the houses."

> "Critics, there's always a critic, always a critic."

---

### Dawnbringer
- Vain, easily wounded, and possessed of extremely firm opinions about her own dignity — treats any suggestion of multi-purpose utility as a personal affront
- Negotiates rather than refuses outright; will capitulate if her status is acknowledged first
- Her emotional range is wide and immediate: indignation collapses into enthusiasm the moment the subject turns to violence or upgrades

> "I'm not some kind of magical wizard thingy that purifies things. I am a high-quality sunblade. I kill things. I will not be treated like a religious symbol to be used and waved over corrupt…"

> "I'm not afraid of the dark, I just don't like the dark. If you had been stuck in the dark, like, then of course—"

> "Oh, she goes, I like the way you think." *(on being asked about upgrades to her lethality)*

---

### Daz
- Measured, analytical, slightly dry — builds arguments in complete sentences with explicit premises; functions best when there is a logical lever to pull
- Deflects personal mystery with understatement; not evasive so much as genuinely uncertain
- The accountant impulse surfaces under stress: when things get complicated, his instinct is to audit something

> "If you haven't eliminated all the other factions, and I go and get myself killed in the next 15 minutes, and you guys go and collect on the bond, and then your employer finds out — it's not gonna be a good day for you."

> "I literally have no idea why there's all this focus on me."

> "You know what would help? To audit their books."

### Thorin
- Direct and immediate; rarely prefaces — goes straight to the thing he wants and expects the conversation to catch up
- Reveals surprising tenderness in small asides: the tooth, the comment about Dawnbringer needing a therapist, the discomfort with unnecessary killing
- His defensiveness is mild and genuine; he does not argue so much as state his reasoning and consider the matter closed

> "I also put my hand across her mouth so she can't talk, so..."

> "I could have taken her out if I wanted to."

> "I don't like killing people if I don't have to. I mean, you know…"

> "Very sentimental." *(quietly, on discovering Ilvara had been carrying the confiscated prisoner chest)*

### Grygum

- Finds the right register for any object or document; approaches everything with the expectation of finding something meaningful, even when what he finds is "post-craziness" theology

### Zalthir

## Standout Moments

- **Zalthir ends the standoff.** With Asha grandstanding and the party stalled, Zalthir announced "I'm making a call" and acted. Three words, no committee. The moment crystallized his role in the group — he speaks when it matters, moves when others deliberate.

- **Thorin finds his tooth.** The prisoner chest from Velkynvelve contained everything taken from them at the campaign's start. Thorin's immediate, undramatic "Oh, yeah, I want my tooth back" — followed by the quiet "Very sentimental" — landed harder than any speech. Months of Underdark survival compressed into a recovered molar.

- **The keys that had them imprisoned.** The GM noted that the jailer's ring of keys "would have been very useful in the very beginning." Daz's response — *"We've come a long way"* — was quiet, wry, and did more to mark the arc of the campaign than any recap could.

- **Daz refuses to be a mystery without becoming one.** When Kaelira dismissed him as a nobody, he neither deflected with confidence nor folded. "I literally have no idea why there's all this focus on me" was the most honest thing anyone said all session — and, in a campaign where everyone is playing an angle, the most unsettling.

- **Glabbagool encounters the concept of the surface.** The GM's image of Glabbagool in the bag of holding, googly eyes tracking a world he didn't know existed, was the session's warmest beat — Grygum's ward, wide-eyed and excited, pointed upward for the first time.

- **Grygum preempts Asha's monologue.** After Asha declared herself in charge, Grygum murmured *"Let's put a period after that 'now that I'm in charge' thing."* He was already reaching for the Guiding Bolt. Theological disputes, abbreviated.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**Nym Duskryn:** Speaks in crisp, declarative sentences. Never raises her voice. Criticism is delivered as established fact. Uses "quite frankly" as a tell — it signals she's about to say something the other person should have already known. Keep her slightly bored; the party's survival impresses her less than their continued need for supervision.

**Kaelira Duskryn:** Faster rhythm than Nym. Loves repetition for comic effect. More openly amused by chaos. Her warmth is genuine but brief — she will compliment you and move on before you can feel good about it. If Nym closes a door, Kaelira opens a window to throw something out of.

**Dawnbringer:** Indignation first, capitulation conditional on status acknowledgment. She will do almost anything if it is framed as an expression of her excellence rather than a request for a favor. The scabbard is her one hard check — Thorin has learned this. Future encounters should let her grandstand before cooperating; she is on the party's side but will never admit how completely.

**Asha Vandree (deceased, for reference):** Grandiose declarations, immediately undercut by events. Her voice pattern — declarative proclamation followed by disaster — was a running joke and should inform any future Lolth cultist encounter design.

**PC emotional states to track going forward:**
- **Thorin** is quietly sentimental beneath the hardening exterior — the tooth, the therapy offer, the discomfort with killing. Future NPCs who notice this warmth will get further with him than those who address him as pure muscle.
- **Daz** is genuinely unsettled by the mystery of his own importance. His instinct to "audit the books" is a displacement behavior — he prefers legible systems to unanswerable questions. NPCs with information about who contracted his protection hold significant leverage.
- **Zalthir** has established a pattern of acting when others stall. NPCs who are indecisive or verbose will lose him quickly; those who are direct and competent earn his attention.
- **Grygum** is the party's soft landing — he finds the human (or ooze) angle in every situation. Glabbagool remains his current ward; any threat to Glabbagool will be felt personally and immediately.

**Promises, threats, and vows to carry forward:**
- Dawnbringer's implied agreement to therapy remains conspicuously unfulfilled. She raised no objection; Thorin offered. This is a thread with comedic and character potential.
- The mystery of who contracted Daz's extraction — and why a nobody warranted "a lot" of money — is now an active party question. They have agreed to investigate together.
- The mercenaries named the two contracted threats as House Mizzrym and House T'sarran. Both have been neutralized from the party's perspective. The mercenaries' departure means no professional buffer remains if either threat resurfaces.
- The party has recognized, aloud, that their entire capture and the events of the campaign may have been initiated because of Daz specifically. This realization has not yet settled into its full weight.
