# Asha's Last Stand and Daz's First Clue

Date: Apr 22nd, 2026

## Summary
With the chaos of battle finally settled, the party turned their attention to the two Drow mercenaries who had fought alongside them — Nym and Kaelira Duskryn — as the pair made a quiet but deliberate move toward the exit. When pressed, the two professionals revealed that they had been hired in Menzoberranzan by an unknown benefactor to ensure Daz's survival, their contract specifically naming Ilvara of House Mizzrym and the forces of House T'sarran as the threats to neutralize. Daz attempted to negotiate continued protection, but Nym and Kaelira Duskryn made it abundantly clear that their services came at a price far beyond what the party could afford. They regarded Asha Vandree — the surviving Drow cleric — as beneath their concern, flatly stating that if Daz couldn't handle someone of her caliber on his own, then whoever had paid for his protection had wasted their coin.

Asha Vandree, meanwhile, had emerged from the chaos utterly convinced that she had been divinely touched by Lolth. She had watched her entire crew descend into madness and ruin, and yet she had survived — a sign, she believed, of her singular blessing. Riding high on this delusion, she immediately began issuing commands to the party, treating the non-Drow members with open contempt and the Drow males with barely concealed disdain. Thorin had heard enough. He moved swiftly, closing the distance and seizing her in a powerful grapple, clamping a hand over her mouth before she could invoke another word of divine authority. Nym watched with visible exasperation, muttering that one does not simply grapple a cleric of Lolth — one takes a blade to their neck. Grygum attempted to strike her down with a bolt of holy light, but Asha's proclamations of divine protection seemed almost prophetic as the attack missed entirely. It was Zalthir who ended it, unleashing a brutal and relentless assault that left Asha Vandree dead on the floor of the outpost. The mercenaries offered a slow, sardonic applause.

With their enemies defeated, the party turned to the grim but satisfying work of looting the outpost. Among the fallen guards and the payroll chest Ilvara had been hauling since the breakout from Velkynvelve, they recovered a substantial sum of gold and silver — the salary of soldiers who would never collect it. A chest of confiscated prisoner belongings yielded something far more personal for Thorin: his tooth, which Ilvara had apparently kept as a trophy throughout her long pursuit of the party. Also tucked inside were a water-damaged journal belonging to a presumed dead prisoner and a flask of Overbright brandy that had somehow survived the chaos completely intact.

Asha's body gave up a holy symbol of Lolth, Drow chain mail, obsidian prayer beads, and a sealed wax pouch bearing her personal seal. When Daz examined the pouch, he recognized it as the kind of private correspondence Lolth's clerics used when they wanted to know if their secrets had been tampered with. Inside was a long, tedious theological treatise attempting to argue that Lolth and Zuggtmoy were one and the same — the written ravings of a mind already lost. Zalthir claimed the Drow cloak of elvenkind found among Asha's belongings, a garment that granted its wearer an uncanny gift for going unseen — though it would crumble to dust the moment sunlight touched it.

Ilvara's own belongings told a darker story. Her prayer book was filled with marginalia written in a different hand, tracking her slow corruption by Zuggtmoy in chilling detail. Sealed orders from House Mizzrym confirmed she had still been receiving instructions from her noble house right up until she vanished — the messages growing increasingly frantic as she stopped responding. A letter from Neverlight Grove, written by her agent before that unfortunate soul became fungus food, referenced something called "the bride" and a meeting that had never taken place. On the body of the Drow mage, the party found a signet ring bearing the seal of House T'sarran, which Daz immediately recognized as both proof of the house's involvement and a tool that could be used to forge orders in their name. Partially burnt documents confirmed what they had begun to suspect: there had been a contract to extract Daz alive and unharmed, and House T'sarran had been the ones behind it.

Thorin investigated the heart of the corrupted fungal mass that had dominated the chamber, finding a dense, pearl-like node at its center radiating a faint but unmistakable necromantic energy — the kind of thing a discerning buyer would pay handsomely for. Grygum then turned his fire breath on the remaining spore corruption atop Ilvara's elevated stone platform, reducing it to ash. Beneath the smoldering remains, they uncovered a leather-wrapped bundle: incense cones, a small silver bowl, and a cloth stitched with the fused sigils of both Lolth and Zuggtmoy, one woven inside the other. It was the physical embodiment of Ilvara's madness — proof that something deeply wrong was spreading through the Underdark, something that could drive even a devoted cleric to abandon her goddess in favor of a demon queen of fungus.

When Nym and Kaelira Duskryn were shown the burnt extraction contract, they examined it with cool professionalism before confirming what it implied: someone had paid House T'sarran a great deal of money to bring Daz in alive. The mercenaries found the whole situation baffling, noting that Daz had no noble house, no known lineage, and no obvious reason to be worth such an investment. Kaelira Duskryn compared him to every orphan who had ever convinced themselves they were secretly important, though even she admitted that the price paid suggested something more than wishful thinking. Daz, for his part, was deeply unsettled — and deeply curious. He proposed to the party that they investigate together, splitting whatever they uncovered, and the group agreed that answers were worth pursuing.

After months of trudging through the darkness of the Underdark, the party finally clawed their way to the surface and into the blinding, glorious light of the world above. Glabbagool, the intelligent gelatinous cube who had traveled with them through it all, peered out from a bag of holding with wide, astonished eyes, seeing sunlight for the very first time. Dawnbringer, the sentient sunblade, threw a minor tantrum at the suggestion of being used as a purification tool rather than the high-quality weapon it considered itself to be, though it was ultimately persuaded — with great reluctance — to help cleanse the corrupted serpent-headed scourge found among Ilvara's belongings. The party set their sights on Candlekeep, the great fortress-library on the Sword Coast, where scholars of every obscure discipline gathered. There, they hoped to unravel the mystery of House T'sarran, learn the truth about their enigmatic companion Jimjar, and perhaps discover why a self-described nobody from Menzoberranzan had become the center of so much dangerous attention.

## Locations
### Menzoberranzan
The primary city of the Drow, mentioned as the location where the contract for Daz's protection was brokered and paid for by an unknown benefactor.

### Drow Outpost
The site of the recent battle where the party recovers the payroll float, prisoner belongings, and various personal effects from the defeated Drow, including Ilvara's elevated stone platform.

### The Overbright
The surface world above the Underdark, characterized by sunlight, where the party finally emerges after months of underground travel. Drow cloaks and equipment disintegrate in its light.

### Candlekeep
A massive fortress-library on the Sword Coast, south of Waterdeep, home to countless scholars. The party plans to travel here to research House T'sarran, Jimjar, and Daz's mysterious background.

## Items
### Payroll Float
A large sum of 480 gold pieces and 1200 silver pieces recovered from the outpost, originally intended as salary for the guards at Velkynvelve. Ilvara had been carrying it since the breakout.

### Jailer's Ring of Keys
The very set of keys that originally kept the party imprisoned at the start of their journey, found among the outpost's belongings — a bittersweet reminder of how far they have come.

### Thorin's Tooth
A sentimental item belonging to Thorin, recovered from a chest of confiscated prisoner belongings that Ilvara had been carrying throughout her pursuit of the party.

### Overbright Brandy
A stoppered clay flask of brandy from the surface world, found somehow still intact among the prisoner's confiscated goods.

### Cloak of Elvenkind (Drow Version)
A Drow-crafted cloak that grants stealth advantages and requires attunement, but disintegrates if exposed to sunlight. Claimed by Zalthir after being found on Asha Vandree.

### Prayer Book of Lolth
A religious text belonging to Ilvara, featuring marginalia written in a different hand that tracks her corruption by Zuggtmoy, providing a clear timeline of her descent into madness.

### Sealed Orders from House Mizzrym
Recent orders found on Ilvara confirming she was still in contact with her noble house before her disappearance. The increasingly frantic messages reveal that House Mizzrym was desperately trying to reach her before she went silent.

### Letter from Neverlight Grove
A partially legible letter from Ilvara's agent who was stationed at Neverlight Grove before dying. It references 'the bride' and a meeting that never took place.

### Living Serpent Head Scourge
A five-tailed whip with living serpent heads as lashes, found on Ilvara. It is corrupted and aerosolizes poisonous spores when used in combat. Dawnbringer can potentially purify it.

### Sealed Wax Pouch
A pouch sealed with Asha Vandree's personal seal, containing a lengthy and tedious theological exploration attempting to reconcile the worship of Lolth and Zuggtmoy as the same entity — evidence of her post-madness beliefs.

### Signet Ring of House T'sarran
A noble signet ring found on the body of the Drow mage, identified by Daz as a tool for forging orders and as proof of House T'sarran's direct involvement in the attack on the party.

### Obsidian Wand
An arcane focus tipped with a captive spider preserved in amber, found on the Drow mage. Not magical, but valued at 150 gold pieces for its craftsmanship.

### Necromantic Pearl Node
A fist-sized, dense, pearl-like node found at the center of the heart fungus. It radiates residual necromantic energy and is worth 300 gold pieces to the right buyer.

### Spore Sacks
Intact biological containers harvested from the fungus. An alchemist in a city could potentially transform them into useful substances.

### Steel Whistle
A standard guard whistle recovered from the bodies of the former Velkynvelve Drow guards, a relic of the outpost's days before everything went wrong.

### Ceremonial Bundle
A leather-wrapped collection uncovered beneath the burnt heart fungus, containing incense cones, a small silver bowl, and cloth stitched with the fused sigils of both Lolth and Zuggtmoy — physical evidence of Ilvara's syncretic madness.

### Vault Master Locking Scabbard
A high-quality, specialized locking scabbard purchased for Dawnbringer, referenced during the debate over using the sunblade for purification. Features a scalable upgrade model, humorously dubbed the 'Vault Master 2000.'

## Spells
### Guiding Bolt
Cast by Grygum against Asha Vandree at the start of combat. The bolt of light failed to connect, with Asha proclaiming her divine protection from Lolth.

### Fire Breath
Used by Grygum to incinerate the corrupted heart fungus and the remaining spore remnant of Zuggtmoy on Ilvara's elevated platform, uncovering a hidden ceremonial bundle beneath the ash.

## NPCs
### Nym Duskryn
A professional Drow mercenary and fighter hired in Menzoberranzan to protect Daz. He is blunt and critical, quick to point out the party's tactical shortcomings — such as their lack of dispel magic — and strictly adheres to the terms of his contract. He views killing a cleric of Lolth as a matter requiring a blade, not a grapple.

### Kaelira Duskryn
A Drow wizard and mercenary partner to Nym, hired alongside him to protect Daz. She is sardonic and dismissive, mocking critics while acknowledging the party's capability after they dispatch Asha Vandree. She helps analyze the mysterious contracts and questions why a 'nobody' like Daz would command such a high protection fee.

### Asha Vandree
A Drow cleric of Lolth who survived the recent chaos and became convinced she was divinely chosen by Lolth to lead. Her arrogance and attempts to assert command over the party led directly to her death at Zalthir's hands. Her body yielded a holy symbol of Lolth, Drow chain mail, prayer beads, and a sealed wax pouch containing her theological writings fusing Lolth and Zuggtmoy.

### Ilvara
The defeated Drow leader whose body and belongings are looted during this session. She had been carrying a chest of prisoner belongings, a prayer book with Zuggtmoy marginalia, sealed orders from House Mizzrym, and a corrupted five-tailed whip with living serpent heads. Her prayer book and the ceremonial bundle found beneath the heart fungus confirm her descent into madness through the syncretism of Lolth and Zuggtmoy worship.

### Dawnbringer
A sentient sunblade carried by the party who throws a temper tantrum at the suggestion of being used as a purification tool, insisting on being treated as a high-quality weapon. Dawnbringer also expresses displeasure at the idea of being in the dark and is intrigued by the prospect of being made more deadly.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent gelatinous cube traveling with the party, experiencing the surface world for the first time by peering out from a bag of holding with wide, googly eyes.

### Ezra
A quest giver who originally discovered and recruited the party. It is noted that the party's rapid rise in power and success has significantly boosted Ezra's reputation as a talent acquisition specialist.
