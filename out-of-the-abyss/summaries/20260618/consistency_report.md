## Consistency Report — Chapter 56: "Never mind the murders."

---

### Issue 1 — Venue mismatch: "Grand Hall" does not exist

**Location:** Summary ("flooded toward the Grand Hall for an emergency debate"), Scene "The Academic Distraction" ("hold an emergency debate in the Grand Hall"), and Locations section ("Grand Hall" entry)

**Issue:** The disputation venue is called the "Grand Hall" in the Summary and one Scene section. No location named "Grand Hall" exists anywhere in the Candlekeep location inventory.

**Evidence:** All GM prep materials consistently name the venue the **Oval Theatre/Theater**. The 20260615\_block2 prep: *"The Oval Theatre. Right now. Half the Avowed are already there."* The Locations section of the same recap correctly adds a separate "Oval Theater" entry. The recap therefore has two Locations entries for one place, and the wrong name in the Summary.

**Suggested fix:** Replace every instance of "Grand Hall" with "Oval Theater." Remove the redundant "Grand Hall" Locations entry; its content should be merged into the existing "Oval Theater" entry.

### Issue 2 — Alkrist's title: "keeper" vs. Great Reader

**Location:** Summary ("Alkrist, a dragonborn keeper"), Scene "Interviews in the Southern Dining Hall" ("Alkrist, a dragonborn keeper"), NPCs section ("A Dragonborn keeper")

**Issue:** Alkrist is called "a keeper" three times. The only "Keeper" title in Candlekeep's hierarchy is **Keeper of Tomes** — Janussi's murdered position. Alkrist holds no such title; he is a **Great Reader** and member of the Council of Twelve (Bookwyrm's nephew per arc materials).

**Evidence:** candlekeep\_murders\_arc.md Session 4: "Alkrist · Drakonoikos · bronze · THE MURDERER." All council-meeting scenes describe him as a Great Reader. candlekeep\_arc\_flowchart.md Session 4: "Reader interviews: A'lai, **Alkrist (the killer)**." The Candlekeep officer/title hierarchy (candlekeep\_locations.md): Council of 12 = Keeper of Tomes + First Reader + eight Great Readers + Gatewarden + Lorekeeper. No "keeper" for a dragonborn scholar.

**Suggested fix:** Replace "a dragonborn keeper" with "a dragonborn Great Reader" (or "a member of the Council of Great Readers") in all three locations.

### Issue 3 — Kazryn Nyantani given a non-existent title

**Location:** NPCs section ("Kazryn Nyantani — The chief cleric")

**Issue:** Kazryn is called "the chief cleric." No such title appears anywhere in the campaign materials. Candlekeep has a Lorekeeper (Kei Tigersteel) and a Keeper of Tomes, but no "chief cleric" role. Kazryn is a Great Reader.

**Evidence:** campaign\_state.md NPC table: "Kazryn Nyantani, A'lai Aivenmore, Alkrist — Council members." candlekeep\_murders\_arc.md: "Kazryn Nyantani — Pavilion Naturalis. Frail, bitter. Janussi's spurned ex-lover. A'lai's current lover — unwitting alibi." No title given. player\_murder\_investigation\_tracker: "Kazryn Nyantani | Distraught." No title.

**Suggested fix:** Change "The chief cleric" to "A Great Reader." Remove or omit the title entirely.

### Issue 4 — 1001 Tashalian Nights: the fictional poisoning method is misquoted in the Summary

**Location:** Summary ("a gift whose pages had been coated in Midnight Tears")

**Issue:** The Summary describes the bookmarked tale as involving "a gift whose pages had been coated in Midnight Tears" — a book. The scene transcript ("Investigation Deepens") and the gm-assist.md both record the actual quote from play: *"a military commander who is poisoned after being given a gift of surrender. A magic* [horn] *whose mouthpiece had been covered with midnight tears."* The delivery vessel in the fictional tale is a **horn**, not a book with poisoned pages. The Summary conflates the real murder's method (book, licked pages) with the Tashalian Nights tale's method (horn mouthpiece).

**Evidence:** Scene "Investigation Deepens": *"The bookmarked tale described 'a military commander who is poisoned after being given a gift of surrender. A magic [horn] whose mouthpiece had been covered with midnight tears.'"* gm-assist.md Memorable Moments: same verbatim quote. Summary says "pages."

**Suggested fix:** Correct the Summary to: "a gift — a horn whose mouthpiece had been coated in Midnight Tears." This preserves the parallel structure to Janussi's murder while accurately reflecting what the book contains.

### Issue 5 — Fheminor Scrivenbark: species discrepancy in Nibbles' identification

**Location:** Summary ("an unpleasant girl gnome"), Scene "The Squirrel's Testimony" ("'the unfriendly girl gnome' (Fheminor Scrivenbark)"), NPCs section (correctly calls her "a halfling" but also says she was "identified by Nibbles as the 'unpleasant girl gnome'")

**Issue:** Nibbles describes the first visitor as a "gnome." The recap parenthetically identifies this as Fheminor Scrivenbark without noting that Fheminor is a **halfling**, not a gnome. The NPCs section thus contains an internal contradiction: "A Great Reader and halfling who visited the apothecary … identified by Nibbles as the 'unpleasant girl gnome.'" Future sessions relying on Nibbles' testimony to confirm Fheminor's identity will hit this discrepancy.

**Evidence:** All campaign sources are unanimous: candlekeep\_day\_one.md: "Old halfling. Careful." player\_murder\_investigation\_tracker: "Old, careful halfling." candlekeep\_murders\_arc.md Session 3: "Old, careful halfling." NPCs section of this recap (internally): "A Great Reader and halfling."

**Suggested fix:** Add a parenthetical or editorial note acknowledging the discrepancy: e.g., *"'the unfriendly girl gnome' (likely Fheminor Scrivenbark — squirrels may not reliably distinguish halflings from gnomes)"*. Update the NPCs entry to flag this: "Nibbles identified her as a gnome; all other sources confirm she is a halfling."
