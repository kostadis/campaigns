## Flags

### [1] Em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> then learned the man who would have fired him was a corpse, and spent one warm hour thinking everything had resolved in his favor — before discovering that the people he'd spent that hour mocking were possibly the only ones in Candlekeep keeping him alive, and that even we were doing the arithmetic on whether it was worth the effort.

**Why:** Prose em-dash; the spec wants measured, precise sentences, and a period sharpens the reversal.
**Suggested rewrite:** Replace ` — before discovering` with `. Then he discovered`.

### [2] Em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> I filed Daral under *desperate*, which is the most useful column a man can occupy, because desperation makes people honest and generous in equal measure. Thorin softened it eventually — "look, dude, we'll save you, we're not that evil" — but the price was already extracted.

**Why:** Two prose em-dashes bracketing the verbatim quote; convert the outer punctuation to commas/period (the quote itself stays verbatim).
**Suggested rewrite:** `Thorin softened it eventually, "look, dude, we'll save you, we're not that evil," but the price was already extracted.`

### [3] Em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> The Oak Tree Apothecary, he said, between the Sea Warden's Tower and the House of Mechanus, a treehouse grown into the roots of an evergreen oak. A satyr druid named Leuwin, elderly and absent-minded, with a pet squirrel, who could identify the poison from the book definitively.

**Why:** No em-dash here — clean. (Noting that the surrounding paragraphs at lines 18, 20, 24, 26, 30, 32 each carry prose em-dashes; convert each: "on the hook anyway — a warm introduction" to a colon; "an hour without supervision and to" already clean; "the cold iron of it" clean; "the spell that stashes" use a comma; "the retrieval token" use a colon; "Glabbagool tried to help — an eye surfacing" use a comma.)
**Suggested rewrite:** For "keep the man on the hook anyway — a warm introduction, he reasoned" use a colon: "on the hook anyway: a warm introduction, he reasoned". For "Glabbagool tried to help — an eye surfacing through the ooze, hopeful — and I came up emptier" use commas: "Glabbagool tried to help, an eye surfacing through the ooze, hopeful, and I came up emptier".

### [4] On-the-nose simile / cliché
> The pattern was there. I simply could not make it resolve, like a name on the tip of a tongue that belongs to someone else.

**Why:** "tip of a tongue" is a stock simile; the spec already gives Daz this exact beat ("a name in the column he can't reconcile") in his own bookkeeping idiom, so the generic simile dilutes it.
**Suggested rewrite:** `The pattern was there. I simply could not make it resolve — a name I could read in every column but the one that names it.`

### [5] Cliché simile
> let the spell read the object the way a clerk reads a ledger he didn't write

**Why:** The spec's author-note explicitly warns against letting "ledger" dominate; here it anchors a simile when the scene already runs heavy on columns/account/filed.
**Suggested rewrite:** `let the spell read the object the way an auditor reads an account someone else kept` (swaps the over-used "ledger" for the spec's rotated vocabulary).

## Verdict
High fidelity — the audit voice, the column-filing, the failed-reframe tell at the close are all squarely Daz, and the "gap in the account" ending is excellent. The strongest issue is mechanical: prose em-dashes recur in nearly every paragraph and should be converted to commas/colons/periods; the only genuine voice softness is the stock "tip of a tongue" simile. Spot-edit, do not re-narrate.
