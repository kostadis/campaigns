## Flags

### [1] em-dash in prose
> Not bad — paper, lamp oil, old stone warmed by sun I had not seen in months — just wrong, the way anything smells when you have spent too long breathing rock.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — em-dashes in narration prose should convert to comma/colon/period; the spec's cadence is declarative full-stops, not parenthetical asides.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Not bad. Paper, lamp oil, old stone warmed by sun I had not seen in months. Just wrong, the way anything smells when you have spent too long breathing rock."

### [2] em-dash in prose
> An eye rose out of the grey of him — slow, hopeful, the way he does everything now — and pointed itself at the bright thing in Daz's hand, wanting to be useful.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — two prose em-dashes; the modifier stack also leans warm ("slow, hopeful") in a single breath the spec would clip.
**Suggested rewrite:** "An eye rose out of the grey of him, slow, hopeful, the way he does everything now. It pointed itself at the bright thing in Daz's hand, wanting to be useful."

### [3] em-dash in prose
> Glabbagool survived Blingdenstone, the ooze crisis, the inside of a bag — and his instinct, every time, is still to reach toward the problem.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — prose em-dash; spec uses a full-stop where another voice would dash.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Glabbagool survived Blingdenstone, the ooze crisis, the inside of a bag. His instinct, every time, is still to reach toward the problem."

### [4] register-wrong vocabulary / generic prose
> So I turned to the murder, because the murder at least had a structure, and structure I can work.

**Why:** "structure" twice is on the scan-list and clinical; the spec grounds him in sensory/tactical vocabulary (tunnel slope, gait, the shape of a threat), not abstract systems-language.
**Suggested rewrite:** "So I turned to the murder. The murder had edges I could get a grip on. That I can work."

### [5] cliché / on-the-nose simile
> A killer who buys two outcomes with one cup of poison is a killer who has already drawn the board and decided where every piece lands.

**Why:** The chess-board metaphor is generic-strategist prose and over-explains the read; the spec has him name a threat once, plainly, not gloss it with extended figuration.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Two outcomes, one cup of poison. Whoever did this had already decided how it ends."

### [6] tell-not-show / explaining reasoning aloud
> Convenient, that it all pointed one direction. I don't trust convenient. But I don't dismiss it either.

**Why:** The spec forbids explaining his reasoning aloud; "Convenient." alone is in-voice (it appears verbatim in the spec), but the two trailing sentences workshop the hedge the way the spec says he never does.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Convenient, that it all pointed one direction. I filed that too."

### [7] generic fantasy simile
> Daral arrived at the name like a man arriving at the last line of a poem he'd written.

**Why:** Literary simile is convergence with a warmer, more lyrical narrator; Zalthir registers behavior flatly ("A drama queen.") rather than ornamenting it.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Daral arrived at the name slowly, savoring it. A'lai Aivenmore."

## Verdict
Fidelity is mostly solid — the dry asides ("Which is a low bar. But."), the buried Glabbagool affection, and the uncertain-monk aphorism all land. The strongest issue is the lyrical/strategist drift in the murder-theory beat (flags 5 and 7), where the prose climbs into figuration the spec explicitly forbids; spot-edit those plus the em-dashes and the "structure" slip rather than re-narrating.
