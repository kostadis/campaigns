## Flags

### [1] em-dash in prose
> And Kazryn Nyantani — Tani — the chief cleric.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — prose em-dashes used as parenthetical; spec sets names down as flat declaratives.
**Suggested rewrite:** "And Kazryn Nyantani, called Tani, the chief cleric."

### [2] em-dash in prose
> The ooze had come out of the bag eager, the way he is now eager about everything on the surface — *we're going on a secret mission!* — and the two of us moved as one thing through the Readers' Tower.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — the dashes bracketing the italic VTT span are prose punctuation (keep the italics, drop the dashes); also "moved as one thing" edges toward warmth the spec keeps cold.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The ooze had come out of the bag eager, the way he is now eager about everything on the surface. *We're going on a secret mission!* The two of us moved together through the Readers' Tower."

### [3] tell-not-show emotional commentary
> Whatever passes for a smile in a grey ooze, he was wearing it. I was not going to say out loud that I found the partnership easy. I just used it.

**Why:** The spec buries the Glabbagool bond in *what he marks*, not in naming the feeling; "I found the partnership easy" states the warmth outright, which the spec says the voice never climbs into.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Whatever passes for a smile in a grey ooze, he was wearing it. I noted it. I just used it."

### [4] em-dash in prose / generic prose
> On the bedside table lay a book — black leather, stars and gold filigree, the sort of thing a man buys to be the kind of man who owns it.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — prose em-dash; spec favors the full-stop or colon for this kind of appositive.
**Suggested rewrite:** "On the bedside table lay a book. Black leather, stars and gold filigree, the sort of thing a man buys to be the kind of man who owns it."

### [6] em-dash in prose
> The bookmark sat on the tale of a military commander, poisoned by a gift of surrender — a magic horn whose mouthpiece had been coated in Midnight Tears.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — prose em-dash; convert to colon, which the spec tolerates for a flat reveal.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The bookmark sat on the tale of a military commander, poisoned by a gift of surrender: a magic horn whose mouthpiece had been coated in Midnight Tears."

### [7] generic prose / over-written
> A staircase of locked doors, each step taller than the last.

**Why:** Decorative metaphor for a plain mechanical fact (each room harder to search); the spec renders constraints as terse tactical statements, not figurative flourishes.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Each room would be harder than the one before."

## Verdict
Fidelity is strong — the in-character interiority of Thorin and Daz rendered as italic asides is a clever fit for how Zalthir reads the room, and the buried-affection discipline mostly holds. The strongest issue is flag [3], where the Glabbagool warmth gets named outright instead of marked-and-filed; spot-edit that line plus the cluster of prose em-dashes rather than re-narrating.
