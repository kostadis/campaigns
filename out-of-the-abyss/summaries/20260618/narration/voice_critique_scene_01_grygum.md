## Flags

### [1] em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> Someone had styled it as an internal topocentric protocol, which is an enormous amount of robe for a poison, but the Underdark teaches you that the people who kill you usually want it documented first.

**Why:** Not an em-dash flag — clean. But "topocentric protocol" is borrowed analytical jargon the LLM reached for; Grygum reasons from what he observed and theology, not technical taxonomy (Low INT, Investigation −1 per spec). The aside lands, but the noun phrase is off-register.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Someone had dressed it up as a sacred protocol, which is an enormous amount of robe for a poison, but the Underdark teaches you that the people who kill you usually want it documented first."

### [2] em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> "Regardless of where you are. So if you did continue to ride west, you could essentially out — out." Outrun it. Stay ahead of the dark side of the world and the poison never finds its hour.

**Why:** The dash inside `"..."` is verbatim VTT dialogue and stays. But the trailing prose "Stay ahead of the dark side of the world and the poison never finds its hour" tips into lyrical fantasy phrasing ("never finds its hour"); Grygum files mechanics flatly rather than rounding them off poetically.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Outrun it. Stay ahead of the dark side of the world and midnight never catches you."

### [3] em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> For that crowd a way to step between planes is a convenient thing to keep in a pocket — which is also why the poison is useless against a mind flayer or a githyanki, who cross between worlds the way the rest of us blink.

**Why:** Prose em-dash, flag for conversion. Also "cross between worlds the way the rest of us blink" is an on-the-nose simile; Grygum's late-arc voice is precision, not flourish (spec: "the theatricality has nearly drained out and what remains is just precision").
**Suggested rewrite:** Replace dash with a period: "...keep in a pocket. Which is also why the poison is useless against a mind flayer or a githyanki, who cross between worlds for free."

### [4] em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> He'd taken my answer to the Question of the Age — the triangle, three options, the thing I'd half-built out of Stroud's Gambit and never expected anyone to take seriously — and he'd turned it into a quadrangle.

**Why:** Two prose em-dashes; convert to commas. The bracketed structure reads as nested subordination, which contradicts the spec's paratactic rule ("coordinate clauses chained with 'and' rather than nested subordination").
**Suggested rewrite:** "He'd taken my answer to the Question of the Age, the triangle, three options, the thing I'd half-built out of Stroud's Gambit and never expected anyone to take seriously, and he'd turned it into a quadrangle."

### [5] em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> The dining room, for one. Where the meals were served — and where, if you were a poisoner, you'd have wanted to be.

**Why:** Prose em-dash; convert to comma. The clause itself is in-voice (tactical, observational).
**Suggested rewrite:** "Where the meals were served, and where, if you were a poisoner, you'd have wanted to be."

### [6] em-dash (mechanical scan A) / voice-spec conflict
> And in his current state he was very much hoping nobody would mention to Zalthir what he'd said about dragonborn — drunk, barroom talk, not meant to be taken seriously.

**Why:** Prose em-dash; convert. Clean tactical read otherwise (passive 18, "notices lying correctly" — in-voice).
**Suggested rewrite:** "...what he'd said about dragonborn. Drunk, barroom talk, not meant to be taken seriously."

### [7] tell-not-show / generic
> He was glowing.

**Why:** Names the feeling as a stock descriptor rather than rendering it through observed behavior; Grygum "buries his feelings in observations about other people" and reads people via specific tells, not a generic "glowing."
**Suggested rewrite:** "He couldn't stand still." or "He had the look of a man with a paper to publish." (an observed tell, per spec's read-people register)

### [8] convergence / lyrical drift
> The goddess of darkness and the goddess of the moon, sitting in the footnotes of a vial.

**Why:** "sitting in the footnotes of a vial" is a writerly conceit that converges on generic literary-narrator house style; Grygum's prose is "noticeably written" but his flourishes are biblical-formal or cataloging, not whimsical metaphor.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The goddess of darkness and the goddess of the moon, both named in the footnotes of a poison. That part actually explained something."

## Verdict
Strongest recurring issue is mechanical: six prose em-dashes that need converting to commas/periods (the dialogue dash on line 18 correctly stays). Voice fidelity is otherwise high — the "I filed the question," "I took notes either way," and the dead-man-possesses-nothing tactical chain are squarely in Grygum's late-arc precision register; the only true voice slips are two lyrical reaches ("glowing," "sitting in the footnotes of a vial"). Spot-edit, not re-narrate.
