## Flags

### [1] em-dash in prose
> Daral gave us the times like a man who knew the times mattered. He'd dropped the book under the evergreen tree, next to the other gifts, around 11am on Deadwinter's Eve, before rushing up to the keeper's Tower for his end-of-year meeting. Then he corrected himself, because the correction was the part he cared about — he hadn't handed it to anybody.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — em-dash in narration prose (outside dialogue/italics) should convert to period/colon.
**Suggested rewrite:** "...because the correction was the part he cared about. He hadn't handed it to anybody."

### [2] em-dash in prose
> Daz to the apothecary — turned out he could talk to the beasts, and there was a squirrel down there might've seen something, which is a sentence I never thought I'd take seriously.

**Why:** Mechanical scan A — em-dash in narration prose.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Daz to the apothecary. Turned out he could talk to the beasts, and there was a squirrel down there might've seen something..."

### [3] cliché / on-the-nose framing
> A murder is a kind of terrain, even if I'd never have called it that before.

**Why:** The spec sanctions terrain vocabulary, but "even if I'd never have called it that before" is the narration flagging its own metaphor — tell-not-show meta-commentary the spec warns against; Thorin states things plain, he doesn't annotate his own framing.
**Suggested rewrite:** "A murder is a kind of terrain. You've got the ground, the timeline, and the man standing on it, and you watch where he puts his feet."

### [4] register-wrong / mannered repetition
> That second one. That's the one I kept circling.

**Why:** "I kept circling" is a writerly-introspective tic (he names a private mental loop) where the spec says Thorin thinks while moving and commits — deferral and circling aren't his shape ("Let me think about it" is on the never-say list).
**Suggested rewrite:** "That second one. That's the one that wouldn't sit down."

### [5] convergence / opening-cadence risk
> I went back over the pieces, because I like to know the ground before I cross it.

**Why:** "because I like to know the ground before I cross it" is the same self-explaining-my-method cadence as scene 05's "because that's how I think" and "because that's how I think" elsewhere — a recurring trailing causal clause that explains Thorin to the reader rather than letting the action carry it.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I went back over the pieces. Know the ground before you cross it."

### [6] tell-not-show emotional commentary
> A scared man who wants to clear his name is a useful man to walk around with.

**Why:** Borderline — this is a tactical read stated as maxim, which is in-voice, but stacked with scene's other maxims ("That's what we get paid for", "I like it that way") it tips toward aphorism-padding. Keep one, not all.
**Suggested rewrite:** (acceptable as-is; flag only if trimming maxims across the scene — recommend cutting one of the three closing "that's how I am" tags.)

## Verdict
High fidelity — the terrain framing, the stone-weight "wall" metaphor, and the plain split landing are all dead-on Thorin. Strongest issue is the recurring self-explaining causal clause ("because I like to know the ground before I cross it" / "because that's how I think"), which annotates his method instead of trusting it; spot-edit those tags rather than re-narrate.
