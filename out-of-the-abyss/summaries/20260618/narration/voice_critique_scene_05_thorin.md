## Flags

### [1] Em-dash in narration prose (mechanical scan A)

Two narration-level em-dashes. (The em-dashes on lines 20, 24, 30, 36, 40, 54 are all inside `"..."` dialogue — VTT-captured speech and a truncated line — and must stay verbatim.)

> Milil stood in an alcove off to one side — the god of bards, open hands, a brass chalice resting in them like an offering nobody had come to collect.

**Why:** Narration em-dash; the clause after it is an appositive gloss.
**Suggested rewrite:** "...off to one side, the god of bards: open hands, a brass chalice resting in them..." (comma + colon).

> *And the other edge,* Zalthir would say — lead-lined cuts both ways.

**Why:** Narration em-dash inside an italic thought-attribution.
**Suggested rewrite:** "*And the other edge,* Zalthir would say. Lead-lined cuts both ways." (period).

### [2] Register-wrong vocabulary (mechanical scan B — judgment call, low confidence)

> Photons don't care about lead.

**Why:** A clinical physics term to explain that his sight isn't the scrying-type magic lead blocks. It is *defensible* — Joe reaches for modern register in his table pivots (private equity, "best practice") — but "photons" is the most analytical word in the scene, and the same beat lands harder in Thorin's stone-and-dark vocabulary.
**Suggested rewrite:** "My eyes don't care about lead. Lead stops the magic that goes hunting; it doesn't stop a dwarf looking in a cup in the dark." [grounded in spec's "mundane frames for supernatural problems"]

### [3] Writerly simile (low confidence)

> a brass chalice resting in them like an offering nobody had come to collect.

**Why:** A touch more lyrical than the examples, where Thorin's images stay blunt and physical ("bloodless flesh becomes rosy"). The chalice literally *is* an offering vessel, so the simile is apt — but it leans pretty. Borderline; keep if you like the mood it sets for the room.
**Suggested rewrite:** "a brass chalice held in them, empty, like nobody'd come to fill it." [grounded in examples — plainer image]

## Verdict

The deduction-by-proxy device — Thorin solving the puzzle by narrating what Zalthir *would* say ("Zalthir would have said it first"; "*And the other edge,* Zalthir would say") — is in voice here; it's flagged as convergence only because the same move recurs in scenes 06 and 07. On its own this scene is strongly Thorin (the giants-don't-rush beat, "Trust statues. They come alive.", "that's how I find the wall"). Spot-edit the two em-dashes; the rest is taste.
