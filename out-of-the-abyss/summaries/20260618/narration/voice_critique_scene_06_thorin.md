## Flags

### [1] Em-dash in narration prose (mechanical scan A)

This scene is em-dash-heavy: nine narration-level dashes. (The dash inside `"...I'm so sorry — this compulsive need..."` on line 16 is dialogue — keep it.) None of these are load-bearing; most are appositive glosses or list intros that a colon, comma, or period carries better in Thorin's stacked, paratactic rhythm.

> I noticed the three of them watching before I'd said a word — three junior avowed lined up near the serving doors

**Suggested rewrite:** "...before I'd said a word. Three junior avowed lined up near the serving doors..." (period)

> I made one more joke about her colleague — "Hatred always lies" — and got told Sprig doesn't always lie

**Suggested rewrite:** commas: "...about her colleague, 'Hatred always lies,' and got told..."

> The Deadwinter supper began at the 5PM bell. Twelve plates — beetroot soup, fried carp, smoked mackerel.

**Suggested rewrite:** colon: "Twelve plates: beetroot soup, fried carp, smoked mackerel."

> Alkrist was cheerful, pitching a project — *Tales of War by the Generals That Waged Them*

**Suggested rewrite:** colon: "...pitching a project: *Tales of War by the Generals That Waged Them*..."

> Daz would've gone at motive first — was anyone here pushing a project that'd never get funded?

**Suggested rewrite:** comma or period: "Daz would've gone at motive first. Was anyone here pushing a project that'd never get funded?"

> Janussi praised it the other way — "the kind of knowledge that could benefit civilization"

**Suggested rewrite:** colon: "Janussi praised it the other way: 'the kind of knowledge that could benefit civilization'..."

> hand out the gifts under the evergreen tree — gave each one to the matching great reader

**Suggested rewrite:** comma: "...under the evergreen tree, gave each one to the matching great reader."

> The wrapping paper turned up in Janussi's office, not here — I kept the two from blurring.

**Suggested rewrite:** period: "...not here. I kept the two from blurring."

> the taster's just collateral — she tastes the poisoned dish, she's dead at midnight too

**Suggested rewrite:** colon: "...the taster's just collateral: she tastes the poisoned dish, she's dead at midnight too..."

> Sylvira only pointed at her throat — too hoarse to talk — and Irony helped her find a pot of honey

**Suggested rewrite:** commas: "...pointed at her throat, too hoarse to talk, and Irony helped her..."

> Looking for something — or not looking.

**Suggested rewrite:** period or comma: "Looking for something. Or not looking."

### [2] Convergence — the deduction-by-proxy device, used twice in one scene

> Daz would've gone at motive first — was anyone here pushing a project that'd never get funded? That's the thread he pulls. So I pulled it.

> And that's where Zalthir's voice came in, the flat way he turns a thing over.

**Why:** Thorin routes both of his deductions through an absent party member's method ("Daz would've...", "Zalthir's voice came in"). Once is a nice nod to the Ember-Vanguard ensemble. Twice in one scene — on top of the same move in scenes 05 and 07 — turns a characterful beat into a structural crutch: it makes Thorin the narrator of *other people's* thinking rather than a man reading the room himself. The spec's Thorin reads terrain directly ("planted himself as bait", "find the wall").
**Suggested rewrite:** Keep one, cut the other. Let Thorin own the second deduction in his own frame: "All the food was shared. So you can't aim it at one plate unless a server's in on it — and the one reason to leave the food alone is you don't want the taster dead." [grounded in spec — terrain-first, names the part that matters]

### [3] Mild idiom (low confidence)

> Then the part that set my teeth.

**Why:** "Set my teeth" is a stock phrase; slightly generic for a man whose discomfort usually shows as a flat understatement or a held silence.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Then the part I didn't like." [grounded in spec's blunt register — "I don't like killing people if I don't have to"]

## Verdict

Voice is fundamentally sound — the shared-plate logic and the tiefling-doesn't-need-a-lantern catch are pure Thorin — but the scene is carrying nine narration em-dashes and leans on the "Daz/Zalthir would've..." device twice. Spot-edit the dashes and collapse one of the two proxy-deductions; no re-narration needed.
