## Flags

### [1] Em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> I asked him who had visited him, what had happened, what the timelines were — and then, because the question I actually cared about needed a frame around it, I asked whether he knew anybody with an interest in the kind of poison we'd found.

**Why:** Prose em-dash; a period or comma keeps the measured, clausal rhythm the spec calls for.
**Suggested rewrite:** Replace ` — and then` with `. And then`.

### [2] Em-dash (mechanical scan A)
> At three, A'lai arrived asking for fennel for indigestion, and Leuwin took him down through the rear exit to the greenhouse below, where he grows herbs year-round with magic. At a quarter past three, the trouble.

**Why:** No em-dash here — clean. (Noting prose em-dashes do appear at line 12 "the gnome — Fheminor — came", line 34 "A'lai, then — that conformed", line 40 "the seam to press — whether the elf"; convert each.)
**Suggested rewrite:** Line 12: "the gnome, Fheminor, came for cold medicine." Line 34: "A'lai, then. That conformed to Leuwin's account." Line 40: "That was the seam to press: whether the elf and the lizard walked in as a pair".

### [3] Register-wrong vocabulary (scan B)
> And then the line that reorganized the whole account

**Why:** "account" is in-voice for Daz (audit vocabulary) — noting it is NOT a flag; "reorganized" pairs cleanly with it. No change.
**Suggested rewrite:** (none — in-voice; recorded for completeness.)

### [4] Cliché / on-the-nose simile
> his jaws were stuffed to the corners, packed like that squirrel out of Ice Age, and there were no more questions a creature in that condition was going to answer.

**Why:** The pop-culture "squirrel out of Ice Age" simile is a fourth-wall break in modern-register terms the spec's prose-voice doesn't use; Daz's deadpan lands drier without the reference (his table humor is "one or two words," not allusion).
**Suggested rewrite:** `his jaws were stuffed to the corners, packed past speech, and there were no more questions a creature in that condition was going to answer.`

### [5] Tell-not-show / on-the-nose
> Nibbles loved nuts. I had nuts. We understood each other immediately.

**Why:** Borderline — the clipped triplet is close to Daz's deadpan, but "We understood each other immediately" states the transaction the surrounding action already shows (nut-for-answer); the spec buries feeling in observation rather than summarizing the rapport.
**Suggested rewrite:** `Nibbles loved nuts. I had nuts. The terms negotiated themselves.`

## Verdict
High fidelity — the bribe-as-interrogation framing, the "witness with a price is the easiest kind," and the two thought-intrusions (Thorin's one-word verdict, the "professional recognition" reframe) are textbook Daz and well-rationed. The strongest issue is the "squirrel out of Ice Age" pop-culture simile, which breaks his prose register; otherwise convert the scattered prose em-dashes. Spot-edit, do not re-narrate.
