## Flags

### [1] Em-dash in narration prose (mechanical scan A)

Eight narration-level dashes. (The dashes inside `"Okay, so — yeah..."` on line 30 and `"...why didn't — you know..."` on line 38 are dialogue disfluencies — keep them. The stammer `under the — the non-Christmas...` on line 22 is verbatim table speech rendered in narration — keep it too.)

> Irony pointed me at it — said if I was really worried about the food, I should go talk to Sprig

**Suggested rewrite:** colon or period: "Irony pointed me at it. Said if I was really worried about the food, go talk to Sprig..."

> the taster's just collateral — she'd have gone down at midnight too

**Suggested rewrite:** colon: "...the taster's just collateral: she'd have gone down at midnight too..."

> So I went to find Sprig — but I worked the gifts before the gossip.

**Suggested rewrite:** period: "So I went to find Sprig. But I worked the gifts before the gossip."

> The tiefling just pointed at her throat — too hoarse to talk.

**Suggested rewrite:** comma: "The tiefling just pointed at her throat, too hoarse to talk."

> It wasn't about Sprig. It was about Irony — Irony who'd been trying to thwart the advances of one of the great readers.

**Suggested rewrite:** colon or period: "It was about Irony. Irony, who'd been trying to thwart the advances of one of the great readers."

> I should go to Orrin — the man who notices everything, even if he hears nothing.

**Suggested rewrite:** comma: "...go to Orrin, the man who notices everything even if he hears nothing."

> Alkrist had been holding a small scrap of paper toward a candle — feeding it to the flame — and when two junior avowed walked in, he dropped it.

**Suggested rewrite:** commas: "...toward a candle, feeding it to the flame, and when two junior avowed walked in, he dropped it."

> Daz's conclusion, and it was the right one — whatever happened, happened under the tree.

**Suggested rewrite:** colon: "Daz's conclusion, and it was the right one: whatever happened, happened under the tree."

### [2] Convergence — the deduction-by-proxy device, used four times in one scene

> I turned that over the way Zalthir would've.

> Daz's question, if he'd been standing there: what was she hunting for, did she say?

> Zalthir would've caught the loophole before me: Irony can't lie.

> Daz's conclusion, and it was the right one — whatever happened, happened under the tree.

**Why:** Four separate deductions, all attributed to an absent party member's method. This is the strongest voice problem across scenes 05–07: Thorin has become a clearing-house for Daz's and Zalthir's thinking instead of a man who reads the room himself. The spec is explicit that Thorin's tactical reads are his own and direct ("his tactical reads aren't guesswork"; "find the wall"). The ensemble nods are good once; at this density they hollow out the narrator.
**Suggested rewrite:** Keep the single sharpest one (the "Irony can't lie / can't lie isn't has to tell" loophole is genuinely a Thorin-grade observation and worth keeping) and let Thorin own the other three in his own voice. E.g. for the taster: "A poison taster. So if the only mark was Janussi, she's just collateral — dead at midnight too, and nobody connects the two." [grounded in spec — terrain-first, names the consequence]

### [3] Awkward / mixed simile

> kept eyeing Dawnbringer like he wanted to kiss the blade's feet and beg it off him

**Why:** A blade has no feet; the image mixes (kiss-the-feet / beg-off) and reads workshopped rather than seen. Thorin's images in the examples are concrete and physically true.
**Suggested rewrite:** "kept eyeing Dawnbringer like a man who'd back out of the room if the sword let him." [grounded in examples — plain, physical, no mixed metaphor]

## Verdict

The proxy-deduction device peaks here — four attributions to Daz and Zalthir in one scene — and that, not the em-dashes, is what's flattening Thorin's voice across the dining-hall run. The "can't lie isn't has to tell" beat shows he can carry the deduction himself; lean on that and strip the other three attributions. Worth a focused spot-edit rather than a re-narration, but if you fix only one thing in scenes 05–07, fix the device.
