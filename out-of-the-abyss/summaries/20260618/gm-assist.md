# Chapter 56: Never mind the murders.

Date: Jun 18th, 2026

## Summary
The academic community of Candlekeep erupted into a frenzy of intellectual excitement when Brother Vareth unveiled a startling new expansion of the Grygumite triangle, transforming it into a quadrangle with a fourth theoretical option. Despite the shadow of a recent murder hanging over the keep, every scholar and avowed member abandoned their duties and flooded toward the Grand Hall for an emergency debate. The chaos proved to be an unexpected gift for the party, as their ever-present minder, Fembris Lancer, sheepishly requested permission to attend the disputation in the Oval Theater — and with his departure, the keep's corridors were suddenly, gloriously empty.

Daral, the paranoid and complicated scholar who had gifted the poisoned book to Janussi, clung to Thorin and Daz like a man drowning in a sea of his own guilt and fear. He was convinced that whoever had framed him for the murder would not stop there, and that the only two people standing between him and a swift, quiet death were the two adventurers he had only just met. He pointed the party toward the Oak Tree Apothecary, describing it as a wooden treehouse nestled in the boughs of a great evergreen oak, run by an elderly satyr druid named Leuwin, who might be able to definitively identify the poison used on the book. Meanwhile, Grygum had quietly slipped the key once worn around Kalan Strongbranch's neck into Daz's keeping, unwilling to hold onto it in a room full of distracted scholars. The party also turned their minds to the stolen sapphire, theorizing that it had been enchanted as a magical anchor — a way to summon a hidden item to its possessor's hand, provided the original owner was no longer alive to claim it.

With their hour of freedom stretching before them, the party split their efforts. Thorin and Daral made for the Southern Dining Hall to question the waitstaff who had served the Deadwinter's Eve feast, while Daz slipped away toward the apothecary. Zalthir, the most shadow-footed of the group, set off with Glabbagool to search the private chambers of their most promising suspects. The investigation was moving on multiple fronts at once, and the clock was ticking.

In the Southern Dining Hall, Thorin's eye was drawn almost immediately to the statue of Milil, the god of bards, standing in an alcove with a brass chalice in its outstretched hands. Daral froze at the sight of it, suddenly remembering that the chalice was lead-lined — a detail that would render it invisible to any magical attempt to locate objects hidden within. His voice barely above a whisper, he suggested that someone might have used it as a hiding place. Thorin reached inside and pulled out a severed heart and a blood-stained meat cleaver, the grim truth of the murder laid bare in his hands. The heart had been removed to ensure that Janussi could never be brought back, never questioned, never allowed to name his killer.

The waitstaff, once located, proved to be a rich source of information. A junior avowed member named Irony, who had a compulsive need to tell the truth, described the twelve-course feast in meticulous detail — the beetroot soup, the fried carp, the spiced wines — and painted a picture of a tense and largely unhappy gathering. She noted that Alkrist, a dragonborn keeper, had been the only one in genuinely good spirits, cheerfully pitching a new research project on military history, while Sylvira Savikas had stormed out of the hall mid-evening after a sharp exchange. Most critically, Irony recalled that at one o'clock in the morning, she had encountered a figure in the kitchens who looked like Sylvira — but who pointed silently at her throat rather than speak, and who was carrying a lantern despite being a tiefling with no need for one. The party exchanged a meaningful look: tieflings could see in the dark, and Sylvira would never need a lantern.

Another waiter, the observant and partially deaf Orrin Glass, added two more crucial details. He had watched Alkrist drop a small scrap of paper into a candle flame the moment junior avowed members entered the room, as though destroying something he did not want seen. More damning still, Orrin had noticed that the string tied around Janussi's Deadwinter's Eve gift had been cut and carefully retied — meaning the book had been tampered with while it sat beneath the evergreen tree, in plain sight, during the feast itself.

At the Oak Tree Apothecary, Daz found Leuwin pottering about his treehouse pharmacy, blissfully unaware that something was amiss. A broken vial on the dangerous toxins shelf, hastily concealed beneath a peppermint-scented rag, told a different story. Leuwin's squirrel assistant, Nibbles, proved to be the most valuable witness in the keep — provided one had enough nuts. Through careful and patient negotiation, Daz coaxed from the small creature a description of three visitors: an unpleasant girl gnome, a clumsy dark-skinned elf, and a large lizard-skinned figure. The gnome had come and gone first. The dark-skinned elf had arrived next and drawn Leuwin away to the greenhouse below, while the lizard-skin — almost certainly a dragonborn — had made a tremendous mess, shattered the vial, and made off with its contents.

Zalthir and Glabbagool moved through the keep like shadows, slipping into the chambers of the most promising suspects. In A'lai Aivenmore's sparsely decorated rooms, they found a dove cage built to house five birds — but only two remained, their companions long since dispatched with messages to unknown recipients. Alkrist's lavish quarters told a far darker story: sealed glass jars lined his shelves, each containing a dead rat with blistered, pustulated skin. On his bedside table lay an elegant book bound in black leather and gold filigree, its pages open to a tale about a military commander who had been poisoned after receiving a gift — a gift whose pages had been coated in Midnight Tears. The bookmark had not been placed there by accident. Bookwyrm's chambers, by contrast, yielded nothing of interest, her rooms neat and unremarkable, with no trace of poison or conspiracy.

By the time the party reconvened, the shape of the murder had grown considerably clearer, even as new questions crowded in. Someone had tampered with the book beneath the tree during the feast. Someone wearing Sylvira's face had crept through the kitchens in the small hours, hiding a heart and a cleaver in a lead-lined chalice. A dragonborn had stolen the components of a deadly poison from the apothecary while a dark-skinned elf provided cover. And Alkrist, the cheerful dragonborn keeper who had been so conspicuously good-humored on the night of the murder, kept a book in his bedroom that read like a blueprint for the crime itself. The web of conspiracy stretched across the keep, and the party stood at its center, pulling threads.

## Locations
### Candlekeep
A legendary library-fortress where the party is currently investigating a murder amidst a sea of distracted scholars.

### Grand Hall
The site where the Avowed have gathered to debate new research, inadvertently leaving the rest of the keep open for the party's investigation.

### Oval Theater
The venue within Candlekeep where the avowed community gathers for academic debates and disputations, where Fembris Lancer went to attend Grygum's disputation.

### Deneir's Sanctum
A location within Candlekeep where the party planned to meet up with Zalthir and Kalan Strongbranch.

### Southern Dining Hall
A large hall in Candlekeep featuring long tables and a statue of the god Milil with a lead-lined brass chalice in an alcove. It was the site of the Deadwinter's Eve feast where gifts were exchanged under an evergreen tree, and where a severed heart and meat cleaver were discovered hidden in the chalice.

### Kitchens
The area adjacent to the dining hall where Irony encountered a silent, lantern-bearing figure resembling Sylvira Savikas late at night, searching for something.

### Oak Tree Apothecary
A wooden treehouse built into the boughs of a huge evergreen oak, located between the Sea Warden's Tower and the House of Mechanus. Run by the elderly satyr druid Leuwin and his squirrel Nibbles, it serves as a pharmacy stocked with ointments, balms, and medicines. A broken vial of dangerous toxins was discovered here, hidden under a peppermint-scented rag.

### A'lai Aivenmore's Chambers
Sparsely but tastefully decorated rooms featuring fine glassware, ceramics, a large mirror, and a portrait of the occupant. A dove cage sized for five birds was found here with only two remaining.

### Alkrist's Chambers
Lavishly decorated quarters filled with expensive furniture, fine materials, and fashionable artwork. Contained sealed glass jars with dead, blistered rats and a bookmarked copy of '1001 Tashalian Nights' open to a tale about a poisoning using Midnight Tears.

### Bookwyrm's Chambers
Spacious and comfortable rooms occupying half of the top floor of the Readers' Towers, neatly arranged without being extravagant. Searched by Zalthir and Glabbagool but yielded no significant evidence.

### Daral
A paranoid and complicated scholar who is terrified for his life after realizing he may have been framed for Janussi's murder via a poisoned book from his private collection. He clings to Thorin and Daz as the only people he trusts, and is deeply motivated to clear his name. He pointed the party toward the Oak Tree Apothecary and recalled the lead-lined chalice of Milil in the dining hall.

### Brother Vareth
An elderly scholar whose new theoretical expansion of the 'Grygumite triangle' into a quadrangle caused a massive stir among the Avowed community, drawing everyone to the Grand Hall for a debate.

### Kalan Strongbranch
The former owner of a mysterious key that Grygum recently handed over to Daz for safekeeping. He had been acting paranoid about the key's safety.

### Janussi
The murder victim whose death is the central focus of the party's investigation. His heart was discovered hidden in the lead-lined chalice of Milil in the Southern Dining Hall.

### Fembris Lancer
An acolyte adjutant serving as the party's minder, who requested to leave his duties to attend the academic disputation in the Oval Theater, giving the party a free hour to investigate.

### Leuwin
An elderly, absent-minded satyr druid who runs the Oak Tree Apothecary. He keeps himself busy distilling tinctures and macerating herbs, and was distracted by a customer in the greenhouse while a theft of dangerous toxins occurred in his shop.

### Nibbles
A friendly squirrel assistant at the Oak Tree Apothecary who witnessed several suspicious individuals and provided eyewitness testimony in exchange for nuts. He identified an 'unpleasant girl gnome,' a 'clumsy dark-skinned elf,' and a 'lizard skin' as recent visitors.

### A'lai Aivenmore
A Great Reader and political rival whom Daral suspects of orchestrating the murder and framing him to clear the path to becoming First Reader. Also seen at the apothecary on the day of the murder.

### Fheminor Scrivenbark
A Great Reader and halfling who visited the apothecary for cold medicine on the day of the murder. Identified by Nibbles as the 'unpleasant girl gnome.'

A Dragonborn member of the Avowed who was uncharacteristically cheerful during the feast, discussing a new research project on military history. ### Sylvira Savikas
A Great Reader and tiefling who stormed out of the dinner and was later seen acting strangely and silently in the kitchens at 1 a.m., carrying a lantern despite being a tiefling who would not need one. The party suspects this may have been someone using magic to impersonate her.

### Kazryn Nyantani
The individual who took it upon herself to distribute gifts from under the evergreen tree to the Great Readers during the feast.

### Irony
A junior member of the Avowed and waitress who claims to always tell the truth. She provided a detailed timeline of the feast and reported the suspicious late-night figure resembling Sylvira Savikas in the kitchens.

A unique key previously worn around the neck of Kalan Strongbranch, handed to Daz by Grygum for safekeeping. Daz used a divination ritual on it and confirmed it is not currently linked to any summoning spell.

### Poisoned Book
A volume from Daral's private collection that was given as a gift to Janussi during the Deadwinter's Eve feast. It is believed to be the primary delivery vector for the lethal Midnight Tears poison. The string on its wrapping was observed to have been cut and retied, suggesting it was tampered with while under the evergreen tree.

### Stolen Sapphire
A gemstone missing from a safe, theorized to be a magical focus for a summoning spell. Possessing the sapphire would allow someone to magically retrieve a specific item, making it potentially useful for recovering a hidden key.

### Lead-lined Chalice of Milil
A brass chalice held by a statue of the god Milil in the Southern Dining Hall. Its lead lining blocks divination magic, making it a perfect hiding spot for objects. It was used during the Deadwinter's Eve revelry as a drinking vessel.

### Severed Heart
A gruesome discovery found hidden within the lead-lined chalice of Milil in the dining hall, presumably belonging to the murder victim Janussi. It was removed to prevent anyone from resurrecting him or questioning him via magic.

### Blood-stained Meat Cleaver
A weapon found alongside the severed heart inside the chalice, likely used in the desecration of the victim's body.

### Broken Toxin Vial
A shattered glass container from the 'dangerous toxins' shelf of the Oak Tree Apothecary, hastily hidden under a peppermint-scented rag. Its contents were stolen by a Dragonborn while A'lai provided a diversion.

A cloth used to hastily conceal the spill from a broken vial of dangerous toxins in the apothecary.

A medicinal remedy provided by Leuwin to Fheminor Scrivenbark for a cold on the day of the murder.

### Messenger Dove Cage
A cage sized for five birds found in A'lai Aivenmore's chambers, with only two doves remaining, suggesting three birds were sent out with messages.

### 1001 Tashalian Nights

## Spells
### Identify
Cast by Daz as a divination ritual, touching the key handed to him to determine if it was under the effects of any ongoing magical spells or enchantments. The ritual confirmed the key was not linked to any summoning spell.

### Speak with Animals
Used by Daz to communicate with Nibbles the squirrel at the Oak Tree Apothecary, gathering eyewitness testimony about the theft of dangerous toxins in exchange for nuts.

### Lesser Restoration
Mentioned as a potential countermeasure to the Midnight Tears poison if prepared and administered in time, noted as likely the easiest solution for those with cleric companions.

### Plane Shift
Discussed as a method to escape the midnight trigger of the Midnight Tears poison by leaving the current plane of existence, though noted as less practical than Lesser Restoration for most.

### Locate Object
Discussed in the context of being blocked by the lead-lined properties of the chalice of Milil, explaining why the chalice was an ideal hiding spot.

### Speak with Dead
Proposed as a future method to question Janussi now that his severed heart has been recovered from the chalice.

### Polymorph
Suspected by the party to have been used by an intruder to impersonate Sylvira Savikas, explaining why the figure seen in the kitchen refused to speak and was carrying a lantern despite being a tiefling.
