# Chapter 56: Never mind the murders.

Date: Jun 18th, 2026

## Summary
The academic community of Candlekeep erupted into a frenzy of intellectual excitement when Brother Vareth unveiled a startling new expansion of the Grygumite triangle, transforming it into a quadrangle with a fourth theoretical option. Despite the shadow of a recent murder hanging over the keep, every scholar and avowed member abandoned their duties and flooded toward the Oval Theater for an emergency debate. As the DM put it, "nothing says that you're an academic institution when you can blow off everything that's important to focus on what really matters. New research." The chaos proved to be an unexpected gift for the party, as their ever-present minder, Fembris Lancer, sheepishly requested permission to attend the disputation in the Oval Theater — and with his departure, the keep's corridors were suddenly, gloriously empty.

Daral, the paranoid and complicated scholar who had gifted the poisoned book to Janussi, clung to Thorin and Daz like a man drowning in a sea of his own guilt and fear. He was convinced that whoever had framed him for the murder would not stop there, and that the only two people standing between him and a swift, quiet death were the two adventurers he had only just met. He pointed the party toward the Oak Tree Apothecary, describing it as a wooden treehouse nestled in the boughs of a great evergreen oak, run by an elderly satyr druid named Leuwin, who might be able to definitively identify the poison used on the book. Meanwhile, Grygum had quietly slipped the key once worn around Kalan Strongbranch's neck into Daz's keeping, unwilling to hold onto it in a room full of distracted scholars. The party also turned their minds to the stolen sapphire, theorizing that it had been enchanted as a magical anchor — a way to summon a hidden item to its possessor's hand, provided the original owner was no longer alive to claim it.

With their hour of freedom stretching before them, the party split their efforts. Thorin and Daral made for the Southern Dining Hall to question the waitstaff who had served the Deadwinter's Eve feast, while Daz slipped away toward the apothecary. Zalthir, the most shadow-footed of the group, set off with Glabbagool to search the private chambers of their most promising suspects. The investigation was moving on multiple fronts at once, and the clock was ticking.

In the Southern Dining Hall, Thorin's eye was drawn almost immediately to the statue of Milil, the god of bards, standing in an alcove with a brass chalice in its outstretched hands. Daral froze at the sight of it, suddenly remembering that the chalice was lead-lined — a detail that would render it invisible to any magical attempt to locate objects hidden within. His voice barely above a whisper, he suggested that someone might have used it as a hiding place. Thorin reached inside and pulled out a severed heart and a blood-stained meat cleaver, the grim truth of the murder laid bare in his hands. The heart had been removed to ensure that Janussi could never be brought back, never questioned, never allowed to name his killer.

The waitstaff, once located, proved to be a rich source of information. A junior avowed member named Irony, who had a compulsive need to tell the truth, described the twelve-course feast in meticulous detail — the beetroot soup, the fried carp, the spiced wines — and painted a picture of a tense and largely unhappy gathering. She noted that Alkrist, a dragonborn Great Reader, had been the only one in genuinely good spirits, cheerfully pitching a new research project on military history, while Sylvira Savikas had stormed out of the hall mid-evening after a sharp exchange. Most critically, Irony recalled that at one o'clock in the morning, she had encountered a figure in the kitchens who looked like Sylvira — but who pointed silently at her throat rather than speak, and who was carrying a lantern despite being a tiefling with no need for one. The party exchanged a meaningful look: tieflings could see in the dark, and Sylvira would never need a lantern.

Another waiter, the observant and partially deaf Orrin Glass, added two more crucial details. He had watched Alkrist drop a small scrap of paper into a candle flame the moment junior avowed members entered the room, as though destroying something he did not want seen. More damning still, Orrin had noticed that the string tied around Janussi's Deadwinter's Eve gift had been cut and carefully retied — meaning the book had been tampered with while it sat beneath the evergreen tree, in plain sight, during the feast itself.

At the Oak Tree Apothecary, Daz found Leuwin pottering about his treehouse pharmacy, blissfully unaware that something was amiss. A broken vial on the dangerous toxins shelf, hastily concealed beneath a peppermint-scented rag, told a different story. Leuwin's squirrel assistant, Nibbles, proved to be the most valuable witness in the keep — provided one had enough nuts. Through careful and patient negotiation, Daz coaxed from the small creature a description of three visitors: an unpleasant girl gnome, a clumsy dark-skinned elf, and a large lizard-skinned figure. The gnome had come and gone first. The dark-skinned elf had arrived next and drawn Leuwin away to the greenhouse below, while the lizard-skin — almost certainly a dragonborn — had made a tremendous mess, shattered the vial, and made off with its contents.

Zalthir and Glabbagool moved through the keep like shadows, slipping into the chambers of the most promising suspects. In A'lai Aivenmore's sparsely decorated rooms, they found a dove cage built to house five birds — but only two remained, their companions long since dispatched with messages to unknown recipients. Alkrist's lavish quarters told a far darker story: sealed glass jars lined his shelves, each containing a dead rat with blistered, pustulated skin. On his bedside table lay an elegant book bound in black leather with stars and gold filigree, its pages open to a tale about a military commander who had been poisoned after receiving a gift — a horn whose mouthpiece had been coated in Midnight Tears. The bookmark had not been placed there by accident. Bookwyrm's chambers, by contrast, yielded nothing of interest, her rooms neat and unremarkable, with no trace of poison or conspiracy.

By the time the party reconvened, the shape of the murder had grown considerably clearer, even as new questions crowded in. Someone had tampered with the book beneath the tree during the feast. Someone wearing Sylvira's face had crept through the kitchens in the small hours, hiding a heart and a cleaver in a lead-lined chalice. A dragonborn had stolen the components of a deadly poison from the apothecary while a dark-skinned elf provided cover. And Alkrist, the cheerful dragonborn Great Reader who had been so conspicuously good-humored on the night of the murder, kept a book in his bedroom that read like a blueprint for the crime itself. The web of conspiracy stretched across the keep, and the party stood at its center, pulling threads.

## Scenes
### The Academic Distraction
#### The party regroups as the academic community of Candlekeep becomes obsessed with a new theoretical debate, providing a window for investigation.
- The party discusses the properties of Midnight Tears poison and how its timing is affected by planetary rotation and magic, riffing off the DM's freshly-written treatise ("On the Punctual Tears," styled as an "internal topocentric protocol" — a joke born when Joe asked whether the poison "uses time zones" and the DM took it as a "challenge accepted").
- Countermeasures to the midnight trigger were laid out: keep riding west to outrun midnight, plane shift away, or have someone cast Lesser Restoration — with the observation that the poison is therefore poorly suited to use against mind flayers or githyanki, who can natively plane shift. "Explains why the drow love it, because nobody knows what time it's midnight."
- Grygum encounters Brother Vareth, whose new research on the 'Grygumite triangle' (now a quadrangle) causes a massive stir among the scholars and was, per the DM, "about to get published."
- The Avowed community abandons their usual duties to hold an emergency debate in the Oval Theater, leaving several areas of the keep — including the dining room — unmonitored.
- Daral clings to Thorin and Daz, terrified that he is being framed for the murder of Janussi via a poisoned book, "hoping you won't tell Zalthir his opinion about Dragonborn" that he had voiced while drunk — "barroom talk, it wasn't meant to be taken seriously."
- Grygum hands over a significant key, previously worn around Kalan Strongbranch's neck, to Daz to keep it safe from the gathering crowd of scholars.
- The party analyzes the theft of a sapphire, learning a key clarification: the summoning spell only works on an item "not in the possession of anyone," and "a dead person can't possess something. An undead can, but a dead person can't" — a point "the followers of Kelemvor have made very clear." This explains why Kalan Strongbranch was paranoid enough to hand off his key.

### The Investigation Deepens
#### The party discusses the murder investigation, the potential for poisoned books, and magical keys while their minder, Fembris, departs for an academic debate.
- The party discusses whether keys can be duplicated; the DM confirms they can, "but the process of duplication requires powerful magic that is not readily accessible" — prompting Daz and Thorin to joke about photographing the key for ChatGPT and 3D-printing a copy, an inquiry Fembris finds "appalled" by.
- Daral expresses extreme paranoia, believing the party are the only ones who don't want him dead — a man who "thought he was gonna get fired, then realizes the guy that was gonna fire him is dead," and is now realizing "the only people he could turn to are these people who just showed up yesterday."
- Thorin confronts Daral with brutal honesty about whether they will even bother to save him: "if we solve the murder, we get any book in the place. We get shit."
- Daral suggests visiting the Oak Tree Apothecary — "between the Sea Warden's Tower and the House of Mechanus" — to identify the specific poison, naming Leuwin the satyr druid as the one who could "identify the poison definitively from the book."
- Fembris Lancer requests to leave the party's side to attend the academic disputation in the Oval Theater, noting that "half the avowed are already there. Bookwyrm will be there. It's a political statement, she can't afford not to be there." The party recognizes that his abandoning his post grants "a whole hour where you could go look at things without your minder."
- Daz casts Identify as a divination ritual on the key given to him by Grygum to determine if it is under the influence of any ongoing spells; the DM confirms the key is **not** linked to the summon spell.
- The party clarifies the sapphire mechanic: the same sapphire must be used both to cast and to retrieve, making it "as good as having the item" — likened by Joe to "AirTags before AirTags."

### Investigation Strategy and the Timeline of the Book
#### The party discusses the mechanics of the murder and plans their next steps while their minder is away.
- The party analyzes the timeline of the poisoned book: Daral dropped it under the evergreen tree "around 11am on Deadwinter's Eve, before rushing up to the keeper's Tower for my end-of-year meeting with the keeper." It was picked up by Janussi during dinner, when it was still wrapped.
- The group theorizes that whoever tampered with the book "either went into his house... or, the dude we're talking to is the killer." Daz presses Daral: "When you put it... under the tree, did it look like it had been tampered with at all?"
- The party confirms the gifts bore explicit name tags, and there was no magical seal on the wrapping — to which Daral exclaims "why would I put a magical seal on the wrapping? It's a gift!" before resolving that "Glyphs of Warning for everyone!" should be a best practice going forward "if I make it through the night, hint, hint."
- The party discusses the nature of the poison, concluding it only turns black upon contact with a humanoid's biological fluids, meaning any remaining residue would not be visibly black but instead clear and "in a vial."
- Daral, accused directly — "someone tampered with it, or you killed them" — protests "Are you accusing me!" and becomes "very committed to clearing his name."
- The party decides to split up: Thorin and Daral will investigate the Southern Dining Hall, Daz will visit the apothecary (where his Speak with Animals could be used on the squirrel), and Zalthir will search suspects' rooms.

### Theories of Betrayal
#### The party discusses potential motives and suspects with Daral, who fears he is being framed for Janussi's murder.
- Daral delivers a melodramatic monologue naming A'lai Aivenmore as the most likely culprit: "Ally would never be able to become my keeper, first keeper. And so framing me makes perfect sense." He frames the killing as a double stroke — "It would clear the deck of Janussi, the person who held the chair, and... clear the wood of me."
- Daral characterizes A'lai's motive as ideological as well as political: "He sees my love of poetry, my love of fiction as a threat to his approach to the universe."
- The group debates why the killer bothered with the roundabout book method rather than poisoning Janussi's food directly; the answer the DM offers through Daral: "Maybe they wanted me to be blamed."
- The party notes that, with everyone away at the conference, only Daz or Thorin can help — leading to Zalthir Glabbagool offering aid, an eye protruding hopefully from the ooze and then retreating "dolefully into the ooze" when Daz's Insight check failed.
- Glabbagool attempts to grow a nose to smell the poison; the DM allows the attempt.
- The party decides to split up to investigate the kitchen, the apothecary, and the private rooms of the suspects.

### Discovery in the Southern Dining Hall
#### Thorin and Daral investigate the dining hall and discover a gruesome hidden cache within a sacred vessel.
- Thorin and Daral enter the Southern Dining Hall — "Long tables cleared from breakfast, not yet reset" — noting the statue of Milil, the god of bards, in its alcove with "open hands, brass chalice." Two waiters stack linen; one greets Daral politely, the other rolls his eyes.
- Daral freezes and exclaims "oh my goodness! We used it for the Midsummer Wine!" — recognizing the lead-lined chalice as a vessel that "prevents divination," and lamenting "How could I have not thought about this?"
- Before searching, Thorin warns Daral against any sleight-of-hand and quips that if a glyph of warding kills him, "I'm not sure that Dawnbringer's gonna take care of you, just be aware."
- Thorin inspects the chalice and discovers a severed heart and a blood-stained meat cleaver hidden inside.
- The party realizes the heart was likely stolen to prevent the victim from being resurrected or questioned via Speak with Dead, and Thorin floats reuniting the heart with the body for questioning later.
- Thorin's broader musing on tracking killers "in a world full of these things" (Polymorph, etc.) yields the insight that "someone planned this murder around Candlekeep's own spell collection" — "it couldn't be just some random person," confirming an inside job by someone who knew the chalice was lead-lined.

### Interviews in the Southern Dining Hall
#### The party interviews the waitstaff in the Southern Dining Hall to reconstruct the events of the Deadwinter's Eve feast and investigate potential poisoning vectors.
- Thorin questions Irony, who introduces herself with a "compulsive need to always tell the truth" and bristles at the obligatory joke about her name.
- Irony describes the feast beginning "at the 5PM bell" with twelve plates — beetroot soup, fried carp, smoked mackerel — and drinks including "fruit compots, spiced wines and ales, honey, vodka, green tea," served by three junior avowed: herself, Orrin, and Sprig.
- Irony notes the gathering was "in a tense and taciturn mood, except Alkrist," who cheerfully pitched a new research project — "Tales of War by the Generals That Waged Them" — to compile "first-hand insight and strategies from warlords and military commanders around the world."
- She recalls Teles scoffing that Alkrist's project "sounded as likely to succeed as Fheminor Scrivenbark['s] program," and Janussi praising it as "the kind of knowledge that could benefit civilization," reminding everyone that "determining which branches of knowledge to further is our privileged responsibility." Daral muttered under his breath, "Oh, you've been right behind one of us all night."
- The party learns that Kazryn Nyantani took it upon herself to distribute gifts from under the evergreen tree; Janussi "took his gift unwrapped, offered thanks to his secret donor, and then excused himself, throwing away the wrapping paper in a wicker basket near the door." (The DM corrected an earlier error: the wrapping paper was canonically found in Janussi's office, not the dining hall.)
- After the meal, Bookwyrm instructed the waitstaff to take leftovers to their dorms; nobody fell sick except from drinking too much.
- Irony reveals she saw a silent figure resembling Sylvira Savikas searching by candlelight in the kitchens at 1:00 AM — the figure "only pointed at her throat by way of reply, suggesting she was too hoarse to speak" — which the party finds suspicious given Sylvira is a tiefling who would not need a lantern.
- The party discovers that Sprig Summerfoot acted as a self-appointed poison taster for the feast; Gabe raises the chilling logic that "killing the poison tester along the way... she'd die at midnight, no one would know anyway."

### Interrogating the Wait Staff
#### Thorin and the party interview the remaining junior avowed who served the Deadwinter's Eve supper to uncover suspicious behavior.
- Sprig "gushes in great detail about the 12 delicious dishes," insisting she "enthusiastically volunteered to be a taster of each of the dishes" despite the chefs telling her it was unnecessary — earning Daral's commendation as a "best practice going forward."
- Irony's rolled-eyes remark that "these avowed, sometimes they're a little bit too interested in the junior avowed" points the party toward a romantic subplot.
- Sprig reveals that Great Reader Teles Ahvoste has been making unwanted advances toward Irony, and that Orrin "notices everything, even if he hears nothing."
- Thorin and the party question Orrin Glass, a waiter deaf in one ear and "a bit of an oddball."
- Orrin reveals that Alkrist "seemed to drop a small scrap of paper that he had been holding towards the candle upon seeing two junior members of the [avowed] arrive[]."
- Orrin notes that the string on Janussi's gift "had clearly been cut and retried," confirming the package was tampered with while under the tree — to which Daz concludes "whatever happened, happened under the tree."

### The Squirrel's Testimony at the Oak Tree Apothecary
#### Daz visits the Oak Tree Apothecary and uses magic to interview a squirrel witness about a stolen toxin.
- Daz arrives at the Oak Tree Apothecary, "a wooden tree house constructed in the burrows of a huge evergreen oak tree located in the west of the Great Library," and meets Leuwin distilling tinctures, crushing seeds, and macerating herbs while Nibbles fetches tools.
- Leuwin recounts the day's customers: at 2:30 Fheminor came "for some cold medicine" (tincture of horehound and licorice); at 3:00 A'lai asked for "fresh fennel for indigestion" and was taken down to the greenhouse via the rear exit; at 3:15 the trouble occurred.
- Daz discovers a "peppermint-covered rag and a broken glass" beneath the shelf labeled "Dangerous Toxins" — a toxin "that if you mix with other things... turns into very dangerous poisons" — that "somebody kind of tried to hide badly."
- Daz asks Leuwin for acorns, then uses Speak with Animals to interview Nibbles, bribing the creature with nuts one batch at a time.
- Nibbles identifies three visitors: "the unfriendly girl gnome" (Fheminor Scrivenbark, who "came and gone by then"), "a big, clumsy, dark-skinned elf" (A'lai), and "a huge lizard skin" (a Dragonborn) who "made a big mess," knocked something with a "BLING," "moved things around," and left.
- Crucially, Nibbles reveals that "Leuwin didn't [see], because Leuwin wasn't in the room. He was helping the big, [un]clumsy, dark-skinned elf" — confirming the dark-skinned elf provided a diversion in the greenhouse while the Dragonborn stole the toxin.
- Daz, himself a drow, "begin[s] to appreciate this conspiracy" — the drow "causing the diversion and not getting their hands dirty with the highly toxic poisons" while the Dragonborn did the dangerous work. Nibbles could not recall any identifying marks on either figure.
- Daz pays out a final handful of nuts and thanks the squirrel, whose jaws are stuffed full "like the squirrel out of Ice Age."

### Searching the Readers' Chambers
#### Zalthir and Glabbagool conduct a stealthy search of several high-ranking officials' rooms to find evidence of the murder.
- The search runs as a series of escalating skill checks (DCs roughly 12, 16, 20, 24), with Glabbagool aiding Zalthir — "we're going on a secret mission!" — and the two functioning as "Voltron."
- Zalthir searches A'lai Aivenmore's chambers — "sparsely but tastefully decorated, including several fine pieces of glassware and ceramics. A large mirror and a portrait of himself" — and finds a cage sized for five messenger doves with only two remaining; the feeding and droppings confirm "there were more than 2."
- In Alkrist's lavishly decorated quarters, the party finds his spellbook, "several tightly sealed glass jars containing dead rats, their skin blistered and [pustulated]," and a locked safe (left unopened — Glabbagool could not fit through any opening less than an inch).
- Zalthir finds "1001 Tashalian Nights," bound in black leather with stars and gold filigree, bookmarked to the tale of the commander poisoned by a gift coated in Midnight Tears. (Insight 18 with Glabbagool's aid revealed the find; a sleight-of-hand check let it be set down without Alkrist noticing.)
- Zalthir and Glabbagool investigate Bookwyrm's chambers — "spacious... occupying half of the top floor of the Readers' Tower... comfortable and neatly arranged without being extravagant" — but a failed final check (a DC 20) turns up nothing of significant interest, and Glabbagool detects no poison.

## Locations
### Candlekeep
A legendary library-fortress where the party is currently investigating a murder amidst a sea of distracted scholars.

### Oval Theater
The venue within Candlekeep where the avowed community gathers for academic debates and disputations, where Fembris Lancer went to attend Grygum's disputation. Half the avowed were already there, and Bookwyrm, Teles, and Fheminor Scrivenbark were all expected to attend. The Avowed gathering here inadvertently left the rest of the keep open for the party's investigation.

### Deneir's Sanctum
A location within Candlekeep where the party planned to meet up with Zalthir and Kalan Strongbranch.

### Southern Dining Hall
A large hall in Candlekeep with long tables cleared from breakfast and a statue of the god Milil — "open hands, brass chalice" — in an alcove. It was the site of the Deadwinter's Eve feast that began at the 5PM bell, where gifts were distributed from under an evergreen tree, and where a severed heart and meat cleaver were discovered hidden in the lead-lined chalice.

### Kitchens
The area adjacent to the dining hall where Irony encountered a silent, lantern-bearing figure resembling Sylvira Savikas at 1:00 AM, searching for something by candlelight; the figure pointed at her throat and was helped to a pot of honey.

### Oak Tree Apothecary
A wooden treehouse built into the burrows of a huge evergreen oak, located in the west of the Great Library (between the Sea Warden's Tower and the House of Mechanus). Run by the elderly satyr druid Leuwin and his squirrel Nibbles, it serves as a pharmacy stocked with ointments, balms, and medicines, with a rear exit leading down to a greenhouse where Leuwin grows herbs year-round with his magic. A broken vial of dangerous toxins was discovered here, hidden under a peppermint-scented rag.

### A'lai Aivenmore's Chambers
Sparsely but tastefully decorated rooms featuring fine glassware, ceramics, a large mirror, and a self-portrait. A dove cage sized for five birds was found here with only two remaining.

### Alkrist's Chambers
Lavishly decorated quarters with expensive furniture, fine materials, and fashionable artwork. Contained his spellbook, sealed glass jars of dead, blistered rats, a locked safe, and a bookmarked copy of '1001 Tashalian Nights' open to a tale about a poisoning using Midnight Tears.

### Bookwyrm's Chambers
Spacious and comfortable rooms occupying half of the top floor of the Readers' Tower, neatly arranged without being extravagant. Searched by Zalthir and Glabbagool but yielded no significant evidence.

## NPCs
### Daral
A paranoid and complicated scholar — described by the DM as having "anger issues" and "logic issues," a man who "gave somebody a book that he wanted the person to enjoy, and then was pissed off that the person was enjoying his book... and then punched him in the face." He is terrified for his life after realizing he may have been framed for Janussi's murder via a book from his private collection. He clings to Thorin and Daz, recalled the lead-lined chalice of Milil, pointed the party to the Oak Tree Apothecary, and would gladly "kiss Dawnbringer's feet" to be saved.

### Brother Vareth
A scholar whose new theoretical expansion of the 'Grygumite triangle' into a quadrangle caused a massive stir among the Avowed community, drawing everyone to the Oval Theater for a debate.

### Kalan Strongbranch
The former owner of a mysterious key that Grygum recently handed over to Daz for safekeeping. He used to carry it around his neck and became paranoid enough to give it up, fearing someone might have anchored a summoning spell to it.

### Janussi
The murder victim and chair-holder whose death is the central focus of the party's investigation. He ate and drank at the feast, took and unwrapped his gift, thanked his "secret donor," and discarded the wrapping in a wicker basket as he left. His heart was discovered hidden in the lead-lined chalice of Milil — removed, the party deduces, to block resurrection or Speak with Dead.

### Fembris Lancer
An acolyte adjutant serving as the party's minder, who sheepishly requested to leave his post to attend the academic disputation in the Oval Theater, giving the party a free hour to investigate. He was "appalled" by talk of duplicating keys.

### Leuwin
An elderly, absent-minded satyr druid who runs the Oak Tree Apothecary, keeping busy distilling tinctures and macerating herbs. He grows herbs year-round with his magic in a greenhouse below the treehouse, and was drawn away there by A'lai while the theft of dangerous toxins occurred in his shop. He was "shocked" and "appalled" to learn a vial had been broken.

### Nibbles
A friendly squirrel assistant at the Oak Tree Apothecary who fetches tools for Leuwin. The most valuable witness in the keep — for the right price in nuts — he identified "the unfriendly girl gnome," "a big, clumsy, dark-skinned elf," and "a huge lizard skin," and crucially revealed Leuwin had been lured to the greenhouse during the theft.

### A'lai Aivenmore
A Great Reader and political rival whom Daral suspects of orchestrating the murder to clear his path to becoming First Reader. He visited the apothecary at 3:00 PM asking for fennel for indigestion and was taken to the greenhouse — the party concludes he was providing a diversion. His chambers held a five-dove cage with only two birds remaining.

### Fheminor Scrivenbark
A Great Reader who visited the apothecary at 2:30 PM for cold medicine. Identified by Nibbles as the 'unpleasant girl gnome,' she had "come and gone" before the theft. Her own research program was mocked at the feast as unlikely to succeed. (Campaign records list Fheminor as a halfling; Nibbles the squirrel's "gnome" is an unreliable identification.)

### Alkrist
A Dragonborn Great Reader, conspicuously cheerful at the feast while pitching "Tales of War by the Generals That Waged Them." Orrin Glass saw him burn a scrap of paper when junior avowed arrived. His chambers contained jars of dead, blistered rats and a copy of '1001 Tashalian Nights' bookmarked to a Midnight Tears poisoning — vaulting him to prime suspect. The party first met him only briefly, at the chapter house bookkeeper's meeting.

### Sylvira Savikas
A Great Reader and tiefling who stormed out of the Deadwinter's Eve dinner mid-evening. Later, a figure resembling her was seen in the kitchens at 1 a.m., silent (pointing at her throat) and carrying a lantern despite being a tiefling who would not need one — leading the party to suspect magical impersonation, possibly Polymorph, to frame her.

### Kazryn Nyantani
A Great Reader, who took it upon herself to take each gift from under the evergreen tree and hand it to the corresponding Great Reader during the feast.

### Irony
A junior member of the Avowed and waitress with a compulsive need to always tell the truth. She gave a detailed timeline of the feast, reported the suspicious 1 a.m. figure resembling Sylvira, and is herself the target of unwanted advances from Teles Ahvoste.

### Sprig Summerfoot
A junior member of the Avowed who enthusiastically volunteered to be the poison taster for every dish at the feast, over the chefs' objections. She pointed the party toward Orrin Glass and revealed Teles's advances toward Irony.

### Orrin Glass
A junior member of the Avowed and waiter, deaf in one ear and "a bit of an oddball," but highly observant. He noticed Alkrist burning a scrap of paper and the cut-and-retied string on Janussi's gift.

### Teles Ahvoste
A Great Reader who scoffed at Alkrist's research proposal during the feast and has been making unwanted romantic advances toward Irony.

### Glabbagool
An intelligent gelatinous ooze accompanying the party who assisted Zalthir in searching suspects' rooms ("we're going on a secret mission!"), proudly announced "I have learned silence," and attempted to grow a nose to detect traces of poison by smell.

## Items
### Kalan Strongbranch's Key
A unique key previously worn around the neck of Kalan Strongbranch, handed to Daz by Grygum for safekeeping. Daz used the Identify ritual on it and confirmed it is **not** linked to the summoning spell. Keys like it can only be duplicated through powerful, hard-to-access magic.

### Poisoned Book
A volume from Daral's private collection given as a gift to Janussi during the Deadwinter's Eve feast. Daral dropped it under the evergreen tree at 11 a.m. before his end-of-year meeting; Janussi picked it up wrapped at dinner. Believed to be the delivery vector for Midnight Tears. Orrin Glass observed that the string on its wrapping had been cut and retied.

### Stolen Sapphire
A gemstone missing from a safe, theorized to be a magical focus for a summoning spell. The same sapphire must be used both to cast and to retrieve, making possession of it "as good as having the item" — a magical anchor for recovering a hidden key, likened to "AirTags before AirTags."

### Lead-lined Chalice of Milil
A brass chalice held by the statue of Milil in the Southern Dining Hall. Its lead lining blocks divination and Locate Object, making it an ideal hiding spot. It had been removed from the statue for use as a drinking vessel during the Midsummer Wine revelry.

### Severed Heart
A gruesome discovery found hidden within the lead-lined chalice of Milil, presumably Janussi's. It was removed to prevent resurrection or questioning via Speak with Dead.

### Blood-stained Meat Cleaver
A weapon found alongside the severed heart inside the chalice, likely used in the desecration of the victim's body.

### Broken Toxin Vial
A shattered glass container from the 'dangerous toxins' shelf of the Oak Tree Apothecary, hastily hidden under a peppermint-scented rag. Per Leuwin, its contents were a base toxin that "if you mix with other things... turns into very dangerous poisons." Stolen by a Dragonborn while A'lai provided a diversion in the greenhouse.

### Peppermint Covered Rag
A cloth used to hastily and badly conceal the spill from the broken vial of dangerous toxins in the apothecary.

### Tincture of horehound
A medicinal remedy (with licorice to chew on) provided by Leuwin to Fheminor Scrivenbark for a cold at 2:30 on the day of the murder.

### Messenger Dove Cage
A cage sized for five birds found in A'lai Aivenmore's chambers, with only two doves remaining; feeding and droppings confirmed more had once been kept, suggesting messages were sent.

### 1001 Tashalian Nights
An attractive book bound in black leather with stars and gold filigree, found on Alkrist's bedside table. Bookmarked to a tale about a military commander poisoned after being given "a gift of surrender" — a magic horn whose mouthpiece had been covered with Midnight Tears.

## Spells
### Identify
Cast by Daz as a first-level divination ritual, touching the key handed to him to determine its properties and whether any ongoing spells affected it. The ritual confirmed the key was not linked to the summoning spell.

### Speak with Animals
Used by Daz to communicate with Nibbles the squirrel at the Oak Tree Apothecary, extracting eyewitness testimony about the toxin theft in exchange for nuts paid out one batch at a time.

### Lesser Restoration
Mentioned as the most practical countermeasure to the Midnight Tears trigger if prepared and administered in time — easiest for those with cleric companions, and flagged as a best practice the clerics should keep pre-prepared.

### Plane Shift
Discussed as a method to escape the midnight trigger by leaving the current plane, convenient for magic users without cleric friends — and the reason the poison is poorly suited against mind flayers and githyanki, who can plane shift natively.

### Locate Object
Discussed as being blocked by the lead lining of the chalice of Milil, explaining why the chalice was an ideal hiding spot; also noted as not working to find substances like the poison.

### Speak with Dead
Proposed as a future method to question Janussi now that his severed heart has been recovered — and the likely reason the heart was taken in the first place.

### Polymorph
Suspected by the party to have been used to impersonate Sylvira Savikas. The figure's silence and need for a lantern led the DM to note it "probably wasn't a very powerful polymorph spell."
