# Interviews in the Southern Dining Hall

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, presence-corrected)

#### Thorin interviews the waitstaff in the Southern Dining Hall to reconstruct the events of the Deadwinter's Eve feast and investigate potential poisoning vectors.
- Thorin questions Irony, who introduces herself with a "compulsive need to always tell the truth" and bristles at the obligatory joke about her name.
- Irony describes the feast beginning "at the 5PM bell" with twelve plates — beetroot soup, fried carp, smoked mackerel — and drinks including "fruit compots, spiced wines and ales, honey, vodka, green tea," served by three junior avowed: herself, Orrin, and Sprig.
- Irony notes the gathering was "in a tense and taciturn mood, except Alkrist," who cheerfully pitched a new research project — "Tales of War by the Generals That Waged Them" — to compile "first-hand insight and strategies from warlords and military commanders around the world."
- She recalls Teles scoffing that Alkrist's project "sounded as likely to succeed as Fheminor Scrivenbark['s] program," and Janussi praising it as "the kind of knowledge that could benefit civilization," reminding everyone that "determining which branches of knowledge to further is our privileged responsibility." Daral muttered under his breath, "Oh, you've been right behind one of us all night."
- Thorin learns that Kazryn Nyantani took it upon herself to distribute gifts from under the evergreen tree; Janussi "took his gift unwrapped, offered thanks to his secret donor, and then excused himself, throwing away the wrapping paper in a wicker basket near the door." (The wrapping paper was canonically found in Janussi's office, not the dining hall.)
- After the meal, Bookwyrm instructed the waitstaff to take leftovers to their dorms; nobody fell sick except from drinking too much.
- Irony reveals she saw a silent figure resembling Sylvira Savikas searching by candlelight in the kitchens at 1:00 AM — the figure "only pointed at her throat by way of reply, suggesting she was too hoarse to speak" — which is suspicious given Sylvira is a tiefling who would not need a lantern.
- Thorin works through the chilling logic that, if the only target was Janussi, killing the poison taster (Sprig Summerfoot) "along the way" cost the killer nothing — "she'd die at midnight, no one would know anyway."
