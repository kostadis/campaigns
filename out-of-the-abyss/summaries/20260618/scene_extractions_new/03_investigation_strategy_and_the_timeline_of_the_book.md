# Investigation Strategy and the Timeline of the Book

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party discusses the mechanics of the murder and plans their next steps while their minder is away.
- The party analyzes the timeline of the poisoned book: Daral dropped it under the evergreen tree "around 11am on Deadwinter's Eve, before rushing up to the keeper's Tower for my end-of-year meeting with the keeper." It was picked up by Janussi during dinner, when it was still wrapped.
- The group theorizes that whoever tampered with the book "either went into his house... or, the dude we're talking to is the killer." Daz presses Daral: "When you put it... under the tree, did it look like it had been tampered with at all?"
- The party confirms the gifts bore explicit name tags, and there was no magical seal on the wrapping — to which Daral exclaims "why would I put a magical seal on the wrapping? It's a gift!" before resolving that "Glyphs of Warning for everyone!" should be a best practice going forward "if I make it through the night, hint, hint."
- The party discusses the nature of the poison, concluding it only turns black upon contact with a humanoid's biological fluids, meaning any remaining residue would not be visibly black but instead clear and "in a vial."
- Daral, accused directly — "someone tampered with it, or you killed them" — protests "Are you accusing me!" and becomes "very committed to clearing his name."
- The party decides to split up: Thorin and Daral will investigate the Southern Dining Hall, Daz will visit the apothecary (where his Speak with Animals could be used on the squirrel), and Zalthir will search suspects' rooms.
