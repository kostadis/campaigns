# Discovery in the Southern Dining Hall

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, presence-corrected)

#### Thorin and Daral investigate the dining hall and discover a gruesome hidden cache within a sacred vessel.
- Thorin and Daral enter the Southern Dining Hall — "Long tables cleared from breakfast, not yet reset" — noting the statue of Milil, the god of bards, in its alcove with "open hands, brass chalice." Two waiters stack linen; one greets Daral politely, the other rolls his eyes.
- Daral freezes and exclaims "oh my goodness! We used it for the Midsummer Wine!" — recognizing the lead-lined chalice as a vessel that "prevents divination," and lamenting "How could I have not thought about this?"
- Before searching, Thorin warns Daral against any sleight-of-hand and quips that if a glyph of warding kills him, "I'm not sure that Dawnbringer's gonna take care of you, just be aware."
- Thorin inspects the chalice and discovers a severed heart and a blood-stained meat cleaver hidden inside.
- Thorin realizes the heart was likely stolen to prevent the victim from being resurrected or questioned via Speak with Dead, and floats reuniting the heart with the body for questioning later.
- Thorin's broader musing on tracking killers "in a world full of these things" (Polymorph, etc.) yields the insight that "someone planned this murder around Candlekeep's own spell collection" — "it couldn't be just some random person," confirming an inside job by someone who knew the chalice was lead-lined.
