# Theories of Betrayal

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party discusses potential motives and suspects with Daral, who fears he is being framed for Janussi's murder.
- Daral delivers a melodramatic monologue naming A'lai Aivenmore as the most likely culprit: "A'lai would never be able to become my keeper, First Reader. And so framing me makes perfect sense." He frames the killing as a double stroke — "It would clear the deck of Janussi, the person who held the chair, and... clear the wood of me."
- Daral characterizes A'lai's motive as ideological as well as political: "He sees my love of poetry, my love of fiction as a threat to his approach to the universe."
- The group debates why the killer bothered with the roundabout book method rather than poisoning Janussi's food directly; the answer the DM offers through Daral: "Maybe they wanted me to be blamed."
- The party notes that, with everyone away at the conference, only Daz or Thorin can help — leading to Zalthir Glabbagool offering aid, an eye protruding hopefully from the ooze and then retreating "dolefully into the ooze" when Daz's Insight check failed.
- Glabbagool attempts to grow a nose to smell the poison; the DM allows the attempt.
- The party decides to split up to investigate the kitchen, the apothecary, and the private rooms of the suspects.
