# The Squirrel's Testimony at the Oak Tree Apothecary

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Daz visits the Oak Tree Apothecary and uses magic to interview a squirrel witness about a stolen toxin.
- Daz arrives at the Oak Tree Apothecary, "a wooden tree house constructed in the burrows of a huge evergreen oak tree located in the west of the Great Library," and meets Leuwin distilling tinctures, crushing seeds, and macerating herbs while Nibbles fetches tools.
- Leuwin recounts the day's customers: at 2:30 Fheminor came "for some cold medicine" (tincture of horehound and licorice); at 3:00 A'lai asked for "fresh fennel for indigestion" and was taken down to the greenhouse via the rear exit; at 3:15 the trouble occurred.
- Daz discovers a "peppermint-covered rag and a broken glass" beneath the shelf labeled "Dangerous Toxins" — a toxin "that if you mix with other things... turns into very dangerous poisons" — that "somebody kind of tried to hide badly."
- Daz asks Leuwin for acorns, then uses Speak with Animals to interview Nibbles, bribing the creature with nuts one batch at a time.
- Nibbles identifies three visitors: "the unfriendly girl gnome" (Fheminor Scrivenbark, who "came and gone by then"), "a big, clumsy, dark-skinned elf" (A'lai), and "a huge lizard skin" (a Dragonborn) who "made a big mess," knocked something with a "BLING," "moved things around," and left.
- Crucially, Nibbles reveals that "Leuwin didn't [see], because Leuwin wasn't in the room. He was helping the big, [un]clumsy, dark-skinned elf" — confirming the dark-skinned elf provided a diversion in the greenhouse while the Dragonborn stole the toxin.
- Daz, himself a drow, "begin[s] to appreciate this conspiracy" — the drow "causing the diversion and not getting their hands dirty with the highly toxic poisons" while the Dragonborn did the dangerous work. Nibbles could not recall any identifying marks on either figure.
- Daz pays out a final handful of nuts and thanks the squirrel, whose jaws are stuffed full "like the squirrel out of Ice Age."
