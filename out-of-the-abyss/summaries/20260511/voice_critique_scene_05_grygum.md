## Flags

### [1] Convergence with house style — "the particular X of Y who Z" template (twice in this scene)

> The monk looked at him — the particular look of a teacher who is deeply not impressed but is in the process of identifying the next problem to solve — and said that what would be interesting was combining the two into something
>
> Glabbagool took this with complete seriousness — the gravity of a student who has finally received confirmation that the work counts.

**Why:** Same construction that appears in scenes 1, 3, and 4. By the fifth scene it has become this narration's house voice rather than Grygum's. Grygum's working register per spec is the "biblical-formal flicker followed by plain conversational English" — short and observed (*"He was offering a door and declining to walk through it with you"* later in this same scene is what right looks like). These two sentences smooth a sharp observation into magazine prose.
**Suggested rewrite (first):** *The monk looked at him the way teachers look at students who have just produced the wrong evidence for the right conclusion. Then he said the way forward was in the combination.*
**Suggested rewrite (second):** *Glabbagool took this with complete seriousness. The work counted; he had been told so.*

### [2] Verbose recap sentence — voice drifts toward report-writing

> I had to concede that Khell-Vire had read the situation correctly. Zalthir's relationship to his monastic tradition was precisely the relationship of a man who had found the manual aggravating and ignored it, developed something better through necessity, and was now being told that the thing he'd developed through aggravation and necessity was the most interesting thing about him.

**Why:** The second sentence runs 49 words and repeats "aggravation and necessity" twice — the second pass is a tic, not a doubling. Grygum's spec is paratactic-short, and Ben's table speech is *"averaging about eight words."* The construction here is fine as analysis, but the prose is unedited journal draft, not the finished filed note.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Khell-Vire had read the situation correctly. Zalthir found the manual aggravating, ignored it, and built something better. He was now being told that the thing he'd built was the most interesting thing about him.*

## Verdict

This is the strongest-voiced scene in the doc — *"I approved of that"*, *"You don't stop dressing like what you were. You just layer something else over it"*, *"Both cases can be in the notes"*, the Stroud-can't-play-chess callout, and *"I filed it under 'Zalthir: things that are not in the records but will be'"* are all dead-center Grygum. Two flags only; both small smoothing edits.
