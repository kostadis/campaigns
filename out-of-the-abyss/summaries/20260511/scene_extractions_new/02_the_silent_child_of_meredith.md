# The Silent Child of Meredith

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### At the River Shining Tavern, the party is approached by a mother seeking help for her afflicted daughter.
- Meredith of the Ford, a weaver by trade, spots Grygum's holy symbol and approaches the party during dinner, describing her seven-year-old daughter Ellen: silent for six days, drawing the same shape on every flat surface, talking in her sleep and saying "it is only the shape that holds."
- Grygum agrees to examine the girl; Daz offers to help, providing advantage on the medicine check — but both roll poorly (a 10 and a 7 respectively), leaving the diagnosis inconclusive beyond "she is not well."
- Dawnbringer intervenes, taking on what the DM describes as "a very pompous voice" and declaring "Let me bring the light to you" before performing a lesser restoration.
- After the restoration, Ellen speaks briefly — "The wells are not wells anymore" — before falling silent again.
- The party concludes this is not a disease; something is twisting Ellen's mind from within. Grygum notes there is no evidence of fungal infection, ruling out a purely Underdark-origin cause.
- Grygum observes that the condition is interior rather than exterior — "not something that can be slapped, or grappled."
- The party determines that the madness previously seen in the Underdark seems to be bleeding into the surface world, affecting people's ability to process what they hear rather than causing a conventional illness.
- Daz checks whether there is any connection between Ellen's condition and the growing pressure in his own head; Grygum rolls a medicine check (23) and confirms a tenuous connection, though she lacks the knowledge to name it precisely.
