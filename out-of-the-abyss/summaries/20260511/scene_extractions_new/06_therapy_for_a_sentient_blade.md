# Therapy for a Sentient Blade

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Thorin and Master Archivist Philemon begin a process of healing for the sentient sun-sword Dawnbringer.
- Master Archivist Philemon greets Thorin and Dawnbringer and immediately refers to the blade as "a remarkable specimen — late Netherese-era recovery, sudden worship origins, sustained darkness exposure of approximately 3,000 to 4,000 years."
- Thorin corrects him immediately: "What do you mean, specimen? This is my friend Dawnbringer." Philemon adjusts, noting with some surprise that the bond between Thorin and the blade is stronger than he originally feared.
- Thorin explains that Dawnbringer struggles with darkness — something they have been working through together.
- Philemon asks Dawnbringer directly about the wielder she was found with — meaning the corpse in which she was discovered. Dawnbringer falters and goes silent.
- Thorin remarks, somewhat flippantly, "The rebound relationship, I get it, okay" — then immediately walks it back: "No, no, it's okay, I'm being flippant. This is a serious topic."
- Philemon gently corrects himself mid-sentence: "Your sword does not need polishing." Then he realizes: "She can't be polished, actually." He reframes: "Your sword needs to remember what daylight feels like without needing to fight, and then when she's ready and you are willing, she needs to grieve. The first work is light. The second work is morning. We will start with the first."
- Thorin and Dawnbringer are effectively signed up for a two-phase therapeutic process; Thorin notes, with characteristic lightness, that hopefully Dawnbringer "grows a plus one" out of it.
