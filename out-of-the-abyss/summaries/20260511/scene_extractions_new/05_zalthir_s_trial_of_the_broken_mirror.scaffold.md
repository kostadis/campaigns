[Scene 5] Zalthir's Trial of the Broken Mirror
Narrator: Grygum
Focus: Grygum watches from the outside as Zalthir is tested, reading the trial's spiritual architecture the way he reads theology — looking for what the mirror is really asking, and what it reveals about a man who leaves no tracks.

- Zalthir is taken to the Echoing Hall in the north inner ward of the history library, to Calvier's lower gallery office where the Shadow Discipline Lineage Records are kept.

- Khell-Vire is a 200-year-old half-elf who left the Garden of Shadow 80 years ago after refusing an order. He wears a faded shadow monk under-robe beneath the avowed gray of Candlekeep.

- On Khell-Vire's desk, Zalthir sees a note reading: "Zalthir, the Underdark, until the Bone King sings." Neither Zalthir nor Khell-Vire can explain it.

- Khell-Vire opens with a cryptic monk question — "What were you sent toward? Not why were you sent here?" — which Zalthir finds immediately annoying in the familiar way of all those shadow monk classes he ignored. When Zalthir asks for clarification, Khell-Vire rephrases: "Does this phrase mean anything to you that you have lived through that resonates with it?" It does not. Khell-Vire tries: "Is it an entity? An event? A person?" Zalthir replies: "That is precisely what I am asking you."

- Khell-Vire declines to answer and instead states: "There are monks here in the inner ward who have read everything and fought nothing. They will want to test you. Let them. You will learn what you do not see, and they will learn what cannot be read. I will not be there. This is your work, not mine."

- A blind half-elf monk enters and immediately says: "Give me a standard shadow step."

- Zalthir shadow steps. The monk criticizes him at once: "You skip the third breath. You don't enter your ki in the hara. You let it bleed into your fingertips like a poison. It is technically wrong in every scroll." He then pauses: "And yet, you arrived at your destination three seconds faster than the grandmaster did. Show me what else you haven't learned."

- Zalthir asks whether he can punch the monk. The DM confirms he can. Zalthir rolls a 20 and a 13 on strikes. The sparring match is evenly balanced — Zalthir could take him if he committed — but the monk is also half-blind.

- The monk pivots to a formal skill challenge. Zalthir must demonstrate one of three techniques: the Weightless Shadow (acrobatics), the Efficient Strike (athletics), or the Eldritch Claw (stealth). Zalthir picks acrobatics. He rolls a 3. The monk stares at him as he falls. "You have learned nothing."

- The monk asks for the Efficient Strike (athletics). Zalthir rolls a 1. The monk sighs: "The corridor was narrow. The form was irrelevant. Did you not practice your forms while traveling?"

- Zalthir tries stealth for the Eldritch Claw demonstration. He rolls a 4, for an 11 total. The monk walks away.

- As the monk departs, Zalthir lands a strike on him from behind — rolling a 30 to hit. The monk stops. "Hmm. Perhaps not all is lost." He is genuinely impressed; no one has hit him that hard in a long time.

- The monk sets a final challenge: throw the brick, Zalthir must shadow step to intercept and smash it mid-air. Zalthir rolls 29 to hit. The brick is "smashed into a variety of pieces. Completely unprecedented form. Still skipping breaths. Still not taking maneuvers. But the results do speak for themselves."

- The monk asks whether Zalthir has any other insights from his schooling. Zalthir demonstrates his shadow tentacles and eldritch tattoo. The monk is not impressed but acknowledges that combining two-hit into something interesting is the next step.

- A sage of xenobiology approaches and asks whether "a mindless digestive system" can achieve enlightenment. Zalthir pushes back: Glabbagool is "by no means mindless" — he is a student, he is already walking, he is already tormenting the pocket drow. The xenobiology sage, apparently satisfied, provides a vial of Resonant Slime. Glabbagool is very pleased.

- Earlier in the session, Zalthir recalls that he once punched through the shadows in the Underdark to create a precise puncture — and proposes demonstrating a version of that maneuver: shadow stepping and striking simultaneously in a way that redeems all other failings. He rolls a 26 to hit on the demonstration. Khell-Vire raises an eyebrow and acknowledges that "there is something special about how you hit," and that the combination of shadow step and the hit "redeems your lack of skills in all other possible areas."

- Brother Khell-Vire grants Zalthir access to a specialized shadow technique — presenting three options, from which Zalthir selects option C.
