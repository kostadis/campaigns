## Flags

### [1] Voice spec conflict — sentence length / structure

> There is a particular feeling when a medicine examination goes badly — not dramatically badly, not the kind where someone collapses or the situation gets worse, just the quiet professional failure of looking at a patient and knowing that you are not seeing what you need to see.

**Why:** 50+ words, three nested subordinate clauses. Grygum's spec is explicit: "sentences are short — averaging about eight words — and paratactic: coordinate clauses chained with 'and' rather than nested subordination." Ben's hesitations are *structural* (ellipses, comma pauses), not parenthetical-expansion. This sentence reads as workshop prose, not as a working priest's journal entry.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Medicine examinations go badly in a particular way. Not dramatically. Just the quiet professional failure of looking at a patient and not seeing what you need to see.*

### [2] Cliché / on-the-nose simile

> The lesser restoration settled over Ellen like a change in air pressure.

**Why:** Workshopped meteorological simile — could appear in any cleric's narration. Grygum's example file describes the Darklake foaming, the manta-ray-then-tentacles reveal, in plain physical terms. The voice spec also notes his cadence drops into "biblical-formal register on purpose" *or* into "plain conversational English" — but not into atmospherics like this.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Lesser restoration settled in. She drew a breath.* (Or simply trust the dialogue beat that follows.)

### [3] Tell-not-show / writerly polish

> I wrote it down. It is what I do. But I also just held it for a moment, the way you hold something before you decide what category it goes in — because there was something in her voice that the restoration hadn't fixed, something underneath the words that the words were pointing toward rather than containing.

**Why:** The first two sentences are dead-center Grygum and excellent. The third drifts into precious workshop register ("words that the words were pointing toward rather than containing") — this is gesturing at the texture of the moment instead of naming it. Grygum's instinct per spec is to file the observation, not to filigree it.
**Suggested rewrite:** *I wrote it down. It is what I do. But I held it for a moment first. Something in the voice was doing work the words weren't. The restoration hadn't fixed that part.*

## Verdict

Scene is mostly tight — the *"Bahamut may or may not have had a contingency plan"* closer and *"I took notes"* refrain land squarely. The flagged sentences are all places where the narration reaches for literary smoothness Grygum's working-priest register doesn't want. Spot-edit only.
