# Voice Critique — Thorin, scene 01: Arrival in Daggerford

**Narration:** /home/kroussos/campaigns/out-of-the-abyss/summaries/20260511/gm-assist-doc.md (Thorin block)
**Voice spec:** /home/kroussos/campaigns/out-of-the-abyss/voice/thorin_voice.md
**Per-char examples:** /home/kroussos/campaigns/out-of-the-abyss/examples/thorin_example.md

## Flags

### [1] Cliché / on-the-nose simile (opener)

> Daggerford came on us like a held breath.

**Why:** "Like a held breath" is workshopped fantasy-mood opener. Thorin's openers in his example file are plain and load-bearing — *"The Stone Giant had been sitting on the rock when we arrived"* — and the voice spec is explicit that he frames the supernatural and the unsettled in *mundane*, terrain-first terms, not in poeticized atmospherics.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Daggerford was the first place we'd walked into in weeks where the silence didn't make sense.* (Names the thing directly; lets the rest of the paragraph carry the mood.)

### [2] Generic fantasy prose

> Townfolk moved through the streets carefully, like they were walking around something they couldn't name.

**Why:** Stock observation — could appear in any narrator's section. Thorin's voice spec says he *names what's wrong*; when he can't, he says so flatly. The simile gestures at the unease instead of pinning it.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Townfolk walked around something none of them would point at. I watched a woman cross the street to avoid a conversation and then pretend she hadn't.* (Keeps the concrete second sentence, drops the gesturing first.)

### [3] Convergence with house style — "the particular way he gets"

> Grygum was quiet in the particular way he gets when something is settling into a larger pattern he's been building.

**Why:** This is the doc's recurring "with the particular X of Y who Z" template — it shows up in three other scenes. Thorin doesn't usually narrate other characters' interior states with this much polish; per the spec, he writes about *moves people make*, not about their inner cataloguing. This is also Grygum's job to describe, not Thorin's.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Grygum was quiet. Not the usual quiet. The kind where he's adding something to the list.*

### [4] Convergence with Daz's sardonic-observer register

> The priest recovered, because whatever else you could say about small-town clergy, they know how to hold themselves together in front of a collection plate.

**Why:** This is a Daz sentence — wry, generalizing about a class of people, calibrated to the audit. Thorin's voice spec explicitly says he doesn't dignify factions or institutions with respect they haven't earned, but his put-downs are *flat* (*"I want to be my faction"*), not workshopped epigrams. The collection-plate cleverness is house style, not Joe.
**Suggested rewrite:** *The priest recovered fast. Probably has to, in his line of work.*

## Verdict

The "particular X of Y who Z" construction and the workshopped opener are the two real problems; everything else (the bronze arm, *"Is it holding a weapon?"*, the closing line about towns that hadn't put up an arm yet) is on-voice and worth keeping. Spot-edit rather than re-narrate.
