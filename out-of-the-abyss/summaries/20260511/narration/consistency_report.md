## ISSUE 3: Thorin's class — Fighter (Battle Master), not a cleric; Dawnbringer's Lesser Restoration

**Location:** Scenes → The Silent Child of Meredith; Spells → Lesser Restoration; NPCs → Ellen; Memorable Moments

**Issue:** The recap states that Dawnbringer performed a Lesser Restoration on Ellen, and the party document confirms Dawnbringer can cast Lesser Restoration. This is internally consistent. However, the recap frames this as Dawnbringer acting independently and "pompously" without Thorin — the wielder — being the explicit channel. This is not a hard error but may create confusion: sentient weapons in 5e act through their wielder. The recap should clarify whether Dawnbringer cast it through Thorin or whether the DM ruled it as a self-directed action by the sword, to avoid future ambiguity about whether Dawnbringer can act independently of Thorin.

**Evidence:** Party doc: "Dawnbringer (attuned) — sentient sunblade; can cast Lesser Restoration." Thorin is listed as Fighter 8 (Battle Master) — he has no spell slots of his own, so the casting must originate from Dawnbringer's own properties, not Thorin's class features.

**Suggested fix:** Add a clarifying line in the spell entry and the scene noting that Dawnbringer cast Lesser Restoration through her own sentient properties (via Thorin as wielder), not as a party spell slot expenditure.

## ISSUE 6: Glabbagool's description in party document vs. recap

**Location:** NPCs → Glabbagool; Items → Bag of Holding; Scenes → Candlekeep Arrival

**Issue:** The party document lists Glabbagool as "now a grey ooze, traveling in bag of holding." The recap throughout refers to him as a "sentient gelatinous cube" and describes him expanding to trap the prisoner, which is consistent with gelatinous cube behavior. If Glabbagool has physically changed form to a grey ooze (a different creature stat block), the description of him as a "gelatinous cube" and the mechanics described (expanding, trapping, six pseudo-eyes) may no longer be accurate. This could be an error in the party document, an in-world transformation, or an oversight in the recap — but it creates a factual inconsistency.

**Evidence:** Party doc: *"Glabbagool (now a grey ooze, traveling in bag of holding)."* Recap throughout: *"sentient gelatinous cube,"* *"he would just expand to trap her,"* *"fixed all six of his pseudo-eyes on her."* Grey oozes do not have pseudo-eyes in standard 5e statblocks; gelatinous cubes do.

**Suggested fix:** Clarify in either the party document or the recap whether Glabbagool remains a gelatinous cube (and the party doc entry is erroneous) or whether he has transformed, and if so, update the physical descriptions in the recap to match his current form.

## ISSUE 9: Beregost described as situated "along the cliffs above the Sea of Swords" — geographic inaccuracy

**Location:** Locations → Beregost; Locations → Way of the Lion; Scenes → The Burning Wizard Inn

**Issue:** The recap describes Beregost as situated "along the cliffs above the Sea of Swords." In the Forgotten Realms, Beregost is an inland town on the Trade Way, well east of the coast. It is Candlekeep itself that sits on coastal cliffs above the Sea of Swords. The Way of the Lion runs from Beregost toward Candlekeep along the cliffs — so the cliffside setting belongs to the road and the keep, not to Beregost.

**Evidence:** Locations → Way of the Lion correctly states: *"The road running along the cliffs above the Sea of Swords leading toward Candlekeep."* The Burning Wizard Inn location entry contradicts this by placing Beregost itself on those same cliffs. The Summary is silent on Beregost's geography and does not introduce this error.

**Suggested fix:** Amend the Beregost location entry to remove the reference to cliffs and the Sea of Swords. Clarify that Thorin's seaside contemplation occurs either during travel on the Way of the Lion after departing Beregost, or on arrival near Candlekeep — not while still in Beregost itself.
