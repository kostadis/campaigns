## Daz — Concentrated Fire

The Dust of Suleiman lands and the battlefield reorganizes itself in my head.

This is the part I'm good at. Not the talking, not the diplomacy, not whatever Thorin does when he walks directly into the emotional centre of a conflict and somehow makes it worse and better simultaneously. This part. Reading the board. Identifying the highest-value targets. Calculating acceptable losses against projected return.

The priorities are clear: Ilvara, and the icon. In that order, or really in no particular order — they're the same problem wearing different faces. Asha was explicit enough about what's riding on this that I haven't been able to fully file it away the way I prefer to file things. It keeps surfacing. The stakes are not abstract. The stakes are Asha, and everything she told me, and the fact that a Zuggtmoy-aligned symbol of office left standing is not a symbol — it's a seed. You don't leave seeds.

So. Fireball. Cast at fourth level. Eight dice.

I have used Fireball the way some people use a favourite coat — comfortable, reliable, something I reach for when precision matters less than coverage. But coverage is not what I need. I need placement. Ilvara is elevated, which is poor positioning on her part and convenient positioning on mine. The icon is beside her. Both targets, one sphere, and the geometry cooperates if I'm exact.

Zalthir and Thorin are inside the problem zone.

This is what Sculpt Spells is for. I carve them out. The fire will know the shape of them by the time it arrives and it will go around — not because fire cares, fire doesn't care about anything, but because I told it the shape of what it cannot touch. It is perhaps the most useful thing I know how to do. Precision is not the opposite of force. It is force, directed.

The sphere lands where I placed it.

Ilvara is quick. She half-saves, which is professionally irritating but not unexpected — she did not survive this long in Menzoberranzan's social ecosystem by being slow. She takes twelve. The icon takes twelve. The fire roars around Zalthir and Thorin and doesn't touch them.

Both targets absorb the blast.

I have found purpose. I am the feared mage of the Ember Vanguard.

The irony is almost invisible, even to me.

Grygum's voice cuts through the settling heat: *"That worked better than I had any right to expect."*

It's an honest assessment, and I appreciate honesty in tactical partners. Two spells, two priority targets hit, the fight still live but the math changed in our favour. Not a perfect outcome. The icon is still standing. Ilvara is still standing. But twelve points is twelve points and the board looks different than it did thirty seconds ago, and that is the only metric that has ever mattered.

I note that Zalthir is unsinged. I note that Thorin is unsinged.

I don't say anything about it. It was the plan. Plans working is not remarkable.

But I notice.
