**[The Spider Descends — Daz Weaves His Illusion]**
Daz commanded his spider familiar to scurry back toward him across the fungal cavern floor while simultaneously weaving a dazzling display of Dancing Lights. The magical luminescence shaped itself into a radiant, glowing form — the silhouette of the Spider Queen Lolth herself — that shrank and condensed into the tiny spider, which then seemed to absorb directly into Daz's body. The light vanished. The performance was seamless.
A breathtaking gamble, staking everything on spectacle and nerve in the dark of the Underdark.

---

**[Glabbagool the "Invisible Rock"]**
Behind Daz, the rest of the party held their breath in the shadows. Glabbagool — the intelligent gelatinous cube — attempted to disguise himself as an inanimate rock, going still and trying to appear as nothing more than part of the cavern.
GM (narrating Glabbagool): "He's like… then he starts talking and has to be reminded that Invisible Rock doesn't talk. Then he opens his eyes, and… I mean, he's not getting this whole staying motionless and looking like rock. Eyes that will look left and right are not very invisible."
Daz: "Glabbagool, I'm taking care of this, it's fine."
An endearingly terrible attempt at stealth that threatened to unravel the entire con.

---

**[Asha Questions the Display]**
Asha Vandry, the paranoid drow priestess, watched the spectacle with wide, crazed eyes, searching for signs of trickery. She spoke first.
Asha: "You're a wizard. A wizard… a wizard who prays to Lolth?"
The question hung in the air — not awe, but suspicion, sharp and immediate.

---

**[Daz Sells the Possession]**
Daz adopted the persona of a vessel, speaking in hushed, reverent tones.
Daz: "I am… you are not speaking to the wizard of this body. I… he… this body has communed with Lolth. And I… I am speaking through this body."
He leaned into the role fully, gambling that conviction could carry what evidence could not.

---

**[Asha Turns the Table]**
Asha's paranoia fired — but not in the direction Daz expected.
Asha: "Aha, you are trying to test me, to see if I was one of those fake followers of Lolth, who are willing to believe every charlatan magic user that is somehow being possessed by the power of Lolth."
She didn't buy the deception — but she reframed it entirely, turning Daz's con into a test of *her* faith rather than his.

---

**[Daz Pivots — The Spider as Proof]**
Daz recovered quickly, abandoning the direct possession claim and leaning into the spider's earlier appearance as evidence.
Daz: "There are many imitations, but as you saw, I came to you in my smaller form, and led you here. You should know the truth."
A deft redirect — grounding the claim in something she had actually witnessed.

---

**[Asha's Second Suspicion — The Spore Theory]**
The deception crumbled a second time. Asha found a new interpretation entirely.
Asha: "Oh, dear goodness, have you two been infected by the spores, possessed by the crazed magic of Zuggtmoy? convinced of the bride's power? Is this another trick of Elvara's?"
For a moment it seemed the whole gambit would collapse — not into disbelief, but into the wrong kind of belief.
