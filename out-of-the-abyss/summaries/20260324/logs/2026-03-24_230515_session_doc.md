## Section — Grygum — Zalthir Opens the Fight

The plan was elegant. That was the first thing I noted.

Zalthir doesn't build up to things. There's no announcement, no dramatic stance. One moment he was behind me, and then he simply wasn't — and Ilvara, high on her platform, had a dragonborn grappler materialized at her elbow with nothing between them. The suddenness was the weapon. It always is, with him.

I tracked the whole thing from the backline, the Dust of Suleiman still warm in my hand. The timing mattered. The positioning mattered. I was running the geometry in my head — where she was standing, where the dust needed to land, how many steps it would take to close that gap once Zalthir moved her into range. This is what I do when other people are hitting things. I take notes. I calculate. I try not to get hit.

The first blow landed. I felt it from thirty feet away — 16 damage, and more importantly, a grip. Ilvara caught. The platform gave her the height, the elevated ground, the psychological advantage of looking down at everyone. Zalthir gave her nowhere to move. I watched her expression — or I watched what I could see of it, from my angle — and I clocked the instant she realized she was *held*. That matters. People make bad decisions when they realize they're held.

Then the second hit. Fifteen. She was exposed now, grappled and unable to create distance, and Zalthir was pressing it the way you press something when you've trained yourself to recognize a window closing. Two strikes, a hold, the momentum entirely his. The geometry was still good. I adjusted my grip on the Dust.

He took to the air.

This was the part of the plan I loved most. Zalthir with Ilvara in his grip, angling toward the drop zone, the whole elegant trap drawing closed like a knot. I was already moving, already calculating the arc, already composing in my head the quiet prayer to Bahamut that I would absolutely deliver once this worked. *A hoard is always valuable*. A plan coming together is its own kind of hoard.

And then she went down.

Not down as in dropped — down as in *through*. Ilvara just *flowed* into the fungal matter beneath the platform, dissolved into it like she'd been liquid all along, and the space where Zalthir's grip had been was suddenly nothing. The grapple broke. She reappeared elsewhere. The GM told us she screamed — furious, he said, at herself — which was almost funny, because I was also furious, but not at myself. I hadn't let go.

She had a legendary action. Of course she did. You don't run Menzoberranzan's slave operation in the Underdark without having a few legendary actions in reserve. I noted it. I noted it in the place in my head where I keep things I should have anticipated.

Behind me — or maybe beside me, I was already recalculating — Gabe's voice carried Zalthir's moment of perfect, helpless bewilderment.

"I'm out of actions and bonus actions, so I guess I'll just kind of look on and be confused."

All that precision. All that choreography. Teleport, strike, grapple, elevate, deliver — and a single legendary action had unwound every stitch of it, and now Zalthir was standing in midair, holding nothing, looking confused.

I could not blame him. I was holding Dust of Suleiman and standing in the wrong place.

The plan hadn't failed. The plan had met something faster than the plan. There's a distinction, and I was already working on what to do about it.
