## Section — Zalthir — Zalthir Opens the Fight

The distance across the cavern was a problem. Then it wasn't.

One moment I was reading the geometry of the space — the elevated platform, the sightlines, the gap between us and her — and the next I was already there, adjacent, inside the radius where a priestess stops being an authority and becomes a target. She hadn't called that. She hadn't dictated anything. I stepped through the dark and arrived before she'd finished deciding what to do, and that moment of her not-deciding was exactly what I needed.

I drove the weapon in. Sixteen. The grip locked.

Grabbing a drow priestess of Ilvara's caliber felt like grabbing a live coal — you know going in that it's going to cost you, you do it anyway because the alternative is worse. But the grapple held. She was in my hands, her movement mine to constrain, and suddenly the battlefield looked different. Not good. But different in a way I could work with.

The second strike came with her pinned and her options narrowed. Advantage. Fifteen more. Thirty-one total. She was hurting and she knew it and I could feel that knowledge in the tension of the hold — the way resistance shifts when something goes from confident to afraid.

Then I looked at the cavern floor. At the Dust of Suleiman area. At the rough arithmetic of: priestess plus that zone equals someone else's problem.

It was a brutal plan. It was also economical. You let the battlefield do the killing when you can. I'd spent enough — I wanted to spend her.

I started moving her toward it. Fly, carry, drag if I had to.

She screamed.

Not at me. The GM's exact words: *at herself.* And I caught that — I filed it, the way you file things that tell you something true. She was furious at herself. Which meant I'd actually hurt her, actually threatened something she thought couldn't be threatened, and the scream was the sound of that particular illusion breaking.

Then she was gone.

Between one heartbeat and the next, she came apart into the fungal matter beneath her feet — flowed through it like water pouring into a crack, like smoke running back into the dark — and reappeared somewhere else on the cavern floor. The grapple didn't break. It simply stopped having anything to hold. My hands closed on nothing. She was out of reach and already putting distance between us, and the scream still echoed off the cave walls like she'd left it there on purpose.

I hung in the air.

Out of actions. Out of bonus actions. Out of everything I'd had when I arrived on that platform thirty-one damage and one tactical calculation ago.

Below me, the battlefield was still happening. People were still fighting, or dying, or doing things that required me to have something left to give, which I didn't. I had altitude and a view and the specific exhaustion of a plan that almost worked.

*She's angry at herself,* I noted, and filed it away, and watched.
