## Section — Zalthir — The Heart Fungus Retaliates

The cloud came without warning.

One moment the Heart Fungus was simply a thing that needed to die, large and wrong and pulsing with something that wasn't quite life. The next, the air was gone. Not gone — replaced. Replaced with a billowing mass of spores so thick they had texture, had weight, settled into the throat and stayed there. My constitution has carried me through worse. Shipwreck. The Underdark's general commitment to killing everything in it. A monastic order that considered deprivation a form of teaching.

It was not enough. The toxin found a way in regardless, and I felt it immediately — that particular burning behind the sternum that means your body is now working against you. Not against them. Against you. Every motion slightly wrong, slightly dragged, like fighting through water that only you can see.

I beat my wings and went up. Ten feet, clean and fast, pulling myself out of the radius while the cloud was still expanding below. Tactical. Deliberate. Not a retreat in any meaningful sense of the word.

The poison disagreed.

Altitude changes nothing once the damage is done. I hung in the cavern air above the dissipating edge of the cloud, breathing air that was marginally cleaner, and took stock of the fact that I was still poisoned regardless of where I had put myself. There is a particular irritation in making the correct decision and being punished for it anyway. I noted it. I moved on.

Then Ilvara's voice moved through the chamber.

And the floor rose.

Four of them. Former Velkynvelve guards — I recognized the shapes before I recognized what had happened to them, which is its own particular unpleasantness. Drow, once. Captors, once. The ones who had run the chains and worked the cells and known exactly which cruelties were efficient and which were indulgent. They had not had good deaths. That was clear from the way they moved now, which was to say without intent, without hesitation, without anything behind the eyes that constituted a self. Fungal growth threaded through them where something else used to be, and they lurched upright with the blank obedience of things that had been emptied out and refilled with something else's purpose.

"Those poor guards," I said. "They've been through quite a lot."

The GM, or the universe, or whatever holds this cavern together, offered the obvious reminder: *Yeah, I mean, that was Valen's fate.*

Right. Valen. I had known that. I had filed it somewhere and not taken it out again because taking it out again didn't serve any tactical function. It served one now — or rather, it didn't serve any function at all, which is its own kind of useful information. There was nothing to do with what Valen's fate meant except know it. The transformation was the point. The emptying was the point. This was what the Heart Fungus was for.

I held my position ten feet up, lungs still burning, and watched the four shapes orient themselves toward us with the unhurried certainty of things that didn't need to hurry.
