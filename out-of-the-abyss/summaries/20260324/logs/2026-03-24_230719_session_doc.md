## Section — Grygum — Concentrated Fire

The Dust of Suleiman left my hand and I watched it go. That's the thing about that particular spell — there's a half-second where it's just out there, committed, and you can't take it back, and you're doing fast arithmetic in your head about whether you got the aim right. I centered it on the Big Heart Fungus because that's where the math pointed. Twenty force damage, no save required, no negotiation. It lands or it doesn't.

It landed.

And then — almost before I'd finished accounting for it — Daz was already shaping his Fireball. I clocked it before it flew: eight dice of fire, fourth level, sculpted around Zalthir and Thorin like he'd mapped them in the dark. Centered between Ilvara and that festering Zuggtmoy icon on her platform. I took note of that. It's what I do. The sculpting alone told me something about Daz I hadn't quite catalogued yet — the precision of it, the care taken to pull the friendly figures *out* of the radius before releasing everything else into it. That's not raw power. That's a man who has thought about consequences.

Ilvara rolled well. She halved it. The icon ate its share of the same.

Still hurt.

I'm standing there watching force damage and fire damage arrive in quick succession on the same targets — two separate spells, two separate casters, no coordination spoken aloud, just the shape of a fight falling into place — and I felt something I don't feel often enough.

Genuine surprise.

"That worked better than I had any right to expect."

I meant that as an honest accounting. A soldier's receipt for a battle that paid out more than it promised. The plan — if you could call it a plan — was thin. The taunt that sent Jorlan running at the trap was thin. The timing on the Dust was thin. The Fireball landing clean was Daz reading the same moment I read and arriving at it from a different angle entirely.

Sometimes the pieces just fall. Bahamut may or may not have arranged the geometry. I took notes either way.
