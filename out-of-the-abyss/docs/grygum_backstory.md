Grygum’s backstory is defined not by the traditional, militaristic dogma of Bahamut, but by a radical, almost heretical theological interpretation of the Dragon God's will
. Born and raised within an orc tribe, Grygum learned early on how to navigate brutal hierarchies, noting that one could terrorize the orcs in his tribe simply by invoking the names of their superiors
. This harsh upbringing forged him into a highly practical creature, one who views survival in the Underdark through a lens of pragmatism rather than blind zealotry
.
His path diverged significantly when he became a disciple of a mentor named Gorg’Bahamut (or Grog'Bahamut)
. Under this tutelage, Grygum studied esoteric texts like The Platinum Chronicle: Discourses on the Abyssal Blight and became fascinated by the historical events surrounding the Cult of the Dragon
. Grygum’s core philosophy is anchored in a legendary chess match between a dwarf named Stroud and the Gold Dragon Protanther, following the events at the Well of the Dragons
. While traditionalists believe Protanther intentionally threw the match to signal a divine alliance, Grygum’s sect believes Bahamut directly guided Stroud's hand, proving that individual agency and mortal choice can alter the cosmos
.
This historical event led Grygum to his most radical belief: dragons are defined by their freedom of choice, not the pigmentation of their scales
. To Grygum, chromatic dragons are not inherently evil by birth, and making judgments based entirely on a creature's race is the ultimate evil
. This worldview fuels his profound disgust for the bigotry he witnesses in the Underdark, such as Eldeth’s rigid hatred for all Duergar and orcs
.
Despite his scholarly interest in linguistics, the origins of names, and his habit of taking meticulous notes on deals and lore, Grygum expertly weaponizes his harmless exterior
. He readily plays the "Platinum Fool," using theatrical cooking performances to disarm and distract drow guards while his allies steal weapons
. Furthermore, he is not above employing deceptive theatrics with his magic, staging dramatic healing rituals complete with preacher-like shouts to manipulate the beliefs of those around him
. He views the chaos of the Underdark as an interconnected web of transactional relationships, believing firmly that doing a good turn for someone will eventually yield a good turn in return
.
