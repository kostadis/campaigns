# Post-OotA Epic Arc: The Discrediting of the Gods

Optional epic-tier continuation after the events of `campaign_master_plan.md` §VI / `FalloftheCityofSpiders.md`.

## Optionality Rule

- Run only if the players want a continuation. If not, the campaign closes at OotA termination.
- The arc is documented for when players are interested, not to pressure them into it.
- The arc's existence should not be leaked during the main campaign unless the party is already investigating Stage 3+ of the Blink-Event Authorship ladder.

## Core Premise

Out of the Abyss ends the immediate demon crisis. It does not end the gods-war that produced it.

Canon chain (see `docs/campaign_design_frame.md` Resolved entries):

- **Lolth** is the Blink's principal. She authored the event to escape a prison Vhaerun put her in — a cross-world imprisonment that is open knowledge inside her cult (any Lolth-cultist NPC will speak of her imprisonment plainly) but whose *jailer* is never named by anyone still interested in surviving. The Blink is her jailbreak.
- **Vhaerun is the jailer**, and the jailer identity is the divine-tier secret of the arc. If it were published, Lolth's cults across the multiverse would come for him. This is why he cannot publicly claim authorship of any part of the affair — he cannot out Lolth without outing himself. His retaliation has to happen through cat's-paws.
- **Gromph Baenre** cast the ritual. It routed through abyssal rails, which is why the Orthodoxy's public "a powerful demon did this" cover held as a plausible misreading rather than a fabrication.
- **Orthodoxy leadership** coordinated components, authorization, and theological cover. They know about Lolth. They do not know about Vhaerun.
- **Vhaerun's angle is spite plus self-protection.** Lolth used her servants to free herself from his prison; he retaliates by wrecking those servants, and he uses the wreckage to recruit. He wants the Orthodoxy burned, the drow race destroyed if possible, and his jailer role buried under the rubble. He did not recruit Vizeran — Lolth's own cruelty produced the weapon, and Vhaerun declined to interfere.
- **Publishing the Blink authorship alone triggers racial genocidal war against the drow.** This is why the Orthodoxy suppresses at any cost. It is also why Vhaerun wants it published (via cat's-paws) — the drow race's destruction = Orthodoxy dead + Lolth's worshipper base erased + his jailer role buried with them = maximum petty victory at zero personal exposure.

The party, having survived Menzoberranzan's apocalypse, now holds enough fragments to publish. They do not yet know publishing alone will end their people. This arc is the path that prevents that.

## Hard Tone Rules

- **Both gods are evil.** Lolth is the tyrant who sold her people to save her position. Vhaerun is the sibling-god who let thousands die to feed his spite. The party is not picking a side between them; they are refusing both.
- **Never portray Lolth as redeemed.** She does not appear in this arc as an ally. Her silence throughout is her contribution.
- **Never portray Vhaerun as a liberator.** His followers believe they are freedom-fighters. The party's job is to show the cultists what they actually are.
- **Eilistraee is the good drow goddess, but she is not a deus ex machina.** She provides the ritual framework and the legitimacy. The work is the party's.
- **The positive outcome must be earned.** No shortcut endings. The heist is real, the revelation is costly, and the final publication carries lasting consequences.

## Act Structure (4 Acts)

### Act I: Escape and Revelation

Menzoberranzan's apocalypse has ended. The cage held; demons died inside it; Vizeran died with the seal resolving. The party escapes outward through the Underdark or along a Gauntlgrym extraction lane, exhausted and bereaved.

**Tonal shift**: claustrophobic fall → open sky. The party emerges onto the surface (Waterdeep outskirts, Silverymoon, or the Sword Coast depending on exit route). Different air, different light, different rules.

**The revelation lands here** — but only partially. The party learns:

- Vhaerun used them. The Orthodoxy's fall is his recruitment window.
- His spite, not his righteousness, was the point.
- They were his tools. Vizeran was his unwitting instrument.
- Doing nothing now means the Underdark and the surface world keep bleeding indefinitely — Vhaerun's new followers will drive the same cycle.

**What they do not yet learn.** The jailer identity — that Vhaerun is the one who imprisoned Lolth in the first place, and that the Blink was her jailbreak — is the Act IV ritual's payoff. At Act I, the revelator stops at "spite + recruitment window." The fuller picture is why the pre-schism artifact is needed: testimony alone cannot carry the jailer claim, because no one who names Vhaerun as Lolth's jailer survives the Lolth cult's multiversal response. Only a divine channel can make the claim stick.

**Who delivers the revelation?** GM picks at the table based on what the party has invested in. Candidates (all canonical):

- **Eilistraee directly** — vision, dream, or avatar appearance as the party reaches the surface. Most heroic, least mediated.
- **An Eilistraee priestess** the party crosses paths with — grounds the revelation in a living NPC; introduces the deity through her servant.
- **A defected Vhaerun cultist** who figured it out before the fall and was already looking for the party — dramatic, adds moral complexity (a cultist doing the right thing).
- **A Gauntlgrym contact** who has been piecing it together from surface observations of the Menzoberranzan event — rewards existing faction investment.

**The "fuckity-fuck-fuck" beat.** The party is tired, grieving, and has just survived a city-scale apocalypse. They now learn they were played. The beat is the emotional pivot from ending to epilogue — fury becomes purpose.

### Act II: Overland to Myth Drannor

The target is a pre-schism elven temple beneath the ruins of Myth Drannor. It predates the Drow/Elf split — before Corellon exiled Vhaerun, before Lolth fell, when the Seldarine was one pantheon. An artifact there will carry the pre-corruption essence of Vhaerun, usable by Eilistraee in the discrediting ritual.

**Surface journey.** Breathing room. Character reset. The party reconnects with geography from their earlier adventures (this is where they began before the Underdark, tonally). Possible beats:

- Resurfacing logistics (travel, supplies, rest).
- Short hostile encounters from parties who don't know the party's story (prejudiced surface folk; orc raiders; bandits). Low-stakes reassertion of competence after the apocalypse.
- An Eilistraee temple or hidden shrine en route, where her priesthood confirms the plan.
- Political aftershock: surface kingdoms are just learning Menzoberranzan fell. The diplomatic air is tense; rumors of the Blink authorship are already beginning to circulate (Vhaerun cultists may be trying to leak fragments early to accelerate publication).

### Act III: The Pre-Schism Temple

**Design premise**: the temple is older than the Drow/Elf split. Vhaerun can't guard it — his modern shadow-domain wards don't reach pre-schism essence. But the site is genuinely dangerous in its own right:

- Pre-split elven magic the party doesn't have context for.
- Forgotten protections that still function without understanding the modern world.
- Whatever has moved in over millennia (fey intrusion, aberration squatters, long-dormant guardians).

**The heist is metaphysical.** Reclaim what Vhaerun's corruption abandoned, use it against his modern self. The dungeon-crawl is the physical work of that moral inversion.

**The artifact.** Form is GM choice at table (relic, fragment, crafted thing, divine seal). Function is fixed: it carries the pre-exile Vhaerun's divine signature in a way Eilistraee can channel. It should feel numinous — when held, the party can sense they are touching a version of a god that no longer exists.

**Dungeon shape**: one major set-piece, one moral test, one genuine surprise. Pre-schism elven architecture (high arches, soft luminosity, no modern runic conventions) as visual signal that this place does not belong to any current faction.

### Act IV: The Ritual and the Settlement

Deliver the artifact to Eilistraee — her temple, her consecrated grove, or a site where her priesthood can host the working.

**The ritual.** Declared magic. Eilistraee, channeling through the pre-schism artifact, communes with every Vhaerun follower simultaneously. The divine echo of the pre-exile god lands in each cultist: *"This is what you used to be."* And then, using the pre-schism artifact as the channel — because the artifact carries the signature of the god Vhaerun was *before* he jailed his sister — Eilistraee forces the next sentence into the same divine voice: *"This is what I did. I imprisoned Lolth. She broke out through the catastrophe you lived through. The drow who died, died for my pettiness. And then I came for the survivors."*

The jailer-reveal is Act IV's payoff. It is the only moment in the arc where the divine-tier secret becomes public — and it becomes public at the one forum where the Lolth-cult retaliation cannot reach in time to silence it, because the claim arrives in the voice of a god Vhaerun has not been for millennia, addressed to Vhaerun's own followers, in their own heads, simultaneously.

**Mass defection.** Vhaerun's followers hear, from a divine source they cannot dismiss as rival propaganda, what their god did and what he became. The corruption becomes legible. Divine rank drops catastrophically. Some cultists will try to deny; the ritual is declared magic and the memory does not fade.

**The defectors become the drow-victim messengers.** Former Vhaerun cultists, joined by Menzoberranzan refugees, assemble the Blink-authorship proof from the fragments the party recovered during Stages 1–3 and publish it from a position the surface and Underdark cannot easily demonize: *"we rejected the god who used us; you cannot hate a race for its gods."*

**Genocide motive collapses.** The reveal reframes: drow ruling class were tools, gods are the authors, drow race are victims. Surface + Underdark response moves from extermination toward sympathy or at least neutrality. The Blink proof's publication happens without catalyzing the racial war.

**Both gods diminished.**

- Lolth loses the Orthodoxy as her barricade. Her comeback stalls.
- Vhaerun loses his followers and his recruitment window. Starved of worship, his divine rank drops toward vestige.
- Eilistraee, as the only drow deity proven to have acted for the drow race rather than used it, gains a path forward as a genuine alternative.

**The drow race is free to choose.** Eilistraee, silence, or something new. No god has a lock on them anymore.

## Vizeran's Vindication

Vizeran's stated goal throughout the main campaign is "Orthodoxy dead, drow finally free." He cannot imagine a path to that outcome that does not also destroy Menzoberranzan and himself. He is wrong. The party, by finishing the investigation he could not and refusing to be the weapon he forged, accomplishes his actual goal by a path he did not see.

He dies in the city having achieved partial success: the Orthodoxy burns. The party, in Act IV, achieves his full goal: the drow are free. He does not live to know they fulfilled his will better than he did. This is the arc's tragic payoff — the man who saw two-thirds of the truth and acted with absolute conviction is vindicated posthumously by the people he thought he was using.

The vindication has a second layer that should land in Act I or Act II of this arc. Throughout the main campaign, Vizeran was not only an architect; he was a guardian. The Duskryn extraction contract that shadowed Daz from Velkynvelve through the Underdark was Vizeran's, placed through cutouts so that the mercs themselves never knew (canon: `daz_heir.md` §VI, `daz_devir_narrative_arc.md`). Daz was kept alive through the Underdark crossing not by luck but by his uncle's quiet, invisible work — work the boy never knew about, work performed by a man who knew he could never claim credit because claiming credit would compromise the protection. When this surfaces post-Vizeran's death — through a Sharran broker, a recovered cutout ledger, a dying retainer — Daz learns that the most paranoid drow alive spent his last decade making sure his nephew survived to be free. The line is heavier because Vizeran is not there to hear it: he protected a boy who never met him as family, and died before either of them could acknowledge the relation. This is the second tragic payoff and it sits inside Daz's Act V identity work.

## Mechanical Guidance (Epic Tier)

- Treat this arc as level 17–20 content (post-OotA tier).
- Use fail-forward outcomes; avoid binary wipe states.
- The heist (Act III) is the main combat/dungeon challenge. Acts I, II, and IV are primarily investigation, travel, and ritual set-piece.
- Track defector count during Act IV — mass defection is narratively guaranteed but the *scale* determines how thoroughly Vhaerun's divine rank collapses. High defection = Vhaerun nearly vestige; low defection = Vhaerun diminished but still active, leaving residual threats that could seed a further continuation.

## DM Guardrails

- **Do not collapse this arc into "kill the god and done."** The power move is discrediting, not deicide. Killing Vhaerun returns his followers to Lolth, the worse of the two.
- **Do not make Lolth benevolent.** She is not an ally in this arc. Her silence is her only contribution.
- **Do not make Eilistraee a dea ex machina.** She provides the ritual framework; the work is the party's.
- **Do not trivialize the genocide stakes.** The racial-war consequence is real and visible. Publishing alone is the wrong ending; the party has to earn the reframing.
- **Keep Daz's identity central.** He is the drow survivor whose family was destroyed by the Orthodoxy; he is the credible voice in the defector-messenger ecosystem; his Stage 4 identity choice (claim / reject / burn the name) interacts with Act IV's settlement. All three of his choices read heroic under this arc — he gets real agency.
- **Preserve agency.** The party can decline this arc entirely and the campaign closes at OotA termination. They can also fail — Act IV's reframing is hard-won, not automatic.

## 60-Second Launch Script

"You survived. You got out. And on the surface, in the first real daylight you've seen in months, someone tells you the truth.

You were not freeing a people. You were feeding a god his favorite meal.

Vhaerun, Lolth's own brother, let you burn Menzoberranzan because it was the only thing he ever wanted. Now he is about to harvest the survivors and become stronger than he has been in a thousand years. If you do nothing, the Underdark keeps dying, and this time the surface dies with it.

There is a temple older than the drow. A version of Vhaerun still lives in it — a version from before he became this. Eilistraee, the one drow god who did not betray her people, can use that older self to tell his followers what he is.

You have a heist to run."
