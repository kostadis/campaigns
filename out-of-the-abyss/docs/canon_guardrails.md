# Canon Guardrails (Session Prep)

Use this before every session to keep the Menzoberranzan-arc canon consistent. Adapted from the Insight_Transfer Canon_Guardrails template to OOTA-current constraints. The Vizeran-only precursor is archived at `notes/imported_underdark/canon_guardrails_checklist_vizeran.md`.

## Hard Constraints

These do not drift. If they drift, the campaign breaks.

### Vizeran Secrecy
- Vizeran is physically hidden in Menzoberranzan. Anti-divination shielding on his sanctuary.
- Vizeran does not appear in public tribunals, council sessions, temple hearings, or mass scenes.
- Non-critical Vizeran directives move through proxies, dead drops, coded notes, and compartmentalized handlers.
- Mission-critical instructions are delivered only in person, inside a sealed safe room, by Vizeran himself.

### Daz Identity Vault (Sharran)
- Daz does not know he is Daz'issin DeVir. His memories were excised by a Sharran priestess before Velkynvelve.
- Daz's cover personality is authentic to him. Zone of Truth / insight reads him as honest.
- The recall trigger is tied to the House DeVir crest at the West Wall Ruins. Other symbols do not unlock him.
- Recall is a choice the player makes when the trigger lands. Do not force recall off-screen.

### Living Circuit
- Vizeran is the containment cage over Menzoberranzan, not merely a tether. His lifeforce, projected through the Dark Heart into the city's ley architecture, seals summoned demon lords inside the city until they die. Demons cannot flee the cage.
- The cage does not kill; it only prevents flight. Demon deaths on the Prime banish essences to the Abyss by MM default. The cage only has to hold until every demon dies inside it.
- Vizeran cannot leave the city limits. If he does, the cage drops and he dies.
- Vizeran intends to die with the city. His death is how the seal resolves when the last demon falls, not a side effect of ritual exhaustion. This is canon, not a twist.
- Vizeran does not have plot armor. The party can kill him at any time (see Recovery Pattern: Early Vizeran Death). Doing so after Tripod activation collapses the cage, not the apocalypse.

### Heat System Invariants
- Heat only moves up from in-world party action or visible consequences. It does not drift up from narration alone.
- Tier transitions (Rumors -> Purge -> Lockdown -> Extermination) require a proximate cause the party can name post-hoc.
- At Extermination, Inquisitors hunt actively; safe rooms degrade; the campaign enters endgame tempo.

## Operational Rules

- Vizeran messages via proxies may be partial, misleading, or cryptic. Mission-critical reveals are never carried by a proxy.
- Party interactions with the Orthodoxy pass through the Social Masquerade: Daz as "Master," the rest of the party as retinue, unless they deliberately break cover.
- Faction assets (Gauntlgrym set, Menzoberranzan-internal dissidents) pay their cost now, in-city, not as post-campaign favors.
- Clues about Vizeran's endgame, Daz's identity, or the Blink event follow the ladders in `decision_ladders.md`. Do not jump stages.

## Fail States

Fail states are when canon has been violated and play cannot continue without repair.

1. Vizeran seen publicly in-city by a non-ally witness.
2. Daz's identity exposed to the party before the West Wall Ruins trigger (off-ladder reveal).
3. Heat escalates to Purge or higher without a proximate cause the party could point to.
4. A mission-critical instruction arrives from a proxy instead of from Vizeran in a safe room.
5. The party learns Vizeran intends to summon demon lords (not stop them) before at least Stage 3 of the Vizeran-disclosure ladder.

## Recovery Patterns

- **Public Vizeran sighting**: retcon to a decoy, projection, or impostor. Restore absence next scene. If the party tests the impostor, the body must fail a verification check they can see.
- **Premature Daz recall**: treat the memory as a fragment (a name, a feeling) not the vault. Full recall still needs the West Wall crest.
- **Heat overshoot**: reveal the proximate cause retroactively via scene (an NPC names what the party did). If no cause exists, walk Heat back one tier with a "calm before storm" scene.
- **Proxy carries mission-critical instruction**: the instruction is false or incomplete. Real instruction arrives in the next safe-room scene and contradicts or corrects.
- **Early Vizeran Death (before Tripod activation)**: campaign shifts to the reconstruction branch. Party reverse-engineers ritual sequencing from partial notes, hostile archives, and field calibration. Slower, higher-Heat, fully viable. Vizeran is not the source of the crisis; he is the fastest operator of one available solution. The same termination choice awaits at the end, only harder.
- **Vizeran Death During or After Activation (cage collapse)**: the Tripod still fires — beacon, portal, and worm stampede are component-driven, not anchor-driven. The apocalypse proceeds. What collapses is the cage. Demons still engage each other (rivalry + game-theoretic necessity, not ritual compulsion), but uncaged losers flee rather than fight to the death. The finale converts from single-boss fight to multi-target pursuit through the collapsing city and surrounding Underdark. Each demon lord the party kills banishes by MM default. Each escapee is Faerzress-trapped on the Prime (they cannot voluntarily plane-shift home), goes to ground, and becomes a post-campaign threat. Track kills and escapes explicitly in `decision_ladders.md`.

## Integrity Checks (Per Session)

Run these at session start and again at session close.

- [ ] Did any hard constraint slip this session? If yes, name which and why.
- [ ] Did the party learn something at a higher ladder stage than prep predicted? If yes, log in `decision_ladders.md`.
- [ ] Did Heat move? If yes, is the proximate cause speakable?
- [ ] Did any mission-critical instruction arrive through a non-Vizeran channel?
- [ ] Did a proxy scene get treated as a safe-room scene by the party? If yes, correct next session.

## Escalation Policy

When a guardrail conflict appears mid-session:

1. **Pause** narration. Do not commit to the violating beat.
2. **Name** the violated constraint out loud (to yourself or to the table, as fits tone).
3. **Choose one**: preserve canon (retcon mid-scene), explicitly revise canon (decide this constraint is now different and log it), or branch (this session follows a new canon; next session reconciles).
4. **Log** the decision in `decision_ladders.md` under a new entry: what changed, why, cost accepted, revisit date.

If the conflict surfaces only in retrospect: apply the same steps before the next session's prep.
