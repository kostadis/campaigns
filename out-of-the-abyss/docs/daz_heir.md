Based on your directive to utilize a Sharran memory modification to preserve Daz's agency, here is the revised profile for Daz.
This approach creates a "Manchurian Candidate" dynamic. Daz is not pretending to be a decent person; he is a decent person because the memories of his noble cruelty and his true identity have been surgically removed. He is the ultimate deep-cover agent because even he doesn't know who he is.
The Profile of Daz: The Empty Vessel
True Identity: Daz'issin DeVir (Heir to the Fourth House). Current Identity: Daz, the wandering mercenary mage. The Mechanism: The Sharran Kiss. Vizeran DeVir did not trust a Drow to keep a secret, so he hired a Priestess of Shar (Goddess of Loss and Secrets) to excise Daz's memories of his name, his House, and the massacre that ended it.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I. The Backstory: The Mercy of Oblivion
The Problem: When House Do'Urden destroyed House DeVir, Vizeran saved his nephew, Daz. However, Vizeran knew that a Drow noble raised in the cutthroat politics of Menzoberranzan would eventually betray him or slip up. A conscious agent was a liability.
The Solution: Vizeran brought Daz to a Sharran cloister in the tunnels. He paid a hefty price to have Daz's identity "vaulted." The Priestess removed the knowledge of his lineage and his noble upbringing, leaving only his magical skills and a vague, fabricated history of being a low-born mercenary.
The Setup: Vizeran planted Daz in Velkynvelve not as a prisoner, but as a time capsule. He arranged for Daz to be "captured" by Ilvara, knowing that when the time was right, he would retrieve his nephew and unlock the memories to use him as the key to the DeVir estate.
Why Daz Is Required (Canonical Constraints):
- Ley-line Constraint: One phase of Vizeran's city-scale ritual requires DeVir blood-signature calibration that Vizeran cannot replicate.
- Access Constraint: Critical DeVir wards, vault-paths, and authorization seals only respond to valid DeVir lineage memory patterns.
- Legitimacy Constraint: Daz's authenticated survival can trigger legal-political fracture against the house that certified DeVir extinction.
- Cognitive Constraint: The Sharran vault preserves procedural recall (phrases, routes, sequence logic) that is inaccessible to Vizeran unless Daz is present.
Net Effect: Daz is not merely useful. Daz is necessary.
II. Why This Protects Player Agency
This narrative structure allows the player to roleplay Daz exactly as they have been—as a "nice guy" or a pragmatic survivor—without contradiction.
Honesty is the Best Camouflage: When Daz says he hates the Drow aristocracy, he isn't lying. The Sharran magic filled the holes in his memory with genuine resentment for the cruelty of the Drow, making his cover identity his true personality for now.
No Deception Checks Needed: Because Daz believes his own backstory, magic that detects lies (like Zone of Truth or the insight of a Stone Giant) reads him as truthful. He is just a mercenary wizard... until he isn't.
The Choice (The Agency): The ultimate agency comes when the memories are offered back. When Vizeran eventually offers to restore Daz's memory, the player must choose: Remain "Daz" the hero, or become "Daz'issin" the avenger.
III. The Trigger: The "Recall" Phrase
The Sharran priestess implanted a subconscious trigger—a phrase, a sound, or a specific location—that will cause the memory block to crumble.
The Location: The trigger is the Gate of House DeVir (now the West Wall Ruins).
The Effect: As soon as Daz sees the specific crest of his family (a specific spider design or rune) on the ruined gates, he will suffer a debilitating headache. He will instinctively know the pass-phrase to open the wards, not because he is guessing, but because muscle memory takes over.
Roleplay Moment: The party is stumped by a magical lock. Daz steps forward, his eyes glazing over, and whispers a word in High Drow he shouldn't know. The door opens. He blinks, confused. "How did I do that?"

IV. Lore Master Evaluation
Status Check: Daz believes he is a renegade Drow wizard with no ties to the nobility. He genuinely considers the party his friends.
Hidden Truths: Vizeran DeVir holds the "key" to Daz's mind. He views Daz not as a person, but as a living storage device for the authorization codes needed to access the DeVir estate's central magical nexus (where Vizeran is currently hiding).
Strategic Doctrine Link: Vizeran's end-state requires synchronized collapse of demonic and political systems; Daz is the only actor who satisfies the DeVir-linked constraints needed to execute that synchronization.
V. Strategy Evaluation
The Demon's Gambit: The demons (Demogorgon, Zuggtmoy) ignore Daz because he has no "smell" of high Drow politics on him. He appears insignificant, which is his greatest defense.
Menzoberranzan Response: House Baenre and the Orthodoxy have no record of "Daz the Mercenary." He is a ghost in their system. If they captured him and interrogated him, he would reveal nothing, because he knows nothing. This makes him the perfect infiltrator.
GM Recommendation (Pressure Point):
The Sharran Contact: In Menzoberranzan (perhaps in the Braeryn), the party might encounter the Sharran Priestess who performed the ritual. She recognizes Daz. She doesn't speak to him, but she gives him a look of profound, pitying recognition. She might whisper to him, "The vessel remains sealed. Good." This hints to the player that there is a missing piece of themselves without forcing the reveal immediately.
