# Campaign Master Plan: The Fall of the City of Spiders

This master document outlines the structural and thematic shifts for running Out of the Abyss with a centralized, high-stakes urban campaign centered in Menzoberranzan. It represents a major revision of the standard module.

Canon constraints referenced here live in `docs/canon_guardrails.md`. Reveal ladders referenced here live in `docs/decision_ladders.md`. This file is the narrative bible; those two files are the load-bearing rules.

## I. Core Premise: The "Choice" and the "Party"

In this version of the Underdark, the Drow are defined by Lolth's Gift of Choice. They are not naturally "evil" by birth, but have been radicalized by a Religious Elite (The Orthodoxy).

- **The Orthodoxy**: A Nazi-esque political party that uses the "Gift of Choice" to enforce absolute conformity.
- **The Powerful**: Noble Houses that go along with the Orthodoxy out of fear and a desire to maintain status.
- **The Masses**: Commoners, "un-house" drow, and slaves just trying to survive the regime.
- **The Outcome**: The campaign is a political thriller/espionage arc that concludes with the apocalyptic destruction of the city by the Demon Lords.

## II. The Base of Operations: Vizeran's Hideout

Instead of the distant tower of Araj, the party operates out of a hidden sanctuary within the city (potentially the ruins of Daz's family estate).

### Vizeran DeVir: The Silent Architect

Vizeran is an "Evil" figure who embodies the "Rule in Hell" philosophy.

- **Motivation**: He views the city as a "Heavenly prison" of order. He seeks to honor the "Gift of Choice" through the ultimate act of free will: Total Annihilation.
- **The Anchor**: Vizeran cannot (and will not) leave. He is the "Living Circuit" for the ritual. If he leaves the city limits, the ritual fails and he dies. He stays to witness the collapse of his enemies.

Hard constraints for Vizeran (secrecy, safe-room protocol, early-death reconstruction branch, Living Circuit) live in `docs/canon_guardrails.md`. Canon guardrails override any story convenience.

## III. Mechanics: Movement and "Heat"

### The Worm-Riders of the Deep

To avoid the slog of Underdark travel, Vizeran provides the party with the tools to ride Purple Worms (Dune-style).

- **The Logistics**: The worms cannot enter the city's magical wards. The party must "park" their worm in the Deep Wilds and trek the "last mile" into the city on foot.
- **The Strike**: Players use worms for lightning raids on fetch-quest locations, trading stealth (the noise of the worm) for extreme speed.

### The "Heat" System

As the party completes quests or interferes with the Orthodoxy, the city's alert level rises:

- **Ghost**: No one knows you are here.
- **Rumors**: Increased patrols in the Braeryn; "undesirables" are questioned.
- **Purge**: The Orthodoxy begins "The Night of Shattered Glass," raiding suspected safehouses.
- **Lockdown**: The city gates are sealed; magical scrying is constant.
- **Extermination**: House Baenre's Inquisitors are actively hunting the party.

Heat invariants (movement rules, proximate-cause requirement) live in `docs/canon_guardrails.md`. Lever-specific deployment detail lives in `docs/risk_levers.md`.

## IV. The Fetch Quests: The Tripod of Ruin

Vizeran requires the party to not only gather items but to "wire" them into the city's Ley-Lines.

| Objective | Target Location | Mechanical Goal |
|---|---|---|
| The Beacon | Narbondel (The Clock) | Etch runes to turn the city's heat source into a demon-baiting signal. |
| The Scent | The Great Rift (The Lungs) | Drop pheromone-infused reagents to guide the Purple Worm army into the city. |
| The Breach | The Pool of Baenre (The Heart) | Pollute the High Matron's communion pool to turn it into a portal for Demogorgon. |

## V. Key Themes for Engagement

To make the final "payout" meaningful, the players must care about the city they are destroying.

- **The Soup Kitchen**: Introduce NPCs among the "masses" who represent the innocent victims of the Orthodoxy.
- **The Artist's Grief**: Show the beauty of Drow architecture and art being "rotted" by the demonic influence.
- **The Social Masquerade**: Daz (the Drow PC) must act as the "Master" of the party to navigate noble districts, forcing a high-tension roleplay dynamic of "Slave and Master."

## VI. The Final Payout: The Apocalypse

The campaign ends not in a remote cave, but in the center of the city.

- **The Ritual Completes**: Vizeran activates the Dark Heart.
- **The Tripod Fires**: Narbondel becomes a faerzress beacon; the Pool of Baenre opens as a portal; the Great Rift pheromone drop pulls the Wormwrithings stampede into the city. Demon lords are drawn through the portal. The worms and arrival trauma tear the city landmarks apart (e.g., Demogorgon snapping the Narbondel).
- **The Living Circuit Is a Cage**: Vizeran's lifeforce, projected through the Dark Heart into Menzoberranzan's ley architecture, seals every demon that enters the city inside a containment field welded to the ley lines. Demons cannot leave. Captivity plus mutual hatred plus the game-theoretic necessity of killing rivals now (any survivor becomes exponentially harder later) drives them into a battle to the death. The cage does not kill; it only prevents flight. Demon deaths on the Prime banish their essences to the Abyss by default (MM canon); the cage only has to hold until every demon dies inside it.
- **Coalition Asset Strikes**: Faction-provided assets (intel, access, null-ward windows, extraction lanes, sabotage teams) handle priestesses, guards, and civilian evacuation while the cage handles demons. Assets do not fight demon lords — they fracture Orthodoxy control in the zones the demons are not currently destroying.
- **Termination**: When one demon lord remains — weakened, alone — the party strikes. Its death on the Prime banishes its essence. The cage's purpose is served at that moment; Vizeran dies with the seal resolving, not from ritual exhaustion.
- **The Final View**: The party escapes (or stays) as the "Choice" is finally fulfilled — Menzoberranzan burns, and the survivors are finally, terrifyingly free.

### If Vizeran is dead at this stage

The Tripod is component-driven, not anchor-driven. The beacon, portal, and worm stampede fire regardless of Vizeran's state. Demons arrive. The city falls. What collapses is the **cage**.

Demons still engage each other — mutual hatred plus game-theoretic necessity drive the fight without ritual compulsion — but uncaged losers break contact and flee rather than fight to the death. The party's finale converts from "kill the weakened sole survivor" to a **hunt**: pursue multiple wounded demon lords through the falling city and the surrounding Underdark before they can reach a redoubt and recover.

Key mechanics:

- The Faerzress trap (the same condition that made OotA's premise work) keeps all demon lords Prime-bound. They cannot voluntarily plane-shift home. Death on the Prime is the only exit.
- Each wounded demon lord the party catches and kills = one fewer loose thread. Each that escapes goes to ground in the Underdark, recovers over months, and becomes a permanent post-campaign threat.
- Track kills and escapes explicitly. A clean hunt kills all escapees; a failed hunt yields 1d3 demon-lord loose threads entering the campaign aftermath.
- The Vhaerun post-arc becomes operationally urgent in this branch — the Blink-authorship archive is the only remaining lever on who resolves the lingering demon presence.

This is not a fail state. It is a messier success — more combat, more aftermath, and a deliberate trade the party chose under pressure. Do not invalidate prior progress; Vizeran-gated calibration steps already completed remain valid.

### Army Replacement Canon

- The campaign does not run mass-combat army management.
- Faction support is represented as limited, high-impact assets with debts and fallout.
- Use `docs/gauntlgrym/` faction assets as the operational framework for mission prep and finale resolution.

### Post-OotA Trajectory (Optional)

The Menzoberranzan apocalypse closes the main campaign but does not close the gods-war that produced it. If players want a continuation, the canonical shape is a four-act discrediting arc:

1. **Escape + revelation.** The party emerges from the falling city onto the surface; the revelation lands that Vhaerun used them — spite motive, Orthodoxy-collapse is his recruitment window, both realms continue to suffer if they do nothing.
2. **Overland to Myth Drannor.** Surface journey, tonal relief, reconnection to geography from the party's earlier adventures.
3. **Heist of a pre-schism elven temple.** Beneath Myth Drannor, the party recovers an artifact of Vhaerun from before Corellon exiled him — a version of him still Seldarine-aligned, still legitimately part of Eilistraee's pantheon.
4. **Eilistraee's ritual + resolution.** She channels the pre-corruption artifact to commune with Vhaerun's modern followers, broadcasting the divine echo of what he was. Mass defection. The defectors plus Menzoberranzan refugees become the credible drow voice publishing the Blink authorship proof. Drow-authorship reframes as drow-victimhood; the genocide motive collapses. Both Lolth and Vhaerun lose legitimacy. Drow race free to choose Eilistraee or no god at all.

Vizeran's stated goal (Orthodoxy dead, drow finally free) is achieved by this path without the genocide — and without his death being the cost. He dies unaware that the party fulfilled his will more completely than his own ritual could have.

Full detail in `docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`. Arc is optional per the Optionality Rule; close at OotA termination if players don't engage.

## VII. Daz and House DeVir: Integrated Clue Arc

Daz's connection to House DeVir is revealed in escalating layers rather than as a single lore dump.

**Design principle**: start with social tells (easy to deny) → escalate to physical evidence → force conflicting testimony → end with hard, irreversible proof tied to player choice.

The full reveal ladder (Stage 1 Ambient Signals → Stage 4 Hard Proof) lives in `docs/decision_ladders.md` under "Ladder: Daz Identity Reveal." Do not jump stages without an entry in the Decision Log.

### Chapter Integration (Remaining Arc)

- **Chapter 9 equivalent (Black Market)**: Introduce forged and genuine DeVir papers in circulation.
- **Chapter 11 equivalent (Archive/Oracle Heist)**: Discover contradictory "official" vs hidden DeVir records.
- **Chapter 12 equivalent (Beacon at Narbondel)**: Node calibration succeeds only when Daz participates directly.
- **Chapter 15 equivalent (City of Spiders hub)**: Social masquerade pressure spikes; Daz must perform rank.
- **Chapter 16 equivalent (Fetid Wedding thread)**: Ilvara's faction tries to weaponize DeVir legitimacy claims.
- **Chapter 17 equivalent (Finale)**: Daz chooses whether to invoke, reject, or burn DeVir authority in public.

### Mechanical Hooks

- **Heat Trigger Bonus**: Each confirmed DeVir clue increases Orthodoxy attention by one step unless actively concealed.
- **Access Benefit**: Confirmed DeVir status grants one of:
  - Safehouse access in old DeVir infrastructure
  - Reduced difficulty on noble district social checks
  - One-time bypass of warded barriers
- **Cost**: Every DeVir benefit creates one collateral consequence (informant exposed, ally targeted, district raid).

### Endgame Choice (Must Be Explicit)

At the Dark Heart activation point, force one clear choice:

- **Claim DeVir Legacy**: Stabilizes resistance command but paints a permanent target.
- **Reject Legacy**: Preserves anonymity, weakens command cohesion in the finale.
- **Burn the Name**: Publicly destroy DeVir claim to deny Orthodoxy and Vizeran both.

Daz's lineage is not background trivia. It is a strategic lever tied to Heat, mission access, faction behavior, and the campaign's final moral outcome.

## VIII. First Contact Canon: Daz and Vizeran

When Daz and the party arrive in Menzoberranzan, Vizeran initiates first contact in secret.

**Canon intent for first contact**: Vizeran identifies Daz and the party as his best available instrument to (1) end the demon plague, (2) destroy Menzoberranzan's current power structure, (3) break the authority of Lolth's clerical orthodoxy.

**Canon disclosure in that scene**:
- Vizeran tells Daz who Vizeran is.
- Vizeran tells Daz why Daz exists (preserved as deliberate leverage, not an accident).
- Vizeran confirms that the Duskryn extraction contract was his, placed through cutouts, with Nym and Kaelira deliberately kept blind. The trigger was Ilvara's escalating cruelty during Daz's Velkynvelve captivity and the trajectory of her continuing pursuit after the outpost fell; House T'sarran's interest at the Fungal Cavern was downstream, not part of the original trigger. He explains why he could not take custody himself: signature ripening, vault decay in proximity, visibility cost, and recognition cost (full canon in `daz_heir.md` §VI).
- Vizeran confirms that Daz is the *only* viable key, not merely the best one. He has tried the alternatives (forced extraction from a captured Do'Urden noble, his own struck-from-the-rolls signature, search for other DeVir survivors); none work. Canon line: *"You are not the best option. You are the only option. I have looked."* The pre-window failure mode (Daz dies before unlock = campaign ends in defeat) is therefore real, not bluff. Full canon in `daz_heir.md` §VII.
- This is a private revelation, never a public declaration.

**Mission flow implication**:
- After first contact, each major mission beat is advanced by a direct safe-room briefing from Vizeran.
- If safe-room access is compromised, the party receives only "hold pattern" instructions until a new room is established.

**Implementation note**:
- Any later public "DeVir reveal" must occur without Vizeran's visible presence.
- Vizeran's role is architect and trigger, not public claimant.
