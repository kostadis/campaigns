This master document outlines the structural and thematic shifts for running Out of the Abyss with a centralized, high-stakes urban campaign centered in Menzoberranzan. This document represents a major revision for the standard Out of the Abyss campaign.
Campaign Master Plan: The Fall of the City of Spiders
I. Core Premise: The "Choice" and the "Party"
In this version of the Underdark, the Drow are defined by Lolth’s Gift of Choice. They are not naturally "evil" by birth, but have been radicalized by a Religious Elite (The Orthodoxy).
The Orthodoxy: A Nazi-esque political party that uses the "Gift of Choice" to enforce absolute conformity.
The Powerful: Noble Houses that go along with the Orthodoxy out of fear and a desire to maintain status.
The Masses: Commoners, "un-house" drow, and slaves just trying to survive the regime.
The Outcome: The campaign is a political thriller/espionage arc that concludes with the apocalyptic destruction of the city by the Demon Lords.
II. The Base of Operations: Vizeran’s Hideout
Instead of the distant tower of Araj, the party operates out of a hidden sanctuary within the city (potentially the ruins of Daz’s family estate).
Vizeran Devir: The Satanic Architect
Vizeran is an "Evil" figure who embodies the "Rule in Hell" philosophy.
Motivation: He views the city as a "Heavenly prison" of order. He seeks to honor the "Gift of Choice" through the ultimate act of free will: Total Annihilation.
The Anchor: Vizeran cannot (and will not) leave. He is the "Living Circuit" for the ritual. If he leaves the city limits, the ritual fails and he dies. He stays to witness the collapse of his enemies.
III. Mechanics: Movement and "Heat"
The Worm-Riders of the Deep
To avoid the slog of Underdark travel, Vizeran provides the party with the tools to ride Purple Worms (Dune-style).
The Logistics: The worms cannot enter the city's magical wards. The party must "park" their worm in the Deep Wilds and trek the "last mile" into the city on foot.
The Strike: Players use worms for lightning raids on fetch-quest locations, trading stealth (the noise of the worm) for extreme speed.
The "Heat" System
As the party completes quests or interferes with the Orthodoxy, the city's alert level rises:
Ghost: No one knows you are here.
Rumors: Increased patrols in the Braeryn; "undesirables" are questioned.
Purge: The Orthodoxy begins "The Night of Shattered Glass," raiding suspected safehouses.
Lockdown: The city gates are sealed; magical scrying is constant.
Extermination: House Baenre’s Inquisitors are actively hunting the party.

Vizeran requires the party to not only gather items but to "wire" them into the city’s Ley-Lines.

V. Key Themes for Engagement
To make the final "payout" meaningful, the players must care about the city they are destroying.
The Soup Kitchen: Introduce NPCs among the "masses" who represent the innocent victims of the Orthodoxy.
The Artist's Grief: Show the beauty of Drow architecture and art being "rotted" by the demonic influence.
The Social Masquerade: Daz (the Drow PC) must act as the "Master" of the party to navigate noble districts, forcing a high-tension roleplay dynamic of "Slave and Master."
VI. The Final Payout: The Apocalypse
The campaign ends not in a remote cave, but in the center of the city.
The Ritual Completes: Vizeran activates the "Dark Heart."
The Arrival: The Demon Lords don't just appear; they tear the city landmarks apart (e.g., Demogorgon snapping the Narbondel).
The Worm Cavalry: The party leads the Purple Worms up through the Great Rift to strike at the Orthodoxy while the demons do the heavy lifting.
The Final View: The party escapes (or stays) as the "Choice" is finally fulfilled—Menzoberranzan burns, and the survivors are finally, terrifyingly free.
