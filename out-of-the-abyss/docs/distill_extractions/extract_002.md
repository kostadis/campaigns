## World Events

- **Day 3, 2nd tenday of Taraskh, 1492**: Prisoners are assigned to four work details — water carrying (Beremil), winch cargo (Balok), chamber pots (Guldor), and cooking (Imbros).
- Gyrgum puts on a theatrical cooking show for drow soldiers; attempts to steal a knife but is caught by Guldor.
- Zalthir discovers a paper with names of people who owe favors to the paper's owner; pockets it. Eldeth finds and pockets a comb.

- Buppido proposes escape, initially only for himself; Thorin manipulates him into including the group.
- Prisoners debate escape routes: Buppido wants Gracklstugh, Stool wants the Great Circle (myconids), Topsy wants Blingdenstone, Suushar wants Darklake first (direct path to Blingdenstone goes through Menzoberranzan).
- **Day 4, 2nd tenday of Taraskh, 1492**: Gyrgum assigned to cooking again; six drow soldiers attend for the show. Topsy and Turvy successfully steal several knives during the distraction.
- Zalthir examines the winch mechanism and spider silk rope for vulnerabilities; takes a rope sample to test against fire breath.
- Jorlan approaches Thorin and offers to facilitate an escape: he will leave the prison door open, create a distraction, and leave the path to the armory unguarded.
- Thorin deduces the prisoners' confiscated equipment is in the chest in Ilvara's quarters.
- Jorlan's plan: escape after a long rest, before daily chore assignments. Use the waterfall to slide down or jump into spider webs then into the water pools.
- All prisoners experience disturbing shared nightmares: dark shadows, endless tunnel mazes, oily tentacles, buzzing/howling, suppurating wounds with spores/maggots/insects — a sense that something far worse than drow lurks in the dark depths.

## Locations

**Drow Outpost (Velkynvelve implied)**
- A drow outpost built 100 feet above the ground on stalactites/hanging structures
- Features: prison cell, multiple hanging towers connected by bridges, a waterfall with a pool of water below, spider webs beneath the outpost, a lake nearby
- Direct fall from the outpost would be suicidal; possible escape routes include sliding down the waterfall into the pool or jumping into spider webs then into water

**Prison Cell**
- Where all prisoners are held; across from it is a stalactite with a door and two bored drow guards beyond
- Stool uses spores here for group telepathic communication

**Mess Hall / Kitchen Cave**
- Gathering and eating place for drow warriors
- Contains four circular zurkhwood tables with five chairs each; food prep and storage area with dried/fresh fungi, dried fruits, cheeses, preserved meat, clay jars of spices; heavy iron brazier for cooking heat and dim light; much food served cold

**Winch Platform**
- Located at the edge of the barracks platform
- Features a giant swinging arm with spider silk cord, pulleys, hand-cranked horizontal spool, and heavy woven basket
- Basket swings out over the chasm and can be raised/lowered; two quaggoth attendants operate it

**Priestess's Tower (Ilvara's Tower)**
- Largest hanging tower; uppermost level is a shrine to Lolth
- **Shrine**: Dark silken mats with silvery web pattern; broad zurkhwood pedestal with 10-foot sculpted spider; spider statue has 8 polished jet "eyes" (4 small worth 5 gp each, 4 large worth 10 gp each); flanked by two heavy silver candlesticks (25 gp each) with thick black unlit candles
- **Ilvara's Quarters** (below shrine, accessed by rope ladder): Walls hung with black mesh spider-web curtains from central ceiling point; thick woven mat floors; low platform bed with cushions/pillows; small table and two chairs; small shrine to Lolth draped in white silk; heavy chest of black-stained zurkhwood at foot of bed (Thorin believes prisoners' confiscated equipment is inside)

**Quaggoth Barracks**
- Cave to the north of the outpost
- At least one quaggoth observed resting inside

**The Lake**
- Near the outpost; where chamber pot waste is disposed of and cleaned
- Appears oddly clean; contains a grey ooze that processes the waste

**Waterfall and Pool**
- Waterfall near the bridge south of the prison cell; pool of water at the base below the outpost
- Thorin noted he could safely jump into the waterfall and land in the pool; identified as a possible escape route

**Spider Web**
- Below the outpost structure, above the pool of water
- Identified as a possible escape route (jump into web, then into pool)

**Gracklstugh**
- Buppido's homeland; a city in the Underdark; one proposed escape destination

**Blingdenstone**
- Deep gnome settlement in the Underdark; Topsy claims there is a way out of the Underdark from there; one proposed escape destination

**Menzoberranzan**
- Drow city; lies on the direct path to Blingdenstone; Suushar warns against traveling through it

**Darklake**
- Body of water in the Underdark; Suushar's people live near/at it; Suushar proposes going there first as an alternate route avoiding Menzoberranzan

**The Great Circle**
- Location of Stool's myconid people; one proposed escape destination

## Threads & Mysteries

- **The paper with names**: Zalthir found a paper listing names of people who owe favors — the city, nature of favors, and context are all unknown. Eldeth saw him take it.
- **Jorlan's escape plan**: Scheduled for after the next long rest. Jorlan will open the prison door and create a distraction; armory path will be unguarded. Prisoners must retrieve weapons from armory and possibly Ilvara's chest, then flee via waterfall or spider webs. Outcome uncertain.
- **Ilvara's instability**: Thorin noted Ilvara appears manic rather than calculating — unusual for drow. The cause of her apparent madness is unexplained.
- **The shared nightmares**: All prisoners experience disturbing dreams of shadows, tentacles, tunnels, buzzing/howling, and spore-bursting wounds. Something far worse than drow is sensed in the deep Underdark. Source and nature unknown.
- **Buppido's "divinity"**: The derro believes he is a god or claims to be one. His true nature, intentions, and the "Divine plan" are unclear. He initially planned to escape alone.
- **Prince Derendil's delusion**: Described as a "deluded Quaggoth" who believes he is a prince. True identity/history unknown.
- **Grey ooze in the lake**: A grey ooze lives in the pool/lake where waste is disposed, processing the filth. Could be relevant to escape or a hazard.
- **Escape route debate**: Unresolved disagreement on destination — Gracklstugh (Buppido), Great Circle (Stool), Blingdenstone (Topsy/Turvy), or Darklake first (Suushar). Direct route to Blingdenstone passes through Menzoberranzan.
- **Confiscated equipment**: Thorin believes it is in the zurkhwood chest in Ilvara's quarters. Retrieving it adds significant risk to the escape.
- **Spider silk rope vulnerability**: Zalthir took a sample to test whether his fire breath can cut it; result pending.
- **Stolen knives**: Topsy and Turvy successfully stole several knives — the prisoners now have some weapons.
- **Eldeth's comb**: She pocketed a comb found in the winch basket; significance unknown.
- **The Underdark's hostility**: Eldeth warned that survival depends on wits, resources are scarce, and outsiders are viewed as exploitable resources.
