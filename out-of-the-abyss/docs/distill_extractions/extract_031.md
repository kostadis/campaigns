## World Events

- Over the 7th–9th days of the 1st tenday of Myrkhul, 1495, the group travels through dark Underdark passages while Eldeth hides their tracks for two days
- On the 9th day of the 1st tenday of Myrkhul, the group enters a Faerzress-suffused cave containing fist-sized glowing quartz crystals erupting from the walls
- In the Faerzress cave, the group encounters two starving human escapees, Fuurm Coppernose and Baedora, hiding in a corner
- Fuurm and Baedora explain they escaped Drow captivity during a demon attack on the Drow, fleeing into the Underdark
- Daz invites Fuurm and Baedora to join the group; they accept
- Before leaving the Faerzress cave, the group collects as many crystals as possible, then smashes the cave to make it appear a monster attacked rather than escaped slaves taking crystals
- On the 2nd day of the 2nd tenday of Myrkhul, the group enters a vast underground fungal forest of bioluminescent and earthy mushrooms
- Shriekers in the fungal forest begin shrieking; Grygum throws a Faerzress crystal at one, producing no unusual magical effect beyond the shrieking continuing
- On the 3rd day of the 2nd tenday of Myrkhul, the group enters a second Faerzress crystal cave and collects more crystals; Eldeth reiterates concern about environmental disturbance; Jimjar accepts Grygum's bet that the crystals will prove useful
- On the 5th day of the 2nd tenday of Myrkhul, the group hears a soft feminine voice in their minds pleading to be freed from the dark as they approach the Lost Tomb of Khaem
- The group arrives at the large double-door entrance to the Lost Tomb of Khaem
- Inside the tomb entrance, the group observes a stone diorama depicting Brysis of Khaem and her retinue, and a mural depicting Netherese floating cities
- The group descends a dusty staircase, passing a Netherese Calendar Stone on one landing, and arrives at a shrine with shredded tapestries on the floor
- Grygum notes recent damage but no tracks in the dust; recalls lore about floating undead tomb guardians
- The group proceeds east into a room containing four stone sarcophagi with carved robed figures on their lids
- Grygum identifies that the northeast sarcophagus rests on hidden stone rollers
- Zalthir casts magical darkness around the northeast sarcophagus; Thorin shoves the lid aside, revealing a hidden passage downward
- Four Spectres emerge from the newly opened passage and attack the group
- The Spectres drain Daz twice with necrotic attacks, significantly weakening him
- Grygum attempts to Turn the Spectres and fails
- Zalthir kills two Spectres through unarmed strikes; the remaining two are also destroyed
- Daz casts Magic Missile against the Spectres, scoring hits on two of them
- During the Spectre fight, the feminine voice cries out "No, you must save me!" in the party's minds
- The group opens the four sarcophagi in the antechamber, finding mummified remains, tattered burial garments, and Netherese treasure of significant monetary and collector value
- The group descends into the lower burial chamber; Thorin leads
- In the lower chamber, the group observes murals of Brysis of Khaem and her floating cities, a gilded sarcophagus, and an invisible stone chest near the sarcophagus's foot
- A Wraith rises from the gilded sarcophagus and declares that the party's deaths will free it and that its victims will serve it in death
- Daz considers Fireball, rejects it due to the Wraith's fire resistance, and casts Magic Missile instead, damaging but not destroying the Wraith
- The Wraith attacks Thorin but misses; it then retreats back into the gilded sarcophagus
- Zalthir casts magical darkness over the entire inner chamber and moves the gilded sarcophagus lid aside
- The feminine voice speaks again, saying "In the sarcophagus! I can help you!"
- Zalthir looks inside the gilded sarcophagus; contents not yet revealed

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Identity of the Feminine Voice**: The soft feminine voice that called to the party from within (or near) the gilded sarcophagus has not been identified. It is distinct from the Wraith (which spoke with menace rather than pleading). It claims to be trapped and offers to help the party. Zalthir has just looked into the sarcophagus; the contents are unrevealed.
- **The Wraith's Fate and Identity**: The Wraith retreated into the gilded sarcophagus before Zalthir opened it. Its current position and relationship to the feminine voice are unresolved. Whether it is a form of Brysis of Khaem or something else is unknown.
- **Return of the Spectres**: Grygum recalled that undead of this type may return when a central evil force persists. With the Wraith (or whatever central evil) still active, the four destroyed Spectres may reconstitute.
- **The Invisible Stone Chest**: An invisible stone chest was observed near the foot of the gilded sarcophagus; it has not been opened or examined further.
- **Fargus Rumblefoot's Warning**: A previously encountered NPC named Fargus Rumblefoot warned the group about a "secret room" or "false tomb" within the Lost Tomb of Khaem. This has not yet been fully investigated; the northeast sarcophagus passage may relate to this, or another secret room may remain undiscovered.
- **Netherese Calendar Stone**: A Calendar Stone was noted on a staircase landing; its purpose was described as "unclear" and it has not been further examined.
- **Fuurm Coppernose's Lost Sister**: Fuurm entered the Underdark searching for his lost sister. Her fate and whereabouts are entirely unresolved.
- **Jimjar's Bet on the Crystals**: Grygum and Jimjar have an active bet that the collected Faerzress crystals will prove useful. The crystals have not yet demonstrated extraordinary utility beyond dim light.
- **The Demon Attack**: Fuurm and Baedora escaped during a demon assault on the Drow. The nature, origin, and current location of these demons are unaddressed.
- **Drow Pursuit**: Eldeth believes pursuit has been reduced to a whisper, but has not confirmed the Drow have abandoned the chase entirely.
- **Grygum's Mending Skills**: Grygum is deliberately concealing his mending skills to avoid being conscripted for ship work; the identity of who he is hiding this from and whether it will become relevant is unresolved.
