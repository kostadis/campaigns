## Threads & Mysteries

- **Rumpadump rescue**: Stool wants the party to find Rumpadump, an unhappy myconid sprout from Stool's circle, who may be with a group of ape-like creatures. Stool insists no one needs to die and the myconids are not enemies. Zalthir deferred this task. The ape-like creatures and their connection to the myconids remain unexplored.
- **Myconid zombie process**: Stool revealed myconids can convert dead enemies into zombies. Implications for the party and broader Underdark situation unclear.
- **Pelek's possession**: Pelek is a ghost possessing a Derro body and fears losing control. How long can this arrangement last? What happens if the Derro regains control?
- **Ongoing battle with Narrak's cult**: Combat is unresolved. Narrak is restrained in darkness but alive. The Ettin is wounded but fighting. Multiple Derro remain.
- **Ritual book**: Narrak possesses a book containing the ritual for mutating stone giants. Its fate and contents beyond the chant are unknown.
- **Madness effect in the ritual circle**: The circle on the platform induces madness. Its full properties and origin are unexplored.
- **Stone giant mutations**: At least two stone giants have been converted to two-headed forms. Can Hgraam truly reverse this? The cult plans to convert at least one more.
- **Demogorgon's influence on Gracklstugh**: The cult's chant references Demogorgon's madness spreading through the city, the Deepking falling, and spirals turning in Whorlstone's depths. The full extent of Demogorgon's influence is unclear.
- **The Deepking's fate**: The chant states "The Deepking falls into endless night" — is this prophecy, plan, or already happening?
- **Bupido's altar**: The altar in Bupido's lair is to "some demonic power or some mad idea" — its nature remains unidentified.
- **Who did Bupido kill?**: Bupido previously killed a member of the party's group; the victim is unnamed in this text.
