## Threads & Mysteries

- **Who killed Janussi?** Poisoned via a book laced with midnight tears; heart removed after death to prevent resurrection; locket/key stolen
- **The two-searcher question:** Two people searched Janussi's apartment — one who knew exactly where the locket-key hid, one who didn't and tore through the safe. Possibly two different culprits
- **The missing locket and High Tower key:** The locket around Janussi's neck (holding one tower key) is gone; whether Kalan's key is genuinely his own or actually Janussi's is unverified
- **Is Bookwyrm involved?** Her whereabouts before 9 AM unknown; she steers toward Sylvira; Kalan thinks she's protecting the killer or was manipulated into firing him
- **Who impersonated Sylvira Savikas?** Walked the halls at night with a lantern; new information to Kalan; the real Sylvira may know who could wear her face
- **The missing part of a book** that should not be missing — possibly a century old, flagged as suspicious and possibly related
- **Were the corpse's words ever truly extracted?** Kalan claims questioning the dead failed because the impostor locked its mouth — but this is hearsay and unverifiable
- **Were the stolen sapphires worth killing for?** Expensive but not rare; tied to the Sapphire summoning spell
- **The Sapphire summoning spell:** Calls a hidden item to the caster's hand; only works on carried (not merely worn) items; lets one hide the key "nowhere at all" and summon it when needed — but reveals who is holding the item
- **Will Daral survive midnight?** If he dies, the party concludes he was the innocent poison victim, not the poisoner
- **Why the rush?** The party is told they can only chase a handful of threads before things move on; Grygum warns "there'll be more murders"
- **The meaning of Glabbagool's koan answer** — understood by the trained monks but not by Zalthir, who missed the silent-communication classes
- **The drow in the bag of holding** still requires watching
- **Kalan's safety:** At risk of being killed over the key; party warned him to beware shapeshifters
