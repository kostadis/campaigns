# Risk Levers

Inventory of actors who can force outcomes on the party. Per lever: holder, what they control, trigger condition, earliest warning signs, blast radius, mitigation, owner. Used at session prep to ask "what's the worst that can happen this session, and what would tip us off early?"

## Deployment Rules

- **Never open with the maximum-force lever.** Inquisitor extermination sweeps are not a Session 1 opener.
- **Prefer one clean deployment over repeated threats.** If the Baenre Inquisitors are brandished twice without biting, their teeth wear down.
- **Preserve ambiguity only when ambiguity is strategic.** Vagueness about whether a lever exists is a lever itself; vagueness about whether the GM will use it erodes trust.
- **Document every deployment.** When a lever fires, log outcome and trust impact in `decision_ladders.md` under the relevant ladder.

## Per-Lever Detail

### Inquisitor Sweep
- **Holder**: House Baenre Inquisitors, acting under Orthodoxy authority.
- **Controls**: physical enforcement in Menzoberranzan. Arrest, interrogation, execution.
- **Why it matters**: strongest in-city threat. Removes a PC from play for a session if deployed cleanly.
- **Trigger condition**: Heat tier hits Purge. Escalates at Lockdown; becomes dragnet at Extermination.
- **Earliest warning signs**: neighborhood Drow going quiet; increased patrol cadence; informants stop returning; a contact's "safe room" gets swept in absentia.
- **Immediate consequences**: one named NPC ally disappears; one safe room burns; a PC accrues a Wanted profile.
- **Downstream consequences**: ally network degrades; remaining safe rooms become scarce; heat tier locks until party completes a Heat-reduction action.
- **Mitigation path**: reduce Heat (Orthodoxy distraction, rival arrest, clean exfil); cycle safe rooms; sacrifice a decoy.
- **Owner**: GM. Do not deploy without a proximate party cause (see Canon Guardrails integrity check).

### Divination Net
- **Holder**: Gromph Baenre or his successor.
- **Controls**: scrying, arcane trace, anti-teleport wards.
- **Why it matters**: silent. Party may not know they have been observed until consequences land.
- **Trigger condition**: cumulative arcane tells (spellcasting in the city without cover, use of rare magical items near wards, repeated teleports in/out).
- **Earliest warning signs**: arcane dissidents (Runebreakers) warn of new wards; a familiar goes missing; Vizeran's anti-divination shielding reports a probe.
- **Immediate consequences**: Orthodoxy learns one party action post-hoc.
- **Downstream consequences**: next operation launches with surveillance already in place; Gromph may intervene personally.
- **Mitigation path**: Null Veil Window asset (Runebreakers); operate under divination-dead conditions; accept trace and misdirect.
- **Owner**: GM. Fire sparingly; a silent lever deployed visibly becomes loud.

### Ritual Collapse
- **Holder**: Vizeran DeVir.
- **Controls**: the Tripod of Ruin sequencing chain before activation. Distinct from cage collapse at or after activation (see Living Circuit Failure).
- **Why it matters**: this is the campaign's ticking clock before the finale fires. Losing sequencing resets the timeline.
- **Trigger condition**: party betrays Vizeran (defects to Orthodoxy, warns a faction, sabotages a component); party kills Vizeran *before* the Dark Heart is activated and the Tripod fires.
- **Earliest warning signs**: Grin Ousstyl's fear visible; Vizeran issues "verification" tests; proxy instructions start to probe party loyalty.
- **Immediate consequences**: Vizeran withdraws; next safe-room briefing does not arrive; the campaign enters the reconstruction branch (see Canon Guardrails).
- **Downstream consequences**: ritual sequencing recoverable from partial notes and hostile archives, but slower and higher-Heat. Reaches the same termination choice, only harder.
- **Mitigation path**: honor commitments if that is the plan; break with Vizeran deliberately at Stage 3 or 4 of the disclosure ladder so the branch is entered cleanly, not by accident.
- **Owner**: GM + player choice.

### Living Circuit Failure
- **Holder**: physics. Vizeran's anchor state is mechanical.
- **Controls**: the containment cage over Menzoberranzan. Vizeran's lifeforce is the power source; the cage holds summoned demon lords inside the city until they die. It does not kill — it prevents flight.
- **Why it matters**: structural constraint on the finale's termination phase. Determines whether the endgame is "kill the weakened sole survivor" or "hunt multiple escapees."
- **Trigger condition**: Vizeran crosses the city boundary (he dies, cage drops); or Vizeran is killed inside the city before the last demon lord falls (cage drops, surviving demons flee).
- **Earliest warning signs**: his hand tremors intensify; ley-line tracing becomes involuntary; arcane sparks jump between his fingers with increasing frequency as the cage takes load.
- **Immediate consequences**: cage collapses. The Tripod still fires (component-driven). Demons still arrive. Demons still fight each other — rivalry and game-theoretic necessity drive that, not the cage. What the cage prevented was **flight**: without it, wounded losers break contact and flee rather than die.
- **Downstream consequences**: the finale converts from single-boss fight to multi-target pursuit. Surviving demon lords are Faerzress-trapped on the Prime (they cannot voluntarily plane-shift home), so they go to ground in the Underdark to recover. Every escaped demon lord is a post-campaign loose thread. Strongly motivates the Vhaerun post-arc.
- **Mitigation path**: do not attempt to move Vizeran; protect him through activation. The party can test the constraint by proposing exfiltration; Vizeran's refusal is diagnostic. Accept the hunt branch deliberately if killing him is the chosen play.
- **Owner**: canon + player choice.

### Demon Lord Arrival
- **Holder**: the Abyss.
- **Controls**: city-scale catastrophe at Tripod of Ruin activation.
- **Why it matters**: this is what Vizeran is aiming at. The point of the campaign's climax.
- **Trigger condition**: Tripod of Ruin activates; Dark Heart talisman placed at all three sites (Narbondel, Great Rift, Pool of Baenre); or demon-site contamination reaches a threshold without the Tripod chain (reconstruction branch alt).
- **Earliest warning signs**: Dark Heart hums louder near sites; Faerzress distortions spike; worms from the Wormwrithings migrate toward the city.
- **Immediate consequences**: one or more demon lords arrive. Orthodoxy defense collapses in their zone. Party's evacuation window opens.
- **Downstream consequences**: Menzoberranzan does not survive the arc. The final act is about who escapes, who stays, who chose what.
- **Mitigation path**: break the Tripod chain mid-activation; kill Vizeran (partial arrival; demons bound back weakly); accept the arrival and survive.
- **Owner**: canon + endgame.

### Social Masquerade Collapse
- **Holder**: Vaelissa T'sarran, Matron Mother of House T'sarran. See `docs/npcs/vaelissa_t_sarran.md`. Iron traditionalist; runs scrutiny through ceremonial protocol, not Orthodoxy investigation.
- **Controls**: Daz's "Master" cover and therefore party access to noble districts.
- **Why it matters**: party's operational surface in Menzoberranzan depends on the masquerade. Vaelissa is not a neutral scrutineer — she has a pre-existing grievance (party destroyed her Velkynvelve kill team) and an open question (who paid House Duskryn to extract Daz alive, and why is he worth extracting). Her probes carry personal motive, not institutional neutrality.
- **Trigger condition**: cumulative Heat + acceptance or decline of one T'sarran invitation. The probe vector is the **Invitation Gauntlet**: formal House functions where ritual greetings, seating order, house-colors etiquette, and memory-of-alliance conversations expose non-native drow. Declining is also a signal — a Master does not decline a matron's invitation without ritually acceptable cause, and the cause is itself inspected.
- **Earliest warning signs**: first T'sarran invitation arrives; "the new Master" is addressed with increasingly specific protocol markers; T'sarran house servants are observed studying Daz at public venues; cross-reference probes appear from House Duskryn (Vaelissa's grievance against Duskryn mercenary interference is itself a pressure channel).
- **Immediate consequences**: cover breaks in a scene. Party loses noble-district access for that session. A failed probe produces a report Vaelissa can hand to the Orthodoxy if she chooses, which can accelerate the Heat tier transition.
- **Downstream consequences**: unmasking propagates to other noble houses. Party forced to operate through Braeryn and Drey or leave the city. If Vaelissa connects Daz to the DeVir bloodline, her grievance converts from house-honor to dynastic — she becomes a permanent Menzoberranzan-arc antagonist.
- **Mitigation path**: swap cover identity (Daz as a different visitor to other houses — but not to T'sarran, where records compound); retreat from the noble district and return later under a new cover; redirect Vaelissa's scrutiny onto House Duskryn by feeding the extraction-contract question. Neutralizing Vaelissa directly is *not* a cheap mitigation — killing or dishonoring a sitting matron mother is a city-shaking event that cascades into other levers.
- **Owner**: GM.

### Authorship Reveal
- **Holder**: fragmented. During the main campaign, the custody chain exists in pieces across Orthodoxy archives, Gauntlgrym dwarven intelligence, Vhaerun cult custodians, and Menzoberranzan black-market brokers. No single faction holds the whole. The party assembles fragments in Stages 1–3. Full assembly (Stage 4) and credible publication happen either during the campaign (party publishes) or during the post-OotA arc (defected Vhaerun cultists + Menzoberranzan refugees assemble and publish from a drow-victim position).
- **Controls**: whether the Blink reveal triggers racial genocidal war against the drow or triggers a gods-discrediting outcome. Not "political stability of one faction" — the stakes are the drow race itself.
- **Why it matters**: Stage 4 of the Blink-authorship ladder is the only stage in any ladder where a single decision can cause a genocidal war. Publication alone names the drow race as the Blink's authors; surface + Underdark response converts to extermination. Suppression keeps Lolth's escape unexamined and reseeds the cycle. Pairing with the Vhaerun proof (the Act IV jailer-reveal in `post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`) is the only path to a positive resolution and it is not automatic.
- **Trigger condition**: Stage 4 of the Blink ladder in `decision_ladders.md` — full custody chain assembled + publish/destroy/pair/trade decision.
- **Earliest warning signs**: Stage 3 conflicting testimony crystallizes around the Lolth layer; Vhaerun cultists attempt to leak fragments early to accelerate publication at the wrong time; Orthodoxy remnants surface a counter-narrative or attempt to buy silence; an Eilistraee contact makes first approach.
- **Immediate consequences**: depends on the decision. Publish alone = genocide motive ignites. Destroy = complicity + reseed. Pair = activates post-OotA arc, party commits to a level 17–20 continuation. Trade = moral-for-operational-capital conversion; the proof remains a live weapon in a named holder's hands.
- **Downstream consequences**: the shape of the campaign aftermath is decided here. Solo publication ends the drow as a people; suppression preserves the cage Lolth escaped into; pairing produces the only outcome where both gods are diminished and the drow race is free to choose; trading leaves the proof as a permanent destabilizer in whatever hands received it.
- **Mitigation path**: hold at Stage 3 until the party can commit to pairing (post-OotA arc) or has decided to destroy; do not let the proof resolve into Stage 4 under Heat pressure or accident. If the party wants to publish alone, let them — but make the genocide consequence real and visible, not softened.
- **Owner**: GM + player choice. See Optionality Rule in `post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`.

### Vhaerun Intervention
- **Holder**: Vhaerun cult custodians.
- **Controls**: entry into the post-OotA epic arc.
- **Why it matters**: shifts campaign from "resolve OOTA" to "contest Vhaerun's information war."
- **Trigger condition**: party chases Blink-authorship investigation past Stage 3 toward Vhaerun.
- **Earliest warning signs**: shadow-touched NPCs contact Daz; Vhaerun relics surface in unexpected places; Lolth-aligned NPCs warn the party off the trail.
- **Immediate consequences**: post-OotA arc activates; see `docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`.
- **Downstream consequences**: campaign extends past OOTA closure into level-20+ content.
- **Mitigation path**: close the investigation at Stage 3; accept the arc if player interest is real.
- **Owner**: GM + players. See Optionality Rule in the Vhaerun arc doc.
