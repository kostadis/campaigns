# Campaign Design Frame

Supplemental to `campaign_master_plan.md` (which is the prose bible). This file captures the design frame the prose omits: what this campaign is *not*, what we are betting on, and what remains open.

## Non-Goals

Explicitly off the table. If a session beat starts drifting into one of these, pull back.

- **Not a sandbox Underdark travelogue.** The published OOTA "wander until you find a biome" cadence is replaced with a political thriller centered on Menzoberranzan.
- **Not a morality lesson about "evil drow."** The Drow are radicalized by an Orthodoxy regime, not innately evil. Do not narrate individual drow as inherently wicked.
- **Not a Vizeran hero arc.** Vizeran is the villain. His plan kills a city and himself. The party may help, oppose, or redirect — but the frame is not "help Vizeran succeed" as a good outcome.
- **Not a rules-lawyering exception campaign.** Zalthir's optimization is canon play, not out-of-character meta. But rules arguments do not override pacing or canon constraints.
- **Not a "save the surface" campaign.** The party may or may not care about the surface; Vizeran lies that his plan saves them; the surface is not the stakes.
- **Not a redemption arc for the Orthodoxy.** The Orthodoxy does not reform on-screen. Post-campaign reconstruction can happen; active reform during play does not.

## Critical Assumptions

What we are betting on being true. If one of these turns out false, the campaign structure needs to shift.

- **Players will lean into the political thriller tone.** If they want dungeon-crawl Underdark, the Menzoberranzan arc lands flat.
- **Daz's player wants the amnesiac-heir reveal.** If the player rejects that structure when probed, `daz_heir.md` becomes an unused file and the DeVir-survival plot thread needs a new anchor.
- **Vizeran lives long enough to be a character.** Early Vizeran death is a viable branch (see `canon_guardrails.md`) but the current prep assumes he survives through Stage 3 of the Vizeran-disclosure ladder. If he dies in Stage 1 or Stage 2, reconstruct prep accordingly.
- **The party will accept morally compromised alliances.** Gauntlgrym faction assets come with objective locks and costs. If the party refuses all of them, the Menzoberranzan arc has to supply parallel resource paths.
- **Heat system creates pressure, not paralysis.** If the party freezes at Purge tier rather than adapting, soften tier transitions or add a Heat-reduction lever (an Orthodoxy distraction, a rival arrest).

## Resolved

Decisions captured elsewhere in canon. Logged here so we do not re-open them.

- **Masquerade scrutiny holder: Vaelissa T'sarran** (Matron Mother of House T'sarran). Ceremonial probe vector — the Invitation Gauntlet at formal House functions. Motive is personal, not institutional: revenge for the Velkynvelve kill-team loss plus curiosity about who paid House Duskryn to extract Daz alive. Uses an existing pursuit thread from the Fungal Cavern battle rather than introducing a new NPC. See `docs/risk_levers.md` "Social Masquerade Collapse" and `docs/npcs/vaelissa_t_sarran.md`.
- **Vizeran dies on-screen** at the moment the last caged demon falls; the seal resolves with his death. Off-screen death is structurally incompatible with the cage mechanic. See `campaign_master_plan.md` §VI "Termination" and `endgame_extension_rationale.md` Change 3.
- **Blink authorship canon: Lolth is the principal, escaping a prison Vhaerun put her in.** The imprisonment itself is open knowledge inside Lolth's cult — any Lolth-cultist NPC will speak of it plainly. The *jailer's identity* is the suppressed fact: if it leaked that Vhaerun jailed her, Lolth's cults across the multiverse would come for him, so he cannot publish the truth himself. The cross-world prison event is referenced canon, not a secret to be discovered on its own. Gromph Baenre cast the ritual; Orthodoxy leadership coordinated components, authorization, and cover; all three layers know the truth of the authorship, none know (or in Vhaerun's case, dare name) the jailer. The ritual had to route through abyssal rails, which is why the Orthodoxy's "powerful demon did this" cover story was a plausible misreading rather than a fabrication. Vizeran knows Gromph + Orthodoxy but not Lolth; no one below the divine tier knows the jailer. Exposure of the drow-authorship alone (any layer below the jailer) triggers an Underdark + surface war of genocidal extinction against the drow race, which is why the Orthodoxy's fanaticism is legible as strategic suppression + theological barricade, not baseline drow zealotry. See `decision_ladders.md` Blink-Event Authorship ladder and `risk_levers.md` Authorship Reveal.
- **Vhaerun's angle: spite plus self-protection.** Vhaerun is Lolth's exiled brother *and* the jailer she escaped. His win condition is (a) the Orthodoxy burns, (b) he gains recruits from the post-collapse wreckage, (c) his jailer role stays buried. Publishing the Blink proof himself would out him — so he uses cat's-paws (drow radicals, investigating adventurers) to trigger publication at arm's length. A genocidal war against the drow = Orthodoxy destroyed + Lolth's worshipper base erased + his role still hidden under the rubble. Vizeran is his unwitting instrument: Vhaerun did not recruit him; Lolth's own cruelty produced the weapon. See `docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`.
- **Post-OotA arc shape: two-proof discrediting via Myth Drannor heist.** The epic continuation exists as a specific four-act shape, not a generic "Vhaerun arc." Escape from falling Menzoberranzan with Vhaerun-revelation → overland to Myth Drannor → heist of a pre-schism elven temple for a pre-corruption artifact of Vhaerun → deliver to Eilistraee, whose ritual communes with Vhaerun's followers and causes mass defection; defected cultists + Menzoberranzan refugees then publish the Blink proof from a credible drow-victim position, defusing the genocide trigger. Both gods diminished; drow free. Arc remains optional per the Optionality Rule. See `docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`.
- **Post-OotA continuation is optional.** Party may close the campaign at OotA termination without engaging the discrediting arc; optionality is preserved. See `endgame_extension_rationale.md` Change 7.
- **Non-Gauntlgrym coalition assets: three factions, three distinct shapes.** Blingdenstone contributes via Senni Diggermattock (asset: Entemoch's Due — one-time Greater Earth Elemental summon inside Menzoberranzan; cost: formal sovereign-city recognition treaty before the finale). Sloobludop-survivors as an institution are gone (Ploopploopeen dead/missing, Sea Mother cult scattered) — the only extant fragment is **Shuushar's Reformists**, a pacifist kuo-toan movement that has quietly reached Menzoberranzan's kuo-toan slave population (asset: Deep Current Underground — shelter + silent messaging via slaves invisible to drow attention; pacifist constraint is absolute, asset burns on any kuo-toan kill). Zhentarim is a first-contact faction, reachable via **Davra Jassur at Gauntlgrym** (not Mantol-Derith, which is Fraz-Urb'luu-corrupted); asset is Black Network Procurement with source-material cost intact: 100% treasure cut + full intel monopoly that degrades Gauntlgrym relationship on activation. Full inventory in `docs/coalition_assets_underdark.md`.
- **All Appendix D demon lords are in play.** Zuggtmoy has had the most table presence (Neverlight Grove, Wedding Feast, Whorlstone lingering influence); Demogorgon has been the Darklake recurring menace. Any of Yeenoghu, Orcus, Graz'zt, Baphomet, Fraz-Urb'luu may also appear. See `endgame_extension_rationale.md` §3 "What's preserved."

## Decision Cadence

- **Per session**: guardrail integrity check, ladder stage log, Heat review.
- **Post-session**: fill `session_review_template.md` (wins / misses / surprises / process updates / next top 3).
- **Per arc transition**: revisit Non-Goals and Critical Assumptions. If an assumption has collapsed, decide now, not mid-scene.
