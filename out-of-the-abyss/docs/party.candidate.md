## Characters

### Zalthir
**Player:** Gabe | **Class/Level:** Shadow Monk 8 | **Species:** Dragonborn (Brass)

Zalthir is a product of a severe, silence-obsessed monastic order that prizes discretion, efficiency, and psychological control above all else. He speaks rarely and deliberately — a vow he maintains except under extreme duress — and processes the world through tactical geometry: who is the most dangerous target, what angle neutralizes them, what noise must be prevented. Beneath this exterior is a figure wrestling with questions of lineage (the Medusa Neheedra mocked the circumstances of his birth) and the moral cost of his methods, though he rarely externalizes either.

**Notable Relationships:**
- *Glabbagool*: Informally accepted a teacher/student relationship; confirmed that monitoring the spy counts as pre-monk training; deepest non-transactional bond in the party
- *Daz*: Rescued from the Gracklstugh rockfall; ongoing tactical partnership (the "Ember Grapple" combo); Daz covers socially for Zalthir when needed
- *Thorin*: Respect, with one unresolved slight (Thorin's "pure dragon" comment)
- *Brother Khell-Vire*: New scholarly contact; awarded Zalthir Option C after formal trial; relationship and depth of ongoing instruction unclear
- *House T'sarran spy*: Detention relationship; Zalthir chose to retain custody rather than surrender her to Candlekeep
- *Jimjar*: Mutually aware; Jimjar winked and vanished after Zalthir's inspection revealed his celestial nature
- *Asha Vandree*: Zalthir was treated as furniture throughout her negotiations with Daz; he registered the erasure and absorbed it without reacting

**Items of Significance:**
- Eldritch Claw Tattoo (attuned): tentacle strikes, 15-foot reach, used for grappling and aerial dragging
- Glowing crystal spectacles: see invisible creatures, see through magical darkness
- Drow spider-silk cloak (stored): kept in a bag to prevent sunlight destruction

### Thorin Giantfriend
**Player:** Joe Beda | **Class/Level:** Battle Master Fighter 8 | **Species:** Dwarf

He is increasingly aware that months in the Underdark have changed him: the optimistic giant-friend who wanted to "be my own faction" has become someone quietly entertaining Zhentarim Tactics and hoarding healing potions he never uses. ## Party Dynamics

**Tactical Doctrine:** The group has converged on a combined-arms approach centered on Zalthir pinning and binding targets ("Ember Grapple"), Daz applying sustained area damage (Cloud of Daggers, Fireball shaped via Sculpt Spells), Thorin holding melee, and Grygum providing healing and offensive divine support. The formation is effective but still has an unresolved ranged gap (Thorin is addressing this with javelins).

**Intra-party Tensions:**
- *Zalthir vs. "the noise":* Zalthir's monastic culture prizes silence and efficiency; the party's overlapping conversations and emotionally-driven decisions (Thorin committing to Rumpadump's rescue against Zalthir's judgment; Grygum impulsively shouting his faith at a Derro patrol) are a recurring source of friction he absorbs without expressing directly
- *Thorin's identity drift:* Thorin is privately wrestling with whether to formalize his Underdark-shaped self (Zhentarim Tactics under consideration); his instinct for bluntness ("We killed several members of the high council?") regularly creates security problems; the party is aware and has learned to anticipate it
- *Daz's growing load-bearing deceptions:* The Lolth-follower cover with Asha Vandree, the Ember Vanguard false reputation, the Menzoberranzan noble persona — Daz runs multiple simultaneous deceptions and the party has been folded into supporting them; Grygum and Zalthir have both noted discomfort at the edges without formally objecting
- *The "pure dragon" remark:* Thorin's offhand comment about wanting a "pure dragon" registered with Zalthir (who is a dragonborn carrying unresolved questions about his own lineage) and has not been resolved beyond a brief attempted apology

**Shared Goals:**
- Reach the surface and find help against the demon lord incursion (achieved — now shifting to surface-world engagement)
- Protect Glabbagool and integrate him into their operational model
- Understand what Jimjar was and why he shepherded them
- Identify Daz's anonymous patron before House T'sarran does

**Trust Baseline:** High. The party has survived Ilvara's obsessive pursuit, the Pudding King, two demon lords, and Buppido's betrayal together. They have each taken significant personal risk for the others without hesitation — Zalthir dug Daz out of a rockfall, Thorin verified Glabbagool's identity before striking, Grygum ran into the Neverlight Grove spore-cloud to watch over Thorin, Daz refused to stay hidden when his companions were in danger. The trust is earned and demonstrated, not assumed.

**The Spy Problem (immediate):** A miniaturized House T'sarran spy is in the bag of holding with Glabbagool. Candlekeep will eventually release her. The party has no plan for her disposition. This will need to be resolved before they leave Candlekeep.
