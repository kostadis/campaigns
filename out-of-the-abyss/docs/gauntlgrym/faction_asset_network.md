# Faction Asset Network (Army Replacement)

## Design Goal

Replace mass-combat logistics with high-impact social/tactical tools that create choices, commitments, and immediate consequences.

The party does not command an army.  
The party assembles a coalition of specialized assets.

## Core Rule

Each allied faction can provide one primary asset at a time.  
Assets are not troops; they are capabilities.

Payment model:
- Asset costs must matter before or during the Menzoberranzan arc.
- Do not rely on "pay us after resurfacing" obligations.
- Each asset requires either an upfront price, an in-city liability, or collateral at activation.

Asset categories:
- Intel (maps, schedules, identities, weak points)
- Access (forged credentials, keys, tunnel routes, legal cover)
- Pressure (public rumor operations, blackmail, market disruption)
- Protection (sanctuary, extraction, false trails, counter-scrying)
- Strike Support (single sabotage team, one-shot device, ritual aid)

## Table Mechanic (Simple)

Each asset has 3 states:
- Ready: full effect, low complication risk
- Strained: usable, but always adds a complication
- Burned: unavailable until repaired through a favor mission

When an asset is used, roll/choose exposure based on Heat:
- Ghost/Rumors: 1-in-6 chance asset becomes Strained
- Purge/Lockdown: 3-in-6 chance asset becomes Strained
- Extermination: asset automatically becomes Strained; second use burns it

## What Factions Actually Give

### Gauntlgrym / Dwarven Holds
- Asset: Deep Route Ledger
- Effect: bypass one dangerous transit segment or close pursuit route
- Cost profile: surrender route copies and one strategic objective lock (Thane selects one target you must prioritize before finale)

### Harpers (or local equivalent intelligence web)
- Asset: Whisper Chain
- Effect: reveal one true fact and one false rumor about any target district
- Cost profile: must extract or shield one named informant cell during the arc

### Zhentarim / Black Network style operators
- Asset: Shadow Procurement
- Effect: obtain one restricted item/service quickly and quietly
- Cost profile: upfront payment (coin/supply cut) plus delivery of one in-city package

### Emerald Enclave / druidic circles
- Asset: Spore Ward
- Effect: temporary protection against contamination, poison, or fungal zones
- Cost profile: designate one sanctum, grove, or refugee pocket the party must preserve

### Local Dissidents in Braeryn
- Asset: Crowd Drift
- Effect: create a civilian surge/distraction that changes patrol geometry
- Cost profile: collateral risk to noncombatants if mishandled

### Arcane Dissidents / anti-Orthodoxy mages
- Asset: Null Veil
- Effect: suppress one ward, glyph net, or scry lane for a short window
- Cost profile: arcane signature leaves trace at higher Heat

## Cost Templates (Use One Per Activation)

- Upfront Price: spend coin, supplies, spell components, or rare charges now.
- Collateral: a named contact enters danger while the asset is active.
- Objective Lock: accepting the asset commits the party to one in-city priority.
- Reputation Stake: failure while using the asset increases Heat or faction distrust immediately.

## Mission Structure (Per Operation)

For each operation, choose up to 2 active assets:
1) Entry asset (how you get in),
2) Pivot asset (how you survive the complication or exit).

Why this works:
- Keeps scenes social and strategic.
- Forces negotiation with factions before action.
- Produces consequences without running war-game turns.

## Consequence Engine (No Army, Still Escalation)

Instead of "army losses," track:
- Asset Attrition: how many coalition assets are Strained/Burned.
- Civic Fallout: raids, purges, shortages, or reprisals after each mission.
- Credibility: factions continue support only if party honors activation terms and protects contacts.

## Finale Conversion (Replacing Army March)

Old model: party leads a large force.  
New model: synchronized coalition actions trigger city-wide rupture.

At finale, each Ready asset contributes one "Collapse Clock" segment:
- 2 segments: partial success, severe survivor cost
- 4 segments: Orthodoxy command fractures
- 6 segments: controlled collapse window (best outcome available)

Strained assets can contribute only if the party accepts a linked collateral consequence.  
Burned assets contribute nothing.

## Fast DM Procedure (90 seconds)

1. State current Heat.
2. Offer 3 faction asks (what each faction wants now).
3. Let party activate up to 2 assets.
4. Run mission.
5. Apply exposure and fallout.
6. Update who still trusts the party.

## Guardrails

- Do not turn assets into faceless troop counters.
- Every asset use should create at least one named NPC consequence.
- Faction help is never free; always attach upfront price, in-city liability, or moral compromise.
- Keep the player decision as the center: who to owe, who to protect, who to burn.
