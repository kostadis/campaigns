# Gauntlgrym: Bruenor Audience Scene Script

## Setup Notes

- Party has surfaced after the Underdark arc.
- They are summoned to Gauntlgrym by King Bruenor Battlehammer.
- Faction representatives are present for the public audience.
- Bruenor knows: demon lords are loose in the Underdark.
- Bruenor does not know: the drow Orthodoxy caused the Blink event.
- The party does not know: the drow caused the Blink.
- Bruenor's private want: Menzoberranzan destroyed. He will not say this publicly.

---

## Scene 1: The Public Audience

### Room

The Vault of Kings throne room. Stone pillars. Iron braziers. Bruenor on the Great Throne of Moradin. Faction representatives seated at a long table to one side — present as witnesses, not equals. The party stands before the throne.

### Tone

Bruenor is direct, unimpressed by ceremony, and clearly running the room on his own terms. He has already heard enough fragmented reports to be alarmed. He does not perform alarm. He performs decision.

### Bruenor's Opening (read aloud or paraphrase)

> "You've been down there. Most people who go that deep don't come back with their minds intact, let alone their boots. So I'll skip the pleasantries and tell you what I know.
>
> Something is wrong in the deep. My scouts have stopped coming back from three routes. Merchants from Blingdenstone are describing things I'd have called madness two years ago. I've had reports of demon lords — plural — active and moving, not just loose but operating with what looks like intent.
>
> I need to know what's happening. Not a guess. Not a prayer. Facts I can use to make decisions about how to protect what's above.
>
> That's the job. Go back down. Find the source. Return with something I can work with."

### If Players Ask About the Drow

Bruenor's answer is measured and deliberate:

> "The drow are suffering along with everyone else down there, far as I can tell. That's interesting, but it doesn't change my concern. Demons don't stop at drow borders. Whatever is loose in the deep is going to become a surface problem eventually. My job is to make sure that eventually doesn't arrive before I'm ready."

He does not editorialize about the drow here. Not in this room.

### Faction Representatives (Brief)

Each faction rep says one line after Bruenor's ask. These are pitch openers, not full speeches:

- **Harpers representative:** "We have informants in Menzoberranzan who have gone silent. We will pay generously for confirmation of what happened to them."
- **Zhentarim representative:** "The Underdark trade routes are worth more intact than collapsed. We have resources to protect that interest."
- **Lords' Alliance delegate:** "Three surface settlements have reported Underdark incursion activity. We need to know if that is going to escalate."
- **Order of the Gauntlet:** "Demon lords are not a containment problem. They are an extermination problem. We are prepared to support anyone willing to treat them as such."
- **Emerald Enclave:** "The Faerzress is disturbed in ways we have never recorded. Something fundamental has shifted. We want to understand it before it reaches the surface ecology."

Bruenor lets them finish, then looks back at the party:

> "Those are the people who will back this. Each of them wants something specific in return. You'll hear their terms separately. Right now the only question I have is: will you go?"

### End of Public Scene

Party gives their answer (or asks questions, negotiates terms, etc.). Faction reps disperse to meet with the party individually — this is when the Gauntlgrym Asset Table activations happen.

Once the room empties, Bruenor does not immediately stand or dismiss the party.

## Scene 2: The Private Word

### Trigger

After the last faction rep leaves and the guards step back, Bruenor remains seated. He pours two cups from a flask at his side. He does not offer one. He just sets it on the arm of the throne where someone could take it if they wanted.

### Tone

No theater. No diplomacy. One king talking to people he has decided to trust with something he cannot say in front of witnesses.

### Bruenor's Private Ask (read aloud or paraphrase)

> "Now I'll tell you the part I don't say in front of faction representatives.
>
> The drow city has raided my people for three hundred years. Enslaved traders. Collapsed tunnels. Assassinated every envoy I've tried to send through that region. Menzoberranzan is not a city that suffers from bad governance. It is a city built on the assumption that everyone beneath it exists to be used.
>
> If the demon crisis ends that city — if whatever is happening in the deep produces a permanent change in who controls that geography — I will rebuild every trade route in the region myself and call it Moradin's will.
>
> I am not telling you to make that happen. I would not say that to anyone. But I am telling you that if it happens, I will not be asking hard questions about how. And I will make sure the people in that room don't ask them either."

He picks up his cup. Drinks.

> "The demons are the problem I'm paying you to address. What you find down there, and what you do with what you find — that's your business. I just want you to know what kind of outcome I can work with."

### Optional: The Private Asset

If the party has read the room correctly and responds to Bruenor's honesty with honesty of their own, he produces one additional asset not on the public table:

**Iron Mandate Letter** — A sealed document bearing Bruenor's personal sigil. It authorizes the bearer to operate in Bruenor's name for purposes of Underdark intelligence gathering. In the right hands, in the right room, it converts a hostile faction into a neutral one — once.

He does not explain this gift. He just places it on the throne arm next to the cup.

> "In case you need a door opened that doesn't open for strangers."

## Scene 3: Bruenor's Posture — If Information Surfaces Later

### This is not a Gauntlgrym scene. It is a standing rule for how Bruenor responds if the party contacts him mid-arc with significant intelligence.

Bruenor said he would "work with" a certain outcome. He does not know the full picture. The party does not know the full picture. Both of those things are true when they leave Gauntlgrym.

If the party ever surfaces information that changes the political calculus — information that gives Bruenor public justification rather than private willingness — his posture shifts accordingly. He moves from quiet ally to active patron. What that looks like depends entirely on what the party discovers and chooses to share.

### DM Options If Party Sends Word to Bruenor During Arc

- New intelligence received: Bruenor responds with asset reinforcement (one Burned asset restored to Strained, or one new asset made available).
- Party keeps information to themselves: Bruenor's support stays at current level. No consequence, no reward.
- Party uses information as leverage with a different faction instead: valid. Bruenor is not informed. He remains a quiet ally.

Each option is valid. The DM does not push any of them.

### Hard Rule

Do not name, hint at, or foreshadow what that information might be. Bruenor's posture note exists so you are ready to respond correctly when the party acts — not to signal that something is coming.

---

## DM Guardrails

- Bruenor is pragmatic, not villainous. He does not want civilian massacres. He wants strategic change.
- Do not have him endorse Vizeran's plan directly. He does not know it exists.
- The private scene only happens if the room is actually empty. Do not run it with witnesses present.
- The Iron Mandate Letter is discretionary. Only offer it if the party engages honestly with Bruenor's private disclosure.
- Bruenor's standing posture rule requires no DM engineering. It activates only if the party chooses to use it.
