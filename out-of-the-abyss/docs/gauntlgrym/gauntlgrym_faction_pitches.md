# Gauntlgrym: Faction Pitch Scenes

## How to Run This

After the public audience disperses, faction contacts approach the party individually over the course of a few hours. Run these in any order. Each pitch is one short scene: one NPC, one ask, one offer, one cost. Do not run all of them back to back — let the party breathe and move between locations.

Target: 3-4 pitches per session, rest handled by brief summary or player-initiated contact.

---

## Pitch 1: Thane Barundin Embermantle
### Asset Offered: Deep Route Ledger

**Location:** A war room off the Vault of Kings. Stone table covered with Underdark survey maps, most of them visibly outdated. Barundin is standing, not seated. He does not offer chairs.

**His read on the party:** Skeptical of anyone who survived the Underdark without losing their mind. Interested in whether that composure is real or performance.

**Opening:**
> "You came back. Most don't. Some of the ones who do come back wrong. I haven't decided which category you're in yet."

He taps one of the maps — a route corridor toward the deep Underdark.

> "My scouts use dead-drop waypoints through three tunnel systems below this hold. Nobody outside this room knows those routes exist. If you're going back down, I can give you access. That means you can move fast and quiet in sectors where fast and quiet is the difference between a mission and a burial."

**His ask (the Objective Lock):**
> "In return, before you do whatever it is you're actually going down there to do — and I don't need to know what that is — you will complete one objective I name. One location. One outcome. My priority, not yours. When I tell you what it is, you will do it."

He looks at the party steadily.

> "I am not asking you to like the arrangement. I am asking you whether you are the kind of people who keep their word when it costs them something."

**If pressed on what the objective will be:**
> "I don't know yet. That's honest. When I know what I need from down there, you'll know. That's the deal — not a specific favor, but a commitment to one priority before your finale. If you can't give me that, you don't get the routes."

**Negotiation note:** Barundin will not budge on the Objective Lock structure. He will accept a time limit (the lock expires if he hasn't named the objective before the party reaches the finale). He will not accept veto rights on the objective itself.

**Closing (if they accept):**
> "Good."

He rolls up one of the maps and hands it over without ceremony.

> "My men will brief you on the waypoints tonight. Don't write anything down."

## Pitch 2: Sister Caldra Scale-Vigil
### Asset Offered: Oathsafe Sanctuary + Seventh Move Counsel

**Location:** The Iron Tabernacle, in a small restored side-shrine to Bahamut. Candlelight. Clean stone. A carved platinum dragon no larger than a hand sits on the altar.

**Her read on the party:** She has already noticed Grygum. The rest of the party are context. Grygum is the question she is here to ask.

**Opening (to the group, but watching Grygum):**
> "Every age produces a moment where the wrong move, made by the wrong person, at the wrong time, changes nothing. And occasionally the right move, made by the right person — who should by all accounts have no business being in that room — changes everything. Bahamut does not explain himself. He just places people."

She lets that sit.

> "I can offer you sanctuary. A sealed location inside the city, warded against casual surveillance, safe for rest and planning. No cost in blood. The cost is a promise: when you have the opportunity to spare or remove civilians from the path of what you are about to do, you take it. Not every time. Not at mission cost. But when the choice is available, you make it."

**On Grygum specifically (if he engages):**
She turns to him directly.

> "I know who you were before you were this. I know what name is attached to the legend people are already whispering in this hall. I am not here to place that weight on you. I am here to ask one question, and you do not have to answer it now."

She pauses.

> "Stroud moved a piece he barely understood. Bahamut — or chance, or something else — let it land correctly. You have been down in the dark longer than Stroud was ever in that room. What do you think moved your hand?"

**The Seventh Move Quest (optional, offered at end of scene):**
> "There is something in this hold I would ask you to retrieve. Not for the mission. For clarity. A relic from the game itself — or what we believe to be from it. A tile. It is in a shrine below the forge level that has become unstable. I would not send just anyone. But I think you will understand what you find when you get there."

**Negotiation note:** The sanctuary is unconditional once the noncombatant promise is made. Caldra does not negotiate the promise itself — she only clarifies that she is not asking for perfection, only for visible effort. The Seventh Move quest is entirely optional.

**Closing:**
> "The location will be marked on a tile — obsidian, carved with a scale — left in your quarters tonight. Memorize it. Don't carry it."

## Pitch 3: Archivist Kheldrim Nine-Rooks
### Asset Offered: Counter-Doctrine Brief

**Location:** A cluttered archive room in the Vault of Kings. Books stacked in towers that should not be stable. A chess board on a side table, mid-game. Kheldrim is seated, already reviewing something, and does not look up immediately.

**His read on the party:** Curious about what they actually know versus what they think they know. Mildly contemptuous of legend. Genuinely interested in primary sources.

**Opening (still looking at his papers):**
> "You were in Menzoberranzan. Or near it. Close enough to have seen how it operates from the inside."

He looks up.

> "That is worth more than anything my network has produced in two years. Faction doctrine, internal command structure, the actual relationship between the noble houses and the Orthodoxy rather than the public-facing version — I want to know what you observed."

**His offer:**
> "In exchange, before any significant social encounter you have down there, I will give you a brief. Two questions, answered as accurately as my sources allow. One answer will be true. One will be partially true — I will tell you which is which, but I cannot always guarantee the partial truth won't mislead you. That is the nature of Underdark intelligence."

**His ask (the Archive Witness):**
> "I want one of my people with you for one operation. A scholar. Non-combatant. She observes, records, and returns. You keep her alive. That is the entire requirement."

He picks up a piece from the chess board. Studies it.

> "I am also going to tell you something I suspect you have already guessed: the Stroud game is not what the legend says it is. The move Stroud made was not the move that won the game. There was a second move — one Protanther made, immediately after — that determined the outcome. Nobody discusses it because it complicates the story. I bring this up because you are about to walk into a city where everyone is telling a version of events that serves them. I would like you to be the kind of people who notice when the second move is missing."

**Negotiation note:** Kheldrim will accept limits on which operation the witness attends (not the finale, not a direct combat mission). He will not remove the witness requirement. He is genuinely indifferent to whether the party likes him.

**Closing:**
> "Tell me three things you observed about how the Orthodoxy actually communicates internally. Not what they say publicly. What you saw. That is the first payment. The brief comes after."

## Pitch 4: Commander Aldric Stoneheel (Ember Vanguard)
### Asset Offered: Breach Team

**Location:** The training yard off the Vault of Kings. Two veterans are sparring. Stoneheel watches them without intervening.

**His read on the party:** He does not care about legend, theology, or politics. He cares about whether the people he is about to send his soldiers in alongside will get those soldiers killed unnecessarily.

**Opening:**
> "My people have been down in the deep. Not the same deep you came from — we were running a different problem. Three of them didn't come back. I want to know if the people I send next are going in with a team that makes good decisions under pressure, or one that makes impressive decisions that get support elements killed."

**His offer:**
> "I can attach a four-person breach unit to one operation per mission cycle. They open doors, clear chokepoints, and extract under fire. They are not your bodyguards. They are a tool. When you need a door open without a full engagement, they open it. When you need to move through a position that would cost you a long fight, they buy you the window."

**His ask:**
> "They take the exposure. If the operation goes loud, they are the ones who get seen. That is the deal. I am not asking you to protect them — they are professionals. I am asking you to make the call correctly so that their exposure is the calculated kind, not the avoidable kind."

He finally looks directly at the party.

> "If one of my people gets burned because you made a bad call that you knew was bad and made it anyway, I pull the asset and I tell Barundin why. We clear?"

**Negotiation note:** Non-negotiable on the exposure clause. Will accept pre-mission briefings where the party explains their plan before he commits the team.

## Pitch 5: Guildmaster Torvin Ashpick (Forgewright Syndicate)
### Asset Offered: Black-Forge Kit

**Location:** The forge district, near a working furnace. Torvin is not a romantic about his craft — he runs a business.

**His opening:**
> "You need something that goes boom, cuts through a warded door, or produces a specific chemical effect at a specific time. I make those things. I don't ask what they're for. You don't tell me. That's the arrangement."

**His offer and cost — direct:**
> "Per kit: you pay for materials upfront. Gold, rare components, or one magic item we can strip for parts. No credit. No deferred payment. You come back with whatever you don't use and I refund the material cost minus labor. You don't come back and I keep everything — which is fine by me either way."

He shrugs.

> "I'm not in this for the cause. I'm in it because Barundin told me to be available to you and Barundin signs my operating charter. So. What do you need?"

**Negotiation note:** Torvin is the least dramatically interesting pitch — run it fast. His value is the asset, not the scene. If players engage him further, he will admit (off the record) that he has supplied both sides of conflicts before and considers that a point of professional pride.

## Pitch 6: Harpers Contact — "Vellanthra" (no surname given)
### Asset Offered: Whisper Chain (intel network)

**Location:** She finds them. The party will notice her before she speaks — she has been in the room for a while already.

**Her opening:**
> "We had twelve informants in Menzoberranzan. Past tense. We have three confirmed alive, two confirmed dead, and seven unaccounted for. Before you go back in, I want to know if you encountered any of them. I have descriptions."

She produces a folded sheet with seven physical descriptions — no names, no roles.

**Her offer:**
> "In return, I give you access to what our remaining network knows. Before any district operation, you can query the chain: two questions, one confirmed answer, one probable answer. The network is strained — do not ask us to expose sources by asking for names. Ask about patterns, schedules, and access points."

**Her ask:**
> "If you encounter any of the seven unaccounted for, you get word to us. Alive or dead, we want to know. And if any of them ask to be extracted — regardless of what they've done to survive down there — you bring them out if you can."

She folds the sheet and holds it out.

> "We are not asking you to like what some of them may have become. We are asking you to bring them home."

**Negotiation note:** The Whisper Chain overlaps with the Counter-Doctrine Brief (Kheldrim). If party takes both, they have strong intel but Vellanthra and Kheldrim are aware of each other and there is mild professional tension — each believes their source quality is superior. This can produce contradictory intel in one session, which is realistic and interesting.

## DM Notes: Running the Pitches

**Pacing:** Run 3-4 pitches in one session. Let the others happen off-screen or at player request.

**Order that creates best drama:**
1. Caldra (sets theological tone, activates Grygum story)
2. Barundin (grounds it in pragmatism, contrast with Caldra)
3. Kheldrim (destabilizes the Stroud legend mid-session)
4. Any of the remaining four based on player interest

**What to watch for:**
- If players take both Whisper Chain and Counter-Doctrine Brief, plan one session with contradictory intel
- If players skip Caldra's sanctuary, they have no guaranteed safe rest location in-city — that is a real consequence
- If Grygum does not engage with Caldra's question in this session, leave it open. She will ask again.

**The question that should be unanswered when they leave Gauntlgrym:**
Kheldrim's observation about the second move. He is talking about the Stroud game. That is all he is talking about. Do not frame it as foreshadowing in play, in notes, or in NPC dialogue. It is a general caution about inflated legends and incomplete histories. If it rhymes with something the party discovers later, that resonance belongs to them — not to a clue you planted.
