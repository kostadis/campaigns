# Grygum as Stroud's Echo - Scene Packet (Gauntlgrym)

## Core Premise

The same player once changed history as Stroud by defeating Protanther at chess.  
In-world, people now treat Grygum as a living echo of that improbable "right move at the right time."

Use this packet to create pressure, admiration, and political leverage without removing player agency.

## 3 NPCs for the Scene

### 1) Thane Barundin Embermantle (Dwarf War Elder)

- Role: Pragmatic loyalist who respects results more than prophecy.
- Read: Wants Grygum to prove that Stroud was not a one-off miracle.
- Tone: Dry, direct, honor-bound.

Opening line:
"I have no use for stories. I have use for outcomes. Show me yours."

### 2) Sister Caldra Scale-Vigil (Bahamut Devotional Keeper)

- Role: Keeper of tablets documenting the Stroud-Protanther game.
- Read: Sees Grygum as a theological test: divine will or mortal agency.
- Tone: Warm but intense, quietly evaluating.

Opening line:
"Some call you coincidence repeated. I call you a question Bahamut asks again."

### 3) Archivist Kheldrim Nine-Rooks (Chess Historian and Skeptic)

- Role: Court scholar who studies the original game line-by-line.
- Read: Thinks the legend is inflated and wants to puncture myth.
- Tone: Polite, sharp, provoking.

Opening line:
"If you are Stroud's echo, then show us a move no careful player would dare."

## 6 Dialogue Lines (Drop-in Ready)

1. "The hand that moved dragons now raises a holy symbol. Convincing, if true."
2. "Was Stroud luck once, or are you proof that courage can be taught?"
3. "Bahamut chose a dwarf before. Perhaps now he chooses an orc."
4. "Do not give us a sermon, Grygum. Give us a decision."
5. "Legends begin as accidents. Civilizations survive by repetition."
6. "If you fail, they will say Stroud was chance. If you succeed, they will call it doctrine."

## Monastery/Temple Favor Quest: The Seventh Move

### Hook

Sister Caldra asks Grygum to retrieve a cracked obsidian tile known as the Seventh Move, a relic said to be from the board used in the Stroud-Protanther game.

### Objective

Recover the tile from a sealed basalt shrine beneath an old forge chapel now unstable due to demon-tainted tremors.

### Complication

At the shrine door, a triune choice must be made:

- Preserve the relic (safe, slow, risks losing tactical timing elsewhere),
- Break the relic to open a faster escape route (pragmatic, sacrilegious),
- Leave it and carry only the etched move pattern in memory (symbolically powerful, no artifact proof).

### Reward

Political and spiritual credibility in Gauntlgrym.  
Barundin and Caldra each support one major request from the party in a later negotiation.

## One Mechanic: Stroud Echo (1/session)

When Grygum makes a declared high-stakes strategic call that materially affects allies (positioning, retreat timing, focus fire call, negotiation gambit), trigger Stroud Echo:

- Gain: Advantage on one related roll for Grygum or one ally who follows the call.
- Cost: Mark 1 Echo Pressure.

At 2 Echo Pressure before a long rest, the table chooses one consequence:

- Rumor Surge: "The echo is fading" skepticism spreads; first social check in Gauntlgrym is at disadvantage.
- Doctrine Split: clergy argue fate vs agency; one ally faction delays support until proof.
- Burden of Myth: Grygum must publicly justify his last call before aid is granted.

Reset Echo Pressure to 0 on long rest.

## DM Use in 90 Seconds

1. Introduce Barundin (results), Caldra (faith), Kheldrim (skepticism).
2. Drop one line from each NPC in any order.
3. Ask Grygum one direct question: "What makes your move yours?"
4. Offer the Seventh Move quest.
5. Trigger Stroud Echo the first time Grygum makes a bold call.

## Guardrails

- Do not imply Grygum is secretly Stroud reincarnated unless the player invites it.
- Keep the theme as social pressure and legacy, not destiny override.
- Reward commitment to a choice even when outcomes are messy.
- Never force "correct" theology; let fate vs agency remain contested.
