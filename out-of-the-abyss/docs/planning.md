## DM Notes

**Jimjar convergence:** Jimjar's reveal is the session's most unresolved mystery. He chose to show himself to Zalthir, which means he wanted to be seen. His divine-tier immunities, platinum coins, and deliberate vanishing suggest he is either a disguised celestial, a demigod, a trickster entity, or something else entirely. His navigational "errors" that led the party into a flooding cave system should be revisited — were those accidents, or tests? His return is confirmed. Plant faint traces: a platinum coin appearing in an odd place, a wink from a NPC that echoes his manner, Zalthir's player noticing something familiar in an unrelated encounter.

**Shal and Juiblex convergence:** Shal's unidentified nature and the Deepking's paranoid purge are structurally identical to demonic influence patterns seen with Zuggtmoy and Juiblex. The party holds evidence (assassination list) suggesting the Deepking is compromised. If Shal is a demonic agent or thrall, Gracklstugh is the third front in the demon lord incursion — which makes the party's assassination list document geopolitically critical, not just locally significant.

**Zuggtmoy / Juiblex collision:** Both demon lords are now independently advancing on the same territory (Neverlight Grove / myconid network). The party's ally Blingdenstone is caught between them — Zuggtmoy already infiltrating from Neverlight, Juiblex expelled from Blingdenstone but declaring intent to consume Zuggtmoy's domain. The pale puffball mushroom growing from the Blingdenstone tunnel wall as the party departed is a foreshadowing beat — use it again, larger, if the party ever returns.

**Basidia and Hgraam as sleeping assets:** Both are withdrawn and locationless but both are allied or sympathetic to the party. Basidia needs a base and a plan to save Phylo; Hgraam is in isolation but holds deep knowledge of Gracklstugh's true state. Either could reach out to the party or be found — both represent significant potential allies if the party chooses to pursue either the Zuggtmoy arc or the Gracklstugh arc.

**The assassination list as a key:** The party holds a document that: names Gartokkar (their transactional ally) as a marked man; names Errde Blackskull (who has leverage over them); implicates the Deepking in a paranoid purge; and annotates Lingrick Xardelvar as "MY MOST GASEOUS ALLY!!!!!!" — which is a thread entirely unexplored. The list is leverage, evidence, and a map of Gracklstugh's political fault lines simultaneously. Remind the players it exists.

**Party notoriety as a mechanical pressure:** Legends of the Ember Vanguard are spreading through Underdark tunnels with exaggerated accounts. This makes them easier to find — by Ilvara, by Uskvil, by Deepking agents, and by anyone seeking powerful allies. Their fame is both a resource and a liability, and the two effects will arrive simultaneously.

**Topsy / Turvy:** Missing since Ghohlbrorn's Lair. No resolution recorded. Their fate is an open wound — they were recurring NPCs and their disappearance has never been addressed. If the party ever has reason to return toward Gracklstugh, a trail or rumor regarding the twins could be planted.
