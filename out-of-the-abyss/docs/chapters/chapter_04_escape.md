# Chapter 04 Escape 
## 4th day of the 2nd Tenday of Taraskh 1493


The body of the dead drow litter the floor. The large corpse of the deluded Prince Derendil stands as a silent monument to the price of the victory. 


Zalthir shows no sentimentality and quickly scours the bodies for any weapons. The rest of the former prisoners do as well. 


Sarith arms himself with studded leather armor, picks up a shield, and places one of the hand crossbows in a strap on the armor. Equipped with the Drow weapons, he looks every part of the warrior caste of the Cult of Lolth. 


Grygum finds some poisoned hand crossbow bolts and a vial of what Serith explains is Drow poison. He hooks them into a loop on his armor. 


Daz stares mournfully at the armor and mutters, “I will not be wearing armor.” 


Thorin opens the door to the guard room, peaks outside, examines the area past the rope bridge where Ilvara’s residence is, and mutters, “We need to get our stuff.” 


The prisoners debate the relative merits of securing their stuff or escaping. Should they escape and risk not having their weapons and gear and face the elements while they travel the Underdark, or should they risk their lives and freedom now? 


Daz feels a thunderous drone, a discordant, raw hum that sets his teeth chattering.  He pokes his head and looks and sees four black, bloated flies. He can see these massive and terrible things, larger than a pony. Barbed legs dangle from their bulbous forms while their elephantine proboscis probes are hungrily forward.


For a moment, he worries that they are looking at him with their multifaceted eyes and breathes a sigh of relief. They are fixated on two other creatures. He wonders what they are, and Just then, a blood-curdling screech pierces the air. He looks at the source and sees a creature with stretched-out feathered wings attached to a back covered in fur, calling out with its jagged beak


Daz knows these monsters and is surprised. So many demons in one place is very odd. He turns to his fellow prisoners, “Demons: four chasme and two vrock.” 


Topsy and Turvy look around and shout, “We could get your stuff  for you for a price.” 


Zalthir stares, “How?” 


Thorin looks at his Drow weapons and decides that leaving everything behind is good. A good, fresh start. He shouts, “Nah, let’s take advantage of the distraction.”


Grygum agrees. He remembers when some Giants’ prisoners tried to steal goods from them instead of escaping, and the prisoners made a good meal. 
![image]()
Topsy and Turvy sigh and then transform into rats. 


Zalthir grabs some silk ropes on the pegs and prepares to drop down into the spidery web below. Buppido clicks his tongue and points out that there are spiders—very large spiders. 


He sighs, “So do we run, or do we first burn the place down?” 


Sarith, who seems to be overcome with bouts of madness, mutters, “The faster we get out of here, the better. Ilvara will be honor bound to pursue us. And let’s not give her more motivation. But -,” and he cackles, “revenge is so tasty.” 


With that, the prisoners decided that escaping now was the best option. So they rushed from the tower to the waterfall and slid down, landing in the water. 


Although aware of a gray ooze, the creature is invisible until it strikes. And strikes it does, landing a pseudopod with a crushing blow on Thorin. 


Above, the demons are fighting amongst themselves and the Drow. On the cavern floor, Grygum takes stock of the options. To the north is Menzoberranzan, a city full of Drow Cultists. Going south to Blingdenston would require going through the pitched battle between Drow and Demons. He sees Topsy and Turvy transform back into gnomes and head down the passage that leads to the Darklake. 


Grygum silently shares his thoughts with his friends and the other prisoners. They all agree, disengage from the ooze, and run towards the tunnel to the Darklake. 


Even Thorin, tempted to smash the creature with his sword, decides it’s not worth it and rushes off. 


The grey ooze thrashes in the water behind them, extending pseudo pods into the air, and then settles down. 


As Daz crosses the cavern floor to the tunnel, an injured vrock lands on the ground. From this close, Daz notices its abnormally long arms, dense with lean muscle, that end in raptor-like claws, scraping the ground. The creature extends its wings, feathered wings out and then back. 


Although excited about the opportunity to study a vrock up close, Daz is more interested in escaping with his life and flees before the creature can strike him down. 


Every other prisoner does the same, except for Ront. Ront is still angry at his cowardice. He still feels that he must prove something. And killing a vrock would redeem him. His first blow cuts through the creature's hide and the gangrenous blood-like substance that oozes out gives Ront hope that he has killed the monster. But he hasn’t. The demon, with startling speed, kills him with one taloned hand, then grabs the lifeless body with the other while it’s beak envelops the head and snaps it off. 


Daz watches as the creature swallows the head and then flies off. A few moments later, as he makes it to the safety of the cave, he hears the thud of the now headless body land on the ground behind him. 


The escapees are now feeling safe in the tunnels of the Underdark. 


Although Jimjar disrupts their feelings of safety, “Anyone wants to make a bet about how many of us will die before any of us escape?” 


Buppido laughs, “The divine plan says I will make it Gracklstugh.” 


Jimjar stares at him, “Put your money where your mouth is?” 


“Yes.” And a wager is made. 


Daz turns to the group, “Anyone know where we are?” 


Sathir perks up, “Yes. I do. Before I was unfairly and unjustly imprisoned, I was a member of the warrior caste, and I have explored this area quite thoroughly.”


They all turn to him. 


“We need supplies. If our destination is Gracklstugh, the fastest way to get there is to go through the Darklake.” 


The overbrighters stare at him, confused about the word “Darklake.” 


He sighs and explains, “The Darklake is a network of underground rivers, natural tunnels, and canals that connect innumerable water-filled caverns and chambers. This vast waterway stretches over a hundred miles across, with ceilings that are miles high in some places and depths that are unfathomable.


Long ago, duergar engineers extended and widened many of the interconnecting passages of the Darklake. They also constructed locks for raising and lowering watercraft to different levels within it, opening up large portions of the network for travel. Many Underdark creatures are experienced in navigating the Darklake, including the kuo-toa.” 


“We can get to Gracklstugh, Blingdenstone, or wherever we want from the Darklake. If what Shuushar says is true, the town of Sloopdopblop is just eight days away, and we can find a navigator and a boat.” 


There is a moment of silence, and then they all agree. 
## 5th day of the 2nd Tenday of Taraskh 1493


The group begins the long march through the darkness after the long rest. The tunnels alternate between pitch-pure darkness or lit with the surreal magic of the *Faerzess (*Fay-YERR-zess).


As Grygum marched, he took note of his new friend. When he woke up, Stool, whom they had met as a fellow prisoner in Velkenyvelve, was curled against him. When the orc tried to push him away, the myconid cried. 


Stool explained that he was lonely, scared, and terrified. He remembered Grygum's inspirational speech yesterday and knew Grygum would bring him home. He knew he wanted to be Grygum’s best friend. 


Grygum stared at the creature. Although its behavior was different, he could tell when a child was looking for an adult figure to protect it. He sighed and told the creature that they could be friends. When stool extended his hand, Grygum took it. And so they marched. 


But it wasn’t only altruism. Having a myconid would allow the group to surprise enemies using the telepathic powers Stool imbued his allies with. 


They moved at a normal pace, trying to find food and water. 


“Well, look at this,” shouts Jimjar. 


The group stops and stares at the fungus he is pointing at. Thorin thinks it looks like a giant’s tongue. 


“Is it edible,” Thorin asks.


“Tastiest thing in the world,” says Sarith. 


Thorin is distrustful and asks Shuushar, who answers, “It is said that when you eat this mushroom, you connect with the gods.” 


Thorin is dubious but chooses to eat the mushroom. He gets the vague impression that it has a psychedelic effect, but he doesn’t feel any different afterward. 


“You know, that Myconid thing probably tastes delicious. And Shuushar, you really  have big eyes.” 


Everyone stares at him, and then the prisoners from the Underdark start to laugh. Buppido asks Thorin, “So tell us the truth. Is it true what they say about Giants?” 


And Thorin attempts to lie but tells the truth. 


And then everyone laughs even harder. 


When the laughter subsides, Shuushar explains, “*The Tongue of Madness* will compel its eater to say the truth.” 


Seeing that answering a question is the only danger, and Thorin is not compelled to respond, the rest eat the mushroom and sit silently. They are all unwilling to reveal anything. 


Zathir stares at the group, ats the mushroom, and declares a vow of silence. 


Buppido looks at him, funny. 


Once they have eaten, the group continues their journey into the dark. They arrive at a large cavern, and as they stretch their arms, a tremor can be felt. And then, before anyone can react, rocks fall. 


Once the dust has settled, Zalthir looks around. Topsy, Buppido, Jimjar, Eldeth, Grygum, and Shuushar are trapped under the rocks. Grygum shouts that he’s fine, but Daz is silent.  


Zalthir examines the mage and concludes that he’s unconscious. And will die if he doesn’t get him out in time.
