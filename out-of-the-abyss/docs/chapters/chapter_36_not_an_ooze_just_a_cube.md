# Chapter 32 Not an Ooze, Just a Cube


The party arrived at a gate deep within the caverns of Blingdenstone, where they were met by a group of huffing and puffing deep gnome guards who had been chasing after them. One of the guards, speaking in a squeaky voice, questioned them about their escape from Velkynvelve and the Drow. He explained that a sergeant had failed to thoroughly read a critical memo stating that all Drow escapees must be escorted immediately to Chief Dorbo Diggermattock of Clan Diggermattock. The guard clarified that Clan Diggermattock had taken this territory from the Drow, and any intelligence about their long-time enemies was of vital importance for preparing their defenses.


The party initially felt suspicious about being ordered around, but one of them used their insight to determine that the request was genuine and stemmed from the deep gnomes' sincere desire to gather information about the Drow. Before agreeing to follow the guards, the party consulted their companion Jimjar, who offered a bet of five empty spell gems. He wagered that meeting with the gnomes was the right thing to do, but hinted that the party would have to deal with the oozes plaguing the community. Glabbagool, the party's friendly gelatinous cube companion, interjected to clarify that he was a cube, not an ooze, and that there were indeed many oozes causing problems for the gnomes. The party accepted Jimjar's bet, understanding that they would likely be sent on a quest to address the ooze infestation.


The guards escorted the party through various parts of Blingdenstone, including a reception hall with a cart and the bustling Trader's Grotto. As they traveled, Glabbagool's presence caused some nervousness among the non-adventuring deep gnomes, who were unaccustomed to seeing a friendly sentient gelatinous cube. The party was eventually led to Diggermattock Hall, a well-lit cavern occupied by two dozen Svirfneblin engaged in various administrative tasks. Some moved briskly with messages while others huddled around stone tables covered in maps and papers.


At the back of the hall, Chief Dorbo Diggermattock and his assistant Quartermaster Senni Diggermattock sat behind stone desks on a raised dais. They turned to greet the party and asked for their identity. The party briefly considered using the name "Emerald Vanguard," but quickly realized this name was associated with a group blamed for destroying a city. Instead, they identified themselves simply as adventurers trying to find their way back home and seeking to reach the Overbright. Dorbo and Quartermaster Senni Diggermattock engaged in a heated, muttering discussion with visible finger-pointing and argument.


When a party member asked if they had a side quest involving oozes, Quartermaster Senni Diggermattock confirmed their suspicions. She explained that while they would normally be delighted to help the party reach the Overbright, their community was suffering from an ooze problem. Since the party had "graciously volunteered," the gnomes expected them to address the infestation before receiving any assistance. Glabbagool once again emphasized that he was a gelatinous cube, a totally different species from an ooze, highlighting his higher viscosity and ability to maintain a square shape. He expressed confusion about why his "fat friends" were causing the gnomes pain.


The conversation turned to shambling mounds when a party member mentioned them, prompting Glabbagool to ask for an explanation since he had never encountered one. After the party described these creatures, Glabbagool became very excited and asked if they contained treasure when they exploded. The party explained that shambling mounds did not retain things like gelatinous cubes do. Glabbagool revealed he had experience with oozes, noting they were "very not talkative," and demonstrated his abilities by extending a pseudo-pod, explaining that he could both absorb things and punch them.
