# Chapter 15 Greengrass Day
### Thorin


Somehow, Thorin remembers that today is Greengrass. 


The festival of Greengrass was a tradition of the farming communities near the giants he lived with. 


The wealthy farmers would bring flowers to the poorer farmers in a peculiar spring festival to encourage the gods to usher in the summer.


The giants always found the festival peculiar. It was a strange obsession of the little people. 


But after the end of the War of the Giants, King Hekaton had decreed peace with the little people, and so peace it was. 


His reverie is interrupted when Zalthir shouts - “rocks ahead.” 


He looks a bit further and sees a boat. A boat that is abandoned.


Daz mutters, “We could use the oars!” 


Thorin agrees and is about to join the war when Zalthir stops him. “Isn’t it a bit ominous?” 


Thorin smiles; ominous sounds fun.


Daz shouts, “You have a point. They probably fell into the water.” 


### Zalthir


The dragonborn sighs. He wonders how Thorin managed to stay alive all of these years. In fact he’s beginning to wonder if he will survive next to Thorin, Daz, and Grygum. 


“We could check to see if there is any blood or gore on the boat?” 


He then looks at the castaways they collected and asks, “Do any of you recognize the boat?” 


The duergar looks at it and says, “Yes, it’s  Duergar Keelboat. You can see the crates.” 
Zalthir is about to say something when Thorin suddenly wises up, “Are they big enough to hold people? I am worried about an ambush.” 


Hemath laughs, “Yes, that would be a good thing to worry about. The Duergar can become invisible for a whole hour.” And he turns invisible. “I will be hiding here.” 


Then Thorin, who, to Zalthir’s pleasant surprise, is becoming a lot more paranoid, asks, “Can’t we do something to detect them, like splash water?” 


Zalthir groans inwardly because Daz will be excited.


“I have shape water!” 


And a few moments later, water is sprayed around the boat, and the outline of a humanoid appears. 


Zalthir is annoyed. They were about to have the element of surprise. If only Daz had carefully timed his water use so that Zalthir could take advantage of that action. 


But it is done. 


He scans his surroundings, eager to spot something, and he succeeds. He invokes Darkness and plunges into the water to attack the foe. Even though the opponent is invisible, Zalthir can pierce through the magical darkness, nullifying its advantage. 


The Dragonborn’s initial strike misses, but the second strikes home with a thud. 


### Thorin


Zalthir has disappeared into the Darkness. Thorin can sense another creature in the water and is tempted to use a handheld crossbow, but he rejects the idea. Diving into the water, he swims next to Zalthir and strikes the invisible creature, wounding it. 


Thorin hears two scorching rays hit the water and hiss harmlessly. He’s about to lose hope when he hears the scream of a Duergar in pain. 


Thorin thinks the creature is still alive. Then he hears Grygum's magical words and the telltale sound of a belch. He ducks. The Duergar is not so lucky and dies. 


Then he hears a scream. He can hear someone from the boat being grappled and pulled overboard.
Two more members of the crew are grappled. 


Thorin swims out, and Jimjar shouts, “You know what the Duergar do for a living?” 


Thorin wonders why the gnome would want to talk about anthropology now, “Nothing good?” 


“Yes, they capture slaves that they sell in Gracklstugh.” 


Thorin overhears Zalthir shout, “At least it’s where we were going.” 


Thorin approaches the boat and notices that Eldeth is not one of those captured. He sighs, relieved. The dwarf checks the others and sees that Stool has been captured. He attacks the Duergar. And the Duergar release Stool. 


Then the area is covered again in Darkness, and he hears the thuds of the Dragonborn’s fists on the Duergar. 


Thorin waits to see if the Duergar is dead. It is not. 


### Daz


“The darkness is annoying,” thinks Daz. He then mutters to himself, “I will need area-of-effect spells.” He smiles as he sees two targets. "The magic missile will do just fine here.”


The Duergar is struck by glowing teardrops of blue-white force sizzle through the air, swooping swift as starlings, unerring in their flight.


It howls in pain and drops the prisoner to flee. 


Daz notices Buppido has his hands over his eyes and asks, “What are you doing?” 


“I am invisible.” 


Daz rolls his eyes, “No, you are not.” 


“Yes, I am!” 


In the water, he can hear the splashing of Thorin’s swimming and then strikes the Duergar, dragging Spiderbait away. 


### 
Zalthir


Zalthir again moves the darkness to cover the enemies. He can hear Daz groan. 


As he swims towards them, he hears Eldeth curse them out as “Demonic spawn of dwarven corruption. Slave trader who deserves to be sent to the nine hells as punishment!” 


Zalthir mutters to himself, “I sense some racial animus.” 


His fists strike the creature that had Spiderbait, killing it.  


Daz shouts, “Leave one uncovered!” 


Zalthir shouts, “It protects you!” 


He can hear Daz’s sighs, and the burst of magic bolts kills the other creature.  


Another belch sounds, and another Duergar dies covered in acid. 


A few moments later, the last of the Deurgar is immobilized, and Zalthir is tired and happy. 


He floats to the keelboat and looks into the casks and the chest. He finds some gold and a jug that he learns later can be used to generate any liquid. The first liquid they generate is mayonnaise. After eating bland mushrooms, mayonnaise will make anything taste much better. 


As he digs through the contents, he can hear the debate on the Kuotoan boat about who should question their Duergar prisoner. 


Buppido shouts, “I have particular expertise and talents.”


Sarith explains, “The Lloth’s guards trained me. I am an expert, but having seen Daz’s approach, I recommend that he be given the first crack at extracting information.” 


### Grygum


The orc has been puzzled by Eldeth’s venom directed at the Duergar. He thinks there might be some rivalry between the Duergar gods and the Dwarven gods, but then he remembers something he read: that religious conflict is not always what it seems. The Dwarves and the Duergar compete for the same resources, and political conflict may matter more than any religious principle or dogma. “This is not the will of any god. This is the will of the people,” he mutters.  


## 9th day of the 1st Tenday of Myrtul
### Daz


The long journey through the dark waters of the Underdark has led him here to the metropolis of the Duergar in this part of the Underdark. 


Over the past few days, Hemeth has said a lot about his home, which sounds unpleasant. It’s a strictly regimented, xenophobic city. Non-Duergars are only permitted to move freely in the Darklake district. Within the city, everyone obeys the laws. The Duergar can become invisible at will; you never know who is watching. And finally, it’s a city of slavers. 


![image]()
And yet, despite everything Hemeth said, Daz is unprepared for what he sees. The city is so unlike the Drow cities Daz has heard of. The Duergar prize order and work over art and beauty. There is none of the opulence and refinement. Instead, the buildings are plain and functional. Whereas the Drow would have decorated their homes with etchings and art, the walls of the Duergar homes are plain. 


And the ambiance is just that of work. The sound of hammers warping metals, the stench and heat, and the light of the smelters make all of Gracklstugh appear as one large work camp. But the acrid smoke makes breathing hard and burns through his sense of smell, which he will remember most from his time here. 


Gyrgum asks, “So what are we doing here?” 


Daz looks at him and is about to answer when Jimjar whispers, “Buy equipment so we can survive more easily. Get information on how to get out of the Underdark.” 


Sethir mutters, “We plan to get equipment and then go to the Lost Tomb of Khem, where we will find treasure!” 


Daz notices that Grygum is satisfied. 


Daz remembers what Hemeth had said about the Blade Bazaar: 


“This marketplace is named after the most abundant goods the duergar offer, but the shops here sell almost everything available in the city, along with stalls set up by visiting merchants. The din of people arguing, mainly in Dwarvish, nearly drowns out the hammering coming from the city’s forges, and the crowds here offer a good chance to slip away from pursuers.”


“To the Blade Bazaar,” he says out loud. 


## Thorin
But before they can get to the bazaar, they must reach the docks. And before they reach the docks, Thorin remembers some castaways. He looks at Brannum Redmarch and Buppido and says, “What are we going to do about them?” 


Realizing that his time as a free person may be ending, Brannum pleads, “You wouldn’t turn me into the guard?” 


Zalthir has been thinking about some magical items he wants and was disappointed when Hemeth said that magical goods could be purchased in Gracklstugh but not in the Darklake District. He asks, “Could you purchase some uncommon magical items?” 


Brannam lights up, “Why didn’t you ask? That’s my business.” 


Hemeth, upon hearing that, grumbles, “I am a weapons trader, and you would go to him?”


Zalthir asks them, “Can either of you get me Eldritch Claw Tattoo?” 


Hemeth shrugs, “No.” 
Brannam lights up, “Yes! I know a guy. But it will take a few days. And then the slate is wiped clean?” 


Thorin realizes that Zalthir is very good at negotiating a deal, but sometimes muscle is needed to terrorize people, “And just so we are clear, if we don’t get what we want, and you betray us, there will be consequences.” 


Brannam shrinks, “Yes, of course.” 


Thorin then points at Buppido, “What about this guy?”


Buppido mutters, “Look, I made mistakes. It was the divine plan. Just let me go. I won’t bother you again.” 


Spiderbait, still seething with rage, wants to kill him on the spot, and although Thorin is tempted, he wants to see what everyone else has to say. 


Zalthir is unsure. “Perhaps he will betray us.” That logic sways Thorin and pulls his sword. “So we kill him?” 


But Grygum intervenes, “There are so many people who will kill us that I am not so sure we should worry about him.” 


Thorin lowers his weapon, uncertain. He looks to Daz, and Daz says, “I vote that we get as close to a guarantee as we can and then, as we leave, let him loose in the city.” 


Buppido is desperate and pleads, “I made mistakes. I was mad. The divine plan called me, and I realized it was a mistake. I have learned from Suushar that I was mad. PLEASE!” 


Thorin is now very confused. 


Then Zalthir hands him a mushroom, The Tongue of Madness, and says, “Eat.” 


And Buppido does and starts talking.


“I just wanted to return to my bolt hole in the caverns near the city and praise the Divine Plan.” 


And then he starts expressing his true opinions of his companions: “You all have been so annoying. Daz has foul-smelling breath. Thorin thinks his muscles are impressive. Eldeth is -not- interested in him.”


At that, Thorin decides he’s heard enough, but Grygum stops him. 


“Zalthir’s scales haven’t been cleaned in ages.” 


Grygum has heard enough, “I am done.” 


But then he says, “Jimjar’s bEttin g is so annoying! GIVE IT A REST.” 


Zalthir smiles, “I like him.” 


And that settles it with Thorin. He turns to Jimjar - “You all need to keep an eye on him.” Jimjar smiles, “Wanna bet?” 


### Zalthir


But just then, Zalthir remembers something, “So - wait, I mean, can he summon Demogorgon like the Kuo-toans?


Jimjar roars with laughter. 


Zalthir can’t help but think Jimjar is judging him. 


Then the gnome sighs, “Obviously, you do not understand the first thing about interplanar or gate travel. There are a great many things Buppido could do, but that is not one of them. The great powers of the outer planes in the Time of Trouble could enter the Prime Material Plane at will. And that created havoc. Aos, the overgod, has ended that era with the Second Sundering. And so now, bringing the avatar of such a power requires powerful magic and artifacts.” 


Jimjar then sighs and stares at Grygum, “The Cult of the Dragon almost brought Tiamat into the realms, and that required artifacts and rituals. No, Buppido has no such powers. But, Zalthir, it does beg the question - who did?” 


### Grygum


The cleric of Bahamut approaches a stall in the Blade Bazaar and tries to engage a merchant in a deal. The merchant, Dulgir, asks if he wants to buy or sell.


“Sell this mace?” 


At that, the Duergar starts cursing Grygum, accusing him of things that, in a different sEttin g, might have provoked a violent reaction. 


Grygum pauses and decides that he should start by buying, and the Duergar suddenly shifts, speaking with the stilted tongue of a Baldurian patriar. 


A few minutes later, he is the proud owner of a new mace. 


As he toys with his mace, he notices Zalthir looking at a list. “I found this list of names earlier.” 


Grygum shrugs.  “I think they might be worth looking into.” 


Grygum shrugs again. 


Zalthir is annoyed that he has nothing more definitive to say and mutters, “They could be people who could help us.” 


Grygum then notices that Dulgir is not the only one acting strangely. Many duergar merchants give different prices to buy or sell the same items whenever they are asked and insist that the buyers they deal with change their terms.


Near a food stall, a merchant suddenly turns invisible during a transaction but keeps speaking as if nothing has happened.


Across the aisle, another weapons merchant threatens a customer, and when the customer protests, the merchant denies ever saying so. 


Next to him, a cloth merchant suddenly stops mid-negotiating and starts talking to someone he refers to as his brother. When the customer asks who he is talking to, the merchant says, “My brother!” 


Grygum asks Dulgir, “I guess his brother is invisible.” 


But Dulgiar answers, “His brother doesn’t exist.” 


The answer disturbs Grygum, who is convinced that some madness afflicts the city, perhaps the same madness that consumed the Kuotoans. 


He steps away from the Bazaar and looks down a road to see if it’s happening elsewhere. The gates at the end of the road keep the Darklake District isolated from the rest of the city. It looks calm. 


And just then, the rhythmic hammering of the city’s forges is drowned for a second by a thundering roar and the sound of crumbling rock. Duergar and visitors alike turn to look as a two-headed, gray-skinned giant burst through a gate, howling madly and lashing out left and right, littering the plaza with rock and stone debris. As he bellows, one of his swings connects with a duergar soldier, whose broken body flies through the air and crashes near you with a sickening crunch.


Grygum thinks, “That’s an illusion!” But a piece of debris hit his toe, and he mutters, “No, it’s not.” 


Thorin, who overhears the giant, shouts, “Stone giants never talk. And they certainly don’t bellow. Something is wrong.” 


Grygum looks at him, “And the two heads?” 


“Two heads is more normal than bellowing.” 


For a moment, the priest of Bahamut wonders if he should just run. “What is in it for me?” he asks. And then he remembers the teachings of Bahamut that when you do a good turn for someone, they may do a good turn for you. 


A sign for Bahamut appears: a Duergar patrol racing towards this giant. 


“If I fight him, we may get some friends.” 


He turns to his companion, raises his holy symbol, and prepares to attack. 


With that, Zalthir and Thorin race into combat. 


Daz strikes first with magic missiles that streak from his hand. 


Zalthir places his circle of darkness just before the giant and enters it. Stepping up to the giant, he strikes with a flurry of blows. But he misses. The giant, although blind, dodges him. So Zalthir uses his power to focus his mind and strikes again. This time it is a glancing blow. 


Thorin rushes after Zalthir; with blindsight, he approaches the mad giant and strikes him with his sword. 


Grygum speaks the words of power that Bahamut has taught him, pointing his holy symbol at the mad giant who burns with necrotic damage and the power of Bahamut. 


The giant roars and smashes Thorin, but Zalthir, using his awareness of the monk, deflects the attack just in time. 


Daz is pleased, and Zalthir places the darkness so that he can still strike him. His fingers move in the air, the incantations are spoken, and bolts of power fly from his finger, striking the giant. 


Zalthir strikes the giant once, focusing his mind on striking again and striking the air. If the giant could see, he would see a dragonborn attack the air. And perhaps the giant would laugh. But this giant is mad and blind and misses this moment of joy. 


Thorin feints and strikes the giant. The creature howls. 


Gyrgum raises his holy symbol again and speaks the words of power, and the giant again burns.
