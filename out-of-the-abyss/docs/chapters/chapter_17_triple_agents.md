# Chapter 17 Triple Agents

## 9th day of the 1st Tenday of Myrtul 1493

### Grygum

Grygum watches as Erdde Blackskull stands. She has an aura of paranoia fixated on her person, and seems disappointed. 

Armed with the scrolls he found amongst her goods in the armory, Grygum prepares to leave when she says something very odd, “I would appreciate it if you were to look for the empty scabbard killers. My underlings have reported their presence after finding bodies with puzzling wounds.” 

Grygum is perplexed. 

Erdde, almost as if she doesn’t want to draw too much attention, tries to act utterly nonchalantly, “Oh, just an old wives tale to frighten children. They kill people with their minds.” She pauses, “Of course, this is nonsense. They are just another figment of the people’s imagination. It’s more likely that they don’t exist, and instead, assassinations are being conducted by some agency in the government, and this is a cover.” 

The dwarf is intrigued and tries to probe some more. But Erdde is unwilling to reveal much. 

“I can’t look for what I don’t know.” 

That does the trick, and Errde brings one of her subordinates into the room, Grimholl Forgebrand.

“Holl to my friends, and you are not my friends,” he announces. 

Holl explains that the bodies were found with no exit wounds or entrance wounds. 

Grygum further probes and learns that two victims were Errde Blackskull’s clan members. 

Errde dismisses the idea that these are assassins, “My uncle could have died for any of many reasons: poison, old age, a bad heart. The clerics found no evidence of that on their bodies.” 

She then pauses, “Look, I am not foolish enough to believe they weren’t assassinated, but some children’s fable come back to life to kill them? Nonsense.”

Grygum is perplexed: dead bodies with no entrance or exit wounds, no evidence of foul play, just dead bodies. 

Daz, known for his fondness of novels, reads voraciously and has managed to get a copy of Milo Goodbarrel’s book detailing the events surrounding the war of the dragon. He notes: “Milo describes this kind of attack as a psychic blade attack. It leaves no trace. There is this one moment where the Kenku Whistler used the psychic blades to fake an attack by Milo on an enemy.” 

Grygum stares at him. 

“I liked the books. They were well written.” 

## 10th day of the 1st Tenday of Myrtul 1493

### Daz

The Ghohlbrorn’s Lair, to Daz’s refined sensibilities, offers the kind of accommodations one could expect from a Duergar: unpleasant, cold, and austere. 	

There is a large, cold, damp dining room and communal chambers where you can sleep. 

The other escapees, except for Topsy and Turvy, have gone missing. 

“Wererats,” thinks Daz. 

Buppido continues to be bound and seems resigned to his fate. 

Jimjar has made a few friends and asks \- “When do you expect us to be able to leave?” 

Daz explains that he wants to learn about the Keepers of the Flame, and Grygum wants to know about this Red Dragon that is kept in some kind of peculiar captivity by the Keepers of the Flame. 

When Jimjar tries to explain that they came here for supplies, and judging from the equipment the group has, they have them and that, “Perhaps we should leave?” 

Daz dismisses him, “I think gathering intelligence is just as important.” 

Jimjar shrugs and slumps into one of the beds. He sighs, “Very well.” 

Zalthir turns to Daz, “And I don’t think we want to cross Errde. She said she could set the Drow on us.” 

Daz leaves the inn and walks to the piers near the “inn.” On one of the piers, a Duergar man appears, looking into the darkness of the Darklake. Like most Duergar, his clothes are dark and plain, but there is the occasional flourish of wealth and taste. 

“And perhaps that’s why,” Daz thinks, “he’s being attacked.” 

Daz thinks that and realizes he’s not just thinking that but watching that. Two assassins, wearing face coverings and wielding glowing weapons, strike the other man. 

“Psychic weapons?” he wonders. 

As Daz watches, Grygum rushes one of the assassins, trying to toss him into the water. He fails. 

When Daz utters the mystical words, motes of light appear above his hand, and three fingers point at one of the assassins. A silent rush of air accompanies the flight of the magic missiles, and when they strike, a burst of light and a crackling sound fills the area. 

Daz watches as Zalthir covers the area with Darkness. 

“I can’t see. And this is why area of effect spells are useful,” he mutters.

Grygum rushes into the fight, and so does Thorin. 

Daz can judge where the Drow is, even in the Darkness. He speaks the words for scorching rays and feels the rush of intense heat as the fiery energy is manifested. He points with his index finger, uses his thumb to sight, squints, and lets the rays loose. They travel quickly, leaving a streak of heat haze. Zalthir’s darkness hides the flash of bright light, but the smell of burning flesh is not. 

### Zalthir

Zalthir stares at the merchant and the dead assassins. Zalthir is uncertain if they made the right decision. 

Thorin mutters, “We assume we picked the right guy. For all we know, he’s the evil dude.” 

The Duergar merchant has no wound on his body.

Zalthir scrutinizes him and concludes that Holl may not have been lying.   
Before Zalthir can stop him, Grygum heals the Duergar. 

“Thank you\! I have no idea why they attacked me,” says the heaving Duergar. “Did they target me? Are they assassins?” 

Zalthir looks around for the weapons, finds none, but does find a charcoal drawing of the merchant on one of the bodies. 

He presents it to the Duergar. 

“Oh my, I was.” There is a pause, “I am Werz Saltbaron. And you did me a good turn. Come by the Shattered Spire tomorrow, and I will reward you. Today, I have places to be.” 

Zalthir watches as the Duergar becomes invisible. 

“Convenient.” 

Zalthir starts to rifle through the assassins' corpses and finds some coins, a pair of bloodstained gloves, and a box of candles. 

He notices they have a pleasant smell and passes them to Thorin. 

The dwarf smiles, “Scented candles\!” And then he says, “God, what I wouldn’t do for a bath and listening to whale sounds.” 

Zalthir chuckles, “I could create that illusion for you.”

Thorin hoots. 

Just then, Zalthir hears a body falling into the water. Grygum has pushed the first corpse into the water. 

“We must get out of here before the guard shows up.” 

Zalthir agrees and helps push the other body into the water.  

The group finds a quiet corner and sits far away from the battle scene. And after resting, he heads out to the Keepers of the Flame.

Errde Blackskull’s passes let him and his friends through the gates that keep all non-Duergars out of the city. The guards who look at the passes are surprised but are not so surprised as to ask any questions. 

The Keepers of the Flame are in the far southeastern corner of the cave, and the journey is uneventful. 

As Zalthir walks, he reflects on the city's quiet and how the monks would be astounded at how peaceful it could be. “Having invisible ruthless guards helps.” He wonders if such a regime could be implemented. 

Finally, he arrives at the entrance, and there is Gartokkar. The Duergar priest wants to lead him to a building carved from stone just outside a monumental cave entrance. But the ground trembles slightly just then, and a thunderous voice echoes against every wall.

“Gartokkar,” the voice calls as a reptilian behemoth comes into view. It scales the color of lava, its bright yellow eyes glowing in the cave's shadows before coming into the light. You didn’t say I was having surfacers for dinner today.”

The dragon chuckles at his wit. His massive body gives the distinct impression that he is overweight, indicating eating habits that don’t bode well.

“Foreigners,” the duergar priest says with great reverence, “meet the Father of Flame, the Everburning, and the Foundry’s Heart — Themberchaud, the Wyrmsmith of Gracklstugh.”

The dragon roars, “I wish to speak to them alone.” 

Zalthir notices how Gartokkar isn’t pleased but points him forward. 

The dragon’s cavern is filled with a hoard of gems and coins. Sitting on top of it is a plump, overweight red dragon. 

Zalthir is stunned. He’s never seen such a thing. 

The Dragon bellows, “If I catch anyone spying, I will kill them.” 

And with that, he breathes fire in a circle around the cavern. When nobody dies or screams, the dragon laughs. 

The dragon turns to Zalthir, “You are a gold dragonborn?” 

“No, a bronze.”

“Good. I never liked gold dragons.” 

Zalthir stares at him and wonders how this fat, obviously out-of-shape red dragon ended up like that. And then, he wonders whether this red dragon could survive in the wild. It is so pampered that it probably has forgotten how to hunt. 

He questions Thorin and Grygum and seems satisfied with the answers.   
When it’s Daz’s turn to be questioned, the dragon asks if Daz is from Menzoberranzan. Zalthir wonders as well. Daz says he is. But Zalthir isn’t convinced that is the truth. 

The dragon laughs, “Excellent\! You have no family associations and nobody that can be blackmailed.” 

Grygum shouts, “We have people who will come looking for us\!” 

Zalthir nods, “Of course.” 

The dragon laughs, “Very well. I am Themberchaud, and I wish for you to be my agent. And as my agents, you will have my protection.” 

Zalthir overhears Daz and asks Grygum, “Do you think this dragon also plays chess? According to Milo Goodbarrel’s memoirs, chess played a significant role in negotiating with the metallic dragons.” 

“Deals with red dragons are never good deals,” thinks Zalthir. 

Daz is curious, “What must we do as agents?” 

The dragon whispers, “Your first mission is to do whatever the Keepers of the Flame ask you to, but report to Themberchaud first.” 

### Thorin 

Having lived with giants, Thorin has picked up the peculiar antipathy towards dragons that giants have. And this red dragon is triggering him.

But even he notices that Themberchaud wasn’t asking him to be an agent; he was telling him he was an agent.

“Its ego is so fragile that it can’t even wait to hear an answer of yes.”  

The dragon roars, “Gartokkar, come.” 

And the Duergar priest shuffles insides, “Yes?”

“Give these humanoids some of the gold badges,” growls the dragon, inches from Thorin’s face. “And the rest of you do what Gartokkar says.” 

Gartokkar hands out some gold badges. Thorin weighs it in his hands. Daz turns to him and asks, “How much gold is this?”   
Thorin follows Gartokkar outside the cave and asks Daz, “Did we find ourselves working for a faction without much choice?” 

Daz, who has experience with the twisted, machiavellian politics of the Drow, replies, “The faction thinks we are working for them. Whether we do is up to us.” 

Grygum, standing next to Thori,n mutters, “I suppose we are double agents, or is it triple agents? We work for the Stone Guards and the Keepers of the Flame.”

Thorin is exasperated and almost shouts, “I am sorry, I can’t deal with these factions. I can’t keep track of who we are working for and which faction we are double-crossing. I want to be my faction.” 

But he manages to keep his frustration bottled up just long enough to make it to Gartokkar’s chambers.

The Duergar priest sits down in his chamber and says, “I am so delighted to see that you accepted Themberchaud’s offer to be an agent of ours.”

Thorin is about to explode, but Daz gets their first, “We never technically accepted an offer. He assumed we accepted. We would like to understand the nature of the offer?” 

“What are the rights and responsibilities for this position?” growls Thorin, outraged to be working for a dragon. 

Gartokkar begins \- “The powers of the Keepers of the Flame derive an equal measure from our psionic abilities, the leadership of the Deepking, and our influence over Themberchaud. We lost face when the Gray Ghosts stole the valuable red dragon egg that would hatch Themberchaud’s replacement. Our war with the Thieve’s guild has been ongoing. The Gray Ghosts stole the egg and created a great disturbance in the Underdark. It feels like a hole has been torn through the world.” 

Thorin thinks the words about holes are just a flex of vocabulary by psionics and ignores them. Instead, he asks, “Does Themberchaud know about this egg?” 

“He doesn’t.”   
   
“But we were hired by him?”

Gartokkar smiles conspiratorially, “Themberchaud thinks we work for him. What Themberchaud doesn’t realize is that he works for us. It’s good that you told us this about Themberchaud. The egg is for when Themberchaud moves on. His replacement.”

“Moves on? How does he move on? Do you kill him?” 

Gartokkar looks appalled, “Of course not\! Eventually, the Dragon moves on because he wants to see more of the world.”

Grygum turns to Thorin, “It’s like when parents say that the pet puppy left even when it dies.” 

Gartokkar continues, ignoring the comment, “We have identified an agent of the Grey Ghosts. His name is Droki.”

Thorin remembers that this is the same person Errde, Captain of the Stone Guards, is looking for. 

Gartokkar says, “As you have already accepted to work for Themberchaud, let me give you this holy symbol of Laduguer and these gold pins featuring Themberchaud’s profile. With them, you can travel within Gracklestugh, but there are limits.”

Thorin is annoyed, “What if we say no?” 

“Well we will turn you over to the Stone Guards.”

“Under what charge?” 

“Very well. As Gartokkar Xundorn, Custodian of the Flame, let me enumerate a list of potential transgressions that the dwarf, Thorin, has committed, which could warrant significant punitive action within the laws of Gracklstugh.

Let me see; I can prepare just such a document. He pulls out a piece of parchment and starts to read off it, adding Thorin’s name in the blank spaces. 

1. **Public Disturbance and Disorder:** Thorin was involved in the altercation with the deranged stone giant. While he claims to have wanted to subdue the giant, his actions could be interpreted as contributing to the ensuing public disorder. Creating such disturbances within the city is a serious offense.  
2. **Interference with City Watch:** Thorin attempted to influence and direct the actions of the Duergar City Watch during the aforementioned incident with the stone giant. While he claims he was trying to help, any interference with the watch's official duties can be seen as obstruction and a challenge to their authority.  
3. **Questioning Authority:** Thorin has repeatedly questioned the authority and motives of various Duergar individuals and factions, including the Keepers of the Flame. Such questioning can be perceived as dissent and disrespect, which is not tolerated within Gracklstugh's hierarchical society.  
4. **Unauthorized Access:** Depending on his movements within the city, Thorin may have entered areas restricted to non-Duergar citizens without proper authorization, even with the passes. Any unauthorized access to restricted areas is a violation of city ordinances.  
5. **Association with Undesirables:** Thorin has been seen associating with individuals of questionable character, including other surface dwellers and individuals who may be considered enemies of the state. Such associations can raise suspicion and lead to accusations of conspiracy, aiding, and abEttin g.  
6. **Possession of Unauthorized Items:** If Thorin is found to own any items considered contraband or restricted within Gracklstugh, such as certain weapons or magical items, he will violate the law.  
7. **Suspicion of Espionage:** Given his questioning and interest in the city's affairs, including the Keepers of the Flame and Themberchaud, Thorin is suspected of espionage or gathering intelligence for potentially hostile entities. This is a grave offense with severe consequences.  
8. **General Suspicion:** As a non-Duergar, Thorin is already subject to suspicion and scrutiny within Gracklstugh. Any deviation from expected behavior or any action that could be interpreted as threatening to the city's stability can lead to arrest and imprisonment.

According to the penal code, the interpretation and enforcement of these laws are at the discretion of the Duergar authorities. Even seemingly minor infractions can be used to justify imprisonment if deemed necessary.’

Gartokkar pauses, “Is that enough?” 

Thorin stares at him. “I am just putting all of our cards on the table. So if we get arrested, we will have to tell the Stone Guards about the egg, right?” 

Gartokkar turns dark and then smiles, “You joke? Sharing that would be a disaster.” 

“For whom?” 

Gartokkar looks at him, “Just find me the egg and the proof of the Gray Ghosts.” 

As they stand outside the building that houses Gartokkar’s office, Daz turns to Thorin while looking at his collection of badges, “So we flash the right one at the right time?” 

Thorin nods. And then he has a peculiar glint in his eye. He turns to Grygum, “I have an idea. These factions are all trying to play us against each other, and this Dragon is one of us. So what if we ride this Dragon out of here, laying waste to everything behind us?” 

Grygum smiles, “As a cleric of Bahamut, I am appalled at how they treat Themberchaud. Let’s do it.”
