# Chapter 14 Journey Through the Darklake
## 9th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493
### Grygum


Jimjar stares at Grygum and asks, “Why are you a follower of Bahamut?” 


And Grygum sighs. 


He has always been a bit of a mystery to other folks—an orc cleric of a dragon god. 


Those from the more civilized parts of Faerûn ascribe it to the strange story of Protanther and the Dwarf and a chess game. 


The Dwarves and the Dragons had a long history of not seeing eye to eye. And although they found ways to acknowledge each other’s existence begrudgingly, they were not friends. 


After the events of the Well of the Dragons, the Dwarf Stroud had gone on a bit of a speaking tour, talking about his victory over the Gold Dragon Protanther and how the Gold Dragon saw that as a sign. He would spend hours walking folks through this thought process, the board, and the inspiration he had when he made the move. 


Many a chess grandmaster stared at the board, dumbfounded, that Protanther did not see the move on a board he had been studying for 400 years. They were also confused by Stroud’s explanation of the move because it would appear that he barely had a passing knowledge of the rules. 


Those who believe in the intrinsic goodness of the Gold Dragons believe Protanther chose to lose. He saw an opening to side with those who opposed Tiamat and made it sound like a sign from the gods. 


Some saw Stroud as the personification of the right humanoid at the right time and place, doing the right thing. It was proof that the individual had agency and that a single person could change the world, 


But others believed that Bahamut moved Stroud’s hand and hid the move from Protanther. 


And so the dragon god, who was never one to seek faithful among those who were not dragons, has gained quite a following among those who saw in his actions a divine plan. 


Jimjar was fishing to find out what Grygum believed, and Grygum was tired. And it was dark, and he didn’t want to get into another argument about free will, the will of the gods, and whether the scale color of a dragon defined the intrinsic goodness of a creature that lived for a millenia. 


Grygum smiles and says nothing. 


Jimjar stares back. 


## 10th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493
### Daz 


The boat suddenly stops, and Daz is tossed forward. 


Sarith shouts, “A sandbar!” 


Daz looks into the war and sees a swarm of those demonic Manta Rays floating in the water.


Zalthir shouts, “Anyone got oars?” 


Sarith laughs, “It would have been useful a while ago…” 


Daz watches as Grygum and Thorin prepare to get into the water to push. 


And then a flash of inspiration hits him, “Stop, I can do this.” 


They look at him, and he smiles, “Shape Water.”


Everyone groans. They have heard Daz explain how he can use Shape Water, and every time, the promise far exceeds what is delivered. He remembers an old saying from his days as an apprentice, “Overpromise and under-deliver!” But the last few days have made him question his memory.  


Daz is aware of their doubt, but this time, he is confident. 
He casts the spell and uses it to push a small wave over the sandbar. The extra water is enough to move the boat forward. He keeps doing it, and pretty soon, the ship rises over the sand and is free. 


As it floats down into the murky darkness, he turns to the group and says, “So?” 


And he enjoys a brief moment of applause and congratulations.
