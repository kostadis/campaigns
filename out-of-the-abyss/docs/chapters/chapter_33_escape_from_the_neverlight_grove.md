# Chapter 29 Escape from the Neverlight Grove. 
### Grygum


The procession is clearly a wedding procession, but not of the wedding itself. This is a rehearsal. 


One thing strikes me as odd: the absence of a groom, and the reference to Araumycos as the groom. 


I turn to my companions, and we talk in hushed tones. What we infer is that Araumycos is the name of a massive sentient fungal growth in the Underdark. From the stories we know, it is the natural habitat of the Myconids. Zuggtmoy intends to marry them. 


The fear in my stomach is real. The Queen of Fungi is the Queen of decay and death. Her union with Araumycos will give her untold power over the world. 


But what can we do? Zuggtmoy is too powerful, and we are too weak. And so we watch to see what more we can learn. 


Daz turns to me, “Unless we have access to some explosives, or some incredibly insane, clever plans, I don’t think we can do anything.”


I turn to him, “All I feel is fear. First, we see Demogorgon. Now we see Zuggtmoy. And the denizens of the Underdark have been driven mad and can’t seem to defend themselves.” 


Zalthir, not one to engage in hyperbole, asks, “What can we do here? We are not powerful enough to fight a demon lord. I don’t know what we can do other than maybe getting Rump-a-dump and Stool out of here.”


Thorin agrees, “We need to tell them that this place is no-bueno.” 


We stare at each other despondently when Jimjar, irritatingly perky, says, “I bet you could get some help from the overbrite. And I bet you could find a way out of the Underdark in Blingdenstone.” 


I pause, he’s right. I turn to my friends, “We need to get out of the Underdark and find help.” 


The rest of the group nods. 


And so we hide to avoid being seen, and the procession continues to where we fought the Yestabrod. 


From our vantage point, the horrific scene plays out. 


The thralls enact a mock wedding, with a chamberlain and a bridesmaid standing in for Araumycos and Zuggtmoy. Yestabrod assumes the role of priest to preside over the vows. As he is dead, three spore servants rise from the garden and carry his corpse, moving it like a puppet to play out the part of a priest. 


And then my head hurts, and instead of seeing what’s in front of my eyes, I see something else, a vision. A vision of the inside of a great tower replaces the tableau of the mock wedding. Spiraling stairs and balconies are carved into its walls, with the interior lit by phosphorescent patches of mold growing in whorls. In the center of the open space floats a humanoid figure, womanlike in form, but made entirely of fungi and mold. She is easily three times the height of the fungal bridesmaids that move up and down the spiraling stairs, tending to their giant mistress. They croon a strange, soothing song as they weave the substance of the giant figure into delicate lichen veils and a long, mycelium train like a bridal gown.


When the vision is over, I see the procession going back down the ledge into the chamber with the large mushroom. 


“Shall we continue?” 


Before we go, Thorin shouts, “Put a wet rag over your mouth; it may help us against any spores.” 


Daz nods his head, his face wrapped in a cloth. 


So we follow the procession into the cavern to the east.  Yestabrod’s Garden of Welcome is a pale reflection of the true horrors surrounding the giant mushroom, what I assume is the creature Yggmorgus. A 20-foot-high crescent-shaped ledge hugs the cavern wall and gradually slopes down to the lower basin. The ledge is covered with a carpet of moss and fungi, scores of variously sized lumps, and pockmarks where pools of vile fluids suppurate and ooze, some drying out and scabbing over.


I have no clear reference to judge the towering mushroom’s size at this distance. Thousands of smaller fungi cling to the central stalk, which itself splits into several lesser stalks, each long enough with a cap big enough to be the top of a great tower. A carpet of fungi covers the cavern floor surrounding the stalk. An eerie luminescence pours through slitted windows carved into the trunk, with the same cacophony of atonal music heard earlier echoing within. A stench of rot and decay wraps around me, seemingly threatening to penetrate my flesh and pervade my soul.


It is both alien and familiar. And I remember when we felt this way, near Zuggtmoy in our dream. 


Just then, Daz starts screaming. 


He is saying bizarre things like - “That’s not possible, a city of drow with no spiders? Elves and Drow living together?” 


I wonder if this is a good vision or a bad one, but the fear in his eyes is telling. He swats his hands at the nightmares he sees. And he shouts out loud, “Dogs? Drow with Dogs as pets?” 


Thorin shouts, “If we advance any further, we might all be afflicted by madness.” 


I agree. So we leave. 


On our way back, we stop to pick up any treasure from the corpse of Xinaya. Zalthir, before we can do anything, has run up to the corpse and stuffed everything in his bag. 


I noticed some scrolls and asked him if he would give them to me. He says, “No.” 


Confused, I ask, “What will you do with them later?” 


He cuts me off, “I will figure it out later.” 


Thorin mutters, “This does not seem fair.” 


Zalthir, then very oddly says, “Nobody said it had to be fair.” 


I turn to Pelek, the ghost, and ask if he knows anything about this, and he says, “No. I am a ghost, not a fungi expert. But if you are asking if a ghost possesses him, no, he is not.” 


And then Pelek turns to me and his eyes gleam, “Aren’t you a cleric?” 


Dawnbringer shouts, “I can cast lesser restoration!” 


And before I can say anything, a wave of magic covers Zalthir. 


He coughs and then looks at us, confused and embarrassed. 


“I don’t know what caused this sudden need to hold onto everything.” 


Zalthir was so worried about losing his mind, and I wasn’t. And here he lost his mind, and I didn’t. 
### 
Thorin


We quickly march back to Rasharoo and Basidia. We tell them everything. And they are horrified. Basidia insists on going to confront Phylo now. Rasharoo urges caution, but when they sees their Sovereign go into danger, Rasharoo follows. 


I look at my friends and ask, “What do we do? We can’t leave Rump-a-dump and Stool in the clutches of this madness. They are our friends.” 


Zalthir agrees. 


But he still worries about infections and worries that Stool and Rump-a-dump might be infected. 


“Maybe we can do that restoration thing?” I wonder out loud. 


Zalthir turns to me, “Phylo thinks we are going to be killed tomorrow. So if we show up and act cool, we can do this as if nothing has happened, maybe we can sneak out.” 


So we run after Rasharoo and Basidia. A few moments later, we are in the Inner Circle, and there is Phylo. He sees us and Basidia and asks, “My fellow Sovereign, did they enjoy seeing the circle? What do they think of the changes?” 


Daz tries to be nonchalant and says, “They seem to be causing problems for some people.”


I think about those mutated corpses in the Circle of Welcome, and wonder what the problem means for a Drow. 


But I am trying to be cool. However, Dawnbringer shedding bright light on everyone isn’t helping right now. The Myconids are retreating from me. 


And just then, Gyrgum starts  jumping up and down and screaming, “DANGER!” 


I can hear Zalthir through the Rapport Spores mutter, “This was not how I meant stealthy.” 


And just when it couldn’t get more awkward, Basidia decides that they have had enough. “Phylo, you have betrayed the Circle. You’ve allied yourself with that monster!” 


At this point, I tense up because it's looking like it’s going to be a fight. And the sentient Zurkhwoods are starting to move. 


I try to persuade Basidia that the time to fight has passed, and now it is the time to flee. We all try, but the Sovereign won’t listen. 


Grygum turns to Rump-a-dump and Stool and manages to convince them. Our time together, and what they have seen, makes them realize that this place isn’t safe anymore. 


Rasharoo shouts into my brain, “If you want to live, come with me.” 
I remember that he had an escape hatch, and so we ran away from the Inner Circle. 
### Zalthir
Leaving Basidia is unacceptable. They tried to save us. And I owe them that much. They won’t listen to reason, but if there is one thing I can do, it is drag people. 


As my allies run away, I run towards Basidia and grapple with them. They resist at first, but then, seeing the madness of the trees and the Inner Circle, they give up. 


As we move away, Phylo doesn’t try to attack us. Instead, they plead, “Basidia don’t go. The Neverlight Grove will be better than ever! And the Great Seeder will make us all happy!” The last words I hear are plaintive, “Basidia don’t go.” 


And if a Myconid could sob, Basidia would be crying. 


Limp in my arms, I drag them to the safety of the Circle of Explorers, where their allies are. Rasharoo, Loobamub, and the circle of hunters and explorers have gathered near one of the exit holes that Rasharoo had identified. I can tell they are in a deep mind meld. 


As we leave the Neverlight Grove and its madness, Basidia turns to us and hands some potions of greater healing and what little treasure the Mykonids had accumulated over the years from passersby.  


As we head into the Underdark, Rasharoo tells us that he can provide us with a guide for half the distance to Blingdenstone, but after that, we are on our own.


Jimjar insists he knows where he is going, “I’ll bet you I can get you there!” 


I look at him and sigh. Grygum tries to explain the absurdity of the bet, but can’t resist taking the money from the curious gnome.

### Daz


And once again into the dark. My friends wonder why Ilvara would still be pursuing us and whether all the measures we take to hide ourselves are truly necessary. 


As Grygum puts it, “Shouldn’t she give up?” 


I smile. Ilvara promised that we would arrive in Menzoberranzan. Who knows what money exchanged hands? And then to make matters worse, the outpost of Velkynvelve was destroyed. She needs to find us, lest she become a Drider. She won’t rest until we are either found or she is dead. 


Zalthir tries to ask about the insanity of it all, and I look at him; he stares back,, and we remember that insanity seems to be a disease affecting everyone these days. 


Today, Rasharoo bids us farewell. 


Before all the Mykonids depart, Rump-a-dump approaches Zalthir. They look at them and say that they have a list of ways in which they could improve. Before Zalthir can tell them that he is not interested in any of them, Rump-a-dump enumerates fifty or so specific things Zalthir could do to be better. Things like - “Listen to Stool, he knows what he is doing. Don’t assume you know everything; other people’s points of view are important. Or my favorite, - Eat more Zurkhwood, it's good for your digestion.” 


Stool and Rump-a-dump look at me, and they say - “Goodbye!” and I think there is a hint of sadness, but they are with their people now.

We carefully walk around the ledge, and as we reach the end, a large eight legged octopus attacks us. 


The battle is fierce and swift, and the creature dies. 


Jimjar apologizes for his mistake.

As we were walking down a tunnel, a tremor caused the passageways ahead and behind us to collapse. 


Jimjar was dumbfounded. He tries to distract us with one of his nonsensical bets. 


“The next emotion Zalthir shows will be as is if it was made of a chipped black obsidian.” 


We stare at him confused. His usually at least can parse. This one is inscrutable. 


He tries to explain the meaning of the riddle and we realize he is just reaching. Finally he sits down, despondent, and declares, “I was just trying to distract you guys.” 


And then his eyes perk up, and he points to a place that we didn’t notice before, a passageway that leads into more caves. 


“We should go in there!” he declares. 


I wish I had learned mold earth, because I could have excavated a path out of this tunnel, but so be it. 


Instead we will go into the tunnels that Jimjar has somehow found for us.
