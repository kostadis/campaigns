# Chapter 23 The Dark Lady 
### Zalthir
The small myconid has become increasingly demanding that we save his friend Rump-a-dump. 
He says that his friend would never threaten him, and then talks about zombie creatures that were harvested from the dead. 
While he whines, the adults have more pressing concerns. We have enough information to create a civil war at best, or a genocidal massacre at worst. 
Jimjar suggests we hold onto it, even betting that we will find it very useful in the future. I find that notion absurd. Once we are out of the Underdark, I do not intend to come back. 
The group is conflicted about what to do, but I calmly remind them of our plan and its wisdom. 
And after some prattling and discussion, they agree that we will hand over the egg to Gartokkar and then escape via the ports. 
“Zalthir, why won’t we go save Rump-a-dump?” 
Daz, who demonstrates the cold detachment of the Drow, is indifferent. But Thorin Giantfriend seems determined to help Stool’s friend. 
At the monastery, children like this would be sent to a room and told only to return when they could understand why they must be silent. 
I patiently explain to Thorin that what we know is that these bipedal mushrooms can mess with people’s minds and control their bodies. 
Stool, of course, insists that his people are harmless. 
As his obstinacy does not affect me, he turns to Thorin for support. 


Which is a good strategy. 


Thorin explains that it is splitting. 
I sense a strategic awareness on the part of the small creature that suggests it is far wiser than I have given it credit. 
Unfortunately, Stool is too successful, and Thorin is off. 
Stool looks at me, and I am convinced that if he could, he would be sticking his tongue out at me. 
I again encourage my allies to prepare themselves against any of the powers of the Myconid. And it is then that Daz explains that we have nothing to fear. 
“They have the power to release spores. The dangerous spores are only hazardous in melee range. They can only turn creatures they have killed into Zombies. Until you are dead, the zombie process doesn’t work.” 


## 3rd day of the 2nd Tenday of Myrtul 


### Thorin 
We soon arrive at the cave with the Myconids, who seem to be dancing as if at a Wandering Veil concert that he attended.


My allies seem confused, and I realized that not everybody is aware of The Wandering Veil. So I explain that this eclectic troupe of bards and mystics has roamed the realms for decades, their music a swirling tapestry of haunting ballads, raucous tavern shanties, and spellbinding improvisations that seem to bend the very fabric of reality. 


Grygum looks at the Myconids and looks at me, and is still confused. So I explain “The Veil’s performances are never the same twice; each show is a living ritual, a communal experience where the lines between audience and artist blur, and even the most hardened adventurer finds themselves dancing beneath a sky painted with arcane light.” 


As we stand there, Serith suddenly starts walking towards the Myconids as if compelled. 


He shouts - "The call... the Dark Lady is calling me! Can't you hear her? She sings in the spores, a song of becoming! Of true purpose! I... I must go to them! They know the way to the bloom!"


Zalthir notices this and stops him. 


Sarith protests, "I... I can feel her now! The whispers... the glory! The pain, the madness... it's all part of the becoming! We must join the Lady's dream! There is no other way now. She commands it. She calls!"


Sarith tries to resist, but seems conflicted. 


Zalthir while holding Sarith looks disapprovingly at Daz and says, “We are seeing effects that are stronger than 30’” 


And just then, Stool starts jumping up and down as he shouts to another sprout, Rump-a-Dump. 


### Rump-a-dump


I stare at my friend Stool and the humanoids he has brought. I sigh. Stool, once again, is speaking constantly, generating words and sounds, and not giving me a chance to think. 


Whereas he attempts to convey with an avalanche of words, I believe in a tersely worded comment that is precise and restrained. 


I shift my weight, looking towards the dwarf, then glance at Stool. I decide his enthusiastic bounciness is not helping but hindering. When I speak, I can hear the worry in my voice. 


"Greetings. I see Stool found you. \*\*Something is not right here.\*\*".


I gesture with a thick, stubby appendage towards the dancing myconids, then point to a taller figure among them. 


"It's Voosbur. He released strange spores... and everyone changed. They started... this.".


### Grygum 


The creature looks uncomfortable and very worried. 


"We were all very happy in Neverlight Grove," His thoughts briefly show a serene, glowing cavern before clouding with confusion. "Then Voosbur's spores came, and the next thing we knew, we were \*here\*. It was... a sudden jump. The others... I think they ate the silly mushrooms\*\* after we arrived.". His thoughts flicker to the shrinking/growing mushrooms you've encountered.


"I did not eat them. That is why I am not... like this.".


Thorin is shocked and asks, “Isn’t that Cannibalism?” 


Rump-a-dump stops and stares, “A mushroom is not a Myconid. It is my understanding that you eat other creatures that are like you? Are you cannibals?” 


I am cringing. 


Daz has his hands in his face, embarrassed and muttering, “Overbrighters. They understand nothing about the complex ecology of the Underdark.” 


"These are not our old dances. And the way they speak... about 'the Lady's dream,' it is confusing. Stool does not understand it either. It is not the way of myconids. We do not dance like this. This is... forced.".


"I am worried. I need to get back to Neverlight Grove. What if Voosbur's strange spores... infected the rest of my myconids back home?". "My Sovereign there, they might know a way out of here... if they are still themselves.".


I sigh, “Another race gone mad.” 


Thorin, who has started to dance as if he were at a Withering Veil concert, says, “I like these Myconids, basically don’t mess with them, they don’t mess with you.” 


Meanwhile, Sarith, who was never sane to begin with, says,  "We will rise! We will transform! This is the only way to escape... to truly live! The Dark Lady... she offers liberation... revenge for all that has been done!"


Zalthir recalls that Sarith’s urge to go to the Myconids increased when he was closer to them, and so pulls him back, and mutters, “The knowledge of the Drow …” 


As I stare at the situation, and mull over the words, “The Dark Lady,” I remember something from my teachings about a demonic power known as the Dark Lady in the holy text The Platinum Chronicle: Discourses on the Abyssal Blight. 



**Excerpt from The Platinum Chronicle: Discourses on the Abyssal Blight**
**On the Blight of Zuggtmoy, Demon Queen of Fungi, Lady of Rot and Decay**
This fiend, Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi, the Lady of Rot and Decay, is a grievous affront to the very essence of creation. Her being is the antithesis of justice, order, and compassion, for where the Platinum Dragon, Bahamut, seeks to foster life and free will, she yearns only for chaos, madness, and utter destruction.
Her powers are not born of strength, but of insidious corruption. She seeks ever to infect the living with her spores, transforming them into her mindless servants, destined to become naught but decomposing hosts for the mushrooms, mold, and other fungi that are her abhorrent spawn. This "gift" she offers is a perversion of life itself, a descent into a perpetual state of rot and decay, where the mind is consumed in a twisted, communal dream.


Let it be known to all who walk the paths of Bahaumuy: Zuggtmoy’s song is not of liberation but of forced perversion. She seeks to extinguish the freedom of choice that Bahamut cherishes for all creatures. To resist this Demon Queen is to champion life against the encroaching rot and madness. Thus, vigilance is paramount against the creeping influence of the Lady of Rot and Decay
I explain to my friends that this Dark Lady is, in fact, the Demoness. 


Zalthir looks particularly clever to be so far away from these Myconids. But Bahamut commands me not to walk away. 


I wonder aloud if it’s possible that whatever controls Voosbur can control us, but Daz explains that, as this is a Demonic curse, the method of propagation is not natural but magical. And in most likelihood requires consent or some exchange of bodily fluids. 


Zalthir, if he were here, would wonder if visiting the Neverlight Grove would be wise. After all, there is a chance that Zuggtomy is there. 


Thorin, terrified or reckless, mutters, “You throw a stone here, you hit a demon.” 


I nod.


And then turn to Thorin, “All roads lead to Voosbur. We must talk to him to discover what is going on.” 
### 
Daz


We approach the Myconid, and it stops its frantic dance for a moment. And then it continues its dance, but we hear it’s voice in our heads. 


“Hey, beautiful souls, you’ve been groovin’ through the dark for a while now, yeah? Dig this — there’s a far-out place, a hidden trip where the Lady herself spins the vibes. It’s like the ultimate underground rave, but in your mind, man. No noise, just the pulse of the spores and the rhythm of the earth.


Thorin laughs, “He’s so totally at a Wandering Veil concert.” 


I ask about the infection, and his voice changes. 


“She’s no infection. She’s the mama of the mushrooms, the queen of the dreamscape, and she’s got a gift — a ticket to ride through her cosmic garden. It’s not an infection, it’s a wild ride, a mind-expanding journey where you can see the world in a whole new light, feel the beat of the Underdark’s heart.


But hey, this ain’t no Sunday picnic. The Lady’s dream is deep, heavy, and real — like a psychedelic trip that changes you forever. You ready to drop in, tune out, and dance with the spores?


Come on, let’s take that trip together. Peace, love, and mushrooms, man. Will you groove with the Lady’s dream?” 


The creature’s hand is outstretched, and I shrug. I have seen the ecstatic drug-fueled madness of the cleric of demons, and have no interest in participating. 


But Thorin, who has a “fuck-it-all” attitude, takes the creature's hand. 


Grygum mutters, “This reminds me of that time. Well, I’ll be going around to make sure it isn’t a bad trip.” And he too is holding onto Voosbur’s hand. 


I step back. 


And then both Grygum and Thorin stop moving. 


Voosbur assures that all will be well. 


I am not sure. 


### Thorin


I open my eyes and realize I am in a different cavern, my heavy boots sinking slightly into the soft, mossy floor. I squint, taking in the scene. 


"Well, now. This air's thicker than a giant’s forearm, feels almost alive, humming softly like a forge that's lost its bellows. Mushrooms, taller than any giant I've ever seen standing straight, stretch right up to the cavern ceiling, their caps glowing in all sorts of fancy colors—violet, teal, amber. They're casting light all over the place, soft and shifting, making patterns on the ground."


I  try a step, my brows furrowing as the ground beneath my feet pulses faintly.


"The ground here feels... squishy, like it's breathing. Odd. And all these tiny spores drifting about, shimmering like motes of dust in a sunbeam. Looks pretty, I suppose."


I take a cautious sniff, then shake my head slightly. A familiar feeling of unease stirs within me.
"Smells earthy, a bit too sweet, with a musk that's starting to make my head swim. Just a little. Like that Tongue of Madness mushroom, but not as direct. All these glowing fungi, pulsing and fading, like the slow beat of some silent drum. The whole place is like… a Veil Concert. Beautiful, but there’s definitely something strange, something otherworldly, lurking beneath it all. This ain't natural, not by a long shot."


I take a deep breath, trying to clear my head. As I do so, I feel my face form a faint frown as the glade's silent song seems to tug at my thoughts, threatening to pull me deeper into its strange rhythm.


I mutter out loud, “Just a trip, Thorin, just a trip."


The experience is too much, and I start breathing shallow breaths. Is it fear, or caution, I don’t know, but I rush down the cavern. 


As I do so, I touch some mushrooms and they start to pulse, as if to warn me not to touch them again. 


I don’t. 


As we leave the cavern, I find myself in another, very different one. 


"Hmph. This looks like a giant, festering wound. There are thick, twisting vines and fungal tendrils, woven together like the pulsing veins of some colossal, subterranean beast. Not a sight for a dwarf with an empty stomach."


I take a deep breath, cough, and swat at the air.


"The air's heavier here, damp and warm, like standing too close to a fire giant’s hearth, but without the fire. And there's this faint, rhythmic thumping, like a slow, steady heartbeat echoing through the stone itself, as if the very walls are breathing. Unsettling."


"And these vines... they twitch sporadically, their surfaces slick with a glistening, translucent slime. Spores that glow cling to the tendrils, casting eerie shadows that dance and flicker with every subtle movement. This all makes it hard to tell what's solid and what's just a ghostly figment."


I take a careful step, and as cautious as it is, my boot still makes a faint squelching sound.


The ground beneath my feet is uneven, tangled with root-like growths that seem to reach out and try to snare a boot. And the smell! It's a blend of damp earth, rot, and something faintly metallic. Not the honest smell of rock and ore, that's for sure.


Every step in here feels like walking through a living maze, and these vines seem almost sentient, watching, waiting for a misstep—too much life in a tunnel for my liking. Give me solid stone any day over this… this thing. 

I turn to Grygum and urge caution and quiet. And for a time, it works, and we go through the vine forest. 


Some thirty feet from the exit of this place, Grygum is making too much noise. The vines start to move to close the path forward. If we don’t make it to the exit, we will be trapped in this nightmare. 


We dash, vines try to lash us and miss. We arrive at the edge of this cave and can see behind us the once clear path covered in vines that are moving, pulsing, and crunching. 


I step outside. 


And into another cave. 


Well, this looks like a blighted festering pit, unfit for any living creature, let alone a dwarf. There's a throne, but it's nothing but fungal growths twisted with bleached bones and decaying wood. 


My gaze sweeps across the looming shapes around me. 


"These giant mushrooms... they stand like sentinels, silent and still, but they ain't natural stone. Their caps are mottled with sickly patches of mold, and their stalks are twisted into grotesque shapes that defy any honest growth. Unnatural. Completely unnatural."


"The air itself feels heavy, thick with… something. And the colors here are muted—sickly yellows, dull browns, and shadowed grays. Like the very life has been sucked right out of the rock."


My eyes fix on the spectral figure at the center, a flicker of grim certainty in their depths. 


And there she is. The 'Dark Lady,' the Demon Queen of Fungi, the Lady of Rot and Decay. Shimmering into view like a fever-dream. They talk of beauty and decay, allure and menace, but I see naught but corruption and the promise of a slow, blighted end. She's a demonic power, that's what she is."


Her face is… unsettling. Delicate features, but framed by tendrils of glowing mycelium that pulse with a sickening inner light. Her eyes shine like twin lanterns, radiant yet hollow, drawing you in with a pull that feels more like a cold, hungry grasp on your mind.


I notice her crown, a halo of glowing spores drifting lazily, casting an otherworldly glow across that twisted fungal throne.


I think that these must be the same spores she uses to turn the living into mindless servants, into decomposing hosts.


I take a look at more closely. Her smile is slow and knowing, a curve that speaks of ancient secrets and fathomless power she shouldn't wield. When she moves, it's as if the very air bends around her, and the stench of damp earth and decay follows in her wake. Her voice, when it reaches my mind, is a haunting melody—at once soothing and commanding, like a lullaby sung by a hungry cave bear after it's caught its prey. It promises to 'bloom beyond the bounds of flesh and time,' but that bloom is a perversion, an infection that steals your very self."


This is it, then. The 'heart of the Lady’s dream,' a place where power and corruption are so tightly woven they're inseparable. Every breath here feels heavy with ancient secrets and dark promises of what she offers.


Just a trip, Thorin, just a trip. 


And then she speaks to me with a soft, melodic tone. 


“Welcome... wanderers of flesh and bone…” She pauses. And then continues with the same tone, *“*You stand at the edge... between waking and dreaming… Where life... and decay... entwine in endless dance…” And she pauses again. And I feel fear in my bones and she once again uses that haunting voice, * “*I am the pulse... beneath the stone… The breath... within the spore… To walk my dream... is to touch the heart… Of all that grows... and all that withers…”


She then looks at me with her eyes, “Do you fear the rot... or do you understand its song?”


I think about this for a moment. Grygum reminds me that every deal with a demon is a curse. It gives you power, and it robs you of your understanding of the need to avoid using power. 


The temptation is there, but I don’t know what will happen and it’s too dangerous. 


“Not this time? I have too many responsibilities on the outside.”  


She looks at me, disappointed -  We would bloom beyond the bounds of flesh and high. You could have lingered with me and found your heart entwined with me, forever. As part of the endless mycelium.” 


I can feel that mindless infinite eternity, and then just as much as I feel it, I am awake.


### Daz
Grygym and Thorin wake up. And they don’t appear to be covered in some kind of rotting mycelium, so that’s good. 


Voosbur sighs, “I guess you guys didn’t want to join us. You still can if you want. But hurry.” 


Spores fly out of him, they glow, and then the dancers walk towards the moss on the edge of the cavern and disappear. 


I hold onto Thorin and Grygum, worried that they will follow. But they don’t.
