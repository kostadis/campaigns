# Chapter 20 The Derro like Demogorgon
## Grygum.


The battle was terrifying. But I am alive. Wounded. But alive. So let me do a bit of Divine magic and heal myself. And that friendly dwarf, the one Thorin likes. 


“May the power of the platinum dragon cure us all!” I shout. That’s a bit of theater that I created for the believing. 


I should be honest, it’s theater I created for myself. Bahamut is boring. Stroud was exciting. Bahamut, not so much. 


## Daz


A good rest is always beneficial, but we need to get out of here.


Zalthir, the good old monk, is scouting ahead, keeping us safe. Good for him. 


“Something’s up ahead.” 


I have to assume it’s not good. So I follow Grygum and Thorin around the turn of the tunnel and see a single Derro standing over a pit, with an unbelievable smell emanating from it.  


I look at him carefully.  He  has blue, gray skin and pale eyes that lack both irises and pupils. He has thin, stringy hair, dull white, constantly looking greasy and unkempt. The creature is wearing scraps of tattered leather and cloth stained with grime and the foul odors of wherever he has been working. Now that’s interesting, he seems to be muttering to himself. Bah, it’s a common trait among the irrevocably insane of the Derro. 


Wait, that’s interesting, he’s got an amulet of Demogorgon. 


I stare at him. I wonder is he the true-believing kind that has warped his world view? Or is he just a dummy that needs someone else to think for him and is therefore a true believer?


I look at him carefully to see some spark of genius. I see nothing. He’s a dummy. I recall the stories of the Derro, creatures that served the mind flayers and had their minds destroyed. They are not well in the best of times. 
## Grygum 


I approach the poor creature. And examine what he is doing. 


Well, that’s interesting. He appears to be tossing body parts over the ledge into the pit. 


The Derro looks at me with fear. Good. 


“Don’t mind me, I am just looking to learn about how the big project is progressing. Are you on schedule?” 


“The great project of Ulnara of the Council of Savants!”


“Yes! Is it on schedule?” 


The creature whimpers before me and pleads, “But Pliinki promised Skiit a better job.” 


I smile, “Only if you succeed at this one, Skiit. I see a lot of zombies.” 


Skiit  looks at me and then points at Spiderbait, “Can I kill that one over there?” 


I am worried, but I know how to deflect, just double down on his anxieties, “I am not going to hand you new body parts because you can’t keep up!” 


I tried to intimidate, and failed. Maybe it is my gentleness from all my time at the temple, because instead of this thing cowering, it puffs itself up and says, “I am a follower of Demogorgon! Pliinki has said that anybody who follows Demogorgon is making the Derro proud and powerful. And we’re going to be great again!” 


And it gets worse. 


“I don’t believe you guys work for us. I think you work for the Duergar or the Mindflayers!” 


Seeing the zombies below, they do need some new body parts, and this one Derro, would be a fine addition. 


I push him over the cliff. At the same time, Daz unleashes a light show. 


## Daz


I approach the ledge, and the first thing that hits me is the smell that reeks of death and decomposed flesh. I open my eyes and absorb how enormous it is. The glow of faerzress is everywhere, seeming to flow like fog around dark shapes shambling across the pit. A crack in the southeast wall of the pit leads to a narrow tunnel, flanked by mounds of fungi and offal.


From there, a voice and an Ogre Zombie emerge. 


“What is the meaning of this? Who are you? Who do you think you are?” 


“We are the Ember Guard,” shouts Grygum. I smile. I like that name. 


“Who do you work for?” she shouts. 


I pause and try to answer. However, we have struck numerous deals with various other parties, and I'm no longer sure what to say. 


Grygym answers - "Ah, my friend, you've just asked him a simple question: "So, Grygum, who do you work for, truly?"


He looks at her with a mix of weariness and a peculiar Orcish amusement. "That is a question that requires more than a simple answer. It is, in fact, a Divine Scrutiny, a profound act of investigation into the very threads of our intertwined fates, here in this churning city of Gracklstugh."


I know what will come next: a long, meandering discussion of the nature of the Divine Scrutiny and how a Divine Query is necessary, and how he must consider Aspects of the Truth and that he must consider the Divine Ledgers or Celestial Archives from which the truth will be drawn. 


Or he could just give them an answer. 


Clerics of Bahamut. 


Now let’s take a look at her more closely. She’s the necromancer in charge of this operation. But she’s a cultist of Demogorgon, and she’s a derro, and fear works well for keeping these creatures at bay. 


The Derro named Skiit, which Grygum tossed into the pit, is starting to wake up. I know what to do. 


I cast Maximillian’s Earthen Grasp and start to crush the poor unfortunate creature. 


Ulnara looks at me, and she has that look of someone who thought they had all the cards, and understood what was going on, and realizes she doesn’t anymore. Her arrogance disappears, and her sniveling, subservient persona emerges. 


The transformation is stunning. 


“Your lordship! Or was it the Ember Vanguard? What a wonderful name for such a powerful group. Do not worry, after he’s dead I will turn him into a zombie and he will seve the cult of Demogorgon forever!” 


I snap my fingers, trying to kill him, but the derro escapes. 


So I cast Toll the Dead, and the deadly chimes echo throughout the caves, but only it’s intended target hears them, and his heart stops, and he dies. 


I look at Ulnara, who realizes that I am Drow. And that the Drow do not mess around. 


“Thank you for this new supply! I know you will tell Pliinki that I Ulnar,a did much to help the Ember Vanguard!” 


“We shall, certainly,” says Grygum in his preacher’s voice. 


## Thorin


The caves go on. We arrive at a door, and we rest. Resting is good. 


A little bit later, we arrive at a door. It’s a big door. However, we have a lot of people, so we open it up and continue. 


“Left or right?” I ask. 


The discussion begins, and I am too tired for this, so I say - “East.” 


Ahead, I see what appears to be a break in the tunnel. I stop because the smell is just awful.


Don’t these guys know how to take a bath? 


Seeing how we will overcome the violent urge to vomit, I go closer. The smell is coming from cages assembled from scrap iron on both sides of the chamber. A spiral path circles into the center of the room, marked off by small stone pylons.
.
I notice two Derro in front of a tent, near a tent. Both are talking while ignoring each other. They are not well. 


“ More of this, always mor,e the mastery demands. Many contributions, the prince is hungry, always hungry,” says one. 


The other intones as it stares intently into the murky bubbling cauldron, tracing an invisible pattern with a dirty finger, “Water it chips, not like the dark lake. There, I pulled something from the dark ones, A scale smooth, this polished obsidian, but it shimmered like madness. In the dim light came from the deep, the deepest part with voices seeing.”


Grygum mutters, “They are mad.”


Zalthir asks - “Do we need to fight them?”


I squint at the tents and the room and say, “No. There is nothing of value here.” 


We agree and move on. 


And so we continue on our journey through the caves, when a Derro patrol stumbles on us. 


Daz insists we march as if we own this place. And it works. The creatures are fearful and somewhat deranged. 


Just a few more yards and we will be past them. 


But Grygum notices something, and he can’t keep quiet. 


“I worship Bahamut!” 


The moment that Grygum mentioned "Bahamut," I knew we were in for it. These Derro, bless their simple, deranged minds, don't take kindly to anything outside their two-headed deity. And sure enough, they immediately attack. 


Grygum shouts, “I thought we could have a deep Phylosophical conversation!” 


I roll my eyes. 


The Derro are Dwarves that were slaves of the mindflayers, who corrupted their fundamental nature until they are barely Dwarves. They are mad. These are not creatures to reason with. 


I moved forward, my heavy armor clanking, but I ignored the sound. My focus was on the nearest Derro. He was a scrawny one, probably thought he was tough. I swung my longsword, a satisfying *thwack* as it connected. "Take that, you blithering fool!" I yelled, though I'm sure it was lost in the chaos. A hit, but not enough to drop him, but enough to know he'd messed with the wrong dwarf. 


The Derro themselves were predictably clumsy. One swung at me, but my armor held. The other landed a hit on Grygum, but our allies were quick to retaliate, dealing a good chunk of damage to him. Zalthir, bless his arcane heart, summoned his darkness, enveloping a couple of them. Then, in a burst of brilliant speed, he critically struck one of the Darrow. Impressive.


Daz, always quick with a spell, launched his magic missiles. A flurry of arcane energy streaked across the tunnel, and the DerroI had struck, already reeling, and fell. One down. Good.


The remaining Derro, true to their limited intellect, continued their mindless attacks. One swung wildly at Zalthir, missing entirely. Another charged Grygum, screaming some incoherent babble about their master. My armor held, and I parried his pathetic attack. "One head is better than two!" I roared, pushing him back.


My chance came again, and I didn't hesitate. I brought my longsword down hard on the Derro, attacking Grygum. The sword cut through his flimsy armor and into his chest, and it dropped like a sack of rocks. Dead. "This is how the Bahamut walks!" I declared, standing over his fallen form, ready to take on the next fool who dared challenge us.


But they were all dead. 
## Daz


Our journey continues until we reach another cave entrance. And this one we must go through. 


And yes, the unspeakable stench that seems to follow the Derro emanates here. This time it is the smell of brimstone and chemicals.


The Ember Vanguard will march in, but we tell our companions to wait outside. Jim Jar, Spiderbait, Sarith, Eldeth, and Stool look at me worried, but I motion for them to stay. 


I walk in quietly. We don’t need light, because we can all see in the dark. 


As I walk in, I can see the light of campfires. 


Grygum shouts, “Watch it!” 


I look down at what he is pointing at, and it’s a poorly disguised pit trap. Whatever is here is at least trying to keep out outsiders. 


Further within, the cavern is cast in an eerie, hellish red glow that swirls into spiral patterns on the walls, creating dancing jet shadows. The air is muted, and the dripping water makes no echo, as if one stood in open air. Fungi grow rampantly in large patches on the floors and walls, adding to the oppressive atmosphere.


And I finally spot the inhabitants, some Derro and Death dogs.


They don’t notice us, so we continue. 


The cavern floor rises to form a natural 5-foot-high platform. A glowing ritual circle dominates the top of the platform. Around the platform are books made from Trillimac. I look carefully and notice a few of the titles - “The Spiraling Descent: A Treatise on Duality and Madness,” and “The Prince's Gift: On the Creation of Favored Spawn”, and “Rituals of the Two-Headed Beast.” 


Errde Blackstaff wants evidence of Demogorgon’s influence, and here it is. And amongst the books are various papers. Knowing these creatures, there is probably enough evidence to convince Errde of whatever she wants. 


I might not want to give it to her. But I know her type; if she doesn’t get evidence, she’ll invent it. 


Just then, one of them picks up a book and starts to read from it. 


Another asks, “Narrack, what are we doing?” 


I look at this Derro, who seems to be the orchestrator of the disturbing scene before me. This is Narrak. His pale, derro features are perpetually contorted, twisting into a grotesque mask of terrifying, spiraling madness. His eyes, perhaps unnervingly bright in the dim light, dart about with a frenetic, almost desperate energy.


He gestures wildly at a few apprentices. There’s an undeniable, warped intellect simmering beneath his frantic movements, but it is utterly consumed by the chaotic evil that seems to radiate from his very being. His presence in this space is deeply unsettling, a chilling testament to the insidious influence of the demon lord he serves. 


He shouts - “We are conducting experiments!” 


They chant back at him - “For what purpose!” 


He screams - “The only purpose that matters! The elevation of the Derro, the Triumph of Demogorgon, and the destruction of the Duergar!” 


## Zalthir


I observe everything and say nothing. My mind is clear. I act without speaking. I do because I know.
