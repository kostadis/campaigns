# Chapter 19 The Ember Vanguard

## Zalthir

He stands in the darkness. He hears nothing. Then he hears a shout. 

“Is everyone okay?” 

Zalthir recognizes the voice; it’s Eldeth.

Thorin shouts, “Yes, we’re all fine.” 

Zalthir sighs and dispels the darkness. 

Daz shouts, “I have the egg.” 

And then an unfamiliar voice of a Derro shouts, “My goodness, this body is uncomfortable.” 

Zalthir tenses. 

“Oh, yes, I am Pelek. I seem to have occupied this body.” 

“The ghost,” he thinks. 

The monk looks around. The upper levels of the mesa are covered in blood and the broken bodies of the derro. Where Eldeth is, near the tunnel entrance is a spectator, a creature similar to but different from a beholder. 

He looks to where the egg should be. There is no egg. There are two dead derro. 

Grygum shouts, “Where is the egg?” 

Then Daz shouts back, “I have it.” 

Zalthir jumps off the mesa and heads to an alcove in the northeast corner. There, he picks up the statue of Demogorgon. 

“Leemoogoogon, Demorgogon, two-headed baby dragons \- …” he thinks to himself.

While Daz and Pelek exchange pleasantries, and Grygum searches the bodies he heads to the northwest corner where there is a desk with some papers. 

He finds a journal by Pliinki. 

Pliinki's Journal

Here are some journal entries from the perspective of Pliinki, detailing her macabre experiments and fervent beliefs:

### Pliinki's Journal

**Day 17 – The Stone of Diirinka**

The Obelisk. Such a magnificent, fractured thing\! Hidden here by Diirinka himself, I am certain. Its black metal hums with quasi-magical energy, a power just waiting to be unleashed. I have tried the Thrazgad ore, as Narrak suggested, to mend its cracks, but it merely sits "discarded and forgotten" next to my workbenches. Foolish duergar, not to understand the true purpose of this ancient power\! It will conquer Gracklstugh, just as I plan. Today, I touched it with a spark of my own will, and it flashed\! A mere flicker, but a sign\! A squeal of glee escaped me. Soon, my Prince, soon.

**Day 25 – The Prince's Gift Arrives**

The Gray Ghosts delivered it\! The Red Dragon Egg\! What a marvel\! The Keepers are in a panic over its loss, but they don't understand the true glory that awaits this "replacement". This is no mere insurance policy; it is the vessel for my greatest creation, a gift worthy of Demogorgon himself. The Deep King will be "delighted" when he sees it, though he knows nothing yet of its true destiny. My work begins at once. The notes are spread, the circles drawn.

**Day 38 – Mutagenic Progress\!**

The experiments are progressing gloriously\! The egg pulses with new life, and my notes confirm my suspicions: the mutations are taking hold. I've surrounded it with the finest reagents: hair, scales, fungi from the tunnels, and... other, more precious viscera. My goal is clear, a two-headed wyrmling, a perfect "Prince's gift" for Demogorgon, to serve as his mount. Grygum, the cleric of Bahamut, spoke of "freedom of choice" for dragons, but what he doesn't understand is that true choice lies in serving the Prince of Demons. The transformation will make it *perfect*.

**Day 47 – The Obelisk Responds**

I returned to the Obelisk today, feeling the madness and corruption of the city seep into my very being. The constant war between the Keepers and the Council, Gartokkar's paranoia, Errde's "conspiracy theories" – it's all part of the spiral. I pressed my hand to the Obelisk again, channeling the energy of the Whorlstone Tunnels into its fractures. It didn't just flash this time; the very air around it flared\! It hungers for more power, more chaos\! This monolith will be the instrument of our ascension, allowing us to conquer Gracklstugh and deliver it as a sacrificial gift to Demogorgon.

**Day 54 – Anticipation and Destiny**

The egg is nearing its time\! Weeks, they say, until it hatches. I can already feel the dual-headed presence forming within. My "creation" for Demogorgon will soon be revealed. The Obelisk, too, senses the coming shift. Its purpose, alongside the egg, is to herald the Prince's arrival and the "genocidal war" that will cleanse this city. All the pieces are falling into place. The path forward is clear, and it is glorious.

Zalthir knows he has enough to condemn the Derro and Duergar to a genocidal war that will result in the utter destruction of the city of Gracklstugh. 

“But is that the right thing to do?” he wonders. 

## Grygum 

Grygum reads what Zalthir has found and is disturbed. And the egg disturbs him further. As he sits there, he remembers some text from the temple where he learned of the power of Bahamut \- 

*"When the egg, crimson-hued, promises but a shadow's ride, does one mend the shell, or seek to free the spark within? The true wyrm's form is not etched by fate's first breath, nor by sorcerous hand. It is the choices that twist the spirit, or set it soaring. To break the chains of another's dark design, even when madness fuels the crafting, is to affirm the very freedom Bahamut cherishes.”* 

He scratches his head. He remembers how Bahamut’s clerics never gave him a straight answer. 

The egg is no longer a dragon. It is a physical manifestation of a demon. The egg should be destroyed. 

“We shall return the egg to Gartokkar,” he declares.   
   
Zalthir stares at him, confused, “What?”

“This is no longer a dragon. And so when this abomination hatches, the dragon Themberchaude will have its revenge on those who would think to control it.” 

\*rattle\* 

Grygum stands at attention and turns to the rattling sound.

There is Thorin with a baby rattle. A giant baby rattle that looks like it would fit a dragon. 

Thorin shouts, “Look what I found.” 

Grygum approaches him, “This is a baby dragon rattle. How curious.” 

He examines it further, “It says made by clan Thrazgad.” 

“How curious. Why would they make a baby rattle for a dragon? Why would this rattle be here?” 

Thorin rattles it some more, “We should get a baby dragon. I would like to have a dragon. It would be so cool if we had a dragon.” 

Grygum is appalled. Zalthir is a dragonborn. And Dragonborn are considered, by some, a draconic abomination.   
   
Grygum coughs. He watches as Thorin stares at him and realizes what he said, “I’m sorry. I didn’t mean that we don’t have a pure dragon, and that I wanted a pure dragon.” He pauses, “Okay, that sounded worse. I am going to stop now.” 

Grygum watches Zalthir clench and unclench.  

## Thorin

“So what do we do? I am still partial to the ride Thermbechaude out of this place.” 

But the loss of Buppido, and the death of the derro, and the destruction of the dragon in the egg, and the near death of Eldeth have made the idea of leaving Gracklstugh a smouldering inferno less appealing. 

Zalthir mutters, “I vote we get out of here as discreetly as possible, creating the least amount of chaos as possible.” 

Thorin sighs, “Yeah.” 

Grygum pulls out a scrap of paper, “I’ve been taking notes on all of the deals we have made. And it seems to me that the best choice we have is to give the egg to Gartokkar and then use him to escape from Gracklstugh. We tell Gartokkar that the Derro didn’t steal the egg, but some other crazed group. We also explain that we need his help to escape because of the deal we made with the Stone guards and that if Errde were to imprison us, we might be compelled to change our story under torture.” 

Thorin stares at him. 

“I take notes. It’s what I do.” 

Thorin nods, “Let’s do it.” 

And so they are ready to leave the chamber of the Obelisk. 

But Thorin stops them. “We need a name.” 

Everyone stares at him.

“A what?” asks Zalthir.

“A name\! Like the First Faction was the legendary heroes that saved the North\! And then there are the legendary heroes of the War of the Giants, the Three Half Pints and the Pint (3HPP)\! We need a name\!” 

There is a moment of silence. 

Grygum mutters, “The Second Faction?” 

Thorin looks at him and arches an eyebrow, but Daz says what he is thinking, “Too derivative.” 

And then he smiles, “I have it \- The Ember Vanguard.” 

Thorin looks around and notices that Zalthir is nodding, Daz is smiling, and even Grygum seems pleased. 

“The Ember Vanguard, Onwards\!” 

The groan from his companions is noticeable. But he doesn’t care. From the cave entrance, they choose not to circle back the way they came. As Zalthir explains, “The Empty Scabbard Killers might want to kill us.” 

Thorin agrees, so they go down a different cave tunnel, which leads to a pair of double doors. 

In front of those double doors is a small squad of derro. 

“Do we attack?” 

Daz turns to him, “No.”  

## Diirdeklin the Derro

Diirdeklin has been at this guard post for days. Pliinki has promised him that this is part of some plan that will free the Derro from the bondage of Duergar. But Diirdeklin has heard that before. 

There is a noise coming from the north. That’s odd. It doesn’t sound like Derro. He’s worried. 

The creatures are advancing, as if they own this place. Well, he’ll show them. 

He reaches for the alarm. And then he hears a booming voice \- “The people you are trying to warn are dead.” 

Diirdeklin’s hand drops the alarm bell and grabs his weapon more tightly. 

It’s then that he sees a Drow Archmage who has summoned a meteor swarm. This is no ordinary group of mercenaries; this is a powerful raid by the Drow of Menzoberranzan. 

He stops. He just wants to live. 

The Drow shouts, “We are The Ember Vanguard, and you will let us pass\!” 

Diirdeklin worried that Diinarnnmyr might foolishly try to fight,   shouts, “Yes, pass\!”

And as they leave the cave, he thinks, “A Drow incursion\! This is not good. And if they killed Pliinki, that means that they have bigger plans.” 

As they leave Diinarnnmyr mutters, “The Ember Vanguard \- the Drow are now working with the Duergar to subdue us\!” 

## Daz

Daz is very pleased with his use of dancing lights and the effect it had on the Derro. His friends congratulate him. 

Thorin shouts, “The Ember Vanguard are in the house\!” 

The mage likes the idea of being a feared group of mercenaries instead of a pathetic group of adventurers who are running around trying to stay alive. 

He has found purpose. He is the feared mage of the Ember Vanguard\!  

Once past the doors, Thorin leads them west, instead of south, where the Empty Scabbard Killers live. 

After walking in the enormous 20-foot-wide cavern for a few minutes, Thorin hears a yelp. 

He turns around and sees Grygum enveloped by a creature that looks like a manta ray. 

Grygum shouts in pain. 

Daz ponders his spell list and has some intriguing ideas, but realizes that the old standby, Magic Missile, is probably the best bet. 

He watches as Zalthir and Thorin attack the creature, trying to get it to release Grygum.

Daz grits his teeth as he hears Grygum’s muffled sounds of pain and watches as the Orc can’t escape. 

So he casts silvery barbs to help Grygum escape, and then Daz follows up with the deadly chimes of the spell toll the dead. 

Zalthir strikes the creature a few more times with his fist, and the beast dies before Grygum can take advantage of the spell. 

But the Orc is grateful, but he looks unhappy as he is covered in the acid slime of the Trapper.
