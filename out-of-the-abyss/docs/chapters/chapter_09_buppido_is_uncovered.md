# Chapter 09 Buppido is uncovered! 
### 1st day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493


Grygum mutters, “I am so upset that the person I have known for a day is dead!” 


Spiderbait screams at Jimjar, “Give me money. I am leaving now!” 


Jimjar quiets him down and reassures him that he’s more likely to live if he stays around. 


Zalthir, whose pleasant dream about what Monks dream about was interrupted, looks around, trying to figure out why everyone is in an uproar. Then, hearing the screaming, he stands to attention, ready to kill. 


“Are we under attack?” 


Shuushar whispers, “No. Yukyuk was murdered, and Spiderbait is very upset.” 


Grygum looks around and says, “Well, we have no other option but to continue and be alert.” 


But Daz, who finds mysteries to be interesting, thinks otherwise. “I want to figure out what happened.”


First, he questions Spiderbait. 


“Would anyone want him dead?” 


“Yukyuk was the kindest, gentlest goblin you could imagine. He and I were pals.”


“Did he have anything of value?” 


“Other than the kindest heart, no.” 
     
Then Spiderbait glares, “I’ll tell you who I think it is - Shuushar. Kuo-toan’s go mad. And when they go mad, they snap, and then they kill people!” 


Daz turns to Zalthir, Grygum, and Thorin - “What do we know? Buppido always talks about the divine plan. Sethir seems a bit off. Topsy and Turvy are lycanthropes. Jimjar makes bets all the time. Eldeth is trying to keep it together and make sure we all arrive in one piece. And Shuushar claims to have figured out the cure to Kuo-toan insanity.” 


Just then, Shuushar approaches them and says, “ I propose we head to Sloopbludop, where we can meet the arch-cleric of the Seamother, who can cast the zone of truth, and we can learn what happened.”
Grygum mutters, “That’s the easy way out. Let’s do it the hard way by applying intelligence, deduction, insight, and perception.” 


Daz shrugs, “I guess I could ask Buppido.” 


“Do you know what happened?” 


Buppido looks at Daz and says, “This was all part of the plan!” 


But Daz knows something’s off. 


“Sounds like a non-answer.” 


Buppido sweats, “Yukyuk’s death was necessary for the divine plan.” 


“Who was responsible for his death?” 


Buppido tries to evade the question, “Who can say? I cannot be held accountable for the divine plan. 


Daz gets annoyed and pushes past Buppido. He examines his equipment and notices that the shortsword may have blood on it from a victim. 


Buppido tries to protest that he’s not a maid, and that’s why it’s got blood on it. 


Daz is fed up, so he casts a mind-sliver on the derro, stares him in the eye, and makes it clear that it could get a lot worse. 


“Ouch!! That hurt. It wasn’t me! It was the agent of the divine plan.” 


Grygum looks at him and says, “That’s what they all say.” 


“It’s not my fault,” Buppido whimpers. 


Another mind-sliver is cast, and the derro whimpers even more. 


Sarith walks up to Daz and says, “That’s an excellent torturing technique. However, I have other more effective ones that I was taught as a member of Lolth’s guard. We could remove his eyes, nails, and other things.” 


Daz looks at him, somewhat annoyed but somewhat pleased: “I am not torturing him; I am providing motivation.” 


Zalthir perks up, “Motivation?” He thinks that in his many years, he had never heard anyone refer to torture as motivation. “Perhaps I should reconsider my prohibition of it.” 


Daz turns to Buppido, “So?” 


“It wasn’t me.” 


Daz stares at him, wondering if he is mentally ill or just different. He has had enough experience with the judgmental overbrighters who assume all Drow are evil to be willing to give everyone a chance. But he realizes that this isn’t someone who has a different perspective; Buppido is mad. 


“Let’s cut him loose.” 


Shuushar says, “No, no. I want us to tie him up. I can try my techniques of mental sanity on him as well!” 


Buppido looks genuinely horrified. 


Daz turns to the derro, “We are going to tie you up, and if you can’t keep quiet, gag you.” 


A few minutes later, a gagged Buppido walks next to Shuushar, who talks endlessly about this technique called Kuo-toan-fulness. The goal is to create a space between the moment an impulse comes to your mind and the moment you take an action, and in that moment, wonder if that impulse is a reaction to the real world or just a response in your mind. 


Buppido pleads for a mercy killing, but all that everyone can hear is the muffled moans.
