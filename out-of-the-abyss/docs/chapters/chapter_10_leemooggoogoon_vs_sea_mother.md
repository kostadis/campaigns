# Chapter 10 Leemooggoogoon vs Sea Mother! 


An hour or so later, the group is ambushed by eight kuo-toans—four creatures attack from the front and four from the rear. 


At first, the escapees are worried that they have met their end, especially when they see a Kuo-Toan monitor. A Kuo-Toan monitor is a special kind of Kuo-Toan leader who provides structured organization over a Kuo-Toan group. Shuushar explains that the monitor’s primary role is to deal with any sudden break of insanity. And that when the monitor is defeated, the Kuo-toans are less likely to keep fighting. 


During the fight, the group discovers that the body of the Kuo-toans releases a particular liquid that causes any melee weapon to stick to their bodies. Only the application of strength can from a weapon from the glue.


In the end, they realize that a collection of hand-held crossbows, combined with a united team, can create a powerful enough force to take down even a formidable group of enemies.  


Three of the Kuo-toans flee into the Underdark, including the monitor. 
Shuushar sighs, “Such an unnecessary battle. Why would this be happening?” 


Sethir mutters, “Should we continue?” 


“Yes,” the Shuushar the Awakened says. 


An hour from Sloopbludop, another kuo-toan party appears. This time, Shuushar is very excited and starts talking to one of them. 


Just then, his interlocutor casts a spell, and suddenly, not only is their language clear, but also the nuances of their communication and body language. 


“This is Ploopploopeen, archpriest of the Seamother.” 


Grygum finds the name very funny and tries not to laugh out loud.  


The archpriest is excited and says, “The Seamother has answered my prayers!” 


It’s then that the fish origins of the Kuo-toans become apparent. Ploopploopeen has come with other members of her kind, and as she talks, she walks around. They follow her like a school of fish, walking here and there in circles. 


The effect is surreal because, like in a school of fish, the other Kuo-toans will go in front of her, around her, and stay in the back. And yet, it all seems very well choreographed. Shuushar follows her as she walks around, yet doesn’t always stay next to her, even as she seems to be talking to him. 


Ploopploopeen explains that the inhabitants of Sloobludop have lived in harmonious service to the will of the Sea Mother for some time. There were occasional “visionaries” who stirred up trouble (at this, he might cast a walleyed glance at Shuushar) but nothing of any great concern. A few weeks ago, Bloppblippodd, Ploopploopeen’s daughter, experienced a powerful vision of “Leemooggoogoon the Deep Father,” proclaiming him the new god of her people. She has backed up her claims with a tremendous increase in magical power, and new followers have flocked to her.


“We are split in two,” Ploopploopeen explains, “fighting among ourselves.” The followers of the Deep Father have been making an increasing number of offerings on his altar — killing blood sacrifices then casting bloody chum into the waters of the Darklake, where it is consumed by … something.”


A bit bored, Topsy asks, “I hear about your problems, and that sounds like a you problem, not like a me problem. What can we help with you?” 


“Oh that’s easy. I wish to use you as bait.” 


Zalthir looks at her, unimpressed. 


The archpriest of the Sea Mother explains he wants to use them as bait. The archpriest of the Deep Father needs humanoid sacrifices, and the party represents a prize collection in that regard. Ploopploopeen will offer them as a “token of peace and reconciliation” to get closer to the archpriest of the Deep Father — except the adventurers will not be helpless prisoners but infiltrators. Ploopploopeen wants them to disrupt the upstart faction’s rituals, allowing his true believers of the Sea Mother to cut off the head of the cult. The archpriest promises that the characters will be “well rewarded” for their aid.


Grygum mutters, “Oh great, pit one weird religion against another!” 


Ploopploopeen is offended. “The Seamother is not a weird religion. It is the traditional faith of the Kuo-toans! It is Leemooggoogoon the Deep Father that is the real evil!” 


Daz interrupts the archpriest, “We need to have a quick chat amongst ourselves.” 


The escapees huddle in a group. Daz mutters, “If Ploopploo-whatever is going to keep talking endlessly like that, maybe we should kill them?” 


Shuushar is aghast, “No, no, no. The Sea Mother is an old god, the Deep Father, that is who we should fight against. The Deep Mother demands humanoid sacrifices, not the Sea Mother.” 


Daz is unmoved, “I don’t want to listen to this drivel all the time.” 


Shuushar thinks momentarily, “Perhaps we should talk to his daughter before we take action? I have learned that one must open themselves to all points of view and then only then make decisions and judgments.” 


Shuushar continues to drone on but thankfully walks away. 


Zalthir shouts to the archpriest, “You said reward?” 


Grygum adds, “I am all about the spiritual stuff, but the temporal have needs too.” 


The archpriest promises 500 gold and two potions of healing. But she sees how they are unimpressed, “Two boats and guides to navigate the Darklake?” 


The escapees nod in agreement. 


Daz asks, “How many members does the other faction have?” 


“Do not worry yourself about that. All you have to do is distract Bloppblippodd enough. I will take care of her, and my followers will take care of the followers of this false god.”
