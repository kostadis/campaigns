# Chapter 43 The Fall of the Pudding King

## Summary

The party stood at the entrance to the throne room, having breached the walls with the help of an Earth Elemental that carved a passage through the stone. As they entered, Zalthir made a bold move to grapple the Pudding King, hoping to seize him and end his reign of terror. But to their horror, the Pudding King transformed before their eyes, shedding his humanoid form and becoming a sentient, telepathic ooze—a manifestation of his complete embrace of Juiblex, the demon lord of oozes. His telepathic voice screamed through their minds, declaring his intention to reduce all of the Underdark into nothing but ooze, though the party quickly asked him to lower his mental volume.

Despite the Pudding King's new form, Zalthir maintained his grapple, wrestling with the acidic creature as it oozed around him. The party discussed using their Bag of Holding to contain the ooze, but realized that while they could temporarily shove him inside to deprive him of action, a sentient ooze could simply ooze back out through the opening. The throne room itself proved treacherous, with green slime hanging from the ceiling ready to drop on anyone who passed beneath it, and the Pudding King's lair actions giving him additional power within his domain.

The battle began in earnest as Daz launched magical missiles at the Pudding King while Prince Livid, one of the ooze king's allies, retaliated with psychic blasts. Thorin carefully navigated around the green slime hazards, and Princess Ebonmire hurled blobs of acidic ooze at the party. The Pudding King then unleashed a devastating slime wave that crashed over multiple party members, forcing them to dive out of the way or be knocked prone and covered in corrosive slime. Those who fell were further assaulted by green slime dropping from above, though most managed to roll clear of the worst effects.

Recognizing that the Pudding King's lair was giving him too much advantage, Zalthir made a tactical decision that would prove crucial. Using his monk abilities to dash at incredible speed while maintaining his grapple, he dragged the struggling ooze out of the throne room entirely. In a bizarre moment, he pulled the Pudding King directly through Glabbagool, their gelatinous cube companion, who remarked that it tickled. Zalthir took damage from being inside Glabbagool's acidic body, but his magical protections helped him resist the worst of it.

As Zalthir passed through the body, he thought that Glabbagool should have the mental discipline to control his body and how the acidity affected anyone inside. He took a mental note to suggest that Glabbagool begin the training to become a monk.

As the battle continued, something strange occurred that threw the party into confusion. When Thorin struck what he thought was the Pudding King with his darts, a familiar voice cried out asking why they were hurting him. The party quickly realized that somehow, impossibly, Glabbagool and the Pudding King had swapped positions or bodies. Thorin tested this by asking where they first met, and the ooze near him responded that they had met in a cave—confirming it was indeed their friendly gelatinous cube ally. The real Pudding King was now a Gelatinous Cube and Glabbagool's was an ooze.

Once the confusion was sorted out, the party coordinated a devastating assault. Daz channeled powerful magical energy into missiles that unerringly struck the Pudding King, while Grygum called upon dark powers to inflict necrotic wounds that caused the ooze to writhe in pain. The Pudding King fought back desperately, unleashing more slime waves and trying to use his lair's green slime against them, but he had been separated from his throne room, and his power was waning. Thorin struck with his radiant sword Dawnbringer, its holy light searing through the corrupted ooze.

Zalthir delivered the final assault, teleporting through shadows to close the distance and unleashing a flurry of strikes enhanced by eldritch energy channeling through his tentacles. Each blow caused the Pudding King's form to destabilize further, his ooze body unable to maintain cohesion under the relentless assault. With a final telepathic scream—"Behold the true feast, the soul's final blend\!"—the Pudding King dissolved completely, his consciousness dissipating as his physical form melted into inert puddles.

With their leader defeated, Prince Livid and Princess Ebonmire fought on, but they stood no chance against the party's combined might. Daz called down a massive explosion of fire that engulfed both ooze creatures, though Prince Livid's resistance to flames allowed him to survive with grievous injuries. The party quickly finished off both minions, with Princess Ebonmire falling to a combination of necrotic magic and physical strikes, while Prince Livid was destroyed by another volley of magical missiles.

As the last enemy fell, the party felt a palpable shift in the atmosphere around them. The toxic, oppressive presence of Juiblex's demonic power began to recede from the stones and air, seeping away like water draining from a basin. However, they noticed that the fungi growing throughout the cavern were becoming more twisted and malevolent in appearance, suggesting that Zuggtmoy, the demon queen of fungi, was already moving to fill the power vacuum left by the Pudding King's defeat. A demonic voice echoed through the chamber, possibly Juiblex himself, declaring that he would be reborn and consume the banquet of the queen of fungi to grow even stronger.

Glabbagool, now an Ooze, seemed remarkably cheerful about the entire experience. He explained that being a sentient gelatinous cube had always been somewhat inconvenient—he moved slowly and people had to wait for him constantly. But now, having experienced being a smaller, more mobile ooze, he saw new possibilities. He suggested enthusiastically that the party could carry him in a jar or even in their Bag of Holding, allowing him to travel with them and see the world while serving as the ultimate garbage disposal. The party discussed the logistics of this arrangement and even mentioned beginning his training as a monk, teaching him to control his form with precision.

Searching the aftermath of the battle, the party discovered treasure that had been consumed by Princess Ebonmire—a spellbook containing numerous magical formulas that would greatly expand their arcane capabilities. More importantly, they had achieved their primary objective: securing a way out of the Underdark. Though the demonic forces of the Abyss still churned in the depths below them, and though Zuggtmoy waited to claim what Juiblex had lost, the party had won a significant victory and earned their ticket to escape the endless darkness beneath the world.

#### Zalthir considers using a Bag of Holding to contain the ooze form of the Pudding King, leading to a discussion of the item's mechanics.

## NPCs

### Pudding King

A former humanoid who transformed into a sentient, telepathic ooze after embracing his connection to Juiblex, the demon lord of oozes. He commanded an army of oozes and possessed powerful abilities including slime waves and green slime attacks. He was grappled and dragged out of his lair by Zalthir, and at one point swapped positions with Glabbagool. He was ultimately defeated and dissolved, screaming 'Behold the true feast, the soul's final blend\!' as he died.

### Glabbagool

An intelligent gelatinous cube who was previously affected by demonic power. During the battle, he somehow swapped positions with the Pudding King, causing confusion for the party. After the Pudding King's defeat, Glabbagool returned to his original form and expressed excitement about potentially being carried in a jar or Bag of Holding, viewing his new mobility as an opportunity to experience the world differently. He sees himself as a potential garbage disposal and is interested in monk training.

### Prince Livid

An ooze ally of the Pudding King who attacked with psychic blasts. He was resistant to fire damage, which allowed him to survive Daz's initial Fireball with only eight hit points remaining. He was ultimately killed by Daz's Magic Missile.

### Princess Ebonmire

An ooze ally of the Pudding King who attacked by throwing blobs and charging at the party. She was killed by the party's combined efforts, and after her death, a spellbook containing many spells was found in her stomach.

### Juiblex

The demon lord of oozes, whose power was channeled through the Pudding King. After the Pudding King's defeat, his infernal influence receded from the area, but a demonic voice claimed he would be reborn by consuming the Queen of Fungi.

### Zuggtmoy

The Lady of Rot and Demon Lord of Fungi. After Juiblex's defeat, the fungi in the area became more twisted, suggesting she was waiting to seize power in the vacuum left by the Pudding King's death.

## Locations

### Throne Room

The Pudding King's throne room, accessed through a hole created by an Earth Elemental. The room contains green slime hazards on the ceiling that can drop on those beneath them. It served as the Pudding King's lair, granting him special lair actions until he was dragged out of it.

## Items

### Bag of Holding

A magical bag capable of storing vast amounts of items and potentially creatures. The party discussed using it to contain the Pudding King, but the DM ruled that while it could temporarily deprive an ooze of an action, a sentient ooze could 'ooze out' of it and escape. Glabbagool later expressed interest in being carried in it.

### Dawnbringer

A magical weapon wielded by Thorin that emits light in a twenty-foot radius and deals radiant damage. It was used to attack the Pudding King after the party realized he had swapped positions with Glabbagool.

### Spellbook

A spellbook containing many spells, found in Princess Ebonmire's stomach after her death. It significantly expanded the party's magical repertoire.

## Spells

### Magic Missile

A reliable force damage spell cast by Daz multiple times during the battle. It does not require a roll to hit and has a range of 120 feet. Daz used it at both first and third level, with the third-level version dealing twenty points of damage to the Pudding King. The final Magic Missile killed Prince Livid.

### Guiding Bolt

A spell cast by Grygum early in the battle that missed its target.

### Stone Skin

A temporary magical effect that grants resistance to a chosen damage type for one round, usable once per long rest. Multiple party members used it to resist acid damage from the Pudding King's slime wave attacks.

### Inflict Wounds

A necrotic damage spell cast by Grygum that requires the target to make a constitution saving throw. It was used successfully against the Pudding King for twenty-three points of damage and against Princess Ebonmire for eighteen points of damage.

### Fireball

A powerful area-of-effect spell cast by Daz that deals fire damage in a large area. It dealt twenty-nine points of damage to both Prince Livid and Princess Ebonmire, though Prince Livid's fire resistance reduced his damage to fourteen points.

### Shadow Step

A monk ability used by Zalthir to teleport short distances, allowing him to quickly close the gap to enemies. He used it to reach the Pudding King and Princess Ebonmire during combat.

### Second Wind

An ability used by Thorin to heal himself during the battle.

### Eldritch Claw

An ability used by Zalthir that adds force damage to his melee attacks, manifesting as damage from his tentacles. Each hit adds 1d6 force damage.

### Revivify

A resurrection spell mentioned by Zalthir as a potential option to help Glabbagool, though it was not cast as Glabbagool was not dead.
