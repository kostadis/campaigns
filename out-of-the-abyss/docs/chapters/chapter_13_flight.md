# Chapter 13 Flight.
## 2nd day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh


Demogorgon roars. And while Leemoogoogoon’s followers cheer in ecstasy, the followers of the Deep Sea Mother are either terrified, driven mad, or planning to stand their ground and fight the demon lord. 


It's too much for Daz, who collapses in tears and moans. 


“We need to get out of here,” mutters Zalthir. 


He’s about to run when he notices that in addition to Daz, Eldeth, Topsy, Turvy, and Shuushar are also screaming and wailing. 


“To the boat?” he overhears someone say. 


The way out on the ground means gEttin g through the mobs of Kuotoans in the village of Sloopbludop. And even if they get through the Kuotoans, Demogorgon is advancing in that direction.  


If they can get to the boat, they may slip past Demogorgon. 


But what about his friends and allies? 


Fortunately, the group figures it out. 


Jimjar picks up Daz, Thorin grabs Eldeth, Topsy and Turvy are carried by Grygum, and Spiderbait carries Shuushar. 


The group arrives at a dock where several Kuotoan monitors stand, guarding. 


Grygum advances boldly and tells them that the Archpriest of the Sea Mother commands them to help the rest of their community.


The Monitors seem relieved to have a purpose and rush off.  


And with that, they board the boat and float away, paddling quietly not to be heard. 


From a distance, they can see Demogorgon destroy the town of Sloopbludop—the screams of the Kuotoans echo in the darkness. 


Shuushar, finally recovered from the sudden appearance of the demon lord, mutters, “Enlightmnet is necessary! We must change as a people. Our imagination brought into existence, Leemoogoogoon.” 


Eldeth mutters, “That was Demogorgon. He’s in the prime material plane. That is not possible.” 


Thorin stares at her, “I am not sure what you think is or is not possible, but I saw what I saw.” 


As the boat floats on the lake, the puzzle pieces all click in Thorin’s head. 


But it’s Grygum who says it out loud - “The spectator warned us about demons. And we saw the demons in Velkynvelve. Demons have made it to the prime material plane in large numbers!” 


Daz reflects on his knowledge of the arcane and his time in the Underdark, “Demons do not just appear in the Underdark. They must be summoned. And the spell to summon a demon lord is some mighty magic.” 


Spiderbait wails, “This is so not good. The Underdark isn’t a friendly place! And rampaging demons and demon lords!” 


Eldeth concurs, “The danger to all of Faerun is undeniable. Even if we escape the Underdark, these demons won’t stay down here. And to think someone summoned demon lords! We need to get a warning to the surface worlds. Dwarven miners are at risk!” 


Sathir mutters, “Where to next?” 


Buppido, who is no longer gagged, says, “Gracklstugh! I know people.” 


Thorin stares at him, undecided. Grygum, however, helps the group decide, “We need better equipment, and we now have the money to buy it.” 


Buppido sighs, “Yes, and I know people who can help you.” 


Jimjar looks at him, “Wanna bet?” 


A few days pass in relative quiet. Shuushar acts as a guide and navigator, pointing out where they can find water to drink and food to eat. 
## 4th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493
### Daz 


Standing on the front of the boat, Daz looks into the darkness. His eyes can see the water and the cave above him. 


A slight current pulls the boat, and the rowers are somewhat relaxed. 
Daz almost misses the rocks that stand out as stone teeth. He shouts.


The boat crew looks at him as he shouts, “Rocks! We need to dodge these rocks.” 


Everyone is confused and starts shouting. Daz realizes he needs to tell everyone what to do: “On my say, heave port or starboard!” 


The boat is heading straight for the first rock, and he shouts, “Port!” The ship turns, and he notices another rock, “Starboard!” The crew moves across the boat again. Daz is about to breathe a sigh of relief when he sees another pair of rocks. He must get between them, so he shouts, “Port! Starboard!” to line up the boat through the space. 


This goes on for a few more seconds, and then the rocks are behind him. 


He looks carefully for any sign of new rocks, then relaxes slightly. 


## 6th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493
### Thorin


Thorin reaches up and touches the cave's ceiling, which is only three feet above the water. He wonders what will happen if it gets any lower. 


He looks at Zalthir, Shuushar, Grygum, Daz, and Sarith, who are uncomfortable sitting down and lowering their heads to avoid banging them on the ceiling. 


“It’s good to be short,” Thorin notes as he sees how everyone else isn’t inconvenienced. 


Unfortunately for Thorin, he thought too soon, and the boat jerked, and he fell in, along with Grygum and Daz. 


As he splutters, he notices that the Darklake isn’t empty. 


The same creatures that were feasting on the kuotoans are here. They look like manta rays, whose jaws, instead of being used to filter plankton, have powerful jaws for rending the flesh of their victims. 


Fortunately for himself and his two companions, Daz and Grygum, there are only four. And unlike the Kuotoans, they are no match for them. 


As he climbs out of the water, he thinks it’s probably not good that a demonic fish is floating around with the local flora and fauna… 


## 8th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493
### Zalthir


“The ability to cast magical darkness and see through it opens up so many tactical opportunities,” he muses. 


Just then, he hears a shout from the watch: “There’s another boat ahead with some Kuotoans.” 


Zalthir stops and thinks and listens. The dragonborn is annoyed that he got caught up in a religious ritual that may or may not have summoned Demogorgon and is now floating through the Darklake in a tiny boat.


And the boat is a tight fit. 


Hemeth seems to be reading Zalthir’s mind when he suggests, “Maybe we take that boat from them? Like the last time I talked to Kuo-Toans, I almost got sacrificed. I was just trying to sell them weapons. I didn’t think they would think sacrificing me was a weapon. Crazy fish people.” 


Before Zalthir can agree, Suushar and Grygum have already decided that talking with more Kuotoans is the right answer. 


Zalthir  steps back and gets ready to attack, just in case. 


The Awakened shouts out to his kinsmen. Shuushar is very excited; at least, it would appear to Zalthir, Shuushar’s excited. 


Finally, their Kuo-Toan companion turns to the group, “They are from Sloopbludop and have seen the danger of the Kuo-Toan madness. And they are receptive to my message! They want to create a better Kuo-Toan world where we are no longer driven mad by our need to create a new god! I have found my calling! And I must leave you!”   


Before Zalthir can point out that Shuushar’s insight into the map of the Darklake is critical to their survival, Thorin says - “Follow your bliss!” 


Then Shuushar turns to his fellow escapees and thanks them for their help: “I have found my people, and we will build a better world!”


Zalthir stands off as he watches the tearful goodbye of Thorin, who is giving him a hug, Grygum spitting into the lake, and Daz looking indifferent. 


As the other Kuo-Toan boat floats off, Shuushar says thank you! 


As they stare into the Dark Lake, Sarith mutters a few moments later, “Erm, guys, I have Shuushar’s instructions, but I am not so sure I can follow them correctly.”
