# Chapter 06 Arrival at the Silken Paths
## 
7th day of the 2nd Tenday of Taraskh 1493


As the caravan of escapees moves through the Underdark, the problem of storing liquids rears its ugly head. 


At the time, ditching their gear and making a break for it seemed like a good idea. But now, the lack of a water bladder is creating problems. 


Daz then proposes an ingenious solution. When they next find water, he will use his ability to shape water to transform it into a block of ice. The block of ice can then be carried. Every hour or so, the water can be refrozen. If someone needs water, a block can be chipped away, and the water can be consumed. 


“All we need is some water,” mutters the monk. 


Fortunately, the escapees find both in the form of a few Barrelstalk mushrooms. Jimjar explains that the Barrelstalk mushroom can produce 1-4 gallons of water and five to 10 pounds of nutritious food. 


Eldeth covers the rear as they travel, trying to obscure any evidence of their travels. Buppido seems particularly interested in her tricks. 


Daz, Gyrgum, Thorin, and Zalthir try to help the dwarf. Eldeth tries not to be annoyed at all of the help. 


As the day progresses, they arrive at a narrow passageway lined with bioluminescent green moss. This moss grows in warm and damp areas and is particularly common near steam tunnels and vents. 


Jimjar explains that it is a fungus called “Ormu. In addition to shedding a dim light for 5 feet, it can be harvested, dried, and made into a phosphorescent powder or pigment.” 


Zalthir collects some fungi - “Never know when you need that kind of pigment.” 


As they exit the narrow passageway, Zalthir turns to Jimjar, “You’re good at hiding things.” 


Jimjar nods. 


“How about you block this passageway in a way that does not look like we were the cause?” 


“Wanna bet that I can?” 


“How much?” 


“5 gold.” 


“I have a pound of food?” 


“I want gold.” 


“Are you not interested in avoiding the drow?” 


“Wanna bet?” 


“We could leave you behind; it would improve our odds.” 


“Wanna bet?” 


“WE HAVE NO MONEY.” 


“That’s okay, we’ll have some later.” 


“And if we don’t, then?” 


“Then you never pay up.” 


Zalthir is gEttin g progressively more annoyed. 


Sensing this could get violent, Daz steps in, “It’s my understanding that Zalthir vowed never to gamble again when he became a monk. And such a bet would violate his oaths.” 


Jimjar is stunned, “I’m so sorry. I didn’t know.” 


But then Grygum steps forward, “I’ll make that bet.” 


Jimjar gets to work and, a little bit later, announces, “You won the bet.” 


“I did? Which side did I bet?” 


“You bet that I could do it.” 


Zalthir is baffled and intrigued, “He bet against himself even though he knew the odds of winning that bet were terrible. His willingness to make these bad bets implies he will make further bad bets going forward.” 


Grygum turns to Zalthir, “Interesting.” 
Zalthir tries to test his theory, “I’ll bet you can’t fly to the roof of this cavern?” 


Jimjar laughs, “I am a gambler, not an idiot.” 


Zalthir can only see that Jimjar is an infinite cash machine. If he can only figure out how to make Jimjar take the side of bets, Zalthir is most likely to win. 


As the group settles into sleep, Daz notices Topsy and Turvy talking to each other. But they are too far away for him to understand what they are saying. 


## 8th day of the 2nd Tenday of Taraskh 1493


Sarith rouses everyone in the morning, “We must continue.” 


And so they do, deeper into the Underdark, hopefully heading to Sloobludop. 


After an hour of marching, Thorin finds a pool of water. Daz immediately starts creating blocks of water for the group. When all the blocks are made, there is no more water. 


A little bit later, Grygum finds some more Trillimac. Jimjar, a botanist, explains it is a mushroom that grows to a height of four to five feet and has a broad gray-green cap and a light gray stalk. The cap’s leathery surface can be cut and cleaned for use in making maps, hats, and scrolls (its surface takes on dyes and inks well). The stalk can be cleaned, soaked in water for an hour, then dried to make a palatable food akin to bread. 


“That one will be worth 6 pounds of food.” 


“How much water will we need?” 


“I don’t know.” 


Zalthir glares at him. 


The rest of the crew cannot find any more of the mushroom. Eldeth shouts at Grygum, “Will you look?”


The half-orc interrupts his reverie and looks around. He shouts, “There!” 


And indeed, there is more Trillimac, enough to make 20 pounds of food.


“But how much water do we need?” 


Shuushar sighed, “Soon, we will be at the Darklake, and we will have more water than we ever knew what to do with it.” 


Zalthir would pull out his hair if he were a humanoid with hair, but he isn’t.  


And so the march continues. 


Sathir shouts, “YES!” 


They all look at him. 


“We have arrived at the Silken Paths. We are heading in the right direction.”


Buppido asks, “Although I know what they are, what do you think they are, Sathir?”


“The Silken Paths are a network of spider webs crisscrossing a 500-foot-deep, 2,000-foot-wide chasm that stretches for nearly five miles. The major strands of the web are traversable. Still, the Silken Paths are ever-changing because old webs disintegrate over time and the giant spiders inhabiting the chasm constantly sping new ones.” 


“So how do we get across?” 


“We start walking and hope we find a way through this maze.” 


Sathir is about to head out when two goblins appear. They are dressed in relaxed, comfortable shoes, baggy pants, and shirts whose button reaches just above their knees, whose sleeves are particularly wide, and their heads are covered with beanies. Their bodies are covered in tattoos written in draconic. The spelling is wrong. And it’s only through happenstance that Zalthir realizes they are writing goblin words using a draconic script. 


The first one, whose color motif is orange, introduces himself, “My name is Yukyuk.” 


The second jump, whose color motif is pink, speaks with an odd accent: the R’s are rolled, and the vowels are clipped oddly. “And I am Spiderbait. We can be your guides at a nominal price of 2 gold per day.” 


Daz admits, “We are destitute.” 


Thorin grabs Daz and nudges Stool to use his spores. 


Then he turns to his allies, “Look, we don’t have the money, and we don’t know how. How about we ask them to demonstrate how to navigate the web? We’ll take notes and then politely decline.” 


Zarith mutters, “Jimjar, you seem to have a knack with words.” 


Jimjar then turns to the two goblins, “I’ll bet you 20 gold you can’t navigate the spiderwebs.” 


“Done!” 


As they head to the web, Grygum realizes that the Jimjar doesn’t have 20 gold. He wonders whether to say anything to the goblins and realizes that Jimjar will pay when he has money. “And they are goblins; we could easily deal with them if it comes to that,” he thinks. 


Thorin observes them coat their shoes with a peculiar substance and start running. 


Grygum stares at them, slides on the strands, and mutters, “They are good at surfing the web.” 


Daz nods, “It’s almost as if we are on a Safari that surfs the web.” 


Grygum asks Jimjar, “Do they know which side of the bet they made?” 


He looks at the cleric and, with a twinkle in his eye, says, “No.” 


When the goblins return breathless and pleased, they turn to Jimjar. 


He nods his head and says, “You won.” Jimjar then looks into his pockets and mutters, “But I am a bit short of cash.” 


Angered, Yukyuk tosses his beanie on the ground and starts marching to the web. Spiderbait, however, has an idea: “Jimjar, how about we be your guides and hang around until you have the 20 gold you owe?” 


Jimjar acts like this was the most awful idea he had ever heard, then pauses and sighs, “I suppose that will have to be the plan.” 


Just as they are about to follow them onto the strands, Zalthir tries to figure out if these two peculiar web-surfing goblins can be trusted. And although he is distrustful, he feels there is no subterfuge about them. 


And with the deal closed, the stiff, formal nature of the two goblins dissolves. Yukyuk turns to Topsy, “  Oh, yeah, man. That was fun. Did you see me nail those tough strings? There was this one moment when I was taking a curve, and the strands broke, but I managed to do that twist and flip, and that's super cool.” 


Topsy stares at him, confused. 


Grygum, always excited with new forms of speech, shouts, “That was an amazing triple spin!” 


Spiderbait, seeing Grygum’s interest, mutters, “Yukyuk took the easy route. I went around the spider and through its legs.”


Zalthir takes note of the spider and asks, “Spiders?” 


“Not the small kind, but the giant kind. You get extra points if you spin a spider.”


Zalthir is now, perhaps the word isn’t worried, but something in the environs of that sentiment, “That sounds fascinating, but given the complexity of your moves, how are we supposed to get across?” 


Yukyuk looks at Zalthir confused and then laughs, “Oh man, you’re not surfing. You’re walking.”


Zalthir then stares at the web, “How attached are you to these webs? Can we burn the web down?” 


Spiderbait looks hurt and then laughs, “Do you remember that crazy Drow wizard who wanted to burn this place down? But the spiders kept rebuilding it faster than he could burn.” 


Yukyuk slaps his forehead, “Oh yeah, man. And then, when the webs were burned, you had to climb down these gnarly cliffs. And then climb up some other cliffs. No, man, the web is the way to go. Just gotta avoid the spiders.” 


Zalthir stares at them, “But when we walk on the web, the spiders will hear our vibrations. Won’t that attract them to us?” 


Yukyuk says, “Yeah, dude. But there is so much stuff bouncing on the webs that they won’t notice your bouncing scales.” 


Zalthir feels compelled to add, “If we don’t make it out alive, we won’t be able to pay.” 


“Yeah, man. We’re, like, the best guides, and, like, all our clients make it out.” 


Spiderbait walks up to Yukyuk and high-fives him. He then turns to the group and says, “We are the best. We are going to have so much fun going through the web with you. And you are an awesome group.” 


Buppido mutters, “I am not sure the divine plan had anything about relying on two semi-sentient goblins for our safety.” 


For once, Zalthir agrees. 


Sathir, seeing that the deal has been made, tells the two goblins where to guide them, “Take us to a passageway that will get us quickly to Sloobludop.”


The two goblins start the hike through the maze of webs. As they walk, they explain that fire can burn through the web, but when it does, anyone within 30 feet of the burning point will likely fall to their death. 


Zalthir asks, “And can we mitigate that fall?” 


The two goblins shrug. But Eldeth, who has done some spelunking, mutters, “We can tie some of the silk rope around each of us. That way, if one of us falls, the others can try to arrest his fall.” 


This innovation intrigues the goblins, who take many mental notes as the escapees tie themselves. 


Spiderbait asks, “But what if the other guy can stop the fall?”


Eldeth answers matter-of-factly, “Well, then the next person must.” 


“And what if he fails? And what if you all fail?” 


“Then I suppose we all die.” 


“Gnarly.” 


After an hour of walking, Daz notices a cocoon. Zalthir notices it is moving and humanoid-sized. He turns to Stool, “We need those spores.”   


Stool is very excited, “I’m helpful, aren’t I? I love being helpful.” 


Grygum turns to him and smiles, “And we love you for it.” 


“Are you going to come with me?” 


Grygum smiles at Stool and says, “Yes, I will. Now let’s go together.” 


So Stool and Grygum advance on the cocoon, and then they hear the voice of a still living halfling in their heads. “I am Fargas Rumblefoot.” The voice turns demanding, “Can you please get me out of here?” 


Grygum is about to cut through the cocoon when Thorin says, “Perhaps we should have him tell us who he is?” 
Grygum stops, “He can't be evil with a name like that. But you’re right.” 


“I was a member of an adventuring band looking for a lost tomb. Gnolls attacked us.” 


“A gnoll?” 


“Yes, gnolls. Feral hyena-headed humanoids attack without warning, slaughtering their victims and devouring their flesh. It is said that spawns are put into a hyena, and when the spawn is born, it rips out of the hyena's body. It is also said that hyenas are one of many species that can be used to store the spawn of gnolls. It is also said that they were created when the demon lord Yeenoghu found his way onto Faerûn and ran amok.” 


Thorin turns to his allies, “I wonder if he’s already been infected? Like, do we free him, and we get a gnoll?” 


“I am not infected.” 


“That’s what everyone thinks, and then it’s the apocalypse.” 


Zalthir - “Why aren’t you infected?”


“Because I escaped. Most of the time, the gnolls just eat their victims.” 


“And what are you doing here?” 


“I am spider snack for later. Can you please stop talking and get me out?” He pauses, “Shit, I can feel the spiders coming.” 


Thorin turns to the guides, “What should we do?” 


“Get out of here?” 


Fargas suddenly realizes he might not be freed,  “I have information. We were coming to look for a tomb. I know the location of the tomb. And more importantly, I know the location of the treasure.” 


Zalthir turns to Thorin, “Can you carry him, or will we need to spend minutes to free him?” 


Thorin takes his blade, saying, “I can cut him loose in a few seconds.” 


Grygum, Zalthir, Daz, and Thorin take a look around. Spiders are walking on strands. Those strands can be cut with fire or a blade. They can stop the spiders and escape if they cut the strands in the right places. They will get trapped if they cut the strands in the wrong place. 


They remember this as a game from their childhood called ‘Cut the Graph.”
