# Chapter 18.2 
## 1st day of the 2nd Tenday of Myrtul 1493
### Daz
Daz looks at Flumph, Jimjar, Sarith, Stool, Eldeth, and Spiderbait, armed with Errde’s weapons. “Perhaps they will help us” as they follow him, Zalthir, Grygum, and Thorin, into the Derro slums of Gracklstugh. 


As he walks he reflects on the image of the egg that Flumph shared. 


The lettering on the egg rings a bell. 


“It’s too late.” 


Grygum stares at him, “What do you mean?” 


“A demon has already possessed the egg. There is no dragon there.” 


The cleric of Bahamut is saddened. Daz detects a resolve to hurt the Derro magician. Daz is pleased. 
### Zalthir


Zalthir marches the group back through the same path they took last time, going over the lake, and then through the fungi forest. He then follows the path towards the Raucus Mesa. 


As he approaches the intersection that leads to the assassin’s lair, two Derro materialise.


They introduce themselves as Grimgrim and some unpronounceable Derro. The Drow not named Grimgrim explains that he can be called - “Rust.” 


Zalthir tries to determine the wisest course of action, but Thorin blurts out, “Are you the assassins or the dudes trying to become besties with Demogorgon?” 


Zalthir notices how pleased Rust is, who says, “You are the enemies of Demogorgon?” 


And Thorin explains, “So you must be the assassins?” 


Zalthir cringes. 


Rust speaks in a serious tone, “Yes. We are the empty scabbard killers.” 


Zalthir, who learned the art of saying little, wonders if he can rip Thorin’s throat out when the dwarf asks, “How do you kill people if you have an empty scabbard?” 
The orc piles on, “ Well, it's easier to kill someone if the scabbard is empty?” 


Zalthir wants them to stop. 


But Thorin won’t, “Oh, is it because you have your weapon in your hand, or is it magical?” 


Rust asks them to move along. 


Zalthir remembers that they had to help Werz. What Werz wants is not to be a target. Killing the assassins is not what he wants. 


Then he remembers what Errde craved, proof that the assassins killed her family.  


Zalthir turns to Thorin, “Maybe we can do them a favor and take care of the cult, and they will drop Werz as a target?”  


Thorin looks at him, “What favor?” 


“Get rid of the cult of Demogorgon.” 


Daz whispers through the telepathy of Stool, “I like the idea of collecting all of the information and then distributing it to all of the power brokers of Gracklstugh simultaneously. We could stab them all in the front simultaneously and flee in the chaos.” 


Thorin turns to Rust and Grim, “Hey, we would love to compare notes on the cult of Demogorgon and see if we can help each other out.” 


Rust shouts, “Let me confer with my leadership to see if they agree with this plan.”
### 
Daz


The Duergar assassin returns and says, “The boss wants to see you.” 


Daz overhears Thorin telepathically asking if Flumph will join them. Flumph says, ‘I want to observe a tragedy, not be in one. I will stay here.”  


Jimjar moves to follow Rust, but Rust turns to him, “Only these four will come, the rest of you,” pointing at Spiderbait, Jimjar, Sarith, Stool and Eldeth, “can wait here.” 


And so Daz, Thorin, Zalthir, and Grygum enter the chambers of the assassins. Rust marches them into a cave that is 70 feet long and 70 feet wide at its widest point. Inside is a large garden. Standing in the garden is Duergar with an eye patch. 


Daz takes note of a desk to the north with papers. 


He also notices three soul blades, the name the Duergar assassins give themselves, standing behind what appears to be their boss. Behind Daz, the mage can feel Rust and Grim standing. 


“Violence is not the answer,” he thinks.  


The Duergar with an eye patch speaks, “My name is Eldgrim. Rust says you may have a deal for us. And in exchange, we are to let you through our territory.” 


Daz collects his wits, “Do you have a hit on a guy named Werz?” 


Eldgrim laughs, “Small potatoes. I can cancel the contract for a small fee and permanently brightlist him for a small fee.” 


“Brightlist?” 


“Yes, put him on a list that will protect him. It’s called bright because we can always see it.”  


Thorin whispers to Daz, “Ask them if they have a talism that we can use for safe passage?”

Daz looks at Thorin, “So we can use it to prove they exist later.” 


Daz asks Eldgrim, who in turn laughs. “An assassin’s guild with a card?” 


The mage shrugs and turns to Werz, “So what is the  fee for Werz?” 


“You get rid of my demogorgon problem, we could move Werz to the allow list for, shall we say, 20 gold?” 


Daz is outraged, “We are going to deal with your problem, and you want 20 more gold?” 


Before Eldgrim can speak, Thorin blurts, “I don’t think a group of demon worshipers is only worth 180 gold, the imbued value based on the calculation from the proposed discount.” 


Daz stares at Thorin, and Thorin shrugs, “I learned about contracts. The Giants needed somebody to make deals with the locals, and I made contracts. It’s easy to make a good contract if the choice is a contract or a bunch of giants raid your town.” 


Thorin then turns to Daz, “Have him sign a contract, we can use that as proof!”  


Eldgrim stares at Thorin, “Daz, that’s what they said you were called. I made a business arrangement with others. If I were to eat that contract, then folks would look at me as weak. But the offer of killing Demogorgon’s followers would remove a source of ongoing cost to my operations. So, although I don’t like it, if you get rid of them, I will move Werz to the brightlist.” 


Daz sensing an opportunity to get proof, asks, “Perhaps we should sign a contract? With a confidentiality agreement to make sure that we keep things quiet.”  


Eldgrim sees through Daz’s efforts, “You want to prove we exist? Do you want to show this contract to someone, and do you really think they will believe that we exist and not think it's a forgery? You’re a fool. Anyone who sees any proof of our existence and knows we exist will deny our existence, and everyone else will laugh at your credulity.” 


He pauses, “As if a group of strangers can come down here and find proof of something that nobody has found, ever.” 


While Eldgrim arrogantly dismisses the mage, Daz notices a piece of paper on the desk that has a mark that looks like a mark from someone with power. 


He turns to Eldgrim, “You are right. Nothing I say or show will prove anything. And I wanted the contract for my selfish reasons. I have been writing a collection of stories that I hope to publish as a book later. It would be nice to jot down your name on a piece of paper so I can include it correctly. Could you do that? 


Eldgrim is flattered and says, “The legend of our guild will spread far and wide.” The other duergar looks at him oddly, and he says, “And if you spread anything but our legend, well then, perhaps we will have to take care of a certain author.”  


The assassin chief walks up to his desk, pulls a piece of paper from the desk, and scribbles. 


Meanwhile, Thorin turns to Grim and Rust and starts asking them about the empty scabbard moniker in an attempt to distract them. 


Daz takes the contract while Eldgrim is distracted, but he knows that if it goes missing, then the assassins will come after him. 


So he starts to cough. 


He motions to Zalthir to come over. The dragonborn marches over and thwacks Daz on the back. Daz whispers to him, “When I hit you, breathe fire at the desk.” 


Daz stops coughing and shouts with a sense of real relief, “I thought I was going to die. My allergies.” Zalthir is still confused. And then Daz slaps him on the back and shouts, “Thank you!” 


Zalthir, on cue, breathes on the desk, burning the papers on it. 


Eldgrim screams - “Fool! I should kill you.” But then he looks at the burned papers and mutters, “Contracts that I have committed to memory. Better destroyed.” 


He then turns to them, “Now go get rid of my problem.” He pauses, “And if you don’t, then you will die. It will be a free contract that I will enjoy fulfilling.”

Daz asks him, “But who wrote it?” 


Thorin points to a mark on the document bearing the personal seal of the Deepking and the royal seal of Gracklstugh. 


Daz mutters, “Yes, we do.” 


Thorin, whose interests in contracts stand in contrast to his focus on brutal strength, smiles. He’s seen many contracts that people should never have signed. He never wrote those. 


Zalthir decides that rather than go through some door that they may not be able to open, it’s safer to try a different route. There is a tunnel entrance that goes past two caves. The caves have  Derro inside. One of the Derro is a member of the Council of Savants. 


Thorin turns to Flumph, Jimjar, Sarith, Stool, Eldeth, and Spiderbait and commands them to be quiet. He demonstrates by walking on his tip toes. 


He then overhears Zalthir’s sigh. The sound of Thorin tiptoeing could wake the dead. Zalthir proposes a new plan: “I will cover you in Darkness and you will just be quiet.” 


### Grygum 


Past the caves they sneaked through, is a much larger tunnel, some 80 feet wide and hundreds of feet long. To the west, the tunnel opens up into a massive cave, glowing with faerzress, bioluminescent fungi, and shafts of white light that fall. GEttin g closer, he sees naturally formed shelves along the walls as well as a rocky mesa at the center of the cavern. His eye is drawn to crudely formed ramps connecting the different levels of the mesa up to the height of its two topmost tiers, which are linked by a sturdy bridge. 


“The egg,” he mutters. Atop one of those tiers sits a large red egg. 


“The obelix,” he notes when he looks atop the other tier. There stands a 50-foot-tall obelisk made of smooth black metal with a few noticeable imperfections, as though small parts of it were somehow chipped away.


A female derro stands close to the obelisk, gently patting and stroking its surface. The obelisk flashes once, the glow of faerzress around the cavern flaring in response. The derro squeals with glee as she snatches an object up from the ground, then writes something down in a small notebook.
