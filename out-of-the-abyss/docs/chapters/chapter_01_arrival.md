# Chapter 01 Arrival 

## 2nd day of the 2nd tenday of Taraskh, 1492

The Underdark, the hidden realm beneath the surface of Toril, is not a welcoming place, even in the best of times. 

It was certainly not welcoming in 1492, two years after the War of the Dragons ended and only a year after the War of the Giants. 

The events that started with the subversion of the Cult of the Dragon and ended with the re-establishment of the Ordning of the Giants were well and truly over. 

New adventurers, however, still tried to ply their trade. And there was always a need for someone to deal with a problem or two. 

And if you can’t find adventure above ground, there is always below ground. 

Or at least that’s what the half-orc Gyrgum thought to himself as he sat in a prison cell \- again. 

At least this time, he wasn’t alone; there was Daz, the Drow Elf Magic User, Thorin Giantfriend, whose experience with giants was radically different than his, and the very quiet Zalthir, a dragonborn monk. 

The adventure had been going so well. They had this cave to explore, and there was some gadget they were supposed to get, and then \- boom, they fell down a chute. And even then, it wasn’t so bad because they were a group of heroic adventurers, and Bahamut had his back. 

Except this time, there was a bigger mission at play. 

A group of Drows found them unconscious in the chute. When they woke up, they were without weapons or armor and being led deeper into the Underdark. 

“Menzoberranzan is our final destination,” remarked Daz. 

“Is that good?” asked Thorin. 

Daz shook his head. Daz explained that the City of Menzoberranzan was a city where the Cult of Lloth was very powerful, and enslaved people didn’t have a very long and healthy life. Drow who were apostates tended to have an even shorter life than other slaves. And apostates that were male Drow Elves, the shortest-lived of all. 

Gyrgum sighed and thought, “Not again.” 

The journey through the Underdark took two, or was it three days? With no sunlight to guide them, the only things that gave him a sense of time were the long periods of rest and the feeling of exhaustion at the end of the day. 

“You will be dropped off here,” said Welnaste Mizzrym, the caravan leader. “This is Velkynvelve. I have secured a reasonable price for you. Another caravan will come to pick you up in a ten day or so to take you to Menzoberranzan.”   
![][image1]  
Grygum looks up and sees Velkynvelve. It's a Drow outpost high in a cavern 100 feet above the rocky floor. The outpost consists of small caves in the cavern walls and four hanging towers. These are hollowed-out stalactites connected by walkways, stairs, and rope bridges. The towers are concealed by the thick webs of giant spiders that stretch below them, so only the lowermost parts of the stalactites are visible from the cavern floor. 

As they climb up, he notes that the small amount of dim light used in the outpost, shielded from the cavern floor below, allows one to walk the entire length of the cleft without becoming aware of the outpost overhead, hidden in the darkness above the ranges of torches and lanterns. 

Daz points out, “There are giant spiders. If someone should arrive in this cavern, they can lower themselves and attack from above. Drow soldiers probably have stockpiles of spider silk that they will use as a rope to drop down as well.” 

At the main entrance to the outpost, Welnaste shouts, “Ilvara, I have the shipment.” 

Ilvara steps forward. She is a haughty, arrogant, well-dressed, and coiffed female drow cleric. Daz snorts, “fits the type.” 

Next to her is another cleric and two male drow fighters. One of them has a disfigured face and is missing two fingers. 

Ilvara turns to the disfigured Drow: “Jorlan, take them to the cell. We will use them until the caravan arrives.” 

Welnaste looks at Ilvara and mutters, “Remember, house sister, money has been exchanged, and you will be held to account if they are lost or die before the caravan arrives.” 

Grygum then is made aware of Drow soldiers surrounding the group and putting bags over his friend's head. Before he can protest, a bag is placed over his. 

### Zalthir

The dragonborn had taken a vow of silence. Not so much about not speaking but only speaking if he had something to say. And right now, he wasn’t quite sure what to say.   
![][image2]  
Looking around, he saw ten fellow prisoners \- a fish man, a mushroom with legs, two deep gnomes that looked like twins, a deep gnome that didn’t, a derro, a hulking quagoth, a half-orc, another drow that was covered in some kind of fungal rot, and a dwarf.   
   
He was carefully considering what to do next and how to get information about his cellmates when Grygum decided to ask. 

“How novel?” he thought. 

“I am Topsy, and this is my brother, Turvy; we’re twins.” 

Turvy mumbles. 

“He says a pleasure to meet you too, Grygum. And I am pleased as well. By the way, everyone is looking at the other Drow.” 

Zalthir had noticed the stares and wondered why. Again, he thought to ask, but the deep gnome just gave away information—“They’re all wondering if you are like Sareth. Sareth was convicted of the murder of one of his fellow drow. The problem is he got caught.” 

Sarith reacts to that comment \- “I didn’t\! It was a setup\! Or was it? Maybe I did do it. Maybe they’ve imprisoned me and are gonna send me to Menzo to be sacrificed or turned into a DRIDER\!”   
![][image3]

Zathir notes Daz’s shudder and can read his body language; Sarith is not sane. 

Grygum approaches Zarith and mutters, “And he’s not a spy. He’s a prisoner just like us.” 

###  Thorin

“A fellow dwarf\! We must plan our escape,” says the other dwarven prisoner. 

![][image4]

Thorin is delighted to meet one of his kind but speaks with an unmistakable Giant accent. Eldeth is taken aback when he hears of his story. She explains that she’s not quite sure what to do. The war of the Giants has devasted the world above, and she’s not one of those who believes that Giants can make peace with what they call the “little folk.” 

“But here is an example of such a possibility.” 

The derro, intently staring at Thorin, finally speaks in a thin, raspy voice. Eldeht scowls at his intrusion, muttering something about Derro’s being some kind of dwarven perversion. 

Conversely, the derro looks personable, gregarious, and talkative. He introduces himself as Buppido. 

“We must escape from this place. It is part of the divine plan.” 

Grygum intrudes, “Divine plan? Yes, it involves many dragons.” 

Buppido smiles, “You are a cleric and a follower of gods. In the future, perhaps, we will work together. And shortly, perhaps, we will work together to escape. For I know that the divine plan tells us, there is only one path forward\!” 

Eldeth tries to avoid rolling her eyes, but Thorin catches her. 

### Daz 

“I have not seen a Kuo-Toan in a long while. They are a curious race of fish-people who were corrupted by the mind flayers. They can create gods from the power of their imaginations. Dangerous and very mad. I must be careful as I speak to them.” 

Zalthir steps before him, sensing that the Kuo-Toan is a monk. 

The fish man speaks soothingly and introduces himself as Shuushar the Awakened. 

“I have found enlightenment.” 

Buppido coughs, “He’s a Kuo-Toan; they always go mad.” 

“My people indeed go mad, but I have found a way to stop that. I was on my way to my Sloobludop near the Darklake. I have navigated those winding roads for years and can surely find my way back.” 

Daz wonders if they can rely on this person to escape. He seems sane, but he is a Kuo-Toan. 

The Drow mage has been trying hard not to be noticed by the Quaggoth. The Drow Nobles of Menzoberranzan have taken an interest in recruiting and training the creatures, encouraging their ferocity while strengthening their obedience. Such a creature, here, is very odd. It could tear them all apart without thinking. 

“So why hasn’t it?” 

Suddenly, the creature speaks with a rarefied accent, typically heard only among the upper kingdom's most powerful elves.   
This shocks Daz, for the Quaggoth were hunted to extinction by the surface elves. The other reason the Drow use them as troops is because they hate surface-dwelling elves almost as much as the followers of Lloth.  

“I am Prince Derendil of the Kingdom of Nelrindenvane in the High Forest. My crown was usurped by the evil wizard Terrestor, who trapped me in this form and exiled me from my people. I would be much obliged if you treated me with the respect my station commands.” 

Daz knows in an instant that Prince Derendil is a liar. He traveled through the High Forest, but no Kingdom of Nelrindenvane existed. He thinks about confronting the fake Prince, but then he thinks that the Fake Prince may be a useful ally as they try to escape. 

Just then, the mushroom speaks, “I am stool. I am a member of the circle\! My circle is called the Neverlight Grove.” 

Daz stares at him, uncertain of what he is—and surprised to see a mushroom with legs that talks. 

He is repulsed but can tell this is a child of whatever species it belongs to. 

### Grygum 

“A fellow orc,” he thinks. 

The orc introduces himself as Ronc and is disappointed when he discovers that Grygum is a cleric but not Gruumsh. Ronc had fought poorly and was looking for absolution from his god. 

Eldeth sneers at him and mocks him \- “You and your kind were defeated at Gauntlygrym. The Shield Dwarves captured their castle, and we will keep it from your kind.” 

Grygum sighs. The dwarves can be so bigoted. 

“We should go there, to Gauntlygrym. It’s the best place for us to escape\!” 

Just then, the other deep gnome speaks, “Wanna bet?” 

Buppido sighs, “Not that again. He keeps bEttin g every day on anything.” 

“You won’t take my bets on your divine plan.” 

“We never bet on the divine plan that would sully the plan.” 

Topsy shouts, “That’s Jimjar, always pretending he’s better than us. He’s from our town, you know.”   
Turvy mumbles something to her; she pauses and then shouts, “We should go to Blingdestone\!” 

Jimjar smiles and says, “I’ll make you bet nobody agrees to go there.” 

Eldeth then turns to Grygum, “The mushroom is a Myconid. A conscious form of mycelium.” 

“Yes, I am a Myconid. And my sovereign is mighty. And he will be thrilled to see me. And I can get you to the Neverlight grove\! And it will be safe.” 

Grygum hears no sounds and is about to ask how he heard that when Bupido explains, “They release these spores that allow them to communicate without speaking.” 

### Thorin 

He approaches the door, “We can so break this door down.” 

Daz looks at it and says, “I have a better plan.” He prepares to cast a spell when Eldeth shouts, “The room will not allow you to cast magic. It’s pointless to try.” 

“So break it is.” 

Eldeth turns to Thorin, “I like your spunk. And it’s a good look. But there are nine drows, a bunch of Quaggoths, and who knows how many giant spiders there are?” 

The “Prince” adds, “And there are three Drow guards right across the door.” 

Grygum turns to Thorin, “Maybe we rethink the charge at a pack of drow with no weapons a little more?”

Buppido then whispers, “Let’s send Runc ahead. He can distract them, and the rest of us can escape.” 

Thorin asks, “Why don’t you do it?” 

“I am more of a big-picture thinker. And frankly, if I reveal my power, then …” 

Grygum is frustrated, “Time to do the big reveal?”

“No.” 

Thorin gets frustrated when Jimjar starts speaking, “Oh man, the Drow polycules are such a mess.” 

The group stares at him. 

“The Drow with the ugly scratch marks on his face, Jorlans is his name, used to be the number one squeeze, but now Shoor is the primary squeeze after Jorlan got injured. Jorlan used to have a wand of globs. Meanwhile, Ashar’s angling for the top job.”

Serith, seeing how Jimjar has become the center of attention, shouts, “I too have information.” He starts to speak and then begins to mutter. Thorinth then watches as Grygum reassures the Drow, who explains, “At the bottom of the cave is a gray ooze in the pools. If we escape that way, we have to be careful.”
