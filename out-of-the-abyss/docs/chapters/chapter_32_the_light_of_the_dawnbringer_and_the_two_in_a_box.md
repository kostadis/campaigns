### Grygum
![image]()
Wow, this place is incredible. My jaw hangs open, and I feel a shiver of awe run through me. We've been tramping through the Underdark for what feels like an eternity, and every twist and turn has brought more of the same—darkness, danger, and the constant, nagging feeling that something is about to jump out at us.
But this is different. It's a vast cavern, but instead of being a dark, oppressive void, it's a living, breathing landscape of impossible color. The cave walls shimmer with a soft, bioluminescent light, casting a gentle glow on everything. The light is a beautiful mosaic of blues, greens, and purples, like a nebula trapped underground. Myconids, the mushroom folk we're traveling with, are everywhere, and they seem to be a part of this vibrant world, glowing with the same otherworldly light.
For a moment, all the dangers and all the worries just vanish. I forget about the Drow pursuers we just shook, the grumpy talking sword that's been driving Daz crazy, and the mysterious disease affecting the Myconids. It's not a scary, alien place; it's a work of art, and I am standing right in the middle of it. This isn't just a cave; it's a cathedral of light and life. It reminds me that even in the deepest, darkest places of the world, there can be something truly beautiful and amazing waiting to be found.
Daz turns to me, “It’s a good thing I left my mushroom recipe book at home.” 


I laugh. 


We have just entered this magical place from a natural tunnel that was formed by a drying underground stream, and that is dimly lit by lichen. 


The access tunnel opens above the grove’s main floor. It gives us a panoramic view of the mushroom forest that covers every surface, including the ceiling, and illuminates the darkness with brilliantly colourful patterns made from the living light of the fungi. 


Beyond the exotic forest, the cavern narrows into a ravine. And beyond the ravine, I catch a glimpse of a majestic mushroom, but it’s too far away for me to see any details. I look at Daz, who shrugs. 


And then I hear Rump-a-dump's delight and Stool’s happiness as they run into the cavern shouting, “We’re home!” 


Sarith, our drow guide, looks unhappy and serene but follows the myconid’s. Jimjar smiles at me and says, “Remember my bet,” as he races after the two myconids and Sarith. 


Zalthir, who finds the idea of mushrooms that turn you into zombies very creepy, covers himself in his orb of darkness and hangs back. 


Thorin and his sword race down with the rest. And the rest of our followers, Eldeth, the goblin, and the humans follow him. 


I shrug and follow. 


We go through a large fungal forest and descend into a central basin. Beyond the mud and mushrooms of the fungal forest, a large, clear pool sits in the midst of the fungal grove. A central mound is the only dry spot in sight, though a small cliff rises above the cavern floor far across the clearing with giant mushrooms visible in the distance. 


On the ceiling, I notice how the living light of the fungi trace strange constellations along the cavern’s ceilings and walls, showing the darkness of the ravine and mist-shrouded smaller cavern beyond. 


I see Rump-a-dump and Stool chatting with two very large mycondis. One of them spreads spores that allow us to talk. 


The creature introduces itself as Phylo and is genuinely happy to see us. 


Stool interrupts the creature to explain how Thorin carried him when he was tired, and that Zalthir was always grouchy but a really nice softer, and saved Rump-a-dump. 


Phylo waves his hand, and the two sprouts are silenced, “You are safe, friendly softers! You arrive at a wondrous time, for Neverlight Grove is on the verge of something great, something marvelous! Celebrate as the day of joy is nigh!”


I thank him. They smile, or at least that is what I feel they do. And then they say, “You should visit and explore our cave and see the wonders of the Neverlight Grove. But please, don’t visit the eastern plateau because the Circle of the Masters is preparing a wondrous and glorious surprise in the Garden of Welcome. Tomorrow, I can give you a peek, if you want.” 


I try to ask any questions but Phylo says we should direct our questions to the Great Seeder. I don’t recognize this god. Phylo says, “She lives beyond the Garden. She is a wonderful entity who will help all of us get closer to her beauty!” He keeps talking about this creature. 


Daz turns to me, “How odd. Myconids have no gender, and in general get confused about the topic. The sovereign’s use of a specific gender and consistent use of one particular gender is quite peculiar.” 


And then I remember that Zugtomy is often referred to as she. And I worry.

### Daz


Basidia has offered to animate a Girk if we can find it.

Engaging the immediate target while allowing an invisible, grappling threat to remain poised to strike is a lapse in security that any house in Menzoberranzan would never permit.


We could proceed with the blunt approach favored by overbrighters and Duergar alike—charging in and hoping luck prevails—but I will not. 


I tell The Ember Vanguard that I have a plan to neutralize the creature’s ability to hide. I explain that I visualize the terrain as a precise grid, and intend to freeze five-foot cube sections of the moss and water using Shape Water. “If a square refuses to freeze, we have found the creature.”

And each player had a set of pieces that the other player had to destroy. As this was Menzoberanzan, if neither player had won by some pre-agreed time, both players lost. 


Much like K’tharr-li, we only have so much time. And unlike K’tharr-li, losing can be deadly.
