# Chapter 28 The Wedding Feast. 
### Thorin


Dawnbringer has been quite chatty of late. She enjoys the open air of the Neverlight Grove, with its bright, cheery light and vibrant colors. As for me, I find the cave poorly excavated and lacking several key structural elements that would both enhance and protect it, never mind the need for defensive structures.


As we approach, Basidia releases their spores and speaks to us. They say, “Phylo is changing our society. I believed that the best way for us to work together was to participate in a group meld where everyone could hear from everyone. But Phylo convinced some of the circle leaders that the Great Seeder told them of another way. That instead of one big circle, each circle leader would have a melding with their group, and then with the leaders.” Basidia sighed, “Phylo said strange things like the need to eliminate cross-functional groups and instead have teams focused on the task at hand. Phylo said that by having that focus, and more narrowly, they would be more productive.” 


This sounds like utter madness. 


Zalthir looks at Basidia and whinces, as if he understands all too well what Basidia means. 


Basidia continues, “So now we have two inner circles, the circle of leaders and the circle of masters. The circle of those closest to them.” 


Basidia sighs and then points ahead to another Myconid, “They is Hepbode, and they are the grower. They are friends of Phylo, not of mine.” 


As we approach, Hepbobe starts talking to us once the Spores of Raport connect with our mind. “We are in charge of growing food for the colony.”


Grygum awkwardly asks, “So wait, you are mushrooms, like the food, and you eat other mushrooms?” 


I blush in shame. 


But Hepbobe acts as if they have seen this before, “It’s a typical misconception of softers who think all mushrooms are just that, mushrooms. We are a special kind of mushroom, or more precisely, we are not mushrooms because we are a sentient species.” 


Hepbobe sensing someone who wishes to learn continues, as if he were explaining something to a small child. “Myconid’s are herbivores.  Much like other creatures, we have particular preferences and cooking styles and ways of meeting, producing, and consuming the food that reflect the wide variety of tastes and pellets of the individual myconids.” 


I think I got it and ask, “So you are a fungus and eat fungi, just like I am a mammal and eat animals.” 


“Exactly.” 


“Gotcha!” 


Hepbobe then seems to be lost in his own tour. He shows the irrigation tunnels and the various kinds of fungi that are grown, “To continuously feed and delight the Myconids that live in this community.” 


I am bored, and he notices that and mutters, “I could give you a detailed analysis of our crop yields and growth, but perhaps another time.” 


Basidia prods us to ask Hepbobe about Phylo. 


Hepbobe must have overheard Basidia because he first stares at Grygum, then Basidia, and then Grygum, and then seems to sigh. “Basidia is concerned that Phylo is doing something that is going to irreparably harm Myconid life. I, on the other hand, believe that these organizational constructs, whether it's one or many, are things that only Sovereigns care about. Those of us who have a job to do to maintain the farming supply do not think that this is meaningful one way or another.” 


The words they say are meaningless. They must be mad. 


However, Grygum seems to be nodding. 


“Basidia believes that if we’re all together, everybody knows what’s going on, but does everybody need to? By focusing, we can deliver better mushrooms, higher quality mushrooms, and not get distracted by the concerns of the various groups. And frankly, there are some members of the group who will just talk endlessly about that topic that nobody cares about. For example, I care about this particular fungus, and other members of the circle of farmers care about it. But when we are in the circle, I start talking about it, and my circle cares, but everyone gets bored. And then I am told to be quiet. And I ask, Thorin, when are we supposed to have our internal conversations?” 


Grygum and Zalthi nod. 


And then Hepbobe becomes quite animated, “I think Basidia is exaggerating their concerns. And frankly, what is wrong with Phylo’s proposal, and that we’re going to head to a better day? Does it always have to be doom and gloom? What if there is a better day for Myconid Kind?”


I ponder that.

Grygum 


The organizational disputes of the Sovereings remind me of the preachings of the clerics of Bahamut. Much like we would spend days wondering which exact prayer would get Bahamut’s attention and please him most, they spend their days pondering how best to organize. 


To be a mushroom is to be connected. To be a sentient mushroom is to be an individual and connected. And surely there are a thousand different ways to connect. 


As we leave the Circle of Growers, Zalthir asks Basidia whether Hepbope’s food supply has the evil spores, and they say no. 


Soon, we arrive at another circle, where a Myconid appears to be dancing. 


When it sees us, it releases spores and they reach our minds. 


Zalthir notes how curious it is curious to see a Myconid dance. I agree. 


The creature, in a sing-songy voice, as if it is in some kind of ecstasy, and then declares, “Welcome, have you come to see the all that Myconids that will be? They are all necessary for Phylo’s great plan and the Greet Seeder who is coming!” 


I notice the fields of myconids that have not yet sprouted. Apparently they grow their kind. 


As he dances, Thorin and I hear the music. I don’t recognize it. But Thorin does, it’s the same melody that we heard when we saw Zuggtomy in his dream. It has no rhythm, no beauty, it revels in a symphony of chaos and evil. It hurts. 


The creature smiles at us and says, “Have you heard of Gasbide’s plan to send us to the Overbright to share our stories?” 


Thorin turns to me, “This is a death cult.” 


I shrug and wonder why is it that there are so many death cults. And then I remember that they don’t think it is a death cult, instead they assume it is a cult that will bring them some great joy.


Like Severin. 


The Circle Leader walks away and heads towards a field of tiny sprouts. I approach.


And then Thorin stops and tells us, “I can hear the music.” And we worry, “No the lyrics. Yrberop, that’s the Myconid’s name, is singing about some Great Seeder, and her “wedding” to the Great Body and how every Myconid is invited bringing joy to all in the world below and above.” 


The infested Myconid is singing to her new sprouts. 


### Zalthir


Spores infecting my mind. And madness is everywhere. 


I will not have my mind infected by these things. I will not become a zombie … 


And then I notice that Jimjar has not made a bet in some time. I ask Basidia if he has been infected and they say no. 


Jimjar must have overheard me, “I don’t see the point in making a bet. Everyone is so happy about this wedding.” 


And I wonder why he must have known about this wedding. And he does look worried. 


And we cross the grove one more time on this tour. And arrive at another of the many myconid circles. 


“Rusharoo is a friend of mine and a leader of the Circle of Explorers.” 


The Myconid enters Basidia’s cloud of spores and starts talking. I notice that there is some nervous energy, a hastiness about them. There is something different. His voice is frantic and full of speed. 


“We are the few, the proud, the explorers, and the pathfinders. We are the ones who scout and see the world beyond the grove.” 


“And what have you seen lately?” 


“I have noticed, as has Basilia, that the Grove is not well. Every time I come back, it changes, and its changes are not for the best.” 


There is a pause. 


“Basidia says I should trust you. I have a few escape routes and nutrient caches should we ever need to flee.” 


He looks at Basidia as if pleading with them to leave now. 


“I have felt that the right way for a Myconid to live is traveling, instead of sticking in one place. The myconid is a nomad.” 


I am surprised and ask, “Ignoring Philo, are you saying that the natural state of a Myconid is nomadic? And that the Sovereigns agree?” 


Basidia, if they could roll their eyes, would. “No, Rusharoo is one of our kind who is always exploring. That is why they are exploring. Myconids stay in one place and cultivate their gardens.” 


“And what about the plans to visit the Overbrite?” 


Rusharoo is disgusted, “That would be a terrible idea. Myconids thrive in the dark. Why would we go into the light of the Overbrite? There is so much more for us to explore in the Underdark. So many tunnels and caves. I think the caves and tunnels are really another Myconid that connects everything. And that we are messages for that myconid.” 


Basidia sighs and then whispers to Rusharoo.


Rusharoo leans in, “If you wish, I could send you into the Circle of Welcome through a secret passageway.” 
### Daz


Gathering intelligence is the Drow way.


The path leads up to a plateau, and beyond that lies a strange garden. We approach it. And as we get closer, we hear strange mutterings. As we get closer, the mutterings turn into a cacophony of moans and hisses. The only thing in this place comes from a few glowing mushrooms, but even in the gloom. I stare in shock. The heads of dozens of underdark creatures peek from the ground. Mold and fungi grow on them, and through them. 


And then a voice, a Drow voice, yells out loudly and in common. She is fighting to speak, “Please, for the love of your gods of light, kill me.” I stare at her some more. She starts to move, I think to prepare a spell, but notice that half her face is rotten and postulated, a bed for the sprouting of mushrooms. She raises what I think is a hand, and it is a spider medallion covered in fungi that have fused the medallion with her body. She moans, “The Great Seeder is here. The Lady of Decay took me.” 


Thorin wants to do as she asks, but Zalthir shows true Drow instincts when he wants to ask questions. 
Thorin asks, “How do we avoid your fate?” 


The thing that was once a drow laughs. “My name is Xinaya, and I was sent to find you. Ilvara thought you would come here.” She cackles, “Who would have thought that I would be begging you to kill me?” 


Zalthir asks if she was here to kill us. 


I stifle a laugh. 


The female Drow coughs, “No, to capture you. Ilvara wanted to kill you herself. I was given orders to capture you. I came here, thinking you would seek refuge amongst the Myconids. But I made the mistake of accepting Phylo’s invitation to see the Circle of Welcome. My squad and I become victims of the madness.”


And then she notices me, perhaps for the first time, and takes the haughty tone of a female Drow follower of Lolth towards any male, “You are from Menzoberanzaan. You must warn the other Drow of the presence of the Demon Queen of the Fungi in the Underdark.” 


The Demon Queen and Demogorgon are here, in the Prime Material Plane and in the Underdark. 


And then I notice a shift. The moans of the victims take on a new tone, a fear of something that moves across the foul ground. 


A disgusting larval creature rises before me. I notice some vestigial fungal growths. I wonder if this might have once been a myconid. 


“Welcome, travelers.” The aberrant myconid’s voice gurgles and spits as it speaks both aloud and in my mind simultaneously. 


“Are you here for the wedding rehearsal? Friends of the bride or her intended? No matter! Let the love of the Great Seeder embrace you as you become one with her chosen, the Great Body!”
### Thorin


Dawnbringer really doesn’t like the dark. And I really don’t like fungal growths that can turn you into abominations. 


We’re all here: Spiderbait, Jimjar, Eldeth, Sarith, Furuum, and the other woman, Aliza Argot, whom we found in the caves. Two Myconids now surround us, along with this fungal abomination and two Drow who have been turned into slaves. 


And it’s a good thing I am carrying Dawnbringer, because the sunlight hurts these creatures. And the bright light emanating from the shift hurts the creatures as if it were from the sun itself. 


I see to my left Jimjar covered in spores, and then he stops moving.  


Grygum approaches the creature to touch it. Is this thing the source of the harmful spores?


The orc cleric of Bahamut approaches the creature gingerly, invokes the power of his god, and inflicts wounds on it. 


Daz shouts, “If you can get out of the way.” 


I can see him calculating. I know what is coming next. “Boom.” 


Just then, a corpse explodes, and the bloody parts somehow cure the abomination. 


“Exploding corpses?” shouts Zalthir. I shrug. “How many do you think there are?” he adds. 


“Enough?” I answer. 


And then boom, as the fire mote flies from Daz’s hand and explodes in a conflagration of fire. 


And then another corpse explodes, drenching us all in acid. 


The creature cackles and shouts, “You’ll now join my mistress!” 


And a spore cloud covers us all.  


Then another one of the Drow spore servants attacks Jimjar. 


And to my horror, Sarith’s head explodes and turns into yet another Drow spore servant. 


I think to myself that I dodged a bullet there with Z. 


Zalthir strikes the fungal abomination, killing it. 


### Grygum

Using a guiding bolt, I wound one of the remaining Myconids. And watch as Daz’s bolts kill them. 


Meanwhile, Jimjar kills poor Sarith. 


And then I distinctly notice Daz giggling.  


Zalthir kills the last of the Myconid creatures. Then he rushes to Xinaya. She is still alive. I am confused. 


Thorin marches up to her, talks to Dawnbringer about the kindness of the gesture, Dawnbringer agrees, and Xinaya is killed. 


Zalthir looks confused, “Is she okay with it?” 


Thorin laughs, “I asked, and she said okay. We’re an arranged marriage.” 


I nod, “Arranged marriages can work very well!” 


We look around and collect what treasure we can. 


Up ahead, we see a path that leads to the giant mushroom, and I think, why not? 


Echoes spill from the misty cavern beyond, a cacophony of wheezing voices that wrap together like some kind of discordant music. The remaining heads planted in the Garden of Welcome begin to croak and groan, joining the horrible song.


Through the mist that shrouds the smaller cavern, I see the parade of creatures responsible for the melody. Their bodies are only vaguely humanoid, with clusters of luminescent lichen and tumescent growths forming chaotic patterns on their decaying flesh, their voices stabbing into my mind, both insane and gleeful in equal parts.
