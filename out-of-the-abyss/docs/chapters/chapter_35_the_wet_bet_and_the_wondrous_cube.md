# Chapter 30 The Wet Bet and the Wondrous Cube 

Jimjar, the deep gnome guide, found himself defending his navigational choices as the party emerged from yet another unexpected detour through the Underdark. He insisted that the shifting passages were to be expected and that finding an exit through their current location was remarkably convenient—perhaps even a shortcut to their destination of Blingdenstone. Thorin, ever the skeptic, predicted that this situation would end in tears, meaning some unfortunate event would befall them before they reached safety. Jimjar, never one to pass up an opportunity, proposed a wager on whether tears—literal or metaphorical—would indeed mark their journey. After carefully defining the terms, Thorin accepted the bet.

As they ventured deeper into the passage, the walls glistened with dark, dripping water that seemed to be increasing at an alarming rate. Thorin's keen dwarven senses detected that the water was not merely pooling but actively rising, flooding the entire complex. Daz investigated further and confirmed the dire situation: an earthquake had caused an underground reservoir to crack open, and water was pouring into the cave system. If they did not find an exit soon, they would all drown. Jimjar wryly noted the ironic fulfillment of his bet about tears, given that water was now threatening to engulf them all.

The party pressed forward through a square-shaped chamber, hoping it would lead to an exit rather than deeper into the flooded labyrinth. As Thorin led the charge, a gray ooze emerged from the rising waters, its amorphous form undulating menacingly. Zalthir stepped forward without hesitation, striking the creature with a series of powerful blows from his bare fists. The ooze stood no chance against the monk's relentless assault and was quickly dispatched. The party continued onward, navigating through the increasingly waterlogged passages.

At an intersection, they encountered another chamber where pools of water suggested more dangers lurking beneath the surface. Thorin attempted to engage the oozes from a distance by shooting darts at them, hoping to avoid the creatures' corrosive touch. However, Glabbagool—a massive gelatinous cube they had just befriended—inadvertently blocked the narrow passage with his ten-foot-by-ten-foot body. The party gently suggested that their new companion might be better suited to the rear of their formation. Daz finished off one of the gray oozes with a devastating spell, and Glabbagool cheerfully acknowledged that perhaps he should indeed stay in the back.

Their meeting with Glabbagool had been an unexpected delight. In a chamber containing the hovering remains of a drow, along with a dark metal mace and scattered coins, they had encountered the sentient gelatinous cube. Glabbagool explained that he had once been an ordinary cube, mindlessly cleaning floors and consuming debris, but had recently gained the ability to think and speak. He was confused by his newfound sentience but eager to understand the world around him. Thorin, seeing an opportunity for both companionship and utility, invited Glabbagool to join their adventure. The cube agreed enthusiastically, promising to eat anything they dropped on the floor and expressing gratitude when Thorin offered him a lucky foot as a welcoming gift.

As they continued through the dungeon, Thorin fell victim to a hidden pit trap, barely managing to grab the edge and pull himself out before a black pudding emerged from the depths. The party quickly decided to retreat to a larger area where they could keep the dangerous ooze at a distance, preventing it from corroding their weapons and armor. They successfully kited the creature, attacking it from range until it was defeated. Glabbagool expressed excitement about consuming the remains, and the party pressed onward, with Thorin now far more cautious about potential traps.

Eventually, they reached a large door that opened into a chamber containing a stone fountain filled with shallow, brackish water. A broken statue rested atop a pedestal in the center, and seven niches were carved into the walls, some empty and others holding strange, formless sculptures. Water seeped through a crack in one of the niches, adding to the sense of decay and abandonment. Glabbagool volunteered to investigate the room first, offering to touch the mysterious sculptures to see what would happen. Thorin encouraged him, promising to sing his praises at the tavern once they escaped the Underdark.

When Glabbagool touched one of the formless sculptures, it immediately materialized into a gray ooze. Zalthir sprang into action, dispatching the creature with a flurry of precise strikes. The party decided to activate the remaining statues one by one to avoid being overwhelmed, and Zalthir made quick work of each ooze that emerged. Glabbagool meticulously cleaned up the remnants, leaving the floor spotless. He then investigated the fountain itself and discovered a hidden cache of treasure: one hundred twelve silver pieces, three gold pieces, a drow dagger, and a vial of oil of slipperiness. The party acknowledged that they would need to wait a few days for Glabbagool to digest and pass the items, but they were grateful for the find nonetheless.

Returning to the main chamber, the party proceeded down an eastern passageway where the sound of pouring water grew louder. They discovered that water was entering through cracks in the ceiling, caused by the same earthquake that had trapped them. Thorin, drawing on his dwarven experience with caves and geology, examined the cracks and concluded that the entire complex was slowly flooding. Eldeth, another dwarf in the party, recognized the phenomenon as an underground river breaking through the stone. She shared stories of people who had successfully escaped similar situations by breaking through the rock, but she also warned of the risk of causing the water to flood even faster.

The party debated their options, considering whether to break through the wall or use magic to create air pockets. One member proposed using a spell to manipulate the water, creating temporary bubbles of air that would allow them to move through the flooded passages safely. They decided to proceed with this plan, creating a progression of air pockets and advancing five feet at a time. Glabbagool confirmed that he did not need to breathe, making him immune to the drowning threat, and he agreed to allow the party to attempt resurrection on any fallen members before consuming them.

Thorin used his considerable strength to break through the wall, causing more of the ceiling to collapse and doubling the water flow. However, this also revealed a diverted underground river that provided a path to safety. As the water level rose to the ceiling, the party swam upward for about thirty feet through the newly revealed passage. They successfully reached the surface, emerging into a large cavern where they could resume their journey. Jimjar declared that the adventure had not ended in tears after all, and he offered to call the bet a draw due to the charming and helpful Glabbagool joining their ranks. Thorin agreed, and the party celebrated their narrow escape, grateful for their new friend and the unexpected shortcut to Blingdenstone.
