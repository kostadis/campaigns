# Chapter 18.05 A Long List of Potential Transgressions

## 10th day of the 1st Tenday of Myrtul 1493

### Thorin

The Centipedes are dead. Thorin, kin of giants, stands over them. Their crunchy, small bodies splattered and smashed. 

The rush of the battle is over, and although he knows a barbarian who will rage until every living thing dies, that is not him. 

“Are there any more?” 

Zalthir mutters, “No.” 

“I need a rest.” 

And just as he is about to rest, the cleric speaks, “Let me help with that.” The words of prayer to the cleric’s dragon god fill the forest, and a few moments later, it’s like he has rested. 

“Where to now?”

Zalthir mutters, “We were chasing Droki.” 

But Droki’s trail is anything but difficult to follow. The Derro is a creature of habit. 

### Zalthir

The cave to the southwest beckons. And so he heads out to see what can be found. 

The tunnel opens into a natural cave, where several fungi dance to a silent tune. Three are five feet tall, and the others are half as tall. One of the small ones stands apart from the rest. 

“These are myconids. And those little ones are sprouts, like stool.”

He stares at their movements, “Stool described myconids and never described them as dancing frantically.” 

The madness of the Underdark has affected them as well. 

Behind the myconids are two spore servants; from their looks, they are quagoths. For a moment, he remembers Prince Derendil, the mad quagoth, who was a polymorphed elf. 

“They look harmless enough.” 

When he tells his allies of his discovery, they agree that perhaps this is an adventure for another time. 

And so he continues through the eerie fungi forest, following Droki’s trail.  

As he exits the forest, a curious creature appears and follows the cave north. The creature looks like a mushroom cap with two tentacles that have eye sockets at their end. And below the mushroom cap are tentacles that float. The creature vents gases, making a “flumph” sound. 

It smiles and starts speaking directly into his head. 

The voice is high-pitched and pleasant. 

“Hello\!” 

Zalthir masks his feelings well. The monks taught him how to do that, and he was very good at it. 

“You see a creature you have never seen before and have nothing to say? At the very least, don’t you want to know why I am speaking directly into your skulls?” It then giggles.  

Thorin starts speaking: “Everybody we come across here in the Underdark has tried to kill us, so we are wary.” 

The creature turns dark blue. “Understandable. Well, I am known as a flumph. Or at least that’s what others call us because we make a flumph sound when we move.”

The creature turns pink, “You didn’t make the obvious joke\! How wonderful\!” Then it pauses, turns green, “You don’t know what a flumph is?” 

Zalthir turns to Daz, “Perhaps he does?” 

Thorin blurts, “I am not trying to assume every Drow knows every creature of the Underdark, because that would be racist, but … Daz?” 

The creature turns pink. “How amusing.” Then it turns light blue, “I am a flumph. And my kind feeds on the psychic energy of creatures. We aren’t parasitic like the Mind Flayers; we feed on the residual background energies. And we do tend to meet one too many Mind Flayers. And Gith. Only the evil creatures.” 

Zalthir has heard that one before, but the Flumph answers Thorin's question of how they determine what is evil by saying, “Well, the evildoers are typically torturing, abusing, feeding, or doing other horrible things.” 

He notices that the creature turns a dark blue. 

Zathir wonders, “Have you seen anything evil?”

And just then, Zalthir’s head is flooded with images. 

“There you saw it\! A chamber with a circular pathway and a pyramid. The one known as Plinki has found this mighty and tasty obelisk and has been trying to make it do something evil.” 

Grygum’s horror at the egg breaks into his voice, “What are the strange markings on the egg?” 

“Plinki has recently converted to the Cult of Demogorgon and wishes to turn the egg into a dual-headed dragon that he can give to his master to ride\!” 

Just then, the Flumph takes on the teal color. 

“I feel so much better\!” 

Zalthir can sense that Thorin is unhappy when he says, “You just trauma dumped on us\!” 

“Trauma Dump? Why yes\! That’s what I do. I let out all the unpleasant memories inside and into the heroes' minds, and now I feel better\!” 

The creature turns green. “You will deal with them? You are heroes?” 

It then turns purple as it waits for an answer. 

Zalthir senses an opening, “Perhaps you can tell us where they are?” 

![][image12]  
And he learns more. There are two ways into the cave with the Obelisk: the main entrance and a side passageway. Through the side passageway, he sees a chamber with a derro that carries the insignia of office of the Council of Savants and the Gray Ghosts. 

And then he gets hit with more sadness. The creature is Trauma Dumping. He learns that the caves were once a hippy place, where Derro and Duergar would come to chill out, party, and be friends. Some months ago, the permanent twilight of the Faerzess stopped, and the cave complex became dark. Everyone was afraid, and the Faerzess blinked on brightly and then was off. And then it blinked once more, and then it returned to its usual color. But something had changed, and soon the various hippy friends stopped being friends. Instead of being a place of friendship, a place where folks could be happy, it became a place of murder and death and conspiracy. The mesa (Area 5\) was once a place where folks got together to get drunk and party, but now it was a war zone between the forces of the cult of Demogorgon and the empty scabbard killers.

### Grygum

   
The trauma dumping seems to affect Thorin, but Grygum is an orc, and a practical creature, and quite frankly, he’s seen worse. 

He looks at the creature, and it’s once again turned teal. He noticed that it had turned dark blue just before the trauma dump and then, as the trauma dump ended, became teal. 

Grygum takes note of this. 

“Did you say assassins?” 

“Yes\!” notes the Flumph. And again, it trauma dumps. This time, Grygum learns that the Flumph had found a tasty snack in the psychic energy released by a mind flayer. 

“A mind flayer?” 

“Yes, they are quite dangerous\! But you are heroes\!” 

The flump then shows how the mind flayer lives down a hallway. 

“Who else lives there?” 

The creature turns Magneta, then says, “I survive by not lEttin g mind flayers know I exist. I just know that the Mind Flayer is there, and that the assassins are also there. The ones you said had a magical light blade that tried to kill that duergar Werz.” 

Grygum snorts, “I never said that.” 

“Oh, right. You didn’t. You thought it.” 

Grygum pauses, “Can you help us?” 

“Absolutely. And don’t worry, although your deaths will be sad, I will absolutely trauma dump on someone else and feel better\!” 

Thorin glares. 

Grygum stares at Zalthir, who interrupts his train of thought \- “What are we trying to do here? I was just trying to kill some time before I got some magical equipment. Saving the Duergar from a mad king and defeating another cult of Demogorgon is not what I am here for.” 

Grygum nods, “I agree, but the egg. As a cleric of Bahamut, I must save the egg.” 

Flumph turns Green, “Why?” 

Grygum explains that Bahamut is the god of good dragons. He is committed to the principle that dragons have free will and that the pigmentation of their scales doesn’t pre-determine their personality. This idea that chromatic dragons are evil is the greatest evil. 

“We need help,” he mutters. Grygum turns to his friends. “We have the rest of the crew holed up in a seedy bar. We could get them weapons from Errde. She was willing to give us a lot of weapons. But we need a reason. And I think we do. We found evidence of the Grey Ghosts doing something evil. And we found evidence of the assassin’s guild. The only problem is how to convince her.”

He smiles and turns to Flumph \-  “Flumph, I’ll need you to trauma dump on someone.” 

The creature turns pink, “That would be so good. I have so much trauma.” 

“We should be able to get support from Gartokkar Xundorn and the Keepers of the Flame.” 

Grygum says to Flumph, “Make that two trauma dumps.” 

“Won’t that be enough evidence?” asks Zalthir. 

Daz, who has been quiet, says, “No. Without physical evidence, the word of a Flumph won’t be taken seriously.” 

Zalthir shrugs, “So, can you tell me what was written on the papers in the obelisk room?” 

Flumph looks at him, “No.” 

“What about the Gray Ghosts and the Council of Savants? Can you tell me anything about them that would convince Gartokkar or Errde?

The Flumph sighs, “Well, look, the Council used to be these nice people, and then after the blink, they became crazy. And now they are working with Plinki to try to summon Demogorgon or make him a two-headed dragon mount\! Look, I don’t read their minds. They are psychics, and they use psychic energy, and I feed on it. Ever since my friend Buppido went stark raving mad… ”

Grygum is silent.   

“How well guarded is this Council person?” 

“She has four guards.” 

Thorin growls, “We don’t have the resources to mount a full frontal assault. So we could just return, give the information, and then boogie out of here. We still have a boat. But I still like the  plan of escaping on the back of Thermbachaud.” 

Grygum mutters, “Bahamut commands that I save the egg.” 

### Daz

Daz, a thoughtful magician and long-time student of magic, was inspired by a commentator on Milo Goodbarrel's practice of writing daily. Milo had mentioned in his book that that practice made him such a good writer. Daz, impressed with Milo’s writing and wanting to improve, began mentally composing entries as if he were writing in a journal back home. This practice became a regular habit since his escape from Velkynvelve. 

*As we leave the Derro hovels and re-enter the city of blades, Zalthir’s question of what to do next is pressing.* 

*First, the factions hiding in the caves will not notice the deaths of Buppido and of some minor fauna.* 

*Now let’s consider our putative employers and their objectives:* 

1. *Errde of the Stone Guards believes the Council of Savants and the Grey Ghosts are behind the increasing madness of the city. She is also pretending not to think that assassins have targeted her clan. She believes that Droki is somehow helping the Council of Savants, and she wants proof of the Council of Savants' involvement with the corruption in the city. She also wants any actual evidence about the Empty Scabbard Killers.*  
2. *Gartokar Xundom of the Keepers of the Flame also wants the Grey Ghosts dead. But more importantly, wants the egg. The egg is their insurance policy against Themberchaude. The Keepers of the Flame consider the Council of Savants their worst enemies. And are engaged in a secret war with them. They, too, want the Council of Savants exposed.*   
3. *Themberchaude, the red dragon, believes that the Keepers of the Flame have plans and is not convinced his position is as secure as he wants to believe. More importantly, he’s not sure that he’s in charge. And he wants information about what’s going on.* 

*Gartokkar and Errde can’t get into the Whorlestone Caves without triggering a war. For Gartokkar, the need for war would reveal the loss of a crucial pillar of his power. In Errde’s case, it would expose her incompetence as the city guardian.* 

As Daz pens the last paragraph, he smiles. The path forward was clear, and indeed, it was very profitable. 

And so he returns to this journal \- 

*Grygum led us to the Keeper of the Flame. Once there, the Flumph trauma dumped on Gartokkar, who seemed curiously relieved. Then, he began making demands that we fetch the egg.* 

*This is how I remember the dialogue:* 

*Grygum \- “We need some help.”* 

*Gartokkar \- “You had some helpers, use them.”*

*Thorin \- “You are not employing them.”* 

*Gartokkar \- “I could give them badges\!”* 

*Zalthir \- “I think we need some compensation.”* 

*Gartokkar \- “Compensation? COMPENSATION.”* 

*The duergar smiled slyly when he realized we were as mercantile as he was.* 

*Gartokkar \- “I want proof of the involvement in demonic worship. Bring me the statue of Demogorgon.”* 

*And then I realized what this madman wanted. He intended to use the proof of the theft and the proof of the demonic worship to convince the Deepking that it was time to eliminate the scourge of the Derro from the city. He was planning to engage in a genocidal war against the Derro.* 

*I was horrified and curious. As for payment, Gartokkar was not against paying for a service—“2000.”* 

*It was then that Thorin and I entered the negotiations. First, we secured an upfront payment of 30% of the 2000 gold. Then we secured a confidentiality rider worth another 1000 gold. We attempted to raise the upfront fee to half of the 3000, but Gartokkar was not biting. In fact, for a moment, he looked like he would go mad and attack us.* 

*But he chose not to.* 

*As he counted the money, he said, “If you betray me, there is no cave, no pool of still water, no river, no place in the dark that I will not find you and hunt you down.”* 

Daz smiles, “Time to talk to Errde and negotiate terms with her.”
