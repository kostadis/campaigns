## Hgraam

Hgraam is a very large and very venerable stone giant residing in the Cairngorm Cavern in Gracklstugh. He sits on a rock at the entrance to a long, gigantic, well-polished tunnel that opens in the eastern wall south of Laduguer's Furrow. He is described as patient and deliberate in conversation, raising an eyebrow to signal Thorin's opening to speak.

When Thorin offers condolences regarding a stone giant who was driven mad, Hgraam acknowledges the tragedy, confirming that the party did not try to kill the giant but did try to stop him, and that the giant "was driven mad."

When Daz impatiently asks what is driving everyone mad, Hgraam delivers a cryptic prophecy: "Something evil stirs in the Underdark. The rock itself cries in pain and horror, and a madness creeps from the blackest depths. Pay heed to the signs surrounding you. A cave with two faces. Rock devoured, and the land overgrown. The pebble believes itself flesh. The earth rejects its wards, and the tunnels shake in fury. By these portents, you shall know of evil's presence and of evil's face. This is what the stones tell me." He is described as being particularly attuned to the rocks, and his prophecy confirms that the demonic incursions throughout the Underdark are not localized incidents.

Hgraam shows visible disapproval of Daz's impetuousness and turns back to Thorin, offering to answer his questions. He tells Thorin that the best way to the Dreamlands is through the city of the Deep Gnomes (Blingdenstone), corroborating Jimjar's earlier claims.

Regarding the tunnels underneath Gracklstugh, Hgraam sighs and explains the Deep King's predicament: "Many have told the Deep King that what goes underneath the city affects what goes on in his city. And what goes on in his city affects what goes on below. And much of what goes on below is evil. The Deep King has tried to keep order and control, and the more he tries, the more the order escapes and tries to take the city from him."

When asked about the Grey Ghosts, Hgraam laments the Deep King's deterioration: "The Deep King creates so many enemies. He used to be so calm. He and I would watch the stones grow. But something has happened to him. He has become paranoid towards old and new friends." This implies a past friendship between Hgraam and the Deep King.

When Thorin asks about a new advisor, Hgraam identifies **Shal** as someone who has become close to the Deep King, and ever since their friendship formed, the Deep King has been "unwilling to listen to any sage advice." Hgraam has asked to meet Shal, but they refuse.

Before departing, Hgraam stands, addresses Daz as "too hasty," and presents a gift: a 19-inch shard of quartz crystal called the Stonespeaker Crystal, which allows the user to cast Speak with Animals, Speak with the Dead, and Speak with Plants, and also aids in casting divinations. His assistant brings the crystal to the party. He then heads back into his cave.

## Shal

Shal is identified by Hgraam as an advisor to the Deep King of Gracklstugh. Ever since Shal became friends with the Deep King, the monarch has become paranoid toward old and new friends alike and unwilling to listen to sage advice. Shal refuses to meet with Hgraam despite the stone giant's requests. No other details about Shal's identity, race, or motivations are revealed.

## Droki

Droki is a derro courier operating in the West Cleft District of Gracklstugh. He is described as dirty, with "a pale face under a floppy hat fitted with two tentacle-like things that bob up and down in time with his brisk pace." He mutters repetitively: "Droki is late! They will be so angry! Stupid, stupid, stupid! Delays, blocks, procrastination, obstructions, pfah! No time, no rhyme, not mine! No! Time!"

Multiple factions in Gracklstugh use Droki because he can traverse the dangerous Derro slums safely and can be trusted to deliver messages and keep secrets. Both Errde Blackstaff and the Keepers of the Flame want to find him. Buppido confirms that Droki can be trusted to deliver messages to groups in the Whorlstone Caverns. The ghost Pelek notes that Droki works for the Council of Savants and is considered "very peculiar" and possibly mad.

Droki walks through the Derro district at an unnaturally fast pace, as if something magical aids his speed, yet he doesn't seem to care if anyone follows him. He wanders seemingly without purpose between houses and merchants, muttering to himself and to other Derro. Eventually he stops between a cluster of hovels in West Cleft, lifts scraps of cloth and canvas against a wall, revealing a narrow fissure, and jumps into the crack leading to the Whorlstone Caverns. A wisp of eerie fog floats out as he disappears.

The party follows his trail through the caverns, finding it well-worn and marked with junk the Derro drops. His trail leads through a crack in the wall too small for anyone to fit through, with Pygmywort and Bigwig mushrooms on either side — suggesting he uses the shrinking mushrooms to pass through. Pelek confirms the tunnel beyond is "tiny all the way through." The party loses his direct trail but continues following his path through the caverns.

## Buppido

Buppido is a derro who had been traveling with the party and was tied up in a back room at Ghohlbrorn's Lair after previously committing murder. When asked about Droki, he laughs and confirms he knows the courier, explaining that Droki can be trusted to deliver messages to groups in the Whorlstone Caverns and keep secrets.

When asked about the Derro community, Buppido becomes enraged, screaming about the Council of Savants who "promised freedom and failed to deliver freedom." He insists he must return to save his people, declaring: "The divine mission requires that I go back! The Council of Savants will fail my people! I must go and save them!"

Buppido guides the party to the western gate of Laduguer's Furrow, demonstrating knowledge of the city's history. He explains that the Duergar built guardhouses to watch their derro slaves in the West Cleft, which were abandoned when Deepking Tarngardt (grandfather of the current monarch) liberated the derro. His presence among the party tempers the hatred directed at the non-Derro members as they pass through the Derro district.

Once inside the Whorlstone Caverns, Buppido rushes away from the party toward the entrance. He is later discovered in a southwestern cave, surrounded by a shrine of humanoid remains arranged in a spiral pattern, in varying stages of decomposition. He has been working busily at something on the floor and greets the party: "I didn't expect you to find me here, with my shrine nearly finished! And power! My faithful are finally honoring me!"

He declares himself a divine figure and attacks the party, shrieking with manic energy and brandishing a makeshift weapon, supported by skeletons. After the party defeats his skeletal minions, Daz kills him with magic missiles. Thorin eulogizes him: "He was a crazy one, but he was our crazy one."

Pelek later reveals that Buppido was his business partner, working with the Council of Savants to move product out of the city without Duergar taxes. Buppido had lured Pelek to the caves and murdered him, cutting up his body. Pelek observed that the Faerzress glowed brightly a few months ago, and after that event, Buppido's temperament changed — he became convinced he was a divine figure.

## Jimjar

Jimjar is found at Ghohlbrorn's Lair playing a game of chance when the party arrives looking for Buppido. He points them to a room out back where Buppido is tied up. His earlier claims about the route to the Dreamlands through the city of the Deep Gnomes are corroborated by Hgraam.

## Pelek

Pelek is the ghost of a Svirfneblin (deep gnome) merchant whose appearance is marred by a gaping wound that looks freshly made. He emerges from the ground in Buppido's shrine cave after learning Buppido is dead, exclaiming "Wonderful!"

Pelek explains that his mother named him after an actor, which caused friction with his father, who was jealous, creating "an undercurrent of hurt and shame" in his family. He became a merchant and aspired to be an actor but engaged in self-sabotage.

He was Buppido's business partner. Buppido was working with the Council of Savants and promised he could move product out of Gracklstugh without dealing with Duergar taxes. Buppido lured Pelek to the caves and murdered him, cutting his body into pieces and arranging the bones as part of his shrine. Pelek has been trapped within 30 feet of his bones ever since, for less than a year.

Pelek provides a key piece of information: a few months ago, the Faerzress "glowed brightly" and then "it was almost as if it was being sucked out, and went dark and then came back brighter than before." After this event, Buppido's temperament changed, and he became convinced he was divine. He dismisses Daz's theory that the Faerzress blinking could have broken walls between planes as a "ghost story."

Pelek requests that the party collect some of his bones and bring them to Blingdenstone so he can stop being a ghost. He mentions his hand went "scurrying about" with a family obsidian ring on it, which the party can keep if found, though only a few bones are needed for his release.

He offers practical advice about Blingdenstone: the party should deny any association to Lolth or Menzoberranzan, and avoid any references to the number eight or spiders.

Pelek also assists the party by scouting through a crack in the wall too small for anyone to fit through. He reports that the tunnel is "tiny all the way through" and identifies Pygmywort and Bigwig mushrooms on the other side — magical fungi that shrink and enlarge the consumer, respectively. He accompanies the party as they continue through the Whorlstone Caverns, carried via one of his bones by Grygum.

## Errde Blackstaff

Errde Blackstaff is mentioned as one of the faction leaders in Gracklstugh who was anxious to find a non-Duergar to venture into the Derro slums to find Droki. The reason is that the Derro slums are too dangerous for Duergar to enter, and sending Duergar through the Derro district would trigger a bloodbath.

## Thermbechaud

Thermbechaud is mentioned only as a geographic reference point — the party's journey to the Cairngorm Cavern begins from Thermbechaud's cave.

## Tarngardt

Tarngardt is identified as the grandfather of the current Deep King of Gracklstugh. He is credited with liberating the derro, allowing them to spread from the West Cleft District into the East Cleft District. The Duergar guardhouses built to watch the derro slaves were abandoned after his decree.
