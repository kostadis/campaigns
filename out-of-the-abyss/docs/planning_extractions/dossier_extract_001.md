## Welnaste Mizzrym

Welnaste is the leader of the Drow caravan that captured and transported the party through the Underdark. She delivered the prisoners to Velkynvelve, announcing to Ilvara that she had "the shipment." She is identified as a "house sister" to Ilvara, suggesting they share a connection through one of the Drow noble houses. She warned Ilvara that money had been exchanged for the prisoners and that Ilvara would be "held to account" if they were lost or died before the next caravan arrived to take them to Menzoberranzan. Her last known status is departing Velkynvelve after completing the handoff.

## Ilvara

Ilvara is the commander of the Velkynvelve outpost. She is described as a haughty, arrogant, well-dressed, and coiffed female Drow cleric. Daz remarked that she "fits the type" of a Drow priestess. She received the prisoners from Welnaste Mizzrym and ordered Jorlan to take them to the cell, stating, "We will use them until the caravan arrives." She is Welnaste's "house sister," and is at the center of a complicated personal dynamic: Jorlan was formerly her "number one squeeze" but has been replaced by Shoor after Jorlan was injured. She remains in command at Velkynvelve.

## Jorlan

Jorlan is a male Drow fighter stationed at Velkynvelve. He has a disfigured face and is missing two fingers. Ilvara ordered him to escort the prisoners to their cell. According to Jimjar, Jorlan was formerly Ilvara's primary romantic partner but was replaced by Shoor after his injury. He previously possessed a wand of globs. His disfigurement and demotion suggest a significant fall from favor at the outpost.

## Shoor

Shoor is a male Drow fighter at Velkynvelve. According to Jimjar, he is now Ilvara's "primary squeeze," having replaced Jorlan after Jorlan was injured. He was present alongside Ilvara and Jorlan when the prisoners arrived.

## Asha

Asha is a Drow cleric at Velkynvelve. According to Jimjar, she is "angling for the top job," suggesting she is positioning herself to challenge or replace Ilvara as the outpost's commander.

## Topsy

Topsy is a female deep gnome prisoner at Velkynvelve. She introduced herself and her twin brother Turvy to the party. She advocated for the group to escape to Blingdenstone, her hometown. She noted that Jimjar is also from their town and seemed dismissive of him, accusing him of "always pretending he's better than us."

## Turvy

Turvy is a male deep gnome prisoner at Velkynvelve and Topsy's twin brother. He is quiet and mumbles rather than speaking clearly. Topsy translates for him, relaying his greetings to Grygum and his suggestion about Blingdenstone.

## Sarith

Sarith is a Drow prisoner at Velkynvelve who is covered in some kind of fungal rot. He was convicted of murdering a fellow Drow but insists it was a setup—though he immediately contradicts himself, wondering aloud if he actually did commit the crime. He is terrified of being sent to Menzoberranzan to be sacrificed or turned into a drider. Daz assessed him as not sane. Despite this, Grygum concluded that Sarith is a genuine prisoner rather than a spy. Later, Sarith provided useful intelligence, warning the group about a gray ooze in the pools at the bottom of the cave that could be a hazard during an escape attempt, though he devolved into muttering while sharing this information.

## Shuushar the Awakened

Shuushar is a Kuo-Toa prisoner at Velkynvelve who claims to have "found enlightenment." He speaks soothingly and says he has discovered a way to resist the madness that plagues his people. He was traveling to his home settlement of Sloobludop near the Darklake when he was captured, and he offered to guide the group there, claiming extensive knowledge of the winding roads of the Underdark. Daz is cautiously interested but uncertain whether a Kuo-Toa can truly be relied upon.

## Prince Derendil

Prince Derendil is a Quaggoth prisoner at Velkynvelve who claims to be a prince of the Kingdom of Nelrindenvane in the High Forest, cursed into Quaggoth form by an evil wizard named Terrestor. He speaks with a rarefied accent typical of upper-kingdom elves and demands to be treated with the respect his station commands. Daz immediately identified him as a liar, knowing that no Kingdom of Nelrindenvane exists in the High Forest, but decided not to confront him, reasoning that the "Fake Prince" could be a useful ally during an escape. Derendil also provided tactical intelligence, noting there are three Drow guards stationed right across from the cell door.

## Stool

Stool is a Myconid child prisoner at Velkynvelve—a sentient mushroom creature described as a "conscious form of mycelium." Stool is a member of a circle called the Neverlight Grove and eagerly offered to guide the party there, claiming it would be safe and that their sovereign would be thrilled to see them. Stool communicates by releasing spores that allow telepathic-like communication without speaking.

## Buppido

Buppido is a Derro prisoner at Velkynvelve. Despite derro having a reputation as "some kind of dwarven perversion" (according to Eldeth), Buppido presents as personable, gregarious, and talkative. He claims to be guided by a "divine plan" and urged the group to escape, saying "there is only one path forward." He showed interest in Grygum as a fellow follower of gods and suggested future cooperation. However, he also coldly proposed sending Runc ahead as a distraction so the rest could escape, and when asked why he wouldn't do it himself, he called himself "more of a big-picture thinker" and cryptically hinted at hidden power he doesn't want to reveal. He refused to let Jimjar bet on the divine plan, calling it sacrilege.

## Eldeth

Eldeth is a female dwarf prisoner at Velkynvelve. She was happy to meet fellow dwarf Thorin but was taken aback by his Giant accent and his positive relationship with giants, expressing skepticism that giants could make peace with "little folk" given the devastation of the War of the Giants. She provided practical tactical counsel, warning the group that the cell blocks magic (preventing Daz from casting spells) and noting the overwhelming opposition of nine drow, multiple Quaggoths, and unknown numbers of giant spiders. She showed strong prejudices—sneering at Runc and mocking orcs for their defeat at Gauntlygrym, and dismissing derro as a "dwarven perversion." She suggested Gauntlygrym as an escape destination, claiming the Shield Dwarves now hold it.

## Runc

Runc is an orc prisoner at Velkynvelve. He was disappointed to learn that Grygum is a cleric of Bahamut rather than Gruumsh, as Runc had fought poorly and was seeking absolution from his god. Eldeth mocked him for the orcish defeat at Gauntlygrym. Buppido proposed using Runc as a sacrificial distraction during an escape attempt.

## Jimjar

Jimjar is a deep gnome prisoner at Velkynvelve from the same town as Topsy and Turvy. He is characterized by his compulsive gambling, constantly offering bets on anything and everything. Despite this eccentricity, he proved to be a valuable source of intelligence, providing detailed information about the interpersonal dynamics among the Drow guards: Jorlan's demotion as Ilvara's lover in favor of Shoor after Jorlan's injury, Jorlan's former possession of a wand of globs, and Asha's ambitions to take the top position. He referred to these relationships as "Drow polycules."

## Terrestor

Terrestor is an evil wizard mentioned by Prince Derendil as the one who cursed him into Quaggoth form and exiled him from the Kingdom of Nelrindenvane. Since Daz knows Derendil's entire story is fabricated, Terrestor likely does not exist.
