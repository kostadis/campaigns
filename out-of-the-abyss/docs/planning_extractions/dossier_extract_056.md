## Milo Goodbarrel

Milo Goodbarrel does not appear in person in this chunk. He is referenced as the signatory on the secondary inscription on the back of the Tarvis Ulain monument in Daggerford's market square. The inscription reads "Witnessed and recorded for the Gauntlet" and is signed by Milo Goodbarrel, identified as the head of some publishing concern and a member of the Order of the Gauntlet. Thorin notes that Milo personally signed off on the monument. No further information about him is given, and he is not present at any point during these events.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool is being transported in the party's bag in miniaturized form alongside the captured House T'sarran spy. When the party arrives at Candlekeep's gate and opens the bag for the guards, both Glabbagool and the spy are visible, which draws the attention of the First Reader. During the subsequent discussion about what to do with the spy, Thorin asks for Glabbagool's perspective on the matter. Glabbagool reports that he has been occupied with prison-watching, which he finds somewhat boring, but that the spy provides entertainment value by repeatedly attempting to kill him. He describes their dynamic: she tries to kill him, he slaps her around, she tries to run, he expands, she screams, he attempts to teach her shadow monk meditation, she screams about the meditation, and then they talk. He characterizes them as having become great friends. When Zalthir confirms that watching and containing the spy constitutes legitimate pre-monk training in patience under provocation, Glabbagool takes this with complete seriousness and declares he will keep an eye on her, fixing six pseudo-eyes on the spy simultaneously. Later, a Candlekeep sage of xenobiology approaches after hearing that a gelatinous cube is being taught the ways of the shadow. When the sage questions whether a mindless digestive system can achieve monastic discipline, Zalthir vigorously defends Glabbagool as a student who is already walking, already learning, and already performing the difficult work of containing an actively hostile prisoner. The sage is satisfied by this defense and produces a vial of Resonant Slime as a gift, which Glabbagool receives with evident pleasure. Glabbagool remains in the bag with the spy at the end of these events.

## Dawnbringer

Dawnbringer is present throughout these events as Thorin's sentient sword. At the River Shining Tavern in Daggerford, she encounters Elin, the silent seven-year-old child. When Thorin mentions Dawnbringer could help, she performs a lesser restoration on the child without waiting for further invitation, announcing "Let me bring the light to you." The restoration is sufficient to allow Elin to speak one sentence — "The wells are not wells anymore" — before the child falls silent again. The restoration does not fully resolve whatever is affecting Elin; the party concludes the problem is something more profound than lesser restoration can fix. At the River Shining that evening, Thorin takes her outside for what is described as his first real encounter with the sea. She lights up everything around them, which defeats the stated purpose of going outside to look at the sea, but Thorin finds the experience peaceful regardless. At Candlekeep, Thorin brings her to Master Archivist Philemon's study for what amounts to a therapeutic consultation. Philemon initially assesses her as a specimen — late Netherese-era recovery, sun-worship origin, sustained darkness exposure of approximately three to four thousand years — before Thorin corrects him and introduces her as a friend. Philemon revises his assessment upward, noting a better bond between Thorin and Dawnbringer than he had feared. When Philemon asks directly about her previous wielder — the corpse she was found in — Dawnbringer goes quiet with the particular silence of someone confronting a long-avoided subject. Philemon's prescription is a two-stage process: first, she needs to remember what daylight feels like without needing to fight; second, when ready, she needs to grieve. He notes she cannot be polished, correcting his initial clinical instinct mid-sentence. Thorin accepts both stages of the process on her behalf and expresses hope she will grow in the course of it. The pronoun question regarding Dawnbringer's gender remains unresolved at the end of the session, with Thorin alternating between she/her and they/them without settling on either.

## The House T'sarran Spy (Princess Ebonmire)

The spy is being transported in the party's bag alongside Glabbagool. When the bag is opened at Candlekeep's gate, she is visible and draws the attention of the First Reader. Gate Warden Kalan Strongbranch is summoned to address the situation. When he explains that Candlekeep will not keep prisoners indefinitely — only hold them before eventually releasing them at the gate with a warning — the spy turns to Kalan directly and announces she is a prisoner who wishes to be formally jailed by Candlekeep's guards, delivering this as though it requires no argument. The First Reader informs her that as a technical guest of the adventuring party, the decision is the party's, not hers. She reacts with considerable displeasure and expresses several opinions about this arrangement. The party decides to retain custody of her. She remains in the bag with Glabbagool at the end of these events, continuing her attempts to kill him and her objections to his meditation instruction.

## Stroud

Stroud does not appear in person in this chunk. He is referenced in connection with two monuments. The Tarvis Ulain monument in Daggerford's market square bears a secondary inscription in Dwarven — *The move is yours* — which Daz identifies as sharing language with the Castle Ward statue in Waterdeep previously connected to Stroud. The village priest acknowledges the monument was funded by a Metalworkers Guild that may have had Stroudite members, and explains the Stroudite inscription was retained because it was a gift and because Tarvis was, after all, trying to save everyone regardless of what happened in the chess game. At the Burning Wizard Inn, Grygum pays the resident bard a silver piece to sing something about Stroud's famous chess game. She produces a love song in which the chess game serves as a metaphor for romantic longing, stripping the theological stakes down to something more singable. Grygum receives this with polite resignation and observes these things must be taken with a grain of salt. The chess game itself and the question of what the Stroudite phrase means on a Bahamutian monument remain unresolved.

## Tarvis Ulain

Tarvis Ulain does not appear in person in this chunk, being dead. His monument in Daggerford's market square is the first thing the party encounters upon arrival. The monument is a bronze-cast severed forearm mounted on a stone pillar, not holding anything. The primary inscription identifies him as a paladin who died in Daggerford three years prior, killed while breaking up a Cult of the Dragon recruitment operation in the town itself. He served Bahamut. The monument was funded by the Metalworkers Guild two years after the war. The secondary inscription in Dwarven reads *The move is yours*, which the party identifies as sharing Stroudite language, and the village priest explains this was a gift whose inscription was retained. A third inscription on the back in Common reads "Witnessed and recorded for the Gauntlet," signed by Milo Goodbarrel. The village priest sells pewter miniature reproductions of Tarvis's hand cast as charitable fundraising for the Order of the Gauntlet. Grygum purchases one. The monument is described as one of many markers across the North of a war that touched everywhere, Daggerford having escaped the Uthgardt barbarian hordes but not the Cult.

## Vareth

Vareth does not appear directly in this chunk beyond being named as Grygum's assigned Candlekeep handler by the First Reader. Grygum mentally notes Tarvis Ulain's monument and the open questions it raises as something to file for Vareth as a theological puzzle. No further information about Vareth is provided.

## Khell-Vire

Khell-Vire is a half-elven monk approximately two hundred years old who left the Garden of Shadow eighty years prior after refusing an unspecified order. He lives and works among the Shadow Discipline Lineage Records in Candlekeep's lower-gallery office, and still wears a faded shadow monk under-robe beneath Candlekeep gray. He is Zalthir's assigned handler. When Zalthir arrives, Khell-Vire has a note on his desk reading *Zalthir, the Underdark, until the Bone King sings* — neither man can identify its origin. He opens the consultation by asking what Zalthir was sent toward rather than why he was sent, a distinction Zalthir finds immediately irritating. He clarifies that the question is about what Zalthir has become through his experiences, not about factual knowledge. He arranges for a blind monk to assess Zalthir through practical demonstration rather than conducting the assessment himself, explaining his role is to provide the door, not to walk through it with Zalthir. After the formal trial concludes — including Zalthir's catastrophic skill rolls, his exceptional combat performance, and his demonstration of the shadow-strike technique developed in the Underdark — Khell-Vire delivers a final assessment: Zalthir's skills show a lack of focus and training, but the combination of shadow step and hit is what redeems all other deficiencies. He observes that Zalthir is beginning to remember all the shadow monks who drove him to develop his own approach. He awards Zalthir a specialized shadow technique from a list of three options. He raises an eyebrow at Zalthir's final demonstration and appears genuinely surprised by the result.

## The Blind Monk (Candlekeep)

The blind monk enters Zalthir's trial without introduction and immediately demands a standard shadow step. Upon observing the result, he provides precise technical critique: Zalthir skips the third breath, does not enter his ki in the hara, lets it bleed into his fingertips like a poison, and is technically wrong in every scroll. He then notes Zalthir arrived at his destination three seconds faster than the Grandmaster and asks to see what else he hasn't learned. When Zalthir asks whether he can punch the monk, the monk permits it and they spar to an even result. The formal skills demonstration that follows goes poorly for Zalthir: the Weightless Shadow ends with Zalthir falling and the monk declaring he has learned nothing; the Efficient Strike produces another failure and the observation that Zalthir apparently did not practice forms while traveling; the Eldritch Claw stealth attempt also fails. He walks away, then stops when Zalthir lands a 30 to hit against him — a result that visibly impresses him and prompts him to reconsider his approach. He sets a final challenge: he will throw a brick, and Zalthir must shadow step to intercept it mid-air. The brick is destroyed by a roll of 29. The monk acknowledges the results speak for themselves, asks whether any lesson might be offered, and receives the shadow tentacles as Zalthir's answer. He responds that the way forward is in the combination. He participates in the final demonstration of Zalthir's Underdark-developed shadow-strike technique and is present when Khell-Vire delivers the concluding assessment.

## Philemon (Master Archivist)

Master Archivist Philemon is an older human scholar at Candlekeep who is assigned as Thorin's handler. His study smells of old ink and something faintly mineral. When Thorin and Dawnbringer arrive, he immediately identifies Dawnbringer as a remarkable specimen — late Netherese-era recovery, sun-worship origin, sustained darkness exposure of approximately three to four thousand years — and reaches for a clay tablet to take notes. When Thorin corrects him and introduces Dawnbringer as a friend, Philemon adjusts his entire approach, sets the tablet down, and engages differently. He notes with evident relief that the bond between Thorin and Dawnbringer is better than he had feared, implying he had concerns before the meeting. He explains the psychological dimension of Dawnbringer's situation: the deep unease that comes from long periods in darkness, disuse, or possession by someone who doesn't truly see a sentient item as a being. He addresses Dawnbringer directly rather than talking around her. He asks about her previous wielder and the circumstances in which she was found. When Dawnbringer goes quiet at that question, he allows the silence to hold. He catches himself mid-prescription — starting to recommend polishing, then stopping when he registers that she cannot be polished — and resets to an honest assessment. His prescription is two-stage: first, she needs to remember daylight without needing to fight; second, when ready, she needs to grieve. He frames the first work as light and the second as mourning, and confirms the party is enrolled in both.

## Sister Yvenne

Sister Yvenne is the Candlekeep Avowed assigned as Daz's handler in the Hall of Divination. She manages the consultation with the practiced patience of someone who has spent decades working with scholars. When Daz opens by asking whether any of the passing tomes might help with his headache, she responds with gentle but firm redirection, asking whether that is truly the question he wishes to have answered. She explains Candlekeep's reciprocal information policy — information for information — and waits for Daz to provide his half of the exchange. When Daz gives a full honest account of his situation (waking in a jail cell with no memories, escaping the Underdark, arriving with a headache and no knowledge of who he is), she expresses genuine surprise and responds that this is quite a bit of information. She proceeds to explain the structure of Menzoberranzan's noble houses, the rule of complete elimination in house warfare, and the story of a house that failed to kill a single surviving child and was destroyed for it. When Daz asks whether he is that blithering idiot child, she does not answer directly and moves to a list of houses. She reads the list, which includes the term *struck* for the fourth seat, and confirms Daz is asking the right question about why that word is different from the others. She explains that the fourth seat has ritual archive privileges in Menzoberranzan, making a struck fourth seat an expensive and deliberate act requiring specific infrastructure. She tells Daz there is one house she cannot speak of in the room because the public record is sealed, and redirects him to other doors she cannot point to directly. She provides a heraldry book, *Geography and Cities, Menzoberranzan, Pillars of Woe*, approximately two hundred years old, which contains a footnote referencing *Daz'issin* (lit. *small shadow*) and an appendix that has been mechanically removed with a razor at least fifty years prior. When Daz asks whether *Daz'issin* might be his full name, she describes it as a softening name that mothers use for boys they want to live, notes there are not many uses of it in the past three centuries, and observes that most houses that used it did not last. When asked directly if she knows his name, she confirms she does, then redirects to the question of his patron. Her body language in the final exchange — when Daz asks what would happen if the relevant houses were not all successfully eliminated — disagrees with her words, a discrepancy Daz notices and interprets, correctly, as meaningful silence.

## Bookwyrm (First Reader)

The First Reader of Candlekeep — a dragonborn sage referred to as Bookwyrm — receives the party at the gate. She assigns handlers without ceremony: Daz to Sister Yvenne, Zalthir to Khell-Vire, Thorin to Master Archivist Philemon, Grygum to Vareth. She notices the House T'sarran spy when the party opens their bag at the gate and finds the arrangement unacceptable. She informs the spy, when the spy attempts to claim formal prisoner status under Candlekeep's custody, that as a technical guest of the adventuring party the decision belongs to the party rather than to Candlekeep. She explains Candlekeep's position on prisoners through Kalan Strongbranch rather than directly: they hold but do not keep, releasing eventually at the gate with a warning.

## Kalan Strongbranch

Kalan Strongbranch is Candlekeep's Gate Warden, summoned to address the situation of the House T'sarran spy when she is discovered in the party's bag. He is efficient and unapologetic in laying out Candlekeep's position: the bell tower has comfortable cells with localized anti-magic that are very rarely used; major offenders are exiled rather than imprisoned; if the party surrenders the spy, Candlekeep will hold her temporarily, will not deliver her to House T'sarran, will not execute her, and will eventually release her at the gate with a warning. He frames this explicitly as a delay, not a solution, and makes clear that if the party needs something else they will have to arrange it themselves. He does not editorialize about what the party ought to do.
