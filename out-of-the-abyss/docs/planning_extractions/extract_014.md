## Revealed Information

**Bahamut / Protanther (referenced, absent)**
- The dwarf Stroud's chess victory over the Gold Dragon Protanther at the Well of the Dragons is a significant recent historical event that has generated a new following for Bahamut among non-dragon humanoids.
- Three competing interpretations of the event circulate: (1) Protanther chose to lose to justify siding against Tiamat; (2) Stroud was an agent of individual agency and chance; (3) Bahamut directly intervened, moving Stroud's hand and concealing the move from Protanther.
- Bahamut has historically not sought faithful among non-dragons, making this new humanoid following a notable religious development.
- Chess grandmasters are reportedly baffled both by Protanther missing the move after 400 years of study and by Stroud's inability to coherently explain it, lending credibility to the divine-intervention interpretation.
