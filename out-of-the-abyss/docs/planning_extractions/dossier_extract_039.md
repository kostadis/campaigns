## Glabbagool

The intelligent gelatinous cube traveling with the party expressed curiosity about being hired and receiving payment after the battle in the Trader's Grotto, ultimately revealing that what he truly desired was friends and a sense of purpose. When Grygum suggested using him to clear the rubble path, Glabbagool eagerly agreed, expressing excitement about having a job and feeling needed. He spent about an hour clearing a passageway through the rubble, impressing a gnome from the mining operations who expressed interest in hiring him for future work.

As the party followed the ooze trail deeper into the caves, Glabbagool began behaving strangely, claiming to hear voices calling him forward. Zalthir persuaded him to stay with the group by appealing to their friendship and promising protection from corrupting forces, which Glabbagool accepted, pleased by the idea of being protected by his friends. He indicated the mysterious voices were coming from the south, guiding the party in that direction. Upon entering the vast cavern filled with countless oozes and the disembodied voice of the Pudding King, Glabbagool expressed discomfort, noting that oozes generally do not speak, which he found unsettling.

## Dorbo Diggermattock

Chief Dorbo Diggermattock was credited by a sergeant for hiring the adventurers to solve the ooze problem, though one citizen accused the Chief of caring only for wealth and neglecting the common people. When the party returned to report their findings, Dorbo questioned if the ooze problem had been dealt with. Upon learning about the Pudding King and his army of hundreds of oozes, he reacted with alarm, calling the situation catastrophic. He called Grygum's plan to use a gnome army distraction "the dumbest he had ever heard," but was silenced by Senni. He raised objections about the gnomes not being equipped to fight oozes and refused to include the Gold Whisker wererat clan, whom he considered squatters. After Daz seized control of the discussion and demanded cooperation, Dorbo agreed to the party's terms after a moment of stunned silence.

## Senni Diggermattock

Senni Diggermattock silenced Chief Dorbo when he dismissed the party's plan, asking if he had a better one. She suggested a full clan meeting including the Stoneheart Enclave and the Miners Guild. She advocated for working with the Gold Whisker clan for the common good, highlighting their abilities as an asset against oozes. After Dorbo agreed to the party's terms, Senni tasked the adventurers with bringing the Gold Whisker clan on board to help fight the oozes.

## The Pudding King

The Pudding King was discovered by Daz's bat familiar sitting on a slime-covered throne in a cave illuminated by phosphorescent lichen, with pools of green slime surrounding him. He was described as a small gnome who was madly holding court and speaking of cleansing Blingdenstone. He was confirmed as the source of the disembodied voice that had filled the vast ooze-filled cavern, welcoming visitors and proclaiming the coming of a Faceless Lord (Jubilex) to enhance Blingdenstone. He controls hundreds of oozes and is surrounded by them, with green slime covering the ceiling of his throne room. The party determined that incapacitating him without a coordinated effort would unleash the uncontrolled ooze army upon Blingdenstone. He is the central source of the ooze problem plaguing the city. Last known whereabouts: his slime-covered throne deep in the caves south of Blingdenstone.

## Jimjar

Jimjar, a deep gnome, placed a bet with Chief Dorbo during the planning discussion, expressing confidence in the adventurers' success and subtly supporting their mission.
