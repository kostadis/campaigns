## Asha Vandree

Asha was approached by Daz in a deception attempt where he tried to pass himself off as divinely touched by Lolth, using Dancing Lights absorbed into his spider familiar as proof. She was not convinced — she recognized the con immediately, having seen this exact type of charlatan performance before in drow society. She called it out directly: "You are trying to test me, to see if I was one of those fake followers of Lolth, who are willing to believe every charlatan magic user that is somehow being possessed by the power of Lolth."

Rather than walking away, Asha constructed her own explanation for Daz's behavior — that he and his companions had been infected by Zuggtmoy's spores and were deluded, possibly a trick of Ilvara's. She then chose to keep Daz anyway, rationalizing: "Perhaps my prayers have been answered through this… confused vessel." She accepted Glabbagool as a bound familiar, which fit her framework of Daz as a deluded but genuinely powerful mage.

Asha revealed deep, personal hatred for Ilvara — not political calculation but something built over long proximity to a corrupted superior. She described Ilvara as a former high priestess of Lolth who had been seduced by Zuggtmoy's abomination, the idea that Lolth wishes to be married. She reported that Ilvara's allies had turned into mindless fungal creatures. She declared: "We need to kill her! We need to butcher her! We need to send her to an unearthly grave! I fear that her disease will spread."

Asha provided critical tactical intelligence: only she and Jorlan remained uninfected — she through devotion to Lolth, Jorlan through hatred. They maintained twenty feet of distance from the infected and kept separate food. The mushroom artifact from Neverlight Grove was the mechanism of corruption. The Big Heart Fungus was the primary weapon and would require sustained damage to destroy.

When Daz asked what the party would receive for killing Ilvara, Asha first offered "the gratitude of Lolth," then added Ilvara's magical items as payment — with the caveat that they might be contaminated.

Her tactical role in the fight: she would not enter at the start but would hold the perimeter, handling anything coming from outside. She had always needed someone else to do the lethal work at close range, and the party served that purpose. She was explicit about what was riding on the fight — the Zuggtmoy-aligned symbol of office left standing would act as a seed of further corruption.

## Ilvara

Ilvara is described as a former high priestess of Lolth who has been corrupted by Zuggtmoy's influence, seduced by the idea that Lolth wishes to be married. She brought a mushroom artifact from Neverlight Grove that served as the mechanism of corruption, turning her allies into mindless fungal creatures.

During the battle, Ilvara was positioned on an elevated platform. Zalthir teleported directly to her and struck twice, dealing 31 total damage while grappling her. She screamed — not at Zalthir but "at herself," furious that she had been genuinely threatened. She then escaped the grapple by dissolving into the fungal matter beneath her feet, flowing through it and reappearing elsewhere on the cavern floor, breaking free of Zalthir's hold entirely.

She called up four spore servants — former Velkynvelve guards whose bodies had been colonized by Zuggtmoy's fungal influence. She half-saved against Daz's 4th-level Fireball, taking 12 damage. As of the end of the session chunk, she is still standing and still fighting.

## Jorlan

Jorlan was identified by Asha as one of only two drow in the group who remained uninfected by Zuggtmoy's spores — his immunity attributed to his hatred rather than devotion. He was positioned across the bridge from the party.

During the battle, Grygum baited him by calling out "Come get it, idiot," and Jorlan charged across the bridge without hesitation, running directly into the Glyph of Warding that Grygum had inscribed into the stone. The glyph detonated for 21 damage, hitting Jorlan and his accompanying spore servants — one servant was destroyed outright. Jorlan took the full force. Immediately after, Thorin closed on him and landed a critical hit with Dawnbringer for 14 damage, then applied the Sap technique to impose disadvantage on Jorlan's next attack. He is described as reeling from the combined punishment but still standing.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool attempted to pass as a rock during Daz's negotiation with Asha Vandree. The deception failed — his eyes drifted left and right with unhurried curiosity, giving him away. Daz told him "I'm taking care of this. It's fine." Asha accepted Glabbagool as a bound familiar, which actually reinforced her framework of Daz as a deluded but powerful mage. Daz privately reflected that Glabbagool's presence may have done more to sell his credibility than the Dancing Lights illusion.

## Grygum

Grygum lent his light crossbow to Thorin before the fight. He inscribed a Glyph of Warding onto the bridge on Jorlan's side, triggered by any hostile creature crossing — it became invisible once finished. He committed the Dust of Suleiman directly onto the Big Heart Fungus. He baited Jorlan across the bridge by calling "Come get it, idiot," triggering the glyph detonation that dealt 21 damage and destroyed one spore servant. He had one 4th-level spell slot, treated as a non-renewable resource. He assessed the combined effect of the Dust and Daz's Fireball with: "That worked better than I had any right to expect."

## Valen

Valen did not appear directly but was reflected upon by Zalthir during the battle. When Ilvara raised four former Velkynvelve guards as spore servants, Zalthir noted that Valen had been one of them — that Valen could have been one of these fungal puppets, differently categorized. The thought carried emotional weight about the thin line between soldier and weapon.
