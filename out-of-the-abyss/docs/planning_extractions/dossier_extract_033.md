## Basidia

Basidia is one of the two Sovereigns of Neverlight Grove, and the one opposed to Phylo's changes. Upon the party's approach, Basidia released their spores and explained the political situation: Phylo has been restructuring Myconid society away from a single group meld (which Basidia preferred) toward a hierarchical model with separate circles — a circle of leaders and a circle of masters — based on supposed instructions from the "Great Seeder." Basidia believes Phylo's changes will irreparably harm Myconid life. They guided the party through the grove, introducing them to various circle leaders and indicating which were friends and which were aligned with Phylo. Basidia confirmed that Hepbobe's food supply was not contaminated with evil spores. They introduced Rusharoo as a friend and leader of the Circle of Explorers, and whispered something private to Rusharoo before Rusharoo offered a secret passageway into the Circle of Welcome. Basidia told Jimjar he had not been infected. They remain the primary ally of the party within Neverlight Grove.

## Phylo

Phylo is the other Sovereign of Neverlight Grove and the architect of the societal restructuring. According to Basidia, Phylo convinced some circle leaders that the "Great Seeder" instructed a new organizational model — replacing the single communal meld with specialized, focused circles under a hierarchy of leaders and masters. Phylo used language about eliminating cross-functional groups and increasing productivity through focused teams. Hepbobe downplayed Basidia's concerns about Phylo, suggesting these were merely organizational preferences that only Sovereigns cared about. The Circle of Welcome, which Phylo invited Xinaya to visit, turned out to be a trap that transformed visitors into fungal spore servants. Phylo's "great plan" is connected to the Great Seeder (Zuggtmoy) and the planned wedding between Zuggtmoy and Araumycos.

## Hepbobe (also referred to as Hepbode)

Hepbobe is the leader of the Circle of Growers and a friend of Phylo, not Basidia. They are in charge of growing food for the colony. When Grygum awkwardly asked about mushrooms eating mushrooms, Hepbobe patiently explained that Myconids are a sentient species of fungus, herbivores with individual preferences and cooking styles. They showed the party irrigation tunnels and various fungi grown to feed the community. When prodded about Phylo, Hepbobe defended the new organizational structure, arguing that the old communal meld was inefficient — people would talk endlessly about niche topics no one else cared about, and specialized circles allow for more focused, productive work. Hepbobe dismissed Basidia's concerns as exaggerated doom and gloom, and suggested Phylo's vision could lead to a better day for Myconid kind. Zalthir confirmed with Basidia that Hepbobe's food supply was not contaminated with evil spores.

## Yrberop

Yrberop is a Myconid circle leader encountered by the party who appeared to be dancing in ecstasy. They spoke in a sing-songy voice about Phylo's great plan and the coming of the Great Seeder, and asked whether the party had heard of Gasbide's plan to send Myconids to the "Overbright" to share their stories. Thorin identified the music Yrberop was dancing to as the same melody heard in his dream of Zuggtmoy — a chaotic, evil symphony with no rhythm or beauty. Yrberop was singing to newly sprouted Myconids about the Great Seeder's "wedding" to the Great Body and how every Myconid was invited, promising joy to all. Thorin concluded this was a death cult.

## Gasbide

Gasbide is mentioned by Yrberop as having a plan to send Myconids to the "Overbright" (the surface world) to share their stories. Rusharoo was disgusted by this idea, noting that Myconids thrive in the dark and going into the light would be terrible. Gasbide does not appear directly but is referenced as part of the faction aligned with Phylo and the Great Seeder.

## Rusharoo

Rusharoo is the leader of the Circle of Explorers and a friend of Basidia. They spoke with frantic, nervous energy about their role scouting the world beyond the grove. Rusharoo reported that the grove is deteriorating — every time they return, it has changed for the worse. They revealed having prepared escape routes and nutrient caches in case the colony needs to flee, and looked at Basidia as if pleading with them to leave immediately. Rusharoo expressed a personal philosophy that Myconids should be nomadic travelers, and believed the caves and tunnels of the Underdark were themselves like another Myconid connecting everything. They were disgusted by the plan to visit the Overbright. Rusharoo offered to send the party into the Circle of Welcome through a secret passageway.

## Xinaya

Xinaya is a Drow priestess of Lolth who was found partially fused with fungi in the Garden of Welcome within the Circle of Welcome. Half her face was rotten and covered in sprouting mushrooms, and a spider medallion had fused with her body through fungal growth. She called out in Common begging to be killed. She revealed that she had been sent by Ilvara to capture the party — not kill them, as Ilvara wanted to do that herself. Xinaya had come to Neverlight Grove expecting the party to seek refuge among the Myconids, but made the mistake of accepting Phylo's invitation to see the Circle of Welcome, where she and her squad became victims of the madness. She recognized Daz as being from Menzoberranzan and demanded he warn the other Drow about the presence of the "Demon Queen of the Fungi" (Zuggtmoy) in the Underdark. After the battle, she was still alive but crying, close to liberation yet still trapped. Thorin, after consulting with Dawnbringer, mercifully killed her at her own request.

## Ilvara

Ilvara is mentioned by Xinaya as the one who sent Xinaya's squad to capture the party. Ilvara wanted the party captured alive so she could kill them herself. She believed the party would seek refuge among the Myconids at Neverlight Grove. She does not appear directly in this session.

## Jimjar

Jimjar, the Deep Gnome NPC traveling with the party, was notably quiet — Zalthir observed that he hadn't made a bet in some time. When asked, Jimjar said, "I don't see the point in making a bet. Everyone is so happy about this wedding." He appeared to already know about the wedding and looked worried. Basidia confirmed Jimjar had not been infected by spores. During the battle in the Circle of Welcome, Jimjar was covered in spores and stopped moving temporarily, but later recovered enough to kill the spore-servant that Sarith had become. At the end, when the party debated whether they were guests or the feast at the wedding, Jimjar muttered, "I don't know."

## Sarith

Sarith, the Drow NPC traveling with the party, met a horrific end during the battle in the Circle of Welcome. His head exploded and he transformed into a Drow spore servant, turning against the party. He was subsequently killed by Jimjar.

## Zuggtmoy

Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi, is the "Great Seeder" referenced throughout Neverlight Grove. Her influence pervades the grove through Phylo's faction. The music associated with her — a chaotic, evil symphony — was heard both in Thorin's earlier dream and in the grove through Yrberop's singing and the wedding procession. Xinaya confirmed that Zuggtmoy, referred to as "the Lady of Decay," is present in the Underdark on the Prime Material Plane. The wedding song revealed her plan: to marry Araumycos ("the Great Body"), joining together to spread her spores throughout the world above and below.

## Araumycos

Araumycos is identified in the wedding song as the intended spouse of Zuggtmoy — referred to as "the Great Body." The wedding ceremony the party stumbled upon is meant to join Zuggtmoy and Araumycos together "heart to heart, becoming one 'til death do part." Araumycos does not appear directly but is central to Zuggtmoy's plan.

## Dawnbringer

Dawnbringer is the sentient sword carried by Thorin. She has been chatty lately and enjoys the open air and bright light of Neverlight Grove. During the battle in the Circle of Welcome, the sunlight emanating from Dawnbringer hurt the fungal creatures as if it were actual sunlight. When Thorin asked about mercifully killing Xinaya, Dawnbringer agreed it was a kind gesture.

## Eldeth

Eldeth is mentioned as being present with the party during the battle in the Circle of Welcome but no specific actions are described for her in this session.

## Aliza Argot

Aliza Argot is mentioned as being present with the party — described as "the other woman whom we found in the caves." She was part of the group during the events at Neverlight Grove but no specific actions are attributed to her in this session.
