## NPC Activity

**Stool (Myconid Sprout)**
Appeared at the myconid cave location. Demanded the party rescue Rump-a-dump throughout the session. Reunited with Rump-a-dump. Used deliberate social strategy — directing appeals to Thorin rather than Zalthir after being rebuffed — demonstrating greater cunning than previously credited. Celebrated loudly upon spotting Rump-a-dump among the dancing myconids.

**Rump-a-dump (Myconid Sprout)**
Found among the dancing myconids at the cave, but not himself dancing or affected. Explained that Voosbur released strange spores and the group was teleported to this location from Neverlight Grove. Confirmed he did not eat the shrinking/growing mushrooms and therefore remains uninfected. Expressed worry that Voosbur's spores may have spread to Neverlight Grove and to his Sovereign there. Communicated via telepathic impressions alongside speech. Referenced that the dancers speak of "the Lady's dream" in ways inconsistent with normal myconid behavior — described it as forced. Desires to return to Neverlight Grove.

**Voosbur (Myconid, infected/corrupted)**
Present among the dancing myconids, identified as the individual who released the corrupting spores that displaced the group from Neverlight Grove. Communicated telepathically with the party, describing Zuggtmoy's influence in ecstatic, drug-culture language — framing her corruption as liberation and a "cosmic journey." Facilitated a vision-trance for Thorin and Grygum by physical contact, transporting them mentally to Zuggtmoy's dream-realm. Upon their return and refusal, released glowing spores; all affected dancers walked into the cavern moss and disappeared. Last seen disappearing with the dancers.

**Sarith (Drow prisoner, party companion)**
Moved involuntarily toward the myconids when within range, shouting about "the Dark Lady" calling him and a "song of becoming." Verbally expressed desire to join Zuggtmoy's dream — speaking of transformation, revenge, liberation, and rising. Physically restrained by Zalthir. Showed internal conflict but was clearly subject to Zuggtmoy's influence at greater than 30-foot range. Confirmed to have been compromised prior to this session; behavior escalated in proximity to the infected myconids.

**Jimjar (Svirfneblin, party companion)**
Referenced in discussion. Advised the party to hold onto the sensitive information (the egg and associated intel) rather than act immediately, predicting it would be useful later.

**Zuggtmoy, Demon Queen of Fungi ("The Dark Lady," "Lady of Rot and Decay")**
Did not physically appear in the material plane. Manifested in a vision-realm accessed through Voosbur's trance — appeared seated on a throne of fungal growths, twisted bones, and decaying wood in a corrupted cavern. Communicated telepathically with Thorin and Grygum within the vision. Offered both the gift of joining her mycelium — described as eternal bloom beyond flesh — and expressed disappointment when refused. Her influence over Sarith operates at magical rather than natural range. Her spores are propagated through magical demonic curse, likely requiring consent or bodily fluid exchange for full infection rather than proximity alone.

## Revealed Information

- **Zuggtmoy is "the Dark Lady"** referenced by Sarith and the infected myconids. Her identity confirmed via *The Platinum Chronicle: Discourses on the Abyssal Blight* — she is the Demon Queen of Fungi, Lady of Rot and Decay.
- **Zuggtmoy's infection method** in this context is magical/demonic rather than natural spore transmission; Daz assessed it likely requires consent or bodily fluid exchange, not mere proximity.
- **Sarith's corruption** is linked to Zuggtmoy specifically. His madness is not incidental — he is either partially infected or being actively called by her influence.
- **Voosbur caused the displacement** of the myconid group from Neverlight Grove via strange spores, and subsequent mushroom consumption by the other myconids deepened the infection.
- **Rump-a-dump avoided infection** by refusing to eat the shrinking/growing mushrooms found at the new location.
- **Neverlight Grove's Sovereign** may still be uninfected and could potentially provide navigational assistance or an escape route — but this is unconfirmed.
- **The vision-realm** Zuggtmoy inhabits contains at least three distinct zones: a glowing fungal cavern (passive/beautiful), a vine-forest with semi-sentient, hostile tendrils, and a throne room where Zuggtmoy herself sits.
