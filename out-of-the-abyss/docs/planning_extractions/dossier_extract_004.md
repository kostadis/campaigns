## Derendil

Prince Derendil is dead, his large corpse standing as a silent monument to the price of the victory against the drow guards. He is described as "deluded," suggesting his claim to royalty was questionable. His body is left behind as the prisoners prepare to escape.

## Zalthir

Zalthir shows no sentimentality after the battle and quickly scours the drow bodies for weapons. He grabs silk ropes from pegs and prepares to descend into the spider web below, then asks whether they should run or burn the place down first. He escapes with the group down the waterfall and through the tunnels. Later, he eats the Tongue of Madness mushroom and declares a vow of silence to avoid being compelled to speak the truth. After a cave-in in a large cavern, Zalthir is one of those not trapped. He examines the unconscious Daz and concludes the mage will die if not freed in time.

## Sarith

Sarith arms himself with studded leather armor, a shield, and a hand crossbow, making him look every part of the drow warrior caste of the Cult of Lolth. He identifies a vial of Drow poison found among the loot. When the group debates whether to escape or retrieve their gear, Sarith advises speed, warning that Ilvara will be honor-bound to pursue them and they shouldn't give her more motivation—but he cackles that "revenge is so tasty," suggesting bouts of madness. He later confirms the Tongue of Madness mushroom is the "tastiest thing in the world." He is referred to as both "Sarith" and "Sathir" and provides detailed knowledge of the area, explaining that he was a member of the warrior caste before being "unfairly and unjustly imprisoned." He outlines the route to the Darklake and suggests heading to Sloobludop to find a navigator and boat, noting it is eight days away.

## Buppido

Buppido warns the group about very large spiders in the web below when Zalthir prepares to descend. After escaping, he laughs and declares that "the divine plan" says he will make it to Gracklstugh, and makes a wager with Jimjar about survival. He asks Thorin an embarrassing personal question while the dwarf is under the truth-compelling effects of the Tongue of Madness. He looks at Zalthir oddly when the latter declares a vow of silence. His stated destination is Gracklstugh.

## Topsy

Topsy (along with Turvy) offers to retrieve the party's gear for a price. When the offer is declined, both Topsy and Turvy transform into rats. Later, on the cavern floor, they transform back into gnomes and head toward the passage leading to the Darklake. Topsy is among those trapped under rocks after the cave-in.

## Turvy

Turvy acts alongside Topsy throughout the escape. They offer to retrieve gear for a price, transform into rats when declined, and later transform back into gnomes on the cavern floor, heading toward the Darklake passage.

## Ilvara

Ilvara does not appear directly, but her residence is noted as being past the rope bridge near the guard room. Sarith warns that Ilvara will be "honor bound to pursue" the escaped prisoners and advises against giving her additional motivation for revenge. The group ultimately decides to leave their gear behind in her quarters rather than risk recapture.

## Jimjar

After the escape, Jimjar disrupts the group's sense of safety by asking if anyone wants to bet on how many of them will die before they escape. He makes a wager with Buppido about survival. During foraging, Jimjar is the one who spots the Tongue of Madness fungus, shouting "Well, look at this." He is among those trapped under rocks after the cave-in.

## Shuushar

Shuushar explains that the Tongue of Madness mushroom is said to connect the eater with the gods, and later clarifies its true effect: compelling the eater to speak only the truth. He provides this information after Thorin's involuntary truth-telling. Sarith references Shuushar's knowledge of Sloobludop, saying "If what Shuushar says is true, the town of Sloopdopblop is just eight days away." Thorin, under the mushroom's effects, comments that Shuushar has "really big eyes." Shuushar is among those trapped under rocks in the cave-in.

## Ront

Ront refuses to flee with the other prisoners when an injured vrock lands on the cavern floor. Still angry at his own cowardice and feeling he must prove himself, Ront attacks the vrock. His first blow cuts through its hide and draws gangrenous blood, giving him hope, but the demon kills him with startling speed—one taloned hand kills him, the other grabs his body, and its beak snaps off his head and swallows it. The vrock then flies off and drops Ront's headless body to the ground. Ront is dead.

## Stool

Stool, a myconid whom the party met as a fellow prisoner in Velkynvelve, curls up against Grygum during the long rest. When Grygum tries to push him away, Stool cries. He explains that he is lonely, scared, and terrified, and that he remembers Grygum's inspirational speech. He tells Grygum he knows the orc will bring him home and wants to be his best friend. Grygum agrees to be friends, and Stool extends his hand, which Grygum takes. They march together. Grygum also notes the tactical advantage of Stool's telepathic powers, which can be used to communicate silently and surprise enemies.

## Eldeth

Eldeth is among those trapped under rocks after the cave-in in the large cavern. No other actions are described for her in this session.
