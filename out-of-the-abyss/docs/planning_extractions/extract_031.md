## NPC Activity

**Brysis of Khaem**
- Referenced via stone diorama depicting her as a Netherese noble surrounded by her retinue, flaunting wealth and power.
- A gilded sarcophagus in the lower chamber is identified as belonging to her.
- A soft, feminine voice — potentially Brysis — called out telepathically when the party approached the tomb entrance, pleading: "Hello? Is someone there? Oh, please, I need your help! I have been trapped in the dark for so long... so very long."

**The Wraith (unnamed, tomb guardian)**
- Rose from Brysis's gilded sarcophagus when the party entered the lower burial chamber.
- Declared telepathically: "Your deaths will free me from this prison, and how my victims will serve me even in death!"
- Attacked Thorin; the attack missed due to armor and divine protection.
- Retreated back inside its sarcophagus after the failed attack.
- Resistant to fire damage; damaged by Magic Missile.
- Capable of creating Spectres from slain victims.
- Status: Still active inside the sarcophagus when Zalthir opened it at session's end.

**The Spectres (four, unnamed, Brysis's retainers)**

- Identified as Brysis's bound retainers.

## Revealed Information

- The Lost Tomb of Khaem is the tomb of **Brysis of Khaem**, a Netherese noble sorceress; murals depict her life and the floating cities of the Netheril Empire.
- The Netheril Empire fell when one of its leaders attempted to seize all magical power, tearing reality apart — contextualizing the tomb's power and danger.
- The Wraith explicitly stated it is imprisoned ("Your deaths will free me from this prison"), suggesting its current state is one of entrapment, not free haunting.
- The feminine voice and the Wraith appear to be separate entities — the voice actively encouraged the party to help it and directed them to look inside the gilded sarcophagus, while the Wraith was hostile.
- The northeast sarcophagus in the false tomb room concealed a hidden passage on stone rollers leading to the true burial chamber.
- An invisible stone chest was detected near the foot of Brysis's gilded sarcophagus.
- The four false tomb sarcophagi contained mummified remains, ancient burial garments, and Netherese treasure of significant monetary and collector value.
- Fuurm and Baedora's escape was enabled by a demon attack on the Drow that created chaos — consistent with prior session intelligence about demonic activity in the Underdark.
