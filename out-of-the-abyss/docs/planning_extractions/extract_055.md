Stated her obligation to travel to Mithril Hall to brief the dwarven lords (Gauntlgrym and Mithril Hall) about the dangers in the Underdark. Made a sworn oath to Thorin that after delivering her report, she would advocate for him to be formally embraced by her clan. Departed west toward Mithril Hall.

**Spiderbait**
Emerged from the Underdark with the party. Expressed naïve optimism about goblin integration into surface society, was corrected by the party, and elected to accompany the group to Candlekeep to seek employment.

**Glabbagool**
Witnessed his first sunrise. Was assigned to guard the drow spy in the bag of holding during travel. Demonstrated earnest commitment to the guard duty. Engaged with the spy inside the bag by producing multiple proliferating eyes to assert control when she grew demanding. Participated in party discussion of entering Candlekeep, showed philosophical confusion about the concept of limited questions per book donation. Was offered a question of his own.

**Dawnbringer**
Expressed emotional reaction to returning to the surface and sunlight after extended underground confinement. Made small verbal exchanges with Thorin, including characterizing him as having "anger issues." Was directed toward the avowed Philemon at Candlekeep for sessions addressing interpersonal conflict between sentient artifact and wielder.

**The Drow Spy (unnamed, House T'sarran)**
Detected following the party at dusk on the road to Candlekeep, approximately 20-40 feet into the northern treeline. Detected by Zalthir, Thorin, and Daz (Grygum initially misread the threat as a deer). Ambushed by Zalthir via misty step, grappled and stunned. Tied up and interrogated. Revealed she was assigned to track the party's surface destination, contacts, and who approaches Daz — not to engage. Stated her matron was waiting for the party to surface and sent multiple agents to the overbright. Identified herself as belonging to House T'sarran but refused to give her name. Claimed her matron mother had ruled House T'sarran for two hundred years and had a specific interest in identifying whoever paid for Daz's protection — with Daz being used as bait/indicator. Proposed a mutual information-sharing deal (rejected by Thorin). Was subjected to a shrinking mushroom (reduced to small child-sized), placed in bag of holding with Glabbagool as guard. Transported to Candlekeep. Upon arrival, vocally protested ("This is highly irregular, and House T'sarran will have its vengeance!"). Demanded information about the prayer beads; refused on grounds of not having donated a book. Continued to be held under Glabbagool's supervision.

**Vareth**
An elderly half-elf avowed at Candlekeep. Specializes in half-orc Bahamut converts — previously a quiet niche field. Emerged in a near-frantic state upon learning a half-orc cleric of Bahamut had arrived. His research focus exploded in significance following the event known as "Stroud's Gambit" (Stroud beating Protanther the gold dragon at chess), which triggered widespread controversy and funding for his field. Latched onto Grygum immediately and began planning an interview and leveraging Grygum's arrival for academic purposes. Received the coined term "Stroud's Gambit" with visible delight and announced it would benefit the field tremendously.

**The First Reader (Candlekeep)**
Received the party's five book donations. Expressed particular enthusiasm for the Velkynvelve Prisoner Journal ("a masterpiece of interesting value" to accompany her collection of drow prison journals). Assigned specialized scholars to each party member's inquiry. Directed Zalthir toward the Jimjar thread and an avowed named Khell-Vire. Directed Thorin and Dawnbringer to Philemon. Flagged that an avowed specifically wanted to speak with Grygum.

**Philemon**

Assigned to Thorin and Dawnbringer.

**Khell-Vire**
Named avowed at Candlekeep. Has a specific interest that connects to the Jimjar inquiry raised by Zalthir. Assigned to the Jimjar thread.

**Jimjar** (referenced, absent)

**Protanther** (referenced, absent)
Ancient gold dragon. Referenced in the account of Stroud's Gambit — had analyzed a chess position for four hundred years with certainty of every possible move, was defeated by Stroud's improbable roll, and was so stunned he joined the alliance. His defeat is the theological/political inflection point that caused the surge of interest in half-orc Bahamut converts.

**Stroud** (referenced, absent)
A dwarf who defeated Protanther the gold dragon at chess through an unexpected dice roll, triggering Protanther's alliance and causing widespread theological controversy about half-orc Bahamut conversion. The term "Stroud's Gambit" was coined by Grygum during this session. Stroud's action is the foundational event for Vareth's entire field of study.

One operative successfully tracked the party before capture. The matron's two-hundred-year tenure and patient operational posture suggest this is not an impulsive move. ## Revealed Information

- **House T'sarran's objective is not Daz himself** but the unknown party who paid for Daz's protection. Daz is being used as bait/indicator. The matron has been waiting for Daz's surface emergence specifically to observe who contacts him.
- **Multiple T'sarran agents were sent to the surface.** The captured spy may not be the only one. Eliminating her does not end the surveillance operation.
- **The matron mother of House T'sarran has ruled for two hundred years**, indicating old, established power rather than a house in an aggressive consolidation phase.
- **The spy expressed confidence in her ability to escape Candlekeep**, suggesting House T'sarran has knowledge of or capabilities within the institution.
- **Glabbagool's googly eyes are affectations.** His actual sensory mechanism is different; he can produce real, proliferating eyes and "see different ways." He adopted the affectations because they made people respond more warmly to him.
- **Asha Vandree's theological document** arguing Lolth and Zuggtmoy are equivalent is now in Candlekeep's possession, where scholars will study it — an outcome she would have opposed.
- **Ilvara's prayer book** contains Zuggtmoy marginalia documenting the corruption timeline Ilvara observed, now also in Candlekeep's possession.
- **"Stroud's Gambit"** — the event of Stroud (a dwarf) defeating Protanther (an ancient gold dragon) at chess through an improbable dice roll — caused Protanther to join an alliance and triggered a widespread theological controversy about half-orc Bahamut conversions that is ongoing and now well-funded as an academic field.
