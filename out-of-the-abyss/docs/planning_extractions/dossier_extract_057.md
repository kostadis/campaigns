## Vareth

Vareth, identified as having worked at Candlekeep for forty-one years, served as Grygum's host and guide in the West Inner Ward. He escorted Grygum through the Immortal Chambers with institutional ease, displaying practiced patience — most notably during the extended encounter with Pizwog, which cost him something visible though he remained respectful throughout.

In his side office, Vareth delivered a prepared overview of the great theological controversy currently consuming Candlekeep: three schools of thought contending over the meaning of Stroud's chess victory over Protanther. He laid out the Stroudite school (mortal choice is sovereign), the Bahamutian school (divine providence moved the pieces), and the Draconic school (the dragons permitted the scope of mortal choosing). He framed the underlying question as cosmological: what kind of universe do we live in? The controversy had produced three duels, institutional chaos, and standing threats from Sister Yvenne over bookplates.

When Grygum proposed a fourth school — fixed points around which choices are constrained, with freedom between them — Vareth's response was a moment of genuine panic before pivoting to excitement. He elaborated the theory himself, connecting it to a god who might control outcomes not through compulsion but through shaping the architecture of evidence and argument. He named Grygum a "weak Stroudite" and both a "strong Bahamutian" (near fixed points) and "weak Bahamutian" (away from them), christening the framework the strong-weak hypothesis. He immediately prepared to begin his own research, barely able to contain himself as he handed Grygum a stack of Stroudite papers and rushed out. He promised Grygum a marked-up version.

Last known whereabouts: departing his office at speed to begin research on what he was treating as a new school of theological thought.

## Bookwyrm (First Reader)

Bookwyrm — identified as a green dragonborn — was the First Reader who summoned the party before the Avowed awoke, via a breathless messenger, to investigate the death of the Keeper of Tomes. She was visibly shaken and weary when the party encountered her in the Chapter House, in the manner of someone who had been awake since the death was discovered.

In the Chapter House, she attempted to hold the council together under conditions of active institutional collapse, repeating "FOCUS, PEOPLE! Focus!" until the shouting subsided. She proposed delegating the investigation to the party using language of divine fate — "seekers who the gods have sent us" — which Grygum recognized as deliberate rhetorical framing designed to make an irregular choice acceptable to the council. She deflected challenges from Daral and successfully neutralized Kalan Strongbranch's attempt to intervene, citing his troubled relationship with the deceased Keeper and ordering him to focus on securing the defenses instead. She agreed to the terms of the party's investigation: one book each, copied at the House of the Binder, upon successful conclusion, with full access throughout the library, subject to signing confidentiality papers.

On the walk to the crime scene, she volunteered — unprompted and before being questioned — that Janussi had asked Kalan to tender his resignation the morning before his death, citing concerns about Kalan's mental faculties and negligence. She declined to specify the nature of the negligence, framing this as not wanting to prejudice the investigation. When pressed by Daz and Thorin, she maintained that position. When Thorin pointed out her testimony was itself evidence and asked where she was at the time of the murder, she did not answer before arriving at the tower.

At the tower, she gave Tadric new orders, confirmed the party's authority to investigate, instructed that nothing be removed from the room, and departed for other business.

The party noted her as a person of interest: her volunteering of information about Kalan, her deflection on the question of negligence, and her non-answer to Thorin's alibi question were all flagged as significant.

Last known whereabouts: departing the tower to attend to other business, having established the party's investigative authority.

## Kalan Strongbranch

Kalan Strongbranch — described as having gray-white beard, piercing blue eyes, and the posture of someone accustomed to being listened to — was the gate warden of Candlekeep. He had previously attempted to put Daz in irons when the party arrived.

He entered the Chapter House meeting after the council had already decided to delegate the investigation to the party. Bookwyrm preemptively cut him off before he could speak, citing his "troubled relationship with the keeper of tomes," the need for impartial investigation, and the council's decision. She ordered him to secure the defenses and ensure no creature entered or left. He bowed in compliance. The bow was correct; his eyes were not.

From Tadric's testimony at the crime scene, Kalan had arrived at the Keeper's tower with Tadric at eight in the morning, spoken briefly with the discoverers (A'lai Aivenmore and Miss Hollypocket), and returned alone at eleven for what he described as a full inspection — careful, deliberate. Tadric noted Kalan looked more determined coming out than going in. He had also performed the Speak with Dead rite on the corpse during this inspection, which meant the rite could not be repeated for some time.

Bookwyrm had disclosed that Janussi asked Kalan to resign the morning before his death due to concerns about his mental faculties and negligence. The nature of the negligence was not specified.

Grygum identified Kalan as the first person he intended to question. The party flagged the combination of: having been asked to resign the day before the murder; returning alone to the crime scene; walking out looking determined; and using the Speak with Dead rite — which either yielded nothing or prevented subsequent attempts — as a cluster of significant facts.

Last known whereabouts: ordered by Bookwyrm to attend to the library's defenses, having been removed from direct involvement in the investigation.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool — the grey ooze — had been confined to the bag of holding for most of the day at Candlekeep. By the evening at the House of Rest, he was agitated and wanting out, which Thorin compared to keeping a puppy in a closet during a thunderstorm. The want was present even if the destination was unclear.

When the party was summoned before dawn to the crime scene, Glabbagool assessed the situation from his corner — door open, party dressed, urgency established, no visible threat — and made the decision to flow into the bag of holding himself, arrange himself inside, and peer out from the opening with two eyeballs tracking the room. Daz observed this and noted: "at least we have that."

Zalthir had been patiently teaching Glabbagool monastic discipline, which Glabbagool had apparently been attempting to formalize into practice during his time in the bag of holding.

## A'lai Aivenmore

A'lai Aivenmore — described as tall, handsome, with a braided beard and smooth presentation — was one of the Great Readers present in the Chapter House. He was one of the two people who discovered Janussi's body, along with the housekeeper Miss Hollypocket, around half past seven in the morning.

In the Chapter House meeting, he opened with the demand that the council hold a vote to restore leadership to Candlekeep. When the question of whether to delegate the investigation to the party arose, he decided that the fastest resolution was the best one and that he was not in a position to judge Bookwyrm's choices. He did not endorse the party; he declined to object. Grygum filed the distinction between endorsement and non-objection as significant.

From Tadric's timeline, A'lai and Miss Hollypocket were the discoverers of the body and the first people Kalan spoke with upon arriving at eight in the morning.

## Zalthir

Zalthir was present throughout the chapter and is a named NPC in the known list. During the tour of the Bastion given by Fembris, Zalthir conducted a continuous tactical assessment of each space: mapping exits, evaluating cover, noting guard positions, assessing the Drakonoikos (built from the scales and bones of the dragon Fyrentennimar) in terms of repurposed threat. He reflected privately on the Garden Shadow's monastic training and specific brothers (Brother Heslin or Brother Arrath) in the context of what he was observing.

During the Chapter House meeting, when a council member pressed him about the "unique skill" mentioned by Brother Khell-Vire, Zalthir responded with "Do you have a request to go on?" — not technically a deflection, but one that required the questioner to justify themselves. When the questioner pressed further, Grygum intervened to redirect to the murder.

At the crime scene, Zalthir performed the precise physical examination of the body: he determined the rib cage had been smashed by something like an axe, multiple close deliberate blows; the heart was missing while other organs were not; and the blood pattern was wrong for the violence of the wound, suggesting Janussi may have been dead before his chest was opened. He agreed with Thorin's inference that the cause of death and the removal of the heart were likely separate events.

On the staircase outside the crime scene, Zalthir responded to Fembris's rationalization of the mysterious cat by saying, very quietly, "Is it?" — which caused Fembris to reconsider whether he should be afraid of the cat.

He had been teaching Glabbagool monastic discipline during the journey.
