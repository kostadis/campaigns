## NPC Activity

**Chief Dorbo**
- Appeared in Diggermattock Hall engaged in a heated discussion with Senni about Blingdenstone's future.
- Explained that ooze infestations are hindering both mining operations and housing construction.
- Agreed the party must deal with the ooze threat before he can assist them with their own concerns.
- Revealed deep suspicion of the Gold Whisker clan, whom he suspects of being wererats, and refuses to cooperate with them.

**Senni**
- Appeared in Diggermattock Hall confronting Chief Dorbo over his failure to adequately address the ooze problem.
- Represents the non-mining population of Blingdenstone.
- Appeared more open than Dorbo to reconciliation with the Gold Whisker clan.

**Pelek** (ghost, deceased)
- Ghost who had been traveling with the party; his bones were carried by Grygum.
- Materialized before Glyphic Shroomlight in the Temple of the Ruby in the Rough, prompting the priest to allow interment.
- Expressed profound relief and gratitude upon having his bones properly interred in the catacombs below the temple.
- Confirmed his fear of being lost forever and tormenting people for eternity.
- Spirit faded away peacefully following the burial ritual performed by Grygum.
- Previously killed by Buppido (referenced, not present this session).

**Buppido** (referenced, absent)
- Named as the killer of Pelek during discussion of why the party was seeking a resting place for his bones.

**Glyphic Shroomlight**
- Priest at the Temple of the Ruby in the Rough, dedicated to Segojan Earthcaller.
- Initially hesitant to inter the bones of an unknown individual.
- Reconsidered immediately upon Pelek's ghost materializing, unwilling to risk being haunted.
- Directed the party to the catacombs below for the interment.
- Requested that Glabbagool remain upstairs to avoid accidents.

**Borough Warden Jadgar**
- Ghost of a former Borough Warden, three feet tall, appeared in the catacombs immediately after Pelek's spirit faded.
- Revealed he defended Blingdenstone against the Drow and other threats during his life.
- Disclosed that he is secretly working to re-establish the Borough Wardens, covertly training young gnomes toward this end.
- Offered the party a deal: one question about the Underdark answered for each ghost they help put to rest.
- Explained that ooze infestations of this scale are highly unusual, as oozes are typically solitary.
- Provided a clarification on the nature of devils versus demons in exchange for Zalthir demonstrating a monk technique: devils corrupt individuals through personal deals; demons empower proxies to cause widespread carnage.
- Advised the party to search for an intermediate agent empowered by a demon as the likely source of the ooze infestation.
- Recommended the party speak with a young gnome he is mentoring — the intended future leader of the new Borough Wardens — once they have gathered more information.

**Glabbagool** (intelligent gelatinous cube, allied)
- Accompanied the party; asked to remain upstairs at the temple by Glyphic Shroomlight.
- Present in Trader's Grotto; drew suspicious looks from gnomes.
- Protested that the non-sentient gelatinous cubes invading the grotto were giving intelligent gelatinous cubes a bad name.
- Advised the party on combat tactics: avoid engulfment, use acid protection, extract engulfed allies quickly.
- Asked Zalthir in wonder whether he was an ooze upon witnessing his eldritch claw tentacles.

## Revealed Information

- Oozes are naturally solitary; a mass infestation is highly unusual and implies external motivation or empowerment.
- Jadgar's framework for demonic versus diabolic influence: demons grant power to proxies to spread carnage broadly, rather than corrupting individuals directly as devils do — suggesting an intermediate agent is controlling or directing the oozes.
- Zalthir raised Juiblex as a possible connection; Jadgar's response (requiring a trade before clarifying) implied this line of inquiry was significant.
- The Borough Wardens are being secretly reconstituted by Jadgar; a young gnome protégé is being groomed to lead them.
- The Temple of the Ruby in the Rough is dedicated to Segojan Earthcaller, the Svirfneblin god of deep earthen nature.
- The Trader's Grotto's petrified fungi are the result of a medusa's spell cast centuries ago.
