## Stool

Stool, the myconid, releases his rapport spores, enabling telepathic communication among the prisoners. This allows the group to coordinate their escape attempt by talking in each other's heads. The experience is initially disorienting but the prisoners gradually learn to focus on specific individuals. Jimjar later demonstrates mastery of Stool's telepathy by forcing everyone to listen to him.

## Zalthir

Zalthir peeks outside the prison area and observes it is very dark, using his Darkvision to scout. He devises the initial escape plan: sneak into the tower, climb to the top floor, arm himself, and bring weapons back to the other prisoners, relying on Daz as backup. The plan immediately falls apart when Zalthir trips over a goblet. When the guards turn toward him and Daz, Zalthir mumbles for the others to come help. He climbs into the guard chamber and tries to shove Imbros but, weakened from days of captivity, botches the move entirely—instead of pushing Imbros, he accidentally caresses him. He later tries to strike one of the two drow guards with his unarmed fists but lands ineffective blows. He also attempts to strike Imbros unarmed and misses. He survives the fight. Last seen in the guard chamber after the battle.

## Imbros

Imbros is identified by Serith as one of the more elite Drow male fighters. He is stationed in the guard chamber playing the Drow game Sava with two other guards. When Thorin tries to overpower him, Imbros is amused rather than alarmed, viewing the prisoner revolt as routine. He chooses not to kill Thorin initially, knowing that killing prisoners is costly, and instead tries to subdue and knock him unconscious. He deftly dodges Zalthir's shove attempt. When the revolt escalates with the prisoners arming themselves, Imbros strikes Thorin unconscious and realizes the situation has become truly dangerous. He is unaffected by Daz's Mind Sliver spell. He is struck by crossbow bolts from multiple prisoners and hit by two volleys of Daz's magic missiles. He resists Grygum's Toll the Dead spell. Despite being battered and barely alive, he kills Prince Derendil with poisoned strikes—using Drow Poison. He fears that if he fails to stop the revolt, Ilvara might punish him the same way Serith was punished, so he refuses to call for help, preferring to deal with it alone. He is ultimately killed by Serith's sword. He was trained in Menzoberranzan from a very young age.

## Serith

Serith is a Drow who has turned against his former comrades and joined the prisoners. He identified Imbros as one of the elite Drow fighters prior to the escape attempt. During the battle, he descends armed from the armory and attacks one of the drow guards—the same one Zalthir had flailed at—cursing him in a uniquely Drow fashion. After Prince Derendil is killed by Imbros, Serith suddenly goes mad and is about to scream at the top of his lungs, deeply affected by the death. Grygum telepathically calms him by redirecting his rage toward spirituality. Serith is confused but calmed, and then delivers the killing blow to Imbros with his sword. His backstory involves some prior punishment or fall from grace referenced by Imbros's fear of suffering "precisely like Serith's" fate at Ilvara's hands.

## Prince Derendil

Prince Derendil declares he is "already armed in this hideous form" and that it would bring him great joy to kill Imbros, then charges at the elite Drow warrior. He fights Imbros in melee but is struck twice with Drow Poison, becoming injured. Despite the efforts of the other prisoners to bring Imbros down, Prince Derendil is ultimately stabbed to death by Imbros. His death deeply affects Serith and is noted as a significant loss to the escape party. He refers to his current physical form as hideous, consistent with his claim of being a transformed prince.

## Bupido

Bupido leads the rest of the prisoners in taking advantage of the distraction caused by Thorin, Zalthir, and Daz's confrontation with the guards. He leads the rush into the guard chamber and scramble into the armory. During the crossbow volley at Imbros, Bupido fires but misses.

## Shuushar

Shuushar (also spelled Suushar) arms himself with a hand crossbow during the armory raid and fires at Imbros during the crossbow volley, successfully hitting him.

## Jimjar

Jimjar arms himself with a hand crossbow during the armory scramble and fires at Imbros but misses. He demonstrates mastery of Stool's telepathy by forcing everyone to hear him say, "You should have taken that bet."

## Eldeth

Eldeth arms herself with a hand crossbow from the armory and fires at the remaining Drow guard from the roof opening, hitting and helping to bring the guard down.

## Topsy

Topsy arms herself with a hand crossbow and fires at the remaining Drow guard from the armory opening, hitting and helping kill him alongside Eldeth and Ront.

## Turvy

Turvy is among the prisoners who scramble into the armory and fire hand crossbows from the roof opening during the battle, though no specific hit is attributed to him.

## Ront

Ront prefers a straight-up melee fight over using crossbows. He squeezes into the battle in the guard chamber and strikes the remaining Drow guard, killing him.

## Ilvara

Ilvara does not appear directly but is referenced by Imbros during the fight. Imbros fears that if he fails to stop the prisoner revolt, Ilvara might decide his fate should be the same as Serith's punishment. Her authority over the outpost and her willingness to punish failure are key motivations for Imbros's desperate last stand.

## Grygum

Grygum (also spelled Gyrgum) decides that an armed cleric is better than a disarmed one and follows the prisoners to the armory to equip himself. He descends from the armory and heals the unconscious, critically wounded Thorin with a magical touch—bloodless flesh becomes rosy, gashes close, and broken bones knit instantly. He attempts Toll the Dead against Imbros, but the Drow resists the necrotic spell. After Prince Derendil's death causes Serith to go mad, Grygum telepathically reaches into Serith's mind, redirecting his rage toward spirituality and calming him. He reflects that "the lessons of his cult" served him well, suggesting he belongs to some kind of religious cult rather than a mainstream faith.
