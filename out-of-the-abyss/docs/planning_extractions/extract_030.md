## NPC Activity

**Themberchaud (Young Red Dragon, Wyrmsmith of Gracklstugh)**
- Appeared flying above the party and dive-bombed toward them before landing heavily near the party's location.
- Declared the party to be his "agents, bound by my protection" and offered them an "express service" past checkpoints and patrols.
- Flew the party through Gracklstugh cavern, performing a forge fire-breathing display for Duergar workers while shouting "I am Themberchaud the Wyrmsmith, bringer of flame!"
- Revealed through behavior and appearance that he is pampered, physically unfit (obese, polished scales, painted claws, no signs of use in combat), and emotionally immature — behaving more like a glorified forge-lighter than an apex predator.
- Demonstrated poor flying ability; a badly executed turn caused multiple party members to fall from his back.
- Expressed ego-driven need for flattery and validation; responded positively once the party reframed the dock visit as gathering intelligence *for* him.
- Landed at the Darklake docks, scattering all present except one Stone Guard.
- Had no awareness of the Duergar's reaction to the party being identified as the Ember Vanguard.

**Unnamed Duergar Stone Guard (Darklake Docks)**
- Stood alone at the docks after all others fled upon Themberchaud's landing.
- Initially attempted to halt the party in the name of the Deep King.
- Recognized the party (or some members) from an Ember Vanguard wanted poster and declared intent to warn Captain Errde Blackskull immediately.
- Was persuaded by Grygum to believe the party were impersonators on a covert mission to flush out the real Ember Vanguard.
- Accepted a caltrop as a "symbol of the mission" and departed, buying the party time to escape.

**Jimjar (Ghoul)**
- Present throughout, accompanying the party on Themberchaud's back.
- Threw a rope to catch Grygum when he fell from the dragon.
- Made a wager — an empty shell gem — that the Neverlight Grove's "circle of joy" would reveal itself to be a "circle of unwilling vows" before the party leaves.
- Described the situation as "a wedding involving demons and mushrooms."

**Rump-a-dump (Myconid)**
- Present on the boat after leaving Gracklstugh.
- Expressed strong desire to travel to Neverlight Grove.
- Insisted to Zalthir that "the circle cannot be corrupted" and that "Our sovereign would never let that happen" — demonstrating dangerous naivety about demonic corruption of the Grove.

**Stool (Myconid)**
- Also present and expressed desire to go to Neverlight Grove alongside Rump-a-dump.

**Serith**
- Announced on the fourth day of travel that the party must disembark and travel on foot, as the path to Neverlight Grove passes the Lost Tomb of Khaem.
- Described as experiencing a strange compulsion drawing him toward the Neverlight Grove, linked to his earlier reaction to dancing Myconids in the Whorelstone Caverns.

**Eldeth**
- Referenced as a figure at a checkpoint who would demand to see what the party found — her reaction expected to trigger violence if the party's discoveries were shared.
- Appeared briefly on the boat; engaged in conversation about the rarity of spellcasters who learn Mending due to naval warfare targeting such casters.
- Last known location: the boat/Darklake travel.

**Captain Errde Blackskull**
- Not physically present but referenced: the Stone Guard declared intent to warn her about the Ember Vanguard sighting.
- Separately referenced by Zalthir as someone who would likely have informed the Drow of the party's passage through Gracklstugh, meaning pursuit may resume.

**Gartokkar**
- Not present but referenced: described as having previously warned the party that everyone in Gracklstugh is on edge, and that the Stone Guard, Grey Ghosts, and Deep King are all searching for the Ember Vanguard.

## Faction Movements

- Themberchaud acted independently of the Keeper of the Flame hierarchy, personally escorting the party past checkpoints and patrols — framing this as bypassing the "short-sightedness" of the Keepers.
- Performed a public forge-firing display, reinforcing his role as a glorified forge-lighter while believing himself to be demonstrating power and dominion.
- His actions inadvertently caused the party to be spotted by Duergar workers and identified (mistakenly) as the Ember Vanguard.

**Duergar of Gracklstugh (General / Stone Guard)**
- Workers at the forge were unafraid of Themberchaud but reacted with panic upon seeing party members — specifically Daz — whom they believed to be the Ember Vanguard.
- Multiple Duergar were observed rushing to send warnings following the flyby.
- Stone Guard at the docks attempted to enforce Deep King authority and moved to alert Captain Errde before being misdirected by Grygum.

**Ember Vanguard**
- Not present, but their reputation is clearly active: the party was mistaken for them by the Stone Guard based on a wanted poster; Duergar workers panicked at the sight of party members associated with the group.
- The Deep King, Grey Ghosts, and Stone Guard are all described as actively searching for them.

**Grey Ghosts**
- Referenced as actively searching for the Ember Vanguard within Gracklstugh.
- Dead grey ghosts were noted littering the streets as the session opened.

**Neverlight Grove / Circle of Sovereigns**
- Referenced destination. Rump-a-dump and Stool are pulling the party toward it.
- Zalthir has knowledge that a "very dangerous and mighty demon" is present there, and that the sovereign may be compromised — information withheld from Rump-a-dump due to the Myconid's youth and naivety.
- Jimjar's wager implies foreknowledge or strong suspicion that a coercive ritual ("circle of unwilling vows") is underway there.

## Threat Arc Events

**Themberchaud Corruption / Manipulation Arc**
- Themberchaud is operating outside normal Duergar oversight by personally claiming the party as his agents and escorting them past security infrastructure — suggesting his growing desire for autonomy and agents of his own, outside Keeper of the Flame control.
- The Keepers' "short-sightedness" comment from Themberchaud suggests active resentment toward his handlers is building.

**Ember Vanguard Arc**
- Party was publicly identified as the Ember Vanguard by a Stone Guard at the Darklake docks, and Duergar workers sent warnings during the dragon flyby — the Ember Vanguard's reputation is actively destabilizing Gracklstugh security and the party's cover.
- Stone Guard's misdirection buys time but does not eliminate the alert; Captain Errde may still receive word.

**Demonic Corruption Arc (Neverlight Grove)**
- Jimjar's wager explicitly anticipates that the Grove's "circle of joy" is actually a "circle of unwilling vows" — implying demonic ritual/compulsion activity is underway.
- Rump-a-dump's denial of possible corruption ("the circle cannot be corrupted") indicates the Myconid population has no awareness of or resistance to the threat.
- Serith's unexplained compulsion toward the Grove (linked to Whorelstone Cavern Myconid encounter) suggests possible demonic influence already acting on a party-adjacent figure.

## Revealed Information

- **Themberchaud's true condition**: He is physically unfit, pampered, emotionally immature, and unaware of how little genuine authority he holds. His scales are polished by handlers; his claws are painted and show no signs of use. He functions as a forge-lighter, not a ruler.
- **Themberchaud vs. Keepers of the Flame**: Themberchaud views the Keepers as short-sighted obstacles and believes himself to be the true power in Gracklstugh — a dangerous delusion the Duergar appear to be deliberately maintaining.
- **Duergar fear the Ember Vanguard, not Themberchaud**: Workers at the forge showed no fear of the dragon but panicked at the sight of suspected Ember Vanguard members, revealing the relative threat assessments within Gracklstugh.
- **Errde Blackskull likely informed Drow of party's passage**: Zalthir's assessment that Drow pursuit will resume once the party leaves the boat confirms Errde is suspected of passing intelligence to Drow contacts.
- **Mending spell scarcity**: Eldeth revealed that spellcasters capable of casting Mending are rare among naval forces because they are the primary assassination target in naval combat — the first kill to enable ship-sinking.
- **Lost Tomb of Khaem**: Located on the overland path between the Darklake and Neverlight Grove; described as containing a weapon the party intends to acquire before approaching the Grove.
- **Neverlight Grove demonic threat**: Zalthir has knowledge of a powerful demon present at the Grove and suspects the sovereign is compromised — information not shared with the Myconid companions.
