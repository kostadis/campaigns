## NPC Activity

**Ilvara** (Drow High Priestess, Commander of the outpost): Appeared in her quarters in the priestess's tower. Received water deliveries from enslaved prisoners. Threatened Prince Derendil with a knife when he objected to serving as a chamber slave. Described as behaving unusually manic compared to typical cold Drow demeanor. Maintains a shrine to Lolth. Possesses a scourge she uses to reinforce her authority. Has a chest at the foot of her bed, believed to contain confiscated prisoner belongings.

**Jorlan** (Drow, former favored of Ilvara): Delivered food to the prison cell. Approached Thorin with an offer to assist a prisoner escape — not out of sympathy, but to humiliate his rival Shoor and make Ilvara regret dropping him. Described his plan: leave the prison door open, create a distraction, leave the armory path unguarded. Confirmed he does not care if the escape succeeds or fails, only that the attempt embarrasses Shoor and reminds Ilvara of her mistake. Acknowledged openly that the prisoners were right not to trust him.

**Shoor** (Drow, current favored of Ilvara): Referenced but not present. Replaced Jorlan as Ilvara's favored after Jorlan was disfigured. Jorlan's escape scheme is motivated entirely by desire to undermine Shoor's standing.

**Beremil** (Drow guard): Oversaw Ront, Prince Derendil, and Thorin on water-carrying duty. Directed them to fill buckets and deliver water to Ilvara.

**Balok** (Drow guard): Oversaw the winch-and-basket operation. Deliberately allowed the basket to drop suddenly as a form of amusement, laughing at the prisoners' distress. Warned prisoners against theft from cargo, citing consequences.

**Guldor** (Drow guard): Oversaw the chamber pot detail. Caught Gyrgum attempting to pocket a knife and confiscated it, issuing a threatening warning. Also warned Daz that the prisoners would be lowered on the winch before they could flee if they attempted escape.

**Imbros** (Drow guard): Assigned Gyrgum, Suushar, Topsy, Turvy to kitchen/cooking duty.

**Jaezred** and **Kalannar** (Drow soldiers): Present in the mess hall, waiting to be fed. Witnessed and enjoyed Gyrgum's cooking performance on the first day.

**Suushar the Awakened** (Kuo-Toan prisoner): Informed Gyrgum about daily chore assignments and named the Drow overseeing each task. Translated Drow speech for Gyrgum, selectively omitting insults before eventually delivering them. Cautioned Gyrgum that exceptional cooking might cause his people to venerate him as a god. Expressed interest in visiting his people to share "enlightenment" and proposed routing any escape through Darklake, then to his people, then toward Blindenstone.

**Prince Derendil** (Quaggoth prisoner, believes himself an elven prince): Recalled Ilvara's original speech upon the prisoners' arrival — she was flanked by two male Drow, one scarred, and used a scourge while delivering the phrase "Accept your fate, learn to obey, and you may survive." Objected to chamber slave duties and received a knife-point threat from Ilvara. Assessed Jorlan's escape offer as self-interested rather than ideological, reasoning there is no abolitionist movement among Lolth worshippers.

**Ront** (Orc prisoner): Assigned to water-carrying duty with Beremil.

**Buppido** (Derro prisoner): Communicated via Stool's spore-link. Declared the group must collaborate per "the divine plan," initially framed escape as serving only himself before Thorin redirected him. Nominated Gracklestugh as the escape destination, claiming it as his homeland. Behavior consistent with believing himself to be a god or divine agent.

**Stool** (Myconid sprout prisoner): Released spores that created a group telepathic link among all prisoners in the cell, enabling covert coordinated planning. Proposed escaping toward the "great circle" and his own people rather than Gracklestugh.

**Jimjar** (Deep gnome prisoner): Repeatedly attempted to place bets on trivial and serious matters throughout both days. Proposed a wager on which escape destination the party would choose. Confirmed he keeps perfect mental recall of all debts and will collect when the party has money.

**Topsy and Turvy** (Deep gnome twin prisoners): Requested Gyrgum create a distraction so they could steal food on day one — succeeded. On day two, used Gyrgum's theatrical cooking performance to steal several knives from the distracted Drow soldiers. Proposed Blindenstone as an escape destination, claiming knowledge of a route out of the Underdark from there.

**Eldeth** (Prisoner, likely dwarf or surface dweller): Pocketed a comb found in the basket during cargo duty. Observed Zalthir pocket a paper from the basket. Offered a grimly pragmatic assessment of Underdark survival — no safety, scarce resources, outsiders viewed as resources to be exploited.

**Sarith** (Drow prisoner): Assigned to chamber pot duty with Daz and Stool. Present during escape planning via spore-link; nodded at Jimjar's comment about escape odds.

**Sethir** (Prisoner, identity not otherwise specified): Identified Jorlan to Thorin, explaining his fall from Ilvara's favor due to acid scarring and loss of a finger.

## Revealed Information

- **Ilvara's chest** at the foot of her bed is believed by Thorin to contain all confiscated prisoner gear, reasoning that Drow would taunt prisoners if they personally held items, and no taunting has occurred.
- **Jorlan's motivation** is purely personal: he wants to embarrass Shoor and force Ilvara to recognize her mistake in dropping him. He has no ideological commitment to the prisoners' success or survival.
- **The pool below the outpost** contains a **grey ooze**, which processes the waste deposited there — explaining why the lake appears clean despite constant filth disposal (recalled by Daz upon waking from nightmares).
- **Ilvara's behavior** is notably manic by Drow standards, which Thorin (raised by Giants, observant of Drow norms) registers as abnormal — potentially significant.
- **Route options out of the Underdark**: Blindenstone has a known exit to the surface (per Topsy/Turvy); the direct path to Blindenstone runs through Menzoberranzan (per Suushar), making Darklake a safer intermediary route.
- **The outpost layout** (partially mapped by prisoners through chore assignments): prison cell, mess hall cave, quaggoth barracks to the north, priestess's tower (shrine level above, Ilvara's quarters below, accessible by rope ladder), winch-and-basket device on the barracks platform edge, waterfall with pool 100 feet below, spider webs between the outpost and the pool.
