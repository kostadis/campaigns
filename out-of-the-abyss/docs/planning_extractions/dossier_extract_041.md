## Tappy Foamstrap

Owner of the Foaming Mug Tavern, a massive cave converted into an inn with rooms that actually fit normal-sized people. Described as a bored deep gnome desperate for new stories, having heard everyone else's tales dozens of times. She interacted with the party during their evening stay and again the next morning, when the party negotiated with her to acquire barrels of spoiled booze to use as weapons against oozes. The deal was made on the condition that the tavern's reputation would be protected.

## Jadger

The Burrow Warden who assigned the party (specifically Glabbagool, Eldeth, Jimjar, and Spiderbait) to tackle the ghost problem. He cheerfully noted that even if they died during the mission, they could still fight in the upcoming battle as ghosts through resurrection magic. He had specifically asked for the rescue of certain ghosts, including Udhask.

## Glabbagool

The gelatinous cube volunteered alongside Eldeth, Jimjar, and Spiderbait to tackle the ghost problem. Asked with curious sincerity whether he would be the first gelatinous cube ghost. That evening at the Foaming Mug Tavern, he became intoxicated from consuming spilled alcohol while cleaning the tavern floor, leading to him singing sea shanties "in a voice that made everyone question their life choices." The next day, he enthusiastically consumed mushrooms in the cultivation cave, plowing through like "a gigantic transparent snowblower" and clearing a path through dirt and gravel. Later, the party sent him to "clean" suspicious winged statues on a stone ledge, which turned out to be gargoyles — his appearance as a friendly gelatinous cube with googly eyes thoroughly confused their ambush plan.

## Udhask

A deep gnome ghost and one of the spirits Burrow Warden Jadger had asked the party to rescue. The party found his skeletal remains inside a burrow next to a stone bed, after chasing a ghost that fled into the area. A hidden compartment beneath the bed contained six valuable gems worth six hundred gold pieces each and a potion of invisibility. Notably, Udhask's bones showed no signs of violent death, suggesting his demise had been peaceful or natural. The party collected his bones to fulfill Jadger's quest.
