## Grygum

Grygum, the cleric of Bahamut, was welcomed by the first Galeb Duhr to what it called "the Realm of the Dragon," though the Galeb Duhrs quickly corrected that it was actually "the Realm of the Rock." Grygum responded with a mischievous grin, joking about being inside a cavern. He immediately recognized the reference to the "Lady of Rot" as Zuggtmoy, the demoness who had destroyed Neverlight Grove and nearly possessed Thorin in a previous encounter, sending waves of alarm through Blingdenstone's leaders. In the Festering Fissure, he summoned a bat familiar to scout the fungal cave ahead, then chanted a protective warding prayer during the lichen harvest attempt. When Zalthir was caught in Daz's hypnotic pattern, Grygum rushed forward and punched him in the face to break the trance—but his first blow was so weak it dealt no damage, forcing him to draw a dagger and stab his companion to properly wake him. At the White Shell Mine, Grygum sensed the Galeb Duhr were waiting for divine authority, and later provided a blessing to aid Zalthir's meditation before the explosive breach of the time-snagged tunnel. At the summoning circle, he was present as Thorin deciphered the giant runes requiring three sacrifices.

## Grunik Tapfinger

Grunik Tapfinger, the cleric who had originally sent the party on the quest to hallow Ogremoch's temple, greeted them warmly upon their return to Diggermattock Hall. As a reward for their success, he presented them with a precious empty fourth-level spell gem capable of being imbued with powerful magic. The party ultimately decided to load it with a healing spell for emergencies.

## Jimjar

Jimjar arrived at Diggermattock Hall with Eldeth, bearing dramatic news of their own success—they had destroyed Vazuk, a deep gnome ghost mad with grief who had been threatening residents. Jimjar proclaimed the battle epic and spectacular, the kind of combat that would inspire songs for centuries to come.

## Eldeth

Eldeth arrived at Diggermattock Hall alongside Jimjar, having helped destroy Vazuk, the tormented deep gnome ghost that had been threatening Blingdenstone's residents.

## Vazuk

Vazuk was a deep gnome ghost mad with grief who had been threatening the residents of Blingdenstone. He was destroyed by Jimjar and Eldeth while the main party was on their temple quest.

## Jadger

Burrow Warden Jadger materialized as a friendly ghost to thank the party for putting two tormented spirits to rest in the catacombs. As a reward for their service to Blingdenstone, he offered them two boons—they could ask him any two questions, and he would answer truthfully. He explained he would be waiting at the Ruby and the Rough temple, training the next generation of Burrow Wardens, whenever they were ready to claim their reward.

## Dorbo Diggermattock

Chief Dorbo Diggermattock expressed concern about the ongoing threat of acid attacks from the puddings, warning that if these creatures destroyed their weapons, their troops would die instantly.

## Naomi Pathshutter

Naomi Pathshutter appeared exhausted in the crowded meeting hall the next morning, having been awake for forty-eight hours attempting to summon earth elementals. She spread a map on the table and explained with frustration that hallowing Ogremoch's altar had not opened the path to Entemoch's boon as expected—the tunnels to the stone prince's throne had shifted and sealed, and the earth seemed frozen, unable to heal. Miners were reporting strange vibrations, as if the rock was trying to speak but couldn't find the words. In an awkward moment, she mistook Daz for an ogre, leading to a tense exchange about respect, though the community rallied to defend their allies. When the party revealed they had dealt with four insane earth elementals and a Medusa, her attitude shifted to genuine respect and admiration. She tasked them with investigating the strange vibrations and finding the lost path to Entemoch's boon.

## Kazook Pickshine

Kazook Pickshine, the local alchemist, arrived with troubling news. He had recalled a legend about a garden of stone deep in the rock blight and had sent his scout Bimble to investigate. Bimble had found the rare Ghost Hold Lichen needed for alchemical preparations but returned infected and raving. In his chambers, surrounded by bubbling beakers and mysterious potions, Kazook had Bimble strapped to a table. When the party cracked a joke about the situation, Kazook was upset and asked for compassion, explaining that Bimble was a dear friend, not just an assistant.

## Bimble

Bimble was Kazook Pickshine's scout, sent to investigate a garden of stone deep in the rock blight. He found the rare Ghost Hold Lichen they needed but returned wrong—infected and raving about a garden that sang to him and "the Lady of Rot feeding the soil." Found strapped to a table in Kazook's chambers, he was coughing up spores that formed tiny laughing faces before dissolving into the air. Through careful investigation of his ramblings, the smells clinging to him, and the textures of rock under his fingers, the party successfully pinpointed the exact location of the cave.

## Zuggtmoy

Zuggtmoy, the Lady of Rot, was identified by Grygum as the demoness who had destroyed Neverlight Grove and nearly possessed Thorin previously. Her corrupting influence permeated the Festering Fissure, where fungi had grown into disturbing shapes resembling deep gnome children. After the party defeated the Shambling Mound and harvested the lichen, a chilling voice echoed in their minds—Zuggtmoy speaking to the Pudding King about her sinister plans to use oozes to "soften the shell" so her garden could "take root in the flesh beneath." This revelation confirmed that Blingdenstone now faced not only Jubilex and the Pudding King but also Zuggtmoy's corrupting influence.

## The Pudding King

The Pudding King was heard in the psychic message from Zuggtmoy, who was speaking to him about her plans to use oozes to "soften the shell" so her garden could "take root in the flesh beneath." He remains an active threat to Blingdenstone, allied with both Jubilex and now confirmed to be in communication with Zuggtmoy.

## Dasco Pickshine

Dasco Pickshine, the mine owner at Pickshine Mine, had tried everything to move four massive Galeb Duhr blocking the path, even striking them with his pickaxe, but the ancient sentinels refused to budge. After the party successfully completed the ancient handshake protocol and the Galeb Duhr opened the way, Dasco grudgingly offered the party two ruby gems worth five hundred gold pieces as thanks.

## Perigrog Scrapedust

Perigrog Scrapedust was encountered at the White Shell Mine, the largest salt mine in the Underdark. He explained that a tunnel had become impassable—impossibly dry, with blinding crystallite walls and absolute, suffocating silence, where shadows appeared painted on walls rather than flickering, as if time itself had stopped.

## The Garden Shadow

The Garden Shadow was an ancient monk who had trained Zalthir. During the lichen harvest attempt, as poison took hold, Zalthir remembered that The Garden Shadow had explained exactly how to cut lichen and tend the trees, telling him the training would come in useful. At the time, Zalthir had had no intention to spend his life in contemplation of the wild, preferring to be out changing the world—but in the moment of crisis, he ruefully acknowledged the wisdom of the training.

## Entemoch

Entemoch is the good elemental prince who had once aided the deep gnomes of Blingdenstone. The Galeb Duhr at the hallowed temple explained the party still needed to obtain Entemoch's boon and argued amongst themselves about where he might be found, eventually agreeing that the gnomes would know since they had visited him regularly in ages past. Naomi Pathshutter reported that the tunnels to Entemoch's throne had shifted and sealed themselves after Ogremoch's altar was hallowed. A summoning circle with giant runes was discovered in a cavern guarded by basilisks, requiring three sacrifices—wealth, a weapon, and blood—to free Entemoch and claim his boon. Thorin had completed the weapon sacrifice (a dart) and the party stood on the threshold with two sacrifices remaining.

## Ogremoch

Ogremoch is the evil elemental earth god who had infested the rocks of the temple for centuries. The Galeb Duhr explained that the party had successfully hallowed the temple from his corrupting power. Naomi Pathshutter expected that hallowing Ogremoch's altar would open the path to Entemoch's boon, but instead the tunnels shifted and sealed, and the earth seemed frozen.
