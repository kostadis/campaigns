**Galeb Duhr (Four, unnamed, Pickshine Mine)** — Blocked the entrance to Pickshine Mine, vibrating rhythmically in an incomplete ancient resonance pattern. Unresponsive to physical force. Relaxed and opened the way after Daz completed the ancient "handshake protocol" using resonant geodes.

**Grunik Tapfinger** — Rewarded the party upon their return to Diggermattock Hall with a precious empty fourth-level spell gem in recognition of completing the temple quest.

**Jimjar** — Returned to Diggermattock Hall with news of having destroyed the deep gnome ghost Vazuk. Proclaimed the battle legendary and song-worthy.

**Eldeth** — Accompanied Jimjar; present for the announcement of Vazuk's destruction.

**Vazuk** — A deep gnome ghost, described as mad with grief and threatening to residents. Destroyed by Jimjar and Eldeth. No longer active.

**Burrow Warden Jadger** — Manifested as a friendly ghost in Diggermattock Hall. Thanked the party for putting two tormented spirits to rest in the catacombs. Offered two boons in the form of two truthful answers to any questions. Stated he would be waiting at the Ruby and the Rough temple to fulfill this reward.

**Chief Dorbo Diggermattock** — Present in Diggermattock Hall. Expressed concern about ongoing acid attacks from puddings destroying weapons and killing troops.

**Naomi Pathshutter** — Appeared exhausted after forty-eight hours of failed attempts to summon earth elementals. Spread a map and reported that hallowing Ogremoch's altar had not opened the path to Entemoch's boon as expected — the tunnels had shifted and sealed. Reported miners hearing strange vibrations as if the rock was trying to speak. Initially mistook Daz for an ogre, causing a tense exchange, but shifted to genuine respect after learning the party had defeated four insane earth elementals and a Medusa. Tasked the party with investigating the vibrations and finding the lost path to Entemoch's boon.

**Kazook Pickshine** — Local alchemist. Arrived at the meeting hall with news that his scout Bimble had found Ghost Hold Lichen in a legend-referenced "garden of stone" deep in the rock blight but had returned infected and raving. Expressed genuine distress over Bimble's condition, clarifying he is a dear friend, not merely an assistant. Led the party to his chambers where Bimble was restrained.

**Bimble** — Kazook's scout. Found Ghost Hold Lichen in the Festering Fissure but returned infected by Zuggtmoy's spores — strapped to a table, coughing up spores that formed tiny laughing faces before dissolving. Through his ramblings, the party was able to pinpoint the cave's location. Raved about "a garden that sang to him" and "the Lady of Rot feeding the soil."

**Zuggtmoy (the Lady of Rot)** — Not physically present but her influence was active throughout the session. Her corruption was identified in the Festering Fissure — the breathing walls, mushroom-children fungi, and the tended garden all bore her mark. After the party defeated the Shambling Mound, her voice echoed in their minds, speaking to the Pudding King about plans to use oozes to "soften the shell" so her garden could "take root in the flesh beneath."

**The Pudding King** — Referenced in Zuggtmoy's mental communication. Zuggtmoy spoke to him directly about coordinating the ooze attacks with her own corruption agenda. Not physically present.

**Shambling Mound** — Present in the Festering Fissure, tending Zuggtmoy's fungal garden. Attacked the party upon being alerted by Zalthir's ruptured poison sac. Defeated by the party.

**Violet Fungi (multiple)** — Present in the Festering Fissure. Incapacitated by Daz's hypnotic pattern, then charmed into confusion. Not destroyed.

**Dasco Pickshine** — Owner of Pickshine Mine. Had been unable to move the four blocking Galeb Duhr, including attempting to strike them with a pickaxe. After the party resolved the situation, he grudgingly rewarded them with two ruby gems worth 500 gp each.

**Perigrog Scrapedust** — Present at the White Shell Mine. Explained that a tunnel had become impassable — impossibly dry, crystallite walls, no flickering shadows, and absolute silence. Provided specialized gnome explosives to assist with the solution.

**The Garden Shadow** — Referenced but absent. An ancient monk who trained Zalthir, specifically teaching him how to cut lichen and tend trees, telling him the training would one day prove useful. This knowledge was recalled — imperfectly — during the lichen harvest.

**Basilisks (two)** — Guarded the summoning circle in the cavern beyond the breached White Shell Mine wall. Moved with mechanical precision rather than natural behavior. One was engaged in melee by Zalthir (eyes averted); the other was enchanted and frozen by Daz. Both still active as of session end.

**Entemoch** — Referenced but not yet contacted. Good elemental prince whose boon the party seeks. His summoning circle was located in the cavern beyond the White Shell Mine wall, inscribed in giant runes requiring three sacrifices: wealth, a weapon, and blood. One sacrifice (a dart, the weapon) was completed by Thorin, turning the runes from yellow to blinding orange.

**Ogremoch** — Referenced but not present. Evil elemental earth god whose corruption had infested the temple for centuries. His influence was cleansed by the hallowing event at the menhir.

## Revealed Information

- **Zuggtmoy is coordinating with the Pudding King.** She communicated to him directly that the ooze attacks are intended to "soften the shell" so her garden can spread into the living flesh of Blingdenstone's population.
- **Hallowing Ogremoch's altar was insufficient.** It did not automatically restore the path to Entemoch's boon — the tunnels remain sealed, and the earth is described as frozen and unable to heal, suggesting Ogremoch's corruption was deeper or differently rooted than expected.
- **Entemoch's summoning requires three sacrifices: wealth, a weapon, and blood.** The weapon sacrifice (a dart) has been completed. The circle's runes shifted from yellow to orange.
- **The Garden Shadow's training** was directly relevant to the lichen harvest — Zalthir's incomplete application of it resulted in a ruptured poison sac and initiated combat.
- **Ghost Hold Lichen grows from giant corpses** and must be cut carefully to avoid bursting adjacent poisonous sacs.
- **The White Shell Mine temporal snag** could only be safely resolved by a Shadow Monk creating a "hole" through shadow space for the backlog of reality to pass through — a direct combat application of Zalthir's training.
