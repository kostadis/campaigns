## Ulnara

Ulnara is identified as a member of the Council of Savants and a necromancer in charge of an operation in a pit filled with zombies, fungi, and decomposed flesh. She is a Derro and a cultist of Demogorgon. She emerged from a narrow tunnel in the southeast wall of the pit alongside an Ogre Zombie, demanding to know who the party was and who they worked for. When Daz demonstrated his power by killing Skiit with Toll the Dead, Ulnara's arrogance vanished and she became sniveling and subservient. She thanked the party for the "new supply" (Skiit's body for zombification) and eagerly asked them to tell Pliinki that she had done much to help the "Ember Vanguard." She appeared genuinely fearful upon realizing Daz was Drow. Her last known status is alive, operating in the zombie pit, believing the party to be powerful allies.

## Skiit

Skiit is a Derro working under Ulnara, tasked with tossing body parts into the zombie pit. He complained that "Pliinki promised Skiit a better job" and asked if he could kill Spiderbait. When Grygum attempted to intimidate him, Skiit puffed himself up and declared himself a follower of Demogorgon, proclaiming that Pliinki said anyone who follows Demogorgon is making the Derro "proud and powerful" and that they're "going to be great again." He accused the party of working for the Duergar or the Mindflayers. Grygum pushed him off the cliff into the pit. He survived the fall and began to wake up, but Daz first attempted to crush him with Maximilian's Earthen Grasp (Skiit escaped) and then killed him with Toll the Dead. Skiit is dead, and Ulnara planned to raise him as a zombie.

## Pliinki

Pliinki is referenced multiple times but never appears directly. Skiit mentioned that "Pliinki promised Skiit a better job," and Skiit also cited Pliinki as the one who declared that followers of Demogorgon would make the Derro proud and powerful. Ulnara also expressed hope that the party would tell Pliinki she had been helpful. Pliinki appears to be a Derro authority figure connected to the cult of Demogorgon, someone both Skiit and Ulnara answer to or seek approval from.

## Narrak

Narrak is a Derro encountered in a deeper cavern that reeked of brimstone and chemicals. He appears to be the leader or orchestrator of ritual activities there. His pale Derro features are perpetually contorted into a grotesque mask of spiraling madness, and his unnervingly bright eyes dart about with frenetic, desperate energy. He gestures wildly at apprentices and possesses a warped intellect utterly consumed by chaotic evil, radiating the insidious influence of Demogorgon. He was observed reading from books and leading chants. When asked by an apprentice what they were doing, he screamed: "We are conducting experiments!" for the purpose of "The elevation of the Derro, the Triumph of Demogorgon, and the destruction of the Duergar!" He was found on a natural 5-foot-high platform surrounded by a glowing ritual circle and books made from Trillimac, including titles like "The Spiraling Descent: A Treatise on Duality and Madness," "The Prince's Gift: On the Creation of Favored Spawn," and "Rituals of the Two-Headed Beast." His location contains evidence of Demogorgon's influence that Errde Blackstaff is seeking. Last seen alive, leading his cult experiments.

## Errde Blackstaff

Errde Blackstaff does not appear directly in this session but is referenced by Daz. She wants evidence of Demogorgon's influence among the Derro. Daz noted that the books and papers found in Narrak's ritual cavern would likely provide enough evidence to convince her. Daz also observed that if she doesn't get evidence, "she'll invent it," suggesting she has a predetermined agenda and is the type to fabricate justification if needed.

## Zalthir

Zalthir is a monk who served as the party's scout, moving ahead through the tunnels to keep them safe. He reported "Something's up ahead" when the party first encountered Skiit. During the Derro patrol fight, he summoned darkness to envelop enemies and landed a critical strike on one of the Derro. He asked pragmatically whether the party needed to fight the two mad Derro near the tent, accepting Thorin's assessment that there was nothing of value. At the end of the session, he observed everything in silence, described as acting without speaking.
