## Hemeth

Hemeth is a duergar and one of the castaways collected by the party. He identifies the abandoned boat as a Duergar Keelboat, recognizing the crates. He demonstrates the duergar ability to become invisible for a whole hour, saying "I will be hiding here" as he turns invisible during the potential ambush situation. Over the days leading up to arriving in Gracklstugh, Hemeth shares extensive information about his home city: it is strictly regimented and xenophobic, non-duergar are only permitted in the Darklake district, the duergar can become invisible at will so "you never know who is watching," and it is a city of slavers. He describes the Blade Bazaar as a marketplace named after the most abundant goods the duergar offer, selling almost everything available in the city. When Zalthir asks about purchasing an Eldritch Claw Tattoo, Hemeth cannot procure it and grumbles when the party turns to Brannum Redmarch instead, saying "I am a weapons trader, and you would go to him?"

## Jimjar

Jimjar is a deep gnome traveling with the party. When the party encounters the duergar keelboat, he informs Thorin that the duergar capture slaves to sell in Gracklstugh. He whispers the party's plan in Gracklstugh: buy equipment to survive more easily and get information on how to get out of the Underdark. When Buppido, under the effects of the Tongue of Madness mushroom, insults Jimjar's betting habit, it is noted as particularly annoying. Jimjar responds to Thorin's instruction to keep an eye on Buppido with "Wanna bet?" He roars with laughter at Zalthir's question about whether Buppido could summon Demogorgon, then delivers a detailed lecture on interplanar travel: the Time of Trouble allowed outer plane powers to enter the Prime Material Plane at will, but Ao the overgod ended that era with the Second Sundering. He cites the Cult of the Dragon's near-summoning of Tiamat as requiring artifacts and rituals, confirming Buppido has no such powers. He then poses the critical question to the group: "it does beg the question — who did?" summoning Demogorgon in the first place.

## Eldeth

Eldeth is a dwarf traveling with the party. During the duergar ambush on the water, she curses the duergar as "Demonic spawn of dwarven corruption. Slave traders who deserve to be sent to the nine hells as punishment!" — prompting Zalthir to note "some racial animus." Thorin checks on her during the battle and is relieved she was not among those captured. Buppido, under the influence of the Tongue of Madness, reveals that "Eldeth is -not- interested in him [Thorin]." She was not captured during the fight.

## Brannum Redmarch

Brannum Redmarch is a castaway traveling with the party. As they approach Gracklstugh, Thorin raises the question of what to do with him. Realizing his freedom may be at risk, Brannum pleads not to be turned over to the guard. When Zalthir asks about purchasing uncommon magical items, Brannum lights up, revealing that trading is his business. He claims he can procure an Eldritch Claw Tattoo, saying "I know a guy. But it will take a few days." He negotiates a deal: he will get the items in exchange for a clean slate with the party. Thorin threatens consequences if Brannum betrays them or fails to deliver, and Brannum agrees nervously.

## Buppido

Buppido is a derro castaway who has previously caused trouble for the party. As they approach Gracklstugh, the party debates what to do with him. Spiderbait wants to kill him on the spot. Buppido desperately pleads for his life, claiming he made mistakes driven by "the divine plan" but has since learned from Suushar that he was mad. Zalthir forces him to eat a Tongue of Madness mushroom, which compels him to speak truthfully. He reveals he wants to return to his bolt hole in caverns near the city to praise the Divine Plan. He then insults his companions: Daz has foul-smelling breath, Thorin's muscles aren't as impressive as he thinks, Eldeth isn't interested in Thorin, Zalthir's scales haven't been cleaned in ages, and Jimjar's betting is insufferable. Despite his insults, Zalthir says "I like him." The party ultimately decides to release him into the city as they leave, rather than killing him. Zalthir raises the question of whether Buppido could summon Demogorgon, which Jimjar thoroughly debunks.

## Spiderbait

Spiderbait is a member of the party's group who was captured by the duergar during the keelboat ambush. A duergar dragged Spiderbait away during the fight, but Zalthir killed the creature holding him. Spiderbait is described as "still seething with rage" and wants to kill Buppido on the spot when the party debates what to do with the derro near Gracklstugh.

## Sarith

Sarith is a drow traveling with the party. When the group debates who should interrogate the captured duergar prisoner, Sarith claims "The Lloth's guards trained me. I am an expert," but recommends Daz be given the first crack at extracting information based on having seen Daz's approach.

## Stool

Stool is a member of the party's group who was captured by the duergar during the keelboat ambush. Thorin noticed Stool had been taken and attacked the duergar, who then released Stool.

## Sethir

Sethir is present in Gracklstugh and mutters about the party's plans: "We plan to get equipment and then go to the Lost Tomb of Khem, where we will find treasure!"

## Suushar

Suushar is mentioned by Buppido during his desperate plea for his life. Buppido claims he "learned from Suushar that I was mad," suggesting Suushar had a calming or enlightening influence on the derro.

## Dulgir

Dulgir is a duergar merchant at a stall in the Blade Bazaar in Gracklstugh. When Grygum first approaches and tries to sell a mace, Dulgir curses him viciously. But when Grygum shifts to buying instead, Dulgir's demeanor completely changes, and he speaks "with the stilted tongue of a Baldurian patriar." Grygum purchases a new mace from him. When Grygum asks about another merchant who appears to be talking to an invisible brother, Dulgir matter-of-factly states "His brother doesn't exist" — an answer that disturbs Grygum and reinforces his suspicion that madness afflicts the city.

## King Hekaton

King Hekaton is mentioned in Thorin's reverie about the festival of Greengrass. After the end of the War of the Giants, King Hekaton decreed peace with the "little people" (the smaller races), and so peace was maintained among the giants and their neighbors.
