## Yuk-Yuk

Yuk-Yuk was one of two goblin companions (along with Spiderbait) who joined the group after being promised 20 gold, convinced by Jimjar to follow into the dangerous tunnels. He traveled with the party through the Silken Tunnels and into the Underdark. On the 1st day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493, Yuk-Yuk was found murdered—his throat cut with a shortsword while the group slept. Spiderbait discovered the body and was screaming. Grygum noted that any member of the group could have been the killer. Current status: dead, murdered in his sleep.

## Spiderbait

Spiderbait was one of two goblin companions (along with Yuk-Yuk) who joined the group after being promised 20 gold, convinced by Jimjar to follow into the dangerous tunnels. On the 1st day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493, Spiderbait discovered Yuk-Yuk's murdered body and was heard screaming, waking the rest of the group. Current status: alive, grieving his friend's death.

## Jimjar

Jimjar convinced the goblins Yuk-Yuk and Spiderbait to join the group by promising them 20 gold. He was excited about finding a tinderbox among the bandit loot, declaring "Toasted Ripplebark, the best thing in the Underdark." During the debate about disposing of corpses near the gorge, Jimjar (along with Topsy and Turvy) believed that leaving the bodies would attract monsters that would hinder their Drow pursuers. He found the name "Blingdenstone" mildly disinteresting as a destination. He took a bet with Grygum about whether Thorin would make it across the gorge, and later took another bet with Grygum about what kind of monsters they'd find in the lost tomb, wagering against skeletons and zombies. He was told to be quiet by the rest of the group while making bets at bedtime.

## Eldeth

Eldeth found waterorbs near a pool of water and explained their properties to the group—bulbous fungi that grow in shallow water and can be squeezed for about a gallon of potable water, with the remaining husk being edible and useful for water storage. Her foraging success made her indispensable to the group, and her ability to hide their tracks may be why they are still alive. She identified the dangerous Timmask mushrooms, warning the group about their confusion-inducing spore clouds when damaged, and pointed out a corpse killed by the mushrooms (the escaped bandit from the gorge ambush). She is frustrated by the constant trudging through the dark ("Another day in the dark"). When Thorin tried to befriend her, Eldeth was put off by his relationship with giants, citing the devastating effects of the War of the Giants on the people of the North. She remained unconvinced by Thorin's defense that "they were different giants." She was among those who realized the bandit leader's story was fabricated.

## Suushar

Suushar the Awakened witnessed Thorin's bout of madness and suggested that the archpriest Ploopploopeen in Sloopbludop could help, praising Ploopploopeen as a follower of the Sea Mother who would welcome his own spiritual awakening and the end of the kuo-toa's madness. When Grygum called the archpriest's name a tongue twister, Suushar was genuinely confused and demonstrated by repeating the name multiple times. During the corpse disposal debate, Suushar argued that trackers couldn't discern anything meaningful from natural scavenging activity. Regarding travel plans, Suushar stated he had no interest in traveling past Sloopbludop but might be willing to help with travel on the Darklake.

## Ploopploopeen

Mentioned by Suushar as the archpriest of Sloopbludop, a follower of the Sea Mother. Suushar suggested Ploopploopeen could help Thorin with his bout of madness, and that Ploopploopeen would be welcoming of Suushar's spiritual awakening. Does not appear directly in these events.

## Buppido

Buppido rolled his eyes and looked upward when Zalthir said there was no way across the gorge, silently prompting Zalthir to notice the climbable ceiling. During the corpse disposal debate, Buppido was emphatic that leaving bodies would leave a trail for trackers—and Zalthir believed him due to the conviction of his argument, leading the group to drop the bodies into the gorge. Buppido correctly predicted the dead bandit found near the Timmask mushrooms had friends when he muttered "He had friends," which was confirmed when more bandits arrived. In the destination debate, Buppido insisted on going to Gracklstugh first, suggesting they could purchase needed equipment there before heading to Blingdenstone.

## Stool

Stool, the young Myconid, spotted and identified a mushroom called Nilhogg's Nose near the bottomless gorge, exclaiming "That's a cool mushroom!" He explained its properties: it's edible but not filling, and after eating it, one's sense of smell is dramatically enhanced—making it easier to find good mushrooms and other scents, but also making bad smells painfully intense.

## Topsy

Topsy (along with Turvy) sided with Jimjar during the corpse disposal debate, believing that monsters attracted to the bodies would hinder their Drow pursuers. Topsy found the name "Blingdenstone" off-putting when it was suggested as a destination.

## Turvy

Turvy (along with Topsy) sided with Jimjar during the corpse disposal debate, believing that monsters attracted to the bodies would hinder their Drow pursuers. Turvy found the name "Blingdenstone" off-putting when it was suggested as a destination.

## Sethir

Sethir seemed particularly affected by the Faerzress's eerie magic while traveling through tunnels rich in it. During the destination debate, Sethir insisted the group should check out the lost temple and find treasure, and also observed that traveling along the Darklake would be less treacherous than traveling through tunnels.
