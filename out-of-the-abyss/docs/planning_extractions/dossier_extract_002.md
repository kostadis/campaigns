## Suushar the Awakened

Suushar is a Kuo-Toan prisoner who serves as translator and cultural guide for the non-Undercommon-speaking party members. When Gyrgum worries about Ront and Prince Derendil being taken away, Suushar reassures him that these are daily chores and identifies the Drow taskmasters by name — Beremil (water duty), Balok (winch duty), Guldor (chamber pots), and Imbros (cooking). He tells Gyrgum, "These are daily chores, nothing more."

During kitchen duty, Suushar relays Topsy and Turvy's request for Gyrgum to create a distraction so they can steal food. When translating for the Drow, he claims to omit offensive words as part of his spiritual practice of finding balance and coping with "the madness of my race," but then proceeds to translate every insult anyway. He is excited about Gyrgum's cooking and tells the orc, "If you were to show this cooking to my people, they might imagine a god like you."

During the escape planning discussion, Suushar advocates traveling to Darklake first, arguing that the direct path to Blingdenstone goes through Menzoberranzan. He insists the group must visit his people "to share the enlightenment." He is currently held in the slave pen at the Drow outpost.

## Ront

Ront is a prisoner assigned to water-carrying duty under the Drow guard Beremil, alongside Prince Derendil and Thorin. He is seen filling buckets of water at the waterfall. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Prince Derendil

Prince Derendil is a Quaggoth who believes himself to be a prince — described as "the deluded Quaggoth." He is assigned to water-carrying duty under Beremil alongside Ront and Thorin. He recalls in detail how Ilvara greeted prisoners upon arrival, describing her silk robes, her scourge, and her signature phrase: "Accept your fate, learn to obey, and you may survive." When Ilvara threatens him for objecting to being a "chamber enslaved person," she pulls a knife and describes in detail how she will punish him. During escape planning, he provides political insight, noting there is "no abolitionist movement amongst those who worship Llolth" and that Jorlan's offer must be self-serving. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Beremil

Beremil is a Drow guard who oversees the water-carrying chore. He takes Ront, Prince Derendil, and Thorin to fill buckets at the waterfall and then escorts them to deliver water to Ilvara's quarters. He shouts orders like "Fill the buckets and then come" and "Now, we take water to Ilvara." Currently stationed at the Drow outpost.

## Balok

Balok is a Drow who runs the winch-and-basket apparatus. He oversees the cargo loading and unloading chore, commanding Zalthir, Buppido, Jimjar, and Eldeth. He warns the prisoners: "You will clean and unload. If you steal anything, then there will be consequences." He enjoys tormenting the prisoners by having the Quaggoth attendants drop the basket suddenly, laughing as it bounces. Currently stationed at the winch platform.

## Guldor

Guldor is a Drow guard assigned to the chamber pot cleaning detail — described by Suushar as having "the dirty job," suggesting he failed at something to earn the assignment. He supervises Daz, Sarith, and Stool. He catches Gyrgum trying to steal a knife in the kitchen and threatens him with severe consequences. During the chamber pot run, he warns Daz that he can get down to the lake before they can run if they try anything. Currently stationed at the Drow outpost.

## Imbros

Imbros is a Drow who assigns the cooking chore. He selects Suushar, Topsy, Turvy, and Gyrgum for kitchen duty. He is only mentioned by name through Suushar's identification.

## Jimjar

Jimjar is a Deep Gnome prisoner with a compulsive gambling habit. He constantly tries to get other prisoners to place bets on everything: who would die first from a fall, how many buckets will be filled, how many people will come up or down in the basket, and which escape route the party will choose. He claims to have "perfect recall" for tracking debts and tells Zalthir not to worry about having no money — "just make your wager, and when you have money, I'll collect." He expresses confidence that the group will escape and survive. He is assigned to the winch duty with Zalthir. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Buppido

Buppido is a Derro prisoner who believes himself to be divine — either thinking he is a god or actually claiming to be one. Gyrgum has "a peculiar expression" when Buppido uses the word "divine," and Daz later learns from the cleric that Buppido's belief in his own godhood is genuine. During the escape planning via Stool's telepathic spores, Buppido initially proposes that the others create a distraction while "I escape" — notably using the singular. When Thorin corrects him to "we escape," Buppido looks surprised and says "We?" Thorin manipulates him by framing the group as pledged to protect him in fulfilling "the divine plan," and Buppido accepts: "You have pledged your swords and your spells! And your lives." When asked about the plan's details, he responds like "any mysterious god or charlatan masquerading as a god" — "In due time." He advocates escaping to Gracklstugh, calling it "my homeland." Currently held in the slave pen.

## Eldeth

Eldeth is a prisoner assigned to winch duty alongside Zalthir, Buppido, and Jimjar. During the escape route discussion, she provides a grim warning about the Underdark: "Safety? This is the Underdark. There is not enough resources or space for anyone. Survival depends on your wits. People tend to distrust outsiders and view them as a resource to be used." She is seen pocketing a comb she finds in the cargo basket. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Topsy

Topsy is a Deep Gnome prisoner and twin sibling of Turvy. During the first kitchen assignment, the twins whisper to each other and propose (through Suushar) that Gyrgum create a distraction so they can steal food — which they successfully do. On the second day, they wink at Gyrgum and successfully steal several knives while the Drow soldiers are distracted by his theatrical cooking performance. During the escape planning, Topsy advocates going to Blingdenstone, claiming to know a way out of the Underdark from there. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Turvy

Turvy is a Deep Gnome prisoner and twin sibling of Topsy. The twins work together during kitchen duty, first stealing food and then stealing several knives on the second day while Gyrgum distracts the Drow soldiers with his cooking performance. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Jaezred

Jaezred is a Drow soldier identified by Suushar as one of two Drow sitting at a table in the mess hall, looking hungry, when Gyrgum arrives for cooking duty. No further details are given.

## Kalannar

Kalannar is a Drow soldier identified by Suushar as the second of two Drow sitting at a table in the mess hall, looking hungry, during Gyrgum's cooking assignment. No further details are given.

## Ilvara

Ilvara is the high priestess of Lolth and commander of the Drow outpost. She resides in the uppermost level of the largest hanging tower (the priestess's tower), which contains both a shrine to Lolth and her personal quarters. The shrine features a 10-foot sculpted spider with eight polished jet "eyes" and silver candlesticks. Her quarters are draped in black mesh resembling spider webs, with cushioned beds, a small shrine draped in white silk, and a heavy chest of black-stained zurkhwood at the foot of her bed — which Thorin believes contains the party's confiscated equipment.

She greets the water-carrying prisoners with her scourge, flicking it toward them, and commands: "This is my quarters. I am the high priestess of Lolth and commander. Do not spill any water, do not touch anything, and fill my bath." When Prince Derendil objects, she pulls a knife and threatens him in excruciating detail. Thorin observes something manic about her — noting that Drow are typically calculating and cold, but Ilvara appears "a bit crazy."

Her romantic life is politically relevant: she dropped her former consort Jorlan after his disfigurement (acid scarring and a lost finger) and replaced him with Shoor. This has driven Jorlan to betray her by offering to help the prisoners escape. Currently commanding the Drow outpost from the priestess's tower.

## Jorlan

Jorlan is a Drow male and former favored consort of Ilvara. After being hit with acid that disfigured one side of his face and neck and cost him a finger, Ilvara dropped him in favor of Shoor. Motivated by jealousy and revenge, Jorlan approaches Thorin during a food delivery and offers to help the prisoners escape — not because he cares about their success, but to humiliate Shoor and make Ilvara regret her choice. He explains: "I don't care if you succeed. I just want you to try. It will make Shoor look ridiculous, and make Ilvara realize what a terrible mistake she's made. And if you do make a mistake, she'll be embarrassed and need my help to find you and realize what a mistake she made."

His plan: leave the prison door open, create a distraction, leave the path to the armory unguarded so prisoners can grab weapons and flee via the waterfall or spider webs into the pools below. He acknowledges the prisoners' distrust, saying "I can sense you don't trust me. You are wise not to trust me." The escape is planned for after a long rest, before the morning chore assignments. Currently stationed at the Drow outpost.

## Shoor

Shoor is a Drow male who replaced Jorlan as Ilvara's favored consort after Jorlan's disfigurement. He was one of the two male Drow flanking Ilvara when she first greeted the prisoners. Jorlan's escape plot is partly motivated by wanting to make Shoor "look ridiculous." Currently stationed at the Drow outpost.

## Sarith

Sarith is a Drow prisoner assigned to chamber pot duty with Daz and Stool under Guldor's supervision. He nods during the escape planning discussion. Sethir (identified elsewhere as Sarith based on context) provides information about Jorlan's backstory — his disfigurement, his fall from Ilvara's favor, and Shoor's rise — and later mentions there is a grey ooze in the pool that processes waste. Currently held in the slave pen.

## Stool

Stool is a Myconid prisoner who plays a critical role in the group's coordination by releasing spores that create a telepathic group mind, allowing all prisoners to communicate and plan without being overheard by the Drow. Stool is assigned to chamber pot duty with Daz and Sarith. During the escape route debate, Stool advocates going to "the great circle" where its people live, saying "No to the great circle! My people!" Currently held in the slave pen.
