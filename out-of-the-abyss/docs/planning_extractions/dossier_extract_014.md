## Jimjar

Jimjar stares at Grygum and asks him, "Why are you a follower of Bahamut?" He was fishing to find out what Grygum believed about the nature of Bahamut's influence—whether it was divine intervention, individual agency, or fate—that led to the famous chess match between Protanther and Stroud. When Grygum smiles and says nothing, Jimjar simply stares back. He appears to be traveling with the party on the Darklake. Last seen on the boat on the 9th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493.

## Protanther

Protanther is a Gold Dragon referenced in the story of the Well of the Dragons. He had been studying a chess board for 400 years when the Dwarf Stroud defeated him. Many chess grandmasters were dumbfounded that Protanther did not see the losing move coming. Those who believe in the intrinsic goodness of Gold Dragons believe Protanther chose to lose deliberately, seeing an opening to side with those who opposed Tiamat and framing the loss as a sign from the gods. Others believe Bahamut hid the move from Protanther. His defeat became a pivotal event that led to Bahamut gaining a wider following among non-dragon faithful.

## Stroud

Stroud is a Dwarf who defeated the Gold Dragon Protanther in a chess game after the events of the Well of the Dragons. He went on a speaking tour afterward, talking about his victory and walking audiences through his thought process, the board, and the inspiration behind his winning move. Chess grandmasters who analyzed the game were confused by Stroud's explanation, as it appeared he barely had a passing knowledge of the rules. Some saw Stroud as the personification of the right humanoid at the right time and place—proof that a single individual could change the world. Others believed Bahamut moved Stroud's hand.

## Sarith

When Zalthir asks if anyone has oars, Sarith laughs and replies, "It would have been useful a while ago…" Last seen on the boat on the 10th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh 1493.
