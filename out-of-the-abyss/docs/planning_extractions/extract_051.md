## NPC Activity

**Asha Vandry**
Appeared in the fungal cavern where she has been hiding. Engaged in extended negotiation with Daz and the party. Expressed fanatical loyalty to Lolth and violent hatred toward Ilvara, whom she accuses of adopting the "bride heresy" — the belief that Lolth wishes to marry Zuggtmoy. Attempted to maintain authority over the conversation by directing all speech at Daz and ignoring Zalthir entirely. Revealed critical tactical intelligence about the Heart Fungus, Ilvara's spores, the mushroom artifact, Jorlan, and the infected drow spore servants. Provided guidance on spell targeting (prioritizing the Heart Fungus over Ilvara directly). Warned Thorin and Grygum away from the mushroom artifact due to a marker left on them from Gracklstugh. Requested the party kill Ilvara and destroy the mushroom artifact in exchange for everything Ilvara had accumulated. Flinched noticeably when the possibility of physical contact arose ("Don't touch me"). Expressed genuine exasperation about being trapped between Ilvara and Jorlan's ongoing post-breakup tension. Briefly warmed when discussing relationship drama, showing unexpected humanizing affect. Became impressed upon learning Glabbagool was presented as Daz's familiar.

**Ilvara**
Former high priestess of Lolth, now corrupted by Zuggtmoy's influence. Observed by the party from a corridor, stationed on an elevated platform sixty feet up, preoccupied with a mushroom token/icon. Visibly transformed — described as corrupted, with fungal growth altering her appearance while retaining her scourge. During combat, demonstrated ability to melt into the floor and reform elsewhere in the cavern, breaking Zalthir's grapple and escaping. Screamed upon reforming. Sustained damage from Zalthir's opening strikes (31 damage), Grygum's Dust of Suleiman, and Daz's precisely targeted Fireball (24 damage). Her mushroom icon was set smoldering by the Fireball. Controls spore servants and the Heart Fungus as mechanisms of power.

**Jorlan**
Named by Asha as one of only three individuals not under Ilvara's spore control — resistant due to personal hatred of Ilvara stemming from a prior romantic relationship she ended. Asha described him as bitter, still nursing the breakup, and difficult to be around. Was present in the cavern on the far side of the bridge from the party's position. Charged Thorin after being taunted ("Come get it, idiot!"), crossed the Glyph of Warding, and triggered the detonation (21 damage). Was struck by Thorin for 14 damage and placed at disadvantage via Sap. Remained standing after Thorin's second swing missed. Still in active combat with Thorin at end of session.

**Glabbagool**
Present throughout the negotiation with Asha Vandry, repeatedly failing to remain still and silent as instructed. Began forming utterances during the negotiation. Eyes emerged visibly from the ooze at one point, alarming Asha. Was introduced by Thorin as Daz's familiar to explain away his presence. Asha accepted this and became more impressed with Daz as a result. Glabbagool's reaction to being called a familiar was not noted as negative.

**Spore Servants (unnamed, former Velkynvelve drow)**
Four spore servants emerged from the ground during combat. Identified by Asha as former drow party members who willingly embraced Ilvara's spores and were fully subsumed. One was destroyed by the Glyph of Warding detonation; a second was left barely functional by the same blast. One climbed the bridge to attack Thorin, landing a 5-damage glancing blow.

**Heart Fungus**
A massive fungal organism serving as the infrastructure of Ilvara's control over the cavern. During combat, deployed a cloud of spores that struck Zalthir severely enough to force him to climb ten feet to breathe. Was targeted by Grygum's Dust of Suleiman (20 force damage per turn) and hit by the overlap of Daz's Fireball. Described as shuddering under the combined assault.

## Faction Movements

**Lolth Orthodoxy (represented by Asha Vandry)**
Asha has been isolated in the cavern for months, holding out against Ilvara's infection. She has recruited the party as proxies to execute an assassination she cannot accomplish alone. By providing tactical intelligence and targeting guidance, she has advanced her goal of eliminating Ilvara and destroying the mushroom artifact, though she remains entirely dependent on the party to execute it.

**Zuggtmoy's Influence / Ilvara's Faction**
Ilvara retains control of the cavern through the Heart Fungus and her spore servants, but both assets are now under active assault. The Heart Fungus is being damaged, the mushroom icon is smoldering, and Ilvara herself has taken significant damage. Jorlan — the only other free agent in the cavern — has been drawn into combat with Thorin and triggered a prepared trap, reducing Ilvara's potential reinforcements.

## Revealed Information

- **Ilvara's spores are the vector for the fungal disease**, not the Heart Fungus itself. The Heart Fungus causes poisoning, pain, breathing difficulties, and damage — but not spore infection. Asha qualified this with "I believe," indicating it is informed assessment rather than confirmed fact.
- **The mushroom artifact is dangerous specifically to Thorin and Grygum** due to a marker left on them from their visit to the Whorestone Caverns pocket dimension in Gracklstugh.
- **Ilvara and Jorlan had a prior romantic affair.** Ilvara ended it. Jorlan remains bitter and consumed by resentment, which is the source of his resistance to her spore influence.
- **Only three individuals in the cavern are free of spore control:** Asha (through sustained Lolth devotion and rejection of the bride heresy), Jorlan (through personal hatred of Ilvara), and the party.
- **The spore servants are former drow** from the original party who voluntarily accepted Ilvara's spores, believing her claims about the bride heresy, and were fully subsumed.
- **Ilvara can melt into fungal surfaces and reform elsewhere** — a combat ability not previously documented.
- **Ilvara's reward cache** (everything she has accumulated as a high priestess running an operation in this cavern) is the offered payment for killing her and destroying the artifact.
