## Jimjar

Jimjar identified Barrelstalk mushrooms for the group, explaining they can produce 1-4 gallons of water and 5-10 pounds of nutritious food. He also identified Ormu (bioluminescent green moss), explaining it can be harvested, dried, and made into phosphorescent powder or pigment. Later, he identified Trillimac mushrooms, explaining their properties — the cap can be used for maps, hats, and scrolls, and the stalk can be soaked and dried into bread-like food. He estimated one find would yield 6 pounds of food.

Zalthir noted Jimjar is "good at hiding things" and asked him to block a narrow passageway to conceal evidence of their passage. Jimjar insisted on a 5 gold bet rather than doing it for free. Despite Zalthir's escalating frustration ("WE HAVE NO MONEY"), Jimjar remained fixated on gambling. Daz intervened by claiming Zalthir had taken a monastic oath against gambling. Jimjar was stunned and apologetic. Grygum then took the bet instead; Jimjar completed the task but announced Grygum had won — meaning Jimjar had bet against himself. Zalthir theorized that Jimjar's willingness to take bad bets could be exploited as "an infinite cash machine." When Zalthir tested this by betting Jimjar couldn't fly to the cavern roof, Jimjar laughed and refused: "I am a gambler, not an idiot."

At the Silken Paths, Zalthir prompted Jimjar to negotiate with the goblin guides. Jimjar bet them 20 gold they couldn't navigate the spiderwebs. The goblins accepted and succeeded. Jimjar acknowledged they won, then admitted he had no money. When Spiderbait proposed they serve as guides until Jimjar could pay the 20 gold debt, Jimjar acted like it was a terrible idea, then sighed and agreed — seemingly having engineered the arrangement to get free guides. He privately indicated to Grygum (with "a twinkle in his eye") that the goblins didn't know which side of the bet they had taken.

## Eldeth

Eldeth covered the rear of the caravan as they traveled, working to obscure evidence of their passage through the Underdark. Buppido was notably interested in her techniques. When Daz, Grygum, Thorin, and Zalthir tried to help her, she "tried not to be annoyed at all the help." She shouted at Grygum to actually look for Trillimac mushrooms, and his subsequent search found 20 additional pounds of food. At the Silken Paths, Eldeth drew on her spelunking experience to suggest tying silk rope around each member of the party so that if one person fell from the web, others could arrest the fall. This innovation impressed the goblin guides. When Spiderbait asked what happens if everyone fails to stop the fall, she answered matter-of-factly: "Then I suppose we all die."

## Buppido

Buppido showed particular interest in Eldeth's techniques for obscuring the group's trail. At the Silken Paths, he asked Sarith to explain what the Silken Paths were, despite admitting he already knew. He expressed doubt about relying on the goblin guides, muttering: "I am not sure the divine plan had anything about relying on two semi-sentient goblins for our safety."

## Sarith

Sarith (also referred to as "Sathir" in the text) roused everyone on the morning of the 8th, urging them to continue. Upon reaching the Silken Paths, he excitedly confirmed they were heading in the right direction and provided a detailed description of the chasm: a 500-foot-deep, 2,000-foot-wide chasm stretching nearly five miles, crossed by a network of giant spider webs that are ever-changing. After the deal with the goblin guides was struck, Sarith directed them: "Take us to a passageway that will get us quickly to Sloopbludop."

## Shuushar

Shuushar reassured the group about their water concerns, sighing and telling them: "Soon, we will be at the Darklake, and we will have more water than we ever knew what to do with it."

## Stool

At the Silken Paths, Stool was called upon by Zalthir to use his rapport spores to communicate with whatever was inside a moving, humanoid-sized cocoon. Stool was enthusiastic: "I'm helpful, aren't I? I love being helpful." Grygum affirmed this warmth, and the two advanced on the cocoon together, enabling communication with the trapped halfling Fargas Rumblefoot.

## Topsy

Daz noticed Topsy and her brother Turvy talking to each other as the group settled to sleep, but they were too far away for him to understand what they were saying. Later, at the Silken Paths, Yukyuk excitedly tried to chat with Topsy about his web-surfing exploits, but she only stared at him, confused.

## Turvy

Daz noticed Turvy and his sister Topsy talking privately to each other as the group settled to sleep. They were too far away for anyone to overhear their conversation.

## Yukyuk

Yukyuk is one of two goblin guides encountered at the entrance to the Silken Paths. His color motif is orange. He wears relaxed comfortable shoes, baggy pants, a long-sleeved wide shirt, and a beanie, and is covered in tattoos written in draconic script (actually goblin words using draconic characters, with incorrect spelling). He introduced himself by name and, along with Spiderbait, offered to guide the party across the Silken Paths for 2 gold per day. When Jimjar tricked them into demonstrating their skills via a bet and then couldn't pay, Yukyuk was angered, tossed his beanie on the ground, and started marching back to the web — but Spiderbait negotiated an arrangement where they'd guide the party until the debt was paid. Once the deal was settled, his formal demeanor dissolved into enthusiastic surfer-like speech, excitedly telling Topsy about his web-surfing moves. He recalled a "crazy Drow wizard" who once tried to burn down the Silken Paths but failed because the spiders rebuilt faster than he could burn, and noted that without the webs, travelers face "gnarly cliffs." He reassured Zalthir that web vibrations from the party wouldn't attract spiders because "there is so much stuff bouncing on the webs." He claimed all their clients make it out alive. When spiders were detected near the cocoon, his advice was simply: "Get out of here."

## Spiderbait

Spiderbait is the second goblin guide at the Silken Paths. His color motif is pink, and he speaks with a distinctive accent — rolled R's and oddly clipped vowels. He shares the same tattoo-covered, casual attire as Yukyuk. He boasted about taking a harder route during the web demonstration: "I went around the spider and through its legs." After Jimjar couldn't pay the 20-gold bet, Spiderbait was the one who devised the solution — proposing that the goblins serve as guides and stay with the group until Jimjar could pay. He was enthusiastic and positive after the deal was struck, high-fiving Yukyuk and telling the group: "We are the best. We are going to have so much fun going through the web with you." He was intrigued by Eldeth's rope-tying safety innovation and took mental notes. He explained that fire can burn through the web but warned anyone within 30 feet of the burning point would likely fall to their death.

## Fargas Rumblefoot

Fargas Rumblefoot is a halfling discovered alive inside a moving cocoon in the Silken Paths. He communicated telepathically via Stool's rapport spores. He identified himself as a member of an adventuring band that had been searching for a lost tomb when they were attacked by gnolls. He described gnolls in detail — feral hyena-headed humanoids connected to the demon lord Yeenoghu. He claimed he escaped the gnolls without being infected and ended up captured by spiders as "spider snack for later." He grew increasingly desperate as the party debated whether to free him, especially as he sensed spiders approaching. To incentivize his rescue, he offered valuable information: he knows the location of the tomb his party was seeking and, more importantly, the location of its treasure. His status at the end of the scene is uncertain — Thorin was preparing to cut him free, but spiders were closing in.
