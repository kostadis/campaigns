## NPC Activity

**Earth Elemental**
Appeared in the throne room following the Pudding King's defeat, straining to hold the passage open while its elemental energy leaked through fissures. Healed by Grygum's Mass Healing Word, it stretched, sighed, deposited a small glowing geode at the party's feet as a token of favor, and dissolved back into the earth.

**Chipgrin**
Found in the Hall of Melting Mirrors leading a wererat chanting vigil to suppress lingering psychic static. Requested the party's spellcasters locate the harmonic center of the room; when Thorin succeeded where the casters had failed, Chipgrin remarked he should have asked the dwarf first, then awkwardly clarified to Zalthir he meant no offense. Later participated in the Blingdenstone council meeting, arguing for wererat control of the upper boroughs. Upon the party's departure, presented Daz with the Voucher of the Varmint — a braided rat's foot token recognized by wererats and outcasts from Blingdenstone to Menzoberranzan.

**Glabbagool**
An intelligent gelatinous cube ally carried in a bag of holding. When Jimjar tossed the bone die onto him, he briefly became a telepathic bridge connecting every ooze in the cavern into a single vast mind. He communicated to the ooze horde that the world was fine and there was no need for violence, after which the entire swarm went mindless and peaceful, dispersing into cracks and stonework. Afterward he described the experience as briefly being part of one large shared mind.

**Jimjar**
Appeared at the support beams, commenting on the party's knack for fixing things. At the Trader's Grotto, he approached Grygum and proposed a wager of ten platinum pieces that he could stop the ooze threat, then tossed the bone die onto Glabbagool, triggering the blink event that pacified the ooze army. Collected his winnings from Grygum. Explained the die's origin: won from a blind Duergar in Gracklestugh who claimed it was carved from the knucklebone of a saint who never told a lie. Before the party's departure, pointed to a pale white puffball mushroom growing at impossible speed from a crack in the wall, warning ominously that Zuggtmoy "prefers to move in while you're sleeping." When Zalthir used a discernment ability to inspect him, Jimjar noticed, offered a knowing wink, and vanished entirely from existence, leaving only the echo of platinum coins. His revealed stat profile: immune to poison and psychic damage, cannot be charmed or frightened, resistant to radiant damage and non-magical physical attacks — a profile consistent with a celestial or divine being.

**Chief Dorbo Diggermattock**
Participated in the Blingdenstone council meeting, advocating for aggressive economic recovery and mithral extraction. Earlier, personally sewed a Diggermattock Miners patch onto Thorin's gear as recognition for his work at the support beams, declaring Thorin an honorary member of the crew. Ultimately agreed to the peace arrangement brokered by the party and announced plans to reopen Blingdenstone for trade with the surface world.

**Senni Diggermattock**
Present at the council meeting as Dorbo's wife and a member of the Gold Whisker clan. Advocated for a functional alliance between gnomes and wererats; her support was noted as influential to Dorbo's decision-making.

**Burrow Warden Jadger**
Spectral leader of the Blingdenstone ghosts. Observed the party's departure from the Northern Exit Tunnels and dipped his spectral banner in salute as they left.

**Dazco Pickshine**
A Miners Guild representative who initially pushed for immediate strip mining to fund city repairs. Agreed to a sustainable mining compromise during the council negotiations.

**Entemoch**
Referenced but not present. Mentioned as the Prince of Good Elementals, noted as feeling favorably toward the party following their aid to the Earth Elemental.

## Faction Movements

**Gold Whisker Wererats (led by Chipgrin)**
Maintained a psychic suppression vigil in the Hall of Melting Mirrors. Secured a significant political gain at the council meeting: the party proposed, and the council agreed, that the Gold Whisker wererats take control of the upper boroughs as a self-funded defense force. This removes the financial burden of military protection from Diggermattock and gives the wererats formal territorial authority. Chipgrin cemented the alliance with the Ember Vanguard via the Voucher of the Varmint.

**Diggermattock Miners Guild**
Reinforced structural repairs at the support beams with party assistance. Participated in council negotiations; initially pushed for aggressive mithral extraction. Reached compromise on sustainable mining practices respecting ghost burial grounds. Chief Dorbo announced plans to reopen Blingdenstone for trade with the surface world, signaling a shift from crisis mode to economic recovery.

**Blingdenstone Ghosts / Burrow Wardens (led by Jadger)**
Represented at the council meeting. Their objection to strip-mining the sanctum — which they consider sacred burial ground — was addressed through the sustainable mining compromise. Dipped spectral banners in salute at the party's departure, indicating good standing with the Ember Vanguard.

**Uncontrolled Ooze Horde (formerly under Pudding King)**
Following the Pudding King's defeat, the oozes lost their binding will and became a chaotic threat, breaching the wall at the Trader's Grotto and threatening the Gnomish defenses. Entirely neutralized by Jimjar's bone die acting through Glabbagool: the horde went mindless and peaceful, dispersing into cracks and stonework. Gelatinous cubes consumed other oozes; black puddings slipped through fissures; gray oozes went still. Threat resolved without combat.

**Zuggtmoy (Demon Lord — absent but advancing)**
Not present, but her influence was observed twice: accelerated fungal growth in the throne room area following Juiblex's expulsion, and a pale white puffball mushroom growing at impossible speed near the Northern Exit Tunnels, noted by Jimjar as smelling "sweetly of rot." Jimjar explicitly identified this as Zuggtmoy moving in covertly now that Juiblex's loud presence has been removed.

## Revealed Information

- **Jimjar is not a gnome (or not merely one).** Zalthir's discernment ability revealed his full resistance and immunity profile: immune to poison and psychic damage, cannot be charmed or frightened, resistant to radiant damage and non-magical physical attacks. This profile matches a celestial or divine being — possibly an angel or deity in disguise. Jimjar confirmed awareness of the scrutiny with a wink before vanishing entirely.
- **The Bone Die's origin:** Carved from the knucklebone of a saint who never told a lie; won by Jimjar from a blind Duergar in Gracklestugh. Jimjar theorized that in a place where the blink had corrupted the world's math, the die could introduce corrective truth. It has been spent and is now inert.
- **Zuggtmoy's strategy:** Per Jimjar, she does not attack openly like Juiblex did. She moves in quietly — through fungal growth, through rot, while attention is elsewhere. The expulsion of Juiblex has created an opening she is actively exploiting.
- **The Voucher of the Varmint** is recognized by wererats and outcasts across the Underdark, from Blingdenstone to Menzoberranzan — suggesting Chipgrin's network extends far beyond Blingdenstone.
- **Entemoch (Prince of Good Elementals)** is noted as holding favorable opinion of the party following their healing of the Earth Elemental, potentially a future resource or ally.
