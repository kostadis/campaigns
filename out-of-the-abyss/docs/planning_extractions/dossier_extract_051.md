## Asha Vandry

Asha Vandry is a drow priestess encountered in the fungal cavern, one of only three individuals not yet enslaved by Ilvara's spores. She remains a devout follower of Lolth and has rejected what she calls "the bride heresy" — the belief that Zuggtmoy's influence represents a further manifestation of Lolth. Her faith and paranoia have kept her free of fungal infection.

When Daz attempted to deceive her by staging a divine visitation from Lolth (using Dancing Lights and a spider familiar), Asha did not fall for it outright. Instead, she reframed the deception as a divine test of her own discernment, believing she had cleverly identified Daz as a charlatan — thereby passing Lolth's test. She then questioned whether Daz and his companions had been infected by Zuggtmoy's spores, wondering if their behavior was a symptom of fungal madness or another trick of Ilvara's.

Asha demonstrated deep, bitter hatred of Ilvara, demanding her death in emphatic terms: "We need to kill her. We need to butcher her! We need to send her to an unearthly grave!" She provided critical tactical intelligence: the Heart Fungus itself does not cause the specific spore illness — that originates only from Ilvara's spores and her mushroom artifact. She qualified this with "I believe," undermining her own authority. She also warned that Thorin and Grygum specifically should not approach the artifact due to a marker they picked up in the Whorlstone Caverns of Gracklstugh.

She advised the party to target the Heart Fungus with their most powerful spell rather than Ilvara directly, dismissing Grygum's suggestion to use Phantasmal Killer on Ilvara with open contempt: "I thought you had strong allies, who had a tactical awareness and understanding how to use their magic for destructive purposes." She consistently directed all conversation toward Daz, treating Zalthir and Grygum with disdain or outright ignoring them. She refused physical contact ("Don't touch me"), revealing a deep personal fear of contamination.

Asha revealed that only three people remained free of Ilvara's control: herself, Jorlan, and implicitly one other. She complained at length about being stuck mediating between Jorlan and Ilvara's unresolved romantic drama, showing unexpected warmth and exasperation: "You would think that in the midst of the abyss with the demons, everyone could get over their relationship issues, but no, no, no, no, no."

She was briefly alarmed when Glabbagool's eyes became visible, but was mollified — and even impressed — when Thorin introduced the ooze as Daz's familiar. She explained that the spore servants were former drow party members who had embraced Ilvara's spores willingly.

The negotiation ended in a tentative alliance: the party would kill Ilvara and destroy the mushroom artifact in exchange for everything Ilvara had accumulated. Asha's last known position was in the fungal cavern, waiting for the party to carry out the assault she could not perform herself.

## Ilvara

Ilvara is a former high priestess of Lolth who has been corrupted by Zuggtmoy's fungal influence. According to Asha Vandry, she has become "seduced by this abomination" — the belief that Lolth wishes to be married (the "bride heresy") — and her allies have been turned into mindless fungal creatures under her control. Asha described "something foul and stinking about her."

When observed from the corridor near the pools, Ilvara was on an elevated platform, visibly corrupted by fungal growth. She still carried her scourge but was otherwise physically altered. She was preoccupied with a mushroom token/artifact and unaware she was being watched. She controlled the cavern through the Heart Fungus and her personal spores, which were the specific vector for the spore illness that enslaved others.

During the battle, Zalthir teleported to her and struck her twice for thirty-one damage. In response, she melted into the floor — dissolving into the fungus itself — and reformed elsewhere in the cavern, screaming, breaking the grapple effortlessly. This demonstrated an ability to merge with the fungal environment. Daz then hit her with a precisely shaped Fireball for twenty-four damage (she partially saved), which also struck her mushroom icon, causing it to smolder.

Her current status at the end of the chapter is wounded, bleeding, with her Heart Fungus being ground down by the Dust of Suleiman and her mushroom icon burning, but still active in the fight.

## Jorlan

Jorlan is a drow who formerly had a romantic relationship with Ilvara. She broke up with him, leaving him deeply bitter. According to Asha Vandry, he is one of only three individuals not enslaved by Ilvara's spores — not because of faith, but "because apparently he really hates Ilvara for something or other." Asha complained extensively about being stuck between them: "They used to have an affair, it used to be this whole thing, and then she broke up with him, and then he was bitter because she broke up with him, and now I've had to be in the middle of these two for the last few months."

The party chose to ally with Asha over Jorlan, and set the Glyph of Warding on the bridge to trigger against anyone approaching from Jorlan's side. During the battle, Thorin taunted him — "Come get it, idiot!" — exploiting his rage to lure him onto the bridge. Jorlan charged directly onto the glyph, triggering a detonation that dealt twenty-one damage, destroyed one spore servant, nearly destroyed another, and damaged the Heart Fungus. Thorin then struck him with Dawnbringer for fourteen damage with the Sap maneuver, imposing disadvantage. Thorin's second swing missed (rolling a 2 against Jorlan's AC of 18). At the end of the chapter, Jorlan was staggered but still standing on the bridge, wounded and furious.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool, the gelatinous cube, was instructed by Daz to remain motionless and silent — disguised as a rock — during the deception of Asha Vandry. He failed at this almost immediately, beginning to narrate his own invisibility and opening his eyes to track the conversation. Daz had to verbally quiet him mid-negotiation. Later, Asha noticed his blinking eyes emerging from the ooze, but Thorin defused the situation by introducing Glabbagool as Daz's familiar. Asha was actually impressed by this, viewing it as evidence of Daz's magical prowess — "An ooze familiar." Grygum noted that Glabbagool deserved something for the indignity of being called a familiar, though the ooze didn't seem to mind.
