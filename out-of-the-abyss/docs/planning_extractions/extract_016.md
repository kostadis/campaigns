## Threat Arc Events

**Brundar's Echo / Underdark Madness Arc**
- Stonespeaker Hgraam, a stone giant, was found in a state of violent madness—behavior described as completely anomalous for stone giants, who are normally taciturn and still
- Duergar patrol confirmed a statistically significant increase in incidents of irrational, self-destructive behavior across the population
- The Deepking himself is exhibiting detachment from reality and increasingly arbitrary lawmaking, per the Halfling's account
- Merchants at the Blade Bazaar were observed engaging in economically irrational and rule-breaking behavior
- Multiple independent data points (giant madness, merchant behavior, Deepking's deterioration) suggest a spreading madness effect centered on or emanating through Gracklstugh

## Revealed Information

- The Keepers of the Flame maintain Themberchaud through a deliberate cycle of pampering, dependency, and eventual assassination when the dragon grows too powerful or resentful—the dragon is never truly in control
- Errde Blackskull's investigation into corruption is personal and politically motivated: success will earn her favor with the Deepking, not simply serve civic order
- The Stone Guards cannot enter the West Cleft District without triggering violence, revealing a hard limit on official authority in that part of the city
- Errde has knowledge of the party's status as escaped slaves and the Drow who may be pursuing them; she is prepared to use this as a bargaining chip
- The Halfling characterized all major factions in Gracklstugh as actively hostile to one another, with the conflict rooted in the Deepking's madness destabilizing the city's previously rule-bound order
