## Dorbo Diggermattock

Chief Dorbo Diggermattock stood before the assembled forces and declared that today would be the day of victory against the Pudding King. He pointed to Daz, proclaiming the dragonborn would lead them to triumph. He unveiled a battle plan: the party would break through a wall cleared by the Galeb Duhr and infiltrate the Pudding King's court from the south, while his forces attacked from another direction as a diversion. He offered three groups of allies—an elemental imbued with Entemoch's power, wererats led by Chipgrin, and the Pickshine Miners. His motivations were revealed to be profit-driven and focused on protecting the mines, in contrast to his wife Senni's people-first concerns. He accepted the party's proposal for a minimal distraction rather than a full-scale assault.

## Senni Diggermattock

Senni Diggermattock, her face etched with concern, explained her fears for the civilian areas of Blingdenstone. She worried that without a proper diversion, the party's failure might go unnoticed until the town was already overrun. Her priorities were shown to be deeply people-focused—she cared about the community they had built, standing in contrast to Chief Dorbo's profit-driven motivations. She was pleased when the party proposed a minimal distraction plan rather than a full assault.

## Dasco Pickshine

Dasco Pickshine, the owner of Pickshine Mines (which he branded as "Dasco Pickshine Mines"), seized every opportunity to promote his brand. He offered "branded tea" and explained his holistic approach to marketing the mining operation. When the party showed interest in branded items, he personally sewed a Dasco Pickshine patch onto Daz's clothes, standing proudly beside the much taller dragonborn. Every piece of his clothing and equipment bore his company's logo. He offered a practical solution to the twenty-foot black pudding pit: his miners could bridge it using mithril braces and salt neutralizers, though they would need to stay behind to maintain the bridge against acid and couldn't back up the party at the Pudding King's doors. The Pickshine Miners set to work and completed the bridge in about twenty minutes while singing cheerful songs.

## Chipgrin

Chipgrin led the wererat scouts and took offense at any suggestion his pack might be noisy, emphasizing their stealth capabilities. He agreed to scout ahead while other allies waited in reserve at area twenty-two. When his second scout didn't return after ten minutes, Chipgrin personally went to investigate and found the scout caught and terrified by psychic effects from gelatinous slime walls and a "brain slime" in the ceiling. He reported the threats in detail and offered two options: his pack could howl at a frequency to drown out the psychic song, allowing the party to walk through safely, but this would draw all oozes to attack the wererats, leaving the party without scouts or backup. The party chose this loud path, sacrificing Chipgrin's continued assistance.

## Jimjar

Jimjar made a bet that Thorin couldn't clear the black pudding gap with his pack on, though Grygum realized Jimjar hadn't specified *how* Thorin should cross and the dwarf could simply walk across the completed bridge. Later, Jimjar joked that Daz's reflection in the gelatinous slime mirrors was better looking than the real Daz.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool, the intelligent ooze, suggested the party could get inside him to pass through the ochre jelly barricade safely, though this would cause acid burns and oxygen deprivation. When asked whether the Pudding King could control him, he promised he would never hurt his friends. However, he recalled an earlier incident where he had heard a voice calling him from a large pool of slimes, suggesting the Pudding King might have some influence over him.

## Dawnbringer

Dawnbringer, Thorin's sentient sword, argued against the dwarf jumping across the black pudding pit, not wanting to get stuck in acid. After a brief dialogue, the blade convinced Thorin that walking across the bridge would be the wiser choice.

## The Pudding King

The Pudding King was observed through the ochre jelly barricade monologuing to his two ooze henchmen, Princess Ebonheir and Prince Livid. He gloated about the impending defeat of Blingdenstone, periodically cheering as he described members of the Blingdenstone army being consumed by oozes. He ranted that Zuggtmoy, the Queen of Rot, thought she could take his kingdom, but Juiblex had promised it to him. He had made an alliance with Juiblex and was trying to convert Blingdenstone's biomass into ooze biomass by having his creatures consume everyone. Green slime patches on the throne room ceiling served as additional defenses. When Zalthir reached him and grappled him, the Pudding King transformed into an ooze form—a shifting, amorphous mass—though Zalthir maintained his grip and dragged him fifteen feet south, isolating him from his henchmen.

## Princess Ebonheir

Princess Ebonheir was one of the Pudding King's two ooze henchmen, present in the throne room as he monologued about victory. She was separated from the Pudding King when Zalthir grappled the villain and dragged him fifteen feet south.

## Prince Livid

Prince Livid was one of the Pudding King's two ooze henchmen, present in the throne room during his monologue. He was separated from the Pudding King when Zalthir grappled and dragged the villain away.
