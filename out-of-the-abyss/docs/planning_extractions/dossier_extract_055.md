## Eldeth

Eldeth does not appear as a viewpoint character but is present at the opening of the session, at the tunnel mouth where the party emerges onto the surface. She finds Thorin shortly after the group adjusts to sunlight and delivers her farewell. She tells him that their journey together ends here, that she must travel to Mithril Hall to inform the dwarves of what is happening in the Underdark, and that she hopes to see him again — and if not, that it was well met and she thanks him for getting her out. When Thorin asks whether he might petition to join her clan, she does not dismiss the idea. She recounts their shared history — fighting together, bleeding together, facing abyssal creatures together — and makes a sworn promise: after she has spoken to the lords of Gauntlgrym and Mithril Hall about the dangers of the Underdark, she is certain they will want to speak to Thorin, and she states it as a matter of purpose that he shall be embraced by her clan. She says "I swear." She gives a practical explanation for why Thorin cannot accompany her directly — clan business, and the story needing to be told before the characters tell it — and implicitly notes that a drow in a hood and a giant-raised dwarf walking into Mithril Hall would complicate the briefing. She extends her hand to Thorin and holds it briefly before departing. Her last direction to the party is to head west to Candlekeep. She then leaves, heading toward Mithril Hall. Last known status: traveling west toward Mithril Hall to brief the lords of Gauntlgrym and Mithril Hall on the Underdark threat.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool is present at the tunnel mouth and experiences sunlight for the first time. He looks up at the sun with a stillness that is distinguished from his usual floor-like passivity — he is witnessed as genuinely witnessing. Zalthir watches him during this moment. Glabbagool is the subject of a discussion about potential monk training Zalthir has apparently offered in some sense, though "offered" is contested — Zalthir's expression indicates the word is doing significant work. Glabbagool has strong feelings about a curriculum involving "run, run, slap, slap, slap." Thorin notes Glabbagool's tactical potential: no fixed appendages, unpredictable, potentially twelve simultaneous strikes per round. During the bird-call signal sequence, Glabbagool attempts a bird impression that is completely unrecognizable — described as the sound a bag of wet sand makes when dropped from height while being squeezed — and is very proud of it. He asks whether birds are a kind of ooze. Grygum tells him they are feathered and promises to introduce him to birds. After the drow spy is captured and miniaturized, Glabbagool is assigned to watch her in the bag of holding. He performs this duty with absolute singleness of purpose — one eye locked on the prisoner without breaking stride, described as the most earnest thing Zalthir has seen in weeks. During the interrogation, Glabbagool demonstrates his actual seeing capacity: he explains that his googly eyes are affectations he adopted because he discovered that cute eyes make people treat him more friendly, and that he can see in different ways as ooze do. To demonstrate authority over the spy, he begins proliferating eyes, which rapidly becomes very creepy. At Candlekeep, he is assigned to keep the shrunken spy under control as a goal. Thorin proposes that Glabbagool receive his own question to ask the monks, given he donated a day's worth of bag-of-holding service. Glabbagool is reportedly deeply confused about why a library with effectively infinite capacity imposes any limit on questions at all — his position is that every book ever seen should be donated and questions asked until the end of time. Zalthir notes that Glabbagool has been carried in a bag for much of his above-ground existence with no way to establish what is normal above ground. Last known status: inside or near the bag of holding at Candlekeep, watching the shrunken spy.

## Dawnbringer

Dawnbringer is present throughout the session, carried by Thorin. At the tunnel mouth, she says nothing when the group sees the sun for the first time, though she is noted to have been waiting longer than any of them. The hilt warms slightly when Thorin says they have each other. She is described as having an observable mood — radiating warmth like a hearth, not directed at anything in particular but present, increasing over the days since reaching the surface. On the evening of the second day, she watches the light die on the horizon. When Thorin expresses frustration about Tremor Sense not working on dirt, Dawnbringer tells him he has anger issues. Thorin responds with something about driving issues. During the interrogation of the drow spy, Dawnbringer's light is brought close as a form of pressure — sunlight proximity used as motivation for a drow captive. Thorin notes he wants Candlekeep to help with Dawnbringer and describes it as needing a therapist rather than a one-question situation. He is directed to an avowed named Philemon, described as specializing in psychological preservation and historical cataloging of ancient sentient artifacts, with a track record of resolving interpersonal conflict issues between sentient artifacts and their bearers. Thorin receives this with enthusiasm and describes it as couples counseling with Dawnbringer. Last known status: with Thorin at Candlekeep, scheduled for consultation with Philemon.

## Spiderbait

Spiderbait is present at the tunnel mouth and announces that he has not been on the surface in his whole life. He immediately declares he is going to explore, expressing optimism that goblins are totally integrated into surface society and no one will attack him for being a goblin. Thorin tells him this is not the case. Daz agrees carefully. Grygum is specific: the state of goblin equality and inclusion is rough at the moment. Spiderbait recalibrates, pivots, and asks whether he can simply hang out with the group — noting they are four big, powerful people and he is a small goblin. He suggests Candlekeep seems like a place where someone like him could find a job doing something innocuous. Grygum endorses the plan. Last known status: traveling with the party toward Candlekeep.

## The Matron Mother of House T'sarran

Does not appear in person in this chunk. She is discussed at length during the interrogation of the drow spy. The spy identifies herself as of House T'sarran and states her matron has ruled House T'sarran for two hundred years. The matron sent multiple agents to the surface — described as "a couple of us" sent to the overbright — to find where the party was surfacing. Her objective is not the party itself but to identify the person who paid to have Daz protected. She is treating Daz as bait: the plan is to observe what happens to him once he reaches the surface, so that whoever paid for his protection will reveal themselves, and that person can then be identified and reported. The spy states that the matron mother cares about finding this person, and therefore the spy cares. No additional motivation is given; the matron's interest is offered as complete and sufficient explanation. The spy warns the party that killing her will not stop House T'sarran from sending more agents. Last known status: in Menzoberranzan or House T'sarran's base of operations, waiting for intelligence from surface agents.

## Asha Vandree

Does not appear in person in this chunk. She is referenced in connection with a theological document she pressed into Grygum's hands — described as a theological argument about Lolth and Zuggtmoy as equivalents. The document is now in the party's possession and is among the five books donated to Candlekeep.
