## Dorhun

Dorhun is a stone giant and former apprentice to Stonespeaker Hgraam. He appeared flanked by Duergar armed with stone weapons after the party's battle with the crazed two-headed stone giant just outside the Blade Bazaar. He stared at Thorin, whose body and blade showed evidence of the fight. When Thorin explained that he had tried to subdue rather than kill the giant, and that it was the Duergar who delivered the killing blow, Dorhun confirmed the story and sighed. He introduced himself along with a companion named Rihaud, identifying them both as apprentices to Stonespeaker Hgraam. He asked Thorin to visit him at Cairngorm Cavern before leaving the city and instructed the Stone Guards to let the party through. His demeanor was somber and consistent with the taciturn nature of stone giants.

## Rihaud

Rihaud is a stone giant and former apprentice to Stonespeaker Hgraam. He was introduced by Dorhun when the two appeared flanked by Duergar Stone Guards after the battle with the crazed two-headed giant. No further details about his words or actions were provided beyond his presence alongside Dorhun.

## Stonespeaker Hgraam

Stonespeaker Hgraam was mentioned by Dorhun as the master to whom both Dorhun and Rihaud were apprenticed. He did not appear in person. His name came up when the stone giants introduced themselves to Thorin near the Blade Bazaar.

## Gartokkar Xundorn

Gartokkar Xundorn is a Duergar who materialized behind Zalthir after the battle with the crazed stone giant. He has notable scars on his ears—four notches that don't look self-inflicted. He identified himself as the Custodian of the Flame and chief of the Keepers of the Flame, the faction responsible for tending to the red dragon Themberchaud, who heats the stones that keep Gracklstugh's furnaces running. He flashed a badge that caused other approaching Duergar to quickly move away, demonstrating his authority. He described the Keepers as the most prized of the Deepking's advisors. He offered the party safe passage through the city and protection from imprisonment in exchange for coming to the Keepers of the Flame headquarters and performing a service, with the conditions that they do nothing the Deepking would disapprove of and not require too much effort on his part. He gave an ultimatum: the offer expires the next day, and if they don't accept, he cannot promise a friendly next encounter. He laughed at Grygum's joke about kindling, noting he hadn't heard a good joke in a long time. He then disappeared.

## Themberchaud

Themberchaud is a red dragon used by the city of Gracklstugh to heat stones for the city's furnaces. He was discussed by Gartokkar Xundorn and later by the halfling outside the Darklake Brewery. According to the halfling, the Keepers of the Flame provide Themberchaud with a hoard and pamper him, but the inherent nature of red dragons means he will eventually realize he is not truly in charge. When that happens—or when the dragon grows too powerful—the Keepers must kill and replace him with a new dragon hatched from an egg. Daz found the arrangement intriguing, questioning why a red dragon would serve as a "lighter" given their nature. Themberchaud did not appear in person.

## Errde Blackskull

Errde Blackskull is the captain of the Stone Guards on the lake in Gracklstugh. She approached the party at the Darklake Brewery with a group of Duergar bearing stone weapons and invited them to her office at Overlake Hold for a conversation. When Daz challenged whether she was threatening them, she was taken aback but smiled and assured them it was friendly. At the Overlake Hold, she served pastries and warm drink before explaining her request: she has been tracking a Duergar named Droki who lives in the West Cleft District among the Derro. She cannot send the Stone Guards there in force without provoking a violent reaction, so she needs outsiders to find him. She believes Droki is the key to uncovering corruption linked to the Council of Savants and possibly elements of the Clan Lairds. She stated that something is deeply wrong in the Deepking's court and that exposing this conspiracy would earn her great favor with the Deepking. She validated the party's observations of rule-breaking at the Blade Bazaar as proof of corruption. She offered 175 gold pieces of equipment from the armory upfront, with 175 more upon delivery of Droki and further proof. She also made a veiled but powerful threat-as-incentive: she can tell any Drow searching for escaped slaves that those slaves are not in the city, making her the one faction that can guarantee the party's freedom.

## Droki

Droki is a Duergar who lives in the West Cleft District among the Derro. He is being tracked by Errde Blackskull and the Stone Guards but cannot be pursued directly due to the risk of provoking violence in the Derro district. According to Errde, he is linked to corruption involving the Council of Savants and possibly the Clan Lairds. He did not appear in person; he was described solely through Errde's briefing to the party.
