## NPC Activity

**Nym Duskryn**
Appeared at the Drow outpost with her sister Kaelira. Revealed she and Kaelira had been hired via Menzoberranzan contract to protect Daz specifically from House Mizzrym and House T'sarran interference. Declined to reveal the identity of their employer. Dismissed Asha as a low-level threat not worth concern. Criticized Thorin's grapple tactic when subduing Asha, advising decapitation instead. Delivered professional assessment that the party's survival to this point was improbable. Quoted the contract scope as concluded once the named threats were neutralized. Refused Thorin's attempt to retain them, stating the fee was beyond the party's means. Expressed genuine puzzlement that an anonymous party had paid substantially to protect a male Drow with no house affiliation. Departed upon contract completion.

**Kaelira Duskryn**
Appeared alongside Nym. Gestured to the T'sarran extraction contract and confirmed the two named threats (House Mizzrym, House T'sarran) constituted the full scope of their assignment. Applauded Zalthir's killing of Asha with slow, ironic clapping. Read the partially burned T'sarran documents and assessed Daz's unknown background with professional skepticism, noting that the expenditure required to contract their services was substantial. Dismissed Daz's self-mythologizing with the observation that "every orphan thinks" there is something noble in their background. Stated the contracting party was unknown to them. Departed with Nym upon contract fulfillment.

**Asha Vandree**
Survived the battle and immediately declared herself in charge of the group. Grygum's divine bolt missed her; she interpreted this as Lolth's blessing. Was grappled by Thorin before she could invoke further abilities. Killed by Zalthir using the Eldritch Claw Tattoo before she could speak or pray. Body was looted: Lolth holy symbol, chain mail, prayer beads, sealed wax pouch (theological manuscript), and a fine Drow spider-silk cloak (taken by Zalthir).

**Ilvara Mizzrym** *(deceased, remains examined)*
Body looted at the outpost. Confirmed deceased. Items recovered: prayer book, Living Serpent Head Scourge, and a chest of confiscated prisoner belongings (including Thorin's tooth). Her prayer book contained Zuggtmoy marginalia in a second or altered hand, documenting a corruption timeline showing her gradual subversion. She had been carrying Thorin's tooth as a trophy. Sealed orders from House Mizzrym found on her correspondence, escalating in urgency and demanding a response to her disappearance. A letter from Neverlight Grove indicated a missed meeting. A ritual bundle (incense cones, silver bowl, cloth stitched with both Lolth's and Zuggtmoy's sigils) was found beneath her platform. A necromantic pearl of unnatural density and finish was recovered from the core of the fungal mass at the center of her ritual chamber.

**The T'sarran Mage** *(deceased, remains examined)*
Body looted. Items recovered: House T'sarran signet ring, obsidian wand with captive spider in amber tip, and partially burned documents. The documents, though damaged, confirmed a contract to extract Daz alive and unharmed on behalf of House T'sarran.

**Dawnbringer**
Refused to participate in a purification ritual, asserting it was a weapon, not a religious implement. Was threatened with the Vaultmaster Locking Scabbard by Thorin, after which it relented and agreed to cooperate.

**Glabbagool**
Peered out of the bag of holding upon the party's emergence onto the surface. Observed sunlight and the surface world for the first time, responding with apparent curiosity and wide attention rather than retreat.

**Jimjar** *(absent, referenced)*
Named as a subject of planned research. The party intends to investigate his disappearance as part of their Candlekeep research agenda.

## Revealed Information

- An anonymous party in Menzoberranzan contracted House Duskryn at significant expense to protect Daz from two named threats (House Mizzrym, House T'sarran). The contracting party took deliberate steps to remain unknown even to the hired agents.
- House T'sarran had an active contract to extract Daz alive and unharmed, confirmed by partially burned documents and a signet ring recovered from the dead mage.
- Ilvara's corruption by Zuggtmoy was gradual and documented; it began from within her existing Lolth practice and worked along theological fault lines. The marginalia in her prayer book record the progression.
- Asha Vandree had composed or received a theological manuscript arguing that Lolth and Zuggtmoy are the same entity, suggesting she was either a convert or a theorist for Zuggtmoy's infiltration of Lolth worship.
- House Mizzrym had been sending escalating unanswered correspondence to Ilvara, meaning the house did not know she had been subverted and is likely operating without accurate intelligence about this outpost.
- A letter from Neverlight Grove in Ilvara's possession indicated a missed political meeting, suggesting Zuggtmoy's forces at Neverlight Grove had been coordinating with Ilvara prior to her death.
- The necromantic pearl recovered from the fungal mass core is unnaturally finished, suggesting it was either placed deliberately or formed through sustained and directed corrupting influence.
