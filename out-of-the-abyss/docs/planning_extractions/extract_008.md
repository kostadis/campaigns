## NPC Activity

**Yuk-Yuk (Goblin)**
- Found murdered during the night; throat cut with a blade (likely a shortsword). Corpse discovered by Spiderbait at the start of the 1st day of the 3rd Tenday. Death occurred while the group slept.

**Spiderbait (Goblin)**
- Discovered the corpse of his companion Yuk-Yuk; was screaming over the body when the group woke.

**Jimjar (Goblin)**
- Convinced Yuk-Yuk and Spiderbait to join the group for 20 gold.
- Made a bet with Grygum over whether Thorin would successfully cross the gorge (lost).
- Excited by the discovery of a tinderbox for cooking Ripplebark.
- Believed monsters attracted to the bandit corpses would impede pursuers rather than leave a trail.
- Engaged in a late-night betting discussion with Grygum about what monsters might be in the lost tomb.

**Eldeth (Shield Dwarf)**
- Primary forager for the group; located Ripplebark and Waterorbs.
- Identified and warned the group about a field of Timmasks, preventing mass poisoning.
- Discovered a corpse in the Timmask field, identified as the bandit who escaped the gorge ambush.
- Expressed discomfort with Thorin's association with giants due to the devastation caused by the War of the Giants in the North.
- Co-identified (alongside Grygum and Zalthir) that the bandit leader's story about being lost was fabricated.

**Suushar the Awakened (Kuo-Toa)**
- Observed Thorin's episode of madness and suggested the archpriest Ploopploopeen in Sloopbludop could help.
- Described Ploopploopeen as a follower of the Sea Mother who would welcome his awakening.
- Argued that trackers cannot meaningfully read the "circle of life" disturbance left by corpses.
- Stated he has no interest in traveling past Sloopbludop but may assist with Darklake travel.

**Buppido (Derro)**
- Emphatically argued that leaving bandit corpses would create a traceable trail; persuaded Zalthir to dispose of the bodies in the gorge.
- After examining the dead bandit in the Timmask field, correctly deduced "he had friends," anticipating the second bandit group's arrival.
- Advocated for traveling to Gracklstugh first to purchase equipment before attempting Blingdenstone.

**Stool (Young Myconid)**
- Identified Nilhogg's Nose mushrooms and explained their properties (enhanced smell, with significant downside to foul odors).

**Sethir**
- Notably affected by the Faerzress magic in the tunnels.
- Advocated for visiting a lost temple to find treasure.
- Observed that traveling along the Darklake is less treacherous than tunnel travel.

**Topsy and Turvy (Deep Gnomes)**
- Found the name "Blingdenstone" off-putting.
- Believed the gorge-side monster disturbance would impede pursuers rather than leave a trail.

**Bandit Leader (Unnamed)**
- Arrived with surviving bandits under a flag of truce the evening of the 10th day.
- Offered information about escaping the Underdark in exchange for food; the deal was accepted.
- Claimed the group had taken too many wrong turns exploring a dungeon — identified as a fabrication by Grygum, Eldeth, and Zalthir.
- Provided the intel that Blingdenstone offers the best chance of escape to the surface.

**Bandit Group (Unnamed, Multiple)**
- Previously ambushed Thorin on the far side of the gorge; defeated in combat, corpses thrown into the gorge.
- One survivor fled but died in the Timmask field (poisoning/confusion).
- Remaining survivors tracked the party and made contact under a flag of truce the following evening.
- Described as desperate, wearing mixed armor and clothing, armed with scimitars and crossbows — suggesting extended time in the Underdark.

**Ploopploopeen (Referenced, Absent — Kuo-Toa Archpriest)**
- Named by Suushar as the archpriest of the Sea Mother in Sloopbludop.
- Described as potentially able to help with madness afflicting the party.
- Suushar believes Ploopploopeen will welcome his awakening as relevant to ending his people's madness.

## Revealed Information

- **Blingdenstone** identified by the bandit leader as the best route to escape the Underdark to the surface.
- **Timmask mushrooms**: heavily damaging or uprooting them releases a 15-ft spore cloud causing poisoning and confusion-like effects — confirmed lethal to at least one bandit.
- **Nilhogg's Nose**: consumable mushroom that dramatically enhances smell but amplifies painful reactions to foul odors.
- **Waterorbs**: Underdark fungus usable as portable water storage, yielding approximately one gallon of potable water each.
- **Corpse disposal debate outcome**: Buppido's argument that corpses attract monsters and create a traceable disturbance in the ecosystem was validated — the surviving bandit was tracked via the Timmask field corpse.
- **Yuk-Yuk's murder**: killed with a shortsword during the night while the group slept; weapon type means any member of the party or NPC group could be responsible.
