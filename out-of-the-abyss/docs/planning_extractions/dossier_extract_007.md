## Zalthir

Zalthir cut one of the web strands in the Silken Paths, initiating the encounter with the Giant Spiders. He participated in the fighting retreat, using his sword to slow the spiders. He confronted Fargas Rumblefoot about the treasure the halfling had previously mentioned, pressing him to keep his bargain. He questioned Fargas's inconsistency about knowing the tomb's location previously but not now, and assessed that the halfling's claims were made out of desperation or bravado, though he was uncertain if Fargas was currently lying. He touched Fargas's belly and announced "No gnoll spawn." He asked Sethir to explain how he knew the tomb's location. He asked hopefully whether the Drow pursuers would be slowed by the Giant Spider encounter. During the Spectator encounter, he attempted to use the myconid mind network to find someone who could convince the creature of benign intent. He performed an acrobatic move, going underneath the spider web strand to try to strike the Spectator from below, but missed. He was then hit by the Spectator's green paralysis ray and was paralyzed. His acrobatic move deeply impressed Yukyuk and Spiderbait, which kept the two goblin guides from abandoning the group. He privately considered convincing the guides to retrieve gold from the mimic. Buppido expressed interest in Zalthir being "part of my divine plan." Zalthir briefly wondered if the Spectator had identified Buppido as the demon but pushed the thought aside.

## Fargas Rumblefoot

Fargas Rumblefoot, the halfling adventurer, was pressed by Zalthir to reveal the treasure he had previously mentioned. He was tempted to deny his earlier words but was dissuaded by Zalthir's expression—and by Grygum producing notes of what Fargas had said. He revealed the existence of the floating tomb of Brysis of Khaem, a half-elf sorcerer interred during the height of Faerûn's great empires of magic. He explained that when Netheril fell, the tomb plummeted into the Underdark. Critically, he shared that he knows about a false tomb: most treasure hunters find a fake sarcophagus and burial chamber, but the real treasure is hidden in another chamber accessible via a secret passageway from the servants' tomb. He admitted he doesn't know the precise location but said it's on the eastern edge of the Darklake. Grygum assessed that Fargas was not lying. He was very miffed when Zalthir touched his belly to check for gnoll spawn.

## Sethir (Serith)

Sethir (also referred to as Serith in the text) confirmed he could lead the group to the tomb of Brysis of Khaem after speaking with Fargas about its general location on the eastern edge of the Darklake. He drew a map using Ormu that Zalthir had collected, showing the group's route from Velkynvelve through the Silken Paths toward Sloobludop, with the Darklake, Gracklstugh, and the tomb's approximate location. He explained that the Darklake is not a conventional body of water but rather a "large water balloon" of intersecting caverns, caves, and tunnels. He agreed with Buppido's suggestion to go to Gracklstugh after Sloobludop, noting it as a large Derro city where they could get weapons and gear, and that the tomb is on the way. He estimated they were three days from Sloobludop. He was glared at Thorin when the dwarf criticized his map's accuracy.

## Yukyuk

Yukyuk, one of the two goblin guides, offered critique after the spider encounter, suggesting the group should have slid under the spiders and cut the web from the other side. He was very impressed by Zalthir's acrobatic move of going underneath the spider web strand to attack the Spectator, calling it "next-level spider walking." He asked for payment after the group cleared the Silken Paths. He and Spiderbait were tempted to abandon the escapees when Jimjar couldn't pay immediately, but Zalthir's impressive combat move elevated the group in their imaginations, and they chose to stay.

## Spiderbait

Spiderbait, the other goblin guide, offered critique after the spider fight, suggesting that using magic was "kind of a cheat" and the group should stick to their skills next time. He explained that whether the Drow pursuers would be slowed by the spider encounter depended on the quality of their guides, noting there are hundreds of routes through the Silken Paths and that he and Yukyuk know the best way. Like Yukyuk, he was tempted to leave when payment wasn't forthcoming but stayed due to being impressed by Zalthir.

## Grygum

Grygum pulled out notes he had taken of Fargas's earlier statements and repeated them back, pressuring Fargas to honor his bargain. He asked if the tomb was full of treasure. He assessed Fargas's truthfulness and declared "Fargas wasn't lying and he isn't lying." He bet Jimjar that the deep gnome couldn't convince the Spectator they weren't demons. During the Spectator fight, he muttered about needing better ranged spells and fired his handheld crossbow at the creature.

## Jimjar

Jimjar took Grygum's bet that he couldn't convince the Spectator the group wasn't demons—and failed. When Yukyuk asked for payment after the Silken Paths, Jimjar promised the goblins they'd get their money "the minute I get it," keeping them strung along. He wanted to make a bet about whether the chest was a mimic, but no one would take it.

## Buppido

Buppido suggested the group should go to Gracklstugh after Sloobludop. He agreed with the group's assessment that the chest was a mimic. After overhearing Daz suggest using the goblin guides as battle fodder, Buppido turned to Zalthir and said, "What an interesting idea, Zalthir. Perhaps you can be part of my divine plan." Zalthir briefly wondered whether the Spectator had identified Buppido as the demon it warned about.

## Brysis of Khaem

Brysis of Khaem was a half-elf sorcerer from the height of Faerûn's great empires of magic. She was interred in a floating tomb. After the fall of Netheril and its flying cities, her tomb plummeted into a crevasse and ended up in the Underdark, where it has remained for centuries. According to Fargas Rumblefoot, the tomb contains a false burial chamber with a fake sarcophagus that deceives treasure hunters; the real treasure is hidden in a separate chamber accessible through a secret passageway from the servants' tomb. The tomb is located on the eastern edge of the Darklake.

## Daz

Daz used magic missiles during the spider retreat. During the Spectator fight, Daz unleashed magic missiles described as "glowing teardrops of blue-white force," which killed the Spectator. After the Silken Paths, Daz muttered that the goblin guides "might prove useful as fodder in a battle," which Buppido overheard.

## Thorin

Thorin was annoyed at the Giant Spiders for not "playing the game as it was meant to be played" during the web-cutting retreat. He noted Sethir's map didn't reflect relative distances accurately. During the Spectator fight, he was hit by a confusion ray but charged and attacked the creature anyway. He was struck by a blue ray from another eyestalk but was unaffected. He used a technique called a "hill strike" learned from a hill giant battle master, though the Spectator proved too strong to be knocked prone.
