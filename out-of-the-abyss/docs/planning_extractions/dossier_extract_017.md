## Erdde Blackskull

Erdde Blackskull, Captain of the Stone Guards, stands with an aura of paranoia and seems disappointed during her meeting with Grygum. She asks him to investigate the "empty scabbard killers," whose presence her underlings have reported after finding bodies with puzzling wounds. She tries to act nonchalant about it, dismissing the killers as "an old wives' tale to frighten children" who "kill people with their minds," suggesting they don't exist and that the assassinations are likely conducted by some government agency using the legend as cover. When Grygum presses for more information, she is initially unwilling to reveal much, but relents when he insists he can't look for what he doesn't know, bringing in her subordinate Grimholl Forgebrand. She reveals that two of the victims were her own clan members, including her uncle. Despite acknowledging they were likely assassinated, she refuses to believe a children's fable has come to life. She previously provided passes to the party that allow them through the gates restricting non-Duergar access to the city, and she also threatened she could set the Drow on them. She is also looking for someone named Droki, the same person the Keepers of the Flame have identified as a Gray Ghosts agent.

## Grimholl Forgebrand

Grimholl Forgebrand is a subordinate of Erdde Blackskull, brought into the room to brief Grygum on the mysterious killings. He announces himself as "Holl to my friends, and you are not my friends." He explains that the bodies found had no exit wounds or entrance wounds. Zalthir later concludes that Holl may not have been lying after examining the Duergar merchant who was attacked by assassins with glowing weapons and bore no wounds.

## Buppido

Buppido, one of the escapees, continues to be bound and seems resigned to his fate while the group stays at Ghohlbrorn's Lair.

## Jimjar

Jimjar, one of the escapees, has made a few friends at Ghohlbrorn's Lair. He asks when the group expects to be able to leave, pointing out that they came for supplies and appear to have them. When Daz insists on gathering intelligence, Jimjar shrugs, slumps into a bed, sighs, and says "Very well."

## Topsy and Turvy

Topsy and Turvy are notable only by contrast — they are the only other escapees who have not gone missing from Ghohlbrorn's Lair. The others have disappeared, which Daz suspects is because they are wererats.

## Werz Saltbaron

Werz Saltbaron is a Duergar merchant who was attacked by two assassins wielding glowing weapons (possibly psychic weapons) on the piers near Ghohlbrorn's Lair. He was found with no wounds on his body. The assassins carried a charcoal drawing of him, confirming he was specifically targeted. After Grygum heals him, he expresses surprise and gratitude, claiming he has no idea why he was attacked. Upon seeing the drawing, he realizes he was indeed a deliberate target. He invites the party to the Shattered Spire the next day to reward them, then becomes invisible and departs, saying he has places to be.

## Themberchaud

Themberchaud is a red dragon known as the Wyrmsmith of Gracklstugh, bearing the titles "Father of Flame," "the Everburning," and "the Foundry's Heart." He resides in a cavern filled with a hoard of gems and coins. He is described as plump and overweight, giving the impression of excessive eating habits — Zalthir wonders whether the pampered dragon could even survive in the wild, having likely forgotten how to hunt. When the party arrives, he makes a joke about having "surfacers for dinner" and demands to speak with them alone, dismissing Gartokkar (who is visibly displeased). He breathes fire in a circle around the cavern to check for spies, laughing when no one screams. He questions the party members individually, asking Zalthir if he is a gold dragonborn (expressing dislike of gold dragons) and asking Daz if he is from Menzoberranzan. Satisfied with their answers and pleased they have no family that can be blackmailed, he declares them his agents — notably not asking but telling them. His ego is described as so fragile he cannot wait for an answer of yes. He orders Gartokkar to give them gold badges and instructs the party to do what Gartokkar says. Their first mission from Themberchaud is to do whatever the Keepers of the Flame ask, but report to him first. He is unaware that the Keepers of the Flame view him as working for them rather than the other way around, and he does not know about the stolen red dragon egg intended to hatch his replacement. The party ultimately begins hatching a plan to free Themberchaud and ride him out of Gracklstugh — Grygum, as a cleric of Bahamut, expresses outrage at how the dragon is treated.

## Gartokkar Xundorn

Gartokkar Xundorn is a Duergar priest and Custodian of the Flame who serves as the party's primary contact with the Keepers of the Flame. He initially tries to lead the party to a building outside the dragon's cavern but is interrupted by Themberchaud's arrival. He introduces the dragon with great reverence using all his titles. After Themberchaud dismisses him, he is visibly displeased but complies. Later, in his chambers, he acts as though the party willingly accepted the role of agents, saying he is "delighted." He reveals the Keepers' political situation: their power derives equally from psionic abilities, the Deepking's leadership, and their influence over Themberchaud. He explains they lost face when the Gray Ghosts (a thieves' guild) stole a red dragon egg meant to hatch Themberchaud's replacement, describing the theft as having created "a great disturbance in the Underdark" like "a hole torn through the world." He conspiratorially reveals that while Themberchaud thinks the Keepers work for him, in reality he works for them. When asked about what happens when Themberchaud "moves on," he claims the dragon eventually leaves to see the world — which Grygum compares to telling children their pet "left" when it died. Gartokkar identifies Droki as an agent of the Gray Ghosts and tasks the party with finding the egg and proof of the Gray Ghosts' involvement. He gives the party holy symbols of Laduguer and gold pins featuring Themberchaud's profile for travel within the city. When Thorin threatens refusal, Gartokkar threatens to turn them over to the Stone Guards and reads off an extensive, pre-prepared list of eight potential legal charges against Thorin. When Thorin counters by threatening to reveal the egg to the Stone Guards, Gartokkar turns dark before smiling and dismissing it, insisting they just find the egg.

## Droki

Droki is identified by Gartokkar Xundorn as an agent of the Gray Ghosts, the thieves' guild that stole the red dragon egg from the Keepers of the Flame. He is also the same person that Erdde Blackskull, Captain of the Stone Guards, is looking for. He does not appear in person during these events.

## Milo Goodbarrel

Milo Goodbarrel is an author who wrote a book detailing events surrounding "the war of the dragon." Daz has obtained a copy. The book describes psychic blade attacks that leave no trace, and includes a scene where a Kenku named Whistler used psychic blades to fake an attack. Daz also references Milo Goodbarrel's memoirs regarding chess playing a significant role in negotiating with metallic dragons.
