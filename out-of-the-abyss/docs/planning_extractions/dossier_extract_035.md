## Jimjar

Jimjar, the deep gnome guide, defended his navigational choices when the party emerged from an unexpected detour through the Underdark. He insisted the shifting passages were to be expected and that their current location was remarkably convenient—perhaps even a shortcut to Blingdenstone, their destination. When Thorin predicted the situation would "end in tears," Jimjar proposed a wager on whether tears—literal or metaphorical—would mark their journey. After the terms were defined, the bet was accepted. When the cave system began flooding, Jimjar wryly noted the ironic fulfillment of his bet about tears, given the rising water. After the party escaped to the surface, Jimjar declared the adventure had not ended in tears after all and offered to call the bet a draw, citing the charming and helpful Glabbagool joining their ranks. Thorin agreed.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool is a sentient gelatinous cube the party encountered in a chamber containing the hovering remains of a drow, a dark metal mace, and scattered coins. He explained that he had once been an ordinary gelatinous cube, mindlessly cleaning floors and consuming debris, but had recently gained the ability to think and speak. He was confused by his newfound sentience but eager to understand the world around him. Thorin invited him to join the party, and Glabbagool agreed enthusiastically, promising to eat anything they dropped on the floor. He accepted a lucky foot from Thorin as a welcoming gift.

During exploration, Glabbagool inadvertently blocked a narrow passage with his ten-foot-by-ten-foot body and cheerfully acknowledged he should stay at the rear of the formation. He volunteered to investigate a chamber containing a stone fountain and formless sculptures, offering to touch the mysterious sculptures. When he touched one, it materialized into a gray ooze. After the party dispatched the oozes, Glabbagool meticulously cleaned up the remains and investigated the fountain, discovering a hidden cache of treasure: 112 silver pieces, 3 gold pieces, a drow dagger, and a vial of oil of slipperiness—though the party would need to wait for him to digest and pass the items.

Glabbagool confirmed he did not need to breathe, making him immune to the drowning threat as the complex flooded. He also agreed to allow the party to attempt resurrection on any fallen members before consuming them. He expressed excitement about consuming the remains of a defeated black pudding. He was last seen with the party after their successful escape to the surface, celebrated as a new companion.

## Eldeth

Eldeth, a dwarf in the party, recognized the flooding phenomenon as an underground river breaking through the stone. She shared stories of people who had successfully escaped similar situations by breaking through the rock but also warned of the risk of causing the water to flood even faster.

## Zalthir

Zalthir, a monk, stepped forward without hesitation to strike a gray ooze with powerful bare-fisted blows, quickly dispatching it. In the fountain chamber, he sprang into action against each ooze that materialized from the formless sculptures, dispatching them one by one with flurries of precise strikes as the party activated the statues systematically.
