## NPC Activity

**Jimjar** (deep gnome guide): Defended his navigational choices after leading the party through an unexpected detour, claiming their current exit point was a convenient shortcut to Blingdenstone. Proposed and accepted a wager with Thorin over whether "tears" would mark their journey. Wryly noted the ironic fulfillment of the bet when flooding threatened the party. Upon escaping, declared the adventure hadn't ended in tears and offered to call the bet a draw, crediting Glabbagool's addition to the party as justification.

**Glabbagool** (sentient gelatinous cube): Encountered in a chamber containing the hovering remains of a drow, a dark metal mace, and scattered coins. Explained that he was formerly a mindless cube that had recently and inexplicably gained sentience and the ability to speak, and expressed confusion and eagerness about his new condition. Joined the party at Thorin's invitation, accepting a lucky foot as a welcoming gift. Inadvertently blocked a narrow passage during combat. Volunteered to investigate a chamber of mysterious sculptures by touching them, triggering a series of gray oozes. Cleaned up ooze remains meticulously after combat. Discovered a hidden cache in the fountain (112 silver pieces, 3 gold pieces, a drow dagger, and a vial of oil of slipperiness), which he consumed for the party to retrieve later. Confirmed he does not need to breathe and agreed to allow resurrection attempts on fallen party members before consuming them.

**Eldeth** (dwarf, party-adjacent): Recognized the flooding phenomenon as an underground river breaking through stone. Shared accounts of people escaping similar situations by breaking through rock, while warning of the risk of accelerating the flood.

## Revealed Information

- Glabbagool was formerly a mindless gelatinous cube performing routine dungeon maintenance (consuming debris, cleaning floors) before recently gaining sentience and speech. The cause of this transformation was not explained and remains unknown to him.
- A drow corpse (remains only, hovering inside Glabbagool's body or nearby) was present in the chamber where Glabbagool was first encountered, along with a dark metal mace and scattered coins.
- The flooding of the complex was caused by an earthquake cracking an underground reservoir. A second breach, caused when Thorin broke through a wall, revealed a diverted underground river that ultimately provided an escape route.
- The chamber with the stone fountain contained sculptures that, when touched, materialized into gray oozes, suggesting a magical trap or containment mechanism of unknown origin.
- The fountain concealed a hidden treasure cache: 112 silver pieces, 3 gold pieces, a drow dagger, and a vial of oil of slipperiness.
