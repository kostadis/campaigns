## Zalthir

Zalthir scouted ahead into a cave to the southwest, where he discovered myconids — several fungi dancing frantically, affected by the madness of the Underdark. He noted that Stool had never described myconids dancing in such a manner. He also observed two quagoth spore servants behind the myconids, which reminded him of Prince Derendil, the mad quagoth who was a polymorphed elf. He reported his findings to the group, and they agreed to leave the myconids alone for now.

While following Droki's trail, Zalthir encountered a flumph and served as the initial point of contact. He received telepathic images from the flumph showing a chamber with a circular pathway, a pyramid, and an obelisk where a derro named Plinki was conducting evil rituals. He also saw, through the flumph's trauma dump, a side passageway containing a chamber with a derro bearing the insignia of the Council of Savants and the Gray Ghosts.

Zalthir questioned the flumph about the papers in the obelisk room and about any actionable intelligence regarding the Gray Ghosts and the Council of Savants that could convince Gartokkar or Errde, but received little concrete information. He expressed reluctance about the mission's scope, stating, "I was just trying to kill some time before I got some magical equipment. Saving the Duergar from a mad king and defeating another cult of Demogorgon is not what I am here for." He later participated in the negotiation with Gartokkar Xundorn, pushing for compensation.

## Droki

Droki was the target the party had been chasing through the Whorlestone Caves. His trail was described as easy to follow because he is "a creature of habit." He was not encountered directly in this session, but his movements continued to guide the party's exploration.

## Stool

Referenced by Zalthir when observing the dancing myconids — Zalthir recalled that Stool, a myconid sprout, had described myconids to the party but never described them as "dancing frantically." Stool did not appear in person.

## Prince Derendil

Briefly remembered by Zalthir upon seeing quagoth spore servants behind the myconids. Zalthir recalled Prince Derendil as "the mad quagoth, who was a polymorphed elf." He did not appear in person.

## Plinki

Revealed through the flumph's psychic trauma dump. Plinki is a derro who has found a "mighty and tasty obelisk" in a chamber with a circular pathway and a pyramid. He has recently converted to the Cult of Demogorgon and wishes to turn a dragon egg into a dual-headed dragon that he can give to Demogorgon as a mount. The flumph described him as conducting evil activities around the obelisk. He is working with the Council of Savants in this effort. His chamber is accessible via a main entrance and a side passageway.

## Buppido

Referenced by the flumph as having been its friend before going "stark raving mad." The party had already killed Buppido earlier in the caves. Grygum fell silent at the mention of his name. The flumph did not appear to know Buppido was dead.

## Werz

Mentioned by the flumph, who read the party's thoughts and referenced the duergar Werz as someone whom assassins with "a magical light blade" had tried to kill. The flumph attributed this knowledge to reading the party's minds. Werz did not appear in person.

## Errde

Referenced multiple times as the leader of the Stone Guards and a potential employer/ally. She believes the Council of Savants and the Grey Ghosts are behind the increasing madness in the city. She is pretending not to think that assassins have targeted her clan. She believes Droki is helping the Council of Savants and wants proof of their involvement with corruption. She also wants evidence about the Empty Scabbard Killers. She was previously willing to provide the party with weapons. She cannot enter the Whorlestone Caves without triggering a war that would expose her incompetence as city guardian. Daz planned to visit her next to negotiate terms. She did not appear in person this session.

## Gartokkar Xundorn

Leader of the Keepers of the Flame. The party brought the flumph to him, which trauma dumped on him; he seemed "curiously relieved" by the experience. He wants the dragon egg retrieved — the egg is the Keepers of the Flame's insurance policy against Themberchaude. He considers the Council of Savants his worst enemies and is engaged in a secret war with them. He demanded the party bring him the statue of Demogorgon as proof of demonic worship, intending to use it to convince the Deepking to wage a genocidal war against the Derro.

He negotiated payment with the party: 2,000 gold for the job, plus a 1,000-gold confidentiality rider, with 30% paid upfront. He resisted attempts to raise the upfront fee to half. He threatened the party directly: "If you betray me, there is no cave, no pool of still water, no river, no place in the dark that I will not find you and hunt you down." He cannot enter the Whorlestone Caves himself without revealing the loss of a crucial pillar of his power (the egg).

## Themberchaude

The red dragon of Gracklstugh. Referenced by Daz in his analysis of the political situation. Themberchaude believes the Keepers of the Flame have plans and is not convinced his position is secure. He is unsure whether he is truly in charge and wants information about what is happening. Thorin also mentioned a plan to escape the city on Themberchaude's back. He did not appear in person.

## Milo Goodbarrel

Referenced by Daz as the author of a book and the inspiration for Daz's journal-writing habit. A commentator on Milo's work had praised his practice of writing daily, which Daz adopted — mentally composing journal entries since his escape from Velkynvelve. Milo did not appear in person.
