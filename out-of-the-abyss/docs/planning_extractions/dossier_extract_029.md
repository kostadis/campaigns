## Grogluk

Grogluk is a Duergar guard stationed at the newly manned guardhouse at the entrance to the West Cleft District, which had previously been abandoned. He is about 4 feet tall with typical ashen grey skin, a scarred and tattooed face, and thin, unsettling lips that curl into a sneer. He wears a crude pendant around his neck — a clenched fist symbolizing loyalty to the Deepking, confirming he works directly for the Deepking's authority.

Grogluk confronted the party as they attempted to pass from the Derro slums into the city proper. He demanded proof they were not the Ember Vanguard, referencing a wanted poster with inaccurate depictions of the party (the Drow wizard depicted as ancient, Zalthir shown as red instead of gold, Thorin flattered, and Grygum made more fierce). He noted the Derro "get details wrong" and speculated the dragonborn might actually be gold. He offered two options: lay down weapons and magic items for inspection, or speak the secret salute of the Stone Guard. He was visibly intimidated when Grygum mentioned their mission from Errde, and after Grygum performed the Stone Guard ritual perfectly, Grogluk confirmed they were not the Ember Vanguard. He then demanded a bribe to pay off his guards, receiving 20 gold from Grygum before allowing the party through. He had at least five invisible guards hidden in the 10-by-30-foot passageway, suggesting his seemingly solitary, defiant posture was an act. Last seen at the guardhouse checkpoint.

## Hgraam

Hgraam is the stone giant leader residing in Cairngorm's Cavern. The party visited him to report that a secret Derro ritual had been transforming his giants into two-headed monsters. Thorin reassured him the ritual was the work of madmen and not representative of the Derro people as a whole. Hgraam sighed in response, offered the party a small pile of coins, and then asked them to leave, declaring the entrance to his cavern would be closed "for a while" — which, given stone giant sensibilities, could mean a very long time. When asked about a route to the surface world, he said he knew of no such place, was unsure one even existed, and wouldn't know what to do with a world without stone overhead. He is withdrawing and isolating himself and his people in response to the threat.

## Gartokkar

Gartokkar is a Duergar who had a prior arrangement with the party to retrieve a dragon egg. When the party arrived at his house, he eagerly asked about the egg. He noticed the strange occult markings on the egg and was worried, but asked Grygum as a cleric of Bahamut to evaluate it. He was pleased by Grygum's assessment that the chromatic dragon egg would produce an evil creature. He honored the deal, providing a bag of coins and upholding their non-disclosure agreement. Before the party departed, he issued a serious warning: the Deepking's agents are searching everywhere for the Ember Vanguard, described as shadowy assassins from Menzoberanzan with powerful magic and soul-rending blades. He warned the party to be careful, noting both the Derro and Stone Guard are on edge and some actively want conflict. Daz interpreted Gartokkar's warning as stemming from genuine fear — fear that the party's actions or words could cause the city to erupt into civil war. Last seen at his house.

## Jimjar

Jimjar is a Deep Gnome traveling with the party. After the group passed through the checkpoint into Laduguer's Furrow, Jimjar proposed a wager that Thorin would do something embarrassing. Thorin took the bet and promptly had his pants fall down when he accidentally unbuckled his belt. Daz observed that Jimjar's goal didn't seem to be winning or losing the bet, but rather lifting the group's spirits after the tense encounter at the wall — and it worked.

Stool is a myconid traveling with the party, mentioned chatting with Rumpadump as the group departed the Whorlestone caves.

## Errde

Errde is referenced as the authority who assigned the party a mission. Grygum invoked Errde's name to intimidate Grogluk at the checkpoint, and the mention visibly made Grogluk sweat, suggesting Errde holds significant authority or inspires fear among the Duergar.

## Themberchaud

Themberchaud is the red dragon of the Keepers of the Flame in Gracklstugh. Zalthir notes that Themberchaud is "not doing dragonkind any favors," reinforcing local prejudices that dragon morality is tied to scale color. As the party departed Gartokkar's house, Thorin recalled that Themberchaud believed he had an agreement with the party — but the party never actually made a deal with the dragon. Themberchaud's current status and reaction to this remain unresolved.
