## Chipgrin

Chipgrin, the wererat leader, was encountered in the Hall of Melting Mirrors where he and his wererats had been maintaining a chanting vigil to suppress lingering psychic static left behind by the mirrors. He asked the party's spellcasters to locate the harmonic center of the room and silence the noise for good. When Thorin—the least magically inclined party member—was the one to successfully identify the location, Chipgrin stared at him for a long moment before muttering that he probably should have asked the dwarf first, then caught Zalthir's eye and hastily clarified he meant no offense.

Later, at the Trader's Grotto, Chipgrin argued with Chief Diggermattock over how to divide newly opened territory, seemingly unbothered by the ooze threat still present. During the council meeting, the wererats demanded the upper boroughs as their own territory. The party proposed that the Gold Whisker wererats take control of the upper burrows to serve as a self-funded defense force, removing the need for Diggermattock to pay for military protection—a compromise that was accepted.

Upon the party's departure, Chief Chipgrin pressed a braided rat's foot into Daz's hand—the Voucher of the Varmint—a token recognized by wererats and outcasts from Blingdenstone all the way to Menzoberranzan, as a sign of friendship.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool, the party's intelligent gelatinous cube companion, played a pivotal role in resolving the ooze crisis at Trader's Grotto. When Jimjar tossed his mysterious bone die onto Glabbagool, a strange blink rippled through the air and Glabbagool briefly became a telepathic bridge between every ooze in the cavern—a single vast mind connecting jellies, puddings, and cubes alike. He told them, in his gentle and earnest way, that the world was fine, that everyone could get along, and that there was really no need to kill anyone. The oozes, apparently persuaded, went mindless and peaceful, slithering away into cracks and stonework. When the party later asked Glabbagool about the experience, he described briefly being part of one big mind with all the oozes before they returned to their mindless states. The bone die rolled out of him a moment later, spent and inert.

## Jimjar

Jimjar, the mysterious deep gnome gambler, played a decisive role in the ooze crisis at Trader's Grotto. He cut through the chaos by approaching Grygum and offering a bet of ten platinum pieces, claiming that Glabbagool had more sense than the Pudding King ever had. He then tossed a small bone die onto Glabbagool, triggering a mysterious "blink" that pacified the entire ooze army by briefly connecting all the oozes into one vast mind through Glabbagool. Grygum paid the ten platinum without complaint.

When pressed about the die, Jimjar explained he had won it off a blind Duergar in Gracklestugh who claimed it was carved from the knucklebone of a saint who never told a lie—a die with a memory for the truth. Jimjar shrugged and said he figured that in a place where the blink had turned the world's math into a bad joke, a little uncertainty could go a long way. He gave the spent die to the party as a keepsake, and later returned Grygum's ten platinum winnings.

He also appeared briefly at the support beams, commenting on the party's knack for fixing things. Before the party's departure, Jimjar delivered a final ominous warning, pointing to a pale white puffball mushroom growing at impossible speed from a crack in the wall, remarking: "Juiblex was a loud, messy neighbor, but this sister, she prefers to move in while you're sleeping"—foreshadowing Zuggtmoy's encroachment.

When Zalthir used a special ability to inspect him, Jimjar was found to be immune to poison and psychic damage, unable to be charmed or frightened, and resistant to radiant energy and non-magical physical attacks—a profile matching no ordinary gnome and bearing a striking resemblance to something divine. Jimjar noticed the scrutiny, offered a knowing wink, and vanished entirely from existence, leaving only the faint metallic clink of platinum coins echoing in the tunnel behind him. The party discussed the possibility that he was a deity or celestial being, noting his stat block resembled that of an angel.

## Dorbo Diggermattock

Chief Dorbo Diggermattock was present at the Trader's Grotto arguing with Chipgrin over how to divide newly opened territory even as oozes threatened to overrun the defenses. Thorin attempted to redirect their attention to the immediate threat, but the argument continued.

During the council meeting, Diggermattock expressed concerns about the city's economy, labor shortages, and defense, and pushed for aggressive economic recovery and mithral extraction. The party brokered compromises on his behalf: a wage agreement between the miners guild and working class, the wererat defense force arrangement (removing his need to fund military protection), and sustainable mining practices respecting the ghosts' sacred grounds. Diggermattock ultimately agreed to the peace arrangement and announced plans to reopen Blingdenstone for trade with the surface world.

Earlier, at the support beams, Diggermattock was so moved by Thorin's effort winching a beam into place that he personally threaded a needle and sewed a Diggermattock Miners patch onto Thorin's gear, declaring him an honorary member of the mining crew.

## Senni Diggermattock

Dorbo's wife and a member of the Gold Whisker clan, Senni advocated for a functional alliance between the gnomes and the wererats during the council meeting. Her support was noted as helpful to Dorbo's decision-making in reaching the peace arrangement.

## Burrow Warden Jadger

The spectral leader of the Burrow Warden ghosts was present during the party's departure from Blingdenstone. He and the ghosts dipped their spectral banners in salute as the Ember Vanguard left the city. During the council meeting, the ghosts' faction objected to the Miners Guild's plans to strip-mine the sanctum, which they considered sacred ground; a sustainable mining compromise was ultimately brokered to respect their burial grounds.

## Dazco Pickshine

A representative of the Miners Guild who initially pushed for immediate strip mining to fund city repairs during the council meeting. He ultimately agreed to the sustainable mining compromise brokered by the party that balanced economic needs with respect for the ghosts' sacred grounds.

## Entemoch

The Prince of Good Elementals, mentioned as feeling favorably toward the party after they assisted and healed the Earth Elemental in the throne room. He was not directly present but his disposition toward the party was noted.
