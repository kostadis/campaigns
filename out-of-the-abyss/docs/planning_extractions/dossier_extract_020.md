## Errde

Errde is the head of the Stone Guard in Gracklstugh. The party visits her at Overlake Hold to report on their investigation into the Grey Ghosts and the Council of Savants. She is deeply paranoid and obsessed with proving that the Grey Ghosts and the Council of Savants are working together, immediately seizing on the Flumph's psychic testimony as proof: "I knew the Grey Ghosts and the Council were working together!" Daz talks her down, explaining that proximity doesn't prove collaboration, and she grudgingly accepts this, though she demands the party return to gather more concrete evidence — specifically papers they failed to retrieve. When Grygum asks for reinforcements, she refuses to send Duergar troops into the Derro slums but offers to arm the party's crew instead. She pressures them on a timeline: "When will I have what I need?" She approves of Daz's plan to create cover by having the party visibly speak with other clans (Clan Ironhead and Clan Xardelva), so that when they enter caves, people will assume they work for those clans rather than for her. She provides intelligence about inter-clan politics: Clan Ironhead is forbidden from taking weapons orders directly and must accept surplus from Clan Steelshadow (the Deepking's clan), and Clan Xardelva fears being absorbed by Clan Steelshadow. Both clans are seen as opposing the Deepking. She later arms the party's crew (Jimjar, Spiderbait, Sarith, and Eldeth) at the Underlake Hold. Daz privately assesses Errde as blinded by her conviction that the Council of Savants is corrupting the Deepking, noting that she and the Keepers of the Flame both want the destruction of the Derro. He considers her desire to kill the Derro as scapegoating — the impulse of a paranoid leader who blames a helpless group for everything that's broken.

## Werz

Werz is a Duergar merchant who arranged to meet the party at the Shattered Spire tavern, built on a broken stalagmite jutting from the Darklake. He gives each party member (including the Flumph) a gemstone worth 10 gold pieces in gratitude for saving his life. He reveals that assassins tried to kill him, though he dismisses the idea of an organized assassin's guild as "a children's fable" — which is why he says the Stone Guards would never believe him. When pressed on why he's being targeted, he admits he is a deal-broker who helped clans make forbidden weapons deals in violation of the Deepking's decree that only Clan Steelshadow may handle weapons orders. He offers the party a favor: facilitating a weapons deal between the Deep Gnomes and the Duergar clans to help the party reach Blingdenstone. Daz negotiates a Non-Disclosure Agreement with him, starting at 1,250 gold and ultimately charging him 1,100 gold for secrecy about their dealings. Daz then separately charges him for the assassin removal job, and Werz pays in gems — which Jimjar later identifies as empty spell gems valuable to Kazook Pickshine, an alchemist in Blingdenstone. Before departing, Werz shares political intelligence: Clan Ironshadow's leader is willing to wait out the Deepking's madness but other members are unhappy; Clan Tharzgad complains about Grey Ghost raids; Clan Xundom will support the Deepking in a war. He also whispers that Stone Guards have been taking bribes — something new that started only a few months ago when the Deepking's madness took hold. He warns the party that the city is ready to explode and providing the wrong faction with the wrong information could trigger all-out war. He insists he cares only about trade, not politics, and wants his assassin problem solved — preferably without his death.

## Buppido

Daz uses this memory to support his belief that the Council of Savants is innocent — victims rather than conspirators.

## Stool

Stool is a myconid sprout traveling with the party. When Zalthir asks him about the dancing myconids the party has observed, Stool is confused and insists that myconids don't dance, suggesting something is wrong with them. He says myconids won't attack unless attacked first. When Zalthir describes another sprout who looks upset and isn't dancing, Stool becomes extremely animated and identifies the sprout as his friend Rumpadump from his own circle. He insists the dancing myconids must also be from their circle and demands the party rescue Rumpadump so they can learn what's going on. Regarding the dead quaggoth found with the myconids, Stool dismisses it with biological determinism, calling quaggoths "bad, mean creatures" who must have attacked first. When Zalthir says they have too many obligations to rescue Rumpadump right away, Stool begins sobbing — which moves Thorin enough that the party agrees to add it to their task list after dealing with the assassins.

## Rumpadump

Rumpadump is a myconid sprout from Stool's circle. He was spotted by Zalthir looking upset and not dancing, unlike the other myconids the party has observed. Stool identifies him by description and becomes frantic to rescue him, believing Rumpadump can explain what is happening with the dancing myconids. His current status and location are unclear, but the party has agreed to attempt a rescue after dealing with the assassins.

## Brannam

Brannam is a Duergar who delivers the magical weapons and items that Thorin and Zalthir had requested. He arrives at the party's location towards the evening of the 10th of Myrtul. No other details about him are provided.

## Thangus Ironhead

Thangus Ironhead is the clan leader of Clan Ironhead. The party secures an audience with him using their badges. He publicly states that Clan Ironhead approves of the Deepking's policies and agrees that the Deepking's Clan Steelshadow should be pre-eminent. Zalthir is unconvinced of his honesty, and Daz flatly declares: "He's lying and trying to survive."

## Grinta Ironhead

Grinta Ironhead is a female Duergar of Clan Ironhead who approaches the party after they leave the Clan Ironhead stronghold. She reveals she knows they intend to travel to the surface through Blingdenstone. She tells them the Deepking has raised prices to astronomical levels for the Deep Gnomes, and proposes that if the Deepking were deposed, Clan Ironhead would be free to trade with the gnomes at discounted rates. She departs immediately after delivering her message, leaving no opportunity for extended conversation.

## Kazook Pickshine

Kazook Pickshine is an alchemist in Blingdenstone. Jimjar identifies him as someone who can transform the empty spell gems (received from Werz) into gems of immense magical power and would pay well for them. He does not appear in person.
