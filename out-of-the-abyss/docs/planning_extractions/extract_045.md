## Revealed Information

- **Zuggtmoy's two-phase strategy**: Oozes are being deployed to "soften the shell" of targets (likely living creatures or settlements), after which Zuggtmoy's fungal garden will "take root in the flesh beneath." This was overheard telepathically during combat in the Festering Fissure.
- **Entemoch's summoning requirements**: The giant-rune circle specifies three sacrifices — wealth, a weapon, and blood — to free Entemoch and receive his boon. The weapon sacrifice has been completed and confirmed functional (dart consumed, runes activated).
- **The time-snag phenomenon**: Magical pressure caused time to "snag" at the White Shell Mine tunnel. Direct intervention would cause a catastrophic explosion; a Shadow Monk was required to redirect the release through shadow space safely. This suggests deliberate or incidental magical interference in the area.
- **Galeb Duhr as sentinels of ancient protocol**: The four Galeb Duhr at Pickshine Mine were not mindlessly blocking the path but waiting for completion of a specific ancient handshake sequence using resonant geodes. They appear to be bound to a role of gatekeeping until the correct authority signal is given.
- **Basilisks as placed guardians**: The two basilisks in the summoning cavern move with mechanical precision rather than natural behavior, suggesting they were deliberately stationed to protect the circle rather than being naturally occurring creatures.
