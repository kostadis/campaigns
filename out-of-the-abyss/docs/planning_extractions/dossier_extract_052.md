## Ilvara Mizzrym

Ilvara Mizzrym, high priestess of House Mizzrym, served as Zuggtmoy's corrupted instrument throughout this confrontation. She was stationed in the cavernous space above the Heart Fungus at the center of the Fungal Cavern (a separate location several days' travel from Blingdenstone, *not* Velkynvelve — Velkynvelve was the original capture/escape outpost).

Zalthir opened the fight by grappling her with his Eldritch Claw Tattoo, dragging her forty feet into the air, striking her twice (including two critical hits for sixteen damage), and dropping her to the stone floor. After the fall, wounded and bleeding, she used a legendary reaction to impose a DC-14 Wisdom saving throw fear effect on Zalthir, successfully frightening him and rendering him unable to approach her — effectively neutralizing the monk's entire close-range combat kit for a full round.

She cast Insect Plague at the cavern entrance, creating a massive swarm that instantly killed her own fungal minions (thirteen hit points each) and dealt twenty-six points of damage to Glabbagool. She continued fighting with focused fury, calling down divine fire on Daz and Nym Duskryn, throwing up fungal growth to reshape the battlefield, and retreating tactically even while wounded. When Thorin destroyed the bridge she was standing on, she leapt clear and survived the fall.

Throughout the fight she muttered prophecy about Zuggtmoy, "the bride," and "the chaos and mayhem to come" — delivered not as threats but as sincere belief in a future she had already accepted. She never begged or offered surrender.

Jorlan Duskryn charged her through the insect swarm, screaming her name — their relationship history (she discarded him after his scarring, per drow society's values) fueling his attack. They traded blows on the cavern floor.

Grygum's Guiding Bolt ultimately killed her. She did not die quietly — her body erupted in a massive cloud of poisonous spores, the demonic corruption venting outward as biology rather than identity. Zalthir and Thorin were caught in the toxic bloom. Grygum noted he was glad to have been standing back. Her death confirmed the extent of Zuggtmoy's claim on her: she was no longer a person aided by a demon lord but Zuggtmoy's work inhabiting a drow priestess's body.

## Jorlan Duskryn

Jorlan Duskryn, the scarred drow warrior, fought alongside the party — or at least near them — during the battle in the fungal cavern. When Ilvara appeared wounded, something older than tactics took over. He broke from his position and charged across the swarm-infested terrain toward her, screaming her name: "ILVARA\!" — described not as a battle cry but as a wound reopening, something uglier and more honest than rage.

His charge was motivated by their personal history: Ilvara had discarded him as a lover after he was scarred, because drow society rewards beauty and punishes everything else. Thorin had an opportunity attack on Jorlan as he broke away but rolled an eight and chose to let him pass, reasoning that Jorlan charging Ilvara made him functionally useful. Zalthir summarized the situation as "the tides have turned."

Jorlan and Ilvara traded blows on the cavern floor after both survived the bridge collapse. Nym Duskryn later hit him with a lightning bolt, apparently channeling her professional frustrations. His final status after the battle is not explicitly stated, though he was staggering from the lightning bolt.

## Nym Duskryn

Nym Duskryn was present in the cavern as part of a contract to extract Daz alive. She and her sister Kaelira had been sent to deliver him intact and get paid. She watched the chaos of the battle with the expression of someone calculating whether their contract was still viable.

She urged Daz to stay out of combat: "Can you please keep your head down and out of combat? We only get paid if you live." Daz refused, declaring he would not leave his companions to fight without him and that he didn't care whether she got paid. Her expression shifted from exasperated professionalism to something more pointed.

She redirected her frustration toward Jorlan Duskryn, asking flatly: "Can I kill your brother?" — a genuine inquiry delivered with professional efficiency. She described Jorlan as "always the dumber of the ones" and noted he was covered in fungus. She later hit Jorlan with a lightning bolt.

When Asha Vandree confronted the Duskryn sisters as potential traitors to Lolth, Daz smoothly introduced them as "friends of Lolth" who were there to help, folding them into his ongoing cover identity.

## Kaelira

Kaelira, Nym Duskryn's sister, was part of the contract to extract Daz alive. During the battle, she physically grabbed Daz's arm and hauled him northeast through the insect-choked cavern, declaring: "Come with me if you want to live, you idiot." She dragged him through the poisonous gas to a safer position where Nym was waiting. She was identified by Daz as a "friend of Lolth" when Asha challenged her presence.

## Asha Vandree

Asha Vandree, a Lolth-devotee, was present in the cavern during the final battle. When she spotted the Duskryn sisters, she immediately moved toward her weapon with zealous energy and challenged them: "Who are you? You must be traitors\! Traitors to Lolth\!" Daz defused the situation by smoothly introducing them as friends of Lolth there to help, which Asha accepted — she had a ready-made slot in her worldview for Lolth-aligned drow.

Grygum noted that Asha's characterization of Daz as "an insane mage who had converted to Lolthism" was "accurately described."

After Ilvara's death, Asha dropped to her knees and began murmuring prayers to Lolth, her voice the only sound left in the settling cavern. She was already reshaping what she had witnessed into something her goddess could use. Both Grygum and Thorin noted this with wariness — Grygum filed it under "watch this space," and Thorin acknowledged that she might actually be answered.

## Glabbagool

Glabbagool, the sentient gelatinous cube companion traveling with the party, was caught in Ilvara's Insect Plague and took twenty-six points of damage. He had been hovering at the edge of the battle attempting to participate, with eyes extended toward the party, earnestly trying to follow the conversation. He survived the damage but was described as "churning and trembling" in the swarm. Grygum's first concern during the Insect Plague was for Glabbagool, and checking on the ooze was also Grygum's first priority once the battle ended. Daz also specifically sculpted his Fireball to exclude Glabbagool from the blast radius, noting the ooze didn't deserve what the cavern kept throwing at him.

## The Matron Mother of House T'sarran

The matron mother of House T'sarran is referenced but does not appear directly. The T'sarran mage, before dying, warned: "The matron mother of House T'sarran will hear about this." The last surviving T'sarran warrior echoed this, retreating without urgency and saying: "We will meet again. Enjoy your victory for the moment." Grygum noted that a threat delivered without heat is always more credible than one made in anger, and resolved to find out who the matron mother is before she finds out who the party is. House T'sarran had sent soldiers and a mage to the Fungal Cavern (during the final Ilvara fight), apparently under contract, expecting an easy kill. Their forces were largely destroyed by Daz's Fireball and the party's combined efforts.
