# Decision Ladders

Stage reveals, threats, and escalations across 4 levels. Each stage offers agency and has a cost. Never jump Stage 1 -> Stage 4 without an explicit reason, logged below.

## The 4 Stages

| Stage | Name | What it is | Who can ignore |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ambient Signals (Deniable) | Rumors, low-confidence hints | Easy to dismiss |
| 2 | Material Evidence (Contested) | Physical evidence, witnesses | Arguable |
| 3 | Conflicting Testimony (Ambiguous) | Sources disagree; forces active investigation | Requires a choice |
| 4 | Hard Proof (Irreversible) | Undeniable; triggers consequence | Cannot avoid |

## Design Rules

- Each stage offers a decision, not only exposition.
- Attach a cost to every upside.
- Record major decisions and rationale in the Decision Log below, immediately.
- Do not jump stages without explicit reason logged.

## Ladder: Daz Identity Reveal

Goal: Daz discovers he is Daz'issin DeVir, heir to a destroyed noble house, memory-vaulted by a Sharran priestess on Vizeran's order. Pairs with `docs/daz_heir.md`.

### Stage 1: Ambient Signals (Deniable)
- **Signals**: Daz suffers mild headaches near Drow noble sigils. NPCs misread his posture as "house-trained." A Sharran cultist in passing holds eye contact a beat too long.
- **Decision options**: ignore; probe casually; write it off as Underdark strain.
- **Consequence if ignored**: signals compound silently. Stage 2 arrives harder.

### Stage 2: Material Evidence (Contested)
- **Evidence**: a sealed dossier names Daz'issin DeVir as "confirmed deceased, 42 years" — timestamped before Daz's earliest memory. A Sharran relic resonates to Daz alone. Someone paid in advance for his safe passage out of Menzoberranzan as a child.
- **Decision options**: investigate the dossier owner; destroy the evidence; show Vizeran and demand answers.
- **Consequence if ignored**: the DeVir-extinction party (House Do'Urden descendants) notice the dossier trail and move first.

### Stage 3: Conflicting Testimony (Ambiguous)
- **Conflict**: a Sharran priestess confirms the vault but claims Vizeran did not pay for it. Vizeran denies knowing which priestess. A surviving DeVir retainer insists Daz died in the purge; they saw the body.
- **Decision options**: believe Vizeran; trust the Sharrans; chase the retainer; refuse all three and cross-reference archive records.
- **Consequence if ignored**: the party splits on who to trust. Social Masquerade becomes harder — Daz cannot perform "Master" convincingly when he does not know his own house.

### Stage 4: Hard Proof (Irreversible)
- **Proof condition**: Daz sees the DeVir crest at the West Wall Ruins. Muscle memory triggers. He knows the pass-phrase. Full recall lands.
- **Required decision**: the player chooses — stay Daz (the mercenary), or become Daz'issin (the avenger). The choice is permanent for the remaining arc.
- **Irreversible consequences**: ritual calibration becomes possible (Daz's blood-signature works). Political fracture becomes possible (an authenticated DeVir survivor destabilizes House-legitimacy order). Whichever identity Daz keeps, the OTHER identity's costs come due.

## Ladder: Vizeran Disclosure (True Endgame)

Goal: the party learns Vizeran does not want to stop the demon lords — he wants to aim them at Menzoberranzan and die with the city. Pairs with `docs/npcs/vizeran_devir.md` and `docs/campaign_master_plan.md`.

### Stage 1: Ambient Signals (Deniable)
- **Signals**: Vizeran refers to districts as "fuel load." He speaks of Gromph with pity, not rage. He asks the party to place ritual components at city landmarks (Narbondel, Great Rift, Pool of Baenre) without explaining why ritual components go there specifically.
- **Decision options**: ask directly (he'll deflect); log the oddities; press a proxy for plain-language answers.
- **Consequence if ignored**: the party commits resources to the Tripod of Ruin without knowing what it does.

### Stage 2: Material Evidence (Contested)
- **Evidence**: the Dark Heart talisman hums louder near demon-activity sites. Grin Ousstyl (Vizeran's apprentice) visibly fears Vizeran; he may try to pass a note to Daz that reads "He won't let us leave." A recovered archival memo by Vizeran reframes "reform" as "irreversible collapse" post-Blink.
- **Decision options**: confront Grin; confront Vizeran with the memo; proceed and hope the evidence is misread.
- **Consequence if ignored**: Vizeran registers the party as reliable and commits the next ritual step.

### Stage 3: Conflicting Testimony (Ambiguous)
- **Conflict**: Vizeran tells the party the ritual saves the surface. A trusted NPC (Bruenor's contact at Gauntlgrym, or a Blingdenstone survivor) insists the ritual configuration matches a summoning, not a binding. Vizeran has a plausible answer. The NPC has a plausible rebuttal.
- **Decision options**: believe Vizeran; break with Vizeran and sabotage the ritual; stay and gather one more piece of proof; confront Vizeran in a safe room with a trusted witness present.
- **Consequence if ignored**: confrontation is deferred to Stage 4, where Vizeran controls the frame.

### Stage 4: Hard Proof (Irreversible)
- **Proof condition**: the Tripod of Ruin activates. Demon lords arrive through the Pool of Baenre portal. Vizeran's anchor state becomes visible — he is the cage, not just the battery. His lifeforce, projected through the Dark Heart into Menzoberranzan's ley architecture, is a containment field. Demons summoned to the city cannot leave it. He cannot leave, he will die, he intends this.
- **Required decision** (one of):
  - **Complete the ritual**: city falls; cage holds; demons forced into mutual slugfest; party kills the weakened sole survivor; all demons banished by MM default on death. Vizeran dies with the seal resolving. Orthodoxy ends. Cleanest ending.
  - **Break the ritual mid-activation**: partial completion; Tripod components misfire; demons partially freed with neither containment nor full summons; higher personal cost; unclear resolution state.
  - **Kill Vizeran**: the Tripod still fires (components are anchor-independent); the city still falls; demons still rage (mutual rivalry + game-theoretic necessity, not cage compulsion). But the cage drops — wounded losers flee rather than die. Finale converts from single-boss fight to multi-target pursuit through the falling city and surrounding Underdark. Each demon lord killed banishes; each escapee becomes a permanent Underdark threat (1d3+ loose threads on failed hunt; Faerzress trap keeps them Prime-bound). Strongly motivates the Vhaerun post-arc.
- **Irreversible consequences**: whichever choice, Menzoberranzan never returns to its pre-ritual state. The demon crisis reshapes. Allies remember what the party chose under pressure. The number of surviving demon lords determines the shape of the aftermath and must be logged in the Decision Log.

## Ladder: Blink-Event Authorship

Goal: the party learns the Faerzress Blink was not a natural event. Someone engineered it. Candidate authors: the Orthodoxy (internal), Vhaerun (external god), a drow archmage other than Gromph. Ties `docs/npcs/vizeran_blink_clues.md` into durable form and seeds `docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`.

### Stage 1: Ambient Signals (Deniable)
- **Signals**: Braeryn market rumors question the official "spontaneous event" story. Apothecary ledger fragment on "Patient V.D." notes cognitive hardening post-faerzress, not identity break. Street witness: "He speaks of districts as fuel load now" (predating the party's arrival).
- **Decision options**: ignore; ask around casually; buy the ledger fragment.
- **Consequence if ignored**: the Orthodoxy's narrative becomes the party's default.

### Stage 2: Material Evidence (Contested)
- **Evidence**: archive time-lock records show pre-blink memos proposing targeted reform; post-blink memos jump to irreversible collapse language. A contraband margin note: "He rewrote the pact clauses to force any council into binary outcomes." Infernal and abyssal linguistic tells bracket the timeline.
- **Decision options**: bring the records to Vizeran (he deflects); trace the archive owner; compare with Orthodoxy-held records.
- **Consequence if ignored**: the party cannot distinguish Vizeran-radicalized-by-Blink from Vizeran-was-always-this.

### Stage 3: Conflicting Testimony (Ambiguous)
- **Conflict**: three testimonies, each pointing at a real layer of the truth. Cross-referenced, they triangulate.
  - An **Orthodoxy defector** claims the Blink was an internal Baenre ritual — not a spontaneous event, not an accident. Authorized, coordinated, ordered. (Partial truth: names the coordinator layer.)
  - A **Gauntlgrym archivist** claims the Blink originated in a Gromph Baenre ritual that "went wrong" on the surface. (Partial truth: names the caster layer, packaged as a cover story.)
  - A **Vhaerun cultist** claims Lolth herself authored the event to escape her imprisonment. The imprisonment is openly acknowledged within Lolth's own cult across the multiverse; the jailer's identity is not, and the cultist does not claim to know it — he says plainly that whoever holds that name has never been named aloud by anyone interested in surviving. (The deepest truth any mortal source can deliver, from the least-trusted witness — the party's instinct is to discount it. The jailer layer is beyond mortal testimony and only surfaces in the post-OotA arc.)
- Vizeran, if consulted at this stage, confirms the Orthodoxy and Gromph layers but denies or disclaims knowledge of a Lolth role. He is not lying. He does not know she is the principal; his grief is aimed at the apparatus, not the goddess he still identifies as ambient and uninvolved.
- **Decision options**: triangulate the testimonies (cross-reference to validate the Lolth claim despite its source); trust Vizeran's more limited account; force an inter-faction cross-examination; withhold trust pending Stage 4 proof.
- **Consequence if ignored**: the party reaches Stage 4 without the Lolth thread prepared; the reveal hits harder and with less of the party's own investigative equity. The post-OotA discrediting arc (`docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`) is still accessible but requires more reconstruction.

### Stage 4: Hard Proof (Irreversible)
- **Proof condition**: fragments recovered across Stages 1–3 resolve into a custody chain. Principal: **Lolth**, escaping a prison she is openly known (inside her cult, across the multiverse) to be held in. Caster: **Gromph Baenre**. Coordinators: **Orthodoxy leadership**. Mechanism: ritual routed through abyssal rails, which is why "a powerful demon did this" held as public cover. The proof is irrefutable at this layer. It does **not** name the jailer — that identity is the divine-tier secret the Stage 4 proof cannot reach without the post-OotA arc.
- **The genocide math**: published alone, this proof is atrocity evidence. Underdark + surface response converts to racial genocidal war against the drow — the Orthodoxy has been suppressing this exact outcome at any cost. "The drow did this" is the weaponizable sentence; the theological nuance does not survive mass publication. This is *why* the custody chain is fragmented and guarded, and why publication is a moral choice, not an investigative one.
- **The Vhaerun pairing**: a second proof — that Vhaerun jailed Lolth, that her escape is what killed the thousands, that her "prison break" is why the drow race spent a generation in the cage the Orthodoxy built, and that Vhaerun then harvested the post-collapse wreckage for recruits — reframes the publication from drow-authorship to drow-victimhood. Acquiring this second proof is the work of `docs/background/post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md` (pre-schism temple heist + Eilistraee ritual + mass defection); the jailer-identity reveal is that arc's Act IV payoff. Defected Vhaerun cultists and Menzoberranzan refugees become the credible publishers. Paired publication defuses the genocide motive.
- **Required decision** (one of):
  - **Publish alone.** Political earthquake. Orthodoxy's cover collapses; Lolth is named; drow race named as the Blink's authors. Genocidal war follows. The party ends the gods-war by ending the drow.
  - **Destroy the proof.** Preserve order. Accept the lie. Lolth keeps her escape unexamined; the Orthodoxy rebuilds under cover; the next cycle is seeded. The party lives with complicity.
  - **Pair with the Vhaerun proof** (activates `post_oota_vhaerun_arc.md`). Epic-tier continuation. The Act IV ritual forces the jailer reveal; defected cultists + refugees publish with the full context. Drow-authorship reframes as drow-victimhood; both gods diminished; Eilistraee becomes a legitimate alternative; genocide motive collapses. Hard-won, not automatic.
  - **Trade the proof.** Use it as leverage on a faction (Orthodoxy remnant, Vhaerun cult custodians, Gauntlgrym, a surface power) for operational capital. Moral capital spent for campaign resources. The proof stays buried but its existence is now a known weapon in others' hands.
- **Irreversible consequences**: whichever choice, the party has handled a proof that can end a race. Allies remember what was chosen under the pressure of that knowledge. Publication timing, publisher identity, and pairing state must all be logged in the Decision Log — the aftermath depends on who spoke, with what, next to what.
