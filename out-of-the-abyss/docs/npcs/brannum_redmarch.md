# Brannum Redmarch

## Identity
- Castaway and traveling merchant/trader
- Has been traveling with the party; encountered prior to their approach to Gracklstugh

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goal: self-preservation and maintaining his freedom above all else
- A dealmaker by nature — visibly excited when trade opportunities arise. Nervous and submissive under pressure, but quick to leverage his connections and skills when he sees a way to make himself useful. Survival-oriented and pragmatic.

## History with the Party
- **Approach to Gracklstugh:** Thorin raised the question of what to do with Brannum. Brannum panicked and pleaded not to be turned over to the guard, fearing for his freedom.
- **Trade Deal:** When Zalthir asked about procuring uncommon magical items, Brannum revealed that trading is his profession. He claimed he could acquire an Eldritch Claw Tattoo, stating "I know a guy. But it will take a few days." He negotiated a deal — he would procure the items in exchange for a clean slate with the party. Thorin threatened consequences if Brannum betrayed them or failed to deliver. Brannum agreed nervously.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** With the party, approaching or arriving at Gracklstugh
- **Active plans:** Attempting to procure an Eldritch Claw Tattoo (and possibly other uncommon magical items) through a contact; this will take a few days
- **Party knowledge:** The party knows he is a trader, that he has underworld or merchant contacts in or near Gracklstugh, and that he is desperate to avoid being handed over to authorities. It is unclear *why* he fears the guard or what his history is before joining the party as a castaway.

## Relationships
- **Thorin:** Tense dynamic. Thorin holds leverage over Brannum and has explicitly threatened him if he betrays the party or fails to deliver.
- **Zalthir:** Transactional relationship. Zalthir is the buyer driving the deal for the Eldritch Claw Tattoo.
- **The Party (general):** Brannum is tolerated for his usefulness; the "clean slate" deal suggests prior friction or distrust.
