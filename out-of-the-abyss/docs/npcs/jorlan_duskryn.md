# Jorlan Duskryn

## Identity
- Scarred drow warrior; former lover of Ilvara
- Low-ranking or discarded member of drow society due to his disfigurement
- First encountered during the battle in the fungal cavern, fighting near (not formally with) the party

## Personality & Motivations
- Driven by a deep, unresolved wound tied to Ilvara — their relationship ended when she discarded him after he was scarred, as drow culture rewards beauty and punishes its absence. His charge toward her was described not as tactical but as "a wound reopening, something uglier and more honest than rage." He is volatile, emotionally compromised, and potentially useful precisely because of it.

## History with the Party
- **Fungal Cavern Battle:** Fought alongside or near the party during a major engagement. When Ilvara appeared wounded, he broke position and charged across swarm-infested terrain toward her, screaming her name. Thorin had an opportunity attack on him (rolled an 8) but chose to let him pass, reasoning Jorlan's vendetta against Ilvara made him functionally useful. Jorlan and Ilvara traded blows on the cavern floor after both survived a bridge collapse. He was subsequently hit by a lightning bolt from Nym Duskryn and left staggering.

## Current Status
- **Last known state:** Staggering after being struck by Nym Duskryn's lightning bolt during the fungal cavern battle. Final fate is **not confirmed** — alive or dead is ambiguous.
- **Location:** Fungal cavern floor, near where the bridge collapsed.
- **Party knowledge:** The party understands his vendetta against Ilvara and his usefulness as an enemy of their enemy. They do not appear to have a formal alliance with him.

## Relationships
- **Ilvara:** Former lover. She discarded him after his scarring. His feelings toward her are raw and obsessive — not purely hatred, but something tangled and painful. He charged her in battle.
- **Nym Duskryn:** Hit him with a lightning bolt, described as "channeling her professional frustrations." The shared surname suggests a familial or house connection; the hostility suggests it is not a warm one.
- **Thorin (PC):** Thorin deliberately let him pass on an opportunity attack, viewing him as a useful weapon aimed at Ilvara. Pragmatic non-interference, not trust.
- **Zalthir (PC):** Observed the situation approvingly — "the tides have turned" — suggesting Zalthir sees Jorlan's vendetta as strategically advantageous.
