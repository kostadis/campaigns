# New notes for Bookwyrm (First Reader) (from dossier_extract_057.md)

Bookwyrm — identified as a green dragonborn — was the First Reader who summoned the party before the Avowed awoke, via a breathless messenger, to investigate the death of the Keeper of Tomes. She was visibly shaken and weary when the party encountered her in the Chapter House, in the manner of someone who had been awake since the death was discovered.

In the Chapter House, she attempted to hold the council together under conditions of active institutional collapse, repeating "FOCUS, PEOPLE! Focus!" until the shouting subsided. She proposed delegating the investigation to the party using language of divine fate — "seekers who the gods have sent us" — which Grygum recognized as deliberate rhetorical framing designed to make an irregular choice acceptable to the council. She deflected challenges from Daral and successfully neutralized Kalan Strongbranch's attempt to intervene, citing his troubled relationship with the deceased Keeper and ordering him to focus on securing the defenses instead. She agreed to the terms of the party's investigation: one book each, copied at the House of the Binder, upon successful conclusion, with full access throughout the library, subject to signing confidentiality papers.

On the walk to the crime scene, she volunteered — unprompted and before being questioned — that Janussi had asked Kalan to tender his resignation the morning before his death, citing concerns about Kalan's mental faculties and negligence. She declined to specify the nature of the negligence, framing this as not wanting to prejudice the investigation. When pressed by Daz and Thorin, she maintained that position. When Thorin pointed out her testimony was itself evidence and asked where she was at the time of the murder, she did not answer before arriving at the tower.

At the tower, she gave Tadric new orders, confirmed the party's authority to investigate, instructed that nothing be removed from the room, and departed for other business.

The party noted her as a person of interest: her volunteering of information about Kalan, her deflection on the question of negligence, and her non-answer to Thorin's alibi question were all flagged as significant.

Last known whereabouts: departing the tower to attend to other business, having established the party's investigative authority.
