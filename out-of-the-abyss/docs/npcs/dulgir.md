# Dulgir

## Identity
- Duergar merchant operating a stall in the Blade Bazaar, Gracklstugh
- First encountered when Grygum approached his stall to sell a mace

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goal: commerce — interested in making sales, not purchases
- Cursed viciously at Grygum when approached as a buyer of goods, but instantly became charming and eloquent when Grygum shifted to purchasing. Speaks "with the stilted tongue of a Baldurian patriar" when in sales mode. Bluntly matter-of-fact about disturbing truths — stated without hesitation that a nearby merchant's invisible brother "doesn't exist."

## History with the Party
- **Blade Bazaar encounter:** Grygum approached Dulgir's stall initially trying to sell a mace and was met with vicious cursing. When Grygum pivoted to buying, Dulgir's demeanor transformed entirely. Grygum purchased a new mace from him. Grygum then asked about a nearby merchant who appeared to be conversing with an invisible brother; Dulgir flatly replied "His brother doesn't exist," an answer that disturbed Grygum and deepened his suspicion that madness pervades Gracklstugh.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** His stall in the Blade Bazaar, Gracklstugh
- **Activity:** Conducting business as usual
- **Party knowledge:** The party knows him as a weapons merchant with a mercurial temperament and an oddly refined sales persona. His offhand comment about the other merchant's nonexistent brother served as early evidence of the city's spreading madness.

## Relationships
- **Grygum:** Customer; Dulgir was hostile until money was on the table, then became accommodating
- **Unnamed delusional merchant (nearby stall):** Dulgir is aware of and unbothered by this merchant's madness, treating it as simple fact
