# Dulgir

## Identity
- Duergar merchant operating a stall in the Blade Bazaar, Gracklstugh
- First encountered when Grygum approached his stall to sell a mace

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goal: commerce — purely transactional in his dealings
- Hostile and foul-mouthed toward those wasting his time (cursed Grygum viciously when he tried to sell rather than buy). Instantly warm and accommodating when money is on the table, adopting the refined speech of "a Baldurian patriar." Bluntly matter-of-fact about disturbing truths, showing no concern for the madness creeping through the city — or at least no surprise at it.

## History with the Party
- **Blade Bazaar encounter:** Grygum approached Dulgir's stall attempting to sell a mace and was met with vicious curses. When Grygum switched to buying, Dulgir's personality flipped entirely, becoming courteous and well-spoken. Grygum purchased a new mace from him.
- **The invisible brother:** Grygum asked Dulgir about a nearby merchant who appeared to be conversing with an invisible sibling. Dulgir flatly stated, "His brother doesn't exist." The casual certainty of this answer unsettled Grygum and deepened his suspicions that madness has taken hold in Gracklstugh.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** His stall in the Blade Bazaar, Gracklstugh
- **Activity:** Conducting business as usual
- **Party knowledge:** The party knows him as a merchant with a mercurial temperament and a source of unsettling local information. His offhand remark about the other merchant's nonexistent brother served as early evidence of widespread madness in the city.

## Relationships
- **Grygum:** Brief commercial relationship; sold him a mace. No lasting bond, but served as an inadvertent informant about the city's deteriorating mental state.
- **Gracklstugh at large:** Appears aware of — but unbothered by — the madness affecting other residents.
