# New notes for Kalan Strongbranch (from dossier_extract_057.md)

Kalan Strongbranch — described as having gray-white beard, piercing blue eyes, and the posture of someone accustomed to being listened to — was the gate warden of Candlekeep. He had previously attempted to put Daz in irons when the party arrived.

He entered the Chapter House meeting after the council had already decided to delegate the investigation to the party. Bookwyrm preemptively cut him off before he could speak, citing his "troubled relationship with the keeper of tomes," the need for impartial investigation, and the council's decision. She ordered him to secure the defenses and ensure no creature entered or left. He bowed in compliance. The bow was correct; his eyes were not.

From Tadric's testimony at the crime scene, Kalan had arrived at the Keeper's tower with Tadric at eight in the morning, spoken briefly with the discoverers (A'lai Aivenmore and Miss Hollypocket), and returned alone at eleven for what he described as a full inspection — careful, deliberate. Tadric noted Kalan looked more determined coming out than going in. He had also performed the Speak with Dead rite on the corpse during this inspection, which meant the rite could not be repeated for some time.

Bookwyrm had disclosed that Janussi asked Kalan to resign the morning before his death due to concerns about his mental faculties and negligence. The nature of the negligence was not specified.

Grygum identified Kalan as the first person he intended to question. The party flagged the combination of: having been asked to resign the day before the murder; returning alone to the crime scene; walking out looking determined; and using the Speak with Dead rite — which either yielded nothing or prevented subsequent attempts — as a cluster of significant facts.

Last known whereabouts: ordered by Bookwyrm to attend to the library's defenses, having been removed from direct involvement in the investigation.
