# Nym Duskryn

## Identity
Drow mercenary/contractor, one of the Duskryn sisters (alongside Kaelira). Hired under contract to extract Daz alive from the Underdark. Previously served as a drow soldier from Velkynvelve. *Also known as: Nym.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goal:** Complete the contract — deliver Daz alive and get paid. Everything else is secondary.
- Coolly professional, transactional, and pragmatic. Calculates cost-benefit in real time, visibly assessing whether the contract remains viable even amid battlefield chaos.
- Frustration manifests not as emotion but as redirected efficiency — when Daz won't cooperate, she simply finds someone she *can* kill.
- Dutiful but pragmatic — follows orders but is not reckless about personal safety. Shows hints of smugness when proven right.
- Holds open contempt for Jorlan Duskryn, whom she considers the family idiot.

## History with the Party
- **Pursuit encounter:** As a Velkynvelve soldier, Nym was among the drow scouts tracking the escaped party through tunnels. Zalthir recognized him by his distinctive gait. When Zalthir accidentally fell from hiding and made noise, Nym drew his weapon and searched the area. Grygum used Thaumaturgy to create a tremor, which convinced Nym the cave was unstable. Believing the prisoners had gone a different direction, he turned back, unaware the tremor was magical.
- **Cavern Battle:** Later appeared alongside Kaelira to extract Daz under their mercenary contract. Urged Daz to stay out of combat to preserve her payday. Daz refused, insisting he wouldn't abandon his companions and telling her the best way to get paid was to keep fighting.
- Frustrated by Daz's stubbornness, she asked with genuine professional inquiry whether she could kill Jorlan Duskryn — her own kin — calling him "always the dumber of the ones" and noting he was covered in fungus. She struck Jorlan with a lightning bolt during the fight, sending him staggering.
- When Asha Vandree confronted the Duskryn sisters as potential traitors to Lolth, Daz defused the situation by introducing Nym and Kaelira as "friends of Lolth" who were there to help, folding them into his ongoing cover identity.
- **Post-combat contract debrief:** Confirmed the contract terms directly to Daz — the mandate was to ensure other drow factions did not get him killed, with House Mizzrym and House T'sarran named explicitly. Acknowledged the party's handling of the T'sarran contingent with three insincere bravos.
- **Critique of Asha containment:** When Thorin grappled Asha rather than killing her, offered pointed professional critique — one does not grapple a cleric of Lolth, one cuts off their head. Followed by the observation that it was amazing the party had made it as far as they had. Was unimpressed that Thorin had covered Asha's mouth.
- **Dismissal of Asha as a threat:** Characterized Asha as a low-level cult initiate and suggested that if Daz could not protect himself from such a person, whoever paid for his survival deserved to lose their money. Assessed that Daz could probably kill Asha in a few seconds.
- **Refusal to extend contract:** When Thorin asked the price to retain the sisters for further protection, delivered the response in three words — that is a fee, that is a price, that he could not afford. No elaboration was offered.
- **Professional puzzlement about Daz:** Expressed genuine puzzlement about Daz's apparent value — no noble house, complete mystery background, yet whoever contracted them had spent a significant amount and interrupted the sisters' schedule more than once. Dismissed Daz's "more to me than meets the eye" as the reflexive self-mythology of every orphan, suggested he might have parental issues, and observed that every drow imagines noble blood waiting somewhere. Allowed the alternative that it could simply be a game played between houses.
- **Departure:** Departed professionally and without ceremony alongside Kaelira after Zalthir killed Asha. Contract fulfilled.

## Current Status
**Last known location:** Departed the cavern alongside Kaelira after the contract was fulfilled — no further destination disclosed.
**Active operations:** Contract complete (named threats neutralized, Daz alive). Refused to extend protection at any price the party could offer.
**Party knowledge:** The party knows she and Kaelira are mercenaries who were specifically contracted against House Mizzrym and House T'sarran on Daz's behalf. Who hired them remains unknown — Nym admitted she was not paid to think about it. Whether the contract is permanently closed or could be reactivated by future threats is unclear.

## Relationships
- **Kaelira Duskryn** — Her sister and partner on this contract. They operate as a unit.
- **Daz** — Her extraction target and source of professional frustration. He refuses to cooperate with her plan to keep him safe, insisting on fighting alongside his companions. She respects (or at least tolerates) his resolve enough to keep working.
- **Jorlan Duskryn** — A relative she holds in open contempt. She asked permission to kill him and then hit him with a lightning bolt. Described him as "always the dumber of the ones."
- **Asha Vandree** — Asha suspected Nym of being a traitor to Lolth. The confrontation was defused by Daz's cover story, not by Nym herself.
- **Zalthir (party member)** — Zalthir knows Nym well enough to recognize him by gait alone, suggesting prior familiarity from captivity at Velkynvelve.
- **The Party** — Nominally allied through Daz. Her loyalty extends exactly as far as her contract. Previously was their unknowing adversary during the pursuit.
- **Velkynvelve drow** — Former fellow soldiers in the pursuit force.

## Notes from Extract 054
Nym Duskryn, one of two Duskryn sisters hired as mercenary protection in Menzoberranzan, appeared alongside her sister Kaelira in the aftermath of the combat at the Velkynvelve outpost. The two did not lower their weapons so much as determine the party was not worth the effort of hostility, and Nym opened the conversation in the courteous-but-not manner characteristic of Menzoberranzan.

Nym confirmed the terms of the contract directly to Daz: their mandate was to ensure that other drow factions did not get him killed. The two named threats in the contract were House Mizzrym and House T'sarran — the latter's mercenary contingent had been handled by the party before the sisters needed to intervene. Nym acknowledged this with three bravos, delivered in a manner that made their insincerity legible.

When Thorin grappled Asha Vandree rather than killing her, Nym offered pointed professional critique: one does not grapple a cleric of Lolth, one cuts off their head. She followed this with the observation that it was amazing the party had made it as far as they had. When Thorin offered that he had covered Asha's mouth, Nym was unimpressed.

Nym dismissed Asha as a threat to Daz with brisk efficiency, characterizing her as a low-level cult initiate and suggesting that if Daz could not protect himself from such a person, whoever paid for his survival deserved to lose their money. She followed this with the assessment that Daz could probably kill Asha in a few seconds.

When Thorin attempted to retain the sisters for further protection and asked their price, Nym delivered the response in three words: that is a fee, that is a price, that he could not afford. No elaboration was offered.

Nym expressed genuine professional puzzlement about Daz's apparent value. She noted that he had no noble house, his background was a complete mystery, and yet whoever contracted them had spent a significant amount — with the specific characterization that the contracting party had interrupted their schedule more than once. When Daz suggested there might be more to him than meets the eye, Nym dismissed this as the reflexive self-mythology of every orphan, suggested he might have parental issues, and observed that every drow imagines noble blood waiting somewhere. She allowed the alternative that it could simply be a game played between houses.

Following Zalthir's killing of Asha Vandree, Nym and Kaelira departed professionally and without ceremony. The contract was fulfilled.
