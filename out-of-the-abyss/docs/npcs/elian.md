# Elian

## Identity
- A clerk/adjutant at Candlekeep who works under Gatewarden **Kalan Strongbranch**.
- First appears in Chapter 57 (session 2026-06-22), seeking out "Inspector Thorin" to praise the party's investigative work.
- The party's conduit for Kalan's investigative methodology: he delivers the memorandum **[[The Threefold Proof]]** (the "Investigation Methodology Paper") via the Ember Grapple.

## Personality & Motivations
- Earnest, slightly star-struck by the party's deductive reputation — flatters Thorin's "very thorough, deductive work" in the kitchens.
- A true believer in Kalan's methodology (no single spell is proof; truth comes from converging independent channels). Wants the approach vindicated and credited.
- Angles for academic credit the same way Fembris does — proposes co-writing a "monograph," is corrected, and settles for Thorin writing an introduction/preface.
- Carries a conspiratorial streak: hints darkly that "there is something rotten here" and bets the party will find that Kalan's advocacy for the methodology is the real reason he was removed.

## History with the Party
- Approached Thorin to praise his kitchen investigation, then shared the Threefold Proof paper, framing it as a tool to crack the murder.
- Pointed the party toward the theory that Kalan's removal is entangled with the murder itself — an early nudge toward the Bookwyrm/Kalan thread.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Candlekeep (staff under Kalan Strongbranch).
- **Activity:** Promoting Kalan's investigative methodology; sympathetic to the party.
- **Party knowledge:** They know his name, that he works for Kalan, that he gave them the methodology paper, and that he believes Kalan's firing is connected to "something rotten" at Candlekeep.

## Relationships
- **Kalan Strongbranch** — his boss; Elian is his methodology's evangelist. See [[kalan_strongbranch]].
- **The party (Ember Grapple)** — admirer and informal ally; shared the Threefold Proof and a conspiratorial lead.

## GM notes
- The paper Elian delivers is the antidote to the party's false confidence after Alkrist's zone-of-truth confession — a single spell is not proof. See the thread [[candlekeep_false_confidence_bookwyrm_lever]]; Bookwyrm, having read Kalan's proposal, can weaponize exactly this standard against the party.
