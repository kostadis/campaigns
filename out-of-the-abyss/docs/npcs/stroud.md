# Stroud

## Identity

Dwarf, notable public figure following the events of the Well of the Dragons. His chess victory over Protanther has become the subject of monuments, songs, and apparently a religious following (the "Stroudites"). No direct meeting with the party recorded.

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations

- Stroud appears to be an unlikely hero—someone who stumbled into greatness rather than pursuing it deliberately
- His willingness to go on a speaking tour suggests he embraces (or at least doesn't shy away from) the fame his victory brought him
- His explanations of the game suggest he may not fully understand the magnitude or mechanics of what he accomplished

## History with the Party

- The party has encountered references to Stroud and his chess game in multiple locations
- In Daggerford's market square, the party observed a monument to Tarvis Ulain bearing a secondary inscription in Dwarven—*The move is yours*—which Daz identified as sharing language with the Castle Ward statue in Waterdeep previously connected to Stroud
- At the Burning Wizard Inn in Daggerford, Grygum paid a resident bard a silver piece to perform a song about Stroud's famous chess game; the bard produced a love song using the chess game as a metaphor for romantic longing, stripping away the theological stakes

No direct interactions with Stroud himself recorded.

## Current Status

- Last known activity: conducting a speaking tour recounting his chess victory over Protanther, walking audiences through his thought process and the board
- No active plans or operations noted
- Public figure whose story is apparently well-known enough to be common knowledge, inspiring monuments, songs, and religious interpretation

## Relationships

- **Protanther** — The Gold Dragon he defeated in chess after the events of the Well of the Dragons
- **Bahamut** — Some believe Bahamut guided Stroud's hand during the match; no confirmation either way
- **Chess grandmasters** — Have analyzed the game and are perplexed by Stroud's explanations, as he appears to barely understand the rules of chess
- **Stroudites** — An apparent religious following that has formed around Stroud and his chess victory; the Metalworkers Guild in Daggerford may have included Stroudite members who funded the Tarvis Ulain monument
- **Tarvis Ulain** — (indirect) A Stroudite inscription appears on Tarvis's monument, retained because the monument was a gift and because Tarvis was trying to save everyone regardless of what happened in the chess game
