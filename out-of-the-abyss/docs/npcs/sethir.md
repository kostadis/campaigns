# Sethir

## Identity
- NPC companion/fellow traveler in the Underdark escape party
- No stated faction affiliation or title
- First appeared during early Underdark travel, part of the group navigating Faerzress-laden tunnels

## Personality & Motivations
- Core drive: treasure-seeking, particularly fixated on the Lost Tomb of Khem
- Eager and somewhat impulsive; repeatedly pushes the group toward treasure opportunities. Speaks bluntly about plans, sometimes to the point of indiscretion (announcing intentions aloud in Gracklstugh). Notably susceptible to strange magical influences — was visibly affected by Faerzress energy, which fellow NPC Daz flagged as making him "a bit off."

## History with the Party
1. **Faerzress Tunnels** — Traveled with the party through Faerzress-rich passages; was particularly affected by the eerie magic. Advocated for seeking the lost temple for treasure and suggested the Darklake route as safer than tunnel travel.
2. **Mid-travel (session with Daz's observations)** — Did not appear directly, but Daz flagged Sethir as suspicious, noting he "seems a bit off."
3. **Kuo-toan Ambush** — Present during the aftermath; asked "Should we continue?" before the group pressed on toward Sloobludop.
4. **Gracklstugh** — Still traveling with the party; openly declared the group's plan to get equipment and then go to the Lost Tomb of Khem for treasure.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Gracklstugh, with the party
- **Active plans:** Focused on reaching the Lost Tomb of Khem for treasure
- **Known vs. hidden:** The party knows Daz considers him suspicious and that Faerzress affected him unusually. The nature or cause of his being "off" remains unexplained.

## Relationships
- **Daz** — Fellow NPC traveler; Daz is suspicious of Sethir and has flagged him to the party
- **The Party** — Traveling companion; functional but not deeply trusted, given Daz's warning

## Arc Score Events
- No explicit arc score changes noted in the source material. Daz's suspicion and the Faerzress sensitivity may be seeds for future triggers.
