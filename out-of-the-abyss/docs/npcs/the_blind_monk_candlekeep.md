# The Blind Monk (Candlekeep)

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Examiner and master-level evaluator at Candlekeep; implied connection to the monastic tradition Zalthir trained under
- **Faction:** Candlekeep (examining body)
- **First Appearance:** Zalthir's formal skills trial at Candlekeep — entered without introduction

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Drive:** Rigorous preservation and honest assessment of monastic technique; appears to hold high standards for practitioners of shadow step and ki discipline
- Blunt, exacting, and unimpressed by reputation — opens the trial with immediate technical criticism and no pleasantries. Demands precision over results, yet is visibly capable of revising his judgment when the evidence demands it. Appears to value honest demonstration over formal compliance, ultimately pivoting from dismissal to genuine engagement when Zalthir earns it.

## History with the Party
1. **Trial Opening:** Entered Zalthir's trial without introduction; immediately catalogued every technical flaw in Zalthir's shadow step — skipped third breath, ki bleeding into fingertips instead of rooted in the hara, errors in every scroll studied.
2. **First Pivot:** Noted, almost as an aside, that Zalthir arrived at his destination three seconds faster than the Grandmaster — signaling something beneath the flaws worth examining.
3. **Spar:** Permitted Zalthir to attempt a punch; they sparred to an even result.
4. **Formal Skills Failures:** Presided over three consecutive failures — Weightless Shadow (Zalthir fell), Efficient Strike (poor execution, noted Zalthir had not practiced forms while traveling), and Eldritch Claw stealth attempt.
5. **Second Pivot:** Began walking away; stopped when Zalthir landed a hit of 30 against him. This visibly impressed him and prompted him to reconsider his approach.
6. **Brick Challenge:** Set a final challenge — threw a brick for Zalthir to intercept mid-air via shadow step. Zalthir destroyed it on a roll of 29. The monk acknowledged the results spoke for themselves.
7. **Closing Exchange:** Asked whether any lesson might be offered; Zalthir answered with shadow tentacles. The monk responded that *the way forward is in the combination.* Participated in the final demonstration of Zalthir's Underdark-developed shadow-strike technique and was present for Khell-Vire's concluding assessment.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Candlekeep, present at the conclusion of Zalthir's trial
- **Active Plans:** None confirmed; role appears to have been examiner for this specific trial
- **Party Knows:** He is a high-level evaluator of monastic/shadow technique, blind, capable of sparring evenly with Zalthir, and ultimately endorsed the path of combining traditions
- **Hidden:** His name, full rank, institutional role beyond this trial, and his relationship to the Grandmaster referenced in his critique are all unknown

## Relationships
- **Zalthir:** Examiner and reluctant-but-genuine acknowledger of Zalthir's talent; moved from contempt to measured respect across a single trial
- **Khell-Vire:** Present alongside him for the concluding assessment — implied shared evaluating authority
- **The Grandmaster (unnamed):** Referenced as a benchmark — Zalthir surpassed the Grandmaster's shadow step speed by three seconds, which the monk noted without explanation

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase (Zalthir → Monk):** 30-to-hit landing after the monk began walking away — visibly changed the monk's posture toward the trial
- **Increase (Zalthir → Monk):** Brick destroyed on roll of 29 — monk explicitly acknowledged the results and opened a genuine instructional exchange
- **Decrease (early trial):** Three consecutive formal skill failures; monk declared Zalthir had learned nothing and appeared to be moving toward dismissal
