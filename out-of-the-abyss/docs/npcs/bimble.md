# Bimble

## Identity
- Scout in the employ of Kazook Pickshine
- Encountered by the party strapped to a table in Kazook's chambers, after returning from a scouting mission gone wrong

## Personality & Motivations
- Little is known of Bimble's original personality; by the time the party met him, he was delirious and infected. His ravings suggest he was once a capable scout willing to venture into dangerous territory on Kazook's behalf. In his compromised state, he spoke reverently and obsessively about "the Lady of Rot" and a garden that "sang to him."

## History with the Party
- **Scouting Mission:** Kazook sent Bimble deep into the rock blight to investigate a "garden of stone" where rare Ghost Hold Lichen could be found. He located the lichen but returned infected and mentally broken.
- **Encounter in Kazook's Chambers:** The party found Bimble strapped to a table, coughing up spores that formed tiny laughing faces before dissolving. He raved about the singing garden and "the Lady of Rot feeding the soil."
- **Intelligence Gathered:** Through careful investigation—analyzing his ramblings, the smells clinging to him, and the textures of rock under his fingers—the party successfully pinpointed the exact location of the cave he had visited.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Kazook's chambers, strapped to a table.
- **Condition:** Severely infected with some fungal or spore-based affliction; delirious and physically deteriorating.
- **What the Party Knows:** The cave's location (derived from Bimble's state), the existence of Ghost Hold Lichen there, and references to "the Lady of Rot."
- **What Remains Hidden:** The true nature of the Lady of Rot, what exactly infected Bimble, and whether his condition is reversible.

## Relationships
- **Kazook Pickshine:** Employer. Kazook sent him on the mission and was keeping him restrained, presumably to contain the infection or extract information.
- **"The Lady of Rot":** An entity Bimble encountered or became aware of in the garden of stone. He spoke of her with a disturbing reverence, suggesting possible psychic or fungal influence.
