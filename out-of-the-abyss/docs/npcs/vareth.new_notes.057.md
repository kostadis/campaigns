# New notes for Vareth (from dossier_extract_057.md)

Vareth, identified as having worked at Candlekeep for forty-one years, served as Grygum's host and guide in the West Inner Ward. He escorted Grygum through the Immortal Chambers with institutional ease, displaying practiced patience — most notably during the extended encounter with Pizwog, which cost him something visible though he remained respectful throughout.

In his side office, Vareth delivered a prepared overview of the great theological controversy currently consuming Candlekeep: three schools of thought contending over the meaning of Stroud's chess victory over Protanther. He laid out the Stroudite school (mortal choice is sovereign), the Bahamutian school (divine providence moved the pieces), and the Draconic school (the dragons permitted the scope of mortal choosing). He framed the underlying question as cosmological: what kind of universe do we live in? The controversy had produced three duels, institutional chaos, and standing threats from Sister Yvenne over bookplates.

When Grygum proposed a fourth school — fixed points around which choices are constrained, with freedom between them — Vareth's response was a moment of genuine panic before pivoting to excitement. He elaborated the theory himself, connecting it to a god who might control outcomes not through compulsion but through shaping the architecture of evidence and argument. He named Grygum a "weak Stroudite" and both a "strong Bahamutian" (near fixed points) and "weak Bahamutian" (away from them), christening the framework the strong-weak hypothesis. He immediately prepared to begin his own research, barely able to contain himself as he handed Grygum a stack of Stroudite papers and rushed out. He promised Grygum a marked-up version.

Last known whereabouts: departing his office at speed to begin research on what he was treating as a new school of theological thought.
