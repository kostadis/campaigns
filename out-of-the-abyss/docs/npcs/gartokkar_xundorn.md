# Gartokkar Xundorn

## Identity
- **Role:** Custodian of the Flame; chief of the Keepers of the Flame
- **Race:** Duergar priest
- **Faction:** Keepers of the Flame — the faction responsible for tending the red dragon Themberchaud, who heats the stones powering Gracklstugh's furnaces. Described as the most prized of the Deepking's advisors.
- **First Appearance:** Materialized behind Zalthir after the battle with the crazed stone giant, offering the party a deal for safe passage through the city.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Retrieve the stolen red dragon egg (the Keepers' insurance policy against Themberchaud). Obtain the statue of Demogorgon to convince the Deepking to wage genocidal war against the Derro. Maintain and expand the Keepers' political power.
- Gartokkar is calculating, authoritative, and manipulative. He frames coerced arrangements as willing agreements ("delighted" the party accepted). He carries a pre-prepared list of legal charges against party members, indicating meticulous planning. He responds to threats with measured menace rather than bluster — turning dark before smiling and moving on. He does have a dry sense of humor, laughing at Grygum's joke about kindling and noting he hadn't heard a good one in a long time.

## History with the Party
1. **First Contact:** Appeared after the stone giant battle. Offered safe passage and protection from imprisonment in exchange for a service. Gave a one-day ultimatum and vanished.
2. **Themberchaud's Cavern:** Attempted to brief the party at a building outside the dragon's cavern but was interrupted by Themberchaud's arrival. Introduced the dragon with great reverence; was visibly displeased when dismissed by the dragon but complied.
3. **Private Briefing:** In his chambers, revealed the Keepers' political dynamics — their power rests on psionics, the Deepking's leadership, and control over Themberchaud. Disclosed the conspiracy: Themberchaud thinks the Keepers serve him, but in reality he serves them. Explained the Gray Ghosts stole the replacement dragon egg, which he described as creating "a great disturbance in the Underdark." Tasked the party with recovering the egg and finding proof of Gray Ghost involvement. Issued travel credentials (holy symbols of Laduguer and gold pins with Themberchaud's profile). When Thorin threatened refusal, Gartokkar read off eight pre-prepared legal charges; when Thorin countered by threatening to reveal the egg to the Stone Guards, Gartokkar turned dark but ultimately smiled and dismissed it.
4. **Flumph Encounter:** The party brought a flumph to him; it trauma-dumped on him. He seemed "curiously relieved" by the experience.
5. **Payment Negotiation:** Agreed to 2,000 gold for the job plus a 1,000-gold confidentiality rider, 30% upfront. Resisted the party's attempt to raise the upfront share to half. Delivered a direct threat: *"If you betray me, there is no cave, no pool of still water, no river, no place in the dark that I will not find you and hunt you down."*

## Current Status
- **Location:** Keepers of the Flame headquarters, Gracklstugh.
- **Active Operations:** Waiting on the party to retrieve the dragon egg and the Demogorgon statue from the Whorlestone Caves. Cannot enter the caves himself without revealing the egg's loss — a crucial pillar of his power.
- **Party Knows:** The egg was stolen by the Gray Ghosts; Droki is a Gray Ghost agent; the Keepers secretly control Themberchaud; Gartokkar considers the Council of Savants his worst enemies and is in a secret war with them; he intends to use the Demogorgon statue to push for war against the Derro.
- **Hidden:** What the Keepers actually do when Themberchaud "moves on" (Gartokkar claimed the dragon leaves to see the world — Grygum compared this to telling children their pet "left" when it died, implying the Keepers kill and replace the dragon).

## Relationships
- **Themberchaud:** Publicly serves the dragon with reverence; privately views the dragon as serving the Keepers. The egg is their contingency plan.
- **The Deepking:** Positions the Keepers as the Deepking's most valued advisors. Leverages this relationship for authority (his badge causes other Duergar to flee).
- **Council of Savants:** Considers them his worst enemies; engaged in a secret war.
- **Gray Ghosts:** Hostile — they stole the egg and humiliated the Keepers.
- **The Party:** Treats them as coerced assets. Pragmatic but threatening. Laughed at Grygum's joke. Has a particularly adversarial dynamic with Thorin, against whom he holds prepared legal leverage.
