# Buppido

## Identity
- Derro, former prisoner at Velkynvelve
- Self-proclaimed divine figure who genuinely believed himself to be a god
- Originally from Gracklstugh; affiliated with no faction, though had past business ties to the Council of Savants
- First met as a fellow prisoner in the slave pen at Velkynvelve

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goal: fulfill "the divine plan," which ultimately meant building a shrine of humanoid remains and being worshipped. He also expressed a desire to save the derro people from the failing Council of Savants.
- Presented as personable, gregarious, and talkative — a charming exterior masking genuine madness. Self-serving to the core; his initial escape plan used the singular "I escape" before being corrected. Willing to sacrifice others (proposed using Runc as a distraction, later murdered Yukyuk and Pelek). Called himself "more of a big-picture thinker." Deflected all accountability onto "the divine plan." Could be insightful and useful — correctly predicted dangers, knew Gracklstugh's layout and history — but was ultimately homicidal and delusional.

## History with the Party
1. **Velkynvelve**: Met as a prisoner. Urged escape, proposed sacrificing others as distractions. Befriended Grygum over shared interest in the divine. Thorin manipulated him by framing the party as sworn protectors of the divine plan. Advocated escaping to Gracklstugh.
2. **Escape & Underdark Travel**: Warned about giant spiders. Fought alongside the group against a giant centipede (killed it with Sarith via crossbow bolts). Showed interest in trail-obscuring techniques. Wagered with Jimjar about survival. Consistently pushed Gracklstugh as the destination. Made cryptic, unsettling comments to Zalthir about including him in the divine plan.
3. **Silken Paths & Gorge**: Questioned Sarith about the Silken Paths despite already knowing the answers. Silently directed Zalthir to notice a climbable ceiling. Persuaded the group to dispose of corpses in a gorge to avoid trackers. Correctly predicted a dead bandit had companions nearby.
4. **Murder of Yukyuk**: Identified as Yukyuk's killer. Blood found on his shortsword. Deflected all questioning, attributing the death to "the divine plan." Daz cast Mind Sliver on him twice; Buppido continued to evade. Daz concluded he was genuinely mad. Party gagged and bound him. Shuushar subjected him to endless lectures on "Kuo-toan-fulness."
5. **Sloobludop**: Fled with the group during the kuo-toa civil war, still restrained.
6. **Approach to Gracklstugh**: Party debated killing him. Under Tongue of Madness (truth compulsion), he revealed he wanted to return to his bolt hole to praise the divine plan. Insulted every party member. Zalthir said "I like him." Party decided to release him rather than kill him.
7. **Ghohlbrorn's Lair**: Kept bound in a back room. Provided intel on Droki (a trusted courier for Whorlstone Caverns groups). Raged about the Council of Savants failing the derro people.
8. **Gracklstugh / Laduguer's Furrow**: Guided the party to the western gate, explaining duergar-derro history. His presence shielded the party from hostility in the derro district.
9. **Whorlstone Caverns — Death**: Rushed away from the party upon entering the caverns. Found in a southwestern cave with a shrine of humanoid remains arranged in a spiral pattern. Declared himself divine and attacked with skeletons. **Killed by Daz with magic missiles.** Thorin eulogized: "He was a crazy one, but he was our crazy one."

## Current Status
- **Dead.** Killed by Daz in the Whorlstone Caverns.
- His death is referenced as one of the party's accumulated traumas from Gracklstugh.
- A flumph in the caverns considered Buppido a friend and did not know he was dead.

## Relationships
- **Daz**: Interrogated him after Yukyuk's murder; cast Mind Sliver on him; ultimately killed him. Daz later used Buppido's complaints about the Council of Savants to inform his own political analysis.
- **Thorin**: Manipulated Buppido by framing the party as sworn to protect him. Delivered his eulogy.
- **Zalthir**: Complex dynamic — Buppido made cryptic comments about including him in the divine plan; Zalthir wondered if Buppido was the demon a Spectator warned about; yet Zalthir also said "I like him."
- **Grygum/Gyrgum**: Bonded over shared interest in gods, though Grygum recognized Buppido's "divine" claims were genuine delusion. Fell silent at the mention of Buppido's name after his death.
- **Sarith**: Fought alongside him; Buppido declared Sarith's rescue was foretold by the divine plan.
- **Shuushar**: Forced to walk together while Buppido was gagged; Buppido later claimed Shuushar taught him he was mad (possibly insincere).
- **Pelek**: Former business partner. Buppido lured him to the Whorlstone Caverns and murdered him, dismembering his body for the shrine. Pelek became a ghost as a result.
- **Council of Savants**: Former business associates (smuggling product out of the city). Buppido despised them for failing to liberate the derro.
- **Droki**: Knew him as a courier; confirmed Droki could be trusted with messages.
- **The Flumph**: Considered Buppido a friend before he went "stark raving mad."
- **Spiderbait**: Wanted to kill Buppido outright before Gracklstugh.

## Arc Score Events
- **Murder of Yukyuk**: Likely trust/threat escalation — party bound and gagged him.
- **Tongue of Madness confession near Gracklstugh**: Revealed true intentions; party chose mercy over execution.
- **Discovery of the shrine and final battle in Whorlstone Caverns**: Terminal — Buppido revealed as irredeemably insane and hostile; killed by the party.
- **Pelek's revelation (post-mortem)**: Faerzress surge changed Buppido's temperament, triggering his descent into believing he was divine — contextualizes his arc as a victim of the Underdark's madness.
