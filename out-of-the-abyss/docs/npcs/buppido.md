# Buppido

## Identity
Derro, former prisoner at Velkynvelve. Self-proclaimed divine figure who genuinely believed himself to be a god. Originally from Gracklstugh; had ties to the Council of Savants and the Whorlstone Caverns. First met as a fellow prisoner in the Velkynvelve slave pen. *Also known as: Bupido.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Presented as personable, gregarious, and talkative — disarmingly sociable for a derro
- Driven by a belief in his own divinity and "the divine plan," which he used to justify all actions, including murder
- Cunning and manipulative, frequently proposing others be sacrificed while positioning himself as too important to risk
- His madness deepened over time, likely accelerated by Faerzress exposure, ultimately culminating in the construction of a shrine from humanoid remains
- Despite his instability, he was perceptive, knowledgeable about the Underdark, and occasionally genuinely useful
- Secretly worshipped a demonic power and maintained a hidden lair with an altar and collected bones

## History with the Party
- **Velkynvelve:** Befriended the group during escape planning, proposing himself as the priority survivor. Thorin manipulated him by framing the party as pledged protectors of "the divine plan." Suggested sending Runc as a distraction. Showed interest in Grygum as a fellow follower of gods.
- **Prison Escape:** Led the other prisoners in rushing the guard chamber during the distraction created by Thorin, Zalthir, and Daz's confrontation with the guards. Participated in the scramble into the armory and fired a crossbow at the guard Imbros (missed).
- **Escape & Travel:** Warned about giant spiders, fought alongside Sarith against a centipede creature, wagered with Jimjar about survival. Showed interest in Eldeth's trail-obscuring techniques. Consistently advocated traveling to Gracklstugh.
- **Silken Paths & Beyond:** Asked Sarith to explain things he already knew. Expressed doubt about goblin guides. Helped the party notice a climbable ceiling across a gorge. Argued convincingly for disposing of bodies to avoid trackers. Correctly predicted bandits near Timmask mushrooms.
- **Yukyuk's Murder:** Identified as Yukyuk's killer. Deflected all questioning, attributing the death to "the divine plan." Daz found blood on his shortsword and cast Mind Sliver twice; Buppido remained evasive. Daz concluded he was genuinely mad. The party gagged and bound him; Shuushar subjected him to endless lectures.
- **Sloobludop:** Fled with the group during the kuo-toa civil war, still bound.
- **Approach to Gracklstugh:** Pleaded for his life, claiming Shuushar taught him he was mad. Under Tongue of Madness, revealed he wanted to return to his bolt hole to "praise the Divine Plan" and insulted every party member. The party decided to release rather than kill him.
- **Gracklstugh / Ghohlbrorn's Lair:** Provided information about Droki (a courier who moves messages into the Whorlstone Caverns). Raged about the Council of Savants failing the derro people. Guided the party through Laduguer's Furrow, his presence tempering hostility toward non-derro in the district.
- **Whorlstone Caverns — Death:** Rushed away from the party upon entering the caverns. Found in a southwestern cave with a shrine of humanoid remains arranged in a spiral. Declared himself divine and attacked with skeletal minions. **Killed by Daz with magic missiles.** Thorin eulogized him: "He was a crazy one, but he was our crazy one."

## Current Status
**Dead.** Killed by Daz in the Whorlstone Caverns. His death contributed to the party's collective trauma and their decision to leave Gracklstugh quietly. His former lair — a grim space filled with dead bones and an altar to a demonic power — was later used by the party as a resting place before their assault on a cultist cavern. Pelek (his former business partner and one of his victims) later needed his bones interred to find rest as a ghost.

## Relationships
- **Daz:** Primary interrogator after Yukyuk's murder; ultimately his executioner. Daz later reflected on Buppido's complaints about the Council of Savants as genuine insight. Uses Buppido's betrayal to justify caution with new allies.
- **Thorin:** Manipulated Buppido by pledging the party to protect "the divine plan." Delivered his eulogy.
- **Zalthir:** Complex dynamic — Buppido once suggested Zalthir could be "part of the divine plan" (after overhearing ruthless suggestions); Zalthir said "I like him" after Buppido's truth-compelled insults; forced him to eat Tongue of Madness.
- **Grygum:** Buppido showed interest in him as a fellow follower of gods. Grygum recognized Buppido's belief in his own divinity was genuine. Fell silent when Buppido was later mentioned.
- **Sarith:** Declared Sarith was sent by the divine plan to rescue him. Fought alongside him.
- **Shuushar:** Subjected Buppido to lectures on "Kuo-toan-fulness" while he was gagged; Buppido later claimed Shuushar helped him see his madness.
- **Pelek:** Former business partner; Buppido murdered him and dismembered his body for his shrine.
- **The Flumph:** Described Buppido as a former friend who went "stark raving mad"; did not know he was dead.
- **Council of Savants:** Buppido despised them, believing they failed the derro people; had worked with them through Pelek to move product out of the city.
- **Faerzress:** Pelek noted that after the Faerzress glowed brightly months ago, Buppido's temperament changed — he became convinced of his divinity.
- **The Party (general):** Deep distrust stemming from his betrayal. His name is invoked as a cautionary tale.

## Arc Score Events
- **Yukyuk's murder** — trust decrease; party gagged and bound him.
- **Tongue of Madness interrogation near Gracklstugh** — slight thaw; party chose release over execution.
- **Whorlstone Caverns shrine discovery and attack** — final break; killed by the party.
