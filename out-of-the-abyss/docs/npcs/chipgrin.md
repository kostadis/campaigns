# Chief Chipgrin

## Identity
- Leader of the Gold Whisker clan, a faction of wererat gnomes in Blingdenstone
- First encountered by the party seated on a stone chair draped with cave badger pelt, flanked by giant rats and guards, on a plateau of manicured green moss
- Initially assumed the party had been sent by the Diggermattocks to eliminate his clan

## Personality & Motivations
Chipgrin is proud, shrewd, and fiercely protective of his clan's autonomy. He refuses to place his people under Chief Dorbo's command or be treated as expendable. He values bluntness and directness—he was immediately impressed by Daz's straightforward approach. He has a dry, self-deprecating wit (his reaction to Thorin outperforming the spellcasters) and is politically savvy, always angling to secure territory and independence for the Gold Whiskers.

## History with the Party
1. **Initial Meeting:** Chipgrin agreed to fight the ooze threat after Daz took personal accountability for outcomes. He shared critical intelligence about the Pudding King—his history, motivations (vengeance, pride, madness, demonic influence), and connection to Juiblex. He guided the party to realize the demon's corrupting influence was bleeding through and affecting all of Blingdenstone's gnomes (heightened greed and aggression). Attended the grand moot.
2. **Earth Elemental Debate:** Present at the leadership meeting; agreed, under pressure from the party's moral and religious arguments, that elementals should only be summoned for defense and warfare—not labor or mining.
3. **Scouting the Ooze Territory:** Led wererat scouts ahead of the party. When a scout was caught by psychic effects from gelatinous slime walls and a "brain slime," Chipgrin personally investigated and reported the threats. He offered two options; the party chose the loud path (wererats howling to suppress psychic effects), which drew all oozes to the wererats and sacrificed Chipgrin's continued scouting assistance.
4. **Hall of Melting Mirrors:** Found maintaining a chanting vigil with his wererats to suppress lingering psychic static. Asked the party's spellcasters to silence it permanently. When Thorin (the least magical party member) succeeded, Chipgrin deadpanned that he should have asked the dwarf first, then nervously clarified to Zalthir he meant no offense.
5. **Post-Battle Territorial Negotiations:** Argued with Chief Diggermattock over newly opened territory. The party brokered a compromise: the Gold Whiskers received the upper burrows as a self-funded defense force, removing Diggermattock's need to pay for military protection.
6. **Farewell:** Pressed the **Voucher of the Varmint** (a braided rat's foot) into Daz's hand—a token recognized by wererats and outcasts from Blingdenstone to Menzoberranzan as a sign of friendship.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Upper burrows of Blingdenstone, now Gold Whisker territory
- **Operations:** Serving as Blingdenstone's self-funded defense force; consolidating control of the upper boroughs
- **Party Knowledge:** The party knows his full situation; no hidden agendas indicated in the notes

## Relationships
- **Daz:** Strong mutual respect. Daz's bluntness and willingness to take personal accountability won Chipgrin over. Gifted Daz the Voucher of the Varmint—his highest sign of trust.
- **Thorin:** Amused respect after the Hall of Melting Mirrors incident.
- **Zalthir:** Slightly awkward—Chipgrin was careful not to offend him after the Thorin comment.
- **Chief Dorbo Diggermattock:** Antagonistic but functional. Chipgrin refuses to serve under Dorbo and actively competes for territory, though the party's compromise created a workable arrangement.
- **The Pudding King:** Chipgrin knew him personally from his time working in the tunnels, and encountered him again post-transformation. Understands his psychology and the demonic influence driving him.

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase:** Daz taking personal accountability for the clan's safety in the ooze fight.
- **Increase:** Party brokering the upper burrows compromise, granting the Gold Whiskers territory and autonomy.
- **Decrease (potential cost):** Party chose the loud path during scouting, forcing Chipgrin's wererats to absorb the ooze aggression—though he honored the agreement without complaint.
