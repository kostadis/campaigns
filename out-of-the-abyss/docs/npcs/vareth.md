# Vareth

## Identity
- Elderly Avowed scholar of Candlekeep; 41 years in residence
- Office in the **Immortal Chambers** (West Inner Ward) — the theology and religion library, deity-symbol tile floor, green rectangular roofs
- Candlekeep handler assigned to Grygum by the First Reader (Bookwyrm)
- First appeared in play at Chapter 53 (2026-05-18) — "Candlekeep's Cold Case: The Grygumite Gambit"

## Personality & Motivations
- **Good guy.** Generous, collegial, allergic to academic territoriality. Wants Grygum to *succeed*, not to be the source he mines and discards.
- **Career-defining excitement, not credit-hoarding.** Now that Grygum's fixed-points theory has given Vareth the original angle he had been searching for, his instinct is to lift Grygum into the academic mainstream alongside him — co-authorship, joint colloquia, shared platforms — rather than to publish ahead of him or appropriate the idea.
- **Obscure-scholar-thrust-into-relevance arc.** For decades Vareth worked on a question almost nobody else found worth asking: *why would a half-orc want to be a cleric of Bahamut?* Orcs and dragons are traditional enemies, occupy similar ecological niches, and a sufficient mass of orcs can mathematically kill a dragon. The conversion ran against species, against survival, against the whole arc of recorded combat. It was the kind of subject that produced one careful paper every fifteen years and a smattering of baffled marginal notes from the peers who bothered to read it. Then Stroud's chess victory three years ago dragged the whole niche into the centre of the Question of the Age — and Vareth, who had been hunting for an original angle of his own and not finding one, was suddenly the senior expert on the subdiscipline everyone wanted to discuss.
- Honest about the limits of his own work: tells Grygum directly that he does not know the answer to the Question of the Age, only the dossier of the controversy.
- Lights up when he encounters readers who know the obscure primary sources (e.g. Milo Goodbarrel's Volumes 1–3 in his library — *"Most don't anymore. Stroud has eaten the air"*).

## Specialty and the Stroud Wake
- Curriculum he walks visiting scholars through: a 7–8 station tour of the Stroud controversy ("The Stroud Wake"), run from his Immortal Chambers office. Three schools on offer for the visitor to pick between (Stroudite / Bahamutian / Draconic).
- Coined for Grygum: *"weak Stroudian and strong Bahamutian around fixed points."* The phrase is now his working label for what he is calling the **strong-weak hypothesis** — and what the wider world will soon know as the **Grygumite School**.
- Holds onto Milo Goodbarrel's Volumes 1–3 as a quieter, less-argued primary source category for visitors from the Order of the Gauntlet.

## Plans for Grygum (collaborative, not extractive)
Vareth will, given any opening, want to propose:
- **Joint papers** on the strong-weak hypothesis — first authorship to Grygum, Vareth as theological framer
- **A book** building out the fourth school — co-authored or with Grygum as primary
- **Colloquia at Candlekeep** with the three school's standing advocates invited to debate the new framework
- **A standing position for Grygum at Candlekeep** (visiting reader, fellowship, or formal appointment) so the Grygumite School has institutional home
- **A conference** drawing scholars from Waterdeep, Triboar, Beregost, and the wider Sword Coast — Stroud himself if he can be coaxed
- **Funding proposals** to the Lords' Alliance and through the Avowed for travel, research stipends, copying costs for distribution of the founding papers

All of this is offered in good faith. If Grygum declines any of it, Vareth will not push.

## History with the Party
1. **Chapter 53 (2026-05-18) — First sitting in the Immortal Chambers.** Vareth led Grygum through the West Inner Ward to his office, pressed a copy of one of Pizwog's syncretic religion tracts into Grygum's hands along the way, and laid out the three schools of thought consuming the Question of the Age. Grygum proposed the fixed-points theory; Vareth electrified, coined the strong-weak labelling, declared a potential fourth school, and pressed his collection of Stroudite notes and papers into Grygum's hands as homework. Left to begin his own research; intends to be a collaborator, not a competitor.
