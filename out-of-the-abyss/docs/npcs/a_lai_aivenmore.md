# A'lai Aivenmore

## Identity
- **Role:** Great Reader; member of the Candlekeep council
- **Affiliation:** Candlekeep
- **First Appearance:** Chapter House meeting following the discovery of Janussi's body; introduced as one of the Great Readers present during the crisis

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Restoring formal leadership to Candlekeep; resolving the current situation quickly and cleanly
- A'lai is politically minded and pragmatic — he opened the council meeting with a motion to restore Candlekeep's leadership structure before any other business. He favors efficiency over sentiment: he withheld endorsement of the party not out of opposition, but because declining to object was the fastest path forward. His smooth presentation and careful distinctions suggest a man who chooses his words with deliberate precision.

## History with the Party
- **Discovery of the Body:** A'lai and housekeeper Miss Hollypocket found Janussi's body around 7:30 AM. He was among the first people Kalan spoke with upon arriving at 8:00 AM.
- **Chapter House Meeting:** A'lai moved to restore leadership to Candlekeep before the investigation was addressed. When the vote on delegating the investigation to the party arose, he did not endorse them but chose not to object — framing it as deferring to Bookwyrm's judgment rather than expressing personal confidence in the party.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The Chapter House, Candlekeep
- **Active Situation:** Pressing for a leadership restoration vote within the council; observing how the investigation unfolds
- **Party Knowledge vs. Hidden:** The party knows he co-discovered the body and holds a clear political agenda around restoring Candlekeep's leadership structure. What motivates that agenda — and whether it connects to Janussi's death — remains unknown.

## Relationships
- **Miss Hollypocket:** Jointly discovered Janussi's body with her; first point of contact for Kalan
- **Bookwyrm:** Publicly deferred to Bookwyrm's judgment on the investigation, though without offering genuine support
- **The Party:** No endorsement given; non-objection only. Grygum specifically noted the distinction as politically significant
