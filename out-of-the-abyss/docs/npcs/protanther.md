# Protanther

## Identity
- Gold Dragon
- Connected to the events at the Well of the Dragons
- Referenced in historical/legendary context; no direct appearance before the party

## Personality & Motivations
- A being of immense intellect and patience, having studied a single chess board for 400 years. Whether his famous defeat was a genuine oversight or a deliberate sacrifice remains debated — but either interpretation speaks to a creature whose motivations operate on a scale far beyond mortal understanding.

## History with the Party
- No direct interaction with the party. Known only through the story of the Well of the Dragons.

## Current Status
- **Last known status:** Unknown; referenced only in historical narrative.
- **What the party knows:** The story of his chess defeat at the hands of the Dwarf Stroud and its theological implications.

## Key Lore
- Protanther was defeated in chess by the Dwarf **Stroud** after studying the board for 400 years.
- Many chess grandmasters were dumbfounded that he did not see the losing move coming.
- **Interpretation 1:** Those who believe in the intrinsic goodness of Gold Dragons hold that Protanther *chose* to lose deliberately, seeing an opening to side with those who opposed **Tiamat**, and framing the loss as a sign from the gods.
- **Interpretation 2:** Others believe **Bahamut** hid the move from Protanther.
- His defeat became a pivotal event that led to **Bahamut** gaining a wider following among non-dragon faithful.

## Relationships
- **Stroud** (Dwarf): Defeated Protanther in the famous chess match.
- **Bahamut:** Potentially intervened on Protanther's behalf (or through him); the defeat expanded Bahamut's worship.
- **Tiamat:** Protanther's loss is framed as an act in opposition to Tiamat by some believers.
