# New notes for Stroud (from dossier_extract_056.md)

Stroud does not appear in person in this chunk. He is referenced in connection with two monuments. The Tarvis Ulain monument in Daggerford's market square bears a secondary inscription in Dwarven — *The move is yours* — which Daz identifies as sharing language with the Castle Ward statue in Waterdeep previously connected to Stroud. The village priest acknowledges the monument was funded by a Metalworkers Guild that may have had Stroudite members, and explains the Stroudite inscription was retained because it was a gift and because Tarvis was, after all, trying to save everyone regardless of what happened in the chess game. At the Burning Wizard Inn, Grygum pays the resident bard a silver piece to sing something about Stroud's famous chess game. She produces a love song in which the chess game serves as a metaphor for romantic longing, stripping the theological stakes down to something more singable. Grygum receives this with polite resignation and observes these things must be taken with a grain of salt. The chess game itself and the question of what the Stroudite phrase means on a Bahamutian monument remain unresolved.
