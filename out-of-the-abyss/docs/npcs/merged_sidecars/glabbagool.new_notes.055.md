# New notes for Glabbagool (from dossier_extract_055.md)

Glabbagool is present at the tunnel mouth and experiences sunlight for the first time. He looks up at the sun with a stillness that is distinguished from his usual floor-like passivity — he is witnessed as genuinely witnessing. Zalthir watches him during this moment. Glabbagool is the subject of a discussion about potential monk training Zalthir has apparently offered in some sense, though "offered" is contested — Zalthir's expression indicates the word is doing significant work. Glabbagool has strong feelings about a curriculum involving "run, run, slap, slap, slap." Thorin notes Glabbagool's tactical potential: no fixed appendages, unpredictable, potentially twelve simultaneous strikes per round. During the bird-call signal sequence, Glabbagool attempts a bird impression that is completely unrecognizable — described as the sound a bag of wet sand makes when dropped from height while being squeezed — and is very proud of it. He asks whether birds are a kind of ooze. Grygum tells him they are feathered and promises to introduce him to birds. After the drow spy is captured and miniaturized, Glabbagool is assigned to watch her in the bag of holding. He performs this duty with absolute singleness of purpose — one eye locked on the prisoner without breaking stride, described as the most earnest thing Zalthir has seen in weeks. During the interrogation, Glabbagool demonstrates his actual seeing capacity: he explains that his googly eyes are affectations he adopted because he discovered that cute eyes make people treat him more friendly, and that he can see in different ways as ooze do. To demonstrate authority over the spy, he begins proliferating eyes, which rapidly becomes very creepy. At Candlekeep, he is assigned to keep the shrunken spy under control as a goal. Thorin proposes that Glabbagool receive his own question to ask the monks, given he donated a day's worth of bag-of-holding service. Glabbagool is reportedly deeply confused about why a library with effectively infinite capacity imposes any limit on questions at all — his position is that every book ever seen should be donated and questions asked until the end of time. Zalthir notes that Glabbagool has been carried in a bag for much of his above-ground existence with no way to establish what is normal above ground. Last known status: inside or near the bag of holding at Candlekeep, watching the shrunken spy.
