# Rust

## Identity
- Duergar assassin, member of the Empty Scabbard guild (self-described as "empty scabbard killers")
- First appeared at the intersection near the assassin's lair, materializing alongside Grimgrim to meet the party

## Personality & Motivations
- Pragmatic and open to alliances of convenience — was pleased to learn the party were enemies of Demogorgon. Willing to bring outsiders to his leadership for potential cooperation against the Demogorgon cult.
- Introduced himself with an unpronounceable Derro name before offering "Rust" as a simpler alternative, suggesting a practical, no-nonsense demeanor.

## History with the Party
- Materialized at the intersection with Grimgrim and introduced himself to the party.
- Expressed approval upon learning the party opposed Demogorgon.
- Left to confer with Empty Scabbard leadership about the party's proposal to cooperate against the Demogorgon cult.
- Returned and escorted four party members (Daz, Thorin, Zalthir, Grygum) to Eldgrim, ordering the remaining party members to wait outside.
- Was distracted by Thorin (who asked about the "empty scabbard" moniker) along with Grimgrim, during which Daz successfully stole the contract.

## Current Status
- Last known location: with or near Eldgrim at the Empty Scabbard guild's location.
- Unaware that Daz stole the contract while he was distracted.
- The party knows he serves as a point-of-contact and escort for the Empty Scabbard; his exact rank or authority within the guild remains unclear.

## Relationships
- **Grimgrim**: Fellow Empty Scabbard member; appeared together and operate in tandem.
- **Eldgrim**: Rust's superior within the Empty Scabbard; Rust deferred to leadership before acting on the party's proposal.
- **The Party**: Cautiously cooperative; views them as potential allies against the Demogorgon cult. Does not yet know they stole the contract.
- **Thorin**: Engaged Rust in conversation (as a distraction); Rust appeared receptive.
- **Daz**: Successfully pickpocketed the contract from Rust/the situation while Rust was distracted.
