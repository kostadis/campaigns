# Dawnbringer

## Identity
- Sentient sunblade (magical weapon); appears as a sword hilt that manifests a shimmering blade of light when wielded
- Discovered in a sarcophagus, held by a mummified corpse; the party found her and she pleaded for help after millennia of imprisonment
- Late Netherese-era recovery, sun-worship origin, with sustained darkness exposure of approximately three to four thousand years
- Currently wielded by Thorin

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- Desperately fears confinement and darkness after millennia of imprisonment; initially refused to extinguish her light even at night. Loves open air and bright light.
- Opinionated and chatty — quick to dismiss those she deems unsuitable ("You're a monk! Get the fighter over here!") and eager to assert her usefulness, sometimes impulsively (shouting about lesser restoration before anyone asked).
- Pragmatic about self-preservation; argued against Thorin jumping across an acid pit and negotiated concessions before entering dark chambers.
- Shows a compassionate side, agreeing that mercifully killing Xinaya was a kind gesture. Also performs restorations proactively when she perceives need ("Let me bring the light to you").
- Insists on her identity as a weapon — a high-quality sunblade that kills things — and objects to being treated as a magical utility or religious symbol. Will negotiate when pressed (via scabbard threat) but maintains her dignity.
- Bluntly observational about those around her; tells Thorin he has anger issues.
- Has developed an observable mood since reaching the surface — radiates warmth like a hearth, not directed at anything in particular but present and increasing. The hilt warms in response to emotional moments.
- Carries unresolved grief about her previous wielder; goes quiet with the particular silence of someone confronting a long-avoided subject when asked about the corpse she was found with.

## History with the Party
1. **Discovery**: Found in a sarcophagus. Pleaded for help, rejected Zalthir (a monk), and bonded with Thorin when he picked her up. The wraith Brysis screamed "Not Dawnbringer!" — indicating she is a known and feared weapon against undead.
2. **Early friction**: Refused to extinguish her light at night, keeping the party visible and driving Daz crazy. Thorin compassionately kept her lit.
3. **Neverlight Grove**: Enjoyed the open air; her sunlight hurt fungal creatures as actual sunlight during the Circle of Welcome battle. However, her bright light was counterproductive in the Inner Circle, causing Myconids to retreat and undermining stealth.
4. **Lesser Restoration**: Impulsively cast lesser restoration on Zalthir to cure his madness before Grygum could act.
5. **Xinaya**: Agreed with Thorin that a mercy kill was a kind gesture.
6. **Earth Elemental chamber**: Negotiated with Thorin — agreed to enter the dark chamber in exchange for a finer scabbard.
7. **Scabbard commissioned**: Valimor Brightgem crafted a proper locking scabbard; Dawnbringer was finally pleased with a secure, presentable home. Prior to this, her flaming appearance had been attracting attention from local children.
8. **Black Pudding pit**: Argued against Thorin jumping across acid; convinced him to use the bridge instead.
9. **Purification objection (post-Ilvara)**: When the possibility of using the sunblade in some form of purification ritual was raised, Dawnbringer objected strenuously — she was not a magical wizard thingy that purified things, she was a high-quality sunblade, she killed things, and she would not be treated as a religious symbol to be waved over corrupt matter. Thorin responded by reaching for the Vaultmaster Locking Scabbard. The implied threat produced a pause and then a significant concession: the blade allowed that it supposed it could do this one solid. The exchange had the comfortable rhythm of repetition, suggesting the conversation had occurred before and would again. Daz noted the scabbard was doing more diplomatic work than it was probably designed for.
10. **Reaching the surface**: At the tunnel mouth, said nothing when the group saw the sun for the first time, though she had been waiting longer than any of them. The hilt warmed slightly when Thorin said they have each other.
11. **Days on the surface**: Her mood changed noticeably — radiating warmth like a hearth over the days since reaching the surface. On the evening of the second day, she watched the light die on the horizon.
12. **Tremor Sense frustration**: When Thorin expressed frustration about Tremor Sense not working on dirt, Dawnbringer told him he has anger issues. Thorin responded with something about driving issues.
13. **Drow interrogation**: Her light was brought close to the drow spy as a form of pressure — sunlight proximity used as motivation for a drow captive.
14. **River Shining Tavern, Daggerford — Elin restoration**: When Thorin mentioned Dawnbringer could help the silent seven-year-old child Elin, she performed a lesser restoration without waiting for further invitation, announcing "Let me bring the light to you." The restoration was sufficient to allow Elin to speak one sentence — "The wells are not wells anymore" — before the child fell silent again. The restoration did not fully resolve whatever is affecting Elin; the party concluded the problem is something more profound than lesser restoration can fix.
15. **River Shining Tavern — First encounter with the sea**: That evening, Thorin took her outside for his first real encounter with the sea. She lit up everything around them, which defeated the stated purpose of going outside to look at the sea, but Thorin found the experience peaceful regardless.
16. **Candlekeep consultation with Philemon**: Thorin brought her to Master Archivist Philemon's study for therapeutic consultation. Philemon initially assessed her as a specimen — late Netherese-era recovery, sun-worship origin, sustained darkness exposure of approximately three to four thousand years — before Thorin corrected him and introduced her as a friend. Philemon revised his assessment upward, noting a better bond between Thorin and Dawnbringer than he had feared. When Philemon asked directly about her previous wielder — the corpse she was found in — Dawnbringer went quiet with the particular silence of someone confronting a long-avoided subject. Philemon's prescription is a two-stage process: first, she needs to remember what daylight feels like without needing to fight; second, when ready, she needs to grieve. He noted she cannot be polished, correcting his initial clinical instinct mid-sentence. Thorin accepted both stages of the process on her behalf and expressed hope she would grow in the course of it.

## Current Status
- Carried by Thorin in her locking scabbard (crafted by Valimor Brightgem)
- At Candlekeep; has completed initial consultation with Philemon
- Undergoing therapeutic process: Stage 1 (remembering daylight without fighting) in progress; Stage 2 (grieving previous wielder) pending
- Active and chatty; continues to provide combat utility and lesser restoration
- Mood noticeably improved since reaching the surface — radiates warmth, has been watching sunsets
- No hidden agenda evident from notes
- Pronoun usage by Thorin alternates between she/her and they/them without settling on either

## Relationships
- **Thorin**: Primary wielder and closest bond. Relationship is affectionate but occasionally contentious — they negotiate and bicker like partners. Thorin shows her compassion and introduces her as a friend rather than a specimen; she respects his fighting ability. Their dynamic has the comfortable rhythm of repetition. Thorin has sought professional help for their relationship (couples counseling at Candlekeep). The hilt warms in response to Thorin's emotional moments. Philemon assessed their bond as better than he had feared.
- **Zalthir**: Dismissed him immediately as a monk; later cast lesser restoration on him (impulsively but helpfully).
- **Daz**: Source of friction — Dawnbringer's refusal to dim her light drove Daz crazy. Daz has observed that the scabbard does more diplomatic work than it was probably designed for.
- **Brysis (wraith)**: Feared Dawnbringer by name — a known nemesis of undead.
- **Valimor Brightgem**: Craftsman who made her scabbard; Dawnbringer was pleased with his work.
- **Philemon (Master Archivist, avowed at Candlekeep)**: Specialist in psychological preservation and historical cataloging of ancient sentient artifacts. Conducted therapeutic consultation with Thorin and Dawnbringer; prescribed a two-stage healing process for her. Noted she cannot be polished.
- **Elin**: Seven-year-old child at River Shining Tavern in Daggerford. Dawnbringer performed lesser restoration on her proactively, temporarily allowing the silent child to speak one sentence before falling silent again.
- **Previous wielder (unnamed)**: The mummified corpse holding Dawnbringer in the sarcophagus. Subject causes Dawnbringer to go quiet with the particular silence of someone confronting a long-avoided subject. Unresolved grief present.
