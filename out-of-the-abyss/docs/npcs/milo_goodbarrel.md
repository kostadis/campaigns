# Milo Goodbarrel

## Identity
- Author and memoirist; wrote a book detailing events surrounding "the war of the dragon"
- Has never appeared in person; known entirely through his written works, which Daz carries and references frequently

## Personality & Motivations
- A prolific writer whose daily journaling habit was publicly praised by at least one commentator. His works blend historical accounts with vivid character portraits, covering topics as varied as psychic blade attacks, metallic dragon diplomacy, and shrewd negotiators. His writing is practical and narrative-driven enough to serve as both entertainment and tactical reference.

## History with the Party
- **Pre-campaign / Ongoing:** Daz obtained a copy of Milo's book at some point before or during the adventure. The book has become a key personal resource for Daz.
- Daz adopted Milo's daily writing practice after reading a commentator's praise of it, mentally composing journal entries since his escape from Velkynvelve.
- Daz referenced Milo's book during a negotiation with **Werz**, citing a passage about a character named **Ezra** who always claimed he couldn't pay but always could — using this as leverage to extract additional payment.
- Daz has also drawn on the book's content regarding **psychic blade attacks** that leave no trace (including a scene where a Kenku named **Whistler** faked an attack using psychic blades) and passages about **chess playing a significant role in negotiating with metallic dragons**.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Unknown; has not appeared in person.
- **Relevance:** Exists purely as an intellectual influence on Daz. His book continues to serve as a source of tactical insight, negotiation strategies, and lore that Daz applies in real situations.
- **What the party knows:** Only what Daz has shared or read aloud from the book. No one has met Milo or knows his current whereabouts.

## Relationships
- **Daz:** Primary connection. Daz is a devoted reader of Milo's work, treats the book as a practical reference guide, and credits Milo (via a commentator) as the inspiration for his journaling habit.
- No known relationships with other NPCs or factions.
