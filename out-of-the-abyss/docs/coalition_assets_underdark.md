# Non-Gauntlgrym Coalition Assets (Underdark)

Supplement to `docs/gauntlgrym/gauntlgrym_asset_table.md`. Three Underdark factions with distinct histories and distinct asset shapes. They are **not** a unified coalition — each one is a separate negotiation, with its own contact, its own cost structure, and its own political exposure.

## Quick Rules

- Standard asset rules apply (see `docs/gauntlgrym/faction_asset_network.md`): up to 2 assets per operation (Entry + Pivot), each asset starts as Ready, Heat-linked exposure can push to Strained / Burned.
- Each asset here has its own **unique** activation cost tuned to the faction's character. These are not interchangeable with Gauntlgrym assets.
- Availability states are different per faction — see the "Gate" column.

## Per-Faction Design Notes

### Blingdenstone (Senni Diggermattock)

**Favor bank**: heavily in the party's favor. The party destroyed the Pudding King, brokered the cross-faction peace between Dorbo's miners and the labor faction, released Entemoch's spirits from forced elemental slavery, and reopened Blingdenstone for surface trade. Senni specifically credits them with "giving us back our people" (the labor faction's autonomy).

**Why Senni, not Dorbo**: Dorbo is profit-driven and mining-focused; Senni holds credibility with the worker population and the Stoneheart Enclave, and she decisively overrode Dorbo on the Earth Elemental slavery question. For an asset that *summons released elementals*, Senni is the only authority whose word will hold.

**Why the treaty ask**: Blingdenstone's strategic problem is that surface powers and Menzoberranzan both view them as a resource, not a polity. Senni's political goal is formal sovereign recognition *before* the Menzoberranzan finale, so that Blingdenstone's position in post-collapse Underdark politics is not assumed to be a vacuum. The ask is high because calling Entemoch's spirits into a drow city is, in her words, "giving the elementals we freed a battle they may not return from." She will not trade that for anything short of her people's sovereignty.

**Scene tone**: practical, warm, clear-eyed. Senni is not suspicious of the party — they earned her trust. She is suspicious of the *coalition* the party is assembling. "Dwarves, gnomes, whoever else you pull into this — they'll all want something after. I want ours written down now, while my people still have the leverage of being the ones you asked."

### Shuushar's Reformists (Shuushar the Awakened, via courier)

**Favor bank**: mixed — but Shuushar himself remains friendly to the party. The Sloobludop betrayal was Ploopploopeen's, not Shuushar's; Shuushar left the party voluntarily, on good terms, to spread the Reformist doctrine. The Reformists have grown since — a quiet movement among kuo-toan slaves, particularly in cities where kuo-toan labor is invisible to the ruling class. Menzoberranzan is one of those cities.

**Why Shuushar is reachable by courier, not in person**: Shuushar now wanders the Darklake teaching pacifist doctrine. He is not stationary. Contact is via kuo-toan messengers at known still-water shrines. A courier carries the party's ask; Shuushar either sends activation instructions back, or he does not. Turnaround: one to three days.

**Why the asset is narrow**: the Reformists are ideologically non-violent. They cannot sabotage, strike, or ward-break. What they *can* do is be **invisible** — drow surveillance and magical scrying pass over kuo-toan slaves without registering them, because slaves are below Orthodoxy attention. This is a protection/access asset, not a combat asset. Its value scales with the fact that no other coalition member has this particular invisibility.

**Why the pacifist constraint is absolute**: the Reformists are a pacifist movement *first*. If the party uses the Underground during an operation where they kill a kuo-toan, the movement's leadership will see the party as indistinguishable from drow slavers. The asset is gone, Shuushar cuts contact, and any downstream Reformist aid (Darklake extraction, kuo-toan translation, etc.) also burns.

**The Extraction Tithe**: every use creates one liberated kuo-toan slave whom the party is responsible for. Cumulatively, this is a growing noncombatant obligation. By the finale, the party may be escorting a dozen liberated slaves, which creates its own pressure on evacuation logistics.

**Scene tone**: the Reformists are the only coalition faction whose aid cannot be activated by transaction. It must be *requested*, and Shuushar may decline if the request feels wrong to him. "The Deep Mother did not teach us to trade in suffering. If what you need from us requires us to become what we are trying to stop being, the answer is no, and no payment will change it."

### Zhentarim (Davra Jassur, at Gauntlgrym)

**Favor bank**: none. The party has not met Davra. She is at Gauntlgrym — not at the corrupted Mantol-Derith trading post — and she recruits selectively, on terms she controls.

**Why Davra, not Mantol-Derith**: Mantol-Derith is canonically compromised by Fraz-Urb'luu's gem; its factions are mad and fractured. The Zhentarim presence there is non-operational. Davra's Gauntlgrym posting is the faction's live door into the campaign.

**Why the cost is steep**: Davra's source-material design is adversarial to Bruenor. She demands 100% treasure + total intel feed precisely so the party cannot work both networks cleanly. This is not a bug; it is the play. The party chooses which patron they trust, and either choice has real cost.

**How Bruenor finds out**: Bruenor runs cross-reference checks on party operations — this is a standing Gauntlgrym practice, not a one-off. The intel monopoly violation is usually detected within one to two sessions of activation. The downgrade to Strained on the Deep Route Ledger is mechanical, not narrative; the party cannot talk their way out of it. Second Davra activation burns the Gauntlgrym relationship entirely and the party operates without the deep-route support network for the remainder of the campaign.

**When this asset is the right choice**: when the party needs *one specific thing* that no other asset delivers — a particular magical consumable, one-shot access to a named Orthodoxy official, forged credentials good enough to pass a matron's scrutiny — and they are willing to spend the Gauntlgrym relationship to get it. This is an "endgame bridge" asset, not a standing coalition relationship.

**Scene tone**: Davra is not a villain. She is a professional operator who respects competence and punishes sentiment. "I don't care what you're trying to accomplish. I care that when I deliver, you paid in full, and when I ask, you answer first. Those are the terms. Take them or don't."

## Pitch Scenes (Brief)

Full scene scripts in the style of `docs/gauntlgrym/gauntlgrym_faction_pitches.md` are out of scope for this inventory. Short pitch notes:

- **Senni (Blingdenstone)**: run in her quartermaster office, stone counters covered in ledgers, her hands chalky from rock dust. Opening: *"You came back. I had a feeling you would. Sit down. Tell me what you need, and then I'll tell you what Blingdenstone needs."*
- **Shuushar (via courier)**: no direct scene. A hooded kuo-toan approaches the party in Gauntlgrym's surface markets carrying a water-marked message. The party writes back. Days pass. Shuushar's reply determines whether the asset is available.
- **Davra (Gauntlgrym)**: she does not host the party. They find a written invitation in their quarters with an address, a time, no name. She is in a private room at a Gauntlgrym drinking hall, alone, sober. Opening: *"Sit. You have one drink's worth of my time. Waste it and you don't get a second."*

## DM Notes

- These three assets are not balanced against each other. Blingdenstone is generous, Shuushar is narrow, Davra is expensive. That asymmetry is the design — it forces real tradeoffs rather than optimization.
- Track each asset's state in the same DM tracker as Gauntlgrym assets (Ready / Strained / Burned). Davra's activation is the most consequential; log it explicitly in the Decision Log with the downgrade to Deep Route Ledger.
- Do not offer the party all three in the same session. Each contact appears when the party's operational need makes them visible — Blingdenstone surfaces when the party starts planning Menzoberranzan entry; Shuushar's courier appears when the party first acknowledges they will need extraction; Davra surfaces when the party asks Bruenor for something he cannot provide cleanly.
- Sloobludop-the-faction is gone. If the party asks whether they can draw on Sea Mother support directly, the answer is no — Ploopploopeen is dead or missing, and the Sea Mother cult has scattered. The Reformists are what survived. This is a canon fact to state plainly, not a puzzle to solve.
