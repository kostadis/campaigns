## Features & Traits

### Monk Features

**Core Monk Traits (PHB-2024 101)**

**Martial Arts (PHB-2024 101)**
You gain the following benefits when unarmed or wielding only Monk weapons, provided you aren't wearing armor or wielding a Shield.
- You can make an Unarmed Strike as a Bonus Action.
- You can roll 1d8 in place of the normal damage from your Unarmed Strikes or Monk weapons.
- You can use Dex. instead of Str. for the attack and damage rolls of your Unarmed Strikes and Monk Weapons. In addition, when you use the Grapple or Shove option of your Unarmed Strike, you can use Dex. instead of Str. to determine the save DC.
- Unarmed Strike: 1 Bonus Action

**Unarmored Defense (PHB-2024 101)**
While you aren't wearing armor or wielding a Shield, your base AC equals 16.

**Monk's Focus (PHB-2024 101)**
Your focus and martial training allows you to harness a well of energy within yourself called Focus Points. You have 8 Focus Points and regain all expended points after you finish a Short or Long Rest. You can expend these points to enhance or fuel certain Monk features. You start knowing three such features:
- Flurry of Blows. You can expend 1 Focus Point to make two Unarmed Strikes as a Bonus Action.
- Patient Defense. You can take the Disengage action as a Bonus Action or expend 1 Focus Point to take both the Disengage and Dodge actions as a Bonus Action.
- Step of the Wind. You can take the Dash action as a Bonus Action or expend 1 Focus Point to take both the Disengage and Dash actions as a Bonus Action, and your jump distance is doubled for the turn.

Features that use Focus Points may require your target to make a saving throw. The save DC equals 8 + Wis. modifier + Prof. Bonus.

## Personality

### Character Backstory
Born in a bustling coastal city known for its vibrant docks and seafaring trade, Zalthir "Zal" Thunderscale grew up amidst the salty air and the roar of the ocean. As a Brass Dragonborn, he bore the distinctive golden-hued scales of his kind, glimmering like the sun on the water's surface. His early life was marked by the stories of his ancestors—noble protectors of the skies who championed freedom and adventure.

Zalthir's parents were skilled sailors, known for their expertise in navigating treacherous waters and trading with distant lands. They instilled in him a love for the sea, teaching him the ways of sailing, survival, and respect for the natural world. From a young age, Zal felt a calling toward the ocean, often spending hours by the docks, dreaming of the adventures that awaited him beyond the horizon.

However, it was not just the thrill of sailing that captivated him. Zalthir's upbringing was also steeped in the teachings of a local monastery, where a sect of monks revered the elements, including the mighty ocean. Drawn to their philosophy of harmony and discipline, he began training under their guidance, learning martial arts and the importance of balance, both in body and mind. His instructors recognized his innate connection to the elemental forces, particularly the gentle but powerful currents of the sea.

As he matured, Zalthir embraced his dual passions: the life of a sailor and the path of a monk. He joined a merchant crew, where he quickly became known for his agility and fierce dedication. The ship became his second home, and the crew, his family. They sailed to distant ports, trading goods and sharing tales of adventure. But the life of a sailor was not without its dangers. Zal faced storms, sea monsters, and rival pirates, all while honing his skills in unarmed combat and meditation during the quiet nights at sea.
