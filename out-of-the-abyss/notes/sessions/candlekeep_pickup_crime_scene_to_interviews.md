## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

By end of session the party should hold a **coherent-but-wrong** theory —
"Sylvira or Daral did it" — and a creeping sense the pieces don't fit.
Mechanically, four things must land or the Day-3 reveals lose their punch:

1. **Poison, not the cleaver, was the killer** — midnight tears, on the
   pages of a gifted book now missing; the cleaver was ritual desecration
   to block resurrection. (IDs in the crime scene; confirmed at Daral.)
2. **Three suspects were in motion overnight** — Sylvira (~1 am),
   Bookwyrm (~1:30 am), A'lai (dawn). (Hollypocket + Tadric.)
3. **The second High Tower key is in one named PC's hand** (Kalan's
   cliffhanger). Note who. They open the High Tower door from outside two
   sessions from now.
4. **Fheminor's ⭐ "Bookwyrm was not surprised"** — the campaign hinge.
   Without it, the Day-3 Bookwyrm pivot works at half strength.

Everything else (the magic-missile chair, the lead locket, the appointment
book, the heart-in-chalice) is *supporting* — good if it lands, survivable
if it slips to next session.

## The Default Trajectory (if the party does nothing clever)

- **Daral dies at midnight tonight** — a fresh dose from re-reading the
  book this morning triggers at the stroke of midnight; found face-down in
  his bath at dawn, ink-stained, thumb at his lips. Module-canon path.
- **Sylvira worsens** (Abyssal Plague from Moziqodo's bite); untreated she
  is bedridden by Day 3 and cannot be the Session-6 battlefield ally.
- **A'lai** keeps his composure, having already pocketed the sapphire and
  (offstage tonight) takes delivery of the **Iron Bands of Bilarro** from
  a Manshoon courier at the Cursed Tower base.
- **Kalan** does not sleep; sits at the Sea Warden's Tower window. If the
  party never accepts his key, the Session-6 door problem gets ugly — keep
  a fallback (Tadric inherits it) but *try* to land the handoff.
- **Bookwyrm** cleans up loose ends, drafts an unsent letter. The cover-up
  holds. The chant fragments again at 2 a.m.

## The Clever Play (so you know when to nudge)

1. **Test the ink, find it clean** → pivot to "what touched his hands?" →
   the missing book becomes the question, not a loose end.
2. **Interview Hollypocket gently** (she's a floor down, making tea and
   crying) rather than interrogating — she volunteers the Queenie-hissed
   tell and the dawn A'lai sighting if treated kindly.
3. **Climb the wooden stair** to Janussi's private floor (see scene 1C) —
   the party hasn't been up yet; reward the instinct.
4. **Treat Sylvira's plague** (`lesser restoration` / DC 18 Medicine) —
   banks her as the Day-3 ally and buys honesty.
5. **Save Daral** (DC 14 Medicine spot in the Bath House) — converts the
   canon corpse into a live Session-5 witness who saw "the lizardskin."
6. **Catch the anti-divination logic** at the lead chalice — *"someone
   planned this around Candlekeep's own spell library"* narrows the killer
   to an insider. This is the thread that eventually fingers Alkrist.

## SCENE 1 — Finishing Janussi's Chamber (~40 min)

### Setup
- **Where/when:** still in the Keeper's tower study, late morning, Day 2
  (Deadwinter Day). Daylight now; snow on the sills. Tadric at the door.
  Fembris recovering outside the window he just vomited through.
- **Offstage:** Hollypocket one floor below (tea, crying). Bookwyrm gone
  to "other business." Kalan somewhere in the keep, freshly humiliated.

### 1A — The forensics they haven't found yet
Let them keep working the room. Clues remaining, in rough findability order:

- **The toppled chair is `magic missile`-scorched.** *(DC 13 Arcana /
  Investigation, or free if anyone examines the chair.)* Someone fought
  *here* with magic — but **Janussi himself wasn't burned.** A second
  person was in this room and a second fight happened. (Truth, GM-only:
  Sylvira's morning struggle. Don't reveal.) ⭐ Name the scorch — don't
  let them walk past the chair.
- **Severed lead chain links** on the floor by the body. *(DC 12
  Investigation.)* Janussi wore a **lead locket** on a chain; the locket
  is gone, the chain cut. **Trophy plant:** the empty locket can be
  claimed later. *Lead defeats divination* — plant the word "lead" now;
  it pays off at the chalice this afternoon.
- **The safe was blasted with `knock`,** not picked. *(DC 13 Arcana on the
  residue.)* Reconcile with what they already saw: the scattered gems mean
  the thief left wealth behind. **One specific thing is missing — a
  sapphire.** (They needn't know *whose* theft yet; A'lai took it at dawn.)
- **Janussi's appointment book** on or near the desk. *(Free if they
  search the desk drawers; DC 10 otherwise.)* Yesterday's page lists a
  meeting with **every Great Reader.** Motive bonanza, and the first time
  the party sees how tangled this council is.

### 1B — The poison, and the gift that carried it
They already know "dead before the chest opened." Push the *how*:
- **DC 15 Medicine/Investigation on the body** — faint bitter-almond at
  the lips, residue on the **fingertips and at the mouth**: an **ingested**
  poison, not a blow. The vector is the scholar's reflex — Janussi **licked
  his thumb to turn the pages**, swallowing what had been dusted on them.
  Identify it as ⭐ **midnight tears** (poisoner's kit, Grygum's healer's
  read, or — most on-theme — looking it up in the library; see Open
  Decisions #1).
- ⭐ **It kills at the stroke of midnight.** Midnight tears does nothing
  until midnight, then DC 17 Con / 9d6 (RAW, DMG p. 258). That delay is
  *why* the candles burned down unattended and *why* the prophecy lands —
  **"the keeper falls"** on the longest night: Janussi died at midnight,
  hours before the body was found. It also sets the clock on **Daral**
  (Scene 5B), who re-dosed himself this morning.
- ⭐ **The book-shaped gap.** In the fine tobacco-ash settled over the desk
  there is a clean rectangle where a book sat until recently — and along
  its edge, the **same midnight-tears residue.** *(DC 15 Investigation.)*
  Whatever Janussi was reading when he died **carried the poison on its
  pages — and it has been taken out of this room.**
- ⭐ **The Deadwinter gift card.** In the wastebasket, or fallen behind the
  desk, a gift tag in a sour, cramped hand: *"To Janussi — read it and
  weep, you old snob. A proper Deadwinter gift. — your loving rival."* The
  blue-ink notes on the desk name the book: `The Golden Ass`. **So: the
  murder weapon was a gifted book, sent by a "rival," and the book is gone.**
  The card names no one — *which rival?* (Several Readers feuded with the
  old snob; keep the doubt alive a beat.)
- ⭐ **Fembris names the rival.** Show him the card, or read it where he can
  hear (he's recovering just outside the window), and the adjutant doesn't
  hesitate: *"Oh — that'll be Daral Yashenti. Those two have been at each
  other's throats for years. He's the sour one who called you sell-swords."*
  The comic-relief aide is, for once, genuinely useful. ⭐ **It's a guess,
  not a signature** — a sliver of doubt survives, but the arrow now points
  squarely at Daral.
- If they test the **midnight-blue ink** (they will — they fixated on it):
  **it's clean.** Not the vector. Steers them off the ink, onto the book.

**Where this points (and why it's a trap).** Once Fembris fingers Daral,
the obvious read is *"Daral gave Janussi a poisoned book — Daral did it."*
That is exactly the
wrong-but-reasonable theory the arc wants them holding by session's end.
The crack that later exonerates him: **Daral is dying of the same poison**
(he took the book back and re-read it all morning) — *a poisoner does not
read his own poisoned pages.* Don't tip it here. Let them build the wrong
case and feel clever; the unravelling is Day 3's job.

### 1C — The wooden staircase (the floor they haven't climbed)
The summary notes a **wooden stair up** the party never took. Reward the
climb. *(GM-fill — keep it mystery-neutral; suggested, not canon:)*
- **Janussi's private bedchamber** — austere, a narrow bed unslept-in
  (he died at his desk), a shrine to Oghma, decades of marginalia.
- A small thing for **interiority, not evidence:** a pressed flower or a
  faded portrait suggesting Janussi and **Sylvira** were once more than
  colleagues. Plants the lovers' thread that pays off in her interview.
- **Nothing here breaks the case.** If the party wants hard clues upstairs,
  the answer is absence — *he lived for the desk, not the bed.*

### Branch — if the party tries to preserve / move the body
Thorin already asked about **ice.** Lean in: the keep's cold-cellar or the
snow on the north parapet will hold the corpse ~10 days for a later `speak
with dead` retry. Bookwyrm permits it (she knows it's already locked —
she cast the spell first). Let them feel clever; the lock holds regardless.

### Branch — if they fixate on the missing book and search the chamber for it
It is not here. The harder they look, the louder the question gets:
*who took a book from a murder scene?* Bank it. (Answer: Daral, this
morning, before the door was sealed — Tadric will half-remember "the sour
one wanted in" if pressed: that's **Fheminor** at 8:30, *not* Daral, so
Daral got the book **the night before** when he punched Janussi. Keep the
timeline honest; don't let them place Daral in the room at dawn.)

### NPC notes
- **Tadric** (Watcher, 23, bowl cut) — wracked with guilt ("I don't think
  I did a good job"). *Wants:* to be told he isn't the reason a man died.
  *Tic:* defends Kalan reflexively. **Hold his 1:30 a.m. Bookwyrm sighting
  for Scene 2** — it should feel dragged out of him, not volunteered.
- **Fembris** (adjutant, half-elf, green) — comic relief, out of his
  depth, loyal. *Wants:* to be useful without seeing another corpse.
  Use him to lighten the room between grim findings.

## SCENE 2 — The Witnesses (~25 min)

### 2A — Miss Hollypocket (gnome housekeeper, one floor below)
She's been making tea and weeping. **Tone is the mechanic:** interrogate
her and she clams; *sit with her* and she pours it out. Two crucial things:
- **"Sylvira came up around 1 a.m. — I heard her voice."** And then: ⭐
  **"Queenie hissed at her. Ten years that cat has known the Great Reader.
  Never once hissed. Not once."** *(The `disguise self` tell — it wasn't
  really Sylvira. GM-only.)*
- **"A'lai came up at dawn"** — *before* the body was found. (He was
  hunting the High Tower key; found the safe already emptied; took the
  sapphire. GM-only.)
- Optional warmth beat: Queenie is on the stair. If a PC is kind to the
  cat, Hollypocket softens fully and adds that Janussi "hadn't been
  sleeping — said he had ten days and a decision to make." (Foreshadows
  both Sylvira's deadline *and* Fheminor's succession.)

### 2B — Tadric, the second pass
Now spend his guilt. Pressed on "was anyone *else* moving last night," he
reluctantly gives up: ⭐ **"I saw the First Reader. In the corridor. Half
past one, near enough. She should have been abed."** He immediately
qualifies it, miserable — *he likes Bookwyrm, he likes Kalan, and his
testimony is hurting both.* That discomfort is the scene.

### Branch — if the party never interviews Hollypocket
She'll keep. But you lose the Queenie/`disguise self` tell, which is the
single best early thread toward "the Sylvira people saw wasn't Sylvira."
If they skip her, have **Fembris** mention "the housekeeper's been asking
to talk to someone" as a late nudge.

### The cliffhanger — Kalan, in the corridor
This is the beat Chapter 53 *didn't* reach. Kalan steps out of a doorway
as they leave. He presses **a small brass key** into one PC's hand — pick
the PC the table will remember (the one most likely to be *at the door* in
the Session-6 climax). Lift verbatim if it lands:

> *"The High Tower has two keys. Janussi wore one. I held the other. I
> have given it now to someone who is not me. If I do not see you

And, if you want the covert-ally line he never got to deliver, fold it in

> *"Whatever Bookwyrm is telling you, listen for what she isn't. I am not

⭐ **Note which PC holds the key. Write it down.** It is load-bearing two
sessions out.

### Branch — if the party interrogates Kalan instead of letting him go
He won't be interrogated. He's already given the only thing that matters.
*"You'll understand by tomorrow. Or you won't, and it won't matter."* Then
he's gone. If they physically follow him → Sea Warden's Tower, alone at the
window, hands shaking, refuses healing. Don't burn the Day-3 goodbye scene
here; give them *"Not tonight. Tomorrow, if you still want to."*

## SCENE 4 — Afternoon: The Heart in the Chalice (~20 min)

### Setup

Gallery (window faces the Cursed Tower — Moziqodo passed close last night).
The **Endless Chant drops three verses in a row.** ⭐ **Daz feels the

### The search
If the party assembled *"the cleaver was ritual + someone hid the
evidence,"* they can search the **Southern Dining Hall.**
- **DC 15 Investigation** systematic; **DC 12** if they specifically check
  altars/statues.
- ⭐ **The lead chalice on the statue of Milil** holds Janussi's **heart**
  and a **bloodstained meat cleaver.**
- **`Speak with dead` on the heart FAILS.** *The failure is the clue* —

  Janussi could say.)
- ⭐ **Lead chalice = anti-divination.** Tie it to the lead locket from

  library.* This narrows the killer to an **insider who knows the

### Branch — if the party doesn't connect the dots to search here
Don't strand the clue. Fembris can relay "the kitchen staff are upset —
something's wrong in the Southern Dining Hall, they won't say what," or
Queenie can be found sitting at the foot of the Milil statue, *staring up.*
The cat knows. Let the cat tell them.

## SCENE 5 — Reader Interviews #1 (~75 min — party may split)

Three available. Encourage rotation/split. These are the *suspects who
look guilty* — the session should end with the party leaning toward
**Sylvira or Daral**, and uneasy about it.

### 5A — Sylvira, the Infernal Fortress (~25 min)
**She is visibly worse than yesterday** — fever, tremor, **sores on her
forearm she keeps covered.** Story: alone last night, no alibi. Truth (if
pressed *honestly*, or shown the bite): the bite is from her son
**Moziqodo;** Janussi knew, **was her lover**, gave her **ten days** to
remove him. She was at the Cursed Tower last night. **Abyssal Plague.**
> *"I did not kill him. I loved him."*

- ⭐ **This is also the ally interview.** She believed the party about the
  demon lords yesterday. *The party is interrogating their own ally.* Let
  that tension sit.
- **Optional Grygum beat:** DC 18 Medicine or `lesser restoration` to
  diagnose/treat the plague. Treating her = she survives to be the
  Session-6 battlefield ally. *"You are not what I expected of a half-orc
  cleric."*
- If the party climbed the stair in 1C and saw the pressed flower/portrait,
  Sylvira's "I loved him" lands like a struck bell. Pay it off.

### 5B — Daral, the Bath House (~20 min) — 🟣 **save-or-let-die**
Soaking, drinking, hung over, hostile. Story: yes, he punched the Keeper;
yes, he **stole back his Deadwinter gift — `The Golden Ass`** (this is
*where the missing book is*). Sleeve still bloody from the punch. Bragged
to two waiters at supper he'd "give that man a real Deadwinter present."

- ⭐ **Daral is already dosed — but he doesn't look it.** Midnight tears is
  silent until midnight (RAW), so there is **no pallor, no symptom** to spot
  this afternoon. He's been re-reading the very book all morning, licking
  his thumb through the pages, carrying a fresh dose that triggers tonight.
  ⭐ *The man who poisoned the pages would not be reading them* — this is the
  crack that exonerates him, **and** the only warning the party gets that
  he's about to die. They save him by *deduction*, not a Medicine read.
- **The gift, honestly.** Yes, he gave Janussi `The Golden Ass` (a barbed
  joke about the Keeper's taste); yes, he punched him and took it back. But
  **it wasn't poisoned when he gave it** — and he doesn't know who dusted
  the pages. *(Truth, GM-only: Alkrist, with midnight tears stolen from
  Leuwin.)* If the party never made the gift-card connection in the
  chamber, his open rivalry with Janussi and the bloody sleeve close the
  loop now.
- **The save is `lesser restoration` or `protection from poison`** (either
  neutralizes the dose) **applied before midnight** — they have all day, but
  must realize the danger. *Optional* faint tell: DC 16 Medicine reads
  "something past the hangover," GM's discretion — RAW gives nothing, so
  treat it as a courtesy nudge, not the intended path.
- 🟣 **Player choice, not script.** Saved → rich Session-5 witness ("I saw
  the lizardskin at the Evergreen Tree at four"). Let die → dies at the
  stroke of midnight, found in his bath at dawn, **midnight tears in the
  stomach lining** (ingested — the autopsy confirms the vector). **Note the
  choice.**

### 5C — Fheminor, Founder's Court cloisters (~20 min) — the hinge
Old, careful halfling; never quite trusts **Daz** (watch him the whole
time). Story: nothing to hide; met Janussi yesterday at 4 p.m.; sorry he's
dead. Truth (**only if earned**): **Janussi was about to name her his
successor.**
> *"He said: 'I am old, and I am tired, and the keep needs a hand that does
> not ache. I will name you tomorrow.'"*

She is now in mortal danger and is investigating in parallel. She watched
Bookwyrm closely the morning of the death. The line that matters:
> ⭐ *"Bookwyrm was not surprised."*

**This is the campaign hinge.** If only one thing survives a rushed
session, make it this. Without it the Day-3 Bookwyrm pivot loses its floor.

## Anticipated player questions

- **"Is the midnight-blue ink the poison?"** → No — test it, it's clean.
  The poison was on the *book* he was reading, and the book is gone. (Steer
  toward "what's missing," not "what's here.")
- **"Where's `The Golden Ass` — and who's the 'loving rival'?"** → The
  book's gone; the poison-residue gap shows it was the vector, and
  **Fembris names the rival as Daral.** The honest in-fiction read is
  *"Daral poisoned a gift"* — let them run with it; it's the intended wrong
  theory. The card itself names no one, so a careful party may hedge — fine.
  The exoneration (he's dying of it too) waits at the Bath House. Don't
  pre-empt it.
- **"Can we just `speak with dead` if we wait 10 days / find the heart?"**
  → The heart-in-chalice still **fails** — it was pre-locked. The 10-day
  retry on the body *would* work mechanically, but by then the keep has
  fallen down around them (Sessions 5–6). Let them try; let it cost time.
- **"Who has the second key?"** → Whichever PC Kalan chose. The GM noted
  it. It opens the High Tower door from *outside* in the climax.
- **"Did Sylvira do it?"** → She had means, motive (the deadline), and was
  on the move at 1 a.m. **She didn't.** Let the party build the wrong-but-
  reasonable case; the disguise tell (Queenie hissed) is the crack.
- **"Can we heal Sylvira / save Daral?"** → Yes to both. Both are
  good-choice-ledger entries with real downstream payoff. Don't make it
  easy; do make it possible.

## Stage directions (timing / silence cues)

- When the party names the **`magic missile` chair** — a beat of silence.
  *A second person was in this room.* Let it hang.
- **Hollypocket's "never once hissed"** — don't editorialize. Deliver it
  flat, through tears, and move on. The party will circle back to it.
- **Kalan's key handoff** — slow it down. Press the brass into a palm,
  hold the eye contact, then leave. *Do not let the party turn it into a

- **`Speak with dead` failing on the heart** — let the player roll, let it

- **Fheminor's "Bookwyrm was not surprised"** — land it last in her scene,
  then end the scene. Don't gloss it.
- **Daral, if let die** — don't narrate the death this session. Let them
  walk out thinking they made a call. The cost arrives at dawn.
