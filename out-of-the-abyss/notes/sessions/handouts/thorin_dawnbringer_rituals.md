## A note from Master Archivist Philemon (Paths B and C)

Thorin —

Your sword does not need polishing. She needs to remember what
daylight feels like without needing to fight. And then, when she
is ready and you are willing, she needs to grieve.

The first work is light. The second work is mourning.

We will start with the first. Below are five rituals for the work.
You may write to me about how they land. I am at Candlekeep until
the question is asked.

— Philemon

## Path A — Radical Transparency (3 rituals)

*[Use this section if Thorin sided with Dawnbringer in Phase 2.
Philemon withdrew. The rituals below are in Thorin's own hand on
plain parchment, written that night at the inn after the quiet
hour with Dawnbringer alone. There is no Avowed letterhead.]*

### 1. Dawn-witness

First light each morning, sword exposed but **not drawn,** blade
unactivated. Both Thorin and Dawnbringer present. No words.

Restores something the dark took. (Easy; anywhere with a horizon.)

### 2. Naming the captor

Once, aloud, Thorin says **Brysis's** name to Dawnbringer. Then —
also aloud — he tells the sword that Brysis is dead, that Grygum
killed her, that the dark she lived in is gone.

Plain words. Single ritual; cannot be repeated. Landing it well is
its own work.

### 3. Music chosen by the sword

Thorin plays, hums, sings, or asks someone in the party to play
whatever Dawnbringer chooses. The sword leads. Finding-each-other
beat.

### 4. A non-combat day

Sheathed, carried, **not used.** Dawnbringer travels with Thorin
through ordinary life — market, conversation, work — without being
asked to fight.

Lets her remember being someone's *companion,* not their *weapon.*

### 5. The unfinished name

When Dawnbringer is ready — *and she will not be ready for a
while* — she attempts to say her **original wielder's** name.
Thorin can ask occasionally; Dawnbringer will deflect until she is
ready.

Each attempt is its own beat. May fail. May produce only fragments.
May produce nothing.

This ritual cannot be completed in a week or a month. It earns its
arrival across seasons.

## Mechanical reward menu (per ritual completed)

GM picks per completion. Up to one reward per ritual.

- **+5 ft** to Dawnbringer's bright + dim sunlight radius (cap at
  the sword's max +30 ft, per the rules).
- **A new compassion charge** — Dawnbringer's *lesser restoration*
  ability gains +1 use per dawn (max +1; recharges as normal).
  *Philemon's framing: she remembers what she was made for.*
- **Dawn-witness threshold** (unlocks at 4 of 5 rituals completed,
  Path C only): the first attack Dawnbringer makes each dawn deals
  an extra **1d4 radiant** damage until the next dawn. Symbolic —
  she greets the day with light.

## The unfinished name — the final reward (Path C only)

Philemon has prepared a sealed letter, dated this Candlekeep
visit, to be opened **only on the day Dawnbringer says her original
wielder's name aloud.**

Inside is a specific advancement tied to the named wielder's faith
or order, to be revealed when the name lands. Philemon has done
this work before. He has the letter ready.

Until then, the letter stays sealed. **Don't open it.**
