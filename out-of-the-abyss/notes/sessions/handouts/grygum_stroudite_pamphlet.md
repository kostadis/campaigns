# Stroudite Pamphlet — *A Critique of the Human Agency Hypothesis*

Handed to Grygum by a travelling Stroudite polemicist in
Mirabar's Smiths' Quarter (Travelogue Episode 2; played at the
table in Ch. 51). Folded broadside, eight pages, printed
cheaply on grey rag-stock. The polemicist watched Grygum
verify it was *divided into paragraphs* before committing to
keep it.

> **GM note:** read aloud or hand to Ben as a physical
> handout. The pamphlet's voice is **deliberate** — early-
> modern Faerûnian polemic, Common-Sense cadence, designed to
> *not* sound like a temple tract. It is the in-fiction
> evidence of why the Stroud school is a *political* movement
> rather than a theology, and why Vareth at Candlekeep treats
> it as a controversy rather than a doctrine.
>
> Ben can read it on the road south, or it can sit in Grygum's
> pack until Vareth's Stroud Wake (Stations 1–3) surfaces it
> as the Mirabar variant of the polemic shelf.

## STROUD
## AND THE HUMAN AGENCY HYPOTHESIS:
## A CRITIQUE.

— — —

*Wherein it is demonstrated, by plain argument and the
testimony of the late events, that DRAGONS and GODS do not
rule; and that the SOVEREIGN RIGHT OF MORTAL CHOICE is the
central truth of existence.*

— — —

**By A FRIEND OF FREE PERSONS.**

— — —

*Printed at Mirabar, in the Forge-Quarter, by the hand of
Borrim Pickwhistle, this third year since the Council of
Waterdeep. Price: a copper, or such offering as the Reader's
conscience may direct.*

— — —

### TO THE READER.

I shall be plain.

There has come, of late, a comfortable saying among the
temples and the chapter-houses, which is repeated until
repetition takes the place of reasoning, viz: *"that the gods
governed the late war, and the dragons obeyed the gods, and
the mortals served as instruments thereof."* This saying I
cannot suffer.

I will not suffer it because it is false. And I will not
suffer it because falsehoods of this kind are not idle, but
**TEACH.** They teach a Reader to expect that great events
arrive by the will of the great. They teach that mortal
persons are *occasions* of history but not *authors* of it.
They teach, in short, the very lesson which every prince and
every priest has had cause to teach since the beginning of
the world: that you, Reader, do not matter.

I write to put it to you that you DO.

---

### I.

Consider what is admitted on every hand. There sat, at the
chess-board, a dwarf — STROUD by name — of no great house,
of no priestly office, bearing no charter from any divine
power. There sat opposite him an ancient gold dragon,
PROTANTHER, four hundred years of preparation behind every
glance. The hour was such as men remember.

What occurred between them? A move was played. A position was
reached. A concession was uttered.

Now I ask the Reader: at the moment Stroud LIFTED HIS QUEEN,
who chose? Was it Bahamut, gentle Lord of the Just, who
reached down and guided the dwarf's hand? Did a celestial
whisper provide the diagram? Was there sent, by Providence, a
memorandum?

There was not.

**Stroud chose.** Stroud, alone, IN HIS OWN PERSON, chose. The
diagram was Tessarra's Lock; the diagram had been published
for centuries and been read by every chess-master who has
eyes; but the *choice to spend the queen* was Stroud's own,
and was not in any record before he made it. The geometry
was always there. The WILL to spend was not.

This is the whole of the matter.

---

### II.

Hear now the temple's reply. They say: *"but the dragons
consented, and the dragons are the agents of Bahamut, and
Bahamut had moved his pieces long beforehand."* I answer:
this is a comfortable account, but it is the account of a
clerk, not of a witness.

For consider — Protanther conceded the game. He did not say
*"my god has bidden me yield;"* he said **"the move is
yours."** Five plain words. Read them, and read them again.
He did not yield to Bahamut. He yielded to STROUD.

And the metallic dragons, observing the concession, FREELY
MOVED against Tiamat — not by celestial command, not by
divine compulsion, but because a public concession had been
freely tendered before all witnesses, and they elected to
honour it. Each was a free act. Each was a chosen thing.
None of them were ordained.

It was, therefore, a chain of mortal and dragon CHOICES — and
at the head of that chain stood one dwarf, who lifted one
piece, on one afternoon, in one hall of the Council. By that
lifting, **Tiamat was denied her power.**

I beg the Reader to feel the WEIGHT of what I am saying.
Tiamat — first among the cruelty of the world, the eldest
appetite, the queen of dragons-that-devour — was defeated not
because the heavens willed it, but because a dwarf chose a
move, and a dragon chose to honour it, and other dragons
chose to follow. Strike out any one of those choosings and
the whole chain falls. There is no thread of providence
running through it that the choosings did not weave.

---

### III.

From this, what shall we conclude?

That the gods are absent? — No. I do not say so.

That the dragons are without honour? — No. I do not say so.

That Providence is mockery? — No. Let priests dispute it.

I say only this: that whatsoever the gods are, and whatsoever
the dragons are, **THEY DO NOT RULE THE CHOOSING.** The choice
belongs to the chooser. The choice belonged to Stroud. The
choice belongs, this very afternoon, in this very
Forge-Quarter, in the hand that holds this pamphlet — **to
you.**

This is the central truth of existence: that **mortal choice
is sovereign,** and is not borrowed, and is not delegated, and
is not on loan from any throne above or below. It cannot be
revoked. It cannot be repossessed. It is the one ground that
no power on the Sword Coast nor under it has ever managed to
take from a creature whose mind remained its own.

Some will say I overstate. Some will say a dwarf at a board
is not a doctrine. To these I answer:

A dwarf at a board, IN PLAIN SIGHT, denied Tiamat her power.

He did this BY CHOOSING.

The world has changed because he chose.

**What more proof is required?**

— — —

## GM cross-references

- **Mirabar polemicist (Travelogue Episode 2):** the
  pamphlet's distributor; the man who handed Grygum the
  copy. Travelling, organized, will recognize Grygum's
  Bahamut symbol on sight and try to recruit. Per Ch. 51

- **Vareth's polemic shelf (Stroud Wake Station 1):** this
  pamphlet is one of the *Mirabar variants;* Vareth keeps
  three editions, two of which contain printer's errors
  Vareth finds *"telling."* Variant in Grygum's hand has
  the Borrim Pickwhistle imprint — Vareth will identify it
  by the imprint alone.
- **Stroud school polemicist canon (`grygum_stroud_dossier.md`):**
  the *humanoid agency* line is the school's central tenet.
  This pamphlet is the popular-tier presentation; Grygum
  will see academic versions of the same argument at
  Candlekeep.
- **Real-world style register:** modeled on pre-revolutionary
  18th-century broadside polemic (Common Sense cadence,
  capitalized emphasis, direct address to the Reader,
  rhetorical climax). The *register* is the point — the
  Stroud school is a **political movement** that arrived in
  pamphlet form, not a theology that arrived in a temple.
  This is why Bahamut institutional clergy find it
  *embarrassing* to engage with: the form is wrong for
  doctrine. Vareth will say so.
