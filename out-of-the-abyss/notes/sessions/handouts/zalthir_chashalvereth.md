# Cha'shal-vereth — The First Watcher's Posture

*Recorded for Zalthir, by Khell-Vire, late of the Garden of
Shadow, in the year [GM picks].*

*[In Khell-Vire's careful hand. The page has been smoothed twice
where his hand paused too long. He has signed it at the bottom
without flourish.]*

---

## What it is

A discipline of attention, not of action. The Garden of Shadow
calls it **Cha'shal-vereth** — *"the silence that listens."* I
have called it the **First Watcher's Posture** for the last sixty
years, after my own master used the phrase. Either name is
correct.

It is not a spell. *Counterspell* does nothing to it. *Dispel
Magic* does nothing to it. It is a *bodily discipline,* taught
hand to hand, and it survives the practitioner.

## Form

While motionless and silent in dim light or darkness, you enter
Watcher's Stillness. The discipline asks for stillness it can
*recognize* — not merely cessation of movement, but the practiced
absence of a listening body.

While in this state:

- You are **functionally invisible to non-magical detection.**
  Passive Perception treats you as not there. Active Perception
  checks to spot you are made at disadvantage. Truesight,
  tremorsense, blindsight, and direct magical detection (such as
  *See Invisibility,* and *Detect Magic* in some readings) defeat
  it normally.
- You gain **advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks** to
  overhear or observe within sixty feet.
- Conversations within **thirty feet** are heard *clearly*
  regardless of ambient noise or normal distance falloff — as if
  the speaker were beside you. The discipline tunes hearing to
  deliberate stillness.
- Lasts up to **one hour,** or until you end it.

## Cost

The discipline ends the moment you take any action, move more
than five feet, speak above a whisper, or attack.

For **one round** after ending Stillness, you are *re-entering
the world* — your movement is halved and you have no reactions.
The discipline asks for proof that you mean to leave the silence
before it lets you back into the noise.

## What it is not

- **Not Stealth.** Stealth hides you from being looked-for.
  Watcher's Stillness hides you from being *noticed.* They layer
  cleanly. Use them together when you want to be both unseen and
  unfound. Use Stillness alone when you want to be *present in a
  room without registering.*
- **Not a saving throw on the target.** It is not affecting them.
  It is altering your relationship with their attention.
- **Not a path of withdrawal.** The discipline is taught for
  *witnessing,* not for retreat. The Watchers of the Garden used
  it to *be present at things they could not stop.* That is the
  oldest meaning. Carry it.

## A discipline of recall

Conversations heard in Stillness can be **repeated verbatim** at
the table. The body has been trained to hold what it hears. If
you are uncertain of a line, you may ask the table to give it
back to you — the discipline forgives the loss of words it has
already gathered.

## A note from Khell-Vire

Zalthir —

You will need to listen more than you fight, in the seasons
coming. I do not know what your masters were thinking when they
sent you out, and I do not know what *the Bone-King* is. But I
know this discipline. It is what kept me sane when the Garden
asked me to do a thing I refused to do. It is what I have given
to one student in eighty years.

I am giving it to you because you arrived. Not because the
notebook said you would.

If you ever find a use for it that I have not described — write
to me. I am still trying to understand what my master meant when
he said *the silence that listens.* Sixty years has not exhausted
the question.

— K.
