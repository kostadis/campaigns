## 1. The piwafwi (cloak)

The drow word for the spider-silk cloaks worn by drow of standing.
*Piwafwi* are not garments first; they are **pieces of architecture**
worn close to the body. Each is woven by a specific weaver-house in
Menzoberranzan or Ched Nasad, signed in the lining with a
character-mark visible only under faerzress, and tuned to its
wearer's *House sigil.* A piwafwi worn by the wrong House looks
*correct* to a surface-trained eye and *insulting* to a drow eye.

Three readings of a piwafwi:

- **Color and weave** — House identity at a glance
- **Hem stitching** — generation of the wearer (eldest line vs.
  cadet branch)
- **Lining mark** — weaver-house, which itself indicates House
  alliance and debt structures

A piwafwi acquired from a corpse is **legible to drow as a stolen
piwafwi.** Wearing it announces theft, and theft *upward* (toward a
higher House) is one of the few crimes that crosses caste.

## 2. Faerzress (the radiance of the deep)

The Underdark's ambient magical radiation. Permeates stone, water,
and air at depth. Has three relevant properties:

- **Drow magic adapts to it.** Spells cast in faerzress are slower
  to fail under interruption but harder to *direct.* Drow casters
  trained underground have unconscious habits that misfire on the
  surface.
- **It interferes with divination.** *Scrying, locate-object,*
  *clairvoyance* — all degrade in faerzress, often unpredictably.
  This is why drow Houses do not need to ward their wards: the
  Underdark wards itself.
- **Surface drow lose it slowly.** A drow new to the surface will
  feel *missing weight* in the air for months — a sense of
  *exposure,* as if the world has thinned. The body adapts. Some
  never adapt to it.

If you cast spells, expect a few months of unpredictable
performance. This is not personal failing.

## 3. Weaponry traditions

Three signatures, all of them visible at a glance to a drow eye:

- **Hand-crossbow** — the most common drow side-arm. Bolts are
  almost always poisoned. Drow children are taught to recognize a
  poisoner's stance — a half-turn of the off-hand wrist — at a
  distance of forty feet. *Note: surface guards often do not.*
- **Twin-blade fighting** — paired short blades, one curved, one
  straight, used in close quarters. The doctrine prizes
  asymmetric defense: one blade always neutral, one always
  active. *Yvenne's marginalia: "If you ever see a drow holding
  both blades active, run. He is afraid."*
- **The piwafwi-and-blade draw** — a movement that uses the cloak
  as feint while drawing the off-hand blade. Visible to drow
  duelists; invisible to outsiders. Many surface duelists have
  died not seeing the second blade.

## 4. Spider-silk and adamantine

Two materials that carry House meaning. *Adamantine* in drow craft
is almost always *House-marked* — a signature on the inside curve
of a hilt or the underside of a shoulder plate that survives
melting and reforging. Drow smiths sign their work *under* the
metal, knowing their work will be reforged by enemies.

*Spider-silk* is the soft counterpart. Used for cloaks, robes,
banner-cloth, ritual veils. The silk itself carries a faint
*chemical signature* keyed to the spider lineage; House-aligned
weavers maintain their own colonies, and silk from a rival House's
colony **is detectable** under faerzress for years after weaving.

If you ever come into possession of drow goods, assume they are
**signed under the surface.** Forgery is possible but expensive.

## 5. House sigil conventions

A sigil reads in three layers:

1. **The animal/object** at the center — the House totem. Spiders
   for most; some Houses have specific spider species, others
   have non-spider totems (rare; usually significant).
2. **The number of points or repetitions** around the central
   image — generation of the House (older Houses have higher
   counts).
3. **The negative-space border** — current rank in the Council.
   *This changes.* A House's sigil border is **re-engraved** on
   their signet rings every time their Council rank shifts.
   A signet with a rank-six border on a House currently ranked
   four is either a relic or a forgery.

This is why Daz's recovered T'sarran ring is dated information.
The border is correct *as of when the ring was last engraved,*
not necessarily now. *(Yvenne's marginalia: "Bring the ring next
time. I can date it within a decade.")*

## 6. Reading a House at a distance

If you ever need to identify a House operative in public without
asking:

- **The piwafwi** is the loudest signal, but easily forged.
- **The signet** is precise, but worn under the cloak.
- **The poisoner's stance** identifies *active* intent, not
  House.
- **The accent** — Menzoberranzan, Ched Nasad, Sshamath, and the
  surface diaspora each have distinct cadences. *Yvenne's
  marginalia: "I will teach you to hear them, if you ever
  ask."*
