## Act 3 — Parallel research lanes (~90 min)

Run as four short scenes back-to-back, **interleaving** so each
player gets ~20–30 min of spotlight. Don't run as montage. Let scenes
breathe.

### Daz with Sister Yvenne

**Setting:** Yvenne's reading room, Inner Ward. Multiple sittings
across 2–3 in-fiction days; ~30 min real time.

**Yvenne's frame** (deliver early): she is half-drow, raised on the
surface by an Eilistraee circle after her mother fled the collapse
of Ched Nasad. She wears Eilistraee's silver crescent openly, drow
features unhidden. She greets Daz with **quiet recognition, not
pity** — she lived through the choice he is about to learn exists.

**The disclosure trade — visible 5-tick track on the table.**

- **0–2 ticks:** surface lore only (Lane 1 + Lane 2 geography)
- **3–4 ticks:** Lane 3 (deities & schism) opens; Yvenne can offer
  the **bloodline-pattern observation** (Half 2 payoff)
- **5 ticks:** named Eilistraee circle introduction; Drow Material
  Culture handout arrives at the inn

Each substantive answer Yvenne gives consumes one tick. Each personal
truth Mike volunteers fills one. Frame to Mike: *"Tell me something
true and I will tell you something true."*

**Lore lanes (deliver in order):**

*Lane 1 — Politics & Houses.* Matron Mothers, Yochlol, the rule of
intrigue (betrayal upward rewarded if it strengthens Lolth's web).
**House DeVir surfaces here** in a list of extinct Houses recited
matter-of-factly. *Stop the table — ask Mike directly:* *"Daz hears
the name. Does he react, or does he stay still?"*

- **React:** Yvenne notes it. Bloodline-pattern observation
  available at one-tick discount. Daz's anonymity narrows.
- **Stay still:** Yvenne moves on. Anonymity preserved. Pattern
  observation costs more.

*Lane 2 — Geography & cities.* Menzoberranzan, Pillars of Woe; Ched
Nasad as Yvenne's personal beat. Footnote: **"Daz'issin"** as a
House DeVir nursery name in a 200-year-old heraldry book. Yvenne
mentions "the masked god" only — *do not name Vhaeraun.*

*Lane 3 — Deities & schism.* Lolth (architectural center, leverage
not love), Eilistraee (Yvenne's faith; surface community option),
"the masked god" sealed.

*Lane 4 — Magic & material culture.* **Delivered as a handout** at
the inn that evening (`handouts/daz_drow_material_culture.md`).

### Daz patron thread — the paired investigation

Two questions, paired investigation. Run T'sarran first.

**Half 1 — The T'sarran question.**

Yvenne's clear-eyed read:

- T'sarran is **not minor.** Significant noble House with standing
  warriors and House mages. Fungal Cavern was a real deployment.
- **Matron Mother is Vaelissa T'sarran.** Iron traditionalist.
  Patient. Personally affronted, not institutionally so.
- Vaelissa is **personally curious about who paid the Duskryns** —
  she is asking the same question Daz is asking, from the other
  side. *Mirror dynamic. Two investigations racing.*
- **Daz was wanted alive specifically.** Yvenne, plainly: *"A
  Matron Mother of Vaelissa's standing does not invest House blood
  and House mages in extracting a 'nobody.' Whatever she thinks
  you are worth, she is willing to pay for it."*

**The cutout — sealed scroll-tube** in the minor-Houses collection,
labeled *"T'sarran — external contracts, [year window ~10 years
ago]."* The tube is **empty.** Wax seal recently broken and resealed
in different color. Pulled three years ago by **Brother Hilmin
Thaer,** who died of natural causes 18 months ago, weeks after a
private audience with a visitor whose name was never logged.

**Half 2 — The protection question (Mike's bigger payoff).**

Yvenne has seen this exact pattern twice in 20 years:

- **Pattern A:** Eilistraee infant smuggling. *"I would know if you
  were one of ours. You aren't."* Ruled out.
- **Pattern B:** Houses protecting heirs in hiding. *"Long-term.
  Patient. Cutout-layered. Resourced enough to reach Candlekeep
  and erase what shouldn't be read."*

Yvenne's line:

> *"I will not tell you what you are. That is for you to decide
> whether to know. But what is happening to you is not random.
> Someone with a stake in a bloodline has decided you are worth
> keeping alive at considerable cost. That is a kind of inheritance,
> whether or not you ever meet the person paying it."*

The DeVir name + Daz'issin nickname + "location unknown, records
purged" survivor entry are now in front of Mike. He can connect them
or not. Vizeran name **stays sealed** — Yvenne doesn't know.

### Zalthir with Brother Khell-Vire and Master Kenshi

**Setting:** Khell-Vire's contemplative office (Inner Ward) and the
Order of the Enlightened Shadow's training hall. ~30 min real time;
multi-day in fiction.

**Khell-Vire:** ~200-year-old half-elf. Left the Garden of Shadow
~80 years ago after **refusing an order he won't name unprompted.**
Wears a faded shadow-monk under-robe (no longer black; not replaced)
under Avowed gray. Patient, observant, speaks rarely.

**The two-way flow:**

- Zalthir researches Jim Jar. Khell-Vire researches Zalthir.
- **Master's private notebook** on Khell-Vire's desk. Pupils paired
  with the places they were sent to walk. Most pairings crossed out
  (deceased). One reads: *"Zalthir — the Underdark, until the
  Bone-King sings."*
- Khell-Vire is asking *what* Zalthir was sent toward, not *why*
  he was sent.
- **Three working hypotheses on the Bone-King**, all live:
  1. An entity (demon lord, lich, named omission)
  2. An event, not a person (a moment, a song heard once)
  3. A person Zalthir was sent to encounter (meet / witness / oppose / kill)
- Khell-Vire commits to none.

**Shared-silence beat:** ten minutes of silence with the open
notebook between them. GM invites Gabe to say whether anything
Zalthir has lived through resonates with the phrase.

- **Yes specific:** Khell-Vire takes margin note; campaign seed
  for GM to confirm/subvert later.
- **No, nothing yet:** *"Then your walk continues. I would be told
  if it ended."*
- **Uncertain:** *"Uncertainty is the most honest thing the
  discipline asks of us. Carry it."*

### The Trial of the Broken Mirror (Day 1 morning)

**Khell-Vire frames it:**

> *"There are monks here in the Inner Ward who have read everything
> and fought nothing. They will want to test you. Let them. You
> will learn what you do not see, and they will learn what cannot
> be read. I will not be there. This is your work, not mine."*

**Master Kenshi:** blind half-elf monk, ~90s, **Custodian of Martial
Lore.** Fifty years reading techniques he has never used. To Kenshi,
Zalthir is a **Living Heresy** — succeeds *because he has unlearned*
the rigid forms.

**Opening interrogation.** Kenshi asks for a standard Shadow Step;
Zalthir does it however Gabe plays it. Kenshi (blind; *hears* the
shape):

> *"You skip the third breath. You don't center your ki in the hara
> — you let it bleed into your fingertips like a poison. It is
> technically wrong in every scroll, and yet... you arrived at your
> destination three seconds faster than the Grandmaster did. Show
> me what else you have unlearned."*

**Gabe picks Zalthir's stance now** — both productive:

- **Respectful:** Order has integrity. Kenshi codifies cleanly.
- **Irritated:** Their book-learning is noise. Kenshi senses it:
  *"You think we have never been hit. Hit me, then."* Brief
  sparring beat that humbles or confirms.

### The Kata of the Void — skill challenge

**3 successes before 3 failures, DC 14 (at level 8).**

Each round, Gabe picks one approach, **narrates first, rolls after:**

- **Acrobatics** — twist a rule of footwork or posture
- **Athletics** — rewrite a strike with brute Underdark efficiency
- **Wisdom (Insight)** — break a centering principle and explain
  why it works anyway

Examples Gabe can pick or invent:

- **The Efficient Strike** — jagged elbow vs. graceful punch.
  *"The corridor was narrow. The form was irrelevant."*
- **The Weightless Shadow** — footwork that should make him fall.
- **The Eldritch Flow** — Eldritch Claw Tattoo reach in baseline
  movement. Kenshi: *martial blasphemy.* Zalthir: *Tuesday.*

**3 successes** → Severed Path technique (Gabe picks one):

- **Flicker-Strike** — Shadow Step + bonus-action unarmed strike,
  no proficiency bonus on that attack. Mobility-aggression.
- **Ruthless Economy** — Flurry of Blows kill at 0 hp returns 1 ki.
- **Shadow-Grapple Optimization** — anchor *Darkness:* designate
  one ally (Daz) who can see through it as dim light, while Zalthir
  is touching that ally or the enemy they're targeting. **Solves
  the Daz/Zalthir Darkness friction directly.**

**3 failures** → undisciplined-not-heretical read; Watcher's
Stillness still given, no Severed Path. Khell-Vire doesn't care.

### Thorin with Master Archivist Philemon

**Setting:** Philemon's Inner Ward study (cluttered with
sentience-resonance instruments). One long afternoon + an evening
hour. ~30 min real time.

**Philemon:** Avowed monk specializing in **psychological
preservation and historical cataloging of sentient artifacts.**
Clinical-by-default, listening-by-capacity. He early-career
cataloged a sentient item that *self-terminated rather than be
classified*; the failure shaped his approach.

### Phase 1 — The Clinical Session (~10 min, comedic/dramatic)

Philemon meets the party in his study. Addresses Thorin first; then
**addresses Dawnbringer in third person** while she lies on a wooden
cradle:

> *"This is a remarkable specimen. Late Netherese-era recovery,
> sun-worship origin, sustained darkness exposure of approximately
> three to four millennia. I would like to begin with a
> non-invasive assessment of arcane stress levels."*

**Dawnbringer's brash deflection** (use her voice):

- *"Specimen? I have a name. He knows it. Use it."*
- *"Arcane stress levels? I am not a headache. I have just spent
  ten weeks killing things in the Underdark with this nice dwarf.
  I am doing **fine.**"*
- *"I am not a magical wizard thingy that purifies things. I am a
  high-quality sunblade that kills things. Ask the Heart Fungus if
  you can find what's left of it."*

She **deflects** Philemon's questions about Brysis, captivity,
isolation, by **redirecting to Thorin and combat exploits.** Brags
about Heart Fungus. Brags about wraith. Complains about dust.

**The Scabbard Dispute.** Philemon notices the **Vaultmaster
Locking Scabbard** (acquired from Valimor Brightgem in
Blingdenstone):

> *"The locking scabbard is psychologically restrictive. It stifles
> the blade's capacity for self-actualization. I would recommend
> removing it for the duration of our consultation — and considering
> its retirement entirely. A sentient weapon kept under threat of
> imprisonment is, by definition, not freely bonded."*

Thorin uses the scabbard's threat as a **working diplomatic tool**
with Dawnbringer. Philemon doesn't know this. The friction is the
seed of Phase 2.

### Phase 2 — The Pivot (~5 min, Thorin's choice)

GM calls it explicitly:

> *"You can side with the sword, side with the academy, or insist
> on partnership. Pick."*

**Path A — Radical Transparency.** Thorin sides with sword.
Philemon withdraws politely. Phase 3 happens privately at the inn,
just Thorin and Dawnbringer. **3-ritual prescription, no sealed
letter.** Layer 2 (the grief) surfaces alone with her or not at
all.

**Path B — Good Parent.** Thorin sides with academy. Dawnbringer
sulks audibly. Philemon completes clinical work. **3 rituals
(1, 3, 4); no Brysis-naming, no unfinished name.** Layer 2 sealed;
available later.

**Path C — Equal Partner (Recommended).** Thorin demands Philemon
address Dawnbringer **as a warrior** — not specimen, not relic.
Philemon adapts. Sets aside instruments. **Full prescription, all
5 rituals, sealed letter.**

### Phase 3 — The Quiet Hour (~10 min, contemplative)

Texture varies by path.

**Path C** — Philemon's gentle question:

> *"Dawnbringer. The corpse you were found in — was it your
> wielder's?"*

Dawnbringer falters. *No.* Brysis was her **captor.** Brysis killed
her **true wielder** (a sun-worshipper) and interred the blade with
her own corpse in deliberate cruelty. Dawnbringer has been carrying
that — *under* the fear of the dark, *under* the fear of
abandonment — for ~3,800 years.

**She cannot yet say the wielder's name.** When invited to try, she
falters and goes silent.

**Philemon's verdict to Thorin:**

> *"Your sword does not need polishing. She needs to remember what
> daylight feels like without needing to fight. And then, when she
> is ready and you are willing, she needs to grieve. The first
> work is light. The second work is mourning. We will start with
> the first."*

**Why Dawnbringer chose Thorin (small reveal):** the original
wielder was a fighter or paladin with **Thorin's gravity** —
direct, honest, contemptuous of subtle things. Thorin is a *cousin*
to whatever the original wielder was. He is the right *kind* of
bearer.

### Phase 4 — The Prescription (~5 min)

Handout: `handouts/thorin_dawnbringer_rituals.md` (path-scaled).

**Dawnbringer's pushback** (in character): *"You want me to sit in
dawnlight not drawn? I am MEANT to be drawn."* **Thorin's
mediation:** *"You've been forced to be silent for thousands of
years. Now you get to choose to be quiet sometimes."* Lean in.

### Grygum with Brother Vareth

**Setting:** Vareth's Inner Ward office, frantically organized. ~30
min real time across 2–3 sittings; multi-day in fiction.

**Vareth:** elderly half-elf Avowed. Decades-long quiet specialty
in half-orc-Bahamut converts — **invaded three years ago** by the
Stroud-Protanther flood. Frantically cataloging. *Thrilled* to meet
Grygum as a living data point.

### The Stroud Wake — guided tour (5–7 stations selected)

Vareth walks Grygum through **what Stroud accidentally built across
the Realms in three years.** Pick stations that fit your table.

Station handouts in `handouts/grygum_stroud_dossier.md`.

**Station 1 — The polemic shelf.** Stroud's own writing for 3
years. Multiple pamphlets, in-progress memoir in installments,
running corrections of every witness. Vareth has them organized by
*month.* Earliest tighter; later already mythologizing. ~3 min.

**Station 2 — The four schools of thought.** ~5 min.

- **Stroudite (establishment):** Bahamut hid the move from
  Protanther for 400 years. Backed by Protanther's own council
  speech. Grygum's default.
- **Protantherist (humility-by-choice):** Protanther was outplayed
  but *chose* to read the loss as divine. The reading was his act.
- **Skeptical (mundane upset):** Stroud got lucky. Theology is
  political cover for the alliance Protanther needed against
  Tiamat.
- **Reconciled:** both real; meaning genuinely contested.

One-page polemic from each available; **Ben can pick Grygum's
school between sessions.**

**Station 3 — The witnesses.** ~5 min. The council dragons —
**Otaaryliakkarnos (Eli), Nymmurh, Illeurath, Taz.** Each has a
different temperament. Plus Stroud's Tyranny of Dragons companions
and a few opportunist confabulators. Show **Illeurath's
deposition** (most reliable) and an **opportunist account** side by
side.

**Station 4 — The cultural wake.** ~3 min. *"Pulling a Stroud"* in
taproom slang. Waterdeep bard's spreading song. *The Stroud Gambit*
(chess opening). One commissioned fresco in a Triboar Bahamut
temple. A Tiamat-cultist polemic that misappropriates Stroud as
anti-metallic-dragon. Vareth tries to hum the song badly.

**Station 5 — The Stroudite lineage.** ~5 min. Grygum is one of
**only four** documented half-orc converts via the Stroud story in
the past 3 years. **First-generation founders without knowing it.**
Vareth offers letters of introduction to all three others.

**Station 6 — The pretenders and frauds.** ~3 min. Waterdeep
pawnshop selling "the actual chess piece" (different piece every
visit). Self-styled "Stroud apprentice" memoirist. A Beregost drunk
who claims to have taught Stroud chess for a copper.

**Station 7 — The disappointed (most poignant beat).** ~5 min.
Letters from people who pilgrimaged to actual Stroud and came back
disillusioned. Stroud is loud, repetitive, not particularly wise —
*but also kind, in his way.* The Yartar-tavern letter: *"He is not
what I expected. He is also not less. I think the story is bigger
than the man, and that is allowed."*

**Station 8 — The chess problem.** ~3 min. The actual move on
vellum, cross-referenced. *(GM picks the specific historical
problem; print a diagram so Ben can play it out.)* Per Ben's pick:
genuinely brilliant, a known problem.

**Station 9 — Protanther's 400 years and his speech.** ~7 min. The
load-bearing fact: Protanther was **mentally replaying the
unfinished Ortho game for 400 years.** Read Protanther's recorded
speech (via Illeurath's deposition):

> *"For 400 hundred years, I have played and replayed this game in
> my head. I have waited for Ortho to finish the game for four
> hundred years. I thought I had thought of every option, every
