# The Three Papers: Campaign Placement

## 1. The Threefold Proof

**Delivery:** The party is accosted after leaving Janussi’s crime scene, before Bookwyrm settles them into the Investigators’ Office.

**Courier:** Elian Voss, a junior records clerk formerly seconded to Kalan at the Sea Warden’s Tower. Bookwyrm returned him to ordinary cataloguing when she removed Kalan from the investigation.

Elian is frightened, indignant, and carrying Kalan’s annotated copy beneath his robes. He does not claim Kalan is innocent or sane. He says:

> “If you are going to investigate the Keeper’s death, do it the way Kalan asked. Record who casts what. Keep a sample nobody touches. Do not let the same Reader hold the evidence and tell you what it means.”

He gives them *The Threefold Proof*. In Kalan’s hand beside the chain-of-custody section is written: **REQUEST DENIED — J. CALLS THIS SOLIPSISM. B. SAYS NOTHING.**

This delivery makes the paper immediately actionable without identifying the murderer.

## 2. Kalan’s petition to Janussi

**Location:** Janussi’s administrative correspondence, misfiled inside the House of Mechanus appropriation folder rather than his personal papers.

**Discovery:** Found by searching Janussi’s office, asking Hollypocket about recent arguments, or following Elian’s statement that the Keeper rejected Kalan’s request.

Janussi has written across the first page:

> **Denied. The Avowed are not laboratory animals for the Gatewarden’s anxieties. No funds. No chambers. No further correspondence on this subject.**

This establishes the intellectual conflict, the requested expense, and Kalan’s insistence that Great Readers be subject to recorded procedure.

## 3. Bookwyrm’s private advice

**Location:** A retained draft in Bookwyrm’s locked correspondence drawer, beneath routine council memoranda. She never sent this copy because she delivered the recommendation orally.

**Discovery:** Best found after the party already suspects Bookwyrm’s cover-up—either during a warranted search of the Reader’s Tower or after Bookwyrm’s death. Finding it earlier makes her look too obviously central.

The document does not confess to murder. It proves that Bookwyrm understood Kalan’s system, recognized that it threatened Great Reader discretion, and offered to remove him from the investigation without allowing Council to debate the theory.

## Recommended sequence

1. **Elian accosts the party with *The Threefold Proof*.** They receive a method.
2. **Janussi’s file yields Kalan’s petition and Janussi’s contemptuous rejection.** They receive motive for the institutional conflict.
3. **Bookwyrm’s drawer yields her private advice.** They learn that the firing was deliberate containment, not merely irritation with Kalan’s Lost Lore obsession.

The three papers escalate cleanly: **idea → rejected reform → political suppression**.
