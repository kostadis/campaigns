## BEAT 1 — The Bookwyrm summons  *(fixed, ~20 min, FREE — framing, not a block)*

**Frame.** Reader's Tower, top floor. Tea poured before they arrive.
Composure worn as armor. She is **Skoda Vanaster**, green dragonborn First
Reader — and the **cover-up**: her nephew **Alkrist** poisoned the book; she
hacked out the heart, pre-locked the corpse with `speak with dead`, and wore
`disguise self` as Sylvira to frame her. **The whole scene is dramatic irony
you hold alone** — to the party she's just the boss who conscripted them.

**Her three hidden objectives (never stated):**
1. **Harvest** — how much have they pieced together? (Also tells *you* what
   Bookwyrm now knows going into Sessions 4–5.)
2. **Steer toward Sylvira / Daral** — the frame she built. Let her try;
   *don't let it stick as truth.*
3. **Gauge the threat** — she expected them to fail. If they're sharp, she
   recalibrates (the seed of why she later sacrifices Alkrist and dies).

**She plants her alibi:** *"I was abed. I came down from the baths at nine
and confirmed Kalan's orders."* ⭐ **Write this down — Deidran's dawn
sighting contradicts it later (she'd just dumped the cloak).**

### Deflection table — what they may report, and her move
| If they report… | Why it threatens her | Her move |
|---|---|---|
| **"Someone disguised as Sylvira"** *(they have this)* | *She* is the disguise | Reframe, don't deny: *"Or Sylvira wishes you to think she was impersonated. A curator of devils knows how a frame is built."* |
| **Corpse was `speak with dead`-locked** | She cast it to silence him | Disarming half-truth: *"That was me. I tried to question him at dawn; the dead would not answer. The killer reached him first."* |
| **Heart out, post-mortem, anti-resurrection** | She hacked it out | Agrees warmly, adds nothing: *"A thorough evil. He was not to speak from either side of the veil."* |
| **Poison / missing book** | Points at Daral (her decoy) | Encourages it: *"Daral loathed him for years. I would start there."* |
| **Two keys / missing locket** | The High Tower is her real prize | Goes **flat and brief**: *"Kalan holds the second. Old security theatre. Not your concern."* |

### Insight ladder (Mike especially)
- **DC 13:** she's steering — returns to Sylvira past what the evidence warrants.
- **DC 16:** the topic of the **keys** makes her shorten her sentences.
- **DC 18+:** one jaw-muscle when the **disguise** comes up. **Bank it, don't
  explain it** — it pays off the instant Fheminor's "not surprised" lands.

**Compliment-as-warning** (use once, when they show real competence):
*"That is a precise observation. You have been thorough."* Warm surface,
cold floor.

**Guardrails.** She **does not break** here. No slip past the DC-18
micro-tell. Don't overspend Session-4 material. She can muddy the disguise
thread, **not erase it** — they leave still holding "a shapeshifter was
here."

**Exit / handoff.** She dispatches them — *"Start with Daral and Sylvira;
report to me"* — which is her frame **and** conveniently the interviews you
want them running. Clean pivot into the sandbox.

**Tadric:** keep him **out of the room.** (His guilty 8am-onward read is
fine; a live flinch here risks tipping her early.)

## BEAT 2 — Kalan's key  *(fixed, FREE, on the way out — TODAY ONLY)*

Kalan steps out of a doorway in the corridor and presses a small brass key
into one PC's hand. The party already *knows* he holds it (Ch.57) — so this
lands as **confirmation and a goodbye**, not a mystery. Slow it down; don't
let them turn it into a negotiation.

> *"Whatever Bookwyrm is telling you, listen for what she isn't. The High
> Tower has two keys. Janussi wore one. I held the other. I have given it
> now to someone who is not me. If I do not see you tomorrow, do not look
> for me at my post."*

⭐ **Note which PC holds the key** — they open the High Tower door from
outside (~Session 6). Pick the player who'll be present and remembered.
