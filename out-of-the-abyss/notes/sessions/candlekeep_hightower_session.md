## What's true coming in (state out of Monday)

- **Bookwyrm is dead** (throat torn). **Key #1 is gone** — the Beast took it off
  her body and it is now with A'lai / bound for Manshoon.
- **Kalan is ALIVE.** He gave both #2 keys away (decoy → party, real → Tadric)
  and so was never the target. He is sidelined, watched, exhausted — and the
  only senior officer left who can touch the wards.
- **The wards are dropping.** The Keeper-of-Secrets line is broken; the keep's
  protective lattice is failing chamber by chamber.
- **The party knows their key was a decoy** (Monday's pivot). The milestone to
  **level 9** already landed Monday night.
- **Manshoon is incoming** — his shape came clear at Monday's cliffhanger.

## ⭐ THE MASTER FORK — resolve before you prep the table

Everything below branches on **Monday's race for Tadric.** Decide which line the
table is on and read only that branch's variant.

| | **LOST the race** (Tadric died / never twigged) | **WON the race** (Tadric lived) |
|---|---|---|
| **Keys** | Beast took the **real key #2** off Tadric → **Manshoon holds BOTH** | Party kept the **real key #2** → **Manshoon holds key #1 only** |
| **High Tower library** | A'lai can open it → **he's inside at the orb** | A'lai is **one key short** → library **stays sealed** |
| **This session is** | the **module-default rails**, nearly intact | the **earned variant** — party a move ahead |
| **A'lai escapes with** | both keys, to the lava chamber | **empty-handed** (if the party keeps #2) |
| **Wards hold at** | **30%** (library breached) | **~50%** (library never opened) |

> **Design note (why this is clean):** the "bad" Monday outcome drops the party
> onto the path the old script already prepped — so losing the race costs them
> position, not playability. Winning earns them a denied Vault front-door and
> leverage (key #2 still in hand). Reward the win; don't punish the loss into a
> dead end.

> ### ⚖️ RULING — what the two keys actually gate (resolves the WON-branch snag)
> The two High Tower keys gate the **lightning orb / ward-core** (commanding the
> keep's wards) — **not** the everyday **library reading room**, which opens to
> the **Gatewarden's authority** (so
> **Kalan, alive, gets the party in to the cryptogram book** in either branch).
> Consequences that make the fork consistent:
> - **Both keys needed to command the orb.** LOST → Manshoon has both → A'lai
>   dropped the wards to 30% and runs free. WON → the keys are **split** → **no
>   one** can fully command the orb → the wards **can't be cut from the core**
>   (Kalan holds ~50%), which is *why* winning the race keeps the wards up.
> - **The lava-chamber Vault is reached by the CRYPTOGRAM**, not the keys — so
>   the race to the Book runs in **both** branches regardless of who holds what.
> - Net: holding key #2 doesn't lock the party out of anything (Kalan's authority
>   covers the reading room); it **locks Manshoon out of the ward-core.**

**Two overlay forks** recur below:
- **Moziqodo (carried from Monday):** *killed* (not present) · *fled* (reappears
  here) · *alive-and-bound* (deployed by A'lai).
- **Sylvira:** **absent by default** — the party skipped her on Day One and she
  is a wary, plague-ill first-contact suspect, **not** a recruited ally. The
  *late-recruit* branch exists only if the party treated her plague **and**
  cleared her of the frame during Monday's wall (GM's call whether that
  happened). If you're unsure, she's **absent.**

# BEAT 1 — The Approach (~15 min)

**Geography (unchanged):** Sea Warden's Tower roof → high covered walkway →
Skyscraping Lobby → staircase to the High Tower library. A Watcher on guard lies
dead. Library door: arcane-locked (9th), `mordenkainen's private sanctum`,
alarm keyed to the dead Keeper.

### Getting up there
- **Kalan (alive) is the party's way up and in.** Where the old script used
  Tadric's `fly`, here Kalan — barred from the case but not from his own
  tower — moves them: a Watcher's stair, a warden's override on a barred
  gallery, or (if Tadric lived) Tadric ferrying two at a time while Kalan works
  the wards below. Kalan does **not** fight. He **aims** them, as he did at the
  seam.
- **Mid-approach, the wards flex** (narrative, no mechanics): each PC gets a
  one-round vision (full per-PC text in the surface-madness thread / arc file
  Beat 2). Daz → Vaelissa's face; Zalthir → Jimjar's voice; Thorin → Brysis's
  hand; Grygum → ⭐ **Bahamut absent** (negative space); Glabbagool → *"Mother."*

### Branch on the master fork
- **LOST:** the library door is **already open** — A'lai used both keys. The
  air past it hums wrong; the lightning orb is lit.
- **WON:** the door is **sealed and holding.** A'lai is **outside** it, at the
  Skyscraping Lobby, and he turns as they arrive — because he needs the key the
  party is carrying. *This is an ambush for key #2, not a breach.*

# BEAT 2 — A'lai On Screen + The Fight (~40–45 min combat)

> **A'lai is banked, not re-proven.** Alkrist named him the brains under *zone
> of truth* in Ch.57. This is the first time the party **sees him act** — the
> payoff, not a deduction. Don't re-litigate whodunit. Let him be calm, courteous,
> and entirely unbothered to be caught.

**Inside / at the door:** A'lai stands holding a **sapphire** (the spell
component he stole — `instant summons`, the Manshoon signal). He meets their
eyes and **smashes it.**

> **A'lai:** "That was the signal. He is on his way. You have three minutes, or
> four, depending on traffic. I would not have chosen to meet you here, but you
> have been so *thorough.*"

### A'lai — scaled CR 9 (archmage chassis; deltas from arc-file scaling table)
- **From MM Archmage**, with: **no 8th/9th-level slots · HP −25% · save DCs −1**.
- Keeps the **`dimension door` scroll** exit (route: the lava chamber).
- **Tactics — he wants to delay 3–4 rounds, not win:** R1 `wall of force` to
  split the party or wall off a melee threat; `counterspell` held; `mirror
  image` up. R2+ `cone of cold` / `lightning bolt` chip; re-`counterspell` Daz's
  big turns. **At HP ≤ 50% he reads the scroll and vanishes.**
- **What he leaves with:**
  - **LOST branch** — **both keys** (he already had them). Gone to the lava chamber.
  - **WON branch** — **whatever he could grab.** If the party kept key #2 on a
    defended PC, he leaves **empty-handed** (and that is a real party win — say
    so with the wards holding at 50%). If a PC was downed/grappled and the key
    was takeable, he gets it and the branches converge toward "Manshoon has both."

### Moziqodo overlay (only if it survived Monday)
- **Killed Monday →** not here. Cut the beat.
- **Fled / alive-and-bound →** enters through the **high windows**, Iron Bands
  hex-marks burning on his neck. **Scaled CR 5** (HP −30%; multiattack 3→2;
  paralysing-breath save DC −2; **Abyssal Plague bite rider intact — it's plot,
  not damage**). He charges melee in confused fury — he doesn't fully grasp who
  he's been pointed at.
  - **Sylvira ABSENT (default):** no one can unbind him. He **fights for A'lai**
    the whole fight, then — when A'lai dimension-doors out and the leash-holder
    is gone — he may break off and flee (carry his state to the Vault session as
    *unbound and loose*). The party gets **no archmage support**; this is the
    raw version of the fight.
  - **Sylvira LATE-RECRUITED (only if earned):** she moves to the broken window
    and attempts `dispel magic` upcast 5th — **DC 19 Arcana, +9, succeeds on
    10.** Iron-band marks fade; Moziqodo *stops*, looks at his mother, looks at
    A'lai, and **turns on A'lai** (by ~R3). Failed first roll: she eats a
    `lightning bolt`, retries at advantage.

### Trophy plants

- **Iron Bands fragment** (only if the binding is broken — late-recruit branch).
- Moziqodo's **hex-rune scar** if examined post-fight.
- ⛔ **No Gatewarden pin** — that trophy lived on Kalan's corpse in the old

# BEAT 3 — The Wards: Kalan Holds His Post (~10 min)

> **The thematic payoff of "The Gatewarden Lives."** The man they couldn't use to
> win the case is the man who keeps the keep standing. Give him this.

- **Cryptogram book** in indestructible glass. Daz transcribes (**DC 10
  Arcana**). **DC 15 Investigation:** two clue-lines carry a **faint thumbprint
  smudge** — A'lai studied this for a while. **He has a head start.** (Six clues
  verbatim: arc file Beat 4 / `candlekeep_vault_session.md`.)
- **The reroute — Kalan runs it** (where the old script used Tadric):

  > **Kalan:** "They took me off the murder. They did not take me off my wards.
  > Stand where I tell you and do not touch the orb."

  10 minutes, **DC 18 Arcana** (Kalan +Tadric if alive +Watchers assisting).
  - **LOST branch →** the library was breached; best they hold is **30%.**
  - **WON branch →** the library was never opened; they restore to **~50%**, and
    Kalan is grimly pleased. *"He will have to come the hard way now."*
- The **Endless Chant has stopped completely.** The silence is its own alarm.

### ⛔ Orphaned-clue cleanup (do this consciously)
- The forged **"Cursed Tower, midnight. Bring no one. — S"** note (the old DC-16
  "A'lai frames Sylvira" tell) lived on Kalan's body. **Kalan's alive → that
  scene never happened.** If you still want the framing on the table, plant it
  **here as an optional find** among A'lai's papers (a half-finished forgery in
  Sylvira's hand). **Low priority** — it's flavor now, not load-bearing; the
  whodunit is already banked.

# BEAT 4 — Manshoon Arrives Below (~10 min, no combat)

Teleportation pulse. From the Sea Warden's Tower below: crossbow fire,
unfamiliar accents. **Manshoon's voice,** `magic mouth`-projected, calm:

> "Citizens of Candlekeep. My name is Manshoon. I am here for one book. It will
> not be missed by anyone who matters. Stand aside and the keep returns to you
> intact in an hour. Resist and the keep returns to you in pieces."

Two routes to the Vault, and the master fork shades which one the party *has*:
- **LOST branch:** Manshoon (via A'lai) has both keys and a head start — the
  party is **chasing.** The cryptogram/lava-chamber route is how they catch up.
- **WON branch:** the front door is **denied** — Manshoon is **forced** to the
  cryptogram/lava-chamber back door, same as the party. They're **racing
  level**, and they made that happen.
