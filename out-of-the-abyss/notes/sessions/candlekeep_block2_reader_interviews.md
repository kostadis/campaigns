# Candlekeep Block 2 — Reader Interviews + Dining Hall

GM working doc. Block 1 spent; Daral is alive and terrified and clinging to
Daz and Thorin. **Run this doc from here.**

---

## State at the cut (do NOT re-deliver)

- **Book in evidence.** Midnight Tears identified by name. Daral admitted the
  punch + took back *The Golden Ass*; licked pages mid-interview; book seized.
  The party has **watched him self-dose** — whether they've connected that to
  midnight is on them.
- **Daral is tagged to the party.** Alive, no symptoms yet (Midnight Tears is
  silent until midnight). Terrified. Volunteering nothing, just staying close.
- **Grygum holds the High Tower key** (Kalan's handoff done).
- **Conclusions lit:** #1 (poison vector ✅, name ✅), #3 (disguise ✅).
  **Pending this block: #2 (lead chalice), #4/#6 (Fheminor hinge), optional #3
  confirm (Teles).**

---

## Win Condition

Party exits Block 2 with:
1. The heart-in-chalice found — and the `speak with dead` failure noted.
2. ⭐ Fheminor's **"Bookwyrm was not surprised."** This is the session's one
   non-negotiable. If only one scene lands, land this.
3. Sylvira interviewed cold. Either treated (ally banked) or at minimum the
   *"I did not kill him. I loved him"* line delivered.
4. Daral's fate decided before midnight (lesser restoration / protection from
   poison = save; inaction = midnight corpse).

---

## Default Trajectory (if party does nothing clever)

- Daral does not connect the dots. Midnight comes. He dies in the corridor
  between scenes, still attached to Daz's arm. Found face-down at the next
  bell, ink-stained, thumb at his lips. The party watched it happen.
- Sylvira doesn't get treated. She's bedridden by Day 3. The S6 battlefield
  ally is gone.
- Teles keeps his secret. He blackmails Bookwyrm in the morning; Bookwyrm
  silences him before Day 3. The party never gets the cloak tip.
- Fheminor tells them nothing beyond pleasantries unless they earn it.

---

## Clever Play (know it so you recognize when to nudge)

- **Save Daral by deduction.** He licked the pages. Midnight Tears. The math
  is there. *A poisoner does not re-read his own pages.* — both his
  exoneration and his death clock, in one sentence. The party saves him by
  reasoning, not a Medicine roll.
- **Treat Sylvira's plague.** Grygum: DC 18 Medicine or `lesser restoration`.
  This is the only path to the S6 archmage ally since the demon-lord-evidence
  handoff from S1 never happened.
- **Stay quiet in Teles's scene.** He's old and bitter and likes to feel
  superior. Let him have the insight. Players who share something real (a
  vulnerability, a real thing they know) unlock him faster than Persuasion.
- **Ask Fheminor what she noticed, not what she did.** She says nothing about
  herself unless pushed. She says everything about *others* — and she watched
  Bookwyrm the morning of the death.

## SCENE A — Southern Dining Hall + Kitchens (~1 block)

These are adjacent — sweep both in the same block.

**Setup.** Southern Dining Hall, afternoon. The long tables cleared from
Deadwinter breakfast but not reset for dinner. Milil's statue in the
alcove — god of bards, open hands, a brass chalice in them. Two supper
waiters are stacking linens. They recognize Daral from last night and go
quiet.

**The waiters** (free NPC moment): These are the two Daral bragged to.
*"Give that man a real Deadwinter present."* They heard it. If the party
presses them, they confirm the quote but can add: *"He was drunk and angry.
He didn't look like a man with a plan — he looked like a man whose feelings
got the better of him."* Useful: it paints Daral's brag as impulsive, not
premeditated.

**The search.**
- **DC 15 Investigation** (room sweep) or **DC 12** if they go straight to
  the altars/statues.
- ⭐ Lead chalice on the Milil statue holds: Janussi's **heart**, a
  **bloodstained meat cleaver**, and **burnt incense ash**.
- **`Speak with dead` on the heart FAILS.** *The failure is the clue.* Say
  nothing. Let the player roll. Let the silence sit.
- ⭐ **Lead chalice = anti-divination.** Tie it to the lead locket chain from
  the chamber: *someone planned this murder around Candlekeep's own spell
  collection.* That's not a passing visitor. **Make sure this is said aloud**
  — even as a PC hypothesis. It lights **#2** and starts narrowing to an
  insider.

**The Kitchens (next door — fold in free or with 2 min extra).**
- **Sprig Summerfoot** (halfling kitchen worker, nervous, specific). At ~1am
  last night: *"Sylvira came through. Didn't say a word — just pointed at
  her throat like she couldn't speak and took one of the big cleavers. Grabbed
  honey off the shelf as cover, I thought. Odd time for honey."*
- ⭐ **The silence tell.** A tiefling who cast `silence` on herself can't speak.
  Sprig doesn't know that — she just thought it was rude. The party may
  assemble the tell: the "Sylvira" was in a silence bubble to avoid the
  *Endless Chant* detection ward. (Or they may not. Bank it.)
- Sprig can also confirm the cleaver matches the one in the chalice by shape
  (she knows her own kitchen tools).

**Branch — if they don't go here.** Fembris finds them: *"The kitchen staff
are upset about something in the Southern Hall. They won't say what."* Or
Daral himself, if he's had time to think: *"We should check the Milil
alcove. After the Yule feast in '82 someone hid a… it doesn't matter. It's
where you hide something in the Hall."* Queenie can also be found staring up
at the chalice. Use whichever is most organic.

## SCENE B — Sylvira, the Infernal Fortress (~1 block)

**⚠️ FIRST CONTACT.** The party has never met her. She's the "devil woman"
from the witness accounts: the one who smelled wrong, who Queenie hissed at.
Open cold — wary, ill, defensive. No prior rapport.

**Setup.** Her quarters in the Infernal Fortress, a cramped scholar's den
that smells like sulphur and fever. She's wrapped in a study robe, forearm
covered. Fever is visible. She does not offer tea.

**Her story (public):** alone last night; no alibi; she and Janussi hadn't
spoken in weeks. She doesn't flinch from being a suspect — she seems almost
past caring.

**Her truth (earned, if pressed honestly or shown the bite mark):** Janussi
was her lover. Gave her ten days to remove her son Moziqodo or he'd report
the breach. She was at the Cursed Tower last night trying to reach him.
The bite on her forearm is Moziqodo's — **Abyssal Plague**. She's been
treating it herself with suppressive scrolls.
> *"I did not kill him. I loved him."*

Deliver that line flat and move on. Don't explain it.

**The Grygum beat.** DC 18 Medicine to diagnose the plague, or just `lesser
restoration` — she accepts if the offer seems genuine. *"You are not what I
expected of a half-orc cleric."* Treating her = she survives to be the S6
battlefield ally. Make the path clear but not easy.

**Insight ladder.**
- DC 12: She's bracing for accusation, not building a defense. That's the
  posture of someone grieving, not someone guilty.
- DC 16: The covered forearm. She's been hiding it all morning.
- DC 18+: When she says she was at the Cursed Tower, something in her loosens
  — relief at the truth, not tension at the lie.

**Kalan's alibi.** She confirms he was with her at 10:15pm. This matches his
own account and his note about the Chamber of Lost Lore. She says it without
prompting if the party mentions Kalan. She liked him.

**Daral complication.** If Daral is in the room: she looks at him for a long
moment. *"I need you to know I didn't know about the book."* She doesn't
know what the book was. She just wants him to know she's not the one who
killed his enemy and left him holding it. Daral believes her.

## SCENE C — Teles Ahvoste (~1 block)

Old. Very old. Thin hair, ink-stained collar, studies that overlook the
Eastern Ward. He teaches the taxonomy of curses. He finds everyone's
pretensions amusing, including his own.

**He'll see them.** He was expecting someone to come. He's been waiting.

**His story (public):** nothing useful; old man who sleeps badly and knows
too much and is too tired to help investigators.

**His truth:** at approximately 2am he saw a figure in a Sylvira-style robe
moving near the Keeper's tower, carrying a lantern. He watched it for a
minute, then:
> *"I thought: that is interesting. Sylvira Narath is a tiefling. Tieflings
> have darkvision. Tieflings do not carry lanterns in the dark. So whoever
> that was — it was not Sylvira."*

He then deduced — correctly — that only one person in the keep had motive,
means, and the right robe: Bookwyrm. He's been sitting on this for two days
because he **intends to use it.** He wants something from Bookwyrm; he
hasn't decided what yet. He's not afraid of her. He's entertained by her.

**He will not share this for free.** Earn it one of two ways:
1. **Share a real vulnerability** — something true, not a bargaining chip.
   He can tell the difference. If a player gives him something genuine about
   why this matters, he responds in kind.
2. **DC 18 Persuasion** — but not cold. He needs to be engaged first; he
   needs to feel like the conversation is worth his time. Get him talking
   about the taxonomy of curses, or about Janussi, or about the irony of
   Bookwyrm covering up what she couldn't prevent. Then roll.

**His blackmail plan.** He hasn't committed. He half-hopes the party catches
Bookwyrm so he doesn't have to decide. If they tell him Bookwyrm's already
under scrutiny, he may hand over what he knows free — but only if he believes
the cover-up is already coming down. Convince him it is.

**Lights #4 (the disguise was Bookwyrm).** This is the piece that, combined
with the Fheminor hinge, gives the party a complete picture of the cover-up
before Day 3.

**Teles on Daral** (if Daral is present): *"You're still alive. Interesting."*
Pause. *"I did wonder."* He knows about Midnight Tears — not because he's
guilty, but because he knows everything about poisons in Candlekeep's
collection. He won't say more unless pressed. If pressed: *"The man who is
dying of his own gift is rarely the one who poisoned it."* He's already
worked it out. He's been watching everyone.

## SCENE D — Fheminor Scrivenbark, Founder's Court (~1 block)

Old halfling. Careful. Moves through the cloisters like she's cataloguing
every crack in the stone. She met you at 8:30 the morning of the death when
she was turned away from the chamber door. She has been piecing things
together since then on her own time.

**She doesn't trust Daz.** She watches him the whole time. She won't be
direct if he's the one asking. Route the question through someone else.

**Her story (public):** nothing to hide; met Janussi yesterday at 4pm; sorry
he's dead; wishes you well with the investigation.

**Her truth (earned — share something she can verify, or DC 16 Insight
shows the party she's holding back):**
> *"He said: 'I am old, and I am tired, and the keep needs a hand that does
> not ache. I will name you tomorrow.'"*

She is now the unnamed successor of a dead Keeper. She is in mortal danger.
She has been conducting her own quiet investigation because if Bookwyrm
figures out what Janussi told her, she is next. She does not say this. But
she will, if the party makes her feel like they're the better option to trust.

**The hinge.** She watched Bookwyrm at the chamber door the morning of the
death. She tells this last, like an afterthought:
> ⭐ *"Bookwyrm was not surprised."*

**Deliver this line and end the scene.** Do not gloss it. Do not editorialize.
Close with the ambient sound of the cloisters. Let the table sit with it.

**Why it matters (GM-only).** Combined with the DC-18 jaw-muscle from
Bookwyrm's own summons scene, this makes the cover-up *feel* rather than
just *be deduced.* It's the emotional engine of the Day-3 pivot.

**Daral's push (if needed).** If Fheminor is hedging: Daral — quietly,
still terrified, still thinking she's the sharpest person in the keep —
*"Fheminor. Tell them."* She'll comply or she won't. Either way it changes
the room.

## Stage Directions

- **The chalice failure** — let the player roll, let it fail, say nothing.
  The silence is the clue. Count to three before speaking.
- **"I did not kill him. I loved him."** Deliver flat. No tears, no drama.
  Move on immediately. The table will circle back.
- **Teles's lantern line** — he says it almost to himself, like he's
  amused that nobody else noticed. Not dramatic. Offhand. That's what makes
  it land.
- **"Bookwyrm was not surprised."** Last line of the scene. Then: close the
  door. That's it.
- **Midnight reminder.** If they haven't saved Daral and you're in the third
  block: *"The keep bells strike eleven."* One sentence. No explanation. If
  they've forgotten, they've forgotten.
