# Regarding the Gatewarden’s Second Petition

*Private advice from Bookwyrm, First Reader, to Janussi, Keeper of Tomes*

**Classification:** Private council correspondence  
**Recommendation:** Deny the petition; remove Strongbranch from sensitive inquiries

Janussi,

I have read Strongbranch’s second petition. Your objection to its expense is sound. Your description of it as solipsism grants the proposal too little credit.

It is not chiefly a theory of knowledge. It is a jurisdictional proposal dressed as research.

The requested rooms, clerks, cabinets, and wards are inexpensive by the standards of this institution. The authority attached to them is not. Strongbranch proposes that every death, disappearance, restricted theft, or irregularity in the wards create an evidentiary jurisdiction independent of the Reader responsible for the place in which it occurred.

Under his practice, a Great Reader’s spell would be entered into a ledger. A sealed room would produce an entry list. A blocked divination would become a fact requiring explanation. A refusal to surrender an object would be preserved as conduct. No Reader could examine evidence alone, and no office could dispose of its own irregularity without creating another record elsewhere.

The paired seals are the heart of it. He asks that the Gatewarden and Lorekeeper jointly control material gathered from any office, including ours. Once accepted, this will not remain a trial concerning suspicious deaths. It will become a permanent power to audit the Avowed under the language of method.

**His most dangerous sentence is the shortest: “Rank is not an evidentiary root.”**

It is difficult to answer because it is substantially correct. It is also incompatible with governance if allowed to become an absolute principle. Candlekeep cannot function if every exercise of learned discretion is treated as a possible contamination event requiring review by a lesser office.

Do not debate him on the merits. His construction turns opposition into further evidence that procedure is needed. Do not call him mad before Council; he will answer with ledgers. Do not attack the Threefold Practice as useless; enough of it is useful that the younger Avowed will mistake usefulness for wisdom.

His unauthorized consultations in the Chambers of Lost Lore provide the cleaner ground. They demonstrate loss of focus, compromised judgment, and reliance upon an unaccountable external intelligence. Require a complete accounting of those visits. Suspend further consultation. Deny the requested appropriation. If he persists, remove him from inquiries requiring institutional discretion and review his custody of restricted keys.

I will do this publicly if you prefer. The First Reader may remove him from a particular investigation without making his theory the subject of Council debate.

One further caution: do not destroy the petition. Enter it as incomplete research and restrict its circulation. Suppression would vindicate him; publication would recruit him followers. Administrative burial is safer than either.

Do not mistake my concern. Strongbranch is not dangerous because he is entirely wrong.

He is dangerous because he has found a method by which being partly right can become an office.

**Bookwyrm**  
First Reader of Candlekeep
