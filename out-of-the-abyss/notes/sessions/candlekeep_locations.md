# Candlekeep — Locations reference card

A distilled GM cheat sheet of just the Candlekeep buildings,
officers, and curators we are likely to actually use at the
table during the OOTA arc. Drawn from
`docs/background/1073077-Inside_the_Great_Library_(Candlekeep_Locations).json`.

For full per-building canon, read the source JSON. For Avowed
scholar dossiers (Yvenne, Khell-Vire, Kenshi, Philemon, Vareth)
and named officer dossiers, see
`notes/npcs/candlekeep_avowed.md`.

For how each location is used in the active session prep, see
`notes/sessions/candlekeep_day_one.md`.

Gatewarden — only they hold keys to the High Tower Library. The
**Council of 12** (Chapter House) seats: Keeper of Tomes, First
Reader, eight Great Readers, Gatewarden, Lorekeeper.

## Active-prep locations (the ones our session uses)

### Arcanium / Hall of Divination — *Daz, with Yvenne*

**Where:** West Inner Ward, runs along the western wall, adjoins
the Sea Warden's Tower.

**What it is:** a magnificent library of arcane magic, with one
section per school. The huge transept on the south side is the
**Hall of Divination** — divining pool + several scrying mirrors
that legally spy on halls of power around Faerûn under
centuries-old contracts.

**Texture:**
- Marble floors, mahogany shelves, priceless art.
- **Spectral mage hands** clasp brooms and dusters, fetch tomes,
  hold out chairs, slap hands. **Touching books is forbidden.**
- Curator: **Teles Ahvoste.**
- Librarian: **Adeilina** — authoritarian high-elf archmage.

**Why it's our pick for Yvenne:** drow-exile-community scrying
feeds give her a plausible scholarly residency here. Daz gets to
sit in arguably the most prestigious arcane library in Faerûn —
which, given his patron-question, is the right altitude for the
research.

### The Atheneum (alcove) — *Daz, quiet sittings + the empty cutout*

**Where:** large complex of buildings in the north section of the
Inner Ward, dedicated to politics, history, society. Curator
**Fheminor Scrivenbark.**

**What it is:** sub-libraries — Temple of Law, Echoing Hall
(history), Heritage House (cultures). The **minor-Houses
political-genealogy collection** lives in one of the side rooms.

**Why we use it:** the empty T'sarran scroll-tube cutout is here.
Yvenne walks Daz across from the Hall of Divination to retrieve
it. Quieter than the Arcanium for the disclosure-trade beats.

### Echoing Hall — *Zalthir, with Khell-Vire*

**Where:** part of the Atheneum, North Inner Ward. Towering
domed rotunda, floor upon floor of historical tomes around a
large central well.

**Texture:** **footsteps echo loudly long after a scholar has
stopped moving.** The motif is "the echoes of history can still
be felt in the present." On the nose for shadow-discipline
lineage research; Khell-Vire likes it deliberately.

### Sea Warden's Tower (sparring rooms) — *Zalthir's Trial of the Broken Mirror*

**Where:** West Inner Ward, adjoining the Arcanium. Stout,
thick-walled tower; main base of Candlekeep's Watchers.

**Texture:** training grounds, sparring rooms, armoury of magic
weapons. Kalan Strongbranch's relocated quarters are upstairs.
The **Order of the Enlightened Shadow** keeps a chamber here
under guest-tenure with the Watchers — Master Kenshi runs Trials
in the central sparring room.

### The Grove — *Zalthir's shared-silence beat*

**Where:** South Inner Ward, garden under Exaltation. Sheltered
from sea breezes, leafy summer shade.

**Texture:** **Pont de Paramours** covered bridge; woodland
cemetery where every successive Keeper of Tomes is buried. Quiet,
liminal, dawn-appropriate.

### Towers of Wonder — *Thorin, with Philemon*

**Where:** westernmost point of the Inner Ward; series of
hollow spires.

**Texture:** **no stairs or floors.** Walls lined top to bottom
with tomes on magic items and wondrous objects; scholars
levitate up and down on **floating disks.** Common to see
Avowed sitting cross-legged in mid-air consulting a tome.

- Curator: **Teles Ahvoste** (also curator of the Arcanium).
- Librarian: **Fonkin** — talkative gnome sage who loves
  recommending reading.

**Why it's the right place for Philemon:** sentient artifact
preservation lives at the seam of "magic items" and "wondrous
objects." Philemon's hovering sentience-resonance instruments
are at home in zero-stair architecture. Glabbagool will be
delighted by the floating disks.

### Reading Salon — *Thorin's Quiet Hour*

**Where:** Exaltation, South Inner Ward — sits under the
Southern Dining Hall.

**Texture:** *officially* the Reading Salon; *unofficially* the
**Silent Salon** — protected by a permanent {@spell silence}
spell. Comfy armchairs, lancet windows for natural light. Most
Avowed live in shared dormitories with one small embrasure if
they're lucky, so quiet + light indoors is a rare luxury.

**Why we use it:** **Dawnbringer cannot speak in here.** The
silence is permanent and mechanical. Philemon books the salon for
Phase 3 when the work requires Dawnbringer to *not* be able to
argue. Use the silence; don't let her bargain out of it.

### Immortal Chambers — *Grygum, with Vareth*

**Where:** West Inner Ward, between the Towers of Wonder and the
House of Mechanus. Series of green-roofed rectangular buildings.

**Texture:** library dedicated to theology and religion. Floor
tiled with **one symbol per deity.** Vareth's office is in one
of the side rooms.

- Curator: **A'lai Aivenmore.**
- Librarian: **Pizwog** — ancient tortle sage. Helpful, friendly,
  moves / talks / acts at **1 mph.** Recurring sight gag while
  Stations 1–8 of the Stroud Wake run.

### Drakonoikos — *Grygum, Station 9 (Protanther)*

**Where:** North Inner Ward.

**Texture:** built **inside the preserved corpse of an ancient
red dragon, Fyrentennimar.** Bones for pillars, scales for
walls, ceiling and floor. Several rooms made entirely from the
wyrm's hollowed-out carcass.

- Curator: **Bookwyrm** (the First Reader).
- Librarian: **Thava Norixius** — elderly dragonborn mage,
  world's leading dragon scholar. Dragon-school sympathizer in
  private, Bahamut-school in public.

**Optional:** the **Dragon Breath Simulation Room** — five
taxidermied chromatic dragon heads above the entrance; call out a
color in Draconic, take an *illusory* breath weapon, suffer a
real Constitution save (psychic damage if reduced to 0; one level
of exhaustion either way). Grygum can or can't.

### House of Alaundo — *Grygum, optional detour*

**Where:** West Inner Ward, center of Founders Court. Brass
statue of Alaundo bearing quill and inkpot on a dais.

**Texture:** repository of Alaundo's nine volumes of prophecies
plus original fragments behind protective glass.

- Curator: **Janussi.**
- Librarian: **Inda** — half-orc sage who **secretly worships
  Alaundo as a deity.** Useful Grygum mirror.

**Locked behind plot:** the **Echoes of Alaundo** — the Seer's
final, most foreboding prophecies, recorded into prismatic
gemstones and stored in the **Vault of Secrets** beneath
Candlekeep. Only the First Reader and Keeper know how to coax
them forth. **Inaccessible to the party.** The accessible
gallery copies still cover Alaundo's main body of work — relevant
for Ilvara's dying prophecy and Jimjar warnings.

### Infernal Fortress — *Demon-lord evidence handoff*

**Where:** North Inner Ward.

**Texture:** constructed from **avernium** — a strange black
alloy that glimmers fiery red in moonlight. Library on the Nine
Hells, the Abyss, fiendish denizens, earthly cults. Tomes are
**chained to black metal shelves** — they cannot be removed even
to a study area. **Lava pool and fountain** in the central hall;
air thick and hot.

**Upstairs:** **summoning chambers** for binding and consulting
devils and demons. Janussi's permission required; some proof of
competency expected; accidents have happened.

- Curator: **Sylvira Savikas.**
- Librarian: **Gruxlug the Tiny** — small bent-over half-orc
  warlock of the fiend; mutters; doesn't like visitors.

**Personal complication:** Sylvira's demonspawn son **Moziqodo**
escaped the Mordenkainen's mansion she'd trapped him in. Now
*the Beast of Candlekeep,* he stalks rooftops at night,
disappearing at the first sign of a Watcher. Half-eaten remains
of pigeons, doves, gulls and crows litter the Cursed Tower.

### Bell Tower — *Polly Pocket containment option*

**Where:** westernmost tower of Exaltation, South Inner Ward.

**Texture:** topped by the belfry that tolls the hours from 6 am
to midnight. Higher floors hold rarely-used guest suites; lower
parts contain Candlekeep's prison cells. Each cell has a small
barred window and is **enveloped by a localized antimagic field.**

**Canonical caveat:** the cells are **very rarely used** — anyone
guilty of a major offense is **swiftly exiled** rather than
imprisoned. The canon explicitly says Candlekeep holds prisoners,
it does not keep them. This is consistent with the party's
chapter-54 instinct that handing Polly to Candlekeep is *delay,
not solution.*

## Latent / future-arc locations (don't run yet, but bank them)

### Chambers of Lost Lore (sub-Candlekeep)

Underground passage network where **Gatewarden Kalan
Strongbranch** has been making weekly trips to consult an
**otherworldly being.** This is the canonical hook on his
character. Plausible directions to land later, *not this
session*: the consulted being is connected to Daz's patron, to
Jimjar's true nature, or to Vaelissa T'sarran's mirror
investigation. **Treat as banked mystery, available for
redirection.**

### High Tower Library (the cryptogram + lightning orb)

Sealed vault above the High Tower's Skyscraping Lobby. Contains
a single book under indestructible glass displaying a
**cryptogram** that hides the location of the **Vault of
Secrets.** Also contains the **lightning orb** that powers
Candlekeep's wards (DC 20 Arcana to deactivate; 10 minutes to
reactivate). Two keys exist; only the Keeper of Tomes and the
Gatewarden hold them.

**Why we care:** atmospheric backdrop. Don't engage unless the
campaign turns hard toward Candlekeep itself.

### Vault of Secrets

Hidden under Candlekeep, accessed by solving the High Tower
cryptogram and filling the inkpot of the Alaundo statue in the
House of Alaundo (the dais slides back to reveal a long
staircase). Holds the most dangerous tomes and (per the locations
supplement's proposed canon) the *Echoes of Alaundo* — the
Seer's gem-stored final prophecies.

### Cursed Tower

Abandoned ruined turret where an arcane experiment killed monks
centuries ago. Sealed with arcane locks; warning signs nailed to
all doors. **The Beast of Candlekeep (Moziqodo) lives here now.**
Anyone non-fiend / non-undead spending 10+ minutes inside makes
DC 13 Con save or gains a level of exhaustion; cumulative on
each subsequent hour.

**Use only if:** the party investigates the half-eaten birds
and pursues the Moziqodo thread, or Sylvira asks for help.

### Jewel of the Styx

Library built from the hull of a captured pirate warship. Mostly
nautical tomes. **Every midnight,** an ethereal crew of wights
materializes to sweep, hoist sails, sing shanties, and swig rum.
**They will attack anyone they don't recognize as Avowed**, but
can't move more than 100 feet from the ship. Disappear at
pre-dawn.

**Use only if:** the party gets curious or wanders the wards at
night.

### House of Records ("the Registry")

L-shaped building south of Founders Court. *In theory* contains a
record of every book ever to enter Candlekeep. *In practice* the
catalogue is so badly catalogued itself that the Avowed joke it
is easier to find a book by physically searching the Great
Library than by looking it up in the Registry. Useful texture
for any "we tried to look it up" beat.

## Ambient flavor — drop-ins for any scene

These are short bits from the supplement that are good for
scene-grounding when you need a sentence of texture and don't
want to invent.

- **Philosopher's Court** (South Inner Ward): triangular
  courtyard with stoas; oversized chessboard with 3-foot marble
  pieces in the center. Most social spot in Candlekeep. *Use if
  Vareth wants to show Grygum where Stroud's Tessarra's Lock has
  been actually played out for fun by Avowed.*
- **The Refectory** (Exaltation): communal dining hall; trestle
  tables; lower-ranked Avowed take turns serving; high table for
  Great Readers, Gatewarden, Keeper of Tomes. Breakfast 8–9 am,
  lunch 12–2 pm, dinner 6–7 pm. *Use for Philemon/Vareth/Yvenne
  passing each other at meals.*
- **Oak Tree Apothecary** (West Inner Ward): treehouse pharmacy
  in a huge evergreen oak. Inhabited by **Leuwin**, an absent-
  minded satyr druid, and his pet squirrel **Nibbles.** Greenhouses
  of medicinal herbs around the base. *Use if anyone needs healing
  / herbal supply / is interested in Feywild flora.*
- **Pavilion Naturalis** (North Inner Ward, natural sciences):
  crowned with a giant translucent mollusk shell visitors can
  walk up; T. Rex skeleton in the central hall; illusory
  butterflies and hummingbirds. Librarian **Frondu** is a
  firbolg sage obsessed with the bizarre mating habits of
  invertebrates. *Default location for Glabbagool's "what am I"
  question.*
- **Whispering Dome** (top of Hall of Momentous Deeds): enter
  alone, meditate 1 minute, the dome whispers a long-dead
  hero's quote at you and gives a one-time mechanical benefit.
  **Roll on the Words of Inspiration table** in the source JSON
  (or pick). *Great Glabbagool / Thorin / Zalthir option for a
  one-shot blessing.*
- **The Astronomicon** (East Inner Ward): zero-gravity dome of
  books in glassy forcefields; the **Orrery** is a 3D animated
  illusion of the planets; the **Stargazer** globe holds a giant
  telescope. Books are read while floating. **Combat in zero G
  rules** are in the supplement if needed. *Use if Daz or
  Zalthir wants to think about Faerûn-scale geography.*
- **Founders Court** (West Inner Ward): quadrangle with hollow
  walls forming passage-tapestries that depict Candlekeep's
  founding. Four corner turrets celebrate four founders;
  House of Alaundo at center.
- **The Old Keep**: the original bastion. Neglected. Several
  copies of *Megfry's Candlekeep Miscellanea* (a slim tome of
  obscure facts and figures, once popular, now forgotten).
  **Animated armor** ready to come to life if anyone tries to
  remove a book. No librarian — the post is too boring.

## Random generators (in the source JSON)

If a PC wanders into a library we haven't named, the supplement
has lookup tables for fast improvisation:

- **Random Library Generator:** building structure (d8),
  interior walls (d6), ceiling (d6), book storage system (d10),
  condition (d10), distinguishing feature (d20), study areas (d8).
- **Random Librarian Generator:** d20 of names + species +
  one-line quirks (e.g. *Kelpu (Kenku) — croaks in rhyming
  couplets*; *Simba (Tabaxi) — narcoleptic*; *Major Proudfin
  (Triton) — former military commander*).

Both tables live in the source JSON. Don't duplicate them here.
