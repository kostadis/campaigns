# Blingdenstone → Candlekeep — Travelogue

GM-facing prelude to the Candlekeep arc. Covers the **~800-mile,
~30-day** overland journey from the Blingdenstone surface exit
in the Silver Marches to Candlekeep on the Sword Coast.

**Mode:** narrated travelogue, not played. *Kim*-via-Kipling
register — episodic, picaresque, observational. The party is
*told* they traveled; the GM narrates each waystop; the players
**fill in personal beats via interview** at each stop. Most
stops are not encounters. Two are.

**Why a travelogue and not "you arrive at Candlekeep":**

- Mike/Joe/Ben/Gabe each have decisions their PCs would have
  made on the road that aren't Candlekeep-flavored — what they
  buy, who they meet, where they sleep. Skipping past it makes
  the Sword Coast feel less real.
- The **surface-madness gradient** needs to *land* before
  Candlekeep so the field-perception thread isn't an ambush.
  The party should arrive at Candlekeep already *suspecting*
  something is wrong with the surface, so Yvenne's *"You felt
  it. Tell me what it is."* is *recognition,* not exposition.
- The **Gorg'Bahamut breadcrumb** (Vareth's standing thread per
  `notes/threads/post_candlekeep_open_threads.md`) lives at
  **Triboar** canonically. The travelogue plants it for free.
- The party has been months in the Underdark; **they need a
  shopping arc.** Waterdeep in particular is the right place to
  buy specifically — fitted hoods, scabbard work, cloaks, ink,
  paper, scrolls — before they walk into the world's most
  prestigious library.

**Alternative if the table is impatient:** skip to "Way of the
Lion" (Episode 7). Run only that in 5 minutes. Lose the
surface-madness gradient and the shopping. **Recommend not.**

## World context — the wars and their aftermath

The party walks south through country that is still healing
from two recent wars. **Both wars are visible from the road**
and shape the texture of every episode except the Sword Coast
proper (Daggerford onward).

### War of the Dragons (~3 years past)

Tiamat's attempted summoning, the Cult of the Dragon
infiltration, the Council of Waterdeep, the metallic-dragon
alliance.

**The campaign has FOUR load-bearing heroes from this war —
the in-fiction *First Faction* — canonically distinct in
shape:**

**Stroud the dwarven rogue.** The war ended at **Stroud's
Gambit** — Stroud, sitting across from the ancient gold dragon
**Protanther**, played a chess position centuries old that none
of Protanther's four-hundred-year preparation had branched on.
Protanther's public concession committed the metallic dragons
to the alliance. **Stroud is *folkloric*** — taverns, songs,
fashion, *"Stroud's Gambit"* chess problems on inn walls. **A
small half-orc Bahamut-cleric tradition has been emerging** in
his wake; Grygum is one of only four documented Stroudite
half-orcs (per Vareth's Stroud Wake canon).

**Milo Goodbarrel, halfling cleric of Bahamut.** Renowned member
of the **Order of the Gauntlet.** Author of multiple volumes of
his account of the war, **published in Waterdeep by Rishaal the
Pageturner.** First book a *"huge success;"* by his last
Waterdeep signing on the 10th day of the 3rd Tenday of Uktar,
Rishaal was pressuring him for a sequel to revitalize sales.
The book signing three days after his arrival was *"a hit"* —
specifically for new artwork Milo crafted depicting the
mountain-top meeting between the metallic dragons and **the
First Faction.** Milo views adventures as opportunities for
*painting, sculpture, and writing,* not just text. **He is
*institutional*** — Order of the Gauntlet shrines, Bahamut
chapter houses, formal hagiographies, lay-saint status in some
Bahamut traditions. ⭐ **Daz has found a copy of *Milo
Goodbarrel's account of the War of the Dragons* and reads from
it constantly.** Daz cites **Malfire** (Milo's companion in the
books) at every available opportunity — *"As Malfire said in
Volume 3"* (chapter 54). The party's running joke; Mike's
quiet character beat.

**Malfire, the quiet ranger.** Two-years-on the world calls him
**"the Vindicator of Nature"** and **"the Winterstalker."**
Personally **convinced the Emerald Enclave to join the war**
alongside the Treants and the Metallic Dragons — a diplomatic
move whose ripple effects are still unfolding. ⭐ **The OOTA
back half / Gauntlgrym endgame depends on the Emerald Enclave
being reachable as an ally; Malfire is *why* they will answer
the door.** In the High Forest, wood elves and rangers speak of
him as a protective presence; the saying is *"the Winterstalker
ensures Tiamat's lingering influence never takes root again."*
**Joe will recognize.** His prior PC. *Honor it.*

**Whistler, the redeemed Ardragon.** ⭐ ***A flying kenku in a
black hoodie.*** Disappeared into the shadows after the war.
Mythologized along three competing axes — all true at once:
- *Common-folk Waterdeep:* "heroic sacrifice" against the white
  dragon **Glazhael.** (A spin operation by Ezra and Remalia
  with Harper assistance — *Whistler did not actually die.*)
- *Black Network (Zhentarim):* the **Ardragon** who secured
  *"the largest hoard in history,"* now retired with his prize.
- *Lycanthrope myth:* **the Shadow Werebear** haunts the trade
  routes; *"a mute knife that chose righteousness over profit."*

Kenku canonically cannot fly. **Whistler bought boots of
flying** and used them constantly — the **flying kenku** is
the canonical visual gag and is preserved in every reference.
Black hoodie. Mute (kenku — only mimics perfectly). Raven-
attracted-to-baubles thief who became *good* via werebear
lycanthropy at level 15 and then could not figure out when
stealing was acceptable; **looked to Milo for moral framing,**
and Milo would say *"we need money for operational expenses,
cost of living etc."* Milo's books shade the redemption
philosophically; the popular legend simplifies it to *"he
doesn't steal money anymore."* **Gabe will recognize.** His
prior PC. *Honor the silence at the table.*

**Four heroes, four registers.** Stroud is folkloric. Milo is
institutional. Malfire is a wilderness-spirit. Whistler is
mythologized and contested. The party will see *all four*
iconographies from Mirabar south, peaking in Waterdeep —
Stroud at the taverns and statues, Milo at the chapter houses
and Order of the Gauntlet shrines, **Milo's books on every
Waterdeep bookshop's front shelf,** Malfire's painted likeness
in the back of Order of the Gauntlet shrines, and Whistler in
bardic songs and Black Network whispers along every trade
route between Triboar and Daggerford.

**Visible aftermath:**
- Tiamat-cultist trial sites in major cities, marked with
  memorial stones.
- Dragon-scale fashion, dragon-claw insignia of the
  **Concordant Order** (the metallic-dragon-aligned chivalric
  order that arose from the Council).
- **Stroudite pilgrims** walking the High Road in twos and
  threes — half-orc converts, robed grey, often heading to
  Waterdeep or Candlekeep.
- **Order of the Gauntlet shrines and Bahamut chapter houses**
  carry Milo's iconography — a multimedia signature: paintings
  of the First Faction meeting, sculptures of Malfire, and
  copies of his books in the chapter library.
- **The First Faction painting** (Milo's last major artwork,
  unveiled at his Waterdeep book signing) depicts the
  mountain-top meeting between the metallic dragons and the
  First Faction — Stroud at center, Milo at his left,
  **Malfire in green at the back, Whistler in his black hoodie
  hovering slightly above the ground via boots of flying.**
  This composition is the iconic image of the war and is
  reproduced across the route — bronze relief at Mirabar's
  shrine; full-size original at Rishaal's bookshop in
  Waterdeep; smaller prints at Daggerford's Bahamut chapel.
- **Bardic songs of the Mute Knife** play in trade-route inns
  from Triboar south. The kenku-who-flew is the chief image
  preserved.

### War of the Giants (recent — within the past year)

Storm King's Thunder events. Giant ordning collapse. Tribal
artifact theft. **Critical canon for this campaign:** in the
aftermath, the **Uthgardt barbarian tribes raided
Triboar, Everlund, and the Silver Marches** in retaliation for
their stolen tribal totems. The retaliation was wide and
indiscriminate. Many cities held; many farmsteads, hamlets, and
shrines did not.

**The destruction zone runs from the Silver Marches south
through Triboar.** South of the **Triboar Trail** the country
is untouched — the Uthgardt did not cross south, the Sword
Coast was spared.

**Visible aftermath, north of Triboar Trail:**
- **Burned hamlets** along the road. Some still smoking.
  Cairns marking where families died.
- **Broken standing stones** — Uthgardt totems destroyed by
  rival Uthgardt tribes blaming each other, or destroyed by
  Silver Marches militias in counter-raids.
- **Defaced shrines to Tempus** (war-god, traditionally
  Uthgardt-friendly; now politically toxic).
- **Refugee movement** still ongoing — families with carts
  walking south along the same roads the party walks. Don't
  romanticize. Don't make every refugee a quest.
- **Dwarven outriders patrolling** from Mithral Hall and
  Mirabar; they salute Eldeth on the first day.
- The **Sundabar fall** — Sundabar, the eastern keystone of
  the Silver Marches, **fell during the raids.** It is a *ruin*
  now. Anyone who took the eastern courier route through
  Sundabar in the past year either didn't come back or came
  back *changed.* (See Eldred at Triboar.)

**Recovery:** Triboar is rebuilding (caravan economy is hot),
Mirabar is holding (mining city walls held), Mithral Hall is
intact (dwarven defenses are dwarven defenses). Everlund is
half-rebuilt. **Silvery Moon** is functional but somber. The
overall feeling traveling south is *the country has been
beaten and is getting up.*

### How both wars feel different on the road

| | War of the Dragons | War of the Giants |
|---|---|---|
| Time elapsed | ~3 years | ~1 year (recent) |
| Visibility on the road | **Memorial.** Statues, fashion, Stroudite pilgrims, trial-site stones, Concordant Order insignia. | **Fresh.** Burned hamlets, broken stones, refugee carts, defaced shrines, smoke. |
| Player register | *"This is something the world is still talking about."* | *"This happened to people we will meet today."* |
| PC reactions | Curiosity, recognition, occasional reverence. Joe will look at every Stroud statue. Ben *is* one. | Quiet. The party has just come up from their own war; this register is familiar. Recognition without commentary. |

> **GM note on terminology:** the user's canon uses *Silver
> Marches* (federation of cities including Silvery Moon, Sundabar,
> Mithril Hall, Citadel Adbar). If your home canon uses *Shadow
> Marches* instead, swap freely; the content is identical.

## Episode 1 — The Road North (Days 1–4)

The party has emerged from Blingdenstone's surface exit into
the **Silver Marches**, mid-spring, late-afternoon sun.

Already played at the table (chapter 54 / file 54): the
sun-adjustment beat, **Eldeth's farewell at the tunnel mouth,
Spiderbait's terrible decision**, Glabbagool meeting daylight.

**What the players were *told* happened next:**

- The party walks south along the **Surbrin** river valley with
  Eldeth for two days. Conversation is light and dwarven. Eldeth
  knows the country and points at landmarks none of them can
  name.
- On the third morning, near the **Mithral Hall outer farms**,
  Eldeth turns east toward the dwarven hold. Brief farewell.
  *"Giant friend. I have said what I said. I will write."*
- The party turns west-and-south toward **Mirabar.** They are
  alone for the first time in months that nobody is hunting them.

**Atmosphere note — the war is fresher than the field.** The
party walks through visible Uthgardt-raid country for most of
these four days. Specific texture (use 2–3 of these, not all):

- **A burned hamlet** maybe a day's walk south of the
  Blingdenstone exit. The thatch is gone; the stone hearths
  still stand. A single iron pot in the ash. The sign over the
  inn was *"The Auroch's Horn."* No one lives here now.
- **A broken standing stone** on the road — a Thunderbeast
  totem, smashed at the base by a hammer larger than a
  Thunderbeast hammer. The remains face away from the road, as
  if turned by hand to greet travelers from the south. Eldeth
  spits without comment.
- **A Tempus shrine** at a crossroads, defaced. The war-god's
  axe-symbol scored out with a chisel. *"They did this to
  themselves,"* Eldeth says. *"After. Different tribe. They
  blame each other now. They are not wrong."*
- **A refugee family** with a hand-cart heading south — three
  generations, one ox, no grandfather. They share the road for
  a day, then peel off west toward Mirabar. **Optional Joe / Ben
  beat:** Thorin or Grygum can offer them coin, food, escort to
  the next way-station. Land it small. The world has many of
  these.
- **Dwarven outriders** from Mithral Hall pass the party in the
  morning of day three. Six riders, full armor, salute Eldeth
  by name. *"Giant-friend. The Hall has been asking when you'd
  come. You go to Gauntlgrym after?"* Eldeth: *"After I have
  said what I have to say. After."*

**Atmosphere note 2 (don't underline):** *underneath* the
visible war damage, the Silver Marches are **unusually quiet**
in a register the war doesn't account for. Locals at a wayhouse
mention it: *"Bears are fewer this year. Pixies haven't sung in
the Stonelands. My grandmother says weather. I am not
certain."* **This is the field at low ambient strain in
low-magical-density country.** Plant only. The party will not
distinguish it from war-trauma quiet until later.

**Interview prompts (light — most of this is bridge):**
- *Joe — does Thorin offer the refugee family anything? Ride
  with them an extra hour?*
- *Ben — does Grygum perform any rite for the burned hamlet, or
  the defaced Tempus shrine?*
- *Mike — does Daz, who has only known the Underdark, ask
  Eldeth what happened? Or read the country in silence?*
- *Gabe — Zalthir watches the broken standing stone. Anything?*

## Episode 2 — The Forge of Mirabar (Days 5–6)

Mirabar is a **dwarven mining city** at the edge of the Silver
Marches. Smoke and red light from the forges visible from miles
out. Caravan traffic. Open gates for honest visitors. **The
city held during the Uthgardt raids; the outer farmsteads did
not.** Refugee population at the smiths' gate, sleeping in
shifts.

**Atmosphere:**

- The smiths' quarter never sleeps. The party can hear the
  hammer-ring all night from the inns above. **Half the orders
  the smiths fill now are armor for refugee militias** —
  Silvery Moon, Everlund, the surviving farmstead clans. The
  smiths say so plainly: they are tired and they will not stop.
- Mirabar dwarves are clan-friendly to strangers but
  professionally curious — *"You came up the river? From where?"*
  Thorin can answer truthfully or not. **Joe's call.** Most
  travelers heading north right now are mercenaries hired
  against Uthgardt remnants; the smiths' first guess is that
  the party is one such company. *Daz's hood gets attention.*
- **Stroud is everywhere.** A bronze Stroud-bust at the
  smiths' guild-hall (donated by a Sword-Coast metalworkers'
  league three years ago). A tavern named *The Knight Beneath
  the Knight* (the position Stroud played, plus a pun). A
  travelling Stroud-school polemicist preaching humanoid agency
  in a corner of the market. **Ben/Grygum is a Stroudite half-
  orc.** The polemicist sees his holy symbol and tries to recruit
  him on the spot. *"Brother. Have you heard the Witness call?
  Are you walking the road for Stroud?"* Grygum may engage or
  decline; Vareth's fuller account waits at Candlekeep. ⭐ **At
  the table in Ch. 51, the polemicist handed Grygum a pamphlet:**
  *Stroud and the Human Agency Hypothesis: A Critique — Dragons
  and Gods Do Not Rule.* **Hand Ben the physical handout** at
  `handouts/grygum_stroudite_pamphlet.md` — pre-revolutionary
  18th-century broadside register, *"is there anything more
  clear?"* climax. Vareth will identify the Borrim Pickwhistle
  imprint at the Stroud Wake.
- **Milo is here too — quietly.** A small **Order of the
  Gauntlet shrine** in the temples' quarter holds a sculpted
  bronze frontispiece reproduction of *Milo Goodbarrel's
  account, Volume 1.* No polemicist; no recruiting. Two
  half-orc lay brothers tend the shrine in shifts. **Daz, if
  he visits, can flip through the chapter library and find
  Volumes 1 and 2** (Volume 3 is sold out and on backorder
  from Rishaal's printers in Waterdeep — a brother apologizes
  for the wait). Daz can purchase Volume 1 here if he doesn't
  already have it; **the Volume-3 acquisition waits for
  Waterdeep itself.** A small framed painting on the wall —
  attributed to Milo, signed in his hand — depicts a halfling
  in monastic robes with one open palm raised. **The
  *Auditor* persona** the canon mentions, painted by Milo
  himself.
- **And — Malfire is here, very quietly.** On the back wall of
  the same shrine, a larger painting nearly full-height: ⭐
  ***Malfire, the Vindicator of Nature*** — a figure in
  weathered green at the edge of a forest, longbow lowered, an
  enormous grey wolf at his side, stars rising over his
  shoulder. Painted by Milo. **Joe will recognize.** A small
  brass plate beneath: *"He spoke for the trees, and the trees
  spoke back. — M.G."* Pose for it. Don't underline.
- A **Sjurkar priest** (lesser dragon-cult; Mirabar tolerates
  them as long as they keep to copper-and-bronze talk and
  carry **Concordant Order seals** — chivalric license issued
  post-war) is doing a public benediction at the West Gate. He
  gets a syllable wrong in the prayer. The dwarven smiths
  around him don't notice. *Plant only.* This is the field at
  *very low* ambient — even licensed priests aren't reliably
  catching their own errors.

**Shopping in Mirabar:**

- **Dwarven smithing.** Thorin can commission armor-adjustment
  work, runic balance etching, or specific weapon repairs.
  Mirabar is the *correct* setting for any "I want my armor to
  fit better after eight months in the dark" request. Two days'
  work fits the timeline.
- **Dawnbringer cleaning and re-attunement.** A specialist
  exists. Joe's call whether Thorin lets a stranger near the
  blade.
- **Daz** can find a stationer; first sustained access to ink,
  vellum, paper, scroll-blanks, sealing wax in months.
- **Grygum** finds a small Bahamut shrine in the temples'
  quarter (the dwarves keep room for half a dozen lawful gods).
  Quiet beat available; he can rest, pray, or tithe.
- **Zalthir** finds a sparring hall. Watches; doesn't engage.
- **Glabbagool** stays in the bag the entire stop. Mirabar does
  not have *category* for an ambulatory ooze.

**Interview prompts:**
- *Joe — what does Thorin commission at the smith? How much does
  he pay? Does he wait for the work or pick it up later by
  courier?*
- *Mike — first stationer's shop in eight months. What's the
  first thing Daz reaches for?*
- *Ben — Grygum at the shrine. Does he tithe? Does he pray for
  anything specific?*
- *Gabe — where does Zalthir actually sleep?*

## Episode 3 — The Wheat that Spirals (Days 11–12)

**Goldenfields.** Walled agricultural temple-city of Chauntea,
Dessarin Valley. Bread-baking smell from a mile out. The temple
welcomes travelers; meals are free; donations expected but not
required. **The Uthgardt did not raid Goldenfields** —
Chauntean diplomacy, a barbarian's need for next-season's wheat,
and three intercessions by old chiefs who remembered Chauntean
mercy in their grandmothers' famines. **Goldenfields is the
first place south of the Triboar Trail where the country looks
unbroken** — and the temple has become an **open shelter for
refugees** from the cities that did burn.

**Atmosphere:**

- The temple courts are crowded with families from Triboar,
  Everlund, and the Silver Marches farmsteads. **Goldenfields
  is feeding thousands of extra mouths.** The Chauntean
  granaries are visibly low; the priestesses are visibly thin.
  No one complains.
- The fields stretch to every horizon, organized in the
  Chauntean concentric pattern — wheat in the inner ring, oats
  and rye outward, vegetables and orchards beyond. **The
  eastern wheat is growing in a slow spiral.** Visible from the
  temple roof. **The harvest will be fine** — wheat is wheat —
  but the *shape* is wrong.
- The high priestess, **Sister Ellune**, is gentle and
  exhausted. Talks freely if asked. *"Chauntea is not silent.
  She is patient. But the land is making patterns I have not
  read in any text. We have asked for guidance. We have not
  received it."* If pressed: she has been awake at night
  reading old Mielikki's-druid records of *"earth-shape-
  disturbance,"* hoping to find precedent. Hasn't. *"And there
  are five thousand people sleeping in my courts who are
  watching the wheat with me."* **The refugee crisis and the
  field-pattern wrongness are happening in the same place at
  the same time.** Sister Ellune cannot disentangle them. *The
  party may not be able to either.*
- **Daz feels something.** He can't name it. This is his **first
  somatic surface-madness tell** in the campaign. It is faint —
  a pressure on the sinuses, the air *thinking* about something.
  He has felt this kind of pressure before. He cannot place where.
  *(He's felt it in the Underdark when Demogorgon-tides rolled
  through tunnels miles away. He doesn't make the connection
  here. Yvenne will.)*

**No combat. No shopping.**

**Interview prompts:**
- *Ben — Grygum is a cleric. Does he stay overnight in the
  temple? Does he ask Sister Ellune what guidance she has prayed
  for? Does he offer any of his own?*
- *Mike — Daz felt something. Does he say so? To whom?*
- *Joe — Thorin and Sister Ellune. Anything?*
- *Gabe — does Zalthir walk the fields at night? What does he see?*

## Episode 4 — Triboar Crossroads (Days 14–15)

**Triboar** — small but vital crossroads town where the
**Long Road** (north-south) meets the **Evermoor Way**
(east-west). Mountain's Mouth Inn famous regionally for ale.

**Triboar was raided.** The Uthgardt came down the Evermoor
Way in retaliation for stolen totems. **Roughly one-third of
the town is still ruin** — the eastern quarter, where the
caravan stables used to be. Two-thirds rebuilt; rebuild
ongoing. **The caravan economy is hot** — every hand that can
hammer is paid above wage; lumber is at five times the
pre-war price.

**Atmosphere:**

- Coming in from the north, the party walks the **memorial
  square** before the inn — recently-laid flagstones around a
  cairn of fieldstones, **names of the Triboar dead carved by
  hand** into a slab leaned against the cairn. Fresh-cut
  letters. Two hundred and forty-three names. Children's names
  among them. Anyone who reads them reads them.
- The Mountain's Mouth Inn itself is intact. **Saved during
  the raid by a single Uthgardt chief** — a Black Lion totem-
  bearer named **Dornal Greyhand** — who happened to be drinking
  there when his own war-band came in, and who stood in the
  doorway with a cup and an axe and said *"this house owes
  me."* No one knows why he said it. Dornal was killed two days
  later in fighting near Yartar; the inn keeps his cup behind
  the bar. The innkeeper, **Brindle Wenth**, will tell the
  story to anyone who pays for it. He doesn't pretend to
  understand it.
- Triboar is *crowded* for its size — caravans staging, traders,
  pilgrims, **Concordant Order knights** in dragon-scale pauldrons
  riding through, the occasional Lord's Alliance courier
  carrying rebuild-permits. The party can blend in here.
- ⭐ **Trade-route whispers — the flying kenku in the black
  hoodie.** Triboar is the Sword Coast's caravan-staging town,
  which means it is the listening-post for every trade-route
  rumor for two hundred miles. **Drovers, teamsters, and
  outriders speak of *the Shadow Werebear* who haunts the
  caravan trails north of Yartar.** The image is consistent:
  ⭐ **a kenku, hooded in black, hovering a foot off the
  ground, who appears at distance and is gone before anyone
  can call.** *"The Ardragon, my granddad called him. The one
  who used to fly the routes for the Black Network. Used to
  steal anything not nailed down. Then something happened to
  him out east, and now he's a — a what — a*ranger*, sort of?
  Stops bandits. Takes nothing. Doesn't speak. Mimics a hawk
  and rides the next thermal. The hoodie is unmistakable. We
  don't pay him; we leave a coin on a rock and he never picks
  it up."* **Gabe will recognize.** His prior PC. *Honor the
  silence.*
  - **DC 13 Insight** (auto for Daz): the drovers are partly
    embellishing and partly not. The Shadow Werebear appears in
    multiple places at once, which means either there are
    multiple imitators or the legend has outpaced the man.
    **Both readings can be true.**
- **The drunk at the inn.** Old man on a stool by the fire,
  talking to himself **in two voices.** One voice is his. The
  other is younger, and answers questions the first voice
  doesn't ask. Brindle, when asked: *"Eldred. He ran courier
  Triboar to Sundabar in the war. Came back from his last run
  two summers past and he was both of him. We feed him and we

## Episode 5 — The City of Splendors (Days 18–24)

The party detours through **Waterdeep.** Six days. **The single
biggest shopping window in the campaign.** Waterdeep was
**untouched by the giant war** (the Uthgardt did not cross
south of the Triboar Trail). It was, however, the **political
center of the dragon war's resolution** — the Council of
Waterdeep met here, the metallic alliance was sealed here, and
**Stroud's Gambit was witnessed here, three years ago.**

**Atmosphere:**

- The city is *too noisy* to perceive the field clearly. Magic
  density is high but distributed; no single warding strain. The
  field is here, but Waterdeep is *louder than its symptoms.*
- **Stroud is everywhere.** A statue in **Castle Ward Square**

  has **Rishaal the Pageturner's** — Milo's publisher's

  *Milo Goodbarrel's account of the War of the Dragons*

## Episode 6 — The Silent Child (Day 26)

**Daggerford.** Two days south of Waterdeep. Small ford town;
sleepy; an inn called the **River Shining Tavern.** They stop
for a night.

**Atmosphere:**

- Daggerford is *quiet,* but a different quiet than the Silver
  Marches. The townfolk move carefully. No one explains why.
- **The Hand of Tarvis** — a bronze-cast severed forearm and
  hand mounted on a stone pillar in the village square. Tarvis
  Ulain was a paladin who died here three years ago breaking a
  Cult of the Dragon cell that had been recruiting in
  Daggerford. The casting was funded **two years post-war by a
  metalworkers' guild that may have been Stroud-school
  sponsored** — there is a small inscription in dwarven on the
  base reading *"the move is yours,"* the same line under the
  Castle Ward statue in Waterdeep. Vareth would find this
  significant; a Stroud-school sponsorship of a Bahamut
  paladin's monument is a deliberate iconographic move.
- **And — Order of the Gauntlet co-sponsorship.** A second
  inscription, smaller, in Common, on the back of the pillar
  (most visitors miss it; DC 12 Investigation): *"Witnessed
  and recorded for the Gauntlet — Milo Goodbarrel, by the
  hand of his publisher, ledger of the year ____."* Tarvis was
  Order of the Gauntlet; **Milo personally signed off on the
  Daggerford monument's official record.** Daz can flag this.
  ⭐ **Trophy plant:** the village priest carries a small
  reproduction medallion of Tarvis's hand cast in pewter.
  *"For donations to the Gauntlet."* Joe will buy one.
- *Daggerford itself was lightly touched by the dragon war and
  not at all by the giant war.* The quiet is something else.
- **Acute surface-madness incident:** at the inn a young mother
  approaches the party — **Maerith of the Ford,** weaver by
  trade — when she sees Grygum's holy symbol. Her daughter,
  **Elin, seven years old, has been silent for six days.**
  Elin draws **the same shape** on every flat surface. Charcoal
  on the table. Finger in flour on the kitchen floor. Quill on
  any unattended parchment. **A six-pointed star.**

  *"She talks in her sleep. She says it is the only shape that
  holds. Please. We have asked the temples. The temples have
  no answer. Will you look at her."*

  - **The party may decline.** That is a real option. They are
    nine days out from Candlekeep and not local clerics.
    Maerith will be sad, not angry. (Note for the campaign: a
    refusal here is a *thing* the campaign will return to.
    Eldeth's letter via the Lords' Alliance may eventually
    surface a Daggerford complaint about *"foreign clerics who
    declined a child."* Plant the consequence; don't moralize.)
  - **If Grygum tries:** DC 18 Medicine to diagnose, or
    `lesser restoration` for *temporary relief.* On success,
    Elin speaks once before slipping back: *"The wells are not
    wells anymore."* Then silent. The relief lasts a few hours.
    Grygum knows: this is not a disease. It is *something
    structurally wrong with the world reaching her ears.*
  - **The shape.** This is **the same six-pointed star Marin
    will arrange with quills, and Brevin with bedclothes, at
    Candlekeep.** Three minds. Three places. Same shape. Plant
    only. **Joe's instinct will be to take a charcoal rubbing
    of the table.** *Trophy plant.* Let him.

**Shopping:** minimal. The inn sells healing potions at a
markup. Grygum can leave a small donation with Maerith.

## Episode 7 — The Pilgrim's Verse (Day 28)

**Beregost.** Last meaningful waystop before Candlekeep.

**Atmosphere:**

- The **Burning Wizard** inn took its name decades ago from a
  wizard burned alive during a much-earlier dragon-cult
  uprising — *long before* the recent Tiamat war. The
  innkeeper, an old gnome named **Festrum**, will tell the story
  to anyone who buys a second pint, and ends it by pointing at
  a soot-mark on the south wall: *"That is where he fell. We
  scrub the floor every season. We do not paint over the wall.
  My grandfather said: the wall remembers."* Old grief, weathered
  smooth. **Useful contrast** to Triboar's fresh memorial — the
  Sword Coast's wars are old; the North's are new.
- ⭐ **The bardic song of the Mute Knife.** A travelling bard
  named **Veyloss** is at the Burning Wizard tonight; she
  performs three sets between dinner and last call. **Her
  closing number** every night this season is *"The Kenku
  Could Not Fly"* — a four-verse rhyming drinking-song with a
  chorus the Burning Wizard regulars all sing along with.
  Sample (Gabe will hear it):
  > *"O the kenku could not fly, but he flew,*
  > *In his black hood and his black hood and his black hood;*
  > *The raven-thief who learned to steal no more —*
  > *Save what was needed, said the small wise man at his side."*

  The fourth verse is about Glazhael. The chorus repeats *"his
  black hood"* three times for a reason no one in Beregost
  remembers. **Gabe will recognize.** *Honor it.* Veyloss takes
  requests; if anyone asks for a different verse, she shrugs
  and says *"all four are the same anyway. He flew. He stole.
  He stopped stealing. He flew away."*
- The **Burning Wizard** inn is full of pilgrims walking south

  Several pilgrims have rough manuscripts in oilcloth. The

- **The pilgrim at the corner table** is a young human scholar

  want to learn. **He gets a verse wrong.** Specifically: he

  substitutes is from a different prayer entirely. **He doesn't
  notice.** No one in the inn corrects him.
  - **Mike (Daz) DC 12 Insight:** notices the substitution, but
    can't say why he noticed. The pilgrim's pattern of breath

  - **Grygum / Vareth payoff** — when Vareth surfaces the

    Earned credit.

## Episode 8 — Way of the Lion (Day 30 — arrival)

The road from Beregost to Candlekeep takes a single morning's
walk. **The Way of the Lion** runs along the cliffs above the
**Sea of Swords.** The keep is visible from miles out — towers,
the High Tower, the gulls.

**The party falls into a slow procession of pilgrims walking
the same road,** each carrying one book wrapped in oilcloth.
**Five pilgrims for the Inner Ward today,** by Fembris's count
when they arrive — *and the party is one of the five.*

**The other four pilgrims:**

1. A young half-elf scholar from Silverymoon with a single
   manuscript on Mielikki's-druid earth-shape lore. (Goldenfields
   tie-in; if Sister Ellune sent her, the party may have met her
   at the temple.)
2. **Two of the Stroudite half-orcs from Waterdeep** —
   travelling together; one carries a copy of Stroud's earliest
   post-match letter (a polemic-shelf donation), the other a
   copy of the Concordant Order's founding charter. They walk
   the road quietly. **They look at Grygum.** Ben's call whether
   he walks with them or apart. As the keep comes into view,
   one of them murmurs the four names — *"Stroud, Milo,
   Malfire, Whistler"* — the way one might murmur a prayer.
   The other nods. ⭐ **The full First Faction in one breath,
   plant and move on.**
3. A weathered Triboar carpenter with a single hand-bound
   journal — names of the Triboar dead, written by survivors who
   wanted them in Candlekeep's archive *because Candlekeep
   remembers what cities forget.* If asked, he says only: *"My
   wife is in here. My son. Goodbye."* He sets the book down
   gently at the gate.

The party brings five books, count of three pilgrims with one
each, and Fembris's count of five donations for the Inner Ward
adds up because **the party is logged as a single donor of
five.** They go in last because Bookwyrm wants to receive them
personally.

**Atmosphere:**

- Sea air. **Cleanest air the party has breathed in months.**
  The sun is almost too bright on the water. *No one is
  shooting at them.* Glabbagool, in the bag, is *quiet* — the
  sea air is calming to him, possibly because Juiblex is a
  swamp lord, not a sea lord. *(Plant only.)*
- **Daz's pressure-headache *sharpens*** the closer they get to
  the keep. **This is the inversion.** The Silver Marches were
  too quiet to feel anything; Goldenfields was a whisper;
  Daggerford was a punch; Candlekeep is a *crescendo.* The
  wards are *holding the most* and therefore *showing the
  most.* Yvenne, in the Hall of Divination tomorrow, will name
  this for him.

**First sight of the keep — the three-beat landing.** Per the
[Wally DM "describe a legendary place" frame](https://wallydm.com/3-world-building-lessons/),
land Candlekeep as **one sense + one local + one question**,
not a tour. Use these three; stop there; let the players ask.

1. ⭐ **The sound, before the silhouette.** Half a mile out,
   on the wind off the water, the party catches a single
   human voice carried across the cliffs — measured, unhurried,
   reciting verse. **The Endless Chant.** It does not stop. It
   does not break. It carries on the wind for the rest of the
   walk in. *(One sense, not a fact. The fact — that this is
   Alaundo's 99 prophecies in a 24-hour relay led by Benedora
   Stoneforge — comes later, from Vareth or another Avowed.
   Right now it's just a voice that won't stop.)*

2. ⭐ **The pilgrim ahead, who isn't impressed.** The
   weathered Triboar carpenter (already in the queue with the
   party — see the four-pilgrim list above) glances up at the
   towers, glances back at his oilcloth-wrapped journal, and
   says quietly to no one in particular: *"They'll lose it.
   They lose half of what we bring. But they don't *forget*.
   That's the difference."* He doesn't elaborate. Then,
   without looking at the party: *"You'll learn the bells.
   Bells from six till midnight. The midnight one — cover
   your ears."* He has been here before. He says nothing else
   about it. *(One local. He describes Candlekeep through
   what bothers him about it, not what amazes him about it.
   Wally's "lived-in POV" lesson.)*

3. ⭐ **One detail no one explains.** As the queue advances,
   the party passes a small grey cat sitting on a stone bollard
   beside the road. Yellow eyes, unblinking. A pilgrim ahead
   of them steps around her without comment, the way you'd
   step around a wet patch you'd been told about. None of the
   Avowed at the gate acknowledge her. She does not move when
   the party passes. *(One question. This is **Queenie**, who
   normally lives on the Keeper's tower stairs — but no one
   tells the players that. The cat is just *there,* and
   everyone behaves as though her presence requires no
   explanation. Players will ask. Don't answer.)*

**That's the description.** Three beats. The towers, the High
Tower, the gulls, the wards, the avowed in robes — let the
players construct the rest from those three anchors as they
walk through the gate. **Do not list features.** Anything they
ask about, answer; anything they don't, save for the days
ahead. The full sensory toolkit for the Candlekeep arc proper
lives in `notes/sessions/candlekeep_sensory_beats.md` — pull
from there for Day One scenes onward.

**Arrival:** the **Emerald Door** of the Outer Ward is open to
the queue. The party joins. They are admitted last (their five
books take longer to log). **Bookwyrm receives them.**

End of travelogue. Begin Session 1 of the Candlekeep arc as
written in `notes/sessions/candlekeep_day_one.md`.

**No interview prompts for Episode 8.** Arrival is arrival.

## Pacing at the table

**Run the travelogue at the start of Session 1.** Estimate
30–40 minutes of table time. The structure:

1. Read the route at-a-glance table to the players. Five
   minutes.
2. Walk the seven episodes in order. For each: 1–2 minutes of
   GM narration, then **interview prompts** (2–3 minutes per
   relevant player). Most episodes have one or two players
   spotlight, not all four. Don't force every player to speak
   at every stop.
3. End with Episode 8 (arrival). No interview.
4. Begin Session 1 of the Candlekeep arc proper.

**Total table time:** 30–40 minutes prelude → 2.5–3 hours
Candlekeep Day One. Fits a single session.

If table appetite is short: **collapse Episodes 1, 3, 7 into one
paragraph each and run only 2/4/5/6/8 in detail.** The shopping
arc (Episode 5) and the Daggerford incident (Episode 6) are the
two episodes you should *never* cut — those carry the most
mechanical and thematic weight.
