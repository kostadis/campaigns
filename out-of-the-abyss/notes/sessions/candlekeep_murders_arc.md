## Design principles

1. **OOTA scholar arcs continue through the murder mystery.** The
   Yvenne / Khell-Vire / Philemon / Vareth threads do not pause.
   They interleave between investigation beats. Each player gets
   spotlight that isn't pure mystery work. Sessions 4–5 are the
   closing windows for those threads.
2. **Good choices = easier climax.** See the *Good-choice ledger*
   below. Earned trust, saved lives, and recruited allies cash in
   as ally-narration cryptogram clues, free dispels, and trophy
   plants in Sessions 7–8. **Bad-choice arc is fully runnable**
   but harder.
3. **The party departs at level 9, not 10.** One milestone level
   at the end of Session 4 (the disguise reveal). No second
   level-up. Calibrated for the OOTA back half.
4. **Manshoon escapes.** He is a recurring villain hereafter, not
   a final-fight corpse. The Book of Vile Darkness fate is the
   variable.
5. **Eldeth → Gauntlgrym is the post-arc call.** Not the Harpers.
   See `candlekeep_day_one.md` *Post-arc contact* section.
6. **Trophy planting.** Joe in particular collects keepsakes; the
   party broadly does. Every significant defeat plants a named,
   identifiable item. See memory note
   `oota_party_trophy_collecting.md`.
7. **The DeVir name is never spoken at Candlekeep.** Mike is a
   sharp investigator — handing him "DeVir" on a list would
   insult his intelligence and waste the disclosure-trade
   structure. Mike triangulates the **Fourth Seat (struck)** +
   **Daz'issin (razor-cut footnote)** + **fresh-ink survivor
   redaction** and fills in the missing house himself. Vizeran
   speaks DeVir back into existence at Stage 4 in his
   Menzoberranzan safe-room. Full DC ladder and disclosure-trade
   ladder live in `candlekeep_day_one.md` Lane 1/Lane 2/Half 2.
   Canon: `docs/daz_devir_narrative_arc.md`.

## Calendar overlay — the questions and the mystery run in parallel

**This is canonical structure, not a stylistic choice.** Per
chapter 54, the party donated five books to ask five questions,
and the Avowed scholars work in **days, not scenes.** Yvenne
reads. Vareth catalogues. Philemon assesses. Khell-Vire opens
his notebook over multiple sittings. **The scholar arcs do not
pause for the murder mystery.** Janussi's death happens *during
the party's stay,* not before. The scholars are still doing
their work on Day 2 morning when Fembris pounds on the inn door
— and they continue doing it on Days 2–3 *while* the party is
investigating the killing.

This is what makes the Candlekeep arc structurally rich: every
PC has **two threads** running in parallel — the question they
asked at the gate (slow, scholarly, days-long) and the murder
they were just conscripted into (acute, time-pressured, hours).
**Don't collapse them.** The GM should be able to point at any
session and say what each PC is doing on *both* tracks.

### What "the questions take days" means at the table

- **Don't try to answer a question in one scene.** Yvenne, Vareth, Khell-Vire, and Philemon all canonically work in *multiple sittings.* A single sitting moves the needle (one tick, one station, one tier, one phase) but does not close the question.
- **Allow offstage scholar progress.** Between on-screen scenes, PCs can say *"I spent the afternoon with Yvenne"* — give them the next-tier reveal as narration without forcing a full scene. **Use this in Session 2** especially, where the murder consumes screen time but the scholars are still working.
- **The mystery affects the scholars; the scholars affect the mystery.** Philemon is rattled because Janussi was his superior. Sylvira is suspected because the cover-up framed her. Vareth gossips loudly about who he thinks did it. Yvenne quietly compares Daz's posture to a grief reaction (it isn't). **Let these reactions surface during scholar scenes** — they earn double duty.
- **A PC who never makes time for their scholar leaves with partial answers.** The big four (Daz, Zalthir, Thorin, Grygum) need *multiple* sittings to reach full payoff. **Glabbagool's question is single-shot;** it resolves in Session 1. Track each PC's scholar ticks/stations/phases per the ledger.
- **The climax depends on what the scholars gave the party.** Session 7's cryptogram race uses the good-choice ledger — every shortcut there is a *cash-in* of multi-day scholar trust. A party who ignored scholars in favor of the mystery has the worst-case ledger and runs Session 7 long.

## The surface-madness thread

The OOTA back half requires the party to *witness* demon-lord
field-effects bleeding to the surface. The murder mystery does
not natively carry this; we inject it in four vectors across the
arc.

**Vectors:**

1. **Kalan Strongbranch** — module canon: weekly visits to the
   Chambers of Lost Lore consulting an "otherworldly being." We
   reframe: the being he consults is itself in the path of the
   upward leak. Kalan is being **bled into**, not voluntarily
   talking. His sanity slip is the field seeping through him.
   He uses one phrase the party will recognize from the
   Underdark before he dies: *"...the two heads agree, the two
   heads agree..."* (Demogorgon's split-brain madness signature).
2. **Peripheral Avowed incidents.** Background acolytes /
   minor scholars showing OOTA-style short-term madness —
   paranoid, manic, depressed, fragmented. Quietly, every day,
   slightly more.
   - Acolyte Marin (Session 3): arranges quills in a six-pointed
     star. *"It's the only shape that holds."*
   - Watcher Brevin (Session 4): found in the surf at the foot
     of the cliffs, reciting *Sloobludop, Sloobludop, Sloobludop.*
     The kuo-toan town Demogorgon destroyed. **No one in
     Candlekeep should know that name.**
   - Brevin again (Session 5): silently arranging bedclothes in
     the same six-pointed star Marin made with quills. *Two
     minds, the same shape, different demon lord, same field.*
3. **Daz's somatic sensitivity.** Months of Underdark exposure
   means Daz feels the field the way a man with frostbite feels
   cold. Subtle, mostly somatic — pressure on the sinuses, air
   thinking about something. Yvenne notices and names it. *"You
   feel it. I have only read about it. Tell me what it is."*
4. **The Endless Chant fragmenting.** Canonically a protective
   function (Alaundo's prophecies recited continuously). It has
   been **dropping verses, doubling back, stuttering** for weeks
   — since the seventh day of the previous month, per Sylvira.
   Avowed dismiss it as winter throats. It isn't. Sylvira knows
   it isn't. **Bookwyrm knows it isn't and is lying.**

**Acute escalations:**

- **Session 5: Glabbagool's bad night.** Through the Stonespeaker
  Crystal: *"The mother voice came back. Pudding King. It said
  my name."* Pudding King is dead — **Juiblex is reaching.**
  Zalthir's chapter-50 oath load-bearing.
- **Session 6: the wards drop.** When A'lai pulls the lever on
  the lightning orb, every PC takes a one-round shared
  hallucination. **Narrative only, no mechanical effect** — the
  recognition is the mechanic.
  - Daz: Vaelissa T'sarran's face in a mirror that isn't there.
  - Zalthir: Jimjar's voice — *"I am sorry I had to leave you."*
  - Thorin: Brysis's hand on the back of his neck.
  - Grygum: **Bahamut absent.** Negative space where the god has
    always been. **The single most frightening thing he has ever
    experienced.** Returns when the wave passes.
  - Glabbagool, through the crystal: *"Mother. Mother. Mother."*
    (Juiblex.)
  - Sylvira (Path B): *"It will get worse before it gets better.
    We are running."*
- **Session 8: the Echoes.** Echo 1 names the field-effect
  directly: *"When three queens move beneath the world, the
  world above will sicken. The chant will stutter. The keep will
  crack. The mad will speak the names of drowned cities."*
  **The seer named what the party has lived through.**

## The good-choice ledger

Tally before each session what the party has earned. Each earned
item cashes in as one cryptogram clue cut to ally narration, one
combat round saved, or one trophy plant. **This is the engine
that makes "good choices = easier climax" mechanical rather than
vibes-based.**

| Earned across Sessions 1–6 | Cashes in as |
|---|---|
| **Sylvira recruited (Path B, Session 5)** | She joins the Session 6 fight as an archmage ally; dispels Moziqodo's binding (DC 19 Arcana, +9, succeeds on 10). Returns to Session 7 with two free cryptogram clues — **Vydykyq** + **Limniz** — known from her own Abyss-research arcana training. |
| **Yvenne trust ≥ 4 ticks (Daz earned)** | She offers a small ritual in Session 7: traces A'lai's `dimension door` planar residue down to the lava chamber. Party gets the destination without needing the full riddle (still need the Alaundo statue to enter). |
| **Vareth / Drakonoikos goodwill (Grygum)** | **Thava Norixius** gives **Vydykyq** for free in Session 7. Vareth knows **Stars at Dawn** opens with F and ends F-A-D-E. |
| **Daral saved (Session 3)** | Daral as a witness in Session 5: *"I had that book in my hands before he died. I saw the lizardskin at the Evergreen Tree at four."* Provides Alkrist ID through a non-Bookwyrm channel. In Session 7: *"Fustilugs. Black Knight chess satire. Marble piece, Philosopher's Court."* One clue cut. |
| **Khell-Vire's Watcher's Stillness (Zalthir earned the Severed Path)** | One free skirmish skip in Session 7 (passes through a raider position unseen). Advantage on the Vault `glyph of warding` Wis save in Session 8. |
| **Glabbagool went to the Whispering Dome (Session 1)** | Banked Words-of-Inspiration boon — available for one cash-in in Session 8 (re-coax a partial Echo, or a one-PC mechanical buff per the locations supplement table). |
| **Polly Pocket released as messenger** | Off-screen this arc; flagged as live for the OOTA endgame. |
| **Walking-permit medallions still worn** | Jewel of the Styx wights treat the party as Avowed-adjacent. They can witness the midnight shanty without being attacked. |
| **PC holds the second High Tower key** (Session 2 plant) | They open the High Tower Library door from outside in Session 6. Without this they spend 1–2 rounds breaking it. |

**Worst-case party (no payoffs):** all six clues at table; all
four raider skirmishes engaged; Manshoon escapes with the Book of
Vile Darkness; Vault arrival is *after* Manshoon. Session 7 runs
long; party arrives wounded.

**Best-case party (most payoffs):** 3 clues by ally narration;
2–3 solved at table; 1–2 raider skirmishes engaged; Vault arrival
is *concurrent* with Manshoon. Manshoon may escape empty-handed.

## Session 2 — *Frosty Reception + Crime & Politics* (CM Ch. 1–2 compressed)

**Goal:** end the session with "we know it was poison, we know
the heart was hacked out *afterward*, we know someone was
disguised as Sylvira."

**Compress Chapter 1 hard.** The party is already through the
gates and past the Magnus Goldfist / Lurkwood Campaign cover
mission. No snowstorm. **Keep one Ch. 1 beat:** the Endless Chant
Deadwinter Prophecy continues as Fembris hurries the party
toward Exaltation. New line lands today: *"...a many-faced man
... his metal hand pulling strings."* (Manshoon. Plant; do not
explain.)

### Beat 1: The Chapter House (~30 min)

Council of Great Readers convenes in Exaltation's executive
meeting room. Present: **Bookwyrm (alive, First Reader),
Alkrist, A'lai, Daral (visibly hung over), Sylvira (visibly
ill — bitten by Moziqodo last night), Fheminor, Teles, Kazryn,
Kalan.**

Bookwyrm publicly:

1. **Removes Kalan from the investigation.** (Real reason: she
   needs the Gatewarden away from procedural rigor.)
2. **Conscripts the party.** *"You are outsiders. You owe this
   institution nothing. Investigate. Report to me."* (Real
   reason: she thinks they're unqualified and won't solve it.)

Each Reader gets one line of texture. Daral storms out muttering
about Janussi. Fheminor watches Daz with wary curiosity. Sylvira
stays seated longer than necessary, leaning on the table.

**Kalan catches the party privately on the way out:** *"Whatever
Bookwyrm is telling you, listen for what she isn't. I am not the
Gatewarden today. But the keep still has corners. Find me at the
Sea Warden's Tower if you need a key turned."*

### Beat 2: The Crime Scene — Janussi's chamber (~45 min)

Forensic core. Findable clues, in order:

- **The body.** Heart hacked from chest, *messily and after
  death.* Cleaver-style wound; **no defensive injuries.** First
  wrongness — he didn't fight whoever cut him open.
- **Open book on desk:** *The Golden Ass.* Pages damp at the
  edges. DC 15 Investigation: residue is **midnight tears
  poison.** DC 18: he died from contact, not blow.
- **`Magic missile`–scorched armchair** across from the desk.
  Someone fought *here* with magic, but Janussi himself wasn't
  burned. (Sylvira morning fight. The party doesn't know yet.)
- **Lead chain links** on the floor near the body, severed.
  Janussi wore a **lead locket** on a chain. **Trophy plant:
  the empty locket itself can be claimed.** Lead, anti-divination.
- **Safe blasted open with `knock`.** One thing missing: a
  sapphire. (A'lai's morning theft.)
- **Janussi's appointment book** lists yesterday's meetings with
  every Great Reader. Motive bonanza.

**Witness — Miss Hollypocket** (gnome servant, one floor below).
Two crucial things:
- Sylvira came up around 1 am. Hollypocket heard her voice.
  **Queenie the cat hissed and hid.** *"She has known the Great
  Reader for ten years. She has never hissed at her once."*
  (`Disguise self` tell.)
- A'lai came up at dawn — *before* the body was found.
  (Looking for the High Tower key.)

**Witness — Tadric** (Watcher; Kalan's lieutenant). Will admit
he saw **Bookwyrm** on the corridor sometime around 1:30 am.
Bookwyrm was supposedly asleep then.

**End-of-scene state:** the party knows three suspects were in
motion overnight (Sylvira, A'lai, Bookwyrm) and that the murder
weapon was *poison*, not the cleaver. The cleaver was ritual
desecration.

### Beat 3: The Investigators' Office + cliffhanger (~15 min)

Bookwyrm gives them an office in Exaltation. Walking-permit
medallions issued. Fembris attaches as adjutant.

**Cliffhanger.** As the party leaves the office, **Kalan finds
them in the corridor and presses a small brass key into one PC's
hand.**

> *"The High Tower has two keys. Janussi wore one. I held the
> other. I have given it now to someone who is not me. If I do
> not see you tomorrow, do not look for me at my post."*

End session. **Note which PC holds the key — they open the High
Tower Library door from outside in Session 6.**

## Session 3 — *Every Reader Hides a Secret*, Part 1

**Goal:** the party works through the *suspects who look guilty.*
End of session, they have a working theory that's wrong (Sylvira
or Daral did it) and a creeping sense the puzzle doesn't fit.

### Atmosphere opening (~5 min)

Two Avowed sweeping broken glass from the Western Gallery floor
(window facing the Cursed Tower — Moziqodo passed close last
night). The chant overhead drops three verses; an Avowed at the
gallery rail is replaced mid-shift. **Daz feels the pressure on
his sinuses for the first time** — the *first surface-madness
move.* Yvenne, when next seen, names it.

### Beat 1: Searching the Southern Dining Hall (~20 min)

DC 15 Investigation if systematic; DC 12 if specifically looking
at altars/statues. **Find: Janussi's heart and a meat cleaver in
the lead chalice on the statue of Milil.** The chalice is
**lead** — defeats `locate object` and divination. *Someone
planning this knew Candlekeep's spell library.* Heart fails to
respond to `speak with dead` (Bookwyrm cast it first to lock the
corpse out). **The failure is the clue.**

### Beat 2: Sylvira interview — Infernal Fortress (~25 min)

She is *visibly worse* than yesterday. Fevered, tremor, sores
she keeps covered. Story: alone in her chambers; no alibi.
Truth (if pressed honestly or shown the bite): the bite is from
her son **Moziqodo.** Janussi knew, was her lover, gave her ten
days to remove him. She was at the Cursed Tower last night. She
has **Abyssal Plague.** *"I did not kill him. I loved him."*

**She believed the party yesterday about the demon lords.** The
party owes her — and she's the prime suspect.

**Optional Grygum beat:** DC 18 Medicine or `lesser restoration`
to diagnose/treat the plague. Sylvira: *"You are not what I
expected of a half-orc cleric."*

### Beat 3: Daral interview — Bath House (~20 min)

Soaking, drinking, hung over and hostile. Story: yes, he
punched the Keeper; yes, he stole back his Deadwinter gift —
*The Golden Ass.* Sleeve still bloody from the punch. Bragged
loudly to two waiters at supper that he was *"going to give that
man a real Deadwinter present."*

**Daral is dying as the party speaks.** Re-reading *The Golden
Ass* in the bath all morning. Same poison, slower kill. DC 14
Medicine: *that man should sit down.* `Lesser restoration` saves
him *if they catch it in time and care enough.*

**Resolution: player choice, not script.** If saved: rich
witness path in Session 5. If not: dies overnight as canon.

### Beat 4: Fheminor interview — Founder's Court (~20 min)

Old halfling, careful, never quite trusts Daz. Story: nothing
to hide; met Janussi yesterday at 4 pm; sorry he's dead.
Truth (only if earned): **Janussi was about to name her his
successor.** *"He said: 'I am old, and I am tired, and the keep
needs a hand that does not ache. I will name you tomorrow.'"*
She is now in mortal danger and is investigating in parallel.
She watched Bookwyrm closely the morning of the death.

> *"Bookwyrm was not surprised."*

**That last line is the campaign hinge.**

### Scholar interleaves (~30 min)

- **Thorin / Philemon Phase 2** (the pivot — defended scabbard /
  good-parent / equal-partner). Reading Salon's permanent
  silence. Philemon doubled-down clinical today (Janussi was
  his hierarchical superior; he is rattled). Right time for
  Thorin to push for Path C if you want C.
- **Daz / Yvenne — the Vaelissa name lands.** Yvenne's clean
  read of House T'sarran. Connects forward to Polly Pocket
  disposition. **Kalan is alive today; tomorrow he won't be.**
  Bell Tower deadline.

### Optional cliffhanger

As the party returns to the inn, they see **a long hunched
silhouette dragging something across the rooftop above the
Cursed Tower.** Gone in a blink. (Moziqodo. Don't explain.)

**Carry-forward:** Daral alive vs. dead; lead-chalice
anti-divination clue noted; Fheminor's "not surprised" line
delivered.

## Session 4 — *Every Reader Hides a Secret*, Part 2 + **milestone to 9**

2. Race him to B3 (worse position).
3. **Go for the Echoes.** *Manshoon does not care.* Cabinet DC
   18 thieves' tools. **This is the correct play.**

### Beat 3: Manshoon fight (~30 min — only if engaged)

**Manshoon (scaled simulacrum, CR 6):**
- HP 74. AC 12 / 15 with mage armor. Speed 30.
- 14th-level wizard slots: 4/3/3/3/3/2/1 (no 8/9). DC 16, +8.
- Prepared: *fly, shield, mage armor, mirror image, counterspell,
  fireball, dimension door, wall of force, scrying, teleport
  (banked exit).*
- No legendary actions.

Doppelganger disguised in the room — reveals mid-fight.

**Tactics:** R1 mirror image / counterspell. R2 fireball or
wall of force. R3+ tries to grapple-position one PC for
capture (Daz, by visible-drow inference). **At HP ≤ 30 or
counterspelled twice, burns 7th-level `teleport` and escapes.**
Does not die.

**Outcomes vary by ledger:**
- **Best case:** Manshoon escapes empty-handed (party blocked
  B3); takes a single lesser tome. Book of Vile Darkness stays.
- **Mid case (canon):** Manshoon escapes with the Book and a
  wound. Long-term Manshoon thread.
- **Worst case:** Manshoon escapes with the Book and unscathed;
  party heavily damaged.

**Trophy plant:** if Manshoon loses an item — a focus, a
**metal-fingered glove (the prophecy's "his metal hand")**, a
signet — it falls. **Whoever wants it grabs it.**

### Beat 4: Echoes of Alaundo payoff (~25 min — load-bearing)

Cabinet opens. Ten prismatic gemstones. Activation phrase:
*"Speak, seer."* Each Echo plays in full **once;** subsequent
activations play fragments. **Glabbagool's Whispering Dome boon
(if banked):** Glabbagool's voice can re-coax a partial Echo.

Six Echoes are GM-picked atmospheric prophecies. **Four are
OOTA load-bearing.** Run them one at a time. Don't pile-on.

**Echo 1 — The Surface Contamination Prophecy:**

> *"When three queens move beneath the world, the world above
> will sicken. The chant will stutter. The keep will crack. The
> mad will speak the names of drowned cities. This is not the
> wedding. This is the symptom. The wedding is what comes
> next."*

**Echo 2 — The Wedding Prophecy:**

> *"In a grove that does not breathe, a queen of rot will marry
> a king of growth. When the union is consummated, the fungal
> lattice of the world will become her wedding band. Above and
> below will be spores. The bride is not yet veiled. There is
> still time. There is not much time."*

**Echo 3 — The Keeper Prophecy (Eldeth's call):**

> *"A dwarf will return to a hall of stone with the names of
> demons in her mouth. The lords of the deep dwarves will
> believe her, because she will not flinch. They will call to
> a giant-raised dwarf and his shadow-walker, his crow, and his
> bright-blade. They will summon them to a forge that has not
> lit in an age."*

*Giant-raised dwarf* = Thorin. *Shadow-walker* = Zalthir. *His
crow* = Daz. *His bright-blade* = Grygum / Dawnbringer.
*Forge that has not lit in an age* = Gauntlgrym's primordial
fire-forge.

**Echo 4 — The Witness Prophecy (Jimjar / Callarduran):**

> *"A god will walk as a gambler. He will bet on lives. He will
> pay his debts and he will not be paid. When his work is done
> he will leave the marker of a knucklebone, and the holder of
> the marker will be heard once."*

**Trophy plant:** the gemstones themselves are takeable —
ten-stone tray. The party may carry them out as recorded
relics.

### Beat 5: B3 — Book of Vile Darkness (~10 min)

Riddle door (answer: *"candle"*). Inside, in cold rancid air,
the Book on a cracked stone pedestal, radiating darkness.
Touching as non-evil = 8d6 necrotic.

**If Manshoon didn't escape with it:** party choice — take it
(Manshoon-pursuit thread), leave it (Avowed reseal), destroy it
(Grygum arc available: `dispel evil and good` → `holy aura` →
holy fire). **Don't push.**

**If he did:** pedestal empty. Manshoon-pursuit thread active.

### Beat 6: The attic — the rocket option (~5 min, optional)

Sign in archaic Common: *"Last Resort. Pull only when nothing
remains worth keeping."* 60 crystal lights. **The lever
launches the entire Vault tower into the Sea of Swords.** Party
choice. Don't tell them what it does until they pull it.
**Manshoon will be furious if pulled.**

### Beat 7: Return to surface (~15 min)

Climb. Inda holding watch at the brass statue. *"You are alive.
The Seer foresaw this also."* Seals the staircase.

Keep stabilises. Tadric's manual reroute holds. **Chant resumes
— fragmentary, but resumed.** Daz feels the pressure ebb.

**Institutional cleanup:**
- **Alkrist:** Bell Tower cells. Tried by surviving council;
  exile likely.
- **A'lai:** dead in Vault / dead in lava chamber / escaped.
- **Bookwyrm, Janussi, Daral (or recovering), Kalan:** dead.
- **Sylvira:** alive (especially Path B). Senior surviving
  Great Reader.
- **Fheminor:** **becomes Keeper of Tomes.** Janussi's chosen
  successor. Thanks the party formally; remains wary of Daz
  but acknowledges the debt.
- **Tadric:** acting Gatewarden.

Small ceremony in the **Oratory** with Lorekeeper Kei
Tigersteel. Party named *guest seekers of the Avowed* with
permanent Inner Ward access.

### Beat 8: Eldeth's letter (~10 min)

Next morning. House of Rest and the Hearth. **Travel-stained
dwarf courier — not Eldeth herself — arrives.**

> *"I have spoken to the Lords of Mithril Hall and the Lords of
> Gauntlgrym. They believe me. They have been believing me for
> two months — they were waiting for someone whose word weighed
> more than a soldier's. Yours does. Come to Gauntlgrym. The
> Council has questions only you can answer, and the forge has
> been cold long enough that someone is going to need to
> relight it.*
>
> *I told them you would come. Do not make me a liar.*
>
> *— Eldeth Feldrun, Mithril Hall sworn, Ember Vanguard
> giant-friend."*

**Echo 3 clicks.** End campaign arc.
