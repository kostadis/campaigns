# Candlekeep — Sensory beat sheet

A deployable description toolkit for running Candlekeep at the
table. Not a tour, not a location card — the goal is to have a
small set of evocative beats the DM can grab mid-scene to make
the place feel *legendary* without monologuing.

Framed against [Wally DM's three world-building lessons](https://wallydm.com/3-world-building-lessons/):

1. **Lead with the senses, not the facts.** One vivid image
   beats a paragraph of architecture.
2. **Show it through someone who lives there.** Locals don't
   describe their home — they describe what *bothers* them
   about it.
3. **Leave space for player questions.** Every beat should
   prompt "wait, what?" rather than close a door.

Source material: `docs/background/1073077-Inside_the_Great_Library_(Candlekeep_Locations).json`
and `docs/background/1073077-Candlekeep_Murders_-_192_Res_-_26th_Dec.json`.
For canonical building/officer reference see
`notes/sessions/candlekeep_locations.md`. For active-day prep
see `candlekeep_day_one.md` through `candlekeep_day_four.md`.

## Layer 1 — Always-on atmosphere (run under every Candlekeep scene)

These are the three sensory anchors. Mention one or two per
scene; never all three at once. They make Candlekeep feel
*inhabited* rather than toured.

- **The Endless Chant.** Somewhere in the keep, always, an
  Avowed is reciting Alaundo's 99 prophecies in relay. A
  full cycle takes 24 hours. The PCs stop hearing it after an
  hour — and then notice they've stopped hearing it, which is
  worse. Currently led by **Benedora Stoneforge**, dwarf
  priest of Milil. *(Lesson 1: a sound, not a fact.)*
- **The bells, every hour, 6am to midnight.** Acolytes cover
  their ears at the midnight peal because the Bell Tower
  doubles as guest suites *and* prison cells. The PCs will
  learn the keep's geography by where the sound hits hardest.
- **Cats, everywhere.** Originally introduced to control mice;
  now adopted as facility pets. **Queenie** — grey, yellow
  eyes — sleeps on the Keeper's tower stairs and dares anyone
  to step over her. Janussi will not move her.

## Layer 2 — One telling image per location

Wally's "one telling image" lesson: when a PC enters a wing,
deliver *one* striking sensory beat and let the player ask
follow-ups. Do not list features.

- **Arcanium (Daz / Yvenne).** Daz reaches for a shelf without
  asking. A spectral mage hand drifts out of the air and
  slaps his wrist. Yvenne does not look up from her book.
- **Jewel of the Styx.** Empty pirate-ship library by day. At
  midnight an ethereal wight crew materializes, swabs the
  decks, sips ghostly rum, sings lewd shanties, and is gone
  by dawn. Nobody mentions this in the daytime tour.
- **Drakonoikos (Bookwyrm's curatorship).** A library built
  *inside* a preserved ancient red dragon. Bones for pillars,
  scales for walls. Five taxidermied dragon heads exhale
  *illusory* breath weapons as you pass. Thorin can feel the
  heat of the red one through Dawnbringer's scabbard.
- **Astronomicon.** Zero-gravity dome. Books float in glassy
  forcefields you open by pressing both palms for five
  seconds. The telescope splits the dome open at night.
  Daz will want to live here. Thorin will hate it.
- **Whispering Dome.** Sit alone long enough and the voices
  of dead heroes start whispering encouragement specifically
  at *you*. Grygum scene; Bahamut question.
- **Pavilion Naturalis.** A T. rex skeleton in a spiraling
  shell-shaped building. Illusory butterflies and birds drift
  through the air. The illusion glitches in one corner — has
  for years; nobody fixes it.
- **Jook's Box (Melodrome).** A turret room. Touch the
  hand-cranked music box on the desk and the room *unfolds*
  into a concert hall with an ethereal orchestra of dead
  legendary bards made of light. Lasts one song.

## Layer 3 — Lived-in NPC fragments (Wally's "describe it through a local")

The Avowed don't see Candlekeep as a wonder. They see it as a
job. Use these fragments when an NPC describes a place — they
complain, they shrug, they assume you already know.

- **Janussi** (Keeper of Tomes, 69, human). His chambers reek
  of pipe smoke so thick that any Perception-by-smell check
  in the room is at disadvantage. He knows. He doesn't care.
  Conversations happen *through* the haze.
- **Miss Hollypocket** (gnome, ancient, the Keeper's servant).
  Lives on the tower stairs with Queenie. Sees everything;
  says little. Already knows about the bag of holding before
  the PCs mention it. Will not say how.
- **Bookwyrm** (First Reader, dragonborn). Frame as ruthless
  and ambitious, not welcoming. Already in our notes as the
  gatekeeper. She opens with a question, not a greeting.
- **Leuwin** (satyr druid, Oak Tree Apothecary). Absent-minded
  herbalist. Pet squirrel **Nibbles**. Calls every PC by the
  wrong name, consistently — gives Daz a different drow's
  name; gives Thorin a Mithril Hall name. Doesn't notice he's
  doing it.

## Layer 4 — Questions left hanging

Every legendary place has rooms the locals won't explain.
Drop these as background detail; never volunteer the answer.

- **The Cursed Tower.** Cold, rank, abandoned. Non-fiend
  creatures who linger gain exhaustion. Nobody volunteers
  what failed there. Bookwyrm will change the subject.
- **The High Tower Library.** Holds a cryptogram and the
  *lightning orb that powers Candlekeep's wards*. Off-limits.
  Only the two Keepers of Secrets (Janussi and Kalan
  Strongbranch) hold keys.
- **The Howling Well.** Plays all of Alaundo's prophecies on
  loop. The Deadwinter Prophecy (the 99th) plays at 6:15pm
  daily. Nobody can explain why that one always plays at
  that time, or why some Avowed leave the room when it does.
- **Kalan Strongbranch's weekly trip** to the Chambers of
  Lost Lore to consult "an otherworldly being." This is in
  `candlekeep_locations.md`. Drop it as a rumor; never
  resolve it unless players push.

## Why this shape

Three layers map cleanly onto Wally's three lessons:

| Layer | Wally's lesson | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Always-on atmosphere | senses over facts | makes the keep *felt* across every scene |
| One telling image | senses over facts | one moment per wing, not a tour |
| Lived-in NPC fragments | local POV | the place is described by people who live there |
| Questions hanging | space for questions | players pull, the DM doesn't push |

**Hooks into existing party threads:**

- **Janussi's pipe smoke** ↔ Daz's patron mystery (a private
  conversation the spy in the bag of holding cannot smell
  through).
- **Whispering Dome** ↔ Grygum's Bahamut question with Vareth.
- **Drakonoikos** ↔ Thorin / Dawnbringer counseling with
  Philemon.
- **Astronomicon** ↔ Daz's research with Yvenne; Zalthir's
  Jimjar inquiry with Khell-Vire.
- **The Howling Well's Deadwinter Prophecy** ↔ a possible
  hook for the demon-lord summoning timeline (left open).

**How to use at the table:** before any Candlekeep scene,
pick one Layer 1 anchor + one Layer 2 image + one Layer 3
fragment. That's the description. Stop there. Let the
players ask.
