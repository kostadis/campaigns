# GM Tips — Out of the Abyss

Living document. Lessons learned at and around the table that
aren't obvious from the prep docs alone. Append over time; don't
prune unless something is genuinely wrong.

---

## Reading the travelogues / session-prep docs

### Atmosphere bullet lists are *menus*, not scripts

When a session-prep or travelogue doc gives a bullet list of
atmosphere details for a location, **pick 2–3 — not all of them.**
The full list exists so the GM has options to pivot to based on
what the players engage with. Reading every bullet aloud:
- bloats table time
- buries the one specific image the players were supposed to
  remember (the iron pot in the ash, Eldred's two voices, the
  spiral wheat)
- makes the location feel like a checklist instead of a place

Rule of thumb: **one specific postcard image per stop**, plus
1–2 supporting textures. Save the rest for if a player asks.

### "Plant only" / "don't underline" mean what they say

When a doc tags a beat with *plant only*, *don't underline*, or
*pose for it*, the instruction is: **drop the detail and move
on.** No emphasis, no follow-up question, no "did you notice
that?" The payoff comes later when the player connects it
themselves — and that connection only lands if you didn't telegraph
it the first time.

If players miss a planted beat, that is *fine*. Plants are seeds,
not contracts.

### Italic GM notes are for the GM

If a paragraph is in italics or labeled *GM note*, it's framing
for you — not narration to read aloud. Same for thematic notes
like *"the field at low ambient strain in low-magical-density
country."* That's a register cue for your voice, not a line.

### ⭐ markers are load-bearing

Starred items in prep docs are the beats that pay off later in
the campaign (recurring iconography, recognition beats for prior
PCs, breadcrumbs that crystallize at Candlekeep). Hit these. The
unstarred bullets are atmosphere; cut them freely.

## Voice / register

### Travelogues use past-tense montage register

The Blingdenstone-to-Candlekeep travelogue (and any future
flashback / montage doc) is **Kim-via-Kipling**: past tense,
third person, slightly distant, episodic. *"On the third morning,
near the Mithral Hall outer farms, Eldeth turned east."* You are
a narrator looking back, not a DM in the moment. Don't slip into
present-tense in-scene voice unless a player engages and pulls
you into a specific moment.

### Specific small details over sweeping description

One iron pot. One brass plate. One verse wrong. The texture lands
because it's particular — not because it's comprehensive.

## Pacing

### Per-episode rhythm in a montage

For a travelogue stop:
1. 1–2 min of GM narration (atmosphere + the one postcard image)
2. Interview prompts to the named player(s) — 2–3 min each
3. Move on

If you spend more than 6–7 min on a single stop, you've drifted
into playing the scene out. Pull back.

### Most stops spotlight 1–2 players, not all four

Don't force every player to speak at every stop. The prompt list
tells you who. Players who aren't prompted are watching the
montage; that is the correct mode for them in that moment.

---

## Interview-style prompts

When a doc gives prompts like *"Joe — does Thorin offer the
refugee family anything?"*, that's a **question to the player
about their PC**, not improv roleplay and not a skill check
(unless the doc marks one).

Whatever the player says becomes canon for the journey. Write it
down. A pass ("Thorin walks on") is also canon.

---

## When the players miss a beat

Don't rescue planted clues by re-narrating them. If the party
walked past the broken Tempus shrine without engaging, the shrine
stays broken and they walked past. The beat may resurface later
through a different vector, or it may not — both are fine.

The corollary: if the party *does* engage a planted beat, **let
them.** Even if it pulls you off-script. The plants exist to be
discovered, not preserved.
