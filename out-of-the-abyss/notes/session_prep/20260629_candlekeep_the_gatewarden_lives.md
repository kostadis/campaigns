# The Gatewarden Lives

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for **Monday** (Deadwinter Day, afternoon → night; Candlekeep, 1495 DR). Picks up *after* Ch.57 (Alkrist's confession, the heart-and-cleaver, the Bookwyrm summons, and Kalan's key — all already played). **Current state = `summaries/20260622/state_draft/`.**

**The shape you set (so I don't drift):** This is the session where the party **can't win the case** — again. They have a confession (worthless alone), the **heart + cleaver** (prove *a* cover-up, not *her*), and **no hard link** putting Bookwyrm at the scene. She **wins the procedure and makes them stay the day.** Then **that night she is murdered** — justice by irony, not verdict. The party can't save the case and can't save her. **What they *can* do is keep the real key from Manshoon (save Tadric — barely) and survive the assault aimed at them — and in doing so the one thing that actually matters survives: Kalan and his methodology live one more day.** Ends in a fight.

> **GM-only — the emotional engine.** "You have less than you thought" pays its largest installment: the case won't land, their key is a fake, and the villain dies before they can pin her. If that were the whole night it'd be a gut-punch with no upside. The upside is **Kalan.** In the module the Gatewarden is the one who dies; here Kalan was clever enough to offload both keys (real → Tadric, decoy → the party) — so the killers spend their violence on the *keyholders*, and the methodical good man and his idea endure. The party doesn't save the case. They save the future of every case. *That's* the win — bittersweet, earned, and the through-line into the Manshoon arc.

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

- **Primary (thematic):** **Kalan and his methodology survive the night.** The module default is the Gatewarden murdered; that *doesn't happen here* because he distributed the keys and the strike lands on the decoys instead. The party leaves knowing the case failed — but the man who can build the *next* case, and the idea that makes it possible, are still standing.
- **Tangible (what the party actively wins):** **deny Manshoon the real key** — reach Tadric before the Beast (near-impossible) — and **survive/break the assault** aimed at them. That's the on-the-table victory and the fight the table wants.
- **Not on the table:** convicting Bookwyrm (guilty, untouchable) or saving her (she dies tonight, fixed).
- The session lands if they end **in the fight**, understand they were **bait** (the decoy key), keep (or heroically fail to keep) the **real key from Manshoon**, and walk out with the murder revealed as the surface of a **Manshoon** operation reaching for the Vault — now personal.

## The Default Trajectory (if the party coasts / leans on the confession)

- They press the confession; **Bookwyrm (Skoda Vanaster)** shreds it with **Kalan's own paper**; the case dies in the room.
- She makes them stay. Night falls. **The Beast kills on schedule:** Bookwyrm for key #1, then it hunts the *other* keyholders. If the party never twigs the decoy, **Tadric dies and the real key #2 goes to A'lai** — Manshoon now holds **both** keys, the wards fail, and the Vault is one step from open.
- They "investigated a murder" and lost the keys, the witness, and the initiative. ← the outcome to make *loom*, then beat.

## The Clever Play (so you know when to nudge)

1. **Stop trying to win the case.** Hear it (from Kalan, or from their own evidence): it won't land on Bookwyrm tonight. Don't burn the day chasing a conviction that isn't there.
2. **Pull the decoy thread.** *Detect magic on their key came back empty.* That's the whole night's lever: fake key → **they're bait** → the real key is on **Tadric** → the Beast is hunting Tadric.
3. **When the killing starts, protect the key (and the man who holds it), not the corpse.** Bookwyrm is beyond saving and beyond justice. **Tadric and the real key are not.**
4. **Turn the cage into a weapon.** Being forced to stay puts them *on-site* when the night turns — the thing meant to control them is what lets them reach Tadric in time.

## The Keys, the Decoy & A'lai's Two Fronts (read before Scenes 3–4)

A'lai (**Manshoon's inside man**) is after the **two keys to the High Tower Library → the Vault of Secrets** (where the *Book of Vile Darkness* waits). The keys are split three ways:

- **Key #1 (Janussi's):** **Bookwyrm** has it (ripped off the corpse with the locket; she kept it on herself — the **heart + cleaver** she'd stashed are already in the party's hands).
- **Key #2 — the REAL one:** **Tadric**, the Watcher Kalan trusts ("guard it with your life").
- **Key #2 — the DECOY:** **the party (Daz).** Kalan half-distrusts the adventurers *and* won't bet everything on one holder, so he split it — a fake to the party, the real one to Tadric. **The tell is already on the table:** the party **cast *detect magic* and got nothing.** A real Vault key would blaze; theirs is inert. They have the clue; they haven't connected it.

**A'lai can't tell which #2 is real, so he hits both — two targets, two weapons:**
- **The Beast (Moziqodo, CR 5) → Tadric.** A lone Watcher is the Beast's perfect prey. Quiet, lethal, aimed at the **real** key. Party stopping it = **damn near impossible**, not impossible.
- **A Manshoon contingency item → the party.** The Beast-kills-a-lone-target trick *breaks* on four adventurers, so against the PCs A'lai looses conjured guardians. **This is the fight** — aimed at the **decoy**.
- **He also kills Bookwyrm for key #1** (the Beast does it; see Scene 4). **A'lai never appears, never fights, is never exposed** — his real fight (CR 9, with Moziqodo, at the High Tower) is a *later* session.

> **GM-only — the personal turn.** The cascade: *our key read as nothing → it's fake → Kalan used us as bait → the real key is on Tadric → the Beast is on Tadric right now.* That's the beat that turns hired investigators into people with a gut reason to move: they were played, and a man is dying for the key they only *pretended* to hold. Make the Tadric race a real long shot — and let them choose it.

## Scene 5 — The Fight + The Race (the payoff)

> **GM-only — the shape.** **Front 1 = the fight** (Manshoon's monsters come for the party / the decoy). **Front 2 = the race** (the Beast goes for Tadric / the real key). Bookwyrm is already dead — **not** a thing they can fix. Give them a *concrete* win to hold: the real key kept (Tadric saved) and the assault broken — and let the warm, quiet truth land that **Kalan is alive and his policy with him.**

### Front 1 — The monsters come for the party (THE FIGHT)
- Conjured guardians manifest to **take the (decoy) key** and kill resistance. A'lai is nowhere. Objective-based: they want the *key*, not a body count.
- **Terrain pays off:** shelving, spectral mage-hands, *Whispering Dome* acoustics, darkness for Zalthir, chokepoints; Glabbagool eating through a wall to flank.
- **The savored irony:** they fight like hell over a worthless key — until the detect-magic memory lands and the floor drops out (*this was never the real one*), flipping the fight into a desperate *"then where's the real one?!"*
- **Monsters — SETTLED, fully statted in Appendix A1:** **3× Helmed Horror** (corporeal constructs). A'lai would never loose undead/shadow at a cleric with **Turn Undead + a sunblade** — constructs are immune to Turn, have no radiant tell, and make Glabbagool's grapple+acid the answer-key to their AC 20. Initiative-ready math, the acid-shred combo, and the Key-Reaver pivot all live in **Appendix A1.** *(Party is **L8** for this fight — the milestone to 9 is the night's reward; see the cliffhanger.)*

### Front 2 — The Beast comes for Tadric (THE RACE — near-impossible)
- **Moziqodo (CR 5)** hunts **Tadric** across rooftops/galleries on a brutal clock. If the party twigged, they can **break off and race** into a fight with a demon-bound Beast that butchers a lone Watcher and tests even them.
- **Damn near impossible, not impossible.** If they pull it off — **Tadric lives, the real key is denied to Manshoon, and they *earned* it.** If they don't — Tadric dies, the Beast takes the real key, Manshoon has **both**, and it's *personal* now.

### What's NOT on the table — and the quiet win
- **Bookwyrm is dead** (throat-torn) — no rescue, no conviction, no key #1 (the Beast took it). Let the irony sit: the murderer they couldn't touch got touched.
- **Kalan lives.** He was never the target — because he gave the keys away. Land it softly, late: Kalan, alive at the Sea Warden's Tower or wherever they find him, says some version of *"You couldn't prove it. Neither could I. But you kept the door shut one more night — and they didn't get what they came for."* **His policy survives another day.** That's the accomplishment.

> **GM-only — make the win land (your goal).** Whatever breaks, give them something **concrete** to hold: the **real key kept** (Tadric saved) and the **assault broken** — and the *meaning*, that the methodical good man and his idea are still standing. The losses (a fake in their pocket, a murderer escaped into a grave, key #1 gone to Manshoon, maybe a Watcher they couldn't reach) aren't a deflation — they're what make it **matter** and turn it **personal.** End on the wards dropping and the shape of the *real* enemy (Manshoon, reaching for the Book) coming clear.

### Branch — Tadric saved (best case)
They kept the real key from Manshoon and saved a good man against the odds. The High Tower stays shut a while longer. Huge — and earned.

### Branch — Tadric dies, real key lost (recommended-likely)
Manshoon holds both keys; the wards fail faster; next session the High Tower is the battlefield. Clean, propulsive, personal — and Kalan still lives.

### Branch — they never twig
They win the decoy fight; Tadric dies offstage; they learn after that their key was fake and the real one's gone. Painful — the theme's final installment — but **Kalan lives**, so the night isn't a wash. Light the fire for next session.

> The night's difficulty is a **puzzle-and-logistics gauntlet**, not HP. The

## A1 · FRONT 1 — The Fight (Manshoon's bound guardians — Helmed Horrors) — initiative-ready

> **Why constructs, in-fiction (this is A'lai being smart).** A'lai is the
> brains, and **he knows this party** — a cleric of Bahamut with **Turn Undead**
> and a **sunblade.** He would **never** loose undead or shadow-stuff at a
> cleric; they'd be turned and radiant-melted in a round. A thinking operative
> sends **constructs**: immune to Turn, no radiant tell, and his contingency
> animates them on a trigger so his hands stay clean and his alibi holds. The
> swap from the shadow roster isn't a balance patch — it's **characterization.**
> (It also closes the "cleric obliterates the whole room" button the undead
> roster handed the party for free.)

**Encounter math (four L8 PCs + Glabbagool ≈ +0.5 PC → Deadly bar ≈ 8,500 XP).**
Helmed Horrors **play above their CR** — **AC 20 + Magic Resistance + 67 HP**
each means raw XP understates them. **Baseline: 3× Helmed Horror** = 3,300 raw ×
×2 (3 creatures) = **6,600 adjusted on paper, but plays ≈ Deadly** (the AC wall
+ MR eats martial and caster output alike). **Dials:** add a **4th** (8,800 adj,
brutal) if all four PCs commit and are winning; **drop to 2** if they split for
the seam or the table's hurting. If they **split**, send one Horror "after the
runners" as a race hazard rather than deleting the rump party.

> **Glabbagool is glass — keep him mobile.** 44 HP / AC 14. He resists
> acid/cold/fire but the Horrors deal **slashing + force**, neither resisted,
> ~26/round each — two hits and he's reeling. He's a **skirmisher and a combo
> piece**, not a front-liner. (Math assumes his Whispering Dome handoff **landed
> Monday daytime** — an owed run-sheet beat; skip it and the fight is plain
> **4 PCs** → drop to 2 Horrors.)

**Objective = the key, not a body count — and this is the pressure valve.** The
guardians are **programmed to seize the decoy key off Daz and extract.** One is
the **Key-Reaver** (grab it, **fly** for the exit); the other two **block and
punish.** The fight is *meant to be interrupted*: the moment the Reaver bolts
with a key that read as **nothing** under detect magic, the framing collapses →
**A2.** They never have to grind 200 HP of plate — they have to **realize.**
A'lai is nowhere.

### Roster quick-ref — Helmed Horror (XMM, CR4, 1,100 XP ea.)
| Field | Value | Play note |
|---|---|---|
| **AC / HP** | **20** / 67 (9d8+27) | The wall — until Glabba grinds it down (below). |
| **Speed** | 30, **fly 30 (hover)** | The Reaver flies the key up and out; deny the exit lane. |
| **Attacks** | Multiattack **2× Arcane Sword** +6, **13 (1d8+4 slash + 1d10 force)** ea. | ~26/round each — splitting the party halves the incoming. |
| **Magic Resistance** | adv. on saves vs. spells/magic | **Daz's save-or-suck is weak here** — favor non-save damage & positioning. |
| **Spell Immunity (3, creator's pick)** | suggest **Heat Metal · Lightning Bolt · Hold Person** | *Don't* immune Daz's single best option — keep them beatable, not anti-fun. |
| **Immunities** | dmg: necrotic, poison · cond: blinded, charmed, frightened, **paralyzed, stunned**, etc. | **Not undead** (no Turn Undead) · **no radiant vuln** · **Blindsight 60** → darkness won't hide PCs from them. |
| **NOT immune to** | **grappled · restrained · prone** | ← the whole reason this flavor works for Zalthir + Glabba. |

### Terrain & the new "answer key" — Glabba's acid + the grapple lock
- **⭐ Glabbagool's combo finally fires — and it's the answer to AC 20.** Zalthir
  **grapples** a Horror (no grapple immunity); **Corrosive Embrace** (2 Ki): DC
  13 Dex save, **14 (4d6) acid** — and because a Helmed Horror is **animated,
  nonmagical plate**, each failed save is a **permanent, cumulative −1 to its AC**
  (20→19→18…, destroyed at AC 10). **Corrosive Surge** (1 Ki, once/turn) does the
  same rider on any pseudopod hit. **Two or three rounds of Zalthir-pin +
  Glabba-acid turns an AC-20 wall into something the martials hit on a 6.** This
  is the sidekick's **debut** — let it land. *(Ruling to keep it clean: treat
  Glabba's ooze acid as a **physical** corrosion, **not** a spell → the Horror's
  Magic Resistance gives **no** advantage on the DC-13 save. The master takes no
  acid — built in.)*
- **Blindsight rewrites the light game.** The Horrors **can't be blinded** and
  **sense 60 ft. without sight** — so **darkness no longer hides the party** from
  them and Zalthir's vanish is muted. Zalthir's job tonight is the **grapple**,
  not the disappear. Glabba (also Blindsight 60) is the one piece fully at home
  in the dark. *Daz's Darkness is now a trap — it blinds the party, not the
  constructs.*
- **Use the room:** chokepoints to deny the flying **Reaver** a clean exit;
  Glabba's **amorphous** 1-inch squeeze + **Shadow Step** to body-block or
  re-grapple it; *Whispering Dome* acoustics for the chase.

### The pivot trigger (built into the objective)
When the **Key-Reaver grabs the decoy and flies for the door**, put the
contradiction in the air: *they're dying over an object a Manshoon construct is
willing to kill for — and that object read **inert** under detect magic.* Don't
say it; let a player ask *"why is this thing stealing a key that isn't even
magic?"* → **A2 opens.** If the Reaver **escapes** with it, cleaner still: they
chased a decoy out the window while the **real** key bleeds across the keep.

## A2 · THE SEAM — the only path to the save (run this as the real "encounter")

> This is where the night is won or lost — **not** in Moziqodo's HP. Four
> gates stand between the party and Tadric. Each one **costs time** while
> Moziqodo's ~2-round clock runs on Tadric (A3). Realistically they **cannot**
> clear all four before the Beast reaches him **unless they move fast and go
> straight to Kalan.** Kalan is the shortcut through gates 3–4.

**Gate 1 — "The key is a fake."**
*Tell already on the table:* detect magic showed **nothing**; a real Vault key
would blaze. **Triggers:** a PC recalls it (let Insight/Arcana or just player
memory land it free); re-cast detect magic mid-fight; or **Dawnbringer /
Glabbagool** (sentient) flag the dead key. *If they're slow,* have the
guardians **fight to the death for a key that reads as inert** — the
contradiction is the nudge.

**Gate 2 — "Then Kalan split it."**
Callback to **Beat 2** (Monday AM): *"I have given it now to someone who is not
me."* They were *told* he distributed the key. Now it parses: **decoy to them,
real one to someone else.** (Insight/recall, or Kalan's exact words re-read.)

**Gate 3 — "…but WHO, and WHERE?"  ← the information wall.**
**Kalan never named Tadric to them.** They do **not** know the holder or the
location. This is the gate that makes the race near-impossible *on its own* —
and the **only** clean way through it is **Kalan himself.**

**Gate 4 — THE SEAM: find & confront Kalan.**
Kalan is **alive** (he gave the keys away — he was never the target). His
Beat-2 breadcrumb: *"If I do not see you tomorrow, **do not look for me at my
post.**"* → he's gone to ground on purpose. **Bolt-hole = the Sea Warden's
Tower** (per arc flowchart S4Kalan). A PC who thinks *"where would Kalan hide,
not where he works"* finds him.
- **What Kalan gives them (the unlock):** *"Tadric. A Watcher — loyal, alone,
  and he does not know what he carries or what's coming for it. Top of the
  Pillars walk / the north galleries. **Run.**"* He can also hand a
  **shortcut** (a Watcher's stair, a key to a barred gallery door) **as a
  reward for reaching him fast** — that shortcut is what turns "too late" into
  "arrive during the attack" (A3).
- **If they confront him mid-fight / send a runner:** even better — Kalan
  *moving them* is the whole point. He won't fight (sidelined, watched) but he
  **aims** them.

**Failure-to-seam:** if they never find Kalan, they're guessing Tadric's
location blind → they arrive (if at all) **after** the throat-tear. Tadric
dead, real key gone to Manshoon. **Kalan still lives** — the theme's quiet
floor holds even on the worst night.

## A3 · FRONT 2 — Moziqodo & Tadric (the clock, not the boss)

### Moziqodo, "The Beast" — CR 5 (1,800 XP)  *[Barbed-Devil chassis, abyssal-shadow reflavor]*
*Medium fiend (demon), unaligned — Sylvira's abyss-born son, bound by A'lai with the Iron Bands of Bilarro*

- **AC** 15 (shadow-knit hide) · **HP** 85 (10d8+40) · **Speed** 40 ft., **climb 40 ft.**
- **STR** 16 (+3) · **DEX** 18 (+4) · **CON** 18 (+4) · **INT** 8 (−1) · **WIS** 13 (+1) · **CHA** 12 (+1)
- **Saving Throws** DEX +7, CON +7 · **Skills** Stealth +7, Perception +4
- **Resistances** cold, necrotic; bludg./pierc./slash. from nonmagical attacks · **Immune** poison; poisoned
- **Senses** darkvision 120 ft. (**unimpeded by magical darkness**), passive Perception 14
- **Languages** understands Abyssal, can't speak (a "Beast")

**Traits**
- **Shrouded.** In dim light or darkness it has advantage on Stealth and is
  lightly obscured to sight-reliant creatures. Rooftop predator — it fights
  from above and from the dark.
- **Pounce.** If it moves ≥20 ft. straight toward a creature and hits with
  **Rend** the same turn, target makes **DC 15 STR save** or falls prone. While
  the target is prone or grappled, Moziqodo may use **Throat-Tear** as a
  **bonus action.**
- **Bound (lore, not a Monday lever).** A'lai holds its leash via the Iron
  Bands. Only the **Sylvira path (S6)** breaks it → it turns on A'lai. Not in
  play tonight.

**Actions**
- **Multiattack.** Two **Rend** attacks.
- **Rend.** +7, reach 5 ft. **11 (2d6+4) slashing.** Hit vs. Large-or-smaller
  → **grappled (escape DC 14).**
- **Throat-Tear** *(recharge 5–6, **or** bonus action vs. a prone/grappled
  target).* +7, reach 5 ft., one grappled/prone creature: **18 (4d6+4)
  slashing.** *This is the lone-Watcher killer — vs. an NPC it drops to ≤0,
  it's a kill.*

**Bonus Actions**
- **Shadow Leap.** Move up to its speed across any surface (walls, rooftops)
  without provoking opportunity attacks — it's never where the party can pin it.

### Tadric — the man on the fuse  *[MM Veteran, CR 3]*
- **AC 17** (splint) · **HP 58** · Multiattack 2× longsword +5 (8 each).
- **He is fleeing, not dueling** — doesn't know a demon is coming. Play him
  running, not trading blows. Moziqodo **Shadow-Leaps + Pounces** → Tadric
  prone → grappled → **Throat-Tear.**
- **The clock:** ~**2 rounds** from the moment Moziqodo engages Tadric to a
  dead Watcher (Pounce R1 → grapple, Throat-Tear R1 bonus if it pounced;
  finish R2). That two-round window **is** the race's finish line.

### Adjudicating the race (clean rule)
Cut between fronts each round (Stage directions). **Tadric dies at the end of
the 2nd round Moziqodo is on him** unless a PC is *present and acting.*
- **Arrive with the Kalan shortcut** → you land **during** R1–R2: Tadric alive,
  Beast in melee, **real fight on** (and at 85 HP vs. even a depleted party,
  winnable — reward the achievement).
- **Arrive without the shortcut** → R3+: you find **Moziqodo over the body**,
  **real key in its claws**, one Shadow-Leap from gone. Maybe you bloody it;
  the key is already lost.
- **Never twig / never reach Kalan** → offstage kill; they learn after that
  the key was fake and Manshoon has both. Light the fire for next session.

**Whatever the outcome:** Bookwyrm is dead (no save), and **Kalan lives.** End
on the wards dropping and Manshoon's shape coming clear — and **award the
milestone to level 9.** Surviving the night *is* the level-up: the compression
that folded the old Reader-interview sessions into Monday moved the milestone
here, and the back-half sessions assume the party is **L9.**
