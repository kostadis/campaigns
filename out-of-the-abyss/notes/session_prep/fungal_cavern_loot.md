# Fungal Cavern Loot — Post-Battle Sweep

**Status:** Staging draft. Human review required before any promotion to grounding docs.

**When:** Immediately after Ilvara's death in the fungal cavern at Velkynvelve. Asha Vandree is on her knees praying; the last T'sarran warrior has retreated; the heart fungus is wilted; spores are dissipating.

**Framing:** The alliance terms were "party kills Ilvara and destroys the mushroom artifact in exchange for everything Ilvara has accumulated, and the gratitude of Lolth." Asha is doctrinally bound to honor this, but she is also a zealot reshaping events into Lolth's narrative in real time. Expect her to:
- Surrender Ilvara's secular wealth freely (she never cared about it).
- Yield priestly regalia grudgingly, with warnings about corruption.
- Try to keep anything ritual-adjacent to Lolth proper ("this remains the goddess's property").
- Quietly pocket anything she thinks the party hasn't noticed.

The whole haul is Asha-brokered: she decides what's on the table. If the party pushes, leverage is her theological fragility — she needs the "divine test passed" narrative to hold.

## 1. Ilvara's Accumulated Hoard

### Priestess-of-Lolth regalia (Asha will surrender, grudgingly)
- **Scourge of the Five Heads.** Ilvara's snake-headed scourge, canonical Lolth-priestess weapon. A five-tailed whip with living serpent heads as lashes, currently Zuggtmoy-corrupted: fungal nodules bloom from the serpent eyes and the heads move sluggishly. *Asha's position:* "It must be purified or broken. It cannot serve as it is."
  - **Stats — corrupted (as found):** Reskinned **Tentacle Rod** (DMG p. 208, rare, attunement) with five heads instead of three, reduced for corruption. While holding the rod, action to direct each of the five serpent heads to attack a creature within 15 ft. Each head: melee attack at +7 (reduced from +9 by the fungal decay), hit deals 1d6 piercing (snake bite). If you hit a target with at least four of the five heads, DC 13 CON save or speed halved, disadvantage on DEX saves, no reactions for 1 minute; on its turns it can take an action or a bonus action but not both; save at end of each turn to end. **Corruption tax:** on any turn you attack with it, DC 10 CON save or be poisoned for 1 round as the spores aerosolize.
  - **Stats — purified:** full **Tentacle Rod** as printed (three tentacles at +9, 1d6 bludgeoning, DC 15 CON on all-three hit), reflavored as the three surviving serpent heads after the two most corrupted were cut away. No corruption tax.
  - **How to purify:** a dawn ritual using Dawnbringer's radiant light over an uninterrupted long rest, OR a cleric of Lolth (Asha qualifies, if she's willing) spending a 3rd-level spell slot in a consecration rite, OR submerging it in holy water from a Lolth shrine. Any of the three works; pick whichever is narratively ugliest for the party.
- **Drow chain shirt, blackened finish.** Sized for Ilvara; armor of elvenkind-adjacent but not magical. Resale among drow: ~200 gp; worthless in the Underworld sunlight trade.
- **Holy symbol of Lolth** — obsidian spider on a platinum chain, one leg snapped off in the fight. Asha wants this burned-and-buried per rite; if the party argues, she'll concede it so long as they promise to return it to a Lolth shrine. (This is a lie-of-convenience lever for Daz later.)
- **Piwafwi cloak** — drow fungal-dyed, light-fragile. Unharmed by the fight because the battle was underground. Deteriorates on prolonged sunlight exposure (canonical drow item behavior). Good for Daz, Zalthir, or Nym.

### Drow outpost wealth (Asha will surrender freely)
- **Coin:** 480 gp in drow-minted coin (spider-marked), 1,200 sp, 300 ep. Drow mintage sells at a discount outside the Underdark — roughly 60% face among surface merchants who recognize it; full value among any drow-friendly fence.
- **Trade bars:** Six iron-banded silver bars, ~50 gp each. Velkynvelve's payroll float.
- **Gems (mixed):** Four pieces of black star sapphire (~100 gp each), a cluster of eighteen small moonstones (~10 gp each), one uncut bloodstone (~50 gp) set aside as a Lolth offering.
- **Jailers' ring of keys.** Opens every manacle and cell in the outpost. Narrative value only — the outpost is gone — but amusing souvenir / Grygum will want it.

### Prisoner effects (callback stash — confiscated from past captives)
Ilvara kept a locked chest of items taken from prisoners who broke early. Anything here should be a callback — props from earlier chapters, things NPCs lost, things that re-surface character threads. Suggested contents (pick 2–3, leave the rest for later sessions):
- A silvered dagger engraved with a surface temple's sigil.
- A journal in Dwarvish, half-water-damaged, belonging to a presumed-dead prisoner.
- A child's carved wooden spider figurine — Underdark provenance, unclear story.
- A ring of Thorin-recognizable make (Mithral Hall style) — hook if Thorin wants one.
- Stoppered clay flask of surface-world brandy, somehow intact.

*GM note:* pull whichever ties to an unresolved thread in `docs/campaign_state.md`. If nothing does, leave the chest for a later session and just hand-wave "miscellaneous personal effects."

### Neverlight Grove pilgrimage items (Asha does NOT want these near her)
Ilvara brought these back from her corruption site. Asha considers every one of them spiritually poisonous.
- **Three stoppered vials of spore wine** from the Grove. Consumed: DC 13 CON save or hallucinate for 1 hour. Can be weaponized (thrown, 15-ft cloud) or bartered with myconids / Zuggtmoy cultists.
- **A dried sovereign fruiting body**, roughly the size of a fist, kept in a lead-lined box. Alchemical component — a myconid druid could use it; anyone else handling it ungloved risks spore exposure (DC 12 CON or 1d4 psychic as suggestive whispers). Asha's position: "Burn it. Do not open the box." (She is right.)
- **A pilgrim's token** — carved mycelium wafer on a cord, inscribed with Zuggtmoy's fruiting-body sigil. Worthless materially; grants social access / recognition among any Zuggtmoy-infected circle. Could be kept as an infiltration prop or surrendered to Asha to burn.
- **The mushroom artifact (destroyed per deal).** Fragments only. Asha wants them pulverized into the spore-dust and scattered. If the party tries to salvage, she will notice and will mark it as the first crack in her "Lolth passed her test" narrative.

### Ilvara's papers (not coin, but worth looting)
- **Prayer book to Lolth**, with later Zuggtmoy marginalia in a different hand. Shows the corruption timeline. Useful for any future deprogramming-style scene; mechanical spellbook value only if the party has a Lolth cleric (they don't — but Asha does).
- **Two sealed orders from House Mizzrym**, unopened. Intel value: confirms Ilvara was still taking instruction from her House when Zuggtmoy overtook her. Reinforces that Mizzrym is a secondary political thread.
- **A letter from Neverlight Grove**, written in mixed Drow and fungal-glyph. Partially legible; references "the bride" and a meeting that never happened.

## 2. Asha Vandree's Personal Kit

Only relevant if the party betrays her, she dies, or they search her. If the alliance holds, she keeps all of this.

- **Scale mail, drow-make**, spider-fretted trim. ~50 gp secular value.
- **Scourge (personal)** — three-head, serviceable, non-magical. Stats: treat as a **whip** (martial, finesse, reach — 1d4 slashing), reflavored as three knotted-leather lashes tipped with small iron spider-fangs. No rider effects; this is priestess flair, not Ilvara's artifact.
- **Holy symbol of Lolth** — silvered spider, worn smooth at one point where she thumbs it during prayer. ~25 gp; significant to her.
- **Spell components pouch** — standard cleric kit, plus a single preserved black widow in resin (material for a Lolth-specific ritual).
- **Coin:** 35 gp, 120 sp. She does not travel rich.
- **Prayer beads** — mundane. Obsidian and bone. Counts as a focus but not a Prayer Beads magic item.
- **Sealed waxed pouch** containing her personal doctrinal notes — her framework for why Zuggtmoy's claim on Lolth is heresy. Would be extraordinarily useful for any future Lolth-heresy scene or for manipulating her. She does not know anyone has seen her write this.
- **A small carved finger bone** on a thong around her neck — claims it is from a matron who was "given to Lolth." May be true, may not. Sentimental to her.

*If she dies:* the finger bone and the doctrinal notes are the two items that matter. Everything else is replaceable drow-priestess kit.

## 3. Cavern Sweep — Everything Else

### The T'sarran dead
Five warriors incinerated by Daz's Fireball; one mage killed by Zalthir; one warrior escaped. Bodies are charred but recognizable. Searchable gear:
- **Three intact hand crossbows** (two ruined by the blast). Drow-make.
- **Roughly 20 crossbow bolts**, of which ~8 are still coated in usable drow poison (DC 13 CON or unconscious 1 hour, canonical drow sleep poison — single dose each, degrades in ~1 minute in sunlight).
- **Three piwafwi cloaks** in salvageable condition. Same sunlight-fragility caveat.
- **Two short swords, one long sword**, drow-make, unremarkable.
- **Coin on bodies:** ~85 gp total, mostly in drow-minted spider coin.

### The T'sarran mage (Zalthir's kill — the intel body)
Search this one carefully. He is the reason the party now has a House problem.
- **Arcane focus** — obsidian wand tipped with a captive spider in amber. ~150 gp. Functional focus, not a magic item.
- **Component pouch**, standard.
- **Signet ring — House T'sarran.** This is the one that matters. Confirms the attacking House, provides a seal for forgery, and is worth keeping hidden from Asha (Houses-playing-Houses is not her theology's problem). Grygum flagged the need to identify the Matron Mother; this ring is a thread Vizeran or any drow-facing broker can pull.
- **Folded parchment** — partial orders in Undercommon cipher. References "the mage Daz" by name, confirms the extraction contract is "alive, unharmed, paid on delivery." No contracting party named.
- **Coin purse:** 110 gp, 3 small amethysts (~50 gp each).

### Fungal minions
Former Velkynvelve drow guards, now dead (Insect Plague). Bodies are spore-blasted — gear fused to flesh, largely unsalvageable. One exception:
- **A steel whistle** on a chain around one minion's neck — Velkynvelve guard muster signal. Still functional. Worthless commercially; potentially useful as a bluff prop.

### The Heart Fungus wreckage
Destroyed by Dawnbringer's radiant damage. The corpse of the thing is a wilted mass ~15 ft across, still twitching.
- **Spore sacs, three intact.** Alchemically active for ~24 hours before they go inert. A capable alchemist or druid could render each into one of: a single-use Fog Cloud grenade, a poison coating for up to 4 weapons (DC 12 CON, 1d4 poison), or a preserving mash (slows rot on dead bodies). GM pick.
- **Heart-stone / core.** A fist-sized dense pearl-like node at the center of the fungus, radiating dim residual necromantic energy. 300 gp to the right buyer (Sloobludop mushroom-market, a Menzoberranzan occultist, or a Zhent fence). Asha will recognize it if she sees it and demand it be destroyed — palm it or take the fight.

### Ilvara's elevated platform
The stone outcrop where Ilvara was perched before Zalthir dragged her down. Searchable, nobody has looked yet.
- **A ritual circle** drawn in chalk and blood, half-smeared from the fight. Not portable; worth sketching / photographing (Daz has Mage Hand, this is a 10-minute job).
- **A leather-wrapped bundle** containing two unused incense cones (Zuggtmoy-coded), a small silver bowl (~40 gp), and a folded cloth marked with both Lolth's and Zuggtmoy's sigils stitched one inside the other — the physical evidence of the syncretism Asha calls heresy.

## Negotiation Notes for the GM

- **The spore wine, the sovereign fruiting body, the heart-stone, and the Zuggtmoy-Lolth cloth** are the items Asha most wants destroyed. If the party keeps any of them, there is a deferred trust cost with her — not immediate, but it compounds.
- **The T'sarran signet, the extraction-contract parchment, and the House Mizzrym sealed orders** are the items Asha does not care about and will not think to claim. Pure party upside.
- **The prisoner-effects chest** is a good place to seed a hook for whichever PC needs one next. Don't spend the whole chest in one scene.
- **Total "liquid" take** — coin + easily-fenceable gems + salvageable drow gear — is roughly 2,800 gp equivalent, before any party-specific magic items are claimed. Adjust if that's wrong for your pacing.
- **Anything marked magical here is provisional.** If the party is already overloaded on items, downgrade the scourge and the heart-stone to flavor props.
