# The Grygumite Disputation

Co-GM working doc. **Day 1 of the investigation — the day Janussi's body was found.** Block 2
reader interviews seeded with the Grygumite lecture device. **Ben (Grygum) absent this session.**

Timeline anchor: Janussi died last night just after midnight. It is now late morning of the same
calendar day. The midnight clock tonight is Daral's death — not tomorrow night.

Pairs with:
- `notes/sessions/candlekeep_block2_reader_interviews.md` — full scene text and DC ladders
- `notes/sessions/candlekeep_evidence_map_forward.md` — live conclusion tracker

---

## The Win Condition

The session lands if the party exits with: (1) the heart found in the lead chalice and the
`speak with dead` failure registered as a clue; (2) Fheminor's **"Bookwyrm was not surprised"**
delivered and absorbed; (3) Sylvira interviewed cold and, ideally, treated; (4) Daral's fate
decided before midnight. Teles is a bonus if the pace allows. Kalan's absence — if the tower
is already empty when they check — is the session's earned surprise.

---

## The Default Trajectory (party does nothing clever)

- Daral doesn't connect the dots. Midnight. He dies in a corridor, face-down, ink-stained thumb at
  his lips. The party watched it happen.
- Nobody treats Sylvira. Bedridden by tomorrow. The S6 archmage ally is gone.
- Teles keeps his blackmail calculation to himself. Party never learns the lantern detail this session.
- Fheminor gives pleasantries. "Bookwyrm was not surprised" stays unsaid.
- Nobody checks the Sea Warden's Tower. Kalan disappears without anyone noticing today.

---

## The Clever Play

1. **Save Daral by deduction, not by roll.** He licked the pages in front of them. Midnight Tears.
   *A poisoner does not re-read his own pages.* That one sentence is both his exoneration and his
   death clock. The party saves him by reasoning through it and casting lesser restoration.
2. **Use the lecture window.** The Dining Hall, Kitchens, Erudite Outfitters are empty while
   everyone's watching Grygum. Go now.
3. **Check the Sea Warden's Tower before the lecture ends.** Kalan said "if you don't see me
   tomorrow" — today is tomorrow. Go while Bookwyrm is visibly onstage and can't be the one
   who made him disappear.
4. **Ask Fheminor what she *noticed*, not what she *did*.** She says nothing about herself
   unless pushed. She says everything about others.
5. **Let Teles feel superior.** He doesn't respond to Persuasion cold. Get him talking about the
   ironies of Bookwyrm's position, then ask.
6. **Dawnbringer can cast lesser restoration.** Grygum isn't here. Thorin has the sword. The sword
   heals others when Thorin asks. This is the Sylvira unlock — Thorin just needs to try.

## OPENING — The Grygumite Disputation

**Setup.** The Floratheka, late morning. Grygum has been working through *Fey-mous Flowers &
Fungi* — the description of midnight tears matches the black discoloration, the midnight timing,
the saliva reaction. It's a strong working theory; he hasn't confirmed it. He's still at the
table when Vareth appears between the stacks. He is vibrating with the particular energy of an
academic whose field has been upended overnight.

> **Vareth's interior:** He's been looking for Grygum since dawn. Word of the Stroud's Gambit
> findings spread faster than the murder news. Three senior Avowed have invoked a Formal
> Disputation — not a seminar, a *debate*. The proposition: *"Does a creature's nature bind its
> acts, or do its acts constitute its nature?"* Grygum's fieldwork is the only empirical evidence
> anyone has produced in three centuries. Vareth needs the witness, not just the theorist. He is
> not asking. He is here because protocol gives him authority and he is using it.

*"The Oval Theatre. Right now. Half the Avowed are already there."* He is not exaggerating:
**Bookwyrm will be there** (absence is a political statement she cannot afford during a lockdown),
**Teles** will be in the back row looking amused, **Fheminor** will attend and leave early,
**A'lai** — a bronze dragonborn — will not sit out a debate about whether his nature determines
his acts. The disputation is happening whether Grygum comes or not; Vareth just needs him in
the room so the empirical half of the argument has a body.

Grygum goes. He has no real choice and doesn't look for one — this is the kind of pull he'd
resent and be privately pleased by.

**The key handoff.** He's not walking into an academic auditorium holding Kalan's key. He passes
it before he goes — to whoever is closest, without ceremony. **Note which PC takes it.** It opens
the High Tower from outside in a later session and the handoff PC is load-bearing.

**Fembris and Vooshadi.** Both want to attend. Vooshadi keeps her face neutral; Fembris does not.

| Decision | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Let them both go | Free movement through Candlekeep; no minder, no fetch service |
| Send one, keep one | Half coverage; Fembris is the better fetch service; Vooshadi the better guard |
| Keep both | Full supervision; Vooshadi's goodwill takes a quiet hit |

> Right answer is probably to let them go. The Dining Hall and Kitchens are better without
> an Avowed present.

## SCENE A — Southern Dining Hall + Kitchens

**Setup.** Late morning. Long tables cleared from breakfast, not yet reset. Milil's statue in the
alcove — god of bards, open hands, brass chalice. Two waiters stacking linens. They recognize Daral.

**Daral in this scene.** He freezes at the threshold. *"That's Milil's chalice. It's lead-lined.
We use it — used it — for the Midsummer wine."* He knows this room; he's attended every feast
for twenty years. The lead detail comes out before he realizes he's said something important.

**The search.** DC 15 Investigation (room sweep), DC 12 if they go straight to the altars.

- ⭐ The lead chalice holds: Janussi's **heart**, a **bloodstained meat cleaver**, burnt incense ash.
- **`Speak with dead` on the heart FAILS.** Let the player roll. Count to three. Say nothing.
  The silence is the clue — not a soft failure, a designed one.
- Lead chalice = anti-divination. Someone planned this murder around Candlekeep's own spell
  collection. That is not a passing visitor. **Make sure this hypothesis is said aloud** — even
  as a player inference. It lights conclusion #2 and starts pointing at an insider.

**The waiters (free).** If pressed: *"Give that man a real Deadwinter present"* — they heard it.
He was drunk and angry. He didn't look like a man with a plan. Useful: paints the brag as
impulsive, not premeditated.

**The Kitchens (fold in, same block).** Sprig Summerfoot, halfling kitchen worker, nervous,
specific. At ~1am: *"Sylvira came through. Didn't say a word — just pointed at her throat like
she couldn't speak and took one of the big cleavers. Grabbed honey off the shelf as cover,
I thought. Odd time for honey."*

> **GM-only:** A tiefling who cast `silence` on herself can't speak. Sprig doesn't know that —
> she just thought it was rude. The silence bubble explains why the shapeshifter avoided
> Candlekeep's Endless Chant detection wards. The cleaver matches the one in the chalice; Sprig
> knows her own tools.

**Branch — if they skip this.** Daral: *"We should check the Milil alcove. After the Yule feast
in '82 someone hid a — it doesn't matter. It's where you hide something in the Hall."* Or
Queenie staring up at the chalice. Use whichever is most organic.

## SCENE A.5 — Oak Tree Apothecary (Leuwin + Nibbles)

Between the Sea Warden's Tower and the House of Mechanus. A wooden treehouse in the boughs of
a huge evergreen oak. Leuwin is an elderly, absent-minded satyr druid. His pet squirrel is
Nibbles.

**Why go here.** This is where conclusion #1 gets confirmed — not theorized, *confirmed*. The
party has midnight tears as a working theory from Grygum's Floratheka research. This is the
proof.

**Leuwin's ledger.** DC 12 Investigation or just ask Leuwin to check his stock. A vial of
midnight tears is missing from inventory. He didn't notice — he's absent-minded. The missing
vial *names the poison*. Conclusion #1 fully lit.

**Nibbles (animal-speech).** Whoever holds the Stonespeaker Crystal can speak with the squirrel.
Nibbles saw someone in the apothecary recently who didn't belong. His description:
*"big lizardskin, brown-yellow scales."* That is **Alkrist** — the bronze dragonborn on the
Council of Great Readers. This is the first pointer at the actual poisoner (#5).

> **GM-only:** Don't name Alkrist. Let Nibbles give the description. The party has seen Alkrist
> at the council meeting; they can make the connection themselves. If they don't, bank it.

**Branch — if they skip this.** The Daral interview also lights #1 (he carried the book, licked
the pages in front of them, the logic forces the conclusion). The apothecary is the cleaner
confirmation and adds #5 groundwork. Run it if you have a block.

## SCENE B — Sea Warden's Tower (Kalan)

**Setup.** During the lecture window. Kalan said "if you don't see me tomorrow" — and tomorrow
is now, today. **Check while Bookwyrm is visibly onstage.** This is the optimal window: if
something has happened to him, at least you know it wasn't her in the last hour.

**If he's there:** Quick scene. He has his own parallel overnight findings — he can share the
Deidran sighting: Bookwyrm detoured behind the Erudite Outfitters at first light (the bloody
cloak is in the bins out back). He's shaken but holding. He wants to know what the party found.
This is the collaboration payoff.

**If he's already gone:** Door unlocked. Research notes on the table, partial but legible.
No sign of struggle. The party must figure out Pont de Paramours later. For now: Kalan is
missing. Bookwyrm is currently onstage with every senior Avowed watching her. These two facts
sit in the same sentence in the party's heads — the GM doesn't need to connect them.

> **Timing threshold:** Kalan disappears this evening per the arc. A check during the lecture
> (midday) should find him present and frightened. An afternoon check after the lecture ends
> may find the tower empty. Decide now which way you want to play it.

## SCENE C — Sylvira, the Infernal Fortress

⚠️ **FIRST CONTACT.** The party has never met her. To them she is the "devil woman" from
Hollypocket's account: wrong smell, wrong light, descending a tower she shouldn't be in.
Open cold. No prior rapport. She is not pleased to receive investigators.

**Setup.** Her quarters in the Infernal Fortress: cramped scholar's den, sulphur and fever smell,
forearm covered under a study robe. She does not offer tea.

**Her story (public).** Alone last night. No alibi. She and Janussi hadn't spoken in weeks.
She doesn't flinch from being a suspect — she seems almost past caring.

**Her truth (earned — if pressed honestly, or if shown the bite mark).**
Janussi was her lover. He gave her ten days to remove her son Moziqodo from the keep or he'd
report the breach of Candlekeep residency rules. She was at the Cursed Tower last night trying to
reach him. The bite on her forearm is Moziqodo's: **Abyssal Plague**, being suppressed with
scrolls she can't afford to keep using.

> *"I did not kill him. I loved him."*

Deliver flat. No tears. Move on. The table will circle back on its own.

**The healing beat.** Grygum isn't here. Dawnbringer can cast lesser restoration — Thorin needs
to ask the sword. The sword will; Thorin may not think to. If Sylvira gets treated today, she
survives as the archmage ally. If not, she's bedridden by tomorrow and unavailable.
DC 18 Medicine to diagnose, or just offer `lesser restoration` — she accepts if the offer
seems genuine. Fetching Grygum from the lecture for this specifically is also valid play.

> *"You are not what I expected of a half-orc cleric."* — she'll say this to whoever steps up.

**Kalan's alibi.** If the party mentions Kalan by name, she confirms he visited at 10:15pm.
She says it without being prompted. She liked him.

**Daral complication (if he's in the room).** She looks at him for a long moment. *"I need you
to know I didn't know about the book."* She doesn't know what the book was. Daral believes her.

**Insight ladder:**
- DC 12: Grief posture, not guilt posture. She's bracing for accusation, not building a defense.
- DC 16: The covered forearm. She's been hiding it all morning.
- DC 18: When she mentions the Cursed Tower, something in her loosens — relief at truth, not
  tension at a lie.

## SCENE D — Teles Ahvoste (run if time; holds clean to Session 4)

Old. Very old. Thin hair, ink-stained collar, studies the taxonomy of curses. Studies that
overlook the Eastern Ward. He finds everyone's pretensions amusing, including his own. He left
the lecture early; this is his right and he exercised it without apology.

**He was expecting someone to come.** He's been waiting.

**His story (public).** Nothing useful. Old man who sleeps badly and knows too much and is
too tired to help investigators.

**His truth (earned — share something real, or DC 18 Persuasion after warming him up).**

> *"I thought: that is interesting. Sylvira Narath is a tiefling. Tieflings have darkvision.
> Tieflings do not carry lanterns in the dark. So whoever that was — it was not Sylvira."*

He deduced Bookwyrm. He intends to use this. He hasn't decided what he wants from her yet.
He isn't afraid of her. He's entertained by her position.

**He will not give this free.** Two paths:
1. Give him something *genuinely true* — a real vulnerability, something that cost the party
   something. He can tell the difference from a bargaining chip.
2. Convince him Bookwyrm's cover is already coming down. If she's already under scrutiny, his
   leverage is shrinking anyway — he may as well be on the right side.

**Teles on Daral (if present).** *"You're still alive. Interesting."* Pause. *"I did wonder."*
He knows about midnight tears — not because he's guilty, but because he knows everything about
Candlekeep's collection. If pressed: *"The man who is dying of his own gift is rarely the one
who poisoned it."* He's already worked it out.

> If the table is full, Teles keeps to Session 4. He does not expire today.

## SCENE E — Fheminor Scrivenbark, Founder's Court ⭐

She left the lecture early. She always does. She moves through the cloisters cataloguing cracks
in the stone. The party finds her in the Founder's Court with no one watching.

**She doesn't trust Daz.** She watches him the whole scene. Route the meaningful question
through Thorin or Zalthir.

**Her story (public).** Nothing to hide. Met Janussi at 4pm yesterday. Sorry he's dead.
Wishes you well with the investigation.

**Her truth (earned — share something she can verify, or DC 16 Insight reveals she's
holding back).** Janussi told her at their 4pm meeting: *"I am old, and I am tired, and the
keep needs a hand that does not ache. I will name you tomorrow."* She is now the unnamed
successor of a dead Keeper. She is in mortal danger. She has been conducting her own quiet
investigation because if Bookwyrm figures out what Janussi told her, she is next. She does
not say this directly. She will, if the party is the better option to trust.

**The hinge. This is the session's non-negotiable. Last line of the scene:**

> ⭐ *"Bookwyrm was not surprised."*

Close the door. That's it. No editorial. Count to three. Let the table sit with it.

**Daral's push (if she's hedging).** *"Fheminor. Tell them."* Said quietly, still terrified,
still thinking she's the sharpest person in the building. She'll comply or she won't. Either
way the room changes.

## DARAL CLOCK

This is the session's structural tension. It runs under every scene.

He licked the pages in front of them. The working theory is midnight tears. *A poisoner does
not re-read his own pages.* That sentence is both his exoneration and his death clock. The
party saves him by *reasoning*, not a Medicine roll.

Note the dramatic texture: they are acting on a *theory*, not a confirmed identification.
Casting lesser restoration on a guess is different from casting it on a fact. Let that sit in
the room if it comes up — don't smooth it over.

| What triggers the save | How |
|---|---|
| Party reasons through it and acts on the theory | Lesser restoration (Dawnbringer / Grygum fetched from lecture) |
| Party confirmed via Apothecary first, then acts | Same — but they *know* rather than guess |
| Party doesn't connect it, or waits too long | Midnight. Corridor. Face-down, ink-stained thumb at lips. |

**Timing cue.** If the party hasn't moved on the Daral question by Scene D: *"The keep bells
strike eleven."* One sentence. No explanation. Let them feel the window.

## Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can we go watch the lecture?"** Yes. Oval Theatre. Observe who sits where, who watches
Bookwyrm, how A'lai holds himself when the nature-of-dragons question turns personal. The room
is a small evidence map.

**"Where is Kalan?"** Sea Warden's Tower — unless the afternoon window has closed (Scene B).

**"What does the lead chalice mean?"** Someone who knows Candlekeep's spell library used it
to block divination. That is not a visitor passing through. The insider hypothesis goes live.

**"Can we speak with the dead heart?"** No. Roll it, let it fail, count to three. The silence
is the clue, not a soft answer.

**"Is Bookwyrm the killer?"** Not directly — she covered it up. The poisoner was Alkrist
(bronze dragonborn; the party doesn't know this yet). When they ask: she steered the
investigation, she was there that night, her pre-9am alibi is a lie. That's enough to want
her to answer for something. Don't give them more than they've earned.

**"Can Dawnbringer cure the plague?"** Lesser restoration removes disease. Thorin has to *ask*
the sword — the sword doesn't do it automatically. If Thorin offers without prompting: the
sword says yes, warm, no drama.

**"What happened to Grygum's key?"** Grygum passed it before the lecture. Note who took it.
It opens the High Tower from outside in Session 6 — the handoff PC is load-bearing.

## Stage Directions

- **The chalice.** Let the player roll speak with dead. Let it fail. Say nothing for three full
  seconds. Then: *"The heart does not answer."*
- **"I did not kill him. I loved him."** Flat. No tears. Move immediately to the next beat.
  The table makes the moment — you don't have to.
- **"Bookwyrm was not surprised."** Last line. Close the door. Silence. That's the scene.
- **Teles's lantern observation.** Almost to himself. Like he's amused nobody else noticed.
  Offhand. That's what makes it land.
- **Eleven bells.** Mid-scene. One sentence. No drama. Let them do the math.
