## T'sarran scout — intimidated, shrunk, pouched

**Scene:** the Act 1 menacing-presence beat from the Candlekeep Day
One prep (`notes/sessions/candlekeep_day_one.md`) — a T'sarran scout
shadowing the party along the surface route, on Vaelissa T'sarran's
orders to track the party's destination and contacts.

### What happened

- **The intimidation beat.** Grygum placed prayer beads on the
  scout's head. Two factors compounded her terror:
  - Her trained understanding of *what Lolth clerics do to
    prisoners* (drow-canon: ritualized, aimed at breaking devotion
    before death). She projected that template onto Grygum at the
    moment of capture.
  - Grygum's combination of **half-orc heritage and Bahamut
    devotion** broke her threat-assessment frame. The half-orc
    presented as physical threat; the prayer beads said *cleric*
    but of *the dragon god of justice* — her training had no clean
    response for the combination. **It was outside the categories
    she had.** The terror was real and structural, not just
    physical.

- **The debate.** Party discussed handling — release-with-vow,
  killing, creative custody. One of them suggested *shrinking her
  with the magical mushrooms recovered from the Underdark.*

- **The custody.** She was shrunk. She is now **tied up, inside
  the bag of holding the party uses (where Grygum's gear is also
  kept), watched by Glabbagool** — the talkative grey ooze, who
  is in the bag with her.

### Where she is now

- Doll-sized (mushroom-shrunk).
- Bound.
- Inside the bag of holding alongside Grygum's stored gear.
- **The bag is held open** so the air stays fresh. Air-rule is
  resolved at the table — she breathes fine.
- **Glabbagool has positioned himself at the opening** —
  *peeping out with his two googly eyes.* Whoever carries the bag
  is also carrying a faintly visible grey ooze peering out the top
  with quiet curiosity. *The party is unsubtle about this.*
- Glabbagool is her *cellmate and her guard.* He is, by all
  appearances, **enjoying the company** — narrating his surface-
  day impressions to a captive audience while keeping watch
  outward at the same time. He is multitasking.
- Her trained drow categories don't fit Glabbagool any better
  than they fit Grygum. Two destabilizers in a row.

### Implications carrying forward

**What she has heard.** Per the revised T'sarran-scout script in the
prep doc — she is an excellent listener and *her Matron pays for
excellent listening.* From the bag, she has been hearing party
conversation. Assume she has clocked: the destination (Candlekeep),
Daz's investigation interests, Thorin's plans for Dawnbringer,
Grygum's Bahamut research, and any operational discussion conducted
within hearing range. **Releasing her without a binding vow is now
an active leak.**

**Glabbagool's role.** He has had no concept of *captive* before.
He is treating her as *company.* This is its own destabilizer —
a guard who enjoys her presence and narrates the surface to her
doesn't fit any drow custody model. Two angles to track:

- He may be **forming an opinion** about her. Glabbagool's moral
  compass is the party's at one remove; whatever he concludes
  becomes table-canon.
- She may, eventually, **respond to him as a person** rather than
  as a guard. That's a slow-burn beat available to the table.

**Vaelissa's personal affront.** Per the revised script, a House
drow shrunk and pocketed is — in drow culture — *a humiliation
deeper than death.* Vaelissa T'sarran's grievance shifts from
**House-honor** (kill-team loss at the Fungal Cavern) to **personal
affront** (her operative shrunk, pocketed, and watched by a grey
ooze). Her endgame masquerade-scrutiny role at Menzoberranzan now
carries this added weight. *Track this in
`docs/risk_levers.md`'s Social Masquerade Collapse lever when this
content gets promoted.*

**Daz's Yvenne sitting — free disclosure-tick credit.** When Daz
brings this story to Yvenne, she names it explicitly:

> *"You shrunk a Matron's daughter and carried her in a pouch. I
> will not tell you that was wrong. I will tell you that she will
> not forget, and her Matron will not forget, and the shape of
> this debt is now part of what is happening to you."*

This is a **free tick on the disclosure trade** — Daz has *acted,*
the action was significant, Yvenne's read of Vaelissa lands sharper
as a result. Apply at the Yvenne sitting.

**Grygum's terror profile.** Grygum is now a *known terror profile*
among House-aligned drow scouts: a half-orc Bahamut cleric who
intimidates with prayer beads. Word will spread within T'sarran's
operatives first, then more broadly. Future drow encounters with
Grygum should reflect *calibrated caution* — the scouts'
training-frame still has no clean answer for him.

**The "talkative grey ooze guard"** — a memorable cell concept the
party invented at the table. Worth referencing back if the scout
ever escapes or is released, and worth keeping consistent: Glabbagool
is the warden, and he *talks.*

**The conspicuous bag.** The bag of holding is open at the top, and
Glabbagool is *peeping out with his googly eyes.* This is **highly
visible** to anyone the party passes. Implications:

- **Beregost / surface road encounters:** townsfolk will see a
  grey ooze in a bag, riding with the party. Memorable. Notable.
  Conversation starter. *"Is that...?"* Yes, it is.
- **Candlekeep entry:** the Avowed at the gates are *professional
  noticers.* They will ask. Yvenne, Philemon, Vareth, and
  Khell-Vire each may register the bag for their own reasons:
  - **Philemon** in particular — sentient-artifact specialist —
    will be *intensely curious.* A sentient grey ooze in a bag
    of holding is precisely his department, even if his actual
    appointment is for Dawnbringer.
  - **Vareth** may have heard of Glabbagool from the party's
    Underdark accounts at the gate; he's a Stroud-archive
    archivist by accident, but his half-orc-Bahamut work means
    he knows about myconid-adjacent companions.
  - **Yvenne** will note the ooze with neutral interest —
    nothing in her field, but her eye for *what makes a party
    visible* is sharp.
- **Stealth is gone going forward** until the bag is closed or
  Glabbagool is moved elsewhere. The party should know this.

### Open questions for the GM

- How long has she been in the bag at session-current time?
  (Affects how much she has overheard.)
- Does Glabbagool have any *opinion* about her yet?
- Does the party intend to release her, deliver her, or keep her
  indefinitely?
- **Does the party know Glabbagool's googly eyes are visible
  from the outside?** With the bag open and an ooze peeking out,
  this party is conspicuous. Anyone they pass — guards in
  Beregost, Avowed at Candlekeep, fellow travelers on the road —
  *will see it.* Stealth is gone.
- Does Vaelissa learn of her *specific* fate (shrunk + pouched),
  or only of her silence and absence?
