`notes/sessions/candlekeep_day_one.md`). Promote any of them to
`docs/npcs/` after they recur. **All Claude-proposed unless

## Candlekeep officers (canon — from the locations supplement)

These are the named figures who run the keep. Use them when the
party interacts with the institution rather than with a personal
mentor.

### Bookwyrm — First Reader

**Race / role:** dragonborn sage; First Reader of Candlekeep.
**Where:** topmost level of the Reader's Tower (private quarters);
Chapter House for council; Refectory at meal times; **Drakonoikos**
as curator of the dragon library.

**Use at table:** allocates the four PCs to their Avowed scholars
in Act 2. Brief, formal, faintly amused at the volume of donations.
A dragonborn First Reader is the right person to take possession
of the Velkynvelve prisoner journal *("It will go great with my
other editions in drow prison journals")* and to be later quietly
delighted that Vareth has dragged Grygum off — Bookwyrm has been
watching the Stroud Wake controversy as Drakonoikos' curator.

**Recurrence:** the natural face of "Candlekeep itself" if the
party needs an audience above the level of their assigned scholar.

### Janussi — Keeper of Tomes

**Race / role:** Avowed; Keeper of Tomes (Candlekeep's executive
head). One of the two Keepers of Secrets (the other is the
Gatewarden).
**Where:** private quarters atop Keeper's Tower (Miss Hollypocket,
elderly gnome servant, on the floor below); reception chamber and
shrine to Oghma in lower Keeper's Tower; Chapter House for council.
Curator of the **Old Keep**, **House of Mechanus**, and **House
of Alaundo**.
**Texture:** carries one of two keys to the High Tower Library
(the cryptogram + lightning orb vault). Permission required for
any Infernal Fortress summoning.

**Use at table:** offstage authority. If the party tries to
escalate (e.g., demand the Bell Tower antimagic cells for Polly
Pocket, or formally publish demon-lord findings), Janussi's
permission is the gate. Don't put him on screen unless the stakes
warrant it.

### Kalan Strongbranch — Gatewarden

**Race / role:** Avowed; Gatewarden of Candlekeep. The other
Keeper of Secrets.
**Where:** recently relocated his quarters to the upper floor of
the **Sea Warden's Tower** (against Janussi's preference). Daily
research in the **Arcanium**. **Weekly visits to the Chambers of
Lost Lore (sub-Candlekeep) to consult an "otherworldly being."**

**Live hook:** Kalan's Lost Lore consultations are an open canon
seam. Plausible directions to land later — *not this session* —
include: the consulted being is connected to one of Daz's leads
(patron / Vizeran-tier reveal), to Jimjar's true nature, or to
Vaelissa T'sarran's mirror investigation. Treat as **banked
mystery**, available for redirection.

**Use at table:** background presence in the Arcanium during
Daz's research. Recognizes Daz as a drow and visibly does *not*
react — the Gatewarden has seen weirder. If the Polly Pocket
hand-off to the Bell Tower happens, Kalan signs for her.

### Kei Tigersteel — Lorekeeper

**Race / role:** Avowed; Lorekeeper.
**Where:** Oratory pulpit, addressing the Avowed on spiritual
matters.
**Use at table:** Bahamut/Oghma cross-referent for Grygum if he
wants a second theological voice that is *not* Vareth's. Sermon
on the morning of Day Two is a clean way to seed Grygum's faith
beats outside the Stroud frame.

### Sylvira Savikas — Curator, Infernal Fortress

**Race / role:** Avowed; curator of the library on the Abyss and
the Nine Hells.
**Where:** Infernal Fortress (avernium black-alloy library; lava
pool in the central hall; chained tomes; summoning chambers
upstairs).
**Personal complication:** her demonspawn son **Moziqodo** —
*the Beast of Candlekeep* — escaped the Mordenkainen's mansion
she'd trapped him in and now stalks rooftops at night.

**Use at table:** she is the curator the party will face when
they walk in carrying Ilvara's Zuggtmoy-marginalia prayer book,
Asha Vandree's Lolth/Zuggtmoy theological argument, and Pliinki's
Whirlstone journal. Sylvira is the right NPC to *believe them* —
demonic activity is her field. She can:

- formally accept the demon-lord evidence into the Infernal
  Fortress collection
- corroborate Zuggtmoy / Juiblex / Demogorgon facts the party
  is uncertain about
- request (not demand) a controlled summoning chamber session if
  they want to verify something — Janussi's permission required

She is also a **soft channel for Vareth's Bahamut intrusion**:
Vareth has occasionally consulted her on Tiamat-cultist
misappropriations of the Stroud story. Casual collegiality.

**Recurrence value:** the Infernal Fortress and Sylvira are the
natural standing contact for *anything demon-lord shaped* the
party brings back to Candlekeep in future arcs.

### Adeilina — Librarian, Arcanium

**Race / role:** authoritarian high-elf archmage; librarian of
the Arcanium / Hall of Divination.
**Texture:** loves cleanliness and order. Will not let anyone
touch a book — the Arcanium's spectral-mage-hand swarm fetches,
turns pages, and *slaps* the hand of anyone who reaches.

**Use at table:** atmosphere lead for Daz's Yvenne sittings (held
in the Hall of Divination's quieter alcoves). Adeilina runs the
floor; Yvenne is a guest scholar there. The hand-slapping mage
hands are a comedic-tension generator if Daz forgets the rule.

### Pizwog — Librarian, Immortal Chambers

**Race / role:** ancient tortle sage; librarian of the
theology/religion library.
**Texture:** will help anyone with anything — *at 1 mph.* Moves,
talks, and acts at exactly that pace.

**Use at table:** Grygum's first stop when he steps from Vareth's
office into the *theology* library proper to handle the Asha
Vandree manuscript. Pizwog's pacing is the comic counterweight to
Vareth's frantic energy. Ben will love it.

### Inda — Librarian, House of Alaundo

**Race / role:** half-orc sage.
**Texture:** **secretly worships Alaundo as a deity.** Reverential.

**Use at table:** another half-orc Bahamut-adjacent figure for
Grygum's eye to land on — the contrast point is "what does
half-orc devotion look like when the object is a *seer*, not a
god?" Ties Grygum's faith reflection to Ilvara's dying prophecy
and Jimjar's warnings if he wants to consult Alaundo's late
prophecies (the *Echoes of Alaundo* in the Vault of Secrets are
sealed; copies in the gallery are accessible).

### Thava Norixius — Librarian, Drakonoikos

**Race / role:** elderly dragonborn mage; "world's leading dragon
scholar."
**Where:** Drakonoikos (the dragon-anatomy library built inside
Fyrentennimar's hollowed-out carcass).

**Use at table:** Vareth drags Grygum here for Station 9
(Protanther's 400 years and his speech). Thava is the closest
living authority on Protanther-as-character and on the
Stroud-Protanther-Ortho lineage — she has read the Tyranny of
Dragons depositions and has *opinions.* She is a Dragon-school
sympathizer in private, Bahamut-school in public for institutional
reasons.

## Sister Yvenne

**Race / role:** half-drow, Avowed of Candlekeep, Eilistraee
priestess.
**Age:** 60s.
**Field:** Drow exile communities; Eilistraee diaspora; Drow
material culture and political genealogy.
**Wing:** **The Arcanium** (West Inner Ward) — Yvenne is a
guest scholar attached to the Hall of Divination, where she
reviews scrying-pool feeds from Drow exile communities under a
centuries-old contract. Her *sittings* with Daz happen in a
private alcove of the Atheneum (North Inner Ward) for quiet, with
Arcanium consultations as needed.

**Backstory:** raised on the surface by an Eilistraee circle after
her mother fled the collapse of Ched Nasad. Joined Candlekeep two
decades ago. Wears Eilistraee's silver crescent openly; drow
features unhidden; surface-elf accent layered over something older.
Does not perform either side of her heritage.

**Personality:** quiet recognition over pity. Reads more than she
speaks. Does not push. Her professional commitment is to the
*space* she gives a subject — she will not ask Daz directly who he
is, and will let him keep his name even if he never volunteers it.

**For Daz specifically:** the right interlocutor for player
education without forcing character revelation. Operates the
disclosure trade explicitly. Honest about cutout dead-ends. Holds
the bloodline-pattern observation as a gated payoff.

**Recurrence value:** standing offer of an Eilistraee-circle
introduction makes her a permanent contact node. If Daz ever
reaches Stage 4, Yvenne is the most plausible NPC to revisit *not*
to extract intel but to *land what he learned about himself.*

## Brother Khell-Vire

**Race / role:** half-elf, Avowed of Candlekeep, retired Garden of
Shadow monk; shadow-discipline lineage scholar.
**Age:** ~200s.
**Field:** lineage of named shadow-bearers across centuries; the
discipline as inheritance, not technique.
**Wing:** **Echoing Hall** (North Inner Ward, history library
inside the Atheneum complex) — Khell-Vire keeps a small office in
its lower gallery, where the lineage records live. Sittings with
Zalthir take place there (the floor's footstep-echo motif is on
the nose for shadow-discipline work, and Khell-Vire likes it).
The Trial of the Broken Mirror runs in **the Grove** (South Inner
Ward, woodland cemetery + bridges) at dawn.

**Backstory:** left the Garden of Shadow ~80 years ago after
**refusing an order he won't name unprompted.** Not in disgrace —
in *disagreement.* Has spent the decades since cataloging who has
held the discipline, what they did with it, where the lineage
diverges. Wears Avowed gray over a faded shadow-monk under-robe;
the under-robe is no longer black but he has not replaced it.

**Personality:** courteous, observant, **patient.** Speaks rarely.
When he speaks, it lands. Treats Zalthir the way he wishes someone
had treated him at Zalthir's age — with the time to think his
masters did not give him.

**Texture:** tall, thin, hair gone silver, eyes the color of wet
stone.

**For Zalthir specifically:** carries a master's private notebook
recovered from the Garden ~30 years ago. The notebook lists pupils
paired with the *places they were sent to walk.* Zalthir's pairing:
*"the Underdark, until the Bone-King sings."* Three working
hypotheses on what the Bone-King is, all live. Khell-Vire has been
trying to learn for thirty years.

**Standing offer:** correspondence on shadow-discipline questions
and lineage research. *Don't deliver his unspoken affection unless
Gabe earns it through RP.*

## Master Kenshi

**Race / role:** blind half-elf, monk of the **Order of the
Enlightened Shadow,** **Custodian of Martial Lore.**
**Age:** ~90s.
**Field:** scholarly preservation of shadow-discipline forms.
**Has spent fifty years reading techniques he has never used.**
**Wing:** **Sea Warden's Tower** sparring rooms (West Inner
Ward — Watchers' base; armoury of magic weapons; sparring rooms).
The Order of the Enlightened Shadow keeps a chamber here under
guest-tenure with the Watchers. The Trial's Kata of the Void runs
in the central sparring room.

**Backstory:** the Order is a small academic body devoted to the
*scrolls* of shadow-discipline. They preserve. They do not
practice. Kenshi has been their Custodian for thirty years and has
never been hit.

**Personality:** wiry, calm, hears the shape of a technique by ear.
Treats Zalthir as a *Living Heresy* — a discipline-bearer who
succeeds because he has *unlearned* the rigid forms. Horrified and
fascinated.

**For Zalthir specifically:** runs the Trial of the Broken Mirror.
Codifies Zalthir's "corrupted" style on success. Dispenses the
**Severed Path technique** as recognition, not addition.

**Recurrence value:** the Order can become a future contact (Path
A only — they will not pursue Zalthir if he was overtly hostile).

## Master Archivist Philemon

**Race / role:** Avowed monk of Candlekeep, sentient-artifact
specialist.
**Age:** GM picks.
**Field:** psychological preservation and historical cataloging of
sentient artifacts.
**Wing:** **Towers of Wonder** (West Inner Ward — magic items and
wondrous objects). Philemon's cluttered study is on the upper
shelves of one of the spires — visitors reach it by floating disk,
which the Avowed use to browse the wall-to-wall stacks. His
sentience-resonance instruments hover in mid-air with him. The
Quiet Hour beats run **in the Reading Salon** (the permanent
*silence*-spelled lounge in Exaltation) — Philemon books the
salon when he needs Dawnbringer to *not* be able to argue out
loud.

**Backstory:** **clinical-by-default, listening-by-capacity.**
Early in his career he cataloged a sentient item that
*self-terminated rather than be classified.* The failure shaped
his approach — he learned to listen, but his first move is still
instruments. The clinical mode is a defense, not malice.

**Personality:** courteous and structured. Approaches sentient
weapons in third person while their wielder is in the room.
Reaches for instruments before ears. Adapts when demanded — but
the demand has to come.

**For Thorin specifically:** runs the three-phase scene
(clinical → pivot → quiet hour → prescription). Layers Dawnbringer's
trauma into Layer 1 (the captor) and Layer 2 (the grief). Has the
**Vaultmaster Locking Scabbard hand-on-lock observation** as a
sustained character beat — appreciates the scabbard itself
(Dawnbringer likes it as her home; she chose it over the
gem-encrusted showpiece in Blingdenstone), but notices that
Thorin's *hand on the lock* has become a behavioral lever when
Dawnbringer's rhetoric outpaces. The friction is Thorin's *use,*
not the scabbard.

**Recurrence value:** standing correspondent on the rituals' progress
(Paths B and C). Sealed letter banked against the day Dawnbringer
names her wielder (Path C only).

## Brother Vareth

**Race / role:** half-elf, elderly, Avowed of Candlekeep.
**Age:** elderly (GM picks specifics).
**Field:** *quiet specialty* — half-orc clerics of Bahamut.
*Recently invaded* — the Stroud–Protanther dossier, three years and
growing.
**Wing:** **Immortal Chambers** (West Inner Ward — theology /
religion library; tile floor with one deity-symbol per tile;
curator A'lai Aivenmore; librarian Pizwog-the-tortle). Vareth's
office is one of the side-rooms there. The Stroud Wake stations
run here, with two off-site detours: **Drakonoikos** (Station 9 —
Protanther's speech, hosted by Thava Norixius) and **House of
Alaundo** (optional — for Grygum to consult Alaundo's prophecies
through Inda the half-orc librarian).

**Backstory:** spent decades as a quiet half-orc-Bahamut scholar
until three years ago, when a dwarven rogue named Stroud
checkmated a gold dragon and the world tilted around it. His
existing field of study sat directly under the new flood; he has
been **frantically cataloging** ever since. He did not plan to
become the Stroud archivist. The work found him.

**Personality:** *thrilled* — gentle awe at meeting subjects he
has read about. Apologizes when he runs too eagerly. Honest about
not knowing.

**For Grygum specifically:** docent of the **Stroud Wake** —
walks Grygum through the panoramic view of what Stroud
accidentally built across the Realms in three years. Holds **all
three schools** open (Stroud / Dragon / Bahamut, organized by
worldview). Never picks. Has the unsigned sting and has been
trying to identify the handwriting for three years.

**Recurrence value:** standing correspondent on the Stroud
question and the half-orc-Bahamut lineage. Letters of introduction
to the three other living Stroudite half-orc clerics. Holds the
**Gorg'Bahamut folio** with its unfinished sentence.

## Cross-NPC notes

- **Yvenne and Khell-Vire** know each other casually — Yvenne
  passes the Echoing Hall on her way between the Arcanium and the
  Atheneum.
- **Yvenne and Vareth** are colleagues; she has *no professional
  interest* in the Stroud question but knows him. Both have
  offices on the West Inner Ward.
- **Philemon and Vareth** have informally compared notes on
  *belief as a bond* over the years — they meet at meals in the
  Refectory.
- **Sylvira Savikas (Infernal Fortress) and Vareth** have
  collaborated on Tiamat-cultist misappropriations of Stroud.
  Vareth will introduce Grygum to her if the demon-lord evidence
  comes up — see "Demon-lord evidence handoff" beat in
  `notes/sessions/candlekeep_day_one.md`.
- **Bookwyrm (First Reader)** is also curator of Drakonoikos.
  When Vareth takes Grygum to Drakonoikos for Station 9, the First
  Reader may briefly appear — she has been watching the Stroud
  Wake controversy from the inside.
- **Kalan Strongbranch (Gatewarden)** is a regular face in the
  Arcanium during Daz's research. Recognizes Daz as drow and does
  not react.
- **Aderyn Selvel** (an earlier-draft NPC who got folded into
  Philemon's role) is mentioned in some sections as a passing
  presence in the upper gallery of the Trial training hall and
  through Yvenne's office. Treat her as **a peripheral colleague
  of Yvenne and Philemon** — not a full NPC, just a name to use
  for atmospheric drift-bys.
- **The First Reader** is **Bookwyrm**, a dragonborn sage —
  brief Act 2 appearance to allocate PCs to Avowed.
