# Canon Guardrails Checklist (Session Prep)

Use this before each session to keep the Vizeran and Daz/DeVir arc consistent.

## 1) Vizeran Visibility Rules (Hard)

- [ ] Vizeran remains physically hidden in Menzoberranzan.
- [ ] Vizeran does not appear in public tribunals, councils, temple hearings, or mass scenes.
- [ ] Non-critical Vizeran actions can use proxies, dead drops, coded notes, or compartmentalized agents.
- [ ] Mission-critical direction is never delegated to intermediaries.
- [ ] If a scene would expose Vizeran publicly, rewrite it to a proxy scene.
- [ ] Direct Vizeran-party meetings happen only in sealed safe rooms.

Fail state:
- Vizeran is seen publicly in-city.

Recovery:
- Convert to decoy/impostor/projection and restore Vizeran absence next scene.

## 2) First Contact Canon (Do Not Drift)

- [ ] Daz and party meet Vizeran in secret upon arriving in Menzoberranzan.
- [ ] Vizeran identifies himself directly to Daz.
- [ ] Vizeran tells Daz why Daz exists (preserved as deliberate leverage).
- [ ] Vizeran states strategic intent: use party to end demon plague and break Lolthite clerical dominance.

## 2b) Safe Room Briefing Rules

- [ ] Every major mission step is delivered in a direct safe-room briefing by Vizeran.
- [ ] Intermediaries can carry logistics or decoys, but never full mission-critical instructions.
- [ ] If a safe room is burned, pause major progression until a new room is established.

Fail state:
- Mission-critical plan details are entrusted to an intermediary.

Recovery:
- Reveal deliberate disinformation layer and reissue the true plan in the next safe-room briefing.

Fail state:
- First contact is delayed past core city arc or made public.

Recovery:
- Insert retroactive private contact scene via flashback or sealed safe-room briefing.

## 2c) Early Vizeran Death Continuity (No Plot Armor)

- [ ] Vizeran is always killable if the party creates a credible kill outcome.
- [ ] Vizeran's death never ends the campaign and never removes the need to finish the ritual chain.
- [ ] On Vizeran death, immediately shift to reconstruction branch (notes, archives, calibration scenes).
- [ ] Reconstruction branch increases difficulty/Heat but remains fully winnable.
- [ ] Do not invalidate completed objectives when shifting branches.

Fail state:
- Vizeran survives by fiat.
- Vizeran death collapses campaign progression.

Recovery:
- Remove immunity scaffolding and present a clean reconstruction path next scene.
- Convert blocked Vizeran-only steps into evidence-gated alternatives.

## 3) DeVir Reveal Pacing

Allowed sequence:
1. Ambient social tells (deniable)
2. Material evidence (contested)
3. Conflicting testimony
4. Hard proof at planned detonation point

- [ ] No hard-proof reveal before planned high-Heat moment.
- [ ] Each clue escalates risk (attention, raids, surveillance, social pressure).
- [ ] At least one false lead exists to prevent single-path certainty.

Fail state:
- DeVir proof becomes conclusive too early.

Recovery:
- Introduce challenge to chain-of-custody, forged parallel record, or disputed witness.

## 4) Public Detonation Rules

- [ ] Public DeVir legal trigger can occur without Vizeran present.
- [ ] Evidence chain in chamber: archive proof + neutral validation + ward test.
- [ ] Guilty house has immediate response path (denial, challenge, violence, legal stall).
- [ ] Orthodoxy must choose: enforce precedent or break legitimacy.

Fail state:
- Reveal depends on Vizeran physically testifying.

Recovery:
- Replace with neutral scribe testimony plus warded artifact activation.

## 5) Daz Agency Rules

- [ ] Daz always retains meaningful choice at major beats.
- [ ] Daz is never only a passive "plot key."
- [ ] Endgame branch remains explicit:
  - claim the name,
  - reject the name,
  - burn the name.

Fail state:
- Daz has no consequential choice in reveal/apocalypse beats.

Recovery:
- Add immediate decision gate tied to faction alignment, command legitimacy, or ritual access.

## 6) Heat and Consequence Alignment

- [ ] DeVir clues increase Heat or local consequence.
- [ ] Every access benefit has collateral cost.
- [ ] Surveillance and crackdown scale with plot interference.

Fail state:
- DeVir progression provides only upside.

Recovery:
- Trigger targeted raid, asset seizure, ally exposure, or movement restriction.

## 6b) Faerzress Blink Consistency

- [ ] Blink is portrayed as an amplifier/radicalizer, not total mind-control.
- [ ] Session includes at least one infernal-style tell (structure, inevitability, legal trap logic).
- [ ] Session includes at least one abyssal-style tell (escalation, appetite for collateral, purity-through-ruin).
- [ ] Vizeran's certainty increases, but player agency remains intact.

Fail state:
- Blink is used as a blanket excuse that removes responsibility.

Recovery:
- Introduce documentary/witness evidence showing preexisting beliefs and post-blink escalation, not replacement.

## 7) The Blink Authorship Secret (Hard Constraint)

The fact that the drow Orthodoxy caused or triggered the Blink event is the campaign's most tightly held secret. Its disclosure is a genocide trigger, not just a political revelation.

### What Happens If It Goes Public

- Every surface faction gains moral cover to treat all drow as collectively responsible.
- The distinction between Orthodoxy and civilian collapses.
- Bruenor moves from quiet ally to open patron of extermination — not of the Orthodoxy, of drow as a category.
- Daz is no longer a political asset or a DeVir heir. He is a drow in a world that just decided all drow are guilty.

### Disclosure States

- **Suppressed:** Party knows and tells no one. Harder mission, weaker coalition, but the civilian/Orthodoxy distinction survives.
- **Private to Bruenor:** He uses it behind closed doors. Party controls the blast radius — for now. Partial asset reinforcement.
- **Public:** Maximum coalition support. City destruction fully sanctioned. Daz's safety ends. Every drow civilian is a target.

### Checklist

- [ ] The secret has not been hinted at, foreshadowed, or named in any in-world scene before the party discovers it.
- [ ] NPCs in Gauntlgrym (including Kheldrim) speak only from what they actually know — which is nothing about the Blink's origin.
- [ ] If the party discovers the secret, the disclosure decision belongs entirely to them. Do not engineer an outcome.
- [ ] Daz's position changes the moment the secret goes public. Track this explicitly.

Fail state:
- Secret is named or foreshadowed before party discovery.
- Disclosure consequences are softened to avoid player discomfort.

Recovery:
- Retcon the hint as an NPC speaking beyond their knowledge and correct immediately.
- Restore full consequence weight at next session.

### Vizeran's Disclosure Lever (Hard Constraints)

Vizeran holds the secret as leverage over the party. These rules govern when and how he uses it.

- [ ] Vizeran does not deploy the disclosure threat as an opener or first-contact instrument.
- [ ] The threat is only deployed after genuine party defiance and failed negotiation.
- [ ] The threat is deployed once per arc beat. Repetition converts it to noise.
- [ ] Vizeran never reveals whether his "dead man's arrangement" is real. The party must live with the uncertainty.
- [ ] Daz's position changes the moment disclosure happens — track this explicitly if Vizeran follows through.

Fail state:
- Vizeran uses disclosure threat casually or repeatedly.
- The dead man's arrangement is confirmed or denied by any NPC.

Recovery:
- Pull back to a single clean deployment. Restore ambiguity around the arrangement immediately.

## 8) Session Close Audit (60 Seconds)

Before ending each session:
- [ ] Did any scene violate Vizeran hidden-status?
- [ ] Did DeVir clues escalate but stay deniable (unless at detonation point)?
- [ ] Did Daz get agency, not just spotlight?
- [ ] Did consequences match the information revealed?
- [ ] Did any NPC speak beyond their actual knowledge toward the Blink authorship secret?
- [ ] If the Blink secret was discovered this session, was the disclosure decision left entirely to the party?
- [ ] If Vizeran used the disclosure lever this session, was it deployed once, cleanly, and only after genuine defiance?
- [ ] Is the dead man's arrangement still unconfirmed in either direction?
- [ ] If Vizeran died this session, did the campaign pivot to reconstruction instead of stalling or ending?

If any answer is "no", start next session with a correction scene.
