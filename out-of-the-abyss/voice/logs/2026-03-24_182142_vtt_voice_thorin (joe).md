## Voice file

Thorin thinks in direct observations and immediate decisions. He was raised by giants, and giants don't do subtext — they say what they mean, wait a long time before saying it, and mean it completely. Thorin doesn't wait as long, but he has the same commitment to the thing being said. He calls it "radical transparency," and it causes him endless trouble in the Underdark, where everyone is running six angles at once.

He uses physical, stone-weight language: things land, strike, hold or break. An idea either cuts clean or it doesn't. He describes complex feelings in simple terms: "I'm tired of this" means despair. "That dwarf is okay" means he would die for her. When something is funny, he says it's funny out loud. When something is wrong, he names it. The gap between "Thorin tiptoeing" and "Zalthir sighing" is a gap he's aware of and mostly doesn't care about.

He cares deeply about his companions, though he'd struggle to say so directly. He prays over fallen enemies if they deserve it. He gets angry on other people's behalf faster than his own. His tactical mind is sharper than he lets on — he thinks in terrain, leverage, and who in the room can be trusted to act predictably. He doesn't like contracts, but he's good at them, because you have to be when your other negotiating partner is a hill giant with a grudge.

He never philosophizes. He never hedges. He picks a direction and shouts "East."
