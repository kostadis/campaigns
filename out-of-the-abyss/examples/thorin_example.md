# Thorin — The Stone Giant, and a Name

The Stone Giant had been sitting on the rock when we arrived, and he was still sitting on it when we started talking. That's the thing about Stone Giants. They don't rush. The Ordning says their place in the world is to look at it, and they take that seriously. Hgraam had been looking at that particular piece of cavern wall for what felt like years before I said anything, and when I spoke, I started with what you're supposed to start with: condolences, spoken in Giant, because that's what you owe a teacher whose student you couldn't save.

He accepted it the way Stone Giants accept everything — with a pause so long you start wondering if you've said something wrong.

Then he said, *"The rock itself cries in pain and horror, and a madness creeps from the blackest depths."*

Giants talk like this when they're telling you something important. You have to learn to unfold it. What he was saying was that the madness we'd seen wasn't local — not just Gracklstugh, not just the Kuo-Toans, not just the Stone Giant who came through the gate screaming. It was the Underdark itself. Something down there was doing it on purpose.

I thought about the Faerzess blinking. I thought about the Spectator in the spider caves. I thought about the manta rays. I said none of this to Hgraam because he didn't need me to. He knew.

The Duergar standing next to him gave me a look when I asked about the tunnels beneath the city. The Giants don't give those looks. That's one of the things I like about them. They tell you what they think. They just take a while to finish thinking.

We took the stonespeaker crystal and left.

---

We were standing outside the caves when it happened. Thorin — that's me — had been quiet for a while, which is not my natural state, and something was building up. We had the egg. We had the papers. We'd survived the derro and the assassins and the trapper and the dragon and the dragon's handler. We were the Ember Vanguard and nobody had said so yet.

"We need a name," I said.

Zalthir looked at me. He looks at me sometimes like I've just knocked over a shelf that he had carefully organized in his mind and the pieces are falling and he's watching them fall.

"The Second Faction," said Grygum, which was wrong, and I said so.

Daz said, "The Ember Vanguard."

Nobody argued. Even Zalthir stopped looking pained.

I raised my fist. "The Ember Vanguard. Onwards."

Grygum groaned. Zalthir was already walking. Daz was smiling in that way he does when he's decided something has worked out according to a plan he had the whole time.

It doesn't matter. We had a name. In the War of the Giants, you fight better when you know what to call the people beside you. You fight better when you're something instead of just a group that survived.

The Ember Vanguard. We are the Ember Vanguard.

Onwards.
