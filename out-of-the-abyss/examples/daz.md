## From chapter_01_exploring_the_prison.md: Velkynvelve prison — Daz on the chamber-pot detail, the spore link, and Buppido's "divine plan"

### Daz


Daz and his crew collect the chamber pots from each tower. Guldur explains that they collect the filth from each tower in a bucket and then take the bucket to the lake, where they dispose of it and clean the filth. 


Their first stop is at the cave to the north, which they notice is a quaggoth barracks. Peeking in, Daz notes one of them resting. 


The next stop is the tower of the high priestess. There, Daz notes that Thorin has a better job.


Finally, he arrives at the winch. Goldur informs Daz that he will be down before they can run if they try any funny business. Daz notices Zalrith and thinks the monk will have some unpleasant fun cleaning up after he comes up. 


Daz, Stool, and Serith walk to the edge of the lake and notice that it is pretty clean, which is odd given the amount of filth being deposited. They look around and see nothing. 


Later that day, Daz is back in the prison cell, surrounded by his fellow prisoners. Jimjar is making more bets. The stool is happy to be talking to everyone and has spread his spores so that everyone in the prison cell is in a group mind. 


Daz notes that this allows them to coordinate any plans without being discovered. 


Topsy and Turvy are very excited about Gyrgum's cooking. Even Suushar seems excited, although he cautions the orc, "If you were to show this cooking to my people, they might imagine a god like you." 


It's then that Buppido speaks, or instead projects his mind, "The Divine plan says we must collaborate and share information." 


Gyrgum has a peculiar expression as Buppido says the word divine. Later, Daz learns from the cleric that Buppido thinks he is some kind of god, or he is a god. 


After sharing the information, Buppido says, "The divine plan requires that you create a distraction while I escape."


Thorin corrects him, "We escape?" 


Buppido looks surprised, "We?"


Thorin, who also picked up on the derro's insanity, says, "Well, how else will we protect you so that you can fulfill the divine plan?" 


The derro stares at Gyrgum and says, "You are quite right. You have pledged your swords and your spells! And your lives." 


Daz thinks, "At least we have that." 


Daz asks, "What is the plan?" 


Bupiddo, like any mysterious god or charlatan masquerading as a god, would say, "In due time."

## From chapter_12_flight.md: Darklake — Daz calls rocks from the bow

### Daz 


Standing on the front of the boat, Daz looks into the darkness. His eyes can see the water and the cave above him. 


A slight current pulls the boat, and the rowers are somewhat relaxed. 
Daz almost misses the rocks that stand out as stone teeth. He shouts.


The boat crew looks at him as he shouts, "Rocks! We need to dodge these rocks." 


Everyone is confused and starts shouting. Daz realizes he needs to tell everyone what to do: "On my say, heave port or starboard!" 


The boat is heading straight for the first rock, and he shouts, "Port!" The ship turns, and he notices another rock, "Starboard!" The crew moves across the boat again. Daz is about to breathe a sigh of relief when he sees another pair of rocks. He must get between them, so he shouts, "Port! Starboard!" to line up the boat through the space. 


This goes on for a few more seconds, and then the rocks are behind him. 


He looks carefully for any sign of new rocks, then relaxes slightly.

## From chapter_14_greengrass_day.md: Arrival at Gracklstugh — Daz measures the Duergar city against the Drow cities he knows

### Daz


The long journey through the dark waters of the Underdark has led him here to the metropolis of the Duergar in this part of the Underdark. 


Over the past few days, Hemeth has said a lot about his home, which sounds unpleasant. It's a strictly regimented, xenophobic city. Non-Duergars are only permitted to move freely in the Darklake district. Within the city, everyone obeys the laws. The Duergar can become invisible at will; you never know who is watching. And finally, it's a city of slavers. 


And yet, despite everything Hemeth said, Daz is unprepared for what he sees. The city is so unlike the Drow cities Daz has heard of. The Duergar prize order and work over art and beauty. There is none of the opulence and refinement. Instead, the buildings are plain and functional. Whereas the Drow would have decorated their homes with etchings and art, the walls of the Duergar homes are plain. 


And the ambiance is just that of work. The sound of hammers warping metals, the stench and heat, and the light of the smelters make all of Gracklstugh appear as one large work camp. But the acrid smoke makes breathing hard and burns through his sense of smell, which he will remember most from his time here. 


Gyrgum asks, "So what are we doing here?" 


Daz looks at him and is about to answer when Jimjar whispers, "Buy equipment so we can survive more easily. Get information on how to get out of the Underdark." 


Sethir mutters, "We plan to get equipment and then go to the Lost Tomb of Khem, where we will find treasure!" 


Daz notices that Grygum is satisfied. 


Daz remembers what Hemeth had said about the Blade Bazaar: 


"This marketplace is named after the most abundant goods the duergar offer, but the shops here sell almost everything available in the city, along with stalls set up by visiting merchants. The din of people arguing, mainly in Dwarvish, nearly drowns out the hammering coming from the city's forges, and the crowds here offer a good chance to slip away from pursuers."


"To the Blade Bazaar," he says out loud.

## From chapter_15_wanna_bet_on_who_dies_first.md: Darklake Brewery — Daz questions the dragon arrangement and listens to the halfling outsider

## Daz


The story of this dragon intrigues Daz. 


"How does a dragon end up turning into a lighter? What's in it for him? Red dragons are not exactly known for their willingness to serve. They want to build a hoard or something. A dragon that flies around lighting fires is odd." 


His friends look at him, and he looks back. 


"Well, we should find out what we can do before we help him. Perhaps we can go to the brewery?" 


And so, from just outside the Blade Bazaar, Daz and his odd collection of travelers arrive at the Darklake Brewery.


The Brewery is closed to non-Duergar, and Daz is disappointed until Grygum, who sneaks a quick peek, says that inside is a pretty dour place where people talk only in hushed whispers. 


Just outside is a Drow and Halfing, and they are drinking beer. Daz and Grygum approach them. 


The halfling looks at Grygum, "You must be new."


"I'm just glad you see an orc and don't run off scared.'


The halfling laughs, "I am in the Underdark. I see so many scary things. You are the least scary thing I have seen this week." He pauses, "You won't turn invisible?" 


He laughs and, after being introduced, asks, "You haven't gotten in trouble with the law?" He looks at them and says, "Of course not; otherwise, you would be in the Overlake Hold and not hear talking to me." 


"Say, you wouldn't be the people who took down the crazed giant?" 


Daz acknowledges that. 


"Good for you." In a conspiratorial tone, he says, "Did any of the factions approach you for a job? You would be crazy to join them, and they are trying to kill each other. It all began with the Deepking's madness." 


Daz describes the madness of the merchants, and the halfling shrugs, "Yes, that kind of madness. However, in Deepking's case, the laws are more arbitrary. We've always had laws in Gracklstugh, but they made sense. Now, they don't. It's as if he is detached from reality."


The halfling again whispers, "You must have met the Keepers of the Flame? They are in a pickle, what with the Gray Ghosts and the Crown."


"How do you know so much?"


"I'm an outsider. And what do I know? Not very much. And as long as I stay an outsider, no one considers me a threat. And I can share what I know with those not in the game. And you are not part of the game, nor am I." He pauses, searching for a phrase, "What is that expression? It's -- Not relevant to the game." 


Daz wonders, "Can you come with us to the meeting with the Keepers of the Flame?" 


"No, I won't. That might make me appear like a player in the game. I like to watch. I would avoid such encounters and actions. Although, it might be impossible to stay alive, anyways." 


At that, Daz asks about the dragon. 


"Yes, the Red Dragon. The city has had one for ages. The red dragon keeps the stones hot enough for the furnaces to burn. Are you wondering about its horde? The keepers of the flame give him a horde, and they pamper him. The problem, and you're right to wonder, is that Red Dragons dislike playing second fiddle. And so, at some point, the Dragon discovers it's not in charge. Then, the Keepers of the Flame have to kill the dragon. There is also the problem of the dragon becoming more powerful over time. So the Keepers must kill and replace the dragon with a new one." 


Daz is about to ask why but remembers how Dragon eggs can be purchased and hatched. 


At this point, the Drow sitting next to the Halfling splutters, "Look, friend, I am from Menzoberanzan. The backstabbing and politicking are nothing compared to the Duergar. I mean, we will do it with style and finesse. Here, it's just slaughter. No torture, no speeches, no art, and I mean no style. It was just a brutal struggle. They just engage in violence for violence's sake. It's disgraceful if you ask me." 


At this point, Daz wonders if they should interview the various factions to find out which one they should join or whether they should join any of them.
