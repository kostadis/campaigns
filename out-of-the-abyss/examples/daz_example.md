# Daz — The Rocks, and What I Was Thinking in Gracklstugh

I had been composing this entry in my head since the sandbar.

*The Darklake is a network of passages, and some of those passages have ceilings low enough to require crouching. The others have the kind of darkness that makes you feel like the cave is breathing. I have been in worse places. I have been in Menzoberranzan.*

The rocks appeared without warning, which is the only way rocks appear. I was at the bow, which is where I'd placed myself because the alternative was sitting in the middle of the boat listening to Jimjar explain the odds on whether the fungus on the eastern wall was edible. I prefer the dark. The dark is quiet.

When my eyes found the first stone tooth rising from the water, I had perhaps two seconds to decide between freezing and shouting. I shouted. "Rocks. Port." The crew looked at me with the specific expression of people who have not yet processed the word "port" but understand the word "rocks" and are becoming alarmed about the gap between those two things.

"Port. Now."

The boat moved. We cleared it. I found the next one. "Starboard." Then another pair I had to thread between — "Port. Starboard." — which is the kind of instruction that sounds insane until the hull doesn't scrape stone and then it sounds like navigation.

When we cleared the last one, no one said anything for a moment. Thorin hit me on the back. I accepted this.

---

*Now I am sitting outside Gartokkar's chambers composing the next section.*

*Let me consider our employers and what they each want:*

*Errde of the Stone Guard believes the Council of Savants is corrupting the Deepking. She wants proof. She also wants her family's killers named, though she will not say this directly because to say it directly would be to admit the killers exist, and to admit the killers exist would be to admit the city she guards has an assassination guild operating inside it.*

*Gartokkar of the Keepers of the Flame wants the Grey Ghosts destroyed and the egg retrieved. He wants the Council of Savants exposed so that the Deepking will permit a war against the Derro. I do not intend to give him this. I have seen what happens when powerful factions get the evidence they've been collecting. I have seen it in Menzoberranzan. I have seen it twice.*

*Themberchaud wants to know what everyone is hiding from him. He is a red dragon who has been told his entire life that he is in charge, which means the moment he understands that he isn't, everything in this city becomes leverage.*

*The path through this is not to pick a side. The path through this is to give each faction exactly what they need to believe they have won, and to be three tunnels east of here when they compare notes.*

I put away the journal. Grygum was walking toward me with the expression he wears when he has had what he calls a divine inspiration and what I call a plan that will require me to improvise.

"I have been taking notes," he said.

"I know," I said.

"So have I," he said.

We went to see Gartokkar.
