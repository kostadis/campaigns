## From chapter_01_exploring_the_prison.md: Thorin negotiates the escape with Jorlan

Sethir says, "That's Jorlan. He used to be the number one, but after he got hit with acid, his face was disfigured and he lost a finger, Ilvara's dropped him, and now Shoor is number one." 


Jorlan turns to Thorin, "If I made it possible for you to escape, would you try?" 


Thorin connected to his friends via the spores and asked, "How do we know that he isn't here to entrap us?" 


Prince Derendeil explains, "Well we know that there is no abolitionist movement amongst those who worship Lloth. It has to be for his benefit." 


Jorlan senses the hesitation, "I don't care if you succeed. I just want you to try. It will make Shoor look ridiculous, and make Ilvara realize what a terrible mistake she's made. And if you do make a mistake, she'll be embarrassed and need my help to find you and realize what a mistake she made." 
Thorin is satisfied. "What's the plan?" 


Jorlan smiles, "I can sense you don't trust me. You are wise not to trust me." He bends closer, "I will leave the door to the prison open, create a distraction. The path to the armory will be unguarded. Grab the weapons and flee." 


"Where to and how?" 


"Must I explain everything to you? Use the waterfall to slide down, or jump into the spiderwebs and then jump into the pools of water." 


"And then where?" 


Buppido smiles, "To Gracklestugh, my homeland!" 


Stool objects, "No to the great circle! My people!" 


Topsy says, "We should go to Blindengstone. I know there is a way out of the Underdark from there." 


Suushar objects, "Yes, but the direct path is through Menzoberranzan. We will have to go to Darklake first, and we must visit my people to share the enlightenment! From there, the path is good." 


Jimjar adds, "Who wants to bet on which path the new guys will want to go? Will they go with the myconid kid? The enlightened kuo-toan? The Destiny Weilding Derro? Or trust the two deep gnomes?" 


Zulthir asks, "We have no money; how do we pay our debts?" 


"Oh, don't worry, I have perfect recall. Just make your wager, and when you have money, I'll collect." 


 Sarith nods. 


Zulthir says, "You seem to be confident we will escape and live." 


"Wanna bet?" 


Gyrgum asks, "Where will we find safety?" 


Eldeth laughs, "Safety? This is the Underdark.  There is not enough resources or space for anyone. Survival depends on your wits. People tend to distrust outsiders and view them as a resource to be used." 
Thorin thinks for a moment, "I know where our stuff is. It's in the chest in Ilvara's room. The Drow leader probably keeps all the loot for herself. And if any other Drow had our stuff, they would taunt us." 


The dwarf turns to the Drow and says, "Your deal is accepted. When?" 


"After a long rest, before you go on your assigned tasks."

## From chapter_02_stealing_weapons.md: The armory revolt — Thorin charges Imbros, falls, is healed, fights on

Thorin rushes into the small chamber, climbing over a sprawled Zalthir and Daz. The chamber has two Drow guards and one of the more elite Drow male fighters. It contains a zurkhwood table and three chairs, a smaller side table, and spider-silk webbing set with hooks for hanging equipment. The table is set with the Drow game Sava. 


He tries to overpower the elite warrior, whom Serith identified as Imbros but fails.


Imbros is, in turn, amused by the turn of events and is tempted to kill Thorin but chooses not. A prisoner revolt is not something new, and killing prisoners is a very costly business. So he tries to subdue the dwarf and knock him unconscious. 


Zalthir stands up and tries to shove Imbros as well, and Imbros, trained in Menzoberranzan from a very young age, deftly dodges the attack. Or so Imbros would choose to believe. Zalthir, who has spent several days in captivity, tried to go to for a classic opening move but forgot to position his hand, and instead of pushing Imbros, he caressed him. 
The rest of the prisoners, led by Bupido, take advantage of the distraction, rush into the chamber, and scramble into the armory. The Drow warriors are unable to react. 


Prince Derendil declares, "I am already armed in this hideous form, and it would bring me great joy to kill this drow." He then charges at Imbros. 


Gyrgum decides that an armed cleric is better than a disarmed cleric and follows his fellow prisoners to the armory.


Imbros realizes that this revolt has become much more dangerous. He strikes Thorin, who falls, bleeding and unconscious. 


Daz focuses his mind on Imbros and speaks the incantation of Mind Sliver, but the Drow is unaffected. 


Seeing Imbros boxed in by the Prince, Zalthir tries to strike one of the two drow guards with his unarmed fists. His fists land ineffectively. 


Just then, an armed Serith comes down and attacks the same guard that Zalthir flailed at. As the Serith strikes his erstwhile comrade, he curses him in a unique way that makes the Drow curse each other. 


Then, from the opening in the roof where the armory is, Eldeth, Bupido, Shuushar, Jimjar, Topsy, and Turvy attack using hand-held crossbows. Eldeth and Topsy hit the remaining Drow guard. Ront, who prefers a straight-up fight, squeezes into the fight and strikes at the guard, killing him. Suushar aims at Imbros and hits. Buppido and Jimjar miss. 


"You should have taken that bet," everyone hears Jimjar say. The Deep Gnome has mastered Spool's telepathy and forces everyone to listen to him. 


Grygum descends from the armory and walks towards Thorin. Grygum stands over the dwarf, and then, with a moment's incantation and a gentle touch, bloodless flesh becomes rosy, gashes close, and broken bones knit instantly with a rush of magical healing. Flush with renewed vigor, the formerly injured Thorin is ready to face the world once more.


Daz has recovered from the disastrous opening gambit and remembers how magic missiles work. It's not one dart, but three darts. 


Imbros reels from the magical attack.  


Realizing that fighting unarmed against Imbros is foolhardy, Thorin slips out of range of the Drow, climbs upstairs, and puts on some armor.

## From chapter_14_greengrass_day.md: Greengrass memory — Thorin remembers a festival from his giant upbringing

### Thorin


Somehow, Thorin remembers that today is Greengrass. 


The festival of Greengrass was a tradition of the farming communities near the giants he lived with. 


The wealthy farmers would bring flowers to the poorer farmers in a peculiar spring festival to encourage the gods to usher in the summer.


The giants always found the festival peculiar. It was a strange obsession of the little people. 


But after the end of the War of the Giants, King Hekaton had decreed peace with the little people, and so peace it was. 


His reverie is interrupted when Zalthir shouts - "rocks ahead." 


He looks a bit further and sees a boat. A boat that is abandoned.


Daz mutters, "We could use the oars!" 


Thorin agrees and is about to join the war when Zalthir stops him. "Isn't it a bit ominous?" 


Thorin smiles; ominous sounds fun.


Daz shouts, "You have a point. They probably fell into the water."

## From chapter_15_wanna_bet_on_who_dies_first.md: The mad stone giant — Thorin tries to save him, then talks with Dorhun

### Thorin


Although Thorin is unfamiliar with the patterns of behavior of the Duergar, the madness of a stone giant is unheard of. The Stone Giants don't move. They are taciturn to a fault. And so he needs to learn more. 
He turns to one of the Duergar, "Are there more crazy people about?" 


The Duergar stares at him, confused. "Crazy?" 


Thorin remembers that the Duergars are literal people but is uncertain how to continue. 


The guard sensing that asks, "Someone who does not follow orders and engages in anti-social behavior, but does it in a way that will bring them no profit and certain doom?" 


Thorin breathes a sigh of relief, "Yes." 


The Duergar turns to the others on his patrol, briefly discusses, and says, "We have noticed that there have been significantly more incidents of this kind of behavior. As a result, the work of the militias has increased." There is a pause, "And we know of no underlying cause. Although a clan leader may have information they have chosen not to share with us." 


Before Thorin can ask a follow-up question, another Stone Giant appears, flanked by Duergar, armed with curious stone weapons. 


The Stone Giant stares at Thorin, whose body and blade show that he fought and perhaps killed the two-headed giant. Thorin remembers that Stone Giants are not talkative and may take a long time before they initiate a conversation as they ponder all possible things.  He begins the discussion by asking about some stone giants he has heard of. 


The Stone Giant doesn't smile, "No. But then again, I don't know many. Who are you?" 


And Thorin explains his curious relationship with giants and how he got here. 


The Stone Giant pauses, then speaks, "I am Dorhun, and this is Rihaud. We were both apprentices to Stonespeaker Hgraam. Did you kill him?" 


Thorin explains how he tried not to kill but subdue him and that it was the Duergar. The Stone Giant confirms his story and sighs again. 


"We have qualified immunity if a Stone Giant is rampaging through our city," one explains. 


The Stone Giant sighs. 


Then he says, "I ask you to visit me at Cairngorm Cavern before you leave the city." Turning to the Duergar with the stone weapons, he says, "Stone Guards, you are to let them through." 


The dwarf watches how the Duergar gives the Stone Giant the briefest of scowls, showing how much they don't want to give this non-Duergar permission to visit their city.
