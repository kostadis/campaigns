## From chapter_01_exploring_the_prison.md: Velkynvelve — Zalthir surveys the outpost and pockets a list of names

### Zalthir


The first thing that Zalthir noticed was that the Drow outpost was 100' in the sky. A direct drop would be suicidal. Perhaps if we has a more powerful monk, he could fall safely, but he is not. 


He looks a little bit more carefully and notices a spider web. Below the spider's web, he notices a pool of water. 


That looks more promising. 


Across from his prison cell is a stalactite. There is a door, and two very bored Drow are past the door. 


He could easily sneak past them, "But to where?" 


Jimjar, next to him, mutters, "Wanna bet on who would die first when we splat on the ground?" 


As he crosses the bridge near the waterfall, he watches the "Prince," Ront, and Thorin filling buckets of water. 


Jimjar turns to anyone, "Wanna get some action on how many buckets will be filled?" 


They march on until they see the basket. 


Attached to the edge of the barracks platform is a winch-and-basket device consisting of a giant swinging arm that carries a thin cord of strong spider silk. The cord runs through several pulleys from a hand-cranked horizontal spool to a heavy woven basket suspended at the end. The basket is kept up on the platform except when it is in use.


Balok shouts, "Stop." 


He turns to his new slaves and points to the apparatus: "You will clean and unload. If you steal anything, then there will be consequences." 


Jimjar again tries to make a bet, "Wanna bet on how many people will come up? How many will come down?" 


The other prisoners, familiar with the chores, pick up the rags and clean the basket. 


Two quaggoth attendants remain by the lift, waiting for a signal from below to lower the basket. 


As the four prisoners enter the basket, Balok pulls a stopper, and the basket flies over the chasm. 


The two quahogs let it drop. The other prisoners are startled but seem to have been expecting this. 


Balok is laughing. 


Jimjar shouts, "Wanna get in on some juicy action on how many times they'll do this?" 


As the basket bounces, Zalthir notices a piece of paper with some names of people who owe a favor to the person who has the paper. It's unclear where, which city, what favor, and why. But he pockets the paper. 


He notices that Elspeth sees him pocket the paper. He notices that she finds a comb. She pockets the comb. He has little use for combs. 


As the basket comes up, he notices Grygum entering a cave.

## From chapter_04_pursuit.md: Drow pursuit — Zalthir invokes the teaching of Korrh Erann and chooses to run

Zalthir notices that Sarith is not under the rocks and thinks, "We have not lost the guide." He sees Thorin reach for Grygum and thinks, "We need the healer." 


He looks at Daz, "If I don't get him  out of there, he will die." He braces against some rocks and shoves them out of the way. Daz is injured and unconcious but alive.


Stool is relieved to see that Gyrgum is alive. Gyrgum tells the frightened Myconid child, "Go help Shuushar." 


And Buppido shouts, "The divine plan said you would rescue me, Sarith!" 


In the darkness, something shifts, legs clicking. Zalthir sees the bulbous shape of a massive, ghastly insect leaving behind a meal of exhumed remains. The thing clicks its mandibles, feather-like feelers probing the darkness for its next meal. It screeches, and the smell of rot and loam overwhelms him. Before the centipede-like creature wraps its entire, staggering length around him, he breathes fire and runs behind Thorin and Grygum. 


So, the creature wraps its tentacle around Grygyum, knocking him out. 


Sarith, Buppido, Topsy, Turvy, and Sarith shoot the creature with hand-held crossbows. The bolts land on the beast, which, in its attempt to avoid gEttin g hurt, exposes its soft, fleshy parts to more bolts. 


Thorin strikes with his short sword, cutting into the carapace. Zalthir tries to burn the creature again, but his fire doesn't perform. The beast sensed his arrival and twisted away. 


The enraged monster bites Thorin, but the dwarf's armor protects him. He then tries to grapple Sarith with his mandible and fails. 


Thorin shouts to the twins, "Save Eldeth and Jimjar." 


Buppido and Sarith kill the creature with their bolts. And then Sarith shouts, "Rations!" 


Zalthir wakes up Daz and Grygyum. When Daz hears about Thorin's decision, he mutters, "Good decision. Maybe, just maybe, our traveling companions will see our actions and choose to help." 


An hour later,  Daz tells Zalthir, "Did you notice that?" 


Zalthir nods. 


Daz thinks, "We are being followed." He hears the careful footsteps of a drow scout, a sound he is very familiar with. He turns to Zalthir, "Drow." 


Zalthir remembers the particular gait of one of the Drow soldiers, Nym, and mutters, "It's the Drow from Velkynvelve. These are their scouts." He remembers reading the story of one of the monks of his order who was trapped in the Underdark and pursued by Drow. The monk, Korrh Erann, had been part of a group pursued for days. Some of their group hoped that killing the scouts would slow the Drow down. Korrh insisted that this was a dangerous gamble and chose to run. He was the only survivor. 


Zalthir, remembering the story, shouts, "We have to run!" 


Because Stool moves very slowly, Thorin shouts, "Get on my back!" 


Stool jumps on and releases his rapport spores. A few moments later, he asks everyone, "Was that a good idea? I think it was a good idea." Before anyone can respond, he says, "I'm really glad you liked my good idea!" 


Zalthir dashes off ahead, using his monk training to speed ahead. As he runs, he looks for anything to use as a weapon or a hiding spot. He sees a tunnel and chooses the one veering left. As his lungs burn, he's about to give up when he notices a space behind some stalagmites that they could hide behind. Using the mind link created by Stool, he lets the group know. 


Thorin, Daz,  Grygum, and the remaining prisoners run to the hiding space. 


Meanwhile, the pursuing Drow, hearing the running, start sprinting and, in their haste, barely avoid a sudden drop in the cave tunnels.


Soon, the Drow scouts arrive at the stalagmites. They hear a large thump. Zalthir has fallen from his hiding place. Nym draws his weapon. He looks around. Then, he feels a tremor. Worried that this cave will collapse and seeing no evidence of the prisoners, he turns to leave. As they leave, Nym mutters, "I told you it was the other tunnel." 


What Nym didn't know was that Grygum's Thaumaturgy was the source of the tremor.

## From chapter_05_arrival_at_the_silken_paths.md: Silken Paths — Zalthir tests his theory that Jimjar takes bad bets

As they exit the narrow passageway, Zalthir turns to Jimjar, "You're good at hiding things." 


Jimjar nods. 


"How about you block this passageway in a way that does not look like we were the cause?" 


"Wanna bet that I can?" 


"How much?" 


"5 gold." 


"I have a pound of food?" 


"I want gold." 


"Are you not interested in avoiding the drow?" 


"Wanna bet?" 


"We could leave you behind; it would improve our odds." 


"Wanna bet?" 


"WE HAVE NO MONEY." 


"That's okay, we'll have some later." 


"And if we don't, then?" 


"Then you never pay up." 


Zalthir is gEttin g progressively more annoyed. 


Sensing this could get violent, Daz steps in, "It's my understanding that Zalthir vowed never to gamble again when he became a monk. And such a bet would violate his oaths." 


Jimjar is stunned, "I'm so sorry. I didn't know." 


But then Grygum steps forward, "I'll make that bet." 


Jimjar gets to work and, a little bit later, announces, "You won the bet." 


"I did? Which side did I bet?" 


"You bet that I could do it." 


Zalthir is baffled and intrigued, "He bet against himself even though he knew the odds of winning that bet were terrible. His willingness to make these bad bets implies he will make further bad bets going forward." 


Grygum turns to him, "Interesting." 
Zalthir tries to test his theory, "I'll bet you can't fly to the roof of this cavern?" 


Jimjar laughs, "I am a gambler, not an idiot." 


Zalthir can only see that Jimjar is an infinite cash machine. If he can only figure out how to make Jimjar take the side of bets, Zalthir is most likely to win.

## From chapter_14_greengrass_day.md: The Duergar ambush — Zalthir fights underwater inside his own magical darkness

### Zalthir


The dragonborn sighs. He wonders how Thorin managed to stay alive all of these years. In fact he's beginning to wonder if he will survive next to Thorin, Daz, and Grygum. 


"We could check to see if there is any blood or gore on the boat?" 


He then looks at the castaways they collected and asks, "Do any of you recognize the boat?" 


The duergar looks at it and says, "Yes, it's  Duergar Keelboat. You can see the crates."
Zalthir is about to say something when Thorin suddenly wises up, "Are they big enough to hold people? I am worried about an ambush." 


Hemath laughs, "Yes, that would be a good thing to worry about. The Duergar can become invisible for a whole hour." And he turns invisible. "I will be hiding here." 


Then Thorin, who, to Zalthir's pleasant surprise, is becoming a lot more paranoid, asks, "Can't we do something to detect them, like splash water?" 


Zalthir groans inwardly because Daz will be excited.


"I have shape water!" 


And a few moments later, water is sprayed around the boat, and the outline of a humanoid appears. 


Zalthir is annoyed. They were about to have the element of surprise. If only Daz had carefully timed his water use so that Zalthir could take advantage of that action. 


But it is done. 


He scans his surroundings, eager to spot something, and he succeeds. He invokes Darkness and plunges into the water to attack the foe. Even though the opponent is invisible, Zalthir can pierce through the magical darkness, nullifying its advantage. 


The Dragonborn's initial strike misses, but the second strikes home with a thud. 

### 
Zalthir


Zalthir again moves the darkness to cover the enemies. He can hear Daz groan. 


As he swims towards them, he hears Eldeth curse them out as "Demonic spawn of dwarven corruption. Slave trader who deserves to be sent to the nine hells as punishment!" 


Zalthir mutters to himself, "I sense some racial animus." 


His fists strike the creature that had Spiderbait, killing it.  


Daz shouts, "Leave one uncovered!" 


Zalthir shouts, "It protects you!" 


He can hear Daz's sighs, and the burst of magic bolts kills the other creature.  


Another belch sounds, and another Duergar dies covered in acid. 


A few moments later, the last of the Deurgar is immobilized, and Zalthir is tired and happy. 


He floats to the keelboat and looks into the casks and the chest. He finds some gold and a jug that he learns later can be used to generate any liquid. The first liquid they generate is mayonnaise. After eating bland mushrooms, mayonnaise will make anything taste much better. 


As he digs through the contents, he can hear the debate on the Kuotoan boat about who should question their Duergar prisoner. 


Buppido shouts, "I have particular expertise and talents."


Sarith explains, "The Lloth's guards trained me. I am an expert, but having seen Daz's approach, I recommend that he be given the first crack at extracting information."
