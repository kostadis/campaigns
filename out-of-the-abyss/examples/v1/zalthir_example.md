# Zalthir — What I Saw in Velkynvelve, and the Silken Paths

The first thing I did when they took the bag off my head was look for the exit.

Velkynvelve was a Drow outpost carved into a cavern wall a hundred feet above the floor. The platform we stood on connected to a series of hanging towers — stalactites, hollowed out, joined by rope bridges and catwalks. Below the platform, spider webs stretched across the drop. Below the webs, a pool.

Most people would think: a hundred-foot drop, giant spiders, a pool of unknown depth. Impassable. I thought: the spiders mean the web holds weight. The pool means the web isn't the last surface before stone. I noted it and said nothing.

I also noted the grey ooze in the water near the base of the waterfall. That mattered later.

The guards assigned me to the winch. Cleaning work. While I cleaned, I checked the silk ropes — diameter, tension, how they ran through the pulleys. Fire would cut them. I'm bronze-blooded, and the fire I breathe is not decorative. I pocketed a length of rope and tested it against my flame that evening. It burns cleanly.

These are the things you notice when you've spent ten years on a ship and twelve years in a monastery that taught you shadows were walls and silence was a weapon. Everything is information. The question is which information you'll need.

The basket, I eventually decided, was redundant.

---

In the Silken Paths, I understood why Korrh Erann ran.

The monk Korrh Erann spent seventeen days running through the Underdark with Drow scouts behind him. I had read his account at the monastery. The lesson was: do not fight what you cannot outrun, and do not outrun what you cannot outlast. The Drow do not tire. They have six hundred years to wait you out.

We ran. Stool on Thorin's back. Daz keeping pace. Grygum, who should not move this fast but does. The scouts following us through the dark, exactly as I remembered from the story.

I found the stalagmites. I sent the location through Stool's spore-link and heard the others change course.

We hid. I had fallen from my ledge — a miscalculation I have noted for later — and lay still on the cave floor while Nym and his scouts swept the area. I could hear him six feet away. I could hear him decide. I heard the tremor that was Grygum's Thaumaturgy, and I heard Nym say to the other scout, *I told you it was the other tunnel.*

Then they were gone.

I stood up. I did not say anything about having fallen. No one asked.

Later, in the Silken Paths proper, I went under the web to attack the Spectator while it was occupied with Thorin. The move worked in principle. The execution was harder than I'd calculated — holding the silk with one hand while striking with the other is a balance problem, not a strength problem — and my first attack missed. My second connected. Yuk Yuk told me afterward that it was the coolest thing he'd seen. I didn't have the heart to tell him it had been less controlled than it looked.

When I was paralyzed by the eyestalk ray and couldn't move anything but my eyes, I spent the time noting which of the Spectator's eyestalks had already fired. Information, always. Even lying flat on a spider web a hundred feet above nothing.

Eventually, someone killed it. Daz, I think.

I got up. We moved on.
