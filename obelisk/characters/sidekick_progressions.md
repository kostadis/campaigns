# Sidekick Level Progressions — Pip, Maela, Veyra

**Scope:** companions for solo rogue Zenvon Foreput (Phandelver and Below, levels 1–12).
**Leveling trigger:** sidekick level always equals Zenvon's level; they level when he does ("if they survive major milestones, they level alongside the rogue" — open.md).
**Design chassis:** loosely Tasha's sidekick classes — Pip = Warrior (Defender), Maela = Spellcaster (Healer), Veyra = Spellcaster (Mage) — adapted to the custom open.md stat blocks. Levels 2 and the rogue-3 signature-feature hook are taken verbatim from open.md's Leveling Notes; everything else here extends that pattern.
**Design rules:**

1. Sidekicks support, never outshine. No Sneak Attack–scale damage; their numbers stay one notch behind a PC of equal level.
2. Hit Dice: a sidekick has (level + 1) Hit Dice (they start at level 1 with 2 HD, as written). HP = average.
3. Proficiency bonus tracks Zenvon's (+2 at levels 1–4, +3 at 5–8, +4 at 9–12).
4. Each sidekick's obelisk sense (Obelisk Shiver / Obelisk Unease / Blue Candle) never becomes a combat nuke — it grows as an investigation tool.
5. ⚠ Open question carried from extraction: Veyra's listed HP (8) vs. computed (9) — table below uses the dice formula; flag stands until ruled on.

## Level 2 detail (the imminent step — verbatim from open.md, plus derived numbers)

**Pip** — 3d8 + 6 = **19 HP**. Gains **Protection Training**: Protective Instinct twice per short rest.
**Maela** — 3d8 + 3 = **16 HP**. Gains one additional 1st-level spell slot (3 total).
**Veyra** — 3d6 + 3 = **13 HP**. Gains one additional 1st-level spell slot (3 total).
No other numbers change (PB stays +2; no new spells known at this step).

## Level 3 obelisk signature features (Claude-proposed — confirm before use)

open.md says: "When the rogue reaches 3rd level, consider giving each sidekick a signature once-per-short-rest feature tied to the obelisk mystery." Proposals, one per character, each 1/short rest:

**Pip — "Returned."** The whispers warn him a heartbeat early. When Pip uses Protective Instinct against an attack made by an Aberration or a creature wielding psionic power, the attack misses outright instead of having disadvantage. *(Defensive, ties to Obelisk Shiver, useless against mundane bandits — keeps him honest.)*

**Maela — Consecrating Touch.** When Maela restores hit points to a creature with a spell, she can also end one charm, fear, or psionic-domination effect on that creature imposed by an Aberration. *(Healer-shaped, scales with the campaign's mind-control threat, no damage creep.)*

**Veyra — Candlelight Reading.** Veyra holds the blue candle to an obelisk fragment, aberrant residue, or psionically touched object and asks one yes/no question about it; the candle's pulse answers truthfully as the *augury* spell's lore allows. *(Investigation tool, not combat power — feeds the mystery forward instead of solving it.)*

## Notes for future levels

- The 4/8/12 ASI cadence mirrors Tasha's sidekick classes; targets chosen to deepen role, not broaden it.
- Veyra's *fireball* at level 9 is deliberately GM-gated: a solo-rogue campaign can be trivialised by one AoE caster. Swap for *dispel magic* if she's overshadowing Zenvon.
- Signature-feature upgrades at level 10 are placeholders (2/short rest); revisit when the table gets there — by then the obelisk plot will say what these powers are becoming.
- If a sidekick dies, the replacement enters at Zenvon's level with the same chassis budget.
