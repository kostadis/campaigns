### Game Mechanics Explanation — Attack Rolls, Armor Class, and Proficiency

Kostadis paused to explain how attack rolls, armor class, and weapon proficiency work in D&D Beyond.

- **Details**
  - Kostadis: When attacking, the player rolls a D20 and adds their to-hit bonus; the total must meet or exceed the target's armor class to hit.
  - Kostadis: The goblin boss had an armor class of 17; Zenvon's dagger roll of 7 (total) was a miss, while the short sword roll of 20 (total) was a hit.
  - Kostadis: Weapon proficiency adds a bonus to hit rolls; without proficiency, Zenvon only has a +1 to hit with the javelin instead of +5.
  - Kostadis: Encumbrance rules exist in D&D but Kostadis does not enforce them, preferring to focus on gameplay over resource accounting.
- **Conclusion**
  - Players should use weapons they are proficient with for maximum effectiveness.
  - The DM does not track encumbrance or arrow counts in this campaign.
