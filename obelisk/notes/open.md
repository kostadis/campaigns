# Phandelver Opening: Neverwinter to Phandalin

# Solo Player Setup

The adventure begins in Neverwinter. The player character is a 1st-level rogue hired by Gundren Rockseeker to escort a wagon of mining supplies to Barthen's Provisions in Phandalin.

Gundren and his warrior escort, Sildar Hallwinter, have already ridden ahead on horseback. They promised to meet the wagon in Phandalin after handling urgent business connected to Gundren's latest discovery.

The rogue is the only official guard. The three sidekicks are fellow travelers sharing the road, each headed to Phandalin for their own reasons. They are not yet a formal adventuring party, but the road will quickly make them one.

# New Opening: Protector's Enclave

# 

Start the adventure in Protector's Enclave, then move to the docks for the wagon departure.

## Opening Location: The Driftwood Crown

A respectable inn and counting-house near the Hall of Justice in Protector's Enclave. It caters to merchants, minor nobles, caravan masters, and adventurers who know how to wipe their boots before entering.

The rogue meets Gundren Rockseeker here to accept the job.

## Scene Read-Aloud

Protector's Enclave gleams in the morning light, all pale stone, polished brass, fluttering banners, and watchful guards in clean cloaks. It is the kind of district where every coin seems counted twice before it changes hands.

Inside the Driftwood Crown, merchants speak in low voices over ledgers and wine. A fire crackles beneath a carved mantle. At a corner table, a broad-shouldered dwarf with a red beard waves you over with the enthusiasm of someone who has already decided you are trustworthy.

"There you are\!" he says. "Gundren Rockseeker. Sit, sit. I've got a simple job, honest pay, and absolutely no time for anyone who thinks those two things don't go together."

## Gundren's Offer

Gundren offers the rogue:  
\- 10 gp to escort a wagon of mining supplies from Neverwinter to Barthen's Provisions in Phandalin  
\- The wagon leaves from the docks at dawn  
\- Gundren and Sildar Hallwinter will ride ahead separately  
\- The rogue is in charge of the wagon once it leaves Neverwinter

Gundren says:

"Nothing glamorous. Just supplies. Picks, shovels, lantern oil, rope, that sort of thing. Important stuff, mind you. Very important. But not the sort of thing anyone gets stabbed over."

Sildar, seated nearby, dryly adds:

"People have been stabbed over less."

## Why Gundren Is Cagey

If the rogue asks why Gundren is in such a hurry, he gives a partial truth:

"My brothers and I are close to something. Something old. Something that could put Phandalin back on every map worth reading."

He does not reveal Wave Echo Cave yet unless you want him to be unusually trusting.

## Sidekick Glimpses In The Enclave

The sidekicks do not fully join yet, but the player can notice them before the wagon scene.

### Pip Thistlewick

Pip is being politely but firmly removed from the inn after "mistaking" a merchant's purse for his own.

"Easy mistake," Pip says. "Same shape. Same color. Same number of coins, briefly."

### Sister Maela Dawnforge

Maela is speaking with a priest or city official about strange reports from miners near Phandalin.

"If miners are hearing voices underground, you don't file that under superstition. You send someone with a lantern and sense."

### Veyra of the Blue Candle

Veyra is at another table surrounded by notes, pretending not to listen when Gundren mentions Phandalin.

Her candle, wrapped in cloth, gives off one faint blue pulse when Gundren says:

"Something old."

## Transition To The Docks

After the contract is agreed:

Gundren drains his cup, claps both hands on the table, and rises.

"Excellent. The wagon will be waiting at Anchorhook Yard in the morning. South side of the docks. Ask for Barthen's shipment. If anyone gives you trouble, tell them Gundren Rockseeker said to stop being difficult."

Sildar gives you a more measured nod.

"We'll ride ahead. Keep the wagon safe. And keep your eyes open once you turn onto the Triboar Trail."

## Next Morning At Anchorhook Yard

This is where the three sidekicks become road companions.

\- Pip is already sitting on the wagon, claiming he was hired as "inventory morale."  
\- Maela has paid for passage as far as Phandalin.  
\- Veyra asks, awkwardly but urgently, whether the wagon has room for one more traveler and several extremely important books.

This gives you a clean two-part start:  
1\. Protector's Enclave for the formal job offer and hints.  
2\. Anchorhook Yard for the party coming together and leaving Neverwinter.

# The Wagon

The wagon carries:  
\- Picks, shovels, lantern oil, rope, and iron spikes  
\- Two small casks of salted pork  
\- A locked strongbox containing payment for Barthen  
\- One sealed crate marked with Gundren's initials  
\- A rolled map case tucked beneath the driver's bench

The rogue is responsible for seeing the wagon safely to Phandalin.

# Why The Sidekicks Are Going To Phandalin

## Pip Thistlewick

Pip claims he is going to Phandalin because "small towns need strong backs and sharper eyes." The truth is messier.

He recently won a strange black stone shard in a dice game near Neverwinter's docks, then sold it to a peddler heading toward Phandalin. Since then, Pip has dreamed of broken black stone beneath the earth and heard whispers saying his name.

Pip wants to find the peddler, buy or steal the shard back, and figure out why it is haunting him.

Opening behavior: Pip walks beside the wagon with a battered shield on his back, eating an apple and insisting he is "professional road security."

## Sister Maela Dawnforge

Maela is traveling to Phandalin after receiving a troubling letter from an old dwarven contact who works claims near the Triboar Trail. The letter mentioned miners finding "black stone not born from any mountain" and hearing voices underground.

Maela believes something ancient is stirring beneath the frontier town. She also knows that if Gundren Rockseeker is involved in any rediscovery of old dwarven sites, trouble is probably close behind.

Opening behavior: Maela sits beside the driver, mending a torn cloak and quietly judging everyone's posture.

## Veyra of the Blue Candle

Veyra is following the trail of her missing mentor, who vanished while investigating fragments of an ancient obelisk. His last known destination was Phandalin.

She has a charcoal map covered in nervous annotations, a blue candle wrapped in cloth, and a half-burned note that reads: "The town is not the prize. It is the threshold."

Opening behavior: Veyra rides in the back of the wagon, trying to read while the road makes her handwriting worse by the mile.

# Opening Scene: The High Road South

Read or paraphrase:

The walls of Neverwinter fall behind you as the wagon creaks south along the High Road. The sea wind fades, replaced by the smell of pine, mud, and open country. Gundren Rockseeker's supplies rattle behind you: shovels, lanterns, rope, barrels, and crates bound for a frontier town called Phandalin.

Gundren himself rode ahead with a human warrior named Sildar Hallwinter, promising to meet you there in a day or two. That leaves you, the wagon, and three unlikely fellow travelers sharing the road.

The journey should be simple. Follow the High Road. Turn east onto the Triboar Trail. Deliver the wagon to Barthen's Provisions.

Simple jobs, of course, are famous for staying that way.

# First Player Prompt

Ask the player:

Where are you riding: at the reins, walking beside the wagon, or keeping watch from the back?

Then introduce the sidekicks naturally.

Pip says: "Just so we're clear, if arrows start flying, I am standing behind this shield. That's not cowardice. That's equipment doing its job."

Maela says: "Ignore him when needed. Feed him when useful. Same as most road companions."

Veyra says: "Has anyone else noticed the birds stopped singing about ten minutes ago, or is that only unsettling to me?"

# Sidekick Bonds To The Player

Pip likes the rogue if the rogue:  
\- Shares food  
\- Lets him take point when danger is obvious  
\- Laughs at his jokes  
\- Does not mock his very serious shield discipline

Maela respects the rogue if the rogue:  
\- Protects civilians  
\- Takes practical precautions  
\- Shows courage without being reckless  
\- Treats wounds and burials seriously

Veyra trusts the rogue if the rogue:  
\- Listens to warnings  
\- Asks about strange magic  
\- Protects her while she studies clues  
\- Does not mock her theories too much

# First Road Omen

Before the goblin ambush, add this short omen.

The wagon wheel strikes a black stone half-buried in the road. It cracks under the wheel with a sound like breaking glass.

\- Pip hears a whisper say: "Returned."  
\- Maela feels a brief chill in her holy symbol  
\- Veyra's wrapped blue candle gives off one pulse of light.

\- The rogue sees, for just a second, the shadow of a tall broken obelisk stretching across the road, though nothing is there.

Then it passes.

This connects the opening journey to the larger Shattered Obelisk plot without revealing too much.

# Goblin Ambush Adjustment

Use the normal Cragmaw ambush setup, but soften the first blow for a solo 1st-level rogue supported by sidekicks.

Instead of four goblins attacking at once, run it like this:

Round 1: Two goblins fire from hiding.  
Round 2: One goblin with a scimitar rushes the wagon.  
Round 3: A fourth goblin appears only if the party is winning easily, trying to flee with a sack from the wagon.

This keeps the fight tense without overwhelming the player.

# Sidekick Combat Roles

Pip:  
\- Moves to the front line, raises his shield, and tries to keep enemies away from the rogue.  
\- Uses shove, positioning, and opportunity attacks to protect the wagon.

Maela:  
\- Opens with bless if the fight looks serious.  
\- Uses sacred flame or warhammer.  
\- Saves cure wounds for when someone drops low.

Veyra:  
\- Uses mage armor if danger is expected.  
\- Casts magic missile if a goblin is hard to hit.  
\- Uses fire bolt when conserving spells.

# What They Find After The Ambush

On the road are two dead horses: Gundren's and Sildar's.

Add one obelisk clue:

One goblin carries a small black stone charm tied with sinew. It is not magical in a normal arcane sense, but Veyra's candle glows near it. Maela says the stone feels "wrong in the hand, like it remembers being part of something larger."

Pip recognizes the material as similar to the shard he sold.

# End Of Opening Session Beat

After the fight, the party has a decision:

\- Follow the goblin trail immediately  
\- Hide the wagon and investigate  
\- Continue to Phandalin first  
\- Send one sidekick ahead while others track

Let the sidekicks offer opinions, but make sure the rogue decides.

Pip says: "Trail's fresh. Also full of goblins. I mention both facts with equal respect."

Maela says: "If Gundren and Sildar live, time matters."

Veyra says: "That charm is connected to something larger. But alive first, mystery second. Probably."

This gives the solo player command of the party right away.

# Sidekick Stat Blocks

These sidekicks are built for a 1st-level rogue. They should support the player, not outshine them. Treat them as 1st-level sidekicks. If they survive major milestones, they can level alongside the rogue.

Medium humanoid, chaotic good

Armor Class 16 (chain shirt, shield)  
Hit Points 13 (2d8 \+ 4\)  
Speed 30 ft.

STR 15 (+2), DEX 13 (+1), CON 14 (+2), INT 10 (+0), WIS 12 (+1), CHA 13 (+1)

Saving Throws Str \+4, Con \+4  
Skills Athletics \+4, Intimidation \+3, Perception \+3, Survival \+3  
Senses passive Perception 13  
Languages Common  
Proficiency Bonus \+2

Protective Instinct. When a creature Pip can see attacks an ally within 5 feet of him, Pip can use his reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. He must be wielding a shield.

Second Wind. Pip can use a bonus action to regain 1d10 \+ 1 hit points. Once he uses this trait, he cannot use it again until he finishes a short or long rest.

Obelisk Shiver. When Pip is within 60 feet of an obelisk fragment or strong psionic magic, he gets a pressure headache and hears faint whispers. He can sense the general direction, but not the exact location.

Actions

Longsword. Melee Weapon Attack: \+4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d8 \+ 2\) slashing damage, or 7 (1d10 \+ 2\) slashing damage if used with two hands.

Javelin. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: \+4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 \+ 2\) piercing damage.

Shield Check. Pip attempts to shove one creature within 5 feet of him. He makes a Strength (Athletics) check contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics). On a success, he either pushes the target 5 feet or knocks it prone.

Tactics  
Pip stands between enemies and the rogue, tries to pin threats in place, and creates openings for Sneak Attack. He is brave, practical, and loud enough to draw attention when needed.

## Leveling Notes

When the rogue reaches 2nd level, each sidekick can gain one additional Hit Die and improve their core role slightly:  
\- Pip gains Protection Training, allowing him to use Protective Instinct twice per short rest.  
\- Maela gains one additional 1st-level spell slot.  
\- Veyra gains one additional 1st-level spell slot.

When the rogue reaches 3rd level, consider giving each sidekick a signature once-per-short-rest feature tied to the obelisk mystery.

# Veyra's Mentor: Professor Orryn Voss

Name: Professor Orryn Voss

Role: Missing occult scholar, Veyra's mentor, early connective tissue to the shattered obelisk mystery

Physical description: A narrow-shouldered human man in his late fifties with umber skin, silver-threaded black hair, and ink stains permanently darkening the first two fingers of his right hand. He wore half-moon spectacles, a travel-stained scholar's coat, and a blue glass signet ring that Veyra now keeps wrapped in cloth.

Personality: Brilliant, distracted, gentle with students, reckless with forbidden questions. Orryn treated fear as useful information rather than a reason to stop. He rarely raised his voice, but when he found a pattern in old records, he would forget meals, sleep, weather, and common sense.

## Backstory

Orryn Voss was once a lecturer in ancient empires and magical catastrophes at a small private academy in Neverwinter. His respectable work focused on Netherese ruins, pre-Spellplague relics, and the way powerful civilizations leave magical scars behind. His less respectable work concerned recurring stories of black stone fragments that appeared near disappearances, strange dreams, and settlements built over older ruins.

Years ago, Orryn found a reference to a broken obelisk somewhere in the region around Phandalin. The source was incomplete, but it described a stone that was not merely an artifact, but a threshold: something that could focus thought, memory, and alien will. Most scholars dismissed the fragment as metaphor or madness. Orryn did not.

Veyra became his apprentice after correcting one of his public lectures from the back row. Rather than taking offense, Orryn invited her to prove him wrong over tea. She did. He hired her within the week. He taught her how to compare conflicting sources, how to identify magical residue, and how to keep one hand free when reading anything that whispers back.

In the months before his disappearance, Orryn grew increasingly anxious. He spoke of black stone shards appearing in trade goods, goblin movements that made no tactical sense, and old ruins beneath Phandalin that seemed older than the town's known history. He believed someone, or something, was searching for pieces of a shattered Netherese obelisk.

His final message to Veyra was half-burned by the time it reached her. It read: "Was in Phandalin. The town is not a prize. It is the threshold."

## What Veyra Knows

\- Orryn came to Phandalin shortly before vanishing.  
\- He believed the black stone fragments were parts of a larger obelisk.  
\- His blue candle reacted to obelisk fragments and certain kinds of psionic magic.  
\- He feared the obelisk was connected to minds, memory, and transformation.  
\- He warned Veyra never to let anyone assemble the fragments.

## What Veyra Does Not Know Yet

\- Orryn may have learned too much about forces operating beneath Phandalin.  
\- His research overlaps with the later Far Realm and mind flayer plot, but he did not understand its full scope.  
\- The blue candle was not invented by Orryn; he found it in a sealed Netherese reliquary and modified it.  
\- Someone may still have Orryn's field journal.

## How To Use Orryn In Play

Orryn should feel present before he physically appears or is confirmed dead. Use him through marginal notes, damaged letters, Veyra's memories, and magical traces that make her blue candle react.

Possible clues:  
\- A goblin carries a scrap of paper covered in Orryn's shorthand.  
\- The black stone in Zenvon's pocket grows cold near one of Orryn's old notes.  
\- Veyra recognizes a charcoal symbol scratched into a cave wall as one Orryn used to mark "dangerous but important."  
\- A later villain refers to "the old scholar who heard the stone singing."

## Veyra's Emotional Hook

Veyra does not simply want to know what happened to Orryn. She wants proof that his work mattered and that he was not losing his mind. The more the party uncovers, the more frightened she becomes that he was right about everything.

## Table Line From Veyra

"Professor Voss used to say a ruin is only dead if nothing remembers it. I thought that was beautiful. Lately, I have started to think it was a warning."

# Next Session Prep: Stonehill Inn

## Purpose Of The Scene

This scene should slow the story down for roleplay before the party follows the goblin trail. It should make Phandalin feel alive, worried, and politically tense while still keeping the rescue of Gundren and Sildar as the emotional priority.

Use the Stonehill Inn scene to:  
\- Let Zenvon feel the town watching him and measuring what kind of person he is.  
\- Seed future Phandalin problems without distracting from the immediate rescue mission.  
\- Give Pip, Sister Maela, and Veyra small moments to react before the party leaves at dawn.

Vibe: Loud, speculative, not entirely reliable but not useless.

Lead: Marauders have attacked caravans on the east end of Triboar Trail; the townmaster is looking for help.

Table line:  
"Goblins, bandits, orcs, Redbrands. Doesn't matter what name they use. Roads aren't safe, and Harbin Wester's still hiding behind notices and wax seals."

Use him to reinforce that the goblin problem may be wider than Gundren's kidnapping.

## Narth

Role: Human farmer, older, observant, superstitious

Physical description: An older human man with weathered skin, a stooped back, and pale eyes that linger on windows, doorways, and bad omens. He smells faintly of hay, rain, and pipe smoke.

Wants: Keep his head down and keep his farm safe.

Vibe: Quiet, rural, notices injuries and omens.

Lead: Sister Garaele recently returned wounded after leaving town.

Table line:  
"Sister Garaele came back limping and pale. Wouldn't say from where. Folk who serve gods learn when not to speak, I suppose."

For Maela:  
"You're temple folk, aren't you? Maybe she'll talk to you before she talks to the rest of us."

## Tuck Stonehill

Role: Toblen's young son, renamed from the published Pip

Physical description: A wiry human boy of about nine, with a mop of sandy hair, a missing front tooth, and knees scuffed from climbing things he was told not to climb. He tries to stand like a guard when adults are watching.

Wants: Be taken seriously and tell the exciting thing he heard.

Vibe: Too curious for his own safety.

Lead: Carp Alderleaf found a secret tunnel in the woods and saw Redbrands nearby. In this version, Tuck also says Carp saw strange goblins acting oddly.

Table line:  
"Carp saw a tunnel\! A real one\! But his ma said not to talk about it because the red-cloaks nearly caught him."

If Zenvon is kind:  
"You're going after Gundren, right? Good. People who get taken don't always come back here."

## Best Scene Flow

1\. Toblen greets the party and gives them rooms.  
2\. Elsa serves food and starts casual talk.  
3\. Tuck approaches if Zenvon seems approachable.  
4\. Freda or Narth quietly drops a Redbrand or Garaele lead.  
5\. End with Toblen asking about Gundren, or with Elmina's worry spreading through the room.  
6\. Morning: the party leaves for the goblin trail.

## Keep The Priority Clear

The tavern should seed Redbrands, Garaele, Halia, Daran, and Carp, but the emotional priority remains clear: Gundren and Sildar are alive somewhere north of the ambush site, and every hour matters.
