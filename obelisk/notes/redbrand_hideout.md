# The House That Glasstaff Built

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the Redbrand Hideout session.

---

## What I understood going in (catch me if I'm wrong)

- Party is Level 2: Zenvon (rogue), Pip (fighter), Sister Maela (cleric), Veyra (mage).
- The module expects Level 2 for this dungeon. You're on schedule.
- Two entry routes: front door through Tresendar Manor (R1, down the cellar stairs) or the south tunnel Carp found (emerges at R8, the crevasse cave).
- Glasstaff is Iarno Albrek — Sildar's missing contact and former Lords' Alliance wizard. Sildar doesn't know this yet. When he finds out, that revelation lands harder than the dungeon does.
- The dungeon has a hidden obelisk beat that should shake Veyra: two dead psionic goblins with elongated skulls under the southern bridge in R8. The nothic killed them before they could cause trouble. The party is walking into the aftermath of a different incursion.

---

## The Win Condition

Glasstaff is captured or killed. The Dendrar family is freed. The party walks out with the Black Spider's letter and Sildar's moment of recognition. Secondary: Veyra sees the dead psionic goblins and connects them to the one Sildar described on the road. The obelisk plot just got a body count inside Phandalin's own basement.

## The Default Trajectory (if the party somehow stalls)

- The Redbrands keep extorting merchants. Mirna Dendrar and her children remain in the cells.
- Glasstaff, alerted by a failed infiltration, moves his correspondence and becomes harder to catch.
- The bugbears in R9 grow impatient and start taking it out on the locals.
- The psionic goblins who sent the dead scouts don't know yet that Ssarnak killed their advance team. They will eventually.

## The Clever Play

1. Use Carp's tunnel (R8 entry) — avoids the barracks in R1/R2 entirely and puts the party right next to Ssarnak, who can be negotiated with for the full layout.
2. Negotiate with Ssarnak before exploring anything — the nothic will share everything it knows for food. It knows all the secret doors, the password for the skeletons, and Glasstaff's habits.
3. Hit R9 (bugbear barrack) before R11/R12 — if the bugbears are alive when Glasstaff flees, they will intercept the party. Take them out first.
4. Approach R12 through the secret passage from R7 — this bypasses the workshop (R11) entirely, so Zeond the quasit can't warn Glasstaff. He's caught flat-footed at his desk.
5. Capture Glasstaff rather than kill him — Sildar will want him transported to Neverwinter, and a live Glasstaff who thinks the Spider might still rescue him will volunteer more than a dead one.

> **GM-only:** The tunnel entry (R8) is the smart play and the one a good rogue should find appealing. If Nikhil takes the front door, that's fine — the dungeon still works. The tunnel route just removes two rooms of noise-generating combat early, which makes Glasstaff's escape a real possibility rather than a guaranteed outcome.

## Entry A: The South Tunnel (R8 — Carp's Route)

Carp Alderleaf leads the party to a cave entrance in the woods south of Tresendar Manor. He will not go inside. His exact words:

> "It goes under the house. I heard things in there. Big things."

He is correct. The tunnel runs north about one hundred feet and opens into the crevasse cave.

### The Crevasse Cave (R8)

>>A cold breeze softly sighs in this large natural cavern, carrying the faint scent of decaying flesh. A crevasse five to ten feet wide and twenty feet deep divides the cavern and is flanked by two rough stone columns that support a twenty-foot-high ceiling. Two arched wooden bridges span the chasm.

The nothic Ssarnak is lurking near the west ends of the two bridges.

**The southern bridge is rigged to collapse** under more than 50 pounds. A DC 10 Perception check near the bridge spots the rigging. Ssarnak knows about this and uses the northern bridge. Any character who crosses the southern bridge without noticing the rigging will fall 20 feet into the crevasse (7 bludgeoning damage, prone, difficult terrain).

**Under the southern bridge, at the bottom of the crevasse:** Two dead goblins with elongated skulls, dressed in filthy rags. Different from the Cragmaw goblins. Ssarnak's claw marks killed them. They were here before the Redbrands moved in.

> **Stage direction:** Let Veyra be the one who notices them first, if she's looking down. Her blue candle gives one pulse of light when she gets within 60 feet of the bodies. She knows what that means.

**At the bottom of the crevasse:** Thel Dendrar's skeleton — the woodcarver murdered by the Redbrands. Picked clean by Ssarnak, but wearing a carved wooden unicorn-head pendant. Mirna in the cells can identify it.

**Ssarnak's hoard** is in a wooden chest hidden in a cubbyhole at the crevasse bottom: 160 sp, 120 gp, five malachite gems (12 gp each), a potion of healing, a potion of climbing, a potion of growth, and the sword Talon — a +1 longsword in a silver-chased scabbard, inscribed with the name "Talon," hilt worked like a bird of prey.

---

### Ssarnak

The nothic communicates via telepathy. It is not good. It is not safe. But it can be dealt with.

**What Ssarnak wants:** Meat. Fresh meat. The Redbrands have been giving it portions of prisoners and travelers who disappear — it's been fed, but it's always hungry.

**How to open the negotiation:** Offer food. Ssarnak responds to this immediately. It will not attack if the party offers something edible and does not threaten it.

**What Ssarnak will trade for food (or the promise of future food):**
- Full layout of the hideout, including all secret doors
- The skeleton password ("Illefarn") for the crypt in R4
- Glasstaff's location (R12, through R7 or R11)
- That there are bugbears in R9 who Ssarnak finds annoying
- That Glasstaff has a glass staff and a little demon that watches his workshop

**Ssarnak's Weird Insight:** Once per rest, the nothic can magically read the mind of one creature it can see and learn one secret. Let Ssarnak use this if the party stays for more than a few minutes.

> **GM-only play options:**
> - Ssarnak looks at Pip and says, flatly: *"The stone calls you. You hear it still."* (Obelisk Shiver, made external and unnerving.)
> - Ssarnak looks at Veyra and says: *"Your teacher is not dead. He is not in a place where dead is easy."* (This is not canon — it's what the nothic thinks it saw. But it will disturb Veyra more than a confirmed death would.)
> - Ssarnak looks at Zenvon and says: *"You took something on the road. It knows it was moved."* (The black stone in Zenvon's pocket.)
>
> Use one of these, not all three. Pick the one that lands hardest for the player.

**If the party wants to kill Ssarnak:** It fights. It's dangerous. It has 58 HP, a Rotting Gaze attack (DC 12 Con save or 10d6 necrotic damage on a hit), and it uses the columns for cover. Let the party negotiate — it's more interesting.

**What Ssarnak knows about the dead psionic goblins:** They came through the south tunnel about two weeks ago. Two of them. They moved like they were looking for something. They ignored Ssarnak until it attacked them. Ssarnak does not know what they were looking for. It ate most of them before deciding they tasted wrong.

## Entry B: The Front Door (R1 — Tresendar Manor)

If the party enters through the manor ruins, they come down stairs into the cellar.

### R1: Cellar

A five-foot landing, stone stairs down to a large cellar with kegs and barrels. The cistern is against the west wall — ten feet deep, filled with cold fresh water.

**Hidden in the cistern:** A waterproof satchel on a submerged rope, 2 feet below the surface. Not visible from above. Contains: potion of healing, potion of invisibility, 50 gp, clean travel clothing. This is Glasstaff's emergency escape kit. If Glasstaff flees, he tries to reach this.

**Alert trigger:** Moving the barrels to search them is noisy and alerts R2.

### R2: Barracks

Three Redbrand ruffians (bandits, use bandit stat block). If they hear noise in R1, they prepare and try to surprise the party.

> **Stage direction:** The barracks smell like sour ale and old boots. The three Redbrands are not cowards — they're enforcers who've been beating up merchants for months. Play them as professional bullies who are annoyed, not frightened.

---

## The Interior

Once inside from either direction, the dungeon has the following logic:

### R3: Trapped Hall

The only passage west from the barracks runs past faux columns to a copper-sheathed double door. The floor in the middle conceals a pit trap: false stone tiles over breakaway timbers.

DC 15 Perception to spot it. 100+ pounds triggers it. A creature falls 20 feet, takes 2d6 bludgeoning damage, lands prone.

There are narrow ledges along the north and south walls. DC 10 Acrobatics to shimmy past on a ledge without triggering the trap.

> Pip will try to walk straight through. He's big. Remind Nikhil about the weight threshold if Pip is in the lead.

### R4: Tresendar Crypts

Three sarcophagi, six animated skeletons in rusty mail. The skeletons attack any creature that comes within 10 feet of either door — unless the creature is wearing a scarlet Redbrand cloak or speaks the password **"Illefarn"**.

Ssarnak knows this password. A captured Redbrand ruffian will reveal it if charmed or persuaded.

**Treasure:** A jeweled gold bracelet (140 gp) hidden among the bones in the female sarcophagus. The Redbrands never found it.

> **GM-only:** Maela's Obelisk Unease trait means she feels something spiritually wrong in this room even before she sees the skeletons. The crevasse in R8 has a faint necromantic aura that's been seeping into the stonework. Give her an unprompted sense that the dead here are not entirely at rest.

### R5: Holding Cells

Three Redbrand ruffians guard three prisoners: Mirna Dendrar (adult woman, terrified and furious in equal measure) and her children, Nars (13, son) and Nilsa (18, daughter). Thel Dendrar, her husband, was murdered by the Redbrands. His remains are at the bottom of R8's crevasse.

**Cell door locks:** DC 10 Thieves' Tools to pick. DC 22 Strength to force.

**Mirna's heirloom:** She has nothing to give immediately, but mentions an emerald necklace hidden in her family's old herb and alchemy shop in Thundertree. Beneath a section of storage shelves, southeast part of town. She never went back after the undead overran the place.

**NPC notes — Mirna:**
- She does not cry in front of strangers. She has been holding it together for her children and she will not stop now.
- What she wants from this scene: To know Thel's fate. She will ask. Don't deflect — she'll push until she gets an answer.
- If the party shows her Thel's unicorn pendant (from R8): she accepts it, holds it, and does not speak for several seconds. Do not fill the silence.
- What she can tell the party: The boss is a wizard. She's heard him called "Glasstaff." There are "tall, furry monsters with big ears" — bugbears — who come and go.

---

### R9: Guard Barrack (Bugbears)

Four bugbears and one goblin, Droop. The goblin is at the bugbears' mercy — the bugbears have been using him as a servant. He faints when the party enters. Another creature can use an action to wake him.

**The bugbears:** They work for the Spider, not Glasstaff. They were sent to provide extra muscle. They are contemptuous of the Redbrands and only minimally loyal to Glasstaff. If the party is wearing scarlet cloaks, the bugbears assume they're Redbrands and are dismissive rather than hostile.

**The bugbears know the location of Cragmaw Castle and Wave Echo Cave. They will not share this willingly.** Fear of the Spider outweighs any pressure the party can apply — except a charm person spell, which bypasses the hierarchy entirely.

**Droop:** Not a threat. He's a Scraptop goblin (scavenger/tinkerer clan, not Cragmaw) who wandered in looking for wagon parts and got press-ganged. He wants to go home.

If the bugbears are dealt with, Droop will ask to be escorted out. He doesn't know Cragmaw Castle's exact location but knows it's north, in the forest. He knows the general layout of this hideout and will share it if asked nicely.

> **GM-only:** Droop is a small character beat, not a major ally. He's useful as a guide out of the hideout if the party is injured and wants a safe exit route. Don't oversell him.

---

### R10: Common Room

Four Redbrands playing knucklebones — two ruffians, two bandits — all drunk. The dice are loaded (one bandit is cheating and winning). If the party bursts in, they surprise the Redbrands. If they're wearing scarlet cloaks, they can try to pass as new recruits and even join the game.

This is a scene, not a fight. Let it breathe if Nikhil wants to play it.

> **Stage direction:** Zenvon is a rogue. He'll notice the loaded dice immediately if he's paying attention — DC 10 Perception. Pointing this out to the Redbrands causes a fight among them before the party is involved. Use this if the energy needs a spark.

---

### R11: The Wizard's Workshop

Glasstaff is not here — he's in R12. But his familiar, **Zeond** (a quasit), is. The quasit is invisible and watching.

**Zeond shares a telepathic bond with Glasstaff.** If the party enters R11, Zeond immediately warns Glasstaff. Glasstaff then has time to grab his scrolls and flee through R12's secret door.

**To catch Glasstaff flat-footed:** The party must either
- Enter R12 through the secret passage from R7 (bypasses R11 entirely), OR
- Kill or banish Zeond before it can communicate — which requires detecting it first (it's invisible).

**The workshop:** Bubbling alembics, tomes on alchemy, an experiment brewing (potion of invisibility, failing). Among the books: a Dwarvish journal by an adventurer named Urmon, describing the history of Wave Echo Cave and the Forge of Spells. Also notes that a magic mace called *Lightbringer* was created by priests of Lathander and lost in Wave Echo Cave.

> **Veyra beat:** Veyra can spend 10 minutes with these notes. She'll recognize Orryn Voss's shorthand in the margins of one page — a single annotation, written in his distinctive cramped hand: "The Spider found this first." She doesn't know what it means. But Orryn was here, or Orryn saw this book, or someone who knew Orryn's notation system was here. File this under: her mentor wasn't just researching the obelisk. He was tracking the Spider too.
>
> This is a Claude-proposed beat — confirm before using. It requires Veyra to be familiar with Orryn's handwriting (she is) and requires the note to be in the book (it is now, if you use it).

---

### R12: Glasstaff's Quarters

**If Glasstaff was NOT warned by Zeond (party used the secret passage from R7):**

He's at his desk in ermine-trimmed robes, studying a tome, with a glass staff leaning against his chair. He's surprised. He will not fight if he has no path out — instead, he starts negotiating immediately, addressing the party as "my honored guests" with exaggerated courtesy.

**If Glasstaff WAS warned (party came through R11):**

He has fled through the northeast secret door, heading for R7 → R8, trying to reach the cistern in R1 and the potion of invisibility. He closes the secret door behind him but not quite fully — the party can find it with DC 10 Perception (advantage) because the door is slightly ajar.

**If Glasstaff is in the hideout, fleeing:** He instructs Ssarnak to stop pursuers, if Ssarnak is still alive and on his payroll. Ssarnak will do so, but without enthusiasm — it's not loyal, just hungry. The promise of the party as a food source might actually work.

**Glasstaff at 8 HP or below, no escape:** He surrenders. He values his life above everything.

---

### Playing Glasstaff

He puts on airs. He calls his bandits "my good fellows" and refers to murders as "those unfortunate events." He addresses Zenvon as "my dear rogue" and Maela as "good sister" with genuine politeness that makes it worse, not better.

**What he'll say in captivity, truthfully:**
- The Black Spider is a drow.
- The Spider sent the four bugbears to reinforce the Redbrands.
- The bugbears know the way to Wave Echo Cave. Glasstaff does not.
- The Spider is searching for the Forge of Spells.
- No one in the Lords' Alliance knows about his betrayal.

**What he will not say:**
- He will not name the Spider (he doesn't know the name, only the title).
- He will not tell them where Cragmaw Castle is (he doesn't know).
- He will not apologize for the Dendrar murder. He calls it "that unpleasant business with the woodcarver."

> **The Sildar moment:** When the party brings Glasstaff to Sildar — or when Sildar enters the hideout and finds the wizard — do not describe Sildar's face. Let the silence carry it. He will say Iarno's name once, quietly, like he's checking whether the word still works. Then he will say: "Lord's Alliance business. I'll arrange the transport." He will not shout, weep, or lecture. He's too tired and too angry.
>
> This is your session's emotional peak. Do not rush it.

---

### The Letter

On Glasstaff's desk, rolled and sealed with a wax spider:

> Lord Albrek, my spies in Neverwinter tell me that strangers are due to arrive in Phandalin. They could be working for the dwarves. Capture them if you can, kill them if you must, but don't allow them to upset our plans. See that any dwarven maps in their possession are delivered to me with haste. I'm counting on you, Iarno. Don't disappoint me.

No name. No location. But the wax seal is a spider. And the Black Spider is, per Glasstaff, a drow.

**Halia's 100 gp is for this letter and anything else in the chest.** She knows the Spider exists. She wants the contact network.

## The Talon Sword

The +1 longsword in Ssarnak's hoard has a name and a history. Sir Aldith Tresendar (the "Black Hawk") died defending this manor from bandits who came up through the hidden caverns. The sword was lost until Ssarnak found it at the bottom of the crevasse.

This is a named weapon with a house attached to it. The manor above belongs to no one living. The party is, in a quiet sense, the first people in generations to reclaim it from the criminals who've been using it.

> Leave this implication unstated. If Nikhil asks who owns the manor now, you can shrug and say "no one alive." The sword is theirs. The history is theirs to do with as they like.

## After the Hideout

**Sildar pays 200 gp** for eliminating the Redbrand threat (regardless of whether Glasstaff is dead or captured).

**What the party now knows:**
- The Black Spider is a drow, interested in the Forge of Spells.
- The Spider orchestrated Gundren's ambush to get his map.
- Cragmaw Castle is the next logical target for finding Gundren. But they still need a location.

**Leads to Cragmaw Castle's location (open to the party now):**
- Droop knows it's north in Neverwinter Wood, general area only.
- The bugbears know exactly, but won't say. A charm person spell breaks that.
- Qelline Alderleaf (Carp's mother, halfling farmer) can direct them to a druid named Reidoth, who knows the woods and can give them precise directions.
- Sister Garaele's banshee (Agatha) might answer a different question entirely, but could also be asked about Cragmaw Castle.
- The party can try to track goblin trails north into Neverwinter Wood.

## Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can we keep Ssarnak?"**
→ Ssarnak is not loyal. It is not a pet. If the party wants an ongoing relationship, it needs to keep feeding it. If Zenvon makes a deal (food, regularly), Ssarnak will hold the crevasse cave indefinitely. It's a useful alarm system for anyone trying to use the tunnel to enter Phandalin. But Maela will have opinions about this arrangement.

**"Can we move into Tresendar Manor?"**
→ Nothing stops them. The manor is empty, structurally questionable, and directly above the hideout they just cleared. It's a large, defensible, rent-free base in the middle of town. Toblen Stonehill will think this is both funny and impressive.

**"What does Halia do with the letter?"**
→ She files away the name "Iarno Albrek" alongside what she learns about the Black Spider's network. She pays the 100 gp without expression and thanks the party with genuine warmth. She is now more useful to them than she was before. She is also more dangerous.

## Open Decisions

- **Zeond, the quasit:** If the party kills it, Glasstaff loses his familiar permanently. If it escapes, it can follow them invisibly. Do you want a persistent nuisance trailing the party, or do you want this loose end closed?
- **Droop's fate:** Does he go home? Do the party take him along? He's a small goblin with wagon repair skills and no combat value. He's also a warm body the party saved. Make a call before the session in case Nikhil adopts him.
- **Orryn Voss's marginal note:** The beat in R11 above is a Claude-proposed addition. Confirm or remove it before play.
- **Thel Dendrar:** The party will probably find his remains at the bottom of the crevasse. Mirna needs to know. Do you want to play that scene at the table or handle it as reported dialogue?
- **Glasstaff's transport to Neverwinter:** This is Sildar's problem, but the party may feel protective of or hostile toward the prisoner. Do you want Glasstaff to have any final line before he goes? If yes, let him have one moment of genuine emotion — not remorse, but something honest. Maybe: "I wasn't wrong that the Forge was worth having. I was wrong about who deserved it." Then he shuts down and says nothing else.
