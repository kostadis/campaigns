# The King's Price

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the Cragmaw Castle session.

---

## Level note (read this first)

The module expects the party to be **Level 3** when they reach Cragmaw Castle. If they're coming here straight from the Redbrand Hideout, they may still be Level 2 — check whether Nikhil has leveled after the hideout.

**Level 3 matters here because:**
- King Grol is a bugbear with 65 HP. He hits for 2d8+4 (average 13). A Level 2 Zenvon has ~15 HP.
- There are four hobgoblins in C12 (guard quarters) and four more in C6. Hobgoblins at Level 2 are a credible TPK threat.
- The grell in C13 (haunted tower) drops from the ceiling with surprise. At Level 2, that's potentially brutal.

**Options if the party arrives at Level 2:**
- Run the castle but give the party easier approaches (C10 postern gate bypasses most of the guard rooms).
- Compress the garrison (fewer hobgoblins in C6 and C12).
- Or lean in and let the difficulty be real — this is a castle held by a king. It was never meant to be easy.

Make this call before the session. The doc below assumes a full Level 3 run but flags the dangerous rooms.

---

## Finding the Castle

Cragmaw Castle is hidden in Neverwinter Wood, about 40 miles northeast of Phandalin (roughly two days of travel). The party needs a way to find it.

**How they might have learned the location:**
- **Droop** (rescued goblin from R9): Knows it's "north, in the forest." Not a precise location, but good enough to start tracking.
- **Bugbears** (R9, via charm person): Know the exact route.
- **Qelline Alderleaf** (Carp's mother, halfling farmer in Phandalin): Points them to a druid named Reidoth who lives in the ruins of Thundertree — Reidoth knows the woods and can give precise directions.
- **Trail tracking:** A DC 15 Survival check near the goblin ambush site can follow the trail north; takes longer and requires multiple days, but works.

> **GM-only:** The cleanest mid-session discovery is if the party rescued Droop and he's with them — he can guide them to the right region of the forest, and tracking handles the rest. If they don't have Droop, Qelline Alderleaf is one conversation in Phandalin away.

---

## The Win Condition

Gundren is alive and conscious when the party leaves. The doppelganger Vyerith is dealt with. King Grol is dead or driven off. The party recovers Gundren's map to Wave Echo Cave, which means they now have the final destination in hand.

## The Default Trajectory (if the party delays)

- King Grol and Vyerith reach a price for Gundren. Vyerith takes Gundren south, intending to kill him and destroy the map before delivering either to the Spider. Gundren dies in transit, the map is gone, and Wave Echo Cave becomes findable only through other means.
- The module does not enforce a tight clock here, but the negotiation in C14 has an end. Give yourself a mental deadline: if the party takes a full week of in-fiction time doing other things after the Redbrand Hideout, Vyerith has left with Gundren.

## The Clever Play

1. Find the postern gate (C10) — the south entrance. It's locked, but it bypasses the banquet hall (C7), the two sets of hobgoblins (C6, C12), and the goblin barracks (C4). The path from C10 to C14 runs through C11 → C13 → C12 → C14, or C11 → C8 → C9 → C14. Both have dangers, but neither funnels the whole garrison at once.
2. Enter quietly and don't trigger the goblin sentries in C3 — if they raise the alarm, goblins and hobgoblins from C4 and C6 hit the party from both directions in C2.
3. Skip C13 (the haunted tower) entirely — it's boarded up for a reason the Cragmaws understand.
4. Isolate C14 before entering — if the hobgoblins in C12 are handled first, one of them runs to warn Grol, giving him 2 rounds to prepare. Take out C12 quietly or take out the messenger before he gets there.
5. In C14: do not let Grol grab Gundren as a hostage — enter fast, from an unexpected direction, and use the element of surprise. The southern bridge approach through C10 helps with this.

> **GM-only:** The biggest threat in this castle is not any single monster. It's the alarm system — if the sentries in C3 raise the alarm, combat in C2 becomes a two-front fight, and then the hobgoblins in C6 and C12 may arrive. Manage noise. The party is carrying a rogue with Cunning Action. This is his dungeon.

## The Approach

### C1: Castle Entrance

Seven crumbling towers, collapsed upper levels. A short flight of stairs to a terrace, broken main doors standing open.

Two goblin bosses in each archer tower (C3 left and right of the entrance) are supposed to be watching — they're bored and glance through arrow slits only occasionally.

**Stealth check:** Everyone rolls. The lowest result is the DC the goblin bosses must beat on their Perception. They're inattentive — their passive Perception is 11. A poor roll from Pip (or Sister Maela in armor) breaks the quiet.

If spotted: the goblins shoot from the arrow slits and shout to C4 and C6.

### C2: Trapped Hall

Corridors north and south, rubble partially blocking the southern hall. A tripwire near the east end is hidden under dusty plaster — DC 20 Perception to spot it. If triggered, a cave-in falls in the marked area (DC 10 Dex save or 2d10 bludgeoning damage). The noise puts C3, C7, C8, C9, and C12 on alert.

> **Stage direction:** Have Zenvon roll Perception automatically when the party moves through the rubble. Don't announce there's a trap — just ask for the roll and describe what he notices if he succeeds.

### C4: Ruined Quarters

Three goblins asleep in bedrolls. If there's loud noise in the main entrance area, one is sent to investigate. If that goblin doesn't return, the other two investigate.

Silent approach or a sleep-equivalent here buys the party the west side of the castle undisturbed.

### C6: Hobgoblin Quarters

Four hobgoblins, bored and quarrelsome. They don't react to noise from C2 or C3, but will defend their tower and respond to an alarm. They plan to overthrow the bugbears someday. Right now they haven't gotten around to it.

> **Level warning:** Four hobgoblins at Level 2 is a hard fight. If the party wants to avoid this room and still reach C14, they can — the C10 postern gate route doesn't pass through C6 at all.

---

## The South Approach (C10 — Postern Gate)

The south side of the castle has an iron door on an overgrown path, sheltered from direct attack. It's locked: DC 15 Thieves' Tools to pick, DC 25 Strength to force. Forcing it makes noise.

The arrow slits above the postern gate look into C7 (the banquet hall). If the party breaks the door noisily, the goblins in C7 come to look.

If unlocked quietly, the postern gate opens into C11 — the ruined tower.

### C11: Ruined Tower

Rotting crates, dusty canvas hanging on the north wall. The canvas blends into the stonework and looks like rubble — DC 15 Perception to spot the footpath leading through it. A DC 10 Perception check works if the party is actively searching the outside of the castle.

No enemies here. The ruined tower is the quietest entry point in the castle.

---

## The Interior (Dangerous Rooms)

### C7: Banquet Hall

The primary gathering place. Six goblins and goblin boss Yegg (the cook, bad-tempered and loud). If Yegg is killed, the remaining goblins flee east or west — they avoid the north door because of C2's trap (they know about it).

Two large tables, a brass brazier, dirty dishes, moldy bread, gnawed bones. Yegg is berating his helpers. This is the noisiest room in the castle.

> Zenvon can hear this room through the north door of C11 — goblin arguing carries. This is useful intelligence: a lot of goblins in one place, but distracted.

### C8: Dark Hall (the Chapel)

Angelic sculpted figures on the walls. Two archways to the north covered with heavy curtains. A cracked brazier between them.

**The grick** lives here. It's Lhupo's pet (C9). It climbs into the shadows of the sculpted figures and watches intruders before dropping. DC 14 Perception check or the party is surprised by the drop attack.

The grick will not eat goblins unless Lhupo says so. The Cragmaws are terrified of it and hurry through in groups.

**The chapel decor:** A character who examines the angels and altar-imagery can make DC 10 Religion to identify the gods: Oghma, Mystra, Lathander, Tymora. This was once a holy place, long before the goblins got to it. Let Maela have this moment.

**Treasure in the brazier:** Under coal, a gold elf statuette (100 gp) wrapped in crimson cloth. A goblin hid it here. Any non-evil creature grasping it can ask one question and receive a telepathic response as if casting augury. One use, then inert. A detect magic reveals divination aura.

> Veyra will want to study this. Let her. If she asks about Orryn Voss, the augury answers obliquely — the nature of augury is yes/no or weal/woe. "Will Orryn Voss be found?" is a valid question. The answer, if you want to plant a hook: *Woe.* Not "dead." Not "alive." Just: woe.

### C9: Goblin Shrine

"Jagged Tooth" — the god the Cragmaws invented, because they wanted a god and the real ones didn't seem to be listening. Goblin boss Lhupo (she calls herself a priest, has no spells) and two goblin acolytes in robes.

If they heard the grick fight in C8, they hide behind the altar and try to surprise the party. Otherwise they're kneeling and praying.

The chalice (180 gp) and censer (150 gp) on the altar are genuinely beautiful objects — too nice for the Cragmaws to have made. Loot from a temple raid.

> **Stage direction:** Don't make the Jagged Tooth worship purely comedic. Lhupo believes it. She just doesn't have actual divine contact, which has made her theology improvised and slightly desperate. She's not mocking religion — she's performing it with available materials. Let the party decide what that means.

### C12: Guard Quarters

Four hobgoblins, keeping better watch than anyone else in the castle. At the start of combat, one hobgoblin runs to warn King Grol and returns 2 rounds later.

> **Level warning:** If the party is Level 2 and hits this room before they've cleared most of the castle, this is where things get hard. The hobgoblins at C12 are professionally competent — they coordinate, use cover, and the runner to C14 gives Grol 2 rounds of preparation.

### C13: Haunted Tower (DO NOT BOARD)

The door is boarded up from the inside. Taking the boards off requires a few rounds of noisy work. The Cragmaws know to leave this door alone.

Inside: a grell in the shadows of the upper collapsed levels. It drops without warning. DC 14 Perception or surprised.

**If the party doesn't need what's here:** Skip it. There's a chest up near the grell's nest (climb DC 15) with 90 ep, 120 gp, a potion of healing, a spell scroll of silence, and a spell scroll of revivify. The revivify scroll is significant. Worth the risk if the party is thinking ahead about what happens in Wave Echo Cave. Up to them.

## C14: King's Quarters — The Only Room That Matters

Any character listening at the door hears two voices in disagreement — a low growling one demanding something, and a calm, smooth reply.

### The Room

Thick furs on the floor. Old trophies on the walls. A brazier burning bright. A large bed to the north. A round table with chairs near the door to the south.

**Present:**
- **King Grol** — an old bugbear with silver-jeweled teeth over his rotting fangs. Gray-streaked fur, stooped shoulders, still agile and strong. He rules by pure intimidation; he's been doing it for decades.
- **Snarl** — Grol's dire wolf. Huge, scarred, loyal to Grol and only Grol.
- **Vyerith** — appears to be a drow woman in gray robes, white hair. She's not. She's a doppelganger and a messenger from the Black Spider.
- **Gundren Rockseeker** — unconscious on the floor, badly beaten, at 0 HP but stable.

### What's Actually Happening

King Grol has the dwarf. Vyerith was sent by the Spider to pick up Gundren and the map. Grol wants to sell them rather than give them away, and they've been negotiating for at least a day.

**What Grol wants:** More gold. He doesn't care about the Spider's plans. He cares about being paid fairly.

**What Vyerith is actually planning:** She's going to betray the Spider. Her plan is to question Gundren to determine if anyone else knows Wave Echo Cave's location — and if not, to kill him and destroy the map, removing the Spider's leverage. She hasn't told Grol this. She's been stalling while she works out the logistics.

> **GM-only:** This means the party is actually walking in on a kidnapping that is about to become a murder, planned by someone who is herself betraying the person who ordered the kidnapping. It's a three-way mess. If the party works fast, they're rescuing Gundren from both of them.

### If the party has been warned (hobgoblin messenger from C12 got through)

Vyerith slips behind the northeast door, leaving it ajar. She wants to attack from the rear when the party is engaged with Grol. Grol stands over Gundren and tells the party to leave or the dwarf dies.

**Grol is not bluffing.** He will kill the dwarf if the party doesn't back off. He doesn't care about Gundren — the dwarf is only worth something alive to the Spider, and Grol is already unhappy with the Spider's deal.

**How to break the hostage threat:**
- A ranged attack that drops Grol in one hit before he can act (Zenvon's Sneak Attack is relevant here).
- A distraction that makes Grol flinch (a loud noise, a spell on Snarl rather than Grol, Pip charging at the wolf rather than the king).
- A deal — offer Grol something. He's a king, not an ideologue. If the party offers gold or an alternative prize, he'll consider it. He just wants to come out ahead.

### If the party enters unseen (good stealth, or C12 messenger stopped)

Grol and Vyerith are still arguing. The party has a surprise round.

> **Stage direction:** Open with the sound through the door — "a loud growling voice demanding payment, and a silky, patient reply." Let Nikhil decide whether to burst in or listen for a moment. If he listens: 30 seconds of dialogue, enough to understand there are two parties and a prisoner. Then he chooses.

## Playing King Grol

He is old. He knows it. He was never subtle and age hasn't made him subtle — just slower to swing and faster to end things. He respects strength, despises excuses, and will kill someone mid-sentence if they bore him.

He doesn't care about the Black Spider's larger plan. He cares about his territory, his tribe's dominance, and not being cheated.

If reduced to 15 HP with no clear path to win: he tries to flee. Not surrender — flee. He's survived this long by knowing when a fight is lost.

> **Table line (Grol, if the party gives him an out):**
>
> "The spider promised gold. You're offering me gold. Why should I care which pocket it comes from?"

He's not cruel for the sake of cruelty. He's just a king making business decisions, and his business is violence.

---

## Playing Vyerith

She looks like a drow woman. She sounds patient and reasonable. She is neither drow nor patient.

Vyerith has been managing this situation for two days and is running out of careful options. She's a doppelganger — she can read surface thoughts and has been adjusting her approach to Grol in real time based on what he responds to. She's good at this. She's been doing it for a long time.

**What she'll do in a fight:**
- She attacks from the flank if given the chance.
- If Grol is down, she immediately tries to reach Gundren first — not to protect him, to kill him.
- If she's losing badly, she changes form — not necessarily to a party member (too obvious) but into an unconscious-looking person on the floor to hide. DC 15 Perception to spot her doing it.

**Her tell:** Doppelgangers don't blink the way humanoids do. It's subtle. If Veyra is studying her (Arcana, DC 12), she notices something is wrong with the face. It's too still between expressions.

**If captured:** Vyerith will shift back to a neutral gray humanoid form and say almost nothing. She doesn't plead. She doesn't threaten. She waits. She's been in worse situations and she knows that the most useful thing she can be right now is a closed door.

> **GM-only:** Vyerith knows what Gundren knows — she's been reading his thoughts in his sleep. She knows Wave Echo Cave's location. She will not share this. If pressed with magic (zone of truth), she describes the location in terms so vague as to be useless and technically doesn't lie.

## Gundren

He is unconscious at 0 HP when the party finds him, stable but badly beaten.

**A healing spell brings him to consciousness.** He won't be fighting — he's too injured — but he can walk and talk.

**His first words when he wakes, seeing the party in the king's quarters:**

> "You came." He says it like he didn't entirely expect them to. "Where's Sildar?"

**What he has:** His map is either on his person (Grol hasn't found it yet — he hid it in a boot lining) or in Grol's possession. If Grol is dead, the map is in a lockbox under the bed (DC 10 to find).

**What he'll offer:** Safe escort to Phandalin, 25 gp each for their help, and a 10% share of Wave Echo Cave's yield once the mine is operational. He says this last part with the confidence of a man who fully expects to be running a mine within the month.

> **Stage direction:** Let Gundren have one moment of genuine fragility before the enthusiasm returns. He's been beaten, left unconscious, and held by a goblin king for reasons he doesn't fully understand. He's been scared. He won't say it directly. But there's a second before the dwarf entrepreneur re-assembles where you can see the person underneath. Give Nikhil that beat to respond to if he wants.

**Also in C5 (Storeroom):** Sildar's gear is here — chain mail, shortsword, heavy crossbow, and a longsword with Neverwinter's emblem. None magical, but Sildar will be visibly pleased if the sword is returned to him.

## After the Castle

The party walks out with:
- Gundren, alive (hopefully)
- The map to Wave Echo Cave
- Confirmation: the Black Spider is a drow
- Two leads on the Spider: Glasstaff's letter mentioned "the Forge of Spells," and Gundren knows the Spider's spies were following him before the ambush

**What this unlocks:** Chapter 3 side quests (Agatha, Old Owl Well, Thundertree/Reidoth) and the approach to Wave Echo Cave (Chapter 4). The party should be Level 3 by the time they hit the cave.

---

## Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can we ransom Gundren from Grol instead of fighting?"**
→ Yes. Grol is a businessman. He'll sell Gundren for 500 gp (he opens at 1,000; he'll come down). This removes the fight entirely, but costs money the party may not have. Halia would probably advance them the gold against future services. Sildar would advance them half of it, reluctantly.

**"What happens if we tell Grol that Vyerith was planning to betray him?"**
→ Grol already half-suspected it. He'll look at her. She'll deny it. He'll kill her — not the party — and then resume negotiations with the party. He's a king; treachery from a Spider messenger confirms what he already thought about the Spider's reliability. This is actually a decent play.

**"Can we let Vyerith go?"**
→ Yes. She'll leave quietly and resurface later in a different form, probably as someone the party has met. This is a long-term loose end. Keep track of it.

**"Is the map the only way to find Wave Echo Cave?"**
→ No. Reidoth the druid (in Thundertree) has been watching the area for decades and knows the general location. Agatha the banshee could answer this question if asked properly. The map is the fastest way, but not the only one.

## Stage Directions

- When the party first hears Gundren's voice through the door, it's not screaming or pleading — it's silence. He's unconscious. The voices they hear are Grol's and Vyerith's. The dwarf is just a body in the room.
- When Grol threatens Gundren as a hostage: hold eye contact with Nikhil. Don't fill it with exposition. Just: "He places one hand on the dwarf's neck. What do you do?"
- If Vyerith changes form mid-fight: pause for a second, describe the skin going gray and the features collapsing inward. Let the party register what they're seeing. It's meant to unsettle.
- Gundren waking up: read it slow. He's been unconscious, possibly for days. He doesn't know where he is when he first opens his eyes. Give him a moment before he becomes the eager merchant again.

---

## Open Decisions

- **Vyerith's fate:** Kill her, capture her, or let her go? Each outcome has a different downstream consequence. If she escapes, she becomes a recurring figure. Decide whether you want that thread.
- **The grell in C13:** Worth it for the revivify scroll? That scroll could matter in Wave Echo Cave. If the party isn't clearing C13, consider slipping the scroll into another room's treasure.
- **How did the party find the castle?** Know this before you run the session — it sets the tone of the approach scene. Droop guiding them through the wood feels different from tracking goblin trails for two days.
- **Gundren's emotional register:** The eager entrepreneur who reassembles immediately after trauma is the published version. You can play him that way, or you can let this have marked him more. The latter creates more interesting material with Zenvon long-term.
- **Sildar's gear in C5:** Does the party find it on their own, or does Gundren mention it? Gundren knows it's here — Grol stored it with a taunt about the useless human. If Gundren mentions it, it's a small grace note in an otherwise brutal session.
