# The Morning After the Cave

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for Session 4: Phandalin social web + commit to a next target.

---

## What I understood from the docs (catch me if I'm wrong)

- Zenvon Foreput (Level 2 rogue, halfling, Nikhil's PC) + three Level 2 sidekicks: Pip (fighter), Sister Maela (cleric), Veyra (tiefling mage).
- After Session 3: Cragmaw Hideout cleared, Klarg dead, Sildar rescued (but still weak), party walking back to Phandalin for a long rest.
- Key unresolved: Gundren missing (Cragmaw Castle, location unknown), Black Spider identity unknown, elongated-headed goblin whispered "You're not what Ruxithid wants" (VTT garbled as "Ruth exceeds") — first psionic-goblin breach into the narrative.
- Phandalin NPC queue is fully loaded: Sildar, Elmina, Linene Graywind, Daran Edermath, Sister Garaele, Halia, Harbin Wester, Tuck Stonehill.
- Session ends when the party picks their next dungeon.

---

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

The party arrives in Phandalin, completes their decompression morning, and leaves the session with a target they chose deliberately — either the Redbrand hideout under Tresendar Manor or a plan to find Cragmaw Castle. The secondary win: Veyra connects the elongated-headed goblin to the obelisk plot and is visibly frightened. The mystery has a face now.

## The Default Trajectory (what happens if the party skips everyone and just sleeps)

- Sildar, once recovered, starts quietly asking locals about Iarno Albrek and gets nowhere — Glasstaff has too much influence in town.
- The Red Brands shake down two more merchants before sundown.
- Halia Thornton pays attention to the party from a distance and decides whether they're useful.
- Gundren is still alive at Cragmaw Castle. King Grol hasn't killed him yet — Gundren has the map location in his head and Grol is trying to extract it for the Black Spider.

## The Clever Play (what the optimal party would do)

1. Return the Lionshield Coster provisions immediately — gets Linene Graywind on their side and she tells them about Cragmaw Castle's general location (northeast, the woods around Neverwinter Wood).
2. Talk to Sildar when he's rested — he reveals Iarno Albrek is his missing contact, now probably operating as "Glasstaff" in the Red Brands. Sildar pays 100 gp if they find him. This turns the Redbrand thread into a double-payoff.
3. Talk to Daran Edermath — he tells them about the Old Owl Well undead and confirms the Red Brands are getting bolder and more organized. Offers boots of striding and springing for clearing Old Owl Well.
4. Talk to Sister Garaele — she's been hurt trying to find Agatha the banshee on her own and will offer a reward (three potions of healing) if Zenvon delivers a question to Agatha instead.
5. Visit Halia — she offers 100 gp for Glasstaff's correspondence. The GM knows she wants his Zhentarim contacts, not justice. The party doesn't know this yet.
6. Use Tuck Stonehill's tip about Carp's tunnel — gives them a stealthy entry into the Redbrand hideout instead of the front door.
7. Skip the front door of the Sleeping Giant. The Red Brands there will fight and raise the alarm; the tunnel approach does not.

> **GM-only:** The natural path through Chapter 2 is Redbrand Hideout → find Glasstaff's papers → papers reference Cragmaw Castle's location. That's how the party finds Gundren. If the party wants to skip straight to Cragmaw, they have no map and no specific location. Let them try — tracking is hard, and Linene's "northeast in Neverwinter Wood" is accurate but vague. Let failing to find the castle push them back toward the Redbrand thread organically.

## Scene 1 — Arrival and Long Rest

### Setup
- Evening, Triboar Trail into Phandalin. Sildar is walking under his own power but clearly exhausted.
- The party enters Phandalin as minor local news: people saw them leave to clear the cave; they're back with Sildar Hallwinter, who everyone knew was missing.
- The Stonehill Inn is their destination. Toblen Stonehill will be visibly relieved that Sildar is alive.

### Arrival beat
Toblen meets them at the door. He takes one look at Sildar and does not ask questions — just opens the door wider and calls for hot water.

> **Toblen:** "You found him. I didn't — I didn't ask anyone for odds, but. Good. Come in. Come in. Whatever you need."

Sildar should be gracious but brief. He thanks Zenvon directly:

> **Sildar:** "I'd buy you a drink, but I suspect I'd fall asleep into it. Tomorrow. I owe you a proper conversation, a proper drink, and a proper fee." (He has 50 gp for the escort, which he'll pay in the morning.)

### The night

Nothing eventful. Veyra spends the evening re-reading her notes about the elongated-headed goblin by candlelight. Her blue candle flares — once — when she writes down the phrase. She does not sleep well.

This is worth one sentence at the table, unprompted:

> **Veyra** (to herself, quietly): "It said *Ruxithid.* I think it said *Ruxithid.*"

Play this as a throwaway — Veyra mentioning something to herself while she writes. Do not explain. Let Nikhil ask or let it sit.

### Branch — if Nikhil asks Sildar more about the elongated-headed goblin tonight

Sildar is exhausted and will defer: "Tomorrow. I want to get that right. I don't want to misremember it."

This is the correct answer — it signals the phrase matters without over-selling it.

## Scene 2 — Morning: Phandalin NPC Web

Run these as **player-directed**, not a forced tour. The party decides where to go. Suggest order only as a fallback.

> **Stage direction:** When the party steps out after breakfast, have Pip say: "So. We've got 50 gold from Sildar coming, provisions to return, and a town full of people who've noticed us. Where do we start?" This hands the agenda to Nikhil cleanly.

---

### Beat A: Sildar Hallwinter (Stonehill Inn — morning)

Sildar pays the 50 gp escort fee and opens a conversation.

**What Sildar reveals unprompted:**
- Gundren was taken specifically — it was not a random robbery. Someone told the goblins to grab Gundren and take the map.
- The Black Spider is the name the goblins used for their contact. Sildar doesn't know if it's a person, a title, or something else.
- Sildar's own mission: he was traveling with Gundren to check on Iarno Albrek, a wizard and old contact who had gone to Phandalin weeks ago on Lords' Alliance business and stopped sending reports.

**What Sildar reveals if asked directly about Iarno:**
> "He's young. Eager to prove himself. Thin fellow, always chewing his thumbnail when he's thinking. He went to Phandalin to help restore order. I've heard nothing since. If he's here, something's wrong, or something's happened to him."

> **GM-only:** Iarno IS Glasstaff. Sildar doesn't know. When/if the party connects them, Sildar's face will do all the work you need.

**Sildar's asks:**
- Can you look for Iarno? I'll pay 200 gp if you find him and bring him back safely. (He'll revise this to "bring him to face justice" if he learns the truth.)
- Find out who the Black Spider is. He's the reason Gundren was taken.

**NPC notes — Sildar:**
- Motivation: Find Iarno, help stabilize Phandalin, fulfill his duty to the Lords' Alliance.
- Voice tic: Military directness that softens when he's embarrassed by his own helplessness.
- What he wants from this scene: To feel like he's contributing, not just recovering.
- Small detail: He notices Zenvon is a halfling and adjusts the height of his handshake without thinking. Use this for warmth.

---

### Beat B: Linene Graywind — Lionshield Coster

The Lionshield Coster is a trading post. Linene Graywind, a sharp human woman in her forties, runs it with undisguised competence.

The party has her stolen provisions (the crates marked with the blue lion from Klarg's cave). If they bring them back or even just report where they are:

> **Linene:** "You're serious. You actually went in there." (She eyes the party with recalibrated respect.) "Right. Fifty gold pieces. And if you need weapons, armor, supplies — I'll deal with you fairly."

**If asked about Cragmaw Castle:**
> "I don't know where it is exactly. But the Cragmaw goblins range out of the northeast. Neverwinter Wood, mostly. If Gundren's there, that's the direction. Finding the specific castle —" (she shrugs) "— you'd need a goblin who's willing to talk, or someone who's been there."

**NPC notes — Linene:**
- Motivation: Protect her business and the trade routes. She resents that the Red Brands have made the roads worse.
- Voice tic: Dry, rapid. She doesn't repeat herself.
- What she wants from this scene: For the party to be people she can call on again.

---

### Beat C: Daran Edermath — Edermath Orchard

A retired drow adventurer, well over 500 years old, tending apple trees with the placid cheerfulness of someone who has outlived every major crisis they've seen.

**What Daran has:**
- Old Owl Well quest: Prospectors being driven out by undead. He believes the ruins predate Phandalin — possibly Netherese. He is specifically concerned because undead without a summoner usually means a bound entity that someone disturbed.
- Reward: Boots of striding and springing. He has them on the shelf; he'll hand them over before the party even leaves.

> **Daran (handing over the boots before being asked):** "Take these. Don't argue. I've been holding onto them for a traveler who had better legs than mine. That's you now."

> **Stage direction:** Give Pip a moment here. Daran recognizes something in the human fighter — the look of someone who's protecting someone else instead of looking out for himself. Daran approves of this, quietly.

**What Daran knows about the Red Brands:**
> "They've been here before. Different name, different gang. Tresendar Manor always seems to attract a certain type. Whoever's running them now is smarter than usual — they're organized. They've got someone with a plan."

**If the party mentions the elongated-headed goblin:**
Daran goes still.
> "You said elongated. As in — the skull had changed?" He sets down his pruning knife. "Old Owl Well has things moving beneath it. And now a goblin with a stretched skull on the Triboar Trail. I don't think those are separate problems."

**NPC notes — Daran:**
- Motivation: He knows something is stirring in the region and has been watching it for decades. The party may be the first group he's seen who's already touching multiple threads.
- Voice tic: Measured, patient. He has had centuries of practice not finishing sentences too fast.
- What he wants from this scene: To give the party better tools than he has.

> **GM-only:** Daran is your "the world is bigger than you know" NPC. He won't spell anything out. But if Veyra asks him about psionic magic or the word "Ruxithid," he'll say: "Write that down. Somewhere safe. And don't say it loud around anything old."

---

### Beat D: Sister Garaele — Shrine of Luck

A young elf acolyte of Tymora, returned to the shrine visibly hurt — one arm in a sling, moving carefully.

**What happened (GM-only):** She went alone to the ruins near Conyberry to visit Agatha the banshee — a centuries-old elf who was once a powerful mage. Garaele wanted Agatha to reveal where a spellbook called Bowgentle's spellbook was lost. Agatha sent her away wounded for entering without a proper gift.

**What she'll ask:** She needs someone to go back with a proper offering — a beautiful silver comb (she has it) — and ask Agatha the question on her behalf.
- Reward: Three potions of healing.
- She is embarrassed about being hurt and will not explain what happened in detail unless pushed.

> **Sister Garaele (to Sister Maela specifically):** "I made a mistake. I was in a hurry. I thought devotion was enough." A pause. "It wasn't." She hands the silver comb to Maela. "There's a woman near Conyberry. She's been dead for a long time, but she remembers everything. If someone brings her this — with respect, not urgency — she'll answer one question."

**NPC notes — Garaele:**
- Motivation: She needs the spellbook for research she won't explain yet. She chose to approach Agatha without backup because she was proud.
- Voice tic: Direct and self-correcting. She'll say she was wrong before you have to tell her.
- What she wants from this scene: For someone else to do the errand well, which she couldn't.

---

### Beat E: Halia Thornton — Phandalin Miners Exchange

A composed human woman in her late thirties. The Exchange is a clean, well-lit operation. Halia is the most settled-looking person in Phandalin, which is its own kind of warning.

**Her offer:** She will pay 100 gp for Glasstaff's correspondence — letters, cipher keys, any documents from his personal quarters. Not Glasstaff himself.

> **Halia (to Zenvon specifically):** "The Red Brands are a nuisance. Glasstaff is a liability. I don't need him — I need to know who he's been talking to. That's worth a hundred gold to me." She says this the way someone explains a very reasonable accounting problem.

She will not explain who she works for (Zhentarim) unless the party specifically knows to ask. Even if asked, she denies it cleanly.

**If Zenvon pushes her on the Red Brands' immunity to her business:**
> "I don't pay them and they don't bother me. That's not an alliance. That's risk management."

**NPC notes — Halia:**
- Motivation: Expand Zhentarim influence in Phandalin; Glasstaff's correspondence would give her his Black Spider contact, which she wants.
- Voice tic: Everything she says is slightly more precise than it needs to be.
- What she wants from this scene: To hire the party without revealing what she's actually buying.
- What she fears: That Glasstaff knows she's been watching him and has already moved the correspondence.

> **GM-only:** Halia is not an enemy — yet. She's a parallel faction. The party can work for her, against her, or around her. Her request is not evil on its face. The complication is what she'll do with Glasstaff's Zhentarim contact list once she has it.

---

### Beat F: Tuck Stonehill (optional — if the party talks to the kid at the inn)

He will approach Zenvon specifically if Zenvon has been kind to him.

> **Tuck:** "Carp Alderleaf found a tunnel in the south side of town. It goes into the woods near Tresendar Manor. His ma said not to go near it because the red-cloaks nearly got him. But — I mean — if someone wanted to get in without them seeing you at the door..."

This is the clean entry into the Redbrand Hideout. Reward Nikhil for talking to the kid.

## Scene 3 — The Decision Point

By the time the party has worked through 3-4 NPCs, the hooks converge naturally:

**Reasons to hit Tresendar Manor:**
- Sildar's Iarno quest (200 gp)
- Halia's correspondence quest (100 gp)
- Linene's anger about the Red Brands
- Daran's concern about organization behind the gang
- The Dendrar family (Thel was murdered, wife and children taken — they're in the hideout)
- Tuck's tunnel = no frontal alarm

**Reasons to find Cragmaw Castle:**
- Gundren is there. He might not survive indefinitely.
- The Black Spider wants whatever Gundren knows.
- But: no map, no location, no safe route.

> **Stage direction:** Let the party argue this out loud. If they're paralyzed, have Pip say:
>
> "Look. The castle's out there somewhere in the wood. We don't know where. The manor is right there, end of Main Street. That tunnel's already been found. Feels like the right order."

This should not be a gentle suggestion. Pip says it like someone who has thought it through. He's not wrong.

**If the party chooses Cragmaw Castle tonight anyway:**
Let them go. They'll search Neverwinter Wood, not find anything specific before dark, and either camp overnight (random encounter table, light) or return. The Redbrand thread will still be there. Daran or Linene can give them a better lead the next time they talk.

## Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can we interrogate Sildar about the Black Spider more?"**
→ Yes, but Sildar genuinely doesn't know. He heard the name once, in passing. He doesn't know species, location, or motive. What he knows is that the goblins were acting on orders.

**"What's in the map case that was stolen?"**
→ Gundren's map to Wave Echo Cave. It's at Cragmaw Castle now, in King Grol's possession. The party can eventually recover it — or learn the location another way.

**"Can Veyra study the black stone fragment Zenvon picked up?"**
→ Yes. Veyra can use Nervous Genius: she'll find the stone feels inert but wrong — like it's waiting. Her blue candle gives a faint pulse when she holds both together. She cannot extract more information at Level 2 without a specific tool or location to focus the investigation.

**"What does 'Ruxithid' mean?"**
→ Nobody in Phandalin knows yet. Daran will say "write it down and don't say it loud." Veyra will be quietly terrified. That's all they get for now.

**"Can we just go through the front door of the Sleeping Giant and beat answers out of the Red Brands?"**
→ Yes. Five Red Brands are there (use the module stats). They'll fight, and one will try to flee toward Tresendar Manor to warn Glasstaff. If the alarm reaches him, he has a head start on escape and the bodies move. Let Nikhil make this choice with full knowledge of the risk.

## Stage Directions (timing / silence cues)

- When Sildar pays Zenvon the 50 gp, don't rush to the next beat. Let it sit a second. He's alive because of the party. Sildar knows this.
- When Veyra's candle flares during the night, say it once, quietly. Do not explain. Move on.
- When Daran Edermath goes still at the mention of the elongated skull — hold that beat. He's 500+ years old. Very little makes him pause. This does.
- If the party picks up Tuck's tunnel tip and uses it to plan a quiet entry: give them a moment of satisfaction. This is exactly the kind of thinking rogues should be rewarded for.
- When the session ends with the party committing to a target: don't close it yourself. Ask Nikhil: "So — where are you going?" Let him say it out loud. The commitment is the beat.

## Open Decisions (for you — don't decide without thinking)

- **Gundren's timeline:** How long does King Grol wait before he kills Gundren? The module leaves this vague. You need a number in your head. Suggestion: Grol gave the Black Spider a two-week window. The party is about 4-5 days in. The clock is real but not immediate.
- **The Dendrar family:** Mirna Dendrar and her children are in the Redbrand Hideout (area R12, the holding cells). If the party clears the hideout next session, they'll rescue them. Mirna has a reward hook (emerald necklace at Thundertree). Do you want to play her as traumatized and practical, or terrified and grateful? Pick before the scene.
- **Halia's faction:** How much do you want to signal Zhentarim this early? You can play her as just a smart businessperson and let the reveal happen at Tresendar Manor (where Glasstaff's papers mention the Black Network). Or you can give Veyra an Arcana check that pings Zhentarim sigils. Up to you.
- **"Ruxithid":** Do you want to plant this as Veyra's theory this session, or hold it as a named reveal? If you want her to say it aloud, the dinner scene with her candle is the right moment. If you want to hold it, have her say "I think I heard the word wrong" and don't follow up yet.
- **Old Owl Well timing:** This is a side quest that gets more interesting once the party knows about the obelisk plot. You can run it anytime after this session. It doesn't have to be next.
