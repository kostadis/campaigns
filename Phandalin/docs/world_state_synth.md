*Authoritative current-state synthesis. Later events override earlier. "Open Threads" tracks unresolved questions; do not treat them as settled.*

## QUICK ORIENTATION (current situation)

The party has destroyed the white dragon **Cryovain** and the entity known as **The Carver** at **Icespire Hold**, cleared the Talos ritual at the **Woodland Manse**, and is now broadly converging on a return to **Phandalin** with a stated longer-term destination of **Neverwinter**. The Cult of Talos threat in Neverwinter Wood is largely broken. Note: source dossiers were written from individual perspectives and disagree on precisely where each character is "now" — treat party location as *in transit between Icespire Hold, the Woodland Manse, Falcon's Lodge, and Phandalin*, recently reunited.

## THE PARTY

### Brewbarry (PC)
Goliath nomad; exiled Uthgardt barbarian who no longer claims the barbarian identity. Full name **Brewbarry Root Smasher Ogalakadu** (family, nickname, clan). Companion and bodyguard to Vukradin, whom he respects deeply; was once the only listener to Vukradin's music.
- **Backstory anchor:** Adopted as a lost child by an Uthgardt tribe; grew to love wind/string/chant music but loathes percussion (too like battle). Banished after a sacred grove was desecrated on his watch — the campaign reveals his **core wound**: he was *sober* when blamed, scapegoated for a failure he did not commit, and has lived under the lie of being a drunkard ever since. Drifted to Neverwinter, found Vukradin's music, and stayed. The tribe's wise women taught him about cursed artifacts (he warned the party against the Woodland Manse effigies).
- **Current state:** Alive; last in/near Phandalin, the Woodland Manse, or Falcon's Lodge.
- **Equipment:** Elemental Cleaver (acid-imbued halberd from a shipwreck, magically enlarged); Dragon Slayer Sword (+1, summoned an Invisible Stalker while at rest; used to kill Cryovain); dragon's breath pouch, scales, and one claw from Cryovain. Returned his treasured bathrobe to Falcon's Lodge and mourns it.
- **Abilities:** Rage ("very clear math"); Goliath Stone Endurance; resistance to planar madness; senses stone-giant speech as a chest-frequency.
- **Drives:** Wants a steady legal income to fund a music studio and buy soft comforts (a bathrobe) — symbols of peace after a violent life. Rejects the Order of the Gauntlet as bullies and the Lord's Alliance for destroying his people. Finishes those who stand and fight; refuses to kill fleeing men.

### Valphine (Valphine Sotorra) (PC)
Drow cleric of Lathander; founder/high priestess of her self-made sect, the **Church of the Searing Light**. Former noble of House Sotorra, Menzoberranzan. The party's best liar and tactical/theological anchor.
- **Backstory anchor:** Fifth daughter of Matron Sotorra (priestess of Lolth). Unlike her sadistic sisters, Valphine is a connoisseur of *experiencing* controlled pain, not inflicting it — a disappointment marked for sacrifice by her mother. Escaped during a demon rampage that scattered House Sotorra. On reaching the surface, the searing pain of sunlight converted her to Lathander; blessed as a priestess by Father Jerome of Helm's Hold and sent to spread her unorthodox devotion.
- **Current state:** Alive; traveling with the party, accompanied by her apostle **Prutha**.
- **Equipment:** Holy symbol of Lathander (vessel for *Daylight*); Dust of Disappearance (recovered, used, forgotten, recalled under *Zone of Truth*); a shield held "until the owner turns up" (druidic oak/iron shield with a dislodged Talos bolt and half-orc tallies); musical instrument; newly manifested **golden eyes** that detect deception.
- **Abilities:** *Sacred Flame, Fairy Fire, Daylight, Bless, Silence*; *Choralum Lux Maleficarum* (Lathanderian/Lolthian choral fusion, used to disarm enemies in combat); Underdark interrogation; reads power dynamics and divine/psionic resonance.
- **Theology:** Lathander's light is "not tender" — "power made radiant, pain made holy." The pain of divine failure is itself a sacrament. Believes in transformation through applied pressure, not surface-style redemption.
- **Family loose ends (see Open Threads):** Her sister **Aletra** (interplanar poison smuggler) has appeared in-campaign as an active antagonist.

### Soma (PC)
Tortle druid of Mielikki, ~45 years old. Quiet, patient watcher; the party's grounding moral compass. Now openly an **interventionist**.
- **Backstory anchor:** Spent most of her long life as a coastal hermit doing the minimum to survive, hoping her hatched eggs found her cabin. Trained for one year by the elven druid **Meril**, a natural latecomer to the craft; Meril lamented finding her so late and left her an intricate ash staff. She struck out as a low-cost adventuring guide — a job guiding a passing bard (Vukradin) set her on her path.
- **Current state:** Alive; with the party. (Note: source material inconsistently uses masculine pronouns in early chapters — these are errors; Soma is female.)
- **Equipment:** **Meril's staff** (see object entry — central artifact, leaf-growing, responsive); summoned rat familiar (telepathic link); a secretly taken staff of bird calls (hidden in her fur, considers it useless); tally of stirges killed per party member.
- **Abilities:** *Wild Shape* (bear, wolf spider, giant toad, elk); *Moonbeam, Find Familiar, Mold Earth, Sending*; speaks with animals (spiders only while in spider form); can retreat into her shell to block sound.
- **Drives:** "I want the weight to come off the world a little… the next generation to have ground to stand on." Wants only a teapot as reward. Sees constructs and planar intrusions as abominations but accepts intervention to prevent greater horror. Considers herself done with Adabra, who exploited her trust.
- **Bonds:** Quiet bond with Boney; adored by Xanthopoulos.

### Vukradin (PC)
Celestial-touched bard; moral conscience of the party, reluctant revolutionary, self-appointed restorer of stolen property. (Name appears variantly as Vukardin/Vucrodin/Vucravinios in sources — all the same character.)
- **Backstory anchor:** Quit a gig at the First Flophouse over moral dissonance with sanctioned "treasure recovery" looting; set out to earn "honest coin." Befriended by Brewbarry (his devoted listener) and guided by Soma. His celestial origin and personal narrative were valued as an asset by the Order of the Gauntlet.
- **Current state:** Alive; with the party near Icespire Hold, en route to Phandalin.
- **Allegiance:** No longer a formal Order of the Gauntlet member (earlier "associate member" status has lapsed); operates on a personal moral code; loose ties to the Church of Lathander via Valphine.
- **Equipment:** Clarinet (often held, rarely played); necklace of fireballs (held for an unknown owner); the "Bluetooth sword" (obsidian blade w/ mental amplifier crystal, looted from the Carver, refuses to keep); "conflict-free" Boots of Elvenkind; wooden labyrinth maze (from Falcon's drawer); a brass ring (documented, not taken, possibly from Iymrith's hoard).
- **Abilities:** Universal Speech; *Hold Person, Silence, Cloud of Daggers, Phantasmal Force, Light, Vicious Mockery, Command*; Bardic Inspiration; recently learned shapeshifting/transformation/planar-door workings.
- **Drives:** Build a "conflict-free" music studio. Believes all money is blood money unless earned through restitution and non-violence; refused the dragon's hoard ("scratched-off serial numbers"). Privately admits he now plays for fans, not purity, and fears it. Returned a six-gold debt (with interest) to Adabra by post.
- **Key rifts:** Estranged from Falcon (post-betrayal at the Druid Council; Falcon has since sent an unconditional apology); distrusts Jenna Roscoe and Ser Kaelen (suspects Kaelen mirrors his vocabulary manipulatively).

## CORE ALLIES & COMPANIONS (current traveling/affiliated NPCs)

### Prutha
Orc zealot and cult leader of Valphine's Church of the Searing Light; her chosen apostle and bodyguard ("I make things safe for Cleric of Lathander"). Alive, at the Woodland Manse with Valphine. Wields a greataxe; carries looted relics (candelabra, bathrobe — entrusted to Valphine). Converted after Valphine's radiant light burned parasites from her arm; driven by the memory of her slain shaman great-uncle ("Dawn comes for everyone, even those born in shadow"). Has claimed kinship with the Eastern Heart banner people.

### Boney
Sentient undead skeletal horse bound to Soma; her tactical asset. Carries a moving **silence enchantment** that suppresses spellcasting (used to disrupt Anchorite casters). Survived a direct lightning strike. Believes it was raised to guard **Lady Alagondar's sword** and aid her quest — but Soma is not Lady Alagondar, and the sword's location is unconfirmed (possible incomplete memory/delusion). Last seen in combat with Soma.

### Vorga ("Long-Memory")
Warchief of the allied orc contingent; alive, escorting the party. Protective of Falcon, Soma, and Brewbarry (gestured "mine" after a War Cry granting them advantage). Wears Falcon's hunter cloak. Bears an old jaw scar and badly-healed nose. Formerly lived in a home once held by "a Dragon" and seeks to return there. Possible deep ties to the concept of "Dawn."

### Xanth / Xanthopoulos
Centaur guide and informant (the two names refer to the same being). Alive at the bend of the Triboar Trail toward Phandalin. Former close friend of **Meril**; reveres Soma with near-worshipful adoration ("this one was blessed by Meril… she will do great things"); recognizes Vukradin's true nature. Holds deep, partly-withheld knowledge of Meril's staff and of histories older than Phandalin. Asserts Meril *chose* Soma deliberately. Knows the Carver's lieutenants (Borg [dead], Sister Kaella, Zax). Views Talos as a "lunatic god."

### Falcon / Falcon the Hunter / "The Falcon"
Alive; reclaimed his estate (formerly the orc-held Woodland Manse area) from the Talosians and styles himself its lord, retraining three orc stewards. Hosts at his lodge under the alias **"the Dragon Slayer."** Has reconciled with Vukradin (unconditional apology via *Sending*; agreed to gift a musical instrument as restitution). Repudiated his former anti-interventionist alignment with Adabra and now embraces decisive action with joy.
- **Backstory truth:** Never a dragon slayer — arrived after a dragon was already slain, sole survivor of his party, looted a **brass ring** and gold scraps from **Iymrith's hoard** as seed capital. Lost his lodge twice (once to fire, once to nature), which spawned his earlier anti-interventionist "liturgy" of *intervention, consequence, loss*. Never actually owned the Woodland Manse.

## PHANDALIN-AREA POWERS & GOVERNANCE

### Phandalin (location)
Thriving, celebrated, undefended (no walls/garrison). Saved from the Carver's planned destruction. Governed in practice by **Harbin Wester** as Townmaster/Lord Mayor under Lord Neverember, via a **Privy Council** (members include Vukradin, Tolbin, Elmer, Halia, Linene, Adabra). A pre-written press release and ceremonial welcome await the party's return. Overlooks Icespire Hold. Stonehill Inn (Toblen Stonehill, proprietor) is the party's social hub; Vukradin performs there. Lionshield Coster run by Linene Graywind (reduced operations since the War of the Giants).

### Harbin Wester
Alive in Phandalin. Townmaster loyal to Lord Neverember; claims political credit for the dragon's defeat and the town's salvation. Uses bureaucracy as leverage; ceded partial authority via the Privy Council under Vukradin's pressure. Brother of Tibor Wester (a connection that alarmed Vukradin — significance unexplained).

### Ser Kaelen Thorne (Order of the Gauntlet)
Alive in Phandalin; liaison and moral authority for the Order. (Appears as "Ser Kaelen," "Ser Kaelen Thorne," and "Ser Kaelen Thorn" — single individual.) Publicly defends Vukradin as a full member and praises his initiative — a claim Vukradin himself and Jenna dispute. Recruited Vukradin into the Order, confirming the Order's funding as "conflict-free gold via donations." Sent an urgent *Sending* about "Issues with the Emerald Enclave," mirroring the party's exact vocabulary ("conflict-free triumph") — Vukradin suspects deliberate manipulation. Identified a lighthouse statue as belonging to **Elara 'Seasong' Meliamne** and demands its return. Opposes Emerald Enclave philosophy; suspects psionic entities behind the Cult of the Kraken. Watches Soma's staff with calculating interest.

### Order of the Gauntlet (faction)
Active but depleted after successive wars (Dragons, Giants). HQ presence in Phandalin. Claims a mission of moral betterment through art and arms; uses Vukradin's music as messaging. Funded in part by **House Margaster** — a fact in tension with its "conflict-free gold" claim and a basis for Adabra's accusations of hypocrisy. Milo Goodbarrel (former strike-force leader vs. a dragon ritual) — status unknown.

### Emerald Enclave (faction)
Active, internally divided over whether planar corruption and "monstrous" orcs are natural phenomena or require intervention. Doctrine: the multiverse is one unified thing without inherent good/evil; "interventionist" is the gravest accusation. **Lyra** restored to full standing; **Adabra** and **Falcon** were aligned against intervention (Falcon has since defected). Receiving urgent reports of planar disruptions, mind-manipulating societies, and the Cult of Talos.

### Adabra
Alive in Phandalin (relocated her shop next to Garaele's house, across from the Shrine of Luck). Emerald Enclave naturalist; leading a campaign to ostracize Lyra and discredit interventionist druids, using the treant's death in the Whispering Wood as central evidence. Publicly denounced and outvoted by the council but unrepentant ("far from done"). Severed ties with Vukradin (whom she views as a traitor for joining the Order) and now labels Soma an "interventionist." Believes intervention of any kind catalyzes cosmic imbalance — an absolute, inflexible conviction. Holds the windmill deed and five conflict-free gold pieces.

### Lyra
Alive, low-ranking Emerald Enclave member (earlier "leader" framing overridden), restored to full standing. Witness/reporter on the Whispering Grove rift; praised Vukradin's council oratory. Led the failed ritual to cleanse the corrupted Treant; now reports failures without shame, morally committed to intervention. Offered to help the party contact Xanth.

### Thomas
Alive, Emerald Enclave member sworn alongside Marian. Reported Adabra's move against Lyra to Vukradin; failed (under Lyra's direction) to contain the metastasized treant; confirmed the party killed it. Respects Enclave protocol over Lyra's unauthorized methods.

## KEY LOCATIONS

### Icespire Hold
Mountain fortress: two stone-and-ice structures joined by a bridge. Main approach blocked by a 20-ft rock slide beside a 200-ft sheer ice wall; a less-obvious side entrance exists. Interior bears old earthquake damage and deliberately shattered (not weathered) stone. Site of Cryovain's captivity, the Carver's ritual, and both their deaths. Now unoccupied; the party emerged into view of it "from the Overbright" (nature of the Overbright undefined). Cryovain's corpse remains on the roof, being harvested.

### Woodland Manse
An hour's walk from Falcon's Hunting Lodge. Crumbling two-story stone manor, severely damaged and ritual-scarred. Cleared of its Talosian/orc occupants by the party; the Talos summoning ritual was disrupted (though Gorthok emerged prematurely). Features: boar-head portico, vine-choked courtyard with a central summoning tree and well, collapsed staircase (deliberately collapsed by the party; gives way over 50 lbs), copper rods in geometric ritual patterns, and **cursed twig effigies shaped like the party members**. Contains the Boots of Elvenkind (party objective). Marked "Active" on the map fragment.

### Falcon's Lodge / Falcon's Hunting Lodge
Built atop a dead dragon's remains, deep in Neverwinter Wood; ~5 miles from the party's recent position. Operated by Falcon as "the Dragon Slayer." Now damaged after battle: missing front door ("open-air concept"), scorched timbers, lightning-blasted gate, and a **wine cellar with every cask destroyed**. Orcs reassigned here have embraced their new roles. Was once intended as a music venue for Vukradin and Fina. (The "Siege of Falcon's Hunting Lodge" — refugee orcs defending the burned-but-standing structure — has resolved into Falcon's reclaimed estate.)

### Circle of Thunder
Ring of ancient standing stones atop a 90-ft hill, altered by Talosian hands (humming stones, unnatural inward-circling storm clouds, a deer with its heart replaced by black glassy material). A **decoy** site, X'd on the map fragment *in advance*. Cleared by the party; a boy from the East was recovered here.

### Dragonbarrow
30-ft dragon burial mound with a hidden stone-plug entrance and a 2-ft passage. Sarcophagi release corrosive dust; haunted by will-o'-wisps at night. The party retrieved the Dragon Slayer Sword here (guarded by an Invisible Stalker, now destroyed). Lady Alagondar's ghost appeared in the northern chamber.

### Neverwinter (location)
The party's confirmed destination. Active quests: return a stolen statue to an art collector before House Markaster intervenes; inform the Order of the Gauntlet they are to be thanked; meet **Elara 'Seasong' Meliamne** to unlock loot. Has a bureaucrat who can produce documents asserting Crown ownership of the frozen dragon treasure. A now-defunct interplanar shipping device was once used by merchants here. A venue for Vukradin's performances.

### Other locations (current state)
- **Butterskull Ranch** — Destroyed and burning (Borg the Hammer's raid). Famed for Petunia's cheese; a chained prisoner's fate unknown.
- **Logger's Camp** — Abandoned, deserted; south shore of the river.
- **Stonehill Inn** — Operational, Phandalin; run by Toblen Stonehill.
- **Triboar Trail** — Active route, unusually deserted; connects Phandalin east (Butterskull Ranch, Dragonbarrow) and west (Conyberry). Designated rendezvous with Xanth at the Phandalin bend.
- **Axelholm** — Site of the out-of-phase dwarf nightmare; a possible refuge/prison.

## RESOLVED THREATS (dead / destroyed / departed)

- **Cryovain (the white dragon)** — Dead at Icespire Hold; killed by Valphine's divine light after being weakened in combat (Brewbarry's Dragon Slayer Sword central to the fight). Was held captive, magically violated (nose/eyes), and dominated by the Carver's sword. Corpse being harvested. *(Note: the generic "white dragon" and "dragon" dossiers describe the same creature; treat as one. The dragon is dead.)*
- **The Carver** — Dead/unmade at Icespire Hold; the true entity was a sentient black obsidian sword wearing a human vessel, communicating telepathically in a pain-inducing tongue. Dissipated when the summoning circle was destroyed, simultaneous with Cryovain's death. Its sword is the "Bluetooth sword" Vukradin now holds and refuses to keep.
- **Sister Kayla / Sister Kaella** — Likely the same individual (spelling varies); former Carver Spymaster turned independent. Defected from the Carver, was captured and revived by Valphine, warned the party of the Carver's plan (bind the white worm at Icespire, burn Phandalin), then vanished — "gone to the wind." Alive but location unknown; Vukradin treats her survival as a strategic failsafe ("as long as Sister Kaella's still breathing, you're good").
- **Corbin** — Dead. Former Carver-gang member turned party aide; was guarding Sister Kaella when he died (cause/location/culprit unknown). A grieving widower and father (of Greenmeadow) who joined bandits out of desperation. His death was widely known on the party's return to Phandalin.
- **Gorthok** — Released prematurely from the Woodland Manse ritual; a primal, lightning-wielding tusked beast immune to Moonbeam, uncharmable. Last advancing through the courtyard (the party engaged it during the manse assault).
- **Anchorites of Talos** — The manse anchorite was destroyed by Valphine's psychic darkness/fog. Talosian forces in the region are broken.
- **Twig blights** — All destroyed (animated from burned effigies; obliterated by Valphine and Brewbarry).
- **Manticore** — Dead (windmill, killed by Brewbarry's halberd).
- **Invisible Stalker** — Destroyed at Dragonbarrow (guarded the Dragon Slayer Sword).
- **Will-o'-wisps** — Destroyed at Dragonbarrow; previously killed Boney (its first death) and Soma's rat familiar.
- **Ogres / Mimic** — Multiple individuals defeated across the campaign (valley ogre, mine ogres; mimic last seen wounded and hostile).
- **Cult of Talos (faction)** — A contagious "outbreak" of divine madness recruiting displaced orcs/half-orcs (shapeshifters, lightning-wielders) centered on the Woodland Manse. Largely shattered by the party's recent actions; cells may persist elsewhere.

## SIGNIFICANT OBJECTS

### Meril's Staff / "the staff"
Soma's central artifact, given by **Meril**. (Dossiers "staff," "the staff," and "Meril's Staff" all describe this one object; leaf-count reports conflict across chapters — see Open Threads.) Grows leaves over time; warm to the touch; heavier than when first received; "talks" to Soma ahead of her senses; responds to her presence and emotional state; detects unnatural/planar elements and revealed psychic residue on phasing rocks. Holds the property of **Resonance** and deep symbolic weight among druids ("standing"). Xanth knows more of its history than Soma does. Most recent reports: concealed in Soma's fur and "disappeared" from sight; a later mention of Soma picking up a *plain* staff is unexplained.

### The "Bluetooth sword"
Obsidian blade with a mental amplifier crystal — the vessel-sword that was **the Carver**. Looted by Vukradin, who refuses to keep it and intends to return it to its "true owner." Known to be level-draining; actively avoided when not strictly needed.

- **Dragon Slayer Sword** — +1 sword wielded by Brewbarry; killed Cryovain.
- **Brewbarry's halberd (Elemental Cleaver)** — acid-imbued, magically enlarged, sharpened.
- **The shield** — druidic oak/iron, with a dislodged Talos bolt and dual half-orc tallies (exterior: five marks; interior: four notches + a slash). Held by Valphine, owner unidentified.
- **The talisman** — dark iridescent object bearing both Talos's lightning bolt and an inverted tentacle-eye (octopus/Kraken iconography); emits divine + psionic energy and a heartbeat thrum. Recovered from Moesko's chamber. Suggests syncretic Talos/Kraken corruption.

## OPEN THREADS & MYSTERIES

### A. The Kraken / Talos / planar-corruption nexus
- The **talisman** fuses Talos and Kraken (octopus/tentacle-eye) iconography — what links the storm cult to the Cult of the Kraken? Ser Kaelen suspects powerful psionic entities behind the Kraken Society.
- **Teega the Smith** and the Kraken Society's "cleansing" of minds — what is "the current"? Is the Kraken literal, metaphor, or a collective consciousness?
- Rimardo & Corrin's cards depict a **drowned coastal town and gate** (Kraken imagery) and the party is told they'll "have context" in **Leilon** — a likely future location/threat.
- The Carver's sword domination of Cryovain: was the obsidian sword's essence fully unmade, or does it linger in the "Bluetooth sword" Vukradin now carries?

### B. Valphine's House Sotorra legacy
- **Aletra** is loose across the planes as a poison-smuggler and active antagonist; the planar machine, its package, and "the boss" remain unresolved.
- Backstory loose ends still in play: **Matron Sotorra** still plans to sacrifice Valphine to Lolth; **Brundar/Grundar Quartzvein** seek revenge on House Sotorra; **Tarranta** (1st daughter) schemes; **Notir** (6th daughter) is missing, possibly tied to **House Margaster** (which also funds the Order of the Gauntlet — a possible collision). **Father Jerome** watches Valphine's "experiment" unfold.
- Valphine's **golden eyes** — divine transformation, Dust of Disappearance side effect, or theological breakthrough? Origin unexplained.

## CANON TIMELINE (chronological reference)

1. **Origins** — Vukradin quits the First Flophouse to earn honest coin; sets out adventuring. Soma, a coastal hermit recently trained by Meril, takes work as a guide and is hired by Vukradin. Brewbarry (exiled Uthgardt) joins from Neverwinter. Valphine, having fled Menzoberranzan and converted to Lathander at Helm's Hold, joins.
2. **Phandalin established** — The party bases at Stonehill Inn; meets Harbin, the Townmaster, Linene, Toblen. White dragon Cryovain attacks Phandalin and is driven off by Vukradin's illusion. Manticore slain at the windmill.
3. **Order of the Gauntlet** — Ser Kaelen recruits Vukradin (later disputed); the Order's funding tied to House Margaster surfaces.
4. **Lighthouse / Moesko** — The talisman with Talos+Kraken iconography is recovered.
5. **Carver's trap** — Butterskull Ranch destroyed by Borg the Hammer; Big Al rescued; Skippy captured and reveals Sister Kayla as the Carver's Spymaster.
6. **Falcon revealed** — At his hunting lodge, Falcon's fraudulent "dragon slayer" persona and the Iymrith brass ring are exposed; he espouses interventionism, then anti-interventionism after losing his lodge.
7. **Mountain's Toe Mine** — Wererat Whiskered Gang (Zeleen) brought under Valphine's authority; Stonetallow dwarves (Horia) negotiate; Don-Jon Raskin's scheme and reported death (carrion crawler).
8. **Planar machine** — Aletra (Valphine's sister) exposed smuggling drow poison; the Chief Accountant reveals the scheme and offers the override code; out-of-phase dwarves at Axelholm.
9. **Dragonbarrow** — The party retrieves the Dragon Slayer Sword; destroys the Invisible Stalker and will-o'-wisps (Boney's first death); Lady Alagondar's ghost appears.
10. **Whispering Wood / treant** — Lyra's cleansing ritual fails; the treant dies amid Talosian blight; Adabra uses this to launch her anti-interventionist crusade and ostracize Lyra.
11. **Druid Council** — Adabra denounces interventionism and is outvoted; Falcon initially sides with her then repudiates it; Vukradin's oratory praised; Soma labeled an interventionist.
12. **Cult of Talos rises** — Woodland Manse becomes a Talos ritual site; Circle of Thunder set up as a decoy; Anchorites, shapeshifting orcs, and lightning-wielders proliferate.
13. **The Carver's plan** — Sister Kaella defects, is revived by Valphine, and reveals the Carver intends to bind the worm (Cryovain) at Icespire Hold and burn Phandalin. Borg confirmed dead.
14. **Corbin's death** — While guarding Sister Kaella; Sister Kayla/Kaella vanishes "to the wind."
15. **Icespire Hold climax** — The Carver dominates the captive, magically-violated Cryovain via the obsidian sword and performs a summoning ritual. The party intervenes: Brewbarry's Dragon Slayer Sword and Valphine's divine light kill Cryovain; the summoning circle's destruction unmakes the Carver. Vukradin refuses the hoard and takes the obsidian "Bluetooth sword" to return it.
16. **Circle of Thunder cleared** — Decoy ritual dismantled; a boy from the East recovered.
17. **Woodland Manse assault** — Party clears the manse: destroys the anchorite (Valphine's psychic darkness), the twig blights, and engages the prematurely-released Gorthok; collapses the staircase; the ritual fails. Cursed party-effigies left behind. Boots of Elvenkind sought within.
18. **Falcon's reclamation & reconciliation** — Falcon retakes the estate from Talosians, becomes "lord" over orc stewards, embraces interventionism, and sends Vukradin an unconditional apology (with an instrument promised as restitution via Valphine).
19. **Vorga's alliance** — Allied orcs under Warchief Vorga aid and escort the party.
20. **Rimardo & Corrin** — The uncanny performers arrive at Falcon's Lodge (now "the Dragon Slayer," wine cellar destroyed), deliver cryptic theatrical prophecy, point toward Leilon, and depart leaving an unsettling invoice.
21. **Aftermath / present** — Cryovain's corpse harvested; the party converges toward Phandalin (Ser Kaelen and a celebration await) with Neverwinter as the longer destination. Horia reports a promising new gold vein at the mine. Don-Jon Raskin resurfaces as the subject of a soul-recall working.
