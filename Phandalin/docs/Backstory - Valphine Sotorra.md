Valphine Sotorra – unorthodox devotee of the Light.

[https://www.dndbeyond.com/characters/144295357](https://www.dndbeyond.com/characters/144295357)
Matron Sotorra was a moderately successful priestess of Lolth, not successful enough to eliminate all rivals, yet strong enough to avoid being eliminated. Her most famous accomplishment was putting down an uprising of Svirfneblin slaves ten years ago, where she painstakingly tortured the slaves until she identified Brundar Quartzvein as the leader and had her sent to the Demonweb pits.

The true leader was Brundar’s brother, Grundar Quartzvein, a well-kept secret taken to the graves of his supporters. After evading identification as the leader, but still remaining enslaved, he went to ground and continued patiently planning his next rebellion, and his revenge against house Sotorra.

Like many houses of Menzoberranzan, Matron Sotorra maintained the infighting valued by Lolth, ensuring that the strongest thrive, and the weakest are eliminated. She engaged in trade, betrayal, raids of more goodly races’ settlements for slaves, and training warriors to advance Lolth’s ambitions. 

She also raised six daughters. The eldest daughter Tarranta proudly follows in her mother’s footsteps, and has sought to advance house Sotorra. The second and third daughters were slain by Tarranta after demonstrating a surprising degree of collaboration attempting to eliminate her from the position of heir of house Sotorra. 

Aletra,  fourth daughter, exposed the scheme of her older sisters. This earned her the appreciation of Tarranta, and the role leading house Sotorra’s budding interplanar trade operation, which uses interplanar storage devices to transport and sell potent drow poisons to the underworld operations of the “Goodly races”.

Valphine Sotorra, fifth daughter, was versed enough in drow intrigue to survive her sisters’ casual assassination attempts, but lacked her sisters’ glee for mayhem (e.g. she was not chaotic evil, she is closer to neutral or chaotic neutral) Her sisters would take great pleasure in inflicting pain and torture on others, but Valphine was more inwardly focused as a connoisseur of experiencing controlled pain. As such, she became quite the disappointment of her mother in recent years, and the subject of a more deliberate plan to be sacrificed by her mother to the benefit of Lolth and the advancement of the house. 

Luckily for Valphine, before this plan could come to fruition, a demon rampage swept through the city of Menzoberranzan, crushing several of the great houses who miscalculated the power of the demons, causing wiser houses like Sotorra to flee, and allowing many slaves (including Grundar Quartzvein) to escape across the underdark. Notir Sotorra, the sixth daughter, remains missing.

Valphine was separated from her house during this invasion, and eventually wound her way for the first time to the surface. When she did, she was met with blinding searing pain from the light of sky. In amazement of the marvelous source of pain, she discovered the Sun God Lathander, and opened a new chapter in her life focused on experiencing more of the strange and gloriously painful brightness of the surface.

After this revelation, she wandered into Helm’s Hold, located two days travel from Neverwinter. The town guard was certainly alarmed to see a drow, but since she was alone and nonthreatening they merely kept a suspicious eye on her. She was drawn by her budding discipleship to Lathander to the small temple in town, where she was received, identified, and blessed as a new priestess of Lathander by Father Jerome. Father Jerome, pleased with his apparently successful conversion of a drow to the worship of Lathander, kindly extended his connections to Valphine and sent her on her way to spread her true but unorthodox devotion to the Sun God.

Character traits:
- Life is pain. Anyone who says differently is selling something.
- More interested in experiencing pain than inflicting it, but certainly capable of protecting herself.
- Fascinated with bright light.
- Has an uncanny eye for detecting intrigue, especially when it means ill for her.
- Quirk: With her new powers as a cleric, discovering the ability to stab her own leg and then heal it.

Available loose ends:
- Brundar Quartzvein: Still seeks his revenge against Matron Sotorra and all of her house.
- Matron Sotorra: Still plans to deliver Valphine as an offering to Lolth, restore and revive the Drow houses.
- 1st daughter Tarranta Sotorra: Still seeks to rise one day as the leader of house Sotorra, and make a name for herself through unknown plans.
- 4th daughter Aletra Sotorra: Was not in town during the demon rampage, and may be encountered across the cosmos as a peddler of exotic poisons.
- 6th daughter Notir Sotorra: Missing, presumed captured by the demons and may have made new allegiances. Perhaps with house Margaster.
- Father Jerome: Fascinated by this strange new disciple of Lathander, and willing to use his network to see the experiment unfold.
