### Character Backstory
Brewbarry Rootsmasher Ogolakanu was born on a wind scoured cliffside where most children learned to walk by clinging to boulders. Brewbarry learned by headbutting them. From the start, he treated every moment as a contest — not because he wanted to win, but because he couldn't imagine life any other way. If someone climbed a ridge, he climbed two. If someone carried a goat, he carried the goat and the herder. If someone drank a mug of ale, he drank the keg.

His clan admired his drive… at first. But Brewbarry's competitiveness didn't stop at trials or hunts. It seeped into everything. Eating became a race. Sleeping became a challenge ("I bet I can fall asleep faster than you"). Even meditation — a sacred Goliath practice — became a competition he loudly declared himself champion of. And then there was the debauchery.

Brewbarry believed that victory meant nothing without celebration, and celebration meant nothing without excess. He threw feasts so wild that the echoes startled mountain goats for days. He invented drinking games that left even seasoned warriors crawling. He once convinced the entire hunting party to skinny dip in a freezing glacial lake "to prove who had the strongest spirit." Half the tribe caught colds. Brewbarry declared himself the winner.

The elders eventually realized that Brewbarry's spirit was too big, too loud, and far too intoxicated for the quiet discipline of the peaks. They didn't exile him — they simply suggested, very firmly, that the world below offered "greater challenges and sturdier taverns."

Brewbarry took this as a blessing. Now he roams the lowlands with a simple creed:
- Life is a competition.
- Glory is earned through effort.
- And every victory deserves a celebration so wild it becomes legend.

He measures friendships in shared battles and shared hangovers. He respects anyone who can outfight him, outdrink him, or out party him. And he seeks challenges not for pride, but because the challenge is the point.

### Additional Notes (Feature: The Subconscious Rage — Barbarian Physiological Mutation, Path of the Giant)

The lingering trauma of your Uthgardt exile has violently mutated your biology. Your subconscious has realized that you were not merely careless in the past; you were surgically neutralized by unnatural trickery. Your body has built a permanent biological firewall against "Seelie condescension" and any magic that attempts to bypass your physical might.

**The Phantom Wings (Narrative Manifestation):** Your paranoia bleeds into your auditory processing. Whenever magic is cast near you, you hear the phantom, high-pitched buzzing of fairy wings echoing in your skull.

**Agentic Resonance (The Primal Radar):** Your paranoia weaponizes your senses. You gain a permanent +4 bonus to Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Insight) checks made specifically to spot unseen or flying threats, or to inspect food and drink for magical tampering. You also add a +4 bonus to Wisdom saving throws made to resist Enchantment magic or the Charmed condition.

**Cosmic Static (The Dietary Rejection):** Your mutated physiology completely rejects magically conjured sustenance. Spells like Goodberry or Create Food and Water provide you zero nourishment and zero healing; to your palate, they taste of ash and trigger visceral memories of your exile. You must rely entirely on hunted or mundane rations.

**Cosmic Static (The Biological Audit):** Your body treats all druidic and fey magic as a disguised sedative. To willingly accept a beneficial spell (such as Cure Wounds or Haste) from a druid or fey, you must expend your Reaction to consciously suppress your primal, anti-magic firewall. If your Reaction is already spent, your body instinctively rejects the magic; the caster must succeed on a Spellcasting Ability Check against your passive Constitution score (10 + your Constitution modifier) to force the magic through your paranoid musculature.

**The Uthgardt Rejection (Threshold Override):** Your rage completely overwrites your biology. You are permanently immune to magical sleep, whether you are currently raging or not.

Your body is mutating because your mind has realized the world is a trap. If you want this new biological firewall to grow stronger, you must play into that realization. The cosmos will reward you when you act on these four primal instincts:

**Trust the Mud, Reject the Spark:** The magic of the fey or those who claim to speak for nature—is inherently manipulative. Real survival comes from blood, sweat, and mundane iron. When you rely on your own brutal resilience instead of their easy magic, your instincts sharpen.

**Perfection is a Predator:** The natural world is flawed and messy. When you encounter someone or something that operates with flawless synchronization, perfect manners, or emotionless logic, it is a predator wearing a mask. Trust your gut over their words.

**The Sky is for Cowards:** The creature that ruined your life attacked from where you couldn't reach. The sky is the domain of tricksters and snipers. Whenever you force a flying or elusive enemy to fight you in the mud on your terms, your primal dominance asserts itself.

**The Hammer is the Only Truth:** Performative diplomacy, bureaucracy, and bureaucratic lies are just high-society magic. When you bypass the 'polite' rules of engagement and use your physical presence to smash through a deception, you remind the world that raw strength cannot be lied to.
