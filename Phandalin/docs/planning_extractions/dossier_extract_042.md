## Vukradin

Vukradin was present for the approach to the Circle of Thunder and participated in planning the assault on the ritual. Observing two half-orc anchorites performing a silent dancing ritual surrounded by twelve twig blights, he worked through the tactical situation with the group. He identified with Valphine that the ritual had no vocal component, meaning his planned Silence spell would be ineffective. He pivoted to Hold Person as an alternative and won initiative, acting first in the encounter. During the battle he successfully cast Hold Person on both anchorites simultaneously, freezing them mid-dance and ending the ritual's progress. He narrated his own success with satisfaction: "Stopping a man in his tracks — both men — that is a very powerful thing."

After the battle, Vukradin moved to collect the holy symbols of Talos from the fallen anchorites, arguing on principle that the symbols should be returned to whoever owned them. He was met with Prutha's counter-logic — that the dead anchorites were the rightful owners, so the items had been returned — and could not fully rebut it. He challenged Brewbarry's remark about reasoning with orcs, asking whether it was racist, and received the clarification that Brewbarry found orcs dumb rather than evil, which Vukradin appeared to find only marginally better. He watched as Prutha and Valphine destroyed the symbols before any resolution was reached. His comment — "God. Valfina, we need to have a conversation about conflict-free goals" — reflected ongoing frustration with the group's tendency toward chaos over principle.

In the caves beneath the henge, Vukradin descended with Brewbarry, Valphine, and Prutha. He observed the cave environment skeptically, calling it "more dungeon than refrigerator." He was present when Brewbarry encountered and insulted the boar in the first cave, precipitating its charge. Vukradin subsequently insulted the boar himself, contributing to the escalation, and defended the outcome afterward: "It was coming right for us."

In Narux's cave — the smallest of the three — Vukradin reviewed the vellum sketches of Gorthok alongside the others and examined the map fragment that Narux had prepared. He noted that the Circle of Thunder was already X'd through on the map, indicating the cult had anticipated losing it. He identified the Woodland Manse as active and Falcon's Hunting Lodge as the convergence point of the entire cult operation. He crossed the completed Circle of Thunder objective off his running list, noting the next two sites were both marked in red — red because he had not yet received an apology from Falcon the Hunter.

He then worked through the accountability question aloud: the party had failed to address the Woodland Manse before the orcs attacked Falcon's lodge, and someone had apparently died as a result. He said "We didn't deal with that" and attempted to contextualize it — "It can't be in five places at once, you know? There's only so much we can do" — but was visibly uncomfortable with the conclusion. He noted Valphine had already laid groundwork with Falcon at the druid conclave, proposing a musical instrument as a gesture of good faith, which Vukradin had not known about and acknowledged with a note of gratitude.

He was unaware throughout the cave exploration that Valphine had found a hidden vial of potent defensive magic and tucked it into his pack without telling him. He carries it now.

Current status: Alive, with the party, departing the Circle of Thunder. Planning to proceed to the Woodland Manse and then Falcon's Hunting Lodge.

## Brewbarry

Brewbarry participated in the assault on the Circle of Thunder from the outset. He observed the ritual alongside the group, watching the silent dancing anchorites and twig blights from the treeline, and contributed tactical commentary — calling the anchorites "a pantomime club" when it was established the ritual had no vocal component.

During the battle he entered a rage state and engaged the twig blights with handaxe throws, using his Elemental Cleaver ability to retrieve thrown axes. His first throw went wide, but subsequent attacks cleared multiple blights. He tracked the battlefield actively and moved to engage the anchorites once Vukradin had frozen them with Hold Person.

After the battle, Brewbarry examined the bodies of the fallen anchorites and noted the distinctive tattoos on each: one bore storm clouds and spiraling open eyes up his neck, the other had crossed boar tusks down both forearms. He was engaged by Vukradin in the debate over the holy symbols, offering the remark "You're trying to reason with an orc" and clarifying under questioning that he considered orcs dumb rather than evil, citing himself as a comparative baseline. He made no strong objection as Prutha and Valphine destroyed the symbols.

In the caves, Brewbarry led the descent and drove the exploration with the stated goal of finding drinks. He discovered the first cave contained a live boar and announced it optimistically as evidence of barbecue preparation. He then watched as Vukradin insulted the boar, precipitating its charge, and expressed sincere moral disapproval afterward: "That wasn't very nice. Why would you insult the boar? The poor boar. That's mean." He accepted the resulting irradiated pork meat with equanimity and resumed the search for beverages.

In the second cave he noted the carved lintel above the entrance, observed the penned boars inside, and delivered an unpunished pun ("I'm tempted to say this is boring"). He read aloud from a half-burned letter fragment recovered from a firepit — the fragment signed with a single letter K, containing the line "dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives."

In Narux's cave he initially assessed the space as containing nothing of interest and proposed leaving. He reviewed the Gorthok sketches and map fragment alongside Vukradin, arriving at the conclusion that Falcon's lodge was "where they hide the drinks — hidden in plain sight." When Vukradin reflected on the failure to protect Falcon's lodge before the orc attack, Brewbarry responded with "Excuses, excuses," without apparent irony.

Current status: Alive, with the party, departing the Circle of Thunder.

## Soma

Soma was present at the Circle of Thunder and participated in the assault. She took a lightning bolt hit during the battle alongside Valphine. During the fight she transformed into an elk and charged one of the paralyzed anchorites, striking him with significant force. She then turned and struck one of the standing stones, apparently acting on the understanding that the henge itself was corrupted and needed disrupting.

After the battle Soma examined the site as a druid, identifying it as a borrowed druidic site — a henge with an original sacred purpose that had been gradually overlaid with Talosian ritual. She inspected the sacrificed deer at the center of the henge, noting it had been split throat to belly and its heart replaced with a hollow of black, glassy, faintly humming material. She touched it briefly and pulled back.

She observed the ideological argument between Vukradin and Prutha over the holy symbols without intervening. She witnessed Prutha's quiet statement about his great-uncle's saying — "dawn comes for everyone, even these ones" — and noted his apparent emotional weight in that moment, distinguishing it from performative grief.

She noticed her staff had stopped trembling during the fight — it had been vibrating since a previous cursed clearing — and discovered a new fourth bud near the crown of the staff, tight and green, apparently generated by the encounter. She interpreted this as the staff registering something counted or completed. She remained with this observation quietly before the group moved to the caves.

Soma did not enter the caves, remaining outside with Boney because the tunnel mouth was too narrow for an elk form.

Current status: Alive, standing watch outside the cave entrance with Boney.

## Valphine

Valphine was present for the approach to the Circle of Thunder and was the first to identify the ritual as a summoning. She named it as Animate Dead based on the quality of the movement. During the battle she took the lightning bolt alongside Brewbarry and Soma. She engaged the twig blights with her radiant guardians, sweeping multiple blights from the battlefield. She delivered her battle cry — "Die ye then" — before killing the final anchorite.

After the battle she participated in the debate over the holy symbols. She placed one of the Talosian holy symbols on a fallen anchorite's skull and destroyed it with her mace. She responded to Prutha's statement about his great-uncle's saying with "Dawn is powerful," which Prutha acknowledged.

In the caves, Valphine entered with Brewbarry, Vukradin, and Prutha. She participated in the boar encounter in the first cave, allowing her radiant guardians to finish the animal after Vukradin's second insult escalated the situation. She observed dryly that the result was "radiated pork meat."

In the second cave she found the satchel with the Talosian wax seal and insisted on destroying the symbol before opening it. She translated the letters inside, which were written in a hand she identified as consistent throughout. The letters revealed: that a half-orc named Narux had been maintaining the henge while another figure named Grannoc was reckless and likely to summon before the Lodge was ready; that someone described as "the Chosen" was cultivating a young man from an eastern clan with fabricated stories, with the leash not yet revealed; and that if the henge fell, the manse would summon in its place, and if the manse fell, the writer would summon personally. She also recovered a half-burned letter fragment from a firepit signed only with the letter K, containing the line "dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives."

She found a hidden floor compartment in the cave that Vukradin had stepped over without noticing. Inside was a single vial of potent defensive magic — the kind she assessed would make a person nearly impossible to put down. Rather than disclose it to the group, she tucked it into the inner pocket of Vukradin's pack without his knowledge, reasoning that he would refuse it on principle if told but would need it for what was coming.

She noted she had already laid groundwork with Falcon the Hunter at the druid conclave, proposing a musical instrument as a gesture of good faith, which Falcon had accepted. She offered to take the lead in smoothing relations with Falcon given Vukradin's complicated standing with him.

Regarding the boar tusk on a leather thong recovered from the bodies, she expressed interest in incorporating it into her sacraments.

Current status: Alive, with the party, departing the Circle of Thunder. Carrying concealed knowledge of the vial she planted on Vukradin.

## Prutha

Prutha participated in the battle at the Circle of Thunder. He threw a javelin into one of the paralyzed anchorites and followed with his greataxe, contributing to bringing the second anchorite down. He moved through the battlefield with focused directness that Brewbarry noted with recognition.

After the battle, when Vukradin argued that the holy symbols of Talos should be returned to their rightful owners, Prutha countered by pointing at the dead anchorites and stating that the owners had been found and the items should go to them. He then destroyed the symbols. He did not engage further in the philosophical debate about whether this logic was sound.

He made a quiet statement while looking at the bodies of the fallen anchorites — his own kind, younger than expected, bearing someone else's faith tattooed on their skin: "My great-uncle said dawn comes for everyone. Even these ones. I did not think dawn would feel quiet." He received Valphine's response — "Dawn is powerful" — with a grunt and a wave of his axe, but the grunt was noted as carrying something different from his usual register.

Prutha entered the caves with the rest of the group. No specific actions by him in the caves are recorded beyond his presence.

Current status: Alive, with the party, departing the Circle of Thunder.

## Falcon the Hunter

Falcon does not appear in this session. He is discussed by the party during the review of Narux's map fragment.

The map converged on Falcon's Hunting Lodge as the primary target of the Talosian cult operation. Vukradin noted that both lines of the cult plan pointed there, and that the lodge markers were listed in red on his running list because Falcon had not apologized to the party for his behavior at the druid council.

Vukradin then worked through the accountability issue: the party had failed to address the Woodland Manse before orcs attacked Falcon's lodge. Someone associated with Falcon — referenced as "his girlfriend" — apparently died in the attack. Vukradin expressed discomfort with this outcome while attempting to contextualize the party's limited capacity.

Valphine disclosed that she had already worked to repair relations with Falcon at the druid conclave, proposing a musical instrument as a gesture of good faith. Falcon accepted the gesture. She offered to take the lead in approaching him, and the group accepted this.

Current status: Not present. Last known location: Falcon's Hunting Lodge. Relationship with party: strained but with active repair efforts underway via Valphine.
