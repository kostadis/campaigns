## NPC Activity

**Narux (deceased)**
Last known location: smallest of three caves at the Circle of Thunder, presumably used as his personal quarters. Found dead (killed prior to or during the session). His cave contained vellum sketches of Gorthok annotated in Orcish ("feed before unleashing," "the leash holds only if the stones sing"), a leather cord possibly related to Gorthok's binding, an unassigned shield, and a torn map fragment of Neverwinter Wood with three cult sites marked. His remains or belongings are actively in play; the party searched his cave and extracted intelligence from it.

**Two Unnamed Half-Orc Anchorites (deceased)**
Appeared at the Circle of Thunder performing a silent ritual dance among twelve twig blights. Both were paralyzed mid-ritual by Vukradin and subsequently killed in combat. Post-mortem examination revealed: one had storm-cloud and coiling-spiral tattoos up his neck (each spiral containing an open eye); the other had crossed boar tusks tattooed down both forearms. Both wore boar masks. Holy symbols of Talos were recovered from their bodies and subsequently destroyed by Prutha and Valphine.

**Twelve Twig Blights**
Served as a second defensive ring around the ritual dancers at the Circle of Thunder. Engaged the party in combat and were destroyed, primarily by Brewbarry and Valphine.

**Grannoc (absent, referenced)**
Named in the first recovered letter as a Talosian operative whose "storm does not hold." Described as reckless and likely to "summon before the Lodge is ready." Believed to be currently active at the Woodland Manse, which is double-circled on Narux's map fragment as an active site.

**"The Chosen" / "K" (absent, referenced)**
Author of at least two of the three letters found in the cave satchel. Signs only as "K" on at least one fragment. Referenced as being "pleased with the boy from the eastern clan." Stated that if the henge falls, the manse summons in its place; if the manse falls, they will summon personally. No face or full identity confirmed. Referenced previously in intelligence that "never quite assembled into a face" (per Valphine's assessment).

**Unnamed "Boy from the Eastern Clan" (absent, referenced)**
Mentioned in the second letter as someone being cultivated by the Talosian network — fed stories, not yet told "about the leash." Status and identity unknown. Implied to be a young person being groomed for cult use.

**Gorthok (absent, referenced)**
Depicted in Narux's vellum sketches as a massive four-legged creature with tusks trailing lightning, roughly the size of a granary. Annotations indicate it must be fed before being unleashed and that its binding ("the leash") holds only if "the stones sing." Not yet summoned as of this session.

**Falcon (absent, referenced)**
Discussed extensively in the aftermath. Named as owner/inhabitant of Falcon's Hunting Lodge, which is the convergence point on Narux's map and a target of the Talosian network. His "girlfriend died" during an orc attack on the lodge — an attack that occurred after the party failed to neutralize the Woodland Manse threat in time. Was described as hostile to the party at a prior druid council. Valphine had previously laid groundwork with him (offered a musical instrument as a gesture of good faith; he accepted). Described as now being willing to give an apology.

**Adabra (absent, referenced)**
Referenced briefly by Soma as having called her an "interventionist" at a prior council. No new activity.
