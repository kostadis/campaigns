## Adabra

Adabra accompanied the party on the long walk back to Phandalin, using the journey to share her philosophy of naturalism with Soma. She argued that disrupting the natural order—whether by driving out orcs or fighting outer-planar entities—is the true source of imbalance, and that labeling creatures as monsters leads to calling people barbarians. She reassured Brewbarry that he was never a barbarian but a nomad.

Upon arriving at Harbin's home, Adabra took charge of the negotiation for payment, speaking forcefully on Vukradin's behalf. She framed him as an idealist and champion rather than a common sellsword, arguing that Phandalin's defense requires principled heroes rather than mercenaries motivated by plunder. She praised Soma and Brewbarry by name but conspicuously omitted Valphine from her advocacy—notably without explanation, leaving open whether the slight was due to Valphine being a Drow or some other reason. Her pressure succeeded in extracting free room and board at the Stonehill Inn from Harbin.

## Harbin (Townmaster)

Harbin once again cowered at the party's approach, initially mistaking Vukradin for a dragon. He resisted paying additional coin beyond the original agreement, citing the arrangement with Lord Neverember that allowed adventurers to claim monster treasure as payment. Under Adabra's furious pressure, he capitulated and offered free room and board at the Stonehill Inn instead of coin, citing bandit activity as the reason for his shortage of funds.

The following morning, Harbin was intercepted by Vukradin in the street before reaching his office. When pressed about destroying the cursed lighthouse, Harbin was dismissive of the evil rumors but firm that Lord Neverember would want the curse removed rather than the structure destroyed. He directed the party to find Ser Kaelen at Barthen's Provisions before he left town.

## Daran Edermath (Silverleaf)

Daran Edermath, a tall and lean half-elf in his later years with silver-streaked hair, emerald-green eyes, and a salt-and-pepper beard, appeared at the Stonehill Inn. He carries himself with the quiet wisdom of a former adventurer now living a simpler life, dressed in practical earth-toned clothing suited to tending his orchard. He pitched Vukradin on a salvage opportunity: an old stone lighthouse west of Phandalin on the coast, which reportedly lures ships to wreck on its rocks, leaving treasure-filled hulks. Vukradin rejected the premise outright as grave-robbing, leaving Silverleaf shocked and apologetic.

## Toblen Stonehill (Spiderman)

Toblen, the tavern keeper of the Stonehill Inn, was approached by Vukradin about performing music. He shared the origin of his childhood nickname "Spiderman": as a child he fell into a hatching spider's nest, was covered in baby spiders, and walked out calmly, earning the name. He served drinks to the party during Ser Kaelen's recruitment conversation.

## Ser Kaelen Thorn

Ser Kaelen Thorn is a man in his early thirties with dark hair, a clean-shaven face, a scar above his right eyebrow, and chain mail armor. He bears the emblem of a gauntlet grasping a sword by the blade—the insignia of the Order of the Gauntlet. He presented himself as composed and capable, carrying himself with a confidence that makes him seem larger than he is.

Ser Kaelen approached Vukradin at the Stonehill Inn after hearing about his reputation and his unconventional stance on treasure and plunder. He offered Vukradin associate membership in the Order of the Gauntlet, framing the organization as a coalition of fighters, mages, storytellers, and clerics dedicated to improving the North—working against plunder and senseless killing, and using art to spread that message. He cited the Order's role in the War of the Dragon and named Milo Goodbarrel as the member who led the strike force that stopped the inner sanctum ritual.

Ser Kaelen assured Vukradin that funding comes from conflict-free donations. He emphasized that Vukradin's celestial origins and personal story, combined with his musical voice, could reach tens of thousands. Vukradin accepted associate membership and received a pin. Ser Kaelen extended an invitation to Valphine as well, but pointedly said Soma's naturalist worldview would not align with the Order's.

When he heard Brewbarry's accusation that the Order bullied his tribe, Ser Kaelen acknowledged the brutality of that war without taking joy in it, and offered a drink as a gesture of reconciliation.

The following morning, Ser Kaelen was found at Barthen's Provisions preparing to leave town. When Vukradin brought the lighthouse situation to him, he was pleased by the initiative and offered intelligence: the curse could have three causes—one requiring a cleric, one involving a haunting monster, and one being simple rumor—and based on what he heard, believed it most likely to be a monster.

Last known whereabouts: Barthen's Provisions, preparing to depart Phandalin.

## Milo Goodbarrel

Named by Ser Kaelen Thorn as a member of the Order of the Gauntlet who led the strike force that reached the inner sanctum and stopped the ritual during the War of the Dragon. Not present in the session; mentioned only in context of the Order's history and credentials.
