## NPC Activity

**Sister Kella**
- Did not appear directly this session; referenced as having previously met with Valphine
- Provided the party with information on how to locate the Carver
- Offered the reward originally intended for Lokudus as a down payment for the party's cooperation against the Carver

**Jena Roscoe**
- Present at the tavern; observed and commented on Vukradin's behavior
- Explained to the party the concept of "cleaning" blood money by investing it in community projects
- Characterized Vukradin as principled but young and inexperienced

**Brin Bundlewine and Giles Slipper-Shine**
- Two pale-skinned, sunburned villagers who approached Valphine at the tavern
- Had heard rumors of a drow who worships Lathander and interprets the sun as purifying pain
- Proposed establishing a chapel dedicated to this interpretation
- Agreed to renovate Tresendar Manor into the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice; accepted twenty-five gold for materials, providing labor for free

**Harbin Wester**
- Appeared at the tavern and facilitated paperwork for converting Tresendar Manor into a temple
- Accepted fifty gold pieces as a private fee, framing the transaction as how things are done in the Overbright rather than formal council business
- Misrepresented the temple funds to Vukradin as a community donation
- Bought a round of beer for the party after the deal

**Ser Kaelen**
- Met with Vukradin the morning after
- Stated he is waiting on information from Neverwinter regarding the rightful owners of the party's found treasure
- Expects answers within a few days
- Suggested the party investigate Mountain's Toe or deliver supplies to the Logger's Camp in the meantime

**Barthen**
- Provided two crates of supplies (dried meats, cheese, bread, ale, oil) loaded onto a cart with an ox named Vincent for delivery to the Logger's Camp
- Directed the party to stop at the Falcon Hunting Lodge for wine

**Corwin**
- Chef and retainer at the Falcon Hunting Lodge
- Greeted the party at the gate, verified Barthen's letter of introduction, and admitted the party

**Falcon the Hunter**
- Proprietor of the Falcon Hunting Lodge; retired adventurer with distinctive mutton chops
- Welcomed the party as friends of Barthen; offered lodging, meals, and wine in exchange for musical entertainment
- Disclosed that orcs have descended from the mountains since a dragon appeared in the region, and that the orcs appear organized and are working with evil half-orcs based at a ruined stone manse approximately ten miles southwest of the lodge
- Revealed at dinner that his lodge was built using his share of a dragon hoard split four ways among his former adventuring group
- Defended keeping monster-defeated treasure as traditional adventuring practice; dismissed movements against adventurer plunder as disruption aimed at the ruling elite
- Demanded and received an apology from Vukradin after the confrontation
- Offered the party a quest involving the half-orcs at the Woodland Mants, with boots of elvenkind as reward; noted the party may need more experience first
- Clarified the boots were purchased legitimately with lodge proceeds, not plunder

**Sridar**
- A drow staying as a guest at the Falcon Hunting Lodge
- Identifies as a collector of curious artifacts connected to planar events
- Her interest in planar phenomena began after a blink event in the Underdark during which demons appeared
- Purchased information from Valphine about a creature collecting strange items for thirty gold pieces
- Identified the Talisman of Moesko as having a definite planar connection; purchased it from the party for one hundred gold pieces
- Arranged for Falcon to provide three bottles of wine to Brewbarry as part of the deal
- Speculated planar incursions could be caused by magic users, planar entities, or reckless meddlers; originally believed the phenomenon was natural, akin to the Faerzress
- Identified herself as an interventionist, opposed to the Emerald Enclave's naturalist non-interference stance
- Offered to help the party analyze future planar clues and artifacts
