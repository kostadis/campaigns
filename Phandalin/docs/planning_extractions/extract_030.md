## NPC Activity

**Zeleen Varnaster** (wererat miner): Appeared outside the mine after the battle and expressed relief at the party's arrival. Claimed Don-jon Raskin had been killed by a carrion crawler. When pressed, admitted through evasive language that he had "conveniently lured" Don-jon to his death, stopping short of using the word "kill." Negotiated with the party, agreeing to give them Don-jon's share of the mine in exchange for resolving the carrion crawler problem. Received Petunia's cheese from Valphine, which delighted him and the other wererats.

**Don-jon Raskin** (deceased mine owner): Did not appear but was central to the session's revelations. Physical evidence at the death scene confirmed he was killed in combat with another party, not by the carrion crawler. He had told the dwarves he found "a sucker in some bard fellow" who had convinced the wererats to work for cheese, and planned to use this cheap labor to dismiss the dwarves entirely. This betrayal triggered the confrontation that killed him.

**Horia** (dwarf leader): Led five dwarves deeper in the mine. Produced a contract showing Don-jon had formally agreed to let the dwarves work the mine, and that the dwarves had been working it before the wererats arrived. Confirmed dwarves killed Don-jon after he revealed his plan to dismiss them as unnecessary. Warned the party that the wererats lack proper mining skills and would collapse the operation within months. Conditionally agreed to a compromise arrangement—dwarves mining, wererats doing menial labor and security—if the wererats apologized for killing dwarves during earlier conflicts. Was deeply offended when Valphine stated contracts exist only to cheat people, and questioned her fitness as a cleric of Lathander.

**The Carrion Crawler** (creature, now departed): Communicated via Soma's magic. Reported feeding on bodies of "deep stone folk" (dwarves) and "false rats" (wererats) who had been killing each other. Had been passively exploiting the ongoing conflict for food. Was persuaded by Soma's argument that the war ending would leave the mine loud and devoid of easy meals, and agreed to seek better hunting grounds. Departed before its lair was searched.

## Faction Movements

**Wererats (Zeleen's group)**: Secured control of the mine surface and claimed Don-jon's share through orchestrated murder. Accepted cheese from the party as a goodwill gesture. Now face pressure to apologize to the dwarves and accept a reduced operational role. Their mining incompetence has been identified as a structural threat to the operation.

**Dwarves (Horia's tribe)**: Hold a legitimate contract for a twenty-percent share of mine output. Killed Don-jon Raskin after his betrayal. Currently deeper in the mine and willing to continue working under a renegotiated arrangement, contingent on wererat apology. Their contract is now under renegotiation by the party.

**The Carver's Former Ogre Allies**: Suffered heavy casualties in the battle outside the mine. Their tribal allegiance to the Carver had already weakened following a disastrous incident involving Borg the Hammer, which had degraded the Carver's power and caused the tribe to shift loyalties. No longer a present threat at this location.

## Revealed Information

- **Don-jon Raskin was murdered by the dwarves**, not the carrion crawler. He was lured to a location where the dwarves killed him after he revealed plans to exploit wererat labor and discard the dwarves.
- **Don-jon had manipulated the wererats** by convincing them to work the mine for cheese, describing the arrangement to the dwarves as finding "a sucker in some bard fellow."
- **The wererats are incompetent miners** and pose a long-term structural danger to the mine's integrity if left as primary operators.
- **The Carver previously commanded ogre tribes** in the region, but suffered a power loss following an incident with Borg the Hammer, causing at least one tribe to abandon allegiance.
- **The carrion crawler was not an aggressor** but an opportunist feeding on casualties from the dwarf-wererat conflict.
