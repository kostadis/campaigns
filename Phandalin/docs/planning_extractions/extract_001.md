## NPC Activity

**Zariel** – Architected the loophole that enabled Tiamat's attempted return to the Material Plane, using Severin and the Cult of the Dragon as unwitting instruments. Her true motive was expelling Tiamat from Avernus. Operated entirely behind the scenes.

**Tiamat** – Attempted to enter the Material Plane via the Cult of the Dragon's ritual. Believed the plan was her own. Thwarted.

**Severin** – Led the Cult of the Dragon in Tiamat's summoning ritual. Was manipulated by Zariel without his knowledge.

**Ilymrith (Great Ancient Blue Dragon)** – Caused significant chaos during the Giant War, nearly destroying the giant kingdoms. Subsequently escaped; whereabouts unknown.

**Sage KP** – Acquired a piece of an unspecified artifact during the chaos of the Giant War.

**Storm Giant King Hekaton** – Forged a formal alliance between giants and the peoples of the Realms following the Giant War.

**Gromph Baenre** – High-ranking Drow of the First House of Menzoberranzan. Cast a spell intended to give Drow control over the Faerzess. Was manipulated by Lolth; the spell instead weakened planar bonds, allowing demon lords entry into the Underdark.

**Lord Neverember** – Following the conclusion of the barbarian invasion and stabilization of the North, redirected political and economic ambitions toward the Neverwinter hinterland, specifically Phandalin. Offering adventurers tax-free, no-questions-asked plunder in exchange for clearing the region of monsters, cults, and evildoers.

**Vukardin** – A musician whose work is underappreciated. Accepted Neverember's offer and is operating as an adventurer in the Phandalin region.

## Faction Movements

**Cult of the Dragon** – Attempted to summon Tiamat into the Material Plane. Plan was thwarted. Faction significantly weakened following the War of the Dragons.

**The Kraken Society** – Revealed during the events of the Giant War. Survived intact despite exposure.

**The Lord's Alliance** – Grew in strength following the War of the Dragons. Forces were intact when the Uthgardt invasion occurred due to the timely reinstatement of the Ordning preventing a simultaneous giant assault.

**The Silvery Marches Alliance** – Dissolved at some prior point; revived through the efforts of agents from Everlund during the crisis period.

**Giants (Allied Forces)** – Under Hekaton's leadership, formally allied with the peoples of the Realms. Combined giant and city forces successfully repelled and eliminated the Uthgardt barbarian invasion from the North.

**Uthgardt Barbarians** – Launched a major invasion of the North, likely provoked by Underdark chaos. Razed Triboar, destroyed Calling Horn, and pillaged villages near Silverymoon and Mirabar. Subsequently defeated and eliminated from the hinterland by the combined alliance forces.

**Lolth** – Operating from a demi-plane prison as part of a multi-century scheme. Manipulated Gromph Baenre into casting the Faerzess spell. Successfully weakened planar bonds long enough to release demon lords into the Underdark. Current status in her demi-plane: unknown.

**Demon Lords (Abyssal Incursion)** – Entered the Underdark following the planar weakening:
- **Graz'zt** – Spread insanity and madness across wide areas.
- **Zuggtmoy** – Disseminated dangerous fungi.
- **Orcus** – Raised and commanded undead armies.
- **Demogorgon** – Attacked and wreaked havoc on cities, including Menzoberranzan.

**Neverwinter (City-State)** – Post-war, pivoting to expansionist settlement policy. Targeting the Phandalin region for pacification and colonization. Financing the effort through adventurer contracts rather than direct military expenditure.

## Revealed Information

- Zariel, not Tiamat, was the true architect of the Cult of the Dragon summoning plot; her goal was removing Tiamat from Avernus.
- The Faerzess is a naturally occurring forbiddance-like substance in the Underdark. It can be magically corrupted, with catastrophic planar consequences.
- Gromph Baenre was a pawn of Lolth, not an independent actor; he believed he was seizing power for the Drow.
- Lolth has been confined to a demi-plane as part of a long-running containment scheme, but successfully executed a contingency that achieved her immediate goal.
- The Ordning's reinstatement was a critical factor in the North's survival — had it not occurred, the Lord's Alliance armies would have faced simultaneous giant and barbarian threats.
