## Harbin

Harbin initially made accusations against Vukradin that the party found deeply offensive. When confronted by both Vukradin and Ser Kaelen in the same scene, he found himself under pressure from multiple sides. He reminded Ser Kaelen that only Lord Nevermember had the authority to replace him as Townmaster. When Vukradin threatened "pitchforks" and began organizing a town meeting on the green, Harbin stalled, insisting such meetings required preparation, agendas, and pre-work. After the white dragon attack was repelled by Vukradin's phantasmal force, Harbin publicly acknowledged Vukradin's heroism and proposed a compromise: a new privy council consisting of himself, Tolbin, Elmer, Halia, Linene, Adabra, and Vukradin, to share governance while preserving his Townmaster title. He remains Townmaster of Phandalin but with reduced unilateral authority, his position now constrained by the newly formed privy council.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen appeared in the aftermath of Harbin's accusations, claiming credit for pushing for a swift trial to clear Vukradin's name and framing it as a matter of the Order of the Gauntlet's integrity. His support of Vukradin was self-serving, focused on the Order's reputation rather than genuine concern. When Vukradin moved toward open revolution, Ser Kaelen pulled him aside and counseled pragmatism, warning that removing Harbin would prompt Lord Nevermember to send troops. He suggested a privy council solution as sufficient. He later told Jenna that Vukradin was a full-fledged member of the Order of the Gauntlet, a claim Vukradin disputed. His association with House Margaster funding was raised by Jenna as a point against him, suggesting his finances are tainted. He remains in Phandalin, now a member of the new privy council alongside Vukradin.

## Sister Kayla

Sister Kayla appeared in an abandoned, decrepit building at the far edge of Phandalin, meeting under cover of darkness. She was expecting Locutus, not Valphine, and immediately identified Valphine by name despite her drow disguise, indicating she had prior knowledge of the party. She killed Locutus without hesitation, striking him down with a rapier and dagger after confirming he had killed Borg. She revealed that the plot to frame Valphine for stealing goods from a merchant had been her own scheme, which failed because Borg did not properly eliminate all witnesses when killing Big Al. She disclosed that she had been working for the Carver but now needed to disappear, fearing the Carver's retribution for her failure. She proposed a new arrangement: she would feed Valphine information to kill the Carver's remaining lieutenants, weakening him enough to draw him out of hiding, after which she intended to kill the Carver herself. She revealed that the Carver possesses a unique ability to communicate telepathically with his people, though the mechanism is unknown to her. She assumed Valphine was an evil drow cleric of Lolth using Lathander as a front, and that her companions were dupes or assets rather than genuine allies. She dismissed Vukradin as too naive, Brewbarry as too loyal to the bard, and Soma as too nature-focused, selecting Valphine specifically as someone she believed shared her worldview. She gave Valphine the payment Locutus was owed for killing Borg as a down payment on their partnership, then vanished before Valphine could respond definitively. Her current whereabouts are unknown. No formal agreement was reached, though Valphine neither accepted nor explicitly refused.

## Jenna

Jenna appeared at the town green during Vukradin's concert, watching him with a smirk throughout, including during the dragon attack. She is a woman in her late thirties with auburn hair, sharp green eyes, and practical high-quality traveling clothes, carrying a dark green wool doublet, sturdy stylish boots, and simple silver rings. Spider-Man identified her as working for the Lord's Alliance, recognizing her insignia and noting agents of that organization have a distinct smell. She later moved to the tavern and was approached by Brewbarry before she could reach Vukradin directly. She disarmed herself of a rapier, a dagger, a second dagger, and a hand crossbow at Brewbarry's insistence before being introduced to Vukradin. She revealed that she had sent the mysterious letter Vukradin previously received, using it as a way to open negotiations. Her purpose was to become Vukradin's agent, offering to book him performances before Waterdeep and Neverwinter nobility and potentially for Lord Nevermember himself. She raised House Margaster's funding of the Order of the Gauntlet as leverage to distance Vukradin from Ser Kaelen. She attempted to reframe Vukradin's "fair-trade gold" philosophy to accommodate taking dungeon treasure, arguing the gold was stolen from the people collectively and that spending it constitutes redistribution. Vukradin flatly rejected this argument and declined her offer, insisting on returning items to rightful owners. She expressed disappointment but there was a visible softening in her demeanor, suggesting genuine admiration for Vukradin even in refusal. She showed contempt toward Brewbarry, whom she called "Uthgartian" with evident disdain for Uthgardt peoples. No deal was reached. Her last known location is the tavern in Phandalin.

## Locutus

Locutus led Valphine to an abandoned building at the edge of Phandalin for the meeting with Sister Kayla, having apparently arranged the rendezvous. He confirmed to Sister Kayla that he had killed Borg. He was killed by Sister Kayla immediately after this confirmation, cut down by her offhand dagger after two missed rapier strikes. He was motivated by the belief that Valphine would send him to the Underdark as reward. He is dead.
