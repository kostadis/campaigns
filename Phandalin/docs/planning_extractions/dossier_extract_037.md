## Cryovain

Cryovain, a white dragon, was found inside a summoning circle on the rooftop battlements of Icespire Hold. The Carver's obsidian weapon — a mental amplifier — was being used to dominate him. His eyes had a glazed film over them, suggesting he was fighting the Carver's influence but losing. He was enraged and launched thirty feet into the air when the party engaged him. He breathed ice on Brewbarry at least twice, nearly pushing the Goliath past what he could absorb. Soma's moonbeam struck him and caused visible pain. Brewbarry landed multiple heavy halberd strikes on him. The summoning circle needed three more rounds to complete the Carver's ritual. Ultimately, Valphine's sacred flame dealt the final 3 points of damage, killing him. When he fell, the burning geometric circle broke and the Carver's essence dissipated with him. The party debated whether he was a victim — Soma had called out "don't kill, don't kill! He was just a victim" — but the fight concluded before that objection could change the outcome. His hoard, roughly 3,000 gold worth of fused ice, residium, gold, gems, and trade bars, was found atop the tower, inseparable without planar expertise.

## The Carver

The Carver was an alien intelligence operating through an obsidian sword — specifically a mental amplifier with a crystal in its pommel. It was conducting a domination ritual on Cryovain within a summoning circle of burning, geometric light on the rooftop of Icespire Hold. The ritual needed three more rounds to complete. When Cryovain was killed and the circle broke, the Carver's essence dissipated entirely — not fled, not retreated, but gone, "unmade by the same stroke that unmade its vessel." A veteran soldier served as the Carver's vessel or agent on the battlefield; this veteran was paralyzed by Vukradin's Hold Person, weakened by Soma's bear claws and moonbeam, and then killed by Brewbarry's halberd. The Carver's weapon remained behind, and the crystal in its pommel was identified as capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience — a property that deeply tempted Vukradin, though he resolved the gem must be returned to its rightful owner.

## Sylvine Wintermoon

Sylvine Wintermoon arrived at Icespire Hold after the dragon was defeated, accompanied by her sister Runa and a group of veteran bandits. She approached Vukradin with a rehearsed performance, calling him a saint and claiming all 3,000 gold of dragon treasure belonged to her and her sister. She invoked the gods as collateral and framed the treasure as already rightfully theirs, presenting the handover as mere formality. She had clearly researched Vukradin's reputation as a principled bard who returns stolen treasure to its owners, and crafted her approach specifically to exploit that reputation. When Vukradin flatly refused and called her a liar, she briefly performed theatrical injury — "I am hurt! You are a thief!" — but dropped the act in under a second, revealing it was never genuine. She immediately pivoted to violence, declaring "Second plan! We kill them all!" The ensuing fight went badly for her side. Her current status after the skirmish is not explicitly stated, though her veterans were killed or fled.

## Runa

Runa accompanied her sister Sylvine Wintermoon to Icespire Hold. She stood behind Sylvine during the con attempt, described as having the expression of someone waiting to remember her cue. When Sylvine claimed the treasure and invoked divine blessing, Runa responded on cue with "Blessed be! Blessed be!" Soma observed that the two sisters had rehearsed this approach on the road. Her fate after the skirmish is not explicitly stated.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen communicated with the party via Sending spell after the battle. His reply to Vukradin's message read: "Praise Torm! Your righteous victory brings light to the north. The gauntlet awaits your arrival in Phandalin. We shall celebrate your conflict-free triumph together." He followed up with a shorter addendum: "Well done. Come to Phandalin quick. Issues with the Emerald Enclave." Vukradin noted that Ser Kaelen had studied his vocabulary — specifically the phrase "conflict-free" — and fed it back to him in twenty-five words, leaving Vukradin uncertain whether to be moved or suspicious. Vukradin assessed him as "about as close as someone we can actually trust as anybody" before immediately correcting himself: "No, I don't trust him. He's as close as anybody." Vukradin specifically noted that Ser Kaelen directed them to Phandalin rather than endorsing their stated plan to head to Neverwinter, and suspected he had arranged something and already had a press release written. Ser Kaelen is currently in Phandalin, affiliated with the Order of the Gauntlet, awaiting the party's arrival. The mention of "issues with the Emerald Enclave" suggests a developing conflict involving Adabra's faction.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester is mentioned only in Vukradin's anxious speculation about who would try to claim the dragon's treasure. Vukradin predicted that Harbin Wester would try to claim credit for the victory, alongside the Lord's Alliance and Neverwinter bureaucrats. He did not appear in person during these events.

## Adabra

Adabra is mentioned briefly in Vukradin's internal reflection about Ser Kaelen's message regarding "issues with the Emerald Enclave." Vukradin identified the Emerald Enclave as "Adabra's world" and noted that Adabra had been "cooling on me for weeks," adding that he had not asked himself hard enough why. She did not appear in person during these events.
