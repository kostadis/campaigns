## Town Master Harbin Wester
Harbin Wester received Shrimpy when the party delivered him to Phandalin's town master upon their return. He was present at the council meeting where the party reported their findings about the Cult of Talos at the Woodland Manse, including details about the shapeshifting orcs and their lightning magic.

## Shrimpy
Shrimpy was waiting with Corbin at a clearing down the path from the Woodland Manse while the party fought the cultists. He was overheard whining and arguing that the party must be dead, attempting to use the sounds of lightning and burning flesh as justification for Corbin to release him. Brewbarry and Valphine were displeased by his behavior and expressed it. He was subsequently handed over to Town Master Harbin Wester upon the party's return to Phandalin.

## Corbin
Corbin waited with Shrimpy in the clearing near the Woodland Manse while the party engaged the cultists. He resisted Shrimpy's attempts to convince him to allow an escape, holding the prisoner despite Shrimpy's arguments that the party was surely dead.

## Ser Kaelen
Ser Kaelen, representing the Order of the Gauntlet on the Phandalin council, expressed concern about the Kraken Society when it was raised at the council meeting, and was the only member to take it seriously, noting suspicions that powerful psionic creatures were involved. He later provided welcome news that Candlekeep research had identified the original owners of a magical item previously recovered from ships, noting this had cost the Order considerable time and money. He used this revelation as a relationship-building gesture toward Vukradin, promising rewards for returning the items while cautioning the process would not be straightforward.

## Jenna Roscoe
Jenna Roscoe, representing the Lords' Alliance on the Phandalin council, dismissed the Kraken Society during the meeting as merely a harmless social club for wealthy elites. However, Valphine sensed she knew more than she was revealing, suggesting she may have deeper knowledge of or connection to the organization.

## Adabra
Adabra attended the Phandalin council meeting and reacted with furious rage when she learned of the party's intervention against the Kraken cult. She identified what the party had witnessed as a temporal violation and planar distortion, arguing that such interventions triggered cascading catastrophic events across the multiverse. She pointed to the Emerald Enclave members' involvement in the cult as proof that interventionists were fundamentally wrong. She stormed out of the meeting in righteous fury, leaving the council divided. The party concluded her extreme positions were wrong and acknowledged this would not be their last encounter with her.

## Brin
Brin was one of two workers helping reconstruct the church in Phandalin. He was ecstatic about the developments during Valphine's sermon, including the unintended new sacrament of ritual blinding and healing, and the placement of a Kraken Society cultist's severed head on a pike outside the church.

## Giles
Giles was the second worker helping reconstruct the church in Phandalin alongside Brin. Like Brin, he was ecstatic about the dramatic religious developments that occurred during Valphine's sermon.

## Teega the Smith
Teega the Smith, a known Kraken Society member, was observed watching Valphine's sermon and its aftermath at the church. She appeared both disturbed and strangely conflicted by the offer of experiencing pain and redemption that Valphine's unintended new sacrament represented.

## Don-Jon Raskin
Don-Jon Raskin was encountered at the tavern in Phandalin, where he regaled listeners with boastful tall tales of his past as a gold prospector, miner, fur trader, privateer, and whaler. He recounted a story about attending a barbarian tribe's party, believing he had been accepted as a member, though the party recognized he had been regarded as a fool. He revealed he was the new overseer for the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine, hired by a consortium in Neverwinter — a consortium Vukradin noticed included at least one person associated with the Kraken Society. Don-Jon admitted he knew nothing about the mine itself but had been required by his employers to hire an escort. His friendly demeanor helped defuse initial tensions with the wererats at the mine entrance. He initially objected to the wererats sharing the mine with mining operations on safety grounds but was persuaded by Vukradin to accept a compromise arrangement.

## Zeleen Varnaster
Zeleen Varnaster is the leader of the Whiskered Gang, a wererat group encountered deep within the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. She explained that her group had been displaced from their previous home at the Shrine of Savras by approximately sixteen orcs and several ogres, some from a tribe the party had previously angered. She proposed that if the party cleared the shrine, her group would vacate the mine, but refused to assist in retaking her own home, claiming she was too valuable to risk and offering only two warriors if pressed. She reacted with offense when Valphine attempted to negotiate a cheese-for-labor arrangement, calling it stereotypical, though she eventually admitted interest in cheese from the special cow at Butterskull Ranch. Valphine sensed Zeleen was lying about her intentions and would likely attempt to keep the mine regardless of any agreement. Zeleen ultimately accepted a deal allowing the Whiskered Gang to live in one section of the mine and work another in exchange for regular cheese shipments from Butterskull Ranch. She remains at the mine under this arrangement, her true intentions still uncertain.
