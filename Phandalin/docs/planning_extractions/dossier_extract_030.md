## Zeleen Varnaster

Zeleen Varnaster is one of the wererat miners working the mine. He appeared after the party secured the area outside, expressing relief at their timely arrival against the orc and ogre attackers. When questioned about the death of Don-jon Raskin, Zeleen gave an evasive and self-incriminating response, stating that "kill is such a strong word" and admitting that Don-jon had been "conveniently lured to his death." He did not deny responsibility but avoided a direct confession. He negotiated with the party, agreeing to give them Don-jon's share of the mine in exchange for dealing with the carrion crawler problem. He and the other wererats were delighted when Valphine presented them with Petunia's cheese. Valphine's subsequent investigation of Don-jon's death scene, however, revealed that dwarf footprints led away from the body, complicating the question of who actually killed Don-jon. Zeleen is currently at the mine, operating under the proposed compromise arrangement in which the wererats would provide menial labor and security while the dwarves handle actual mining, contingent on the wererats apologizing for killing dwarves in earlier conflicts.

## Horia

Horia is the leader of a group of five dwarves encountered deeper in the mine when the party followed dwarf tracks from the scene of Don-jon's death. When the party asserted legal ownership of the mine, Horia produced a contract demonstrating that Don-jon had previously made an agreement with the dwarves and that their tribe had worked the mine long before the wererats arrived. Horia revealed the true circumstances of Don-jon's death: Don-jon had told the dwarves he had found "a sucker in some bard fellow" who had convinced the wererats to work for mere cheese, and that he intended to use this cheap labor while dismissing the dwarves as no longer necessary. This betrayal led to a deadly argument. Horia warned the party that the wererats were dangerously incompetent miners whose improper techniques and safety violations would cause the entire operation to collapse within months. Horia agreed to consider the party's proposed compromise — dwarves handling mining, wererats providing labor and security — on the condition that the wererats formally apologize for killing dwarves in their earlier conflicts. Horia was deeply offended when Valphine stated that contracts exist only to cheat people, and questioned aloud whether she was truly a cleric of Lathander. The party then began renegotiating the dwarves' original contract, which had promised them a twenty percent share, hoping to reduce that figure.
