## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester was waiting for the party at the town boundary of Phandalin upon their return from Dragon Barrow and the mine. He had arranged a full festival reception — banners, townsfolk arranged in a semicircle, and a rehearsed demeanor of modest pride. He praised the party warmly, extolling their bravery, sacrifice, and decisive role in ending the dragon threat. He notably credited his own foresight in establishing the Privy Council as the mechanism that brought such capable heroes to Phandalin in the first place. Valphine observed this as a calculated political maneuver — lashing his name to the party's victory before that victory could acquire its own independent narrative. The festival continued under his orchestration while the party received various briefings and dealt with internal matters.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen broke the pageantry of Harbin's welcome festival by drawing Soma aside with quiet urgency to deliver bad news. He briefed the party on Adabra's political maneuvering within the Emerald Enclave — specifically that Adabra was moving to ostracize Lyra from the druidic order and was building a case against Soma as a dangerous interventionist who held Meril's Staff without the Enclave's blessing. Ser Kaelen's briefing set the stage for the party's journey to the druid council to defend Lyra.

## Adabra

Adabra had been actively working against the party during their absence. She used the events at the Whispering Wood — specifically Lyra's failure and the party's intervention — as political leverage within the Emerald Enclave. She moved to ostracize Lyra from the order and built a case against Soma as a dangerous interventionist who held Meril's Staff without Enclave blessing and who taught others to interfere rather than observe.

Adabra personally walked to the sacred grove in the Whispering Wood three weeks prior to the council and confirmed the treant was dead — roots collapsed, crown black, grove ruined. She used this firsthand evidence as the foundation of her case. She referenced her earlier conversation with Vukradin, during which she had accepted his account and paid him five gold pieces, now framing that interaction as a moment she had been deceived: "That day, I believed him."

At the council, Adabra was measured, careful, and direct. She did not raise her voice or dress anger as grief. Soma used the staff's Resonance power to check whether Adabra was controlled, deceived, or lying — the staff confirmed she was none of these. She was a true believer. Vukradin confirmed: "Yeah, no, she's a true believer."

Her philosophical position was that displacement and planar anomalies were downstream consequences of intervention, and that the solution was withdrawal — allowing the ecosystem to find equilibrium without help. When challenged on what her solution actually was, she articulated this clearly: "The displacement is downstream of the intervention. The answer is to stop the cascade by withdrawing. The ecosystem finds equilibrium without help."

She had strategically called the council during Reidoth's absence — the nominal head of the local Emerald Enclave chapter — ensuring the person most likely to complicate her majority was not present. She had recruited Falcon the Hunter and Aldric Stone Path to her cause.

After Thomas and Lyra openly confessed to the failed intervention and the party's killing of the metastasized treant, Adabra seized on Corbin's death as further evidence: "See, this is the price of interventionism. Dead bodies everywhere, by careless, careless intervention."

The council ultimately ruled against her. The presiding druid stated her case "just does not hold" and called her personal attacks on Vukradin "absolutely disgraceful" and said they "crossed the line." Adabra left the council without speaking — seething, with the posture of someone who had been outvoted but was far from finished. Soma expressed concern about her returning "in greater numbers." Falcon later expressed regret for supporting her, saying "I don't even know why I ever supported Adabra."

## Lyra

Lyra was the subject of Adabra's ostracization effort within the Emerald Enclave. The party traveled to the druid council specifically to defend her. At the council, Lyra spoke first, explaining what had happened at the rift — the failing ritual, planar damage spreading through the forest, and the treant's death. She had prepared her remarks carefully. Together with Thomas, she openly confessed: "Lyra asked us to go deal with the metastasized treant. We tried, we failed, the party showed up, they killed it." There was no shame or defiance in her voice — just plain statement of fact.

The council ruled in her favor. Lyra was restored to full good standing within the Emerald Enclave. Afterward, she offered to buy the party drinks at the Stonehill Inn when they returned to Phandalin. Soma noted that some of Lyra's gratitude seemed aimed at the absent Vukradin.

## Thomas

Thomas found the party on the road to the druid council, materializing in the manner of one of Lyra's well-trained agents — not from nowhere, but from the specific nowhere that trained agents occupy when wanting to seem casual. He carried news from Lyra: Adabra was moving to ostracize Lyra from the Emerald Enclave. He delivered this with the weariness of someone who had thought about it extensively.

Thomas noted that even if Reidoth were present, he could not procedurally stop Adabra from speaking — but acknowledged that Reidoth's physical presence would change the texture of what Adabra could do. At the council itself, Thomas stood and openly confessed alongside Lyra about the failed intervention and the party's killing of the metastasized treant, speaking without shame.

## Falcon the Hunter

Falcon attended the druid council sitting beside Adabra, aligned with her anti-interventionist position. He had been recruited to her cause and was described as someone who had opinions about how balance should be kept and who should keep it.

During the council, Falcon stood and publicly attacked Vukradin: "She's right! She's right about what needed to happen. You all wanted it simple. Well, this is what simple looks like. Trees are dead because of men like *him.*" When Vukradin pulled him aside to ask why, Falcon said: "You intervened, and in your intervention, people died. Adabra sees it clearly." When Vukradin pointed out Falcon's own history of intervention, Falcon replied: "And look what it got me." He then stated: "Ever since I spoke to Adabra, I have learned the error of my ways."

When Vukradin challenged him to confess his own history of intervention to the council, Falcon complied with what amounted to a genuine, tearful confession. He told the council about his adventuring days, the dragons he had failed to fight, and the lodge that burned. He referenced the party's failure to protect Falcon Lodge: "Once again, nature tore it down. Once again, the Falcon Lodge was burnt. Once again, I asked heroes to intervene, to protect my lodge, and what did they do? They did nothing, and my lodge burned down." This confession paradoxically made him more sympathetic to the council rather than less.

The presiding druid noted that Falcon "depends on money that these interventionists saved him, and he needs them to save him," undermining his anti-interventionist stance.

After the council's verdict, Valphine approached Falcon and engaged him in a private conversation. She recognized him as a skilled man warped by Adabra's influence rather than ruined — someone who had mistaken exhaustion for judgment and allowed Adabra to put a collar on his guilt and call it philosophy. She shared her own story of becoming a drow cleric of Lathander, offering him a different framework for understanding pain and guilt.

Falcon responded to Valphine's counsel with a shift in perspective: "I don't even know why I ever supported Adabra. We need to go deal with those orcs. I probably owe the bard an apology." He asked Valphine to mediate a future conversation with Vukradin, admitting he lacked the language to apologize properly. Valphine advised a substantive gesture — a valuable musical instrument or a studio space at Falcon's retreat — rather than mere words.

## Brin Bundlewine

Brin (along with Giles) came around the corner during the party's time in Phandalin, having been conducting a fundraiser — a public religious parade through town on behalf of Valphine's Temple of Lathander. He was scarred and sunburnt in patterns consistent with self-flagellation. He was smiling beatifically, with the radiance of a convert who had proven something to himself. Valphine noted faint burn marks tracing his forearms and regarded him with the satisfaction of a priestess watching doctrine take physical form. The townspeople watching the parade appeared unsettled.

## Giles Slipper-Shine

Giles (along with Brin) participated in the public religious fundraiser parade through Phandalin for the Temple of Lathander. Like Brin, he bore scars and sunburn consistent with self-flagellation, and was smiling with convert's radiance. The townspeople's reaction to the parade was varied and largely unsettled.

## Meril

Meril does not appear in this chunk but is extensively discussed. Meril's Staff is the central object of the Emerald Enclave's political conflict. Meril had stayed with Soma late in her life, took tea with her, taught her names for things she had been doing by instinct for years, then left her the staff and moved on. Soma describes him as an old friend who left a simple thing that got heavier later.

The staff's leaves have been diminishing over time, while simultaneously awakening new powers — a pattern Soma finds uncomfortable. Soma suspects Meril was himself torn between the two druidic philosophies (letting the wild be versus protecting it through intervention) and may have chosen someone outside the Enclave's habits to resolve what he could not.

Xanthopoulos stated at the council: "I know whose staff that is. I know what it means that Meril gave it to her. He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he… picked her." After the council, Xanthopoulos quietly remarked: "Perhaps Meril made the right choice." Soma kept those seven words somewhere careful.

## Xanthopoulos

A centaur who arrived at the druid council unexpectedly. He was deliberate in the way old creatures are deliberate — considered, not slow. He did not arrive carrying a banner for either side, and had the air of someone who had watched both pure interventionism and pure naturalism become follies when held too tightly.

He looked at Meril's Staff in a way that tightened something in Soma's chest. He told the council: "I know whose staff that is. I know what it means that Meril gave it to her. He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he… picked her."

He delivered critical news: "I have news. Corbin is dead. Sister Kayla walked out. She's gone to the wind." He stated plainly that he was not aligned with either faction: "I am not on your side. I'm not on theirs. I'm telling you what my people saw and what happened."

After the council, as he took his leave of Soma, he made a quiet remark: "Perhaps Meril made the right choice" — a statement Soma found deeply significant and kept carefully.

## Corbin

Corbin does not appear alive in this chunk. Xanthopoulos reported his death to the druid council: "Corbin is dead. Sister Kayla walked out. She's gone to the wind." Corbin had been a farmer turned bandit who may have been beginning to understand the weight of his choices. The party had left him guarding Sister Kaella, and she apparently killed him and escaped. Soma reflected that they had "left a farmer turned bandit with a deadly assassin because they were in a rush to intervene."

Adabra seized on Corbin's death as evidence against interventionism. The presiding druid partially dismissed this, noting that Corbin "was a criminal," though acknowledging his death was unfortunate. Soma refused to sugarcoat what had happened but also refused to let Adabra claim Corbin's death as a trophy for her argument.

## Sister Kaella

Sister Kaella does not appear in this chunk but is reported on. Xanthopoulos announced at the council that she had "walked out" and was "gone to the wind" — having apparently killed Corbin, whom the party had left guarding her, and escaped. Soma described her as "a killer in priest's cloth." The party had left Corbin watching her because they were moving fast, and this decision resulted in his death and her escape. Her current whereabouts are unknown.

## Boney

Boney does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced. When Soma learned of Corbin's death at Sister Kaella's hands, she asked: "And Boney stood by and did nothing?" — referring to the skeletal horse of uncertain moral standing that was apparently present. Soma acknowledged this was not the right thing to say and that it came out sideways.

## Toblen Stonehill

Toblen Stonehill does not appear in this chunk but is referenced. After the council verdict restored Lyra to good standing, Lyra offered to buy the party drinks at the Stonehill Inn (Toblen's establishment) when they returned to Phandalin.

## Reidoth

Reidoth does not appear in this chunk but is significantly discussed. He is the nominal head of the local Emerald Enclave chapter, and his absence was a critical element of Adabra's strategy — she called the council during his absence to ensure the person most likely to complicate her majority was not present. Thomas noted that even if Reidoth were present, he could not procedurally stop Adabra from speaking, but his physical presence would change the texture of what she could do. The party discussed reaching Reidoth and kept it as an option in reserve. Vukradin ultimately used a Sending spell to contact Reidoth after leaving the council, informing him of the schism, Adabra's faction, Falcon's confession, and the council's turn.

## Aldric Stone Path

Aldric Stone Path attended the druid council aligned with Adabra's anti-interventionist position. He was described as a weathered mountain man who had said the same thing for years and been treated like wind through grass — until Adabra finally listened to him, and he fastened himself to her for it. Soma understood his loyalty: "Adabra had listened to him. That mattered."

When Soma pressed him on what Adabra's actual solution was — asking what she proposed to do about the planar anomalies and blights — Aldric opened his mouth, closed it, and had no answer. He then sat down, which Adabra noticed as a loss.

The presiding druid cited Aldric specifically in the ruling against Adabra: "Aldric over here admits freely that if we were to listen to you, the orcs — what's going on — is perfectly normal."
