## NPC Activity

**Adabra**
- Located at her windmill atop Umbrage Hill, besieged by a manticore for a short period before the party's arrival.
- Refused to return to Phandalin, insisting her role in the natural order requires her to remain at the windmill and craft potions.
- Revealed knowledge of the Emerald Enclave's internal schism between naturalist and interventionist factions; identified herself as a naturalist.
- Tested Soma by asking which side of the schism she supported; shifted demeanor significantly upon hearing Soma's answer.
- Confirmed awareness of Townmaster Harbin's request to his Lordship for help, and acknowledged the party as the promised aid.
- Provided the party with a potion of healing (enhanced with scent and taste) and a written note for Townmaster Harbin instructing him to reward the party.
- Prepared and served a meal of fresh-baked bread, vegetable and herb stew with foraged ingredients.
- Corrected Soma on druidic knowledge, noting that manticores have a documented preference for human flesh over other humanoid races.
- Reversed her decision to stay when she learned Vukradin's reluctance to take monster-loot gold aligned with naturalist principles; agreed to accompany the party to Phandalin as a fellow naturalist.
- Defended Brewbarry against the word "barbarian," reframing the term as a tool of interventionist ideology used to exclude beings from the natural order.

**Corbin**
- Admitted to wearing armor stripped from a dead man; revealed a history as a former farmer from Greenmeadow, east of the High Forest.
- Disclosed that his village was destroyed by Black Lion Uthgardt barbarians during the chaos following the breaking of the Ordning; his wife Elara and daughter Lily were killed.
- Survived by being knocked unconscious and left for dead; subsequently drifted south, surviving through petty theft.
- Joined a bandit crew out of desperation and lack of alternatives.
- Assigned by Vukradin to camp duties; allowed to keep the scavenged armor temporarily pending proper fair-trade replacement.
- Assisted Adabra with meal preparation at the windmill.
- Was briefly targeted by the fleeing manticore as a preferred human prey target.

**The Manticore**
- Confirmed sapient; spoke during the encounter, demanding food and offering to spare the party in exchange for Corbin.
- Expressed a stated preference for human meat over other humanoid flesh.
- Killed by Brewbarry; head severed by Vukradin as a trophy.

## Faction Movements

**Emerald Enclave**
- Adabra identified as an active member aligned with the naturalist faction.
- Naturalist position established: accepts divine interventions and the multiverse as part of the natural order; opposes the drawing of lines between civilized and uncivilized beings.
- Interventionist faction characterized by Adabra as complicit in the slaughter of monsters and seizure of their treasures, which she equates to barbarism and vandalism.
- Adabra's decision to travel to Phandalin was motivated by her identification of the party as fellow naturalists, suggesting the Emerald Enclave's naturalist faction may extend influence or goodwill toward the party.

## Revealed Information

- The Emerald Enclave is divided between a **naturalist faction** (accepts the multiverse and divine intervention as natural; opposes destructive intervention) and an **interventionist faction** (draws distinctions between civilized and uncivilized; associated with monster-slaying and treasure-seizure).
- Adabra is aware that **Townmaster Harbin** sent a note to **his Lordship** requesting aid, and that his Lordship promised to send help — confirming a chain of authority above Harbin that dispatched the party.
- Manticores demonstrably prefer human flesh over other humanoid races — confirmed by Adabra as a matter of druidic knowledge Soma had not been taught.
- Corbin's backstory confirms the **breaking of the Ordning** as a destabilizing event that drove Uthgardt tribal violence and displaced civilians across the North, feeding bandit recruitment.
- Adabra holds a legal deed to the land around Umbrage Hill and has maintained the dwarven cairns on the site; the ruined structure predates her occupation.
