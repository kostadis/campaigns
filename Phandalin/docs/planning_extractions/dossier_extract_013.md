## Adabra

Adabra appeared at her shop in the main square of Phandalin, next to Garaele's house and across from the Shrine of Luck, when Soma and the party came to confront her. She dropped her kind demeanor immediately upon sensing Soma's fury, becoming cold and patronizing. She revealed her ideological position explicitly: she belongs to a faction within the Emerald Enclave that considers the interventionists — members like Lyra who perform cleansing rituals — to be aggressors waging "a war on nature" by rejecting the multiverse's diversity. She views their rituals as acts of violence and frames planar phenomena as natural expressions of universal beauty rather than threats to be purged.

Adabra admitted that she had been "cast out" and "reduced to begging for crumbs of resources" within the Emerald Enclave due to her anti-interventionist views, which drove her to recruit outside allies — including Soma — to oppose the ritual. She expressed disappointment that Soma had apparently sided with Lyra, though Vukradin's deflections confused her enough to prevent an accusation of outright betrayal.

She was successfully deceived regarding the Treant's fate. When Vukradin described the Treant as being "in stable condition," she believed him. When she pressed further, Vukradin's vague answer about the planar tear and Valphine's interjection about the strange creature distracted her from the line of inquiry entirely. The mention of the creature sent her into a state of apparent ecstasy; she identified it as "a planar magpie," a symptom drawn to the energy rather than a cause of the disturbance, and she was pleased to hear the party had not killed it.

She paid Vukradin five gold pieces and expressed cautious hope for him, while declaring herself wrong about Soma. She made a brief attempt to use a music analogy to persuade Vukradin that the interventionists were wrong to reject certain "music" — certain planes — before departing. Soma privately characterized her extended philosophical monologue as meaningless nonsense. Last seen leaving the main square after accepting Vukradin's invitation to hear music, though her subsequent attendance at the tavern is not confirmed.

## Lyra

Lyra arrived at the main square simultaneously with the party, accompanied by her agents from the Emerald Enclave. She was furious throughout the encounter with Adabra, at one point screaming "You are insane!" at the older woman. Vukradin repeatedly deflected her attempts to speak, which further enraged her. When he suggested they go listen to music, she refused and delivered a parting condemnation: "If you cannot see the evil this woman represents and her fanaticism and its danger, then you are all fools." She then left the square with most of her agents.

Through her agents Thomas and Marian, her broader situation was later revealed at the Stonehill Tavern. Lyra had discovered the ritual independently and chose to implement it without bringing it to her Emerald Enclave superiors, because the organization was paralyzed by internal debate over whether any intervention was appropriate. She had a particular personal affection for the Whispering Grove and was frustrated that nothing was being done. She convinced her agents that the forest and the gods would protect them and that they would succeed. The ritual failed and the group nearly died. Her agents acknowledged she had "done what was best for her" rather than for the Enclave, and that her quest — to save the tree and the forest — had entirely failed, leaving her "very upset." A promotion or advancement in status within the Enclave had also apparently been riding on the ritual's success. Last known whereabouts: departed the main square; not present at the tavern scene.

## Thomas

Thomas was one of Lyra's two named agents from the Emerald Enclave who joined the party at the Stonehill Tavern after Vukradin invited them. He was the oldest of the group. He drank the beer Spiderman provided and served as the primary spokesperson for Lyra's situation, explaining that she had discovered the ritual herself, acted unilaterally rather than bringing it to Enclave leadership, and had been motivated by personal ambition and frustration with organizational inaction as much as by genuine concern for the Whispering Grove. He confirmed that a promotion was connected to the ritual's success. He showed some distress when recounting the near-death experience and the failure of the quest. He also questioned Valphine directly about her background, asking whether she was from the Underdark and expressing shock at her description of self-harm as a devotional practice to Lathander. When Vukradin offered to speak on Lyra's behalf to her superiors, Thomas firmly advised against it. He and Marian declined Vukradin's invitation to join the party, stating that their oath to the Emerald Enclave bound them. Last seen departing the Stonehill Tavern with Marian.

## Marian

Marian was the second named Emerald Enclave agent who joined the party at the Stonehill Tavern. She offered a more sympathetic characterization of Lyra than Thomas did, describing her as a good boss and leader who saw an opportunity, acted on past experience of being right, and trusted herself when others in the Enclave were paralyzed by argument. She noted that Lyra had earned that trust before and simply "wasn't right this time." Marian also questioned Valphine about whether her apparent practice of harming herself and then using healing spells was a personal ritual, expressing visible surprise at Valphine's confusion over the question. She departed with Thomas after declining to join the party. Last seen leaving the Stonehill Tavern.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen appeared at the Stonehill Tavern and approached Vukradin. He inquired about Vukradin's work and plans. Vukradin did not trust him. When asked about the rightful ownership of treasure recovered from the ship, Ser Kaelen sighed and muttered something about how hard that was, which failed to reassure Vukradin. He praised Vukradin's goals, which pleased the bard given his need for universal approval. The two also discussed Vukradin's music in the context of the Order of the Gauntlet, with Vukradin asserting that such things could not be rushed and that whatever he produced would surpass anything Ser Kaelen had previously shared. His current status and organizational role were not further clarified in this session.

## Linene Graywind

Linene Graywind, described as a 35-year-old woman managing the Lionshield Coster, received the party when they came to sell their pelts. She was notably enthusiastic and asked repeated questions about where and how the pelts were found. Vukradin answered honestly, which Valphine privately considered a waste of leverage. Linene offered more than twice Barthen's price, which she appeared to justify by referencing her bosses' interest in the planar effects associated with the goods — suggesting the Lionshield Coster has organizational ties or commercial interests connected to interplanar phenomena. Valphine recalled reading in Neverwinter that merchants had possessed an interplanar shipping device that had stopped working, and connected this to Linene's enthusiasm. Linene also attended the evening performance at the Stonehill Tavern, where she specifically praised Valphine as the one who made the ensemble shine, to Vukradin's brief annoyance. Last seen at the Stonehill Tavern.
