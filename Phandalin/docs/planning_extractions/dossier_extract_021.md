## Teega the Smith
Teega the Smith was encountered standing rigidly at the riverbank on the eastern shore, which was covered by an unnatural dense fog. Her eyes were glassy and unfocused, and she spoke in an eerie monotone about metal bending, minds being cleansed, and waves seeking uniformity. When questioned about the missing Emerald Enclave patrol, she cryptically stated they were trying to fix a leak with a wooden plug and that the current had found them, reforming their brittle thoughts. She drew a mysterious spiral symbol in the mud. Her eyes briefly cleared when Vukradin persuaded her, but they glazed over again and she wandered into the nearby glade. After the combat in the glade, she wandered further into the wilderness, where Soma tracked her in bear form and subdued her, bringing her back to the group. She remained under mind control as long as the Cult Fanatic lived. Her last known status is as a recovering prisoner en route to Phandalin, freed from mental compulsion after the Cult Fanatic was executed by Valphine.

## Tibor
Tibor is the chief of the Loggers Camp. He was referenced during the party's discussion of what to do with their prisoner Shrimpy. Tibor refused to transport Shrimpy to Neverwinter, which led the party to decide to bring Shrimpy to Phandalin instead. He did not appear further in the session.

## Lyra
Lyra is a low-ranking Emerald Enclave member whom the party had previously saved. The party devised a plan to credit the rescue of the missing Emerald Enclave patrol to her in order to boost her standing within the faction and cultivate her as a valuable ally. One of the rescued Enclave patrol members confirmed that it was Lyra who had sent the party to rescue them, indicating she acted on the arrangement. Lyra herself did not appear directly in this session but her influence was felt through the rescued patrol's testimony. Her standing within the Emerald Enclave is being deliberately elevated by the party's actions.

## Shrimpy
Shrimpy is a prisoner who desperately wanted to be released. The party debated his fate and decided to transport him to Phandalin for imprisonment after Tibor refused to take him to Neverwinter. He was present at the Loggers Camp at the start of the session and is being transported to Phandalin. No further details about his identity or crimes were revealed.

## The Cult Fanatic
The Cult Fanatic was encountered in the glade on the eastern shore, wielding a black coral dagger and accompanied by three mind-controlled Emerald Enclave patrol members moving with robotic precision. He coldly informed the party they were interfering with necessary adjustments and that his zealots served a greater purpose. He spoke of a rising current flowing toward Neverwinter and offered the party a chance to submit. During combat he attempted to compel Brewbarry's mind, forcing him to flee, and even while unconscious he exerted control over his minions. When briefly interrogated, he muttered only vague warnings about tides rising and waves coming, refusing to betray his cause. He wore a spiral emblem identifying him as a member of the Kraken Society. On the second day of travel, a Talosian orc raiding party ambushed the party demanding his death, identifying him as their enemy. Given a final chance to speak, he referred only to petty squabbles that meant nothing to what was coming. Valphine executed him, and the orcs took his head as proof of victory. His death broke the mind control over all his captives. The Kraken Society Echoes are implicated in his activities.

## The Anchorite Half-Orc
The anchorite half-orc identified himself during the ambush as a cleric of Talos embedded within the Talosian orc raiding party. After Valphine executed the Cult Fanatic, he declared the deceased cultist one more obstacle crushed by Talos. He then warned the party to leave the forest. His presence among the orcs suggested he held a position of religious authority within the raiding party. Last known location is the forest where the ambush took place.
