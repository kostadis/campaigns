## Vukradin

Vukradin fought against a mimic alongside the party, taunting it with declarations of confidence ("We have it outnumbered! It's stunned! We shall kill it before it lays a hand on us!") while his spells repeatedly failed to affect it. Mid-battle, he engaged Soma in a philosophical debate about naturalist druidism, Aasimar, and Tieflings, taking offense when Soma dismissed his "poetic license" in describing celestial-touched beings. After the fight, he questioned Soma's decision to take gold from the chests, declaring himself already rich due to owning sending stones.

In the audience with King Korboz and King Gnercli, Vukradin methodically dismantled the logic of their dual monarchy through pointed questioning—challenging how decisions are made when kings disagree, what happens when a new king is elected, and whether seniority exists—successfully driving a wedge between the two kings, with Korboz erupting that he deserved preferential treatment as the senior king. He then cut off the chaos he created by demanding the promised magical hat, receiving it along with a dismissal from an enraged Gnercli.

During the journey to Umbrage Hill, Vukradin initially pushed to negotiate higher pay than 25 gold for rescuing Adabra, motivated explicitly by wanting to fund his studio ("my studio is going to take forever to build at this rate"), before being overruled.

During the bandit ambush, he was briefly paralyzed by shock, then sprinted after and killed a fleeing bandit at Valphine's crossbow-wounded prompting. He invited a surrendered, frightened bandit to dinner to learn more about the Carver, the bandit's boss, displaying calculated charm. He also reminded Soma that a druidic scroll taken from the bandits should be returned to its rightful owner.

He had previously heard of a legendary play in Triboar featuring a demon character named Bob and expressed strong desire to stage a revival, intending to travel to Triboar to find the script.

## Soma

Soma fought the mimic alongside the party, with her poison puff proving ineffective against it before she ultimately landed the killing blow. She expressed disgust at the mimic as an unnatural magical creation and, when the gnome inventors Fiddlestib and Dabblebob discussed harvesting the creature's entrails for a new construct, held her tongue despite her belief that such inventions were the origin of northern abominations.

She took gold from the chests without apology ("I am not opposed to some gold of questionable provenance") and dismissed Vukradin's moral posturing. She characterized the 25-gold reward for rescuing Adabra as fair, arguing that "sometimes the reward is knowing you did the right thing," and stated she only wanted a new teapot, not a studio. She expressed a general kinship with all life while also being willing to kill bandits who threatened her.

During the bandit ambush, Soma wild-shaped into a spider, climbed to engage a bandit, bit and poisoned him to death, and attempted to spare a fleeing spider-phobic bandit by shouting "Let him go"—realizing too late she was speaking in spider form. She found a druidic scroll among the bandits' belongings and, after Vukradin challenged her, was made to question whether keeping it made her no better than a bandit.

She is currently serving as Vukradin's paid guide, with his tab growing, and is content to wait for payment.

## Valphine

Valphine observed Vukradin's political destabilization of the dual monarchy with amusement and respect, comparing his technique to good Drow practice. She inserted herself once to suggest the kings needed "a clear approver structure," drawing on her experience watching commune-style cultures collapse into betrayal and violence.

She was annoyed at the 25-gold rate for rescuing Adabra, having been cautioned about it beforehand by Lathander's Cleric. During the approach to Umbrage Hill, she spotted a shadow slipping behind a boulder and tensed, then dismissed the instinct, reminding herself she was not in the Dark Realms. During the bandit ambush, she was struck by a bandit's crossbow bolt. She coolly shot a fleeing bandit in the back with her hand crossbow, enabling Vukradin to finish him.

## Brewbarry

Brewbarry struggled throughout the mimic fight, unable to free his halberd and missing with his initial attempts before producing handaxes at Vukradin's prompting. During the bandit ambush, he decapitated one bandit with his halberd, emerging covered in blood, then chased the fleeing spider-phobic bandit. Rather than killing him, Brewbarry dropped his halberd, shouted "Friends," and offered an apology: "I am so sorry that the Uthgardts hurt you. They were mean to me, too." The gesture surprised the bandit into grief rather than flight. Brewbarry's motivation appears rooted in personal experience of Uthgardt cruelty and a recognition of shared suffering.

## King Korboz

King Korboz co-rules the gnome settlement in a dual monarchy with King Gnercli. He originally complained that Gnercli "doesn't listen to him and believe a single word he has to say," suggesting long-standing friction. Under Vukradin's questioning about seniority, Korboz erupted, shouting that as senior king he should have preferential treatment—exactly the fracture Vukradin was probing for. He also shouted at Gnercli, "That you didn't believe existed!" in reference to the shapeshifter threat. Last seen at odds with Gnercli, with their political relationship significantly destabilized.

---

## King Gnercli

King Gnercli co-rules alongside King Korboz. He defended the dual monarchy's structure to Vukradin, insisting there is no seniority, that the best argument wins decisions, and that the system exists specifically to prevent "despotic autocratic decision making of a single individual who could be rendered unfit for leadership because of a shapeshifter." He grew visibly angry as Vukradin dismantled his arguments and ultimately shoved the promised magical hat at the party and told them to leave. Last seen furious at Vukradin and in renewed conflict with Korboz.

---

## Fiddlestib

One of the two gnome inventors who hired the party to deal with the mimic. Excited to receive proof of the creature's death, she immediately began discussing what construct could be made from its entrails—behavior Soma found deeply troubling. After overhearing the debate about the dual monarchy, Fiddlestib declared herself in favor of "a bicameral legislature with a unitary executive" and became absorbed in arguing with Dabblebob about the design of a tamper-proof voting box.

---

## Dabblebob

The second gnome inventor, partnered with Fiddlestib. After overhearing the kings' political argument, Dabblebob countered Fiddlestib's bicameral proposal by advocating for more kings to enable a vote. Became engaged with Fiddlestib in an argument over whether a tamper-proof voting box is even possible. Last seen absorbed in this debate.

---

## The Carver

Referenced by name only through a surrendered bandit's account. The Carver is described as "the big boss" and is physically large. The bandit had never met him personally, operating instead under a middleman named Jax. The Carver is implied to be responsible for the organized banditry in the region, with the bandit explicitly blaming him for his circumstances ("It's the Carver's fault"). Current location and full nature unknown.

---

## Jax

Named by the surrendered bandit as the direct superior he worked under, serving as intermediary between the bandit crew and the Carver. No further details given. Current whereabouts unknown.
