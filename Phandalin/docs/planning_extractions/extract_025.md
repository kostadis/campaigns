## Threat Arc Events

**Planar Distortion**
- *Score increase trigger:* Soma used her staff to sense the corruption within Axelholm and identified a confirmed **planar anomaly** that has warped spatial reality inside the fortress — distances are out of phase, light behaves in a mathematically unnatural way, and the inhabitants have been physically and mentally transformed by prolonged exposure.
- *Score increase trigger:* The paralyzing ability of the out-of-phase dwarves manifests as victims being "packaged and delivered" — suggesting the planar corruption has a specific, bureaucratic or mercantile character rather than generic chaos, pointing to a distinct extraplanar influence with its own internal logic.
- *Score increase trigger:* Additional out-of-phase dwarves materialized from the unnatural light mid-combat, indicating the anomaly is actively generating or sustaining hostile entities rather than being a passive environmental hazard.

## Revealed Information

- The dwarven inhabitants of Axelholm were not killed but **transformed** by the planar anomaly — they retain physical form but have parchment-like ink-covered skin, gold coins embedded in flesh, sewn-shut mouths, and multiplied out-of-phase limbs.
- The creatures are **not undead** — Valphine's Turn Undead caused fear responses rather than the usual effect, confirming they are living but warped beings.
- The anomaly produces consistent, thematically coherent symptoms: obsession with efficiency, packaging imagery, gold as a physical corruption, bureaucratic/clerical aesthetics (red tape, quills scratching inside walls, ticking clocks). This suggests the planar intrusion originates from a plane or entity associated with **trade, contracts, or mechanical order**.
- Spatial distortion is active throughout the fortress — twenty feet of movement may only cover one foot of actual distance, complicating navigation and combat.
