## Vukradin

Vukradin interrogated Corbin upon their first meeting, recognizing the stolen armor from a dead man and commanding Corbin to remove it before relenting and promising to find him "proper fair trade armor." He led the group up Umbrage Hill toward the windmill where Adabra was trapped, insisting on quiet approach. During the manticore confrontation, he attempted to negotiate with the creature, offering payment, and tried twice to affect it with a spell — both attempts failed, leaving him frustrated and briefly blaming Soma for encouraging his second attempt. After Brewbarry killed the manticore, Vukradin cut off its head and presented it to Adabra as proof of the creature's death. He repeatedly interrogated Adabra about whether Corbin had robbed her, and questioned her ownership of the dwarven house, accusing her of entitlement. He attempted to convince Adabra to return to Phandalin through music, which she praised highly but which did not change her resolve. His motivation for bringing her back was explicitly financial — he needed "organic, conflict-free, fair-trade gold" to fund his studio, and refused payment derived from looting monsters. He ultimately secured Adabra's agreement to accompany them by framing the mission as one undertaken by fellow naturalists of the Emerald Enclave. Last known status: preparing to depart Umbrage Hill with the group and Adabra.

## Soma

Soma spent the opening scene in spider form, experiencing the world through heightened senses before reverting to her tortle body and startling Corbin. During the approach to the windmill she pushed back against Vukradin's suggestion to extort more money from Phandalin, insisting they simply kill the manticore and save Adabra. In combat she conjured a familiar and sent it into the manticore's mouth as a distraction, which enraged rather than satisfied the creature. She encouraged Vukradin's second spell attempt and was visibly hurt when he blamed her for its failure. She tried to soothe both Adabra during the siege and Corbin after the battle. When Adabra asked her position in the Emerald Enclave schism between naturalists and interventionists, Soma instinctively identified as a naturalist, which dramatically shifted Adabra's attitude toward the group. She was stung by Adabra's casual criticism of gaps in her druidic training — her master Meril had only one year near her home before prompting her to follow Vukradin, and she is aware she has little time left. That evening she listened to Corbin's full account of his destroyed family and his descent into banditry. She used Adabra's naturalist convictions the following morning to finally convince her to come to Phandalin, framing the mission around shared values rather than obligation. Last known status: departing Umbrage Hill with the group.

## Valphine

Valphine fired the opening shot against the manticore with her hand crossbow, hitting it but failing to do significant damage. She absorbed a volley of tail spikes when the manticore singled her out in retaliation. She attempted a second shot later in the fight and missed. When Adabra emerged from the windmill, Valphine immediately offered to teach her about Lathander. She reflected internally on the manticore's offer to take Corbin in exchange for sparing the rest of the group, noting it was "a fair trade" she would have considered in a different life. She observed Vukradin's uncertainty in combat with the eye of someone who has read gamblers before. She noted that the food Adabra prepared was exceptional and attributed its quality to Lathander's glory. Last known status: present at Umbrage Hill, preparing to return to Phandalin.

## Adabra

Adabra was found barricaded inside her windmill on Umbrage Hill, besieged by a manticore for a short time before the party's arrival. Upon being rescued she refused to leave, insisting her role in the natural order required her to stay and craft potions. She presented the party with a special healing potion enhanced with scent and flavor, and produced a written note for Townmaster Harbin confirming she would not leave and requesting the heroes be rewarded. She showed knowledge of Soma's druidic training gaps, informing her that manticores have a documented preference for human flesh — a fact Soma had not been taught. She holds strong views aligned with the naturalist faction of the Emerald Enclave, which she contrasted passionately with the interventionist faction's "barbaric slaughtering of monsters." She objected to the word "barbarian" as a concept, reframing Brewbarry as a "nomad" and part of the natural world. When Soma identified as a naturalist and Vukradin explained his fair-trade philosophy as the reason for wanting her in town, Adabra's entire disposition changed and she agreed to accompany the group, declaring them "fellow naturalists." She prepared a hearty meal of bread, herb stew, fungi, foraged vegetables, and berries for the group and offered them lodging overnight. She holds a deed to the land around the windmill and has tended the nearby dwarven cairns. Last known status: agreed to travel to Phandalin with the party.

## Corbin

Corbin appeared before Vukradin as a new recruit, wearing scavenged leather armor taken from a dead man. Under interrogation he admitted to being a former bandit who took the armor out of survival necessity. He was commanded to remove it, then reprieved when Vukradin promised to find him fair-trade armor. During the manticore fight the creature identified him as its preferred target — a human — and flew toward him, forcing the party to act quickly to prevent his death. After the battle he arrived at the windmill looking worse and smelling badly, prompting another round of suspicion from Vukradin. He broke down and recounted his history to Soma: a farmer from Greenmeadow east of the High Forest, married with a young daughter named Lily, whose village and family were destroyed by the Black Lion Uthgardt barbarians during the chaos following the breaking of the Ordning. He survived by accident, drifted south, committed petty theft to survive, and eventually joined a bandit crew out of desperation. He helped Adabra prepare the evening meal at Vukradin's direction. Last known status: traveling with the party, unarmed and in scavenged armor.
