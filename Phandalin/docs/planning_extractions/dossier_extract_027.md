## The Chief Accountant

The Chief Accountant appeared at the end of the battle against six out-of-phase dwarven figures on the upper floor of the ancient dwarven fortress. She manifested as a luminous dwarven figure who glitched and flickered like corrupted data, her face composed of shifting geometric shapes with ink-like shadows leaking from her eyes. Her voice blended a grandmother's warmth with screeching static.

She opened by ranting about ledgers that wouldn't close and regulators dividing by zero, and addressed Valphine directly as "sister of the sun," accusing her of carrying the scent of bleach and betrayal. During the engagement, she unleashed a supernatural wail that rendered both Valphine and Vukradin unconscious. When she noticed Valphine's collapsed form, her demeanor shifted abruptly to panic—she frantically attempted to stabilize Valphine using blue-white static energy, crying out about acid damage and depreciation and expressing terror that "the auditor" would liquidate her for closing Valphine's account. After this outburst she became incorporeal and fled through a closed door into an adjacent room.

After the party healed and rested, the Chief Accountant cautiously returned and disclosed what had befallen the fortress. She revealed that Valphine's sister Aletra, the fourth daughter of the Satora family, had struck a deal with the king to improve the mine's efficiency, exploiting his jealousy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant operations and their revenue. Aletra had promised an installed machine would solve everything; instead it malfunctioned and unleashed chaos throughout the keep. The Chief Accountant admitted that when she complained to Aletra about the books not balancing, Aletra laughed at her and then likely killed her.

She offered the party a deal: the override code to shut down the machine in exchange for her freedom and their silence regarding what had occurred. The party accepted her directions, and she sent them to the machine room on the lower level. Her current status is unknown following this exchange—she is presumably free, having provided the code as agreed.

## Aletra

Aletra is the fourth daughter of the Satora family and Valphine's sister. She was encountered standing calmly beside the malfunctioning machine in the lower-level machine room, typing on a glowing keypad with one hand and holding a rapier in the other, with four spectral spiders made of flickering static clinging to the ceiling above her. Valphine identified her before seeing her face, recognizing the Satora family scent—lavender perfume mixed with chemical bleach from the family's poison labs—and confirmed her identity upon seeing her sharp cheekbones and cold violet eyes.

The Chief Accountant revealed Aletra's background: she had negotiated directly with the king of the fortress, exploiting his envy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant revenues by promising the installed machine would solve his efficiency and profit problems. Instead the machine broke and caused the chaos now afflicting the keep. Aletra had laughed when the Chief Accountant raised concerns about the unbalanced books, and is implied to have killed her shortly after.

Valphine discerned that Aletra's true purpose at the machine was extracting drow poison she had been smuggling through the multiverse using the device. Aletra addressed Valphine in Undercommon with characteristic condescension, dismissing her and the party as surface dwellers to stay out of the way while "the adults" handled their deadline. She presented the party with a coercive proposal: kill the four spectral spider constructs guarding the machine so she can safely extract her cargo, after which she will allow them to use the override code to shut the device down. She warned that if they refused, she might attempt the extraction regardless, risking an explosion that would destroy the entire keep.

Aletra is described as someone who exposed traitors not for honor but for profit, consistent with her current operation smuggling contraband through multiversal channels. Her last known position is beside the machine in the lower-level machine room, with the party at a standoff weighing her proposal.
