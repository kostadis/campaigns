## NPC Activity

**Harbin Wester**
Staged a public welcome ceremony at Phandalin's town boundary upon the party's return from Dragon Barrow. Delivered a speech praising the party's valor while prominently crediting his own foresight in establishing the Privy Council as the reason such heroes came to Phandalin. Performed political credit-claiming before the victory could develop independent historical standing.

**Adabra**
Called an Emerald Enclave council meeting during Reidoth's absence, having pre-secured sufficient votes to make the outcome a foregone conclusion rather than a genuine deliberation. Presented physical evidence: she personally walked to the sacred grove in the Whispering Wood three weeks prior and found the treant dead, the roots collapsed, the crown black, and the grove ruined. Accused Soma of dangerous interventionism, of holding Meril's Staff without Enclave blessing, and of teaching others to interfere rather than observe. Used Vukradin's earlier account to her (for which she paid him five gold pieces) as evidence of deliberate deception when she found the account did not match the ruined grove. After losing the council vote, departed the glade in silence with the posture and affect of someone who had been outvoted rather than persuaded. Did not accept the verdict.

**Lyra**
Present at the Emerald Enclave council. Stood before the council alongside Thomas and gave an open account of what happened at the Whispering Wood rift: her people had been tasked with handling the metastasized treant, they failed, the party arrived and killed it. Delivered this without apparent shame or defiance. Was restored to full good standing within the Emerald Enclave following the council's verdict.

**Thomas**
One of Lyra's trusted agents. Intercepted the party on the road to the druid council to warn them that Adabra was moving to ostracize Lyra. Disclosed that Reidoth was away and that Adabra had timed the council to his absence. Later stood alongside Lyra at the council and corroborated her account of the Whispering Wood events.

**Reidoth**
Absent from the council. Nominal head of the local Emerald Enclave chapter. His absence was deliberate from Adabra's perspective — she called the council during his absence to reduce procedural resistance. Vukradin used Sending to contact Reidoth before leaving the glade, informing him of the schism, Adabra's faction, Falcon's confession, and the council's condition.

**Falcon**
Appeared at the Emerald Enclave council seated beside Adabra, having been recruited to her cause. Publicly attacked Vukradin by name, stating that "trees are dead because of men like him" and endorsing Adabra's position. When Vukradin confronted him privately and challenged the hypocrisy of a former interventionist condemning interventionism, Falcon gave a public confession to the council about his adventuring past, the dragons he failed to fight, the lodge that burned while heroes stood by, and his accumulated losses. Framed this as evidence of the futility of intervention. After the council ruled against Adabra, was counseled by Valphine. Expressed regret, acknowledged he owed Vukradin an apology, stated he did not know why he had supported Adabra, and said the party needed to deal with the orcs. Asked Valphine to mediate his apology to Vukradin. Last seen in the glade following Valphine's counsel.

**Xanthopoulos** (centaur)
Arrived at the Emerald Enclave council from the high valleys. Explicitly stated he was on neither Adabra's nor Soma's side, positioning himself as an independent witness. Identified Meril's Staff and stated that Meril had not given it to Soma for sentiment but had deliberately chosen her. Delivered news that Corbin was dead and that Sister Kayla had escaped — "walked out" and "gone to the wind." Departed the glade after the council concluded. Made a quiet remark, apparently not fully intended for Soma's ears, that "perhaps Meril made the right choice" in selecting her.

**Aldric Stone Path**
A druid present at the council, seated with Adabra's faction. Had his position undercut when Soma asked plainly what Adabra's actual solution was to the planar infestations, and Aldric could not answer. Subsequently sat down. Later cited by the presiding druid as having "admitted freely" that following Adabra's position would treat the orc situation as normal and acceptable.

**Corbin**
Confirmed dead. Was left by the party guarding Sister Kayla. Killed, presumably by Sister Kayla, who then escaped. His death was raised by Xanthopoulos at the council and used by Adabra as evidence of the costs of careless interventionism. Not physically present; discussed as a casualty.

**Sister Kayla**
Escaped from Corbin's custody. Described as having "walked out" and "gone to the wind." Current whereabouts unknown. Previously characterized as a killer in priest's clothing.

**Bryn and Giles**
Members of Valphine's Temple of Lathander congregation. Conducted a public religious fundraising parade through Phandalin involving self-flagellation. Returned to the group visibly scarred and sunburnt in patterns consistent with extended self-mortification. Were smiling and appeared spiritually fulfilled. Observed by the party and townspeople upon return from the druid council road.

**Meril**
Referenced throughout; not present. Former holder of the staff now carried by Soma. Described as having trained Soma late, having named things she already knew instinctively, and having deliberately chosen her as the staff's recipient rather than leaving it sentimentally. Xanthopoulos stated Meril "picked her." Soma reflects that Meril may have been unable to resolve the interventionism debate himself and selected someone outside the Enclave's habits to hold the question. Deceased or departed; exact status not specified.

## Faction Movements

**Emerald Enclave**
Experienced a formal internal schism at a council convened by Adabra during Reidoth's deliberate absence. Adabra's faction, which included Falcon and Aldric Stone Path, argued for strict non-interventionism and sought Lyra's ostracism and Soma's censure. The moderate faction ruled against Adabra: Lyra was restored to full standing, Soma's interventionism was upheld as correct, and Adabra's personal attacks on Vukradin were declared to have "crossed the line." Adabra departed without accepting the verdict; her faction remains intact and potentially organizing outside the formal council structure. Reidoth was not present and is aware of events via Vukradin's Sending. The council expressed desire for Vukradin to return to perform music — their "deepest disappointment" per Brewbarry's account.

**Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice (Valphine's church)**
Received a transfer of unclaimed property via Vukradin, framed as moral hygiene. Growing congregation in Tresendar Manor. Publicly demonstrated doctrine through Bryn and Giles's self-flagellation parade. Valphine used the post-council period to counsel Falcon, positioning the church as a resource for purposeful suffering and redemption rather than passive grief. Falcon now has a relationship with Valphine and has requested her mediation with Vukradin.

**Privy Council (Phandalin)**
Harbin Wester publicly claimed credit for the dragon's death by association through the Privy Council's existence. No structural changes noted this session.

## Revealed Information

- **Adabra's council was pre-arranged**: She deliberately convened during Reidoth's absence, having already secured the votes needed. Thomas confirmed Reidoth's presence could not stop her speaking but would have changed the council's texture. This indicates Adabra had sufficient political infrastructure to act unilaterally within the Enclave.
- **Adabra paid Vukradin five gold pieces** for intelligence about the grove (specifically about the "planar magpie") and believed him at the time. She later discovered his account did not match the dead treant she found personally. She used this as evidence of deliberate deception before the council.
- **Meril's Staff is actively responding to Soma's interventionism**: The staff's leaves are diminishing while its powers are growing. Soma has observed this directly. Xanthopoulos confirmed Meril chose Soma deliberately, not sentimentally, suggesting the staff's behavior may be intentional design rather than malfunction.
- **Xanthopoulos confirmed Meril's deliberate selection of Soma**: "He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he picked her." This is the first external corroboration that Soma's possession of the staff was Meril's intentional choice rather than an informal bequest.
- **Falcon's support for Adabra was emotionally rather than doctrinally motivated**: His public confession revealed he had attached himself to her framework because it gave his accumulated losses a principled name. After Valphine's counsel, he disavowed Adabra and stated the orcs needed to be dealt with.
- **Corbin is confirmed dead; Sister Kayla escaped**: The party left a former criminal guarding a dangerous prisoner without adequate precautions. Xanthopoulos reported the outcome without editorializing.
