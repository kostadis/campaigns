Welcomed the party, allowed Vukardin to pay for meals with music, offered complimentary beer after hearing original compositions, warned the party quietly about white dragon sightings in the area, and directed them to the townmaster's quest board for paid work.

Posted three active quests on the job board. Paid the party for completing the Dwarven Excavation quest. **Dazlyn Grayshard** – Shield dwarf prospector. Located at ancient dwarven ruins in the canyon southwest of Phandalin. Introduced himself and partner to the party, explained the history of the Abbathor temple site, revealed they had found something frightening deeper in the temple, offered sending stones as payment for clearing the threat. Expressed delight when the Orcs were killed and cooked a celebratory meal for the party.

**Norbus Ironrune** – Shield dwarf prospector and business partner of Dazlyn. Present at the same ruins. Confirmed to Vukardin that found items would be returned to rightful owners (veracity unclear). Otherwise acted in concert with Dazlyn throughout the encounter.

**Linene Graywind** – Master of the Lionshield Coster outpost in Phandalin. Greeted the party, purchased looted orc weapons and armor, acknowledged disruption to the Coster's preferred transportation network (implied to be their interdimensional supply chain), and shared a rumor about strange magic pervading Neverwinter Wood that confounds navigators and obscures ancient ruins.

## Faction Movements

**Lord Neverember / Neverwinter Governance** – Actively using adventurers as proxies to pacify the Phandalin hinterland ahead of formal military and legal expansion. Policy confirmed by a Wintershield guard in flashback: adventurers clear threats, loot freely, and Neverwinter follows to establish order. Found treasure can be surrendered to Wintershield for a 60-day holding period, after which unclaimed goods are split between the guard's retirement fund and the finder. This policy is operational and being administered through townmaster quest boards.

**Lionshield Coster** – Suffering disruption to their previously superior interdimensional supply chain. Now operating on the same footing as conventional suppliers. The nature of the disruption is unconfirmed but Linene's phrasing suggests it is ongoing and significant. The faction's logistics network, previously described as an "unstoppable force in the realms," is currently compromised.

**Abbathor Temple Cult (Historical)** – No living members. The dwarven settlement was destroyed by an earthquake and avalanche believed to have been caused by Abbathor himself when priests' sacrifices were deemed insufficient. The temple structure still stands partially, with deeper sections unexplored and reportedly containing something that frightened the dwarf prospectors.

- Toblen Stonehill independently warned the party of white dragon sightings becoming more common.

- The Umbrage Hill quest confirms the dragon's range extends south of Phandalin, endangering isolated residents.

The dragon has not been encountered directly but its influence is shaping all active quest activity in the region. Arc score should reflect: established territorial presence, multiple independent confirmations, active suppression effect on other threats (bandits), and quest infrastructure being organized around it.

## Revealed Information

- **Lord Neverember's hinterland strategy**: Adventurers are being used as an unofficial vanguard to pacify the Phandalin region. Formal Neverwinter military and legal presence will follow once the area is cleared. This is deliberate policy, not improvisation.
- **Lionshield Coster's interdimensional network is down**: The supply chain disruption is confirmed as affecting the Coster specifically and appears tied to something beyond ordinary trade disruption. Linene's comment about Neverwinter Wood's strange magic may be related.
- **Neverwinter Wood anomaly**: Strange magic is pervading Neverwinter Wood, confounding navigators and obscuring ancient ruins. Source and nature unknown.
- **Abbathor temple history**: The temple was the site of blood and gemstone sacrifices during solar eclipses. The god destroyed his own worshippers when sacrifices were found lacking. The deeper sections of the temple contain an unidentified threat that scared the dwarf prospectors.
- **Ogre on the mountain path**: A second ogre encountered on the route to Gnomengarde. Soma claimed the party had killed its brother, which provoked a rage response — suggesting the previous ogre and this one may be related or known to each other.
- **Dazlyn and Norbus's claim about returning artifacts**: Norbus stated found items would be returned to rightful owners. This claim is unverified and contextually implausible given their stated purpose of treasure-seeking.
