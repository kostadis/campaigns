## NPC Activity

**Lyra (Emerald Enclave Leader)**
- Appeared trapped in the Whispering Grove alongside other Enclave members, ensnared by blighted brambles.
- Identified herself as a member of the Emerald Enclave and explained that the Treant was formerly the Grove's Guardian, now corrupted by a planar infection the Enclave was attempting to cleanse when it turned on them.
- Called on the party to fight the Treant and prevent the corruption from spreading.
- Produced four crude ritual stones and explained the cleansing ritual mechanics to the party (six successes required, three failures means total failure).
- Was temporarily freed from the brambles, then re-ensnared by the Treant during combat.
- After combat, admitted to the party that she had not thought the plan through carefully.
- Delivered an extensive explanation of the Emerald Enclave's internal schism between Naturalists and Interventionists, tracing the dispute to the War of the Dragons, Tiamat's summoning, the Council of Waterdeep, Delaan Winterhound's decision to join the fight at the urging of the hero Malfire, and the subsequent argument that Tiamat's defeat triggered the War of the Giants by inspiring the Giant gods to shatter the Ordning.
- Confirmed that the Treant had been consuming people and was no longer sustained by sunlight but by the planar pools.
- Expressed distress when the ritual failed: "This did not work the way I had hoped."
- Last seen in the Whispering Grove as the party withdrew.

**The Corrupted Treant (Grove's Guardian)**
- Appeared at the center of the blighted clearing, bark obsidian-black, limbs bent in impossible geometries, dripping glowing sap into a dark pool.
- Warned the party to leave its grove, claiming it would not attack if left alone.
- Declared: "You think I'm wrong! You think there is something wrong with me! That I must be changed back! I am… happy to be powerful and dangerous!"
- Attacked an Enclave Warrior, leaving them at one hit point.
- Re-ensnared Lyra with vines during combat.
- Killed by Soma and the first Enclave Warrior (poison and arrows).
- Corpse subsequently harvested by the Puppet Creature.

**The Puppet Creature**
- Emerged from the planar rift after the ritual failed and the clearing destabilized.
- Described as phased out, shimmering at the edge of perception, moving with unsettling marionette-like grace, eyes distorted.
- Collected specific ingredients from the Treant's corpse in a deliberate, purposeful manner.
- Withdrew back into the planar well after harvesting.
- Identified by Brewbarry as being under external control: "He's a puppet that's being controlled."

## Faction Movements

**Emerald Enclave (Interventionist Faction)**
- Dispatched Lyra and at least two Warriors to the Whispering Grove to perform a planar cleansing ritual on the corrupted Treant.
- Mission failed: ritual was not completed (reached three failures), the rift expanded rather than being sealed, and the party withdrew.
- Suffered casualties: one Warrior reduced to 1 hit point during combat, requiring healing from Vukradin; broader losses implied by the trapped and battered state of the group on arrival.
- Resources lost: the ritual stones were used but the ritual was not completed; Treant corpse harvested by an unknown outside actor.
- Lyra confirmed the Enclave remains internally divided between Naturalists (who accept planar intrusions as part of the multiverse's natural cycle) and Interventionists (who treat planar intrusions as corruption requiring active cleansing).

**Naturalists (Internal Emerald Enclave Faction / Adabra's Alignment)**
- Not physically present but represented philosophically. Lyra confirmed this faction views the planar wells, corrupted Treant, and blighted grove as natural phenomena within the broader multiverse and opposes ritual intervention.
- Adabra (Soma's mentor) is implicitly aligned with or sympathetic to this position, though her awareness of a "motivated blight" or puppet actor is unconfirmed.

**Unknown Controlling Entity (behind the Puppet Creature)**
- Demonstrated purposeful intelligence: sent a controlled operative to harvest specific materials from the Treant's corpse immediately after the ritual failure destabilized the grove.
- Operative vanished back into the planar rift, suggesting the entity has access to or operates through the planar tears.
- Motive and identity unknown.

## Threat Arc Events

**Planar Distortion — Score Increase**
- The cleansing ritual failed (three failures reached). The iridescent pools and planar wells in the Whispering Grove visibly expanded, pulsing with intensified sickly light. The earth shuddered with unnatural internal tremors. The rift is now described as possessing the grove rather than merely afflicting it. The wells remain active and growing.
- An entity sent a controlled operative through the rift to harvest Treant corpse materials, indicating the rift is actively being exploited by an outside force.

## Revealed Information

- The Emerald Enclave's Naturalist/Interventionist schism originates in the War of the Dragons. The Interventionist faction, persuaded by the hero **Malfire**, convinced Enclave leader **Delaan Winterhound** to join the Council of Waterdeep's coalition against Tiamat's summoning by **Severin**. The Naturalist faction holds that this intervention violated their oaths.
- The Naturalist faction further argues that Tiamat's defeat was itself the cause of the War of the Giants, as it inspired the Giant gods to shatter the Ordning — framing Interventionist action as directly responsible for subsequent catastrophe.
- The Whispering Grove's Treant was confirmed via Soma's mentor-gifted staff to be unnatural: the staff glowed in response to the Treant, the pools, and the brambles, identifying all three as planar-corrupted and outside the natural order.
- The Treant was no longer sustained by sunlight but by the planar pools, and had been consuming people.
- The Puppet Creature's harvest of the Treant corpse was deliberate and targeted, suggesting the corrupting entity has a specific use for materials from planar-infected sources.
- Adabra (Soma's mentor) has apparently never described the possibility of a "motivated blight" — corruption with a directing intelligence behind it — which Soma now recognizes as a significant gap in Adabra's account.
