## Valphine

Valphine participated in the siege at Falcon's Lodge, serving as tactical planner, combatant, and divine channeler throughout the engagement.

Her primary tactical contribution was conceiving and executing the Silence strategy: she cast Silence on Boney's saddle, directing the skeletal horse in a looping arc around the back wall of the courtyard (avoiding opportunity attacks) to position the silence bubble over the Anchorites, cutting them off from spellcasting. She explained this plan to Vukradin when he initially misread it as a plan for her to ride Boney into the enemy casters. The plan worked against the Anchorites inside the bubble, though one Anchorite stepped clear of the zone and continued casting. Boney's attack of opportunity on that fleeing Anchorite missed.

In combat, Valphine stripped Anchorite Number 1 of his sight, blinding him and causing him to stand motionless during the melee — a condition she described as "taking her sacrament." The blindness eventually wore off, but the Anchorite had lost his moment. She subsequently sent a bolt in Lathander's name toward the most wounded cultist, nearly finishing him. She invoked the power of Lathander explicitly during these actions, consistent with her established practice of naming her god when she acts.

After Vukradin broke the last surviving Anchorite with a vision of Lathander, Valphine responded to his screaming confession with quiet satisfaction, saying something about "needling" that Brewbarry noted but did not fully catch. She observed Prutha's spear strikes as "pretty effective" and filed the information away in her characteristic manner.

In the aftermath, Valphine approached three of the surviving allied orcs directly and proposed that they stay at the lodge as stewards. Three agreed. She then disengaged from the outcome entirely: "Now it's not my problem." She sang *The Hand Obould Offered* alongside Vukradin during the post-battle celebration, harmonizing with him in front of the orc war party.

She raised the question of Don Jon Raskin — specifically, a creative ritual to power the working that recalls a soul from the far side of death entire, one that would not require a cooperative cleric. Her eyes had "that particular quality they get when she's entertaining an idea she knows is irregular," per Soma's observation. She did not elaborate further in this session.

Valphine offered the practical assessment when Falcon shot at fleeing orcs: "If you don't kill them, they're just gonna come back" — presented as the logic of her upbringing underground, where nothing that fled came back a friend, rather than as a moral argument.

Last known status: present at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, having just concluded the siege engagement.

## Vukradin

Vukradin opened the battle at Falcon's Lodge with a mass Hypnosis working that charmed ten of the enemy orcs, leaving six uncharmed — Gorthok among the latter. He immediately cautioned all allies not to attack the charmed orcs ("As long as they are hypnotized, they will remain hypnotized... the only thing is that somebody else could wake them"), delivered with characteristic pleasantness and the bard's instinct to make sure everyone understands the rules. He counted the charmed tokens twice, reaching ten both times, and expressed restrained disappointment that he hadn't gotten more.

The charmed orcs were subsequently woken by the uncharmed orcs — a hand on a shoulder was all it took — and three of Vukradin's charmed converts shook free. His response was brief: "F. Fine. I didn't need those workings anyway." He processed the loss and moved on.

He attempted a Command on Gorthok that failed ("Asshole"), then confirmed with Soma that no one was currently holding any luck resource he could use. He subsequently issued a dual Command — *Grovel* — targeting both the Anchorite nearest Prutha and Gorthok simultaneously, dropping both. He expressed particular satisfaction at Gorthok's snout pressing into the dirt and narrated the outcome with evident pleasure: "On your face, and everything you had is gone."

His explicit motivation for targeting the Anchorite near Prutha was stated plainly: "We gotta save Prutha." He named Prutha as his first named convert and connected this to his prior loss of Corbin, though he consciously chose not to let himself dwell on that connection during the fight.

When Gorthok revived (a stored Revivify-like effect), Vukradin and Soma exchanged a brief confirmation — "Word" / "He died to the moonbeam" / "Moonbeam" — as the repositioned Moonbeam finished the job at the end of Gorthok's turn.

After Soma went down and the Moonbeam extinguished, Vukradin tracked the loss in real time: "No, no more Moonbeam." He noted the gap in Boney's silence coverage when one Anchorite remained outside the bubble.

Against the final surviving Anchorite, Vukradin deployed a vision of Lathander — rendering the god in whatever light lives inside a bard's head — which broke the cultist entirely. The Anchorite screamed a fractured confession: "She's real! The drows, she's real! Talos, my god! Talos!" and "I burned the shrine! I broke the handles! I did what you asked! Why is he here?" Vukradin does not appear to have interrogated the confession's specific contents in this session.

In the aftermath, Vukradin joined Valphine in singing *The Hand Obould Offered*, remarking that he had always thought it was a cheap manipulative song and was surprised to find himself singing it and meaning every word. He observed that Falcon "has no real core convictions," not unkindly. He retreated to the guest house, citing exhaustion — "I threw everything I had at them, and most of it bounced" — and cautioned the party not to steal any treasure while he napped. He remained audible through the wall, tracking the conversation about Don Jon Raskin and offering "Red Wizards of Lathander" as a half-joking suggestion. He listed new workings acquired: the art of becoming someone else's shadow, the art of rewriting a body entire, and a door that opens onto elsewhere.

Last known status: retreating to the guest house at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, exhausted but alive.

## Brewbarry

Brewbarry fought throughout the battle at Falcon's Lodge as a frontline combatant using his halberd (the Elemental Cleaver, taken from a dead man) and Barbarian rage.

He took a lightning bolt from an Anchorite outside the silence bubble early in the fight — the bolt caught him and Falcon both, hitting him in the left shoulder through his scale mail. The heat spread in a way that made his teeth ache but did not drop him. He described surviving worse from Cryovain's breath and walking.

When Soma went down to Gorthok's tusks, Brewbarry did not pause to process it — "you grieve later" — and entered rage. He described it as borrowing from "a bigger version of yourself," with the Elemental Cleaver producing acid damage. His first swing on an Anchorite connected across the collarbone, the acid hissing after the steel; his second swing went wide. Using confidence lent by Vukradin's earlier working, he landed a follow-up strike that dropped the Anchorite.

In the later phase of the fight, he cleaved through the last orc with a swing that still had enough momentum to carry into the next target. He was operating at approximately twice his normal size due to rage. When Vukradin deployed the Lathander vision against the final Anchorite, Brewbarry crossed to the broken cultist and delivered a killing blow — noting internally that the Anchorite was already dying but that "a kill deserves the full count." He finished the swing out of principle.

After the battle, Brewbarry descended to the cellar and discovered that the Anchorites had staved the wine casks before the fight, emptying them into the earth. His grief was described as genuine and full-sized. He stood looking at the wreckage and said: "Oh my god. I am depressed." He added: "I know Talos is the god of chaos. But come on. Gotta be some limits."

He noted with quiet pleasure that he would now be going first in initiative — "going first, actually going first, for once, nice" — reading this from a page, suggesting a new ability or feature acquired at level-up.

Last known status: present at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, alive, grieving the wine.

## Soma

Soma fought in elk form for the majority of the battle at Falcon's Lodge, maintaining the Moonbeam and directing Boney's positioning throughout.

She tracked Boney's movement geometry aloud during the planning phase, confirming that looping him around the back wall to cover the Anchorites would require a full push. When Vukradin misread this as Boney needing to die, she corrected him without inflection: "To get back there without drawing a strike, yeah, that's a full push."

In combat, she held the Moonbeam over multiple targets sequentially — repositioning it over Anchorite Number 2 and Gorthok — and connected with a hoof strike on Gorthok that she described with brief satisfaction: "Get hooved, baby." She stripped Anchorite Number 2 to near-nothing through a combination of Boney's opportunity strike and the Moonbeam's lingering burn.

Against Gorthok, she rolled a twenty-nine on the Moonbeam (two tens and a nine), which was halved by Gorthok's resistance but still left him barely alive. She then announced that her hoof strike would kill him unless she rolled double ones — and immediately recognized she had cursed herself. She did not roll double ones. Gorthok died to her hoof, then revived via a stored Revivify-like effect. The Moonbeam was still positioned over him and finished the job at the end of his turn.

Gorthok hit Soma twice in succession, dropping her out of elk form. The Moonbeam extinguished when she fell. She confirmed its loss to Vukradin flatly: "Yep. Moonbeam goes away." She noted that she lay flat on the ground with Meril's staff somewhere on the dirt nearby — five leaves, none reachable from that position — and conducted a quiet internal accounting of what she had left. "Not much. But I was still inside it."

After the battle, she objected to Falcon shooting at fleeing orcs ("Such senseless violence") and objected again to Prutha's axe work ("Guys, calm down"), calling both unnecessary. She engaged Vukradin briefly on the point — "there's a well of orcs, there are always more orcs beyond the horizon" — without full resolution. She did not sing during the post-battle celebration but stood and listened as Vukradin and Valphine harmonized with the orc war party, and thought of Meril: "Maybe he knew something."

She offered "To steal their life, not their treasure" after Vukradin's parting remark about the treasure, noting herself that it was advice for a different situation entirely. She observed Vukradin's listing of new workings and called the door-to-elsewhere ability "always nice."

At the end of the session she sat on a section of ruined fence, felt new depth and reach she didn't have before — described as feeling weather coming in, a readiness not yet given — and remained there as dusk settled.

Last known status: seated at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, alive, sensing new capability.

## Vorga

Vorga is the Warchief of the allied orc contingent that fought alongside the party at Falcon's Lodge. Her name was spoken by Prutha earlier with particular weight, and she is identified as "Long-Memory."

During the battle's resolution, Vorga let out a War Cry that Soma felt physically before understanding — "a sound that doesn't just carry volume but instruction." The cry granted advantage to Falcon, Soma, and Brewbarry, all of whom were within its radius. Vukradin was not in range.

After the battle, Vorga shouted at full war-chief volume — Soma's Orcish was insufficient to parse all of it — but the shape was clear: she was very pleased, gesturing at the party with an arm-sweep that meant *mine*, claiming them as members of the tribe. Her warriors echoed the claim with a sound Soma described as "a storm deciding to be happy instead."

No further details about Vorga's background, plans, or relationship to the Talosian cult are established in this session beyond her role as allied war-chief and her post-battle adoption of the party into her tribe.

Last known status: present at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, celebratory.

## Prutha

Prutha is an orc who was established earlier as Vukradin's first named convert. He fought in the allied orc line during the battle at Falcon's Lodge.

During the melee, Prutha charged an enemy orc in the cultist line and addressed him in Orcish — a torrent described as guttural, specific, and clearly anatomical in its imagery. Valphine translated this for Vukradin as "greeting each other in traditional orchest form," which Vukradin accepted as covering the fact that they were simultaneously swinging swords at each other.

He put two spear strikes into a prone Anchorite, both landing. Valphine noted this as "pretty effective."

In the final phase, Prutha charged an Anchorite while screaming, first in Orcish, then in the common tongue: "Die, you scum of scums!" He then screamed: "For uncle, for my uncle, and the sun god, and Lathander" — and something about Lathander burning all of them under his fiery bright light. Valphine's response was a dry "Alright." Vukradin watched Prutha drive into the Anchorite and reflected on the night he had acknowledged him as his first convert — noting the difference between a name you give someone and the weight they choose to carry it at, and observing that Prutha was carrying his at a run, screaming theology.

After the battle, Prutha used his axe on at least one of the fleeing or defeated orcs, prompting Soma's "Guys, calm down." He also shouted something in Orcish about the gods and the sun.

The reference to "uncle" during his battle cry has not been previously established in available session notes and may represent new character information.

Last known status: present at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, alive, having fought hard for Lathander and his uncle.

## Falcon the Hunter

Falcon the Hunter is the lord of Falcon's Lodge, which was under Talosian siege during this session.

During the battle, Falcon fired twice at the nearest Anchorite. One arrow hit cleanly; the second skipped wide. He did not comment on the miss. He was struck by the same lightning bolt that hit Brewbarry — from an Anchorite outside Boney's silence bubble.

In the later phase of the fight, Falcon landed three consecutive longsword strikes on an Anchorite, dealing real damage across all three hits. He touched his brass rings before charging and muttered something to someone who wasn't there. Brewbarry did not ask about the rings. Soma observed: "This is like, you know, the defining moment of the Falcon, right?" This was noted as a contrast to his earlier stated survival strategy of refusing to act.

After the battle, Falcon shot at two orcs fleeing toward the treeline before the dust had settled — prompting Soma's "Such senseless violence" objection. He subsequently reversed completely, shouting "Huzzah!" with an enthusiasm that "would have embarrassed him a week ago" and putting his arm around an orc's shoulders, vibrating with the joy of a man who found out that stepping in is fun when it works.

Vukradin observed: "He has no real core convictions," not unkindly. Soma compared the transformation to "finding a stranger in the Alps."

Falcon immediately adopted a lord-of-the-estate register when addressing the three orcs who agreed to stay as stewards: "The first thing we'll have to do is teach you how to dress properly. The three of you will become the finest stewards in all the North."

Last known status: present at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, newly enthusiastic about direct action, overseeing the integration of three orc stewards into his household.

## Gorthok

Gorthok is a giant boar, described as a creature of Talosian divine connection, present at the siege of Falcon's Lodge. It stood at the heart of the courtyard with flanks sheened in static electricity. It threw off Vukradin's Hypnosis working and was counted among the uncharmed six.

Its lightning attack was established as not a spell but something older and reflexive — a thing the creature simply does, like dragons breathe — and therefore not suppressible by Silence.

Gorthok hit Soma twice in succession, folding her on the first strike and ensuring her fall with the second. The tusks were described as the color of the inside of a cloud during a real storm — pale blue-white — and every hit came with thunder that didn't roll but landed.

Soma's hooves connected on Gorthok for meaningful damage. The Moonbeam dealt twenty-nine damage (halved by resistance) and left him barely alive. Soma's follow-up hoof strike killed him.

Gorthok then revived — not a natural rally but a convulsion of something beneath the flesh, a stored defiance reconstituted from Talosian will or bound-god stubbornness, described as analogous to Revivify. He rose after Soma's killing blow.

The Moonbeam was still positioned over him. At the end of his turn, the Moonbeam's lingering burn finished him. He did not rise again.

Soma confirmed: "He died to the moonbeam." Vukradin echoed: "Moonbeam."

Last known status: dead at Falcon's Lodge.
