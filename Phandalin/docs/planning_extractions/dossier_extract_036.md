## Cryovain

Cryovain, a white dragon, was found on the rooftop battlements of Icespire Hold at the center of a burning summoning circle of alien geometry. His eyes were glazed and filmed over — present but not fully himself — as the Carver's obsidian weapon worked to dominate him mentally. He was fighting the influence, or had been; how much of him remained was unclear. He launched off the battlements, climbed thirty feet into the air in one wingbeat, and breathed ice over Brewbarry twice — hits severe enough to push the Goliath past his limits. Soma's moonbeam struck him repeatedly, Vukradin's bardic inspiration cracked something vital, and Valphine's sacred flame landed a final 3 points of damage that killed him. With his death, the Carver's essence dissipated entirely. Soma argued he was a victim of the Carver's influence and urged the party not to kill him; the plea went unheeded. His body remained on the rooftop. His hoarded treasure — approximately 3,000 gold fused with residium and ice into an inseparable block through planar magic — was left unspendable and unresolved.

## The Carver

The Carver was an alien intelligence operating through an obsidian weapon — described as a mental amplifier — to dominate Cryovain via a burning geometric summoning circle on the battlements of Icespire Hold. It needed three more rounds to complete the ritual when the party engaged. When Cryovain was killed, the Carver's essence dissipated instantly and completely — not fled, not retreated, but unmade by the same stroke. Its obsidian sword remained behind, its crystal pommel containing a gem capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience. Vukradin recognized the gem as belonging to a rightful owner and refused to keep it despite the instrument being perfectly suited to his ambitions. The Carver had no further presence after the dragon's death.

## Sylvine Wintermoon

Sylvine Wintermoon arrived at Icespire Hold shortly after the battle with a companion named Runa, presenting herself and her sister as the rightful owners of Cryovain's hoard. She opened by asking whether Vukradin was the bard known for returning stolen treasure, then launched into a prepared performance: invoking the gods, calling Vukradin a saint, and framing the treasure as already spiritually returned with the handover a mere formality. Soma identified the approach immediately as rehearsed and calculated. Vukradin rejected her outright, calling her a liar and a cheat and telling her to leave. Sylvine performed wounded pride for less than a second before dropping it entirely — the tell, in Soma's assessment, that the injury was never real. She then pivoted without hesitation to "Second plan — we kill them all," revealing the con's backup. She led what followed with veterans as muscle. The fight ended with her forces defeated; her own final status is not explicitly recorded, though the veterans were killed or fled.

## Runa

Runa accompanied her sister Sylvine Wintermoon to Icespire Hold as part of a scripted approach to claim Cryovain's hoard. She spoke only to echo Sylvine's performance — "Blessed be! Blessed be!" — on cue, with the affect of someone waiting to remember their lines. Soma characterized her as having rehearsed the approach on the road. She had no independent action recorded during the confrontation or the subsequent fight.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen received Vukradin's Sending — "The dragon is dead, so is the Carver. We're heading to Neverwinter" — and replied with a message that Vukradin read as both warm and carefully managed. He praised Torm, called the victory a "conflict-free triumph" using Vukradin's own vocabulary, and redirected the party from Neverwinter to Phandalin, where he said the Gauntlet awaited them. He added a secondary message: "Well done. Come to Phandalin quick. Issues with the Emerald Enclave." Vukradin noted that Kaelen had absorbed and fed back his specific language, and couldn't determine whether this reflected genuine understanding or active management. He assessed Kaelen as "as close as anybody" he could trust — immediately correcting himself to clarify that he did not, in fact, trust him. Kaelen's last known position is Phandalin, where he is awaiting the party's arrival and has framed a celebratory reception. The nature of the "issues with the Emerald Enclave" was not elaborated upon.
