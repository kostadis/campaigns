## NPC Activity

**Adabra**
- Accompanied the party on the walk to Phandalin, philosophizing about the nature of monsters and interference with natural order.
- Directly confronted Townmaster Harbin on the party's behalf, demanding increased payment and delivering an extended speech advocating for principled champions over mercenary sellswords.
- Notably omitted Valphine from her advocacy speech; her reasons are unclear but suspected to be related to Valphine's Drow heritage.
- Secured room and board at the Stonehill Inn from Harbin when coin payment was refused.

**Townmaster Harbin Wester**
- Refused additional coin payment, citing bandit activity depleting town funds.
- Referenced a standing agreement with Lord Neverember permitting adventurers to pay themselves from monster treasure.
- Under pressure from Adabra, arranged free room and board at the Stonehill Inn as compensation.
- Confirmed knowledge of the coastal lighthouse and its cursed reputation, insisting Lord Neverember would want the curse removed rather than the tower destroyed.
- Directed the party to Barthen's Provisions to locate Ser Kaelen Thorn.

**Toblen Stonehill ("Spiderman")**
- Present at the Stonehill Inn as tavern keeper.
- Revealed origin of his nickname: as a child he walked calmly through a hatching spider's nest covered in baby spiders.
- Served drinks to the party and Ser Kaelen Thorn.

**Daran Edermath ("Silverleaf")**
- Half-elf, former adventurer, now an orchard keeper near Phandalin.
- Approached Vukradin at the Stonehill Inn with a treasure pitch: a cursed coastal lighthouse west of Phandalin that wrecks ships, with the implied reward being salvage from the wrecks.
- Received a sharp rebuke from Vukradin regarding plundering the dead; apologized for causing offense.

**Ser Kaelen Thorn**
- First appearance: observed at the Stonehill Inn, wearing chain mail and bearing the emblem of the Order of the Gauntlet (a gauntlet grasping a sword by the blade).
- Initiated private contact with Vukradin, declining to speak privately and instead speaking openly.
- Revealed membership in and recruitment on behalf of the Order of the Gauntlet.
- Cited the Order's role in the War of the Dragon and specifically named **Milo Goodbarrel** as the Order member who led the strike force that reached the inner sanctum and stopped the ritual.
- Extended membership offer to Vukradin as an associate member, promising wider reach for his message and music through Order backing.
- Extended a secondary membership offer to Valphine, which she declined implicitly.
- Explicitly excluded Soma from consideration, citing ideological incompatibility with naturalist philosophy.
- Acknowledged the brutal history between the Order and Brewbarry's tribe; offered a drink as a gesture of closure.
- Encountered again at Barthen's Provisions preparing to depart town.
- Assessed the cursed lighthouse situation as most likely caused by a monster haunting, with secondary possibilities of a curse requiring a cleric or mere unfounded rumor.

## Faction Movements

**Order of the Gauntlet**
- Actively recruiting in Phandalin; Ser Kaelen Thorn operating as a field recruiter.
- Successfully recruited Vukradin as an associate member.
- Framed recruitment around ideological alignment: opposition to plunder, championing the underdog, art as mission vehicle.
- Funding described as conflict-free gold sourced from donations.
- Claimed credit for ending the War of the Dragon via Milo Goodbarrel's strike force action.
- Demonstrated awareness of and antipathy toward naturalist philosophy (Soma excluded deliberately).
- Historical antagonist to Brewbarry's nomadic tribe during an unnamed war; Ser Kaelen acknowledged the war as brutal and "over nonsense."

## Revealed Information

- The Order of the Gauntlet was active during the **War of the Dragon**; a member named **Milo Goodbarrel** led the strike force that penetrated the inner sanctum and stopped an unspecified ritual.
- The coastal lighthouse west of Phandalin has a reputation for wrecking ships; the Townmaster considers the "evil lighthouse" framing to be rumor, and Ser Kaelen assesses the most probable cause as a haunting monster.
- Lord Neverember has a standing policy permitting adventurers to self-compensate from monster treasure, which Harbin uses to avoid direct payment.
- Phandalin is experiencing bandit activity that has strained the Townmaster's finances.
- Ser Kaelen Thorn holds a negative view of naturalist ideology and deliberately excluded Soma from Order recruitment consideration.
