## Sister Kella

Sister Kella is an enemy of the party who made contact with them prior to this session, offering to work with them against the Carver. She provided information on how to find the Carver and offered the party the reward she had previously promised to Lokudus as a down payment for their cooperation. Her motivations appear to be opposition to the Carver rather than any alignment with the party's values. Her offer caused significant internal conflict within the party, particularly with Vukradin, who refused to accept what he considered stolen goods or work with an evil person. Kella herself did not appear directly in this session; her actions were relayed by Valphine during the tavern discussion.

## Brin Bundlewine

Brin Bundlewine is a pale-skinned, sunburned villager who approached Valphine at the tavern alongside Giles Slipper-Shine. He had heard rumors of a drow who worshiped Lathander and believed in the purifying pain of the sun, and was eager to establish a chapel dedicated to this interpretation of the sun god. He agreed to renovate Tresendar Manor into the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice for twenty-five gold pieces to cover materials, providing his own labor for free.

## Giles Slipper-Shine

Giles Slipper-Shine is a pale-skinned, sunburned villager who accompanied Brin Bundlewine in approaching Valphine at the tavern. He shared Brin's enthusiasm for founding a chapel based on the interpretation of Lathander as a god of purifying solar pain. He participated in the agreement to renovate Tresendar Manor into the new temple, sharing in the arrangement to provide labor for free while twenty-five gold pieces covered materials.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester is the townmaster and leader of the Privy Council. He appeared conveniently during the temple planning discussion and offered to arrange the paperwork to convert Tresendar Manor into the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice for a modest personal fee of fifty gold pieces. He framed the transaction as a private arrangement outside the full council, describing it as simply how things were done in the Overbright, suggesting a pattern of quiet corruption or informal deal-making. When Vukradin returned from outside, Harbin quickly covered the transaction by claiming the funds had been a community donation, successfully avoiding Vukradin's scrutiny. Feeling generous after the deal, he offered to buy a round of beer for the tavern. He also requested the supply delivery quest to the Logger's Camp on behalf of his brother.

## Jena Roscoe

Jena Roscoe observed Vukradin storming out of the tavern and explained to the remaining party members that he was a man of principle who believed possessions belonged to their original owners. After Harbin's cover story satisfied Vukradin, she took the opportunity to explain to the party how investing adventuring money into the community effectively launders it, transforming blood money into legitimate community improvement. She suggested that Vukradin was young and might come to understand this concept with time.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen spoke with Vukradin the morning after the tavern discussion. He is awaiting information from Neverwinter regarding the rightful owners of treasure the party has found, and expected to have answers within a few days. He suggested the party could use the waiting time to check out Mountain's Toe or deliver supplies to the Logger's Camp. His last known location is in town.

## Barthen

Barthen operates Barthen's Provisions and filled the supply order for Harbin Wester's brother at the Logger's Camp. He provided two heavy crates of dried meats, cheese, bread, ale, and oil loaded onto a cart pulled by an ox named Vincent. When Brewbarry noticed no wine was included, Barthen suggested the party stop at the Falcon Hunting Lodge along the way, noting it was known for fine wine. He also provided a letter of introduction that granted the party access to the lodge. His last known location is his provisions shop in town.

## Corwin

Corwin is the chef and retainer at the Falcon Hunting Lodge. He greeted the party at the gate after they rang the bell, verified Barthen's letter of introduction, welcomed the party inside, and fetched Falcon the Hunter on their behalf. He is described as operating in a hospitality and service capacity at the lodge.

## Falcon the Hunter

Falcon the Hunter is the proprietor of the Falcon Hunting Lodge, a fortified compound in Neverwinter Wood surrounded by a ten-foot-high log palisade. He is described as having impressive mutton chops and expressed great delight at receiving guests, particularly friends of Barthen. He offered the party lodging, meals, and access to his fine wine cellar in exchange for musical entertainment from the party's bard.

Falcon explained that the unusually large palisade was built in response to orcs descending from the mountains since a dragon appeared in the area. He noted the orcs seemed more organized than usual and appeared to be working with evil half-orcs living in a ruined stone manse about ten miles southwest of the lodge. He offered a potential quest to deal with the half-orcs at what he called the Woodland Mants, with a pair of boots of elvenkind as a reward, though he suggested the party might need more experience first. He emphasized the boots were purchased legitimately with proceeds from his establishment, clearly trying to avoid provoking Vukradin again.

Falcon revealed during dinner that he had built the lodge using his share of treasure from a dragon's hoard his adventuring group had killed and split four ways. This caused Vukradin to accuse him of stealing from victims of the dragon, which genuinely confused Falcon. He mentioned hearing rumors of a new movement against adventurers using plunder, which he dismissed as an attempt to disrupt the ruling elite and undermine the traditional adventuring lifestyle. He demanded and received an apology from Vukradin, which Falcon accepted graciously despite it being insincere. He also arranged for Brewbarry to receive three bottles of wine as part of the talisman transaction with Sridar.

Falcon identified himself as an interventionist who believed in actively stopping planar incursions, contrasting himself with naturalists in the Emerald Enclave. He has retired from active adventuring, holding the philosophy that there are only young adventurers and old retired ones. His last known location is the Falcon Hunting Lodge.

## Sridar

Sridar is a drow staying as a guest at the Falcon Hunting Lodge. When Valphine asked if she was a cleric of Lathander, Sridar was confused, asking if it was some kind of elaborate ruse. She described herself as a collector of curious artifacts and items that weren't quite right, particularly those connected to planar events. Her interest began after a blink in the Underdark when demons appeared, and she has since dedicated herself to understanding planar transformations and disruptions.

Sridar paid thirty gold pieces for detailed information Valphine shared about a creature collecting strange items. She then identified the Talisman of Moesko the party was carrying as having a definite planar connection and purchased it for one hundred gold pieces, with Falcon also providing three bottles of wine for Brewbarry as part of the arrangement. This transaction was welcomed by Vukradin as legitimate fair trade.

Sridar speculated that the planar incursions could be caused by magic users, planar entities, or careless meddlers, though she had originally believed the events were a natural phenomenon analogous to the Faerzress in the Underdark. She found Brewbarry's suggestion that the incursions might be intentional intriguing. She identified herself as an interventionist alongside Falcon, contrasting their position with the Emerald Enclave's naturalist non-interference stance. She offered to help the party piece together clues about planar events if they brought her more information in the future. Her last known location is the Falcon Hunting Lodge.
