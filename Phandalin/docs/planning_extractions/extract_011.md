## NPC Activity

**The Shimmering Stag**
Appeared in the Whispering Wood, exhibiting planar instability: teleporting short distances, displaying constantly shifting antler shapes, and leaving brief afterimages. Spoke with Soma (via Speak with Animals), confirming the forest's disturbance began at its center. Reported that other unnamed individuals had previously visited, claimed they would "fix it," and had not done so. Accepted mushrooms from Gnomengarde as payment for information. Territorial but non-hostile once the party withdrew from its marked range.

## Threat Arc Events

**Planar Distortion — Multiple triggering events this session:**

- A thicket of brambles exhibiting planar leakage (pulsing unnatural colors, necrotic damage on contact, apparent regeneration) was encountered and destroyed by the party. The brambles regenerated during combat before being rendered inert.
- Wolves with magically glowing eyes and a supernatural howl capable of magically rooting targets were encountered and killed.
- A pool of water displaying distorted reflections of observers was discovered in a forest clearing. Contact with the pool inflicted overwhelming sensory visions and psychic trauma. Vukradin lost consciousness after entering; Brewbarry suffered a fear response and had to be physically restrained. Both exhibited signs of temporary madness.
- The stag confirmed the disturbances originated from **the center of the Whispering Wood**.

## Revealed Information

- The forest disturbances have a clear point of origin: **the center of the Whispering Wood**.
- An unknown prior group visited the stag and claimed they would resolve the disturbance. They have not done so. Their identity, affiliation, and current status are unknown.
- The pool's visions include a fragmented entity or presence that stated: *"He saw beyond, but only what he was meant to see. The numbers. Always the numbers."* The source and referent of this statement are unidentified.
- Brewbarry possesses knowledge of Uthgardt cultural lore, identifying such pools as "Shadows to other places" that "appear unnaturally" and offer knowledge — suggesting the phenomenon may have precedent in regional traditions.
