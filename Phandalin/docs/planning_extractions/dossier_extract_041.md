## Soma

Soma appeared in two distinct phases across these events. In the battle at the gully, she entered Wild Shape as a giant elk early in the fight, using the form to charge and ram the anchorite of Talos, driving him prone into the dirt. She absorbed significant damage in elk form throughout the fight, taking javelin hits from neutral orcs who reconsidered their neutrality and attacks from multiple combatants. She corrected Brewbarry mid-fight on the tactical advantage of attacking a prone target, which allowed Valphine to roll again and land the killing blow on the anchorite. When she had taken enough hits and the tactical situation shifted, she deliberately disengaged, accepting attacks of opportunity as she withdrew, dropped Wild Shape, and mounted Boney to remove both herself and the skeletal horse from the immediate melee. Her reasoning: get the abomination and the delicious meat away from the guys.

Boney — Soma's skeletal horse companion — fought throughout the battle, striking the prone anchorite with a longsword and surviving attacks from Orc 9, which Brewbarry intercepted. Soma's management of Boney as a tactical asset and her willingness to absorb punishment on his behalf was noted.

After the battle, Soma witnessed Xanth's departure. The centaur tapped her shell, named her as one blessed by Meril, and stated his belief she would do great things before leaving. She received this without visible response, but the narration makes clear she is actively processing the weight of it — the staff's three leaves, Xanth's certainty, the knowledge that Meril chose her on purpose. She is arguing with herself about what this means in the dark hours before dawn.

At the treeline below the Circle of Thunder, Soma perceived the staff respond to the presence of whatever was saturating the air above — a subtle shift, described as a dormant quality waking. She connected this internally to Meril, though did not complete the thought aloud. She is aware that whatever drew Meril to this region and whatever rot exists at the summit are directly related to why she is carrying the staff. She engaged in an argument with Vukradin about the necklace of bones — she wants to use it, he refuses to trust her with it. She also assessed the tactical situation at the henge, noting the twig blights were not clustered enough for Fireball.

Current status: at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder, staff in hand, preparing to move on the henge.

## Brewbarry

Brewbarry was the primary melee combatant in the gully battle. At the outset, Vukradin redirected a greataxe blow from Orc 2 intended for himself, converting it into advantage for Brewbarry's next attack. Brewbarry used this advantage to land a significant hit on Orc 7, killing him.

The anchorite's lightning bolt struck Brewbarry along with Vukradin and Xanth. He remained standing. When Soma drove the anchorite prone, Brewbarry moved through the orc line — surviving both attacks of opportunity — to reach the anchorite, landed a clean hit with the halberd bolstered by Bless, missed the second swing, then rolled again with advantage from the prone condition and scored an eighteen, killing the anchorite. He was raging at the time.

He intercepted an attack from Orc 9 that was aimed at Boney, taking the hit himself while raging. Later, he killed Orc 9 after Valphine told him to stomp the orc — the non-lethal call came too late, and the orc died. This was noted with mild rueful acknowledgment from Vukradin. Brewbarry successfully knocked Orc 10 unconscious with a declared non-lethal strike — fifteen points of force, one hit, clean. A fleeing orc received a swing that missed, read by the narration as a deliberate miss signaling the orc was not worth the expenditure.

Throughout the battle, Brewbarry operated as an effective but blunt instrument, sometimes killing targets that others wanted kept alive. His thinking in combat is immediate and reactive. Vukradin expressed affectionate exasperation at the pattern. Brewbarry's relationship with rage is described as simplification rather than loss of self.

Current status: present at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder, preparing to advance on the henge.

## Valphine

Valphine's decision to let radiant light bloom from her hands early in the confrontation was the trigger for the anchorite naming her a false cleric and ordering the orc line into hostility. The three orcs Vukradin had been reaching through his speech were still hesitating, but the remainder committed to the fight at that moment.

In combat, Valphine identified the anchorite as the priority target and directed Brewbarry toward him. She called the killing blow — rolling an eighteen on the re-roll enabled by the prone condition — and the anchorite died. She also called Brewbarry's stomp on Orc 9 (resulting in unintended death) and the non-lethal declaration on Orc 10 (successfully executed).

She attempted to address the orcs in Orcish mid-combat, calling on them to kneel before Lathander. Her self-assessment afterward was frank: the compound liability of being a drow, arriving with an undead skeletal horse and a barbarian in a dread helm with glowing red eyes, and delivering an unstructured mid-combat religious exhortation did not constitute a legible theological proposition. One orc who had been neutral returned to hostility as a direct result of her appeal. She received the observation that orcs don't like drows and that this was not the best organized religious ceremony without apparent argument.

Post-battle, she suggested the conversion name Thrall for Prutha, which Vukradin rejected loudly on ethical grounds. She participated in stringing the dead anchorite up as a warning, which Prutha enthusiastically assisted with. She reached for a Religion assessment of the bone whistle ritual and described her result only as "oh my god," suggesting the dice failed her — but she arrived at a genuine insight anyway, that the sunrise whistle tradition could be incorporated into her own evolving practice and that Lathander was moving closer to her through it rather than further.

Her internal narration of the battle reflects a Menzoberranzan-trained tactical mind: kill the spellcaster first, read the hierarchy, understand why a violent theology holds together and why it dissolves. She understands the appeal of Talos from structural familiarity and names it explicitly as small — rationed violence, spent carefully, always at risk of running out. She left a similar system behind not for moral reasons but because it was insufficient.

She noticed that Vukradin named Lathander before correcting himself mid-speech. She filed this observation and said nothing about it.

Current status: present at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder. Proposed Spirit Guardians plus Dash as the tactical approach for the summit assault.

## Vukradin

Vukradin opened the confrontation with the orc warband by attempting diplomacy in Orcish, delivering a speech about Lathander timed to reach the orcs before violence began. He rolled a twenty-six on the persuasion, moving seven orcs toward receptivity. He was subsequently informed by Valphine that she also speaks Orcish, which she had not mentioned — a revelation he received with the stillness of someone calculating how many special abilities he had spent unnecessarily. He remarked on having made a point of talking in orc, finding himself in the position of a man who had paid for a specific service the room didn't require.

During the battle, he redirected Orc 2's greataxe blow away from himself and converted it into advantage for Brewbarry — described by Brewbarry's narration as the thing he always does, taking a hit meant for him and turning it into something useful for someone else. He was struck by the anchorite's lightning bolt along with Brewbarry and Xanth. He was hit again by Orc 2 when the orc's offer of money in exchange for their lives was declined — Vukradin told him the followers of Lathander don't make such demands, the orc picked the greataxe back up, and the hit landed.

Post-battle, he expressed ongoing commitment to conversion over killing, calling out to fleeing orcs as they ran and urging Brewbarry toward non-lethal options — sometimes with enough lead time to matter, sometimes not. He described the need to work on the spectacle of their religious presentation.

The conversion of Prutha was his primary success of the session. He delivered a sincere Lathander speech to the last standing orc, framing the scene — the drow in impossible light, the dead Talosian anchorite, the gully itself — as a genuine picture of available truth. When Prutha declared himself a follower, Vukradin said "Wow. We've got a follower!" with complete sincerity. He strongly objected to the conversion name Thrall on ethical grounds, calling it harsh and inappropriate for their new friend. Prutha kept his name.

He received Prutha's account of his great-uncle — killed deliberately by Talosians to break the clan's resistance — and the old shaman's line about dawn coming for everyone, even those born in shadow. He said "Wow, it's quite poetic" and meant it. He told Prutha he was his first, then observed that since Lathander is perpetual dawn, the bone whistle that greets the dawn could theoretically be blown at any time. He appeared genuinely pleased by how cleanly this resolved.

He objected to the corpse-stringing that followed but did not intervene. He disapproved of Prutha's enthusiastic kicks to the anchorite's body in a way he also did not act on.

The narration notes that Vukradin named Lathander before correcting himself mid-speech. The nature of the correction — and what name preceded Lathander — is not specified, but Valphine observed and filed it.

He carried a thought about Corbin throughout the session — the flash of the face, the weight settling. He did not complete the accounting in the middle of the gully. When someone made a joke about whether Corbin would have liked Prutha, the DM called it cold. Vukradin agreed it was cold, and also believed Corbin would have liked Prutha very much and would have recognized something in the whistle, the great-uncle's line, and the terms offered and survived through compromise.

At the treeline, he argued with Soma about the necklace of bones — he is holding it, refuses to trust her with it, maintains they are not allowed to use it. He proposed Spirit Guardians plus Dash as a tactical combination for the summit. He noted Thunderwave as another option.

Current status: at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder, necklace of bones in his possession, preparing to advance on the henge.

## Xanth

Xanth does not appear in this chunk beyond his departure scene. Before leaving, he stood before the group and looked at Soma's staff with recognition — not admiration but memory, the look of someone confirming something they already knew. He tapped Soma on the shell, told her the staff was blessed by Meril, and stated his belief that she would do great things. His parting words were "Peace, Beauty." He then looked at the staff one final time — a look described as measuring, or saying goodbye to it, or confirming something for himself — and departed into the trees.

Vukradin broke the resulting awkward silence by appreciating the phrase "hooves off" as Xanth did exactly that. Soma watched until the trees took him.

The narration frames Xanth's farewell as significant specifically because of what he knew about the staff and Meril — the certainty he carried about Soma having been chosen on purpose. This information, delivered at departure, is described as the kind of thing you receive and then carry at arm's length while figuring out what to do with it. Soma is actively doing that.

Last known whereabouts: departed the gully, direction unspecified.

## Prutha

Prutha is the surviving orc from the Circle of Thunder warband who converted to Lathander during this session. He was the last orc standing after the battle, holding his greataxe and working out the arithmetic of his situation. Vukradin's speech reached him. He set down the greataxe, looked at Valphine — this radiant, sunlit figure no Talosian-raised orc could have anticipated — and declared himself a follower of the Drow Cleric of Lathander in halting Common: "Talos offer power. Lathander — more power. Lathander all-powerful." He then rushed toward Valphine to announce himself. When Valphine suggested the conversion name Thrall, Vukradin objected and Prutha kept his own name.

Prutha provided the following information about his history: his great-uncle had been his clan's shaman. The Talosians killed him deliberately and early, to break the clan's resistance and make recruitment easier, calling him weak, abomination, thunder meat. They then offered the clan Talos's terms — fight or become what the old man was. Prutha had fought. Three skirmishes. Never killed by choice.

He produced a small bone whistle from his clothing, explaining that his clan had greeted the sunrise each morning by blowing it — an eastern tradition, his great-uncle's tradition. He held it for a long moment, then blew one note. Then he began to cry. Valphine's read was that the tears were for the gap between the last time he blew the whistle and now — not the dead uncle or the skirmishes, but the duration of the silence.

He recited his great-uncle's guiding phrase: "Dawn comes for everyone, even those born in shadow." The table went quiet. Vukradin called it quite poetic and meant it completely.

Prutha was enthusiastic about stringing the dead anchorite's body up as a warning — visibly so, in a way that Vukradin noted but did not act on. He kicked the anchorite's corpse several times to underscore his theological transition. He also pledged himself again to Valphine a second time, firmly, in case anyone had missed the first declaration.

He laid out his terms for the path forward: orcs who are no longer following Talos should be spared; committed Talosians are another matter. Convert or die. He accepted Vukradin's blessing — "Lathander blesses you today, my child" — with the response that he thought that was awesome and had lots to think about.

Prutha is now a declared follower of Lathander through Valphine. His bone whistle tradition may be incorporated into Valphine's evolving practice. Vukradin described him as his first.

Current status: present with the party at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder.
