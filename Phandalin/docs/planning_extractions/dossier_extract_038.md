## Zeleen Varnaster

Zeleen Varnaster is the leader of the Whiskered Gang, the were-rat guards stationed at Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. When the party arrived at the mine, he greeted them with forced optimism ("Hey, boss! What's up?") and immediately tried to cover for the fact that no mining was happening. He claimed the dwarves had been "working really, really hard" despite the total absence of any mining sounds or activity.

When pressed by Valphine and Brewbarry about why no gold was being produced, Zeleen explained that the dwarves needed an "Earthstone Gem" before they could mine, but argued that his crew was hired to protect the mine, not "go on fetch quests." He attempted multiple times to wriggle out of the responsibility, noting that retrieving the gem would require going into orc-infested forest, and repeatedly tried to confirm that his crew could just stay at the mine.

After Valphine delivered a blistering rebuke — calling the were-rats "a waste of cheese" — Zeleen's will broke. He capitulated loudly, promising "we could make that happen, Mr. Boss. Mr. Right," and was heard screaming at his were-rats that the task was completely taken care of. He then led his delegation into the forest to retrieve the gem, motivated further by Brewbarry tapping his halberd against his palm. Valphine and Brewbarry had low confidence in his ability to succeed, prompting them to arrange a backup plan (a fake gem) with Horia Stonetallow.

## Dwolin the Grout

Dwolin the Grout is the dwarven foreman at Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. He explained to the party, with great gravity, that the gold vein is "shy and must be treated with absolute respect" — that without proper treatment, the gold simply won't come out. He insisted that mining could not proceed without an Earthstone Gem, a requirement tied to his personal rituals and superstitions about how gold veins behave. His colleague Horia Stonetallow described him as "the best seeker of gold I have ever known," noting he has "his little rituals, and his little gimmicks, and his little things." His belief that earth metal veins have feelings is what created the entire production stoppage at the mine.

## Horia Stonetallow

Horia Stonetallow is a dwarf working alongside foreman Dwolin the Grout at Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. She defended Dwolin with weary affection, describing him as the best gold seeker she'd ever known while acknowledging his eccentricities. After Zeleen's were-rats were dispatched to find the real Earthstone Gem, Horia revealed she had been planning to propose that the party give her money to buy a fake gem as a workaround for Dwolin's superstitions. Brewbarry designated this as "Plan B" in case the were-rats failed.

Horia requested fifty gold pieces from the mine's own accounts to fund the fake gem purchase, citing a "no-skimming clause" that Vukradin ("Mr. Bucherton") had insisted on in the contract, which prevented her from simply taking mine gold. After Brewbarry threatened her with his "Barbarian in your face clause" and offered to draw her blood for ink, she quickly agreed that the addendum could be considered signed without formalities. She conspiratorially suggested — leaning up toward Brewbarry's kneecap due to the height difference — that they keep the arrangement between themselves, believing Vukradin would not be open to such negotiations. She remains at the mine managing operations alongside Dwolin.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester is Phandalin's townmaster. During the heroes' welcome celebration after the defeat of Cryovain, he positioned himself at the front of the crowd and delivered a speech carefully claiming a share of credit he did not earn. He specifically praised "Mr. Vucrodin" and the privy council, framing the dragon's defeat as the result of Vukradin's leadership and management. Brewbarry noted that Harbin had been afraid and was doing "strange things with credit," but let it pass. He remains in his role as townmaster of Phandalin.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen is a knight of the Order of the Gauntlet stationed in or near Phandalin. He arrived at the edge of the celebration with clear urgency, sought out Soma, and brought the party to the Temple of Lathander for a private council.

He delivered several pieces of critical intelligence: Adabra Gwynn had discovered the truth about what happened at the Whispering Grove — the treant, the concealment — and was marshaling allies to convene a druid gathering. She intended to argue that the party's "reckless interventionism" had harmed the forests. Kaelen characterized Adabra as "confused," explaining she saw the harm caused by defenders against the dragons but mistakenly identified the defenders as the problem rather than the dragons themselves, because she never experienced the planar incursions directly.

When asked whether the Lords' Alliance or Order of the Gauntlet could help, Kaelen admitted the Lords' Alliance didn't want to intervene, and the Order lacked the manpower after successive wars (the War of the Dragons, the War of the Giants, and others). He confirmed it was "all on" the party.

He then raised the matter of Soma's staff, noting it held "standing" and symbolic significance among druids that could tip the balance at the conclave — but cautioned that some at the gathering had suggested evaluating whether Soma should even retain the staff. When Soma declared no one would take it, Kaelen affirmed her position with conviction, touching the hilt of his own sword and declaring that no order could take his sword either.

He also noticed Brewbarry's halberd, complimenting it as "a very fine halberd" and offering to spar ("We should parry"), which Brewbarry declined. Ser Kaelen remains in Phandalin, seemingly preparing the party for the coming druid conclave.

## Adabra (Adabra Gwynn)

Adabra Gwynn does not appear directly in this session but is the subject of urgent discussion. According to Ser Kaelen, she discovered the truth about what really happened at the Whispering Grove — including the treant and the concealment — and has decided to convene a gathering of druids. Her goal is to convince them that the party's "reckless interventionism" has brought harm to the forests.

Kaelen described her as "confused" — she experienced the consequences of war but not the planar incursions that preceded it, leading her to identify the defenders as the problem rather than the actual threats. Soma reflected on a prior conversation with Adabra where she had looked at Soma with an expression meaning "you understand," and Soma had let her think that, which now sat uneasily.

Soma characterized Adabra's actions as "bringing her own war out here." She has gathered allies and is heading to the druid conclave, where some attendees have apparently suggested that Soma's right to retain Meril's staff should be evaluated. Her current whereabouts are somewhere in the wilds, rallying support among druids for the upcoming gathering.

## Meril

Meril does not appear in this session but is referenced multiple times as Soma's druid teacher. Soma trained under Meril for only one year before being sent out — a very short time for druidic education that typically takes decades. Meril gave Soma her staff, which Soma still doesn't fully understand, and never quite explained why she was sent out with it. Soma describes Meril's hands as being "in the grain" of the staff, and notes the staff now has fewer leaves than it once did. The staff is the last physical thing connecting Soma to Meril, and Soma regards its significance as something she needs to work out for herself. Meril's current status is not mentioned.

## Cryovain

Cryovain is the white dragon the party killed on the battlements before this session's events. Its body was still warm when the party began harvesting it. Brewbarry found it unexpectedly difficult to process that the dragon had a name. Soma methodically harvested valuable components from the carcass, including a breath pouch from its throat — described as a strange, swollen, heavy organ radiating an ancient, metaphysical cold — and pale, supple scales that caught the light like polished stone. The dragon parts were brought back to Phandalin as evidence of its defeat, and the crowd recognized them immediately. The party discussed using the scales for dragon scale mail and the breath pouch as a tool. Cryovain is dead; its remains are being processed as resources.
