## NPC Activity

**Vorga**
Encountered at Falcon's Hunting Lodge as de facto leader of the orc refugee group. She was wearing Falcon's hunter cloak for warmth — not as a trophy. Evaluated the party quickly and competently, dismissed the escort option after calculating that slower members of her group could not outpace a pursuing summoned creature. Argued for staying and fighting at the lodge rather than attempting to flee. Suggested posting someone on the palisade wall to prevent Falcon from firing on the party when he arrived. Showed tactical familiarity with defensive positions. Origin confirmed: displaced from their southern mountain home by Cryovain the dragon.

**Drubbak**
Elder orc male, present in the lodge courtyard, leaning on a spear. Immediately fixated on Valphine's holy symbol upon her entry, with an expression of long-denied spiritual hunger. Spoke a single word to her in Orcish: *"Dawn."* Later, during the storm's arrival, stood motionless near the gatehouse listening to the weather and identified that the storm "came wrong" — no chant preceded it. Noted the absence of ritual vocalization as a sign that the summoning was irregular or already complete. Identified by Vorga as too old and slow to survive a retreat through open forest.

**The Falcon (Jay)**
Sent a clean, unqualified apology via Sending in response to Vukradin's message — no hedging, no qualifications. Stated he was on the road home and hoped to see them at the lodge. Arrived at the lodge from the treeline with war paint on his face and bow already in hand. When briefed by Vukradin on the full situation — orcs, alliance, incoming Talosians, defensive plan — pledged his sword without hesitation. Took up position with his bow for the defense. Described as different from his demeanor at the Enclave council: not healed, but purposeful. Valphine had done preparatory groundwork on his behalf prior to Vukradin's Sending.

**Prutha**
Recognized banners at the lodge as belonging to his uncle's clan — specifically the "Eastern Heart / Sun and Stone" clan markings. Approached the gate deliberately, hands open, performed a formal orcish greeting gesture (fist to left shoulder, head lowered), and blew the bone whistle dawn prayer. This de-escalated the guards immediately. Spoke with the gate sentry and secured entry for the whole party. Was noted by Vorga as someone she recognized or assessed carefully, with her gaze moving between Valphine and Prutha after introductions.

**Gorthok**
Confirmed present among the attacking forces at the lodge — not awaiting full summoning ritual completion but already manifested and moving with the Talosian assault. Described as a massive, wrong-feeling presence, air distorting ahead of him. Arrived with Anchorites and Talosian forces through the destroyed gate.

## Faction Movements

**Talosian Forces / Anchorites of Talos**
Executed a coordinated attack on Falcon's Hunting Lodge. The assault opened with aimed lightning strikes — one to the river, one to a barrel inside the compound, and one that destroyed the gate entirely. Gorthok was already present among the attacking force, arriving before the party had expected based on their understanding of the summoning timeline. The attack formation included Anchorites clustering behind Gorthok and Talosian fighters converging on the breach. The party's delay to negotiate and rest allowed the summoning or activation of Gorthok to advance further than anticipated.

**Orc Refugees (Vorga's Group / Eastern Heart Clan)**
Approximately thirty individuals total (a dozen warriors, plus elders, children, and infants) occupying Falcon's Hunting Lodge as displaced persons following Cryovain's attack on their southern mountain home. Not aligned with Talosians despite prior Talosian lodge use. Chose to remain and fight rather than flee, based on Vorga's tactical assessment. Three orc archers were positioned in the gatehouse tower at arrow slits for the lodge defense. Effectively joined the party's defensive coalition upon Prutha's recognition of clan banners.

## Revealed Information

- The orc refugees at the lodge were displaced by Cryovain, not allied with Talosians. The Talosians had previously used the lodge as their own before the refugees arrived.
- The Talosian organizational documents (Flenz's sealed letters, Narux's vellum sketches, Yargath's half-burned scrap) confirmed: primary summoning ritual at Woodland Manse, backup ritual standing by, and a personal summoning attempt planned as last resort — deliberate, layered redundancy.
- Gorthok was expected to require full summoning completion before manifesting. His early presence at the assault suggests the timeline was shorter than assessed or the ritual succeeded during the party's travel/rest period.
- Drubbak identified the arriving storm as ritually abnormal — no chant present where there should be one. This is a meaningful departure from expected Talosian summoning practice.
- Valphine had been conducting background communications with Falcon to prepare the ground for reconciliation with Vukradin prior to the Sending.
- The Anchorites had previously moved personnel through Falcon's Lodge (established in planning discussion), linking the lodge to broader Anchorite operations.
