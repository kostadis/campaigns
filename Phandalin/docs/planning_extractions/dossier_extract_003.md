## Valphine

Valphine participated in the battle against an ogre on a mountain path during the morning of 02-01 of Taraskh 1495. She attempted a mace strike on the prone ogre but missed badly, earning mockery from Soma. She fired a crossbow bolt at the fleeing ogre after it attempted to run, wounding it but not killing it. After the battle, she looted the ogre's body and found a whistle, coins, and a key.

At Gnomengarde, Valphine declined to climb the cliff initially, requiring Soma to bring her a rope before she ascended. She watched the entry negotiations with guards Pog and Ulla without engaging directly.

In the inventors' workshop, Valphine identified herself as a cleric to gnome inventors Fibblestib and Dabbledob, who dismissed clerical magic in favor of their own inventions. She privately concluded she could not cure King Korboz and that the party lacked the funds to pursue that avenue regardless. When hearing about the missing gnomes Warren and Orrin, she immediately considered torture as a remedy for the king's madness, finding herself aligned with Brewbarry's instincts before Vukradin redirected the group toward music.

When confronting guards Pog and Ulla near the barrel room, Valphine accused both gnomes of being the shapeshifter, deliberately using Ulla's own words against her and successfully turning the two gnomes on each other in paranoid accusation. She found the exercise amusing.

In the cellar, Valphine located the mimic by pointing a crossbow at the first barrel, which provoked it to transform and attack. She called out the discovery to the group, positioned herself clear of Soma's Ice Knife blast radius, and quoted a childhood rhyme after observing Vukradin's verbal attacks visibly hurt the creature.

## Soma

Soma guided the party to Gnomengarde by locating the river leading to the hold. At the waterfall entrance, she mocked Valphine's missed mace swing during the earlier ogre fight. She followed Vukradin through the waterfall searching for a nonexistent secret room, getting soaked, and eventually pressured him to leave. She climbed the cliff to the cave entrance and retrieved a rope for Valphine rather than leaving her behind.

At the guard post, Soma verbally challenged Pog and Ulla's logic by pointing out that shapechangers would not choose their particular forms, though the gnomes dismissed this reasoning. She muttered that she sensed more senseless violence approaching before Vukradin's diplomatic argument and Brewbarry's outburst resolved the standoff. She reacted with disbelief when bureaucratic procedure ultimately granted them entry.

In the inventors' workshop, Soma attempted to explain the value of clerical magic to Fibblestib and Dabbledob but was drowned out. She reminded Vukradin during negotiations with King Gnercli that their reward from Townmaster Harbin Wester depended on bringing back a device, not necessarily a useful one, effectively convincing him to accept the hat of wizardry deal. She quipped to Vukradin during the king encounter that he was "more of a tour guide" than a musician.

In the cellar fight against the mimic, Soma prepared to cast Ice Knife, with Valphine notably repositioning herself away from Soma's line of fire in anticipation of a poorly aimed spell.

## Vukradin

Vukradin delivered the killing blow on the fleeing ogre, justifying it to Soma by stating the ogre hated music and had likely stolen treasure from others. He looted mushrooms from the pool islands at Gnomengarde's entrance, identifying red oily mushrooms of unclear use, green mushrooms suitable for flour, and purple mushrooms fermentable into wine. He insisted on checking behind the waterfall for a secret treasure room, finding nothing.

Vukradin led negotiations with guards Pog and Ulla, countering their shapeshifter accusations with a logical argument about the intent behind standing orders, which partially impressed the male gnome Pog. He played music for Pog and Ulla, earning their approval. After being directed to the inventors' workshop via a route through spinning blade traps, he reported the blade room as a death trap to Ulla, who revealed an alternate northern route.

In the large northern room, Vukradin and the party were attacked by an automatic rotating crossbow device built by the gnome Facktore. Vukradin chose to disable the device rather than kill Facktore, who fled during the encounter.

In the inventors' workshop, Vukradin explained the party's mission to Fibblestib and Dabbledob — seeking a device to help fight the dragon on behalf of Townmaster Harbin Wester. He proposed using music to treat King Korboz's madness and asked about a piano, prompting Fibblestib to offer to build a modified version.

At King Korboz's door, Vukradin announced himself and was immediately accused of being a shapeshifter by the king. He negotiated with co-King Gnercli, who offered a hat of wizardry in exchange for eliminating the shapeshifter. Vukradin objected to the reward's limited utility against a dragon but accepted the deal after Soma reframed the terms. He led the group back through the spinning blade room, this time disabling the blades, and proceeded toward the cellar. During the mimic fight, he used verbal invective with an underlying enchantment that visibly caused the mimic to flinch.

## Brewbarry

Brewbarry fought the ogre alongside the party, missing a halberd strike that rang loudly off the rocky ground. He received a full axe blow from the ogre but was completely unharmed due to his Uthgardt barbarian rage and mystical resilience, which stunned both the ogre and Valphine. The ogre's failure against him triggered its retreat. Brewbarry pursued but could not bring the ogre down before Vukradin finished it.

At Gnomengarde's guard post, Brewbarry announced himself as "Brewbarry, killer of Ogres" to assert he was no shapeshifter. When the gnomes identified him as an Uthgardt Barbarian, he clarified he used to be one, which unexpectedly resolved a bureaucratic ambiguity around a standing attack order against Uthgardtians and allowed the party entry.

In the inventors' workshop, Brewbarry cut off Valphine's implied suggestion of torture with the declaration "We talk! We cure!" aligning himself with the diplomatic approach. In the cellar mimic fight, he charged the mimic and struck it with his halberd, wounding the creature but getting the weapon stuck in its body. The mimic's retaliatory pseudopod attack missed him entirely.

## Fibblestib

Fibblestib is one of Gnomengarde's foremost inventors, working alongside Dabbledob in the laboratory. When Vukradin's party arrived seeking a device to fight the dragon, Fibblestib dismissed the request as unimportant compared to the crisis of King Korboz's madness. He is developing a "sanity ray" to cure the king, a project he argues is superior to Dabbledob's proposed straitjacket design. He and Dabbledob maintain a contentious working relationship, arguing loudly and constantly.

Fibblestib briefed the party on the situation: two gnomes named Warren and Orrin went missing, and King Korboz claims a shape-changing creature is responsible. He invited the party to ask questions in case they spotted something the inventors missed. He acknowledged that music has helped in some circumstances when Vukradin proposed it as treatment for the king. He also laughed when Vukradin complained about the rotating blade trap, claiming it as his own invention and declaring the party's survival proof of its superiority over Dabbledob's designs. Last known location: the inventors' laboratory.

## Dabbledob

Dabbledob is Fibblestib's coworker and fellow inventor in Gnomengarde's laboratory. She opposes Fibblestib's sanity ray proposal, arguing there is no evidence it would work, and advocates instead for a straitjacket design, though Fibblestib dismisses her version as merely "a shirt with no holes for the hands." She rolled her eyes at Fibblestib's claim about the rotating blade invention. She acknowledged, reluctantly, that music has helped in some circumstances when Vukradin proposed it as a treatment for the king. Last known location: the inventors' laboratory.

## Facktore

Facktore is a deranged gnome inventor residing in a large chamber north of the cave entrance. He built a rotating platform fitted with four automatically reloading heavy crossbows and chose to test the device on Vukradin and his companions when they passed through. Vukradin's party disabled the device. Facktore fled the chamber during or after the encounter. Current status: fled, whereabouts unknown within Gnomengarde.

## Pog

Pog is a male gnome guard stationed at the cliff-top entrance to Gnomengarde alongside Ulla. He challenged the party under orders to screen for shapechangers. He appeared genuinely uncertain when Vukradin challenged the logic of their standing orders and seemed partially swayed by the argument. He enjoyed Vukradin's music. After the party passed through, Valphine deliberately provoked him and Ulla into mutual accusations of being the shapeshifter, causing them to turn on each other. Last known location: guard post at Gnomengarde's entrance.

## Ulla

Ulla is a female gnome guard working alongside Pog at the cliff-top entrance. She took a harder line than Pog in challenging the party, repeatedly deflecting their arguments with "that's what a shapechanger would say." She ultimately allowed the party entry after a bureaucratic ruling about Brewbarry's Uthgardt status. She directed Vukradin to the inventors' workshop and, when he complained about the spinning blade trap, revealed an alternate northern route. She was later manipulated by Valphine into accusing Pog of being the shapeshifter, responding by turning the accusation back on him. Last known location: guard post at Gnomengarde's entrance.

## King Korboz

King Korboz is the co-ruler of Gnomengarde, currently suffering from madness. He is in a state of hysterics, screaming, and immediately accused Vukradin of being a shapeshifter upon hearing his name. Inventors Fibblestib and Dabbledob are entirely focused on finding a cure for his condition. He believes a shape-changing creature is terrorizing the cave system and claims it caused the disappearance of gnomes Warren and Orrin. Current status: confined to his room, actively hysterical.

## King Gnercli

King Gnercli is King Korboz's co-ruler and the functional decision-maker during Korboz's illness. He recognized the party as adventurers despite Vukradin's claim of a diplomatic mission. He offered a hat of wizardry as payment if the party eliminated the shapeshifter, and when Vukradin complained about its utility against a dragon, confirmed he had little else to offer. He suggested the cellar — full of large barrels the creature could mimic — as the most likely hiding spot. Current status: in the throne room area, last heard through the door.
