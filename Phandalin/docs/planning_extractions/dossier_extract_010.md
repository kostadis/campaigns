## Adabra

Adabra approached Soma in the town square after the party arrived in Phandalin, asking whether they had ventured to the Whispering Woods. When told no, she was disappointed and said, "This is important Soma," then walked away. When Vukradin asked to speak with her, she greeted him with bemused condescension, asking if he needed a health token or midwifery assistance. Upon realizing he had lost or removed his Order of the Gauntlet pin, she explained her philosophy at length: that the Order strives to change the fundamental nature of the world, and that such interventionism forces evil to respond in kind, hurting everyone. She revealed she had originally helped Vukradin financially because she believed he cared about music and shared her worldview alongside Soma, but that when he publicly chose the Order of the Gauntlet, she stopped dealing with him directly. She characterized the Order's champions as caring only for the kill and a higher ideal, not understanding that the multiverse holds no good or evil, only balance. She had previously spoken to Harbin to secure more money for Vukradin's goals. Adabra is a member of the Emerald Enclave. She had also alerted Soma to a magical blight spreading through the Whispering Wood, which she attributed to interventionist meddling near Phandalin and Leilon rather than the Order of the Gauntlet. Ser Kaelen privately suggested she may have gone mad since some Emerald Enclave members began declaring demons part of the natural world following the intervention against Tiamat. Last seen in the Phandalin town square, walking away after her conversation with the party.

## Linene Graywind

Linene Graywind, proprietor of the Lionshield Coster in Phandalin, informed Vukradin that the War of the Giants had severely disrupted her supply chains. She explained she could no longer reliably or quickly obtain goods such as scrolls, and that fulfilling requests would now require sending out messages and feelers with no guarantee of results. She did not appear to have any further involvement in the session's events. Last seen at the Lionshield Coster in Phandalin.

## Harbin Wester

The Townmaster of Phandalin answered his door surprised to see the party alive. When informed of the Talos infestation at the lighthouse and that the party had sanctified the altar, he was visibly relieved. He expressed that Lord Neverwinter would be pleased and promised to send him a note, but showed no urgency about repairing the lighthouse light. When Vukradin suggested delivering the note personally to Lord Neverember, Harbin warned against it, noting that Neverember was very busy and that appearing before the ruler with their mission incomplete was inadvisable, adding through narrowed eyes that it was not his place to tell them what to do — a clear cautionary signal. He acknowledged problems remained in the town and reminded the party they had signed on to address them. Last seen at his home in Phandalin.

## Ser Kaelen Thorne

Ser Kaelen Thorne was found at the Stonehill Tavern in Phandalin. He greeted the party warmly, congratulating them on securing the lighthouse, though he admitted he had not believed there was genuine evil there, calling it mostly rumors. When told the threat involved a servant of Talos with a green light for a heart, his expression shifted to deep worry, and he concluded that no ordinary Talos shaman could accomplish such things, suggesting a hidden force was behind the events. When Vukradin accused him of revealing the party's plans and causing the ambush, Ser Kaelen theatrically demonstrated that everyone in the tavern had known of the party's destination, dissolving the accusation. He explained he had come to Phandalin seeking agents to locate and flush out a bandit called the Carver, who was unusually difficult to find because he was hiding from magical detection and even from his own henchmen. He acknowledged the Whispering Wood reports and Adabra's warnings but admitted he had dismissed them as low priority given the dragon, bandit, and orc threats, privately speculating that Adabra had gone mad. He agreed to look into the rightful owners of the treasure recovered from the wrecked ships at the lighthouse when Vukradin listed the items. He explained the Order of the Gauntlet does not hire agents but finds true believers, and joked that the Harpers would pay for missions. He made clear his respect for the party's results while maintaining an air of authority over them. Soma and Valphine exchanged a knowing look suggesting they do not fully trust him, particularly regarding the locked chest and its secret code. Last seen at the Stonehill Tavern in Phandalin.

## Brewbarry

Brewbarry was present at the Stonehill Tavern and reminded Vukradin that the tavern patrons loved his music, helping defuse the tension over the ambush accusation. He participated in a betting pool on whether the party would all survive the lighthouse mission, with a side bet on whether Vukradin would be ditched or killed. He later joined Vukradin and Valphine in a musical session at the tavern, using his halberd as a percussive instrument in a style recalling Uthgardt tribal music, contributing to a notably successful performance that earned the group additional coin. Last seen at the Stonehill Tavern in Phandalin.
