## Adabra Gwynn

Adabra does not appear directly in this chunk but is extensively discussed and her earlier actions are described.

At a Druid Enclave meeting, Adabra conducted what is described as a public trial structured around a personal grudge against Vukradin. She raised the matter of five gold pieces she had previously given him, using it as load-bearing evidence of his character before the assembled druids. The personal nature of the attack surprised even Vukradin, who had expected procedural argument rather than petty grievance. She had telegraphed hostility toward him in the weeks prior through clipped sentences, conspicuous avoidance of eye contact, and glances at Vukradin characterized as reconsidering something once found useful.

She fumed when she left the Enclave after losing whatever verdict was sought.

Xanth later referenced Adabra by name during his fireside explanation of Talos, noting that she would characterize the Cult of Talos as representing "the necessary element of distraction in the cosmic balance." Valphine internally assessed this framing as mistaking slaughter for ecology, comparing it to arguments she recognized from Menzoberranzan.

In response to the loss and her public use of the debt, Vukradin addressed an envelope containing six gold pieces — five plus one for interest, drawn from fair-trade organic gold — and sent it to her through Phandalin's postal system. His reasoning: now that she had lost, she could have her money back, and she could never again truthfully say he had stiffed her.

Last known whereabouts: departed the Druid Enclave after losing.

## Xanth

Xanth met the party at the bend of the Triboar Trail toward Phandalin, where Vukradin's Sending spell had directed them. He was already present when they arrived, described as having hooves in the dirt like he had been there a long time without minding it. He greeted them warmly, leading with praise for Soma specifically — "Soma, you were superb" — and expressed that Meril had been very interested in the party's recent activities, though he did not elaborate on what Meril's interest entailed.

He guided the party through the forest over approximately thirty miles on the first day, scouting and selecting a campsite when the light thinned.

Around the campfire that evening, Xanth delivered an extended account of the forest's history and current situation when Vukradin asked for stories. He described the pre-dragon balance of the forest as not peaceful but stable, and explained how Cryovain's winter had broken that equilibrium, scattering animals and radicalizing orcs who had grown frightened. He characterized the new Talosian orcs as "fundamentally more evil" and "fundamentally more dangerous than the typical orcs," using language Valphine internally identified as theological rather than purely moral.

When asked how the orcs had become more dangerous, Xanth named the Cult of Talos as the answer. He explained Talos as a deity of storms and destruction whose presence is not localized but lives "in the breast of all those who seek the power to destroy." He described the cult historically as small, unstable, self-destructive, and outlawed by all civil societies. He attributed Adabra's view — that Talos represents a necessary element of cosmic distraction — and then immediately rejected it as "nonsense," characterizing Talos as "a lunatic of a god" who empowers people whose violence is rooted in "fantasies of destruction."

On the question of a new Chosen of Talos, Xanth confirmed there was most likely one — "There is always a chosen, there's always some cleric" — but said the individual was not in this forest, or he would know who it was and would take the party there, adding as a joke that they would probably die but would at least learn something. He estimated the lair of the half-orc spellcasters was another day's hike from the campsite.

When the party encountered the cursed clearing with the burned pine and the bundled effigies, Vukradin asked Xanth whether the dolls were typical Cult of Talos. Xanth declined the implied expertise: "I am not a cultist of Talos. I don't know what is popular and what is not popular," then added he knew more about them than that, but not much.

Last known whereabouts: traveling with the party through the forest toward the Circle of Thunder lair.

## Jenna Roscoe

Jenna Roscoe was present at the Stonehill Inn upon the party's return to Phandalin, seated at a corner table nursing something dark and watching the door — described as a woman waiting to be noticed back. Brewbarry observed her but did not engage immediately.

Later, she approached Vukradin and made a pitch on behalf of the Lord's Alliance: resources, prestige, assistance locating Sister Kaella, and support for a musical studio, framed around Vukradin's obvious talents and noble character.

Vukradin listened without apparent interest and redirected her toward Valphine's church, suggesting she might want to make a donation. When the nature of Valphine's religious affiliation registered on Jenna — she characterized it as a "torture cult" — Vukradin partially conceded, correcting it to "more like a masochism cult."

Jenna then pivoted to Brewbarry and began a pitch framed around the idea of an Uthgardian barbarian being "civilized" by joining the Lord's Alliance. Brewbarry responded with a loud, sincere burp. Jenna got the point and walked away.

Last known whereabouts: Stonehill Inn, Phandalin, departing after her pitch failed.

## Tibor Wester

Tibor Wester does not appear in this chunk but is discussed.

During the party's encounter with young druids confronting a logging crew in the forest, the druids identified Tibor Wester — described as Harbin's brother — as the operator of a wildcat logging operation. He had been hiring new crews who did not know or did not respect the permit agreements governing sanctioned cutting in the forest. The loggers themselves appeared to be workers who had taken available employment without full knowledge of or investment in Wester's scheme.

When his name was raised, Vukradin made a sound characterized as "a soft warning" rather than a word.

After Vukradin's speech moved the loggers to set down their axes and express remorse, they indicated they would report back to the city about what Tibor Wester had been running in the forest.

## Lyra

Lyra entered the Stonehill Inn behind the party in an incandescent state, described as glowing with energy that had nowhere to go. She launched into an enthusiastic account of Vukradin's conduct at the Druid Enclave, praising his strategy of absorbing Adabra's hostility, walking out to render her arguments pointless, and enabling Soma to step in. She called it "brilliant" multiple times and said she had not seen such brilliant oratory in her life.

She directed Toblen Stonehill to bring Vukradin at least two beers, and when Vukradin redirected this toward Brewbarry, she confirmed two beers for Brewbarry as well, saying it twice to make sure it landed.

She noted — with mild horror — when she realized Vukradin intended to use Sending to contact people, asking whether he was going to be sending messages to everybody all day. She offered to help make contact with Xanth before being cut off by Vukradin saying he had it handled.

She was pleased by the tavern performance that evening.

Last known whereabouts: Stonehill Inn, Phandalin, at time of the party's departure planning.
