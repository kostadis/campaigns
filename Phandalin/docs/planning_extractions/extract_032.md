## NPC Activity

**Boney (skeletal horse)**
- Rose from bones and rotting saddle in a southern niche of the Dragonbarrow when Soma approached
- Communicated via Speak with Animals, initially believing Soma to be Lady Alagondar
- Accepted the party's deception that Soma was Lady Alagondar reincarnated
- Revealed it had been brought to the dungeon to guard something for Lady Alagondar and was subsequently killed by Will-o'-wisps
- Provided directional intelligence: the sword was buried "across the way" from where Lady Alagondar rested, pointing north
- Confirmed Will-o'-wisps as the threat in the dungeon, describing them as evil and uncommunicative
- Accepted the name "Boney" after initially stating its name was "Barney"

**Will-o'-wisps (multiple)**
- Operated throughout the Dragonbarrow, phasing through walls and attacking from unexpected angles
- One attacked Brewbarry after he fell into a pit trap, dealing shock damage before turning invisible and retreating through rock
- One was turned by Valphine and fled in terror
- One was concealed inside a sarcophagus in the sepulcher chamber; emerged when Vukradin opened it
- One lurked near the archway leading north; was exposed when Brewbarry used Thaumaturgy
- Multiple destroyed during the session through combined party efforts

**Lady Alagondar**
- Referenced only through Boney's recollections; a historical figure
- Was laid to rest in the Dragonbarrow; Boney indicated her burial location was across from where the Dragon Slayer Sword was placed
- Had arranged for Boney to be brought back from the dead to serve her on a quest and guard her sword

## Revealed Information

**Lady Alagondar and the Dragonbarrow**
- Lady Alagondar is buried in the Dragonbarrow, in a chamber across from the northern room where the Dragon Slayer Sword was kept
- She arranged for her horse Boney to be magically reanimated to accompany or guard her in death, suggesting significant arcane resources or allies
- The Dragon Slayer Sword was intentionally placed atop a dragon's skull in the northern chamber, across from her resting place — the placement appears deliberate and ceremonial

**Will-o'-wisps**
- The Will-o'-wisps inhabiting the Dragonbarrow are responsible for killing Boney after it was brought to the dungeon
- They are described by Boney as malevolent and non-communicative
- They use sarcophagi as concealment points and phase through solid stone, making them difficult to engage
