## NPC Activity

**Adabra (Midwife, Emerald Enclave)**
- Appeared in Phandalin's town square; approached Soma directly, bypassing Vukradin
- Expressed disappointment that the party had not yet visited the Whispering Woods
- Explained her philosophy of cosmic balance to Vukradin: the multiverse is one unified thing, not divided into good and evil; interventionist efforts to improve the world force equal and opposite responses from evil forces
- Revealed she had previously gone to Harbin on Vukradin's behalf to secure additional payment, but withdrew that support after Vukradin publicly aligned with the Order of the Gauntlet
- Stated her belief that the Whispering Wood blight is caused by interventionist meddling (ritual cleansing around Phandalin and Leilon), not the Order of the Gauntlet directly
- Clarified she does not believe the Order of the Gauntlet is responsible for the blight—they are "too busy chasing dragons and fighting demons"
- No longer communicates directly with Vukradin; addresses matters through Soma only

**Ser Kaelen Thorne (Order of the Gauntlet)**
- Present at Stonehill Tavern; welcomed the party warmly on their return
- Expressed genuine concern upon hearing the Talos shaman had a glowing green heart, noting a typical shaman could not produce such effects and suggesting a hidden force is involved
- Confirmed no secrecy existed around the party's mission to the lighthouse; the entire tavern knew, including a betting pool on the party's survival
- Briefed the party on the Carver: a bandit leader operating in the region who is unusually skilled at concealing himself from divination magic and hiding even from his own henchmen
- Dismissed the Whispering Wood blight as low priority given ongoing threats (dragon, bandits, orcs); admitted no one has investigated it
- Acknowledged townsfolk would be grateful for investigation of the grove
- Stated the Order of the Gauntlet does not pay agents—they seek "true believers"—and suggested the Harpers do pay for missions
- Offered to look into the rightful owners of the treasure recovered from the shipwrecks at the lighthouse

**Linene Graywind (Lionshield Coster)**
- Informed Vukradin that the Lionshield Coster's capacity to source magical goods (e.g., scrolls) has been severely degraded since the War of the Giants; results now uncertain and slow

**Harbin Wester (Townmaster)**
- Received the party's report on the Talos infestation and lighthouse sanctification with relief
- Stated he would send a note to Lord Neverwinter praising their work
- Strongly discouraged the party from traveling to Neverwinter themselves while their mission in Phandalin remains incomplete, implying it could be dangerous to approach Lord Neverember prematurely

**"A Friend to True Justice" (Anonymous Letter Writer)**
- Sent a letter to Vukradin from Neverwinter, delivered via the Stonehill Inn
- Accused the Order of the Gauntlet of accepting funding from House Margaster, an organization rumored to be connected to unsavory and potentially evil operations in Waterdeep
- Alleged the Order has forcibly appropriated artifacts from neutral parties, not only villains
- Framed the letter as concern for Vukradin's principles around conflict-free gold and honest wages
- Identity unknown; letter originated in Neverwinter

**Talos / Hidden Force at Lighthouse**
- Ser Kaelen flagged that the green-light heart phenomenon exceeds known Talos shaman capabilities, suggesting a greater force or patron behind the lighthouse cult remains unidentified *(arc trigger: confirmed supernatural amplifier or unknown backer behind Talos activity)*

## Revealed Information

- The Order of the Gauntlet does not pay agents; they recruit ideological volunteers. The Harpers do pay for completed missions.
- The Carver is unusually capable of evading divination magic—even his own henchmen do not know his location.
- The Whispering Wood is only one hour's walk from Phandalin, far closer than Soma had understood.
- House Margaster is alleged to fund the Order of the Gauntlet; they are separately rumored to have cult connections in Neverwinter.
- Adabra previously lobbied Harbin Wester to pay Vukradin more, motivated by belief in his devotion to music and beauty—a belief she no longer holds.
- The entire Stonehill Inn population knew the party's destination and timeline before their departure; secrecy around missions is effectively zero in Phandalin.
- Ser Kaelen and much of the Order's leadership regard the Emerald Enclave's position on cosmic balance as eccentric or delusional, particularly post-war ideological drift.
