## Brewbarry

Brewbarry appeared throughout the session across multiple scenes at Falcon's Hunting Lodge and the approach to it. He is the narrator of the first and third sections, providing a first-person account of events.

During the post-battle equipment discussion, Brewbarry examined the recovered shield — dark oak with iron bands, druidic carvings, and a Talosian bolt nailed crookedly over the face. He supported Vukradin's position that the shield was not theirs to keep, agreeing it belonged to whoever had left the half-orc tally (four notches and a slash) carved on the inside rim. When the group turned attention to his new halberd taken from the shipwreck, he deflected with the line "I got a divorce" and allowed Vukradin to supply the cover story that it was his original halberd, recently sharpened. He said nothing further and let the matter close.

Brewbarry listened to Valphine's theology of Lathander — the argument that light is power rather than tenderness, that it burns and harms and is worshipped out of fear of the dark. He reflected on this privately in terms of his own Uthgardt relationship to Talos, noting he was sober when found guilty, which was a long time ago. He did not share these thoughts aloud.

At the lodge, Brewbarry discovered a broken cask whose contents — alcohol — had soaked into the floor planks and been wasted rather than consumed. He emerged from the lodge interior screaming, announced "I will kill them all!" to the courtyard, and was talked down by Vukradin, who promised more alcohol would be found. Brewbarry accepted this reluctantly.

During the siege, Brewbarry observed the storm arrive wrongly — sudden darkness, panicking boars, and lightning strikes inside the compound. He received a defensive working from Valphine that he felt as an extra layer of protection. He checked his grip on the Dragon Slayer, noted his Dread Helm's red-glow effect, and positioned for combat. When Vukradin revealed Gorthok was already present among the attacking forces, Brewbarry's response was direct: *They are savages. We kill them all.* He had his sword in hand when the gate was destroyed by a directed lightning bolt and the Talosian forces began their assault.

Brewbarry reflected privately on Uthgardt stories of the Great Boar — a thing conjured from storm and lightning, older than the gods claiming to own it — and concluded that you probably don't stop something like that. You just get in front of it.

## Vukradin

Vukradin appeared throughout the session as a consistent voice of tactical assessment, social mediation, and strategic planning.

During the shield discussion, Vukradin immediately identified the problem with a Cleric of Lathander carrying a Talosian-marked shield: "The Cleric of Lathander is wielding a shield that has the image—" He didn't finish because he didn't need to. He declared the shield not theirs and not Prutha's either when Prutha pushed back, stating both times with finality: "Not yours." He also advised the Talosians generally to do a better job hiding their crown merchandise, meaning the bolt insignia. He provided the cover story for Brewbarry's new halberd unprompted — that it was the halberd he'd had all along, just sharpened — and repeated it with comic emphasis. He observed of Valphine's theology: "I like that the cleric is changing the god instead of the god changing the cleric."

On the question of next destination, Vukradin announced he would refuse to act on Falcon's lodge problem without an apology from Falcon first. He framed the demand using an anecdote about Lord Halueth Verres and a war-mage from Waterdeep: Lady Verres contacted her ten years after a confirmation hearing to demand an apology, and the war-mage actually replied. This was the "indignant tone" he wanted — audacity, as if doing Falcon a favor by asking. He burned a Sending spell, asking whether Falcon was ready to apologize for the Druid Council and noting the party needed to address the lodge. Falcon's reply was categorical: *I apologize*, and he said he was on the road home and hoped to see them there. Vukradin accepted this without qualification: "Sounds like he did apologize, though. Okay. We can go."

He reviewed the recovered documents — Flenz's letters, Narux's vellum sketches, Yargath's half-burned scrap — and identified the Talosian plan: a primary summoning ritual at the manse, a backup ritual, and a personal summoning attempt if both failed. He was puzzled by the Talosian attack on what had been their own lodge. He inventoried his remaining magical resources and noted he had burned two minor workings and two heavy ones, including the Sending. He asked whether anyone had something to resist lightning; receiving no answer, he noted darkness doesn't prevent area targeting. He deferred the rest-or-press-on decision to Valphine, meaning it genuinely.

At the lodge, Vukradin announced to the assembled orc refugees "We killed the dragon" with his chin slightly elevated. He engaged sincerely with Vorga's refusal to evacuate, proposing Brewbarry carry everyone and offering Boney as transport, before accepting that Vorga had already done the geometry of survival and her plan — kill the creature where they knew it was coming from, then walk to Icespire — was correct. He moved immediately to talk Brewbarry down from the wasted-alcohol breakdown, deploying every scrap of warmth and persuasion he had.

When Falcon arrived at the palisade with war paint and a bow already drawn, Vukradin called down warmly ("Jay! You made it!"), came down from the wall, and facilitated the full briefing. He noted "We're friends now. He apologized." When Soma pointed out they hadn't expected Gorthok until the summoning was complete, Vukradin maintained they'd made the right call coming to the lodge. As the siege began, he identified the Talosians as nicely grouped and noted he needed a working that could take them all at once, regretting he had spent the fire-burst elsewhere. He agreed enthusiastically with Brewbarry's assessment that they should kill them all. Last seen assessing enemy positions and searching for a crowd-control working as Gorthok's forces came through the destroyed gate.

## Valphine

Valphine appeared throughout the session as a tactician, theologian, and social mediator.

During the shield examination, Valphine tilted her head with suppressed interest, then reached out and wiggled the Talosian bolt free — it had been stuck on crookedly from the start and came loose immediately. She turned the shield over, examined the newer leather straps smelling of pine tar and wet dog, and found the half-orc tally on the inside rim. When Vukradin declared the shield not theirs, she confirmed: "Done." She deflected Prutha's suggestion she use it — "You can use my shield" — and when Vukradin overrode that offer too, she stated without warmth: "It's not mine. Be sure." She tucked the shield under her arm to hold until the owner turned up. She added to the halberd cover story: "And it looks a little waterlogged."

Valphine then spoke at length about Lathander. Her argument: people misunderstand what light is. It burns twelve hours a day, hurts everything at full strength, and people worship it because the alternative is worse — worship of power in plain sight, unashamed, causing harm constantly, called good because the dark is worse. This provoked Vukradin's observation about the cleric changing the god rather than the reverse.

Valphine had done background groundwork with Falcon in advance — Vukradin felt the apology was ready when it came, and attributed it to her preparation. She added context to Vukradin's Sending, suggesting he clarify that the party was considering clearing the lodge.

She reviewed the Talosian documents alongside Vukradin, sorted the two scenarios regarding the lodge attack (Talosian and then destroyed by the party, or not Talosian and yielding intel), and suggested approaching the lodge from the south. She recommended obscuring mist or darkness as a counter to lightning targeting.

At the lodge, Valphine opened negotiations in Orcish with Vorga and the elder. She answered the elder's single word — *Dawn* — with: "Yes. I'm the bringer of the dawn. A painful dawn." She let him see her holy symbol clearly. She relayed in Common that the clan came from the southern mountains and had been told about the nearby Talosian threat. She flagged to Vukradin that Falcon's hunter cloak on Vorga's back was "not worthy" of pressing on — not a trophy but a practical garment — and Vukradin agreed. She cast a defensive working on the party during the pre-siege lull, described as bracing rather than healing, making bodies harder to drop. She positioned three orc archers at the gatehouse arrow slits, noting the fantastically defensible positions there.

During the storm's arrival, Valphine's staff sprouted a new leaf, which she noted with dry humor: "Guys, it's sprouting!" and "It means an angel got its wings, don't worry." Last seen with the party in the courtyard as the gate came apart and Gorthok's forces moved through.

## Prutha

Prutha appeared in both the equipment discussion and the lodge arrival.

During the shield discussion, Prutha watched the exchange with the expression he gets when adults are doing something confusing. He enthusiastically suggested: "Valphine, use good shield! Valphine, cleric of sun!" He was rebuffed by both Valphine and Vukradin. When he protested, Vukradin looked at him directly and said "Not yours either." Prutha went quiet.

At the lodge, Prutha moved before anyone else spoke. He had been watching the banners and identified them aloud: "Eastern Heart! Three banners on the wall! I see one! Sun and Stone! My uncle's clan march! They are family!" He walked deliberately to the gate with hands open, brought his right fist to his left shoulder, lowered his head in formal greeting, drew the bone whistle from inside his armor, and blew the dawn prayer. The sentry returned the gesture, lowered her bow, and called down to open the gate. He exchanged verbal recognition with her before the invitation was extended to the whole party. The sentry's invitation — "Bring your drow, bring your Aasimar, bring all of them. We are listening" — framed him as the entry point: *your drow*, *your Aasimar*, possessive to Prutha.

Prutha provided intelligence on the lodge from orc songs: the orcs had taken it, there were dogs, and he knew of three factions — former tribe, Talosian-aligned, and independent. He confirmed the Anchorites had moved their own people through. A recovered note mentioned a boy from the East described as a good recruit, which Soma observed while looking at Prutha, though she said nothing aloud. Vukradin also noted this detail without acting on it.

During the pre-siege period, Prutha was standing quietly, having apparently learned that stillness is safer than motion. Last known position: inside the palisade as the siege began.

## Soma

She confirmed to Vukradin that she had nothing to resist lightning. Last seen positioned in the courtyard as the gate was destroyed and Gorthok's forces began the assault.

## Falcon the Hunter

Falcon arrived at the lodge late in the session, coming out of the treeline with war paint on his face and a bow already drawn. Vukradin was on the palisade wall to provide a friendly face; Falcon's bow came down when Vukradin descended to meet him. He listened to the full briefing — orc refugees, the alliance, the incoming Talosians, the plan — and when Vukradin finished, said: "You have my sword."

Earlier in the session, Falcon was not physically present but was the subject of significant discussion. He had stood up at the Druid Council and taken shots at Vukradin, making himself useful to Adabra at Vukradin's expense, costing the party something in those negotiations. Vukradin had let it sit without contact since, deciding the next move was Falcon's. Valphine had been doing background work with Falcon in the interim. When Vukradin sent the Sending demanding an apology for the Druid Council and noting the lodge needed clearing, Falcon's reply was categorical and unhedged: *I apologize*, with the addition that he was on the road home and hoped to see them there.

His hunter cloak was found on Vorga's back inside the lodge — she had taken it as the warmest thing available, wearing it practically rather than as a trophy. Valphine and Vukradin agreed it was not worth pressing on. The dead partner whose cloak had been left on its hook was referenced as part of Valphine's pre-arrival briefing on Falcon's situation, though no further details were given. Soma observed that this version of Falcon — paint on his face, weapon ready, walking toward a fight with purpose — was different from the grief-worn version at the Enclave council. Not healed, but aimed. Last seen inside the lodge compound, committed to the defense.

## Vorga

Vorga was the leader of the orc refugees occupying Falcon's Hunting Lodge. She came down from the walkway when Prutha's recognition signal opened the gate — middle-aged, gray at the temples, broken nose healed badly, a scar along her jaw from something old and serious. She was wearing Falcon's hunter cloak over her own leather, taken as the warmest available garment rather than as a trophy.

Vorga's group numbered approximately thirty, including a dozen warriors, elders, old men around a cookfire, women with infants, and children playing against the far wall. They had been displaced by Cryovain from the southern mountains and had moved into the empty lodge out of necessity. She had already done the geometry of survival before the party arrived: the open woods against a pursuing force, twenty people who couldn't run fast enough, the old man near the wall who needed carrying as the illustration.

When Vukradin offered evacuation — Brewbarry carrying people, Boney as transport, Soma's elk — Vorga walked through each option and rejected it, repeating "Creature will catch up" until it landed. Her plan was to kill the creature at the lodge, where they knew it was coming from, and then walk to Icespire in safety. She confirmed the party that killed the dragon and accepted the alliance on that basis — "You killed Dragon?" / "Dragon is dead. That home is now free to go back?" — and chose to stay and fight rather than attempt evacuation.

She suggested someone stand on the wall so Falcon would see a friendly face and not come in shooting. She positioned her archers at the arrow slits in the gatehouse tower when Valphine pointed out the defensive value. Last known position: inside the lodge compound during the siege.

## lord_halueth_verres

Lord Halueth Verres does not appear in this chunk. He was referenced by Vukradin during the planning discussion as a rhetorical model for the tone Vukradin wanted to strike in his Sending to Falcon. Vukradin described the story: during Verres's confirmation hearing for elevation to Lord Magister of Neverwinter, a war-mage from Waterdeep testified against him on matters involving an artifact and a contract with apprentices. Verres received the seat anyway. Vukradin used this story to identify the register he wanted — "Lady Verres on a Sending" — meaning indignant, as if doing the recipient a favor by asking at all. ## Drubbak

Drubbak was an elder orc among Vorga's refugees, described as leaning on his spear the way men do when the spear has outlasted their sons. His eyes fixed on Valphine's holy symbol the moment she entered the courtyard and did not leave it — not hostility but hunger, the look of someone who had been praying a long time without answer.

He said one word in Orcish: *Dawn.* Valphine answered him in Orcish, confirmed she was the bringer of the dawn, and let him see her holy symbol clearly, adding: "A painful dawn."

During the siege, Drubbak went very still when the storm arrived wrong. He tilted his head as if listening for something underneath the wind, then said quietly: "It come wrong. There is no chant. Where is the chant?" He recognized from the storm's character — its absence of accompanying chant — that something was different about this summoning. He had knowledge of storm-working tradition, understanding storms the way Brewbarry understands a weapon's balance. He did not move from his place by the gatehouse. Last known position: standing by the gatehouse, still listening, as the gate was destroyed.
