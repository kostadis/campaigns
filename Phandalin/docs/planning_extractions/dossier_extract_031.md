## Horia Stonetallow

A proud shield dwarf from the Stonetallow clan, which traces its ancestry to a disgruntled engineer from Gauntlgrym who was the only one who knew how to build a proper archway. Horia launched into a detailed etymological explanation when Vukradin questioned the oxymoronic name "Stonetallow," revealing it derives from "stone to allow," referring to allowing arches to pass — reflecting the clan's fixation on doing things properly. She sees value in the wererat protection arrangement because it frees the dwarves to focus entirely on perfecting the art of mining. She finalized the mine agreement by shaking hands with Zeleen Varnaster and exchanging contracts, though the dwarves viewed the wererats' modifications to the mine with skepticism. She discussed potential weekly earnings with Vukradin, estimating around one hundred gold pieces per week, possibly more with investment. Last seen in the mine overseeing the new arrangement.

## Zeleen Varnaster

Leader of the wererats, operating with a distinctly gangster demeanor. Vukradin's insight revealed that Zeleen and his people never truly wanted to mine and always preferred acting as muscle over hard labor. When the party presented their proposal — dwarves handle mining, wererats act as protectors of their territory — Zeleen initially demanded one thousand gold pieces upfront for six months of protection. Brewbarry countered by pointing out that the wererats had been chased from their homes and were now receiving shelter, cheese, and protection without being kicked out, with Brewbarry's still-bloody axe serving as an unspoken threat. Zeleen was sufficiently impressed by both the argument and the implied intimidation to agree to protect the mine for free, reframing it as protecting his home and family. He offered Vukradin a piece of Petunia's cheese as a goodwill gesture, which Vukradin found as exceptional as advertised. He warned the party that orcs, ogres, and a dragon were still causing chaos in the area and suggested the Shrine of Savras as a source of information about the dragon. When Vukradin attempted to deceive him by claiming dragons could polymorph into smaller forms to enter caves, Zeleen rolled a natural 20, saw through the deception entirely, and treated it as a good joke, which strengthened the relationship rather than damaging it. He also warned that the dragon's raids on farming were so severe that there would be no beer brewed this year. He shook hands with Horia to finalize the mine agreement. Last seen in the mine, now serving as its de facto protector.

## The Falcon

Owner of the Falcon's Hunting Lodge, encountered by the party on the Triboar Trail while they were traveling to Dragonbarrow. He was visibly distraught, having lost his lodge to orcs and ogres allied with the Anchorites of Talos who are attempting to muscle everyone out of Neverwinter Wood. He expressed frustration that the party never cleared the Woodland Manse, which had driven them away with fireballs and lightning. He is unwilling to return to the lodge himself and is instead heading to Phandalin to settle debts and plan his next moves. To incentivize the party to reclaim his lodge, he offered Vukradin a particular type of wood for his music studio and proposed transforming the Falcon Lodge into a performance venue featuring Vukradin and Valfine with ale, arguing this would raise rents and attract people. He was previously noted to have shown stolen valor by appearing after someone else had already fought a dragon. Last seen departing for Phandalin.

## Xanth

A centaur encountered peacefully near Dragonbarrow. His arrival startled Vukradin, who had never seen a centaur before and considered it an abomination, though Soma calmed Vukradin by explaining centaurs were woodland creatures and not a threat. Xanth warned the party about strange will-o'-wisps that appear over Dragonbarrow at night, describing the hill as haunted by restless spirits of the dead, and stated he only visits during daylight hours. He also informed the party that Neverwinter Wood has been overrun by orcs allied with half-orc spellcasters — the Anchorites of Talos — who perform dark rites at a circle of standing stones atop a cave-ridden hill, and confirmed these were the same group that previously tried to kill the party. He offered to guide the party to the Anchorites after their business at Dragonbarrow was complete. He recognized Valphine as a famous cleric of Lathander and praised her courage, commenting on the idea of pain as purification. Last seen near Dragonbarrow, awaiting the party's return from the tomb.

## Meryl

A druid who previously educated Soma about will-o'-wisps, describing them as evil lights that emerge in natural surroundings. This prior knowledge allowed Soma to recognize Xanth's description of the lights haunting Dragonbarrow as potentially will-o'-wisps before the party entered the tomb. Meryl does not appear directly in this session but is referenced as a source of Soma's expertise. Current whereabouts unknown.
