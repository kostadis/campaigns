# The Anchor and the Kill: How Iymrith Dies Permanently

## The Gold Anchor

Iymrith figured out something no other dragon understood: the real power source of Tiamat's ritual wasn't divine energy — it was **greed**. Specifically, the accumulated desire, theft, and covetousness that attaches to stolen gold over time.

This is why Iymrith never spent her hoard. Not because she was typically dragon-greedy, but because she understood that centuries of stolen gold — carrying the weight of everyone it was taken from — constitutes a **permanent soul anchor** to the material plane.

As long as the hoard exists with its accumulated greed intact, Iymrith can always come back. Kill her body, her soul bounces back. Send her to Avernus, she has a tether. Destroy one form, she finds another. The anchor makes her functionally immortal.

This is what Zariel miscalculated. She brought Iymrith to Avernus thinking she could control the Doom of the Desert. But Iymrith was never truly trapped — the anchor means Avernus is a waiting room, not a prison. Zariel can torment her, but can't own her.

---

## Zariel's Laboratory (Iymrith's Prison in Avernus)

Frustrated by her inability to truly control Iymrith, Zariel designed a laboratory specifically to torment her:

- **Half-dragon pixies** — mockeries of Iymrith's life's work. The fey/dragon hybrids she spent centuries perfecting, but wrong. Broken. Insulting parodies of her research.
- **Scientific equipment that doesn't quite work** — every instrument is slightly miscalibrated, every formula almost right. For a perfectionist scientist, this is exquisite torture.
- **The aesthetic of achievement, hollowed out** — Zariel gave her everything she wanted in form, and nothing in substance. A laboratory where no real work can be done.

This is where the party must eventually go. Not to fight Iymrith in a cage — but to finish her after the anchor is broken.

---

## The Solution: Restitution

### Vukradin's Insight

The answer to Iymrith's immortality isn't a bigger sword, a more powerful spell, or a divine intervention. It's **returning the hoard to everyone it was stolen from.**

Break the greed. Make the gold conflict-free. Once every piece of stolen wealth is returned to its rightful owner (or their descendants, or their communities), the accumulated desire dissolves. The anchor loses its power. Iymrith becomes mortal. Killable. Final.

### Why Vukradin Sees This

Vukradin's entire worldview is built on the idea of **conflict-free gold** — wealth without exploitation, value without theft. Everyone else sees this as naive optimism. The Silver Tongue worldview that "sees no tension" in wanting prosperity without harm.

But against Iymrith, this isn't naivety — it's the **precise diagnosis**. Vukradin's philosophical commitment to ethical wealth is the lens that reveals what the greed-anchor actually is and how to break it. The bard who insists gold can be clean is the one who understands why *this* gold must be made clean.

The warriors can't sword their way through this. The druids can't nature their way past it. The clerics can't pray it away. Only the character who has spent the entire campaign insisting that gold carries moral weight — that wealth is never neutral — can see that the anchor is made of stolen desire and can only be unmade by returning what was taken.

## The Epic Tier Quest: The Reverse Heist

The party's endgame isn't stealing a hoard — it's **returning one**.

### The Challenge

Iymrith has hoarded stolen gold for **centuries**. The rightful owners include:
- Peoples long dead (descendants must be found)
- Civilizations that no longer exist (what does restitution even mean?)
- Entities who don't know they were robbed
- Communities that have rebuilt without it

This isn't a simple delivery quest. It requires:
1. **Research** — identifying who Iymrith stole from across her centuries of accumulation
2. **Tracking** — finding descendants, successor states, or rightful inheritors
3. **Judgment** — deciding what "returning" means when the original owners are dust
4. **Logistics** — physically moving a dragon's hoard across the world to dozens of recipients
5. **Moral clarity** — understanding that the gold must be given freely, not used as leverage

### Structural Design

Each "return" can be its own quest or arc:
- Some are simple (find the dwarven clan, give back their ancestral gold)
- Some are morally complex (the kingdom that was stolen from no longer exists — who inherits a dead nation's wealth?)
- Some are dangerous (the gold was stolen from other dragons, from fiends, from entities that will demand more than just the gold back)
- Some require the party to confront uncomfortable truths about how wealth moves through the world

### The Temporary Wins

If the party stops Iymrith's current artifact-grab but doesn't complete the restitution:
- She's defeated for now
- She retreats, regroups
- The anchor still holds — she'll come back
- The party gets a genuine win, but knows it's not permanent
- This is the off-ramp if players are satisfied

### The Permanent Win

Complete the restitution → anchor breaks → go to Avernus → find Zariel's laboratory → kill Iymrith in her broken paradise → she dies for real → the timeline is safe.

## Open Questions

1. **How does the party learn about the hoard's composition?** Who catalogued what Iymrith stole? Is there a ledger in Ascore? Do the 3HPP have records?
2. **How many "returns" constitute enough?** Is it all-or-nothing, or does the anchor weaken progressively as pieces are returned?
3. **Does Iymrith know about her vulnerability?** Does she try to prevent the returns, or is she so arrogant she doesn't believe anyone would bother?
4. **What's in Avernus besides Iymrith?** Does Zariel oppose the party entering? Does she welcome it (she's tired of Iymrith too)?
5. **How does Vukradin discover the insight?** What's the in-game moment where it clicks? Is it a gradual realization or a flash of understanding?
6. **What role do Brewbarry and Soma/Valphine play in the restitution?** Each character's perspective on wealth and justice should shape how they approach different returns.
7. **Can the hoard be corrupted further?** Could Iymrith strengthen the anchor by stealing MORE, making the task harder if the party delays?
