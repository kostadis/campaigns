# Epic Tier Finale: The Doom of the Desert

## The Villain: Iymrith

Iymrith, the ancient blue dragon known as the Doom of the Desert, is the campaign's ultimate antagonist. She transcended being "just another big blue dragon" centuries ago, becoming a biological researcher obsessed with legacy and recognition.

### Her Research Program

- **Drake hybrids** — her early, established successes
- **Fey/dragon hybrids** — her masterwork. The concept: a fairy that walks into a room undetected, then transforms into a dragon. The perfect assassin.

### Her Investors (Historical)

| Investor | What They Wanted | Status |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| Tiamat | Funded original fey/dragon hybrid research | Failed to enter the Prime Material Plane; funding dried up |
| The Giant Kings | Bought into the hybrid program | Dead — slaughtered by Hekaton at Ascore |
| The Kraken Society (Mind Flayers) | Wanted the fairy hybrid weapon | Showed up at Ascore as Mind Flayers; now trapped on Toril |
| The Seelie Court | Provided fey substrate for research | Fled Ascore during the brawl |

---

## What Happened at Ascore (The 3HPP Campaign)

The 3HPP (Thistle, Orsik, Vardis, Unla Kee) confronted Iymrith at her lair in the desert ruins of Ascore. The events:

1. Iymrith was hosting a "grand reveal" — a showcase for her investors (giant kings, Kraken Society/Mind Flayers, Seelie Court)
2. Everyone believed **Laela** was the successful fey/dragon hybrid. She is not — what Laela actually is remains unknown.
3. The 3HPP convinced Iymrith they could get Laela to demonstrate her hybrid nature
4. Instead, they brought **Bob Saget** (a demon who can be polymorphed into a dragon) to do a massive beatdown
5. The showcase turned into a brawl — all the giant kings present, chaos erupting
6. **Hekaton** (Storm Giant King, allied with the Lord's Alliance) showed up and slaughtered the giant kings
7. The Seelie Court fled
8. **Bob Saget had been trapped in a nested bag of holding** — an ISD (Interplanar Storage Device). This revealed the existence of ISDs and the Mind Flayer / Githyanki war over them.
9. **Iymrith was killed** — but before dying, she invoked a pact with **Zariel** (Archdevil of Avernus)
10. Her soul went to Avernus. She is not destroyed. She is planning.

### Consequences of Ascore

- The "War of the Giants" — Hekaton's massacre of the giant kings, dissolution of the Ordning
- The Kraken Society / Mind Flayer presence on Faerun exposed
- ISDs and the interplanar war revealed to the players
- Iymrith removed from the board — but not permanently

**The 3HPP campaign ended because the players got bored.** The confrontation at Ascore was the climactic event but not a clean resolution.

## The Mind Flayer Situation

### How They Got Here

- Mind Flayers discovered Faerun was **outside** the Githyanki/Mind Flayer war zone
- Faerun was **not under the ISD protocol** — meaning MPE (Multi-Planar Energy) could be released into the astral plane for harvesting without regulation
- They established the Kraken Society as their surface operation, literally dominating a kraken (sourced from "Call from the Deep")
- Then **KP trapped them on Toril** — his manipulation of the cosmic fabric (streamlining interplanar routes) sealed them in as a side effect

### The Schism

**Main Faction (The Kraken Society proper):**
- Controls the kraken
- Has settled in on Faerun
- The kraken is becoming a **replacement for the Elder Brains** — this is heretical
- They represent an existential threat to traditional Mind Flayer hierarchy

**Splinter Faction (Phandalin operations):**
- Loyalists to the Elder Brain hierarchy
- Want to **escape Faerun** and return to the proper network
- See the main faction's kraken-as-Elder-Brain as a threat to all Mind Flayers
- Their activity in Phandalin (Teega, the drowned gate, psionic manipulation) = escape attempts
- They want the **Ubbalux artifact**

## KP's Role

Kazneporium Ketternopappux (KP) is the cosmic engineer whose actions created the current mess:

- His "1489 Export Regulation on Interplanar Storage Devices" was meant to solve the Valentine and Bonk / Githyanki / Mind Flayer crisis
- His manipulation of interplanar routes trapped the Mind Flayers on Toril (intentionally or as side effect)
- He sent **Aletra Sartorra** to clean up cascading planar disturbances on Faerun
- He wants his **Planar Energy Conduit** back from Iymrith's lair

## The Three Artifacts

| Artifact | Location | Who Wants It | Purpose |
|----------|----------|--------------|---------|
| The Ruinstone | House of Thalivar, Leilon (Border Ethereal) | Iymrith | Netherese artifact that undoes decisions; normally limited to 24 hours |
| The Ubbalux Artifact | Connected to the Kraken Society / drowned gate | Splinter Mind Flayers; Iymrith | Tied to Ubbalux's philosophy of psionic elevation; massive psionic power source |
| KP's Planar Energy Conduit | Iymrith's abandoned lair (Ascore) | KP (via Aletra); Iymrith | Stabilizes/reroutes cosmic forces; channels interplanar energy |

---

## Iymrith's Plan: The Endgame

**Goal:** Rewrite history.

The Ruinstone as written undoes one action within 24 hours. But **supercharged with the Ubbalux artifact and KP's Planar Energy Conduit**, it breaks the time limit entirely. She can reach back across years and undo any decision.

**The decision she wants to reverse:** Not killing Orsik, Thistle, Vardis, and Unla Kee when she had the chance.

### If She Succeeds:

- The 3HPP die in the past
- The con at Ascore never happens
- The giant kings survive — her investor showcase succeeds
- Hekaton's alliance with the Lord's Alliance may never form
- The War of the Giants never happens (or plays out differently)
- The entire cascade of events that shaped the current world **unravels**
- The Phandalin party's reality ceases to exist as they know it

### Why This Works as an Epic Villain:

- It's **personal** — not "destroy the world" but "correct my one mistake"
- It's **intellectually consistent** — she's a scientist who made an error and wants to fix it
- The stakes are **existential** without being generic — the party's world ends not with a bang but with a rewrite
- She's not evil for evil's sake — she's brilliant, wounded, and utterly convinced she deserves her do-over
