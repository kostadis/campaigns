Based on the integrated **Gazetteer of Neverwinter (1495 DR)**, here is an updated outline for **"The Siren’s Song at the Cerulean Salon."**
This update weaves the specific political tensions, locations, and hidden factions of the city into the adventure, deepening the conflict between **Jena Roscoe (Lord’s Alliance)** and the **Order of the Gauntlet**, while firmly rooting the **Kraken Society** threat within the city's existing power struggles.
**Adventure Context: The Jewel of the North (1495 DR)**
The adventure occurs in the **Protector’s Enclave**, the only stable district in a city otherwise plagued by rebels and monsters. Lord Protector **Dagult Neverember** is pushing the "New Neverwinter" propaganda to legitimize his rule, making a high-profile cultural event like the Cerulean Salon a political powder keg.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Part 1: The Road to Neverwinter (The "Nasher" Ambush)**
• **The Setup:** Ser Kaelen sends the party to return the statue to **Elara Meliamne**.
• **The Gazetteer Update:** As the party travels the **High Road** near the **Blacklake District** (the rebel stronghold), they are ambushed by the "Gilded Bolt" mercenaries.
• **The Twist:** To hide **House Margaster’s** involvement, the mercenaries are dressed as **Nashers** (the violent faction of the Sons of Alagondar).
    ◦ **Insight:** A character with knowledge of the city (or a successful Insight check) might notice the gear is too high-quality for typical rebels, or that they lack the "minotaur head" symbol usually worn by true Nashers.
    ◦ **Political Consequence:** If the party kills them, **Jena Roscoe** will later use this to claim the Order of the Gauntlet is "provoking the rebels," further destabilizing the peace.
**Part 2: Arrival in the Protector’s Enclave**
• **The Location:** The party enters the **Protector’s Enclave**, the heavily policed heart of Neverember’s rule. They likely pass the **Seven Suns Coster Market**, which is bustling with the "startup boom" economy.
• **The Venue:** **Lord Cassian Meliamne’s Manor** is located on the cliffs overlooking the sea, deliberately positioned to rival the **Moonstone Mask**, the famous floating inn and fest-hall run by **Liset Cheldar**.
    ◦ **Context:** Lord Cassian is trying to outdo the Mask’s reputation for opulence to prove that House Meliamne is the future of "New Neverwinter." Liset Cheldar may even be a guest, watching her competition with a critical eye.
**Part 3: The Cerulean Salon (Faction Warfare)**
The Salon is not just a party; it is a battlefield for the city's secret masters.
• **The Host (Kraken Society):** Lord Cassian is serving **"Deep-Water Cordial"** and using psionic music to subvert the elite. This aligns with the **Kraken Society’s** goal of infiltrating high society to find the **"Drowned Gate"** beneath the city.
• **The Guests (Ashmadai):** Among the guests is **Mordai Vell**, the charismatic Tiefling leader of the **Ashmadai**.
    ◦ **Interaction:** Mordai Vell is arguably the most socially powerful person in the room (besides Neverember, who is absent). He is *immune* to the psionic charm of the music due to his infernal pacts. He watches Vukradin with amusement. If Vukradin exposes the Kraken Society influence, he unwittingly aids the Ashmadai by removing a rival faction.
• **The Government (Abolethic Sovereignty):** **Mayor Soman Galt** is also present. As a puppet of the **Abolethic Sovereignty** (located in the Chasm), he feels the "wrongness" of the Kraken Society’s psionics, as they compete with his masters' own mind control. He may inexplicably panic or try to leave, adding tension.
**Part 4: The Climax & Jena’s Gambit**
• **The Performance:** Vukradin must perform to break the psionic hold on **Elara Meliamne**.
• **Jena’s Move:** When **Jena Roscoe** steps in to claim credit, she leverages the **Lord’s Alliance** reputation. She spins the narrative that the **Order of the Gauntlet** (Ser Kaelen) was reckless to send the statue through "rebel territory" (referencing the ambush), while the Alliance ensured the *cultural* victory.
• **The "Conflict-Free" Reward:** Elara is overjoyed and rewards Vukradin. However, instead of just gold, she gives him a **Letter of Credit** for the **Waterclock Guild**.
    ◦ **The Trap:** The Gazetteer reveals the **Waterclock Guild** has been infiltrated by the **Ashmadai**. By accepting this "patronage," Vukradin is technically taking money laundering from devil worshippers. This gives Jena future leverage: she can eventually reveal this to Vukradin to prove that "all gold is dirty," pushing him toward her pragmatic worldview.
