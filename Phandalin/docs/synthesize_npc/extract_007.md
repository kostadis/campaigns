## NPC Activity

Offered Vukradin a piece of Petunia's cheese as a goodwill gesture. Warned the party that orc, ogre, and dragon activity was devastating local farming, threatening beer production. Saw through Vukradin's deception attempt regarding dragon polymorphing (natural 20), but treated it as a joke, cementing rapport. Shook hands with Horia Stonetallow to finalize the mine protection agreement.

**Horia Stonetallow**
Appeared at the mine. Provided an etymological explanation of the Stonetallow clan name ("stone to allow the arch to pass"). Finalized the mine agreement with Zeleen via contract and handshake. Expressed skepticism at the wererats' modifications to the mine. Estimated weekly earnings of approximately 100 gold pieces, pending overhead calculations.

**The Falcon**
Encountered on the Triboar Trail traveling toward Phandalin. Revealed that orcs and ogres (subsequently confirmed as Anchorites of Talos) had overrun his Falcon's Hunting Lodge and were driving people out of Neverwinter Wood. Expressed distress that the party had never cleared the Woodland Manse. Refused to return to his lodge. Offered Vukradin a special wood for his music studio and proposed converting the lodge into a music venue with ale if the party reclaims it. Departed for Phandalin to settle debts and plan next moves.

**Xanth (Centaur)**
Encountered at Dragonbarrow. Warned the party about will-o'-wisps haunting the barrow at night. Revealed that Neverwinter Wood has been overrun by orcs allied with half-orc spellcasters (the Anchorites of Talos) who perform dark rites at a circle of standing stones atop a cave-ridden hill. Offered to guide the party to the Anchorites after their visit to Dragonbarrow. Confirmed the half-orc spellcasters are the Anchorites of Talos — the same group that previously attempted to kill the party. Recognized Valphine as a cleric of Lathander. Found bloodied and standing outside Dragonbarrow at the end of Chapter 32 as the party emerged.

**Boney (Skeletal Horse)**
Rose from bones in a tunnel niche within Dragonbarrow when Soma approached. Communicated via Speak with Animals. Believed it had been brought back to serve Lady Alagondar. Accepted the party's deception that Soma was Lady Alagondar reincarnated. Revealed it had been killed by will-o'-wisps while guarding something for Lady Alagondar. Directed the party northward, indicating the sword would be "across the way" from where Lady Alagondar rested. Present with the party as the Dragon Slayer Sword was claimed.

**Lady Alagondar (Ghost)**
Materialized in the northern chamber of Dragonbarrow after the party defeated the invisible stalker and claimed the Dragon Slayer Sword. Did not speak. Stared with hollow, mournful eyes in the direction of the Ice Spire Mountains before fading away. Her bones are interred in the northern chamber of Dragonbarrow, across from where the Dragon Slayer Sword rested atop the dragon skull.

**Invisible Stalker**
Ambushed Valphine in the northern chamber immediately after she picked up the Dragon Slayer Sword. A summoned elemental set to guard the sword or eliminate those who take it. Ceased attacking when Valphine dropped the sword and held position over it. Destroyed by combined moonbeam, Cloud of Daggers, and melee attacks. Brewbarry subsequently reclaimed the sword.

**Sister Kaella**
Arrived outside Dragonbarrow at the end of Chapter 32, injured, armor slashed, cloak missing. Fell to her knees before the party and delivered an urgent warning: the Carver is no longer fully human, speaks to his blade in a maddening tongue, and is marching on Icespire Hold that very night intending to bind the white dragon and use it to burn Phandalin. Was paralyzed mid-warning by Vukradin, then rendered unconscious by non-lethal strikes from Soma and Valphine. Last known status: unconscious, in the party's custody outside Dragonbarrow. Critically, she is the only known person who knows the location of Icespire Hold.

**Jax**
Appeared from the tree line outside Dragonbarrow at the end of Chapter 32, identified as the last of the Carver's agents. Led an ambush force including thugs and a Rot-Tusk Ogre. Thanked Vukradin for incapacitating Sister Kaella. Paralyzed by Vukradin's enchantment. Attacked by Soma in bear form while paralyzed. Killed during the ambush.

**Rot-Tusk Ogre**
Part of Jax's ambush force. Member of the same Rot-Tusk clan the party made enemies of earlier in their adventures. Paralyzed by Vukradin's enchantment. Killed by Brewbarry while paralyzed.

Timeline is critical: he was moving on Icespire Hold the same night Sister Kaella delivered her warning.

Jax confirmed as the last surviving agent of the Carver. Executed an ambush outside Dragonbarrow with thugs and a Rot-Tusk Ogre. Deliberately allowed Vukradin to silence Sister Kaella before revealing themselves — suggesting awareness that her warning needed to be stopped. Jax and the Rot-Tusk Ogre killed; surviving thugs' status unclear.

**The Carver / Carver's Echo — MAJOR ESCALATION**
Sister Kaella's warning constitutes a critical arc escalation: The Carver has transcended human form, is communing with his blade in an unknown dark tongue, and is actively moving to bind the white dragon of the Ice Spire Mountains. If successful, he intends to use the dragon to destroy Phandalin. This represents the Carver threat moving from a regional menace to an existential one. Score increase warranted.

The Anchorites of Talos are confirmed to be conducting active dark rites at a standing stone circle in Neverwinter Wood. The site sits atop a cave-ridden hill and is the focal point of organized orc and half-orc ritual activity. Combined with the Carver's apparent empowerment through his blade, this suggests an ongoing pattern of dark ritual activity in the region. Score increase warranted.

**Will-o'-Wisp / Restless Dead (Dragonbarrow)**
Multiple will-o'-wisps encountered and destroyed within Dragonbarrow. Lady Alagondar's ghost manifested and silently directed the party's attention toward the Ice Spire Mountains before fading. The ghost's appearance and directional gesture may indicate a connection between her death, the dragon, and current events.
