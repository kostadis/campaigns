## NPC Activity

**Fiddlestib and Dabblebob (gnome inventors)**
Appeared at their workshop to receive proof of the mimic's death. Immediately began discussing how to create a construct from the creature's entrails. Later overheard the party's conversation with the two kings and began arguing about bicameral legislatures and tamper-proof voting boxes. Fiddlestib declared herself in favor of a bicameral legislature with a unitary executive; Dabblebob preferred more kings for voting purposes.

**King Korboz and King Gnercli (dual monarchs)**
Appeared in the throne room to reward the party. Korboz complained that Gnercli does not believe him. Gnercli asserted his equal authority. Both kings were destabilized by Vukradin's rhetorical questioning about seniority and authority within a dual monarchy. Korboz subsequently asserted that as senior king he deserved preferential treatment. Gnercli provided the promised magical hat reward and dismissed the party. Their political relationship has been significantly strained.

**Adabra Gwynn (healer/potion-maker, Umbrage Hill)**
Found besieged by a manticore atop Umbrage Hill windmill. Refused to leave her windmill despite the party's attempts to escort her, citing her role in the natural order and her potion-crafting work. Revealed herself to be a member of the Emerald Enclave, naturalist faction. Warmed significantly to Soma upon learning of her naturalist druidic beliefs. Provided a note for Townmaster Harbin confirming the party's service and requesting their reward, along with a medical note for Harbin's treatment. Hosted the party overnight, providing a meal. The following morning, reversed her refusal to travel to Phandalin upon learning Vukradin's principled objection to monster-plunder gold, declaring solidarity with the naturalist cause. Accompanied the party back to Phandalin. Confronted Townmaster Harbin directly and advocated loudly for increased payment to the party. Before the party departed for the Tower of Storms, approached Soma privately near Barthen's Provisions to report an unnatural corruption affecting an old grove past Whisper Woods.

**Townmaster Harbin Wester**
Paid the party their reward (25 gold, split four ways) for rescuing Adabra. Under pressure from Adabra's confrontation, offered free room and board at Stonehill Inn as additional compensation, citing bandit activity as reason for limited coin. Later encountered by Vukradin regarding the Tower of Storms; confirmed the lighthouse is real and known to him, dismissed rumors of evil, and clarified that Lord Neverember would want the curse removed rather than the tower destroyed.

**Corbin (former bandit)**
Joined the party after being spared following the bandit ambush. Revealed his backstory: a farmer from Greenmeadow whose village was destroyed by the Uthgardt Black Lion tribe during the chaos following the breaking of the Ordning. His wife Elara and daughter Lily were killed. He drifted into banditry out of desperation, operating under a leader known as "Jax," who in turn answered to a figure called "the Carver." Corbin states he has never met the Carver and describes him only as "big." Assigned by Vukradin to help Adabra. Confirmed he has never robbed Adabra. Wearing scavenged armor taken from a dead man.

**Daran Edermath / "Silverleaf" (half-elf, retired adventurer)**
Appeared at Stonehill Inn. Described a stone lighthouse west of Phandalin on the coast where ships are drawn and wrecked. Presented this as a treasure opportunity to Vukradin, who rejected the framing entirely. Apologized when Vukradin objected to the concept of plundering wrecked ships.

**Ser Kaelen Thorn (Order of the Gauntlet)**
Appeared at Stonehill Inn. Described as a man in his early thirties with dark hair, a scar above his right eyebrow, and chain mail bearing the emblem of a gauntlet grasping a sword by the blade. Recruited Vukradin as an associate member of the Order of the Gauntlet, citing his principled stance against plunder and his potential as a public messenger for the Order's values. Referenced a member named Milo Goodbarrel as the leader of the strike force that entered the inner sanctum and halted a ritual during the War of the Dragon. Expressed a negative reaction when Soma was suggested as a potential recruit, stating her naturalist views would not align with the Order. Subsequently encountered again at Barthen's Provisions preparing to leave town; provided the party with three possible explanations for the Tower of Storms curse—a cleric-solvable curse, a haunting monster, or unfounded rumors—and assessed the most likely cause as a monster. Later encountered again in Phandalin after the party escorted Elara and Jarek back; expressed approval of Vukradin's conduct. Shared the Order's chant when pressed: *"Steal and shield, Heart and band. We stand as one. A steadfast band."* Acknowledged the chant was embarrassing and that Vukradin's talents were needed to improve it.

**Elara (merchant) and Jarek (groom)**
Found sheltering behind an overturned trade cart on the road. Reported being attacked by orcs. Elara noted the attackers moved with unusual discipline and bore odd markings—described as a swirl or an eye—before losing the detail. Jarek reported hearing a non-audible hum in his head immediately before the attack. Escorted back to Phandalin by the party. Elara directed her monetary reward to Valphine in Lathander's name.

**Martisha Vinetalker (Wayside Inn proprietor)**
Half-elf innkeeper described as cold, calculating, and charismatic. Directed the party to the coastal trail leading toward the Tower of Storms.

**Backes Dunfield**
Human bartender at Wayside Inn. Scarred face, crooked smile. No further interaction recorded.

**Cooragh Struckt**
Cook at Wayside Inn. Notable for well-spiced stews and meat pies.

## Faction Movements

**Emerald Enclave (Naturalist Faction)**
Adabra identified herself as a naturalist member of the Emerald Enclave. She articulated the naturalist position: that the multiverse, including divine interventions and extra-planar phenomena, is part of the natural order and should not be opposed. She distinguished this from the interventionist faction, which she characterized as prone to drawing lines between civilized and uncivilized beings and engaging in destructive slaughter of creatures. She expressed that the interventionists had been responsible for the corruption of constructs and abominations in the north. Before the party's departure, Adabra reported a separate matter to Soma: a grove past Whisper Woods, formerly associated with Chauntea, is now unnaturally corrupted—trees twisted in impossible shapes, colors wrong, creatures with too many eyes and erratic movements. She muttered something about interventionists and the Enclave in connection with this corruption. Soma agreed to investigate.

**Order of the Gauntlet**
Ser Kaelen Thorn recruited Vukradin as an associate member. The Order operates through donations and conflict-free funding, according to Thorn. It played a role in the War of the Dragon, with member Milo Goodbarrel leading the strike force that stopped a ritual in an inner sanctum. The Order has a negative disposition toward the Emerald Enclave naturalist faction. Thorn expressed approval of Vukradin's public profile as a principled anti-plunder artist as useful for messaging the Order's values to a wider audience. Thorn was preparing to depart Phandalin when last seen.

**The Carver's Bandit Network**
The party encountered and killed or routed a bandit group operating in the hills near Phandalin. Corbin, a surviving member, revealed that the group was organized under a local leader named Jax, who reported to a larger figure called "the Carver." Corbin has never met the Carver directly and describes him only as physically large. The network has been disrupted locally but its command structure remains intact.

**Unknown Organized Orc Force**
An orc warband ambushed the party on the coastal trail. Observed to be unusually disciplined for surface raiders. Included an orc shaman or cleric. Bore unusual markings described as a swirl or eye symbol. Elara and Jarek's earlier encounter with this group or an affiliated group included a reported psychic hum heard by Jarek before the attack. The party identified the ambush in advance, took higher ground, and attacked from the rear.

The combination of organized orc behavior, unusual sigils, and a possible psychic phenomenon represents a potential escalating threat. **Trigger: coordinated attack by non-chaotic orcs bearing unidentified faction markings; reported psychic phenomenon associated with attack.**

She connected this to interventionist Emerald Enclave activity. This may represent a planar distortion or magical corruption event. **Trigger: confirmed unnatural environmental corruption in a named location; associated with aberrant creature behavior.**

- The Carver is the regional leader of the bandit network operating near Phandalin. He is described as physically large. His subordinate Jax led the cell Corbin operated in.
- The Order of the Gauntlet was involved in stopping a ritual during the War of the Dragon. Milo Goodbarrel led the strike force.

- A grove past Whisper Woods is suffering unnatural corruption. Adabra suspects Emerald Enclave interventionist involvement.
- Manticores have a confirmed preference for human flesh over other humanoids.
- The coastal trail to the Tower of Storms passes through unnatural gullies that Vukradin noted cannot be explained by river erosion given the dry climate. Valphine assessed these as the result of magical forces, possibly related to the Faerzess or similar defensive/offensive magic.
- Toblen "Spiderman" Stonehill earned his nickname as a child by walking calmly through a hatching spider nest.
