**Jenna Roscoe**
- Suggested to Brewbarry that he could fund his music studio by abandoning his principles and "selling out." He declined.

**The Chief Accountant (Named NPC, undead dwarven figure)**
- Revealed that Valphine's sister Aletra had made a deal with the Axeholm king to install the planar machine, promising improved efficiency and profit margins.
- Stated the king had been jealous of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant operations.

**Aletra (Drow, fourth daughter of the Satora/Xorlarrin family, Valphine's sister)**
- Was found operating the broken planar machine in Axeholm, extracting a crate of Midnight Tears poison vials labeled "do not drop."
- Manipulated the party into fighting the Rift Weavers by marking Brewbarry as a spatial anomaly, redirecting all spider aggression onto him.
- Conversed with Valphine in Undercommon, displaying characteristic condescension and references to shared family history.
- Successfully extracted her cargo of interdimensional poison before the machine collapsed.
- Opened a shimmering portal and escaped, but fumbled and dropped several vials due to Valphine's blinding curse.
- Departing words referenced "the boss" being unhappy with the outcome and acknowledged that Valphine had at least tried to stop her.
- Personal quarters discovered in a former barracks: contained a silk bedroll, Zurkhwood folding desk, a forged ledger (altering "iron for 500 pounds" to "Midnight Tears - crate 4 - do not drop"), and personal notes in the machine's operator manual including "use only if KP isn't watching."
- Hidden cache found containing platinum coin, malachite gem, fine wine, and four vials of Midnight Tears.

**Zeleen Varnaster (Wererat, mine operative)**
- Greeted the party after the battle outside the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine with expressed relief.
- Claimed Don-jon Raskin had been killed by a carrion crawler; under pressure admitted this was misleading, describing it as Don-jon being "conveniently lured to his death."
- Agreed to a negotiated arrangement giving the party Don-jon's ownership share in exchange for handling the carrion crawler.
- Received Petunia's cheese on behalf of the wererats, reacting with delight.

**Don-jon Raskin (Deceased)**
- Physical evidence at the scene confirmed he was killed in combat with dwarves, not by the carrion crawler; the creature fed on his corpse afterward.

**The Carrion Crawler**
- Communicated via Soma's magic; described feeding on bodies of dwarves ("deep stone folk") and wererats ("false rats") after they killed each other.
- Convinced by Soma's arguments to vacate the mine and seek better hunting grounds.
- Lair contained magical darkvision goggles and Don-jon's chain.

**Horia (Dwarf, mine faction leader)**
- Produced a contract showing dwarves had a prior agreement with Don-jon and had been working the mine before the wererats arrived.
- Confirmed the dwarves killed Don-jon after he revealed his intent to dismiss them as unnecessary labor.
- Warned that the wererats were dangerously incompetent miners and would cause structural collapse within months.
- Agreed to consider a compromise arrangement if the wererats formally apologized for killing dwarves.
- Was deeply offended by Valphine's statement that contracts exist only to cheat people, questioning her devotion to Lathander.

## Threat Arc Events

**Planar Distortion**
- *Score increase trigger:* The Model 1489 Spatial Rectification Calliope had warped space-time throughout Axeholm for an extended period, transforming all dwarven inhabitants into out-of-phase entities and creating measurable spatial distortions on the upper floor. The machine's collapse resolved the immediate distortion, but evidence of its operation (Rift Weaver silk, dropped poison vials, the operator's manual) confirms a sustained and deliberate planar breach.
- *Score increase trigger:* Aletra's notes reference "use only if KP isn't watching," implying the machine's installation was conducted covertly against the oversight of an unknown superior—suggesting unsanctioned planar activity at scale.

**Kraken Society Echoes (or equivalent multiplanar trade conspiracy)**
- *Score increase trigger:* Recovery of a bill from "Interplanar Trade Consultants Incorporated" for 42,000 gold pieces indicates an organized multiplanar commercial entity was contracted to place or service the device. The scale and nature of this operation suggests institutional backing beyond a single rogue agent.

## Revealed Information

- **Aletra's employer:** Her notes reference "KP" as a supervising figure whose awareness of her activities she sought to avoid. Her departing words about "the boss" not being happy confirm she operates within a hierarchy.
- **Machine's origin:** Aletra installed the planar machine as a deal with the Axeholm king, promising improved mine efficiency. The king had been motivated by envy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional trade revenue.
- **Midnight Tears pipeline:** Aletra was using the planar machine as a smuggling conduit for Midnight Tears poison, part of House Xorlarrin's illegal drug trade across dimensions.
- **Don-jon Raskin's death:** Forensic evidence and dwarf testimony confirm Don-jon was killed by Horia's dwarves after revealing he intended to cheat them out of their contractual position using cheap wererat labor.
- **Axeholm's condition:** The fortress is structurally sound and cleared of hostile entities. It contains serviceable armor, smithing tools, a functioning hot spring, and storage capacity—viable as an evacuation site.
- **Mechanical mosquito constructs:** The sampling drones found in the smithy were collecting blood samples; their origin and purpose remain unidentified.
