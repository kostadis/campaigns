## NPC Activity

**The Carver (the Obsidian Sword)**
- Appeared at Icespire Hold performing a ritual over the bound dragon Cryovain at a summoning circle carved/burned into the rooftop stone.
- Moved out of Vukradin's Silence zone with legendary-quality footwork, drawing no opportunity attacks, demonstrating exceptional tactical awareness.
- When the vessel (the veteran soldier) was paralyzed by Hold Person, the sword spoke directly into Vukradin's mind: *"Coward's magic. You mute the ritual because you lack the strength to shatter the vessel. And now, now I shall have everything I always wanted."*
- Abandoned the ritual entirely and executed an immediate domination of Cryovain, dissolving the chains binding the dragon rather than completing the slower consumption the ritual had been building toward.
- After transferring into Cryovain, the sword went visually inert — black, still, emptied.

**Cryovain**
- Found chained at the center of a summoning circle on the roof of Icespire Hold, physically and magically restrained.
- Exhibited signs of severe degradation: scales discolored to the shade of old shadow-ice, eyes hollow and wrong, facial structure altered (nose apparently absent or consumed).
- Soma assessed his stillness as not unconscious but held — with compressed, non-cognitive rage beneath, past the point of rational waiting.
- Upon the Carver dissolving his chains, Cryovain came under full domination. A three-round window was identified before his mind would be entirely subsumed.
- Under domination, attacked the party: breathed a frost cone at Brewbarry (full hit, significant damage), then lifted thirty feet above the battlements to reposition.
- Struck by Valphine's radiant bolt and Vukradin's thorn whip (critical hit), the latter visibly surprising the dragon and breaking its contempt.
- Previously displaced the Icespire orc population by claiming the hold; those orcs then attacked settlements in the lowlands.

**Mother Drake**
- Returned to her lair in the mountain pass to find her young dead (killed by the Carver, who passed through in a straight line without deviation).
- Circled in wide arcs over the party, vocalizing repeatedly — behavior interpreted as grief, not active hunting.
- Responded to Vukradin's performance of the Moonlight Sonata (joined by Brewbarry) by descending gradually and settling beside the dead young.
- Permitted the party to pass without aggression; no combat occurred.

## Revealed Information

- **The Carver is the sword, not the man.** The veteran soldier is a vessel only. The obsidian sword is the actual threat actor and apparent seat of consciousness and intent.
- **The sword speaks telepathically** — directly into the minds of those who disrupt it, without using the vessel's mouth.
- **The ritual at Icespire Hold was a slow consumption process.** The sword was building toward something more refined; domination was a shortcut taken under duress when the ritual was interrupted.
- **Cryovain is not a willing or stable asset.** Even under domination, he is described as not thinking — pure compressed rage. The Carver has a weapon it may not be able to fully aim.
- **The chains binding Cryovain were magical and dependent on the ritual's continuation** (or the Carver's deliberate will) — they dissolved immediately when the Carver chose to release them, not when the party did anything.
- **The Carver killed the drake young in Icespire Pass** as a direct-line-of-travel action, confirming a behavioral pattern of treating all non-goal life as having no standing to matter.
- **The orc displacement from Icespire Hold is Cryovain's doing**, and restoring the hold would allow the orcs to return, ending their attacks on lowland settlements.
- **The Carver's sword was visibly glowing purple with accumulated power** before the domination; it is now inert, suggesting significant resource expenditure.
