## NPC Activity

**Townmaster Harbin Wester**
Appeared at Townmaster's Hall in Phandalin. Posted three bounty quests on the job board (Dwarven Excavation 50gp, Gnomengard 50gp, Umbrage Hill 25gp). Paid the party 50gp for delivering the dragon warning to the dwarven prospectors. Became upset when Vukradin challenged the ethics of Lord Neverember's adventurer plunder policy. Revealed the policy framework: adventurers pacify the hinterland, Neverwinter guards follow to establish law. During initial contact, refused to open his door, shouting about being too thin for a dragon's meal.

**Toblen Stonehill**
Proprietor of Stonehill Inn, Phandalin. Native of Triboar. Negotiated music-for-meals arrangement with Vukradin. Warned the party quietly about white dragon sightings in the area. Revealed that orc raids are a past threat, not current, and that the barbarian invasions were the most recent major danger.

**Dazlyn Grayshard**
Shield dwarf prospector. Appeared at the ancient dwarven ruins southwest of Phandalin. Revealed the site is a temple of Abbathor, the evil dwarven god of greed, destroyed by divine punishment during a failed sacrifice. Hired the party to clear oozes (or orcs, as it turned out) from the temple interior. Offered sending stones as payment. Cooked a meal for the party after the orcs were killed. Confirmed to be operating as an archaeological treasure hunter with no stated intent to return finds to any institution or owner.

**Norbus Ironrune**
Shield dwarf prospector, partner to Dazlyn. Present at the same ruins. Corroborated Dazlyn's account of the site and the threat within. Confirmed the sending stones offer. No independent actions noted beyond Dazlyn's account.

**Linene Graywind**
Master of the Lionshield Coster outpost in Phandalin. Purchased looted orc equipment from the party. Confirmed that the Lionshield Coster's preferred supply chain has been disrupted (implied interdimensional or trade-route disruption). Shared a rumor that strange magic pervades Neverwinter Wood, confounding navigators and obscuring ancient ruins. Still operational and stocking adventuring goods despite disruption.

**Pog**
Gnome guard at Gnomengarde's lower entrance. Challenged the party in Gnomish. Participated in bureaucratic debate with Ulla over whether to allow entry. Ultimately permitted entry after the standing order against Uthgardt barbarians was deemed unclear and lapsed.

**Ulla**
Gnome guard at Gnomengarde's lower entrance. Conducted most of the spoken challenge to the party. Revealed that Gnomengarde has standing orders to attack shapechangers and had a (possibly lapsed) order to attack Uthgardt barbarians on sight. Directed the party toward the inventor's workshop via the long route after learning they lacked Mage Hand. Later became suspicious of Pog when Valphine provoked her.

**Facktore**
Deranged gnome inventor encountered in the large room with the rotating crossbow platform. Activated the device against the party without warning. Fled after the party disabled the weapon. Described as deranged; no further information given.

**Fibblestib**
One of Gnomengarde's foremost inventors. Focused entirely on curing King Korboz's madness. Proposed a "sanity ray" as the solution. Argued with Dabbledob throughout. Credited the rotating blade trap as his invention and declared the party's survival proved its superiority over Dabbledob's approach. Willing to build a non-standard piano for Vukradin.

**Dabbledob**
Co-inventor at Gnomengarde. Proposed a straitjacket (without arm holes) to restrain King Korboz. Argued against Fibblestib's sanity ray. Noted that music has helped in some circumstances. Rolled her eyes at Fibblestib's claims of inventive superiority.

**King Korboz**
Co-ruler of Gnomengarde. In a state of hysterical madness, screaming and convinced that shapechangers are present. Refused direct interaction with the party. His madness is the central crisis of Gnomengarde. Claims that two missing gnomes, Warren and Orrin, were taken or replaced by a shapechanger.

**King Gnercli**
Co-ruler of Gnomengarde, currently functional. Engaged directly with the party. Confirmed two gnomes (Warren and Orrin) are missing and that a shapechanger is believed responsible. Offered a Hat of Wizardry as reward for killing the shapechanger. Suggested the creature would hide among large objects in the cellar. Currently managing the crisis in Korboz's place.

**The Mimic**
Shapechanger creature hiding in Gnomengarde's barrel cellar. Revealed itself when Valphine pointed a crossbow at it. Attacked Brewbarry with a pseudopod (missed). Had its weapon arm stuck with Brewbarry's halberd embedded in its body. Still alive and engaged in combat at end of session.

## Faction Movements

**Lord Neverember / Neverwinter Expansionism**
Advancing a policy of using adventurers as proxies to pacify the Phandalin hinterland before deploying Wintershield guards to establish formal governance. The policy guarantees tax-free, no-questions-asked plunder as incentive. A Wintershield guard in Neverwinter offered a 60-day found-property holding service as a fig leaf for ethical adventurers. The policy is actively operating: Townmaster Harbin Wester in Phandalin is the local administrator executing bounty quests funded by this program.

**Lionshield Coster**
Supply chain disrupted by unknown means (implied interdimensional or magical trade route interference). Forced to rely on conventional suppliers. Still operational in Phandalin. Linene Graywind shared intelligence about magical interference in Neverwinter Wood as a rumor, possibly connected to the supply disruption. No active quests offered.

**Gnomengarde (Gnome Clan)**
Internal crisis: two gnomes (Warren and Orrin) missing, one king (Korboz) driven mad, a mimic confirmed present in the cellar. The clan has activated defensive protocols including shapechanger-detection challenges at entry points and (possibly lapsed) orders to attack Uthgardt barbarians. Inventors are divided on how to treat the king's madness. The clan's capacity to produce useful magical items is intact but attention is consumed by the internal crisis. Currently negotiating with the party to resolve the mimic threat in exchange for a Hat of Wizardry.

**Cult of the Dragon / Giants' Ordning Conflict (Historical)**
Referenced in the lore framing only. Both resolved prior to session events.

**Uthgardt Barbarians (Historical)**
Referenced as having raided and destroyed Triboar, Calling Horn, and villages near Silverymoon and Mirabar during the chaos caused by Gromph Baenre's spell. Defeated by combined Giant and city forces. Gnomengarde still had a standing order (possibly lapsed) to attack Uthgardt barbarians on sight as a residual effect of these raids.

## Revealed Information

- **Lord Neverember's hinterland policy** is explicitly extractive: adventurers are proxies for eventual Neverwinter annexation of the Phandalin region. The 60-day property holding offered by Wintershield guards is a formalized method of laundering looted goods.
- **The Lionshield Coster's fabled interdimensional supply network has been disrupted.** Linene Graywind did not explain the cause, but strange magic in Neverwinter Wood is a rumored factor.
- **The temple southwest of Phandalin is dedicated to Abbathor**, the evil dwarven god of greed. It was destroyed by a divine-caused earthquake and avalanche, apparently as punishment for insufficient sacrifices. The settlement was buried at the same time.
- **Gnomengarde's internal crisis** involves: a missing-persons event (Warren and Orrin), a maddened king (Korboz), and a confirmed mimic hiding in the barrel cellar. King Gnercli believes the shapechanger would shelter among large, numerous objects.
- **Gnomengarde has bureaucratic standing orders** that include attacking shapechangers on sight and (possibly lapsed) orders against Uthgardt barbarians, suggesting prior contact with or casualties from both threats.
- **The white dragon's presence is actively suppressing bandit activity** on the Triboar Trail, an unintended secondary effect noted by Soma.
- **Facktore** is a gnome inventor operating within Gnomengarde whose sanity is in question; he activated a lethal rotating crossbow platform against visitors without apparent provocation.
