## NPC Activity

**Moesko (Orc Anchorite)** — Found seated in his chamber at the top of the Tower of Storms, wearing armor made from giant octopus hide, holding the opalescent conch belonging to Miral the Sea Elf. Defeated in combat by the party. His heart was removed and placed in the lighthouse beacon as a power source, continuing to beat after his death and producing the corrupt green light. A concealed compartment in his chamber held a talisman bearing the symbol of Talos on one side and an inverted eye with tentacle-like coils on the reverse.

**Miral the Sea Elf (Ghost)** — Manifested after the party returned her opalescent conch. Revealed that Moesko was not acting entirely of his own will: "I felt the shadows that moved within his wines, twisting his devotion to Talos into something colder, more calculating. It is not the Strage of the Stormlord that consumed him, but something foreign." Issued a warning: "Beware, surface dweller… the true storm gathers not in the sky, but in the depths below Leilon." Vanished after speaking. Spirit now at rest.

**The Giant Crab** — Appeared at the shoreline after the sharks were defeated. Expressed gratitude. Identified the +1 halberd recovered by Soma from the Star Crossed Lover as the treasure he had intended to retrieve for the party.

**Adabra (Midwife, Emerald Enclave)** — Encountered in Phandalin's town square. Expressed disappointment that the party had not yet investigated the Whispering Wood. Refused to engage substantively with Vukradin after he joined the Order of the Gauntlet, explaining she had stopped trusting him when he publicly chose a faction. Articulated her philosophy: the multiverse is one interconnected thing; striving to change its fundamental nature forces evil and good to respond, hurting everyone. Identified the Whispering Wood blight as likely caused by "interventionists" meddling, not the Order of the Gauntlet. Last seen in Phandalin town square.

**Linene Graywind (Lionshield Coster Proprietor)** — Informed the party that the Lionshield Coster's supply capabilities have been severely degraded since the War of the Giants. Scroll procurement now requires extended outreach with no guarantee of results. Last seen at the Lionshield Coster, Phandalin.

**Harbin Wester (Townmaster)** — Received the party's report on the Tower of Storms and the Talos infestation. Expressed relief at the lighthouse cleansing. Indicated he would send word to Lord Neverember. Strongly discouraged the party from traveling to Neverwinter with their mission incomplete, warning it could result in a hostile reception. Last seen at his residence in Phandalin.

**Ser Kaelen Thorne** — Met the party at the Stonehill Tavern. Expressed surprise that genuine Talos activity was present at the tower, noting a typical shaman could not produce the effects described and suggesting "some hidden force" is behind events. Confirmed widespread knowledge of the party's lighthouse mission, undermining Vukradin's suspicions that he had betrayed them. Disclosed that a bandit known as the Carver is operating in the area, hiding from detection spells and concealing himself even from his own henchmen. When informed of the Whispering Wood, dismissed it as low priority given the dragon, bandits, and orc movements, but acknowledged townfolk would be grateful if the party investigated. Agreed to look into the rightful owners of the salvaged shipwreck treasure when Vukradin disclosed the full inventory. Last seen at Stonehill Tavern, Phandalin.

**Stonehill (Innkeeper/Tavern regular)** — Revealed a betting pool had been running on whether all four party members would return from the lighthouse, with side bets on whether Vukradin would be ditched or killed. Won his bet by backing Vukradin's survival due to his music. Paid Vukradin 10 gold. Delivered the anonymous letter to the party's room, having received it from Neverwinter the previous night.

**The Stag** — Encountered in the Whispering Wood. A planar distortion has caused it to involuntarily teleport and continuously shift the shape of its antlers. Confirmed the strangeness began at the center of the forest. Mentioned that "other nice people" had previously spoken to it and promised to fix the distortion but had not done so. Accepted mushrooms from the party. Last seen in its territory within the Whispering Wood.

**Lyra** — Leader of an Emerald Enclave group trapped in the central clearing of the Whispering Wood by animated, thorny bramble vines. Identified herself and her companions as having been attempting to cleanse the grove's Treant guardian of a planar infection when it turned on them. Appealed to the party for aid. Current status: trapped in the clearing.

**The Treant (Corrupted Grove Guardian)** — Found at the center of the Whispering Wood's blighted clearing. Its bark has turned obsidian, its limbs bend in impossible geometries, and its movement is discontinuous and disorienting. Dripping glowing, iridescent sap into a dark pool. Reality tears pulse nearby. Spoke to the party with overlapping, whispering voices, claiming ownership of the grove and warning the party to leave it undisturbed. Resisting cleansing efforts by the Emerald Enclave. Current status: active and hostile, holding Enclave members captive via animated brambles.

## Faction Movements

**Order of the Gauntlet** — Ser Kaelen Thorne operating from Phandalin, focused on the Carver bandit threat, orc movements, and the dragon. Has not investigated the Whispering Wood. Relationship with Adabra strained. Anonymous letter received by the party alleges the Order accepts funding from House Margaster and has forcibly seized artifacts from neutral parties.

**Emerald Enclave** — A field team led by Lyra entered the Whispering Wood to cleanse the corrupted Treant guardian. The operation failed; the Treant turned on them. The team is now physically trapped by animated brambles in the central clearing and unable to cast spells. Adabra in Phandalin has been monitoring the blight and attempting to bring it to the party's attention. Adabra severed communication with Vukradin after he joined the Order of the Gauntlet. Historical note provided: Malfire Morazana convinced Delaan Winterhound to commit Emerald Enclave forces, including an army of Treants, during the War of the Dragons.

**Talos Cult (via Moesko)** — Operations at the Tower of Storms ended with Moesko's defeat. However, Miral's testimony indicates Moesko's devotion was being manipulated by an external, non-Talos force. The talisman recovered bears a symbol inconsistent with Talos worship: an inverted eye with tentacle coils, emanating psionic rather than divine energy.

**House Margaster** — Named in the anonymous letter as alleged donors to the Order of the Gauntlet. Previously associated in Neverwinter with evil cults. No direct operatives encountered. Authorship of the letter unconfirmed.

**The Carver** — Bandit operating in the Phandalin region. Concealing himself from divination spells. Unusually security-conscious even relative to his own henchmen. Location unknown. The party has a prior encounter with a low-ranking lackey connected to this organization.

- The Stag in the Whispering Wood is involuntarily teleporting and experiencing continuous physical alteration of its antlers due to planar bleed.
- The bramble thicket exhibits unnatural bioluminescence, color cycling, and apparent regeneration consistent with planar contamination.
- Wolves in the Whispering Wood displayed glowing eyes and a magically rooting howl.
- The pool at the center of the Whispering Wood reflects distorted alternate-reality visions and induces madness in those who enter it; Vukradin lost consciousness after entering; Brewbarry experienced acute terror and dissociation.
- The central clearing contains an immense corrupted Treant with obsidian bark, discontinuous movement, reality tears, and glowing iridescent sap pooling on the ground.
- Lyra's account confirms an active planar infection was identified and a cleansing attempt made, which failed and caused the Treant to turn hostile.

**Kraken Society Echoes / Deep Entity** — Triggering events:
- The talisman recovered from Moesko's concealed compartment bears a symbol of an inverted eye with tentacle coils and emanates psionic rather than divine energy, suggesting a non-Talos, non-standard entity.
- Miral's ghost explicitly stated that a "foreign" force, something "colder, more calculating" than Talos, was manipulating Moesko from within.
- Miral warned that "the true storm gathers not in the sky, but in the depths below Leilon."

## Revealed Information

- Moesko was not acting solely under Talos's influence; an unidentified external force was manipulating his devotion from within, described by Miral as "colder, more calculating" than the Stormlord.
- The talisman recovered from Moesko has psionic energy at its core and bears an inverted eye with tentacle markings—not a Talos symbol.
- The Whispering Wood blight originates at the grove's center and has been ongoing for months. A prior group (likely the Emerald Enclave team) visited the stag and promised to fix things but did not.
- The Emerald Enclave attempted and failed to cleanse the corrupted Treant; their team is now trapped.
- The Carver actively evades magical detection and maintains operational security even from subordinates, making him an unusually sophisticated bandit threat.
- The Order of the Gauntlet is alleged (via anonymous letter from Neverwinter) to receive funding from House Margaster and to have seized artifacts from neutral parties. Source and truthfulness unverified.
- Adabra stopped communicating with Vukradin specifically because he publicly joined the Order of the Gauntlet; she views faction allegiance as a form of interventionism incompatible with her worldview.
- The shipwreck inventory disclosed to Ser Kaelen: +1 halberd (Star Crossed Lover, from the captain's skeleton), blue quartz mermaid statue (Sea Urchin, locked chest), Cloak of Many Fashions (Vainglory, from the captain's skeleton), six pearls in a black silk pouch (Golden Gull, locked chest), spellbook and Wand of Secrets (Orca).
