

**The Thistle’s Echo Score: Telemetry of the Stolen Watch**  
**Definition:** A hidden, DM-tracked metric (scaled 0–10) measuring Brewbarry's gradual realization that his exile from the Uthgardt tribe was orchestrated by a specific fairy. He believes his failure was due to drunken negligence, unaware that he was surgically neutralized by Thistle Wendrod, a minor member of Queen Titania's Seelie court who viewed the local inhabitants as mere props.  
**I. Catalysts for Increase (The Thistle Triggers)**  
The score increases (+1) only when the party’s actions inadvertently recreate the specific tactics Thistle used during the *Storm King's Thunder* campaign:

* **The "Goodberry" Sedation:** Thistle was known to use *goodberries* to magically induce fullness and pacify threats, famously using them to neutralize a goblin ambush without bloodshed. If Soma (or any other character) uses magically conjured food, berries, or herbal remedies to solve a problem, Brewbarry’s memory jogs. He remembers that his "mead" that night tasted unnaturally sweet and left him feeling magically, artificially satiated before he blacked out.  
* **The Hiss of the Drake:** Thistle was constantly accompanied by a guard drake named Thuul, who fought alongside him in close-quarters combat. If the party encounters drakes, or if Brewbarry hears a reptilian hiss in the dark, it triggers a suppressed auditory memory of the creature that stalked the perimeter of his camp while the fairy worked.  
* **Death from Above:** Thistle’s signature combat protocol was to fly to an elevated, unreachable position and strike with lethal precision using his bow. Whenever a party member or enemy utilizes an aerial archery advantage, Brewbarry’s Danger Sense flares unnecessarily, triggering a subconscious reflex to look up into the canopy—a tactical habit he developed too late.  
* **The Seelie Condescension:** Thistle operated with the distinct arrogance of the fey courts, often manipulating or deceiving those he deemed lesser. If Vukradin or Valphine use deception or performative manipulation to trick a guard or sentry, Brewbarry experiences a phantom surge of resentment, subconsciously realizing he was on the receiving end of a similar performance.

**II. The Escalation Tiers (Symptoms of Awakening)**

| Score | Psychological State | Narrative Manifestations |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **0–2** | **The Heavy Hangover** | Brewbarry actively blames his own lack of discipline. He aggressively shuts down any conversation about the night he lost his honor. He dislikes magical food, preferring raw, mundane meat. |
| **3–5** | **The Phantom Wings** | The memory bleeds through the psychological wall. When a trigger occurs, Brewbarry hears the distinct, rapid buzzing of fairy wings and the snap of a bowstring. He begins questioning the timeline: *"I am a Goliath. One cask of mead does not put me to sleep. Something else was in the cup."* |
| **6–8** | **The Subconscious Rage** | His barbarian rage begins to target the sky. He develops an intense, seemingly irrational hatred for flying creatures, fey, and tricksters. He actively distrusts Soma's druidic magic if she attempts to offer him potions or enchanted sustenance. |
| **9–10** | **The Uthgardt Reckoning** | The memory is fully restored. Brewbarry remembers the exact face of Thistle Wendrod, the Seelie fairy who ruined his life. His shame evaporates, replaced by a cold, multiversal hunger to find this specific ranger and his drake. |

**I. Lore Master Evaluation**

* **Status Check:** Brewbarry currently operates as the physical anchor for the "Protocol Shell-Crackers" party. He relies on his brute strength and Uthgardt resilience, coping with his deep-seated guilt over the desecration of his tribe's sacred ground.  
* **Hidden Truths:** \[Meta-knowledge/Spoilers\] Brewbarry was not a negligent guard. He was a victim of Thistle Wendrod. During one of Thistle's many chaotic operations across the North, the fairy required access to the Uthgardt sacred ground (perhaps searching for a relic for the Oracle of Annam, or hunting a giant). Rather than fighting the towering Goliath, Thistle slipped a magically potent *goodberry* concoction into Brewbarry's drink, causing an irresistible, magical slumber, while Thuul the drake stood watch.

**II. Strategy Evaluation**

* **Faction Reactions:**  
  * **The Uthgardt Tribes:** If Brewbarry proves that his failure was the result of high-level Seelie court interference rather than mundane drunkenness, his tribe may retract his exile, viewing him as a survivor of a cosmic trick rather than a coward.  
  * **The Emerald Enclave:** The Enclave's delicate treaties with the fey will become a friction point. If Brewbarry seeks vengeance against the Seelie court, Soma will be caught between her druidic allies and her Goliath protector.  
* **The Chessboard:** The "Coordinating Force" (KP) observes this anomaly with intense fascination. By allowing the player's current character (Brewbarry) to slowly realize he was victimized by the player's *past* character (Thistle), KP is testing the structural integrity of the player's own multiversal memory. KP will subtly introduce fey-related anomalies to accelerate this collision of timelines.  
* **GM Recommendation:** **The "Stolen Rations" Trigger.** The next time the party rests, have them discover that normal rations have spoiled, leaving them to rely entirely on magically conjured food (like Soma's *Goodberries* or a similar spell). As Brewbarry consumes the magical berry, describe the overwhelming, unnatural sensation of sudden fullness—and immediately tell the player that this exact, chilling sensation is the last thing Brewbarry remembers feeling on the night he lost his honor. Log a \+1 to the **Thistle's Echo Score**.
