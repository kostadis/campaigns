### **Dossier: The Splinter Colony & The Kraken Society Hierarchy**

#### **Tier 1: The Biological Anchor (The Deep Trench)**

At the absolute bottom of the hierarchy—geographically, though they are the apex in power—lies the biological engine of the entire conspiracy.

- **The Enslaved Kraken:** A physical, leviathan-class entity captured by the illithids when they crash-landed. It is physically chained in an unreachable oceanic trench. Its massive, primal cerebral cortex is used as a biological amplifier to broadcast psionic signals across the continent.
- **The Elder Brain (The Impatient Core):** Surgically grafted onto the Kraken's brain. While it is the center of the colony, the sheer scale of the Kraken's primal rage and the vastness of the planetary quarantine have made the Elder Brain impatient and erratic. It secretly disagrees with its own lieutenants and is actively orchestrating a shadow rebellion on the surface to accelerate their escape.

#### **Tier 2: The Deep Triumvirate (The Patient Wardens)**

These three ancient mind flayers reside in the deep trench alongside the Kraken. They believe that Knazreponnium Ketternopappux's (KP's) multiversal quarantine is a gift—an "incubator" that keeps rival empires out while they slowly and safely subjugate Toril. They are unaware that the Elder Brain is actively subverting their patient doctrine.

- **Xeltharuun, The Network Weaver:** The psionic spymaster who monitors the "official" surface proxies. Xeltharuun issues orders to deeply embedded spies, aiming to suppress chaotic variables (like wars or dragon rampages) that might draw multiversal attention to Toril.
- **Qor'tash, The Biological Architect:** The flesh-grafter who fused the Elder Brain and the Kraken. Qor'tash views the quarantined planet as a pristine laboratory and designs ecologically integrated "overseers" to permanently replace the region's natural apex predators.
- **Ul'vattix, The Quarantine Warden:** The planar mathematician tasked with defending KP's lockdown. Ul'vattix actively hunts down and destroys high-level planar artifacts (like the *Ruinstone* or the *Planar Energy Conduit*) to ensure no surface-dweller accidentally breaches the quarantine before the mind flayers are ready to harvest the planet.

#### **Tier 3: The Rogue Surface Faction (The Elder Brain's Rebels)**

Operating in the shallows and coastal caves, these rogue mind flayers take direct, secret orders from the Elder Brain. They bypass the Triumvirate's cautious approach, pushing for immediate, violent ascension and the breaking of the planar quarantine.

- **Syrkloc, The Instigator:** Embedded near Leilon, Syrkloc creates chaotic smokescreens by intentionally escalating the violent conflicts between the local cults of Talos and Myrkul. Syrkloc is responsible for the erratic, aggressive mental compromises found in regional leaders.
- **Vell'thyk, The Excavator:** A brutal commander who eschews stealth. Vell'thyk utilizes heavily armed Kraken Society thralls and salvaged illithid-tech weaponry to violently excavate "The Drowned Gate" beneath Leilon, seeking the artifacts necessary to shatter KP's quarantine.

#### **Tier 4: The Kraken Society (The Unwitting Proxies)**

This massive network of humanoids operates on the surface, entirely unaware that they are serving a fractured alien colony. They believe they are worshipping a sea god, serving a secret mercantile elite, or advancing the teachings of Ubbalux, a long-forgotten mind flayer philosopher who theorized about "elevating" the kraken into godhood.

- **Serel Mardun (Diplomatic Attaché):** A high-level deep agent embedded in Lord Neverember's court. Mardun provides the Society with political cover and misdirects investigations. Xeltharuun watches directly through this agent's eyes.
- **Captain Tharros (Leilon Guard Commander):** A loyal soldier who has been mentally compromised by Syrkloc's dream entities. He issues illogical defensive orders that leave Leilon vulnerable to the Rogue Faction's excavations.
- **Brother Halgar (Cleric of Oghma):** A quiet scholar in Leilon who has become obsessed with deciphering "dragon dreaming". His research into planar memory manipulation is being secretly funded and guided to unlock the location of Ubbalux's ancient relics.

### **I. Lore Master Evaluation**

- **Status Check:** The party is currently executing a raid on Icespire Hold to prevent the Carver (Xal'vosh) from binding the white dragon Cryovain. They still view the Kraken Society strictly through the lens of a fringe cult and are entirely unaware of the deep, systemic civil war occurring within the aberrant ranks.
- **Hidden Truths:** Xal'vosh (the sentient Drow/Illithid sword) is terrified of the Deep Triumvirate's slow suffocation of the planet. It is trying to secure a draconic host specifically because a flying, unkillable vessel is the only way to escape the impending planetary harvest.

### **II. Strategy Evaluation**

- **Faction Reactions:**
  - **The Deep Triumvirate:** If the party destroys Xal'vosh, the Triumvirate will actually be pleased, as the sentient sword represents an unpredictable, highly volatile variable that threatens the stability of their "incubator."
  - **The Rogue Surface Faction:** Syrkloc and Vell'thyk will view the party's success at Icespire Hold as proof that the party is highly lethal. They will immediately attempt to manipulate the party into attacking the Triumvirate's assets, using them as unwitting pawns in the mind flayer civil war.
- **The Chessboard:** The "Coordinating Force" (KP) mathematically approves of the Deep Triumvirate's goal to keep the quarantine sealed, but views the Elder Brain's rogue faction as a critical system error. KP will subtly adjust planar probabilities to ensure the party crosses paths with Syrkloc and Vell'thyk to neutralize the rebels.
- **GM Recommendation:** **The Schism Reveal.** In the next tier of play, allow the party to intercept a Kraken Society communication or witness a skirmish between cultists. Instead of fighting town guards, the party should witness Kraken Society thralls violently assassinating *other* Kraken Society thralls. If they search the bodies, they find conflicting orders: one set of missives (from the Triumvirate) ordering the burial and concealment of a planar artifact, and another set (from the Rogue Faction) demanding its immediate excavation and use. This introduces the terrifying reality that the cult is at war with itself.
