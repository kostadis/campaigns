# Chapter 27 When the Machine Screams: A Family Affair

The party found themselves in a tense standoff with Valphine's sister, Aletra, who was working to extract a mysterious package from a broken planar machine. The room was covered in interplanar spider webs, and six Rift Weavers—spider-like creatures bound to the ceiling—stood guard over the malfunctioning device. Aletra explained that the machine had been a gift to her boss and was causing dangerous spatial anomalies throughout the area. She claimed they were in a "spatial equilibrium" and warned that if she couldn't retrieve her package, she had no reason to destroy the machine, leaving everyone to face the consequences of its catastrophic failure.

As the party prepared to engage the Rift Weavers, Valphine attempted to slow the creatures with magic, successfully hampering three of them. Brewbarry and Vukradin moved into position while Soma summoned a sphere of roiling flames that scorched several of the spiders and began burning away the webs. However, Aletra revealed her true intentions when she pointed her wand at Brewbarry, causing him to glow with an ominous red light. The machine immediately detected him as a spatial anomaly, and all the Rift Weavers turned their attention toward him, fixated on eliminating the perceived threat. Aletra had manipulated the situation perfectly, using the party as pawns while she worked on the machine in relative safety.

The battle intensified as the Rift Weavers attacked Brewbarry relentlessly. One of the creatures managed to ensnare him in sticky webs, restraining him while others attempted to bite through his defenses. Valphine cast protective magic on Brewbarry, granting him resilience against the onslaught, while the Rift Weavers used their cosmic abilities to free themselves from the slowing effects. Aletra shouted to Valphine, reminding her of a previous trap they had set together using a stuffed creature as bait, suggesting she simply needed to keep Brewbarry alive long enough for Aletra to complete her work. The drow sister continued manipulating the machine, cursing its dwarven creators for their inefficient design.

Soma's flaming sphere proved crucial in the fight, as the party discovered that the Rift Weavers were deeply disturbed by magical fire. The sphere not only damaged the creatures but also burned through the webs restraining Brewbarry, preventing future attempts to ensnare him. When Valphine struck one of the spiders with radiant energy, it recoiled violently, revealing another vulnerability. Aletra observed this with sardonic amusement, making a cutting remark about their mother saying Valphine wasn't the sharpest tool in the shed, though she did enjoy being the target of pointy tools. The sisters exchanged barbs even as the battle raged around them.

Brewbarry and Vukradin fought with devastating efficiency once the webs were cleared, their weapons cutting through the Rift Weavers with brutal precision. The creatures chittered in frustration as their attempts to restrain the party failed repeatedly, their webs burning away in the magical flames. One by one, the spiders fell, dissolving into crackling sparkles as they were destroyed. The remaining Rift Weavers became confused, torn between attacking Brewbarry and the malfunctioning machine itself, which was beginning to emit warnings of catastrophic failure.

As the last of the Rift Weavers fell, Aletra successfully extracted a box containing vials of interdimensional poison—part of House Sartorra's illegal drug trade. She called out to Valphine, asking if she had the shutdown code and urging her to use it. With dark humor, Aletra remarked that she would hate to have to collect Valphine's body parts and bring them back to their mother, then admitted she unfortunately couldn't leave any evidence behind. She pressed a small device, opening a shimmering portal before her.

In a desperate attempt to stop her sister's escape, Valphine struck Aletra with a blinding curse. Though Aletra had positioned the portal directly in front of herself and managed to step through despite her sudden blindness, the spell caused her to fumble and drop several of the precious vials onto the ground. As she disappeared through the portal, she muttered something about "the boss" not being happy with this development. Aletra's final words acknowledged that Valphine had at least tried to stop her, which was better than simpering and crying like the last time, though she couldn't quite remember if Valphine had been moaning in pain or pleasure during that previous encounter.

With Aletra gone and the machine screaming warnings of imminent catastrophic failure, Valphine rushed to the keypad and entered the shutdown code. The machine immediately ceased its alarming countdown and began to fold in on itself, collapsing and shrinking as it prepared to return to whatever dimension it had come from. The party watched as the device completely disappeared, leaving behind only a small piece of paper—a bill from "Interplanar Trade Consultants Incorporated" detailing payments for services rendered. The remaining Rift Weaver, having nothing left to repair with the planar device gone, simply winked out of existence.

The party stood in the now-quiet room, surrounded by the aftermath of battle and the dropped vials of poison that Aletra had left behind. The spatial anomalies that had plagued the area were finally resolved, though questions remained about the true nature of the machine, Aletra's mysterious boss, and what other schemes the drow sister might be involved in. The bill from the interplanar trade consultants suggested that the machine's presence had been part of a much larger operation, one that extended far beyond the immediate conflict they had just resolved.
