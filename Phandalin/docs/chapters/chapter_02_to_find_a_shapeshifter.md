# Chapter 02 To find a shapeshifter

## 02-01 of Taraskh 1495
### Valphine
“The ogre is prone on the ground under the sun. Like Lathander obliterates the darkness with his light, I will smash his body,” Valphine thinks, her muscles tensing, a grim determination set on her face. The morning sun, usually a source of warmth and comfort, now illuminates the brutish form of their downed foe, a testament to the battle that has raged through the early hours. She sees an opportunity, a chance to end this struggle decisively.
With a grunt of exertion, her mace swings through the air, a metallic arc against the backdrop of the rugged mountain peaks. The weighty weapon descends, intended to crush, to obliterate the ogre. But fate, or perhaps simply a moment of misjudgment, intervenes. The mace lands with a dull thud against the ogre's hide, a glancing blow that carries no force, no impact. The creature, surprisingly resilient, remains unhurt.
From her left, Valphine hears the unmistakable sound of Soma’s snigger, a quiet, mocking sound that grates on her nerves. “A broad side of a barn would have been harder to miss,” Soma jibes, her voice low but cutting, a constant irritant amidst the chaos of battle. Valphine’s jaw tightens, a flush of annoyance rising in her cheeks.
Then, from her right, she hears the roar of Brewbarry, a sound that promises devastation. The Uthgardt barbarian, a force of nature in his own right, swings his formidable halberd down on the ogre. The massive weapon slices through the air with a menacing whisper, a testament to Brewbarry’s strength and fury. But just like Valphine’s strike, the blow misses its mark. The halberd, instead of finding purchase on the ogre’s flesh, strikes the rocky ground with a deafening clang that echoes through the tight mountain valley. The sound resonates, a stark reminder of their collective failure to land a decisive blow.
Just in front of her, a puff of green gas, acrid and foul, suddenly surrounds the ogre. It coughs, a guttural sound that speaks of pain and discomfort. The gas, evidently a potent concoction from one of their more magically inclined companions, has found its mark. The creature is hurt, its movements momentarily hampered, but it is not felled.
With a surprising burst of energy, the ogre stands, its beady eyes scanning the faces of its attackers. It looks at Valphine, then at Brewbarry, its monstrous mind weighing its options, deciding who to strike next. A moment of chilling deliberation, and then its gaze settles on the towering barbarian. It picks the Uthgardtian.
Valphine watches in a moment of suspended horror as the ogre’s crude, but massive, axe lands on Brewbarry. A sickening thud, and for a minute, the barbarian buckles, his enormous frame swaying. But then, a miracle. Thanks to the legendary rage of the Uthgardt barbarian and his mystical powers, the axe does nothing. The blow, intended to cleave him in two, glances off his hardened skin, absorbed by an invisible resilience.
The failure of such a mighty blow catches both the Ogre and Valphine by surprise. Valphine, her own heart pounding, watches the creature stare in disbelief, its brutish face contorted in a mix of confusion and fear. And then, a truly astonishing sight: the ogre turns and runs, its powerful legs carrying it away from the fight, its previous aggression replaced by a primal instinct for self-preservation.
Valphine, her mind still reeling from the unexpected turn of events, swiftly puts an arrow in her handheld crossbow. Her movements are practiced, instinctual. She aims at the fleeing creature, a dark silhouette against the sun-drenched mountainside, and shoots. The bolt whistles through the air, finding its mark. The beast is wounded, a fresh spurt of blood staining its hide, but it continues to run, its speed undiminished. Even Brewbarry, his fury still simmering, cannot bring him down.
“Vukradin does the killing blow,” Valphine thinks, as she watches the ogre breathe its last. A quiet satisfaction settles over her, a sense of accomplishment despite the messy end to the encounter.
And from the rear, she hears Soma shout to Vukradin, her voice laced with indignation, “He was running away! This was senseless.” Soma, ever the pragmatist, sees only the unnecessary expenditure of effort, the pursuit of a defeated foe.
Valphine is surprised by Vukradin’s answer, a calm, almost philosophical response that seems out of place in the aftermath of battle. “He hated music,” Vukradin states, his voice even. “And he probably had some treasure he had stolen from someone else.” A shrug, a simple justification for an act that, to Soma, seemed utterly pointless. The battle may be over, but the quirks and motivations of her companions continue to surprise Valphine.
Valphine looks over the body and finds a whistle, some coins, and a key. 
### Soma
The rest of the journey to Gnomengarde is uneventful. Soma finds the river that leads to the gnomish hold. At the end of the river is a waterfall whose erosion of the rock has created a natural concavity. The roaring waterfall creates a cloud of mist as it plunges into a shallow pool of water, within which rise two small islands covered with two-foot-tall red, green, and purple mushrooms. Several cave openings overlook the pool from rocky ledges twenty to thirty feet above. The mountain blocks any sunlight from reaching this place. 
“Where to?” 
And before she can answer, Vukradin is wading through the water towards the mushrooms. She follows him closely. 
The mushrooms are two feet tall and grow atop small islands in the middle of the 3-foot pool. 
Soma watches Vukradin as he examines them. 
“These red ones are oily. Not sure what they are useful for. These Green ones remind me of a mushroom that can be turned into flour. We should forage as many as we can. And these purple mushrooms can be fermented to make mushroom wine.”

Some has a delighted expression on her face as she imagines what it tastes like, “I want some of those.”  
Vukradin doesn’t understand her expression and assumes she is jesting and says, “Mushroom wine tastes as bad as it sounds.” 
Soma shrugs.
With the mushrooms in his bag, Vukradin shouts, “Let’s go find the secret treasure room behind the waterfall.”
Soma stares at him. He announces, “There is always a secret treasure room behind the waterfall.”

She follows him and stands under the waterfall, getting soaked. And behind the waterfall is nothing but a rock wall. Vukradin taps at the rock, touches the rock, and pokes at the rock, but it is - just that - rock. 
“Can we get out of here?” she asks. 
Vukradin nods. He looks at the cliff wall next to the waterfall, and notices that there is an entrance 20 feet above. 
He starts to climb up. 
Soma shrugs, and follows him. 

Valphine and Brewbarry stand and watch. 
The druid shouts, “You coming?” 
“No,” answers the Drow. 
Soma looks at her and sighs. The Drow wants a rope. So the druid climbs down, takes the rope from the Drow's hands, and climbs up again. 
Once up the 20’ cliff, Soma walks up stairs that lead 15 feet deeper into the mountain and open up into a rough chamber with a hallway to the north, east, and a slick rock wall to the south, atop which is a guard post. 
A gnome shouts in Gnomish. Soma stares at him. Then another female voice shouts, “Who goes there?”
Vukradin answers, “Vukradin, the Bard!” 
The gnome asks, “Are you a shapechanger?” 
“No.”  
“Can you prove it?” 
Soma grouses, “Would we take these shapes if we were shapechangers?” 

The female gnome talks to the male gnome and then says, “That’s what a shapechanger would say!” 
Soma rolls her shoulders, and mutters, “I sense more senseless violence coming.” 
But Vukradin isn’t done. 
“Your standing orders are only to attack shape changers; your interpretation would lead to attacking anyone. Is that the intent of the orders?” 
The male gnome looks at Vukradin, and the female gnome shouts, “Perhaps, but that’s what a shapechanger would say.” 
Brewbarry has had enough. “I am Brewbarry, killer of Ogres! I am no shapeshifter!” 
The male gnome and the female gnome talk some more. The female gnome announces, “We’ve decided that he’s a Uthgardt Barbarian and not a shapeshifter.” 
Brewbarry growls, “I used to be one.” 
At that, the female gnome smiles, “Oh, that’s so much better. There used to be a standing order to attack Uthgardtians on sight as a result of the events of the last few months. But since you used to be one, it’s unclear whether that standing order stands. Furthermore, at the last meeting, it was unclear if the ‘attack uthgardian barbarians’ order was renewed and so given that we have decided to let you pass.” 
Soma stares in disbelief, “Bureaucracy saved us.” 
### Vukradin

“I want to learn more about our new friends. Tell me, friends, who are you? Do you like music? I am a bard.”

The male gnome shouts, “Pog,” and the female gnome shouts, “I am ulla! And yea, we like music. Mostly gnomish music.” 
Vukradin tries a few notes, and the gnomes seem pleased. 
Pog waves his hand, as if to tell Vukradin to stop, and asks, “Why are you here?” 
Vukradin explains that the mayor is looking for some kind of magical device that can help fight against the threat of the dragon. 
Ulla scratches her head and says, “You should go to the inventor workshop.” She tells Vukradin that the best way to get there is to cross the hanging bridge from where they entered the caves. 
Vukradin thanks them and marches towards the inventor’s workshop. After crossing the bridge, he enters a room that is lightly obscured by the mist of the waterfall. But he notices, just in time, that the room contains two rapidly spinning devices that look like turnstiles fitted with stacks of long, sharp blades spaced 1 foot apart. The northern turnstile spins counterclockwise, while the southern one spins clockwise.
He stares in disbelief and marches back to the where Pug and Ulla were sitting. 
“That was a death trap!” 
Ulla is surprised. “I thought you had a mage who could cast Mage Hand. Well if you don’t, then you’ll have to go the long way through the northern hallway. Go north past the first passageway going east, past a large room, and then you’ll see another passageway going east out of the mountain. Go out from there. Then cross the river, and climb up the cliff wall, where you will enter the sleeping chambers of most of the gnomes. From there, there is a passageway going south that leads to the inventors’ workshop.” 
Vukradin thanks her and heads north. 
The first large room is not empty. It has a rotating platform equipped with four heavy crossbows that reload automatically. The inventor is a deranged gnome, as they learn later, named Facktore, who decides to test his device on Vukradin and his companions. 
Although Vukradin is tempted to kill him, he decides that perhaps disarming the device is sufficient. 
With the bow disabled and Facktore having fled the scene of the battle, Vukradin climbs down the cliff wall, wades through the river, stopping on the island of mushrooms to pick a few more, and then climbs the cliff wall again. 
Once he’s climbed the walls, he enters a large cave that is strewn with the remnants of old campfires. Four side caves serve as sleeping areas, with five small wooden cots crammed into each one. 
To the south is the entrance to the laboratory of the gnomes. 
### Valphine
The Drow cleric stares at the two gnomes arguing. She is familiar with these debates among inventors, arguing over details that don’t matter. 
Vukradin explains that they are there to look for help to fight a dragon, and the inventors brush him off. 
“That’s unimportant compared to the calamity facing our community. My name is Fibblestib, and this is my coworker, Dabbledob. We are Gnomengarde’s foremost inventors, and we are entirely focused on a cure for King Korboz’s madness.” 
Dabbledob looks at him and grouses, “If you think your proposal will do anything you’re a fool. There is no evidence that any such ray would work.” 
She turns to Valphine, “A sanity ray!” 
Fibblestib is offended and immediately starts shouting, “You want to build a straitjacket instead. A jacket with no holes for the hands! That’s not a straitjacket, that’s a shirt with no holes for the hands.” 
Before Valphine can interrupt them, Dabbledob and Fiddlestib argue some more amongst themselves. 
Valphine looks at them, “I am a cleric!” 
Both gnomes stare at her aghast, “We are gnomes, we invent our way out of illness.” 
Soma tries to explain the value of cleric spells, but the gnomes just keep shouting louder. 
Valphine turns to Vukradin, “I don’t think I could cure him, and I also don’t think we have the money.” 

Vukradin counts the money in his head and sighs, “Yes.” 
Fiddlestib turns to Valphine, “I think we should walk you through what happened. It will allow us to revisit all of the salient facts. And please interrupt us with any questions! You may see something that we missed.” 
And so the two inventors explain that two gnomes, Warren and Orrin, went missing and that King Korboz claims it was a shape-changing creature. 
Valphine hears what they have to say, and before she can suggest torture, Brewbarry says, “We talk! We cure!”
She smiles, the barbarian and she are on the same wavelength. Torture is what purifies the crazed mind. 
But before she can make the case, Vukradin starts talking again and explains that he would like to try music. 

Dabbledob sighs, “Music has helped in some circumstances.” 
Vukradin asks if they have a piano, and Fiddelstib regretfully says no, but offers to build one. “It won’t be a normal piano! But it will play music.” 
### Vukradin

Vukradin explains what happened with the rotating blades and the rotating crossbow assault system.

The bard and his friends go through the room with the spinning blades, this time disabling the blades , then cross the bridge, and arrive where Pog and Ulla are sitting. 
### 
Valphine

Valphine, who knows about more dangerous shapeshifters in the Dark Realms, stares at the two gnomes, convinced they must be the shapeshifters. 

Ulla looks disturbed and shouts, “We are real gnomes!” 
Valphine shouts, “That’s what a shapeshifter would say!” 
Ulla turns on Pug, “I noticed how he looked a little bit different the past few days!” 

Pug turns on Ulla, “Me? You’ve looked different.” 
The two continue to argue in front of the Drow, who smiles. 
From there, she follows the passage north, and instead of going to the room where they found the rotating crossbow, she takes a right into a room full of barrels. 
This room contains twelve forty-gallon barrels set into wide alcoves. A wooden brace secures each barrel. The barrels in the south alcove have been tapped with wooden spigots. Two of those barrels are half full, and two are nearly empty.
She steps into the room and points a crossbow at the first barrel. 
And the barrel immediately transforms into a mimic, its pseudo-pods reaching out to strike, and its massive mouth full of razor-sharp teeth opens. 
“We found it!” shouts Valphine
Brewbarry charges at the creature, striking with his Halberd. The beast is wounded, but the weapon is stuck in the creature’s body. 
Valphine sees Soma prepare to cast Ice Knife and makes sure she is out of range of the clumsy druid’s spell. 
The mimic advances on Brewbarry, attacks with a pseudo that misses the barbarian entirely. 
The bard unleashes invectives, and at first, Valphine is confused but sees the underlying enchantment that causes the mimic to flinch visibly. 
She smiles and repeats a rhyme she remembers from her youth, “Sticks and stones may break my bones, and words can also hurt me.”
