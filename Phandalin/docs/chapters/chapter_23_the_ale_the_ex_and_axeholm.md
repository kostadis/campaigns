# Chapter 23 The Ale, the Ex, and Axeholm
The party returned to Phandalin after successfully negotiating a peace agreement at the gold mine. Harbin Wester, the Lord Mayor, greeted them warmly and expressed satisfaction with the recent changes in the community. However, his mood turned serious when Brewbarry inquired about the upcoming ale supply. Harbin warned that the dragon occupying the mountains and the resulting orc incursions were preventing farmers from tending their crops, threatening the town's precious ale production for the season.

Harbin presented the party with several new quests, including investigating Dragon Barrow, securing Axeholm fortress, and exploring the Woodland Manse. These missions, he explained, were funded by Neverwinter's tax system and offered rewards that could help Vukradin establish his music studio. When Vukradin questioned the safety of transporting gold from Neverwinter, Harbin revealed the city's troubled history—decades of catastrophes, corruption, and crime that Lord Neverember was attempting to remedy through progressive reforms. Vukradin realized with some embarrassment that he had been so devoted to his musical studies that he had completely missed these major events unfolding around him.

The party then turned their attention to ensuring a steady cheese supply for the wererats at the gold mine. They sought out Petunia, Big Al's prized cow, and through magical communication learned that she missed the alfalfa fields of her former home at Butterskull Ranch. Big Al mentioned that Qelline Alderleaf, a local farmer, had offered to let Petunia graze on her land for one gold piece per week, though he lacked the funds to pay.

When the party approached Qelline to negotiate, they immediately sensed tension. She recognized that Big Al had sent them instead of coming himself and revealed her deep resentment toward him. During his prosperous days at Butterskull Ranch, Big Al's competitive pricing had severely impacted her farm's income. The one gold piece price, she admitted, was deliberately high—a "hurt price" meant to make Big Al suffer financially. After some discussion, she offered to reduce the fee to five silver pieces per week, but only if Big Al came to apologize to her personally.

Returning to Big Al with this condition, the party pressed him about the nature of his relationship with Qelline. Under their questioning, the large man broke down in tears and confessed the painful truth. He and Qelline had once been engaged to be married, but Big Al had abandoned her the night before their wedding. Though he had always loved her, he hadn't been ready to settle down at the time. The party insisted he tell Qelline the full truth, and Big Al, still emotional, picked up Petunia and headed to her farm to make amends. The party wisely chose not to interfere in their private reconciliation.

Later, Lyra appeared in town, reunited with the two Emerald Enclave members the party had rescued. Despite this success, she was deeply discouraged about her leadership abilities, citing the treant incident and her men's capture as evidence of her failures. Vukradin offered her encouragement, and one of the rescued men revealed that the party had claimed it was Lyra's plan that led to their rescue. Heartened by this support, Lyra agreed to let Vukradin help convince the Emerald Enclave of her competence, though they would need to wait until the next day when the organization was meeting.

Ser Kaelen approached the party with important information about the statue they had recovered from the lighthouse. His contacts had identified it as belonging to Elara Seasong Meliame, a wealthy art collector currently attending an extravagant gala in Neverwinter hosted by her eccentric cousin, Lord Cassian Meliame. However, he warned that agents of House Maragaster—a family notorious for demonic alliances—intended to intercept the statue to embarrass the Meliames. Ser Kaelen requested that the party escort the statue to Neverwinter and present it to Elara personally, making sure to mention the Order of the Gauntlet's assistance in hopes of securing future donations. Vukradin, ever the opportunist, considered thanking multiple organizations to maximize potential favors.

That evening, the party performed at the local tavern. While Valphine struggled with her performance, Vukradin's music energized the crowd, and Brewbarry created an entirely new spectacle with impressive physical displays and dance moves that the audience began imitating. Spider-Man, the tavern owner, rewarded them with free drinks and the crowd showered them with applause and coin. After a night of rest, the party prepared for their next adventure.

The following day, they embarked on a journey to Axeholm, traveling across grassy lowlands that skirted the rocky foothills of the Sword Mountains. Cool winds and rain from the Sword Coast made the trek unpleasant, but they pressed on. Upon arrival, they found the entrance to the ancient dwarven fortress—a dark passageway carved into a gray mountain, flanked by towering bulwarks of seamless stone. A rusted iron portcullis blocked their way, and the arrow slits carved into the walls showed no signs of occupation.

Brewbarry attempted to force the portcullis open with an incredible display of strength, but even his mighty effort couldn't overcome the massive stone block jamming the mechanism. The party realized they would need an alternative approach. Soma summoned a rat familiar to scout through the narrow arrow slits, establishing a telepathic link to explore what lay beyond. As the tiny creature prepared to venture into the darkness of Axeholm, the party stood ready to discover what secrets the abandoned fortress held.
