# Chapter 10 The stag, the brambles, the wolves, and the pool. 
### Soma

There are no signs of rotting trees or obvious blight in the Whispering Wood. And yet, I can feel something is different about this place. 
Suddenly, a majestic stag materializes before us. Its fur shimmers and flickers. This creature is caught between here and some other place. Its antlers are constantly moving and changing shape. And then it teleports 15 feet away. 
Is this the unnatural blight?

"Do you want to talk to this stag?" Vukradin asks, ever the practical one. "I do," I reply, casting Speak with Animals. Approaching the shimmering form, my first question is simple: "Are you okay?".
The stag pauses, then replies with a surprising territoriality. "I was okay... Until you walked into my territory. Why are you in my territory? Get out of my territory," it insists. I try to calm it, explaining, "We're just passing through, we mean no harm, we just... we notice that you seem... Out of sorts". 

I look around and can see the boundary of the stag’s territory. There is a broken branch here, a dropping there, and nibble over there. 

Valphine quickly moves out of the territory, but Brewbarry and the shell sprout don’t move. 

"Brewbarry, can you back off a little?" I ask, then turn to Vukradin, "Could you back up a little? Just give the stag some privacy". Once they've retreated, I continue, "We've cleared your territory. Is there something weird going on, though? I'm not used to seeing stags teleport around and shimmer like you do".
The stag seems to consider this, then teleports again, leaving a brief afterimage that quickly fades. When it reappears, it confirms my suspicions: "Yes, things have not been normal for some time". It explains how it "used just to be able to walk and prance and eat the vegetables," but now it can "blink" and its antlers are "Different" every time it looks at them. "The whole forest has gotten weird. It's almost as if everything has changed.”

The stag mutters that it’s constantly changing antlers make it hard to avoid branches and brambles. 
Then, with an abrupt change of subject, the stag asks if we have "anything to eat?". We offer some of the mushrooms we collected from Gnomengarde. Satisfied, it provides a crucial piece of information: "it all started in the center of the forest". It mentions "other nice people" who also "spoke to me, too" and said they would "fix it, but they haven't".
With this new lead, we decide to head towards the center of the forest. As I lead the group, I keep thinking about the stag. Something unnatural festers here.

### Valphine

My drow senses, accustomed to the subtle currents of intrigue and the raw, often terrifying, distortions of the Underdark, hummed with an uncomfortable awareness. Soma seemed unconcerned. I felt at home. 

Ahead, I noticed a dense thicket of brambles with leaves pulsing with unnatural colors, sometimes glowing, withering to black. This is not a disease. Soma, the naturalist, calls it "Planar leakage, bleeding."

How droll. I thought the Underdark and its mad mages were without peer in their distortion of reality. But this was new. And powerful. And it could hurt. How delightful. 

Soma, always eager to understand the natural world, approaches the blighted growth within ten feet. Foolish. The brambles, as if sentient, lash out. Thorny tendrils strike her, dealing piercing and necrotic damage. I observe her wince, a faint, fleeting flicker of pain. How wonderful. 

The Thicket, this source of pain, must be dealt with. I call upon the gloriously painful light of Lathander, my patron deity, channeling a Sacred Flame. My divine fire, meant to obliterate darkness and inflict pain, merely glances off. 
Brewbarry, always direct, moves within ten feet, and the thorny tendrils immediately attempt to strike him. They miss. His rage ignites, a visible aura of fury, and he attacks with his halberd, tearing the branches apart—such beautiful and efficient violence. 
Soma transforms into a bear and strikes the bushes. Vukradin follows with a biting commentary. I notice how the thicket appears to regenerate.

And then the bramble stops moving. Brewbarry and the Bear have rendered it inert. We move through it before it can reanimate. 
### 
Vukradin 

We faced some wolves. They had an unnatural howl that rooted me to the spot. And their eyes glowed. Others may say it is fear. I say it was magic. The creatures were quickly defeated, and then we took their pelts. 

I was very pleased to seize their pelts. Because a dire wolf pelt is, by definition, conflict-free gold, and more importantly, the unnatural magic of this place may make them more valuable to the discerning collector. 

After skinning the creatures, we continue deeper into the Whispering Wood, a place that makes the hairs on my arms prickle. It's more than just a strange energy; it's a disharmony, a discordant note in the natural world that offends my artistic sensibilities. 

The fear of the wolves, a magical fear, prompted me to reflect on my own personal fears. Adabra said she thought I only did it for the music. It was the music in its purest essence that motivated me. But was it? Was I just afraid to admit that it was about the fans? I saw the fans at Stonehill Tavern so happy, and I realized it is about the fans. I won’t be afraid of doing it for the fans. And if it is about the fans, and not my music, should I just stop trying to make them love what I love and instead do what they love? 

And then we stumble into a clearing, and there in front of us is a pool of water. 
I look carefully and realize that this is something different. The surface reflects a distorted version of ourselves. Valphine asks, “Will I see myself happy and content?” 
I look at her more carefully. She looks at the pool, but avoids staring at it. It’s almost as if she is afraid of being happy. As I was scared of admitting that I liked to play for the fans, I thus became consumed by my desire to focus solely on the music. And in my pursuit of music, I walked away from what truly made me happy. Was I sabotaging my music? Trying to make it less likable to the fans? 

This pond may help me see more clearly. So I look in. 

And there I see a version of myself clutching coins constantly. Is this the price of pursuing art or the price of pleasing my fans? Is this a version of me trying to convey a message to me? 

I must know more. This is a planar scar, a window into fractured realities. I jump in. 
The moment I step in, a dizzying cacophony assails my mind. It's "people, scenes, faces looking at me all at the same time”. A barrage of fragmented visions, whispers, and fleeting emotions. This is knowledge, raw and unadulterated, but it's overwhelming. I try to hold onto my composure, but I cannot. 
A chill seeps into my very being, a creeping sense of unease that twists my perception. Then, through the swirling chaos, I see a distorted creature pleading, saying, "He saw beyond, but only what he was meant to see. The numbers. Always the numbers.”  The numbers. My mind latches onto it. Is it the counting of "blood gold", the endless tally of "stolen merchandise"? The cold, hard accounting of a world driven by plunder rather than art? It's a terrifying vision, affirming my convictions about "conflict-free gold" and the "sordid morality" of this world.
The intensity is too much. I need to escape this place. I stumble out of the pool, feeling an immense drain on my energy, and then, without warning, I fall unconscious. The world simply... ceases to be.
The next thing I know, a sharp, stinging sensation on my cheek brings me back to the jarring reality. Someone has revived me with a slap. I blink, trying to clear the lingering haze of madness.
Soma, ever the voice of pragmatism, but sometimes, in her "matronly" way, warns us not to enter the pool, for our "own sanity”. But Brewbarry, bless his loyal but bumbling heart, decides to look into the pool anyway. He mentions something about Uthgardt legends of such pools being "Shadows to other places" that "appear unnaturally" and offer knowledge.

Once again, Brewbarry demonstrates a curious intellect and awareness of the world that his background and superficial intellect hide. Perhaps it’s that deeper connection that allows him to appreciate my music. 

He asks the pool, "What side of the bread should I butter?". A question so utterly Brewbarry. What he sees is a version of himself that says, "butter both sides simultaneously." The sheer absurdity of it almost makes me laugh.
Suddenly, Brewbarry is terrified and starts running away. He's screaming about "both sides". Soma transforms into her bear form and subdues him. 

Long after the hysteria is over, he mutters, “Both sides!” 

Valphine wants to ask "more intelligent questions of the pool.”  

Soma, in her motherly tone,  advises against it, for our "own sanity!" 

Why isn’t Soma more worried? This pool, and the distortions of this forest, should bother her. The compulsive needs of druids to make things natural should have provoked something in her. Instead, she seems curiously only concerned with us and uninterested in the world around her. 
I shake my head. 

The image of me counting money, and the distorted visions in the pool, tell me we have to move on. This place is too dangerous for abstract contemplation or the pursuit of forbidden knowledge. We have a mission, and this pool, while fascinating, is a distraction that threatens to unravel our minds. 

As we walk closer to the center, the whisper about numbers keeps poking at my mind like a splinter in my eye.
