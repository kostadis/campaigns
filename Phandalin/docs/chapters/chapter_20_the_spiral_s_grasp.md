# Chapter 20 The Spiral’s Grasp
The party gathered at the Loggers Camp to discuss their next move, with Vukradin learning that he had inadvertently built a reputation as a noble adventurer after saving a town from a dragon and advocating for fair wages. Despite his protests that he simply wanted to open his own studio, the public perception had taken hold. The group recalled information about a missing Emerald Enclave patrol and devised a plan to rescue them, crediting the success to Lyra, a low-ranking member they had previously saved, in order to boost her standing within the faction and gain a valuable ally. After debating what to do with their prisoner Shrimpy, who desperately wanted to be released, they decided to transport him to Phandalin for imprisonment, as Tibor the loggers chief refused to take him to Neverwinter.
Following a long rest, the party consulted Joe’s notebook for directions to the Emerald Enclave meeting point and set off through the wilderness. Brewbarry consumed some fermentable mushrooms along the way, entering an altered state of consciousness. They successfully navigated the terrain and arrived at a riverbank where an unnatural, dense fog covered the eastern shore. Standing rigidly by the water was a Teega the Smith with glassy, unfocused eyes who spoke in an eerie monotone about metal bending, minds being cleansed, and waves seeking uniformity. When questioned about the missing patrol, she cryptically mentioned they were trying to fix a leak with a wooden plug and that the current had found them, reforming their brittle thoughts. She drew a mysterious spiral symbol in the mud before her eyes briefly cleared when Vukradin persuaded her, only to glaze over again as she wandered into a nearby glade.
The party crossed the river and immediately felt a strange tingling in their minds, with sounds echoing oddly and light flickering through the shadows in a way that reminded them of a planar anomaly they had encountered before. In the glade, they discovered a grim-looking man wielding a black coral dagger, accompanied by three figures whose movements were unnaturally precise and robotic. The party recognized these individuals as the missing Emerald Enclave patrol, now clearly under some form of mind control. The man, a Cult Fanatic, coldly informed them that they were interfering with necessary adjustments and that the zealots now served a greater purpose. He spoke of a rising current flowing toward Neverwinter and offered the party a chance to submit, but they refused.
Combat erupted as the Cult Fanatic attempted to control Brewbarry’s mind, compelling him to flee, while his mind-controlled warriors attacked with devastating precision. Soma transformed into a massive bear and charged the cult leader, while Valphine channeled healing energy to keep her companions standing. Vukradin raged and attempted to strike down the fanatic but struggled to land a blow. The battle was fierce, with the warriors dealing heavy damage to the party, but eventually Brewbarry broke free from the mental compulsion and delivered a crushing non-lethal strike that knocked the Cult Fanatic unconscious. The moment their leader fell, the mind-controlled warriors collapsed to the ground, screaming and clutching their heads in agony.
The party quickly secured their new prisoners, tying up the unconscious Cult Fanatic and his warriors, who wore green cloaks with gold badges bearing the same spiral symbol. When they attempted to wake the cult leader, they discovered he could still exert control over his minions even while unconscious, causing them to move robotically toward him. Soma pursued the possessed Teega the Smith who had wandered off, tracking her through the wilderness in bear form before subduing her and bringing her back to the group. The party experimented with separating the prisoners and attempting restorative magic, but nothing seemed to break the mind control as long as the Cult Fanatic lived.
Realizing they needed to transport six prisoners back to Phandalin, the party debated their route and decided to keep the Cult Fanatic sedated for the journey, with Brewbarry tasked with maintaining the sedation. They attempted to interrogate the cult leader when he briefly regained consciousness, but he only muttered vague warnings about tides rising and waves coming, refusing to betray his cause. The party searched the prisoners and found they were affiliated with the Kraken Society, based on the spiral emblems they wore.
On the second day of their journey, a large raiding party of Talosian orcs ambushed them, demanding the Cult Fanatic and identifying him as their enemy. The party, noticing the orcs’ wariness of their strength and the presence of a drow among them, negotiated carefully. The orcs explained that the cult leader must be killed, and after tense discussion, the party agreed to hand him over in exchange for safe passage. The Cult Fanatic, given one final chance to explain himself, spoke only of petty squabbles that meant nothing to what was coming. Valphine executed him swiftly, and the orcs took his head as proof of their victory. An anchorite half-orc, a cleric of Talos, identified himself and declared the deceased cultist as one more obstacle crushed by Talos before warning the party to leave their forest.
With the Cult Fanatic dead, the mind-controlled Emerald Enclave members finally awoke, though their minds were still reeling from the experience. One of them, a member of Lyra’s old crew, expressed relief that she had sent the party to rescue them. The warriors described the horrifying sensation of having their thoughts invaded, feeling orders that weren’t their own dripping like poisoned water into their skulls. They revealed they had been forced to steal an inscription that spoke of a drowned gate beneath Yllyn, somewhere deep in the Underdark. The party realized this was connected to the Kraken Society and represented a significant threat.
As the rescued patrol members recovered, Brewbarry, having drunk all of his ale, wondered where he could get more booze. Realizing they were only five miles from the Falcon’s Lodge, he insisted they return to fetch more alcohol. Despite some reservations about the lodge’s atmosphere, the party decided to make the short detour for rest and wine. The rescued Enclave members would need time to recover fully, and the party would need to decide how to present their success to Lyra and the Emerald Enclave, while also investigating the mysterious drowned gate and the rising threat of the Kraken Society’s influence in the region.
