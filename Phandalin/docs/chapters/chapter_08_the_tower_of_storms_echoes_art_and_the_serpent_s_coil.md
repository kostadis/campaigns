# Chapter 07 The Tower of Storms: Echoes, Art, and the Serpent's Coil

### Vukradin's Perspective

The wind whips around us as we approach the Tower of Storms, a grim sentinel on its rocky perch. The air immediately fills with discord: three harpies descend, their songs a cacophony that grates on my ears. Before I can process their screeching, one of them, a male harpy dressed in grey rags, begins to sing, and I see Valphine and Brewbarry succumb, a vacant look in their eyes as they become charmed.

This will not do. I act swiftly, casting Silence, a 20-foot radius sphere of pure, blessed quiet. Immediately, the charming melodies are nullified, and the charm loosens its grip on my allies. The harpy in the grey rags, no longer able to weave its magic, stops its performance, though its meager Intelligence suggests it will try again if given the chance. The battle is on, and at least now, we fight on even terms.

Later, as a female attempts to sing, I find myself charmed. Annoying\! But I have an idea. I use my Bardic Inspiration, turning my failure into a success. It feels like failing upward, a perfectly Vukradin maneuver. I then strike the female Harpy with a spell, breaking its concentration and ending its song. This is how a true artist fights—with wit and timely intervention.

I engage the male harpy with the grey rags directly with my daggers, landing one hit but missing a second. It's a frustrating, slow grind. But then, with a flourish, I deliver the final blow to that harpy using Vicious Mockery, quipping, "He is mocked to death. To death." The silence is now truly complete.

We take a much-needed short rest. Brewbarry retrieves his thrown handaxes, which lay "a few feet away", a testament to his—ahem—enthusiastic aim. I reflect on the harpies' "music" during the battle. "Their music is just… They're not joining the Academy, that's for sure". Conflict-free gold is one thing, but listening to that? Unacceptable.

Our exploration leads us to a 15-foot-high empty room. Through a dirty window, I can see shipwrecks below. These possessions, abandoned to the depths, cry out for their rightful owners. I declare my intent to repatriate these items, or at least deliver them to "errors\! Successors\!". Soma dismisses my principles as belonging to a "colonial paradise" or "mercantile" system. She simply doesn't understand the finer points of moral acquisition.

Deeper within the tower, a battle against Moesko, the Orc Anchorite, begins. After Soma transforms into a brown bear and lands some hits, I seize the opportunity to cast Cloud of Daggers, conjuring whirling blades that trap Moesko within a 5-foot cube, inflicting 9 initial damage. He'll take more damage if he stays there. This combat is certainly more dramatic than the harpies, an actual test of our combined talents, if not our sanity, as Moesko responds with a devastating Lightning Bolt and Thunderwave.

### Soma's Perspective

The harpies are a nuisance, their songs a crude manipulation of emotions. As Vukradin's Silence spell takes effect, I see the charm break on Valphine and Brewbarry. This is effective, but these creatures are not bright, so they'll likely continue trying if we leave the sphere of silence.

The combat is a "long… slow battle". I decide against my usual bear form for this tight space. Instead, I transform into a wolf spider, allowing me to scale the tower walls easily. I crawl towards the female harpy, attacking and dealing much damage. The harpies have surprising resilience, but my venomous fangs find their mark. Meanwhile, Brewbarry swings his handaxes, but he struggles to land a blow, missing multiple times. He's a brute, but perhaps needs some lessons in precision.

Later in the fight, another female harpy in blue rags attacks me, causing some damage. I retaliate, striking it for much more, helping to wear it down. When another harpy female starts singing, I must resist, and I do. "Soma's heard better songs". This constant singing is tiresome.

After the harpies are dealt with and Vukradin prattles about "repatriating" shipwreck salvage, I note his mindset: it's either a "colonial paradise" or "mercantile" system. It's a stark contrast to my own naturalist views.

We move through a room with frescoes depicting stormy seas and a "dark and terrible god", then find a stone altar with lightning bolts carved into it and a metal lightning rod. On a nearby ledge, we discover a harpy nest containing a potion of water breathing. Vukradin questions taking "blood money," but it's simply a resource found.

Upstairs, we find the Orc Anchorite, Moesko, seated in a barnacle-encrusted chair, clad in armor made from giant octopus hide, holding an opalescent conch – the very artifact belonging to Miral the Self.

The battle begins. I open with a minor poison spray attack, then, sensing the need for more power, I transform into a brown bear. In this form, I utilize my multi-attack, delivering significant damage to Moesko. He's tougher than he looks, responding with a potent Lightning Bolt that deals staggering amounts of damage (reduced to by half for most of us, thank Mielikki, as everyone but Brewbarry is struck). Then he casts Thunderwave, dealing more damage and pushes me back.

Brewbarry, finally finding his footing, charges Moesko and hits him for some damage, bringing the anchorite down to death’s door. The tight corridor of the tower makes my bear form cumbersome, so I revert. I deliver the final strike, ending the Orc Anchorite's threat.

### Valphine's Perspective

Upon arriving at the Tower of Storms, the air is thick with the cloying, clashing melodies of three harpies. My instincts, honed in the subtle cruelties of the Underdark, scream danger. Immediately, I am charmed by the male harpie’s song, a disorienting, unpleasant sensation. Thankfully, Vukradin's Silence spell descends, cutting off the offending music and freeing me from its influence.

The combat is a protracted, "long… slow battle". I aim my crossbow at the male, critically wounding it for marginal damage. Later, the creature attacks me, then disengages, but I miss my attack of opportunity. I am more successful against the female harpy, hitting it once and then another time to finally kill it. My crossbow is more reliable than Brewbarry's flailing handaxes, which seem to miss consistently. 

After the harpies are dispatched, and Vukradin ponders the "repatriation" of sunken treasure, I notice the irony. These shipwrecks are a result of the tower's curse. The concept of "colonial paradise," as Soma calls it, resonates with my drow perspective—power dictates ownership. 

While searching through the tower, we find a harpy nest containing a potion of water breathing. Vukradin debates taking "blood money," but to me, it's merely the spoils of the fallen. Why would you leave valuable items behind?

We proceed upstairs and discover the Orc Anchorite, Moesko. He sits in a barnacle-encrusted chair, clad in armor made from giant octopus hide, and in his lap rests an opalescent conch, an artifact I recognize as belonging to the undead creature, Miral the Sea Elf. This is the prize.

The battle is fierce. Soma, in her impressive brown bear form, lands significant blows, and Vukradin cleverly traps Moesko in a Cloud of Daggers. But Moesko retaliates with a Lightning Bolt that scorches most of us, reducing my hit points and leaving me battered. It's a sharp, glorious pain, a reminder of Lathander's raw power.

Brewbarry, to my surprise, manages to charge Moesko and lands a hit for some damage, bringing the anchorite to a single hit point. Finally, Soma, shedding her large bear form to navigate the cramped space, delivers the final, decisive strike, bringing the Orc Anchorite down. The conch is ours. The pain of battle has been invigorating, and the reward, tangible.
