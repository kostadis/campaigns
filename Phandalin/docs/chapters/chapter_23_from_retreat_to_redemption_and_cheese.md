# Chapter 22 From Retreat to Redemption (and Cheese)

## 

The party found themselves in a desperate situation at the Woodland Manse, battered from the first attack, a shapeshifting cultist of Talos unleashed devastating lightning upon them. Valphine called upon her divine powers to heal her companions, restoring their strength just as another Anchorite, disguised as a wild bull, struck Soma and Vukradin with crackling bolts of electricity. As more cultists emerged from within the house and the surrounding area, the party realized they were dangerously outnumbered.

Faced with overwhelming odds and the prospect of more lightning-wielding enemies, the adventurers made the tactical decision to retreat. Vukradin, ever mindful of his potential share in a gold mine, strongly advocated for withdrawal rather than a glorious but pointless death. Brewbarry, swift in his giant form, carried Vukradin to safety while Soma grabbed Valphine and put her on her back, and the party successfully disengaged from the cultists. As they fled, the talking boars they had befriended earlier expressed disappointment that the strangers hadn’t dealt with the impostors who were violating proper boar culture and etiquette.

As the party reached the clearing where Corbin and Shrimpy were waiting, a little bit down the path to the Woodland Manse, they overheard Shrimpy whining. He was trying to argue that the party must be dead, and that Corbin should let him go. The sound of lightning and burning flesh was undeniable. Brewbarry and Valphine were displeased and showed it in their own special way. The party picked up the Emerald Enclave fighters from the Falcon Hunter and then went south to the edge of the forest just a few miles west of Conyberry. From there, they traveled west along the Triboar Trail toward Phandalin, covering nearly thirty miles before stopping to rest and recover from their harrowing encounter. After a much-needed rest to restore their strength and magical abilities, they continued their journey to the small trading post.

Upon arriving in Phandalin, Vukradin called a council meeting to report their findings. They delivered Shrimpy to Town Master Harbin Wester and informed the council about the Cult of Talos at the Woodland Manse, describing the shapeshifting orcs who wielded powerful lightning magic. When the party inquired about the Cult of the Kraken, most council members were unfamiliar with it, though Ser Kaelen of the Order of the Gauntlet expressed concern about the organization and mentioned suspicions of powerful psionic creatures being involved. Jenna Roscoe of the Lords’ Alliance dismissed the Kraken Society as merely a harmless social club for wealthy elites, though Valphine sensed she knew more than she was revealing.

The meeting took a dramatic turn when Adabra learned of the party’s intervention against the Kraken cult. She became furiously enraged, identifying what they had witnessed as a temporal violation and planar distortion. In her view, the party should never have interfered, as such interventions only triggered cascading catastrophic events across the multiverse. She pointed to the Emerald Enclave members’ involvement in the cult as proof that interventionists were fundamentally wrong, then stormed out of the meeting in righteous fury, leaving the council divided. Brewbarry was confused, but Soma was done with her. And soon the party decided that Adabra’s extreme positions were wrong. And that they realized that this was not the last time they would have to deal with her.

Ser Kaelen provided some welcome news, revealing that research at Candlekeep had identified the original owners of one of the magical items the party had previously found on ships. He emphasized that this effort had cost the Order of the Gauntlet considerable time and money, hoping to strengthen their relationship with Vukradin. Though he promised rewards for returning the items, he made it clear the process wouldn’t be straightforward.

Valphine returned to the partially reconstructed church and delivered a powerful sermon that led the congregation into religious ecstasy. However, the worshippers misinterpreted her message about experiencing the glorious pain of Lathander’s light, believing they must stare directly at the sun until blinded to prove their worthiness. When Valphine realized she could cure the blindness with her divine magic, an unintended new sacrament was born—congregants would intentionally blind themselves and then be healed as proof of their devotion. She also placed the severed head of a Kraken Society cultist on a pike outside the church as a warning. Brin and Giles, the two workers helping reconstruct the church, were ecstatic about these developments. At the same time, the Teega the Smith, and Kraken Society member, observing the proceedings, appeared both disturbed and strangely conflicted by the offer of experiencing pain and redemption.

The party then encountered Don-Jon Raskin, a verbose and boastful man who regaled the tavern with tall tales of his past as a gold prospector, miner, fur trader, privateer, and whaler. He shared a long story about attending a barbarian tribe’s party, proudly believing he had been accepted as a member, though the party quickly realized he had been seen as an idiot. Don-Jon revealed he was the new overseer for the Mountain’s Toe Gold Mine, hired by a consortium in Neverwinter—a consortium that Vukradin noticed included at least one person associated with the Kraken Society. Despite his bravado, Don-Jon admitted he knew nothing about the mine itself but had been required by his employers to hire an escort for the journey.

As the party escorted Don-Jon toward the mine, they discovered six dead orcs scattered across the rocky ground within five miles of their destination. Examination revealed the orcs had been killed by extreme cold rather than violence, clear evidence of the white dragon’s presence in the area. The orcs were not Talosians, which considerably boosted Don-Jon’s confidence. When they finally arrived at the mine entrance, marked by a wooden plank carved with its name, they were confronted by two wererats in hybrid form who claimed ownership of the mine.

Don-Jon’s friendly demeanor helped defuse initial tensions, especially after the wererats reacted with hostility toward Valphine for being a drow. The wererats invited the party to meet their leadership deeper in the mine, guaranteeing their safety. There they met Zeleen Varnaster, leader of the Whiskered Gang, who explained that her group had been displaced from their previous home at the Shrine of Savras by approximately sixteen orcs and several ogres—some from a tribe the party had previously angered.

Zeleen proposed that if the party cleared the orcs from the shrine, the wererats would return there and vacate the mine. However, she refused to help reclaim her own home, claiming she was too valuable to risk and offering only two warriors if pressed. When Valphine attempted to negotiate a cheese-for-labor arrangement, Zeleen found the suggestion stereotypical and offensive, though she eventually admitted interest in cheese from the special cow at Butterskull Ranch. Valphine sensed the wererats were lying about their intentions and would likely try to keep the mine regardless of any deal.

Don-Jon initially insisted the mine could not serve as both a living space and a working mine for safety reasons, but Vukradin’s persuasive arguments convinced him that the wererats could nest in one section while working another. The party struck a deal: the wererats would work the mine and live in part of it in exchange for regular cheese shipments from Butterskull Ranch. This arrangement secured the mine’s operation without requiring a dangerous confrontation with sixteen orcs and several ogres, leaving those enemies undisturbed at the Shrine of Savras for another day. The party had successfully turned a potential disaster into a profitable arrangement, though the long-term consequences of their creative solution remained to be seen.
