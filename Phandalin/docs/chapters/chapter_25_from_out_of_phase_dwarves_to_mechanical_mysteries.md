# Chapter 25 From Out-of-Phase Dwarves to Mechanical Mysteries
The battle in the mustering hall of Axeholm raged on as the party fought against the twisted dwarves caught in an interdimensional nightmare. Valphine fired her crossbow at one of the out-of-phase creatures, bringing it to the brink of death. Soma, still in her powerful bear form, found herself paralyzed by a dwarf's unnatural touch, but her constitution proved strong enough to shake off the effect. Together, the party systematically eliminated the remaining dwarves, with Brewbarry delivering crushing blows and Vukradin supporting his allies with inspiring words.
After the combat concluded, Valphine took time to tend to the party's wounds, channeling healing energy to restore their strength. The group then began exploring the strange fortress, noting how the spatial dimensions seemed to shift unnaturally, with objects appearing closer than they should at certain distances. Blinking lights flickered on the upper level, and a pervasive stench of death hung in the air. They discovered a narrow tunnel leading upward, just large enough for a humanoid to squeeze through.
Valphine led the party through the cramped passage to an upper level, where they found a ransacked room filled with broken furniture and debris. A blackened fireplace dominated one wall, its chimney rising to the outside world. Investigating the chimney, they discovered it led to the mountain face where they had originally entered Axeholm, though the view offered nothing of particular interest. The party decided to return downstairs and continue their exploration.
Their search brought them to what had once been a dwarven shrine, its walls carved with images of dwarves performing sacred rites. The eastern section had collapsed, leaving rubble strewn across the floor along with the skeletal remains of several dwarves. Among the debris, they discovered a platinum amulet bearing the symbol of Moradin, the dwarven god. Valphine carefully inspected an unlocked steel coffer, finding it packed with silt that protected a valuable potion of superior healing. The party debated whether these treasures should be surrendered to Townmaster Wester or held by the Privy Council for the benefit of Phandalin.
Continuing their methodical exploration, the party encountered a locked door. Valphine skillfully picked the lock using her thieves' tools, only to find the room completely empty. The discovery puzzled them—why would someone clean out a storage room and then lock it? The mystery lingered as they pressed onward through the abandoned fortress.
The party's exploration led them to a grand pillared hall, where Valphine stealthily peered inside and spotted two more twisted dwarves. The air in the chamber felt wrong, filled with a low, tooth-rattling hum like a massive heart beating deep within the stone. Dust on the floor danced in unnatural geometric patterns. Soma struck first, her bear form surprising one of the dwarves and mauling it to death instantly. The party quickly dispatched the second dwarf, but their victory was short-lived.
From behind a limestone throne flanked by granite statues of dwarf warriors, a horrifying figure emerged—what had once been the Dwarven King, now twisted into a three-headed monstrosity. Another corrupted dwarf appeared alongside him. The air rippled like heat haze, and the creatures screeched in pain before recovering. The King moved with terrifying speed, his claws finding Soma and paralyzing her. His three mouths descended in a flurry of bites, and Soma collapsed unconscious, reverting from her bear form.
Valphine channeled divine energy, turning one of the lesser dwarves who fled up the fireplace in terror. Brewbarry flew into a rage, his enlarged form squeezing through the doorway as he engaged the King. Vukradin attempted to assault the King's mind with an illusion of Moradin himself, but the creature's will proved too strong. The King advanced on Brewbarry, suffering grievous wounds from the barbarian's defensive strikes but pressing forward nonetheless. His attacks found their mark, though Brewbarry's rage allowed him to shrug off much of the damage.
Valphine rushed to Soma's side, channeling healing magic to revive her fallen companion. Vukradin tried his mental assault again, this time successfully conjuring the image of a giant bear in the King's mind. The illusion dealt psychic damage and distracted the twisted monarch. In his confusion, the King turned on Valphine, but fortune smiled upon her—every claw swipe and bite attack missed completely. Soma rejoined the fray, and together the party wore down the three-headed horror until it finally collapsed, defeated.
As the King fell, the party felt a surge of power—they had grown stronger from their harrowing experiences. On the King's body, they found a signet ring bearing the Axeholm crest, depicting a hand clutched in a dwarven gauntlet. Valphine recognized it not only as a valuable artifact but also as a key of some kind.
The party continued exploring, discovering a grand dining room with a massive stone table and iron chandeliers whose candles had long since melted away. An alcove contained a deep pit descending sixty feet—a trash chute where the fortress's inhabitants had once disposed of refuse. Valphine identified its purpose, and the party wisely decided not to investigate its depths.
In the adjacent kitchen, they found typical dwarven implements—metal dishware, utensils, and drinking mugs. Most remarkably, a stone sink still functioned, its faucet spouting hot water. Brewbarry examined the water carefully and determined it came from a natural hot spring, a testament to the dwarves' engineering prowess.
Finally, the party entered the smithy, where rusty anvils stood before blackened hearths. A fluttering noise drew their attention to one of the fireplaces, and Valphine investigated. A strange creature emerged—a tiny, mechanical mosquito-like construct with a steampunk aesthetic, complete with a small glass jar containing blood. Valphine carefully subdued the creature without killing it. Vukradin examined the captured construct, his aesthetic sensibilities offended by its unnatural design. He noted markings indicating it was some kind of sampling device, raising troubling questions about who had created it and for what purpose. With this mysterious discovery in hand, the party prepared to delve deeper into the secrets of Axeholm.
