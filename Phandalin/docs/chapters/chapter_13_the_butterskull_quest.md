# Chapter 13 The Butterskull Quest
## 1-02 Taraskh 1495

### Valphine 
The night before, we discussed our plans in private. We had no intention of being ambushed again. 

We decided to go to Butterskull Ranch. Harbin had posted this quest on his quest board, “Orcs have attacked Butterskull Ranch, five miles east of Conyberry along the Triboar Trail! Travel there with haste, assess the damage, and help any way you can. Ranch owner Alfonse, also known as Big Al Kalazorn, is a retired sheriff who can reward you for your efforts. If he’s dead, return to Townmaster Harbin Wester with proof of Kalazorn’s demise to receive a reward of 100 gp.”

The money was good. And if I read the quest correctly, we could go there, discover the Ranch Owner was dead, do nothing more, and collect 100 GP for our troubles. 

And so we leave Phandalin and head east on the Triboar Trail. 

Along the way, we encounter a merchant. The merchant is friendly and annoyed. We ask him where he is coming from and he says the town of Coneyberry. 

“The place was ransacked and a wreck. Nothing was there. Lord Neverember promised to fix that.” 

The story of ransacked buildings excites Vukradin who sees yet another opportunity to go on a quest, “We must go there and quickly secure the property.” 

The merchant tries hard not to laugh, “The place was destroyed years ago, and the town is utterly looted. There is nothing to secure.” 

“We have to go find out whose property was stolen and then find the thieves and then return it to the next of kin!” 

The merchant stares bug-eyed and mutters, “That’s very hard.” 

And Vukradin, with a theatrical flourish, declares, “Just because something is hard, doesn’t mean we don’t do it!” 

## 4-02 Taraskh 1495 

As I enter Conyberry, I notice an unsettling silence that hangs in the air, broken only by the whisper of the wind through the hollowed-out homes.  The homes are now little more than skeletal remains. Their roofs have long since collapsed inward, their wooden frames rotted and blackened by fire and time. The wattle-and-daub walls have crumbled to dust, and the streets are overgrown with thorny vines and tenacious weeds. 

And yet, this devastation is nothing compared to what would happen to a noble house that was marked for extinction by the Noble Houses of Menzoberanzan. 

We turn the corner and notice four brigands. Our aide, Corbin, explains that they are members of Carver’s gang. 

Vukradin encourages him to talk to his former gang mates. 
Corbin tries to explain the folly of such an action: “You don’t exactly quit a gang without there being consequences.” 

But Vukradin will have none of that and insists he go.
