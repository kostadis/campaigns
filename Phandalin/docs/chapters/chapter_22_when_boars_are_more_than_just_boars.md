# Chapter 21 When Boars Are More Than Just Boars

The party arrived at the Falcon Hunting Lodge with their collection of prisoners and rescued companions in tow. Among them was a disoriented Teega the Smith, two Emerald Enclave guards who had been freed from captivity, and a criminal named Shrimpy. Falcon greeted them with surprise, particularly pleased to see his friend Vukradin had returned safely. The party presented their prisoners, and Falcon was impressed by their success in the field.

Teega the Smith, still confused and suffering from memory gaps, asked who the party was and why she was there. She explained that the last thing she remembered was working at the Wayside Inn, a tavern with an attached forge located on the corner of the High Road and Tribor Trail. Vukradin attempted to recount the tale of her capture and heroic rescue, though his storytelling was only moderately compelling. The woman revealed she had been experiencing blackouts for some time, and that she had been called by something known as “the Kraken,” which promised peace after the endless wars and chaos that had plagued the Sword Coast.

Through careful observation and examination, the party determined that the woman’s mind had been manipulated through psionic means rather than traditional magic. Her brain had been rewired, leaving gaps in her memory and making her susceptible to the cult’s influence. Brewbarry, drawing on his instincts and experience, determined that despite the psionic nature of the manipulation, this was not the work of Mind Flayers. Valphine attempted to intimidate the woman about her poor life choices in following the Kraken cult, while Vukradin tried a gentler approach, offering her a position as a blacksmith for the Church of Lathander. The woman declined the immediate offer but expressed interest in returning to the Wayside Inn first, saying she might visit the church later.

Falcon then shared troubling news about one of his investments. Mountain Toe’s Goldmine, located fifteen miles northeast of Phandalin, had suffered from poisoned waters that threatened to shut down operations. Falcon revealed he was part of a Neverwinter mining consortium that owned the mine, having received a discounted share in exchange for his consulting work scouting the area. The investment had strained his finances considerably, leaving him without ready cash to offer as a reward. Instead, he proposed giving the party a share of the gold mine itself if they would investigate the poisoning and resolve the situation.

The party also discussed the Woodland Manse quest that Falcon had mentioned previously, where orcs and half-orcs had occupied an old manor. The reward for clearing it would be a pair of magical boots. The Emerald Enclave guards expressed urgency about reporting back to their order regarding the planar disruptions, mind-manipulating societies, and the cult of Talos they had encountered. However, they agreed to remain at Falcon’s Lodge for two nights to rest and recover. Vukradin successfully convinced Falcon to provide free accommodation for everyone, reminding him of their future business partnership. The party debated their priorities, with Falcon preferring they address the gold mine first, but ultimately decided to pursue the Woodland Manse quest to obtain the magical boots.

Early the next morning at five o’clock, the party set out for the Woodland Manse, following a trail through the woods. They noted that the mysterious Drow woman who had been interested in artifacts had departed from the lodge during the night. Falcon provided clear directions, advising them to stick to the trail to avoid getting lost. The journey covered ten miles through the forest, and they successfully navigated the path without incident.

The party arrived at a foggy clearing where a crumbling two-story manor stood, nearly hidden beneath thorny ivy. The structure sat atop a six-foot-high stone foundation, with a balcony overlooking the main entrance. Wild pumpkins grew in patches all around the manor, and several wild boars were feeding among them. The boars snorted with contempt as the party approached, and Soma snorted back with equal disdain. Vukradin carefully observed the scene, noting that the boars seemed evenly distributed around the property.

Soma used her ability to communicate with animals to speak with the boars, hoping to learn more about the manor’s inhabitants. The first boar completely ignored him, but a second boar proved more talkative. The creature complained about “new boars” that had arrived recently and didn’t speak proper boar language or respect the established etiquette of the pumpkin patches. The talking boar explained there were real wild boars and orc wild boars, and while he and another boar to the north hadn’t spoken in years, they at least respected each other’s territories. He squinted suspiciously at a third boar to the south, identifying it as particularly rude and new, having appeared only a few days ago. The party recalled that Anchorites of Talos possessed the ability to shapeshift into animals, raising immediate suspicions.

Valphine briefly objected to attacking without attempting to parley with the manor’s inhabitants, but the party decided to test their theory. Brewbarry summoned a beam of radiant moonlight down upon the suspicious third boar. The creature failed to resist the magical effect and was forced to drop its disguise, revealing itself to be an Anchorite of Talos. The talking boar, witnessing this violent revelation, began to flee while protesting that this was not how proper protocol should be handled and expressing concern about his pumpkin patch.

Combat erupted immediately. Valphine struck first, hurling a bolt of radiant energy that struck the Anchorite with devastating accuracy, tearing through its defenses and marking it for the next attack. Brewbarry entered a rage and charged forward with advantage, but his strikes went wide despite his fury. Soma followed up with a successful attack that wounded the creature further, bringing it close to defeat. Vukradin attempted to silence the Anchorite to prevent it from calling for help or casting spells, but the cultist resisted the magical effect.

The Anchorite stepped out of the moonbeam’s radius and shouted loudly to alert others in the manor. Then it retaliated with terrible force, unleashing a bolt of lightning that struck Valphine, Soma, and Brewbarry in a devastating line of electrical energy. Brewbarry called upon his stone-like endurance to absorb much of the impact, while Valphine managed to partially dodge the worst of the blast. Brewbarry, however, took the full force of the lightning and was left badly wounded. As the session ended, the party found themselves in the midst of a fierce battle, with the sounds of the Anchorite’s shout likely bringing reinforcements from within the crumbling manor.
