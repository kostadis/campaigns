# Chapter 18 To the Logger’s Camp

### 

## 09-02- Taraskh 1495

### Vukradin 

The Falcon Hunter was a fraud and an artist. And a thief, I must never forget that he is a thief. He has a ring in his possession that belonged to someone. 

Somewhere, someone has lost a magical ring. And I will find the owner. 

And it’s then that I realize we have gone pretty deep into the Neverwinter Wood forest. Brewbarry has been dragging the cart with the supplies for Tribar Wester. At first he complained, but ever since the Falcon Hunter filled it with booze, he has insisted that nobody but him touch the cart. 

The sun is high above, and the air is refreshing and cool. 

When we started our hike into the forest, Valphine let Brewbarry march ahead. But now Valphine is marching right next to him. 

I wonder why.

And we move forward. 

A few hours later, Valphine demands we stop. She points ahead where a poorly concealed Orc is in the bushes ahead. 

“I wonder why he’s there?” 

Soma looks at me and mutters, “You are wet-behind-the-shell Vukradin. The orc is a scout for a raiding party. Don’t make any noise. And don’t try to talk to them.” 

Valphine turns to Brewbarry, “Orcs do make such fine slaves.” 

### Soma 

Leaving my clutch behind, I head out and transform into a bear to get a closer look. Hopefully, the orc won’t think I am a threat. 

But no such luck. He sees me and decides to climb a tree. While climbing the tree, he makes hand motions to the north, east, and west. I notice that beyond the scout is a small clearing. And realize that this was an ambush. 

We could still have the advantage if we approach quietly. 

And then I hear Brewbarry stumble through the woods. 

I want to stuff my head in my shell and mutter, but I can’t. The orcs are here, and now whatever element of surprise we have has been lost. 

But perhaps not, the orc next to Brewbarry shouts, “You are a useless turd of a scout. Of all the scouts, I got one who got distracted by a bear\! I only took you because your mother is my mother’s best friend\! Why if it had been up to me…” 

I climb the tree and swipe at him with my claws, and miss, but his foot is so close to my jaws, and I bite the foot. He howls in pain. 

The scout, despite the bleeding foot, disengages. 

I scream Coward, but it sounds more like “ROWARHHHH\!”  

From 10 feet out, he turns and fires an arrow into my flesh, and I feel the sting of pain.  

### Valphine

I advance. I pull out my crossbow. I fire an arrow. I miss. 

### Brewbarry  

The first orc, who wears a necklace of bones,  moves towards me. I have seen such creatures in the north. They are not harmless, and they are dangerous. The first one throws a javelin at me. It lands in front of my feet, I pick it up and split in two, snarling. 

The second orc, who has a scar on his forehead, shouts,  “You were the dumbest scout then, and you are the dumbest scout now.” 

I am enraged. I remember how in my village, I was exiled because of such accusations.

The first orc attacks me with his scimitar and then slips out of range. 

And then the second orc stops moving. Vukradin has cast some magic on him. The orc's eyes glow. And then he moves to attack the scout.  

The orc with the necklace of bones screams, “You attack my brother’s nephew? Are you mad? Who do you think you are\!” 

I am pleased that this Orc Scout will not die thinking he was banished from his clan. If only my clan showed as much compassion as this Orc.   
From where Soma is, I hear a roar and a crash. The bear has jumped from the branch onto the ground and shoved the scout next to the two orcs. 

###  Valphine

Moonbeam. I abhor that light. It is but a pale imitation of the searing pain of Lathander. In fact, it doesn’t hurt at all. It is not sunlight. Even though Soma says it is. 

I know sunlight. I know the pain. 

And my contempt for her spell knows no bounds, as the orcs are barely hurt.  

But my crossbow finally hurts one of these creatures.  

I smile, as an orc scout looks at me, in horror \- “A Drow who worships Lathander. A bear that casts Moonbeam\!” And then he starts to run. 

I think, “Run, little slave. Run.” 

### Vukradin

I am curious to see what the orc I have not charmed will do. Will he attack one of us? Or will he attack the orc attacking his family member? 

So many possibilities. 

“Once I am done killing this goliath, I will kill you\!” 

Brewbarry scoffs, “Bring it on, shrimp.”  

“Who you calling shrimp?” shouts the orc. 

“You\! Because you smell like one.” 

The orc is confused. He has been insulted in a wide variety of ways, but this really confuses me. 

Brewbarray adds, “I don’t know what it smells like, but I know you smell like one.” 

But then the charmed orc is charmed no more. But I am not done playing with him. I cast phantasmal force, and he sees in his head another copy of the orc with the necklace.  

Wonderful, the orc attacks the image of the orc\! The creature is convinced that his erstwhile ally wants him dead.   

The real orc with the necklace screams, “Foul DROW\!” and attacks Valphine. 

It’s a real shame that folks assume that particular races are particularly evil. It is convenient, but a real shame.  

And then I hear some branches break, and Brewbarry has finally become enraged. 

He has decided to protect Valphine. 

### Brewbarry 

Enraged and enlarged, I swing my halberd in a 20’ arc, and scream, “You will not touch my wine.” 

My swing is true, my roar is good, but I miss. 

Meanwhile, Soma has hit one of the orcs. 

And from the clearing, I can hear the scout stop running, turn, and fire his bow at Valphine. The scout misses. 

### Valphine 

I load my crossbow bolt. I aim. I shoot. I miss. 

It is the unholy light of the moonbeam. 

And the scout flees. 

And I chuckle as the orc with the scar strikes air. Vukradin, the bard, has charmed another with his special kind of music. 

He advances to strike with his daggers and misses. No matter the magic of his music, it hurts the orc with a scar, and he dies. 

And then I am struck by another orc, who sees me as the incarnate will of Lolth, and then flees. I have seen this before, this false bravado. In the Underdark, I would hunt him down and kill him. But in the overbrite, he can see just as well as I can.   

Just then, Soma’s jaws clamp down on the orc with the necklace, and I can tell he will not be long for this world. 

I overhear Brewbarry tell Soma,  “This orc was getting ready to flee. I thought you didn’t attack creatures that flee?” 

The poor creature doesn’t understand hypocrisy, bloodlust, and the thrill of killing and maiming. 

But I do, and I will hunt down this scout. 

I race after him, pursuing the fear and the terror. But this is not the Underdark, where I can see and my quarry cannot. And the scout, escapes. 

When I return, I see Brewbarry and Vukradin have captured the orc with the necklace.  

The orc shouts, “We don’t work for anyone. We are independent. We are an independent orc raiding party.”

Brewbarry growls, “You are now barbecue orc.” 

The orc cowers, and I think that Brewbarry has potential as an interrogator. 

“This wasn’t personal. Ever since we were thrown out of our mountain home by that dragon, it’s been an orc-eat-orc world. We used to live near the Ice Spire, in the Sword Mountains. But with the war of the dragons, and then the war of the giants, a white dragon decided that it wanted our home. We had to flee. We ended up in the valley. Our chief has fallen with some half-orc cultists of Talos and many of our tribe followed him. But I believe orcs should work for orcs, and nobody else.” 

I perk up; perhaps there is a connection here to the other Cultists of Talos. And then my mood darkens, this Orc has opinions about his freedom and whom he must serve, no, that must be fixed right away. 

“You work for us.” 

The creature snivels and asks, “Will I get paid?” 

Brewbarry snarls, “You will get paid with your life.” He then grabs the orc and pulls him toward the cart. The orc’s hands are bound on the cart, and he will now pull it. 

Vukradin asks, “What’s his name?” 

And Brewbarry snarls, “Shrimpy.”  

###  Vukradin 

Just a little bit longer until I arrive at the Logger’s Camp and collect my conflict free reward. 

The trees abruptly end, and  the logging camp spreads along the south shore of the river, where a dozen tents are arranged on a sandy beach. Near a deck, a cabin stands with logs stacked under its awning. Older cabins close by have been torn down to leave only stone chimneys and foundations.

I want to scream. Nobody is here. Worse, a grim silence hangs over the camp.

“If I don’t find Triber, I don’t get my money\! This is awful\!” 

Brewbarry turns to me, “Don’t worry, Vukradin. We will find him.”  

### Valphine 

The orc is tied up. And he will cost us supplies, worry, and care. If this were Menzoberanzaan, I would sacrifice him to Lolth now. But Lathander disapproves of such sacrifice. 

Pity. 

It saves so much time and effort.
