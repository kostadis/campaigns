## Party Dynamics

**The Ethical Fault Line — Vukradin vs. Everyone.** Vukradin's "conflict-free gold" and fair-trade principles are the party's most persistent internal tension. Valphine openly views loot disputes as amusing. Soma internally disagreed with his "all money is blood money" stance but chose not to voice it. Brewbarry is loyal to Vukradin personally but has no philosophical investment in the principle. The party has developed workarounds: Valphine hides things from him (the defensive vial, the 50gp fake gem contingency) and makes deals he isn't present for. Soma lets him hold contested items (the necklace). None of this has caused an open rupture, but Soma has noted privately that his persuasive abilities are generating latent resentment in people around Phandalin who feel "handled."

**Brewbarry and Vukradin — The Foundational Bond.** The party's most stable relationship. Brewbarry's loyalty to Vukradin predates the group and has deepened through shared combat, music, and — most recently — Vukradin learning the true story of Brewbarry's exile. Brewbarry was moved to tears by Vukradin's logging speech and was personally wounded by Adabra's council attacks on him. This bond is the party's emotional anchor.

**Soma as Mother, Reluctant.** Soma's maternal instinct toward the "shell-sprouts" is real but tested. She has objected to Brewbarry killing a paralyzed veteran, to Vukradin pushing Corbin into danger, and to Valphine attempting to intimidate a confused victim — none of these objections changed outcomes. She accepts her limits without escalating, but the accumulation is visible. Her growing uncertainty about her own philosophical framework (no longer certain she is a naturalist) adds internal weight.

**Valphine's Double Life.** Valphine is cultivating a heterodox pain-cult while presenting as orthodox to outsiders. She is the party's most effective intelligence operator, most comfortable liar, and most covert actor — qualities that serve the group but which Vukradin would find deeply incompatible with his principles if he understood their extent. Her relationship with Vukradin is protective and genuinely warm, but built partly on his not knowing what she actually does.

**The Corbin Problem.** Corbin is dead because the party left him guarding Sister Kaella, a deadly assassin, while rushing to intervene elsewhere. Vukradin pushed Corbin into gang contact against his explicit warnings; Vukradin acknowledged this grief directly when Prutha was named. Adabra weaponized Corbin's death at the Enclave council. The party has reframed it from "revenge" to "avenging," but the weight of it is present.

**Emerging Group Purpose.** The party has drifted from a loose adventuring band toward something more intentional: a Privy Council seat in Phandalin, a growing temple, a gold mine stake, an orc convert, and a public reputation that now operates independently of them. They are becoming an institution. Whether that suits all of them — Soma is aging and wanted only to keep the world safe, Brewbarry measures glory in battles — is an open question.
