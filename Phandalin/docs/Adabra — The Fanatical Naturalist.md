### **Adabra — The Fanatical Naturalist**
Adabra’s hatred for the Interventionist faction has become a consuming fire, fueled by ideology rather than personal loss. She sees them not just as misguided, but as enemies of the true natural order, and she believes their violent crusade threatens the delicate balance of all realms.
Though Phandalin itself was spared the worst horrors, Adabra’s mind has been poisoned by the belief that the Interventionists’ actions elsewhere justify extreme measures. To her, the ends of preserving the multiverse’s natural harmony justify any means—including assassination.

### **Mindset and Motivations**
- Blind Ideologue: Adabra no longer listens to reason or compromise. She views Interventionists as fanatics who must be stopped before their purging spreads destruction further.
- Cold and Calculating: She’s calm and patient, but beneath that lies a ruthless determination. Killing is a tool to cleanse the Enclave from what she views as corruption.
- Justification: She tells herself that Interventionists started the war on nature by rejecting the multiverse’s diversity, so their deaths are necessary sacrifices for a greater good.
- Isolation: Living near Phandalin, she operates in the shadows, gathering intelligence and waiting for the right moment to strike. She avoids unnecessary attention but will not hesitate to eliminate targets.

### **How She Sees the Party Members (Soma & Vukradin)**
- Soma: A potential ally and weapon if properly guided. Adabra believes Soma’s naivety and naturalist leanings make her malleable. She may even consider training Soma in covert tactics or assassination techniques.
- Vukradin: A moral idealist whose principles could be bent. Adabra respects his reverence for life but believes he must understand that sometimes life must be taken to preserve the greater whole. She wants to challenge his beliefs on “theft” to justify their cause.

### **Adabra’s Manipulative Strategy — Turning Allies Into Assassins**
- Subtle Indoctrination: She will first appeal to their shared ideology but gradually test their limits by proposing morally gray actions, such as sabotaging Interventionist operations.
- Escalation: Once trust is gained, she might push for direct action—spying, sabotage, and eventually assassination.
- Rationalization: She frames killing as a sacred duty to protect nature’s true balance, portraying Interventionists as unnatural abominations who threaten all life.

### **Adabra’s Role Before the Party Knows About the Kraken Society**
- What She Knows:Adabra only sees Interventionist activity around Leilon and Phandalin—patrols, secretive expeditions, and aggressive moves she interprets as dangerous zealotry threatening the natural balance. She suspects the Interventionists are hiding something sinister but doesn’t know about the Kraken Society’s manipulation.
- Her Message to the Party:She warns that the Interventionists are becoming a dangerous force, disrupting nature and silencing dissent. She urges the party to be cautious of Interventionist patrols and consider opposing them to protect the land’s true balance.

### **Party’s Discovery of the Kraken Society**
- Initial Contact:As the party investigates Interventionist patrols or missions, they encounter clues—cryptic symbols, unusual behavior, or intercepted messages—that hint at a third party influencing events.
- Emerald Enclave Patrol Encounter:During a tense confrontation or negotiation with an Interventionist patrol, the party learns of strange orders or references to a covert threat undermining the Enclave’s efforts. This leads them to uncover the Kraken Society’s involvement.
- Revelation:The party realizes that the Interventionists are reacting to Kraken Society manipulation, not acting purely out of zealotry. This revelation complicates their view of both factions and forces them to reassess alliances.

### **Dramatic Effect and Roleplay Opportunities**
- Adabra’s Unwitting Accuracy:The party can later reflect on how Adabra’s warnings were true but misdirected—she didn’t know about the Kraken Society but sensed that the Interventionists’ actions were dangerous.
- Tension with Adabra:Once the party uncovers the Kraken Society plot, they may try to convince Adabra, who might resist or double down on her hatred of the Interventionists, complicating potential alliances.
- Moral Complexity:The party must navigate a web of competing agendas: the Interventionists trying to stop the Kraken Society, Adabra’s radical naturalism, and their own goals.
