### Ambush: The Hired Blades of the Lowlands

This encounter can occur as the party travels through the wilderness, perhaps on the "uneven ground rising to a ridge" \[1\] common to the Sword Coast near Phandalin \[2\].

\*   \*\*Description:\*\* The air crackles with an unusual tension, a silence that feels out of place in the rustling woods. Suddenly, the silence is shattered by guttural battle cries as \*\*a band of exceptionally disciplined orcs, unlike typical disorganized raiders\*\*, burst from the undergrowth \[3\]. Their crude armor is reinforced with scraps of actual metal, and their axes gleam with a sharpness that speaks of recent care, not neglect. As they charge, you might glimpse a few \*\*freshly minted coins\*\* sewn into a pouch on one orc's belt, or hear a snarl of "Paid!" \[User's Query\]. They move with a coordinated aggression that suggests they are not merely hungry, but driven by a purpose beyond simple plunder. Leading them is a figure draped in crude, storm-weathered leathers, clutching a symbol of a jagged lightning bolt \[1\].

\*   \*\*Encounter Details:\*\*
    \*   \*\*Opponents:\*\* The ambush consists of:
        \*   \*\*One Anchorite of Talos\*\* (Moesko's stat block, but could be a different named anchorite if Moesko is still alive at the Tower of Storms) \[1\]. This individual is a direct combatant and provides tactical support.
        \*   \*\*Two Orcs per player character\*\*, including any sidekicks \[1, 4\].
    \*   \*\*Tactics:\*\* The anchorite will immediately cast \*bless\* on up to three orcs, enhancing their combat effectiveness \[1\]. The orcs will charge to engage in melee, aiming to overwhelm the party quickly. Their coordinated movements suggest training, hinting at a competent, unseen force behind them \[3\].
    \*   \*\*Setting the Scene:\*\* Consider using terrain features like a "sixty-foot-wide, ten-foot-deep gully" \[1\] to create an initial ranged exchange before melee, or dense trees for cover and concealment.

\*   \*\*Loot (after combat):\*\*
    \*   \*\*Per Orc:\*\* 2d4 gp, a few copper coins, and their crude weapons (greataxe, javelins). You might find one or two of them have an extra 5-10 gp in newly minted coin.
    \*   \*\*Anchorite of Talos:\*\* 20-30 gp, a crude lightning bolt amulet, and perhaps a small, non-magical, weather-worn leather pouch containing \*\*a few silver pieces stamped with a symbol you don't immediately recognize\*\* – it looks like a crude, stylized "C," but nothing overtly linking to the Carver's full organization \[User's Query, 27\]. This is a very subtle, untraceable hint that avoids revealing the broader plot.

This encounter focuses on a tough, straightforward combat \[1\], showcasing the immediate threat without delving into the "creeping paranoia" \[5\] or "cosmic distortion" \[6\] that will come with later revelations. The orcs are simply a dangerous, paid-for threat \[User's Query, 27\].
