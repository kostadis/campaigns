### The Carver's Blades
This is not a ragtag group of desperate highwaymen; it is a burgeoning criminal enterprise built on brutality and fear. The Carver has used the chaos caused by the dragon Cryovain to consolidate power, recruiting the desperate and preying on the weak. His organization is a significant threat to Phandalin and the entire region.
### Hierarchy and Key Figures
#### Leader: The Carver
A ruthless, 40-year-old human bandit lord who rules with an iron fist. He is a pragmatic and terrifyingly competent predator, focused on results and utterly merciless in his punishments. He commands his organization from a fortified location, letting his lieutenants handle the daily risks.
(Challenge: A deadly solo boss or a major threat with his personal guard. Use stats for a Bandit Lord or a heavily customized Gladiator or Veteran with legendary actions.)
#### The Lieutenants (The Carver's Hands)
- Jax, the Gutter-King: The man who recruited Corbin. Jax is the Carver's right hand for manpower. He has a keen eye for desperation and knows how to turn a broken farmer or a runaway youth into a loyal, fear-driven asset. He's sly and manipulative, managing the rank-and-file bandits and ensuring the Carver's orders are followed without question.(Challenge: A tough mini-boss. Use stats for a Master Thief or a Veteran with high Charisma (Deception) and the ability to command others.)
- "Sister" Kella, the Spymaster: A sharp, severe woman in her thirties, Kella manages the Blades' intelligence network. Using a combination of trained birds, intimidated villagers, and a network of dedicated scouts, she knows the patrol schedules of the guards, the routes of the caravans, and the routines of wealthy prospectors. Little happens on the roads around Phandalin that she doesn't know about.(Challenge: A cunning opponent who likely won't be fought alone. Use stats for a Spy or Scout with legendary actions focused on escape and calling reinforcements.)
- Borg the Hammer: A hulking half-orc who serves as the Carver's enforcer and the commander of the organization's muscle. When a target needs to be intimidated, a lesson needs to be taught, or a fortified location needs to be cracked open, Borg and his crew are sent in. He is brutally effective and fiercely loyal to the Carver, who he respects as a stronger predator.(Challenge: A straightforward and brutal combat encounter. Use stats for a Berserker or Half-Ogre.)
#### Specialists
- The Reavers (4): The Carver's elite shock troops and personal bodyguards. These are not desperate men, but hardened killers who are paid well for their loyalty and brutality. They are better equipped and more disciplined than the average bandit.(Challenge: A tough fight for the party. Use stats for Bandit Captains or Veterans.)
- Old Man Hemlock: A bitter, elderly hedge wizard who was cast out of a proper order for his unsavory experiments. The Carver provides him with protection and rare reagents in exchange for magical support—creating thick fog to cover raids, mending equipment, or brewing potent sleep poisons.(Challenge: A "squishy" but tactical threat. Use stats for a Cult Fanatic or Mage with a limited, utility-focused spell list.)
#### Rank-and-File
- Blades (15-25): The backbone of the organization. This is a mixed group of thugs, bullies, and broken men like Corbin. They perform the day-to-day work of ambushes, guard duty, and extortion. What they lack in skill, they make up for in numbers and desperation.(Challenge: The main fodder for encounters. Use stats for Bandits and Thugs.)
### Base of Operations: The Old Sawmill
The Blades have taken over a large, abandoned sawmill in a secluded part of the Neverwinter Wood. The river provides a barrier on one side, and the thick forest conceals it from the main roads. The main mill building has been fortified with crude watchtowers and palisades, and the network of cellars beneath it is used to store plunder and hold prisoners. It is always guarded by a significant force of Blades and at least one of the lieutenants.
### Goals and Tactics
The Carver's goal is to become the undisputed power in the region, using the dragon's presence as a smokescreen. His Blades employ brutal, effective tactics. Scouts identify high-value targets, Hemlock's magic provides a tactical edge, and Borg's crews execute swift, overwhelming ambushes.
This brutality has been amplified by an alliance of convenience with the **Rot-Tusk ogre tribe**. Pushed from their territory by Cryovain and starving, the ogres, led by a dim-witted but powerful chieftain named Gruk-Gruk, have struck a deal with the Carver. He provides them with a steady supply of meat (stolen livestock, mostly) in exchange for their muscle. The Carver uses the ogres as a sledgehammer—unleashing them to smash through caravan guards or create terrifying diversions while his more disciplined Blades secure the actual loot. This alliance makes the Carver's forces a multi-pronged threat, combining brute force with cunning. They aim to control all trade moving in and out of Phandalin, bleeding the town dry until it is completely under their thumb.
