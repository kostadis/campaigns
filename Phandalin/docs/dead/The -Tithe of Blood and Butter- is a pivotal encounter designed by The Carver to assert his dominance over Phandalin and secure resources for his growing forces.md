The "Tithe of Blood and Butter" is a pivotal encounter designed by The Carver to assert his dominance over Phandalin and secure resources for his growing forces. This event is not merely a raid but a strategic move intended to test the town's resilience and send a chilling warning to anyone who would defy The Carver's influence, including the player characters.
Here is a comprehensive encounter for "The Tithe of Blood and Butter":
# The Tithe of Blood and Butter Encounter
**The Carver's Motivation and Overall Goal:** The Carver's overarching goal is to become the undisputed power in the region, using the presence of the white dragon Cryovain as a smokescreen. He employs brutal, effective tactics, combining cunning with brute force. This particular event is designed to **test Phandalin's resolve and feed his allies** simultaneously. His forces aim to control all trade in and out of Phandalin, bleeding the town dry until it is completely under his thumb. The Carver is also acutely aware of the player party's interference, explicitly naming and seeking to **"eradicate" them brutally** as a clear warning.
**The Event and Hook:** The Carver dispatches his enforcer, **Borg the Hammer**, along with elements of the **Rot-Tusk ogre tribe**, to raid **Butterskull Ranch**. This isn't just a livestock theft; the raid is a devastating assault where the ogres **smash infrastructure, burn barns, and take the ranch owner, Alfonse Kalazorn (known as "Big Al"), hostage**. The intent is to **enforce a regular "tithe" of food** from Phandalin.
The party might get involved when they hear **rumors of the attack**. Perhaps they are in Phandalin, potentially at the Stonehill Inn or Barthen's Provisions, when news of the ranch's destruction and Big Al's kidnapping reaches the townmaster, Harbin Wester. Given the party's prior interactions, Harbin Wester might directly appeal to them for aid, especially since they have a history of fulfilling his "petty demands" which the Carver sees as undermining his influence.
**The Location: Butterskull Ranch** Butterskull Ranch is located **five miles east of Conyberry**. It's a thriving operation, originally a cattle and horse ranch, later expanded to include a pig farm, chicken coops, vegetable gardens, corn fields, and an apple orchard. Big Al Kalazorn, a retired sheriff from Triboar, is famous for his "butter skulls" made from the milk of his prized cow, Petunia.
When the party arrives, they find:
- The front door of the main house leads to a kitchen with a large butter churn and a skull-shaped wooden butter mold.
- Other rooms include an empty foyer, a dining room decorated with cattle skulls, a downstairs closet, and a common room with scattered playing cards and Dragonchess pieces.
- The ranch also features **boar pens** and potentially other cells used to hold animals for sacrifice.
- **Evidence of the attack:** The barns are burned, infrastructure is smashed, and the general state of the ranch clearly indicates a brutal raid.
**The Adversaries:** The primary foes are the **Rot-Tusk ogres** led by their dim-witted but powerful chieftain, **Gruk-Gruk**, and overseen by **Borg the Hammer**, The Carver's enforcer.
- The **Rot-Tusk ogres** were pushed from their territory by Cryovain, the white dragon, and are starving. They have allied with the Carver in exchange for a steady supply of meat (mostly stolen livestock). The Carver uses them as a "sledgehammer".
- **Borg the Hammer** is a cunning half-orc commander who executes swift, overwhelming ambushes. He represents The Carver's disciplined Blades.
- The Carver is aware of the specific party members: the **Tortle** (Soma), the **Drow** (Valphine), the **Uthgardt brute** (Brewbarry), and the "insufferable **Aasimar who claims to hate music**" (Vukradin), noting a misinterpretation of Vukradin's actual dislike for those who hated the arts. He also knows about Corbin's past reluctance and expects "no more hesitation" from his agents.
**The Challenge for the Party:** This encounter presents a **multi-faceted challenge** for the party:
1. **Tracking and Initial Assessment:** The party must first track the attackers to the besieged ranch. This might involve survival checks to follow tracks or investigation checks to find clues left behind by the destructive ogres. The presence of **two valuable ogres previously lost** due to the party's interference is known to the Carver, so Borg may be prepared for them.
1. **Tactical Battle:** The party will face a **difficult tactical battle** against the ogres and Borg. This could involve:
  - **Overcoming Defenses:** The ranch might be fortified or have traps set by Borg.
  - **Hostage Rescue:** Big Al is being held hostage, adding pressure to the combat. The party will need to devise a plan to reach and free him without further harm.
  - **Specific Threat Focus:** Borg and the ogres may specifically target the party members known to the Carver, such as Soma, Valphine, Brewbarry, or Vukradin, as they have orders for their "eradication".
1. **Moral and Strategic Dilemma (The Tithe):** Even if the party defeats the immediate threat and rescues Big Al, they must then **decide how to deal with the Carver's demand for a regular food tithe** from Phandalin. This decision has far-reaching implications:
  - **Direct Confrontation:** They could refuse, leading to a larger, potentially more dangerous confrontation with The Carver's wider organization later.
  - **Negotiation:** They might attempt to negotiate terms to protect Phandalin from starvation, which would be a complex and difficult process with a ruthless individual like The Carver.
  - **Seeking Alternatives:** They might look for alternative food sources or ways to undermine the Carver's control without direct conflict, though the Carver explicitly states this cannot be allowed to continue.
**Aftermath and Implications:** Depending on the party's actions, the encounter's aftermath will vary:
- **Success:** If the party successfully rescues Big Al and defeats Borg and his ogres, they will be heroes in Phandalin, further shoring up Townmaster Wester's authority. However, The Carver will be even more determined to eliminate them for their continued interference and the loss of his ogre forces. This could lead to The Carver issuing a direct "Eradication Order" for the party, specifying them by type and trait.
- **Failure:** If the party fails to save Big Al or repel the ogres, The Carver's grip on Phandalin's resources tightens, and the town will suffer from enforced starvation, highlighting the Carver's ruthlessness. This would likely increase the fear and paranoia within Phandalin.
This encounter combines direct combat, a rescue mission, and a significant moral/strategic dilemma, showcasing The Carver's brutal tactics and the escalating threat he poses.

## **Borg the Hammer (Half-Orc Enforcer)**
Role: Tough melee leader with tactical smarts and brutal attacks.
Stats (Use as a custom Half-Orc Warrior with enhancements):
- AC: 16 (chain mail + shield)
- HP: Around 85 (12d8 + 24)
- Speed: 30 ft.
- Attack:
  - *Greatsword:* +6 to hit, 2d6+4 slashing damage.
  - *Handaxe (Thrown):* +6 to hit, range 20/60 ft., 1d6+4 slashing damage.
- Special Abilities:
  - *Battle Tactics (Recharge 5-6):* Borg can use a bonus action to command one Rot-Tusk Ogre within 30 ft. to make an immediate melee attack.
  - *Relentless Endurance:* When reduced to 0 HP, Borg can drop to 1 HP instead (once per long rest).
  - *Intimidating Presence:* As an action, Borg can attempt to frighten one enemy within 30 ft. (DC 13 Wisdom save).
- Saves: Strength +6, Constitution +5
- Skills: Intimidation +5, Athletics +6
- Senses: Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 11

## **Rot-Tusk Ogres (Brutish Minions)**
Role: Heavy hitters with brute strength, less tactical but terrifying in numbers.
Stats (Modified Ogre stats):
- AC: 11 (natural armor)
- HP: About 45 (6d10 + 12)
- Speed: 40 ft.
- Attack:
  - *Greatclub:* +5 to hit, 2d8+3 bludgeoning damage.
  - *Javelin (Thrown):* +5 to hit, range 30/120 ft., 2d6+3 piercing damage.
- Special Traits:
  - *Brute:* A melee weapon deals one extra die of its damage when the Rot-Tusk Ogre hits with it (included above).
  - *Pack Tactics:* Ogres gain advantage on attack rolls against a creature if at least one ally is within 5 ft. of the target.
- Saves: Strength +5, Constitution +4
- Senses: Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 10

## **Encounter Tactics**
- Borg’s Role:Borg fights at the front, focusing on the strongest or most threatening party member (especially if they are known to him). He uses *Battle Tactics* to coordinate ogre attacks, maximizing damage output. He uses *Intimidating Presence* early to disrupt party strategy.
- Rot-Tusk Ogres’ Role:Ogres advance aggressively, using *Pack Tactics* to flank and overwhelm weaker party members. They throw javelins initially to soften targets, then close in with their greatclubs.
- Environment Use:Borg may have set traps or barricades at the ranch entrance or around key buildings. Ogres might try to use the terrain to their advantage, pushing PCs into chokepoints or near burning debris.
- Hostage Element:Big Al is held in a fortified area; the party must balance dealing with foes and protecting the hostage. Borg may threaten or use Big Al as leverage.

## **Adjustments for Party Strength**
- If the party is larger or stronger than expected, add an extra ogre or give Borg an extra use of *Battle Tactics*.
- For smaller or weaker parties, reduce ogre HP slightly or limit Borg’s *Intimidating Presence* usage.
