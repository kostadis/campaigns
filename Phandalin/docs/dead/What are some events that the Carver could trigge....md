Of course. Based on the Carver's personality and the organization you've established, here are several events he could trigger to create compelling challenges for a 5th-level party.
### 1. The Tithe of Blood and Butter
- **The Event:** The Carver decides to test Phandalin's resolve and feed his ogre allies at the same time. He sends his enforcer, **Borg the Hammer**, along with the **Rot-Tusk ogres** to raid a key food source for the town: **Butterskull Ranch**. They don't just steal livestock; they smash infrastructure, burn barns, and take the owner, Alfonse Kalazorn, "hostage" to enforce a regular "tithe" of food from the town.
- **The Challenge:** This is a multi-faceted challenge. The party must first track the attackers to the besieged ranch. They then face a difficult tactical battle against brutish ogres led by a cunning half-orc commander. Afterwards, they must decide how to deal with the Carver's demand for a food tithe, which could lead to a larger confrontation or a difficult negotiation to protect Phandalin from starvation.
- **Key Characters Involved:** Borg the Hammer, Gruk-Gruk and the Rot-Tusk ogres.
### 2. The Poisoned Spring
- **The Event:** To assert his dominance and sow chaos, the Carver tasks his shadiest specialists with a subtle but devastating attack. **Old Man Hemlock** is sent to poison the water supply at the **Mountain's Toe Gold Mine**. The poison isn't immediately lethal but causes a debilitating sickness. The mine's production grinds to a halt, miners fall ill, and panic spreads that the dragon's presence has corrupted the land itself.
- **The Challenge:** This is primarily an investigation. The party might hear rumors of a "curse" at the mine. They must travel there, use their skills (Medicine, Nature, Investigation) to determine the cause is poison, not disease, and then track the poisoner back to a hidden camp. The final confrontation would be with Old Man Hemlock and a squad of his bandit protectors.
- **Key Characters Involved:** Old Man Hemlock, a squad of Carver's Blades.
### 3. The Whisper Campaign
- **The Event:** Realizing that brute force has its limits, the Carver initiates a more insidious plan orchestrated by **Jax, the Gutter-King**. Jax sends several of his more presentable bandits, including men like **Corbin**, into Phandalin. They pose as desperate refugees displaced by Cryovain. They spread rumors in the Stonehill Inn, complaining about Townmaster Wester's incompetence, claiming the adventurers are making things worse by provoking the dragon, and suggesting that a "strong leader" like the Carver could provide real protection for a price.
- **The Challenge:** This is a social and roleplaying challenge. The party must uncover the deception, counter the misinformation, and win back the trust of the townsfolk. A straight fight would turn public opinion against them. They need to gather evidence, expose the spies, and perhaps even turn one of them (like the disillusioned Corbin) against their boss.
- **Key Characters Involved:** Jax (from a distance), Corbin and other bandit "refugees."
### 4. Framing the Heroes
- **The Event:** The party is becoming a thorn in the Carver's side. He decides to remove them by turning the law against them. He has his spymaster, **"Sister" Kella**, orchestrate a raid on a merchant caravan on the Triboar Trail. During the raid, the bandits intentionally leave behind an item belonging to one of the party members (something they may have lost or sold in town). A "survivor," coached by Kella, reports the attack to Phandalin's authorities, tearfully identifying the adventurers as the culprits.
- **The Challenge:** The party suddenly finds themselves wanted criminals. They must evade capture by the town guard, clear their names by investigating the crime scene, and unmask the conspiracy. This forces them to operate outside the law and could put them in direct conflict with factions like the Lord's Alliance, who value order above all.
- **Key Characters Involved:** "Sister" Kella and her network of scouts and spies.
### 5. An Offer You Can't Refuse
- **The Event:** The Carver targets a key town figure to create a chokehold on a vital service. His Reavers kidnap **Adabra Gwynn**, the midwife and apothecary from **Umbrage Hill**. A letter is left behind: the Carver now controls the supply of potions and healing in the region. For a hefty price, she can be "contracted" out. For a much higher price, she can be returned. This move puts the party and all of Phandalin in a terrible position when the next person is wounded by a monster or one of the Carver's own thugs.
- **The Challenge:** This is a classic rescue mission that can't be ignored. The party must track the elite Reavers back to a fortified outpost—perhaps a cave system or ruins separate from the main Sawmill base. They will face the Carver's best fighters in a high-stakes battle to rescue the town's most vital non-combatant.
- **Key Characters Involved:** The Reavers (the Carver's elite guard).
