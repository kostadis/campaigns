### Name:** Corbin**
### Appearance:
Corbin is a gaunt man in his late twenties, with the haunted, hollowed-out look of someone who has stared into the abyss and found it staring back. His hair is a matted, dirt-brown tangle, and a patchy, uncared-for beard clings to his jaw. His eyes, a pale blue, are often distant and unfocused, but can sharpen with a desperate, feral intensity when threatened. He wears ill-fitting leather armor scavenged from a dead man, and his movements are tinged with a weary resignation, punctuated by moments of nervous energy.
### Background:
Corbin was not born a bandit. He was a farmer from a small, forgettable village called Greenmeadow, nestled in the plains east of the High Forest. His life was simple and good; he worked the land his father had worked, was married to a woman named Elara, and had a young daughter, Lily, who loved the wildflowers that grew at the edge of their fields.
Then the Ordning was shattered, and the world broke with it.
Giants, emboldened and lawless, began to carve paths of destruction across the North. The chaos they sowed emboldened others. One smoke-filled afternoon, the Uthgardt barbarians of the Black Lion tribe descended on Greenmeadow. They were not raiding for supplies; they were a storm of pure destruction, a wildfire in human form, proving their strength in a world gone mad.
Corbin fought. He was no warrior, armed only with a wood-axe and a farmer's grit, but he fought for his home. It was not enough. He watched his fields burn, his home collapse into embers, and his family… he watched them fall. He only survived because he was knocked unconscious by a charging barbarian's shield and left for dead in a ditch, buried under the body of his neighbor.
### Path to Phandalin:
He awoke to silence and ash. With nothing left but the clothes on his back and a soul scoured clean of hope, Corbin began to walk. He drifted south for months, a ghost haunting the roads, taking what he could to survive—a half-eaten apple from a cart, a discarded blanket, a few coins from a sleeping traveler's purse. Each petty crime chipped away at the man he had been.
He ended up in the vicinity of Phandalin, drawn by rumors of work but finding only more hardship. The fear of the dragon, Cryovain, had a stranglehold on the town, and no one had time or charity for a broken, empty-eyed stranger. Starving and freezing in the woods, he was found by a man named Jax, a flint-hard lieutenant in the Carver's gang. Jax didn't offer charity; he offered a choice. "Starve, or earn your keep."
For Corbin, it wasn't a choice. It was just the next logical step down a path he never wanted to walk.
### Current State:
Corbin now serves the Carver, though he has likely never met the man himself, taking his orders from Jax. He does what he's told—robs travelers, intimidates prospectors, and stands guard over stolen goods. He is quiet and withdrawn, a ghost among the other, louder bandits. He works not for greed or glory, but for the next meal and a place to sleep where he won't freeze. The fire in him is nearly out, replaced by a cold, simmering resentment for a world that took everything and left him this. He is a dangerous man, not because he is evil, but because he has nothing left to lose.
