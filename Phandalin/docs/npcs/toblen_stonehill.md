# Toblen Stonehill

## Identity
- **Role:** Proprietor of the Stonehill Inn, Phandalin
- **Faction:** None noted; independent innkeeper
- **First Appearance:** The party encountered him at his inn, a modest two-story roadhouse at the center of Phandalin

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Running a successful, welcoming inn; keeping his clientele entertained and informed
- Originally came to Phandalin from Triboar to prospect, but discovered innkeeping suited him far better. He is short, friendly, and community-minded — the sort to quietly pass along useful warnings to guests he trusts. He has a shrewd eye for talent, recognizing Vukardin's musical value both commercially and personally.

## History with the Party
- **Early visits:** Allowed Vukardin to pay for meals with music, requiring popular songs from Neverwinter or Waterdeep. After several performances, approached Vukardin unprompted and asked him to play something of his own choosing, rewarding him with a complimentary beer.
- **White dragon warning:** Quietly warned Vukardin about rumors of a white dragon in the area.
- **Return visit:** Again persuaded Vukardin to perform for the inn's clientele.
- **Meeting with Ser Kaelen Thorn:** Served drinks to Vukardin's table during this encounter at the inn.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The Stonehill Inn, Phandalin
- **Active Operations:** Running the inn as normal
- **What the party knows:** Toblen is friendly, discreet, and a reliable source of local rumors. He goes by the nickname "Spiderman," earned from a childhood incident in which he fell into a hatching spider nest, was covered in baby spiders, and walked out completely calm and unafraid.
- **Hidden information:** None noted

## Relationships
- **Vukardin:** Warm, mutually beneficial relationship; Toblen values him as a performer and has shown personal interest in his original music
- **Ser Kaelen Thorn:** Hosted the meeting at his inn; nature of relationship beyond innkeeper unclear
- **Party broadly:** Friendly and helpful; willing to share local intelligence quietly
