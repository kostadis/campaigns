# Lord Halueth Verres

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Lord Magister of Neverwinter (post-confirmation). The Lord Magister is a confirmed seat in Neverwinter governance; elevation requires a public confirmation hearing.
- **Faction:** Neverwinter nobility
- **First Appearance:** Not yet met. Referenced only as a famous court scandal Vukradin uses as a tone analog.

*Also known as: Lord Verres, Lord Magister Verres.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Nothing established at the table about Verres himself. He is, so far, the *subject* of a scandal rather than a present character.

## History with the Party
- The party has not met Lord Verres or Lady Verres. Vukradin invoked the scandal in dialogue at Falcon's Hunting Lodge (2026-05-19) during the planning discussion when describing the "indignant tone" he wanted for his Sending to Falcon. The reference functions as common Neverwinter court gossip — Vukradin treats it as a story everyone would know. Vukradin used the story to identify the register he wanted — "Lady Verres on a Sending" — meaning indignant, as if doing the recipient a favor by asking at all.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Neverwinter (implied; nothing more specific established)
- **Tenure:** Lord Magister, confirmed approximately ten years prior to the famous Sending incident. Whether he still holds the seat is unspecified.
- **Active Plans:** None established.
- **Party Knowledge:** Vukradin knows the scandal in enough detail to use it as a rhetorical model. Other party members were present when he cited it but have no independent knowledge.

## The Scandal

Verres' confirmation to Lord Magister was contested. **A war-mage from Waterdeep** travelled to Neverwinter and testified against him at the public hearing. The substance of her testimony involved:

- **An artifact** — nature unspecified.
- **A contract with apprentices** — nature unspecified.
- "The whole mess" — phrasing implies the artifact and the apprentice contract were the named pieces of a larger pattern.

Verres was confirmed anyway. He received the seat.

**Ten years later, Lady Verres** initiated contact with the Waterdhavian war-mage via *Sending* to ask whether she was finally ready to apologize to her husband for what she said at his confirmation. The war-mage actually replied (content unspecified, but the reply is preserved as part of the famous-bit-of-court-history).

The exchange became famous enough in Neverwinter social circles that Vukradin reaches for it as the canonical example of *audacity as a tone* — the wronged party demanding an apology from the one with the actual grievance.
