# Brewbarry

## Identity
- **Role:** Wandering warrior, former Uthgardt Barbarian; traveling with the party as a free agent
- **First Appearance:** Fought alongside the party against an ogre; integrated into the group from that engagement onward

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Unclear long-term, but demonstrates strong drive toward camaraderie, diplomatic resolution, and protecting companions — particularly Valphine
- Brewbarry is boisterous and direct, occasionally blundering tactically, but consistently pulls toward mercy and connection over violence when given the choice
- His Uthgardt past is a source of both pride and pain; he acknowledges the cruelty of his former people, identifies with others who have been hurt by them, and seems to be actively building a different identity
- He shows unexpected emotional depth — the orc prisoner's exile briefly moved him, and his apology to the fleeing bandit suggests genuine empathy rooted in personal experience
- Has a possessive streak regarding items he claims (refused to let anyone else touch the supply cart after it was filled with alcohol)
- Uses insults and taunts in combat, sometimes confusing his opponents

## History with the Party

**Ogre Fight**
Brewbarry fought the ogre alongside the party, missing a halberd strike that rang loudly off the rocky ground. He received a full axe blow from the ogre but was completely unharmed due to his Uthgardt barbarian rage and mystical resilience, which stunned both the ogre and Valphine. The ogre's failure against him triggered its retreat. Brewbarry pursued but could not bring the ogre down before Vukradin finished it.

**Gnomengarde**
At Gnomengarde's guard post, Brewbarry announced himself as "Brewbarry, killer of Ogres" to assert he was no shapeshifter. When the gnomes identified him as an Uthgardt Barbarian, he clarified he *used to be* one, which unexpectedly resolved a bureaucratic ambiguity around a standing attack order against Uthgardtians and allowed the party entry. In the inventors' workshop, he cut off Valphine's implied suggestion of torture with the declaration *"We talk! We cure!"* aligning himself with the diplomatic approach.

**Mimic Fight**
Charged the mimic and struck it with his halberd, wounding the creature but getting the weapon stuck in its body. The mimic's retaliatory pseudopod attack missed him entirely. He struggled throughout the fight, unable to free his halberd and missing with his initial attempts before producing handaxes at Vukradin's prompting.

**Bandit Ambush**
Decapitated one bandit with his halberd, emerging covered in blood, then chased the fleeing spider-phobic bandit. Rather than killing him, Brewbarry dropped his halberd, shouted *"Friends,"* and offered an apology: *"I am so sorry that the Uthgardts hurt you. They were mean to me, too."* The gesture surprised the bandit into grief rather than flight. Brewbarry's motivation appears rooted in personal experience of Uthgardt cruelty and a recognition of shared suffering.

**Stonehill Tavern (Phandalin)**
Helped defuse tension over an ambush accusation by reminding Vukradin that the tavern patrons loved his music. Participated in a betting pool on whether the party would all survive the lighthouse mission, with a side bet on whether Vukradin would be ditched or killed. Joined Vukradin and Valphine in a musical session at the tavern, using his halberd as a percussive instrument in a style recalling Uthgardt tribal music — the performance was notably successful and earned the group additional coin.

**Supply Cart Ambush**
Pulled the supply cart intended for Tribar Wester solo and, after the Falcon Hunter filled it with alcohol, refused to let anyone else touch it. Stumbled through the woods during the ambush and gave away the party's position. Picked up a javelin thrown at him and snapped it in two. Became enraged when the orc with the necklace attacked Valphine and swung his halberd in a wide arc to defend her, though he missed. Insulted the orc by calling him a "shrimp," leaving the creature genuinely confused. After the battle, took charge of the prisoner — named him "Shrimpy," bound his hands to the cart, and forced him to pull it. The orc's lament about being exiled from his clan triggered a personal memory of Brewbarry's own exile, and he was briefly moved that the orc's clanmates showed loyalty the orc scout lacked. Reassured Vukradin that Tribar Wester would be found.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Phandalin area; involved in the mission to locate Tribar Wester
- **Active:** Managing prisoner "Shrimpy," who is bound to the supply cart and forced to pull it
- **What the Party Knows:** Former Uthgardt, exiled or estranged; has personal grievances against his old people
- **Hidden:** The specific circumstances of his exile remain unexplored

## Relationships
- **Valphine:** Protective instinct evident — defended her during the orc fight (became enraged when she was attacked), cut off her implied torture suggestion. Relationship has friction but also clear loyalty. Collaborates with her musically
- **Vukradin:** Friendly; backed him socially at the tavern, collaborated musically, bet on his survival (and potential abandonment)
- **"Shrimpy" (orc prisoner):** Claimed him as a ward post-battle and named him; the orc's story of clan exile quietly resonated with Brewbarry's own experience. Currently forcing him to pull the supply cart
- **The fleeing bandit:** Showed him unexpected compassion based on shared experience of Uthgardt cruelty; outcome of that encounter unresolved
- **Uthgardt:** Exiled; views his former people's cruelty with shame and regret. His own exile appears to have been painful and informs his empathy for others who have been cast out
