# Brewbarry

## Identity
- **Role:** Barbarian warrior, party member
- **Faction:** Former Uthgardt tribal member (tribe now an antagonistic memory rather than affiliation)
- **First Appearance:** Present during the mimic battle; met the party as part of the group from early sessions

## Personality & Motivations
- Defines himself as a "barbarian" and is uncomfortable when others try to reframe that identity, even sympathetically
- Carries complicated, personal wounds from Uthgardt violence — both as a perpetrator by association and as a victim himself
- Demonstrates unexpected empathy under pressure, particularly toward others who have been harmed or bullied; capable of disarming moments of vulnerability
- Loud and joyful in combat victories; prone to self-frustration when he makes tactical errors

## History with the Party
1. **Mimic Battle:** Struggled to free his halberd, was called out by Vukradin for lacking ranged options, and improvised with handaxes after thumping himself in frustration.
2. **Bandit Ambush near Umbrage Hill:** Decapitated a bandit in combat, then chased a fleeing spider-phobic man named Corbin. Stopped when Corbin's fear triggered empathy. Dropped his halberd, apologized on behalf of the Uthgardt, and admitted "They were mean to me, too." This confession prompted Corbin to break down, reveal his own victimhood at Uthgardt hands, and ask to join the group.
3. **Manticore Fight (Windmill):** Landed handaxe throws on the manticore's wings, engaged in close combat on the second floor, absorbed claw attacks through rage, and delivered the killing blow — celebrating loudly afterward.
4. **Ser Kaelen Thorn's Arrival:** Recognized the Order of the Gauntlet's emblem and told the party the Order had fought his tribe, calling them bullies who stole. Tension was de-escalated when Ser Kaelen acknowledged mutual atrocities and offered Brewbarry a drink, which he accepted.
5. **Adabra's Speech:** Adabra refused to use the word "barbarian" around him and delivered a passionate defense of his humanity. Brewbarry remained visibly uncomfortable and insisted on the label; Adabra overruled him.
6. **Approach to Tower of Storms:** Spotted concealed ambushers in a gully by reading subtle tracks and a glint of hidden armor, confirming Valphine's instinctive read of danger.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Traveling with the party toward the Tower of Storms
- **Active:** No independent operations; fully integrated into party movement
- **What the Party Knows:** His Uthgardt background, his personal pain at the hands of (and within) the tribe, his empathy for Corbin, his hostility toward the Order of the Gauntlet
- **Hidden:** The full nature of what the Uthgardt did to him specifically remains unspoken

## Relationships
- **Corbin (bandit, now ally):** Brewbarry's act of empathy directly caused Corbin's defection and request to join the group; a bond forged through shared Uthgardt trauma
- **Vukradin:** Called Brewbarry out publicly during the mimic fight over his lack of ranged weapons — minor friction, no lasting animosity apparent
- **Adabra:** She actively advocates for his dignity in a way he finds uncomfortable; he does not share her framing of his identity but has not rejected her
- **Ser Kaelen Thorn (Order of the Gauntlet):** Historical enmity; cautious, drink-brokered truce; Brewbarry views the Order as bullies and thieves
- **Valphine:** Worked in tandem during the Tower of Storms approach; he confirmed her instinct about the ambush

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase — Empathy toward Corbin:** Dropping his halberd, apologizing, and sharing his own pain directly caused Corbin's defection; a significant character moment demonstrating depth beyond combat
- **Increase — Tactical contribution:** Independently spotted the concealed ambush near the Tower of Storms, providing real value outside of melee
- **Neutral tension — Identity conflict with Adabra:** Ongoing discomfort around how others define him; unresolved, suggests future arc potential
