# Aletra Satora

## Identity
- **Role:** Smuggler and operative for an unnamed boss; affiliated with House Sartorra's (Xorlarrin's) illegal interdimensional poison trade
- **Faction:** House Sartorra / House Xorlarrin poison operations; works under an unidentified superior, possibly referred to as "KP"
- **Family:** Fourth daughter of the Satora family; younger sister of party member Valphine
- **First Appearance:** Discovered standing calmly beside the malfunctioning planar machine in the lower-level machine room, rapier in one hand, typing on a glowing keypad with the other, four spectral spider constructs clinging to the ceiling above her

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Extract a shipment of interdimensional Midnight Tears poison from the malfunctioning machine; complete a delivery for her unnamed boss on a tight deadline; leave no evidence and no witnesses
- **Drives:** Profit over principle — she exposed traitors for money, not honor, and negotiated with a king not to help him but to exploit his envy as cover for her smuggling operation
- Aletra is cold, calculating, and openly condescending, particularly toward Valphine and surface dwellers generally. She weaponizes charm and dark humor, trading cutting remarks mid-combat without losing focus on her objective. She is pragmatic to the point of ruthlessness — she is implied to have killed the Chief Accountant for raising inconvenient questions, and she sacrificed Brewbarry as a decoy without hesitation or apology.

## History with the Party

**First Encounter — The Machine Room (Standoff)**
The party found Aletra calmly working the malfunctioning planar machine. Valphine identified her by the Satora family scent — lavender perfume and chemical bleach — before seeing her face. Aletra addressed Valphine in Undercommon with characteristic condescension, dismissing the party as surface dwellers who should stay out of the way while "the adults" handled their deadline. She presented a coercive offer: kill the four spectral spider constructs so she could safely extract her cargo, and she would provide the override code to shut the machine down. She warned that refusal might force her to attempt the extraction anyway, risking an explosion that would destroy the entire keep.

**Second Encounter — The Machine Room (Battle)**
When the party returned, six Rift Weavers now guarded the machine. Aletra framed this as a "spatial equilibrium" — no extraction, no reason to destroy the machine. Partway through the confrontation, she aimed her wand at Brewbarry, marking him with a red glow that caused the machine to identify him as a spatial anomaly, redirecting all six Rift Weavers onto him. She openly acknowledged the manipulation to Valphine, calling back to a past trap the sisters had set together using a stuffed creature as bait. Throughout the fight she traded barbs with Valphine — referencing their mother's low opinion of Valphine's sharpness, darkly joking about collecting body parts, and needling Valphine about a previous encounter where she had done nothing but simper and cry. She successfully extracted a box of vials — Midnight Tears poison — but as she stepped through a self-opened portal, Valphine struck her with a blinding curse. She fumbled and dropped several vials, leaving them behind. Her parting words: *"The boss will not be happy with this."* She escaped through the portal; destination unknown.

**After Her Escape — Quarters Search**
The party searched Aletra's makeshift quarters in an old barracks after the interdimensional device collapsed into the void. They found a high-quality silk bedroll and a folding Zurkhwood desk. A ledger showed she had been forging inventory records — crossing out legitimate entries and substituting contraband notations (e.g., *"Midnight Tears - crate 4 - do not drop"*). A hidden bag contained a platinum coin, a malachite gem, a bottle of fine wine, and four vials of Midnight Tears (total value: 2,000 gp). Her annotated copy of the *Operator's Guide to the Model 1489 Spatial Rectification Calliope* included the note *"use only if KP isn't watching,"* indicating she operates under some oversight. The party took the forged ledger and the manual as evidence.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Escaped through a self-opened interdimensional portal; destination unknown
- **Active Plans:** Delivering (a now-reduced) shipment of Midnight Tears to her unnamed boss; managing fallout from the botched extraction
- **What the Party Knows:** She is Valphine's sister; she ran an interdimensional smuggling operation through the planar machine; she works for an unnamed boss; she is tied to House Sartorra / Xorlarrin poison operations; she dropped several vials and is aware the boss will be displeased
- **What Remains Hidden:** The identity of her boss; the identity of "KP" and what oversight role they play; her portal destination; the full scope of the smuggling network

## Relationships
- **Valphine (party member):** Sisters, deeply antagonistic. Aletra holds Valphine in contempt — calling back to their mother's dismissive comments and a previous encounter where Valphine allegedly did nothing. She is nonetheless familiar with Valphine's capabilities and history, and referenced past collaborations (the stuffed-creature trap). Valphine successfully cursed her on the way out, costing her part of the shipment — the first time, Aletra noted, that Valphine had actually tried to stop her rather than simply crying
- **Brewbarry (party member):** Used as a disposable decoy without hesitation or remorse
- **The King of the Fortress:** Manipulated via his envy of Lionshield Coster revenues; promised the machine would solve his profit problems; it didn't
- **The Chief Accountant:** Raised concerns about unbalanced books; implied to have been killed by Aletra shortly after
- **Unnamed Boss:** Aletra answers to this figure and feared their reaction to the lost vials; identity unknown
- **"KP":** Referenced in her personal notes as someone whose attention she was avoiding; possibly a supervisor or rival within her organization

## Arc Score Events
- **Decrease — Valphine:** Condescension, use of Brewbarry as a decoy, mid-combat taunts about their mother and body parts, and the implication of past betrayal or inaction
- **Decrease — Party (general):** Coerced the party under threat of a keep-destroying explosion; manipulated them throughout the encounter; escaped cleanly with most of her cargo
