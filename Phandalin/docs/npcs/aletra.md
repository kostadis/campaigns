# Aletra Satora

## Identity
- **Role:** Smuggler and operative for an unnamed boss; affiliated with House Sartorra's (Xorlarrin's) illegal interdimensional poison trade
- **Faction:** House Sartorra / House Xorlarrin poison operations; works under an unidentified superior, possibly referred to as "KP"
- **Family:** Fourth daughter of the Satora family; younger sister of party member Valphine
- **First Appearance:** Discovered standing calmly beside the malfunctioning planar machine in the lower-level machine room, rapier in one hand, typing on a glowing keypad with the other, four spectral spider constructs clinging to the ceiling above her

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Extract a shipment of interdimensional Midnight Tears poison from the malfunctioning machine; complete a delivery for her unnamed boss on a tight deadline; leave no evidence and no witnesses
- **Drives:** Profit over principle — she exposed traitors for money, not honor, and negotiated with a king not to help him but to exploit his envy as cover for her smuggling operation
- Aletra is cold, calculating, and openly condescending, particularly toward Valphine and surface dwellers generally. She weaponizes charm and dark humor, trading cutting remarks mid-combat without losing focus on her objective. She is pragmatic to the point of ruthlessness — she is implied to have killed the Chief Accountant for raising inconvenient questions, and she sacrificed Brewbarry as a decoy without hesitation or apology.
- **Physical Description:** Sharp cheekbones and cold violet eyes; carries the distinctive Satora family scent—lavender perfume mixed with chemical bleach from the family's poison labs

## History with the Party

**First Encounter — The Machine Room (Standoff)**
The party found Aletra calmly working the malfunctioning planar machine in the lower-level machine room. She was typing on a glowing keypad with one hand and holding a rapier in the other, with four spectral spiders made of flickering static clinging to the ceiling above her. Valphine identified her before seeing her face, recognizing the Satora family scent—lavender perfume mixed with chemical bleach from the family's poison labs—and confirmed her identity upon seeing her sharp cheekbones and cold violet eyes. The Chief Accountant had revealed Aletra's background: she had negotiated directly with the king of the fortress, exploiting his envy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant revenues by promising the installed machine would solve his efficiency and profit problems. Instead the machine broke and caused the chaos now afflicting the keep. Aletra had laughed when the Chief Accountant raised concerns about the unbalanced books, and is implied to have killed her shortly after. Valphine discerned that Aletra's true purpose at the machine was extracting drow poison she had been smuggling through the multiverse using the device. Aletra addressed Valphine in Undercommon with characteristic condescension, dismissing the party as surface dwellers who should stay out of the way while "the adults" handled their deadline. She presented a coercive offer: kill the four spectral spider constructs so she could safely extract her cargo, and she would provide the override code to shut the machine down. She warned that refusal might force her to attempt the extraction anyway, risking an explosion that would destroy the entire keep.

**Second Encounter — The Machine Room (Battle)**
When the party returned, six Rift Weavers now guarded the machine. Aletra framed this as a "spatial equilibrium," warning the party that if she couldn't retrieve her package she had no reason to destroy the machine, leaving everyone to suffer the consequences of its catastrophic failure. Partway through the confrontation, she aimed her wand at Brewbarry, marking him with a red glow that caused the machine to identify him as a spatial anomaly, redirecting all six Rift Weavers onto him. She openly acknowledged the manipulation to Valphine, shouting to simply keep Brewbarry alive long enough for her to finish, referencing a previous trap the sisters had set together using a stuffed creature as bait. Throughout the fight she traded barbs with Valphine — referencing their mother's low opinion of Valphine's sharpness ("mother said you weren't the sharpest tool in the shed"), darkly joking that she would hate to collect Valphine's body parts to return to their mother before clarifying she unfortunately couldn't leave any evidence behind, and needling Valphine about a previous encounter in which Valphine had been moaning (ambiguously suggesting pain or pleasure), while acknowledging that Valphine at least tried to stop her this time rather than simpering and crying. She successfully extracted a box of vials—Midnight Tears poison, identified as part of House Sartorra's illegal drug trade—but as she stepped through a self-opened portal, Valphine struck her with a blinding curse. She fumbled and dropped several vials, leaving them behind. Her parting words: *"The boss will not be happy with this."* She escaped through the portal; destination unknown.

**After Her Escape — Quarters Search**
Aletra had fled the area before the party arrived to search her quarters, escaping after the interdimensional device collapsed into the void. The party searched her makeshift quarters in an old barracks. They found a high-quality silk bedroll and a folding Zurkhwood desk covered in modern parchment, identified as belonging to House Xorlarrin's poison operations by the lingering scent of lavender and perfume. A ledger showed she had been forging inventory records—crossing out legitimate entries such as "iron for 500 pounds" and replacing them with contraband notations (e.g., *"Midnight Tears - crate 4 - do not drop"*). A hidden bag contained a platinum coin, a malachite gem, a bottle of fine wine, and four vials of Midnight Tears (total value: 2,000 gp). Her annotated copy of the *Operator's Guide to the Model 1489 Spatial Rectification Calliope* included the note *"use only if KP isn't watching,"* indicating she operates under some oversight, possibly from a figure known as KP. The party took the forged ledger and the manual as evidence.

- **Last Known Location:** Escaped through a self-opened interdimensional portal; destination unknown; current whereabouts unknown
- **Active Plans:** Delivering (a now-reduced) shipment of Midnight Tears to her unnamed boss; managing fallout from the botched extraction
- **What the Party Knows:** She is Valphine's sister; she ran an interdimensional smuggling operation through the planar machine; she works for an unnamed boss; she is tied to House Sartorra / Xorlarrin poison operations; she dropped several vials and is aware the boss will be displeased; her access to the machine, her familiarity with its dwarven construction, and her ability to open an interdimensional portal suggest significant resources and organizational backing

## Relationships
- **Valphine (party member):** Sisters, deeply antagonistic. Aletra holds Valphine in contempt—calling back to their mother's dismissive comments ("mother said you weren't the sharpest tool in the shed") and a previous encounter where Valphine allegedly did nothing but simper and cry. She is nonetheless familiar with Valphine's capabilities and history, and referenced past collaborations (the stuffed-creature trap). Valphine successfully cursed her on the way out, costing her part of the shipment—the first time, Aletra noted, that Valphine had actually tried to stop her rather than simply crying
- **Brewbarry (party member):** Used as a disposable decoy without hesitation or remorse
- **The King of the Fortress:** Manipulated via his envy of Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant revenues; promised the machine would solve his efficiency and profit problems; it didn't
- **The Chief Accountant:** Raised concerns about unbalanced books; Aletra laughed at her concerns; implied to have been killed by Aletra shortly after
- **Unnamed Boss:** Aletra answers to this figure and feared their reaction to the lost vials; identity unknown
- **"KP":** Referenced in her personal notes as someone whose attention she was avoiding ("use only if KP isn't watching"); possibly a supervisor or rival within her organization
