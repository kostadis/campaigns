# Sridar

## Identity
- **Role:** Independent artifact collector; self-identified interventionist (opposed to Emerald Enclave's non-interference stance)
- **First appearance:** Falcon Hunting Lodge, where she was staying as a guest when the party encountered her

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Collecting and studying artifacts connected to planar disruptions; understanding the cause and pattern of planar incursions
- Scholarly and analytical — she originally theorized the incursions were natural phenomena before entertaining the possibility of deliberate interference
- Pragmatic and direct: pays fairly for useful information and goods, and offers reciprocal arrangements
- Her interventionist stance suggests she intends to act on what she learns, not merely observe

## History with the Party
- Met the party at the Falcon Hunting Lodge; was briefly confused when Valphine asked if she was a cleric of Lathander, suggesting she may be unfamiliar with the ruse or testing its meaning
- Paid the party 30 gp for detailed accounts of planar events they had witnessed, including a creature collecting strange items
- Purchased the **Talisman of Moesko** for 100 gp after immediately identifying its planar connection; also arranged three bottles of wine from Falcon for Brewbarry as part of the deal (notably pleasing Vukradin as an example of fair-trade gold)
- Engaged in speculation about the cause of planar incursions; found Brewbarry's theory that events are **intentional rather than coincidental** intriguing and worth pursuing
- Offered to help the party interpret future findings and piece together clues
- **Departed the lodge overnight**, before the party left for the Woodland Manse — no farewell, no stated destination

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Falcon Hunting Lodge (departed overnight; whereabouts now unknown)
- **Active plans:** Likely pursuing leads on the planar incursions; may be independently investigating the intentionality angle raised by Brewbarry
- **What the party knows:** Her name, her collector role, her interventionist philosophy, and that she left quietly
- **What remains hidden:** Her true affiliation (if any), her destination, why she left without a word, and how she recognized the Talisman of Moesko so readily

## Relationships
- **The party (general):** Transactional but warm; a potential ally who offered future collaboration
- **Brewbarry:** Took particular interest in their theory about intentional planar interference
- **Vukradin:** Viewed favorably as an honest trading partner
- **Emerald Enclave:** Ideologically opposed — she explicitly rejects their non-interference naturalism
- **Falcon (lodge host):** Known to him as a guest; arranged wine through him as part of her deal
