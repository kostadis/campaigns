# The Carver

## Identity
- **Role:** Criminal overlord (apparent); true nature: a sentient obsidian sword, an alien intelligence using humanoid vessels as instruments
- **Faction:** Unknown — operated through intermediaries including a middleman named Jax and an unnamed human veteran vessel
- **First appearance:** Referenced by name only via a surrendered bandit's testimony; later described by Sister Kaella; true nature revealed during the confrontation at Icespire Hold

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Binding and dominating Cryovain the white dragon; using the dragon as a weapon to burn Phandalin as a demonstration of power
- The Carver was patient and methodical, building toward a slow ritual of consumption — but abandoned preparation instantly when interrupted, seizing domination by force rather than adapt to obstruction
- It communicated in contempt: *"Coward's magic. You mute the ritual because you lack the strength to shatter the vessel."* Proud, strategic, and reactive to perceived disrespect
- Spoke in a tongue that caused ears to bleed; was described by Sister Kaella as no longer fully human (a description that, in retrospect, reflected the hollowed vessel rather than the sword itself)
- Functioned as an alien intelligence using an obsidian weapon — specifically a mental amplifier — to project its will and domination

## History with the Party

**Early sessions — The name surfaces**
The party learned of "the Carver" from a surrendered bandit, who described him as "the big boss" and physically large. The bandit had never met him directly, operating through a middleman named Jax. The bandit explicitly blamed the Carver for his circumstances ("It's the Carver's fault"), identifying him as the organizing force behind regional banditry. Location and nature unknown at this stage.

**Pre-confrontation — Sister Kaella's warning**
Sister Kaella reported that the Carver — still believed to be a person — was no longer fully human, described as a hollow shell that speaks to his blade in a tongue that causes ears to bleed. He was actively marching on Icespire Hold with the intent to perform a dark chant to bind the white dragon. His stated plan following that binding: use the dragon like a suit of armor and burn Phandalin to the ground as a test of his new power.

**Icespire Hold — The confrontation**
The party arrived to find the human vessel performing a binding ritual over the chained Cryovain inside a burning geometric summoning circle on the rooftop. The ritual needed three more rounds to complete. Vukradin dropped Silence over the circle, choking the ritual entirely. The vessel stepped cleanly out of the silence zone without drawing attacks — precise, legendary footwork that marked it as no ordinary opponent.

Vukradin then cast Hold Person, paralyzing the vessel mid-motion. With the vessel stopped and silence dropped, the sword spoke directly into the party's minds, abandoning the ritual of slow consumption and dissolving Cryovain's chains immediately to seize direct domination — the expedient path, bypassing all prior preparation. 

During the battle, Vukradin's arcana knowledge revealed the truth: the black obsidian sword — glowing purple with accumulated power — was the Carver itself. The human veteran was merely a vessel, a body wearing the intelligence and will of the sword. The veteran was weakened by Soma's bear claws and moonbeam, and then killed by Brewbarry's halberd.

After completing the domination ritual, the sword went visually inert: no longer glowing purple, no glow, no presence, simply black and still — apparently emptied by the effort to achieve what it wanted.

**Resolution**
When Cryovain was killed and the circle broke, the Carver's essence dissipated instantly and completely — not fled, not retreated, but unmade by the same stroke that unmade its vessel. Its obsidian sword remained. The crystal pommel gem was identified as capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience — a property that deeply tempted Vukradin, though he recognized the gem as belonging to a rightful owner and refused to keep it despite the instrument being perfectly suited to his ambitions.

## Current Status
- **The Carver:** Destroyed — dissipated completely upon Cryovain's death, unmade by the same stroke
- **The obsidian sword:** Remains at Icespire Hold; visually inert, pommel gem intact
- **The human vessel:** Killed during the confrontation by Brewbarry's halberd after being weakened by Soma's attacks and paralyzed by Vukradin's Hold Person
- **The pommel gem:** Recognized by Vukradin as belonging to someone else; not claimed; capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience

## Relationships
- **Jax:** Middleman who relayed the Carver's criminal operations to street-level bandits; no further detail in notes
- **The human vessel:** Unnamed veteran; served as physical host for the sword's intelligence; tactical competence reflects the sword's own capability; killed by Brewbarry during the Icespire Hold confrontation
- **Cryovain:** Target and instrument — the Carver needed the dragon to execute its endgame; its destruction ended the Carver entirely
- **Vukradin:** Primary magical antagonist during the confrontation; the Carver addressed him directly with contempt; Vukradin's arcana knowledge revealed the sword's true nature mid-battle; deeply tempted by the pommel gem but chose to return it to its rightful owner
- **Sister Kaella:** Provided the party their most detailed intelligence on the Carver prior to confrontation
- **Soma:** Weakened the human vessel with bear claws and moonbeam during the confrontation
- **Brewbarry:** Delivered the killing blow to the human vessel with his halberd
