# New notes for Soma (from dossier_extract_044.md)

Soma fought in elk form for the majority of the battle at Falcon's Lodge, maintaining the Moonbeam and directing Boney's positioning throughout.

She tracked Boney's movement geometry aloud during the planning phase, confirming that looping him around the back wall to cover the Anchorites would require a full push. When Vukradin misread this as Boney needing to die, she corrected him without inflection: "To get back there without drawing a strike, yeah, that's a full push."

In combat, she held the Moonbeam over multiple targets sequentially — repositioning it over Anchorite Number 2 and Gorthok — and connected with a hoof strike on Gorthok that she described with brief satisfaction: "Get hooved, baby." She stripped Anchorite Number 2 to near-nothing through a combination of Boney's opportunity strike and the Moonbeam's lingering burn.

Against Gorthok, she rolled a twenty-nine on the Moonbeam (two tens and a nine), which was halved by Gorthok's resistance but still left him barely alive. She then announced that her hoof strike would kill him unless she rolled double ones — and immediately recognized she had cursed herself. She did not roll double ones. Gorthok died to her hoof, then revived via a stored Revivify-like effect. The Moonbeam was still positioned over him and finished the job at the end of his turn.

Gorthok hit Soma twice in succession, dropping her out of elk form. The Moonbeam extinguished when she fell. She confirmed its loss to Vukradin flatly: "Yep. Moonbeam goes away." She noted that she lay flat on the ground with Meril's staff somewhere on the dirt nearby — five leaves, none reachable from that position — and conducted a quiet internal accounting of what she had left. "Not much. But I was still inside it."

After the battle, she objected to Falcon shooting at fleeing orcs ("Such senseless violence") and objected again to Prutha's axe work ("Guys, calm down"), calling both unnecessary. She engaged Vukradin briefly on the point — "there's a well of orcs, there are always more orcs beyond the horizon" — without full resolution. She did not sing during the post-battle celebration but stood and listened as Vukradin and Valphine harmonized with the orc war party, and thought of Meril: "Maybe he knew something."

She offered "To steal their life, not their treasure" after Vukradin's parting remark about the treasure, noting herself that it was advice for a different situation entirely. She observed Vukradin's listing of new workings and called the door-to-elsewhere ability "always nice."

At the end of the session she sat on a section of ruined fence, felt new depth and reach she didn't have before — described as feeling weather coming in, a readiness not yet given — and remained there as dusk settled.

Last known status: seated at Falcon's Lodge at dusk, alive, sensing new capability.
