# Lyra

## Identity
- **Role:** Low-ranking member of the Emerald Enclave; field leader of a small intervention team
- **Faction:** Emerald Enclave (interventionist faction)
- **First Appearance:** Leader of a group of Enclave members trapped in the Whispering Grove by blighted brambles; called out to the party upon their arrival and urged them to fight the corrupted Treant

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protect the Whispering Grove and natural places from planar corruption; advance her standing within the Emerald Enclave; prove herself as a capable leader
- **Drives:** Personal affection for the Whispering Grove motivated her to act unilaterally when the Enclave was paralyzed by internal debate; she is willing to take risks but prone to poor planning
- Lyra is passionate and principled, aligning with the interventionist position that planar corruption is alien and must be cleansed. She can be hot-tempered, as shown by her screaming confrontation with Adabra. She is also self-aware enough to admit fault — "I don't think I thought this through very carefully" — though this candor has not yet translated into more cautious decision-making.

## History with the Party

**Session 1 – The Whispering Grove**
Lyra and her agents were trapped by blighted brambles when the party arrived. Freed by Soma's attack, she distributed ritual stones and explained the sealing ritual mechanics. She attempted to assist in the fight against the corrupted Treant but was re-ensnared by its vines. The ritual failed, causing the planar wells to expand. Questioned by the party, she provided detailed exposition on the Emerald Enclave's ideological schism (naturalists vs. interventionists), tracing it to the War of the Dragons and Delaan Winterhound's decision to follow the hero Malfire into fighting Tiamat's summoning. She confirmed the Treant had killed and eaten people. The party subsequently withdrew from the grove.

**Session 2 – Phandalin Main Square**
Lyra arrived at the main square simultaneously with the party during an encounter with Adabra, accompanied by her Enclave agents. She was visibly furious with Adabra, calling her insane and dangerous. Vukradin repeatedly deflected her attempts to engage, which further enraged her. She ultimately left the square, delivering a parting condemnation of the party as fools for not seeing Adabra's danger. Later, through her agents Thomas and Marian at the Stonehill Tavern, it was revealed that Lyra had pursued the grove ritual entirely without authorization from her superiors, motivated by personal attachment to the grove and frustration at the Enclave's inaction. A promotion had apparently been contingent on the ritual's success. Her agents acknowledged she had acted for herself rather than the Enclave, and that she was left "very upset" by the total failure of her mission.

**Session 3 – Rescue Credit Scheme**
The party devised a plan to credit the rescue of a missing Emerald Enclave patrol to Lyra in order to elevate her standing within the faction. Lyra did not appear directly but acted on the arrangement — one rescued patrol member confirmed she had sent the party to find them. Her influence was felt through their testimony.

**Session 4 – Reunion in Phandalin**
Lyra reunited with the two rescued Enclave members. Despite this success, she remained deeply discouraged, citing the treant failure and her men's capture as evidence of her poor leadership. Vukradin offered encouragement; one rescued agent revealed the party had told him it was Lyra's plan that saved him. Heartened, she agreed to let Vukradin help vouch for her competence at an upcoming Emerald Enclave meeting scheduled for the following day.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Phandalin, preparing for an Emerald Enclave organizational meeting
- **Active Plans:** Allowing Vukradin to assist in rehabilitating her reputation before the Enclave; positioning herself for advancement within the organization
- **What the Party Knows:** Lyra acted unilaterally on the grove ritual without Enclave sanction; a promotion was riding on its success; she is personally discouraged but being deliberately cultivated as a faction ally
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent to which the Enclave leadership knows of or disapproves of her unauthorized action; whether her superiors are aware of the party's role in the patrol rescue and how credit is being framed

## Relationships
- **Vukradin:** The party member most actively invested in her advancement; has offered her encouragement and arranged to vouch for her at the Enclave meeting. She has had friction with him (he deflected her during the Adabra confrontation) but the relationship has since warmed considerably.
- **Soma:** Engaged her most substantively in conversation at the grove; drew out her exposition on the Enclave's schism and the Treant's history.
- **Valphine:** Questioned her judgment at the grove alongside Vukradin.
- **Adabra:** Lyra considers her dangerous and fanatical; regards the party's tolerance of Adabra as foolish.
- **Thomas and Marian:** Her Enclave agents who were trapped with her at the grove; they later spoke candidly about her failures and motivations at the tavern.
- **Delaan Winterhound:** Historical Enclave leader whose decision to fight Tiamat she used as a reference point for the interventionist/naturalist schism; not a personal relationship.
- **Emerald Enclave (as institution):** Her relationship is complicated — she is loyal in conviction but acted against organizational process; she is now dependent on party support to recover her standing.
