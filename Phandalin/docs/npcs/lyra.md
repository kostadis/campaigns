# Lyra

## Identity
- **Role:** Low-ranking member of the Emerald Enclave; field leader of a small intervention team
- **Faction:** Emerald Enclave (interventionist faction)
- **First Appearance:** Leader of a group of Enclave members trapped in the Whispering Grove by blighted brambles; called out to the party upon their arrival, identifying herself and her organization, explaining that the Treant had once been the Grove's Guardian but had been corrupted by a planar infection, and urging them to fight the creature and prevent the corruption from spreading

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protect the Whispering Grove and natural places from planar corruption; advance her standing within the Emerald Enclave; prove herself as a capable leader
- **Drives:** Personal affection for the Whispering Grove motivated her to act unilaterally when the Enclave was paralyzed by internal debate; she is willing to take risks but prone to poor planning
- Lyra is passionate and principled, aligning with the interventionist position that planar corruption is alien and must be cleansed. She can be hot-tempered, as shown by her screaming confrontation with Adabra. She is also self-aware enough to admit fault — "I don't think I thought this through very carefully" — though this candor has not yet translated into more cautious decision-making.
- She convinced her agents that the forest and the gods would protect them and that they would succeed in the grove ritual, demonstrating both her conviction and her tendency toward overconfidence.

## History with the Party

**Session 1 – The Whispering Grove**
Lyra and her agents were trapped by blighted brambles when the party arrived. She called out to the party, identifying herself and her organization, explaining that the Treant had once been the Grove's Guardian but had been corrupted by a planar infection, and urging them to fight the creature and prevent the corruption from spreading. Once freed by Soma's attack, she immediately produced four crude ritual stones and explained the mechanics of the sealing ritual: six successes required, with three failures meaning total defeat. She attempted a Moonbeam against the corrupted Treant but was re-ensnared by its vines in the process. When questioned by Vukradin and Valphine about whether she had planned to attempt such a complex ritual with so small a group, Lyra admitted candidly, "I don't think I thought this through very carefully." Under Soma's direct questioning, she provided the most substantive exposition of the session, explaining the ideological schism within the Emerald Enclave between naturalists and interventionists. She traced the fracture to the War of the Dragons, when Delaan Winterhound was persuaded by a hero named Malfire to commit the Enclave to fighting Tiamat's summoning. She noted that those who had opposed intervention later pointed to Tiamat's defeat as the catalyst for the War of the Giants and the shattering of the Ordning by the Giant gods. She described the naturalist position as accepting even planar intrusions as part of the multiverse's natural cycle, and the interventionist position as viewing such intrusions as alien corruption to be cleansed. She explained the specific history of the corrupted Treant: it had once been a good guardian that protected the forest until the planar scar emerged, after which it was no longer sustained by sunlight but by the planar pools. She confirmed that the Treant had eaten people. When the ritual ultimately failed, causing the planar wells to expand and grow more violent, Lyra was visibly distressed, whispering, "This did not work the way I had hoped." The party subsequently withdrew from the grove.

**Session 2 – Phandalin Main Square**
Lyra arrived at the main square simultaneously with the party during an encounter with Adabra, accompanied by her Enclave agents. She was furious throughout the encounter, at one point screaming "You are insane!" at Adabra. Vukradin repeatedly deflected her attempts to engage, which further enraged her. When he suggested they go listen to music, she refused and delivered a parting condemnation: "If you cannot see the evil this woman represents and her fanaticism and its danger, then you are all fools." She then left the square with most of her agents. Later, through her agents Thomas and Marian at the Stonehill Tavern, it was revealed that Lyra had discovered the ritual independently and chosen to implement it without bringing it to her Emerald Enclave superiors, because the organization was paralyzed by internal debate over whether any intervention was appropriate. She had a particular personal affection for the Whispering Grove and was frustrated that nothing was being done. The ritual failed and the group nearly died. Her agents acknowledged she had "done what was best for her" rather than for the Enclave, and that her quest — to save the tree and the forest — had entirely failed, leaving her "very upset." A promotion or advancement in status within the Enclave had also apparently been riding on the ritual's success.

**Session 3 – Rescue Credit Scheme**
The party devised a plan to credit the rescue of a missing Emerald Enclave patrol to Lyra in order to elevate her standing within the faction and cultivate her as a valuable ally. Lyra did not appear directly but acted on the arrangement — one rescued patrol member confirmed that it was Lyra who had sent the party to find them, indicating she had acted on the arrangement. Her influence was felt through the rescued patrol's testimony.

**Session 4 – Reunion in Phandalin**
Lyra reunited with the two rescued Enclave members in Phandalin. Despite this success, she remained deeply discouraged, citing the treant failure and her men's capture as evidence of her poor leadership. Vukradin offered encouragement; one rescued agent revealed the party had told him it was Lyra's plan that saved him. Heartened, she agreed to let Vukradin help vouch for her competence at an upcoming Emerald Enclave meeting scheduled for the following day.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Phandalin, preparing for an Emerald Enclave organizational meeting
- **Active Plans:** Allowing Vukradin to assist in rehabilitating her reputation before the Enclave; positioning herself for advancement within the organization
- **What the Party Knows:** Lyra acted unilaterally on the grove ritual without Enclave sanction; a promotion was riding on its success; she is personally discouraged but being deliberately cultivated as a faction ally
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent to which the Enclave leadership knows of or disapproves of her unauthorized action; whether her superiors are aware of the party's role in the patrol rescue and how credit is being framed

## Relationships
- **Vukradin:** The party member most actively invested in her advancement; has offered her encouragement and arranged to vouch for her at the Enclave meeting. She has had friction with him (he deflected her during the Adabra confrontation) but the relationship has since warmed considerably.
- **Soma:** Engaged her most substantively in conversation at the grove; drew out her exposition on the Enclave's schism and the Treant's history.
- **Valphine:** Questioned her judgment at the grove alongside Vukradin.
- **Adabra:** Lyra considers her dangerous and fanatical; regards the party's tolerance of Adabra as foolish.
- **Thomas and Marian:** Her Enclave agents who were trapped with her at the grove; they later spoke candidly about her failures and motivations at the tavern.
- **Delaan Winterhound:** Historical Enclave leader whose decision to fight Tiamat she used as a reference point for the interventionist/naturalist schism; not a personal relationship.
- **Malfire:** Historical hero who persuaded Delaan Winterhound to commit the Enclave to fighting Tiamat's summoning during the War of the Dragons; referenced in her exposition; not a personal relationship.
- **Emerald Enclave (as institution):** Her relationship is complicated — she is loyal in conviction but acted against organizational process; she is now dependent on party support to recover her standing.
