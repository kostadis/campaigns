# Ser Kaelen

## Identity
- **Role:** Knight, recruiter, and field operative of the Order of the Gauntlet; member of the Phandalin privy council
- **Faction:** Order of the Gauntlet (insignia: a gauntlet grasping a sword by the blade)
- **Appearance:** A man in his early thirties with dark hair, a clean-shaven face, a scar above his right eyebrow, and chain mail armor bearing the Order's emblem
- **First Appearance:** Approached Vukradin at the Stonehill Inn in Phandalin after hearing of his reputation and unconventional views on treasure and plunder

*Also known as: Ser Kaelen Thorn, Ser Kaelen Thorne, Sir Kaelen.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Advancing the Order of the Gauntlet's political influence, financial resources, and reputation; combating senseless violence and plunder across the North; using art and storytelling as tools for cultural change; building goodwill with useful individuals like Vukradin
- **Traits:** Pragmatic and politically calculating — his support is consistently framed around institutional benefit rather than personal loyalty. He is perceptive enough to absorb and reflect Vukradin's own language back at him, leaving it unclear whether this reflects genuine rapport or deliberate management. He counsels caution over confrontation and prefers structured, deniable solutions. Composed, confident, and deliberate — he carries himself with practiced authority that makes him seem larger than his modest frame. He reads people well and recruits strategically, approaching Vukradin specifically for his celestial origins, personal story, and musical voice. He has an embarrassed, self-deprecating side, as shown by his reluctant recitation of the Order's battle chant. He is quick to downplay threats he has underestimated and slower to admit he was wrong. When principles align with his institutional goals, he can speak with genuine conviction — such as his affirmation that no one could take Soma's staff, comparing it to his own sword. He is willing to interrupt public ceremonies when he judges the intelligence he carries to be sufficiently urgent.

## History with the Party

1. **Recruitment (Stonehill Inn):** Approached Vukradin with an offer of associate membership in the Order of the Gauntlet, framing the Order's mission around anti-plunder values, artistic outreach, and the legacy of the War of the Dragon. He presented the organization as a coalition of fighters, mages, storytellers, and clerics dedicated to improving the North—working against plunder and senseless killing, and using art to spread that message. He cited the Order's role in the War of the Dragon and named Milo Goodbarrel as the member who led the strike force that stopped the inner sanctum ritual. He assured Vukradin that funding comes from conflict-free donations and emphasized that Vukradin's celestial origins and personal story, combined with his musical voice, could reach tens of thousands. Vukradin accepted and received a membership pin. Extended an invitation to Valphine as well. Explicitly excluded Soma, citing incompatibility with the Order's worldview due to her naturalist perspective. He praised Vukradin's goals — effective given the bard's need for approval — and discussed music in the context of the Order. His vague, sighing response to questions about rightful ownership of recovered treasure did not reassure Vukradin.

2. **Brewbarry Encounter (Stonehill Inn, same evening):** Heard Brewbarry's accusation that the Order had bullied his tribe. Acknowledged the brutality of that war without deflecting blame or showing pride in it. Offered Brewbarry a drink as a gesture of goodwill and reconciliation.

3. **Lighthouse Intelligence (Barthen's Provisions, following morning):** Found preparing to depart Phandalin. When Vukradin raised the lighthouse situation, Kaelen responded positively and shared intelligence: the curse likely had one of three causes — a matter requiring a cleric, a haunting monster, or simple rumor. He assessed the monster explanation as most probable. He told Vukradin he was awaiting word from Neverwinter on the treasure's rightful owners (expected within days) and suggested the party occupy themselves with Mountain's Toe or the Logger's Camp in the meantime.

4. **Return with Elara and Jarek:** Present when the party returned to Phandalin escorting Elara and Jarek. Vukradin reported the incident to him, and he responded with genuine warmth, expressing that Vukradin's attention to good deeds on an individual level aligned with the broader values of the Order, affirming his decision to recruit Vukradin: "Bringing you into the fold was the right thing to do." When pressed for an Order chant, he stammered and produced an embarrassing verse: *"Steal and shield, Heart and band. We stand as one. A steadfast band."* He acknowledged it was poor and that Vukradin's talents could improve on it.

5. **Lighthouse Debrief and the Carver (Stonehill Tavern):** Congratulated the party on securing the lighthouse, though he admitted he had not believed there was genuine evil there and had dismissed the threat as rumor. When told the antagonist was a servant of Talos with a green-glowing heart, his expression turned to deep concern; he concluded no ordinary Talos shaman could accomplish such a thing and that a hidden force must be behind events. He deflected Vukradin's accusation that he had exposed the party's plans by demonstrating that everyone in the tavern had known of their destination. He revealed his current mission: locating a bandit called **the Carver**, who evades both magical detection and his own henchmen. He admitted he had deprioritized the Whispering Wood reports and Adabra's warnings, privately assuming Adabra had gone mad. He agreed to look into the rightful owners of treasure recovered from wrecked ships at the lighthouse. He clarified that the Order does not pay agents — it finds true believers — and noted the Harpers would pay for missions. He made clear his respect for the party's results while maintaining an air of authority over them.

6. **Harbin Affair:** After Harbin accused Vukradin, Kaelen claimed credit for pushing for a swift trial, framing it as protecting the Order's integrity. His support was self-serving, focused on the Order's reputation rather than genuine concern. When Vukradin moved toward open revolution, Kaelen pulled him aside and warned that removing Harbin would prompt Lord Nevermember to send troops — recommending a privy council solution instead. He told Jenna that Vukradin was a full-fledged Order member, a claim Vukradin disputed. Jenna raised his connection to House Margaster funding as a point against him.

7. **Council Meeting:** At the Phandalin privy council, Kaelen was the only member to take the Kraken Society threat seriously, noting suspicions of powerful psionic involvement. He also delivered Candlekeep research identifying the original owners of a magical item recovered from ships — framing it as a costly effort by the Order (considerable time and money) and using it as a relationship-building gesture with Vukradin, with promises of reward for returning the items while cautioning the process would not be straightforward.

8. **Statue Mission:** Approached the party with intelligence identifying a lighthouse statue as belonging to Elara Seasong Meliame, a wealthy art collector currently attending a gala in Neverwinter hosted by her eccentric cousin, Lord Cassian Meliame. He warned that House Margaster agents—a family notorious for demonic alliances—intended to intercept the statue to embarrass the Meliame family. He tasked the party with personally escorting it to Neverwinter and presenting it to Elara — with the explicit request that they credit the Order of the Gauntlet, in order to secure future donations.

9. **Statue Reminder:** Spoke briefly with the party prior to their departure from Phandalin. Reminded them of the unfulfilled obligation to return the statue. Brewbarry persuaded him that the gold mine delivery was a higher priority, citing a personal need for money to fund a music studio. Kaelen accepted this reasoning and did not press further.

10. **Sending Exchange:** Received Vukradin's Sending announcing the dragon's death and the Carver's death, and the party's intention to head to Neverwinter. His reply praised Torm, used Vukradin's own phrase "conflict-free triumph," and redirected the party to Phandalin — where he said the Gauntlet awaited them and noted "issues with the Emerald Enclave." The full message read: "Praise Torm! Your righteous victory brings light to the north. The gauntlet awaits your arrival in Phandalin. We shall celebrate your conflict-free triumph together." He followed with: "Well done. Come to Phandalin quick. Issues with the Emerald Enclave." Vukradin noted that Kaelen had studied and absorbed his vocabulary, feeding it back in twenty-five words, leaving him uncertain whether to be moved or suspicious. Vukradin suspected Kaelen had arranged something and already had a press release written.

11. **Druid Conclave Warning (Temple of Lathander):** Arrived at the edge of the celebration with clear urgency, sought out Soma, and brought the party to the Temple of Lathander for a private council. He delivered critical intelligence: Adabra Gwynn had discovered the truth about what happened at the Whispering Grove — the treant, the concealment — and was marshaling allies to convene a druid gathering. She intended to argue that the party's "reckless interventionism" had harmed the forests. Kaelen characterized Adabra as "confused," explaining she saw the harm caused by defenders against the dragons but mistakenly identified the defenders as the problem rather than the dragons themselves, because she never experienced the planar incursions directly. When asked whether the Lords' Alliance or Order of the Gauntlet could help, Kaelen admitted the Lords' Alliance didn't want to intervene, and the Order lacked the manpower after successive wars (the War of the Dragons, the War of the Giants, and others). He confirmed it was "all on" the party. He then raised the matter of Soma's staff, noting it held "standing" and symbolic significance among druids that could tip the balance at the conclave — but cautioned that some at the gathering had suggested evaluating whether Soma should even retain the staff. When Soma declared no one would take it, Kaelen affirmed her position with conviction, touching the hilt of his own sword and declaring that no order could take his sword either. He also noticed Brewbarry's halberd, complimenting it as "a very fine halberd" and offering to spar ("We should parry"), which Brewbarry declined.

12. **Interrupting Harbin's Festival:** Broke the pageantry of Harbin's welcome festival by drawing Soma aside with quiet urgency to deliver bad news. He briefed the party on Adabra's political maneuvering within the Emerald Enclave — specifically that Adabra was moving to ostracize Lyra from the druidic order and was building a case against Soma as a dangerous interventionist who held Meril's Staff without the Enclave's blessing. His briefing set the stage for the party's journey to the druid council to defend Lyra.

- **Active Plans:** Managing the party's integration into Order operations; addressing the developing conflict with the Emerald Enclave and Adabra Gwynn regarding the Whispering Grove incident and Adabra's efforts to ostracize Lyra and challenge Soma's possession of Meril's Staff; the statue obligation remains open
