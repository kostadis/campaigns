# Vukradin

## Identity
- **Role:** Bard; entrepreneur and aspiring studio founder
- **Faction:** Loosely affiliated with the Emerald Enclave (invoked instrumentally); acquainted with the Church of Lathander
- **First Appearance:** Gnomengarde — arrived with the party seeking a device to aid against the dragon on behalf of Townmaster Harbin Wester

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Build and fund a personal music studio; stage a revival of a legendary Triboar play featuring a demon character named Bob; accumulate wealth through what he considers ethical means ("organic, conflict-free, fair-trade gold")
- Vukradin is intellectually assertive — he argues from logic, dismantles others' reasoning, and takes genuine offense when his ideas are dismissed as mere poetry
- He is calculating in his charm: inviting surrendered bandits to dinner, leveraging faction identities, and using music as a social tool
- He is deeply financially motivated but maintains a personal ethical code around looting and fair dealing, which occasionally conflicts with the party's pragmatism
- Refuses payment derived from looting monsters; insists on ethical wealth sources to fund his studio
- Can be gentle and supportive when offering help (as with Teega)
- Exhibits both verbal confidence in combat and practical frustration when his spells fail repeatedly

## History with the Party

**Gnomengarde (Entrance & Mushrooms)**
- Looted mushrooms from the pool islands at Gnomengarde's entrance, identifying red oily mushrooms of unclear use, green mushrooms suitable for flour, and purple mushrooms fermentable into wine
- Insisted on checking behind the waterfall for a secret treasure room, finding nothing
- Delivered the killing blow on a fleeing ogre, justifying it to Soma by stating the ogre hated music and had likely stolen treasure from others

**Gnomengarde (Entry Negotiations)**
- Led negotiations with guards Pog and Ulla, countering their shapeshifter accusations with a logical argument about the intent behind standing orders, which partially impressed the male gnome Pog
- Played music for Pog and Ulla, earning their approval
- After being directed to the inventors' workshop via a route through spinning blade traps, reported the blade room as a death trap to Ulla, who revealed an alternate northern route

**Gnomengarde (Facktore's Crossbow)**
- In the large northern room, Vukradin and the party were attacked by an automatic rotating crossbow device built by the gnome Facktore
- Chose to disable the device rather than kill Facktore, who fled during the encounter

**Gnomengarde (Inventors' Workshop)**
- Explained the party's mission to Fibblestib and Dabbledob — seeking a device to help fight the dragon on behalf of Townmaster Harbin Wester
- Proposed using music to treat King Korboz's madness and asked about a piano, prompting Fibblestib to offer to build a modified version

**Gnomengarde (Royal Negotiations & Mimic)**
- At King Korboz's door, announced himself and was immediately accused of being a shapeshifter by the king
- Negotiated with co-King Gnercli, who offered a hat of wizardry in exchange for eliminating the shapeshifter
- Objected to the reward's limited utility against a dragon but accepted the deal after Soma reframed the terms
- Led the group back through the spinning blade room, this time disabling the blades, and proceeded toward the cellar
- During the mimic fight in the cellar, taunted the mimic with declarations of confidence ("We have it outnumbered! It's stunned! We shall kill it before it lays a hand on us!") while his spells repeatedly failed to affect it
- Used verbal invective with an underlying enchantment that visibly caused the mimic to flinch
- Mid-battle, engaged Soma in a philosophical debate about naturalist druidism, Aasimar, and Tieflings, taking offense when Soma dismissed his "poetic license" in describing celestial-touched beings
- Afterward, questioned Soma's decision to take gold from the chests, declaring himself already rich due to owning sending stones

**Gnomengarde (Royal Audience)**
- In the audience with King Korboz and King Gnercli, methodically dismantled the logic of their dual monarchy through pointed questioning—challenging how decisions are made when kings disagree, what happens when a new king is elected, and whether seniority exists
- Successfully drove a wedge between the two kings, with Korboz erupting that he deserved preferential treatment as the senior king
- Cut off the chaos he created by demanding the promised magical hat, receiving it along with a dismissal from an enraged Gnercli

**Journey to Umbrage Hill**
- Initially pushed to negotiate higher pay than 25 gold for rescuing Adabra, motivated explicitly by wanting to fund his studio ("my studio is going to take forever to build at this rate"), before being overruled
- During a bandit ambush, was briefly paralyzed by shock, then sprinted after and killed a fleeing bandit at Valphine's crossbow-wounded prompting
- Invited a surrendered, frightened bandit to dinner to extract information about their boss, the Carver, displaying calculated charm
- Reminded Soma that a druidic scroll taken from the bandits should be returned to its rightful owner

**Umbrage Hill**
- Confronted Corbin upon meeting him, recognizing stolen armor from a dead man and commanding him to remove it before relenting and promising to find him "proper fair trade armor"
- Led the group up Umbrage Hill toward the windmill where Adabra was trapped, insisting on quiet approach
- During the manticore confrontation, attempted to negotiate with the creature, offering payment
- Tried twice to affect the manticore with a spell — both attempts failed, leaving him frustrated and briefly blaming Soma for encouraging his second attempt
- After Brewbarry killed the manticore, cut off its head and presented it to Adabra as proof of the creature's death
- Repeatedly interrogated Adabra about whether Corbin had robbed her, and questioned her ownership of the dwarven house, accusing her of entitlement
- Attempted to convince Adabra to return to Phandalin through music, which she praised highly but which did not change her resolve
- Ultimately secured Adabra's agreement to accompany them by framing the mission as one undertaken by fellow naturalists of the Emerald Enclave

**Woodland Manse**
- Reunited with Falcon, who was visibly relieved and particularly pleased to see him safe
- Recounted Teega's rescue story with moderate success
- Took a gentle approach with Teega, offering her a position with the Church of Lathander
- Persuaded Falcon to provide free accommodation for the whole party by invoking their future business partnership

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Woodland Manse clearing, mid-combat
- **Active Plans:** Funding and constructing his music studio; intends to travel to Triboar to locate the script for the legendary demon-Bob play
- **What the Party Knows:** He is financially motivated, ethically particular about wealth sources, and an effective negotiator and social manipulator
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full nature of his sending stones and who he uses them to contact; the precise scope of his partnership with Falcon

## Relationships
- **Soma:** Recurring philosophical tension — debates on naturalism, celestial beings, and druidic ethics; Soma's pragmatism (taking loot, dismissing his "poetic license") irritates him
- **Falcon:** Warm, established prior relationship; Falcon was notably relieved at his safety; Vukradin is leveraging this into a business partnership that includes free accommodation
- **Corbin:** Morally suspicious of him (stolen armor); made a promise to find him proper fair-trade armor
- **Adabra:** Tense — questioned her entitlement and ownership; ultimately cooperated through shared Emerald Enclave framing
- **Valphine:** Prompted him to pursue the fleeing bandit; functional field cooperation
- **Brewbarry:** Benefited from Brewbarry killing the manticore; no noted conflict
- **The Carver (bandit boss):** Has secondhand intelligence; actively seeking more information
- **Teega:** Offered her gentle support and a position with the Church of Lathander
- **Pog and Ulla (Gnomengarde guards):** Impressed them with logic and music to gain entry
- **Fibblestib and Dabbledob (gnome inventors):** Explained party's mission to them; prompted Fibblestib's piano offer
- **King Korboz and King Gnercli:** Systematically destabilized their dual monarchy through philosophical questioning; received hat of wizardry from Gnercli during an enraged dismissal
- **Facktore (gnome inventor):** Chose to disable rather than destroy his crossbow device
