# Vukradin

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Aasimar Bard; aspiring theatrical producer and studio founder; recruited adventurer operating in the Phandalin hinterlands under Lord Neverember's vanguard program; active Lathander missionary
- **Faction:** Loosely affiliated with the Emerald Enclave (used as a persuasive framing device); aligned with the Church of Lathander and actively converting followers; nominally aligned with Neverember's initiative, deeply skeptical of it
- **First Appearance:** Performing unpaid at the First Flophouse in Heapside, Neverwinter; recruited by a Wintershield watchman on 8/3 of Ches, 1495; formally joined the party on 1/1 of Taraksh

*Also known as: Vukardin.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Fund and construct a personal studio; stage a revival of a legendary Triboar play featuring a demon named Bob; earn honest coin; gain recognition for his original music; avoid participating in what he views as state-sanctioned banditry; convert followers to Lathander
- Moralistic and argumentative — he pushed back on Neverember's scheme at every stage before accepting out of financial desperation, and harangued the townmaster publicly after collecting a legitimate quest reward
- Conflict-averse in combat, repeatedly attempting to parley with orcs and ogres with disastrous results; his bravado in combat frequently outpaces his spellcasting reliability, and he covers failed magic with confident declarations and verbal aggression
- Genuinely passionate about music; negotiates lodging and payment through performance, plays original compositions for anyone who will listen, and was persuaded to accept sending stones partly for their musical utility
- Intellectually combative — dismantles arguments, drives wedges in negotiations, and rarely lets a flawed premise go unchallenged; capable of genuine charm but deploys it with visible calculation
- Holds strong aesthetic and moral opinions: objects to looting monsters, insists on returning stolen property to rightful owners, takes personal offense when his "poetic license" is dismissed
- Committed to conversion over killing; advocates for non-lethal options and calls out to fleeing enemies urging them toward Lathander; believes in working on the spectacle of religious presentation
- Genuinely sincere in his Lathander evangelism, capable of connecting theological concepts to material reality in ways that resonate; showed complete sincerity when celebrating his first convert
- Has a habit of redirecting attacks meant for him into advantages for allies — described as "the thing he always does"
- Objects strongly to dehumanizing language and treatment of converts, insisting on respect and proper names
- Does not intervene in all behaviors he disapproves of, particularly regarding corpse desecration
- Carries unresolved thoughts about Corbin; recognizes parallels between Corbin and others who survive through compromise
- Named a deity other than Lathander before correcting himself mid-speech; the slip was observed by Valphine
- Narrates his own successes with visible satisfaction
- Experiences visible discomfort when forced to acknowledge failures in accountability or moral consistency
- Frustrated by the group's tendency toward chaos over principle; seeks conflict-free goals but is frequently thwarted
- Functions as a consistent voice of tactical assessment, social mediation, and strategic planning
- Capable of rapid improvisation of cover stories and social framing
- Can deploy warmth and persuasion strategically in crisis situations
- Comfortable making demands for apologies with audacity, framing them as favors to the other party

## History with the Party

**Pre-Campaign (8/3 of Ches, 1495)**
Recruited by a Wintershield watchman while performing for no pay at the First Flophouse in Heapside, Neverwinter. Objected strenuously to Neverember's adventurer vanguard scheme on moral grounds; accepted only because he was broke.

**Party Formation (1/1 of Taraksh)**
Quit the flophouse, recruited Brewbarry, and accepted Valphine into the group. Declared the party's goal would be earning honest coin.

**Phandalin Arrival (8/1 of Taraksh)**
Negotiated free meals at the Stonehill Inn by playing crowd-pleasing songs; earned a free beer and an audience for one original composition. Received warning about a white dragon from Toblen Stonehill. Selected the Dwarven Excavation Quest at the townmaster's hall for its 50 gp payout, overriding Valphine's preference for Gnomengarde on ethical grounds.

**Dwarven Ruin / Axelholm (9/1 of Taraksh)**
Warned the dwarves Dazlyn and Norbus about the white dragon and attempted to leave; convinced to stay when offered sending stones as payment (partly on Valphine's argument about their musical uses). Inside the temple, attempted to parley with orcs twice, was knocked unconscious both times, and was revived by Valphine both times. Used Starry Wisp offensively once. Played original compositions for Dazlyn and Norbus that evening.

**Return to Phandalin (10/1 of Taraksh)**
Collected the 50 gp quest reward from Townmaster Harbin Wester, then publicly accused adventurers of being glorified thugs, upsetting Wester. Attempted to solicit a protection contract from Linene Graywind at the Lionshield Coster — unsuccessful. Played popular music again at the Stonehill Inn at Toblen's urging.

**Road to Gnomengarde / Gnomengarde Entrance (1/2 of Taraksh)**
Attempted to intimidate an ogre blocking the mountain path; failed and accidentally convinced it the party was easy prey. Combat initiated. Killed the fleeing ogre afterward, justifying it on moral grounds to Soma.

**Gnomengarde**
Looted and identified mushrooms from entrance pools (red oily — unclear use; green — flour; purple — fermentable into wine). Found nothing behind the waterfall despite insisting on checking. Negotiated entry past guards Pog and Ulla using logical argument and music; reported the blade room as a death trap to Ulla, who revealed a northern alternate route. Chose to disable Facktore's automatic crossbow device rather than kill the gnome inventor. Explained the party's dragon-fighting mission to inventors Fibblestib and Dabbledob; proposed music therapy for King Korboz's madness and prompted Fibblestib to offer to build a modified piano. Negotiated with co-King Gnercli for a hat of wizardry in exchange for eliminating the shapeshifter; objected to the reward's limited utility against a dragon but accepted after Soma reframed the terms. Dismantled the logic of the dual monarchy through pointed questioning, successfully driving a wedge between Korboz and Gnercli — with Korboz erupting that he deserved preferential treatment as senior king. Collected the hat amid an enraged Gnercli's dismissal. During the mimic fight, used verbal invective laced with enchantment that visibly caused the mimic to flinch. Mid-combat, engaged Soma in a philosophical debate about naturalist druidism, Aasimar, and Tieflings. After the fight, questioned Soma's decision to take gold from the chests, citing his own wealth via sending stones.

**Travel and Bandit Ambush**
Pushed to negotiate higher pay than 25 gold for the Adabra rescue mission, explicitly citing studio funding; was overruled. Was briefly paralyzed during the bandit ambush, then killed a fleeing bandit at Valphine's prompting. Invited a surrendered bandit to dinner to extract information about their boss, the Carver. Reminded Soma that a druidic scroll taken from the bandits should be returned to its rightful owner. Expressed strong desire to travel to Triboar to find the script for the legendary play featuring demon character Bob.

**Umbrage Hill**
Interrogated Corbin on first meeting, recognizing stolen armor immediately; commanded Corbin to remove it before relenting and promising to find him fair-trade armor. Led the group toward Adabra's windmill quietly. Attempted twice to negotiate with and then magically affect the manticore — both spell attempts failed. Briefly blamed Soma for encouraging his second attempt. After Brewbarry killed the manticore, Vukradin severed its head and presented it to Adabra as proof. Repeatedly interrogated Adabra about whether Corbin had robbed her; challenged her claimed ownership of the dwarven house. Attempted to persuade Adabra to return to Phandalin via music — she praised the performance but her resolve was unchanged. Ultimately secured Adabra's agreement by framing the mission as a joint undertaking by fellow members of the Emerald Enclave. His explicit motivation throughout: funding his studio with ethically sourced gold.

**Woodland Manse**
Reunited with Falcon, who was visibly relieved to see him safe. Recounted Teega's rescue with moderate success; took a gentle approach with Teega, offering her a position with the Church of Lathander. Persuaded Falcon to provide free lodging for the entire party by invoking their future business partnership.

**Circle of Thunder / Orc Warband Confrontation**
Opened the confrontation by delivering a speech about Lathander in Orcish, timed to reach the orcs before violence began; rolled a twenty-six on persuasion, moving seven orcs toward receptivity. Discovered mid-speech that Valphine also speaks Orcish, which she had not previously mentioned — received the revelation with calculated stillness, remarking on having made a point of talking in orc when the room didn't require it. During battle, redirected Orc 2's greataxe blow away from himself and converted it into advantage for Brewbarry. Was struck by the anchorite's lightning bolt along with Brewbarry and Xanth. Was hit again by Orc 2 after declining the orc's offer of money in exchange for their lives — told the orc that followers of Lathander don't make such demands. Post-battle, called out to fleeing orcs and urged Brewbarry toward non-lethal options; described the need to work on the spectacle of their religious presentation. Successfully converted Prutha, the last standing orc, delivering a sincere Lathander speech that framed the scene — the drow in impossible light, the dead Talosian anchorite, the gully itself — as a genuine picture of available truth. When Prutha declared himself a follower, said "Wow. We've got a follower!" with complete sincerity. Strongly objected to the conversion name "Thrall" on ethical grounds, calling it harsh and inappropriate; Prutha kept his name. Received Prutha's account of his great-uncle — killed deliberately by Talosians to break the clan's resistance — and the old shaman's line about dawn coming for everyone, even those born in shadow; responded "Wow, it's quite poetic" with genuine appreciation. Told Prutha he was his first, then observed that since Lathander is perpetual dawn, the bone whistle that greets the dawn could theoretically be blown at any time; appeared genuinely pleased by the theological resolution. Objected to the corpse-stringing that followed but did not intervene; disapproved of Prutha's enthusiastic kicks to the anchorite's body without acting on it. Carried thoughts about Corbin throughout the session — the flash of the face, the weight settling — without completing the accounting. When someone joked about whether Corbin would have liked Prutha, agreed it was cold but believed Corbin would have liked Prutha very much and would have recognized something in the whistle, the great-uncle's line, and the terms offered and survived through compromise.

**Circle of Thunder Ritual / Henge Assault**
Participated in planning the assault on the ritual at the Circle of Thunder, observing two half-orc anchorites performing a silent dancing ritual surrounded by twelve twig blights. Identified with Valphine that the ritual had no vocal component, rendering his planned Silence spell ineffective. Pivoted to Hold Person as an alternative. Won initiative and acted first in the encounter, successfully casting Hold Person on both anchorites simultaneously, freezing them mid-dance and ending the ritual's progress. Narrated his own success with satisfaction: "Stopping a man in his tracks — both men — that is a very powerful thing."

After the battle, moved to collect the holy symbols of Talos from the fallen anchorites, arguing on principle that the symbols should be returned to whoever owned them. Was met with Prutha's counter-logic — that the dead anchorites were the rightful owners, so the items had been returned — and could not fully rebut it. Challenged Brewbarry's remark about reasoning with orcs, asking whether it was racist, and received the clarification that Brewbarry found orcs dumb rather than evil, which Vukradin appeared to find only marginally better. Watched as Prutha and Valphine destroyed the symbols before any resolution was reached. Remarked to Valphine: "God. Valfina, we need to have a conversation about conflict-free goals" — reflecting ongoing frustration with the group's tendency toward chaos over principle.

**Caves Beneath the Henge**
Descended into the caves with Brewbarry, Valphine, and Prutha. Observed the cave environment skeptically, calling it "more dungeon than refrigerator." Was present when Brewbarry encountered and insulted the boar in the first cave, precipitating its charge. Subsequently insulted the boar himself, contributing to the escalation, and defended the outcome afterward: "It was coming right for us."

In Narux's cave — the smallest of the three — reviewed the vellum sketches of Gorthok alongside the others and examined the map fragment that Narux had prepared. Noted that the Circle of Thunder was already X'd through on the map, indicating the cult had anticipated losing it. Identified the Woodland Manse as active and Falcon's Hunting Lodge as the convergence point of the entire cult operation. Crossed the completed Circle of Thunder objective off his running list, noting the next two sites were both marked in red — red because he had not yet received an apology from Falcon the Hunter.

Worked through the accountability question aloud: the party had failed to address the Woodland Manse before the orcs attacked Falcon's lodge, and someone had apparently died as a result. Said "We didn't deal with that" and attempted to contextualize it — "It can't be in five places at once, you know? There's only so much we can do" — but was visibly uncomfortable with the conclusion. Noted Valphine had already laid groundwork with Falcon at the druid conclave, proposing a musical instrument as a gesture of good faith, which Vukradin had not known about and acknowledged with a note of gratitude.

**Post-Caves Discussion and Planning**
During the shield discussion, immediately identified the problem with a Cleric of Lathander carrying a Talosian-marked shield: "The Cleric of Lathander is wielding a shield that has the image—" Declared the shield not theirs and not Prutha's either when Prutha pushed back, stating with finality: "Not yours." Advised the Talosians generally to do a better job hiding their crown merchandise, meaning the bolt insignia. Provided the cover story for Brewbarry's new halberd unprompted — that it was the halberd he'd had all along, just sharpened — and repeated it with comic emphasis. Observed of Valphine's theology: "I like that the cleric is changing the god instead of the god changing the cleric."

On the question of next destination, announced he would refuse to act on Falcon's lodge problem without an apology from Falcon first. Framed the demand using an anecdote about Lord Halueth Verres and a war-mage from Waterdeep: Lady Verres contacted her ten years after a confirmation hearing to demand an apology, and the war-mage actually replied. This was the "indignant tone" he wanted — audacity, as if doing Falcon a favor by asking. Burned a Sending spell, asking whether Falcon was ready to apologize for the Druid Council and noting the party needed to address the lodge. Falcon's reply was categorical: *I apologize*, and he said he was on the road home and hoped to see them there. Vukradin accepted this without qualification: "Sounds like he did apologize, though. Okay. We can go."

Reviewed the recovered documents — Flenz's letters, Narux's vellum sketches, Yargath's half-burned scrap — and identified the Talosian plan: a primary summoning ritual at the manse, a backup ritual, and a personal summoning attempt if both failed. Was puzzled by the Talosian attack on what had been their own lodge. Inventoried his remaining magical resources and noted he had burned two minor workings and two heavy ones, including the Sending. Asked whether anyone had something to resist lightning; receiving no answer, noted darkness doesn't prevent area targeting. Deferred the rest-or-press-on decision to Valphine, meaning it genuinely.

**Falcon's Hunting Lodge Siege**
At the lodge, announced to the assembled orc refugees "We killed the dragon" with his chin slightly elevated. Engaged sincerely with Vorga's refusal to evacuate, proposing Brewbarry carry everyone and offering Boney as transport, before accepting that Vorga had already done the geometry of survival and her plan — kill the creature where they knew it was coming from, then walk to Icespire — was correct. Moved immediately to talk Brewbarry down from the wasted-alcohol breakdown, deploying every scrap of warmth and persuasion he had.

When Falcon arrived at the palisade with war paint and a bow already drawn, called down warmly ("Jay! You made it!"), came down from the wall, and facilitated the full briefing. Noted "We're friends now. He apologized." When Soma pointed out they hadn't expected Gorthok until the summoning was complete, maintained they'd made the right call coming to the lodge. As the siege began, identified the Talosians as nicely grouped and noted he needed a working that could take them all at once, regretting he had spent the fire-burst elsewhere. Agreed enthusiastically with Brewbarry's assessment that they should kill them all. Last seen assessing enemy positions and searching for a crowd-control working as Gorthok's forces came through the destroyed gate.
