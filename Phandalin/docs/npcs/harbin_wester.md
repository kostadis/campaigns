# Harbin Wester

## Identity
Townmaster and Lord Mayor of Phandalin; head of the Privy Council. Operates out of a sturdy stone hall with a bell tower. Operates under the authority of Lord Neverember as the local administrator handling bounties, civic matters, and Phandalin's governance within Neverwinter's regional apparatus.

*Also known as: Harbin, Harbin Townmaster, Town Master Harbin Wester, Harbin (Townmaster).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintain order in Phandalin, keep Lord Neverember informed and pleased, preserve his title and authority, and quietly profit from his administrative position where possible.
- Cowardly and self-protective by default — famously shouts through locked doors about being too bony (or too thin) for a dragon's meal. Initially mistook Vukradin for a dragon. Warms considerably once he recognizes someone as non-threatening. Avoids confrontation but will hold firm on financial matters until sufficiently pressured.
- Pragmatic to the point of low-grade corruption: arranges private fee-for-paperwork deals outside the full council and frames graft as simply "how things are done." Describes such informal deal-making as standard practice in the Overbright, suggesting a pattern of quiet corruption.
- Procedurally minded and bureaucratic — stalls, deflects, and hides behind process when pressured. Invokes hierarchy and proper authority as shields rather than principles. Defers to Lord Neverember's authority as his primary justification for decisions, using the lord's name as a shield against demands. Capitulates under pressure but only to the minimum degree necessary.
- Responds poorly to moral challenges (visibly upset by Vukradin's critique of the adventuring economy) but will cover himself smoothly when caught (reframed the bribe as a community donation without missing a beat).
- Capable of political flexibility when cornered, pivoting to compromise to protect his position rather than out of genuine conviction. Dismissive of supernatural concerns he finds inconvenient.
- Credit-seeking and opportunistic — positions himself at the front of celebrations and carefully claims shares of credit he did not earn, framing others' victories as results of his leadership or management.
- Politically calculating and skilled at narrative control — orchestrates public events to shape how victories are remembered and to bind his name to others' achievements before independent narratives can form.

## History with the Party

**8/1 Taraksh** — First contact through a locked door. Communicated by shouting through the locked door that he was too thin and bony to make a good meal for a dragon. Confirmed posted quests with the party without meeting them face to face.

**10/1 Taraksh** — Opened the door after recognizing Vukradin (or Vukardin) as the musician from the inn. Confirmed the 50 gp Dwarven Excavation reward and defended the adventuring economy by citing Lord Neverember's tax-free plunder guarantee. Visibly upset by Vukradin's extended moral critique of the adventuring economy.

**Payment Dispute** — Returned to collect payment. Harbin resisted paying beyond the original agreement, citing Neverember's arrangement that adventurers claim monster treasure as their compensation. Under intense pressure from Adabra, he buckled and offered free room and board at the Stonehill Inn as a compromise instead of coin, blaming bandit activity for his lack of funds.

**Lighthouse Report** — Answered the door surprised to see the party alive. Relieved to hear the Talos infestation at the lighthouse had been sanctified; promised to notify Lord Neverember (expressing that Lord Neverwinter would be pleased and promising to send him a note) but showed no urgency about repairing the light or the lighthouse light itself. Warned the party — with narrowed eyes — against approaching Neverember directly while their mission remained incomplete, noting that appearing before the ruler with an incomplete mission was inadvisable. Reminded them they had signed on to address the town's problems. Last seen at his home in Phandalin during this encounter.

**Lighthouse Discussion** — The following morning, Vukradin intercepted Harbin in the street before he reached his office. Vukradin pressed him about destroying the cursed lighthouse. Harbin dismissed the evil rumors surrounding it but was firm that Lord Neverember would prefer the curse removed rather than the structure destroyed. Directed the party to seek out Ser Kaelen at Barthen's Provisions before Kaelen left town.

**Butterskull Ranch Quest** — Posted a notice board quest offering 100 gp to assess orc attack damage at Butterskull Ranch (five miles east of Conyberry on the Triboar Trail); full reward payable even if owner Alfonse "Big Al" Kalazorn was found dead upon presentation of proof.

**Temple / Tresendar Manor Deal** — Appeared conveniently during the temple planning discussion. Offered to arrange paperwork converting Tresendar Manor into the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice for a private (or modest personal) fee of 50 gp, framing it as outside normal council process and describing the transaction as a private arrangement outside the full council, suggesting this was simply how things were done in the Overbright. When Vukradin returned (from outside), successfully covered the transaction by claiming the gold (or funds) was a community donation, successfully avoiding Vukradin's scrutiny. Bought a round of drinks (or offered to buy a round of beer for the tavern) afterward, feeling generous after the deal. Also requested the supply delivery quest to the Logger's Camp on behalf of his brother.

**Post-Gold Mine Return** — Greeted the party warmly upon their return from the gold mine peace negotiations, expressing satisfaction with recent changes in the community. His mood turned serious when Brewbarry raised the topic of the town's ale supply. Warned that the dragon occupying the mountains was driving orc incursions that threatened the season's ale production and prevented farmers from tending their crops. Presented new quests (Dragon Barrow, Axeholm, Woodland Manse), noting rewards were funded through Neverwinter's tax system and could help Vukradin establish his music studio. When Vukradin questioned the safety of gold transport from Neverwinter, shared Neverwinter's troubled history of catastrophes, corruption, and crime spanning decades, and Neverember's progressive reform efforts to remedy these issues.

**Axeholm Briefing (Referenced, Not Present)** — Party cited Harbin as having warned that Phandalin might need to evacuate to Axeholm if dragon attacks became imminent, establishing stakes for that mission. He did not appear in person during this session.

**Axeholm Payment** — Ecstatically paid the party 250 gp for securing Axeholm. Clarified it was a last-resort backup plan, not a new permanent home — disappointing some party members. Grew nervous when Brewbarry appeared wearing the dread helm with glowing red eyes and asked if he had been possessed by a demon. Backed down after Vukradin used intimidation to remind him of his council role (or position as head of the Privy Council). Tasked the party with delivering cheese to Mountain's Toe Gold Mine on behalf of Qelline Alderleaf, noting no one else would make the trip given ongoing threats from dragons and orcs. Reiterated that orc pressure (on farming communities) would persist until the dragon threat was resolved.

**Accusations Against Vukradin** — Made accusations against Vukradin that the party found deeply offensive, creating immediate antagonism. When confronted simultaneously by Vukradin and Ser Kaelen (in the same scene, finding himself under pressure from multiple sides), invoked Lord Neverember's authority as the only power that could remove him from office, reminding Ser Kaelen that only Lord Nevermember had the authority to replace him as Townmaster. When Vukradin threatened to organize a town meeting on the green (threatening "pitchforks"), Harbin stalled by insisting proper meetings require preparation, agendas, and pre-work.

**White Dragon Attack** — After Vukradin repelled the white dragon attack using phantasmal force, Harbin publicly acknowledged his heroism.

**Privy Council Formation** — Proposed a compromise: formation of a privy council (himself, Tolbin, Elmer, Halia, Linene, Adabra, and Vukradin) to share governance while preserving his Townmaster title.

**Shrimpy Custody** — Received the prisoner Shrimpy from the party (when they delivered him to Phandalin's town master) upon their return to Phandalin.

**Cult of Talos Briefing** — Present at a council meeting where the party reported their findings on the Cult of Talos at the Woodland Manse, including intelligence on shapeshifting orcs and their lightning magic.

**Dragon Victory Speculation (Referenced, Not Present)** — Vukradin anxiously speculated that Harbin Wester would try to claim credit for the dragon victory, alongside the Lord's Alliance and Neverwinter bureaucrats. He did not appear in person during these events.

**Heroes' Welcome Celebration** — After the defeat of Cryovain, positioned himself at the front of the crowd during the heroes' welcome celebration and delivered a speech carefully claiming a share of credit he did not earn. Specifically praised "Mr. Vucrodin" and the privy council, framing the dragon's defeat as the result of Vukradin's leadership and management. Brewbarry noted that Harbin had been afraid and was doing "strange things with credit," but let it pass.

**Dragon Barrow Victory Festival** — Waited for the party at the town boundary of Phandalin upon their return from Dragon Barrow and the mine. Had arranged a full festival reception — banners, townsfolk arranged in a semicircle, and a rehearsed demeanor of modest pride. Praised the party warmly, extolling their bravery, sacrifice, and decisive role in ending the dragon threat. Notably credited his own foresight in establishing the Privy Council as the mechanism that brought such capable heroes to Phandalin in the first place. Valphine observed this as a calculated political maneuver — lashing his name to the party's victory before that victory could acquire its own independent narrative. The festival continued under his orchestration while the party received various briefings and dealt with internal matters.
