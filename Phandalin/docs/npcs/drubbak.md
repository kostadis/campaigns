# Drubbak

## Identity
- **Role:** Elder warrior among Vorga's refugee orcs
- **Faction:** Vorga's people
- **First appearance:** Entered the courtyard with the refugees; was noted immediately for his age, bearing, and the way he fixed on Valphine's holy symbol

## Personality & Motivations
- Driven by a long, unanswered need for faith — his attention to Valphine's symbol reads as spiritual hunger rather than hostility
- Carries deep traditional knowledge, particularly of orcish storm-working practices and their ritual forms
- Quiet and observational; reacts to crisis by going still and listening rather than acting

## History with the Party
- On first meeting Valphine, Drubbak spoke a single word in Orcish — *Dawn* — and watched her holy symbol throughout. When she confirmed herself as the bringer of a painful dawn and showed him the symbol clearly, he received it without further words.
- During the siege, when the storm arrived without its accompanying chant, Drubbak recognized immediately that something was wrong with the summoning. He asked aloud where the chant was, identifying the storm's unnatural character through traditional knowledge.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Standing at the gatehouse, listening to the storm, at the moment the gate was destroyed
- **Status uncertain** — he did not move from his position as the gate fell
- The party knows he has knowledge of storm-working tradition; what he knows about the specific storm or its source remains unexplored

## Relationships
- **Valphine:** A fragile, significant connection — he has been praying a long time without answer, and she was the first response. She spoke to him in his language and showed him her symbol. The thread is thin but present.
- **Vorga's people:** An elder among the refugees; his standing within the group is implied by his bearing, not stated explicitly.
