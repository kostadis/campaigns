# Horia Stonetallow

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Clan representative and presumed leader for the Stonetallow dwarves at the mine
- **Faction:** Stonetallow clan (shield dwarves; lineage traces to a disgruntled Gauntlgrym engineer)
- **First Appearance:** At the mine, during negotiations between the dwarves and the wererats

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Restore proper dwarven operation of the mine; ensure the craft of mining is practiced with exactitude and pride
- **Drives:** A deep obsession with doing things *correctly* — inherited from a clan founded on the principle of proper craftsmanship
- Proud, thorough, and easily drawn into lengthy explanations when her clan's honor or history is questioned. She is pragmatic enough to strike deals she dislikes if they serve the clan's long-term interests, but makes no effort to hide her skepticism.

## History with the Party
- The party (including Vukradin) encountered Horia at the mine during negotiations over the wererats' occupation
- When Vukradin questioned the apparent contradiction in the name "Stonetallow," Horia delivered a full etymological breakdown: the name derives from "stone to allow," referring to allowing arches to pass — a nod to the clan's founding obsession with proper archwork
- Horia finalized a formal agreement with Zeleen via handshake and exchanged contracts, accepting the new arrangement on the grounds that it frees dwarves to focus purely on mining rather than guard duties — though she and her kin remained visibly skeptical of the wererats' modifications to the mine
- When Vukradin asked about expected weekly earnings, Horia estimated approximately **100 gold pieces per week**, potentially higher with investment, pending final overhead calculations

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The mine
- **Active Plans:** Working out overhead costs and finalizing the operational details of the new arrangement with the wererats
- **What the Party Knows:** Horia has agreed to the deal, estimated ~100gp/week in earnings, and is skeptical of the wererats' changes to the mine
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether the dwarves have any private reservations serious enough to undermine the agreement; no information on clan resources or broader Stonetallow interests

## Relationships
- **Zeleen:** Counterparty to the formal contract; professional relationship, functional but not warm — dwarves openly skeptical of wererat modifications
- **Vukradin:** Engaged directly in conversation; answered his questions without hostility, though she required full explanations be heard on her terms
- **Stonetallow Clan:** Speaks on behalf of and embodies the values of the clan; her decisions appear to carry clan authority
