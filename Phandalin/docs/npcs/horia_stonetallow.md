# Horia Stonetallow

## Identity
- **Role:** Dwarven mine representative and clan liaison for the Stonetallow clan; leader of a five-dwarf mining crew with a prior legal claim to the mine; works alongside foreman Dwolin the Grout at Mountain's Toe Gold Mine
- **Faction:** Stonetallow clan (shield dwarves; ancestral roots trace to a disgruntled engineer from Gauntlgrym who was the only one who knew how to build a proper archway)
- **First Appearance:** Encountered deeper in the mine after the party followed dwarf tracks from Don-jon's death site
- *Also known as: Horia.*

*Also known as: Horia.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Intensely proud of clan heritage — gave an etymological lecture when Vukradin called "Stonetallow" an oxymoron, explaining it derives from "stone to allow," referencing the clan's mastery of archways and fixation on doing things properly
- Principled and contract-minded; produced documentation when challenged on the dwarves' claim to the mine
- Sharp sense of honor; visibly offended by cynicism toward agreements and legal obligations
- Pragmatic enough to accept the wererat arrangement, viewing it as freeing her people to focus on what they do best — perfecting the art of mining
- Deeply offended by Valphine's dismissal of contracts; questioned her fitness as a cleric of Lathander
- Capable of pragmatic workarounds when dealing with difficult situations (such as proposing to buy a fake gem to manage Dwolin's superstitions)
- Shrewd negotiator who understands contractual limits and when to work around them
- Shows weary affection toward colleagues like Dwolin despite their eccentricities

## History with the Party
1. Party followed dwarf tracks to Horia's group and asserted ownership of the mine.
2. Horia produced the dwarves' contract with Don-jon, establishing prior legal claim.
3. Revealed Don-jon's betrayal — Don-jon had told the dwarves he had found "a sucker in some bard fellow" who had convinced the wererats to work for mere cheese, and that he intended to use this cheap labor while dismissing the dwarves as no longer necessary. This betrayal led to a deadly argument and Don-jon's death.
4. Warned the party that the wererats were dangerously incompetent miners whose improper techniques and safety violations would cause the entire operation to collapse within months.
5. Agreed to consider a compromise arrangement (dwarves mine, wererats do labor and security), contingent on a formal wererat apology for killing dwarves in prior conflicts.
6. Was deeply offended by Valphine's dismissal of contracts (stating that contracts exist only to cheat people) and questioned her fitness as a cleric of Lathander.
7. Party began negotiating to reduce the dwarves' contracted 20% share.
8. Met the party during negotiations brokering a protection deal between the Stonetallow dwarves and the wererats under Zeleen Varnaster. Voiced skepticism about the wererats' modifications to the mine but accepted the arrangement as pragmatically sound.
9. Formally concluded the agreement by shaking hands with Zeleen Varnaster and exchanging signed contracts.
10. Spoke with Vukradin about projected earnings, estimating approximately **100 gp per week**, with potential for more given further investment.
11. When Zeleen's were-rats were dispatched to find the real Earthstone Gem, Horia revealed she had been planning to propose that the party give her money to buy a fake gem as a workaround for Dwolin's superstitions. Brewbarry designated this as "Plan B" in case the were-rats failed.
12. Requested fifty gold pieces from the mine's own accounts to fund the fake gem purchase, citing a "no-skimming clause" that Vukradin ("Mr. Bucherton") had insisted on in the contract, which prevented her from simply taking mine gold.
13. After Brewbarry threatened her with his "Barbarian in your face clause" and offered to draw her blood for ink, she quickly agreed that the addendum could be considered signed without formalities. She conspiratorially suggested — leaning up toward Brewbarry's kneecap due to the height difference — that they keep the arrangement between themselves, believing Vukradin would not be open to such negotiations.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Mountain's Toe Gold Mine, managing operations alongside Dwolin the Grout
- **Active Operations:** Managing the mine under the wererat protection deal; monitoring dwarven operations; handling practical workarounds for Dwolin's superstitions
- **Known to Party:** Agreed to the protection arrangement; estimated weekly earnings; skeptical of wererat modifications; has a valid prior contract; working with Dwolin; negotiated funding for a fake gem purchase
- **Hidden from Party:** Suggested keeping the fake gem funding arrangement hidden from Vukradin specifically

## Relationships
- **Don-jon (deceased):** Former employer who betrayed the dwarves; Horia or her crew killed him in the resulting confrontation
- **Zeleen Varnaster:** Counterpart in the mine agreement; relationship is transactional and cautious — Horia shook hands and exchanged contracts but the dwarves remain skeptical of wererat involvement
- **The Wererats:** Hostile history — dwarves were attacked by them in earlier conflicts; Horia required a formal apology before cooperating
- **The Party:** Cautiously transactional; willing to negotiate but unimpressed by Valphine's attitude toward contracts
- **Valphine (party):** Specifically offended — questioned her sincerity as a cleric of Lathander after her anti-contract remarks
- **Vukradin (party):** Engaged in financial discussion; also the one who triggered Horia's clan-name lecture; Horia believes he would not be open to informal contract workarounds and suggested keeping certain negotiations hidden from him
- **Brewbarry (party):** Negotiated the fake gem funding arrangement with him; responded to his theatrical threats by quickly agreeing to informal contract modifications; conspiratorially suggested keeping their arrangement private
- **Dwolin the Grout:** Mine foreman she works alongside; defends him with weary affection, describing him as the best gold seeker she'd ever known while acknowledging his eccentricities
- **Stonetallow Clan:** Deeply loyal; sees her role as protecting the clan's ability to focus on proper mining craft
