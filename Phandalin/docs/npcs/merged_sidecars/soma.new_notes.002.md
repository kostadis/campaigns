# New notes for soma (from dossier_extract_002.md)

Soma is a tortle druid of Mieliki who encountered the party just outside Neverwinter on 1/1 of Taraksh, offering to guide them to Phandalin in exchange for company on a road she had traveled several times. She possessed knowledge of a white dragon rumor in the area that she deliberately withheld from the party. On 8/1 of Taraksh she guided the group safely along the High Road and Triboar Trail, provided a description of Phandalin and its people, produced a town map, and relayed rumors about the Lionshield Coster's disrupted supply network.

At the dwarven ruins on 9/1 of Taraksh, Soma examined the settlement and noted both its thorough ransacking and the ominous nature of the temple facade dedicated to Abbathor. She interrupted Vukardin's moral objections to encourage hearing the dwarves' offer. In combat she cast Thorn Whip to drag an orc off a twenty-foot bluff, used Poison Spray to kill another orc, and accidentally knocked out Valphine with a miscast Ice Knife. After combat she rushed to heal Valphine and recommended the party rest for the night. She noted Dazlyn and Norbus cooked a fine meal in gratitude for the orcs' deaths. She retrieved an unspecified item from the roadside ogre's corpse on the return journey, which Vukardin chose not to acknowledge.

On 10/1 of Taraksh during the ogre encounter on the road back, Soma commented "The kid is going to get us killed" as Vukardin approached the ogre, then used Poison Spray against it. She described their party dynamic to Brewbarry as "not a well-oiled machine yet," noting she had only just met them on the road.

On 1/2 of Taraksh, Soma led the expedition toward Gnomengarde through the broken hilly terrain south of Phandalin and up into the Sword Mountains. When a second ogre blocked a narrow valley path and declared it wanted to eat turtle, Soma shouted that the party had killed its brother, enraging it, then coated it in poison and withdrew. Last known status: in the Sword Mountains on the road to Gnomengarde, in combat with an ogre.
