# New notes for Brewbarry (from dossier_extract_042.md)

Brewbarry participated in the assault on the Circle of Thunder from the outset. He observed the ritual alongside the group, watching the silent dancing anchorites and twig blights from the treeline, and contributed tactical commentary — calling the anchorites "a pantomime club" when it was established the ritual had no vocal component.

During the battle he entered a rage state and engaged the twig blights with handaxe throws, using his Elemental Cleaver ability to retrieve thrown axes. His first throw went wide, but subsequent attacks cleared multiple blights. He tracked the battlefield actively and moved to engage the anchorites once Vukradin had frozen them with Hold Person.

After the battle, Brewbarry examined the bodies of the fallen anchorites and noted the distinctive tattoos on each: one bore storm clouds and spiraling open eyes up his neck, the other had crossed boar tusks down both forearms. He was engaged by Vukradin in the debate over the holy symbols, offering the remark "You're trying to reason with an orc" and clarifying under questioning that he considered orcs dumb rather than evil, citing himself as a comparative baseline. He made no strong objection as Prutha and Valphine destroyed the symbols.

In the caves, Brewbarry led the descent and drove the exploration with the stated goal of finding drinks. He discovered the first cave contained a live boar and announced it optimistically as evidence of barbecue preparation. He then watched as Vukradin insulted the boar, precipitating its charge, and expressed sincere moral disapproval afterward: "That wasn't very nice. Why would you insult the boar? The poor boar. That's mean." He accepted the resulting irradiated pork meat with equanimity and resumed the search for beverages.

In the second cave he noted the carved lintel above the entrance, observed the penned boars inside, and delivered an unpunished pun ("I'm tempted to say this is boring"). He read aloud from a half-burned letter fragment recovered from a firepit — the fragment signed with a single letter K, containing the line "dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives."

In Narux's cave he initially assessed the space as containing nothing of interest and proposed leaving. He reviewed the Gorthok sketches and map fragment alongside Vukradin, arriving at the conclusion that Falcon's lodge was "where they hide the drinks — hidden in plain sight." When Vukradin reflected on the failure to protect Falcon's lodge before the orc attack, Brewbarry responded with "Excuses, excuses," without apparent irony.

Current status: Alive, with the party, departing the Circle of Thunder.
