# New notes for lyra (from dossier_extract_012.md)

Lyra appeared as the leader of a group of Emerald Enclave members trapped in the Whispering Grove by blighted brambles. She called out to the party upon their arrival, identifying herself and her organization, explaining that the Treant had once been the Grove's Guardian but had been corrupted by a planar infection. She urged the party to fight the creature and prevent the corruption from spreading.

Once freed from the brambles by Soma's attack, Lyra immediately produced four crude ritual stones and explained the mechanics of the sealing ritual: six successes required, with three failures meaning total defeat. She attempted a Moonbeam against the corrupted Treant but was re-ensnared by its vines in the process. She survived the encounter and witnessed the ritual's failure, which caused the planar wells to expand and grow more violent.

When questioned by Vukradin and Valphine about whether she had planned to attempt such a complex ritual with so small a group, Lyra admitted candidly, "I don't think I thought this through very carefully." Under Soma's direct questioning, she explained the ideological schism within the Emerald Enclave between naturalists and interventionists, offering the most substantive exposition of the session. She traced the fracture to the War of the Dragons, when Delaan Winterhound was persuaded by a hero named Malfire to commit the Enclave to fighting Tiamat's summoning. She noted that those who had opposed intervention later pointed to Tiamat's defeat as the catalyst for the War of the Giants and the shattering of the Ordning by the Giant gods. She described the naturalist position as accepting even planar intrusions as part of the multiverse's natural cycle, and the interventionist position as viewing such intrusions as alien corruption to be cleansed.

Lyra explained the specific history of the corrupted Treant: it had once been a good guardian that protected the forest until the planar scar emerged, after which it was no longer sustained by sunlight but by the planar pools. She confirmed through the conversation with Soma that the Treant had eaten people.

When the ritual ultimately failed and the planar wells expanded, Lyra was visibly distressed, whispering, "This did not work the way I had hoped." Her current status is alive and present at the grove at the moment of the ritual's failure, though the party subsequently withdrew from the location.
