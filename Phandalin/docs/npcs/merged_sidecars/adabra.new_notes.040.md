# New notes for Adabra (from dossier_extract_040.md)

Adabra does not appear directly in this chunk but is extensively discussed and her earlier actions are described.

At a Druid Enclave meeting, Adabra conducted what is described as a public trial structured around a personal grudge against Vukradin. She raised the matter of five gold pieces she had previously given him, using it as load-bearing evidence of his character before the assembled druids. The personal nature of the attack surprised even Vukradin, who had expected procedural argument rather than petty grievance. She had telegraphed hostility toward him in the weeks prior through clipped sentences, conspicuous avoidance of eye contact, and glances at Vukradin characterized as reconsidering something once found useful.

She fumed when she left the Enclave after losing whatever verdict was sought.

Xanth later referenced Adabra by name during his fireside explanation of Talos, noting that she would characterize the Cult of Talos as representing "the necessary element of distraction in the cosmic balance." Valphine internally assessed this framing as mistaking slaughter for ecology, comparing it to arguments she recognized from Menzoberranzan.

In response to the loss and her public use of the debt, Vukradin addressed an envelope containing six gold pieces — five plus one for interest, drawn from fair-trade organic gold — and sent it to her through Phandalin's postal system. His reasoning: now that she had lost, she could have her money back, and she could never again truthfully say he had stiffed her.

Last known whereabouts: departed the Druid Enclave after losing.
