# New notes for Valphine (from dossier_extract_042.md)

Valphine was present for the approach to the Circle of Thunder and was the first to identify the ritual as a summoning. She named it as Animate Dead based on the quality of the movement. During the battle she took the lightning bolt alongside Brewbarry and Soma. She engaged the twig blights with her radiant guardians, sweeping multiple blights from the battlefield. She delivered her battle cry — "Die ye then" — before killing the final anchorite.

After the battle she participated in the debate over the holy symbols. She placed one of the Talosian holy symbols on a fallen anchorite's skull and destroyed it with her mace. She responded to Prutha's statement about his great-uncle's saying with "Dawn is powerful," which Prutha acknowledged.

In the caves, Valphine entered with Brewbarry, Vukradin, and Prutha. She participated in the boar encounter in the first cave, allowing her radiant guardians to finish the animal after Vukradin's second insult escalated the situation. She observed dryly that the result was "radiated pork meat."

In the second cave she found the satchel with the Talosian wax seal and insisted on destroying the symbol before opening it. She translated the letters inside, which were written in a hand she identified as consistent throughout. The letters revealed: that a half-orc named Narux had been maintaining the henge while another figure named Grannoc was reckless and likely to summon before the Lodge was ready; that someone described as "the Chosen" was cultivating a young man from an eastern clan with fabricated stories, with the leash not yet revealed; and that if the henge fell, the manse would summon in its place, and if the manse fell, the writer would summon personally. She also recovered a half-burned letter fragment from a firepit signed only with the letter K, containing the line "dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives."

She found a hidden floor compartment in the cave that Vukradin had stepped over without noticing. Inside was a single vial of potent defensive magic — the kind she assessed would make a person nearly impossible to put down. Rather than disclose it to the group, she tucked it into the inner pocket of Vukradin's pack without his knowledge, reasoning that he would refuse it on principle if told but would need it for what was coming.

She noted she had already laid groundwork with Falcon the Hunter at the druid conclave, proposing a musical instrument as a gesture of good faith, which Falcon had accepted. She offered to take the lead in smoothing relations with Falcon given Vukradin's complicated standing with him.

Regarding the boar tusk on a leather thong recovered from the bodies, she expressed interest in incorporating it into her sacraments.

Current status: Alive, with the party, departing the Circle of Thunder. Carrying concealed knowledge of the vial she planted on Vukradin.
