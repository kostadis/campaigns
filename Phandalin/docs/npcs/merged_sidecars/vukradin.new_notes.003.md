# New notes for vukradin (from dossier_extract_003.md)

Vukradin delivered the killing blow on the fleeing ogre, justifying it to Soma by stating the ogre hated music and had likely stolen treasure from others. He looted mushrooms from the pool islands at Gnomengarde's entrance, identifying red oily mushrooms of unclear use, green mushrooms suitable for flour, and purple mushrooms fermentable into wine. He insisted on checking behind the waterfall for a secret treasure room, finding nothing.

Vukradin led negotiations with guards Pog and Ulla, countering their shapeshifter accusations with a logical argument about the intent behind standing orders, which partially impressed the male gnome Pog. He played music for Pog and Ulla, earning their approval. After being directed to the inventors' workshop via a route through spinning blade traps, he reported the blade room as a death trap to Ulla, who revealed an alternate northern route.

In the large northern room, Vukradin and the party were attacked by an automatic rotating crossbow device built by the gnome Facktore. Vukradin chose to disable the device rather than kill Facktore, who fled during the encounter.

In the inventors' workshop, Vukradin explained the party's mission to Fibblestib and Dabbledob — seeking a device to help fight the dragon on behalf of Townmaster Harbin Wester. He proposed using music to treat King Korboz's madness and asked about a piano, prompting Fibblestib to offer to build a modified version.

At King Korboz's door, Vukradin announced himself and was immediately accused of being a shapeshifter by the king. He negotiated with co-King Gnercli, who offered a hat of wizardry in exchange for eliminating the shapeshifter. Vukradin objected to the reward's limited utility against a dragon but accepted the deal after Soma reframed the terms. He led the group back through the spinning blade room, this time disabling the blades, and proceeded toward the cellar. During the mimic fight, he used verbal invective with an underlying enchantment that visibly caused the mimic to flinch.
