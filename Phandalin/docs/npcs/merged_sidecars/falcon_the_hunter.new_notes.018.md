# New notes for Falcon the Hunter (from dossier_extract_018.md)

Falcon the Hunter is the proprietor of a hunting lodge who built his reputation and business on the lie that he is a dragonslayer. When Vukradin broke into his private bedroom and pried open a locked drawer, he uncovered artifacts that contradicted this claim. The drawer contained a brass ring inscribed with the Elvish word for "lightning," housed in a miniature iron dragon's head box, as well as a wooden square carved with a labyrinth maze and a pair of wooden elven ears on a chain inscribed with "my dearly beloved."

The following morning at breakfast, Falcon confronted the party about the disturbed drawer, but wrongly attributed the intrusion to the **Cult of Talos**, whom he accused of attempting to steal the ring as an offering to their god. Vukradin successfully misdirected him, and Falcon, feeling cornered by the implication the cult had nearly succeeded, confessed the truth about his past.

Falcon admitted he was the sole survivor of his adventuring party after an encounter with the blue dragon **Iymrith**, and that he arrived at her hoard only after others had already plundered it. He collected residual gold scraps and the brass ring, using them as seed capital to purchase land and construct his lodge. He perpetuates the dragonslayer fiction deliberately, reasoning that "the fantasy of dining with a dragon slayer" is good for business and that paying guests "want to believe the story."

He granted Vukradin permission to document the ring. His last known location is his lodge, where he continues operating his business. The ring's connection to Iymrith and lightning links him tangentially to broader threats the party faces in the region.
