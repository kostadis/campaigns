# New notes for The Dwarven King (from dossier_extract_026.md)

The Dwarven King appeared from behind a limestone throne flanked by granite statues of dwarf warriors in the grand pillared hall of Axeholm, revealed as a three-headed monstrosity twisted by the same interdimensional corruption affecting the other dwarves in the fortress. He emerged alongside one additional corrupted dwarf after the party defeated two other twisted dwarves in the chamber.

The King demonstrated terrifying speed and ferocity, immediately targeting Soma and paralyzing her before delivering a flurry of bites from his three mouths that rendered her unconscious and reverted her from bear form. He pressed his assault against Brewbarry despite suffering grievous wounds from the barbarian's defensive strikes, shrugging off the damage through his corrupted resilience. He successfully resisted Vukradin's first attempt to assault his mind with an illusion of Moradin, though he succumbed to a second attempt conjuring the image of a giant bear, suffering psychic damage and confusion. In his distracted state he turned on Valphine, but failed to land any of his attacks. The party ultimately wore him down and defeated him.

On his body the party found a signet ring bearing the Axeholm crest depicting a hand clutched in a dwarven gauntlet, which Valphine recognized as both a valuable artifact and a key of some kind.

The King is dead, slain in the pillared throne room of Axeholm.
