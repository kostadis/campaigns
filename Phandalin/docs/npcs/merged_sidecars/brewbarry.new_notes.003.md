# New notes for brewbarry (from dossier_extract_003.md)

Brewbarry fought the ogre alongside the party, missing a halberd strike that rang loudly off the rocky ground. He received a full axe blow from the ogre but was completely unharmed due to his Uthgardt barbarian rage and mystical resilience, which stunned both the ogre and Valphine. The ogre's failure against him triggered its retreat. Brewbarry pursued but could not bring the ogre down before Vukradin finished it.

At Gnomengarde's guard post, Brewbarry announced himself as "Brewbarry, killer of Ogres" to assert he was no shapeshifter. When the gnomes identified him as an Uthgardt Barbarian, he clarified he used to be one, which unexpectedly resolved a bureaucratic ambiguity around a standing attack order against Uthgardtians and allowed the party entry.

In the inventors' workshop, Brewbarry cut off Valphine's implied suggestion of torture with the declaration "We talk! We cure!" aligning himself with the diplomatic approach. In the cellar mimic fight, he charged the mimic and struck it with his halberd, wounding the creature but getting the weapon stuck in its body. The mimic's retaliatory pseudopod attack missed him entirely.
