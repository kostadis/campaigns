# King Korboz

## Identity
- **Role:** Co-King of Gnomengarde (senior king by his own claim)
- **Faction:** Gnomengarde
- **First Appearance:** The party encountered him confined to the royal chamber, in a state of severe hysterical madness

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Being recognized as senior king with preferential authority over Gnercli; resolving the gnome disappearances (though his madness impedes effective action)
- Paranoid and prone to hysteria — he fixated on Vukradin as a shapeshifter mid-conversation based on little more than the bard announcing himself
- Beneath the madness, he is politically competitive and quick to assert dominance when given an opening; Vukradin's philosophical prodding snapped him briefly back to coherent rivalry with Gnercli

## History with the Party
1. **First encounter:** Party found Korboz screaming and hysterical in the royal chamber. He warned them of a shapeshifting creature responsible for the disappearances of gnomes Warren and Orrin, then became convinced Vukradin *was* the shapeshifter upon hearing him announce himself.
2. **Second interaction:** During a discussion of the dual monarchy, Vukradin's philosophical questioning about seniority pulled Korboz out of his panic. He seized on the framing, loudly asserting his rights as senior king and inflaming his rivalry with Gnercli.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Royal chamber, Gnomengarde
- **Active Situation:** In open conflict with co-king Gnercli; rivalry freshly inflamed by Vukradin's questioning
- **What the party knows:** He believes a shapeshifter caused the disappearances; he is mentally unstable but responsive to appeals to his ego and status
- **What remains hidden:** The true nature or source of his madness; whether his shapeshifter claim is accurate

## Relationships
- **King Gnercli (co-king):** Deep rivalry; Korboz feels Gnercli dismisses and disbelieves him; currently in active conflict
- **Vukradin (party):** Initially accused him of being the shapeshifter; later warmed to him after Vukradin validated his claim to seniority — relationship is volatile and ego-dependent
