# Sister Kaella

## Identity
- **Role/Faction:** Spymaster and chief intelligence operative of the Carver; ran his spy network in Phandalin. Now operating independently after a violent falling out — formerly his most capable henchwoman, currently his prisoner-turned-defector. Apparent religious figure (referenced sacraments, invokes God).
- **First Appearance:** Named by Skippy as the Carver's intelligence asset before any direct encounter; later met Valphine in person in an abandoned building at the far edge of Phandalin; ultimately stumbled wounded into the clearing outside the Carver's chamber to deliver an urgent warning to the full party.
- *Also known as: Sister Kayla, Sister Kella.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Survival, above all else — and once survival is secured, killing the Carver herself and dismantling his organization on her own terms. Her loyalty to the Carver degraded entirely once he accused her of intelligence failures and her survival became contingent on his death.
- **Personality:** Ruthless, methodical, and composed under extreme pressure. She killed Locutus without hesitation the moment he was no longer useful. Her first conscious words after near-death were a calm actuarial assessment of what Jax would have done to her. She is perceptive and calculating — she identified Valphine through a drow disguise, had pre-profiled every party member, and engineered her initial meeting with Valphine entirely on her own terms. She operates through manipulation and controlled information, selecting partners she believes she can predict or use. She is pragmatic about moral ambiguity and openly acknowledges operating in gray spaces. She has a long-term orientation: as long as she is alive, she considers herself to have options.

## History with the Party

1. **Named by Skippy (Early Sessions):** Identified by Skippy as the Carver's Spymaster — the intelligence source behind the Carver's detailed knowledge of Harbin's quest board operations. Not present at Butterskull Ranch.

2. **Direct Meeting with Valphine:** Met Valphine in an abandoned building after dark, having arranged the meeting expecting Locutus. Immediately saw through Valphine's drow disguise and named her, demonstrating prior surveillance of the party. Killed Locutus on the spot after confirming he had killed Borg — a loose end she needed to close. Disclosed that the scheme to frame Valphine for stealing merchant goods had been her own plan, and that it failed because Borg failed to eliminate all witnesses when killing Big Al. This failure put her in danger with the Carver. Proposed a partnership: she feeds Valphine intelligence to systematically eliminate the Carver's remaining lieutenants, weakening him enough to force him out of hiding, at which point she intends to kill him personally. She paid Valphine with Locutus's owed payment as a down payment, then vanished before any formal agreement was reached.

3. **Tavern Discussion (Relayed by Valphine):** Valphine relayed Kaella's offer to the party. Vukradin refused to accept what he considered stolen goods or cooperate with someone he viewed as evil. Significant party tension resulted. No formal agreement was reached.

4. **Silence:** Kaella had promised to contact the party about the Carver situation. She never followed through — her next appearance was in the clearing, wounded.

5. **The Clearing:** Appeared wounded and breathless — armor slashed, cloak gone, barely standing — with an urgent warning: the Carver had become a hollow, maddened shell, was marching on Icespire Hold that night, and intended to possess the white dragon Cryovain to burn Phandalin. Mid-warning, Vukradin paralyzed her with Hold Person. Soma and Valphine prevented her execution; she was knocked unconscious with non-lethal force. The party immediately recognized the problem: she alone knew the location of Icespire Hold.

6. **Interrogation and Disclosure:** Valphine revived her through physical pressure rather than magical healing. Kaella confirmed the Carver's madness and provided directions to Icespire Hold without requiring further persuasion. She disclosed that she had made a private prior arrangement with Valphine, bypassing Vukradin deliberately — stating he had difficulty operating in morally gray environments and was therefore not a viable contact. Vukradin told her she would do well in prison.

7. **Transfer to Corbin:** Left in Corbin's custody when the party departed for Icespire Hold. Vukradin authorized Corbin to beat her to one hit point and no further. She began screaming the moment the party walked away, calling Corbin a lunatic. Her stated preference for imprisonment was Axholm.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Camp; in Corbin's custody
- **Active:** Screaming at Corbin as of last contact
- **What the Party Knows:** She was the Carver's Spymaster who turned when her survival depended on it; she killed Locutus in front of Valphine; she engineered the framing plot against Valphine; she had a private arrangement with Valphine; she provided genuine directions to Icespire Hold; she wants to be imprisoned at Axholm
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full nature of her prior arrangement with Valphine; the full extent of her spy network; how much she actually knows about the Carver's remaining assets, capabilities, or whereabouts; her true long-term intentions after the Carver is dead; what she actually intends once in custody or if freed

## Relationships
- **Valphine:** Private prior arrangement, confirmed still in force during interrogation. Valphine is her functional contact in the party and the reason she is alive. Initially selected Valphine as an asset, believing her to be an evil drow cleric using Lathander as a cover — considers her the only party member who "shares her worldview." No formal agreement existed before her capture.
- **Vukradin:** Open antagonism. He paralyzed her during her warning, authorized her to be beaten to within one hit point, told her she'd do well in prison, and refused her offer of alliance outright. She assessed him as incapable of operating in morally gray environments and dismissed him as too naive to be useful — a calculated assessment, not an insult.
- **Soma:** Intervened to prevent her execution alongside Valphine. She had previously dismissed him as too nature-focused to be a useful contact.
- **Brewbarry:** Dismissed as too loyal to Vukradin to be a useful contact.
- **Corbin:** Currently her custodian. She was screaming at him as the party left. Valphine has been teaching Corbin certain sacraments — relevance to this relationship unclear.
- **The Carver:** Former employer, now a target. Loyalty broke when he blamed her for intelligence failures after the party killed his muscle. She calculated he would have had her tortured to death had Jax won. She fears his retribution and originally intended to kill him personally.
- **Locutus:** Killed him at the Valphine meeting; viewed as a liability and a loose end after he killed Borg.
- **Borg:** Was working with him; his failure to eliminate witnesses when killing Big Al caused the intelligence failure that triggered her break with the Carver.
