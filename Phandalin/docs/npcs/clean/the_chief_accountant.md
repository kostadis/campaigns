# The Chief Accountant

## Identity
- **Role:** Former accountant and administrator of an ancient dwarven fortress; a corrupted, possibly undead or spectral entity who persisted beyond death, driven by a compulsive need to maintain accurate, settled accounts. Name unknown.
- **Faction:** None current; formerly in service to the fortress and its operations
- **First Appearance:** Manifested at the end of the battle against six out-of-phase dwarven figures on the upper floor of the fortress
- *Also known as: Chief Accountant.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Balance the books, avoid punishment from "the auditor," and secure her own freedom
- Projects a grandmother's warmth undercut by screeching static and erratic, glitching behavior. Her speech blends mundane accounting obsessions (ledgers, depreciation, regulators) with genuine distress and paranoia.
- Methodical and high-strung; capable of a ten-minute screaming fit over an incorrect bill, yet able to achieve genuine peace once she considered her ledgers closed.
- Volatile but not purely hostile — her panic over harming Valphine revealed a capacity for fear and remorse beneath the madness.

## History with the Party
1. **First Contact:** Appeared after the party defeated the out-of-phase dwarven combatants. Immediately fixated on Valphine, calling her "sister of the sun" and accusing her of carrying "the scent of bleach and betrayal."
2. **Hostile Engagement:** Unleashed a supernatural wail that knocked both Valphine and Vukradin unconscious.
3. **Panic and Flight:** Upon seeing Valphine collapse, shifted abruptly into distress — frantically attempted to stabilize her using blue-white static energy, expressing terror that "the auditor" would liquidate her for closing Valphine's account. Fled incorporeally through a closed door into an adjacent room.
4. **Return and Deal:** After the party rested, she cautiously returned and disclosed the history of the fortress. Revealed that Aletra (Sotorra family, fourth daughter) had brought the malfunctioning machine to the fortress, and had laughed at and likely killed the Chief Accountant when she raised concerns about the books. Offered the machine's override code in exchange for her freedom and the party's silence. The party accepted. She provided directions to the machine room on the lower level.
5. **Peaceful Departure:** Encountered again as a spirit after the spatial distortions ceased. Informed that Aletra had fled and the machine had imploded; visibly relieved. The party presented her with an interplanetary bill for 42,000 gold pieces, triggering an extended (~10-minute) outburst over its outrageous and incorrect nature. Once the outburst subsided, she declared "The book is closed" and departed peacefully.

## Current Status
- **Deceased / Departed.** Her spirit has fully departed; she is gone permanently.
- Her former quarters contained a dusty bedroom with moldy mattresses and a soot-stained fireplace. A secret door hidden within the fireplace led to a small side room. The iron chest in that room held a **dread helm** and **gauntlets of ogre power** — now available to the party.

## Relationships
- **Aletra (Sotorra family, fourth daughter):** Aletra struck the deal that brought the malfunctioning machine to the fortress. When the Chief Accountant raised concerns about the books, Aletra laughed at her and likely killed her. Deep grievance.
- **Valphine:** Fixated on her as "sister of the sun"; volatile mix of accusation and desperate protectiveness. The connection to Aletra appears to be the source of both hostility and fascination.
- **The Auditor (unknown entity):** A figure she feared above all else — believed this entity would punish or destroy her if Valphine's "account" was closed on her watch.
- **The King:** Mentioned as a third party Aletra exploited — his jealousy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant operations made him susceptible to her promises.
- **The Party:** Brief but memorable — she screamed at them for ten minutes before finding peace.
