# Horia Stonetallow

## Identity
- **Role:** Dwarven mine representative and clan liaison for the Stonetallow clan; leader of a five-dwarf mining crew with a prior legal claim to the mine
- **Faction:** Stonetallow clan (shield dwarves; ancestral roots trace to a disgruntled engineer from Gauntlgrym)
- **First Appearance:** Encountered deeper in the mine after the party followed dwarf tracks from Don-jon's death site
- *Also known as: Horia.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Intensely proud of clan heritage — gave an etymological lecture when Vukradin called "Stonetallow" an oxymoron, explaining it derives from "stone to allow," referencing the clan's mastery of archways
- Principled and contract-minded; produced documentation when challenged on the dwarves' claim to the mine
- Sharp sense of honor; visibly offended by cynicism toward agreements and legal obligations
- Pragmatic enough to accept the wererat arrangement, viewing it as freeing her people to focus on what they do best
- Deeply offended by Valphine's dismissal of contracts; questioned her fitness as a cleric of Lathander

## History with the Party
1. Party followed dwarf tracks to Horia's group and asserted ownership of the mine.
2. Horia produced the dwarves' contract with Don-jon, establishing prior legal claim.
3. Revealed Don-jon's betrayal — Don-jon had planned to replace the dwarves with wererats working for cheese, which triggered a deadly argument and Don-jon's death.
4. Warned the party that wererat mining practices would cause a full operational collapse within months.
5. Agreed to consider a compromise arrangement (dwarves mine, wererats do labor and security), contingent on a formal wererat apology for killing dwarves in prior conflicts.
6. Was deeply offended by Valphine's dismissal of contracts and questioned her fitness as a cleric of Lathander.
7. Party began negotiating to reduce the dwarves' contracted 20% share.
8. Met the party during negotiations brokering a protection deal between the Stonetallow dwarves and the wererats under Zeleen Varnaster. Voiced skepticism about the wererats' modifications to the mine but accepted the arrangement as pragmatically sound.
9. Formally concluded the agreement by shaking hands with Zeleen Varnaster and exchanging signed contracts.
10. Spoke with Vukradin about projected earnings, estimating approximately **100 gp per week**, with potential for more given further investment.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The mine, overseeing the newly established arrangement
- **Active Operations:** Managing the mine under the new wererat protection deal; monitoring dwarven operations
- **Known to Party:** Agreed to the protection arrangement; estimated weekly earnings; skeptical of wererat modifications; has a valid prior contract
- **Hidden from Party:** Nothing explicitly noted as concealed

## Relationships
- **Don-jon (deceased):** Former employer who betrayed the dwarves; Horia or her crew killed him in the resulting confrontation
- **Zeleen Varnaster:** Counterpart in the mine agreement; relationship is transactional and cautious — Horia shook hands and exchanged contracts but the dwarves remain skeptical of wererat involvement
- **The Wererats:** Hostile history — dwarves were attacked by them in earlier conflicts; Horia required a formal apology before cooperating
- **The Party:** Cautiously transactional; willing to negotiate but unimpressed by Valphine's attitude toward contracts
- **Valphine (party):** Specifically offended — questioned her sincerity as a cleric of Lathander after her anti-contract remarks
- **Vukradin (party):** Engaged in financial discussion; also the one who triggered Horia's clan-name lecture
- **Stonetallow Clan:** Deeply loyal; sees her role as protecting the clan's ability to focus on proper mining craft
