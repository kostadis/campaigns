# Vukardin

## Identity
- **Role:** Avant-garde Aasimar bard; adventurer-for-hire
- **Faction:** Loosely contracted under Lord Neverember's scheme to clear the Phandalin hinterlands; no formal affiliation
- **First Appearance:** Performing at the First Flophouse in Heapside, Neverwinter, earning nothing. Recruited by a Wintershield watchman on 8/3 of Ches 1495 to adventure in the Phandalin region.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Earn honest coin; find an audience that appreciates his original compositions; avoid becoming what he despises — a mercenary thug operating under legal cover.
- **Personality:** Vukardin is a principled idealist who talks first in almost every situation, even when it gets him knocked unconscious. He has a persistent anti-establishment streak, openly lecturing NPCs about the ethics of adventuring, but his principles bend under financial pressure. He is drawn to anything that might have a musical angle — including ogres and sending stones.

## History with the Party
- **8/3 of Ches 1495:** Recruited by a Wintershield watchman while broke and playing for no pay. Pushed back morally on Neverember's scheme but accepted out of desperation.
- **1/1 of Taraksh:** Quit the flophouse, recruited Brewbarry, accepted Valphine's offer to join the group. Declared their goal would be honest coin.
- **8/1 of Taraksh:** Led the group to the Stonehill Inn; negotiated room-and-board by playing popular songs, earning Toblen's goodwill and one free beer before being allowed to play an original composition. Received a white dragon warning from Toblen. Selected the Dwarven Excavation Quest (50 gp) over Gnomengarde, reasoning it was the most honest option.
- **9/1 of Taraksh:** At the dwarven ruin, immediately warned Dazlyn and Norbus about the white dragon and attempted to leave. Was persuaded to stay when Valphine noted the sending stones could serve as musical instruments. Inside the ruin, attempted to parley with orcs twice, was knocked unconscious both times, and was revived by Valphine both times. Also cast Starry Wisp on an orc. Played original music for Dazlyn and Norbus that evening.
- **10/1 of Taraksh:** Attempted to communicate with a whistling ogre on the road, assuming it might be a musician or enslaved creature; was charged immediately. Cast Starry Wisp to illuminate the ogre, then hid while the party handled the fight. Back in Phandalin, collected the quest reward from Harbin Wester but delivered an unsolicited lecture accusing adventurers of being glorified thugs, upsetting the townmaster. Attempted (and failed) to solicit a protection contract from Linene Graywind at the Lionshield Coster. Played popular music again at Toblen's request that evening.
- **1/2 of Taraksh:** En route to Gnomengarde, attempted to intimidate a mountain ogre blocking the path, failed, and accidentally convinced it the party was easy prey.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Sword Mountains, on the road to Gnomengarde — mid-combat with an ogre that Vukardin inadvertently provoked.
- **Active Plans:** Completing the Gnomengarde quest; continuing to seek legitimate income and musical recognition.
- **What the Party Knows:** Vukardin is broke, idealistic, frequently unconscious, and genuinely committed to his ethical code — even when it makes things worse.
- **Hidden from the Party:** Nothing noted in source material.

## Relationships
- **Valphine:** Traveling companion and repeated savior; has revived Vukardin twice. Valphine's pragmatism occasionally overrides Vukardin's hesitation (e.g., sending stones argument).
- **Brewbarry:** Recruited by Vukardin on 1/1 of Taraksh; relationship otherwise undetailed.
- **Toblen Stonehill:** Friendly patron; tolerates Vukardin's originals as a reward for playing crowd-pleasers. A reliable source of lodging and local information.
- **Harbin Wester (Townmaster):** Strained — Vukardin collected his reward then lectured him about adventurers being thugs.
- **Linene Graywind:** Attempted to pitch her a protection contract; rebuffed.
- **Dazlyn & Norbus:** Positive; Vukardin played original compositions for them and they provided the sending stones as payment.
- **Lord Neverember:** Skeptical and reluctant benefactor; Vukardin accepted his scheme while openly condemning it.
