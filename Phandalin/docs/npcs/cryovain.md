# Cryovain

## Identity
- **Role:** White dragon; apex predator of the Icespire region
- **Faction:** None — solitary creature, ultimately a victim of the Carver's domination
- **First Appearance:** Discovered by Soma during spider-form scouting of Icespire Hold's rooftop battlements, bound at the center of a summoning circle

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- Whatever drives Cryovain once possessed are no longer relevant — by the time the party encountered him, coherent intent had collapsed into compressed, mindless rage held barely in check by the Carver's chains.
- Soma assessed his eyes as evidence he was not a sane or reasoning creature, and that freeing him would have resulted in indiscriminate destruction. Whether any of his original self remained was never determined.
- He was fighting the Carver's influence, or had been; how much of him remained was unclear. He was not waiting or biding time — if freed, he would destroy everything within reach without thought or choice.

## History with the Party
1. **Scouting Encounter:** Soma discovered Cryovain chained at the center of a burning summoning circle of alien geometry on Icespire Hold's roof. His scales had gone the color of old ice left too long in shadow; his eyes were hollow, glazed, and filmed over — wrong. His nose appeared physically altered or missing. He was not unconscious — he was *held*, dominated, with something seething underneath the stillness. He was present but not fully himself.
2. **The Carver's Seizure:** When Vukradin paralyzed the Carver's human vessel and the silence spell dropped, the Carver's obsidian sword — revealed to be the true intelligence behind the ritual and a mental amplifier — spoke directly into the party's minds, declaring it would take everything it had always wanted. The sword dissolved Cryovain's chains entirely (not breaking them — *dismissing* them), and assumed direct domination over him. The party was warned they had approximately three rounds before his mind was consumed entirely. The summoning circle needed three more rounds to complete the Carver's ritual.
3. **Pre-Combat Debate:** The party debated whether killing the Carver's vessel would dissolve the chains and release an uncontrolled dragon, and whether the dragon might show gratitude if freed. Soma was categorical that his eyes showed he was not a sane creature and would not reason.
4. **Combat:** Cryovain launched off the battlements and climbed thirty feet into the air in one wingbeat, pulling out of melee reach. He attacked with a full cone of frost breath, hitting Brewbarry directly and pushing through his armor and rage to find softer places underneath — severe enough to push past his limits. Brewbarry landed two clean strikes with the Dragon Slayer Sword (and multiple heavy halberd strikes). Valphine's radiant light struck him, and Vukradin's critical thorn whip also connected. From the air, he breathed ice over Brewbarry at least twice — both hits severe, nearly pushing the Goliath past what he could absorb. Soma's moonbeam struck him repeatedly, causing visible pain. Vukradin's bardic inspiration cracked something vital.
5. **Death:** Valphine's sacred flame dealt the final 3 points of damage, killing him. With his death, the burning geometric circle broke and the Carver's essence dissipated entirely. Soma had argued he was a victim and urged the party not to kill him — calling out "don't kill, don't kill! He was just a victim" — but the plea went unheeded and the fight concluded before that objection could change the outcome.
6. **Harvesting:** His body remained on the rooftop, still warm when the party began harvesting it. Brewbarry found it unexpectedly difficult to process that the dragon had a name. Soma methodically harvested valuable components from the carcass, including a breath pouch from its throat — described as a strange, swollen, heavy organ radiating an ancient, metaphysical cold — and pale, supple scales that caught the light like polished stone. The dragon parts were brought back to Phandalin as evidence of its defeat, and the crowd recognized them immediately. The party discussed using the scales for dragon scale mail and the breath pouch as a tool.
7. **Quest Removal:** The dragon quest was subsequently removed from the Phandalin quest board following his death. Vukradin noted he would never entirely stop feeling complicated about Cryovain's death.

## Current Status
- **Dead.** His body remains on the rooftop of Icespire Hold; his remains are being processed as resources.
- His hoard — approximately 3,000 gold worth of fused ice, residium, gold, gems, and trade bars into an inseparable block through planar magic — remains on the rooftop, unspendable and unresolved without planar expertise.
- The dragon quest has been removed from the Phandalin quest board.

## Relationships
- **The Carver's Sword:** The instrument of his domination and, ultimately, his destruction. The sword's seizure of control over Cryovain triggered the final battle. The obsidian weapon was a mental amplifier being used to perform the ritual over him.
- **Soma:** The party member most insistent that Cryovain was a victim. She recognized the wrongness in his eyes but argued for mercy; the argument was overruled. She later methodically harvested his remains.
- **Brewbarry:** Received the brunt of Cryovain's attacks; landed the most significant strikes against him with the Dragon Slayer Sword and multiple halberd hits. Found it unexpectedly difficult to process that the dragon had a name.
- **Valphine:** Delivered the killing blow with sacred flame.
- **Vukradin:** Landed a critical thorn whip hit and provided bardic inspiration that cracked something vital. Continues to feel complicated about Cryovain's death.
- **Xanth:** Referenced in fireside accounts; noted that the white dragon's winter broke the forest's balance, scattering animals and contributing to orc radicalization.
