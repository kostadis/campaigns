# Corbin

## Identity
- Role: Party aide and errand-hand; no formal faction affiliation
- Former affiliation: Carver's gang (bandit crew); prior to that, subsistence farmer from Greenmeadow east of the High Forest
- First appearance: Presented himself to Vukradin as a new recruit, wearing scavenged leather armor taken from a corpse

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- Core drive: Survival at any cost — has repeatedly compromised his values (joining bandits, petty theft) simply to stay alive after losing everything
- Deeply conflict-averse about his bandit past; visibly distressed when forced to confront former associates
- Demonstrated unexpected loyalty and steadiness under pressure — held his post guarding Shrimpy's prisoner even when convinced the party was dead
- Something unresolved and dangerous lived in him around Sister Kaella: both Vukradin and Valphine noted the specific hunger of someone who has been made small and is now offered power over another
- May have been beginning to understand the weight of his choices before his death

## History with the Party
1. **First meeting:** Appeared before Vukradin in dead man's armor; admitted to banditry under interrogation; reprieved when Vukradin promised fair-trade armor
2. **Manticore encounter:** Singled out as the creature's preferred target (human); nearly killed; survived due to party intervention
3. **Windmill arrival:** Arrived looking and smelling badly, triggering renewed suspicion from Vukradin; broke down and told Soma his full history: a farmer from Greenmeadow east of the High Forest, married with a young daughter named Lily, whose village and family were destroyed by the Black Lion Uthgardt barbarians during the chaos following the breaking of the Ordning. He survived by accident, drifted south, committed petty theft to survive, and eventually joined a bandit crew out of desperation. He helped Adabra prepare the evening meal at Vukradin's direction.
4. **Conyberry:** Identified four brigands as members of Carver's gang; resisted Vukradin's order to approach them, explaining that leaving a gang carries serious consequences; Vukradin overruled him and insisted he go speak to his former associates
5. **Woodland Manse:** Held position with Shrimpy guarding a prisoner while the party engaged cultists; refused to let Shrimpy talk him into fleeing despite Shrimpy's insistence the party was dead
6. **Sister Kaella assignment:** Assigned to guard Kaella while party traveled to Icespire Hold; responded that it would be his pleasure and immediately asked if he was permitted to beat her; Vukradin authorized one hit point worth; Valphine noted she had been teaching him sacraments of her practice; Kaella began screaming the moment the party left the tent
7. **Death:** Killed by Sister Kaella, who escaped while the party was away; the party had left him — a farmer turned bandit — alone with a deadly assassin because they were in a rush to intervene elsewhere

## Current Status
- **Status:** Deceased — killed by Sister Kaella
- **Last known location:** Camp, where he had been guarding Sister Kaella
- **Aftermath:** Xanthopoulos reported his death to the druid council; Adabra seized on Corbin's death as evidence against interventionism; the presiding druid dismissed this somewhat, noting that Corbin "was a criminal," though acknowledging his death was unfortunate; Soma refused to sugarcoat what had happened but also refused to let Adabra claim Corbin's death as a trophy for her argument

## Relationships
- **Vukradin:** Commanding figure; Corbin obeyed but had pushed back when genuinely frightened (Conyberry); Vukradin watched him with ongoing suspicion
- **Soma:** Recipient of Corbin's confession about Greenmeadow and his family; the most personal connection in the party; reflected on having "left a farmer turned bandit with a deadly assassin because they were in a rush to intervene"
- **Valphine:** Had been instructing him in sacraments of her practice; nature and intent of this instruction unclear
- **Sister Kaella:** Had been charged with guarding her; was authorized to cause harm; both Vukradin and Valphine read something volatile between them; she killed him and escaped
- **Carver's gang:** Former crew; departure carried implied threat; he remained visibly afraid of them
- **Shrimpy:** Held firm against Shrimpy's pressure at the Woodland Manse; relationship functional but untested
- **Adabra:** Assisted her in preparing the evening meal under Vukradin's direction; she later used his death as evidence against interventionism
- **Xanthopoulos:** Reported Corbin's death to the druid council
- **Lily:** His young daughter, killed by Black Lion Uthgardt barbarians
- **Wife (unnamed):** Killed by Black Lion Uthgardt barbarians alongside their daughter
