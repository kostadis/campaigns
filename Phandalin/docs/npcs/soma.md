# Soma

## Identity

Tortle druid of Mieliki; guide-for-hire; likely affiliated with the Emerald Enclave (naturalist faction). First appeared 1/1 Taraksh, outside Neverwinter, offering to guide the party to Phandalin in exchange for company on a road she had traveled several times.

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations

- **Core goals:** Serve as a guide while pursuing her own druidic path; deepen her training before time runs out (her master Meril gave her only one year before sending her on)
- Pragmatic and unsentimental about coin and violence, but holds genuine ethical lines around unnatural magical creations and cruelty
- Dry and sardonic — quips under pressure, mocks inconsistency in others, and is not above moral hypocrisy when she notices it in herself
- Instinctively identifies as a naturalist within the Emerald Enclave, a position that shapes her discomfort with gnomish constructs and her sympathy for creatures living as nature intended
- Expresses a general kinship with all life while also being willing to kill bandits and enemies who threaten her
- Not opposed to taking gold of questionable provenance

## History with the Party

**1/1 Taraksh** — Met the party outside Neverwinter; offered herself as a guide to Phandalin in exchange for company. Possessed knowledge of a white dragon rumor in the area that she deliberately withheld from the party.

**8/1 Taraksh** — Guided the group safely along the High Road and Triboar Trail; provided a description of Phandalin and its people, produced a town map, and relayed rumors about the Lionshield Coster's disrupted supply network.

**9/1 Taraksh** — At the dwarven ruins, examined the settlement and noted both its thorough ransacking and the ominous nature of the temple facade dedicated to Abbathor. Interrupted Vukardin's moral objections to encourage hearing the dwarves' offer. In combat: cast Thorn Whip to drag an orc off a twenty-foot bluff, killed another with Poison Spray, and accidentally knocked out Valphine with a miscast Ice Knife. After combat rushed to heal Valphine and recommended the party rest for the night. Noted Dazlyn and Norbus cooked a fine meal in gratitude for the orcs' deaths. Quietly retrieved an unspecified item from the roadside ogre's corpse on the return journey, which Vukardin chose not to acknowledge.

**10/1 Taraksh** — During a road ogre encounter, muttered "The kid is going to get us killed" about Vukardin as he approached the ogre; used Poison Spray against it. Described the group to Brewbarry as "not a well-oiled machine yet," noting she had only just met them on the road.

**1/2 Taraksh** — Led the expedition south toward Gnomengarde through the broken hilly terrain and up into the Sword Mountains. Provoked a valley ogre by shouting that the party had killed its brother, then poisoned it and withdrew.

**Gnomengarde visit** — Located the river route to the hold and guided the party to the waterfall entrance. Mocked Valphine's missed mace swing from the earlier ogre fight. Followed Vukardin through the waterfall searching for a nonexistent secret room, getting soaked, and eventually pressured him to leave. Climbed the cliff to the cave entrance and retrieved a rope for Valphine rather than leaving her behind. At the guard post, verbally challenged Pog and Ulla's logic by pointing out that shapechangers would not choose their particular forms, though the gnomes dismissed this reasoning. Muttered that she sensed more senseless violence approaching before the standoff was resolved. Reacted with disbelief when bureaucratic procedure ultimately granted them entry. In the inventors' workshop, attempted to explain the value of clerical magic to Fibblestib and Dabbledob but was drowned out. During negotiations, reminded Vukardin that the reward from Townmaster Harbin Wester required a device, not a useful one, steering him toward accepting the hat of wizardry deal. Quipped to Vukardin that he was "more of a tour guide" than a musician. In the mimic fight, prepared to cast Ice Knife (with Valphine notably repositioning away from Soma's line of fire); her poison puff proved ineffective before she ultimately landed the killing blow. Expressed disgust at the mimic as an unnatural magical creation and held her tongue when Fibblestib and Dabbledob discussed harvesting the creature's entrails for a new construct, despite her belief that such inventions were the origin of northern abominations.

**Post-Gnomengarde** — Took gold from the chests without apology. Argued the 25-gold Adabra reward was fair ("sometimes the reward is knowing you did the right thing"), stating she only wanted a new teapot, not a studio, and dismissed Vukardin's moral posturing. During the bandit ambush, wild-shaped into a spider, climbed to engage a bandit, bit and poisoned him to death, and attempted to spare a fleeing spider-phobic bandit — shouting "Let him go" while still in spider form, realizing too late she was speaking in that form. Found a druidic scroll among the bandits' belongings; was challenged by Vukardin and made to question whether keeping it was any better than banditry.

**Umbrage Hill / Manticore fight** — Spent the opening scene in spider form, experiencing the world through heightened senses before reverting to her tortle body and startling Corbin. Pushed back against Vukardin's suggestion to extort Phandalin; insisted on simply killing the manticore and saving Adabra. Conjured a familiar and sent it into the manticore's mouth as a distraction, which enraged rather than satisfied the creature. Encouraged Vukardin's second spell attempt and was visibly hurt when he blamed her for the spell's failure. Tried to soothe both Adabra during the siege and Corbin after the battle. When Adabra asked her position in the Emerald Enclave schism between naturalists and interventionists, instinctively identified as a naturalist, which dramatically shifted Adabra's attitude toward the group. Was stung by Adabra's casual criticism of gaps in her druidic training — her master Meril had given her only one year near her home before prompting her to follow Vukardin, and she is aware she has little time left. That evening listened to Corbin's full account of his destroyed family and his descent into banditry. The following morning used Adabra's shared naturalist values to finally convince her to travel to Phandalin, framing the mission around shared convictions rather than obligation.

**Orc ambush (most recent)** — Left her clutch behind and transformed into a bear to scout the concealed orc ahead of the party. Noticed the orc making hand signals to the north, east, and west, and identified the clearing beyond as an ambush site. When Brewbarry stumbled and gave away their position, climbed a tree and attacked the scout, biting his foot despite missing with her claws. Took an arrow from the scout in retaliation. Leaped from a branch to shove the scout toward the other orcs; cast Moonbeam (to Valphine's contempt); struck one of the orcs; and clamped her jaws down on the orc with the necklace of bones, mortally wounding him. Brewbarry questioned her about attacking a fleeing creature, implying she had shown prior reluctance to do so.

## Current Status

- **Last known location:** In combat or just after combat at an ambush clearing en route — presumably between Umbrage Hill and Phandalin
- **Active:** Serving as Vukardin's paid guide (tab growing); content to wait for payment
- **Hidden from party:** Deliberately withheld knowledge of a white dragon rumor near Neverwinter; nature of the item retrieved from the roadside ogre's corpse is unknown to the party

## Relationships

- **Vukardin:** Exasperated patience; calls him "the kid," challenges his moralizing and dismisses his moral posturing, but has also been hurt by his blame-shifting. Serving as his paid guide.
- **Valphine:** Strained — accidentally knocked her out with Ice Knife; Valphine now visibly repositions away from Soma's line of fire. Soma did retrieve a rope for her rather than leave her behind.
- **Brewbarry:** Cordial; was candid with him about the group's dynamic early on. He has begun probing her ethical consistency, questioning her about attacking fleeing creatures.
- **Corbin:** She took time to listen to his full story of his destroyed family and descent into banditry after the manticore fight. Also startled him when reverting from spider form.
- **Adabra Gwynn:** Genuine connection through shared naturalist convictions; Soma used that bond to finally move Adabra. Was stung by Adabra's casual criticism of gaps in her druidic training.
- **Meril (master, off-screen):** Gave Soma one year near her home before prompting her to follow Vukardin. Soma is aware she has limited time and incomplete training.
- **Pog and Ulla (Gnomengarde guards):** Challenged their logic at the gatehouse, though they dismissed her reasoning.
- **Fibblestib and Dabbledob (Gnomengarde inventors):** Attempted to explain clerical magic value to them but was drowned out. Disapproves of their construct-building.
