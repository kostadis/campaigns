# Meril

## Identity
Druid teacher and mentor to Soma. Has not appeared directly in sessions; known only through Soma's references and through the political controversy surrounding the staff he left her. *Also known as: meryl.*

*Also known as: meryl.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goals and drives are unknown; motivations for sending Soma away prematurely remain unexplained.
- Implied to be deliberate and enigmatic — gave Soma a staff of significant power without explanation, and cut short a druidic education that typically spans decades after only one year.
- Knowledgeable about natural threats, particularly dangerous fey and magical phenomena.
- Soma suspects Meril was himself torn between the two druidic philosophies (letting the wild be versus protecting it through intervention) and may have chosen someone outside the Enclave's habits to resolve what he could not.
- Did not give the staff for sentiment; according to Xanthopoulos, Meril gave it because he "picked her" — a deliberate choice of successor or champion for reasons still unclear.

## History with the Party
- **Prior to current campaign arc:** Meril educated Soma about will-o'-wisps, describing them as evil lights that emerge/manifest in natural environments/surroundings. This knowledge proved relevant when Soma recognized Xanth's description of strange lights haunting Dragonbarrow as a potential will-o'-wisp threat, allowing the party to enter the tomb with forewarning.
- **Prior to current campaign arc:** Late in his life, Meril stayed with Soma and took tea with her. He taught her names for things she had been doing by instinct for years. He then left her the staff and moved on. Soma describes him as an old friend who left a simple thing that got heavier later.
- **Referenced in Emerald Enclave council:** The staff Meril gave Soma has become the central object of political conflict within the Emerald Enclave. Xanthopoulos declared at the council: "I know whose staff that is. I know what it means that Meril gave it to her. He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he… picked her."
- **After the council:** Xanthopoulos quietly remarked to Soma: "Perhaps Meril made the right choice." Soma kept those seven words somewhere careful.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Unknown. Soma did not mention where Meril is or whether he can be reached. Described as having stayed with Soma "late in his life," suggesting he may have moved on to parts unknown or possibly died.
- **Active plans:** Unknown.
- **Party knowledge vs. hidden:** The party knows Meril is Soma's teacher, gave her the staff, and educated her about dangerous natural creatures like will-o'-wisps. The Emerald Enclave leadership (at least Xanthopoulos) recognizes the staff's significance and understands that Meril deliberately chose Soma for a specific purpose. The true nature of the staff, Meril's current whereabouts or fate, his internal conflict between druidic philosophies, and whether he is still alive all remain mysteries.

## Relationships
- **Soma:** Mentor-student relationship, though Soma characterizes it as an old friendship. Soma trained under Meril for only one year — extraordinarily brief for druidic training that normally takes decades. Soma holds deep reverence for Meril and treats the staff as her last physical connection to him. Meril taught her to name what she had been doing instinctively, gave her the staff with Soma's essence "in the grain," and left without full explanation. There is an undercurrent of unresolved questions — why the short training, why the staff, why her specifically. Soma believes the staff's significance is something she needs to work out on her own, though she suspects Meril left her to resolve the philosophical conflict he himself could not.
- **Xanthopoulos:** Xanthopoulos knew Meril and recognizes both the staff and the significance of Meril's choice. He publicly acknowledged that Meril "picked" Soma deliberately, and privately admitted Meril may have made the right choice — suggesting respect for Meril's judgment even amid political tension.
