# Sister Kaella

## Identity
- **Role/Faction:** Spymaster and chief intelligence operative of the Carver; ran his spy network in Phandalin. Now operating independently after a violent falling out — formerly his most capable henchwoman, currently his prisoner-turned-defector-turned-escapee. Apparent religious figure (referenced sacraments, invokes God).
- **First Appearance:** Named by Skippy as the Carver's intelligence asset before any direct encounter; later met Valphine in person in an abandoned building at the far edge of Phandalin; ultimately stumbled wounded into the clearing outside the Carver's chamber to deliver an urgent warning to the full party.

*Also known as: Sister Kayla, Sister Kella.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Survival, above all else — and once survival is secured, killing the Carver herself and dismantling his organization on her own terms. Her loyalty to the Carver degraded entirely once he accused her of intelligence failures and her survival became contingent on his death.
- **Personality:** Ruthless, methodical, and composed under extreme pressure. She killed Locutus without hesitation the moment he was no longer useful. Her first conscious words after near-death were a calm actuarial assessment of what Jax would have done to her, framed as relief and gratitude to God. She is perceptive and calculating — she identified Valphine through a drow disguise, had pre-profiled every party member, and engineered her initial meeting with Valphine entirely on her own terms. She operates through manipulation and controlled information, selecting partners she believes she can predict or use. She is pragmatic about moral ambiguity and openly acknowledges operating in gray spaces. She has a long-term orientation: as long as she is alive, she considers herself to have options and shows no indication of viewing her captivity as permanent. Her composure never breaks, even when calculating her own near-execution or being left in Corbin's custody. A killer in priest's cloth — capable of extreme violence when necessary to secure her freedom.

## History with the Party

1. **Named by Skippy (Early Sessions):** Identified by Skippy as the Carver's Spymaster — the intelligence source behind the Carver's detailed knowledge of Harbin's quest board operations. Not present at Butterskull Ranch. Current whereabouts presumed to be Phandalin, where she operates the spy network.

2. **Direct Meeting with Valphine:** Met Valphine in an abandoned, decrepit building at the far edge of Phandalin after dark, having arranged the meeting expecting Locutus. Immediately saw through Valphine's drow disguise and named her, demonstrating prior surveillance of the party. Killed Locutus on the spot with rapier and dagger after confirming he had killed Borg — a loose end she needed to close. Disclosed that the scheme to frame Valphine for stealing merchant goods had been her own plan, and that it failed because Borg failed to eliminate all witnesses when killing Big Al. This failure put her in danger with the Carver, causing her to fear his retribution and need to disappear. Proposed a partnership: she feeds Valphine intelligence to systematically eliminate the Carver's remaining lieutenants, weakening him enough to force him out of hiding, at which point she intends to kill him personally. Revealed that the Carver possesses a unique ability to communicate telepathically with his people, though the mechanism is unknown to her. She assumed Valphine was an evil drow cleric of Lolth using Lathander as a front, and that her companions were dupes or assets rather than genuine allies. She dismissed Vukradin as too naive, Brewbarry as too loyal to the bard, and Soma as too nature-focused, selecting Valphine specifically as someone she believed shared her worldview. She paid Valphine with Locutus's owed payment as a down payment, then vanished before any formal agreement was reached.

3. **Tavern Discussion (Relayed by Valphine):** Valphine relayed Kaella's offer to the party. Vukradin refused to accept what he considered stolen goods or cooperate with someone he viewed as evil. Significant party tension resulted. No formal agreement was reached.

4. **Silence:** Kaella had promised to contact the party about the Carver situation. She never followed through — her next appearance was in the clearing, wounded.

5. **The Clearing:** Stumbled into the clearing outside the Carver's chamber with her cloak gone, armor slashed, gasping for breath and barely standing. Fell to her knees before the Dragon Slayer Sword and delivered an urgent warning: the Carver had become a hollow, maddened shell speaking to his blade in a tongue that makes ears bleed, was marching on Icespire Hold that night, and intended to bind the white dragon. She warned that if not stopped before completing his dark chant, he would wear the dragon like a suit of armor and burn Phandalin to the ground to test his power. Mid-warning, Vukradin paralyzed her with Hold Person. Soma and Valphine prevented her execution; she was knocked unconscious with non-lethal force. The party immediately recognized the problem: she alone knew the location of Icespire Hold.

6. **Interrogation and Disclosure:** Valphine revived her through physical pressure rather than magical healing. Her first words upon regaining consciousness were relief at being alive, framed as gratitude to God — she had calculated that if Jax had won, she would have been tortured to death over a protracted period as punishment for perceived intelligence failures after the party killed the Carver's muscle. She was entirely composed about this assessment. She confirmed the Carver's madness and provided directions to Icespire Hold without requiring further persuasion. She disclosed that her loyalty to the Carver had not been absolute throughout, and that after the Carver accused her of intelligence failures, her survival had become contingent on his death. She explained she had made a private prior arrangement with Valphine, bypassing Vukradin deliberately — stating he had difficulty operating in morally gray environments and was therefore not a viable contact. When challenged, she acknowledged her prior arrangement with Valphine, and Valphine confirmed it was still in force. She expressed the philosophy that as long as she was alive, there was always hope. Vukradin told her she would do well in prison.

7. **Transfer to Corbin:** Left in Corbin's custody when the party departed for Icespire Hold. Vukradin authorized Corbin to beat her to one hit point and no further. Her stated preference for imprisonment was Axholm. She began screaming the moment the party walked away, calling Corbin a lunatic, the screaming continuing as the party departed. The party had left Corbin watching her because they were moving fast.

8. **Escape and Murder of Corbin:** Killed Corbin and escaped from custody. Xanthopoulos announced at the council that she had "walked out" and was "gone to the wind." Her current whereabouts are unknown. Soma described her as "a killer in priest's cloth."

- **Active:** At large as of the council meeting where Xanthopoulos announced her escape

- **What Remains Hidden:** The full nature of her prior arrangement with Valphine; the full extent of her spy network; how much she actually knows about the Carver's remaining assets, capabilities, or whereabouts; her true long-term intentions after the Carver is dead; her current whereabouts and plans following her escape

## Relationships
- **Valphine:** Private prior arrangement, confirmed still in force during interrogation. Valphine is her functional contact in the party and the reason she is alive. Initially selected Valphine as an asset, believing her to be an evil drow cleric of Lolth using Lathander as a cover — considers her the only party member who "shares her worldview." Valphine revived her using physical pressure techniques rather than magical healing. No formal agreement existed before her capture.
- **Vukradin:** Open antagonism. He paralyzed her during her warning, authorized her to be beaten to within one hit point, told her she'd do well in prison, and refused her offer of alliance outright. She assessed him as incapable of operating in morally gray environments and having difficulty dealing with morally gray situations, making him not a viable contact — a calculated assessment, not an insult.
- **Soma:** Intervened to prevent her execution alongside Valphine. She had previously dismissed him as too nature-focused to be a useful contact. He described her as "a killer in priest's cloth."
- **Brewbarry:** Dismissed as too loyal to Vukradin to be a useful contact.
- **Corbin:** Murdered him to secure her escape from custody. She had been screaming at him as the party left, calling him a lunatic. Valphine has been teaching Corbin certain sacraments — relevance to this relationship unclear.
- **The Carver:** Former employer, now a target. Loyalty broke when he blamed her for intelligence failures after the party killed his muscle. She calculated he would have had her tortured to death had Jax won. She fears his retribution and originally intended to kill him personally. She disclosed that her loyalty to him had not been absolute throughout.
- **Locutus:** Killed him at the Valphine meeting with rapier and dagger; viewed as a liability and a loose end after he killed Borg. She had been expecting him at the meeting rather than Valphine.
- **Borg:** Was working with him; his failure to eliminate witnesses when killing Big Al caused the intelligence failure that triggered her break with the Carver.
