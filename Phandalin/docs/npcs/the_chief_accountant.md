# The Chief Accountant

## Identity
- **Role:** Former accountant and administrator of an ancient dwarven fortress; a corrupted, possibly undead or spectral entity who persisted beyond death, driven by a compulsive need to maintain accurate, settled accounts. Name unknown.
- **Appearance:** Manifested as a luminous dwarven figure who glitched and flickered like corrupted data, her face composed of shifting geometric shapes with ink-like shadows leaking from her eyes.
- **Faction:** None current; formerly in service to the fortress and its operations
- **First Appearance:** Manifested at the end of the battle against six out-of-phase dwarven figures on the upper floor of the fortress
- *Also known as: Chief Accountant.*

*Also known as: Chief Accountant.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Balance the books, avoid punishment from "the auditor," and secure her own freedom
- Projects a grandmother's warmth undercut by screeching static and erratic, glitching behavior. Her speech blends mundane accounting obsessions (ledgers, depreciation, regulators) with genuine distress and paranoia.
- Methodical and high-strung; capable of a ten-minute screaming fit over an incorrect bill, yet able to achieve genuine peace once she considered her ledgers closed.
- Volatile but not purely hostile — her panic over harming Valphine revealed a capacity for fear and remorse beneath the madness.
- Opens with rants about ledgers that wouldn't close and regulators dividing by zero.

## History with the Party
1. **First Contact:** Appeared at the end of the battle against six out-of-phase dwarven figures on the upper floor of the ancient dwarven fortress. She manifested as a luminous dwarven figure who glitched and flickered like corrupted data, her face composed of shifting geometric shapes with ink-like shadows leaking from her eyes. Her voice blended a grandmother's warmth with screeching static. Immediately fixated on Valphine, calling her "sister of the sun" and accusing her of carrying "the scent of bleach and betrayal."
2. **Hostile Engagement:** Unleashed a supernatural wail that knocked both Valphine and Vukradin unconscious.
3. **Panic and Flight:** Upon seeing Valphine collapse, shifted abruptly into distress — frantically attempted to stabilize Valphine using blue-white static energy, crying out about acid damage and depreciation and expressing terror that "the auditor" would liquidate her for closing Valphine's account. Fled incorporeally through a closed door into an adjacent room.
4. **Return and Deal:** After the party rested, she cautiously returned and disclosed the history of the fortress. Revealed that Aletra (Sotorra family, fourth daughter) had brought the malfunctioning machine to the fortress, striking a deal with the king to improve the mine's efficiency by exploiting his jealousy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant operations and their revenue. Aletra had promised the installed machine would solve everything; instead it malfunctioned and unleashed chaos throughout the keep. When the Chief Accountant complained to Aletra about the books not balancing, Aletra laughed at her and then likely killed her. Offered the machine's override code in exchange for her freedom and the party's silence. The party accepted. She provided directions to the machine room on the lower level.
5. **Peaceful Departure:** Encountered again as a spirit after the spatial distortions ceased. Had become calm and relaxed once the disturbances disappeared. Informed that Aletra had fled and the machine had imploded; visibly relieved. The party presented her with an interplanetary bill for 42,000 gold pieces, triggering an extended (~10-minute) outburst over its outrageous and incorrect nature. Once the outburst subsided, she sighed, declared "The book is closed," and disappeared entirely, apparently at peace now that all accounts were considered settled.

## Current Status
- **Deceased / Departed.** Her spirit has fully departed; she is gone permanently.
- Her former quarters contained a dusty bedroom with moldy mattresses and a soot-stained fireplace. A secret door hidden within the fireplace led to a small side room. The iron chest in that room held a **dread helm** and **gauntlets of ogre power** that had belonged to her — now available to the party.

## Relationships
- **Aletra (Sotorra family, fourth daughter):** Aletra struck the deal with the king that brought the malfunctioning machine to the fortress, promising it would improve the mine's efficiency. When the Chief Accountant raised concerns about the books, Aletra laughed at her and likely killed her. Deep grievance.
- **Valphine:** Fixated on her as "sister of the sun"; volatile mix of accusation and desperate protectiveness. The connection to Aletra appears to be the source of both hostility and fascination.
- **The Auditor (unknown entity):** A figure she feared above all else — believed this entity would punish or destroy her if Valphine's "account" was closed on her watch.
- **The King:** A third party Aletra exploited — his jealousy of the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant operations made him susceptible to her promises about improving the mine's efficiency.
- **The Party:** Brief but memorable — she screamed at them for ten minutes before finding peace.
