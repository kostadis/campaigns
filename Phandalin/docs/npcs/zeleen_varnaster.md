# Zeleen Varnaster

## Identity
- **Role:** Leader of the Whiskered Gang, a wererat crew operating in Mountain's Toe Gold Mine
- **Faction:** The Whiskered Gang (formerly based at the Shrine of Savras)
- **First Appearance:** Encountered deep within the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine when the party moved to clear it

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Security and comfort for his people — shelter, food, and a role as muscle rather than laborers; never truly wanted to mine
- **Traits:** Operates with a gangster demeanor: transactional, proud, and easily offended by perceived slights. Responds well to shows of strength and straight dealing. Capable of genuine warmth when treated with respect — saw through Vukradin's dragon deception and treated it as a good joke rather than an insult, which actually warmed the relationship.
- **Behavioral Patterns:** Prone to evasion and attempting to wriggle out of responsibilities; responds to direct confrontation and implied threats; will capitulate under sufficient pressure, especially when his competence or value is publicly questioned.

## History with the Party

1. **Initial Contact:** Zeleen explained the Whiskered Gang had been displaced from the Shrine of Savras by roughly sixteen orcs and several ogres (including members of a tribe the party had previously angered). She proposed a deal: party clears the shrine, wererats vacate the mine. She offered only two warriors to assist, claiming she was too valuable to risk.
2. **The Cheese Negotiation:** Valphine attempted a cheese-for-labor deal, which Zeleen found stereotypical and offensive — but ultimately admitted genuine interest in cheese from the special cow at Butterskull Ranch. **Valphine sensed Zeleen was lying and likely intended to keep the mine regardless of any deal.**
3. **Don-Jon Raskin's Death:** When questioned about the death of Don-Jon Raskin, Zeleen gave a semi-confession — "kill is such a strong word" — and admitted Don-Jon had been "conveniently lured to his death." He did not fully confess. He agreed to give the party Don-Jon's share of the mine in exchange for handling the carrion crawler problem. *Note: Valphine's investigation of the death scene found dwarf footprints leading away from the body, complicating actual culpability.*
4. **Relief at Orc Attack:** Zeleen appeared after the party secured the area outside the mine, expressing relief at their timely arrival against the orc and ogre attackers.
5. **Cheese Delivery:** He and the other wererats were delighted when Valphine presented them with Petunia's cheese.
6. **Final Agreement:** Brewbarry countered Zeleen's demand for 1,000 gp upfront by pointing out the wererats were already receiving shelter, cheese, and protection — with a blood-stained axe making the subtext clear. Zeleen was sufficiently impressed by both the argument and the implied intimidation to agree to protect the mine for free, reframing it as protecting his home and family. He shook hands with Horia to seal the deal.
7. **Parting Intelligence:** Warned the party that orcs, ogres, and a dragon are still active in the area. Suggested the Shrine of Savras as a source of information on the dragon. Noted the dragon's agricultural raids are so severe there will be no beer brewed this year.
8. **The Earthstone Gem Crisis:** When the party arrived at the mine and found no mining occurring, Zeleen greeted them with forced optimism ("Hey, boss! What's up!") and tried to cover for the dwarves, claiming they had been "working really, really hard." When pressed by Valphine and Brewbarry about the lack of gold production, he explained the dwarves needed an "Earthstone Gem" before they could mine, but argued his crew was hired to protect, not "go on fetch quests." After multiple attempts to wriggle out of the responsibility and Valphine's blistering rebuke calling the were-rats "a waste of cheese," Zeleen's will broke. He capitulated loudly, promising "we could make that happen, Mr. Boss. Mr. Right," and led his delegation into the orc-infested forest to retrieve the gem, motivated further by Brewbarry tapping his halberd against his palm. Valphine and Brewbarry had low confidence in his ability to succeed, prompting them to arrange a backup plan (a fake gem) with Horia Stonetallow.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Mountain's Toe Gold Mine — now its de facto protector
- **Active Arrangement:** Wererats provide security and menial labor; dwarves handle mining; Whiskered Gang receives regular cheese shipments from Butterskull Ranch and shelter in their section of the mine. Arrangement is contingent on the wererats apologizing for killing dwarves in earlier conflicts.
- **Current Task:** Last seen leading his delegation into orc-infested forest to retrieve the Earthstone Gem required for mining operations
- **What the Party Knows:** Zeleen is bound by a handshake agreement with Horia and appears genuinely invested in the arrangement, though he attempts to avoid responsibilities beyond basic protection when possible
- **What Remains Hidden:** Valphine's read suggests Zeleen's original intentions were to keep the mine regardless of any deal — whether that ambition is truly set aside is unconfirmed

## Relationships
- **The Whiskered Gang:** Their leader and protector; deeply motivated by keeping his people safe and comfortable
- **Horia Stonetallow:** Sealed the mine agreement with a handshake; primary point of formal agreement. Horia was involved in creating a backup plan (fake gem) when Zeleen was sent to retrieve the Earthstone Gem
- **Brewbarry:** Earned Zeleen's respect through blunt intimidation and sound argument; Brewbarry's still-bloody axe and later his halberd-tapping served as effective motivators for Zeleen's compliance
- **Vukradin:** Offered a piece of Petunia's cheese as a goodwill gesture; their rapport was strengthened when Zeleen saw through Vukradin's dragon bluff and laughed it off. Vukradin's insight revealed that Zeleen and his people never truly wanted to mine and always preferred acting as muscle over hard labor
- **Valphine:** Tense history — the cheese negotiation caused offense; Valphine privately believes Zeleen cannot be fully trusted. Later delivered a devastating rebuke calling the were-rats "a waste of cheese," which broke Zeleen's resistance
- **Orcs/Ogres/Dragon:** Active threats Zeleen is aware of and concerned about; his former home (Shrine of Savras) is currently occupied by these forces
