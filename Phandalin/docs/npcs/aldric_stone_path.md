# Aldric Stone Path

## Identity
- Druid aligned with Adabra's anti-interventionist faction
- Attended the druid council where Adabra's position was debated
- Described as a weathered mountain man

## Personality & Motivations
Aldric has long held anti-interventionist views — the same position for years — but was largely ignored by other druids, treated like "wind through grass." His core drive is loyalty to Adabra, who was the first person to actually listen to him. That validation matters deeply to him, and he fastened himself to her cause because of it. He is earnest but not intellectually rigorous in defending the position he champions.

## History with the Party
- **Druid Council Session:** Aldric was present supporting Adabra's anti-interventionist stance. When Soma directly challenged him — asking what Adabra actually proposed to *do* about the planar anomalies and blights — Aldric opened his mouth, closed it, and had no answer. He sat down in silence. Adabra noticed this as a loss to her position.
- The presiding druid cited Aldric by name in ruling against Adabra, using his own admission to argue that following Adabra's logic would mean treating the orc situation and other threats as "perfectly normal."

## Current Status
- Last seen at the druid council after sitting down, having failed to defend the anti-interventionist position
- His inability to answer Soma's question was used as ammunition against Adabra's faction
- No known active plans beyond his alignment with Adabra

## Relationships
- **Adabra:** Deeply loyal. She listened to him when no one else would, and he attached himself to her cause in return. His failure at council was a visible blow to her standing.
- **Soma:** Soma understood the nature of Aldric's loyalty but also exposed its limits by pressing him on substance he couldn't provide.
- **The Presiding Druid:** Used Aldric's own concession against Adabra's faction in the ruling.

## Arc Score Events
- Aldric's inability to answer Soma's challenge contributed to the council ruling against Adabra (likely a decrease in Adabra's faction influence / arc score).
