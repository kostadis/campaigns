# Lady Alagondar

## Identity
- **Role:** Deceased noble; original owner of the Dragon Slayer Sword
- **Faction:** Unknown
- **First Appearance:** Does not appear in person; known through Boney's recollections and the physical arrangement of the Dragonbarrow. Her ghost later materialized in the northern chamber.

*Also known as: "Lady Alagondar's Ghost", Lady Alagondar S Ghost.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Her core goals and drives are unknown beyond having undertaken some important quest for which she required a revived companion.
- Her character is inferred only through Boney's loyalty and the care with which her burial arrangement was constructed.
- As a ghost, she was silent and mournful — no communication was attempted. Her gaze carried clear directionality toward the Ice Spire Mountains, suggesting either a warning or an unresolved connection to the White Dragon.
- Did not speak when she appeared; stared with hollow, mournful eyes.
- Made good choices in companions — specifically in her selection of Boney as her mount, earning Soma's respect for her taste in horses.

## History with the Party
1. Lady Alagondar never interacted with the party directly in life.
2. Boney revealed that she was his original master, and that he was brought back from the dead specifically to aid her on an important quest.
3. Boney believed he had been left in the Dragonbarrow to guard something on her behalf after her death.
4. Boney indicated that Lady Alagondar was laid to rest in a chamber within the Dragonbarrow, and that the Dragon Slayer Sword was buried "across the way" from where she rested, pointing north.
5. The party discovered the Dragon Slayer Sword resting atop a dragon's skull in the northern chamber of the Dragonbarrow, confirming Boney's account of its placement "across the way" from her resting chamber.
6. After the party defeated the invisible stalker and secured the Dragon Slayer Sword, her ghost materialized in the northern chamber. She stared fixedly with hollow, mournful eyes toward the Ice Spire Mountains, then faded without a sound. Her gaze prompted Vukradin to recall that the White Dragon lairs in that region.
7. Soma reflected on Lady Alagondar while riding Boney, noting that whoever Lady Alagondar was, she made good choices and had excellent taste in horses.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Entombed in a chamber within the Dragonbarrow; ghost faded silently from the northern chamber
- **Active plans:** None — deceased. Her ghost's connection to the Dragon Slayer Sword appears significant (she appeared only when it was secured). Her fixed gaze toward the Ice Spire Mountains may indicate unfinished purpose tied to the dragon threat.
- **Party knows:** She was a noble, owned the Dragon Slayer Sword, was buried in the Dragonbarrow, inspired deep loyalty in Boney, and her ghost pointed toward the White Dragon's territory
- **Hidden:** The nature of her quest, why the sword was buried separately, what Boney was guarding, and whether she will appear again

## Relationships
- **Boney:** Her revived companion and loyal servant; his entire identity is oriented around service to her. Now serves as Soma's mount.
- **Soma:** Respects Lady Alagondar's taste in horses and choices in companions based on Boney's qualities
- **The Dragon Slayer Sword:** Her appearance coincided directly with the party securing it — connection unknown but likely significant
- **The White Dragon (Ice Spire Mountains):** Her gaze pointed toward this region; nature of connection unknown
