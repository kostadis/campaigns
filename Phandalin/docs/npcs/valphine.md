# Valphine

## Identity
- **Role:** Cleric of Lathander (surface-convert, Drow origin)
- **Faction:** Traveling with the party; nominal affiliation with Lathander's clergy
- **First Appearance:** Battle against an ogre on a mountain path, morning of 02-01 Taraskh 1495

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Serving Lathander while reconciling her Underdark instincts with surface morality; accumulating power and resources on her own terms.
- Pragmatic and unsentimental — her first instinct in most situations is leverage, coercion, or controlled violence. She finds torture a reasonable tool and "fair trades" of lives an unremarkable concept.
- Demonstrates dry amusement at manipulation and political chaos, respecting Vukradin's destabilization techniques as recognizably Drow in character.
- Actively adjusting to Lathander's expectations, noting the tension between her Underdark reflexes (sacrifice, hunting, dominance, the thrill of killing) and her adopted faith. The adjustment is genuine but incomplete.

## History with the Party

**Ogre Ambush (02-01 Taraskh 1495)**
Participated in the mountain path battle. Attempted a mace strike on the prone ogre but missed badly, earning mockery from Soma. Wounded the fleeing ogre with a crossbow bolt but did not kill it. Looted the body afterward, recovering a whistle, coins, and a key.

**Gnomengarde**
Declined the cliff climb initially, requiring Soma to bring her a rope before she ascended. Watched the entry negotiations with guards Pog and Ulla without engaging directly.

In the inventors' workshop, identified herself as a cleric to gnome inventors Fibblestib and Dabbledob, who dismissed clerical magic in favor of their own inventions. Privately concluded she could not cure King Korboz and that the party couldn't afford to pursue it regardless. When hearing about the missing gnomes Warren and Orrin, immediately considered torture as a solution to the king's madness, finding herself aligned with Brewbarry's instincts before Vukradin redirected the group toward music.

When confronting guards Pog and Ulla near the barrel room, accused both gnomes of being the shapeshifter, deliberately using Ulla's own words against her and successfully turning the two gnomes on each other in paranoid accusation. Found the exercise amusing.

In the cellar, located the mimic by pointing a crossbow at the first barrel, which provoked it to transform and attack. Called out the discovery to the group, positioned herself clear of Soma's Ice Knife blast radius, and quoted a childhood rhyme after observing Vukradin's verbal attacks visibly hurt the creature.

**Post-Gnomengarde / Dual Monarchy Debrief**
Watched Vukradin's political destabilization of the dual monarchy with approval and amusement, comparing his technique to good Drow practice. Inserted herself once to suggest the kings needed "a clear approver structure," drawing on her firsthand experience watching commune-style cultures collapse into betrayal and violence.

**Umbrage Hill Approach**
Was annoyed at the 25-gold rescue rate for Adabra, having been cautioned about it beforehand by Lathander's Cleric. Spotted a shadow slipping behind a boulder on approach and tensed, then dismissed the instinct, reminding herself she was not in the Dark Realms. During the bandit ambush, was struck by a bandit's crossbow bolt. Coolly shot a fleeing bandit in the back with her hand crossbow, enabling Vukradin to finish him.

**Manticore Fight at Umbrage Hill**
Fired the opening shot against the manticore with her hand crossbow, hitting it but failing to do significant damage. Absorbed a full volley of tail spikes when the manticore singled her out in retaliation. Attempted a second shot later in the fight and missed. Internally noted the manticore's offer to take Corbin in exchange for the group was "a fair trade" she would have considered in another life. Observed Vukradin's combat uncertainty with a gambler-reader's eye. When Adabra emerged from the windmill, immediately offered to teach her about Lathander. Noted that the food Adabra prepared was exceptional and attributed its quality to Lathander's glory. Last seen preparing to return to Phandalin from Umbrage Hill.

**Orc Scout Encounter (Return Journey)**
Led the march and was first to spot the poorly concealed orc scout in the bushes. Remarked that orcs make fine slaves. Fired her crossbow repeatedly in battle, missing several times before landing a hit. Expressed open contempt for Soma's Moonbeam, comparing it unfavorably to Lathander's searing sunlight. Noted that the fleeing orc scout appeared to recognize her as a Drow worshipping Lathander and panicked. Pursued the fleeing scout into the forest but lost him — the surface terrain gave him equal visibility and neutralized her tracking advantage. Returned to interrogate the captured orc (with Brewbarry) regarding orc cultists of Talos; asserted dominance by declaring he now worked for the party.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Returning to Phandalin from Umbrage Hill
- **Active:** Holding a captured orc she has conscripted as a party asset; aware of orc cultists of Talos operating in the region
- **Party Knows:** She is a Drow, a Lathander cleric, and has a violent, domineering background
- **Hidden:** The full depth of her Underdark moral calculus (e.g., having genuinely weighed trading Corbin's life to the manticore); the specific nature of her adjustment to Lathander's faith and how tenuous it may be; her ongoing struggle with Underdark instincts of sacrifice, hunting, and the thrill of killing

## Relationships
- **Soma:** Mild antagonism. Soma mocked her early miss; she has returned contempt, openly criticizing Soma's Moonbeam as inferior to Lathander's light.
- **Vukradin:** Genuine respect. Recognizes his manipulation techniques as familiar and effective; views him as a capable peer. Observed his combat uncertainty with a gambler-reader's eye.
- **Brewbarry:** Ideological alignment on interrogation and coercion methods. Coordinated naturally with him during the orc prisoner interrogation.
- **Adabra:** Immediate missionary instinct upon meeting her; offered to teach her about Lathander.
- **Corbin:** Privately assessed him as an acceptable loss in the manticore exchange. No demonstrated warmth.
- **Lathander's Cleric (unnamed):** Warned her about the 25-gold Adabra rate; some prior rapport implied.
