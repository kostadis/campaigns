# Prutha

## Identity
- **Role:** Former Circle of Thunder warrior, newly converted follower of Lathander
- **Faction:** Circle of Thunder (defected); now aligned with Valphine's emerging Lathanderian practice
- **First Appearance:** Last orc standing after the battle at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder. Surrendered following Vukradin's speech and declared himself to Valphine on the spot.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Survival through theological pragmatism — he converted because Lathander seemed stronger than Talos, not out of spiritual crisis. That said, the bone whistle moment suggests something real underneath the arithmetic.
- Blunt, enthusiastic, and uncomplicated in his loyalties once they are declared. He pledged himself to Valphine twice, in case once wasn't enough.
- Harbors real hostility toward committed Talosians and has a clear personal theology: orcs who left Talos should be spared; true believers convert or die.
- His violence is performative in service of belonging — the corpse-kicking reads as demonstration, not cruelty for its own sake. Vukradin noticed.

## History with the Party
1. **Conversion:** After the battle, Prutha was the last orc standing. He assessed his odds, set down his greataxe, and declared for Lathander upon seeing Valphine — framing it as a power upgrade: *"Talos offer power. Lathander — more power. Lathander all-powerful."*
2. **Name:** Valphine suggested the conversion name Thrall. Vukradin objected. Prutha kept his own name.
3. **Backstory shared:** His great-uncle was the clan shaman. The Talosians killed him early and deliberately — calling him "thunder meat" — to break clan resistance before offering terms: fight for Talos or share the old man's fate. Prutha fought. Three skirmishes. Never killed by choice.
4. **The whistle:** Produced a bone whistle from his clothing, explained it was his great-uncle's tradition — blowing it at sunrise each morning. He held it, blew one note, and wept. Valphine read the tears as grief for the silence between then and now, not for the uncle or the fighting itself.
5. **Great-uncle's phrase recited:** *"Dawn comes for everyone, even those born in shadow."* The table went quiet. Vukradin called it poetic and meant it.
6. **Post-battle conduct:** Enthusiastically strung up the dead anchorite's body as a warning and kicked the corpse several times. Vukradin noted the enthusiasm without acting on it.
7. **Blessing received:** Accepted Vukradin's blessing — *"Lathander blesses you today, my child"* — and responded that he thought that was awesome and had a lot to think about.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Present with the party at the treeline below the Circle of Thunder.
- **Active situation:** Newly converted, newly attached to Valphine. No independent operations.
- **What the party knows:** Everything above — he was forthcoming.
- **What remains hidden:** Whether his conversion has any depth beyond pragmatism. The whistle moment suggests it might, but this is unresolved.

## Relationships
- **Valphine:** Primary attachment. Declared follower, twice over. The bone whistle tradition may be incorporated into Valphine's evolving practice — potential meaningful thread.
- **Vukradin:** Respect, warmth. Vukradin called him *his first* and blessed him. Vukradin also clocked the corpse-kicking enthusiasm and the name dispute (sided with Prutha over Valphine's suggestion).
- **Great-uncle (deceased):** The emotional center of his backstory. Shaman. Killed by Talosians. Source of the whistle tradition and the dawn phrase.
- **Circle of Thunder:** Former faction. His stated position is that non-committed orcs should be spared and offered conversion; committed Talosians get no quarter.
