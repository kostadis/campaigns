Withering Grove 
As a GM and TTRPG designer with a passion for surrealistic adventures that bend the very fabric of reality, I'm excited to help you flesh out "The Withering Grove" encounter. This quest provides an excellent early opportunity to introduce the hidden forces at play in your meta-campaign, blurring the lines between natural blight and cosmic distortion, and laying the groundwork for the creeping paranoia and existential threats to come.
Here's how we can inject a deep sense of unsettling ambiguity and surrealism into "The Withering Grove," weaving in the machinations of the Kraken Society and the unintended consequences of KP's planar experiments:
# The Withering Grove: A Planar Scar on the Land (Levels 3–4)

**Goal:** Investigate a once-thriving grove near Phandalin that is now dying, with strange magical blight spreading.
**Location Integration:** This quest can occur after or alongside the "Butterskull Ranch Quest," with **Adabra's suspicions arising in the nearby woods**. The grove itself should feel unnaturally *wrong*, hinting at forces beyond simple disease or even traditional dark magic.

**1. The Blight's Surreal Manifestation: KP's "Minor Miscalculations"**
The "blight" isn't merely decay; it's a symptom of **planar instability caused by Knazreponnium Ketternopappux (KP)**. KP, a gnome scholar obsessed with "multi-planar optimum for economic efficiency" regardless of "local social cohesion," views such chaos as mere "minor miscalculations" in his grand experiments to "streamline interplanar routes".
- **Visual Distortions:** The grove's plants don't just die; they become **unnaturally vivid or sickly, with bizarrely twisted shapes**. Foliage might pulse with a non-photosynthetic internal light, or bark might feel like hardened flesh. Colors could be subtly off, creating an unsettling palette that is both vibrant and diseased.
- **Sensory Anomalies:** Pockets of localized, unnatural fog might drift aimlessly. The air could hum faintly with an unsettling, non-magical energy. **Minor, localized gravity shifts** or **phantom sounds that seem to phase in and out of reality** might occur, representing "residual energies" from KP's prototypes and initial attempts at "cosmic engineering".
- **Planar Distortion Score Impact:** Experiencing or investigating these localized planar anomalies related to flora or fauna, especially in Aletra's presence (if she is introduced observing covertly, as per "The Unnatural Harvest" encounter), or simply by encountering these *wrong* phenomena, **increases the party's hidden Planar Distortion Score by +1**. This silently tracks their proximity to understanding KP's influence.

**2. Adabra's Fanaticism: Unwittingly Accurate Warnings**
**Adabra Gwynn**, the local midwife and acolyte of Chauntea, is a **fanatical naturalist**. She views the Interventionists as "dangerous meddling" that threatens nature's true balance.
- **Misdirected Truth:** Adabra will claim the Interventionists are "a disease upon the land" and that "their mere presence, their *interference*, draws these calamities!". Crucially, her warnings are **true but misdirected**; she senses a profound danger and disruption, but she **doesn't know about the Kraken Society's manipulation** behind the Interventionists' actions or KP's cosmic blunders.
- **Indoctrination:** She will use "subtle indoctrination," appealing to the party's desire to protect nature and framing her requests as a "sacred duty". If **Soma**, the tortle druid, is present, Adabra might directly ask her if she aligns with the "naturalist or interventionist side" in the "great schism".
- **Call to Action:** Adabra urges the party to aid her efforts to "heal" the land, which often involves disrupting Interventionist activities she deems harmful. This choice can affect her "Fury of the Wild" track later in the campaign, potentially pushing her towards alliance with Tiamat if she feels betrayed or desperate.

**3. The Interventionists: Victims of Subtle Manipulation**
The **Interventionists** claim they are trying to "cleanse a planar corruption source beneath the grove". However, they are **secretly combating the Kraken Society's influence** and are reacting to manipulation rather than acting purely out of their own zealotry.
- **Psionic Distress:** If the party encounters an Interventionist patrol (e.g., two Veterans and a Scout), these individuals might appear weary and exhibit signs of **psionic influence**. They could frequently rub their temples, complaining of **persistent "headaches" or "disturbing dreams"** they can't recall clearly. This is a subtle hint of the **Kraken Society's memory tampering**.
- **Strange Orders:** The Interventionists might carry official Emerald Enclave scrolls with seemingly normal orders, but some sections are **marred by unusual, geometric symbols or faint ink smudges resembling tentacles**. A successful **DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana or Investigation) check** could reveal these as out-of-place, suggesting an outside influence. This discovery is an **Echoes Score trigger (+1)** for the Kraken Society's hidden presence.
- **Unknowing Struggle:** Their discussions might reveal frustration with "conflicting reports" or "unseen forces" making their work difficult, reinforcing that they are unaware of being manipulated. They are genuinely attempting to contain "unnatural ecological disturbances".

**4. Surreal Encounters within the Grove**
The creatures within the grove should enhance the feeling of reality breaking down:
- **Blighted Animals:** Not just diseased, but **fundamentally altered**. A rabbit might have too many eyes, a deer's antlers might be made of shimmering, distorted light, or a pack of wolves might move with a jerky, inhuman gait. Their behavior is often contradictory – unnaturally calm in the face of danger, or randomly aggressive towards nothing. This is a direct consequence of the localized planar anomalies stemming from KP's "residual energies".
- **Corrupted Treants:** These ancient protectors are twisted parodies of themselves. Their bark might be polished like obsidian, constantly weeping a viscous, iridescent sap. Their branches could twist into impossible, non-Euclidean angles. When they move or speak, their voices might be **overlapping echoes of many whispers, hinting at fractured minds**.
- **Planar Rift Creatures:** Instead of standard elementals, the party might encounter beings that are **partially phased into reality, flickering like old film**. They might leave behind bizarre, glowing footprints that distort light, or emit phantom sounds. These could even be disoriented **Mindflayer thralls** caught in the planar eddies inadvertently created by KP's past "optimizations" of interplanar routes. Such direct manifestations **increase the Planar Distortion Score by +2**.

**5. Player Choices and Deepening Ambiguity**
The party's decisions in "The Withering Grove" are crucial and will resonate throughout the campaign:
- **Moral Dilemma:** The true ambiguity lies in the fact that **both Adabra and the Interventionists are operating on incomplete information**, albeit with different motivations and methods. Adabra is a zealot, but her sense of danger is keen. The Interventionists are well-intentioned, but they are puppets. The **Kraken Society is the shadowy hand** exploiting the chaos, and KP is the unwitting architect of the underlying cosmic mess.
- **Impact on Alliances:**
  - **Aiding Adabra** might involve direct conflict with the Interventionists, earning Adabra's trust but deepening the rift between factions. This could also unknowingly empower Adabra's dark transformation later.
  - **Supporting the Interventionists' rituals** might bring the party closer to discovering definitive evidence of Kraken Society manipulation, revealing the broader conspiracy and complicating their view of all parties involved.
- **Unseen Hand:** Regardless of their choice, the underlying threat of KP's machinations and the Kraken Society's subtle infiltration continues to grow. The party's actions here determine if they become pawns, enlightened combatants, or unwitting tools in a cosmic struggle far beyond a mere local blight.
By carefully layering these elements, "The Withering Grove" transforms from a simple monster hunt into a **surrealistic first encounter with the insidious forces reshaping reality**, planting seeds of paranoia and the unsettling truth that not all dangers are what they seem.

They claim to purge planar corruption, but their meddling *spreads* the sickness. They silence those who question, and the land suffers for it.”

Tell me—where do you stand in this great schism? With the naturalists who honor the earth’s will, or with the Interventionists who force their will upon it?”
She holds Soma’s gaze, earnest and unyielding. “This is a sacred duty. Will you stand with me to protect the grove? To disrupt their harmful rituals and restore the land’s true wildness before it’s lost forever?”

# Encounters on the way to the grove**1. The Flickering Stag**
- Description: A majestic stag with shimmering, flickering fur that phases in and out of reality. Its antlers seem to bend physics, curving impossibly.
- Challenge: The stag is territorial but confused by its own flickering existence. It attacks with erratic, teleporting charges.
- Mechanics:
  - The stag can teleport 15 feet as a bonus action, leaving behind brief afterimages that distract foes (granting disadvantage on the next attack against the stag).
  - Its hooves create temporary zones of distorted gravity (difficult terrain for 1 round).
- Narrative: The stag’s presence hints at planar instability—nature itself is unraveling.

### **2. The Whispering Fog**
- Description: A low-lying fog drifts through the path, carrying faint whispers in an unknown language. The air tingles with static.
- Challenge: Anyone passing through must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or be *charmed* by the fog for 1 minute, hearing compelling but cryptic voices urging them deeper into the grove.
- Mechanics:
  - While charmed, affected characters move toward the heart of the blight, ignoring danger.
  - The fog obscures vision, imposing disadvantage on Perception checks.
- Narrative: The fog is a subtle planar echo, pulling the party toward the source of distortion.

### **3. The Blighted Bramble Patch**
- Description: A dense thicket of brambles with leaves that pulse with unnatural colors—sometimes glowing, sometimes withering to black.
- Challenge: The brambles lash out when disturbed, attacking with thorny tendrils.
- Mechanics:
  - The brambles have a reach of 10 feet and deal piercing damage plus a lingering necrotic effect.
  - They regenerate 5 HP at the start of their turn unless exposed to radiant damage.
- Narrative: The brambles are corrupted by the planar blight, a living barrier that resists intrusion.

### **4. The Shattered Reflection Pool**
- Description: A small, still pool reflects not the party, but twisted, distorted versions of themselves—faces warped in anguish or joy.
- Challenge: Gazing into the pool risks mental strain.
- Mechanics:
  - Anyone who looks into the pool must succeed on a DC 14 Intelligence saving throw or gain one level of short-term madness (DMG p. 258).
  - The pool can be used to communicate with faint echoes of past victims or KP’s experiments, giving cryptic clues.
- Narrative: The pool is a planar scar, a window into fractured realities.

### **5. The Echoing Howl**
- Description: Distant howls echo unnaturally, sometimes overlapping or out of sync with the environment.
- Challenge: Wolves or wolf-like creatures with distorted features (e.g., extra eyes, flickering forms) hunt the area.
- Mechanics:
  - The wolves have pack tactics but also an unsettling “jerky” movement pattern that grants them advantage on Dexterity saving throws.
  - When they howl as a pack, all creatures within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened for 1 round.
- Narrative: The howls are a warning and a symptom of the grove’s corruption.

# Battle map 

You step into a clearing roughly 60 feet across, but something feels deeply wrong. The dense woods surrounding you lean inward unnaturally, their twisted black bark gleaming like obsidian in the faint, eerie light filtering through the broken canopy above. Shadows flicker independently of any light source, as if the grove itself watches you. The air hums faintly with a strange energy.
Pools of iridescent sap shimmer at scattered points, their colors shifting hypnotically. The thick underbrush beneath your feet is blackened and thorny, snagging at your armor and skin. At the heart of the clearing stands an immense treant-like figure, its limbs bending in impossible geometries, dripping glowing sap into a pool below. Near it, faint, flickering tears in reality pulse softly, whispering promises of power and madness.

Size & Shape:A roughly circular clearing about 60 feet in diameter, surrounded by dense, twisted woods that seem to lean inward unnaturally. The canopy above is thick but broken in places, allowing eerie shafts of pale, unnatural light to fall unevenly.

Key Features:
1. Twisted Trees (Edges of the Grove):
  - Ancient, gnarled trees with blackened, glossy bark that reflects light like obsidian. Their branches twist into impossible angles, some seemingly bending through space itself.
  - These trees provide partial cover (+2 AC and Dexterity saving throws) for creatures behind them.
  - Occasionally, a branch moves subtly, as if alive and watching.
1. Iridescent Sap Pools (3 spots, scattered):
  - Small pools of shimmering, viscous sap that glow faintly with shifting colors.
  - Standing in a sap pool reduces movement speed by half and causes mild hallucinations. Creatures must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw at the start of their turn or be *confused* until the start of their next turn.
  - The sap can be collected or disturbed, provoking a reaction from the Corrupted Treant or other guardians.
1. Planar Rifts (2 spots near center):
  - Flickering, semi-transparent tears in reality. They pulse with faint light and emit low hums.
  - Creatures within 10 feet of a rift have disadvantage on saving throws against psychic effects.
  - Occasionally, a distorted creature (like a Planar Rift Shade) might phase partially through one to ambush the party.
1. Blighted Underbrush:
  - The ground is thick with blackened, thorny underbrush that counts as difficult terrain.
  - Moving through it without care risks minor damage (1d4 piercing per 5 feet moved) unless a Dexterity saving throw (DC 12) is made.
1. The Heart Tree (Centerpiece):
  - A massive, corrupted treant-like figure stands at the center, its bark polished obsidian, limbs bending in impossible geometries.
  - Its iridescent sap drips steadily, pooling into one of the sap pools.
  - The Heart Tree is the main antagonist here, controlling the grove’s corruption and summoning planar distortions.
1. Flickering Shadows:
  - Shadows in the grove seem to move independently, occasionally shifting or flickering oddly.
  - These shadows can obscure vision, imposing disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight beyond 20 feet.

Tactical Notes:
- The mix of cover, difficult terrain, and environmental hazards encourages strategic movement and positioning.
- Planar Rifts can be used by enemies for surprise attacks or retreats, adding unpredictability.
- The sap pools and underbrush create zones players might want to avoid or use tactically.
- The Heart Tree’s impossible geometry and overlapping whispers can be described vividly to enhance the surreal atmosphere.
