### **Encounter: The Anonymous Letter**
Setting:The party is resting or conducting business in a bustling tavern or the Order’s headquarters. The atmosphere is lively but tense due to recent rumors.
How the Letter is Found:
- The letter is slipped under the door of Vukradin’s quarters or the party’s meeting room early in the morning.
- It bears no signature but is sealed with a wax emblem resembling a gauntlet, though subtly altered—an intentional forgery.
- The letter contains detailed accusations about House Margaster’s donations, claiming they fund slave trades, illicit businesses, and “blood gold,” directly contradicting the Order’s principles.
Letter Excerpt (example):*"To those who cherish truth and honor, beware the golden gifts that come wrapped in shadows. House Margaster’s coin carries the weight of chains and betrayal. The Order’s ‘conflict-free gold’ is stained with the suffering of innocents. Question the gifts, or become complicit in the taint."*

### **Jena Roscoe’s Reaction**
- Initial Demeanor: When the letter is brought to her attention, Jena feigns shock and concern. She presents herself as a loyal patron and ally who “shares your pain” over these revelations.
- Subtle Manipulation: She urges caution, suggesting the letter might be a smear campaign by enemies of the Order or the Lord’s Alliance.
- Offer of Aid: Jena offers to help “investigate discreetly,” positioning herself as a trusted confidante and resource.
- Hidden Agenda: Behind the scenes, she’s pleased the letter has sown doubt and is ready to escalate the campaign if needed.

### **Party Investigation Opportunities**
1. Inspecting the Letter:
- Forgery Clues: A successful Investigation or Insight check reveals the wax seal is a clever fake, not matching official Order seals.
- Ink and Paper: Arcana or History checks might detect unusual ink or paper from Waterdeep, suggesting outside involvement.
2. Gathering Rumors:
- The party can question tavern patrons, merchants, or minor nobles.
- Some might hint at recent unusual visitors or overheard conversations about “dirty money” and House Margaster’s shadowy dealings.
3. Tracking the Source:
- Following the trail leads to a contact or agent working for Jena, who may be overheard bragging about “playing the long game” or “shaking the foundations.”
- The party could set a trap or tail suspicious figures delivering similar letters.
4. Confronting Jena:
- If the party confronts Jena, she remains composed, denying direct involvement but dropping veiled threats or warnings about “dangerous truths.”
- A Persuasion or Intimidation check might pressure her into revealing partial truths or backing off temporarily.

### **Possible Outcomes**
- Expose the Plot: The party reveals Jena’s manipulation, restoring trust in the Order and House Margaster (or uncovering real corruption).
- Fall for the Deception: The party’s doubts weaken the Order’s standing, allowing Jena and the Lord’s Alliance to gain influence.
- Gray Area: The truth is murky; some donations are tainted, but the Order’s cause remains just, forcing difficult choices.

### **Scene: Jena’s Introduction to the Party (Unaware of the Letter)**
Setting: A bustling inn or market in Phandalin or a nearby town where the party is present.

Jena’s Approach:
Jena approaches with calm confidence, dressed in practical but elegant clothing suited for travel and discreet dealings. Her gaze is sharp but polite, and her tone is professional yet warm.
Jena:*“Good day. I am Jena Roscoe, acting on behalf of Lord Neverember and the Lord’s Alliance. I am currently looking into some troubling reports concerning House Margaster—specifically, their possible involvement with agents and events in this region.”*
(She inclines her head slightly, gauging the party’s reactions.)
*“It’s said that their influence may be extending into less savory dealings near Phandalin, and I’ve been tasked with uncovering the truth. If you have encountered anything unusual or suspicious related to the Margasters, I would be grateful to hear of it.”*
(She offers a small, earnest smile.)
*“Information is often the sharpest tool in these matters. Should you wish to speak further or require assistance, I am here to help.”*

### **How This Leads to the Letter**
- The party, having found the anonymous letter implicating House Margaster, now has a natural and credible contact to bring it to.
- Jena’s official investigation status makes her a trustworthy figure to share concerns with, even if she doesn’t yet know about the letter.
- This also positions Jena as a potential ally or adversary depending on how the party interacts with her and whether they uncover her true motives.
- 
1. Party shares the letter or mentions suspicious donations openly
Party:*"We have something that might interest you—a letter accusing House Margaster of shady dealings and tainted donations to the Order."*
Jena (feigning surprise and concern):*"This is troubling indeed. Such allegations, if true, could undermine everything the Order stands for. Thank you for bringing this to my attention. I will look into this matter personally. Your vigilance is commendable."(She subtly watches the party for signs of doubt or weakness.)*

2. Party is cautious and asks about Jena’s intentions
Party:*"Why should we trust you? What exactly is the Lord’s Alliance hoping to gain from this investigation?"*
Jena (smiling diplomatically):*"A fair question. The Lord’s Alliance seeks stability and prosperity for the North. Corruption anywhere threatens us all. I am here to ensure that influence is wielded wisely, and that no one—noble or commoner—acts above the law. Your cooperation would be invaluable in this pursuit of justice."*

3. Party withholds information or tests Jena
Party:*"We haven’t seen anything concrete yet. Perhaps you should share what you’ve found so far."*
Jena (with a slight, knowing smile):*"Investigations require patience and discretion. I assure you, the threads I follow are tangled and delicate. But if you come across anything—letters, strange meetings, unusual donations—please think of me. Together, we can uncover the truth."*

### **After the Letter is Presented to Jena**

Jena (after reading the letter, carefully composed):*"This letter… it raises serious concerns. However, anonymous accusations can be tools of deception. We must be cautious not to act on whispers alone. I propose we work together to verify these claims. If House Margaster’s influence is as insidious as this suggests, it will reveal itself under scrutiny."(She subtly steers the party toward gathering more evidence, all while maintaining her guise of allyship.)*

### **Potential Subtext and Roleplay Tips for Jena**
- She never outright denies the letter’s claims but always emphasizes caution and investigation, buying time.
- She positions herself as the party’s best resource, encouraging them to share information and rely on her.
- Her tone is friendly but calculating, balancing sympathy with subtle pressure.
- If pressed too hard, she might hint at dangers in pursuing the truth too aggressively, implying powerful enemies.
### **1. Inconsistencies in Her Story**
- Method: Careful questioning about her investigation’s scope or specific details about House Margaster’s activities.
- Clue: Jena might slip up or avoid answering certain questions, contradicting earlier statements, or be vague about her exact sources.
- Example: When pressed about who exactly sent her or the nature of her orders, she might give evasive or conflicting answers.

### **2. Observation of Her Behavior**
- Method: Watch her interactions with others, especially nobles or agents linked to the Lord’s Alliance or House Margaster.
- Clue: She may share private, coded signals or show subtle signs of manipulation (e.g., signaling a contact when the party is distracted).
- Example: A party member with Insight or Perception might notice her exchanging secretive glances or whispered words with suspicious figures.

### **3. Discovering Her Network**
- Method: Investigate her known associates or follow leads she provides to the party.
- Clue: Some of her contacts might be shady characters, mercenaries, or spies rather than honest investigators.
- Example: Tracking one of her “informants” leads to a known smuggler or double agent.

### **4. Cross-Referencing Information**
- Method: Compare her version of events with independent sources (e.g., local nobles, merchants, or informants).
- Clue: Conflicting reports about her presence or activities in certain places, or rumors that she’s stirring trouble rather than solving it.
- Example: A merchant warns the party that Jena’s “investigation” seems designed to discredit rivals or justify aggressive moves by the Lord’s Alliance.

### **5. Finding Evidence of Sabotage or Manipulation**
- Method: Discover forged documents, planted evidence, or intercepted communications linking her to the campaign to destabilize the Order.
- Clue: A letter or coded message revealing her role in spreading rumors or orchestrating “accidental” leaks.
- Example: The party intercepts a message coordinating the anonymous letters or finds a ledger showing funds funneled through her agents.

### **6. Social Challenges and Interrogation**
- Method: Confront her directly with evidence or suspicions during a tense negotiation or interrogation scene.
- Clue: Her reaction—defensiveness, slipping into veiled threats, or revealing partial truths—can confirm her true agenda.
- Example: A successful Insight or Intimidation check might cause her to admit she’s playing a long game to weaken the Order’s influence.

### **7. Magical Means**
- Method: Use spells like *Detect Thoughts*, *Zone of Truth*, or *Clairvoyance* during interactions.
- Clue: Uncover hidden thoughts, lies, or secret meetings that reveal her duplicity.
- Example: During a social encounter, *Detect Thoughts* picks up her calculating plans or concealed doubts about the party’s loyalty.
