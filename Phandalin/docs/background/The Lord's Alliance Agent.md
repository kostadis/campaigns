### Overview
Jena Roscoe is a clandestine agent of the Lord's Alliance, operating under the guise of a talent scout and patron of the arts from Waterdeep. She is the embodiment of the Alliance's political will: pragmatic, sophisticated, and utterly ruthless when the stability of the North is threatened. Her mission is to assess the bard Vukradin, whose populist philosophy on monster treasure could undermine the very system the Alliance uses to keep its territories safe. She is empowered to use whatever means necessary to resolve the situation, whether that be co-opting him with promises of fame or silencing him permanently.
### Appearance
Jena is a woman in her late thirties with an air of refined, unassuming elegance. Her auburn hair is typically tied back in a neat, intricate knot, and her sharp, intelligent green eyes seem to assess everything with a merchant's calculating gaze. She is neither overtly beautiful nor plain; her features are pleasant and easily forgettable, a trait she cultivates.
She dresses in the high-quality, practical attire of a traveling guild representative or minor noble. Her doublet of dark green wool is well-tailored, her leather boots are sturdy but stylish, and the simple silver rings on her fingers are her only adornment. She carries a fine leather satchel for her papers and correspondence. To a casual observer, she carries no obvious weapons, but a discerning eye might notice the reinforced spine on her satchel (concealing a blade) or the slightly-too-heavy pommel on her walking stick.
### Demeanor and Philosophy
Jena is a chameleon. In her role as a talent scout, she is charming, witty, and effusive in her praise of art and music. She can discuss poetry, composition, and performance with genuine knowledge, making her patronage feel authentic. However, beneath this polished veneer is a core of pure pragmatism. Jena serves the Lord's Alliance not out of faith or a sense of abstract justice, but because she believes it is the only bulwark against the chaos that constantly threatens to consume civilization.
She views people as assets or threats. Vukradin is currently an emerging threat, but he has the potential to become a valuable asset. Her morality is fluid, dictated entirely by the needs of her mission and the goals of the Alliance. She believes that for the many to prosper, the few who disrupt the peace must sometimes be managed or removed. It is a regrettable but necessary cost of maintaining order.
### Approach to the Mission
Jena's strategy is two-fold, a classic carrot-and-stick approach.
- **The Carrot (Recruitment):** She will approach Vukradin as a potential patron, captivated by his "passion and unique perspective." She will offer him what he craves most: fame. She'll speak of salon performances in Waterdeep, introductions to powerful nobles who appreciate "raw, untamed artistry," and the funding to make him a household name. She will not ask him to abandon his philosophy, but to *refine* it. "Of course the treasure is stolen," she'll agree, "Stolen from the people! Let the adventurers be the fist that reclaims it, and the Lord's Alliance will be the hand that distributes it to rebuild and fortify our communities." She will offer to make him the celebrated voice of this "noble endeavor."
- **The Stick (Elimination):** While she dangles this golden opportunity, Jena will be meticulously studying him. She will identify his routines, his friends, his fears, and his vices. If he proves to be an incorruptible zealot who rejects her offer and continues to sow dissent, she will not hesitate. Her report to her superiors will be concise, and her methods will be quiet and clean. A fall from a cliff during a "private recital," a tainted bottle of wine left as a gift, or a "tragic bandit attack" on the road. His death will be a footnote, a sad story about another artist who met a grim fate in the Savage North, and no one will ever connect it to the charming patron from Waterdeep.
