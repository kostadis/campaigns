Brewbarry - Kind but bumbling drunkard.

[https://www.dndbeyond.com/characters/145998835](https://www.dndbeyond.com/characters/145998835) 
Brewbarry wasn't always the kind but bumbling drunkard the village knew. Once, he was just a lost boy, barely old enough to tie his own shoes, when he wandered deep into the unforgiving northern wilds. Cold, hungry, and utterly alone, he stumbled upon the encampment of an Uthgard tribe, fierce barbarians known for their imposing stature and unwavering loyalty. Surprisingly, they didn't turn him away. Instead, they offered him shelter, a warm furskin, and a place by their crackling fire, embracing him as one of their own.
Life among the Uthgard was harsh but fulfilling. Brewbarry grew strong, learning the ways of the wilderness and the ancient chants of his adopted people. He developed a deep, abiding love for music, though his tastes were particular. The rhythmic pounding of drums and the clatter of percussion always grated on his ears, reminding him too much of battle. He preferred the soaring melodies of a wind instrument, the soulful strumming of a lute, or the ethereal beauty of a chanted saga. 
As a young man, Brewbarry officially became an Uthgard Barbarian, a proud warrior entrusted with defending his tribe. He took his duties seriously, or at least he tried to. The cold nights on watch could be long, and the mead, unfortunately, was often too plentiful. One fateful evening, after a particularly exuberant celebration, Brewbarry found himself succumbing to its warm embrace. He had intended to just rest his eyes for a moment, but the mead, coupled with the exhausting day, dragged him down into a deep, oblivious slumber.
When he awoke with a splitting headache and the bitter taste of regret, the sun was high, and the tribe was in an uproar. Their sacred site, a hallowed grove where ancestral spirits were said to commune with the living, had been desecrated. The ancient totems were toppled, the sacrificial altar defaced, and the ground churned by unfamiliar boots. An invading group of what the Uthgard scornfully called “half-pints”, small, nimble raiders, possibly goblins or gnomes, had slipped past his watch under the cover of darkness.
The shame was immediate, burning hotter than any hangover. Brewbarry had been derelict in his duties, a grave offense in the Uthgard way. The tribal elders, their faces grim and unforgiving, convened quickly. Despite his years of service and his genuine remorse, the law was clear. For his unforgivable lapse, for allowing the desecration of their most sacred ground, Brewbarry was kicked out of the tribe.
He left with nothing but the clothes on his back, his heart heavy with regret and the sting of banishment. The wild that had once embraced him now felt vast and indifferent. He wandered for years, ultimately finding his way to Neverwinter, where as he stumbled into the pub, he discovered Vukradin and the haunting, otherworldly compositions that Brewbarry considered a perfect 10/10. He’d spend hours listening, lost in the intricate tapestry of sound.
The memory of his tribe is a bittersweet echo in his mind, and the taste of strong drink a persistent companion, dulling the sharp edges of his sorrow and transforming him into the bumbling but undeniably kind figure he is today.
