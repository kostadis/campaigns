# WORLD STATE — CAMPAIGN CANON REFERENCE

*Living document. Reflects the current state of the world following the events at Icespire Hold, Falcon's Lodge, and the Woodland Manse (chs. 42–45). Where source facts were uncertain, items are flagged as open threads rather than asserted as settled.*

## PARTY ROSTER

The party travels together as an unlikely band of moral idealists, pragmatic survivors, and converted zealots. Their shared (and bickered-over) goal: fund Vukradin's music studio with "conflict-free" coin. Reconciled current membership: **Vukradin, Soma, Valphine, Brewbarry**, plus traveling companions **Prutha**, **Boney** (skeletal mount), and at times the orc warchief **Vorga**'s contingent. *(Note: "Narrator," "Speaker," and "I" in source dossiers are first-person narrative voices of party members — primarily Soma and Vukradin — not separate NPCs. Their facts have been folded into the relevant profiles.)*

### Vukradin — Bard (party leader / moral compass)
**Status:** Alive. En route to / near Phandalin.
**Background:** Former musician of Heapside's First Flophouse in Neverwinter; quit performing rather than launder adventuring loot. Associate (now estranged) member of the Order of the Gauntlet; believes his membership pin is lost.
**Drive:** To build a music studio and spread his music across Faerûn, funded only by honestly earned coin. Deepest need is *validation* — to be loved for himself and his art, not used as a tool. Haunted by the pool-whisper "The numbers. Always the numbers." Disillusioned that the gold mine's "honest" profits feel hollow after the Commission's cut.
**Abilities:** Master of Bardic Inspiration; spells include *Sleep, Silence, Hold Person, Phantasmal Force, Cloud of Daggers, Silvery Barbs, Zone of Truth, Universal Speech, Starry Wisp*. Can speak with animals. Signature "Vukradian Scales" compositional style.
**Notable possessions:** Clarinet & instruments; Boots of Elvenkind; the obsidian "Bluetooth sword" (a mental amplifier he refuses to keep but holds for an unknown owner); a necklace of fireballs (held for its rightful owner); sealed envelope for Adabra with 6 gp; sending stones. **An unidentified vial sits hidden in his pack — origin unknown to him (open thread).** Carries (unknowingly) a potion of superior healing Valphine planted to keep him alive.
**Relationships:** Deep unspoken bond with Soma; trusts Brewbarry (his only 10/10 reviewer); collaborates musically with Valphine; reconciled with Falcon after a clean apology but remains wary; distrusts Ser Kaelen (believes Kaelen mirrors his language to manipulate him); cool toward Adabra; views Jenna Roscoe as a "no-good treasure hunter."

### Soma — Tortle Druid (guide / voice of consequence)
**Status:** Alive. Recently killed by Gorthok's tusks (reverted to tortle form as she fell) and revived; her shell endures. Last with the party in/around the Woodland Manse and Falcon's Lodge.
**Background:** Old by tortle standards (40+). Lived a solitary coastal life; hopes her hatched eggs survive somewhere. Trained for one year, late in life, by the elven druid **Meril**, who "picked her" and left her his staff. Formerly tied to the Emerald Enclave; now an estranged naturalist who has become a reluctant interventionist.
**Drive:** Keep the world safe and ensure all creatures have ground to stand on. Wants no legacy — only not to be forgotten for the quiet things she protected. Grieves Corbin; seeks to honor (not avenge in rage) his death as sacred duty. Severed ties with Adabra after discovering her manipulation.
**Abilities:** Wild Shape (brown bear, giant wolf spider, giant toad, elk; covets owlbear); *Moonbeam* (signature), *Speak with Animals, Find Familiar* (rat), *Flaming Sphere, Spirit Guardians, Protection from Energy, Pillar of Light, Sending*.
**Notable possessions:** **Meril's Staff** (see Objects — central to her arc); +1 Halberd from a shipwreck (*note: ownership of "the halberd" is contested in narration — see Objects*); Staff of Bird Calls (taken silently, knowing Vukradin would disapprove); in-progress shark-tooth necklace; druidic scroll; three still-unopened locked chests from shipwrecks.
**Relationships:** Quiet bond with the centaur Xanthopoulos; trusts Boney implicitly; increasingly frustrated by but still believes in Vukradin.

### Valphine Sotorra — Drow Cleric of Lathander (spiritual anchor / enforcer)
**Status:** Alive. At the Woodland Manse.
**Background:** Fifth daughter of Matron Sotorra of Menzoberranzan; raised amid drow intrigue but inwardly a connoisseur of *controlled* pain rather than cruelty. Was marked for sacrifice by her mother before a demon rampage scattered House Sotorra. Reached the surface, experienced sunlight as searing revelation, and was blessed a priestess of Lathander by Father Jerome of Helm's Hold. Now founder and high priestess of her own sect, the **Church of the Searing Light**.
**Theology / Drive:** Lathander's light is power made radiant — pain made holy, correction without apology. Has built a "theology of failure" (missed divine strikes are sacred offerings). Considers Lolth's and Talos's doctrines mechanically identical to each other — empowering destruction under different names. Morality defined by utility and conviction: *"Monsters are what we call things we are allowed to kill."* Considers herself "good"; everything else — deception, torture, manipulation — is "her own department."
**Abilities:** *Sacred Flame, Fairy Fire, Daylight* (via holy symbol), *Spirit Guardians* (toothed radiant form), *Protection from Energy*, bracing spells, divine healing, radiant body-bursts, blinding curses, conjured psychic darkness ("Doom Sphere"). Possesses **golden eyes** (granted after a Zone of Truth event) that let her detect lies. Best liar and best truth-detector in the party.
**Notable possessions:** Dragon Slayer Sword *(disputed — see Objects)*; a shield she insists isn't hers (held for its owner); Dust of Disappearance; holy symbol; sending stones.
**Relationships:** Mentors zealot recruit Prutha; tolerates Vukradin musically (created a hybrid Lathanderian/Lolthian register sung with him); respects Brewbarry's brutal honesty; interrogated and made a quiet arrangement with Sister Kaella; recruited Falcon as a "correctable" resource; bonded with Boney. **Aletra is her estranged sister** — a hostile poison-smuggler she struck with a blinding curse.

### Brewbarry — Barbarian (physical enforcer / Vukradin's protector)
**Status:** Alive. Last in the Phandalin/lodge bathhouse.
**Background:** A lost boy taken in and raised by an Uthgardt tribe; became a warrior. Exiled not for drunkenness but, in his telling, for *sobriety/negligence* — blamed for a sacred grove's desecration while he slept on watch. Wandered to Neverwinter, where he found Vukradin's music (a perfect "10/10"). Insists he is a **nomad, not a barbarian**. Trained by his tribe's wise women to never touch cursed artifacts.
**Drive:** Secure a steady, legal revenue stream (mine shares) to help fund the music studio. Loves soft things and good drink; grief over lost brew is visceral.
**Abilities:** Rage; can grow to **giant size (15–30 ft)**, doubling reach and strength (exact mechanism undefined). "The rage is math" — clear, not mindless. Identifies and names magic items precisely.
**Notable possessions:** Dragon Slayer Sword *(disputed — see Objects)*; +1 Halberd *(disputed — see Objects)*; gleaming handaxes (one named "Elemental Cleaver"); Dread Helm (eyes glow red); Dust of Disappearance; **Mine Shares** (primary financial stake); a "breath pouch" and scales from the dragon Cryovain.
**Relationships:** Deeply loyal to Vukradin; respected by the party; tense rapport with the wererats and Zeleen; agreed (after a failed Wisdom save) to help rebuild the lodge/brewery for the promise of beer.

## KEY ALLIES & COMPANIONS

### Prutha — Orc Zealot of Lathander
Alive, at the Woodland Manse. Convert from Talos worship to Lathanderite zealotry; Valphine's devoted bodyguard ("I make things safe for Cleric of Lathander"). Leads a small band of converted orcs (five joined him after a vision, ch45). Driven by vengeance/redemption for his great-uncle (a clan shaman murdered by Talosians), whose saying guides him: *"Dawn comes for everyone, even those born in shadow."* Retains emotional ties to his orcish heritage (recognized the Eastern Heart Clan's banner). Carries a great axe, javelin, bone whistle, and looted candelabra/bathrobe.

### Boney — Sentient Skeletal Horse
Alive. Loyal mount believing himself revived to serve **Soma as Lady Alagondar reincarnated**. Wields a two-handed longsword; wears a rotting saddle. No memory of his prior life; was killed by will-o'-wisps in Dragonbarrow's tomb. Bonded to Valphine; ridden by Vukradin during evacuations; named an honorary orc (ch44). Passively accepts enchantment ("simply goes where directed"). Valphine plans to enchant his saddle with *Silence*.

### Vorga — Orc Warchief
Alive, at the lodge, preparing to lead her allied orc contingent home (their former quarters, freed now that the dragon is dead). Addressed as "Long-Memory." Warm rapport with the party; long-suffering toward Falcon. Deeply protective of her people's elderly and infirm. Her people's word "Dawn" carries spiritual weight.

### Xanthopoulos ("Xanth") — Centaur
Alive, with or near the party in the high valleys. Ancient, patient observer who reveres the late **Meryl/Meril** and recognized her staff and her deliberate choice of Soma. Praised Soma as "superb." Hates Talos as a "lunatic god" and sees the Talosian orcs as a clear corruption. Believes the forest is sentient and still hurting from the white dragon's incursion. *(Source split this entity into two near-identical dossiers — "Xanth" and "Xanthopoulos." Treat as one character.)*

### Falcon ("The Falcon," "Falcon the Hunter") — Lodge Owner / "Dragon Slayer"
Alive, hosting at his reclaimed lodge. **His "Dragon Slayer" reputation is a fraud** — he arrived after Iymrith's hoard was already plundered and built his hospitality empire on the carcass, using the legend as a business tool. Once Adabra's disciple who believed intervention only brings ruin; after ch43 he has joyfully re-embraced action, defeating Anchorites and mentoring three orcs as lodge staff ("Employees of the Year"). Reconciled unconditionally with Vukradin (ch42). Still carries quiet past trauma (touches brass rings, mutters to absent figures). **He never owned the Woodland Manse** — his entire manse narrative was a ruse to get the party to disrupt the orc assault. Wishes to hire the performers Rimardo and Corrin.
*(Source contained three Falcon dossiers — "Falcon," "The Falcon," "Falcon the Hunter" — reconciled here as one evolving character.)*

## ANTAGONISTS & FACTIONS

### The Carver / The Obsidian Sword — **DEFEATED**
The Carver's true form was a sentient, level-draining black obsidian sword (the "Bluetooth sword"/mental amplifier) that hollowed out and animated a human vessel — "emptied of ordinary selfhood," its passage warping reality, leaving violet-glowing footprints through stone and ice. It traveled the Glacier of Whispers to Icespire Hold to bind the white dragon Cryovain and "wear it like armor" to burn Phandalin. The party defeated the Carver and dismantled its influence. **The sword itself is now in Vukradin's keeping (held for an unknown owner) — its sentience presumed broken, but unconfirmed.**

### Cryovain (the White Dragon) — **DEAD**
Once a sentient white dragon and apex predator of the Icespire Mountains (he displaced the orcs from their home, driving them into human lands). Captured and bound in a summoning circle atop Icespire Hold, mind erased by the obsidian sword. Slain by the party (credited to Vukradin and Valphine). Body left warm despite his frost nature; nose and eyes mutilated by the binding process. Scales and a claw were carried to Phandalin as proof. His ~3,000 gold hoard remains unrecovered at Icespire Hold — **Vukradin refused to take it as stolen property.**

### Gorthok — Active Threat
A massive, autonomous boar-storm entity summoned prematurely from the Woodland Manse ritual. Immune to silence, hypnosis, and Moonbeam radiance; generates reflexive lightning; smells of ozone and "something older." Killed Soma (twin tusks) before she was revived. Has **no interest in submission or alliance** and serves no one. Last seen advancing at the lodge courtyard. *(Whereabouts after ch43 and the fate of the six uncharmed orcs with it are open.)*

### Cult of Talos / Anchorites — Active but Unstable
A decentralized extremist cult with no fixed hierarchy; manifests wherever individuals embrace destruction and "hear Talos within." Self-annihilating by nature. Recently swelled with orcs whose shaman was killed. **Anchorites** (clawed, consecrated spellcasters who summon blights, shapeshift into boars, and cast lightning) served as frontline casters. Most Anchorites encountered are now dead; the cult's remnants and corrupted sites (Woodland Manse, Circle of Thunder, Falcon's Hunting Lodge) still "fester."

### Aletra Sotorra — At Large
**Valphine's sister.** Drow agent of House Sartorra/Sotorra's interplanar poison cartel ("Midnight Tears"). Escaped through a portal after extracting her cargo when the planar machine collapsed, leaving Brewbarry under a red-glow tracking curse. Motivated by profit; condescending toward Valphine. **Reports to an unnamed "boss" (open thread).** A "KP" referenced in her notes is unidentified.

### Jax — Defeated/Incapacitated
The Carver's last field operative. Ambushed the party with thugs and a Rot-Tusk Ogre; paralyzed by Vukradin's *Hold Person* and defeated by Soma, Valphine, and Brewbarry. Threatened Kaella with torturous death. **His final fate (dead, dying, or held) is unconfirmed.** Kaella had emotionally pre-grieved a world where Jax wins.

### Borg the Hammer — At Large
Leader of the Rot-Tusk Ogres, former Carver enforcer. Led the burning of Butterskull Ranch and executed a fleeing bandit. Associated with a "disastrous incident" that weakened the Carvers' power (nature unspecified). Current location and allegiance unknown.

### Sister Kaella / Sister Kayla — Defected, At Large
**Likely the same individual across dossiers** (the names and arc converge): a former lieutenant/spymaster of the Carver who turned against him, providing the party critical intelligence on Icespire Hold and killing fellow lieutenants (e.g., Locutus). Grateful to Valphine ("the only one who showed up") and bound to her by an unspoken arrangement. Her survival was tied to the Carver's death. After Corbin's death she "went to the wind." Vukradin treats her as a living failsafe — *"as long as Sister Kaella's still breathing, you're good."* **Current location and the exact nature of her failsafe role are open threads.**

### Adabra Gwynn — Hostile Rival
Naturalist firebrand of the Emerald Enclave, former mentor to Vukradin. Confirmed the sacred grove's treant truly dead and used it to mount a council campaign to ostracize the "interventionists" Soma and Lyra. **Outvoted at the Enclave council but undeterred — "choosing a different battlefield."** Believes the multiverse is one unified thing with no good or evil, only natural process; sees all intervention as cosmic violation. Likely still at the windmill on Umbrage Hill. Her future moves are an open thread.

### Ser Kaelen Thorn(e) — Order of the Gauntlet Liaison
Official face of the Order in the region; manipulative, prioritizing institutional image. Recruited Vukradin as an associate member ("brother"), now coaches and mirrors his language to control perception. Distrusts Soma's staff; considers Adabra "mad." Orchestrated Vukradin's public celebration in Phandalin. **Whether he genuinely cares for Vukradin or merely manages him is a recurring, unresolved tension.** *(Source split him across "Ser Kaelen," "Ser Kaelen Thorne," "Ser Kaelen Thorn" — one character.)*

### The Emerald Enclave — Active but Fractured
Druidic order split between **naturalists** (non-interference, even "let the world burn") and de-facto **interventionists** (action to prevent greater harm). All members reject the "interventionist" label as the gravest insult. Phandalin chapter nominally led by Reidoth. Internally paralyzed over whether orc displacement is natural or aberrant. Members include Lyra, Thomas, and historically Soma/Meril.
- **Lyra** — low-ranking member (earlier called "leader," now superseded); accepts the failed Whispering Grove ritual with quiet realism; credits Vukradin for defusing Adabra; offered to connect the party with Xanth.
- **Thomas** — Lyra's agent; warned Vukradin & Soma that Adabra was moving against Lyra.

### The Order of the Gauntlet — Active but Depleted
Holy order of principled action, funded by **House Margaster**, tied to Candlekeep. Believes art (Vukradin's music) embodies their mission to "make the north better" without plundering. Numbers reduced by past wars (including against Brewbarry's and Soma's tribes — a legacy of enmity). Vukradin is nominally a "full-fledged"/associate member, though disengaged.

### The Lord's Alliance — Active
Political/economic faction, rival to the Zhentarim, hostile in Brewbarry's view. Sought to claim the dragon's hoard. **Jenna Roscoe** is its agent (likely the same "woman in green" who sought out Vukradin to become his agent and connect him to elite networks in Waterdeep/Neverwinter and Lord Nevermember; she favors laundering stolen treasure into fair-trade redistribution).

### Zeleen Varnaster — Wererat Leader (uneasy ally)
Leader of the Whiskered Gang, hired by Valphine to protect Mountain's Toe Mine (now their "home"). Manipulative and survival-driven; secretly responsible for Don-Jon Raskin's death; deceives Valphine that the (silent) mine is actively worked.

## LOCATIONS

### Phandalin
Thriving mining settlement in the Sword Mountains, saved from the Carver/dragon threat. Hub for recovery and celebration; planned site of Vukradin's studio. **Harbin Wester** governs as Townmaster / "Lord Mayor" / head of the Privy Council — appointed by and answerable only to Lord Nevermember; obsessed with controlling the narrative of Phandalin's salvation and claiming credit for the dragon's defeat. Other figures: **"Spiderman,"** owner of the Stonehill Tavern (despises the Lord's Alliance); **Linene Graywind**, manager of the Lionshield Coster (seeks planar artifacts for unnamed "bosses"). Icespire Hold is visible from town on clear days.

### Icespire Hold
Mountain fortress of stone and ice, 55 miles from Phandalin — two ancient structures joined by a stone bridge, with a gatehouse. Site of Cryovain's binding and death and the Carver's defeat. The dragon's ~3,000 gp hoard may remain here.

### Falcon's Lodge / Falcon's Hunting Lodge
Falcon's reclaimed hospitality enterprise (formerly overrun by Talosians), an hour from the Woodland Manse. Battle-scarred: missing front door ("open-air concept"), scorched timbers, the **destroyed wine cellar** (a grief Brewbarry deeply mourns). Staffed by three orcs in livery; bathrobe amenity (formerly Brewbarry's). Talosian residue still "festers" here. *(Source's separate "Lodge" / "Hunting Lodge" dossiers reconciled as the same site post-reclamation.)*

### Woodland Manse
Burned, ivy-choked two-story manor an hour from the lodge, atop a stone foundation; courtyard with a vine-erupting well, spiked staircase, collapsed ceiling. Former site of an active Talosian summoning ritual (which loosed Gorthok prematurely) and cursed effigies of the party. **Not Falcon's property** — built by a scholar, despite Falcon's earlier ruse. Where the party currently operates.

### Circle of Thunder
A corrupted ancient druidic henge atop a forested hill, "borrowed" by Talosian ritual. A windless grey-green vortex hovers above; the stones hum at a tooth-aching frequency. Site of a ritual involving a sacrificed deer (heart replaced with black glassy material) and tattooed half-orc anchorites.

### Dragonbarrow
A 30-ft dragon-burial mound and dungeon, haunted at night by will-o'-wisps (now mostly destroyed). Source of the **Dragon Slayer Sword**, which was guarded atop a dragon skull by an Invisible Stalker (destroyed). One secret passage collapsed via a pressure plate.

### Neverwinter
City with multiple party quests: returning a stolen statue (the Order of the Gauntlet to be credited) before House Margaster/Markaster intervenes; locating the rightful owners of frozen dragon treasure (which a Crown functionary claims). A key performance hub. Vukradin's music is controversial here. **The party has blown past a prior deadline to reach it but still intends to go.**

### Other Sites
- **Mountain's Toe Gold Mine** — owned in shares by Brewbarry and Falcon; overseen by foreman **Horia Stonetallow** (shield dwarf of disgraced Gauntlgrym lineage); guarded by Zeleen's wererats; barely profitable (30 gp ledger) pending a new "speaking" gold vein. Bureaucratic schemes (fake Earthstone Gem, "no-skimming" loophole) swirl around its accounts.
- **Butterskull Ranch** — abandoned, burned ruin 5 miles east of Conyberry; destroyed by Borg.
- **Logger's Camp** — deserted staging point on the river's south shore.

## OBJECTS OF NOTE

### Meril's Staff ("the staff")
Central to Soma's arc. An intricate ash staff given to Soma by her mentor **Meril/Meryl** — not from sentiment, but because he "picked her." Warm to the touch, heavier than before, communicates non-verbally ahead of Soma's senses, recognizes unnatural intent, and **grows leaves in response to powerful magic/events** (progressed from three leaves to a fourth bud to four leaves; trembling ceased after the fourth emerged, replaced by a deeper "presence"). Holds "Resonance," used to test truthfulness (including Adabra's and Soma's own). Currently concealed in Soma's fur.
> **Open thread:** In ch45 Soma hides "a staff, plain enough" in her fur — unclear whether this is the same staff now concealed or a plain decoy/replacement. The new leaf's meaning (resurrection? divine sign?) is unexplained. *(Source split this object across three dossiers — "staff," "the staff," "Meril's Staff" — reconciled as one.)*

### The Obsidian "Bluetooth" Sword
The Carver's true form — sentient, level-draining, a mental amplifier that projects music into minds. Now held by Vukradin, who refuses to keep it and seeks its rightful owner. Whether its will is truly broken is unconfirmed.

### Dragon Slayer Sword (+1)
Recovered from atop the dragon skull in Dragonbarrow (guarded by the now-destroyed Invisible Stalker); glows when wielded. **Possession is contested across narration — claimed at various points by Brewbarry, Valphine, and the party's narrating voice.** Most combat-recent facts place it in **Brewbarry's** hands during the assault on Cryovain; treat Brewbarry as current holder unless play establishes otherwise.

### The Halberd (+1, acid-live)
A magic halberd recovered from a shipwreck (the *Star Crossed Lover*), recently sharpened and "still live with acid." **Ownership is disputed in narration** between Brewbarry (frequent wielder) and the party's first-person narrator (who asserts "It's mine"); both Soma and Brewbarry are associated with a +1 halberd in their dossiers. Treat as a single contested weapon; resolve in play.

### Other Items in Play
Necklace of fireballs (Vukradin, held for owner); Boots of Elvenkind (Vukradin/Falcon both claim a pair); Dust of Disappearance (held by Brewbarry and Valphine — possibly two finds); Staff of Bird Calls (Soma); the planar poison "Midnight Tears" (escaped with Aletra); the unidentified vial in Vukradin's pack (origin unknown).

## OPEN THREADS & MYSTERIES

### The Studio (the campaign's emotional engine)
Both Vukradin and Brewbarry treat the music studio as their "north star." It is **not yet built or owned.** Vukradin grows disillusioned that "honest" gold (mine cut, Commission caveats) feels hollow, and fears he'll "be poor forever" rather than compromise.

### The Carver's Aftermath & the Obsidian Sword
Is the sword's sentience truly broken? Who is its rightful owner? Vukradin's vial — who planted it, when, and why?

### Aletra & House Sotorra's Interplanar Network
Aletra escaped with the poison and a tracking curse on Brewbarry. Her unnamed "boss," the identity of "KP," and the full scope of the interplanar drug trade (and a bill from "Interplanar Trade Consultants Incorporated" suggesting a larger operation) remain unknown. Valphine's wider family loose ends loom: Matron Sotorra still plans to sacrifice Valphine; sister Tarranta seeks to lead the house; sister Notir is missing (possibly allied with House Margaster); the freed slave Grundar Quartzvein seeks revenge on the whole house.

### The Emerald Enclave Schism
Adabra, outvoted but "far from done," may return "in greater numbers." Soma asked the council to warn her. The planar rift at the Whispering Grove was **never sealed** — the treant is dead and the corruption spreads. The faction's official stance on intervention is genuinely unresolved.

### Multiple Gods of Fate
The performers (Rimardo & Corrin) raised a theological impossibility: traditionally **Savras** is the sole god of fate, yet multiple "gods of fate" appeared — implying that if more than one controls fate, there is no destiny. This unsettles Vukradin and the party. Rimardo hints **Leilon** holds the context to understand the "performance."

### Rimardo & Corrin — The Performers
Two cryptic, possibly cosmic comedians who reframe events as theater ("contracts never signed but always enforced"). Vanished from the lodge; Falcon wishes to rehire them. They warn of "the storm you've met" as a touring entity and a sealed door beneath the lodge stage ("management insists is structural"). Rimardo promised Vukradin: *"If you stick with it at the very end... we'll perform together."* What lies in Leilon, and what they truly are, is unknown. (Compare: the ghost **Miral** in Leilon warned of "true dwellers" and a "true storm" gathering in the depths beneath the city.)

### Ser Kaelen's True Intentions
Does he love Vukradin or merely manage him? What has he arranged for the party's arrival in Phandalin?

### Sister Kaella the Failsafe
She has "gone to the wind," yet her survival is somehow tied to the party's safety. The mechanism, her location, and her arrangement with Valphine are unresolved.

### Falcon's Apology, Reconciled — but the Talosian Lodge Riddle Remains
Why did the Talosians attack their *own* former lodge? (Valphine's two scenarios remain untested.)

### Loose Personal Threads
- **Soma:** the fate of her hatched children; whether Meril lives and will cross her path again; the meaning of his blessing and his choice of her.
- **Brewbarry:** accidents left in his wake across the North; the missing lead actor of a Triboar theatre troupe (possibly tied to Rimardo/Corrin); his unresolved Uthgardt exile.
- **Vukradin:** Jenna's standing offer to make him famous (unaccepted); his wounded feeling that Falcon never came to his performances.
- **The Shimmering Stag** (warped, teleporting forest guardian) still awaits the "fix" promised by unnamed others — a symptom of the wider planar corruption.

## CANON EVENTS TIMELINE (chronological reference)

1. **(chs 1–3)** Vukradin quits the First Flophouse over loot ethics; the party forms in Neverwinter/Phandalin. Valphine, fled from fallen Menzoberranzan and newly converted to Lathander, attaches herself to Vukradin. First ogre encounters; temple exploration; sending stones acquired.
2. **(chs 3–6)** Dual-monarchy/shapeshifter affair (King Gnercli). Manticore slain atop Umbrage Hill windmill; Adabra and the naturalist potion-maker introduced. Ser Kaelen recruits Vukradin into the Order of the Gauntlet.
3. **(chs 5–9)** Whispering Wood/lighthouse troubles; the ghost Miral in Leilon warns of "true dwellers." Bandit conflicts trace back to **the Carver**.
4. **(chs 11–13)** The corrupted Shimmering Stag; the Whispering Grove ritual **fails** — the treant dies, the planar rift is left unsealed. Lyra's interventionism collapses.
5. **(chs 15–18)** Carver's enforcers active (Borg burns Butterskull Ranch; Jax operates). Falcon confesses his fraudulent "Dragon Slayer" legend. Cult of Talos rises among grieving orcs.
6. **(chs 21–24)** Falcon's Lodge / Woodland Manse intrigue; Teega and the Kraken Society; Neverwinter quests (stolen statue) take shape.
7. **(chs 27–30)** The planar machine / interdimensional poison affair: Valphine confronts her sister **Aletra**, who escapes with "Midnight Tears" and curses Brewbarry; the Chief Accountant subplot; the machine returns to its dimension.
8. **(chs 31–33)** Dragonbarrow: will-o'-wisps, the Invisible Stalker, and recovery of the **Dragon Slayer Sword**. Boney revived. Jax's ambush defeated; the party learns the Carver heads to Icespire Hold.
9. **(ch 34)** The Carver crosses the Glacier of Whispers, warping reality. Kaella defects and provides intelligence; Corbin is later killed by Sister Kaella/Kayla as she escapes — galvanizing Soma and the narrating party.
10. **(chs 35–36)** Icespire Hold: **Cryovain** found bound and mind-erased; the party defeats the Carver and **slays Cryovain** (Vukradin & Valphine credited). Vukradin **refuses the dragon's hoard.** Phandalin saved.
11. **(chs 36–39)** Return toward Phandalin; Ser Kaelen stages Vukradin's public celebration. The **Emerald Enclave council**: Adabra's bid to ostracize the interventionists is outvoted; she withdraws "to a different battlefield." Soma's staff and Meril's legacy come to the fore via Xanth/Xanthopoulos.
12. **(chs 40–43)** Circle of Thunder and the Talosian campaign; **Falcon's Lodge** retaken from Talosians amid battle with Anchorites and orcs. **Gorthok** loosed prematurely; **Soma killed by Gorthok and revived.** Prutha's mass conversion to Lathander; Vorga's orcs allied. Falcon reconciles with Vukradin and re-embraces action.
13. **(chs 44–45)** The cosmic performers **Rimardo & Corrin** reframe events as theater and raise the "multiple gods of fate" paradox, pointing toward **Leilon**. Stirge battles at the lodge bathhouse. The party operates at the **Woodland Manse**; the staff's fourth leaf and concealment; disillusionment with the gold mine's "honest" profits. Party poised to return to Phandalin / press on toward Neverwinter.
