### Miral

**Current status:** Deceased (ghost)  
**Current location:** Manifesting near the opalescent conch in Leilon (last known manifestation site)  
**Allegiance/faction:** Sea elf spirit, formerly bound to Moesko’s service  
**Current possessions / notable items:** Opalescent conch (reclaimed, now in party’s possession at time of manifestation)  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:** Spirit at rest, no longer serving any mortal; last known relationship was with Moesko, whom she identifies as her undoing  
**Defining recent actions:** Manifested as a ghost after the opalescent conch was presented to her by the party; thanked the party, revealed that Moesko was corrupted by foreign shadows in his wine that twisted his devotion to Talos, warned of “true dwellers” and a “true storm” gathering in the depths below Leilon, then vanished permanently.  
**Revealed motivations or secrets:** Her death was caused by Moesko’s corruption — not by his own will, but by external, shadowy forces manipulating his faith. She seeks no vengeance, only release, and urges the party to beware the deeper threat beneath Leilon.

## Uncertainty
- The exact nature of the “shadows” that corrupted Moesko is unspecified — no further details on origin, identity, or mechanism.  
- Whether Miral’s ghost remains bound to the conch or can manifest elsewhere is unclear; she vanished after speaking, and no subsequent manifestation is recorded.  
- The phrase “Miraal appeared to claim her conch” appears twice — once before her ghostly manifestation and once during it. It is ambiguous whether the first instance refers to a pre-death event (contradicting her ghost status) or is a narrative repetition. Later context confirms the manifestation is posthumous, so the first mention may be a misattribution or poetic phrasing — but the chronology is unclear.  
- It is uncertain whether Miral ever physically held the +1 halberd, or if her claim of it was merely verbal — the halberd is never confirmed as her possession, only as the treasure “he was going to get for us” (note: “he” may be a misattribution or typo; Miral is referred to as “she” elsewhere).
