### Lathander

**Type:** Deity / Faction  
**Current Status:** Active and revered  
**Domain:** Perpetual dawn; renewal, light, and order  
**Allegiance / Following:** Clerics and devotees who uphold structured righteousness, particularly those seeking healing or purpose after trauma  
**Key Tenets:**  
- Condemns murder and violence against sentient beings  
- Permits and sanctifies violence directed at monsters  
- Embraces and rehabilitates damaged or broken individuals through hierarchical spiritual structure  

**Current Influence / Recognition:**  
- Revered as an all-powerful deity by followers (e.g., Prutha)  
- Power is explicitly recognized as absolute by at least one worshipper  
- Associated with the Sacred Flame, channeled by clerics such as Valphine  
- Symbolized as “perpetual dawn” by the party and worshippers  

**Notable Practitioners:**  
- Valphine (channels Sacred Flame, invokes Lathander for divine power)  

**Recent Actions / Manifestations:**  
- Continues to empower clerics with divine light (Sacred Flame)  
- Maintains institutional presence among clergy who serve as healers and moral guides  
- Actively draws in and transforms individuals marked by trauma or disarray  

## Uncertainty
- The organizational structure of the Lathander faction (hierarchy, temples, leadership) is not described beyond clerical practice.  
- Whether Lathander’s doctrine extends beyond monster-killing exceptions to other forms of justified violence (e.g., self-defense, execution) is unconfirmed.  
- No current headquarters, major temples, or political alliances of the faction are named.  
- It is unclear whether “Lathander, to his credit, is very good with such material” refers to Lathander as a deity or a person — but given context, it is likely a personification of the deity’s influence; still, attribution is ambiguous.
