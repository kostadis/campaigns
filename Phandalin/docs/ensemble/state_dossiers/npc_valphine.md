## Identity
*Also known as: Valphine Sortorra*

### Valphine

**Current Status:** Alive  
**Current Location:** Woodland Manse (following the events of Chapter 45)  
**Allegiance/Faction:** Cleric of Lathander, founder and high priestess of the Church of the Searing Light (her own sect)  
**Current Possessions / Notable Items:**  
- Dragon Slayer Sword (claimed from atop a dragon skull)  
- Shield (tucked under her arm, insists it is not hers; holds it until owner surfaces)  
- Dust of Disappearance (recovered from a hollowed book in the library)  
- Key, whistle, and coins (recovered from the ogre in Chapter 3)  
- Holy symbol of Lathander (used as a vessel for Daylight)  
- New golden eyes (granted after the Zone of Truth event; allows her to detect lies)  
- Hybrid Lathanderian/Lolthian singing register (refined over time)  
- Sending stones (known utility for long-distance musical transmission)  

**Notable Spells/Feats:**  
- Sacred Flame  
- Fairy Fire  
- Daylight (cast via holy symbol)  
- Spirit Guardians (manifests as radiant, toothed spectral protectors)  
- Protection from Energy (in her toolkit)  
- Bracing spell (enhances physical resilience)  
- Divine healing (used to revive companions, break paralysis)  
- Radiant burst (emitted from her body, incinerating stirges)  
- Blinding curse (used against Aletra)  
- Silence enchantment (planned for Boney’s saddle)  
- Conjures inky psychic darkness (used in Chapter 44)  

**Current Assignment / Role:**  
- Leading a mission to spread the Church of the Searing Light across Faerûn, with Prutha as companion  
- Acting as the party’s spiritual anchor, tactical observer, and pragmatic enforcer  
- Serving as the party’s best liar and most reliable truth-detector (via golden eyes)  
- Managing alliances with orcs and dwarves through direct, unflinching negotiation  
- Maintaining arrangements with spymasters and underground networks  

**Key Relationships:**  
- Prutha: Companion and missionary recruit; chosen to accompany her to spread her sect  
- Vukradin: Frequent musical collaborator; tolerated despite ideological friction  
- Brewbarry: Respected for his brutal honesty; she views his blood rejoicing as one of the “more honest prayers” in the party  
- Soma: Respects her patience as a form of power; observes her with clinical detachment  
- Aletra: Former sister; targeted with a blinding curse during their confrontation  
- Falcon: Recruited as a resource; she believes his damage is correctable with pressure  
- Lathander: Worshipped as power incarnate — radiant, painful, indifferent, and holy; she has constructed a theology around missed divine strikes as offerings  

**Defining Recent Actions:**  
- Summoned spirit guardians to annihilate approaching bandits in the fortress corridor  
- Entered the shutdown code to halt a catastrophic machine in Chapter 28  
- Cast a radiant burst that turned a swarm of stirges feeding on her into ash  
- Used golden eyes to confirm truthfulness of a wererat’s claims and to assess the party’s integrity  
- Declared herself the party’s best liar and accepted the role  
- Burned cursed effigies, claiming she was “called by Lathander to do it”  
- Secured the Dragon Slayer Sword from the dragon skull  
- Transformed into a bear to capture an orc scout  
- Successfully negotiated with orcs to stay and work at the hunting lodge, then declared, “Now it’s not my problem”  
- Created a hybrid Lathanderian/Lolthian musical register and sang it with Vukradin  
- Used Underdark interrogation techniques on Sister Kaella — applying pressure points without healing  
- Rejected conventional morality, declaring: “Monsters are what we call things we are allowed to kill.”  

**Revealed Motivations / Secrets:**  
- Believes Lathander’s light is not benevolent, but a searing, indifferent force — pain as divine expression, not punishment  
- Rejects the surface world’s softness; sees ritual and structure as scaffolding for power the uninitiated cannot comprehend  
- Once a priestess of Lolth; now sees Lolth’s theology as “small” and rationed, while Lathander’s is vast and unapologetic  
- Has developed a theology of failure: missed divine strikes are not errors but sacred offerings  
- Believes Talos’s doctrine is identical in mechanics to Lolth’s — both empower destruction, just with different names  
- Has moved beyond the binary of good/evil; her morality is defined by utility, power, and personal conviction  
- Secretly placed a potion of superior healing in Vukradin’s pack to ensure his survival, knowing he would refuse it if asked  
- Feels a “particular sweetness” in being chosen as a vessel — letting creatures believe they’ve won before destroying them  
- Considers herself “good,” but all other actions — deception, torture, manipulation — are her own department

- The identity of the shield’s true owner remains unknown; Valphine insists it is not hers but refuses to let it go.  
- It is unclear whether the “Dust of Disappearance” was acquired before or after the Zone of Truth event — her forgetting of it suggests temporal dislocation, but no cause is given.  
- The source of her new ability to channel radiant bursts from her body (Chapter 45) is unexplained; no prior indication of such power exists.  
- Whether her claim that “Lathander is more powerful” is theological conviction or a pragmatic dismissal of competing deities (e.g., Savras) is ambiguous.  
- The full extent of her “arrangements with spymasters” is unspecified — who they are, where, and how they serve her remains opaque.  
- It is unclear if Prutha’s companionship is voluntary or coerced; Valphine frames it as a mission, but his agency is not confirmed.  
- The “hybrid Lathanderian/Lolthian register” is described as refined — but whether this is musical, liturgical, or metaphysical is not defined.  
- The “Church of the Searing Light” is her sect, but whether it has any formal structure, followers, or temples beyond her immediate circle is unknown.
