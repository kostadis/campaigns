## Identity
*Also known as: Ser Kaelen, Ser Kaelen Thorn*

### Ser Kaelen

**Current status:** Alive  
**Current location:** Phandalin (as of ch36); present at the Stonehill festival (ch37–39)  
**Allegiance/faction:** Order of the Gauntlet — full member and representative; speaks with institutional authority  

**Current possessions / notable items:**  
- Sword (held with symbolic defiance: “no order can take my sword”)  
- Sending stone (used to communicate with Vukradin)  
- Prepared press release (for public events)  

**Spells / feats:** Not specified  

**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Official liaison and public face of the Order of the Gauntlet in the region  
- Tasked with managing public perception, recovering artifacts, and suppressing destabilizing actions (e.g., Adabra’s interference in the Whispering Grove)  
- Reports to and represents the Order’s interests; acts with autonomy but under its doctrine  
- Maintains a close, manipulative relationship with Vukradin: praised, coached, and emotionally manipulated using his vocabulary (“conflict-free triumph”)  
- Urgently communicates with Soma regarding her staff and Adabra’s actions  
- Has a formal, strained alliance with Vukradin (referred to as “brother” in ch06, now ambiguous — “do I know if he loves me or is managing me?”)  
- Views Adabra as mentally unstable (“gone mad”) and Soma as a potential threat due to her staff  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Identified the lighthouse statue as belonging to Elara Seasong Meliame and demanded its return to an art collector (ch24, ch31)  
- Orchestrated Vukradin’s public celebration in Phandalin after his victory, using carefully curated language borrowed from Vukradin’s own speech (ch36)  
- Interrupted a festival to urgently confront Soma about Adabra’s actions in the Whispering Grove (ch37–38)  
- Observed Soma’s staff with intense, calculating scrutiny (ch39)  
- Publicly defended Vukradin’s honor and insisted on a trial to protect the Order’s integrity during Harbin’s accusations (ch16)  
- Advocated for political compromise over revolution, fearing Lord Nevermember’s retaliation (ch16)  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Prioritizes institutional stability and public image over justice or personal loyalty  
- Uses psychological manipulation: absorbs and mirrors others’ language to control perception and create false intimacy  
- Believes heroism is a necessary duty, not defiance — aligns with the Order’s doctrine of “conflict-free” action  
- Secretly monitors and assesses potential threats (Soma’s staff, Adabra’s madness)  
- May be grooming or containing Vukradin as a tool — their bond is emotionally charged but institutionally instrumental
