### Circle of Thunder

- **Type:** Location (ancient henge)  
- **Current State:** Active, corrupted, and energetically charged  
- **Location:** Summit of a hill, within a forested region  
- **Physical Description:** A ring of ancient standing stones, now humming at a frequency that causes physical discomfort (back teeth ache)  
- **Atmospheric Condition:** A unnatural, windless vortex of grey-green clouds moves inward and circles above the henge, held in place by intent — not weather  
- **Occupants:**  
  - Two half-orcs standing among the stones  
  - Twelve twig blights arranged in a second ring around the half-orcs, bark-dry and motionless  
- **Notable Feature:** At the center of the ring lies a deer, split open from throat to belly by a clean, deliberate cut  
- **History/Significance:** Once a druidic site; later corrupted by Talosian ritual — borrowed from anchorites, not built by them  
- **Current Assignment / Role:** Site of an active, ritualistic performance — the two half-orcs are engaged in a non-celebratory, negotiation-like dance with an unseen force, surrounded by the twig blights as sentinels or components of the ritual  

## Uncertainty
- The exact identity or purpose of the two half-orcs is not stated — only their presence and action are confirmed.  
- The nature of the "something that does not speak" being negotiated with is unnamed and unexplained.  
- Whether the deer was killed as part of the ritual or was already dead prior is ambiguous.  
- The source of the humming frequency is not attributed — whether it’s the stones themselves, the ritual, or an external entity.  
- The current allegiance or affiliation of the half-orcs (e.g., Talosian cultists, rogue druids, etc.) is inferred but not explicitly confirmed beyond the site’s corruption.
