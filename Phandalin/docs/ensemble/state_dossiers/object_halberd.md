### Halberd

- **Current holder/location:** Owned and wielded by the narrator (not Brewbarry).  
- **Current condition:** Appears more lethal after being sharpened; looks waterlogged; still live with acid.  
- **Notable items/spells/feats:** Enhanced lethality due to recent sharpening; magically active with acid (still live with acid).  
- **Origin:** Obtained from a shipwreck.  
- **Recent actions:** Used in the final strike against the Anchorite while still live with acid; previously used to kill Orc 7 and the anchorite; used to open a sarcophagus from 10 feet away (earlier use); wielded in multiple skirmishes including the fortress battle.  
- **Key relationships:** Formerly associated with Brewbarry, but now confirmed owned by the narrator: “I had the halberd from the shipwreck. It's mine.” (ch42); Brewbarry no longer possesses it, as confirmed by Vukradin’s claim (“You have my sword, and my halberd.” ch34) and the narrator’s ownership assertion (ch42–43).  

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction between Brewbarry’s repeated use of the halberd (chs 3, 4, 5, 19, 25, 31, 36, 37, 40) and the narrator’s claim of ownership (chs 16, 42, 43). The later facts (ch34, ch42, ch43) establish the narrator as current owner, implying Brewbarry’s earlier use was either temporary, mistaken, or occurred before transfer — but the exact moment of transfer is unrecorded.  
- Unclear if “Brewbarry” and “the narrator” are the same person — the text uses “I” for the narrator and “Brewbarry” in third person, suggesting separate entities, yet Brewbarry is never described as anyone else.  
- “Still live with acid” (ch43) is the latest state, but the origin of the acid enchantment is unexplained.  
- “Waterlogged” (ch42) and “live with acid” (ch43) may conflict physically — no explanation given for how a waterlogged weapon retains or channels acid magic.
