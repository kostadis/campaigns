### Emerald Enclave

**Current status:** Active but deeply divided.  
**Current location:** Presence noted in Phandalin (with local chapter); broader network extends to Waterdeep and regions affected by planar corruption and orc displacement.  
**Allegiance/faction:** Internally fractured between naturalist and interventionist wings, though all members formally reject the label “interventionist” and claim to preserve nature.  

**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:** Not specified.  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Local chapter in Phandalin is nominally led by Reidoth.  
- Members swear their lives and honor to the organization.  
- Historically allied with Ser Kaelen Thorne during the War of the Dragons; attended the Waterdeep council.  
- Does not hire agents; recruits only true believers.  
- Recent internal conflict centers on whether orc displacement is a natural phenomenon or an aberration requiring action.  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Debated internally whether to act against planar corruption, with no consensus reached.  
- Split over the status of demons following the intervention against Tiamat — some now regard them as part of the natural order.  
- Engaged in escalating accusations within the faction over whether orc movements constitute “intervention,” leading to infighting and paralysis.  
- Still referenced as the source of unresolved issues requiring attention in Phandalin.  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Core belief: “All things are beautiful”; heroism is viewed as an act of defiance against the natural order — yet also deemed necessary to prevent evil from crushing beauty.  
- Naturalist wing holds that non-interference is paramount, even if it means “letting the world burn.”  
- All members believe they are preserving nature; labeling another a “interventionist” is the gravest insult within the faction.  
- Despite ideological rifts, the faction maintains a loose alliance with members who hold opposing views, acknowledging shared foundational values.  

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction between [ch17] (naturalists believe in non-interference even if the world burns) and [ch10] (the Enclave believes heroism is necessary to save the world). The latter may reflect the interventionist wing’s view, but the faction’s official stance is unclear.  
- Unclear whether Reidoth leads the entire faction or only the Phandalin chapter — no evidence of central leadership.  
- The term “interventionist faction” is used in early chapters ([ch05]) as if distinct, but [ch13] claims no member accepts that label — suggesting the term is pejorative, not organizational.  
- Unclear if the “some members” who declared demons part of nature ([ch10]) represent a majority, minority, or schismatic group.  
- No current information on whether the faction still maintains ties with Ser Kaelen Thorne or other external allies.  
- The nature of “issues with the Emerald Enclave” in [ch36] is unspecified — political, operational, or existential?
