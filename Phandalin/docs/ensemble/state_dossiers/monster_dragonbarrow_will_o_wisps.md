## Identity
*Also known as: Will-o'-wisp, will-o'-wisps*

### Will-o'-wisp

**Current status:** Destroyed (two instances confirmed)  
**Current location:** None (both instances destroyed in Dragonbarrow tomb, Chapter 32)  
**Allegiance/faction:** None (independent predatory entity)  
**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:** None (no possessions; attacks with innate lightning/shock damage)  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:** None (no assignment; previously associated with Dragonbarrow tomb and sarcophagus; killed Boney in his past life)  
**Defining recent actions:**  
- Emerged from a sarcophagus after Vukradin opened it.  
- Attacked Vukradin, dealing 9 points of lightning damage.  
- Was caught in Vukradin’s Cloud of Daggers and subsequently destroyed by Soma’s Moonbeam (12 damage).  
- A separate will-o’-wisp attacked Brewbarry, dealt 9 damage, then fled through rock — later destroyed by Brewbarry’s halberd during rage.  
- Another was turned by Valphine and fled in terror.  
- Previously triggered by movement (e.g., rat passing), turning on/off as a predatory lure.  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Exhibits predatory, ambush-based behavior: remains invisible, activates upon proximity or disturbance, and phases through walls.  
- Associated with necrotic or cursed locations (sarcophagus, tomb).  
- Boney’s death confirms they are lethal to living creatures and possibly bound to the tomb’s curse.  

## Uncertainty
- Two will-o’-wisps were destroyed in Chapter 32: one by Soma’s Moonbeam (after Cloud of Daggers), another by Brewbarry’s halberd. It is unclear if these were two distinct entities or the same one misreported.  
- Valphine turned a will-o’-wisp in the room with Boney — it fled, but its current status (alive or later destroyed) is unconfirmed.  
- The will-o’-wisp that attacked Brewbarry and fled through rock may be the same one later destroyed by Brewbarry — but the text does not confirm if it returned or if this was a second instance.  
- It is ambiguous whether the will-o’-wisp that emerged from the sarcophagus and was targeted by Cloud of Daggers is the same one later destroyed by Moonbeam or a separate entity.  
- The will-o’-wisp that killed the rat in Chapter 31 is not referenced again — its current status is unknown.
