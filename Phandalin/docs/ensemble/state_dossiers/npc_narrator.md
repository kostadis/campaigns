### Narrator

**Current status:** Alive  
**Current location:** Unspecified, but actively engaged in a mission tied to Lathander’s divine vision and the aftermath of the Anchorite conflict.  
**Allegiance/faction:** Devoted to Lathander as a divine instrument; formerly associated with the Enclave (as a druid), now operating as a self-styled agent of Lathander’s will.  
**Current possessions / notable items:**  
- Meril’s staff (held, not owned; feels borrowed, offered, or expected; has recently activated new potential)  
- Dragon Slayer Sword  
- Halberd from the shipwreck (considered his, no dispute)  
- Obsidian sword (recognized as level-draining; actively avoided; last seen in possession of others, not carried by narrator)  
- Boney (mount/ally; planned to be enchanted with silence)  

**Notable spells/feats:**  
- Can cast *silence* (planned for Boney or his saddle)  
- Can cast *moonbeam* (understood its limitations; confirmed it “goes away”)  
- Has been taught sacraments (likely druidic or ancestral) and teaches them to Corbin  
- Recently gained two new divine gifts (warm, heavy, like a key in a lock)  
- Grew to twice his size during battle (likely via divine or magical transformation)  
- Can speak Orcish  
- Can sense possession and its distinctions from deeper corruption  

**Current assignment / role:**  
- To create “Lathander’s divine arm”: a body of true believers to enforce divine will against aberration and evil.  
- To act as a conduit for Lathander’s vision — not merely a priest, but a weaponized agent of unyielding dawn.  
- To guide, observe, and manipulate outcomes: teaching sacraments, interpreting staff signs, assessing loyalty, and dismantling violent hierarchies.  
- Key relationships:  
  - Reports to no one but Lathander (per vision)  
  - Travels with Vukradin, Brewbarry, Corbin, Prutha, and Boney (last seen together)  
  - Respects and is subtly influenced by Meril’s legacy  
  - Feels a complex, unspoken bond with the mother drake and the orcs  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Accepted a divine vision from Lathander to build a “body of true believers,” rejecting passive prayer for active enforcement.  
- Felt two new divine gifts settle into him — permanent, resonant, and binding.  
- Successfully used Boney to plan a silent flank against Anchorites.  
- Finished the swing on a dead Anchorite out of principle.  
- Interpreted the staff’s quiet growth as divine validation of actions taken.  
- Spoke to orcs in their tongue, commanding them to kneel before Lathander.  
- Recognized the loggers’ hardship as mirror to his own people’s past suffering.  
- Stopped confusing converts with grief.  
- Refused to argue about the halberd’s ownership, asserting possession with quiet certainty.  
- Acknowledged his own transformation: “still here,” but a hollow shell of his former self.  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Desperately wanted a music studio but has made no progress — this desire persists as a quiet, unfulfilled anchor.  
- Believes true believers are not subtle, cautious, or negotiable — and now embodies that ideal.  
- Feels Lathander’s power as non-negotiable: “brightness need not be gentle, power must be continuous, the dawn does not ask permission.”  
- Knows he is being used — and perhaps has chosen to be used.  
- Understands lies now feel like skin caught in the open — a divine sensitivity to deceit.  
- Believes Meril sent him on this path because he saw what others had become too numb to see.  
- The staff answers him — a truth he will not say aloud.
