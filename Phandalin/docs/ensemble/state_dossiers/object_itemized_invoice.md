### Itemized Invoice

- **Current location:** Pinned to a table by a small knife left behind when the performers vanished.  
- **Current condition:** Physically intact, with the words “balance outstanding” written in a hand that is not Corrin’s.  
- **Current holder:** Unclaimed, left at the scene; no current possessor named.  
- **Notable content:** Lists a charge for “emotional damages, Brewbarry’s wine, one cask, sentimental value, assessed at zero.”  
- **Significance:** Appears to be a surreal or satirical bill presented by Rimardo (or attributed to Corrin), with an unpaid balance marked in an unknown hand.

## Uncertainty
- Contradictory attribution: The invoice is described as presented by Corrin in one quote and as presented by Rimardo in another — it is unclear who authored or delivered it.  
- Unclear origin of “balance outstanding” handwriting: The hand is confirmed not to be Corrin’s, but no identity is given for who wrote it.  
- Ambiguity in presentation: It is unclear whether Corrin and Rimardo are the same person, collaborators, or if one is misattributing the invoice to the other.
