### Lathander

**Current status:** Alive (as a deity)  
**Current location:** Not applicable (divine entity)  
**Allegiance/faction:** None (independent deity)  
**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:** None (divine essence only)  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Deity of light, correction, and radiant power, worshipped by drow clerics including Valphine.  
- Embodies light as a principle — not a weapon, but a force of unyielding, visible, holy pain.  
- Radiates pain openly, indifferently, and universally; its light is power made radiant, pain made holy, correction delivered without apology from the sky.  
- Power is visible on the skin and does not conceal the wounds it inflicts.  
- Associated with psychic resonance in certain regions; its light can be specifically invoked as a ward against psychic pressure.  
- Valphine, a drow cleric, draws her power directly from Lathander and interprets his will through moments of decisive, non-subtle divine action (e.g., the killing of Cryovain).  
- Prutha invokes Lathander as the sun god who burns cultists under his fiery bright light.  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- His light was actively invoked by Valphine as a ward against psychic pressure in a glacial region.  
- His will was manifested in a precise, final divine strike that killed Cryovain, interpreted by Valphine as a deliberate, non-subtle affirmation.  
- His presence continues to be felt as a source of divine power by Valphine and invoked by others (e.g., Prutha) as a purifying, destructive force.

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Does not traffic in platitudes; his nature is real, unyielding, and indifferent to comfort.  
- Surface dwellers mistakenly interpret his radiating pain as warmth.  
- His light is not gentle — it is correction, not compassion.  
- His relationship with psychic phenomena is doctrinally significant, though the full nature of this link remains unexplained.

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction between "radiates pain openly, indifferently, on everyone" (ch39) and "Valphine associates the pain of sunlight with Lathander" (ch19) — is the pain universally felt by all, or only perceived by worshippers?  
- Unclear if Lathander’s light as a “ward” (ch34) is a unique doctrinal ability or a general property of his power.  
- No consensus on whether Lathander’s pain is physical, spiritual, or psychic — only that it is “visible on the skin.”  
- Prutha’s invocation of Lathander burning cultists (ch43) is not confirmed as a direct divine action or merely a metaphorical belief — attribution is ambiguous.  
- The nature of “psychic resonance” tied to Lathander’s light (ch34) is never explained — only that it exists.
