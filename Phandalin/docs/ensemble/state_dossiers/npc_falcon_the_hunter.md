## Identity
*Also known as: Falcon, The Falcon*

### Falcon

**Current status:** Alive  
**Current location:** On the road home to his lodge (last seen heading there after ch42); currently hosting at his lodge (ch45)  
**Allegiance/faction:** Formerly interventionist; now re-embraced active intervention after ch43; no longer aligned with Adabra or Talosians; operates independently but hosts under the persona of “the Dragon Slayer”  
**Current possessions / notable items:**  
- Boots of elvenkind (purchased with adventuring proceeds, worn during council and later)  
- Brass rings (touched before combat, muttered to someone who wasn’t there)  
- Longsword (used to strike three consecutive hits on an Anchorite)  
- Bow (used in ch42 and ch43; carried with war paint)  
- Ownership stake in Mountain Toe’s Goldmine (still held, though finances remain strained)  
- The lodge at Mountain Toe (reclaimed and now operational, hosting performances)  

**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Owner and host of the Falcon Lodge, operating under the title “the Dragon Slayer” to attract clientele  
- Recently reconciled with Vukradin after a categorical apology (ch42); Vukradin now acknowledges him as the lodge’s owner  
- Has re-established trust with the party, inviting them to return to his lodge (ch42, ch45)  
- Now mentors three orcs as “the finest stewards in all the North” (ch43)  
- No longer reports to any faction; acts as independent operator with renewed conviction in action  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Apologized unconditionally to the party and Vukradin for his behavior at the Druid Council (ch42)  
- Returned to active intervention, defeating Anchorites in combat with lethal precision (ch43)  
- Reclaimed his lodge from Talosian occupation and restored it as a functioning establishment  
- Embraced his past identity as a “Dragon Slayer” as a profitable persona to attract patrons  
- Hosted and praised the comedy act of Rimardo and Corrin, planning to book them for two nights per ten-day at Waterdeep-level prices (ch45)  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Once believed intervention led only to ruin, a belief instilled by Adabra — now rejects this, having found purpose and joy in acting  
- Confessed to past failures (failed dragon slaying, lodge burned while heroes did nothing) but no longer defines himself by them  
- Uses the “Dragon Slayer” persona knowingly as a business tool — the lie is now a performance, not a delusion  
- Still carries emotional weight from his past (touches brass rings, mutters to absent figures), but no longer paralyzes him  

## Uncertainty  
- Contradiction: Falcon claimed he “learned the error of [his] ways” as an interventionist (ch38), yet by ch43 he is actively intervening with joy — this is not framed as a return to old ways but a new understanding; no explicit reversal of belief is stated, only behavioral change.  
- Ambiguous attribution: Both Valphine and the narrator are recorded as being asked to mediate with Vukradin (ch38) — unclear if both were asked separately or if one quote is a duplicate/variant.  
- Unclear possession: It is stated he “has” the lodge (ch45) and “hosts under the name ‘the Dragon Slayer’” — but whether he fully owns it, leases it, or holds it through the consortium is not clarified post-Talosian takeover.  
- Unclear status of Vukradin’s presence: Vukradin acknowledges Falcon as lodge owner (ch45), but whether he is currently residing there or merely visiting is not stated.  
- Unclear origin of brass rings: Their significance or origin is never explained beyond being touched before combat.
