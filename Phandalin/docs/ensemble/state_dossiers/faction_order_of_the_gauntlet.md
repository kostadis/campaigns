### Order of the Gauntlet

**Current status:** Active but depleted.  
**Current location:** No single seat of power identified; operates across the North with presence in Candlekeep and likely tied to House Margaster’s influence.  
**Allegiance/faction:** Independent holy order; publicly aligned with principled action, art, and civilization-building; no longer allied with or subordinate to any other faction.  
**Current possessions / notable items:** Holds a magical object recently recovered (credit to be given to them); possesses a pin symbolizing membership; funded by House Margaster.  
**Current assignment / role:** Engaged in cultural outreach through art and music (e.g., Vukradin’s compositions), historical research (Candlekeep), and preservation of moral order; seeks to “make the north better” through non-plundering means.  
**Key relationships:**  
- Vukradin is a full-fledged member and is creating music for them.  
- Ser Kaelen is a representative who defends their integrity.  
- House Margaster is a financial patron.  
- Previously fought wars against Brewbarry’s tribe and the narrator’s tribe — legacy of enmity remains.  
- Opposed by Adabra, who views them as destructive idealists.  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Funded and conducted extensive research at Candlekeep.  
- Successfully recovered a magical object (credit to be publicly attributed to them).  
- Maintained public image of moral authority despite accusations (e.g., Harbin’s scandal).  
- Attempting to expand their influence through art and narrative, as seen in Vukradin’s work.  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Believe art embodies their mission to save the world without plundering it.  
- See themselves as protectors of balance, though critics (Adabra) accuse them of imposing their ideal of “civilization” through force.  
- Their numbers are critically reduced due to prior wars (Dragon, Giants, and unnamed conflicts), limiting operational capacity despite continued ambition.

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction between their stated non-plundering ethos and their history of violent war against tribes — whether this is seen as defensive or imperialistic is unclarified.  
- Unclear if Vukradin’s membership is active or symbolic — he is called a “full-fledged member” by Ser Kaelen and works for them, but no evidence confirms his current duties beyond music.  
- The “war…” referenced in ch37 is unnamed — could be a third major conflict or a conflation; chronology of wars is ambiguous.  
- Whether House Margaster’s funding is ongoing or a past transaction is not specified.  
- No current location or headquarters is confirmed — only that they operate in the North and have ties to Candlekeep.  
- Adabra’s critique is presented as an external view — whether it reflects internal dissent or corruption within the Order is unknown.
