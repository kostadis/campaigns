## Identity
*Also known as: Carver*

### The Carver

**Current status:** Alive, but non-human — a vessel for a sentient black obsidian sword.  
**Current location:** Roof of Icespire Hold, standing over Cryovain.  
**Allegiance/faction:** None; operates as an autonomous entity. The sword is its true form.  
**Current possessions / notable items:** Black obsidian sword (level-draining, mental amplifier, sentient, the true Carver).  
**Spells/feats:** Capable of telepathic communication with followers (though none are currently present); emits psychic residue that warps rock and causes it to phase in/out of existence; speaks in a tongue that makes ears bleed.  
**Wounds:** None noted — body is paralyzed but not destroyed; remains animated by the sword’s will.  
**Current assignment / role:** Performing a ritual to bind and control the white dragon Cryovain, intending to wear it like armor and burn Phandalin to the ground.  
**Key relationships:** The obsidian sword is the true entity; the human form is a hollow vessel. Previously allied with ogres (past). No companions currently observed.  
**Defining recent actions:** Walked unimpeded through the drake’s lair, killing the young drake without hesitation; traveled alone through mountain passes, warping rocks unconsciously; arrived at Icespire Hold and initiated ritual on Cryovain; paralyzed by a *Hold Person* spell but remained active through the sword’s will; continues ritual despite physical immobility.

## Uncertainty
- The fate of the Carver’s former followers — while telepathic communication was established, no current followers are observed or confirmed present.
- Whether the Carver’s body will recover from the *Hold Person* paralysis or if the sword’s will alone sustains the ritual.
- Whether the plan to burn Phandalin is still active — it was stated as intent, but the current focus is entirely on Cryovain.
- The origin or nature of the sword’s sentience — it is described as the true Carver, but its history or creation is unmentioned.
- Whether the “tongue that makes ears bleed” is a spell, psychic effect, or inherent property of the sword — no mechanism is specified.
