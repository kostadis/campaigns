### Woodland Manse

- **Current status:** Burned, occupied, destroyed.  
- **Current location:** An hour’s walk from Falcon’s Hunting Lodge.  
- **Allegiance/Faction:** Occupied by orcs and anchorites of Talos.  
- **Current possessions / notable items:**  
  - Brass candelabra on a dining table.  
  - Cursed effigies made of dried sticks on a mantel (with scorch marks where the party’s likenesses were burned off).  
  - Kitchen shelves (contents unspecified).  
- **Notable structural features:**  
  - Crumbling two-story manor nearly hidden beneath thorny ivy.  
  - Situated atop a six-foot-high stone foundation.  
  - Stone balcony at 10 feet height overlooking the main entrance.  
  - Flagstone courtyard with a 5-foot stone well and thick vines erupting from it.  
  - Vines wrapping support pillars.  
  - Main staircase with spikes below.  
  - Collapsed ceiling with a hole.  
  - Doorway leading to darkness above the upper floor.  
  - Upper hallway.  
  - Rooms repurposed for dark rituals.  
- **Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
  - Site of an active Talosian ritual building toward something loud and terrible.  
  - Location from which Gorthok emerged ahead of schedule.  
  - Previously attempted and failed to be cleared by the party due to fireballs and lightning.  
  - Double-circled and marked “Active” on a Talosian map fragment; next target after the henge fell.  
- **Defining recent actions:**  
  - The party entered through the portico in the morning, recalling their prior defeat here.  
  - The cultists’ rituals have caused physical manifestations: vines erupting from the well, cursed effigies of the party on the mantel, and the emergence of Gorthok.  
  - The manse remains a focal point of Talosian activity despite prior destruction.  

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction between [ch22] describing the balcony as “overlooking the main entrance” and [ch44] specifying it is “at a height of 10 feet” — no conflict in substance, but the original 6-foot foundation height is not reconciled with the 10-foot balcony height.  
- Unclear whether the “burned, occupied, destroyed” state from [ch39] is fully consistent with the active ritual and intact interior features described in [ch44]–[ch45] — possible that fire damage is localized or recently repaired by cultists.  
- No confirmation whether the orcs and anchorites are still physically present inside at this exact moment, only that the manse is “occupied” — absence of recent encounter logs leaves current occupancy ambiguous.  
- Attribution of the “devastating lightning” and “fireballs” in [ch23] and [ch31] to the same source or different entities is unclear — possibly cultists, possibly the ritual itself.
