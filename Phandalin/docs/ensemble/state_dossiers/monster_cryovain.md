## Identity
*Also known as: dragon, white dragon*

### Cryovain

**Current status:** Dead  
**Current location:** Rooftop battlements of Icespire Hold, within the summoning circle  
**Allegiance/faction:** Dominated by the black obsidian sword (no longer autonomous)  
**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:**  
- Bound by a magical restraint that vibrates at a frequency setting teeth together  
- Missing nose, likely due to magical or physical alteration  
- Damaged eyes: hollows where alertness should live are empty  
- Scales: white and almost translucent at the edges, now turned the color of old ice left too long in shadow  
- Capable of emitting a cone of frost that penetrates armor and causes deep cold damage, reorganizing living tissue  
- Capable of launching thirty feet into the air with a single wingbeat  

**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Held in the summoning circle on the rooftop battlements of Icespire Hold as a weaponized entity under the control of the black obsidian sword  
- Last seen as the source of a devastating breath weapon used in combat; no indication of allegiance to any living faction beyond the sword’s domination  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Launched into the air with one wingbeat from the summoning circle  
- Used a frost breath weapon capable of penetrating armor and reorganizing living bodies  
- Was killed in combat, though its body remained warm despite its frost-breath nature  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Once a sentient white dragon, now stripped of autonomy and identity by the obsidian sword — “whatever was left of Cryovain's mind was gone entirely”  
- Its winter is a defining ecological event: “before Cryovain's winter broke the quiet” — implying its presence or influence reshaped the land long before its current confinement  

## Uncertainty
- The exact moment of death is not specified — only that the body was found warm after death, but no witness describes the killing blow or who delivered it.  
- Contradiction: Ch35 describes Cryovain as “sleeping,” then immediately corrects that he was “held,” not sleeping; Ch36 and Ch37 confirm he was active and then dead. The “sleeping” description is overwritten by later facts.  
- Attribution of the obsidian sword’s dominance is clear, but the identity of the entity wielding or channeling the sword is never named — only that the sword seized Cryovain.  
- Whether the magical restraint is still active after death is unconfirmed — no fact describes its state post-mortem.  
- The cause of the missing nose and eye damage is described as “something had been taken” or “done to him,” but the agent or method is unknown.
