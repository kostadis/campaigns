### Cult of Talos

**Current status:** Active but unstable.  
**Current location:** No fixed headquarters; operates in scattered, transient cells — recently observed in orc communities near cursed clearings.  
**Allegiance/faction:** Independent extremist cult; no formal hierarchy or external patron.  
**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:** None specified. Clerics claim divine inspiration but no specific artifacts or magical items are named.  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:** Followers commit violence as acts of faith; clerics believe they receive direct divine messages from Talos. Recent converts include orcs whose shaman was killed. No known leadership structure or chain of command.  
**Defining recent actions:** Orcs have joined the cult en masse after their shaman’s death; they perform battle cries as holy rituals, not intimidation. A cursed clearing has been linked to their activity, interpreted as a signature of their presence.  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:** The cult is not centralized — it manifests wherever individuals embrace destruction and hear Talos’s voice within themselves. It is self-annihilating by nature: it explodes in violence, then collapses inward. Its power lies not in organization, but in the raw, chaotic faith of those who revel in destruction.  

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction in scale: Ch18 implies a coordinated attempt to steal an artifact (suggesting some cohesion), while ch39 repeatedly emphasizes structural incoherence and lack of stability. Cannot reconcile whether the theft attempt was a lone act or representative of broader cult capability.
- Unclear attribution: Was the artifact theft (ch18) carried out by cult members acting independently, or by a rogue cell? No actor is named.
- Ambiguous chronology: The orc conversions (ch20) occurred before the events of ch39, but it is unclear if those same orcs are still active in the cult or if they were absorbed, killed, or dispersed.
- Unclear if the cursed clearing is a cult hallmark or an isolated event — Vukradin asks if it’s “typical,” but no confirmation beyond Xanth’s acknowledgment of its association.
