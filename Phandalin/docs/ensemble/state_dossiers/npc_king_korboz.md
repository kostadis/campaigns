### King Korboz

**Current status:** Alive  
**Current location:** Unknown (last observed in diplomatic setting with King Gnercli)  
**Allegiance/faction:** Co-ruler of the Dual Kingship (with King Gnercli)  
**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:** None specified  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Co-king alongside King Gnercli; claims seniority based on length of reign.  
- Reports to no one; asserts right to preferential treatment as the “senior king.”  
- Relationship with King Gnercli is adversarial: Gnercli rejects Korboz’s claims of seniority and refuses to grant him authority over decisions.  
- Last seen shouting in protest after Gnercli reaffirmed the dual-king structure and dismissed Korboz’s demands.  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Recovered from prior hysterics and now actively asserts political claims.  
- Publicly challenged Gnercli’s leadership structure, demanding preferential treatment as the longer-reigning monarch.  
- Reaffirmed disbelief that shapeshifters existed — implying his earlier hysteria may have been based on ignorance, not insight.  
- Used the phrase “Together” to endorse collective decision-making, though his actions contradict this by demanding unilateral privilege.  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Seeks to elevate his status above Gnercli despite the formal equality of their rule.  
- May be manipulating the “shapeshifter” accusation as a political tool — first used in panic, now referenced to undermine Gnercli’s system.  
- Believes legitimacy derives from tenure, not consensus — a belief directly opposed by Gnercli’s governance model.  

## Uncertainty
- The exact location of King Korboz is not specified in any chapter after ch04.  
- It is unclear whether his claim of being “senior king” is factually true (i.e., whether he has truly reigned longer than Gnercli) — this is asserted by Korboz but never verified.  
- The nature of his recovery from hysteria is unexplained: was it due to external reassurance, internal realization, or political calculation?  
- It is ambiguous whether his statement “That you didn’t believe existed!” refers to shapeshifters in general or specifically to Vukradin — the quote is fragmented and lacks clear referent.  
- Whether Korboz still believes Vukradin is a shapeshifter is unresolved — he now denies believing shapeshifters existed at all, which contradicts his earlier outburst.
