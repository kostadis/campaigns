### Adabra

**Current Status:** Alive  
**Current Location:** Phandalin (likely still residing at the windmill atop Umbrage Hill, though not explicitly confirmed present in recent chapters)  
**Allegiance/Faction:** Naturalist faction of the Emerald Enclave; opposes the Order of the Gauntlet and all forms of interventionism  

**Current Possessions / Notable Items:**  
- Deed to the land where the dwarven house stands atop Umbrage Hill  
- Vial of healing potion with enhanced scent and taste (previously produced; current stock unknown but implied still in practice)  
- Meril’s Staff (used to test her own conviction; confirmed she wielded it to verify she was not deceived, controlled, or lying to herself)  
- Five conflict-free gold pieces (previously given to Vukradin; may still hold others)  

**Current Assignment / Role and Key Relationships:**  
- Leading a factional campaign within the Emerald Enclave to ostracize interventionists, particularly Lyra and Soma  
- Publicly accusing Soma of being a dangerous interventionist for holding Meril’s Staff without the Enclave’s blessing and for teaching interference over observation  
- Mobilizing support among Enclave members to enforce non-interventionist doctrine; arranged and led a council meeting to present her findings on the Whispering Wood  
- Former mentor to Vukradin, now viewed with cold dismissal; he is no longer regarded as an ally  
- Hostile toward Valphine, avoiding eye contact and speaking in clipped sentences  
- Respected and feared by followers such as Falcon, whom she taught to interpret pain as proof that action corrupts  
- Reported to be a druid at heart, though now operates as a doctrinal enforcer  

**Defining Recent Actions:**  
- Walked to the sacred grove in the Whispering Wood three weeks ago and confirmed the treant was dead — roots collapsed, crown blackened, grove ruined — not merely wounded or sleeping  
- Investigated discrepancies between eyewitness accounts of the grove’s condition and the official narrative, identifying gaps and contradictions  
- Publicly denounced Soma and Lyra as interventionists who disrupted natural balance, using their actions as leverage to ostracize them from the Enclave  
- Gave a meticulously delivered council speech defending non-intervention, framing ecological collapse as a consequence of meddling, not its cause  
- Paid Vukradin five gold pieces for his earlier account of the planar magpie — then later rejected his narrative after visiting the grove, calling his story false  
- Accused the Order of the Gauntlet of being transparent, zealous, and destructive, claiming they kill creatures and cheer their “good deeds” with no regard for balance  
- Fumed after being outvoted at the Enclave council but showed no sign of retreat — walked out with the posture of someone choosing a different battlefield  

**Revealed Motivations or Secrets:**  
- Believes the multiverse is one unified thing — there is no inherent good or evil, only natural processes, including planar shifts, divine interventions, and fiendish incursions  
- Views intervention of any kind — whether by adventurers, druids, or paladins — as a violation of cosmic balance that forces destructive responses from higher powers  
- Sees herself as a true believer, not a manipulator; her conviction is absolute, and she is furious that reality does not conform to her internal framework  
- Believes the answer to ecological and planar blight is withdrawal — to let nature find equilibrium without aid, even if that means accepting death and decay as sacred  
- Considers the term “barbarian” a tool of oppression, used to justify the destruction of those deemed outside the “natural order”  
- Her personal grief over the treant’s death is not emotional, but ideological — its destruction confirms her worldview: intervention always leads to ruin
