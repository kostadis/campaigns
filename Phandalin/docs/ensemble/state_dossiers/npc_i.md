### I

**Current status:** Alive  
**Current location:** Unknown (last observed in proximity to the Circle of Thunder and the Whispering Grove, actively engaging with Valphine and Ser Kaelen)  
**Allegiance/faction:** Independent; no longer aligned with either faction, though maintains a pragmatic, low-trust association with Ser Kaelen  
**Current possessions / notable items:**  
- A staff with three leaves (held at the Circle of Thunder)  
- A halberd (used in service to a town; still carried)  
- Twenty-five words (known and usable; implied to be a personal lexicon or spellcraft limit)  
- Ability to invoke Lathander’s light as a ward (recently confirmed; not fully understood)  

**Current assignment / role and key relationships:**  
- Acting as an independent agent seeking to heal corruption, particularly in the Whispering Grove (now known to be possessed by a planar rift)  
- Travels with Valphine; has watched her reason toward hazards her entire life  
- Maintains a tense, ambiguous relationship with Ser Kaelen: does not trust him, but considers him “as close as anybody”; listens for what he does not say  
- Previously associated with Adabra, but now questions why Adabra has been cooling toward them  

**Defining recent actions:**  
- Confirmed the Carver did not see them by surviving in place — a method they now prefer for verification  
- Observed and analyzed Cryovain’s seething, compressed rage beneath his chains; worried that killing the Carver might cause the chains to evaporate  
- Accepted the town’s barrel as fair exchange for their halberd and service — a moment of moral clarity  
- Rejected the cleric’s violent interpretation of Lathander’s light; chose to proselytize light as a principle, not a weapon  
- Held the three-leaf staff at the Circle of Thunder, silently recalling Meril  
- Declined to touch certain entities after learning their danger (“I’m not touching them”)  
- Acknowledged the cold, useless satisfaction of being right  

**Revealed motivations or secrets:**  
- Once believed themselves a naturalist; now sees corruption as “sickness” requiring healing, not balance  
- Struggles with identity: questions whether their abilities (like invoking Lathander’s light) were always within them, or merely unexamined  
- Carries unresolved grief over Corbin and Meril — thoughts of them surface “briefly, painfully”  
- Feels trapped in darkness — a memory tied to personal trauma, possibly from their time with the tribe  
- Wants only their music studio — a buried longing that underscores their displacement  
- Believes most conflicts stem from misunderstandings escalated by volume — and refuses to match it
