### Rimardo

**Current Status:** Alive  
**Current Location:** Unknown (vanished after Chapter 44; last seen exiting Falcon’s Lodge; referenced in Chapter 45 as a retired performer Falcon wishes to hire)  
**Allegiance/Faction:** None (self-identifies as independent performer; not bound to Falcon’s Lodge or any known organization)  
**Current Possessions / Notable Items:**  
- A small case  
- A deck of cards  
- A small knife (used to pin the invoice to the table)  
- A single gold coin (given to Vukradin)  
- An elegant, slightly too-fine coat  

**Current Assignment / Role:** Retired comedy performer (per Falcon’s intent in Chapter 45); previously billed as contracted for an eight o’clock show at Falcon’s Lodge — though the show never occurred on schedule.  
**Key Relationships:**  
- Last seen with Corrin (silent companion, carries items, writes invoices in non-Corrin handwriting)  
- Promised Vukradin: “If you stick with it at the very end, you’ll find out, and we’ll perform together.”  
- Has interacted with the party as theatrical critic and cryptic guide; refers to them as “a wonderful audience” who will become “a better one in Leilon.”  

**Defining Recent Actions:**  
- Entered Falcon’s Lodge uninvited, reframing the party’s battle with a boar and subsequent apocalypse as a botched theatrical production.  
- Delivered a sweeping critique of events using theatrical metaphors: called the dragon an “understudy playing the lead,” the gods of fate “six opening acts,” and the wine cellar’s destruction “the real tragedy.”  
- Declared the party “honorary members of an orc tribe” after their absorption into orc culture.  
- Offered rounds for survivors and near-dead, paused for silence over the wine cellar, and praised mid-apocalypse wine-drinking as “terrible theology and flawless hospitality.”  
- Issued the “House Rules”: (1) “No requests. We are the request. You simply haven’t been billed yet.” (2) “Non-refundable, academic, as no one has paid.” (3) Redacted — “We’ve stopped asking.”  
- Warned of “the storm you’ve met” as a touring entity and of a sealed door beneath the stage that “management insists is structural, lying as management always does.”  
- Vanished after the hall doors shut, leaving no trace of who closed them.  

**Revealed Motivations / Secrets:**  
- Claims to be an actor and comedian, not a treasure hunter: “We’re here for the show. Top Billing.”  
- Believes reality is a performance where contracts are never signed but always enforced, and memory is the only legal defense.  
- Suggests the universe operates under unspoken theatrical rules — gods, storms, dragons, and even death are roles being played by actors unaware they are not the headliner.  
- Implies he has been in the “acting biz for eons” and cannot remember its beginning or end.  
- Hints that Leilon holds the context necessary to understand the full performance — the party’s current events are only an act.  

## Uncertainty
- Rimardo’s current whereabouts after vanishing — Chapter 45 confirms Falcon wishes to hire him, but does not confirm he is alive, reachable, or in Leilon.  
- Whether Rimardo and Corrin are still together — Corrin was last seen carrying items and writing the invoice, but no later interaction confirms their continued association.  
- The nature of “the end” Rimardo refers to — no definition or context is given.  
- Whether the “single gold coin” given to Vukradin was the only payment, or if Rimardo still holds other valuables.  
- Whether Rimardo’s claim of being “retired” (per Chapter 45) means he has permanently ceased performing, or if it is a stage persona — the text does not resolve this.  
- Attribution of the invoice’s handwriting: it is “not Corrin’s,” but who wrote it remains unattributed.  
- Whether the sealed door beneath the stage still exists and is still “lying about being structural” — no follow-up confirms its current state.
