### Soma

**Current Status:** Alive  
**Current Location:** Inside the Falcon’s abandoned manor, having just cleared a upper-level chamber after a battle with stirges and animated twig blights.  
**Allegiance/Faction:** Independent druid of Mieliki; formerly associated with the Emerald Enclave, now estranged from its formal structures. Identifies as an interventionist in the great schism.  
**Current Possessions / Notable Items:**  
- Meril’s Staff — old, with one leaf sprouting anew; carries psychic residue and responds to her presence; no longer just a tool but a living connection to her mentor; she knows its tremors have become a shout.  
- +1 Halberd — retrieved from the skeletal captain of the *Star Crossed Lover* shipwreck.  
- Staff of Bird Calls — recently acquired from the manor; she took it without speaking, knowing Vukradin would disapprove, though she considers it “phenomenally useless” to a druid.  
- Shark tooth necklace — in progress, made from teeth of sharks slain in giant toad form.  
- Druidic scroll — acquired from a ruined temple; contains a druidic spell.  
- Three locked chests — ferried from shipwrecks, still unopened.  
- Potion of ground — carried for terrain manipulation.  
- One leaf on her staff — newly sprouted, which she interprets as “an angel got its wings.”  

**Notable Spells / Feats / Abilities:**  
- Wild Shape — can transform into brown bear, giant wolf spider, giant toad, elk, and possibly owl bear (expressed desire). Recently burned both remaining wild shapes to assume a larger form.  
- Moonbeam — her signature spell; tethered to her wakefulness; used to reveal, damage, and control enemies.  
- Speak with Animals — frequently used to communicate with beasts, including boars, stags, pigs, and carrion crawlers.  
- Find Familiar — summons a rat familiar to scout; maintains sensory link.  
- Flaming Sphere — used to burn webs and clear enemy ranks.  
- Protection from Energy — known, but competes with Spirit Guardians for concentration.  
- Spirit Guardians — summons a whirling radiant presence around herself in serious fights.  
- Pillar of Light — used to force shapeshifters back into their true forms.  
- Mold Earth — considered but not learned.  
- Resistance — not learned.  
- Sending — used to communicate; understands its purpose fully.  

**Current Assignment / Role and Key Relationships:**  
- Acting as guide, protector, and moral compass for the party, especially Vukradin — whom she views as a naive shell-sprout in need of grounding.  
- Recently severed ties with Adabra after discovering manipulation and betrayal regarding planar interventions.  
- Respects and maintains bond with Boney, the skeletal horse of Lady Alagondar; trusts him implicitly.  
- Has a quiet, unspoken connection with Xanthopoulos, the centaur, whom she healed and with whom she shared a moment of mutual understanding.  
- No longer seeks payment; her role is now defined by necessity and legacy.  
- Vukradin’s actions increasingly frustrate her, but she still believes in his potential.  
- The party’s recent actions have made her the de facto voice of reason and consequence.  

**Defining Recent Actions:**  
- Transformed into a giant wolf spider to scale the icy cliff of Icespire Hold and drop ropes for the party.  
- Shifted into a bear to scout upper levels of the manor and later tore through encroaching plant creatures.  
- Summoned a rat familiar to scout arrow slits and confirmed the manor’s layout.  
- Used Moonbeam to reveal and damage invisible stalkers and the paralyzed veteran — not to kill, but to remove threat.  
- Declared the Falcon a “chode” and dismissed his claim to the manor.  
- Recognized the animated twig figures as “Twigged lights!” with delight.  
- Took the Staff of Bird Calls without comment, knowing Vukradin would disapprove.  
- Kept a precise tally of stirges killed by each party member — “Two for her. She kept a count.”  
- Announced “Guys, it’s all clear!” — unaware of two hidden stirges.  
- Said “Oh, dear” when the world had arranged itself against them — her signature phrase for inevitable, unchangeable doom.  
- Reverted to tortle form after being struck down by Gorthok’s tusks, dying briefly before being revived — her shell the only constant.  

**Revealed Motivations / Secrets:**  
- Does not seek legacy, throne, or name in history — only wants to keep the world a safer place and ensure all creatures have ground to stand on.  
- Feels responsible for Meril’s staff — a gift given not for sentiment, but because he *picked her*.  
- Believes the Druids Enclave’s purpose is to save the forest, not the multiverse — and that intervention is sometimes necessary to prevent greater harm.  
- Thinks all money is blood money until proven otherwise, but accepts gold of questionable provenance if it serves survival.  
- Has seen too much to be surprised — but still grieves Corbin’s death, and now seeks to avenge him not out of rage, but as a sacred duty.  
- Feels the land is a “negative space” — pressing inward, wounded, and watching.  
- Has grown into her role as an interventionist — not by choice, but because the world demanded it.  
- No longer believes in celestial touching — “You’re just another creature born into this world.”  
- Her greatest fear is not death, but irrelevance — that the world will forget the quiet things she protected.  

## Uncertainty  
- The exact nature and origin of the new leaf on Meril’s Staff — whether it signifies resurrection, divine intervention, or a metaphysical shift — is not explained.  
- Whether Soma was truly killed by Gorthok’s tusks in ch43 or merely incapacitated — the text says she “reverted as she fell” and “crumpled onto the mud,” but later actions in ch44 and ch45 imply she was revived. No explicit revival moment is described.  
- The identity of “Meril” — whether he is the same as “Meryl” mentioned in ch31 — is ambiguous; the names are phonetically similar but not confirmed as identical.  
- Whether Soma is still traveling with Vukradin — she was last seen in the Falcon’s manor, but no confirmation exists of her current companions after the stirge encounter.  
- The current status of the three locked chests from the shipwrecks — whether they were opened, looted, or remain untouched — is unrecorded.  
- Whether the Staff of Bird Calls was taken for practical use, irony, or symbolic rebellion — her reasoning is silent.  
- The meaning behind her muttering “ten thousand for a hammer” — context is absent.  
- Whether her statement “We are.” in ch34 refers to the party, druids, or all living things — the referent is unstated.
