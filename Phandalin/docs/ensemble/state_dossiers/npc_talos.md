### Talos

**Current status:** Deity (non-corporeal, not bound to physical location)  
**Current location:** Not physically present; exists as an abstract force within those who seek destruction  
**Allegiance/faction:** Worshipped by the Anchorite (ch43)  
**Current possessions / notable items, spells, feats, wounds:** None (non-corporeal entity)  
**Current assignment / role and key relationships:** Embodiment of destruction; invoked as a divine source by the Anchorite, who claims to have burned a shrine and broken its handles in Talos’s name. Talos is not tied to a physical temple or avatar — his presence is felt only in the hearts of those who revel in destruction.  
**Defining recent actions:** The Anchorite performed a ritual act of desecration — burning a shrine and breaking its handles — as an offering or act of devotion to Talos.  

## Uncertainty
- The nature of Talos’s "presence" is described metaphorically by Xanth ("in the breast of those who seek to destroy") but the Anchorite treats him as a personal god to whom prayers are spoken — this contradiction in interpretation (abstract force vs. personal deity) is unresolved.  
- It is unclear whether the shrine burned by the Anchorite was the *only* known shrine to Talos, or if others still exist — no other shrines or worshippers are mentioned.  
- The Anchorite’s invocation ("Talos, my god!") implies personal devotion, but no evidence confirms whether Talos responds, influences, or even acknowledges such worship — the entity’s agency or awareness remains unobserved.
