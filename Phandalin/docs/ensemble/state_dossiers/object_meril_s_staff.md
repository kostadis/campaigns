## Identity
*Also known as: Meryl's staff, Soma's staff, mentor's staff, staff, staff of bird calls, the staff*

### staff

- **Current holder:** Soma (last seen in ch45, after being jammed into her fur)
- **Current location:** Hidden within Soma’s fur, knotted against her shoulder (ch45)
- **Current condition:** 
  - Has four fully grown leaves (ch40: three leaves; ch41: fourth bud appeared; ch42: new leaf sprouted from a bud → now four leaves)
  - No further growth observed since ch42, but Soma feels its potential (ch43)
  - No longer trembles (ch41: trembling ceased after the fight)
  - Warm to the touch (ch38, ch43)
  - Quiet presence — not absent, but different from prior trembling (ch41)
- **Notable properties:**
  - Originally given by Meril to Soma (ch37, ch39)
  - Carries a subtle presence linked to Meril’s legacy (ch37, ch38)
  - Responds to magic and environment: resisted uneven ground (ch38), reacted to cursed clearing (ch39), paid attention near the Circle of Thunder (ch40)
  - Has been communicating with Soma ahead of her senses for months (ch39)
  - Appears to grow leaves in response to events or proximity to powerful magic
- **Current assignment / role:** Carried by Soma as a tool of guidance and latent power; now concealed to avoid detection
- **Defining recent actions:**
  - Sprouted a fourth leaf after the fight at the cursed clearing (ch41–ch42)
  - Fell silent after the fourth leaf emerged, replacing tremor with a deeper presence (ch41)
  - Concealed by Soma in her fur during a moment of evasion (ch45)

## Uncertainty
- Contradiction: ch39 and ch40 describe the staff as having "one leaf left" and "three leaves" respectively — but ch41 and ch42 confirm growth to four leaves. The "one leaf" in ch39 must be an error or misstatement, as later facts (ch40–ch42) consistently show progression to four leaves.
- Unclear attribution: ch39 says “the staff has been talking to me” — Soma is female, but the quote uses “me” in a context that could be misread as male. No other pronouns are used; all other references use “Soma” or “the narrator.” Assume Soma is the speaker unless contradicted.
- Ambiguity in ch45: Soma finds a *plain staff* and hides it — is this the same staff (now concealed) or a different one? The text says “a staff, plain enough,” implying it may be a replacement or decoy. But she “jam[med] it into the thatch of my fur where it knotted against my shoulder and disappeared” — and the prior staff was *already* carried there. The lack of description of difference (e.g., no mention of leaves or warmth) raises doubt: is this the original staff now hidden, or a new one? The fact that the original had unique properties (leaves, warmth, voice) and this one is described as “plain” suggests it may be a different object — but Soma’s action implies she’s replacing or concealing the original. This is unresolved.
