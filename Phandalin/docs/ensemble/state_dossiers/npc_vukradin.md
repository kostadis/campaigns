## Identity
*Also known as: Bard, Vukardin*

### Vukradin

**Current Status:** Alive  
**Current Location:** Likely en route to or in Phandalin, following the conclusion of the events at the Woodland Manse and the discovery of the gold mine.  
**Allegiance/Faction:** Former associate member of the Order of the Gauntlet; now operates independently, though still aligned with its ideals of conflict-free action. No longer formally affiliated.  

**Current Possessions / Notable Items:**  
- A vial of unknown origin, tucked into the inner pocket of his pack behind folded notes — he carries it without awareness of its nature.  
- The obsidian sword known as the “Bluetooth sword,” which he refuses to keep but has not yet returned — he recognizes it as a mental amplifier capable of projecting music directly into minds.  
- A necklace of fireballs, held as a kindness for its unknown rightful owner.  
- The Boots of Elvenkind, referred to by him as “our fair, organic, fair-trade, conflict-free Boots of Elvenkind.”  
- His clarinet and other musical instruments — he refuses to leave them behind.  
- A pin from the Order of the Gauntlet — he believes it is lost.  
- A sealed envelope addressed to Adabra containing six gold pieces (five plus one for interest).  

**Spells / Feats / Abilities:**  
- Master of Bardic Inspiration, often using it to enhance allies’ actions or turn failures into successes.  
- Proficient in spells including: *Sleep*, *Silence*, *Hold Person*, *Phantasmal Force*, *Cloud of Daggers*, *Silvery Barbs*, *Zone of Truth*, and *Universal Speech* (once per long rest, up to four creatures).  
- Uses musical performance as a form of enchantment and psychological influence — his music is described as a weapon of persuasion and moral clarity.  
- Known for a unique compositional style called “Vukradian Scales,” which reorders music away from harmony — controversial but admired.  
- Possesses a rare gift of magical speech, used to communicate directly with animals (e.g., boars).  

**Current Assignment / Role and Key Relationships:**  
- **Role:** Independent advocate for ethical adventuring, seeking to fund his music studio through conflict-free gold.  
- **Primary Motivation:** To build a music studio to spread his music across Faerûn, funded only by honest, earned coin — not plunder.  
- **Key Relationships:**  
  - **Soma:** Respects her as the most careful person he knows; relies on her judgment; their bond is deep but unspoken.  
  - **Brewbarry:** Trusted companion; confidant; the only person who ever paid him a 10/10 for his music.  
  - **Valphine:** Musical collaborator; he acknowledges her contributions elevate his art; trusts her enough to share his vision.  
  - **Falcon:** Former ally; now estranged; Vukradin insists on a personal apology before reconciliation.  
  - **Adabra:** Respects her fairness; returned her five gold pieces plus interest; their relationship is complicated by moral dissonance.  
  - **Ser Kaelen:** Distrusts him deeply; believes Kaelen absorbed and repurposed his language and ideals.  
  - **Sister Kaella:** Views her as an agent of the Carver; spared her life under strict conditions.  
  - **Corbin:** Former colleague; Vukradin believes he and Kaella will get along.  
  - **Jenna Roscoe:** Believes she lied to him and calls her “a nasty, no-good treasure hunter.”  

**Defining Recent Actions (Collapsed):**  
- Successfully led the group in defeating the Carver, the sentient obsidian sword, and dismantling its influence over Icespire Hold and the White Dragon.  
- Refused to take the dragon’s hoard, insisting it was stolen property, even though it would have solved his funding problems.  
- Returned the Talisman of Moesko and other recovered items to their rightful owners or potential heirs, including the scholar who built the Woodland Manse.  
- Used *Zone of Truth* to force confessions about looted items among the party, revealing internal moral fractures.  
- Declared the group’s work done in the Woodland Manse and insisted they return to Phandalin.  
- Refused to allow the orcs to convert other tribes, rejecting expansionist zealotry.  
- Composed a marching song in three-four time during the ascent to Icespire Hold and later sang *The Hand Obould Offered* with Valphine, fully meaning its words despite previously calling it manipulative.  
- Took responsibility for the group’s ethical direction, even when it isolated him — insisting on returning the entire building to its owner, not just its contents.  
- Reconciled with Falcon after receiving a clean, unqualified apology — though he remains wary of Falcon’s lack of core convictions.  
- Expressed profound disillusionment with the gold mine’s profits, realizing the Commission’s cut and bureaucratic caveats made the “honest” coin feel hollow.  

**Revealed Motivations / Secrets:**  
- Vukradin’s deepest motivation is not wealth, but validation: he wants his music to be loved, and he wants to be loved for who he is — not just as a tool for change.  
- He is haunted by the whisper of “The numbers. Always the numbers,” from the pool in the Whispering Wood, a constant reminder of a world driven by transactional morality.  
- He believes he is a “shell-sprout” — a flawed, self-deluding idealist — but refuses to abandon his principles even when they cost him financially.  
- He secretly fears he will be poor forever, and that his studio will never be built — yet he would rather die poor than compromise.  
- He views himself as a missionary, not a warrior — his weapon is truth, music, and moral clarity, not blades or spells.  
- He is deeply wounded that Falcon, whom he admired, never came to see his performances — a personal betrayal that cuts deeper than any theft.  

## Uncertainty
- **Ambiguous Chronology:** The exact timing of Vukradin’s departure from the druid council and his return to Phandalin is unclear — was it before or after the final deliberation? The facts state he left early and the council’s disappointment centered on his absence, but the sequence of his final actions in the manse and his decision to return to Phandalin is not fully pinned to a single moment.  
- **Contradictory Attribution:** In ch03, Valphine thinks Vukradin delivered the killing blow to the ogre; in ch02, he claims he didn’t notice what Soma picked up from the corpse — but Soma’s role in the ogre’s death is never confirmed. Who actually killed the ogre? The facts conflict on attribution.  
- **Unclear Current Status of Items:** The obsidian sword and necklace of fireballs are held “for the owner,” but no owner has been identified. Are they still in his possession? Are they still unclaimed? The dossier cannot confirm their current location beyond “he carries them.”  
- **Ambiguous Loyalty to Order of the Gauntlet:** He believes his pin is lost and says he doesn’t trust them — but has he formally resigned? Are his principles still aligned with theirs, or has he outgrown them? The facts suggest disengagement, but not formal severance.  
- **Unresolved Emotional State:** He says “I’m gonna be poor forever,” yet he also says he’s “not making very much progress on funding my studio.” Is this resignation, or a rhetorical device? The emotional weight of this statement is real, but its permanence is unconfirmed.  
- **Unclear Fate of the Vial:** The vial in his pack is unknown to him — who placed it there? When? Why? The fact is stated, but no source or intention is provided.  
- **Status of the “Bluetooth Sword”:** He says it “has to go back to its owner,” but has he attempted to return it? Is it still in his possession? The facts confirm he picked it up and refuses to keep it, but not whether he has acted to return it.
