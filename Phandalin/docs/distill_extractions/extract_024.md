## World Events

- The party was attacked at the Woodland Manse by a shapeshifting Talosian cultist using lightning, and an Anchorite disguised as a wild bull struck Soma and Vukradin with electrical bolts.
- Valphine used divine healing powers to restore her companions during the battle.
- Additional cultists emerged from within the Woodland Manse and the surrounding area, leaving the party dangerously outnumbered.
- The party made a tactical decision to retreat from the Woodland Manse.
- Brewbarry carried Vukradin to safety in giant form; Soma carried Valphine during the retreat.
- The talking boars expressed disappointment that the party had not dealt with the Talosian impostors who were violating boar culture.
- The party reached the clearing where Corbin and Shrimpy were waiting; Shrimpy was arguing that the party was dead and that Corbin should release him.
- Brewbarry and Valphine expressed displeasure at Shrimpy's behavior.
- The party retrieved Emerald Enclave fighters from the Falcon Hunter.
- The party traveled south to the edge of the forest west of Conyberry, then west along the Triboar Trail toward Phandalin, covering nearly thirty miles before stopping to rest.
- After resting, the party continued to Phandalin.
- Vukradin called a council meeting in Phandalin; the party reported on the Cult of Talos at the Woodland Manse and the shapeshifting, lightning-wielding orcs.
- Shrimpy was delivered to Town Master Harbin Wester.
- Ser Kaelen expressed concern about the Kraken Society and mentioned suspicions of powerful psionic creatures being involved.
- Jenna Roscoe dismissed the Kraken Society as a harmless social club; Valphine sensed she knew more than she revealed.
- Adabra became furiously enraged upon learning of the party's intervention against the Kraken cult, declared it a temporal violation and planar distortion, cited Emerald Enclave members' cult involvement as proof against interventionism, and stormed out of the meeting.
- Ser Kaelen revealed that Candlekeep research had identified the original owners of a magical item previously recovered from ships, noting the significant cost to the Order of the Gauntlet.
- Valphine delivered a sermon at the partially reconstructed church in Phandalin, inducing religious ecstasy in the congregation.
- Congregants misinterpreted Valphine's message and began staring at the sun until blinded to prove their worthiness.
- Valphine cured the blindness with divine magic, inadvertently creating a new sacrament in which congregants blind themselves and are then healed.
- Valphine placed the severed head of a Kraken Society cultist on a pike outside the church.
- Teega the Smith, a Kraken Society member, observed the church proceedings and appeared disturbed and strangely conflicted.
- The party encountered Don-Jon Raskin in the tavern, where he told tall tales of his past and revealed he was the new overseer for the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine.
- Vukradin noticed that Don-Jon's Neverwinter employer consortium included at least one person associated with the Kraken Society.
- The party escorted Don-Jon toward the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine.
- Six dead orcs were found within five miles of the mine, killed by extreme cold, indicating the white dragon's presence in the area.
- The party confirmed the dead orcs were not Talosians.
- The party arrived at the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine entrance, marked by a carved wooden plank.
- Two wererats in hybrid form confronted the party, claiming ownership of the mine; they reacted with hostility toward Valphine for being a drow.
- The wererats invited the party inside to meet their leadership; Don-Jon's friendliness helped defuse initial tension.
- The party met Zeleen Varnaster, leader of the Whiskered Gang, inside the mine.
- Zeleen explained the Whiskered Gang had been displaced from the Shrine of Savras by approximately sixteen orcs and several ogres, including some from a tribe the party had previously angered.
- Zeleen proposed the party clear the orcs from the Shrine of Savras in exchange for the wererats vacating the mine; she refused to personally participate and offered only two warriors if pressed.
- Valphine attempted to negotiate a cheese-for-labor arrangement; Zeleen found it offensive but admitted interest in cheese from Butterskull Ranch.
- Valphine sensed the wererats were lying about their intentions and would likely attempt to keep the mine regardless of the deal.
- Vukradin persuaded Don-Jon to accept an arrangement where wererats inhabit one section of the mine while working another.
- A deal was struck: the Whiskered Gang would work the mine and live in part of it in exchange for regular cheese shipments from Butterskull Ranch.

## Threads & Mysteries

- The Cult of Talos remains entrenched at the Woodland Manse; the party did not neutralize them and retreated. Their broader goals are unknown.
- The talking boars at the Woodland Manse area are dissatisfied that the Talosian impostors have not been dealt with; the party made no resolution with them.
- Shrimpy's ultimate fate and allegiances beyond his custody by Harbin Wester are unresolved.
- Corbin's full role and loyalties are not established.
- Jenna Roscoe (Lords' Alliance) is suspected by Valphine of knowing more about the Kraken Society than she revealed; her true knowledge and motives are unknown.
- The Kraken Society's actual nature, goals, and leadership remain unknown; Ser Kaelen suspects powerful psionic creatures are involved.
- At least one member of the Neverwinter consortium that hired Don-Jon Raskin is associated with the Kraken Society, raising questions about the consortium's interest in the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine.
- Adabra's claims about temporal violations and planar distortions caused by the party's interventions are unresolved; the nature and truth of her philosophy, and what she knows, remain open questions. The party anticipated they would have to deal with her again.
- Magical items previously found on ships: Candlekeep research has identified original owners, but returning them is described as not straightforward. The items, their owners, and the complications of return are unresolved.
- The white dragon's presence is confirmed in the area near the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine (evidenced by six orcs killed by extreme cold); the dragon has not been confronted.
- The Shrine of Savras is occupied by approximately sixteen orcs and several ogres (some from a tribe previously angered by the party); this threat has been deliberately left unresolved and may need to be addressed in the future.
- Zeleen Varnaster's true intentions are suspected to be deceptive; Valphine sensed the wererats intend to keep the mine regardless of any deal. The long-term consequences of the mine arrangement are explicitly noted as uncertain.
- Teega the Smith's conflicted reaction to Valphine's church activities may indicate a potential shift in allegiance or an internal Kraken Society complication.
- The Emerald Enclave members' involvement in the Kraken cult, cited by Adabra, is not fully explained or resolved.
- The new sacrament created at Valphine's church (intentional blinding and divine healing) may have long-term religious or social consequences in Phandalin.
