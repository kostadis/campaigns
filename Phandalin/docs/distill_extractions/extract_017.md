## World Events

- Late at night, Vukradin left his sleeping companions and infiltrated the Main Lodge of Falcon's property alone.
- Vukradin cast the ritual spell Silence over Falcon's bedroom and the adjacent hallway to prevent detection.
- Vukradin pried open a locked drawer in Falcon's private bedroom.
- Vukradin discovered multiple items in the room and drawer: wooden elven ears on a chain inscribed "my dearly beloved," a carved wooden square depicting a labyrinth maze, and a small iron box shaped as a realistic dragon's head.
- Vukradin wound a key and opened the iron dragon's head box, finding a brass ring inscribed in Elvish with the word for "lightning."
- Vukradin recognised the labyrinth carving as resembling the architectural structure described in the libretto of "The Undercity Chorale."
- Vukradin speculated the brass ring originated from the hoard of the blue dragon Iymrith.
- The following morning at breakfast, Falcon confronted the party about the disturbed drawer, attributing the intrusion to the Cult of Talos.
- Vukradin convincingly feigned ignorance, allowing Falcon to maintain his belief in the Cult of Talos cover story.
- Falcon, feeling cornered by the implied near-theft, confessed the true history of his past to Vukradin.
- Falcon admitted he was the sole survivor of his adventuring party after an encounter with Iymrith, not her slayer.
- Falcon admitted he reached Iymrith's hoard only after it had already been plundered by others, taking only residual gold scraps and the brass ring.
- Falcon admitted to using those items as seed capital to purchase land and construct his lodge, and to maintaining the dragonslayer lie for commercial purposes.
- Vukradin secured permission to document the brass ring rather than taking it.
- The party completed their rest and prepared to proceed with delivering goods.
