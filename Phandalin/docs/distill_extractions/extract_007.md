## Threads & Mysteries

- **The Cursed Lighthouse**: A lighthouse west of Phandalin on the coast is allegedly drawing ships to wreck on the rocks. Ser Kaelen believes a monster is the most likely cause. The party appears to be heading there to investigate. The nature of the curse and any entity responsible are unknown.
- **The Neverwinter Wood Rumours**: Multiple rumours are circulating about strange events in Neverwinter Wood. The nature of these events is unspecified. Soma is drawn to investigate.
- **Adabra's Attitude Toward Valphine**: Adabra conspicuously excluded Valphine from her advocacy and appeared to ignore her existence. Whether this is racial prejudice against Drow or a personal matter is unresolved.
- **Valphine's Corrected Silence**: When Ser Kaelen expressed that Lolth-worshippers "only see the path of death and destruction," Valphine was startled and chose not to clarify what she actually meant by calling them "boring." Her true view of Lolth and her former faith remains ambiguous.
- **Vukradin's Order of the Gauntlet Membership**: Vukradin is now an associate member. The obligations of membership ("when we reach out for help, you must come to us as a brother") have not yet been tested. The full agenda of the Order and what they will ask of Vukradin is unknown.
- **The Order of the Gauntlet's War with Brewbarry's Tribe**: The war is stated to be over, but Brewbarry's grievances (killing of tribe members, theft) are unresolved emotionally and politically. The current status of his tribe is unknown.
- **Soma's Children**: Soma's children are travelling the world independently. Their whereabouts and safety are unknown and a source of concern for Soma.
- **Bandit Activity Around Phandalin**: The Townmaster cited bandits as a reason for his financial constraints. The identity, scale, and goals of these bandits are unknown and unaddressed by the party.
- **Lord Neverember's Plunder Agreement**: The standing agreement allowing adventurers tax-free plunder from monsters was cited without full elaboration. Its broader implications for the region and Vukradin's ethics remain a potential source of conflict.
