## Factions

**Emerald Enclave**
- Current goals: Seal the planar rift in the Whispering Grove; cleanse or destroy sources of planar corruption in the natural world (Interventionist wing)
- Recent actions: Dispatched Lyra and two warriors to the Whispering Grove to perform a cleansing ritual; became trapped by blighted vines before the party's arrival; failed to complete the ritual independently; attempted the sealing ritual with the party's assistance; ritual ultimately failed
- Relationships: Internally divided between Naturalist and Interventionist factions; Interventionist wing previously joined the Council of Waterdeep during the War of the Dragons
- Key members: Lyra (leader of this expedition), Delaan Winterhound (historically the wisest, now absent), two unnamed warriors

**Naturalists (Emerald Enclave internal faction)**
- Current goals: Non-intervention; acceptance of planar phenomena as part of the natural order of the multiverse
- Recent actions: Argued within the Enclave that the corruption in the Whispering Grove is natural and should not be interfered with
- Relationships: In ideological conflict with the Interventionists; their position aligns with Adabra's teachings; their position is described by Lyra as the minority view that opposed joining the War of the Dragons
- Key members: None named beyond general Enclave membership; Adabra implied to hold compatible views

**Interventionists (Emerald Enclave internal faction)**
- Current goals: Protect the Prime Material Plane from planar intrusion and corruption; seal rifts and cleanse infected natural sites
- Recent actions: Organized the expedition to the Whispering Grove; previously joined the War of the Dragons to prevent Tiamat's entry into the Prime Material
- Relationships: Opposed by Naturalists; credited by some with preventing Tiamat's victory; blamed by Naturalists for the subsequent War of the Giants
- Key members: Lyra, Delaan Winterhound (historically), Malfire (allied hero, not a member)

**Unknown Entity (Puppet Master)**
- Current goals: Unknown; actively collecting materials from the corrupted Treant's corpse
- Recent actions: Directed the puppet creature to harvest ingredients from the Treant following the ritual's failure
- Relationships: Unknown relationship to any named faction; appears to have benefited from the ritual's failure and the rift's expansion
- Key members: Unknown; the puppet creature is its instrument

## World Events

- The party arrived at the Whispering Grove to find it corrupted by a planar rift, with iridescent pools, blackened thorny undergrowth, and a corrupted Treant present.
- Several members of the Emerald Enclave, including their leader Lyra, were found trapped by blighted vines.
- Vukradin attempted to Command a blighted vine and failed.
- The corrupted Treant declared ownership of the grove and warned the party to leave.
- Soma attacked the blighted vines in bear form, took damage, and accidentally struck herself before landing a successful hit, freeing Lyra and one other Enclave member.
- Lyra produced four ritual stones and explained that a sealing ritual requiring six successes and tolerating no more than two failures was needed to close the planar rift.
- Vukradin cast Starry Whip against the Treant, connecting successfully.
- Vukradin attempted the ritual and failed (first failure); suffered psychic damage from the planar aura.
- The Treant attacked the second Enclave Warrior, leaving them at one hit point.
- Lyra cast Moonbeam on the Treant and was subsequently re-ensnared by the Treant's vines.
- Brewbarry enlarged himself, missed a vine attack, entered the Treant's madness aura, and successfully resisted its psychic influence.
- Valphine cast Guiding Bolt on the blighted vines (hit), then cast Fairy Fire on the Treant, then fired a crossbow bolt at a vine.
- Soma dropped bear form; attempted and succeeded on the ritual (first success); suffered psychic damage from the aura; cast Moonbeam on the Treant.
- Vukradin healed the second Enclave Warrior back from near death.
- Vukradin succeeded on the ritual with Soma's assistance (second success; tally: two successes, one failure).
- Brewbarry succeeded on the ritual (third success; tally: three successes, one failure).
- Soma failed a ritual attempt (second failure; tally: three successes, two failures).
- Soma and the first Enclave Warrior killed the corrupted Treant.
- Soma used her mentor's staff; it confirmed the Treant, iridescent pools, and blighted undergrowth are all unnatural.
- Soma attempted the ritual and succeeded (fourth success; tally: four successes, two failures).
- Soma attempted the ritual again and failed (third failure; ritual failed entirely).
- All party members and Enclave members suffered searing psychic pain simultaneously.
- The earth trembled unnaturally; the iridescent pools and planar wells expanded and pulsed with increased intensity.
- Lyra expressed that the outcome was not what she had hoped.
- The party began to withdraw from the grove.
- A puppet creature emerged from the planar rift, phased partially out of reality, and collected ingredients from the Treant's corpse in a deliberate, calculated manner.
- The puppet creature retreated back through the planar well into the rift.
- The party departed the Whispering Grove with the planar wells still active and expanding.

## Locations

**The Whispering Grove**
- What it is: A roughly sixty-foot clearing in a dense forest, formerly home to a Treant guardian
- Current state: Actively corrupted by a planar rift; iridescent pools and planar wells present and expanding after ritual failure; blighted vines and blackened undergrowth throughout; Treant dead; planar rift uncontained and growing; described as "a wound in the world"
- What happened here: A planar infection corrupted the grove's Treant guardian, turning it hostile; the Emerald Enclave attempted a cleansing ritual, became trapped, and required the party's assistance; the combined effort to seal the rift failed; the wells expanded; a puppet creature harvested materials from the Treant's corpse and retreated into the rift

**Waterdeep (referenced)**
- What it is: A major city; site of a council convened during the War of the Dragons
- Current state: Not directly visited; referenced historically
- What happened here: A council was formed to coordinate response to Tiamat's summoning; the Emerald Enclave attended; Delaan Winterhound was persuaded by Malfire to join the war effort

## Threads & Mysteries

- The planar rift in the Whispering Grove remains open and is now actively expanding; it is unresolved and potentially spreading corruption to the surrounding forest.
- The identity, nature, and goals of the entity controlling the puppet creature are entirely unknown; its deliberate harvesting of Treant materials suggests a larger, purposeful agenda.
- It is unknown what the puppet creature collected from the Treant's corpse or what those materials will be used for.
- Adabra's position as a naturalist is now in direct ideological conflict with what Soma witnessed and confirmed with her staff; Soma intends to confront Adabra for an explanation.
- The reference to a "motivated blight" — an intentional, puppet-directed corruption — was not accounted for in Adabra's teachings; it is unresolved whether Adabra is ignorant of this, complicit, or simply holds a different boundary definition.
- The broader Naturalist/Interventionist schism within the Emerald Enclave is unresolved and ongoing.
- The causal relationship between the Interventionists' defeat of Tiamat and the subsequent War of the Giants (the shattering of the Ordning) is raised but not settled; it is disputed whether this was a direct consequence.
- Soma's philosophical identity as a druid is unresolved; she has rejected her previous certainty but has not committed to a new position.
- The full history and current status of Delaan Winterhound is unknown.
- The identity and current whereabouts of the hero Malfire are unknown.
- It is unresolved who or what is behind the planar incursions and whether they are coordinated.
- The tormented deer referenced by Vukradin is unresolved; its significance to Soma's decision-making suggests prior unresolved context.
