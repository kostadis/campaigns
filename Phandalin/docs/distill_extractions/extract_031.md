## World Events

- Waves of orcs and ogres attacked the party outside the mine entrance
- Valphine's divine guardians manifested and killed multiple orcs through celestial fire
- Brewbarry used supernatural resonance and glowing red eyes to terrorize enemies before killing them
- A second wave of enemies emerged from around the hill containing the mine
- Soma rolled a sphere of flames into the second wave and fought in beast form
- One orc landed a devastating blow on Brewbarry, who was barely affected due to his rage
- Brewbarry killed the orc, then killed the ogres with thrown axes
- The party searched the fallen enemies and found alchemist's fire, coins, gems, and a symbol of Talos
- The party identified the ogres as former allies of the Carver, whose power had weakened after an incident with Borg the Hammer
- Zeleen Varnaster appeared and claimed Don-jon Raskin had been killed by a carrion crawler
- When pressed, Zeleen admitted Don-jon had been "conveniently lured to his death"
- The party negotiated with Zeleen to receive Don-jon's share of the mine in exchange for dealing with the carrion crawler
- Valphine presented the wererats with Petunia's cheese, which delighted them
- After a rest, Valphine investigated Don-jon's death scene and determined he died in combat with another party, not the carrion crawler
- Dwarf footprints leading away from the scene were discovered, implicating the dwarves in Don-jon's death
- Soma used magic to communicate with the carrion crawler in its lair
- The carrion crawler described feeding on "deep stone folk" (dwarves) and "false rats" (wererats) who had killed each other
- Soma persuaded the carrion crawler to leave the mine and seek better hunting grounds
- The party searched the carrion crawler's lair and found magical goggles granting darkvision and a chain belonging to Don-jon
- The party followed dwarf tracks deeper into the mine and encountered five dwarves led by Horia
- Horia produced Don-jon's contract with the dwarves, asserting their legal claim to work the mine
- Horia revealed Don-jon had told the dwarves he planned to exploit the wererats as cheap labor and discard the dwarves as no longer necessary
- The dwarves killed Don-jon following this revelation
- Horia warned that the wererats' incompetent mining techniques would collapse the operation within months
- The party proposed a compromise: dwarves handle mining, wererats provide labor and security
- Horia agreed to consider the arrangement conditional on a wererat apology for killing dwarves
- Valphine stated that contracts exist only to cheat people, deeply offending Horia
- Horia questioned whether Valphine was truly a cleric of Lathander
- The party began renegotiating the dwarves' original twenty percent share downward
