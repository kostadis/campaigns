## Zeleen Varnaster

Leader of the wererats at the mine. Gives off a gangster vibe and was observed by Vukradin to prefer acting as muscle over hard labor. When the party presented their proposal — wererats act as protection while dwarves do the mining — Zeleen initially demanded 1,000 gold pieces upfront for six months of protection. Brewbarry countered by pointing out that the wererats had been chased from their homes and were now receiving shelter, cheese, and protection, using his still-bloodied axe as an implicit threat. Impressed by both the argument and the implied danger, Zeleen agreed to protect the mine for free, framing it as defending their home and family. He offered Vukradin a piece of Petunia's cheese as a gesture of goodwill, which Vukradin found as delicious as advertised. Zeleen warned the party that orcs, ogres, and a dragon were still causing chaos in the area, that farming had been so disrupted there would be no beer brewed this year, and suggested the Shrine of Savras as a source of information on the dragon. When Vukradin attempted to deceive him about dragons polymorphing into smaller forms to enter caves, Zeleen rolled a natural 20, saw through it entirely, and treated it as a good joke, which strengthened their rapport. He shook hands with Horia Stonetallow to finalize the mine agreement. Last seen at the mine.

## Horia Stonetallow

A proud shield dwarf of the Stonetallow clan, whose lineage traces to a disgruntled engineer from Gauntlgrym who was the only one who knew how to build a proper archway. When Vukradin questioned the apparent oxymoron of "Stonetallow," Horia launched into a detailed etymological explanation: the name derives from "stone to allow," meaning to allow arches to pass, reflecting the clan's foundational obsession with doing things properly. She views the wererats' occupation of the mine as a disaster and sees value in the new arrangement because it allows dwarves to focus entirely on perfecting the art of mining rather than acting as guards. She finalized the agreement with Zeleen by shaking hands and exchanging contracts, though the dwarves regarded the wererats' modifications to the mine with open skepticism. When Vukradin asked about expected weekly earnings, Horia indicated she needed to work out overhead but estimated around 100 gold pieces per week, potentially more with investment. Last seen at the mine.

## The Falcon

Owner of the Falcon's Hunting Lodge, encountered on the Triboar Trail while the party traveled toward Dragonbarrow. He was distraught because the party had never cleared the Woodland Manse — a previous failure involving fireballs and lightning that drove the party away — and because orcs and ogres (later confirmed to be Anchorites of Talos) had since overrun his lodge and were attempting to muscle everyone out of Neverwinter Wood. He was unwilling to return to the lodge himself and was heading to Phandalin to settle debts and plan his next moves. He offered Vukradin a particular wood for his music studio as payment if the party cleared the lodge, and proposed converting the Falcon Lodge into a music venue featuring Vukradin and Valfine with ale, arguing this would raise rents and attract people. Brewbarry enthusiastically endorsed the ale component. The Falcon then departed for Phandalin. Last seen on the Triboar Trail heading toward Phandalin.

## Xanth

A centaur encountered near Dragonbarrow when the party arrived at the burial mound. He approached the party peacefully and warned them about will-o'-wisps haunting the hill at night, describing them as restless spirits of the dead that he avoided by only visiting during daylight. He also informed the party that Neverwinter Wood had been overrun by orcs allied with half-orc spellcasters — later confirmed as the Anchorites of Talos — who were performing dark rites at a circle of standing stones atop a cave-ridden hill, causing farmers to flee the area. He offered to guide the party to the spellcasters after their business at Dragonbarrow was concluded. He recognized Valphine as the famous cleric of Lathander and praised her courage and the idea of pain purifying. By Chapter 32, when the party emerged from Dragonbarrow, Xanth was found standing bloodied outside in the fading light, suggesting he had been attacked or had encountered trouble while waiting. Last seen outside Dragonbarrow, bloodied.

## Boney

A skeletal horse that reanimated from bones and a rotting saddle found in a southern niche within the Dragonbarrow tunnel after Soma approached to investigate. Soma cast Speak with Animals to communicate with it. The horse initially believed Soma to be Lady Alagondar, whom it had been brought back from death to assist on an important quest. The party convinced Boney that Soma was Lady Alagondar reincarnated, which the loyal horse accepted without question. It expressed confusion and sadness about its missing mane and other physical features despite being fully skeletal. It revealed it had been brought to the dungeon to guard something for Lady Alagondar but was killed by will-o'-wisps before it could complete its duty. The horse stated its original name was Barney, but the party persuaded it to embrace the name Boney, which it accepted happily. Boney indicated that the sword was buried "across the way" from where Lady Alagondar rested, pointing north, which proved accurate. He watched with quiet amusement as the party debated who should carry the Dragon Slayer Sword, remarking that irregularity was no longer a problem for him in his partially-alive state. His stats are low at only 13 hit points. He had bonded particularly with Valphine by Chapter 32. Last known location: northern chamber of Dragonbarrow.

## Lady Alagondar

A figure from the past whose horse Boney was brought back to serve. Boney believed it had been guarding her sword and that she was buried "across the way" from where it had been left — which proved to be the northern chamber, where her remains lay near the dragon bones and the Dragon Slayer Sword. After the party defeated the invisible stalker and claimed the sword, Lady Alagondar's ghost materialized in the chamber as a sorrowful specter. She did not speak. She stared with hollow, mournful eyes in the direction of the Ice Spire Mountains — where the White Dragon lives — before fading away. Her apparition appeared to be a warning or a plea related to the dragon. Last seen as a fading ghost in the northern chamber of Dragonbarrow.

## Sister Kaella

Referenced throughout earlier chapters as a contact who was supposed to inform the party about the Carver's situation but had never made contact. She appeared in Chapter 32, stumbling into the clearing outside Dragonbarrow with her cloak gone, her armor slashed, and gasping for breath. She fell to her knees, raised her empty hands toward the Dragon Slayer Sword, and delivered an urgent warning: the Carver was no longer a man but had become a hollow shell, speaking to his blade in a tongue that made ears bleed, and was marching on Icespire Hold that very night to bind the white dragon. If not stopped before completing his dark chant, he would control the dragon like a suit of armor and then burn Phandalin to test his new power. Vukradin, who had long distrusted her as a potential agent of the Carver, moved with quiet deadly intent before she could finish speaking. He paralyzed her with an enchantment, silencing her mid-warning. Soma and Valphine persuaded him not to kill her outright, and the party struck her with non-lethal blows until she fell unconscious. The party then realized with alarm that Sister Kaella was the only one among them who knew the location of Icespire Hold. Last seen unconscious on the ground outside Dragonbarrow.

## Jax

One of the Carver's agents, who emerged from the tree line outside Dragonbarrow in Chapter 32 alongside thugs and a hulking Rot-Tusk Ogre immediately after the party had incapacitated Sister Kaella. Jax smiled coldly and thanked Vukradin for dealing with "that little bit of business" — implying that silencing Sister Kaella served the Carver's interests. He then initiated the ambush: a net was thrown over Vukradin and Valphine was momentarily blinded by dark magic. However, Vukradin seized both Jax and the Rot-Tusk Ogre in a paralyzing hold, leaving them frozen and exposed. Soma in bear form tore into the immobilized Jax, and Valphine's radiant spectral guardians burned through the attackers. Brewbarry delivered crushing blows to the paralyzed Jax. Jax fell under the party's combined assault. Last known status: defeated and killed outside Dragonbarrow.
