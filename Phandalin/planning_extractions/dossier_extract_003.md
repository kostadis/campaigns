## Moesko

Moesko, referred to as the Orc Anchorite, was found seated in a barnacle-encrusted chair at the top of the Tower of Storms, clad in armor made from giant octopus hide. He held an opalescent conch in his lap—an artifact belonging to the undead sea elf Miral. Upon his defeat, a strange hole was discovered in his chest, as his heart had been removed and placed in the lighthouse beacon, where it continued to beat and emit a corrupted green light that caused shipwrecks along the coast. A concealed compartment in his quarters contained a dark talisman etched with the stylized lightning bolt of Talos on one side and an inverted eye with coiling tentacle-like lines on the reverse, which radiated both divine and psionic energy. Miral's ghost later revealed that Moesko was not entirely acting of his own will—shadows moved within him, twisting his devotion to Talos into something colder and more calculating, suggesting a foreign power beyond Talos was behind his actions. Soma delivered the killing blow after Brewbarry reduced him to a single hit point. His heart was subsequently destroyed by Vukradin, ending the lighthouse's corrupted beacon.

## Miral

Miral, identified as a sea elf whose ghost was tied to the Tower of Storms, had been killed by Moesko. The party's quest involved recovering an opalescent conch that belonged to her. Upon receiving it, her spirit manifested and thanked the party, stating her spirit could finally rest. Before vanishing, she delivered a warning: she had felt shadows moving within Moesko's will that twisted his devotion into something foreign and calculating, not the rage of Talos. She warned the party, calling them "surface dweller," that "the true storm gathers not in the sky, but in the depths below Leilon." After delivering this warning, she disappeared, her spirit apparently at rest.

## Adabra

Adabra is a midwife in Phandalin and a member of the Emerald Enclave. She had previously formed a connection with Vukradin, believing he was devoted to music and beauty for their own sake, and on his behalf had secured additional payment from Harbin Wester for the party's work. When Vukradin publicly affiliated with the Order of the Gauntlet by wearing their pin, she stopped communicating with him and began speaking only to Soma. In the town square she approached Soma to ask whether the party had yet visited the Whispering Wood, expressing disappointment when told they had not. When Vukradin requested to speak with her, she obliged but with bemused distance. She explained to him her worldview: that the multiverse is a single balanced thing with no absolute good or evil, that striving to change the world's fundamental nature forces both good and evil to respond in ways that hurt everyone, and that the Order of the Gauntlet exemplifies this harmful interventionism. She confirmed she had helped Vukradin because she thought he cared only about music and beauty, but his choice to join a faction revealed he was instead committed to changing the world. Regarding the Whispering Wood, she believed the blight was not caused by the Order of the Gauntlet but by the actions of unnamed "interventionists" meddling in affairs they should not. She described seeing colors wrong in the trees, leaves too bright or too sickly pale, and a strange magical blight spreading through the grove. She was last seen in the Phandalin town square, having walked away from Vukradin after her explanation.

## Linene Graywind

Linene Graywind, proprietor of the Lionshield Coster in Phandalin, informed Vukradin that the War of the Giants had severely disrupted her ability to provide services. Where she could previously obtain a scroll quickly, she now had to send messages and feelers out with no guarantee of results. No further details about her motivations or current activities were provided.

## Ser Kaelen Thorne

Ser Kaelen Thorne, a knight bearing a scar on his eyebrow and brown eyes, was found at the Stonehill Tavern in Phandalin. He greeted the party warmly upon their return from the Tower of Storms, congratulating them on their victory while admitting he had believed the tower's evil was mostly rumor. When told about Moesko's nature as a shaman of Talos with a heart removed from his chest powering a corrupted beacon, his smile faded into deep worry, and he stated that a typical shaman of Talos could not accomplish such things and that some hidden force must be behind it. When accused by Vukradin of having revealed the party's plans leading to their ambush, he demonstrated by asking the tavern who had known of their mission, and the entire room raised their hands, including an innkeeper who noted a betting pool had been running on whether all four would return and whether the drow would kill the bard. Ser Kaelen informed the party that 10 gold was his winnings from betting that Vukradin's music would protect the group, which he gave to Vukradin. On the subject of the Carver, he described the bandit as unusual because most bandits brag and make themselves findable through divination spells, but the Carver had been hiding from spells and from his own henchmen. Regarding the Whispering Wood, he acknowledged Adabra had been insisting something was wrong but said he had paid little attention because no one had been hurt and the town faced more pressing threats including a dragon, the Carver, and orcs. He offered that the townsfolk would be grateful if the party investigated. He confirmed he would look into the rightful owners of the salvaged items Vukradin listed from the lighthouse wrecks. Soma noted internally that he could not be fully trusted. He was last seen at the Stonehill Tavern in Phandalin.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester, the Phandalin Townmaster, answered his door visibly frazzled and surprised the party had survived. When informed of the Talos infestation at the lighthouse and the party's sanctification of the altar, he expressed relief and indicated he would send a note to Lord Neverwinter informing him of the successful cleansing. When Vukradin pressed about delivering the note personally or visiting Lord Neverwinter, Harbin cautioned that Lord Neverwinter was very busy and that arriving while the town's mission remained incomplete would be unwise, adding with narrow eyes that it was not his place to tell them what to do—a warning the party recognized. He took no immediate action regarding finding a new lighthouse keeper beyond the note. He was last seen at his residence in Phandalin.

## Lyra

Lyra is a woman and apparent leader of a group of Emerald Enclave members found trapped in the corrupted clearing at the center of the Whispering Wood. She and her companions were ensnared by thorny blighted vines that prevented spellcasting and restricted movement. She identified herself by name and shouted to the party for help, explaining that the immense twisted Treant at the center of the clearing had once been the grove's guardian but had been corrupted by a planar infection. She stated that her group had been attempting to cleanse the Treant of this infection when it turned on them, and she urged the party to fight it and prevent the corruption from spreading. She was last seen trapped in the undergrowth at the edge of the corrupted clearing.
