## Jenna Roscoe

Jenna Roscoe appeared at the tavern having sought out Vukradin specifically, arriving armed with a rapier, two daggers, and a hand-held crossbow before disarming herself at the table. She introduced herself as a member of the Lord's Alliance. She had been searching extensively for Vukradin and came to make him an offer to advance his musical career, claiming she could get him performing before Waterdeep and Neverwinter nobility and potentially before Lord Neverember himself.

She was initially confused and troubled by Vukradin's apparent non-exclusive arrangement with the Order of the Gauntlet, having expected him to be a full member rather than a friendly associate. She showed surprise at his stated goal of simply earning fair-trade gold and spreading his music to everyone regardless of faction. When he challenged her philosophy of redistributing treasure taken from monster hoards—arguing that spending adventuring gold stimulates the community and effectively returns it to the people from whom it was stolen—Vukradin refused to compromise his morals and rejected the offer. She was visibly affected by his refusal but admitted admiration for him, and the mask she maintained softened noticeably despite her not changing her position.

She mispronounced Brewbarry's name and exchanged sharp words with him, showing contempt toward him while reserving warmth exclusively for Vukradin.

Later, at the Phandalin council meeting following the party's encounter with the Kraken Society cultists, Jenna dismissed the Kraken Society as merely a harmless social club for wealthy elites, though Valphine sensed she was withholding more than she revealed on the subject.

Her last known whereabouts were Phandalin, where she attended the council meeting.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester, identified as the townmaster and leader of the Privy Council of Phandalin, appeared opportunistically when the party decided to convert Tresendar Manor into the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice. He offered to arrange the paperwork for a private fee of fifty gold pieces, framing the transaction as simply how things were done in the Overbright and not something that needed to involve the full council. The deal was struck and completed.

When Vukradin returned from outside and learned of the transaction, Harbin quickly reframed the payment as a community donation, satisfying Vukradin's scruples.

Harbin had previously commissioned the party to deliver supplies to the Logger's Camp for his brother Tibor Wester. He also accepted the prisoner Shrimpy upon the party's return to Phandalin, taking the orc into custody.

At the subsequent council meeting, Harbin greeted the party warmly and expressed satisfaction with community changes. He warned that the white dragon occupying the mountains and resulting orc raids were threatening the season's ale production. He presented the party with new quests including Dragon Barrow, Axeholm, and the Woodland Manse, noting these were funded through Neverwinter's tax system. He revealed Neverwinter's troubled recent history of catastrophes, corruption, and crime that Lord Neverember was working to address.

His last known whereabouts were Phandalin.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen is a member of the Order of the Gauntlet whose characterization of Vukradin as a full member caused Jenna Roscoe to seek the bard out under false assumptions about his allegiances. He was referenced during Vukradin and Jenna's conversation as the source of her mistaken belief.

At the morning-after council meeting following the Kraken Society encounter, Ser Kaelen expressed concern about the Kraken Society—unlike Jenna, who dismissed it—and voiced suspicions that powerful psionic creatures were involved with the organization. He had previously arranged research at Candlekeep at considerable expense to the Order of the Gauntlet to identify the rightful owners of a magical item the party recovered from ships, hoping this effort would strengthen his relationship with Vukradin. He promised rewards for returning the items while acknowledging the process would not be straightforward.

Later, Ser Kaelen brought the party intelligence that the statue recovered from the lighthouse belonged to Elara Seasong Meliame, a wealthy art collector attending a gala in Neverwinter hosted by her eccentric cousin Lord Cassian Meliame. He warned that House Maragaster—a family with notorious demonic alliances—intended to intercept the statue to embarrass the Meliames. He requested the party escort the statue to Neverwinter and present it to Elara personally, asking them to mention the Order of the Gauntlet's assistance in hopes of securing future donations.

His last known whereabouts were Phandalin.

## Falcon the Hunter

Falcon the Hunter is the proprietor of a fortified hunting lodge in Neverwinter Wood, described as having impressive mutton chops. He welcomed the party on their first visit in exchange for musical entertainment from Vukradin, offering lodging, meals, and access to his wine cellar. During dinner he casually mentioned having built his lodge with his share of a dragon hoard split four ways among his adventuring companions, which provoked Vukradin's fury. He was genuinely confused by the accusation, dismissing a rumored new movement against adventurers using plunder as nonsense meant to disrupt ruling elites. After Vukradin stormed out, Falcon demanded an apology upon his return and accepted the insincere apology graciously.

The following morning, Falcon confronted the party about a disturbed locked drawer in his room, attributing the intrusion to the Cult of Talos rather than suspecting Vukradin. When cornered by this framing, he confessed to Vukradin that he was not in fact a dragonslayer. He had been the only member of his adventuring party to escape Iymrith's wrath, arriving at her hoard only after others had already plundered it. He found residual gold scraps and a brass ring inscribed with the Elvish word for lightning, which he used as seed capital to acquire land and build the lodge. He maintained the dragonslayer story because the fantasy was good for business. This confirmed Vukradin's view of him as a fraud, artist, and thief.

When the party returned to the lodge a second time with six prisoners and rescued companions in tow, Falcon greeted them with surprise and pleasure, impressed by their success. He shared troubling news about his investment in Mountain's Toe Goldmine, which had been poisoned, straining his finances severely. He offered the party a share in the mine if they investigated and resolved the poisoning. He also reiterated the Woodland Manse quest, offering a pair of boots of elvenkind as reward and noting he had purchased the boots legitimately to avoid another confrontation with Vukradin. He suggested the party might need more experience before tackling it, though they ultimately chose to pursue it immediately.

Falcon identified himself as an interventionist who believed in actively stopping planar incursions, contrasting himself with naturalists in the Emerald Enclave. He provided the party with clear directions to the Woodland Manse and supplied wine for Brewbarry as part of the talisman deal.

His last known whereabouts were the Falcon Hunting Lodge in Neverwinter Wood.

## Sridar

Sridar is a drow woman staying as a guest at the Falcon Hunting Lodge who identified herself as a collector of curious artifacts related to planar events. When Valphine asked if she was a cleric of Lathander, Sridar seemed confused and asked if it was an elaborate ruse. She explained her fascination with planar transformations began after a blink in the Underdark when demons appeared, and she had become deeply invested in understanding planar disruptions.

She offered thirty gold pieces for detailed information about planar events the party had witnessed, including a creature collecting strange items. When shown the Talisman of Moesko, she immediately identified it as having a definite planar connection and purchased it for one hundred gold pieces. As part of the deal she also arranged for Falcon to provide three bottles of wine for Brewbarry. The sale pleased Vukradin greatly as an example of fair-trade gold.

Sridar speculated the planar incursions could be caused by magic users, planar entities, or reckless meddlers, though she had originally thought the events were natural phenomena like the Faerzress of the Underdark. She found Brewbarry's suggestion that the events were intentional rather than coincidental intriguing and worth pursuing. She identified herself as an interventionist opposed to the Emerald Enclave's naturalist non-interference stance and offered to help the party piece together clues from future findings.

She departed the Falcon Hunting Lodge during the night before the party set out for the Woodland Manse. Her current whereabouts are unknown.

## Teega the Smith

Teega the Smith was discovered standing rigidly at a riverbank near a dense unnatural fog on the eastern shore, with glassy unfocused eyes. She spoke in an eerie monotone about metal bending, minds being cleansed, and waves seeking uniformity. When questioned about the missing Emerald Enclave patrol, she spoke cryptically of trying to fix a leak with a wooden plug and the current reforming brittle thoughts. She drew a spiral symbol in the mud. Her eyes briefly cleared when Vukradin persuaded her before glazing over again as she wandered into a nearby glade.

During the combat with the Cult Fanatic, Soma tracked her through the wilderness in bear form and subdued her, bringing her back to the group. Her mind control did not break when the Cult Fanatic was killed along with the others.

When examined at the Falcon Hunting Lodge, she was still confused with significant memory gaps, identifying herself as a worker at the Wayside Inn, a tavern with attached forge on the corner of the High Road and Tribor Trail. She explained she had been experiencing blackouts and had been called by something known as the Kraken, which had promised peace after the endless wars and chaos of the Sword Coast.

The party determined her mind had been manipulated through psionic rather than magical means, with her brain rewired and left with gaps susceptible to cult influence. Brewbarry determined this was not the work of Mind Flayers. Valphine attempted to intimidate her about her life choices while Vukradin offered her a gentler alternative—a position as a blacksmith for the Church of Lathander. She declined the immediate offer but expressed interest in returning to the Wayside Inn first.

Later in Phandalin, she was observed watching Valphine's church proceedings and appeared both disturbed and strangely conflicted by the offer of experiencing pain and redemption.

Her last known whereabouts were Phandalin.

## The Cult Fanatic

The Cult Fanatic appeared in a glade beyond the foggy riverbank, described as grim-looking and wielding a black coral dagger, accompanied by three figures with unnaturally precise and robotic movements—the mind-controlled Emerald Enclave patrol. He coldly informed the party they were interfering with necessary adjustments and that his warriors now served a greater purpose. He spoke of a rising current flowing toward Neverwinter and offered the party a chance to submit.

In combat he attempted to control Brewbarry's mind and compel him to flee, while his warriors attacked with devastating precision. He was knocked unconscious by a non-lethal strike from Brewbarry. Even while unconscious, he continued to exert control over his minions, causing them to move robotically toward him. When briefly revived for interrogation, he only muttered vague warnings about tides rising and waves coming, refusing to betray his cause. The party found Kraken Society spiral emblems on him and his warriors.

On the second day of the return journey, a Talosian orc raiding party ambushed the group demanding the Cult Fanatic, identifying him as their enemy. After negotiation, the party handed him over. Given one final chance to explain himself, he spoke only of petty squabbles that meant nothing to what was coming. Valphine executed him and the orcs took his head as proof of their victory. His death broke the mind control over the surviving Emerald Enclave members.

## Lyra

Lyra appeared in Phandalin reunited with the two Emerald Enclave members the party had rescued. Despite this outcome she was deeply discouraged about her leadership abilities, citing both the treant incident and her men's capture as evidence of her failures. Vukradin offered encouragement, and one of the rescued men revealed that the party had credited Lyra's planning as the reason for their rescue. Heartened, she agreed to let Vukradin help convince the Emerald Enclave of her competence, though they needed to wait until the organization's next meeting day.

Her last known whereabouts were Phandalin.

## Adabra

Adabra attended the Phandalin council meeting and became furiously enraged upon learning of the party's intervention against the Kraken cult. She identified what the party witnessed as a temporal violation and planar distortion, insisting the party should never have interfered as such interventions trigger cascading catastrophic events across the multiverse. She cited the Emerald Enclave members' infiltration by the cult as proof that interventionists were fundamentally wrong, then stormed out of the meeting in righteous fury. The party concluded her extreme positions were wrong and recognized this would not be their last confrontation with her.

Her last known whereabouts were Phandalin, though she departed the council meeting abruptly.

## Don-Jon Raskin

Don-Jon Raskin is a verbose and boastful man encountered at the Phandalin tavern who regaled guests with tall tales of his past as a gold prospector, miner, fur trader, privateer, and whaler. He shared a story about attending a barbarian tribe's party believing he had been accepted as a member, though the party recognized he had been perceived as an idiot by his hosts.

Don-Jon revealed he was the new overseer for Mountain's Toe Gold Mine, hired by a Neverwinter consortium that included at least one person Vukradin noticed was associated with the Kraken Society. Despite his bravado he admitted knowing nothing about the mine itself, and his employers required him to hire an escort for the journey. He initially insisted the mine could not serve as both a living space and a working mine for safety reasons, but Vukradin's persuasion convinced him that wererats could nest in one section while working another. He accepted the arrangement the party negotiated with Zeleen Varnaster.

His last known whereabouts were Mountain's Toe Gold Mine.

## Zeleen Varnaster

Zeleen Varnaster is the leader of the Whiskered Gang, a wererat organization that had occupied Mountain's Toe Gold Mine after being displaced from their previous home at the Shrine of Savras by approximately sixteen orcs and several ogres. She explained her group's situation and proposed that if the party cleared the orcs from the shrine, the wererats would return there and vacate the mine. She refused to help reclaim her own home, claiming she was too valuable to risk, and offered only two warriors if pressed.

She reacted with hostility toward Valphine for being a drow. When Valphine attempted to negotiate a cheese-for-labor arrangement she found the suggestion stereotypical and offensive, though she eventually admitted interest in cheese from the special cow at Butterskull Ranch. Valphine sensed Zeleen was lying about her intentions and would likely attempt to keep the mine regardless of any deal made.

Ultimately she accepted a negotiated arrangement: the wererats would work the mine and live in part of it in exchange for regular cheese shipments from Butterskull Ranch.

Her last known whereabouts were Mountain's Toe Gold Mine.

## Qelline Alderleaf

Qelline Alderleaf is a local Phandalin farmer who had offered to let Big Al's cow Petunia graze on her land for one gold piece per week. When the party approached her to negotiate, she revealed deep resentment toward Big Al—during his prosperous days at Butterskull Ranch his competitive pricing had severely damaged her farm's income. She acknowledged the one gold piece fee was deliberately a hurt price meant to make him suffer.

She offered to reduce the fee to five silver pieces per week but only on condition that Big Al came to apologize to her personally. The party's questioning revealed the deeper truth: she and Big Al had once been engaged, but he had abandoned her the night before their wedding.

Her last known whereabouts were her farm outside Phandalin, where she was awaiting Big Al's personal apology.

## Big Al

Big Al is a Phandalin resident who owns Petunia, a prized cow he needed to graze on Qelline Alderleaf's land. He mentioned that Qelline had offered grazing rights for one gold piece per week but that he lacked the funds to pay. Under the party's questioning about his relationship with Qelline, he broke down in tears and confessed that they had once been engaged to be married, but that he had abandoned her the night before their wedding, not being ready to settle down despite loving her. After the party insisted he tell Qelline the full truth, he picked up Petunia and went to her farm to make amends.

His last known whereabouts were Qelline Alderleaf's farm.

## Elara Seasong Meliame

Elara Seasong Meliame is a wealthy art collector identified by Ser Kaelen's Candlekeep contacts as the rightful owner of the statue the party recovered from the lighthouse. She was currently attending an extravagant gala in Neverwinter hosted by her eccentric cousin Lord Cassian Meliame. House Maragaster, a family with notorious demonic alliances, intended to intercept the statue to embarrass the Meliames. The party was asked to escort the statue and present it to her personally while mentioning the Order of the Gauntlet's assistance.

Her current whereabouts are Neverwinter, attending Lord Cassian Meliame's gala.
