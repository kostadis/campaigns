## Xanth

A centaur encountered wounded and bloodied outside the chamber after the ambush by Jax's forces. He was healed by Soma, after which he rose and introduced himself as Xanth (full name Xanthopoulos), noting that most people cannot pronounce his full name. He recognized Vukradin by name, addressing him as "Vucravinios" in Centaurian. He expressed warm gratitude to Soma, saying he could kiss her, and she permitted it.

Xanth provided extensive intelligence about regional events: a white dragon had descended into the Icespire Mountains, displacing orcs into the valleys below and creating the pressure that now threatened settled communities. He named the Carver's three lieutenants — Borg, Sister Kaella, and Jax — and confirmed that word of Borg's death had reached him through the network of animals and living things in the forest. He knew of Meril's Staff, recognizing it immediately as a powerful object that once bore more leaves, and stated that Meril and he were old friends. The staff's presence visibly affected him, and he told Soma that because Meril must know her, he would help her.

Xanth expressed strong opinions about the Emerald Enclave's current dysfunction, describing ideological infighting over whether the orcs' displacement represented a natural event or one requiring intervention, and was relieved to find Soma shared his weariness with this paralysis. He was baffled by Vukradin's reluctance to claim the dragon's hoard, unable to understand the ethics involved, and appealed to Soma as an interpreter of Vukradin's thinking.

He agreed to carry Vukradin and Valphine on his back as far as the foothills near the gold mine, after Vukradin specifically asked him not as a horse but as a centaur. He accepted the distinction with partial satisfaction. Last seen departing the party's company at the foothills, having fulfilled his transport role.

## Sister Kaella

An agent of the Carver who stumbled into the clearing after the ambush, disheveled and injured, with urgent information: the Carver had become something no longer fully human — a hollow shell speaking to his sword in a tongue that made ears bleed. She warned that he was marching on Icespire Hold to bind the white worm, intending to wear the dragon like a suit of armor and burn Phandalin to the ground. She was attacked and knocked unconscious by Vukradin and the party before she could finish delivering this warning.

She was subsequently bound, interrogated by Valphine, and revived with controlled medical pressure rather than healing magic. Upon regaining consciousness, her first statement was relief that the party had survived rather than Jax, explaining that if Jax had won, her death by torture would have been a certainty rather than merely a possibility. She engaged with Valphine directly and with evident recognition, acknowledging their prior arrangement — she had approached Valphine rather than the full party because she had observed that Vukradin struggled with morally gray environments, a characterization that angered him. She confirmed that after the party killed the Carver's muscle and the Carver blamed her for intelligence failures, her survival became contingent on the Carver's death, which aligned her interests with the party's.

She provided directions to Icespire Hold and noted that the Carver was heading there simultaneously. She requested to be sent to Axholm prison as the most convenient option, making a reasonable case for it. She appeared to have been building toward a remark about drow clerics of Lathander and the significance of darkness and light, directed specifically at Valphine, before Vukradin interrupted. She was left in Corbin's custody with instructions to keep her at one hit point. She began screaming immediately upon Corbin's assignment, identifying him as a lunatic and calling out to the departing party. Last known status: alive but captive, in Corbin's keeping.

## Lady Alagondar's Ghost

A spectral figure that appeared after the party secured the Dragon Slayer Sword and completed their short rest in the northern chamber. She looked at the party with sadness and then stared in the direction of the Ice Spire Mountains, indicating the dragon's location without speaking. She did not communicate verbally. Her appearance suggested a connection to the sword and to the dragon's presence. Last seen fading after delivering this wordless direction.

## Jax

Described as the last of the Carver's agents. He led an ambush on the party immediately after Sister Kaella was knocked unconscious, arriving with several thugs and a Rot-Tusk Ogre. He was immobilized by Vukradin's Hold Person spell and subjected to automatic critical hits while paralyzed. He was attacked by Soma in bear form, damaged repeatedly by Valphine's Spirit Guardians, and struck for significant critical damage by Brewbarry. He was defeated by the combined efforts of the party. Killed during the ambush.

## The Carver

A powerful antagonist described by Sister Kaella as no longer fully human — a hollow shell that talks to his sword in a tongue that makes ears bleed. His nature was further clarified by physical evidence encountered on the trail: his footprints ran in a perfectly even, mechanical line directly over rocks and obstacles without deviation, leaving psychic residue that warped stone and caused rocks to phase in and out of reality. The stone giants in the mountain pass noted his passage as "the little one with the obsidian sword" and chose not to intervene, considering him a small-creature concern beneath their attention. The Glacier of Whispers confirmed through psychic impression that he walks without rest, without breath, without soul — a technical description indicating complete hollowing of the original person.

He carries an obsidian sword identified by Brewbarry and Soma as level-draining — capable of taking something that healing cannot restore. He was traveling alone through the mountain pass. He is heading to Icespire Hold to bind the white dragon, intending to possess it and use it to burn Phandalin. Last known position: ahead of the party on the trail to Icespire Hold, moving in a straight undeviating line through the mountains.

## Corbin

A former member of the Carver's organization, now apparently working with or under the party's direction. He was assigned to guard Sister Kaella while the party traveled to Icespire Hold. When asked if he could beat her, Vukradin authorized force up to one hit point. Valphine noted she had been teaching him some of her sacraments, without elaborating on which. Valphine observed in his expression something she categorized as an unresolved history with Kaella — not cruelty exactly, but a specific hunger belonging to someone who had been made small by others and was now presented with an opportunity to be otherwise. The screaming that followed the party's departure confirmed his willingness to act on the authorization. Current status: guarding Sister Kaella at the party's last camp.
