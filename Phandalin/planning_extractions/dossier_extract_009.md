## Cryovain

Cryovain was discovered on the roof of Icespire Hold, chained at the center of a summoning circle, in a state well beyond unconsciousness. Soma scouted him in giant wolf spider form and reported his scales had gone the color of old ice left too long in shadow, his eyes hollowed of alertness, his face subtly wrong in ways she could not entirely articulate — "where's his nose?" she asked, and no one had a satisfying answer. He was not sleeping. He was held, and something had been taken from him by degrees.

Beneath the physical and magical restraint, Soma observed something seething — a rage compressed past the point of intelligence, past waiting, past anything that could be called intention. If the chains came off, he would not think. He would destroy everything within reach.

The chains were load-bearing on the Carver's ritual. Vukradin interrupted that ritual by dropping a Silence spell over the summoning circle, choking the working mid-performance. The Carver — revealed to be a vessel for the black obsidian sword rather than its wielder — responded by abandoning the slow ritual consumption entirely and seizing Cryovain through direct domination instead. The chains dissolved. Not broke: dissolved, as though they had never had confidence in their own existence.

The sword spoke into Vukradin's mind: *Coward's magic. You mute the ritual because you lack the strength to shatter the vessel. And now, now I shall have everything I always wanted.*

The party was given three rounds before whatever remained of Cryovain's mind was gone entirely. Valphine struck first with a bolt of radiant light that briefly scattered the dragon's attention. Brewbarry closed immediately, landing two clean blows with the Dragon Slayer Sword, and felt the impact travel up his arms in the way that means something real has been done. Cryovain responded by breathing a cone of frost directly into Brewbarry's face — cold that pushed through armor and rage alike to find the softer things beneath. Brewbarry did not fall.

Cryovain then beat his wings and lifted thirty feet off the battlements, pulling out of melee reach. Vukradin, from cover behind a stone wall, landed a critical hit with a thorn whip that drew a sound from the dragon suggesting genuine surprise at its own vulnerability.

Last known status: airborne above the Icespire Hold battlements, thirty feet up, dominated by the Carver-sword, with the window for whatever remained of his own mind closing.

## The Carver

The Carver is the sword. This was confirmed during the confrontation at the summoning circle. The man — identified as a veteran — was always a vessel. The black obsidian sword, glowing purple with accumulated power, was the actual entity. The man's legendary footwork — stepping cleanly out of Vukradin's Silence zone without drawing a single attack — demonstrated the nature of what was actually operating the body.

Evidence of the Carver's presence preceded the party throughout their approach. A young drake lay dead in the pass below Icespire Hold, killed without hesitation, in a straight line, with no deviation around the sleeping creature's lair. Vukradin noted the Carver had walked as something walks when it has already decided nothing in its path has standing to matter. The snow around the young drake was black. The eye sockets were hollow. The mother drake circled in grief above.

At the summoning circle, the Carver's ritual was interrupted by Vukradin's Silence spell. Rather than attempting to continue or flee, the sword abandoned the ritual's slow methodology — presumably a more controlled or refined consumption of Cryovain — and achieved its objective through direct domination instead. It spoke into Vukradin's mind unprompted, describing the Silence as cowardice and announcing that it had now obtained everything it always wanted.

The veteran's body was paralyzed by Vukradin's Hold Person spell. While the body was locked, the sword completed the domination of Cryovain. After the domination was achieved, the sword was described as simply black and still — emptied out, having spent its accumulated power to get what it wanted.

The paralyzed veteran's last known status is on the floor of the summoning chamber. The sword's current status is attached to that body, drained, while Cryovain — its obtained instrument — is airborne and three rounds from complete mental erasure.
