## Harbin Wester
Harbin Wester was the Townmaster of Phandalin who had originally tasked the party with securing Axeholm as a potential evacuation site if dragon attacks became imminent. Upon the party's return after clearing the fortress, he was ecstatic and paid them two hundred fifty gold pieces for their efforts. He clarified that Axeholm was intended only as a last-resort backup plan rather than a permanent new home for the town, disappointing some party members. When Brewbarry approached him wearing the dread helm with glowing red eyes, Harbin nervously asked if he had been possessed by a demon. He was subsequently intimidated by Vukradin, who reminded him of his position as head of the Privy Council. Harbin asked the party to deliver cheese to the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine on behalf of Qelline Alderleaf, explaining that no one else was willing to make the journey given the ongoing dragon and orc threats. He emphasized that the orc pressure on farming communities would continue until the dragon threat was resolved.

## Aletra
Aletra is Valphine's sister and the fourth daughter of the Satora family. She was found in the machine room of Axeholm, calmly typing at a glowing keypad beside a massive, malfunctioning planar device while holding a rapier. Valphine identified her not by sight but by the distinctive sickly-sweet scent of lavender perfume and chemical bleach from the Satora family poison labs. Aletra had made a deal with the Dwarven King to install the machine in Axeholm, promising it would improve the mine's efficiency and profit margins to rival the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional merchant operations. Instead, the machine broke and unleashed the spatial anomalies that drove the fortress's inhabitants mad and transformed them into out-of-phase creatures. The Chief Accountant revealed that when she complained to Aletra about the books not balancing, Aletra had laughed at her and likely killed her.

Aletra's true purpose was extracting interdimensional drow poison—specifically Midnight Tears—that she had been smuggling through the multiverse using the machine. She manipulated the party into fighting the Rift Weavers by pointing her wand at Brewbarry to make the machine flag him as a spatial anomaly, drawing all the spiders' attention to him while she worked undisturbed. She spoke to Valphine in Undercommon with characteristic condescension, told her to go play in the mud with the surface dwellers, and referenced past shared experiences including a previous trap involving a stuffed creature as bait and an earlier encounter she described with dark ambiguity. She made sardonic remarks about their mother saying Valphine wasn't the sharpest tool, and acknowledged that Valphine had at least tried to stop her rather than simpering and crying.

After the Rift Weavers were defeated, Aletra successfully extracted a box of Midnight Tears vials and opened a shimmering portal. Valphine struck her with a blinding curse as she stepped through, causing her to fumble and drop several vials. Her final words noted that her mysterious boss would not be happy with the development. Her personal notes in the Operator's Guide contained the phrase "use only if KP isn't watching," suggesting she was operating under some form of oversight. Evidence from her makeshift quarters—a forged ledger crossing out iron shipments and substituting Midnight Tears, and the dropped poison vials—confirmed she was running an illegal drug smuggling operation connected to House Xorlarrin's poison trade. Her last known location was through a portal she stepped into blindly after being struck by Valphine's curse.

## The Chief Accountant
The Chief Accountant was a luminous dwarven figure who had once served Axeholm before being killed by Aletra when she complained about the books not balancing after the machine was installed. She appeared as a glitching, flickering entity with a face of shifting geometric shapes and ink-like shadows leaking from her eyes, her voice a blend of a grandmother's warmth and screeching static. She spoke of ledgers that wouldn't close and regulators dividing by zero.

When she first encountered the party, she called Valphine "sister of the sun" and accused her of carrying the scent of bleach and betrayal. She unleashed a devastating wail that knocked both Valphine and Vukradin unconscious. However, when she saw Valphine fall, she panicked and frantically attempted to stabilize her with blue-white static energy, crying out about acid damage and depreciation and expressing terror that "the auditor" would liquidate her for closing Valphine's account. After this outburst she became incorporeal and fled.

After the party rested, she reemerged and explained the full history of what had happened to Axeholm: Aletra had installed the machine on behalf of a jealous king who coveted the Lionshield Coster's multi-dimensional profits. She offered the party the machine's override shutdown code in exchange for being allowed to leave and for the party not reporting back to Aletra what had happened. The party accepted her terms and followed her directions to find the machine.

When the party later returned and showed her the interplanetary bill for forty-two thousand gold pieces, she screamed for approximately ten minutes about its outrageous incorrectness. She then sighed, declared "The book is closed," and disappeared permanently, apparently at peace now that all accounts were finally settled. Her former quarters contained a secret door hidden in the fireplace that led to a room housing four audit drones and an iron chest containing the dread helm and gauntlets of ogre power that had belonged to her in life.

## Sir Kaelen
Sir Kaelen briefly spoke with the party upon their return to Phandalin, reminding them about returning a statue. Brewbarry persuaded him that the gold mine was a higher priority, citing his need for money to start a music studio. Sir Kaelen's current status and location are in Phandalin, and the matter of the statue remains unresolved.

## Jenna Roscoe
Jenna Roscoe appeared briefly during the party's time in Phandalin after their return from Axeholm. She suggested to Brewbarry that he could easily fund his music studio if he abandoned his principles and sold out. He declined, insisting on doing things his own way.

## Zeleen Varnaster
Zeleen Varnaster was the wererat leader at the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. He was present during the standoff with the ogres and orcs who were demanding the mine in exchange for sparing the wererats. After the party cleared the attackers, he appeared and expressed relief at their timely arrival. However, his account of Don-jon Raskin's death by carrion crawler was immediately suspicious to the party. When pressed directly about whether he killed Don-jon, Zeleen evasively replied that "kill is such a strong word," admitting that Don-jon had been "conveniently lured to his death." The party negotiated to receive Don-jon's share of the mine in exchange for dealing with the carrion crawler. He and the other wererats were delighted when presented with Petunia's cheese. Subsequent investigation by Valphine confirmed that Don-jon had not died by carrion crawler at all but in combat, with the creature only feasting on his corpse afterward—though dwarf footprints at the scene pointed to the true killers being the dwarves rather than Zeleen directly. His current status is at the mine, where a proposed compromise would have the wererats provide menial labor and security while the dwarves handle the actual mining, contingent on the wererats apologizing for killing dwarves in their earlier conflicts.

## Horia
Horia was the leader of a group of five dwarves encountered deep in the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. When the party asserted legal ownership of the mine, she produced a contract showing that Don-jon had made an agreement with her tribe to work the mine and that the dwarves had been working it long before the wererats arrived. She revealed the true circumstances of Don-jon's death: he had told the dwarves he had found "a sucker in some bard fellow" who convinced the wererats to work for mere cheese, and planned to exploit this cheap labor while dismissing the dwarves as no longer necessary. The resulting argument turned deadly. Horia warned the party that the wererats were completely incompetent miners who would cause the operation to collapse within months through improper techniques and safety violations. She was open to a compromise arrangement in which the dwarves handled the mining while the wererats provided menial labor and security, but stipulated that the wererats would need to apologize for killing dwarves in their initial conflicts. She was deeply offended when Valphine stated that contracts existed only to cheat people, and challenged whether she was truly a cleric of Lathander. The party was in the process of renegotiating the dwarves' original twenty percent contract share when the session ended.
