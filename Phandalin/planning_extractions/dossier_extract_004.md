## Lyra

Lyra is the leader of an Emerald Enclave chapter, discovered trapped and ensnared by blighted vines within the corrupted Whispering Grove. Upon being freed by Soma's bear-form assault, she immediately produced four crude stones and announced the need to complete a ritual to seal a planar rift, explaining the mechanics: six successes required, with three failures meaning total failure. She expressed surprise and wonder at encountering Valphine, a drow cleric of Lathander, remarking "A cleric of a drow that is a cleric of Lathander? Can this be?"

During the ritual attempt, Lyra was re-ensnared by the Treant's vines and unleashed a Moonbeam against it. After the ritual failed catastrophically—expanding the planar rift rather than sealing it—she whispered "This did not work the way I had hoped."

When the party returned to Phandalin and confronted Adabra, Lyra arrived simultaneously. She exploded at Adabra, shouting "You are insane!" and later, when Vukradin repeatedly deflected her arguments, she declared furiously, "If you cannot see the evil this woman represents and her fanaticism and its danger, then you are all fools," and departed.

During the extended conversation in the main square, Lyra provided significant exposition on the internal schism within the Emerald Enclave. She explained the philosophical divide between interventionists—those who see planar incursions as corruptions to be fought—and naturalists, who consider even planar energy part of the multiverse's natural order. She traced the schism to the War of the Dragons and the debate over whether to stop Severin's ritual to summon Tiamat, noting that Delaan Winterhound was persuaded by a hero named Malfire to commit the Enclave to the fight. She further argued that Tiamat's defeat inspired the Giant gods to shatter the Ordning, giving the naturalists ammunition for their position. Her own view: "I see what I see here and see an alien force corrupting our world."

Regarding the Treant specifically, she confirmed it was once a good and protective guardian of the forest but had been corrupted by the planar pools, no longer sustained by sunlight.

Her motivations for undertaking the ritual unilaterally were revealed by her agents Thomas and Marian: she had a personal affection for the Whispering Grove, was frustrated by the Enclave's internal paralysis, discovered the ritual herself, and chose to act without bringing it to her superiors—partly out of ambition for recognition and advancement. Thomas summarized: "She didn't do what was best for the Emerald Enclave; she did what was best for her."

Last known status: departed Phandalin in anger after the confrontation with Adabra. Her two agents, Thomas and Marian, remained briefly to share a drink with the party before departing, bound by their oath to the Emerald Enclave.

## Adabra

Adabra is a naturalist philosopher aligned with the Emerald Enclave's anti-interventionist faction, previously established as a trusted mentor figure to Soma. She had set up shop in Phandalin's main square next to Garaele's house, across from the Shrine of Luck.

When Soma confronted her furiously upon returning from the Whispering Grove, Adabra's demeanor shifted immediately from warm to cold and contemptuous. She lectured Soma in a patronizing tone: "I had such hope for you, Soma. I thought you heard the true whisper of the word. But you let the interventionists fill your head with the noise. They speak of cleansing and planar infections because they fear what they cannot control. You help them wound the world to make themselves feel righteous."

She questioned why Lyra was present and whether the party had attempted the ritual. Vukradin successfully confused and deflected her sufficiently to prevent her from concluding that the party had betrayed her cause. When told the Treant was "in stable condition," she accepted this and did not press further. She believed the ritual had failed and the Treant survived.

Her worldview was further articulated during the confrontation: she believes interventionists began a war on nature by rejecting the multiverse's diversity, and that their rituals and cleanses are acts of violence. She framed her outreach to the party as a last resort: "I was forced, forced! To reach out to any ally I could find." She attempted to use a music metaphor with Vukradin, arguing that calling some music bad because it crosses planar boundaries is equivalent to the interventionists' rejection of planar energies.

When asked about the creature that emerged from the rift collecting materials from the Treant's corpse, Adabra reacted with ecstasy rather than alarm: "A symptom of the process, not the cause. It was likely drawn to the energy, a planar magpie. Did it… did you kill it too? Or did you stop to wonder what its purpose was in the grand design?" She was pleased to learn the party had not intervened with it.

She rewarded Vukradin with five gold pieces, described by the narrative as "conflict-free."

Soma's internal assessment: Adabra used her trust to manipulate her into impeding people trying to save the prime material plane, and Soma now views her as someone who took advantage of her inexperience.

Last known status: still in Phandalin, now a member of Harbin's newly formed privy council.

## Thomas

Thomas is one of Lyra's two Emerald Enclave agents who remained in Phandalin after Lyra's angry departure. He joined the party at the Stonehill Tavern at Vukradin's invitation. He confirmed that Lyra's quest had been personal as much as professional—she had particular affection for the Whispering Grove and was frustrated by the Enclave's internal gridlock. He explained that she discovered the ritual on her own and acted unilaterally instead of bringing it to superiors who might have assembled more capable personnel. He acknowledged the failure plainly: "She didn't do what was best for the Emerald Enclave; she did what was best for her." He also confirmed that promotion and recognition were part of what Lyra hoped to gain. When Vukradin offered to speak to her superiors on Lyra's behalf, Thomas warned this would be a bad idea. When asked if he and Marian would join the party, he declined: "Our oath binds us." He departed with Marian after the conversation concluded.

## Marian

Marian is the second of Lyra's Emerald Enclave agents, present at the Stonehill Tavern alongside Thomas. She offered a more sympathetic account of Lyra's motivations than Thomas, describing her as a good leader who had been right in the past and genuinely believed the gods and the forest would protect the ritual party. "She saw everybody in the Emerald Enclave arguing over whether anything could be done or should be done. She said she knew how to make it happen. We trusted her. She's been right in the past. She wasn't this time." She departed with Thomas after declining Vukradin's invitation to join the group.

## Ser Kaelen

Ser Kaelen is affiliated with the Order of the Gauntlet and present in Phandalin. He intervened immediately after Harbin's accusations against Vukradin, declaring that he had insisted on a swift trial to clear Vukradin's name and that the accusation against a member of the Order was "scandalous." He expressed frustration with Harbin's leadership and advocated for a town meeting to address community governance issues.

However, when Vukradin pushed toward removing Harbin entirely and invoking democratic revolution, Ser Kaelen pulled him aside and counseled pragmatism: Lord Neverember appointed Harbin, and removing him would be interpreted as an attack on Neverember's authority, potentially bringing military retaliation. He proposed instead a privy council that would effectively govern while leaving Harbin as a puppet figurehead.

When Vukradin ultimately succeeded in convening a town meeting and a white dragon appeared, Ser Kaelen went pale at Vukradin's use of the word "revolution" and urged him to avoid the term. After the dragon was repelled and Harbin announced the new privy council, Ser Kaelen could see Vukradin was tempted to seize the Townmaster position outright and again counseled restraint, framing the privy council compromise as a noble form of practical idealism.

He had previously sent Vukradin a letter, revealed later in the session by Jenna to be connected to House Margaster—which she described as dealing only in blood money.

Last known status: present in Phandalin, apparently satisfied with the privy council outcome.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin is the Townmaster of Phandalin, appointed by Lord Neverember. He made accusations against the party—implied to involve blood money—which prompted Vukradin's confrontation. When pressed by Vukradin and physically intimidated by Brewbarry stepping close to him, he acknowledged "a tiny little misunderstanding" and proposed a town meeting. He repeatedly delayed convening such a meeting, citing the need for proper agendas, format discussions, and preparation. Under pressure—Vukradin threatening "pitchforks"—he ultimately relented.

After the white dragon was driven off by Vukradin's phantasmal force, Harbin initially tried to blame the situation on the adventurers talking instead of working. Vukradin retorted that killing the dragon would prove Harbin's own incapacity. In the aftermath, Harbin announced the formation of a privy council including himself, Tolbin, Elmer, Halia, Linene, Adabra, and Vukradin—framing it as a response to community need while maintaining his own position and Lord Neverember's authority intact.

He insisted that only Lord Neverember has the authority to replace him, and this was borne out when the privy council structure was accepted as the compromise solution.

## Corbin

Corbin is a former member of Carver's gang now traveling with the party as an aide. At Conyberry, when four brigands from Carver's crew confronted the party, Vukradin dispatched Corbin to parley with his former gang mates. Corbin warned that quitting a gang without consequences was not realistic, but was sent anyway. His fear undermined the attempt, and the bandits drew steel. After combat, with one bandit captured and sleeping, Corbin was left to guard the prisoner Skippy while the party continued to Butterskull Ranch.

During the looting discussion, Corbin asked whether he could take Skippy's armor and whether it would be "conflict-free armor"—a question that annoyed Soma and prompted Vukradin to deny the request on principle. Corbin then proposed staying behind to guard Skippy himself, which the party accepted, though Vukradin warned him explicitly that Skippy had better be alive on their return. The narrative notes that Corbin appeared to want revenge, and Vukradin noticed this.

Later, Valphine brought Corbin along for her nighttime meeting with Sister Kayla, presenting him as her assistant. Sister Kayla killed him during the meeting—her first two rapier strikes missed, but her offhand dagger cut him down—as a demonstration of intent and to arrange a private conversation with Valphine.

Last known status: deceased, killed by Sister Kayla.

## Skippy

Skippy is a bandit in Carver's employ, captured after the skirmish at Conyberry when Vukradin put him to sleep. Under interrogation, terrified by Brewbarry's size, Vukradin's presence, and the blood on Soma's claws, he revealed that the Butterskull Ranch attack was a deliberate trap designed to generate a quest that would lure adventurers into Borg the Hammer's ambush. He confirmed that the Carver anticipated the party would respond to Harbin's quest board. He also revealed that Carver's spymaster and intelligence network within Phandalin is run by Sister Kayla. He was left in Corbin's custody while the party proceeded to the ranch.

Last known status: unknown. Left with Corbin, whose desire for revenge was noted by both Soma and Vukradin.

## Borg the Hammer

Borg the Hammer is a half-orc enforcer working for the Carver, described as leading the ambush at Butterskull Ranch. He was accompanied by three Rot-Tusk Ogres bearing the insignia of the tribe previously encountered by the party. He had captured Big Al Kalazorn and kept him alive as bait, intending to lure the adventurers into a trap.

When the party bypassed his ambush entirely and freed Big Al without confrontation, Borg emerged to find his trap had failed. He located the fleeing fourth bandit from Conyberry and executed him on the spot for incompetence. In his rage at being denied his intended bloodshed, he set fire to the farmhouse, cornfields, and gardens—described as wanton, gratuitous destruction rather than tactical necessity. He then split his forces, sending the three Rot-Tusk Ogres in one direction while he departed in another, with the party electing to follow Borg.

His death was confirmed later: Valphine presented his severed head to Sister Kayla as proof of the kill, which Locutus claimed credit for before being cut down by Kayla.

Last known status: deceased.

## Big Al Kalazorn

Big Al Kalazorn, full name Alphonse Kalazorn, is a retired veteran and former Sheriff of Triboar—predecessor to Darathra in that role. He is the owner of Butterskull Ranch, five miles east of Conyberry on the Triboar Trail, and the subject of Harbin's posted quest. Vukradin found him in the ranch's cellar, tied to a chair with a burlap sack over his head, surrounded by shelves of butter skulls.

He confirmed that orcs first raided the ranch, after which the Rot-Tusk Ogres under Borg arrived, killed the orcs, and seized the property—keeping Big Al alive as bait for the party. After being freed, he refused to leave without his prized cow, Petunia, and offered a suit of mithril chainmail in exchange for her recovery. He confirmed the chainmail was legitimately purchased with his own funds as a retired veteran. The party accepted the trade and located Petunia using Soma's Speak with Animals.

Last known status: presumably evacuated safely to Corbin's camp with Petunia while the party tracked Borg.

## Sister Kayla

Sister Kayla is Carver's spymaster and the operator of his intelligence network within Phandalin, revealed by Skippy under interrogation. Valphine met her at an abandoned building on the edge of town during a nighttime clandestine meeting arranged through Locutus.

Upon Valphine and Locutus's arrival, Sister Kayla immediately killed Locutus—her first two rapier strikes missed before her offhand dagger finished him—as a demonstration of capability and to arrange a private conversation. She then addressed Valphine by name, revealing she already knew her identity despite Valphine's attempt to disguise herself as a generic Underdark drow.

She explained her situation: the plot to frame the party for stealing merchant goods had depended on Borg killing Big Al and leaving no witnesses. Borg's failure destroyed the scheme. She is now in danger from the Carver, who she believes will hold her accountable for the plan's collapse. Her proposal: she will provide Valphine with information to eliminate the Carver's remaining lieutenants, which will force the Carver—who communicates with his people telepathically, method unknown—out of hiding. She intends to kill the Carver herself once exposed, but made clear "by I, I mean you" when speaking of the actual killings.

She assessed the party's members individually: Vukradin as too naive, Brewbarry as in Vukradin's pocket, Soma as too nature-focused. She selected Valphine as the only party member she could deal with, assuming—based on racial stereotyping—that Valphine's apparent devotion to Lathander was a cover and that she was truly a cleric of Lolth running the others as "dupes" or "assistants."

She provided a bag of gold—the payment Locutus was to have received for killing Borg—as a down payment on their arrangement, then disappeared before Valphine could respond.

Her physical description: cold grey eyes, short-cut hair, a scar near her neck, armor under her cloak, carrying rapier and dagger.

Last known status: disappeared into the night after the meeting. Has not yet received Valphine's answer.

## Jenna

Jenna is a woman in her late thirties observed by Vukradin during his impromptu town meeting on the green. She did not react to the dragon with fear—she smirked at Vukradin. Spiderman identified her to Brewbarry as being from Neverwinter and carrying the Lord's Alliance insignia.

At the Stonehill Tavern that evening, she approached Brewbarry rather than Vukradin directly, seeking an introduction. She complimented Vukradin's music and expressed that it deserved a wider audience. When Brewbarry questioned her motives, she described her role as maintaining law, order, and the preservation of society against chaos. A moment of contempt slipped through when she paused before the word "Uthgartian," drawing Brewbarry's fury before she recovered.

She revealed she was the one who sent the letter Vukradin recently received, apparently from or on behalf of the Order of the Gauntlet. Her stated purpose in approaching: she believes Vukradin's commitment to conflict-free treasure means he should be warned that House Margaster—associated with the Order of the Gauntlet letter—deals only in blood money. She framed this as being in Vukradin's interest.

She requested an introduction to Vukradin through Brewbarry, positioning herself as an ally warning him away from corrupt entanglements. Brewbarry's response was to demand she hand over her weapons.

Last known status: at the Stonehill Tavern, facing Brewbarry's demand. The session ended without resolution of this confrontation.

## Linene Graywind

Linene Graywind is the 35-year-old manager of the Lionshield Coster in Phandalin. When the party brought pelts from the corrupted grove to sell, she offered more than twice Barthen's price and asked detailed questions about where and how the goods were found—questions Vukradin answered honestly, to Valphine's frustration. She mentioned that her superiors would be very interested in the planar effects described. She also asked whether the party would perform that evening and specifically requested Valphine's participation, noting she felt Valphine made the ensemble shine. This annoyed Vukradin. She subsequently appeared at the town meeting on the green and was named to Harbin's new privy council.
