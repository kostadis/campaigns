## Toblen Stonehill

Toblen Stonehill is the proprietor of the Stonehill Inn in Phandalin, a modest two-story roadhouse at the center of town. He is a short, friendly male human, originally from Triboar to the east, who came to Phandalin to prospect but discovered he was far better suited to innkeeping. He allowed Vukardin to pay for meals with music, stipulating it had to be something popular from Neverwinter or Waterdeep. After Vukardin played several such songs, Toblen approached him and asked him to play something of his own choosing, then rewarded him with a complimentary beer. He quietly warned Vukardin about rumors of a white dragon in the area. On the party's return, Toblen again persuaded Vukardin to perform for his clientele.

## Harbin Wester

Harbin Wester is the Townmaster of Phandalin, operating out of the townmaster's hall, a sturdy stone building with a pitched wooden roof and a bell tower. He is notably fearful, shouting from behind his locked door that any dragon knocking should know he is too thin and bony to make a good meal. He posted three quests on the job board outside his hall: the Dwarven Excavation Quest (50 gp to warn dwarf prospectors of the white dragon), the Gnomengard Quest (50 gp for a useful magical device to fight the dragon), and the Umbrage Hill Quest (25 gp to convince Adabra Gwynn to return to town). He paid the party for completing the Dwarven quest, though he was visibly upset by Vukardin's accusations that the adventuring system amounted to state-sanctioned plunder. He defended Lord Neverember's policy of tax-free no-questions-asked plunder for adventurers.

## Dazlyn Grayshard

Dazlyn Grayshard is a shield dwarf prospector and business partner of Norbus Ironrune. He and Norbus discovered ancient dwarven ruins in a canyon southwest of Phandalin and spent several months clearing rubble to excavate what proved to be an old temple of Abbathor, the evil dwarven god of greed. He explained the temple's history — that Abbathor caused an earthquake and avalanche after finding the priests' sacrifices of blood and gemstones lacking. When the party arrived with the dragon warning, Dazlyn offered them sending stones as payment to clear something frightening deeper in the temple, which turned out to be orcs. He and Norbus were delighted when the orcs were killed and celebrated by cooking a fine meal for the party.

## Norbus Ironrune

Norbus Ironrune is Dazlyn Grayshard's shield dwarf business partner and fellow prospector. He appeared alongside Dazlyn at the excavation site and corroborated the offer of sending stones as payment for clearing the temple's deeper danger. When Vukardin pressed whether they intended to return any finds to their rightful owners, Norbus assured him they would. He and Dazlyn were visibly pleased at the orcs' deaths and cooked a celebratory meal for the party. They also seemed to enjoy Vukardin's original musical compositions, though they would have preferred more popular material.

## Linene Graywind

Linene Graywind is the master of the Lionshield Coster outpost in Phandalin. She purchased the broken axes and hide armor the party looted from the orc bodies. She confirmed that the Lionshield Coster's preferred mode of goods transportation had been disrupted, acknowledging that tariffs had affected their trade and that they were now relying on conventional suppliers like the rest of the market. She shared a rumor that strange magic pervades Neverwinter Wood, confounding navigators and obscuring ancient ruins, hinting this may be connected to what has disrupted the Coster's fabled internal supply network. She assured the party the Coster could still supply adventuring needs despite the disruption.

## Adabra Gwynn

Adabra Gwynn is the local midwife of Phandalin and an acolyte of Chauntea. She lives alone in a stone windmill on the side of a hill a few miles south of Phandalin. The Townmaster posted a quest offering 25 gp to persuade her to return to town given the increasing danger of dragon sightings in the area. She did not appear directly in this session.

## Pog

Pog is a male gnome guard stationed at one of the cave entrances to Gnomengarde. He challenged the party in Gnomish upon their arrival, demanding to know if they were shapechangers. He conferred with Ulla before allowing the party to pass, ultimately accepting Brewbarry as not a shapechanger on the grounds that he used to be an Uthgardt barbarian rather than currently being one, which placed him in an administrative grey area regarding the standing attack order against Uthgardtians. After Valphine accused him of being a shapeshifter, he turned on Ulla, noting she had looked different lately.

## Ulla

Ulla is a female gnome guard at Gnomengarde who served as the primary spokesperson when the party arrived. She challenged the party over their identities and debated with Pog whether to let them pass, ultimately deciding Brewbarry was acceptable due to the lapsed or unclear standing order against Uthgardt barbarians. She directed Vukardin toward the inventors' workshop via the northern hallway when he reported the spinning blade trap. When Valphine accused her of being a shapeshifter, she immediately turned on Pog, noting he had looked a little different in recent days.

## Facktore

Facktore is a deranged gnome inventor residing within Gnomengarde who tested a rotating crossbow platform of his own design on the party when they passed through a large room on their route to the inventors' workshop. Vukardin chose to disable the device rather than kill Facktore, who fled the scene during the encounter. His current whereabouts within the cave system are unknown.

## Fibblestib

Fibblestib is one of Gnomengarde's foremost inventors, working alongside Dabbledob. He is focused entirely on finding a cure for King Korboz's madness and is developing a sanity ray for this purpose. He argued vigorously with Dabbledob over the merits of his ray versus her proposed straitjacket approach. He dismissed the party's offer of clerical healing as irrelevant, insisting gnomes invent their way out of illness. He briefed the party on the disappearance of two gnomes, Warren and Orrin, and King Korboz's claim that a shapechanger was responsible. He acknowledged that music had helped in some circumstances when Vukardin proposed using it on the king. He also laughed when Vukardin described the rotating blade trap, claiming it was his invention and that the party's survival proved it superior to Dabbledob's designs.

## Dabbledob

Dabbledob is Gnomengarde's other foremost inventor and Fibblestib's coworker and rival. She favors building a straitjacket to restrain King Korboz rather than Fibblestib's sanity ray, though Fibblestib mocked this as merely a shirt with no holes for the hands. She dismissed the sanity ray as having no evidentiary basis. She rolled her eyes at Fibblestib's claim of superiority and was visibly unimpressed by his self-congratulation. She confirmed to the party that music had sometimes helped in treating Korboz's condition.

## King Korboz

King Korboz is one of the two co-kings of Gnomengarde and is currently suffering from severe madness, screaming and in a state of hysterics when the party reached his chamber. He claimed that the disappearances of gnomes Warren and Orrin were caused by a shapeshifting creature and became convinced upon hearing Vukardin announce himself that the bard was the shapeshifter. His madness is the central crisis consuming the gnomish inventors' attention. He is confined to the royal chamber with his co-king Gnercli.

## King Gnercli

King Gnercli is Korboz's co-king and the more lucid of the two, speaking for both of them when the party arrived at the throne room. He confirmed the shapechanger threat and offered the party a Hat of Wizardry in exchange for locating and eliminating the creature, suggesting it might be hiding in the cellar among the barrels due to its non-bipedal form. He acknowledged he had little else of value to offer. He identified the party as adventurers despite Vukardin's insistence on calling the mission diplomatic.
