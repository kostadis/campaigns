## Soma
Soma participated in the battle against the mimic, where her puff of poison failed to affect the creature. She expressed frustration, muttering that the mimic was an "unnatural magical creation that has no place in the material world." When Vukradin asked if she was a naturalist druid, she confirmed this mid-battle, explaining that constructs and extra-planar creatures are abominations and disruptions to the natural order. She was unfamiliar with the terms "Aasimar" and "Tiefling," genuinely confusing Vukradin, and pushed back on his poetic framing by stating Aasimar are simply creatures born into the world with no celestial touching.

After the battle, Soma looted the room for gold, dismissing Vukradin's moral objections by stating she was "not opposed to some gold of questionable provenance." She accompanied the group to deliver proof of the mimic's death to inventors Fiddlestib and Dabblebob, but held her tongue when they discussed making a construct from the creature's remains, internally noting this was how abominations came to be.

During the mission to save Adabra, Soma transformed into a spider using her wildshape ability, climbing and biting a bandit, killing him with poison. She attempted to call off the pursuit of a fleeing bandit, but was in spider form and could only produce spider sounds. She later picked up a scroll containing a druidic spell from the bandit camp but, after Vukradin pointed out it should be returned to its rightful owner, conceded he had a point and questioned whether keeping it would make her no better than a bandit.

At Umbrage Hill, Soma cast a conjured familiar into the manticore's mouth as a distraction, which enraged rather than satisfied the creature. She encouraged Vukradin to attempt his spell a second time on the manticore, citing probability, but when it failed again Vukradin blamed her for his misplaced trust, visibly hurting her. After the manticore was killed, she attempted to soothe Adabra and encouraged the woman to flee while the creature was distracted.

When Adabra asked Soma about her position in the "great schism" of the Emerald Enclave, Soma identified as a naturalist. This triggered Adabra's full trust and engagement. Adabra then schooled Soma on gaps in her druidic education, including the manticore's preference for human flesh, which deflated Soma. Soma reflected privately that her training under Meril had been only one year and was incomplete, and that she had little time left.

At the Stonehill Inn, Soma heard a rumor about the Neverwinter Wood that reminded her of home. She listened to Corbin's full backstory of loss at the hands of the Uthgardt Black Lion tribe, his survival, and his slide into banditry. She reflected that his journey paralleled her own turn when she met Meril.

On the road back from Umbrage Hill, Soma listened to Adabra's naturalist philosophy about the Emerald Enclave schism and the dangers of drawing lines between civilized and uncivilized. She agreed with Adabra's worldview and helped mediate the tension between Adabra and Brewbarry over the word "barbarian."

Soma was approached by Adabra privately outside Barthen's Provisions and told about a withering grove past Whisper Woods where trees twist unnaturally, colors bleed wrong, and creatures have too many eyes and jerky movements. Adabra connected this to interventionist activity in the Emerald Enclave. Soma agreed to investigate.

Soma's tab with Vukradin continued to grow. She expressed contentment with simple things, telling the group that 25 gold was plenty and that she only wanted a new teapot. She privately noted she was aging and had little time left, and that she followed Vukradin partly at Meril's prompting.

Last known status: traveling with the party toward the Tower of Storms along the Triboar Trail and high road.

## Vukradin
Vukradin struggled during the mimic battle, with his deterrent spell failing twice as the mimic appeared to adapt to his magic. He initiated a theological debate with Soma mid-combat about naturalist druidism, Aasimar, and Tieflings, becoming indignant when Soma dismissed his "celestial touching" language as mere poetic license. He declared himself a poet and insisted on his right to take poetic license.

After the battle, he questioned Soma's looting of gnome gold, contrasting it with his own principled stance, and claimed to be rich because he owned sending stones. He accompanied the group to deliver proof of the kill to inventors Fiddlestib and Dabblebob, then led the group to receive payment from the two gnome kings, Korboz and Gnercli.

In the kings' throne room, Vukradin systematically dismantled the dual monarchy by pressing questions about seniority and decision-making authority, successfully driving a wedge between Korboz and Gnercli that Valphine observed would keep them at each other's throats for months. He collected the magical hat reward and departed.

Back in Phandalin, Vukradin performed at the Stonehill Inn and learned from a patron about a legendary performer named Bob who played a demon character in a theatrical production in Triboar. Vukradin confirmed he had heard of this play and had been seeking the script for years, proposing a possible revival.

When negotiating the reward for saving Adabra, Vukradin initially pushed for more than 25 gold, citing the need to fund his studio, but was overruled by the group. He traveled to Umbrage Hill, where during the manticore fight he attempted a command spell twice without success and became openly annoyed at Soma for encouraging the second attempt. After the fight, he cut off the manticore's head and brought it to Adabra as proof of the kill, then attempted to persuade her to come to Phandalin, using music as a persuasive tool. His performance moved Brewbarry and delighted Adabra, though it did not initially change her decision.

He read Adabra's note to Townmaster Harbin aloud and used Adabra's advocacy—once she learned Soma was a naturalist and came willingly—to negotiate increased payment from Harbin. He received free room and board at the Stonehill Inn in lieu of additional coin.

At the inn, Vukradin performed again and collaborated musically with Valphine for the first time, initially resistant but ultimately acknowledging their musical partnership showed promise.

He met Daran Edermath, known as Silverleaf, who pitched the Tower of Storms and its shipwrecks as treasure-hunting opportunities. Vukradin rejected this framing on ethical grounds, insisting treasure from the dead was theft.

He was recruited by Ser Kaelen Thorn into the Order of the Gauntlet as an associate member, attracted by the promise of reaching tens of thousands with his music and story and by the assurance that funding was conflict-free donation gold. He accepted a pin as a symbol of membership. He was told that as a member he must come to the Order's aid as a brother when called, and vice versa. He later confirmed his membership with Ser Kaelen after helping Elara and Jarek.

Vukradin insisted the party escort Elara and Jarek back to Phandalin after rescuing them from the orc attack, framing the reward as the ideal type of conflict-free gold. He commissioned a piece of music for the Order of the Gauntlet, heard their chant, and declared the chant embarrassing and in need of his improvement.

He asked Ser Kaelen about lifting the curse on the Tower of Storms after the Townmaster confirmed Lord Neverember would want the curse removed rather than the tower destroyed. Ser Kaelen suggested the cause was likely a monster haunting the lighthouse.

On the trail to the Tower of Storms, Vukradin noted the unnatural gullies in the terrain could not be formed by natural river flow given the dryness of the region.

Last known status: traveling toward the Tower of Storms after departing Phandalin, having joined the Order of the Gauntlet.

## Valphine
Valphine observed Vukradin's manipulation of Kings Korboz and Gnercli, amused by the spectacle of their dual monarchy being destabilized. She inserted herself to note they needed a "clear approver structure," drawing on her experience watching communes collapse into betrayal and politics. She watched with satisfaction as Vukradin drove a wedge between the two kings.

During the bandit ambush on the road to Umbrage Hill, Valphine sensed something in the shadows but dismissed it, noting internally she was not in the Dark Realms. She was struck by a bandit's ranged attack. After the fight, she shot a fleeing bandit in the back with her hand crossbow, killing him.

At Umbrage Hill during the manticore encounter, Valphine fired her crossbow at the manticore twice and missed both times, expressing frustration with her weapon. She offered to teach Adabra about Lathander unprompted. After Brewbarry killed the manticore, she reflected briefly on the kill.

At the Stonehill Inn, Valphine told Vukradin about Toblen "Spiderman" Stonehill's childhood encounter with hatching spiders, noting privately that in her former life such a person might have been recruited, killed, or tortured by her house. She proposed joining Vukradin musically and performed her composition *Choralum Lux Maleficarum*, a fusion of Lathanderial Choral music and Prayers to Lolth, which balanced Vukradin's complex music and won over the crowd. After the performance, Vukradin acknowledged she should play with him regularly.

When Ser Kaelen Thorn arrived, Valphine unsettled him by describing Lathander as "deliciously painful" and calling single-minded devotion to destruction "dull." Ser Kaelen declined to recruit her into the Order of the Gauntlet, and she did not press the matter.

Valphine was annoyed that Adabra ignored her entirely during the advocacy to Townmaster Harbin, reflecting she would have killed the woman in her former life but had moved past that world.

During the journey toward the Tower of Storms, Valphine identified the unnatural gullies as potentially magical in origin, drawing on her Underdark experience with the Faerzess and reflecting that Lathander's abundance makes overbrighters sloppy and enables war and suffering. She felt a pleasant shiver and privately thanked Vukradin for allowing her to see more of the divine plan. She also mused about founding her own cult of Lathander focused on the aspect of pain.

At the ambush site near the Tower of Storms approach, Valphine's drow instincts recognized the ambush setup before it was sprung. She noted the orcs displayed unusual discipline and organization, identified the presence of an orc shaman or cleric, and recognized the tactical sophistication of their plan—a blocked exit, elevated attack positions, and a killing ground. She described this as adding "enjoyable complexity" to the confrontation.

Last known status: traveling with the party toward the Tower of Storms, having reached the high road after the Triboar Trail.

## Brewbarry
Brewbarry struggled in the mimic battle, unable to free his halberd. He was reminded by Vukradin that he had no ranged weapons, thumped his own head in frustration, and then produced a pair of handaxes. During the bandit ambush near Umbrage Hill, he decapitated one bandit with his halberd, leaving himself covered in blood. He then sprinted after a fleeing spider-phobic bandit but stopped when the man's fear triggered an unexpected moment of empathy.

Brewbarry dropped his halberd and apologized to the bandit, Corbin, for the harm the Uthgardt had caused, saying "They were mean to me, too." This was the act that led Corbin to break down, confess his victimhood at Uthgardt hands, and ask to join the group.

During the manticore fight, Brewbarry threw handaxes that struck the manticore's wings and then engaged it in close combat on the second floor of the windmill, striking it twice and absorbing its claw attacks through barbarian rage. He delivered the killing blow on the manticore and was loudly joyful about it.

When Ser Kaelen Thorn arrived, Brewbarry recognized the Order of the Gauntlet's emblem and told the group the Order had fought against his tribe, calling them bullies who stole. Ser Kaelen acknowledged the war was brutal and characterized it as a conflict where both sides killed innocents. He offered Brewbarry a drink, which Brewbarry accepted, de-escalating the tension.

Adabra refused to use the word "barbarian" around Brewbarry, insisting he was a nomad and not uncivilized, delivering a passionate speech about how drawing lines between civilized and uncivilized enables destruction. Brewbarry remained uncomfortable, insisting he was a barbarian, but Adabra overruled him.

During the approach to the Tower of Storms, Brewbarry spotted the hidden ambushers in the gully by detecting subtle tracks and the glint of concealed armor, confirming Valphine's instinctive suspicion of danger.

Last known status: traveling with the party toward the Tower of Storms.

She then became absorbed in arguing with Dabblebob over whether a tamper-proof voting box was possible.

## King Gnercli

He became visibly annoyed at Vukradin's persistent questioning about government, eventually asking where the bard had learned so much about governance. After the debate inflamed Korboz's demands for seniority, Gnercli handed over the promised magical hat and told the party to leave.

Last known status: in conflict with Korboz, in the gnome settlement.

## Corbin
Corbin is a gaunt man in his late twenties with hollow eyes, matted dirt-brown hair, a patchy beard, and pale blue eyes that shift between distant and feral. He wears ill-fitting leather armor scavenged from a dead man. He was a bandit working under a figure named Jax, who in turn worked for a boss called the Carver. He had never met the Carver directly.

Corbin was the fleeing bandit who triggered Brewbarry's unexpected show of compassion. He was persuaded to join the party as a camp attendant after Vukradin invited him to dinner and questioned him about his criminal connections. He confessed he did not want to be a bandit, blamed his circumstances on the Carver, and asked to join the group.

He admitted to Vukradin that his leather armor had been taken from a dead man. When Vukradin ordered him to remove it, Corbin appealed to Brewbarry, who also wears none. Vukradin ultimately relented and promised to get him fair trade armor.

In spider-form, Soma startled Corbin when she waved at him, causing him to panic upon learning the spider was a tortle.

Corbin later arrived at Umbrage Hill looking worse than before and smelling. During Adabra's meal, he shared his full backstory with Soma: a farmer from Greenmeadow in the plains east of the High Forest, married to Elara, with a daughter named Lily. The Uthgardt Black Lion tribe destroyed his village during the chaos following the breaking of the Ordning. He survived by being knocked unconscious and buried under a neighbor's body. He drifted south, surviving through petty theft, until bandits recruited him under threat of death.

During the manticore encounter, the manticore spotted Corbin and declared it would take him and leave the rest of the party alive, citing a preference for human meat. He survived because the party killed the manticore before it could reach him.

Last known status: traveling with the party as camp attendant, en route to the Tower of Storms.

## Adabra
Adabra is a past-middle-age woman of weathered appearance living at Umbrage Hill in a windmill she legally owns with a deed, adjacent to old dwarven ruins whose cairns she tends. She has calloused hands, a grim expression, and piercing eyes that suggest fanatical conviction and ruthless determination. She is a member of the naturalist faction of the Emerald Enclave and a potion-maker whose specialty is healing potions with added scent and taste.

She had been held under siege by the manticore for only a short time before the party arrived. After the manticore was killed, she refused to leave her windmill, insisting she must stay to craft potions and that the natural order required people to come to her. When Vukradin played music she was delighted and praised Aasimar as truly touched by the Celestial planes. She gave the party a note for Townmaster Harbin requesting they be rewarded, along with instructions for a medicine she was preparing for Harbin.

She hosted the party for dinner, making hearty bread, vegetable and herb stew with local fungi and wild greens, and was delighted to have overnight guests. She corrected Soma's druidic education, informing her that manticores have a genuine preference for human flesh above other humanoids. She chastised Vukradin for assuming she had stolen the dwarven ruins and explained that she held a legal deed.

Her decision to accompany the party to Phandalin the next morning came only when she learned they were naturalists who shared her opposition to the interventionist faction of the Emerald Enclave's practice of slaughtering monsters for treasure. She gave a passionate speech to Townmaster Harbin on Vukradin's behalf, advocating for increased payment on the grounds that principled champions were an investment in the moral fabric of Phandalin's defense. Her advocacy notably excluded Valphine.

On the road back to Phandalin, she delivered a naturalist philosophy lecture connecting Emerald Enclave factionalism to the broader problem of drawing civilizational lines that enable destruction. She insisted Brewbarry was a nomad, not a barbarian, and refused to permit the word "barbarian" in her presence.

Outside Barthen's Provisions, Adabra privately told Soma about a withering grove past Whisper Woods: trees twisting in impossible shapes, wrong colors, creatures with too many eyes and jerky movements. She connected the phenomenon to interventionist meddling and asked Soma to investigate.

Last known status: returned to Umbrage Hill after accompanying the party to Phandalin.

## Daran Edermath (Silverleaf)
Daran Edermath, known as Silverleaf, is a tall, lean half-elf with silver-streaked dark hair, a salt-and-pepper beard, sharp emerald-green eyes, and a weathered, noble appearance suggesting years in the wilds and in battle. He wears simple earth-toned clothing suited to tending his orchard and wandering. He is a former adventurer.

At the Stonehill Inn, Silverleaf pitched Vukradin on the Tower of Storms, describing a lighthouse on the coast west of Phandalin that lures ships to crash on the rocks, implying the wrecks were full of treasure. Vukradin rejected this as looting from the dead. Silverleaf was taken aback and apologized for causing offense.

Last known status: at or near Phandalin, identity confirmed as a local orchard owner and former adventurer.

## Ser Kaelen Thorn
Ser Kaelen Thorn is a man in his early thirties with dark hair, a clean-shaven face, and a scar above his right eyebrow. He carries himself with confident authority and wears chain mail armor bearing the emblem of a gauntlet grasping a sword by the blade—the symbol of the Order of the Gauntlet.

He approached Vukradin at the Stonehill Inn after hearing stories of Vukradin's ethical stance on treasure and plunder, framing the bard's unique perspective as a rare and valuable asset. He recruited Vukradin as an associate member of the Order of the Gauntlet, promising that the Order's backing would expand Vukradin's reach from hundreds to tens of thousands. He emphasized that the Order's funding came from conflict-free donations. He told Vukradin that membership required him to answer calls for aid as a brother, with the same obligation returned.

He declined to recruit Valphine, showing no hostility but choosing not to offer membership. He declined to recruit Soma, his face darkening when the possibility was raised, stating that naturalist and Order perspectives would not align.

He confirmed the Order had been involved in the War of the Dragon, noting that a member named Milo Goodbarrel led the strike force that reached the inner sanctum and stopped the ritual.

He was still in Phandalin when the party returned with Elara and Jarek, and was pleased to see Vukradin acting on his principles at a small scale. He confirmed that recruiting Vukradin had been the right decision.

When asked about the Tower of Storms curse, Ser Kaelen identified three possible explanations: a cleric-removable curse, a haunting monster, or false rumors. He assessed the most likely explanation as a monster haunting the lighthouse. He confessed the Order's battle chant—"Steal and shield, Heart and band. We stand as one. A steadfast band"—was embarrassing and said Vukradin's genius was needed to improve it.

Last known status: in Phandalin when the party departed for the Tower of Storms.

## Townmaster Harbin
Townmaster Harbin is the civil authority of Phandalin. He consistently greets Vukradin by pleading to be ignored by dragons. He originally commissioned the rescue of Adabra for 25 gold and deflected Vukradin's attempt to negotiate higher payment by pointing out that Vukradin could have paid himself in Manticore treasure under Lord Neverember's standing arrangement with adventurers. Adabra's arrival and forceful advocacy on Vukradin's behalf resulted in Harbin offering free room and board at the Stonehill Inn in lieu of additional coin.

Harbin confirmed he had previously sent a note to Lord Neverember requesting help and had been promised it, which was the origin of the party's assignment. He also confirmed that the town was under siege from bandits, leaving him short of coin.

When Vukradin proposed destroying the Tower of Storms lighthouse, Harbin responded that Lord Neverember would be appalled and would want the curse removed, not the tower destroyed. He directed Vukradin to Ser Kaelen Thorn at Barthen's Provisions for more information.

Adabra was also preparing medicine for Harbin, referenced in the note she gave the party.

Last known status: in Phandalin.

## Elara (merchant)
Elara is a middle-aged human merchant who was found with her groom Jarek behind an overturned trade cart after being attacked by orcs. She was visibly shaken, her pack horses scattered. She described the orc attackers as moving with unnerving discipline, unlike typical raiding packs, with odd markings resembling a swirl or an eye, though the memory faded quickly. She accepted Soma's offer to mend the cart and Brewbarry's retrieval of a runaway horse.

She gave the reward for the party's assistance to Valphine as Lathander's cleric rather than directly to Vukradin, dedicating it to the Morninglord. She interpreted Valphine's unconventional declaration that Lathander was "painful" as an unfamiliar but sincere teaching of the faith.

Last known status: safely returned to Phandalin by the party.
