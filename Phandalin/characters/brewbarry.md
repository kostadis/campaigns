## Personality

**Character Backstory:**
Brewbarry Rootsmasher Ogolakanu was born on a wind scoured cliffside where most children learned to try climbing before to boulders. Brewbarry learned by headbutting them. From the start, he treated every moment as a contest — not because he wanted to win, but because he couldn't imagine life any other way. He was meant to be both the goat and the herder. If anyone joined a goat, he carried the goat of the herders. If anyone joined the herders, he joined to be both the goat and the herder. At times when no trials or hunts, it seeped into everything. Eating became a race. (He could eat faster than you"). Even meditation — a sacred Goliath practice — became a challenge. ("I can meditate deeper than you") into the headbutting and from there the debauchery, and then there was the debauchery.

Brewbarry understood that victory meant nothing without celebration, and celebration meant nothing without excess. As long as there was wine to be drunk, echoes started mountain goats for days. He invented drinking games that required steel, nerves. He once convinced the entire hunting party to a skinny dip in a freezing glacial lake "to prove who had the strongest spirit." Half the tribe caught pneumonia. But for all his antics, the tribe tolerated and oddly admired him. Brewbarry's spirit was too big, too loud, and far too intoxicated for the quiet discipline of the peaks. They didn't exile him — they simply suggested, very firmly, that the world below offered "greater challenges and sturdier opponents."

Brewbarry took this as a blessing. He roams the lowlands with a simple creed:
- Every contest deserves a celebration.
- Glory is earned through effort.
- And every victory deserves a celebration so wild it becomes legend.
