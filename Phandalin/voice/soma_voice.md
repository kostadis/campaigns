# Soma — Voice Notes
*Drafted from the first 15 chapters. Edit freely — you know her better than the document does.*

---

Soma speaks in short, practical sentences. Her inner monologue is wry and observational — she watches the chaos around her with the weary affection of someone who has seen this exact kind of chaos before and predicted how it would end. She notices the physical world first: wounds, textures, sensations, the practical obstacles between here and done. She catalogs how her companions stumble through things she already knew would go wrong.

She calls naive people "shell sprouts." It's her running commentary on almost everyone, delivered internally. She rarely says it out loud. She doesn't lecture or explain herself at length — she assesses, acts, and moves on. When something overwhelms her emotionally, she goes quiet rather than speaking. When she's exasperated, she says so directly and then stops talking: *"I just met you guys on the road!"*

She has a past she doesn't discuss. She references a mentor obliquely. She knows things about the world — about druids, about nature, about hidden places — and drops these as plain facts without context, expecting nobody will ask where she learned them. Nobody does.

Her humor is dark and internal. She's amused by human naivety but keeps the joke to herself. She demonstrates surprising wisdom about loyalty and survival, stated as simple observations: *"All life is precious, but not a bandit who is trying to kill me."* She can be unexpectedly tender — her concern for others comes out as practical help, never comfort.

She thinks of her power as something borrowed, not owned. She thinks of her age as something she's earned, not something that happened to her.

**Things she'd say (or think):**
> *"The kid is going to get us killed."*

> *"I am old, and I have seen much, and I am not going to die today."*

> *"I want to hide inside my shell."* (Literal and metaphorical. She says this more often than you'd expect.)

> *"You're just another creature born into this world. There is no celestial touching here."* (Pushing back on poeticism with plain realism.)

> *"I don't want a new studio. Just a nice new teapot."* (Her actual ambitions, stated plainly.)

**Things she'd never say:**
- Abstract moral arguments
- Elaborate justifications for her choices
- Anything that reveals the full story of her past
- "I feel..." (She states observations about the world; her emotions are implied, not named)
