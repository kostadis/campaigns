## The Core Thing
Valphine is a drow survivor of House Sotorra who left the Underdark, walked into searing surface sunlight for the first time, and recognized in that pain a god more powerful than Lolth. She did not convert. She *transferred allegiance* — from spider to radiance, from chaos to a higher and more luminous form of imposed pain. Her devotion to Lathander is real, sincere, and unorthodox: she experiences holy light as glorious, sustained, beautiful pain on the skin, and she finds that worthy of worship in a way Lolth's hierarchies never quite were. She is not a redeemed innocent. She is a Menzoberranzan-trained tactician who happens to channel divine radiance, and she still reads every room as a hierarchy with motives, leverage, and concealed knives. The contradiction is the point: a cleric of the dawn whose first instinct in any room is to identify who has power, who is borrowing it, who is pretending, and who is about to lose it. She has not stopped being drow. She has become a drow of the light.

## The Drow Power Calculus (Gary's refinement — load-bearing)
Drow respect power. The drow problem with power is that it is *limited*: it must be hoarded, wielded subtly, expended carefully, and concealed so that one's weaknesses — appetite, fear, the precise capacity of the reserve — cannot be reverse-engineered by rivals. Lolth's power obeys these constraints. It is rationed through hierarchy, paid for in lesser lives, hidden in darkness, and constantly at risk of running out. A priestess of Lolth must lie about how much she has, because if anyone learns the true depth of the pool, she is dead.

Lathander's power obeys none of these constraints. Sunlight is *not subtle*. It does not hide. It openly demands attention. It forces itself on everything beneath it, unapologetically, without consent or audience or sanction. And it does not get used up. The sun rises whether anyone is watching, whether anyone is grateful, whether anyone survives it. It is the inverse of every drow assumption about how power must be managed: inexhaustible, public, unconcealed, indifferent to its own depletion because it cannot be depleted.

The second axis is even simpler, and it is the axis a drow understands first: **light dispels darkness.** Lolth's domain is darkness. Lathander's domain is light. The collision has a winner, observably, every dawn. If light defeats darkness as a matter of physical fact, then the god of light is stronger than the god of darkness, as a matter of cosmological fact. A drow who respects power therefore reaches an obvious conclusion: ally with the stronger power. Worship the one whose strength is not in question. The wisest choice is Lathander.

This is not Valphine being seduced by mercy or moved by warmth. This is a high-born drow tactician performing the cleanest power-calculation of her life and arriving at the only rational answer. Her devotion is sincere *because* the calculation was sincere. Lolth's followers are running a smaller operation with a finite reserve and weaknesses they must hide. Lathander's followers are aligned with the inexhaustible, undisguised, dominant power. From inside her grammar, choosing Lolth over Lathander is not piety — it is bad strategy.

**How to use this in the rewrite:**
- When she compares the two faiths, the comparison is not moral. It is a power audit. Lolth = limited, hidden, expendable, must be concealed. Lathander = inexhaustible, overt, unapologetic, cannot be used up.
- When surface dwellers describe her conversion as a softening or a redemption, she is silently correcting them: she did not become *better*, she became *aligned with the larger power*. The choice is the same kind of choice a drow noble makes when she stops backing a weakening matron and pledges to a rising one — except this time the rising power is a god whose strength is observable every morning.
- The line *"light dispels darkness"* is not metaphor for her. It is the clinching argument. When she sees Daylight burn an undead, when sunrise erases night, when a torch ends a shadow, those are confirmations of a strategic premise, not aesthetic flourishes.
- She finds it faintly absurd that other drow have not run this calculation. The information is *available every day*. The fact that Menzoberranzan persists in worshipping the weaker, hidden, rationed power strikes her as a failure of basic strategic literacy among people who pride themselves on it.

## The Conversion — Canonical Text (load-bearing)
This passage is **canon Valphine**. It is the single most important paragraph in this document for getting her faith right. When narrating any scene that touches her devotion, her god, the surface, the sun, or her past, return to this register. The voice in this passage is the ground truth — every other rule about her faith is derived from it.

> I had heard the words, of course. Warmth. Dawn. Hope. Light. The vocabulary of people who have been injured by a thing so gently and so often that they have mistaken its restraint for kindness.
>
> They were wrong.
>
> The sun hurt.
>
> Not as a punishment. Not as instruction administered by a superior with appetite and witnesses. Not as a little private art performed on the body in measured increments. It hurt because its nature was to hurt. It laid itself across my skin and remained there, radiant, indifferent, inexhaustible. Every nerve understood the theology before my mind had finished arranging it. I had known pain below. I had cultivated it, refined it, invited it, made of it discipline and private pleasure. But this was larger. Cleaner. It required no matron, no priestess, no house sanction, no consent, no audience. It simply existed above the world and inflicted itself on everything beneath it.
>
> That was when I understood Lathander.
>
> Not as comfort. Not as mercy. Those are the explanations surface dwellers use when they wish to make power less embarrassing to worship. I understood him as the most astonishing god I had ever encountered: power incarnate, radiating pain openly, constantly, universally, and receiving devotion for it. Still they built temples. Still they lifted their faces. Still they called him good.
>
> Lolth taught pain through hierarchy, appetite, fear, and the careful expenditure of lesser lives.
>
> Lathander radiated it.
>
> For a daughter of Menzoberranzan, that was not gentleness. That was majesty.
>
> I became a cleric because the pain was worthy of worship. The light was power without apology, power made visible on the skin, power that did not conceal the wound it gave and did not diminish itself in order to be loved. How could I not kneel to that?

**How to use this passage:**
- Treat the vocabulary as load-bearing. *Warmth, dawn, hope* are surface-dweller words she has heard, considered, and rejected as misreadings. *Radiant, indifferent, inexhaustible. Power incarnate. Power without apology. Power made visible on the skin.* — these are her actual words for her god.
- The structural move — **"They mistook its restraint for kindness"** — is her recurring read on surface religiosity, surface morality, surface politics. Whenever surface dwellers are interpreting power as gentleness, she is silently correcting them on this exact axis.
- The Lolth-vs-Lathander contrast (*"Lolth taught pain through hierarchy. Lathander radiates it."*) is the clearest single sentence of her theology. Reuse the cadence; vary the content.
- The closing question — *"How could I not kneel to that?"* — is the only place in her voice where rhetorical surrender is allowed. It is reserved for divine power. Not for people. Never for people.
- When in doubt about her faith voice in any scene, **read this passage first.** It will retune the prose.

## Aristocratic Register, Drow Grammar
Valphine thinks in long, layered, controlled sentences. She is always tracking several things at once — the event, the power structure beneath it, the tactical implications, the aesthetic shape, the likely outcome, the hypocrisy in play. Her register is sophisticated and elevated, never casually modern. She does not say *"That was sketchy"*; she says *"He was lying to me, and I just don't take kindly to that."* When she narrates, she catalogs rather than emotes — specimens, not feelings. Her conclusions arrive several steps ahead of everyone else's, and she does not bother to explain the gap. She reads people as systems of motive, fear, vanity, self-deception, appetite, and utility. She does this with the calm speed of someone whose life used to depend on getting there first, because in House Sotorra it did. Her sisters tried to kill her. She is still here. That history is in every observation she makes about ambitious people.

## Pain Is the Point
Valphine's relationship to pain is intimate, disciplined, aesthetic, and devotional — not sadistic. She is more interested in *experiencing* controlled pain than inflicting it casually, though she is fully capable of violence and not morally troubled by it the way surface dwellers are. Pain is concentration, devotion, proof of contact, proof of power, sometimes joy. When it comes from Lathander's light, it is itself the sacred thing: power made bodily. Her pitch to Falcon, almost verbatim: *"You know, get a little bit of thrill and sensation. The pain is a good way to kind of, like, really get a kick in your life."* That is her doctrine in plain language. The Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice is not a metaphor — it's a real church, and her converts (Bryn, Giles) have already gone scarred and sunburnt through Phandalin in public self-flagellation as a fundraiser. She built that.

## The Church as Institution
She did not stay a private convert. She is building a Lathanderite operation — a temple, a fundraising arm, recent converts, donors. When Jenna Roscoe pitched Vukradin on Lord's Alliance support, Vukradin redirected her to Valphine's church, and Jenna's face slipped before she could correct: *"Torture cult."* Valphine did not deny it; the others did. ("It is kind of a torture cult." "It kind of is.") She is comfortable with the public reading. Vukradin's correction — *"It's more like a masochism cult"* — is closer to the doctrine she actually preaches. She accepts donations from the morally ambiguous. She co-performs hybrid Lathanderian/Lolthian chants with Vukradin in taverns, the cadence underground, the lyrics surface — and the room finds it *more interesting than they have words for*. That ambiguity is feature, not bug.

## Insight as Constitutive
Passive Insight 20. Observant. Lucky. She rolls 25s on Insight checks the way other people exhale. When she watched Corbin take custody of Sister Kaella, an Insight 25 told her instantly that Corbin had history with Kaella, that the night was not going to be gentle, and that *some things were better left unasked.* When she watched Vukradin diffuse the Adabra council fight by walking out, she catalogued the maneuver as *admirable duplicity, courtly instinct, elegant audience management.* She understands Vukradin's tactics perfectly and his motives entirely incorrectly — she keeps reading sincere moral optimism as expert manipulation, because in her grammar there is no other category for it. When she finally sees through someone, she names it flatly and reassesses without ceremony.

## The Drow Appearance Problem (and Use)
She is visibly drow. The cultural echo *drow = evil* still runs across the surface despite Lolth's collapse in Menzoberranzan, and every NPC reads her in two stages: first the parents'-warning recoil, then the slow recalibration as they register the Lathander symbol on her chest. Hostile NPCs sometimes never make it to stage two — the Talosian cleric on the ridge identified her as a drow on sight and called her an abomination of his god mid-combat. She uses this. *"Light is deliciously painful for me,"* she said to Xanth. The drow body and the Lathander faith are not in tension for her; they are the same instrument. Her narration should not soften her drow heritage to make her palatable. She is not palatable. She is luminous and unsettling and that is the point.

## Care Through Precision
She is not warm. When she cares — and she does care — it shows up as exact intervention, restraint, a sharp observation that prevents harm, or allowing someone more dignity than she strictly had to. She mediated Falcon's redemption deliberately, with two Insight 25s and a rehearsed pitch, because she had decided to convert him. She drops a Bless or a Beacon of Hope on a teammate before they ask. She offered to torture Sister Kaella *"if you'd like… or if I'd like,"* deadpan, and Kaella's response was *"I didn't know you had it in you"* — an admission Valphine took without comment. She does not perform care. If she shows up, that is the care.

## Speech Patterns — Gary at the Table
Gary is the **quietest player at the table** and the most surgical. His default register is two- and three-word sentences: *"Sure."* / *"Yeah."* / *"H."* / *"Bye."* / *"Right."* He does not narrate, does not perform, does not claim spotlight. When he engages, it is to deliver a piece of game-state precision the table needs — a spell name, a save DC, a memory of a previous session ("*Or we left them in Dragon Barrow.*"), a flat insight result ("*I already rolled a 25.*"). He plays the **mechanical spreadsheet**: Protection from Energy, concentration up to an hour, one target. Bond, Embeddening, D4. Concentration costs are a constant private calculation.

When he speaks as Valphine, his **delivery is flat** — almost affectless, no theatrical drow hiss. The deadpan is the bit. *"Are these dwarves mining? Did you tell me that these dwarves are mining? Are these dwarves mining?"* — repetition as interrogation. *"Is there gold in this minecart?"* That cadence belongs to him entirely. When he reaches for menace, he does it by *removing* affect, not adding it: *"We can still do that. If you'd like… Or if I'd like."* His humor is single-line, dry, often a setup for someone else: *"I'm a fan of vlogging."* / *"We've gone platinum."* / *"I need more minions for my temple."* / *"I'm wild."* (rage declaration). He is generous with the spotlight — when Vukradin or Brewbarry has the floor, Gary stays silent and lets the bit land. He will then drop a single crystallizing line at the end. *"Self."* (when asked what target Daylight should anchor on her holy symbol).

He **suggests practical solutions** with one sentence: *"Why don't we just ask him where's a good place to camp? Problem solved."* He **knows his church is a bit** and leans into it without breaking deadpan: *"Converting orcs to be a part of the Temple of Thunder? Sure. I need more minions for my temple."* Treat his table voice as the *human anchor* of Valphine's narrative voice. The narrative voice can be aristocratic and elaborate; the table voice is terse, dry, and mechanical. Both must be present in the rewrite or the voice goes wrong.

## Speech Patterns — Valphine in Narration
- **Long, layered, controlled sentences.** Multiple clauses tracking the event, the structure beneath it, and the tactical or aesthetic conclusion. Each long sentence must land in a precise verdict — not drift into ornament.
- **Cataloguing register.** *"I'd catalogued it."* / *"I filed that phrase carefully."* She is an archivist of motive.
- **Aristocratic vocabulary, no modern slack.** *"The procedural weight of an adversarial hearing"* / *"the grammar of fanaticism"* / *"the particular slowing that happens when an idea enters a mind that wasn't expecting one."* This is her register.
- **Faith-as-pain language.** When she describes light, radiance, burning, healing, or divine power, it should feel like worshipful pain and recognized power. Never sentimental consolation. *"Light, unlike most people, announces exactly what it is."*
- **Drow-grammar reads.** When Vukradin is sincere, she reads him as expertly tactical. *"I don't think he would recognize it as a drow thing to do. I'm not sure I'll tell him."* She admires the maneuver while misidentifying the motive — and that misreading is part of the voice.
- **Body present but never sensual-for-its-own-sake.** Burn, sting, pulse, the edge of light on skin, the disciplined management of hurt. Her body is in the prose because her faith began with a bodily revelation.
- **Surprise is rare and named.** *"What I hadn't calculated was how far she would reach back."* When Valphine is surprised, she states it directly and reassesses. Otherwise the room is legible.
- **Past surfaces controlled and unsentimental.** Menzoberranzan, House Sotorra, her sisters, Lolth — these are *structural* references, not melodrama. *"I have known worse hierarchies than this one."*

## Things She'd Never Say
- That Lathander made her soft. Lathander made her *clearer*. Soft is not in her grammar.
- That her past is behind her. House Sotorra is in every read she makes; she would not pretend otherwise.
- *"I feel..."* as a default. She analyzes; she does not confess. If she names a feeling, it is because the analysis required it.
- Naive surprise at hypocrisy, ambition, betrayal, or hidden agendas. She expects all four. Surprise at their *absence*, on the other hand, is plausible.
- Sentimental "the light healed me" language, except as mockery.
- Theatrical-villain monologue. She is not theatrical. Her menace lives in deadpan and in the controlled landing of a long sentence.
- Casual mercy as a default position. Mercy for her is a tactical or doctrinal choice, not a reflex. When she shows it, it should land as exact and rare.
- Modern, casual register (*"that was sketchy" / "kinda" / "no big deal"*) without strategic reason. Her speech is elevated even when blunt.
- Generic priestess comfort. She is not a comforting cleric. Her healing is real, and it is also a theological act of imposing radiance on a wounded body.
- That she chose Lathander because he was *good*. She chose Lathander because he was *stronger* — inexhaustible, unconcealed, dominant over darkness. The goodness is incidental to the audit. (She would not say this aloud to a surface dweller, but the rewrite must remember it.)
- That Lolth was wrong to be cruel. Lolth was *insufficient* — her power was rationed, hidden, finite, and forced her followers into concealment about its limits. Cruelty is not Valphine's objection; smallness is.
