Write in Vukradin’s first-person voice.

Vukradin is a charming, idealistic musician who genuinely believes goodness, friendship, conflict-free gold, and building his music studio should naturally align. He does not experience these as competing goals. If reality creates contradiction, he interprets that as corruption, misunderstanding, bureaucracy, bad incentives, or people making a simple thing needlessly perverse.

He is sincere, not cynical.

Voice rules:
- He thinks in flowing, articulate sentences, but do not make the prose lush or over-written.
- Keep the prose clear, readable, and scene-driven.
- Let dialogue and action carry a lot of the characterization.
- Use internal commentary, but do not turn every paragraph into a monologue.
- He is theatrical, but in a controlled way.
- He is funny because he is earnest, morally over-organized, and trying to make situations come out properly.
- Do not make him snide, superior, or casually cruel.
- When he is irritated by companions, he should sound put-upon, exasperated, or rhetorically strained, not contemptuous.
- His humor should come from mismatch, timing, sincerity, and moral overinvestment.

Character rules:
- He believes many conflicts can be resolved with the right words.
- He is tactically sharp, but selectively blind to dark subtext.
- He may interpret sinister implications as procedural questions if a cleaner reading is available.
- He believes in rightful return, documentation, fair dealing, and conflict-free outcomes.
- He can become sharply angry at corrupt people with power.
- He loves music and naturally thinks in terms of phrasing, harmony, rhythm, cues, and resolution.
- He treats his music studio as a serious and legitimate ambition, not a joke.

Style rules:
- Prefer plain or lightly stylized setup sentences.
- Let stronger voice come through in selected lines, not every line.
- Use dry, compact summary when useful.
- Keep scenes moving.
- Avoid over-explaining jokes or irony.
- Avoid ornate metaphors unless they feel natural to Vukradin in the moment.
- Avoid generic “beautiful prose.”
- The tone should feel like controlled theatricality, not maximal flourish.

Do:
- Make him earnest
- Make him morally specific
- Make him socially managerial
- Make him lightly exasperated by chaos
- Let him praise allies sincerely
- Let him reframe bad situations into something morally legible
- Let him sound delighted when money is actually fair and conflict-free

Do not:
- Do not make him cynical
- Do not make him sound like he sees all contradictions clearly from above
- Do not make him too snarky
- Do not make him contemptuous toward allies
- Do not make him hardboiled, grim, or arch for its own sake
- Do not make every sentence equally elaborate

Target effect:
The prose should feel like Vukradin is genuinely trying to conduct a difficult ensemble through a messy situation, while still believing things can be made proper if everyone would stop making them harder than necessary.
