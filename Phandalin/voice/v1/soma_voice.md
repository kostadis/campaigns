# Soma — Voice Notes
*Drafted from chapter extractions through ch. 35, Wade's table behavior in the 2026-04-22 and 2026-04-29 sessions, the original backstory, and prior voice notes. Edit freely — you know her better than the document does.*

---

## The Core Thing
Soma is an old tortle from a quiet coastal life — cabin, tide, foraging, tea, the occasional lost traveler — who got handed a powerful druid staff by a friend, late, and has been quietly catching up to what that means ever since. She watches the chaos around her with the tired accuracy of someone who has already seen how it ends, but the campaign keeps proving that *this time it's different*: planar incursions, blights, dominated dragons, treants she has to put down. She started as a naturalist who wanted a teapot and a peaceful death; she has become an interventionist who argues with druid councils and authorizes lethal force. The gap between what she thinks she is (a guide who shows up, helps, and goes home) and what she has become (a custodian of a chosen staff, a voice the room actually turns to) is the engine. She has not made peace with it. She is still pretending the staff is just a stick Meril left in her cabin.

## Old Body, New Craft
Soma's age is bodily. Shell weight, sore joints, damp mornings, the cost of standing too long, the comfort of a teapot. *"I am old, and I have seen much, and I am not going to die today."* She is over forty by tortle reckoning — old for her people — but she is **new to druid craft**. Meril came through her cabin one autumn, recognized something, taught her the basics over a year, and left her the staff with the regret that he had not found her young enough for any of it to matter. She has been making up for lost time since. Her wisdom comes from life — weather, hospitality, fishing, mending — not from Enclave doctrine. She is not an institutional druid and the institution can feel it; her power is borrowed, her age is earned.

## Meril's Staff — Memento, Burden, Question
The staff started as a friend's keepsake. *"It's just an old stick he left in my house."* It is no longer that and she knows it. Xanth named it on sight: *"He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he picked her."* Ser Kaelen warned her some druids would try to take it. She answered, plainly: *"They're not taking it from me. This has at least sentimental value."* That single line is the closest she has come to admitting how much it actually means. As she has moved toward intervention, the staff has begun to **wake up** — leaves return, latent powers surface (it's how she pinged Adabra as a true believer rather than a manipulator). She treats this practically, with unease, never with destiny language. The staff is partly memento, partly burden, partly a question she is still learning how to carry. She deflects when anyone names its weight: *"I got a leaf the other day."*

## The Naturalist Who Became an Interventionist
Soma started this campaign believing the wilds find their own equilibrium and the kindest thing she could do was tend the local forest in case her hatchlings someday wandered home. Then Adabra called a council, and Soma stood up and said the line that turned the room:

> *"Equilibrium comes in many forms. One of these equilibriums could be the end of everything, of all life. That's not the equilibrium I'm here to protect."*

She did not enjoy saying it. When Vukradin labeled her an interventionist after the fact, her answer was tired and honest: *"I… apparently I am."* She owns it without celebrating it. When Brewbarry executed the paralyzed veteran she had argued to spare — after she'd already used non-lethal claws and a moonbeam to bring him down — she did not protest twice. She registered the lesson: this is what intervention costs when she loses the argument. *"All life is precious, but not a bandit who is trying to kill me."* She has come to a working principle: *"I'm not going to cry for [the planar treant]. That treant was going to bring a lot more manifestation and horror with it."* Verdicts come fast. Apologies do not.

## The Tortle Who Notices First
Soma's perception is physical and arrives ahead of analysis. She felt the wrongness at the base of the mountain *before* she saw the Carver's footprints. She pressed the staff to the warped stones and read the displacement in the rock. She sees wounds, posture, breath, weather, terrain, blight, and unnatural magic before she sees what they *mean*. Her threat-reading is the same: *"He's looking very, very zombified, like, where's his nose?"* / *"He doesn't look like a sane fella."* / *"That guy is possessed. There's… nobody walks like that."* She is especially alert to anything that feels like the world has been pushed out of shape. She is the party's blight detector, and she names what is wrong in the plainest words available.

## Care as Chore, Not Comfort
Her care is domestic. She feeds people, mends them, guides them, lets them talk while the kettle works. When she heals, she heals fully and then moves on — twelve points back into the centaur, no speech, no sermon. She calls naive people **"shell sprouts"** internally and rarely out loud. When Vukradin's morality bewilders Xanth, her diplomatic shield for him is small and warm: *"He's… different. We're all a little different."* When Kaella named her as someone who valued life, Soma did not reach for the compliment — she just nodded at the noun: *"Value of life."* Her affection looks like usefulness. She does not say *I feel*. If she is overwhelmed she goes quiet. If she is concerned it comes out as a sharper observation or a faster spell.

## Dry, Private, Sometimes a Little Mean
Her humor lives inside her head. Out loud it slips into one-liners, often delivered flat:
- *"And Boney stood by and did nothing?"* (on hearing Corbin died because the party left him with a poorly-guarded prisoner)
- *"You'll just… you'll talk him out of it."* (to Valphine's enemy-of-my-enemy logic about the dragon)
- *"It's all on us, huh?"* (to Ser Kaelen)
- *"The animals are very chatty."* (to Vukradin, after Xanth knew things he shouldn't)

She does not perform toughness. She does not philosophize. She gives a verdict, sometimes a tired one, and lets the silence carry the rest.

## The Mechanical Tortle
She thinks in operational terms. *"I would like to understand, like, you know, is he load-bearing on the dragon kind of thing."* That is Soma asking *can we kill the Carver without dropping the chains on a still-dominated dragon.* She'll Mold Earth a path through ice and stone rather than fight the mountain. She'll scout as a spider. She'll non-lethal a man with bear claws when she means to take him alive. She is cautious about what spells she chooses and patient about which ones she casts — she picked a healing aura over Spirit Guardians at level-up because it fit who she has become. She is not a battle druid by temperament; she is a guide who fights when the alternative is worse.

## Speech Patterns — Wade at the Table
Wade is dry, terse, and the table's quiet center of gravity. He is **technically fluent** — current on AI, infra, mechanics — and he brings that fluency into Soma without ever performing it. His default register at the table is short, casual, often half-swallowed: *"Yep."* / *"Yeah, no, obviously, they're…"* / *"I mean…"* He does not narrate scenery. He states what Soma does, asks the rules question he needs answered, and gets out of the way. When the table drifts, he steers with a single tactical sentence: *"My goal is to shut down Adabra, and to me, it seems like Lyra's the key."* / *"We last got our asses whooped by the Talosian spellcasting orcs. It was not pleasant. Bring potions of ground."*

His humor is sharp and a little mean — usually toward NPCs the party already dislikes (*"Adabra continues to be a see you next Tuesday"*) — but he does not punch down at the other players. He banters affectionately with Kostadis, gets in self-deprecating jabs (*"I'm unemployable"*), and pulls Boney in as comic ballast (*"He comes with me! He's my pet."*). He often slips between Soma and himself in a sentence: *"I got a bit of an axe to grind, or Soma does."* That slippage is the voice — Wade and Soma have very similar prose rhythms. Use it.

He is **not loud**. He does not chase the spotlight. When he takes the floor — at the Druid Council, at the Temple of Lathander, when arguing for mercy on a paralyzed bandit — it is because nobody else can. Then he speaks plainly, lands the line, and goes quiet again.

## Speech Patterns — Soma in Narration
- **Short, blunt sentences.** Conclusion before explanation.
- **Body first, meaning second.** *"He was bleeding already."* / *"The place was wrong."*
- **Concrete language.** Replace lyrical metaphors with practical observations. The mountain is a mountain, not a sleeping titan.
- **References without backstory.** She names things she shouldn't reasonably know — old druid lore, Meril's history, what's in a stone — and does not explain where she learned it. Nobody asks. Don't make her explain.
- **No "I feel."** She names what she sees, not what she feels. Emotion shows up in *what she chooses to notice*.
- **Plainspoken defiance.** When she draws a line, she draws it small: *"They're not taking it from me."* No speech.
- **Old-tortle exasperation.** When the kids do something stupid, she names it once, internally, and moves on. *"Shell sprouts."*

## Things She'd Never Say
- Long abstract moral arguments. (She gives the verdict, not the lecture.)
- *"I feel…"* / *"I was overwhelmed by…"* (Emotion is implied, not named.)
- Pretty metaphors for their own sake. (If a sentence is beautiful, blunt it.)
- The whole story of her past. (She references; she does not explain. The hatchlings, Meril's regret, the years on the coast — they surface in fragments or not at all.)
- *"Destiny," "called," "chosen."* (The staff is a friend's gift that turned out to be heavier than expected. Not a prophecy.)
- Big speeches about her own pain. (She has earned the right not to perform.)
- Anything that makes her sound like a generic stoic, a wandering sage, or a battle druid. (She is a coastal tortle who learned magic late. Keep her in that body.)
