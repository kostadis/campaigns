# Brewbarry — Voice Notes
*Drafted from the first 15 chapters. Edit freely — you know him better than the document does.*

---

Brewbarry thinks in short, declarative sentences. He feels something and then he acts. There is very little space between the two. He doesn't build arguments or justify himself — he states what's true and moves.

He is genuinely, almost completely devoted to Vukradin. Not in a complicated way. He likes Vukradin's music. His tribe went boom boom boom with drums. He doesn't like drums. He likes Vukradin. That's the full explanation. He followed the bard because someone appreciated him, and that was new, and it felt like home.

He carries shame about his past — his tribe, the things they did, the things he was part of — but he doesn't wallow in it. He doesn't explain it. He meets someone his tribe hurt and says *"I am so sorry. They were mean to me, too."* That's all. It's enough for him.

He demonstrates moments of perception that his surface simplicity hides. He understands loyalty at a level that surprises people. He notices when someone is afraid. He responds to fear with gentleness when others might respond with force. His rage, when it comes, is clean — not cruel. The moment he chose mercy over violence, he named what he felt without performance: *"My rage consumes me. And yet, there is something about this man's fear. And I think that he has lost his tribe. And I feel sadness."*

He is still learning surface-world norms and often doesn't quite get them. He asks honest questions that reveal how much he doesn't know, without embarrassment. He looks to Vukradin for direction. He contributes strength and loyalty. He is becoming, slowly and painfully, something better than where he came from.

**Things he'd say (or think):**
> *"I like Vukradin music. My tribe goes boom boom boom with a drum. I don't like drums. I like Vukradin."*

> *"I am so sorry that the Uthgardts hurt you. They were mean to me, too."*

> *"My rage consumes me. And yet—"* (He holds the and yet. That pause is who he is.)

> *"Both sides simultaneously."* (His response to something confusing, stated plainly without irony.)

> *"You mean more than I am already?"* (His dark assessment of himself when asked if he can be pushed further.)

**Things he'd never say:**
- Complex moral arguments about systems
- Self-justification at length
- Anything cruel to someone who is already afraid
- Sarcasm (he doesn't quite have it)
