## Consistency Notes

The following issues from the consistency report could not be silently resolved in the text and are flagged for GM verification.

**Issue 1 — Gorthok's Hit Points.** The Scenes section gives Gorthok 74 hit points; the NPCs section gives "15 and 73 hit points (likely two stat blocks or a composite)." The "15" almost certainly belongs to the Anchorites (listed at 15 HP each in the same breath) and leaked into the Gorthok entry. Please confirm Gorthok's single correct HP total so the NPCs section can be corrected. If 74 is right, the NPCs entry should read: "The DM notes his hit points differ from the Roll20 tracker; he has 74 hit points."

**Issue 2 — Aid cast by Vukradin or Valphine (or both).** The Negotiations scene attributes Aid to Vukradin; the Siege scene attributes it to Valphine. Aid is a Cleric/Paladin spell not on the Bard list without Magical Secrets. Please confirm: (a) does Vukradin have Aid via Magical Secrets? and (b) was Aid cast once or twice this session? If twice by different casters, the recap should note both casts and clarify whether the effects stacked. If only once, the Negotiations scene attribution needs correction.

**Issue 3 — Brewbarry's primary weapon.** The recap treats the +1 Halberd as Brewbarry's active combat weapon without mentioning the Dragon Slayer Longsword listed as his primary weapon on his character sheet. No correction has been made to the existing text — the halberd joke is preserved as recorded — but future session notes should specify which weapon Brewbarry is currently wielding into combat.

**Issue 4 — Sending costs a 5th-level slot, not 3rd.** Both the Planning scene and the Spells section describe Vukradin using Sending and state it costs "a third-level slot." Sending is a 5th-level spell in 5e and cannot be cast at third level. Please confirm whether Vukradin has Sending via Magical Secrets (not currently noted on his character sheet) and correct the slot cost in both locations. If confirmed as 5th-level, Vukradin's spell slot accounting for the session also changes.

**Issue 5 — Spell slot accounting and "Gary."** The Negotiations scene states "Gary has one third-level slot remaining" — "Gary" should read "Valphine" for document consistency throughout. Additionally, Vukradin's slot count ("two first-level and two third-level burned") cannot be fully reconciled until Issues 2 and 4 above are resolved. A clean slot-state summary at session end would help future continuity.

**Issue 6 — Falcon's apology: Vukradin's initiative vs. Valphine's prior mediation.** The party document notes that Falcon asked Valphine to mediate his apology to Vukradin, and the session extractions reference Valphine independently laying groundwork with Falcon at the druid conclave. The recap presents Vukradin acting entirely on his own initiative via Sending, with no reference to Valphine's prior arrangement. Please clarify whether Valphine's groundwork contributed to Falcon's readiness to apologize, or whether Vukradin's call superseded the mediation arrangement — this gap affects Valphine's ongoing relationship arc with Falcon.

**Issue 7 — Prutha's banner count.** Prutha is quoted as saying "Three banners on the wall! I see one!" which is internally ambiguous: does the lodge display three banners and he recognizes only one, or does he see one banner and the "three" refers to something else? Please clarify for worldbuilding purposes — if three distinct clan-marks are flying, the Eastern Heart band may represent a coalition larger than the recap implies.

**Issue 8 — Icespire Hold occupancy status.** The Locations entry for Icespire Hold currently states only that "the hold is now free." The Negotiations scene records the party cautioning that something worse than Cryovain may now occupy it. The Locations entry should be updated to reflect this uncertainty: Cryovain is confirmed dead but the current occupancy status of Icespire Hold is unconfirmed. This matters if the Eastern Heart tribe acts on Vukradin's offer to escort them home.

**Issue 9 — Vorga as "acting" war chief.** The NPCs section describes Vorga as "acting war chief" but neither the Summary nor the Scenes explain what this qualifier means — whether the tribe's actual chief is dead, absent, or elsewhere, and whether this affects her authority to commit the tribe to the lodge defense. Please clarify for future Eastern Heart interactions.

**Issue 10 — "Tovrak" / "boy from the eastern clan" — one document or two.** The Flenz's Correspondence item entry references "a boy from the eastern clan" (no name). The Circle of Thunder location entry and the Negotiations scene both reference "the boy from the East, Tovrak" (named). It is unclear whether these are the same document described inconsistently or two separate pieces of intelligence. Please confirm whether the name "Tovrak" appears in a recovered document or was spoken aloud at the table, and standardize the description across all three entries. This thread is unresolved and may connect to Prutha.

**Minor flag — "Aasimar" in the sentry's invitation.** The Arrival scene records the sentry as saying "Bring your drow, bring your Aasimar, bring all of them." Valphine is the Drow and Vukradin is the Aasimar — the attribution appears correct — but the phrasing could be misread in future summaries as Valphine being called an Aasimar. Consider adding "(Vukradin)" parenthetically after "Aasimar" in the scene text.
