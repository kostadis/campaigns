# Voice Critique — Soma, scene 4: Negotiations at the Hunting Lodge

**Narration:** `gm-assist-doc.md` (lines 208–293)
**Voice spec:** `voice/soma_new_pipeline.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/soma.md`

## Flags

### [1] Particular-X tic — heavy concentration in this scene (five hits)

> with the particular timing she has that I still haven't fully mapped
>
> [and]
>
> she moved through her own reasoning with the efficiency of someone who has made this calculation before, in other places, with other stakes
>
> [and]
>
> with that particular stillness of someone who has learned that stillness is safer than motion
>
> [and]
>
> They settled in with the quiet competence of people who know how to hold a position.
>
> [and]
>
> the particular warmth of a reconciliation he'd been waiting on

**Why:** This is the most concentrated single tic in the document. Soma's voice is "short, practical sentences. Conclusion before explanation." Her examples observe directly — "*Mountain's Toe. The dwarves of yore.*" / "*I want to hide inside my shell.*" / "*And on cue, I heard Vukradin question my choices.*" She doesn't classify people via *with the X of someone who Y* — she just says what she sees. Five hits in one scene is a systemic problem.
**Suggested rewrite (Prutha):** *Prutha stood the way he usually does. Still. He'd learned that one early.*
**Suggested rewrite (orc archers):** *The orc archers settled into the arrow slits. They knew the work.*

### [2] Voice spec conflict — "I filed it" is Valphine's verb, not Soma's

> I caught that — the slight tilt of Vorga's head, the flicker of something calculating in her eyes as they moved from the drow to the orc at our backs. I filed it and said nothing.

**Why:** Spec for Valphine: she is "an archivist of motive." Spec for Soma: she "notices the physical world first: wounds, posture, hands, breath, mud, blood, weather, terrain, distance, obstacles, pain, weight, practical limitations." Soma's reflex on noticing something is to *wait on it* or *keep her mouth shut* — see examples ch. 03: "*But I kept my mouth shut.*" Filing is bureaucratic; Soma is a coastal tortle, not a clerk.
**Suggested rewrite:** *I caught it. Said nothing. That's the kind of look you wait on.*

### [3] Voice spec conflict — abstract philosophical aside about dragons

> I'd seen that before. After Cryovain came down from the mountain, a lot of creatures relocated to where the cold hadn't reached. The dragon had rearranged an entire valley's worth of lives without ever intending to. I wondered if it had known, or cared, or if dragons simply don't think in those terms at all.

**Why:** Spec: "Do not write long abstract moral arguments. She gives the verdict, not the lecture." And: "Do not make her sound chatty, rhetorical, or philosophically abstract." The "I wondered if it had known, or cared, or if dragons simply don't think in those terms at all" tricolon is exactly the rhetorical philosophy the spec forbids. Soma would render the displacement physically (the cold pushing creatures down the valley) and let the meaning sit.
**Suggested rewrite:** *I'd seen that before. After Cryovain came down, every creature that could move had moved. Refugees with claws.*

### [4] Generic prose — "the quality of a sentence in a language" simile

> They stared. Twenty people, some armed, some not, all of them going absolutely still. It had the quality of a sentence in a language they understood but hadn't expected to hear spoken aloud in their presence.

**Why:** This is workshopped literary fiction. Soma notices "the physical world first" — wounds, posture, breath. She does not reach for "the quality of a sentence in a language they understood but hadn't expected to hear spoken aloud." The simile is also self-defeating: a sentence in a language they understood is just… a sentence. The line wants to be deep; it isn't.
**Suggested rewrite:** *They stared. Twenty of them, going absolutely still. Even the children stopped moving.*

### [5] Voice spec conflict — staff narration in someone else's POV

> Valphine gathered herself during the lull — I heard the quiet particular way she has of doing it, like a held breath released on purpose — and then the working went out, a warm brace moving through us, the kind that doesn't close wounds but steadies the body against the ones coming, deepens the reserve you draw on before you finally go down. I noted it, filed the difference.

**Why:** Two issues. (a) Another particular-X tic. (b) "a warm brace moving through us, the kind that doesn't close wounds but steadies the body" is a four-clause Vukradin-style mechanical description of Aid in someone else's voice — Soma's spec says she thinks of power as "borrowed, not owned" and would render the working as something she *felt* in the body, not analyze its categorical difference from healing. "I noted it, filed the difference" doubles down on the Valphine-verb problem.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Valphine cast something — not a healing. A bracing. The kind that makes a body harder to drop. I felt it in my shell.*

### [6] Voice spec conflict — "the Talosians have not yet met Brewbarry properly"

> I watched him and thought: the Talosians have not yet met Brewbarry properly. They are going to.

**Why:** Borderline — close to good Soma dry humor — but the "have not yet met X properly. They are going to." structure is generic narrator-foreshadowing. Soma's verdict-shaped lines in the examples land flatter: "*And Boney stood by and did nothing?*" / "*You'll just… you'll talk him out of it.*" She tells you what is, not what is going to be.
**Suggested rewrite:** *The Talosians had not met Brewbarry yet. That was going to be a short conversation.*

## Verdict

The particular-X tic alone is worth a full re-run with the spec hoisted — five hits in one scene drowns out the verdicts. The dragon-displacement philosophy and the Aid description are the next-tier cuts. Soma's bones in this scene (the Boney handoff, the shell-sprouts line, the cask-wail beat) are pure voice and worth protecting.
