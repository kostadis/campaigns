# Chapter 42: A Storm of Bolts and Orcs

Date: May 20th, 2026

## Summary
The party's attention turned first to the spoils of their recent battle — a striking heater shield of dark oil-blackened oak, banded in iron and carved with concentric rings of standing stones in the old druid style. Valphine examined it closely and discovered that the hammered iron disc bearing Talos's jagged bolt had been crudely chiseled into the central boss over the original druidic work, sitting crookedly atop the older carving the way a brand sits on skin. With a firm wiggle, the disc popped free entirely, revealing it had never truly belonged there. The leather straps inside were newer than the shield itself, smelling faintly of pine tar and wet dog, and the inner face bore a half-orc tally mark — four notches and a slash. Valphine declared she would hold the stolen druidic relic until its rightful owner could be found, while Brewbarry quietly insisted that the suspiciously sharp and lethal-looking halberd he was carrying was simply his old one, freshly sharpened on a whetstone.

Before setting out, Valphine made clear she had no intention of lifting a finger for the ranger known as the Falcon without a proper apology for his conduct at the Druid Council. Using a magical Sending spell, Vukradin contacted him with the indignant energy of someone who had been waiting a long time to make this call, demanding his apology before agreeing to deal with the infestation at his hunting lodge. Falcon responded with a categorical, unambiguous apology and expressed his hope to meet them there. Satisfied, Valphine shared what the party knew: the henge intel — Flenz's sealed letters, Narux's vellum sketches of Gorthok with a marked regional map, and a half-burned scrap from Yargath's firepit — pointed to multiple Talosian summoning rituals being attempted across the region, and the Woodland Manse and Falcon's Hunting Lodge were the two most pressing concerns. After weighing their options and consulting Prutha about the three orc factions operating in the area — those who stayed with the tribe (the Eastern Heart refugees), those who aligned with the Talosians (cult-aligned bands), and those who became independent players for hire (free agents, like the orcs the Carver previously employed) — the party resolved to head for the lodge, roughly two hours to the south.

When the hunting lodge came into view, it was clear something had changed. The wooden palisade was intact but had been repaired in places with mismatched planks lashed with rough cords, and three sentries stood on the walkway behind it, bows drawn but not raised, watching the tree line. Prutha immediately recognized the banners on the wall — the Sun and Stone clan, his own family. He stepped forward alone at a deliberate pace, hands open, and at thirty feet from the gate performed the dawn-bow: right fist to left shoulder, head down. He then drew a bone whistle from inside his armor and blew the dawn prayer into the still air. Above the palisade, the sentry lowered her bow, returned the gesture, and called out to the rest of the band — the entire party was invited inside.

The courtyard was not what anyone had expected. It was full of people — not warriors, but refugees. Women with babies, an old man warming himself by a cookfire, children playing a game with carved sticks near the wall. Only about a dozen of the thirty or so orcs present looked like fighters. They watched the party the way refugees watch anyone who walks into their armed camp. A middle-aged orc woman with a broken nose, a scar across her jaw, and the Falcon's own stolen hunter cloak draped over her orc-style leather came down from the walkway to meet them. Beside her stood an elderly orc leaning on a spear like a walking stick, who looked directly at Valphine's holy symbol and said a single word in orcish: "Dawn."

Valphine spoke to Vorga Long-Memory in fluent orcish, quickly intuiting that this tribe — the Eastern Heart, flying the Sun and Stone clan-mark on the palisade — had been driven from their ancestral home at Icespire Hold by the dragon Cryovain. She warned Vorga of the imminent Talosian threat, presenting the henge correspondence and Narux's marked map as evidence and describing the cultists staging nearby. Then came the revelation that silenced the entire courtyard: the party had killed Cryovain. The orcs stared in disbelief, and Vorga's posture shifted entirely. She chose to stay and defend the lodge rather than risk her non-combatants in the open woods, reasoning that the fortified palisade gave them their best chance. The party agreed, positioning orc warriors with bows behind the arrow slits of the gatehouse tower, and Valphine bolstered everyone's resilience with a blessing before the fight. Brewbarry, meanwhile, discovered the lodge's entire alcohol supply had leaked from broken casks onto the ground and declared, with murderous fury, that he would kill them all.

The Falcon arrived not long after, face covered in war paint and bow in hand, clearly prepared for a very different kind of welcome. The party talked him down and brought him into the fold just as the sky began to change. The storm rolled in fast — not the slow gather of a ritual henge, but something direct and impatient. Air pressure dropped in everyone's ears all at once, and the afternoon sky darkened to evening in a single breath. The animals in the pens panicked and crashed against their enclosures. The elderly orc Old Drubbak went very still and said quietly that it had come wrong — there was no chant he could hear, and there should have been a chant. (The chant was there, in fact — drifting from Grannoc's ritual at the Woodland Manse miles to the south — but too distant for Drubbak to read.) Lightning struck the tree line, then the river, then split an empty barrel inside the palisade itself. Soma noticed her staff had sprouted a new leaf. Then a massive bolt of lightning screamed out of the darkness beyond the walls and blasted the lodge's main gate clean off its hinges, collapsing the doors in a shower of splinters. The Talosian forces — Anchorites and the fearsome creature Gorthok among them — began converging on the broken entrance, and the siege of Falcon's Hunting Lodge had begun.

## Scenes
### The Spoils of War
#### The party examines a newly acquired magical shield and discusses its origins and ownership.
- Vukradin opens the session by asking the DM how different the +1 shield looks from a standard one — framing the question as out-of-character before promising to roleplay whatever is established. The DM confirms he has tables for this.
- The DM describes the shield: a heater shield of dark, oiled, blackened oak, banded in iron. The face is carved with concentric rings of standing stones — old druid craft, "the same geometry" — with names above. The central boss has been chiseled out and replaced with a hammered iron disc bearing Talos's jagged bolt.
- Vukradin immediately recognizes this is a problem for a Cleric of Lathander: "The Cleric of Lathander is wielding a shield that has the image of—"
- The DM prompts Valphine to roll Investigation; she rolls a natural 20 (total with modifiers). The DM notes that, just as Valphine is about to comment, Soma's observations about druids prompt her eyes to focus on the bolt — and she realizes it was stolen by the Talosians.
- Valphine wiggles the bolt and it pops free: "It was just a poorly attached decoration." The original carving was never sanded smooth; the bolt sat crookedly atop the older work the way a brand sits on skin. The leather straps inside are newer than the shield itself and smell faintly of pine tar and wet dog. The inside bears a half-orc tally mark — four notches and a slash, meaning five somethings: kills, storms weathered, or something else.
- Prutha interposes with enthusiasm — "Valphine, use good shield! Valphine, cleric of sun!" — and is told flatly by Vukradin: "You can give them your shield, you can do whatever you want with your shield. But that shield? Not yours." Then, to Prutha directly: "Not yours either."
- Valphine decides to hold onto the shield until the rightful owner is found, marking it in her inventory as such. The group recommends the Talosian symbol imagery be better hidden in future.
- Brewbarry defends his possession of a magical halberd by claiming it is simply his original halberd, well-sharpened. Vukradin endorses this version of events: "This is the halberd you've had all along. You just sharpened it on a sharpening stone. It just looks more lethal." The DM adds it also looks "a little waterlogged."
- Kostadis takes a moment to articulate his vision of Valphine's theology: Lathander is not gentle light but pure, total power causing harm twelve hours a day, with people worshipping it as good and begging for it to return when it's gone — closer to Lolth's dynamic than anyone admits, but in plain sight. Gary confirms this reading fits Valphine perfectly, and Kostadis adds that Lathander "approves of her curious new interpretation of his power."
- Vukradin observes: "I like that the cleric is changing the god instead of the god changing the cleric." Kostadis: "That's how it always has been in Dungeons and Dragons. The gods reflect the choices, not the other way around."

### Negotiations at the Hunting Lodge
#### The party negotiates with the displaced orc tribe occupying the lodge, sharing vital intelligence and forging an alliance against the incoming Talosian threat.
- Valphine opens in orcish, making the conversation private from the rest of the party. She asks why the tribe is here, opting for a persuasion-based approach; she rolls an 11, which the DM rules is enough given the tribe is not hostile.
- Vorga explains in flawless orcish — the DM notes that orcs are highly intelligent and simply have a speech impediment when speaking Common — that the tribe has been looking for a new place to stay and found the lodge abandoned.
- Valphine rolls Insight with a 28 and immediately recognizes the tribe as Eastern Heart refugees displaced by Cryovain from Icespire Hold. The DM confirms: she also intuits that the Talosian cult has a plan to attack them.
- Valphine warns Vorga of an imminent attack by Talosian cultists performing summoning rituals nearby, using the henge correspondence and Narux's map as evidence. Vorga asks: "Do you have proof?" Valphine presents the notes and invokes the Falcon — "a local ranger named the Falcon, who had seen them staging an area east of here called the Woodland Manse" — and describes disrupting one of their rituals at the Circle of Thunder a few hours ago.
- The party switches to Common to brief the others. Vukradin asks if the orcs were displaced by the dragon; Gary confirms it, and Vukradin announces to the courtyard: "We killed the dragon."
- The assembled orcs stare in disbelief. Vorga: "Dragon is dead. That home is now free to go back?" Vukradin: "Yeah, you can go back." Gary adds the caveat that something worse than the dragon may now occupy their former home; Vukradin pivots and offers to escort them.
- Vorga chooses to stay and defend the lodge. Her reasoning is military and explicit: the non-combatants cannot move fast enough through open woods to escape a pursuing force. She points to the old orc to illustrate. The party floats various alternatives — Brewbarry carrying people, riding Boney, the elk — but Vorga's decision holds.
- Vorga states: "Cult summon creature today. We kill creature today. We safely go to Icespire." She explains that in the open, the creature attacks where it wants; in the fort, they know where it is coming from.
- Vorga suggests that someone stand on the palisade to signal the Falcon when he arrives, so he doesn't attack. Vukradin volunteers, noting warmly: "We're friends now. He apologized."
- Brewbarry investigates the lodge's interior and discovers the alcohol supply. The DM narrates: "You hear Brewbarry screaming in rage." He appears holding a broken cask, furious. The contents have all leaked onto the ground — wasted, not drunk. Vukradin rolls 21 persuasion to calm him down, promising more alcohol will be found. Brewbarry: "I will kill them all!" The Talosians have made a powerful enemy.
- The DM raises the note recovered at the Circle of Thunder commenting on "the boy from the East" as a good recruit. The party speculates the boy may in fact be Prutha — though they do not resolve the question.
- The party takes a short rest. Valphine uses her Channel Divinity to regain a spell slot. The party discusses remaining resources: Vukradin has burned two first-level and two third-level spell slots; Valphine has one third-level slot remaining. Valphine casts Aid at second level, adding 5 hit points to the party's maximum for eight hours.
- The Falcon arrives to find Vukradin on the palisade. He has come prepared for battle — war paint on his face, bow in hand. Vukradin calls down to him, waves him in, and explains the situation: the orcs are allies, they have agreed to help defend the lodge, and the plan is to deal with the incoming Talosians together. Falcon: "You have my sword." The party positions orc warriors with bows behind the arrow slits of the gatehouse tower roof — as many as can reasonably fight from there, settled at three.

### The Siege of Falcon's Hunting Lodge
#### An unnatural storm heralds the arrival of the Talosian cultists, and a massive lightning bolt shatters the lodge's gate, beginning the siege.
- The DM sets the scene: "The storm rolls in fast, not the slow gather of a henge. It's direct, impatient. The air pressure drops in your ears all at once. Outside the palisade, the sky goes from afternoon to evening in the span of one breath."
- Wind rises. The boars and other animals in the Falcon's pens panic and crash against the walls of their enclosure.
- Old Drubbak goes very still. He says: "It come wrong. There is no chant. Where is the chant?" — registering that an unchanted storm of this speed and intensity should not be possible. (Chronicle note: Grannoc's ritual at the Woodland Manse is driving the storm from miles to the south; the chant is real but too distant for Drubbak to perceive.)
- Lightning strikes: first the tree line 100 yards out, then the river, then inside the palisade itself — splitting an empty barrel. The DM notes: "This cult is here, but no Anchorite called this storm."
- Soma notices her staff sprouts a new leaf as the storm manifests around the lodge. She announces it to the group.
- Valphine casts Aid at second level before combat begins, increasing the party's hit point maximums by 5 for eight hours.
- The party establishes defensive positions: orc archers in the gatehouse tower behind arrow slits, the party spread through the courtyard, Boney at the stable house inside the palisade, Falcon in position with his bow.
- Initiative is rolled. Vukradin rolls a 22, going first in the order. Soma rolls a 20. The Falcon rolls a 7 (rolled by Vukradin on his behalf). The DM rolls for the orc groups and Gorthok, confirming that Gorthok is present among the attacking forces — a development the party had not anticipated arriving this early. The DM notes Gorthok has 74 hit points; the Anchorites have armor class 13 and 15 hit points each.
- From 120 feet out, a bolt of lightning — fired by the Talosian forces — screams out of the darkness and blasts the lodge's main gate. The DM draws the collapsed section on the map. The doors are destroyed. Talosian forces — Anchorites and Gorthok — begin converging on the broken entrance.
- The session ends on this cliffhanger. Vukradin, going first in the initiative order, has prepared Hypnotic Pattern as a likely opening move, noting with some frustration that bards do not get Fireball.

## Locations
### Falcon's Hunting Lodge
A two-story timber and stone building enclosed by a ten-foot-high wooden palisade, featuring a guest house, a stable house and smithy joined into one structure, an outhouse, and pens for animals. The palisade has been repaired in places with mismatched wood lashed with rough cords — evidence of occupiers improving the defenses rather than demolishing them. Currently serving as a fortified refugee camp for the displaced Eastern Heart orc tribe, it becomes the site of a major Talosian siege when a magical lightning bolt destroys the main gate at the session's end.

### Woodland Manse
Discussed by the party as the site where a summoning ritual is believed to be underway — the other pressing option before the party chose to head to the lodge instead. Chronicle note: Grannoc's ritual here is the actual source of the unnatural storm that strikes the lodge, though too distant for Old Drubbak to hear the accompanying chants.

### Circle of Thunder
The site where the party had been before heading to the lodge. The henge intel — Flenz's letters, Narux's sketches and map, Yargath's burned scrap — was recovered here last session. Among the recovered notes is one referencing "the boy from the East" as a recruit. The party does not know who this refers to and speculates it may be Prutha after encountering his family connections at the lodge.

### Gatehouse Tower
A defensive structure at Falcon's Hunting Lodge featuring arrow slits that provide a tactical advantage for ranged combatants. The party positioned three orc warriors here to defend against the Talosian assault. The DM confirms that combatants placed here are treated as being behind arrow slits.

### Icespire Hold
The ancestral home of the Eastern Heart orc tribe, from which they were driven by the dragon Cryovain. The party's revelation that Cryovain has been slain stuns the assembled courtyard into silence and shifts the entire negotiation. Cryovain is confirmed dead, but the party cautions that something worse may have moved into the hold in the dragon's absence — current occupancy status: unconfirmed.

Acting war chief of the Eastern Heart band squatting at Falcon's Hunting Lodge — the previous war chief was killed during the tribe's displacement, and Vorga now holds full operational authority over this band. Middle-aged female orc with gray at the temples, a broken nose, and a scar across her jaw from something old. She wears Falcon's stolen hunter cloak over orc-style leather. She speaks flawless orcish — the DM notes the in-universe explanation that orcs are highly intelligent and simply have a speech impediment in Common. She is pragmatic and militarily minded: when the party suggests the tribe leave, she refuses, citing her non-combatants' inability to outrun whatever is coming. Her position: "Cult summon creature today. We kill creature today. We safely go to Icespire." She becomes positively predisposed toward the party after receiving the Talosian warning and especially after learning Cryovain is dead.

### Old Drubbak
An elderly orc who leans on a spear like a walking stick. He is the first to look at Valphine's holy symbol and acknowledge the dawn, saying a single word in orcish: "Dawn." When the unnatural storm arrives, he is the first to sense something is wrong, going very still and saying: "It come wrong. There is no chant. Where is the chant?" His sensitivity to the ritual absence suggests significant spiritual experience.

### Gorthok
A formidable creature associated with the Talosian cult, confirmed by the DM to be present among the attacking forces at the lodge — arriving earlier than the party had anticipated. The DM notes his hit points differ from the Roll20 tracker; he has 74 hit points. His Narux sketches — four-legged silhouette with lightning along the tusks — are among the intel the party carried into the lodge.

### Anchorites of Talos
Cultists who worship the god of storms, referred to repeatedly as "Anchorites" — a word the DM acknowledges keeps tripping him up. They lead the Talosian assault on the lodge, arriving with the Talosian orc forces. Their opening strike — a lightning bolt fired from 120 feet — destroys the main gate. The DM notes they have armor class 13 and 15 hit points each, and that their positioning keeps them behind the front lines of the assault rather than leading it directly.

### Boney
The party's skeletal horse companion, brought along on the march to the lodge. Stabled in the lodge's stable house inside the palisade for the duration of negotiations and positioned near the stable door as the siege begins. Wade is emphatic that Boney not be forgotten: "Don't forget Boney!" The party briefly debates whether Boney also gets the Aid spell.
