# Voice Critique — Vukradin, scene 2: Planning the Next Move

**Narration:** `gm-assist-doc.md` (lines 70–152)
**Voice spec:** `voice/vukradin_new_pipeline.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/vukradin.md`

## Flags

### [1] Banned tic — "the shape of X" (twice in one scene)

> Infinite choices, technically. But the shape of the moment made it two.
>
> [and later]
>
> The shape of it was clear enough: a summoning ritual confirmed underway at the manse…

**Why:** `_genre.md` explicitly bans "the shape of X" as a Claude tic. Vukradin in particular names things — "Foolish!", "Phonies!", "evil" only when he means it. He does not gesture at "the shape of" something; he renders the verdict. Two hits in one POV section is the most concentrated violation in the doc.
**Suggested rewrite (first):** *Two, really. Manse or lodge.*
**Suggested rewrite (second):** *Their plan was clear enough: a summoning ritual confirmed underway at the manse, a backup ritual waiting…*

### [2] Voice spec conflict — lyrical magic when spec says procedural

> I felt the spell open between my hands — the reach and cost of it, the attention it required, the way it compressed everything I meant to say into a few precise words and sent them across whatever distance separated us.

**Why:** Spec: "He treats Sending the way a modern person treats texting — casual, ambient, used to keep the team connected across distance." And: "He speaks in clean procedural D&D mechanics when coordinating — hit points, ranges, durations, conditions, save DCs, spell slots." This sentence is a literary set-piece treating Sending as ritual magic. The examples never reach for "the spell open between my hands" energy — Vukradin opens a Sending the way someone opens an iMessage thread.
**Suggested rewrite:** *I burned a Sending — twenty-five words, one shot.*

### [3] Generic prose — workshopped fantasy simile

> There's a way to demand an apology that makes you look small — the aggrieved party rattling its grievances into a silence that has already moved on.

**Why:** "The aggrieved party rattling its grievances into a silence that has already moved on" is the literary-fiction narrator. Vukradin's voice is principled, not aphoristic — he calls people *Phonies!*; he doesn't compose epigrams about how grievance lands in silence. The spec warns: "Do not give him hardboiled realism… the world keeps disappointing him, and he has not concluded that disappointment is its nature."
**Suggested rewrite:** *Demanding an apology from a man who has already moved on makes you look small. I needed it to land differently.*

### [4] "Particular X of someone who" pattern (three hits in this scene)

> She looked at me with the particular quality of attention she uses when she's determining whether I'm being genuine or performative.
>
> [and]
>
> "Scenario 1," Valphine said, with the cadence of someone sorting options for their own amusement
>
> [and]
>
> Soma offered a fallback with the dry patience of someone who had seen too many apologies fail to materialize.

**Why:** This is a systemic Claude tic — naming someone's behavior by gesturing at "the X of someone who…" rather than rendering what Vukradin actually noticed. Vukradin watches *people*, not abstract behavioral signatures. Compare the examples: he reads the room by naming specifics ("the only human looks like a bandit and is wearing armor that he stole from a dead body") not by typing them.
**Suggested rewrite (first):** *Valphine looked at me the way she does when she's deciding whether I mean it.*
**Suggested rewrite (third):** *Soma gave me a clean out — clear the lodge, skip the protection. The kind of fallback she offers when she's already decided the apology won't come.*

### [5] Voice spec conflict — generic editor's-craft aside

> You learn to edit. The working only carries so much.

**Why:** "You learn to edit" is the narrator stepping outside Vukradin to deliver a craft observation about Sending. Vukradin would *think in word-count* (Sending is 25 words; he'd reference the limit directly) rather than offer "you learn to edit" as a generic life lesson. The line reads as the AI commenting on the constraint, not the character living inside it.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Twenty-five words. You learn to compress.*

### [6] Tell-not-show — the relief at the end

> It was a relief, if I'm being honest — not the apology itself, but that he'd given it cleanly. Valphine had done something with him beforehand; the readiness of it told me that. But the call had been mine, and the answer was yes, and now we could move without me carrying the weight of having let it go.

**Why:** "It was a relief, if I'm being honest" names the feeling instead of rendering it. Vukradin's emotional landings in the examples are verdict-shaped — *Done.* / *Phonies!* — not introspective. The "if I'm being honest" softener is also out of register: Vukradin doesn't soften, he updates and moves.
**Suggested rewrite:** *He apologized clean. Valphine had done the groundwork — I could feel it in how ready the answer was. But the ask had been mine. Done.*

## Verdict

Two "shape of" hits and a lyrical Sending paragraph are the biggest single offenders — they directly contradict the spec's bans on the tic and on ornate magic. Worth a targeted re-run or manual edit; the scene's bones (the principled Anita Hill move, the working list, the camp-or-go beat) are good Vukradin and worth preserving.
