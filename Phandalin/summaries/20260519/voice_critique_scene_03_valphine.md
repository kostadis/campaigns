# Voice Critique — Valphine, scene 3: Arrival at Falcon's Hunting Lodge

**Narration:** `gm-assist-doc.md` (lines 156–205)
**Voice spec:** `voice/valphine_new_pipeline.md`
**Per-char examples:** `examples/valphine.md`

## Flags

### [1] Generic prose — opening sentence drifts into aphorism

> The lodge looked the way I expected it to look, which is to say: occupied by people who had every reason to be afraid, and were doing something about it.

**Why:** Spec: "Her observations are sharp, and her conclusions arrive several steps ahead of everyone else's. She does not bother to explain the gap." This opener telegraphs the conclusion ("people afraid, doing something about it") instead of letting Valphine *see* and *catalogue*. Her examples open inside the calculation, not on the colon-and-summary structure. Also, "looked the way I expected it to look, which is to say" is a Claude framing tic — the self-correcting clause.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Two stories. Timber and stone. The palisade newer in patches than the wood it had been lashed to — repairs under pressure. Not vandals. Tenants.*

### [2] Voice spec conflict — Falcon backstory dump in close-third register

> I had been here before this moment, in a way. Not to this compound — but to the conversation I knew was coming. Falcon had trusted me enough to ask me to mediate his apology to Vukradin, which meant I knew something about the cost of what had happened here, and something about the shape of whatever came next. The dead partner. The attack the party had failed to prevent. The cloak that was probably still on its hook inside, unworn, because no one who mourns reaches for their lover's clothes.

**Why:** Two issues. (a) "the shape of whatever came next" — banned tic from `_genre.md`. (b) The whole paragraph is exposition the reader needs but Valphine wouldn't think — *she* knows the backstory; she wouldn't recap it to herself in this register. And the closing aphorism "no one who mourns reaches for their lover's clothes" is the literary narrator, not Valphine. Her drow grammar reads people as systems; she doesn't write greeting-card observations about grief.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Falcon had asked me to mediate his apology to Vukradin. That gave me the floor here in advance. The dead partner, the cloak left on its hook — I had inventoried all of that before we walked in.*

### [3] Generic prose — "I was wrong about the cloak" landing

> I was wrong about the cloak.

**Why:** This *should* be a strong Valphine landing — the spec says "If she is surprised, she names it directly and reassesses." But because the prior paragraph already telegraphed the expectation (cloak on a hook, unworn), the reversal lands as plot-mechanical rather than as a recalculation. The line is fine; the setup neutralizes it.
**Suggested rewrite:** Cut the prior paragraph's cloak sentence so this reversal earns its line. The four words can stand exactly as written.

### [4] Particular-X tic — softer here but present

> The older orc was already looking at me. He stood beside the woman — Vorga, I would learn — leaning on his spear with the ease of someone who had done that for decades and found it a more reliable support than younger men.

**Why:** "the ease of someone who had done that for decades and found it a more reliable support than younger men" is the same gestural construction flagged across the doc. Valphine's drow grammar reads *people* (this man, this hierarchy, this leverage) rather than the abstract category "someone who has done X for decades." The phrasing is fine prose; it's just not Valphine's lens — she'd commit to a specific judgment about *him*.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Vorga's elder. Leaning on his spear the way men do who have used it longer than they've used their sons.*

### [5] Voice spec conflict — generic possessive observation

> *Your drow.* Not "the drow." Possessive. Prutha's. I filed that.

**Why:** Strong instinct, weak rendering. Valphine the archivist of motive *should* notice the possessive — that part lands. But "I filed that" is also used in scene 4 (Soma) repeatedly, and the four-staccato-fragment rhythm ("Possessive. Prutha's. I filed that.") reads as the doc's universal Claude shorthand for "character is being analytical," not as Valphine specifically. Her examples don't fragment-bullet; they layer.
**Suggested rewrite:** *"Your drow" — possessive, not categorical. Prutha's. I noted what that meant for the alliance she thought she was joining.*

### [6] Voice register slip — "a painful dawn" works; the buildup softens it

> I keep my face composed by long practice — Menzoberranzan does not reward readable expressions, and the habit survives in me even now, even in the woods of the surface world with the sky open overhead. But something in my chest answered that word before I could assess it.

**Why:** "Something in my chest answered that word before I could assess it" is the only line in the scene that risks Valphine confessing pre-analytic emotion — spec says: "She does not confess emotion. She analyzes. If she names a feeling, it is because the analysis required it." The "something in my chest" framing is uncharacteristic; she would *name* what answered (the Searing Pain doctrine, the recognition of a shared faith calculus, the bodily revelation of her conversion) rather than gesture at a vague chest-feeling.
**Suggested rewrite:** *Menzoberranzan does not reward readable expressions; the habit holds even under the open surface sky. But the word *Dawn* in his mouth touched the bodily place my own conversion had started from. The doctrine answered before the analysis did.*

## Verdict

The strongest Valphine voice in the whole doc — Vorga's wall, the orcish exchange, the *painful dawn* landing all hold. The opener's colon-summary and the cloak/grief paragraph are the costliest drifts; both are the Claude-narrator overwriting the drow analyst. Spot-edit, don't re-run.
