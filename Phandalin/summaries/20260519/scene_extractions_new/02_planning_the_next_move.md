# Planning the Next Move

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party debates their next destination and Vukradin uses magic to demand an apology from Falcon before agreeing to help him.
- The DM presents the party with two primary options: go to the Woodland Manse to interrupt a summoning ritual, or go to Falcon's Hunting Lodge to deal with the infestation there.
- Vukradin declares immediately that he refuses to do anything more for Falcon without an apology for his conduct at the Druid Council. Wade suggests that if the apology isn't forthcoming, the party could take care of the infestation without actually protecting the lodge.
- Vukradin uses a Sending spell — his third-level slot — to contact Falcon, framing the message with the indignant tone he describes as "Justice Thomas's wife calling Anita Hill": informing him the party is considering dealing with his lodge problem, but demanding to know if he is ready to apologize first.
- Falcon accepts the magical contact and responds with a categorical, unambiguous apology — the DM confirms it is a "thank you and yes" — and tells the party he is on the road home and hopes to see them there. (Continuity: Falcon had previously asked Valphine to mediate this apology, and Valphine had laid groundwork with him at the druid conclave; the readiness of Falcon's "thank you and yes" reflects that prior work, even though the actual call was Vukradin's unilateral move.)
- The party reviews the henge intel: Flenz's sealed letters, Narux's vellum sketches of Gorthok and the marked regional map, and Yargath's half-burned scrap. The DM recaps the intelligence: a summoning ritual is confirmed underway; it is one of two being attempted; if the first fails, a second will be used; if that fails, whoever is in charge will attempt the summoning themselves.
- The party puzzles over why the Talosians — who previously controlled the lodge — would now want to attack it.
- Soma and Valphine roll Nature and Religion checks respectively to assess whether both rituals could be running simultaneously: the DM rules that given the complexity of running rituals at two locations at once, only one is likely active at any given time.
- Prutha is consulted about the three orc factions in the region: those who stayed with the tribe (Eastern Heart refugees), those who aligned with the Talosians, and independent free agents. He confirms what the party already knows but adds that the Anchorites moved their own people through the area.
- The party notes their depleted spell slots and discusses the two-hour walk to the lodge. Valphine wants to take a long rest to prepare new spells; the DM clarifies the lodge is only about two hours away, not a full day's journey. The party discusses resistance to lightning — no one in the party has it prepared.
- They resolve to head to the lodge and make do with reduced magical reserves rather than delay. Valphine notes they should approach from the south.
