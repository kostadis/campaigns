# Arrival at Falcon's Hunting Lodge

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party arrives at the fortified hunting lodge and negotiates entry with a group of orcs who have taken up residence there.
- The party approaches the lodge from the south. The DM describes a two-story timber and stone building with a guest house, a stable house and smithy joined into one structure, an outhouse, and pens. A wooden palisade ten feet high encloses the compound.
- The palisade is intact but has been repaired in places with mismatched wood, newer planks lashed in with rough cords — evidence that whoever holds the lodge has been improving its defenses, not demolishing them.
- Three figures stand on the walkway behind the palisade, watching the treeline. Two have bows drawn but not raised; the third is shouting something into the courtyard.
- Prutha spots the banners and calls out: "Eastern Heart! Three banners on the wall! I see one! Sun and Stone! My uncle's clan march! They are family!" He immediately moves to approach. (Three distinct clan-marks fly on the palisade; Prutha recognizes Sun and Stone as one of them — his uncle's clan. The other two banners are not yet identified.)
- Prutha walks to the palisade alone at a deliberate pace, hands open. At thirty feet from the gate, he performs the dawn-bow: right fist to left shoulder, head down. He pulls a bone whistle from inside his armor and blows the dawn prayer.
- The sentry on the palisade lowers her bow, returns the gesture, and shouts to the rest of the band in orcish. She then calls out to Prutha — and the answer back is relayed: "Bring your drow, bring your Aasimar (Vukradin), bring all of them. We are listening."
- The party enters. Soma leaves Boney near the stables inside the palisade before the group moves into the courtyard proper.
- Inside: women with babies, old men around a cookfire, children playing a game with carved sticks near the wall, maybe a dozen warriors among approximately thirty orcs total. The DM describes: "They watch you the way refugees watch anyone who walks into their armed camp."
- A middle-aged orc woman with gray at the temples, a broken nose, and a scar across her jaw comes down from the walkway. She wears Falcon's hunter cloak — taken from his closet — over orc-style leather. Wade notes the stolen clothing; the DM asks if anyone is going to call it out, and the group decides to let it slide.
- Beside her stands a much older orc leaning on his spear like a walking stick. He looks directly at Valphine's holy symbol and says a single word in orcish: "Dawn." Valphine confirms she speaks Orcish, and responds: "Yes, I'm the bringer of the dawn. A painful dawn."
