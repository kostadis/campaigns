# Chapter 35: The Carver's Command

Date: Mar 25th, 2026

## Summary
The party pressed onward through biting winds and treacherous slopes, climbing the jagged, snow-covered flanks of Icespire Peak — a mountain so immense it could be seen from Phandalin on a clear day. At its summit loomed Icespire Hold, a stone fortress split into two structures connected by a narrow bridge, its battlements half-swallowed by drifting clouds. As the adventurers surveyed the approach, they spotted the white dragon Cryovain sleeping atop the main fortress roof, its pale scales nearly indistinguishable from the snow around it. A winding path corkscrewed up the mountainside to the smaller gatehouse, but the party debated how to enter and had no intention of walking through the front door.

Rather than take the obvious route, the group chose stealth over speed. Soma shifted into the form of a giant wolf spider and skittered up a sheer fifty-foot cliff of ice, dropping a rope for the others to follow. Once up, Soma continued her climb to the roof to spy on Cryovain. What she saw chilled her more than the mountain air: Cryovain was bound within a glowing summoning circle, its massive body held in place by magical chains, its eyes wild and unfocused. The Carver stood over the dragon, deep in the throes of a dark ritual, his obsidian sword pulsing with a sickly purple light. Soma could sense the dragon's state — a seething, psychotic rage barely contained by the ritual's power — and knew that releasing it without care would be catastrophic. She returned to the party with this crucial information.

The party then explored the first floor of Icespire Hold, slipping into the fortress through the rubble-strewn ruins left by an old earthquake. They moved through crumbling hallways where Cryovain had once smashed through doors and walls killing orcs, and discovered the old home of the orcs — a filth-covered room whose furnishings had long since burned in the fireplace. Valphine, ever perceptive, discovered a secret door where the orcs were living. However, armed with Soma's intelligence, the party decided to stop exploring and go upstairs to the roof to confront the Carver directly.

Valphine acted swiftly, casting a field of magical silence over the summoning circle and cutting off the ritual mid-incantation. Combat erupted immediately, and the Carver proved far more dangerous than a simple spellcaster — he moved with uncanny speed, disengaging from Brewbarry's reach before the dwarf could land a blow. Vukradin seized the moment and paralyzed the Carver with a powerful enchantment, leaving the man frozen in place. 

Abandoning the careful ritual, the Carver cast a direct domination over the dragon, and the magical chains dissolved. Vurkadin was in shock. How could a paralyzed human cast dominate a monster? A voice rang out in the minds of every party member — cold, triumphant, and utterly inhuman: "Cowards. You mute the ritual because you lack the strength to shatter the vessel. And now I shall have everything I always wanted." In that moment, the purple glow faded from the obsidian sword, leaving it looking like nothing more than a plain black blade — and an arcane flash of insight struck Soma: the Carver was never the man. The Carver was the sword. The human veteran had been nothing but a vessel all along, and the sentient blade had now partially seized control of Cryovain, though the dragon's own madness still warred against it. The party had three precious rounds before the sword's grip became absolute.

Valphine struck first, lancing the dragon with a bolt of radiant light that left it momentarily exposed. Soma abandoned her spider form and surged into the shape of a great bear, positioning herself for close combat. Brewbarry, roaring with battle fury, drew the dragon-slaying sword and charged — landing two devastating blows that sent Cryovain reeling. The dragon answered with a thunderous blast of freezing breath that engulfed Brewbarry in a cone of ice and frost, then beat its wings and climbed thirty feet into the air, hovering above the battlements and out of easy reach. The party scrambled for cover behind the fortress walls, arrow slits their only shelter, as the battle hung in the balance with the clock ticking down on the sword's domination of the great white dragon.

## NPCs
### Cryovain
A young white dragon whose scales blend with the snow, found chained within a summoning circle atop Icespire Hold. The Carver was performing a ritual to consume and dominate it. When the ritual was interrupted, Cryovain's magical chains dissolved and it entered a state of homicidal, psychotic rage — partially dominated by the sentient sword but still lashing out at everything around it, including breathing frost at Brewbarry and taking to the air.

### The Carver
Revealed to be a sentient black obsidian sword that uses a human veteran as a vessel. It was performing a dark ritual to consume and fully dominate Cryovain. When the party interrupted the ritual, it abandoned the careful approach and directly cast a domination spell on the dragon, declaring victory even as the party fought back. The sword lost its purple glow after the ritual was disrupted, appearing as a plain black obsidian blade.

### The Veteran
The human vessel possessed and used by the Carver. He was paralyzed by Vukradin during the initial moments of the confrontation and remained immobile throughout the early combat.

### Sister Kaella
Referenced in the session through recalled information — she had previously told the party that killing the Carver would cause everything to fall, and that they should 'chop off the head and the monster will fall.' She was left incapacitated by the party in a prior session.

## Locations
### Icespire Peak
A massive, jagged, snow-covered mountain so enormous it is visible from Phandalin on a clear day. It features a stone fortress at its summit and a 200-foot sheer ice wall as an alternative route.

### Icespire Hold
A stone fortress situated atop the icy spur of Icespire Peak, consisting of two separate structures joined by a stone bridge. It features rubble-filled halls from an old earthquake, arrow slits, a room formerly occupied by orcs, and a rooftop summoning circle where the Carver performed his ritual over Cryovain.

### Phandalin
A settlement visible from Icespire Peak on a clear day, referenced as the landmark from which the mountain can be seen.

## Items

Magical beads capable of producing fire, which Brewbarry possesses. The party repeatedly suggested using them during the climb and approach, but Brewbarry firmly refused each time.

A standard climbing tool used by the party to ascend the 50-foot icy cliffs after Soma scaled them in giant wolf spider form and dropped it down.

### Dragon Slaying Sword
A longsword with special properties designed to deal devastating extra damage to dragons. Brewbarry wielded it during the confrontation with Cryovain, landing two significant blows against the enraged white dragon.

A sentient black obsidian sword revealed to be the true identity of the villain known as the Carver. It had previously glowed with a purple light during the ritual, but after the ritual was interrupted it appeared as a plain, dark blade. It used a human veteran as a vessel and sought to dominate and consume the dragon Cryovain.
