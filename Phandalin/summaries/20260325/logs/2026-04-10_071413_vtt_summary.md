## Session Summary

# Session — 2026-04-10

## Overview
The party reached the summit of Icespire Peak and infiltrated Icespire Hold by bypassing the gatehouse entirely — Soma scaled a cliff in giant wolf spider form and dropped a rope for the others. Inside, they discovered the white dragon Cryovain chained in a summoning circle on the rooftop, with the Carver performing a dark ritual over it. Moving to confront the Carver, Vukradin cast Silence to disrupt the ritual and then paralyzed the human veteran with Hold Person. But the true Carver — revealed to be the sentient black obsidian sword, not the man — abandoned its careful ritual and cast Dominate directly on the dragon, dissolving the chains and partially seizing control of Cryovain. The party now has three rounds to kill the dragon before the sword's domination becomes absolute. After a furious first round of combat — Brewbarry landing devastating blows with the Dragon Slayer Sword, Cryovain retaliating with a freezing breath weapon and taking to the air — the session ended mid-fight with two rounds remaining.

## Session Events
- Party completed the climb to Icespire Peak without exhaustion (checks resolved in prior session).
- Arrived at Icespire Hold: two stone structures joined by a bridge atop the peak, visible from Phandalin on clear days.
- Observed Cryovain sleeping on the main fortress rooftop, white scales camouflaged in snow.
- Spotted the Carver's trail leading to the smaller gatehouse; chose to bypass it via stealth.
- Rejected the 200-foot sheer ice wall route due to lack of climbing equipment.
- Soma Wild Shaped into a giant wolf spider, scaled a 50-foot icy cliff, and dropped a rope; all party members climbed up successfully (DC 7).
- Entered the fortress through earthquake rubble, bypassing the gatehouse entirely.
- Moved through a hallway where Cryovain had previously smashed through searching for prey.
- Discovered a former orc encampment room (filth, burnt furnishings) — consistent with known orc displacement by the dragon.
- Valphine rolled a 29 Insight check and found a secret door leading deeper into the hold.

- Soma observed Cryovain chained in a summoning circle, zombified, with the Carver performing a ritual. Animal handling check (24) revealed the dragon was in seething psychotic rage. Failed Arcana check yielded no magical insight.
- Party decided to head directly to confront the Carver rather than explore further.
- **Combat begins on the rooftop.** Vukradin cast Silence centered on the summoning circle, disrupting the ritual. Initiative: Vukradin won tiebreaker vs. the Carver (16 vs 11).
- Brewbarry raged and dashed but couldn't reach the Carver due to wall routing.

- Despite the veteran being paralyzed, the sentient sword cast Dominate on Cryovain. The chains dissolved. The sword's voice echoed telepathically: *"Cowards, magic, you mute the ritual because you lack the strength to shatter the vessel. And now, now I shall have everything I always wanted."*
- Soma's Arcana check (13) revealed the critical truth: **the Carver was always the sword; the human was just a vessel.** The sword had been performing a ritual to consume the dragon; interrupted, it used Dominate as a desperate backup with incomplete control. The party has **3 rounds** to kill the dragon before full domination.

- Valphine cast Guiding Bolt on Cryovain: 15 radiant damage, granting advantage on next attack.
- Soma used spider form to climb over a wall, then Wild Shaped into bear form (second and final charge).
- Brewbarry attacked Cryovain twice with the Dragon Slayer Sword: 21 damage (with advantage from Guiding Bolt) and 22 damage. **Total: 43 damage.**
- Cryovain retaliated with cold breath (30-foot cone) targeting Brewbarry: 40 damage, reduced to 30 after Stone's Endurance. Rage does NOT reduce cold damage.
- Cryovain flew 30 feet into the air, hovering above the battlements.
- The paralyzed veteran failed to break free of Hold Person on his turn.
- Vukradin, with limited ranged options against the airborne dragon, cast Thorn Whip and scored a **critical hit for 26 damage**.
- **Session ended after round 1 of 3** in the domination countdown.

## Voice Keeper Notes

- **The Carver (sentient sword):** Telepathic voice — imperious, contemptuous, speaks in measured declarative sentences. Frames all conflict as strength vs. cowardice. Should reference the party's "coward's magic" (Silence, Hold Person) in any future taunting. The sword is now bonded to Cryovain — its voice should carry draconic resonance going forward.

- **Vukradin's naivety is real, not performative.** Dave explicitly corrected the GM's assumption that Vukradin was pragmatically ignoring Corbin's behavior. Future NPCs who assume cynicism from Vukradin should be met with genuine confusion. He is also actively trying to reform Corbin — this should be referenced.

- **Soma operates as the party's strategic brain.** She scouts, assesses consequences, and plans routes. NPCs should treat her as the one who asks the hard questions ("is he load-bearing?"). Her dry humor masks genuine anxiety about cascading consequences.

- **Valphine is increasingly a voice for pragmatic alliances** — "enemy of my enemy" logic. Future NPCs with morally ambiguous proposals should find her the most receptive audience.

- **Brewbarry's fireball boundary is absolute.** He gave Vukradin the Necklace of Fireballs but will not use them himself. Vukradin covets them but respects the boundary... barely. This tension should persist — any NPC who suggests using them will trigger a party dynamic.

- **Vow/Promise:** Vukradin declared he is "saving the world" — played for laughs but sincere in intent. The party has committed to killing both the Carver (the sword) and the dragon within a three-round window. Any survival of either entity should reference this moment.

- **The dragon Cryovain is in "seething psychotic rage"** — if it survives, it will attack everything indiscriminately. Soma explicitly warned the party not to unchain it. Any future encounter with a freed Cryovain should carry the weight of that warning.

- **Political thread:** The party has casually committed to orc repatriation of Icespire Hold ("Eastern Homeland Reclaiming"). This could become a genuine diplomatic subplot or come back to haunt them.
