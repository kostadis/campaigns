## Brewbarry — Battle Against Cryovain

The sword was already in my hand before Vukradin said it.

"Crewbury, kill the dragon."

I didn't correct the name. There wasn't time, and also — he could call me whatever he wanted. He always could.

What I needed was the Dragon Slayer Sword. I'd been carrying it for this. For exactly this. The weight of it felt different now, with the dragon filling the sky above the battlements, all white fury and snapping teeth. I could feel the cold coming off it like a wall. I had the sword. That was enough to move.

Valphine had already put something into Cryovain — a bolt of radiant light that hit like a small sun, and I saw the dragon flinch and reorient, its attention briefly scattered. Good. That was the door. Soma was already repositioning — I caught the moment she shifted form, the spider giving way to something larger, heavier, a bear hauling itself into close range. She burned both her remaining wild shapes to do it. I heard her mark it off without complaint. That was worth me sacking around for. That's what she said. I filed it away. Soma never wastes her breath.

I went straight at the dragon.

The first blow landed clean. The Dragon Slayer Sword connected with something that wasn't just scale — there was resistance, and then there wasn't, and I felt the impact travel all the way up my arms in the best way, the way that means you've done something real. The second blow came right after. Also clean. Both of them, landing the way I always hope they land and rarely do. The sword was made for this. I was made for this. In that moment, neither of those facts felt strange.

Cryovain did not appreciate it.

The dragon's head swung toward me. The mouth opened.

The cold that came out of it wasn't like winter. I know winter. I grew up in it. This was something that winter would run from — a cone of frost that hit me full in the face and didn't stop, pushing through my armor, pushing through the rage that was supposed to keep me standing, finding the places underneath where things are softer and hurting them. I didn't go down. I am not saying it didn't hurt. I am saying I didn't go down, and that felt like the most important fact available to me in that moment.

What I noticed, when I could notice things again, was that the dragon was moving.

It beat its wings once and lifted — thirty feet straight up, pulling itself above the battlements, out of easy reach. Out of my reach. I looked up at it. I looked at my sword. I could calculate the distance, and the calculation was not good. I am not a calculator, normally, but I know when something is outside of what I can do, and a thirty-foot leap at a white dragon while I was still feeling the frost settle into my chest — that was outside.

Vukradin had taken cover behind a section of stone wall, tucking himself against it, looking out through one of the arrow slits. I could see his face and I could see what was on it. He was doing the thing he does when he's looking at a problem that doesn't have clean edges. I recognized it. He doesn't like problems without clean edges. He looked at me, he looked at Soma, he looked at whatever Cryovain was doing forty feet above us.

"I got nothing, guys," he said. "Other than Fireball."

Soma said: "You're actually gonna use it?"

"Nope."

I understood that. The fireball was his. It came from him. He'd made that choice about it already, somewhere private, and the choice was no. I didn't argue. I don't argue with Vukradin about what he keeps for himself. He has kept things back from me that he's told other people, and I have kept things back from him that I've told no one, and we understand each other on this.

But I could see him working. His jaw was tight. He was thinking something through.

What he landed on was a thorn whip.

I want to be honest about my first reaction, which was: a thorn whip. Against a dragon that had just breathed frost on me and was now circling thirty feet overhead with contempt in every wingbeat. A thorn whip is something you use on things that are afraid of thorns. I was not confident this dragon was afraid of thorns.

Then it hit.

Not just hit — it was the kind of hit that lands so perfectly that even the thing being hit seems surprised. The whip caught Cryovain somewhere important and the dragon made a sound that was not the sound of something in contempt. It was the sound of something that had just been reminded it could be hurt.

Vukradin went, "I hit! I even got a critical!"

He said it the way you say something when you didn't quite believe it was possible and then it happened and now you have to reorganize your face around the new reality. I know that feeling. It's a good feeling.

"Crushed it!" he said.

I looked up at the dragon. I looked back at Vukradin. I still had the Dragon Slayer Sword. The frost was still settling somewhere in my chest. We had time — not much time, the window was counting down, the Carver was threading something dark into the edges of this fight, but we had *some* time — and Vukradin had just put a thorn through a white dragon's pride with a piece of vine.

I have seen a lot of things. That was a good one.
