- Witty, sarcastic, and theatrical — oscillates between mock-heroic declarations and dry tactical pessimism. Loves creative problem-solving even when the rules shut him down. Celebrates his own successes with gusto and narrates his despair with equal flair.
- Maintains his moral positioning even mid-dungeon, casually referencing his ongoing project to reform Corbin and his willful ignorance of unsavory realities.

> "I got nothing, guys. Other than Fireball." / "You're actually gonna use it?" / "Nope."

### Soma (Wade)
- Pragmatic, dry, and cautious — the voice of strategic restraint. Approaches every situation by weighing consequences before committing. Delivers deadpan humor under pressure and quietly celebrates when others succeed because of her sacrifice.

> "Go, guys, don't, don't unlock the dragon."
> "That was worth me sacking around for. I'm not upset about it at all. Yep."

### Valphine (Gary)

- Loyal and straightforward, speaks through action rather than words. His offer of the fireball beads to Vukradin is pure uncomplicated generosity.
> "You have my sword, you have my halberd, the beads are yours."

### The Carver / The Sword (GM)
- Cold, contemptuous, and triumphant. Speaks telepathically with an echoing quality. Frames the party's tactics as cowardice, then pivots instantly to seize what he truly wanted all along.
> "Coward's magic, you mute the ritual because you lack the strength to shatter the vessel. And now, now I shall have everything I always wanted."

### GM (Kostadis) — Narration

## Standout Moments

- **Vukradin casts Silence on the summoning circle**, sacrificing his concentration to disrupt the Carver's ritual. It's a bold, world-saving play — that backfires catastrophically when the Carver simply abandons the ritual and dominates the dragon directly. The moment where heroism creates a worse problem.

- **Soma warns the party not to free the dragon** after rolling a 24 Animal Handling check and reading Cryovain's psychotic rage. Her instinct to understand before acting — "is he load-bearing on the dragon" — defines her cautious leadership.

- **The Carver is revealed to be the sword, not the man.** Soma's Arcana check (13) cracks the mystery: "Yeah, the human is just a vessel." The party's entire understanding of the enemy shifts in a single round, and the tone pivots from triumph to dread.

- **Vukradin lands a critical Thorn Whip on the airborne dragon**, dealing 26 damage in a moment of pure catharsis after declaring he had nothing useful to contribute. The table erupts — GM included: *"You gotta be joking."* Wade cheers: *"Way to go, face!"*

- **Soma burns both wild-shape charges** in a single tactical sequence — spider to climb, then bear to fight the dragon — accepting the cost without complaint. Her quiet "That's all I get" carries real weight.

- **Brewbarry takes 40 points of cold breath damage** without rage reduction, weathering the hit with Stone's Endurance. The mechanical reality of fighting a dragon lands hard.

- **Valphine rolls a 29 Insight** and discovers the secret door, showcasing her uncanny perceptiveness. The party debates whether to explore it, then Soma cuts through: *"We're not fucking around."*

## Voice Keeper Notes

- **The Carver (Sword):** Speaks with cold telepathic contempt. Frames opposition as weakness. Uses "coward" as a philosophical accusation, not an insult. Should continue to taunt the party's strategies as insufficient — he sees himself as inevitable. Key line to reference: *"And now, now I shall have everything I always wanted."*

- **Vukradin's Fireball Refusal** is now a recurring bit with real dramatic weight. He consistently refuses to use the Necklace of Fireballs even in desperate circumstances. Future NPCs and encounters should test this principle — the beads are Chekhov's gun.

- **Soma's "load-bearing" caution** should inform how NPCs deliver information to the party. She wants to understand systemic consequences before acting. Reward this with meaningful Arcana/Nature checks; punish rushing.

- **Vukradin's willful naivety about Corbin's methods** is an active tension point. Valphine's "This is fine" signals she sees through it. Future Corbin encounters should force Vukradin to confront what he's been ignoring.

- **The domination timer on Cryovain** is active — three rounds to kill the dragon before the Carver (sword) gains full control. This is the immediate cliffhanger entering next session.

- **Sister Kaella's unfinished warning** — "Chop off the head and the monster will fall" — was never actually delivered to the party in-character (Vukradin attacked her first). The GM introduced it as recalled knowledge this session. Vukradin protested: *"She never said that."* This meta-tension about what the party "knows" vs. what was interrupted should be handled carefully.

- **Brewbarry's simple loyalty** ("You have my sword, you have my halberd, the beads are yours") contrasts with Vukradin's quiet aside: *"That's what he thinks. Bless his heart."* This dynamic — Brewbarry's earnest generosity, Vukradin's affectionate condescension — is a core party texture to preserve.
