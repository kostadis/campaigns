# Session — 2026-04-10

## Overview
The party completed their assault on Icespire Hold, slaying Cryovain the white dragon with Valphine's Sacred Flame after Brewbarry weathered two blasts of freezing breath on 6 hit points and Vukradin converted a miss into a telling blow via Bardic Inspiration. The Carver's essence dissipated the instant the dragon died. A brief moral debate followed over the fate of the Carver's paralyzed bandit-vessel; Soma argued for mercy, Brewbarry killed him anyway. The party looted a 3,000 gp treasure slag fused into residium-infused ice by planar trickery (all identifying marks destroyed) and recovered the Carver's obsidian sword, whose pommel crystal is a mental amplifier the party immediately dubbed "the Bluetooth Sword." Before the party could rest, Sylvine Wintermoon and her sister Runa arrived claiming the slag as their own; when Vukradin refused and accused them of being thieves, they attacked with their veteran guards. The skirmish ended with two veterans killed (paralyzed by Hold Person, finished by Brewbarry), one fled citing better career prospects, and Sylvine and Runa's fate unresolved in the notes. Vukradin sent word to Ser Kaelen; Kaelen replied with an urgent summons to Phandalin, citing problems with the Emerald Enclave. The party set course for Phandalin.

## Session Events

**Dragon Fight — Cryovain and the Carver**
- Combat opened with Cryovain airborne at 30 feet, partially possessed; ~2 rounds before domination became irreversible.

- The Carver's essence dissipates entirely on the dragon's death.

## Character Voices

### Vukradin (David Mendenhall)
- Operates in two registers that collide constantly: the principled idealist who insists on conflict-free gold and proper provenance, and the exhausted pragmatist who just wanted a music studio and somehow ended up saving the world. The comedy lives in the gap between them.
- Dismissals are flat and final. When he's done with someone, he doesn't argue — he pronounces. Accusations of theft are returned without heat, just weariness.
- Prone to immediate, unilateral renaming of things. Does not negotiate on this.

> "I just wanted my goddamn music studio, and I ended up having to go save the world."

> "I am enraged at all the corruption and evil that is stinying me from my music studio."

> "It is absolutely the Bluetooth sword. Done."

---

### Brewbarry (Stéphane Bourdeaud)
- Short declarative sentences. Goliath rhythm — subject, verb, object, done. Victory boasts are formal, almost ceremonial, as though upholding a tradition that requires proper wording.
- Operates on a binary: things that are honorable (fighting, winning, celebrating) and things that are not (phonies, people who lie about treasure). Contempt is blunt, not cruel.
- Celebration and violence are continuous — he wants to drink, he wants to fight, and he sees no meaningful difference between one ending and the other beginning.

> "Root Smasher stands, you don't. That's the story."

> "Try not to die too quickly, I want to enjoy this."

> "I was thirsty."

---

### Soma (Wade Brown)
- Speaks in naturalist generalities when reasoning aloud — scale ("the plane of existence") over specifics. Uses this to deflect credit, blame, and attention equally.
- Dry, reluctant self-awareness. When forced to confront a contradiction in her own values, she acknowledges it simply and moves on without drama.
- Objects to killing on principle, but briefly. She says her piece once and does not repeat herself.

> "Look, the universe is safe, right? Like, or, you know, the plane of existence is safe — that's what matters most, nothing else."

> "I… apparently I am."

---

### Sylvine Wintermoon (GM)
- Arrives with theatrical warmth, pivots to theatrical offense, pivots to open violence — all within the same scene, with complete conviction at each stage. Registers shift fast and without shame.
- Uses the language of providence and blessing as rhetorical cover for naked opportunism. "Blessed be" is her partner's job; her job is the pitch.

> "Runa, didn't I tell you that that saint, Vukradin, would come and get our treasure for us? I knew it, I knew that if all we had to do was pray to the gods, a kind soul like Vukradin would come here and deal with that dragon, and give our treasure back."

> "Second plan! We kill them all!"

---

### Ser Kaelen Thorn (GM)
- Formal, elevated register. Uses titles, passive constructions, and abstract nouns ("righteous victory," "conflict-free triumph") that feel drafted rather than spoken. Information density is low; rhetorical polish is high.
- Confirms useful facts ("come to Phandalin, issues with the Emerald Enclave") then immediately wraps them in ceremony that makes everyone suspicious.

> "Praise Torm! Your righteous victory brings light to the north. The gauntlet awaits your arrival in Phandalin. We shall celebrate your conflict-free triumph together."

---

### The Carver (GM — brief in-character riff)
- Self-pitying alien logic. Frames conquest and annihilation as simple personal ambition being rudely interrupted. Adopts the cadence of a reasonable person who has been misunderstood, while listing atrocities in parentheses.

> "You know, here I am — I'm just an alien entity trying to take possession of a dragon, and you guys are too rude to just let me be. It's not like I was gonna do anything to anyone other than burn down your favorite town and kill and maim and, you know, try to build up my own despotic empire. Naturalists would have let me go."

## Standout Moments

- **Vukradin's exhausted dismissal of Sylvine.** "There's nobody left worthy" — delivered not as a villain line but as a genuine expression of moral fatigue. He came up here to fight a dragon and return treasure, and the treasure immediately sprouted claimants. The line captures everything wrong with trying to be principled in a world full of opportunists.

- **Brewbarry's formal victory declaration.** "Root Smasher stands, you don't. That's the story." — Delivered after cutting down the last veteran, it functions as a tribal epitaph. Brief, complete, requiring nothing after it.

- **Soma's quiet concession.** When told she has become an interventionist, she doesn't argue the point or justify herself. "I… apparently I am." Four words that contain an entire ideological shift.

- **The Bluetooth Sword.** Vukradin finds an obsidian sword with a mental amplifier crystal — clearly significant loot — and within thirty seconds has renamed it after wireless technology. The GM's frustrated "there was a lot of care and attention that went into the selection of that phrase" makes it funnier. The name will stick.

- **Valphine's Sacred Flame kills the dragon.** The party spent the session mocking the spell. It ended the fight. The GM's delivery — "It's 3, so he died" — was perfectly flat.

- **The Copper Wire pun.** The Sending spell requires copper wire as a material component. Per. Phone. Kostadis held this until after the session. David got it immediately. A very good bit.

**Vukradin's current emotional state:** Morally exhausted. NPCs offering him shortcuts or compromises should expect flat dismissal — but it is starting to cost him something to keep saying no. The music studio is the concrete symbol of everything he is deferring. Reference it to apply pressure.
