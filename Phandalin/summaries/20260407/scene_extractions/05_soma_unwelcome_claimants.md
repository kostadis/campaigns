[Scene 5] Unwelcome Claimants
Narrator: Soma
Focus: Soma watches Sylvine praise Vukradin as a saint sent by the gods and sees, with her 22-insight clarity, exactly how this cynical performance will unfold before anyone else in the room does.

- Sylvine Wintermoon and her sister Runa appear, praising Vukradin and claiming the treasure slag belongs to them.
<!-- A red-haired woman appears in the fortress after the dragon's defeat and addresses Vukradin -->
Sylvine: "Are you Vukridin the Bard? The one who returns the treasure to those that it has been stolen from?"
<!-- Responding to Sylvine's flattering introduction -->
Vukradin: "No, I haven't done very well with the return."
<!-- Turning to her sister, praising Vukradin as a divine instrument -->
Sylvine: "Runa, didn't I tell you that that saint, Vukradin, would come and get our treasure for us? I knew it, I knew that if all we had to do was pray to the gods, a kind soul like Vukradin would come here and deal with that dragon, and give our treasure back."
<!-- Nodding in support of her sister's claim -->
Runa: "Blessed be! Blessed be!"
<!-- Drily observing the speed of this claim -->
Brewbarry: "Well, that's the quickest we've ever found treasure's owners before."

- Vukradin expresses deep skepticism regarding their claim, accusing them of being thieves and phonies.
<!-- Flatly rejecting Sylvine's claim -->
Vukradin: "Lady, I don't know who you are, I don't know how you think that all 3,000 gold that this dragon hoarded over its long life is somehow yours, but I don't really believe you. <!-- Reacting with theatrical offense -->
Sylvine: "I am hurt. You are a thief!"
<!-- Returning the accusation with exhausted contempt -->
Vukradin: "I am tired of all these liars and cheats in this land. Get out of here. There's nobody left worthy."
<!-- Agreeing with Vukradin's assessment -->
Brewbarry: "Phonies, these goddamn phonies!"

- Insulted by Vukradin's refusal and accusations, Sylvine, Runa, and their veteran companions initiate an attack on the party.
<!-- Abandoning the theft attempt and calling for violence -->

<!-- Entering rage and addressing the paralyzed enemies -->

<!-- Fleeing the battle and offering his rationale -->
