[Scene 4] The Spoils of Victory
Narrator: Vukradin
Focus: Vukradin finds the mental amplifier crystal — a perfect instrument for his studio dream — and immediately declares it stolen goods he cannot keep, which is the most Vukradin thing that has ever happened to him.

- The party discovers a block of treasure worth roughly 3,000 gold pieces, consisting of coins, gems, and trade bars fused together in residium-infused ice and melted beyond recognition.

- Soma identifies that the ice was fused through some kind of planar trickery, suggesting the need for a planar theorist.

- Vukradin discovers the obsidian sword's crystal pommel acts as a mental amplifier capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience, but expresses deep concern over its power and declares the gem must be returned to its owner.

<!-- Discovering the obsidian sword's mental amplifier crystal and immediately recognizing its implications for his studio dream -->
Vukradin: "It seems like this was sent by the devil to tempt me, yes. Yes, save me."
<!-- After the GM carefully explains the crystal is a "mental amplifier," not Bluetooth -->
Vukradin: "Handcrafted for my personal quest, got it, thank you."
<!-- Immediately and definitively renaming the sword despite GM protest -->
Vukradin: "It is absolutely the Bluetooth sword. Done."
<!-- Protesting the nickname with evident frustration at the players -->
GM: "Hey, there was a lot of care and attention that went into the selection of that phrase."

- Vukradin declares the gold treasure is stolen goods, and the party discusses the moral implications of its ownership and the likelihood that Harbin Wester and the Lord's Alliance will try to claim credit for their victory.
<!-- On why she doesn't care that Harbin Wester will steal credit for the dragon kill -->
Soma: "Look, the universe is safe, right? Like, or, you know, the plane of existence is safe, that's what matters most, nothing else."
<!-- Justifying his decision to kill the veteran despite Soma's protest -->
Vukradin: "I am enraged at all the corruption and evil that is keeping me from my music studio."

<!-- On the question of whether the melted treasure slag is stolen -->
Vukradin: "What, so that means it's not stolen? Like, because you scratched the serial number off? Like a criminal would? Exactly."

- The party learns that by choosing to hunt the dragon, they missed the window to return a statue to an art collector in Neverwinter before House Margaster interfered.
<!-- Explaining the consequence of the party choosing the dragon over the Neverwinter statue delivery -->
Narrator: "There was a choice you had to make. You made a choice... The particular individual that you wanted to return it to is no longer available for you to return it."
<!-- Reacting to the news that House Margaster has killed the art collector -->
Vukradin: "Well, then that, you know, fuck them. We gotta go... Her next of kin? And they're in Neverwinter."
<!-- Challenging Vukradin's moral certainty -->
narrator: "You could have your music studio. All you have to do is compromise a few principles here and there."
<!-- Defending himself -->
Vukradin: "You don't get it."
<!-- Explaining his frustration -->
Vukradin: "I just wanted my goddamn music studio, and I ended up having to go save the world."
