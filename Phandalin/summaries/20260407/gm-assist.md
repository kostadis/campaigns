# Chapter 36 A dragon defeated, a bard tempted, a barbarian's vengeance.

Date: Apr 9th, 2026

## Summary
Deep within the dragon's mountain fortress, the party discovered Cryovain not as a fearsome predator on the hunt, but as a prisoner of something far worse — magically bound within a glowing summoning circle, undergoing a ritual that would hand his body over to the malevolent alien entity known as the Carver. The dragon, enraged and only partially possessed, tore free of the circle and launched himself into the air, placing himself well out of reach of most of the party's weapons. Time was running out, and the Carver's grip on the great beast was tightening with every passing moment.

Valphine called down a bolt of divine light aimed at the soaring dragon, but the strike went wide, the radiance scattering harmlessly into the cold mountain air. Soma, ever attuned to the natural and supernatural alike, conjured a towering cylinder of moonlight that reached up through the cavern and seared the dragon with its radiant glow. Brewbarry, refusing to be left out of the fight simply because the enemy had taken to the skies, channeled a primal power that swelled his body to a massive size, his reach extending far enough to swing his halberd in great arcing blows that connected with the dragon's scaled hide with thunderous force.

Cryovain, furious and wounded, turned his wrath upon Brewbarry and unleashed twin blasts of freezing breath that would have felled a lesser warrior outright. Somehow, through sheer physical resilience and an almost supernatural stubbornness, Brewbarry remained standing — battered, frost-covered, and barely clinging to consciousness, but standing nonetheless. The rest of the party pressed the attack, with Vukradin landing a critical strike aided by a surge of bardic inspiration, and Soma's moonbeam continuing to burn the dragon from above. When Cryovain had been reduced to little more than a wounded, desperate creature, Valphine — who had struggled throughout the entire fight — called down a burst of sacred radiance that struck true. The dragon collapsed, and the Carver's essence, robbed of its vessel, dissipated into nothing.

With the dragon slain, the party turned their attention to a tough bandit who had been paralyzed during the chaos of the ritual. Though some among the group, particularly Soma, argued for mercy, Brewbarry's battle rage had not yet cooled. The barbarian brought his halberd down with finality, and the bandit breathed his last. The debate over the man's fate was brief, and the party moved on to survey what remained of the fortress.

The loot they found was a disappointment of a particular kind — roughly three thousand gold pieces worth of coins, gems, and trade bars, all fused together into a solid block of residium-infused ice and melted beyond all recognition. Soma studied the strange mass and recognized that planar trickery had been at work, suggesting the treasure had been touched by forces from beyond their world. Any markings that might have identified its original owners had been destroyed entirely, leaving the party to debate the ethics of claiming it as their own. Vukradin, ever principled, declared that the gold treasure was stolen goods and insisted that proper investigation was required before anyone could call it theirs.

More intriguing than the treasure slag was the obsidian sword that had once housed the Carver's essence. When Vukradin took it up, he discovered that the crystal set into its pommel was no ordinary gemstone — it was a mental amplifier, capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience and creating a perfect, hall-filling sound without the need for any physical structure. The party immediately dubbed it the Bluetooth Sword, much to Vukradin's theatrical dismay. It seemed almost impossibly well-suited to his lifelong dream of building a music studio, and more than one party member suggested, with a grin, that it had been handcrafted by the universe specifically to tempt him. However, Vukradin was very concerned about its power and declared that the magical gem must be returned to its rightful owner.

Their moment of reflection was interrupted by the arrival of two women — Sylvine Wintermoon, a lean and brash redhead, and her muscular, cold-eyed sister Runa. The pair praised Vukradin as a saint sent by the gods and immediately claimed the entire block of melted treasure as their own. Vukradin, deeply skeptical and thoroughly exhausted by the dishonesty he had encountered throughout his travels, accused them of being thieves and phonies and refused to hand anything over without proof. Insulted and apparently unwilling to provide any, Sylvine declared a change of plans and the sisters, along with their bandit companions, attacked.

The skirmish that followed was chaotic but decisive. Valphine summoned a protective aura of spirit guardians that burned the approaching bandits, though she lost her concentration on the spell after taking a hard blow. Soma shifted into bear form and tore into the fighters with her claws. Vukradin paralyzed two of the bandits with a well-placed enchantment, leaving them frozen and helpless. Brewbarry, still riding the high of having survived a dragon's breath twice, waded in with his halberd and cut down the paralyzed enemies with brutal efficiency. The last surviving bandit, seeing the tide of battle turn decisively against him, made a pragmatic decision and fled down the hallway, muttering something about better career opportunities elsewhere.

In the quiet that followed, Vukradin reached out across the distance using a magical sending to contact Ser Kaelen of the Order of the Gauntlet. He informed the paladin that both the dragon and the Carver were dead, and that the party was preparing to move on. Ser Kaelen's reply came swiftly — formal, effusive, and suspiciously polished in its phrasing — congratulating them on their conflict-free triumph and urging them to come to Phandalin immediately, as issues had arisen with the Emerald Enclave. When Vukradin declared that the party must head back to Neverwinter to return the stolen statue, he was reminded that the party had already happened, and perhaps House Margaster had done irreparable harm to the owner. With the dragon dead, the Carver gone, and a new crisis already forming on the horizon, the party set their course for Phandalin — and whatever trouble awaited them there.

## NPCs
### Cryovain
A white dragon that was magically chained within a summoning circle and in the process of being possessed by the Carver. Enraged, it fought from the air using its freezing breath before being slain by Valphine's sacred flame.

### The Carver
A malevolent alien entity tied to an obsidian sword that was attempting to take full possession of Cryovain in order to build a despotic empire. Its essence dissipated entirely upon the dragon's death.

### Veteran
A tough bandit involved in the Carver's ritual who was paralyzed by magic during the combat. Despite some party members' desire for mercy, he was killed by Brewbarry.

### Sylvine Wintermoon
A lean, brash woman with red hair pulled back into a ponytail. She appeared in the fortress after the dragon's defeat, praising Vukradin as a saint and claiming the treasure slag belonged to her and her sister. When her claim was challenged, she turned hostile and led an attack on the party.

### Runa
A muscular woman with black hair and cold blue eyes, sister to Sylvine. She supported her sister's claim to the treasure and joined the attack against the party when negotiations broke down.

### Harbin Wester
The Townmaster of Phandalin, mentioned as someone likely to seize credit for the party's victory over the dragon.

### Ser Kaelen
A member of the Order of the Gauntlet who communicated via the sending spell to congratulate the party on their victory and urgently summon them to Phandalin to deal with issues involving the Emerald Enclave.

## Locations
### Dragon's Mountain Fortress
A cold, imposing stronghold where the dragon Cryovain was found magically chained within a glowing summoning circle. The site of the ritual, the dragon battle, and the subsequent skirmish with treasure claimants. Contains the remnants of the Carver's ritual circle and a block of melted treasure slag.

### Neverwinter
A major city the party intends to visit. They learn that by choosing to fight the dragon first, they missed their window to return a statue to an art collector there, who has since been killed by House Margaster.

### Phandalin
The local town where the party is urgently summoned by Ser Kaelen. The party suspects Harbin Wester will attempt to claim credit for slaying the dragon, and Ser Kaelen warns of rising issues with the Emerald Enclave.

## Items
### Necklace of Fireball Beads
A magical necklace worn by Valphine containing fireball beads. The party briefly debated using it against the dragon, but ultimately chose not to.

### Dragon Slayer Sword
A specialized blade held by Brewbarry, intended for use against the dragon. Brewbarry switched to his halberd during the fight to compensate for the dragon's height in the air.

### Halberd
A polearm used by Brewbarry to reach the dragon in flight after growing to large size, delivering the heavy strikes that significantly wounded Cryovain.

### Treasure Slag
A block of roughly 3,000 gold pieces worth of coins, gems, and trade bars fused together in residium-infused ice, melted beyond all recognition by planar trickery. Its original owners cannot be identified. Declared as stolen goods by Vukradin and contested by Sylvine Wintermoon and Runa.

### Obsidian Sword
A black blade formerly used by the Carver to house its essence. Its crystal pommel acts as a mental amplifier capable of projecting music directly into the minds of an audience, creating a perfect hall-filling sound without the need for a physical building. Vukradin noted it seemed tailor-made for his personal quest for a music studio, but expressed deep concern over its power and declared the gem must be returned to its owner.

### Sending Stones
A set of magical stones noted in the party's inventory during their short rest in the fortress.

## Spells
### Guiding Bolt
Cast by Valphine at an upcast level against the flying dragon Cryovain. Despite using bardic inspiration to try to improve the roll, the bolt failed to connect with its target.

### Moonbeam
Cast by Soma as an upcast 40-foot-high radiant cylinder of light. It successfully and continuously damaged Cryovain while the dragon was in the air, and was later moved onto the veteran to further weaken him.

### Hold Person
A paralyzing enchantment that kept a veteran immobile throughout the final moments of the dragon encounter. Later cast again by Vukradin during the skirmish, paralyzing two of the treasure-claiming veterans and leaving them vulnerable to Brewbarry's finishing blows.

### Sacred Flame
A burst of radiance called down by Valphine that delivered the killing blow to the weakened Cryovain, causing the dragon to collapse and the Carver's essence to dissipate.

### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Valphine during the skirmish with Sylvine's group, creating a damaging aura around her that hurt the approaching veterans. She lost concentration on the spell after being struck by a veteran's attack.

### Wild Shape
Used by Soma to transform into a bear, providing frontline melee capability during both the aftermath of the dragon fight and the skirmish with the treasure claimants.

### Sending
Cast by Vukradin to send a short message to Ser Kaelen informing him of the party's victory over the dragon and the Carver. Ser Kaelen was able to respond immediately, warning of issues with the Emerald Enclave in Phandalin. The party likened it to a magical text message that works across planes of existence.

### Word of Radiance
Used by Valphine during the skirmish in an attempt to damage multiple enemies at once, though the veterans made their saving throws and the effect was minimal.
