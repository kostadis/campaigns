## Events

- The party returned to Phandalin after their exploits at Icespire Hold and the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. Harbin Wester held a festival in their honor, taking credit for forming the Privy Council that brought them to town.
- Ser Kaelen pulled Soma aside during the festival to warn her that Adabra was moving against her within the Emerald Enclave, using events at the Whispering Wood and Lyra's failure to build a case.
- Bryn and Giles, recent converts to the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice, returned from a public self-flagellation/religious parade through town, appearing scarred and sunburnt but joyful.
- The party leveled up to level 6 (confirmed in out-of-character discussion). Vukradin gained two new bard features: Bardic Inspiration dice can be kept on a failed roll, and he can speak to any creature in any language once per long rest for 1 hour.
- Valphine discovered she can cast Bless four times per day for 10 minutes each.
- Lathander communicated approval of the direction Valphine is taking the church.
- The party traveled to the Emerald Enclave council meeting. En route, they were intercepted by Thomas, one of Lyra's agents.
- Thomas warned the party that Adabra was seeking to ostracize Lyra, that Reidoth (nominal head of the local chapter) was absent, and that Adabra called the meeting specifically during his absence. The party debated contacting Reidoth but concluded it would not stop Adabra from speaking.
- Vukradin decided to keep contacting Reidoth as a back-pocket option.
- At the council, Adabra delivered her accusation: she personally visited the Whispering Wood three weeks prior, found the sacred treant dead (roots collapsed, crown black, grove destroyed), pressed Lyra's people and then other witnesses, and concluded the party had falsified their report. She revealed she gave Vukradin 5 gold pieces based on what she now believes was a deliberate lie.
- Thomas and Lyra both testified honestly that Lyra's chapter tried and failed to deal with the metastasized treant, the party killed it to stop a planar infestation, and neither expressed remorse.
- Soma used the Resonance of Truth ability via Meril's Staff on Adabra. Result: Adabra is not being mentally controlled, magically manipulated, or consciously lying — she is a genuine true believer.
- The party debated strategy in a sidebar, deciding not to deny the facts but instead to lean into proud interventionism and shift the mood of the moderates in the room.
- Vukradin sent a Sending spell to Reidoth (taken via Magical Secrets) to inform him of the ideological conflict and council taking place in his absence.
- Soma addressed the council, arguing the land is hurting and intervention is the only way to protect it, declaring they are proud interventionists.
- Falcon rose to publicly side with Adabra and pointed directly at Vukradin, blaming him personally for trees being dead. Vukradin pulled Falcon aside privately and confronted him about his hypocritical past as an adventurer.
- Falcon publicly confessed to the room that he had once believed in fighting dragons, had failed, and had learned the error of his ways through Adabra's teachings. He cited the burning of his lodge as evidence that intervention produces no lasting good.
- Deeply offended by the personal insults and hostile atmosphere, Vukradin walked out of the council and returned to Phandalin. He gave no response to Brewbarry's question about whether to kill anyone.
- Brewbarry asked Soma if he should kill the dissenters; Soma refused, stating they must preserve the druids to protect the land.
- Soma approached Aldric Stone Path, who had been sitting with Adabra. He explained he had spent decades mapping the mountains and supported Adabra because she was the first person willing to listen to his observations about the land's decline.
- Soma pressed Aldric on what Adabra's actual proposed solution was to the orc incursions and disturbances he had witnessed. Adabra responded that the ecosystem finds equilibrium without help. Soma countered that one form of equilibrium could be the end of all life, and that is not the equilibrium she is there to protect.
- This argument visibly shifted Aldric and caused the room's mood to turn against Adabra.
- Xanthopoulos the centaur arrived at the council during or just after this exchange. The session excerpt ends here.

## NPC Interactions

- **Harbin Wester:** Welcomed the party back to Phandalin with a festival, praised their role in defeating the dragon, and took credit for forming the Privy Council. Party reacted with internal seething rage.
- **Ser Kaelen:** Pulled Soma aside to warn her that Adabra was mobilizing the Emerald Enclave against her, using Lyra's failure at the Whispering Wood as the basis. Explained Adabra's philosophy stems from witnessing damage caused by dragon fighters during the War of the Dragons.
- **Bryn and Giles:** Appeared scarred and sunburnt after participating in a public religious parade and self-flagellation ritual as a fundraiser. Spoke enthusiastically about the purifying essence of pain of the sun.
- **Thomas:** Intercepted the party on the road to the council. Warned them Adabra was seeking to ostracize Lyra, that Reidoth was away and the meeting was called deliberately in his absence, and that even Reidoth's presence would not stop Adabra from speaking. Testified at the council that Lyra's chapter tried and failed to deal with the metastasized treant before the party intervened.
- **Reidoth:** Discussed but not present. Described as believing in consensus and open communication. A Sending spell was dispatched to him informing him of the council and ideological conflict.
- **Adabra:** Delivered a formal accusation at the council. Stated she personally visited the Whispering Wood three weeks prior, found the treant dead, pressed witnesses whose accounts did not match the party's report, and concluded the party—specifically Vukradin—had lied to her. Revealed she gave Vukradin 5 gold pieces based on the false account. Confirmed by Soma's staff ability to be a genuine true believer, not manipulated or lying. Responded to Soma's challenge to Aldric by arguing the ecosystem finds equilibrium without intervention. Visibly furious when the room's mood shifted against her.
- **Lyra:** Testified at the council honestly, admitting that her chapter tried and failed to save the treant and that the party killed it. Expressed no shame or remorse, stating this was the right thing to do.
- **Falcon:** Publicly sided with Adabra and pointed directly at Vukradin, declaring trees were dead because of men like him. Quoted: *"I came because someone finally sat long enough to listen. You can keep cheering your victories, I've seen what comes after them."* When pulled aside privately by Vukradin and confronted about his past as an adventurer, he stood up and publicly confessed his history—that he once believed in fighting dragons, failed, built a comfortable life on interventionism, and then had his lodge burned anyway. He concluded he had learned the error of his ways through Adabra's teachings.
- **Aldric Stone Path:** A weathered human in his early forties who had spent decades mapping the mountains. Supported Adabra because she was the first person willing to listen to his observations about the land's decline. Soma confronted him about what Adabra's philosophy actually proposes as a solution to orc incursions and disturbances he had witnessed, leaving him unable to answer satisfactorily. The room's mood shifted as a result.
- **Xanthopoulos:** Arrived at the council near the end of the excerpt. Previously described as having a moderate stance (idiots on both sides). Previously brought the party to the edge of the mountain before departing due to his horse hooves being unsuitable for climbing.

## PC Actions & Decisions

- **Soma:** Used Resonance of Truth via Meril's Staff on Adabra. Result: Adabra is not controlled, manipulated, or lying — confirmed true believer. Cast Sending to Reidoth to inform him of the council. Delivered a rousing interventionist speech to the room. Confronted Aldric Stone Path with the logical consequences of Adabra's philosophy, successfully shifting his position and the room's mood. Rolled 19 Insight on a DC 13 check during the Aldric conversation.
- **Vukradin:** Attempted to cross-examine Adabra on the two-week gap between when she heard the report and when she personally witnessed the dead treant. Pulled Falcon aside privately and confronted him about building his life on interventionism while now condemning it. When Falcon publicly insulted him, Vukradin walked out of the council entirely and returned to Phandalin without another word. Gave no response to Brewbarry's question.
- **Brewbarry:** Asked Soma if he should kill the dissenters. Was told no. Asked Vukradin if he should kill Falcon as Vukradin walked away; received no response.
- **Valphine:** Present but no significant individual actions described in this excerpt beyond being informed of Lathander's approval of her church's direction.
- **Party decision:** Chose not to deny the facts of what happened with the treant. Chose to argue proud interventionism rather than legal maneuvering. Chose not to go fetch Reidoth, keeping that as a back-pocket option. Chose not to kill the dissenters.
