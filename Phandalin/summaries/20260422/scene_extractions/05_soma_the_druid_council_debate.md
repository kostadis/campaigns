[Scene 5] The Druid Council Debate
Narrator: Soma
Focus: Soma is the one who holds the line after Vukradin leaves — confronting Aldric, owning the Corbin mistake, and landing the interventionist argument that visibly turns the room.

- Soma confronts Aldric Stone Path, a weathered human in his early forties who has spent years mapping the mountains and initially supports Adabra because she was the first person to listen to his observations about the mountains' decline. Aldric has been trying to get the council to pay attention to the events in the mountains with no success. 

**Wade (Soma)** — *confronting Aldric Stone Path about Adabra's actual solutions*
> "I don't know how you don't see those as a problem as well, sir. She's bringing in yet another problem — these planar anomalies, these infestations, these blights on the land. Adabra says let nature take its course and get obliterated, basically. What does Adabra say the solution is?"

- Aldric is silent and unable to respond. Adabra can sense the crowd is shifting against her. 

**GM as Adabra** — *responding to Soma's challenge with naturalist philosophy*
> "The displacement is downstream of the intervention. The answer is to stop the cascade by withdrawing. The ecosystem finds equilibrium without help."

- Soma argues that ignoring planar anomalies and orc threats leads to the destruction of the land, forcing Aldric to confront the consequences of supporting Adabra's philosophy.

**Wade (Soma)** — *delivering the rebuttal that shifts the room*
> "Equilibrium comes in many forms. One of these equilibriums could be the end of everything, of all life. That's not the equilibrium I'm here to protect."


- Akdric sits down. Adabra has lost one of her allies. 

- Xanthopoulos the centaur arrives at the council, offering a moderate perspective that both pure interventionism and pure naturalism are follies.

- Xanthopoulos recognizes Soma's staff as belonging to the highly respected druid Meril, noting that Meril did not give it for sentiment — he chose her.

<!-- arriving at the council and immediately recognizing Soma's staff -->
Xanthopoulos: "I know whose staff that is. I know what it means that Meril gave it to her. He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he… picked her."

<!-- reacting with deliberate understatement to the staff's significance -->
Soma: "It's just a staff! Isn't it?"

- Xanthopoulos delivers shocking news: Corbin has been killed by Sister Kaella, who escaped after the party left them poorly guarded at Dragon Barrow.

<!-- delivering the shocking news about Corbin and Sister Kaella -->
Xanthopoulos: "I have news. Corbin is dead. Sister Kayla walked out. She's gone to the wind."

<!-- explaining why Corbin died -->
Soma realizes: "They left a farmer turned bandit with a deadly assassin because they were in a rush to intervene."

<!-- reacting to Corbin's death with dry resignation -->
Soma: "And Boney stood by and did nothing?"

- Adabra attempts to use Corbin's death as proof of the dangers of interventionism, but Xanthopoulos pushes back, stating he is not on her side.

<!-- seizing on Corbin's death to score a political point -->
Adabra: "See, this is the price of interventionism. Dead bodies everywhere, by careless, careless intervention."

<!-- shutting Adabra down flatly -->
Xanthopoulos: "I am not on your side. I'm not on theirs. I'm telling you what my people saw and what happened."

<!-- owning the mistake without sugarcoating it -->
Soma: "This was… we were planning on our… I can't sugarcoat it. The ask was, do I sugarcoat it, or do I fess up? I'm not sugarcoating it."

<!-- arguing against Adabra's characterization of the treant's death -->
Soma: "The interplanar treant? Yeah, that's… I'm not going to cry for that. That treant was going to bring a lot more manifestation and horror with it."

<!-- citing the Axelholm incident and the Talosian blight as evidence for interventionism -->
Soma: "We've seen things… if left unchecked, the Talosians are starting to blight the land itself. Given that the Falcon's Cabin was overtaken by Talosians — and it's weird, you know, the Falconer asked us to intervene, but now he's an anti-interventionist. He's mad that we failed to intervene, and yet…"
