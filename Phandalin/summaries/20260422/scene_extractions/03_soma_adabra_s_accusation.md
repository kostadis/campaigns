[Scene 3] Adabra's Accusation
Narrator: Soma
Focus: Soma is the one who turns Meril's staff on Adabra and discovers the most unsettling truth of the session — that their accuser is no villain, just a genuine true believer who cannot be argued away with facts.

- Adabra addresses the council, claiming she personally visited the Whispering Wood and found the sacred treant dead — roots collapsed, crown black — and the grove destroyed.


<!-- Adabra addresses the Druid Council, presenting her case against the party and Lyra -->
Adabra: "I will be brief. I am not here to argue philosophy with you, I'm here to tell you what I found. Three weeks ago, I went to the Whispering Wood myself. I walked to the Spring Grove. The treant is dead. Not wounded. Not sleeping. Dead. Roots collapsed. Crown black. The grove that Lyra's chapter was assigned to protect is gone. I pressed Lyra's people on what happened. Their account did not match what I found. I pressed further. They named the Bard. I then spoke with two others who were present at the grove that night. Their account also did not match. What they described is not what was reported to us. The party concealed what happened. They did not make a mistake and tell us. They made a choice."

- She accuses the party, specifically the bard Vukradin, of lying to her and falsifying reports about what happened at the grove.

- Adabra reveals she gave Vukradin five gold pieces based on his false account and demands the council examine the physical evidence before listening to anyone's interpretation.
<!-- continuing her council speech, describing how she was deceived -->
Adabra: "The last time I spoke with this party, I believed the ritual had failed. The tree had still lived. When I heard there was a planar creature collecting materials near the grove, I was — I will say this plainly — pleased. I called it a planar magpie. I said it was the world working as it should. I gave the bard five gold pieces for what he told me. That day, I believed him. Now, I have walked to the grove itself. The treant is dead. The creature is still there. What I called the world healing itself was the world responding to a wound I was not told had been made. He did not only lie to Lyra's people, he lied to me. These are the facts. A sacred grove is dead. A report was falsified, and I gave gold to the man who falsified it because he is very good at what he does. I'm not asking you to condemn anyone today. I'm asking you to look at what I found before you listen to anyone tell you what it means."


- Soma uses Meril's staff to determine that Adabra is a true believer — not being magically manipulated or consciously lying.
<!-- revealing the result -->
GM: "The answer to all of those is no."

<!-- reacting to the result -->
Vukradin mutters under his breath: "Yeah, no, she's a true believer."

- Falcon is observed sitting next to Adabra, appearing to support her naturalist stance.

- Vukradin attempts to cross-examine Adabra, questioning the two-week gap between when she heard the account and when she personally saw the dead treant.

<!-- Vukradin attempts to challenge the timeline of Adabra's account -->
David cross-examining Adabra: "So, between… how much time passed between when you heard this account, which you claim was false, and when you saw that the treant was dead? How much time between those two points?"

<!-- responding to Vukradin's cross-examination -->
Adabra: "Two weeks."

<!-- pressing the gap in Adabra's evidence -->
David: "Two weeks. Two weeks. A lot can happen in two weeks. How do you know that what you heard from — I believe you were referring to me — wasn't true, and that the treant died within that two-week period before you personally went and saw that it had died? You don't know that."

- Thomas and Lyra testify to the council, admitting they failed to save the metastasized treant and that the party ultimately killed it to stop a planar infestation — and they are not ashamed of it.

<!-- testifying honestly at the council -->
Thomas: "Lyra asked us to go deal with the metastasized treant. We tried, we failed, the party showed up, they killed it."
