# Chapter 38 Mic Drop: Enclave Edition

Date: Apr 22nd, 2026

## Summary
The party returned to Phandalin to a hero's welcome, greeted by the pompous Townmaster Harbin Wester, who threw a festival in their honor and made a great show of congratulating them on defeating the dragon — conveniently taking credit for forming the Privy Council that had brought them to town in the first place. The celebration was short-lived, however, as Ser Kaelen pulled Soma aside during the festivities to deliver urgent news. Adabra, the staunch naturalist druid, was moving against her within the Emerald Enclave, using the events at the Whispering Wood to paint Soma as a reckless interventionist and seeking to have Lyra ostracized from the order entirely. Meanwhile, at the Temple of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice, Valphine's newest converts — Bryn and Giles — returned from a public religious parade through town, scarred and sunburnt, beaming with the joy of the faithful.

On the road to the Emerald Enclave council, the party was intercepted by Thomas, one of Lyra's trusted agents, who warned them of the political trap being laid. He explained that Reidoth, the nominal head of the local Enclave chapter, was away from town — and that Adabra had called the meeting specifically during his absence to push her agenda without opposition. The party briefly considered reaching out to Reidoth and kept the option in reserve, though Thomas warned that even his presence would not stop Adabra from speaking her piece. Soma resolved to face the council head-on, deciding that the best strategy was not to deny what had happened, but to stand proudly as an interventionist and rally those who shared her convictions.

The council was already assembled when the party arrived, and Adabra wasted no time. She addressed the gathered druids with cold precision, describing how she had personally traveled to the Whispering Wood three weeks prior and found the sacred treant dead — roots collapsed, crown black, the grove destroyed. She revealed that she had pressed Lyra's people for answers, and their account did not match what she found. She had then spoken with others who were present that night, and their stories also failed to align with what had been reported. Most damning of all, she confessed that she had given Vukradin five gold pieces based on his account of events — an account she now believed to be a deliberate lie. She stepped back when she was finished, her case laid out like a blade on a table, and waited.

Vukradin attempted to challenge Adabra's account, pressing her on the two-week gap between when she heard the report and when she personally witnessed the dead treant, arguing that much could have changed in that time. But Thomas and Lyra both testified honestly, admitting that the party had killed the corrupted treant to stop a planar infestation — and neither of them showed a shred of remorse for it. As far as they were concerned, they had done the right thing, and they said so plainly. The party then huddled briefly, debating their approach, and agreed that denying the facts would get them nowhere with people who had no interest in legal maneuvering. The goal was to shift the mood of the room, not to win an argument on technicalities.

Soma used Meril's staff to quietly assess Adabra during the proceedings and came to a sobering conclusion: she was not being manipulated, not deceived, not acting under any outside influence. She was a true believer, utterly convinced of her naturalist philosophy. That realization changed the calculus entirely — there was no unmasking to be done, no hidden puppet master to expose. The only path forward was to win the hearts of the moderates in the room. Soma stepped forward and made her case with passion, arguing that the land was hurting and that intervention was the only way to protect it, declaring that if that made them interventionists, then so be it — they were proud of it.

The mood in the room began to shift, but then Falcon rose to speak. The former adventurer, who had once hired the party himself, publicly sided with Adabra and pointed directly at Vukradin, declaring that trees were dead because of men like him. Vukradin pulled Falcon aside and confronted him privately, reminding him that his comfortable life — his lodge, his fame, his gold — had all been built on the very interventionism he now condemned. Falcon confessed to the room that he had once believed in fighting dragons and had failed, and that he had since learned the error of his ways through Adabra's teachings. The personal attack was too much for Vukradin, who, deeply offended, turned and walked out of the council entirely, leaving the rest of the party to carry on without him.

With Vukradin gone, Soma turned her attention to Aldric Stone Path, a weathered human in his early forties who had spent years mapping the mountains and initially supported Adabra because she had been the first person willing to listen to his observations about the land's decline. Soma pressed him directly: what did Adabra actually propose as a solution to the orc incursions and the disturbances he had witnessed? The question hung in the air, and Aldric found he had no satisfying answer. The logic of Adabra's philosophy, when applied to the very problems he cared about most, began to unravel before him.

Then Xanthopoulos the centaur arrived, and the atmosphere in the council shifted again. He greeted Soma warmly and immediately noticed her staff — the one that had belonged to the respected druid Meril — remarking that Meril had not given it out of sentiment, but because he had chosen her. The other druids stirred at the mention of Meril's name, clearly aware of his significance. Xanthopoulos then delivered grim news: Corbin was dead, killed by Sister Kaella, who had escaped after the party left the two of them behind at Dragon Barrow under inadequate guard. Adabra seized on the death as proof of the dangers of interventionism, but Xanthopoulos shut her down flatly, making clear he was not on her side.

Soma brought the full weight of the party's experiences to bear on the council — the twisted dwarves at Axelholm, the planar anomalies they had encountered, the Talosian blight spreading across the land, and the fact that Falcon's own lodge had been overtaken by Talosians. She argued that the interplanar treant was not a creature of the natural world and that its death had been necessary to prevent further corruption. Lyra testified alongside her, explaining that a planar rift had been bleeding the forest dry and that the ritual to stop it had failed, leaving no other option. Adabra delivered a furious final lecture, condemning the party's interventionist mindset in increasingly elaborate terms, but the room had already made up its mind.

The council deliberated briefly and returned with their verdict: Adabra's case did not hold. The purpose of the Emerald Enclave was to protect the forest and the region, and simply waiting for nature to sort itself out made no sense when the threats were planar in origin and actively destroying the land. Lyra was restored to full good standing within the order. Adabra, seething with rage, walked out of the meeting without another word. Soma, in a moment of triumphant theatrics, let Meril's staff drop to the ground with a resounding crack — a mic drop for the ages. The druids, for their part, expressed their greatest regret of the evening: that Vukradin had left early, because they had heard he played very good music.

After the council dispersed, Valphine sought out Falcon, who was visibly despondent and adrift. He shared his own story — how he had come up from the Underdark, how the demons had ravaged his home, and how the brilliance of the sun had struck him like a revelation when he first emerged into the overworld. Something in that account cracked through Falcon's nihilism. He admitted he no longer knew why he had ever supported Adabra, expressed a desire to deal with the orc threat that had been plaguing the region, and asked Valphine to help him find the words to apologize to Vukradin. Valphine suggested that Falcon might consider giving Vukradin a musical instrument of value or perhaps a studio room in his retreat — gestures that would go a long way toward mending the rift.

As the dust settled, Lyra invited the party to celebrate at the Stonehill Tavern, overjoyed at her restored standing and grateful beyond words. The party reflected on what had been accomplished — the Emerald Enclave's moderates had sided with them, Adabra's influence had been checked, and a potential new ally in Falcon had been pulled back from the edge of despair. Xanthopoulos offered one final remark to Soma as he departed, saying quietly that perhaps Meril had made the right choice. With Lyra's name cleared and the council behind them, the party turned their eyes northward, toward Neverwinter — though the orc threat along the road promised that the journey would not be a quiet one.
