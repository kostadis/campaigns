## Flags

### [1] Convergence with Valphine's faith vocabulary

> "and when they did she lit up like morning and the orc bellowed *behold the power of the sun* loud enough to shake dust from the rafters."

**Why:** "like morning" is a dawn-theology simile that belongs to Valphine's self-perception of her own radiance. Brewbarry observes Valphine's power as a bodily event — bright, loud, effective — and has no interest in theological framing. His spec says combat description is "procedural and bodily"; his examples never reach for simile at all. "Like morning" is Valphine's inner vocabulary, not his.
**Suggested rewrite:** "and when they did she blazed and the orc bellowed *behold the power of the sun* loud enough to shake dust from the rafters."

## Verdict

Scene holds Brewbarry's voice throughout — the short declaratives, the bodily combat focus, the unsentimental ally-observation, and "let the heat run down out of me, slow" as a closing line are all on-spec. One simile slips into Valphine's theological register and should be blunted.
