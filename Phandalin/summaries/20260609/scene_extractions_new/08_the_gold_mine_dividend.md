# The Gold Mine Dividend

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party stops at the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine to check on operations and collect their meager first earnings.
- The party arrives and finds the wererats working with conspicuous diligence; after the intimidation of the party's last visit, they "jump to attention," promising everything is going according to plan.
- A wererat anxiously assures Valphine that "the mine is very busily working," recalling that last time she had noticed nobody working; her new golden eyes leave him "a little bit disturbed."
- Horia presents a detailed ledger covering startup costs, professional costs, back-office costs, worker insurance, salaries, food supplies, and a "remote danger bonus."
- After the Neverwinter Commission's cut — the Commission being the actual owner of the land — the party receives a disappointing dividend of only thirty gold pieces for five days of operation.
- Vukradin asks to examine the books; Valphine (and the DM) confirm the records are honest.
- Vukradin likens the whole arrangement to a Nigerian prince scam — "There's all this money in the mine, but you need to buy me a gem before."
- Horia and the lead dwarven miner explain that a major new vein is close — "it's been speaking to me, so all we gotta do is give it a little bit of time, but if we pressure it too much, it's gonna disappear" — promising they'll soon "be rolling in the cash."
- The wererats fret about leaving their posts to fetch their promised cheese (sourced from Petunia's "happy cows"), with one carefully asking whether being overwhelmed by an attack while fetching cheese would count as abandoning their posts. Brewbarry: "Do we have to spell out everything for you? Come on."
- Valphine offers to escort one wererat to town the first time to show him how to secure the cheese; Vukradin objects to "babysitting" them.
- The party notes the Neverwinter Commission's large cut as something to renegotiate — Soma likens it to "the HOA of the land."
- Brewbarry and Vukradin lament their lack of personal wealth — Vukradin no closer to funding his music studio, Brewbarry desperate for his own Falcon's Lodge bathrobe — despite owning a gold mine and an untransformed lump of dragon's gold from Cryovain's hoard they refuse to use.
