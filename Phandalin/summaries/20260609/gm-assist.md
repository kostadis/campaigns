# Chapter 45 Victory Lap

Date: Jun 9th, 2026

## Summary
The party found themselves scattered across the crumbling interior of the Woodland Manse, a ruined scholar's estate choked with ivy and shadows. Soma had clambered up through a hole to the upper level while Brewbarry and Vukradin remained stranded below a collapsed staircase. Without warning, a swarm of stirges — vicious, mosquito-like creatures with needle-sharp proboscises — dropped from the darkened rafters and latched onto Soma, beginning to drain his blood. Brewbarry, fueled by a primal fury that swelled his body to a terrifying size, launched himself up the full eight-foot height of the ruined staircase to join the fight. He seized the creatures clinging to Soma and tore them apart with his bare hands, snapping one in half and vaporizing another with a thunderous cleaving blow.

The battle was far from over. More stirges descended from the rafters, buzzing angrily and latching onto Brewbarry in retaliation. Vukradin, still below, unleashed a torrent of magically-charged mockery that rattled one of the creatures into a fatal mistake, while Valphine and her devoted half-orc follower Prutha moved swiftly toward the hole in the floor to join their companions. Soma, still in the form of a great bear, bit one of the stirges so savagely that it exploded into a gruesome mess, then raked his claws across another that had been feasting on Brewbarry. Vukradin was ambushed from above as three more stirges dropped directly onto him, one managing to sink its proboscis in before Brewbarry systematically hunted down and exterminated the last of the pests with brutal efficiency.

With the rafters finally silent, Valphine allowed the last few hidden stirges to drop onto her before releasing a brilliant burst of radiant light that incinerated them where they fell. Prutha, wide-eyed with religious awe, thumped his chest and proclaimed the power of the sun — then added, with characteristic half-orc bluntness, that it could apparently also kill mosquitoes. The party took a moment to catch their breath before exploring the upper levels of the manse, discovering a circular room with a cast iron bathtub fed by a hot spring pump, a luxury that had clearly not been enjoyed in a very long time.

Searching the master bedroom, Vukradin correctly deduced the location of a hidden secret door by comparing the layouts of the manse's floors. While he moved into the adjoining room, Soma — still in bear form — quietly discovered a Staff of Bird Calls hanging in the bedroom and stuffed it deep into the tangled mess of fur, hoping no one had noticed. Meanwhile, Valphine explored the manse's library, its shelves bowed and broken, and found one undamaged book titled "Elvish Idioms." When she opened it, she discovered a tiny leather packet of magical powder tucked inside — a find she quietly pocketed alongside the book.

The final unexplored room on the ground floor brought the party to an abrupt halt. The dining hall walls were covered in crude, blood-painted drawings of boars chasing stick-figured humanoids, and resting on the mantelpiece were small twig figures — one for each member of the party. Brewbarry immediately warned the group not to touch them, recalling a hard-learned lesson from the wise women of his village about cursed artifacts. Valphine, reasoning that leaving such things behind would be irresponsible, decided to destroy her own effigy from a safe distance. The moment it crumbled, the remaining figures lurched to life as twig blights and launched themselves at the party. They were dispatched quickly — Vukradin obliterated one with a lance of radiant starlight, and Valphine's light reduced the rest to splinters — but not before Vukradin pointedly noted that Valphine had triggered the very trap she had been trying to avoid.

With the manse cleared, Valphine gathered the party together and cast a spell of compelled truth, creating a magical aura that prevented deliberate lies. One by one, the party's secrets came tumbling out. Soma confessed to the Staff of Bird Calls. Valphine admitted to the Dust of Disappearance. And Brewbarry, the fearsome Uthgardt barbarian, sheepishly revealed that he had taken a soft satin bathrobe and a pair of slippers from Falcon's Lodge — because he simply liked how they felt. Prutha, ever loyal to Valphine, attempted to be evasive and quietly shuffled out of the magical zone before he could be fully questioned, though not before it was noted that he had also helped himself to a bathrobe. The party debated the ethics of their findings at length before agreeing, more or less, to return what they had taken.

Returning to Falcon's Lodge — the siege of the previous sessions now behind them, Gorthok and the anchorite force defeated — the party found the orcs split: some settled comfortably into their new roles as Falcon's employees, dressed in his livery, while others were preparing to return home to Icespire Hold. The Falcon himself was in high spirits, gushing about the bards Rimardo and Corrin, whom he called the finest act he had seen north of Neverwinter. He pressed an unsealed letter into Vukradin's hands, asking him to deliver it to the pair — a formal offer of free room and board, a generous cut of the proceeds, and excellent hunting in exchange for a regular performance at his lodge. Vorga, the warchief, had clearly grown tired of the Falcon's enthusiasm and fixed the party with a pointed look, reminding them of their promise to escort her tribe home to Icepire Hold.

The two-day journey north was filled with songs, food, and the particular energy of a large orc tribe in good spirits. Upon arriving at Icepire Hold, something extraordinary happened. As Valphine spoke to Prutha about building a church and converting the other orc tribes of the mountains to the cause of Lathander, her eyes transformed — permanently — into a blinding, molten golden hue, luminescent as the midday sun. It was a divine blessing, a revelation from Lathander himself, charging her with establishing a militant arm of an existing faith dedicated to fighting aberrations and evil. Prutha, witnessing the transformation, fell into a state of fervent religious awe, and five more orcs immediately pledged themselves to the cause. Inspired by the transformation, Prutha and five newly converted orcs were moved to act — and seeing their fervor, Valphine instructed them to carry Lathander's cause to the other orc tribes of the mountains. Valphine cheerfully turned to leave, while Vukradin stared after her and observed, with some alarm, that she had just sent a group of religious maniacs to convert an entire mountain range of orcs.

The party made their way to the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine, eager to collect their share of the profits from the operation they had established with the wererats. Horia, the mine's sharp-eyed manager, greeted them with a detailed ledger and a proud smile, walking them through the startup costs, worker salaries, insurance premiums, remote danger bonuses, and the substantial cut taken by the Neverwinter Commission. After all of that, the party's dividend for five days of operation came to a grand total of thirty gold pieces. Vukradin and Brewbarry exchanged a long, pained look. Horia assured them that a promising new vein was close — the superstitious dwarven miner insisted the mountain was speaking to him — and that patience would yield far greater rewards. The wererats, meanwhile, were working with conspicuous diligence, clearly still intimidated by the party's last visit, and nervously raised the question of whether they might be permitted to leave their posts to fetch their promised cheese rations without being considered deserters.

The party departed the mine with their thirty gold pieces and a growing list of ambitions. They noted that the Neverwinter Commission's cut was something that would need to be renegotiated, and Brewbarry made a quiet mental note to strongly suggest to whoever was managing the dragon's recovered hoard that a finder's fee would be the right and proper thing to offer. Vukradin lamented that despite defeating dragons, clearing manses, and owning a gold mine, he still had not made meaningful progress toward funding his music studio. As the group turned their boots toward Phandalin, there was much to discuss — a town council to address, a legal process called escheatment that Valphine had apparently researched, and the small matter of a holy orc army now marching through the mountains in Lathander's name. Whatever came next, it was clear that the consequences of their choices were only just beginning to unfold.

## Locations
### Woodland Manse
A ruined stone manor overgrown with ivy and vines, formerly belonging to a scholar who studied elven ruins. It features a collapsed staircase, rafters infested with stirges, a library with broken bookcases, a master bedroom with a cast iron bathtub fed by a hot spring pump, and a dining hall decorated with crude, bloody drawings. The party cleared it of hostile creatures and discovered several hidden items. One Talosian anchorite remained at the manse and was dealt with; Grannoc himself had died in the assault on Falcon's Lodge.

### Falcon's Lodge
A hunting lodge where the party reports their success at the Woodland Manse. The Falcon has dressed several orcs in livery as employees. Known for its comfortable amenities, including soft satin bathrobes that Brewbarry was reluctant to return.

### Icepire Hold
The former quarters of Vorga's orc tribe, located in the mountains. The party escorts the orcs here, and it becomes the site where Valphine receives her divine revelation and establishes a new holy militant order of Lathander.

### Mountain's Toe Gold Mine
A gold mining operation staffed by wererats under the management of Horia. The party collects their first share of profits — a meager thirty gold pieces — and learns that a promising new vein may soon yield much greater returns.

### Phandalin
The nearby town where the party plans to return to discuss local governance and secure a cheese supply line for their mine workers.

## Items
### Staff of Bird Calls
A wooden staff found by Soma hidden in the master bedroom of the Woodland Manse. It allows the user to mimic the sounds of various birds. Soma discreetly stowed it away without telling the others, though it was later revealed under Valphine's Zone of Truth. The party considered it phenomenally useless for a druid.

### Dust of Disappearance
A tiny leather packet of magical powder found hidden inside a hollowed-out copy of 'Elvish Idioms' in the manse's library. Its existence was revealed when Valphine admitted to finding it under the Zone of Truth.

### Elvish Idioms
A rare, undamaged book found among the ruined shelves of the manse's library. It served as a hiding place for the Dust of Disappearance, which was tucked inside it.

### Twig Figures
Small cursed effigies made of twigs found on the mantelpiece in the manse's dining hall, each crafted to resemble one of the adventuring party members. When Valphine destroyed her own figure with magic, the remaining ones animated as twig blights.

### Candelabra
A decorative candelabra worth twenty-five gold pieces, scavenged from the manse's dining room by Prutha and discreetly handed to Valphine as a gift, with instructions not to mention it to the others.

### Falcon's Lodge Bathrobe
An exceptionally soft, satin-lined robe that Brewbarry took from Falcon's Lodge because he enjoys the texture. He was compelled to admit to taking it under Valphine's Zone of Truth, and ultimately agreed to return it.

### Boots of Elvenkind
Magical boots given to the party by Falcon as the promised reward for clearing the Woodland Manse. They allow the wearer to move silently.

### Falcon's Note
An unsealed letter addressed to the bards Rimardo and Corrin, written by the Falcon. It pitches them a permanent performance contract at his lodge, offering free room and board, a generous cut of proceeds, and excellent hunting.

### Horia's Ledger
A detailed financial record book maintained by Horia, documenting the gold mine's startup costs, worker salaries, insurance, remote danger bonuses, and the Neverwinter Commission's cut. Valphine questioned Horia verbally under Zone of Truth and confirmed the ledger's contents were honestly reported.

## Spells
### Rage
Brewbarry tapped into a primal fury, increasing his physical prowess and allowing him to assume a giant-like stature. He used it to leap up the collapsed staircase and tear through stirges with devastating cleaving blows.

### Vicious Mockery
Vukradin unleashed a string of magically-charged insults at a stirge, eroding its resolve and causing it to fail in its efforts to harm the party.

### Silvery Barbs
Vukradin used this spell to magically distract a creature that had succeeded on a saving throw, forcing it to re-roll and ultimately ensuring its demise.

### Bless
A divine favor cast to bolster the party's accuracy during the chaotic fight against the stirges in the Woodland Manse.

### Bardic Inspiration
Vukradin provided musical encouragement to allies, granting them extra focus to help land difficult hits during the stirge encounters.

### Word of Radiance
Valphine emanated a brilliant burst of radiant light, incinerating stirges that fell from the rafters onto her, and later destroying twig blights. Prutha celebrated each use as a demonstration of the power of the sun.

### Starry Wisp
Vukradin launched a mote of radiant starlight to instantly destroy one of the animated twig blights in the dining hall.

### Zone of Truth
Valphine cast this spell to create a magical aura preventing those within it from speaking deliberate lies. She used it to compel the party to confess any items taken from the Woodland Manse or Falcon's Lodge, and later to question Horia verbally about the accuracy of his mining ledger, confirming he was being honest.

## NPCs
### Stirges
Vicious, mosquito-like winged monsters that hide in the rafters of the Woodland Manse and attack by latching onto victims to drain their blood. The party systematically exterminated them throughout the upper levels of the manse.

### Twig Blights
Small animated plant creatures that sprang to life from cursed twig effigies on the mantelpiece in the manse's dining hall. They were quickly destroyed by the party.

### Prutha
A half-orc who has recently converted to the worship of Lathander. He is fiercely loyal to Valphine, whom he views as a glorious divine figure. He assists in combat, attempts to sneak Valphine a valuable candelabra, and is ultimately tasked with converting other orc tribes in the mountains to Lathander's cause.

### Boney
A skeletal companion who methodically pretends to eat pumpkins outside the manse despite having no need for food.

### Vorga
The warchief who leads her tribe. She is done with the Falcon's enthusiasm and requests that the party escort her tribe back to Icepire Hold. She shows mild interest in plunder but ultimately just wants to return home.

### Falcon
The owner of the lodge who commissioned the party to clear the Woodland Manse. He is a massive fan of the bards Rimardo and Corrin, calling them the finest act he has seen north of Neverwinter, and gives Vukradin a note to deliver to them offering a performance contract.

### Horia
The manager of the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine who maintains the financial ledger and explains the high operational costs to the party, presenting them with their first dividend of thirty gold pieces.

### The Dwarven Miner
A superstitious expert at finding gold veins who works at the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. He insists that the mountain must be treated with patience and that the gold 'speaks' to him, promising a big new vein is coming.

### The Wererats
The labor force of the Mountain's Toe Gold Mine. They are intimidated by the party and work hard to prove their efficiency, while also worrying about their promised cheese rations and whether they can safely leave their posts to fetch supplies.
