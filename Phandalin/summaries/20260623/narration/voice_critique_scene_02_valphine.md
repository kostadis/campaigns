## Flags

### [1] Mechanical — em-dashes in narration prose (11 instances)

Valphine's register is formal, interior, and built from long layered clauses, so most of these em-dashes are functioning as deliberate appositive interruptions and are defensible. House rule is still to convert narration em-dashes; below is each occurrence with a judgment and a replacement. None is locked dialogue (the two em-dashes inside `"..."` on lines 70 and 78 are correctly left alone).

> The Overbright never let me forget it had followed me indoors — light leaking under the shutters, the lamp-glow doubling off every polished tankard, the whole room a small persistent sting against the back of my eyes.

**Why:** Introduces a sensory list; a colon is the cleaner mark and reads more like her cataloguing voice.
**Suggested rewrite:** `...followed me indoors: light leaking under the shutters, the lamp-glow doubling off every polished tankard...`

> Vukradin asked his question the way he asks all his questions — as if money were a problem the world had merely forgotten to solve for him.

**Why:** Convertible to a comma with no loss; borderline (the dash gives a beat of contempt).
**Suggested rewrite:** `...the way he asks all his questions, as if money were a problem the world had merely forgotten to solve for him.`

> And so they did — the dragon, the henge, the boar that would not die until Soma's moonlight settled the matter — and the room loved it...

**Why:** Borderline-keep. The paired dashes wrap a list that already uses internal commas, so they aid clarity; if converting, make the first a colon and keep the close as a comma.
**Suggested rewrite:** `And so they did: the dragon, the henge, the boar that would not die until Soma's moonlight settled the matter, and the room loved it...`

> The owners of the recovered treasure had been located, he reported — more of them, all in Neverwinter, none here.

**Why:** Colon fits the report-and-tally rhythm better than a dash.
**Suggested rewrite:** `...had been located, he reported: more of them, all in Neverwinter, none here.`

> I felt the moment land — that flinch men give when a thing they have a category for stops fitting the category.

**Why:** The dash sets up an appositive definition; a colon does the same with more of her specimen-cataloguing tone.
**Suggested rewrite:** `I felt the moment land: that flinch men give when a thing they have a category for stops fitting the category.`

> This careful Gauntlet man, going pale at the thought that the Morning Lord might have reached down through all his sunlit creation and touched the one drow in the room — and his solution, his frightened, decent solution, was to send me to a priest.

**Why:** Joins two independent clauses with a contrast; a semicolon or full stop carries the verdict harder.
**Suggested rewrite:** `...and touched the one drow in the room. And his solution, his frightened, decent solution, was to send me to a priest.`

> Afraid that something *else* moved behind the gold — that a drow Lathanderite blessed this plainly meant the world had slipped a gear.

**Why:** Appositive elaboration; colon is cleaner and keeps the analytic register.
**Suggested rewrite:** `Afraid that something *else* moved behind the gold: that a drow Lathanderite blessed this plainly meant the world had slipped a gear.`

> The fool who taught me his rituals and never once suspected I had no intention of taking his weak gospel into my body — months, years, of bowing to a man who thought the Morning Lord was a god of comfort...

**Why:** The dash precedes a duration aside; a comma carries it without breaking the long clause.
**Suggested rewrite:** `...no intention of taking his weak gospel into my body, months, years, of bowing to a man who thought the Morning Lord was a god of comfort...`

> And then — turning the thought over the way I turn a blade in lamplight to find the edge — a better possibility opened, golden and warm at the back of my burning eyes.

**Why:** Borderline-keep. The paired dashes wrap an interior aside cleanly; if converting, commas work without disrupting the layered sentence.
**Suggested rewrite:** `And then, turning the thought over the way I turn a blade in lamplight to find the edge, a better possibility opened...`

### [2] Register-wrong vocabulary (scan B hit)

> He was learning the shape of his own gift and finding it cheaper than he'd hoped.

**Why:** "shape" is a flagged LLM-default register word; here it stands in for the *workings/economy* of Vukradin's bardic gift, which the sentence then prices ("cheaper than he'd hoped") — Valphine reads people as systems of utility, so a mechanism/cost noun is truer to her than a geometric one.
**Suggested rewrite:** `He was learning the price of his own gift and finding it cheaper than he'd hoped.` (or `the mechanism of his own gift`)

### [3] Voice-spec conflict — soft Lathander vocabulary

> ...a better possibility opened, golden and warm at the back of my burning eyes.

**Why:** "warm" is one of the surface-dweller words for her god the spec names as misreadings she has rejected (warmth, dawn, hope); pairing it approvingly beside "burning eyes" softens the radiant-indifferent-inexhaustible register the spec demands, even though the burn imagery partly redeems it. Borderline.
**Suggested rewrite:** `...a better possibility opened, golden and radiant at the back of my burning eyes.` (or `golden and burning`)

## Verdict

A strong, deeply on-voice block — the cataloguing interiority, the power-and-hypocrisy reads, the body-present faith ("the dawn *burns*"), and the deliberate "soft, squishy / *Of his weak ways*, I corrected" self-policing of her own register are exactly right and need no touching. The only genuine substantive notes are one soft "warm" and one "shape"; the dominant recurring issue is the mechanical em-dash load, most of which is defensible to her formal register but should still be converted per house rule.
