# Session 2026-06-23

Date: Jun 23rd, 2026

## Summary
The party made their way back to Phandalin with full hearts and ambitious plans. Brewbarry had become consumed by a new vision — a bathrobe business, born from a deep personal desire to atone for generations of violence by bringing softness and comfort to the people of the world. Vukradin, meanwhile, lamented that despite slaying a dragon and clearing cursed mansions, his dream of funding a musical studio remained frustratingly out of reach. The group discussed pushing for direct mayoral elections, the legal redistribution of recovered treasure, and the still-unresolved matter of Prutha's missionary campaign among Vorga's tribe at Icespire Hold — a congregation of Lathander's Searing Pain of Justice now taking root in the mountains. Whatever came next, the consequences of their choices were only just beginning to unfold.

Upon arriving in Phandalin, the party was greeted warmly but without the fanfare of their earlier triumphs — yesterday's heroes, as it were. At the Stonehill Inn, Toblen welcomed them back with open arms, and Vukradin earned the whole house a round of the good beer with a rousing performance. Ser Kaelen, however, had more pressing matters on his mind. He informed the party that additional rightful owners of their recovered treasure had been located — in Neverwinter — and reminded them, with barely concealed frustration, that the mermaid statue belonging to Elara Meliamne had still not been returned. He also noticed Valphine's golden eyes and, with a look of quiet concern, suggested that when the party reached Neverwinter, Valphine should pay a visit to Brother Aldric Sunmantle at the Spire of the Morning Lord — the priest who first received her conversion — to seek guidance on what Lathander might be trying to tell her through the golden eyes.

The party then turned their attention to the town itself. After a failed attempt by Vukradin to pressure Harbin Wester into calling a public meeting, the townmaster eventually agreed to gather the citizens himself. Vukradin performed on the town green, drawing out residents from every corner of Phandalin — Qelline Alderleaf, Linene Graywind, Big Al Kalazorn, Ser Kaelen, and even Harbin Wester himself. When the crowd was warm and swaying, Vukradin stepped forward and delivered a passionate speech, declaring that treasure hoarded by monsters rightfully belonged to the people, not to adventurers or distant governments. He announced the creation of Universal Basic Treasure — UBT — and the crowd erupted in cheers.

Harbin Wester, sensing the political winds, immediately tried to take credit for the idea, stuttering through an endorsement and claiming the Privy Council had been considering something similar for weeks. Linene Graywind pulled out a Candlekeep text on income distribution and volunteered to lead a subcommittee to draft the formal rules. Jenna Roscoe, appalled by what she called mob rule, stormed out of Phandalin and headed back to Neverwinter ahead of the party — giving her a head start to brief Lord's Alliance contacts before the party arrived in the city. The party donated the fused dragon gold slag from Cryovain's hoard to the new UBT fund, though Soma quietly noted a troubling planar anomaly surrounding it — the kind of magic that would require an expert far beyond Phandalin's reach to unravel. Brewbarry and Soma were declared honorary citizens of Phandalin so they could qualify for the fund, and the crowd cheered as though it were the most natural thing in the world.

With the political business settled, the party enjoyed a lengthy wine and cheese tasting hosted by Qelline Alderleaf, during which Brewbarry surprised everyone by selecting the finest creamy blue cheese with genuine discernment. Afterward, Brewbarry sought out Linene at the trading house and laid out his vision: a bathrobe factory, blessed by Lathander's priests, bearing Vukradin's logo, and distributed through the Lion Shield Coster. Linene agreed to serve as his wealth management consultant, wrote him a letter of introduction to the Counting House in Neverwinter, and mentioned a troubling supply chain disruption with the Lion Shield Coster that she hoped the party might investigate. Before departing, Ser Kaelen delivered one final piece of news — a note identifying the Alagondar family as the rightful owners of the necklace of fireballs, their ancestor's belongings stolen by grave robbers a generation ago, with a man named Perrin Alagondar waiting to be found.

The journey to Neverwinter was uneventful, save for Brewbarry's relentless monologue about thread counts, embroidery patterns, and the ideal softness of a luxury bathrobe. Upon arriving at the city gates, the party was checked against a writ sent ahead from Phandalin and welcomed through. Almost immediately, a trio of young humans in roughhide cloaks with charcoal tribal markings on their forearms stopped dead in their tracks — they recognized Brewbarry. The fans were starstruck, but when they realized Vukradin was standing right beside him, the debate turned awkward: one insisted Vukradin had sold out, that his early recordings were the real art, the authentic stuff before fame corrupted him. Vukradin, rather than argue, simply pulled out an old song from deep in his memory and played it right there at the city gates, leaving the young musician in stunned, reverent silence as a small crowd gathered around them.

As the party moved deeper into the Protector's Enclave, they passed a carved stone plaque honoring the "liberators of the Ordning" and a Lord's Alliance herald distributing broadsheets featuring a dwarf named Orsik, Defender of Parnast and Champion of the North. The back of the broadsheet displayed a map of the Lord's Alliance's territorial jurisdiction — with a dotted line extending into Neverwinter Wood, Leilon, and Phandalin itself. The party exchanged glances but pressed on, eventually finding a high-end armorer who worked with exotic materials. The proprietor recognized Valphine immediately as the Drow follower of the Morning Lord that Brother Aldric had spoken of, and he was overcome with reverence. He agreed to craft the party's dragon scales into a suit of scale mail for Brewbarry — emblazoned with the emblem of Lathander on the back — for a reduced price of three hundred gold pieces in exchange for a blessing, all while enthusiastically declaring that nothing bad could ever come of a Drow embracing the warmth of the sun. The party arranged to collect the finished armor before leaving the city, and with that, Neverwinter opened before them, full of unfinished business and unanswered questions.

### The Universal Basic Treasure Proclamation
#### Vukradin gathers the citizens of Phandalin to propose a radical new way of distributing adventuring wealth.
- Vukradin attempts to intimidate Harbin Wester into calling a town meeting but rolls only a 10. Harbin: *"your intimidation lands as flatly as your sweet tongue would have landed well."* The mayor refuses; Vukradin resolves to gather the people himself.
- The party holds a performance on the town green — Vukradin's idea to throw a "rock concert" to get the crowd happy and bouncing, with Brewbarry booming his voice to announce the gathering — drawing out residents and council members alike (Petunia, Qelline Alderleaf, Big Al Kalazorn, Linene Graywind, Ser Kaelen, Toblen, Elsa, Barthen, the woodworker, Brin and Giles).
- Vukradin addresses the town, criticizing the government's handling of treasure and proposing 'Universal Basic Treasure' (UBT), declaring the recovered gold was stolen from the people and should be returned to them.

- Vukradin commits, on his special abilities, to returning treasure to rightful owners where possible and distributing the rest as UBT — rolling a 17 with advantage where a flat roll would have been a 1.
- The crowd cheers enthusiastically; children ask whether they'll get UBT too, with Soma noting they'll never have to work a day in their lives "as long as adventurers come."
- Harbin Wester unexpectedly supports the plan, stuttering over Vukradin's name and claiming the Privy Council had been considering a similar initiative for days, then leading cheers of "Huzzah, huzzah, huzzah!"
- The town pauses to settle a name for the adventuring group; suggestions include "Slayers of Cryovain" and joking band names, with Soma cast as "manager" and tour guide rather than band member.
- The party officially donates a fused slag of dragon gold to the new UBT fund — only to learn it is fused inside a magical containment that must first be extracted; Vukradin tasks Ser Kaelen with handling it.
- Ser Kaelen raises a "sidebar" on eligibility — does the Order of the Gauntlet get a share, and what defines a "citizen of Phandalin"? — sparking a lengthy debate over property owners, renters, building a wall, immigrants from Neverwinter, a 50%+ residency requirement, primary residence, paying taxes in Phandalin, and attesting under a Zone of Truth spell.

- Linene Graywind produces a Candlekeep text — "the Sage's Universal Theorem for Income Distribution" — and offers to lead a subcommittee, under Vukradin's supervision, to draft the distribution rules within a week or two.
- The party negotiates a 5% "organic fair trade" cut of their treasure — gold effectively "laundered" through the UBT fund and returned to them, distributed tax-free.
- Jenna Roscoe, appalled by the populist move, leaves town in a huff and rides off toward Neverwinter; Vukradin: *"I don't know who you are, and I don't care."*
- The party discusses the magical nature of the dragon slag — debating Dispel Magic and Remove Curse — with Soma identifying a planar anomaly (via Meril's Staff) that requires an expert in Neverwinter to resolve.

The ruler of Neverwinter, mentioned as taxing the populace heavily to fund the reconstruction of Castle Never — and, the party drily notes, unlikely to be a fan of UBT applied to his own gold.

### Orsik
A dwarf featured on Lord's Alliance broadsheets as Defender of Parnast and Champion of the North, depicted in a wood-cut portrait in full plate, blunt-nosed and serious.

### Perrin Alagondar
Presents himself as heir to the Alagondar royal line and claims the Necklace of Fireballs as a recovered dynastic treasure. **GM note (not yet known to party):** Perrin is a Nasher / Son of Alagondar loyalist — his claim to the necklace is sincere as a restoration symbol, but the provenance documentation was manufactured by a Margaster-connected source. The real beneficiary of the handoff is House Margaster, who wants the necklace as an arson weapon, not Perrin, who wants it as a banner. Ser Kaelen located him in good faith through a compromised source and does not know the match is forged. Ch. 47+ encounter; see `notes/session_prep/20260623_necklace-of-fireballs-neverwinter.md`.

### The Proprietor
A high-end armorer and follower of the Morning Lord who operates a shop in the Protector's Enclave of Neverwinter. He recognizes Valphine on sight as the drow Brother Aldric spoke of, is deeply impressed by her status as a chosen of Lathander, and agrees to craft dragon scale armor for Brewbarry at a reduced rate of 300 gold pieces in exchange for a blessing on himself and his family — declaring the embrace of the drow vindicated before launching into a string of racial stereotypes.

## Items
### Mermaid Statue
An artifact recovered by the party long ago that the party believes belongs to Elara 'Seasong' Meliamne of Neverwinter. Ser Kaelen reminds the party that its return has been long delayed and is visibly upset they forgot about it. **Urgency:** House Margaster agents have previously been intercepting this statue. The party plans to deliver it during their visit to Neverwinter — delay increases the risk of interception. **GM note (not yet known to party):** Elara Meliamne was killed by House Margaster eight days before the party's arrival in Neverwinter. Her cousin, **Lord Cassian Meliamne**, now carries her anti-Margaster case and her commercial and genealogical reach in the city. Cassian is the relevant contact for the statue — and is actively building a case against Margaster that a second exposed forgery (the necklace provenance) would strengthen. See `notes/session_prep/20260623_necklace-of-fireballs-neverwinter.md`.

### Dragon Treasure Slag
A massive, fused hunk of gold from Cryovain's hoard (valued around 3,000+), encased in a magical containment field. The party donates it to the UBT fund, but it cannot be distributed until the magical fusion is resolved. The party debates Dispel Magic and Remove Curse; Soma identifies a planar anomaly around it via Meril's Staff, comparing it to "that stag" and "those weird gnome things," and concludes an expert in Neverwinter will be needed.

### Creamy Blue Cheese
A fine round of cheese selected by Brewbarry during a tasting session with Qelline Alderleaf. Chosen over the dried-out varieties as the finest, creamy and bluish, it impresses Qelline with Brewbarry's discerning taste.

### Linene's Note
A written recommendation from Linene to the Counting House in Neverwinter to assist Brewbarry in securing business capital for his bathrobe venture.

### Dragon Scales
Raw materials harvested from a dragon, brought to a high-end armorer in Neverwinter's Protector's Enclave to be fashioned into protective scale mail for Brewbarry; the party considers bartering them (rather than Soma's Staff of Bird Calls) toward the commission cost.

### Dragon Scale Mail
A custom suit of armor commissioned for Brewbarry, crafted from dragon scales by a Morning Lord-following armorer in Neverwinter for 300 gold. It features a prominent emblem of Lathander on the back and is to be picked up before the party leaves the city.

### Lord's Alliance Broadsheet
A publication distributed by a blue-and-gold herald in the Protector's Enclave, featuring a wood-cut portrait of Orsik, Defender of Parnast and Champion of the North, and a back-page map showing the Lord's Alliance's territorial jurisdiction, including a dotted line extending into Neverwinter Wood, Leilon, and Phandalin.

### Boots of Elvenkind
Held by Vukradin (acquired ch43 at the Woodland Manse). Not featured in this session's events but remain in his possession.

### Staff of Bird Calls
Soma's magical staff, which the party considers bartering to help cover the costs of the dragon scale armor commission — though Vukradin objects that, as "blood money," Phandalin law now requires it be turned over to the people.

### Necklace of Fireballs
Grave-goods recovered from the Alagondar burial mound at Dragonbarrow (ch33) — the same site where Lady Alagondar's ghost rose and stared toward the Ice Spires, and where Boney was found. The Alagondars are Neverwinter's deposed royal dynasty; Neverember rules in the absence of their heir. Ser Kaelen's recovery office matched the necklace to a claimant (Perrin Alagondar) and the party intends to return it. **GM note (not yet known to party):** the provenance match was routed through a Margaster-connected source; the claimant is a Nasher loyalist and the "rightful owner" designation is a forgery. The necklace is seven beads of Fireball — a portable arson arsenal. The real beneficiary is House Margaster, not the restoration cause. Ch. 47+ setup; see `notes/session_prep/20260623_necklace-of-fireballs-neverwinter.md`.

## Spells
### Zone of Truth
Mentioned during the UBT debate as a proposed requirement for citizens to attest under oath to their residency status (50%+ of the year, primary residence) in order to qualify for the Universal Basic Treasure fund.

### Dispel Magic
Discussed by the party as a potential method to break the magical fusion on the dragon gold slag before it can be distributed to the people of Phandalin.

### Remove Curse
Suggested by the party as an alternative way to treat the tainted dragon treasure slag, prompting Brewbarry's musing "when you remove the curse, where does the curse go?" — though it was ultimately determined that the magic involved is planar in nature and beyond a simple curse removal.
