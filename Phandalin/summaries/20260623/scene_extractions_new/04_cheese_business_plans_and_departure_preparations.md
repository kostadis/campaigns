# Cheese, Business Plans, and Departure Preparations

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party enjoys a cheese tasting with Qelline Alderleaf and Brewbarry discusses his new business venture before the group prepares to head to Neverwinter.
- Qelline Alderleaf, Big Al Kalazorn, and Linene observe that the extra cash in people's hands creates opportunity to sell more cheese, and offer the party a tasting from across their range — fancy, not-so-fancy, expensive, even cheese sorted by which part of the field Petunia grazed.
- Valphine asks for "the regular one," prompting Qelline to mutter "Philistines."
- The party engages in a multi-hour cheese and wine tasting with Qelline Alderleaf and Big Al Kalazorn, with the were-rat assistants (Brin and Giles) to be sent later to collect the cheese.
- Brewbarry tastes the cheeses, sets aside the dried-out ones, and selects a creamy, bluish cheese as his favorite, impressing Qelline: *"who would have thought that Brewbarry was a man of taste and vision?"* Big Al, asked if he knew he had friends of such class, replies: *"neither did I."*
- Brewbarry consults with Linene at the trading house about his plan to start a bathrobe factory as a form of personal redemption — sourced from Phandalin cotton, blessed by Lathander's priests, bearing Lathander's logo, a note for Vukradin's upcoming music studio, and a 5% discount for buyers of Vukradin's album ("Black Cylinder").
- Vukradin needles Brewbarry that he is "the ideas guy" while Linene does all the work; Brewbarry confirms: *"this is my vision."*
- Linene agrees to act as a wealth management consultant for Brewbarry and suggests he seek capital at the Counting House in Neverwinter, writing him a letter of introduction.
- Brewbarry warns Linene against screwing him on this personal quest; she replies in earnest: *"Brewbarry, this is your personal quest, now it is my personal quest."* Valphine's Insight (19) confirms her sincerity — and that the supply-chain problem is of genuine, deep concern to her.
- Linene mentions a supply chain issue with the Lion Shield Coster — their formerly cheap distribution method has been replaced by costlier ones — and asks the party to investigate in Neverwinter while flagging her past obsession with planar-magic goods (the phasing animal hides from the Whispering Grove).
- Ser Kaelen leaves a note identifying the Alagondar family as the rightful owners of the necklace of fireballs, whose ancestor's belongings were stolen by grave robbers a generation ago, with Perrin Alagondar named as the claimant. The note reads, in part: *"a family that lost their deads belonging to grave robbers a generation ago and never stopped looking. The recovery office matched the provenance last 10 day. You can put it down, you can actually do the thing you keep saying you want to do."*
- The party decides to head toward Neverwinter, explicitly abandoning the quest for the Shrine of Savras.
