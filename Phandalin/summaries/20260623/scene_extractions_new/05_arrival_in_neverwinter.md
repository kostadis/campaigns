# Arrival in Neverwinter

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party arrives at the gates of Neverwinter after an uneventful journey, encountering fans of their past exploits and navigating the city's changing political landscape.
- The party bypasses Mountain's Toe Gold Mine despite the planned payday stop, heading directly to Neverwinter. Mine money collection, Zeleen's Earthstone Gem retrieval, and Horia's secret addendum remain outstanding obligations.
- The party travels to Neverwinter with Brewbarry spending the entire trip talking about bathrobe designs, thread counts, and the softest possible thread, until Vukradin tells him: *"Dude. ... bathrobes. Give it a rest. You sound like me with UBT."*
- They consider, then dismiss, side-stops at the Hunting Lodge, Dragon Barrow, and Logger's Camp — all previously cleared.
- As they approach, they see Castle Never in the distance — "known as Castle Never because it has never been conquered" — and note Lord Neverember's heavy taxation funding its reconstruction.
- The party passes through the city gates after being checked against a writ sent ahead from Phandalin, confirming they are expected.
- A trio of young humans in early 20s — roughhide trim cloaks, charcoal tribal markings on their forearms, one in a painted leather helm with red-daubed eye slits, another with an ornamental never-used hand axe, all barefoot on the cobblestones — stop dead, recognizing Brewbarry. They are nodding along to dissonant, technically accomplished music from a nearby window that "sounds like someone furious at beauty" — which Valphine recognizes as Vukradin's old work.
- A guard, starstruck, asks Brewbarry to sign a paper for his son; Brewbarry offers to sign in blood and asks for a drop of the guard's, prompting an awkward decline.
- The fans recognize Vukradin too but devolve into a "sold out" debate, praising his early, pre-fame recordings as the "real stuff" and dismissing the famous man in front of them as not the real Vukerton.
- Vukradin learns that his old haunt, the Common Chord, has become an exclusive venue with weeks of reservations and sky-high prices due to his rising fame — described by one fan as part of the displacement of the common man from the docks as the Protector's Enclave expands. Vukradin offers an autograph in exchange for a table; the fan can't deliver one.
- Vukradin performs an authentic old song "from the vault" for the skeptical fan, letting the local musician jam along, drawing a crowd and creating a scene at the gate — with more Brewbarry-styled fans gathering, hinting at a growing movement.
- The party observes a carved stone plaque honoring the "liberators of the Ordning" (crediting the combined strength of the Lord's Alliance for restoring the giant kingdoms) and a Lord's Alliance herald in blue and gold distributing propaganda broadsheets.
- The party notes that the Lord's Alliance broadsheet — fronted by Orsik, Defender of Parnast — shows their territorial jurisdiction now includes Neverwinter Wood, Leilon, and Phandalin via a dotted line.
- The group plans to commission dragon scale armor (deciding to barter Dragon Scales rather than the "blood money" Staff of Bird Calls) and discusses returning the necklace of fireballs to the Alagondar family.
