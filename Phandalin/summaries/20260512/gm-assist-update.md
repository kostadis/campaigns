Date: May 12th, 2026

## Summary
The party arrived at the Circle of Thunder, a henge of ancient standing stones crackling with dark energy, to find two half-orc anchorites of Talos — Flenz and Narux — leading a sinister summoning ritual. The air was thick with ozone and the low hum of the stones, while a swarm of twig blight minions prowled the perimeter. Rather than chanting, the anchorites danced in eerie silence, their movements precise and deliberate. Valphine recognized the ceremony for what it was — a summoning — while the rest of the party struggled to make sense of the wordless ritual. With no time to waste, the group readied themselves for battle.

The fight erupted with terrifying force when one of the anchorites unleashed a bolt of lightning that tore through several party members standing in a line. But Vukradin answered swiftly and decisively, reaching deep into his power to paralyze both spellcasters at once, freezing them mid-dance and leaving them utterly helpless. With the anchorites locked in place, the party surged forward. Boney charged in and tore at one anchorite with his bare-jawed bite, Prutha hurled a javelin with furious hatred, and Soma transformed into a massive elk and delivered a thunderous charging ram into one of the frozen priests. Valphine summoned a swirling host of spectral guardians that radiated holy light, tearing through the twig blights in waves of radiant energy. Brewbarry hurled his handaxe again and again at the swarm, cutting down blight after blight with thrown strikes (the axe being a returning-weapon improv he called "Elemental Cleaver" at the table), while Vukradin peppered the remaining anchorite with shimmering bolts of starlight. Soma, still in giant elk form, delivered the killing blow — wheeling on the last paralyzed anchorite and trampling him under hoof, ending the battle.

In the silence that followed, the storm above slowly unraveled — the clouds peeling back in a reluctant spiral, the ozone fading to the smell of wet leaves and old rain, and the standing stones going utterly inert. When the party removed the boar-head masks from the fallen anchorites, they found two young half-orc faces beneath them, their necks and forearms covered in elaborate tattoos — Flenz with storm clouds and coiling spirals up the neck (each with an open eye), Narux with crossed boar-tusks repeated down both forearms. At the center of the henge lay a deer corpse split open from throat to belly, its heart replaced by a hand-sized hollow of black, glassy material that was cool to the touch and still faintly humming. The party also recovered a mysterious leather binding glyph — a leather thong strung with a single boar tusk inscribed with a strange rune — from one of the fallen priests. Valphine identified it as a magical leash, a tool of binding and restraint, and pocketed it carefully.

The aftermath brought its own kind of chaos. The party found holy symbols of Talos on the bodies, and an immediate argument broke out over what to do with them. Vukradin wanted to return them to their rightful owners, while Valphine wanted to take them for further study. Prutha pointed out that the owners were dead, and solved the problem by destroying the symbols himself, reflecting his anger at the cult leaders. The orc warrior then turned to Valphine and acknowledged the power of Lathander's dawn with a series of approving grunts and a wave of his great axe — a small but meaningful gesture of respect. Quietly, he added: "My great-uncle said dawn comes for everyone. Even these ones. I did not think dawn would feel quiet." Near the crown of Meril's Staff, Soma noticed a fourth bud — tight, green, the size of a thumbnail — had appeared. The staff gains as Soma accepts her role.

Brewbarry, driven by an unshakeable thirst, led the group into the series of caves surrounding the henge in search of something fermented. The first cave yielded a live boar, which Vukradin immediately attempted to unsettle with a barrage of magical insults — only for the boar to shrug off every word with complete indifference before charging. The ensuing skirmish ended with Valphine finishing the creature off with a burst of radiant light, leaving Brewbarry to note approvingly that they now had radiated pork, perfect for a barbecue. A second cave — Flenz's quarters — held more penned boars beneath a lintel stone carved with the image of a boar chasing a fleeing humanoid, and more importantly, a satchel sealed with the lightning-bolt wax sigil of Talos. Inside, Valphine found three letters of cult correspondence: one identifying Grannoc as a rival anchorite at the Woodland Manse whose ritual was faltering ("Your storm holds. Grannoc's does not. He is reckless; he will summon before the Lodge is ready"); another mentioning a "boy from the eastern clan" being fed stories, with a warning that he must never be told about the leash; and a third warning that if the henge fell, the manse would summon in its place — and if the manse fell, the unnamed author would summon Gorthok personally, ending with "The boar comes. The storm comes. You are not to fail twice." A separate, half-burned vellum scrap from Yargath's firepit in another cave — written in a different, sharper hand and signed only "K." — read: "…dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives." While Vukradin was distracted, Valphine quietly noticed a hidden compartment beneath the cave floor and slipped a potion of invulnerability into his pack without a word.

The final cave was Narux's quarters, which yielded no drinks for Brewbarry but did produce a buried +1 Shield and a torn map fragment of Neverwinter Wood. The map's cult semantics were specific: the Circle of Thunder was X'd through in advance (the anchorites had expected to lose the henge), the Woodland Manse was double-circled (the active backup ritual site where Grannoc was preparing the same ritual), and Falcon's Hunting Lodge was drawn as a target — the endpoint both rituals pointed at. As they prepared to leave, conversation turned to Falcon the hunter, whose relationship with the group had grown strained — he had felt betrayed when the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, and orcs had subsequently attacked his lodge, killing his girlfriend in the process. Valphine considered how best to smooth things over, recalling Falcon's earlier acceptance of her musical-instrument proposal at the end of the druid conclave, and the deferred boots of elvenkind reward tied to clearing the Manse. With the Circle of Thunder silenced and the ritual stopped, the party turned their eyes toward the road ahead, carrying with them secrets, a mysterious leash, the "K." signature, and the unsettling promise of worse things yet to come.

## Locations
### Circle of Thunder
A henge of standing stones where Anchorites of Talos were performing a summoning ritual involving dancing and lightning magic. The atmosphere was heavy with the smell of ozone and the hum of magical energy until the party intervened. The site is a repurposed druidic ritual location, not an anchorite construction. The site also contained three caves used by the anchorites for storage and keeping penned boars (Flenz in C2 NW, Narux in C3 NE, Yargath in C4 SE), where the party found cryptic notes, a satchel, a potion of invulnerability, a +1 Shield, and a map fragment.

### Falcon's Hunting Lodge
A location drawn as a target on the discovered map fragment — the cult's intended endpoint for both summoning rituals, not itself a ritual site. The home of Falcon the hunter; previously attacked by orcs after the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse threat in time.

### Woodland Manse
A site double-circled on the discovered map fragment — identified by the cult correspondence as the active backup summoning ritual site, where the anchorite **Grannoc** is preparing the same ritual the party just interrupted at the henge. The party previously failed to deal with a threat there, which led directly to the orc attack on Falcon's Lodge. With the henge ritual broken, the Manse becomes the live threat.

## Items
### Brewbarry's Handaxes (thrown)
Brewbarry has multiple handaxes per his sheet; in the henge fight he threw them at the swarm of twig blights, calling the action by an improvised name ("Elemental Cleaver") at the table. The Halberd +1 is his primary melee weapon and was not used in this beat. Handaxe counts as melee even when thrown, so his barbarian killing-with-melee-weapon bonus action still triggered.

### Holy Symbols of Talos
Religious icons found on the fallen anchorites. After a heated debate — Vukradin wanting to return them, Prutha pointing out the owners were dead — Prutha destroyed them. Valphine then placed one on the skull of a fallen anchorite and smashed it with her mace for good measure.

### The Leash
A leather thong with a single boar tusk inscribed with a binding glyph, found on one of the fallen anchorites. Valphine identified it as a magical binding restraint. The cryptic notes found nearby warned that the boy from the eastern clan must not yet be told about it.

### Black Glassy Hollow
A hand-sized object of black, glassy material found where the heart of a sacrificed deer should have been. It was cool to the touch and hummed faintly with the same energy as the standing stones of the henge.

### Satchel of Talos
A leather satchel sealed with a wax sigil depicting a circle with a single vertical lightning bolt — the canonical symbol of Talos. Found in Flenz's quarters (C2, the NW cave), it contained three letters of cult correspondence.

### Cryptic Notes
Two distinct sets of correspondence, in different hands. **Flenz's satchel (C2)** held three letters in the same hand, sealed with Talos's lightning-bolt sigil: (1) "Your storm holds. Grannoc's does not. He is reckless; he will summon before the Lodge is ready" — establishing Grannoc as a rival anchorite at the Woodland Manse whose ritual is faltering. (2) "The Chosen is pleased with the boy from the eastern clan. Continue feeding him with stories. Do not yet tell him about the leash." (3) The most alarming, verbatim: "If the henge falls, the manse summons in its place. If the manse falls, I summon myself. The boar comes. The storm comes. You are not to fail twice." **Yargath's firepit (C4)** held a half-burned vellum scrap in a different, sharper hand, signed: "…dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives. — K." The "K." signature ties this scrap to the unnamed "Chosen" referenced in Flenz's letters — the campaign's emerging antagonist.

### Potion of Invulnerability
A magical potion discovered by Valphine in a hidden compartment beneath the floor of one of the caves, found while Vukradin was distracted. Valphine slipped it silently into Vukradin's pack without his knowledge — added to what the rest of the table jokingly calls "the blacklist," the OOC ledger of loot kept off Vukradin's naïve notice.

### Torn Map Fragment
A piece of a map of Neverwinter Wood from Narux's quarters (C3), with three sites marked in specific cult semantics: the Circle of Thunder is X'd through in advance (the anchorites expected losses there), the Woodland Manse is double-circled (the active backup ritual site), and Falcon's Hunting Lodge is drawn as a target (the cult's intended endpoint for both rituals).

### +1 Shield
A magical shield recovered from a buried cache in Narux's quarters (C3, the final cave at the Circle of Thunder). Currently unassigned within the party.

### Boots of Elvenkind
Magical boots previously offered by Falcon as the reward for clearing the Woodland Manse, referenced during the party's discussion of their next steps. The quest — and the boots — remain deferred.

## Spells
### Lightning Bolt
Cast by one of the half-orc anchorites during combat, striking several party members who were lined up together. The party had to make dexterity saving throws to reduce the damage.

### Hold Person
Cast by Vukradin at third level, this spell successfully paralyzed both anchorite spellcasters simultaneously, preventing them from acting and making them vulnerable to automatic critical hits from melee attacks. It proved decisive in turning the tide of the battle.

### Bless
A supportive enchantment active during the battle that allowed party members to add a bonus die to their saving throws. Stacked on the Bless D4 was a College of Eloquence bardic inspiration D8, applied to the same saving throw via Vukradin's Eloquence-Bard feature (which extends the inspiration die to a save rather than the standard ability check).

### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Valphine, this spell summoned spectral guardians that swirled around her in a radius, dealing radiant damage to enemies that entered or started their turn in the area. It proved highly effective against the twig blights and was maintained through the cave exploration to finish off the boar.

### Starry Wisp
A luminous projectile launched by Vukradin to strike the remaining anchorite from a distance of 60 feet, dealing significant damage.

### Vicious Mockery
A cantrip used by Vukradin to psychically insult and wound a boar found within the caves. The boar rolled a natural twenty on its saving throw, completely resisting the mockery, before combat broke out.

## NPCs
### Flenz and Narux (Anchorites of Talos)
Two young half-orc Anchorites of Talos wearing rotting boar-head masks and bearing elaborate storm-themed tattoos. **Flenz** wore storm clouds and coiling spirals up the neck, each with an open eye; **Narux** wore crossed boar-tusks repeated down both forearms — "different markings, same hand inked them." Both were younger than the party expected. They led a summoning ritual at the Circle of Thunder using dancing and lightning magic, and were ultimately paralyzed by Hold Person and slain. They carried clubs, hide armor, and holy symbols of Talos. **Flenz's quarters (C2)** held the satchel of cult correspondence and the Potion of Invulnerability; **Narux's quarters (C3)** held the torn map fragment, his sketches of Gorthok, and a buried +1 Shield.

### Twig Blights
Numerous small, animated plant creatures that served as minions for the anchorites, attacking the party in a swarm before being decimated by area-of-effect magic and physical attacks.

### Boney
A talking skeletal horse that serves as a companion to the party. He participated in combat by tearing into twig blights with a bare-jawed bite (1d10 damage in stat-block terms; narrated as a bite in-fiction), scored an autocritical hit on a paralyzed anchorite, and showed protective instincts toward Soma.

### Prutha
An orc warrior — first Lathander convert and new orc ally of the party — who harbors a deep hatred for the anchorites of Talos. He charged into combat with javelins and his greataxe throughout the fight. He is fiercely loyal and follows the party closely, but is extremely literal-minded: when Vukradin argued the Talosian holy symbols needed to be returned to their owners, Prutha pointed at the dead anchorites — "We found owners. We give to them" — before destroying the symbols himself. He spoke quietly after the fight, telling Valphine: "My great-uncle said dawn comes for everyone. Even these ones. I did not think dawn would feel quiet." — his first introspective acknowledgment of grief and the first time he articulates his Lathander theology aloud. NPC companion, DM-controlled.

### Falcon
A hunter who resides at his namesake lodge. He has a strained relationship with the party due to perceived betrayals — the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, leading to an orc attack on his lodge and the death of his girlfriend. He had previously offered boots of elvenkind as the reward for clearing the Woodland Manse, and accepted Valphine's proposal involving a musical instrument at the end of the druid conclave.

### Adabra
An NPC the party previously encountered and attempted to save, though she insisted she did not need saving. The party discussed her briefly, noting the ambiguity of whether she actually needed rescuing.

### Soma
A druid companion who transforms into a giant elk form for combat. She participated in the battle by delivering a charging ram against a paralyzed anchorite and later trampling the last paralyzed anchorite under hoof for the killing blow. In elk form she cannot fit into the cave tunnels, so she and Boney stood watch outside during the cave exploration.
