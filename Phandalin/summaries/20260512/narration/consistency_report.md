## ISSUE 5: Prutha's Javelin — Natural 20 on a Ranged Attack Does Not Auto-Crit Against Paralyzed Target (Rules Clarification That May Confuse Future Sessions)

**Location:** Scenes → The Battle at the Circle of Thunder

**Issue:** The recap notes Prutha *"scoring a natural 20 on the attack roll; because it is a ranged attack the hit does not auto-crit."* This explanation is incorrect under standard 5e rules. A natural 20 is always a critical hit regardless of attack type (ranged or melee). The reason a ranged attack would *not* automatically crit against a paralyzed target is that the paralyzed condition grants advantage and auto-crit only to melee attacks made within 5 feet — not to ranged attacks. A natural 20 still crits; it is the *paralyzed condition bonus* (auto-crit for melee within 5 feet) that does not apply to ranged attacks.

**Evidence:** D&D 5e rules: Paralyzed condition — attack rolls against a paralyzed creature have advantage, and any attack that hits the creature is a critical hit *if the attacker is within 5 feet*. A natural 20 is always a critical hit by the attack roll rules, independently of the paralyzed condition.

**Suggested fix:** Correct the note to: *"Because it is a ranged attack, the hit does not benefit from the paralyzed condition's auto-crit bonus (which applies only to melee attacks within 5 feet). However, the natural 20 itself is still a critical hit."* Clarify in the record whether the 11 points of damage recorded reflects a crit or not, as this affects the accuracy of the combat log.

## ISSUE 12: Bless Described as Granting a Bonus to Saving Throws via a D4 and a Bardic Inspiration D8 Together

**Location:** Scenes → The Battle at the Circle of Thunder; Spells → Bless

**Issue:** The recap states Gary *"had rolled two ones on his bonus dice — a 1 on the D4 and a 1 on the bardic inspiration D8"* during the lightning bolt saving throw, implying both Bless (D4) and Bardic Inspiration (D8) were simultaneously active and applied to the same saving throw. Bless grants a D4 to attack rolls and saving throws. Bardic Inspiration (College of Eloquence) can in some versions be applied to ability checks, not typically saving throws (depending on edition/subclass specifics). More importantly, the framing presents these as two separate stacking bonus dice on a single saving throw, which should be flagged for rules verification.

**Evidence:** Party document does not specify the exact Bard subclass mechanics for Vukradin's Bardic Inspiration usage on saving throws. College of Eloquence's Bardic Inspiration features focus on ability checks (Unsettling Words targets Wisdom saving throws as a specific ability, but the general Bardic Inspiration applies to checks, not saves, in standard 5e).

**Suggested fix:** Verify whether the table's house rules or the specific Bardic Inspiration use here (possibly Unsettling Words or a different mechanic) permitted the D8 to apply to a saving throw. If it was a house ruling or a misapplication, note it as such so it is not treated as a precedent in future sessions.
