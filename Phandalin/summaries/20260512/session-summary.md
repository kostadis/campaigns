# Chapter 41 Unraveling the Storm God's Secrets

Date: May 12th, 2026

## Summary
The party arrived at the Circle of Thunder, a henge of ancient standing stones crackling with dark energy, to find two half-orc anchorites of Talos leading a sinister summoning ritual. The air was thick with ozone and the low hum of the stones, while a swarm of twelve twig blight minions prowled the perimeter. Rather than chanting, the anchorites danced in eerie silence, their movements precise and deliberate. Valphine recognized the ceremony for what it was — a summoning — while the rest of the party struggled to make sense of the wordless ritual, with Soma, Vukradin, and Brewbarry each rolling poorly on their religion checks. With no time to waste, the group readied themselves for battle.

The fight erupted with terrifying force when one of the anchorites unleashed a bolt of lightning that tore through several party members standing in a line. But Vukradin answered swiftly and decisively, reaching deep into his power to paralyze both spellcasters at once, freezing them mid-dance and leaving them utterly helpless. With the anchorites locked in place, the party surged forward. Boney charged in and tore at one anchorite with his bare-jawed bite, Prutha hurled a javelin with furious hatred, and Soma transformed into a massive elk and delivered a thunderous charging ram into one of the frozen priests. Valphine summoned a swirling host of spectral guardians that radiated holy light, tearing through the twig blights in waves of radiant energy. Brewbarry hurled his handaxe again and again at the swarm, cutting down blight after blight with thrown strikes (the axe being a returning-weapon improv he called "Elemental Cleaver" at the table), while Vukradin peppered the remaining anchorite with shimmering bolts of starlight. Soma, still in giant elk form, delivered the killing blow — wheeling on the last paralyzed anchorite and trampling him under hoof, ending the battle.

In the silence that followed, the storm above slowly unraveled — the clouds peeling back in a reluctant spiral, the ozone fading to the smell of wet leaves and old rain, and the standing stones going utterly inert. When the party removed the boar-head masks from the fallen anchorites, they found two young half-orc faces beneath them, their necks and forearms covered in elaborate tattoos of storm clouds, coiling spirals, and crossed boar tusks. At the center of the henge lay a deer corpse split open from throat to belly, its heart replaced by a hand-sized hollow of black, glassy material that was cool to the touch and still faintly humming. The party also recovered a mysterious leather binding glyph — a leather thong strung with a single boar tusk inscribed with a strange rune — from one of the fallen priests. Valphine identified it as a magical leash, a tool of binding and restraint, and pocketed it carefully.

The aftermath brought its own kind of chaos. The party found holy symbols of Talos on the bodies, and an immediate argument broke out over what to do with them. Vukradin wanted to return them to their rightful owners, while Valphine sought to take them for further study. Prutha solved the problem decisively by pointing out that the owners were dead and then destroying the symbols himself, reflecting his deep anger at the cult leaders. He then turned to Valphine and acknowledged the power of Lathander's dawn with a series of approving grunts and a wave of his great axe — a small but meaningful gesture of respect.

Brewbarry, driven by an unshakeable thirst, led the group into the series of caves surrounding the henge in search of something fermented. The first cave yielded a live boar, which Vukradin immediately attempted to unsettle with a barrage of magical insults — only for the boar to shrug off every word with a natural twenty on its saving throw before charging. The ensuing skirmish ended with Valphine finishing the creature off with a burst of radiant light, leaving Brewbarry to note approvingly that they now had radiated pork, perfect for a barbecue. A second cave held more penned boars beneath a lintel stone carved with the image of a boar chasing a fleeing humanoid, and more importantly, a satchel sealed with the lightning-bolt wax sigil of Talos in Flenz's quarters. Inside, Valphine found three letters of cult correspondence: one identifying Grannoc as a rival anchorite at the Woodland Manse whose ritual was faltering ("Your storm holds. Grannoc's does not. He is reckless; he will summon before the Lodge is ready"); another mentioning a "boy from the eastern clan" being fed stories with a warning that he must never be told about the leash; and a third warning that if the henge fell the manse would summon in its place, and if the manse fell, the unnamed author would summon Gorthok personally — "The boar comes. The storm comes. You are not to fail twice." A separate, half-burned vellum scrap from Yargath's firepit in another cave, written in a different sharper hand and signed only "K.," read: "…dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives." While Vukradin was distracted, Valphine quietly noticed a hidden compartment beneath the cave floor and slipped a potion of invulnerability into his pack without a word.

The final cave was Narux's quarters, which yielded no drinks for Brewbarry but did produce a buried +1 Shield and a torn map fragment of Neverwinter Wood. The map's cult semantics were specific: the Circle of Thunder was X'd through in advance (the anchorites had expected to lose the henge), the Woodland Manse was double-circled (the active backup ritual site where Grannoc was preparing the same ritual), and Falcon's Hunting Lodge was drawn as a target — the endpoint both rituals pointed at. As they prepared to leave, conversation turned to Falcon the hunter, whose relationship with the group had grown strained — he had felt betrayed when the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, and orcs had subsequently attacked his lodge, killing his girlfriend in the process. Valphine considered how best to smooth things over, recalling Falcon's earlier acceptance of her musical-instrument proposal at the end of the druid conclave, and the deferred boots of elvenkind reward tied to clearing the Manse. Back outside the henge, Soma noticed a fourth bud — tight, green, the size of a thumbnail — had appeared on Meril's Staff, the staff gaining as she accepted her role. With the Circle of Thunder silenced and the ritual stopped, the party turned their eyes toward the road ahead, carrying with them secrets, a mysterious leash, the "K." signature, and the unsettling promise of worse things yet to come.

## Scenes

### Pre-Battle Observations at the Circle of Thunder
#### The party arrives at the Circle of Thunder and observes a group of anchorites performing a mysterious summoning ritual, debating how to intervene.
- The party observes two half-orc spellcasters and twelve twig blight minions performing a ritual at the Circle of Thunder.
- Valphine rolls a 17 on her religion check and identifies the activity as a summoning ritual; Soma rolls a zero, Vukradin (rolling for Soma via proxy) also rolls poorly, and Brewbarry rolls a 10 — none of the others recognize the ceremony.
- The party debates whether to cast Silence to interrupt the ritual, but the DM confirms the anchorites are dancing rather than chanting — there is no vocal component to disrupt.
- Vukradin briefly considers whether the ritual can be terminated mechanically, consulting the module notes before determining the ritual will only be stopped by disrupting the dancers themselves.
- Brewbarry observes that the dancers resemble a "pantomime club," given the eerie silence.
- Initiative is rolled as the party prepares to engage; Vukradin wins several tie-breaker rolls against the party, going first among them.

### The Battle at the Circle of Thunder
#### The party engages in a fierce combat against Anchorites of Talos and their twig blight minions to stop a dark ritual.
- Twig blights activate first and dash toward the party; the DM notes twelve blights in total, prompting Vukradin to quip, "Two twigs and an anchorite — the start of a joke."
- An anchorite fires a lightning bolt, striking several party members who had lined up together; Vukradin had already foreseen this, remarking, "It's nice that we're all lined up in a perfect line there — that's convenient for him." The party makes DC12 dexterity saving throws; Valphine rolls a 6 and takes 31 points of damage, taking half for a total of 15.
- Vukradin casts Hold Person at third level (DC15 wisdom save), successfully paralyzing both anchorite spellcasters simultaneously; both fail their saves and are frozen mid-dance.
- Boney rushes forward and tears into one of the paralyzed anchorites with a bare-jawed bite, scoring an automatic critical hit for 5 points of damage, reducing that anchorite to 46 hit points.
- Prutha charges into the fray, hurling a javelin at the second paralyzed anchorite and scoring a natural 20 on the attack roll for 11 points of damage.
- Soma transforms into a giant elk and delivers a powerful charging ram against a paralyzed anchorite at 60 feet, scoring an automatic critical hit for 25 points of damage and reducing that anchorite to 10 hit points.
- Valphine summons Spirit Guardians in a 15-foot radius, swirling spectral light around him that obliterates twig blights in waves of radiant energy; the spell kills twig blights 3 and 4 outright in its first activation, and subsequently kills blights 6, 8, and others as Valphine moves through the battlefield.
- Bless is active during the battle; Gary notes he had rolled two ones on his bonus dice during the lightning bolt save — a 1 on the Bless D4 and a 1 on the College of Eloquence bardic inspiration D8 (Vukradin's Eloquence-Bard feature that extends the inspiration die to a saving throw). Vukradin calculates this as roughly a 1-in-40 chance of occurring.
- Vukradin uses Starry Wisp at 60 feet to strike the remaining anchorite for 14 points of damage, needing to move 25 feet to get within range before retreating.
- The anchorites continue to fail their wisdom saving throws each round to break free of Hold Person; Vukradin notes, "Hold Person is a very powerful spell."
- Brewbarry activates Rage and throws his handaxe (with a magically-returning-weapon improv he called "Elemental Cleaver"), rolling low on his initial attack but recovering with a bardic inspiration D4 and rolling a spectacular 8 on the die, producing a total to-hit of 19 and dealing 10 points of damage to kill one twig blight; he then uses a bonus action from his barbarian ability (killing with a melee weapon — handaxe counts as melee even when thrown) to kill a second blight, and follows up with a second attack to threaten a third. He explicitly notes Cleave doesn't apply since it requires a greataxe or halberd.
- Prutha charges the remaining anchorite through twig blights, killing twig 2 on his way in and then striking the anchorite with his greataxe for 13 points of damage including an autocritical bonus, reducing the anchorite to 16 hit points.
- Soma, in her elk form, smashes one of the standing stones of the henge rather than attacking — the DM later confirms this is a repurposed druidic ritual site.
- Soma, still in giant elk form, delivers the killing blow — wheeling on the last paralyzed anchorite and trampling him under hoof for the auto-critical finisher.
- The storm clouds above dissipate: "The clouds peel back from the hinge in a slow, reluctant spiral, like something exhaling. The pressure in your ears drops, the ozone smell fades to wet leaves and old rain. The standing stones, which had been humming faintly, go inert. But there's no thunderclap."
- Valphine also attempts to use her Cleric of Peace teleportation ability during combat — to intercept an attack on Soma by teleporting adjacent to the attacker so Brewbarry would take the hit instead — but the DM rules the mechanic requires the redirected attacker to be able to physically reach Brewbarry at the teleportation destination, which is not possible given the positioning, so the damage stays on Soma (3 points).

### Aftermath and Ideological Clashes
#### With the enemies defeated, the party investigates the ritual site and debates the fate of the religious artifacts, ending with Prutha destroying them in anger.
- The storm above slowly dissipates; the DM delivers an extended atmospheric description of the standing stones going inert and the ozone smell fading to wet leaves and old rain.
- The party discovers the gruesome remains of the ritual: a deer corpse split open from throat to belly, its heart replaced by a hand-sized hollow of black, glassy material — "not stone, not metal, cool to the touch, faintly humming the way stones were humming a minute ago."
- The fallen anchorites are revealed to be younger half-orcs than expected, with elaborate storm-themed tattoos: one has storm clouds and coiling spirals up the neck, each with an open eye; the other has crossed boar tusks repeated down both forearms — "different markings, same hand, ink them."
- Valphine asks in character whether the henge is an anchorite construction or a repurposed druidic site; the DM confirms it is a repurposed druidic ritual.
- Holy symbols of Talos and hide armor are found on the anchorites' bodies.
- Vukradin immediately argues the party should return the holy symbols, saying they need to "return these to the people who owned them." Prutha, with simple logic, gestures at the dead priests: "We found owners. We give to them." Vukradin rolls a 17 on persuasion to argue back; Brewbarry observes, "You're trying to reason with an orc." Vukradin counters, "Are you a racist?" Brewbarry replies that he said orcs are dumb, not evil — "I'm dumb, but he's dumber. It's like dumb and dumber."
- While the argument unfolds, Prutha destroys the symbols. Valphine then places a holy symbol on the skull of a fallen anchorite and smashes it with her mace for emphasis. Vukradin is dismayed: "God. Valfina, we need to have a conversation about conflict-free goals."
- Prutha turns to Valphine and quietly says: "My great-uncle said dawn comes for everyone. Even these ones. I did not think dawn would feel quiet." — his first introspective acknowledgment that killing fellow orcs in Talos's service carries weight, and the first time he articulates his nascent Lathander theology aloud rather than reacting with grunts.
- Prutha acknowledges the power of Lathander's dawn by making suitable grunts and waving his axe in approval.
- Soma notices a change in Meril's Staff: the three existing leaves stop trembling and, near the crown of the staff, a fourth bud — tight, green, the size of a thumbnail — has appeared. It wasn't there before the fight. It hasn't opened. The staff goes quiet for the first time since the cursed clearing. The growth is silent and *additive* — the staff gains as Soma accepts her role, never depletes — a sign of progress earned by stopping the ritual.
- The party discovers a mysterious leather binding glyph — a leather thong with a single boar tusk inscribed with a glyph — on one of the fallen anchorites; Valphine's religion check identifies it as a binding glyph called "a leash." Gary immediately notes, "That sounds very useful. I can put that in my sacraments," while Brewbarry observes it "sounds very S&M."

#### After stopping the ritual, the party explores the surrounding caves in search of supplies and information, led by Brewbarry's thirst.
- Brewbarry leads the way into the tunnels in search of alcohol ("I'm thirsty"), followed by Vukradin, Valphine, and Prutha; Soma and Boney stand watch outside because Soma in elk form cannot fit in the tunnel.
- Vukradin quips that it looks "more dungeon than refrigerator"; Valphine suggests it might be a wine cellar. Brewbarry agrees enthusiastically: "It's perfect."

The boar rolls a natural twenty and is completely unaffected, and combat breaks out. Brewbarry rebukes Vukradin: "That wasn't very nice. Why would you insult the boar? The poor boar. That's mean."

- The group moves to a second cave where they find several boars penned up beneath a lintel stone carved with a boar chasing a humanoid figure. Brewbarry remarks, "I'm tempted to say this is boring."

He is reckless; he will summon before the Lodge is ready" — identifying Grannoc as a rival anchorite whose ritual is faltering. The second reads: "The Chosen is pleased with the boy from the eastern clan. Continue feeding him with stories. Do not yet tell him about the leash." This is the first physical sighting of the "K." signature that has been recurring in Talosian intel.
- Vukradin rolls a 9 on perception while distracted, and Valphine uses her high passive perception (17) to notice a hidden compartment beneath the cave floor; she quietly pockets a potion of invulnerability and slips it into Vukradin's pack without his knowledge — adding it to what the rest of the table jokingly calls "the blacklist," the OOC running ledger of loot the party keeps off Vukradin's notice, since his naïve conflict-free-gold ethos would make him refuse it on principle.

### The Final Cave and the Map Fragment
#### The party investigates the last cave at the Circle of Thunder, discovering a torn map that reveals future summoning sites.
- The party explores the final cave — Narux's quarters (C3) — and recovers a buried **+1 Shield** alongside a stack of Narux's crude vellum sketches of Gorthok (a four-legged silhouette as large as a building, lightning along the tusks) annotated in Orcish with notes including "feed before unleashing" and "the leash holds only if the stones sing." The shield is added to party inventory, currently unassigned.
- Brewbarry has still found nothing to drink ("There's nothing in this cave. Let's beat it.") and is unimpressed by the loot on his own terms.
- A torn map fragment of Neverwinter Wood is also discovered in Narux's quarters, showing three marked sites with very specific semantics: **the Circle of Thunder is X'd through in advance** (the anchorites expected to lose the henge), **the Woodland Manse is double-circled** (the active backup ritual site where Grannoc is preparing the same ritual), and **Falcon's Hunting Lodge is drawn as a target** (both rituals point at it). The party realizes the henge they just cleared was always meant to be sacrificed.
- Brewbarry immediately fixates on the Falcon's Hunting Lodge entry: "That's where they hide the drinks. Hidden in plain sight."
- The party notes these are the next quest destinations; Vukradin deletes the "horse with no rain" tracker entry, confirming the current quest is complete.
- Conversation turns to their strained relationship with Falcon the hunter. The DM reminds the party that Falcon had previously agreed to Valphine's proposal involving a musical instrument (offered at the end of the druid conclave), and that the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, after which orcs attacked his lodge and his girlfriend was killed. Vukradin acknowledges: "It can't be in five places at once, you know? There's only so much we can do." The DM notes those locations marked in red on the tracker reflect that Falcon has not yet given the party an apology.
- Valphine considers using persuasion to smooth things over with Falcon; the DM notes that Falcon is ready to give the apology and that Valphine may be able to help broker it.
- The party notes Falcon had previously offered boots of elvenkind as a reward for dealing with these threats; Vukradin confirms this is on record.
- The session ends with the party planning their next moves toward the Woodland Manse and Falcon's Hunting Lodge, carrying with them cryptic notes, the leash, and the unsettling promise of worse things yet to come.

## Locations

### Circle of Thunder
A henge of ancient standing stones where Anchorites of Talos were performing a summoning ritual involving dancing and lightning magic. The atmosphere was heavy with the smell of ozone and the hum of magical energy — the stones humming faintly until the ritual was stopped. The DM confirms the site is a repurposed druidic ritual location, not an anchorite construction. After the battle, the clouds peeled back in a slow reluctant spiral and the stones went utterly inert. The site also contained a series of caves used by the anchorites for storage and keeping penned boars, where the party found cryptic notes, a satchel, a potion of invulnerability, and a map fragment.

### Falcon's Hunting Lodge
A location drawn as a target on the discovered map fragment — the cult's intended endpoint for both summoning rituals, not itself a ritual site. The home of Falcon the hunter; previously attacked by orcs after the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse threat in time. Brewbarry is additionally motivated by the conviction that the lodge contains large quantities of alcohol.

### Woodland Manse
A site double-circled on the discovered map fragment — identified by the cult correspondence as the active backup summoning ritual site, where the anchorite **Grannoc** is preparing the same ritual the party just interrupted at the henge. The party previously failed to deal with a threat there, which led directly to the orc attack on Falcon's Lodge. With the henge ritual broken, the Manse becomes the live threat.

## Spells

### Lightning Bolt
Cast by one of the half-orc anchorites during combat, striking several party members who had lined up together — a formation Vukradin had already noted was convenient for the anchorite. The party made DC12 dexterity saving throws to reduce the damage; Valphine rolled a 6 and failed, taking the full 31 points of damage (halved to 15 on a pass).

### Hold Person
Cast by Vukradin at third level (DC15 wisdom save), this spell successfully paralyzed both anchorite spellcasters simultaneously, preventing them from acting and making them vulnerable to automatic critical hits from melee attacks. Both anchorites continued to fail their repeated wisdom saving throws each round throughout the battle. Vukradin and Soma's player both remarked it is "a very powerful spell."

### Bless
A supportive enchantment active during the battle that allowed party members to add a bonus die to their saving throws. Stacked on the Bless D4 was a College of Eloquence bardic inspiration D8, applied to the same saving throw via Vukradin's Eloquence-Bard feature (which extends the inspiration die to a save rather than the standard ability check). Gary rolled ones on both bonus dice during the lightning bolt save — an outcome Vukradin calculated as roughly a 1-in-40 chance.

### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Valphine, this spell summoned spectral guardians in a 15-foot radius that dealt radiant damage to enemies that entered or started their turn within the area. It proved highly effective against the twig blights and was maintained through the cave exploration to finish off the boar (dealing 15 points of radiant damage). The group briefly debated the exact triggering conditions — whether enemies must move into the area or merely start their turn there — with the DM ruling that the version in use requires entry or starting a turn in the area.

### Starry Wisp
A luminous projectile launched by Vukradin at the remaining anchorite from approximately 60 feet away, requiring Vukradin to move 25 feet to get within range first. It hit on a roll of 25 (plus 7 to hit, with advantage already available) and dealt 14 points of damage.

### Vicious Mockery
A cantrip used by Vukradin to psychically insult and wound a boar found within the caves (DC15 wisdom save). The boar rolled a natural twenty on its first saving throw, completely resisting the mockery. On a second casting it took 7 points of psychic damage. Brewbarry scolded Vukradin: "Why would you insult the boar? The poor boar? That's mean."

## NPCs

### Flenz and Narux (Anchorites of Talos)
Two young half-orc Anchorites of Talos wearing rotting boar-head masks and bearing elaborate storm-themed tattoos. **Flenz** wore storm clouds and coiling spirals up the neck, each with an open eye; **Narux** wore crossed boar-tusks repeated down both forearms — "different markings, same hand inked them." Both were younger than the party expected. They led a summoning ritual at the Circle of Thunder using dancing and lightning magic, and were ultimately paralyzed by Hold Person and slain. They carried clubs, hide armor, and holy symbols of Talos. **Flenz's quarters (C2)** held the satchel of cult correspondence and the Potion of Invulnerability; **Narux's quarters (C3)** held the torn map fragment, his sketches of Gorthok, and a buried +1 Shield.

### Twig Blights
Twelve small, animated plant creatures that served as minions for the anchorites, attacking the party in a swarm before being decimated by Spirit Guardians, Brewbarry's thrown handaxes, and physical attacks from Boney and Prutha.

### Boney
A talking skeletal horse that serves as a companion to the party. He participated in combat by tearing into twig blights with a bare-jawed bite, scored an autocritical hit on the paralyzed anchorite for 5 points of damage, and showed protective instincts toward Soma. Brewbarry questioned whether Boney could count, prompting Vukradin to remind the table that Boney is a talking horse — and that talking and counting are two different things.

### Prutha
An orc warrior — first Lathander convert and new orc ally of the party — who harbors a deep hatred for the anchorites of Talos. He charged into combat with javelins and his greataxe throughout the fight. He is fiercely loyal and follows the party closely, but is extremely literal-minded: when Vukradin argued the Talosian holy symbols needed to be returned to their owners, Prutha pointed at the dead anchorites — "We found owners. We give to them" — before destroying the symbols himself. He spoke quietly after the fight, telling Valphine that his uncle Seidan "comes for everyone — even these ones," then admitted he did not "feel very good." He acknowledged the power of Lathander's dawn with approving grunts and axe-waving. The DM notes his potential religious development as a follower of Lathander is being watched with interest.

### Falcon
A hunter who resides at his namesake lodge in Neverwinter Wood. He has a strained relationship with the party due to perceived betrayal — the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, leading to an orc attack on his lodge and the death of his girlfriend. The DM clarifies this was something Valphine helped him understand in the past — "that those were excuses." He had previously offered boots of elvenkind as a reward for clearing the Woodland Manse, and accepted Valphine's proposal involving a musical instrument at the end of the druid conclave. His quest locations are currently marked in red on the party's tracker, reflecting the unresolved tension.

### Adabra
An NPC the party previously encountered and attempted to save, though she insisted she did not need saving and was very clear about this. The party discussed her briefly before the session began, with Vukradin noting that "in that ambiguity, everything was created." Vukradin confirmed she is a significantly expanded homebrew character — Dave had looked her up in the original module and found she bore only the same name.

### Soma
A druid companion who transforms into a giant elk form for combat. She participated in the battle by delivering a charging ram against a paralyzed anchorite for 25 points of damage and later smashed one of the henge's standing stones. In elk form she cannot fit into the cave tunnels, so she and Boney stood watch outside during the cave exploration. She cast Moonbeam as an option but ultimately prowled the perimeter instead.
