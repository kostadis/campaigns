[Scene 4] Exploring The Caves Of The Circle Of Thunder
Narrator: Valphine
Focus: Valphine's drow instincts — opportunism, a nose for hidden things, and her eye for what others overlook — make the cave search hers, culminating in the covert discovery she places in Vukradin's pack without a word.

- Brewbarry leads the way into the tunnels in search of alcohol ("I'm thirsty"), followed by Vukradin, Valphine, and Prutha; Soma and Boney stand watch outside because Soma in elk form cannot fit in the tunnel.

- Vukradin quips that it looks "more dungeon than refrigerator"; Valphine suggests it might be a wine cellar. Brewbarry agrees enthusiastically: "It's perfect."

- The party discovers a cave containing a live boar, which Vukradin immediately attempts to kill with Vicious Mockery (DC15 wisdom save). The boar rolls a natural twenty and is completely unaffected, and combat breaks out. Brewbarry rebukes Vukradin: "That wasn't very nice. Why would you insult the boar? The poor boar. That's mean."

- The boar takes 7 points of psychic damage from a second Vicious Mockery attempt, dropping it to 4 hit points.

- Valphine finishes the boar with her Spirit Guardians for 15 points of radiant damage. The DM remarks: "You now have radiated pork meat." Brewbarry approves: "Perfect for barbecue. Now all we're missing are drinks."

- Vukradin defends himself: "It was coming right for us." Gary confirms: "It was."

- The group moves to a second cave where they find several boars penned up beneath a lintel stone carved with a boar chasing a humanoid figure. Brewbarry remarks, "I'm tempted to say this is boring."

- Valphine discovers a satchel sealed with a wax sigil — a circle with a single vertical lightning bolt, the canonical symbol of Talos — identified via Arcana. Valphine destroys the wax seal before opening it, then finds an extremely low roll has minimal consequence.

- Inside the satchel, Valphine finds three letters of cult correspondence in the same hand. The first reads: "Your storm holds. Grannoc's does not. He is reckless; he will summon before the Lodge is ready" — identifying Grannoc as a rival anchorite whose ritual is faltering. The second reads: "The Chosen is pleased with the boy from the eastern clan. Continue feeding him with stories. Do not yet tell him about the leash." The third, most alarming, reads verbatim: "If the henge falls, the manse summons in its place. If the manse falls, I summon myself. The boar comes. The storm comes. You are not to fail twice."

- Retroactively, the party also recovers a half-burned vellum scrap from Yargath's firepit in a separate cave — in a different, sharper hand from the cult letters and signed only "K.": "…dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives. — K." This is the first physical sighting of the "K." signature that has been recurring in Talosian intel.

- Vukradin rolls a 9 on perception while distracted, and Valphine uses her high passive perception (17) to notice a hidden compartment beneath the cave floor; she quietly pockets a potion of invulnerability and slips it into Vukradin's pack without his knowledge — adding it to what the rest of the table jokingly calls "the blacklist," the OOC running ledger of loot the party keeps off Vukradin's notice, since his naïve conflict-free-gold ethos would make him refuse it on principle.
