# Chapter 41 Unraveling the Storm God's Secrets

Date: May 12th, 2026

## Summary
The party arrived at the Circle of Thunder, a henge of ancient standing stones crackling with dark energy, to find two half-orc anchorites of Talos leading a sinister summoning ritual. The air was thick with ozone and the low hum of the stones, while a swarm of twig blight minions prowled the perimeter. Rather than chanting, the anchorites danced in eerie silence, their movements precise and deliberate. Valphine recognized the ceremony for what it was — a summoning — while the rest of the party struggled to make sense of the wordless ritual. With no time to waste, the group readied themselves for battle.

The fight erupted with terrifying force when one of the anchorites unleashed a bolt of lightning that tore through several party members standing in a line. But Vukradin answered swiftly and decisively, reaching deep into his power to paralyze both spellcasters at once, freezing them mid-dance and leaving them utterly helpless. With the anchorites locked in place, the party surged forward. Boney charged in with his longsword, Prutha hurled a javelin with furious hatred, and Soma transformed into a massive elk and delivered a thunderous charging ram into one of the frozen priests. Valphine summoned a swirling host of spectral guardians that radiated holy light, tearing through the twig blights in waves of radiant energy. Brewbarry waded into the fray with his elemental cleaver, its acid-laced blade cutting down blight after blight, while Vukradin peppered the remaining anchorite with shimmering bolts of starlight. Prutha delivered the final blow to the last anchorite with a savage swing of his great axe, and the battle was over.

In the silence that followed, the storm above slowly unraveled — the clouds peeling back in a reluctant spiral, the ozone fading to the smell of wet leaves and old rain, and the standing stones going utterly inert. When the party removed the boar-head masks from the fallen anchorites, they found two young half-orc faces beneath them, their necks and forearms covered in elaborate tattoos of storm clouds, coiling spirals, and crossed boar tusks. At the center of the henge lay a deer corpse split open from throat to belly, its heart replaced by a hand-sized hollow of black, glassy material that was cool to the touch and still faintly humming. The party also recovered a mysterious leather binding glyph — a leather thong strung with a single boar tusk inscribed with a strange rune — from one of the fallen priests. Valphine identified it as a magical leash, a tool of binding and restraint, and pocketed it carefully.

The aftermath brought its own kind of chaos. The party found holy symbols of Talos on the bodies, and an immediate argument broke out over what to do with them. Vukradin wanted to return them to their rightful owners, while Valphine wanted to take them for further study. Prutha pointed out that the owners were dead, and solved the problem by destroying the symbols himself, reflecting his anger at the cult leaders. The orc warrior then turned to Valphine and acknowledged the power of Lathander's dawn with a series of approving grunts and a wave of his great axe — a small but meaningful gesture of respect.

Brewbarry, driven by an unshakeable thirst, led the group into the series of caves surrounding the henge in search of something fermented. The first cave yielded a live boar, which Vukradin immediately attempted to unsettle with a barrage of magical insults — only for the boar to shrug off every word with complete indifference before charging. The ensuing skirmish ended with Valphine finishing the creature off with a burst of radiant light, leaving Brewbarry to note approvingly that they now had radiated pork, perfect for a barbecue. A second cave held more penned boars beneath a lintel stone carved with the image of a boar chasing a fleeing humanoid, and more importantly, a satchel sealed with the wax sigil of Talos. Inside, Valphine found several cryptic notes: one mentioned that "your storm holds Grannoc"; another spoke of a boy from the Eastern clan being fed stories, with a warning that he must never be told about the leash; and a third, most chilling of all, warned that if the henge fell, a manticore would be summoned in its place — and if the manticore fell, something far worse would come personally, ending with the ominous words, "You are not to fail twice." While Vukradin was distracted, Valphine quietly noticed a hidden compartment beneath the cave floor and slipped a potion of invulnerability into his pack without a word.

The final cave yielded no drinks for Brewbarry, but it did produce a torn map fragment of Neverwinter Wood, marked with three sites: the Circle of Thunder, the Woodland Manse, and Falcon's Hunting Lodge. Double circles around certain locations suggested they were active summoning sites, and the party recognized them as their next destinations. As they prepared to leave, conversation turned to Falcon the hunter, whose relationship with the group had grown strained — he had felt betrayed when the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, and orcs had subsequently attacked his lodge, killing his girlfriend in the process. Valphine considered how best to smooth things over, recalling that Falcon had once promised a pair of enchanted boots in exchange for their help. With the Circle of Thunder silenced and the ritual stopped, the party turned their eyes toward the road ahead, carrying with them secrets, a mysterious leash, and the unsettling promise of worse things yet to come.

## Locations
### Circle of Thunder
A henge of standing stones where Anchorites of Talos were performing a summoning ritual involving dancing and lightning magic. The atmosphere was heavy with the smell of ozone and the hum of magical energy until the party intervened. The site also contained a series of caves used by the anchorites for storage and keeping animals, where the party found cryptic notes and a map fragment.

### Falcon's Hunting Lodge
A location marked with an 'X' on a discovered map fragment, identified as one of three summoning sites in the Neverwinter Wood region and the home of Falcon the hunter. It was previously attacked by orcs.

### Woodland Manse
A site marked on the discovered map fragment, identified as one of the three summoning locations in the region. The party had previously failed to deal with a threat there.

## Items
### Elemental Cleaver
A powerful hand axe used by Brewbarry that deals additional elemental acid damage. It can be thrown and magically reappears in the wielder's hand whether it hits or misses. Brewbarry used it to devastating effect against the twig blights and anchorites.

### Holy Symbols of Talos
Religious icons found on the fallen anchorites. After a debate about their proper fate, Valphine placed them on the skull of an anchorite and smashed them with his mace.

### The Leash
A leather thong with a single boar tusk inscribed with a binding glyph, found on one of the fallen anchorites. Valphine identified it as a magical binding restraint. The cryptic notes found nearby warned that a certain individual should not be told about it.

### Black Glassy Hollow
A hand-sized object of black, glassy material found where the heart of a sacrificed deer should have been. It was cool to the touch and hummed faintly with the same energy as the standing stones of the henge.

### Satchel of Talos
A leather satchel sealed with a wax sigil depicting a circle with a single vertical boar — the symbol of Talos. Found in a cave used by the anchorites, it contained several cryptic notes about their plans.

### Cryptic Notes
Fragments of messages found within the Talosian satchel. One mentions 'your storm holds Grannoc'; another references a 'boy from the Eastern clan' being fed stories and warns not to tell him about the leash; a third warns that if the henge falls a manticore is summoned in its place, and if the manticore falls something greater comes, ending with 'you are not to fail twice.'

### Potion of Invulnerability
A magical potion discovered by Valphine in a hidden compartment beneath the floor of one of the caves, found while Vukradin was distracted.

### Torn Map Fragment
A piece of a map showing Neverwinter Wood with three sites marked, including the Circle of Thunder, the Woodland Manse, and Falcon's Hunting Lodge. The double circles around certain locations suggest they are active summoning sites.

### Boots of Elvenkind
Magical boots previously offered by Falcon as a reward for dealing with threats at the Woodland Manse or his lodge, referenced during the party's discussion of their next steps.

## Spells
### Lightning Bolt
Cast by one of the half-orc anchorites during combat, striking several party members who were lined up together. The party had to make dexterity saving throws to reduce the damage.

### Hold Person
Cast by Vukradin at third level, this spell successfully paralyzed both anchorite spellcasters simultaneously, preventing them from acting and making them vulnerable to automatic critical hits from melee attacks. It proved decisive in turning the tide of the battle.

### Bless
A supportive enchantment active during the battle that allowed party members to add a bonus die to their saving throws, helping at least one character resist the anchorite's lightning bolt.

### Spirit Guardians
Cast by Valphine, this spell summoned spectral guardians that swirled around him in a radius, dealing radiant damage to enemies that entered or started their turn in the area. It proved highly effective against the twig blights and was maintained through the cave exploration to finish off the boar.

### Starry Wisp
A luminous projectile launched by Vukradin to strike the remaining anchorite from a distance of 60 feet, dealing significant damage.

### Vicious Mockery
A cantrip used by Vukradin to psychically insult and wound a boar found within the caves. The boar rolled a natural twenty on its saving throw, completely resisting the mockery, before combat broke out.

## NPCs
### Anchorites of Talos
Two half-orc spellcasters wearing boar-head masks and bearing elaborate storm-themed tattoos and boar tusk markings. They were leading a summoning ritual at the Circle of Thunder involving dancing and lightning magic, and were ultimately paralyzed and slain by the party.

### Twig Blights
Numerous small, animated plant creatures that served as minions for the anchorites, attacking the party in a swarm before being decimated by area-of-effect magic and physical attacks.

### Prutha
A ruthless warrior committed to the Order of the Gauntlet who harbors a deep hatred for the anchorites. He charged into combat and hurled a javelin at the anchorites.

### Boney
A talking skeletal horse that serves as a companion to the party. He participated in combat by attacking twig blights with a longsword and showed protective instincts toward Soma.

### Prutha
An orc warrior who fights alongside the party with a great axe and javelins. He is fiercely loyal and follows the party like a puppy, but is very literal-minded — he attempted to 'return' the Talosian holy symbols to their dead owners by placing them on the corpses. He acknowledged the power of Lathander's dawn with grunts and axe-waving.

### Falcon
A hunter who resides at his namesake lodge. He has a strained relationship with the party due to perceived betrayals — the party failed to deal with the Woodland Manse, leading to an orc attack on his lodge and the death of his girlfriend. He had previously offered boots of elvenkind as a reward for dealing with these threats.

### Adabra
An NPC the party previously encountered and attempted to save, though she insisted she did not need saving. The party discussed her briefly, noting the ambiguity of whether she actually needed rescuing.
