## Session Summary

# Session — 2026-03-26

## Overview

With the battle at Dragonbarrow behind them, the party interrogated the captured Sister Kaella and extracted critical intelligence: the Carver has gone mad and is marching on Ice Spire Hold to possess the white dragon through a dark ritual. Time-critical, the party arranged transport via Xanth the centaur, Soma's wild-shaped bear form, and Boney the skeletal horse to avoid exhaustion, left Kaella in Corbin's unsettling custody, and set out directly for the mountains. The journey proved harrowing — the Carver's trail left warped, phasing reality in its wake, and the party navigated a stone giant defile, the psychically haunted Glacier of Whispers, and a grieving mother drake's lair before arriving at a boulder-blocked pass with Ice Spire Hold somewhere beyond. The session ended on a cliffhanger as Soma attempted to use Mold Earth to carve a route through the blockage.

---

## Session Events

- **Post-battle assessment:** Jax, the ogre, and all remaining combatants confirmed dead; Sister Kaella alone was spared via non-lethal force.
- **Xanth healed:** Soma cast Cure Wounds (12 HP) on the critically wounded centaur Xanthopoulos, restoring him to full health.
- **Xanth's exposition:** Xanth explained that a white dragon moved into the Icespire Mountains, displacing orc tribes southward and fracturing them into multiple factions, including a Talosian-aligned group. He confirmed the Carver had three lieutenants: Borg (dead), Jax (dead), and Sister Kaella (captured).
- **Staff recognition:** Xanth recognized Meril's Staff carried by Soma (previously noted as carried by Valphine in some sources — see Out-of-Character note) as a powerful artifact belonging to his old friend Meril, noting it once had many more leaves and now has only one. He pledged his aid upon seeing it.
- **Brewbarry's Goodberry memory:** A casual mention of Goodberries triggered Brewbarry's recollection of his exile — after his tribe's artifact was stolen, he found a Goodberry, ate it, and his tribe mistook his unusual calm for drunkenness and exiled him. He had not discovered actual drinking until afterward, leading to his declaration: *"Sobriety was wasted youth."*
- **Kaella revived and interrogated:** Valphine used Medicine (roll: 15) to revive Kaella to 1 HP. Vukradin and Valphine interrogated her using Intimidation (roll: 16 with advantage), visibly impressing Kaella.
- **Kaella's revelations:** The Carver has descended into madness and is marching on Ice Spire Hold to perform a ritual to possess the white dragon and take vengeance on his enemies. Kaella admitted she had been covertly working against him after he accused her of failing her intelligence duties, reasoning her survival odds improved if he died. She approached Valphine rather than Vukradin because she believed Vukradin's strong moral compass would make him unreliable in morally gray operations. She provided directions to Ice Spire Hold and requested imprisonment at Axeholm rather than Phandalin.
- **Carver detail noted:** Stone giants would later describe the Carver as carrying an obsidian sword; the party suspects it may be a level-draining weapon.
- **Travel planning:** Direct route costs 1 level of exhaustion; going through Phandalin costs 2. Vukradin (poorly rolled Insight, GM offered the consideration anyway) decided against stopping at Phandalin partly to avoid the political optics of appearing to need help as council head. Three-mount strategy devised: Xanth carries Vukradin and Valphine, Soma wild-shapes into a bear to carry another rider, Boney carries the remaining party member.
- **Kaella left with Corbin:** Corbin enthusiastically accepted custody of the bound, near-death Kaella. Vukradin authorized keeping her at 1 HP. Valphine (Insight: 25) recognized Corbin and Kaella share a dark personal history and that Corbin intends to make her suffer. The party chose not to intervene.
- **Long rest taken** before departing.
- **Xanth carries party partway:** After Persuasion roll (17), Xanth agreed to carry the party approximately 35 miles to the mountain foothills, near the area of the gold mine. He declined to go into the high peaks ("my feet are not for those things").
- **The Carver's trail discovered:** Perfectly straight, rhythmic, human-shaped footprints cutting directly over rocks rather than around them — inhuman and mechanical in character. Soma sensed a disruption in the natural order at the mountain base.
- **Mountain navigation:** Three skill checks required; party succeeded on three (Survival 20 by Vukradin, Athletics by Brewbarry, Perception 23 by Valphine).
- **Stone giant encounter:** In a narrow mountain defile, the party encountered stone giants troubled by strangely behaving rocks. Brewbarry and Valphine spoke with them in the giant language. The giants confirmed "the little one with the obsidian sword" passed through alone and was no concern of theirs. Soma pressed Meril's Staff to the affected rocks, revealing the Carver's passage had caused them to phase in and out of reality through psychic warping. The giants, upon witnessing the demonstration, gifted the party a Potion of Greater Healing and a Potion of Hill Giant Strength and delivered a philosophical speech about giant non-intervention — referencing the War of Giants, Tiamat's defeat, and Hekaton's lessons — before firmly telling the party to go handle the Carver and stop talking.
- **Glacier of Whispers:** Ice stained violet, frozen screaming faces visible within. The Carver's footprints glowed with psychic energy. Psychic whispers filled the party's minds: *"He walks without rest, without breath, without soul."* Navigation checks: Perception 23 (Valphine, success) and one failed check (net: one success, one failure). Valphine recalled Lathander's blessing could grant advantage against psychic effects here, though the party passed without invoking it.
- **Young drake's corpse:** The party found a frozen young white drake with its eyes burned out by necrotic energy — the Carver had walked straight through its lair.
- **Mother drake encounter:** An enraged mother drake circled above. Vukradin played calming music; Brewbarry joined in. The mother drake calmed, descended, and lay beside her dead young, allowing the party to pass peacefully.
- **Impassable terrain:** A wall of massive boulders and ice blocked the final approach. Soma used Mold Earth to attempt to carve a path, with Vukradin providing Bardic Inspiration. Soma's Survival roll (DC 15) result was **not resolved** — session ended on this cliffhanger.
- **Necklace of Fireballs noted as useless:** The party recognized the white dragon's fire immunity renders Vukradin's Necklace of Fireballs ineffective for this confrontation.

## Character Voices

### Xanth (Centaur NPC)
- Speaks with a warm Greek register — formal introductions, diminutives, unsolicited philosophical asides. Effortlessly slips between grandiose pronouncements and blunt pragmatism.
- Dispenses flattery freely but it always contains a barb or a lesson. Never condescending, but always certain he's the wisest person standing.
- Treats moral complexity as a landscape feature: navigable, unremarkable, requiring only good sense.

> "My name is Xanth. You can call me Xanth. My real name is Xanthopoulos. Most people can't pronounce it."
> "I really appreciate your willingness to see beyond the stereotypical impressions of orcs that permeate the local literature in Waterdeep. This shows you are a man of talent in the arts."
> "Why shouldn't you kill her? I didn't say you shouldn't kill her — I said you should get the information. Then, if you kill her, I have no problem."

---

### Sister Kaella (NPC)
- Dry, unsentimental, and difficult to rattle. Speaks in complete sentences under duress. Self-interest is always visible but never shameless — she frames it as reason.
- Treats Vukradin's moral posturing as a known variable she already accounted for. Never apologizes; explains.
- Optimism as a survival mechanism, not a character trait.

> "After the Carver accused me of not doing my work from an intelligence-gathering perspective, I kind of thought that if the Carver died, my life expectancy would increase."
> "Vukradin — I had noticed that you had a difficulty in dealing with morally gray environments."
> "As long as I'm alive, there's always hope."

---

### Vukradin (PC)
- Brisk, witty, perpetually moving toward the next practical step. Moral clarity expressed as impatience rather than lecture.
- Deflects flattery the way he deflects danger — quickly and without breaking stride.
- Will deliver a cold line and walk away. The joke is always real.

> "You'll do really well in prison. There are a lot of morally gray people there, you can hang out with them for many years."
> "Keep hoping, babe."
> "Yeah, some things... I gotta focus on the music studio. Killing a dragon, returning all this treasure to its rightful owners. I got a lot going on right now."

---

### Soma (PC)
- Economical with words. Wry when she speaks at all. The maternal wisdom surfaces sideways — in a single dry observation rather than a speech.
- Carries grief quietly; her compassion emerges as an aside before she moves on.
- Has earned the right to tease Vukradin.

> "Yeah, they can't even agree on terms."
> "I like how your code of honor is just about loot."
> "We are." *(in response to Vukradin's "Mothers are resilient")*

---

### Brewbarry (PC)
- Candid to the point of confession. Delivers personal revelations with the same blunt energy as combat reports.
- Physical self-awareness deployed as punchline — the beer belly is exhibit A.
- Genuine conviction in absurd conclusions.

> "I'll have you note that I did not drink before the loss of the artifact and my banning from the Uthgartian tribe."
> "Yes, but since I discovered drinking — I think that was wasted youth."

---

### Valphine (PC)
- Minimal words, maximum effect. Lets her nature do the unsettling work before she speaks.
- The Lathander devotion is sincere and genuinely strange — she means it when she calls light *deliciously painful*.
- Recognizes cruelty from memory, not judgment.

> "Light is deliciously painful for me."
> "The corrupted one seeks to corrupt the dragon."

---

### Stone Giant (NPC)
- Speaks in a register of monumental indifference. "Little ones" is not an insult — it is a taxonomic observation.
- Repeats itself without embarrassment. The second saying of a thing is not emphasis; it is conclusion.
- Capable of launching into full historical sermons without registering that no one asked.

> "He is a little one. Is of no concern of ours."
> "You would no longer be here and not talking to us."

## Voice Keeper Notes

**Xanth** — Affectionate diminutives (*Vukradinhos*), rhetorical asides, unprompted historical context. Condescension expressed as generosity. Never raises his voice. Use him for exposition that doubles as gentle mockery of the party's assumptions.

**Sister Kaella** — Composed under pressure, self-interested but not villainous. Frames every decision as cost-benefit analysis. Does not apologize; recalibrates. Her line *"As long as I'm alive, there's always hope"* is her thesis statement — use it if she returns.

**Stone Giant** — Slow, repetitive, sermonizing. "Little ones" as neutral taxonomy. Will help if convinced the cause matters at giant scale; otherwise dismissive without hostility. Capable of derailing conversations with unsolicited historical lectures — this is a feature, not a bug.

**Corbin** — Eagerness for violence framed as professionalism. *"Can I beat her?"* delivered as a logistical question. His history with the Carver's crew and his trauma should surface when he has power over someone connected to that world.

**PC Emotional States for NPC Use:**
- Vukradin has publicly walked away from moral complexity while privately exercising it — NPCs who point this out (as Kaella did) will get under his skin.
- Soma's connection to Meril's Staff is not yet understood by her; Xanth's recognition of it should be treated as a seed. Future NPCs who know Meril should react to her with similar reverence.
- Brewbarry's confession about drinking is newly public within the party — he tied his exile directly to discovering alcohol and framed pre-exile sobriety as *wasted youth*. This is gallows humor about genuine shame.
- Valphine's silence at the Corbin/Kaella handoff was noted as memory, not approval. Her dark past should be treated as present-tense instinct, not backstory.

**Promises and Vows Made This Session:**
- Vukradin told Kaella she would be jailed in Phandalin on the way to Icespire Hold — this is now a stated commitment.
- Corbin was authorized to beat Kaella to one hit point — if this comes to light in Phandalin, it has political and moral consequences.
- Xanth pledged to guide the party to the halfway point into the mountains, then offered to watch Kaella in the tomb as an alternative — both offers declined.
- Vukradin instructed *"save it for the real guy"* before the drake encounter — the party deliberately preserved resources for the Carver. This choice should be remembered when those resources are tested.
