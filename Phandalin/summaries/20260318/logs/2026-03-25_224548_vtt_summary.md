# Session — 2026-03-18

## Overview
Following their defeat of Jax and the Rot-Tusk Ogre outside Dragonbarrow, the party interrogated Sister Kaella and closed the critical intelligence gap about Icespire Hold's location. After a long rest, they launched an urgent overland push toward the mountain, using a creative multi-mount travel arrangement to avoid exhaustion. The journey took them through harrowing terrain—psychically warped stone giants' defile, a glacier stained violet with necrotic energy, a drake mother grieving her slaughtered young—all bearing evidence of the Carver's inhuman, straight-line march toward the dragon. The session ended mid-skill-check as the party attempts to carve through a boulder-field blocking the final approach to Icespire Hold.

---

## Session Events

- Soma cast Cure Wounds on Xanth the Centaur (12 HP), restoring him to full health and enabling conversation.
- Xanth provided regional lore: the white dragon's arrival in the Icespire Mountains displaced orcs southward, fracturing them into multiple factions (including the Talosian anchorites) and seeding ongoing regional instability. Confirmed the Carver had three lieutenants—Borg (dead), Jax (dead), Sister Kaella (captured)—and urged the party to extract information from Kaella before deciding her fate.
- Xanth recognized Meril's Staff and confirmed he knew Meril personally, stating Meril "must know" Soma.
- Valphine used a Medicine check (15) to stabilize Sister Kaella to 1 HP and an Intimidation check (16) to press her into cooperation.
- Sister Kaella revealed: the Carver has "gone completely mad" and is marching alone on Icespire Hold to possess and bind the white dragon; he plans to burn Phandalin as a demonstration of power. She had covertly turned against him after he blamed her for intelligence failures following Borg's death. She had approached only Valphine (not the full party) because she assessed Vukradin as unable to operate in morally gray environments.
- Sister Kaella provided exact directions to Icespire Hold. She requested imprisonment at Axeholm rather than Phandalin jail and recommended evacuating Phandalin's civilians to Axeholm.
- Party decided to leave Sister Kaella bound and gagged in Corbin's custody at the Dragonbarrow tomb rather than transport her. Corbin accepted eagerly; Valphine's Insight (25) revealed Corbin has a personal score to settle with Kaella from their shared time in the Carver's organization. Vukradin authorized keeping her at 1 HP.
- Party completed a long rest before departing.
- Travel plan devised to avoid exhaustion: Soma in bear form as a mount, Boney as a mount, Xanth carrying two lighter party members. Vukradin persuaded Xanth (roll: 17) to carry the party approximately 35 miles (roughly halfway to the mountains) before turning back.
- Vukradin gave Bardic Inspiration to Soma before the mountain climb.
- Approaching the Sword Mountains, Soma sensed deep disturbance in the natural order near Icespire Hold.
- Party observed the Carver's trail: perfectly straight, robotic footprints going directly over obstacles rather than around them, consistent with a possessed or non-human state.
- Party successfully passed the first mountain skill checks (3 of 6 successes needed), navigating the initial approach.
- At the Stone Giants' Defile, Brewbarry spoke with several stone giants in Giant. They confirmed the Carver ("the little one with the obsidian sword") passed through alone. The giants cited Hekaton's post-war neutrality doctrine and refused to intervene, but one gave the party a **Potion of Greater Healing** before resuming a lengthy sermon about the War of Giants and the breaking of the Ordning.
- Soma used Meril's Staff to detect psychic warping in the phasing rocks—reality had been distorted along the Carver's exact path, confirming the extent of his power.
- At the Glacier of Whispers: ice stained violet with psychic energy, frozen faces in silent screams, faint psychic resonance in the Carver's glowing footprints, whispers in party minds. Party succeeded on Group Wisdom saves against the psychic effect.
- Party found frozen corpses of young white drakes with necrotic-burned-out eyes—the Carver walked straight through their lair. The mother drake was circling above in grief and rage.
- Vukradin played music to calm the grieving mother drake (roll succeeded). The party slipped past while she lay beside her dead young.
- Party reached impassable boulder-strewn terrain. Soma proposed using Mold Earth to carve a path. **Session ended mid-roll on Survival DC 15 check.**

## Character Voices

### Vukradin (David)
- Dry, dismissive wit deployed as a weapon — he doesn't argue, he deflates. Coldness delivered with casual musicality.
- Pragmatic moral framing: his ethics are real, but he's learned to package hard choices inside mundane to-do lists.
- Drops into vernacular informality ("babe," "yeah") precisely when he wants to signal contempt without raising his voice.

> "They don't tell us very much. It's more like… signs and portents."
> "Keep hoping, babe."
> "I gotta focus on the music studio. Killing a dragon, returning all this treasure to its rightful owners. I got a lot going on right now."

---

### Valphine (Gary)
- Economy of words — she enters a scene, delivers the line that counts, and lets others fill the silence.
- Her drow heritage and Lathanderian faith coexist without contradiction in her speech; pain and light are the same thing.
- Intimidation reads as courtesy. The warmth in her greeting is the threat.

> "Light is deliciously painful for me."
> "Hello, Sister Kaella."
> "I've been teaching him some of our sacraments."

---

### Soma (Wade)
- Quiet, self-deprecating sincerity — she states profound values as if they're modest ones.
- One-line observations that puncture pretension without cruelty.
- Speaks about purpose in communal, humble terms rather than heroic ones.

> "We're just kind of trying to keep the world a safer place right now, trying to make sure everybody's got a place to live, trying to keep peace among the land. I don't have big aspirations."
> "I like how your code of honor is just about loot."

---

### Xanth (GM)
- Formal and slightly theatrical, deploying mock-scholarly cadence to deliver gentle mockery.
- Warmly condescending — he likes the party, which only makes his corrections more pointed.
- Mixes ancient wisdom with blunt practicality; the philosophical pivot to "then kill her after, I have no problem" lands because his register never changes.

> "My name is Xanth. You can call me Xanth. My real name is Xanthopoulos. Most people can't pronounce it."
> "I really appreciate your willingness to see beyond the stereotypical impressions of orcs that permeate the local literature in Waterdeep, and this shows you are a man of talent in the arts."
> "Why shouldn't you kill her? I didn't say you shouldn't kill her, I said you should get the information. Then, if you kill her, I have no problem."

---

### Sister Kaella (GM)
- Survives on composure. Negotiates from captivity as if she still holds cards, because she knows she might.
- Analytically candid — she will tell you exactly why she made every choice, including the ones that cost her.
- One line of defiance delivered flat: not a boast, just a fact she intends to make true.

> "I had noticed that you had a difficulty in dealing with morally gray environments. And so, to be honest, I figured that a drow cleric of Lathander, who understands the importance of darkness and light… would be a better approach."
> "As long as I'm alive, there's always hope."
> "If you wanted to put me in prison in Axeholm, that would even be better."

---

### The Stone Giant (GM)
- Monosyllabic contempt dressed as neutrality. "Little ones" is not an insult — it is a classification.
- Answers questions about help by pointing at the door.
- Carries the weight of a people who learned their lesson about ambition the hard way; wisdom delivered without warmth.

> "He is a little one. Is of no concern of ours."
> "You would no longer be here and not talking to us."
> "When Am broke the sundering, some thought that it was time for giants to seize power over the little ones. Those giants failed."

### Corbin (GM)
- Former brigand operating under thin reformational varnish. The eagerness is genuine and that is the problem.
- Asks clarifying questions about violence the way a professional would ask about scope of work.

> "Can I beat her?"

## Standout Moments

- **Xanth recognizes Meril's staff and immediately shifts allegiance.** A wounded centaur who didn't owe the party anything announces "Smerald and I were old friends" and offers to guide them — the staff doing more diplomatic work than any persuasion roll could have.

- **Vukradin chooses not to fight the mother Drake.** Standing over a lair of young Drakes with burned-out eyes, faced with a grieving creature the Carver walked through without a second thought, Vukradin says only: *"Save it… save it for the real guy."* The restraint lands harder than any combat monologue.

- **The Stone Giant's eulogy for giant ambition.** Asked why giants won't intervene in the dragon crisis, the giant delivers an unprompted lesson: *"When Am broke the sundering, some thought that it was time for giants to seize power over the little ones. Those giants failed."* No bitterness. Just the settled calm of a people who burned their hands and chose not to forget it.

- **Valphine's greeting is the whole interrogation.** She wakes a woman who fully expected to be killed, says *"Hello, Sister Kaella,"* and the scene is already decided. Kaella's relief does all the work.

- **Sister Kaella negotiates her own prison placement.** Captive, outnumbered, freshly revived at one hit point, she calmly expresses a preference between Phandalin jail and Axeholm — and the party seriously considers it. She still operates like someone with options.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**Xanth** — Maintain formal academic register with warm condescension. He respects the party but cannot conceal that he finds their ignorance instructive. Addresses Vukradin as "my friend" before correcting him. Never raises his voice. Uses the Centaurian name affectation as both greeting and mild test.

**Sister Kaella** — She does not beg and she does not flatter. She analyzes, negotiates, and files information away. Even imprisoned, she is making calculations. Future encounters should reflect that she has had time to think. Her line — *"as long as I'm alive, there's always hope"* — is both comfort and warning. She means it literally.

**Stone Giant** — Minimal words, maximum implication. Future encounters with giants should carry the same sense of civilizational patience: they have seen ambition fail at scale, and they are no longer curious about it. They help by not interfering. They warn by pointing.

**Corbin** — Reformed by circumstance, not by nature. He asks permission for violence with professional earnestness. Valphine's "sacraments" line suggests she is shaping him; future encounters should let that influence show — he is becoming something, and it is not entirely clear what.

**PC Emotional States for NPC Dialogue:**
- Vukradin is resolving moral discomfort through pragmatic framing and humor. NPCs who press him on ethics will get the cold deflection, not the argument. He responds better to facts than to appeals.
- Soma's humility is genuine and slightly disarming — NPCs who expect heroic posturing will find her patience more persuasive than intimidation.
- Valphine is in full command of her identity right now. NPCs who try to destabilize her with her drow past or her faith will not find purchase.

**Vows and Commitments to Track:**
- Vukradin promised Kaella delivery to Phandalin jail (or Axeholm) — this should be honored or explicitly broken on screen.
- Corbin has been authorized to beat Kaella to one hit point — consequence of this arrangement (her condition on arrival, her disposition thereafter) needs accounting.
- Vukradin's decision to spare the mother Drake is an implicit promise; if the party encounters her again, she should remember.
