**Calculating the Route**
The party weighs two paths to Ice Spire Hold — 55 miles through mountain terrain as the crow flies. Going through Phandalin first would cost two levels of exhaustion; cutting directly would cost one. Vukradin turns the options over in his mind, aware that arriving depleted against a dragon and a mad ritual-caster is not a viable strategy.
The calculus of survival pressing against the need for speed.

---

**Xanth Offers a Ride**
Vukradin persuades Xanth to carry members of the party to the foothills (Persuasion 17). Xanth agrees readily enough, though he makes clear his hooves are no good for mountain climbing — he will take them as far as the area near the gold mine, no further.
A centaur's generosity, with honest limits attached.

---

**The Political Calculation**
Vukradin weighs the idea of swinging through Phandalin to seek reinforcements. The thought of what that detour might cost him politically — handing ammunition to his opponents back home — settles the matter. He says nothing about it aloud, but the consideration passes across his face.
The price of leadership measured in miles and reputation.

--- 

**Deciding Kaella's Fate**
The party hands Sister Kaella over to Corbin, who accepts the assignment with visible enthusiasm. Corbin asks: "Can I beat her?" Valphine's Insight check (25) reads the room immediately — there is a personal history between these two, and Corbin intends to make her suffer for it. Kaella screams and curses at the party as she is handed over, calling Corbin a lunatic. The party tacitly lets it stand.
Some arrangements are better left unexamined.


**Devising the Mount Strategy**
The party works out the transport plan: Xanth carries Vukradin and Valphine on his back, Soma wild shapes into bear form to carry another rider, and Boney the horse handles the rest. It is an ungainly arrangement, but it means arriving at the foothills with no exhaustion levels instead of one.
The indignity of riding a centaur weighed against the indignity of dying tired.

---

**The Long Rest**
Before setting out, the party takes a full long rest — spells replenished, wounds closed, strength returned. Vukradin settles in, instrument nearby, knowing what the morning will demand.
The last quiet before the mountain.
