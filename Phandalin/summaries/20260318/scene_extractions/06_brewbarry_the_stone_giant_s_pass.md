**Entering the Narrow Pass**
The mountain trail narrowed into a defile of compressed stone, and Brewbarry felt the wrongness before he could name it — rocks that should have been still were shimming faintly, rolling without wind, phasing at the edges as though the world itself had grown uncertain. The Carver's trail cut straight through here, over every obstacle, with the inhuman rhythm of something that no longer needed to think about where its feet fell.

**The Giants' Murmuring**
A group of stone giants stood gathered in the pass, speaking in their low, resonant language — deep enough to feel in the chest. Brewbarry understood their words. They were troubled by the rocks. Something was off, and they knew it.

**The Carver's Passage — Confirmed**
GM as Stone Giants: "The little one with the obsidian sword came through this path."
Vukradin: "Did you try to stop him?"
GM as Stone Giants: "He is a little one. Is of no concern of ours."
Soma: "I wouldn't be so sure."
Vukradin: "Is there a way we can help you?"
GM as Stone Giants: *(points to the path, indicating the way they could help is by dealing with the other little one)*
Vukradin: "So, how would us dealing with him help you?"
GM as Stone Giants: "You would no longer be here and not talking to us."
The giants' patience for conversation was not so much thin as nonexistent — Brewbarry recognized the tone, if not the sentiment.

**Questioning the Giants on the Dragon**
Vukradin: "But the dragon? They don't… the dragon doesn't bother them?"
GM as Stone Giants: "When Am broke the sundering, some thought that it was time for giants to seize power over the little ones. Those giants failed, and they were tempted by… and Am was tempted by the power of Tiamat and her plans to rule the world with her dragons. We have learned from Hekaton that now the war with the dragons is over, and the war with the little ones is over."
Valphine: "So you're not interested in fighting dragons."
GM as Stone Giants: "Yes, the dragon can stay where the dragon lives, and the dragon does not bother us."
Soma/Brewbarry: "He will soon."
The giants were unmoved — colossal introverts who had learned their philosophy from disaster and had no intention of revisiting the lesson.

**Valphine Presses the Evidence**
Valphine: "The corrupted one seeks to corrupt the dragon."
GM as Stone Giants: "Do you have any proof?"
Soma pressed Meril's staff against one of the displaced stones, and the truth of it surfaced — psychic energy had warped reality, causing rocks to phase in and out along the Carver's exact path. The evidence was there in the stone itself. The giants looked, and acknowledged it, and still would not move.
GM as Stone Giants: "Hekaton has said, and the failure of the breaking of the sundering has shown, that giants must take care of giant things."

**One Final Question**
Vukradin: "Was there anybody else with… the little one?"
GM as Stone Giants: "No."
The Carver had come through alone.

**The Skill Challenge — Brewbarry's Ascent**
The mountain ascent was a test of body and will — six possible chances to prove the party could handle terrain this unforgiving, and three successes needed before they could claim the pass. Brewbarry put his Goliath frame to work on the Athletics check and succeeded, hauling himself and his weight up over stone and ice where a smaller person might have lost their footing entirely. The mountain demanded something physical in return for passage, and he gave it.

**The Giants' Gift**
Before the party moved on, one of the stone giants pressed two potions into their hands — a Potion of Greater Healing and a Potion of Hill Giant Strength. The gesture was wordless and without ceremony. The giants had nothing else to say, and everything about their posture suggested the party had already stayed longer than welcome. Brewbarry took note of the gifts: the kind of things you carry when you expect the worst is still ahead.

**Speculating on the Obsidian Sword**
Soma/Brewbarry: "Level-draining sword."
Vukradin: "I know that I don't want to come anywhere near it."
Nobody had proof. But the Carver was alone, marching on a dragon, carrying something made of obsidian — and the rocks behind him were still phasing in and out of existence where his feet had touched them.
