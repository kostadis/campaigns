**Sensing the Wrongness**
As the party approached the base of the mountain, Soma felt it before she saw anything — a prickling at the edges of her druidic awareness, a disturbance pressing against the natural order of things.
GM (narrating): "You get a natural sense that something is very off. That the natural order of things has been disrupted."
The sensation was not pain, but wrongness — the land itself registering something that should not be.

---

**Discovering the Carver's Trail**
The footprints appeared soon after — human in shape, but nothing about them felt human.
GM (narrating): "The Carver has left a disturbing trail… it's like he's following a straight line. If there's a rock, he goes over it, he doesn't go around it."
GM (narrating): "There's like a perfect rhythm to them. Like a robotic… normal human…"
Vukradin: "Although they were made by, like, a construct, not by a…"
Soma: "Or a guy who's under some sort of, like, demonic spell or something, like, who's possessed."
The trail cut through the terrain without deviation or hesitation — no stumble, no detour, no sign that the body making these prints had any instinct for self-preservation left.

---

**The Stone Giants' Pass**
The narrow defile opened onto a gathering of stone giants, murmuring to one another in deep, resonant tones. Brewbarry and Valphine understood their words — something was wrong with the rocks.
GM as Stone Giants: "The little one with the obsidian sword came through this path."
Vukradin: "Did you try to stop him?"
GM as Stone Giants: "He is a little one. Is of no concern of ours."
Soma: "I wouldn't be so sure."
Vukradin: "Is there a way we can help you?"
The giants responded by pointing down the path — their help was for the party to stop talking and deal with the problem themselves.
GM as Stone Giants: "You would no longer be here and not talking to us."

---

**Reading the Warped Rocks**
Soma pressed Meril's Staff against one of the displaced, shimmering stones and felt the truth of what had passed through here.
Soma: "Touch the staff to some of the out-of-place rocks. What do we get?"
GM (narrating): "They are rocks that have been warped… some kind of psychic energy has warped reality."
Soma: "That guy is possessed. There's… nobody walks like that. Possessed by, I don't know."
The rocks phased in and out of existence along the Carver's path — reality itself refusing to sit still where he had walked.

---

**Warning the Giants**
The stone giants absorbed the evidence with the patience of mountains, then fell back on their philosophy.
GM as Stone Giants: "Hekaton has said, and the failure of the breaking of the sundering has shown, that giants must take care of giant things."
Soma (alongside Brewbarry): "He will soon."
The warning landed without purchase. The giants had chosen their stillness, and nothing the party said would move them from it — though they did press two potions into the group's hands before the adventurers moved on.

---

**The Giants' Final Question**
Before departing, Vukradin asked one last thing of the giants.
Vukradin: "Was there anybody else with… the little one?"
GM as Stone Giants: "No."
The Carver was traveling alone.

---

**Speculating on the Obsidian Sword**
As the party moved away from the giants, the sword weighed on Soma's mind.
Soma: "Obsidian Sword, though, do we know anything about that?"
Soma (alongside Brewbarry): "Level-draining sword."
Vukradin: "I know that I don't want to come anywhere near it."
Nobody disagreed.
