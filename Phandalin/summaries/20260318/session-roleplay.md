# Roleplay Highlights — Session — 2026-03-26

## Character Voices

### Brewbarry (Kostadis)
- Earnest and guileless; delivers profound personal revelations with casual simplicity, often punctuated by physical comedy (patting his belly, stretching). Speaks in blunt declaratives that accidentally land as philosophy.
- Defensive about his past but disarmingly honest about his present vices. His loyalty to Vukradin surfaces as practical protectiveness.
> "Yes, but since I discovered drinking, I think that was wasted youth."
> "Uthgardians don't do alcohol. Well, I mean, they do alcohol, but it's really foul-tasting. It does not have the varieties and the textures and the flavors and the colors of wine and beer and hard liquor. It's more like... the thing that makes you drunk, in condensed form."
> "We are going to go face dragons. We need fireballs."

### Vukradin (David)
- Sharp-tongued moralist who weaponizes his principles as both shield and sword. Oscillates between genuine idealism and sardonic dismissiveness. Quick with a quip, especially when cornered ethically. His refusal to accept "unearned" treasure borders on the absurd and he knows it.
> "This is not my necklace. I am holding the necklace until we can find its rightful owner."
> "Keep hoping, babe."
> "I do a lot to get my music studio. I have to kill a dragon and a lot of orcs."

### Soma (Wade)
- The warmest voice in the party — quiet, grounded, maternal. Speaks in short, gentle sentences that carry unexpected weight. Her wisdom manifests not as pronouncements but as simple observations that reframe conversations. Identifies deeply with motherhood and nature.
> "You know, Vukradin, the animals talk a lot."
> "I don't have big aspirations."
> "We are." *(quietly, after Vukradin says "mothers are resilient")*

### Valphine (Gary)
- Cool, understated menace wrapped in priestly courtesy. Delivers threats with the same tone as greetings. Her Drow instincts surface in moments of interrogation — she leans into intimidation with relish and recognizes the hunger for revenge in others.
> "Light is deliciously painful for me."
> "We can still do that. If you'd like."
> "Or if I'd like."

### Xanth the Centaur (GM/Kostadis)
- Exuberant Greek-accented philosopher-warrior with a flair for flattery and backhanded compliments. Speaks in run-on sentences that loop between genuine wisdom and social commentary. Deeply knowledgeable about regional ecology but delivers information through tangential storytelling. Openly affectionate toward Soma, openly baffled by Vukradin's ethics.
> "Soma, that, that is, I could kiss you."
> "Oh, my friend Vukradein, you know nothing."
> "Soma, the little one, does he not? The one with feathers, doesn't he understand these things?"

### Sister Kaella (GM/Kostadis)
- A survivor's voice — calculating, self-aware, darkly pragmatic. Speaks with the resigned clarity of someone who has already accepted every possible bad outcome and is just negotiating which one. Delivers cutting character assessments with the detachment of an intelligence analyst.
> "As long as I'm alive, there's always hope."
> "And Vukradin… I had noticed that you had a difficulty in dealing with morally gray environments."
> "You guys going to Ice Spire Hold and failing, and the dragon, you know, and Carver takes over the dragon and tries to kill everybody, that would be also bad, but at least we tried. And by we, I mean… you."

### Stone Giants (GM/Kostadis)
- Terse, monolithic speech. Sentences like boulders — dropped, immovable. View all smaller beings as equally insignificant and equally noisy. Their philosophy of non-intervention is absolute and unmoved by argument or evidence.
> "He is a little one. Is of no concern of ours."
> "You would no longer be here and not talking to us."
> "Hekaton has said, and the failure of the breaking of the sundering has shown, that giants must take care of giant things."

## Memorable Exchanges

### Brewbarry's Secret Origin — The Goodberry Confession
*Around the campfire, the word "goodberries" triggers something unexpected from the party's cheerful drunk.*

**Brewbarry:** "So I haven't told anybody for this. But, after the artifact was stolen, I found one of these berries, and they were good, and they healed you."
**Vukradin:** "And you were like, not hungry anymore for the whole day."
**Brewbarry:** "Yeah, and that's why they thought I was… I was asleep on the job, and didn't take care of things."
**Vukradin:** "And really, you were just drinking your wine?"
**Brewbarry:** "No, I was sober, and I had a... I had a..."
**Vukradin:** "Whoa! You're never sober."
**Brewbarry:** "I'll have you note that I did not drink before the loss of the artifact in my banning from the Uthgartian tribe."
**Vukradin:** "Oh. I guess I never knew you in those days..."
**Brewbarry:** "Yes, but since I discovered drinking, I think that was wasted youth."
**Vukradin:** "Sobriety was wasted youth."
**Brewbarry:** "Yes, yes, I… instead of drinking when I should have been, I was sober."

### The Interrogation of Sister Kaella
*Valphine revives the captured spymaster. What follows is a masterclass in the party's contrasting approaches to moral complexity.*

**Valphine:** "Hello, Sister Kayla."
**Sister Kaella:** "Thank God you're alive!"
**Vukradin:** "You're the one who should be lucky he's alive."
**Sister Kaella:** "Oh, no, I… there was the other possibility. Jax swings, and I'm alive, and Jax is now going to torture me to death for several, you know, painfully long, protracted periods."
**Valphine:** "We can still do that. If you'd like."
**Sister Kaella:** "Yes, but it's an if. Whereas with Jax, it was a certainty, so now there's at least a…"
**Valphine:** "Or if I'd like."
**Sister Kaella:** *(impressed)* "I didn't know you had it in you."

### Kaella Reads Vukradin Like a Book

**Sister Kaella:** "And so, to be honest, I figured that a drow cleric of Lathander, who understands the importance of darkness and light, and all the…"
**Vukradin:** "You'll do really well in prison. There are a lot of morally gray people there, you can hang out with them for many years."
**Sister Kaella:** "As long as I'm alive, there's always hope."
**Vukradin:** "Keep hoping, babe."

## Standout Moments

- **Brewbarry reveals the goodberry was the cause of his exile** — the enchanted berry's sustaining magic kept him fed and content while an artifact was stolen on his watch. His tribe assumed dereliction; he was actually sober and alert, undone by a druid's gift. The irony that he only started drinking *after* being punished for seeming drunk deepens his Redemption arc significantly.

- **Sister Kaella's surgical assessment of Vukradin's moral rigidity** — "I had noticed that you had a difficulty in dealing with morally gray environments" — delivered to his face, confirming that his principled stance is both his greatest strength and a genuine liability in espionage situations.

- **Soma's quiet "We are"** — when Vukradin casually says "mothers are resilient" about the grieving drake, Soma's two-word response carries the weight of her entire character philosophy, connecting her to the bereaved creature and affirming the maternal identity that drives her adventuring.

- **Valphine's escalating interrogation** — her progression from "We can still do that. If you'd like" to "Or if I'd like" shows the Drow instinct surfacing beneath the Lathander devotion, and the GM's note that she "knows what's going on" when Corbin volunteers to guard Kaella suggests she recognizes her own darker nature in others.

- **Vukradin discovers he has political opponents** — "I have opponents?" — the genuine surprise revealing that his principled moral stance has been so all-consuming he hasn't noticed the political consequences of his Privy Council position.

- **Xanth recognizes Meril's Staff** — the centaur's demeanor shifts entirely upon seeing the artifact, pledging aid based on an old friendship with Meril. Soma's humble response — "I don't have big aspirations" — contrasts powerfully with the cosmic significance the staff may hold.

- **Soma warns the Stone Giants: "He will soon"** — after the giants dismiss the dragon as irrelevant to their affairs, Soma's simple warning carries the weight of someone who understands ecological disruption better than the giants do.

- **The Glacier of Whispers** — the party receives their first psychic impression of the Carver's true nature: *"He walks without rest, without breath, without soul."* The warped rocks, robotic footprints, and killed drake confirm the Carver is no longer human.

- **Soma identifies the Carver as possessed** — "That guy is possessed. There's… nobody walks like that. Possessed by, I don't know." Her druidic attunement to the warped natural order gives the party its clearest picture yet of what they're walking toward.

## Voice Keeper Notes

**NPC Speech Patterns to Maintain:**
- **Xanth:** Effusive Greek-accented speech, loves giving people elaborate Hellenized versions of their names, alternates between genuine warmth and bewildered commentary on the party's choices. Deeply loyal to Meril's legacy and by extension Soma. Flatters Vukradin extravagantly while clearly finding his ethics incomprehensible.
- **Sister Kaella:** Speaks like an intelligence analyst giving a debrief — calm probability assessments even about her own death. Respects Valphine's capacity for darkness, finds Soma's compassion admirable, and views Vukradin's moral absolutism as a tactical weakness. Uses phrases like "on a deeply personal level" and "strictly speaking."
- **Stone Giants:** Maximum three sentences per response. Subject-verb-object. No flourish. Everything smaller than a giant is a "little one." Their worldview is absolute non-intervention post-Hekaton.
- **Corbin:** Eager to please but with a dark edge — his enthusiasm for guarding Kaella reveals unresolved trauma from his time in the Carver's organization. Takes to Valphine's "sacraments" readily.

**PC Emotional States for Future NPC Dialogue:**
- **Vukradin** has been directly told his moral inflexibility is a weakness by an enemy. NPCs should continue to test this — especially when dragon hoard treasure becomes available. His political naivety ("I have opponents?") should create consequences in Phandalin.
- **Brewbarry's** revelation that he was sober when his tribe's artifact was stolen — and only became a drinker afterward — should inform how NPCs from his past interact with him. The goodberry connection may link to druidic characters.
- **Soma** is quietly carrying the party's emotional weight. Her connection to Meril's Staff is deepening (Xanth confirmed it as a powerful artifact). NPCs attuned to nature should react to the staff.
- **Valphine** is walking a razor's edge between Lathander and her Drow nature. Her comfort with intimidation and her recognition of vengeance in Corbin should inform encounters that test her Light's Path.

**Promises, Threats, and Vows to Reference:**
- Vukradin told Sister Kaella she'll "do really well in prison" — this becomes a concrete obligation if the party survives.
- Corbin was authorized to beat Kaella "until she has one hit point" — her condition upon the party's return should reflect this.
- Xanth pledged to help Soma based on his friendship with Meril — he guided them partway but refused the mountains. He remains a potential ally for forest-based encounters.
- The Stone Giants were warned by both Soma and Brewbarry that the Carver/dragon situation would eventually affect them. If the Carver succeeds, the giants' non-intervention philosophy should be tested.
- Valphine confirmed her "arrangement" with Sister Kaella still stands — the nature of this deal should have future consequences.
- The Glacier of Whispers delivered a psychic message: "He walks without rest, without breath, without soul." This should echo in the Carver encounter.
