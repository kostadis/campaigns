# Session 2026-03-18

Date: Mar 18th, 2026

## Summary
The dust of battle had barely settled when the party took stock of what remained. Jax and his ogre lay dead, and the rest of their company had been cut down in the fighting. Only Sister Kaella had been spared — deliberately, and with purpose. Vukradin reminded the others that they had a great deal on their plate: a dragon to confront, stolen artwork to return, and a world that seemed to be unraveling at the seams. Brewbarry, meanwhile, found himself unexpectedly distracted when someone mentioned goodberries. The word triggered something deep in his memory — a recollection of finding one of those magical little berries long ago, after the artifact of his tribe had been stolen. He had eaten it, felt full and strangely healed, and his tribe had mistaken his unusual calm for drunkenness. That misunderstanding had cost him everything. He had not discovered actual drinking until after his exile, and he now reflected with great conviction that sobriety had been wasted youth.

The party turned their attention to the wounded centaur who had been present throughout the chaos. Soma stepped forward and called upon divine power to mend the creature's injuries, and the centaur rose to his full, impressive height. He introduced himself as Xanthopoulos — Xanth, for those who found the full name a mouthful — and he spoke with the cadence and warmth of someone who had seen a great deal of the world. He explained that a white dragon had moved into the Icepire Mountains some time ago, displacing the orc tribes that had long called those peaks home. The orcs had fractured into multiple factions as they spilled southward, some falling under the sway of the storm god Talos, others clinging to their original loyalties, and all of them pressing ever closer to Phandalin and the surrounding settlements.

Xanth's eyes fell upon the staff Valphine carried, and his expression shifted immediately. He declared it was no simple stick — it was a powerful artifact, and it had belonged to his old friend Meryl. The single leaf clinging to its wood — newly grown, Xanth recognized, not a survivor of some depleted season — told him that Meryl must know Valphine, or at least know of her. (The staff once bore many leaves in a prior era of the artifact, but the leaves currently on it are growing back under the new bearer; the staff gains leaves as the bearer accepts the role, never loses them.) He pledged his help to the group on the spot. He also spoke of the Carver, the shadowy criminal leader who had orchestrated so much of the region's suffering. The Carver had three lieutenants: Borg, who the party had already killed; Zax, also now dead; and Sister Kaella, who lay bound and barely conscious at their feet.

With that, the party revived Kaella just enough to question her. Vukradin leaned into the interrogation with a forcefulness that visibly surprised her — she admitted she hadn't known he had it in him. Under pressure, she revealed that the Carver had descended into madness and was now marching toward a place called Ice Spire Hold, intent on performing a ritual to possess the white dragon and unleash its fury upon everyone who had wronged him. She confessed that she had been quietly working against him ever since he accused her of failing in her intelligence duties, reasoning that her survival odds improved considerably if he were dead. She had approached Valphine because Valphine was the only one who had shown up, and because she believed Vukradin's strong moral compass would have made him an unreliable conspirator. She provided the party with directions to Ice Spire Hold and warned them plainly: they had to get there before the ritual was complete.

The party debated their route. The mountains were roughly fifty-five miles away as the crow flies, but the terrain would make every step harder than the last. Going through Phandalin first would cost them precious time and energy. Xanth offered a solution — he would carry members of the party on his back as far as the foothills, sparing them the worst of the early march. The group devised a plan: Xanth would carry two riders, Soma would take bear form and carry another, and Boney the horse would handle the rest. Sister Kaella would be left behind in the custody of Corbin, who accepted the assignment with an enthusiasm that made Valphine smile knowingly. She recognized something in his eyes — a history between him and Kaella that the others didn't fully understand. Some things, she decided, were better left unasked. The party took a long rest, gathered their strength, and set out for the mountains.

As they approached the peaks, Soma felt it first — a wrongness in the natural order, a disturbance that prickled at the edges of her awareness. The party found the Carver's trail soon after, and it was deeply unsettling. His footprints followed a perfectly straight line through the terrain, cutting over rocks rather than around them, maintaining an inhuman rhythm that suggested something mechanical, or something possessed. The footprints were human in shape, but nothing about the path they traced felt human at all.

The trail led them into a narrow mountain pass where a group of stone giants had gathered, murmuring to one another in their deep, resonant language. Both Brewbarry and Valphine understood their words — the giants were troubled by the rocks, which had been behaving strangely. Valphine pressed Meryl's staff against one of the displaced stones and felt the truth of it: the Carver's passage had warped reality itself, causing the rocks to phase in and out of existence, shimmering and rolling without wind or cause. The giants confirmed that the little one with the obsidian sword had passed through, and that he was of no concern to them — though they made clear that if the party wished to deal with him, they would not object. Before the adventurers moved on, one of the giants pressed two potions into their hands: one to restore health, one to lend the strength of a hill giant to whoever drank it.

Beyond the pass lay the Glacier of Whispers, and it was unlike anything the party had encountered before. The ice was stained a deep, unsettling violet, and frozen faces pressed against it from within, their mouths open in silent, endless screams. The Carver's footprints glowed with faint psychic energy along the glacier's surface, and as the party moved through it, whispers crept into their minds unbidden: *He walks without rest, without breath, without soul.* Valphine recalled something she had half-dismissed from her priestly studies — that Lathander's blessing could be invoked in places like this to shield the mind from psychic assault. The party pushed through with their wills intact, shaken but unbroken.

Higher still, they found the frozen corpse of a young white drake, its eyes burned out with necrotic energy. The Carver had not gone around the creature's lair. He had walked straight through it. Above them, the mother drake circled in confused, furious grief, her cries echoing off the stone. Vukradin raised his instrument and began to play — something slow and mournful, a melody that acknowledged her loss without pretending to fix it. Brewbarry joined in, and together they filled the cold mountain air with music. The mother drake's circling slowed. She descended, lay down beside her dead young, and let the party pass.

The final stretch before the summit presented a different kind of obstacle: a wall of massive boulders and ice that blocked the path entirely. Soma studied the terrain carefully, searching for any purchase in the frozen earth, any seam in the stone that could be worked. She found what she needed — just enough soil and loose rock to manipulate — and began the painstaking work of carving a passage through the impassable terrain. Vukradin offered his bardic inspiration to steady her focus as she worked. Whether the path would hold, and what waited for them on the other side, remained to be seen — but the party pressed on, one handhold at a time, toward Ice Spire Hold and the ritual they had to stop.
