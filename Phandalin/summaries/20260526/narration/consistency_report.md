## ISSUE 1 — Elemental Cleaver misidentified as a halberd attachment

**Location:** Summary; Items (Elemental Cleaver); Scenes (The Battle at the Lodge)

**Issue:** The recap describes Brewbarry's halberd as "crackling with corrosive acid" from the Elemental Cleaver ability, and the Items section describes Elemental Cleaver as a magical ability that infuses his halberd with acid damage. The party document lists Elemental Cleaver as a **returning handaxe**, not a subclass infusion or weapon-enhancement ability for a halberd.

**Evidence:** Party document under Brewbarry's Items of Significance: *"Elemental Cleaver (returning handaxe)."* Path of the Giant's Elemental Cleaver is a subclass feature that allows the barbarian to imbue **a single weapon** with elemental damage — this would apply to whichever weapon Brewbarry designates, but the recap consistently describes it as halberd-plus-acid without clarifying that the handaxe is the designated Elemental Cleaver weapon or that the feature was transferred to the halberd this session. The description in the Items section is particularly misleading, calling it "a magical ability used by Brewbarry to infuse his halberd."

**Suggested Fix:** Clarify that Elemental Cleaver is a handaxe (or note explicitly that Brewbarry applied the Elemental Cleaver infusion to his halberd for this battle per the Path of the Giant feature). The Items section description should not describe it as generically "a magical ability" — it is a specific named weapon that also carries the subclass's elemental infusion mechanic.

## ISSUE 8 — Moonbeam damage dice inconsistently described between scenes

**Location:** Scenes (Vorga's War Cry); Spells (Moonbeam)

**Issue:** In the Vorga's War Cry scene, the recap states Soma *"rolls 3d10 at third level, scoring two 10s and a 9 for 29 radiant damage. Gorthok makes his Constitution save and takes 14 points."* The Spells section confirms Moonbeam is cast at third level (3d10). However, the Summary section states *"rolling an exceptional 3d10 — two tens and a nine — for 29 radiant damage before halving it to 14 on a failed Constitution save."* These two descriptions contradict each other: the Scenes section says Gorthok **made** the save (and took 14 points), while the Summary says Gorthok **failed** the save (and took 14 points as the halved result).

**Evidence:** Moonbeam on a failed save deals full damage; on a successful save, half damage. 29 halved is 14 (rounded down). The Summary's version (failed save, halved to 14) is internally consistent with the math. The Scenes version (made save, took 14) is also mathematically consistent. These are mutually contradictory accounts of the same roll.

**Suggested Fix:** Confirm with the session recording or GM notes whether Gorthok passed or failed the Moonbeam Constitution save on this roll. Correct the inconsistent version. (The Summary's *"failed"* reading is more consistent with the narrative — Gorthok dropping to 3 HP suggests full tracking of damage — but this should be verified.)
