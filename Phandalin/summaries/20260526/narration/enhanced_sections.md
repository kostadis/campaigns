## Scenes
### The Battle Begins — Charms and Positioning
#### The party initiates a confrontation against a large group of orcs and anchorites, utilizing powerful enchantments to thin the enemy ranks before the main melee begins.
- Vukradin casts a powerful hypnotic enchantment that charms ten orcs and two anchorites, effectively removing them from the initial fray; six orcs and Gorthok resist entirely.
- Vukradin uses his silver tongue as a free action to warn allies not to disturb the hypnotized enemies: "As long as they are hypnotized, they will remain hypnotized" — clarifying that unlike sleep, they could not be woken by damage, only by another creature using its action to shake them.
- The party identifies Gorthok, a giant boar that resisted the charm, as a primary threat; Vukradin notes it doesn't cast spells but produces its lightning attack like a breath weapon — not a spell — so silence would not suppress it.
- Soma prepares to engage, planning to move the Moonbeam through the battlefield while remaining in elk form, maintaining concentration throughout.
- Soma conjures a third-level Moonbeam, targeting three enemies in a 5-foot radius cylinder; two make the DC 15 Constitution save and take 5 damage, one fails and takes 10.
- Valphine casts Silence on Boney's saddle, creating a 20-foot radius mobile zone of absolute quiet; only Soma and Valphine can see the aura on Roll20 — the other players see only a green line and a yellow dot, frustrating attempts to coordinate.
- Boney dashes across the battlefield in a full 120-foot movement, looping around the back wall to avoid attacks of opportunity, positioning the silence bubble over the Anchorites; one Anchorite attempts to flee the zone and provokes an attack of opportunity from Boney, who whiffs.
- The party coordinates token markers — hearts — on the map to denote charmed enemies, helping allies focus fire on the six uncharmed orcs.

### The Battle at the Lodge — Silence and Lightning
#### The party engages in a chaotic skirmish against orcs and Anchorites of Talos, utilizing magical silence and area-of-effect spells to control the battlefield.
- Soma moves the Moonbeam and strikes several enemies; at least one Anchorite in the beam is forced to revert to natural form, losing shapeshifted status.
- Valphine's silence on Boney's saddle proves effective — the Anchorites within the bubble cannot cast spells that require verbal components, though one Anchorite who is not within the bubble attempts a Thunderwave later in the battle.
- Boney dashes across the battlefield, repositioning the zone of silence to envelop the Anchorites.
- The orcs, rather than attacking, use their actions to shake their charmed comrades awake; one of the Anchorite leaders shouts out to disrupt the charm: "Their buddies have been attacked — they were charmed, and the first thing you do when your buddy's charmed is shake them" (GM paraphrase). Three charmed orcs are woken, leaving seven still charmed.
- Gorthok the Thunder Boar does not get its lightning breath on this roll of the die, but attacks with its lightning tusks: 22 to hit, dealing 12 slashing plus 7 lightning and 12 bludgeoning plus 7 thunder — 38 total points of damage — against Soma, dropping the druid to 17 hit points.
- Gorthok's lightning attack also scorches both Brewbarry and Falcon the Hunter earlier in the round: the Anchorite controlling the lightning bolt targets them both, dealing 37 divided by 2 (18 points) to those who fail the DC 12 Dexterity save.
- Falcon fires two arrows at Anchorite Number 2 as his ranged option, hitting once for 11 points of damage; the second shot misses.
- Brewbarry enters a primal rage, gains a 20-foot reach as a giant, and moves into position; his first halberd attack hits for 8 acid damage plus 2 rage bonus plus 1d6 elemental cleaver damage — 16 total. His second attack rolls an 11, misses, but he uses Bardic Inspiration (automatically guaranteed rather than rolled) to hit, dealing 9 more points of damage.
- Brewbarry strikes his target down: "9 puts it down? Okay" (GM).

### The Chaotic Melee — Allies and Enemies Clash
#### The party and their orc allies engage in a massive melee against the Anchorites of Talos and the monstrous Gorthok, with the battlefield descending into chaos.
- Prutha the orc charges into the fray, engaging in a brutal duel with a rival orc while shouting insults in Orcish. Valphine translates diplomatically for the party: "They're greeting each other in traditional orcish form."
- Vukradin attempts Command Grovel on Gorthok — DC 15 Wisdom save. Gorthok makes the save: "Fine, I didn't need 3rd level spells anyway."
- Soma, in the form of a giant elk, moves the Moonbeam across the battlefield to strike Gorthok and Anchorite Number 2; both fail their saves and take 7 points of damage each. Gorthok goes down to approximately 50 hit points.
- Soma's elk hoof strikes Gorthok for 20 damage in a follow-up attack, bringing the beast's total damage taken to 30 points removed.
- Valphine magically blinds Anchorite Number 1, sending the cultist into a panicked stumble: "I feel the power of Lathander" (Valphine). The Anchorite does nothing during his turn, frozen in panic, before making a DC 15 Constitution save at the end of his turn and recovering his sight.
- Boney moves through the combat, maintaining the zone of magical silence that disrupts the Anchorites' ability to cast lightning spells.
- Gorthok retaliates against Soma later in the round, dealing another 19 points of damage and knocking the druid unconscious — reverting them from elk form. The Moonbeam drops as concentration breaks, but crucially, Gorthok ends his turn just outside the Moonbeam's position, so he does not immediately burn.
- The battlefield descends into mass melee as the party's orc allies clash with the cultist orcs; the GM adjudicates the allied orc combat in bulk, deducting 5–9 hit points per side per round to keep things moving.
- An Anchorite, unable to cast spells within the silence, attempts a Thunderwave from outside the bubble, killing two allied orcs outright and knocking another back with 8 points of damage; the Anchorite then flies back from the blast.
- An Anchorite recovers from the blindness at the end of his turn, shaking off the magical affliction with a successful Constitution save.

### Vorga's War Cry and the Tide Turns
#### The party and their orc allies press their advantage as Vorga the Orc Warchief inspires the group, and Vukradin's command spell leaves Gorthok and an Anchorite groveling on the ground.
- Vukradin casts a second-level Command Grovel targeting both Gorthok and the Anchorite near Prutha simultaneously; both fail the DC 15 Wisdom save. Gorthok collapses prone: "Prone, and you lose all actions and reactions for this round" (Vukradin).
- Valphine strikes a wounded Anchorite with a Guiding Bolt, leaving that cultist at 2 hit points.
- Boney moves to keep the silence bubble repositioned and attacks Anchorite Number 2 on an attack of opportunity as the cultist flees, landing a 22-damage hit; the Anchorite is struck by the Moonbeam as well when it moves over them, taking a further 19 damage (one fails the save, one makes it) — leaving Number 2 at just 1 hit point.
- Boney is struck by a bolt of lightning from an Anchorite — failing a DC 12 Dexterity save — taking 25 points of damage. "Boney, no!" (Soma).
- Gorthok remains groveling on the ground thanks to Vukradin's magic; Soma moves the Moonbeam over the prone beast and rolls 3d10 at third level, scoring two 10s and a 9 for 29 radiant damage. Gorthok makes his Constitution save and takes 14 points — dropping to 3 hit points.
- Soma's elk hoof attacks finish Gorthok — but Gorthok rises back to 1 hit point through a supernatural Revivify ability: "Alright, the first attack knocks him down dead, but he has a Revivify ability, so he's back up to 1 hit point." The Moonbeam, already repositioned over him, deals the killing blow at the end of his turn.
- Vorga, the Orc Warchief, lets out a powerful war cry that grants advantage on attacks to Falcon, Soma, and Brewbarry for the following round: "Everybody within 30 feet gets advantage on attacks for your next round."
- Prutha and the allied orc tribe overwhelm several enemy orcs, killing multiple foes in rapid succession; allied orcs call out kills while shouting about the gods and the sun.

### The Fall of Gorthok and the Last Anchorite
#### The party focuses their final efforts on the monstrous Gorthok and the remaining Anchorites of Talos, bringing the battle to a close.
- Soma, reverting to elk form after being knocked unconscious and revived (the GM rules that the Grovel command expired and corrects an earlier oversight about Gorthok's turn), charges and hooves Gorthok; the Moonbeam finishes him at 1 hit point: "Oh no, in that case, he dies. He died to the Moonbeam."
- Brewbarry, enlarged and wielding his elemental halberd with advantage from Vorga's war cry, cleaves through an enemy orc for 16 damage (kills it, it had 15 hit points), then cleaves to a second target for 14 damage, then attacks the last Anchorite for 13 acid damage — dropping it from 2 hit points to dead: "He only had 2 hit points, he's dead." Brewbarry rolls the final damage die anyway, on principle.
- Falcon the Hunter charges Anchorite Number 1 with his longsword, bolstered by Vorga's war cry; after spending most of the session missing, he hits all three attacks this time for 18 points of damage: "This is like, you know, the defining moment of the Falcon, right?"
- Prutha delivers brutal spear strikes to a prone Anchorite, hitting twice for 26 total points of damage: "Pretty effective" (Valphine).
- Vukradin conjures a phantasmal image of the god Lathander to terrify the final Anchorite; the cultist fails his Intelligence save and, rolling to disbelieve on his turn with disadvantage, falls well below the DC 15 threshold. The Anchorite descends into a panicked state of religious guilt: "She's real! The drows, she's real! Talos, my god! Talos!" and then "I burned the shrine! I broke the handles! I did what you asked! Why is he here?!" The phantasm deals 7 points of psychic damage.
- Brewbarry delivers the final, crushing blow with his elemental halberd to slay the last remaining Anchorite: "And you kill him."

### The Battle's End and the Orcish Alliance
#### The party concludes their fierce battle, leading to a surprising diplomatic resolution with the orc tribe.
- Falcon the Hunter ruthlessly shoots down two fleeing orcs with his bow, sparking a debate about unnecessary violence; Soma calls it "senseless violence" while Vukradin argues practically that they will just come back.
- Prutha "takes her axe and mashes the orc into lots of little pieces," settling a tribal score in brutal fashion.
- Vorga, the Orc Warchief, declares the party honorary members of her tribe in recognition of their prowess in battle; her warriors echo the proclamation with bloodlusty cheers. Gary notes the similarity to the Ewok celebration at the end of Return of the Jedi.
- The Falcon's reaction to the honor: "He goes, huzzah! And… this is what happens when you stand and intervene, and don't just let everything happen to yourself" — the GM notes he has "gone fully the other way" in terms of his convictions.
- Valphine proposes to three orcs that they stay at the lodge as hired hands; three agree. Falcon immediately addresses them as stewards: "The first thing we'll have to do is teach you how to dress properly," promising to make them "the finest stewards in all the North." Vukradin notes with amusement that "he immediately turns into a douchey boss."
- Vukradin, exhausted, wanders toward the lodge's luxury guest room: "I've had kind of a long day. You know, I casted some spells, they weren't very effective… I'm kind of wandering back to the out. Like, finally we're done." He warns the party: "Don't steal any of that treasure. I'm gonna be napping."
- The party discusses the destruction of their wine casks by the Anchorites, which is revealed to have happened prior to the session — Brewbarry discovered the casks were damaged when he went down to the cellar. Stephane: "Oh my god, I am depressed." The GM: "Direct your rage appropriately — it was the Anchorites." Stephane: "I know Talos is the god of chaos, but come on. Gotta be some limits."
- The party discusses plans to explore the now-empty Woodland Manse and to recover the Boots of Elvenkind, described by Vukradin as "our fair, organic, fair-trade, conflict-free Boots of Elvenkind."
- The party levels up to seventh level; notable gains include Soma gaining access to a new level of wild shape and higher-level spells, Brewbarry gaining advantage on initiative rolls from a new subclass instinct, and Vukradin gaining access to 4th-level spells including Greater Invisibility, Polymorph, and Dimension Door.
- The party discusses the possibility of resurrecting Don Jon Raskin, with Valphine wondering aloud about a creative ritual to power True Resurrection; Vukradin half-jokes about "Red Wizards of Lathander" as a possible avenue.
- The GM suggests seventh level would be an ideal entry point for returning player Jarek: "We should all send him a message, like a group hug kind of thing."

## Locations
### Falcon's Hunting Lodge
The site of the climactic battle where the party and a friendly orc tribe defended against the Anchorites of Talos and their summoned beast, Gorthok. The battlefield was a large enclosed space with walls that restricted movement, forcing Boney to dash around the perimeter to reposition the silence bubble. It features a luxury guest room where Vukradin retreats to rest after the conflict, and a cellar where Brewbarry discovered the Anchorites had damaged or consumed the wine casks prior to the session.

### Woodland Manse
The suspected base of the Anchorites who attacked the party, believed to be empty following the battle — as the GM confirms: "The people at the Woodland Manse are the people who just attacked you." The party plans to explore it to recover the Boots of Elvenkind.

## Items
### Elemental Cleaver
A magical ability used by Brewbarry to infuse his halberd with corrosive acid during his rage, adding an extra 1d6 acid damage to each strike throughout the battle. Brewbarry confirms the 1d6 applies even to his enlarged halberd attacks and uses it consistently across both attack rolls each round.

### Falcon's Brass Rings
Rings worn by Falcon the Hunter, which he touches as a sentimental gesture — a reference to a long-lost love — before charging into melee combat against the Anchorites. The GM narrates this quietly before Falcon's decisive charge: "You see him fidget his brass rings — he fringes, he touches his brass ring and then mutters something to his long-lost love, and charges straight at the anchorite."

### Boots of Elvenkind
Magical footwear the party hopes to recover from the now-empty Woodland Manse, described by Vukradin as "our fair, organic, fair-trade, conflict-free Boots of Elvenkind." Vukradin specifically notes it is "an actual magical item that I might be able to use."

### Wine Casks
The party's wine stores that were destroyed, spilled into the ground, or drunk by the Anchorites of Talos prior to the session. Brewbarry discovered the damage when he went to the cellar early in the session. Stephane's reaction upon hearing about it at the end of the session: "Oh my god, I am depressed." The GM confirms it was the Anchorites, not the tribal orcs, who were responsible.

## Spells
### Hypnotic Pattern
Cast by Vukradin at the start of the battle, this spell created a twisting pattern of colors that successfully charmed ten orcs and two anchorites, effectively removing them from the initial fight. Six orcs and Gorthok made their saves and remained active. The party benefited from BLESS being active on several of the charmed orcs, which had aided their saving throws in other contexts but here counted against the party as it helped some orcs resist.

### Moonbeam
A silvery pillar of radiant light conjured and maintained by Soma throughout the battle at third level (3d10 damage). It was moved across the battlefield to strike multiple enemies, dealing consistent damage to Gorthok and the Anchorites, and proved instrumental in ultimately slaying Gorthok — both by wearing him down and by delivering the killing blow after his Revivify brought him back to 1 hit point. Notably, the Moonbeam also forces shapechangers to revert to their natural form when they fail the save, though this strips them of their shapeshifted benefits rather than dealing extra damage. Soma can maintain and reposition it while in wild shape form.

### Silence
Cast by Valphine onto Boney's saddle, creating a mobile 20-foot radius sphere where no sound could exist. Boney carried this zone across the battlefield, preventing the Anchorites within it from using their vocal spells. The GM clarifies that silence does not suppress an already-cast spell like Moonbeam — only new verbal-component spells are blocked. Notably, Gorthok's lightning attack is not a spell but a breath-weapon-like ability, so silence would not have suppressed it regardless. Only Soma and Valphine could see the silence aura rendered on Roll20; other players saw only partial visual indicators.

### Bless
A divine enchantment active on several party-allied orcs at the start of the battle, adding a 1d4 bonus to their attack rolls and saving throws. The GM notes it helped some orcs resist the Hypnotic Pattern, contributing to six orcs remaining active rather than being charmed.

### Silvery Barbs
A reactionary spell available to Vukradin, briefly considered when Gorthok was dealing damage to allies. Vukradin confirms he does not have the Lucky feat to otherwise force rerolls, making Silvery Barbs the primary option. It is also briefly considered and then withdrawn by Vukradin when he initially misidentifies who has taken damage in one exchange.

### Command
Used by Vukradin to force Gorthok and an Anchorite to grovel on the ground. The first attempt (single-target) on Gorthok fails — Gorthok makes his DC 15 Wisdom save: "Fine, I didn't need 3rd level spells anyway." The second attempt (second-level, targeting both Gorthok and the Anchorite near Prutha simultaneously) succeeds — both fail, fall prone, and lose all actions and reactions for the round. The prone condition from Grovel gives melee attackers advantage and is distinct from a normal prone in that the groveling creature cannot act to stand.

### Blindness/Deafness
Cast by Valphine to blind Anchorite Number 1, choosing blindness over deafness. The cultist is sent into a panicked stumble, unable to aim his spells and doing nothing on his turn. Valphine: "I feel the power of Lathander." The Anchorite eventually shook off the effect at the end of his turn with a successful DC 15 Constitution save: "He makes it, he is no longer blind."

### Guiding Bolt
A flash of divine radiance sent by Valphine that struck Anchorite Number 1 (down to 2 hit points at that point), leaving the cultist on the verge of death heading into the final exchanges of the battle.

### Phantasmal Force
Cast by Vukradin on the final Anchorite, manifesting a terrifying illusion of the god Lathander: "His vision will be the god of Lathander breaking him down." The Anchorite failed his initial Intelligence save. On his turn, the GM rolls his attempt to disbelieve with disadvantage against DC 15 — he fails completely. The Anchorite screams: "She's real! The drows, she's real! Talos, my god! Talos!" and then dissolves into a guilt-ridden confession: "I burned the shrine! I broke the handles! I did what you asked! Why is he here?!" The spell deals 7 points of psychic damage and occupies the Anchorite so completely he can do nothing else.

### True Resurrection
A powerful and expensive divine spell discussed by the party after the battle as a means to bring back Don Jon Raskin. The cost is acknowledged as steep, and finding a willing cleric is noted as a challenge. Valphine muses about a creative ritual to power it; Vukradin half-jokes about "Red Wizards of Lathander" as a solution. The GM remains open to creative approaches: "If you guys come up with something…"

## NPCs
### Gorthok
A massive, monstrous boar-like creature associated with Talos. It possesses lightning-infused tusks dealing slashing/lightning and bludgeoning/thunder damage simultaneously, and a lightning bolt-like breath weapon that functions as a supernatural ability rather than a spell (and thus cannot be suppressed by Silence). It resisted the party's initial Hypnotic Pattern and proved a devastating combatant, dealing 38 points of damage in a single attack against Soma and later knocking the druid unconscious with 19 more. It survived a killing blow through a supernatural Revivify ability, rising to 1 hit point, before being finally slain by the lingering Moonbeam at the end of its turn.

### Anchorites of Talos
Cultists and shapechangers devoted to the god of storms, capable of casting lightning and thunder magic including Lightning Bolt and Thunderwave. Several were hypnotized by Vukradin's opening enchantment. They are numbered by the GM for tracking (Number 1, Number 2, etc.) and coordinated effectively — one shouting to disrupt the charm before the party could exploit it further. The silence zone suppressed their verbal-component spells effectively, though one Anchorite outside the bubble used Thunderwave, killing two allied orcs. The last Anchorite broke completely under Vukradin's Phantasmal Force, screaming a guilt-ridden confession about burning shrines and broken handles before being slain.

### Falcon the Hunter
A skilled fighter and ally of the party who defends his home with a longsword and bow, capable of three melee attacks per round. He fights for justice and his farm — the GM narrates him saying something heroic "about interventionism and fighting for justice, and no one's taking his farm again." He touches his brass rings as a sentimental gesture before his decisive charge. He suffers a long string of poor rolls (missing two of three on his first longsword charge), but bolstered by Vorga's war cry, lands all three strikes against the final Anchorite for 18 points of damage in the session's defining moment. He shows a ruthless streak by shooting down fleeing enemies but cheerfully accepts honorary membership in the orc tribe, immediately swinging to an enthusiastic new worldview: "This is what happens when you stand and intervene, and don't just let everything happen to yourself." He then promptly turns into a demanding employer, telling his three new orcish stewards the first order of business is learning to dress properly.

### Prutha
A fierce orc warrior allied with the party who has seemingly converted to the worship of Lathander, screaming "for my uncle, for my uncle, and the sun god, and Lathander — Lathander will burn all of you under his fiery bright light!" while charging into battle. She fights with brutal efficiency: 26 points of damage across two spear hits on a prone Anchorite, and a messy axe execution on a fallen enemy at the battle's end. Valphine describes her orcish battle-taunts diplomatically as "traditional orcish greetings."

### Vorga
An Orc Warchief who leads her tribe in alliance with the party. She uses a powerful war cry ability that grants advantage on attacks to all allies within 30 feet (Falcon, Soma, and Brewbarry) for the following round, proving pivotal in Brewbarry's cleave sequence and Falcon's decisive charge. At the battle's end she declares the party honorary members of her tribe, shouting "all sorts of appropriate orc-level, bloodlusty things" as her warriors echo the proclamation.

### Boney
The party's horse and mount, used strategically to carry a zone of magical silence across the battlefield to shut down the Anchorites' spellcasting. Boney dashes the full length of the room (120 feet) in round one to reposition. He provokes and attempts an attack of opportunity on a fleeing Anchorite (misses), lands a 22-damage hit on another Anchorite later in the battle, and is struck by a bolt of lightning for 25 points of damage — "Tony, no!" (Soma). His longsword attacks are mostly ineffective (rolls of 10 or below on several attempts) but his role carrying the silence zone proves consistently valuable.

### Don Jon Raskin
A deceased individual whom the party discusses resurrecting through a costly True Resurrection ritual after the battle concludes. The GM confirms a willing cleric would be needed, and the party entertains creative ritual alternatives — including, fancifully, the "Red Wizards of Lathander."

## Consistency Notes

The following issues from the consistency review could not be silently resolved in the text and require GM verification before future recaps treat them as settled canon.

**ISSUE 1 & 2 — Elemental Cleaver identity and Brewbarry's weapon choice.** The party document lists Elemental Cleaver as a returning handaxe, not a subclass infusion applied to the halberd. The recap's Items section describes it as "a magical ability used by Brewbarry to infuse his halberd," which is misleading. The Path of the Giant's Elemental Cleaver feature can designate any single weapon — it is possible Brewbarry designated his halberd for this battle — but this is not stated anywhere in the recap. Separately, Brewbarry's primary weapon per the party document is the Dragon Slayer Longsword; no explanation is given for why it was set aside in favour of the halberd. **GM should confirm:** (a) which weapon Brewbarry designated as his Elemental Cleaver for this session, and (b) whether the halberd was a deliberate tactical choice given the reach benefit from Path of the Giant enlargement
