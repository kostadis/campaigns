## Scene 1 — Valphine: The Battle Begins — Charms and Positioning

### [1] Generic prose / cliché simile

> The enchantment broke over the battlefield like a held breath finally released.

**Why:** A stock atmospheric opener that could front any fantasy section. Valphine's examples open cold and precise ("The henge stank of old power and recent panic. I catalogued the exits first — habit"), never with a soft "like a held breath" simile. Also note this exact image recurs in Scene 4 (see verdict).
**Suggested rewrite:** "Vukradin's working rolled out across the enemy line and I watched it take — not with my eyes, with the older sense Menzoberranzan never quite scrubbed out of me."

### [2] Voice spec conflict — register too warm

> It is also, if I am honest, a little deliciously maddening to wait through.

**Why:** "deliciously" is a bubbly, effusive word; the spec says her humor is "grim," "understated," and explicitly bars writing her warm or bubbly. Her edge is dry, not delighted.
**Suggested rewrite:** "It is also, if I am honest, a thing I have learned to wait through without comment."

### [3] Voice spec conflict — softness she wouldn't feel

> here we were, on the edge of a Talosian siege, and the main tactical discussion concerned what colour circle indicated holy silence on a skeletal horse.

**Why:** The framing word two lines up — "I found both charming and slightly absurd" — gives her a fond, indulgent register. Valphine reads absurdity coldly; she doesn't find a pre-siege squabble *charming*.
**Suggested rewrite:** Keep the observation, lose the fondness: "…what colour circle meant holy silence on a dead horse. In Menzoberranzan we had arguments like this too. People died during them."

## Scene 2 — Brewbarry: The Battle at the Lodge — Silence and Lightning

### [1] Cliché / on-the-nose simile

> every hit came with a sound like a bell struck by something too heavy for it.

**Why:** A workshopped simile inside an already-long sentence. Brewbarry's register is short, concrete, subject-verb-object; his examples never reach for "a sound like a bell struck by…". The fragments that follow ("Deep. Wrong.") are pure Brewbarry — the simile is the outlier.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Every hit came with a sound too deep for the thing that made it. Deep. Wrong. Thunder that doesn't roll — it lands."

### [2] Too clever for his register

> that particular snap that isn't sound so much as the air deciding to be somewhere else.

**Why:** "the air deciding to be somewhere else" is abstract personification — a narrator flourish. Brewbarry notices physical fact plainly ("the weight of a weapon, the smell of a place").
**Suggested rewrite:** "that snap that hits your body before it reaches your ears."

## Scene 3 — Soma: The Chaotic Melee — Allies and Enemies Clash

### [1] Recurring narrator tic + tell-not-show

> She said, with the particular serenity she reserves for moments of someone else's suffering:

**Why:** The "with the particular X of someone who / she reserves for…" construction is a house-style tic that recurs four times across this doc (here, Scene 4's "the look of a man who," Scene 6 twice). It also tells the emotion (serenity) rather than rendering it. Soma's lens is observation of concrete behavior, not psychological labeling.
**Suggested rewrite:** "She said it the way she does when the one hurting is someone else — quiet, exact, unbothered: 'I feel the power of Lathander.'"

### [2] Abstract / non-naturalist for Soma

> The battlefield had that quality of things accelerating beyond anyone's ability to choreograph them.

**Why:** "choreograph" is a performance metaphor; Soma frames the world through nature, weather, and tide (her examples: "I have watched a great many impressive things turn out to be weather"). The line reads generic rather than tortle-naturalist.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The battlefield had reached the point a storm reaches when no one is steering it anymore."

## Scene 4 — Vukradin: Vorga's War Cry and the Tide Turns

### [1] Convergence with house style — repeated image

> A sound that doesn't just carry volume but instruction — the body knows to straighten when it hears it, the shoulders settle back, the held breath releases.

**Why:** "the held breath releases" repeats the Scene 1 opener ("like a held breath finally released") — the same image surfacing under two different narrators is exactly the cross-section convergence this pass exists to catch. The rest of the sentence is strong Vukradin; only the recycled image is the problem.
**Suggested rewrite:** "…the body straightens before the mind agrees — shoulders back, lungs filling on their own."

### [2] Voice spec conflict — muddied where he should be sharp

> The difference between a name you give someone and the weight they carry it at are two different things

**Why:** It's tautological ("the difference between A and B *are two different things*") and grammatically snarled. Vukradin is the most articulate member of the party; a clumsy aphorism undercuts the one thing his voice is built on — polish.
**Suggested rewrite:** "A name you give someone and the weight they choose to carry it at are not the same thing — and Prutha was carrying his at a run."

## Scene 5 — Brewbarry: The Fall of Gorthok and the Last Anchorite

This scene is the cleanest in the file — "A kill deserves the full count. That's the only ceremony I have," "You don't ask about brass rings," and the closing "too large for the body, looking for somewhere to go" are all pitch-perfect Brewbarry. One borderline note only:

### [1] Slightly elevated for his register

> He broke the other way, the worse way — the inside-out kind where everything you thought you believed turns on you all at once.

**Why:** "the inside-out kind" is a touch abstract before he lands the plain, earned follow-up ("I've known that feeling. Not the god part."). Minor — the payoff redeems it; flagging only for completeness.
**Suggested rewrite (optional):** "He broke the other way — the bad way, where the thing inside you turns on you all at once."

## Scene 6 — Soma: The Battle's End and the Orcish Alliance

Note: "Such senseless violence," "There's a well of orcs…," and "the tortle equivalent of a strongly worded letter" are her canonical spec lines and correctly load-bearing — not flagged.

### [1] Recurring narrator tic (twice in this scene)

> vibrating with the particular joy of someone who has discovered that intervention is actually quite fun when it works.

> with the particular satisfaction of someone who has just handed off a parcel.

**Why:** Both are the same "the particular X of someone who…" construction flagged in Scene 3 — two more instances in a single scene. It's the dominant systemic tic of the doc.
**Suggested rewrites:** "vibrating with the joy of a man who just found out that stepping in is fun when it works." / "Valphine said it like a woman setting down a parcel she'd carried too far."

### [2] Generic level-up prose

> And then something shifted — the kind of shift you feel before you can name it, a deepening, a new weight settling into the bones alongside the old ones.

**Why:** "the kind of shift you feel before you can name it" plus "a deepening" is stock interiority. Four lines later she nails her own register ("the way you feel weather coming in") — this earlier sentence should reach for the same naturalist image instead of generic abstraction.
**Suggested rewrite:** "And then something shifted — the way the air shifts before weather you can't see yet. A new weight, settling into the bones beside the old ones."

---

## Verdict

The dominant, fixable problem is a single recurring construction — **"the particular X of someone who…" / "the look of a man who…"** — which appears four times across Scenes 3, 4, and 6 and is the strongest house-style fingerprint in the file; it both flattens distinct narrators into one rhythm and tells emotion instead of rendering it. A secondary cross-scene echo is the "held breath" image shared by Valphine (Scene 1 opener) and Vukradin (Scene 4). Per-narrator fidelity is otherwise high — Brewbarry's Scene 5 and Soma's canonical dry lines are excellent. This is spot-edit territory, not a re-narration: hand-fix the four tic instances and the two shared images and the chapter is clean.

**Highest-leverage fix:** search the file for "particular" and "of someone who" / "of a man who" and rewrite those four attributions in each narrator's own register.
