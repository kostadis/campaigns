# Chapter 43 The Aasimar has Landed

Date: May 26th, 2026

## Summary
The battle at Falcon's Hunting Lodge erupted with a brilliant opening gambit from Vukradin, whose powerful enchantment washed over the battlefield like a tide of irresistible color and light. Ten orcs and two of the Anchorites of Talos were swept into a hypnotic stupor, their weapons hanging slack at their sides as their minds drifted into blissful oblivion. Vukradin, ever the silver-tongued bard, immediately called out to his allies to leave the entranced enemies undisturbed, knowing that a single shaken shoulder could unravel everything. The party turned their attention to the real threats still standing — the free-willed orcs, the Anchorites who had resisted the charm, and most terrifyingly, Gorthok, the massive lightning-infused boar who had shrugged off the enchantment entirely.

Soma moved swiftly, conjuring a searing pillar of moonlight that swept across the battlefield, burning cultists and beast alike with celestial radiance. Valphine called upon the power of Lathander and bound a zone of absolute silence to Boney's saddle, turning their loyal mount into a roving suppressor of the Anchorites' storm magic. Boney galloped across the field, carrying the bubble of eerie quiet deep into the enemy's midst, cutting off the cultists' ability to call down lightning and thunder. Meanwhile, Brewbarry threw himself into a primal fury, his halberd crackling with corrosive acid as he charged toward Gorthok, landing a punishing blow against the beast's thick hide.

The battlefield descended into chaos as the orcs began shaking their charmed comrades awake, desperate to even the odds. Gorthok retaliated with terrifying force, unleashing a bolt of lightning that scorched both Brewbarry and Falcon the Hunter before goring a target with its electrified tusks, dealing devastating thunderous damage. Falcon, bloodied but unbowed, touched the brass rings on his fingers — a quiet gesture to a long-lost love — before shouting a challenge and loosing arrows at a nearby Anchorite. Prutha, the orc warrior who had found faith in Lathander, came screaming into the fray, hurling orcish insults at her tribal enemies while invoking the sun god's name with genuine fervor.

Vukradin spoke a single commanding word that forced both Gorthok and an Anchorite to collapse to the ground in a groveling heap, leaving them utterly vulnerable. Valphine followed up by blinding another Anchorite with a flash of divine power, sending the cultist into a panicked stumble, unable to see friend or foe. Vorga, the Orc Warchief fighting alongside the party, let out a thunderous war cry that sent a surge of battle-fury through Falcon, Soma, and Brewbarry, sharpening their instincts for the fight ahead. The allied orcs, inspired by the turning tide, overwhelmed several of the cultist orcs in brutal melee, cutting them down one by one.

Gorthok remained groveling on the ground thanks to the magic of Vukradin. Worn down to near death and then dropped by a killing blow, the beast clawed back to life through a supernatural revival — only for the moonbeam's lingering radiance, already positioned over it, to finish the job for good at the end of its turn.

With Gorthok fallen, the party turned on the last remaining Anchorite. Vukradin conjured a terrifying phantasm — a vision of Lathander himself, blazing with divine wrath, bearing down on the cultist with righteous fury. The Anchorite, unable to distinguish illusion from reality, broke completely. He screamed about burning shrines and broken handles, begging his god Talos to explain why Lathander had come for him, his confession spilling out in a torrent of guilt and terror. Falcon the Hunter, after a string of frustrating misses throughout the battle, finally found his rhythm — charging the cultist and landing three decisive sword strikes in a moment that felt like the defining act of his life. Brewbarry finished the job with a crushing blow from his elemental halberd, and the last Anchorite fell.

The surviving enemy orcs broke and fled, with Falcon ruthlessly shooting down two of them as they ran and Prutha executing another with her axes in a display of brutal tribal justice. Vorga raised her weapon to the sky and declared the party honorary members of her tribe, her warriors echoing the proclamation with bloodlusty cheers. Valphine, ever the diplomat, convinced three orcs to start working for Falcon. Falcon immediately addressed them as staff, calling them stewards, and suggested they needed to change how they dress. Vukradin, exhausted and battered, wandered toward the lodge's guest room for a well-earned rest, barely registering the celebration around him. The first great battle of their journey was over, and the party — now seventh-level adventurers and honorary orcs — turned their eyes toward the now-emptied Woodland Manse — Grannoc having marched its anchorites out to die at the Lodge — the Boots of Elvenkind waiting within, and the costly question of whether the long-dead Don Jon Raskin could ever be brought back.

## NPCs
### Gorthok
A massive, monstrous boar-like creature associated with Talos. It possesses lightning-infused tusks and a lightning bolt-like breath weapon. It resisted the party's initial charm and proved a devastating combatant, nearly felling Soma. It survived a killing blow through a supernatural revivification ability before ultimately being slain by the lingering moonbeam.  
- Kostadis Roussos: “The bull… the boar does not cast a spell. It's like… it's like a lightning bolt from a dragon.” — Kostadis Roussos
