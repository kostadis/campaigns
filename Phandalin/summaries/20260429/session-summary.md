# Chapter 39 The Charge of the Light Brigade

Date: May 3rd, 2026

## Summary
In the aftermath of the tense and deeply personal confrontation at the Druid Council, Vukradin returned to Phandalin with something weighing on his mind. Adabra had made a great show before the Council of how Vukradin had stiffed her of five gold pieces, using it as ammunition in her vicious attack on his character. Unwilling to let the matter rest, Vukradin carefully counted out six gold pieces — the original five she had claimed, plus one more for interest — sealed them in an envelope addressed to Adabra, and sent the whole thing off through the local postal system. The gold, naturally, was organic fair trade gold. He removed the six pieces from the party's tracked inventory himself. As he explained it: now that she had lost, she could have her money back.

Back at the Stonehill Tavern, Lyra was practically glowing with admiration. She had watched Vukradin walk out of the Druid Council at the most dramatically perfect moment, and she could not stop talking about it. She declared that he had focused all of Adabra's rage on himself and then walked out, rendering her arguments pointless and meaningless — that she had never seen such brilliant oratory in her entire life, and that it had been done on a matter of principle. She called for Spider-Man to bring Vukradin at least two beers, and the rest of the party was not forgotten either. Brewbarry received two beers of his own. Soma, true to form, asked only for tea. Valphine looked aloof, as ever.

Vukradin took the stage and performed for the tavern. The performance was decent — a 13 on the dice, improved slightly with assistance — well received by the patrons, and Lyra was pleased, though she noted that the other druids of the Conclave had been very disappointed that the party had left early. Vukradin accepted the mild critique and moved on.

The party also found time to report to Ser Kaelen, informing him of Sister Kaella's escape and the tragic death of Corbin. The mood was somber on that front, and Soma in particular carried the weight of it. The group agreed that tracking down Sister Kaella was a task better suited to organizations with broader reach, and the Lord's Alliance was suggested as a candidate for the job. That suggestion, however, opened the door for Jenna Roscoe, a composed and unflappable representative of the Alliance, to make her pitch. She spoke at length about the power and prestige the organization could bring to someone like Vukradin, and even dangled the possibility of helping his musical career and his studio. When Vukradin deflected by suggesting she might also consider donating to Valphine's church, Jenna paused — visibly unsettled at the idea of donating to what Vukradin cheerfully acknowledged was, in effect, a torture cult — before recovering her composure. When she turned her attention to Brewbarry and began musing about the civilizing potential of an Uthgardian barbarian joining the Lord's Alliance, Brewbarry's response was immediate, decisive, and unmistakably clear: a long, resonant burp. Jenna Roscoe got the point and walked away.

With the evening's business concluded, the party turned their attention to the road ahead. Three orc-related threats loomed over the region — the Woodland Manse, Falcon's Hunting Lodge, and the Circle of Thunder — but it was the Circle of Thunder, the lair of the half-orc spellcasters devoted to Talos, that drew the most concern, in part because the party had already been badly battered by Talosian spellcasting orcs once before. The Shrine of Savras also remained uncleared. Vukradin reached out across the distance to Xanth using the Sending spell — a choice that prompted Lyra to regard him with something between amusement and horror, wondering aloud whether he was simply going to send messages to everybody all day — arranging a meeting at the junction of the Triboar Trail. The party gathered their supplies: three healing potions between them, one greater, one superior, and one of uncertain and unrepeatable contents that Vukradin firmly declined to inquire further about. They set off into the wilderness with Vukradin's horse Boney trotting faithfully alongside, represented in the party as a level-six sidekick.

They met Xanth at the agreed-upon spot, and together the group hiked thirty miles into the forest before making camp for the night. Xanth, who was extremely pleased with the party's recent performance — singling out Soma for particular praise — led them to a safe campsite. Around the fire, he shared what he knew of the forest's recent troubles. The white dragon's disruption had driven something darker into the woods — orcs unlike any he had seen before, more dangerous and more purposeful. He explained that these were followers of Talos, the deity of storms and destruction, a god found not in any one place but in the breast of all those who seek the power to destroy. The cult itself, Xanth observed, was historically small and outlawed by every civil society — not a stable force, more like a disease that explodes, destroys everything around it, and then turns on itself. The most likely explanation for its sudden growth was a new chosen: some cleric who had been blessed with divine power and filled with fantasies of ruin. Where that chosen was, Xanth did not know. Not in this forest, at least — and if he were, Xanth noted with dry black humor, he would be taking the party directly there. They would probably die, but at least they would learn something.

The next morning, the journey continued, and the party stumbled upon a heated argument between a group of young, inexperienced druids — clearly first-level, clearly out of their depth — and a band of wildcat loggers working for Tibor Wester, brother of Phandalin's own Harbin Wester. The druids, upon spotting Soma and her staff, immediately latched onto the party with excitement and began explaining the situation at great speed. The loggers were cutting without permits and without any regard for the forest's balance. The druids were not opposed to logging in principle, they clarified — they understood the importance of balance and competing interests — but these men were simply wildcatting. Soma's response was not to side immediately with the druids against the loggers. Instead, she turned to the druids and suggested they use this as a teaching moment: show these men the ropes, explain what proper, sustainable logging looks like. That creative approach earned a persuasion roll, and Vukradin stepped in to help with a natural 20 and a result of 27. The speech that followed — alive with wit and warmth, quips and genuine moral conviction — covered the sanctity of nature, the importance of the balance, sustainable forestry, fair trade, and the dignity of honest work. By the time he was finished, the loggers had put down their axes, were apparently weeping, and had agreed to report Tibor Wester's illegal operation to the city of Neverwinter. Brewbarry, for his part, was also moved to tears, thinking guiltily of all the trees he had personally pulled out of the ground with his bare hands.

It was not long before they came upon something far more unsettling. In the middle of a wide forest clearing stood a forty-foot pine tree, charred black and stripped of its limbs long ago, its dead form rising like a needle against the sky. Tied to its base were ten ghastly dolls, each one fashioned from twigs and bound with black hair. Brewbarry studied them carefully with a religion check of 19 and delivered his verdict: an old Uthgardian tale held that these were left behind by those who wished to torment and hurt their enemies. Voodoo. No touch. Xanth confirmed the dolls were not typical of anything he recognized in Talosian practice. The party debated whether to take them down, burn them, or leave them alone, with Brewbarry arguing that whoever hung them would not appreciate the dolls being disturbed, and Vukradin pointing out, not unreasonably, that the party had come specifically to kill the people who presumably hung them. And then Vukradin, unwilling to leave evil standing, destroyed one of the dolls with a burst of radiant energy.

A still-beating pig's heart tumbled to the ground. The moment it was destroyed, the dead tree's roots erupted from the earth in a violent surge, thrashing and coiling across the sixty-foot clearing. Every character in the clearing had to make a DC-12 Dexterity saving throw. Most made it. Boney did not — the horse was caught fast, trapped by the writhing roots, taking five points of bludgeoning damage and becoming restrained. Soma also failed her saving throw and was similarly ensnared before breaking free by wild-shaping into a giant elk, whose immense strength — augmented by a d4 from Bless and Gary's assistance — was enough to tear loose. Vukradin pressed on, methodically destroying doll after doll and heart after heart, with Valphine assisting. The party confirmed the trap did not stack — each additional heart destroyed did not cause additional root eruptions — and one by one, all ten hearts were purged. The cursed tree fell still. The party took a short rest in the forest to recover, with Vukradin's bardic inspirations refreshing for the group and Soma recovering her lost hit points via hit dice.

They had not gone much farther when the ridge ahead of them came alive with movement. Across a sixty-foot gully ten feet deep, ten orcs crested the opposite ridge with battle cries that shook the trees, led by a humanoid figure with elongated claws — unmistakably a cleric of Talos. Vukradin, undeterred, called out to them using Universal Speech, weaving an impassioned account of the wonders of Lathander and the possibility of a better path. Some of the orcs hesitated — they would move forward but would not dash, their advance slowing as his words reached them. The Telosian cleric, however, was not moved to reflection. He was moved to fury.

Valphine stepped forward and raised her holy symbol high, casting Daylight upon it. A brilliant, blazing light erupted outward in a sixty-foot radius of radiant glory — the light of Lathander made manifest, flooding the gully and the ridge beyond. The Telosian cleric screamed across the divide, denouncing her as a false cleric and an abomination of Lathander, and ordered his followers to destroy the party. The orcs, whatever hesitation Vukradin's words had planted in them, now surged forward with renewed purpose. The battle for the Circle of Thunder was about to begin.

- The party reflects on the fallout from their meeting with the Druid Council and Adabra's hostile departure. The DM notes that Adabra left fuming, and that Vukradin's departure was either a strategic retreat or him being absolutely done — likely the latter.

### Lore of the Storm God
#### Xanth the Centaur shares the history and dangers of the Cult of Talos as the party travels through the forest.
- Xanth explains that the white dragon's presence disrupted the peace of the forest, caused the orcs to horde, frightened even the animals, and introduced orcs that are "fundamentally more evil" and "fundamentally more dangerous" than typical orcs.
- Vukradin asks where the Cult of Talos originates. Xanth explains that Talos is the deity of storms and destruction, and that he exists not in a specific location but "in the breast of all those who seek the power to destroy" — wherever people pursue and revel in destruction, Talos is present.
- The party presses on whether something has recently changed to cause the cult's growth. Xanth calls this a question demonstrating "awareness and wisdom far beyond your age" (Vukradin) and explains that the Cult of Talos is historically small, outlawed by all civil and polite societies, and not a stable force. It is like a disease: it explodes, destroys everything, and then turns on itself.
- Xanth suggests the most likely explanation is a new chosen: some cleric who has been blessed by Talos, given power, and filled with "fantasies of destruction." Vukradin names this a "new Chosen." Xanth confirms this is almost certainly the case, though he does not know who it is.
- Xanth notes that if the chosen were in this forest, he would know — and he would have taken the party directly there. "You would probably die," he concedes, "but at least we'd learn something." He clarifies it was a joke.
- The party proceeds the next morning.

### Daylight
Cast by Valphine on her holy symbol during the ridge encounter, conjuring a 60-foot radius of brilliant magical light to display the glory of Lathander — and to directly challenge the Talosian cultists. The light prompts the Talosian cleric to scream denunciations and order his followers to attack, effectively ending any prospect of a peaceful conversion and beginning the battle in earnest.

## NPCs
### Adabra
A woman who attacked Vukradin's character and honor during a trial before the Druid Council, making a great show of claiming he owed her five gold pieces. Vukradin sends her six gold pieces via mail — five for the debt, plus one for interest — in organic fair trade gold, as a petty but pointed repayment. His stated rationale: now that she has lost, she can have her money back. The DM notes that Adabra left the Druid Council fuming, and that her departure reads as genuine rage rather than strategy.

### Lyra
A woman who accompanied the party from the Druid Council and has developed a pronounced crush on Vukradin, deeply admiring his principles and his refusal to let his honor be sullied. At the Stonehill Tavern, she delivers an effusive and detailed account of why Vukradin's walkout was a tactical and rhetorical masterpiece, credits it with enabling Soma to step in and win, declares she has never seen such brilliant oratory in her life, and calls for Spider-Man to bring Vukradin two beers at minimum. She is visibly pleased by the party's performance, though she notes the other Conclave druids were disappointed the party left early. She reacts with mild horror at the discovery that Vukradin can cast Sending freely.

### Ser Kaelen
A contact in Phandalin who is informed about Sister Kaella's crimes and the death of Corbin. He meets the party at the Stonehill Tavern and notices immediately that Soma has kept her staff. The party suggests he work with the Lord's Alliance to track down Sister Kaella; he notes that broader organizations may be better positioned for that task.

### Jenna Roscoe
A composed and unflappable representative of the Lord's Alliance who attempts to recruit Vukradin and Brewbarry at the Stonehill Tavern. She offers the organization's resources to help find Sister Kaella, hints at support for Vukradin's musical studio, and makes an argument about the power and prestige the Alliance could bring to someone like him. She is visibly unsettled — just briefly — when Vukradin deflects by suggesting she donate to Valphine's church. She then turns to Brewbarry and begins a speech about "an Uthgardian barbarian who joined the Lord's Alliance and accepted the civilizing…" at which point Brewbarry burps loudly. Jenna Roscoe gets the point and walks away. The DM notes that she is "one of those people who never loses her cool" — except, apparently, at the prospect of donating to a torture cult.

### Sister Kaella
An agent who murdered Corbin and has since fled, becoming a target for the party's vengeance. The party informs Ser Kaelen of her crimes and discusses having her tracked down, with the Lord's Alliance identified as a possible resource. The DM confirms she has gone "to the wind." Soma in particular carries the emotional weight of Corbin's death.

### Xanth
A centaur ally who guides the party through the forest toward the Circle of Thunder. At the agreed meeting point on the Triboar Trail, he greets the party warmly and praises Soma specifically for her recent performance. He leads them to a safe campsite and spends the evening sharing tales and lore, including a detailed account of Talos, the cult's historical instability, and his belief that a new chosen champion is driving the current surge of cultist activity. He narrowly avoids being ensnared by the animated roots of the cursed tree (making his DC-12 save with a +2 modifier). During the ridge encounter, he fights alongside the party.

### Boney
Vukradin's loyal horse and sidekick, tracked as a level-six human warrior sidekick in the party roster. Boney travels with the party into the wilderness and becomes the primary casualty of the cursed clearing trap — failing his Dexterity saving throw (rolling a 9 with a +1 modifier), taking 5 bludgeoning damage, and becoming restrained by the animated roots. He is eventually freed as the party destroys the remaining dolls and hearts and Soma moves to assist. The DM asks Wade to manage Boney's combat actions going forward.

### Tibor Wester
The brother of Harbin Wester, mentioned as the individual running an illegal wildcat logging camp in the forest. His loggers are confronted by the party in the encounter with the novice druids. Following Vukradin's speech (a natural 20, total 27), his former employees agree to put down their axes and report his illegal operation to the city of Neverwinter.

### Telosian Cleric
A humanoid leader of the orc warband with elongated claws who serves Talos. He is enraged — not merely unpersuaded — by Vukradin's proselytizing about Lathander, which the DM confirms actively inflames him further. Upon Valphine's casting of Daylight, he screams across the gully: "Kill the false cleric of Lathander! Kill the drow cleric of Lathander, the abomination! Destroy! Destroy the false god!" He then orders his followers to charge. The party's stated plan is to focus their attacks on him specifically while continuing to attempt conversions of the regular orcs.
