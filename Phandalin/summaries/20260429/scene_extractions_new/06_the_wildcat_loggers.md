# The Wildcat Loggers

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party encounters a dispute between novice druids and illegal loggers in the woods.
- The party finds young, clearly first-level druids arguing with loggers. Upon spotting Soma and her staff, the druids become immediately excited and begin explaining the situation at high speed.
- The druids explain that Tibor Wester — Harbin Wester's brother — is running an illegal wildcat logging camp. The druids are not opposed to logging in principle (they understand the balance of nature and the need for sustainable practice) but object specifically to these loggers operating without permits and without regard for the forest.
- Soma suggests, creatively, that the druids should use this as a teaching moment and show the loggers how to log properly rather than simply confronting them. This earns a persuasion roll.
- Vukradin assists (Soma asks if he can help; Vukradin asks "what am I saying?" to confirm the approach). The roll is a natural 20, total 27.
- The DM describes the resulting speech as an "epic monologue" delivered not in a "tedious, professorial tone" but with "quips and retinue" — covering the sanctity of nature, the importance of balance, sustainable foresting, fair trade, and honest labor.
- The loggers put down their axes, are moved to tears, apologize, and agree to report Tibor Wester's illegal logging operation to the city of Neverwinter.
- Brewbarry is also moved to tears, feeling guilty about all the trees he has pulled out of the ground with his bare hands.
- The DM notes that Brewbarry's chest-thumping also contributed to the atmosphere of the scene.
