# Return to Phandalin

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### The party returns to Phandalin after their encounter with the Druid Council, settling into the Stonehill Tavern to discuss their next moves.
- The party meets Ser Kaelen at the Stonehill Tavern and informs him of Sister Kaella's escape and the death of Corbin. Ser Kaelen notices immediately that Soma has retained her staff.
- Lyra celebrates Vukradin's 'heroism' at the Druid Council in effusive and detailed terms, attributing his dramatic walkout to brilliant tactical thinking and unimpeachable principle. She calls for Spider-Man (the tavern keep) to bring Vukradin at least two beers; Brewbarry also receives two beers. Soma asks only for tea.
- Valphine observes with characteristic aloofness that the druids were disappointed they did not get to hear great music, prompting Vukradin to perform.
- Vukradin and Valphine perform a musical show at the tavern. The performance rolls a 13, improved with assistance, and is well received — decent but not spectacular. Vukradin declines to spend Bardic Inspiration on a tavern performance.
- The party discusses their various quest options: the Woodland Manse (burned and occupied by orcs), Falcon's Hunting Lodge (overrun), the Circle of Thunder (lair of half-orc Talosian spellcasters, with Xanth offering to guide them), the Shrine of Savras (still uncleared, the dragon now dead), and the Bob Saget the Musical quest (a callback to the previous campaign involving an actual demon of dad jokes who nearly caused a zombie apocalypse in Silverymoon before being sent to the Astral Plane).
- Jenna Roscoe of the Lord's Alliance attempts to recruit Vukradin, offering the organization's resources to help find Sister Kaella and hinting at support for his musical studio. Vukradin deflects by suggesting she might donate to Valphine's church; Jenna is visibly unsettled. The DM describes Valphine's church as "a torture cult." Vukradin and the players agree this is basically accurate — "more like a masochism cult."
- Jenna pivots to recruiting Brewbarry, suggesting an Uthgardian barbarian who joined the Lord's Alliance and accepted the "civilizing…" — at which point Brewbarry delivers a loud burp. Jenna gets the point and walks away.
