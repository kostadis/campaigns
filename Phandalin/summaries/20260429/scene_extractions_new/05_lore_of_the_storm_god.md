# Lore of the Storm God

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Xanth the Centaur shares the history and dangers of the Cult of Talos as the party travels through the forest.
- Xanth explains that the white dragon's presence disrupted the peace of the forest, caused the orcs to horde, frightened even the animals, and introduced orcs that are "fundamentally more evil" and "fundamentally more dangerous" than typical orcs.
- Vukradin asks where the Cult of Talos originates. Xanth explains that Talos is the deity of storms and destruction, and that he exists not in a specific location but "in the breast of all those who seek the power to destroy" — wherever people pursue and revel in destruction, Talos is present.
- The party presses on whether something has recently changed to cause the cult's growth. Xanth calls this a question demonstrating "awareness and wisdom far beyond your age" (Vukradin) and explains that the Cult of Talos is historically small, outlawed by all civil and polite societies, and not a stable force. It is like a disease: it explodes, destroys everything, and then turns on itself.
- Xanth suggests the most likely explanation is a new chosen: some cleric who has been blessed by Talos, given power, and filled with "fantasies of destruction." Vukradin names this a "new Chosen." Xanth confirms this is almost certainly the case, though he does not know who it is.
- Xanth notes that if the chosen were in this forest, he would know — and he would have taken the party directly there. "You would probably die," he concedes, "but at least we'd learn something." He clarifies it was a joke.
- The party proceeds the next morning.

## Verbatim moments

**[The Party Travels with Xanth — Evening Camp]**
- The party has met up with Xanth and is travelling through the forest; they camp when they reach the 30-mile mark of the day's hike.
- Xanth confirms it is another full day's hike to the lair of the half-orc spellcasters.
- Vukradin invites Xanth to share stories around camp.

**[Vukradin]** — *inviting Xanth to speak*
> "Zanz, how much further to the lair of the half-orc spellcasters?"

**[GM]** — *as Xanth*
> "Another day's hike."

**[Vukradin]** — *prompting campfire stories*
> "Got any good stories to tell us tonight?"

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, beginning to hold forth*
> "Stories, my friend, I have stories to tell you."

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, narrating the forest's decline*
> "he does observe that this was a much safer place before that white dragon, disrupted the piece."
> "And caused all the orcs to hoard now. Now even the animals are afraid, and…"
> "That these new orcs aren't even normal orcs, they're… there's something fundamentally more evil about them, something fundamentally more dangerous than the typical orcs."

**[Vukradin]** — *pressing for the cause*
> "How could they become more dangerous? What happened?"

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, naming the cult*
> "He pauses and goes, why, they have become members of the cult of Talos."

**[Vukradin]** — *asking about the cult's origins*
> "cult of Talos originate from?"

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, defining Talos*
> "He goes, the story of Talos. Talos is the deity of storms and destruction."
> "Wherever there are those who pursue destruction."
> "wherever, and who wish to spread destruction, Talos is there."
> "It is not… he is not… he is not in some specific place. He is in the breast of all those who seek the power to destroy, and hear his message of destruction, and revel in that destruction."

**[Vukradin]** — *pressing on recent change*
> "What's changed recently? I mean, I'm sure Talos has been around for a long time, he's been corrupting people for centuries. Like, has something happened that he's caused him to gain lots of power recently?"

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, complimenting Vukradin before answering*
> "He pauses and goes, that is a very good question."
> "Demonstrating an awareness and wisdom far beyond your age."

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, describing the cult's historical character*
> "the cult of Talos was a small cult, it's not a very…"
> "very popular thing. It doesn't have very big structures or nuts."
> "They're mostly outlawed by all those societies."
> "That are civil and polite."
> "The cult of Talos is like a disease. It explodes, it destroys everything, and then it destroys upon themselves. They are not a very… how should I say, stable god force."

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, adding an aside about Adabra's framing*
> "Adabra would say that they represent the necessary element of distraction in the cosmic balance."
> "I would say nonsense. He's a lunatic of a god who wants to destroy things left, right, and center, and finds other stupid people who want to destroy things."
> "And he empowers them, and they have these fantasies of destruction, and he gives them power to implement these fantasies of destruction."

**[Vukradin]** — *naming the likely cause*
> "So he has a new Chosen."

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, confirming*
> "Yes, most likely. There is always a chosen. There's always some cleric who somehow became convinced by Talos that he has some divine message and inspiration."

**[Vukradin]** — *pressing for more*
> "Find out."

**[GM]** — *as Xanth, acknowledging ignorance and making a dark joke*
> "He's not in this forest, that I do know. If he was in this forest, I would know who he was, and I would be taking you there. You'd probably die, but at least we'd learn something about him."
> "That was a joke."
> "I hope you appreciate the talk."

**[Vukradin]** — *responding*
> "I appreciate it."

**[GM]** — *as Xanth*
> "Excellent."
