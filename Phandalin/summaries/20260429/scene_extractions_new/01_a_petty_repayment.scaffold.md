[Scene 1] A Petty Repayment
Narrator: Valphine
Focus: Valphine's drow political instincts and transactional morality make her the sharpest observer of who is owed what, and why it matters.

- The party reflects on the fallout from their meeting with the Druid Council and Adabra's hostile departure. The DM notes that Adabra left fuming, and that Vukradin's departure was either a strategic retreat or him being absolutely done — likely the latter.

- Vukradin, feeling betrayed by Adabra's personal attacks during the trial, decides to return the five gold pieces she claimed he owed her. He clarifies that while he knew Adabra would be hostile, he had not anticipated how *personal* she would make it.

- Vukradin prepares an envelope containing six gold pieces — the original five plus one gold for interest — specifically using 'organic fair trade gold.' He removes the six pieces from the party's tracked inventory himself.

- The payment is addressed to Adabra and sent via the local postal system — the same postal infrastructure Vukradin has used previously via Sending — to ensure she can no longer claim he is stiffing her of money.

- Vukradin's stated rationale: "Now that she's lost, she can have her money back."

- The DM declares this petty, funny, and awesome, in that order.
