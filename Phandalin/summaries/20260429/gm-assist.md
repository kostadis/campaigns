# Chapter 39 The Charge of the Light Brigade

Date: May 3rd, 2026

## Summary
In the aftermath of the tense and deeply personal confrontation at the Druid Council, Vukradin returned to Phandalin with something weighing on his mind. Adabra had made a great show before the Council of how Vukradin had stiffed her of five gold pieces, using it as ammunition in her vicious attack on his character. Unwilling to let the matter rest, Vukradin carefully counted out six gold pieces — the original five she had claimed, plus one more for interest — sealed them in an envelope addressed to Adabra, and sent the whole thing off through the local postal system. The gold, naturally, was organic fair trade gold.

Back at the Stonehill Tavern, Lyra was practically glowing with admiration. She had watched Vukradin walk out of the Druid Council at the most dramatically perfect moment, and she could not stop talking about it. "You warmed up the crowd," she told him breathlessly. "You turned everybody, you focused all of Adabra's rage on yourself and then walked out, rendered her arguments pointless and meaningless." She called for Spider-Man to bring Vukradin at least two beers, and the rest of the party was not forgotten either. Brewbarry, for his part, accepted his drinks with the quiet dignity of a man who had earned them.

The party also found time to report to Ser Kaelen, informing him of Sister Kaella's escape and the tragic death of Corbin. The mood was somber on that front, and Soma in particular carried the weight of it. The group agreed that tracking down Sister Kaella was a task better suited to organizations with broader reach, and the Lord's Alliance was suggested as a candidate for the job. That suggestion, however, opened the door for Jenna Roscoe, a composed and unflappable representative of the Alliance, to make her pitch. She spoke at length about the power and prestige the organization could bring to someone like Vukradin, and even dangled the possibility of helping his musical career. When she turned her attention to Brewbarry and began musing about the civilizing potential of an Uthgardian barbarian joining the Lord's Alliance, Brewbarry's response was immediate, decisive, and unmistakably clear: a long, resonant burp. Jenna Roscoe got the point and walked away.

With the evening's business concluded, the party turned their attention to the road ahead. Three orc-related threats loomed over the region, but it was the Circle of Thunder — the lair of the half-orc spellcasters devoted to the storm god Talos — that drew the most concern. Vukradin reached out across the distance to Xanth, a centaur ally, using magic to send a message arranging a meeting at the junction of the Triboar Trail. The party gathered their supplies, noted they had a handful of healing potions between them, and set off into the wilderness with Vukradin's horse Boney trotting faithfully alongside.

They met Xanth at the agreed-upon spot, and together the group hiked thirty miles into the forest before making camp for the night. Around the fire, Xanth shared what he knew of the forest's recent troubles. The white dragon's disruption had driven something darker into the woods — orcs unlike any he had seen before, more dangerous and more purposeful. He explained that these were followers of Talos, the deity of storms and destruction, and that the cult had likely found a new chosen champion, some cleric who had been blessed with divine power and filled with fantasies of ruin. Talos, Xanth noted with dry certainty, was not a god who inspired stable institutions.

The next morning, the journey continued, and the party stumbled upon a heated argument between a group of young, inexperienced druids and a band of wildcat loggers working for Tibor Wester, the brother of Phandalin's own Harbin Wester. The druids were not opposed to logging in principle, they explained, but these men were cutting without permits and without any regard for the forest's balance. Vukradin stepped forward and delivered a speech — not a lecture, not a sermon, but something alive with wit and warmth — about the sanctity of nature, the importance of sustainable forestry, and the dignity of honest, fair trade labor. By the time he was finished, the loggers had put down their axes, wiped tears from their eyes, and agreed to report Tibor Wester's illegal operation to the city of Neverwinter. The druids were stunned. The party moved on.

It was not long before they came upon something far more unsettling. In the middle of a wide forest clearing stood a forty-foot pine tree, charred black and stripped of its limbs long ago, its dead form rising like a needle against the sky. Tied to its base were ten ghastly dolls, each one fashioned from twigs and bound with black hair. Brewbarry studied them with the grim recognition of old Uthgardian tales and delivered his verdict with characteristic economy: voodoo, no touch. The party debated. Xanth confirmed the dolls were not typical of anything he recognized. And then Vukradin, unwilling to leave evil standing, destroyed one of the dolls with a burst of radiant energy.

A still-beating pig's heart tumbled to the ground. The moment it was destroyed, the dead tree's roots erupted from the earth in a violent surge, thrashing and coiling across the clearing. Boney was caught fast, trapped by the writhing roots, and the rest of the party scrambled to avoid the same fate. Soma moved quickly to free the horse while Vukradin pressed on, methodically destroying doll after doll and heart after heart. Valphine called upon her druidic power and transformed into a massive giant elk, using the creature's immense strength to tear herself free. One by one, the hearts were destroyed, and at last the roots fell still. The cursed tree was purged. The party took a moment to rest and recover before pressing on toward the Circle of Thunder.

They had not gone much farther when the ridge ahead of them came alive with movement. Across a sixty-foot gully, ten orcs crested the opposite ridge with battle cries that shook the trees, led by a humanoid figure with elongated claws — unmistakably a cleric of Talos. Vukradin, undeterred, called out to them using a magical gift of universal speech, weaving an impassioned account of the wonders of Lathander and the possibility of a better path. Some of the orcs hesitated, their advance slowing as his words reached them. The Telosian cleric, however, was not moved to reflection. He was moved to fury.

Valphine stepped forward and raised her holy symbol high. A brilliant, blazing light erupted from it, flooding the gully and the ridge beyond with a sixty-foot radius of radiant glory — the light of Lathander made manifest. The Telosian cleric screamed across the gully, denouncing her as a false cleric and an abomination, and ordered his followers to destroy the party. The orcs, whatever hesitation Vukradin's words had planted in them, now surged forward with renewed purpose. The battle for the Circle of Thunder was about to begin.
