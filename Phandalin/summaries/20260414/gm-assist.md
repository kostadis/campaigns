# Chapter 37 A Gem of a Problem, A Rat of a Solution

Date: Apr 18th, 2026

## Summary
In the aftermath of their hard-won victory over the young white dragon at Icespire Hold, the party took time to reflect on their growing power and the new abilities they had earned through their trials. Valphine, the cleric of Lathander, discovered a deepened connection to her deity that allowed him to forge a mystical bond among allies, bolstering their resolve in the heat of battle. She also contemplated the power of a protective aura capable of tremendous healing, ultimately choosing it as a cornerstone of his growing arsenal. The group discussed how a sixth-level gift of the bond could allow a protector to teleport to an ally's side and absorb a blow meant for them — a power that would prove invaluable in the battles to come.

Before leaving Icespire Hold, Soma turned his considerable skill to the grim but rewarding task of harvesting the slain dragon's remains. With extraordinary precision, she extracted a basket-sized pouch from deep within the dragon's throat — a reservoir of magical frost that could be unleashed with devastating effect. She also carefully detached supple, interlocking scales from the dragon's hide, plates that a skilled blacksmith could one day fashion into formidable armor resistant to cold and attuned to the presence of white dragons. The party noted that the breath pouch alone was worth a small fortune, and the scales even more so if properly crafted, though the cost of such work would require considerable resources they did not yet possess.

With their prizes secured, the party made their way toward Phandalin, stopping first at the Mountain Toes Gold Mine. They were greeted by the nervous were-rat leader Zeleen Varnaster and his Whiskered Gang, who assured the party that everything was in order — yet the mine was utterly silent. Investigation revealed that the dwarven miners had gone on strike, led by the superstitious foreman Dwolin the Grout, who refused to dig a single inch until a specific Earth Elemental gem was found to bless the gold vein, believing that gold was shy and must be treated with reverence. Valphine, unimpressed, flooded the mine with a blinding burst of holy light before delivering a devastatingly flat interrogation that left Zeleen trembling. After being told in no uncertain terms that he was a waste of cheese, Zeleen hastily dispatched several of his were-rats into the orc-controlled forest to retrieve the gem.

While Vukradin remained occupied elsewhere, Brewbarry and Valphine slipped away to negotiate a secret contingency with the pragmatic dwarven leader Horia. She proposed that fifty gold pieces from the mine's own coffers could secure a convincing fake gem to trick Dwolin into resuming work, should the were-rats fail in their errand. Brewbarry, in a moment of inspired diplomacy, pressed his thumb into the agreement as a binding signature, and the two parties shook on a discreet addendum to the existing contract — one that Horia made very clear should never reach Vukradin's ears.

The party arrived in Phandalin to a hero's welcome, with townspeople spilling out of their homes to cheer and children rushing forward to greet them. The townmaster, Harbin Wester, seized the moment to deliver a self-congratulatory speech crediting his own leadership for assembling such a capable group of adventurers, before organizing a festival on the town green complete with food, music, and libations. The innkeeper brought out a special reserve for Brewbarry, insisting it was the very good stuff, and the barbarian reserved judgment until he had tasted it himself.

The celebration was tempered by an urgent summons from Ser Kaelen, who drew the party aside and led them to the Temple of Lathander for a private council. He revealed that Adabra, the absolutist naturalist who had long been a thorn in the party's side, had uncovered the truth about what had transpired at the Whispering Grove — including Vukradin's deception — and was now rallying local druids to her cause. She intended to argue before a conclave that the party's interventionism had brought ruin to the forests, framing them as the source of the land's suffering rather than the planar incursions they had fought so hard to contain. Ser Kaelen explained that Adabra was not evil, merely misguided — she had witnessed the devastation of war without ever experiencing the otherworldly forces that had ignited it, and her passion had drawn enough followers to pose a genuine threat.

Ser Kaelen warned that both the Lords Alliance and the Order of the Gauntlet were stretched too thin to intervene, leaving the burden squarely on the party's shoulders. He told Soma that her staff — a relic of deep symbolism among the druids of the region — might be the key to swaying the conclave, though he cautioned that some among the assembled druids might seek to strip Soma of it entirely. Soma's response was quiet but resolute: no order would take what was hers. With the festival still ringing outside and the weight of a new conflict settling over them, the party steeled themselves for the diplomatic battle that lay ahead.

## Locations
### Icespire Hold
The site of the young white dragon's defeat, where the party harvested scales and a magical breath pouch from the dragon's carcass.

### Mountain Toes Gold Mine
A gold mine owned by the party, guarded by the Whiskered Gang of were-rats and worked by dwarven miners. During this session, the mine was found silent due to a dwarven strike, and the party negotiated to resolve the situation.

### Phandalin
A town where the party is celebrated as heroes upon their return with proof of the dragon's defeat. A festival was held in their honor on the town green, and Ser Kaelen held an urgent private meeting with the party at the local temple.

### Temple of Lathander
Also referred to as the Temple of the Searing Pain of Justice by Valphine's followers, this temple in Phandalin served as the private meeting location where Ser Kaelen briefed the party on the Emerald Enclave threat.

## Items
### Dragon Breath Pouch
A basket-sized organ harvested from the back of the young white dragon's throat, containing magical liquids that can produce a destructive frost breath effect. It can be used as a powerful one-time weapon or crafted into a wondrous item called Dragon's Wrath for repeated use, at significant cost.

### Young White Dragon Scales
Supple, flexible plates harvested from the young white dragon's hide. They can be fashioned by a blacksmith into rare dragon scale mail that provides resistance to cold damage, advantage on saving throws against dragon breath weapons, and the ability to sense white dragons within 30 miles.

### Earth Elemental Gem
A specific magical gem required by the dwarven foreman Dwolin to 'bless' the gold vein before mining can resume. The were-rats have been sent to find a real one in orc-controlled forest, while a fake version is being arranged as a backup.

### Soma's Staff
A magical staff of great symbolism and power among the local druids. Ser Kaelen warns that it may be a point of contention at the upcoming druid conclave, with some druids potentially challenging Soma's right to keep it.

### Brewbarry's Halberd
A large, fine polearm carried by Brewbarry, admired by Ser Kaelen and described as a 'big stabby pokey thing.' Brewbarry is seen tapping it meaningfully during the confrontation at the mine.

## Spells
### Daylight
Cast by Valphine inside the Mountain Toes Gold Mine, creating a sudden blinding burst of sunlight that caused everyone's eyes to hurt and effectively intimidated the were-rat guards, asserting the party's dominance without a word.

### Spirit Guardians
Discussed by the party as a powerful combat spell with high utility when facing multiple enemies, though noted to be less useful against a flying dragon.

### Revivify
Briefly discussed by the party as a vital utility spell worth selecting, with no one able to turn it down.
