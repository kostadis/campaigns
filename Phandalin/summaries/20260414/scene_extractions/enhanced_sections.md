## Scenes
### Leveling Up and Strategic Planning
#### The party gathers to discuss their growth in power and analyze new spells and abilities following their recent victory.
- The party members, except for Vukradin, gain a level in experience.
- Valphine explores new clerical abilities, including a Peace Domain feature that allows for a mystical bond between allies to bolster their resolve with a bonus die.
- The group discusses the tactical advantages of a sixth-level ability that allows a protector to teleport next to an ally and intercept damage meant for them.
- Soma considers the utility of abjuration magic for creating safe zones or imprisoning extra-planar aberrations, noting its one-minute casting time makes it unsuitable for direct combat.
- The party debates the merits of various spells, comparing the utility of Spirit Guardians against a potent healing aura, and Soma ultimately selects a powerful healing aura spell.

### Harvesting the Dragon
#### Following the dragon's defeat at Icespire Hold, the party harvests valuable components from the young white dragon's remains.
- The party discusses harvesting body parts from the slain young white dragon.
- Soma performs a nature check with exceptional results, rolling a double natural 20, to harvest the dragon's remains.
- The party successfully extracts a dragon breath pouch from the back of the dragon's throat, containing magical liquids capable of producing a destructive frost breath effect.
- Valuable supple scales are harvested from the dragon's hide, suitable for crafting into dragon scale mail.
- The party discusses the potential for crafting these items — the breath pouch into a Dragon's Wrath wondrous item and the scales into rare dragon scale mail — and notes the significant gold cost involved.
- The group decides to head toward Phandalin, planning a stop at the Mountain Toes Gold Mine along the way.

### Inspection and Strike at Mountain Toes Gold Mine
#### The party arrives at their gold mine to find the were-rat guards nervous and the dwarven miners on strike.
- The party arrives at the Mountain Toes Gold Mine and is greeted by the Whiskered Gang of were-rats, led by Zeleen Varnaster, who appear nervous.
- The party notices a complete absence of mining sounds and investigates, with one character rolling a high insight check and another rolling a natural one.
- The party discovers the dwarves have gone on strike, led by foreman Dwolin the Grout, who believes the gold vein is 'shy' and cannot be mined until a specific Earth Elemental gem is found to bless the operation.
- Valphine casts Daylight to create a sudden blinding burst of sunlight, intimidating the were-rat guards and asserting the party's dominance.
- Brewbarry and Valphine confront Zeleen Varnaster about the mine's lack of productivity, with Valphine delivering a flat, deadpan interrogation that causes him to panic.
- Under the threat of being called a 'waste of cheese,' Zeleen agrees to send were-rats into the orc-controlled forest to retrieve the required Earth Elemental gem.
- The party negotiates a secret 'Plan B' with the dwarf leader Horia, involving 50 gold pieces to buy a fake gem to trick Dwolin into resuming work if the were-rats fail.
- Brewbarry signs a secret contractual addendum with a thumbprint to authorize the use of mine funds for the scheme, keeping it hidden from Vukradin.

### A Hero's Welcome in Phandalin
#### The party returns to Phandalin as dragon-slayers and is greeted with a celebration organized by Harbin Wester.
- The townspeople cheer for the party as they arrive, with children high-fiving the heroes.
- The party brings dragon parts as evidence of their victory.
- Harbin Wester publicly praises Vukradin and the party for abating the dragon threat, taking some credit for himself in the process.
- A festival is organized on the town green with libations provided, including a special reserve from the innkeeper for Brewbarry.
- Ser Kaelen approaches the party to discuss urgent matters regarding the Emerald Enclave, requesting a private meeting.

### Urgent Council at the Temple of Lathander
#### Ser Kaelen briefs the party privately at the Temple of Lathander on Adabra's political moves and the upcoming druid conclave.
- Ser Kaelen reveals that Adabra has discovered the truth about what really happened at the Whispering Grove, including Vukradin's deception.
- Adabra is calling a conclave of local druids to argue that the party's reckless interventionism has brought harm to the forests.
- Ser Kaelen warns that the Lords Alliance and the Order of the Gauntlet are stretched too thin to intervene directly.
- Ser Kaelen suggests that Soma's magical staff holds great symbolism and power among the druids and could tip the balance at the conclave.
- Ser Kaelen warns Soma that some druids may attempt to challenge her right to keep the staff.
- The party discusses Adabra's misguided nature — she experienced the consequences of war but not the planar incursions that caused it — and the potential loss of the druids as allies against planar threats.
- Ser Kaelen admires Brewbarry's halberd as the party prepares for the upcoming diplomatic challenge.

## Locations
### Icespire Hold
The site of the young white dragon's defeat, where the party harvested scales and a magical breath pouch from the dragon's carcass.

### Mountain Toes Gold Mine
A gold mine owned by the party, guarded by the Whiskered Gang of were-rats and worked by dwarven miners. During this session, the mine was found silent due to a dwarven strike, and the party negotiated to resolve the situation.

### Phandalin
A town where the party is celebrated as heroes upon their return with proof of the dragon's defeat. A festival was held in their honor on the town green, and Ser Kaelen held an urgent private meeting with the party at the local temple.

### Temple of Lathander
Also referred to as the Temple of the Searing Pain of Justice by Valphine's followers, this temple in Phandalin served as the private meeting location where Ser Kaelen briefed the party on the Emerald Enclave threat.

## Items
### Dragon Breath Pouch
A basket-sized organ harvested from the back of the young white dragon's throat, containing magical liquids that can produce a destructive frost breath effect. It can be used as a powerful one-time weapon or crafted into a wondrous item called Dragon's Wrath for repeated use, at significant cost.

### Young White Dragon Scales
Supple, flexible plates harvested from the young white dragon's hide. They can be fashioned by a blacksmith into rare dragon scale mail that provides resistance to cold damage, advantage on saving throws against dragon breath weapons, and the ability to sense white dragons within 30 miles.

### Earth Elemental Gem
A specific magical gem required by the dwarven foreman Dwolin to 'bless' the gold vein before mining can resume. The were-rats have been sent to find a real one in orc-controlled forest, while a fake version is being arranged as a backup.

### Soma's Staff
A magical staff of great symbolism and power among the local druids. Ser Kaelen warns that it may be a point of contention at the upcoming druid conclave, with some druids potentially challenging Soma's right to keep it.

### Brewbarry's Halberd
A large, fine polearm carried by Brewbarry, admired by Ser Kaelen and described as a 'big stabby pokey thing.' Brewbarry is seen tapping it meaningfully during the confrontation at the mine.

## Spells
### Daylight
Cast by Valphine inside the Mountain Toes Gold Mine, creating a sudden blinding burst of sunlight that caused everyone's eyes to hurt and effectively intimidated the were-rat guards, asserting the party's dominance without a word.

### Spirit Guardians
Discussed by the party as a powerful combat spell with high utility when facing multiple enemies, though noted to be less useful against a flying dragon.

### Revivify
Briefly discussed by the party as a vital utility spell worth selecting, with no one able to turn it down.

## NPCs
### Zeleen Varnaster
The leader of the Whiskered Gang of were-rats guarding the Mountain Toes Gold Mine. He is easily intimidated by the party and, after being called a 'waste of cheese,' agrees to send his followers into orc-controlled forest to retrieve an Earth Elemental gem for the dwarves.

### Dwolin the Grout
The superstitious dwarven foreman of the Mountain Toes Gold Mine who refuses to resume mining until a specific Earth Elemental gem is found to bless the gold vein, believing gold is 'shy' and must be treated with respect.

### Horia
A pragmatic dwarven leader at the mine who vouches for Dwolin's skill as a gold-seeker while also proposing a secret Plan B: using 50 gold pieces to buy a fake gem to trick Dwolin into resuming work. She is a stickler for procedures and negotiates a secret addendum to the party's contract.

### Harbin Wester
The townmaster of Phandalin who publicly credits Vukradin and the party for defeating the dragon and abating the dragon threat, organizing a festival in their honor while taking some credit for himself.

### Ser Kaelen
A man of sword and action associated with the Order of the Gauntlet who meets privately with the party at the Temple of Lathander. He warns them that Adabra is rallying local druids against them and that Soma's staff may be key to swaying the upcoming conclave.

### Adabra
An absolutist naturalist who has discovered the truth about what happened at the Whispering Grove. She is calling a conclave of local druids to argue that the party's interventionism has harmed the forests. Ser Kaelen describes her as misguided rather than evil — she experienced the consequences of war but not the planar incursions that caused it.
