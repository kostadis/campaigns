[Scene 5] Urgent Council at the Temple of Lathander
Narrator: Soma
Focus: Soma alone hears Ser Kaelen's warning about the staff as a personal threat — her quiet defiance ("They're not taking it from me") carries the full weight of Meril's gift, her incomplete training, and whatever time she has left.

- The party head to the Valphine's Temple in Phandalin to hear Ser Kaelen's entreaties about the Emerald Enclave. 
- Valphine's acolytes are pleased to see her and share their new wounds and blisters from too much sun. 

- Ser Kaelen reveals that Adabra has discovered the truth about what really happened at the Whispering Grove, including Vukradin's deception.

- Adabra is calling a conclave of local druids to argue that the party's reckless interventionism has brought harm to the forests.

  <!-- Briefing the party on Adabra's conclave at the Temple of Lathander -->
  Ser Kaelen: "Adabra has found out about what really happened at the Whispering Grove. She has decided, she has gone with her allies and is going to attempt to convince the Druids that your reckless interventionism has brought harm to the forests."

- The party discusses Adabra's misguided nature — she experienced the consequences of war but not the planar incursions that caused it — and the potential loss of the druids as allies against planar threats.

<!-- Explaining Adabra's fundamental misreading of events -->
Ser Kaelen: "She's confused. She sees the harm that was caused by those who defended against the dragons, and thinks that the problem was that the defenders against the dragons were the problem, not the dragons. She sees the planar incursions as… not the symptoms, because she never experienced those symptoms. She experienced the consequences of war."

<!-- Responding to Ser Kaelen's summary of Adabra -->
Soma: "Yeah, she's bringing her own war out here."

- Ser Kaelen warns that the Lords Alliance and the Order of the Gauntlet are stretched too thin to intervene directly.

  <!-- Explaining why neither the Lords Alliance nor the Order can intervene -->
  Ser Kaelen: "The Lord's Alliance doesn't want to intervene, and the Order of the Gauntlet, although we would like to intervene, cannot, because, well, the War of the Dragons, and then the War of the Giants, and then the war… Let's just say there's fewer of us to go around these days."

  <!-- Reacting to being told it falls to the party -->
  Soma: "It's all on us, huh?"

- Ser Kaelen suggests that Soma's magical staff holds great symbolism and power among the druids and could tip the balance at the conclave.

  <!-- Pushing back on Ser Kaelen's suggestion that the staff gives her standing with the druids -->
  Soma: "Standing with the druids — I'm barely a druid myself!"

  <!-- Acknowledging he doesn't fully understand druidic customs -->
  Ser Kaelen: "Yes, but apparently this staff is… I don't… look, look, druids have rituals and beliefs that I, as a… as a man of sword and action, don't always understand."

- Ser Kaelen warns Soma that some druids may attempt to challenge her right to keep the staff.

  <!-- Warning Soma that some druids may challenge her right to keep the staff -->
  Ser Kaelen: "The only caution I give you, Soma, is that there are some there who have suggested that they should even evaluate whether you should retain the staff."

  <!-- Quiet defiance at the possibility of losing the staff -->
  Soma: "They're not taking it from me. This has at least sentimental value."

- Ser Kaelen reflects on what he would do if someone tried to take his sword. 

  !-- Empathizing via his own sword, patting it -->
  Ser Kaelen: "I agree with you. A stat — that no order can take my sword."

- Ser Kaelen admires Brewbarry's halberd as the party prepares for the upcoming diplomatic challenge.

<!-- Admiring Brewbarry's halberd -->
Ser Kaelen: "Is that a halberd? Because that is a very fine halberd, Brewberry. We should parry."

<!-- Warning Ser Kaelen off the idea -->
Brewbarry: "You don't want to have a conversation with it, trust me."
