# Voice Critique — Brewbarry, scene 04: Incursion into the Woodland Manse

**Narration:** session_doc_scene_04_incursion_into_the_woodland_manse.scrubbed.md
**Voice spec:** voice/brewbarry_new_pipeline.md
**Per-char examples:** examples/brewbarry.md

## Flags

### [1] Generic fantasy prose (too layered for Brewbarry)

> The stone steps climbed up to the portico, a balcony hanging over us at the height of two tall men stacked, vines strangling the pillars that held it, the railing already half gone to rubble.

**Why:** Cascading architectural clauses don't fit Brewbarry's short declarative mode. His examples run in tight noun-verb sentences: "Order of Gauntlet. I know them. They fought against my tribe. They killed many." The accumulated subordinate clauses here read as someone else's narration. "Vines strangling the pillars" is also a stock ruin image.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Stone steps up to the portico. A balcony, high as two men. Half the railing gone. Vines on the pillars."

### [2] Voice spec conflict (ornate simile)

> Warmth poured down my spine, steady, like a hand on the shoulder before the gate opens.

**Why:** "Like a hand on the shoulder before the gate opens" is a literary simile too crafted for Brewbarry. His spec says short declarative sentences; his examples render inner states bare ("My rage consumes me." / "I feel sadness." / "Drink good."). The simile reaches for emotional resonance Brewbarry would just name directly.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The song started in my spine and spread. Warmth. Ready."

### [3] Vocabulary bleed from Valphine

> the way her god always feels — bright and a little bit like a wound.

(in: "and the drow's prayer settled over us, light and cold at once, the way her god always feels — bright and a little bit like a wound.")

**Why:** "Bright and a little bit like a wound" is Valphine's theological register — pain as proof of contact with Lathander — transposed into Brewbarry's perception. He would register the physical sensation, not analyze the theology behind it. This is Valphine's vocabulary speaking through Brewbarry's mouth; the spec warns against this kind of bleed between adjacent narrators.
**Suggested rewrite:** "Valphine's prayer came over us. Light and cold at once. I don't understand her god. But I trust her hand."

### [4] Cliché / on-the-nose simile

> standing upright in the wreckage like the last warrior on a field of the dead.

**Why:** "The last warrior on a field of the dead" is high-fantasy stock. The spec warns against ornate description in Brewbarry's sections; his combat and object narration is procedural and bodily. A lone bottle earns a short sentence, not a battlefield elegy.
**Suggested rewrite:** "One bottle, standing." (The period is the observation.)

## Verdict

"Something in my chest went very simple and very loud." / "Who's responsible for the destruction of the wine." / "For this one. For all the ones that didn't make it." — all three are exactly right and must stay. Four flags concentrated in over-literary description. Flag [3] is the most important to fix because it breaks narrator distinctiveness; Brewbarry speaking in Valphine's theological register is the kind of bleed that makes both characters read flatter.
