# Voice Critique — Soma, scene 06: Exploring the Ruined Manse

**Narration:** session_doc_scene_06_exploring_the_ruined_manse.scrubbed.md
**Voice spec:** voice/soma_new_pipeline.md
**Per-char examples:** examples/soma.md

## Flags

### [1] Cliché / on-the-nose simile

> A single story is nothing to a bear who can scale a tree faster than a frightened squirrel

**Why:** "Frightened squirrel" is a generic stock comparison. Soma's similes are grounded in her specific coastal/natural-world experience — the very next sentence earns its comparison ("Old timber takes a claw the way river mud takes a footprint") because it draws on things she's actually done. A squirrel she'd know, but the comparison is too common to belong distinctively to her.
**Suggested rewrite:** "A single story is nothing to a bear who wants the ground before her feet decide to find it."

### [2] Voice spec conflict (ornate simile)

> his rage cutting out mid-roar like a snuffed candle.

**Why:** "Snuffed candle" is an evocative literary image. The spec says "if a sentence is beautiful, blunt it." Soma would register Brewbarry falling through the stairs with a flat bodily description of what she heard — the sudden stop, the silence — not a candle metaphor.
**Suggested rewrite:** "his rage cutting out mid-roar — one note, then nothing."

### [3] Voice spec conflict (staff agency / borders on "chosen" framing)

> The staff knew it before I did, the way it always does, ahead of my own senses.

**Why:** "The way it always does, ahead of my own senses" gives the staff reliable prescience — it's not just responding, it's habitually ahead of her. The spec explicitly forbids "destiny, called, or chosen language about herself or the staff." The staff gains leaves; Soma notices physical changes in it; she reads it as a natural object she's learning. Framing it as an active sensing agent she counts on crosses into the chosen-instrument territory the spec prohibits.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The staff had gone still in a particular way. Not rest. The kind of quiet a living thing makes before something finds it."

## Verdict

Strong scene — the bear-recon structure, Brewbarry's fall, and the stirge ambush cliffhanger are well-paced and bodily. "Patient thing. I approved of it." and "they knew it better than I did" are excellent. Three flags; flag [3] is the most important because it touches the staff-as-chosen-instrument line the spec prohibits.
