# Voice Critique — Valphine, scene 02: Reflections on Fate and Morning Plans

**Narration:** session_doc_scene_02_reflections_on_fate_and_morning_plans.scrubbed.md
**Voice spec:** voice/valphine_new_pipeline.md
**Per-char examples:** examples/valphine.md

## Flags

### [1] Voice spec conflict (conversion framing)

> I knew the thing had hooked him the way the Overbright once hooked me, deep and unwilling.

**Why:** "Hooked me, deep and unwilling" frames Valphine's conversion to Lathander as something that seized her involuntarily — a loss of control. The spec is explicit: the conversion was a power calculation, not a surrender. She recognized the larger power and aligned with it; she "became clearer," not captured. "Unwilling" directly contradicts the rational-agent framing and reads like the drow-fallen-to-the-light cliché the spec rejects.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I knew the thing had hold of him the way Lathander had hold of me, before I understood what kind of hand it was."

### [2] Generic fantasy prose

> this was the deeper thing, clean and whole, the kind of performance that makes a room forget to breathe.

**Why:** "Makes a room forget to breathe" is a stock performance description. Valphine as archivist of motive would note the specific tactical effect — what shifted in Rimardo's posture, what leverage Vukradin's performance created, who the room was watching — not the generic crowd-silence idiom.
**Suggested rewrite:** "this was the thing that sits under competence — the kind that moves rooms without appearing to; I noted the shift in Rimardo's stance and filed it."

### [3] Cliché / on-the-nose simile

> I could see it the way you see a wound through a bandage.

**Why:** Medical simile (wound through bandage) is generic for Valphine's drow-precise vocabulary. She reads people as systems of motive, tactical tell, and self-deception — not bodily metaphors borrowed from a surface-world healer's handbook. The observation is correct; the image isn't specific to her.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I could see it in the way he tried to settle himself at the table and couldn't."

### [4] Voice spec conflict (metaphor vocabulary)

> I had meant to plant them here as new settlers; that, apparently, was a poor seed in poor soil.

**Why:** "Poor seed in poor soil" is an agricultural/surface-world metaphor that doesn't fit Valphine's tactical, aristocratic, drow register. She frames failed plans in terms of leverage, structural miscalculation, and misread resistance — not gardening. The phrase also has a wry surface-dweller-folksy quality that doesn't match her elevated vocabulary.
**Suggested rewrite:** "I had meant to place them here as new settlers. The ground, it turned out, had not been prepared for what I had in mind."

## Verdict

The theological reasoning (Savras's problem, not mine; the two-claimants arithmetic; "my Lathander / the Church of the Searing Light") is the scene's strongest material and should stay. Four flags; the conversion-framing flag [1] is the most important — it misaligns the pivotal backstory and it recurs in scene 05, so fixing one without the other leaves the characterization inconsistent.
