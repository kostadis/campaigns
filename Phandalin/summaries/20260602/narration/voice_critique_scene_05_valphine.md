# Voice Critique — Valphine, scene 05: Battle in the Courtyard

**Narration:** session_doc_scene_05_battle_in_the_courtyard.scrubbed.md
**Voice spec:** voice/valphine_new_pipeline.md
**Per-char examples:** examples/valphine.md

## Flags

### [1] Cliché / on-the-nose simile (possible convergence with Soma's register)

> The fight came down like a dropped cup.

**Why:** "Like a dropped cup" is a domestic-accident idiom — sudden, irreversible, fragments. The image has logic but it's not Valphine's register. Her similes come from drow tactical culture and power analysis ("In Menzoberranzan you learn early that the creature you greet is the creature that kills you" — adjacent in the same paragraph). Dry domestic humor is also Soma's lane; the dropped-cup simile risks reading as Soma bleeding into this scene.
**Suggested rewrite:** "The fight was already over before I had finished deciding to start it."

### [2] Generic vocabulary

> a sphere of inky, pressing black wide enough to drink the whole room

**Why:** "Inky" is common and slightly pedestrian — not the elevated, aristocratic vocabulary the spec mandates for Valphine. When narrating her god's power in action, she reaches for precise and exacting words, not the casual color-adjective used in children's books.
**Suggested rewrite:** "a sphere of absolute dark, dense and pressing, wide enough to swallow the room entire" — "absolute" does the precision work "inky" doesn't.

### [3] Voice spec conflict (conversion framing — recurring from scene 02)

> the same searing pressure I once mistook for the sun

**Why:** "Once mistook for the sun" frames the conversion as a prior error she corrected — but the spec says she became *clearer*, recognized the larger power, made the calculation correctly. "Mistook" implies she got it wrong first; Valphine does not frame her history as a sequence of mistakes. This is the same conversion-framing problem as scene 02 flag [1]: both read the Lathander encounter as something that overcame her rather than something she recognized and chose.
**Suggested rewrite:** "the same searing pressure I had already sorted and filed the first time I felt it"

## Verdict

"In Menzoberranzan you learn early..." / "deliciously, finally still" / Vukradin-as-always-the-perfect-angle are all excellent and should stay. Three flags; flag [3] is the most significant because it's the same pattern as scene 02 flag [1] — if that line is fixed, this one must be fixed for consistency. Both say the same wrong thing about Valphine's conversion.
