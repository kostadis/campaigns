# Voice Critique — Vukradin, scene 01: The Late Curtain Call

**Narration:** session_doc_scene_01_the_late_curtain_call.scrubbed.md
**Voice spec:** voice/vukradin_new_pipeline.md
**Per-char examples:** examples/vukradin.md

## Flags

### [1] Generic fantasy prose

> the room held its breath without my asking it to

**Why:** "The room held its breath" is a stock atmospheric phrase. Vukradin as cheerleader-conductor narrates crowd response in terms of specific people — who leaned in, who stopped mid-grumble, what the room actually did. The abstract "room" as collective agent doesn't land in his register; he sees individual faces, not aggregate ambience.
**Suggested rewrite:** "the survivors at the back tables had been mid-grumble when I started and they weren't mid-grumble anymore"

### [2] Tell-not-show emotional commentary

> I can't imagine Rimardo and Corrin give two shits about any of this, I thought, and that was somehow worse.

**Why:** "And that was somehow worse" names the emotional effect instead of rendering it. The spec says Vukradin goes quiet when genuinely frightened — this is the moment where the show has genuinely unsettled him, and "somehow worse" is him half-admitting it on the page rather than letting the thought's blankness do the work. The vagueness of "somehow" is the tell.
**Suggested rewrite:** End the sentence at "I thought" and cut the clause. The silence of not completing the thought does the work.

### [3] Tell-not-show emotional commentary

> meant something I didn't want to understand yet

(in: "Somewhere on his way out the door, I was fairly sure, Corrin would have winked at that — *there are other ways to make money* — and meant something I didn't want to understand yet.")

**Why:** Vukradin surfaces his own resistance to understanding, which is a more confessional move than the spec supports. His strategic-ignorance pattern ("Keep me in the dark, man") is active and announced; here the avoidance is narrated as if he's cataloging his own fear. The spec says his unease shows as silence or a pivot to the next practical thing, not as self-observation.
**Suggested rewrite:** "and meant something" — the sentence stops there; the trailing stop is more in-spec.

## Verdict

Strong scene. The ledger reflex, studio sincerity, cheerleader-conductor energy, and procedural mechanics are all solidly present. The coin landing, "Not blood money," and the soppy-musicals riff on free will are excellent and should stay. Three mild flags, all tell-not-show or generic-phrase level; none requires re-narration.
