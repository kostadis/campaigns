# Chapter 40 The Power of the Darkness and the Light

Date: May 5th, 2026

## Summary
The confrontation at the gully began with Vukradin raising his voice in the orcs' own tongue, calling out to the warband across the ravine with words of Lathander's light and mercy. His speech gave several of the orcs pause, their weapons lowering ever so slightly as doubt crept into their eyes. But Valphine, never one for subtlety, unleashed a blazing display of radiant holy energy that sent the Talosian anchorite into a fury. The anchorite of Talos branded her a false prophet and screamed for blood, and the battle was joined.

The anchorite proved a fearsome foe, calling down a crackling bolt of lightning that tore through Vukradin, Brewbarry, and their centaur companion Xanth in a single devastating strike. Vukradin and Xanth made their saving throws, each absorbing 14 points of damage; Brewbarry failed his and took the full 29. Xanth shook off the pain and returned fire with his longbow, wounding the cleric. Then Soma moved — her body rippling and expanding as she transformed into a massive giant elk, her hooves thundering across the earth as she charged headlong into the anchorite and sent him crashing to the ground. With the cleric prone and vulnerable, Boney the skeletal horse surged forward and delivered a punishing blow, and Brewbarry, roaring in a battle rage, brought his halberd down with bone-shattering force and ended the anchorite's life for good.

With their leader dead, the warband began to fracture. Orcs fled in every direction, some hurling javelins in desperation as they ran, others simply dropping their weapons and disappearing into the trees. One orc, seemingly oblivious to the carnage around him, calmly demanded money from Vukradin and threatened to let him live in exchange — a shakedown that ended poorly for the orc when Vukradin declined and the axe came swinging. Brewbarry knocked another unconscious with a single thunderous blow, choosing mercy over the kill. Soma, sensing the worst of the fighting was over, dropped her elk form, leapt onto Boney's back, and rode the skeletal horse away from the front lines to spare any further bloodshed.

When the dust settled, only one orc remained standing — a broad-shouldered warrior who set his great axe slowly on the ground as Vukradin spoke to him one final time. The orc looked at Valphine, the Drow cleric wreathed in sunlight, and something shifted behind his eyes. He declared, with blunt and earnest conviction, that Lathander was clearly more powerful than Talos, and that a Drow who wielded the sun's might was proof enough for him. He rushed toward Valphine and pledged himself to Lathander on the spot. The party learned his name was Prutha.

Prutha's story was not a simple one. He revealed that his great uncle had been the shaman of his clan — a gentle man who had been killed by the Talosians for being too weak, too soft, too tied to the old ways of greeting the dawn. Prutha had fought for Talos not out of devotion, but out of survival, never once killing by choice across three skirmishes. Then, with a trembling hand, he reached into his furs and produced a small bone whistle — the very instrument his great uncle had used each morning to greet the sunrise. He blew it for the first time in years, and the sound that came out was thin and mournful and full of everything he had buried. Prutha wept. Valphine, moved by the ritual, resolved then and there to weave the bone whistle into the evolving rites of her Lathandrian faith, a living thread connecting the sun's glory to the grief and resilience of those who had nearly forgotten it.

The party looted a healing potion from the fallen anchorite's body, and Valphine and Brewbarry strung up the cleric's corpse as a warning to any who might follow. Vukradin disapproved but said nothing, turning instead to walk with Prutha, who had already vowed to help them hunt down the remaining Talosian clerics — declaring that those unwilling to convert must be dealt with accordingly. Xanth, their centaur guide, paused at the edge of the treeline and looked one last time at a mysterious staff before turning to the party. He told Vukradin that their paths now separated, offered a quiet acknowledgment to Soma — noting that she had been blessed by "Meril" and was destined for great things — and then galloped away into the wilderness without another word. (Open lore question: whether "Meril" is Xanth's regional name for Mielikki or a reference to Soma's mortal druid mentor was not confirmed in-session.)

The party pressed on toward the Circle of Thunder, climbing a ninety-foot hill as ominous storm clouds gathered and churned overhead. At the summit, a great ring of ancient standing stones loomed against the darkening sky, and within the henge, two ghastly figures danced in a slow and terrible ritual. Around them capered twelve small, twisted creatures of bark and twig, their movements erratic and wrong. The party studied the scene from the treeline, debating how best to approach — whether to unleash the Necklace of Fireballs, sweep through with Spirit Guardians, or find another way entirely. Whatever they decided, the Circle of Thunder was waiting, and the storm above it was only growing darker.

## Locations
### Circle of Thunder (Gully Approach)
A 60-foot gully with a ridge on the opposite side where ten orcs and an anchorite of Talos were positioned. The site of the party's fierce skirmish with the Talosian warband. (Combat tracker used numbers 1–11 with orc 8 appearing twice as a duplicate label; confirmed count is ten orcs.)

### Circle of Thunder (Hilltop)
A 90-foot tall hill topped with a large ring of standing stones. Ominous storm clouds gather overhead as two ghastly Talosian anchorites perform a dark ritual dance within the henge, surrounded by twelve twig blights.

### Woodland Mounts
The forested area the party travels through to reach the Circle of Thunder, where they are guided by the centaur Xanth.

## Items
### Potion of Greater Healing
A potent magical restorative found on the body of the defeated anchorite of Talos after the skirmish at the gully. The party now holds two potions of greater healing (one carried before this session, one looted here), plus one potion of superior healing and one scented potion of healing.

### Bone Whistle
A traditional orcish instrument carried by Prutha, used by his clan to greet the dawn each morning. It holds deep religious significance tied to his great uncle, a shaman killed by Talosians. Prutha blew it for the first time in a very long time after converting to Lathander, moving himself to tears. Valphine decided to incorporate the whistle ritual into her evolving Lathandrian worship.

### Necklace of Fireballs
A magical necklace held by the party and discussed as a potential weapon to use against the cluster of twig blights at the Circle of Thunder, though the party debated whether the enemies were sufficiently grouped for it to be effective.

### Mysterious Staff
An object of apparent significance that Xanth paused to look upon one final time before departing from the party.

## Spells
### Silvery Barbs
Cast by Vukradin to force an orc to re-roll a successful attack against him, causing the orc to miss. The spell also granted Brewbarry advantage on his next attack roll, which he applied to a reaction attack of opportunity against orc 7. (Table ruling: the advantage from Silvery Barbs may be used on a reaction attack; RAW it applies to "the next attack roll" — the table confirmed this includes reactions.)

### Universal Speech
Used by Vukradin to communicate with and persuade the orcs in their own tongue. Over multiple rounds, he successfully moved several orcs from hostile to neutral or receptive, and ultimately convinced Prutha to convert to Lathander.

### Lightning Bolt
Cast by the anchorite of Talos against the party, striking Vukradin, Brewbarry, and Xanth simultaneously with a powerful electrical blast. Spell DC 12: Vukradin and Xanth saved, each taking 14 points; Brewbarry failed his save and took the full 29 points.

### Emboldening Bond
Valphine's Peace Domain class feature (not a spell; no concentration required). She bonded up to three willing party members for 10 minutes; while any bonded creature was within 30 ft. of another, each could add a d4 once per turn to an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. Active throughout the battle. (The GM referred to it as "Bless" at the table — corrected: Bless requires concentration; Emboldening Bond does not.)

### Thunderwave
Discussed by the party as a potential area-of-effect option for dealing with the twig blights gathered at the Circle of Thunder.

### Spirit Guardians
Discussed by the party as a strategy for the upcoming confrontation at the Circle of Thunder, noted for its effectiveness against enemies that approach the caster in melee.

## NPCs
### Xanth
A centaur guide who led the party through the wilderness to the Circle of Thunder. He warned the party about the dangerous followers of Talos and fought alongside them during the skirmish, firing his longbow at the anchorite. Upon reaching the destination, he bid the party farewell, offering a special acknowledgment to Soma, noting she had been blessed by "Meril" — whether this is a regional name for Mielikki or a reference to Soma's mortal druid mentor is an open lore question for DM clarification.

### Jenna Roscoe
A representative of the Lord's Alliance who attempted to recruit the party, as mentioned in the session recap. Her pitch was met with Brewbarry's thunderous burp.

### Anchorite of Talos
An anchorite of Talos who led the orc warband at the gully. He was enraged by Valphine's display of radiant holy energy, branded her a false prophet, and ordered the attack. He unleashed a devastating lightning bolt against the party (DC 12; Vukradin and Xanth saved for 14 each; Brewbarry failed for 29) before being knocked prone by Soma's elk charge and ultimately slain by Brewbarry.

### Prutha
An orc warrior formerly aligned with the cult of Talos. After witnessing the party's victory and hearing Vukradin's persuasive words, he set down his great axe and converted to the worship of Lathander. He revealed that his great uncle was a shaman killed by Talosians, and that he carried a bone whistle used by his clan to greet the sunrise — a tradition he had abandoned until this moment. He joined the party, vowing to help them deal with the remaining Talosian clerics.

### Boney
A skeletal horse serving as a Warrior sidekick to the party. He attacks with a longsword two-handed per his sidekick stat block (no shield slot). He participated in combat by charging and striking the prone anchorite, and was targeted by orcs who viewed him as an abomination. Soma rode him away from the front lines to reduce further bloodshed.

### Twig Blights
Twelve small, capering plant-like creatures of neutral evil disposition surrounding the Talosian anchorites during their ritual at the Circle of Thunder. They are considered fodder for area-of-effect magic by the party.

### Anchorites of Talos (Circle of Thunder)
Two ghastly spellcasters performing a ritual dance within the stone henge at the top of the hill. They are considered non-convertible enemies by the party.
