The orc came out of nowhere, screaming for Talos.

I heard it before I saw it — that rising battle-cry, the kind that's meant to rattle you, and I felt Vukradin shift beside me. The big one, the one they'd call Orc 2 if this were a ledger entry and not a gully full of killing, had his greataxe up and was charging straight at Vukradin with everything he had.

And then something strange happened.

Vukradin did that thing he does — that thing I still don't fully understand, where the air around him gets a little heavier, a little more intentional, and the world sort of bends to accommodate his will. The orc swung. The blow that should have connected just... didn't. It went wide. I felt the advantage of it pass into me like heat from a fire, a certainty in my hands I don't usually have until I'm already bleeding. Vukradin's voice, calm behind me: *We'll give Brewbarry advantage on his next attack.*

That's Vukradin. That's always been Vukradin. He takes a hit that was meant to hurt him and turns it into something useful for me.

I didn't have long to feel grateful.

The anchorite hit us all at once.

Lightning doesn't feel like much in the instant it touches you — it's the half-second after, when every muscle in your body seizes and your teeth crack together and the light burns a white line across your vision. The bolt came down the gully and found Vukradin, found Xanth, found me. I heard the centaur curse in words I didn't know, the kind of raw syllables that mean the same thing in every language. I was still standing — barely — but the hit had reorganized something inside me, pulled the air out of my chest and replaced it with a kind of ringing absence. The anchorite stood at the far end of the field looking pleased with himself.

That was a mistake on his part.

Xanth got his longbow up fast — two shots, both of them high, lost in the trees. He looked disgusted with himself in the way only someone who genuinely expected better can look.

Then Soma moved.

I have watched Soma transform before. It never gets less strange. One moment there is a tortle elder, patient and heavy-shelled, and then the air warps and there is something enormous where she was — a giant elk, head down, already moving, already building speed before the form has fully settled. She hit the anchorite like a wall of forest. The ram drove him into the dirt and kept going, and when the dust cleared he was on his belly, prone, and Soma's voice came out of that massive throat with something like satisfaction: *On your belly.*

The anchorite had a moment to consider what it meant to insult a druid.

Around me, the orcs were finding their angles. One went for Vukradin again. Another tried Soma's elk form and missed. A third — one of the ones that was supposed to be neutral, which is a thing I find funny, the idea of a neutral orc in a fight — launched a javelin at the elk and got through. I saw Soma absorb it. She didn't go down, but she was eating damage now, which meant the clock was running.

Then Boney arrived.

Boney is a skeleton on a horse. I mean — Boney is a skeletal horse who carries a sword and fights like he knows what he's doing, because apparently that is a thing that happens when Soma is involved. He came through the middle of everything, found the prone anchorite, and hit him hard with that longsword. Two-handed, because Boney doesn't carry a shield. Of course he doesn't. He's a skeleton. I found myself briefly grateful that I didn't have to explain this to myself anymore.

The anchorite was still breathing. Not for long.

Orc 7 decided to charge into the cluster of us, which was brave, if unwise. My body moved before I decided to move — the Dragon Slayer was already coming up, the first swing gone wide, and then Vukradin's gift was still in my hands, the advantage still warm, and the second swing connected with everything I had behind it. The orc went down hard and didn't get up.

From somewhere to my left, Vukradin's voice carried that particular note of someone who is trying to sound irritated but is actually just amused:

*"How are we gonna win them over if you're slaughtering them?"*

I didn't have an answer for that. The orc had charged. The sword was already out. What exactly was I supposed to do.

Valphine spoke for me, which was fair: *"But he came to attack us."*

And then it was my turn.

I raged. There is something that happens in that moment — not loss of self, not quite, but a simplification. The gully got cleaner. The anchorite was prone. He had lit up Vukradin with lightning. He had lit up Vukradin.

I moved through the two orcs between me and the anchorite. They both took their swings. Neither of them connected. Sometimes the world wants you to get somewhere, and it steps aside. I brought the halberd down on the prone anchorite — once, with the weight of Bless behind it, with the certainty of Vukradin's gift still singing faintly in my muscles — and the hit was clean and total. I could feel how little was left in him.

The second swing missed. But then Soma pointed out the obvious thing — he was prone, which meant I had advantage — and Valphine rolled again.

Eighteen.

The anchorite died.

Vukradin: *"Alright!"* And then, because he is who he is: *"No more killing things, let's convert everybody!"*

I stood over the body and thought about that for a moment.

Orc 9 came at Boney next, which I hadn't expected — something about the skeleton offended him, maybe, or maybe Boney just had the look of something valuable. I saw the swing coming and I stepped into it. The hit landed on me instead. While raging it felt like being tapped on a bruise — real, but containable. Boney lived. I figured Soma would want that.

On the other side of the gully, Orc 10 threw a javelin at Valphine and missed completely, the kind of miss that has a story behind it. Orc 11 got through the elk's side for something.

Then Orc 2 — the first one, the one Vukradin had redirected — apparently decided he'd been patient enough with all this fighting. He put his greataxe down. Not dropped it. Set it down. Deliberate. And then he said, in the way that men say things when they've run the numbers and don't like the result:

*"Give me money and I let you live."*

I looked at Vukradin. Vukradin looked at the orc.

*"The followers of Lathander don't make such demands."*

The orc picked his greataxe back up. Of course he did. He swung. And I heard, underneath the impact, underneath Vukradin's grunt of pain, the orc's whole logic laid out in the way he pulled back: *I don't want to kill you, man, I just want the money.*

Xanth's second round of shots went high. Both of them.

And then Soma, who had been taking hits in elk form and absorbing them one by one, made a decision. She stepped back, took the attacks of opportunity that came with moving — one of them connecting, one glancing off nothing — and then she dropped the form. Just let it go. The elk vanished. Soma was a tortle again, smaller, standing in the middle of a gully full of orcs, and she climbed onto Boney's back like it was the most natural thing in the world.

*"So we get the abomination and the delicious meat away from the guys,"* she said.

Boney moved. Orc 9 swung at him as he went. Missed.

And that was how it sat — the anchorite dead in the dirt, the orcs still circling, the gully still loud with the smell of lightning and blood and something that might have been possibility, if Vukradin had anything to say about it.

He usually did.
