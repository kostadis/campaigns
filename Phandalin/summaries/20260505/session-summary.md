# Chapter 40 The Power of the Darkness and the Light

Date: May 5th, 2026

## Summary
The confrontation at the gully began with Vukradin raising his voice in the orcs' own tongue, calling out to the warband across the ravine with words of Lathander's light and mercy. His speech gave several of the orcs pause, their weapons lowering ever so slightly as doubt crept into their eyes. But Valphine, never one for subtlety, unleashed a blazing display of radiant holy energy that sent the Talosian anchorite into a fury. The anchorite of Talos branded her a false prophet and screamed for blood, and the battle was joined.

The anchorite proved a fearsome foe, calling down a crackling bolt of lightning that tore through Vukradin, Brewbarry, and their centaur companion Xanth in a single devastating strike. Vukradin and Xanth made their saving throws, each absorbing 14 points of damage; Brewbarry failed his and took the full 29. Xanth shook off the pain and fired back with his longbow twice, but both shots missed. Then Soma moved — her body rippling and expanding as she transformed into a massive giant elk, her hooves thundering across the earth as she charged headlong into the anchorite and sent him crashing to the ground. With the cleric prone and vulnerable, Boney the skeletal horse surged forward and delivered a punishing blow, and Brewbarry, roaring in a battle rage, brought his halberd down with bone-shattering force and ended the anchorite's life for good.

With their leader dead, the warband began to fracture. Orcs fled in every direction, some hurling javelins in desperation as they ran, others simply dropping their weapons and disappearing into the trees. One orc, seemingly oblivious to the carnage around him, calmly demanded money from Vukradin and threatened to let him live in exchange — a shakedown that ended poorly for the orc when Vukradin declined and the axe came swinging. The DM noted afterward that a couple of copper pieces would have sent the orc on his way; Vukradin's principled refusal cost him 9 points of damage. Brewbarry knocked another orc unconscious with a single thunderous blow, choosing mercy over the kill — though the non-lethal call came a fraction too late for the preceding target. Soma, sensing the worst of the fighting was over, dropped her elk form, leapt onto Boney's back, and rode the skeletal horse away from the front lines to spare any further bloodshed.

When the dust settled, only one orc remained standing — a broad-shouldered warrior who set his great axe slowly on the ground as Vukradin spoke to him one final time. The orc looked at Valphine, the Drow cleric wreathed in sunlight, and something shifted behind his eyes. He declared, with blunt and earnest conviction, that Lathander was clearly more powerful than Talos, and that a Drow who wielded the sun's might was proof enough for him. He rushed toward Valphine and pledged himself to Lathander on the spot. The party learned his name was Prutha.

Prutha's story was not a simple one. He revealed that his great uncle had been the shaman of his clan — a gentle man who had been killed by the Talosians for being too weak, too soft, too tied to the old ways of greeting the dawn. Prutha had fought for Talos not out of devotion, but out of survival, never once killing by choice across three skirmishes. Then, with a trembling hand, he reached into his furs and produced a small bone whistle — the very instrument his great uncle had used each morning to greet the sunrise. He blew it for the first time in years, and the sound that came out was thin and mournful and full of everything he had buried. Prutha wept. Valphine, moved by the ritual, resolved then and there to weave the bone whistle into the evolving rites of her Lathandrian faith, a living thread connecting the sun's glory to the grief and resilience of those who had nearly forgotten it. The DM noted privately that Valphine was not feeling further from Lathander in this moment — if anything, the god seemed closer.

The party looted a healing potion from the fallen anchorite's body, and Valphine and Brewbarry strung up the cleric's corpse as a warning to any who might follow. Vukradin disapproved but said nothing, turning instead to walk with Prutha, who had already vowed to help them hunt down the remaining Talosian clerics — declaring that those unwilling to convert must be dealt with accordingly. Xanth, their centaur guide, paused at the edge of the treeline and looked one last time at a mysterious staff before turning to the party. He told Vukradin that their paths now separated, offered a quiet acknowledgment to Soma — noting that she had been blessed by "Meril" and was destined for great things — and then galloped away into the wilderness without another word. (Open lore question: whether "Meril" is Xanth's regional name for Mielikki or a reference to Soma's mortal druid mentor was not confirmed in-session.)

The party pressed on toward the Circle of Thunder, climbing a ninety-foot hill as ominous storm clouds gathered and churned overhead. At the summit, a great ring of ancient standing stones loomed against the darkening sky, and within the henge, two ghastly figures danced in a slow and terrible ritual. Around them capered twelve small, twisted creatures of bark and twig — twig blights — their movements erratic and wrong. The party studied the scene from the treeline, debating how best to approach — whether to unleash the Necklace of Fireballs, sweep through with Spirit Guardians, or find another way entirely. The twig blights were noted to have only 4 hit points each but were not sufficiently clustered for the Necklace of Fireballs to be immediately effective. Whatever they decided, the Circle of Thunder was waiting, and the storm above it was only growing darker.

### Anchorites of Talos (Circle of Thunder)
