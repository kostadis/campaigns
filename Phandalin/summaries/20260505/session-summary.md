# Chapter 40 The Power of the Darkness and the Light

Date: May 5th, 2026

## Summary
The confrontation at the gully began with Vukradin raising his voice in the orcs' own tongue, calling out to the warband across the ravine with words of Lathander's light and mercy. His speech gave several of the orcs pause, their weapons lowering ever so slightly as doubt crept into their eyes. But Valphine, never one for subtlety, unleashed a blazing display of radiant holy energy that sent the Talosian anchorite into a fury. The anchorite of Talos branded her a false prophet and screamed for blood, and the battle was joined.

The anchorite proved a fearsome foe, calling down a crackling bolt of lightning that tore through Vukradin, Brewbarry, and their centaur companion Xanth in a single devastating strike. Vukradin and Xanth made their saving throws, each absorbing 14 points of damage; Brewbarry failed his and took the full 29. Xanth shook off the pain and returned fire with his longbow, wounding the cleric for 6 points. Then Soma moved — her body rippling and expanding as she transformed into a massive giant elk, her hooves thundering across the earth as she charged headlong into the anchorite and sent him crashing to the ground, dealing 12 total points of damage on her charge and forcing a DC 14 Strength save that the anchorite failed. With the cleric prone and vulnerable, Boney the skeletal horse surged forward and delivered a punishing blow, and Brewbarry, roaring in a battle rage, brought his halberd down with bone-shattering force and ended the anchorite's life for good.

With their leader dead, the warband began to fracture. Orcs fled in every direction, some hurling javelins in desperation as they ran, others simply dropping their weapons and disappearing into the trees. One orc, seemingly oblivious to the carnage around him, calmly demanded money from Vukradin and threatened to let him live in exchange — a shakedown that ended poorly for the orc when Vukradin declined and the axe came swinging. Brewbarry knocked another unconscious with a single thunderous blow, choosing mercy over the kill. Soma, sensing the worst of the fighting was over, dropped her elk form, leapt onto Boney's back, and rode the skeletal horse away from the front lines to spare any further bloodshed.

When the dust settled, only one orc remained standing — a broad-shouldered warrior who set his great axe slowly on the ground as Vukradin spoke to him one final time. The orc looked at Valphine, the Drow cleric wreathed in sunlight, and something shifted behind his eyes. He declared, with blunt and earnest conviction, that Lathander was clearly more powerful than Talos, and that a Drow who wielded the sun's might was proof enough for him. He rushed toward Valphine and pledged himself to Lathander on the spot. The party learned his name was Prutha.

Prutha's story was not a simple one. He revealed that his great uncle had been the shaman of his clan — a gentle man who had been killed by the Talosians for being too weak, too soft, too tied to the old ways of greeting the dawn. Prutha had fought for Talos not out of devotion, but out of survival, never once killing by choice across three skirmishes. Then, with a trembling hand, he reached into his furs and produced a small bone whistle — the very instrument his great uncle had used each morning to greet the sunrise. He blew it for the first time in years, and the sound that came out was thin and mournful and full of everything he had buried. Prutha wept. Valphine, moved by the ritual, resolved then and there to weave the bone whistle into the evolving rites of her Lathandrian faith, a living thread connecting the sun's glory to the grief and resilience of those who had nearly forgotten it. Vukradin, rolling a 27 on his Insight check, understood the depth of what the moment meant — that Prutha had not blown the whistle in a very long time, not since the Talosians killed his great uncle for doing so.

The party looted a potion of greater healing from the fallen anchorite's body, and Valphine and Brewbarry strung up the cleric's corpse as a warning to any who might follow. Vukradin disapproved but said nothing, turning instead to walk with Prutha, who had already vowed to help them hunt down the remaining Talosian clerics — declaring that those unwilling to convert must be dealt with accordingly. Xanth, their centaur guide, paused at the edge of the treeline and looked one last time at a mysterious staff before turning to the party. He told Vukradin that their paths now separated, offered a quiet acknowledgment to Soma — noting that she had been blessed by "Meril" and was destined for great things — and then galloped away into the wilderness without another word. (Open lore question: whether "Meril" is Xanth's regional name for Mielikki or a reference to Soma's mortal druid mentor was not confirmed in-session.)

The party pressed on toward the Circle of Thunder, climbing a ninety-foot hill as ominous storm clouds gathered and churned overhead. At the summit, a great ring of ancient standing stones loomed against the darkening sky, and within the henge, two ghastly figures danced in a slow and terrible ritual. Around them capered twelve small, twisted creatures of bark and twig — twig blights — their movements erratic and wrong. The party studied the scene from the treeline, debating how best to approach — whether to unleash the Necklace of Fireballs, sweep through with Spirit Guardians, or find another way entirely. Vukradin noted that the blights were not sufficiently clustered for Fireball to be optimal; Spirit Guardians combined with a dash was raised as a strong alternative. Whatever they decided, the Circle of Thunder was waiting, and the storm above it was only growing darker.

### Prutha
An orc warrior formerly aligned with the cult of Talos. After witnessing the party's victory and hearing Vukradin's persuasive words (Persuasion 17 vs. Wisdom 12), he set down his great axe and converted to the worship of Lathander. He revealed that his great uncle was a shaman killed by Talosians — called "weak," "abomination," and "Thunder Meat" — and that he had fought for Talos only out of survival ("Uncle said: either fight or die. I fought"), never killing by choice across three skirmishes. He produced a bone whistle used by his clan to greet each sunrise, blew it for the first time since his great uncle's death, and wept. He quoted his great uncle's teaching: "Dawn comes for everyone, even those born in shadow." He joined the party, vowed to help them deal with the remaining Talosian clerics, and was enthusiastically converted. His name was generated at the table from an NPC name generator; the suggestion to rename him "Thrall" was rejected by Vukradin's player (who meant it pejoratively) and firmly vetoed by Vukradin's player Dave ("That is not appropriate for our new friend"). The DM noted that Prutha approves of stringing up the anchorite's body and considers Valphine's aggressive display of power entirely consistent with what he values.

### Boney
A skeletal horse serving as a Warrior sidekick to the party. He attacks with a longsword two-handed per his sidekick stat block (no shield slot, as the DM confirmed: "I actually was the one who said it would be that way, because you can't really carry a shield"). He participated in combat by charging and striking the prone anchorite for 13 points of damage. Several orcs targeted him as an abomination; one orc specifically threw a javelin at him rather than a party member because "Boney offends him for some reason." Brewbarry used his reaction to intercept one attack aimed at Boney. Soma later rode him away from the front lines to reduce bloodshed. His token caused ongoing Roll20 technical difficulties — he appeared at the wrong size and under insufficient player permissions, requiring DM intervention mid-combat.

### Twig Blights
Twelve small, capering plant-like creatures of neutral evil disposition (Intelligence 4) surrounding the Talosian anchorites during their ritual at the Circle of Thunder. Dave checked the stat block and confirmed 4 hit points each. They are considered fodder for area-of-effect magic by the party, though noted as not sufficiently clustered for a single Fireball to catch them all.

### Anchorites of Talos (Circle of Thunder)
Two ghastly spellcasters performing a ritual dance within the stone henge at the top of the hill. They are considered non-convertible enemies by the party. The DM confirmed there are exactly two of them when the party asked whether the "smaller capering creatures" might be convertible.
