# The Rout of the Talosians

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Brewbarry cuts down the anchorite and several orcs while Vukradin continues his preaching, breaking the warband's morale.
- Brewbarry enters a rage and charges the prone anchorite of Talos, delivering a lethal strike that kills the anchorite.
- Vukradin continues using Universal Speech over multiple rounds, successfully moving several orcs from hostile to neutral or receptive. He rolls a 26 on his persuasion check, moving orcs 1, 2, 3, 4, 8, 10, and 11 toward neutrality or receptivity. The DM confirms that those who succeed on Wisdom saves against the roll move to receptive — no longer interested in attacking.
- Valphine attempts to intimidate the remaining hostile orcs in Orcish (she also speaks Orcish), rolling a 13. Two orcs are unimpressed, and one neutral orc is flipped back to hostile. The DM cites Valphine's Drow heritage as a complicating factor: "Orcs don't like drows." The DM also notes the undead horse and general disorganisation of the party's presentation as confounding factors: "This was not the best organized religious ceremony."
- Orc 1 (receptive) throws a javelin at Boney, finding the skeletal horse an abomination. Brewbarry is offered a reaction attack of opportunity as orc 1 flees; Brewbarry swings but misses — in character, described as an intimidation gesture rather than a genuine attempt to kill.
- Brewbarry strikes down a charging orc (orc 9) with a massive blow. The attack deals 11 points of damage, killing the orc outright. The non-lethal call is raised a moment too late; the DM rules the kill stands.
- Brewbarry knocks orc 10 unconscious with a non-lethal strike, rolling an 18 to hit. The blow deals 15 points of base damage — enough to drop the orc. Orc 10 is marked as unconscious.
- Orcs 1, 4, 5, and 8 flee the battlefield entirely. Orc 11 also flees. Orc 3 strikes Vukradin once before fleeing; Vukradin casts Silvery Barbs on the attack, forcing a re-roll — the re-roll is confirmed to have worked, and orc 3 then departs. Orc 6 (receptive) looks around at his fleeing companions, decides life is too short, and runs. The DM: "He's like, there's this bright light, he's kind of going to himself… he's kind of decided that life's too short."
