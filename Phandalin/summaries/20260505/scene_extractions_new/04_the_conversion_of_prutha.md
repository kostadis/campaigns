# The Conversion of Prutha

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Vukradin successfully convinces one of the remaining orcs to abandon Talos and follow the path of Lathander.
- With most orcs fled or dead, only orc 2 remains. Vukradin makes a final persuasive speech, rolling a 17 against the orc's Wisdom save of 12.
- The orc sets down his great axe. He observes the Drow cleric wreathed in sunlight and draws an immediate conclusion: Talos offered power; Lathander, evidently, offers more. He vocalises this in halting Common: "Lathander, more power… Lathander, all-powerful." He then rushes toward Valphine and declares himself a follower of the Drow Cleric of Lathander.
- The party learns the orc's name is Prutha (generated by the DM using an orc name generator mid-session). The DM explicitly offers the party the option to give him a Lathandrian "conversion name"; the suggestion of "Thrall" from Valphine is objected to by Vukradin. The name Prutha stands.
- Prutha reveals his backstory: his great uncle was the clan's shaman, killed by Talosians to break resistance and facilitate recruitment. The shaman was called "weak," "abomination," and "thunder meat." Prutha fought for Talos only because his great uncle made clear it was fight or die; he never killed by choice across three skirmishes.
- Prutha produces a small bone whistle — a traditional instrument his clan used to greet the sunrise each morning. He blows it for the first time in a very long time. He then begins to cry. Valphine's player rolls Insight (27); the DM confirms that Prutha is weeping because he realises he has not blown the whistle in an extremely long time.
- Prutha's great uncle taught him: "Dawn comes for everyone, even those born in shadow." The table reacts with genuine surprise at the line's poetry.
- Vukradin's player asks whether the whistle has a range of notes. The DM confirms: it is a whistle. One note.
- Valphine attempts a Religion check (GM called it for Valphine, not Vukradin), boosted by Vukradin's Bardic Inspiration and her Bless (final result approximately 12 — a poor roll despite the bonuses). Despite the low roll, the DM rules Valphine gains a religious insight: the whistle ritual could be incorporated into her evolving Lathandrian worship, absorbing solar rites from other traditions. The DM notes that Valphine does not feel further from Lathander; if anything, the god seems closer. Vukradin's player and the DM discuss how Lathander appears to find Valphine's approach — clarity, focus, ordered power so pervasive it reads as good — genuinely interesting.
- Vukradin blesses the orc: "Lathander blesses you today, my child." The orc's response: "Lathander, power, beyond doubt." He kicks the fallen anchorite's body several times for emphasis.
- Prutha vows to help the party kill the remaining Talosian clerics. He clarifies his terms: orcs no longer following Talos are spared; committed Talosian followers are not. The party agrees: "Convert or die." The DM dryly notes that this is a very fluid approach to religion.
- The party loots a potion of greater healing from the fallen anchorite's body.
- Valphine and Brewbarry string up the anchorite's corpse as a warning. Prutha approves enthusiastically. Vukradin disapproves but does not intervene. The DM observes that Prutha's enthusiasm is consistent with Valphine's underlying theology of power, which the DM characterises as "power so all-pervasive that people think it's good" — drawing a contrast with Gruumsh-style chaotic dominance.
- The party briefly discusses whether orc 10 (unconscious) might be revived and converted. Vukradin asks; the DM rules the attempt can proceed, though the party ultimately presses on.
- Someone makes a Corbin joke ("Here's Corbin — oh wait, Corbin's dead"), which the DM acknowledges was cold.
