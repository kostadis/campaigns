# Aletra Sotorra — Character Description for Artist

**Reference:** High fantasy Dr. Aphra. A drow rogue-scientist who deals in exotic poisons and alien technology, working as a field agent for a cosmic entity she finds intellectually fascinating and morally irrelevant.

**Species/Build:** Drow elf. Obsidian-dark skin, white hair kept practical — cropped short or pulled tight back, nothing that gets in the way of work. Lean and athletic, built for climbing through ruins and running from things she probably shouldn't have touched. She looks like someone who sleeps in her gear and is fine with it.

**Age:** Young adult equivalent by drow standards. Old enough to have survived her sisters' assassination attempts and built an interplanar trade empire. Not old enough to have lost the grin.

**Face/Expression:** Sharp features, angular in the way drow are, but her expression is what sells her. Amused. Not cruel — *interested*. She looks at everything like it's a specimen she hasn't catalogued yet. There's a warmth there that's easy to mistake for kindness — it's actually curiosity. She finds the world hilarious and dangerous in equal measure, and that delights her.

**Clothing/Gear:** Utilitarian, not ceremonial. She left Menzoberranzan's court fashion behind and doesn't miss it. Think field operative:
- Dark leather armor, fitted but worn — scratched, stained, lived in
- A bandolier or belt harness carrying small vials of iridescent liquid (drow poisons — her trade goods, various colors, faintly luminous)
- Goggles pushed up on her forehead or hanging around her neck — she works with things that flash and leak planar energy
- One or two devices that look *wrong* for a fantasy setting — too smooth, too geometric, faintly metallic with no visible seams. These are KP's interplanar tech: storage devices, containment tools, a scanner. They should look like they belong in a different genre — alien artifacts in a medieval world
- Gloves, one on, one tucked in her belt. She works with her hands.
- Boots meant for climbing, not marching

**Posture/Body Language:** Confident. Leans against things. Holds dangerous objects casually, the way a chemist holds a beaker — not because she doesn't know it's dangerous, but because she knows exactly how dangerous it is. One hand usually near a vial or a device. She doesn't stand at attention; she occupies space like someone who's already figured out three exits.

**Key Props (pick 2-3):**
- A vial of Midnight Tears poison held up to the light, examining it
- An interplanar storage device — a small, impossibly smooth cube or sphere that doesn't quite reflect light correctly
- A worn leather satchel overflowing with rolled notes, stolen research pages, and a device wrapped in cloth
- A faint, contained rift behind her — a crack in reality she's studying, not afraid of

**Mood/Palette:** Dark purples, deep blacks, but punctuated by the unnatural glow of the vials and the alien sheen of KP's tech. She should feel like someone who belongs in the Underdark's shadows but has chosen to work in the light — not because she converted, but because that's where the interesting problems are.

**What she is NOT:** Not a villain pose. Not menacing. Not seductive-assassin. She's a scientist-smuggler who happens to be a drow. Her danger is intellectual, not theatrical. She looks like someone who would sell you something fascinating and slightly lethal, and genuinely enjoy the conversation.
