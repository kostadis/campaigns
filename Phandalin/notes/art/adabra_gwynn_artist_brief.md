# Adabra Gwynn — Character Description for Artist

**Reference:** A druid who became a radical. Think a village midwife who reads too much theory and has started organizing — someone whose hands still smell of herbs and poultice, but whose eyes have gone somewhere harder. She is not a cackling villain. She is a woman who has watched catastrophe and drawn the wrong conclusion with absolute conviction.

**Species/Build:** Human. Middle-aged to older — she has weathered decades on Umbrage Hill alone, making potions, delivering babies, growing things. She is not frail. She's the kind of sturdy that comes from hauling water and grinding herbs and refusing to leave a stone windmill during a manticore siege. Solid. Rooted. Her body says: I have survived worse than you.

**Face/Expression:** This is the key. Two modes:

*The warmth:* When she's with someone she considers aligned — or someone she's trying to bring into alignment — her face is open, generous, almost maternal. She listens with her whole face. She leans in. She touches your arm when she agrees with you. You would trust her immediately. She would mean it.

*The withdrawal:* The moment she decides you are on the wrong side, the warmth doesn't fade — it *evaporates*. Instantly. What's left is not anger. It's a settled, immovable disappointment. She has already reclassified you. Her eyes are the same eyes, but they are no longer looking at a person. They are looking at a symptom.

**For the portrait: catch the moment between the two.** The warmth not yet gone, but something behind the eyes has already decided.

**Hair/Features:** Grey-streaked hair, long, usually tied back practically or loosely braided — she's a working herbalist, not a court druid. Her face has deep lines, especially around the eyes and mouth. These are laugh lines that have been repurposed for something else. Sun-weathered skin. She has been outdoors for thirty years.

**Clothing/Gear:** Practical druid-herbalist, not ceremonial:
- A heavy linen or wool dress or tunic, earth-toned — greens, browns, undyed natural fibers. Nothing dyed bright. She disapproves of excess.
- A leather apron or herb-stained work smock layered over the tunic — she was working when you arrived and she didn't change
- Pouches and belt pockets holding dried herbs, small vials of tinctures, folded cloth packets of prepared remedies. This is her trade.
- A wooden Emerald Enclave symbol — not prominent, not hidden. Worn like a wedding ring: it's just part of her.
- Bare hands, stained at the fingertips from years of potion-making. Her hands are her most important feature — they are healer's hands being used for something else now.
- No staff, no weapon. She doesn't need one. Her power is not martial.

**Setting/Context:** Her windmill on Umbrage Hill is the image — a stone windmill on a bare hillside, isolated, self-sufficient. Or: the Phandalin town square, standing apart from the crowd, watching the party with that expression that's already decided. She occupies space like someone who chose this ground and will not be moved from it.

**Posture/Body Language:** Still. She does not fidget. She does not pace. She stands or sits with the absolute stillness of someone who knows exactly what she thinks and is waiting for you to catch up. Arms crossed or hands folded — not defensive, *settled*. When she does move, it's deliberate: pointing at evidence, laying something on a table, turning away from someone she's done with.

**Key Props (pick 1-2):**
- A bundle of dried herbs in one hand, held absently — she was in the middle of something when this interruption happened
- A mortar and pestle on a table beside her, half-ground remedy abandoned mid-preparation
- A small pouch she opens to show someone: a pressed leaf or flower from the Whispering Grove, preserved, carried as evidence
- A council seat she occupies like she built it herself

**Mood/Palette:** Earthy. Muted greens and browns. No bright colors. The only brightness should be in her eyes — whatever mode they're in. She is the color of the ground she's standing on. The warmth, when it's there, comes from her face, not her clothes.

**What she is NOT:** Not a hag. Not a witch. Not sinister-looking. Not visibly corrupted. She looks like someone you would ask for help if you were lost. She looks like someone who would help. That is what makes her dangerous — everything about her exterior says *safe*, and she was, once. The radicalization is invisible. It lives in the eyes and the stillness, not in her appearance.

**The trajectory (for an artist doing multiple pieces):** She starts warm, rooted, generous — the village herbalist. Over time the stillness deepens. The hands that made remedies start making lists. The eyes that watched the forest start watching people. By the end, if she reaches Tiamat's alliance, there is a faint draconic quality at the edges — her wild shapes carry something sharper, her shadow falls wrong in certain lights. But she still looks like a midwife. That's the point.
