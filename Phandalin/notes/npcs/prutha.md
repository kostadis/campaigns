# Prutha — orc convert to Lathander (DRAFT)

**One-line hook:** A conscripted Talosian who was waiting, without knowing it, for someone to make sense of his shaman's last teaching.

## Origin

- **Eastern Heart tribe**, displaced from the Icespire foothills ~1 year ago when Cryovain took the Hold (same wave that produced Shrimpy's faction).
- His **great-uncle was the band's shaman** — the one the Talosians murdered to break the band's resistance and recruit the survivors. The Anchorites called the shaman *"weak,"* *"abomination,"* *"thunder-meat."*
- Prutha was conscripted by survival, not belief. He's fought in three skirmishes; never killed by choice.

## The shaman's teaching (load-bearing)

- His great-uncle taught one core doctrine: *"The dawn comes for everyone — even those born in shadow."*
- Eastern Heart orcs traditionally greeted sunrise with a **bone whistle** and a single bowed posture (right fist to left shoulder, head down — the **dawn-bow**).
- Prutha still carries his uncle's whistle inside his armor. He has not blown it since the murder.
- The Talosians beat the dawn-doctrine out of the band. Prutha kept it private — buried, not abandoned.

## The conversion moment

He fights the whole ridge engagement. Doesn't break ranks, doesn't desert — the Talosians have his band on a leash and he knows what happens to deserters. But he watches.

He watches Valphine the entire fight:
- Drow eyes squinting against her own Daylight, attacks at disadvantage from her own god's gift
- Still channeling. Still healing. Still standing.
- And he watches the acolyte — the man who murdered his uncle and called the shaman *weak* — die screaming that this cannot be.

The Talosians promised him **strength**. The strength is dead in a heap around him. The drow who should not be able to stand under her own light is standing.

When the dust settles, Prutha is the last orc upright. Wounded. Surrounded. No reinforcements. No exit.

He drops his axe. Kneels. Performs the **Eastern Heart dawn-bow**. Pulls the bone whistle from inside his armor and blows the **dawn-prayer** for the first time since his uncle's murder.

**This is not surrender.** It's the greeting his tribe used to perform after surviving a hard night. Valphine should recognize it as ritual, not capitulation. The party can choose to read it either way — but the gesture is theological, and the whistle is the load-bearing detail.

## Why this lands harder than mid-combat

- The cult's lie is **disproven by corpses**, not rhetoric. He doesn't need the conversion explained — it's strewn around him.
- Valphine doesn't *do* anything to convert him. She just keeps standing. That's the whole point of her doctrine — pain as honest devotion — and Prutha is the first creature outside the church to see it from the outside and recognize what it is.
- It puts the choice on the **party**, not the encounter. Vukradin's instincts will say interrogate; Soma will see the ritual; Brewbarry has Uthgardt-vs-orc history but respects ritual. The bow buys him about thirty seconds.

## Voice (immediate aftermath)

Won't speak until spoken to. When he does, broken Common, present tense:
- *"Sun hurts you. You carry. Acolyte died saying it cannot be. Was true the whole time."*
- *"My uncle blew this whistle. They killed him for it. I did not blow it. Until now."*
- If asked his name: *"Prutha. Eastern Heart. Last."* (rolls the *r* — name is the only thing he says clearly)

Calls the Talosians **thunder-eaters** — Eastern Heart word for "those who consume their own."

## What he unlocks

- **Contrast with Shrimpy.** A free orc Lathanderian in the temple vs. Shrimpy caged in Harbin's custody — Vukradin's "Eastern Homeland Reclaiming" framing finally has an orc voice attached to it.
- **Map intel.** Can name the surviving Eastern Heart bands and where they're scattered — useful when the party tries to repopulate the Icespire region post-Cryovain.
- **A second-tier convert for Valphine's church.** Brin and Giles are devout humans. Prutha is the first proof that the *Searing Pain of Justice* doctrine generalizes — pain-as-honest-worship works for any creature whose god hurts to serve. Theological payoff for Valphine's whole arc.
- **The bone whistle** as a minor sacred item — Eastern Heart dawn-prayer integrated into Valphine's heterodox liturgy.
- **Adabra-bait.** A converted orc walking around Phandalin is exactly the kind of "interventionist abomination" Adabra's faction will weaponize. Free political fuel.

## Risks at the table

- Don't let the conversion be free — at least one player will want to kill him. The bow has to read as ritual, not begging.
- If the dice say he dies before the bow lands, his whistle on the ground is also a complete beat — the doctrine survives him.
