# Lodge Orcs — Eastern Heart breakaway band

*Status: staging notes (notes/, not canon). Tribal identity, displacement history, and cultural elements are **canonical** (sourced from `notes/npcs/prutha.md`). Specific band name, leader, numbers, and current disposition are **Claude-proposed** pending DM confirmation before going to the table.*

---

## Quick reference

- **Identity:** A surviving band of the **Eastern Heart tribe** — the orc clan displaced from the Icespire foothills ~1 year ago when Cryovain seized the Hold.
- **Current location:** Occupying Falcon's Hunting Lodge as refugees, not as a war party. They squatted; they did not raid.
- **Composition:** Whole-tribe presence (warriors, women, elders, children) — not a strike force. They have nowhere else to go.
- **Posture:** Defensive. They expect to die soon and they're trying to choose where.
- **Relationship to cult:** Refused Talosian coercion when the Anchorites came offering "alliance." This is the band that said no.
- **Relationship to party:** Don't know the party exists yet, or have only heard rumors of "the dragon-killers from the south." Either way, the party killed Cryovain — the dragon that took their home — though the orcs may not have processed this fact as relevant to them yet.

## Canonical history (from `notes/npcs/prutha.md`)

- The **Eastern Heart tribe** lived in the Icespire foothills (northeast spur of Icespire Peak).
- Approximately 1 year ago, **Cryovain seized the Hold**, killing the orc war chief and forcing the survivors to scatter into the foothills and Neverwinter Wood.
- **Anchorites of Talos** moved on the displaced bands, offering "alliance" in exchange for service. Their method was consistent: kill the shaman, name the survivors *thunder-meat*, force the rest into the fold.
- **Some bands accepted** (became Cryovain's cult-aligned footsoldiers — Yargath's patrol, the orcs serving Grannoc at the Manse, the orcs who were originally at Icespire Hold under cult guidance).
- **Some bands refused** — including Prutha's band (whose shaman was murdered) and **at least one other intact band**: the one now holding Falcon's Lodge.
- Prutha's band was effectively destroyed by the conscription; he is one of the last. The Lodge band is one of the **surviving Eastern Heart bands** he can name — Prutha can tell the party who they are if asked.

## Cultural elements (canonical)

These are real and load-bearing. The party can use them to make first contact survivable.

- **The dawn-doctrine:** *"The dawn comes for everyone — even those born in shadow."* Spoken by every Eastern Heart shaman. The doctrine the Talosians beat out of the cult-aligned bands. The Lodge band still holds it.
- **The bone whistle:** A small carved-bone whistle every Eastern Heart adult carries. Blown at sunrise. Used in greeting after surviving hard nights. Prutha kept his after his uncle's murder.
- **The dawn-bow:** Right fist to left shoulder, head down. Ritual greeting and submission gesture. **Not surrender** — it's the gesture of someone who survived the night.
- **The slur:** Eastern Heart orcs call the cult-aligned orcs **"thunder-eaters"** — those who consume their own. The Lodge band will use this word when describing the Talosian-aligned bands.
- **Language:** Orcish primary; the warriors and elders speak broken Common; the war-chief candidates and shaman-equivalents would have been schooled in some Common for trade. The party's Speak with Animals / Universal Speech / Tongues options all apply if they want to skip the language barrier.

## What the band looks like — Claude-proposed

(All names, numbers, and personalities below are **Claude-proposed**. Replace with canon or modify before running.)

- **Band size:** Roughly **40 souls**. ~15 warriors of fighting age (some wounded), ~10 women, ~10 elders/non-combatants, ~5 children. Not a war party; a refugee camp.
- **Acting war chief:** **Vorga Long-Memory** — a middle-aged orc woman, ~40, formerly the war chief's second. Took command after the war chief died at Icespire. Pragmatic, exhausted, fluent in broken Common. Has been trying to keep her band alive without picking a side that will get them killed faster.
- **Acting shaman:** **None.** Their shaman was killed by Talosians during the coercion attempt that the band refused. The doctrine survives orally through the elders. **This is a vacuum Valphine could fill, theologically — or could be filled by an Eastern Heart survivor from another band.**
- **Notable elder:** **Old Drubbak** — a war-veteran elder who remembers the Hold before Cryovain. He's the band's living memory. If anyone has the standing to authorize a "we go home" decision, it's him.
- **The dangerous one:** **Kresh Stormfist** — a young warrior, ~20, hot-blooded, lost a sibling to the Talosians. Wants to die fighting cult orcs, doesn't trust Vorga's caution. **Potential flashpoint in any negotiation.** Could plausibly be a faction of one inside the band who'd defect to a more aggressive path.

## What they want

In rough priority order:

1. **Survive the next storm.** They've seen Talosian storms before. They know what's coming. They're rationing food and waiting.
2. **A place to live.** The Lodge is a stolen home, and they know it. They'd give it back for somewhere safe.
3. **Their dead avenged.** Specifically the war chief, the shaman, and any siblings/parents lost during the displacement and the Talosian coercion. They do not yet know Cryovain is dead — when they learn it, this priority shifts dramatically. The dragon was the original wound; the Talosians are the festering one.
4. **To go home.** Icespire Hold is their actual ancestral fortress. **They believe it is still held by Cryovain.** They have not heard otherwise. The path home is technically clear (dragon dead, Stone-Cold Reavers thinned, fortress empty), but they don't know this — they are living in a world where home is still impossible. The party brings the news that makes home possible again. That is a gift bigger than the immediate alliance.

The party can offer them all four of these. **This is rich diplomatic territory.**

## What they know about the cult

- **Anchorites of Talos** are the enemy. Came as recruiters; killed shamans; took warriors; left wreckage.
- **"Thunder-eaters"** is what they call orcs who said yes.
- **Grannoc** is a name they would know — he was one of the recruiters during the displacement, and is now at the Woodland Manse. He's killed Eastern Heart orcs personally.
- **Gorthok** — they call it **"the leashed thing"** or **"storm-tusk."** They've never seen it but they know the cult summons it. Their stories say the storm follows it and lightning runs along its tusks.
- **The Chosen ("K.")** — likely unknown to them by name, but they may know there is *someone* above the Anchorites. The "boy from the eastern clan" reference in Flenz's letter is **almost certainly someone they know** — a member of an Eastern Heart band that said yes. If Vorga or Old Drubbak hears about the recruit, expect a reaction.

## What they know about the party

The party killed Cryovain only 3–4 days ago. **The forest does not know yet. The Lodge band does not know yet.** They have no rumor mill, no Phandalin gossip line, no Sending stones. The dragon's death has not reached them. Plan accordingly:

- "The drow cleric of the southern town." Valphine's reputation as a drow Lathanderian *may* have reached them in fragments via traders or scouts — drow news travels fast because it's alarming — but they have no detail. This is unsettling to the Lodge orcs (drow = bad omen) but not actively hostile.
- **They do not know Cryovain is dead.** They do not know who killed him. They do not know there is a small adventuring party from Phandalin that has been clearing the region. To them, **Cryovain is still alive in the Hold, still the reason they can't go home.** This is the load-bearing emotional fact of their daily life when the party walks up.
- **They do not associate the party with anything specific.** No reputation, no rumor, no expectation. The party is "the armed group at our gate." This means the party has to *earn* every inch of credibility — but it also means there is no hostile baggage to dig out from under.
- **Prutha:** If Prutha walks in with the party, **Vorga and Old Drubbak both recognize him.** He's "the boy from Bratha's band" (Bratha = Prutha's great-uncle, the murdered shaman). His presence changes everything about how the first contact goes — they will not attack a group that travels with one of their own. **He is the party's diplomatic key.**

### The "Cryovain is dead" reveal — a real beat

Because they don't know, **telling them is a scene, not a sentence.** They will not believe it on words. The party has to produce **physical proof from Cryovain's body** — a claw, a scale, the breath pouch (all of which the party harvested at Icespire Hold). Old Drubbak holds the relic, smells it, sits down on the ground. The non-combatants go quiet. Vorga needs a minute.

**This is the moment Vorga decides she can trust the party.** Until this beat lands, treat her as guarded and transactional. After it lands, she will defend the Lodge alongside the party without hesitation.

## How the first contact plays — depending on approach

### If the party shows up unannounced and armed

Vorga's warriors are at the palisade with weapons up. The non-combatants are inside the lodge. They will not attack first; they will demand the party explain themselves; if the party speaks Orcish or speaks through Prutha, they'll listen. If the party tries to enter the lodge by force without parley, they fight.

### If Prutha walks point

Prutha does the dawn-bow at the palisade. The warriors recognize the gesture; one of them returns it; Vorga comes out and asks Prutha what band he's from. **The party gets a parley.** This is the easy mode.

### If the party uses Vukradin's Universal Speech / a Tongues spell

They can negotiate cold. Vorga is pragmatic enough to listen. The conversation is slower and more brittle without Prutha; expect rolls.

### If the party comes in with banners / Lord's Alliance optics

The Lodge orcs assume they're being hunted. Hostility climbs. **Avoid Ser Kaelen visibility in this scene** — the Order of the Gauntlet is exactly the kind of organization that has killed Eastern Heart bands before, and Vorga will read his presence as a kill team.

## The diplomatic offers the party can make

In ascending order of weight:

1. **Truce + warning.** "A storm is coming. We will help you defend or evacuate."
2. **Help defending against the Counterattack.** Genuine alliance for the duration of the cult threat. Vorga accepts immediately if she trusts the party.
3. **Falcon's Lodge as a temporary refuge under written agreement.** Falcon would resist this, but he's currently in Phandalin and Vukradin holds his goodwill. Could be brokered.
4. **Icespire Hold returned.** The party currently holds it. Vukradin's stolen-goods ethic *requires* this — the hoard was stolen from these people. Returning the Hold resolves the dragon hoard problem (they can have the ice-block; it's theirs) AND gives the band a home AND closes Vukradin's planar-theorist bottleneck. **This is the maximalist offer and it costs the party almost nothing they wanted to keep.**

The Lord's Alliance / Jenna Roscoe will hate option 4. Ser Kaelen will find it awkward. The Emerald Enclave (Lyra) will be cautiously supportive. Adabra would call it interventionist abomination, which is now a feature, not a bug.

## Prutha's stake

If the Lodge band's people are killed in the Counterattack, **Prutha is the last Eastern Heart orc with the bone whistle.** That makes him the doctrine's final carrier and ends the tribe.

If the Lodge band survives, **Prutha is no longer the last** — he becomes a member of a small but real diaspora that has both a temple (Valphine's, if she chooses) and a settlement (Icespire Hold, if the party gives it).

Both outcomes are dramatic. The first is tragic-and-personal; the second is generative-and-political. The party gets to pick.

## The "boy from the eastern clan" — Claude-proposed

The Flenz correspondence references a recent Eastern Heart recruit to the cult: *"The Chosen is pleased with the boy from the eastern clan. Continue feeding him with stories. Do not yet tell him about the leash."*

This person almost certainly comes from one of the cult-aligned Eastern Heart bands — the ones that said yes. But the framing ("pleased," "feeding stories") suggests **a young orc the Chosen is grooming as something more than a footsoldier.** A potential heir to the doctrine. A future Chosen-in-training.

**Proposed identity (Claude — confirm before running):** A young warrior named **Tovrak**, originally of the same wave of displacement, whose band accepted Talosian alliance after their shaman fell. Vorga and Old Drubbak knew his mother. He went to the Talosians voluntarily after the coercion; the doctrine the Eastern Heart elders try to preserve, he gave up.

If this works, the Manse arc and the Lodge counterattack can both surface him by name. He's the cult's answer to Prutha — same starting conditions, opposite choice. The eventual K. confrontation gets a personal axis: K. has been raising Tovrak the way Valphine has been raising Prutha.
