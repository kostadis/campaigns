# Zariel's Role: The Breaking of Dr. Iymrith

## What Zariel Wants

Zariel doesn't want Dr. Iymrith — the scientist, the researcher, the intellectual who transcended being "just another big blue dragon." Zariel wants the **Doom of the Desert**. The ancient blue dragon of incomparable destructive power. A weapon for the Blood War.

Iymrith spent centuries reinventing herself. She cleaned up. Put on the lab coat. Told herself and the world she was *more*. But Zariel sees through it. To Zariel, "Dr. Iymrith" is a gangster in a nice suit. The Doom is what's underneath, and the Doom is what Zariel needs.

## Zariel's Master Plan (Stages)

1. **Expel Tiamat from Avernus** — done. Engineered the Tyranny of Dragons. Tiamat failed to enter the Prime; Zariel has Avernus to herself.
2. **Acquire the Doom of the Desert** — done. Iymrith invoked the death pact at Ascore. Her soul came to Avernus.
3. **Break Dr. Iymrith** — in progress. Strip away the scientist identity until only the Doom remains.
4. **Deploy the Doom in the Blood War** — pending. Once broken, Iymrith becomes Zariel's ultimate weapon against the demons.
5. **Execute the Elturel plan** — preparing. Drag the city of Elturel into Avernus (Descent into Avernus). The Doom would be instrumental in holding Elturel against demonic counterattack.

## The Torment-Laboratory: How Devils Break You

### The Nature of Infernal Cruelty

A demon destroys. Tears you apart, consumes you, annihilates you. That's the Abyss — chaos, destruction, entropy.

A devil corrupts. Takes the thing you value most about yourself and makes you watch it fail until you abandon it voluntarily. That's the Nine Hells — order, patience, contracts.

Zariel isn't torturing Iymrith's body. She's torturing her **self-concept**.

### The Lab's Design

Zariel gave Iymrith exactly what she asked for:

- **A laboratory** — everything a dragon-scientist could want, in form
- **Research subjects (half-dragon pixies)** — fey/dragon hybrids, her life's work made manifest
- **Equipment** — every instrument, every tool, every apparatus

And every piece of it is **almost right:**

- The equipment is slightly miscalibrated. Every formula almost works. Every experiment is close enough to maintain hope, broken enough to ensure failure.
- The half-dragon pixies aren't just mockeries — they're proof that Iymrith's methods produce monsters, not breakthroughs. They are the answer to her life's question, and the answer is: you failed.
- The aesthetic of achievement, hollowed out. A laboratory where no real work can be done. A scientist's paradise that produces nothing.

### The Mechanism

Day after day. Century-grade patience. Zariel is waiting for Iymrith to conclude **on her own** that the scientist was always a lie.

Because a devil doesn't want to force you. A devil wants you to **choose**. Zariel needs Iymrith to look at the broken equipment, the failed hybrids, the endless almost-successes, and say: *"I was always just the Doom."*

That moment of self-abandonment — freely given — is when Zariel owns her completely. The gold-anchor protects Iymrith's soul from being **claimed**. It doesn't protect her identity from being **surrendered**. If Iymrith gives up the scientist and embraces the Doom by choice, Zariel wins even against the anchor.

### Why It Hasn't Worked (Yet)

Iymrith is brilliant, proud, and stubborn. She has not broken. But the lab chips away at her:

- Every failed experiment whispers: *you were never a scientist*
- Every mocking pixie says: *this is what your genius produces*
- Every almost-functional instrument says: *close doesn't count, and close is all you'll ever get*

The longer she stays, the more the Doom leaks through the cracks in Dr. Iymrith.

## Iymrith's Escape as Identity Survival

Iymrith's plan to escape Avernus (via Glazhael subverting the Myrkul ritual) is not just a jailbreak. It's **existential self-defense.**

She is fleeing the thing that is trying to unmake who she chose to be.

Her obsession with rewriting history — using the supercharged Ruinstone to undo her mercy toward the 3HPP — is about proving the showcase at Ascore **would have worked**. If she can go back and kill the 3HPP before they conned her, then:

- The investor showcase succeeds
- The fey/dragon hybrids are validated
- The giant kings survive as her patrons
- Her reputation as a scientist is cemented
- **Dr. Iymrith was real. The Doom was a phase she outgrew.**

The temporal rewrite isn't just revenge. It's Iymrith proving Zariel wrong.

## Implications for the Epic Tier

### Zariel's Posture Toward the Party

Zariel's stance depends on where she is in the breaking process:

**If she still thinks she can break Iymrith:**
- She opposes the party — they're trying to kill her future Blood War weapon
- She blocks entry to Avernus, or demands a devil's bargain for access
- She might try to use the party's presence as another torment tool ("even mortals have come to end you")

**If she's concluded Iymrith can't be broken:**
- She facilitates the party — Iymrith is a failed project cluttering up Avernus before the Elturel operation
- She lets them in, maybe even helps, but extracts a price (always a price)
- Getting rid of Iymrith before Elturel is strategically clean

**If the Elturel plan is imminent:**
- Zariel is preoccupied — the party might slip in during the chaos of preparation
- Iymrith's lab becomes unmonitored territory
- Zariel doesn't care about one dragon soul when she's about to drag a city into Hell

### The Jekyll/Hyde Clock

The longer the party takes to complete the restitution and get to Avernus, the more the Doom emerges:

- **Early arrival:** Iymrith is still mostly the scientist. Defiant. Proud. Possibly even willing to negotiate. The fight is against an intelligent, calculating foe.
- **Late arrival:** The lab has done its work. Iymrith is slipping. The scientist is cracking. The Doom is showing through. The fight is against something more primal and more dangerous — but also more tragic.
- **Too late:** Dr. Iymrith is gone. The Doom of the Desert stares at them with nothing behind its eyes but destruction. Zariel has her weapon — or would, if the anchor weren't still holding. The party faces a monster that used to be a person, and the kill feels less like victory and more like mercy.

### What the Party Finds in the Lab

When the party reaches Zariel's laboratory in Avernus, the state of the lab tells them how far the breaking has progressed:

- **Notes on the walls** — Iymrith's research notes, growing increasingly erratic. Early notes are precise, scientific. Later notes are fragmentary. The last ones are claw marks.
- **The half-dragon pixies** — Are they cowering? Feral? Do they speak? Do they beg the party for help? Are they already dead, torn apart by the Doom breaking through?
- **The equipment** — Has Iymrith tried to fix it? Smashed it? Rebuilt it wrong? The state of the lab is a portrait of her mental state.
- **Iymrith herself** — Is she in dragon form or humanoid form? Is she working at a bench or crouched in a corner? Does she recognize the party as people, or as threats?

## Open Questions

1. **Has Zariel assigned wardens to the lab?** Devils monitoring the breaking process, reporting to Zariel on Iymrith's psychological state?
2. **Can the party interact with Zariel directly?** Does she appear, or is she entirely offscreen dealing with Elturel prep?
3. **Is there anything in the lab that's real?** One piece of working equipment, one successful experiment — something Iymrith clings to as proof she's still the scientist?
4. **Does the breaking progress affect Iymrith's material-plane manifestations?** When she's possessing Claugiyliamatar or operating through agents, can the party detect signs of the Doom leaking through?
5. **What happens to the Doom if Iymrith is killed after being fully broken?** Does Zariel lose a weapon, or does the Doom become something else — an unbound force in Avernus?

## The Climactic Moment: The Doom Emerges

### Zariel's Gambit

Zariel sees the party's entire quest — the escape, the failures, the restitution, the confrontation — as an opportunity. She doesn't need to break Iymrith herself. The heroes will do it for her.

Every time Iymrith fails to acquire the artifacts, it chips away at Dr. Iymrith. Every temporary win the party scores whispers: *your plan doesn't work. You can't fix the past. The showcase will never be vindicated.*

And then the restitution — returning the hoard — is the killing blow. Not to Iymrith's body, but to her identity. The gold was proof that she was powerful, that she took from the world and the world couldn't take back. When it's returned, she has nothing. No hoard, no anchor, no plan, no legacy.

She's just the Doom.

### The Moment of Choice

The final fight isn't triggered by a spell or a ritual. It's triggered by **Iymrith choosing to stop being Dr. Iymrith.**

When the restitution is complete and she realizes she's failed — that there is no do-over, no showcase, no vindication — she doesn't surrender. She doesn't negotiate. She doesn't scheme.

She becomes what Zariel always said she was.

The lab coat comes off. The scientist dies. And what's left is an ancient blue dragon of terrifying, primal, annihilating power. No plan. No restraint. No identity except destruction.

**This is freely given.** Exactly what Zariel wanted. Exactly what infernal cruelty is designed to produce.

### The Final Fight

The party doesn't fight Dr. Iymrith. They fight the **Doom of the Desert** — a dragon that has abandoned reason, strategy, and self-preservation. This is:

- Not a puzzle fight (the puzzle was the restitution)
- Not a political fight (the politics were the artifact race)
- A raw, terrifying, epic-tier combat against something that has nothing left to lose
- A dragon that is no longer trying to win — just trying to destroy everything before it dies

The party broke the anchor. That makes her killable. But it also makes her *more dangerous* in the moment — because the thing holding her back (the scientist identity, the careful plans, the self-preservation instinct tied to the hoard) is gone.

### Zariel Wins Either Way

**If the party loses:**
- The Doom kills them
- No anchor means no attachment to the material plane
- No scientist identity means no goals beyond destruction
- Zariel collects a weapon that doesn't care about anything except violence
- Perfect for the Blood War
- The Doom doesn't care about Faerun, about the 3HPP, about the showcase — only about the next thing to destroy

**If the party wins:**
- Iymrith is dead — permanently this time
- Zariel concludes: if mortals can kill her, she was never Blood War material
- The soul still exists somewhere in the infernal bureaucracy for Zariel to find and torment later
- But Zariel has bigger concerns now — Elturel is imminent
- The party has won, but they haven't beaten Zariel. They've just proved Iymrith wasn't worth Zariel's time.

### Thematic Payoff

The party's moral action (returning stolen wealth) triggers the villain's identity crisis, which creates the most dangerous version of the villain they've ever faced. The right thing to do made the fight harder — but also made it the only fight that matters. Without the restitution, they could "beat" Iymrith a hundred times and she'd always come back. With it, they face the Doom once — and if they survive, it's over forever.

Vukradin's insight (conflict-free gold) is what made the kill possible. But it's also what made the kill terrifying. The bard who believes in ethical wealth is responsible for both the solution and the escalation.

## Design Note: The Third Option

The party should have a way to **prevent Iymrith from making the choice** — to stop her from abandoning Dr. Iymrith and becoming the Doom. An alternative to the final boss fight.

### Requirements

- It must be **epic** — not a simple persuasion check or a clever trick
- It must **cost the party something insane** — this isn't a free win, it's a different kind of sacrifice
- It must be **harder to achieve than the fight itself** in some dimension (emotionally, morally, or in terms of what they give up)
- It must feel **earned** — something that could only work because of who these specific characters are and what they've done across the entire campaign
- It must **deny Zariel her win** — this is the one outcome where the house doesn't win. Zariel doesn't get the Doom, and doesn't get to dismiss Iymrith as unworthy. That's what makes it truly heroic.

### What It Might Look Like (Undetermined)

The party somehow convinces Iymrith that Dr. Iymrith was real — that the scientist wasn't a costume. Or they offer her something that proves she's more than the Doom without needing to rewrite history. Or they sacrifice something that makes the restitution unnecessary (absorbing the greed themselves?). Or they find a way to validate her work that she couldn't achieve alone.

Whatever it is:
- It has to be something Zariel didn't anticipate
- It has to be something only mortals with free will could offer
- It has to cost enough that choosing the fight would have been easier
- It should leave the party changed — not broken, but marked by what they gave up

### Why This Matters

A great campaign finale gives the players agency. The Doom fight is the "expected" climax — epic, terrifying, satisfying. But the third option is for the table that wants to do something no one saw coming. The table that finds a way to beat the devil's game entirely.
