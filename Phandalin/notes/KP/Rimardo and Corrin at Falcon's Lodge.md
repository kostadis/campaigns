Rimardo and Corrin at Falcon's Lodge

Context  
The battle at Falcon's Hunting Lodge has ended. Vukradin opened with a powerful enchantment that disabled much of the enemy force. Soma's moonbeam burned through cultists and Gorthok. Valphine used Silence on Boney's saddle to shut down Anchorite storm magic. Brewbarry and the allied orcs broke the enemy line. Gorthok died groveling under Vukradin's magic and moonlight. The final Anchorite broke under a phantasm of Lathander and confessed in terror before Falcon and Brewbarry finished him. The surviving enemy orcs fled, Falcon shot down two, Prutha executed another, and Vorga declared the party honorary members of her tribe. Valphine persuaded three defeated orcs to work for Falcon, who immediately began treating them as staff.

This is the perfect landing point for Rimardo and Corrin because the scene contains all the material their act needs: heroism, spectacle, questionable aftermath, employment paperwork, fleeing enemies shot in the back, and Vukradin accidentally becoming the most effective battlefield artist in the North.

Entrance  
Bring Rimardo and Corrin in after the celebration starts, not during the fight.

The party is bloody. Falcon is trying to look like this was all under control. Vorga has just declared them honorary orcs. Valphine has converted enemy survivors into lodge staff. Soma is already halfway to bed.

Then, from inside Falcon's Lodge, there is applause.

Not from the orcs. Not from Falcon's staff.

From inside.

Falcon frowns and says:  
"I did not book entertainment tonight."

Then Rimardo walks out carrying a wine glass, perfectly composed, with Corrin behind him holding a small placard:

INTERMISSION

The Bit  
Rimardo explains that he and Mr. Corrin were hired for post-conflict entertainment and casualty-adjacent accounting, but the battle delayed their performance.

He looks across the field: dead Anchorites, fleeing orcs, new employees, Falcon trying to become a legend, Vukradin pretending this was a moral victory, Prutha covered in righteous violence.

Rimardo says:  
"Ah. Excellent. The comedy has already begun."

Corrin silently produces a ledger titled:

Falcon's Hunting Lodge: Revised Staffing, Casualty, and Theological Liability Report

Then the routine begins as a post-battle audit.

Why This Works  
For Dave / Vukradin  
Rimardo immediately clocks Vukradin's spellcraft as performance.

Rimardo says:  
"You opened with color, silence, command, divine hallucination, and a collapsing pig-god. I withdraw all prior doubts. You are not a musician. You are a stage manager for violence."

Corrin silently applauds. Once.

For the Plot  
The ledger contains one thing that should not be there: an entry for the Boots of Elvenkind at Woodland Manse, already marked as pending recovery. That pushes the party toward the next location without forcing it.

For the Fans  
Rimardo evaluates the whole battle in old Rimardo terms: payment, liability, fate, legal classification of murder, and whether honorary tribal membership is taxable.

Best Dialogue  
Rimardo to Falcon:  
"You missed repeatedly, then struck decisively at the moment witnesses were available. That is not incompetence. That is branding."

Corrin holds up:  
HEROIC TIMING

Rimardo to Valphine:  
"You converted prisoners into staff. Efficient. Morally unstable. Very promising."

Corrin produces three tiny employee hats.

Rimardo to Vukradin:  
"You compelled a divine thunder-beast to grovel until moonlight killed it. Have you considered charging admission?"

Corrin flips a placard:  
HE HAS

Then:  
NOT ENOUGH

The Grimdark Turn  
After the jokes land, Rimardo's tone thins out.

He looks at the dead Anchorite who confessed under Vukradin's phantasm. Corrin kneels beside the body and pulls from its mouth a wet scrap of paper that was not there before. It bears a storm-mark, but the ink keeps crawling into a different shape: an eye, a spiral, or a bit of KP-style planar notation.

Rimardo says:  
"Talos was loud. That made him useful. But someone else was listening."

That gives the next-layer clue: this battle was not just Talos. The Anchorites were being pushed, observed, or modeled. Maybe by Kraken Society paranoia. Maybe by KP's planar distortions. Maybe by Adabra's escalating ideology. Rimardo does not explain. He just makes the party realize the field has another shadow.

Exit  
Falcon asks who they are.

Rimardo bows.

"Rimardo and Mr. Corrin. We perform inevitability, accounting, and selected reversals."

Corrin holds up:  
NO REFUNDS

Then they vanish into the lodge.

But the new orc stewards insist they saw Corrin leave through the front door. Falcon insists Rimardo went upstairs. Vukradin finds a ticket stub in his pocket for a performance tomorrow night.

Title on the ticket stub:  
Predestination, Plunder, and Other Legal Fictions  
Special Guest: Vukradin, unwilling opening act.
