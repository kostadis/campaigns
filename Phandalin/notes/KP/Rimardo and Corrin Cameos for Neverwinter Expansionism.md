Rimardo and Corrin Cameos for Neverwinter Expansionism

Purpose  
Use Rimardo and Corrin as a recurring comic omen: each appearance should be funny onstage, useful to the live plot, and loaded with old-campaign fan service. They are not quest-givers. They are punchlines that reveal the next layer of the plot.

Campaign Logic  
In Neverwinter Expansionism and the North, the active tensions already include Neverwinter's no-questions-asked plunder economy, Vukradin's search for honest coin, KP's Planar Distortion, Aletra's work as KP's agent, the Kraken Society's manipulation, Adabra's escalation, and the Brundar/Valphine Demonweb thread.

Rimardo and Corrin fit by becoming comedy as counterforce: Rimardo speaks in legal, theological, and economic systems; Corrin silently breaks those systems open with timing, props, receipts, knives, and impossible reveals. Their comedy is funny because it is true, and unsettling because the truth is operational.

1\. The Golden Ledger Audit  
Best first appearance.

Vukradin receives his certified conflict-free mine payout as a Letter of Credit, but the bank has hired outside auditors: Rimardo & Mr. Corrin, Divine Accountants and Occasional Entertainers.

Rimardo performs an audit of Vukradin's moral economy as a stage routine. Corrin silently produces receipts from places he should not know about: Mountain's Toe Gold Mine, Butterskull cheese logistics, Neverwinter investors, hidden optimization fees.

Why it works:  
\- Amuses Dave: Rimardo dismantles Vukradin's conflict-free gold philosophy with fake-serious accounting.  
\- Advances plot: Reveals Jena Roscoe/Lord's Alliance or Zhentarim pressure on the mine.  
\- Fan service: Classic Rimardo legalism plus Corrin silent business.

Rimardo line:  
"You have not refused dirty money. You have merely laundered it through paperwork with better penmanship."

Corrin silently stamps the ledger: COMEDICALLY COMPLIANT.

2\. The Wayside Inn Booking  
Rimardo and Corrin are booked at the Wayside Inn shortly before the undead attack/Talos reveal.

The show is ridiculous: Rimardo lectures on the legal difference between murder, assassination, and adventuring, while Corrin silently demonstrates with stage knives and volunteer cards. During the act, Corrin keeps choosing random audience members who are actually Talos cultists.

Why it works:  
\- Amuses Dave: Vukradin gets professional rivalry. These two hacks are killing with the crowd.  
\- Advances plot: Exposes Backes Dunfield or hints that the inn staff are not what they seem.  
\- Fan service: Corrin silently identifying enemies before anyone else sees the danger.

The punchline becomes a clue.

3\. The House of Thalivar Ghost Show  
This is the Miller-from-The-Expanse version.

At House of Thalivar, Vukradin hears applause from an empty room. Rimardo is on a ruined stage, mid-routine, talking to an audience that is not there. Corrin is doing silent prop work with broken pieces of the planar beacon.

Rimardo seems funny, but wrong. Repeating himself. Half alive as a performance, half divine diagnostic.

Why it works:  
\- Amuses Dave: Old character returns as a haunted bit.  
\- Advances plot: Points toward KP's Erratic Beacon, Aletra's interference, or missing planar texts.  
\- Fan service: Rimardo's comedy has become metaphysics, which is exactly the right kind of absurd.

Rimardo line:  
"The problem with KP is not that he cheats fate. Everyone cheats fate. The problem is that he itemizes the cheating."

4\. The False Mediator Performance  
During the Kraken Society arc, Rimardo and Corrin appear at a public negotiation or civic event in Leilon.

Rimardo performs a routine about peace talks as theater. Corrin silently rearranges name cards at the negotiation table. The new seating chart exposes the actual manipulation: one neutral mediator is tied to the Kraken Society, or Captain Tharros's advice is compromised.

Why it works:  
\- Amuses Dave: The bit is literally diplomatic table magic.  
\- Advances plot: Reveals the Kraken Society's False Mediator move.  
\- Fan service: Corrin solves the problem without speaking; Rimardo takes credit.

Corrin's placard:  
THIS MAN IS NOT NEUTRAL

Then he flips it:  
NEITHER IS THIS ONE

Then he points to Rimardo, who says:  
"That is slander. Accurate slander."

5\. The Aletra/KP Interruption  
When Aletra appears as KP's agent, Rimardo and Corrin should intrude on the scene as unwanted counter-programming.

Aletra talks about efficiency, stabilization, routing, and social cohesion as a secondary concern. Rimardo appears as if continuing a show from another room:

"And now, ladies and gentlemen, the classic gnomish trick: making consequences disappear\!"

Corrin silently opens an empty box. Inside is a displaced object from the party's past, proving KP's optimization has already altered something personal.

Why it works:  
\- Amuses Dave: Rimardo heckles KP's entire worldview.  
\- Advances plot: Makes Planar Distortion concrete.  
\- Fan service: Rimardo vs KP feels like unfinished old-party business.

6\. The Final No-Audience Routine  
At the Drowned Gate / Interplanar Crossroads climax, there is no crowd. Just Vukradin, the party, Rimardo, and Corrin.

Rimardo admits the routine has been hijacked by Istus as counterforce against KP. Comedy is how fate reintroduces contingency into a system KP is trying to optimize.

Corrin silently lays out three cards:  
CONTROL  
FATE  
JOKE

Then removes the middle card.

That is the decision point. The party cannot simply pick fate against KP's system. They need the joke: the unpredictable act, the artistic refusal, the human-scale choice that KP's model cannot absorb.

Why it works:  
\- Amuses Dave: The old comedy act becomes cosmically important.  
\- Advances plot: Clarifies Istus vs KP.  
\- Fan service: Rimardo and Corrin's epilogue matters without them stealing the campaign.

Use Principle  
Rimardo and Corrin should enter funny, leave unsettling, and every time Dave laughs, something important should click.

The structural rule: Rimardo explains the system, Corrin breaks the system, and Vukradin recognizes the break as art.
