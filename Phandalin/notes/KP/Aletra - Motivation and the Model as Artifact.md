# Aletra \- Motivation and the Model as Artifact

# Core Thesis

Aletra is working for KP because she intends to steal the model.

She does not see KP's work as a theory, a paper, or a neutral research instrument. She sees it as an artifact: a rare constructed object that grants leverage over reality to whoever can wield it.

KP thinks he is building a model.

Aletra sees a magical artifact without a vault.

Her goal is not multiversal optimization. She lacks KP's visionary scope. She does not want to save the multiverse from stagnation, build a general theory of circulation, or reduce suffering at scale. She wants the power her family lost, and she recognizes KP's model as a tool that can help her take it back.

# Family Context

Aletra is the sister of Valphine and Sartorra, part of a Drow family whose world collapsed after Lolth's clerics weaponized the Faerzress.

The Faerzress had been treated as a condition of the Underdark: magical background, environmental constraint, and practical fact. It inhibited teleportation and shaped movement, secrecy, defense, and power. Then the clerics discovered that this condition was also a lever. They refocused the Faerzress to free Lolth from prison, and Aletra's world was broken by the consequences.

That history did not teach Aletra caution. It taught her recognition.

When she sees KP's model, she understands the pattern immediately. A hidden structure has become legible. A thing everyone else thinks is descriptive has become usable. The question is not whether it is dangerous. The question is who will hold it first.

# The Artifact Frame

Aletra treats KP's model like a powerful magical artifact.

As a Drow noble, she understands artifacts differently than KP does. Artifacts are never only what their wielder thinks they are. Their apparent use is always smaller than their actual consequence. They have hidden costs, secondary effects, histories, hungers, and meanings that exceed the person holding them.

KP has made an artifact out of information.

The model's obvious use is economic: predict flows, route trade, reduce shocks, manage hoards, and optimize circulation.

Its deeper use is political: make choice legible enough to steer.

Aletra does not need KP's theory. She needs his instrument.

# Why She Works for KP

Aletra works for KP because proximity is the path to theft.

From inside KP's project, she can learn the model, identify its dependencies, map its control points, and understand what KP does not know he has built. She can present herself as useful, loyal, practical, and security-minded while quietly positioning herself to take the artifact when it becomes usable.

Her outer role is collaborator, assistant, analyst, and Drow political interpreter.

Her inner agenda is theft.

She wants to be the first person who controls the model. Working for KP is how she steals it from everyone else: KP, the Sartorras, Savras, hostile powers, and any institution slow enough to mistake access for ownership.

# Aletra's Evil

Aletra's evil is not visionary tyranny. It is possessive ambition.

KP is dangerous because he sees too much system and too little person.

Aletra is dangerous because she sees too little system and too much instrument.

She does not want to optimize the world. She wants a weapon sharp enough to restore family power. She knows the model is larger than she is, but she believes that is precisely why it must be taken before others take it first.

Her moral failure is not that she misunderstands the model's danger. Her moral failure is that she understands enough of the danger and still wants it.

# The Axehome Poison Experiment

The movement of poison through Axehome was an early experiment in the power of the artifact.

It was not only a criminal operation, assassination attempt, or logistical scheme. It was a proof of concept.

Aletra was testing whether the model could be used to move a dangerous substance through a complex social and physical environment while hiding intent, exploiting incentives, predicting responses, and routing consequences onto others.

In miniature, Axehome showed what the model could do:

* identify channels  
* predict behavior  
* exploit trust  
* move harm through legitimate routes  
* mask agency behind ordinary transactions  
* turn a local system against itself

To KP, the same kind of movement might look like logistics, circulation, or systems behavior.

To Aletra, it looked like artifact attunement.

The poison was not the true weapon. The route was.

# Relationship to KP

KP does not understand Aletra because he assumes the central danger is intellectual misuse or bad governance.

Aletra's danger is more direct. She does not need to win the argument. She needs access.

KP thinks trust means intellectual partnership. Aletra treats trust as a corridor.

KP thinks the model's danger lies in what it might reveal. Aletra understands that its danger lies in what it lets a wielder do.

# Relationship to the Sartorras

The Sartorras see what KP is doing. Aletra knows this.

That does not make her oppose them from moral principle. It makes her race them.

Her family connection makes the conflict intimate. She is not merely stealing from KP for herself. She is trying to seize the artifact before her siblings, rivals, or kin can define the terms of its use. Whether she intends to restore House power, outmaneuver her family, or become indispensable to them can remain flexible in play.

The important point is that Aletra's ambition is dynastic and personal, not universal.

She wants the lost power back.

# Key Lines

KP thinks he built a model. Aletra knows he built an artifact.

I do not need his vision. I need his instrument.

The poison was not the true weapon. The route was.

Loyalty is a proximity strategy.

You call it a model because you are a scholar. I call it an artifact because I know what power looks like before men give it a shrine.
