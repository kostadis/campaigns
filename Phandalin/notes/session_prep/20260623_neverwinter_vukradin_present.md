**Table time:** full session (~3–4 hrs). **Party:** full (Vukradin, Soma, Valphine, Brewbarry + Boney; Prutha optional). **Player level:** 7. **Directive:** stage every Vukradin payoff as a *branch*, not a rail — land whichever the table grabs.

## What changed since the last prep

The original doc was built on a single structural joke: *the city has opinions about a man who isn't here.* Almost every load-bearing payoff was deliberately deferred to "when Dave is present." Dave is now present. So this session inverts:

> **Old spine:** Neverwinter forms opinions about an absent Vukradin.
> **New spine:** Vukradin walks into a city that has *already decided who he is* — as an artist, as an economic actor, and (through Orsik) as a legend he didn't author and can't correct in person.

Four threads that were "held" are now **live and resolvable this session:**
1. **The Golden Ledger Audit** — Rimardo & Corrin audit Vukradin's conflict-free finances (NEW scene; see Scene V2). This is the comedy centerpiece.
2. **Neverember + the studio** — the meeting can actually happen and close. The "deflationary bard" texture now lands *on Dave*, in real time.
3. **Orsik** — the Lord's Alliance propaganda finally has its trip-wire at the table (Scene V3). Circlet truth stays locked behind Parnast.
4. **Cullen Sharpe's Margaster distribution pitch** — now delivered to Vukradin's face, not relayed (Scene 2 overlay).

Everything else from the companion doc still runs as written. Vukradin's presence just adds a *reactor* to each existing scene — noted per-scene below.

## The Win Condition (Dave-present version)

The session lands when **Vukradin has been forced, at least once, to feel the gap between the man he is and the man Neverwinter has decided he is** — and the party has made one choice that commits them inside the city's factional board. The comedy is the delivery mechanism; the weight is identity. Vukradin came here for clean money and a studio. The city wants to give him both — on terms that quietly indict his entire philosophy. He doesn't have to accept. He does have to *see it clearly.*

Not a session they solve. A session that takes Vukradin's worldview out of the drawer and turns it over in the light.

Carry over all five bullets from the companion doc (fashion district, Margaster's second approach, Lathanderite inquiry, Hazel's paper, Neverember's second invitation), **plus:**

## The Clever Play (what the optimal party would do)

1. **Redeem the Letter of Credit and survive the audit with his philosophy intact-but-bruised.** The optimal Vukradin doesn't win the argument with Rimardo (nobody wins the argument with Rimardo) — he takes the hit, laughs, and *spends something on purpose.* The studio down-payment is the move. Circulation as a choice, not a surrender.
2. **Take the Neverember meeting — and negotiate, not accept.** Neverember solves the deflationary-bard problem *for* Vukradin by funding the studio. The clever read is to notice he's being solved, and to take the deal on terms that keep the studio his and not the Lord Protector's soft-power asset.
3. **Clock the Orsik propaganda and ask who's pushing it, not whether it's true.** The win isn't debunking Orsik this session (can't — that's Parnast). It's recognizing the *machine* lionizing him, and that the same machine's map has a dotted line through Phandalin.
4. **Tell Vukradin about Elara before he hears Cullen's pitch as flattery.** The party holds knowledge Vukradin doesn't: the house offering to "handle his distribution" murdered the woman they came to help. The clever play is to brief him *first.*
5. Everything Soma/Valphine/Brewbarry from the companion doc still applies — follow the staff, let the golden eyes do political work, let Brewbarry own or be consumed by the Exile Aesthetic.

## Scene-by-scene overlay (what Vukradin's presence changes)

### Scene 1 — The Exile Aesthetic → now the author is in the room
Run the companion doc's arrival beat. **The flip:** the young fan worshipping "Vukradin's early work, before he went commercial" is now standing in front of *Vukradin.*
- **The comedy:** the fan does not recognize him. Vukradin is older, cleaner, politically embedded — he reads as exactly the "sellout" the movement defines itself against. If he announces himself, the fan's first instinct is *disbelief, then disappointment.* "No — the real Vukradin would never wear that coat."
- **The weight:** the city built a philosophy out of his worst, loneliest year and then decided the actual him is an impostor of it. He cannot reclaim his own early work without sounding like he's bragging.
- **Branch — Vukradin performs to prove it's him:** dangerous and great. A real Vukradin performance in the street either converts the crowd (he reclaims authorship — let it be a genuine win) or overshoots into "polished, therefore fake" (the crowd decides the *impostor* is a good cover act). Let Dave choose the register; reward sincerity over flash.
- **Branch — Vukradin lets it ride / says nothing:** he learns what it is to be a genre other people perform. File the ache; it pays off at Neverember.
- Brewbarry's fashion-icon beat from the companion doc runs underneath, unchanged. The two of them being recognized *as a movement neither consented to* is the scene.

### Scene 2 — The Mermaid Runs (Dead on Delivery) → Cullen pitches Vukradin to his face
Run Cassian/statue/Margaster exactly as written (Elara is dead; Cassian carries her threads; the Bimble Nackle gnome-trap stays planted — **do not surface Bimble**).

## Scene V2 — The Golden Ledger Audit (NEW — the centerpiece) {.feature}

> Flagged in `notes/KP/Rimardo and Corrin Cameos for Neverwinter Expansionism.md` as the **best** R&C Neverwinter first appearance. This is the scene the Dave-absent prep could not run. Build the session around being *able* to reach it.

### Setup
**Where:** A Neverwinter counting house / chartered bank in the Protector's Enclave (the party planned a Mountain's Toe payday en route — resolve it here instead: the mine's commission and Vukradin's cut are issued as a **certified conflict-free Letter of Credit**, redeemable in Neverwinter. This is cleaner than coin and very on-theme: even his "clean" money is now an abstraction the city controls.)
**Trigger:** the moment Vukradin tries to redeem, convert, or even just *verify* the Letter of Credit.

### The bit
The bank has retained outside auditors before releasing certified conflict-free instruments: **Rimardo & Mr. Corrin, Divine Accountants and Occasional Entertainers.** They are *expecting* him. There is a small folding table. There is a queue of zero other people. There is, somehow, a velvet rope.

Rimardo audits Vukradin's **moral economy** as a stage routine. Corrin silently produces receipts from places he could not possibly have them — Mountain's Toe payouts, Butterskull cheese logistics, the Neverwinter investors circling his early compositions, "hidden optimization fees" nobody itemized.

Lift verbatim if it lands:
> **Rimardo:** *"You have not refused dirty money. You have merely laundered it through paperwork with better penmanship."*

> **Corrin** silently stamps the ledger: **COMEDICALLY COMPLIANT.**

### What it reveals (the punchline is the clue)
Pick the layer most active for your table when they arrive — Corrin's receipts expose **one** real thing:
- **Default pick:** the Lord's Alliance (via Jenna Roscoe) or Zhentarim-adjacent pressure is already attached to the mine's revenue — Vukradin's "conflict-free" payout has a string he didn't sign. (Sets up Orsik/Alliance in Scene V3 and Neverember.)
- Alt: a Margaster fee is *already* embedded in the Letter of Credit's routing — the murderers are skimming his clean money and he didn't know. (Sets up Scene 2's pitch as already-true.)
- Alt: KP's "optimization fee" appears as a line item nobody can source — the planar-distortion footprint, itemized. Corrin's knife goes through the words "borrowed mathematics." (Sets up the KP through-line; resonates with the Woodland Manse playbill.)

### Branches
- **Vukradin argues the philosophy:** he loses — gracefully, and the loss is the gift. Rimardo's whole function is to show how the trick is done, which is how he breaks it. Let Dave score *points* (he's an Eloquence bard) but never the *match.*
- **Vukradin plays along / out-performs them:** best outcome. Rimardo respects a fellow professional. This is where the "perform with you at the very end, for one gold coin" promise (ch44) gets a callback — Corrin can slide a single gold coin across the table, unprompted, and take it back.
- **The party tries to grab/interrogate/dispel Corrin:** he cannot be captured, dispelled, or interrogated (he is "not present in any way that admits capture" — CounterForce doctrine). The grab passes through the bit. Don't make it a combat; make it a *genre error* the party commits and the scene shrugs off.
- **Vukradin refuses to redeem at all:** then the Letter of Credit stays a number, and Rimardo stamps it **HOARDED — SEE PAPER NO. 27** and tips his hat. The deflationary-bard thesis just got notarized.

### NPC notes
- **Rimardo** — speaks fluent system (legal/theological/economic). Enters funny, leaves unsettling. Every time Dave laughs, something should click. He is the *voice* of the CounterForce; he reveals the mechanism.
- **Corrin** — silent, prop-and-receipt work, the *hands* of the CounterForce. Every clue comes from Corrin, never Rimardo's mouth. Tone rule: **joke first, dread second.** The party laughs before they worry.

## Scene V3 — Orsik on the Broadsheet (NEW activation — Dave's stone in the road) {.feature}

> The companion doc explicitly held this for Dave: *"the person who should trip on it is Vukradin."* He's here. Let him trip.
> **Ground truth (`notes/neverwinter/history_of_neverwinter.md`):** The Lord's Alliance needs a mortal face for the Giant War and has chosen **Orsik** — Dave's prior PC. Their message: *institutions win wars; Orsik proves it.* What Neverwinter *can* learn here: Orsik went home to Parnast, took back his guard captaincy, sought no title. **The circlet secret (he left the Headband of Intellect behind) is NOT available in Neverwinter.** It requires going to Parnast and earning it from **Vardis** (Wade's prior PC) face-to-face. Do not leak it here by hint, NPC, or document.

### The activation
Run the companion doc's Orsik monument + Lord's Alliance herald beat — the plaque (*"Liberators of the Ordning… through the combined strength of the Lord's Alliance…"*), the **ORSIK — CHAMPION OF THE NORTH** broadsheet, and the back-page map with a dotted jurisdiction line through Neverwinter Wood, Leilon, and **Phandalin.**
- In the absent version, only Valphine noticed. Now: **Vukradin reads his own prior character's face on Lord's Alliance propaganda, in a city extending its reach toward his home town.**
- **Hold the table.** Do NOT narrate significance, do NOT say "you recognize him," do NOT explain the metanarrative. Put the broadsheet in Dave's hands and stop talking.

### Branches
- **Vukradin engages the herald:** the herald is a true believer in the institutional story and will happily expand it. He does not know about the circlet; he believes every word. Vukradin can argue, and the herald will be *politely immovable* — institutions, resources, legitimacy, that's why Orsik won. (Vukradin, of all people, arguing that an *individual* made the difference, against the institutional line — that's the character collision. Let Dave feel it.)
- **Vukradin asks who funds/prints this:** points toward the Lord's Alliance soft-power machine — same apparatus Jenna Roscoe works for, same machine whose map eats Phandalin. Connect it to Scene V2's revealed string on the mine money if you ran that layer.
- **Vukradin goes looking for the counter-narrative:** the underground **Stroudite reading** (individual agency made the hero; no institution can account for either his choice to fight *or* his choice to stop) circulates in a back room. Candidate venue: a back room at the **Fallen Tower** (neutral-zone tavern) — *this is an open decision, see below.* The Stroudite contact gives him the *visible-facts* argument only: Orsik went home, made no announcement, just stopped. **Not the circlet.** That door is in Parnast.

### Stage direction
The full dramatic weight is Dave's, and most of it is *deferred to Parnast* — where his two characters finally meet through Vardis. This session is the *itch,* not the scratch. The right outcome is Vukradin leaving Neverwinter wanting to go to Parnast and not fully knowing why. If Wade (Soma) catches the Vardis name and reacts, that's his own prior PC surfacing — stay out of the way; that reunion is also Parnast-only and cannot be rumored into existence.

## Scene V4 — Neverember & the Studio (NEW — now resolvable; the thematic spine)

> Companion doc delivers the *invitation* (steward **Aldus Hern**, lion livery). With Dave present the *meeting* can happen and close. Stage it for the back third of the session, after V2 and at least one win.

### The pitch
Lord Dagult Neverember wants to **fund Vukradin's music studio in Phandalin.** Framed as cultural patronage. Actually: the city solving the deflationary-bard problem by getting Vukradin to circulate wealth on the one thing he genuinely wants. Neverember is smooth, transactional, and not wrong about anything.

### What Neverember actually wants (GM-only)
- A visible, fundable cultural asset in the Phandalin hinterland = soft Neverwinter/Alliance presence on the same map the Orsik broadsheet drew a dotted line across. The studio is a flag.
- A bard who owes him a favor and circulates wealth on his terms. Solving "the Vukradin problem" and acquiring an asset are the same move.
- He will, at least once, make the dragon comparison Aldus Hern foreshadowed — gently, to watch Vukradin's face.

### Branches
- **Accept as offered:** studio gets built; Vukradin is now, structurally, a Neverember/Alliance cultural asset with a string. The deflationary bard finally circulates — by becoming what he distrusts. Played straight, this is a real bittersweet win, not a trap-sprung "gotcha." Let it cost something quiet.
- **Negotiate (clever play):** Vukradin takes funding while keeping ownership/programming independent — no Alliance branding, no veto on who performs. Neverember will concede surprisingly much; he cares about the *flag existing*, less about the fine print. Reward the negotiation.
- **Decline:** the studio stays unbuilt, Vukradin stays "poor," his principle stays intact, and Neverember files him as a known quantity. Honest to the character. Don't punish it — but the Candlekeep thesis gets the last word, and that ache is the point.
- **Counter — Vukradin asks Neverember to fund it with the Cryovain hoard / something he refuses to touch:** interesting jiu-jitsu. Let Dave invent; Neverember is a dealmaker and will engage any structure that ends with a studio standing.

### NPC notes
- **Aldus Hern** — twenty years of arranging meetings for powerful people; patience of stone; the most gently devastating man at the table. He carries the Candlekeep pamphlet and does not produce it unless it'll land.
- **Neverember** (if met) — not a villain. A competent ruler who has correctly diagnosed Vukradin and is offering him exactly what he wants in exchange for exactly what the city needs. The discomfort is that the deal is *fair.*
