# The Boar Who Was Already Booked

*Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the first Rimardo & Corrin appearance, at Falcon's Lodge, the night after the Gorthok siege. Spine and cosmology locked in conversation 2026-05-31; the elevated KP/CounterForce canon those rest on is staged but **not yet written into `KP.md` / `CounterForce.md` / `Aletra.md`** — treat the cosmology notes here as the source of truth until those dossiers are updated.*

The siege is **won** (Ch. 43): Gorthok dead, last Anchorite broken, enemy orcs fled, party now **7th level**, Falcon converted to interventionism and bossing three new orc "stewards," Vukradin gone to nap in the luxury guest room, wine casks destroyed. ## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

By end of scene the table should have: **(1)** laughed — genuinely, first; **(2)** gotten unsettled — *how do these two hacks know our whole story, and the Lodge's real story?*; and **(3)** acquired, without being quest-given, a **map of the next phase**: a coastal town, the storm-cult they already know touring there again, an attack from the water, something sealed beneath the town, an inn on the road there. Vukradin (Dave) should privately clock that *the bit is too well-built to be improvised — it's a map.* That's the landing. **KP is the answer to a question the players don't know to ask yet** — do not let them ask it tonight.

**Hard guardrail:** Planar Distortion threshold 6 = "KP philosophy voiced openly by an NPC." R&C must stay **below** that. They gesture at a headliner nobody announced; they never name him, never say "the Sage," never touch Aletra, never say cage/Codex/Mists. Trip that line and you've spent the slow burn on night one.

## The Default Trajectory (if the party does nothing / just watches the show)

- The act lands, the party laughs, the dread creeps, R&C leave before anyone can corner them. **This is a fine outcome** — they're an omen, not an encounter.
- The party still has every existing reason to head up the High Road (Neverwinter statue, Wayside Inn where Teega the Smith sits Kraken-compromised) — **Leilon is on that road.** The bit makes that route ominous instead of routine.
- Underneath, unseen: Kraken Society (Syrkloc, Vell'thyk) keep excavating the Drowned Gate beneath Leilon; Talos and Myrkul cults move toward open war over the town; the Ruinstone surfaces. R&C just told the party all of this as a comedy routine and the party will only realize it later.

## The Clever Play (so you know when to nudge — not a script for them)

1. **Write down what Corrin shows.** The silent reveals *are* the clue list. A party that records the card-flips has the Leilon phase pre-mapped.
2. **Notice the contradiction with Falcon.** The bit re-confirms "*one lodge, seized by the displaced*" — which already cracked Falcon's occupation story. A sharp party re-interrogates Falcon.
3. **Don't try to capture or interrogate R&C.** They can't be held (see NPC notes). A party that *tries* gets the best dread beat — the failure to pin them is the point.
4. **Connect "the headliner" to nothing yet.** There's nothing to connect it to. The correct player move is to file it, uneasy. Reward that later.

## Scene 1 — The Meal

### Setup
- **When/where:** That night, the Lodge's long hall. Late in Taraksh, 1495 DR. Storm spent; the air has that scrubbed-clean post-thunder stillness.
- **Present:** Vukradin (asleep upstairs until summoned — see below), Soma, Valphine, Brewbarry; Falcon playing lord-of-the-manor; the three new orc stewards being drilled on cutlery; Vorga's Eastern Heart Clan loud and bloody-happy; Prutha; Drubbak; Boney outside.
- **Mood:** earned exhaustion, bad wine (the casks are gone — Falcon has produced something "ceremonial and regrettable"), the specific giddiness of people who expected to die today and didn't.

### Approach — the meal lands first
Play a full beat of *victory* before anything uncanny. Let Brewbarry mourn the wine. Let Falcon make the stewards announce courses they can't pronounce. Let Valphine be quietly worshipped by Drubbak. **The dread only works if the room is warm first.**

### The arrival — uninvited, "already booked"
The doors open. **No one opened them.**

> A tall man in a slightly-too-good coat strides in like he's done it a thousand times, talking before anyone can object. A pace behind: a **short, silent** fellow carrying a small case and a deck of cards, who makes eye contact with exactly one person and holds it a beat too long.
>
> **RIMARDO:** "Apologies for the late curtain. The Boar engagement ran long — well. Ran *short*, really. He was already booked elsewhere." *(beat, to the room)* "Permanently."

Falcon will insist he hired no one. Lean into it — that's the first cold spot:

> **FALCON:** "I didn't hire any entertainment."
> **RIMARDO:** "No. You didn't." *(consulting a ledger that has no business being accurate)* "And yet. *Falcon's Lodge — one night only — attendance violently encouraged.*" *(warm, to the party)* "We go where the booking is. The booking, it transpires, is wherever you are."

**Casting note (Penn & Teller):** Rimardo never stops talking; Corrin never speaks. Corrin does the *magic* — the props, the timing, the reveal. Every clue tonight comes out of **Corrin's hands**, not Rimardo's mouth. Rimardo sells the frame; Corrin delivers the truth.

### Getting Vukradin into the room — Corrin sends for him *(locked)*
He's asleep upstairs. Mid-arrival, **Corrin silently hands a steward a folded playbill and points at the ceiling.** The steward goes up and wakes Vukradin: *"The performers are asking for you. By name. By your stage name — the one you haven't used since—"* Dave gets summoned to his own creations. When he comes down, the show **waits for him** — Corrin turns to face him and holds it.

**Corrin's held eye-contact is for Vukradin.** Dave is Holden; the Investigator wears the face Dave built. Let Dave notice he recognizes them before his character can place why.

## Scene 2 — The Act (this is the whole session)

The routine is framed as Rimardo announcing **"our next engagement"** — a tour-date patter that is, secretly, the party's coming road. Funny as a bit; a map underneath.

### Beat 1 — warm-up (pure comedy, no clue yet)
Rimardo does five minutes on the siege as a *review*: critiques Brewbarry's dice luck ("a tragedian"), Vukradin's hypnotic pattern ("crowd work — derivative, but it killed"), Falcon's late-game heroics ("a third-act redemption the critics will call *unearned*"). Corrin silently awards Brewbarry a card reading **"PARTICIPATION."** *Let the table just laugh.* No dread yet.

### Beat 2 — the tour dates (the Leilon map, in comedy)
Rimardo "announces the tour." Each line is a real Leilon beat. Corrin produces a prop for each — **the props are the clue list:**

> **RIMARDO:** "We move on, as one does. The next engagement is a coastal town. Lovely. Sinking, but lovely."
> — *Corrin flips a card: a town, half-underwater.* **(Leilon)**
>
> "The storm act you've *met* — they're touring again, naturally. Weather's a reliable draw. Encore after encore."
> — *Corrin spins a small thunderhead token on the table; it keeps spinning a beat too long.* **(Talos — already known to the party; no new cult named tonight)**
>
> "There's a water feature. There's *always* a water feature. It does not stay in the tank."
> — *Corrin pours a glass; it overflows and keeps overflowing onto the floor.* **(the attack from the sea — Nixoxious / sahuagin)**
>
> "And beneath the stage — every good theater has one — a door. Sealed. Management insists it's structural. Management is lying; management always is."
> — *Corrin lays down a card of a drowned gate and weights it with a black coral chip.* **(the Drowned Gate / nexus beneath Leilon)**
>
> "You'll want lodging on the way. There's an inn. The smith there does *excellent* work." *(a flicker of something)* "Recently. Very... attentive work."
> — *Corrin produces a tiny perfect suit of armor that turns its own head to look at the party.* **(Wayside Inn; Teega the Smith, Kraken-compromised; the animated armor)**

**Stop and let them laugh and write.** This is where a smart table starts taking notes. Don't rush it.

### Beat 3 — the bigger powers (hinted, NOT named)
Now Rimardo's patter goes cosmic — but stays in *show-business* metaphor, which is how you hint at the cage **without** naming KP. This is the "bigger powers" register:

> **RIMARDO:** "Now. The *bill* says the acts are the headliners. Gods, even — minor ones, regional, the kind that think the marquee is about them." *(leaning in, delighted)* "Here's the trick of marquees. Every god of fate believes he's the headliner. That's *precisely* how you know none of them are. You don't get *six* headliners. You get six opening acts and a house that hasn't announced who they're warming up *for.*"
>
> *Corrin slowly turns a card that is blank. Then turns it again — still blank. Then a third time. Rimardo doesn't look at it.*
>
> **RIMARDO:** "There's a prop in the third act that un-happens things. Audiences adore it. *Terrible* for continuity."
> — *Corrin holds up a smooth grey stone, then makes it not have been in his hand — no flourish, it's simply un-happened.* **(the Ruinstone — "undoes deeds")**
>
> "And the dragon." *(sighing, professional)* "There's a dragon. There's a casting problem with the dragon. The understudy is playing the lead, and *no one told the audience*, and the understudy is — between us — a far older and far worse actress than the part requires."
> — *Corrin flips a card of a green dragon. Then peels that card like a sticker; underneath is a different dragon's eye — older, colder, lightning-pale.* **(Iymrith inside Claugiyliamatar — the dragon who isn't who anyone thinks)**

That's the ceiling for tonight. **The "house that hasn't announced who it's warming up for" is the cage / the Dark Powers / KP — and that's as close as you go.** No name. No "Sage." No math. No Aletra.

### Beat 4 — the ONE deferred breadcrumb (GM-only; do NOT let it land tonight)
Plant exactly one thing that pays off *much* later, when the party has KP context — and that even Gary cannot decode now because nothing connects to it yet:

> As Rimardo bows, **Corrin does one last silent bit no one asked for.** He produces the evening's "bill" — an itemized invoice for the show — and runs a fingertip down it as if checking the sums. Every line is a joke (one charge reads *"emotional damages: Brewbarry's wine, 1 cask, sentimental value assessed at zero"*). At the very bottom, in a hand that is *not* Corrin's loose stage-scrawl but tiny, precise, ruled: a final line the party can read if they lean in —
>
> > *balance outstanding.*
>
> Corrin taps a small knife through the words once, leaves the blade in the table, and says nothing.

**Why it's safe:** read tonight, it's a comedian's gag invoice with an ominous flourish. It will only *re-read* as "someone itemizes everything; someone keeps a ledger of fate; someone finds this whole show *inefficient*" once the party meets KP's fingerprint downstream. **Flag in your notes: this is the KP seed. It is payoff-deferred. If the players start decoding it tonight, Rimardo cuts them off with a joke and the duo is gone.**

### Beat 5 — exit (leave unsettling)
They don't take questions. Rimardo: *"You've been a wonderful audience. You'll be a better one in Leilon — you'll have *context.*"* The lamps gutter; when they come back up, **the hall doors are shut and there's no one to have shut them.** On the table, and they **stay**: the thunderhead token (now still), the overflow puddle (real, still spreading — *salt water*), the drowned-gate card under its black coral chip, the little self-turning suit of armor, the blank card, and the knife through *balance outstanding.* Let the party handle them; let them keep them. Physical residue is the proof the night happened.

## NPC notes

- **Rimardo (Penn / the system).** Wants: to *announce* what's already true, dressed as a tour. Voice: legalistic, theatrical, generous, never cruel — "a contract clause performing stand-up." Tic: cites a ledger no one can check and is always right. **Never explains himself. Never gives a quest.** What he wants from the scene: the laugh first, so the recognition lands as art, not omen.
- **Corrin (Teller / the break).** Silent. *Every clue comes from his hands.* Holds eye contact with Vukradin one beat too long. Tic: the impossible reveal done without flourish — he doesn't sell it, it's just *true.* What he wants: to put the map in the room and the knife in the table.
- **What they ARE (GM-only):** the CounterForce — Savras's caught, foreign (Oerth) fate-current, worn as Dave's old characters. Played like **Miller from The Expanse**: a familiar reconstruction, private to one receiver (Vukradin/Dave), working a *case* it half-understands, pointing not directing, real pathos bleeding through the bit. They cannot be captured, dispelled, or interrogated — "not present in any way that admits to capture." A *detect magic* finds nothing; a *zone of truth* has nothing to bind; grappling Corrin closes on a coat.
- **Falcon.** Genuinely did not hire them; rattled, covers with bluster. If pressed on "*one lodge, seized by the displaced*," he gets defensive — his occupation story is cracking and he knows it. Don't resolve it tonight.
- **The orc stewards.** Comic relief valve — use them to break tension if the table gets *too* spooked too early. A steward solemnly trying to refill the bottomless overflow glass is a good pressure-release laugh.

## Anticipated player questions

- **"Do we know these guys?"** → To the *players*: Dave does (he made them). To the *characters*: no — but Vukradin feels the performance grammar is uncanny, *constructed.* Give Dave the structural read; don't confirm or deny in-world. ("You'd swear you'd written this bit. You haven't. Have you?")
- **"Can we grab/follow/scry them?"** → No catch, no trail, no scry. The *failure* is the dread. Reward the attempt with a great beat, not a capture.
- **"Are they sending us to Leilon?"** → They're **not** quest-givers. "They told you what's already happening, in the wrong genre." The party chooses Leilon (and they have road-reasons to — Neverwinter, Wayside Inn). Don't let R&C *assign* it.
- **"Who's the headliner / the house?"** → Unanswered, on purpose. "Someone who hasn't announced themselves." File it. **Do not name KP.**
- **"What's the invoice / 'balance outstanding'?"** → A comedian's gag with a chill. If they push hard, Rimardo jokes it away and they're gone. GM-only: it's the KP seed.
- **(Gary, possibly, OOC) "...is this KP's thing?"** → Hold the line. There is nothing in tonight's content that names or proves KP. Let Gary *wonder* — wondering is correct. Confirming is the mistake.

## Stage directions (timing / silence)

- **Warm the room first.** Full victory-meal beat before the doors open. No uncanny until they've laughed at the stewards.
- **Joke first, dread second — every beat.** If the table is only worried and not laughing, you've inverted it; back off and let Rimardo be funnier.
- **The reveals are silent.** When Corrin flips a card, *say nothing and wait.* Let the players read it and react before Rimardo's next line. The silence is the trick.
- **Don't fill the exit.** When the doors are shut and no one shut them — leave a long beat. Let someone else speak first.
- **The props are real.** Hand them the masks, the knife, the salt puddle. Physical residue sells "that happened" better than any narration.
- **If they decode too fast, cut and exit.** Better to leave the breadcrumb un-decoded than to confirm KP early.
