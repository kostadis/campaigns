# Counterattack at Falcon's Lodge — Session Prep

*Status: staging notes (notes/, not canon). Builds on `circle_of_thunder.md`, `manse_arrival.md`, and `npcs/lodge_orcs_eastern_heart.md`. Assumed sequence: party hit henge → hit Manse → arrives at the Lodge with a window before the cult strike. Specific NPCs, tactical details, and aftermath beats are **Claude-proposed** pending DM confirmation.*

---

## Design call

This is the **Gorthok climax** for the Talosian arc in your campaign. The published Counterattack beat is repurposed as **K. (the Chosen) personally summons Gorthok to wipe out the Eastern Heart band that said no** — orc-on-orc warfare with cult-aligned Eastern Heart orcs attacking their own kin, the boar smashing the palisade, and the party choosing who survives.

**Three things this scene has to do:**

1. **Stake a real ally for the party.** Vorga's band survives or doesn't. If they survive, the party gets a regional ally that Lord's Alliance hates and the Order finds awkward. If they die, the party fails *and* loses the Icespire Hold cleanup path.
2. **Establish K.'s direct authority.** K. is summoning Gorthok without an anchorite proxy. This is their first solo working, on-screen via storm rather than presence. The party sees what K. can do without seeing K. yet.
3. **Make the Eastern Heart schism visceral.** The orcs storming the lodge are *also* Eastern Heart — bands that said yes. Prutha will recognize faces. The fight isn't faction vs. faction; it's the tribe killing itself under cult command.

**What this scene deliberately does NOT do:**

- Reveal K.'s identity or location
- Put Tovrak on screen (keep him in reserve as K.'s second for the eventual K. fight — see `npcs/lodge_orcs_eastern_heart.md`)
- Resolve the Icespire Hold question (that's the post-scene political beat)

## Setup — where the party is coming from

The party may arrive at the Lodge **before** or **after** clearing the Manse. The scene plays the same shape either way; the *summoner* changes, and so does what the party knows when they arrive.

### Branch A — Manse first (assumed in original prep)

- Party left the Manse with **Grannoc dead, ritual interrupted**, the "lightning from nothing" beat as the tell that K. has taken over personally
- They have all the intel from the henge (Flenz/Narux/Yargath) and the Manse (K.'s letter to Grannoc, the broken whistle, the dated map showing Lodge attack timing, the R&C playbill if they searched the chamber)
- **Gorthok at the Lodge is K.'s working** — summoned remotely, no anchorite proxy. This is the path the original Counterattack design assumed: K.'s first solo summoning, on screen via storm rather than presence.
- Party already has the displacement-chain context (from the Manse playbill if they read it; from K.'s letter regardless). The Eastern Heart truth is *partly* surfaced; Prutha's banner-recognition at the treeline confirms it.

### Branch B — Lodge first

- Party went **directly to the Lodge** after Falcon's framing held in their heads — they expected to find Talosian-allied cultists and clear them for the boots of elvenkind
- **Prutha at the treeline is the only safety rail** — see his sun-and-stone banner read below. If the party ignores or sidelines him, they risk killing the people they came to defend. If they use him as scout, the truth surfaces before any arrows fly.
- They have **only the henge intel** (Flenz/Narux/Yargath letters and sketches): they know about Grannoc, the Manse, K., and "the boy from the eastern clan," but they don't have K.'s letter to Grannoc, the dated map, or the Manse playbill.
- **Grannoc is still alive at the Manse**, doing his thing. Two DM choices for what that means at the Lodge:
  - **B1: Grannoc completes his ritual.** Gorthok at the Lodge is **Grannoc's** working, aimed by the borrowed-mathematics geometry. K. stays offstage. The "lightning from nothing" K.-reveal does **not** fire today — it waits until the party reaches the Manse afterward and finds the chamber empty (Grannoc fled or dead from his own ritual recoil) with K.'s letter still on the desk.
  - **B2: Grannoc's ritual sloppiness backfires.** No Gorthok at the Lodge. The cult attack is anchorites + orcs only, no boar. The party clears the Lodge, then arrives at the Manse to find Grannoc dead in his own chamber from the ritual rebounding, K.'s letter intact, and the "lightning from nothing" tell hits *there* instead.
- **Recommendation:** B1 if you want Gorthok as the climactic threat tonight (with K. revealed later in the arc); B2 if you want to extend the Talos arc by one more session and keep Gorthok in reserve for a K.-led set piece.

### Common to both branches

- Prutha is with them, doctrine surviving in his bone whistle
- Soma's staff is quiet (still)
- Falcon is **not** at the Lodge — he's in Phandalin, has been there since chapter ~36. He doesn't know what's about to happen to his property.

**The clock:** Per the cult's plan, the attack was scheduled for some specific window. The party can plausibly arrive a few hours before the storm hits in either branch. That's the negotiation window.

## Approach to the Lodge

Half-day's march northwest from the Manse, following the trail. The fog thins; the boars thin; the woods become recognizably normal forest again. They cross a river and reach the cleared land around the Lodge.

### What they see from the treeline

Read aloud:

> The Lodge stands where Falcon described it — a two-story timber and stone building, a guest house off to the side, a stable house and smithy joined into one structure, the outhouse, the pens. A wooden palisade ten feet high encloses the compound. Smoke rises from one chimney; the others are cold.
>
> The palisade is intact, but it's been repaired in places with mismatched wood — newer planks lashed in with rough cord. Whoever holds the Lodge has been improving its defenses, not demolishing them.
>
> Three figures stand on the walkway behind the palisade, watching the treeline. Even from here you can tell they've seen you. Two of them have bows drawn but not raised. The third one is shouting something behind her into the courtyard.

**Prutha's read (free, if he's with them):**

> *"Eastern Heart. Three banners on the wall — I see one. Sun-and-stone. My uncle's clan-mark. They are family."*

The "sun-and-stone" banner (Claude-proposed) — a sun rising over a mountain — is the Eastern Heart tribal mark. The Lodge orcs are flying it. They're not hiding what they are.

## First contact — negotiation window

### If Prutha walks point

He approaches the palisade alone, at a deliberate pace, hands open. At thirty feet from the gate, he performs the **dawn-bow** — right fist to left shoulder, head down. He pulls the bone whistle from inside his armor and blows the dawn-prayer.

Above the palisade, **Vorga Long-Memory** lowers her bow and returns the bow. She shouts down to him in Orcish; he answers. Then she yells to the rest of the band in the courtyard, and yells back to him: *"Bring your drow. Bring your aasimar. Bring all of them. We are listening."*

The gate opens.

### If the party comes in cold without Prutha leading

Vorga calls down in broken Common: *"You walk armed. You walk with drow. You walk with one of ours. Speak before the bows rise."*

Prutha will step forward if asked. If the party tries to negotiate without using him — possible, but they're rolling Persuasion against a tired, scared, suspicious leader who has seen her people coerced and killed by outsiders for a year. DC 17 or 18. Vukradin can probably make it; almost no one else.

### If the party comes in armed and pushy

Vorga's bows raise. *"Leave the trees. We will not take more dying today."* The band will not attack first, but they will not let the party through the gate. If the party tries to force entry, the fight starts and the cult arrives later to find both sides bleeding.

## Inside the Lodge — what they see

If they get inside (recommended):

> The courtyard is full of people. Not warriors — *people.* Women with babies. Old men around a cookfire. Children playing a game with carved sticks near the well. Maybe a dozen warriors among them, the rest non-combatants. They watch you the way refugees watch anyone who walks into their camp armed.
>
> Vorga Long-Memory comes down from the walkway. She's middle-aged, gray at the temples, broken nose, a scar across her jaw from something old. She wears Falcon's hunter's cloak — taken from his closet — over orc-style leather. She makes no apology for it.
>
> Beside her stands a much older orc, leaning on a spear like a walking stick. **Old Drubbak.** His eyes go directly to Valphine's holy symbol of Lathander and stay there. He says one word in Orcish that Prutha translates as: *"Dawn."*

(Note: Drubbak is recognizing the dawn-doctrine in Lathander. This is **Valphine bait** — her heterodox pain-doctrine is theologically *adjacent* to the Eastern Heart dawn-prayer in a way that no one expected. Lean into this if Gary plays it.)

## The negotiation

Three things the band needs to hear:

1. **The cult is coming today.** Show the dated map from Grannoc's quarters. Vorga will believe this — they've been expecting it for months.
2. **Grannoc is dead. Flenz and Narux are dead.** This is news. The middle managers who organized their displacement are gone. The relief is real but cautious — *they don't know about K.*
3. **Cryovain is dead — and the party killed him.** They don't know either fact. The kill was only 3–4 days ago; forest rumor moves slower than that, and the Lodge band is isolated. This is **the big one** and it is *news*, not confirmation.

   They will not believe it on words alone. The party has to produce proof — and they have proof, harvested from Cryovain's body at Icespire Hold: **a white dragon claw, scales, the breath pouch.** Have someone — Vukradin or Valphine is the natural choice — set a claw the size of a forearm at Old Drubbak's feet.

   Drubbak holds it. Smells it. Compares it to a memory in his head. **Then he sits down on the ground.** Vorga will not react publicly but will need a minute. The non-combatants in the courtyard go quiet without knowing why.

   The negotiation changes shape from here. The party is no longer "armed outsiders offering a deal." They are *the people who killed the dragon that took our home* — a fact that has been true for several days but has only just become real to anyone in the Lodge. This is the moment Vorga decides she can trust them. **Until this beat lands, treat her as guarded and transactional.**

Three things the party can offer in return:

1. **Defend the Lodge today.** Immediate, transactional. Vorga accepts on the spot — she has no other option.
2. **Eventual return of Icespire Hold.** The party currently holds it; the dragon is dead; the hoard belongs to the people Cryovain stole it from. This is the Vukradin ethics play, and it resolves the planar-theorist bottleneck (the orcs can have the ice-block hoard; it's theirs by every right). Vorga will not believe this until she sees it.
3. **Honor for their dead.** Vukradin commission, Valphine ritual, Soma plant-something. Low-cost, high-symbolic-value.

**What Vorga commits to:** Her fifteen warriors fight alongside the party. The non-combatants will be moved to the root cellars and the stable house. If the Lodge falls, Old Drubbak will lead the survivors south on a path he knows. **If Vorga falls, command goes to Kresh Stormfist** — and Kresh wants death-by-glory more than survival, so this is a worse outcome.

## The storm arrives

Once negotiation is settled, **the storm rolls in fast.** Not the slow gather of the henge. This is K.'s working — direct, impatient, untrained in subtlety.

Read aloud:

> The air pressure drops in your ears all at once. Outside the palisade, the sky goes from afternoon to evening in the span of one breath. Wind rises. The boars in Falcon's pens panic and crash against the walls of their enclosure.
>
> Old Drubbak goes very still. He says: *"It comes wrong. There is no chant. Where is the chant?"*
>
> The first lightning strike hits the treeline a hundred yards out. The second strike hits the river. The third — the third comes down inside the palisade and splits an empty barrel.
>
> The cult is here. But no anchorite called this storm.

**DM note for the players:** This is the Soma's-staff moment. **The fourth bud — the one that appeared at the henge — opens.** A new leaf unfurls near the crown of the staff. This is the first leaf the staff has grown in Soma's hands since Meril gave it to her; it marks the moment she chose to defend the refugees rather than walk past them. The staff *gains* leaves as Soma accepts her role — never loses them. Direction matters; this is canonical to her arc. (No combat mechanical effect; major arc-score event — likely +3 or +4 on the Meril's Legacy track for Active Intervention / Planar Harmony, DM's call.)

## The attack itself

### Composition

**Cult forces:**
- **Gorthok the Thunder Boar** (CR 6 per DoIP statblock) — arrives smashing through the palisade gate at the start of round 1
  - *Branch A (Manse first):* Gorthok is **K.'s** summoning, riding the storm from offscreen. The "comes wrong, no chant" Drubbak line lands hard — no anchorite called this storm.
  - *Branch B1 (Lodge first, Grannoc's ritual succeeds):* Gorthok is **Grannoc's** summoning, aimed by borrowed-mathematics geometry. The arrival *has* a chant — distant, off the wind from the south where the Manse is. Drubbak says: *"It comes wrong. The chant is there, but small. Far."*
  - *Branch B2 (Lodge first, Grannoc's ritual fails):* **No Gorthok.** Cut him from the encounter. The cult attack is anchorites + orcs only; scale up the orc charge by ~30% and add a third anchorite to compensate. Keep Gorthok in reserve for a later K.-led set piece.
- **~16–20 cult-aligned Eastern Heart orcs** (orc statblock, CR 1/2 each) — pour through the breach after Gorthok (or through the gate as the main wave in B2)
- **2 Anchorite of Talos as battlefield commanders** (CR 2 each — claim they're from another band the party hasn't met) — flanking the orc charge, casting Bless and Shillelagh. *In B2 only: bump to 3 anchorites; one of them is Grannoc himself, who left the Manse when his ritual rebounded.*
- **No K. on screen** in any branch. The party will not encounter them today.

**Lodge defenders:**
- The party (3 PCs + Boney + Prutha)
- **Vorga + 15 Eastern Heart warriors** (orc statblock for ~10 of them; the rest are wounded, older, or under-equipped — treat as CR 1/4 for tactical purposes). Vorga herself is a Veteran (CR 3) — tactical leader, longbow + greatsword.
- **Old Drubbak** organizing the non-combatants in the root cellar and stable house. He is not a combatant; he is the evacuation route.
- **The terrain** — palisade, two-story Lodge with balcony, stable house, well, livestock pens, plenty of cover

### Encounter scaling note

This is balanced for a 7th-level party with NPC ally support. Gorthok is the big number; the orcs are tactical pressure; the anchorites are the brain. Adjust orc count if the band's warriors are reading as too soft.

**If you want to make Gorthok feel even bigger:** Have him arrive at the start of round 2 instead of round 1. Round 1 = the gate goes down, orcs pour through, anchorites Bless their crew, the party engages. Round 2 = thunderclap, Gorthok lands in the courtyard from the *sky* (lightning-borne entry, not just smashing through), and the storm intensifies. This is more dramatic than RAW.

### The orc-on-orc dimension — the load-bearing beat

The attacking orcs are **Eastern Heart**. Prutha sees faces he knows. Vorga's warriors see cousins, brothers, in-laws. **The first round of combat is shot through with calls in Orcish across the palisade:**

> *"Prutha?! You walk with drow now?"*
> *"Bratha's nephew — drop the whistle and we kill you fast!"*
> *"That is my mother's brother! Don't you—"*
> *"They lied to you, cousin, drop your weapons—"*

This is texture, not mechanics. **But mechanically:** if at any point during the fight a player makes a deliberate effort to convert or spare cult orcs — Vukradin Universal Speech / Persuasion, Valphine Daylight + divine compulsion, Prutha riding into the middle of the line with the bone whistle — call for one roll, DC 16. **On a success, 1d4+1 cult orcs drop their weapons and either surrender or flee.** This is a real player choice and it should pay off when it's earned.

If a player tries this on Gorthok or the anchorites — no. Gorthok is a primal spirit, not a soldier; the anchorites are committed. The conversion only works on the coerced orc rank-and-file.

## Tactical guidance for the fight

### Gorthok's behavior

- Round he arrives: smash through the gate (or land in the courtyard if you're doing the lightning entry). Free knock-down for anyone in the path.
- His priority targets: large humanoids first (Brewbarry), then casters (Vukradin/Valphine). He's drawn to whoever's putting out the most radiant or storm-tinted magic. **Valphine's Daylight is a target marker.** This is fine — she can handle it, and it makes her radiant doctrine matter.
- Lightning on tusks: published statblock — every gore attack should feel like a thunderclap.
- He fights to the death per RAW. **But:** if you want a partial-win exit (Gorthok flees), let K. recall him when he drops below ~25% HP. The recall is a thunderclap that pulls him *up* into the storm. He vanishes. The storm dissipates a few rounds later.

### The anchorites

Use them to keep the orc line cohesive. Bless on round 1; Spiritual Weapon and Shillelagh in melee; Sacred Flame at clustered defenders. They will NOT retreat — they are committed Talosians. Same standing rules as the henge: Silence shuts them down hard, Hold Person ends the encounter on whoever it lands.

### The orc charge

Fifteen-plus orcs is a lot of bodies. Don't roll every individual attack — clump them into groups of 3–4 and resolve as mob attacks. The point of their presence is *pressure on the non-combatants in the root cellar*, not statblock accounting. **Some of the orcs will break off from the main fight to hunt for the women and children.** This is the moment Vorga's warriors take their stand at the stable house door. If the party doesn't help that line, the non-combatants die.

### Falcon's Lodge as a battlefield

- The palisade gate is the obvious breach point but the walls can also be broken (lightning, Gorthok charge, Brewbarry rage)
- The main Lodge building has cover, a balcony for archers, and a trophy hall full of mounted heads that may or may not include something secretly alive (DM call — DoIP doesn't make this canon but you could)
- The stable house holds non-combatants AND Falcon's livestock; you have permission to set part of the structure on fire if you want to add evacuation pressure
- The well is the courtyard's center of mass; Gorthok will probably end up fighting near it
- The pumpkin patches around the Lodge (Falcon doesn't grow pumpkins; the Eastern Heart band brought seeds from the Manse area when they fled there) are flammable

### Win condition variants

- **Clean win:** Gorthok dies, anchorites die, most cult orcs die or flee, band has minimal casualties. Lodge mostly intact. **Path to the Icespire Hold offer fully open.**
- **Pyrrhic win:** Gorthok dies but Lodge burns or Vorga falls. Old Drubbak leads the survivors south. The Icespire Hold offer still stands but the party has to repair a lot of trust.
- **Recalled-Gorthok:** Gorthok survives (K. pulls him out). Anchorites die, orcs flee. Band survives but the boar will be back. **K.'s power confirmed; tension preserved for the K. fight.**
- **Failure:** Band dies. Lodge burns. Prutha is the last Eastern Heart orc. This is a campaign-altering loss — only run this if the dice take you there.

## Mid-fight beats you can drop in

Use as needed for pacing:

- **The bone whistle moment.** Prutha rides Boney into the gap and blows the dawn-prayer at the cult orcs. One of them lowers his weapon. Another one shoots him. Reaction is mixed; the moment is real.
- **Vorga's stand.** She and three warriors hold the stable house door against a wave of cult orcs. If the party arrives, they survive. If not, she goes down with the warriors.
- **Old Drubbak's voice.** Mid-fight, from the root cellar entrance, Drubbak begins the Eastern Heart sunrise chant. The non-combatants pick it up. The chant becomes audible across the courtyard. **Some of the cult orcs flinch when they hear it.** This is the doctrine fighting back.
- **The lightning sigil.** A lightning bolt hits the center of the courtyard and burns a sigil into the flagstones — the three-lightning-bolts-converging mark from the Manse. K. is signing the attack. If anyone reads it (Religion DC 13), they know K. has been watching.
- **Valphine's Daylight vs. the storm.** The natural light is gone; the storm has made it twilight. Valphine's Daylight from her holy symbol is the only true light. This is a *visible* miracle to the Eastern Heart band — Drubbak's "dawn" word made literal in the middle of the fight. Worth a Searing Dawn Score event.

## Aftermath — read-aloud frame

If the party wins (assume clean or pyrrhic):

> The storm doesn't disperse. It *retreats* — pulled away to the south, sucked back into the sky in a way that storms don't do. The lightning stops. The wind dies. The light comes back.
>
> Around the courtyard: bodies. Eastern Heart on Eastern Heart. Boars cooked by their own god's lightning. The boar — Gorthok — gone, either dead at your feet or pulled up into the sky with the storm.
>
> Vorga Long-Memory is alive [or not]. She stands in the wreckage of the palisade gate. She looks at you. She looks at her own dead on the ground. She does not thank you. Not yet.
>
> Old Drubbak comes out of the root cellar leading a line of children. None of them are crying. They are all looking at Valphine.
>
> Prutha kneels in the courtyard and starts collecting whistles from the dead cult orcs. One by one. There are a lot of whistles.

## The Rimardo and Corrin playbill (end-of-battle reveal)

**Canon reference:** `docs/CounterForce.md`. This is the CounterForce signal landing at the Lodge — Branch B's analogue to the Manse playbill (see `manse_arrival.md`). **Always run it in Branch B**, since the Manse version hasn't appeared yet. **In Branch A**, the Manse playbill already ran; whether you place a *second* playbill at the Lodge is your call (recommendation: skip, and let R&C resurface next at the Wayside Inn — over-signaling thins the dread).

### Placement

After the read-aloud frame above. The party is sitting in the smoke. Vorga is alive (or being buried). The wounded are being counted. **Then someone notices it.**

Two natural locations:
- Pinned to a splintered palisade post near the gate breach, with a tiny dagger driven through it
- Pinned to one of Falcon's trophy mounts inside the main hall — likely the largest stag head — dagger through the paper, through the mount, into the wood

Pick whichever the party occupies at the end of combat. The dagger is real steel; the paper is real paper; there is no detectable magic. *That* is the unsettling part.

### Front of the playbill (read aloud)

> Rimardo and Mr. Corrin
> Predestination, Plunder, and Other Legal Fictions
> Special Engagement: The Boar Who Was Already Booked
> ~~Woodland Manse~~ **Falcon's Hunting Lodge**
> Performance proceeded out of advertised order
> Attendance: previously violently encouraged; now retroactively appreciated

The strikethrough on "Woodland Manse" is hand-corrected in ink — the printed playbill says one thing, *someone* updated it to reflect what actually happened.

### Back of the playbill (read aloud)

> One dragon, displaced by larger wars.
> One mountain tribe, displaced by the dragon.
> One forest, made crowded by consequence.
> One lodge, ~~seized by~~ **defended by** the displaced.
> One storm cult, recruiting from the desperate.
> One thunder beast, scheduled for punishment.
> One ritual, using borrowed mathematics.
> One audience, ~~not yet aware~~ **now aware** it is participating.
> Balance outstanding.
>
> Signed:
> R. and C.

**Corrin's dagger is driven through the phrase "balance outstanding."** That's the tell. The party just paid something — orcs dead on both sides, lodge half-burned, possibly Vorga lost — and the dagger says: *this is being kept score of.*

### What the playbill does, mechanically

Same four jobs as the Manse version, repurposed for *retrospective* rather than *predictive*:

1. **The displacement chain is named.** Giant War → Cryovain → orcs → Lodge. The party has now lived the chain, but seeing it spelled out clarifies it as a *pattern*, not a sequence of bad luck.
2. **"Defended by the displaced"** confirms the Lodge truth the party just discovered through Vorga. R&C are *agreeing* with what the party figured out — not informing them, validating them. That validation is itself the discomfort: someone was watching closely enough to score this.
3. **"Borrowed mathematics"** remains the KP signature. The party just survived a Gorthok summoning that was *too precise* to be Talos alone (B1: precise enough to aim him at the Lodge; B2: precise enough to almost work, even with Grannoc's sloppiness). The dagger isn't through this phrase, but the phrase is still on the page. Hold it.
4. **"Now aware it is participating"** closes the bit. The last playbill (if they saw one at the Manse) said *not yet aware*; this one says *now aware*. The party has stepped onto the stage. R&C are no longer narrating *at* them — they're narrating *with* them.

### Vukradin's stage-vision (trigger: same as Manse — touch, music near the wreckage)

If Vukradin handles the playbill, or plays his flute in the courtyard, he gets the vision. **Same five cards, reordered to reflect the order the party played them.**

> You stand before a rain-soaked stage. The audience is hidden in darkness. The applause from the last act has not fully died.
>
> Rimardo stands in the spotlight, holding a ledger. Corrin waits beside him, silent, holding five cards.
>
> Rimardo says:
> *"A monster is only random until someone sells tickets to the charge."*
>
> Corrin turns over the cards, one at a time:
> **GIANT WAR**
> **CRYOVAIN**
> **FALCON'S LODGE**
> **GORTHOK**
> **WAYSIDE INN**
>
> Rimardo looks down at the cards. Frowns. Reshuffles them once, then once more. Sets them aside.
>
> *"Out of order. But acceptable. The fifth card is the fifth card."*
>
> The vision ends with applause that sounds like rainfall, hoofbeats, and distant orc war-drums.

**What this seeds:**
- The same fifth card. **WAYSIDE INN** is where R&C resurface, regardless of which two acts the party did first.
- Rimardo's "out of order, but acceptable" — confirms R&C don't care about the prescribed path. They care that the show is happening.
- "The fifth card is the fifth card" — invariant. The Wayside Inn beat is fixed. The party can rearrange the first four; they cannot reorder the fifth.

### DM notes — running this beat

- **Joke first, dread second.** The party should chuckle at "retroactively appreciated" before they get nervous at "balance outstanding." If the tone lands serious-first, the comedy hasn't done its job.
- **No exposition.** Even though the playbill is *agreeing* with what the party figured out, R&C do not explain themselves. The dread comes from the fact that someone *graded their performance* before they knew they were on stage.
- **Soma's staff:** quiet. Same as the Manse — the staff is a planar/natural sensor, not a fate-signal receiver. Its silence is itself a tell.
- **If Vorga or Drubbak sees the playbill:** Vorga reads it (Common) and looks at Vukradin. *"Your people leave strange notes."* Drubbak does not read Common but can read the dagger — his comment: *"This is not Talos. This is a different hand."* Then he goes back to the cellar. Don't elaborate.

## Aftermath beats to play

1. **Vorga's eventual thanks** — not effusive, not in the moment. Probably the next morning, after the dead are sorted. *"You came when no one else came. We will remember. We will not forget."*
2. **Old Drubbak speaks to Valphine** — privately, in halting Common, asks her whether the dawn-prayer can be sung in a temple. He is the first orc cleric-candidate in your campaign. **Open question for the DM.**
3. **The Icespire Hold offer** — Vukradin makes it formal. The hoard, the fortress, the path home. Vorga's response will be measured; Old Drubbak's will be: *"We will go. We will go with weapons. We will go when we have buried."*
4. **The whistles** — Prutha has collected ~16 whistles from the dead cult orcs. He gives them to Drubbak. Drubbak will give them to the families who never knew where their sons went. This is the moment that closes the schism — *some* of it.
5. **K.'s next move**
   - *Branch A:* K. has lost both summoning sites and Gorthok (if dead). They have **Tovrak** and whatever cult cells the party doesn't know about. The next time K. moves, it will be personal. Set this up for the next arc.
   - *Branch B:* K. is **not yet outed** — the party has cleared the Lodge but the Manse is still standing. Their next session should be the Manse cleanup, where K.'s letter to Grannoc and the "lightning from nothing" tell finally land. K.'s reveal is pushed one session later but lands harder because the party arrives knowing what they're walking into.
6. **Falcon's Lodge** — partially burned, palisade broken, but standing. Falcon will weep when he sees it. Vukradin's promised studio wood is going to need to be renegotiated; the music venue partnership is now *also* a refugee camp partnership. Falcon will accept this. Probably.

## Open questions / DM decisions before running

1. **Round-1 vs round-2 Gorthok entry.** RAW says the gate-smash arrival is round 1. The dramatic "lightning entry round 2" gives the players one round of orc-fighting first. **Recommendation: round 2 entry, for pacing.**
2. **Whether Gorthok dies or gets recalled.** Recall preserves him as a threat under K.'s control; death closes the loop. **Recommendation: recall on first run if HP allows, to keep K. menacing.**
3. **Whether Vorga survives.** Drama suggests she might fall. Story suggests she should live to negotiate the Hold transfer. **Recommendation: keep her alive unless the dice strongly say otherwise.**
4. **Old Drubbak as cleric-candidate.** This opens a whole branch — orc Lathanderian, Valphine's heterodox church gains a second-tribe convert. Big arc fuel or unnecessary distraction depending on what you want. **Recommendation: leave the door open in the scene, decide later.**
5. **Falcon's reaction to the Lodge being a refugee camp.** This needs handling next session. **Recommendation: he accepts grudgingly because Valphine and Vukradin both vouch.**
6. **The dragon hoard ice-block.** Vukradin's intent is to give it to the Eastern Heart band. Mechanically this needs Vorga to accept and the ice to be transportable (or just left at the Hold for them to take possession of). **Recommendation: it goes with them when they march on the Hold.**
