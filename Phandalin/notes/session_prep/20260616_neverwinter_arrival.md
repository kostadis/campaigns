## The Economic Texture — The Deflationary Bard

> **GM-only framing.** Don't deliver this as a lecture. Let it surface as texture, escalating across the session.

Working Paper No. 27 from Candlekeep's Vault of Mercantile Histories argues (correctly, within Faerûnian economics) that adventurers are agents of *circulation* — they move wealth from dragon hoards back into the economy. Adventurers who refuse to spend recovered treasure are, in this model, functionally indistinguishable from the dragons they slay. They sequester wealth. They are deflationary.

Vukradin has:
- A 3,000gp block of fused residium-gold he refuses to spend.
- An obsidian sword he treats as "stolen goods pending documentation" rather than selling.
- Thirty gold pieces a week from the mine that he is holding rather than investing.
- Early compositions being commercially exploited by others because he never monetized them.

He is, from the Faerûnian economy's perspective, a problem.

**How this surfaces in session:**

**Level 1 (early session):** In the Seven Suns Coster Market, a stall selling "Exile Aesthetic" merchandise also sells a cheap pamphlet woodcut: a dragon wearing a lute and a bard's hat. Title: *The Hoarding Hero: Or, Why Some Adventurers Are Economically Indistinguishable From The Monsters They Slay. A Summary of Candlekeep Working Paper No. 27.* Price: 2cp. It's next to the imitation Dread Helms.

**Level 2 (mid session):** If the party mentions the mine commission, the Neverwinter Commission clerk pulls out a copy of the paper and explains, gently, that the commission's cut exists precisely because adventuring parties historically fail to circulate their wealth. "We've found it necessary to... ensure the economy receives its share." The clerk is not hostile. This is just policy.

**Level 3 (end of session):** Neverember's steward, while delivering the invitation, mentions that the Lord Protector "has read with interest the economic correspondence about your party's activities" and looks forward to "discussing the mutual benefits of wealth circulation." The steward is very smooth about this. It is vaguely threatening.

> The point: this pays off next session when Vukradin is present and Neverember offers to fund the studio — which is literally the city getting Vukradin to circulate wealth by spending on something he actually wants. Neverember is solving the Vukradin problem.

## Scene 1 — The Exile Aesthetic (Opening — 20 min)

### Setup
**Time:** Morning. The party arrives at Neverwinter's western gate — the Protector's Enclave entry.
**Who's present:** Brewbarry, Valphine, Soma (+ Boney, Prutha if traveling with them).
**Vukradin is not here.** Let the city tell them it already knows he exists.

### The moment

Read (or riff from):

> *The gate guards check your faces against a writ — someone from Phandalin sent word ahead, you're expected. While you wait for the duty officer, three young humans in their early twenties walk past you. They are wearing rough-hide trimmed cloaks with charcoal tribal markings on their forearms. One has a painted leather helm with two eye-slits daubed in red. Another is carrying a hand axe — ornamental, clearly never used — strapped across their back at a jaunty angle. All three are barefoot despite the cobblestones. They are nodding along to music coming from a nearby window. The music is dissonant, uncomfortable, and technically accomplished in ways that feel deliberately hidden. It sounds like someone furious at beauty.*
>
> *One of them looks at Brewbarry and stops walking.*
>
> *"That's... wait. Is that—"*

The trend has a name in Neverwinter social circles: **The Exile Aesthetic**. It combines Vukradin's early compositions (raw, alienated, deliberately unpolished) with Brewbarry's tribal visual style, into a philosophy that positions authenticity as something only possible *outside* the system. Young people who feel suffocated by Neverember's commercial rebuilding have adopted it as protest fashion.

**What they don't know:** Brewbarry's exile was unjust. He didn't choose it. He didn't romanticize it. And the early Vukradin music they're listening to wasn't made to be a movement — it was made by an idealistic young man who got embarrassed when nobody came to his shows.

> **The comedy:** The fan wants to be told his aesthetic is *correct*. What he gets is Brewbarry.
>
> **The weight under the comedy:** The city has built a philosophy around someone's worst year. Brewbarry can feel that. Don't undercut it.

### Branch — if Brewbarry leans into being recognized
He gets immediately surrounded. A small crowd forms. Someone produces a scrap of parchment for his signature. An older merchant watches with the expression of a man who has seen trends come and go and is calculating how to sell this one to the next group.

### Branch — if Brewbarry is uncomfortable
The fan reads the discomfort as *authenticity* and is more impressed. "That's exactly what the music is about. You don't have to perform it for us." Brewbarry cannot win this interaction on its own terms.

### Branch — Prutha (if present)
He is baffled. These people dress like warriors but are clearly not warriors. He pokes one of the ornamental axes. He has opinions about this but cannot find the right word in Common.

### The Vukradin thread surfaces here

The fan, or a passerby, or a market crier near the gate is humming or drumming along to the same music. If someone asks what the music is: *"It's Vukradin's early work. The stuff he recorded before he got famous and went commercial. The real stuff."* 

The phrasing "went commercial" is doing work. Vukradin's pivot toward polish and political influence is understood here as a sellout. His actual current work — the *Choralum Lux Maleficarum*, the Phandalin Privy Council position — is seen as abandoning the purity of his early period.

> **GM note:** Vukradin is not here to respond to this. That's the joke. And it's the point. The city has a version of him that he didn't author and can't correct.

If Valphine glances at the portrait: she recognizes the rendering. She was there.

## Scene 2 — The Mermaid Runs (Dead on Delivery)

### Setup
**Location:** The Moonstone Mask — teleportation circle from below in the Protector's Enclave, then up.
**Who:** Lord Cassian Meliamne (sea elf, Elara's cousin). And, five minutes after the delivery, Cullen Sharpe (House Margaster).

> **What changed:** Elara Seasong Meliamne is dead. She was killed by House Margaster eight days ago, after she filed a formal counter-claim at the Harbor authority. The authoritative session record established this in session 20260407. The grounding docs were never regenerated after that session — they still show her alive, which is a doc-maintenance error, not canon.

The party arrives at the Moonstone Mask expecting Elara. Lord Cassian is already there, at the best table, waiting. He rises when he sees them — specifically when he sees the bag.

> *"You have the statue. Good. My cousin told me someone was bringing it."* A pause. *"She was killed eight days ago. House Margaster. I thought you should know before you handed it to anyone."*

He lets that land.

*"I'm Cassian. Elara's cousin. Please sit."*

**Lord Cassian Meliamne:** Sea elf, older than Elara by elven standards, Neverwinter commercial house. He was at the Cerulean Salon some months ago. He knew about the statue hunt. He has been managing the aftermath of Elara's death for eight days and has not been idle. He is running at a quiet controlled burn underneath a very composed exterior.

> **GM-only:** Cassian does not know exactly how Margaster arranged it — he suspects they used the Harbor authority connection to get to Elara after she filed the counter-claim. He has been building a case, slowly and privately. He wants the statue because it is genuinely his family's and genuinely his cousin's last project. He would like the party to understand that House Margaster kills people, not merely applies pressure. He does not say this directly. He doesn't need to.

### The delivery

If they hand over the statue: he holds it for a moment without speaking. It's a blue quartz mermaid, roughly 18 inches tall. He says, quietly: *"She was twelve when she last held this."* He puts it on the table.

He then looks at them like he is deciding something, and passes on what Elara was working toward:

*"She was investigating the interplanar shipping device at the docks before she died. The Displacement Manifold — it stopped working about seven weeks ago. The Commission was operating it as a commercial service: goods arrive in Neverwinter by sea, then the Manifold distributes them to stores across the region. Lionshield Coster was their primary client — all of their regional distribution ran through it. When it went dark, their stores stopped receiving goods. Nobody official will say why. She believed the timing wasn't coincidental."*

*"The device had a regular maintenance technician. A gnome. Elara described him — she met him twice at the docks when she was tracking the shipping gap. Small, older, very precise. The kind of person who asks questions that go past the job — she wrote that he seemed more interested in *why* the planar routing worked than in whether it was functioning. Like he was mapping something. She found it unsettling at the time and didn't know why."*

A pause.

*"He hasn't been seen since the Manifold stopped. She didn't have a name for him. Just the description and the questions he asked."*

Cassian passes this forward because Elara's threads have no one else to pull them. He's a commercial strategist, not a field operator. He's handing the party what he has.

**What Cassian knows:**
- The Commission operated the Manifold as a paid service to Lionshield Coster — they built it, they ran it, and they collected fees on both the port intake and the distribution. Elara found the original commercial contract in the Harbor authority's records before she died.
- House Margaster has stepped into the distribution role the Manifold vacated — they are now routing goods to Lionshield's stores through conventional means, at a margin the Commission's Manifold couldn't match. The timing of their expansion and the Manifold's failure is not coincidental.
- House Margaster's side business: recovering "salvage" from wrecks before rightful owners can file claims, via their arrangement with the Harbor authority. Also documented in Elara's notes.
- The maintenance technician: gnome, older, precise, obsessed with planar architecture in a way that unsettled Elara. No name. Last seen at the docks. Gone.

> **GM note — The Gnome Trap (players only, not characters):**
>
> KP is a gnome. The players know this. Cassian just described a small, older gnome who was mapping planar architecture and vanished when things went wrong. Let that land. Don't correct it. Don't add detail that complicates the assumption. The party will conclude this is KP.
>
> They are wrong at the identity level and partially right at the causal level. The missing gnome is an ordinary maintenance technician who got close enough to the real reason the Manifold broke that someone arranged his disappearance. His notes map the same planar architecture KP operates in because he was independently mapping it from the inside. He is alive, hiding somewhere in Neverwinter's lower docks district, and he is completely ordinary.
>
> The name — when it eventually surfaces — is the reveal. Do not surface it this session. Let the players carry the KP assumption as long as possible. When they finally find him in a basement or a ship hold, relieved, ordinary, delighted someone came — the name lands as its own joke. He is not KP. He is Bimble Nackle.
>
> **Do not surface Bimble this session.** Plant the hook and let the players form the assumption. When Bimble eventually surfaces — next session or the one after — he should be found in a basement or a ship hold, relieved, ordinary, delighted someone finally came.
>
> The Manifold is KP-adjacent. Toril's treaty status is KP-adjacent. The gnome is not KP. All three of these things are true simultaneously.

### Cullen Sharpe arrives

Five minutes after the delivery, a human man in well-tailored dark green makes his way to their table. He introduces himself as **Cullen Sharpe**, associate to House Margaster. He is, somehow, still very pleasant.

> *"Lord Cassian. Protocol Shell-Crackers."* He knows the party name. *"House Margaster maintains a prior salvage claim on the piece — documented, if you'd like to review the Harbor authority's writ. We're prepared to offer a fair market rate. No need for this to become complicated."*

He is addressing the table. Treating the statue as still in play.

The subtext the party has that Cullen doesn't know they have: House Margaster killed the person they were coming to help. Cullen is either unaware of the operational specifics, or he is very good at not knowing things. His pleasantness is structural — he does not need to be anything else.

**What Cassian does:** Says nothing for a beat. Then: *"I'll let our guests respond."* He has had this conversation before. He is watching to see what the party does with it.

### Branch — if the party confronts Cullen about Elara's death

> *"The death of Elara Seasong Meliamne is a tragedy. House Margaster had no involvement in that matter."* A beat. *"The Harbor authority's salvage claim is a separate administrative procedure."*

He doesn't flinch. He is either telling the truth about what he knows, or he has been briefed to tell this truth precisely. This is the more frightening version.

### Branch — if the party negotiates

Cassian has resources — House Meliamne commercial assets. He can offer something tangible in exchange for support in formalizing the counter-claim. He needs a week; Elara's paperwork gives him the legal standing. He needs the party to not cooperate with Margaster's timeline.

### Branch — if the party refuses Cullen outright

Cullen thanks them graciously and leaves. He is building a file. The next contact from House Margaster will not be Cullen.

### The Vukradin pitch (regardless of how the statue lands)

Before he goes, Cullen pivots — pleasantly, as if this is a separate topic entirely:

> *"Separately — and I raise this only because I understand your party has commercial interests in the Phandalin hinterlands — House Margaster has developed considerable expertise in regional distribution since the unfortunate disruption at the docks. We're in a position to arrange the transfer of goods to rightful owners efficiently and discreetly. If your colleague — the bard — finds himself in need of that kind of arrangement, we'd welcome the conversation."*

He doesn't wait for an answer. He's planted the seed. He leaves.

> **GM-only — what "transfer of goods to rightful owners" means:** This is Margaster's salvage language applied to a logistics pitch. Their actual business model is intercepting goods (from wrecks, from disrupted supply chains like the Manifold failure) and charging to return them to "rightful owners." Cullen uses the phrase naturally because from his perspective it's accurate in both senses. He is offering Vukradin a distribution partner. He is also describing what Margaster does.
>
> **The pitch is targeted:** Vukradin has the obsidian sword he treats as "stolen goods pending documentation" — has refused to sell it, refused to declare it, refuses to circulate it. He has mine output that needs to reach market. He is the deflationary bard the Candlekeep pamphlet was written about. Margaster is offering to solve that problem. They are the wrong solution.
>
> **Vukradin isn't here to hear this.** The party has to decide what to tell him when he arrives — and whether to mention that the company offering to handle his distribution is the one that killed Elara eight days ago. Cullen was very pleasant about it.

### NPC notes
- **Lord Cassian Meliamne:** Sea elf, older, running controlled grief. Wants the statue; has it. Wants something done about House Margaster; doubts it will happen through official channels. Is passing Elara's threads to the party because he doesn't have the operational capacity to pull them himself. Voice: careful, precise, completely without self-pity.
- **Cullen Sharpe:** Professional, contained, pleasant in a way that is actually more frightening now that the party knows what his employers do. Voice: the tone of someone explaining a parking fine. He has been doing this long enough that the weight of it doesn't show.

## Scene 3A — Valphine's Golden Problem (woven throughout, or its own scene if they linger in the Enclave)

### The mechanic
Valphine's eyes are permanently molten golden. She cannot hide this. Every religious figure in Neverwinter will react.

**In Scene 1 (arrival):** A gate guard does a double-take. Asks if she's afflicted. Backs off when she says it's divine.

**In Scene 2 area (Protector's Enclave):** A priest of Lathander — conventional, mid-level, devoted — sees her from across the market. Stops walking. His expression cycles through awe, confusion, and theological alarm in about three seconds. He approaches.

> *"Forgive me — the Dawn Father's mark is unmistakable. I have studied it in scripture. I have never seen it in a living face."* He pauses. *"You are Drow."*

He's not hostile. He's just trying to process information that doesn't fit his model. He will invite her to the nearest Lathanderite shrine. He will ask, politely, whether her order has been registered with the Church's central authority. He will become progressively more uncomfortable as her theology surfaces.

**If Valphine describes her doctrine (pain = holiness, light = wound):**
The priest has the expression of a man who has just learned that the map he's been using is of the wrong country. He's not going to condemn her. He doesn't have the authority. He says he'll need to write to Neverwinter's senior Lathanderite cleric. He says this in the tone of a man who is already composing the letter.

> **GM note:** This is not a combat scene and not a social obstacle. It's a mirror. The priest is a reasonable man encountering something genuinely strange. Play him curious and slightly frightened, not obstructionist.

**The Order of the Gauntlet angle:** If the party passes near the Hall of Justice, a paladin of Tyr flags them — not aggressively, just names recognition. Ser Kaelen's correspondence has reached Neverwinter. The Order knows this party. The paladin is assessing Valphine's eyes with something between respect and concern.

**The Ashmadai angle (plant, not resolution):** If Valphine is in the Enclave for more than an hour, at some point a well-dressed tiefling with gold eyes watches her from across the Seven Suns Coster Market for a moment too long, then leaves. She may or may not notice. This is a Mordai Vell associate. Do not escalate this today.

## Scene 4 — Soma and the Blue Lake (if they reach Bluelake — probably late session or next session)

### Setup
**Bluelake District** is quieter than the Enclave by day — mostly living quarters, older merchant fronts, a few stubborn noble estates. The Blue Lake sits at its center. But this district is nocturnal: honest residents are at work elsewhere by day and lock their doors at night. Its true occupants don't emerge until dark.

**Getting there:** To reach Hazel's lab (adjacent to the lake, between the Tatters and the Lakeside area), the party passes through or near **The Tatters** — Nasher-controlled residential territory. Nobody stops them. Armed parties don't get jumped on first contact. But they are clocked.

*Read-aloud when the party passes through The Tatters:*

> "You get an uneasy feeling when entering this area. In general, it does not seem to be much activity here, but looking around you can see people look at you through their windows. When you look back, their gaze disappears, and the windows are empty. You can feel your every action be thoroughly inspected from afar."

**The Flickering Ember** sits on the route between the Tatters and the lake — a general goods shop run by **Horst**, an older human man with a merchant's pleasantness and something wound very tight underneath it. The shop is a Nasher weapon-laundering front; a hidden passage beneath it (DC 15 Investigation) connects to the **Nasher Warehouse** two blocks away.

> **CRITICAL — Valphine flag:** Years ago, Horst was seduced and robbed by a young drow sorcerer named **Valanthe**, who vanished with his possessions. His hatred of drow — and sorcerers, particularly drow sorcerers — has calcified into something he no longer bothers to rationalize. If Valphine enters the shop, Horst will be professionally warm. Within the hour, he will **send people after her.** He does not need provocation. He needs to see a drow.
>
> If neither player is drow or sorcerer, he is just a shopkeeper with a slight edge, who will offer coin for information on any drow or sorcerer in the city.
>
> **This is a timer, not a trap.** Don't spring it in the shop. Let the attack come later — at Hazel's lab, at the inn, mid-conversation with someone else. The moment Horst sees Valphine, the clock starts. The party won't know why.

**Vellgard Manor** is visible from the district's main road — three stories, metal gates, inner wall that could stop a small army. It belongs to **Mordai Vell**. If the party asks a local about it, they'll be told it's a private noble estate, nothing more. It is the operational center of the Ashmadai in this district. The same tiefling associate the party may have noticed watching Valphine in Scene 3A was dispatched from here.

> **GM note:** Don't name Vellgard Manor as Ashmadai unless the party digs for it. Let it be background texture — an imposing building they notice and probably don't investigate this session. File it.

**The Nasher Warehouse** is a short distance from the Flickering Ember — looks like a storage facility. Guards outside try to blend in as laborers. Empty by day; active at night. Hidden hatch to the Ember (DC 15). Not relevant this session unless the party goes looking for trouble.

> **Why this scene lands (GM-only):**
>
> Soma knows this lake. She left her family on the coast near Neverwinter to see the world, ended up in the city, and became a guide for travellers heading to Phandalin — that's how the party met her. She worked out of Neverwinter for long enough to notice the Blue Lake's properties and to not know what to do about them. The water has been wrong for a while. She watched it and filed it under things she couldn't address, which in her old naturalist framework she would have called "natural succession" — Adabra's language, the vocabulary of someone who has decided that watching is the same as wisdom.
>
> She left. She got Meril's staff. She cleared corrupted henges and Abolethic nodes and argued down Adabra at the council. She has five leaves. She comes back to the lake that troubled her when she had no tools, and now she has tools.
>
> She does not explain any of this. She walks to the edge of the water and the staff reacts and she doesn't look surprised. She looks like someone whose suspicion has just been confirmed.
>
> The Adabra axis is the emotional spine: the old Soma would have stood here and told herself this was natural. She knows now it isn't. She knows now what "motivated blight" looks like. The question the scene asks is not "what is this?" — she already suspects — but "what does she do about it with the tools she has?"

Meril's Staff gives Soma warmth when they approach the lake — not the warmth of something good, but the warmth of something *active*. Like pressing a hand to a wall and feeling a heartbeat on the other side.

**Hazel the alchemist** (her lab is adjacent to the lake, between the Tatters and the Lakeside area): short female human with amber eyes, welding goggles pushed up on her forehead, soot on her face, a big smile that today is stretched thin. She sees Soma's staff first.

> *"Oh. Oh, that reacts. Can you — can you bring it closer to the water?"*

**What Hazel knows:** She's been studying the sludge at the lake's bottom for months. It has anomalous magical properties. She thought it was Spellplague residue. Six days ago, overnight, something new appeared in the sludge: structures. Not natural formations. Geometric. And when she looked at them through her scope, some of them had what she describes as "apertures" — features that look like eyes.

She hasn't published this. She's not sure she should.

**What Soma's staff shows her:** The staff's leaves tremble when she stands at the lake edge. If she holds the staff in the water, the fifth leaf grows slightly — the one that grew during the Falcon's Lodge storm. It's not fully extended. It's *reaching*.

Below the lake, in the Underdark beneath Neverwinter, something is maintaining something. This is not Spellplague residue. This is a deliberate process. The geometric structures in the sludge are the surface echo of whatever is below.

> **GM-only:** This is Abolethic Sovereignty influence. The aboleths are using the water column as a contact medium. The sludge is psychic residue from prolonged Aboleth presence. The "eyes" Hazel is seeing are forming because the aboleths are *looking back*. Mayor Soman Galt is their surface agent; the lake is their long-range sensory apparatus. Soma should detect that this is intentional and deeply unnatural — not resolve it.

**Hazel's ask:** She doesn't want treasure. She wants someone to tell her she's not imagining things. She would also like to know if someone has a way to go *under* the lake without drowning. She hasn't been able to arrange that part.

**When the players ask how to fix it:**

They will ask. Give them a real answer.

Hazel has been working on this. The lake is a symptom, not the source — she figured that out two months ago when she established that the sludge is being *produced*, not accumulating. Something below is generating it continuously. She has mapped three possible entry points to the Underdark beneath Neverwinter: a collapsed cistern at the base of the Bluelake bluffs, a sealed maintenance tunnel under the old Alagondar aqueduct, and the flooded lower level of a warehouse on the eastern quay that nobody has owned in thirty years.

She hands them her notes. They are meticulous. She has been waiting for someone to give them to.

Soma's staff confirms the source is below — the fifth leaf isn't reaching toward the lake, it's reaching *down*. Surface intervention won't touch it. The node generating the contamination is in the Underdark, and that's where it has to be addressed.

> **What the players leave with:** A dungeon entrance (three options), Hazel's research, and the knowledge that fixing the lake means going down. This is not a dead end — it's a marked door. They can come back with preparation, with Vukradin, with a plan. The session ends with the problem scoped and the path visible.
>
> **What they don't get today:** Any sense of what's actually down there, how large the node is, or whether Soman Galt knows they've been asking. He does. The lake is his eyes.

## Scene 4B — House of a Thousand Faces (optional — if the party wants intelligence)

A comfortable tavern in the Bluelake District, built inside a former boutique clothing store for nobles. The interior is full of mirrors and mannequins in antiquated fashions — the reflections give it the name. Lush couches, quiet conversation, a fire that actually draws well. It feels out of place in this district, which is the point.

**The basement** is the Neverwinter chapter's **Harper** meeting place.

The Harpers in Neverwinter have a complicated recent history: a senior member was caught acting as a double-agent between Neverember and the Sons of Alagondar. Nobody is certain what they were actually trying to accomplish. The chapter survived but is operating carefully, vetting anyone new very slowly.

**What the Harpers know that's useful:**
- They have been watching the Manifold situation (the broken interplanar shipping device at the docks) and suspect it wasn't mechanical failure. They don't know who or what caused it.
- They know Bimble Nackle (the missing maintenance gnome) by name. They haven't found him. They'd like to.
- They have a file on House Margaster that they would share, cautiously, with a party that demonstrated they weren't aligned with either Neverember or the Sons.

**How to access this:** The Harpers don't advertise. A party member who makes a discreet inquiry — specifically mentioning a need for "historical context on Neverwinter's current political situation" — to the right tavern staff will be offered a quiet seat and a longer conversation. This is a Scene 4B only if the party is the sort to ask. Otherwise, the tavern is just a nice place to drink.

> **GM note:** Don't push this scene. It rewards curiosity, not momentum. If nobody asks about the Harpers, the House of a Thousand Faces is just atmosphere.

## Scene 5 — The Board Laid Bare (optional — Tower District, any point)

### Setup
**Location:** Tower District, in the shadow of the Cloak Tower. Small building, no visible signage from the street.
**Trigger:** This scene activates if the party moves toward the Tower District at any point — or if Soma, unprompted, suggests somewhere for a meal without explaining her reasoning. She won't explain until they're inside.

When Soma worked as a guide out of Neverwinter, the Board Laid Bare was the one place in the city that felt like home. A Tortle chef in a city is uncommon enough that she would have sought Lim out by instinct — the way anyone does when they find someone like them somewhere they didn't expect. They are not close, exactly. But they know each other in the way that requires no explanation.

> **GM-only:** Lim has been running this restaurant for at least six years. No one knows who owns it. Bellows has been there the entire time. The food is inexplicably cheap — locals have two theories: conjured ingredients, or black-market suppliers. Both are wrong; neither theory will be confirmed this session. Lim has watched the orc occupation of the Cloak Tower from across the street, watched Neverember's militia fail to evict them, watched the orcs leave of their own accord. Three weeks ago, associates of the Many-Starred Cloaks arrived and spent a day inside the tower — specifically in the basement levels. They left empty-handed. She noticed because she notices everything. She doesn't know what they were looking for.

### The arrival

**Bellows** meets them at the door. He is tall, very still, and states the table options with precision unusual even for servers.

> *"Three tables available. The table near the window offers the best view of the tower. The corner table is preferred by guests who do not wish to be observed. I recommend the corner table."*

He does not follow up. He takes them to a table.

If anyone asks what's on the menu:

> *"What we have today."*

He places a handwritten card. Fresh fish, fried vegetables, potatoes, two sauces. No alcohol. The prices are wrong — too low by half. If anyone names this:

> *"It is priced to achieve full occupancy."*

This is accurate and completely unhelpful.

### Lim's entrance

Lim comes out of the kitchen when she has a moment. Older than Soma by Tortle reckoning — shell worn smooth, unhurried. She is carrying a tray with what she has decided they want rather than asking. She sees Soma. She puts the tray down.

> *"You came back. I thought you'd gone somewhere northern."*

She is not sentimental about it. She sits down across from Soma, which is not something she does with most tables.

**What Lim will volunteer, if the conversation opens:**
- The Tower District has been quieter since the orcs left. Not safer. Quieter is different.
- The Cloak Tower had visitors three weeks ago — the Many-Starred Cloaks' people, or at least they dressed that way. They came in by the basement stairs. She watched from across the street. They were there most of the day and they left without bringing anything out.
- She doesn't know what they were looking for. She noticed because whatever it was, they didn't find it.

**If the party asks about Halavar's Universal Pantograph** (if they've heard the name from any source):

Lim: *"The coin thing? People have been looking for that since before those orcs moved in. I stopped thinking about it."* A pause. *"If it were easy to find, someone would have found it."*

If they push on why the Cloaks' people would look now:

Lim: *"Because something changed. They weren't looking in the tourist places. They came in from the basement stairs."* She picks up the tray and goes back to the kitchen. *"I don't know what changed."*

> **GM-only — Pantograph thread:** Either the device is still in the tower below the visible floors, hidden well enough that professional Many-Starred Cloaks associates couldn't locate it — or it was moved before they arrived. This is a Cloak Tower dungeon hook (see `notes/neverwinter/tower_district.md`).
>
> **Campaign resonance:** The Candlekeep Working Paper No. 27 thread lands differently when the party learns there's a Netherese device in this city that duplicates coins. The economists say adventurers who don't circulate wealth are like dragons. A coin duplicator is an answer to that problem — and probably a catastrophic one. Let that sit unresolved.

**What Lim doesn't know and won't answer:**
- Why the food costs what it costs (*"Good suppliers."* End of topic.)
- Who owns the restaurant (*"It runs itself."* End of topic.)
- What Soma's been doing (*she asks this question instead of answering it*)

### NPC notes
- **Lim:** Older, unhurried, not remotely impressed by adventurers. Wants Soma to eat. Wants to know where she's been. Wants to continue running her restaurant without complications. The mystery of the restaurant is real — she is not its mastermind; she is genuinely uninvested in explaining it. If pressed harder, she becomes less helpful, not more.
- **Bellows:** Literal and precise. Every question gets a technically accurate answer that misses the point entirely. If someone asks who owns the place: *"The proprietor is not on premises."* He is not being evasive. He simply does not have the information, and he doesn't think that's a problem.

## Neverember's Invitation (deliver at any natural pause — last 15 min ideal)

A steward in Lord Protector's livery (lion device, deep blue) finds the party — they're visible enough in the Protector's Enclave that this isn't hard. He is the kind of man who has been arranging meetings for powerful people for twenty years and has the patience of stone.

> *"Protocol Shell-Crackers. On behalf of Lord Dagult Neverember, Lord Protector of Neverwinter, I extend a formal welcome to your party. The Lord Protector has followed your recent activities with considerable interest. He wishes to arrange a meeting at your earliest convenience."* A beat. *"He particularly wished to speak with the bard. The aasimar. Vukradin."*

The steward's name is **Aldus Hern**. He will note, neutrally, that the bard is not present. He will arrange the meeting for whenever Vukradin is available. He is professionally impervious to questions about what Neverember wants — "the Lord Protector prefers to speak directly." He has the Candlekeep pamphlet in his satchel. He does not produce it.

> If the party asks what Neverember wants with Vukradin specifically:
> *"The Lord Protector has an interest in Neverwinter's cultural future. And, separately, an interest in the economic questions recently raised by the Candlekeep report regarding adventuring parties and the circulation of wealth."* He pauses one beat. *"He is particularly curious whether your colleague is aware of the comparison some economists have drawn between his financial philosophy and that of a large reptile."*

Aldus Hern is not being cruel. He is describing the situation accurately. He is the most gently devastating man the party has ever met.

## Anticipated Player Questions

**"Where do we start?"** → The Seven Suns Coster Market is the obvious hub. Brewbarry-core merchandise is there. The Moonstone Mask appointment (Cassian, not Elara) can be picked up from there. Valphine's eyes cause a reaction wherever they go in the Enclave. Just start them moving.

**"What's the Neverwinter Commission office?"** → Near the Hall of Justice; the clerk's name is **Mira Tollund**, a methodical human woman who has this conversation with adventurers approximately twice a month. She has the pamphlet. She is sympathetic and immovable. Renegotiating the commission cut requires either a meeting with Mayor Soman Galt (bad idea — he's an aboleth puppet) or Lord Neverember himself (which is the meeting already being arranged). File for next session.

**"Can Soma wild-shape and swim to the bottom of the Blue Lake?"** → Yes. The sludge at the bottom is cold, dark, and slightly bioluminescent. The geometric structures are there. They are large — each structure maybe six feet across. They look like architectural features — arches, lattices — but made from the sludge itself. They shift when she approaches. The "eyes" are apertures in the lattice work that are actively oriented toward her. She can make it back to the surface easily. She will feel observed for the rest of the session.

**"Does anyone recognize Valphine's order?"** → Not yet. The militant Lathanderite order is too new; it has no formal name recognized outside Phandalin. The priest who approaches her knows the divine mark but not the institution. This is useful — she gets to define it before anyone else does.

**"Where's Vukradin?"** → "Attending to business in Phandalin." The party is not obligated to explain further. Aldus Hern does not need an explanation. The fashion fans are faintly disappointed but file this under "enigmatic absence," which is on-brand.

**"Can we meet Neverember without Vukradin?"** → Aldus Hern will arrange it. But Neverember will spend the meeting asking about Vukradin, which is annoying and slightly humiliating. Better to wait. (This is also true mechanically — the studio deal needs Vukradin present to execute.)

**"What's that big manor with the gates in Bluelake?"** → Any local they ask will call it a private noble estate. A DC 14 History or a good Insight on the local's reaction will tell them the person they're asking is being careful about how they phrase that. Mordai Vell's name is known in Bluelake the way a landlord's name is known in a bad neighborhood — acknowledged flatly, not discussed.

**"Are there Harpers in Neverwinter?"** → Officially, the Harpers are present across the Sword Coast. Locally, they're not advertising after the double-agent incident. The right question in the right bar (the House of a Thousand Faces, Bluelake) opens a door. The wrong question to the wrong person does nothing.

**"Why is someone following Valphine / attacking us?"** → If Horst's timer fires this session, the party has no immediate in-fiction way to connect the attack to the shopkeeper they passed on the way to Hazel's lab. This is the intended state. Let them theorize. If they investigate (DC 13 Investigation, asking around the Tatters), a local will describe "an older man who runs a goods shop" who sometimes "takes an interest" in certain visitors. Don't give them Horst's name without work.

**"Is there a graveyard in Neverwinter?"** → Yes. **Neverdeath**, in the Bluelake District.

**"How does Soma know that Tortle chef?"** → Soma worked out of Neverwinter as a guide for years before the party met her. A Tortle chef in a city is rare enough that she would have found Lim on instinct. The depth of that history is a GM decision — the scene gives you room to leave it sketched rather than explained. Let Lim's response do the work; the party will read it correctly.

**"What's the coin duplicator in the Cloak Tower?"** → Halavar's Universal Pantograph — a Netherese device that can duplicate any metal object up to throne-size, including coins. Believed to be somewhere in or below the Cloak Tower; no one has found it, including the Many-Starred Cloaks' associates who searched three weeks ago. If it exists and works, it is the most economically dangerous object in Neverwinter.

**"Why is the Board Laid Bare so cheap?"** → Nobody in the Tower District knows. The two running theories are conjured ingredients or black-market suppliers. Neither Lim nor Bellows will give a satisfying answer. This is a deliberate open question — don't close it this session. Tall walls around it. Residents of Neverwinter burn their dead now because the Spellplague caused corpses in Neverdeath to animate and attack. The graveyard is sealed, not cleared — the walls contain it. If Valphine asks whether she can do anything there from a Lathanderite perspective, the honest answer is that the undead inside are numerous, varied, and have been accumulating for decades. It is not a one-session problem. It is the kind of problem that would make someone's name.

## Stage Directions

**The Brewbarry moment:** Leave a beat after the fan recognizes him. Don't fill it. Let Stephane decide what Brewbarry does with being seen. If he gets emotional under the comedy, stay there.

**The Cullen Sharpe conversation:** Cullen should be *pleasant*. The menace is structural, not tonal — and it lands harder now because the party knows what his employers did to Elara. Let that weight sit with the players; don't narrate it. Cullen doesn't know they know. He's pleasant because he doesn't need to be anything else. If the players try to read his alignment from his manner, they get nothing useful.

**Hazel's reveal about the sludge eyes:** She says "apertures" very carefully, like she's trying to be precise about something that might be nothing. She says "that look like eyes" in a quieter voice. Do not elaborate. Let Soma's staff do the work.

**Aldus Hern's pamphlet reference:** Time it. Let the session breathe before dropping it. Best moment: after Brewbarry has been recognized as a fashion icon, after the statue is delivered, after at least one thing has gone well — *then* Aldus mentions the dragon comparison. The contrast is the joke.

**If the session ends mid-thread:** Good. Neverwinter is not a place that wraps up cleanly. Any mid-scene ending is an invitation to come back.

**The Lim/Soma reunion:** Don't announce that they know each other. Let Lim putting down the tray and sitting across from Soma do the work. The players will read it. If Soma's player (Wade) picks it up and runs, stay out of the way. If he doesn't, Lim can ask directly where Soma's been — that names the relationship without narrating it.

**Bellows:** Play him flat. Do not editorialize his answers. Do not linger after he speaks. The comedy lives in the silence that follows a technically correct non-answer. Any GM commentary kills it.

**The Orsik broadsheet:** If any player engages with the plaque or the portrait, keep it brief — don't narrate significance, don't say "you recognize him." Let them read and ask questions if curious. The scene's full dramatic weight belongs to Dave (Vukradin/Orsik), who isn't here. File it. The underground Stroudite reading of Orsik is based only on his visible choices (went home, declined appointments) — the circlet is a closely guarded secret that requires a trip to Parnast and a conversation with Vardis to surface. Don't hint at it here.

**The Tatters read-aloud:** Deliver it straight, without narrating that it's Nasher territory. Let the paranoia land before the explanation. If they ask what faction this is, tell them. If they don't ask, let them carry the unease into Hazel's lab.

**Horst (if the timer fires):** Don't signal it. Horst was pleasant. The attack comes from strangers on a side street, with no apparent motive. The party will try to read alignment, gang affiliation, reason — give them nothing obvious. The knife-edge is that it feels like city randomness, not a personal hit. That ambiguity is the hook.

## Open Decisions (for the GM — don't decide for the table)

- **Prutha:** Is he with the party in Neverwinter? He's devoted to Valphine, which means he'll react to the Lathanderite priest confrontation. If present, he complicates every social scene in interesting ways. If left in Phandalin, simpler but less rich.
- **Boney in Neverwinter:** A skeletal horse in a city is unusual. Does the party leave him at the Shaundakul Stables? Does he react to anything in the city? Lady Alagondar's connection to the Alagondar royal house — and the Vault of the Nine under Castle Never — is a thread for a later session, but if Boney reacts on approach to the Bluelake area (where Castle Never is), that's a free hook.
- **The Ashmadai watcher:** Does Mordai Vell make a play for Valphine contact this session or wait? Seeing her in the Enclave is enough for him to send someone. The approach can be a note under her door at whatever inn they choose, or a face-to-face from a charming intermediary. Hold until you know what session pace allows.
- **The Displacement Manifold:** Linene Graywind mentioned this device stopped working. Cassian now carries Elara's research and names it formally. Is this the session you want the party to register it as significant, or background noise for now?
- **The "gnome who serviced the Manifold hasn't been seen since":** This is either a missing persons hook or a dead body waiting to be found. Your call when to surface Bimble Nackle.
- **Lord Cassian Meliamne's counter-claim:** Elara filed formally; Cassian inherited the paperwork. If the party supports him, they are now inside a legal process with House Margaster — one that cost Elara her life. Does Cassian have the means to pursue it, or does he need the party to do the operational work while he provides the legal standing?
- **Lord Verres:** Vukradin established this Neverwinter court scandal in narration (doc: `notes/Lord Verres - Neverwinter scandal.md`). It's available texture if the party ends up in a social setting where court gossip surfaces. No obligation this session.
- **Horst's timer:** He's already running if Valphine entered the Flickering Ember. The attack can happen this session (late, inconvenient) or early next session. Late this session creates a cliffhanger; next session gives you a better setup window. The attack itself can be a street mugging by a couple of Nasher-adjacent thugs with no obvious motivation — or you can escalate to a more deliberate ambush if you want the party to ask harder questions.
- **The Dead Rats / mine connection:** Zeleen Varnaster and the Whiskered Gang (party's mine wererats) are an offshoot of the Luskan thieves' guild. The Dead Rats under Rsolk in Neverwinter's sewers are the same guild, different city. This is a dormant connection you can activate at any point — either as leverage (someone Zeleen answers to operates under Neverwinter's Bluelake), or as a complication (the Dead Rats have heard the party is in town and want to know who authorized wererats in the Phandalin territory). Don't surface this session unless the party goes into the sewers.
- **The Harper double-agent:** Who were they actually working for and why? This is a cold case in Neverwinter. If the party engages the Harpers at the House of a Thousand Faces, this question will come up. You need a position on it before that conversation. The most interesting answer is that they were trying to protect someone, not betray someone — but that's your call.
- **Neverdeath and Valphine's order:** Clearing Neverdeath would be a significant feat for a militant Lathanderite order to hang its reputation on. It's also a multi-session dungeon with decades of animated dead. File as a "Valphine's order goes from local temple to regional institution" arc beat — but not before they have more people.
- **Lim's history with Soma:** How much shared history do they have? Did Lim ever give Soma a standing corner table, warn her away from certain parts of the city, or hold something for her? The texture of that relationship is yours to define — Soma's player will lean into whatever feels earned. Start minimal and let the table push if they're interested.
- **Halavar's Pantograph — what changed?** The Many-Starred Cloaks' associates searched the Cloak Tower three weeks ago and left empty-handed. Something prompted that search. What was it — new information about the tower's interior, a changed magical signature, someone selling a tip? This determines whether the Pantograph hook is a dungeon arc (it's still inside and well-hidden) or a city-wide mystery (someone moved it before the Cloaks arrived, and it's now elsewhere in Neverwinter).
- **Orsik's contested symbolism:** The Lord's Alliance is distributing "Orsik as institutional champion" propaganda in the Enclave. The underground Stroudite counter-narrative is based on what's publicly visible (he went home, declined appointments) — the circlet abandonment is a secret that requires going to Parnast and speaking with Vardis to uncover. The Neverwinter layer of this thread is: where does the party encounter the underground Stroudite reading, and who is that contact? The Parnast layer (the circlet truth) can only happen when the party goes there. Hold both until Vukradin is present — this thread is most load-bearing for Dave.
- **The Board Laid Bare's impossible economics:** Is this actually a mystery with a resolution, or perpetual ambient strangeness? If you want a payoff, the most interesting one is that the ingredients are conjured by something in the basement — possibly connected to the Netherese resonance in the Cloak Tower across the street. If you don't want a payoff, Lim and Bellows are just inexplicable, which is also fine.
