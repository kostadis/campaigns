# Woodland Manse — Arrival Scene Prep

*Status: staging notes (notes/, not canon). Structural call (Manse as Grannoc-interrupt + Chosen-forcing beat) is DM-approved. Campaign overlays and intel reveal are **Claude-proposed** pending DM confirmation. Rulings table is consistent with `circle_of_thunder.md` — same logic applied.*

---

## Design call

This is the **second parallel ritual interrupt** — same shape as the Circle of Thunder, different stakes.

**Key difference from the henge:** Per Flenz's letter, *"If the manse falls, I summon myself"* — the Chosen ("K.") personally takes over Gorthok summoning when Grannoc dies. So **interrupting Grannoc's ritual forces the Chosen into the open** as the only remaining summoner. That makes K. the natural boss for the climactic Gorthok fight, wherever it lands.

This is the right shape because:
- It rewards the party for moving fast (Manse before Lodge)
- It prevents the party from quietly snuffing out the cult by killing middle managers — the head reveals itself
- It makes the eventual Counterattack a Chosen-led event, not an anchorite-led one

---

## Where the party is coming from

- They've left the Circle of Thunder hilltop, after the post-battle scene
- They have the intel from Flenz's letters, Narux's sketches, and Yargath's burned scrap (if they searched all three caves)
- They know: Grannoc + Manse, Lodge as target, the Chosen "K.", "boy from the eastern clan"
- They have Prutha with them; Boney with them; Xanth has departed
- Soma's staff is quiet for the first time since the cursed clearing

The Manse is to the northeast (per published geography — the trail north from the Manse map leads to the Circle, so the Circle is *north* of the Manse; the party is coming *down* from the henge).

## Travel beat (short)

Half a day's march through Neverwinter Wood, descending out of the foothills. The forest changes:

- The blight visible around the henge thins out
- Pumpkin vines appear along the trail — runners with fat, sickly orange fruit, growing in patches the forest shouldn't support
- The boars start. Single ones first, then pairs, snuffling in the undergrowth, watching the party pass and **not running away**
- The fog comes up off the ground in patches and stays there. Sound dampens.

If they're paying attention, this is the cult's outer defense layer: the disguised anchorite boars (W1) range farther than the published radius. They're being watched.

**Soma's staff:** No leaf reaction yet, but the staff is heavier in her hand than it was an hour ago. *Different* heavy than the henge — that was sharp, this is dull and waterlogged.

## Approach to the clearing — RAW boxed text

When the party comes within sight of the manse:

> Following a trail through the woods, you come to a foggy clearing, in the midst of which stands a crumbling two-story manse all but hidden beneath thorny ivy. The large house is set atop a six-foot-high stone foundation, and the main entrance has a balcony above it. Pumpkins grow wild in patches around the manse, with several wild boars feeding among them. The boars snort with contempt as you draw near.

**Campaign overlay to add:** The fog is thicker than the henge fog. It pools around the foundation. The pumpkin patches form a rough triangle around the manse — nine patches, one boar per patch. If anyone counts the boars (Insight, Perception, or just a player thinking about it), there are exactly nine. *Too even.*

## W1 — Pumpkin Patches (RAW + campaign overlay)

**RAW:** 9 boars total. 6 are real boars. 3 are disguised anchorites in boar form. d6 roll if the party engages a specific boar (5–6 = anchorite). Anchorites ignore the party until they try to leave (per "Leaving the Manse" RAW) — they're standing watch.

**Campaign overlay — Prutha's read:**

If Prutha is at the edge of the clearing with the party, he stops. He smells the fog, looks at the boars, and says quietly:

> *"Three boars wrong. Smell wrong. Stand wrong. Eastern Heart boars eat with heads down. These eat with eyes up."*

This is **Prutha's gift** — a free identification of the three anchorites without a roll. Players who use him as a scout get this. Players who leave him at the back of the formation don't.

(DM note: do not give this away if Prutha isn't explicitly involved in the scouting. It's a reward for using him as a partner, not a freebie.)

## The decision point — how the party approaches

Three plausible routes, each with different downstream consequences. Don't railroad — let them pick.

### Route A — Stealth around the pumpkin patches (RECOMMENDED PATH)

Skirt the patches, enter via the balcony (W2 — DC 15 Athletics) or the kitchen (W6 — back side, less guarded). Stay quiet through W8 (courtyard with the Gulthias tree) and into W10 (Grannoc's ritual chamber).

**Reward:** They catch Grannoc mid-ritual without him knowing. DC 15 Religion to recognize the ritual; any prep caster (Vukradin, Valphine) auto-succeeds given the henge precedent. Silence / Hold Person from the doorway = clean interrupt without the W18 orcs even knowing.

**Cost:** Slow. Multiple stealth rolls. If anyone fails, see Route C.

### Route B — Scout, talk to the disguised anchorites

Some party member tries to ID and confront the disguised anchorites in the patches *without* killing them — e.g., Vukradin or Valphine wants to talk. The anchorites will hold their cover as long as they can; they break and attack when threatened, OR they break and flee toward Grannoc to alert him.

**Reward:** Information about Grannoc's defenses, possibly the orcs in W18, possibly the Gulthias tree if the anchorite slips.

**Cost:** Almost always alerts the manse. Now Grannoc has a turn or two of prep before they get to him. Roll initiative on entry.

### Route C — Frontal assault

Party clears the pumpkin patches first, then storms the front door. The disguised anchorites and 6 real boars all fight. Loud as anything. Grannoc and the W18 orcs both hear it (per RAW "Sounding the Alarm" mechanic).

**Reward:** No surprises later — they fight everyone in sequence and come out the other side.

**Cost:** Grannoc has multiple turns to potentially finish his ritual. *This is the path where Gorthok actually gets summoned at the manse* — the only path where the party doesn't get the interrupt cleanly.

## W10 — Grannoc's ritual chamber

The published encounter, when the party catches him mid-ritual:

> What used to be some sort of laboratory lies in ruins, its furnishings broken and heaped against the walls. In the middle of the room, painted on the floor with mud, is a ten-foot-wide symbol depicting three lightning bolts joined at their tips.
>
> Where the lightning bolts converge, a half-orc wearing hide armor performs an eerie dance while consuming the entrails of a dead possum. Standing around the half-orc are several small twig figures.

**Enemies:** Grannoc (anchorite of Talos, CR 2) + twig blights (3 per PC, not including sidekicks — so ~9 baseline for 3 PCs; bump to 12 if you want to account for Prutha + Boney as effective combatants).

**The ritual:** DC 15 Religion to recognize that Grannoc is *praying for Talos to rain destruction on Falcon's Hunting Lodge.* This is the published RAW text. **In your campaign with the Flenz letter intel, the party already knows this is the second summoning attempt; the Religion check just confirms it.**

**Campaign overlay — what Prutha says under his breath:**

> *"Possum. Coward's sacrifice. He cannot afford a deer here. He is rushing."*

This is the tell. Grannoc is sloppy compared to Flenz and Narux. The henge had a deer; the manse has roadkill. Grannoc isn't ready — he's improvising because the henge fell and the timeline accelerated.

## Rulings — same as the henge

Same logic applies (cross-reference `circle_of_thunder.md` for full table). Short version:

| Tactic | Ruling |
|---|---|
| Silence on Grannoc | Ritual interrupted. Gorthok not summoned at the manse. |
| Hold Person on Grannoc | Same. Bonus: paralyzed + the twig blights are still there → coup-de-grace easy. |
| Disrupt the lightning-bolt symbol (mud on the floor — *break the symbol*) | **Ritual interrupted.** Action to scuff the mud; possibly contested by Grannoc if he's still up. |
| Just kill Grannoc fast | If the ritual was nowhere near complete, no Gorthok. **Most plausible outcome at the table.** |
| Slow grind / party fails to interrupt for 4+ rounds | **Gorthok summoned at the manse.** Roll for him; smashes out of W8 courtyard. Big problem. |

## The "If the manse falls, I summon myself" trigger

This is the load-bearing payoff from the Flenz letter intel.

**When Grannoc dies (or is captured / incapacitated) without completing the ritual:**

- Nothing visibly happens at the manse. The party finishes the room, finishes the W18 orcs, searches Grannoc's quarters.
- **The next time the party is in a position to witness a Talosian storm** — at the Lodge, at Phandalin, or somewhere else — the storm rolls in *without an anchorite to channel it.* That's the Chosen "K." personally summoning. The party sees the storm in a place no anchorite is present, and Gorthok arrives.
- This is the Chosen's first on-screen action. Don't say their name; let the storm itself be the introduction.

If you want a clear narrative trigger: **as Grannoc dies, a single bolt of lightning hits the manse roof from a clear sky.** Read aloud:

> The roof above you splits. A bolt of lightning lances down — not from the storm clouds gathering outside, but from *nothing*, from clear air the fog has parted around. It hits the lightning-bolt symbol on the floor and the symbol burns black. The mud crisps. The twig blights at the edge of the room flinch *toward* the symbol, like dogs hearing a whistle.
>
> Outside the manse, the fog goes still.
>
> Somewhere — you don't know where — someone has just looked up.

That's K. taking notice. Save the rest for later.

## W18 — The orcs sharpening weapons

**RAW:** 4 orcs + 1 per PC (so ~7 baseline), claiming the upper guest room, sharpening weapons for the Lodge attack.

**Campaign overlay — these are Eastern Heart orcs, cult-aligned faction.**

Per the Prutha doc canon, the cult absorbed several Eastern Heart bands. The orcs in W18 are *almost certainly* from those bands. This means:

- If Prutha sees their faces, **he recognizes some of them.** Cousins. Childhood acquaintances. The boys he grew up with who said yes.
- They will recognize him in return. Use their first reaction: confusion, then anger, then the moment one of them — call him **Krath** if you need a name (Claude-proposed) — yells "*Prutha! Thunder-meat! You walk with the drow!*"
- This is the moment to play the Eastern Heart schism on screen. Prutha doesn't beg. He blows the bone whistle. The cult-aligned orcs flinch.

**Then:** combat. They're committed Talosians; per Prutha's rules of engagement (spare those who left Talos, kill committed Talosians), they die. But the scene has texture now.

**Possible outcome:** One of the orcs hesitates. If a player reads the room and tries to extend the dawn-bow doctrine (Vukradin with a Persuasion roll, Valphine with a Religion / Channel Divinity moment, or Prutha himself), there's room for **one defection in the middle of the fight.** Don't bait it; let it happen if a player goes for it.

If a W18 orc defects on the spot, they survive the fight and can be brought to the Lodge band as evidence that the cult orcs aren't all committed. That's leverage Vorga and Old Drubbak will weigh heavily.

## What's in Grannoc's quarters

The published treasure for the Manse (cross-rooms):

- **W5 (Dining Room):** Tarnished silver candelabrum, 25 gp
- **W11 (Library):** *Elvish Idioms* book hiding *Dust of Disappearance*
- **W12 (Master Bedroom):** *Staff of Birdcalls* hidden in the fireplace mantel
- **W13 (Magic Item Vault):** *Cloak of Billowing* + *Immovable Rod* (behind a secret door on the balcony level)

**Campaign-specific intel — Claude-proposed, parallel to the henge correspondence:**

In Grannoc's personal effects (in W10 or W12, your call):

1. **A folded map** (the published one — RAW gives Grannoc a map showing the Circle of Thunder location) — annotated with the same wax sigil from Flenz's letters (circle with vertical bolt), this time **marked with a date** indicating when the Lodge attack was to launch.
2. **A letter from "K."** addressed to Grannoc directly:
   > *"You are not Flenz. You will not have his luxuries. The boy from the eastern clan is now with me. If you fail at the manse as Flenz failed at the henge, the third summoning is mine. Do not embarrass me."*
3. **A bone whistle** — broken in half. From an Eastern Heart shaman Grannoc killed. (This is gut-punch material for Prutha; he can identify whose whistle it was. Don't put a name on it unless you're ready — could be Bratha's, Prutha's great-uncle, which would be devastating.)

The letter is the key item. K. is **escalating their own visibility** — they signed this letter the way they signed Yargath's burned scrap. They are running out of intermediaries.

## Aftermath options

After the Manse falls, the party has the same decision tree as before but with the timer accelerated:

1. **Lodge first** — race to warn the Lodge orcs (Eastern Heart breakaway band) before K. summons Gorthok. **This is the strongest narrative path** because the party has Prutha, has the Manse intel, and now has urgent information for the band.
2. **Phandalin first** — fortify the town and Falcon. K. might target Phandalin instead of the Lodge if the cult is rattled enough.
3. **Hunt K. directly** — but they have no location. The letter is unsigned beyond the initial.

The "lightning from nothing" beat at Grannoc's death is your tell that the timer is now real. K. is summoning. The Gorthok set piece is on its way; the party just doesn't know where it'll land.
