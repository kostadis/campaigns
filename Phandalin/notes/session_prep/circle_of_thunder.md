# Circle of Thunder — Session Prep

*Status: staging notes (notes/, not canon). Structural call is DM-approved. Specific intel content (correspondence, sketches, burned scrap, the "K." signature, "leash" terminology, "eastern clan" reference) is **Claude-proposed** pending DM confirmation before going to the table.*

---

## Design call (DM-approved)

The henge is **not** the climax of the Talosian arc. It is a **ritual interrupt + intel reveal + cave loot**, designed to be a short, atmospheric scene. Gorthok stays uncalled here and is preserved for a later set piece (Counterattack — separate prep).

**Why:** With Vukradin's Silence and the Vukradin+Valphine Hold Person stack, the henge fight would resolve in 2–3 rounds against CR 2 anchorites. Trying to make it a boss fight is fighting the party's tools. Better to lean into it — let them feel competent — and put the Gorthok beat where it can carry more weight (civilians at stake, Lodge / Phandalin context).

---

## Published encounter recap (RAW reference)

**Location:** 90-ft hill, ring of 8 standing stones (10-ft uprights + 3-ft lintels each). Stones can be toppled by combined Str 80+.

**Enemies inside the ring:**
- **Flenz** and **Narux** — Anchorites of Talos (CR 2), wearing rotting boar-head masks, dancing around a deer carcass
- **Twig blights** — 2 per PC (baseline ~6; with Prutha/Boney adjust as needed)
- Storm clouds gathering above

**RAW Gorthok trigger:** "The boar arrives once both anchorites are dead." No qualifier — strict RAW summons him regardless of ritual state.

**Caves (C2–C4):** 3 caves dug under the hill, one per anchorite. Each: entryway with boar-skull warning, boar pen with companion boar (flees and attacks if released by non-anchorite), sacrifice cell (25% chance of harmless deer), anchorite quarters, secret exit tunnel. Three secret trapdoors on the hilltop (DC 10 Wisdom (Perception)) lead down.

- **C2 (NW, Flenz):** lintel carved with a boar chasing a stick-figure. *Potion of Invulnerability* buried in his quarters under a random 5-ft square.
- **C3 (NE, Narux):** bare lintel. *+1 Shield* buried in his quarters.
- **C4 (SE, Yargath):** lintel with three stick-figures struck by lightning. No treasure (Yargath's *Potion of Greater Healing* was on his body in the gully — verify party looted in Ch 39).

## Rulings table

Decide these BEFORE the fight so calls are consistent at the table.

| Player tactic | RAW | DM ruling for this campaign |
|---|---|---|
| Silence both anchorites, then kill | Both dead → Gorthok arrives | **No Gorthok.** Silence breaks the ritual's verbal component; trigger condition (completed summoning) never met. Stones go inert when the chant cuts off. |
| Hold Person both, then coup-de-grace | Both dead → Gorthok arrives | **No Gorthok.** Same logic — paralysis halts the ritual. Auto-crit on melee within 5 ft makes the kill trivial. |
| Topple ≥4 standing stones | Not explicitly RAW | **Ritual breaks.** Published text says the stones "focus the anchorites' magic to make the summoning of Gorthok possible." Toppling enough of them = no focus = no boar. Brewbarry in rage + Path of the Giant enlarge clears Str 80 alone. |
| Destroy/desecrate the deer carcass | Not RAW | **Ritual breaks.** The sacrifice is the ritual's anchor. Soma or Valphine can recognize this on a Religion DC 12. |
| Just kill both anchorites without any of the above | RAW: Gorthok arrives | **Gorthok arrives.** Don't punish players for not finding the interrupt — if they go straight melee, RAW fires. They probably won't, given their toolkit. |

**Shapeshifter ruling:** The Talosian anchorites in this campaign shift to boar/orc forms (Moesko, gully shaman both established). Rule that **shifting is an action**, consistent with prior anchorite combat. Hold Person paralyzed = no actions = can't shift to escape the spell.

## Post-battle read-aloud (when the second anchorite drops)

> The chant has been gone for a while now — first Flenz cut off mid-syllable, then Narux. But the storm above doesn't know that yet. It keeps gathering for one more heartbeat, two — and then comes apart.
>
> The clouds peel back from the henge in a slow, reluctant spiral, like something exhaling. The pressure in your ears drops. The ozone smell fades to wet leaves and old rain. The standing stones, which had been humming faintly under your palms, go inert.
>
> The twig blights stop capering. One by one they sag, twitch, and crumple into ordinary deadwood at the foot of the deer carcass.
>
> No thunderclap. No boar.

**DM note:** Pause. Let them feel the win.

## The bodies and the henge

> The two boar-head masks have rolled free of the bodies. Beneath them are half-orc faces — younger than expected. Flenz has tattoos up his neck: storm-clouds in coiling spirals, each one with an open eye. Narux's tattoos are different — boar-tusks crossed, repeated down both forearms. Different markings; same hand inked them.
>
> The deer at the center has been split open from throat to belly. Its heart is gone. Where the heart should be is a hand-sized hollow of black, glassy material — not stone, not metal, cool to the touch, faintly humming the way the stones were humming a minute ago.

**Loot from bodies:** Standard anchorite gear. Flenz wears a leather thong with a **single boar tusk** inscribed with a binding glyph — Valphine recognizes on Religion DC 12 as *"a leash."*

## Prutha's reaction

Prutha approaches but stops at the edge of the henge. Doesn't enter the ring. To Valphine, quietly:

> *"My great-uncle said dawn comes for everyone. Even these ones. I did not think dawn would feel quiet."*

He watches the storm disperse and lets out a long breath — first to actually relax.

## Soma's staff

The three leaves on Meril's staff stop trembling. Near the crown of the staff, **a fourth bud — tight, green, the size of a thumbnail — has appeared.** It wasn't there before the fight. It hasn't opened. It's waiting.

The staff is quiet for the first time since the cursed clearing. The growth is silent.

**DM note:** For Soma's player. The staff *gains* leaves as Soma accepts her role — never loses them. The new bud is the signal that this fight earned her *progress* toward a fourth leaf, not the leaf itself. Hold the opening for a bigger arc moment (Active Intervention, Planar Harmony, or a decisive Counterattack beat). No score change tonight — wait to see how she takes it.

## Intel reveal — Claude-proposed, confirm before running

Three pieces of evidence distributed across the caves. Players have to actually search to get all three.

### 1. Flenz's correspondence (C2 satchel)

Three letters, same hand, sealed with **a wax sigil: a circle with a single vertical bolt through it.** None of them are signed by name. Excerpts to read aloud:

> *"Your storm holds. Grannoc's does not. He is reckless; he will summon before the Lodge is ready. Restrain him through the usual channel."*
>
> *"The Chosen is pleased with the boy from the eastern clan. Continue feeding him with stories. Do not yet tell him about the leash."*
>
> *"If the henge falls, the manse summons in its place. If the manse falls, I summon myself. The boar comes. The storm comes. You are not to fail twice."*

### 2. Narux's sketches (C3)

Crude vellum diagrams of Gorthok (four-legged silhouette as large as a building, lightning along the tusks) with annotations in Orcish:
- *"feed before unleashing"*
- *"the leash holds only if the stones sing"*
- *"three valleys was Yargath's count"*

Plus a **torn map fragment** showing Neverwinter Wood with three marked sites: the henge (X'd through in advance — they expected losses), the **Woodland Manse** (circled twice), and **Falcon's Hunting Lodge** (drawn as a target).

### 3. Yargath's burned vellum (C4 firepit)

Mostly ash. One surviving line, **different hand from the letters** — sharper, more practiced:

> *"…dawn cannot rise where the storm already lives. — K."*

## What the party can piece together

- The henge was **one of at least two summoning sites**. The other is the **Woodland Manse**, where the anchorite **Grannoc** is preparing the same ritual.
- **Falcon's Hunting Lodge is the target.** Both rituals point at it.
- There is a **Chosen** — addressed in correspondence, unnamed, signing only "K." in the burned scrap. Recruiting from "the eastern clan" (Prutha's clan — let him react).
- Gorthok is described as a **leash**, not a creature. The cult thinks of him as something they hold, not something they ally with.

**If asked about "the boy from the eastern clan,"** Prutha's face goes still:

> *"My clan is the eastern clan. The boy is not me. There are not many of us left who would say yes to them. I would like to know which one said yes."*

Seed for the Chosen's identity. Don't reveal more tonight.

## Forward hooks (for next session prep)

The party's decision tree after the henge:

1. **Manse first** — race to interrupt Grannoc before he summons Gorthok. Cleanest play; rewards quick action; Counterattack never fires.
2. **Lodge first** — try to warn / fortify the Lodge before the storm reaches it. Complicated because the Lodge is held by the displaced Icespire orcs (refugees, not cult — see Lodge orc dossier prep, separate file).
3. **Phandalin first** — statue delivery is still pending; Phandalin is potential Counterattack target if everything else is delayed.

**Per Flenz's letter:** If the Manse falls (Grannoc dies before completing his ritual), **the Chosen "K." summons Gorthok personally** — which makes K. the natural climactic boss for whenever you stage the actual Gorthok fight.
