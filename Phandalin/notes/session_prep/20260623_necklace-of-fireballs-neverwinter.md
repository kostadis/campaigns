# The Grave-Heir's Bargain

Co-GM working doc. Not canon yet — staging for the Neverwinter session where the party "returns" the Necklace of Fireballs to the owner Ser Kaelen found.

**Tone:** Heist / complication. **Owner:** a front — wrong hands. **Party:** Protocol Shell-Crackers, **level 7**.
**In-fiction:** Neverwinter, Protector's Enclave (the Moonstone Mask) → the Docks / eastern quay. Date stamp your call (see Open Decisions).
**Timing:** this is **not** the arrival session — it's a **follow-on (Ch. 47+)** that pays off Cullen Sharpe's Ch. 46 distribution pitch. Run the arrival prep first.

## How this slots into the existing prep (read first)

This session is **the trap Cullen Sharpe set in Ch. 46, now sprung.** The established prep already built every piece; this doc only adds the necklace as the *object* that moves through them. Don't re-invent — connect:

- **Cullen's pitch was the bait.** In the arrival/Vukradin-present docs, Cullen Sharpe (House Margaster) offers Vukradin help with *"the transfer of goods to rightful owners."* That is Margaster's actual racket, stated to the party's face: **intercept goods, manufacture a "rightful owner," charge to "return" them** — all laundered through **Harbor-authority salvage writs.** The Necklace of Fireballs is the first time the party *takes that service* — except here Margaster isn't returning the party's goods, it's using a forged "owner" to pry a weapon loose. Same desk, same writ, same lie that killed Elara.
- **Elara is dead — use Cassian, not Elara.** My earlier draft leaned on Elara Meliamne as the provenance check; the canon (session 20260407, not yet in the stale grounding docs) is that **House Margaster had her killed eight days before arrival.** Her cousin **Lord Cassian Meliamne** now carries her anti-Margaster case and her Neverwinter commercial/genealogical reach. He is the provenance lever now — and helping the party expose a *second* Margaster forgery is exactly the case he's building.
- **The Manifold / Bimble Nackle guardrail still holds** (see below). The necklace-Fixer and the missing gnome stay in separate boxes; let the players do the wrong connecting.
- **Theme lock:** Vukradin's *"return every item to its rightful owner"* is, to Neverwinter's economists, the **deflationary-bard** behavior the whole arrival arc needles (Candlekeep Working Paper No. 27). Margaster's "return to rightful owners" service is the *wrong solution* to his exact compulsion. This session is that thesis weaponized: his virtue, routed through Margaster's machine, becomes the delivery mechanism for an arson weapon. If you ran the Golden Ledger Audit, Rimardo already told him *"you have not refused dirty money — you have merely laundered it through paperwork with better penmanship."* This is that line made literal.

## What you (the GM) need to hold in your head

The Necklace of Fireballs was **not** a generic loot drop. Vukradin pulled it from a **sarcophagus in Dragonbarrow** (ch33) — the Alagondar burial mound, the same one where **Lady Alagondar's ghost** rose and stared north toward the Ice Spires. The Alagondars are **Neverwinter's deposed royal dynasty**; Lord Neverember rules the city they once held. So this object is:

1. **A weapon** — seven beads, seven Fireballs, a portable arson arsenal. (The Carver's whole plan was to *burn Phandalin*. That register is alive.)
2. **A royal relic** — grave-goods of the bloodline Neverember supplanted. Whoever "owns" it owns a claim.

Ser Kaelen, doing exactly what he does — coordinating artifact recovery, being *helpful* — ran the necklace through his recovery ledger and his contact **matched it to an heir.** That contact is funded, one link removed, by **House Margaster** ("blood money"). The match is a forgery. Kaelen does not know that. **He was managed by his own source, and he is about to manage Vukradin into the same trap in good faith.**

That is the whole engine — and here's the sharp part. Vukradin **already distrusts Kaelen**; the docs are blunt that his distrust is *"deep and warranted."* So this isn't a betrayed-trust story. It's worse: his *virtue* — **return every item to its rightful owner** — is the one lever strong enough to make him act *through a man he doesn't trust anyway*. Margaster didn't have to fool Vukradin. They only had to fool **Kaelen**, and let Vukradin's own ethic carry the weapon the last mile. The trap is built out of the best thing about him.

> **GM-only — who actually wants the necklace, and why. Two parties, two different wants — that gap is the whole mystery.**
>
> **Perrin is a Nasher / Son of Alagondar loyalist** — the restoration faction loyal to the deposed dynasty, the same Nashers already on your Bluelake map (Horst's shop, the Tatters, the Nasher Warehouse) and the faction at the heart of the Harper double-agent thread (Neverember vs. the Sons of Alagondar). Perrin wants the necklace as a **royal relic** — a recovered Alagondar treasure to legitimize the cause, a banner. He may be a sincere true believer (recommended) or a cynic wearing the colors (your call — see Open Decisions). Either way, *he is not the real threat.*
>
> **House Margaster is the hidden hand** — funding/steering the Nasher cell as a deniable front, because Margaster wants the **seven beads as a weapon** (deniable arson at a Northern target — Phandalin, a Lord's Alliance asset, a Neverember property; pick at the table) **and** the longer game of a **destabilizing Alagondar pretender** to embarrass Lord Neverember. Margaster does not care about restoration; the Nashers are the cover, not the client.
>
> **So the two men at the handoff want different things from the same object** — Perrin reveres a *symbol*, the Fixer eyes *ordnance* — and a sharp party notices the seam. The party only needs to smell *one* thread (the weapon, the pretender, or the Perrin/Fixer mismatch) to start pulling. **And the cruelest truth underneath all of it:** the necklace is **Lady Alagondar's grave-goods** — a dead queen's burial offering, not a transferable heirloom. The party knows this better than anyone alive: they have her ghost's memory and **Boney, her own horse.** There is no rightful living Alagondar owner. The Nasher claim is sincere and hollow at once.
>
> **This plugs into apparatus that already exists in canon — don't invent it fresh.** The forged "recovery office" and the Fixer's warehouse are *the same machine* the arrival prep already established. Margaster's Neverwinter racket is the **Harbor-authority salvage writ**: they intercept goods (from wrecks, from the disrupted Displacement Manifold supply chain), file a documented "prior salvage claim," and charge to deliver them to a "rightful owner" — exactly the writ **Cullen Sharpe** waves over the mermaid statue ("documented, if you'd like to review the Harbor authority's writ"), and exactly the procedure they used to reach **Elara** after she filed a counter-claim. The necklace's forged Alagondar provenance is *one more writ off the same desk.* That's why the trail has somewhere real to lead — and why exposing it strengthens **Cassian's** case. Possible deep cut: **Notir Sotorra** (Valphine's missing youngest sister, "possibly with House Margaster") sits inside this apparatus — see Open Decisions before pulling that pin.
>
> **Guardrail — do NOT conflate with Bimble Nackle.** If the party reaches the Manifold, the missing gnome there is *not* part of this trap and *not* KP. That's a separate dramatic-irony thread the players are meant to misread on their own. Keep the necklace-Fixer and the missing gnome in different boxes; let the party do the (wrong) connecting.

## The Win Condition (state plainly to yourself)

The necklace does **not** walk out of Neverwinter as seven anonymous bombs. By session's end the party has (a) recognized the claimant is a front, (b) kept or redirected the necklace to a disposition *they* chose rather than one Kaelen's ledger chose for them, and (c) given Vukradin's **already-deep distrust of Kaelen its first piece of hard proof** — the "blood money / House Margaster" line stops being an unsubstantiated allegation the moment his clean restitution turns out to be Margaster-routed. The session "lands" when Vukradin realizes *doing the right thing through someone else's logistics is not the same as doing the right thing* — that his virtue needs his own discernment attached, not Kaelen's ledger.

**Note on the money:** this is *not* a "refuse the gold and stay pure = win" session. The party **should** end up compensated for the find if they want it — they're broke, the studio's unbuilt, and they earned this treasure. Taking Perrin's purse is a legitimate landing; the only sting is learning later whose coin it really was. The win is *clarity*, not poverty — that the party (and Vukradin especially) chose with their eyes open, money and all.

## The Default Trajectory (what happens if the party does nothing clever)

- They keep the appointment, hand the necklace to Perrin, pocket the purse, shake hands, feel righteous. Perrin and the Fixer vanish — and the beads go to Margaster, not the cause.
- **Within a tenday, somewhere burns** — your pick. The party hears about it secondhand and may never connect it. (If you want the gut-punch: it's a target they care about.)
- Kaelen, embarrassed, never learns his source played him unless the party tells him. The "is he managing me?" question stays unanswered — the *worst* outcome thematically, because it means Vukradin learned nothing.
- House Margaster banks a win and a relationship: they now know exactly which virtue to pull to move this party.

## The Clever Play (so you know when to nudge)

1. **Verify the claim before the handoff.** Anyone who checks Alagondar genealogy (Cassian, Ostavar's Salvage Archive, the House of Knowledge / Oghma library, the Harbor-authority records) finds the claimed line doesn't survive where the writ says it does. **The fastest check is free and in-house:** ask **Boney**, or trade on their memory of Lady Alagondar's ghost — they *know* an Alagondar, and Perrin's story can't survive it.
2. **Notice the necklace is grave-goods, not an heirloom.** Perrin should grieve a person; instead he quotes a *cause*. He reveres the dynasty and knows nothing of the woman whose tomb it was. Tell, not law.
3. **Split Perrin from the Fixer.** They want different things — Perrin a banner, the Fixer a bomb. Show the loyalist that Margaster means to walk the beads to the house, and he flips: a sincere Nasher is the party's *ally*, not their enemy. Turning him beats beating him.
4. **Follow it up, not down.** Perrin leads to the Nasher cell *and* to Margaster's exploitation of it; the Fixer leads to the house. Either thread reaches the corrupt Harbor-authority desk (Drest) that proves it all.
5. **Use Kaelen instead of trusting him.** He has real reach in this city. Turned, his guilt makes him the best lever they have. The clever party weaponizes his shame productively.
6. **Disposition with intent.** Hand the necklace to the Crown openly (neutralizes the pretender angle), to a temple to be unmade, give it back to Dragonbarrow/Lady Alagondar, or keep it and *own* the responsibility. Any deliberate choice beats the managed one.

## Scene 1 — The Briefing (the favor that feels like love)

### Setup
- **Where/when:** Road's end into Neverwinter, or a quiet room the night before the meet. Kaelen reaches the party by **Sending** (his established channel) or in person if you want his face in the room.
- **Present:** The party. Kaelen (voice or body). Offstage: the "heir," the fixer, Margaster.
- This rides on top of the party's existing Neverwinter business — but by now (post-arrival) they've **already met Cullen Sharpe, already learned Margaster had Elara killed, and already heard Cullen's "transfer of goods to rightful owners" pitch.** Kaelen's errand looks like a clean, unrelated favor from a *different* direction (Phandalin, the Order). That apparent unrelatedness is the hook — they have to *connect* it back to Margaster themselves.
- **Quiet irony for you to hold:** Kaelen's "rightful owner" and Cullen's "rightful owners" pitch are the same desk. The party may walk into the handoff without realizing the favor from home and the murderers from last session are the same machine. Let the recognition land *at the table*, not in narration — ideally on the player who first connects "Harbor-authority writ" to the statue claim.

### Approach — Kaelen makes it easy
Kaelen is warm, specific, and *useful* — which is exactly why it should itch. Lift if it lands:

> "You've been carrying that necklace like a debt. I found who it belongs to." A beat. "An Alagondar. Quiet branch, no title, no army — just a family that lost their dead's belongings to grave-robbers a generation ago and never stopped looking. The recovery office matched the provenance last tenday. You can put it down, Vukradin. You can actually do the thing you keep saying you want to do."

What Kaelen genuinely believes: every word. He is not lying. He is *relieved* for Vukradin. That's what makes it land like love and not management — and what makes the later turn hurt.

> **GM-only — Kaelen's interiority.** He *needs* this to be clean. His funding is dirty and he knows the party suspects it; brokering a flawless restitution is how he proves to himself (and to Vukradin) that the dirty money does clean things. He did not vet the match hard because he wanted it to be true. That's his flaw this session: motivated belief, not malice.

### Branch — party accepts at face value
Proceed to Scene 2 with no guard up. The complication still ambushes them there; they just walk in colder. Fine — this party going off the "trust Kaelen" rail *is* the rail.

### Branch — party interrogates the provenance now
Good. Kaelen can only give them his source's answer: "the recovery office, a clerk named **Havel Drest**, the Margaster-endowed desk." Saying that name out loud *to Vukradin* is the first crack — Kaelen hears himself say "Margaster" and you can let a flicker cross his face. Don't resolve it. Let him talk past it.

### Branch — they ask Kaelen to come to the handoff
He can't / won't (Order business, the optics of an armed knight at a private restitution, your call) — but he gives them a **token** that gets them in the door, which becomes useful leverage later when they need him to vouch. If you'd rather have him present, having Kaelen *witness* the turn live is a stronger scene; see Scene 3.

### NPC notes
- **Ser Kaelen Thorne** — wants this clean so badly he skipped his own due diligence. Voice: precise, knightly, lands on the word *debt* and *belongs*. What he wants from the scene: to give Vukradin a win and, underneath, to launder his own conscience.

## Scene 2 — The Handoff Goes Sideways (the heist beat)

### Setup
- **Where:** A **Harbor-authority notary's parlor** in the Protector's Enclave (or a private room off the **Moonstone Mask**, if you want continuity with the statue scene). Respectable, documented, public enough to feel safe — the bureaucratic veneer *is* the trap. The writ is real city paper; only the claim behind it is forged.
- **Present:** **Perrin** (the claimant — a Nasher / Son of Alagondar loyalist fronting as the recovered relic's heir), **the Fixer** (his "associate" — actually a Margaster operative who answers to **Cullen Sharpe**, there to make sure the *beads* end up with the house, not the cause), a real Harbor-authority notary (hired, doesn't know the score). **Cullen himself** may be present *or* deniably absent — see NPC notes.
- The meet is staged to be *fast*: sign the writ, exchange, gratitude, gone. The speed is the tell.

### Approach — the thing that's wrong
The master tell is that **the two men across the table don't want the same thing.** Perrin treats the necklace as sacred; the Fixer treats it as cargo. Seed 2–3 of these; let players catch any one:

- **Perrin reveres the cause but not the woman.** He can recite the Alagondar grievance — the stolen city, the deposed line, the restoration — fluently. Ask him about *Lady Alagondar herself* (whose tomb this came from) and he has nothing; he knows the *dynasty*, not the *person*. A real heir mourns a grandmother; a loyalist quotes a manifesto.
- **The Fixer knows it has seven beads** before the case is open, and wants it **loose, not in the case** — "the case stays with us." Perrin doesn't notice he's being handled; the relic he thinks is a banner, his partner is counting as bombs.
- **The provenance doesn't touch the claim.** The notary's writ cites the **Dragonbarrow** recovery, but the forged line claims the necklace was *stolen from a house in Neverwinter* — not interred in a *tomb in the Sword Coast wilds.* Grave-goods, not heirloom. (Cleanest hard clue — an Investigation/History or a careful read of the document surfaces it.)
- **Boney / the ghost contradict him outright.** If the party thinks to check Perrin's story against what they *actually know* of Lady Alagondar — through Boney, or their memory of her ghost — nothing he says survives contact. He's claiming kinship to a woman the party's own undead horse knew.

### The money on the table (play this *before* the sideways)
Perrin doesn't ask for charity — **he offers real coin.** Frame it as a restitution reward for recovering a lost relic: a heavy purse, **several hundred gold in actual coin** (or a Harbor-authority draft for more — your number; enough that a party who gave up the 3,000gp Cryovain hoard and still hasn't built the studio *feels* it). He slides it across with the writ. *"The cause does not forget who returns what was taken."*

This is the trap's sweetest tooth, and it's aimed at the whole table at once:
- **For Vukradin** it's a genuine ethical knife. His rule is *return every item to its rightful owner* — and here's an owner offering honest payment for the return. Is a reward "blood money"? It *feels* clean. Refusing it is pure; refusing it also means the party stays poor again because of his conscience. (If you ran the Golden Ledger Audit, this is Rimardo's whole thesis walking up and putting cash in front of him.)
- **For Brewbarry / Soma / Valphine** it's the first time someone's offered them a fair price for treasure Vukradin would otherwise hand back for free. **The party should get compensated for the find** — and they'll *want* this. Real party-internal pressure: his purity vs. their empty pockets.

> **GM-only — the money is the hook, not a gift.** The purse is **Margaster blood money laundered through the Nasher cause** — Perrin offers it sincerely (cause coffers), not knowing its source. Taking it is exactly what Margaster wants: it *binds* the party to the transaction, makes them paid participants in arming the house, and converts Vukradin's act of virtue into a sale. The beads are worth far more to Margaster as a weapon than any purse, so they'll pay generously. **Don't make refusing it the "right answer" mechanically** — let the party take the money if they want; the cost is meaning, paid later, not a GM penalty now. The richest outcome is they take it, *then* learn whose coin it was.

**Pick your sideways.** Choose the complication that fits how your table is leaning:

- **(A) They balk at the table.** The Fixer cuts losses — goes for the necklace by force/snatch and runs; Perrin, blindsided, either bolts with him or freezes (he didn't know it would come to this). → chase, Scene 3.
- **(B) They hand it over, then clock the tell as the pair leaves.** Now it's a recovery: tail, intercept, get it back before it's broken up. → Scene 3 as pursuit.
- **(C) Third party crashes the meet.** **Neverember's Graycloaks / Crown agents** hit the parlor hunting the *Son of Alagondar*, not the party (the Crown watches the Nashers — Harper-double-agent thread). Or a rival Nasher cell, or Margaster cleaning up. Sudden three-way; the necklace is loose on the floor in the chaos. → scramble, Scene 3.

### Branch — combat erupts in the parlor
Keep it *short and spatial*, not a slog. Stakes are the **necklace on the table** and **the Fixer reaching the door**, not HP. A dropped bead is a live Fireball — telegraph that the room itself is now dangerous (even a level-7 party eats a single 7-bead detonation as a brutal hit; the *threat* of it is the scene, never spring it as a gotcha). Failure here = the Fixer escapes *with* the necklace → Scene 3 is a manhunt instead of a cleanup.

### Branch — they let the pair leave on purpose to tail them
Excellent, reward it. Skip the fight, go to Scene 3 as a clean shadowing job, and the Fixer leads them somewhere that *proves* Margaster — a counting-house, a warehouse, a coach with a covered crest.

### Failure is material (not "you take damage")
- Lose the necklace to the Fixer → it's now a countdown, and the party did the losing.
- Tip their hand too early/loud → Perrin bolts, the Fixer goes quiet, and the **Margaster trail goes cold** — they can stop the bombs but never prove who sent them, which keeps Kaelen's source plausibly clean and the trust question unresolved.
- Hurt the **innocent notary** in a Fireball scare → Neverwinter Watch involvement, reputation cost, and Vukradin's "no blood" ledger takes a hit he'll feel.

### NPC notes
- **Perrin (Nasher / Son of Alagondar loyalist)** — fronts as the relic's heir; *believes* he's recovering a royal treasure for the restoration (default: sincere; alt: cynic in the colors — Open Decisions). Wants the necklace as a banner, not a bomb — which is exactly why he's useful to Margaster and exactly why he can be *turned*: he does not know his "associate" means to walk the seven beads straight to the house that wants them as a weapon. Voice: earnest, a little zealous, quotes the cause; gentility that frays when pressed on the *person* behind the relic. He is the party's *door* — to the Fixer, to the Nasher cell, and (if they're kind) to an ally who'd rather have the truth than the relic.
- **The Fixer (Margaster operative, answers to Cullen Sharpe)** — the real professional in the room, riding shotgun on Perrin's cause to make sure the *beads* reach the house. Will sacrifice Perrin without blinking. Voice: economical, never raises it, treats everyone — Perrin included — as a logistics problem. Wants: a clean exit with the necklace; failing that, no witnesses who can name the house.
- **The Nasher / Sons of Alagondar wiring (already on your map).** Perrin's cell connects to the Bluelake Nasher infrastructure — **Horst's Flickering Ember**, the **Tatters**, the **Nasher Warehouse** — and to the city-wide Sons-of-Alagondar restoration thread the **Crown watches** (the Harper double-agent was caught between Neverember and the Sons). Following Perrin *up* leads here; it's also where Margaster's exploitation of the cause becomes provable. (Caution: if Valphine tripped **Horst's drow-hatred timer** in the arrival session, walking the party into Nasher turf to chase Perrin can collide with that — a feature, not a bug, if you want the heat.)
- **Cullen Sharpe (House Margaster) — the face the party already knows.** Per the arrival prep: pleasant to the point of menace, *"the tone of someone explaining a parking fine,"* never the muscle himself. Keep him that way. If he's at the handoff, he's the smiling overseer who "facilitated the restitution as a courtesy" — and his Ch. 46 line about *"transfer of goods to rightful owners"* is now visibly *this.* If the party already clocked Cullen as Elara's likely killer, watching him broker Vukradin's act-of-virtue should turn their stomachs. He stays deniable: the Fixer does the dirty part; Cullen does the paperwork and the smile. **Do not let Cullen panic, threaten, or drop the manners** — his unflappability is the horror, and it's canon.

## Scene 3 — Recovery / Exposure (Neverwinter in motion)

### Setup
This is the heist's back half — a **chase, a tail, or a confrontation** depending on how Scene 2 broke. Terrain: the **Docks district** (Margaster's logistics live near water) or the bridges between Enclave and Docks. Use Neverwinter's verticality, crowds, the river, the half-rebuilt ruins.

### The shape, by branch
- **If pursuing the Fixer with the necklace:** intercept before a handoff to a *third* courier (the bombs were never going to stay in one pair of hands — Margaster compartmentalizes). Catching the Fixer mid-handoff is where the **second courier** reveals the *destination* (the arson target) — that's the intel that lets the party stop the actual harm, not just recover an object.
- **If they have the necklace and are chasing proof:** they're tailing the Fixer *to* Margaster — a warehouse, a counting-house, a crested coach. The goal is **evidence**, the thing that turns Kaelen's "unsubstantiated allegation" into a named fact.
- **If a third party crashed Scene 2:** the party is now negotiating *custody* with Crown/Lord's Alliance agents who also want the pretender. Leverage: the party has the necklace and the forger; the Crown has authority and questions. A deal is possible (see Resolution).

### The lever the clever party finds: turn Kaelen
At any point they can loop Kaelen in (Sending token from Scene 1, or in person). His reaction is the emotional core: **he goes white.** He vouched for **Havel Drest**. He realizes, in real time, that his clean restitution was his dirty money wearing a halo. Played right, his **shame becomes the party's best resource** — he has the Order's reach in this city and will spend it recklessly to fix what he caused. Let the party *use* him. That's healthier than either trusting or discarding him, and it's the most interesting place the relationship can go.

> **GM-only.** Do NOT let Kaelen off the hook with an apology. The good version is Vukradin watching Kaelen be *useful and ashamed at once* and not knowing what to do with it. The answer to "does he love me or manage me" is **both, and he doesn't know the difference either** — that's the truth to leave them with, not a clean acquittal.

### Branch — they grab (or turn) Perrin instead of the Fixer
If he's the **sincere** loyalist (default): the interesting beat isn't interrogation, it's *recruitment.* Show him the seven-beads truth — that his "associate" is Margaster and the royal relic is being walked off as a weapon — and watch the cause curdle in his hands. He gives up the Fixer, the Nasher meeting place, maybe the Drest name, and may *join the recovery* to clean up what he started. But he genuinely **doesn't know the arson target** (Margaster compartmentalized him); he can expose the *fraud and the house* but not the *plan*. They still have to chase the beads — now with a Nasher ally who feels personally betrayed. If he's the **cynic** version, he just sings for his skin; same intel, no ally.

### Branch — they decide to keep the necklace and walk
Totally legitimate. Then the scene is *getting out of Neverwinter clean* with Margaster now aware the party has both the weapon and the knowledge. Reframe Scene 3 as evasion + a parting threat from the Fixer. The necklace-disposition question rolls forward to a later session — flag it.

### NPC notes
- **Havel Drest (the Margaster-endowed recovery clerk at the Harbor authority)** — offstage unless the party digs to him; the seam between Kaelen's "recovery office" and the house. He's the clerk who stamps the salvage writs — a coward with a ledger. If reached, he's the documentary proof that ties Kaelen's source, the necklace forgery, the statue claim, *and* Elara's counter-claim to one corrupt desk. The single highest-value target in the session.
- **Lord Cassian Meliamne (Elara's cousin) — the provenance lever, replacing Elara.** Elara is *dead* (Margaster killed her; see arrival prep). Cassian carries her anti-Margaster case and her Neverwinter commercial/genealogical reach. He can confirm the Alagondar claim is fabricated in one conversation — and he *wants* to, because a second documented Margaster forgery is precisely the case he's quietly building. Voice (per arrival prep): careful, precise, controlled grief, no self-pity. Caution for the party: helping Cassian means stepping inside the legal fight that cost Elara her life.
- **Ostavar, The Salvage Archive (near the Docks) — independent provenance check.** A retired half-elf adventurer who identifies wreck/Underdark salvage and writes honest provenance cards (*notes uncertainty explicitly*). A neutral, non-political way to establish that the necklace is Dragonbarrow grave-goods, not a Neverwinter heirloom — i.e. that the writ's claim is false. Good for a party that doesn't want to owe Cassian or Jenna anything.
- **Jenna Roscoe (Lord's Alliance, Neverwinter)** — if the party wants institutional muscle or a buyer for the *political* angle, she's it. She cares about Neverember's stability and would *love* to know Margaster is minting Alagondar pretenders. Two cautions: Vukradin already calls her a *"nasty no-good treasure hunter,"* so bringing her in trades one manager for another; and she's the one who dismissed the Kraken Society as a "harmless social club," so her threat-read is unreliable — she'll take *this* seriously precisely because it's the political kind of threat she's tuned for.
- **Linene Graywind (Lionshield Coster) — optional fingerprint lever.** Her broken interplanar shipping (the Manifold collapse) is the *same* Margaster-shaped hole in the world; a party that connects "Lionshield can't ship" → "Margaster seized the distribution the Manifold vacated" arrives at the warehouse from a different direction entirely. The Docks city-guide encounter — *the Lionshield factor losing the rate argument to the Margaster factor at the Harbormaster's Table* — is free atmosphere that points the same way. Lateral-thinking gift, not a required path.

## Resolution — what disposition means

Leave this to the party, but know the options so you can play each owner honestly:

- **To the Crown (Neverember's people), openly.** Neutralizes the pretender weapon and earns goodwill — but hands the dragon-treasure-Crown-claim subplot a new wrinkle, and means trusting *another* institution. Clean, political, not free.
- **To a temple to be unmade.** The "no weapon left in the world" answer; very Vukradin. Costs them a useful item and possibly a favor owed.
- **Keep it, own it.** The party becomes the thing they accused others of being — holders of a weapon "for the right reasons." Richest for Vukradin's arc; flag that Soma's ch45 staff-secret already cracked the "we return everything" assumption, and this is the same fault line widening.
- **Back to Dragonbarrow / Lady Alagondar.** The mythic answer — return the grave-goods to the *grave*. Re-opens the dormant "ghost staring at the Ice Spires" hook. If you want a future thread, this is the one that pays later.

## Anticipated player questions

- **"Can we just verify the claim before we hand anything over?"** → Yes, and you *want* them to — Cassian, Ostavar's Salvage Archive, the House of Knowledge, the Harbor-authority records, **or just asking Boney** all crack it. Don't gate it behind a hard DC; gate it behind whether they think to ask. If they don't, Scene 2's tells are the safety net.
- **"Do we get paid? Can we take the money?"** → Yes — Perrin's purse is real coin and it's *theirs* if they take it. Don't punish it mechanically. The catch is whose money it is (Margaster, laundered through the cause), which lands later. Expect this to split the table: Vukradin's instinct is to refuse a reward for returning what isn't his; the rest of the party is broke and will reach for it. Let them argue it out — that friction *is* the scene.
- **"Is this Perrin guy lying? Is he a bad guy?"** → He's a Nasher / Son of Alagondar loyalist who believes he's recovering a royal relic for the restoration. Default: *sincere* — he's being used, not running the con. The real operator is the "associate" beside him. Played right, Perrin is a potential *ally* the party can turn, not a villain to beat.
- **"Is Kaelen in on it?"** → No (default) — and say so only through play, never narration. He's a dupe, not a traitor. If your table would rather he be complicit, that's a bigger, darker campaign turn; don't decide it casually mid-session (see Open Decisions).
- **"Why does an Alagondar relic matter politically?"** → The Alagondars ruled Neverwinter before Neverember; the **Nashers / Sons of Alagondar** still want the line restored. A "recovered royal treasure" in loyalist hands is a banner to rally the restoration — which is why **Margaster** wants to mint exactly that pretender and why **Neverember's Crown** watches the Nashers closely.
- **"Can we sell it / use the Fireballs ourselves?"** → Mechanically yes. Let them — then let the *meaning* land later. This is Vukradin's "no blood money" ledger; the table consequence is internal, not a GM penalty.
- **"What about our other Neverwinter errands?"** → Braid them in. Cassian is provenance-verification AND the live statue/counter-claim thread; the Docks chase passes the Manifold housing (Bimble hook — don't surface him), the eastern-quay warehouses, and the Crown-claim thread. Reward errand-combining with momentum.

## Stage directions (timing / silence cues)

- When Kaelen says **"You can actually do the thing you keep saying you want to do,"** stop. Let it sit on Vukradin. Don't rush to the next beat — the gift has to feel real before it can curdle.
- The first time a player names a tell at the handoff table ("wait — how does he know it's seven beads?"), **don't confirm or deny.** Let Perrin keep talking and the table do the work.
- When Kaelen realizes his source is the rot — **silence.** Don't have him apologize fast. A knight discovering his clean hands are dirty should need a moment. Give it to him on screen.
- If a Fireball bead ever hits the floor, **describe the room going quiet around it** before anyone rolls. The threat is the scene.
- If they hand the necklace to the *real* Crown at the end, let it be **anticlimactic** — a clerk, a receipt, no fanfare. Doing the right thing the un-managed way should feel smaller and better than the staged restitution would have.

## Open decisions (for you — I won't decide these)

1. **Kaelen: dupe or complicit?** Doc is built on **dupe** (preserves the ally, sharpens the trust question, keeps Margaster the villain). Complicit is a campaign-altering choice — decide before play, not in the moment.
2. **The arson target.** What were the seven beads *for*? Phandalin (Carver echo), a Lord's Alliance asset, a Neverember property, or kept vague. Picking a target the party loves raises the stakes hard.
3. **In-fiction date stamp.** Set the Tenday date; the docs don't pin "now" precisely. (Campaign references 1489 DR in the export-regulation background file.)
4. **Perrin: sincere loyalist or cynic in the colors?** Doc defaults to **sincere** (he's being used by Margaster; turnable into an ally; his betrayal-by-the-cause is the emotional beat). Cynic is simpler — a paid front who sings for his skin. Sincere is the richer, recurring-NPC choice; decide before the handoff so you can pitch his reverence accordingly.
5. **How much is the purse, and what's in it for the party?** Set Perrin's reward figure (recommend: enough that the broke, studio-less party genuinely feels it — several hundred gold minimum, or a larger Harbor-authority draft). Decide whether it's pure coin (spendable now) or an instrument that has to be redeemed (which can itself become a Margaster-string reveal). The party *should* walk away compensated for the find if they take it — that's the point you asked for; the only sting is the source.
6. **Does Notir Sotorra touch this?** Valphine's missing youngest sister is "possibly with House Margaster" — and Margaster's Neverwinter apparatus is exactly where she'd sit. Optional, potent reveal: the Fixer could know her, work for her, or *be* her handler. Only pull this if you want Valphine's family arc to detonate here; it will eclipse the necklace and turn a heist into a Sotorra-family episode if you do. Default: keep her offstage, leave a *thread* (a name in a ledger, a drow seen at the warehouse) the party can chase later.
7. **How much Nasher do you want in frame?** Perrin can be a lone front with a thin cell, *or* a genuine doorway into the Bluelake Nasher network (Horst, the Tatters, the Warehouse) and the Sons-of-Alagondar restoration politics the Crown is policing. The deeper you wire it, the more this session bleeds into the city's faction map — powerful, but it stops being a one-and-done.
8. **How much Manifold do you want in frame?** The trap can stay a clean two-desk Margaster job, *or* you can route Scene 3 through the **Displacement Manifold housing / eastern quay** so it shares scenery with the Cassian/Bimble-Nackle hub. More resonant, but watch the guardrail — the missing gnome stays unconnected to this.
9. **Does Cullen Sharpe appear in person, or stay deniable?** In-person makes the gut-punch immediate (the party watches Elara's killer broker Vukradin's act of virtue) but spends Cullen's menace early. Deniable (the Fixer fronts; Cullen is "the name on the writ") keeps him a slow-burn recurring threat. Either is canon-consistent; decide before play.
10. **Session ordering.** This is a **follow-on (Ch. 47+)**, not the arrival session — it assumes the party has met Cullen and learned Elara's fate. If you'd rather, you *can* fold a compressed version into the arrival session's Cullen scene as the bait-and-immediate-payoff, but that crowds an already-full Ch. 46. Recommend: let Ch. 46 plant the pitch, run this later.
11. **Necklace endgame.** If they keep it, where does the disposition question resurface? Queue it now so it doesn't evaporate.
