# Neverwinter Docks — Reference

*Structure, Beached Leviathan interior, Driftwood Tavern, and Tarmalune Trade House from Guide to Neverwinter; earthmote history, citizen psychology, Port Crevice hook, and 24hr rhythm from New Neverwinter.*

---

## District Structure

**Guide to Neverwinter** treats the Docks as a standalone district.

**New Neverwinter** folds the Docks into the **River's District** alongside the Tower District (Cloak Tower, Fallen Tower, Shard of Night). The Docks are a sub-section, not their own district. Use Guide's framing for district identity; use New Neverwinter for the Tower District locations that share the same area.

> **Geographic note:** New Neverwinter places the Beached Leviathan in the Blacklake District. Guide to Neverwinter places it here in the Docks. The tavern's description is consistent between sources — only the placement differs. This reference uses the Guide's placement.

---

## History

Neverwinter's commercial wealth was built on its port — one of the few harbors on the Sword Coast North.

**Spellplague (1385 DR):** Wild magic struck the Docks hard. Chunks of land broke away and rose into the sky, forming **earthmotes**. Surviving residents adapted by connecting the floating motes to shore with ropes, chains, and bridges.

**Mount Hotenow (1451 DR):** The eruption destroyed the earthmote bridges and sent tidal waves that swamped whole sections of the port. The docks became a rotting ghost town — only a handful of fishers and the occasional pirate ship still called.

**Neverember's Rebuild:** Rebuilding the Docks was Neverember's top priority. He ordered the Upland Rise logged for construction timber. By 1479 DR, the Docks were fully operational again — the heart of commerce of the Jewel of the North.

---

## Atmosphere & Rhythm

The Docks are active **24 hours**. Ships dock and sail at all hours; people move at all times of day and night. Unlike every other district in Neverwinter, there is no meaningful day/night divide here.

**Citizen psychology:** Two visibly different types of people occupy the Docks. Visitors are in awe — the harbor bustle, the earthmotes still visible above, the sense of a city reborn. Long-term residents have lived through the orc occupation, the Spellplague, the tidal waves. They are economically required to be welcoming and suppress a real undercurrent of resentment at being marveled at for things that caused their families pain. Play them as *open yet grumpy.*

> **DM note:** If players want something to do after dark without a specific goal, hint that people are moving toward the docks. Honest work and shady figures both operate here at all hours.

## Locations

### The Beached Leviathan

The *Leviathan* was an infamous pirate vessel that prowled the Sword Coast. Its captain, **Harrag**, was feared across the Sea of Swords. Sometime after Mount Hotenow, the Leviathan ran aground in northern Neverwinter and punctured its hull. Harrag stayed as the city rebuilt around the wreck and converted it into a thriving inn and tavern.

**The Neverember arrangement:** Neverember expunged Harrag's extensive criminal record to make the establishment possible, making Harrag a genuine loyalist. Neverember then turned Harrag's own shadow into a **sentient spy creature** to monitor the facility. Neverember's watch is aware of all illegal activity in the Leviathan — and deliberately never intervenes. The tavern is too useful as a passive intelligence source.

**Historical incidents:** In 1479 DR, the Leviathan was attacked during the Neverwinter succession conflict over the Crown of Alagondar; saved by adventurers and General Sabine. Later that year, Ashmadai cultists and demons infiltrated it and killed one of Sabine's advisors as part of the mage Elden Vargas's plot to weaponize Asmodeus's power.

#### Interior Layout

| Level | Name | Contents |
|-------|------|----------|
| First | "The Hold" | Kitchen and main tavern; entered from the docks; tables for all party sizes |
| Second | "The Quarters" | Inn; 12 rooms of various sizes for rent |
| Third | "The Deck" | Private parlor rooms and tables, available for hire at a higher price |
| Above deck | Captain's cabin | Harrag's private quarters |

Levels connected by a central spiral staircase and a pulley-operated cargo lift. Interior is well-crafted oak and cherry woodwork — once elegant, now well-worn from years of brawling. Nautical trophies throughout from Harrag's pirate days.

**Atmosphere:** Nautical slang is universal — newcomers are "privateers," drunkards are "ballast." Gambling is frequent. Brawling is frequent but will get you thrown off the ship. Smugglers, thieves, and press gangs conduct business openly. The occasional drunken sailor is enslaved. None of this is secret.

### The Driftwood Tavern

Located in the **northern docks**. Almost more of a museum than a tavern — upscale, expensive, and predates the Spellplague (in operation since before 1385 DR). After the 1451 cataclysm, it sheltered refugees and served as Neverwinter's makeshift city hall while the city found its footing.

The interior is packed with relics, bric-a-brac, and historical objects — treated as a tribute to Old Neverwinter by regulars and a destination in itself by travelers who pay the premium prices just to sit among the artefacts.

Run by **Madame Rosene** (~70 years old). In 1479 DR she was secretly the leader of the **Sons of Alagondar** (the Graycloaks) — the rebel faction that opposed Neverember's rule. Her faction has since made peace; many members enlisted in Neverwinter's army, judging it better to control their own military than let Mintarn mercenaries hold power. Rosene is a fount of city history and will talk freely to anyone who buys a drink.

> **Intelligence value:** Rosene knows Neverwinter's recent political history better than almost anyone alive. She was inside the Sons of Alagondar. She watched Neverember's rise. She made the call to stand down. She has opinions about all of it and the wisdom to deliver them obliquely.

---

### The Tarmalune Trade House

A large warehouse complex on the **southern docks**, operated by a trade cartel from the distant city of Tarmalune. Outside the warehouse, stalls and booths sell their goods directly. They are aggressively trying to establish a commercial foothold in Neverwinter to counter rival traders from Lylorn who recently set up a route through nearby Luskan.

> **DM hook:** If the party needs something very specific and hard to acquire, the Tarmalune network can probably procure it — but expect a high price and significant wait time. They're not a magic shop; they're a long-haul trading operation.

---

### The Port Crevice *(New Neverwinter only)*

A dockside tavern managed by a social, unnamed young gnome who just giggles when asked his name. Popular with passing sailors — prime location for sea rumors: krakens, underwater dungeons, unexplored treasure, things seen in distant ports.

**Hidden hook:** If the party stays more than one night, they will notice a raggedy orc who sits at the exact same spot every evening, keeping to himself. He is the **last Many-Arrows Orc in Neverwinter** — on a covert scouting mission, looking for an opening for his clan to invade again.

## Active Factions

| Faction | Presence | Method |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Neverember's watch** | Overt patrols, covert monitoring | Never intervene at the Leviathan; use it as passive intel |
| **Harrag** | Leviathan owner | Neverember loyalist; sentient shadow spy operates from his establishment |
| **Smugglers / press gangs** | Throughout the docks | Operate openly at the Leviathan; tolerated by Neverember |
| **City militia** | Harbor patrols | Focus on preventing hostile ships from docking; otherwise permissive |
| **Mercenary crews** | Ship escorts | Hired by individual vessels; no organized presence |
| **Last Many-Arrows scout** | Port Crevice | Covert; scouting for invasion opportunity; appears to be just a quiet regular |

## Adventure Hooks

- **Harrag's Shadow** — Neverember has a sentient spy creature made from Harrag's shadow. What does it report? What happens if the party discovers it? Markul "Lookout" might know — for a price.
- **Ettain's Secrets** — The handyman knows every inch of the ship "including its secrets." What's hidden in the Leviathan that even Harrag doesn't know about?
- **Madame Rosene's Files** — The former Sons of Alagondar leader has been watching Neverwinter's politics for decades. What does she know about House Margaster? The Displacement Manifold? The Ashmadai's reach into Neverember's circle?
- **The Port Crevice Scout** — The Many-Arrows orc is patient and careful. If the party identifies him and reports to the militia, he vanishes. If they approach him, he is surprisingly candid — he doesn't want a war, he wants proof that Neverwinter can be taken. The question is what they do with that information.
- **The Tarmalune Rival** — Lylorn merchants are undercutting Tarmalune's routes through Luskan. If someone wanted to escalate that trade war into something more interesting, the Tarmalune traders have resources and motivation.
- **Neverember's Non-Intervention** — Press gangs are enslaving sailors in the Leviathan. Neverember knows. He does nothing. A rescued/escaped sailor shows up asking for help, and the party has to decide whether to force Neverember's hand or work around it.
