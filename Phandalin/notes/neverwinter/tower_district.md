# Tower District — Reference

*Shard of the Moon history, Cloak Tower lore (Pantograph, animated cloaks, Selûne restoration), and Fallen Tower full history from Guide to Neverwinter; district atmosphere, Cloak Tower read-aloud, Board Laid Bare, and day/night rhythm from New Neverwinter.*

---

## Overview

The Tower District earned its nickname from the concentration of wizard towers that once defined it. Pre-cataclysm it housed Neverwinter's merchant class and its wizard community. Post-cataclysm it was seized by Many-Arrows orcs, who were expelled approximately two years ago. It is now considered cursed or haunted by most residents — the surviving magical towers dominate the skyline and create an atmosphere of mystery and low-grade dread.

**Day/night:** Honest citizens largely avoid the district after dark. Visitors drawn by the towers and their magical phenomena arrive at all hours. The combination produces the district's characteristic duality: long-term residents who carry suppressed resentment at being marveled at for things that caused their families pain, presenting as open yet grumpy to the tourists their income depends on.

> **Note:** Guide to Neverwinter treats the Tower District as a standalone district. New Neverwinter folds it into the "River's District" alongside the Docks.

## Locations

### The Shard of the Moon *(formerly the Shard of Night)*

A floating tower with a long and strange history across the Material Plane and Shadowfell.

**Origins:** The tower began as **Moonshadow Hall** — a temple in the city of **Yhaunn**, over a thousand miles from Neverwinter. In the late 14th century DR, the **Shadovar** (Netherese dwellers of the Shadowfell) assaulted the temple and tore away one of its towers, sending it to the Shadowfell. They discovered they could manifest it at arbitrary locations in the mortal realms.

**Over Neverwinter:** During the Spellplague, the Shadovar positioned the tower floating over Neverwinter. It served as a portal between Neverwinter and the Netherese city of **Xinlenal**. The tower was darkened black and cast no shadow — because it had been magically altered to *absorb* shadow, which powered its teleportation devices. Anyone who entered never returned.

**Current state:** In 1479 DR, adventurers successfully raided the tower and allowed priests of **Selûne** to perform a purification. The tower was restored to **alabaster white** and renamed the Shard of the Moon. At night, when three magical shards are placed within or below the tower, a **stairway of solid moonlight** descends from the entrance. Priests of Selûne admit only trusted visitors. Those who take a long rest inside receive an extra healing surge until their next long rest.

> **Note:** New Neverwinter mentions the "Shard of Night" only in passing, as one of the district's imposing magical structures. All substantive detail is from Guide to Neverwinter.

### The Cloak Tower

The citadel and meeting place of the **Many-Starred Cloak** order of wizards, who served Lord Nasher Alagondar during his reign.

**The disappearance:** During the Spellplague in 1385 DR, the tower — and every wizard inside — vanished entirely. The tower returned approximately six months later, in a new location. The wizards did not. Their **colorful cloaks** remained hanging on the pegs where they had been left, untouched.

**Halavar's Universal Pantograph:** The tower was widely believed to contain a powerful Netherese device recovered from a ruin — **Halavar's Universal Pantograph**, capable of duplicating any metal object up to the size of a large throne. This included coins. The device attracted thieves almost immediately after the wizards' disappearance. None found it. The cloaks came to life and smothered the thieves to death; one survivor escaped to tell what he saw.

**Orc occupation:** The Many-Arrows tribe invaded and claimed the tower. Residents assumed the orcs would trigger the same defenses that killed the thieves. They did not. Neverember had no means to dislodge them and tolerated the occupation. The orcs eventually left voluntarily to help their kin fight dwarves to the east.

**Current state:** The Many-Starred Cloaks have become active again; some ranking members are working with Lord Neverember. Custody of the tower is presumed to have returned to the order. In 1479 DR, adventurers raided the tower and allowed priests of Selûne to perform a purification, restoring it to **alabaster white**.

**Read-aloud (New Neverwinter):**
> *"You noticed this building from afar, but now that you have entered the district you can get a closer look at it. A large stone tower with enormous roots that climb up on the sides of it. Near the middle of the building, parts of the tower have separated from the building itself and are floating next to it. Some of them pillars, some statues, multiple stones are almost static in the air, hovering."*

> **Discrepancy:** Guide to Neverwinter presents the Cloak Tower as restored and returned to the Many-Starred Cloaks. New Neverwinter presents it as an active dungeon — arcane wards, orc traps, and unknown hazards still inside — with the floating debris visible externally suggesting pre-purification state. These may reflect different campaign timelines. If running it as a dungeon, use New Neverwinter's framing; if treating it as a functioning tower, use Guide's.

> **DM note (New Neverwinter):** Good location to reward casters (staves, wands, scrolls) or non-casters (rare books and scripture for the House of Knowledge). One would assume orc traps remain, as well as the wizards' own arcane wards and puzzles.

> **Open question:** Halavar's Universal Pantograph was never found. If it still exists somewhere in or under the tower, it can duplicate coins up to throne-size. This is the most dangerous MacGuffin in the district.

### The Fallen Tower

A wizard's tower destroyed in a dramatic confrontation with the Arcane Brotherhood, now operating as a tavern and the district's only reliable neutral ground.

**History:** The tower was owned by **Llomnauvel "Firehands" Oloadhin** — a powerful and by-all-accounts unhinged wizard. The **Arcane Brotherhood** coveted his collection of magical items and attacked the tower one night. Oloadhin had anticipated the raid and prepared extensively: magical traps throughout, including spells that transformed intruders into sea creatures. The traps slowed but did not stop the Brotherhood.

Rather than let them take his tower and its contents, Oloadhin triggered a final massive destruction spell. The tower came down in a magical blaze. Everyone inside died — including Oloadhin.

**The Nightly Show:** Every night, at the exact moment Oloadhin triggered the spell, **silent phantasmic visions** replay at the ruined base. Brotherhood mages appear ablaze, falling to their deaths. The Brotherhood's Overwizard falls similarly — while his **limbs transform into eels** as he descends. The visions are harmless.

Entrepreneurs converted the spectacle into a ticketed tavern attraction: the ruins renovated, the show railed off, tables arranged around it. The tavern eventually collapsed into mediocrity — overpriced drinks, a steep cover — and was abandoned before the district's wider decline.

**Under New Management:** Many-Arrows orcs discovered the abandoned tavern and its wine stores during their occupation. The phantom show frightened them out the first night. After realizing the visions were harmless, they returned. An enterprising orc named **Vagdru One-Ear** restored it as a functioning tavern. Lord Neverember and the Many-Arrow leadership reached a mutual agreement: the Fallen Tower serves as **neutral ground** for all races in Neverwinter.

Anyone who breaks the peace inside is beaten senseless by the bouncers and — crucially — by the other patrons, who treat the neutral-zone tradition as sacred.

**Current offerings:** Cheap wine and ale, satisfactory. Raw meat of **unknown origin**, served uncooked for preparation over an open fire.

**DM hooks (Guide to Neverwinter):**
- **Technical Difficulties** — Something goes wrong and the previously-harmless nightly visions turn real; the phantasmic flames scorch actual patrons. Adventurers must contain the Fallen Tower's magic before the business is ruined.
- **Diplomatic Immunity** — The Fallen Tower is the ideal location for a parley between adventurers and factions that wouldn't normally meet at a neutral table.
- **The Other White Meat** — Vagdru One-Ear commissions the party to go hunt some level-appropriate monsters and return with the corpses. To the party's horror, he serves the monster meat to patrons as that evening's dinner.

### The Board Laid Bare *(New Neverwinter only)*

A small restaurant — no equivalent in Guide to Neverwinter.

Fresh fish, meat, fried vegetables, and potatoes prepared in a variety of sauces. No alcohol. Calm dining experience. The prices are **surprisingly cheap** and no one in the district can explain how they stay that low. Rumors split between two theories: the kitchen magically produces its ingredients, or the ingredients are smuggled in.

There is **no apparent owner**. The establishment is run by:
- **Lim** — older female Tortle chef
- **Bellows** — Warforged waiter

> **Hook:** A Tortle and a Warforged running a mysteriously cheap, mysteriously ownerless restaurant in a district considered cursed and largely avoided by honest people. The cheap prices are specifically flagged as unexplained by both the text and by locals. This is not ambient flavor — it is a door left open.

## Adventure Hooks

- **Halavar's Pantograph** — The coin-duplicating Netherese device was never found in the Cloak Tower. It may still be there, hidden behind whatever defenses the Many-Starred Cloaks built around it. The surviving thief from decades ago is probably dead, but someone has his account.
- **The Missing Wizards** — The Many-Starred Cloak wizards vanished completely in 1385 DR. Their cloaks stayed. Their bodies were never found. The tower returned without them. Where did they go? The cloaks may know — if the priests of Selûne would permit a conversation.
- **The Real Nightly Show** — Technical Difficulties (Guide hook): the phantasmic visions at the Fallen Tower go real. Scorching flames, actually-falling mages. Vagdru One-Ear needs this fixed before it kills customers and ends his neutral-zone enterprise.
- **Lim and Bellows** — Who owns the Board Laid Bare? How does it stay this cheap? A mundane investigation or a Detect Magic near the kitchen may reveal more than expected. If the ingredients are being conjured, something is powering the conjuration.
- **The Shard at Night** — The three magical shards that summon the moonlight stairway are not in the priests' possession. They were used once; their current location is not documented. Someone who found them could access the tower without Selûne's approval.
- **Llomnauvel's Unfinished Business** — Oloadhin destroyed his tower rather than let the Brotherhood take it. His personal magical collection — the thing they came for — was presumably destroyed with him. But wizards of that caliber rarely keep everything in one place. His real vault, if it exists, is somewhere else.
