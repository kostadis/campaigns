# Bluelake District — Reference

*Physical description, sub-areas, and lore from Guide to Neverwinter; faction buildings, NPCs, and operational detail from New Neverwinter.*

---

## Overview

Formerly the **Blacklake District**, Bluelake was home to most of Neverwinter's noble estates. The sturdy construction of those estates meant most survived the cataclysm physically intact — but their owners largely did not. The abandoned buildings became fertile ground for the city's shadow factions, and the district became extremely dangerous to traverse. Seemingly every criminal faction in Neverwinter — from the Sons of Alagondar to the Dead Rats — took up residence here.

In recent years the situation has improved, but only on the surface. The honest residents work elsewhere during the day and lock their doors at night. The district truly comes alive after dark: weapon-smuggling, stolen goods transport, faction clashes, and wererat raids are nightly activities. Visitors who know better stay away.

---

## Bluelake (the Lake)

Formerly called Blacklake. The Mount Hotenow eruption filled it with ash, leaving it vile and sludge-choked. Lord Neverember made dredging and restoration a priority, eventually renaming it Bluelake. In summer, the water level drops dramatically and the edges become somewhat swampy.

**Current state:** **Hazel**, a cheerful female human alchemist, has been working to clear the lake. She has successfully removed the top layer of sludge, restoring the clear blue water — but a thick layer of **mysterious magical sludge remains at the bottom**. Hazel's analysis was inconclusive: the sludge has strange, almost magical properties whose origin she cannot explain.

> **Hook:** The bottom sludge is an unresolved mystery. Where did the magical properties come from?

## Locations

### Alchemist's Lab
Run by **Hazel** — short female human, amber eyes, light brown curly hair to her shoulders, welding goggles on her forehead, soot on her cheeks. Energetic and welcoming. Located between The Tatters and the Lakeside area. The cheerful exception in an otherwise grim district; her work on the lake is genuine.

*Read-aloud when players meet her:* "Before you stand a short female human with amber eyes, light brown curly hair that goes down to her shoulders with a big smile. She has what seems to be welding goggles on her forehead and some soot on her cheeks and neck. She greets you with a hearty, energetic 'Hello, hi!' and stretches out her hand."

---

### House of a Thousand Faces
A comfortable tavern built in a former boutique clothing store for nobles. The interior is still full of mirrors and mannequins displaying antiquated fashions — their many reflections give the tavern its name. Features lush couches where citizens sit and converse.

**The basement** is the secret meeting place for Neverwinter's **Harper** chapter. The local chapter has a complicated reputation: a high-ranking Harper was caught acting as a double-agent between Lord Neverember and the Sons of Alagondar.

---

### The Tatters
A residential area that appears to be ordinary working-class housing. In reality it is controlled by **Nasher rebels** and serves as their primary living quarters. Anyone entering feels watched — people observe through windows, then disappear when noticed. Locals stay clear. Thugs operate openly here.

*Read-aloud when players enter:* "You get an uneasy feeling when entering this area. In general, it does not seem to be much activity here, but looking around you can see people look at you through their windows. When you look back, their gaze disappears, and the windows are empty. You can feel your every action be thoroughly inspected from afar."

---

### Nasher Warehouse
The main Nasher headquarters in the Bluelake District. Disguised as an ordinary stone-and-wood warehouse with barrels and crates. Guards outside try to keep a low profile. Empty by day; numerous active rebels by night.

**Hidden hatch** (DC 15 Investigation) leads to a tunnel connecting to **The Flickering Ember**.

---

### The Flickering Ember
A general goods shop run by **Horst** — older human man. The goods are a front for **Nasher weapon-laundering**.

Horst has a personal vendetta: during his early days as a merchant, he was seduced and robbed by a young drow sorcerer named **Valanthe**, who vanished with his possessions. His hatred has only grown. If given any opportunity to strike at a drow, sorcerer, or drow sorcerer, he will struggle to restrain himself. If any party member is drow or half-drow, he will send people after them. If neither, he will pay for information on the whereabouts of such targets.

**Hidden passage** (DC 15 Investigation) connects to the Nasher Warehouse.

---

### Vellgard Manor
The headquarters of **Mordai Vell's** Ashmadai cell. A large 3-story stone building flanked by metal gates and an inner wall capable of stopping a small army, with a large garden. Mordai inherited the manor after the 1451 DR cataclysm killed all his human relatives, and repurposed it for the cult.

---

### The Beached Leviathan
A 3-tiered tavern built in and around the refurbished wreckage of the pirate ship *Leviathan*, overlooking the sea to the west. Frequented by criminals, smugglers, slavers, sailors, and merchants. A prime location for rumors and gossip — also a prime location to be mugged, killed, or beaten if you stand out. Staff are well-built, very vocal, and do not mind violence.

> **Note:** Guide to Neverwinter places this in the Docks District; New Neverwinter places it in Blacklake/Bluelake. Either placement works — the tavern itself is described consistently in both sources.

---

### The Sewers
An unmapped sewer system beneath Bluelake. Home to an increasingly large **Dead Rats** wererat gang led by **Rsolk** (also spelled Rsalk — same character). They ambush nighttime traders from the gutters and store stolen goods in a hidden vault somewhere in the sewers. They operate with care: when exposure risks getting too high, they retreat underground. If left unchecked, their activity will deter settlers and merchants from the district entirely.

## Sub-Districts

### The Chasm District
When Mount Hotenow erupted, a giant canyon tore open in the southeastern quarter of Neverwinter, plunging into a Spellplague-laden section of the Underdark. The area was essentially leveled. Unnatural beasts emerged from the rift; anyone who approached experienced anxiety and unpleasant mental effects. A wall was erected to contain the horrors.

With immense effort and expense, Neverember had the chasm **sealed with powerful magic**. The ruins were then declared open land — anyone bold (or poor) enough to settle there may claim it. Settlers are beginning to trickle in.

---

### Neverdeath
Neverwinter's graveyard, divided into two sections:
- **Pauper's section** (southwest): rows of humble, often unmarked graves — nameless souls lost in one of Neverwinter's many disasters
- **Noble section**: elaborate mausoleums for the wealthy and titled

Both sections were severely affected by the Spellplague. In addition to physically altering the landscape, the Spellplague caused **corpses to animate and attack visitors**. Tall walls now contain the graveyard's horrors. Neverwinter's residents **no longer bury their dead — they burn them**.

## Active Factions

| Faction | Base | Method |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Nashers** (Sons of Alagondar) | Nasher Warehouse + The Tatters | Overt thuggery, riots, assassination; anti-Neverember |
| **Ashmadai** | Vellgard Manor | Shadow infiltration of rebels and city guards; devil summoning |
| **Dead Rats** (wererats) | The Sewers | Night raids, theft, ambushes; backing off when hunted |
| **Harpers** | House of Thousand Faces (basement) | Information brokering; complicated local reputation |

The Nashers and Ashmadai share the district uneasily — clashes happen mostly in the shadows, occasionally in the open. The Ashmadai are actively planting agents inside the Nasher ranks and the city guard simultaneously.

## Adventure Hooks

- **The Bottom of the Lake** — Hazel's mysterious magical sludge at the bottom of Bluelake has an unexplained origin. Investigating requires diving in or finding another way to sample the depths.
- **The Flickering Ember's Vendetta** — If any party member is drow or sorcerer, Horst will quietly arrange for them to have an accident. The players may not immediately connect it to the friendly shopkeeper.
- **The Harper Double-Agent** — Who was the high-ranking Harper caught between Neverember and the Sons of Alagondar? Are they still active? What did they actually want?
- **The Sewer Vault** — The Dead Rats have a hidden vault of stolen goods somewhere in the unmapped sewers beneath Bluelake. Finding it means navigating wererat territory blind.
- **Settling the Chasm** — Bold settlers are claiming land in the former Chasm District. Something may be stirring in the newly-sealed ground, or factional interests may have followed the settlers in.
