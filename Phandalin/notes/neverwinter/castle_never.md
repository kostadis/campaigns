# Castle Never — Reference

*Physical description from Guide to Neverwinter; faction content from New Neverwinter.*

---

## Location & Physical Description

Castle Never towers over the western end of Neverwinter and is impossible to miss. It sits on rocky cliffs and outcroppings on the north side of the estuary where the Neverwinter River and the Sea of Swords meet.

The castle has a circular rock around its main structure from which **three bridges** radiate out over the Neverwinter River:
- **Sleeping Dragon Bridge**
- **Winged Wyvern Bridge**
- **Dolphin Bridge**

These bridges are essential city infrastructure, connecting the Protector's Enclave and Bluelake districts. The castle straddles the boundaries of the Protector's Enclave, Neverwinter Docks, and Bluelake districts — best considered its own district entirely.

The castle's interior and exterior are both in severe disrepair. Too dangerous for habitation.

---

## History

Castle Never is over a millennium old, commissioned by the sun elf **Lord Halueth Never**, who conquered the city of Illusk (whose ruins now lie under modern Luskan). Construction ran roughly from -10 DR (conquest of Illusk) to 87 DR (Neverwinter's founding as a city).

In **1451 DR**, the cataclysm (eruption of Mount Hotenow) struck Neverwinter. Castle Never was hit by the pyroclastic flow, killing nearly all inhabitants including the ruling Alagondar lineage. The castle has been haunted by their spirits ever since.

After the **Spellplague of 1385 DR**, wild magic altered the ward protecting the Neverneath catacombs, giving it a malevolent sentience.

## Interior Locations

### The Atrium
Now boarded over to keep sunlight out and the heat of rot inside. Contains an eerie display of exotic bird skeletons in gilded cages. Currently home to territorial **Myconids** that will attack and feast on intruders.

### Hall of Mirrors
A long hall where nobility once practiced gait and posture. Any mortal intruder triggers a magical recreation of the hall's destruction — shards of flying mirror glass and fiery ruins are launched at anyone who lingers too long.

### Neverneath (the Endless Maze)
The magically protected catacombs beneath Castle Never, originally built to maintain the castle's structural integrity and protect the buried corpse of **Lord Halueth Never**. After the Spellplague, the ward gained a malevolent sentience and the ability to reshape corridors at will — creating dead ends, tunnels that loop back, and doors that open to new locations each time. Anyone who enters risks being trapped in an infinite magical loop full of **deadly traps and animated statues**.

> **DM Note:** The Neverneath is not general knowledge — seed it as tavern rumor. Content is for higher-level parties.

### Vault of the Nine
The tomb of the **Neverwinter Nine** — the royal bodyguards of Neverwinter's kings and queens before the cataclysm. Located in the depths of Castle Never.

- **Outer chamber:** Everburning torches that light when mortals enter. Undead lurk in the shadows.
- **Inner chamber:** Sealed behind a giant stone door bearing the sigil of Neverwinter. Nine marble coffins encircle a throne. Visitors who rest here may awaken to find the spirits of the Nine standing vigil.

**The Crown of Neverwinter** is rumored to be here — a legendary item said to shield the wearer from extremes of heat and cold, and the deciding factor in legitimate succession. A false heir is allegedly burned by frost and fire. Rumors of the Crown have resurfaced recently, making control of the Vault a major political flashpoint.

> **DM Note:** The Vault is rumor-level knowledge, not public — best seeded at a tavern or from other adventurers.

## Active Factions (Current Situation)

As of ~1479 DR, Castle Never is an active battleground. No single faction controls it.

### Valindra Shadowmantle (Thayan Forces)
A necromancer operating inside the castle. Neverember suspects she is attempting to **resurrect Lord Halueth Never** and use him as a puppet. Clearing her out will require strength, not stealth.

### Beholder Talgath
Controls a portion of the castle interior, contesting the Thayan forces.

### The Nashers (Sons of Alagondar)
A violent anti-Neverember faction based in the Bluelake District. Their leader, **Arlon Bladeshapper**, claims descent from one of the Neverwinter Nine and believes the Vault will prove his legitimacy to the throne. Symbol: a minotaur head slain by a sword. Methods include assassination, theft, sabotage, and rioting — no concern for civilian casualties. They frequently clash with other rebel factions over their brutality.

### The Ashmadai (Devil Cult)
A secretive society with members burrowed into every organization in Neverwinter. They worship or fear **Asmodeus** and bear a curious brand on their chests. Two internal sects:
- **Mordai Vell** (tiefling) — manipulative, shadowy; close to Neverember personally
- **Favria** (dwarf) — power-hungry, essentially runs a faction of loyal thugs

The cult's long game: remove Neverember from Neverwinter and install a puppet they control in both Neverwinter and Waterdeep.

### The Dead Rats (Wererat Thieves' Guild)
Operating from the sewers around Bluelake. An offshoot of Luskan's thieves' guild. Leader: **Rsolk**, a one-eyed wererat. Goal: use Neverwinter as a hub for criminal activity across the Sword Coast.

## Neverember's Restoration

Lord Dagult Neverember has made Castle Never's restoration a city priority — a symbolic pursuit of Neverwinter's rejuvenation, likely to take years or decades, funded by high taxation.

The first functional area restored: the **dungeons ("The Holes")**. High-crime convicts are sent there; executions happen daily at noon, two days after sentencing.

---

## Adventure Hooks

- **Murderous Merchants** — High taxation for the restoration has enraged powerful merchants with dangerous connections. Neverember needs the party to protect him and eliminate his would-be killers.
- **Sentimental Relics** — An elderly noble lost a loved one in the cataclysm. Tasks the party with entering the ruins to retrieve a relic from their body.
- **Construction Blocked** — Restoration crews hit a magical barrier they cannot breach. The party must investigate.
- **The Necromancer's Gambit** — Valindra Shadowmantle is inside the castle attempting to resurrect Lord Halueth Never. If she succeeds, Neverember loses political legitimacy and a puppet ruler walks.
- **The Crown's Heir** — The Crown of Neverwinter resurfaces as a rumor. Multiple factions (Nashers, Ashmadai, adventurers) are now racing to reach the Vault of the Nine first.
