# Neverwinter — proper-noun inventory

Complete inventory of NPCs, locations, factions, items, and concepts
extracted from campaign reference documents in notes/neverwinter/.

This reflects the **campaign's working picture of Neverwinter**, not raw source material.
For session-specific NPC adjustments see relevant notes files.

## NPCs

### Principals

- **Aletra Sotorra** — Valphine's sister; on-screen agent of cosmic entity KP; first documented direct intervention against Neverwinter was the Displacement Manifold ultimatum to the Commission
- **Arlon Bladeshapper** — Leader of the Nashers (Sons of Alagondar), claims descent from one of the Neverwinter Nine, seeks the Crown to prove legitimacy
- **Dagult Neverember** / **Lord Neverember** / **Lord Protector** / **Neverember** / **Open Lord of Waterdeep** — Lord of Waterdeep who arrived in 1467 DR with Mintarn mercenaries, proclaimed himself Lord Protector of Neverwinter, and founded the New Neverwinter movement; ousted as Open Lord of Waterdeep in 1489 DR
- **Gommoth** / **King of Hotenow** — Fire giant who leads a sect of fire-worshipping creatures and has proclaimed himself king of Mount Hotenow

- **Harrag** / **Captain Harrag** — One-legged former pirate captain, owner of the Beached Leviathan, Neverember loyalist with his shadow converted into a spy creature
- **Len-jes** / **Harbormaster** — Actual harbormaster of Neverwinter; watersoul genasi; semi-regular at the Beached Leviathan
- **Liset Cheldar** — Half-elf proprietor of the Moonstone Mask; friendly and openly flirtatious, ownership claim ambiguous but politically accepted by Neverember
- **Lord Neverember** / **Neverember** — Current lord of Neverwinter; brokered neutral-zone agreement at the Fallen Tower with Many-Arrow leadership

- **Corrin** — Manifested counterweight to KP alongside Rimardo; Savras's instrument; part of the Counterforce
- **Rimardo** — Manifested counterweight to KP; Savras's instrument; part of the Counterforce operating through individuals rather than institutions

- **Iymrith** / **the Desert Wind** — Ancient blue dragon and true architect of the War of the Giants; engineered disruption of the Ordning; escaped to Avernus rather than being defeated [Campaign Canon]
- **Jawbone** — Thay lieutenant last spotted near the eastern Enclave; summons undead when confronted
- **Severin** — Leader of the Cult of the Dragon who attempted to manifest Tiamat in the mortal realm; defeated at the war's end
- **Talgath** / **Beholder Talgath** — Beholder controlling a portion of Castle Never's interior, contesting Thayan forces
- **Zariel** — Archdevil, co-resident of Avernus with Tiamat; true architect behind Tiamat's summoning attempt — used Severin and the Cult of the Dragon as her loophole to expel Tiamat from Avernus

- **Seldra Tylmarande** — Used a replica Crown of Neverwinter in a male disguise to falsely claim the throne in 1479 DR; plot failed

## Deities

- **Asmodeus** — Archdevil worshipped or feared by the Ashmadai cult, whose members bear a brand on their chests
- **Bahamut** — Dragon deity worshipped by Milo Goodbarrel (cleric) and the Order of the Gauntlet
- **Corellon** — Elven deity; Vardis is a cleric of Corellon (Light Domain), exiled from every temple for theological flexibility
- **Helm** — God of protection; one of the patron deities of the Order of the Gauntlet
- **Hoar** — God of retribution; one of the patron deities of the Order of the Gauntlet
- **KP** — Gnome wizard / planar optimizer; offstage structural antagonist; cosmic entity whose on-screen agent is Aletra Sotorra; documented in docs/KP.md
- **Lathander** — Dawn deity; Valphine Sotorra became a devoted follower after her religious experience with sunlight on the surface
- **Lolth** — Spider goddess, trapped in a demi-plane as part of a multi-century plan; her pawn Gromph Baenre's spell inadvertently triggered the Out of the Abyss crisis
- **Myrkul** — God of death; active in the North (Dragon of Icespire Peak canon), potential architect of undead presence at Pirates' Skyhold
- **Oghma** — God of knowledge; primary deity of the House of Knowledge, Neverwinter's great library
- **Savras** — Deity whose manifested counterweight (Rimardo and Corrin, the Counterforce) opposes KP's planar-optimizer model
- **Selûne** — Moon goddess who intervened in 1479 DR by sending shards to Toril, enabling the purification of the Shard of Night
- **Tiamat** — Dragon goddess, co-resident of Avernus; attempted to manifest in the mortal realm via Severin and the Cult of the Dragon; true loophole architect was Zariel
- **Torm** — God of duty; temporary priesthood installed in the Hall of Justice by Neverember during Tyr's absence
- **Tyr** — God of justice; primary deity of the Hall of Justice; died in the late 1300s DR and was later resurrected

## Locations

### Beached Leviathan
- **Captain's Cabin** *(Beached Leviathan)* — Harrag's private quarters, above deck on the Beached Leviathan
- **The Deck** *(Beached Leviathan)* — Third level of the Beached Leviathan; private parlor rooms available for hire
- **The Hold** *(Beached Leviathan)* — First level of the Beached Leviathan; kitchen and main tavern
- **The Quarters** *(Beached Leviathan)* — Second level of the Beached Leviathan; 12 inn rooms for rent

### Bluelake District
- **Alchemist's Lab** *(Bluelake District)* — Hazel's lab located between The Tatters and the Lakeside area; cheerful exception in the grim district
- **House of a Thousand Faces** *(Bluelake District)* — Tavern built in a former noble boutique; mirrors and mannequins throughout; basement serves as the Harper chapter's secret meeting place
- **Nasher Warehouse** *(Bluelake District)* — Main Nasher headquarters in Bluelake; disguised as a stone-and-wood warehouse; connects via tunnel to The Flickering Ember
- **Neverdeath** *(Bluelake District)* — Graveyard district where undead began rising after the Spellplague; residents eventually stopped burying dead and burned them instead
- **The Beached Leviathan** *(Bluelake District)* — 3-tiered tavern built around the wreckage of the pirate ship Leviathan; frequented by criminals, smugglers, and sailors
- **The Chasm District** *(Bluelake District)* — Former giant canyon in the southeastern quarter of Neverwinter; sealed with powerful magic by Neverember; now open land for settlers
- **The Flickering Ember** *(Bluelake District)* — General goods shop run by Horst as a front for Nasher weapon-laundering; hidden passage connects to the Nasher Warehouse
- **The Sewers** *(Bluelake District)* — Unmapped sewer system beneath Bluelake; home to the Dead Rats wererat gang and their hidden stolen-goods vault
- **The Tatters** *(Bluelake District)* — Residential area controlled by Nasher rebels; serves as their primary living quarters
- **Vellgard Manor** *(Bluelake District)* — Headquarters of Mordai Vell's Ashmadai cell; large 3-story stone building with gates and inner wall

### Castle Never
- **Atrium** *(Castle Never)* — Interior chamber boarded to keep out sunlight; currently infested with territorial Myconids
- **Dolphin Bridge** *(Castle Never)* — One of three bridges radiating from Castle Never's circular rock over the Neverwinter River
- **Hall of Mirrors** *(Castle Never)* — Long hall where nobility once practiced gait; magically recreates its own destruction to attack intruders
- **Neverneath** *(Castle Never)* — Magically protected catacombs beneath Castle Never; gained malevolent sentience after the Spellplague and reshapes corridors at will
- **Sleeping Dragon Bridge** *(Castle Never)* — One of three bridges radiating from Castle Never's circular rock over the Neverwinter River
- **The Holes** *(Castle Never)* — First restored section of Castle Never — the dungeons; houses high-crime convicts with daily executions
- **Vault of the Nine** *(Castle Never)* — Tomb of the Neverwinter Nine in the depths of Castle Never; rumored resting place of the Crown of Neverwinter
- **Winged Wyvern Bridge** *(Castle Never)* — One of three bridges radiating from Castle Never's circular rock over the Neverwinter River

### Faerun
- **Lylorn** *(Faerun)* — City whose merchants recently set up a trade route through Luskan, rivaling the Tarmalune cartel
- **Sword Coast North** *(Faerun)* — Regional context for Neverwinter's harbor, one of few on this stretch of coast

### Faerun (distant)
- **Tarmalune** *(Faerun (distant))* — Distant city whose trade cartel operates the Tarmalune Trade House in Neverwinter

### Netherese ruins / North
- **Xinlenal** *(Netherese ruins / North)* — Netherese location powered by shadow energy teleported from the Shard of Night

### Neverwinter
- **Blacklake** *(Neverwinter)* — Lake filled with ash from the cataclysm; dredged and renamed Bluelake by Neverember; Aboleth Sovereignty maintains presence beneath it via water column
- **Blacklake District** *(Neverwinter)* — District where New Neverwinter places the Beached Leviathan (Guide to Neverwinter places it in the Docks)
- **Blue Lake** *(Neverwinter)* — Body of water beneath which the Aboleth Sovereignty is active
- **Bluelake** *(Neverwinter)* — Neverember's renaming of Blacklake after dredging began; 'eyes' forming in the lake sludge suggest Aboleth Sovereignty active long-range observation
- **Bluelake District** *(Neverwinter)* — Formerly Blacklake District; former noble estate quarter, now dominated by shadow factions and nightly criminal activity
- **Castle Never** *(Neverwinter)* — Ancient castle begun by Halueth Never; gained malevolent sentience and corridor-reshaping ability after the Spellplague; ruined by the cataclysm
- **Chasm** *(Neverwinter)* — Massive rift opened in the southeastern quarter by the cataclysm, plunging into a Spellplague-infected section of the Underdark; magically sealed in 1485–1486 DR
- **Eigersstor** *(Neverwinter)* — Original name of the settlement founded in 87 DR around Castle Never; later translated to Chondathan as Neverwinter
- **Hall of Justice** *(Neverwinter)* — Major building in the Protector's Enclave; antechamber holds a bronze relief of Stroud's chess gambit; Order of the Gauntlet chapter holds Milo Goodbarrel's volumes and the First Faction painting
- **Harbor authority** *(Neverwinter)* — Commission sub-body controlling the port; connected to House Margaster's relationship with the Commission
- **Neverwinter Docks** *(Neverwinter)* — Active commercial port district, 24-hour operation, earthmotes still visible overhead
- **Neverwinter River** *(Neverwinter)* — River flowing through Neverwinter; never freezes due to heat from fire elementals beneath Mount Hotenow
- **Protector's Enclave** *(Neverwinter)* — District of Neverwinter; home of the market district, the Hall of Justice, and the carved stone plaque commemorating the giant-mortal alliance
- **River District** *(Neverwinter)* — District that declined, causing older merchants to relocate to the Seven Suns Coster Market
- **River's District** *(Neverwinter)* — Broader district in New Neverwinter that encompasses both the Docks and Tower District
- **School of Wizardry** *(Neverwinter)* — Founded in 457 DR by the Covenant (Aganazzar, Ilyykur, Presper, Grimwald); destroyed by Red Wizards in 1081 DR
- **Shard of the Moon** *(Neverwinter)* — Purified form of the Shard of Night after Selûnite intervention; alabaster white with a moonlight stairway that descends when three shards are placed within or below it
- **Tower District** *(Neverwinter)* — Formerly wizard-tower-rich merchant district; seized by Many-Arrows orcs post-cataclysm, now considered cursed
- **Upland Rise** *(Neverwinter)* — Area logged on Neverember's orders to supply timber for rebuilding the Docks

## Planes / Cosmology

- **Abyss** — Planar source of the demon lords who entered the Underdark when Gromph Baenre's spell weakened the planar bonds
- **Avernus** — First layer of the Nine Hells; where Tiamat and Zariel co-reside; where Iymrith fled after surviving the War of the Giants
- **Far Realm** — Plane to which the Cloak Tower and all wizards inside it were sent during the Spellplague in 1385 DR
- **Material Plane** — Prime material realm where the Shard of the Moon's history unfolds
- **Plane of Fire** — Elemental plane; source of the small fire elemental cat sold at the Grand Emporium, found near Mount Hotenow
- **Shadowfell** — Plane to which the Shadovar sent a tower from Moonshadow Hall, eventually becoming the Shard of Night over Neverwinter
- **Underdark** — The Chasm broke through into a Spellplague-laden section of the Underdark beneath Neverwinter

## Items / Artifacts / Ships

- **Boots of Flying** — Magic boots worn by Whistler; allow a kenku to hover, preserved in every iconographic reference to him
- **Crown of Alagondar** — Disputed succession artifact that triggered conflict at the Beached Leviathan in 1479 DR
- **Crown of Neverwinter** — Legendary relic rumored to be in the Vault of the Nine; shields the wearer from heat and cold and is the deciding factor in legitimate succession — a false heir is burned by frost and fire
- **Death Knight-Dreadnaught** — Named undead warship rumored to be anchored below the Pirates' Skyhold earthmote; tied to Myrkul's presence in the North
- **Dragon Slayer Sword** — Brewbarry's signature weapon [Campaign Canon]
- **Faerzress** — Naturally occurring magical substance of the Underdark; Gromph Baenre's spell attempted to give the Drow control of it but instead weakened planar bonds to the Abyss

## Events / Concepts / Eras

- **Cataclysm of 1451 DR** — Eruption of Mount Hotenow sent a pyroclastic flow through Neverwinter, killing nearly all Castle Never's inhabitants including the Alagondar lineage
- **Commission Era and Hinterland Expansion** — ~1492–1495 DR; post-war period when the Commission pivoted from reconstruction to territorial expansion and commercial enclosure of the hinterlands
- **Conquest of Illusk** — Lord Halueth Never's conquest circa -10 DR that preceded the founding of Neverwinter and the construction of Castle Never
- **Crown Affair** — 1479 DR; Seldra Tylmarande used a replica Crown of Neverwinter in a male disguise to falsely claim the throne; plot failed
- **Displacement Manifold** — Referenced in adventure hooks as something Madame Rosene may have intelligence on; apparent political or magical apparatus in Neverwinter
- **Displacement Manifold Incident** — ~1493 DR [Campaign Canon]; Commission refused Aletra Sotorra's treaty ultimatum, Manifold went dark, Bimble Nackle was disappeared, Lionshield Coster's distribution network collapsed, House Margaster filled the gap
- **Hotenow eruption** — Volcanic eruption in the mid-1400s DR that snapped the original rope tethers anchoring the Moonstone Mask earthmote, causing it to drift into the Sea of Swords for months
- **Many-Arrows occupation** — Orc seizure of the Tower District post-cataclysm; ended approximately two years before the campaign's present day
- **Mount Hotenow eruption** — Volcanic cataclysm of 1451 DR that filled Bluelake with ash, tore open the Chasm, and killed most of Neverwinter's noble families
- **Neverember's Rebuild** — Reconstruction effort that made the Docks fully operational again by 1479 DR, logging the Upland Rise for timber
- **Neverwinter Succession Conflict** — 1479 DR conflict over the Crown of Alagondar during which the Beached Leviathan was attacked and saved by adventurers and General Sabine
- **New Neverwinter** — Commercial and physical reconstruction movement begun by Neverember in 1467 DR following his arrival with Mintarn mercenaries
- **Orcgates Affair** — 955 DR; Red Wizards aided orc hordes, beginning centuries-long feud between Thay and Neverwinter
- **Out of the Abyss** — ~1491–1492 DR; Gromph Baenre's spell unleashed demon lords into the Underdark; collapsed Menzoberranzan's influence; Valphine fled to the surface
- **Shard Purified** — 1479 DR; Selûne intervened and Lady Jasmine with Selûnite priestesses cleansed the Shard of Night into the Shard of the Moon
- **Spellplague** — 1385 DR catastrophe affecting all of Toril; in Neverwinter it caused earthmotes, vanished the Cloak Tower, raised undead in Neverdeath, allowed the Shard of Night to be positioned, and infected the Underdark beneath the city
- **Spellplague of 1385 DR** — Wild magic event that altered the ward protecting the Neverneath, giving it malevolent sentience
- **Stroud's Gambit** — Decisive chess game played by Stroud against gold dragon Protanther; Protanther's concession committed the metallic dragons to the alliance against Tiamat
- **the cataclysm** — 1451 DR; adventurers at Gauntlgrym awakened Maegera, causing Mount Hotenow to erupt and destroying most of Neverwinter; opened the Chasm
- **the Era of the Three Wars** — Campaign Canon era label for the combined War of the Dragons, War of the Giants, and Out of the Abyss (~1489–1492 DR)
- **The first fall of Neverwinter** — Earlier destruction of Neverwinter referenced by the Driftwood Tavern's decor and Madame Rosene's memory
- **The Spellplague** — Magical catastrophe that altered Neverdeath's landscape and caused corpses to animate; also left a Spellplague-laden section in the Underdark below the Chasm
- **Tyr's death** — Death of the god Tyr in the late 1300s DR; caused the Hall of Justice to cease function for an extended period
- **Tyr's resurrection** — Return of Tyr after his death; prompted rededication of the Hall of Justice and improved Neverember's public standing
- **Uthgardtian barbarian uprising** — Part of the War of the Giants; raided Triboar, obliterated Calling Horn, pillaged outskirts of Silverymoon and Mirabar; suppressed by giant-mortal alliance
- **Wailing Death** — Plague striking in 1372 DR that killed most of Neverwinter's residents within ten days of onset, resisting magical healing
- **War of the Dragons** — ~1489–1491 DR; Tyranny of Dragons analog; Tiamat's attempted manifestation via Severin and the Cult of the Dragon; ended with Severin's defeat; true architect was Zariel
- **War of the Giants** — ~1491–1492 DR; Storm King's Thunder analog; culminated in Hekaton aligning with the Lord's Alliance; official record omits Iymrith's escape to Avernus [Campaign Canon]
