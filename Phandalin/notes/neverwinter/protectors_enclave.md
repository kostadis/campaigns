# Protector's Enclave — Reference

*Hall of Justice history, House of Knowledge, and Tyr/Torm lore from Guide to Neverwinter; political framing, named government figures, commercial infrastructure, Driftwood Tavern description, Grand Emporium, Ironclad Defense, and Order of the Gauntlet quests from New Neverwinter.*

---

## Overview

The Protector's Enclave is the westernmost district of Neverwinter — its location protected it from the Mount Hotenow eruption, which devastated the city's eastern quarters. It is the commercial and governmental heart of the city: most of Neverwinter's shops, marketplaces, temples, and civic institutions are here.

**Political atmosphere:** The Enclave is Neverember's showcase district — heavily policed, heavily taxed, and carefully managed. Neverember was formerly called "**Lord Pretender**" by the public; General Sabine's Mintarn Mercenaries openly punished anyone who used the name. That era has passed. Neverember stabilized a city on the verge of ruin, and the general public is now on his side. Opposition still exists but operates behind closed doors.

**Governing structure:**
- **General Sabine** — commands the Mintarn Mercenaries; operational control of the district
- **Soman Galt** (dwarf) — mayor; handles administrative duties

> **GM-only:** Soman Galt is an aboleth puppet. The lake in Bluelake is his eyes. He has been aware of Hazel's research. Any formal inquiry about the lake routed through the mayor's office is compromised.

**Heraldry:** Neverember's **lion sign** appears throughout the Enclave. His Waterdeep coat of arms (a scepter of water topped with a flame ember) is deliberately **never displayed** here — a calculated choice to maintain local trust by not reminding citizens he is a Waterdeep outsider governing their city.

**Commercial infrastructure:** Houses almost all of Neverwinter's shops and marketplaces. Key commercial features:
- **Seven Suns Coster Market** — the city's main market hub; where older merchants relocated after the River District's decline
- **Manycoins Bank** — modest stone building with an arched entrance
- **Auction house** and **guild registry** also present
- **Moonstone Mask** — upscale tavern accessible via teleporter from the Enclave; popular tourist destination (covered separately in the Floating Earthmotes reference)

## Locations

### Hall of Justice

The primary temple to **Tyr** in Neverwinter. Located on a cliff overlooking the Sea of Swords, on the north bank of the Neverwinter River near its mouth. Built from stone, iron, and wood — grand stone walls, towering pillars, and a high domed roof. Enormous interior: large enough to house giants and dragons comfortably.

**History:**
- Originally functioned as temple, city court for civil justice (excluding nobles, non-citizens, and murder cases), and military training ground for Tyr's devoted
- Ceased function after **Tyr's death in the late 1300s DR**; residents refused to repurpose it for other deities
- Neverember installed a temporary **Torm** priesthood (reasoning theological similarity), then used the Hall as his personal base of operations and residence — both acts met with public disapproval
- After **Tyr's resurrection**, the Hall was rededicated; Neverember moved to a private villa; citizens viewed the restoration as a genuine act of good faith and it meaningfully improved his standing
- Now used for legal proceedings and high crimes; frequented by Tyr and Torm devotees

**The Hall of Justice Vaults:**
Beneath the Hall lies a series of vaults, crypts, and storage chambers dating back hundreds of years. Contains the remains of Neverwinter's honored nobles and heroes, as well as stored treasures. Widely believed to be haunted — popular myth holds that the honored dead will rise and slay anyone who disturbs their rest.

> **DM note:** When players ask about the vaults, most citizens confirm the haunting rumors flatly, without elaboration. Nobody goes down there.

### House of Knowledge

The primary temple to **Oghma** and Neverwinter's great library. Architecturally one of the most beautiful buildings in the city — abundant windows, an impressive arched roof. Its vast holdings contain centuries of records, rare tomes, artifacts, and currently also modern government documentation (Neverwinter lacks a formal seat of government).

**The cataclysm and Atlavast:**
Mount Hotenow's eruption nearly destroyed the House entirely. A dedicated young Loremaster named **Atlavast** saved much of the collection by sealing himself inside the inner sanctum, trapping himself with the library. He survived alone through the cataclysm — and the isolation cost him. He emerged reclusive and a little mad, obsessively recataloguing and reorganizing the collection, fiercely protective of anything in it.

During the chaos, the outer sections of the House were occupied by **Ashmadai cultists**. Since the chasm was sealed and order restored, the faithful of Oghma have returned and reclaimed the building.

**Current state:**
**Grand Scribe Spivey Liethennson** — stern, iron-fisted, short-tempered — oversees reconstruction. The exterior is largely restored; interior work continues. The House is again functional for research and records access.

> **DM hook:** Accessing specific or sensitive lore from the House requires navigating either Liethennson's short fuse or Atlavast's paranoid over-protectiveness. Either may demand the party prove themselves before granting access. This is not a library you walk into and browse freely.

### Driftwood Tavern

> **Location discrepancy:** Guide to Neverwinter places the Driftwood Tavern in the northern Docks District. New Neverwinter places it in the Protector's Enclave. Both sources agree on what it is and who runs it. This reference uses the Enclave placement, as New Neverwinter provides significantly more operational detail here.

A three-story building decorated with relics of Neverwinter before its destruction — old statues in the corners, ornate doors salvaged from wreckage serving as tables. Almost more of a museum than a tavern. In operation since before the Spellplague (pre-1385 DR). After the 1451 cataclysm, it sheltered refugees and served as a makeshift city hall. Drinks, meals, and rooms are expensive; clientele skews wealthy and nostalgic.

*Read-aloud on entry:* "In front of you stands the Driftwood Tavern, a three-story building decorated with curiosities and relics of Neverwinter before its first fall. Upon entering you notice old statues decorating its corners, ornate doors pulled from the wreckage that could also be used as fine tables."

**Madame Rosene:** Female human, mid-70s. Long white hair to her chest. Golden necklace shaped like a vase set with green jewels. Wears finer dresses with a hip sash where she keeps her money pouch. Kinder to older patrons — those who might remember Neverwinter before the fall. Every order comes with a history lesson about the city. She is the leader of the **Greycloaks** — the conservative, non-violent faction of the Sons of Alagondar — and the Driftwood's basement is the Greycloaks' hidden meeting place.

**What Rosene knows:** She was inside the Sons of Alagondar during Neverwinter's most politically volatile years. She knows the city's modern political history better than almost anyone alive and delivers it obliquely to people who buy her drinks and listen.

**Patron gossip — three theories for why Neverwinter doesn't freeze:**
1. The city's founder Lord Never defended against an orc attack; "Never's Winter" shortened to Neverwinter over time
2. The city's prestigious gardeners could keep gardens in bloom even through hard winters
3. Fire elementals beneath Mount Hotenow heat the Neverwinter River — the river and harbor never freeze even in winter

### Grand Emporium

A modest one-floor brick-and-wood building. Inside: a large orange carpet leads to a wooden counter backed by cages of varying sizes. Run by **Brimine** — red-skinned female tiefling, early 30s, kind-hearted, talks to animals in a baby voice.

**Stock — three tiers:**
- Normal pets: dogs, cats, owls, snakes, reptiles
- Rare/large pets: wolves, falcons, oxen, cockatrices
- Magical pets: baby gryphons, displacer beasts

**Brimine's featured item:** A small fire elemental from the Plane of Fire, shaped like a black cat, leaving black ashen tracks wherever it goes. Can be commanded to trigger a tiny fiery explosion — but is temperamental and difficult to control, as cats are.

*Her read-aloud presentation:* "But you're just the cutest, aren't you?"

**The Hotenow thread:** DC 10 Persuasion to learn she found the elemental near Mount Hotenow during an expedition. She'd go back for more, but the volcano is now under the dominion of **Gommoth** — a fire giant who leads a sect of fire-worshipping creatures and has proclaimed himself King of Hotenow.

> **Hook:** Gommoth is a named power operating out of Mount Hotenow — the volcano that destroyed Neverwinter. He has not been confronted. His sect controls whatever remains up there.

### The Ironclad Defense

A plain stone recruitment building staffed by mayoral employees and instructors. Its function: building Neverwinter's new city watch from scratch.

The Mintarn Mercenaries under General Sabine were hated for their violence against anyone who expressed dissent with Neverember. They faded in both popularity and numbers. In the gap, the city's four districts formed their own local militias. Neverember now wants the transition formalized — he is recruiting experienced adventurers to train and guide the new recruits, who are mostly inexperienced but willing members of the public.

> **Hook:** This building is the bridge between Neverember's mercenary past and whatever the city watch becomes. Adventurers who train the watch gain influence over the institution — and Soman Galt's administration will notice.

### Order of the Gauntlet Outpost

An unassuming building in the Enclave. A faction presence for the Order, offering intelligence, assistance, and work for parties with good intentions.

**The Order's ethos:** Primarily paladins and clerics of Tyr, Helm, Torm, and Hoar. When laws, agreements, or codes are broken, the Order acts without waiting for permission. Goal: smite evil, enforce justice, enact retribution. Despite the martial framing, the Order values friendship and camaraderie intensely — members treat each other as family.

**Named contact:** **Baern "Reckoning" Broodfist** — dwarven marksman, devoted to Helm.

**Ready-to-run quest hooks:**

| Quest | Type | Summary | Reward | Contact |
|-------|------|---------|--------|---------|
| **Agriculture** | Guard duty | Protect farms and windmills outside the city walls for 4 days; bandits attack nightly | 50 gp | Baern |
| **Masonry** | Escort | Escort masons repairing a breach in Neverdeath's wall while holding off undead; may include a Thay necromancer depending on party level | 100 gp | Yevelda (half-orc paladin of Tyr) |
| **Elimination** | Hunt | Track and eliminate Thay lieutenant "**Jawbone**," last spotted near the eastern Enclave; she summons undead when confronted; higher-trust mission | 300 gp + lieutenant's gear | Baern |

> **DM note:** These quests are city-orienting hooks for when the party doesn't bite on main plot threads. The Masonry quest directly connects to the Neverdeath situation flagged in the Bluelake reference.

## Adventure Hooks

- **The Vault Disturbance** — Someone has been entering the Hall of Justice Vaults. The honored dead are stirring. It may be Thay, it may be the Nashers looking for Neverwinter Nine proof, or it may be something older. The Order of the Gauntlet wants it investigated quietly.
- **Atlavast's Secret** — The Loremaster sealed himself in the inner sanctum for years. He saved the books. He also had years alone to read things no one else has read. Something he found in the collection changed him — and he won't say what. The party needs information he is guarding.
- **Gommoth's Reach** — The fire giant king of Hotenow has been sending scouts into the city. The elemental cat at the Grand Emporium is not the first thing to wander down from the mountain. Someone needs to establish whether Gommoth is an existential threat to Neverwinter or a contained curiosity.
- **Training the Watch** — The Ironclad Defense needs instructors. Adventurers who take this on gain influence over who the city watch becomes — and access to what the recruits see and report from all four districts.
- **Jawbone's Backers** — The Order's elimination quest targets a Thay lieutenant near the Enclave. If she's here, she's not just hiding — she's watching something or someone. Completing the quest cleanly earns Order trust; investigating before acting reveals what she was surveilling.
- **Madame Rosene's Price** — The Greycloaks have information. Rosene will share it, but she is measuring every party that walks into her tavern against the people she used to be, and the cause she used to lead. What she wants isn't coin. It's proof that the city is worth protecting again.
