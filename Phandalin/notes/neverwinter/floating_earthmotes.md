# Floating Earthmotes — Reference

*Physical history, Moonstone Mask founding lore, Pirates' Skyhold, and Fisher's Float from Guide to Neverwinter; Moonstone Mask operational texture and staff detail from New Neverwinter. Pirates' Skyhold and Fisher's Float are Guide-only — New Neverwinter does not cover them.*

---

## Overview

The Spellplague of 1385 DR caused chunks of Neverwinter — and other areas across Toril — to rise up and float away. Residents managed to tether a few of these earthmotes with ropes before they drifted into the Sword Coast. Three earthmotes remain associated with Neverwinter:

| Earthmote | Location | Current use |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Moonstone Mask** | Above the Protector's Enclave docks | Luxury inn, festhall, intelligence hub |
| **Pirates' Skyhold** | Northwest, off the Sword Coast | Abandoned; rumored treasure and mystery |
| **Fisher's Float** | Southwest of Neverwinter | Fishermen's guild — insular, rumors of sea-pact |

The Hotenow eruption later snapped the original rope tethers on the Moonstone Mask earthmote; it drifted out to sea for months before being retrieved and secured with chains. New Neverwinter's historical overview notes: *"The docks district became earthmotes, including the land on which the tavern Moonstone Mask was built on. To this day it's a floating mote of earth, bound by a shaky bridge and chains."*

---

## The Moonstone Mask

Neverwinter's premier luxury inn, built on the earthmote above the Protector's Enclave docks. Both sources agree on the basic physical layout and proprietor; they differ on access, staff composition, and the founding history.

### Physical Layout (both sources agree)

Five-storey building:
- **Floor 1:** Dining room — large hearth, lanterns along the grand staircase, comfortable lounge atmosphere
- **Floors 2–4:** Soundproofed rentable guest rooms (ranging from single-bed studios to large upper-floor suites), meals included, alcohol extra
- **Floor 5:** Festhall with music and dancing (soundproofing means guests below don't hear it)
- **Roof:** Landing platform for winged steeds and flying ships

Rumored: hidden wall panels accessible via magical words that allow secretive movement within the inn. Rumored basement with assorted magical items.

### Access

> **Discrepancy:** Guide to Neverwinter describes the Mask as tethered to the Protector's Enclave docks by chains, implying it can be reached on foot or by bridge. New Neverwinter describes it as floating *high in the sky above the city*, accessible only via flight or via a **teleportation circle located below in the Protector's Enclave**. The teleportation circle version is more operationally interesting and consistent with the earthmote having drifted significantly post-Hotenow. **Use the teleportation circle access** — it explains why the Mask is simultaneously the most prestigious address in Neverwinter and effectively invitation-only.

### Proprietor: Liset Cheldar

A half-elf woman. Friendly, openly flirtatious. Both sources agree on her name and manner. They differ on her legitimacy:

- **Guide:** Liset is the descendant and rightful heir of Ophala Cheldarstorn, the Mask's founder. Her claim was accepted by Neverember, who funded the restoration. The inn served Neverember and his officers throughout the 1470s–1480s DR — the Mask's renewal was partly a political bargain.
- **New Neverwinter:** *"She runs the Moonstone Mask with a wink and a smile — she loves flirting, some say that's how she owns the joint even though her claim to it was unclear."* This framing leaves her ownership ambiguous and her charm as possibly functional rather than merely decorative.

> **GM note:** These two framings are not contradictory — Liset's claim may have been legally unclear but politically accepted because Neverember wanted the inn operational. How much of her hold over the Mask is genealogy vs. performance is an open question.

### Staff

Both sources describe staff in sheer black gowns with glowing moonstone-trimmed masks and magical amulets. The amulets provide immunity to mind-reading and mind-control, and allow silent mental communication with the proprietor — a security feature for encountering unruly guests.

> **Discrepancy — gender composition:**
> - Guide: Staff no longer exclusively women; all customer-facing employees are "exceptionally attractive."
> - New Neverwinter: Staff is composed **only of females**; however, the chefs are all male (the kitchen is male-only by New Neverwinter's account).
> **Use New Neverwinter's split:** female-only front-of-house; male kitchen staff. The gender distinction feels intentional and is the more specific claim.

- **Guide:** The masks provide Darkvision/Infravision, allowing staff to navigate dim or darkened areas of the inn.
- Staff never reveal their true identities.

### Founding History (Guide only — Ophala Cheldarstorn)

The Moonstone Mask was built by **Ophala Cheldarstorn**, a mage and member of the **Many-Starred Cloak** (the wizard order whose tower is in the Tower District). Ophala created the magical masks and amulets the staff still wear. Before the earthmote rose, the inn was rumored to have:
- Secret entrances for guests arriving or departing unnoticed
- Basement tunnels connecting to dwarven-held sections of the Underdark

After the Spellplague raised the earthmote, the Hotenow eruption (mid-1400s DR) snapped the rope tethers. The Mask drifted over the Sea of Swords for months before being recovered and tethered with chains. It then attracted too few guests, went insolvent, and shuttered for decades — until Neverember's restoration funded by Liset's deal.

> **Campaign note — Many-Starred Cloak connection:** The Mask's founder was a Many-Starred Cloak wizard. The Many-Starred Cloaks also created Halavar's Universal Pantograph. The masks and amulets still in use every day at the Moonstone Mask were made by the same order whose tower is now a dungeon across the city. If the party enters the Mask and notices the magical properties of the staff's equipment, a Detect Magic or Arcana check can flag the distinctive Ophalan enchantment style — which connects to the Cloak Tower thread.

### DM Hooks (Guide)

- **Knowledge Base:** Liset and the staff know city rumors and non-confidential information. They will never betray a client's confidence, but they're the best passive intelligence source in Neverwinter.
- **Good Company:** Neverember himself is not an unusual sight at the Mask. It's the correct venue for notable run-ins and for meetings too sensitive for the Hall of Justice.
- **Baubles in the Basement:** The storage rooms in the Mask's basement reportedly contain all manner of magical items — along with the rumored Underdark tunnels.

## Pirates' Skyhold *(Guide to Neverwinter only)*

A floating earthmote northwest of Neverwinter, just off the Sword Coast.

**History:** After the Spellplague, a band of pirates with a skyship claimed the earthmote as their base and built modest wooden fortifications on it. At some point, a **mysterious tragedy** struck — most or all of the pirates were killed or disappeared. Their skyship remains docked on the earthmote, unmanned.

**Current state:** Abandoned. No one knows what happened. Rumors in Neverwinter cover the full range:
- A dragon attacked and claimed the hoard
- Netherese are using it
- It's haunted by the pirates' ghosts

**Treasure:** The pirates stored their plunder in the Skyhold. Given their sudden end, it's assumed the hoard is still there.

**Access:** Requires aerial or magical transportation. This is not a casual excursion.

### DM Hooks (Guide)

The source explicitly flags this location as **intentionally left open** from the 4e Neverwinter Campaign Setting — no canonical answer to what happened:

- **Dragon's Hoard:** A younger dragon attacked, killed everyone, and kept the loot.
- **Zombie Pirates:** Myrkul has been active in the region (Dragon of Icespire Peak canon). The Skyhold is now a fortress of undead with the **Death Knight-Dreadnaught** anchored below the earthmote.
- **I'll Take One of Each:** Myrkul sent a Dracolich or Ghost Dragon, which lords over an army of undead pirates sitting on the hoard.

> **Campaign note — Myrkul thread:** The Death Knight-Dreadnaught is a direct connection to this campaign's Dragon of Icespire Peak heritage. If Myrkul's presence in the North is an ongoing theme, the Skyhold is an available escalation point — reachable only once the party has aerial transport or magical options.

## Fisher's Float *(Guide to Neverwinter only)*

A floating earthmote southwest of Neverwinter.

**Current use:** Home and business headquarters of a guild of fishermen. Austere wooden shanties — not a wealthy community. They supply a significant portion of Neverwinter's seafood.

**Reputation:** The residents keep almost entirely to themselves. This insularity generates rumors. The most persistent: the fishermen are in some form of allegiance with **evil sea gods and/or sea monsters**.

> **Campaign note — Aboleth thread:** The Aboleth Sovereignty is already active beneath Neverwinter's Blue Lake. Fisher's Float's rumored sea-pact, combined with an insular community that supplies a large portion of the city's food supply, is a quiet escalation vector. Nothing in the source suggests this is canon — but it's a natural thread if the Aboleth influence is expanding its reach above ground.

## New Neverwinter Coverage Summary

New Neverwinter covers **only the Moonstone Mask** among the three earthmotes. Pirates' Skyhold and Fisher's Float do not appear. Its Moonstone Mask detail is primarily operational and visual — layout, staff, access — without the founding history or Ophala backstory.

**What New Neverwinter adds that Guide lacks:**
- Explicit teleportation circle access
- "Rare wares" are for sale (not just lodging and companionship)
- Kitchen staff are explicitly male (front-of-house female)
- Liset's ownership framed as charming ambiguity rather than settled inheritance

**What Guide adds that New Neverwinter lacks:**
- Ophala Cheldarstorn founding history and Many-Starred Cloak connection
- Mask and amulet mechanics (Darkvision, mental communication)
- Underdark tunnel rumor in the basement
- Pirates' Skyhold and Fisher's Float entirely

## Adventure Hooks

- **The Mask's Basement** — Rumored magical items in the storage rooms, plus possible Underdark tunnel access. Getting in unobserved requires bypassing staff who have Darkvision and who can communicate silently with Liset at any moment.
- **Liset's Ledger** — What has Liset heard in two decades of running the most discreet inn in Neverwinter? She won't sell information about current clients. Former clients are another matter, particularly if the party offers something she values more than professional discretion.
- **The Skyhold's End** — Something killed a fortified band of pirates with a skyship. Whatever it was is still there. The hoard may be worth the risk; the skyship almost certainly is.
- **Fisher's Float Pact** — Persistent rumors that the fishermen are allied with something beneath the sea. If the Aboleth Sovereignty thread is active, this is the surface-level entry point.
- **Ophala's Amulets** — The magical amulets worn by every Moonstone Mask staff member were created by a Many-Starred Cloak wizard. A cloak from the Tower District that knows about Ophala might want them back — or want to study them. The staff would notice immediately.
