# Adabra's Endgame: Tiamat's Instrument
*Extension of adabra_arc.md — what happens after the adjudication*

---

## Tiamat's Calculation

Tiamat is not recruiting Adabra because she loves nature or because she believes in naturalism. She recruits her because she is watching two threats:

**Zariel** is playing games in Avernus — specifically, she is holding Iymrith as a trophy, torturing her in a laboratory designed to break her. This is not containment. This is Zariel trying to make something she doesn't fully understand do something useful. Zariel with a leashed Doom of the Desert is a destabilized Avernus. If Iymrith escapes — which Tiamat suspects is inevitable, given the gold anchor — whatever plan Zariel thought she had goes wrong in unpredictable ways.

**Iymrith** is running her own long game. The artifact program, the research, the history rewrite. Iymrith's endgame involves rewriting the conditions that led to the War of the Dragons. Which means rewriting a past where Tiamat lost. Tiamat does not know the details. She knows enough to know she doesn't want Iymrith to succeed.

What Tiamat needs is disruption. Not a champion. Not an army. A force that will throw itself at whoever is winning, motivated by something that has nothing to do with her own interests. A weapon that aims itself.

Adabra is perfect. She is powerful, ideologically driven, and already pointed at the right targets — not because Tiamat directed her, but because the Interventionists Adabra hates are the same party trying to stop both Iymrith and Zariel. If Adabra disrupts the party's ability to act, she slows both threats simultaneously. And she will never stop, because her motivation is genuine.

Tiamat would kill Adabra at the first opportunity that was convenient. Adabra does not know this.

## The Recruitment (Levels 7–10)

The recruitment happens in the aftermath of the adjudication. Both outcomes lead here — the path just differs.

**Branch A (Adabra wins):** She has organizational power but it is immediately constraining. The local chapter has gone naturalist. Now she has to deliver results within a structure that moves slowly, argues constantly, and has Aldric Stonepath quietly questioning what the plan actually is. She has the apparatus and it is already frustrating her. She is looking for a source of real power — something the Enclave cannot give her and cannot take away. Tiamat finds her in this gap.

**Branch B (Vukradin wins):** She watched a bard make her evidence disappear. She was not wrong. The evidence was real. And it didn't matter. The system is captured — words beat facts, charm beats truth, and the Interventionists have won another round by talking rather than by being right. The Enclave cannot be the vehicle. She needs something that cannot be smoothed away. Tiamat finds her in the wreckage.

**The approach:** Tiamat does not appear directly. Her agents find Adabra in a moment of isolation — after the adjudication, alone, when the result has settled in. The message is simple: *a primal force of nature was attacked by the Interventionists. Her children were turned against her. Her domain was invaded. The people who did this are still operating. You know this. We know this. There may be a way to act on what you know.*

Adabra does not need to be deceived. She has already built the mental architecture to receive this message. She has been saying exactly this for years. What Tiamat offers is not a new idea — it is resources, connection, and the sense that her conclusion will finally have consequences.

She does not ask what Tiamat would do if she won. She already knows what happens when Interventionists win. She has been living in the aftermath.

## What Adabra Becomes (Levels 10–13)

She is not a cultist. She does not wear Tiamat's symbol or preach her name. She remains a druid of the Emerald Enclave — or its remnant, depending on Branch A or B — pursuing naturalist philosophy with increasing ferocity.

What changes is her capacity and her targets.

**Draconic gifts:** Tiamat's alliance gives Adabra access to draconic power she did not have before. This is not transformation — she does not become a dragon worshipper. It manifests as druidic power that has been... sharpened. Corrupted slightly at the edges. Her wild shapes begin to carry a draconic quality. Her spells hit harder. She can summon things she should not be able to summon. Soma, if she is paying attention, will notice that Meril's Staff does not respond warmly to Adabra's presence the way it once did.

**Her targets:** Adabra is aimed at the party, but not only at the party. She is aimed at anything that looks like organized Interventionist consolidation. The cult activities in Leilon — both Talos and Myrkul — are nominally enemies of intervention, but Adabra will not ally with them. They are using the natural world as a battlefield, which is exactly what Interventionists do. She is pointed at everyone who tries to impose order on chaos.

This makes her, functionally, a disruptive force in every major conflict of levels 7-13. She is not aligned with any faction. She is aligned with the conclusion that no faction should win.

**Tiamat's actual use of her:** Every time Adabra disrupts the party's ability to gather resources, allies, or intelligence — she slows the artifact retrieval that Iymrith needs and the party's ability to act against Zariel's machinations. Tiamat is not micromanaging this. She doesn't need to. Adabra does it herself, from her own convictions.

## The Crack (Levels 11–13)

The redemption crack has always been: Adabra witnesses Tiamat destroy something she loves. The natural world she has been fighting to protect, consumed by the force she decided was a victim.

The endgame design sharpens this. What Tiamat will eventually destroy — when it is convenient — is the Neverwinter Wood. Or a part of it that Adabra knows. The forest she has been living in, protecting, arguing about for decades. The oaks near the Circle of Thunder. The grove where Lyra's chapter was stationed.

Tiamat does not do this to punish Adabra. She does it because it is useful to her for some other reason — clearing ground for a dragon, destabilizing a region, removing an obstacle. Adabra is not consulted. She simply watches something she loves burn.

This is the moment. Not an argument the party makes. Not Soma saying "I told you so." Just: the thing she chose over everyone who warned her, burning the thing she said she was protecting.

What she does with that knowledge is still her choice.

---

## The Resolution Outcomes

**Redemption:** Adabra has to act against Tiamat's interests — at personal cost, without guarantee of survival. She knows what she has done. The people she drove out, the communities she left unprotected, the Lyra situation. The Ledger does not clear. If the party pursues this path, they have to create the conditions for the crack moment and then offer her the choice without soft-pedaling the cost. She does not come back as an ally. She comes back as someone who has stopped being a weapon.

**Death:** She chose this. All the way to the end. The party kills her and it is a loss — not a triumph. She was never stupid. She was never evil in her own mind. She saw real harm and drew the wrong conclusion and followed it faithfully. The table should feel the weight of what a genuine true believer looks like when she loses.

**Tiamat discards her:** Before the party even reaches her, Tiamat decides Adabra is no longer useful and destroys her — or has her destroyed. The party finds out after. This is the worst outcome narratively because it removes Adabra's agency entirely. Reserve this only if the story has genuinely run out of room for her.

---

## Connection to the Larger Arc

Adabra's arc resolves within levels 1-13. But its consequences extend.

If she is redeemed: she has spent months as Tiamat's instrument disrupting the artifact situation. She knows things about Tiamat's awareness of Iymrith and Zariel that the party does not. A redeemed Adabra is an intelligence asset for the epic tier.

If she dies: Tiamat has lost a tool and knows it. She will look for another. The party has closed one vector of disruption. Tiamat will open another.

Either way: **Tiamat is watching the same board the party is playing on.** She does not want Iymrith to win. She does not want Zariel to consolidate power. She does not want the party to succeed cleanly either — a party capable of breaking the Gold Anchor and killing Iymrith permanently is a party capable of acting against her. Adabra is not the last move Tiamat makes. She is the first one the party can see.
