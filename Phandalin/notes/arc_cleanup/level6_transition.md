### Rot-Tusk Ogre Clan — Adjudication Consequence

The ogre clan's trajectory branches directly from the Emerald Enclave adjudication outcome.

**Branch A — Adabra wins:**
The local Enclave chapter goes naturalist. The Rot-Tusk Ogres are part of the natural displacement cascade — the naturalist position is that the ogres pushing into the lowlands is the world finding its own equilibrium. The Enclave will not act. Nobody in a position of local authority will act. The ogres become a worsening problem through levels 7-9: settlements pressured, roads less safe, the lowland communities absorbing the displacement the forest can't contain. By the time the party is deep in Leilon, the Rot-Tusk situation back in the Phandalin region has deteriorated.

*Note:* This is also the consequence Aldric Stonepath hasn't let himself think about. The orc displacement cascade in the Peaks is the same logic, larger scale. If the party encounters Aldric again during levels 7-9 and the Rot-Tusk situation has gotten bad, that's the real-world version of his question — and the naturalist answer ("let it happen") is now visible in what it produces.

**Branch B — Vukradin wins / moderates hold:**
The moderate faction has standing. The Enclave wants to demonstrate that interventionism is responsible, not reckless — which means they need tangible results in the Neverwinter Wood. They ask the party to resolve the Rot-Tusk situation: stop the ogres from coming down into the lowlands. Not necessarily destroy them — find a solution. Relocate them, negotiate a boundary, address whatever is pushing them out of the upper forest.

This becomes a level 6 quest in its own right. It also gives the party a chance to ask: *what is pushing the Rot-Tusk Clan down?* The answer connects to the orc displacement cascade and the giant activity in the Peaks. Aldric Stonepath's problem and the Rot-Tusk problem are the same problem at different scales.

## 3. Transition to Leilon

The soft transition from Dragon of Icespire Peak (levels 1-6) to Storm Lord's Wrath (levels 7-9).

**The trigger:** Miral's warning is already in play — *"true storm gathers in the depths below Leilon."* This is the narrative hook pulling the party toward Leilon. It does not require a hard stop; the party can follow it when the forest business is done.

**What the party knows going in:**
- A storm threatens Leilon (Miral's warning)
- The Talosian cult has been active in the region (they've seen the anchorites)
- The Kraken Society is a known enemy (severed head on pike; beacon they're carrying)
- Leilon is a settlement in reconstruction — political opportunity for a party with Phandalin influence

**What the transition should feel like:** The party finishes their Phandalin-region business — lodge, manse, circle, Neverwinter returns — and then follows the storm warning south. The threat escalates. The Talosian presence in the forest was the edge of something larger.

**Phandalin threads that persist into levels 7+:**
- Mountain's Toe Gold Mine (~100 gp/week passive; wererats still present as guardians)
- Privy Council position (Vukradin; Harbin Wester remains hostile)
- Adabra arc (the adjudication is the first beat; her radicalization continues through level 10)
- Aletra / KP / Planar Distortion (ongoing, low score)
- Grundar Quartzvein / House Sotorra threat (Brundar's Echo at 0; not yet active)

**What can be left open:** Not everything needs to resolve before Leilon. The mine runs itself. The political threads follow the party. The only things that truly need resolution before departure are the forest threats (they'll follow the party otherwise) and the statue return (time-pressured).
