# Emerald Enclave — Campaign Reference
*Compiled from: Factions of Phandalin, Sword Coast Adventurer's Guide, Heroes of Faerûn, campaign state*

---

## What the Emerald Enclave Is

"A widespread group of wilderness survivalists who preserve the natural order while rooting out unnatural threats."

- **Decentralized, hardy, and reclusive.** No binding central authority at the local level. Outcomes of internal disputes are social and reputational, not formal rulings.
- Founded over a thousand years ago in the **Vilhon Reach**. Headquarters on the island of **Ilighôn**, where an **Elder Circle** of druids leads the faction. Arcane magic doesn't function on Ilighôn; primal and divine sources are unaffected.
- Protected by the **Seven Sentinels of Silvanus** — colossal elementals that deter uninvited visitors.
- Members include druids, rangers, barbarians, and others with a strong tie to the land. Good and neutral alignments most common.
- Members wear **emerald green clothing** and the emblem of a **stag's head**. Insignia at Springwarden rank is a copper cloak clasp in the shape of a leaf, accented with green enamel.

---

## Goals and Beliefs

**Goals:**
- Restore and preserve the natural order
- Destroy all that is unnatural
- Keep the elemental forces of the world in check
- Keep civilization and the wilderness from destroying each other

**Beliefs:**
- The natural order must be protected and preserved
- Forces that upset the natural order must be destroyed
- Civilization and the wilderness must learn to coexist peacefully

## Guild Contact (Phandalin Region)

**Reidoth** — druid, found in Thundertree (east of Neverwinter).
- Lean and haggard with a white beard. Bit of a hermit but genuinely cares about people.
- Knows Neverwinter Wood intimately.
- Old friends with **Qelline Alderleaf**, halfling farmer in Phandalin.
- Communicates via Sending spell on a semi-regular basis; will visit Phandalin to meet potential apprentices.
- Deep love of nature and natural order. Does not hate orcs — recognizes them as part of nature disrupted, not inherently wrong.
- **Key:** He is the legitimate authority for this region. Adabra convenes the adjudication without him deliberately. His absence is a political choice on her part.

## The Internal Schism: Naturalists vs. Interventionists

This is a campaign-specific layer on top of the canonical structure.

**Origin:** The Emerald Enclave has always had philosophical tension between those who believe civilization must be protected (interventionists) and those who believe the natural order supersedes mortal concerns (naturalists). The tension exploded after **Malfire convinced Delaan Winterhound to intervene** at the final battle against Tiamat. To radical naturalists, this proved that even moderate members would compromise when pushed.

**Interventionists:**
- Believe protecting people *is* protecting the natural order — civilization and wilderness must coexist
- Lyra's faction; active in combating planar corruption, sealing rifts
- Currently hold more institutional power but are in disgrace after the Whispering Grove ritual failure
- Key members in this region: Lyra (leader, in disgrace), Thomas, Marian

**Naturalists (Radical Wing):**
- Believe intervention itself is the corruption — every mortal action that disturbs the natural order causes more harm than the threats it addresses
- Read the aftermath of the War of Dragons and War of Giants as proof: the "victories" caused more suffering than the threats would have
- Key figure: Adabra Gwynn

**The Enclave as a whole:** Most members are moderate. They care about results. Soma's Autumnreaver renown at level 5 is evidence of results. The moderates aren't ideologues — they follow effectiveness. This is exactly what Adabra cannot accept.

- Rank: Autumnreaver (her politics block advancement she believes she deserves)

- Rank: Autumnreaver (leader of local interventionist chapter)

## How the Enclave's Structure Shapes the Adjudication

- **No binding rulings.** Adabra cannot expel Soma or formally strip her renown — she can only turn local opinion. Outcomes are social.
- **Reidoth's absence matters.** A formal review would involve the guild contact. By going local, Adabra avoids his moderating influence but also limits her authority.
- **Persuasion is the game.** The moderates decide the outcome. This is Vukradin's domain — and Adabra knows it.
- **Physical evidence is the equalizer.** The dead Treant cannot be argued away. Adabra's strategy is to lead with the body before Vukradin can set the frame.
- **The institution reflects most members' values.** Most Enclave members think like Soma. Adabra is the outlier. She will never accept this.
