# Running the Adjudication
*How to manage this session at the table*

---

## Two Physical Objects and One Rule

That's all you're managing. Everything else is in your notes before the session starts.

---

## Object 1: The Track Card

Before the session, write this on an index card:

```
ADABRA  ←  [ -3 | -2 | -1 | 0 | +1 | +2 | +3 ]  →  PARTY
```

Put a coin or a die at 0. When something happens, slide the coin. You never calculate anything. You just glance at where the coin is.

---

## Object 2: The Room Card

One card, five lines:

```
FALCON    — her name / the ring       (no roll)
ALDRIC    — what's the Peaks plan?    (DC13 read, DC15 push)
BRYNN     — Soma leads, not Vukradin  (disadv if Corbin unowned)
OSSIAN    — answer the question       (no roll, coin moves from answer)
ADABRA    — she doesn't argue         (evidence only, one comeback line)
```

When you need to know what an NPC needs, look at the card.

---

## The One Rule in Your Head

**Player owns something → coin slides right. Player performs → coin stays.**

Owning = paying a cost. "The Treant is dead and we chose that." "I handed Corbin to someone I didn't understand and he's dead." Coin moves.

Reframing = not paying. "It was complicated." "We had no choice." "You don't understand the full situation." Coin stays, Adabra gets a response.

---

## The Practical Problem You'll Actually Have

The hardest moment is right after Xanthopoulos drops the Corbin news. The table goes quiet. You'll feel the urge to fill it — move to the next NPC, give Adabra a line, keep things moving.

Don't. Let the quiet happen. That silence is the most important 10 seconds of the session. What Vukradin does *into* that silence is the moment you're running the whole session to create. It cannot happen if you fill it first.

Your job in that moment is to say nothing and watch the players.
