# The Emerald Enclave Adjudication — What Actually Happened
*Session record and consequence ledger. Session date: ~2026-04-21.*
*Prep docs: `adabra_adjudication.md`, `dm_prep_adjudication.md`, `running_the_adjudication.md`, `session_adjudication.md`, `falcon_true_thing.md`, `aldric_loaded_question.md`.*

## Beat-by-Beat (as run)

1. **Adabra's opening.** Delivered her evidence-first statement as designed. Treant dead, party concealed it, Vukradin's account to Lyra's rescued members was false, planar creatures now present in the Wood.

2. **Vukradin tries to reframe.** Argues Adabra is mistaken about what happened. Adabra does not argue with him — she turns to **Lyra and Thomas**, who were there.

3. **Lyra and Thomas confirm — and do not apologize.** They do not deny killing the Treant. They do not treat it as wrong. They believe they did the right thing. This is the first thing that breaks Vukradin's frame: his reframe depended on the interventionists themselves being uncertain, or ashamed, or needing rescue. They are not.

4. **Vukradin is confused.** He cannot route around confessed interventionism. He shifts tactics and **approaches Falcon.**

5. **Falcon attacks Vukradin personally.** Goes straight for him — the persona, the charm, the bard act. (The Senne beat was not reached; Falcon was wielding the grief, not opening it.)

6. **Vukradin gets upset and leaves.** Walks out of the meeting. **Brewbarry asks if he should kill anyone.** Vukradin ignores him and is gone.

7. **Soma stands up.** With the bard out of the room, she takes over — and does not ask for reframing help. She fights for her cause directly.
   - **Owns the Treant.** Takes ownership for the decision. Does not soften it.
   - **Frames the Wood as disaster.** Explains what was actually happening in the forest — not a debate, a description.
   - **Names Axehome and the alien dwarves.** Brings evidence the Enclave has not catalogued and does not have a framework for. Specifics, not philosophy.

8. **Xanthopoulos arrives with the Corbin news.** Flat, no drama.
   - **Valphine:** does not care. *"He's just one more body."*
   - **Brewbarry:** indifferent.
   - **Soma:** cares. Says clearly that the party made a mistake with Corbin — *and* that they still had to do what they did. They had to go save Phandalin from the Carver. They did. She owns the mistake and owns the choice in the same breath.

9. **Aldric starts muttering.** Cracking visibly.

10. **Soma turns the Peaks question directly on him.** Tells him: the naturalists would tell you to do nothing about the orcs.

11. **Aldric stammers.** Cannot answer.

12. **Adabra cannot help herself.** Says out loud: *"Nature will heal itself."*

13. **Aldric gives up.** In public. In front of the moderates. The bloc visibly fractures.

14. **Adabra is furious.**

15. **Lyra's trial.** Lyra is, again, unapologetic. Explains what happened, stands behind it.

16. **Soma again explains why the naturalist position is wrong.**

17. **Adabra sees the room slipping.** Storms off.

18. **The council decides.** Adabra was wrong. **The purpose of the Enclave is to protect the forest, not to let extraplanar abominations destroy things.** Institutional ruling against Adabra's frame.

## Post-Meeting — Valphine and Falcon

Valphine approaches Falcon. **Does not attack, does not argue.** In contrast to Vukradin's approach:

- **Talks about the mine.** Takes responsibility for the party's inaction on the orcs. Apologizes for not having moved on them sooner. Promises to do something about it.
- **Tells her life story.** Menzoberranzan. The demon incursion. Leaving. Seeing the sun.
- **Does not pitch Lathander as a sales move.** Lets the sun-story carry its own weight.

**Falcon realizes he has been giving up.** Adabra's framework gave him permission to stop trying; Valphine named what stopping-trying actually is. He does not convert. He is **open but not convinced** — specifically open to *not giving up.*

## Final Score / Outcome

**Score equivalent: +3 (Adabra loses decisively).**

The council ruled against her frame. Lyra survived with standing (unapologetic through the whole process, which — against a bloc that has collapsed — reads as conviction, not recklessness). Soma did not just survive institutional review; she made institutional review land against the person who called it.

This is the **best possible outcome at the table** and structurally the *worst* outcome for Adabra — because in the designed arc, "Adabra loses badly" is the trigger for Tiamat's clean recruitment. She lost not to process-capture, not to a bard's reframe, but to being **contradicted by a better druid in front of the institution she was trying to move.**

## What Each Character Did

### Soma — Tier-3 interventionism defining beat
- Claimed interventionism as **identity, not circumstance**
- Owned the Treant publicly
- Owned Corbin **without** retreating from the choice that cost him
- Out-specified a naturalist on Peaks geography via Axehome
- Cracked Aldric with his own faction's position turned back on him
- Forced Adabra to say the quiet part out loud (*"nature will heal itself"*)
- Made Meril's staff a public symbol the institution now respects

**Arc implication:** Soma is not a late-life tortle finding her way. She has answered. The staff now has a person-shaped match for what Meril bet on. Everything downstream — the staff's awakening, the eventual encounter with Adabra's Tiamat-sharpened power — has a Soma who committed publicly.

### Valphine — first real priesthood act
- Did not lead with Lathander doctrine
- Did not use pain-theology (did not reach for *"gloriously painful light"*)
- Apologized for inaction, committed to action, told her own exile story
- Reached Falcon through the exact move Adabra's framework cannot generate: **apologizing for giving up**

**Arc implication:** Valphine discovered her priesthood by not performing it. Every prior conversion attempt (Adabra, Teega, shocking Thomas at the tavern) failed on the pain-theology pitch. This one worked because she paid cost, apologized, and didn't attach strings. Flag this for Gary — her next conversion attempt, if she reverts to the old pitch, should feel different to her.

### Vukradin — unpaid debt
- Did not own Corbin at the meeting — **someone else did, on his behalf**
- Walked rather than crack in public
- Brewbarry's *"should I kill someone"* offer was ignored, but it is on the record
- The bard worldview just met the one person who could not be sung to, and his response was to leave

**Arc implication:** The Silver Tongue worldview has its first public failure. Vukradin will be different when he returns. The Corbin cost is still owed — Sister Kaella is a loaded gun on a shelf that will return when the heat is highest. How Vukradin returns to the party and what he says first is a beat that needs to happen early next session.

### Brewbarry — indifference registered
- Asked if he should kill anyone during Vukradin's exit
- Indifferent at Corbin's announcement

This is on the record with the moderates. Brewbarry is *not* Soma — his indifference to a dead man is noted, specifically by Brynn (who is a ranger who lost her village and has a specific sensitivity to casual death). Not a crisis, but a character marker.

### Adabra — her worst possible session
- Prepared for a duel with Vukradin; he refused to be the target. Confirmed her thesis, deprived her of the victory.
- Got contradicted by a better druid in public.
- **Said the quiet part out loud.** *"Nature will heal itself"* — the line she had managed to never have to say. Aldric gave up in real time because of it.
- Watched her bloc collapse during Lyra's trial.
- Stormed off.
- Council ruled against her frame institutionally.
- Does not yet know Valphine reached Falcon after. She will find out eventually.

### Lyra — unexpected ally of conviction
- Was designed to be gutted, authority suspended in practice. Played unapologetic instead.
- Paired with Thomas, also unapologetic — two voices saying the same thing.
- The moderates read a bloc that stands behind what it did, not people who need rescuing.

**Arc implication:** Lyra's institutional standing is not just preserved — it is elevated. She went into the adjudication "in disgrace." She came out of it having been publicly vindicated by the council. This is material for future Enclave relations; her standing in the interventionist wing is now load-bearing rather than fragile.

### Aldric — cracked at the meeting, not later
- The designed crack was level 7–9. He gave up in public at the adjudication itself.
- Adabra said *"nature will heal itself"* and he heard his faction's answer for the Peaks out loud.
- He is now **available** — not immediately, not as an ally, but as a man who no longer has a faction.

### Falcon — open, not convinced
- Used his grief as a weapon against Vukradin and drove him out.
- Watched the bloc he chose collapse while he was still standing in it.
- Valphine stayed when Vukradin walked. That framed the mine conversation.
- Apologized-for-inaction frame landed because it is what Adabra's framework cannot generate.
- Realizes he has been giving up — the lodge, the ring, the consortium, Senne. All of it.
- Open to not-giving-up. Not converted to Lathander. **Specifically** open to starting again.

### Xanthopoulos — delivered Corbin flat, held the room
- Did not need to play the Meril card beyond the arrival weight.
- The Corbin news landed as designed, but Soma owned it, which inverted what the moderates were supposed to take away.

### Brynn — was Vukradin's target, ended up Soma's
- Watched a bard walk and a druid stand. Weighted the druid.
- Watched a cleric stay with a grieving man after the bard left. Weighted that too.
- Will seek out Soma privately next session. Question, not pitch.

## Arc Consequences

### Tiamat recruitment — canonical trigger achieved
`adabra_endgame.md` flagged: *"Tiamat's agents find Adabra in a moment of isolation — after the adjudication, alone, when the result has settled in."* That moment is now real:

- Vukradin denied her the duel.
- Soma refuted her in public.
- She herself said *"nature will heal itself"* and watched Aldric break on it.
- The council ruled against her.
- Her grievance carrier is quietly being reached by a drow she cannot touch.
- The moderates respected the position she has spent thirty years calling impossible.

She is not defeated. She is **resolved in a direction she has not yet taken.** Tiamat's pitch lands clean because it is not a lie — it is affirmation of a conclusion she has already drawn. The only new element is access to power the Enclave cannot match.

### Soma's arc track
Update as Tier-3 interventionism defining beat. She has committed publicly. Meril's staff should begin responding — subtle awakening beat visible only to her, within a session. The staff now has a Soma-shaped person to match.

### Valphine's arc track
First successful priesthood act. Flag that the pain-theology pitch is not the thing that works. The apology-and-commitment frame is the one that reached Falcon. Her character growth direction is pointed here.

### Vukradin's arc track
Unpaid Corbin cost. Persona-first impulse cost him the room. Next session needs a return-to-party beat where Dave tells the table what Vukradin's walkout meant.

### Falcon's disposition change
- Dragonslayer persona: terminal, not yet public.
- Lodge: wants it back; will go with the party to reclaim it.
- Mine: now a Corbin-sized wound he has a chance to prevent.
- Adabra: no longer has grip on him.
- Ring: may come out of the drawer on its own.
