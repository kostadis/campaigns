# Aldric's Loaded Question
*Notecard — the opportunist's crack*

---

## Who Aldric Is

**Aldric Stonepath (Autumnreaver).** Fifteen years mapping the Icespire Peaks. He has walked every pass, catalogued every valley, watched the orc displacement cascade work through an ecosystem in real time.

The War of the Giants pushed the giants through their old territories. The giants pushed the orcs. The orcs flooded down into lower elevations — overhunting, clear-cutting denning grounds, driving out the things that lived there before. Not from malice. From displacement. Aldric documented all of it. He brought it to the Enclave with maps.

They argued ideology. They always argued ideology.

Then Adabra listened. Not just nodded — *listened*. She asked questions about the Peaks. She understood the cascade logic. She said the Enclave needed to stop wasting its energy on philosophical debates and start directing its resources at real problems. He believed her. He joined her bloc.

He has not asked her what she would specifically do about the orcs in the Peaks. He tells himself he hasn't needed to. He tells himself there's time to get into the specifics after she wins the organizational fight. He does not examine why he keeps not asking.

**The truth Aldric hasn't looked at:** Adabra's naturalist philosophy applied to the Peaks cascade produces one answer: the displacement is the downstream result of the War of the Giants, which followed from the War of the Dragons, which followed from the defeat of Tiamat — i.e., from an act of intervention. The correct response is to stop intervening further and let the ecosystem find its own equilibrium. Which means: the orcs stay where they are. The displaced species adapt or don't. Nature rights itself without help.

That is not what Aldric wants. Aldric wants someone to *do something*. He is, functionally, an interventionist who has allied with the anti-interventionist faction because she was the only person who made him feel heard.

---

## The Crack

**What Aldric is performing in this room:** Confidence. The mapper who knows exactly what he wants and has found the right vehicle to get it. He talks about organizational capacity. He talks about having the right people in place. He is good at this. He has been navigating the Enclave's internal politics for fifteen years.

**What is underneath the performance:** He is careful never to answer the specific question of what happens after Adabra wins. He talks around it. He pivots to process. He has been doing this for months, including with himself.

**The Insight read (DC 13):**
On a success: the player notices that Aldric pivots every time the conversation approaches specifics. He'll describe the problem in precise detail — he knows the Peaks like cartography — but when asked what the plan is, he becomes general. That pivot is not confidence. That's someone steering away from something.

**The question that cracks him:**
*"What does Adabra say should be done about the orcs in the Peaks?"*

Not hostile. Not rhetorical. Just: what is the plan?

This question works best coming from Ossian — an elderly Enclave member who has no agenda, who just wants to know if there's a plan before he lends his weight to it. Ossian doesn't ask it to embarrass Aldric. He asks it because he's old enough to know that winning a vote without a plan just means you own the failure.

It can also come from the party. If the party has passed the Insight check and read the pivot, they can ask it directly: *"You've described the problem in the Peaks very precisely. What does Adabra say the solution is?"*

**The Persuasion (DC 15):**
On a success: Aldric says out loud that he hasn't asked. Or he tries to answer for Adabra and can't get specific. The DC represents not the persuasion of Aldric — you can't persuade him of anything here — but the skill of keeping him in the gap long enough that the room sees it.

---

## What Happens When It Lands

Aldric doesn't answer the question. He starts to. He says something about the Enclave having resources and Adabra understanding the importance of the Peaks — and then he stops. Because he knows what the naturalist answer is. He's been in enough meetings.

One of two things happens:

**Option A — Aldric stalls:**
He says he'd need to speak with Adabra directly about specifics. Which is true. Which means he hasn't. The room notices the gap between "I joined her bloc" and "I haven't asked her the central question."

**Option B — Adabra answers for him:**
If Adabra is watching this happen and steps in to answer, she is forced to say the naturalist position out loud: the displacement is downstream of intervention, the answer is to stop the cascade by withdrawing from it, the ecosystem finds equilibrium without help. Which means: let it happen. Let the orcs stay where they are. Let whatever adapted go.

Aldric's face does something when she says it. He doesn't walk out. He doesn't flip. But the room sees that he heard something he didn't want to hear. And Ossian sees that look. Ossian has been Enclave long enough to know what it means when an ally's confidence gets quiet.

---

## What This Does to the Session

- **Doesn't flip Aldric.** He stays in Adabra's bloc. The alliance is too recent and his need is too strong for one uncomfortable moment to break it.
- **Creates a crack that persists.** In levels 7-9, if the party encounters Aldric in the Peaks — and they will, eventually — he is no longer fully committed. He has been watching for what Adabra's win actually produced for the Peaks. If the answer is nothing, he becomes available.
- **Costs Adabra something.** She had to say the quiet part out loud. The moderates heard it. Aldric heard it. She was prepared for Vukradin. She was not prepared to have her own ally used as the question.
- **Ossian's question shifts momentum regardless of the DC.** If Ossian asks it and Aldric stalls, that's a momentum shift on its own — no Persuasion needed. The DM decides: did the party create the opening for Ossian to ask? If yes, Ossian asks, and the gap opens.

---

## DM Logistics

- Insight DC 13: read the pivot — available to any player paying attention to Aldric's pattern
- Persuasion DC 15: keep him in the gap long enough for the room to see it (or force Adabra to answer)
- Ossian asking it is a freebie if the party has been building momentum and Ossian has been watching
- If Adabra answers instead of Aldric: she says the honest naturalist position. That is the correct answer for her. It is not what Aldric joined her to hear. Don't soften it.
- Aldric does not become an enemy. He becomes someone who is no longer all the way in.

---

## The Line to Have Ready (Ossian)

*"I've watched the Peaks problem for some time too. Before I cast with anyone, I'd like to know — what does the naturalist answer actually do with the orcs? Not in principle. In practice."*

He doesn't look at Adabra when he asks. He looks at Aldric.
