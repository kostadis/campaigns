# Session Design: The Emerald Enclave Adjudication
*Immediate next session — party arrives in Phandalin after defeating the Carver*

## Context

Adabra Gwynn has called a gathering of local Emerald Enclave members. The stated subject is Lyra and the Whispering Wood failure. The real subject is Soma.

**Why now:** Soma's renown has crossed 20 — Autumnreaver, the same rank as Adabra. Soma is burning through renown at twice the expected pace for her level, doing exactly the interventionist work Adabra despises, and the moderates love her for it. Adabra has the experience and knowledge of a Winterstalker but her politics keep blocking her advancement. She can't understand why. This gathering is her last real attempt to prove the institution wrong through legitimate channels.

**Key mechanical fact:** The Emerald Enclave is decentralized. No binding central authority. Reidoth (guild contact, Thundertree) is not present — this is a local matter, deliberately convened without him. Outcomes are social and reputational, not formal rulings.

**Adabra's evidence:**
1. The Treant at the Whispering Grove is dead — she went to the forest herself
2. The party concealed the Treant's death from her
3. Vukradin's account to Lyra's rescued members was false
4. Planar creatures are now present in the Wood (she interprets as: intervention causes corruption)

**What the planar creatures actually are:** KP's cosmic engineering has broken something in the local fabric of reality and reality is sending repair entities. The creatures are maintenance, not monsters. Adabra has the causation backwards. The party may not have enough information to make this case credibly at level 5-6.

---

## The Corbin Situation

While the party was at Icespire Hold dealing with the Carver, they left **Corbin** guarding **Sister Kaella** — the Carver's spy, a defector who had provided the party with directions to Icespire Hold.

**What the party didn't fully reckon with:**
- Sister Kaella is a **10th level rogue assassin**
- Corbin is at best a 2nd level angry man with a cruel streak and a grudge
- Vukradin authorized Corbin to use force on Kaella "up to one hit point"
- Valphine had been teaching Corbin her sacraments — controlled pain as spiritual practice
- Corbin had a visible hunger for power over the vulnerable, flagged by Vukradin himself but not acted upon

**What happened:** Sister Kaella endured what Corbin did to her, waited for her moment, and killed him. She is now gone — fled the region, carrying full knowledge of the party's operations, Xalvosh's true nature, and her own reasons for needing to be somewhere else. She is a loaded gun on a shelf. She can come back when the story calls for it.

**Why this matters for the session:** This is the second body in the party's wake. The dead Treant is one thing — a concealment, a lie. Corbin is another — a man in the party's care, killed because Vukradin handed a 10th level assassin to a 2nd level angry man and didn't understand what he was doing. The party meant no harm. They didn't see it. That is precisely the problem Adabra has been describing all along.

## The People in the Room

### Adabra Gwynn (Autumnreaver — presiding)
Calls the gathering. Presents evidence. Does not argue philosophy — she leads with physical facts because she knows Vukradin will come for the philosophy. Her strategy is to win on process before he can set the frame.

### Lyra (Autumnreaver — diminished)
Present but gutted. Her authority is already suspended in practice. Does she speak? Does she defend herself or fall on her sword? Player-facing question — let it happen at the table.

### Thomas (field agent — Summerstrider)
Loyal to the Enclave above personal relationships. Candid, measured. Not Adabra's ally, not the party's ally. Will speak plainly if asked. Wild card.

### Marian (field agent — Summerstrider)
Loyal to Lyra. Will defend her character. Less useful to the party than Thomas.

---

## The Moderates — Four Faces Adabra Doesn't Know

### Xanthopoulos / Xanth (loosely affiliated — no formal rank)
Independent centaur ranger/scout. Critical of both factions — has called them "idiots who are saying one thing, idiots who are saying the other." Adabra has not dealt with him before. No prior allegiance to either side.

**Why he matters:**
- Old friends with Meril — the druid who gave Soma her staff
- Recognized Meril's Staff on sight in a previous encounter; declared he would help Soma because "Meril must know her"
- When Adabra argues Soma is a reckless interventionist given too much power, Xanthopoulos can say: *I know whose staff that is. I know what it means that Meril gave it to her.*
- Arrives with the Corbin news — learned it through his forest intelligence network

**The news he brings:** Corbin is dead. Sister Kaella escaped. She has gone to the wind. He states this flatly, without drama. Everyone in the room can do the math.

**Position:** Wild card. Pragmatic, not ideological. The moderates watch him.

---

### Brynn Ashwalker (Summerstrider)
Half-elf, mid-20s, ranger. Joined the Enclave after the War of Dragons burned her village. Not ideological — she joined because she watched the world fail to protect her home and decided someone had to. Drawn to interventionism instinctively but hasn't committed to the label. Adabra's arguments about the costs of war resonate with her personally — she lived those costs — which makes her genuinely torn.

**Position:** Reachable from either side. The one person in the room who might actually change her mind during the gathering. Vukradin's best persuasion target — but she's harder than she looks, because she's been hurt by exactly the kind of well-meaning intervention Vukradin embodies.

---

### Ossian Greymoss (Autumnreaver)
Elderly human herbalist and part-time Enclave scout. Operates out of a small homestead near the Triboar Trail. Has been Enclave longer than most people in the room have been alive. Doesn't care about either faction's arguments — he cares about whether the Enclave is still doing its job. Quiet. Economical with words. When he speaks, people listen because he almost never does.

**Position:** The room's conscience. Not a vote either side can count. His one question, if he asks it, cuts through everything: *"Is the wood safer or not?"* That can go either way.

## Session Shape (90 minutes)

### Beat 1 — Arrival in Phandalin (~15 min)

- Party arrives from Icespire Hold, fresh from the Carver
- Someone fills them in: Adabra has called a gathering; stated subject is Lyra and the Whispering Wood; unstated subject is Soma
- Thomas finds the party privately first — candid, not warm. Tells them what's coming. Does not offer to help.
- Soma learns her renown standing is part of why this is happening now

### Beat 2 — The Gathering (~50 min)

**Adabra's opening:** Physical evidence first, before Vukradin can set the frame.
1. The Treant is dead. She went to the forest. Here is what she found.
2. The party concealed it.
3. Vukradin's account to Lyra's rescued members was false.
4. Planar creatures are now present in the Wood that were not there before.

Her ask is procedurally reasonable — formal review of Soma's renown, suspend Lyra's authority pending review. She is trying to win on process. That is the trap.

**Vukradin enters:** He tries to reframe. He partially succeeds with Brynn and the moderates. But he cannot reframe the dead Treant. Adabra anticipated him:

*"He will tell you what you want to hear. He always does. I am telling you what I found."*

**Xanthopoulos arrives — mid-scene:**
He walks in and the room shifts. He is known. He is not from either faction. He greets Soma — references the staff. Then he delivers the Corbin news. Flatly. Without drama.

The room goes quiet. Someone in the party's care is dead. Vukradin authorized it. He meant no harm. He didn't see what he was doing. That is precisely what Adabra has been saying about interventionists all along.

**The double blow lands:**
- The dead Treant: the party concealed something
- Corbin's death: the party's naivety got someone killed

**Ossian's question** — if he speaks, it's here. One sentence. *"Is the wood safer or not?"* Let the table sit with it.

**Vukradin's crack:** Long sentences disappear. The music stops. He is looking at something his worldview cannot reframe. He can explain intent. He cannot explain Corbin.

**Soma's choice:** Does she own the Treant or fight it? Does she speak to Corbin? These are the player's calls. Both paths feel consequential.

**Brynn** watches Soma more than she watches anyone else. She joined the Enclave for the same reason Soma did. She is trying to figure out if Soma is what she hoped existed.

### Beat 3 — The Cliffhanger (~15 min)

- The moderates call for a recess — they want to think
- Adabra reads this correctly: time bought, not a win
- She says nothing more. She leaves.
- Session ends suspended. Adabra's expression unreadable.
- The Corbin news hangs in the air — Sister Kaella is out there, armed with everything she knows

## The Two Branches (Resolve in a Future Session)

**Branch A — Adabra wins:**
Local chapter goes naturalist. Lyra formally suspended. Soma under institutional review. Adabra has organizational power but is constrained by it. Tiamat finds her later — when she has more to offer and more to lose.

**Branch B — Vukradin talks his way through:**
The moderates don't fully turn. The party avoids the worst. Adabra watches a bard make her evidence disappear. This is the moment Tiamat finds her — not in strength but in the confirmation that the system is captured and words beat facts. The only power that can't be smoothed away is real power.

**The players can do anything.** Both branches are designed rails. The table may find something better.

## Open Questions

1. Does Lyra speak? Does she defend herself or fall on her sword?
2. Does Xanthopoulos openly defend Soma or let the facts land without comment?
3. If the party invokes Reidoth — sends for him, uses him as authority — what is Adabra's prepared response?
4. Can the party use the planar creatures as deflection without fully understanding KP? Does it work with the moderates?
5. Sister Kaella is gone for now. When she comes back, it's on her terms.

## Adabra's Bloc — The Radicals

Adabra is not alone. She arrives with two members who stand with her. This matters — a lone ideologue is easy to dismiss; a faction with faces is harder to ignore. But her bloc is more fragile than it looks. Only Adabra has thought the naturalist position all the way through. Her followers are there for different reasons, which means they're useful to her but not truly loyal to her cause.

---

### Falcon the Hunter (Autumnreaver — grievance carrier)
The party knows him. He felt like a potential ally once. He is standing on the wrong side of the room.

Falcon lost the love of his life in the cascade of consequences from the War of Dragons. Not on a battlefield — in the way wars destroy things that aren't in the fight. The Interventionists cheered the victory. Adabra didn't argue with him about who was right. She just agreed. That's all a grievance carrier needs.

The lodge, the misrepresentation, the performance of the self-sufficient ranger — all of it is a man who built a persona after losing the thing that mattered most. Adabra found the crack in the persona and put her framework inside it.

**He doesn't believe in naturalist philosophy.** He believes the right people are responsible for what happened to her. Adabra gave him that. He'll follow her until the cost gets too high — or until something cracks the framework she gave him.

**In this scene:** He's harder than the party has seen him before. The bravado is still there but the warmth is gone. He won't engage philosophically — he'll go personal, fast. He's the one most likely to say something that makes the room uncomfortable.

**His wobble condition:** If someone acknowledges her death directly — not as collateral damage or a systemic cost, but as her, specifically — he may not be able to stay in the room. He has built everything on not doing that.

---

### Aldric Stonepath (Autumnreaver — opportunist)
New face. Human, early 40s, weathered. Former Enclave scout who spent fifteen years mapping and managing the Icespire Peaks ecosystem before the orc displacement. Knows that territory better than anyone in the room. Speaks in specifics — never philosophy. When he talks about the Peaks he uses names: this pass, that valley, the elk herd that hasn't been seen since the third orc migration wave.

**The real problem he's watching:** The Icespire Peaks displacement is a cascade. Orcs move down, push into farmland and forest, farmers fight back, forest caught in the middle, mountain predators have no prey, stone giants don't care. The whole system unraveling. He went to Lyra's faction — too busy with the Whispering Wood ritual. He went to the naturalist faction — "the orcs are part of the natural order, let it resolve itself." He watched the resolution: burned farmsteads, dead rangers, forest edges pushed back.

**Why he's with Adabra:** She sat still and listened to him describe what was actually happening. She gave him a hearing when nobody else would. He interpreted that as shared purpose. It wasn't — she was recruiting. He hasn't figured that out yet.

**He's not wrong about the problem.** He's making a bad bet on the solution. He thinks that once Adabra has organizational power he can direct it toward the Peaks. He hasn't asked her directly what her answer to the orc displacement is. He's afraid of it.

**Position:** Adabra's bloc, but conditionally. His loyalty is to the mountains, not to her ideology. If someone in the room — the party, Ossian, Xanthopoulos — acknowledges the Peaks problem seriously and proposes something concrete, he wobbles. He's the crack in Adabra's bloc that a smart party can find.

**His wobble condition:** He knows Adabra's real answer to the orc displacement is "let nature resolve it." He's seen what that resolution looks like. If anyone forces him to confront that gap between what he needs and what she actually believes, he has nowhere to go.

---

## What Adabra's Bloc Tells Us About Her

She has a true believer (herself), a grievance carrier (Falcon), and an opportunist (Aldric). This is exactly how ideological movements work in the real world — the true believer provides the framework, the grievance carriers provide the emotional fuel, and the opportunists provide the organizational muscle. It looks like a coalition. It isn't. The moment Adabra goes somewhere Falcon's grief or Aldric's pragmatism can't follow, it fractures.

The party doesn't need to destroy the bloc. They need to find the cracks.
