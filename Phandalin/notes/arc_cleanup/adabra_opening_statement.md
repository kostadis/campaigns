# Adabra's Opening Statement
*Notecard — read aloud, ~2 minutes, no interruptions*

---

*She does not look at Soma when she speaks. She does not look at Vukradin. She looks at the room.*

---

"I will be brief. I am not here to argue philosophy with you. I am here to tell you what I found.

Three weeks ago, I went to the Whispering Wood myself. I walked to the Whispering Grove. The Treant is dead. Not wounded. Not sleeping. Dead. Roots collapsed. Crown black. The grove that Lyra's chapter was assigned to protect is gone.

I pressed Lyra's people on what had happened. Their account did not match what I found. I pressed further. They named the bard.

I then spoke with two others who were present at the grove that night. Their account also did not match. What they described is not what was reported to us. The party concealed what happened. They did not make a mistake and tell us. They made a choice.

I want you to understand what that choice cost. The last time I spoke with this party, I believed the ritual had failed. The Treant still lived. When I heard there was a planar creature collecting materials near the grove, I was — I will say this plainly — pleased. I called it a planar magpie. I said it was the world working as it should. I gave the bard five gold pieces for what he told me that day. I believed him.

Now I have walked to that grove myself. The Treant is dead. The creature is still there. What I called the world healing itself was the world responding to a wound I was not told had been made.

He did not only lie to Lyra's people. He lied to me.

These are the facts. A sacred grove is dead. A report was falsified. And I gave gold to the man who falsified it because he is very good at what he does.

I am not asking you to condemn anyone today. I am asking you to look at what I found before you listen to anyone tell you what it means."

*She steps back. She does not sit. She is done.*

---

## DM Notes

- She finishes before Vukradin can set the frame — the evidence is already on the table
- "He lied to me" lands harder than any accusation she could make about Lyra's people — she is not a credulous person, and the room knows it
- The five gold pieces detail is devastating: it shows the deception was precise, not sloppy. He didn't just mislead her — he left her feeling good about it
- "I am not asking you to condemn anyone today" is deliberate: it sounds moderate, but every moderate in the room is now processing the evidence on her terms
- The planar creatures are KP's repair entities — Adabra has the causation backwards, but the party can't prove this at level 5-6. Critically: she does NOT call them harmful or wrong. She calls them evidence of a wound she wasn't told about. Her earlier "grand design" framing is intact — what changed is the context she was given
- If Vukradin jumps in immediately to reframe, Adabra's pre-planted line lands: *"He will tell you what you want to hear. He always does. I am telling you what I found."*
- Falcon doesn't speak here. He doesn't need to. He's watching to see who blinks.
