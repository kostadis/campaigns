# Custom Arc: Adabra's Escalation
*Draft for review — working toward a cleaned-up campaign arc.md*

## The Core

Adabra Gwynn is not a villain who chose evil. She is someone who witnessed real catastrophe — the War of Dragons bankrupted societies, destroyed communities, burned cities — and drew a conclusion that is internally consistent but catastrophically wrong: **the Interventionists caused more harm than the forces they fought.**

Her evidence is real. The War of Dragons was not a clean heroic victory. And the War of the Giants followed directly from it — because Tiamat's defeat created a power vacuum the giant gods exploited. The body count from "winning" is enormous. Adabra can see every cost of intervention with crystalline clarity.

What she cannot see is what non-intervention would have produced. That is her blind spot. And Tiamat knows exactly how to tend it.

Her arc is a portrait of ideological radicalization: a real grievance, a coherent framework, and a series of steps each of which feels justified from inside the framework — until she is making cause with someone who would destroy everything she loves, because her hatred of the Interventionists has blinded her to what her ally actually is.

**The party can kill her or redeem her. Both are viable. Redemption is harder.**

---

## Soma's Thread

Soma carries Meril's staff without knowing what it is. Meril — an interventionist druid — found a 40-year-old tortle with a natural gift and almost no time left. He couldn't know what she would become. He taught her what he could, left her the staff, and trusted that someone who loves the world that much would figure out what it's worth protecting.

That is Meril's move: not a mission, not a mandate. A bet on a person.

Adabra never had that. She came to her conclusions alone, through real grief, and she is certain she is right in a way that Meril — with his open-handed gesture — is not. When Soma walks into Umbrage Hill carrying that staff, Adabra sees exactly what she is. Two druids. Same roots. Opposite answers.

**Meril:** uncertain, hopeful, trusts the person more than the ideology.
**Adabra:** certain, grieving, trusts the conclusion more than the person.

Soma sits between them — she chose interventionism unprompted, but she is still figuring out what that means.

## Progression Track: Fury of the Wild (0–10)

Each beat below notes the track movement. The track reflects how far Adabra has moved from grievance to action to alliance to confrontation. It is a DM tool, not something the party can see directly — but its effects are visible in how Adabra behaves when the party encounters her.

---

## Movement 1: The Grievance (Levels 1–6)

**Module hook:** Umbrage Hill Quest (*Dragon of Icespire Peak*). Party is sent to urge Adabra to return to Phandalin.

**What the party finds:** A druid living alone in a stone windmill. Self-sufficient, wary of towns, skeptical of the party's world. She'll help — she's not hostile — but she has opinions. Sharp ones.

**What Adabra sees:** Soma's staff. She recognizes it as an interventionist's tool — the ash, the craftsmanship, the faint residue of intent in the wood. She doesn't say this. She files it away.

**Her position at this stage:** Legitimate critic, not yet dangerous. She will articulate real costs of intervention — communities that suffered because of the wars, landscapes that were burned in the fighting, the natural world treated as a battlefield for mortal politics. She is not wrong about any of these things. She is drawing the wrong conclusion from correct observations.

**Fury Track: 0–1**

---

## Movement 2: The Radicalization (Levels 7–10)

This is where philosophical disagreement becomes active opposition.

Each interventionist action the party takes — each time they defend a community, each time they fight to protect something — Adabra reinterprets through her framework. The Kraken Society's mind-controlled victims are "proof that meddling with the natural order invites alien corruption." The cults fighting over Leilon are "what mortals always produce when they decide they know better than nature."

She is not manufacturing this. She genuinely believes it. Every event confirms what she already thinks because her framework has no mechanism for disconfirmation.

**The Tiamat Pact**

Tiamat's agents find Adabra — or she finds them. It does not matter which. What matters is that Tiamat presents herself as a wronged party: a primal force of nature that mortal Interventionists decided to make war on. They invaded her domain. They recruited her children against her. They broke the natural order in their arrogance.

Adabra does not need to be deceived. She has already built the mental architecture to receive exactly this message. Tiamat is not lying about having been attacked. The War of Dragons was real. The costs were real. Adabra hears this and thinks: *yes. This is what I have been saying.*

What she does not ask is what Tiamat would do if she won.

**Meril's Staff**

If the party encounters Adabra during this period, she may notice that the staff has begun to change — awakening in response to Soma's choices, her growth as a druid, her interventionist actions in the world. This is not a conversation Adabra will have easily. It is evidence she does not want.

**Fury Track: 2–6**

---

## Movement 3: The Confrontation (Levels 11–13)

Adabra is now fully committed. She is not a pawn — she knows what she is doing and she believes it is right. The party is not going to argue her out of this. She has answers for every argument. She has heard them all.

**The Crack**

Redemption, if it comes, does not come from debate. It comes from a moment Adabra witnesses herself — something Tiamat does that she cannot rationalize. Not an argument the party makes. A moment.

Tiamat does not protect nature. Tiamat burns it. Somewhere, at the right time, Adabra has to see this with her own eyes: the thing she has been fighting to protect, destroyed by the ally she chose. The natural world she loves, consumed by the force she decided was a victim.

This is the crack in the container. It does not automatically produce redemption — it produces a choice. She can rationalize it (find a reason Tiamat was justified), or she can feel what she has been refusing to feel for years: that she was wrong about the most important thing.

**The Party's Choice**

If the party pursues redemption, it must be earned not given:
- They have to create the conditions for the crack to happen — put Adabra in a position to witness the truth
- They have to resist the easier path (killing her ends the threat cleanly)
- They have to decide whether they can accept someone who did real harm back on their side

If redemption succeeds, Adabra lives with what she has done. The people she hurt, the Interventionists she drove out, the communities she left unprotected — that ledger does not clear. She knows it. The party knows it. That is what makes it real.

**If the party kills her:** It is a loss, not a triumph. She was never stupid. She was never evil in her own mind. She was someone who saw real harm and drew the wrong conclusion, and followed it all the way to the end. The table should feel that weight.

**Fury Track: 7–10**

---

## What Adabra's Arc Is NOT

- She is not trying to free Iymrith. She does not know about Iymrith's scheme.
- She is not serving as Iymrith's agent. Her Tiamat alliance is entirely her own.
- Her arc resolves within the local campaign (levels 1–13). It does not extend into the epic tier.

---

## Endgame: Tiamat's Instrument

See `adabra_endgame.md` for the full design. Summary:

After the adjudication (either branch), Tiamat recruits Adabra — not because she believes in naturalism, but because she needs a self-aiming weapon that disrupts both Iymrith's artifact scheme and Zariel's Avernus games simultaneously. Adabra becomes an agent of chaos and planar vengeance through levels 10–13, armed with draconic gifts that corrupt the edges of her druidic power.

Tiamat would kill Adabra at the first convenient opportunity. Adabra does not ask what Tiamat would do if she won.

The crack: Tiamat burns something Adabra loves — not to punish her, but because it is useful. The natural world Adabra chose over everyone who warned her, destroyed by the ally she chose it for.

Resolution: redemption (intelligence asset for the epic tier), death (a loss, not a triumph), or discarded by Tiamat (worst outcome — removes her agency).
