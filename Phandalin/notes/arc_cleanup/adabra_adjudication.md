# The Emerald Enclave Adjudication — Design Notes
*Immediate next session beat — Adabra triggers a formal Enclave adjudication over the Whispering Wood events*

## The Setup

The party has just finished dealing with the Carver. They are called to Phandalin where the Emerald Enclave is convening to adjudicate the Whispering Wood situation.

**What Adabra has discovered:**
- The Treant at the Whispering Grove is dead — the party concealed this from her
- Vukradin lied about what happened (misrepresented the party's role to Lyra and others)
- The planar creatures appearing in the Whispering Wood (which she interprets as proof that intervention causes corruption)

**What has changed since Adabra last encountered Soma:**
- Soma's interventionism score is over 20 — Tier 3
- Adabra expected a naive late-life tortle finding her way. What she found instead is a true believer.
- This infuriates her. Soma didn't stumble into interventionism. She chose it with full understanding.

**Adabra's goal at the adjudication:**
- Use Lyra's disgrace as a referendum on interventionism broadly
- Use the evidence (dead Treant, Vukradin's lie, planar creatures) to damage the party's credibility
- Get the Enclave to turn on Soma
- Lyra is the proximate victim — Soma is the real target

---

## The Planar Creatures — What They Actually Are

The creatures Adabra found in the Whispering Wood are not consequences of the party's intervention. They are **planar workers** — entities that exist because KP's cosmic engineering broke something in the fabric of local reality, and reality is attempting to repair itself. They are maintenance, not monsters.

Adabra has the causation backwards. She sees alien creatures appearing after the party got involved and her framework receives it as confirmation. The actual cause — KP's pre-existing engineering upstream — is invisible to her.

This creates a potential defense for the party: if they can explain what those creatures actually are, they undermine Adabra's main argument. But at this level they may not have enough information about KP to make that case credibly.

**Note on Valphine:** The player who created Valphine invented KP as a worldbuilding contribution. Valphine the character knows nothing about KP. Her connection is personal — her sister Aletra works for KP. The party's knowledge of KP's engineering is limited at this stage.

---

## Proposed Outcome: Adabra Wins the Battle, Not the War

**Lyra:** Takes serious additional damage. Already in disgrace. The evidence pushes moderate Enclave members away from defending her. Authority gutted, not expelled.

**The party:** Officially distrusted. Vukradin's lie is on the record. The concealed Treant is on the record. Not expelled or attacked — but marked.

**Soma:** Survives with standing barely intact. Her Tier 3 score is what saves her — the moderates can't dismiss results. But she is now isolated within the Enclave. No institutional protection going forward.

**Adabra:** Does NOT get the Enclave to formally adopt her naturalist position. Overcorrects by demanding they turn on Soma — one step too far for the moderates. The center shifts toward her but she doesn't win the war. Her ideology gets in the way of her full victory.

**Why this outcome is right:** Adabra wins on the evidence because the evidence is real. The party lied. The Treant is dead. The creatures are there. She should win this. But she overreaches and the moderates won't follow her all the way.

## Open Design Questions

1. Who are the Enclave moderates? Do they have names and faces, or are they background figures?
2. Does Lyra get a moment to speak? Does she defend herself or fall on her sword?
3. What does Soma do when Adabra reveals the dead Treant? Does she own it or fight it?
4. Is there a moment where Vukradin can use his silver tongue — and should it work or fail?
5. Can the party use the planar creatures as a deflection even without fully understanding KP? ("These aren't from our intervention — something else is causing this.")
6. What are the mechanical consequences of the official distrust? Enclave quests denied? NPCs less cooperative?
