# DM Prep: The Emerald Enclave Adjudication
*Everything decided about this session in one place*

---

## The Situation

The party returns to Phandalin after defeating the Carver at Icespire Hold. Adabra Gwynn has called a local Emerald Enclave gathering. The stated subject is Lyra and the Whispering Wood failure. The real subject is Soma — whose renown has crossed 20 (Autumnreaver) at level 5, burning through ranks at twice the expected pace.

Adabra's goal: use Lyra's disgrace as a referendum on interventionism, damage the party's credibility with the moderates, and get the Enclave to formally review Soma's standing. She is not trying to expel Soma — she is trying to win on process.

This is Adabra's last attempt to work within legitimate channels. If she loses here, she loses the last reason to stay inside the system. Tiamat finds her in the aftermath.

---

## The Room

### Adabra's Bloc
**Adabra Gwynn (Autumnreaver)** — True believer. Presides. Leads with physical evidence, not philosophy. She anticipated Vukradin. Her key line: *"He will tell you what you want to hear. He always does. I am telling you what I found."*

**Falcon the Hunter (Autumnreaver)** — Grievance carrier. The party knows him. He lost the love of his life in the War of Dragons cascade. Adabra gave his grief a target. He doesn't believe in naturalism — he believes in blame. Harder than the party has seen him. Will go personal fast. **Cannot be reached by argument. Can only be reached by someone naming her directly — her name, her death, what she loved. No roll. Either someone says the right thing or they don't.**

**Aldric Stonepath (Autumnreaver)** — Opportunist. New face. Fifteen years mapping the Icespire Peaks. Watching the orc displacement cascade destroy an ecosystem while the Enclave argues ideology. Joined Adabra because she listened. Thinks he can direct her power toward the Peaks once she wins. Hasn't asked her what her answer to the orc displacement actually is. He's afraid of the answer. **His crack: force him to confront that gap.**

### The Diminished
**Lyra (Autumnreaver)** — Present, gutted. Authority already suspended in practice. Does she speak? Does she defend herself or fall on her sword? Let it happen at the table.

**Thomas (Summerstrider)** — Loyal to the Enclave above all. Candid, measured. Not anyone's ally. Will speak plainly if asked. Wild card.

**Marian (Summerstrider)** — Loyal to Lyra. Will defend her character. Less useful to the party than Thomas.

### The Moderates
**Xanthopoulos / Xanth (no formal rank)** — Independent centaur. Old friends with Meril. Recognized Meril's Staff. Arrives mid-scene with the Corbin news — flat, no drama. When Adabra argues Soma is a reckless interventionist given too much power, he can say: *"I know whose staff that is. I know what it means that Meril gave it to her."* Wild card. The moderates watch him.

**Brynn Ashwalker (Summerstrider)** — Half-elf, mid-20s, ranger. Joined after the War of Dragons burned her village. Drawn to interventionism but hasn't committed. Adabra's costs-of-war argument resonates personally. Watching Soma more than anyone else — trying to figure out if Soma is what she hoped existed. Vukradin's best persuasion target. **Roll with disadvantage if he hasn't owned Corbin first.**

**Ossian Greymoss (Autumnreaver)** — Elderly herbalist/scout. Been Enclave longer than most people in the room have been alive. Almost never speaks. When he does, people listen. His one question if he asks it: *"Is the wood safer or not?"* No roll needed — the room's reaction to whoever answers shifts momentum.

## Adabra's Evidence (in order she presents it)
1. The Treant at the Whispering Grove is dead. She went to the forest herself.
2. The party concealed the Treant's death from her.
3. Vukradin's account to Lyra's rescued members was false.
4. Planar creatures are now present in the Wood that were not there before.

**What the planar creatures actually are:** KP's cosmic engineering has broken the local fabric of reality and reality is sending repair entities. Adabra has the causation backwards. The party may not be able to prove this at level 5-6 — they don't have enough KP knowledge yet.

---

## The Corbin Bomb

Xanthopoulos arrives mid-scene with this: Corbin is dead. Sister Kaella escaped. She has gone to the wind.

**What actually happened:** Sister Kaella is a 10th level rogue assassin. Corbin was a 2nd level angry man. Vukradin authorized him to use force on her "up to one hit point." Valphine had been teaching him her sacraments. Kaella endured it, waited for her moment, and killed him.

**Why it matters:** This is the second body in the party's wake. The dead Treant is a concealment. Corbin is naivety made fatal — Vukradin handed a 10th level assassin to a 2nd level man with a cruel streak and didn't understand what he was doing. He meant no harm. He didn't see it. That is precisely what Adabra has been saying about interventionists.

**Sister Kaella's status:** Gone for now. Loaded gun on a shelf. Returns when the story calls for it.

## The Momentum System

Hidden track: **-3 to +3**. Starts at 0. Negative = Adabra's direction. Positive = party's direction. Moderates decide at the end based on final momentum plus one last action from each side.

**Momentum shifts from player choices, not dice:**
- Player makes a choice that costs them something → **+1**
- Player tries to reframe without owning the evidence → **0**, Adabra gets a free response
- Player owns something they did wrong → **+1** regardless of how it lands narratively
- Player finds a crack in the bloc and pushes it → **roll DC 13**, success +1, failure -1

**Dice only at genuine pressure points:**
- Vukradin persuades Brynn: reachable, but **roll with disadvantage** if he hasn't owned Corbin first
- Pushing Aldric on the Peaks question: **Insight DC 13** to read the wobble, then **Persuasion DC 15** to crack him
- Falcon: **no roll**. Specific true thing said to his grief, or nothing
- Ossian's question: **no roll**. Momentum shifts from the answer itself

**Final tally:**
| Score | Outcome |
|-------|---------|
| +3 | Adabra loses badly. Moderates side with party. Lyra survives with standing. |
| +1 to +2 | Party wins the recess. Moderates call for time. Branch B path. |
| 0 | Genuine split. Reidoth gets called in. Outcome deferred. |
| -1 to -2 | Adabra wins the recess. Branch A path begins. |
| -3 | Adabra wins decisively. Soma's renown under formal review. Lyra suspended. |

---

## The Two Branches

**Branch A — Adabra wins:**
Local chapter goes naturalist. Lyra formally suspended. Soma under institutional review. Adabra has organizational power but is constrained by it. Tiamat finds her later — when she has more to offer and more to lose.

**Branch B — Vukradin talks his way through:**
Moderates don't fully turn. Party avoids the worst. Adabra watches a bard make her evidence disappear. Confirmation that the system is captured — words beat facts. This is the moment Tiamat finds her. The only power that can't be smoothed away is real power.

**The players can do anything.** Both branches are designed rails. The table may find something better.

## Session Shape (90 minutes)

**Beat 1 — Arrival (~15 min)**
- Party arrives from Icespire Hold
- Thomas finds them privately first — candid, not warm, tells them what's coming, offers nothing
- Soma learns her renown is why this is happening now

**Beat 2 — The Gathering (~50 min)**
- Adabra opens with evidence, not philosophy — delivers her statement before anyone can interrupt
- Vukradin tries to reframe; partially succeeds with moderates; cannot reframe the dead Treant
- Xanthopoulos arrives mid-scene; drops the Corbin news flat
- Vukradin's crack moment — long sentences disappear, music stops
- Soma's choice — own the Treant or fight it; both paths feel consequential
- Brynn watches Soma; Ossian may ask his question; Aldric's wobble available if the party finds it

**Beat 3 — The Cliffhanger (~15 min)**
- Moderates call for a recess
- Adabra reads it correctly: time bought, not a win
- She says nothing more. She leaves.
- Sister Kaella hangs in the air unresolved

## Three Things to Have Ready

1. **Adabra's opening statement** — two minutes, physical evidence only, no philosophy. Written out. Delivered before anyone can interrupt. *(Draft in separate notecard note)*

2. **Falcon's true thing** — decide in advance what cracks him. Her name. What she loved. What she was doing when she died. You know it when a player stumbles into it. No roll — just recognize it.

3. **Aldric's loaded question** — if the scene stalls or the party is losing momentum, have Ossian or Thomas ask Aldric directly: *"And what does Adabra say should happen to the orcs in the Peaks?"* Watch his face. Let the table figure out what it means.
