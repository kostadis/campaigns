# Recruitment Through Integrity

The Lord’s Alliance recruits agents not through ideology, coercion, or loyalty tests, but by rewarding demonstrated moral behavior — specifically, honesty and adherence to principle under pressure. This is exemplified by Azam’s immediate recruitment pitch to Unla Kee after he truthfully reported a 200 GP transaction rather than pocketing the money.

Unlike [[status-driven-behavior]] or [[defensive-deference]], which describe suppression of voice under authority, this concept describes the *leveraging* of moral courage as a recruitment metric. The Alliance values trustworthiness over obedience — order requires integrity, not compliance.

This pragmatic moral calculus distinguishes the Lord’s Alliance from other factions: the Harpers are idealists, the Zhentarim are profit-driven, the Order of the Gauntlet are zealots. The Alliance seeks reliable instruments — and integrity is the most reliable trait in a chaotic world.
