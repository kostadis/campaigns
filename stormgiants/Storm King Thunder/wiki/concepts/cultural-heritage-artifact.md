# Cultural-heritage-artifact

A cultural-heritage-artifact is an object of profound symbolic, historical, or familial significance to a noble house, lineage, or cultural group. Unlike mere treasure, its value is not monetary but existential—it anchors identity, validates legitimacy, and connects the present to ancestral memory.

In the context of this campaign, such artifacts are not passive relics but active political tools. The [[lighthouse-statue-of-elara-seasong]] exemplifies this: its recovery and return to House Meliame is a ritual of honor, while its interception by House Margaster is an act of cultural erasure. This extends the concept of [[symbolic-targeting-of-cultural-artifacts]] beyond religious iconography (e.g., the Angharradh statue) to include aristocratic heritage, revealing that power in this world is often wielded through the control of memory and meaning.

The loss or theft of such an artifact triggers social collapse, not because of its material worth, but because it signals the vulnerability of the house’s entire narrative. Protecting or restoring it becomes a moral imperative, elevating mundane quests into matters of legacy and dignity.
