# Heroic Death Wish

A character’s deliberate pursuit of a fatal, noble end not as punishment, but as moral closure — a final act of integrity that redeems past failures without requiring confession or atonement.

Unlike [[execution-as-redemption-mechanism]], which is externally imposed, the Heroic Death Wish is self-chosen. It is not about forgiveness — it is about finality. Orsik embodies this concept: he seeks death against Iymrith not to save the world, but to end his own internal torment — his betrayal of the Harpers, his role in Llorkh’s ruin, his complicity in the party’s moral decay.

This arc rejects the notion that redemption requires speech or apology. Instead, it demands action: a final, decisive, and self-sacrificial act that restores dignity through silence. Vardis’s grief over Orsik’s wish highlights its emotional weight — the party does not stop him because they understand: his death is not defeat. It is his only remaining truth.

The Heroic Death Wish is a narrative mechanism for characters who have lost faith in systems — divine, institutional, or moral — and find meaning only in the final, unspoken act.
