# Unilateral Decision-Making

The act of making critical choices without group consensus, even when those choices affect the entire party — often justified as necessary, efficient, or morally superior.

Orsik’s disclosure of Harper teleportation circles to the Zhentarim and his rejection of King Hekaton’s military aid are prime examples. He believes his experience and intelligence make him the only one qualified to decide — a mindset inherited from his military past and amplified by the Headband of Intellect.

This behavior mirrors the institutional corruption of the Zhentarim and SEER: power is centralized, accountability is absent, and dissent is suppressed. The party, having rejected external manipulation, now replicates its internal structure.

Unilateral decision-making fractures trust. Vardis feels betrayed. Unla Key feels excluded. Thistle, already morally compromised, sees it as confirmation that no one will stop him. The party’s unity is not broken by Iymrith — it is eroded by their own leaders.

This concept reveals that the greatest threat to the party is not the dragon — it is their own internalization of authoritarianism.
