# Ideological split in the Emerald Enclave

The ideological split in the Emerald Enclave is the foundational fracture that divided the organization into interventionist and non-interventionist factions. It originated during the War of the Dragons, when Delaan Winterhound, a senior leader and staunch non-interventionist, was persuaded by the hero Malfire to commit Enclave forces against Tiamat’s summoning.

This decision — though widely regarded as wise and morally necessary — shattered the Enclave’s unity. Those who supported intervention, led by figures like Lyra, came to view Delaan’s choice as the only defensible path in the face of existential threat. Those who opposed it, later crystallizing under Adabra Gwynn’s leadership, saw it as a betrayal of naturalist principles — a slippery slope into destructive meddling.

The split is not merely political; it is philosophical. It reveals that non-interventionism is not monolithic: Delaan exemplified a flexible, situational non-interventionism — one that could be overridden by moral urgency — while Adabra developed a rigid, dogmatic version that rejected persuasion entirely. The Enclave’s current tensions are not new debates, but echoes of a decision made centuries ago.

This schism is the root of [[weaponization-of-personal-debt]]: just as Aldric Stone Path’s silence was used to condemn Adabra, Delaan’s decision was used to condemn non-interventionism itself. The Enclave’s identity is now defined by this historical rupture — a wound that never healed.
