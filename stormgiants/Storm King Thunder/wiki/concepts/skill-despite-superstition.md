# Skill-Despite-Superstition

Skill-Despite-Superstition is the phenomenon in which an individual’s professional competence is not diminished — and may even be enhanced — by deeply held irrational or superstitious beliefs.

Dwolin the Grout exemplifies this concept: his belief that gold veins are sentient entities requiring ritual appeasement does not impair his ability to locate and extract gold — it defines it. His superstition is not a flaw; it is the framework through which he operates with unmatched precision.

This concept challenges the assumption that rationality and effectiveness are synonymous. It parallels [[true-believer-verification]] in Adabra Gwynn’s campaign, where sincerity — not doctrinal correctness — is the metric of legitimacy. Unlike Dabbledob’s [[evidence-based-pragmatism]], which demands empirical validation, Skill-Despite-Superstition accepts outcomes as proof, regardless of mechanism.

It is not ignorance — it is a different epistemology. The setting does not judge Dwolin’s beliefs as primitive; it observes that they work. His existence proves that tradition, when rooted in experience, can be a form of expertise.

This concept expands the campaign’s moral and epistemic diversity: competence is not monolithic. It can be forged in the fires of myth.
