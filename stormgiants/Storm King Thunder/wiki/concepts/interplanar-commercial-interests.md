# Interplanar-commercial-interests

Interplanar-commercial-interests refer to the systematic acquisition, valuation, and movement of goods whose worth derives from their origin in, connection to, or resonance with planar phenomena — operating within institutional, quasi-legal commercial frameworks rather than criminal networks.

Unlike [[interdimensional-smuggling]], which is clandestine, illicit, and driven by profit through concealment (e.g., Aletra Satora’s Midnight Tears trade), interplanar-commercial-interests are openly conducted under the guise of legitimate trade, with pricing, sourcing, and logistics structured around institutional demand. The Lionshield Coster, under Linene Graywind, exemplifies this model: it pays double market value for planar-associated goods not because it is hiding something, but because its leadership explicitly values such items as strategic commodities.

This concept introduces a new economic layer to the campaign’s cosmology: planar phenomena are not merely threats or magical curiosities — they are marketable assets. The “bosses” of Lionshield Coster treat planar resonance as a commodity class, akin to rare metals or enchanted materials, but with higher volatility due to cosmological instability.

The existence of interplanar-commercial-interests challenges the assumption that all interplanar activity is covert or corrupt. It reveals a normalized, elite economy where merchants operate with institutional backing, using social warmth and rumor-gathering as tools of intelligence — not deception. This transforms [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] from a tactic of concealment into a framework of specialization.

The disruption of supply chains by the War of the Giants demonstrates that these commercial interests are deeply entangled with cosmic events. Their vulnerability to “tariffs” — likely metaphysical or bureaucratic barriers — shows that even legitimate trade is subject to the collapse of divine and planar order.

Interplanar-commercial-interests represent the institutionalization of cosmic exploitation: not by warlords or cults, but by merchants who quietly buy the fragments of a broken universe and store them for future use.
