# Moral-Blind-Spot-in-Pragmatism

The psychological and ethical failure of individuals who operate under utilitarian logic — prioritizing efficiency, profit, or survival — while completely ignoring spiritual, emotional, or moral dimensions of their actions. Unlike villains who knowingly commit evil, these figures are not malicious; they are simply unaware.

[[daran-edermath-silverleaf]] exemplifies this: he sees the lighthouse wrecks as salvageable treasure, not as graves. His surprise at [[vukradin]]’s objection reveals he never framed the act as anything but economic. He is not corrupt — he is numb.

This concept extends [[emerald-enclave]] by inversion: where Adabra refuses to act to preserve balance, Daran acts without moral reflection. Both are forms of detachment — one through withdrawal, the other through absorption.

The danger of this blind spot is its invisibility. It does not scream. It does not rage. It simply asks, “Why wouldn’t you take it?” And in a world where gods are silent, that question becomes the loudest lie of all.
