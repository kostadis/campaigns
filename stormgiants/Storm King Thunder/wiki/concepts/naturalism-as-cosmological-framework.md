# Naturalism as cosmological framework

A philosophical lens that rejects moral binaries (good/evil, natural/artificial, monster/human) and instead treats all phenomena — including planar entities, cults, and magical distortions — as expressions of an indifferent, self-regulating cosmos. All things are part of a greater, amoral balance.

Adabra Gwynn’s worldview is grounded in this framework. She does not see demons as evil, but as natural; she does not see the Cult of Talos as corrupt, but as “the necessary element of distraction in the cosmic balance.” She reframes “barbarians” as “nomads,” and “blights” as ecological responses.

This is not mere tolerance — it is ontological equivalence. All forces, however destructive, are equally valid components of the whole. This makes her philosophy deeply resistant to empirical refutation: any attempt to “correct” nature is, by definition, an error.

It is the metaphysical foundation of [[anti-interventionism]]. However, it creates an unresolvable paradox when confronted with *motivated blight* — corruption that is intentionally engineered by sentient agents (e.g., drow, cults). If all things are natural, then even deliberate sabotage is natural — rendering the concept of “balance” meaningless.

This framework challenges the wiki’s existing assumptions about [[soul-manipulation]] and [[divine-knowledge-as-strategic-tool]], suggesting that moral categories themselves are the root of imbalance.
