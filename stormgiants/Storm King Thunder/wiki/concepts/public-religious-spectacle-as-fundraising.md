# Public Religious Spectacle as Fundraising

Public Religious Spectacle as Fundraising is the strategic use of theatrical, emotionally charged rituals to attract attention, legitimize unorthodox doctrine, and solicit material support. Brin Bundlewine and Giles Slipper-Shine conduct parades through Phandalin, displaying their self-inflicted scars, carrying the severed head of a Kraken Society cultist, and performing acts of ritual blinding — all framed as divine miracles.

These spectacles are not merely acts of worship; they are carefully staged performances designed to provoke awe, fear, and curiosity. The unsettling nature of the displays ensures they are remembered, discussed, and ultimately funded. The townspeople may be unsettled, but they are engaged — and engagement translates into donations of wood, stone, and coin.

This mirrors [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] and [[divine-knowledge-as-strategic-tool]]: religion becomes infrastructure, and devotion becomes a public relations campaign. The spectacle does not seek to convert through argument, but through visceral impact. It is faith as theater — and in a world where truth is guarded by silence, spectacle becomes the only voice loud enough to be heard.
