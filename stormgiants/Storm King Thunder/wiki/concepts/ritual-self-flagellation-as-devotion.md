# Ritual Self-Flagellation as Devotion

Ritual self-flagellation as devotion is the practice of inflicting controlled physical pain upon oneself as an act of worship, purification, and proof of spiritual sincerity. In the context of Brin Bundlewine’s movement, this takes the form of deliberate burning and laceration of the skin, often under the midday sun, to emulate the “searing pain of justice” attributed to Lathander.

Unlike penitential practices in other faiths, which aim to atone for sin, Brin’s followers embrace self-harm as a celebratory act — a joyful offering that proves their faith is not theoretical, but embodied. The resulting scars and sunburns are not hidden, but displayed as sacred marks, akin to [[true-believer-verification]] through Meril’s Staff. The pain is not endured — it is *chosen*, and thus transformed into glory.

This practice blurs the line between martyrdom and aesthetic performance. It is not driven by guilt, but by ecstatic conviction. It echoes the [[guilt-as-internal-prison]] of Blagothkus and Blasius, but replaces psychological torment with physical radiance. The body becomes both altar and offering — a living testament to a theology that demands proof not in words, but in wounds.
