# Oath-Bound Duty

The concept that an agent's commitment to an organization (specifically the [[emerald-enclave]]) is a binding constraint that supersedes personal friendships, social invitations, or individual desires. This principle is exemplified by [[thomas]], who refused to join the adventuring party despite cordial interactions, citing his oath as a primary motivator for his actions.
