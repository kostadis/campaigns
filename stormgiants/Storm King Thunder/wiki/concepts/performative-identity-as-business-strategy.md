# Pragmatic Fabrication

**Pragmatic Fabrication** is the practice of constructing and maintaining a public persona—often involving exaggerated claims or false reputations—to ensure economic survival and social legitimacy. Unlike criminal fraud, this strategy is rooted in "emotional commerce"; it prioritizes the provision of hope and comfort over historical accuracy to navigate environments of scarcity.

This concept highlights the intersection of personal ego and the practical requirements of entrepreneurship in a dangerous frontier. It reveals how the performance of heroism can become a necessary survival mechanism for those who are not inherently heroic but must maintain a viable presence in a harsh world.

### Case Study: [[falcon-the-hunter]]
A primary example is seen in [[falcon-the-hunter]], who perpetuates the myth of being a legendary "dragonslayer" to ensure the profitability of his hunting lodge. While he does not explicitly claim to have slain the dragon, he allows guests to believe it because the fantasy provides the emotional comfort necessary for his business to thrive. 

The reality of his situation—being the sole survivor of an encounter with [[iymrith]] who scavenged the hoard after others died—remains hidden from the public. This strategy mirrors [[disguised-influence]] and [[cover-identity-as-merchant]], but applies it to personal legacy rather than espionage or trade. The fragility of this identity is revealed when exposed by [[vukradin]]; such an exposure triggers a psychological unraveling not because the lie was criminal, but because the individual is forced to confront that they needed the myth to sustain their own existence.
