# Performative Identity as Business Strategy

A strategy in which an individual constructs and maintains a public persona not to deceive for malice, but to sustain economic survival and social legitimacy. [[falcon-the-hunter]] embodies this concept by cultivating the myth of being a “dragonslayer,” despite being the sole survivor of an encounter with [[iymrith]] who scavenged the hoard after others died.

He does not claim to have slain the dragon — he allows guests to believe it. The fantasy, he says, is good for business. His lodge thrives not on truth, but on the emotional comfort of myth. This is not fraud in the criminal sense, but a form of emotional commerce: he sells hope, not history.

This strategy mirrors [[disguised-influence]] and [[cover-identity-as-merchant]], but applies it to personal legacy rather than espionage or trade. It reveals how survival in a world of scarcity often requires the performance of heroism — even when one is not heroic. The collapse of this identity, when exposed by [[vukradin]], triggers Falcon’s psychological unraveling — not because he is exposed as a liar, but because he is exposed as a man who needed to believe his own lie to keep living.
