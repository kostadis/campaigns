# Information asymmetry in NPC encounters

A core narrative and mechanical condition in which player characters possess incomplete, misleading, or entirely withheld information about an NPC’s true identity, affiliations, motives, history, or capabilities. This asymmetry is deliberately engineered — not accidental — to generate suspense, moral ambiguity, plot twists, and thematic depth.

The gap in knowledge forces the party to act on partial or uncertain information, often leading to misjudgments, missed opportunities, or unintended consequences. It mirrors the broader theme of [[divine-silence]] — truth exists, but is guarded, fragmented, or inaccessible.

Unlike outright deception, this mechanic relies on omission, ambiguity, and controlled revelation rather than active manipulation. It is more insidious and narratively rich because the player’s uncertainty stems from what is *not said*, not from what is falsely presented.

## Examples

- **[[azan]]** — Entire identity and purpose are withheld; a blank slate awaiting encounter. The party knows only that he is a caravaneer to be met.
- **[[azam]]** — Known superficially as a merchant, but secretly an agent of Hsing and the SEER.
- **[[baash-a-kweelani]]** — Known by name and appearance, but all deeper details (affiliation, inventory, location, true intent) are concealed.

## Design Function

This technique serves as a structural tool for pacing and worldbuilding. It is frequently paired with:
- [[pre-meeting-narrative-structure]] to build anticipation and misdirection
- [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] to normalize concealment within everyday roles
- [[disguised-influence]] and [[layered-command-structure]] to extend the web of hidden agendas

While related to deception, information asymmetry is distinct: it does not require the NPC to lie — only to remain silent, evasive, or opaque. This preserves narrative flexibility and deepens player engagement through curiosity and cautious interaction.
