# Layered Command Structure

A hierarchical model of authority in which public-facing agents (like Azam) execute operations while deferring to hidden, unaccountable superiors (like Hsing). This structure ensures plausible deniability, operational security, and psychological distance between decision-making and field consequences.

Azam is the visible leader of the caravan and the party’s point of contact, but he never makes final decisions — he consults Hsing, who remains silent until issuing absolute commands. This mirrors the [[divine-oracle]]’s mediated truth-telling, but in a secular, bureaucratic context: authority is not divine, but institutional, and its source is deliberately obscured.

This structure creates emotional strain on the executor (Azam’s anxiety, fear of failure) while shielding the decision-maker from accountability. It reflects a broader trend in the setting: power is not wielded openly, but channeled through intermediaries — whether divine or human — to maintain control without responsibility.
