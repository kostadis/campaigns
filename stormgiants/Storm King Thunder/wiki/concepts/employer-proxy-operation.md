# Employer-Proxy-Operation

The *Employer-Proxy-Operation* is a structural mechanism in which an organization deploys a public-facing agent — often unaware of the true nature of their employer’s goals — to manage a sensitive operation, location, or relationship. The proxy serves as a buffer: their ignorance protects the organization from exposure, while their presence provides legitimacy, stability, or social cohesion. This model enables powerful entities to operate in plain sight, using the emotional labor, social capital, and physical vulnerability of the proxy to achieve outcomes that direct action could not.

Don-Jon Raskin is the clearest example: hired by the Neverwinter Consortium to oversee the Mountain’s Toe Gold Mine, he believes he is fulfilling a straightforward contract. He is unaware that his employer is tied to the [[kraken-society]], nor does he comprehend the deeper purpose of the mine — whether it is resource extraction, ritual access, or the testing of social engineering models. His role is to be the face of the operation, not its architect.

Similarly, [[falcon-the-hunter]] serves as a proxy for the [[neverwinter-consortium]], scouting threats, securing access to the Mountain’s Toe Gold Mine, and absorbing the risks of regional instability in exchange for a financial stake. He is not a corporate executive — he is a front. His identity as a “dragonslayer” and lodge owner masks his role as a corporate scout. When the mine is poisoned or the Woodland Manse becomes a threat, the blame falls on him, not the consortium. His vulnerability — his financial dependence, his emotional investment in the lodge — makes him the perfect instrument.

This model mirrors other campaign patterns — such as Azam’s role under Hsing, or Aletra Satora’s under “KP” — but with a critical distinction: Raskin and Falcon are not complicit. They are not willing agents, nor corrupted souls. They are *useful idiots* — a term not of contempt, but of narrative function. Their charm, optimism, or self-justifying myths (Raskin’s tall tales of whaling expeditions; Falcon’s self-image as an independent hero) make them ideal for maintaining appearances and deflecting suspicion.

The Employer-Proxy-Operation is a form of institutional camouflage. It is not mere manipulation — it is *absorption*. The proxy is not used to deceive others — they are used to *prevent* deception from being detected. Their perceived autonomy shields the true power behind the scenes.

This concept reinforces the campaign’s central theme: power does not always wear a crown. Sometimes, it wears a miner’s hat, smiles too much, and tells tall tales about whaling expeditions that never happened. Other times, it hides behind a dragonslayer’s cloak, profiting from fear while pretending to slay it. In this world, the most dangerous actors are not monsters or dragons — they are the institutions that outsource their violence, corruption, and secrecy to those who believe they are acting for themselves.
