# Ritualized Resource Extraction

Ritualized Resource Extraction is the practice of embedding symbolic, superstitious, or ceremonial acts into economically critical labor processes — not as decoration, but as perceived prerequisites for success.

At Mountain’s Toe Gold Mine, Dwolin the Grout performs rituals involving the Earthstone Gem to “appease” the gold vein, believing its cooperation is necessary for extraction. His rituals are not optional; they are non-negotiable conditions of labor. This mirrors the [[divine-oracle]]’s constrained truth-telling: both systems operate under rules that defy rational utility but are enforced as sacred.

This concept contrasts with corporate efficiency models, yet persists because it works. It is not ignorance — it is tradition codified into practice. Similar patterns appear in the [[true-believer-verification]] of Adabra Gwynn, where sincerity is validated by outcome, not magic.

Ritualized Resource Extraction reveals that even under industrial oversight, folk epistemologies endure when they produce results. It is a form of [[tolerated-eccentricity-in-labor-hierarchies]] institutionalized through necessity.

This concept bridges the gap between myth and labor, showing that belief systems are not relics — they are operational frameworks.
