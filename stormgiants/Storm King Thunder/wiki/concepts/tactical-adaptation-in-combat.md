# Tactical-adaptation-in-combat

The ability to dynamically adjust force composition, strategy, and objectives in response to battlefield developments. [[Bad Fruul]] demonstrated this by escalating his assault on Parnast: after his initial test wave of goblins and wolves was destroyed, he deployed ogres, half-ogres, cave bears, and finally an owlbear — adapting to enemy strength rather than relying on brute force.

This behavior contradicts the stereotype of the mindless giant and reveals a commander capable of learning and responding. His use of poison in Ambush Gap and his reliance on intelligence from Kragota Threeteeth further demonstrate layered planning.

His adaptation, however, was constrained by the limitations of his intelligence network and the instability of his artifact-driven mind. His failure to land a single attack despite these adaptations suggests his tactical awareness was not fully aligned with his physical or magical capabilities — a fatal flaw rooted in his possession by [[Narcellia Varam’myr]].

This concept challenges the assumption that non-humanoid or monstrous commanders are inherently predictable or unstrategic.
