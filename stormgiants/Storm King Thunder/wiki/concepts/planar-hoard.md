# Planar Hoard

A planar hoard is a collection of treasure — typically gold, gems, trade bars, or magical artifacts — fused into a single, inseparable mass through planar magic. Unlike conventional hoards, these cannot be separated, sold, or used without specialized knowledge of planar engineering or ritual disentanglement.

Cryovain’s hoard, estimated at 3,000 gold worth of materials, was fused into a single block of ice, residium, and metal — a physical manifestation of the Carver’s ritual anchoring the dragon’s essence to the site. The hoard is not stolen or hidden — it is *locked*, rendered economically useless and narratively inert.

This phenomenon extends beyond Cryovain, suggesting a pattern: powerful entities subjected to planar rituals or domination may have their wealth bound to their fate. Planar hoards serve as narrative dead ends — symbols of lost value, trapped potential, and the collateral cost of cosmic interference.

They contrast sharply with the fluid economies of the SEER or House Sartorra, where wealth moves invisibly. A planar hoard is a monument to stagnation — a treasure that cannot be spent, only mourned.
