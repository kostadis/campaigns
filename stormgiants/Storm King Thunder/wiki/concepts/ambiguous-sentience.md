# Ambiguous Sentience

Ambiguous Sentience refers to entities whose nature — including species, intelligence, or consciousness — is deliberately left undefined within the narrative and record-keeping. Unlike NPCs whose ambiguity stems from incomplete intelligence (e.g., Azan), entities with Ambiguous Sentience are structurally opaque, with no canonical classification provided even in internal documentation.

The most prominent example is [[belg]], the companion of Mama Manyknuckles. Described only as “beloved,” Belg’s lack of any taxonomic or behavioral data creates a narrative void that invites interpretation. This ambiguity is not an oversight — it is a design choice that preserves mystery, enables future revelation, and deepens emotional resonance by forcing characters and players to project meaning onto the unknown.

Ambiguous Sentience contrasts with [[disguised-identity-as-race]], where a being’s true nature is hidden but known to the world. Here, even the world does not know. This makes Ambiguous Sentience a tool for existential unease — a reflection of a world where bonds, identities, and even the nature of life itself are unstable.

It may also echo [[spiritual-abandonment]]: if divine and natural orders are fraying, then the very definition of “being” may be in flux. Belg may not be a creature at all — but a remnant of a lost order, a ghost of loyalty, or a symbol of what the world has forgotten how to understand.
