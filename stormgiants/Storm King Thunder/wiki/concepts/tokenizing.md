# Tokenizing
Tokenizing is a method of manipulation used by various entities, including Fiora, Ghelryn Foehammer, the Ghost (Cloud Giant), and Imber Chen. It involves strategic manipulation, shared cultural values, and acts of bravery, ensuring that the party's actions align with the manipulator's goals.

## Overview
Tokenizing is a form of manipulation that leverages strategic manipulation, shared cultural values, and acts of bravery. Entities such as Fiora, Ghelryn Foehammer, and the Ghost (Cloud Giant) use these elements to influence others, while Imber Chen employs a different tactic by converting social situations into commercial deals.

### Fiora's Use of Tokenizing
Fiora employs tokenizing as a means to manipulate others. This involves strategic manipulation, shared cultural values, and acts of bravery. These elements are essential to her strategic approach. She uses these tactics to influence and control the party, ensuring their actions align with her goals.

### Ghelryn Foehammer's Use of Tokenizing
Ghelryn Foehammer uses tokenizing to gain loyalty and recognition. Shared cultural values and acknowledging acts of bravery are crucial aspects of his manipulative strategy. Through this method, he aims to influence others effectively. He leverages these elements to secure the party's support and ensure their actions are aligned with his objectives.

### Ghost (Cloud Giant)'s Use of Tokenizing
The Ghost (Cloud Giant) controls the oracle and its mechanisms to manipulate the party's actions and decisions. By doing so, he ensures the party follows his directives to achieve his vengeance quest. The key elements include manipulation and control. He uses the oracle and its rules to guide the party's actions, thereby influencing their behavior to suit his needs.

### Imber Chen's Use of Tokenizing
Imber Chen's method of manipulation involves converting social situations into commercial deals. This demonstrates his strategic approach to leveraging social interactions for personal gain. His tactic is distinct from the others, focusing on turning social exchanges into opportunities for financial benefit.
