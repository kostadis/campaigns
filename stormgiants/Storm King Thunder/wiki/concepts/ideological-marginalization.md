# Ideological marginalization

The process by which an individual is excluded, silenced, or delegitimized within their own ideological or institutional community due to holding views deemed too radical, extreme, or disruptive.

Adabra Gwynn was marginalized by the Emerald Enclave’s interventionist wing, then further isolated when her own faction’s leadership (Reidoth) was excluded from the council she called. Her radical naturalism — which equated demons with natural phenomena and condemned all intervention — made her a pariah even among fellow druids.

This mirrors [[divine-silence]] and [[spiritual-abandonment]]: the feeling of being forsaken by the very structures one once trusted. But unlike divine silence, ideological marginalization is human-made — a political act, not a cosmic one.

Adabra’s response was not retreat, but escalation: she built an external coalition (Soma, Falcon, Aldric) and waged a public campaign against her former peers. Her marginalization did not break her — it radicalized her.

This concept reveals how ideological purity can become a form of self-imposed exile — and how the most dangerous radicals are not those who reject society, but those who were once part of it.
