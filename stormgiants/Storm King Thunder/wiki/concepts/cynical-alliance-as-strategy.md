# Cynical-alliance-as-strategy

A political and moral framework in which alliances are formed not for mutual benefit, but to weaken third parties so that the actor may rise in their absence. Blagothkus allied with the Cult of the Dragon and Sandesyl Morgia not to achieve peace or power directly, but to ensure the destruction of “small peoples” — mortals, dwarves, and other non-giants — so that giants could inherit a weakened world.

This strategy is cynical because it requires moral compromise: the sacrifice of innocent lives for abstract, long-term gain. It is a perversion of [[divine-knowledge-as-strategic-tool]]: instead of using divine insight to guide just action, Blagothkus used it to justify atrocity.

Unlike [[cover-identity-as-merchant]], where deception hides operations, cynical alliance openly embraces betrayal as policy. It is not hidden — it is celebrated as wisdom. But as Blagothkus learned, such strategies breed isolation, resentment, and inevitable collapse. His downfall was not caused by enemies — it was caused by the moral vacuum his strategy created.
