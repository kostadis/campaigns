# Administrative Weaponization of Authority

The use of titles, procedures, and institutional rules not to serve order, but to evade moral responsibility, deflect accountability, and suppress dissent.

Harbin Wester weaponizes authority by invoking “Lord Neverember’s orders,” insisting “proper meetings require agendas,” and claiming he “cannot act without paperwork.” He uses proceduralism as a wall — not to ensure fairness, but to stall, confuse, and silence. When Vukradin threatens a town meeting, Harbin responds not with dialogue, but with bureaucracy: “pre-work,” “preparation,” “formal notice.”

This is the inverted [[divine-oracle]]: where the divine oracle answers truthfully within limits, Harbin creates artificial limits to avoid truth. Where the Oracle’s constraints are sacred, Harbin’s are self-serving. His authority is not a tool of governance — it is a weapon of evasion.

He does not need magic to control. He needs a stamp, a signature, and a rulebook. In a world where gods are silent, the most effective tyranny is the one that wears a badge.
