# Motivated Blight

A critical philosophical blind spot in the [[anti-interventionist-faction]]'s worldview, and a central paradox in [[soma]]'s arc. [[motivated-blight]] refers to a deliberate, puppet-controlled corruption of an ecosystem — engineered by sentient agents (e.g., drow, cults, planar entities) — that contradicts the doctrine of [[naturalism-as-cosmological-framework]].

Unlike spontaneous ecological decay, planar bleed, or natural imbalance, a motivated blight is *intentional*, *targeted*, and *strategic*. It is not an accident or consequence of imbalance, but an act of malice disguised as nature.

The destruction of the [[whispering-grove]] exemplifies this phenomenon: though interventionist rituals contributed, the event was accelerated and manipulated by [[narcellia-varammyr]], bound within the Headband of Intellect, who used the grove as a conduit for her own ends. This is a motivated blight.

[[adabra-gwynn]]’s naturalist philosophy cannot account for this. If all things are natural, then even deliberate sabotage is natural — rendering her entire framework incoherent. Yet she refuses to acknowledge it, attributing the grove’s ruin solely to interventionist actions. This creates a fatal flaw in [[anti-interventionism]]: it cannot distinguish between chaos and malice, treating agent-driven corruption as mere “natural” disorder.

Soma’s crisis stems from this contradiction. Taught by Adabra to see the world as inherently balanced, she must now reconcile that worldview with the reality of puppet-controlled corruption. The existence of motivated blight implies that not all forces are equal; some are *agents*, not expressions — undermining the foundational assumption of naturalism as a cosmological framework.

This concept exposes the core tension between passive observation and active malice, and forces a reevaluation of non-interference as a moral or ecological imperative.
