# Truth-Constraint Mechanism

A system that delivers truth but is bound by fixed, non-negotiable limits — whether divine (the Oracle’s six questions per day) or institutional (the SEER’s intelligence gaps). These constraints prevent abuse, ensure humility, and maintain control over knowledge.

The Oracle of the Eye of the World exemplifies this mechanism: it cannot lie, but it can refuse to answer trick questions, laugh at manipulation, and end interaction through ritual acts (such as torch extinguishing). Its constraints are sacred, ancient, consistent, and self-enforcing — designed not as arbitrary restrictions, but as moral and cosmic boundaries. Truth is accessible only to those who respect its form: literal answers, no prophecy, no speculation, and strict limits on quantity.

In parallel, institutional systems like the SEER operate under bureaucratic and accidental constraints — gaps in coverage, delayed responses, or filtered data — that serve the same function: truth is too dangerous to be free. Both divine and institutional variants transform knowledge from a gift into a privilege, and truth from a revelation into a transaction. Access requires submission to structure — whether through ceremony, silence, or protocol.

This mechanism extends [[true-seeing]] by introducing systemic, rather than perceptual, truth. It also informs how divine silence can coexist with active revelation: truth is available, but only to those who honor its boundaries. It is the foundation of power in a world where direct intervention is forbidden — whether by gods or by governments — ensuring that wisdom remains a mirror, not a weapon.
