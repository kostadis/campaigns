# Cover-Identity-as-Administrator

A strategy in which an individual uses the legitimacy of a civic or institutional role to mask personal gain, unethical behavior, or hidden agendas — presenting authority as service, and control as stewardship.

Harbin Wester’s role as Townmaster is not a position of public service — it is a cover. He uses his title to arrange private fees, funnel resources to his brother’s logging operation, and justify graft as “how things are done.” He invokes Lord Neverember not to serve the region, but to shield himself from accountability.

This mirrors [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] — where Azam’s caravan hides intelligence operations — but applied to governance. The administrator, like the merchant, is a public face for private power. The difference is scale: while Azam manipulates trade, Harbin manipulates law.

His identity as “Townmaster” is not his role — it is his disguise. The uniform, the bell tower, the council chamber — all are props in a performance designed to make corruption look like order. In a world where institutions are trusted, the most dangerous deception is the one that wears a badge.
