# Governance Reform via Majority Voting

Governance reform via majority voting is a political proposal to increase the number of decision-makers in a governing body to enable decisive, representative outcomes through simple majority rule, as opposed to consensus-based or bicameral systems. It is driven by the belief that deadlock arises not from too many voices, but from insufficient representation.

Dabbledob advocates this model for Gnomengarde, opposing Fibblestib’s bicameral proposal. Her argument implies that governance should reflect the diversity of thought within the scientific community, not constrain it through layered veto structures. This reform seeks to make decision-making more responsive, transparent, and democratic.

The concept parallels the use of the [[divine-oracle]] as a strategic tool: both systems attempt to channel truth or authority through constrained mechanisms. But while the Oracle limits quantity, majority voting seeks to expand participation. The theoretical possibility of a [[tamper-proof-voting-box-theory]] further complicates this: can procedural integrity be engineered, or is it inherently vulnerable to manipulation?

This concept reveals Gnomengarde as a society wrestling with the same epistemic tensions as the wider world: how to balance freedom of thought with the need for decisive action.
