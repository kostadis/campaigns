# Magical shrinking curse

A form of non-lethal magical alteration that reduces a creature’s physical size without killing it, often tied to cursed locations or ancient enchantments. The most documented instance is the curse of the cave that shrank Blooddrinker from a full-sized bear to a cat-sized companion.

Unlike [[hybridization-experiments]], which fuse biological traits across species, magical shrinking preserves the creature’s core identity while altering its scale — yet it may induce secondary psychological or spiritual changes, as seen in Blooddrinker’s shift from aggression to affection-seeking. The curse appears to be location-bound and possibly sentient, as it selectively affects creatures within its domain.

The mechanism is unknown, but its effects suggest a form of dimensional compression or soul-entanglement. It may be related to [[planar-travel]] artifacts or ancient giant enchantments. The curse’s persistence after the creature’s removal from the site implies it is now an intrinsic part of the subject’s being.

Blooddrinker’s case is unique in that the shrinking coincided with trauma and rescue, leading to behavioral transformation beyond mere physical change — suggesting the curse may interact with emotional states or spiritual bonds.
