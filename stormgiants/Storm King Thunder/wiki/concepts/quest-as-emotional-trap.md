# Quest-as-emotional-trap

A narrative structure in which a seemingly straightforward mission — offering material reward for rescuing or retrieving something — is designed to lure characters into a scenario whose true purpose is emotional resolution, not material gain.

Harbin Wester’s quest posting for Alphonse "Big Al" Kalazorn is a canonical example. The stated goal — recover the sheriff and claim the mithril chainmail — was a decoy. The real objective was to force Alphonse into confronting Qelline Alderleaf and acknowledging his abandonment.

This device leverages [[information-asymmetry-npc-encounters]] and [[pre-meeting-narrative-structure]] to create suspense and moral complexity. The party is not merely completing a job — they are unwitting agents of emotional justice. The quest’s success is measured not in gold or gear, but in tears, confession, and the quiet step toward reconciliation.

It represents a shift in campaign design: from treasure hunts to soul hunts.
