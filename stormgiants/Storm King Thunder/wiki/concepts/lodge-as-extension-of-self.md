# Lodge as Extension of Self

The physical structure of [[falcon-the-hunter]]’s lodge is not merely property — it is his identity, legacy, and emotional anchor. Built with the proceeds of his survival from [[iymrith]]’s hoard, the palisade, the wine cellar, the guest rooms — all are extensions of the myth he constructed to survive his trauma.

Its destruction by the [[cult-of-talos]] is not just a loss of property — it is the collapse of his self-narrative. When he says, “Once again, the Falcon Lodge was burnt,” he is not speaking of bricks and timber — he is speaking of his soul. The lodge’s architecture reflects his internal state: fortified, performative, isolated.

This concept extends [[castle-as-character]] into civilian life: a home becomes a mirror of the psyche. Its ruin signals not just defeat, but existential erasure. Falcon’s desire to rebuild it as a performance venue is not a business plan — it is an attempt to reassemble his identity from the ashes, not as a dragonslayer, but as a man who finally dares to be heard.
