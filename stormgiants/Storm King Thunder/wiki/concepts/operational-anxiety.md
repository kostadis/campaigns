# Operational Anxiety

The psychological burden borne by intelligence operatives who are responsible for outcomes but lack control over decisions. Azam spent two weeks in Parnast convinced the party had failed — a period of escalating dread, isolation, and self-doubt — despite having no authority to alter their actions or course.

This anxiety stems from the dissonance between accountability and agency: he must deliver results, but cannot command the agents who produce them. His deference to Hsing is not just obedience — it is emotional surrender. He is the conduit, not the source.

Operational anxiety mirrors the broader cultural trauma of [[spiritual-abandonment]]: just as mortals suffer under divine silence, operatives suffer under institutional silence. The absence of clear direction, combined with the weight of expectation, creates a quiet, pervasive dread — the emotional cost of covert work in a world where gods and agencies alike have withdrawn from direct intervention.
