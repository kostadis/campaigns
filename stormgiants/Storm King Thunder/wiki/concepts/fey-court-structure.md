# Fey Court Structure

The Seelie Court operates as a highly formalized, hierarchical bureaucracy where titles confer not just status, but tangible magical and social power. Unlike chaotic portrayals of fey in other mythologies, the Seelie Court is structured with administrative roles — most notably the Chief of Staff and the Satyr — that blend ceremonial duty with operational authority.

Blasius’s tenure as Chief of Staff reveals that these roles involve managing court logistics, mediating disputes, and controlling access to the Prince. His reinstatement as “personal Satyr” is a demotion in title but a continuation of influence — a euphemism for surveillance. The Satyr, traditionally seen as a wild, hedonistic creature, is here a court functionary, entrusted with heraldry, diplomacy, and ritual.

Authority in the Seelie Court is not derived from magic alone, but from institutional recognition. This enables [[fey-portal-bypass]] — the use of one’s title to override magical wards — and reinforces [[disguised-influence]]: power is wielded through perceived legitimacy, not overt force.

This structure mirrors mortal courts and the [[divine-oracle]]’s constrained authority: power is real, but only when properly invoked. The Court tolerates dysfunction (e.g., Blasius’s drunkenness) not out of weakness, but because it understands that control is maintained through inclusion, not suppression.
