# Sensory-driven characterization

A narrative technique in which a character’s presence, personality, or emotional impact is conveyed entirely through non-verbal, sensory cues — such as taste, smell, sound, texture, or visual detail — rather than dialogue, backstory, or plot involvement.

This method is exemplified by [[cooragh_struckt]], whose identity is defined solely by the quality and spicing of her food. The party learns nothing of her origins, motivations, or history — yet her cooking leaves a lasting impression, particularly on [[valphine]], who finds in it a rare moment of quiet comfort. 

Sensory-driven characterization bypasses exposition to create immediacy and emotional texture. It is especially effective in environments of [[narrative-economy]], where minimalism enhances immersion. It can transform mundane details — a meal, a scent, a sound — into anchors of emotional memory.

This technique contrasts with traditional character development and is often used to elevate [[background-npc]]s into [[quietly-resonant-npc]]s — entities that affect internal states without ever speaking.
