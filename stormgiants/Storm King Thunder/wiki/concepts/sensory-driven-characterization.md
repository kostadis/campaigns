# Culinary Impression

A narrative technique—often categorized as **Sensory-driven characterization**—in which a character’s presence, personality, or emotional impact is conveyed through non-verbal, sensory cues such as taste, smell, sound, texture, or visual detail rather than dialogue, backstory, or plot involvement. 

In the specific form of a **Culinary Impression**, this technique utilizes food to introduce a character to the party. By focusing on the quality and seasoning of dishes, a character's presence and skill are established without requiring immediate verbal engagement. This method bypasses exposition to create immediacy and emotional texture, transforming mundane details into anchors of emotional memory.

This approach is particularly effective in environments of [[narrative-economy]], where minimalism enhances immersion. It serves to elevate [[background-npc]]s into [[quietly-resonant-npc]]s—entities that affect the internal states of others without ever speaking. 

This is exemplified by cooragh-struckt at the [[wayside-inn]]. Her identity is defined solely by the quality and spicing of her food; while the party learns nothing of her origins or motivations, her cooking leaves a lasting impression, providing a rare moment of quiet comfort for characters such as [[valphine]].
