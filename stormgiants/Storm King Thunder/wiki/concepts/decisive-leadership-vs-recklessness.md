# Decisive Leadership vs. Recklessness

Within the [[emerald-enclave]], a tension exists between two modes of operation: the "deadlocked" approach of argument-driven deliberation and the "decisive" approach of action-oriented leadership.

## The Decisive Approach
As noted by [[marian]], some leaders (such as [[lyra]]) prioritize intuition and past experience to break political or tactical stalemates. From this perspective, actions that appear "reckless" to outsiders are viewed as necessary decisive strikes made when the organization is paralyzed by debate.

## The Argumentative Approach
The opposing view holds that decisions should only be made through consensus and rigorous debate. This approach is often seen as more stable but can lead to paralysis during crises, a state that proponents of decisive leadership argue is a failure of the organization.
