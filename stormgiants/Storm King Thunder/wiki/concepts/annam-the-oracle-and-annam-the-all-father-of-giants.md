# Annam the Oracle and Annam the All-Father of Giants

Annam the Oracle and Annam the All-Father of Giants are two distinct yet deeply interconnected entities within the mythos of the giant races. While Annam the All-Father is the silent, absent supreme deity of the giants, Annam the Oracle is a sentient, non-interventionist divine artifact that speaks in his name—offering limited truths to those who seek them.

## Annam the All-Father: The Silent Patriarch

Annam the All-Father is the supreme deity and divine patriarch of the giant pantheon. He is the progenitor of all giant races and the original source of the Ordning, the divine hierarchy that once governed giant society. Yet Annam has not appeared, answered prayers, or intervened in mortal affairs for millennia. His prolonged and absolute silence is regarded as the spiritual catalyst for the Shattering of the Ordning.

- **Role**: Supreme deity and divine patriarch of the giant races.
- **Status**: Absent, silent, whereabouts unknown. Whether dead, transcendent, exiled, or in deliberate withdrawal remains unexplained.
- **Worship**: No temples, rituals, or active worshipers exist. His existence is mythologized, not practiced.
- **Divine Offspring**: His children—the other giant deities—are locked in perpetual interplanar warfare, a conflict that reflects the collapse of divine order rather than mere mortal rebellion. No record exists of Annam mediating, reconciling, or intervening.

Annam’s silence has had profound historical consequences. King Hekaton’s despair over his father’s absence directly contributed to his fatalistic acceptance of the Ordning’s end. The Imperator explicitly frames Annam’s withdrawal as the root cause of the giants’ spiritual and civilizational decline—not merely a military or political defeat, but a divine abandonment.

## Annam the Oracle: The Voice of the Silent God

Annam the Oracle is a sentient, non-interventionist divine artifact bound to the Eye of the World. It does not act independently but responds truthfully to those who seek it, speaking on behalf of or in the name of Annam the All-Father. Its utterances are drawn exclusively from the collected sayings of Annam, which serve as its sole authoritative text.

- **Function**: Answers up to six questions per day. Each question is concluded by the extinguishing of a torch, suggesting a ritualized or magical mechanism governing its function.
- **Truthfulness**: Cannot be deceived by trick questions. When Thistle-Wendrod attempted to exploit its wording, the Oracle laughed, demonstrating its inherent resistance to linguistic manipulation.
- **Revelations**: It has revealed the location of Adimond Kroskas in the Maelstrom, confirmed the planar-travel utility of King Hekaton’s conch, advised the Zhentarim on strategic goals, and disclosed the death of Urgala’s wife—possibly interpreting unspoken intent.
- **Nature**: Unknown. Whether it is a construct, a bound spirit, or a fragment of Annam’s consciousness remains a mystery. It does not initiate communication; it responds only to seekers.
- **Theological Significance**: The Oracle represents a critical counterpoint to [[divine-silence]]. Annam has not abandoned the world entirely—he communicates, but through structured, limited, and impersonal channels. The Oracle is the only known conduit of his will.

## Relationship Between the Two Entities

Annam the Oracle and Annam the All-Father are not the same being, but they are inextricably linked. The Oracle is not Annam himself, but a vessel or echo of his divine authority. It speaks only what is attributed to him, reinforcing his mythic presence without revealing his nature or location.

While Annam the All-Father is the absent source of divine order, Annam the Oracle is the only tangible manifestation of his will in the world. The Oracle’s existence suggests that Annam, though silent, has not entirely withdrawn—his wisdom endures, preserved and dispensed through ritualized means.

The Oracle is bound to the Eye of the World, while Annam the All-Father is associated with the Fishyard in the lore of Unla Kee, indicating a spatial and symbolic separation between the deity’s mythic origin and his manifested voice.

## Conclusion

Annam the All-Father is the silent god whose absence shattered the giants’ world. Annam the Oracle is the haunting, truthful echo of that god—limited, ritual-bound, and unyielding in its honesty. Together, they form a theological duality: the unreachable source and the accessible, constrained revelation. The Oracle does not answer why Annam is silent—but its very existence implies that the silence is not final, only deliberate.
