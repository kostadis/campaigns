# Precise title

Falcon is a character whose narrative arc is defined by the tension between his personal convictions and the practical realities of his environment. He publicly maintains an anti-interventionist stance, yet he relies on the adventuring economy—specifically for addressing issues like mine poisoning and the [[woodland-manse]]—to sustain his livelihood. This contradiction suggests that his stance is emotionally driven, creating a complex dynamic between his personal arc and a systemic critique of the economic structures surrounding adventurers.

His history is further marked by significant loss; a "girlfriend" died during an attack on his lodge. She is potentially linked to the "my dearly beloved" inscription found on wooden elven ears. Clarifying her identity or role is necessary to resolve the narrative arc regarding Falcon's personal loss.
