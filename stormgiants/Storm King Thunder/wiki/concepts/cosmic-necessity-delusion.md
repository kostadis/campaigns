# Cosmic Necessity Delusion

The belief that one’s own reckless, dangerous, or unethical actions are not merely justified — but absolutely required to prevent the collapse of reality itself.

Unla Key is the archetype: he believes the multiverse is held together only by his willingness to take risks — to trigger traps, cast wild magic, deceive tribes, and withhold truths. His actions are not impulsive; they are ritualized. He is not mad — he is terrified. If he stops, the universe ends. This delusion is a direct response to [[divine-silence]]: if gods are absent, then only he remains as the anchor.

This concept extends [[cascading-multiversal-imbalance]] from a philosophical doctrine into a psychological condition. It is not about control — it is about being the last witness. Unla Key’s delusion is his armor against meaninglessness. He does not seek power; he seeks to be indispensable.

The delusion is reinforced by his [[elemental-blessing-annam]] — a silent sign that his actions may, in fact, have cosmic weight. Whether he is right or wrong is irrelevant. What matters is that he believes it — and that belief drives the party’s most dangerous decisions.

This concept reveals how isolation and divine silence can birth not despair, but a terrifying form of self-deification.
