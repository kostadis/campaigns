# Convert's Radiance

**Convert’s Radiance** is a visible, non-magical transformation in a true believer’s demeanor and physical appearance following a profound spiritual awakening. It is characterized by a radiant, serene expression, heightened coloration (typically sunburn or flushed skin), and an unmistakable aura of quiet joy — often mistaken for divine favor or magical blessing, though it arises entirely from internal conviction and ritual practice.

Unlike magical auras or holy light, convert’s radiance is a psychological and physiological byproduct of sustained devotion, particularly within the unorthodox Lathanderite movement centered on solar pain. It is most prominently observed in Giles Slipper-Shine, whose sunburned skin and ecstatic smile after Valphine’s sermon exemplify the phenomenon. The radiance is not performative; it is involuntary, persistent, and deeply authentic.

This concept extends the understanding of [[holy-disfigurement]] by adding an emotional dimension: the suffering is not merely a mark of piety, but a catalyst for inner peace that manifests outwardly. It reinforces the legitimacy of [[unorthodox-lathanderite-interpretation]] and [[ritual-self-flagellation-as-devotion]] by showing their effects are not confined to pain — they produce joy.

Convert’s Radiance serves as a social signal: it draws attention, discomfort, and sometimes reverence from onlookers, making it a key element in [[public-religious-spectacle-as-fundraising]]. It is the quiet proof that belief can transform the body and soul without magic, ritual, or institutional sanction.
