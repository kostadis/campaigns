# Harvesting as Moral Continuation

Harvesting as moral continuation is the act of collecting and repurposing the remains of a slain, sentient, or dominated being — not as exploitation, but as a ritualized acknowledgment of its existence and suffering. It transforms death into utility, and tragedy into responsibility.

Soma’s methodical collection of Cryovain’s scales and breath pouch — despite her plea for mercy — exemplifies this concept. She did not abandon his body; she honored it by ensuring its components were not wasted. This mirrors her earlier harvesting of the treant’s remains after its death in the Whispering Grove.

The act is morally ambiguous: it suggests that to truly acknowledge a victim, one must not only mourn, but carry their legacy forward. It is not hypocrisy — it is pragmatism rooted in deep moral conviction. Yet it leaves unresolved tension: Can one honor a soul by using its flesh? Does utility negate grief, or deepen it?

This practice stands in contrast to the party’s usual moral flexibility. They spared undead, negotiated with demons, and questioned violence — yet they killed Cryovain and then harvested him. The act of harvesting becomes the only remaining gesture of moral reckoning.
