# Defensive Deference

Defensive deference is a psychological coping mechanism characterized by yielding to authority figures or dominant personalities to avoid conflict, criticism, or perceived rejection. It often masks underlying insecurity, unresolved trauma, or a fragmented sense of self. Individuals exhibiting defensive deference may appear agreeable or compliant on the surface, but their compliance is not voluntary—it is a survival strategy.

Amy exemplifies this pattern: she consistently avoided asserting her creative opinions about her role, even when she had clear preferences. When challenged by Kaelen, she withdrew. When confronted by “Geoff Mamet” (Orsik), she surrendered immediately—not because she was convinced, but because the tone of authority overwhelmed her resistance. Her cheerful demeanor is a performance that shields a guarded past, though the nature of that past remains unknown.

Thistle Wendrod identified defensive deference in Amy as emblematic of a broader social phenomenon in Triboar and beyond: individuals who have learned that self-expression invites risk, and silence is safer. This concept extends beyond Amy to other non-combatant characters whose voices are muted by social or institutional power structures.
