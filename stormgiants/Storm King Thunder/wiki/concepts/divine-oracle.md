# Divine Oracle

A sentient, limited-capacity conduit of divine knowledge, operating under fixed, sacred rules that prevent exploitation while preserving the integrity of divine truth. Unlike direct divine intervention, the Oracle does not act — it only answers. It is not a god, but a mechanism — possibly a relic, construct, or echo — that preserves and dispenses divine wisdom without altering the natural order.

The Oracle of the Eye of the World is the only known example in the campaign. It speaks exclusively on behalf of Annam the All-Father, citing his collected sayings as its sole authority. Its constraints are absolute and ritualized: typically limited to six questions per day, immunity to deception or manipulation, and mandatory closure procedures (e.g., extinguishing torches). It cannot be tricked, coerced, or misled — its answers are always true, but never expansive beyond its prescribed bounds.

This system redefines [[divine-silence]]: the deity has not abandoned the world, but has chosen to communicate only through constrained, non-intrusive channels. The Oracle’s limitations are not failures, but covenantal features — divine discipline designed to prevent misuse and preserve the sanctity of truth.

It contrasts with [[true-seeing]] — which enhances sensory perception — by providing factual knowledge, not sensory clarity. It also differs from prophecy: it answers specific, bounded questions, not open-ended future outcomes.

A secular parallel is the [[seer]], an intelligence network with limited coverage that provides accurate information (e.g., Joon’s location) but is constrained by human limitations — blind spots, incomplete data, and operational failures. Unlike the Oracle, whose constraints are sacred and consistent, the SEER’s gaps are not features but flaws: compromises born of chaos, not covenant.

This contrast reveals a fundamental tension in the setting: truth can be constrained by law (divine) or by chaos (human). One is a covenant; the other, a compromise.
