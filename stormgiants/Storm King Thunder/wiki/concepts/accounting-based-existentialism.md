# Accounting-Based Existentialism

A phenomenon observed in corrupted or spectral entities where their spiritual peace, torment, or very existence is tied directly to the state of financial ledgers.

In the case of the-chief-accountant, her ability to find rest was not dependent on traditional funerary rites, but on the successful "closing of the books" and the settlement of all outstanding accounts.
