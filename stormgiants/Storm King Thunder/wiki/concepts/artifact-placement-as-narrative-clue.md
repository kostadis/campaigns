# Artifact-placement-as-narrative-clue

The deliberate positioning of physical objects within a space to encode hidden meaning, trigger events, or reveal unstated narrative truths — without verbal explanation. The artifact’s location is not decorative; it is instructional.

In the Dragonbarrow, Lady Alagondar’s Dragon Slayer Sword was buried "across the way" from her tomb, placed atop a dragon’s skull and oriented northward. This placement confirmed Boney’s account, triggered the manifestation of her ghost, and pointed toward the White Dragon’s lair — all without a single word spoken.

This technique relies on the player’s or party’s observational skills and interpretive intuition. It replaces exposition with environmental storytelling, making discovery an act of reverence or deduction rather than exposition.

It is often paired with [[ghost-as-unresolved-purpose]] and [[emotional-resonance-through-absence]], forming a triad of silent narrative devices that prioritize atmosphere, mystery, and earned revelation over direct exposition.
