# Shattering of the Ordning

The Shattering of the Ordning was a cataclysmic cosmic collapse that dismantled the divine hierarchy once governing giant society, triggering widespread warfare, spiritual decay, and existential decline across the giant races. Once a stable, divinely sanctioned order maintained by Annam the Allfather, the Ordning disintegrated into chaos following his prolonged silence—an event now understood as the foundational cause of the catastrophe.

## Spiritual Collapse: The Silence of Annam

Annam’s withdrawal of divine presence precipitated a cascading failure of cosmic and cultural order. His silence was not merely an absence but an active abandonment, leading to:

- The despair and abdication of **King Hekaton**, who abandoned his role as guardian of the Ordning
- The fracturing of the **divine patrilineage**, as Annam’s offspring turned against one another in power struggles and ideological schisms
- The erosion of faith among all giant subraces, resulting in cultural disintegration, loss of identity, and spiritual malaise

This theological rupture transformed the Ordning’s collapse from a mere political or military failure into a profound cosmic tragedy of divine abandonment.

## Consequences

The collapse of the Ordning unleashed cascading effects across the planes and societies of the giants:

- **Interplanar warfare** erupted among giant deities and their champions, further destabilizing the cosmic balance
- **Loss of identity and purpose** among giant subraces led to the dissolution of traditional roles, hierarchies, and social cohesion
- **Rogue entities** exploited the vacuum of authority, including [[adimond-kroskas]], who rose to prominence through soul-manipulation and the fabrication of false identities to replace the lost divine order
- **Soul-manipulation** and identity fraud became widespread, as giants sought to reconstruct meaning in the absence of divine sanction

## Historical and Theological Context

The **Imperator** explicitly identifies Annam’s silence as the root cause of giant decline, a theological interpretation that has superseded earlier narratives emphasizing only mortal conflict. While some naturalists within the [[emerald-enclave]] link the Shattering to the faction’s loss of neutrality—facilitated by the influence of [[malfire]]—the dominant understanding now centers on the divine failure as the primary catalyst.

The event is inextricably tied to the [[war-of-the-giants]], which followed and intensified the collapse, and is often viewed as both symptom and consequence of the Ordning’s unraveling. The Shattering marks the definitive end of the giants’ golden age and the beginning of their long, fragmented decline.
