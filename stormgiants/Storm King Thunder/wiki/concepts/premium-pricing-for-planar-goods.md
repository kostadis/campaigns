# Premium-pricing-for-planar-goods

Premium-pricing-for-planar-goods is a market mechanism in which items associated with planar phenomena — such as proximity to rifts, exposure to planar energy, or origin in disrupted zones — command prices significantly above their material or magical value, based on institutional demand rather than utility.

Linene Graywind pays double the price offered by local buyers like Barthen for orc armor and pelts, not because they are enchanted, but because they were acquired in areas affected by planar instability — such as the Neverwinter Wood. This pricing is not speculative; it is strategic. It signals that her “bosses” have a structured, non-negotiable demand for planar-touched materials, treating them as a distinct commodity class.

This concept introduces economic logic to the campaign’s cosmology: planar resonance is not a mystical curiosity — it is a market signal. The premium is not for rarity, but for *provenance*. The value lies in the item’s history, not its function.

This mechanism distinguishes institutional interplanar trade (Lionshield Coster) from criminal smuggling (Aletra Satora). Aletra’s Midnight Tears are valuable because they unmake souls; Linene’s pelts are valuable because they carry the echo of a broken world. One is poison; the other is archive.

The existence of premium pricing implies a hidden economy — one where planar artifacts are collected, cataloged, and stored by unseen actors. The defunct interplanar shipping device referenced by Valphine suggests this is not an isolated practice, but part of a broader, institutional infrastructure.

Premium pricing also creates moral ambiguity: is Linene exploiting the party’s labor? Or is she offering fair compensation for goods that carry invisible trauma? Her warmth and social attentiveness suggest she does not see herself as a predator — but as a conduit.

In a world where gods are silent, the most valuable things are not the ones that speak — but the ones that still carry their voice.
