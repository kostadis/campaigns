# Internal Hum (sensory phenomenon)

A non-auditory, internally perceived sensation — described as a "hum" — experienced solely by Jarek moments before an orc ambush. The sensation was not heard externally but felt as a vibration or resonance within the mind, and vanished abruptly as the attack commenced. No other party member reported the phenomenon.

This phenomenon is the first documented case of a pre-attack internal sensory cue potentially linked to coordinated hostile action. Its origin remains unexplained, but plausible hypotheses include:

- **Psionic resonance**: A hidden psychic link among the orcs, possibly amplified or transmitted by an artifact.
- **Planar interference**: A side effect of planar leakage from interdimensional smuggling operations (e.g., the Model 1489 Calliope), affecting sensitive individuals.
- **Divine or constrained signal**: A silent, non-verbal communication from a higher authority — akin to the Divine Oracle, but internalized and limited to one observer.
- **Artifact-induced sensitivity**: Jarek may be an unwitting conduit or receiver of a control mechanism similar to the Headband of Intellect, though without overt manipulation.

The hum’s disappearance suggests it is not a permanent condition, but a triggered signal. Its specificity to Jarek raises questions: Is he uniquely sensitive? Was the signal targeted? Is it a one-time anomaly or part of a broader pattern?

This concept challenges assumptions about orc behavior as purely tribal or instinctual, introducing the possibility of non-physical, coordinated control mechanisms operating beneath the surface of conventional warfare.
