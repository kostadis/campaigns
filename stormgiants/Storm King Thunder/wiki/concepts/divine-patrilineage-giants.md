# Divine Patrilineage (Giants)

The theological structure of the giant pantheon, centered on Annam the Allfather as the sole progenitor deity from whom all other giant gods descend. This patrilineal hierarchy was the foundation of the Ordning — the cosmic order that governed giant society.

## Structure
- Annam: Supreme patriarch, source of divine authority
- His offspring: Deities of specific giant races (frost, fire, storm, etc.)
- Each offspring: Ruler of a domain and lineage within the Ordning

## Collapse
The ongoing warfare among Annam’s offspring indicates a breakdown of familial and divine order. Without Annam’s guidance or intervention, the divine children turned against each other, fracturing the hierarchy from within. This internal divine conflict mirrored and accelerated the collapse of mortal giant civilization.

## Significance
The patrilineage model explains why the shattering of the Ordning was not merely political or territorial, but deeply familial and spiritual. The war among the gods was a civil war of blood — making the decline of the giants a tragedy of inheritance and abandonment.
