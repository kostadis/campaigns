# Complicit Silence

The collective, unspoken agreement to avoid moral reckoning — to let wrongdoing persist without confrontation, not out of fear, but out of a shared belief that addressing it would destroy the group.

The party’s most consistent behavior is silence. They do not confront Thistle’s moral drift. They do not challenge Orsik’s betrayal of the Harpers. They do not question Unla Key’s deceptions. They do not demand Vardis explain the ring he stole.

This silence is not weakness — it is strategy. They have learned that truth fractures. They have learned that accountability kills. Their unity is maintained not by virtue, but by omission.

This concept is the narrative engine of the campaign. It is how the party survives Iymrith — and how they become the very thing they fight: a system that hides its corruption behind a facade of heroism.

Complicit silence is the final stage of [[narrative-economy]]: not just withholding detail, but actively erasing moral responsibility. It is the sound of a group choosing to stay together — even if it means becoming monsters.
