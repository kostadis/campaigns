# Divine-worthiness-test

A metaphysical standard by which a being’s moral or spiritual fitness is judged — not through revelation or oracle, but through silent, internal, and often unspoken condemnation. Blagothkus was declared “unworthy” by Amn after killing his son Eigeron at the Eye of the All-Father, despite no divine voice being heard.

Unlike the [[divine-oracle]], which provides constrained, verbal truth, the divine-worthiness-test operates without communication. It is a cosmic verdict rendered in silence, internalized by the subject as guilt, shame, or existential collapse. Blagothkus’s entire arc — his passivity, deflection, and eventual breakdown — stems from this unspoken judgment.

This concept challenges the assumption that divine judgment must be audible or visible. It suggests that gods may not need to speak to condemn — their absence itself is the verdict. The test is not administered; it is experienced.

Harshnag’s “oracle worthiness test” joke among giants implies this concept is culturally recognized — a shared understanding that worthiness is not earned through ritual, but lost through moral failure. The test is not a question to be answered — it is a shadow that follows the guilty.
