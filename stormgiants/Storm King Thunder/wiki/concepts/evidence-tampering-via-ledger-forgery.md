# Evidence Tampering via Ledger Forgery

Evidence tampering via ledger forgery is a form of bureaucratic corruption in which official records are systematically altered to conceal illicit activity. Aletra Satora maintained a forged inventory ledger in her quarters, crossing out legitimate entries (e.g., “iron for 500 pounds”) and replacing them with coded notations for contraband (e.g., “Midnight Tears - crate 4 - do not drop”).

This is not random fraud — it is institutionalized deception. The ledger was not hidden; it was *visible*, but designed to be misread by auditors unfamiliar with the code. This tactic exploits trust in administrative systems, assuming that no one will question the legitimacy of official documentation.

Ledger forgery is the perfect crime for a technocratic smuggler: it requires no magic, no violence, only precision and access. It allows the operation to function under the guise of legality, while the true cargo moves unseen. The discovery of the ledger by the party was not a triumph of investigation — it was a failure of oversight.

This concept extends [[divine-knowledge-as-strategic-tool]] and [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] into the realm of criminal bureaucracy, proving that the most effective lies are not spoken — they are written.

**Key Features:**  
- Use of official formats to mask illegality  
- Code-based notation systems  
- Reliance on institutional trust  
- Low visibility, high impact  

**Significance:**  
In a world where gods are silent and magic is common, the most enduring corruption is the one that looks like paperwork.
