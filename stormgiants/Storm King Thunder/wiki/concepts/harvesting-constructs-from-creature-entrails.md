# Harvesting constructs from creature entrails

The practice of **harvesting constructs from creature entrails** refers to the ethical boundary-crossing act of repurposing the biological remains of slain magical creatures — such as mimics — into new inventions or mechanical components. Fibblestib enthusiastically advocates for this practice, viewing sentient or semi-sentient beings as raw materials rather than entities with intrinsic value.

This behavior reveals a profound moral detachment: the death of a creature is not a tragedy, but an opportunity. It reflects a worldview in which innovation justifies the erasure of biological and spiritual boundaries. Soma’s visceral discomfort with this attitude highlights its emotional cost — it is not merely unethical, but existentially alienating.

This concept extends [[unregulated-invention]] by distinguishing between *unregulated* (Facktore) and *regulated but amoral* innovation (Fibblestib). Fibblestib operates within institutional norms — he does not break rules — but he ignores the moral ones. His behavior normalizes the commodification of life, making him a quiet but dangerous figure in a world already saturated with trauma.

The practice is not unique to Fibblestib — it echoes the harvesting of dragon parts, the use of mimic skin in armor, and the repurposing of undead remains. But Fibblestib’s open enthusiasm makes him its most visible proponent. His lack of remorse is not villainy — it is the quiet erosion of empathy in the name of progress.
