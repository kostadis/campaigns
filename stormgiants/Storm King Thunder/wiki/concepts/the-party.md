# The Party

The Party refers to a group of individuals whose actions directly influenced the fate of Halisa, a dryad bound by the Seelie Court’s legal decree known as **Tree Protection Schedule D**. Halisa was originally punished by the Seelie Court for speaking honestly about Queen Titania’s attire—a transgression deemed disrespectful in the rigid hierarchy of the Fey Courts. As punishment, she was exiled, but her release was not an act of mercy.

Instead, Halisa was freed through the trickery of a figure who impersonated Prince Thornacious. This impostor orchestrated her release, but it was formally framed not as a reward, but as an extension of her punishment. Halisa herself cited **Tree Protection Schedule D**—the legal instrument governing her exile—to refuse assistance from the Party, asserting that aiding them would violate the terms of her sentence.

Despite her resistance, the Party’s actions ultimately led to her liberation. Evidence suggests that both Halisa and Helia were freed through the Party’s deception, as noted by a sprite emissary. Halisa later declared her intent to return every 100 years to taunt Helia, indicating a lingering, bitter connection to the events set in motion by the Party.

The Party’s involvement thus intersects with key entities: the Seelie Court, which imposed the original sentence; Tree Protection Schedule D, the legal mechanism used to bind and later manipulate Halisa’s freedom; and the dryads, of which Halisa is one, bound by ancient fey laws and seasonal cycles.
