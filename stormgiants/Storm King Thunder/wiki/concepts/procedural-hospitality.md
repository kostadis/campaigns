# Procedural Hospitality

[[procedural-hospitality]] is a method of managing guests in established or high-security locations where access is not granted by mere presence, but through the verification of specific, formal credentials.

In practice, this involves a gatekeeper (such as corwin) performing a structured check—such as reviewing a letter of introduction—to bridge the gap between a stranger's arrival and their formal reception by a person of interest. This concept reinforces the theme of structured, professional service and social hierarchy within stable settlements.
