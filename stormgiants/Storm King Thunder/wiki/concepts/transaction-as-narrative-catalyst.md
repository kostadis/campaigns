# Transaction as Narrative Catalyst

A low-stakes, realistic exchange — such as a purchase, recommendation, or simple service — that unexpectedly enables critical plot progression, character development, or thematic resonance. Unlike dramatic interventions, these transactions derive power from their ordinariness, grounding high-stakes narratives in believable, human-scale interactions.

While not directly illustrated in Boney’s dossier, this concept is reinforced by the narrative economy of his origin: his entire arc is triggered by Soma’s simple act of approaching skeletal remains — a mundane investigative action that leads to reanimation, loyalty transfer, and critical intelligence. Similarly, the renaming from “Barney” to “Boney” is a transaction of identity, not magic.

This concept complements [[narrative-economy]] by showing that the most powerful narrative moments often arise not from grand events, but from quiet, unremarkable choices: a word spoken, a name accepted, a question asked.

Boney’s story proves that the smallest transactions — of name, of loyalty, of presence — can carry the weight of entire arcs.
