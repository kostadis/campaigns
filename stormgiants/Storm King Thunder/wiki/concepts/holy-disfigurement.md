# Holy Disfigurement

Holy Disfigurement is the aesthetic and theological embrace of bodily damage — scars, burns, blindness, and mutilation — as visible signs of divine favor. In Brin Bundlewine’s sect, the pale skin seared by the sun, the arms marked by ritual burns, and the eyes blinded in ecstatic ritual are not signs of punishment, but of sanctification. These disfigurements are worn with beatific smiles, transforming what would be considered grotesque in other contexts into sacred iconography.

This concept inverts traditional fantasy tropes of holy figures as radiant, pristine, and untouched. Instead, holiness is marked by decay, pain, and transformation. The disfigurement is not accidental — it is cultivated, celebrated, and paraded. It serves as both personal testimony and public spectacle, reinforcing the core tenet of the Unorthodox Lathanderite Interpretation: that true devotion is proven through suffering.

Holy Disfigurement parallels the use of the Kraken Society cultist’s severed head as a relic — both are violations of the body turned into symbols of transcendence. It challenges the party’s assumptions about beauty, purity, and divine presence, suggesting that the most profound spiritual truths may be written in flesh, not scripture.
