# Ordning

The **Ordning** refers to the established order and hierarchical structure of the giant kingdoms. It serves as a central framework for the geopolitical landscape in which current conflicts unfold, providing essential context for the actions of various giant lords and the party's overarching mission.

## Nature and Significance
The Ordning is characterized by a strict hierarchy that requires submission from subordinate leaders to those of higher rank. It is not merely a social custom but a crucial element of factional stability and structure.

* **Factional Structure:** For the [[hill-giants]], the Ordning is fundamental to their social organization.
* **Restoration:** A primary driver of the current narrative is the quest to restore the Ordning, which has been disrupted.
* **Geopolitical Context:** The state of the Ordning influences the motivations of major players, such as [[duke-zalto]], and defines the relationships between different giant factions.

## Hierarchical Dynamics
The hierarchical nature of the Ordning is explicitly demonstrated through the requirements of leadership:

* **Submission Rituals:** Restoration of the Ordning often requires formal acts of submission. For example, King Hekaton has explicitly stated that Chief Guh must kneel before him to facilitate the restoration of the order.
* **Internal Tensions:** The requirement of submission creates significant internal tension, particularly when a leader's personal motivations or existing authority (such as that of Chief Guh) conflict with the necessity of submitting to a higher power like King Hekaton.

## Narrative Implications
The state of the Ordning challenges preconceived notions regarding hierarchy within the magical and giant communities and serves as a key motivator for the party's involvement in the conflict. Discrepancies between the mandates of high-ranking leaders and the actions of subordinate chiefs may indicate deeper instabilities within the giant kingdoms.
