# Veteran-identity-as-armor

A psychological pattern in which a character’s past role in law enforcement, military service, or structured authority is used to project stoicism, competence, and emotional control — masking underlying vulnerability, guilt, or fear of intimacy.

Alphonse "Big Al" Kalazorn, former Sheriff of Triboar, embodies this concept. His professional credibility and tough demeanor shield his emotional fragility, particularly regarding his abandonment of Qelline Alderleaf. His background lends him authority, but also isolates him — he is expected to be unshakable, making his eventual breakdown all the more powerful.

This concept deepens [[defensive-deference]] and [[status-driven-behavior]] by showing how institutional roles can become emotional prisons. The armor of the veteran is not worn for protection from enemies, but from the risk of being seen as weak — especially in matters of the heart.
