# Music as philosophical metaphor

A rhetorical device in which harmony, dissonance, and rhythm are used to represent cosmic order, imbalance, and moral truth. Adabra Gwynn employs music as her primary metaphor for the multiverse: interventionist actions are “discordant notes” that disrupt the natural symphony; true balance is “harmony without force.”

She uses this metaphor to persuade Vukradin, framing the Order of the Gauntlet as rejecting “certain music” — certain planes, entities, or forces — and thus creating imbalance. Her argument is poetic, not logical; emotional, not empirical.

This parallels [[drinking-as-fey-social-mechanic]]: both use ritualized cultural practices to encode deeper philosophical truths. But while fey drinking is about emotional evasion, Adabra’s music is about moral clarity — even if that clarity is flawed.

The metaphor is powerful because it bypasses rational critique. To argue against music is to argue against beauty. Yet it obscures the reality of *motivated blight* — a deliberate, dissonant note played by a hand, not a force of nature.

It is the language of the true believer: not to convince, but to resonate.
