# Anti-interventionism (druidic)

A radical druidic doctrine asserting that all forms of external interference — religious, planar, military, or magical — disrupt the natural equilibrium of the multiverse. Rooted in **naturalism as a cosmological framework**, it rejects moral categorizations like “evil,” “monster,” or “barbarian,” viewing all phenomena as expressions of natural diversity.

Adabra Gwynn is its most prominent modern proponent. She argues that planar anomalies, blights, and even cults like the Cult of Talos are downstream consequences of intervention, not root causes. The solution is withdrawal: “allowing the ecosystem to find equilibrium without help.”

This philosophy is metaphysically verified in Adabra’s case — she is a *true believer*, confirmed by Meril’s Staff’s Resonance power. Her worldview is internally consistent and sincere, yet politically toxic and morally blind. It fails to account for *motivated blight* — deliberate, puppet-controlled corruption — creating a fatal flaw in its explanatory power.

Anti-interventionism is the secular analog of [[divine-silence]]: both describe a world where higher powers (gods or nature) are silent, and intervention is seen as both arrogant and destructive. Unlike divine silence, however, anti-interventionism is an active, militant philosophy — not passive acceptance, but enforced non-action.

It stands in direct opposition to the [[order-of-the-gauntlet]] and the interventionist wing of the [[emerald-enclave]]. The tension between these factions mirrors the party’s own moral compromises: when is non-action complicity? When is action destruction?
