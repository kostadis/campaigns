# Intelligence Network with Limited Coverage

The recognition that even powerful, well-resourced intelligence agencies — such as the SEER — have critical blind spots. The SEER knows the location of Joon and the identity of Thule, yet has zero information on Joon’s organization, suggesting either its extreme secrecy, non-human nature, or existence outside conventional frameworks of power.

This concept contrasts with the [[divine-oracle]], whose limitations are ritualized and truthful. The SEER’s gaps are not rules — they are failures, gaps in data, or systemic blind spots. This introduces narrative vulnerability: the assumption of institutional omniscience is false. Power is not absolute; knowledge is incomplete.

This limitation is not a weakness to be fixed — it is a feature of the setting. It forces characters to act without full information, to trust intuition over data, and to confront the unknown not as a puzzle to be solved, but as a presence to be endured.
