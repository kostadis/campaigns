# Drinking as Fey Social Mechanic

In the Seelie Court, alcohol is not merely a vice, but a ritualized social tool — a mechanism for bonding, evasion, and emotional regulation. Blasius’s constant drinking is not comic relief, but a culturally embedded strategy: it lubricates alliances, masks vulnerability, and provides plausible deniability for failure.

He uses alcohol to bond with Unla Kee, to celebrate victories, to avoid combat, and to justify his false accusations (“I was drunk”). His vision quest, which grants the ability to neutralize poison, is conducted through ritualized intoxication — suggesting that fey magic is often accessed through altered states.

Drinking functions as both connection and escape: it is the social glue of the court and the shield of the guilty. Unlike mortal societies where alcohol is a personal indulgence, in the Seelie Court, it is a shared language — a way to communicate trust, deference, and emotional withdrawal without words.

This mechanic extends [[status-driven-behavior]] and [[defensive-deference]] into the realm of ritual: one does not speak truth sober, but drinks to speak truth — or to avoid it entirely.
