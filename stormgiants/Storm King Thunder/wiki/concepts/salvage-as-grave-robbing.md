# Salvage-as-Grave-Robbing

The ethical ambiguity of recovering treasure from shipwrecks, ruins, or battlefields that may contain unburied human remains — framed as legitimate salvage, but experienced by others as desecration. This concept captures the tension between utilitarian pragmatism and spiritual reverence for the dead.

In the campaign, [[daran-edermath-silverleaf]] embodies this tension: he sees the lighthouse wrecks as economic opportunity, not as graves. His shock at [[vukradin]]’s moral objection reveals he never considered the possibility of bodies — not because he is evil, but because he has normalized the violence of adventuring life. His worldview is one of quiet indifference, not malice.

This concept contrasts with [[weaponization-of-innocence]] (using non-combatants as bait) and [[moral-ambiguity-of-inaction-undead]] (non-intervention by sentient undead), as it is human-centric and non-magical. It is not about control or manipulation — it is about erasure of empathy.

Salvage-as-grave-robbing is not a crime in the lawbooks — but it is a moral fracture in the soul. It is the quietest form of violence: the refusal to see the dead as anything but resources.
