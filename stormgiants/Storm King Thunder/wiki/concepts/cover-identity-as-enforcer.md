# Cover Identity as Enforcer

**Cover identity as enforcer** is a structural archetype in which a visible, physically imposing agent — often violent, tactical, and emotionally volatile — serves as the public face of a hidden, more sophisticated authority. The enforcer’s role is to execute operations, absorb blame, and draw attention away from the true architect.

[[borg-the-hammer]] is a canonical example: a half-orc enforcer commanding [[rot-tusk-ogres]], who carries out the violent legwork of **The Carver**. His brutality, tactical awareness, and emotional instability make him an effective field commander — and a perfect distraction. The party focuses on him, assuming he is the threat, while The Carver remains unseen.

This pattern mirrors [[azam]] (merchant front) and [[aletra-satora]] (engineer front), extending the concept of [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] into the domain of direct violence. Where merchants disguise intelligence operations as trade, enforcers disguise criminal infrastructure as banditry or mercenary activity.

The enforcer is never the master — he is the mask. His failure does not end the operation; it confirms its depth. The Carver does not need Borg to succeed — he needs Borg to be *seen*. The enforcer’s existence proves the system is real; his destruction proves the system is resilient.

This concept reveals a core narrative truth: in this world, the most dangerous figures are not the ones who strike — but the ones who never show their face.
