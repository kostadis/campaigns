# Sacrificial Decoy Tactics

Sacrificial decoy tactics are premeditated strategies in which an ally, subordinate, or innocent is deliberately exposed to danger to achieve a primary operational objective. These tactics are not spontaneous acts of betrayal but calculated, often emotionally weaponized maneuvers designed to exploit moral ambiguity, suppress resistance, and maximize mission efficiency at the cost of expendable lives.

Aletra Satora employs this tactic with chilling precision, marking Brewbarry with a red glow that causes the [[model-1489-spatial-rectification-calliope]] to identify him as a spatial anomaly — redirecting all six [[rift-weavers]] onto him. She openly acknowledges the manipulation to Valphine, instructing her to “keep Brewbarry alive long enough for me to finish.” This is not a momentary decision — it is a planned, rehearsed act of moral detachment, rooted in her belief that loyalty is transactional and lives are expendable. She references a past trap using a stuffed creature as bait, suggesting this is not her first time.

In parallel, Corbin’s assignment to guard Sister Kaella while the party traveled to Icespire Hold exemplifies the same tactic under a veneer of pragmatism. He was a reformed bandit with no combat training, emotionally fragile, and explicitly terrified of Kaella. Vukradin authorized only one hit point of force against her — a symbolic gesture that underscored his lack of faith in Corbin’s ability to control the situation. The party left him alone with a lethal assassin because they were “in a rush.” His death was not an accident — it was the predictable outcome of a system that treats compassion as a liability.

Both cases reveal the tactic’s core structure:  
- **Pre-planned sacrifice** — the victim is selected in advance based on perceived expendability  
- **Public or implicit acknowledgment of intent** — whether through open declaration (Aletra) or systemic neglect (Corbin’s assignment)  
- **Emotional manipulation of witnesses** — Aletra weaponizes transparency to paralyze moral resistance; Corbin’s fate forces complicity through silence and inaction  
- **Use of technology or institutional rules to automate targeting or enable neglect** — Aletra uses the spatial rectification calliope; the party uses mission urgency and minimal force protocols to justify abandonment  

The tactic’s power lies in its duality: it can be overtly cruel (Aletra) or covertly bureaucratic (Corbin), but both are equally lethal. In Aletra’s case, cruelty is flaunted to prove moral superiority; in Corbin’s, it is buried under procedural rationality to mask moral cowardice.

This tactic extends [[betrayal-by-subordinate]] and [[tactical-adaptation-in-combat]] by introducing premeditation and emotional engineering. Unlike spontaneous betrayal, this is planned, rehearsed, and emotionally weaponized. It is structurally identical to [[cynical-alliance-as-strategy]] and [[moral-ambiguity-of-inaction-undead]], but applied to living, redeeming individuals — making their loss more tragic and the moral decay more profound.

It is the ultimate expression of [[narrative-economy]]: a character’s humanity is acknowledged, then discarded for plot efficiency. The most effective predators do not hide their cruelty — they normalize it, whether through spectacle or silence.

**Significance:**  
Sacrificial decoy tactics reveal that the most dangerous systems are not those that conceal their brutality, but those that make it seem inevitable — even reasonable. They do not ask you to approve of the sacrifice; they ask you to accept that there was no other choice.
