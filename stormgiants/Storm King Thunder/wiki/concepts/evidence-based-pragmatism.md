# Evidence-Based Pragmatism

Evidence-based pragmatism is a methodological stance that prioritizes solutions grounded in observable, repeatable, and verifiable data over theoretical speculation, ideological purity, aesthetic appeal, or outlandish proposals. It is not mere utilitarianism or cynicism, but a disciplined commitment to testing claims before endorsing them.

Characterized by reluctant open-mindedness — belief is revised only under evidentiary pressure, not social influence or emotional appeal — this approach demands measurable outcomes and rejects unverified inventions such as the “sanity ray.” It stands in contrast to speculative innovation (as embodied by [[fibblestib]]) and ideological rigidity (as seen in Adabra Gwynn’s anti-interventionism). Unlike Adabra, who withdraws based on philosophical conviction, Dabbledob adapts when data changes.

Dabbledob is the archetype: he tests, observes, and adapts. He respects data, not dogma. His work within [[gnomengarde]] exemplifies how this approach can sustain innovation without descending into chaos. He collaborates with rivals like Fibblestib not to win arguments, but to refine understanding — embracing empirical feedback even from opposing viewpoints.

However, evidence-based pragmatism does not guarantee ethical outcomes. Facktore’s actions demonstrate that the same tools of observation and testing can be weaponized when divorced from moral constraints. He collected data — but without consent, without accountability, without humanity. His “evidence” was extracted from living beings, turning the methodology into a tool of exploitation.

This concept must be tempered with ethical vigilance. Pragmatism without conscience is not wisdom — it is a tool for exploitation. The contrast between Dabbledob and Facktore reveals that the difference between responsible science and predatory experimentation lies not in method, but in moral boundaries. Evidence-based pragmatism, therefore, must be paired with ethical frameworks to prevent harm — especially when applied to [[unregulated-invention]] or [[innocent-as-test-subject]] scenarios.

This approach parallels the [[truth-constraint-mechanism]]: both operate within self-imposed limits to preserve integrity, though one is divine and the other human. In narrative design, it provides a model for rational character agency — where decisions are driven by method, not destiny, trauma, or magic. It is a quiet, persistent force for accountability in worlds saturated with the unproven.
