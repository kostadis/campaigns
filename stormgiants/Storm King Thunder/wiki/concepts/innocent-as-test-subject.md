# Innocent as test subject

Innocent as test subject is the ethical violation of using sentient beings — particularly those with no prior hostility or involvement — as experimental subjects under the guise of scientific inquiry. This concept distinguishes predatory experimentation from legitimate research by the absence of consent, justification, or reciprocal benefit.

Facktore’s ambush of the party exemplifies this: he did not engage them in dialogue, offer warning, or seek permission. He triggered his crossbow system as they passed through his chamber, treating them as disposable data points. His behavior is not madness — it is moral detachment masked as curiosity.

This concept contrasts sharply with [[evidence-based-pragmatism]], where observation is bound by method and restraint. Here, observation becomes violence. The victim is not an adversary, but an unknowing participant in a test they never agreed to. The psychological impact on survivors — such as Vukradin — is profound, as it undermines trust in the very institutions meant to safeguard rationality.

Innocent as test subject is not a fringe behavior — it is a systemic failure. It reveals how easily the language of science can be weaponized to justify cruelty when accountability is absent.
