# Social-attentiveness-as-commercial-tool

Social-attentiveness-as-commercial-tool is the deliberate, strategic use of warmth, praise, memory, and interpersonal engagement to build trust, extract information, and influence behavior — not as a means of deception, but as a method of commercial intelligence gathering.

Linene Graywind exemplifies this concept: she attends performances, remembers names, singles out individuals for public praise (e.g., complimenting Valphine at the Stonehill Tavern), and asks detailed, enthusiastic questions about the origins of goods. Her behavior is not manipulative in the sense of Amy’s [[defensive-deference]] — she does not yield out of fear. She engages out of intent.

This is not charm for its own sake — it is a tool. By making the party feel seen and valued, she lowers their guard, encouraging them to reveal more about their sources, routes, and experiences — particularly regarding planar disruptions in places like Neverwinter Wood. Her redirection of Vukradin’s escort offer into a rumor exchange demonstrates her prioritization of intelligence over transaction.

Unlike [[disguised-influence]], where authority is falsely assumed (e.g., Orsik as “Geoff Mamet”), social-attentiveness-as-commercial-tool operates through authenticity. Linene is genuinely warm — but her warmth is calibrated. She is not lying; she is *listening*.

This technique transforms the merchant from a passive vendor into an active intelligence node. Her outpost is not just a shop — it is a listening post. Her praise is not flattery — it is a probe.

In a world where divine truth is constrained and cosmic forces are hidden, the most effective way to gather information is not through interrogation — but through kindness.
