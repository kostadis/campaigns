# Ambush as experiment

Ambush as experiment is a narrative and tactical mechanism in which a hostile encounter is not designed for conquest, defense, or intimidation, but purely for the collection of empirical data on weapon performance, environmental response, or target behavior. Violence becomes a laboratory protocol.

Facktore’s use of a rotating platform with four automatically reloading heavy crossbows against the party exemplifies this concept. He did not attack to kill, eliminate, or retaliate — he attacked to observe. The ambush was calibrated, automated, and repeatable — a controlled test environment disguised as a trap. The party’s survival, resistance, and countermeasures were not incidental — they were the data.

This concept blurs the line between combat and observation, weapon and instrument. It transforms hostility into a form of inquiry — one that requires no justification, no dialogue, and no remorse. It is the logical endpoint of [[unregulated-invention]]: when science is divorced from ethics, even violence becomes a measurement.

Ambush as experiment is not merely cruel — it is dehumanizing. It reduces sentient beings to variables in a formula. The party did not face an enemy — they were subjected to a test. And in a world where gods are silent, the most chilling threats are not those that roar — but those that measure.
