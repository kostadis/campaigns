# Spiritual Abandonment

The psychological and cultural trauma experienced by a civilization when its divine patrons withdraw presence, guidance, and validation. In the context of giant society, this is the direct consequence of Annam’s silence.

## Manifestations
- Loss of identity and purpose among giant races
- Despair and fatalism in leadership (e.g., King Hekaton)
- Erosion of religious institutions and ritual practice
- Rise of nihilism, heresy, and soul-based corruption

## Historical Impact
King Hekaton’s belief that “the age of giants had passed” was not a political calculation but a spiritual collapse. His surrender to entropy was fueled by the conviction that the divine had forsaken them. This mindset enabled the Ordning’s collapse by removing the will to preserve it.

## Broader Implications
Spiritual abandonment may be a recurring theme in the setting. It suggests that civilizations are not merely defeated by enemies, but undone by the loss of meaning — a vulnerability exploited by entities like [[the-carver]] and [[adimond-kroskas]].
