# Supply-chain-disruption-by-planar-conflict

Supply-chain-disruption-by-planar-conflict is the phenomenon in which large-scale cosmological events — such as the War of the Giants or the collapse of the Ordning — directly and systematically impair the movement of goods, information, and resources across regions, planes, and economic systems.

Linene Graywind’s inability to reliably obtain scrolls or ship goods is not due to banditry, weather, or political instability — it is due to “tariffs” imposed by the unraveling of planar boundaries. These tariffs are not literal taxes, but metaphysical or bureaucratic barriers: unstable rifts, displaced ley lines, or divine decrees that render traditional routes impassable or unreliable.

This concept extends [[ordning-shattering]] beyond its theological and political consequences into the economic domain. The collapse of the giant pantheon did not merely leave giants without gods — it broke the infrastructure that moved goods between planes. The War of the Giants, as referenced in the source, is not just a battle of titans — it is a logistical catastrophe.

The disruption affects both criminal and legitimate actors: Aletra Satora’s smuggling routes are compromised, and Linene’s institutional trade network is paralyzed. This reveals that interplanar commerce — whether illicit or institutional — depends on a stable cosmological framework. When that framework fractures, even the most well-funded networks fail.

The term “tariffs” used by Linene is a euphemism — a linguistic adaptation to make the incomprehensible manageable. It suggests that the disruption is perceived not as chaos, but as regulation — a sign that even in collapse, systems attempt to impose order.

Supply-chain-disruption-by-planar-conflict transforms the campaign’s setting: the world is not just haunted by gods or monsters — it is economically wounded. The scarcity of scrolls, the premium on planar pelts, the silence of shipping devices — all are symptoms of a universe whose plumbing has ruptured.

In this world, the most valuable resource is not gold, magic, or power — it is reliable transit.
