# Credential-based-access

**Credential-based-access** is a system of trust in which authority, entry, or privilege is granted not through reputation, relationship, or magical verification, but through the presentation of a physical or written token — such as a letter, seal, or permit. This mechanism replaces personal recognition with institutional validation, making identity contingent on documentation rather than character.

Corwin’s acceptance of Barthen’s letter of introduction exemplifies this principle: the party is admitted not because they are known, feared, or respected, but because a trusted third party has vouched for them in writing. This system is resilient to deception — it does not require the gatekeeper to judge the visitor’s soul, only to verify the token’s origin.

This concept extends [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] and [[disguised-influence]] into the realm of social infrastructure. It is the mundane counterpart to magical identification spells — and in a world where identities can be forged, stolen, or hidden, it is often more reliable. The letter is not magic, but it functions as if it were.

Credential-based-access reveals a deeper truth: in this campaign, legitimacy is not earned — it is issued. And those who control the issuance — like Barthen — wield quiet, unacknowledged power.
