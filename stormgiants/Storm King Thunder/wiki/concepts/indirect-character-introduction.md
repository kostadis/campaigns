# Indirect-character-introduction

Indirect-character-introduction is a narrative technique in which a character is established through reputation, third-party references, and external stakes before direct appearance. The character remains unseen, their personality undefined, and their motives ambiguous—allowing the world and other characters to define them.

Elara Seasong Meliame is a canonical example: she is known only through Ser Kaelen’s assignment, Vukradin’s recommendation, and House Margaster’s interference. Her absence is not an oversight—it is intentional, leveraging [[pre-meeting-narrative-structure]] to build suspense and player investment. The party knows what is at stake, who she is claimed to be, and why she matters—but not who she is.

This technique deepens [[information-asymmetry-npc-encounters]] by making the character’s true nature a mystery to be uncovered, not a fact to be revealed. It preserves narrative flexibility, avoids over-exposition, and aligns with the campaign’s broader aesthetic of silence, omission, and emotional resonance. The most powerful characters are often the ones the party has yet to meet.
