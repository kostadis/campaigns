# Sentient Veins

“Sentient Veins” is a dwarven folk belief that gold-bearing geological formations possess a form of sentience, will, or emotional responsiveness — akin to living entities that must be courted, respected, and appeased to yield their bounty. This is not a metaphysical doctrine, but a practical cosmology embedded in mining tradition.

Adherents believe gold veins are “shy,” “sullen,” or “easily offended,” and that mining without proper ritual invites silence or collapse. The belief does not require proof — it is validated by outcomes. Dwolin the Grout’s unmatched success is cited as evidence, even by skeptics.

This concept extends the theme of [[coexistence-compromise-with-monster-groups]] to non-living entities, framing resource extraction as a negotiated relationship rather than a conquest. It parallels the [[divine-oracle]] and [[ritualized-questioning]] in its structure: a system of interaction governed by rules, not logic.

The belief is culturally entrenched at Mountain’s Toe Gold Mine and tolerated by corporate management, despite contradicting the Neverwinter Consortium’s profit-driven pragmatism. Whether the veins are truly sentient, or whether the belief is a placebo that enhances focus and precision, remains unverified — and intentionally so.

Sentient Veins represent a form of [[skill-despite-superstition]]: irrational belief as a catalyst for superior performance.
