# Prospecting-as-narrative-entry-point

Prospecting-as-narrative-entry-point is a storytelling technique in which the mundane, economically motivated activity of mineral exploration serves as the natural, grounded gateway into deeper lore, hidden locations, and supernatural phenomena. Unlike traditional adventure hooks rooted in mystery, vengeance, or divine calling, this approach begins with practical labor — digging, panning, mapping — and allows the extraordinary to emerge organically from the ordinary.

Dazlyn Grayshard and Norbus Ironrune exemplify this concept: their pursuit of gold led them to the ruins of an ancient dwarven settlement and ultimately to the Temple of Abbathor. Their motivations are transparent, their methods realistic, and their discovery feels earned — not handed to the party as a quest, but uncovered through persistent, low-stakes effort.

This technique enhances immersion by anchoring fantasy elements in tangible, relatable human (or dwarf) behavior. It avoids the trap of “quest bait” NPCs and instead builds world depth through economic realism. It also aligns with [[narrative-economy]] — the entry point is simple, but the implications are rich.

This model contrasts with [[pre-meeting-narrative-structure]] (where the NPC is a mystery to be solved) and [[cover-identity-as-merchant]] (where trade masks deception). Here, trade is honest, and discovery is accidental.
