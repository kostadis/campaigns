# Magical resonance with artifacts

A phenomenon in which certain magical objects trigger involuntary physiological or behavioral responses in beings with latent magical ties — even if those beings are not the original intended targets. Blooddrinker’s convulsions upon the opening of the stone chest exemplify this concept.

Unlike [[headband-of-intellect-narcellia]], which imposes direct mental control, or [[spectral-control-via-technology]], which uses architecture as a prison, magical resonance is passive and reactive. It suggests the artifact and the creature share an unknown magical signature — possibly due to shared origin, cursed lineage, or soul entanglement.

The stone chest’s effect on Blooddrinker implies it is not merely a container, but a receiver, battery, or prison for magical energy tied to the bear’s altered state. The party does not understand the mechanism, leaving open questions: Is the chest a key? A lock? A mirror? This concept introduces a new subtype of artifact interaction — one that bypasses will, language, or ritual, and speaks directly to the soul or magic embedded in a living being.
