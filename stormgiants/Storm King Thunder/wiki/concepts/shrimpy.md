# Shrimpy

A prisoner delivered to Harbin Wester by the party and held in custody in Phandalin. Shrimpy’s identity, origin, and fate remain entirely unknown. Harbin does not disclose their nature, nor does he indicate any plan for their disposition.

Shrimpy is a narrative void — a symbol of Harbin’s refusal to engage with moral complexity. The party delivers a living being, and Harbin simply takes custody, offering no explanation, no trial, no release. His silence on Shrimpy’s fate mirrors his broader pattern of emotional avoidance.

Shrimpy may be a criminal, a victim, a spy, or a magical entity — but their ambiguity is intentional. They are not a character — they are a question. And in a world where gods are silent, the most haunting mysteries are the ones no one dares to name.
