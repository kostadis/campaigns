# Moral Drift

The gradual, unremarked erosion of ethical boundaries through rationalization, habit, and mutual non-confrontation.

Thistle Wendrod exemplifies this concept: he feeds vampires to Uthgardt barbarians, frames House Margaster for ethnic violence, and executes cooperative cultists — not out of malice, but because “it works.” He justifies each act as necessary for Laela’s rescue, and the party, including Vardis and Orsik, does not stop him. Their silence is not approval — it is exhaustion.

Moral drift is not a single act of evil, but a pattern of incremental compromise. It thrives in environments of shared trauma and institutional secrecy. The party’s refusal to confront Thistle mirrors their broader pattern of [[complicit-silence]] — they avoid moral reckoning because they fear losing their fragile cohesion.

This concept contrasts with [[emerald-enclave]]: Adabra acted from principle; Thistle acts from pragmatism without conviction. His drift is not ideological — it is emotional. He is not a villain. He is a man who has stopped asking whether his actions are right — only whether they are effective.

Moral drift is the quiet corruption of a group that has forgotten how to say no.
