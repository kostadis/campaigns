## Name
- **Aliases:** None

## Source Extracts
- [1]: Greeted the party upon their arrival in Yartar as their host and contact, sent by Krowen.
- [2]: Directed the party to the Wink and Kiss tavern as the Hand of Yartar's known gathering place and confirmed that rogues were pervasive throughout the city.
- [3]: Reacted with intense indignation when Thistle mentioned Adimond Kroskas, insisting the Cult was destroyed. Claimed personal involvement in research on the ritual and held firm that the Spring's First Blossom was not connected to it.
- [4]: Invited Thistle to discuss improvements to his inaccurate Seelie Court illusion garden over tea; was rebuffed.
- [5]: Struck by a bolt of lightning and collapsed. A small, unidentified figure stood over him and spoke the words *"you meddling little fool"* in an unnaturally large voice. Died before the party's return to Harper headquarters.
- [6]: Thistle's examination of Kolbaz's papers revealed that his research into Adimond Kroskas was the proximate cause of his death. The papers contained a critical discovery: **Adimond Kroskas is an alias — he is not who he claims to be.**
- [7]: The physical damage to Kolbaz's chambers suggested something massive materialized in the room, destroyed everything, and dematerialized. Gauche knew that other individuals had come through the teleportation circle around the time of death but withheld this from the party.

## Notes
- **Krowen:** His Harper superior; held a professional grievance over Krowen's policy of using non-sworn contractors.
- **Thistle:** Brief but pointed interactions — mutual friction over the Cult of the Dragon and Seelie Court culture; Thistle was the one who examined his papers after death.
- **Adimond Kroskas:** His investigation into Kroskas's true identity was almost certainly the direct cause of his death.
- **Vardis:** Vardis recalled Kolbaz's death and the chamber damage, connecting it to Adimond and identifying it as consistent with a **Blue Dragon**.
- **Gauche:** Knew details about activity near the teleportation circle at the time of death; withheld this from the party.
