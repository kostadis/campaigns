## Identity
- **Role:** Mud mephit; self-declared opportunist and "New King of the Swamp"
- **Faction:** Nominally aligned with the party; previously navigating between Mama Manyknuckles's half-ogres and the water weird
- **First Appearance:** Marsh of Chelimber — emerged from hiding in vine-covered trees after the party defeated the water weird

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Drive:** Self-preservation and status. Feetor aligns with whoever he perceives as the strongest power at any given moment.
- Theatrical and calculating — his "freed from captivity" speech was entirely fabricated and he privately noted it worked.
- Responds to indignation and flattery in equal measure; can be bought, titled, or hugged into cooperation.
- Fundamentally unbothered by chaos — when the mephit civil war immediately resumed after his grand proclamation, he shrugged it off with "oh well."

## History with the Party
1. **Post–Water Weird:** Feetor emerged from hiding after watching the party defeat the water weird, assessed them as the new dominant power, and delivered a rehearsed speech claiming they had freed him from years of captivity. Declared mud mephits serve whoever is toughest and cast his lot with the party.
2. **Intel & Negotiation:** Provided information on marsh politics — the water weird is immortal and reforms within days, Mama Manyknuckles leads a half-ogre band deeper in the marsh, and mephits were split between the two factions. Argued that killing Mama Manyknuckles would end the civil war.
3. **The Gold Coin:** Initially pushed back on Orsik's questions about mud mephits, accused him of tokenizing. When Orsik clarified he wanted to recruit other mephits, Feetor pivoted instantly, demanded a gold coin as proof of the party's power, and disappeared into the swamp. Returned to report the other mephits agreed to "wait and see."
4. **The Bet with Unla Kee:** Approached Unla Kee privately, claimed to have "figured him out," and roped him into mephit betting action on whether the party would kill the half-ogres — fronting special mud as stakes.
5. **Post-Victory Fallout:** After the party's victory over the half-ogres, Feetor proclaimed himself "New King of the Swamp" and paid Unla Kee his winnings — in special mud, not gold. When confronted, he attempted to hug his way out of trouble and presented Unla Kee with a stone that can be used to call on the mephits for aid, naming him an honorary mud mephit.
6. **The Duel Threat:** When Unla Kee threatened a duel, Feetor immediately capitulated, agreed to retrieve actual gold in exchange for being named "#2," and dramatically declared all mephits now followed Unla Kee. The declaration instantly reignited mephit squabbling over leadership. Feetor was indifferent.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Marsh of Chelimber
- **Active:** Feetor has agreed to retrieve gold owed to Unla Kee; outcome unconfirmed
- **What the Party Knows:** He is opportunistic, unreliable with currency, and responsive to titles and shows of strength. The mephits as a faction are nominally allied with the party but perpetually on the verge of internal collapse.
- **Hidden:** The depth of Feetor's actual influence over other mephits is unclear — his grand proclamations consistently outpace results.

## Relationships
- **The Party (general):** Aligned as the current "strongest power"; loyalty is entirely pragmatic
- **Orsik:** Initial friction over perceived tokenizing; resolved when Orsik framed the request correctly and paid up
- **Vardis:** Co-contributed the gold coin used to secure mephit allegiance
- **Unla Kee:** Closest individual relationship — identified him as a kindred schemer, cut him into a betting operation, owes him gold, and has named him an honorary mud mephit and "leader" of the mephits
- **Mama Manyknuckles:** Former power player in the marsh; now defeated. Feetor had been playing both sides between her faction and the water weird.
- **The Water Weird:** Ancient, immortal, and still reforming somewhere in the marsh. Feetor does not know the location of its lair.
