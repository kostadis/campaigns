# Cryovain

Cryovain was a white dragon and former apex predator of the Icespire region, now known primarily as a victim of the Carver’s domination. Once a solitary and powerful creature, his autonomy was entirely erased by the-carver using the [[the-carver]], which functioned as a mental amplifier and ritual conductor. He was discovered bound at the center of a ritual summoning circle on the rooftop of [[icespire-hold]], his scales dulled to the color of old shadowed ice and his eyes hollow, glazed, and filmed over — described by soma as “wrong.” He was not unconscious, but held in a state of suppressed sentience, his will overwritten by an external force. Though he fought against the chains of control before being fully seized — suggesting residual selfhood — no coherent intent remained.

His death, delivered by [[valphine]] using *sacred flame*, triggered the collapse of the Carver’s ritual and the dissolution of his essence. His demise was not celebrated, but mourned as a tragedy. soma pleaded for mercy, recognizing him as a victim of [[mental-domination]], while [[brewbarry]] struggled to reconcile that the dragon had a name. [[vukradin]] continues to express deep moral complexity regarding the act, and Cryovain’s legacy challenges assumptions about dragon resilience, soul integrity, and the ethics of killing a dominated being.

## Harvested Remains

Following the battle, soma harvested several valuable components from his carcass:

- **Breath Pouch:** A swollen, heavy organ from the throat that radiates a unique [[metaphysical-cold]], described as ancient and unnaturally chilling.
- **Dragon Scales:** Pale, supple scales with a polished, stone-like texture, suitable for potential use in dragon scale mail.
- **Cryovain's Hoard:** A block of fused ice, gems, and gold valued at approximately 3,000 gold. Due to [[planar-instability]], the hoard is currently an inseparable, unspendable block requiring planar expertise to resolve — now referred to as a [[planar-hoard]].

## Moral Complexity

The death of Cryovain remains a profound ethical dilemma within the party. While some viewed his destruction as a pragmatic necessity to prevent indiscriminate destruction, others — particularly soma — argued that he was a sentient being under [[mental-domination]], deserving of mercy and restoration, not annihilation. His existence underscores the moral ambiguity of killing a dominated creature: was he a monster to be slain, or a victim to be saved? His story has become a touchstone for debates on agency, compassion, and the cost of survival in a world where even dragons can be turned into weapons. The act of harvesting his remains is itself seen by some — including soma — as a form of moral continuation: a ritual of remembrance, not merely utility.
