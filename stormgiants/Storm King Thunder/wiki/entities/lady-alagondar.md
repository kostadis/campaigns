# Lady Alagondar

**Lady Alagondar** was a deceased noblewoman whose identity, motivations, and final fate remain shrouded in mystery, known primarily through the testimony of her former mount boney, the arrangement of her tomb in the [[dragonbarrow]], and the artifacts she left behind. Though she never interacted with the current adventuring party during her lifetime, her legacy endures through the unwavering loyalty she inspired, the spectral echoes of her will, and the belief that [[soma]] is her reincarnation.

A deceased noble whose legacy endures through silent ritual, loyal undead service, and symbolic artifact placement. She never appeared to the party in life, and her ghost communicated only through gaze and presence — never speech.

## Identity & Role
Lady Alagondar was a noble of significant standing, though the specifics of her lineage are unrecorded. Her faction and origins are intentionally obscure. She commissioned the sentient undead skeletal horse boney to guard a secret within the [[dragonbarrow]], likely tied to the [[dragon-slayer-sword]], an artifact central to her legacy. Her burial within the dragonbarrow and the deliberate placement of the sword — positioned "across the way" from her tomb — suggest a meticulously planned final act, possibly intended to contain or confront a great evil, such as the White Dragon of the [[ice-spire-mountains]], as indicated by her spectral gaze.

## Personality & Motivations
Her character can be inferred through the devotion she inspired and the structure of her final arrangements:
* **Decisive Leadership:** She selected high-quality companions, such as boney, and earned the deep respect of [[soma]], indicating a commanding presence and exceptional judgment of character.
* **Purposeful:** Her burial and the symbolic placement of the [[dragon-slayer-sword]] imply a ritualistic, intentional design — not merely a grave, but a monument to an unfinished quest.
* **Mournful & Tragic:** As a spectral entity, she appeared silent and hollow-eyed, suggesting her death—or the unresolved nature of her mission—was marked by profound sorrow or tragedy. Her silence, like that of Annam, echoes through the actions of those bound to her.
* **Transcendent Influence:** Her bond with boney transcended death. His reanimation, emotional core, and enduring loyalty are entirely oriented toward serving her. Her absence is the anchor of his identity; his devotion is his only remaining connection to her.

## Spectral Manifestation
Upon the party securing the [[dragon-slayer-sword]] and placing it atop a dragon’s skull in the northern chamber of the [[dragonbarrow]], Lady Alagondar’s ghost materialized. She did not speak, but used [[spectral-directionality]] to stare fixedly toward the [[ice-spire-mountains]], a region identified by [[vukradin]] as the territory of a White Dragon. This silent directive implies her unfinished business is connected to that dragon, and that her spirit remains bound until its defeat—or until her purpose is fulfilled through another. Her presence is not one of vengeance or haunting, but of unresolved purpose.

## Legacy & Loyalty Transference
Lady Alagondar’s influence extends beyond her tomb and weapon. boney mistook [[soma]] for her reincarnation and transferred his devotion without hesitation, demonstrating a profound spiritual or metaphysical bond that outlasted death. This act of loyalty transference underscores the depth of her impact — her legacy persists not through memory, but through the unwavering devotion of a creature bound to her will.

She is a paradigm of [[emotional-resonance-through-absence]] and [[ghost-as-unresolved-purpose]]: her silence amplifies her narrative weight. Her story is not told — it is felt. Her name and status imply noble lineage, but whether she was a protector, a manipulator, or a victim remains ambiguous. Her most enduring legacy is not in what she did, but in what she left undone — and the living who still serve her unspoken command.
