# Lady Alagondar

**Lady Alagondar** was a deceased noblewoman whose identity and motivations are primarily understood through historical context, the testimony of her former mount [[boney]], and the specific arrangement of her tomb in the [[dragonbarrow]]. Though she never interacted with the current adventuring party during her lifetime, her legacy continues through the artifacts she left behind and the misplaced or transferred loyalty of her former servants. The party has leveraged the belief that [[soma]] is her reincarnation to secure the service of [[boney]].

## Identity & Role
Lady Alagondar undertook a significant, though currently unknown, quest. She was the original owner of the [[dragon-slayer-sword]], an artifact central to her legacy. Her presence endures not only through this weapon but also through the enduring loyalty she inspired in her companions, particularly [[boney]], and the reverence shown by [[soma]], who is believed by some to be her reincarnation.

## Personality & Motivations
While her specific goals remain shrouded in mystery, several defining traits can be inferred from her actions and legacy:
* **Decisive Leadership:** She selected high-quality companions, such as [[boney]], and earned the deep respect of [[soma]], indicating a commanding presence and strong judgment of character.
* **Purposeful:** Her burial arrangement within the [[dragonbarrow]] and the deliberate placement of the [[dragon-slayer-sword]] suggest a highly intentional final plan, likely tied to her unfinished quest.
* **Mournful:** As a spectral entity, she appeared silent and hollow-eyed, suggesting her death—or the unresolved nature of her mission—was marked by profound sorrow or tragedy.

## Spectral Manifestation
Upon the party securing the [[dragon-slayer-sword]], Lady Alagondar’s ghost materialized in the northern chamber of the [[dragonbarrow]]. She did not speak, but used [[spectral-directionality]] to stare fixedly toward the [[ice-spire-mountains]], a region identified by [[vukradin]] as the territory of a White Dragon. This silent directive implies her unfinished business is connected to that dragon, and that her spirit remains bound until its defeat—or until her purpose is fulfilled through another.
