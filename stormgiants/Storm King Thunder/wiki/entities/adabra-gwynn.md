# Adabra Gwynn

Apothecary, herbalist, and druid of the Emerald Enclave’s anti-interventionist faction. Operates a shop in Phandalin’s town square and holds land around the Umbrage Hill windmill. A verified *true believer* via Meril’s Staff’s Resonance power — her convictions are metaphysically authentic, not deceptive or coerced.

Adabra is a central figure in the ideological schism within the [[emerald-enclave]]. Known for her radical naturalism and ideological rigidity, she is widely regarded as a primary antagonist to interventionist figures such as [[lyra]] and [[soma]]. Her worldview is rooted in **naturalism as a cosmological framework**: she rejects moral categories like “evil,” “monster,” or “barbarian,” viewing all phenomena — including planar incursions and cults — as natural expressions of cosmic diversity. She believes interventionism — whether by the Order of the Gauntlet, divine rituals, or military action — disrupts ecological equilibrium and triggers cascading instability across the multiverse. Her solution: non-interference. “Allow the ecosystem to find equilibrium without help.”

## Philosophy

Adabra is a "true believer" in radical naturalism, asserting that all external interference—whether religious, planar, or military—causes cascading imbalances in the multiverse. She views heroic actions, including those of the party, as "temporal violations" and "planar distortions" that trigger the very blights they seek to prevent. Her belief in cosmic balance is real — but her inability to distinguish between *natural* chaos and *motivated* corruption (e.g., drow sabotage) reveals a fatal blind spot. She regards such corruption as merely another expression of nature’s diversity, a perspective that may stem from personal extremism or reflect a hidden undercurrent within the Enclave.

Her views on the Cult of Talos — as “the necessary element of distraction in the cosmic balance” — exemplify her radical theological inversion. She does not see herself as a fanatic, but as a guardian of universal equilibrium, and her convictions are metaphysically verified, making her a tragic figure: not evil, but profoundly isolated by her truth.

## Ideological Conflict

Adabra stands in direct opposition to the interventionist wing of the [[emerald-enclave]], particularly [[lyra]], whom she has actively worked to ostracize following the [[whispering-grove-incident]]. Lyra characterizes Adabra as "insane" and a dangerous fanatic whose ideological extremism threatens the Enclave’s cohesion. Their conflict epitomizes the broader tension between [[interventionism-vs-naturalism]] within the organization.

Her campaign against the party culminated in a formal Enclave council hearing, where she weaponized a five-gold debt against [[vukradin]] and cited Corbin’s death as evidence of interventionist failure. Her case was rejected by the council, and her personal attacks were deemed “absolutely disgraceful.” Despite her defeat, she departed fuming, with no indication she has abandoned her mission.

## Key Relationships

* **[[soma]]**: Her former protégé, whom she now considers a dangerous interventionist. Adabra has attempted to build a formal case against her within the Enclave, viewing her departure from naturalist principles as a personal and ideological betrayal. Soma now seeks to confront her over the paradox of her metaphysical truth and her failure to recognize motivated corruption.
* **[[vukradin]]**: A former associate whom she once financially supported, believing he shared her naturalist philosophy. Their relationship deteriorated when he joined the [[order-of-the-gauntlet]]. Adabra weaponized their past financial transaction — a five-gold debt — to undermine him during a formal Enclave council hearing. He repaid her six gold pieces to remove her moral leverage, but she remains emotionally and ideologically bound to the betrayal.
* **[[lyra]]**: Her primary ideological opponent. Adabra orchestrated efforts to have Lyra expelled from the Enclave after the Whispering Grove ritual failed, seeing it as proof of interventionism’s destructive consequences. Lyra, in turn, labels Adabra a dangerous fanatic.
* **[[valphine]]**: A missionary of the [[clergy-of-lathander]], who targeted Adabra for conversion, viewing her naturalist extremism as a heretical deviation from divine order. This interaction intensified Adabra’s distrust of organized religion.
* **Falcon the Hunter** and **Aldric Stone Path**: Druids she recruited to her cause, though Falcon later expressed regret over his alignment with her.

## Recent Activity

Following the [[whispering-grove-incident]], Adabra intensified her campaign against the party and the interventionist faction of the Enclave. Though she recently lost a formal hearing at the Enclave council, she remains a potent political threat. Her influence endures through her network of sympathizers, her control over apothecary resources, and her unwavering conviction. She is reportedly "far from finished," and her actions suggest she is preparing deeper, more covert measures to enforce her vision of natural order.

Her ideological rigidity, while internally consistent and metaphysically verified, is emotionally vengeful and politically toxic. She continues to rally druids and operate under the banner of [[anti-interventionism]], with her actions increasingly framed as attempts to correct what she perceives as “motivated blight” — a term she uses to describe deliberate corruption she refuses to acknowledge as such. Her legacy is one of uncompromising truth, profound isolation, and unresolved danger.
