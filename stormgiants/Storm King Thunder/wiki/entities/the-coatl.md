# The Coatl

A divine serpentine spirit bound to or associated with a tribal totem, The Coatl serves as a spiritual guardian and judge of its affiliated tribe. It manifests as a sentient, otherworldly entity that demands reverence and responds to moral transgressions with subtle, unsettling cues rather than overt aggression.

## Role & Nature
- **Role/Title:** Divine serpentine spirit, tribal guardian and judge
- **Faction:** Unknown — operates independently, driven by a personal code of spiritual purity
- **Form:** Serpentine, often emerging from or intrinsically linked to a tribal totem
- **Nature:** Expects deference and gratitude; visibly wounded by indifference. Displays displeasure through physical tics — bulging eyes, rapid tongue-flicking — rather than rage. Rewards righteous acts with potent boons, but holds grudges when its efforts go unacknowledged.

## Motivations & Core Goal
- **Primary Objective:** The destruction of Wormblod, whom it regards as a corruptor who has perverted the spiritual and moral order of the tribe
- **Driving Force:** Restoration of ancestral purity and divine balance within the tribe
- **Mechanism of Justice:** Offers boons in exchange for acts deemed righteous — particularly the elimination of Wormblod — treating such deeds as sacred obligations

## History with the Party
1. **First Contact:** The Coatl emerged from a tribal totem as Orsik passed by, directly addressing the party with a demand: destroy Wormblod in exchange for a divine boon.
2. **Boon Granted:** When Thistle asserted that Wormblod was already dead, the Coatl accepted the claim without verification and bestowed upon every party member a **Charm of Restoration**.
3. **Awkward Exit:** The party’s lukewarm reception — lack of gratitude or awe — visibly distressed the Coatl. It departed muttering, *“Usually, they appreciate me more,”* its tongue flicking in quiet offense.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The totem site where it first appeared
- **Active Plans:** Unknown — its immediate task (rewarding the claim of Wormblod’s death) is complete, but its deeper purpose remains unfulfilled
- **What the Party Knows:** It sought Wormblod’s death and rewarded the claim. It expects reverence and is sensitive to perceived disrespect.
- **What Remains Hidden:** 
  - Whether it can verify Wormblod’s true status
  - What consequences follow if Wormblod is still alive
  - The full extent of its divine authority, origins, or limitations
  - Whether it is bound to the totem, or if the totem is merely a vessel

## Relationships
- **Wormblod:** Views him as the ultimate abomination — a corruptor who has defiled the tribe’s sacred traditions. This grievance is central to its existence.
- **The 3HPP:** Transactional and strained. The Coatl fulfilled its end of the bargain but departed deeply dissatisfied with the party’s lack of reverence.
- **Thistle:** The de facto point of contact. Thistle’s claim was accepted as binding; should Wormblod be proven alive, Thistle may bear the spiritual weight of that falsehood.
