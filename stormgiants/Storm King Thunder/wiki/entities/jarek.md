# Jarek

Jarek is the groom and traveling companion of [[elara-seasong-meliame]], a devout merchant and follower of Lathander. He is not a combatant, nor a member of any known faction, but a quiet, observant figure whose primary narrative function is to provide a firsthand account of an unexplained internal phenomenon: the "hum."

## Personality and Temperament

Jarek is characterized as cautious, observant, and somewhat reserved. He demonstrates a pragmatic approach to safety, notably having warned Elara prior to the ambush about the need for travel protection — a warning that went unheeded. This reveals a temperament shaped by caution and a subtle need to justify inaction, often appearing self-vindicated when his warnings prove prescient. He embodies non-heroic realism: his value lies not in action, but in perception — and his silence may hold more weight than any shouted warning.

## Key Intelligence

During and after an orc ambush on the road, Jarek provided critical testimony that shaped understanding of the event:

- **Unusual Discipline:** He corroborated observations that the attackers exhibited a level of coordination, tactical discipline, and distinctive markings inconsistent with standard [[talosian-orc-raiding-party]] behavior.
- **Sensory Phenomenon:** He reported experiencing [[the-internal-hum]] — a sensation not heard externally, but felt internally within the head — which abruptly vanished immediately before the ambush began. This phenomenon is also referenced as [[internal-hum-sensory-phenomenon]].

## Relationships

- [[elara-seasong-meliame]]: Employer and traveling companion. Jarek’s loyalty and quiet vigilance are tied to his role in her service, though he maintains emotional distance.
- elara: Alternate reference to Elara; used informally in some accounts.

## Current Status

Jarek returned to Phandalin with Elara following the ambush. His current activities are unknown. He remains a figure of quiet significance — a witness whose observations challenge assumptions about orcish behavior and the nature of the internal hum, offering a grounded, human perspective amid supernatural mystery.
