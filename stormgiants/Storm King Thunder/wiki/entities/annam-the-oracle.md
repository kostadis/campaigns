# Annam (the Oracle)

A sentient, non-interventionist divine oracle bound to the Eye of the World, this entity answers questions truthfully but is strictly limited to six per day. It speaks on behalf of or in the name of Annam the All-Father and cites the collected sayings of Annam as its sole authoritative text. It cannot be deceived by trick questions, as demonstrated when it laughed off Thistle-Wendrod’s attempt to exploit its wording. Each question is concluded by the extinguishing of a torch, suggesting a ritualized or magical mechanism governing its function.

The Oracle revealed the location of Adimond Kroskas in the Maelstrom, confirmed the utility of King Hekaton’s conch for planar travel, advised the Zhentarim on strategic goals, and disclosed the death of Urgala’s wife — a revelation that may have been volunteered or interpreted from unspoken intent. Its nature remains unknown: whether it is a construct, a bound spirit, or a fragment of Annam’s consciousness. It does not act independently, but responds only to those who seek it.

It represents a critical counterpoint to [[divine-silence]]: Annam has not abandoned the world, but communicates through structured, limited channels.
