# Wererats of Mountain’s Toe

The Wererats of Mountain’s Toe are a semi-autonomous group of were-rats who have inhabited the lower tunnels of the Mountain’s Toe Gold Mine for at least two decades. Originally displaced by mining incursions, they adapted to the subterranean environment, developing a rudimentary social structure centered on territorial control, resource hoarding, and avoidance of human conflict.

For years, their presence was met with violence. Miners attempted to poison, trap, or drive them out — resulting in escalating casualties on both sides. The wererats, though not inherently aggressive, defended their territory fiercely, and their nocturnal raids on supplies and equipment made the mine increasingly unprofitable.

The arrival of Don-Jon Raskin and the intervention of Vukradin marked a turning point. Raskin, despite his ignorance of their nature, approached them with openness rather than fear. Vukradin, recognizing their sentience and capacity for negotiation, brokered a compromise: the wererats would retain control of the lower levels, including water sources and nesting chambers, while human miners operated the upper shafts under agreed-upon hours and safety protocols. A neutral corridor was established for communication, and a weekly tribute of food, tools, and salt was instituted.

This arrangement, formalized as a [[compromise-cohabitation]], is unprecedented in the region. The wererats have not been subdued — they have been acknowledged. They now operate with a degree of autonomy, even trading minor mineral fragments for goods. Their leader, a scarred elder known only as “Gristle,” communicates through a mute intermediary.

The wererats are not allies of the Consortium — they are tenants. They are unaware of the Kraken Society’s involvement, and they care little for the politics above. Their survival depends on the stability of the compromise. Their continued existence in the mine is a quiet rebuke to the assumption that non-human entities must be eradicated — and a testament to the power of mutual restraint.

Their presence in the mine may also serve a deeper, unknown purpose for the Consortium — perhaps as biological sentinels, or as living wards against planar incursions. Their true origins remain unexplored.
