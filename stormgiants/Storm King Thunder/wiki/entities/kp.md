# KP

KP is an unidentified supervisory figure whose existence straddles two seemingly contradictory domains: the academic theorist of interplanar economics and the unseen enforcer of a drow smuggling network. Known in scholarly circles as a gnomish sage whose formal analysis argues for a theoretically achievable multi-planar economic optimum, KP’s work remains controversial for its deliberate disregard of social disruption and human cost. Yet in the underworld of Aletra Satora, KP is feared as the apex of the [[layered-command-structure]], a black box of absolute authority referenced only in hushed tones: “use only if KP isn't watching.”

Aletra’s terror at the botched extraction — “The boss will not be happy with this” — reveals KP is not merely a superior but a gatekeeper of survival. Whether KP is a person, an AI construct, a magical entity, or a codename for a higher authority within House Xorlarrin remains deliberately obscured. This ambiguity is not accidental; it reinforces the theme of institutional control through obscurity, turning KP into a mythic figure whose presence enforces discipline, evaluates loyalty, and determines expendability.

KP’s theoretical framework on multi-planar economic efficiency appears to align — whether intentionally or incidentally — with the operational logic of the interdimensional smuggling network overseen by Aletra. Local merchants in the Alaestra/Narth area have been indirectly harmed by practices justified under KP-style economic reasoning, suggesting the theorist’s abstract models have been weaponized into real-world exploitation. The Lionshield Coster’s business model further echoes KP’s theories, hinting at broader institutional adoption beyond the drow underworld.

What remains hidden: KP’s true identity, physical form, current whereabouts, and whether they are aware of or care about critiques of their academic work. The party knows KP as a famous gnomish sage whose research is foundational — but not as the shadow that watches Aletra’s every move. The convergence of these roles implies KP may be using academic legitimacy as cover for a far more sinister operational authority — or that the smuggling network has co-opted and corrupted the theory into a tool of control.
