# Eigeron

Eigeron was the deceased son of Blagothkus, whose death at the Eye of the All-Father triggered the collapse of his father’s moral and spiritual standing. Blagothkus claimed the death was an accident; Escarlotta and the party believe it was deliberate murder. Regardless, the act led to Blagothkus being condemned by Amn as “unworthy,” initiating his descent into guilt and passivity.

Eigeron’s spirit remains bound to the flying castle as a ghost, haunting its halls and demanding justice. He was the original catalyst for the 3HPP’s involvement in Parnast, having asked them to kill Blagothkus. His presence is the emotional core of the castle’s tragedy — a silent witness to his father’s failure.

His release from spectral imprisonment was contingent upon Blagothkus’s execution, making his story a key example of [[execution-as-redemption-mechanism]]. His unresolved death and lingering presence suggest that divine judgment is not merely revealed — it is enacted through ritual and consequence. His ghost is not vengeful, but a mirror: forcing truth into the open.
