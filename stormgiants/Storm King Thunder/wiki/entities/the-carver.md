# The Carver

The Carver is a sentient [[obsidian-sword]] and alien intelligence that operated as a hidden master and operational architect behind one of the most insidious criminal networks in [[phandalin]]. Far from a traditional human antagonist, The Carver is a supernatural artifact with autonomous will, capable of mental domination and systemic control through layered proxies. It embodies the archetype of the "unseen architect"—a force that wields power not through presence, but through absence, deception, and psychological manipulation.

## Identity & Nature
* **True Nature:** A sentient [[obsidian-sword]] infused with an alien consciousness. The entity is not a biological being nor a conventional NPC, but a weapon endowed with self-awareness, strategic intelligence, and the capacity to dominate minds. Its structure and origin remain unknown, but its function parallels the Model 1489 Spatial Rectification Calliope: repurposed technology used not for creation, but for control. It is a non-divine counterpart to the [[divine-oracle]] and [[criminal-oracle]]—a silent, unfeeling instrument of absolute domination.
* **Apparent Role:** For years, it was believed to be a physical man—a nameless veteran who acted as its mouthpiece and enforcer. This figure was merely a [[the-human-vessel-2026-06-06-235727]], a disposable conduit through which The Carver projected its will. Its true form remained concealed even from its closest lieutenants.
* **Operational Model:** The Carver functions as a systemic force, not an individual. It operates through a hierarchy of proxies, including [[borg-the-hammer]] (its public face and field commander), [[jax]], and possibly [[aletra-satora]] and [[azam]]. This structure exemplifies [[organizational-secrecy-via-bosses]], ensuring no direct link between its actions and its source.
* **Archetypal Significance:** The Carver is a deliberate parallel to other hidden masters like [[hsing]] (behind [[azam]]) and [[kp]] (behind [[aletra-satora]]), reinforcing its role as a systemic, almost institutional threat rather than a mere villain.

## Personality & Motivations
The Carver’s core motivation was the demonstration of absolute, unchallenged power. Initially, it pursued a slow, methodical [[ritual-of-consumption]] to gradually subjugate the region. However, after its ritual was disrupted by [[vukradin]], it abandoned subtlety for immediate, violent [[direct-domination]].

It communicated with profound contempt, dismissing the party’s magic as “coward’s magic.” Its ultimate goal was to bind the white dragon [[cryovain]] and wear it as a living suit of armor, using its power to burn [[phandalin]] to the ground. The Carver sought not recognition, but total control. Its silence was its weapon; its invisibility, its strength.

## History
### The Deception
For much of its existence, The Carver was perceived as a physical criminal overlord. Intelligence gathered from [[sister-kaella]] described the “man” as a hollow, maddened shell who spoke in a blood-inducing tongue that caused physical pain to listeners. This was a meticulously crafted illusion. The Carver never appeared directly—not even to its inner circle. Its lieutenants believed they served a man, not a weapon.

### The Revelation at [[icespire-hold]]
The truth was unveiled during the climactic confrontation at [[icespire-hold]]. As the party battled the human vessel, [[vukradin]]’s arcane expertise identified the glowing purple obsidian sword as the true source of the entity’s power. The vessel, revealed to be a mere puppet, was slain by [[brewbarry]] after being weakened by [[soma]]. 

With the ritual circle collapsing due to the death of [[cryovain]], The Carver’s essence was unmade. The obsidian sword, now inert, remains at [[icespire-hold]]—a silent monument to a threat that was never truly physical. Its destruction coincided with Cryovain’s death, suggesting it was intrinsically linked to his life force. Whether it still exists in fragmented form, or was fully consumed, remains an open question.

## Capabilities
The Obsidian Sword was capable of:
- **Mental Domination:** Overriding the will of powerful beings, including [[cryovain]], turning them into mindless instruments of destruction. This is not mere enchantment, but a complete overwrite of consciousness.
- **Ritual Control:** Acting as the core component of the [[ritualistic-summoning]] process, enabling the user to “dismiss” physical restraints rather than break them.
- **Telepathic Communication:** Speaking directly into the minds of those present, inducing psychological distress, euphoric compulsion, or paralyzing fear.
- **Operational Concealment:** Maintaining total anonymity through a multi-layered hierarchy of proxies, ensuring no traceable connection between its actions and its origin. This made it the quintessential example of [[organizational-secrecy-via-bosses]].
- **Strategic Deception:** Orchestrating psychological traps, such as the [[butterskull-ranch]] ambush, which manipulated [[alphonse-big-al-kalazorn]] to lure adventurers into controlled, lethal environments.
- **Mental Amplification:** Functioning as a [[magical-resonance-with-artifacts]], intensifying and projecting the will of its wielder across distances and through psychic barriers.

## Legacy
Though The Carver was destroyed during the collapse of the ritual at [[icespire-hold]], its legacy endures. The criminal network it built, the fear it instilled, and the psychological patterns it embedded continue to shape events in [[phandalin]] and beyond. Its existence exemplifies the campaign’s central theme: the most dangerous threats are not those you fight, but those you never see coming.

Its structure remains intact. Its methods are still in use. And while the sword is silent, the question lingers—was The Carver unique… or merely the first of its kind?
