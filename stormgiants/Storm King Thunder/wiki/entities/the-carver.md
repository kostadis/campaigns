# The Carver

[[the-carver]] is an antagonist and criminal leader who once commanded a significant spy and criminal network in [[phandalin]]. While initially perceived as a physical man and a "big boss" of regional banditry, it was eventually revealed to be a sentient [[obsidian-sword]] and an alien intelligence. The Carver operates as a hidden master within a layered criminal hierarchy, with [[borg-the-hammer]] serving as his field commander and public face. His identity remains entirely undisclosed, and his broader objectives are unknown — making him a persistent narrative mystery and an active, latent threat.

## Identity & Nature
* **True Nature:** A sentient [[obsidian-sword]] and alien intelligence, serving as both weapon and consciousness. The entity is not a human, nor a traditional NPC, but a supernatural artifact with autonomous will and the capacity for mental domination.
* **Apparent Role:** A criminal overlord operating through [[human-vessels]] to interact with the physical world and project its will. The most prominent vessel was a nameless veteran who acted as its mouthpiece and enforcer. The Carver’s influence extends through proxies like [[borg-the-hammer]], [[jax]], and possibly [[aletra-satora]] and [[azam]], reinforcing a [[layered-command-structure]] and [[operational-secrecy-via-obscurer-authority]].
* **Methodology:** Utilizes a strategic, highly reactive intelligence. Its organization specializes in deception, psychological traps (e.g., the [[butterskull-ranch]] ambush via manipulation of alphonse-big-al-kalazorn), and targeted ambushes — luring adventurers into controlled environments rather than simple raiding. Its approach is surgical: not random violence, but targeted erasure.
* **Current Status:** **Destroyed.** The entity's essence dissipated completely upon the death of [[cryovain]] during the collapse of the ritual circle at [[icespire-hold]]. However, its operational legacy and the structure it built remain intact, suggesting potential for resurgence or replication.

## Personality & Motivations
The Carver’s primary goal was a demonstration of absolute power. While it initially pursued a methodical [[ritual-of-consumption]] to slowly influence the region, it abandoned this long-term strategy for immediate, violent [[direct-domination]] after its ritual was interrupted by [[vukradin]]. 

The entity communicated with profound contempt, viewing the magical interventions of the party as "coward's magic." Its ultimate objective was to bind [[cryovain]] the white dragon to use as a weapon to burn [[phandalin]] to the ground, intending to "wear" the creature as a suit of armor. 

Its silence is its strength. Its absence is its weapon. It does not seek recognition — only control. Its operational model mirrors that of [[hsing]] (behind [[azam]]) and [[kp]] (behind [[aletra-satora]]), positioning it as a systemic force rather than a mere individual.

## History
### The Deception
For much of its existence, the party believed the Carver to be a physical man operating through intermediaries like [[jax]] and high-level enforcers such as [[borg-the-hammer]]. Intelligence from [[sister-kaella]] suggested the individual was a "hollow, maddened shell" who possessed a telepathic link to his people and spoke in a blood-inducing tongue that caused physical pain to listeners. This was a carefully constructed illusion — the Carver never appeared directly, even to its own lieutenants.

### The Revelation at [[icespire-hold]]
The truth was revealed during the confrontation at [[icespire-hold]]. The party discovered that the "man" was merely an unnamed veteran serving as a [[human-vessel]] for the sentient weapon. During the battle, [[vukradin]]'s arcane expertise identified the glowing purple obsidian sword as the true entity.

After the vessel was killed by [[brewbarry]] (following weakening strikes from [[soma]]), the Carver's essence was unmade when the ritual circle broke due to the death of the dragon.

## Capabilities
The Obsidian Sword was capable of:
- **Mental Domination:** Overriding the consciousness of powerful creatures, such as [[cryovain]], turning them into mindless tools.
- **Ritual Control:** Acting as the primary mechanism for the [[ritualistic-summoning]] process, allowing the user to "dismiss" rather than break physical restraints.
- **Telepathic Communication:** The sword demonstrated the ability to speak directly into the minds of those present during the ritual, often inducing psychological distress or euphoric compulsion in listeners.
- **Operational Concealment:** Maintaining total anonymity through layered proxies, ensuring no direct link between its actions and its source. This made it a quintessential example of [[operational-secrecy-via-obscurer-authority]].

## Legacy
Though destroyed, The Carver’s influence lingers. The criminal network it built, the psychological patterns it instilled, and the fear it cultivated continue to shape events in [[phandalin]] and beyond. Its existence exemplifies the campaign’s theme: the most dangerous threats are not those you fight, but those you never see coming.
