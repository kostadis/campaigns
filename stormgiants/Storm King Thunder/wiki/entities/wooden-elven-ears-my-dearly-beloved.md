# Wooden Elven Ears ("my dearly beloved")

A pair of carved wooden elven ears on a simple chain, inscribed with the phrase “my dearly beloved.” Found in [[falcon-the-hunter]]’s private drawer alongside the brass ring and labyrinth square, these ears are his most intimate and unexplained possession.

They are not functional, not magical, and not of obvious origin. Their craftsmanship suggests personal, not commercial, creation — likely a gift from someone he loved. Falcon never speaks of them, and no one knows who wore them. Vukradin refers to “his girlfriend” who died in the lodge’s destruction — a possible connection, but never confirmed.

The ears embody [[narrative-economy]] and [[ambiguous-sentience]]: their emotional weight is immense, yet their story is deliberately withheld. They are not a plot device — they are a wound. Their presence in the drawer, alongside the ring and labyrinth square, suggests they are part of a private ritual of memory, not a relic of conquest. Their silence speaks louder than any confession.
