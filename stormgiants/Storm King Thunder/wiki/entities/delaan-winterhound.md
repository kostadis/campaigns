# Delaan Winterhound

Delaan Winterhound was a senior, possibly highest-ranking, member of the [[emerald-enclave]] during the [[war-of-the-dragons]]. According to historical accounts provided by [[lyra]], he was regarded as one of the wisest members of the Enclave.

## Historical Significance

Delaan is the pivotal figure responsible for the current ideological fault line within the [[emerald-enclave]]. Originally, he adhered to a strict non-interventionist philosophy, believing the Enclave’s role was solely to preserve natural order rather than engage in mortal or political conflicts. This stance aligned him with the [[emerald-enclave]] and the broader principles of [[emerald-enclave]].

However, during the existential threat of [[tiamat]]'s attempted summoning into the Prime Material Plane, he was persuadable — a quality that proved historically consequential. Moved by the moral appeal and urgency presented by the hero [[malfire]], Delaan reversed his position and committed the Emerald Enclave’s forces to join the coalition against the dragon goddess.

This decision, though widely regarded by contemporaries like Lyra as “the wisest” of its era, fractured the Enclave along ideological lines, birthing the enduring divide between [[interventionism]] and [[emerald-enclave]]. His shift is remembered as the foundational moment of the [[ideological-split-in-the-emerald-enclave]].

## Personality

Historical records suggest Delaan was a principled, cautious, and deeply philosophical leader. While firmly rooted in naturalist doctrine, he was not dogmatic. His willingness to reconsider core tenets in the face of overwhelming existential threat demonstrated pragmatic wisdom — a contrast to figures like [[adabra-gwynn]], whose rigid adherence to doctrine prevented similar adaptation.

He is remembered by interventionists as a moral exemplar who chose responsibility over isolation, and by non-interventionists as a tragic betrayal of the Enclave’s sacred covenant with nature.

## Current Status

Unknown. Delaan vanished from all historical records after the War of the Dragons. His fate remains unrecorded. No direct interaction with the party has occurred. His presence is felt only through historical echo, Lyra’s reverence, and the enduring ideological schism he precipitated.

His legacy endures as a foundational archetype: a leader whose non-dogmatic non-interventionism could yield to existential threat — a benchmark against which later Enclave leaders are measured.
