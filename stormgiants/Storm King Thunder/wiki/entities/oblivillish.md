# Oblivillish

A noble figure of the Seelie Court, Oblivillish was exiled after Blasius falsely accused him of killing the sacred stag during a drunken confession. Though Blasius later recanted and cleared his name, Oblivillish’s fate remains ambiguous.

He is described as having attacked Blasius upon hearing the accusation — suggesting either righteous fury or a deeper, unspoken grievance. Blasius privately believes Oblivillish is seeking revenge for an unspecified past indiscretion — a suspicion that may be paranoia, or a buried truth.

His exoneration was orchestrated by Blasius not out of moral awakening, but to escape his own administrative prison. Oblivillish’s silence since his return suggests he has not forgiven, and may be waiting. His presence in the court remains unconfirmed, and his potential return as an avenger is a lingering threat.

Oblivillish embodies the cost of Blasius’s guilt — a quiet, unresolved wound in the fabric of Seelie Court justice.
