# Soma

[[soma]] is a Tortle druid of Mieliki and a member of the adventuring party, serving as the party's paid guide — though her relationship with her primary employer, [[vukradin]], is strained by financial debt and moral friction.

## Affiliations and Background
* **[[emerald-enclave]]**: Soma is a member of the [[emerald-enclave]], specifically aligned with the [[naturalist-faction]] and its anti-interventionist doctrines. She holds a quiet contempt for magical constructs and "unnatural" creations. Her worldview is rooted in **naturalism-as-moral-framework**, which rejects moral binaries and insists that corruption must be passive, emergent, and unguided.
* **Protégé and Trainee**: She was the primary protégée and druid-in-training of [[adabra-gwynn]], who viewed Soma as her ideological heir. Recruited under the belief that they shared full alignment, Soma internalized Adabra’s teachings completely — and allowed Adabra to believe they were in full agreement. This **tacit complicity** enabled Adabra’s radicalization.
* **The Chosen**: She is wrestling with the existential weight of being "chosen" by [[meril]], a sentiment reinforced by the centaur [[xanth]].
* **Bond-Initiator**: Soma initiated the bond with boney, a skeletal horse, by using *Speak with Animals* to communicate with his reanimated form in the Dragonbarrow. Mistaking her for Lady Alagondar’s reincarnation, Boney transferred his loyalty to her and, by extension, the party. She remains instrumental in his integration into the group, but is deeply conflicted about his moral standing — her haunting question, “Why did you stand by when Corbin died?”, reveals her struggle to reconcile non-human sentience with human ethics. She does not condemn Boney, but cannot fully absolve him. This dynamic anchors her in the tension between compassion and judgment, and reflects the broader theme of the **moral-ambiguity-of-inaction-undead**.

## Personality and Philosophy
Soma is characterized by a sardonic, blunt, and pragmatic personality. She frequently uses mockery to cut through philosophical or bureaucratic tangles. While she presents an unsentimental exterior regarding gold and violence, she is deeply invested in protecting life and acting with purpose.

She is currently undergoing a profound ideological crisis triggered by the **motivated blight paradox**: Adabra’s naturalist doctrine insists that corruption must be passive and unguided — yet Soma has witnessed deliberate, puppet-controlled corruption (e.g., Narcellia Varammyr’s influence on Bad Fruul). When she used [[merils-staff]] (also referenced as [[meril's-staff]]) to verify Adabra as a *true believer*, it confirmed the sincerity of Adabra’s worldview — not its completeness. This created an unresolvable tension: if Adabra’s truth is real, how can the party’s necessary interventions be justified? And if the party’s actions are morally required, how can Adabra’s philosophy remain coherent?

Soma now views Adabra’s campaign as adversarial — “bringing her own war.” She was conspicuously omitted from Adabra’s advocacy to Harbin Wester and later became the target of Adabra’s political case at the Enclave council, accused of teaching others to interfere rather than observe. Her journey is no longer one of learning — it is one of unlearning.

She is not a traitor — she is a seeker. Her crisis deepened after witnessing the party’s actions and Lyra’s failure, particularly the deliberate destruction of a treant. She now confronts the central paradox: *How can a philosophy that claims to honor all life justify the motivated blight? How can silence be ethical when it enables violence?*

She has resolved to confront Adabra directly — not to defeat her, but to understand: “How can you accept a *motivated* blight?” She seeks not to overturn Adabra’s truth, but to reconcile it with the reality she has witnessed.

Soma is the empathetic bridge: she gives voice to the voiceless — whether it be the reanimated Boney, the silent forests, or the hidden truths she carries — but is haunted by the limits of her understanding. She represents the tension between compassion and judgment — a druid who seeks harmony, yet must confront the moral gray zones of a world where even the dead remember.

## Combat and Tactics
Soma is a tactically sharp combatant. She utilizes [[wild-shape]] (specifically elk, bear, and spider) not merely for damage, but for crowd control, scouting, and strategic repositioning. She is known to correct allies mid-combat to optimize tactical outcomes.

### Party Dynamics
There is noted tactical and ideological friction between Soma and [[valphine]], particularly regarding combat efficacy and spell usage.

## Key Assets and Mysteries
### Assets
* **[[merils-staff]]** (also known as [[meril's-staff]]): A reactive druidic staff that has recently grown a **fourth bud** following the encounter at the Circle of Thunder. It played a pivotal role in Soma’s verification of Adabra’s sincerity and remains the physical anchor of her existential crisis.
* **boney**: A skeletal horse that serves as her tactical companion and combat asset — and the living embodiment of her moral ambiguity. His loyalty to her is absolute, but his past inaction haunts her.

### Withheld Information
Soma has deliberately withheld knowledge regarding:
* A white dragon rumor.
* The nature of an unidentified item retrieved from an ogre's corpse.

She carries these secrets not out of deceit, but as part of her internal reckoning — each withheld truth a mirror to the unspoken conflict within her.

> **Soma is the living question at the heart of Adabra’s tragedy.**
