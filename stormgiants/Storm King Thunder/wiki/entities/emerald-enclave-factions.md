# Emerald Enclave Factions

The Emerald Enclave is internally divided into two ideological wings: the interventionist faction and the anti-interventionist faction.

The **interventionist faction**, represented by figures like Lyra, believes in actively defending natural spaces from external threats — cults, planar incursions, and monstrous entities. They view their actions as necessary preservation, even if they involve violence or disruption.

The **anti-interventionist faction**, led by Adabra Gwynn, holds that all intervention — even defensive — triggers cascading multiversal imbalances. They advocate for non-action, withdrawal, and the acceptance of all entities as natural expressions of cosmic diversity. They reject moral categories like “evil” and “monster,” and view cleansing rituals as acts of violence against nature.

This schism is not merely doctrinal — it is political. Adabra strategically excluded Reidoth, the nominal head of the local chapter, from the council hearing to ensure her majority. Her campaign against Lyra and Soma reveals a faction willing to use personal grievances, public trials, and ideological purges to enforce conformity.

The party’s actions have intensified this divide. The interventionist wing sees them as heroes; the anti-interventionist wing sees them as agents of cosmic decay. The Enclave’s future hangs on whether reconciliation is possible — or whether the schism will become a civil war.

This division mirrors the broader tension in the campaign: between action and inaction, between protection and non-interference, between justice and balance.
