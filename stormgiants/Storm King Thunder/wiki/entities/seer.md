# SEER

The Secret Eyes of the Earth and Realm — a covert intelligence network operating in the North, embedded within and likely subordinate to the Lord’s Alliance. It functions as a secular analog to the [[divine-oracle]]: providing accurate, actionable intelligence — such as the location of Joon and knowledge of Thule — but is constrained by critical blind spots. Notably, it has no information on Joon’s organization, suggesting either extreme secrecy on the part of its target or internal fragmentation.

Unlike the divine oracle, which is bound by ritual and divine law, the SEER’s limitations are human: incomplete data, operational gaps, and possibly internal rivalries. It uses merchant caravans, disguised agents like Azam, and embedded informants to gather intelligence. It recruits based on demonstrated competence and integrity, as seen in its interest in Unla Kee, operating through [[recruitment-through-integrity]] and masking its operations under [[cover-identity-as-merchant]].

The SEER is not omniscient — its power lies in precision, not breadth. Its existence challenges assumptions about institutional infallibility and introduces narrative vulnerability: even the most powerful networks can be blind to the most dangerous threats. It is a system of constrained knowledge without divine sanction — a cold, calculating mirror to the Oracle of the Eye of the World.

The SEER offered each party member exactly what they desired — Vardis’s reconciliation with Corellon, Orsik’s survival, Unla Kee’s cosmic validation, Thistle’s rescue of Laela — in exchange for compliance. All four refused. This unanimous rejection is unprecedented and defines the party’s moral identity. The SEER, once a source of aid, is now a symbol of everything they refuse to become: a system that trades freedom for safety.

The SEER still holds private information on each member — particularly Unla Kee — and has not abandoned hope of using them after Iymrith’s fall. Their final note suggests they view the party not as enemies, but as tools in waiting.

The SEER is the dark mirror of the [[divine-oracle]]: where the Oracle speaks truth under constraint, the SEER enforces silence under threat. Both are systems of control — but only the party chose to walk away. This act of collective rejection places the party at the center of a broader [[collective-rejection-of-manipulation]] and reshapes the [[narrative-economy]] of power in the North.
