# Brin Bundlewine

Brin Bundlewine is a pale-skinned villager and laborer from [[phandalin]] who became the central figure in the establishment of the [[temple-of-lathanders-searing-pain-of-justice]]. Once a simple construction worker, Brin’s encounter with [[valphine]] — a drow priestess of Lathander — ignited a radical spiritual transformation. He interprets Lathander not as a god of gentle dawn, but as a deity whose divinity is revealed through purifying pain, particularly the searing heat of the sun.

His devotion is not the product of manipulation, but of ecstatic self-surrender. He sees Valphine not as an outsider, but as a vessel of divine truth — inverting Faerûn’s racial and theological hierarchies. His quiet, unyielding conviction makes him more unsettling than any villain: he is not a fanatic, but a true believer who has redefined holiness through suffering.

## Identity & Personality
Brin is an aspiring chapel builder characterized by an intense, almost ecstatic religious zeal. He bears heavy sunburns and scars — physical evidence of his devotion to the "purifying pain of the sun." He demonstrates a "beatific radiance" and a gleeful, uncritical acceptance of extreme religious phenomena, including ritual blinding and the public display of severed heads. His skin, marked by deliberate burns and self-inflicted wounds, is regarded by him as [[holy-disfigurement]] — sacred badges of divine favor.

## Religious Devotion
Brin is a central figure in the practical application of [[lathanderian-practice-valphine]]. His worship is grounded in the [[theology-of-searing-pain]] that suffering is the highest form of devotion. Key practices include:
- **Ritual self-flagellation:** Used as a method of spiritual discipline and purification.
- **Ritual burning:** Deliberate exposure to heat and flame to emulate the sun’s purifying wrath.
- **Theology of Searing Pain:** A belief that physical suffering, especially solar-induced pain, is the truest path to divine communion.
- **Labor as Devotion:** He offers his labor without pay, accepting only materials for construction — viewing physical toil as a sacrament.

## Current Activities
Brin is currently managing the renovation of [[tresendar-manor]] into the [[temple-of-lathanders-searing-pain-of-justice]], transforming it from a ruin into a monument not to doctrine, but to personal revelation. He has entered into a low-cost labor agreement, requesting only 25 gp for materials. He frequently conducts public religious parades through Phandalin alongside [[giles-slipper-shine]], displaying a severed Kraken Society cultist’s head as a sacred relic. He celebrates events like ritual blinding as miraculous affirmations of faith.

## Relationships
- **[[valphine]]:** Views her as a patron and a true believer in her ministry; works as a contractor for her religious cause. He sees her not as an outsider, but as a divine vessel — a radical inversion of Faerûn’s norms.
- **[[giles-slipper-shine]]:** A close associate and fellow construction worker/religious enthusiast; they lead public rituals and fundraising activities together.

Brin’s transformation from laborer to zealot embodies the core tenet of his faith: that holiness is forged not in comfort, but in fire.
