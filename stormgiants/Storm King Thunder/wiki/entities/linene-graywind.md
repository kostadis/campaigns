# Linene Graywind

Linene Graywind is the 35-year-old proprietor of the [[lionshield-coster]] outpost in [[phandalin]], a merchant operating at the intersection of legitimate commerce and institutional interplanar trade. She serves as a mid-level agent for an unnamed leadership structure referred to as her “bosses,” who have a specialized interest in goods tied to planar phenomena. Linene embodies a new archetype: *calm commercial opacity* — not a criminal, not a spy, but a normalized node in a quiet, elite commercial network that treats cosmic phenomena as legitimate trade goods.

## Profile
- **Role:** Outpost Master / Merchant / Interplanar Commercial Agent
- **Affiliation:** [[lionshield-coster]]
- **Location:** [[phandalin]]
- **Archetype:** Calm Commercial Opacity

## Key Observations

### Commercial Practices
- **Premium Pricing for Planar Goods:** She pays significantly above market value — often double the price offered by local buyers like Barthen — for goods with planar associations, such as orc gear and pelts. This reflects [[interplanar-commercial-interests]], indicating a structured, non-criminal market for planar commodities.
- **Justification:** She attributes these premiums to her “bosses’” demand for items resonant with interplanar energy, reinforcing the institutional nature of her operations.
- **Supply Chain Disruptions:** The War of the Giants has severed her supply chains, making it impossible to reliably obtain scrolls or ship goods. She attributes this to “tariffs” — likely bureaucratic or planar barriers — directly linking her trade to [[supply-chain-disruption-by-planar-conflict]] triggered by the collapse of the Ordning.

### Social and Diplomatic Behavior
- **Social Attentiveness as a Tool:** Linene engages warmly with customers, remembers personal details, and publicly praises performers at the [[stonehill-inn]] — notably Valphine. This exemplifies [[social-attentiveness-as-commercial-tool]], using emotional engagement to build trust and extract intelligence rather than relying on coercion or secrecy.
- **Deflection Strategy:** She adeptly redirects direct offers of service (e.g., from [[vukradin]]) toward local rumors, particularly about magical disruptions in Neverwinter Wood. This transforms social interaction into a form of [[rumor-as-intelligence-gathering]].
- **Unspoken Awareness:** Her public praise of Valphine suggests a quiet, unspoken recognition of Valphine’s possible connection to interdimensional trade, hinting at deeper network awareness without overt confirmation.

### Institutional Context
- **Organizational Secrecy:** Linene operates under the direction of unnamed “bosses,” whose priorities and structure remain deliberately obscured — a hallmark of [[organizational-secrecy-via-bosses]].
- **Broader Infrastructure:** Valphine suspects Linene’s network may be connected to a defunct interplanar shipping device once used in Neverwinter, implying a broader, institutional infrastructure for planar logistics. Linene’s awareness of this device remains unconfirmed, but her behavior implies she operates within a system far larger than her individual role.
- **Normalization of Planar Commerce:** Her operations are not hidden or illegal — they are deliberately unspoken, operating in plain sight under the guise of ordinary trade. This reflects [[planar-phenomena-as-commodity]] and [[interplanar-commercial-interests]].

## Relationships

- **[[lionshield-coster]] Leadership:** Operates under the direction of unnamed “bosses” who dictate procurement priorities regarding planar goods.
- **[[vukradin]]:** A recurring contact who has attempted to provide protection and procurement services; Linene consistently deflects these offers, maintaining transactional distance.
- **[[valphine]]:** A recipient of Linene’s public praise during a performance at the [[stonehill-inn]]. Their interaction suggests mutual, unspoken recognition of interdimensional connections.
- **Barthen:** A local buyer whose lower pricing contrasts with Linene’s premium model, highlighting the institutional divergence in her trade practices.

## Summary

Linene Graywind represents the institutionalization of interplanar commerce: not hidden, not illegal, but deliberately unspoken. Her calm, controlled demeanor contrasts with the paranoia of Aletra Satora or the anxiety of Azam, making her a quiet but pivotal figure in a growing network that treats cosmic phenomena as legitimate, if unacknowledged, trade goods.
