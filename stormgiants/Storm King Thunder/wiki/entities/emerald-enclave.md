# The Emerald Enclave

The [[emerald-enclave]] is a druidic organization dedicated to preserving natural balance and protecting wild places. Traditionally characterized by its commitment to naturalism and neutrality, this stance was famously broken during the [[war-of-the-dragons]] due to the influence of [[malfire]], whose actions ignited lasting ideological fractures within the group.

## Internal Factions

The Enclave is currently divided into two opposing ideological wings, each claiming to uphold the true spirit of druidic philosophy:

- **Interventionist Faction**: Believes in active measures — including cleansing rituals, magical restoration, and direct confrontation — to heal ecological damage and repel planar threats. This faction views [[malfire]] as a hero who broke the Enclave’s paralyzing neutrality to confront existential dangers. Led by [[lyra]], they argue that preserving balance sometimes requires decisive action, even if it disrupts the status quo.

- **Anti-interventionist Faction**: Advocates for strict non-interference, believing all external action disrupts natural equilibrium. Their core tenet is “allowing the ecosystem to find equilibrium without help.” Led by [[adabra-gwynn]], this faction rejects moral categories entirely, redefining demons, cults, and even [[motivated-blight]] as natural phenomena. Her radical views — which deny the legitimacy of moral judgment in ecological matters — have been deemed extreme even by many within her own wing.

## Political Conflict and Consequences

The factional divide has escalated into open political conflict. Adabra Gwynn’s ideology culminated in a formal council hearing, where she weaponized personal debt and accused the party of ecological destruction. The council ruled against her, calling her personal attacks “absolutely disgraceful.” Despite her marginalization, her ideas persist and continue to influence fringe elements within the Enclave.

This schism mirrors the broader cosmic tension between [[divine-silence]] and [[divine-oracle]]: when truth is constrained, who gets to speak? The Enclave’s inability to reconcile these views has not only fractured its unity but also contributed to the destabilization of regional power structures, including the [[war-of-the-giants]] and the [[shattering-of-the-ordning]].

The Enclave is now a battleground not just for land, but for the soul of druidic philosophy — caught between the imperative to act and the sacred duty to let nature be.
