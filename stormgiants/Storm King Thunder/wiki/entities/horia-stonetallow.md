# Horia Stonetallow

Horia Stonetallow is a dwarven mine representative and clan liaison for the [[stonetallow-clan]]. She leads a five-dwarf mining crew and manages operations at the mountain-toe-gold-mine.

## Personality and Philosophy
Horia is a shrewd, principled negotiator who places immense value on [[contractual-integrity]]. She is intensely proud of her clan's heritage, specifically their mastery of engineering and archways. This pride was demonstrated when she corrected [[vukradin]] regarding the etymology of her clan name, explaining it derives from "stone to allow."

While she maintains a rigid adherence to formal documents, she is capable of [[pragmatic-workarounds]] when necessary. She holds a deep disdain for those who dismiss legal obligations, specifically noting a conflict with [[valphine]], whose anti-contract stance led Horia to question her religious fitness as a cleric of Lathander.

## History and Operations
Horia's crew holds a prior legal claim to the mountain-toe-gold-mine via a contract with the deceased [[don-jon]]. She revealed that [[don-jon]]'s attempt to replace skilled dwarven labor with "cheap" wererat labor led to a fatal confrontation. 

Currently, she is managing a compromise arrangement where the dwarves handle mining while the wererats, represented by [[zeleen-varnaster]], handle labor and security. She estimates the mine's earnings at approximately 100 gp per week.

## Relationships

### Professional Colleagues
* **[[dwolin-the-grout]]**: A colleague and mine foreman. Horia serves as a character witness for Dwolin, providing context for his eccentricities. While acknowledging his "little rituals" and "gimmicks," she treats him with weary affection and vouches for his unparalleled skill in locating gold veins, implying a level of professional respect that transcends his odd methods.
* **[[zeleen-varnaster]]**: A transactional and cautious counterpart in the mine's protection deal.

### Negotiating Partners & Associates
* **[[vukradin]]**: Horia engages him in financial discussions but views him as too rigid for informal negotiations, leading her to hide certain "workarounds" from him.
* **[[brewbarry]]**: Horia entered into a secret, informal "Plan B" agreement with him to fund a fake gem to satisfy [[dwolin-the-grout]]'s superstitions, bypassing formal constraints.
* **[[valphine]]**: Viewed as a disruptive influence due to her dismissal of contracts.
