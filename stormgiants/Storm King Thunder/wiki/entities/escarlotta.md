# Escarlotta

Escarlotta is the spectral wife of Blagothkus, bound to the control console of the flying castle at Parnast. Though deceased, she retains full consciousness and control over the castle’s systems, using a mirror periscope and interface to manipulate Blagothkus and direct the 3HPP.

She is the moral center of the castle’s tragedy: she orchestrated Blagothkus’s downfall by exposing his lies, confronting him with his guilt over Eigeron’s death, and ultimately commanding his execution as the necessary condition to free her son’s spirit. Her control over the castle exemplifies [[spectral-control-via-technology]] — a fusion of ghostly will and architectural sentience.

Escarlotta’s relationship with Blagothkus cycled from bitter conflict to fragile reconciliation, culminating in her final act: ordering his death not out of vengeance, but as a ritual of spiritual justice. She is neither villain nor savior, but an embodiment of divine judgment made manifest through architecture. After Blagothkus’s death, she assumed full control of the castle, leaving its future and her intentions unknown.
