# Harshnag

A legendary giant ally whose ritualized practice of asking questions to a silent divine presence — known as the “oracle worthiness test” — is a well-known cultural joke among cloud giants. This practice serves as a coping mechanism: a way for giants to laugh at the silence of the gods, contrasting sharply with the performative divine interaction of the [[divine-oracle]].

Harshnag’s joke underscores a deeper thematic contrast in the narrative: where the Oracle is a system of constrained truth, the divine-worthiness-test is a silent verdict — and Blagothkus, in his confrontation with the divine, failed it. Blagothkus referenced this joke during his first encounter with the party, revealing that the Oracle’s claim of being “unopened for a thousand years” is not reverence, but cultural satire. In Blagothkus’s case, the laughter turned to ash.

## Fate

Harshnag is confirmed deceased. He sacrificed himself during the confrontation at the [[eye-of-the-all-father]], deliberately collapsing the temple to allow the party to escape while he fought [[iymrith]]. His death cemented his legacy not only as a warrior but as a symbol of quiet, unspoken divine judgment — a living embodiment of the oracle-worthiness test he once mocked.
