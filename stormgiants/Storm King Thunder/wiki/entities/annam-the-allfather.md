# Annam the Allfather

Chief deity and divine patriarch of the giant pantheon. Annam has not appeared directly in recorded history and does not answer prayers. His prolonged silence is cited by the Imperator as the primary spiritual catalyst for the collapse of the Ordning.

## Identity
- Role: Supreme deity of the giant races
- Status: Absent, silent, whereabouts unknown
- Worship: No known temples, rituals, or active worshipers; existence is mythologized, not practiced

## Divine Offspring
Annam’s children — the other giant deities — are locked in perpetual interplanar warfare. Their conflict suggests a collapse of divine order, not mere mortal rebellion. No record exists of reconciliation, mediation, or divine intervention from Annam.

## Historical Impact
King Hekaton’s despair over Annam’s silence directly contributed to his fatalistic acceptance of the Ordning’s end. The Imperator explicitly links Annam’s withdrawal to the broader decline of giant civilization, framing it as a spiritual abandonment rather than a mere military or political defeat.

## Current Status
Annam remains uninvolved in mortal affairs. Whether he is dead, transcendent, exiled, or in deliberate withdrawal is unknown. His silence is absolute and unexplained.
