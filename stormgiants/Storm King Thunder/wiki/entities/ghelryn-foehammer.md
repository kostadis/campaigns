# Ghelryn Foehammer
Dwarf craftsman / blacksmith; proprietor of Foehammer's (Forehammer's) Forge in Triboar; operates out of location T18.

## Identity
- **Role:** Dwarf craftsman / blacksmith; proprietor of Foehammer's (Forehammer's) Forge in Triboar; operates out of location T18
- **Faction:** None noted (independent)
- **First Appearance:** Party visited his forge while investigating a cat burglary, tracing the origin of a grappling hook; later sought out the party himself at the Troll Tavern; also encountered at the site of a slain fire giant in Triboar

*Also known as: Ghelryn, Gherlyn.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Values privacy strongly, but draws a clear moral line: his principles do not extend to protecting criminals — initially coy about who purchased the grappling hook, then forthcoming once shown respect.
- Responds warmly to displays of martial prowess and dwarven glory; Orsik's story of slaying a hill giant and crushing orcs won him over quickly.
- Proud of honorable conduct and recognizes genuine bravery; takes it upon himself to formally acknowledge acts of valor even when they are tactically imperfect.
- Demonstrates esoteric or arcane knowledge beyond typical smithcraft — aware of magical uses for monster components.
- Appears opportunistic or curious in sharing such knowledge: quietly offers unusual information rather than broadcasting it openly.
- Too old to be a suspect himself; more of an information source than an active player in events.

## History with the Party
1. **Grappling Hook Inquiry:** The party visited Foehammer's Forge tracing a grappling hook used in a burglary. Ghelryn was reluctant to give up his customer until Orsik regaled him with tales of giant-slaying and orc-crushing. Suitably impressed, he confirmed the hook was his work and disclosed that he sold it to **Darz Helgar**, caretaker of the northern campgrounds, who lives in a cottage on site. He cited that his respect for privacy does not shield burglars.

2. **Fire Giant corpse (Triboar):** Ghelryn observed Vardis examining the dead fire giant's corpse and quietly informed him that the creature's heart could be harvested and transformed into a *living lamp* — a magical item allowing the caster to project light into any room. Thistle overheard and remarked it would be useful for the theater. Vardis attempted to extract the heart but failed.

3. **Letter of Mark (Troll Tavern):** Ghelryn appeared at the Troll Tavern during the party's morning deliberations and presented a sealed letter of mark to Vardis. He acknowledged that engaging the fire giant directly was not the tactically optimal choice — defeating the giant's allies attacking the town would have been safer — but declared the party's refusal to retreat from a hated enemy an act of bravery deserving formal recognition. He instructed them to deliver the letter to King Morinn or Queen Tithmel at Citadel Felbar to claim their reward.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Troll Tavern (brief appearance), presumably returned to his smithy at T18
- **Active plans:** None known beyond delivering the letter of mark
- **Party knows:** He crafted the grappling hook sold to Darz Helgar; he has issued them a letter of mark redeemable at Citadel Felbar; he has knowledge of monster part alchemy/magic
- **Hidden:** His relationship with Citadel Felbar and what authority he holds to issue letters of mark; his background and motivations beyond smithcraft; whether he has further uses for monster components

## Relationships
- **Darz Helgar:** Customer — sold him the grappling hook (transactional, not necessarily friendly).
- **King Morinn / Queen Tithmel (Citadel Felbar):** Has enough standing or connection to issue a letter of mark redeemable by the crown.
- **Vardis:** Singled him out to share the living-lamp information; later addressed directly when presenting the letter of mark; appears to regard Vardis as a point of contact with the party.
- **Orsik:** Won Ghelryn's cooperation through shared dwarven warrior culture and boasting.
- **Thistle:** Overheard the living-lamp exchange; no direct interaction recorded.
