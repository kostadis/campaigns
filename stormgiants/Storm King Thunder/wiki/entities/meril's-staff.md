# Meril's Staff

A druidic artifact capable of verifying the sincerity of a person’s belief in naturalist philosophy. When held by an individual, it resonates if their conviction is genuine — not if their claims are factually accurate, but if their inner alignment with the principles of non-intervention and cosmic balance is absolute.

Adabra Gwynn used Meril’s Staff to confirm her own sincerity and later to verify Soma’s alignment with her worldview. The staff’s resonance with Adabra confirmed she is a “true believer,” elevating her from a radical to a tragic figure whose convictions are authentic, even if her conclusions are flawed.

The staff does not detect deception, control, or falsehood — only depth of belief. This makes it a tool of ideological validation, not moral judgment. It parallels the [[truth-constraint-mechanism]] of the Oracle: both systems affirm truth within limits, but neither guarantees objective reality.

Its existence challenges the party’s assumptions: if a person can be verified as sincere and still be dangerously wrong, then truth is not always aligned with virtue. Soma later used the staff to confirm Adabra’s sincerity — a moment that deepened her internal conflict, not resolved it.

Meril’s Staff is not a weapon — it is a mirror.
