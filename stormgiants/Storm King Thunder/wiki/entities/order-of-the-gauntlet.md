# Order of the Gauntlet

A militant order dedicated to eradicating evil, protecting the innocent, and upholding moral order. Its members are often seen as crusaders, templars, and champions of justice. The Order’s philosophy is the direct counterpoint to [[anti-interventionism]]. Where Adabra sees balance in non-action, the Order sees justice in action. Where Adabra sees nature as amoral, the Order sees evil as real and must be purged.

This ideological opposition is not merely tactical — it is metaphysical. The Order assumes moral categories are real; Adabra assumes they are illusions. Their conflict is the central tension of the campaign’s moral landscape.

Vukradin’s journey — from funded naturalist to Gauntlet champion — embodies this tension. His internal struggle is not about loyalty, but about whether justice can exist without moral certainty. Adabra Gwynn and the anti-interventionist faction view the Order as the primary agent of cosmic imbalance — their rituals, purges, and crusades are seen as violent disruptions of natural harmony. Vukradin’s membership in the Order triggered Adabra’s ideological rupture with him.

## Notable Members and History

The Order's credibility is bolstered by historical successes, such as the actions of [[milo-goodbarrel]] during the [[war-of-the-dragons]], where his leadership in breaching an inner sanctum demonstrated the group's tactical and martial prowess. [[ser-kaelen]] has invoked the Order's history to establish authority and context.
