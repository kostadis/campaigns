# Order of the Gauntlet

A militant, interventionist organization dedicated to eradicating evil, protecting the innocent, and restoring balance through direct action. They view planar incursions, cults, and monstrous threats as existential dangers that must be confronted, purged, or contained — regardless of the cost. Their members are often seen as crusaders, templars, and champions of justice, operating with unwavering moral certainty.

The Order’s philosophy is the direct counterpoint to [[anti-interventionism]] and its druidic adherents led by [[adabra-gwynn]]. Where Adabra sees cosmic balance in non-action and nature as amoral, the Order sees evil as real, absolute, and must be purged. They do not question whether intervention causes imbalance — they assume that inaction is the greater evil. This ideological opposition is not merely tactical — it is metaphysical. The Order assumes moral categories are real; Adabra and her faction assume they are illusions.

Vukradin’s journey — from funded naturalist to Gauntlet champion — embodies this tension. His internal struggle is not about loyalty, but about whether justice can exist without moral certainty. His public alignment with the Order triggered Adabra’s personal and political campaign against him, seen by her as a betrayal and a rejection of the naturalist worldview she had nurtured. The anti-interventionist faction views the Order as the primary agent of cosmic imbalance — their rituals, purges, and crusades are seen as violent disruptions of natural harmony.

The Order’s methods — crusades, cleansing rituals, and direct combat — are condemned by druidic opponents as destructive and hubristic. Yet its members believe they are the last bulwark against chaos, and their historical successes — such as [[milo-goodbarrel]]’s leadership in breaching an inner sanctum during the [[war-of-the-dragons]] — have cemented their credibility as effective defenders of order. [[ser-kaelen]] has invoked the Order’s history to establish authority and context.

The party’s actions often align with the Order’s goals, making them de facto allies — and ideological enemies of Adabra’s faction. The conflict between the Order and the anti-interventionists is not just about tactics — it is a fundamental clash over the nature of morality, responsibility, and the cosmos itself. This tension forms the central moral landscape of the campaign.
