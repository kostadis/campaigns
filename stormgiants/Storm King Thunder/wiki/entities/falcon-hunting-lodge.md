# Falcon Hunting Lodge

The [[falcon-hunting-lodge]] is a remote, ritually structured estate located in a secluded woodland region, serving as the private retreat of [[falcon-the-hunter]]. It is not a public inn or commercial establishment, but a domain governed by formalized protocols of entry and service, functioning as a social and intellectual hub for those adhering to the interventionist philosophy.

Access is strictly controlled: visitors must ring a bell at the gate, present a written credential (such as [[barthen]]’s letter of introduction), and await verification by corwin, the lodge’s chef and gatekeeper. Only after this procedural validation is the guest admitted and the master summoned. This system reflects a broader cultural norm of [[credential-based-access]], where trust is mediated through documentation rather than reputation or personal connection.

The lodge’s interior is implied to be austere and functional, with no indication of luxury or ostentation — consistent with Falcon’s withdrawn, regretful nature. Corwin’s presence as both chef and gatekeeper underscores the fusion of domestic service and institutional control within the estate.

The lodge exemplifies [[hospitality-as-ritual]] and [[gatekeeping-as-service]], and serves as a narrative anchor for [[falcon-the-hunter]]’s isolation and the quiet authority of mundane systems in a high-magic world.

It is also the site where the party first encountered [[sridar]], who shares Falcon’s views on the necessity of responding to planar disruptions.
