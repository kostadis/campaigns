# Brin Bundlewine

Source document detailing the life, devotional practices, and theological radicalization of Brin Bundlewine, a villager of Phandalin who became the primary architect of the Temple of Lathander’s Searing Pain of Justice. This source captures his interactions with Valphine, his public acts of devotion, and the aestheticization of suffering as sacred expression. His actions represent a grassroots theological movement untethered from institutional orthodoxy, driven by personal trauma, ecstatic surrender, and performative martyrdom.

Key events documented include his free labor on Tresendar Manor, his ritual self-flagellation, the public display of a Kraken Society cultist’s severed head, and his beatific radiance marked by sunburn and scars. His devotion is not coerced but self-actualized, making him a unique case study in radicalized faith.

This source is critical for understanding how divine symbolism can be reinterpreted through embodied suffering, and how religious movements can emerge from the margins without institutional backing.
