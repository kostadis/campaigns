# Source: delsephine.md

## Key Entities

### Central Entities
- **Delsephine** (Person): Captain of the *Dragon's Breath*. A former high-ranking member of a specific Cult of the Dragon splinter. She acts as a courier, transport specialist, and negotiator for Klauth. (New to Wiki)
- **Klauth / Klamath** (Entity/NPC): An ancient red dragon necromancer. Delsephine’s employer and object of intense personal devotion/attraction. (New to Wiki)
- **The Dragon's Breath** (Object/Vehicle): A sky vessel equipped with sail-like sails and sleigh runners, used for transport across various regions. (New to Wiki)

### Peripheral Entities
- **Severin** (Person): Leader of the Tiamat-focused faction of the Cult of the Dragon. Delsephine views him as a failure/fool. (New to Wiki - potentially related to existing "Cult of the Dragon" lore).
- **Adimond Kroskas** (Person): A high-level cult figure who failed to deliver promised magic under Severin. (Already in Wiki; this source adds her specific history with Delsephine and the internal rift between cult factions).
- **Bressik** (Person/Entity): A character involved in a contract negotiation regarding "living" and "undead" clauses. (New to Wiki - potentially related to existing "contractual morality" themes).
- **Orsik / Oriskus** (Person): A party member whose aging secret Delsephine knowingly protects. (New to Wiki; note: may be distinct from or a variant of "unla-kee" or other members, but appears as a specific individual in this text).
- **Unla Kee** (Person): Party member with pegasus wings. (Already in Wiki).
- **Thistle Wendrod** (Person): Social analyst/party member. (Already in Wiki).
- **Vardis** (Person): A party member who provides mission direction to Delsephine. (Already in Wiki).

## Key Concepts
- **Cult Schism (Tiamat vs. Dracolich)**: The internal ideological split between those seeking Tiamat's favor and those pursuing dracolich research. This defines the political landscape of the Cult of the Dragon. (New to Wiki)
- **Contractual Negotiation (Undead Clauses)**: Specific legal/magical negotiations regarding "living" vs. "undead" status, highlighting the pragmatic side of necromancy as a business. (Related to *contractual-morality*).
- **Professional Loyalty vs. Personal Devotion**: Delsephine’s character arc explores the blurred line between being a competent agent and a devoted follower/admirer. (New to Wiki)
- **Information Asymmetry (The Secret of Age)**: The deliberate choice by Delsephine to withhold knowledge regarding Orsik's true age/status as a professional courtesy. (Related to *information-asymmetry-npc-encounters*).
