## Key Concepts
*   **Planar Instability** (Phenomenon)
    *   **Definition:** A state where reality flickers, causing physical manifestations like "shimmering fur" and "shifting antlers."
    *   **Importance:** It provides a tangible, visual indicator of the "weirdness" affecting the world.
    *   **Status:** Related to *planar-phenomena-as-commodity* and *interdimensional-smuggling*.
*   **Transactional Communication** (Method)
    *   **Definition:** Interaction based on clear exchange (gift for information) rather than emotional appeal or social nuance.
    *   **Importance:** Highlights the Stag's pragmatic nature and the party's successful navigation of its boundaries.
    *   **Status:** New specific application to non-human entities.
*   **The "Other Nice People"** (Narrative Device)
    *   **Definition:** A vague reference to previous failed attempts by others to fix the forest.
    *   **Importance:** Creates a sense of history and potential future plot hooks regarding who these people were.
    *   **Status:** New; potentially links to *failed-investigations*.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The Stag as a Living Barometer:** The Stag is not just an animal but a "canary in the coal mine." Its physical mutations (teleporting, afterimages) are direct evidence of the escalating planar instability.
*   **Evidence of Failed Intervention:** The mention of "other nice people" who failed to help establishes that the current crisis is not new and that previous attempts at resolution were unsuccessful or incomplete.
*   **Strength of Evidence:** High. The Stag's physical state provides clear, observable evidence of environmental decay.
