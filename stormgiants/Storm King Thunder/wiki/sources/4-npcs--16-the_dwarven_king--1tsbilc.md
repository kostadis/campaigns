    *   Type: Boss / Fallen Ruler.

## Key Concepts
*   **Interdimensional Corruption**
    *   Definition: A transformative force that mutates physical forms and erodes identity (e.g., turning a king into a three-headed monstrosity).
    *   Importance: It is the primary environmental hazard of Axeholm and the mechanism behind the "corrupted" status of the fortress.
    *   Status: Exists in wiki (related to *midnight-tears* and *house-sartorra* themes).
*   **Signet Ring as Key**
    *   Definition: A physical artifact that serves both as a symbol of office and a functional tool for unlocking secrets.
    *   Importance: Establishes the "key" mechanic in the Axeholm/Throne Room narrative.
    *   Status: New specific item (Axeholm Signet Ring).
*   **Corrupted Resilience**
    *   Definition: The ability of a mutated entity to shrug off physical damage that would normally be fatal.
    *   Importance: Highlights the threat level of corrupted beings compared to standard monsters.
    *   Status: New concept/mechanic.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The King as a "Hollow" Antagonist:** The source confirms that by the time of encounter, the King possessed no personality or agency; he was a "feral, aggressive combatant." This reinforces the theme of **victim-of-dominance** (similar to Cryovain), where the individual is erased by an external force.
*   **The Mechanics of Corruption:** The transition from a ruler to a three-headed monstrosity provides physical evidence of the scale of interdimensional influence in the region.
*   **Evidence Strength:** High. The encounter was direct, and the resulting loot (the signet ring) provides a tangible narrative hook for future exploration.
