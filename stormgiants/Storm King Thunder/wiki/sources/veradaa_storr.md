## Key Concepts

*   **Information-as-Legacy:** The concept that a character's primary role is to provide information after death, turning their "life" into a tool for the party.
*   **Reputation of the Dead:** The phenomenon where an NPC’s reputation (being sought out by four different people) precedes them even in death, creating a sense of a larger world beyond the immediate scene.
*   **The Treasure Map as Narrative Anchor:** Using physical notes to bridge multiple locations (the abandoned outpost and the Greypeaks).

## Main Arguments & Findings

*   **Function over Persona:** Veradaa Storr serves primarily as a "loot-map" mechanic. Her character is defined by what she *knew* rather than who she *was*.
*   **The Mystery of her Death:** The fact that four different people sought her out independently suggests she was a known figure in the region, yet her death remains unexplained, creating an immediate hook for investigation (who killed her?).
*   **Evidence Strength:** High. Her role as a "scout" or "cartographer" is clearly established through the physical evidence of her notes being used by the party across multiple dates/locations.
