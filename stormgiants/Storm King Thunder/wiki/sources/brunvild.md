# Source: brunvild.md

## Key Entities
- **Brunvild** (Frost giant jarl) - **Central**. A high-ranking frost giant who serves as a primary witness to Blagothkus's fall and a victim of his failed alliances.
- **Blagothus** (Cloud giant lord) - **Central**. Brunvild’s primary antagonist; the source of Brunvild's resentment and the reason for his long imprisonment.
- **Escarlotta** (Spectral entity/Castle controller) - **Central**. The one who ultimately claimed ownership of the castle after Brunvild's failed salvage claim.
- **Thistle Wendrod** (Social analyst/Party member) - **Central**. The primary negotiator who convinced Brunvild to assist the party in Sandesyl’s lair.
- **Orsik** (Antagonist/Warrior) - **Central**. The physical threat that forced Brunvild to abandon his claim on the castle.
- **Harshnag** (Deceased legendary ally) - **Peripheral**. Mentioned as a potential source of authority for Thistle's claims.
- **Sandesyl Morgia** (Castellan/Traitor) - **Peripheral**. The one who imprisoned Brunvild in his lair.
- **The "Flying Kenku"** (Legendary figure) - **Peripheral**. A mythical or legendary prisoner-tale entity that Brunvild claims to have encountered, linked to the Cult of the Dragon.

## Key Concepts
- **Scapegoat/Proxy Victim**: Brunvild represents the cost of Blagothus's "cynical alliances." He is a tangible example of how the "small peoples" (giants) were betrayed by the "larger" powers (dragons/vampires).
- **Salvage Rights vs. Ownership**: The tension between Brunvild’s claim to the castle as a victim and Escarlotta's claim as the rightful owner.
- **The Myth of the Flying Kenku**: A piece of prisoner lore that blurs the line between reality and legend, potentially indicating a hidden history involving the Cult of the Dragon.
- **Fate of the "Flying" Influence**: The recurring theme of flying creatures (Kenku, Thistle) as catalysts for change in Brunvild's life.

## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Brunvild as a Living Record:** Brunvild serves as the primary source of truth regarding Blagothus’s transgressions (the deals with dragons and vampires). His testimony was the catalyst for Escarlotta's rage.
- **The Failure of Ambition:** Brunvild’s attempt to claim the castle highlights his transition from a desperate prisoner seeking "salvage" to a defeated survivor who must now navigate the aftermath of Blagothus's death.
- **Evidence Strength:** High. His role in delivering the killing blow and his specific testimony regarding the "dealings" provide concrete narrative weight to the fall of the cloud giant.
