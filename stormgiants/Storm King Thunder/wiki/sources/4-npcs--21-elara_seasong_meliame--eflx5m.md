# Source: npcs/elara_seasong_meliame.md

This analysis covers the character profile for **Elara Seasong Meliame**, a high-status figure whose presence in the narrative currently functions as a "destination" and a catalyst for political tension rather than an active participant.

## Key Entities
*   **Elara Seasong Meliame (also Elara Seasong Meliamne):** A wealthy art collector and member of House Meliame. She is a high-status, non-combatant figure who serves as the "target" for the party's delivery mission. (New/Existing: Exists in current wiki).
*   **House Meliame:** A noble house in Neverwinter. They are currently being targeted by their rivals, House Margaster. (Existing).
*   **Lord Cassian Meliame:** Elara’s eccentric cousin and host of the gala in Neverwinter. (Existing).
*   **Ser Kaelen:** The contact who identified Elara as the rightful owner of the lighthouse statue. (Existing).
*   **House Margaster:** A rival noble house seeking to sabotage House Meliame by intercepting the statue. (Existing).
*   **Vukradin:** A party member/ally who advocated for visiting Elara to identify loot. (Existing).

## Key Concepts
*   **[house-meliame] & [house-margaster]:** The core of the "Noble Intrigue" arc. This represents the transition from personal questing to high-stakes political maneuvering in Neverwinter.
*   **[luxury_as_political_shield]:** While not explicitly named as a concept yet, Elara’s role at an "extravagant gala" suggests that her status provides a layer of protection and social complexity compared to the more direct threats like The Carver or Aletra.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The "Absent Presence":** Elara currently functions as a narrative anchor. While she hasn't appeared yet, her existence is defined by what others say about her (Ser Kaelen’s identification) and the fact that others are trying to stop the party from reaching her (House Margaster).
*   **The Statue as Catalyst:** The lighthouse statue is not just "loot"; it is a symbol of heritage. Its ownership by Elara makes it a target for House Margaster, turning a delivery mission into a high-stakes escort/protection mission.
*   **Evidence Strength:** High. Her role in the narrative is clearly defined as a destination point and a victim of corporate/noble sabotage.
