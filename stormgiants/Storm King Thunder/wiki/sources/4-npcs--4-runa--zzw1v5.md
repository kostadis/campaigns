## Key Concepts
*   **Scripted Performance / Rehearsed Role**: The idea that Runa’s actions are not driven by personal conviction but by a pre-planned narrative. This reinforces the theme of "performance" seen in other characters like Amy.
    *   **Importance:** Highlights the lack of agency and the "hollow" nature of her role compared to more complex NPCs.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Relates to existing themes of *status-driven behavior* or *performative identity*.
*   **Flat Affect / Cue-Based Interaction**: Runa’s specific manner of speaking ("Blessed be! Blessed be!") and waiting for cues.
    *   **Importance:** Establishes her as a "hollow" character, potentially used to contrast with the "true believers" like Brin Bundlewine.
    *   **Wiki Status:** New nuance for the "Sisterhood" dynamic.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Lack of Agency:** Runa is presented almost entirely as an extension of Sylvine's will. She has no independent goals or motivations recorded.
*   **The Scripted Nature of the Sisters:** The observation by Soma that they rehearsed on the road suggests a high level of coordination, but also implies a lack of spontaneity in their actions.
*   **Ambiguity of Intent:** It is currently unknown if Runa is a willing participant or a coerced subordinate. This creates a "hollow" character who serves as a foil to more complex figures like Brin Bundlewine (who has intense conviction) or even Boney (who has independent sentiment).
