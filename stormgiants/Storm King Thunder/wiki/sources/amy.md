## Key Concepts
*   **Status-Driven Behavior** (Concept)
    *   **Definition:** Motivation rooted in social approval and the avoidance of conflict to maintain standing.
    *   **Significance:** This is the primary lens through which Amy's actions are interpreted by Thistle.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Already exists in the index.
*   **Defensive Deference** (Concept)
    *   **Definition:** Yielding to authority figures despite personal disagreement, often used to mask insecurity or a hidden past.
    *   **Significance:** Explains why Amy refuses to advocate for her creative choices against Kaelen.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Already exists in the index.
*   **Information Asymmetry (NPC)** (Concept)
    *   **Definition:** The gap between what the party knows about an NPC and the truth of that NPC's life/intentions.
    *   **Significance:** Amy’s "mysterious past" is a prime example of this, as her true motives remain hidden from the players.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Already exists in the index.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Core Claim:** Amy's behavior is not a reflection of artistic preference but a manifestation of psychological defense mechanisms (status-seeking and fear of conflict).
*   **Evidence:** 
    *   She avoids definitive statements and uses "vague uncertainty" to shield herself.
    *   She folds immediately when confronted by Orsik’s commanding tone, despite having genuine creative disagreements with Kaelen.
    *   Her gratitude toward Orsik is based on a misunderstanding of his role (believing he was a patron), showing her willingness to project "correct" social narratives onto others.
*   **Strength of Evidence:** High; the contrast between her internal frustrations and external compliance provides a clear profile for Thistle's analysis.
