## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The Nature of the Antagonist:** The "Carver" is not a person but a sentient, alien intelligence residing within an obsidian sword. This distinction is crucial for understanding its behavior: it was not "brave" or "mad," but rather a high-functioning, non-human intellect that viewed the human body as a mere tool.
*   **Tactical Adaptation:** The Carver demonstrated extreme competence in combat (the "legendary footwork") because the sword's intelligence was piloting the body
