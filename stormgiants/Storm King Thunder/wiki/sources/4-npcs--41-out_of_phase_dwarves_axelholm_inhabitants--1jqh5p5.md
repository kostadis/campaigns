# Source: npcs/out_of_phase_dwarves_axelholm_inhabitants.md

## Key Entities

*   **Out-of-Phase Dwarves (Axelholm Inhabitants)**
    *   **Type:** Monstrous/Transformed Humanoids.
    *   **Role:** Primary antagonists in the Axelholm segment.
    *   **Status:** Existing as a collective "horde" rather than individual NPCs. They represent a physical manifestation of a planar anomaly.
*   **Soma**
    *   **Type:** Party Member (Druid-in-training).
    *   **Role:** Central victim/target in the current arc; currently in a state of critical vulnerability due to the "paralysis" effect.
*   **Brewbarry**
    *   **Type:** Party Member (Barbarian).
    *   **Role:** Witnessed the "cold storage" effect; serves as a point of contrast for how different characters react to the anomaly's influence.

## Key Concepts

*   **Out-of-Phase Existence**
    *   **Definition:** A state where beings exist partially outside standard reality, manifesting as physical distortions (e.g., extra limbs, shifting ink).
    *   **Significance:** Establishes the "horror" of the Axelholm anomaly; it is not just a location but a spatial/planar distortion.
*   **Paralysis ("Cold Storage")**
    *   **Definition:** A magical or planar effect that freezes a target in time/space.
    *   **Significance:** Serves as a primary mechanical threat and narrative tool to create tension (the "cliffhanger").
*   **"Incorrect Packaging"**
    *   **Definition:** The specific phrase uttered by a disembodied voice when targeting Brewbarry.
    *   **Significance:** Suggests that the anomaly or its masters view biological entities as mere cargo or materials to be sorted/processed.
*   **Efficiency (Obsessive Muttering)**
    *   **Definition:** The only coherent thought remaining in the broken minds of the dwarves.
    *   **Significance:** Highlights the loss of individuality; they have been "re-purposed" by a system that values output over life.

## Main Arguments & Findings

*   **The Horror of Dehumanization:** The dwarves are not just monsters; they are *remnants*. Their physical descriptions (sewn mouths, embedded coins) suggest they were processed into currency or components for a larger machine/system.
*   **Scale of the Threat:** The "escalating numbers" and emergence from "unreal light" indicate that the party is fighting an environment as much as an enemy. The fortress itself is "producing" these entities.
*   **The Cliffhanger Mechanic:** The use of a paralysis effect on Soma creates a high-stakes narrative hook, moving the threat from "combat encounter" to "urgent rescue/survival."
