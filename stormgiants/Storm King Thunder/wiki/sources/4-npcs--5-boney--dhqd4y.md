# Boney

Detailed dossier on Boney, an undead skeletal horse who serves as a loyal, sentient, and emotionally nuanced companion to the party. Originally the mount of [[lady-alagondar]], he was killed by [[will-o-wisps]] while guarding a secret in the [[dragonbarrow]]. Reanimated by unknown means upon [[soma]]’s approach, he transferred his loyalty to the party after being convinced that Soma was Alagondar’s reincarnation. Renamed “Boney” by the party, he retains his cheerful personality, self-deprecating humor, and deep emotional attachment to his former master.

His presence during the death of corbin raises unresolved moral questions about non-human agency and complicity, embodying the theme of the *moral ambiguity of inaction*. His existence challenges traditional undead tropes by exhibiting sentience without corruption, loyalty without divine reinforcement, and emotional depth without malice.

Boney provided critical intelligence leading to the discovery of the Dragon Slayer Sword and formed a primary emotional bond with [[valphine]]. He is a key example of an *undead-animal-companion* and a case study in *loyalty-transference* across death. His naming—“Boney”—represents identity reclamation, transforming a label of decay into a symbol of personhood.

Key themes: loyalty beyond death, undead sentience, moral ambiguity of inaction, naming as identity reclamation.
