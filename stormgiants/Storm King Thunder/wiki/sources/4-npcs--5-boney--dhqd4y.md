## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core Identity:** Boney is not merely a mount but a sentient, self-aware undead being defined by his devotion. His transition from "Barney" to "Boney" signifies his acceptance of his current role in the party's narrative.
- **The "Reincarnation" Loophole:** The party successfully integrated him into their group by convincing him that Soma is Lady Alagondar’s reincarnation. This creates a stable but potentially fragile social contract based on a shared delusion/convenience.
- **Reliability as an Information Source:** Boney provided critical navigation (the location of the Dragon Slayer Sword), proving his utility as a "living" map of the area's history.
- **Evidence Strength:** High. The text clearly defines his motivations, physical state, and specific interactions with key party members.
