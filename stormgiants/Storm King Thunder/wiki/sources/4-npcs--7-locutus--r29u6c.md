## Key Concepts

*   **Transactional Loyalty / Brokerage:** Locutus represents a character whose role is purely functional—a middleman who operates on a system of rewards and punishments (the "fixer" archetype).
*   **The Reward as Motivation:** The specific goal of "passage to the Underdark" highlights the high stakes of the current narrative arc.
*   **Information-Triggered Execution:** Locutus’s death was not random; it was a mechanical consequence of him providing a specific piece of information (the confirmation of Borg's death). This reinforces the theme of **information-as-a-lethal-commodity**.

## Main Arguments & Findings

*   **The Role of the "Fixer":** Locutus serves as a narrative bridge. His existence highlights that even minor players in this world operate within complex, high-stakes webs of negotiation and clandestine deals.
*   **The Fragility of Negotiation:** The fact that Locutus was killed immediately upon fulfilling his role (confirming Borg's death) suggests a "no-trust" environment where information is traded at the cost of life.
*   **Failed Transaction:** His failure to receive the promised reward from Valphine underscores the theme of **failed promises** and the ruthlessness of the factions involved.
