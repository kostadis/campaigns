## Key Concepts
- **"Spiderman" Nickname**
    - **Definition:** A childhood moniker earned after Toblen survived being covered in baby spiders.
    - **Significance:** Serves as a character quirk and a point of humor/interaction with Brewbarry.
    - **Wiki Status:** Should be noted under the toblen-stonehill page.
- **Transaction-based Hospitality**
    - **Definition:** The practice of trading goods (food/drink) for services (entertainment).
    - **Significance:** Establishes Toblen as a pragmatic businessman rather than a charitable figure.
    - **Wiki Status:** Relates to [[transaction-as-narrative-catalyst]].
- **Narrative Economy**
    - **Definition:** The use of stable, non-complex NPCs to ground the world.
    - **Significance:** Toblen provides a consistent "safe" location in Phandalin without requiring complex subplots.
    - **Wiki Status:** Already exists as a core concept.

## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Stability of Local Infrastructure:** The Stonehill Inn serves as a stable, recurring hub for the party. Unlike many other locations that are sites of conflict or mystery, the inn represents "normalcy" in Phandalin.
- **Pragmatic Neutrality:** Toblen is portrayed as a man who does not hold grudges (e.g., toward Brewbarry) and focuses on the immediate survival and prosperity of his business.
- **Information Hub:** While he isn't a "spy," Toblen acts as a local barometer, aware of white dragon sightings and orc raids, providing the party with environmental context.
