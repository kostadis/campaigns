## Key Concepts

*   **Spectral Manifestation as Narrative Pointer**
    *   **Definition:** A ghost appearing not to interact, but to provide a visual cue (the gaze toward Ice Spire Mountains).
    *   **Importance:** It functions as a "silent" quest marker and atmospheric storytelling tool.
    *   **Wiki Status:** New nuance for existing `[[ghost-as-unresolved-purpose]]`.
*   **Loyalty Transference**
    *   **Definition:** The shift of an entity's devotion from a deceased master to a new one (Boney to Soma).
    *   **Importance:** Highlights the theme of enduring loyalty in a world of silence.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Already exists as `[[loyalty-transference]]`.
*   **Quest-as-Emotional-Trap / Legacy**
    *   **Definition:** The discovery that Alagondar’s "quest" was so significant it required the resurrection of a companion.
    *   **Importance:** Establishes the weight of her history without requiring direct dialogue.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Related to `[[quest-as-emotional-trap]]`.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The "Silent" Narrative:** The source reinforces that Lady Alagondar is a character defined by *absence*. She never speaks, yet her presence is felt through the physical layout of the Dragonbarrow and Boney's memories.
*   **Geographic Foreshadowing:** Her ghost’s gaze toward the Ice Spire Mountains serves as a non-verbal narrative bridge to the White Dragon threat.
*   **Validation of Choice:** The interaction between Soma and Boney regarding Alagondar’s "taste" in horses establishes a shared respect for her judgment, even in death.
