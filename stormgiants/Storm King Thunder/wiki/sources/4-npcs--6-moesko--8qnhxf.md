## Key Concepts
*   **Corrupted Devotion:** The transformation of a standard religious faith (Talos) into something "colder" and "calculating" due to external influence.
*   **Hybridized Guardianship:** Moesko’s role as a guardian who is not an autonomous agent but a "partially unwilling instrument" of another power.
*   **Sacrificial Mechanics (The Heart):** The use of a living being's heart as a literal power source for a magical beacon/curse.
*   **Dual-Symbolism / Hidden Agenda:** The talisman’s dual faces (Talos vs. Alien Eye) represent the "truth gap" between public appearance and hidden reality.
*   **Psionic Influence:** The presence of "alien psionic energy" as a distinct, non-divine force.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Moesko as a Proxy:** The primary finding is that Moesko was not an independent villain but a puppet or vessel. His identity as an Orc Anchorite and his devotion to Talos were "masks" for the alien influence.
*   **The Nature of the Curse:** The Tower's curse was physically tied to Moesko’s biology (the heart in the chest). Breaking the curse required the destruction of the organ, not just a ritual or combat victory.
*   **Evidence of External Threat:** The talisman provides physical evidence of an organized, non-human presence in the region that utilizes "cold" and "calculating" tactics rather than raw, chaotic power.
