## Key Concepts
*   **War of the Dragons** (Event)
    *   **Definition:** A historical conflict involving Tiamat's summoning.
    *   **Significance:** The catalyst for current political and theological divisions in the world.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Exists as a foundational historical event.
*   **Persuadable Non-Interventionism** (Theory/Method)
    *   **Definition:** The state of being convinced to act against one's core philosophy (as seen in Delaan’s shift).
    *   **Significance:** Highlights the fragility of ideological stances when faced with high-stakes rhetoric.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Exists as a nuance within the Emerald Enclave lore.
*   **Catalyst of Cataclysm** (Phenomenon)
    *   **Definition:** The role Malfire plays in the eyes of "Naturalists"—the person whose actions triggered the War of the Giants and the shattering of the Ordning.
    *   **Significance:** Establishes Malfire as a polarizing figure of historical weight.
    *   **Wiki Status:** Related to [[shattering-of-the-ordning]] and [[divine-silence]].

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Malfire as a Polarizing Icon:** The primary finding is that Malfire serves as a "Rorschach test" for the party's internal conflicts. To interventionists, he is a savior; to naturalists, he is the architect of the current era's decay.
*   **The Power of Persuasion:** The source highlights that Malfire’s primary attribute was not necessarily martial prowess, but the ability to shift the political and moral trajectory of an entire organization (the Emerald Enclave).
*   **Narrative Utility:** Currently, Malfire functions as a "mythic" figure. He provides weight to the history of the world without requiring immediate interaction, serving to deepen the stakes of the Druidic schism.
