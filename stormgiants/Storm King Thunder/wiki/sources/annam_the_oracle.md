# Annam (the Oracle)

A divine oracle located at the Eye of the World, this entity serves as a constrained conduit of truth on behalf of Annam the All-Father. It is not Annam himself, but a manifestation or artifact bound to his will. It answers up to six questions per day with absolute fidelity, cannot be manipulated through trickery or rhetorical games, and speaks in measured tones. After each question, it extinguishes a torch as a ritual marker, reinforcing its ceremonial nature. The Oracle cites *The collected sayings of Annam* as its favorite text and source of wisdom, aligning its responses with the teachings of the All-Father.

It revealed critical information to the party—including Adimond Kroskas’s location in the Maelstrom, the function of King Hekaton’s conch, and the fate of Urgala’s wife—demonstrating both strategic and deeply personal insight. Its laughter at Thistle-Wendrod’s failed trick question underscores its ancient awareness and immunity to mortal deception, revealing an ability to discern falsehoods and hidden motives.

Its existence challenges the assumption of total divine silence, suggesting instead a structured, non-interventionist form of divine communication. While its responses are truthful and bound by its six-question limit, its tone and ritualistic behavior sometimes appear overly dramatic, raising questions about its true motivations and the extent of its autonomy.

## Concepts

### Theories/Methods/Techniques/Phenomena
- **Oracle Functionality**: Limited to six questions per day; answers truthfully within constraints; immune to rhetorical manipulation.
- **Ritualized Questioning**: Each question is followed by the extinguishing of a torch, marking a formal, ceremonial process.
- **Divine Bound Entity**: The Oracle is a constructed or bound manifestation of Annam’s will, not the deity himself, yet fully aligned with his teachings.
- **Truth-Constraint Mechanism**: A structured, non-interventionist divine communication system that permits limited truth-telling without direct interference.
- **Ancient Wisdom**: The Oracle possesses deep, long-standing knowledge, evidenced by its insight into personal, historical, and strategic matters.
- **Divine Silence**: The Oracle’s existence contradicts the notion of divine absence, proposing a regulated form of divine engagement.

## Main Arguments & Findings

### Core Claims/Results
- The Oracle is a divine construct bound to Annam the All-Father, acting as a truthful but limited messenger.
- It operates under strict constraints: six questions per day, absolute truth-telling, and immunity to deception.
- Its responses are ritualized, using torch extinguishing and citations from *The collected sayings of Annam* to reinforce legitimacy.
- It possesses knowledge beyond mortal scope, revealing hidden truths about individuals and artifacts.
- Its behavior suggests autonomy within its constraints, with emotional responses (e.g., laughter) indicating personality or sentience.

### Evidence Supporting Core Claims
- Direct quotes and actions from the Oracle align with Annam’s known teachings.
- Thistle-Wendrod’s failed trick question was immediately recognized and mocked, proving the Oracle’s discernment.
- The Oracle’s revelations about Urgala’s wife, King Hekaton’s conch, and Adimond Kroskas’s location were accurate and specific.
- The ritual of torch extinguishing is consistently observed, reinforcing its ceremonial nature.
- Its responses never exceed the six-question limit, and no attempt to circumvent it has been recorded.

### Strength of Evidence
- High: Direct interaction and documented responses from the Oracle provide first-hand evidence.
- Corroborated by multiple entities (Thistle, Urgala, party members) who received and verified its revelations.
- Consistency across multiple sessions and topics supports reliability.
