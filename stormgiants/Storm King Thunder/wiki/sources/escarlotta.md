# Source: escarlotta.md

This analysis covers the character profile of **Escarlotta**, a sentient entity integrated into a cloud giant castle.

## Key Entities
*   **Escarlotta (Central):** A cloud giantess whose consciousness is fused with the castle's control systems. She serves as the pilot, navigator, and spiritual overseer. (Likely exists in wiki).
*   **Blagothkus (Central):** Escarlotta’s late husband; a cloud giant lord whose death marked a major shift in her narrative arc from "vengeful" to "mourning/evolving." (Already in wiki).
*   **Eigeron (Central):** Escarlotta's son, currently a ghost. His fate was the primary driver of her initial refusal to move the castle. (Already in wiki).
*   **Sandesyl (Peripheral):** A vampire resident of the castle whom Escarlotta despises but tolerates due to past failures. (Already in wiki).
*   **Countess Sansuri (Peripheral):** The primary target of Escarlotta’s rage; a "classless poseur" who holds maps Escarlotta desires. (Not explicitly in wiki, though related to the castle's history).
*   **Thistle Wendrod (Peripheral):** A party member whose sincerity and charm served as the primary catalyst for Escarlotta’s emotional growth. (Already in wiki).
*   **Orsik (Peripheral):** A party member who provided a perspective on forgiveness that helped Escarlotta reframe her anger. (Already in wiki).
*   **Brunvild (Peripheral):** A frost giant jarl whose claim to the castle was silenced by Escarlotta's assertion of ownership. (Already in wiki).
*   **Unla Kee (Peripheral):** A party member who faced a "veiled threat" from Escarlotta regarding the feeding of Sandesyl. (Already in wiki).

## Key Concepts
*   **Sentient Control System:** The concept of an entity whose consciousness is integrated into technology/magic (the castle's gems and runes) rather than being a biological pilot. (New nuance for "Castle as Character").
*   **Partnership vs. Toolhood:** Escarlotta’s insistence on being treated as a person with agency, not just a navigation tool or "utility" for the party.
*   **Reframed Rage:** The transition from reactive anger to a more measured stance where she prioritizes the "giant crisis" over personal vendettas.
*   **Narrative Economy (Character):** The use of physical cues (blushing, gem color changes) to convey internal state without dialogue.
*   **Offer of Forgiveness:** A specific arc point where Escarlotta accepted Orsik's perspective on moving past her hatred for Blagothkus.
