### Key Concepts
- **Philosophical Critique**: The Gold Dragon’s rejection of both the Cult of Bahamut and Cult of Tiamat due to their doctrinal constraints (Matter)
- **Self-Determination**: Core motivation for The Gold Dragon’s philosophy (Matter)

### Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core Claim**: The Gold Dragon seeks to establish a new philosophical path for dragonkind that prioritizes genuine freedom over doctrinal constraints.
- **Evidence**: His rejection of the Cult of Bahamut and Cult of Tiamat, and his killing of the party, indicate his desire to forge a new path.
- **Strength of Evidence**: Strong, supported by his explicit statements about rejecting doctrinal limitations and his actions against the party.
