## Key Concepts

*   **Economic Displacement via Transdimensional Trade:** The phenomenon where high-efficiency "shortcuts" (like Lionel Coster portals) render traditional, physically-bound trade routes obsolete for smaller entities.
*   **Logistical Dependency:** The forced reliance of independent merchants on organizations like the Zhentarim to maintain viability in a market dominated by transdimensional competitors.
*   **Market Asymmetry:** The disparity between "fast/cheap" interplanar goods and "slow/expensive" local goods, leading to the erosion of independent merchant autonomy.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Economic Conflict:** Calder serves as a living example of the friction between high-level planar commerce and ground-level trade. His bitterness highlights that the expansion of transdimensional trade isn't just a logistical shift but a disruptive economic force that creates "winners" (Lionel Coster) and "losers" (independent shopkeepers).
*   **Infrastructure Gap:** The fact that Calder must use Zhentarim transport underscores the divide between those with "portal access" and those without.
