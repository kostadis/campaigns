# Source: deciduous_dryad.md

## Key Entities
*   **Deciduous Dryad** (Entity): A combatant of the Unseelie Court. She is a central figure in the specific encounter involving Orsik and the party's defense against the Unseelie. (New to Wiki)
*   **Coniferous Dryad** (Entity): A fellow Unseelie combatant who worked in tandem with the Deciduous Dryad. She is significant because she maintained the charm on Unla Kee after the Deciduous Dryad died. (New to Wiki)
*   **Orsik** (Entity): A target of the dryads' manipulation; he was successfully charmed by the Deciduous Dryad during combat. (Already exists in Wiki - see *disguised-influence*)
*   **Unla Kee** (Entity): A party member who killed the Deciduous Dryad but remained under a magical charm sustained by her counterpart. (Already exists in Wiki)
*   **Thistle / Thuul** (Entities): Members of the party whose combined actions broke the Deciduous Dryad's concentration. (Thistle exists; Thuul is likely related to the *thule* mystery or a similar entity).

## Key Concepts
*   **Unseelie Court Tactics**: The use of charm and social manipulation as primary combat tools rather than direct violence. This highlights the "predatory" nature of Unseelie diplomacy. (New/Refinement)
*   **Dual-Link Charm**: A tactical maneuver where two entities maintain separate, concurrent magical effects (the Deciduous Dryad charmed Orsik; the Coniferous Dryad charmed Unla Kee). (New)
*   **Concentration Break**: The mechanical and narrative vulnerability of a caster whose concentration is broken by physical/elemental damage (acid/arrows), leading to the immediate failure of their influence. (Refines *tactical-adaptation-in-combat*)

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Tactical Sophistication of Unseelie**: The dryads did not fight as "monsters" in a traditional sense but as manipulators. The Deciduous Dryad specifically used the narrative of "friendship" to isolate Orsik from the party's collective defense.
*   **Redundancy in Magic**: The fact that Unla Kee remained charmed after her killer died suggests that Unseelie magic (or the specific school they utilize) can be layered or sustained by secondary sources, making it harder to "break" a spell by simply removing the primary caster.
