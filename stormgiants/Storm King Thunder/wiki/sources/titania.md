### Main Arguments & Findings

- **Core Claims:** 
  - Queen Titania is a capricious, manipulative ruler who uses emotional leverage and false ultimatums to control those around her.
  - She is transactional rather than magnanimous, earning approval through trickery and approving the party's actions due to their use of trickery.
  - She has a history of punishing Dryads for minor offenses, such as honest opinions about her appearance.
  - She has tasked the party with investigating and dealing with an Unseelie Fomorian threat, with the condition that the task must be completed successfully.
  - She has given the party a Blessed Horn as a gift, but the strings attached to it remain undisclosed.

- **Evidence Supporting Claims:**
  - Titania's treatment of Halisa and Helia, who were punished for minor offenses.
  - Her leniency towards the party, which was earned through trickery.
  - The task assigned to the party regarding the Fomorian threat.
  - The Blessed Horn, which was given but the conditions attached to it are unknown.

- **Strength of Evidence:**
  - Strong evidence from Titania's actions and the tasks assigned to the party.
  - Moderate evidence from the dryads' perspective and Thistle's observations.
