## Key Concepts

*   **Divine Judgment/Guardianship:** The Coatl functions as a moral arbiter rather than just a monster, seeking to "restore spiritual or moral order."
*   **Transactional Divinity:** The Coatl offers boons for specific actions (the destruction of Wormblod), establishing a contract-based relationship with the divine.
*   **Ego-Driven Divine Interaction:** The Coatl’s offense at the party's lack of gratitude highlights a "divine ego" where its role is tied to being revered, not just obeyed.
*   **Charm of Restoration:** The specific boon granted by the Coatl.
    *   **Status:** New item/mechanic; should be noted as a reward from this entity.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The "Truth" Gap:** A major tension exists regarding whether Wormblod is actually dead. The Coatl accepted Thistle's claim without verification, creating a potential "ticking clock" or hidden consequence if the lie is discovered.

*   **The Cost of Apathy:** The Coatl's physical tics (bulging eyes, tongue-flicking) suggest that while it is "rewarding," its patience with those who do not show proper reverence is thin.
