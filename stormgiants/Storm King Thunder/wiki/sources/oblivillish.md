# Source: oblivillish.md

### Key Entities
- **Sir Oblivillish / Oblivilish**: Royal Pixie; Zhentarim intelligence asset; Chief of Staff to Prince Thornacious (reinstated); central figure.
- **Prince Thornacious**: Liege and employer; central antagonist; status-conscious and contemptuous of Oblivillish.
- **Blasius / Blassios**: Former friend; falsely accused of killing the stag; reconciled with Oblivillish; central to Oblivillish’s arc.
- **The Zhentarim**: Handler/employer; split loyalties; central to Oblivillish’s operations.
- **Thistle**: Seelie pixie; Tense relationship with Oblivillish; respected Seelie lineage.
- **Uncle Joon**: Family tie used by Oblivillish to identify himself to Thistle; peripheral.
- **Orsik**: Zhentarim agent tasked with finding Oblivillish; peripheral.
- **Wallace**: Prompted Orsik to reread the Zhentarim notice; peripheral.
- **Vardis, Unla Kee**: Attempted post-battle hugs; peripheral.
- **Queen Tatiana**: Exiled Thornacious; central to the broader narrative.

### Key Concepts
- **Curse**: Oblivillish's involuntary and unpredictable visibility/invisibility; central to his character and plot.
- **Political Maneuvering**: Oblivillish’s strategic use of political dynamics to advance his goals; central to his actions.
- **Revenge**: Potential for Oblivillish to seek revenge against those who wronged him; central theme.
- **Zhentarim Operations**: Oblivillish’s covert activities for the Zhentarim; central to his dual allegiances.

### Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core Claims/Results**: Oblivillish was reinstated as Chief of Staff by Prince Thornacious after being falsely accused of killing a stag. His exile was orchestrated by Blasius, and he now operates covertly for the Zhentarim while maintaining a facade of loyalty to Thornacious.
- **Evidence Supporting**: 
  - Thistle’s accidental comment about Oblivillish being a "hunting master."
  - Blasius’s internal monologue revealing the false accusation.
  - The successful mission and Oblivillish’s reinstatement.
- **Strength of Evidence**: Strong, corroborated by multiple sources within the narrative.
