## Key Concepts
- **Stabilizing Presence**
    - **Definition:** An individual whose presence alters the social or political dynamics of a group, even if they do not take direct action.
    - **Significance:** Highlights Reidoth's role as a "counterweight" to Adabra’s radicalism.
    - **Status:** New nuance for the Emerald Enclave factional dynamic.
- **Strategic Absence**
    - **Definition:** The deliberate timing of an event (the council) to ensure a specific person is not present to complicate a desired outcome.
    - **Significance:** Illustrates Adabra’s tactical, rather than just ideological, approach to leadership.
    - **Status:** New nuance for the "Adabra" character arc.
- **Information Event**
    - **Definition:** The transmission of critical intelligence (the *Sending*) that changes the status of a faction or relationship without immediate physical interaction.
    - **Significance:** Marks the transition from internal party knowledge to external political maneuvering.
    - **Status:** New mechanic for tracking narrative progression.

## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Reidoth as a Political Counterweight:** The text establishes Reidoth not just as a leader, but as a "stabilizing" force. His absence was a calculated move by Adabra to ensure her radical faction could dominate the council's agenda without opposition from the established leadership.
- **The Power of Informationed Silence:** While Reidoth has not appeared physically, his role is significant because he now possesses the same critical information as the party (the schism, Falcon’s confession). This creates a "ticking clock" or "latent ally" dynamic; the party has successfully moved a piece on the board without knowing how that piece will move next.
- **Evidence Strength:** High. The text clearly defines his role in the hierarchy and the specific tactical reasons for his absence during key events.
