## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Victimhood vs. Agency:** The source establishes Teega not as a villain or a willing cultist, but as a victim of high-level psionic coercion. Her "loyalty" to the Kraken Society was a byproduct of mental rewiring rather than conviction.
*   **The Nature of the Threat:** By specifying that her manipulation was *not* Mind Flayer in origin, the text suggests a broader, perhaps more clandestine, threat within the region (potentially linked to the Kraken Society's higher leadership).
*   **Complex Reaction to Faith:** Teega’s presence at Valphine’s sermon—despite being "disturbed and conflicted"—suggests that while she is not yet a convert, the radical nature of the new Lathanderite doctrine has an unsettling resonance with her own experience of "cleansing."
