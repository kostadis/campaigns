## Key Concepts
*   **Ordning Shattering**
    *   **Definition:** The collapse of the traditional social and divine hierarchy of the giants.
    *   **Significance:** Annam’s silence is cited as a primary catalyst for this event.
    *   **Status:** Exists in the wiki (related to `ordning-shattering`).
*   **Spiritual Abandonment**
    *   **Definition:** The psychological and cultural impact of a deity ceasing to respond to their followers' prayers.
    *   **Significance:** This is the primary "character" of Annam in the narrative—he is defined by his absence rather than his actions.
    *   **Status:** Exists in the wiki.
*   **Divine Silence**
    *   **Definition:** The distinction between a deity withdrawing all communication versus being bound by specific constraints (like the Oracle).
    *   **Significance:** Contrasts Annam’s total withdrawal with the "mediated" truth of the Oracle.
    *   **Status:** Exists in the wiki.

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Characterization through Absence:** The source establishes that Annam is not a traditional "active" antagonist or ally; his role in the narrative is to serve as a symbol of abandonment.
*   **Causal Link to Decline:** The text explicitly links Annam's silence to the decline of the giant races and the subsequent shattering of their social order (the Ordning).
*   **Information Gap:** There is a deliberate distinction between what the party knows (he is silent) and what remains hidden (why he is silent), creating potential for future plot hooks regarding his true motives.
