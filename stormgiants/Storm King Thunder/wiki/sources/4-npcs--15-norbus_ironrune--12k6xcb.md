## Key Concepts

*   **Contractual Morality / Pragmatic Integrity**
    *   **Definition:** The practice of making ethical commitments (e.g., returning items to owners) specifically to secure cooperation or navigate social hurdles.
    *   **Significance:** Norbus uses this to bypass Vukardin's moral objections, showing that in this world, "good" intentions can be a calculated business strategy.
    *   **Status:** Related to existing concepts like [[contractual-integrity]] and [[pragmatic-integrity]].

## Main Arguments & Findings

*   **Strategic Diplomacy over Moral Conviction:** The text suggests that Norbus's pledge to return items to their "rightful owners" may be a tactical maneuver rather than a personal conviction. This creates an **Information Asymmetry** regarding his true intent.
*   **Investment in the Party:** His visible disappointment when the party tried to leave after the dragon warning indicates he views the party as a valuable, long-term asset or "tool" for his business goals.
*   **Evidence Strength:** High (within the context of the narrative). The interaction with Vukardin is a clear example of an NPC using specific social maneuvers to ensure a contract remains viable.
