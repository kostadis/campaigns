# Source: Daran Edermath (Silverleaf)

This source documents the introduction of Daran Edermath (Silverleaf), a retired half-elf adventurer turned orchardist, who approaches the party with a salvage proposal targeting a lighthouse west of Phandalin. His calm demeanor and unassuming presentation mask a profound moral blind spot: he frames the recovery of treasure from shipwrecks as legitimate economic activity, unaware or unwilling to acknowledge the possibility of unburied dead. His reaction to Vukradin’s moral objection — shock and apology, not defensiveness — reveals a character who has internalized the pragmatism of adventuring life without confronting its ethical costs.

The source establishes key narrative mechanics: Daran functions as a [[narrative-placeholder-npc]] and exemplifies [[information-asymmetry-npc-encounters]], as his personal connection to the lighthouse remains entirely withheld. His retirement to orchard life mirrors [[retired-sheriff-and-rancher]] but without redemption — he has not confronted his past, only buried it. The lighthouse site, though unvisited, is positioned as a potential future quest hub with emotional and possibly supernatural weight.

This source enriches the campaign’s moral landscape by introducing a non-villainous figure whose harm stems not from malice, but from indifference — expanding the spectrum of ethical ambiguity beyond manipulative antagonists like Aletra Satora or Borg the Hammer.
