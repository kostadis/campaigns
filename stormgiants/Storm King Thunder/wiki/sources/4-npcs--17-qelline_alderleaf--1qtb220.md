## Key Concepts
*   **Hurt Price (Economic Retribution):** The practice of setting prices not based on market value, but as a punitive measure for past grievances. Qelline’s 1gp/week fee was a weaponized economic tool.
*   **Quest-as-Emotional-Trap:** A narrative structure where a seemingly mundane task (negotiating grazing rights) is actually designed to force a character into a moment of personal reckoning or reconciliation.
*   **Transaction as Narrative Catalyst:** Using a low-stakes, realistic exchange (the 5sp/reward for an apology) to move the plot forward without requiring high-action drama.
*   **Redemption through Acknowledgment:** The specific arc type where a character's growth is achieved by facing their past mistakes and offering a sincere, personal apology rather than just "doing good."

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **The Core of the Conflict:** Qelline’s hostility isn't toward the party; it is specifically directed at Big Al. The conflict is rooted in **historical economic damage** (his aggressive pricing during his prosperous years) which ruined her livelihood.
*   **Strategic Negotiation:** Qelline is portrayed as sharp and observant. She recognized the "proxy" nature of the party's involvement and used that fact to demand a higher price, showing she understands the power dynamics of negotiation.
*   **The Resolution Mechanism:** The resolution was not achieved through combat or magic, but through **personal presence**. The requirement for Big Al to appear in person is the pivot point from "transactional" to "relational."
