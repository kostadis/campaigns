*   **Layered Contingencies / Performance as Tool:** The use of emotional displays (wounded pride, religious invocation) that are discarded instantly when they fail to achieve the desired result.

*   **Two-Stage Plan (Con then Violence):** A tactical progression from social manipulation to physical confrontation when the "soft" approach fails.
    *   **Significance:** Illustrates her role as a professional criminal rather than a desperate scavenger.

## Main Arguments & Findings

*   **Sophistication of Villainy:** Sylvine is not a "wild" villain but a sophisticated one. Her ability to pivot from a theatrical plea to a violent command in under a second suggests a high level of discipline and lack of genuine empathy.
*   **The Failure of the Con:** The core finding is that her failure was caused by **[information-asymmetry]**. She assumed she could manipulate Vukradin's reputation, but his actual integrity acted as a hard wall against her performance.
*   **Evidence Strength:** High. The text explicitly notes Soma’s observation that her "hurt" feelings were not real and the transition was instantaneous, confirming her role as a professional actor in her crimes.
