# Source: npcs/fibblestib.md

## Key Entities
*   **Fibblestib (also known as Fiddlestib)** - *Central*. A chief inventor at Gnomengarde. She is a primary source of information regarding the "sanity ray" and local political theories. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Dabbledob** - *Central*. Fibblestib's co-inventor and rival. Represents the contrast between speculative invention and evidence-based pragmatism. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **King Korboz** - *Central*. The ruler of Gnomengarde, currently suffering from madness. He is the primary "patient" for Fibblestib's inventions. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Warren & Orrin** - *Peripheral*. Two missing gnome inventors whose disappearance fuels the mystery of a potential shapechanger. (Already exist in wiki).
*   **Soma** - *Central (Party Member)*. A druid-in-training who experiences moral distress regarding Fibblestib's disregard for the sanctity of remains. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Vukradin** - *Central (Party Member)*. A former ally of Adabra; interacts with Fiblestib on the topic of music as a treatment for madness. (Already exists in wiki).

## Key Concepts
*   **Sanity Ray** - A speculative magical device intended to cure mental illness/madness. It represents the "technology-as-cure" philosophy. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Straitjacket Solution** - A non-technological, physical restraint method for managing madness; it is the primary rival design to Fiblestib's sanity ray. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Bicameral Legislature with a Unitary Executive** - A specific political theory championed by Fiblestib. It highlights her role as an intellectual who engages in high-level governance debate. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Harvesting Constructs from Creature Entrails** - The practice of repurposing biological remains into mechanical components; it serves as a point of moral friction between the party and Gnomengard's scientific community. (Already exists in wiki).
*   **Tamper-proof Voting Box** - A specific engineering project currently being debated by Fiblestib and Dabbledob, highlighting their competitive design philosophies. (New detail for existing "Gnomengarde" context).

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Intellectual Arrogance as a Character Shield:** Fiblestib's dismissiveness of the party’s dragon-related concerns isn't just rudeness; it reflects a prioritization of her own intellectual goals (the King's madness) over external threats.
*   **The "Mad Science" Spectrum:** The contrast between Fiblestib and Dabbledob illustrates two different ways to approach innovation: one driven by bold, potentially dangerous experimentation (Fiblestib) and the other by grounded pragmatism (Dabbledob).
*   **Moral Friction via Technicality:** The conflict with Soma regarding the mimic's entrails highlights a core theme in the wiki: the tension between "scientific/technical utility" and "moral/spiritual sanctity."
