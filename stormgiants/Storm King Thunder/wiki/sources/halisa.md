# Source: halisa.md

### Key Entities
- **Halisa**: Seelie Dryad, former courtier of the Fey Court, exiled guardian of the Grandfather Tree, currently unknown location.
- **Queen Titania**: Seelie Queen, Halisa's punisher for speaking honestly about her attire.
- **Prince Thornacious**: Alleged liberator of Halisa, revealed to be Orsik in disguise.
- **Helia**: Seelie Dryad, former romantic partner of Halisa, now engaged in a mutual antagonistic relationship.
- **The Party (Thistle)**: Refused to assist Halisa, citing the terms of her punishment.
- **The Party (Orsik)**: Freed Halisa through impersonation, unaware of the deception.
- **Seelie Court (Sprite Emissary)**: Aware of Halisa's release through the party's trickery.

### Key Concepts
- **Tree Protection Schedule D**: A legal document Halisa was bound under, which she cited to refuse assistance from the party.
- **Dryads**: Mythical forest spirits, Halisa and Helia are both guardians of the Grandfather Tree.
- **Fey Court**: The ruling body of the Seelie Court, where Halisa served as a courtier.

### Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core Claims**: Halisa was initially punished by the Seelie Court for speaking honestly about Queen Titania's attire. She was released from her exile by Prince Thornacious, who impersonated the actual Prince Thornacious. Halisa's release was framed as a punishment to her, not a reward.
- **Evidence Supporting Claims**:
  - Halisa's initial encounter with Thistle, where she cited "Tree Protection Schedule D" to refuse assistance.
  - Halisa's declaration of intent to return every 100 years to taunt Helia.
  - The sprite emissary's note that both Halisa and Helia were freed through the party's trickery.
- **Strength of Evidence**: Strong, corroborated by multiple sources within the narrative.
