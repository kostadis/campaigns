## Key Concepts

*   **Enforcer Archetype:** Borg functions as a "field commander" who executes specific, high-stakes tasks for a hidden superior (The Carver).
*   **Tactical Awareness vs. Brutality:** The contrast between Borg's calculated movements (splitting forces) and his impulsive reactions (burning the ranch in a rage).
*   **Quest as Emotional Trap:** The use of Big Al as bait illustrates how villains utilize "human" elements to lure heroes into specific locations.
*   **Information Asymmetry:** The gap between what the party knows about Borg (he is dangerous/tactical) and what remains hidden (his ultimate destination and his report to the Carver).

## Main Arguments & Findings
*   **Borg as a Tool of Strategy:** The document establishes that Borg is not just a "brute" but a competent officer. His decision to split his forces indicates he understands how to manage pursuit and tactical positioning.
*   **The Failure of the Ambush:** The party successfully avoided the primary trap, but Borg's reaction (burning the ranch) suggests a high level of frustration when his specific "scripted" outcome is denied.
*   **Evidence Strength:** High. The document clearly defines his role, his relationship to the Carver, and his tactical capabilities based on observed actions during the Butterskull Ranch incident.
