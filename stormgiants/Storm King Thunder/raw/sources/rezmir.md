# Rezmir

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Black Dragon Wyrmspeaker; Cult of the Dragon
- **Rank:** One of five Wyrmspeakers, reportedly in charge of the castle and responsible for moving the hoard as part of the draconic ritual
- **First Appearance:** Never met in person — identified through physical remains and documents discovered by the party

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Advancing the Cult of the Dragon's ritual to move the hoard; protecting her operations from internal oversight
- Rezmir was politically defensive and suspicious of internal cult scrutiny, framing audits of her operation as acts of sabotage rather than legitimate oversight. She invoked Tiamat's unity as ideological cover against challenges to her authority, suggesting pride and a tendency toward self-justification.

## History with the Party
- **Discovered posthumously:** The party found partial remains of a half-dragon in purple Wyrmspeaker garb in a room north of the barracks. Thistle identified the body as Rezmir, the Black Dragon Wyrmspeaker.
- **Documents recovered:** Orsik found a note written in Rezmir's voice protesting an audit of her operations. The note accused the auditors of being spies who had infiltrated the Black Dragon Hatchery, and demanded that any future auditing involve all five Wyrmspeakers collectively or be treated as enemy action.

## Current Status
- **Deceased.** Remains located in the room north of the barracks within the castle.
- **What the party knows:** She was in charge of the castle, was responsible for moving the hoard, and died before they arrived — likely due to internal Cult of the Dragon politics based on the tone of her recovered note.
- **What remains hidden:** The exact circumstances and perpetrator of her death are unconfirmed. It is unclear whether the audit dispute directly led to her death or whether another faction was responsible.

## Relationships
- **Cult of the Dragon / Wyrmspeakers:** Held a leadership role but was in active conflict with unnamed internal auditors she believed were saboteurs. Viewed collective Wyrmspeaker authority as the only legitimate check on her operations.
- **Black Dragon Hatchery:** Closely associated with its operations; accused the auditors of having infiltrated it.
- **The Party:** Known only through remains and documents; never interacted directly.
