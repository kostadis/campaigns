*Also known as: Mirren.*

# Mirran

## Identity
- **Role:** Eldest daughter of King Hekaton; princess of the Maelstrom; co-conspirator with her sister Nym
- **Faction:** Aligned with Nym and, through them, with the blue dragon Adminond
- **First Appearance:** Great hall of the Maelstrom underwater stronghold, performing a musical duet with Nym when the party arrived

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Prevent Serissa from forming alliances or relationships with outsiders; claim the queenship for herself and Nym in place of Serissa
- **Key Drive:** Contempt for Serissa's perceived weakness and explicit disgust at the idea of inter-species alliance (demonstrated by her reaction to Orsik's matchmaking claim)
- Poised and performative in public — uses warmth as a weapon — but drops the facade quickly once she believes she has the upper hand. When her schemes fail, she reverts to petulant insistence, stamping her foot and pouting before the Imperator. She is politically calculating but emotionally reactive when challenged.

## History with the Party

1. **First Contact (Arrival Session):** Mirran was performing with Nym when the party entered. She intercepted Orsik's claim of a matchmaking appointment with Serissa, laughed it off, and stalled — coordinating with Nym to buy time to assemble giant lords and set a trap.

2. **The Ambush:** Mirran escorted the party toward the pool chamber under a guise of hospitality, then dropped the pretense and ordered four assembled giant lords (Duchess Tartha, Count Vaal, Jarl Hellenhild, Thane Braxow) to kill the party, framing them as assassins in Giant so the lords would act. She took a swipe at Orsik as he fled (missed), then struck Vardis and Unla Kee with a lightning bolt before departing.

3. **Aftermath Investigation:** Mirran descended to the portal chamber after the fight went quiet, found it empty, and concluded aloud that the giants had killed the party and departed. She did not detect the hidden party.

4. **Throne Room Confrontation (Observed by Thistle):** Mirran argued before the Imperator that she and Nym should be queen rather than Serissa. She framed the party as assassins and cheaters. Adminond the blue dragon was directing her arguments from behind the scenes.

5. **Hekaton's Return and Reckoning:** When Hekaton returned, Mirran performed a joyous greeting before being exposed by Serissa's account. Hekaton condemned her alongside Nym for the same betrayals. She attempted to protest before being removed by guards on Serissa's order.

6. **Current Confinement:** Confined to her chambers in the Maelstrom, fate to be decided by Hekaton.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Chambers in the Maelstrom, under confinement
- **Active Plans:** None known — her schemes have collapsed with Hekaton's return and exposure
- **What the Party Knows:** She orchestrated the assassination attempt, framed them publicly, argued for Serissa's removal, and has been condemned by Hekaton
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full nature and depth of her arrangement with Adminond; whether she has any remaining allies or contingency plans within the Maelstrom

## Relationships
- **Nym (sister):** Close co-conspirator; the two coordinated the trap, shared the framing of the party, and jointly argued for the queenship. Nym is the dominant figure — a shark referred to Mirran dismissively as "Nym's pet."
- **Serissa (sister):** Rival and target; Mirran worked actively to undermine and replace her
- **Adminond (blue dragon):** Off-screen backer; directed Mirran's arguments in the throne room confrontation
- **King Hekaton (father):** Condemned by him following Serissa's testimony; relationship now broken
- **The Party:** Considers them enemies and assassination targets; Thistle specifically recognized her political trap and warned against harming her

## Arc Score Events
- **Decrease — Ambush and Lightning Bolt:** Mirran ordered the party killed and personally attacked Vardis and Unla Kee
- **Decrease — Political Framing:** Publicly labeled the party as assassins before giant lords and the Imperator
