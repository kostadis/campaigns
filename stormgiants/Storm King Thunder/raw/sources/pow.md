# Pow

## Identity
- **Role:** Majordomo / Chief of Operations / COO of the Grand Dame (under Lord Drylund); now co-venture partner and operational lead of the *Kee and Wing Casino*
- **Faction:** Previously aligned with Lord Drylund aboard the Grand Dame; now independent
- **First Appearance:** Met by the party at the docks upon their arrival at the Grand Dame; intercepted them to ask how many chips they would be purchasing

*Also known as: Pow Ming, the Majordomo.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** While at the Grand Dame, keep operations running smoothly and protect Lord Drylund's time and interests. Now: maximize profitability of gambling operations; expand the casino venture into a mobile, tax-free enterprise across multiple cities.
- Polite but forceful — controlled access and information aboard the Grand Dame with quiet authority. Efficient and observant, catching Vardis's offhand remark about meeting Drylund and acting on it without being prompted.
- Pragmatic, entrepreneurially sharp, and quick to recognize opportunity — he grasped the flying casino concept immediately and engaged constructively rather than needing to be convinced.
- Patient and professional in explaining business fundamentals (e.g., calmly correcting Unla Kee's request to rig games in his favor).
- Demonstrated good judgment and appropriate alarm when confronted with genuinely dangerous information (the demon summoning script).

## History with the Party

**Aboard the Grand Dame (as Pow Ming, Majordomo):**
- Intercepted the party immediately upon arrival; asked about chip purchases and directed Orsik toward the Sava tables when he inquired about a high-rollers table.
- Overheard Vardis express a desire to meet Lord Drylund and arranged a private meeting in Drylund's office on the party's behalf.

**Post-Drylund / Casino partnership:**
1. **First Meeting at the Docks:** Pow was introduced to the party as COO of the Grand Dame. Orsik pitched the flying casino concept; Pow responded with immediate enthusiasm, identifying the mobile tax-avoidance angle as a key advantage they had not previously considered.
2. **Deal Sealed:** Accepted Unla Kee's naming rights condition without resistance. The casino will be called the *Kee and Wing Casino*. Shook hands with Unla Kee to formalize the partnership.
3. **Renovation Planning:** Took an active role in scoping the castle conversion. Hired ten Shou laborers at double standard rate (6 sp/day each) due to the unusual working conditions. Provided cost estimates: ~1,000 gp startup, 50,000 gp for a serviceable casino, 500,000 gp for a full grand casino, with profits intended to fund reinvestment over time.
4. **Demon Script Incident:** Reacted with horror upon learning that a play script Thistle mentioned was actually a demon summoning ritual.
5. **Magic Item Delay:** Arranged contacts to grant Unla Kee and Thistle a few days' window to purchase magic items before the group's departure.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The flying castle (renovation site), actively overseeing construction and operational planning
- **Active Plans:** Managing the 1,000 gp startup phase; building toward a functional casino with a crew of ten Shou laborers; long-term plan is to reinvest profits toward the 50,000 gp serviceable casino threshold
- **What the Party Knows:** His costs, renovation timeline expectations, and business philosophy
- **What Remains Hidden:** Nothing currently flagged as concealed in the notes

## Relationships
- **Lord Drylund (former):** Direct subordinate; handled everything Drylund did not wish to deal with personally; gatekeeper to him aboard the Grand Dame. Drylund is now deceased; Pow has moved on to the casino partnership.
- **Unla Kee:** Business partner; the casino bears Unla Kee's name. Pow handled Unla Kee's naive request to rig games diplomatically rather than dismissively.
- **Orsik:** Received the original pitch from Orsik; views him as the conceptual architect of the venture.
- **Thistle:** Present during the demon script revelation; Pow was alarmed by this information.
- **Laborers:** Employs ten workers of apparent Shou origin; paying a premium for the unusual working conditions, suggesting he values reliability and loyalty in his crew.
- **The Party (3HPP):** Professionally courteous historically; now a formal business partner.
