# Kolbaz

## Identity
- **Role:** Harper operative and regional agent; leader of the Harper headquarters in Yartar
- **Faction:** The Harpers
- **First Appearance:** Greeted the party upon their arrival in Yartar as their host and contact, sent by Krowen

## Personality & Motivations
- A proud, old-guard Harper who believes Harper membership should carry weight and meaning — openly contemptuous of Krowen's use of non-sworn contractors.
- Took personal and professional pride in his illusion work and his history of active service, including combat at the Well of Dragons against the Cult of the Dragon.
- Stubborn in his convictions: dismissed the idea that the Cult would target a fairy and was certain the Spring's First Blossom played no role in the ritual, though he acknowledged the situation was "very odd."
- Had a genuine, if uninformed, interest in Seelie Court aesthetics; was noticeably hurt by Thistle's critique of his illusory garden.

## History with the Party
1. **Arrival in Yartar:** Kolbaz met the party with suspicion, demanded identification, and voiced his displeasure at working with non-sworn agents. He formally welcomed them as their host.
2. **Briefing:** Directed the party to the Wink and Kiss tavern as the Hand of Yartar's known gathering place and confirmed that rogues were pervasive throughout the city.
3. **Cult of the Dragon:** Reacted with intense indignation when Thistle mentioned Adimond Kroskas, insisting the Cult was destroyed. Claimed personal involvement in research on the ritual and held firm that the Spring's First Blossom was not connected to it.
4. **The Garden & Tea:** Invited Thistle to discuss improvements to his inaccurate Seelie Court illusion garden over tea; was rebuffed.
5. **Death (2nd day, 1st tenday of Flamerule):** Struck by a bolt of lightning and collapsed. A small, unidentified figure stood over him and spoke the words *"you meddling little fool"* in an unnaturally large voice. Died before the party's return to Harper headquarters.
6. **Post-mortem Discovery:** Thistle's examination of Kolbaz's papers revealed that his research into Adimond Kroskas was the proximate cause of his death. The papers contained a critical discovery: **Adimond Kroskas is an alias — he is not who he claims to be.**
7. **Scene Evidence:** The physical damage to Kolbaz's chambers suggested something massive materialized in the room, destroyed everything, and dematerialized. Gauche knew that other individuals had come through the teleportation circle around the time of death but withheld this from the party.

## Current Status
- **Deceased.**
- His significance is now primarily as a victim and a source of intelligence; his research into Adimond Kroskas appears to have gotten him killed.

## What the Party Knows vs. What Remains Hidden
- **Knows:** Kolbaz is dead; his research exposed Adimond Kroskas as using an alias; the damage is anomalous and violent.
- **Hidden from party (at time of discovery):** Gauche knew other individuals came through the teleportation circle around the time of death and deliberately withheld this.

## Relationships
- **Krowen:** His Harper superior; held a professional grievance over Krowen's policy of using non-sworn contractors.
- **Thistle:** Brief but pointed interactions — mutual friction over the Cult of the Dragon and Seelie Court culture; Thistle was the one who examined his papers after death.
- **Adimond Kroskas:** His investigation into Kroskas's true identity was almost certainly the direct cause of his death.
- **Vardis:** Vardis recalled Kolbaz's death and the chamber damage, connecting it to Adimond and identifying it as consistent with a **Blue Dragon**.
- **Gauche:** Knew details about activity near the teleportation circle at the time of death; withheld this from the party.
