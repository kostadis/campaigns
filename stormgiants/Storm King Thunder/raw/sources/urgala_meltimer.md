# Urgala Meltimer

## Identity
- **Role:** Retired adventurer; innkeeper and proprietor of the Northshield House Inn in Triboar
- **Faction:** Politically libertarian; openly anti-Lord's Alliance; no formal affiliation. Implied past connections to intelligence networks or adventuring circles; familiar with the Zhentarim, Hand of Yartar, and various criminal creditors.
- **First appearance:** Met by the party at the Northshield House Inn (cooperative on the Dessarin Hills); later spotted by Thistle during the fire giant attack on Triboar, fighting in the market with a short bow

*Also known as: Urgala.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Living quietly in retirement; maintaining her inn; keeping the peace in her small community — though willing to answer one last call when the stakes are high enough; community resilience, loyalty to friends and allies, and quiet pragmatism about morally complex situations.
- Calm and grounded under pressure — when her inn burned, she shrugged it off with "Houses burn, but they can be rebuilt." She laughs heartily at impractical suggestions and excuses herself before she loses composure.
- Direct, warm, and emotionally guarded; volunteers personal information selectively and shuts down manipulation firmly when she recognizes it.
- A seasoned adventurer beneath the innkeeper exterior — she has seen fast-talkers before and is not moved by charm when she has made up her mind.
- A realist with a complicated past. Withholds judgment on others' moral choices while acknowledging her own sins, and is capable of long-suppressed satisfaction when people she dislikes are humbled.
- Holds strong moral convictions and is not afraid to voice blunt judgments (e.g., her flat opinion that Kaelen should have been killed on the spot).
- Flirtatious and warm with the party; engages in easy banter with Vardis and Orsik.

## History with the Party

**Northshield House first contact:**
Urgala provided Thistle with a hand-drawn map and detailed notes on the Dessarin Hills, describing the wildcat mining pattern — miners strike it rich, abandon the site, and monsters or bandits repurpose it. She explained that one mine in particular connects to the Underdark, the same mine where her wife was lost more than ten years ago.

**Affair revealed:**
When pressed about Mirak Hysstryn, Urgala admitted to a period of involvement with him following her wife's disappearance, framing it as a grief-driven relationship. She insisted she had no part in his disappearance and that she never believed rumors of other women — she believed Mirak was devoted solely to her. She then implied she suspects Mirak's wife Tolmarra discovered the affair, before catching herself and asking Thistle to keep the information quiet.

**News of Mirak's death:**
Urgala received the news emotionally, briefly crying, then composed herself and remarked it was never going to end well. She thanked the party for the peace they brought her and offered one free night's stay with breakfast. When Thistle pushed for more, she firmly shut him down, making the limits of her generosity clear.

**The Fire Giant Attack (Triboar):**
Urgala fought during the fire giant assault, sustaining burn marks from magmins while her inn burned to the northeast. After the battle she met the party at the Talking Troll, offered mild criticism of their battlefield choices, and rewarded their help with a lead: her former adventuring companion **Harthos Zymorven** at Zymorven Hall northwest of Silverymoon possesses a giant-slaying longsword and will part with it if the party drops her name and states their intent to fight giants. As she left, she returned to add that she has a saved map to an unexplored mine near where her wife died.

**The Kaelen Debt Crisis (Northshield House):**
Urgala invited the party to the Northshield House to deliver bad news: **Kaelen** had borrowed 5,000 gold in the party's name from multiple creditors including the Hand of Yartar, the Zhentarim, and local criminals. She outlined the stakes — a month of sold-out performances needed — and identified the production's specific problems (bad script, poor actors, disastrous set), laughing off any suggestion of delay. She also directed the party to retrieve **Chancellor Geoffrey's CGI device**, a magical invitation-maker stolen by kobolds south of Yartar in the Dessarin Hills. When asked how she knew, she said only: *"I wasn't always an innkeeper."* After opening night succeeded, she told Vardis they had a hit and that audiences wanted to return the next evening.

**The Cindrell/Hyuth Situation (Northshield House):**
Thistle visited Urgala the morning after meeting Cindrell. She described **Hyuth Kolstaag** as a difficult but reliable adventuring-circuit figure — self-centered but dependable in a fight. When Thistle presented the contract and revealed Cindrell had asked the party to kill Hyuth, she was unsurprised. She recounted the assassination attempt and expressed clear disapproval of Hyuth's refusal to compensate the dead stable hand's family, but stopped short of endorsing killing him — saying she was not in a position to judge, given her own past. She suggested that making him "a better neighbor" would be a good thing. As the party left, she muttered that she never imagined Cindrell could be that cold-blooded.

**Recruited for Silverymoon (2nd day, 2nd tenday of Eleint):**
Urgala was approached at her inn. She greeted Lord Zymorven warmly but grew worried upon seeing Orsik's father and the 3HPP together. After hearing the threat to Silverymoon explained, she agreed to join, calling it "one last adventure" and framing Silverymoon's defense as something of a shared tradition.

**Planning session:**
During debates over Kaelen's fate, Urgala cut off the argument by reminding the group that Kaelen's original scheme was the mass murder of all of Triboar — which the 3HPP stopped — and stated flatly that in her opinion he should have been killed then and there.

**The Zymorven Aftermath (battle location):**
Urgala was present at the battle and its aftermath, initially worried she bore some responsibility. She proposed the party flee before spells could reveal the truth, allowing Lord Zymorven's cover story to fill the vacuum — a move Orsik called brilliant. She revealed she is **Lord Zymorven's ex-partner**, their relationship having begun during their shared adventuring days. She expressed long-suppressed satisfaction at seeing Zymorven and Darathra humbled, pledged future assistance to the 3HPP, and had to excuse herself before losing her composure. She engaged in brief suggestive banter with Vardis and Orsik about the nature of Orsik and Vardis's friendship.

**The Oracle (not present):**
The party spent one of their limited Oracle questions on Urgala's wife. The Oracle revealed she fell to her death in a well that led to the Underdark. Thistle found the answer anticlimactic.

- **Active:** Has pledged future assistance to the 3HPP; holds a map to an unexplored mine near where her wife died
