# Rebecca Deakin

## Identity
- **Role:** Archivist and scholar employed by Wyrmworks (cataloging department), specializing in dragon-related topics; later serving as **advisor to the Lords of Silverymoon**
- **Faction:** Wyrmworks; appears sympathetic to anti-Spellguard, anti-mage sentiment
- **First Appearance:** Thistle sought her out in Silverymoon as a paid researcher on Adimond Kroskas (fee: 1,800 gp)

## Personality & Motivations
- Possesses deep, specialized expertise in dragons and dragon-related lore — described as having unparalleled intellect on the subject
- Holds strong ideological views: distrusts magic users and considers the Spellguard a "crypto-fascist tool of the mages and a force of control," believing non-magic users are treated as lesser people
- Caught herself speaking too freely about these views, suggesting she is aware of the political risk of her opinions
- Skeptical and challenging toward the party — questions their credibility even while having previously worked for them

## History with the Party

1. **First Contact:** Thistle hired Rebecca at Wyrmworks in Silverymoon to research Adimond Kroskas for 1,800 gp. She agreed and asked the party to return in one week.

2. **Research Delivery:** On the party's return, Rebecca expressed surprise they were still alive, implying the investigation was known to be dangerous. She delivered the following findings:
   - Adimond Kroskas is a powerful spellcaster capable of transforming Drakes from Dragon eggs (distinguishing pure Drakes from lesser Drakes)
   - He was hired by the **Cult of Tiamat** to develop a **fay-dragon drake hybrid**
   - A man claiming to be **Thistle's Uncle Joon** had contacted her and relayed that Adimond entered **the Fae** to obtain the **First Flower of Spring** as a component for his spell research
   - Fairies may be involved as test subjects, components, or "treats"
   - She suspects Adimond Kroskas **may himself be a dragon** — the Cult of the Dragon referred to him alternately as a dragon and as a dragon's pet
   - During this meeting she also made politically charged comments about the Spellguard before visibly catching herself

3. **Lords of Silverymoon Rotunda:** Rebecca appeared as an **advisor to the Lords of Silverymoon** during a formal meeting. She challenged the party's credibility openly, calling it "almost absurd" that four adventurers could uncover anything about a legendary dragon of unsurpassed power, and implied they might be behind the events themselves. She was not persuaded by Orsik's diplomatic counter invoking Everlund, but backed down when political dynamics shifted. (Note: this session also references her being asked to research **Iymrith**, suggesting the party's inquiry had broadened.)

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Silverymoon, present at the Lords' rotunda as an official advisor
- **Active Role:** Advising the Lords of Silverymoon on dragon-related matters
- **Hidden:** The depth of her anti-Spellguard ideology and what she may have shared with Uncle Joon (or others) remains unclear; her prior contact with someone claiming to be Thistle's uncle was never fully explained

## Relationships
- **Thistle:** Her primary contact in the party; hired by Thistle twice. Has a functional working relationship but no evident warmth
- **Orsik:** Directly challenged by him during the rotunda meeting; was not swayed but did not escalate
- **Uncle Joon (claimed):** Was contacted by a man claiming to be Thistle's uncle, who fed her information about Adimond's movements — the legitimacy of this contact is unverified
- **Wyrmworks:** Employer (cataloging department)
- **Lords of Silverymoon:** Now formally advising them
- **The Spellguard:** Openly hostile ideology; views them as instruments of mage oppression
