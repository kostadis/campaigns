# Brunvild

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Frost giant jarl
- **Faction:** None currently confirmed; formerly associated with Blagothkus's forces (against his will, apparently)
- **First Appearance:** Discovered imprisoned and emaciated in a cave within Sandesyl's lair, kept alive as a food source

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Survival and freedom; reclaiming his dignity and position after prolonged captivity
- **Drives:** Deep resentment toward Blagothkus, whom he holds personally responsible for his imprisonment, his men's deaths, and the deaths of dwarves who trusted him
- Brunvild is proud and initially suspicious — he questioned Thistle's size and doubted the party's motives before agreeing to help. He shows a pragmatic willingness to act when persuaded the risk is worth it, and a rare moment of personal reflection when he noted that flying creatures have had an outsized influence on his life.

## History with the Party

**Sandesyl's Lair (Earlier Session)**
Thistle approached him directly and claimed Harshnag sent them to free him. After initial skepticism, Brunvild agreed to serve as a distraction, escaping through the southern tunnel to draw guard drakes and other minions away from the lair. Before departing, he shared cryptic remarks about flying creatures affecting his fate — first a Kenku, then Thistle — stunning Thistle, as Kenkus do not fly and the tale of a flying Kenku defeating the Cult of the Dragon is a prisoner legend. He lumbered out through the southern tunnel as planned.

**The Flying Castle — Confrontation with Blagothkus (Later Session)**
Brunvild was present aboard the flying castle during the party's confrontation with Blagothkus. He informed Escarlotta of the true account of how Blagothkus reacquired the castle — deals with dragons and a vampire — triggering her rage. He echoed her accusation ("And a vampire!") and ultimately delivered the killing blow to Blagothkus using one of the cloud giant's own hammers. Afterward, he attempted to claim the castle on salvage rights, but backed down when Orsik moved to fight him and Escarlotta asserted ownership. He retreated deeper into the castle.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Somewhere aboard the flying castle, having retreated after his salvage claim was refused
- **Active Plans:** No confirmed agenda beyond finding somewhere to sleep aboard the castle; his broader goals are unknown
- **What the Party Knows:** That he is a frost giant jarl, that he despises Blagothkus, that he assisted in killing Blagothkus, and that he has some mysterious prior connection to a flying Kenku
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full story of his history with Blagothkus; the nature of his encounter with the alleged flying Kenku; what he intends to do now that Blagothkus is dead and he is free

## Relationships
- **Blagothkus:** Deep hatred. Holds Blagothkus responsible for his captivity, his men's deaths, and the deaths of dwarves who trusted him. Killed Blagothkus with his own hammer.
- **Escarlotta:** Aligned with her rage against Blagothkus during the confrontation; deferred to her claim on the castle when pressed.
- **Orsik:** Backed down from a direct challenge when Orsik appeared ready to fight him over the castle.
- **Thistle:** Warily cooperative. Agreed to help after Thistle's persuasion. Noted meaningfully that Thistle, like a Kenku before her, is a flying creature who has changed the course of his life.
- **Harshnag:** Brunvild did not reject the claim that Harshnag sent the party, suggesting some existing respect or familiarity.
- **The Mysterious Kenku:** An unnamed flying Kenku Brunvild claims to have personally encountered — connected to a legend known among prisoners about defeating the Cult of the Dragon.
