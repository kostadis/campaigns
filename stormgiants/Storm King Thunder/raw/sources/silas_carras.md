# Silas Carras

## Identity
- **Role:** Retired resident of Parnast; widower of the town's herbalist
- **Faction:** None apparent
- **First Appearance:** The party visited his home seeking antitoxin for Unla Kee; he was already aware of their reputation as the Defenders of Parnast

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Preserving his late wife's memory and garden; correcting the persistent misconception that he is the herbalist
- Warm and welcoming, with a dry sense of humor (joking about "wakeup sauce" for Unla Kee)
- Visibly protective of his wife's legacy — the trampled bushes genuinely upset him
- Underestimates others; he expected the puzzle-locked garden to stump the party far longer than it did

## History with the Party
1. Greeted the party at his home, acknowledging their reputation; deflected herbalist questions by explaining (not for the first time) that his deceased wife held that expertise
2. Described his wife's nested-puzzle system hidden in the garden layout — her method of gatekeeping herbs from those who wouldn't pay
3. Was visibly surprised when Unla Kee quickly solved the puzzle and produced the antidote
4. Raised an unprompted concern about recently trampled garden bushes, which led Thistle to track a trail from the garden directly to Gundalin Cartwright's front door

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** His home and garden in Parnast
- **Active Concerns:** Damage to his late wife's garden; unaware of what, if anything, Gundalin Cartwright's connection to the trampling means
- **What the Party Knows:** He is not an herbalist; his wife was, and she built the garden's puzzle system
- **What Remains Hidden:** No information withheld from the party is noted in current records

## Relationships
- **Deceased Wife:** Deeply devoted to her memory; maintains her garden as a tribute; she was the herbalist and puzzle-builder
- **Gundalin Cartwright:** Unknowing neighbor; the trampled bush trail leads to Gundalin's door — Silas appears unaware of any direct connection
- **The Party:** Friendly and cooperative; amused by but respectful of them
- **Unla Kee (specifically):** Subject of his opening joke; impressed by her speed in solving the garden puzzle
