# Queen Neri

## Identity
- **Role:** Queen of the Storm Giants; wife of King Hekaton; mother of Serissa, Mirren, and Nym
- **Status:** Deceased (murdered prior to campaign events; assassination per Thistle's conspiracy theory orchestrated by Iymrith)
- **First Appearance:** Never appeared in play; introduced posthumously through NPC exposition

*Also known as: Queen Nerissa, Neri, Nerissa.*

## Personality & Motivations
- Core drive: fostering alliance and goodwill between giants and the small folk (humans and other humanoids)
- Repeatedly urged Hekaton to respect the small folk and seek alliance rather than conflict
- Known to visit the Sword Coast regularly, rising from the sea to meet humans on shore — an act of unusual humility for a Storm Giant queen

## History with the Party
- **The Imperator's account:** Described Neri's philosophy and her practice of visiting small folk settlements, framing her as a moderating influence on Hekaton.
- **Interrogation aboard the Grand Dame:** Orsik relayed Neri's backstory to Lord Drylund, connecting her murder to the Golden Goose gambling chit found on her assassins and establishing her death as the catalyst for the current crisis.
- **Thistle's conspiracy theory:** Implicated Iymrith as the orchestrator of Neri's assassination. According to Thistle, Iymrith arranged the murder hoping to provoke Hekaton into war against smaller peoples — a conflict Iymrith intended to exploit. The plan was thwarted when Serissa persuaded Hekaton to restrain himself.

## Current Status
- Deceased. Killed during a visit to a small island on the Sword Coast where she regularly met with humans.
- Her assassins were found dead alongside her; a Golden Goose gambling chit from Yartar was found on the bodies.

## Relationships
- **King Hekaton (husband):** Her death shattered him, triggering grief, a near-declaration of war on coastal territories, and ultimately his disappearance while investigating her fate.
- **Serissa (daughter):** Has taken up her mother's philosophy of alliance with the small folk.
- **Mirren & Nym (daughters):** Referenced as her children; no further detail in notes.
- **The Small Folk:** Championed their welfare and sought alliance with them; ironically killed by them.

## Arc Score Events
- N/A — NPC is deceased and has no active arc.

*Note for GM: Neri functions as the moral and narrative origin point of the campaign's giant crisis. Her death is the wound everything else radiates from. The Golden Goose chit is the key evidence thread connecting her murder to Yartar.*
