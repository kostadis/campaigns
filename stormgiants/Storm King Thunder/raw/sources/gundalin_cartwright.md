# Gundalin Cartwright

## Identity
- **Role:** Civilian resident of Parnast / cartwright by trade; also functions as a Parnast militia member; suspected saboteur
- **Faction:** Unknown; politically opposed to Ragnar's leadership; loosely aligned with the Parnast militia
- **First Appearance:** Introduced in two registers — (a) as a named saboteur suspect during Ragnar's briefing to the party, (b) physically present inside the Golden Tankard alongside Raggnar and Chandra when the party arrived

*Also known as: Gundalin.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core motivations:** Defend Parnast and hold outside powers accountable for the town's suffering. Underlying motive around the sabotage allegations is unclear — Ragnar frames him as an opportunist or agent working against Parnast's defenses.
- Combative and vocal, quick to shout defiance or alarm in high-pressure moments.
- Follows Raggnar's lead in defense matters but is capable of forming his own positions when given information.
- Known to openly challenge Ragnar's authority and has rallied "cronies" to question his decisions daily — blames Ragnar for the Cult of the Dragon's involvement in Parnast's troubles.
- After the battle, showed a willingness to shift sympathies based on what he learned from Wallace and Orsik about the SEER and the Lord's Alliance.

## History with the Party

1. **Ragnar's accusation:** Ragnar names Gundalin as his primary suspect in the sabotage campaign: the disappearing tools, the watchtower collapse, and a broken cart axle — the last of which Gundalin personally repaired (and may have tampered with). The party had not yet spoken to him directly at this point.

2. **Footprint trail:** Thistle follows footprints from the trampled bushes in Silas Carras' garden; the trail leads directly to Gundalin's front door, implicating him in whatever occurred there.

3. **First in-person meeting:** Was waiting at the Golden Tankard when the party arrived; went outside expecting an orc horde but found nothing. No direct interaction recorded.

4. **Bad Fruul's ultimatum:** Shouted defiance alongside Raggnar, refusing to surrender any townsperson to Bad Fruul's demands.

5. **Wyvern plan:** Reacted with alarm to Orsik's plan to release the wyvern, calling it "a desperate ploy of a madman."

6. **Post-battle:** Was initially on the verge of siding with Raggnar in accusing the party. Participated in arguments about rebuilding. Later, while pursuing Orsik out of breath, he articulated a sympathetic position — blaming the SEER and the Lord's Alliance for the machinations that caused death and destruction in Parnast, drawing on what he understood from Wallace and Orsik.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Parnast, post-battle
- **Active Situation:** Footprints connecting him to Silas Carras' garden have been discovered but not yet acted upon (the unresolved sabotage suspicion); skeptical of the SEER and Lord's Alliance; cautiously sympathetic to Orsik's perspective
- **Party Knowledge:** He is the primary saboteur suspect; circumstantial evidence (the cart axle, the footprints); after the battle he shifted toward a more nuanced, sympathetic position
- **Hidden:** His actual motive for any sabotage; whether he is acting alone or on behalf of a faction; the depth of his post-battle conviction and whether the shift is lasting or situational

## Relationships
- **Ragnar (Town Commander / Redtooth):** Open political adversary; publicly undermines Ragnar's decisions and blames him for the Cult of the Dragon situation. Yet stood with him during Bad Fruul's ultimatum.
- **Wallace Cartwright (Son):** Loyal and disbelieving — reacted with sharp skepticism when told his father was a suspect (*"Right, my father is doing his best to betray the town so that Orcs will take over? Really?"*). Cited by Gundalin as a source for his post-battle views on the SEER and Lord's Alliance.
- **Silas Carras:** Connected circumstantially by the footprint trail leading from Carras' garden to Gundalin's door.
- **Orsik:** Chased him after the battle, but expressed understanding of Orsik's distrust of outside powers.
- **Raggnar:** Close ally in the militia; follows his lead, though capable of diverging.
- **The Party:** Initially near-hostile post-battle; current disposition uncertain but less adversarial than Raggnar; have circumstantial evidence against him but have not confronted or questioned him directly.

## Arc Score Events
- **Slight positive shift** post-battle: broke from Raggnar's accusatory stance and voiced sympathy for Orsik's position, suggesting emerging distrust of the party's institutional backers rather than the party itself.
