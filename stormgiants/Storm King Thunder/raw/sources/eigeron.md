# Eigeron

## Identity
- **Role:** Deceased son of Blagothkus (Cloud Giant) and Escarlotta; ghost/spirit trapped within the Eye of the All-Father
- **Faction:** None (deceased); formerly of interest to Iymrith as a potential pawn
- **First Appearance:** Referenced before being encountered directly; the party met his ghost inside the Eye of the All-Father oracle

## Personality & Motivations
- Consumed by rage and grief over his murder at his father's hands; his primary drive is revenge against Blagothkus
- In death he has become volatile and dangerous — upon learning Blagothkus still lives and his castle is nearby, he went mad and attempted to possess a party member
- His spirit remains trapped in the Eye of the All-Father, howling for vengeance

## History with the Party

1. **Before the party's involvement:** Eigeron visited the Eye of the All-Father with his father, Blagothkus. The oracle urged him to step out of his father's shadow. This triggered a confrontation in which Blagothkus slew him — apparently killing him rather than allowing the ordeal's intended outcome. His death caused Amn to declare Blagothkus unworthy. Iymrith had originally intended to use Eigeron as part of a scheme to start a Giant war; his death by his father's hand derailed that plan.

2. **After death (Orsik's recollection):** Eigeron was subsequently killed a second time by the First Faction while Blagothkus was away in the Spine of the World. He asked the heroes to avenge his death by killing Blagothkus.

3. **Encounter at the Eye of the All-Father:** The party met Eigeron's ghost inside the oracle. He asked about his father. Upon learning Blagothkus was alive and his castle was present, he went mad and attempted to possess one of the heroes. Vardis invoked Corellon's power and turned him. The party quickly deposited the artifacts, received the oracle's information, and fled through the portal, leaving Eigeron howling behind them.

4. **The control room confrontation:** Eigeron was not present, but Thistle invoked his name strategically to Escarlotta, implying Blagothkus was responsible for Eigeron's death and ghostly imprisonment. Blagothkus responded that "it was an accident." Escarlotta screamed for Blagothkus' death so that Eigeron could be freed.

5. **Unla Kee's knowledge:** Unla Kee recalls that Eigeron told him precisely where to find Blagothkus's castle, suggesting Eigeron had contact with Unla Kee at some point before his death.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Trapped as a ghost in the Eye of the All-Father, in a turned/howling state when the party last fled
- **Active situation:** Spirit remains imprisoned; his freedom may be contingent on Blagothkus's death (unconfirmed)
- **What the party knows:** He is Blagothkus and Escarlotta's son, killed by his father at the oracle, now a dangerous ghost bent on revenge
- **What remains hidden:** The full mechanics of how his spirit might be freed; whether "it was an accident" holds any truth

## Relationships
- **Blagothkus (father):** Murdered by him at the Eye of the All-Father; Eigeron is consumed with rage toward him
- **Escarlotta (mother):** Escarlotta screamed for Blagothkus's death upon learning of Eigeron's ghostly imprisonment, suggesting she is motivated by freeing him
- **Iymrith:** Had intended to use Eigeron as a pawn in a Giant war scheme; his death forced her to change plans
- **Unla Kee:** Eigeron told him where to find Blagothkus's castle (circumstances unknown)
- **The Party:** Attacked Vardis via attempted possession; Thistle has weaponized his memory in negotiations with Escarlotta
