# Kraddock

## Identity
- **Role:** Storm giant shaman; caretaker and restorer of sacred giant shrines
- **Faction:** Serves the All-Father (chief giant deity); aligned with giant civilization broadly, though not uncritically
- **First Appearance:** Summit shrine in the Greypeak Mountains, after the party defeated the giant owls guarding the site. Kraddock spoke to them through an open-air pipe before revealing himself.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintain and restore sacred giant holy sites; serve the All-Father's will even when he personally disagrees with it
- **Drives:** A deep, quietly sorrowful investment in tolerance between giants and smaller peoples — he mourns the ending of that era alongside the age of dragons
- Kraddock is measured and non-violent despite his terrifying power; he prefers curses and oaths over bloodshed, and chose patient confrontation over force when dealing with Unla Kee's theft
- He is politically aware and openly critical of the coming war in private terms, but remains bound by faith and duty — a giant who disagrees with his god but serves him anyway
- Thistle assessed him as capable of killing the entire party without effort; he did not need to prove it

## History with the Party
1. **First contact at the shrine:** Party arrives at the desecrated summit shrine in the Greypeaks. Kraddock addresses them through a pipe, having traveled there after learning of the desecration days earlier. He outlines his restorative duties: tattered drapes, roof repairs, brazier restocking, fresco restoration, altar polishing, and removal of dead cultists.
2. **Confrontation over the gold plates:** Kraddock stares down Unla Kee over the stolen ritual plates. He listens through several transparent lies without threatening violence, instead stating he would have invoked an All-Father curse rather than kill the party. Unla Kee returns the plates; Kraddock polishes them carefully.
3. **Intelligence briefing:** Kraddock explains the current geopolitical situation — Tiamat's defeat inspired a giant uprising; giants now seek to reclaim Ostoria (historically spanning from the Spine of the World to Amn, Sword Coast to the eastern desert). He warns the party that most of the North is now dangerous giant territory.
4. **King Hekaton:** Mentions the giant king by name, noting Hekaton had advocated more inclusive attitudes toward smaller peoples, influenced significantly by his missing wife. Kraddock implies this tolerant faction is losing influence.
5. **Confronted by Vardis:** When pressed on what he will personally do, Kraddock admits he can only pray — and acknowledges this is effectively useless. As the party departs, he gives them several potions, framing it as "perhaps more than just thoughts and prayers."
6. **Departure:** Declines Unla Kee's invitation to join the party. As they leave, expresses private hope that the All-Father will come to understand the war will bring glory to no one.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Summit shrine, Greypeak Mountains — actively restoring the desecrated site
- **Active Plans:** Shrine restoration; continued service to the All-Father
- **What the party knows:** He is sympathetic to smaller peoples, disagrees with the war, is bound by faith, and gave them potions as a parting gift
- **What remains hidden:** The full extent of his influence (if any) within giant society; whether his private dissent ever becomes action; details about King Hekaton's missing wife beyond the brief mention

## Relationships
- **The All-Father:** Kraddock's divine patron and the object of his service — a relationship defined by duty in tension with personal disagreement
- **King Hekaton:** Knows of him; seems to hold the king's tolerant views in quiet esteem
- **Hekaton's wife:** Mentioned as having influenced the king's inclusive attitudes; her disappearance noted with implied sadness
- **Unla Kee:** Confronted him directly over the stolen plates; did not punish him after restitution, but the interaction was tense — Kraddock's patience was not warmth
- **Vardis:** Pressed Kraddock on his inaction; Kraddock gave an honest and somewhat deflated answer — no apparent hostility, but Vardis clearly struck a nerve
- **Party overall:** Departed on neutral-to-cautiously-positive terms; the potions suggest a degree of goodwill
