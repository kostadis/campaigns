# Lord Zymorven

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Noble lord; patriarch of House Zymorven; owner of Zymorven Hall
- **Faction:** Silverymoon political circles; cultivated ally of the party
- **First Appearance:** Zymorven Hall, when Thistle arrived ahead of the group to deliver news of Harthal's rescue

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protect his family's standing, preserve House Zymorven's reputation, and insulate himself from political and legal blowback at all costs
- A charming, self-possessed aristocrat with a dry sense of humor and genuine warmth — until his interests are threatened, at which point he pivots to calculated self-preservation with practiced ease
- Projects the image of a magnanimous patron while quietly engineering situations to his own advantage; Urgala holds a long-standing negative opinion of him as self-righteous
- Has adventuring history he speaks of nostalgically, though he is careful to keep himself out of danger now

## History with the Party

**Harthal's Rescue (Session ~1–2)**
Thistle arrived at Zymorven Hall alone; Lord Zymorven feared his son Harthal was dead. As Thistle laid out the full story — murder charge, death row, prison break, vampire jailer — he absorbed it with surprising good humor, noting he'd need to avoid the vampire rather than Yartar entirely, and laughed. When Harthal arrived with the rest of the group, Zymorven was visibly delighted. He paid the party their promised reward, called it "a good old fashioned prison break," and winked. Noticing Orsik carrying a family sword, he expressed hope it would bring Orsik better luck than it had brought his son.

**Castle Approach / Bob Saget Crisis**
When a flying castle approached his estate, Zymorven met the party in full armor, scrying-alerted and ready to fight. He recognized Vardis, Unla Kee, and Thistle but was puzzled by an unfamiliar dwarf — Orsic — until the giant slayer sword identified the family connection. Upon learning of Bob Saget's zombie plague threatening Silverymoon, he immediately demanded the city's leaders be warned, but accepted the argument for a quiet strike team. He commented favorably on how different Orsic seemed from his son Orsik. Learning of a nearby vampire *and* a Dracolich, he became visibly alarmed and insisted the castle be repositioned away from Silverymoon to prevent vampiric infestation. In planning, he argued against killing Kaelen in favor of interrogation and was partially overruled. He offered to handle all public messaging and ensure the party received credit for their heroic sacrifice.

**The Theater Battle & Aftermath**
Zymorven was present at the battle. When Silverymoon citizens were killed in a bag-of-holding explosion, he was momentarily outraged. Afterward, he methodically shifted all operational responsibility onto Orsik, stating Orsik had acted alone without consulting him and that he would have made different choices. He declared all debts between their families cleared. He then constructed and insisted upon a formal cover story: Bob used fire spells in the theater, caused the conflagration, the party fought valiantly but could not save the theatergoers, and Bob was a demon who has been defeated. He noted carefully — and pointedly — that *there is no evidence Bob was a demon that could hold up in a court of law*, further insulating himself legally. When 200+ prominent Silverymoon citizens were confirmed missing, he expressed alarm at the social implications. When the cover story conversation briefly touched on blaming non-human races, he quietly slipped out of the room. He departed the theater before it was set ablaze.

**Referenced by Lord Drylund**
Drylund mentioned that Zymorven's son Harthal had been among nobles who disappeared and was freed by adventurers — an event reported in the papers, described by Drylund as "a terrible blow for the Waterbaron." Noble kidnappings appear connected to political maneuvering around the Waterbaron election.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Departed the theater before it was burned; whereabouts after that unknown
- **Active Operations:** Managing public messaging around the theater disaster; enforcing the agreed-upon cover story
- **What the Party Knows:** He has distanced himself from the operation, formally absolved himself of responsibility, and declared his debts to the party cleared
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent of his political maneuvering; whether he is pursuing any agenda connected to the Waterbaron election or the noble disappearances

## Relationships
- **Harthal Zymorven (son):** Loves him; was genuinely distressed at the thought of his death; paid the party handsomely for his rescue
- **Orsik:** Gifted Orsik a family sword; after the battle, formally severed the sense of obligation between their families and shifted blame onto him
- **Orsic (Orsik's father):** Met briefly; commented favorably on how different Orsic seems from Orsik
- **Urgala:** She holds a long-standing negative view of him as self-righteous; he was overruled on the Kaelen question partly at her urging
- **Thistle, Vardis, Unla Kee:** Recognized as known allies; cultivated as a relationship by the party
- **Lord Drylund:** Aware of the Harthal rescue; views the event through a political lens tied to the Waterbaron election
- **The Waterbaron:** Zymorven's family and political standing appear entangled in the broader Waterbaron election conflict

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase:** Paid the party in full and with good humor after Harthal's rescue; offered to champion the party publicly in Silverymoon; showed genuine warmth toward returning adventurers
- **Decrease:** After the theater battle, deliberately and formally shifted all blame onto Orsik; declared debts cleared in a manner that functioned as a dismissal; constructed a self-protective legal cover story while insulating himself from consequences the party must live with
