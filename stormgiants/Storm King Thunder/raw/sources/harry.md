# Harry

## Identity
- Role: Wild giant elk; no faction affiliation
- First appearance: Day 5, 2nd tenday of Eleasis, at Flint Rock. Met when Thistle approached him and his mate Mary using *speak with animals* while the party was conducting an excavation.

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goal: To be left alone to graze in peace with Mary.
- Vocally atheistic and politically opinionated — dismisses the Feywild and fae religion as tools of oppression used to keep giant elks subservient. Has a particular grudge against neighboring giant elks called "the Joneses," whom he accuses of acting superior. Generally irritable toward bipedal creatures, whom he blames for stealing grass.
- Gruff and cantankerous, but not unreasonable — willing to cooperate once given a satisfactory reason to do so.

## History with the Party
- When the party arrived at Flint Rock, Harry and Mary were grazing nearby. Thistle used *speak with animals* to open dialogue.
- Harry was hostile and complaining but was persuaded by Thistle that the party had a right to be there.
- Harry and Mary physically pushed the stone aside to assist with the excavation.
- Upon the party's departure, Harry expressed relief that they were finally leaving.

## Current Status
- Last known location: Flint Rock, grazing with Mary.
- Last known action: Expressing relief at the party's departure.
- No known active plans beyond resuming peaceful grazing.
- **Party knows:** His name, his mate Mary, his atheism, his general grievances. **Hidden:** Nothing of note — Harry is transparently what he appears to be.

## Relationships
- **Mary:** His mate; the two act as a unit.
- **The Joneses:** Neighboring giant elks Harry resents for perceived arrogance.
- **Thistle:** The party member who negotiated with him directly; Harry responded to her persuasion.
- **The party broadly:** Tolerated, barely. Categorized alongside all bipedal creatures as grass thieves.
