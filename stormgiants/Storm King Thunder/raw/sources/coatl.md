# The Coatl

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Divine serpentine spirit, bound to or associated with a tribal totem
- **Faction:** Unknown — appears to act as a spiritual guardian or judge of the local tribe
- **First Appearance:** Emerged from a totem as Orsik passed it; approached the 3HPP with a task

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** The destruction of Wormblod, whom it believes has perverted the tribe
- **Drives:** Restoring spiritual or moral order to the tribe; offering boons in exchange for righteous acts
- The Coatl carries itself with an expectation of reverence and gratitude, and is visibly wounded when that deference is not offered. It is quick to reward but clearly accustomed to being appreciated for doing so. Its displeasure manifests in small, unsettling physical tics — bulging eyes, tongue-flicking — rather than outright anger.

## History with the Party
1. **First Contact:** The Coatl appeared from a totem as Orsik passed, demanding the party destroy Wormblod in exchange for a boon.
2. **Boon Granted:** When Thistle claimed the party had already killed Wormblod, the Coatl accepted this and granted every party member a **Charm of Restoration**.
3. **Awkward Exit:** The party's lukewarm response to the gift visibly irritated the Coatl. It complained, *"Usually, they appreciate me more,"* and slithered away muttering, tongue flicking.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The totem site
- **Active Plans:** Unknown — its immediate task (rewarding the party) is complete
- **What the Party Knows:** It wanted Wormblod dead and rewarded the claim that he was. It expects gratitude.
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether it can verify Wormblod is truly dead; what consequences follow if he is not; the full extent of its power or divine authority

## Relationships
- **Wormblod:** Views him as a corruptor of the tribe — this appears to be a deep grievance
- **The 3HPP:** Transactional at best. The Coatl fulfilled its end of the bargain but left dissatisfied with the party's reception
- **Thistle specifically:** The Coatl dealt directly with Thistle's claim, making Thistle the de facto point of contact — and the one most responsible if the claim proves false
