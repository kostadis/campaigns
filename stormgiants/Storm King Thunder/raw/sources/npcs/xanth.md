# Xanth

## Identity
- **Role:** Wilderness guide, regional informant; affiliated with (though frustrated by) the Emerald Enclave
- **Species:** Centaur (described elsewhere as an elder/emissary among "his people")
- **First appearance:** Encountered peacefully near Dragonbarrow; warned the party about will-o'-wisps and offered to guide them to the Anchorites of Talos

*Also known as: Xanthopoulos.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Protecting the natural world; resolving the orc displacement crisis that the Emerald Enclave has failed to address; honoring old friendships (notably with Meryl/Meril)
- Pragmatic and action-oriented — deeply frustrated by institutional paralysis and the Enclave's inability to reach consensus
- Warm but has clear limits: accepted carrying Vukradin and Valphine on his back with visible reluctance, framing the concession explicitly as a favor to Soma, not Vukradin
- Treats near-death and logistical inconvenience with equal equanimity; greets crises like administrative problems to be solved
- Standing enormous once restored to health, he greets the party with the enthusiasm of someone whose near-death had been a brief administrative inconvenience
- Finds city dwellers' failure to understand animal communication networks a form of willful blindness
- Deliberate in the way of very old creatures — considered, unhurried, precise. Carries no banner and aligns with no faction; positions himself as a neutral observer and truth-teller. Has watched both pure interventionism and pure naturalism become follies when held too tightly. Commands quiet authority and speaks plainly
- Patient traveler — described with hooves in the dirt like he had been there a long time without minding it
- Describes the forest's history and politics in broad, systemic terms; frames issues theologically when appropriate but without personal investment in doctrine

## History with the Party

**Early contact (near Dragonbarrow):**
Xanth encountered the party near Dragonbarrow — startling Vukradin, who had never seen a centaur and considered it an abomination. Soma calmed the situation by explaining centaurs were woodland creatures and not a threat. Xanth warned the party about strange will-o'-wisps that appear over Dragonbarrow at night, describing the hill as haunted by restless spirits of the dead, and stated he only visits during daylight hours. He also informed the party that Neverwinter Wood has been overrun by orcs allied with half-orc spellcasters — the Anchorites of Talos — who perform dark rites at a circle of standing stones atop a cave-ridden hill, and confirmed these were the same group that previously tried to kill the party. He offered to guide the party to the Anchorites after their business at Dragonbarrow was complete, framing it as conflict-free treasure. He recognized Valphine as a famous cleric of Lathander and praised her courage, commenting on the idea of pain as purification. He awaited the party's return from the tomb.

**Outside the tomb (brief appearance):**
Found wounded and bloodied in the fading light outside the tomb chamber as the party emerged, standing in the clearing as the sun dipped toward the horizon. Did not speak. Status: injured but present.

**After being healed by Soma:**
Soma healed Xanth following a battle, after which he rose to his full, formidable size and immediately greeted the party with energy. He named Vukradin by the Centaurian equivalent "Vucravinios." He recognized **Meryl's Staff** in Soma's possession at once — identifying it as a powerful object that once had far more leaves than its current single surviving one — and stated that Meryl was an old friend. He noted that Meryl must know Soma to have left the staff with her. He asked Soma directly what she wanted, treating his assistance as an extension of that friendship. Soma's answer — that she had no grand ambitions, only to keep the world safer and ensure everyone had a place to live — satisfied him as correct.

He explained the regional crisis in full: a white dragon descended into the Icespire Mountains, displaced the orcs, who then pushed into inhabited valleys, creating a cascade of emergencies. He found Vukradin's reluctance to claim a dragon's hoard genuinely baffling and looked to Soma for interpretation. He described the Emerald Enclave's internal paralysis over the orc situation, explaining that factions accuse each other of everything and fail to agree on whether the orcs represent a naturalist phenomenon or an intervention requiring action. He found immediate common ground with Soma on this point. He confirmed Borg's death through animal communication networks and named the Carver's known lieutenants: **Borg, Sister Kaella, and Zax.**

He agreed to carry Vukradin and Valphine to the foothills near the gold mine, on the explicit condition that he not be asked to enter mountain terrain. He was not entirely reconciled to being asked to serve as transport and noted pointedly that Vukradin was asking him to be a horse. He accepted the arrangement — on Soma's account rather than Vukradin's.

**Druid Council session:**
Arrived unexpectedly at the council and immediately recognized **Meril's Staff** in Soma's possession. Told the council he understood the significance of Meril choosing Soma — *"He did not give it for sentiment. He gave it because he… picked her."* His recognition of the staff visibly affected Soma. Stated explicitly to the council that he represents neither faction — only reporting what his people witnessed. Delivered critical intelligence: **Corbin is dead**, and **Sister Kayla has walked out and disappeared** ("gone to the wind"). Upon departing, told Soma quietly: *"Perhaps Meril made the right choice."* Soma took this remark to heart and kept it carefully.

**Rendezvous on the Triboar Trail and journey to the Circle of Thunder:**
Met the party at the bend of the Triboar Trail toward Phandalin, where Vukradin's Sending spell had directed them. He was already present when they arrived, described as having hooves in the dirt like he had been there a long time without minding it. He greeted them warmly, leading with praise for Soma specifically — "Soma, you were superb" — and expressed that Meril had been very interested in the party's recent activities, though he did not elaborate on what Meril's interest entailed.

He guided the party through the forest over approximately thirty miles on the first day, scouting and selecting a campsite when the light thinned.

Around the campfire that evening, Xanth delivered an extended account of the forest's history and current situation when Vukradin asked for stories. He described the pre-dragon balance of the forest as not peaceful but stable, and explained how Cryovain's winter had broken that equilibrium, scattering animals and radicalizing orcs who had grown frightened. He characterized the new Talosian orcs as "fundamentally more evil" and "fundamentally more dangerous than the typical orcs," using language Valphine internally identified as theological rather than purely moral.

When asked how the orcs had become more dangerous, Xanth named the Cult of Talos as the answer. He explained Talos as a deity of storms and destruction whose presence is not localized but lives "in the breast of all those who seek the power to destroy." He described the cult historically as small, unstable, self-destructive, and outlawed by all civil societies. He attributed Adabra's view — that Talos represents a necessary element of cosmic distraction — and then immediately rejected it as "nonsense," characterizing Talos as "a lunatic of a god" who empowers people whose violence is rooted in "fantasies of destruction."

On the question of a new Chosen of Talos, Xanth confirmed there was most likely one — "There is always a chosen, there's always some cleric" — but said the individual was not in this forest, or he would know who it was and would take the party there, adding as a joke that they would probably die but would at least learn something. He estimated the lair of the half-orc spellcasters was another day's hike from the campsite.

When the party encountered the cursed clearing with the burned pine and the bundled effigies, Vukradin asked Xanth whether the dolls were typical Cult of Talos. Xanth declined the implied expertise: "I am not a cultist of Talos. I don't know what is popular and what is not popular," then added he knew more about them than that, but not much.

**Departure (from the gully)**
Before leaving, stood before the group and looked at Soma's staff with recognition — not admiration but memory, the look of someone confirming something they already knew. Tapped Soma on the shell, told her the staff was blessed by Meril, and stated his belief that she would do great things. His parting words were "Peace, Beauty." He then looked at the staff one final time — a look described as measuring, or saying goodbye to it, or confirming something for himself — and departed into the trees. Vukradin broke the resulting awkward silence by appreciating the phrase "hooves off" as Xanth did exactly that. Soma watched until the trees took him.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Departed the gully, direction unspecified
- **Active:** Gone; has left the party's immediate company
- **Condition:** Healthy at time of departure

**What the party knows:** His connection to Meril/Meryl, his frustration with the Emerald Enclave, his knowledge of regional threats and animal communication networks, the names of the Carver's lieutenants, his understanding of the white dragon displacement crisis (including how Cryovain's arrival broke the forest's stable equilibrium and radicalized the orcs), his explicit neutrality at the druid council, his network of "his people" who serve as witnesses and observers, his theological knowledge of Talos and rejection of Adabra's view of the god, that Meril has been very interested in the party's recent activities, and that there is most likely a new Chosen of Talos (but not in this forest).
