# Soma

## Identity
Tortle druid of Mieliki; member of the Emerald Enclave (naturalist faction); currently serving as the party's paid guide, with Vukradin's tab growing. Carries a staff given to her by her master Meril — a staff with three leaves that responded subtly to whatever presence saturates the air at the Circle of Thunder, and which has since grown a fourth bud.

*Also known as: (none).*

## Personality & Motivations
- Core goals: following the road alongside Vukradin's group (for now), practicing her druidic calling, and deepening her training — she is aware her master Meril gave her only a year before sending her off, and that her formal education is incomplete
- Pragmatic and unsentimental about gold, violence, and moral posturing, but genuinely invested in protecting life and acting with purpose; she will kill bandits without hesitation while also shouting "Let him go" at a fleeing one
- Dry and sardonic in conversation — she mocks freely, pushes back on idealism, and cuts through bureaucratic or philosophical tangles with blunt practicality
- Identifies instinctively as a naturalist within the Emerald Enclave schism, holds quiet contempt for magical constructs and unnatural creations, and keeps certain opinions (e.g., about gnomish inventions) to herself when it serves the group
- Tactically sharp and willing to correct others mid-combat to improve outcomes
- Actively wrestling with the weight of being chosen by Meril — Xanth's declaration that she is "blessed by Meril" and will "do great things" has left her arguing with herself in the dark hours about what this means and why Meril chose her on purpose

## History with the Party

**1/1 Taraksh — Outside Neverwinter**
Met the party and offered to guide them to Phandalin. Possessed knowledge of a white dragon rumor in the area that she deliberately withheld.

**8/1 Taraksh — High Road and Triboar Trail**
Guided the party safely, described Phandalin and its people, produced a town map, and shared rumors about the Lionshield Coster's disrupted supply network.

**9/1 Taraksh — Dwarven Ruins**
Examined the ruins, noted the thorough ransacking and the ominous Abbathor temple facade. Interrupted Vukradin's moral objections to keep negotiations with the dwarves on track. In combat: dragged an orc off a bluff with Thorn Whip, killed another with Poison Spray, and accidentally knocked out Valphine with a miscast Ice Knife. Rushed to heal Valphine afterward and recommended the party rest. Retrieved an unidentified item from the roadside ogre's corpse; Vukradin chose not to acknowledge it.

**10/1 Taraksh — Road Back to Phandalin (Ogre Encounter)**
Muttered "The kid is going to get us killed" as Vukradin approached the ogre. Used Poison Spray against it. Described the party to Brewbarry as "not a well-oiled machine yet."

**1/2 Taraksh — Sword Mountains Approach to Gnomengarde**
Led the expedition through broken hilly terrain. When a second ogre blocked a narrow valley path and declared it wanted to eat turtle, she shouted that the party had killed its brother — enraging it — then poisoned it and withdrew.

**Gnomengarde**
Located the river leading to the hold. Mocked Valphine's missed mace swing. Followed Vukradin through a waterfall searching for a nonexistent secret room; got soaked; eventually pressured him to leave. Retrieved a rope for Valphine at the cliff entrance rather than leaving her behind. At the guard post, challenged Pog and Ulla's shapechanger logic, then muttered that more senseless violence was coming before the standoff resolved. In the inventors' workshop, attempted to explain the value of clerical magic but was ignored. During negotiations with King Gnercli, convinced Vukradin to accept the hat of wizardry deal by reminding him Harbin Wester needed a device, not necessarily a useful one. In the cellar fight, her Poison Spray failed against the mimic before she landed the killing blow. Expressed disgust at the mimic as an unnatural creation and held her tongue when the gnome inventors discussed harvesting its entrails.

**Road from Gnomengarde — Bandit Ambush**
Took gold from the gnomes' chests without apology. Wild-shaped into a spider, climbed to engage a bandit, and bit and poisoned him to death. Attempted to spare a fleeing spider-phobic bandit by shouting "Let him go" — forgetting she was in spider form. Found a druidic scroll among the bandits' belongings; Vukradin challenged her claim to it, prompting her to question whether keeping it made her no better than a bandit.

**Umbrage Hill — Adabra and the Manticore**
Opened the session in spider form, reverting to tortle and startling Corbin. Pushed back against Vukradin's suggestion to extort more money from Phandalin. In combat, conjured a familiar and sent it into the manticore's mouth as a distraction — this enraged rather than subdued the creature. Was visibly hurt when Vukradin blamed her for his spell's failure. Soothed both Adabra and Corbin after the battle. When Adabra asked about the Emerald Enclave schism, Soma instinctively identified as a naturalist, dramatically improving Adabra's attitude toward the group. Was stung by Adabra's casual criticism of gaps in her druidic training. Listened to Corbin's full account of his destroyed family and descent into banditry. The following morning, used Adabra's naturalist convictions to convince her to travel to Phandalin.

**Road to Phandalin — Orc Ambush**
Transformed into a bear to scout, left her clutch behind. Identified a concealed orc making hand signals and recognized the clearing beyond as an ambush site. When Brewbarry gave away the party's position, climbed a tree and attacked the scout — biting his foot, taking an arrow in return. Leaped from a branch to shove the scout toward the other orcs. Cast Moonbeam (to Valphine's contempt). Clamped her jaws on the orc wearing a necklace of bones, mortally wounding him. Brewbarry questioned her afterward about attacking a fleeing creature, implying inconsistency with earlier behavior.

**Gully Battle — Anchorite of Talos**
Entered Wild Shape as a giant elk early in the fight, charging and ramming the anchorite of Talos prone into the dirt. Absorbed significant damage in elk form throughout the fight, taking javelin hits from neutral orcs who reconsidered their neutrality and attacks from multiple combatants. Corrected Brewbarry mid-fight on the tactical advantage of attacking a prone target, which allowed Valphine to roll again and land the killing blow on the anchorite. When she had taken enough hits and the tactical situation shifted, deliberately disengaged, accepting attacks of opportunity as she withdrew, dropped Wild Shape, and mounted Boney to remove both herself and the skeletal horse from the immediate melee — reasoning: get the abomination and the delicious meat away from the guys. Witnessed Xanth's departure; the centaur tapped her shell, named her as one blessed by Meril, and stated his belief she would do great things before leaving. She received this without visible response but is actively processing the weight of it in the dark hours before dawn — the staff's three leaves, Xanth's certainty, the knowledge that Meril chose her on purpose.

**Treeline Below Circle of Thunder**
Perceived the staff respond to the presence of whatever was saturating the air above — a subtle shift, described as a dormant quality waking. She connected this internally to Meril, though did not complete the thought aloud. Is aware that whatever drew Meril to this region and whatever rot exists at the summit are directly related to why she is carrying the staff. Engaged in an argument with Vukradin about the necklace of bones — she wants to use it, he refuses to trust her with it. Assessed the tactical situation at the henge, noting the twig blights were not clustered enough for Fireball.

**Circle of Thunder — Assault on the Henge**
Participated in the assault. Took a lightning bolt hit during the battle alongside Valphine. Transformed into an elk and charged one of the paralyzed anchorites, striking him with significant force. Then turned and struck one of the standing stones, apparently acting on the understanding that the henge itself was corrupted and needed disrupting. After the battle, examined the site as a druid, identifying it as a borrowed druidic site — a henge with an original sacred purpose that had been gradually overlaid with Talosian ritual. Inspected the sacrificed deer at the center of the henge, noting it had been split throat to belly and its heart replaced with a hollow of black, glassy, faintly humming material. She touched it briefly and pulled back. Observed the ideological argument between Vukradin and Prutha over the holy symbols without intervening. Witnessed Prutha's quiet statement about his great-uncle's saying — "dawn comes for everyone, even these ones" — and noted his apparent emotional weight in that moment, distinguishing it from performative grief. Noticed her staff had stopped trembling during the fight — it had been vibrating since a previous cursed clearing — and discovered a new fourth bud near the crown of the staff, tight and green, apparently generated by the encounter. She interpreted this as the staff registering something counted or completed. She remained with this observation quietly before the group moved to the caves.

**Circle of Thunder — Cave Entrance**
Did not enter the caves, remaining outside with Boney because the tunnel mouth was too narrow for an elk form. Currently standing watch at the cave entrance.
