# Laela

## Identity
- **Role/Nature:** Fairy (fey creature) with draconic capabilities; Iymrith's captive and experimental subject; Thistle's beloved
- **Faction:** None established; independent
- **First Appearance:** Never physically present; first referenced by the Seer as Unla Kee's beloved, then established as Thistle's lost love through his inquiries in Mirabar

*Also known as: Leela, Leala.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core drives:** Unclear beyond survival and reunion with Thistle; appears to act on personal loyalty and strong dislikes (notably elves)
- Fiercely independent and difficult to coerce — she has maintained a state of "fey meditation" (fey-fullness) purely to deny Iymrith what she wants
- Personality is fully fey in temperament: squeaky-voiced, opinionated, petty grievances (wingmuffs), simultaneously relieved and scolding when hearing from Thistle
- Vardis observed that her claim to have been *looking* for Thistle contradicts the framing of her as a passive captive, suggesting she retains meaningful agency even in captivity

## History with the Party

1. **Pre-campaign:** Laela was Thistle's "dearest" and lost love; her disappearance is the subject of his dream-time and ongoing search. She was originally captured alongside Thistle and Thuul by a cult; when the cult disintegrated, Thistle and Thuul escaped, but Adimond Kroskas (Iymrith) fled with a number of captives, Laela among them — believed to be subjects in his hybridization experiments.
2. **Mirabar (downtime):** Thistle's inquiries confirmed she passed through the city heading east toward the Lurkwood. Merchant Gale Wonderbrow reported she single-handedly slaughtered a group of brigands surrounding a caravan; the only survivors were Laela and a young man hiding in a wagon.
3. **Oracle consultation:** Thistle used one of six daily oracle questions to locate her. The oracle revealed she is held by Duke Zalto.
4. **Seer's intelligence:** The Seer reported rumors that Laela lives and that agents of allies of the Fire Giants captured her. Thistle's reaction (staring) was noted.
5. **Ironslag approach:** Thistle speculated aloud that Laela is likely a prisoner near Ironslag, but stated he must prioritize saving Silverymoon — believing Laela herself would demand he do so rather than rescue her at the city's expense.
6. **Sending stone contact (Iymrith's lair):** Iymrith provided the stone as proof of life. Laela recognized Thistle's voice immediately, expressed relief, scolded him for his absence, and claimed she had been looking for him. She objected to Vardis's presence on the grounds that she and Thistle had agreed they don't like elves. Iymrith cut off the communication.
7. **Queen Serissa conversation:** Serissa indicated that locating Laela is tied to finding King Hekaton, as only Hekaton knows where Iymrith resides. Orsik raised the possibility — provocatively — that Laela may already be a fairy-dragon hybrid super-mutant, bred by Iymrith as a weapon in the giant civil war. Thistle rejected this, stating there was no evidence at the time.
8. **Escarlotta's redirect:** Thistle shared an account of Laela (described by Escarlotta as a "sob story") that convinced Escarlotta to redirect the party's priorities away from pursuing Sansuri and toward finding Laela instead.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Caged in Iymrith's lair
- **Condition:** Physically held but actively resistant — she refuses to shift into dragon form, sustaining a fey-meditative state Iymrith calls "fey-fullness"
- **Active situation:** Iymrith cannot demonstrate her experiment's success without Laela transforming; she cannot compel the shift. This is Iymrith's central problem with Laela as a subject.

**What the party knows:**
- She is held in Iymrith's lair in a cage
- She is alive, communicative, and clearly resistant to Iymrith's control
- She has draconic capabilities alongside her fey nature
- She was found by Iymrith while torching a caravan in dragon form
- She dislikes elves (and specifically Vardis)

**What remains hidden:**
- Why she was torching the caravan
- Who the young man from the Mirabar caravan incident was and what became of him
- The full nature of Iymrith's experiment and what it achieved
- The true extent of Laela's agency — whether she is captive, collaborator, or something in between
- The connection (if any) between "Duke Zalto has her" (oracle) and Iymrith holding her

## Relationships
- **Thistle:** Her "dearest"; the emotional core of his arc. She is devoted to him, furious at his long absence, and (per Thistle's belief) principled enough that she would not want him to sacrifice Silverymoon for her rescue.
- **Unla Kee:** Also described as Unla Kee's "beloved" per the Seer's intelligence — the nature of this relationship relative to Thistle's is unresolved.
- **Iymrith:** Captor and experimenter. Laela refuses to cooperate and is actively frustrating Iymrith's goals. Iymrith describes her as still "very much a fairy" in personality.
- **Vardis:** Immediate hostility; Laela objected to Thistle working with an elf and cited a prior agreement between her and Thistle about not liking elves.
- **Orsik:** Deduced that Laela's permanent fey anger is the source of her power; no direct relationship established.
