# Annam (the Oracle)

## Identity
- **Role:** Divine oracle, manifestation or servant of Annam (the All-Father of giants)
- **Faction:** Neutral/divine — no clear factional alignment
- **First Appearance:** The Eye of the World; the party consulted it over two consecutive days

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core drive:** To answer questions truthfully within the constraints of its function (six questions per day).
- Ancient, composed, and utterly unflappable. When Thistle attempted a trick question, the Oracle laughed and dismissed it with the remark "I have been doing this for a very long time" — signaling sharp awareness and long experience with mortals trying to game its responses.
- Each deliberate extinguishing of a torch suggests ritual power and a taste for dramatic punctuation, or possibly a mechanic tied to the questioning process.

## History with the Party
- **Day 1:** The party asked about its favorite books; the Oracle answered "the collected sayings of Annam" and extinguished the last torch, ending questioning for the day.
- **Day 2:** The party asked five questions and received the following answers:
  - Adimond Kroskas is located at the Maelstrom.
  - King Hekaton's conch will take Thistle to the Maelstrom; the path they choose will lead to it.
  - To most impress the Zhentarim: end the Giant War, restore trade routes, and secure preferential treatment from the Storm Giant King for Zhentarim merchants.
  - Evil giants seek power, glory, and divine favor in order to be elevated when the ordning is remade.
  - Urgala's wife fell to her death in a well that led to the Underdark.
  - Thistle's trick question about burial grounds and Duke Zalto was seen through; the Oracle laughed and extinguished a torch.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** The Eye of the World.
- **Active operations:** Passive — responds to those who seek it out; does not appear to pursue independent action.
- **What the party knows:** The Oracle is limited to six questions per day, is ancient and wise, and cannot be tricked. It speaks on behalf of or in the name of Annam.
- **What remains hidden:** The full nature of the Oracle (divine construct, bound entity, avatar of Annam), the cost or consequences of asking questions, and whether it can be consulted again.

## Relationships
- **Annam the All-Father:** Named directly as the source of its wisdom; answers the collected sayings of Annam as its favored text.
- **The Party (Thistle in particular):** Thistle was the primary questioner; the Oracle singled out Thistle's trick attempt for pointed amusement.
- **Urgala:** The Oracle revealed a deeply personal secret about Urgala's wife — the nature of that knowledge and how Urgala would react to it being shared is unresolved.
- **Adimond Kroskas / King Hekaton / Duke Zalto / Zhentarim:** Referenced in answers; no direct relationship implied.
