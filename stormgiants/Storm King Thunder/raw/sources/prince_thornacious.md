# Prince Thornacious

## Identity
- **Role:** Royal Fairy Prince; exiled noble of the Seelie Court; subject of Zhentarim intelligence interest
- **Faction:** Seelie Court (exiled faction / personal court in the forest)
- **First Appearance:** Referenced before appearing on-screen — in Zhentarim mission briefings to Orsik and via Thistle's invocation in battle ("For Thornacius!"). Originally encountered when he directed the party away from his hunting grounds. Later appeared in person during the "Return of the Spy" sequence and the pitch for Fey assistance.

*Also known as: Thornacious, Thornacius.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Building a Fey empire — by his own declaration, "an empire or die trying." He has been actively expanding his sphere of control into territory overlapping with other factions.
- Pompous, possessive, vain, and easily flattered. He considers all stags his personal property and exiled an entire court rather than admit he was banished by the Queen.
- Per Thistle's reflection, may view Zhentarim spying on him as a mark of prestige rather than a threat — suggesting vanity or political shrewdness, or both.
- Responds to credible stories, good gifts, and deference to his ego. He scoffed at the party's explanation about the bear and lion but accepted it when they confirmed Blasius's account directly.
- Frames proximity to Queen Tatiana's favor as the highest possible honor, suggesting his ambitions coexist with genuine reverence (or political calculation) toward the Seelie Court.
- Some characters, including Blassios privately, regard him as a "buffoon" — implying his pretensions are not universally respected.

## History with the Party

1. **Pre-campaign:** Thornacious has a prior relationship with Orsik — at some point he gave Orsik a gift that later protected him from a hybrid dragon's poisonous stinger during combat.

2. **Early Contact:** Before major interactions, Thornacious directed the party away from his hunting grounds, asserting possessive control over the area.

3. **The Stag Incident (Background):** Sir Oblivillish killed a stag Thornacious considered his own. Thornacious cursed Oblivillish and exiled him. He also compelled his entire court into exile alongside him, though the Queen could have sent him alone.

4. **Blasius's Misery:** In Oblivillish's absence, Thornacious forced Blasius into a chief of staff role, which Blasius found miserable. Thornacious also destroyed a Fey circle in pursuit of his empire-building, with his territorial expansion directly destabilizing the region around Gralm's encampment via disruptive Fey music.

5. **Return of the Spy:** The party arrived before Thornacious via Fey portal. He initially called Blasius "traitorous scum." After Blasius recanted accusations, credited Oblivillish, and the party confirmed the account, Thornacious reinstated both — Blasius as his Satyr, Oblivillish as Chief of Staff. He asked Thistle directly to confirm Blasius's account.

6. **The Pitch for Fey Aid:** At his forest camp (Weathercote Forest), the party pitched redirecting his imperial ambitions toward the High Forest with Star Mountain as a grander realm. He deliberated and agreed to help fight Bad Fruul, sending Blasius and two sprites. He also pointedly asked Oblivillish whether he was a hunter.

7. **Fine Dinner:** On the 4th day, Thornacious ordered a fine dinner for the party — a small but notable gesture of goodwill.

8. **Post-Battle Party:** Appeared as an unexpected guest at the celebration. Informed Thistle he had mentioned them to Queen Tatiana, who was pleased, framing this as the highest honor he could convey.

9. **Kagrota's Revelation:** When Thistle reduced Kagrota to 1 HP, Kagrota screamed that her forces had a deal with Prince Thornacious — suggesting an undisclosed arrangement or alliance with him that she believed the fairy's presence had betrayed.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Weathercote Forest camp, then present at the post-battle celebration.
- **Active Operations:** Empire-building expansion; territorial encroachment ongoing. His forces or name appear entangled with Kagrota's faction in an unexplained prior deal.
- **What the Party Knows:** He is a vain, exiled Fey prince with imperial ambitions; he can be flattered and managed; he is now nominally an ally and has spoken well of Thistle to Queen Tatiana. Orsik holds a Thornacious-gifted item of protective magical value.
- **What Remains Hidden:** The nature of any deal between Thornacious and Kagrota. The full scope of his empire-building plans and what other factions or deals he may have made. The original reason for his exile from the Seelie Court (he claims it was voluntary; in reality Queen Titania exiled him).

## Relationships
- **Sir Oblivillish:** Exiled him for killing the stag; has since reinstated him as Chief of Staff. Relationship is transactional and fragile — he directly tested Oblivillish by asking if he was a hunter. Oblivillish privately holds him in open contempt.
- **Blasius / Blassios:** Forced Blasius into a miserable administrative role; the relationship is one of compelled loyalty. Blasius serves as Thornacious's intermediary with the party and is also a close personal friend of Thistle's.
- **Thistle:** Appears to hold Thistle in genuine regard — asked them directly to confirm the party's account and later reported them favorably to Queen Tatiana. Thistle invoked his name as a battle cry ("For Thornacius!").
- **Orsik:** Prior relationship; gifted Orsik something of protective magical value before the campaign began.
- **Queen Tatiana / Titania:** Exiled by her, though he publicly frames it as his own choice. Still operates within Seelie Court social hierarchy and seeks her notice.
- **Kagrota:** Some prior deal or arrangement existed between Thornacious and Kagrota's forces. Nature unknown.
- **Gralm:** Thornacious's territorial expansion directly caused Fey music to disturb Gralm's encampment, making him an indirect antagonist to Gralm even if unintentionally.
- **Zhentarim:** Subject of their intelligence interest; Oblivillish was their embedded spy in his court.
- **Party (general):** Now considered an ally per Unla Kee's assessment. Relationship cultivated through flattery, gift-giving framing, and a shared enemy (Bad Fruul).
