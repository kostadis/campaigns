# Ragnar Redtooth

## Identity
- **Role:** Proprietor and barkeep of The Golden Tankard tavern, Parnast; also a member of the Parnast militia ("the Orsik's Militia") and a defender of the town
- **Appearance:** Heavyset, brown-haired, with enormous muttonchop sideburns and large belly
- **Faction:** Former Cult of the Dragon associate (claims he infiltrated to protect the town); now acting as informal defense organizer for Parnast and a host for Lord's Alliance / SEER agents
- **First Appearance:** The party met him at The Golden Tankard in Parnast when they entered seeking information; he intervened in a confrontation between a traveler and Unla Kee over accusations of cheating at gambling

*Also known as: Ragnar, Raggnar.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protect Parnast from imminent attack by Bad Fruul's army; restore unity among the villagers; identify and neutralize the saboteur undermining defense efforts; support the Lord's Alliance network operating through his establishment.
- Mercenary by instinct — he'll squeeze coin from anyone throwing money around — but demonstrates genuine investment in Parnast's survival.
- Pragmatic and politically aware, recognizing that Gundalin's craftsmen are too essential to alienate and that the real problem is morale and trust, not muscle.
- Reads people quickly, pivoting his appeal away from Orsik and toward Vardis as the party member he perceives as most capable of solving his problem.
- Decisive and confrontational when threatened, willing to step into conflict to defuse or face it directly. Loyal to Parnast and its defenders, demonstrated by his defiant refusal to surrender during Bad Fruul's demands.
- Operates with quiet discretion, hosting covert meetings without drawing attention to them.
- Emotionally reactive to loss — his immediate response to the battle's aftermath was to confront the party about the destruction.
- Willing to follow sound reasoning quickly; validated Orsik's criticisms of the SEER and Lord's Alliance without much hesitation once he overheard the argument.

## History with the Party

**First Encounter (Golden Tankard):**
- Greeted the party at The Golden Tankard, immediately noting they'd been talking to him without buying anything.
- Once drinks were purchased, confirmed knowledge of Chandra Stol's idol quest, calling it foolish; noted merchant Azam refuses to travel to Parnast due to road dangers; observed significantly more eastbound than westbound traffic.
- Offered vague speculation on the party's treasure map ("somewhere in the North"), accepted one gold piece for it, then provided his finest sausage and cabbage in gratitude.
- Charged five gold for a dingy room, another five for sheets, defending the pricing unapologetically.

**Confrontation intervention:**
- Intervened when a traveler accused Unla Kee of cheating at gambling, preventing a fight. The traveler left but notably memorized Unla Kee's face before departing.

**The back-room meeting:**
- Gathered the party and led them to a hidden back room of the Golden Tankard, where he introduced them to Azam and Hsing, revealing his connection to "important friends."

**Azam's payment:**
- Referenced as the recipient of coin left by Azam before Azam departed the Golden Tankard to meet the returning party. Ragnar was not present in the scene.

**Second Encounter (sabotage briefing):**
- Found bidding farewell to two militia members and a young half-elf scout named Tove when the party arrived; greeted them warmly and had Elspeth bring food and ale.
- Described three incidents of suspected sabotage during Orsik's 20-day absence: daily tool disappearances from the central toolshed; collapse of the newly built southern watchtower (killing militiaman Donnal, with a witness reporting a light near the base beforehand); and a cart breaking its second axle within a tenday — wheels and axle having been replaced by Gundalin Cartwright.
- Named Gundalin as the likely saboteur; offered 50 gold each for *evidence*, explicitly not for Gundalin's death.
- Explained that the tower collapse led villagers to believe scavenged materials from Old Parnast were cursed by the Cult of the Dragon, severely damaging morale.
- Acknowledged that villagers still hold him responsible for his Cult of the Dragon involvement; claims he joined to work from the inside and save the town — Thistle was openly skeptical.
- Shared Tove's intelligence: monsters are constantly on the move and destroying what they find, but Tove could not locate their main camp; stated his belief that Bad Fruul's army attack is imminent.
- Warned that if the villagers cannot be united, they will flee into the hills and be slaughtered.

**Orc alarm:** When an orc alarm was raised, Ragnar emerged from the Golden Tankard ready to fight. He and Gundalin investigated but found nothing.

**Before the final assault:** Approached Orsik privately asking *"do you have any plan?"* Received the answer about releasing the wyvern; no further reaction recorded.

**Bad Fruul's ultimatum:** During Bad Fruul's pre-battle demands for surrender, Ragnar shouted defiance alongside Gundalin — *"NEVER! The Orsik's Militia will never surrender one of our own! Go back before you die!"* — helping to steel the town's resolve and trigger the battle.

**Aftermath of battle:** Confronted the party openly: *"What have you done to this town?"* Reported that approximately half the militia was killed. Participated in the argument about rebuilding.

**Following Orsik out of town:** Raced after Orsik when he marched out, overheard his explanation, and told him he made excellent points — becoming one of the first to openly validate Orsik's concerns about the SEER and the Lord's Alliance.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Parnast, in the immediate aftermath of the battle
- **Active Operations:** Engaged in discussions about rebuilding the town; managing fragile village morale; has begun openly siding with Orsik's skepticism toward the SEER and the Lord's Alliance
- **What the Party Knows:** His Cult of the Dragon past; the three sabotage incidents; the 50 gp evidence bounty; Tove's monster intelligence; he runs the Golden Tankard, has ties to Lord's Alliance agents (Azam and Hsing), and is a committed defender of Parnast
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent of his Cult involvement; whether his "working from the inside" claim is truthful; the full extent of his relationship with the Lord's Alliance / SEER's network and how long that arrangement has been in place; any other intelligence sources or plans he hasn't disclosed

## Relationships
- **Gundalin Cartwright:** Suspects him as saboteur; views him as an obstacle but recognizes his craftsmen are essential — wants him neutralized politically, not killed. Yet they fought side by side during the orc alarm and the stand against Bad Fruul; they appear to be close allies within Parnast's defense (consistent with Ragnar wanting to neutralize politically rather than kill).
- **Azam & Hsing:** Hosts them in a private back room; Azam left payment for him, suggesting an ongoing and trusted working relationship.
- **Orsik (party member):** Respects him as a good soldier but did not see him as the right person for the sabotage problem; quickly redirected his appeal away from him. After the battle, raced after Orsik and openly validated his SEER critique.
- **Vardis (party member):** Views Vardis as the most capable party member for handling the sabotage situation; directed his primary appeal toward them.
- **Thistle (party member):** Thistle is openly skeptical of his Cult of the Dragon explanation.
- **Unla Kee:** Intervened to protect him from a confrontation; nature of relationship beyond that moment unclear.
- **Tove (half-elf scout):** Source of current monster intelligence; was present at the tavern during the second encounter.
- **Donnal:** Militia member killed in the watchtower collapse.
- **Elspeth:** Staff member at The Golden Tankard.
- **Villagers of Parnast:** Distrust him due to his Cult association; this undermines his ability to organize defenses.
- **The Party (general):** Initially confrontational over the destruction caused during the assault; relationship is now warming through shared frustration with the Lord's Alliance.
