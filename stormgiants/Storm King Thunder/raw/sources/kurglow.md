# Kurglow

## Identity
- **Role:** Chieftain of the Spinripper Clan (identified by green sash)
- **Faction:** Formerly allied with Bad Fruul; alliance broken during the Battle of Parnast
- **First Appearance:** Discussed in absentia before appearing as a major combatant in the battle for Parnast

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protecting his son; maintaining his identity and reputation as a wild, dangerous orc chieftain
- Described by an orc woman of his clan as "a terrible husband, but a good father" — his paternal love is his most exploitable trait and his most genuine one
- He is proud to the point of rage: Orsik's letter implying his whelp had gone soft and pampered was a deeper insult than any battlefield taunt, and Kurglow charged through a gauntlet of opportunity attacks to reach him
- Pragmatic when his son's safety is at stake — he negotiated a withdrawal rather than fight to the death, and followed through on the deal

## History with the Party

**Before the Battle**
The party captured one of Kurglow's whelps during an earlier engagement. An orc woman guarding the whelps — relieved her own child was spared — told the party that Kurglow would abandon Bad Fruul's alliance to recover his son. The party attempted to exploit this via a spy channel through Jarlor and Larrish. Orsik drafted the message, threatening to withhold dessert and bedtime stories from the whelp.

**Battle of Parnast**
Kurglow led Spinripper Clan forces in the battle alongside a blue-sashed orc chieftain. Vardis blinded him with Correllon's invocation, but he still dealt 15 hit points of damage before losing sight. When he recognized the insult embedded in Orsik's earlier message — that his whelp had become soft and pampered — he fixated on Orsik personally and charged him, absorbing opportunity attacks from Chandra, Vardis, Wallace, and Unla Kee. Unla Kee's mind spike penetrated his mental defenses on the second attempt.

After Orsik killed the green-sashed chieftain, Kurglow shifted posture: he demanded the return of his son and threatened to kill the party if refused, but promised to withdraw if the child was handed over.

**The Exchange**
When his son was brought forward, Kurglow turned on Naz, accusing her of valuing her own child over his and being willing to let his whelp die for her own ends. He rejected her denials, refused her plea to leave with him, and delivered a final remark — *"Unlike you, I can take my whelp and leave you to die"* — before departing. His withdrawal immediately triggered Bad Fruul's rage, who perceived it as betrayal.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Departed Parnast alive with his son following the negotiated exchange
- **Active Plans:** Unknown; his immediate goal (recovering his son) is complete
- **What the Party Knows:** He is alive, no longer allied with Bad Fruul, and departed on terms that were hostile but not unresolved — the exchange was honored on both sides
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether he bears lasting grudges against the party (particularly Orsik), and what his clan does now that the Bad Fruul alliance is severed

## Relationships
- **His Whelp (Son):** The axis around which all his decisions turn; his defining vulnerability
- **Bad Fruul:** Former ally; departure enraged Bad Fruul, who viewed it as betrayal — relationship likely severed
- **Naz:** Contemptuous; accused her of sacrificing his son to protect her own child and rejected her outright
- **Orsik:** Personal animosity; the bedtime story letter was taken as a profound insult to his identity, and Kurglow charged him specifically through the entire party
- **Blue-Sashed Orc Chieftain:** Fought alongside him in the battle; that chieftain was killed by Orsik
- **The Orc Woman (Clan Member):** She facilitated the negotiation by informing the party of Kurglow's weakness — unclear if Kurglow knows this

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase (toward departure/neutrality):** Honored the exchange and withdrew peacefully once his son was returned — demonstrated he is a man of his word when his child is the stakes
- **Decrease (toward hostility):** His personal rage at Orsik; the death of the green-sashed chieftain at Orsik's hands; the implicit humiliation of needing to negotiate with the party at all
