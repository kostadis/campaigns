# Chandra Stol

## Identity
- **Role:** Cleric of the Shrine of Axes (Temple of Axes), Parnast; villager of Parnast; one of Bad Fruul's named targets
- **Origin:** Hails from Evereska; offspring of Luistara (also rendered as "Luistarra")
- **Faction:** None confirmed; expresses skepticism toward the Lord's Alliance; draws favorable comparisons to Zhentarim pragmatism
- **First Appearance:** Approached the party outside the sealed Wyvern stables in Parnast, attempting to dissuade them from disturbing the creature

*Also known as: Chandra.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protecting Parnast and its people; recovering the idol of Angharradh for her shrine; neutralizing the centuries-long threat of the drow Narcellia, who killed her mother and has plagued her family for generations; survival and protection from the family nemesis driving Bad Fruul's assault.
- Elderly, sharp-tongued, and dignified — uses dry humor under stress and responds to sincerity with unexpected warmth.
- Pragmatic and knowledgeable — corrects misconceptions (the wyvern, the trade network) with confidence and detail.
- Stubborn about withholding information until her conditions are met, but not dishonest; she explicitly confirms when she is holding something back.
- Emotionally guarded but her loyalties run deep, particularly toward Wallace, whom she loves like a son.
- Deeply community-rooted: erupts when outsiders impose abstract categories (civilian vs. militia) on Parnast's dead, whom she knew personally.
- Was shaken and intimidated by Orsik's forcefulness but ultimately compliant when persuaded.

## History with the Party

**Opening scene — Parnast:** Chandra is introduced as a Parnast resident.

**Trade-network conversation:** Explains to Thistle why a Lionshield Coster transdimensional market is unavailable in Parnast, citing the Zhentarim-established trade network and referencing local merchant Calder.

**Early Contact — Wyvern Stables:** Chandra approached the party to warn them away from the Wyvern. Explained that the Cult of the Dragon left it behind after their defeat, and that villagers maintain it as a deterrent. Refused to share information about the Cult or Adimond Kruskas until her idol was returned. Under pressure from Thistle, she volunteered one piece: the Cult flew a large castle over Parnast to move treasure — which Orsik recognized as the castle that crash-landed near Waterdeep. She directed the party toward the job: a merchant named Azam is stranded in Vuerthyl with her idol (a statue of Angharradh), and a middleperson named Fiora was meant to find mercenaries to retrieve it.

**Wyvern consultation:** Advised Orsik in detail on how to free the chained wyvern from the former stables — use orc corpses as a distraction, remove the chain from the hoop, and ensure the nearest living thing in its path is the giant. Simultaneously expressed strong opposition: *"Are you mad? He will kill us all!"*

**Horse Poisoning Scene — Parnast Center:** Arrived at the scene of the afflicted horses. Expressed genuine warmth toward the party, crediting them with restoring her temple and saving the village from raiders. Suggested the late wife of Silas Carras was a herbalist who might help. Quietly appealed to Thistle to defuse escalating tension between refugees and residents before it became a riot.

**The Golden Tankard — After Orsik's Return:** Waiting inside the tavern when the heroes arrived. Responded to Orsik's suboptimal outcome with dry humor about "charred meat." Granted him permission, with an air of noblesse oblige, to have the captain of the guard feed the Wyvern.

**Temple of Axes — Narcellia Revelation:** When Orsik reported the Seer's findings, Chandra confirmed the account in full. Revealed that Narcellia has plagued her family for centuries and personally killed her mother 30 years ago, after which Chandra went into hiding and lost track of the drow. Explained that her ancestor Luistara went mad after Narcellia destroyed her village and used hag magic to bind the drow into the headband. Offered intelligence: Narcellia has a deep fear of the fey, sprites in particular, and suggested Prince Thornacious might be useful. Initially told the group there was nothing they could do for her — then was won over by Thistle's earnest gesture with herbs from Syla's garden. Agreed to join the group only after consulting Unla Kee, who told her refusal would mean the end of the world.

**Day 4 confrontation:** Orsik argues she is unsafe without his protection; she is shocked and intimidated but agrees to his terms.

**Forest Boundary — Prince Thornacious:** After Thistle secured fey assistance, Chandra begged Orsik to convince Thornacious to let her remain with the fey rather than continue with the group. Clarified she didn't need Orsik's protection — only the fey's. This wounded Orsik's pride.

**Battle of Parnast:** Cast *bless* on Orsik (her protector), Thistle (a target that draws Bad Fruul's focus away from her), and Wallace (known to her since his birth; she loves him like a son).

**Post-Battle — Parnast Aftermath:** Watched events from near the well. Mourned the death of her pet Wyvern, insisting it fought heroically (a claim Orsik rejected). Erupted at Vardis's civilian/militia distinction, asserting Parnast's dead were friends and family, not abstractions. Joined Raggnar and Gundalin in debating how to rebuild. Accused Orsik of endangering her life when he attempted to withhold the headband from the Seer — but when challenged on why she trusts the Seer (who also endangered her), she only sneered. Later, overhearing Orsik's explanation as he left, she began to reconsider. Questioned what benefit the Lord's Alliance has actually brought Parnast, drew a comparison to the Zhentarim's more pragmatic assistance against the Cult, and observed that the Alliance's machinations contributed to the town's destruction.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Parnast, near the well, in the aftermath of the battle
- **Active situation:** Under Orsik's protection by agreement (though she has tried to substitute fey protection); Bad Fruul previously demanded she be handed over alongside the statue of Angharradh — now resolved with his death
- **Active Concerns:** Town rebuilding; distrust of the Lord's Alliance solidifying; grief over the Wyvern
- **What the Party Knows:** Her family history with Narcellia; her role maintaining the Wyvern; her connection to the Shrine of Axes and the missing idol; her fear of Narcellia and reliance on fey protection; she is from Evereska; she is the offspring of Luistara
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent of what she knows about the Cult of the Dragon and Adimond Kruskas (she has withheld this explicitly and the idol has not yet been confirmed returned); the full nature of her significance to Bad Fruul beyond her parentage; the depth of her ideological shift regarding the Lord's Alliance

## Relationships
- **Thistle:** Positive and growing; Thistle's sincerity (herb gesture, direct appeals) has moved Chandra on multiple occasions; she explained the trade-network logistics to him; cast *bless* on him as a tactical choice — his status as a target benefits her.
- **Orsik:** Complicated; mutual respect undercut by friction — she wounded his pride at the forest boundary; he dismissively rejected her grief over the Wyvern; she accused him of endangering her life, but his reasoning gave her pause. Has agreed to his protection; cast *bless* on him in the battle.
- **Wallace:** Knows him since birth; loves him like a son; cast *bless* on him out of genuine affection.
- **Ragnar Redtooth (barkeep):** Familiar and cooperative; he confirmed her weeks-long search for someone to retrieve the idol.
- **Raggnar & Gundalin:** Allied in post-battle debate over rebuilding Parnast.
- **Vardis:** Antagonistic; she directly challenged his framing of the dead as "civilians."
- **Prince Thornacious:** Sought his protection; views the fey as a necessary refuge against Narcellia.
- **Narcellia:** Generational enemy; responsible for her mother's death and her ancestor Luistara's madness.
- **Bad Fruul:** Named target; he demanded her person specifically, suggesting her significance goes beyond that of an ordinary villager (now resolved by his death).
- **Luistara (parent):** Referenced by Bad Fruul as "horrid"; the source of the enmity directed at Chandra.
- **Calder (local merchant):** Referenced in the context of the Zhentarim trade network; relationship otherwise unspecified.
- **The Seer:** Distrusts, but her position is inconsistent — she accepts the Seer's intelligence while resenting the risk the Seer imposed on her.
- **Lord's Alliance:** Increasingly skeptical; compares them unfavorably to the Zhentarim in terms of practical benefit to Parnast.
