## Characters

### Vardis
**Class/Level:** Cleric 13 (Light Domain) | **Species:** Wood Elf | **Player:** Wade Brown

Vardis is a pragmatic, war-tested cleric whose theological flexibility — offering to gods beyond his own — earned him exile from every temple of Corellon Larethian. He describes himself as "pragmatic" when pressed on it, and his expulsion from the church seems to have left him more philosophically restless than bitter. Privately, he has begun to imagine founding an alternative believers' cult of Corellon, inspired by watching Sandesyl's unapologetic long-term planning — a nascent ambition he has not shared with the party.

**Notable Relationships:**
- *Orsik:* Lifelong friend from Waterdeep; Vardis has expressed genuine grief at the thought of Orsik dying against Iymrith, and was visibly moved hearing Orsik's hero-death ambitions
- *Corellon Larethian:* Strained to the point of absurdity — the deity recently covered Vardis in bird droppings in response to a prayer; Vardis was offered reconciliation by the SEER and refused it
- *Conservative Corellon clergy (Waterdeep elf cleric):* Overtly rejected him as a legitimate cleric; relationship hostile
- *The SEER:* Offered both reconciliation with his temples and assurance of Orsik's survival in exchange for compliance; Vardis refused both

**Items of Significance:**
- Mace of Terror (attuned; used effectively against undead and giant opponents)
- Amulet of Proof against Detection and Location (attuned)
- Ring of Djinni Summoning (attuned)
- Dragon Wand (attuned)
- Mithral Half Plate + Shield (AC 19)
- Ring found in Sandesyl's treasure (pocketed without disclosure)
- Scroll of Lesser Restoration (from Oghma's shrine in the tower)
- Resistance to air damage (elemental blessing, Temple of Annam)

### Orsik
**Class/Level:** Fighter 11 / Artificer 2 | **Species:** Mountain Dwarf | **Player:** David Mendenhall

Orsik is a disgraced professional soldier turned reformed hero, whose intelligence was dramatically augmented by the Headband of Intellect he now wears. Where the old Orsik charged first and regretted later, the new Orsik recognizes and elevates good ideas from others — though his emotional volatility and Zhentarim loyalties still create friction. He has committed himself to a heroic death fighting Iymrith, and Vardis's visible grief over this ambition has not softened it. He is currently operating under the alias "Orsic" (pretending to be his own father) in some contexts.

**Notable Relationships:**
- *Vardis:* Best friend since Waterdeep; the relationship is the emotional center of his character; Orsik deflects Vardis's grief with humor ("Remember NRRRR")
- *Parnast militia:* Strong loyalty; they named themselves "Orsik's Guards"; his role as their captain is his clearest moment of belonging
- *The SEER:* Deep mutual antagonism; SEER left a note suggesting he "do the realms a favor" after Iymrith; Orsik had her forcecaged and was physically restrained from striking her
- *Zhentarim (Uzorel "The Viper" Hornbalde):* Confirmed agent; betrayed Harper teleportation circles to them; faction views him favorably
- *King Hekaton:* Dismissed the king's offer of military alliance, insisting the party could handle the giant lords alone — a unilateral decision the rest of the party did not fully endorse
- *Escarlotta:* Relationship shifted from transactional to genuine partnership after Orsik acknowledged her as part of the team; she told him "That is what heroes are"
- *Harshnag:* Giant ally; last seen fighting Iymrith in the collapsing Eye of the All-Father — fate unknown; Harshnag referenced "that THING" Orsik did in Waterdeep, unspecified
- *Darathra Shendrel:* Severed all ties following the Bob Saget theater operation; "We are done"
- *Old Keller (dwarf stonemason, Llorkh):* Orsik has an open redemption debt; Keller refused further contact until Orsik proves himself

### Unla Key

He believes he has decoded the end of the universe and that he alone is the reason the world continues — a conviction that makes him functionally reckless in dangerous situations ("the multitude of planes are safe only if he takes risks"). His sobriety lapses and gambling impulses are recurring vulnerabilities, but he has also demonstrated genuine cleverness in political maneuvering, business structuring, and lateral problem-solving under pressure. He privately considers himself the intellectual of the group and was visibly unsettled when Orsik's new intelligence surpassed him.

## Party Dynamics

**The core bond** is Vardis and Orsik — a friendship forged in Waterdeep through years of shared military failure. This relationship is the party's emotional anchor. Vardis has expressed grief at the thought of Orsik's death wish; Orsik deflects with humor and refuses to soften his position. The two sometimes break from the group to make decisions as a unit, which can leave Thistle and Unla Key feeling excluded.

**Thistle and Unla Key** have developed a genuine secondary bond, particularly after the Evermoors marsh skirmish and Unla Key's explicit declaration of willingness to protect Thistle physically. Their relationship is warm and playful in a way neither has with the others — Thistle's trickster instincts and Unla Key's chaos appreciation find common ground.

**Tensions in the group:**
- Unla Key's recklessness (triggering traps, breaking the Ironslag wheel early, casting stinking cloud on poison-immune undead, withholding the SEER's book contents from the party) creates recurring friction with Orsik, who has grown increasingly strategic
- Thistle's increasing moral drift — directing vampires to feed on Uthgardt barbarians, framing House Margaster for ethnic violence, executing a cooperative cultist — has been noted with alarm by Vardis and Orsik but not directly confronted
- Orsik's unilateral decisions (declining Hekaton's military aid without party consultation, disclosing Harper teleportation circles to the Zhentarim out of wounded pride) create quiet resentment, particularly from Vardis
- The Bob Saget summoning and theater cover-up are a shared moral compromise that the party has largely processed through mutual complicity rather than reckoning
- Vardis's consistent advocacy for caution and moral restraint sometimes puts him at odds with both Orsik's aggression and Unla Key's indifference to civilian harm

**Shared goals:**
- Defeat Iymrith (unanimous, though motivations differ: Orsik wants a heroic death, Thistle wants revenge and Laela's rescue, Unla Key frames it as cosmologically necessary, Vardis is more driven by loyalty to the group than the dragon herself)
- Protect Laela (high priority for Thistle; the rest of the party has accepted this as a mission parameter)
- The cloud castle and Kee and Wing Casino represent the party's first collective material investment — a shared economic stake that is both a resource and a liability
- The five rogue giant lords remain entirely unaddressed; Hekaton's alliance is contingent on them, creating a long unresolved obligation the party has not seriously discussed

**The SEER situation** is a persistent structural tension: she offered each party member exactly what they wanted in exchange for compliance, and all four refused. This unanimous rejection was a rare moment of solidarity and represents the party's clearest statement of collective identity — they would rather pursue their own path than be instruments of anyone else's agenda. The SEER's final note suggests she has not given up on them being useful to her after Iymrith falls.
