# Tamalin Zoar

## Identity
- **Role:** Owner-proprietor of the Calling Horns Inn; de facto mayor and law of the settlement; founder of her own town on the Evermoor Road between Yartar and Everlund; legendary member of the Hand of Yartar; retired adventurer
- **Faction:** Zoar family (politically connected); sister of High Captain Horix Zoar of Everlund's army; institutionally regarded within the Hand of Yartar
- **First Appearance:** First referenced by Eltio (who indicated her endorsement carried institutional weight); first met in person at her inn in Calling Horns when the party arrived and Orsik initiated contact with flattery about her business acumen

*Also known as: Tamalin, Tamlin Zoar.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protecting Calling Horns and the business she built; mentoring promising adventurers; resolving the escalating troll threat from the Evermoors; regional stability.
- Approximately 60 years old but still physically capable and sharp. Takes evident pride in her self-made success and transformation from criminal to respected founder.
- Shrewd and well-connected; asks pointed follow-up questions and shares useful knowledge freely — but not without purpose.
- Responds warmly to genuine flattery and interest in her expertise — the prospect of mentoring young adventurers clearly appeals to her at this stage of life.
- Direct and socially perceptive: publicly corrected Orsik's sales pitch timing, then recognized she had overstepped and apologized — an unusual combination of bluntness and emotional awareness that surprised the party.
- Shows subtle bias: she redirected the party's attention to useful Silverymoon contacts while implying Thistle specifically would not be welcomed there.
- Sympathetic when the subject of Silverymoon's moral contradictions arises, suggesting her own complicated history with the city.

## History with the Party

1. **Reputation precedes her (via Eltio):** The party learned of "Tamlin Zoar" through Eltio, who indicated that her personal endorsement or a letter of recommendation from her would be sufficient to waive the 500 gold fee for information on Harthal. Calling Horns was identified as their next destination, presumably in pursuit of finding her.

2. **First meeting (Calling Horns):** Orsik flattered her business sense; she cleared the main table and bought food and drinks to share her experiences. Gave the party two free rooms.

3. **Troll attack:** Witnessed the party dispatch trolls attacking her stables, though two of her horses were killed in the process.

4. **Post-fight conversation:** Invited the party for drinks and presented her request — investigate the increasing frequency of troll descents from the Evermoors (locally called the Trollmoors), with information as the minimum deliverable and a solution preferred. Offered a **Letter of Mark** in return.

5. **Revealed connections:** Disclosed her Zoar family membership and directed the party to speak with **Dral Thelev** at **Danivarr's House** in Everlund, who could arrange assistance from her brother, the captain of Everlund's guard.

6. **Evermoors Debrief:** Upon the party's return, Tamalin listened to their full report on Fire Giants and Trolls operating together. She expressed genuine surprise, echoing Thistle's earlier "cats and dogs" framing.

7. **Giant Lore briefing:** Provided an unprompted briefing on giant social hierarchy — Fire Giant leaders are dukes, Hill Giant leaders are chiefs, Stone Giant leaders are thanes, Cloud Giant leaders are counts.

8. **The Vonindod:** Admitted she had no knowledge of the Vonindod and directed the party toward Everlund and Silverymoon for further research, with the caveat about Thistle's reception in the latter city.

9. **Favor Note:** On the party's departure, she revealed her brother is the High Captain of Everlund and provided a written note to be used as a one-time favor with him.

10. **Subsequent ripple effects:** Does not appear in person again, but her Letter of Mark proved politically significant — it impressed Eltio and secured cooperation from the Hand of Yartar. Dral confirmed her brother is **High Captain Horix Zoar**. Captain Zoar himself described her history with pride and credited her letter as the key to his cooperation with the party.

11. **Referenced by Unla Kee** as one of the party's cultivated allies.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Calling Horns, running her inn (presumably her town on the Evermoor Road between Yartar and Everlund)
- **Active Concerns:** Escalating troll activity from the Evermoors threatening her settlement
- **What the party knows:** Her full backstory (via Captain Zoar) — former cutthroat, brigand, and member of a women's thieves gang in Triboar before going legitimate and founding Calling Horns; her brother is High Captain Horix Zoar; her institutional standing in the Hand of Yartar; her complicated relationship with Silverymoon
- **What remains hidden:** The nature and full extent of her past criminal operation; the specifics of her history with Silverymoon; whether she has any ongoing agenda beyond innkeeping

## Relationships
- **High Captain Horix Zoar (brother):** Close; he speaks of her transformation with great pride. Her letter of introduction was sufficient to unlock his cooperation with the party.
- **Dral Thelev:** Runs Danivarr's House in Everlund on behalf of the Zoar family; acts as the point of contact between Tamalin and her brother.
- **Eltio:** Aware of her reputation; spoke of her as a figure of significant standing within the Hand of Yartar; knows of her criminal past and spoke of it with some familiarity; their prior connection is implied.
- **Thistle:** Notably excluded from her recommendation to seek contacts in Silverymoon; reason unstated.
- **Orsik:** Warm but complicated — she flattered him back, publicly corrected him, then apologized. Orsik has praised her to Dral. His comment about Silverymoon's hypocrisy drew a sympathetic reaction from her, suggesting shared sentiment.
- **The Party (3HPP generally):** Employer/patron relationship; views them as promising adventurers worth investing in.
