# Chapter 18 - Attack on the Cabin
## 2nd day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul 

The wyvern howls its morning wake-up call, and with it, the village of Parnast comes to life. 
The adventurers slowly filter into the Tavern of the Golden Tankard to plan their next moves. Wallace has a particular spring in his step and a smile. Orsik and Vardis are rubbing their heads from too much drink. Unla Kee is getting his first drink to settle his nerves. And Thistle is freshly rested after a beautiful eight hours of peaceful sleep. 
“To the cabin?” shouts Ragnar.
Thistle responds, “I suppose so!” 
The rest of the group looks at the overly cheery ranger and nods in agreement. Soon they are following the trail that led to where the mother of Tove Brandimen was supposedly held, hostage. The evidence that Tove had produced the night before supported his claim. 
Vardis and Orsik had had their doubts. 
Vardis had looked at the note supposedly from Kagrota Threeteeth – Mother of Bones - and had whispered to Orsik – “Remember when we pulled the hostage scam?”
Their journey continues for a few hours, in post-drunken or drunken silence, until they crest a hill and spot a cabin amid massive boulders and old fir trees. 
“How pretty,” muse Thistle. “Except for the screaming.” 
Thistle tries to make out what they are saying and gathers that they are human voices. He listens carefully and detects tell-tale slang from the area. And then he blushes and claps his hands over his ears. 
The others turn to him, surprised. 
“Oh my. Those are inappropriate things to be saying.” 
Vardis and Orsik, whose head hurts from the alcohol, decide that there has been enough screaming for one day and charge into the clearing. Thistle shrugs and follows. Wallace mumbles something about first scouting before attacking but follows his mentor. Seeing everyone charge, Unla Kee charges as well. 
Orsik sees a cage in the clearing where humans are trapped. To the east of the cell is a firepit. And behind the pen and the firepit is the Brandimen cabin. 
Orsik sees the prisoners and thinks that if he kills them, the screaming will stop. But he is surprised to discover that the screaming isn’t just some local singing but the human reaction of fear. A goblin is torturing the prisoners with a hot poker.  

Unla Kee, who was following Orsik, is surprised to see Orsik surprised to see Orcs and Goblins. 
The wild charge, unfortunately, was noticed, and the goblins and orcs had taken positions behind cover to ambush the five charging adventurers. 
One of the goblins is wearing a dirty green jacket. The goblin steps behind a shed to the east of the fire pit and fire an arrow at Unla Kee. 
“Why me?” 
Orsik mutters, “because you’re in range and aren’t wearing armor.”
Unla Kee doesn’t think that he should get cover. The goblin, on the other hand, gets behind cover. 
Just then, an orc fires a javelin from the cabin over the prisoners and misses Orsik. 
The orc with a necklace of skulls standing next to the prisoner gets hurt when an arrow from Vardis pierces his armor for 9 HP, and Wallace finishes him off with his sword.
Thistle and Thuul move to attack two goblins. The first goblins thwack at Thuul but cannot hurt the Ranger and his beast. Seeing how ineffectual his scimitar is, the goblin runs. The other goblin fires an arrow and also misses. 
An orc steps out from cover, throws his javelin at the robe-wearing wizard and misses. Unla Kee taunts him – “can’t hit the broadside of a barn!” The orc then runs into cover. Unla Kee, knowing that the multitude of planes are safe only if he takes risks,  refuses to hide.

The goblin with the green jacket, who missed Unla Kee, now tries to hit Orsik. This time his arrow hits the dirt in front of Orsik. 
“Charge!”  shouts the Orc with the necklace of skulls and rushes Orsik. 
The group wonders why, and then the reason is apparent, Kagrotra is screaming orders at her troops, 
“Crush the Puny creatures!”. She is displeased at how her troops have performed. “Do I have to do everything?” she shouts as she hurls a javelin. Unfortunately, the Javelin slips through her fingers. The weapon misses the target and embeds itself in the dirt. 

Vardis reaches for his scorching ray spell and fires at the two goblins that fleed Thistle and Thuul. Only the one who fired the arrow is hit a severe blow.
Wallace, who sees the Ogre within reach, slashes, and misses. 
Unla Kee stares at the battlefield and ponders how to twist fate. He knows exactly what to do and casts Rimes Binding Ice. The ogre is trapped, and the orc standing next to him is not. 
Thistle kills the goblin that attacked him with a scimitar. 
The goblin with the bow hits Unla Kee and shouts, “finally!” 
Unla Kee still refuses to find cover. 
An orc advances on Wallace and Orsik, and his axe attack hits the ground with a great thud. The location of the ground is nowhere near Wallace and Orsik. 
The ogre looks down at his god pleadingly. 
Orsik kills the Orc that had advanced on him. 
The goblin with the Green Jacket steps out from cover and mumbles, “We must kill the wizard!” His arrow hits for 3 HP. 
Unla Kee still refuses to find cover. 
Orsik shouts, “go ahead and try and kill us puny creatures! You can’t even kill an unarmed wizard!” 
But then Kagrota surprises everyone when she casts a spiritual weapon in the form of a hammer and hits Unla Kee. 
Unla Kee, realizing that if he were to flee, the universe would end, stands his ground. 
Vardis tries to help and casts faerie fire, but only one of the orcs is affected. Wallace turns to Unla Kee, who gets the hint and twists the fates to help Wallace strike a critical hit on the Ogre. The Ogre is wounded. 
Unla Kee then points to where he wants Wallace to stand, advances, and casts Aganazzer’s scorcher. The Ogre avoids most of the damage; an orc that had been fighting Wallace doesn’t, and a goblin behind the Ogre dies. 
Orsik had shouted, “get a healing potion,” but was drowned by the burning fire and the screams of death. 
Thistle flies up to get a better angle of attack on the Ogre, reducing Kagrota to 1 HP. She screams, “Fairies! We had a deal with Prince Thornacious!” 
Unla Kee is standing beside an Orc, unarmored, and the Orc misses again. 
The creature stares at his weapon in confusion. Unla Kee stands his ground smugly. 
Orsik kills that orc and then advances on the Ogre. 
Meanwhile, the Greek Jacket Goblin exits the shadows and fires at Unla Kee. And squeals when he sees Unla Kee fall. 
The Ogre attacks Wallace with her spiritual weapon, strikes the flanked Orsik with her hammer, and swings over his head. 
Vardis, who sees Unla Kee on the ground, rushes to his side and heals him. 
Wallace punches the Ogre, who dies. 
Unla Kee, now conscious, rushes up to an orc next to Orsik. He then uses another scorcher spell, killing the Green Jacket Goblin and wounding the Orc. Unla Kee, demonstrating his complete and utter lack of sense of self-preservation, distracts the Orc, who Orsik kills. 
After picking the dead bodies for treasure, the adventurers find scattered coins that add up to 50 gold, and six sapphires worth 100 gold. 
Finally, the group turns to the prisoners once the gold is collected. 
At first, the prisoners speak over each other. The man tries to explain things, and a woman interrupts him and says he’s wrong. And then, finally, another woman explains that they were all part of a commune that Kagrota raided. Bad Fruul had sent Kagrota to destroy the commune, and as a result, several members died.
Orsik and Vardis attempt to express sorrow. 
Thistle, remembering their mission, asks to see their hands to see if any finger is missing. Seeing none, he asks if they have seen a woman matching Tove’s description of his mother. The prisoners shake their heads sadly. One of the women sees the finger and starts to cry hysterically. Her friends console her and explain that that belonged to one of the commune's members. 
Orsik turns to them and asks if they had ever seen a half-elf matching Tove’s description.
One of the women says, “Oh yes! They were thick as thieves with Kagrota.” 
“I told you we should have fed him to the Wyvern.” 
Orsik asks if they know nothing about Bad Fruul.
“Bad who?” 
With that, the group enters the cabin. Inside is a small rustic affair, with animal skins and antlers hanging from the walls. 
“What an ordinary woodman’s cabin,” says Thistle. 
“What a mess,” says Wallace, who almost pukes from the rotting food and discarded trash scattered about the room.
Unla Kee examines a large pile of tools that lay in a haphazard pile in one corner.
Thistle finds a trapdoor under the rug. 
“Classic,” shouts Orsik.
Thistle takes a steep ladder that leads down into a small earthen room beneath the cabin. 
He gasps. The room is not a root cellar. It is far more disturbing. Long multicolored ropes of braided human hair strung with small bells that tinkle as he moves through them hang from the ceiling. 
Orsik and Vardis jump down, and all three notice a crude altar on the far side of the chamber whose top bears the painted image of a black sunburst. 
Two black candles flanking an elven woman’s skull and the shattered remains of a jade statuette are resting atop that stand. Beside the altar lay several chests and bags filled with treasure. 
“Bane,” they both mutter. 
Everyone turns to them, but Thistle, whose Uncle Joon explained everything to him, explains. 
*Bane is a ruthless god of tyranny who believes himself to be the rightful ruler of all the planes and is bent on dominating the Realms. He demands absolute loyalty from his followers and deals harshly with his enemies. When Torm slew Bane during The Time of the Troubles, Cyric absorbed most of his followers. Cyric and his worshipers are chief among Bane’s foes for this crime.  Bane returned in 1372 DR, stronger than before and eager to resume his conquest of the Realms. At that point, many of his former devotees repented and returned to his church. Today his followers can be found everywhere, even among the Lord’s Alliance and the Zhentarim. Worshipers of Bane adhere to a strict hierarchical structure and brook no dissent.*
And with that, the group heads back to Parnast. Foremost on their mind is that  Chandra Stol will be so upset that she has to feed the Wyvern cooked meat instead of living prey.
