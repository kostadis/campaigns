# Chapter 52 - Banishment
The 3HPP, with the goblins, led by Tlega and Mirk, enter Maergun’s throne room. The throne room is newly finished with smooth walls, a massive throne, Drauchanar hanging from a chain, and a cyclops, displacer beast, and the Fomorian. 
Thistle stares at the Fomorian and tries to remember what he knows. He sighs. It’s from the official textbook of the Seelie kingdom, which is a tad bit biased. He recalled that the book said, “*They are the most hideous and wicked of all giantkind are the godless Fomorians, whose deformed bodies reflect their vile demeanors. Some have facial features randomly distributed around their misshapen, warty heads. Others have limbs of grossly different sizes and shapes, or emit terrible howls each time they draw breath through misshapen mouths. However, their wretched appearance rarely evokes sympathy, for the Fomorians brought their doom upon themselves with the evil that rules their hearts and minds.”* 

Thistle looks at Maergun and thinks, “Definitely got the ugly, right.” And then he remembers the official histories of the elves that were mandatory reading, “*The elves remember when the Fomorians were among the most handsome of races, possessed of brilliant minds and unrivaled magical ability. However, that physical perfection did not extend to their hearts as a lust for magic and power consumed them.*
*The Fomorians sought to conquer the Feywild and enslave its inhabitants, claiming those creatures’ magic for themselves. When the fey united to defend their realm, the Fomorians fought them and were subjected to a terrible curse.*
*One by one, the giants fell as their bodies were warped to reflect the evil in their hearts. Stripped of their grace and magical power, the wretched horrors fled from the light, delving deep beneath the world to nurse their hatred. Cursing their fate, they have ever after plotted vengeance against the fey that wronged them.”*
Thuul rushes to the Cyclops and bites. Thistle follows up the splash of acid with a pair of arrows that wound the one-eyed monster.

The other human on the still uninjured Bullette commands Orsik and Vardis to flee, but they see through the magical tones of the voice and ignore him. 

Thistle casts Hunter’s Mark and safely strikes the Bullette with arrows from the sky.

Orsik grumbles at that because the Bulette strikes him with its jaw, even though his buddy uses blinding light to distract the subterranean monstrosity. 

“When you aim for Orsik, don’t miss,” gnarls Orsik, who then kills the creature and the rider whose last words are, “You shall not seize the stone from the cult of the black earth!” 

Orsik can’t resist as his opponent bleeds from the wound, “How do you know we were taking the stone? We were just walking along the field. You attacked us for no reason. 

The fairy chimes in, “The stone belongs in a museum!” 

The other human shouts, “You would let us keep the stone?” 

“I don’t trust somebody who just attacks me. And you’re both dying. So we’ll just continue with the killing.” 

Thistle, Thuul, and Unla Kee strike the two humans from the safety of the sky. Before they die, both try to command Vardis and Unla Kee. The first fails, and the second tells Orsik to flee. Unfortunately, the command doesn’t stop Orsik from striking him down when the last surviving combatant tries to run away. 

The battle is over, and the 3HPP scavenges equipment from their assailants. The stone breastplate is a strange construction that is unheard of. 

Sky Pony tribesmen appear as the 3HPP pluck the treasures from their dead enemies from the tree line. 

Unla Kee, Thistle, and Orsik debate how to deal with the situation, and while they are arguing, Vardis, who has some experience with religious matters, picks up the body of a dead assailant and shouts, “We defended the stone from these thieves!” 

The Shaman shouts, “The Sky Pony avatar has saved our relics!” And then he shouts, “Where is the one that had the wings of a Sky Pony!”  

Unla Kee thinks, “Should we go for the I am the vengeful avatar or the super-cool avatar that everyone wants to be friends with?” 

He dismisses his invisibility and shouts, “I am here. Bow before me.” It’s the most amazingly cool entrance the Shamans and Sky Pony tribes have ever seen. Suddenly, a halfling with the 30’ wing span of a Pegasus appears over their heads. They are in awe and immediately start bowing. 

The Shaman shouts, “We will stage a massive festival in your honor. We will hunt, get drinks, and prepare! And to protect the totem, we will leave 20 of our best warriors here while we bring a thousand more!” 

Orsik is aghast. This has gone terribly, and he thinks, “Great work, Unla Kee; instead of 20, we now have a 1000.” 

Unla Kee is now very annoyed. This was such a brilliant plan: Convince the tribe that he was the Avatar, and then they would let him steal their rock. But now that they see the almost theft as a failure, they weren’t there to protect the rock; they want to deploy an entire army to defend it. 

Orsik shouts, “Let them have the party; we can ask them questions while drunk.” 

That’s when Unla Kee hits upon a great idea; they failed him, so they should do something as penance—“I command you to go on a vision quest, three days out, then spend a whole day fasting, and then return in three days. The vision quest will tell you how to protect this rock, always!”  

The Shaman stares at Unla Kee, decides that this avatar of the Sky Pony speaks the truth, and shouts to his tribesmen. 

As they leave, they salute and cheer the Sky Pony. 

Unla Kee turns to Vardis, “So we have seven days to figure out this rock.” The mage pauses, “I want us to do something nice for them, like make them a new totem or something.” 

Vardis stares at him, “Are you getting soft?” 

The halfling smirks, “Of course not. I want them to worship me, and a benevolent god always attracts more followers than one that does not bestow gifts.” 

With the Shaman and the Sky Pony tribe gone, Unla Kee and Orsik examine the rock. 

“We can’t carry this.” 

“Nope, too heavy for the airship.” 

Then, Unla Kee notices some peculiar lines on the rock. He stares at them for a bit, and it hits him: These are trace lines. He’s read about this somewhere and remembers that you must trace the lines in the proper order, and the artifact's power will be unlocked.  

The diviner asks a power, and it tells him how to trace the lines. When the last line is traced, he is covered in oozing blisters. Fortunately, thanks to his blessed horn, he can cast cure wounds, and the illness disappears as fast as it came. 

Unla kee realizes the rock can be shrunken and enlarged thrice daily. It also can create an effect similar to the wand of wonder. 

“How glorious!” 

The rest of the 3HPP, upon hearing that, groan.

Unla Kee then turns to where the rock was and uses a stone-shape spell to craft an image of himself. Orsik places a magical bean and an enchanted egg in a bowl, which, when broken, creates a water pool. 

“I hope they figure out how to use this.”
