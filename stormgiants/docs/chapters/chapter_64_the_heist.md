# Chapter 64 - The heist. 
## Orsic
Orsic was tormented. He was at a crossroads. From a disreputable hired hand known throughout the North, he had found inside himself the leader of the soldiers that defended Parnast. And then, thanks to the miraculous effects of a ghost that had aged him, he had become his father. 
He was free of his reputation both externally and internally. 
But he was conflicted. 
Bob Saget was in Silverymoon. And the demon was planning to destroy the city. 
“Should I abandon them to their fate?” 
He pauses. 
“The chaotic destruction of Silverymoon would be quite the feather in my cap and probably get a notable promotion within the ranks of the Zhentarim.” 
He pauses. 
“But do I want to be known as a member of the infamous 3HPP? Do I want to have to hide my face wherever I go?” 
His reverie is interrupted. 
“Okay, I think I found the location of the conch,” states the fairy. 
Orsic listens carefully. 
And then stops, “Thistle, tell us what to do.”
## Thistle
The fairy is shocked. Ever since the dwarf got his intelligence boost, he’s been insufferable. 
But he can see how the dwarf is distracted. 
And so reluctantly, he explains his plan. First, he shows them the map. 

“We have two problems. The first is that there are a lot of giants.”
Unla Kee shouts, “I have a fireball.”
“They are fire giants.” 
Unla Kee is not deterred, “I have cloud kill.” 
Thistle stares at him, “There are at least 20 Fire Giants.” 
Unla Kee mutters, “I could dominate them?” 
“They are giants.” 
Unla Kee is miffed, “What’s the point of knowing these spells if I can’t use them?” 
Thistle wonders if Unla Kee is toying with the idea of dominating him and then returns to his plan. 
“With all of the giants, a frontal attack will fail. Therefore, I propose a distraction.” 
He then points to the carts that circle the upper level and says, “The plan is you get into the carts and let the carts take you around the upper level. When you enter the tunnel leading to the pressing mill, you jump off and then carefully and quietly sneak into the elevator area. Then you go down the elevator shaft.”
“And what will you be doing?”
“Wiri mentioned that whenever the wheel breaks, the giants rush in. I will make the wheel break by magically reducing a key part. With the giants distracted, I’ll fly through the Vodinoid chamber and catch up with you near the elevator on the lower floor.” 
Thistle watches as Orsic mulls it over and is pleased when the dwarf nods. 

## Orsic
“What did he say?” he thinks. 
Orsic is too busy thinking about his reputation and the fun of not being hunted across the North to pay attention to Thistle’s plan. 
“So I get into the cart and then down the elevator,” he mumbles. 
“Good, good.” 
## Thistle
“There is one problem: where the tracks end, there is a fire salamander that we must kill quietly.” 
Thistle turns to Unla Kee, “Do you have a silence spell?”
“No.” 
Thistle remembers the last heist that Unla Kee was involved in, Silverymoon, and how he didn’t have a silence spell there either. 
“Figures.” 
## Unla Kee
The wizard is irritated but unbowed. 
“There are other ways to deal with this problem,” he mutters. 
And from his head, a shimmering lance of power flew out and struck the creature. It writhed from the psychic pain and was stunned. 
Thistle strikes him twice with arrows, and the creature winces, caustic blood dripping. 
Unla Kee casts a toll on the dead, but the creature somehow avoids doing so. 
Orsic strikes and also fails to kill him. 
But Thistle’s arrows finish the job.
“No noise,” mutters Unla Kee. 
He’s pleased when the rest of the group acknowledges his achievement. Although he does notice Vardis sprawled on the ground. 
It’s only then that Unla Kee notices the dwarven prisoners. 
“Please let us go.” 
Unla Kee turns to Thistle: " If they stop filling the carts, the giants will notice. Do we want them to keep filling them?”
“Nah.”
“Do we want to fill the cart with Salamander parts?” 
“Yes, the cart behind us.”
## Thistle
The fairy watches as the 2HP loads the carts and moves towards the pressing mill. He pauses and thinks, “No time like the present.”

He flies down to the whee lroom and observes the situation.  A large fireplace on the north keeps the area warm and list. The enormous wheel with wooden spokes dominates the room.
He looks at the wheel more carefully and notes that the wheel is rigged to the bucket chain that travels through the forge. 
Thistle then examines the prisoners moving the wheel. There are ten shield dwarves with iron manacles, and all look exhausted.
“They won’t be of any use,” he thinks. 
“And there is the muscle,” he mumbles. He notes two ogres clad head to toe in spiked armor, ensuring none of the dwarves stops working. 
## Orsic
As Orsic floats on the slowly moving bucket chain, he wonders, “Does Thistle realize that he needs to wait before breaking the wheel?”
And the bucket chain stops. 
“No, he didn’t.” 
## Thistle
Thistle is thrilled with the results. The wheel is destroyed, and the Fire Giants seem to be converging on it. 
He can hear Duke Zalto rushing up the stairs. 
“Time to go catch up with my friends.”
Then his heart turns cold. 
“Oops.”’
Thistle flies to his stranded allies.  
## Orsic
“This was your plan?” mutters the very irritated dwarf. 
Now that the dwarf is no longer distracted by metaphysical considerations of his reputation and is focused on surviving, he remembers why he always comes up with plans. 
“Well, we had a lot of plans that we were toying with, and it was easy to get confused,” explains a very apologetic Fairy. 
Orsic is unimpressed and frustrated. He grabs the map Thistle drew of the lower level, examines it, and then starts speaking. 
“Okay, so we have a new plan. We jump from bucket to bucket to the edge of the room with the Vodinoid, jump down and head down to where the elevator room is, and enter the dining hall from there. And then hit the bedrooms.” 
“Jump?” asks Vardis.
Orsik looks at him and realizes that his friend is not happy at the height. He thinks for a moment if he should tell him and decides that it’s probably for the best, “I can cast feather fall.” 

## Vardis
As he floats to the ground, he glares at the fairy, who whispers, “Okay, now that we’re here, we need to quickly go through the elevator shaft into the dining hall.” 
“Will the goblins be there?” asks Orsik
“Probably?” says Thistle.
Vardis notices the frustration in Orsik’s face. 
As they approach the dining hall, they huddle about how to get past them. 
“Intimidate or bluff?” asks Vardis.
Unla Kee can’t resist, “Heare me out, I could cast dominate person.” 
Vardis laughs as Orsik turns to him and growls, “Okay, so you dominated one of them. What about the rest?” 
Vardis watches as a disgruntled Unla Kee marches, acting as if he is in charge. The cleric observes how the goblins ignore them, even while watching them. “They probably see a lot of strange people.” 
Before Orsik can say anything, Thistle is holding the lever of the north door and pointing to Orsik, urging him to push the door. 
Thistle takes a bow when the door opens, thanks to the heroic feat of strength. 
Vardis watches how an infuriated Orsik mutters, “Good for you! You did such a good job!”
## Thistle
“I did. I flew up here and moved the door handle nine feet up in the air. It’s not like we have anyone else who can do that.” 
And with that, the fairy enters the room. 

Vardis takes one look at the room and says, “No kink shaming.”
Thistle is surprised, so he looks around, trying to see what is there. There is an enormous bed with a frame made from crisscrossing iron sword blades welded together. 
“What’s he talking about … “he asks, then notices that dangling above the bed are large chains and manacles, the purpose of which is known only to the duke and the duchess. 
Behind the bed, Thistle notes a 15-foot-diameter shield made from adult black dragon scales displayed on the north wall behind the bed. 
He looks up at the ceiling, where an iron chandelier hangs over the center of the room, near the foot of the bed. Thistle’s eye is drawn to a chain connected to the chandelier, snagged on an iron hook mounted to the east wall above a 10-foot-tall basalt table with some valuable items resting on it and a large iron chest tucked underneath it. 
Two wardrobes of fire-scorched wood stand against the west wall. A giant barrel in the southeast corner serves as a stool and rests in front of a burnished, full-length mirror mounted in the south corner of the east wall. An iron brazier hangs from a hook mounted on the south wall near the door.
Thistle is about to try to steal some of the treasure from the basalt table, but Orsik points to the chest and shouts, “Open it now.” 
Unla Kee casts a knock and then opens the chest. Thistle was too close and could not avoid the blast of incendiary fire. When the fire clears out, he notices that the mage is dead. He also sees the cleric flat on his back, having tripped trying to get out. 
“Typical.” 
At this time, Thistle is particularly pleased with his recommendation to close the door when they enter. He knows Orsik will be impressed with his tactical awareness.
## Orsik
He is not impressed.
Inside the chest is a giant-sized adamantine crown set with six black opals, which are worth a lot of gold. 
Thistle smiles even more when he notices thousands of gold pieces underneath the ashes in the chest. 
And he’s particularly pleased with the discovery of the conch. 
There are several more magic items in the chest: a javelin of lightning, a wand of the warmage, and adamantine-scale armor. Orsik picks up the magical ring and sighs, “Another ring of jumping.” 
Vardis nudges him, “I probably need one, given how clumsy I am.” 
“Alright, let’s go. Let’s get the hell out of here.” 
Thistle asks, “What about all of this stuff?” He points at the treasured items on the table. 
“We don’t need it.”
Orsik is surprised when the fairy says, “No, I am going to put this stuff into our bag of holding.” 
Orsik tries not to laugh as he sees the fairy struggle to move a 750-pound hairbrush. 
“I don’t think so.” 
“Orsik, come up here!” 
“I want to leave. Dude, we just made 15,000 gold. Let’s get the fuck out of here.” 
Orsik is confused by Thistle’s sudden greed. 
“There are 5000 more right here!” shouts the Fairy. 
“I don’t want to fight 20 fire giants.” 
And with that, Orsik groans as Unla Kee interjects, “Why is getting this last item going to produce 20 Fire Giants?” 
“Because it’s going to take time!” 
“let’s take a vote!” shouts Thistle. 
“YOU TAKE A VOTE! I AM LEAVING!” says the dwarven artificer and marches off. 
As Orsik marches towards the door, he notices Unla Kee reach for his rock of wonder. The wizard is about to invoke some spell when Orsik points at the door and shouts, “How are you going to open it without me?” 
Thistle turns to Vardis, “Do you want the treasure?” 
“Nah. Nothing piques my interest.” 
“I guess we could come back to kill all the giants?” observes Thistle.
“Yeah, like ram the cloud castle until the giants die!” mutters Orsic.
“Or unleash Bob Saget’s zombie army?” suggests Unla Kee. 
With that, the group heads out. They pass the goblins setting the dinner tables and use the elevator to go up to the top of Ironslag. There, Orsik demonstrates another feat of strength when he opens the massive iron portcullis that bars the exit to the Yak Folk village. 
He smiles and begins the long rope climb into the castle. As he looks from the walls, he notes that the Zombies are still there. 
Finally, he enters the control tower.
## Escarlotta
Escarlotta has been waiting anxiously for the 3HPP to return. She’s made her husband’s life miserable, complaining that she shouldn’t have to rely on little people to get her revenge. 
And then she feels the dwarf enter her control tower.
“Is she dead?” 
“We have the conch,” mutters Orsik. 
“I sent you to kill the Duchess! IS SHE DEAD?”
“Yes, she’s dead!” 
Escarlota imagines a suitably gruesome death and, for a moment, is happy. 
She’s even happier when Vardis starts singing, “Ding dong, the Duchess is dead.” 
Then he shouts, “Now let’s kill Sansuri! That horrible excrement of cumulous cloud dares to pretend she is favored among all other cloud giants!” 
## Orsik
Orsik once again is thinking about Bob. He’s thinking about how much better his life is now that he’s a hero and older. 
He turns to Vardis, “My son says we should ally with Bob against the city of Silverymoon.”
Vardis stares at him momentarily and thinks – “His method acting is getting a little bit weird.” 
Unla disagrees, “Let’s kill him.” 
Thistle reminds Orsik of the original plan – “That we take down Bob and frame house Margaster. To do that, we have to let some chaos reign.”
“Demons and the like slaughtering folks through the city?” 
Unla Kee surprises Orsik, “I was just gonna say that sound’s pretty fucked up, and I could get behind it.” 
“You wanted to kill Bob?” 
“I want the riskiest plan.” 
“There are a lot of things that could go wrong with trying to pint the slaughter of innocent hundreds of people on a greater devil.” 
The fallen cleric of Corellon interjects, “Normally, I am with Orsik, but this time, I think we just let it be. Bad things happen.” 
Orsik stares dumbfounded, “Aren’t we gonna take the blame because it’s our show?” 
The cleric who was banned from every temple of his god says, “I don’t know. There’s a cambion, and there will be a lot of chaos. There’s no guarantee we get blamed.” He then pauses, “And I am not sure Silverymoon is long for this world. Furthermore, our track record of being conniving is not as strong as you think. “
Thistle observes, “Bob is going to figure out how to pin the blame on us – unless we explicitly fight him.” 
Orisk hears this and thinks about his newfound reputation, “Taking him out would be the best for our reputation.” 
“For our reputations, yes. For our lives, walk away.” 
Thistle observes, “We need allies.” 
Orsik enumerates all the allies they could get: “Lord Zymorven, the Hand of Yartar, Urgula from Triboar, soldiers from Everlund, members of the watch of Silverymoon who are fans of the 3HPP.”
Unla Kee can’t resist, “Hsing of the Lord’s Alliance?” 
Orsik ignores him and continues, “We don’t have an ally problem. Our problem is how do convince people that we didn’t bring a demon into the city?” 
And then Thistle has a brilliant idea – “We didn’t. We go to the Lord of Silverymoon and declare that Bob Saget, our friend, is dead. A demon killed him. And we have come to deal with that foul creature.” 
“What if they try and use spells on us to see if we are lying?” 
“We are emissaries of Everlund. They won’t use magic.” 
Orsik mulls it over, “We kill Bob.” 
Vardis mutters, “Walking away is always the better option.” 
Orsik ponders whether there is a reason he had the reputation he had.
