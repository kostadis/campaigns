# Chapter 71 - Seeing through new eyes
And it’s just then that Adimond Kroskas’ latest manipulation of the fairy happens. 
Thistle is covered in a green, gooey substance. 
Thistle stares at the pool of water and shrieks as the gooey substance evaporates.
“This is horrible.”
The giant queen, the imperator, the storm giant guards, the other half-pints, and the pint stare at the screeching fairy. 
Thistle flies up and screams - “LOOK AT THIS!” 
They all stare at him, uncertain. 
Taking each eye in one hand, he stretches them open and shouts – “HE GAVE ME DRAGON EYES!” 
Silence. 
“THIS IS AWFUL.”
Orsic coughs, “Is that all?” 
“Oh, no, I have true seeing as well, "Thistle announces, his voice trembling with a mix of fear and excitement. True seeing, a rare and powerful ability that allows him to perceive the world as it truly is, beyond any magical illusions or disguises. 
“That’s cool.” 
“Yes, that’s cool, but dragon eyes? WHAT WILL I SAY AT THE GRAND BALL? And what will my love say.” 
The giants are trying hard to squint and see the tiny eyeballs on the 18-inch fairy. 
Queen Serissa coughs, “I am not sure I can see the issue.” 
Thistle flies into her face, opens his left eye, stretches the eyelid open, takes his left and pulls the eyelids even further apart, and then, with his right hand, points at the eyeball, which looks very dragon-like—“This is an abomination.” 
Serissa squints and stares and mutters, “It’s quite fetching!” 
“IT’S DRAGON EYES!” 
The Imperator, trying to calm everyone down, points out that it comes with a blessing: True Seeing. 
Thistle glares at him. 
And just then, Orsic steps forward - “Do I look different? I have been trying to change and want to know if True Seeing reveals anything.” 
“REALLY? THAT’S WHAT YOU ARE WORRIED ABOUT? HOW ABOUT FOCUSING ON ME FOR A MOMENT? Adimond Kroskas is messing with me again.” He starts to sob, “I am not one of his freakish experiments.” And after his sobbing stops, he mutters, “I am not a freak.” 
Orsic shrugs, “Okay, not the right time.” 
Thistle stares at him, “Orsic.” 
“Yes,” says the dwarf expectantly.
“Nothing’s changed.” 
Queen Serissa coughs, “Can we return to the matters of importance?” 
Thistle flies right into her face, opening his eyes wide, “THIS IS IMPORTANT!” 
The Queen stares at the rage in Thistle and smiles, “You are like the storm that rages. But now, I need you to focus so we can save my father and get your revenge on Adimond Kroskas.” She waves her hand at Unla Kee, “The mage claims to know where this,” she holds up a wooden coin with a golden goose on it, “comes from. He says it’s a chip used on a gambling ship in Yartar, called ‘The Golden Goose.’” 

“A booze cruiser,” corrects Unla Kee. “Didn’t let me in. Said I wasn’t good enough.” He pauses and mumbles, “Some of you thought it was because I had no money and am a diviner; I think it’s racism.” 
The Imperator coughs, “Perhaps we can return to the topic of hand.” 
“The Maelstrom has a magical device that can teleport you anywhere you want. And where would that be?” 
“Yartar, I guess?” mutters Orsik. He recalls the mess that was Yartar—the home of the Harpers that was destroyed by Adimond Kroskas, his betrayal of the Harpers, and the rescue of Lord Zymorven’s son from inside the prison where a vampire was involved in some kind of magical experimentation. Then he wonders, or was it torture? 
Unla Kee – “What about the castle? I want my castle back. We fought and clawed and worked our way to get that castle. And it’s so cool.”  
Orsic sighs, “We’ll have to deal with the Vampire.”
“I have sunbeam,” mutters Vardis.
“That will be critical.” 
With that, the group leaves the Queen and is teleported to just outside the control room of Blagothkus’ castle. As they enter, Thistle shouts, “I AM NOT A FREAK.” 
Blagothkus is taken aback. He tries to lower himself to see what Thistle is shrieking about, but he can’t get that close to the ground. So he lowers his hand and brings the 18-inch creature to a few inches from his eye. 
“Oh, you have dragon eyes! That’s so cool.” 
Thistle pouts, “My arch nemesis did this to me. It’s not cool.” 
Escarlotta asks in the soothing, silky voice of a mother to her child, “Do you have any cool powers?” 
Thistle brims with pride, “Yes.” 
“He can see me for who I am,” answers Orsik. 
“That’s so cool!” exclaims Escarlotta as if she were talking to a child trying to decide if he likes his birthday present. 
“I thought you hated dragons,” mutters Thistle. And with as much snark as Queen Titania could muster asks, “You -are- a giant, right?” 
Escarlotta sighs, “It was Blagothkus’ infatuation with power and obsession with Dragons that brought down the Ordning and killed my son.” 
Blagothkus laughs uncomfortably, the repaired relationship still fragile, “I did hate them, but not anymore. I have a relationship with them.” 
“What’s the difference,” asks Vaardis. 
“I wouldn’t invite them for dinner, whereas in the past, I would have them for dinner?”
“Live and let live?” pipes Orsik.
“As long as they live over there and we live over here. I know you think that’s segregation and it’s racist. But I don’t. I think different people want to be with their kind. And we’re all better off when they are there, and we are here.” 
Unla Kee turns to Orsik – “Racism.” Orsik nods. 
Escarlotta turns the conversation around – “You have been gone for several days. The Vampire needed to be fed. We found some places. They seem to be very happy and satiated.” 
“Little people?” asks Orsik. 
The moonstones in the control room turn bright red, almost as if Escarlotta is embarrassed. 
“Yes. There are so many of them and we had a problem. But we noticed that you didn’t like the Uthgars so we set the Vampires on a tribe of them we spotted.” 
At this point, Unla Kee says, “All of this good vs evil is not working for me. What I see is a bunch of racists and bullies who are causing harm.  Is it really about killing a giant or a drow or a human who is evil, or is it about fighting the evil in their hearts, racism?” 
“And how do we do that?” 
“Kill them all?” 
There is a pause. The control room disappears, and the sky is visible. 
“I am assuming that killing is only one option? My husband and I have reformed.” 
“I suppose,” as Unla Kee absorbs the apparent threat that Escarlotta is making, mess with her and hers, and Unla Kee might fall into the void. 
“Enough of this; tell us what happened at the Maelstrom?” 
So Thistle walks them through what happened. Both cloud giants are horrified when he gets to the part about Adimond Kroskas. 
“What did the Bane of the Desert want to do?” 
“The who?”
“The blue dragon you described is the Bane of the Desert. She is a powerful, ancient blue dragon. And she wanted to make a hybrid fey-dragon? And that fool Serissa was working with her?”
Blagothkus shudders, “We must go find Hekaton. We need his strength to defeat the dragon.” 
“But first, we need to get rid of this Vampire,” observes Unla Kee. “The Vampire is the worst kind of racist. It feeds on those it thinks are inferior. And this particular vampire, Sandesyl, wants to turn every dragon into a purer form of dragon. I mean, she’s all kind of wrong.” 
With that, the group turns to debating how to kill the Sandesyl. Eventually, they remember her offer to leave the castle if they went on a quest for her. 
Thistle argues that before attempting to kill a vampire, perhaps the quest won’t be as damning as they think and less life-threatening. 
Blagothkus notes the urgency of saving the realms and suggests that this is not the time to remove unwanted guests. 
Unla Kee will have none of that and shouts, “What do we want? It's a vampire-free zone. When do we want it? Now!” 
Thistle wins the day and approaches Sandesyl’s tower in the middle of the day. 
The vampire hides in a shroud of darkness, obscuring everything inside the tower. What Sandesyl doesn’t know is that Thistle can see in the darkness. 
“I’ve never had a fey vampire spawn. Would you like to be one?” 
Vaardis can’t resist, “A fey-vampire-dragon? A triple threat!” 
“I am not a freak!” 
The cleric is shocked, “I didn’t say you were!” 
“I am not a freak!” 
The vampire coughs, “I presume you are here to go on the quest to find something for me?” When Sandesyl sees the group be silent, she explains – “What I am looking for is a particular tome that when the apostate Severin attempted to bring Tiamat into the world, was lost.” 
When probed about its contents, she deflects – “Some arcane details of historical importance. Nothing that would interest anyone other than a member of the cult.” 
Orsik asks, “A play?” 
“A romance novel?” queries Thistle, remembering her interests. 
And with that, Sandesyl turns to one of her spawns, “It’s not the final book in the series. This is for official cult business.” 
The spawn, safely hidden in the dark, rolls his eyes at the Vampire. Thistle, who can see the expression on his face, tries hard to suppress laughter. 
The vampire continues, “The magical tome is in Xanothal’s keep. I would get it myself, but there is an interdimensional puzzle that you need to go through that is always bathed in bright sunlight. As for why it was there? Towards the ritual's climax, the Cult was split between traditionalists and apocalypse seekers. Severin needed to rally everyone to the cause and offered to hand over a Blue Dragon mask to a traditionalist who would investigate the mask. To do that investigation, he needed the book. Unfortunately for Severin, an inner circle member, Iskander betrayed the cult to the Lord’s Alliance, who sent the Heroes of the War of the Dragon to the tower. The heroes secured the mask but missed the tome. I want that time.” 
Vaardis sighs, “And let me guess; it’s one of those wizard towers with all sorts of crazy puzzles that reset after everybody escapes?” 
“Yes. “ 
“How cliché.” 
“I agree,” sighs the Vampire. “All these powerful wizards acquiring all sorts of power, and what do they do? They go and create an automatically re-stocking and re-populating wizard tower.” 
Thistle sighs, “I don’t want to help the cult of the dragon. What’s in it for us?” 
“This castle? I am a powerful and ancient vampire who has at her beck and call an ancient white dracolich. This is the only way you regain control of this castle.” 
She pauses, “Although the idea of a fey-vampire spawn is starting to look attractive.” 
After Thistle squirms, “I have a privileged position in this castle. But my problem is that I can’t control it. Escarlotta is impervious to my charms. And so I have had to make arrangements with folks who take control of the castle. Now, this opportunity to get this important historical tome has presented itself, and I think to myself, maybe a new home is just what I need. I will find myself a nice dark lair, study the lore of the cult, and re-establish some of the rituals that were interrupted due to the usurper.” 
“That’s a long-term career plan,” observes Vardis. After his unfortunate dismissal from the temples of Corellon, he has been wondering what the future holds. Her bold proposal to reanimate a cult makes him think that perhaps a personal long-term career goal of establishing an alternative believers cult of the Elf god might be an option. 
Sandesyl continues while Vardis is distracted by his musings, “After all, the true believers know that the Usurper had it wrong. Salvation is not when Tiamat enters the world, but when all dragons are turned into dracoliches and rule Faerun.” 
“Tell me about the Doom of the Desert before I answer?” 
“Ah yes, Ilymrith. She is an ancient blue dragon.” And if it was possible for a vampire to show love, Sandesyl’s eyes and body glitter with a powerful lust and passion for the creature. “She is one of the greatest of dragon kind.” And then she let slip, “With that time, I could convince her to embrace the endless life of the Dracolich and the immortality it offers.” Then she continues holding herself as if embracing the dragon in her arms, “I am told that the Doom is famous for its experiments. She has bred new kinds of dragons and new kinds of species.”
“Dragon-fey?”
At that, Sandesyl’s reverie is interrupted, and she starts to laugh, “Don’t be ridiculous; that’s impossible.” And then she turns to Thistle, “Ilymrith has been working to undermine the Giant world. Not because she cares about Giants, but she needed access to the Storm Giant’s castle to continue her experiments on hybridization of dragons,” pauses and turns to Thistle, “and fey.” Then she cackles at the idea. 
Thistle whispers, “I’ll have to talk with my colleagues.” 
“Take your time; it’s not like I am going anywhere…” 
Thistle turns to his friends – “We can just charge?” 
Unla Kee, ever the gambler, stops him. “Look, I am all for charging, but I think a clever ploy has a better chance of success.”
“Let’s go find out what exactly this Dracolich who is on the tight leash of this Vampire is really about,” mutters Vardis. 

The 3HPP heads into the giant glacier that Blagothkus’ castle is built around and descends 50 feet into a large chamber through icy tunnels of blue ice. The walls are translucent, revealing objects embedded in the ice – coins, helmets, livestock, a handful of kobolds, and an ogre or two. Frost coats the tunnel floors and crunches underfoot as they advance. 
Finally the 3HPP arrives in the main vault, a glittering cavern of ice with jagged walls and icicles the size of stalactites. The grand vault is split into two levels: an egg-shaped upper level with a sheer ledge overlooking a sunken level 30 feet below, where a massive pile of treasure rests beneath an icy glaze. Clinging to the ceiling above the hoard, wings tucked in tight along its sides with claws gripping the ice, is a vast white dracolich. 
Thistle then turns to Vardis, “I know what a Dracolich is. It’s a dragon born from an egg that a Vampiric stork brings.” 
Before Orsik and Thistle can argue, Unla Kee interrupts them – “A Dracolich, like a lich, has a phylactery that is used to store their life essence. If you control the phylactery, you control the dragon.” 
“I’ll handle this,” mutters the dwarf. 
“Hello, neighbor.” 
The dracolich opens an eye and then closes it, obviously uninterested. 
Unla Kee looks at Orsik and mutters, “Never send a fighter to do a wizard’s job.”
“Oh, great and powerful dragon!” 
At this, the Dracolich opens its eyes, spreads its wings, and lands just far enough to be dangerous. 
“We wanted to hear your story and how it is that you are here.”
The Dracolich sighs and begins, “I am Glazhael the Undying. I was once Glazhael the Cloudchaser who, with Talis, ruled over the skies of the north. Until we met the thrice-damned heroes of the war of the dragons, I made a deal with them at our first meeting: they would go their way, and I would go mine. And I did, for a time, until Talis called on me to tell me that the White Wyrmspeaker had been captured. Severin had declared that she was the new Wyrmspeaker and wanted me to bask in her glory in the coming of Tiamat. It was a short-lived partnership. We found the heroes at the Tomb of Diderius, and I was killed for the first time. The dwarf Stroud striking me down with his bow.”
“The first time?” 
“Yes, the first time. I was brought back from death as a Dracolich through an arcane ritual Jorgen used in Xonathal’s tower. The ritual required the use of a Dragon Mask. Normally, a dragon must be alive, but Jorgen had found a way to harness the Blue Mask’s power to transform a dead dragon into a Dracolich. Jorgen thought I would be grateful to him, but I was Talis the White Wyrmspeaker’s partner, and Jorgen was her enemy. I left and ran to Talis. But Severin was adamant that a Dracolich could not serve the cult. I was hurt and ready to head into the yawning abyss when Talis observed the small matter of a settlement in the north that needed someone to spy on. And so I went to the Mkoz glacier. And there – as the fates would have it – I would meet Stroud and the First Faction again. And just as I was about to kill them all, they told me that they had found the blue mask and they told me where to find it. And so – knowing how much this meant to Talis – I left to find it.” 
“They lied to you?”
“Yes, and so, to clear my dishonor, I agreed to a plot to murder the First Faction. And once again, I was defeated. When I recovered, I discovered that the First Faction and the grand alliance had destroyed the cult, Talis was no more, and the vampire Sandesyl had my phylactery.” 
Orsik smirks; he has his opening, “So, how do you feel about that?” 
“I AM A DRACOLICH,” she roars, and ice shards fall from the cavern ceiling. “I do not bow to anyone.”
“Oh, so you aren’t at her beck and call?” 
And with that, Glazhael unleashes a torrent of dragon curses that make even Orsik turn a little pale. 
Orsik asks when she stops speaking – “what if we made a deal with you? What if you helped us kill her?” 
The dragon stops fuming, “How? If she sees me, she can destroy my phylactery.” And then the dragon pauses, her skeletal jaw forms into a cruel smile as she remembers something -“During the battle when you have weakened, I can destroy her. When you have almost won, I will seal your victory. But not a moment sooner. I know how to kill the moonelf romance-book-loving vampire.” 
“How?” 
“That is my secret. Now go.”
