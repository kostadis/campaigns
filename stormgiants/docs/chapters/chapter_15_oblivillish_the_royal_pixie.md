“Well, we’ll deal with this ettin, and then I suppose I will take you back to Thornacious.” 
“Yes, Prince Thornacious may not be so happy to have me back; after all, there was that misunderstanding about the stag.” 
Oblivillish becomes annoyed when Vardis ask, “well, who killed the stag?”
“I did! But I didn’t know it was his stag.”
Thistle perks up, “he was quite explicit when we met him that all stags belong to him.” 
*“When did the lower classes get permission to question their betters?”*
“Well, maybe I missed that royal memorandum. And it was so delicious.” 
“Have you tried groveling?”

 “It sometimes works with nobles.”

Thistle rolls his eyes.
“Perhaps, that wasn’t what I was supposed to do.”
“No.” 
Oblivillish gets on his knees and says, “Is this better?” 
Thistle stares at the awkwardly kneeling noble and sighs, “yes.” 
Oblivillish stands up, and Thistle asks, “Do you know about an ettin named Gralm?” 
“Yes, I worked for him until you killed those goblins. Their death probably ended my employment.” 
“I think not.” Thistle’s eyes twinkle with an idea. “You are the last member of your group that was wiped out by a group of Bad Fruul’s goons. We saved you and killed the goons. And we asked to come to see who your boss was and offer our sword.”
Oblivillish likes the plan and quickly agrees. 

For the next bit little, Oblivillish swallows his pride and attempts to learn the art of the grovel. Wallace, Orsik, and Vardis try hard not to laugh. 
A few hours later, a nearby tree flickers and glows more brightly than the others with an iridescent blue flame. An outline shaped like a door lights up. 
Everyone stares at the door quietly. 
Except for Oblivillish, who says, “That better not be what I think it is… The Forgotten Forest is trying to help us, but why does it always do this? I’ll bet a fistful of fire ants that door leads to a puzzle room. Well, at least it’s probably a shortcut… hopefully not to our inevitable demise.”
The blue flames in the doorway swirl like a distant galaxy. A friendly, toothy maw coalesces and yips enthusiastically before abruptly disappearing.
Unla Kee declares, “I’m going in.” 

Everyone stares at him and then shrugs and follows. 
Unla Kee

He hears disembodied giggles, snickers, and low guffaws to make matters creepier.
And then, just as it couldn’t get weirder,  a small dog with a vibrantly-colored mane appears before
The dog yelps excitedly.
Well, it doesn’t quite yelp. In Thistle’s head are the sound of yipping and the dog’s voice. “Travelerrrrs! Travelerrrrs! I knew YOU would come. I know. I RRRREEEU. I have been so bored. But in order to let you through first, we play--word game. I’m learning lots of new words now. Tell me what you seek. At this very very moment!
Unla Kee stares at it. 
“My name is Pandi, and I want to play a game! Can we play a game? CAN WE?”

It then shouts, “what do you want?”
Unla Kee casually observes the wrong guesses of the group. 
Thistle first guesses a grail and then a hallway. Pandi is  very dismissive, “they don’t teach fairies anything these days in fairy school.” Vardis tries, “keep walking through an exit.”
Pandi is becoming frustrated and tries to help them, but they keep failing to figure this out. The fairy creature sighs, “this might take a while. I hope you have food and water.” 
Unla Kee smiles and, like a showman, whispers, “a passage?” 
The dog is delighted and laughs and leaps around the room in joy. Unla Kee stares smugly at the rest of the group. 
“Well done, Unla Kee! But now begins the rrreal fun.”

Pandi bites at the air, and a rainbow of various shapes cascades out of an invisible pocket.
“As rrrue you can, my pawwws arrren’t verrry good fur picking up tiny, tiny rrrobjects. Isn’t that the saaadesst thing everrr? My rrrrooom needs rrrarrrtwork for the rrralls! Will you put these ruetogether to make a picture? For me? Forrr me? For ME? Can you can you?”
Unla Kee stares at the Tangram puzzle and, seeing how Vardis and Thistle eagerly begin to assemble the pieces, joins in.
A few minutes later, the puzzle is solved. The picture scintillates in a hypnotic pattern and attaches itself to the wall. Pandi howls with joy.

“Is this for me? Rrrreally rrrreally? Have I been a good boy? Well, if this gift is for me, then the gift of passage is for you!”
Good luck on your journey!”
With another uncomfortable jolt, the scenery shifts, and Unla Kee is outside again. He looks around and notices some hills. And just over the nearest hillcrest, he can see the rise of smoke and hear the occasional drums of a nearby encampment
## The encampment of Gralm

The group advances to the top of the hillcrest to check out Gralm’s army. And what at an army it is. His encampment can be heard from at least a mile away. Bonfires billow to the sky. Tents, huts, and various ramshackle buildings blend into one another as a sea of humanoids is busy. Countless guards patrol the outskirts of the camp. 
Thistle turns to Oblivillish, “what is the way in? And don’t forget what we said.”
Vardis turns to Thistle, “Do you think he pulls this plan of yours off?”
“He’s a spy. He should be able to deceive some guards.”
“If he’s any good at it!”
Oblivillish snorts, “I am a great spy.” He quickly puts on his goblin attire and snorts, “let’s go.” 
He leads the group to one of the guarded entrances of the encampment. A few minutes later, the guards let him and the group pass. 
As the group walks through the encampment, the camp’s various goblins and hobgoblings glare at them suspiciously. 

Unla Kee notices a hill giant under some construction but decides he must be mistaken.
The guards stop. 
Thistle, Unla Kee, and Vardis stare at the center hut. A hut that is over 30 feet tall. The guard approaches a wall and pulls a flap open to reveal a floor made entirely of skulls. 
As their eyes adjust to the darkness, they notice a ragged wooden throne, and on that throne sits the ettin Gralm. They are wearing simple loincloth and deerskin boots. Gralm stands deliberately.
Orsik notices that he’s a wee bit short for an ettin at only nine feet and is about to make a crack. Still, the presence of the ettin and his four piercing eyes subdues his impulse to be snide. 
“We knew you were coming,” smiles the head with fancifully braided blonde hair. 
“Shh. Who what interrupt quiet! Aargh!” shouts the bald head, fire in his eyes. 
“We want to hear them speak. Elaborate. Tell us why we should not devour you!” 
“No. Less talk. More eat! Hungry! YOU LOUD! MUSIC EVERYWHERE! QUIET!” 
“Patience. We always have patience. Speak your case posthaste,” says the blonde head and waits. 
Thistle prods Oblivillish. 
“Sire, these adventurers saved me from the agents of Bad Fruul.” 
“Interesting, and why should we care?” 
“Because we seek an alliance with you,” says Thistle. 
“An alliance with me?” 
As the conversation progresses, Unla Kee notices that the balding head is suffering from sleep deprivation caused by the Fey. 
Gralm waves his hand, and everyone is silent. “You have made an excellent case for the danger that Bad Fruul presents, and he is indeed an enemy of mine. But what do you suggest?” 
Thistle picks up the SEER’s contract, “we have this contract here that you could sign promising to protect us against our common enemy.” 
“An alliance? Is that the only offer on the table?” 
“Do you have a counterproposal?” 
The bald head shouts, “No more talking. Eat Fairy!” 
Vardis objects, “that is not a proposal we’re willing to make.” 
Unla Kee whispers to his friends, “that guy has a sleep deprivation problem. We should see if we can do anything to help him.” 
Vardis agrees, “wouldn’t hurt.” 
The blonde hair Gralm explains, “my dear friend hates fey music. Since the Fey decided to expand into the Forgotten Forest, their music has kept him up all night.” 
Unla Kee has an idea, “I can help him.” He takes out two pairs of magmin shells with powerful insulation properties and creates two pairs of ettin-sized earmuffs.  

Everyone stares at him as he demonstrates a proficiency none knew he had. 
“I like sound and am very particular to hearing the sounds I want, not the sounds other people want me to hear.” 
The bald head takes the earmuffs and then sighs and falls asleep. 
The blonde-haired head takes the other pair, puts them on, sighs, and then removes them. 
“You have proven yourselves to be enemies of my enemy and friends of mine. But to earn my trust, you must perform feats of strength and drinking!” 
Orsik smiles, “my kind of fun!” 
“To prove your worth, you  must defeat Grindle!” 

A massive ogre, almost 13 feet tall, enters the tent, “I Grindle.” The ogre notices the tiny Fairy and approaches it, “toy for me?” Thistle bristles – “no touching.”
Gralm seeing the situation potentially spiraling out of control commands Grindle and says, “no, they are not a toy for you.” 
Grindle pouts. 
Unla Kee, who recalls that he manipulated the fates twice today, tries to convince Gralm that perhaps they could do this contest tomorrow.
The blonde-haired Gralm thinks and then says, “that would’ve been an excellent idea. Unfortunately, we have already consumed this evening’s food. This is the entertainment. And so, I would hate to be unable to provide entertainment for the camp. They might suddenly become very unreasonable and view the slaughtering of you as a reasonable alternative entertainment. They lack discipline in this matter.”
Unla Kee sighs. 
Gralm claps his hands, and six drinks appear. “You must first drink Pixie Sangria! It is a sickly sweet garnet concoction of my own creation.” 
All six contestants drink, and although they can feel the power of the drink, they hold it down. 
Vardis remarks, “I suppose we can all hold our liquor.”

The group is deflated. 
Thistle tries to get the boar to cooperate by casting – “speak with animals.” The boar agrees to be still, but Thistle cannot lift the creature. 
Vardis and Wallace also fail.
Grindle is about to cheer when Unla Kee has another twinkle in his eye. He casts reduce on the boar, walks up, and picks up the tiny animal.
Grindle howls, but Gralm is impressed.
“The halfling wins!” Gralm shouts.

## Blasius
Near a small pond surrounded by an open area and a thicket of trees, Blasius, the Satyr, is drinking from a hip flask. A giant eagle with majestic brown and golden wings and an ornamental leaf necklace sits next to him, its head constantly looking towards the sky.
Blasius is startled when he hears a Sprite shout, “that blasted Satyr Blassios is why I was banished from Thornacious’s service! We should attack – now!!!” 
Blasius recognizes Thistle and Oblivillish. He is relieved to see his old friend is trying to protect him. The Fairy stands up, carefully positions himself to grab the angry sprite, and flings himself at Oblivillish. Except Oblivillish was going in the opposite direction. Thistle lands on the forest floor, alone. 
Blasius cringes and is now really worried. But he feels a sense of hope when Orsiktries to tackle the tiny sprite. Still, Oblivillish is trained in the secret art of the Spra-Magaw and deflects the dwarf into the dirt. 
*I’m going to die. Better dead than a chief of staff. *
The killing blow doesn’t come. Blasius opens his eyes and sees a sleeping Sprite with a smug Unla Kee standing behind him. 
Blasius is in shock. Once he recovers his ability to speak, he takes a swig of booze from his hip flask and asks, “what are you doing here?”
Thistle ignores his question, “is Thornacious here?”
Blasius ignores the lack of protocol from the Fairy and then stares at Oblivillish, who is sleeping. “Ox, no! Thees is oblivilliss. No guonder he was so unchappy.”

“I was drunk. Which is not surprising. But we must begin at the beginning. Prince Thornacious wants to build a great empire. I just want to go back home. He even destroyed the Fey circle saying we would either build an empire or die trying.” 
Thistle interrupts, “Agreed! And you should build it in a forest that is as grand and glorious as Prince Thornacious!” 
Blasius is annoyed at the interruption and attempts to continue. 
“Yes, but you know.” 
“Pshaw, Blasius. This forest is beneath Thornacious! It is not a PRINCE Thornacious worthy place.”
“I am not the boss who makes these decisions. And you are interrupting me! He then pauses, looks down at his hoofs, and says, “It turns out I was drunk when I told Prince Thornacious that Oblivillish killed a stag.” He starts to cry, “It was a moment of weakness. I was not thinking. How could I do this to my dear friend Oblivillish?” 
Blasius pauses and perks up, “Prince Thornacious does not like me like he used to. But if we could come to an arrangement where Oblivilliss and I did something good for the Prince, he would take Oblivilliss back.” He then stops, “I mean, you do something good for Prince Thornacious that we take credit for.” 
“Well, the greatness and the expansiveness of Prince Thornacius deserves a far grander forest rather than these piddling tiny forests. We have heard of a much larger forest. The high forest with star mountain at its center. That would be a fitting forest for the Prince.”
