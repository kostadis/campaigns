# Chapter 16 - Return of the Spy
## The Battle with the Unseelie 3rd day of the 3rd Tenday Of Taraskh
The heroes stare at the Unseelie, and the Unseelie stare back, their game of cards disrupted. 
One of the Dryads, whose leaves and branches remind one of a conifer, casts entangle on the group. The ground around the group sprouts branches and vines. Thistle and Vardis, and Unla Kee are trapped. Orsik and Wallace, however, use their strength to break free. 
The Coniferous Dryad is not entirely pleased; he rushes to a tree nearby and reappears next to another tree some 50 feet away from the group.
“Teleportation!” mumbles the Dwarf.
And then he is hit with a magic missile from the halfling mage, wearing a dirty unseemly robe. 
“Can’t resist that,” he grumbles, “but thank goodness for the buffs of my friend Vardis that absorbed some of the damage!” 
Vardis takes that as a cue and casts a scorching ray aiming at the Confirous Dryad and the Deciduous looking Dryad but misses. 
He grouses, “I can’t move and can’t hit.” 
The Deciduous-looking Dryad calls Orsik, “This isn’t a battle you want to fight in, and we are your friends!” Orsik shockingly agrees and sits down. Thistle shouts, “the damn charm effects of the Dryads, kill that creature, and its charm will be broken!” 
Wallace rolls his eyes, “really? Really?” and then promptly misses. 
Thistle looks at Wallace and Orsik, gives a command to Thuul, and fires an arrow. Shortly after that, the Deciduous Dryad is covered with acid, with an arrow sticking deep into its body. The Dryad can no longer focus on Orsik, and the Dwarf stands up, ready to kill. 
Unla Kee finishes the job with a pair of magic missiles. He directs the last missile at the Coniferous Dryad. 
The Coniferous Dryad recognizing the danger, once again steps through the forest to be close enough to Unla Kee and tells him – “now is a good time to sit down and get a drink.”
The gnome pulls out his flask and begins to drink while mumbling about Blasius and Oblivilish are just sitting and drinking, so why shouldn’t he? 
And then, the creature disappears into the trees and reappears some 40 feet away on the other side of the grove. 
The Unseelie Halfling casts a magic missile again, this time at Wallace. 
Vardis realizing that the entanglement is making it harder to hit their targets casts faerie fire on the Dryad and the Halfling. Only the magic user is affected and is now covered in an eerie pink glow. 
Wallace strikes the halfling with his sword, cutting at the creature deeply. But the Unseelie wizard continues to live. 
Orsik charges at the halfling and throws a handaxe that hits. 

Thistle seeing Unla Kee drinking, pleads with him to get up, but to no avail. The beautiful voice of the Dryad makes his colleague’s voice sound like a screeching bleating whine.   
Just then, a lion and a bear show up. 
Oblivillish shouts – “those creatures ate the stag!” 
Vardis looks at him – “I thought you did?” 
“I was framed. “ 
Vardis understands. 
The Dryad seeing Vardis near the edge of the entanglement, steps into a tree, steps out next to Vardis, swings her staff and misses, and then steps away into the tree. 
Vardis misses as the creature flees, then fails to break from the roots, branches, and vines that hold him in place. 
Wallace, meanwhile, kills the halfling mage. And when the bear charges at him, he dodges the creature’s attacks. 
Seeing the Dryad still alive and his natural hatred of the Unseelie coming to the fore, he commands Thuul to get close and then fires an arrow that misses badly. 
Unla Kee’s drunken stupor ends when he sees the lion charging at him. He raises a finger and casually casts Rhyme’s Binding Ice. The lion escapes the worst of the blast, however. 
The creature stares at Thistle, recognizing him as a Seelie, and pounces. Thistle is dropped, and the lion prepares to tear his throat with his fearsome jaw and gets tangled up in the branches. 
Vardis wonders what to do, “can’t hit anything with my hands caught in these weeds, but maybe …” And his scorching rays hit the lion, killing it, and is even able to wound the bear. Vardis rubs his ring of Correlon. He shouts, “that is how it’s done!” 
Wallace kills the bear and roars as if he had killed Tiamat. Vardis shouts back, “I softened him up for you!” Wallace glares. 
Thistle and Thuul try again to hit the Dryad, and although the creature is covered in acid, it does not relinquish the control of Unla Kee. 
Orsik finishes the job, knocking the Dryad prone on the ground and then cutting it in half with his blade. 
At this point, Oblivillish, freed from the entanglement, flits about and shouts, “I am free!” 
## Return of the Spy

Blasius wakes up from his drunken stupor and asks, “Unla Kee, another drink? Anyone another drink?” He then sees the Dryads dead and shouts, today is a great day! Because today is the day, I am no longer the Chief of Staff of Prince Thornacious.” 
Oblivillish is so happy that he kisses Blasius and then tries to hug Orsik, Vardis, Thistle, and Wallace, who all glare at his unwanted advances. 
Blasius hands Unla Kee a ring of jumping and declares, “To my true honored drinking friend.” And then promptly falls asleep. 
With the Unseelie singers dead, the group heads back to Gralm to get him to sign the contract. Orsik’s mastery of persuasion, the victory in the drinking games, and the magnim sound-canceling headphone are enough to sway Gralm, who announces that he will deal with Bad Fruul. The ettin signs the contract in Grolm’s precise script to cement the deal. 
After signing the contract, he asks, “Will you stay for a small celebration and a drink?” 
Unla Kee stares, “how about a flight?”
“Finally, a civilized adventurer!” Gralm calls his goblin butler, who brings the fine crystals, each filled to the brim with the various brews that the Ettin has manufactured. 
Everyone stares at the gnome, expecting the booze to have some magical effect on him. 
“Some more pleeeze?” 
At this point, Wallace, Orsik, and Vardis are worried. Apparently, some of the effects of the booze can be pretty alarming, so Vardis shouts, “hey buddy, we have some much better booze in our tent!” 
And Unla Kee follows him out. 
In the morning, with their head spinning, the group heads out to meet Blasius and Oblivillish, who is still in the clearing with the dead Unseelie. 
From there, Blasius opens another Fey portal and shouts, “come on!” 
Vardis, worried about the last time they went through a portal and had to solve some puzzles, shouts, “what about the trickster fey?” 
“Not a problem. I am chief of staff. A perk of being chief of staff. The faster way to get there!” 
And with that, the group appears in front of the Prince. 
“What are you doing with this traitorous scum?” 
“Your highness, I was drunk when I made those accusations. Was it a – joke? But thanks to the leadership of Oblivillish and these heroes, we found the true killers: a bear and a lion.”
Prince Thornacious scoffs.
“And there were Unseelie agents that Lord Oblivillish commanded the heroes to attack, and under his leadership, they were defeated! The threat of the Unseelie incursion was averted, and the true killers were found!” 
The Prince turns to Thistle, “is this what happened?” 
Thistle and the rest of the group nod. 
“Blasius, you are now once again my Satyr! And you Oblivillish are once again my chief of staff!” 
Orsik smiles – the mission is complete. 
## The Vision Quest 5th day of the 3rd Tenday of Taraskh
The Satyr is so happy that he steps in front of the group and shouts, “a vision quest, my friends. We will drink and ponder and think!”  
Orsik – “anything is better than doing the bidding of a blind pseudo dragon.” 
The vision quest expands the mind, poisons the liver, and allows our heroes to come into contact with the essential elements of the forest and the Fey. The experience shows them how they can neutralize poison, should they ever be poisoned.
Blasius shouts, “only works once per vision quest.” 
Unla Kee slurs, “sign me up!” 
Six days later, in Parnast, our heroes arrive. 

## Azam 1st day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul 
*It’s been two weeks since they left. Surely they failed. And the SEER will be disappointed. And I have sat in Parnast for days. The only place to drink is the Golden Tankar*
*Although I must admit the Festival of Greengrass is far more pleasant in a small village than in the desert. Plenty of food, drink and smiling faces and games. Why in the desert, it’s almost a joke to celebrate. *
Azam stares at his drink. He sighs. 
In the distance the sound of small children squealing, “Thistle is coming!” breaks his reverie. 
Azam gets up, stretches out his clothes, brushes off some crumbs, leaves some coin for Ragnar and Elspeth, and walks out into the main square of Old Parnast. 
He shouts, “congratulations! You have secured your rewards!” and hands over a magical potion and a pouch full of gold to Unla Kee.
When Unla Kee does not betray his friends and tells them that the deal he struck was for 200 GP, Azam concludes that perhaps the gnomish wizard could work for the SEER.
He walks up to him, “would you like to join the Lord’s Alliance?” 
The wizard stares at him, “who are they?” 
“Ah yes. The North lacks any central authority to impose order and ensure safety and property laws. Why? Because the North has so many different secret factions, each using its influence and power to advance its agendas. The Harpers are anarchists opposed to a central authority. The Zhentarim are only interested in controlling the trade of any goods and are a parasite on the productive functions of society. The Order of the Gauntlet sees evil behind every stone and is too busy trying to save the world from one evil-doer or another. The Emerald Enclave is devoted to the trees and opposed to productive resource extraction of the lands. It is the Lord’s Alliance that is in favor of order and centralization and safety.”
Azam is convinced the pitch will work.
Unla Kee takes a swig and burps, “no.”
