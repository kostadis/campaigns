# Chapter 83 -  Kee and Wing Casino

Lord Khaspere Drylund was dead. And the darkest secret of the entire War of the Giants was revealed: King Hekaton was alive. More importantly, Orsik knew precisely where he was. 

Now, all he had to do was get out of the sewers. 

But to get out of the sewers, he had to get out of this circular chamber. 

And to get out of that room, he had to get through four Kuo-Toans. 

Easy-peasy. 

The Kuo-Toan, in the water, tosses a net to capture him. It falls to the side harmlessly. 

Still, in the room at the end of the walkway, Thule rushes out the door past the Kuo-Toan, who was trying to hold the door shut. Seizing the opportunity, they strike and miss. 

Thule returns the favor with a blast of his breath weapon. 

Thistle sees two Kuo-Toans further down the ramp, casts a lightning arrow, and then hits them both. 

With the two Kuo-Toans that tried to strike Thistle but could not hit Vardis, the cleric rushes through and uses his mace of terror. A field of powerful energy fills the room. Only one of the Kuo-Toans is unaffected. The affected three jump into the water and submerge themselves as far from Vardis as possible. 

To the north and south of the room are two valves. Next to each valve is a Merrow. They simultaneously turn them, and water rushes in from two pipes to the east. The room starts to fill with water. 

The one remaining Kuo-Toan Whip tries to capture Thistle with his net, but he deftly avoids him. 

Unla Kee uses his telekinesis to open one of the drainage valves, slowing the water flow. 

Orsik stares at the water and remembers his ring of swimming: " I never thought I would need this thing.” He jumps into the water, swims south, reduces the Merrow to 5 HP, and then closes the valve using his superior strength. 

The mysterious Oosith finally reveals himself. He is an aboleth, and his first action is to dominate Unla Kee. His attack on Thistle fails when Vardis uses his powers as a light cleric to blind the creature. 

The three Kuo-Toans, who were frightened, now flee through the drainage opened drainage hole.

Thistle strikes the Merrow to the North. Again, the Merrow is not killed, but the damage caused the domination of the Aboleth to be broken. The creature sees what’s going on and decides to flee. 

Vardis opens another drainage valve, and now the water level drops. 

Seeing the water level drop, the only Kuo-Toan whip in combat closes the drainage valve Unla Kee had turned on. 

The aboleth commands Unla Kee - “Destroy them!” 

Unla Kee ponders the options for a moment. On the one hand, this is the perfect time to use a dominant person, but of course, he didn’t prepare that spell. “Fireball,” he thinks and then discards it. “I know,” he says, pulling out his rock, the wand of wonder. 

And he becomes invisible. 

The Aboleth shrieks in everyone’s mind, “What the hell did you just do?!” 

Orsik and Vardis mutter, “Yeah, that’s Unla Kee for you.” 

Orsik swims from the southern valve to where the last Kuo-Toan stands and kills him. 

The Aboleth shrieks, “The Kraken will have its revenge!”

Orsik stares at the dead body of Lord Drylund, turns to his friend, and asks, “Hey, Vardis, can we raise him?” 

“I am a light cleric, not a necromancer.” 

Orsik groans, “You can cast raise dead?” 

“Oh, right.” Vardis steps close to the body and announces, “Like all Politicians, that’s impossible. His brain is gone.” 

Thistle observes that True Resurrection would work, but that would cost 50,000 gold pieces, and that is that. 

Thistle then quickly scours the body for any treasure and finds a ring with keys, 120 gold pieces, and three platinum pieces. 

Orsik mutters, “I want my Champagne Du Stomp, back.” 

Thistle looks at the body, wondering what to do about it, and Orsik shouts, “Take the gold, leave the body.”

Thistle agrees. Thuul picks it up in his jaws and drops it in the room. Thistle covers it up and locks the door to the best of his ability. 

A few minutes later, the 3HPP emerged, covered in sewage, into the sunshine. 

Thistle mutters, “Lady Thalia? There’s a power vacuum, and someone needs to fill it.” 

Orsik retorts, “She wanted us to kill the Aboleth, and the Aboleth is very much not dead.” 

Vardis looks at him oddly, confused that his friend is so unwilling to run a trivial scam. He sighs and says, “We’ll lie?” 

“What about Pow?” asks Thistle. 

“The COO. That sounds like an excellent idea,” acknowledges Orsik. Orsik is delighted that his newfound intelligence allows him to entertain other ideas. “What would we offer him?” 

“Not to die,” say Vardis and Thistle in unison.

Orsik is disappointed and realizes that his role is to help the team improve their ideas. “That’s not a good plan. You can threaten him once, but once he leaves, he will have no loyalty to us. We are not going to stay here forever.” 

Thistle thinks for a moment, “We can offer him more?” 

“He can easily say I want a hundred percent as this is my casino.” 

Thistle jokingly says, “What if we put the Casino in the Castle?” 

Orsik smiles, “Now that is a good idea.” 

“And we could have shows? We gotta have shows!” 

“Of course, it’s a casino.” 

Orsik rubs his chin, “This is a big upgrade from a boat docked in Yartar. A flying castle going around the world with live theater is a really good idea. Yeah, that’s a great idea.” 

Thistle red teams the proposal, “What about the customers? Will they want to go on a Casino for an indefinite period of time?” 

Vardis turns to him, “It’s like a cruise ship. When we land, they can come on and off. We can even make it so they can visit any place before we go and pillage and mess it up.” 

“I am not sure about the business model.” 

Orsik pooh-pooh’s him, “It’s a casino, the strongest model. Who could bankrupt a casino?”

Thistle and Vardis stare at Unla Kee. 

Thistle insists they talk to Lady Atalia first after deciding on their plan. 

Sitting at another fashionable cafe, Lady Atalia smiles when she sees the 3HPP. Her smile evaporates when she discovers that they didn’t kill the Aboleth. 

“You let that thing escape? Damn you.” She stands up, straightens her clothes, and marches out of the cafe, furious. 

As the 3HPP tries to catch up to her, a small group of female rogues stops them. 

“Lady Atalia is under our protection.” 

“We did just that. We got rid of someone who threatened her. A sorcerer named Oosith.” 

The rogues look at the very well-armed 3HPP and shrug, “In that case, we’ll just follow you.” 

When they approach her house, Lady Atalia is standing outside looking very pissed off. Thistle tries to speak to her, and she starts snarling at him with a very plebian dock accent, “You were supposed to kill him. I don’t want anything to do with you. We’re done.” 

Thistle is taken aback, “You are not going to become the new Waterbarron with an attitude like that.” 

She stares at him, “The Kraken doesn’t like it when anyone disrupts its plans. Drylund’s going to come back, and he’s going to be …” 

Orsik mutters, “I don’t think he’s coming back. But you knew that?” 

Lady Atalia sighs, “I would if I had drunk my potions. It’s amazing how easy it is for people to read if they think you can read their minds.” 

And with that, the door is closed. 

Just then, one of the Hands turns to Orsik, “We would be delighted to take an equitable share of the Grand Dame, as well as an operating fee and a security fee.” 
Thistle asks, “And that would be how much?” 

“95% of the turnover and an annual security fee.”

The 3HPP nods politely and heads out to the docks. Their stench allows them to quickly go through the crowds that work to avoid being near them. 

Thistle again cautions against the Casino plan as they head to the Grand Dame - “A flying casino will have a repeat business problem.” 

Vardis brushes him off - “It’s like a cruise ship.” 

Once at the ship, they meet Pow. 

Orsik explains the plan, and Pow is intrigued. He speaks with the accent of a citizen of Shou - “There is a tremendous opportunity here that I had not considered. We land in a city, open up the rooms for gambling, and then fly to a different location for more gambling. The nature of our flying ship makes it possible for us to avoid any taxation and protection fees. We also have a lot more space and rooms.” 

Thistle notes, “And there are kitchens and no monsters. Like there is no undead, and this whole area in the ice caves could be a winter wonderland.” 

Unla Kee mutters, “I am only going along with this if I get naming rights. I want this place to be called *Kee and Wing Casino*.” 

Pow shakes hands with the halfling. 

Thistle then flies off and heads to Lord Drylund’s room. There, he finds no incriminating paperwork or notes, and there appears to be no correspondence. He is surprised. Any organization needs some way to communicate. 

He does find some treasure, including an ornately decorated weapon with an octopus pommel. 

Before he heads down, he suddenly remembers the crazy woman in Everlund who warned about the Kraken. She was a member of a group called “Fighters of the Light, " a group that the 3HPP had helped and, as a result of helping, had become members.
