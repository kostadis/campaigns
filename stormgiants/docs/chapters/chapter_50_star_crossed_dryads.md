# Chapter 50 - Star crossed Dryads
## Unla Kee
Unla Kee is the first to notice the Grandfather Tree from the airship. The tree's crown is over 350 feet high and spreads 800 feet in diameter. As the ship approaches the ground, he notices four more trees under the canopy. Just before it lands, he catches a glimpse of the ground. Two large rings of raised earth circle the tree, an outer and inner ring. 
As he considers the long hike, he remembers that he could be the proud owner of Cloud Giant’s castle. He thinks that Eigeron had told him precisely where he could find Blagothkus. And then, he considers that his current allies might not be as willing to hand over the castle to him. They might want to share it or, worse, control it. 
Thistle might not care; he’s only on an errand of love and vengeance. 
“No,” he realizes, “this will have to wait.” 
## Thistle
Thistle is in a rush to find the artifact, get back to the Oracle, get the Conch, and find the Maelstorm where Adimond Kroskas is hidden. 
And, only if strictly necessary, deal with the threat to the realms. 
As he approaches the southernmost tree under the canopy, a dryad’s voice tells him to go no further. 
The dryad introduces herself as Halisa, explaining that she has been exiled from the Fey because she offered her honest opinion about the Queen’s dress. 
Thistle squirms visibly when he hears that, covering his eyes with the tips of his wings. 
Thistle tries to explain that they are pursuing an artifact of great importance and asks if she knows where it is. 
She says no and explains that the Tree cannot be touched because that was the terms of her punishment. 
“Tree Protection Schedule D,” asks Thistle. 
“The very one!” replies the dryad in her indirect light voice. 
Orsik intervenes and infuriates the dryad, who tells him to leave because he’s a dwarf. Orsik explains that he was once a dwarf scout and understands the importance of not damaging nature. But Halisa is unimpressed and shoos him off. 
Thistle sighs as Orsik predictably marches off in a huff. He did that in Parnast and now here. 
Halisa then explains to Thistle that other Dryads might be willing to help him. 
As Thistle is about to leave, he’s startled to see Prince Thornacious. 
Halisa is terrified at the idea that royalty is coming and demands they leave immediately. 
Thistle is still confused about how Prince Thornacious magically appeared here. Still, observing the awkward flying, the expression on the Prince’s face, and his inability to understand Fey, he realizes what has happened. 
“Orsik?” 
“Yes.”
“We can use this.”
“I hope so!” 
And with that, he arrives at the easternmost tree. There, another dryad sits in the tree and introduces himself as Helia. He is delighted to see Thornacious and let’s loose a stream of insults, hoping to provoke the sprite. But Orsik-Thornacious understands nothing and misinterprets some of the gestures. After a time, Helia gives up. 
He then explains how he got here. He was Halisa’s plus-one at the ball. When Halisa expressed herself freely, Queen Titania demanded that the plus one present itself. 
“I tried to explain that I wasn’t her date or interested in Halisa, and I even dumped her on the spot. But Queen Titania didn’t care. And now I am stuck here for an eternity!” He pauses, “is that long?” 
Thistle makes the same request, and he is once again rebuffed. But Helia offers an option, “I would give you permission if you could convince Halisa to apologize!” 
Orsik, Thistle, and Vardis discuss their options. Vardis points out they can bend the fates using Unla Kee’s powers. Orsik proposes violence. Thistle is unsure and suggests they check the other dryads in the trees. 
The tree on the northern cardinal point is an old dryad with a grumpy, breathy voice. Thistle introduces himself and has been searching for his Uncle Joon.
“Joon!” 
“How much does he owe you,” sighs Thistle. 
“He owes me a joke. A good joke! I want to hear a good joke.” 
Thistle ponders the jokes he heard throughout his travels and remembers an old joke he used to share with his beloved. The joke only works if you are a fey, and it’s about a Dryad and how the charm interacts with the movement of a tree. Thistle lands the joke nicely. And the Dryad laughs heartily. 
Seeing him laugh, Thistle asks for permission to complete his quest. And this elderly dryad turns him down, “unless you get Helia and Halisa to stop arguing. I am tired of hearing their bickering.” 
“How did she trap them here?” 
The Dryad ponders the question and mutters, “I am not sure. Queen Titania told them that if one left, the other would be safe, and the one leaving would die. They hate each other so much that they can’t find the love to sacrifice themselves. So they sit there bickering.” The dryad slouches and puts his head in his hands, “and thus we are canceling the moon festival.” 
Thistle is shocked; it’s performative, but shocked nonetheless. 
The elderly creature mutters, “Get them to stop bickering or to leave, and I will give you permission.” 
Thistle flitters off to the last tree on the western cardinal point. 
After the introductions, Thistle learns that her name is Sleth and that she is upset that Helia and Halisa are not caring for their trees. She tries to point out what is wrong, but Thistle only sees a perfect tree. 
Sleth rolls her eyes, “that tree branch is growing in the wrong direction. Why, in the next 30 years, it will create a terrible imbalance. And this is exactly what happens when Queen Titania sends whatever dregs she can muster.” 
Thistle is surprised, “what? You aren’t trapped here?” 
Sleth laughs, “Oh no, this is a positively amazing assignment. Or it was. This is the greatest tree in the Universe, and I get to tend to it.” 

Thistle hits his head with his forehead, and the Orsik-Thornacious stares at him, “Of course! Queen Titania would pull this kind of stuff all of the time. She’s told them a lie, and neither loves the other enough to sacrifice himself. So they sit there stubbornly for all time.” 
Orsik-Thornacious looks at him – “So now what.”
“So now we say that Prince Thornacious came to this tree to be cured of his muteness and to tell both of these love dryads that they can leave!” 
The dwarf in the sprite’s body signals to his friend to create the right effect as he touches the tree. Then Orsik-Thornacious shouts, “Blessed be Queen Titania, I can speak.” And then he mumbles, “but only in COMMON!” 
He then flies over to Halisa. Her reaction is surprising – “Helia is going to be stuck here, right? That knot dumped me just as Queen Titania was about to send me here. We could have been happily in love instead of rotting away.” 
Thistle’s eyes roll. 
She declares emphatically, “I plan to come here every 100 years and taunt Helia!” 
When Halisa is sufficiently far away, Orsik-Thornacious and Thistle fly to Helia. The dryad is upset that Halisa is leaving, which is odd given how much he insists he hates her. Orsik-Thornacious explains that this is a pardon and that he, too, can go. 
Helia stares at him momentarily and then flies before the Prince chases his mind. 
The old Dryad and Sleth appear next to the 3HPP when both dryads are gone. 
“FINALLY LEFT!” groans the elderly Dryad. 
“Yes, and as I kept telling them, there was no curse. They had cursed each other! Imagine a hundred years of aggravation because you believe it’s a prison. What’s worse is our aggravation, but now – finally – peace.” 
Thistle turns to Orsik-Thornacious and suggests they bid everyone farewell.
With the permission to search given, the 3HPP quickly finds the artifact hidden in a chamber in the tree's roots. It is a nonmagical electrum torc with Giant runes, formerly the nose-ring of a mighty hill giant chief. 
 As they leave, they notice a group of centaurs approach. The centaurs are rank and are muttering about a cursed temple deeper in the forest. It’s unclear where this cursed temple is other than somewhere in the woods. The centaurs, they learn, are here for the magical powers of the tree to cure them of the disease they acquired. Spending the night here has the same effects as the Greater Restoration spell. 
Just before they get to the airship, they sneak up on a group of barbarians of the Tree Ghost Tribe, led by a young man who they understand to be the son of the Great Chief Boorvald Orcbane, talking to Sleth. The leader is furious when he overhears that the precious artifact of his tribe has been stolen, promising vengeance. 
As they sneak away, Sleth says, “Prince Thornacious was so gracious and helpful!”
