# Chapter 59 - Enter Adimond. Exeunt Harshnag

## Eleasis 8th day of the 2nd tenday of 1492

Blagothkus sat silently in the control room.
The situation was clear; a vampire, [Sandesyl](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/the-forgotten-realms-kostadis/a/sandesyl-morgia-person-1), had been treating this castle as her domain. The vampire had been content to let Blagothkus travel around the world, engaging in whatever plots he might be interested in as long as he supplied a steady stream of victims. 
But more food was needed when Blagothkus got into a funk and parked the castle in its current location north of Parnastded. Sandesyl reacted by eating through all of Blagothkus’ minions. 
Over the intervening months, that supply of victims had been slowly and brutally harvested.
The 3HPP clearly understood that the only living things that could be eaten were themselves, Blagothkus, and a pair of Ogres named Worug and Tax. 
As they stood outside the tower, they could feel the eyes of the Vampire staring at them through the unseeing eyes of her zombie spawns. 
And now Blagothkus introduces a new character to this menagerie: his wife, who apparently controls the castle. 
As Thistle enters, he notices a staircase of ice leading from the tower’s ground floor to a higher chamber, the walls and ceiling of which sparkle and glitter. Jewels are everywhere in the room. He sees glowing moonstones, thumb-sized emeralds, shining silvery mithral wands, and strange spheres covered in turquoise and gold, and dozens of copper levers and golden spheres embedded in the walls. After a moment, the walls seem to disappear, providing a perfect aerial view in all directions, as if there were no castle and no cloudstuff around. More than a dozen glowing white runes wink into existence, drifting about the room like snowflakes.
The cloud giant Blagothkus is sitting cross-legged, looking harried, tired, unshaven, and disheveled. 
He points at the runes and screams, “SHE WON’T LET THE CASTLE GO ANYWHERE!” And then dramatically waves his hand through a rune, “See?” 
And with that, the voice of Blagothkus’ wife, Escarlotta, booms - “I am done with him.” 
Vardis asks, “Do you want us to drive?” 
“No, I want him dead. He had this insane plan to help the Dragons bring Tiamat, and then the idea was that when they failed, the little people would be so weakened that the giants could rule. And so he goes to the Eye of the All-Father and kills his son. He couldn’t just let his son kill him. Amn declared him unworthy, and he proved how unworthy he was.” She snivels, “And what makes it worse is that we aren’t one of the cloud giant families making a play for rulership.” 

Thistle asks, “And who is?”
“Countess Sansuri. She’s a vain, glorious, greedy, and ugly cloud giantess who lives in Lym Armaal, her castle. The Countess is untethered from reality, surrounded by enemies who yearn to topple her. And so she is full of herself. HER family head magical artifacts when Ostoria fell, and SHE will find them.” 
Blagothkus is about to say something, and she shouts, “Instead of going and playing your games with dragons, you should have seized the maps from the Countess!” 
The giant stretches his hands at the voice as if to say, “See what I am dealing with?” 
The fairy asks, “Would you like our help getting rid of him?” 
“No,” shrieks Escarlotta, “I want him dead as all of the floating icons surround Blagothkus and arrange in an arrow pointing at him.
The 3HPP is intrigued and discovers that Escarlotta is furious at Blagothkus for trapping her in this console. 
But there is something more, senses Thistle. 
“Let’s say we help you kill your husband here.” 
As Blagothkus rouses himself and prepares for combat, Vardis rushes to stand before him and says,  “Hypothetically! It’s just a hypothetical.” 
“The problem is that if we kill Blagothkus, the vampire will come in, which won’t work for us. 
Escarlotta starts to become petulant. “I don’t care. Maybe it would be better when he’s a vampire. He never had a soul anyways.” 
Vardis whispers, in a tone to soothe tempers, “That was a mean thing to say…” 
Blagothkus, realizing he finally has someone on his side in the endless arguments with his wife, sits down again. 
At this point, the 3HPP debates what they want and need to do. Unla Kee wants the castle because he’s tired of walking. The group learns that Escarlotta hates the vampire. But Syndesia kept the castle clean, and no rats were on board. However, at some point, when Escarlotta realizes that the 3HPP will not kill Blagothkus because of their fear of fighting the vampire, she screams at Blagothkus for not having gotten rid of the Vampire when Escarlotta had told him to or, as she put it - “Bring Tiamat into the world, he’s got time for,  but dealing with a Vampire in his house? No. Not important for the big shot!” 
Finally, the 3HPP decides to get Escarlotta to fly this castle. Killing the vampire is not something they can do at this time, so Thistle fills her in on their adventures. He makes a particular point of talking about the Fire Giants and mentions Duke Zalto’s ambitions and his collection of artifacts. 
Blagothkus and Escarlotta laugh when they hear that Duke Zalto is making a play for rulership - “Fire Giants? How absurd.” Then they proceed to crack jokes about Fire Giants and how silly they are, but the jokes don’t translate into common. 
Thistle is pleased, “laughter is the basis of any good relationship.” And so he ventures to ask for her help. 
There is a moment of silence. And then she explains her terms - “You have to kill Countess Sansuri. I hate her. She is a cow!” 
Vardis laughs, “So we are doing body shaming now?” 
“No. That was uncalled for. She’s an arrogant, selfish, horrible, absurd creation that should be dead.” 
Unfortunately, that then turns into Esclarlotta screaming at Blagothkus for his actions that led to them not making a play for the rulership of the Giants. He explains that it was never in the cards, and then she explains that if he had done the decent thing and let Eigeron kill him, maybe she would be queen now.
Sensing this could go on for hours, Thistle asks about the Fire Giants. That causes them both to laugh. Escarlotta agrees she will help but points out she doesn’t know where they are. 
When Thistle announces they can find that information at the Oracle, the castle starts to move.
“Well, why didn’t you lead with that?” 
As the castle starts to fly, there is a knock on the door. 
Thistle flies out and sees a vampire under an umbrella to avoid being burned by the sun. It’s a spawn dressed in the purple of the cult. 
“It would appear that the castle is moving. And our mistress would like to know where we would stop for a meal?” 
Thistle shouts, “Silverymoon.” 
“There are so many different establishments between here and Silverymoon. Why that one in particular?” 
“Because we have a vendetta against House Margastar.” 
The vampire is stunned. It says, “Well, that is a surprise.” And it leaves. 
As the night settles, Sandesyl stands in the courtyard surrounded by her zombies and several of her spawns. 
“Did I hear correctly someone has a vendetta against Margastar?” 
Thistle shouts, “Yes, that would be me!” 
“Come outside so we can talk about it.” 
“How about not?” 
“You don’t trust me?” 
“I can hear you just fine from inside here.” 
“I am so disappointed that you don’t trust me.” She smiles evilly and taps her fingers against her legs for a few minutes. “I am not interested in fighting a war with the Margastar’s. Asmodeus protects them. And I refuse to upset the archfiend’s plans. Only an insane person would do that.” 
Unla Kee shouts, “An insane plan? Cool!” 
The moments pass, and finally, Sandesyl suggests a village of humans along the way. Thistle counters with a village of orcs, and then they both remember the Uthgartd barbarians of Flint Rock. 
Sandesyl agrees and waits patiently for the Castle to arrive at Flint Rock. 

Seeing the deal that has been made, Delsephine notes the 3HPP, telling them that Klauth wishes them the best of luck in their pursuit of ending the war of the giants. 
## Night of Eleasis 1st to morning of 2nd day of 3rd tenday 1492
Sandesyl’s vampires find a collection of Uthgardtian nomads mourning their despoiled artifact. They are quickly captured and brought to the castle that has settled some miles away. 
The vampires leave convincing evidence and a survivor to spread the tale that this was done by monsters under the control of House Margastar in service of Silverymoon. 
Over the next several days, until the 3HPP arrives at the Eye of the All-Father, the sound of screams of horror of the victims of Sandesyl and her vampires echo through the castle. 
## Eleint 3rd day of 1st tenday of 1492
On the morning of the 3rd day of the 3rd tenday of Eleasis of 1490, the castle arrives at the Eye of the All-Father. The 3HPP march, hurrying past the domed entrance and the six pillars carved like giants, down the hallway to get to the main temple of Annam, where Harshnag awaits. 
They explain that they have a castle, and when he asks about Blagothkus, they are non-commital. 
He picks up Thrymm’s great axe, touches the Ise run on the portal, and walks into the Eye. 
There, the 3HPP meets Eigeron, who asks about his father. When he learns he is not dead, but his castle is here, Eigeron goes mad and tries to possess one of the heroes. 
Vardis invokes the power of Corellon, and the monster is turned.
Sensing that they don’t have time, the heroes deposit each artifact in turn, and the oracle reveals the location of the leaders of the giants who have taken the mantle to rule over all giants. 
For the hill giant chief, the Oracle says - 
“Travel south over tree, hill, and vale. Let the great river be your guide. On one of its eastern arms, you’ll find Grudd Haug — the den of the hill giant chief. The conch you seek is close to her.”
For the stone giant Thane, the Oracle says - 

“Travel southeast, o’er lands high and low. Cross the great forest to the gray peaks and search for Deadstone Cleft, a canyon of the stone giant thane. The conch you seek is in her possession.”
For the Frost Giant Jarl, the Oracle says - 

“Travel west, o’er mountains, to the Sea of Moving Ice. Among the glaciers, you’ll find Svardborg — the berg of the frost giant jarl. The conch you seek rests on his icy throne.”
For the Fire Giant Duke, the Oracle says - 

“Travel east, o’er mountains and snow, to distant spires. There, on a cold mountainside, you’ll find a village of yakfolk and, below that, Ironslag — the forge of the fire giant duke. The conch you seek is in his quarters.”

For the Cloud Giant Countess, the Oracle says - 

“Travel southeast to the untamed moors, and look to the sky for Lyn Armaal — the castle of the cloud giant countess. The conch you seek is in a secret chest. Search the highest spire!”
Before the turning of the ghost ends, the heroes march out of the portal, leaving Eigeron howling for his revenge and marching into the main temple. 
And there Thistle sees a wondrous sight, Adminond Kroskas.
Thistle prepares to attack, but Adminond Kroskas sees Harshnag and transforms into an Ancient Blue Dragon. 
Harshnag shouts, “I will distract the dragon. You must go and save the realms.” 
Thistle and Unla Kee hesitate. Thistle wants revenge. Unla Kee sees an epic battle that he knows he will win. 
Vardis and Orsic start to run toward the exit, but seeing their allies stop, they linger momentarily. 
Harshnag realizes that they aren’t planning to leave. 
Seeing his warnings fall on deaf ears, Harshnag swings his greataxe at the statue of Annam the All-Father and chips it. The entire temple shudders. The frost giant scowls at his enemy and the 3HPP. He dodges the dragon and strikes the statue again, breaking off a large chunk. This act of desecration causes cracks in the ceiling, and the mountain begins to fall around you. “Flee!” yells Harshnag. “Your fate lies elsewhere.” 
Seeing no other choice, the heroes run. The Ancient Blue Dragon seems uninterested in the fleeing humans and attacks Harshnag. As the heroes rush through the hallway to their castle, they see the hallway and the temple where the two monsters fight collapse behind them.
