# Chapter 81 - On the Grand Dame

After a few days of an uneventful voyage in the clouds, Estrella shouts out 
“We are approaching Yartar.”
The 3HPP’s journey has led them to the unexpected location of Yartar and, more intriguingly, to a unique gambling boat known as the Grand Dame. Imber, a merchant on the docks, informed them that the ship was owned by Lord Khaspere Drylund, who was manipulating votes to become the next Waterbaron. The connection of a minor power's petty politician to the disappearance of the Storm Giant King remained unclear. 
“There is more to this boat than we think. Someone on the boat must know who could have been involved in a scheme to disappear a storm giant king,” Orsik declares, his determination palpable. 
Orsik then remembers how Waterbarons get elected. The job is a lifetime appointment. When the current Waterbaron dies, an election occurs. Thus, the current Waterbaron works to keep his enemies from uniting, and his friends close to evade a premature end to their tenure. This creates a very peculiar and lethal bit of politics in the city. 
Orsik then turns to the 3HPP, “Unla Kee is the gambling den expert, he should be on point here,” he says, showcasing their resourcefulness. 
“Right. Okay, how much money do we have?”
“A lot.” T
“We gamble it all.” 
Vardis glares at Orsik, who then turns to the crazed Wizard, “By on point, I mean you can give us your thoughts and ideas rather than make all the decisions. Our biggest problem is getting an invite onto the boat.” 
“Where do you want me to land the castle?” interrupts Escarlotta.
Thistle, who has added more formal gatherings than he remembers, sighs and says, “We could sink the boat and steal all the gold?”
Orsik gives him a dismissive glare, “We need information, and dead people can’t give us information.” 
And then the dwarf remembers getting invited to the Grand Dame was a problem.
Thistle, irritated and a master of court etiquette in the realm of the fey, mutters, “If we land the castle near the boat, it screams. 
“And what about the gambling?” mutters the halfling diviner. 
The crafty dwarf mulls over his options, “We could give him the only known bottle of Champagne de Stomp.”
Thistle sighs, “Look, if we show up with a cloud giant castle, a bottle of Champagne de Stomp, and announce that we are here to gamble, the Grand Dame will gladly let us in. There is nothing like arriving at a Casino in fancy clothes and an expensive transport that screams – take my money.” 
At this point, they all look at their clothes, “and we don’t have fancy clothes.” 
Fortunately, ever since Vardis was kicked out of the Cult of Correlon, he had to learn how to make his vestments. Using the fabric of the Cloud Giantess, he creates some epically showy numbers for the 3HPP. 
Orsik’s attempts are not as successful. Escarlota tsk his attempts, but he retorts, “I don’t have the practice and skill to create what is in my head.” 
As the group gets dressed, they stand on the tower walls, watching their castle land near the docks of Yartar.
“We have landed,” shouts Escarlotta. 
Thistle flies off the tower and heads to the ground; as he does that, a series of arrows lands nearby. 
He looks around and notices that the docks are abandoned. Several hundred feet away are the city walls, where everyone is very well-armed, apprehensive, and confused that a fairy has appeared. 
A few seconds later, Unla Kee lands and shouts, “WE HAVE 1,000,000 GOLD, AND IT’S BURNING A HOLE IN MY POCKET! I just ransacked a dragon hoard and stole a giant cloud castle. I WANT TO GAMBLE!!!” 
As the tower residents stare at the sight of a halfling with a 12-foot wingspan, he pulls out two bags of holding and shows them. 
The captain of the guard is about to shout when a voice from the gates shouts, “Captain, if they wanted us harm, they would have harmed. Let me speak to them.” 
A few moments later, Lord Khaspere Drylund and his retinue appear and introduce themselves. 
Orsik, who took a stroll, steps in front of Unla Kee and presents a bottle. “Lord Drylund, we have heard there is no better place to gamble on the Grand Dame. And I hope this bottle of Champagne de Stomp will get us in?” 
Confused, Lord Drylund stares at the bottle and says, “The Grand Dame is exactly where you should be. And I promise the food and alcohol will be comped.” 
He takes the bottle and hands it to one of his courtiers. 
Orsik is disappointed that the Lord doesn’t recognize the bottle's rarity but resists the urge to lecture him. 
Realizing that the cloud giant castle isn’t the herald of an invasion, the city guard re-opens the gates to the docks, and merchants and on-lookers quickly arrive. 
The 3HPP are gently whisked towards the Grand Dame, the owner wanting to ensure they get most of the money these foolish adventurers wish to gamble. 
As they board the boat from the docks, there is a minor bit of drama when the 3HPP is asked to give up its weapons, but Orsik remembers that he has other options now. 
From the dock, they enter the main cabin, which has been transformed into a gambling hall. The centerpiece is a gold-plated wooden sculpture of a goose resting on a table at the aft. Glasses of fine wine, free for the taking, are arrayed around it. An unflattering portrait of a fat, foppish, grinning man (Lord Drylund) hangs on the wall across a wooden spiral staircase leading up to the dance hall. 
Most of the floor space is taken up with tables of card games. From dusk until dawn, attractive dealers sit behind the tables, distributing cards to the players while small crowds look on. No actual currency passes into the hands of the hired help. Instead, bets are made with coin-sized wooden chips called golden geese, so named because each bears Lord Drylund’s golden goose emblem. 

Before the 3HPP can start to mingle, Pow Ming steps in front of them and asks, “How many chips will you be buying?” 
Orsik stares at him and asks, “What’s your job?” 
“I am in charge of everything so Lord Drylund can do his job.” 
The Shou human pauses, politely, and forcefully asks, “How many chips do you want?” 
“How many are required for the high-rollers table?”
He declares, “You want the Sava tables.” 
Thistle turns to Orsik - “Sava is a drow variant of the game of chess. Two players play against each other using ebony pieces resembling drow warriors, mages, and priestesses. The goal is to destroy the other player’s noble house. It is a game of skill, deception, and insight.” 
The fairy then muses, “In a city with this many lethal conspiracies, it’s no surprise that the rich and powerful play this game.” 
“So, how do we find a player?” 
“You get invited to play a game.” 
Thistle starts scanning the room to see who could be an easy mark. He spots a beautiful woman clad in a gorgeous purple dress. She has black hair that is cropped short on the sides. She’s wearing a stunning necklace set with black stones around her neck. 
Before Thistle can determine her skill, her white-haired noble opponent pushes a stack of golden geese across the table. He then rises from the plush chair and declares, “I don’t know how you do, Lady Atalia; you seemed to know my every move before I knew it myself. Thank you for the game,” and then bends down and kisses the woman’s extended hand. “Now I must find another table lest I squander all the coin I’ve spent a lifetime acquiring.” 
She softly says, “You do that, Lord Hubbard. Until next time.” 
Unla Kee quickly sits down to face her. 
Lady Atalia smiles, says, “Are you rich?” and then laughs. “Of course you are; you announced your fortunes the minute you arrived.” 
“What are the stakes?” 
“5000?” 
“Only?” 
“I thought we would play for 5000 and then see where it takes us. You are the great adventurers who stole a castle from cloud giants. I am a poor lady playing these games of skill, chance, and treachery.” 
Orsik realizes she’s trying to play them. Unla Kee, on the other hand, is convinced she is a poor lady who is out of her depth. 
Meanwhile, Thistle finally finds a mark, Unla Kee. 
As Orsik stares at the money he’s about to lose, he notices that Lady Atalia is probing his brain with telepathy. 
Orsik turns to Thistle and Vardis and says, “I can’t watch.” He pulls them away from what he hopes is her mind-reading range and explains the situation. Vardis observes that they have no magic they can use to counter her power. Thistle notes that she probably has a lot of information that she might be willing to trade. 
“Why?” 
“Because we could threaten her with that expose?” 
Vardis declares grimly - “But not in a dark alley. We do not threaten women in dark alleys.” 
Orsik turns to Vardis, “Okay.” 
The cleric then suggests that they lose the game and tries to convince her to talk and share information. 
With that, they approach the table. Unla Kee is excited and is about to pull off the golden geese, which are worth some 10,000 gold on the table. 

Orsik realizes that even if he told Unla Kee how she was cheating, the diviner would be confident that his victory was even more certain. So the dwarf first asks Unla Kee to let him play, but Unla Kee refuses. Then he tells him to get a drink, and Unla Kee calls a server. Finally, the fighter has had enough, pushing him out of the chair, “I am playing first because I’m smart. I’ve got the headband. I’m only going to come in for 1000. I can win this game because I am smart!” 
Unla Kee is momentarily upset, but the drinks arrive, and he forgets. 
Lady Atalia appears to be losing, and Orsik is a bit surprised and is about to go for the kill when she – obviously aware of his plans – counters and then wins. 
She puts the golden geese in a silk pouch and says, “Another game?” 
Orsik stares at her and says in a quiet but dangerous voice, “Oh, my fair lady, it’s not a fair game when you read my mind.” 
Lady Atalia denies it in an equally threatening and dangerous voice. 
Orsik thinks Lady Atalia doesn’t even want the whiff of scandal, so he mutters, “Perhaps we could have a private conversation about some other topics? You like gold, and we have a lot of it.” 
She stares and then speaks directly to his mind, “We need to talk. Not here. Follow me when the boat returns to the docks. Nod if you understand me.” 
With that, Orsik stands up and leaves. Unla Kee sees the open slot and rushes to sit down, but Orsik once again stops him. 
As the evening progresses, Vardis mutters about wanting to meet Lord Drylund. Pow overhears that and says Lord Khaspere Drylund would like to meet them for a private drink in his office. 
The private offices are decorated in the nouveau riche style, where expensive, tasteless things are displayed. The windows have purple velvet curtains and scented candles in gaudy candelabras. In the middle of the room is a table with a large aquarium tank resting atop it. The aquarium contains a coral reef that hosts Lord Drylund’s pet octopus. Other furnishings include a silk hammock, a writing desk (covered with quills and loose sheets of blank parchment), a small table holding a silver wine decanter and matching goblet, and an iron stove. 
“I see you already gambled with Lady Atalia?” 
Orsik smiles. 
“She’s perfect.” 
But Orsik isn’t very talkative. This annoys Drylund, who picks up the last remaining bottle of Champagne du Stomp outside of Ravenloft – “Shall we drink this?” 
Orsik turns pale, “My lord, you should hold onto that. Perhaps a lesser vintage?” 
“You’re right. I should save this for a better occasion when I become the Waterbaron?” The Lord laughs, “That’s exactly what I should do.” 
Thistle says, “Happy to share it with you then.” 
“Of course you would.” 
“And in that vein,” asks Orsik, “how can we help?” 
Lord Khaspere Drylund smiles, “Your arrival here has been a godsend and a positive endorsement for my candidacy.” 
“But unfortunately, when the election happens is unclear.” 
“People die every day,” observes Orsik. 
“I know such a tragedy. You know there’s been a rash of nobles disappearing.” 
“Thrown out of windows?” 
“Oh no! And how much could it hurt to fall from 10 feet up?” He pauses, “A group of adventurers freed one and magically reappeared next to this father.” He thinks, “Lord Zymorven’s son!” 
“Lord Zymorven’s son?” asks Thistle. 
“It was in all the papers?” asks Vardis. 
“Yes. And it was a terrible blow for the Waterbaron.” 
Orsik cuts to the chase. “Who are your key rivals? We like your establishment. We think you are the right choice for Yartar and want to ensure you end up where you deserve to be.” 
Thistle asks, “Who would be foolish to oppose you?” 
He stares at the group, “are you offering to help?” 
“We could kill all your opponents?” observes Orsik.
The Lord splutters and then composes himself. “No, that won’t work. Too many guilds and assassins would come looking for their killers. There are three ways we win elections. The first is convincing people we are the best person for the job. The second is by bribing folks with promises after you are made Waterbaron. The third is by holding a loved one’s ransom.” 
Thistle turns to Orsik, “Are you making him an offer to kidnap?”
Orisk brushes the fairy aside, “We’ve had some entanglements with various groups, and it would be good to have an entire city on our side. Certain cities are not on our side. You seem like the guy we can get behind and help each other.” 
Lord Drylund stares at them and tries to size them up. After a few moments, he says, “When I become the Waterbaron, I promise that Yartar will be friends with the 3HPP. And because of that friendship, the 3HPP has committed to protecting our cities from cloud giants!” 
The owner of the Grand Dame swallows the last remaining alcohol, “I think it’s best if we continue this conversation in a different private location. I need you to meet some associates.” 
Orsik smiles, “Very well then.” 
With that, Lord Drylund ushers the group out. As Orsik gets up, he decides that now is a good time to ask about the coin and why it might have been found where King Hekaton disappeared.
