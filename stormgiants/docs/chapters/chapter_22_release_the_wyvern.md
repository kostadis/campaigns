# Chapter 22 - Release the Wyvern
“Die Unla Kee!” screams the Orc as his axe slices through the robes of the gnome. 
“The prophecy says otherwise,” thinks Unla Kee as he falls down. 
The orc chieftain stares at the dead halfling and laughs, seized with blood lust charges at Vardis. The cleric sees him advance and calls on Correllon to blind his enemy. The chieftain is blinded but still lands his blow for 15 hit points. 
Thistle looks at the two orc chiefs, the one wearing a blue sash and the one wearing a green sash, and says, “these look like they are pretty tough!” 
Vardis stares at the blue Orc, “he’s been hurt a few times. It’s not going to be too hard.” 
Thistle stares at the injured orc chief a little bit and realizes he’s the father of one of the orc whelps they brought from the caves. 
“Huh, why wasn’t he afraid of our threats?” 
“Maybe because Orsik’s threat was to *send the whelp to bed without dessert and will not read a bedtime story,*” mumbles Jarlor, the one-time spy. 
While this dialogue continues, Wallace is running various simulations in his head about the optimal path necessary to attack one of the orcs to maximize his charge. He runs 20 feet north, then turns around at a sharp angle, and then runs 10 feet east; charging at the Orc, he kills with one blow. The Orc flies 10 feet away from the force of the blow. 
Vardis casts a scorching ray killing an injured orc and seriously wounding another – “try threatening him like your mom used to?” 
Orsik nods and shouts in common, “if you don’t stop fighting, your whelp will be forced to eat all of his vegetables.” 
The entire town stares at Orsik. He turns to them – “I hated vegetables.” 
Kurglow, the orc chieftain of the Spinripper Clan, is unimpressed. 
Orsik shouts, “I AM NOT NEGOTIATING WITH MONSTERS AGAIN!” 
Thistle agrees and kills two orcs. 
Unla Kee, whom Vardis has since healed, casts a mind sliver on the Orc Chieftain with the Green sash to no effect. 
The Orc Chieftain then attacks Orsik and does 32 HP of damage. Orsik stands. 
Kurglow is now very angry at Orsik, partly because he threatened his son and because he insulted his fathering. The threats only made sense if Kurglow’s whelp was a soft, overly civilized, and pampered creature, not a wild, dangerous orc like his father. And that Kurglow spoiled his whelp. That list insult is too much for him to bear, so he charges at Orsik. 
Chandra, Vardis, Wallace, and Unla Kee each get a wack at Kurglow as he runs toward Orsik. 
Wallace hits the other orc chief and screams, “healing!” 
Vardis shouts, “many need healing. Let me see what I can do!” He walks up to Orsik and heals his wounds. 
Unla Kee tries his mind-spike again on the Orc with the green sash, and this time it penetrates the Orc’s mental defenses. 
Orsik mumbles, “enough talking, more killing,” and strikes twice with his blade at the green Orc, killing him. 
Kurglow seeing the other chieftain dead, shouts, “give me my son, or I will kill you, you piece of shit!” 
Orsik stares at him, confused. 
And then Orc that protected the whelps appears and starts screaming at Kurglow in orcish. 
After the screaming ends, Kurglow again turns to Orsik – “give me my child, or you will die.” 
“Does that mean you will also leave?” 
“Yes.” 
Orsik calls one of the villagers of Parnast to return the child. 
At that point, the female caregiver starts screaming at Orsik and Kurglow. Once done crying, she throws dirt at everyone. 
Kurglow snarls at her, then the chieftain points his finger at her and shouts at her. 
The female caregiver falls down on her knees and bangs her fists into the ground as Kurglow walks away.
The group stares, confused. 
One of Orsik’s militia, who happens to speak common, walks up and explains – “well, boss, here’s the deal. Kurglow was furious at Naz because she was alive, and his son was a prisoner. Naz insisted that she did everything to ensure Kurglow’s child was safe. Kurglow laughed at her and said that she only cared about her child, and if she could save hers and sacrifice his, then she would have. Naz denies the accusation and begs Kurglow to take her with him. He laughs at her and says – I think this is what he said – ‘Unlike you, I can take my whelp and leave you to die.’” 
Naz sits near the well in the middle of the town, sobbing. 
At this point, Bad Fruul howls in anger at the betrayal of Kurglow. He starts preparing for the final assault on his mammoth, with several ettins and a hoard of orcs. 
Thistle feeds everyone good berries – “Uncle Joon always said you shouldn’t fight on an empty stomach!” 
Raggnar looks at Orsik – “do you have any plan?” 
Orsik smiles – “it is time for us to release the wyvern!” 
Chandra shouts – “are you mad? He will kill us all!” 
Gundalin screams – “it’s a desperate ploy of a madman!” 
Orsik laughs, “yes, and it is time for us to risk everything. We will free the Wyvern and hope they and the giant fight.” 
Just then, Blassios and two sprites appear – “My friends! My great friends! My drinking buddy Unla Kee! We have come to help you fight this Bad Fruul!” 
Thistle is delighted, “Blassios, you came!” 
“Of course I did!” 
Meanwhile, Orsik talks to Chandra – “so how do I do this?”
Chandra scratches her head and says, “The Wyvern is tied to a post in the middle of the former stables. To free it, you will have to get past it. The good news is that this Wyvern likes fresh meat, and we have a lot of it right now.” She points to the dead orcs surrounding the battlefield. “Throw dead bodies in one corner, and then rush and remove the chain from the hoop. The Wyvern will notice it’s free and charge at the nearest living thing.” 
“So I better make sure it’s the giant!” 
“Yes.” 
As Bad Fruul climbs his mammoth and approaches the center of Parnast, Thistle shouts, “do we have time to rest?” 
“No,” shouts Chandra, “my family’s nemesis is guiding his actions. She knows that she must attack now, or she will lose her chance for vengeance.” 
Vardis says – “let’s drink our potions of healing.” 
“And the mud potions of healing!”
Orsik looks at the advancing forces, “they look big.” 
“And tough!” 
“There is no shame in fleeing,” says the voice in Orsik’s head.
Vardis sees his friend’s eyes and says, “yes, there is.” 
“Indeed!”
## Orsik 
Orsik stares at the battlefield. The orcs are coming in from the south and north, and Bad Fuul and his giants from the east. The last of the militia kill one advancing Orc from the south and then are overrun. 
It is up to him. 
As he wonders what help he can count on, Naz stops sobbing and stands up. 
“I will get my revenge on Kurglow after we kill this idiot of a giant and the orcs that follow him to their doom.” 
He smiles and heads to the stables. Chandra shouts, “I thought you were going to send the guards?” 
“No, it is my deadly plan, and I am the leader, and I will do it.” 
And for a moment, Orsik realizes that that thing that always got in his way may – possibly – be a thing of the past.
## Thistle
Thistle flies up and sees the field of battle in front of him. He thinks for a moment to attack one of the ettins and then reconsiders and strikes one of the orcs trying to block any escape. 
He watches helplessly as his friend Blassios complains about the rudeness of an orc who strikes him with a Javelin. 
And then, to his horror, the hill giant shouts – “I hate fairies that fly!”, smashes through the houses of Parnast and casts hold person.  
“I don’t want to plummet to my death!” thinks Thistle and wills himself to keep moving. 
“*Uncle Joon never said anything about spell-casting Hill Giants. He said Hill Giants were dumb and fat.*” 
“That didn’t work! That didn’t work!” shouts Bad Fruul as he bangs his head. 
## Vardis 
*I’ve been saving my fireball for a special occasion. This is it.*
The orcs from the north are caught up in the fire, wind, and heat. Three are dead, but three survive. 
And he smiles when Chandra casts bless on Orsik, who has promised to protect her, Thistle because apparently Bad Fruul sees him as a target and a target that is not her is a target to preserve, and Wallace, whom she has known from the day he was born and loves him like a son. 
## Wallace 
Wallace has a freaky ability to understand the specific distances between elements in a 60-foot radius, allowing him to plot movement that helps him charge at his enemies. 
He decides to attack the Orc that hurt Blassios, but he misses. 
“Need to rethink my planning.” 
From the rear, he hears the sound of a Javelin hitting Vardis. 
“T’is but a flesh wound!” 
And then Wallace is struck by an orc. 
As he turns to the Orc, he hears the roar of a mammoth charge. The creature smashes through the houses, sending glass, wood, and trinkets flying in every direction. Dust settles, and the beast is closer than Wallace would like. 
## Blassios
“For Thornacius!” shouts Thistle. 
*Certainly not for that buffoon. But why am I here? I am the bravest of all Seelies, but this is crazy. This is all Oblivilish’s fault. It’s his revenge for my indiscretion. And I am too close for comfort. I do not want to be fighting with my sword. At least if I don’t have to, and I most certainly don’t!*
Blassios takes cover and fires an arrow at the Orc that struck him with a Javelin.
He signals to the sprites to attack the mammoth, and they poison the creature but do not put it to sleep. 
And he smiles even more, when Naz strikes the mammoth with a guiding bolt. 
## Unla Kee
*No one can kill me until the end of times. At this point, time will end, and then who cares. *
He fumbles for his wand of web and toys with the idea of using it or casting another spell. 
The mammoth is caught in the web. 
Unla Kee, having dealt with that threat, moves directly in front of the giant. 
## Orsik 
He opens the stable doors closest to Bad Fruul. There is the Wyvern feasting on the dead corpses, and its feasting is more interesting than the dwarf. Orsik uses that distraction to go past the creature and free it. The Wyvern realizing they are free, perks its draconic head up, looks at the bloody mess in front of them, and smacks its lips upon seeing the giant outside.  

With a quick flap of its wings, it rushes out. 
In the background, Orsik hears Vardis getting attacked by an Orc from the north. And Thistle commands Thuul to approach one of the Orcs to the south. The next thing Orsik hears is the thud of the Orc’s body on the ground. And then the sound of a fireball. 
Orsik smiles. 
And then he rushes out the other door. 
He hears the wyvern strike the giant. And then hears an ettins mace connect with the Wyvern and scream 
– “I thought you said they would not free the Wyvern!” 

Thistle flies to a roof. He looks around and notices that there are plenty of wounded orcs.
