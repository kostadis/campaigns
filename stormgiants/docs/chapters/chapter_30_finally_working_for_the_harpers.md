# Chapter 30 - FInally working for the Harpers
## 5th day of the 3rd day of the month of Kythorn
### Krowen Valharrow
Krowen stares at the adventurers in his tower and inner sanctum, and he wonders if they are the tool he can use to unlock the secrets behind the fall of the Ordning.
Since Ezra had discovered that using mercenaries derisked core Harper agents and provided plausible deniability, the Harpers had completed missions that would have been impossible in the past. Krowen was an old-timer and believed in the old ways, but these were desperate times, and this group had been sent by Dalthara herself. 
“So, are you in?”
They stare at him. 
“Damn it,” he thinks, “I am coming off desperate. How did Ezra pull this off with the First Faction?” 
The dwarf looks at him and says, “we have been doing this for some time, so yes.” 
“Excellent!” 
After a few moments, the dwarf asks him – “so, how do we go out?” 
Krowen looks at him and thinks, “the reports on the dwarf are that he is close to the Zhentarim.” 
“The front door!” 
His friend, the elf, looks at Krowen confused, “but you went to all of these lengths to send us through a special teleport spell involving some alcohol to hide our identity, and now you want us to walk out the front door?” 
“Drat,” Krowen thinks, “he saw through my ruse to burn Orsik.” 
Krowen thinks, “excellent point! This entirely slipped my mind.” 
The elf stares at him, confused. Krowen puts on his best confused old mage look and says, “I can’t believe I forgot that!” 
“Well, where should we go next?”
“Yartar to find the sword.”
Vardis asks – “so much must we pay?”
“Nothing!”
“Only our loyalty,” mumbles the dwarf.
“Is that a problem?” 
“No!” says the dwarf apologetically, “I always wanted to work for the Harpers.” 
And then the fairy asks Krowen, “off-topic, have you ever enchanted a flying tower?” 
Krowen explains that the orb necessary to turn a tower into a flying castle is a device that can only be obtained from the Cloud Giants, either by persuasion or violence. 
When Vardis tries to suggest that they could handle a cloud giant, Krowen blanches – “those fools will get themselves killed. Let me see how to dissuade them.”
“My dear young man, if a fire giant is a fully grown adult, then a cloud giant is superior specimen of a human being, trained to fight like the greatest of fighters and armed to the teeth with magical weapons and defenses of all kinds.”
Seeing the look on the elf reassures Krowen that he has nothing to fear from this.
And with that, Krowen smiles as they teleport to his agent in Yartar.  
### Thistle 
“Time to get that sword!” shouts Orsik.
And with that, Thistle finds himself in a garden with statues of frolicking dryads and satyrs situated among the flowers and stone benches. Thistle stares at it and groans, “this is all wrong! That fairy would never be frolicking with Alciabes! She hated him!” And then the illusion falls apart, and he sees that he is in a 40-foot-square guardian patio enclosed by an 8-foot-high wall of ivy-covered stone. The garden is in disrepair, with broken benches, weed-infested flowerbeds, and shattered statuary surrounding the circle they just arrived in. 
As Thistle stares at this embarrassing attempt to turn his culture and people into props of a garden, a masculine-looking person arrives and shouts, “who are you?” 
Before he can answer, Orsik shouts, “Agents of the Harpers.” 
“Agents or Contractors.” 
Thistle stumbles, “I don’t recall signing an explicit contract.”
The old man grumbles, “where did you come from?”
“Everlund?”
“You must have spoken to Krowen.” 
Vardis affirms that. 
“That pompous two-bit self-aggrandizing … He’s decided that having agents, even agents that are not sworn to the cause of the Harpers, is better than no agents. Being a Harper meant something. It wasn’t just a job!” 
“Sounds about right.” 
The old man grumbles. 
“I am Kolbaz and your host. Welcome to Yartar.” 
### Orsik
“Harthal? Do you know where we can find him?” 
Kolbaz stares at him. 
Thistle nudges Orsik and whispers, “hey, I think Harthal was dating a rogue, and the local rogues guild probably knows where he is if we can get in touch with them.”
Orsik nods, “should we ask about them?”
But Orsik can tell Thistle is uncomfortable meeting with the Hand of Yartar after their attempted assassination.
As Thistle and Kolbaz talk, Orsik notices how Kolbaz is dumbfounded that Thistle is an assassination target. “They don’t do that kind of wet work.”
Thistle, visibly annoyed, says, “the pointy end of their blades were pointed at my neck.” 
“Well, you must have really pissed them off. Perhaps you could tell me what you have done?”
“I did nothing!” 
“Well, fine, then, can you tell me about yourself? Perhaps we can find out.” 
Orsik nudges Thistle, “ask about the Lord’s Alliance.”
“How do you feel about the Lord’s Alliance?” 
“The Lord’s Alliance is a league of wealthy lords whose only interests are the powerful and their powerful families. They do not care for the little people. We are just bodies to be used to fight their wars and sacrifice in their vainglorious conquests. They are attempting to create a supernational state that will unite the various city-states, but it will never happen because those of us who value freedom will never let it happen.”
Thistle is satisfied and then asks, “You aren’t allied with the Cult of the Dragon?”
Orsik smirks, that faux-pas is what he used to do all the time. But then he remembers that Thistle doesn’t know the history of the War of the Dragon. The Harpers first identified the risk and rallied the various factions to fight. 
Kolbaz straightens his back, “I FOUGHT THE CULT AT THE WELL OF THE DRAGONS!” 
And Orsik is impressed as Thistle doesn’t even miss a beat, “so you know Adimond Kroskas?” 
“Nope never heard of him.” 
“But he was a lead cultist. I thought you harpers knew about him. Look, the cult of the dragon is trying to assassinate me.”
Kolbaz laughs, “that sounds preposterous. Why would they want to kill a fairy.”
Orsik is intrigued, “why is that preposterous?”
“Because the cult was destroyed. Why would they waste time on him?”
Thistle is indignant, “because I am looking for him, and they want to clean up their mistakes. A key ingredient for the ritual was the spring’s first Blossom that Adminod collected.”
Koblaz thinks. 
“The Spring First’s Blossom is a powerful artifact.” 
The old mage sputters and shouts, “I know what the Spring’s First Blossom is. I got a first-class education. I’ll have you note!” 

He then pauses, “but that wasn’t used in the ritual. I was involved in the research, which was not used in the ritual.”
“Are you sure?” 
“Yes. Very odd.” 
“Are you really sure?”
“YES!” 
Orsik realizing that Kolbaz’s professional pride has been hurt attempts to soothe him by praising the quality of the illusion and his role. “Only Thistle saw through it.”
“Yes, it was an inaccurate representation of the Seelie court. Look at all of these errors and that!” Thistle starts to point to all sorts of mistakes of the illusion.
Kolbaz stares at him – “why we should meet up and have some tea and discuss how to improve it.”
Thistle glares, “to make your magic fantasy land a bit more realistic as you treat my people as props to amuse?” 
Kolbaz is taken aback. Orsik turns to him, “about the Hand of Yartar?”
“Yes, I don’t have links to the Hand, but I know where you can find them. The Wink and Kiss is their hangout. Go to the tavern and ask a server. The rogues are all over the town.”
Vardis and Orsik are surprised, “there are a lot of rogues in this town?”
Thistle stares at them, “you mean you didn’t notice?” 
“Alright, let’s go,” shouts Orsik.
Thistle demures, “I don’t want to go to a bar full of people who want to assassinate me.”
Vardis and Orsik tell him not to worry and that they will defend him.
Even Unla Kee, still recovering from his ordeal, shouts, “they will have to get through me to get to you, Thistle. All 6 of my hitpoints.” 
Thistle stares at his companions and realizes he has allies, “I love you guys.” 
## 6th day of the 3rd day of the month of Kythorn
The Wink and Kiss Tavern is near the easternmost gate of Yartar and is famous as a safe place to do business. Inside, it has a maze–like collection of compact rooms, concealed side passages, and sequestered passageways. The fest hall was a bawdy and fun establishment that didn’t take itself too seriously. It was an excellent place for those who wanted to find easy-going friends for the evening. 
Due to its distinguished patrons, the Wink and Kiss is declared a safe haven for anyone who enters. Weapons were checked at the door, and rowdy behavior was not tolerated. 
“Good thing we have spells,” mutters Unla Kee as they are led to one of the rooms. 
### Vardis
Vardis notices an old painting of the former owners, “Asklar and Beldorm, I think I knew them. Can’t remember for the life of me.” 
As the group settles down at a wooden table, with finely crafted wooden chairs stained dark cherry, Orsik mutters, “we are broke.”
“Completely?” 
“Well, we sold everything to get him raised.” 
“And I love you guys.” 
Vardis turns to Orsik, “so we have no money and have to bribe someone. Maybe they’ll want something else?” 
“Loyalty? I am so done with that.” 
“I was thinking companionship.” 
Thistle gulps.
“I am the best looking.”
“That’s true. You do have that goth elf look,” says Orsik.
“Thank you.” 
A tiefling barhop arrives at the table and asks for their orders. 
Vardis gulps and says, “we’ll split an order of water. That’s free, right?” 
She glares.
“Just joking. Can I have a look at the menu?” 
She hands him a menu and then comes back 10 minutes later.
“So?”
“I need to go use the restroom,” says Thistle, and Vardis thinks, “coward.” 
“We haven’t been this broke since you did the THING, Orsik,” mutters Vardis. 
The tielfling sizes them up and says, “if you are that broke, then I know a gal.” 
“You’re going to offer us a job even though you know nothing about us?”
She looks at them – “you’re the group that defeated the giant in Triboar and has been advertising the play? Right?” 
Thistle nods vigorously, “that’s us!” 
Vardis turns to Unla Kee – “the pump part of the pump and dump cycle is going well.” 
“Yeah, the thing is, you don’t have a name. See, I have a name, Maia, short for Qumaia. And folks have been calling you the ‘three half-pints and a pint.’
Vardis laughs hard at Orsik’s discomfort. 
Thistle flutters, “No, our name is – Thistle Wendrod and Companions!”
Vardis laughs harder when Orsik blusters, “so now it’s all about you!” 
“Yeah, you guys are local celebrities.” 
“So buy us a round.”
“Not that famous.” 
“Think of the traffic we will bring in,” Unla Kee whispers.
“See, I know this gal who needs people to get some other people. And you guys are handy with a sword, and you took down a giant.”
“Two,” insists Orsik.
She smiles and corrects herself, “two fire giants. I’ll be back in a few.”
Unla Kee glares at Vardis, “we should gamble. I change the fates.” 
“Yeah, but if you die, we can’t raise you. And that’s a problem because the world will end.” 
“No, I won’t be there to STOP the end of the world.” 
And so over the next five days, “The three half-pints and a pint” are bounty hunters. They split into two groups. Unla Kee stalks his prey, exploiting his ability to twist the fates, and captures “Dashing” Dallon Flek, a handsome redhead that has bright eyes and rogueish good looks. The others capture Tink Urbelle, a grubby gnome with curly hair and animated facial expressions. 
## 30th day of the 3rd tenday of the month Kythorn
The Hand of Yartar handing out the bounties, Elicia “Kal” Kallaphan, is a severe half-elf that sees through the “Three half-pints and a Pint” attempt at humor. She has blond hair, wears a cloak at all times, and carries a finely crafted rapier. 
“Here’s your payout, 200 Gold, 50 for me, and so 150 for you.
The group takes their money glumly. That’s a lot but not enough.
And so later that evening, they are again at the Wink and Kiss, and the tiefling Maia is also there. 
“So now, will you give us the information we need?” 
“Oh, no, I’ll give you the information you need to get the information you need.”
“And?” asks Vardis.
“That will be five gold.” 
“Seriously?”
“Yes.”
So he hands her the five gold, and she points to another lady dressed in white at the bar named Eltio. Vardis sizes her up as a sullen, distant, and dangerous player. 
As Maia leaves, he says, “this is the best beer I have ever had.” 
Vardis turns to him, “longest run of sobriety?” 
Meanwhile, Unla Kee had been on a lengthy drinking binge and has now been sober for two weeks. His eyes are sharper than usual, and he mutters, “the longest.” 
Eltio explains that it will cost them 500 gold to get information on Harthal, or they can get Tamlin Zoar to waive the fee. 
“Who is that?” 
Eltio calls Maia, “I thought you said they were serious people?” 
Maia tries to defend the introduction, and ultimately Eltio sends her off with a warning. 
“Tamlin is a legendary member of the Hand of Yartar who built her own town on the Evermoor Road on the way to Everlund. If she thinks we should help you gratis, then we’ll help you.”
Vardis can’t resist, “are you a patron of the arts?”
“Yes!” 
“Well then, how about we give you some tickets to the up-and-coming production of our play!” 
“I don’t think so.”
“We will make you a GOLD sponsor.” 
She looks uncertain and says, “No, that’s not what I want to spend my money on.”
“But you are a patron?” 
Orsik turns to her and grouses, “look, we’ll get this letter as long as you promise to bring 50 friends to our play!” 
She looks at him and says, “deal.”
