# Chapter 48 - The end of a relationship
## Orsik 
He was pleased with himself. After a day and a half in Silverymoon, the 3HPP had armed themselves with magic and weapons. They had spent their coin and eaten in the fine restaurants of the fair city. 
“Yes, life was good.”
When a patrol of the guards of Silverwatch stopped in front of him, what appeared to be a captain addressed him. 

“The High Lord of the Silver Marches as well as the ruler of Silverymoon and the High Marshal of the Argent Legion, the army of the League of the Silver Marches, High Lord Methrammar Aerasumé wishes to speak with you.” 
“He wants an audience with us?”
“No. You are diplomatic envoys from the City of Everlund. And you are expected to present your credentials to his lordship.” 
“Okay, we’ll get to it eventually.”
The captain coughs.
Orsik sizes them up and then thinks better of it, “Diplomatic Immunity.” 
With a tinny nasal voice, the captain says, “And that’s why we are here to escort the 3HPP and ensure that nobody gets in your way.” 
“We don’t need protection. Very nice of you, though.” 
The captain continues, “You may not know this about diplomatic immunity, but you can be expelled regardless of said immunity. And more importantly, his Lordship wants to talk to you.”
Orsik, realizing the opportunity, agrees, as does Thistle. 
Ten minutes later, Orsik and the 3HPP enter the audience chamber. 
Thistle. 
Thistle and the 3HPP are ushered into the audience chamber of the High Lord. The room is filled with courtiers discussing matters of state, budgets, and trivia of all kinds. 
The aide coughs and introduces the 3HPP; the letters of introduction are handed over to the High Mage – Taern “Thunderspell” Hornblade, who examines them and declares that they are to his satisfaction. 
“We are here to offer you a cultural exchange with the city of Everlund,” answers Thistle to the High Lord’s question about why they are here. 
The courtiers laugh. “That’s a mere village town, a hovel,” declares a lord. 
Thistle overhears Orsik outraged, muttering, “Does he know he just insulted Everlund’s diplomats?” For a moment, he worries that the dwarf will do something foolish, but then he is quiet. 
The High Lord smiles and asks, “What could the city of Everlund share with our city?” 
“We have a traveling play.” 
The High Lord interrupts, “You didn’t tell me, High Mage.” Thistle is about to continue, but the High Lord interrupts him, “You are the play's producers?” 
Thistle smiles and nods.
“I am in love with it. I have seen it twice already.”
“Twice? And are you a fan of Bob Saget?” 
“Yes! I love the segment in the middle of the play, Mordekainens’ Best Home Illusions, where local mages present a short imaginary tale, and the audience votes on the best one. And the play ends differently every time. It keeps bringing the audience back for more.” 
Vardis is super excited and shouts, “There are 20 different endings!” 
“No spoilers,” demands the High Lord. “That would cause a diplomatic incident!” The High Lord is excited and says, “I demand we arrange for a long run of this show. Chamberlain, please work with these producers and arrange for an extended run in the royal theater.” 
Thistle is excited and about to say something when Unla Kee shouts, “And 50,000 gold pieces. And if you can’t front that cost, we’ll return to Everlund.” 
The fairy and the dwarf try to stop Unla Kee, who can’t stop talking. “It’s for the pre-production costs, not for us!” 
And that seems to have done the trick; the High Lord says, “If that’s what it takes!” 
The High Treasurer is appalled and interrupts the High Lord with words about budget, fiscal policy, deficit spending, and inflation. 
High Lord Aerasumé sighs and says, “I can’t give you 50,000, but I can give you a thousand. And I can certainly tell the Chamberlain that the Royal Theater will be offered for free.” 
Thistle notes the Chamberlain’s tight and very fake smile. 
Unla Kee, to Thistle’s chagrin, doesn’t. “For that amount, we consider giving you a discounted pair of free tickets.” 
That does not land as well. Thistle watches as Orsik steps into smooth things over, “what my friend Unla Kee was trying to say was that we thank you for your support, and you will, of course, have two reserved seats for the play.” 
With that taken care of, Thistle turns to another topic. “Can you help us with the extradition of Xamlyn Margaster?”
Sensing confusion, Thistle continues, “She has committed unpardonable sins in Everlund, and we have come to pick her up.” 
The High Lord presents a sad face, “Lady Margaster, of the noble house of Margaster, is an ally of the High Lord and a member of a respectable Waterdhavian noble house. Lady Margaster is a guest in our city, and I will not deport her.” 
Thistle worries that they might run into trouble if they talk to Margaster and asks, “Is it okay if we talk to her?” 
“Lady Margaster is a member of this community, and she is free to speak with anyone or not speak with anyone, like everybody.” The High Lord looks at the Chamberlain and then back to Thistle, “I am truly delighted that the city of Everlund sent such famous producers as their emissaries. High Captain Horix Zoar corrected our mistake when we through you off the city because of a terrible misunderstanding.” 
And with that, Thistle and his companions are shown to the outside of the High Lord's Residence. 
Once outside, Thistle turns to his companions, “Killing Xamlyn is too easy. We need to disgrace her and to do that, we need to expose his devils.”
“Dispel Magic?” asks Vardis. 
“No, because magic is used to alter the appearance of folks and is equivalent to pulling off someone’s wig,” he mutters.
“How did you know that?” 
“Uncle Joon.” 
“Don’t want to know.” 
Finally, after some discussion, they decide that perhaps an approach would be to ally themselves with the Senneth, the Cleric of Mielikki, who raised Thistle from the dead. 
Surprisingly, Senneth is not surprised to discover that there are devils in the city. “In a city of 40,000, at least one mortal is a devil; why not a fiend?” But she dismisses the idea of any cults operating, “after the cult of the dragon was defeated, there was a general crackdown on cults. The smart ones packed their bags and left the city.” 
Thistle decides to take a risk and describes the appearance of the Cambions that showed up at the trial. 
Senneth is shocked, “those are the men who follow Lady Margaster.” The cleric then pauses, “I do recall some unpleasantness with you and the Margasters.”
Thistle, realizing this might go badly, changes the topic and distracts her from this line of questioning. 
Unla Kee brings up the play, trying to change the topic of conversation. 
But Senneth sees unwanted connections, “interesting that you bring up a story about a devil and a play with a demon.” 

## Vardis

Vardis, sensing a potential disaster, turns to Senneth and asks – “What library do you recommend we visit for research on Cambions?” 
Her train of thought interrupted, she says, “The Lady’s College!” 
The heroes leave Mielikki’s Glade, and the high priest crosses the Moonway, traverses the city, goes over the invisible bridge to the north, and reaches the college. 
Thistle, that repository of the obscure of the North, recalls from his uncle that the college was famous for not having a “master-apprentice” form of education but instead a classroom-style education. 
Arriving at the Lady’s College, many young mages, scholars, and teachers are running around.  
Vardis wonders where to go when a bright young tiefling wearing the “Ask Me” sign approaches him.
“Welcome to the Lady’s College! Are you a student? Parents? Or visitors?”
Vardis stares at her and says, “We want to learn about Cambions.” 
The young tiefling Anakis is giddy – “Cambions are my favorite! There’s a lecture by Sage Vusk on the topic just down the hall.” 
“Lecture?”
Anakis giggles and then composes herself, “Right, at the Lady’s College, material is presented in prepared speeches. Students can choose to attend or not choose to attend. Some lectures have not had a person show up in years, but the Sages keep giving the lectures because they are required to. Sage Vusk is a sweetheart, and nobody goes to his class.” She pauses, “Cambions are cool, but nobody in Silverymoon will ever see an infernal creature …” 
Anakis notices that Orsik is staring at her, “Yes, I know, not a goodly race. But the Lady’s College is not as narrowminded…” 
Vardis takes leave and enters the large, empty room. At the front is a podium, and in front are a set of sparsely populated rows of chairs. He sits down and listens.
Sage Vusk discusses the origins of Cambions and observes that a few centuries ago, it was assumed that they were the progeny of male demons and female humans. Still, now we know this was not the case and that a Cambion is the name given to the progeny of any union between a demon and a non-demon. He then continues that as part of this more profound research into Cambion anthropology, some modernist sages believe that a Cambion can have free will. In contrast, others like Sage Vusk believe that, ultimately, the infernal nature of the Cambion means that that is impossible. He bolsters that argument with the observation that the Cambion is the foot soldier of blood war. 
Vardis shouts, “Disposable? Who disposes of them?” 
“Their masters.” 
“You seem to be the kind of people who want to kill a Cambion. This might be of more interest. Although many have focused on the Cambion’s raw brutality, their ability to charm their enemies using their fiendish charm makes them valuable. A single Cambion did in many adventuring groups, like The Noble Daggers.” 
“Is there any way to resist this charm?” 
“Great question! There are the usual means. But I read in a monograph that some sages believe the Fiendish Charm requires the Cambion to see their enemy. It is possible magical darkness, or blindness or invisibility could provide sufficient protection.” 
Orsik asks, “So, Professor,” 
“I am not a professor; I am a traveling scholar.” 
“Sorry, Mr. Scholar, sir, I understand that Cambions can disguise themselves as humans using magic?” 
“Does Dispel magic work?” 
“Yes, but the problem is that so many people use magical illusions to disguise their appearance that casting the magical spell might not have the intended effect, and some consider it a grave breach of etiquette. It’s not unheard of for there to be duels!” 
Vardis is now curious about what Orsik’s next move will be. 
“Is there any movement to ban magic?” 
At that, Sage Vusk laughs, “In Silverymoon? The city that was ruled by the chosen of Mystara, the goddess of magic? The city that invented Magical Schools? No.” 

Vardis becomes frustrated and sighs. 
Thistle taps him and Orisk on the shoulder, and they walk outside, “we could tell Bob Saget to create a groundswell against magic as a hidden theme in his play?” 
Vardis likes the idea, as does Orsik. 

## Orsik

Late in the evening, an elf wearing a purple robe and quite obviously carrying a symbol of the Harpers approaches. 
“Krowen Valharrow has asked me if you ever plan to deal with the problem of the Giants. We have noticed that you went to the Oracle and discovered critical information to ending the war, but instead of ending the war, Silverymoon is doing goodness knows what.” 
“This feels a little bit like micromanagement.”
The Harper agent betrays a flash of anger, “A Frost Giant practically sacked Bryn Shander, and Hill Giants attacked Goldenfields. While you sit here and plot your revenge, people are dying, and the Giants continue to assemble magical devices to prove their worthiness to Amn. And Krowen wants to know if you plan to do anything?” 
“Absolutely.” 
“Perhaps I was unclear; there was access to some magical pathways given to you. If you don’t start moving today, you will lose access.” 
“We have other ways to move around.” 
“Yes, and you have chosen to ally yourselves with cultists.” 
Thistle bristles, “former.” 
Orsik sees Vardis looking at him and nods; he’s dealt with managers who think they can manage him – “So this is a performance improvement plan? I know a lot about them, and they are a farce. Every single one I was put on led to me getting fired. So I don’t do them.” 
The Harper smiles, “Oh no, we aren’t going to put you on a performance improvement plan. You are a contract. We wouldn’t renew. But Krowen still sees value and thinks if we made an offer of treasure maps. Or, as Krowen said – stop this random questing and go save the North.” 
Orsik looks at him and says, “You should take those maps and dig that treasure.”
“What?” 
“We’re done.”
The elf grabs the maps and glares at Orsik, “This idea of using contractors instead of full agents is because of Ezra. There are those of us who never thought that was a good idea. Mercenaries don’t do things because it’s right; they do it because they have to.” 
Orsik carefully controls his rage and turns to the 3HPP, “So, are we done?”
Unla Kee sips from a glass, “done.” The other two nod their agreement to Orsik. 
The elf picks up the maps and leaves.
Orsik turns to his friends, “I know what we do next.”.  

The next day, the group splits up. 
Thistle uses the time to find a scholar to research Adminod Kroskas and finds Rebecca Deakin, an archivist who works for Wyrmworks in their cataloging department. Her intellect is beyond doubt, and her mastery of all dragons-related topics is unparalleled. And she happens to have some free time. She agrees to look into the question for a modest fee of 1800 gold pieces. 
“Come back in a week.” 
Orsik uses his time to talk to the local Zhentarim agent, Uzorel “The Viper” Hornbalde. The Viper is, in fact, distantly related to Taern Hornblade, the former ruler of Silverymoon, and is reluctant to meet Orsik until he discovers that it’s the Orsik of Parnast and Triboar fame. Uzorel is a relatively diminutive figure with lanky limbs, pale skin, and a sharp, angular goatee. He makes up for what he lacks in physical presence with tact and charm.
Orsik then betrays the location of the circles he knows, the one in Everlund and the one in Yartar. 
“There’s no profit in that information for you? You give it?” 
“I am just sharing with friends.” 
The Viper reaches for his sword, “Friends?” 
Realizing that The Viper is paranoid, he reassures the Black Agent, “I had a beef with a high-ranking Harper. And as far as I am concerned, this gets back at him where it hurts.” 
“Revenge, I get that.”
“But no reward?”
“I just want the higher-ups to know I am one of them.” 
The Viper releases his grip on his sword in its sheathe and smiles toothily, “They will. And they will be pleased.” 
When Orsik tells everyone what happened, they realize that Yartar and Everlund may become too hot for them to visit. Thistle begins to see a pattern emerge and understands why Orsik is treated the way he is across the realms – “he gets angry when someone doesn’t give him the respect he thinks he deserves, and then lashes out by leaving.” 
Later that evening, the group has left Silverymoon and is talking to the crew on the Great Airship, a gift from the ancient red dragon Klauth. 
As they fly above the Evermoors, west of Silverymoon, Delphine asks – “where to?” 
Thistle shouts, “the Grandfather tree.” Everyone looks at him, “why?” 

“Because we need to find information that will lead us to a giant so we can get the conch and get to King Hekaton. And apparently, the only way we can do that is if we collect these artifacts. And because I have information that my beloved is with the Fire Giant Duke, we need to collect several because the Oracle didn’t specify which artifact would get that information. And the Grandfather Tree is right here.” 
“Touchy,” think Vardis and Orsik. 
As they fly towards the Grandfather Tree, Brazzik, one of the crew members, approaches the 3HPP and says, “I am a bit of a loresmith and know quite a bit about the grandfather tree and would be happy to share it, for a small fee.” 
Thistle looks at Brazzik, thinks for a moment, and then says, “I do know that it’s a big tree. And I do know about the Uthgardt Tree Ghost Tribe that treats it as sacred ground.” 
At that, Thistle laughs as he remembers something else. “There was a pair of Seelie Dryads, Halisa, and Helia, who misspoke at a ball, offended the Queen, and were punished for protecting this tree for some indeterminately long period.” 
Orsik and Vardis look at each other and give Brazzik 50 gold, hoping for better information. 

Brazzik takes the coin – “Your friend was accurate, but his information was incomplete. If you kill the Dryads, there are tales of powerful and dangerous consequences. Furthermore, there are patrols of the tribe that sometimes come through here. So you need to be careful.”
Thistle laughs, “great! Fey is coming on a pilgrimage with a cleric of light!” 
Orsik adds, “With a neutral evil wizard and a wanted criminal!” 
Vardis turns to him, irritated, “Aren’t you supposed to be disguised?” 
His dwarf friend responds, “We’re all friends here?” 
Everyone nods. 
At that point, Vardis realizes that the Cultists have become friendlier since Orsik hurt the Harpers, something he could not resist bragging about. For these ex-cultists, it’s a little payback for the events of the Cult of the Dragon and the role of the Harper Remalia Haventree in organizing the North against Tiamat. 
Orsik, seeing the approval, goes back to his annoyance of the Harpers, “Totally trying to micromanage us.” And then sarcastically says, “It’s as if the whole point of being part of a powerful organization is so that you can mindlessly do whatever your superiors say.” 
Vardis looks at him and mutters, “Don’t remind me of the work I quit.” 
One of the cultists, who looks elven, stares at him, “Is that why you were kicked out of Lorien’s cult?” 
Brazzik interrupts the elf, adding, “The other thing to be aware of is that these dryads have allied with various creatures around the forests to ensure the trees protection.”
