# Chapter 49 - Killing a dragon
## Vardis 
“Well that was awkward.” 
His master plan to obliterate the army of kobolds that was surely hiding in the chamber, foiled by the fact that only a single kobold was hiding in the chamber.
Worse, his arrogance had caused him to fall into a pit. 
After clambering out of the pit, he picked over the charred remains of the kobold. A shiny idol of Tiamat piqued his interest. 
Outside, his friend Orsik shouts, “hey, let’s go down this passageway.” 
Vardis groans. That would mean squeezing for him. 
As they walk through the twisty passage, Orsik keeps pointing out the shoddy workmanship and how Dwarves do a better job, and “No Dwarf would ever make a mess of things this way.” 
Vardis tries hard to stifle a yawn. 
They reach an intersection, and Orsik shouts, “right hand rule.” And so they follow that simple rule and re-emerge into the same large area that they had left, just some 20 or so feet further down.
“What a waste of time.” 
 He hears something. A very specific sound that is unmistakable. And then he remembers something that Orsik had said and Thistle had repeated, that the Netherese Mountain Range was home to blue dragons. 
“That’s a blue dragon,” mutters Thistle. 
Vardis nods and tries to remember why he is here, “How did I get here?” And he remembers. When they had first arrived in Everlund, they stumbled across this old lady called Mary who was shouting – “*one day, that monster, the Kraken, will return to destroy our city with its powerful tentacle and mind control! The Kraken will come with his allies; we need to prepare because they are more powerful than anything we have seen before!” *She was head of an organization called the “Fighters of the Light” and it had a 5 GP membership fee. “We were broke,” he remembers. And then thinks, “I am about to fight a blue dragon to save 5 Gold.” 
“Seriously, saving 5 gold?” 
Orsik turns to him, “there are three things of value in these mountains, granite, dragon hoards and Netherese Artifacts. The first is the most valuable, but the last the most powerful magic items in the realms. If there is a chance.” 
Vardis nods, “So what's the plan.” 
Orsik and him start to sketch out a plan, when Thistle shouts – “Pro or anti-Tiamat?” 
The dragon roars. 
Thistle reaches for his bow, “you’re dead.” 
Vardis whispers, “what did he say?”
“Tiamat for-ever!” mutters Thistle. 
“I guess we are doing this.” 
“Yes, we are,” says Orsik as he gets ready to attack. 
## The Battle
Thistle flies off, tells Thuul to advance on the dragon, fires two shots that hit. The dragon roars in pain, just in time to get stung with a squirt of acid from the guard drake. 
Orsik dashes to the dragons, slashes at it four times causing the creature to bleed while sheathing the creature in booming energy. 
Before Vardis can act, two kobold seemingly blink into existence and attack. 
Seeing his situation as precarious, the dragon decides to disengage and flies from the eastern most part of this enormous cavern, 80 feet to the middle of the cavern, just before it turns south. 
Unla Kee is pleased, the dragon is just inside of Fireball range. And as he reaches for the spell components, Orsik shouts, “hey dude, you cast all of your third and fourth level spells.” 
“Huh?” 
“Yeah.” 
“Well what am I supposed to do with these first and second level spells?” 
“Kill Kobold.” 
Unla Kee visibly pouts and then casts sleep. 
Thistle orders Thuul forward, and then fires two arrows at the follower of Tiamat. Unfortunately his arrows miss. 
Vardis shouts, “it’s always the light cleric.” The fireball hits, but it’s a glancing blow. The dragon dodges most of the damage. 
Orsik, meanwhile, has ran towards the dragon, and although more than 30 feet away, throws his hand axes and hits once. 
The dragon howls and then breathes lightning on Orsik. The bolt courses through his body as it reduces his strength in half and then the dragon flies to the west, outside of their field of vision.  
Unla Kee and Vardis, meanwhile, are finding themselves surround by kobolds that keep blinking into existence. The wizard shouts, “good thing it’s just kobolds” as he unleashes Rime’s binding ice, killing four of the materialized creatures. 
Thistle flies towards the dragon and can’t see him. Rather than dash into combat, he decides to find a nook to hide in and prepare to fire an arrow if the dragon appears in his field of view. 
Orsik, seeing that he can’t reach the dragon, spots a corridor the group ignored before that should drop him behind the dragon. 
The dragon seeing that everyone is sneaking around, decides to investigate his surroundings and spots Thistle. He tries to move into position, but Thistle sees him and fires an arrow. Orsik holds his breath and exhales when he see that it misses. The dragon then breathes on Thistle, but this fairy has faced a lot of dragons and knows what to do. 
Meanwhile at the other side of the cavern… 
Unla Kee shouts, “running out of 2nd level spells.” 
Vardis shouts back, “magic missile?” 
And then three kobolds are dead.
And another light blinks and a kobold materializes and critically misses. 
And near the dragon, Thistle moves closer to where the dragon was and hides. 
Orsik meanwhile emerges from the tunnel that he had crawled into and hits the dragon with a Firebolt. Thistle stares at him, shocked. Orsik had a perfectly good bow that he could have used to hit the Dragon twice. But no, Orsik wanted to use a spell. Thistle shakes his head in disbelief. 
The dragon bloodied and bruised shouts in common, “do you think we could reach an accommodation?” 
Orsik growls, “No.” 
“I just felt I should ask.” 
“What a polite dragon.” 
“But still no?” 
“Yes.” 
Vardis overhears this and mutters, “he came out charging at us.” 
Unla Kee corrects him, “no, what happened is that Thistle asked him about Tiamat, and the Dragon choose poorly and Thistle attacked.” 
Vardis nods as he misses an attack on a kobold with his sword, “right.” 
The Dragon meanwhile flies south into a cave that has a 60 foot steep high climb and hides deep in the cave. 
Orsik knows that this is the last stand for the Dragon. There’s no where else for the dragon to run, so he charges up the hill and is just five feet from the top. 
Thistle who had hidden, finds himself too far away to move into position to hit the dragon. He chooses to fly closer and stay hidden. 
Unla Kee and Vardis, meanwhile, are still fighting kobolds. One of the dog like creatures charges at Unla Kee and misses. Another strikes Vardis and misses. Another snores. 
Unla Kee has had enough, and decides to run towards the dragon. 
Vardis kills a kobold that was about to strike Unla Kee with his bow. 
The Dragon laughs, and then strikes Orsik with a lightning bolt. Orsik dodges the bulk of the blast and is still alive. 
The Dragon stops laughing. He’s holding on by a thread with only 6 HP. Orsik is hanging by another thread of 8 HP. 
Thistle is very happy, he knows the Dragon is dead. But perhaps Thistle was counting his chickens before they hatched, because he misses twice. 
Orsik glares at the fairy, “twice?” 
Vardis who has been keeping an eye on the combat, mutters, “three times?” 
The dwarf was planning to use his spell, but decides that perhaps this is not the time. He rushes up the last five feet, charges at the dragon, and swings his sword at the creature, killing it. 
“Hallelujah!” 
Orsik is breathing heavily, Thistle is firing a few more arrows into the body while the cleric and magic user are racing towards them. 
Unla Kee finally arrives next to the dragon’s corpse, and smiles. “Time to extract the most valuable body parts.” 
Thistle and Orsik, meanwhile, head back to the dragon’s hoarde which is almost 300 feet away from their current location. There they find 2,000 gp, fourteen gems worth 250 gp each, a *potion of mind reading*, a *bag of beans*, and a *potion of superior healing*
They also find a peculiar gem in the shape of a star. 
By the time they arrive back at the dragon’s corpse, the bloodied Unla Kee is admiring his carving – “Let’s see, some dragon meat, dragon blood for potions of dragon strength, dragon teeth that can be turned into dragon arrows for our favorite fey, dragon bone that can be turned into dragon weapons and a dragon horn that we can use to craft a dragon wand.” 

Unla Kee shouts, “well shall we continue?” 
Orsik looks at the bloody mess and the fact that he barely survived, and that the spell casters are out of spells and announces, “nah, we got what we came for.”
