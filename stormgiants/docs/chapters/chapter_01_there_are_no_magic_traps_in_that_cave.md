# Chapter 01 - There are no magic traps in that cave.

*The War of the Cult of the Dragon, Excerpt from the Great Sage Konstantinious the 12th*
The defeat of Tiamat at the Well of the World was a victory for the people of the North. The Cult of the Dragon had achieved what no one thought possible unity of all factions in the face of extinction. 
And yet, the unity was short-lived. 
The divisions that kept the Harpers, Zhentarim, Lord’s Alliance, Order of the Gauntlet, and Emerald Enclave from working together reared their ugly heads. Their distinct visions for the future were at odds with each other. The common ground with no extinction-level event to focus the mind evaporated as quickly as the portal summoning the Queen of Dragons.
As for the people of the North, rebuilding was the word of the day. Many towns had been destroyed or plundered to create the hoard to entice Tiamat. And the villagers were waiting to see how much of the recovered treasure would make it back to their towns. 
Not all was bad. The evil dragons had been soundly defeated and were no longer an existential threat to the realms. And the armies of the North were so battered and bruised that war seemed unlikely. And the Cult of the Dragon was destroyed. Their mad project of creating a new undead Dragon race, the dracolich, was over. 
In short, the realms were at peace. 
Rumors persisted, however, that there was some treasure that the Cult Leaders had hidden away and not handed to Tiamat. Some Cultists had a plan B, just in case their plots for Armaggedon failed or perhaps even succeeded. Or maybe it was just plain avarice. 
According to this sage, most of these maps were primarily cons. An opportunity to make some coin from some foolish adventurer, down-on-their-luck soldier, lost soul, or an unlucky fool. Some maps lead nowhere. Some towns would use the story of a map to deal with enemies encroaching on their territories. “Oh, those goblins were allied with the Dragon and have treasure!” was a common way to get the naïve to eliminate tribes that were in the wrong place at the wrong time. There was always treasure, but was it related to the Cult? Who knows.

## Orsik and Vardis

Orsik and Vardis had run out of garrisons, armies, city guards, and factions that wanted to hire them. If what they had heard at Secomber, Zelbross, Loudwater, Llorkh, and Parnast was true, their preferred employment choice was no longer an option. 
No one wanted to hire them.
And Parnast was, literally, the end of the road. And so when they heard that a treasure hunter, Veradaa Storr, had a map of Cult gold, they saw a new employment opportunity. Veradaa would need someone to help her guard her treasure, and she would be willing to pay for it.
But there was a deeper reason, the two of them had a treasure map, and perhaps Veradaa would be someone who could reveal a clue about that map. 
But they just had to get to her first before anyone else got the job.  
And that’s how they ended up in a clearing along the Black Road staring at her dead body. 
## Thistle Wendrod

“Being a fairy is hard work,” thought Thistle. “And it’s tough to work when you are looking for someone.” 
And sometimes, it was hard to stay motivated, but he had to find her. And he had three things going for him; the first was his unshakeable conviction that he would find her. Two, he had the stories his uncle had told him about the world. And three, he had a burning desire to get vengeance on the Cult that had put him in prison for a long time. Fairy’s hate enclosed spaces. 
The story of Cult treasure always piqued his interest. After all, what was more valuable than his friend? 
Most of the stories had gone nowhere, but he knew a guy, who happened to be a Harper, that had told him that Veradaa was the real deal. 
And that’s how he ended up near Parnast staring at the very odd pair of a dwarf and an elf, looking like they had seen better days, standing over a dead body. 
## Unla Key

“Luck is what other people call my intuition,” was how he liked to think about the world. He had this peculiar talent to be in the right place at the right time, most of the time. And hearing about Veradaa’s map was a stroke of luck. 
He knew this was the real thing. After all, weren’t his instincts always right? 
And so when he heard a rumor about Cult gold, and those around him dismissed it, he quickly set out to find this treasure hunter and offer his services. 
When he arrived at the appointed location, he saw her dead body. And he was shocked that there were three other people there. 

“Would they all come to blows?” he thought.
He was relieved when the other three introduced themselves and said they had come to work for Veradaa as an assistant. 
“They were all obviously trying to scam the poor woman,” he thought. 
After the introductions were over, Unla Key joined the others to look through her belongings. 
It was then that the one called Vardis said, “our travels were uneventful, but there were rumors of Giant activity – rune-covered boulders, huge footprints, and missing livestock.
Unla Key dismissed this as just talk, “Giants this far south is unheard of.” 
## Thistle Wendrod 

“Found it!” he shouts. And presents a set of notes to the group. 

***The First Cache. ****The first treasure cache was hidden by the Cult of the Dragon in an abandoned cave at the base at of the southernmost mountain in the Greypeak range. While it was thought to be the smallest of the caches, it is also not thought to be protected by any traps or magic. The caves and tunnels of the complex, according to my sources, are not very structurally sound. Goblins are known to inhabit the area.
****The Second Cache. ****My sources confirmed that one cache was buried beneath a unique structure: a pyramid of stone resting on a flat field of stone in the middle section of the Greypeaks, directly between the two highest mountains of that section of the range. The area was once rent with volcanic activity, making the flat stone field difficult to navigate. My sources could not tell me who or what built the boulder pyramid.
****The Third Cache. ****The source I questioned about this cache said that it was carefully hidden behind a secret entrance in the wall of a green-sided mountain. There is only one of those in the Greypeak range, according to my research. The source also said that the cultist who hid the treasure was an expert in magical curses.
****The Fourth Cache. ****This cache was secured in an abandoned dwarven outpost used for treating ore that was being prepared for use in weapon-smithing. My sources implied that the place had been overrun by something terrible and immortal, causing the dwarves to leave it centuries ago. Subterranean volcanic activity has been reported in the area regularly over the years.
****The Fifth Cache. ****At the top of the tallest summit of the Greypeaks, one contact told me that there is an abandoned shrine once revered by giants. Dragons used the area as a perch since then, and a cache is going to be hidden there. Someone also told me that occasionally, when the wind is blowing in just the right direction, a pealing of loud bell can be heard from there.*
All at once, debate starts. Thistle notices that the dwarf and the elf talk hushed tones about a map. The one known as Unla Key seems lost in his head. As for Thistle, he tries to remember any story his uncle told him but can’t.
Thistle tries to get people to listen to him. “The group is still storming,” he reflects, “not yet norming or performing.”
Eventually, consensus emerges, and Thistle happily heads towards the first cache because everyone agrees it must be the safest because there are no magical traps. 
## Sergeant Gronk

“This mission has turned into a disaster. We came with two giants, one died in a cave-in, and the other is a tiny little creature, and to top it all off, half our soldiers are little toys.” 
He shouts to the other goblins, “all right, we need to guard this cave and not let anything pass until whatever magical curses we triggered end.” 
Just then, he hears a group heading towards the cave entrance debating about whether they should enter and how they should enter. He smiles; he’s got them where he wants them. Setting up his troops to take advantage of partial cover, he waits for the fools to enter. 
The cavern he picked to make his last stand isn’t too bad of a defensive position. There’s a tunnel to the south he could flee into if things get really ugly, and there’s a lot of cover to hide behind. As he sits and waits, the dust in the air tickles his nose, “can’t cough, he thinks.” He then looks at the Giant recently buried in a cave-in on the east tunnel that produced all of the dust. 
At first, the battle goes his way; two of the invaders are dead. The pompous dwarf is slaughtered. And the flying creature is put down. And then disaster, a magic-user casts thunderwave, killing two of his goblins. 
“Damn it,” he thinks, “will that giant ever recover?”
And that’s his last thoughts before he dies. 
## Unla Key

Unla Key did not die. “I am knocked down, but I get up again,” he thinks. 
And now he’s looking at the Fairy, who is flitting away down the southern hallway when he hears scurrying and a high-pitched squeaking echo.
He expects to see rats but instead sees a swarm of goblin-sized mice charging at him. Their tiny scimitars and shields seem almost comical at first until he realizes how many of them there are.
Just then, the lights go out as they swarm all over him. 
As the goblins in the swarm are killed, they grow back to normal size, and pretty soon, the entire area in front of the southern cave is almost impassable with dead goblin bodies. 
And that clues Unla Key and the group that whatever is cursing these creatures is not permanent, and it might be wise for them to advance. 
## Orsik and Vardis

The two anti-heroes follow the Fairy from a safe distance, “never be too close to the front or the rear” is their motto. 
And they see over Thistle’s head another cavern with lanterns. Their eyes are drawn to a small creature dressed in ratty furs wielding a club standing in front of a stone chest that is even with his eyes. The thing is grumbling and fuming about something. 
As Thistle enters the cave proper, a yappy growl alerts the creature. That’s when Orsik and Vardis recognize what it is, a tiny giant. They try hard not to laugh.
The tiny Giant screams, “Slayermighty the Giant will tear you apart and feed you to Blooddrinker! I’ll be ruling all of Giant-kind soon!”
But the Giant cannot lay a hand on the group, and Orsik smiles when Slayermight dies. He turns to Vardis and says, “Didn’t even get a chance to swing its club!” 
At that point, they both notice that the fearsome bear, Blooddrinker, turns on his belly expecting to be rubbed. 
## Thistle Wendrod 

As Vardis and Orsik open the chest and look for the treasure, Thistle plays with the tiny bear. And just then, it starts to convulse. Thanks to some quick thinking, he saves the creature. The bear quickly bonds to him. Blooddrinker is the size of a cat. And is adorably ferocious. 
As Thistle plays with his new friend, he hears Orsik groan, “Another giant came here before us and left a note - 

*“Stupid runt-giants of the hills,
*
*You are too slow and dim-witted to challenge us. The treasure is ours. You are probably feeling small now, aren’t you? If you think you are going to prevail, you are mistaken. By the way, prevail means win, idiots.*
*
Clan True-Nimbus of the Clouds”*

Thistle is disappointed but is pleased to hear that it’s not a complete loss. Slayermighty had two vials of alchemist fire, a potion of healing, and a necklace of 20 copper charms.
