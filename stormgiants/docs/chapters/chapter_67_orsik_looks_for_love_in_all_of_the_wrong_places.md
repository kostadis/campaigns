# Chapter 67 Orsik looks for love in all of the wrong places. 

Having explored the rooms to the north, the 3HPP decides to go upstairs. 

Orsik, Vardis, Thistle, and Unla Kee emerge from the 40-foot-wide hall. Their careful footsteps ensure that no sound is made as they walk on the polished stone floor. As they round the corner, the sight that greets them is breathtaking.

The great hall stretches before them, a cathedral of hewn rock bathed in the ethereal glow of a colossal crystal window. The ceiling soars high above, vaulted and adorned with intricate carvings that dance in the light. Pillars, thick as ancient trees, rose from the floor, their surfaces encrusted with barnacles and draped in vibrant coral.

Looking through the window, they realize that Orsik was right; they are deep under water. Unla Kee whispers, “How does light get to this deep a place?” Vardis rolls his eyes, “Magic.” 

Two figures stood bathed in the spotlight on a raised platform to the north. One, tall and statuesque, her voice soaring with the power of a storm, is Mirran, the eldest daughter of King Hekaton. The other, Nym, her sister, moves with practiced grace. Her whalebone mallets tapa rhythmic melody on the shell of a hulking crab, its body a living instrument that pulses with each note.

Below them, a gathering of giants mingled, their forms casting long shadows in the flickering light. A cloud giant, his beard a wispy cloud, stood in animated conversation with a fire giant, her eyes burning with an inner fire. His breath frosted the air, and a frost giant shared a laugh with a stone giant, his skin as rough as granite.

Orsik stares for a moment and boldly walks in. His footsteps echo over the music. He coughs. The music stops. Everyone stares at him. 

He announces, “I am looking for Serissa. I joined this “Dwarf and Giants that Love Each Other” matchmaking service, and when we were matched, I was told to come here. It’s been a bit of a journey, and I wondered where she was.” 

There is silence. 

And then Mirran laughs and says, “My goodness, I am sure Queen Serissa will be delighted that love came through. But she’s not ready to meet anyone right now, and indeed, not a manly dwarf like you. She wasn’t expecting you! Now, she has important activities of state to manage, and I know our sister will want to look her best for her new beau.” 

She turns to Nym, “Nym, when do you think she will be free?” 

“In a few hours?” whispers the other sister. 

Orsik looks around and realizes that they are overmatched. He turns to Unla Kee - “You got anything?” The halfling surprisingly shakes his head. 

“Well, I guess we’ll wait. Has she said anything about me?” 

Nym smiles evilly, “She was like our mother, always interested in the little people. We knew she had a fascination, but not this. Just like her mother.” 

Orsik groans, “Casual racism.” 

Vardis corrects him, “It’s fetishism.” 

At that, Nym can’t shut up, “It is not a fetish. It is racism. Giants are superior to little people.” 

Orsik, playing the role of a man who shows up to pick his girlfriend up only to discover the family disapproves, grabs Vardis and mutters, “We’ll go downstairs and wait for the queen.” 

The two storm giant guards march them downstairs to the easternmost hallway, where guestrooms for the little people exist. 

They each settled in for the night. 

Just then, Mirran arrives in the doorway.

Vardis turns to her, “Who designed this, midgets?” 

“It was my mother. She had a thing for your kind.” Mirran sounds as sweet as possible. The 3HPP suspects everything.  

“She’s waiting for you! Come, come!” 

Vardis whispers to Orsik, “How big of a trap is this?” 

“We’re going to find out.” 

They walk towards the large cavernous room with the pool and teleportation circle. With a wave of her hand, it becomes brightly lit. And there are four giants, the same giants that were upstairs. 

She turns to them as she rushes past them, “Show your loyalty to your queen, and whoever might become queen, by getting rid of these unwanted guests that would claim that a Storm Giant would want to mate with a,” and she pauses as her face clearly shows her disgust, “dwarf.” 

Unla Kee is confused, “Are they the unwanted guests?” 

The four giants laugh and advance. 

Thistle mutters, “I think we are.” 

Orsik shouts, “The door to the guest room is too big for them to march in; let’s go in there and fight from there.”

Vardis nods and starts to run back. Thistle and Unla Kee pause and then follow him. 

Unla Kee turns to Orsik, “So we doing the bag of holding trick?” 

Orsik looks at him and says, “No.” 

As Mirren rushes past the advancing giants, she shouts in Giant, “They are assassins. Kill them all. Show your loyalty.” 

Orsik turns to the rest of the crew, “We have been accused of worse.” 

They all nod. 

Thistle, who has experience in court intrigues, reflects on what is happening. “The situation reminds me of the time that Queen Titania made a fool of that formorian,” he thinks. That’s when it clicked, and just in time, Orsik was getting ready to make a cardinal sin, “Don’t kill the sister.” 

“Why?”

“Because if we kill her, then the Queen will believe the other sister that we are assassins.” 

Orsik looks at him, “Damn, that’s cold.” 

Unla Kee shouts, “So, everyone but the Storm Giant?” 

Sizzling with energy, a bolt of lightning leaps from his hand, racing towards the fire giant. But why stop at just the one? Upon contact, it splits, and three new currents of electricity ricochet off to other giants.

The giants laugh. And then the fire giant bellows, “Well played, little one. But these paltry tricks of yours are not enough to stop four lords of the giants. I am Duchess Tartha, Count Vaal, and to my right is the Jarl Hellenhild, and the quiet one is Thane Braxow. You have come to kill the Queen, and we will do our duty and kill you.” 

Unla Kee runs towards the room's safety but still hears the tail end of the exposition. 

Orsik touches the giant slayer blade Lord Zymorven granted him, whispering, “Well, this is what I saved his son for.” But first, he thinks, “Some magic.” 

He takes a moment to say a few short syllables quickly, then shudders with a chill as the magical power of Expeditious Retreat raises the hair on his neck and arms. He is unsure if the world is a little slower or he is just faster—or both—but it seems each step he takes can carry him farther than before.

With that, he rushes towards the Stone Giant Thane and swings the sword twice, striking him both times.  The giant is unaffected by the dwarf’s magic sword, other than the bleeding. Orsik growls, focusing his energy on attacking and striking twice more. This time, the Giant succumbs to the sword's power and falls prone. And with him prone, Orsik strikes one more time and kills him. 

Duchess Tartha laughs, “So this will be a challenge. Perhaps the Queen did see something in you. But I can assure you you will not see her in this life.” 

She prepares to strike him and is stunned when he moves out of her reach. 

Mirren, who was near him, took a swipe as he ran but missed. 

Vardis casts a wall of fire across the hallway entrance, separating him and the rest of the 3HPP from the giants. 

Count Vaal clicks his tongue, and his feet break free of the bonds of gravity and rocket into the air, unbound by such petty things as natural laws.

Orsik grumbles, “Flying? Can cloud giants fly? I should have known that.” 

Count Vaal mocks him, “Yes, little one, you should have.” 

The Duchess stares at the fire, marches right through unaffected, and hits Thistle with a slash of her giant sword. 

As she does, she also walks by Orsik, who strikes her. 

As arcane syllables tumble from Thistle’s mouth, jolts of electricity crackle about his weapon, warping it into an actinic new form. The air itself seems to spark with anticipation, charged with the raw energy of a storm. It is no mundane projectile, instead a manifestation of the tempest. It is chained destruction, ready to fly.

And fly it does, exploding on Tartha. 

Then, before she can recover, another of his arrows strikes her. 

And then Thuul opens his mouth, and a rush of ice like that of an ice dragon comes out of his mouth and hits the Fire Giant. 

Vardis, Orsi, K, and Unla Kee turn to the Ranger. “Thuul can do that?” 

Thistle shrugs. 

Unla Kee remembers how Thistle when fighting at the climactic battle of Parnast, chose not to use his bow as efficiently as he could. Unla Kee thought that Thistle had a tendency to create challenges that he could overcome instead of attacking with all of his abilities at once. 

But then the mage thinks some more, “No, he’s hiding his abilities so that if and when one of us decides to betray him, we are surprised by what he can do.” Thus, Unla Kee’s estimation of Thistle grows.

As a parting gift before she leaves, Merrin hurls a lightning bolt that lands on Vardis and strikes Unla Kee as well, as the electric charge spreads around Vardis. The wizard and cleric are hurt, but not too severely. 

The Jarl stares at the wall of fire. She can’t run into it and worries that Firegiant Duchess will steal the glory. But then she notices Thistle flying above the wall. And throws a rock. The boulder misses the target by a lot. 

Vaal stares at her, mouth agape, and Tartha taunts her. Hellenhild mutters, “I was trying some new powder for a better grip.” 

Unla Kee, dazed by the lightning, pokes his head out and sends a mote of fire from his hands streaking past Tartha to a point equidistant between Count Vaal and Duchess Tartha. The mote leaves harmless sparkles in its wake. But when it arrives at its final destination, it explodes with fury. 

Tartha was just in range, but the fire didn’t affect her; instead, it seemed to invigorate her as for Vaal and Hellenhild, the fire sears. Hellenhild is beginning to look worse. 

Orsik maneuvers to strike Tartha, but as he hits her, he remembers that his flaming sword is less effective against the Fire Giant. The giantess resists the sword's magic from the first strike, but the second strike leaves her prone. Orsik moves away from her. 

Vardis is about to cast a guiding bolt when Unla Kee giggles. 

“Anything you would like to share?”
