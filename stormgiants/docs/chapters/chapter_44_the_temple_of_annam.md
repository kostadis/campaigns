# Chapter 44 - The Temple of Annam
The journey from the Golden Dragon’s cave to the causeway that leads to the Temple of the All-Father, is blessedly uninterrupted and takes two days

## 3rd day of the 1st tenday of Eleasis 1492
Thistle notices fresh human tracks. He asks Harshnag, but the Frost Giant is confused. 
The causeway ends before a stone dome carved from the mountainside. Six 30-foot-tall stone pillars support the dome. 
Orsik declares, “there is no danger of collapse of this dome, even if we were to destroy the pillars. 
Vardis stares at him. 
“I know stuff.” 
“Apparently.”
Each pillar is carved with a wraparound bas-relief. Harshnag points to each one North to South and explains what they are seeing. 
“This is a hill giant lifting a rock above it’s head. You can tell from the small size of the rock and the large belly.” 
“The next one is of a proud and honerable and great Frost Giant cutting down a pine.” 
“Birds in the sky, and the bird brained Cloud Giants are flying with them.” 
“I tell you that the Storm Giants are deadly. Here you can see one tossing lightning at a ship.” 
“Well that’s interesting, a stone giant climbing down a mountain. They don’t like to be on the surface.” 
And then he smirks and mutters, “See Orsik, the Fire Giants are a plague on all of us. They could choose anything and they choose to represent themselves as slavers with dwarves chained against the wall.” 

And with that the 3HPP advance forward. Unla Kee is in a rush and dashes in when he nears noises. Thistle tries to urge caution because of the possibility of traps, but the diviner has other ideas.
Above Unla Kee’s head, Thistle notices massive stone blocks that are held in place by some kind of contraption over the hallway. It’s unclear what the contraption is or how to disarm it. Before Thistle can tell Unla Kee to stop, the halfling has run into the main hallway past the two massive blocks. 
And then just before the stairway, the group is ambushed by Uthgardtian barbarians and their pet white dragon. The barbarians attack from the ledges with their bows and hide in the alcoves. However, eventually the battle tied turns. And in particular when Harshnag enters the battle furious at the idea of a dragon in this holy place. 
And then the battle is over, except for the Shaman who flees into the temple. 
Harshnag stands over the corpses of the Uthgardtian defilers, turning to Orsik, “These are members of the Great Worm Tribe. They found this Temple and came to plunder it. They were foolish.” 
He marches up to the door at the end of the hallway and says as he pushes open the doors with a giant heave, “Only a giant could open the door to the Temple of Annam.” 
Vardis looks around, “so this is some kind of ante-chamber?” 
“No, this is an impenetrable fortress.”
“One that these,” Orsik points to the corpses, “tribesman penetrated.” 
Harshnag explodes. “Antechamber? Penetrated? Bah!” He points to the ledges, “You see those ledges if there were giants here, they would toss rocks, not arrows! And those blocks would have crushed you or trapped you!”
And then he goes on for another 10 to 15 minutes explaining how this is a monument to the tremendous giant civilization of Osteria, “40,000 years ago, magic-wielding Giants built this.” 
Vardis turns to him, “Wait a sec, the Toril is only 5,000 years old!” 
Harshnag rolls his eye, “One of those cranks,” and he makes the Giant gesture for Loon, “A creationist!” 
As they are about to enter the Temple, Vardis picks up an amulet from the chieftain and hangs it around his neck underneath his armor. 
And with that, the 3HPP walk into the Temple of Annam. 
Vardis stares at the statue and notices that the face of the All-Father is blank. Harshnag also sees that and says, “The sculptors did not know what he looks like and chose to not offend.” 
Unla Kee rushes to the far eastern edge of the room, where a giant portal stands. The archway is 40 feet wide and 40 feet tall. Six runes are carved into it, each inlaid with mithral, and a glowing mist fills the arch. Niches in the walls on either side of it contain empty, ice-covered sconces made of iron.

He runs through the mist and collides with a wall.
“Nothing to see behind here.” 
Harshnag glares at him and then shouts, “Look at the runes. We need to do something with the runes.” 
Orsik turns to Unla Kee – “You’re a diviner; surely you have some divination spell?”
The blank expression convinces him that Unla Kee does not. Unla Kee explains, “I know the future; what else is there to divine?”
Orsik stares at the runes on the wall. 
“From left to right, they are hill, cloud, fire, storm, stone, and frost.” 
Orsik turns to Harshnag, “Didn’t you recognize that one?”
The Grim stares at it and says, “Ah yes, that’s an old version of the rune. The current one is much simpler.” 
“I guess it’s been 40,000 years.” 
“Yes, that is 10 generations of giants.” 
The 3HPP stares at the runes and then glares at Unla Kee, who doesn’t have a single divination spell memorized or even in his spell book. 
Harshnag stares at the runes and has an idea, “That’s interesting; all of the giants have weapons, and.”
Orsik tells him to shush, “Let’s see if we can manipulate the runes.”
Thistle looks at him, “should we start with the Storm Giant one?”
“It does match the ordning.” 
And so the fairy flies up and touches the storm rune. 
After he touches it, a storm cloud forms over the center and spreads over the whole room. 
There is a deafening roar of thunder. 
Thistle shouts, “What do we do next?” 
“Try touching it again? Maybe that will turn it off!” 
Thistle nods, touches, and nothing happens. 
Then acid rain begins to fall. 
Vardis shouts, “Let’s do the bottom of the Ordning hierarchy?” 
Orsik, who remembers what Harshnag said, notices that the giant that looks like a paragon of a Frost Giant has no weapon and marches up to the Frost rune and touches it. 
A magical ice storm is triggered, hitting the entire group.
Unla Kee is knocked unconscious. 
Harshnag stares at the storm over the god Annam and shouts, “We should run!” 
Orsik looks up and agrees, “Let’s get to that ledge.” 
And with that, the group runs through the room. Unfortunately, the room is 480 long, and when Thistle touched the Storm rune, he trigged a Storm of Vengeance. And after the deafening sound and the acid, the storm triggers six powerful lightning bolts that kill everyone in the group. 
Harshnag stares at the bodies, “Well, shit.” 
He quickly picks up Orsik, Thistle, Unla Kee, and Vardis, dumps them into his satchel, and runs for the entrance to the Temple. As he runs, he thinks, “Thank goodness it’s three tiny people and an elf.” 
Once outside in the temple hallway entrance, he dumps the bodies on the ground. Harshnag then rummages through the stuff of the 3HPP and finds a healing potion. He stares at it and then shoves it in Vardis’ mouth. 
## 4th day of the 1st tenday of Eleasis
The following day, the group is healed. 
Unla Kee muses, “That was fun! Let’s go try that again!” 
They all stare at him.
Orsik turns to the group, “Let’s devise a better plan.” 
Vardis muses, “Maybe the problem is who touched the statue.” 
Harshnag mumbles, “Why are the weapons removable?” 
Orsik and Vardis stare at him and then say in unison, “It’s the weapons.” 
“Huh?” 
Orsik shouts, “You need to take a weapon and use that weapon to touch the corresponding rune!” 
“Brilliant!” 
“Yes!” agrees Orsik.
“But which one?” 
“I think we should do the hill giant rune,” suggests Harshnag. “The storm giant rune was deadly.” 
And with that, Harshnag marches up to the hill giant statue, picks up its 500lb bone club, and touches the hill rune; the rune glows, and then a series of howling magical mad voices emanating from giant distended mouths surround him. 
The Grim seems unaffected, and the starts to act as if under the influence of a confusion spell.
When the spell effects wear off, he marches back to the 3HPP outside the Temple and says, “Well, that didn’t work.”
Orsik turns to him, “Well, why not try touching the Frost Giant Rune? You are immune to frost damage.” 
Harshnag stares at him and then marches back to the archway. He touches the rune. The rune glows blue, and then above him, he sees a blue dot shimmer, and then a magical ice storm pelts him. 
A few minutes later, he returns to the hallway and grumbles, “That didn’t work either.” 
“Why not use the Frost Giant weapon?” asks Vardis. 
“Didn’t I mention that it’s missing?”
“Oh, right.” Vardis nods. 
“Maybe it’s somewhere in the temple?” asks Orsik. 
“But where?” 
After some debate, the 3HPP decided to head through the double doors north of the Temple of Annam. 
The doors open into a massive room with giant stairs on the left, an alcove with a statue of a stone giant to the right, a hallway heading north, and another set of stairs past the alcove also going up. 
“Let’s explore this hallway,” shouts Orsik. 

And so they head through a 60’ archway into a hallway. Fourteen 30-foot-tall archways spaced along the west and east walls lead to 60-foot-square chambers furnished with frost-covered stone couches that serve as beds. Some of these beds have tattered furs atop them. 
Harshnag sighs, “Beds fit for a giant.” 
As he marches down the hallway, Vardis, who is following him, notices in one room there is a crack in the wall.
“Hey, let’s check this out,” he shouts. 
The rest of the 3HPP agree and go through the medium-sized tunnel. Harshnag takes the furs on the bed and lies down, sighing contentedly. 
The tunnels are 10’ high and 5’ wide and dark. Thistle leads the group down the snaking tunnel until they reach an intersection. 
Before he can react, spiders hiding in the shadows attack. 
Thistle’s one thought is – “I hate spiders.” 
Fortunately for him, Orsik likes killing them.
