# Chapter 17 - Big Trouble in Little Parnast
## Orsik 1st day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul 

As he counts the money from the reward Hsing gave him, he thinks, “this town looks shabbier than the last time we were here.” 
He turns to Vardis, “did this place look this dumpy when we left?” 
Unla Kee yawns, “after a vision quest, everything looks uglier.” 
Orsik shrugs. He pulls out a flower that he had put in his hair,  sniffs it,  sighs, and then looks around more carefully. There are make-shift shelters where strangers live. Animals are in pickets that were hastily set up. 
“Feels gloomy,” he thinks. 
Just then, a member of the militia, called Orsik’s guards, walks up to him, executes a miserable salute that Orsik carefully returns, and then starts talking in a torrent, “Commander Orsik, you know that army we were pretending didn’t exist? Well, it’s a bit more real these days. First, one farm was sacked, and now there are all of these people who have fled into town!” 
“We attack at once.” 
“Um, boss, you might want to talk to Ragnar. He’s been putting together some local defenses in your absence. Not really that successful, but he’s trying.” The guard stares at him, “you’ve been gone for 20 days?”
“A little bit less.”  Orsik notices that he is being judged. Orsik hates being judged and puts a bit of menace into his voice. 
“No judgment, boss. I just thought you could help him. He’s not as good as you at running a town’s defenses. And he’s had some bad luck.” 
Orsik is about to head off when that Fairy asks, “any ettin show up?” 
The guard turns pale, “why would they?” 
Sensing a need to lighten the atmosphere, Orsik shouts, “off to Ragnar the Red.” 
“He’s not red. He’s got brown hair. And his name is Ragnar Redtooh.” 
“See, I was right, Ragnar the Red.”
“There’s nothing red about him.” 
“Well, he’s got a red tooth.” 
“That’s his given name!” 
And then the guard starts to laugh, “Gods, we missed you, Orsik.” 
Orsik pats him on the shoulder and says, “good job, soldier.” 
Orsik then marches to the Golden Tankard. Inside, Ragnar Redtooth, who has brown hair, a large belly, and prominent muttonchops, is bidding three men goodnight. The two are members of the militia that Orsik recognizes, and the third is a short half-elf clad in worn furs.
Ragnar shouts, “get out … my old friends! I need your help!” He turns to the kitchen, “Elspeth, some food.” And she shows up with a plate full of cheese, fresh bread, some oil, a pitcher full of ale, and some mugs. 
“eat!”
After everyone has taken some food, he begins. “Orsik, I have tried my best to fill in your gargantuan shoes to organize a defense since you left. But I’ve encountered resistance and ill fortune at every turn. I suspect foul play.” 

At that moment, Orsik notices Wallace is no longer at the table and sees him outside talking to Elspeth. He smiles. 
Ragnar continues, “it’s Gundalin Cartwright. I will pay 50 gold each for any information you might uncover.” 
“So you want him dead.” 
“no information.” 
“just making sure there is no double entendre.”
“None.” Ragnar pauses and continues, “as a military man Orsik, you know how bad it is when people question your decisions. And every day Cartwright or one of his cronies are questioning a decision of mine.” He sighs, “I need their help. They are the most skilled craftsmen, and without their help, we won’t be able to defend the town.” Orsik notices how the tavern owner looks carefully at Thistle, “they still blame me for the whole Cult of the Dragon nonsense. I saved the town by joining. I was the man working from the inside, but they still refuse to see it that way.” 
Thistle can’t resist, “I wonder why.” And then mutters, “I’ll deal with you later.” 
Ragnar ignores the Fairy and continues talking to Orsik, “If Gundalin is behind the sabotage, I need proof. If he’s not, I need you to reason with him to help me defend the town.” 
Orsik is starting to nod off. 
“Perhaps, I should talk to Vardis?”
## Ragnar 
*Orsik’s a good soldier, but Vardis is whom I need now. I need someone who has brains and judgment, not a strong sword arm. *
“Mr. Vardis, if we don’t get the villagers to work together, all is lost. They will all flee into the hills and be slaughtered.” 

Vardis nods and thinks, “not sure why I care, but I’ll go along with this. There might be some coin, and Orsik seems particularly attached to the safety of this town.” 
“Did you notice that young half-elf Tove? He brought me military intelligence. He said that there are monsters on the move and that the forces are constantly moving, destroying what they find. He claimed he couldn’t find their main camp.” 
The tavern keeper then takes a deep breath, “I’m telling you an attack by Bad Fruul’s army is imminent.”
And then Vardis asks, “what sabotage?” 
He smiles. “First, we have seen tools disappear from our toolshed in the center of town.”
“How often?” 
“A few every day, just to be annoying and slow us down. Not enough to claim a robbery.”
“Second, the day after the southern watchtower was completed, it collapsed, killing a militiaman named Donnal.”
“Explain more?” 
“Well, I am no carpenter, but it was sturdy by my eyes. One of the men thinks he saw the light near the tower’s base just before it collapsed. And after the collapse, the men were convinced it was because we were using materials scavenged from Old Parnast that had been cursed by the Cult of the Dragon. I can assert that there is no more activity of that Cult here.”
“Is that all?”
“No, this morning, the cart we haul materials from Old Parnast broke its second axle in a tenday. The wheels and axel had been replaced by Gundalin.” 
Ragnar is relieved when he hears Vardis say, “we’ll see what we can do.” 
The group

The group brainstorms over what to do next. Unla Kee wants to go check out the shed to see if there is anything good. Orsik intends to stake out the shed, see if anyone steals anything, and follow them. Thistle wants to check out the watchtower and stakeout Gundalin. Ragnar suggests they could talk to the villagers and learn what’s happening. 
As they walk outside the Golden Tankard, Orsik shouts at Wallace that they must get going. Wallace kisses Elspeth and promises to talk later. Wallace rolls his eyes when Orsik explains what Ragnar thinks about his father. “Right, my father is doing his best to betray the town so that Orcs will take over? Really?” 
Just then, Azam runs at them – “I forgot to tell Mr. Underwood where his uncle is.” 
Thistle pauses, “yes?”
“He’s in the town of Triboar. It’s about 400 miles away. And we have more disturbing news.” 
“What did Uncle Joon do this time?”
“The good news is that he did not join the Cult of the Dragon. The bad news is that he joined some secretive organization that even the SEER doesn’t know about. 
Azam knows that Thistle will leave, as will Orsik and Vardis, who want to learn more about their map, which would affect the SEER’s plan, so Azam becomes desperate. 
“The SEER wants me to let you know that the protection of Parnast from Bad Fruul is of paramount importance to the Lord’s Alliance. If you were to successfully defend the town, you would earn the admiration and support of a most powerful personage.”
Thistle, Orsik, and Vardis realize that the town would be destroyed if they were to leave. An earlier Orsik might have left, but this Orsik is loyal to the guards he trained. So Orsik shouts, “this Joon is he going anywhere?” 
“Oh no, he has set up shop, and if he were to leave, the Lord’s Alliance would keep track of him.”
Their conversation is cut short when they overhear villagers screaming in the center of town. Arriving at the scene, they see three afflicted horses; two lay on the ground, one trembling violently, and the other is dead. A third is on its feet, staggering as Eryn tries to steady it. 
Just then, Chandra Stol, the local cleric, arrives and stares. She just stares. 
Thistle examines the horses and concludes that nothing is amiss. “These are inferior beasts of burden to Drakes. Therefore I have a Drake. Nothing to see here. Let’s move on.” 
 Wallace steadies the trembling horse. He then examines the animals further and shouts, “My Gods! The animals must have been poisoned.” Wallace then dramatically announces – “and that one is dead!” 
Wald and Eryn, the two villagers that own the horses, just stare at him and then roll their eyes. Eryn, a childhood chum of Wallace, mutters, “the knowledge one accumulates as an adventurer!” 
Unla Kee then turns to examine the horses, using his standard investigative stance of one eye opened very big and one closed. He stares at the teeth and announces, “there are some evergreen branches in the horse’s mouth and hay!” 
Thistle seeing all of the excitement, uses his medical knowledge to examine the horses and concludes, “yep, inferior beasts of burden. Nothing to see here.” And then pats Thuul on the head. 
Chandra Stol, who has been silent this whole time, squeaks, “my, the adventurers who brought me my temple and saved the village from those raiders!” 
Vardis mutters, “saving horses right now!” 
“I want to help!” 
“Can you cast a spell?”
“Oh no. But Silas’ wife, who passed away, may Kelemvor keep her safe, was a herbalist and may have something that could help.” 
Vardis sneers, “well, I have a spell.” And he casts lesser restoration on the horse on the ground near death. Afterward, he turns to Chandra, “Since I am worried about those assassins, tell me about this herbalist?” 
Thistle is surprised when he sees the dying horse stand up, “strange. I never knew horses could recover from poison like that.” Intrigued, he casts ‘*speaks with animals*’ on the horse to learn what happened. 
“So what happened?” 
The one Vardis healed says - “We ate the hay, and then our stomachs felt gross, so we ate more hay, and then we noticed the hay had a funny taste, so we ate more hay, and then we got sick.” 
The sick one moans, “why shouldn’t we eat more hay? It’s food. Food is always good.” It pauses, “I am not feeling so good,” and then projectile vomits at Thistle, who barely avoids getting drenched. 
Realizing that the vomit didn’t cure the horse, Vardis shouts, “let’s go find this herbalist.” 
At this point, the group takes stock of what’s happening with the rest of the villagers. And realizes that they are in the midst of massive debate. Some argue that they should kill the horse because it’s the plague. Others say this horse was poisoned as retribution for Weld’s actions during the last Greengrass festival. Still, others suggest they rush over to Silas’ garden, tear everything up, and feed it to the horse. Fingers point at individuals, men tsk-tsk, children run around, and in general chaos as every debate, argument, and medical issue of every animal, person and beast are being re-litigated. 
Chandra then asks Thistle, “can you folks do something.” 
Thistle examines the evergreen and asks loudly, “does anyone know anything about that?” 
At which point, the conversation erupts again. Some folks argue that this plant was dragged in by the newcomers. Others say that this plant was placed by someone as a way to scare the newcomers. Still, others shout that this plant is not native to this part of the realm. And at that point, the tension starts to rise as the friction between the refugees and the residents of Parnasts escalates. 
Chandra whispers, “please figure this one out. We can’t have a riot.” 
Orsik can’t resist – “I agree with those who think that it’s a right of every law-abiding citizen of Parnast to carry poison.” 
That then triggers another round of shouting. 
“That poison is the kind of poison we should never allow.” 
“That poison is useful, and only irresponsible people let their horses eat poison.”
“Are you saying Well is irresponsible?” 
“The key to safe poison usage is having access to antitoxins and proper stowage.” 
“Yes, but accidents happen!” 
“This wasn’t an accident.” 
Vardis and Orsik pocket the evergreen as Thistle says, “let’s go check out this herbalist.” 
## Silas Carras
“Why, why isn’t it the Defenders of Parnast, and Mr. Orsik, the head of the militia? You must want some of my *wakeup sauce* for that gnome?” 
The gnome stares at him blankly. 
Silas whispers, “you know, the thing people drink in the morning after having drunk a little bit too much in the evening….” 
“No, some antitoxin for this venomous evergreen, actually,” says Unla Kee. Silas smiles. He’s got a new customer. 
Silas explains for the n-th time that he’s not the herbalist. His dead wife was. And that he has no idea if there is any antitoxin available, but the Defenders are welcome to go look in the backyard. 
Silas laughs as Thistle announces that he will use his survival and nature skills to find this antitoxin, “you’ll need to think through this puzzle,” mutters Silas.  
Silas’ wife liked puzzles, which made it into her garden layout. And to find what you wanted, you had to solve the set of nested enigmas she used to hide herbs from folks who didn’t want to pay.
He knew this would take a very long time. 
But is shocked when Unla Kee hands Thistle the antidote. 
Silas now thinks that maybe these folks can help with another mystery. “Hey, you’re not the only people who found something in this garden. Some of my wife’s bushes were trampled recently.”
Thistle examines the trampled plants and finds a trail he chooses to follow alone that leads out of the garden.  
Silas is about to shout, “what about the horses?” but just then, Unla Kee, who was investigating, stands up and shouts, “the evergreen is Yew, and this is the antidote.”
## Thistle

As  Thistle follows the trial, he overhears the farmer’s joy that their horses are healed. He can also hear that the debate Orsik started about poison rights has not ended. 
“That’s interesting. These footsteps lead right to Gundalin Cartwright’s house. And they lead to the front door.”
