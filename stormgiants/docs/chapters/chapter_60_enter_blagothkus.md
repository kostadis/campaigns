# Chapter 60 - Enter Blagothkus

## Eleasis 8th day 2nd tenday 1492

After the death of the Ogre Zombies in the stern tower that was Blagothkus’ lair, Vardis and Orsik engage in a bit of intellectual debate about the nature of free will. 
“We have a Bob problem.” 
“No kidding.” 
“He’s evil.” 
“Well, he could choose to change?” asks Orsik.
“That’s unfortunately not how the outerplanes work. Creatures that are native to the prime material plane have agency. They can choose to be good or evil. It’s pure and rabid planism, I get it. But it also happens to be how the Universe works. It’s why Tieflings are always viewed as – evil – and Aasimar are always viewed as – good. Folks assume that a Tiefling is intrinsically evil because of their relationship to the outer planes.” 
“So the prime material plane is superior to other planes? Wow. That’s rabid planism.” 
“No, I didn’t say that.”
“But you said free will only exist in the prime material plane.” 
“And many outer planar entities view that viewpoint as bizarre and awful. It’s a bit of prime-material-plane bias.” 
“I see. That’s why clerics hate the undead because they lack agency?” 
The conversation becomes tedious, so Unla Kee and Thistle explore the rest of the tower. Unla Kee becomes invisible, and Thistle sneaks. They climb up to the second floor. The room looks like a series of battles have taken place. There are scorch marks from magical fires, gouges on the bed from swords, broken arrows strewn across the room, and frozen blood pools on the ground. The walls of this room are sculpted with icy murals depicting cloud giants riding giant birds. An enormous, smashed bed with a headboard of ice sculpted to resemble clouds dominates the room. The headboard has taken heat damage and is partly melted. Chunks have been broken off and are on the ground. Bear furs are heaped upon the bed, and two large wooden chests rest at the bed’s foot. 
Standing in the middle of the room is a large Frost Giant skeleton. Thistle stares, confused, “Why would a Frost Giant be in a Cloud Giant’s castle?” 
Unla Kee remembers a class he took, or was it a story he was told, or was it a vision of the future? And says, “This castle played a prominent role in the final defeat of Tiamat. At the time, Frost Giants were staffing the castle, not Cloud Giants.” 
Thistle is now confused, “How did Blagothkus get control of the castle again?” 
And then both realized that perhaps the friendly vampire wasn’t as pleasant as she appeared. Maybe she was the actual proprietor of the castle. Possibly, the Vampire wanted a base in the sky that they could use to conduct necromantic experiments. And what exactly was inside the glacier? 
Thistle quietly empties the chests of the treasure of Blagothkus. And the two of them, unnoticed, return to Orsik and Vardis, who are still debating the complex issue of agency, planism, and whether free will is good or bad. 
With the treasure in tow, Unla Kee shouts, “Screw Bob and his play.” He points to the haul, “This is enough to retire on.” 
They all stare at him. 
They exit the stern tower and sneak on the starboard side of the castle past a room that contains a Frost Giant. 
While everyone else debates what to do, an invisible Unla Kee stares. 
Unla Kee asks himself, “Are they immune to psychic magic?” He remembers that he was drunk when that topic was covered, and trusting his gut casts psychic lance. 
The giant skeleton is immobilized. And then promptly destroyed. 
Unla Kee stares at the dead creature, smiles, and declares – “it doesn’t matter what I thought I knew. I know the future, and the future said the spell worked.” 
Inside the room, there is nothing but detritus. A battle tore the room apart, and nobody came by to clean it. 
Now, in the central outer courtyard at the aft of the cloud castle, they see a staircase that leads to a second tower. The door is barred heavily shut. 
Orisk casts knock, and the door opens. 
Two gaunt-looking ogres scream in terror. The dwarven fighter stares at them. He is confused; everyone else is a zombie, but these are alive. 
He ponders momentarily and says – “do you speak common?” 
One ogre with a scar on his face asks, “My name is Worug. Do you have food?” 
And Orsik responds, “You’re not undead.” 
The other ogre with a bandaged right-hand says, “My name is Tak, and we are hungry.” 
Orsik turns to the ranger, staring at some gnawed ogre bones. Thistle squirms and says, “Goodberries?” 
After consuming several, the ogres are looking less feeble and not as hungry. Worug tries to hug Thistle and says, “No, dude, bad touching!” 
Orsik then asks, “Why aren’t you dead?” 
And Worug says, “It turns out that if you don’t let a Vampire in, it can not enter your house.” 
“And I guess all the other ogres let the vampires in?” 
“Yes, they were stupid. Didn’t keep the door closed. So this purple lady says, "I am the big boss lady. Let me in. And they say sure, and then she comes in, and there is chaos, mayhem, and blood. And well, we said no.” 
Unla Kee infers that the Ogre’s and Blagothkus’ chambers are safe from the Vampire. 
The ogres then continue, “The crazy vampire lady killed everyone, some quickly and some slowly. As for Blagothkus, he was crying and saying that he had killed his son. Frankly, I don’t understand what is going on. But the Vampires don’t’ come out during the day, so we use that to get food.”
The ogres explain the horrors of the last few months. “After Blagothkus came to Parnast, he’s been unwilling to move and is holed up in the control room. The vampire got hungry and decided to feast on Blagothkus’ retinue. When the remaining creatures decided to attack the vampires, the vampires turned the dead into undead so that we couldn’t move during the day. The last few days, there’s been a bit of an ogre and elf game, where we try to sneak around to get food and not get noticed.”
At this point, a bored Thistle asks, “What’s in it for us?” 
Vardis turns to him and asks, “When did you get so - Orsik?” 
Worug, the Ogre with a scratch on his face, says, “I have a secret. If you get us off this castle, we will tell.” 
Vardis shrugs, “It could be that one of you likes the other.” 
Tak, the Ogre with the bandage on his right hand, coughs a little too eagerly, “That’s ridiculous.” 
Worug looks at Tak, “Why didn’t you say something?” 
Then, there is a long, awkward pause. 
Tak turns to the group, “If you promise to get me and Worug off this castle, we will tell you. I promise.” 
Unla Kee shouts, “Sure, I’ll gamble.” 
Worug explains that inside the glacier the Castle is built around is a den of a dracolich, Glazhael. That white dracolich was originally the steed of Talis the White. The First Faction killed Glazhael. The dragon, now a dracolich, tried to capture the castle but was led on a wild goose chase to find some artifact. When the return of Tiamat was thwarted, Blagothkus took control of the castle and she came back here. 

Orsik whistles, “A vampire, a dracolich, and an army of undead. How badly do we want this castle?” 
The group sighs as Unla Kee puts on a sorrowful face. 
Tak puts his arm around Worug’s as they leave and declares, “We won’t leave this tower. Come here if you need rest and safety from the vampire!” 
As Orsik walks away, “Why would the vampire kill us?” 
Unla Kee remarks, “because the vampire might conclude it needs three, not four heroes.” 
Thistle remarks, “I am sleeping on the ground, gentlemen.” 
Vardis wonders if he can get Delsephine to come and pick them up. 
As the group descends from the stairs that lead to Worug and Tak’s tower, they arrive at the barracks on the starboard side of the castle on the upper deck. They see what Worug and Tak described: a scene of dead bodies, chaos, and mayhem. The room has been torn apart, and several ogres are still in a state of decomposition. 
Across the upper courtyard on the port side of the ship, they see another Frost Giant Skeleton in one of three guest rooms. And once again, Unla Kee incapacitates it with a psychic lance. But his allies cannot kill it this time, and it strikes back with a Freezing Stare. Unla Kee barely survives. The creature, however, is killed. 
To the aft of the guest rooms is a room that housed kobols. From the look, the Vampire used them as a snack room. The kobolds are tied to their beds and are dead. The vampires used them as a living blood bank,  slowly killing each kobold. The look of horror on the poor, miserable creatures would rend the heart of any hero who cared. 
Vardis remarks, “maybe we kill the Vampire?”
Orsik shuts that idea down, “We need the vampire.” 
And with that marches to the control room at the aft of the cloud castle and knocks on the door. 
A male giant’s voice booms, “I will not let you, Sandesyl, or any of your hellspawn enter my tower. Do you hear that?” 
“We’re not vampires!” 
The giant voice shouts, “That’s what she said last time!” 
The voice goes silent, and suddenly, a periscope appears over the top of the tower of the control room. 
The voice shouts, “Yes, you are not Vampires. The periscope works with mirrors, and I could see you.”
 Now, with a much calmer voice, it asks, “What are you doing here, and what do you want?” 
“Have you spoken to the Oracle?” 
The giant starts to cry, “That’s how it all began! Me talking to the Oracle!” 
“When did you talk to the Oracle? The chamber hadn’t been opened in a thousand years.” 
The giant laughs, “Harshnag! You must have talked to Harshnag! It’s a standing joke. Harshnag brings humans and claims that this is the first time in a thousand years that anyone has been there.” 

Vardis is aghast, “but we had to solve a puzzle and almost got killed. Items we needed were strewn across the dungeon.” 
“Harshnag likes to make things interesting for any little people. He hides the items he needs in the temple. He thinks they are worthy if little people can figure out and survive. If they can’t do well, they are unworthy.” 
Orsik interrupts them and says, “Okay, but why not go to the Oracle now?” 
“Because my, she won’t let us go!” 
“The Dracolich?”
The giant scoffs, “that interloper? No. My wife.”
