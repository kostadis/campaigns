# Chapter 10 - The SEER

## Group

Our heroes quietly advance on the half-ogres sitting around the fire with cat-like tread, catching them unawares. 
Vardis opens with a guiding bolt that streaks through the night sky and plunges into the half-ogres body. It is visibly hurt. 
Unla Kee prepares his spell and thinks, “so the Freetor let me bet we would kill the half-ogres. The way I see it, if I win, I get money. If I am dead,  it doesn’t matter.”
“For the 50!” he mutters.     
And he charges up to the ogres and casts Rimes Binding Ice on the pair. Unla Kee manipulates the fates just a little, and the first is killed. The second is too groggy from being woken up to react in time. The ice wounds him and traps him. He is in shock and immobile. 

Orsik throws a handaxe. And Vardis kills the remaining ogre with a single arrow. 
“That’s how it’s done, Wallace.” 
Wallace, who had been surprised, smiles sheepishly. He then asks – “He’s good. I’m impressed. Do you guys hang out a lot? You went to school together?” 
The dwarf is annoyed, “He’s my best friend.” 
“Your best friend?” says an obviously hurt Wallace. 
“I met you like one week ago.” 
Orsik stares at Wallace. Wallace stares at Orsik in awe. 
Meanwhile, Thistle decides to explore the Razervine while the rest of the crew searches the corpses and the huts around the fire. 
Thistle flies into the bushes and into a swarm of poisonous snakes on the branches. The snakes drop on his body, biting him with their fangs and injecting their poison. Thistle barely escapes with his life.
He remembers what Unla Kee said – “I think we should stick together.”
And his flippant response, “I am a scout. I scout.” 
He beats a hasty retreat and thinks, “I scouted!” 
Meanwhile, Orsik, Unla Kee, Vardis, and Wallace search the bodies. 
In one of the huts is a dwarf. Orsik stares, “my kinsman.” Now it turns out that the word kinsman among the dwarves is only used among members of the same clan. But Orsik is a modern dwarf and rejects the clannish exclusionary behavior of his kind. And takes a more pan-dwarven view of the world, that every dwarf is a kinsman. Or it might be that Orsik’s relationship with Vardis and his actions got him kicked out from his clan, and no other self-respecting clan wants him.
Orsik shows the golden beard clips he removed from the dead to Thistle, “look what we found. What did you find?” 
“Snakes.” And then he proceeds to munch some goodberries. 
At this point, the group pauses and discusses what to do next. Straight to the north is the sinkhole, which is probably where  Mama Manyknuckles lives. To the northwest is a bunch of Razervine with poisonous snakes. To the northeast is also Razervine, a beach that will allow the group to reach the northern camp. 
The group stealthily arrived at the northern island near an open wooden pavilion stained with gore. They see six large ramshackle wooden hunts huts past the gazebo, surrounded by a massive fire pit.  
Orsik mutters, “perfect.”
Thistle notices that the camp could accommodate 12 giants, but all he can see are 2 half-ogres. Thistle tries to see if he can use his tracking skills to determine how many creatures are there but fails. The tracks are unclear. 
“Let’s advance quietly.” 
Wallace is not paying attention as the rest of the group surprises the two creatures. 
Thistle strikes with his bow and sends Thuul up ahead.  
Orsik is too far for his handaxes and dashes.
Vardis’s strike is accurate, and one of the two half-ogre dies.  
The other one is in shock and slow to react but screams, “Mama Mia!” 
Wallace misses and thinks, “I gotta step up my game.” 
Orsik hurls his handaxes. Vardis strikes again. 
Unla Kee strikes with a magic missile. Thuul, who has reached the half-ogre, smashes him at Thistle’s command. 
And the creature dies. 
## Mama Manyknuckles
*Those knuckleheads. *
She quickly signals to her two guards to quietly approach the north ramp. 
*They have no idea what they are about to get into.*
She whispers to her mud mephits, but she doesn’t hear them respond. 
“Damn annoying creatures!” 
She overhears them talking about taking a breather, “maybe they will go away,” she hopes. 
But then she hears them trying to surprise her by flanking her from the south. 
She whispers to her half-ogres, “quickly get into position for an ambush.” 
The ambush is a partial success. The invaders are surprised and they are frightened. But their will is too strong, and Mama Mia can only kill one with her death gaze. 
Mama Mia, or Mama Manyknuckles, is a hag, and she reveals herself as such. 
Orsik is killed when he doesn’t look away in time, and Mama Mia smiles her devilishly grin. But that doesn’t last long. As the rest of the group overwhelms her ogres and her beloved Belg. 
She tries to flee, but this time there is no escape. Her last thoughts are, “Damn you, Bad Fruul. Ever since my gang signed up with you, I knew this was inevitable.” 
# Unla Kee
*I gambled and won, and the world continued for another day. *
He notices Feetor, “New king of the swamp!” 
Feetor hands him his winnings. 
“Where’s my gold?”
Orsik looks at Unla – “Where’s the gold?” 
Feetor is confused, “this is special mud! It makes you look prettier and heals you from bad wounds!” 
“We have altered the deal, Mephit,” says Vardis in a deep raspy voice. “Give us the gold!” 
Unla thinks it has come time to teach the Mephits who is boss. And as he is about to attack, he notices that the swamp is bubbling. 
He overhears Thistle shouting, “we are not fighting these Mud Mephits.” 
Feetor trying to defuse the situation gives Unla a hug and says, “you are an honorary Mud Mephit!” 
The dwarf snickers, “Congratulations, we are now part of the tribe.” 
“And because you are a tribe member, here’s a stone you can use to ask for our help!” 
The dwarf whispers to Wallace, “Good lesson. Never give your money to a mud mephit.” He pauses, “but you knew that?” 
When Wallace explains to Unla Kee that he should never give money to a mud mephit, Unla decides he’s had enough. 
“I’ll make a new deal. How about we bet this mud that I can kill you in a duel?”
Feetor is impressed and frightened, “I will get your money, big boss! But can you say I am #2?” 
Unla sighs, “sure.” 
Feetor shouts, “Today is a great day! The civil war has ended. We are now all followers of Unla Kee!” 
But something odd happens. The Mud Mephits are not convinced that Unla Kee is the strongest. And pretty soon, there is bubbling and squabbling, and the civil war erupts again as mud mephits argue over whether that Orsik is the strongest because Wallace is already following him. Others say that Vardis is the clear leader because of his grace. Some are fans of Thistle and see his command of the Drake as proof of his leadership skills. 

Regardless the battle continues, and soon, the place is quiet again.
Feetor shrugs, “oh well.” 
## Orsik and Wallace

Exhausted, they take a rest. In the morning, they are awakened by the whistling noise announcing the arrival of a golden pseudodragon with milky-white eyes.

“That again,” mumbles Orsik. 
Wallace turns to Orsik, “can I ask a question?” 
“Sure.”

Hsing whistles, “I just teleported here. I am the emissary of the SEER. You’re supposed to be paying attention to me.” 
Orsik stares at the creature, “it’s always about you.”
“Yes. But enough about me and my relationship with the Great SEER!” 
“The SEER believes you have found a site long sought, the tower of the Wizard of the Crag. She wishes to encourage you to explore the tower. She is interested in the repository of Lore he is rumored to have had. If you retrieve these manuscripts on ancient forms of magic, we shall pay you handsomely.”
Orsik stares at the blind creature, “Killing it would be awesome! We could get some serious cred with the Zhentarim.” 
Wallace likes the idea but urges caution, “The SEER is the top Spymaster of the Lord’s Alliance. And if she can find us here, she can find us anywhere. Not sure we will survive to collect the reward.” 
And with that, Orsik lets Thistle question Hsing, and they learn that the Wizard was a master of at least the elemental powers of water and earth and that he worked with all of the elements. 
They also learned that he was a devotee of both Mystara and Oghma. 
And last, they learn what the SEER hopes to get from this tower. Apparently, she needs any kind of help to deal with Bad Fruul, whose emergence as a local power thwarted the Zhentarim. 
And with that, he turns to them and says – “I can’t do everything for you. I am behind on my naps as is. This is the place, your job to enter. Coming out with just one miserable spellbook is not acceptable. Use the pendant that you found. I hear SEER calling me,” and he apparently teleports away.
