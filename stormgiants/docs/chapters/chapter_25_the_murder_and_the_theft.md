# Chapter 25 - The Murder and the Theft
A summary. 
- Jacob and Persson Hysstryn convinced Thistle to dig into the mysterious death of their brother Mirak. It turns out his brother had an affair with an ex-adventurer, Urgala Meltimer, who owns the Northshield House Inn. They then find out from a drunk priest of Helm, currently passed out in the Talking Troll, that Mirak’s body is in the Six Windows Inn. 

Thanks to Thistle's brilliant distraction at the six windows inn, the rest of the group sneaks in and uncovers, in the attic, the remains of Mirak. Using the remains, they convince the Lord Protector to at least put Tolmara in prison until a cleric can be found who can cast speak with the dead. The Lord Protector Darathra demands the heroes stay in town as they did cause quite a commotion until the next day. 

Urgala Meltimer tells them of the death of her first wife in the Underdark. And of the many places to scrounge for treasure in the Dessarin Hills. A place where there was enough to mine as a wildcat miner but not enough to be worthy of controlling. Also, bandits and uncivilized types lived there. 

She provides a map of one area she thought was of extreme interest. 

- When Orsik and Vardis decide to go shopping, they hit the Lionshield Coster Outpost, Lion’s Share. They meet the owners Alaestra and Narth, who are in disarray because their vault was breached. This vault included special keys to get inventory through their interplanar supply chain. 

The Lionshield Coster chain leverages interplanar trade routes. The famous gnomish sage KP first analyzed the impact on trade across the entirety of existence. KP’s research has been controversial because although achieving the multi-planar optimum for economic efficiency is possible, the equations ignore the impact on local social cohesion. Orsik and Vardis were now thrust into the exact kinds of social disruption that planerization and its advocates dismissed. The undercutting of local merchants made the locals look at Lion’s Share with caution and disgust. Alaestra is relieved to find outsiders who can help. 

The only clue was a leather harness and a masterly crafted grappling hook. 

The group first talks to the master harness maker Orthovir, who admits he didn’t make the harness. And he is sure it was made in Waterdeep. It can fit a thin person that is about 150 pounds. 

They then go to Harriet Uldinath (T17), who doesn’t recognize the grappling hook and insists it isn’t from her shop. She does admit that it is better than anything she could make, and it might be the work of her competitor Ghelryn Foehammer. 

Ghelryn Foehammer (T18) is a dwarf that believes in privacy. But is convinced that privacy ends at the limits of burglary. He reveals that it was sold to Darz Helgar. 

Darz Heglar is the campground overseer and an ex-criminal from Waterdeep. When presented with the harness immediately reveals that Kaelen, who runs the Troll Tavern, forced him to the caper. He was in Waterdeep over ten years ago, and spent time in their dungeons. Orsik is very interested and offers to keep this secret if Darz reveals anything about the thieves guild. 

“It was ten years ago, but I will.”
