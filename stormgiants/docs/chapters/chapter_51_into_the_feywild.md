# Chapter 51 - Into the Feywild
Somewhere deep in Orsik’s brain, a part of him was contemplating the nature of intelligence. Intelligence was the process of inventing new ideas from observation. Wisdom is measuring the validity of those ideas against the world’s reality.
Orsik had suddenly become supremely intelligent; was his intelligence overwhelming his wisdom? His life experiences had not prepared him for the new ideas he had. Was he like a child who could see things without contextualizing them correctly? Would he become a force for good? For evil? Or for Orsik?
Was his desire to age an act of wisdom or foolishness? A brilliant insight or an impetuous action of a child?
But that is a thought for another time. 
Right now, Orsik was under a large tree, attacked by ghosts, and needed to focus. 
“It’s gone,” shouts Thistle. 
Orisk rests for a moment, and then his warrior training kicks in. He scans his surroundings and sees a human-sized shape barely distinguishable from the background. 
“Ghost!” he shouts. 
Vardis says the words of power, and a raw blast of coruscating divine energy flies from his outstretched hand, but the ghost slips away into the two arrows from Thistle’s bow.
The ghost glares and Orsik burns him with a firebolt, but it is still not dead. 
Vardis shouts, “FIREBALL!” 
Unla Kee prepares the Bat Guano and then has a change of heart and strikes with a mind sliver, shattering whatever mind was there. 
“Two more to go!”
At this point, Orsik’s inner voice reminds him that if they had paid the guide on the ship 50 gold, they might have avoided all of this. 
And Orisk says out loud, “We didn’t get rich by writing a lot of checks!” 
And the inside voice responds, “You got rich by spending money irresponsibly!” 
Orsik shouts, “It was a joke.” 
Vardis, seeing his friend troubled, “is this about the 50 gold?” 
“Yes,” grumbles Orsik. 
“Well, it wasn’t real estate.” 
Vardis suddenly runs from underneath the giant tree and shoots at something in the sky, “Missed!” 
Thistle’s bow strikes true, and he mutters, “Amateurs.” 
Orsik is annoyed at Thistle, so to show him who the boss is, he throws a hand ax at the ghost and barely scratches it. 
The ghost approaches the 3HPP, and the pleasant face of the dead Uthgartdian chieftain is replaced by the horror of the undead, scaring everyone and unnaturally aging Thistle. 
Orsik is annoyed, “damn it! Why didn’t I get older.” 
This time, Orsik is pleased to see Vardis’ arrow hit the creature, and as it dissipates, he bangs his sword against his shield and shouts, “Okay, one more to go, guys!” 
The air shimmers next to Unla Kee as the final ghost materializes next to him. 
Orsik observes the spell Vardis cast has no effect and mutters, “Bummer.” 
The ghost attempts to possess Unla Kee, but the diviner twists the fabric of time and space, and the spirit cannot overwhelm the mage’s mental defenses. 
Orsik advances on the ghost, whose peculiar existence shows the damage of Thistle’s two arrows, 
A few hours later, with the skull of some Giant that radiates necromancy in their bag of holding and covered in dirt from digging, the 3HPP approach their airship. 
Thistle
The Fairy was worried. Titania, the Goddess of the Fey and ruler of the Fey court, had punished someone for insulting her in that awful way the Fey goddess punished. And she wasn’t usually the forgiving type. 
“Forgiving? Hah! Vengeful, spiteful, cruel, and heartless.” 
And just then, the sky in front of him sparkled, and a sprite with a high-pitched squeaky voice, wearing pink pants and an orange camis, appeared and said in common.
“I appear here at the request of the Seely Court! It has come to our attention that you have interfered in the ongoing punishment that Queen Titania, in all of her righteousness, imposed on Helia and Halisa. She commends your subterfuge and subtlety in freeing the two Dryads and is pleased that it was to advance a personal and selfish goal.” 
Just then, Orsik-Thornacious appears and smiles, waving his hand. 
Thistle is worried, and his worries are justified when the Sprite says in fey – “Just because those ignorant Dryads fell for that trick, doesn’t mean I will. But Titania was pleased with the use of trickery. And she is also pleased that she can use Thornacious’ involvement in this sordid affair as another reason to keep him out of the Feywild.” 
The Sprite then returns to common, “Unfortunately, the Queen’s wroth cannot ignore the insult of you revering one of her decisions, and she has decided to give you a small little task. A Formorian of the Unseelie Court has taken refuge in the upper lands. You are to investigate and deal with the problem.” 
Orsik-Thornacious whispers to Thistle, “We can kill that thing?” 
Thistle turns pale white, “If this is all we have to do, we got off light.” 
The sprite squeaks in delight, “Wonderful! There were, of course, others arguing for more drastic punishments. In her infinite wisdom, the Queen appreciated how you dealt with your Uncle Joon.” 
Thistle can see the challenge between Orsik’s intelligence and his wisdom. The intelligence tells him that Orsik could probably kill the Sprite without anyone noticing and then deny the crime. And he’s about to do it when Thistle intervenes, “Do you really think that the Seelie Court won’t know who killed their emissary?” 
The Sprite then waves its hand, and a portal emerges – “In you go!” 
“I thought it was near this forest.” 
“Yes, it is near this forest in the Fey.” 
Orsik-Thornacious is pissed off and about to strike. 
Thistle tries calming him, “This is normal, totally normal!” But Thistle’s voice betrays his worry, yet Thistle somehow persuades Orsik-Thornacious to come through the portal. 
The ranger squirms when he hears Orsik-Thornacious mutter, “Prince Thronacious is delighted to come through a scary portal on behalf of the Queen. It’s not like anything bad happens when you go through a portal.” 
A disorienting moment later, Thistle is in the Feywild. And the colors finally make sense to him, bursts of bright oranges, yellows, purples, and greens flood his vision. As the colors continue to bloom, he begins to see kaleidoscopic patterns. The patterns spin in slower and slower circles until finally floating further from him and settling into the environment. The oranges and yellows press into leaves and flowers, the purples nestle into the sky, and the greens vein trees and the grass beneath your feet.
“Home,” he thinks.

Just then, an elven hunter appears, smiling and announces himself as Drauchanar, or Chan or the Knife. He quickly explains that he’s no ordinary hunter but hunts rare game like the Formorian! 
Just as Thistle accepts the offer, he realizes Orsik still looks like Thornacious. 
“The Prince will come? But if the Prince comes, I will not be permitted to keep the prize!” 
Thistle has a brain wave and says, in common, “Perhaps the Prince should disguise himself as something that nobody would suspect, perhaps a dwarf?” 
Drauchanar, in a sing-songy high-pitched voice, squeals – “Wonderful and how gracious! This way, the Prince can guide us in the hunt, the Knife can keep the head, and all the forms of society and culture can be preserved!” 
And with that, he shouts, “Follow me! A hunting Fomorians we shall go!” 
Thistle follows the Knife reluctantly but assumes this guide must have some idea where he is going. And yet, as they walk, he can not help but feel that something is watching him. 
As the sun sets, Drauchanar declares, “We shall rest here! And I shall sing a song!” 
“That is not necessary,” pleads Thistle.
“Oh, but it is!”
Drauchanar sings an old song, and Thistle notices how it enthralls Vardis. It is an elvish song that speaks to the elven soul in ways that surprisingly penetrate even the nearly impervious shell of cynicism Vardis has built up over the years. It’s about the love of Corellon and Araushnee before the sundering. 
Orsik wakes everyone up with a shout, “Where is he?” Thistle is immediately awake, bow in hand, “who?” 
“Your friend led us into the forest and abandoned us.” 
Thistle quickly scans the area and patiently explains that The Knife was ambushed and dragged off by some kind of giant-like creature and a four-legged beast. 
Orsik is annoyed and wants to abandon the quest, but Thistle, empowered by the strange magic found in the Feywild and his belief that pissing off Titania will not be a good thing, convinces him to continue. 
And so the 3HPP follows the trail and arrives at a cave opening 20 feet in diameter set into a cave wall that rises out of the forest. Thistle notices that a cyclops is standing guard. 

Orsik mutters, “Just when I finally need Harshnag.” 
And then Thistle points to a waterfall. Orsik mutters, “Yes, a waterfall. There is always treasure behind a waterfall.” 
A few minutes later, the 3HPP is wet and standing in a tunnel behind.
“I told you!” gloats Orsik. 
But there is no treasure. And so Orsik tries hard to disbelieve the walls. “There’s always a treasure chest behind the waterfall!” 

As he walks around the tunnel, a goblin dressed in studded armor pokes their head and asks, “What do you want?”
When they are informed that the 3HPP want to kill a Formorian, they squeal in delight and a few moments later return with a much larger goblin that speaks with a very nasal, squeaky voice and says, “Goblin #1, yes, we use numbers, said that you were looking for a Formorian?” 
Orsik growls, “It’s pronounced Treasure Chest.” 
“A Fomorian or a treasure chest?” they ask, confused. 
Thistle assures them that it’s a Fomorian. 
“My name is Tlega.” 
“Hello Tlega, I am Thistle.”  
The goblin is touched by Thistle’s flawless pronunciation. It explains that the typical Bully/Bullied relationship with the Fomorian Maegurn has fallen apart, as Maegurn has become abusive, not just a bully. 
“It’s time for him to go.” Tlega encourages the 3HPP to follow him into the caves. Orsik, being suspicious, cautions the Fairy who says, “I looked into their eyes, and I saw their soul. They are not lying.” 
Orsik nudges Thistle, “Then let’s get them to help us.”
Tlega is delighted with the proposal and urges that all of the Goblins assemble to attack. And with that, Mirk, another Goblin boss, appears. Mirk is obviously unhappy at Tlega’s decision and explains that the Bullied/Bully relationship is the core of Goblin society and that the line is just an imaginary line. The two begin to argue, and Tlega and the other goblins become deflated. 
Orsik is about to try his luck and intimidate Mirk when Unla Kee says, “I got this spell, Jim’s Glowing Coin.” Orsik smiles, “Yes, that’s awesome. That will work great.” 
Mirk and Tlega, distracted, quickly agree that dispatching Maergurn is the right next thing to do to advance the needs of this tribe.
