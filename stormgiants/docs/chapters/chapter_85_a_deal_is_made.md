# Chapter 85 - A deal is made

The blue dragon—purple worm hybrid roars, and Orsik feels his blood boil. He is alive, and soon, he will be a hero, dying fighting these abominations. Using all of his skill and sophistication, he savagely hurts the creature. It bleeds. It howls. 

He smiles. 

The creature strikes him with his tail. 

“Wait, that’s unfair. I am not near his tail,” he screams. 

It turns out that the hybrid's tail was far more flexible than Orsik had guessed. And it is much longer. It swings out from under the creature. 

The sting misses. 

Thuul rushes to the dragon coming from the south and attacks. Thistle spreads his arrows between the two dragons. For a moment, he thinks, “Those arrows of dragon slaying would have been useful.” 

Then, lightning strikes crossed from opposite sides of the courtyard, striking Unla Kee, Thistle, and Vardis. 

Unla Kee tries to use a counterspell, but Orsik shouts, “Lair actions. They are not spells.” 

And then the dragon Orsik was fighting strikes again, and this time doesn’t miss. Orsik uses the gift Thornacious gave him so long ago and avoids being poisoned. 

Vardis summons a djinni and tells him to attack the Dragon that Orsik is fighting. 

The hybrid howls and strikes Orsik again with his purple worm stinger. Orsik is bloodied and injured and mutters, “Today is good to die!” 

Meanwhile, the other hybrid kills Thuul with his stinger. 

The Djinni attacks the Hybrid Dragon, threatening Orsik with his blade and causing thunderous damage to the dragon, but it lives. 

The first dragon prepares to breathe, but it uses its frightening aura before that. Only Orsik is affected. “I can’t be afraid. I am Orsik.” And, indeed, he is not afraid of the dragon; the magic works on his mind, and the fear his mind has is that somehow he won’t die. That he won’t die and he won’t be the hero. 

The dragon opens its jaw and a bright light forms from it. The air crackles with electric charge and a blast of lightning stretches out from his mouth to a point 90 feet away. Orsik feels the charge hit him and thinks, “This is it. I die now, and I am a hero.” For a moment, he hears a voice telling him to try and live, and he thinks, “No, this is it. I am going to be a hero!” 

Unla Kee is also hit by the lightning bolt and is struck dead. 

Vardis is practically at death’s door from the other dragon. The cleric desperately tries to heal his friend but is struck dead before he has a chance. 

Thistle flies out and fires two arrows. The 3HPP is down to him, and he’s not powerful enough to kill two adult dragons. 

“Maybe I can call the SEER now?” 

This is it. He came this close to saving his love, but his unwillingness to be patient, gather allies, and rush in means he will fail. 

The bitter taste of failure stings. 

For a moment, he’s tempted to blame the damned circlet of intelligence. Perhaps that thing was a curse?  

The dragons turn to him, and he prepares to fight. If this is how it ends, he won’t go down without some kind of fight. 

But before anyone can take action, a portal appears, and a gargantuan blue dragon steps through it. 

Thistle recognizes this creature from the Maelstrom. It’s Iymrith. 

He realizes that it’s over. He closes his eyes. Aims his arrows at her in one final valiant and pointless gesture of rebellion. 

And she starts clapping. 

He notices cultists who jump off her back and rush to heal Orsik, Unla Kee, and Vardis. 

He relaxes his bow. 

Orsik realizes he is alive. Never has a dwarf been so angry to be alive. “I WAS GOING TO BE A HERO! I WAS GOING TO DIE AND BE A HERO, AND YOU SAVED ME! I WAS THREE SECONDS AWAY FROM BEING A HERO!!!!!” 

The cultist who healed him walks away as quickly as he can. 

Iymrith stands on her two hind legs and paces around the area, her giant frame wrecking the remaining walls. Her body causes the sand to shake and redistribute, uncovering old floors and covering others. 

Her size is immense. The two hybrids are nothing compared to her. Iymrith is not just a dragon. She is a legendary force. 

Orsik mutters, “I had such a clever plan. Such a clever plan.” 
But Iymrith is ignoring him. She is talking to Thistle, “My dear boy, Thistle, it’s so good to see you.” Thistle glares at her, and she ignores him. “For centuries, I have been working on a new hybrid dragon. These creatures that you fought are deadly, but they are mindless. I have to keep them in the desert to keep out unwanted guests. I wanted something better. I wanted something that had the innocence of a fairy and the destructive power of draconic kind.” 

“I want her back.” 

The dragon smiles. “Of course you do, and I could not think of anything more amazing than you having her back. And you will.” 

She stretches her neck, and her massive head approaches Orsik - “My dear dwarf, you are everything I was promised you would be.” 

“By the SEER?” asks a dubious Thistle.

“I don’t work with her. She’s the real power of the Lord’s Alliance. She’s committed to a long-term plan to end the freedom of creatures like me to do as we please. She wants to impose an order. Orsik is right to distrust her. She is evil.” 

Orsik looks at her and says, “I WAS THREE SECONDS AWAY FROM DYING AND BEING A HERO.” 

But she ignores him in a soft voice, or at least what passes as a soft voice for an ancient power such as herself, “Every so often, I call a meeting of the five clans. The heads of the various chromatic dragons and I show them my latest creations. It would be awesome if you four fought Laela at this festival.” 

There is a gasp.

“No, I don’t want you to kill her. I want you to prove she is a Fey Dragon hybrid and as lethal as I claim she is.” 

“And I can leave afterward with her?” 

Iymrith seems taken aback and says, “I am a scientist. Of course, you can leave. I am not a slave keeper. I am an artist. She’s a creation of mine. I just need a demonstration to show my critics and patrons that their support in my experiments and investigations was worthwhile.” 

She pauses and, with almost casual indifference, says, “Or I could kill you all right now.” 

Thistle thinks and shouts, “This meets my goals.” 

When Orsik hears that a dragon might kill him, he thinks, “I CAN STILL BE A HERO!” He decides he must stop the fairy. 

“Your girlfriend is some kind of freak fairy-dragon hybrid, and you still want to be with her?” 

Thistle will not hear this, “My goal is to rescue her from Iymrith.” 

“But it’s not her; she’s like some dragon hybrid,” he says, pointing to the monstrous hybrids. 

Thistle begins to waver - “I have to see her.” 

Iymrith sighs, “The whole point of the experiment was to create a creature that could exist simultaneously in the feywild and the prime material plane. The creature could swap its physical form by forming an interplanar link and swapping out its body. Laela is still very much a fairy. She has a squeaky voice and always complains about her wingtips needing wingmuffs. The dragon would be the ultimate infiltration force. The shapeshift would not be magical. It would be intrinsic to their nature. Furthermore, as a fairy, no magical detection would work.” 

Orsik can feel his chance slipping, “She’s an abomination.” 

Thistle is upset, “Why are you calling her an abomination? We haven’t seen her. Like, how do you know she’s an abomination.” 

“She’s been transformed into an evil dragon.” 

Vardis, sitting in the sand, looks at the two arguing and shouts to Orsik, “It’s been a long dry spell. He’ll take anything.” 

“It’s been three years,” pleads Orisk. He wants Iymrith to kill him. He wants to be a HERO. 

Iymrith can see Thistle wavering - “I just need your help to show how effective she is as a dragon.” 

Orsik says, “No. I am not helping you. This is not about being a hero. Helping a dragon is not heroic.” 

Thistle pauses, “I am a good person, and although this meets my goals, it is questionable.” 

The dragon stares, “I believe there has been a misunderstanding about my goals. I infiltrated the dragons to get investors.”

“You started a draconic civil war that has caused the realms to go into chaos.” 

“I needed investors, and when you need investors, you do what you must do.” 
Thistle chimes in, “We had investors. Some of them weren’t good people. I can relate.” 

Orsik thinks momentarily and asks, “Who are the investors?” 

“Important people.” 

She realizes that’s not enough, “A variety of important people.” 

Orisk stares at her and shrugs, “Look, we are going to do this demonstration in front of them, so it’s not like we won’t see them.” 

Iymrith exhales, “Of course, of course. Well, there are the various giant lords whom I have been playing off. They thought I was a giant, but now - thanks to you - know I am a dragon.” She pauses and whines, “It’s why I need this. Things will get ugly if they don’t see results, and you and your girlfriend will not live.” 

Orsik can sense she wants to say more, “And a delegation of Red Wizards.” 

“That can’t go bad.” 

“And a representative of the Unseelie court.” Iymrith looks at Thistle, hoping he won’t hear. But when she speaks, the mountains tremble.

“Now you have gone too far,” shouts Thistle, who cocks his bow. 

“And the Seelie court.” 

Thistle stares, “Did you say the Seelie court?” 

Iymrith looks genuinely surprised, “You didn’t know that? You thought I was experimenting with her subjects in Queen Titania’s realm, and she didn’t know or approve?” She laughs. A tear falls from her face and splashes on the ground. “There’s a reason you were kicked out of the Seelie court.”

Thistle puts his arrow into his quiver and mutters, “It checks out.” 

Iymrith is relieved. “Look, I need this. And if you do this, the experiment will be declared a success, and we will all get to go home. The contracts will be satisfied, and I will get to leave.” 

Orsik says, “It seems to me that you being in breach of contract is far better for us.” 

The dragon pauses, puts a claw to its mouth, and then sits down. “I will still escape. You’ll be dead. Dragons will be fighting. Laela will be imprisoned and used in some horrible way. She will probably use it to make more hybrids. Or if you do this, you rescue the girl, make Thistle happy, stick it to the Seelie and Unseelie courts.” 

“Not heroic,” says Orsik. 

“But everyone gets what they want?” 

“You misread me.” 

“You’re right. You want to be dead.” 

“I would have been a hero! All you had to do was delay arrival for three seconds.” 

Iymrith is frustrated, “Can someone please tell him to be quiet?” 

The cleric who healed Orsik is worried that Iymrith may not be happy with their performance on the battlefield. 

Orsik shouts to Thistle, “Let’s talk.” 

Iymrith smiles and walks away. 

“Let’s say yes and then use the time to rescue your girlfriend.” 

“Works for me.” 

Thistle shouts, “We agree, but I must see proof of life.” 

Iymrith pulls out a sending stone and hands it to him. It’s a tiny thing in her gargantuan claws. 

And then he hears Laela’s voice - “That’s you?” You’re alive?” 

“I’ve come to get you!” 

“You’ll get yourself killed. And where the hell have you been? I’ve been looking all over for you.” 

“I’ve been looking for you!” 

Vardis notes, “If she’s been looking for you, Thistle, that’s not consistent with Iymrith’s version of the facts.” 

Iymrith notes, “One last question.” 

“Where are you?”

“In a cage in Iymrith’s lair.” 

Vardis, annoyed, blurts out, “That you have been looking all over.” 

Laela is outraged, “Who’s that annoying person? Why is there an elf? Are you working with elves now? I thought we said we don’t like elves!” 

“I needed helpers,” whimpers Thistle. 

The voice is cut off. 

Iymrith picks up the stone and says, “Fine, she’s not working for me. I found her. She torched a caravan as a dragon. She was in her dragon form. I captured her. But now she refuses to switch into her fey form. She practices this powerful form of fey meditation, fey-fullness.” 

Thistle smiles, “She has to be one with nature.” 

Orsik laughs, “I get it. The secret is that she’s always angry!” 

Iymrith and Thistle glare at him. The dragon looks glum, “I admit it. I can’t get her to turn into a dragon. Everybody believes I have lost the mojo. They think I am not the great Iymrith. I -need- to show them that I still got it.” 

Orsik looks at her, “For an all-powerful dragon, you sure have a self-confidence issue.” 

Vardis asks, “Iymrith is trying to get her groove back. Can’t you just breathe lightning on everyone?” 

The dwarf adds, “Like I am more confident than you.” 

Iymrith suddenly takes on the posture of a terrifying ancient blue dragon and looks at him, “I am convinced of my powers as a dragon. I’m good in my skin. I just want to be known as something more than just another powerful dragon.” She pauses and cocks her head up, “I want to be known as an artiste, a creator. I need this. It will validate my work and my credentials. I won’t just be the Dragon that got lucky with drakes. ”

Orsik turns to Vardis, “Lots of parallels with Sandesyl’s experiments.” 

The group confers momentarily, and Orsik says, “Okay, so where does this happen?” 

“At the coliseum.” 

“The what?” 

She draws a map on the ground, a map of stunning detail and precision. 



“As I said, Laela emerges from the hole in the ground. You guys go for a few rounds, and then you go.” 

“Does she have to appear as a dragon from the hole?” 

“No, she just needs to turn into a dragon.” 

Unla Kee, who has been drinking, shouts, “This seems like an incredibly bad idea.” He pauses and then shouts, “I’m in.” 

Orsik thinks, “We’ll have to talk to her before the event, probably for a few hours. And figure out why she’s not turning into a dragon. We don’t want her to get nervous.” 

Vardis coughs, “And have some performance anxiety.” 

Orisk continues, “You don’t want it to go badly. Can we make it go well by having a short conversation?” 
Iymrith is exhausted and frustrated. She plops on her belly, puts her claws over her head, and then stands up on her hind legs. She stretches her neck to its full extent, roars, and then lets loose a bolt of lightning that overwhelms the sun. 

She starts screaming, “I SHOULD NEVER HAVE DONE THIS EXPERIMENT!!!!” She then pleads to Thistle, “How was I supposed to know that combining Dragons and Fairies would create this much drama?” 

“Perhaps a play?” asks Thistle. 

Orsik realizes his plan: “Great, then we are agreed. We’ll come a few days early, have a script, and work with Laela. We will have some dialogue. We want a top performance.” 

Iymrith is confused. 

Thistle says, “And we need two skin-tight unitards.” 

Orsik says, “You, of course, see the need, Iymrith?” 

The dragon looks confused. 

“Of course you do. I was being presumptuous. You understand what we are doing and have thought of it before. We’re just repeating the details for our benefit.”

The dragon agrees, “Of course I had, but keep repeating the details so I can make sure you missed nothing.” 

Orsik turns to Thistle - “How many rehearsals? A few hours might not be good enough.” 

Vardis notes, “The last time we rehearsed that little, we got Bob.” 

“Right, we need more time. We should come days in advance.” 

The dragon tries to object, but Orsik continues, “We’ll need time to focus on our craft. We can’t be distracted. There might be other things in your lair, and we need focus.” 

Thistle adds, “We believe in method acting. You have to be in the location. And if you are not, you can’t sense the experience.” 

Iymrith, desperate and thinking this idea of a play might just work, says excitedly, “YES!” 

Orsik whispers to Thistle, “Anything else?” 

Thistle turns to Orsik, “I don’t have the headband of intellect.” 

Thistle shouts, “We’re good.” 

The cleric who healed Orisk is relieved. There was a possibility that Orsik’s death would be a mercy killing to what the cleric would have had to suffer. 

“Well, it’s a three-day hike to my lair.” 

Orsik asks, “Can’t you fly us? Or teleport us?

Iymrith looks at him contemptuously, “You can’t teleport because the coliseum has been magically protected from Scrying and Teleport. And as for flying on me, only those who worship my greatness can fly on me. But these abominations can carry you.” 

“You have many worshippers?” 

“Enough. I have people who see me as the pinnacle of my type. They recognize my creative, artistic, and scientific brilliance.” 

“Think how many more you will have after the performance!” 

Thistle stares at her briefly to see what he can learn. And what he realizes is that Iymrith is indeed different from other dragons. She has tools she appears to be using. And equipment she seems to be wearing. But even for all her power, and the wonder of her tools, she is in a jam. Even a creature as powerful as her can make one too many deals. 

Thistle mutters, “Why not just eat the Red Wizards?”

“Because they are the minions! I made a deal with Szaas Tham.” She knew it was a mistake and pleaded, “I needed investors.” 

Orsik can’t keep his mouth shut and asks, “Don’t you have a hoard? Why did you self-fund this initiative?” 

Iymrith suddenly looks angry, horrified, and confused and roars, “SPEND. MY. HOARD!”
