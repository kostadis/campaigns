# Chapter 80 The end of Sandesyl and Blagothkus

The cavernous room is lit brilliantly; the ice shakes with a roaring sound, and then silence. 

Orsik shrugs and looks inside the chest. There are thousands of gold coins and hundreds of platinum pieces. “Sandesyl chose to live in squalor,” he thinks. 

As he dumps the coins into his bag of holding, he finds a ring. 

He looks at it for a bit and hands it to Vardis. Vardis looks at it and puts it into his pocket. 

After emptying the last of her treasure, Orsik stretches his back and says, “We should see the Dracolich.” 

“Excuse me,” shouts the cloud giant. “I need some healing.” 

Thistle observes that he doesn’t think he turns to mist when brought to death’s door and, therefore, could use some healing himself. 

Vardis sighs and mutters, “Fine, let’s camp and find this undead dragon.” 

Thistle mutters, “We have two hours before the vampire reforms.” 

An hour later, they stand in the glacier's center, inside Glazhael the Undying's lair. She is surprised to see them living. 

“Sandesyl is dead?” 

“Mostly. We need your help to finish the job. Do you know where her lair is?” 

The dracolich smiles, and its eyes glow, “Yes. Do any of you have the magic to break through this ice?”

Unla Kee casts a firebolt. Glazhael rolls her eyes and sighs. She walks up to the same location and flicks her tail at the wall. The wall shatters, and behind it is a plain-looking coffin.

“Get me my phylactery.” 

Thistle is uncertain, “How do we know this is the right coffin?” 

“She’ll be inside the coffin. Open it, and you will find my phylactery. Once I have my phylactery, I will leave and you can do whatever you want.” 

Orsik and Vardis agree, “It was the deal.” 

Unla Kee can’t wait anymore. He opens the coffin, and there is the perfectly preserved corpse of Sandesyl, along with a small box inlaid with gold and locked shut. 

As he hands the box to Glazhael, Orsik asks her, “How do we kill a vampire?” 

Glazhael laughs, “I don’t know.” 

As he watches her leave, Unla Kee mutters - “I know.” 

Orsik turns to him, “Wooden stake through the heart? Holy wafers? Sunlight?” 

Unla Kee shakes his head. “Obviously, you went to a second-rate institution for your studies in necromancy, Orsik. At my alma mater, we learned that that was all rumors and superstitions. A vampire simply needs to be killed in its coffin. The how is irrelevant.”

Orsik shrugs.

Unla Kee examines Sandesyl and considers the power he could have if he were a vampire. He thinks he could manipulate her using telekinesis. 

But before he can, Orsik notices what the halfling is up to, pushes him asid, and stabs the vampire. 

The 3HPP and Blagothkus are in the control room a little bit later. There is Brunvild and a furious Escarlotta. 

“This is all your fault. You made a deal with dragons!” 

Brunvil adds, “And a vampire!” 

“And a vampire!” 

Escarlotta has heard from Brunvild the real version of how Blagothkus reacquired his castle and is unhappy. 

Orsik wants to kill Blagothkus because he doesn’t trust him. Thistle agrees but wants to make sure that Escarlotta doesn’t shut down the castle. He then remembers the ghost in the Eye of the All-Father. 


He begins, “Blagothkus, when we were,” and pauses for emphasis as if he can’t remember, “Where was it? Oh, yes, the oracle. We did run across this ghost that said he was trapped for all eternity.” 

Escarlotta’s gems form two narrow slits, and two red gems act as pupils that pulsate and stare at Blagothkus. 

Thistle continues, “I believe his name was Eigeron.”

“Our son!!! Is a ghost? What does he need to find peace?!” 

“He sent us here. He wanted revenge on the person who turned him into a ghost.” 

Escarlotta shouts, “Who?” 

Thistle let’s the question hang. 

Blagothkus whimpers, “That’s not what happened. It was an accident.” 

Escarlotta screams in rage, “Kill him! FREE MY SON!!!!” 
Orsik shrugs, “Happy to oblige.” 

Before Blagothkus can react, Vardis strikes him with a guiding bolt, Orsik smites him with the sword, Thistle pierces his critical arteries, Unla Kee burns him with electricity, and Brunvild finishes the job with one of Blagothkus’ old hammers. 

Escarlotta sobs. 

At this point, Brunvild tries to make a case for the castle being his because of salvage rights. Orsik, having killed one annoying giant, is itching to kill another. Brunvild can see the danger and mutters, “I salvaged the castle. It doesn’t belong to me, I just want … ” 

As the two of them get closer to blows, Escarlotta shrieks, “THIS IS MY CASTLE!” 

The room becomes silent. 

“You are passengers on my castle. You saved my son. So you have earned safe passage on my  castle.” 

Thistle mutters, “Delighted to be your guests!” 

Brunvild backs out of the room and mutters, “I’ll just find a nice place to sleep. Big castle, after all!” 

Before Orsik can offer any other helpful suggestions, Escarlotta’s gem forms two hands that are clasped together and, in the town of a disapproving mother, says - “Have you forgotten your mission?” 

“Put on a play?Frame Silverymoon for looting the Uthgardtian artifacts? 
“No, find Hekaton, defeat the blue dragon, save Thistle’s beloved?” 

Orsik can’t resist, “Uncover the plot of the Margasters?”  

She growls, “HEKATON is the priority. We have a clue. It’s in Yartar, and we are going there.” 

Later that day Orsik is looking down at the clouds and below that the Evermoors. He muttes to Thistle who is next to him, “It’s not that fast.” 

Escarlotta overhears him, “Are you saying something about my speed?” 

Thistle, who can sense that she is getting annoyed, mutters, “ No, we are not saying anything about the speed of your castle. It’s a fantastic castle!” 

The sun has set, and below are the lights of Yartar shimmering in the dark. The Grand Dame, is still at dock, getting ready to set sail for it’s nightly voyage. Escarlotta shouts, “So what’s the plan? Are we going to land and sink the boat? Are we going to sneak around? Queen Serissa’s clue isn’t -that- helpful. I mean, a wooden coin from a gambling casino? It could be a coincidence.” 

Thistle shouts, “We shall sneak. Take a position up here, and I will fly down.” 

Unla Kee watches him fly down and remembers that there are vampires in Yartar. “Perhaps that’s a way for me to achieve immortality. To what end? I have seen the end of the world, and I can save the world. Would being a vampire change anything?” He shakes his head. “No, it would not.” 

A few hours later, Thistle arrives back on the castle deck. 

“It’s a regular boat.” 

It’s nighttime, and they have time before the morning. 

“The problem is that we have this tenuous clue, a chip from a casino. We need to find a way to get someone to talk, ideally the ship’s owner,, Lord Khaspere Drylund.”

Vardis agrees with his friend, “We need to get him to talk.” 

Unla Kee asks, “What if we sank the boat and stole all the money from the bottom of the sea?” 

Thistle remarks, “Yes, but that would get in the way of us saving my girlfriend and defeating Adimond Kroskas. So first we get the information, then we can discuss sinking boats.” 

Orsik pats Unla Kee on the back, “We have 75,000 gold pieces in our treasure room. Let’s get down there and gamble some of it away. Lord Drylund wouldn’t let us in the last time; now he will beg to let us gamble on his Grand Dame!”

Thistle thinks, “One roll of the dice, and it will be my Grand Dame.”
