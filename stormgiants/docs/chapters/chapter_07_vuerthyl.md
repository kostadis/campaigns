# Chapter 07  - Vuerthyl
## On the road 1/4 of Taraskh
The road to Parnast is not well-traveled. Once controlled by the Zhentarim, the Cult of the Dragon took over in the events leading up to the Battle at the Well of the Worlds but then abandoned the route after Tiamat’s entry into the North was blocked. 
The Zhentarim attempted to reassert control over the Black Road through great desert Anauroch, but their hold remains tenuous. 
Our heroes were crossing the Dawn pass, the easiest route through the Greypeak Mountains on the Grayvale Run road, when they stumbled on a massive pair of black bears that saw them as a tasty snack.

Thistle
To be fair, Thistle didn’t entirely stumble on them. He spotted them and had time to cast “Speak with Animals” so he could ask, “Hello! How goes it in bear land?” 
“HUNGRY!” they both shout. 
Meanwhile, Thistle’s pet bear hugs him, saying, “Mommy!” 
The fairy thinks and then casts a goodberry spell and drops the berries in two separate piles near them. 
The bears rush up, swallow the five berries each, sit down, patting their full bellies, and purr contentedly. 
Fed and happy, Thistle asks them questions about dangers on the road east. The bears can only say that significant threats are coming from behind them. When the ranger tries to ask what, they keep saying danger. 
He knows danger is ahead, and he is determined to find it. 
For the next few hours, Thistle scans the horizon, looking for danger. As the road winds downwards to the hilly dry country, the weather changes, from cold and moist, to cold and dry. Vegetation gets sparser, and the dirt is dry; Thistle recognizes this as the rain shadow of the Greypeak mountains. 
And then, out of the dusty hills, two scorpions emerge, racing towards him and his friends, co-travelers, some guys he picked up and can’t shake off, allies in his holy crusade to find the love of his life and get vengeance, he’s still undecided.
Orsik shouts to him, “we could run?”
“But then they would get the next travelers.” 
“When did we start caring about the next travelers?” 
Thistle grunts and fires an arrow at the lead scorpion. The scorpion charges straight into Vardis, who flanks the creature and lands a hard blow on the monster. Orsik brings it to near-death letting Unla Kee kill it with a magic missile. 
The second scorpion charges at Orsik and misses both with its claws and stinger. Vardis seeing his friend in danger flanks the creature and smashes it again with his sword. 
The scorpion surrounded by Unla Kee, Orsik, and Vardis lashes out at each of them. Orsik is grappled, and Vardis is struck, poisoned, and almost dead forever. All that time drinking booze with Orsik taught his endocrine system to digest poisons of various kinds more efficiently, so he could survive what should have killed him. 
Orsik screams, “VARDIS!” 
Thistle sees what happened and flies a little higher to be out of range of the stinger. 
With Vardis down, the creature grabs the grappled Orsik and flees to the hills. But the beast is killed when Orsik smashes its skull and exults in his vengeance. 
“Let’s track them to their nest!” 
“Really?” 
The trail leads towards the desert, not towards Parnast, and Thistle says, “another day.” 
## Welcome to Parnast 1/7 of Taraskh

The town or village or hamlet of Parnast existed as the last settlement before the Black Road. Control was a source of power for the Zhentarim and later the Cult. But now, it lies abandoned. The village has a few dozen houses and a small square, with buildings that include a tavern, a stable, and a shrine. A small group of locals stares at the adventurers distrustfully. 
Vardis examples the temple and mumbles, “Shrine of Axes!” 
Orsik notes the absence of guards, sighs, and then smiles when he sees the tavern’s name, “The Golden Tankard!” 
But the sealed doors of what look like stables draw everyone’s attention. 
Orsik knocks on the door, and a dangerous roar comes from inside. 
Vardis dismisses the sound, “you knocked over something,” and prepares to enter.
Thistle shouts, “It’s a wyvern!” 
From the temple, an elderly elven woman approaches – “ are you planning to deal with the wyvern?” The group shrugs. “Oh, well, if you’re not planning to deal with it, would you mind not going in there. After they were defeated, you see, the Cult of the dragon left this Wyvern in the, uh, in the stables.” 
The ranger perks up. Vengeance against the Cult is possible! He asks, “Oh, so this is a minion of the cults of the dragon. And you are looking for someone to take care of it? 

“Oh, no, no. We’re looking for you to stop knocking on the door. The problem is that the last person to go in there ended up getting injured and moaning and dying and it was really awkward as we had to listen to them begging for help and healing. The Wyvern took days to kill him.” 
Thistle is now intrigued and asks about the situation, wondering if it would be possible to kill the creature from a distance when the elf explains that the Wyvern is chained to the ground. She laughs at him and explains that the chain is something Wyvern can easily break. And the only reason the Wyvern hasn’t broken the chain is that the villagers keep feeding it. 

She opens a slit into the stables, and everyone takes a turn looking into a wide-open area with dead carcasses picked clean. 
Thistle explains that they could kill it from afar. The elf is aghast and explains that that would put the entire village at risk because it might escape. 
“If you want to go kill it, you must go into the stables; that way, the Wyvern will think of them as food.”
She then realizes that this group is really determined to kill the Wyvern and switches gear, “We like the Wyvern. And it simplifies prison sentences. The threat of the Wyvern is good enough to keep the peace here.” 
The group stares at the creature and decides that this is a monster for another day. 
The elf looks at this strange group and asks, “if it’s work you are looking for, then I have a job. A merchant Azam is stuck in Vuerthyl, bringing back a religious artifact that we sent into the desert during the war. The middle person, Fiora, said she would find us some mercenaries.” 
Thistle glares at her; he knows she is keeping information from him.
“I want to know about the cult.”
“And I want my idol.” 
“I want to know about Adimond Kruskas.” 
“And I want my idol, and what information I may have is only to be exchanged when I have my idol.” 
They glare at each other, and then Thistle backs off grumbling, “she isn’t going to talk until we get her idol.” 
She walks off, and Thistle glares while Orsik moves to the center of the town. He notices the empty shrine and a wheelwright shop to go along with the stables and tavern. 
He takes in the town and gets the distinct impression that this town is used to having a faction controlling the village and the locals profiting from them. They are neutral to all conquerors, as long as they can go about their business and trade. 
The elf stares at the group once more and then sighs and marches up, “the cult of the dragon brought a big flying castle over here to move a lot of treasure.” 
Orsik stares – “that’s the castle that crashlanded near Waterdeep that convinced everyone that there was some deeper, more sinister plot and led to the alliance to defeat the Cult at the Well of the Worlds.” 
“I need a drink,” he says and marches to The Golden Tankard. Outside he notices a yellow tankard over the door. He smirks, steps inside, and sees the gloom, “false advertising,” he thinks. 
And then he notices the quiet as all conversation stops and all eyes turn in his direction. 
The silence is broken by a tall, heavyset man with enormous muttonchop sideburns steps forward and asks, “What can I get you, travelers?”
“We’re on our way to pick up on an idol for your local cleric.” 
“Chandra Stol? She’s been looking for fools to get her idol for weeks. Azam won’t come because of dangers.” 
He pauses, “I have already spoken to you for two minutes, and you haven’t bought anything.” 
“What’s on tap?” 
“A copper for ale and a silver piece for the mead.”
Vardis turns to Orsik, “we could buy the place. A nice turnkey operation?” 
The barkeep’s till a bit fuller puts him in a good mood, and he explains that business has been slow as very few folks have come from the east. He remarks, “many more going east.” 
Orsik and Vardis show him the treasure map, and he sighs and says, “yes, yes! I know that place.”
They get excited. “Yeah, that’s gotta be the silver marches or the spine of the world.” He then thinks more, “or maybe the spine of the world, that’s where the biggest mountains are.” Orsik knows where this is going, “the Icespires are a good mountain range! Or maybe it’s the Nethermountains!”
“So basically somewhere in the North?” 
“Yes!” 
Orsik groans, “here’s a gold piece for your information.”
The keeper’s eyes light up. As he picks up the gold, he says, “well, for that kind of generosity, I’ll give my finest sausage and cabbage. And never let it be said that Ragnar Redtooth isn’t grateful to those who do him a kindness!” 
“Kindness when paid,” snarks Vardis.
“I’m good with that. I’m transactional,” says Orsik. 
They both nod. 
Of course, when Ragnar charges them 5 gold for a dingy, smelly room and another five for the use of sheets, their enthusiasm for the new friend ends. 
“You’re treating us like marks!” 
“When a poor man sees a fool throwing coins around, he’s a fool if he doesn’t try to get some more.”
The next day our heroes head out to Vuerthyl on the Black Road.
Arriving at Vuerthyl 2/1 of Taraskh

The journey east is peculiarly safe and eerily quiet. The trip through the desert is hot and miserable. Massive dunes roll across the landscape in every direction, and an even bigger sky looms overhead.  
Finally, up ahead is the oasis of Vuerthyl, and around it, a motley collection of sun-bleached tents. Looking around, Orsik, who has a nose for these things, finds the tent that passes for a tavern. 
A pair of tieflings, unaffected by the heat, approach him warily as he enters. 
As his eyes adjust to the dim interior, he feels the relief of no more sun or wind, even if it is still hot. 
He looks around and takes in the sounds and sights. A stringed instrument fills the air, and people are hunched over food and conversation.
Just then, a Dragonborn waves to Orsik and guides him to a private table. Orsik asks about Azam, and the waiter nods and says, “he will be here shortly.” 
The Dragonborn waiter delivers water and dates, olive, and bread. 
And then, soon enough, a lean half-elven man with a long, craggy face and dark skin and hair that shows his Rashemi ancestry sits down. 
Vardis notices his impressive mustache.
Unla Kee notices a golden-scaled pseudodragon that lays draped around his shoulders. 
It stirs, blinks with milky irises, and sniffs the air. The creature gives the man a nod and then shifts its wings and falls back to sleep.
The man gestures at the pseudodragon, “Hsing, my patron is most pleased you have come. We have a business to discuss, and hopefully, you are ready for a long journey. I am Azam, and we would like you to deliver a statue to Parnast.” 
Thistle is curious, “what kind of statue?” 
He sighs, “the statue for the Shrine of Axes was made in the Anauroch desert by artisans here. It’s not small, and it’s not light. I don’t know if it’s magical, but I don’t really care. I’ve been paid to transport it securely, and that is what I plan to do.” 
Orsik wonders if he can see it. Azam gives Hsing, the golden scaled pseudodragon, a questioning glance, and the little dragon nods his head in a regal gesture of assent. Azam takes the characters to a worn wagon where a stout-looking half-orc woman carefully wraps the statue in canvas for its journey. 
Azam explains that the statue is of a complicated piece of woodwork by an artisan who has exiled himself to the Anauroch desert. “Never understood why the need to make it this big,” he sighs. “It’s five feet tall, four feet wide, and very heavy.” 
Vardis examines the statue and realizes it is the Triune Queen, the threefold deity Angharradh. Examining the base, he finds an inscription 

*“Unity and diversity bring strength. Be ever vigilant against She Who Was Banished and work together in defending the lands of the Fair Folk from those who would work evil. Celebrate the One and the Three for their collective purpose and individual expressions of life. Through the melding of widely different skills and interests, creativity, life, and artistry are nurtured, and new ideas are discovered.” *
“This is the wisdom of Angharradh,” says Vardis as he feels a calming presence. 
Thistle snarks, “fair folk? I presume it means the fey.” 
“Elves.” 
Vardis looks at him, “Well, the elves are part fey.” 
“The better part.” 
Vardis rolls his eyes, and everyone else stares at Thistle, who feels like he said something awkward. 
“Fine.” 
Azam turns to the group, “My patron Hsing has more work for you if you complete the mission.”
Thistle laughs, “if we are dead, I guess then no more missions?”
“No, if you don’t bring the statue. Although Hsing has been known to forgive.” 
Hsing opens his eyes, “when there is a seed, it must be let to grow before it is killed.” 
Azam nods, “as my patron so wisely said.” 
At this point, Thistle wants answers, “have you seen a Drake named Thule?” 
Azam is taken aback at this, but Hsing smiles and says, “where there is the yellow sky, there is the drake you are looking for.”
