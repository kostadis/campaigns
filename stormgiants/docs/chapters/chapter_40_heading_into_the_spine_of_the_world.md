# Chapter 40 - Heading into the Spine of the World
## Harshnag the Grim
The frost giant sits under a tree, pondering the events he has heard. To the North, the Frost Giant Jarl has been laying waste to settlements as he searches for some magical artifact that will allow him to turn all of the lands of the North into Ice. To the east, he hears tales of the Fire Giant Duke, who wants to build and repair a magical device that will give him the power to master the dragons. To the south, he has met refugees that speak of the mad Thane who wants to wipe out all the non-Giant races and has already laid waste to many towns near Orlbar and Llorkh. 
As for the cloud giants, they are being mighty secretive and hiding in their clouds. “And as for the Hill Giants, surely no one should take them as a serious threat,” he thinks. 
In the distance, he spots the 3HPP advancing toward him; he gets up, stamps out the fire, and smiles.
“We must go to the eye of the All-Father, now.”
“Nope. We intend to go to Mirabar Mines  to check out the mines.” 
Harshnag is stunned. 
“Look, if you folks don’t care about your people being destroyed, then Krowen Valharrow has allied with the wrong people.”
“No, we care, but we want to stop by the Mines first.”
At this point, the half-elf interrupts, “Where are we going?”
When Harshnag is done talking, Vardis tells him – “We could go south over the pass, or we could go through this valley and climb up to the Eye of the All-Father?” 
He scratches his head, looks at his map, and says, “Yes, that might work.” 
“So great the mine is on the way,” shouts Orsik. 
Harshnag sighs. 
## Orsik
A day later, Orsik arrives at the Mines of Mirabar. It’s not the mines but more like the picket fence and the guard post at the edge of the land that may contain the Mines of Mirabar. 
A guard approaches and informs them that they can advance only further with the appropriate passes. 
A very cold Thistle shouts, “I need to buy some wing muffs! I NEED TO BUY SOME WING MUFFS!” 
Another gruff-looking guard stares at Thistle and grunts – “Wing muffs!” Clearly, this second guard doesn’t think such a thing should be a thing. 
Orsik looks at the guard and then, doing his best – “Hey, I am the hero of Mirabar because I caught the weevil routine,” convinces the guard to get the Foreman, who in turn is convinced that Orsik has been properly paid for his services. 
“You got paid for your work. Now please go.” 
Orsik is aghast. He gathers his wits and asks with a hint of desperation, tinged with malice, “Perhaps you need a bounty hunter?” 
The foreman stares at Orsik, and something twinkles in his eye, “I may need it in the morning. Why don’t you have dinner tonight in the lodge, and we can speak in the morning.” 
As the 3HPP enter the compound, their weapons are handed to the guards. 
## Thistle 
Thistle is cold. The Feywild is not a cold place. Well, Queen Tatiana is cold, but that’s more about her heart than the actual temperature. And it’s a cool kind of cold, not a disrespectful kind of cold. 
He really needs some wing-tip warmers. 

As he marches into the lodge, he notices its size. There are at least 200 men in one vast and tall room. The room is heated thanks to several fireplaces lit with magic because there is no smoke. Everyone is eating ravenously, apparently having returned from a shift in the mines. 
Thistle approaches one of the miners, a dwarf named Fruf. 
“What do you need?”
“Wing muffins.”
“Wing, what?” 
“Wingtip muffins.” 
“Well, I don’t have any of those, but I have some extra socks.” 
Thistle stares at the warm poofy socks and thinks – “if only I had someone who could craft them.” And then he remembers that one of the half-pints has demonstrated a remarkable knack for making things. And he turns to Orsik, who sighs, “Fine.” 
## Vardis
Later that evening, the cleric of Corellon Larethian, Vardis, is interrupted in his drinking when another elf taps on his shoulder. 
“Are you a cleric of Corellon?” 
Vardis, whose relationship with the Cormiira, the college of the high-ranking priests, is so toxic that he’s not welcome in any temple of his god, is uncertain.
“I have a relationship with that god.”
The elf smiles and makes a sign with his hand.
Vardis, unsure about what it means, says, “Peace be with you.” 
At that point, the elf stiffens. 
Orsik turns and says, “he’s from Waterdeep.” 
“A progressive cleric of Larethian,” the elf spits out the words and then says, “I am not even sure you are a cleric of our god.” 
Vardis shrugs as the elf turns away.
Orsik looks at him, “what was that about?”
“Look, I don’t know how it’s with you, Dwarves, but Elves live a long time, and there is a lot of interpretation of religious symbols, influences, and rituals, and we don’t all agree. Some of us believe that the great war that split the elves was a mistake, and others think that we have not done enough to end the great war.”
Vardis stares at his friend and knows Orsik didn’t hear a single word. 
## The Foreman
“I need to get rid of these yahoos. I know the type pretending to be interested in helping. For all I know, they are agents of the Weevil, or maybe he told them things,” the Foreman thinks as the 3HPP approach him in the early morning. 
The one called Orsik is smiling. 
“So?” 
The Foreman stares and remembers how Orsik claimed he was responsible for getting Weevil to reveal the details of the caravan code last night. 
He had spoken to the Ax using the unique sending stones and learned that was an exaggeration. 
Orsik had told them about Weevil’s offer, but the Ax had extracted the information. 
“Well, yes, I think I have just the job for you.” 
“Oh?”
“There are crag cats a day's hike out.” 
The Frost Giant and the Ranger try to urge Orsik to drop this quest, and the Foreman is upset. “Those crag cats are rather annoying, and I need them gotten rid of,” he thinks.
But the progressive cleric of Lorethainen comes to the rescue when he points out how much money is to be made from their pelts. 
## Into the wild. 
The 3HPP head out of the restricted areas of the mines into the hilly frozen terrain that leads into a valley whose name is unknown to those who live in the warmer climates to the south. The valley sits between two arms of the mountain range known as the Spine of the World that blocks the Reghed Glacier from advancing to the south. 
After a day’s hike, the 3HPP finds a tent with a burning fire and a corpse. Before they can pillage the body, four frost drakes attack them. 

The battle is short and brutal. Three drakes are killed, and one flees. 
“No crag cats,” mutters Orsik, who had already spent the money from the pelts on fine ale in his head. 
But not all is lost because the body has a map with an X on it. 
Harshnag stares at the map and says, “I know where that is. It’s a bit out of the way, but we can get there, and it won’t add too much time to our trip.”
