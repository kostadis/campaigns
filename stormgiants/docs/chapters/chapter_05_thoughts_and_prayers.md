# Chapter 05 - Thoughts and Prayers
## Orsik 6th day of the 1st tenday of Taraksh 

The climb to the highest peak in the Greypeak mountain range is tedious. The weather is a little bit warm, and the sky is a brilliant blue with but trace clouds racing across the sky. 
Orsik stares at the quiet sky and shouts, “there is nothing to excite the nerves!” 
Everyone stares at him. 
“Whaaat?” 
Then Vardis points in the distance, a griffon. 
Orsik stares at the griffon.  
Vardis stares at him. 
Onla Kee grouses, “you do realize that this is your fault? You could have been quiet, but you taunted fate!” 
Orsik turns to the group, “What does he have against us?” 
And then realizes it is not personal as they are a meal to the creature. 
He then dumps his food in a pile, urges everyone else to do the same, and hide. 

At first, there is confusion, “some or all,” they ask. Orsik looks up and realizes that they are four meat sacks, and they must look pretty tasty. There needs to be another bigger meat sack in front of them. So he shouts, “all,” and pretty soon, there is a substantial pile of food that the griffon is drawn to. 
The creature lands in front of the pile. Orsik observes it pecks at the jerky first, then the dried fruit. After it’s eaten most of the food, it purrs contentedly. Then the griffon rubs its belly and flies off. 
Unla Kee turns to him and asks, “do we have to worry about flying poop? Is their poop like a bird’s?” Orsik rolls his eyes but not before Thistle says, “great question. My uncle never saw a griffon poop.” 
## The group  

A few hours later, the group reaches the final push to the peak. The air has gotten thin and cold, and as they pass through a layer of cloud. They emerge into the sunlight. The scene before them is magnificent, an endless sea of white as they stare down at the clouds.
Unla Kee notices some writing on the wall. He casts comprehend languages and notices that they form mantras. Most extol the virtues of Knowledge, Magic, Intelligence, Meditation, and Understanding. 
He sighs, “worthless religious claptrap.” 
Meanwhile, the rest of the group reaches the top of the sloping path. A large brass bell and a cave entrance are at the end of the trail.
As they stand there, four giant owls swoop in to attack. The first owl flies 30’ and attacks Orsik, the dwarf tries to strike as it flies off, but the owl is too tricky. “Ranged weapons,” he shouts.
Orsik hurls two hand-axes, and they fall down into the cloud cover, “useless!” 
Another Owl flies in from the south, strafes Thistle, and misses him. 
The wind howls and the sounds of owls hooting are eerie. 
“Let’s go inside,” shouts Vardis.
“I at least want to try using my bow!” The arrow flies and hits the owl, a severe blow but not a mortal one. 
“I have a plan,” shouts Vardis, who steps as close to the edge as he feels is safe. He pulls out the symbol of Corellon Larethian, a silver circlet, summons his god’s power, and a blast of light burns everywhere. 
One owl hoots one last time and falls to its death.
Another owl seeing the danger that Vardis presents, swoops by to attack, but Vardis dodges it at the last second. The claws of the bird uselessly clutch air. 
The Magic-User stares at the flight patterns of the owls. He realizes that they are flying predictably, probably something they were trained to do. He summons his divination skill to twist the fates in his favor, and two of the birds fly closer than they should. He casts sleep on them. 
Vardis and Orsik cheer as they see two owls fall into the abyss. 
Thistle hits with his bow one more time. Vardis’ new bow is too new, and he misses. 
Vardis, Thistle, and Orsik huddle together, ready to hit the bird if it comes close.
The bird sees Unla Kee 20 feet away and attacks the gnome. The gnome is surprised, and the owl almost deals with a killing blow. 
Unla Kee bellows, “I SURVIVED AN ATTACK! I AM INVINCIBLE!” 
The bird is skewered dead with a magic missile. 
Vardis asks, “I thought owls were friendly!” 
Thistle responds, “well, I noticed that they flew in a particular pattern, suggesting there is some kind of trainer.” 
Unla Kee, meanwhile, is popping good berries. 
Orsik is fed up, charges into the open passageway, and bounces off a magical forcefield. Vardis shouts, “the bell?” 
Unla Kee steps forward and reads a riddle etched into the bell: 

*What doubles when you share it, is unseen but gives power, and is useless unless practiced?*
Everyone has an opinion.
“Music!” observes Thistle. 
“Magic!” shouts Vardis from a safe distance. 
Unla Kee notes, “Friendship? I mean, it’s useless if not practiced. If you don’t practice it, you have no friends.” 
Orsik grouses, “why would giants have a riddle about friendship?”
Unla Kee then remembers the mantras, and Thistle agrees that “Understanding” is the word they want to use.
He turns to Unla Kee and says, “well, I am a little bit tougher than you. I’ll do it.” Unla Kee nods. Thistle waves his hand, “you might want to stand a little further away and not quite as close to the ledge.” 
The bell is rung, the word said, and the force field collapses.
Orsik runs in. 
## Thistle

The tough fairy rushes in with Orsik. The size of the place is enormous. The entrance is big enough for even the largest of giants. 
Walking a short distance, the entrance opens into a massive cavern with 50’ tall ceilings. He stares at it and notices that it has collapsed in a few places, littering the floor with debris and large rocks. 
Orsik nods that the place is safe. 
Once he understands it is safe to enter, he notices four huge braziers made of copper that decorate the room. They appear useable, so he flies up to see if they can be lit, but they are empty with some residue coal and insense. 
The sight of a long stone slab 15-feet high catches his attention from where he is. There is a tattered cloth that decorates the top. 

The decorations on the cloth help bring into focus what he was missing, that the walls were also decorated in similar patterns, but much of the wall paints are ruined.
Orsik and Unla Kee rush to the front while he hangs back near the exit near one of the braziers. 
Orsik looks at him, Unla Kee says, “I take risks.” 
## Unla Kee 

“And I was lucky!” he thinks. All the braziers magically light up. And then, in the south of the room, where Vardis and Thistle were hanging back, two creatures that look like living icicles appear. They shriek and gibber in pain and frustration. Their sharp claws clack. 
The one from the southeastern brazier runs behind Vardis and breathes a blast of icy frost. Thistle is also caught up in the cold wind but manages to hide behind some rocks. 
Vardis is ready to strike when he notices the brazier’s incredible heat that burns his skin. “I need to get away from this.” But he stands his ground and strikes a blow that the creature dodges at the last minute. 

Thistle takes a step to get a better angle on the creature attacking Vardis and misses. 
Unla Kee thinks, “I ventured, and I gained. The world will not end today!” Then casts toll the dead on one of the creatures. It is not over. 
Orsik finishes it off, and it explodes on everyone but Unla Kee. “Lucky again,” he thinks. 
The remaining creature casts fog cloud, making everyone effectively blind. 
Unla Kee sees a flash of magic power streak through the cloud, a shriek from the creature, and a satisfied Vardis standing. 

Unla Kee watches as Orsik climbs. He shouts, “well, there are some gold plates and some dead cultists a few minutes later.” 
“Are they worth a lot?” 
“Yes, Unla Kee, but it would be bad if angered a god. I think there’s the same writing as before.” 
“Would it be less sacrilegious if we melted the gold first?” 
“No!” shouts Vardis. 
“I’ll take them anyways.” 
Meanwhile, Thistle loots the bodies of the cultists and shouts fairy curses.
As the group walks out, they hear the booming voice of a storm giant who speaks with an open-air pipe. 
Thistle whispers to Orsik, “I think he can kill us without breaking a sweat.” 
The giant smiles and says, “I assume you’re not here simply to loot this ancient holy site. You can trust me on what I’m about to say because I have never spoken a truer statement in my life. Annam, the all-father is in no mood to be trifled.” The storm giant chuckles, but you can see a great sadness in his eyes. I am Kraddock. I was told that this shrine was desecrated several days ago, and I’ve come to see what state this place was left in. What brings you here?”
Kraddock then stares at the halfling, trying to hide the large gold plates behind his back. 
Shamelessly Unla Kee declares, “we stopped those bandits from stealing the gold plates.” Kraddock’s face becomes sterner. Meanwhile, the halfling notices that his friends are backing away from him. He tries a different tack, “we’re treasure hunters, and we got this map, and we didn’t think it belonged to anyone.” 
Unla Kee stares at the giant and thinks, “I could twist the fates just enough to make him fear me. Or not. And then it would be a very brief battle. I’m not sure I would call it a battle. But if I don’t take risks, the world may end!” 
“Kraddock, look, we just got here today. This is what it looked like when we got here.” 
“I’m sure that you were just trying to ensure that the gold plates were not stolen.” 
“These gold plates?” 
“Yes, these sacred gold plates.” 
Unla Kee smiles, “why didn’t you say so!” And hands them back to the storm giant. 
“I wasn’t going to kill you. I was just going to invoke a curse of the All-Father.” He takes the plates and carefully shines them. 
## Kraddock 

The storm giant stares at the little people with sadness because he knows that the All-Father has made decisions. And those decisions will affect them. He doesn’t agree with those decisions, but he serves the All-Father.
“I have bad tidings for you. I am here because the Dragon Queen Tiamat failed in her plots to conquer this world. But in failing, she inspired the giants, and they are at war with you. All of the creatures of the earth, air, and sea will probably be caught up in the fracas in some way or another. The age of dragons is over.”
He notices the small fairy asking about the cult. He smiles. He knows why this poor creature suffered. “The Cult of the Dragon was pursuing a spell that Tiamat told them would let her enter the world. But she failed. Now the age of the dragons is over, and our age has begun.” 
He looks at them and feels a great sadness, “You want to tell your friends and loved ones to steer clear of locations where giants live or even where giants were rumored to live.”
The dwarf asks, “where is that?” 
“Great question, this part of the world that you little people live in.” 
“Not cool!” 
“We’re big. You’re little.” 
“You should think about your language. It can cause harm.” 
Thistle shouts, “you need to understand how your communication is being received instead of how you think it is intended.” 
“You are so right. And King Hekaton has talked about having a newer inclusive way of talking about all people. To not otherwise your kind. His great wife who disappeared had much to say.” He coughs. “I worry that the age of tolerance is over as well. But where should we go? As far away as the ancient kingdom of Ostoria. The kingdom of Ostoria stretches from the Spine of the world to the great desert to the east, to the sword coast to the west, and then to the kingdom of Amn in the south.” 
“The entire North?” 
“Yes, there was a giant kingdom here. And over the centuries, the under 16 feet crowd.” He looks at them expectantly.
Everyone nods their heads that this is better. 
“Invaded from the south and drove the giants to the north and to isolated pockets of giant power.”
Vardis observes, “so now they see this as a time to reclaim what is rightfully theirs and may end up terrorizing the entire sword coast! What will you do, Kraddock?”
“I am just a shaman. Clean it up, just trying to make sure that this temple is safe. I’ve got to, “ he looks at the tattered drapes, “I’ve got to fix the drapes. There’s the roof over there that needs patching. The braziers haven’t had charcoal in years, and then there are the frescoes. And plus, I have to polish the alters, get rid of the dead body.” 
“The complete destruction of the sword coast is at risk, and as long as your shrine is fine,” screams Vardis. 
“I must pray to the All-Father to understand what he wants. I offer you my thoughts and prayers and will ask him to protect you from what must come.” 
“So basically nothing.” 
“Yes.” 
He hands Vardis some potions as they leave, “perhaps more than just thoughts and prayers.” 
Unla Kee, the thief, asks, “If you came with us, that would help even more.” 
“There is such a thing as pushing your luck?” 
As they leave, Kraddock sighs, “perhaps the All-Father will learn that this war will bring no glory to anyone.” 
## Group 8th day of the 1st tenday of Taraksh

After a long rest, the group heads back to Llorkh. The weather has turned to the worse and is cold. And they have no food. 
They quickly explain their circumstances and then buy some of his game. 
The rabbit is gutted by Thistle. Apparently, Vardis and Orsik spent too much time writing subversive poetry and never learned that life skill. It is one of the best rabbits the group has ever eaten. 
The next day there is snowfall. And at first, they attempt to walk through the snow, but soon it’s too cold, and they are too tired. Thistle starts a fire, and the group waits it out.
Vardis says, “let’s double time to Llorkh.”
As they can almost taste Nonnie’s food, a giant centipede erupts. 
Orsik shouts, “Seriously, this single giant centipede pops out in front of us in this massive field.” 
A few seconds later, it is dead. The group arrives at Nonnies, Aunt Nonnie, as she likes to be called. She is as friendly and welcoming as always.
“What’s wrong with her Vardis?” asks Orsik. 
“I don’t know. She’s probably affiliated with an evil cult.” 
After they get their room and eat their vegetarian entrees, Aunt Nonnie says, “There have been so many reports of kobolds coming around. And those guards seem unwilling to do very much but stay in their tower.”
“I’d do that,” mumbles Orsik.
“No, you wouldn’t,” she stares at him, “because of that THING you did.”
