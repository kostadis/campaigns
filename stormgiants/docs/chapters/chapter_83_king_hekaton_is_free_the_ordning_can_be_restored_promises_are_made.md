# Chapter 83  - King Hekaton is free. The Ordning can be restored. Promises are made. 

Orsik sees the Wizard and throws two hand axes. The first ax hits, and the second embeds itself in the mast behind the wizard.

“What the hell happened?” 

Unla Kee sniggers, “He had a contingency spell and probably teleported to somewhere safe.” 

Orsik picks up the hand ax that didn’t disappear with the wizard and kills one of the cultists climbing up the mast. 

Vardis looks down into the sleet storm. Orsik shouts, “Light them up.” 

A few moments later, the mote disappears into the sleet storm, and a bright spherical light forms. Correlon's cleric is unsure how many died, but he heard several screams. 

And one of the cultists, drenched and shivering, appears on the top aft deck of the ship. He is outside the storm and sees Vardis. Using the armed ballista, he shoots, and Vardis is wounded. 

Unla Kee, flying with his pegasi wings, looks at Thistle, who nods and then unleashes a fireball on the few remaining cultists, burning Thule. 

Thistle looks into the storm, but his eyes of True Seeing don’t reveal an invisible Wizard. 

Orsik shouts, “Free the Giant, then let’s use the Conch to get out of here.” 

Thistle nods but wants to kill the Wizard, so he scouts around the fore part of the ship. But sees nothing. 

Orsik and Vardis climb into the sleet storm and arrive at the chains binding Hekaton.

Thule tries to burn the chains with his acid and fails. 

Orsik swings his blade, and one of the chains breaks. “This will work!” 

Vardis is about to swing his sword when Orisk realizes that magical weapons must break the chains. Vardis acknowledges his brilliant friend and casts a spiritual weapon. The weapon strikes the chains to no effect. 

The archmage appears inside the aft cabin and casts prismatic spray. And once the spell is cast, close the door. 

Orsik feels a blue light envelopes him and freezes him. His body aches from the cold, but he lives. More importantly, the pain does not distract him from keeping the spell working. 

As for Vardis, an indigo light covers him but does nothing. 

Unla Kee shrieks, “This sleet storm is a nuisance. I can’t burn anything I can’t see!” Then he remembers that he can cast haste. He flies into the storm but cannot fly and lands prone on the deck. 

Just then, the first mate opens the door to the same cabin with the wizard and strikes down Unla Kee with a poisoned weapon. 

Orsik screams, “Just get some healing to me, and I will break these chains!!!” 

Thistle acknowledges and flies towards Orsik, but a chunk of sleet knocks him onto the ship's deck. Thistle mutters, “This storm is getting in the way.” 

Orsik then shrugs his shoulders, tries to move through the storm, slips, gets up, and breaks one chain. “Two chains left, and it’s all on Vardis.” He shouts, “YOU CAN DO THIS!!!!” He then thinks, “Damnit, my DNRRR has no Do not Reanimate clause. And with my luck, this wizard is a necromancer.” 

The wizard opens the door and glares at the storm. He thinks for a moment and then casts a cone of cold. However, Unla Kee saw him and countered the magic. The wizard glares and re-enters his room. 

The first mate shouts, “You will die, halfling.” And indeed, Unla Kee is brought to death’s door. 

But Thistle was ready and struck him with an arrow. The first mate staggers inside. Thuul uses that opportunity to rush in.

Orsik staggers toward a chain. He stands and swings his sword. The blow misses the chain. Then he raises his blade again and hits the metal, but it is a glancing blow. He glares. 

Vardis brings his spiritual weapon down on one chain and damages it, but the other slash is as ineffective as the first. 

The Wizard steps around Thuul, twirling his hand, and a whirlwind erupts from it to roar outward. Flakes of snow and shards of ice materialize in the freezing air, whipping in ever-widening circles as the blast fans out from his fingertips. The skin of his hand grows pale and tingles with the cold, but it’s nothing compared to what the spell inflicts on the remaining members of the 3HPP. 

And this time, the sleet storm is gone. Orsik has survived one murderous attack after another and kept focused, but this last one was too much. And as the spell evaporates, he can look around at the carnage of the battle. Burned corpses were soggy from the storm, blood dripping from the bodies that fell from the fast onto the deck, and Unla Kee was barely hanging on. Vardis, he can tell, is also scarcely alive. 

Thistle can see the first mate, aims an arrow, and hits the broken door the first mate used for cover. But Thistle has a second shot, and his aim is true this time. 

But Vardis’s spiritual weapon slashes through one chain and then moves again and slashes through another. Vardis swings his magical mace and destroys the damaged chain. 

The Wizard stares down at his opponents, and for a moment, he wonders if he should turn into a Tyrannosaurus Rex and rip their guts from end to end. But then he thinks, “Perhaps one of them might have some other trick up their sleeve? No, I need to kill them all at once.” 

And Vardis sees the familiar twist of his hand and the snow and shards of ice whipping in ever-widening circles from the wizard’s fingertips. He tries to avoid the worst damage but is still barely alive. 

As for Orsik, he’s out. 

Seeing Orsik out and thinking that Cleric can be finished shortly, the first mate attempts to kill Thuul. And the summoned beast disappears. He smiles; he has enough time to kill Vardis, but Vardis sees him and summons Correlon's light, and the first mate misses. 

Thistle, barely alive, shouts, “I have a plan!” 

Vardis stares at him and thinks, “This is going to be another one of those hare-brained schemes of his.” He shouts, “JUST HEAL ORSIK SO WE CAN TELEPORT OUT OF HERE!” 

Thistle shouts, “I plan to pop a goodberry into Orsik’s mouth.” 

And he does.

Orsik recovers from his near-death experience, pulls out the conch, and blows. 

Moments later, they find themselves in the grotto in the basement of the storm giant stronghold, the Maelstrom, sitting on an arcane gate. 

Hekaton stands up. 

The Storm Giant King fills the space, his head nearly scraping the vaulted ceiling carved from a single, colossal geode that pulsed with a faint, internal light. He is a mountain of a being, sculpted not from granite but from the very essence of a tempest.
His skin holds a thunderhead's deep, bruised purple, laced with a network of fine lines like lightning scars across a vast plain. Hair, the color of winter moonlight, cascaded down his broad shoulders, mingling with a beard as thick and tangled as a storm-wracked forest. 
His eyes, though. Those were the proper windows to his soul. They were the color of a storm-churned sea, shot through with flickering veins of amethyst light, currently swirling with a maelstrom of grief and suppressed rage. Those eyes had witnessed the rise and fall of empires, the slow crawl of glaciers, and the birth of stars. Now, they held the crushing weight of recent loss, the raw wound of betrayal festering beneath his regal composure.
He is clad in armor crafted from the scales of a beast known only in legends. Each scale is larger than a shield and overlaps like the plates of a fortress. The armor shifts and shimmers with each subtle movement, reflecting the geode's light in a dazzling, chaotic dance. Around his waist is a belt of woven lightning, from which hang talismans of bone and carved gemstones, humming with barely contained power. 
The air crackles around him, heavy with the scent of ozone and the distant rumble of thunder. He is a storm-given form, a king stripped bare by grief yet still radiating an aura of power that could topple mountains and silence the heavens. He stands there, a colossus silhouetted against the shimmering light, a king teetering on the precipice of despair, his every breath a promise of the storm to come. He is Hekaton, and the very stones trembled before him. Heavy with the weight of a thousand storms, his gaze sweeps across the 3HPP. He opens his mouth, and when he finally speaks, his voice is the rumble of distant thunder, laced with the chilling whisper of the wind. "Where am I? Who are you?" he begins. 
Thistle flies up to him, saying, “We rescued you from a boat you were chained into. The Kraken society had captured you.” 
Orsik adds, “We were sent by your daughter to rescue you.”
“Yes, the one not trying to betray you,” adds Thistle helpfully. 
“And not trying to steal the throne,” finishes Orsik. 
“My daughters?” he roars. “I must go to the throne room at once.” 
Orsik sits down, “I suppose we should follow.” 
“You need healing, buddy,” mutters Vardis. 
And he is healed. And Orsik is feeling much better and runs after the King. 
As the dwarf enters the throne room, he sees Queen Serissa delighted at seeing her father again. And he tries to look as dashing as possible; after all, she will someday be the Queen. The Queen sees him and smiles. 
Meanwhile, the Imperator shouts, “So wonderful to see you alive!” 
“Daddy, we’re so happy to see you!” shout Nym and Mirran. 
Unla Kee, who has caught u,p mutters, “No, you aren’t.” 
After a few more moments of joyous family reunion, King Hekaton turns to the 3HPP and says, “This is a moment of great joy, but we have problems.” 
Thistle nods, “Yes, we have to rescue my girlfriend.” 
King Hekaton smiles, and his stern face softens, “The affairs of the heart are paramount, but the realms are in danger.”

“Yes, from the same blue dragon, Iymrith, who tried to steal your kingdom.” 
“The doom of the desert?” 
“Precisely!” 
King Hekaton turns to his daughter, “Serissa, explain.” 
And she does. And as part of this tale, Mirran and Nym’s failures are laid bare. 
The King is startled and angry, but he is careful. He turns to the Imperator and asks, “I presume you were the one who left no stone unturned to find me?” 
The Imperator attempts to deceive, but the 3HPP clarifies that Serissa remained hopeful while the Imperator offered little support. 
The King marches up to the Wyrmskull Throne and sits. He turns to Serissa and says, “You are now the Imperator.” He then turns to the old Imperator and says, “Brother, Uthor, you are dismissed.” 

Uthor stares at his brother, and the storm rages in his body, and he smiles, “Yes, my brother, it is time I took care of other business.” He hands his insignia of office to Serissa and marches out of the throne room. 
There is a stunned silence. 
“As for you two, Nym and Mirran, you betrayed me, you betrayed Serissa, who was your Queen, and you allied yourselves with a,” the intensity of his rage and anger and fury explodes from his voice, “dragon.” He pauses, “Go to your chambers. I will decide your fates later.” 
Mirran and Nym try to protest, but the new Imperator orders the guards to take them to their chambers. The new Imperator follows the guards, enjoying this moment too much.
The King puts on his obsidian crown and turns to the 3HPP, “My friends, you have done much for me. And I wish to do more for you. But my kingdom is in ruins. The All-Father had visions of grandeur that were foolish. And he broke the Ordning, and now, thanks to you, I can put it back as it was. And yet, it galls me that Iymrith will live while I bring Chief Guh of the Hill Giants, Thane Kayalithca of the Stone Giants, Jarl Storvald of the Frost Giants, Countess Sansuri of the Cloud Giants, and Duke Zalto of the Fire Giants to kneel before me. If you were to kill them all or convince them of their ways' errors, I could join you in one final battle against the Doom of the Desert.”
Orsik turns to his friends and says, “Thanks for the offer, but we’re good. We can do this on our own.” 
The rest of the 3HPP are not as sure. Thistle knows that the love of his life is somewhere near Duke Zalto’s kingdom.
Vardis knows his friend may not care about the map to the treasure, but he does, and the map is near Zalto’s abode. 
And Thistle knows that Escarlotta will not be happy to discover that Countess Sansuri will not be defeated. 
As for Unla Kee, he’s already running past the King to the teleportation chamber to get back to his cloud castle and kill an ancient blue dragon.
