Orsik shows him the grapple. Gherlyn is initially coy about who he sold it to. Still, Orisk appeals to his Dwarven pride, and Gherlyn explains that he sold it to Darz Helgar, who lives in a cottage on the northern campgrounds as the caretaker.

 The group headed towards the Northern Campgrounds, which they had noticed when they arrived near the Market. The campgrounds are a fenced-in field where wagons can park and contain oxen. There’s also plenty of space to pitch tents and build campfires. To one side is a stone well for drawing water.

Orsik ignores all of that and marches towards the cottage. He shows Darz the harness and the grapple and asks him obliquely if he knows anything about it. 

Darz sighs and sits down on the steps of the cottage, defeated. “Yeah. It was me. I stole the gold and the bags. Didn’t wanna do it. Didn’t have a choice. That scum Kaeln - y’know, the guy who runs the shite tavern - told me he knew about my past and would go blabbering to the Lord Protector. He told me I had to steal it or he’d yap. But I guess the game’s up now, isn’t it? Good. I’m tired of running, tired of hiding. I did ten hard years in Waterdeep’s dungeon. I can do a few out here in Triboar. Just promise me you get that bastard; get him good!” 

Orsik’s eyes lighten, “You have contacts with the Thieves Guild? Give me whatever you have, and I promise you nobody has to find out.”
