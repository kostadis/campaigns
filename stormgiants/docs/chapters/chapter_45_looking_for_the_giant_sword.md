# Chapter 45 - Looking for the Giant Sword
## 5th day of the 1st Tenday of Eleasis

### Thistle Wendrod
Thistle hated spiders. Something about giant spiders confusing fairies and sprites with flies creeped him out. 
He was reluctant to go on ahead, but the idea of having the halfling charge up ahead filled him with even more dread. 
So he fluttered ahead, with his wing muffs, through the dark tunnels, which eventually emerged into a large cavern covered in spider webs with three obviously giant cocoons. 
“Yuch.” 
As he stared at the mass of webbing, one thing came to his head, “larder.” 
Orsik lumbered right after him and used his magic to burn through the webs that filled the room and reach the cocoons that must have treasure.

The first is the body of a goat that has been bled dry. Thistle watches as the dwarf slashes through the goat’s belly, hoping to find something, and doesn’t. 
“But they eat everything!” he mutters in disappointment. 
Thistle watches as Orsik burns through the webbing to the second cocoon. 
“It’s a dwarf!” 
“Any treasure?” shouts Vardis. 
“17 copper pieces, a deck of cards, and a set of dice.” 
“Meh.” 
Thistle wonders what the third cocoon will hold. Thistle flies up for a better vantage point and smiles when he sees a person that doesn’t look dead. Thistle flies down and hands her some berries. 
### Orsik
“My name is Orsik; what’s your name?”
The female human is clearly attired in the garb of a member of an Uthgardt tribe. Orsik stares at her intently and figures out that she belongs to the same tribe as the chieftain they killed. 
“Probably not worth sharing that detail, he thinks.” 
“Noori,” she says. 
Orsik notices how she stares intently and with great hatred at Unla Kee. 
“I need to get out of here and find my tribe.” 
“You came here to this temple with your tribe?” When she nods, Orsik continues, “We found them trying to get in, and they were all dead.” 
Orsik smiles internally when he realizes Noori does not see through the deception. 
After a few more minutes, the group agrees to take her out of the warrens they found and return to the main temple. 

When they arrive at the entrance of the warren, they see Harshnag The Grim sleeping on a bed, covered in rags, snoring loudly. 
Orsik mutters to Nuri, “Be quiet; this giant can be very particular about people in his temple.” 
Nuri smiles at Orsik and runs off. 
Orsik turns to the group, “Let’s finish exploring these caverns.” 
And so once again, they go into the crack in the wall, past the larder, and continue to follow the right-hand rule. 
After a few minutes of walking, the tunnels reach an opening to the outside. They are standing next to a gap in the mounting above the valley they came up on the day before. They are over 400 feet from the ground. The descent is very steep and treacherous. 
“Let’s go back.”
As Orsik head back into the tunnel, something hits him. “Hey, these are not part of the original construction. These caves were formed when the mountain moved and cracked. Creatures have used these caverns as their homes.” 
Vardis looks at him, “You know this sort of stuff?”
“It’s a dwarf thing.” 
### Vardis

Orsik’s knowledge of stonework is surprising to Vardis. He thought he knew everything about his friend but clearly did not. When they were friends in Waterdeep, Orsik was a gruff, dangerous, fun-loving guy who liked to hang out at bars and taverns and never demonstrated any interest in caves. 
“More magic ring stuff,” he thinks. 
Vardis moves forward carefully through the caves, passes several intersections, and finally arrives at another chamber. 
Before he can get his bearings, Unla Kee rushes in.
Vardis takes in the sight that Unla Kee’s light reveals. The floor is the same rough-hewn ground, but in the middle of the room is a single column that supports a domed ceiling. Curtains of the web stretch from the walls to the spider. 
Vardis groans internally when he realizes that Unla Kee will not stop running in and that whatever is in those spider webs will target the mage. 
But Orsik, to Vardis’ relief, was ready and struck twice with his green blade magic. The spider bites Orsik, but his natural dwarven resistance blocks the poison. 
Vardis, seeing his friend under attack, uses his bow to kill the creature, only to suddenly notice that a pile of spider eggs suddenly hatch and swarm under the spider. 

“Shit, the mage is going to be eaten alive,” thinks Vardis. 
But the mage strikes with a thundering clap, and Orsik finishes the creature. 
Vardis sighs in relief and then walks up to the dead spider. There he finds a frozen potion of climbing and an old axe with a spectacularly beautiful sapphire. 
### Orsik
Once again, the 3HPP emerges in the room where Harshnag the Grim sleeps. He pokes the giant to wake him up.
Harshnag yawns and mumbles, “Haven’t slept in a proper giant bed in ages.” 
Vardis and Orsik gape, and then Vardis asks, “Where have you been living?” 
“Finding a giant-sized bed in the land of the little people is hard.” 
Orsik is confused, “you’re homeless?” 
Harshnag is infuriated, and Orsik enjoys poking the Grim, “My roof is the stars, my walls are the trees, and my bed is the ground.” 
When Vardis observes, “Hey guy, you could have built yourself a house, and you could have slept in a hammock,” Orsik smiles because his friend is doubling down on their mockery. 
“I don’t like hammocks.” 
And with that, Harshnag gets up, tosses the pelt to the side, exits the room, and continues down the main hallway. 

As Harshnag walks north down the hallway, he stares into the empty bedrooms, muttering, “Nice beds.” 
Orsik rushes after him. 
And just as he catches up with Harshnag, he hears an ominous thump. All of the entrances to all of the bedrooms have been sealed with magical walls of stone. 
“What the???” 
A massive 40’ diameter sphere of solid granite materializes in front of Orsik and starts to roll south down the hallway. 
Orsik watches as Harshnag smashes at one of the stone walls and makes progress, but not enough. Thistle, meanwhiles flies above the ball. 
Unfortunately, the rest of the 3HPP and the giant cannot avoid getting hurt by the onrushing ball. 
When the ball reaches the end of the hallway, Orsik is dumbstruck by the fact that it’s coming back, but this time slower. 
He tells everyone to squeeze in the edge of the walls. And they do. Avoiding getting crushed. And as the ball moves past Orsik, he notices what is moving it; the stone giant statue that stood at the hallway’s south entrance is pushing the ball. 
The stone giant appears uninterested in the 3HPP, to Orsik’s relief. And the dwarf stares in amazement as the granite ball disappears into the northern wall. 
“Magic.” 
And with that, the stone walls disappear. The stone giant somberly walks south, finally stands on his pedestal, and becomes immobile.
“Let’s not do that again,” thinks Orsik. 
Harshnag, clobbered by the granite ball twice, agrees and mutters, “Let’s use one of these rooms to sleep it off.” 
Orsik turns to his friend, “Wasn’t he sleeping like 10 minutes ago?” 
“Is he old?” 
“I am not old. I haven’t slept in a proper giant bed and room in eons.” 
“I’m just saying, you can’t be a toddler if you haven’t done something in a long time,” remarks Vardis. 
Harshnag is annoyed, “I am not old by giant years. And a long time is long by your reckoning, not mine.” 
## 6th day of the 1st tenday of Eleasis

### Harshnag The Grim 

Although Harshnag the Grim told the 3HPP that he was not old, the truth was that he was. And the comfort of a bed, and a room built to fit a giant, was comfortable in a way that he hadn’t felt in a very long time. 
As he stretches and yawns after a very deep sleep, he says, “I haven’t slept this well in ages!” 
Then Harshnag leaps out of bed and shouts, “Let us find this giant blade!”
They quickly walk south past the immobile stone giant to some stairs north of the doors to the temple of Annam. 
One of the little people insists they go up the stairs, so Harshnag follows.
The stairs lead to another long hallway and more rooms. The entrance to the hallway has a life-size statue of a female cloud giant wearing an angry mask, her arms positioned in a way that suggests she’s casting a spell.
Harshnag watches with curiosity as the group inspects the statue, expecting it to come to life at any moment. 
Soon they decide there is nothing to worry about. Harshnag is about to head north down the hallway when the fairy stops him. 
“How about I fly and avoid stepping on any pressure plates.”
Harshnag agrees and watches as the fairy flies off. 
### Thistle

Much like the downstairs hallway, there are rooms with two beds sized for giants. Unlike the floor below, the northern end of the hallway has collapsed.
In one of the rooms, Thistle finds a giant bag. 
“How odd,” he thinks, then proceeds to investigate it carefully.
Inside, he finds a bong, a gong, and a waterskin. Thistle laughs as he imagines a giant engaged in meditation banging on a gong while he smokes from the gong. He’s about to leave the room when he finds a long and slender shard of ice roughly the size of a dagger, and within it, the rune symbol of it glows.
