# Chapter 04 - Clan Licehair

## The Group 3rd day of the 1st tenday of Taraskh

“Good berries!” proclaims Thistle. “I have good berries every morning for everyone!” 
Vardis, Unla, and Orsik take their share and thank the ranger. 
After several days of hiking through the mountains in an uncommonly warm spring, the group arrives at an old abandoned dwarven outpost. The gentle weather seems a reasonable recompense by the gods for the almost destruction of the world that Tiamat and their negligence nearly triggered. 
On the 5th day of the 1st tenday of Taraskh, Veradda Stoor’s notes bring the group to a hallway that leads inwards. 
Thistle observes that the dust and cobwebs have been disturbed by many footprints, “too many to tell what has gone through here,” he shouts. 
Orsik stares at the stonework that is centuries old and mutters, “my kinsmen built this.” 
Vardis mumbles, “as if your kinsmen want to have anything to do with you any more than mine with me.” 
Orsik turns pink. He’s embarrassed.
Vardis stares, “well?” 
Orsik turns pink, “I’d rather not talk about it.”
At this point, everyone stares at him.
“Fine, it’s clan Licehair.” 
The group erupts in laughter. Vardis slaps Orsik on the back and laughs loudest.
Orsik gruffly shouts, “Clan Licehair is a proud clan that secured the massive gem hoard of a black dragon. Of course, I am not a member because I don’t have lice in my hair.”
Vardis, who had tried to contain his laughing, laughs louder. When he recovers, he turns to Orsik, “well, there may be a gem hiding there.” 
With that, the group heads into the hallway. As they turn the corner, they enter a room that contains four small creatures, “kobolds” whispers Thistle. 
The kobolds are behind a small barricade blocking the passageway to the south. 
The middle of the room has a small stone building with no apparent entrances, although it does have holes. 
Before they can react, a kobold sees them and shouts, “begone foul creatures!” 
## Agra

In the stone building are two kobolds. Their red scales are scared with burn marks. And their clothes look like they are stinky even by the standards of their kind. 
“That damned fool will fight those heavily armed adventurers,” Agra mutters to Gen, standing right next to him. 
Agra notices that Gen is about to do something, clamps his hand over his mouth, and shouts, “Let’s sit tight and wait.” 
Gen nods. And is silent.
Agra hears Nug argue with Thistle over whether the fairy is or is not a filthy creature. 
“Good, lure them into the trap,” Agra thinks. 
But then, to his horror, the trap failed. The Kobolds that were hidden move so loudly that they could wake up the undead. 
The sound of stones from the hidden kobolds falling on the adventuring group brings him a smile. “Filthy interlopers!” 
But then he starts hearing other sounds, the telltale sounds of a sleep spell, and then the dull sound of dead bodies dropping to the ground.
Gen turns to him, “what will we ever do?” 
Agra looks at him, “surrender and pray for the best?” He grabs something that looks white and steps outside, “we surrender!” 
The group stares at Agra, and he wonders why they won’t talk to him. He then notices a halfling chewing some berries. 
“We are trying to surrender here,” shouts Agra. 
Thistle remembers how the Kobolds were the foot soldiers of the Cult of the Dragon’s foot soldiers and shouts, “did you work for the Cult? Did you praise Tiamat?” 
Agra stares, “no, it can’t be!” and then turns pale. The fairy has the telltale marks of one who was tortured by the Cult of the dragon. “look, it was an accident, right? I was under orders. I didn’t mean any of it!”
“Where is my girlfriend?” shouts the enraged fairy. 
Agra is terrified, “with the defeat of the cult and so many dragons, I simply do not know.” He almost looks disgusted with himself, “we have been serving giant masters now.” 
The fairy flirts up in the most ridiculous poses and says in a voice that cracks Agra up, “where is my girlfriend?” Agra sees the gesture and assumes that Thistle has forgiven him for everything. 

So he makes an offer, “do you need bootlickers?” 
The elf shouts, “do you want to be our slaves?” 
Agra is offended, “no, employees. We are very fluid in our loyalties.” 
Vardis sighs, “we don’t have enough money.” 
The kobold sighs. Then it says, “I suppose this is when I offer you information?” 
Seeing the pointy ends, he begins. “With the defeat of the dragons, our tribes have been reduced to working for Giants. A fire giant hired us after our Red Dragon left.” 
The elf interrupts him, “How many giant types are there in general?”
“I don’t know, but others were hired by hill and cloud giants. There seems to be some kind of trouble brewing between them. They seem to be competing for something or other. I am not quite sure. It is odd because they don’t normally do that.” 
Thistle interrupts, “my girlfriend?” 
“Well, after the Red Dragon, the boss was in a bit of a situation. So he made a deal with a cloud giant. When the cloud giants arrived in their castle, we ran for the hills. The fire giants sent us here to find the treasure of the cultists. They said it was because this was too small of an area for them. But Gun heard that the place was cursed. So we hid in the stone building. When these buffoons,” and he looks at the dead kobolds and corrects himself, “dead buffoons, entered the room to the south, creatures emerged and attacked. When the creatures died, they exploded. Our leader Nug said there was a treasure in the corner of the room to the south, a glowing magical fire symbol, and a river of lava.”  
Thistle growls at him, “where is my girlfriend?” 
“We don’t know,” he cowers. 
Orsik steps in, “got it, cloud giants, girlfriend. Go on!” 
“Well, we don’t have anything else to say. And Thistle, now that we are friends, you wouldn’t mind if we just left?” 
The halfling that didn’t die says, “kobolds make good shields?” 
Agra is paralyzed with fear, and then he hears Gen speak, “perhaps if we offered some treasure?” 
Agra breathes when the dwarf takes the treasure and shouts, “go and no looting.” 
As they leave, Vardis shouts, “Stop.” He then turns to his friend, “should we be worried about them telling the Fire Giants about us?” 
Agra coughs, “if I may, the Fire Giants expect us to show up with some treasure, or not at all. They made it clear that failure to show up with treasure would result in a, how shall we say, negative review of our performance that would result in our termination.” Agra picks up some dirt, squishes it in his hand, and swallows. 
## Orsik
“Let them go,” shouts Orsik.
Vardis looks at him.
Orsik is tired of being looked down upon by everyone. That thing he does, he’s decided to stop doing it. “I’m going in.”
Vardis stares, “are you okay?” 
“Yes, I am done with being the guy everyone hates. The fact that Old Keller didn’t even want to talk to me got to me. It’s like I am not even good enough to be a member of Clan Licehair. LICEHAIR!” 
Orsik moves through the barricade into the southern room. 
The area in front of him is full of smoke, making it hard to see. He hears the comforting sound of crackling large fires, and deeper in the room, he sees large fires. In the middle of the room, he notices a brighter glow that splits the room in half, giving the impression of a river. 
He walks in. And just then, he hears and sees a large burst of fire from a prominent symbol etched into the wall in the southeastern corner of the room. 
The smell of dead burned kobolds is rank. 
Orsik pauses and thinks and says, “hey Thistle, why don’t you go on ahead.”
## The Group

Vardis smiles.
Thistle looks around and processes the scene. He approaches one of the fires and notices that he burns if he gets within 10 feet. So he carefully plots a route around the hot spots until he reaches the fire symbol. 
Once there, he begins to examine the symbol and realizes that disabling it will be necessary if they are to take the silver. 
He’s unable to make heads or tales of what god it represents. He stares some more and tries to remember what his uncle had said.
“Right got it,” he thinks. He starts to damage the symbol by firing arrows at it. 
Orsik, Vardis and Unla watch.
The symbol belches fire, and a magmin emerges from the fire approaching Thistle. 
Thistle rushes to the corner to be as far as possible, but in so doing, the magmin hiding in the lava come out to play. 
He’s surrounded. 
Orsik turns to Vardis, who turns to Unla, and they silently agree to help Thistle. 
Unla Kee uses his thunderwave to push them away from Thistle, and they explode harmlessly. Orsik tosses his hand-ax at one and kills the last one. Unfortunately, in all the excitement, Thistle had a dying magmin explode in his face and another leave lava that he had to brush off to avoid burning to death. 
Vardis helps by healing Thistle.
The fairy once again starts firing his arrows on the symbol.
A few arrows later, he shouts, “okay, I now need to cast a spell.” 
Orsik shouts, “What?” 
“Yeah, there are a few more steps.”
“How many?”
“Well, after I cast the spell, I need to figure out how to sever the connection to the god.”
“How are you going to do that?”
“I’ll figure it out.” 
Unfortunately, he doesn’t in time, and another magmin emerges from the lava. And the battle begins again. 
Thistle ignores the creature and focuses on the altar. “C’mon, what did uncle Yorn say about these gods?” 
Thistle dodges the arms of the creature, and while doing so, it finally makes sense, “got it!” 
And with that, the symbol becomes inert. 
Meanwhile, Orsik attacks the Magmin and kills it. Unfortunately, Thistle was next to the creature, and its exploding entrails burned him. 
Thistle shrugs. 
And with that, the smoke in the room clears. 
Orsik realizes that this room was used as a forge before whatever evil artifact took over. 
The group searches the room, grabs some silver, and extracts several magmin shells prized by spell casters. 
As they leave, Orsik turns to Vardis, “you know, I have heard legends of heroes who stayed in one place, killing hundreds of summoned monsters until they had become powerful beyond measure.”
Vardis yawns, “that’s a legend. Most of the time, the summoning creatures get annoyed, and before you know it, something really awful gets summoned that kills the hero.” 
Orsik scratches his beard and sighs.
