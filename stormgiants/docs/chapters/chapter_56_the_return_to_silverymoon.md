# Chapter 56 - The return to Silverymoon
## 2nd day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis
As Orsik passes the totem, a Coatl emerges. It demands that they destroy Wormblod, who has perverted the tribe, and the creature promises a boon for this act.
Thistle, remembering killing Wormblod in the Eye of the All-Father, says, “We did it!” 
Vardis tries to stop him, thinking, “We should haggle for something more.” 
But it’s too late; they have all been blessed with a Charm of Restoration. 
Orsik sighs, “I guess that’s useful.” 
The creature’s eyes bulge, “you are not appreciative of my boon?” 
The 3HPP nods their heads like annoyed teenagers when their parents ask them to say thank you to the annoying uncle and say, “Yeah, we appreciate it.” 
The creature slithers its tongue in and out of its mouth and mutters, “Usually, they appreciate me more!” 
Further down in the cave, the 3HPP finds the Remorhazz’s lair. As they steal the gold and gemstones and the minor magical artifacts, Vardis notes that if they had not bothered to attack with the fireballs, they might have been able to get the gong without fighting the creatures. 
“But fireballs are fun,” mutters Unla Kee. 
Back on the Dragon’s Breath, Bressik is delighted about his newfound freedom and the opportunity to work on background research and the play.
Ironically, Thistle never thinks to act Bressik about his girlfriend. Bressik finds that odd. It’s not like he’s a well-informed Candlekeep scholar with deep knowledge about the occult and goings on of the area. And of the curious case of the fairy, Uncle Joon, and his journeys. 
Bressik is pleased and thinks “that information will cost them.” 
“And the buffoons, Orsik and Vardis, who refused to pay me a fair price, and now they will never know where their precious little treasure map goes.” 
## 3rd day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis
The following day, as the sun rises over Silverymoon, the 3HPP with Bressik arrives at the gate. 
There is a bit of a dialogue of what angle to use to enter the city, and they quickly reject the use of the letter from the Ambassador, and decide to mention that they are business partners with his [Lordship](https://www.worldanvil.com/heroes/campaign/npc/9857dbfe-91d8-41b5-a71a-3f7711bf7003). 
“Working with the non-goodly races?” spouts the guard. 
Orsik glares and the guard decides to confirm with his captain, [Sir Cedric Hollowcrest](https://www.worldanvil.com/heroes/campaign/npc/69b2ab4a-f8c2-4b19-aeef-ad714e17de68/), who arrives a few moments later.
“I am so sorry. These are the famous 3HPP! The redeemed Orsic!”
Orsic gruffly announces, “I am Orsic Sr.” 
He points to the Fairy - “The brokenhearted Thistle!” 
Then beams at Vardis and says - “The honest Vardis!” 
“Bob Saget’s play has made you heroes. His performance was divine!” 
And with that, the original guard says, “YOU’RE UNLA KEE! Omg! OMG! I am so in love with you! You are unafraid of anything, blast with fire and ice attacks, ignore all dangers, and laugh in the face of death!” 
Unla Kee stares at him, “What's the most dangerous thing around us right now?” 
“You.” 
“Am I scared of myself? Of course not! If I could look at myself in the eye and feel not an inch of fear.” 
The guard pulls out a note – “Could you sign this? Care of Borin Greycastle?
“One.” 
The guards sigh. 
“Cheer up; you can sell this in the open markets or collector for 20x!” 
“Sell this? Never.” 
As the 3HPP moves through the Mulgate and heads towards the Moonbridge on Moonway, they realize that Bob Saget has taken several liberties with their story. And in so doing has made them minor celebrities. 
Thistle, who paid good money, asks the group to head to Wyrmworks, where Rebecca Deakin has been working on some research for him. 
As they walk in, she’s excited and shouts, “Oh, you are alive!” 
Orsik raises an eyebrow. 
She answers, “I figured anyone stupid enough to look into Adimond Kroskas would be dead by now.” And then, unprompted, “My research into Adimond has reaffirmed my faith that Bob Saget is right. Silverymoon must abandon magic. The Spellguard is a crypto-fascist tool of the mages and a force of control.” 

Rebecca pauses and looks around furtively, “I spoke too much.” 
And then continues, ‘The thing is that the wizards treat us non-magic users as lesser people. Why else do you think we have the ‘goodly’ races? It’s a natural response to the wizard's oppression. Then the wizards create a school, and all of these out-of-towners going to the Wizard school act as if they are all better than the rest of us. They have their special classes, and their sages and their discussions. And it’s all fun and games until one of those idiots casts a spell or does some experiment with an elixir which turns into a plague.” 
She mutters, “Bob’s right. Just because they can cast spells doesn’t make them better. They should respect us. Those of us who don’t cast spells are valuable. And frankly, some of us can do what magic cannot do. I do research as an archivist and get information the old-fashioned way. I open books. I do research. I use my brain. I don’t snap my fingers, and some demon magically tells me the answer in exchange for the souls of 1000 of my co-citizens.” Seeing Unla Kee, she says, “I am talking about the Silverymoon and the Spellguard! The closest they get to dirty hands is if they touch a tomb that one of their apprentices had not dusted off before they touch it.” 
With that, she turns to Thistle, “So let me see. Adimond Kroskas is a powerful spell caster who knows the spell that allows one to transform Drakes from Dragon eggs.” 
“The what spell,” shouts Thistle. “This drake is not a spawn of a spell.”
Rebecca looks at him and says, “Right. He’s a lesser drake.” 
“Lesser Drake?” 
“Yes. Pure Drakes were created from a Dragon egg, and lesser drakes were their offspring.” 
Thistle glares at Rebecca, then turns to his increasingly annoyed Drake and says, “Maybe you should sit outside.” 
Rebecca continues, “Now he was hired by the Cult of Tiamat to work on a fay-dragon drake hybrid.” 
Thistle is shocked. 
She continues, “So this guy I met, who said he was your uncle, Joon, said that Adimond went into the fae to get the First Flower of Spring because it was a critical component of his spell research. The fairies were also important, unclear if it was as test subjects, components, or treats.” 
And then she pauses, “The weirdest part is what Adminod Kroskas is.  The Cult of the Dragon referred to him as a dragon, and some of them referred to him as a Dragon's pet. I am betting on the Dragon.” 
Thistle shouts, “UNCLE JOON! I am going to kill him.” 
Vardis remembers the death of Kolbaz, who did research Adimond last, and the odd damage in his chambers. “A Blue Dragon,” he mumbles. 
Thistle, when he recovers from his rage, suddenly understands with horror that the stories of his girlfriend’s violent behavior may not be as innocent as he initially thought. 
Orsik, more concerned with the play than a Blue Dragon, species experiments, and lost girlfriends, shouts, “C’mon, you got your answers.” 
And with that, they head out to the High Palace. Outside is a banner announcing that the play is sold out for the next two months. People are lining up to buy tickets, and the minute the 3HPP shows up, they are mobbed. 
Unla Kee is delighted and shouts, “FINALLY, THE RECOGNITION!” Orsik is also happy with the adoration, “I am a hero!” They both autograph the papers that are put in front of them. 
Just then, a pair of guards approach and announce that  [Lord Methrammar Aersume](https://www.worldanvil.com/heroes/campaign/npc/9857dbfe-91d8-41b5-a71a-3f7711bf7003) wishes to meet them.
He smiles warmly as they enter the room, “It’s so good to see you! The play has been a smashing success. Profits will delight all of you. Bob Saget had a brilliant stroke of genius to incorporate you into the play. At the end of the performance, when he’s about to destroy the world in some explosive, the four of you defeat him in a battle of wits.” 
He laughs. 
“Not a battle of wits, but a real battle. Orsik uses all his battle mastery, Vardis summons his god’s powers, and Unla Kee unleashes his spells.” He takes on a dreamy look, “It’s amazing. Especially the effects, the lights, and the explosions. There is a rumor that some CGI device is being used.” 
Orsik is intrigued, “who portrays us?” 
“I don’t know. You should ask Bob.”
Thistle wonders why he wasn’t part of the final combat, and his Lordship realizes that. 
Lord Aersume turns to Thistle, “We couldn’t find an actor small enough, so we used a puppet stuck on a stick. It’s an amazing likeness.” 
Orsik, who is gung ho on the idea of a new play, is shot down by his Lordship.
“People aren’t bored. The play has become a spiritual investigation. Bob has made the play about the dangers of magic and that all of us are accountable to ensure nothing bad happens. The heroes are four individuals of question repute who save the day. It plays into individual accountability, fears of magic, and the power of anyone.” 
Orsik is delighted. And wonders whether the son of Laeral Silverhand, one of the seven anointed of Mystara, agrees that magic is dangerous. Has Bob swayed him?
“There is danger in everything. There is danger in the sword. There is a danger in your questions,” he says menacingly. “The story is a metaphor about understanding the limits of what we can do and that anyone can be a hero. After all, the original playwright had wanted to write a play that would summon a demon that would destroy the entire town of Triboar and was thwarted by the four of you.” 
Sensing Orsik’s question, he glares, “I will not participate in these debates about Magic being too risky. Magic has served Silverymoon for generations. Lady Laeral Silverhand, my mother, favored of Mystara, once ruled the city, and if she thinks that magic can serve the city going forward, so say I. Those who say otherwise are traitors to Silverymoon.” 
Orsik then thinks that might be an opening to get the play changed to avoid paying Delsephine. “We have certain business concerns involving a red dragon that necessitate small changes in the play. And given how particular red dragons are about their money, if you could give us 6,000 gold pieces, we could get out of the situation or change the play.” 
“My dear boys, you don’t understand how profitable this play has become.”
He hands them 12,000 gold pieces and says, “You can cover that 6,000 from your share.” 
Unla Kee is worried, “what percentage is that? Is it from total earnings, profit?” 
His Lordship smiles, “There are only two truths in theater, Unla. The first is that there is never a profit. The second is when the producer gives you some money, you take it and don’t ask questions.” 
“I am never buying a flying castle,” Unla Kee whines. 
“We could always take Blagothkus’ castle,” Thistle points out.
Later that evening, the group meets with a very different-looking Bob Saget, who is delighted to see you. 
“My muses! And before you ask, everything is great. The theater is full every night to hear – my – story. Every night, their minds get twisted more, per your instructions against magic. It’s nothing more than a successful perversion of society that will achieve your, not my, stated aims in 30 days.” 
The 3HPP tries to probe who plays them, and Bob is very cagey. He is dismissive, “nobodies. They get their moment in the sun, and we replace them.” 
“The scene works best if it's amateurs rather than real adventurers. The actors show real gut-wrenching horror as they are making their attack. And it’s that terror that makes the whole scene work.” 
The heroes try to get some seats in the play, but Bob insists that none can be provided. When they press him, “I’m afraid not. It will not work at all. [Kaelen](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/the-forgotten-realms-kostadis/a/kaelen-sarssir-person) wrote the play that summoned me. He saved me from an eternity of bad dad jokes. And now we work together to improve his play. Your presence would be a – distraction.”  
Orsik smiles politely and decides to get in, so he purchases some invisibility potions.
