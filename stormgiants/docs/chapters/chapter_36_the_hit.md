# Chapter 36 - The Hit
“Let’s stay for the party,” grumps Orsik as they look at their options. 
And so the 3HPP head out that evening to where the rich and powerful live, in a series of ranches that ring the town of Triboar.

Their destination is Westpharl Ranch, the home away from home of Cindrell. The ranch has sprawling grounds and a large master’s house. By the time they arrive, the party is in full swing. There is music, Bob Saget the demon is cracking jokes, folks are dancing, and there is food of every kind and variety.
As the party continues through the night and the group is having fun, a page comes to the 3HPP and invites them for a private audience.
Orsik is shocked, “are you sure she said all of us?” 
She smiles when they are taken into her study, a large room with bookshelves, art of various kinds, a lush carpet and a fireplace. 
Orsik can’t help but ask, “You want all of us simultaneously?” 
Cindrell realizes that Orsik thought this was a different kind of private audience. She looks at him with sorrow, the type of sorrow a rich person looks at someone who doesn’t have the class to be seriously considered as someone they might be interested in and doesn’t have enough money to make up for that lack of style. 
Orisk is oblivious. Vardis tries not to laugh and mutters, “Old sport.” 
And then, with all the grace of her noble upbringing, she turns to the 3HPP and says, “Sit down.”
And so they sit on the couch and chairs around her desk. 
“I don’t want to speak ill of my neighbors, but Hyuth Kolstaag, ever since he has arrived, has been a frightfully bad presence for the town. He’s a rich retired adventurer, unbelievably arrogant, with an astonishing sense of his own importance, utterly undeserved, and without a touch of class. He has rejected every one of my invitations to introduce him into Triborian society.” 
Orsik is confused, “okay?”
“Now, normally, I wouldn’t concern myself. Yet another misguided adventurer, who is a misanthrope with too much money and no class, has become a nuisance. He made several enemies, and they periodically showed up to try and kill him. The violence will cause a drop in the property values of Triboar.” 
She stares at them, expecting to hear a gasp. When none is forthcoming, she continues. 
“A few weeks ago, a pair of assassins slipped into town and attempted to kill him. We were awoken to peels of thunder and blasts of lightning. When the assassination attempt was foiled, the assassins stole a horse from the Cart and the Coin and murdered the stable hand.” 
“We will track down and kill the assassins,” grunts Orsik.
“Oh, no. I mean, if you want. I have a different job for you. This arriviste brought his trouble to our town and refused to recompense the owners of the Cart and Coin for their lost horse and buy a table at the luncheon in support of the stable hand’s family I threw.” 
“I am not sure what the issue is.” 
“He’s not a good person. He’s a bad person. And he’s bad for the town, and people are getting killed. And the  Lord Protector of Triboar,” and she pauses as if in pain, “Darathra Shendrel has only thought it appropriate to warn him with notices and stern words.”
Still, nobody understands what she is getting at.
“Let me explain. I ran for high office and was defeated by that nobody. I wouldn’t care, but nobody is a Harper Agent. I believe Triboar must be allied to the Lord’s Alliance, which will not happen while Darathra is here. I need to win the next election, and getting rid of this problem would help me win the election. It would show her ineffectual leadership and unwillingness to deal with the riff-raff.” 
Orsik gets the hint, “So you want us to finish the job the assassins botched?”
“Whatever you think is appropriate.” 
Orsik turns to Cindrell, “We need to talk.” 
Orsik is not in favor of killing random people. It’s not who he is now. But Thistle tells him he has a plan, so the fighter shrugs and nods.
The fairy turns to Cindrell, “Could we have an IOU made out to us? We know you are good for your money, but we’ve had some bad luck with other people.” 
She’s outraged and tries to solicit Vardis to intervene, but he refuses, “They have a mind of their own.”
Thistle insists.
Cindrell then tries to appeal to Orsik directly by offering to clear up his name in Waterdeep. Still, when he asks for all of the North, she laughs and says, “I am not a miracle worker.” 
Finally, she concedes, “Name your price.” 
Orsik proposes 10,000 gold, and she snorts, “With that price, I could get a magic user to erase him for every timeline of every existence.” 
“How about 500? 550 if I win the election?” 
Orsik shrugs, “Well, good luck with your election. You have a lot of schmoozing and hanging out and glad hanging out and pressing the flesh to do if you want to win the election.”
Seeing him leave, she shouts to Thistle, “What about you?” 
Thistle stares at her, “I’ll do it if you sign the IOU.” She’s resistant at first but relents when he unleashes the twinkle of his fairy eyes, the same eyes that Thistle’s lost love fell in love with. 
“Is this really necessary?”
“Ever since the Dragon Wars, you can’t rely on people to pay their bills.” 
She sighs and hands him a paper outlining the nature of the deal without getting into too many specifics. 
As they leave the ranch, Orsik asks, “What are you thinking?”
“Look, we don’t want the Lord’s Alliance here. So I think we go to this guy and tell him about the assassination, then to Darathra and tell her about this. And basically, ensure that Cindrell doesn’t win the election.” 
Orsik fists bump him.

Orsik and the rest of the gang don’t realize that Thistle is a member of the Seelie court. It’s why Prince Thornacious recognized him, and Blasius was his friend. He was a minor member but still a member. For him, intrigue is what members of the court do for fun. And seeing how bad Cindrell was at conspiracy, he had to show her how it was done. 
The following day, Thistle heads out to Urgala and tells her about the seriously odd shenanigans that Cindrell has asked him to do without revealing anything. He makes it known to her that Cindrell’s plans for the next election of the Lord Protector are underhanded, to say the least. 
Urgala is confused.  
Thistle says, “Well, she did ask us to kill Hyuth Kolstaag.” And then presents the contract. Urgala is curiously not surprised, “I do expect anything from her.” She then pauses, “But why him?” 
She stares at a corner in her drawing room in the Northshield House and says, “Hyuth was one of the more pain-in-the-ass magic users who went on the adventuring circuit. It was always about him. But you could rely on him during a fight. And now he is retired and for reasons he decided to come here. Seemed like a good idea, right? No. A guy like him has enemies. And he brings trouble, the kind of trouble that makes people feel unsafe.” She stops.
“Did you hear about what happened a couple of weeks ago?”
Thistle lies, “No.” 
She repeats the story of the assassins and then adds, “When you’re an adventurer, and you set up shop in a new time, and your old enemies come around to try and kill you and then kill a stablehand and steal a horse, it’s kind of expected to give the folks some money and sound sorry. This guy, Hyuth, summons some gargoyles to protect him and refuses to pay anything. He won’t even send a note to the family. Seriously, that’s some pretty shitty behavior. Makes all of us retired adventurers look bad.” 
“So we should kill him?”
“Now, I wouldn’t say that. I am not the person to judge. My book has some bad pages, and I have sinned.” 
Thistle feigns surprise.
“Yes, I have. And I don’t think I should judge. But if someone were to get him to be a better neighbor, that would be a good thing….” 
As they leave, Urgala mumbles, “I never imagined that Cindrell would be that cold-blooded.” 
Thistle then rejoins the 3HPP crew at the Talking Troll, and they all head to Darathra at the central tower. 
She is pleased to see them and congratulates them on the success of their play. She then says, “All this new business you brought into town! People are going to love you. Thank goodness you aren’t running for office,” she winks.
Thistle doesn’t waste any time, “we have a contract she signed to kill Hyuth, and as Lord Protector, we know you might object. And since we are blood brothers, I thought we should bring this to you.”
Darathra smiles like a cat about to pounce on a rat, “You did the right thing. I am going to bury her with this. And I will use this to get Hyuth to calm down because the next hit won’t just show up at my desk. And when he calms down, without murder, I will win in a landslide. I need to go talk to that shitty magic user right now.” 
Orsik intervenes, “We can do that. We’ll make sure he knows it’s from you.” 
“Tell him that I, the Lord Protector intervened to stop this incident. And that I may not be able to intervene in the future. And he may want to reconsider being a productive citizen and thus protected by the 12.” 
The group leaves, and she shouts, “Bring the contract back when you are done. I’ll need it for safekeeping.”
As the 3HPP approach the mansion of Hyuth, they notice its size, its opulence, and the missing roof that has been obliterated and has scorch marks everywhere. 
A whiny voice screeches, “Who are you?” 
“Here to repair your roof,” Orsik says. Thistle had suggested that they use that as an angle to get in. 
“I don’t want it fixed.” 
“Well, it’s free.” 
“I don’t trust you. I don’t trust anybody. Everyone is out to kill me.” 
“The Lord Protector asked us to come to fix the mansion. The roof damage affects property values, creating an eye sore, and she offered to foot the bill.” 
“PROPERTY VALUES! THIS IS HARASSMENT!” he screeches. Then pauses, “She offered to pay?” 
And with that, the group enters the house and sees a 55-year-old, tired man. He has a paunch, grey hair, and crow’s eyes. He stares at the 3HPP as they walk in and says, “Most construction workers don’t carry great axes, swords, spells, and a bow.” 
Orsik says, “We use magic.” 
Hyuth sighs and then leads the group to his second-floor study. It is a complete disaster area. Burned papers are all over, the desk is smashed, the roof is trashed, and the rain, wind, and sun have damaged whatever was left. 
At this point, Orsik turns to Thistle, “So what’s the next move.” But poor Thistle wasn’t very good at Seelie court intrigues and is unsure.
