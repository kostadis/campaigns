# Chapter 47 - Quests Major and Minor
## Vardis
He groans when he hears his friend ask – “what’s your favorite book” and the Oracle responds with the “collected sayings of Annam” and the last torch winks out. 
“Well that’s it,” mutters the arrogant dwarf, as he marches towards the exit of the room.
Later that evening as they rest in one of the rooms of the faithful, the elven cleric turns to his friend, “what next?” 
Orsik pulls out two little note books from his pockets, one marked, “main quests” and the other titled, “side quests” he skims through them and turns to Yasmeen, “we should probably bring you back to civilization.” 
“That would be nice,” mutters the freezing gnome. 
Vardis turns to him, “but we can ask more questions.” 
“Six per day?” 
Thistle overhears them and shouts, “I want to ask where Adimond Kroskas is.” 
“You would.” 
“Yes.”
“Fine.” 
And the next morning the Oracle answers the question, “Maelstorm.” 
## Thistle
“And how do I get to this Maelstorm?” 
“King Hekaton’s conch will take you there. The path you choose here will lead you to it.”
Thistle reflects on what he has learned so far, that the Storm King’s court is in chaos, that a conch of teleportation will take him to the Maelstorm which may be the name of the court, that his enemy Adimond Kroskas is there. 
And of course, Orsik asks – “what act can I perform that will most impress the Zhentarim?” 
There is a moment of silence, and Annam says, “End this Giant War thus restoring the trade routes and secure preferential treatment from the Storm Giant King for Zhentarim trade merchants.” 
Thistle turns to Unla Kee hoping for something better, and the halfling stares smugly back and whispers, “I know how the world ends, this Oracle has nothing to teach me.” 
Remembering a game he played in the fey court where he won the game two truths in a lie with the royal lawyers, Thistle asks, “tell us which of the many burial grounds contains the artifact that will lead us to Duke Zalto?” 
A deep laughter echoes in the chamber, and the Oracle says, “I have been doing this for a very long time.” 
And a torch winks out. 
He flutters his wings – “what do the evil giants want?” 
“Power, glory, and the favor of their gods, so that when the ordning is remade, they are elevated to the top.”
Orsik smirks at Thistle who is just a tad bit annoyed.
And then he asks – “what actually happened to Urgala’s wife?” 
“She fell to her death in a well that lead to the Underdark.” 
“Anticlimactic,” he thinks.
## Orsik

As they reach the entrance of the Eye of the World, Orsik notices a strange sight, a ladder hanging in front of him.
He looks up and there in the sky is a strange vehicle, hanging. It looks like a small ship with sleigh runners, held aloft by a giant red balloon.
Before he can do anything, Thistle flies up. 
Grumbling he starts the climb up the ladder, ready for combat, and is surprised to see a group of people clad in black leather, with black leather masks staring at them. 
When everyone else arrives, a female voice begins – “I am Delsephine, captain of this ship. And here is my crew, Nyzroth, Brassik, Laz, Oriskus, Perella, Tralt, and Zalthia. We come to you on behalf of Klauth, the Great Dragon of the North! Klauth offers you this vessel and our services as a gift, so that you may cross his vast dominion while avoiding the many perils of the land below. War against the giants is inevitable, but the Great Dragon commands you forge ahead and face your destiny! The ordning of the giants is broken, and they are not united. Slay their leaders, and you may yet prevail. If you succeed in quelling the giant threat, Klauth bids you come to his hidden vale, so that he can reward your bravery.”
Orsik turns to the 3HPP and shouts, “huddle.” 
He reminds everyone that the Giants and the Dragons are two different ethno-religious groups with a belief in their ultimate triump and domination of the world and as a result hate each other. Klauth, as a Dragon, is well aware that his group was terribly wounded at the well of the dragon and that the last thing he wants is the Lord’s Alliance to come sniffing into the Spine of the World. 
“The enemy of the enemy,” he says, and they all agree. 
“So you’ll take us anywhere?” 
“Yes.” 
“Then to Everlund.” 
Delsephine looks at him, “I am not sure how this helps us in our mission.” 
“It’s really complicated, and we don’t trust so you for reasons of operational security, we’ll just keep this to the 3HPP.” 
She stares at him and commands her crew to set sail.
As the sky boat floats, Orsik notes how the fairy is increasingly uncomfortable about something.
“What do you feel about the Cult of the Dragon?” 
Delsephine glares at Thistle and for a moment she seems ready to hurt the small creature and then stares out into the sky and explains, “Severin was a fool who redirected the cult to invest all of its resources in the insane plot to bring Tiamat into the world. He walked away from the one divine purpose of the cult which was to end the insane war between Tiamat and Bahamut through the creation of a new purer kind of Dragon, the Dracolich.” She laughs, bitterly. “Tiamat thought the plan to enter the world was hers, but she was played for a fool by Zariel, her hostess in the planes of Avernus in the Nine Hell. She thought she could claim this world for her own, and thus end the war, but she failed.” Delsephine laughs, “and of course she failed.”
“So you were a member during these events?”
“No. I left after I was told that all Dracolich research was to be put on hold while we secured loot for the great ritual. Klauth offered me an opportunity, and I seized it.”
She then begins to talk about Klauth and what she feels for the great red dragon. Orsik, who is an open minded kind of guy, is a little taken aback by her deep sexual attraction for the great wyrm that she can barely hide.
Orsik is relieved when Thistle asks her about his nemesis.
She spits, “Damn him. Severin trusted that creature to provide him powerful magics. And he or was it a she? Did not.” 
Orsik is intrigued that Kroskas is someone who was important enough to be working with the powerful leader of the Cult of the Dragon. 
As they fly off, Orsik turns to his friend Vardis, “well here we are, and no frosty.” 
“He bailed on us.”
Thistle tries to explain that he felt that he needed to stick around and guard the eye but then shrugs, and says, “yeah. I mean he rarely entered the fight.”
“Like pulling teeth.”
Orsik
After several days of airborne travel, Orsik found himself once again in Danivarr’s House talking to Dral Thelev. 
“Have you solved the mystery of the Ordning? Have you brought peace to the world?” 
“We’re working on that.” 
Vardis adds, “it’s an ongoing project.” 
And Orsik finishes his thought, “the world’s a really big place. And there are a lot of cities you have to visit.” 
Dral’s face is covered in a momentary look of concern as he mutters, “I am sure Krowen Valharrow chose you for a reason.” He then smiles, “Some of Silvery Moon’s finest?” 
Thistle says, “we are looking for Captain Zoar.” 
Dral smiles, “that should be easy enough.” He tells one of the boys  in the tavern to go find the Captain. 
Orsik drinks the free wine waiting for the Captain who eventually shows up with three guards. He mutters at Dral, “why was I summoned?” 
Dral steps forwards and points to the 3HPP and Orsik who smiles as he tips a glass at the Captain.
“Smelly adventurers?” 
Things are about to go sideways when Thistle abruptly hands over the letter of Mark from Tamalin Zoar, Captain Zoar’s sister and the mayor of Calling Horns. 
The Captain is taken aback, “you have a letter of introduction from my scoundrel of a sister?” He smiles ruefully, “she started off as a cutthroat, a brigand, a cut-purse, robbed folks up and down the coast, became a member of the gang of women thieves in Triboar, and then got older, bought a house on a road, has built a town and is now mayor.” He then puffs his chess out, “she’s become a person of character and substance. I am very proud of her. If she thinks you are worthy of a letter of introduction, then you are.”
“Of course we are,” says the smoothe Vardis in a silky tone of voice.
Captain Zoar reads the letter carefully and then asks, “you need an introduction to the elders of Everlund? A fine meal?”
Thistle interjects, “we are looking for a passage to Silvery Moon. The last time we were there we had some difficulty getting in and out of town. Could you write a note saying we are in Silvery Moon as your agents?”
“So you want me to give you a letter with my mark, saying that you are on Captain of the Guard business and the agents of Silvery Moon should give you all assistance and grace?” 
Orsik tries to say yes, and groans when Thistle corrects him, “yes and we want to make sure there is a reference to a criminal organization operating in Silvery Moon that is trafficking demons.” 
To Orsik’s surprise, Captain Zoar is excited. “Dral get me a drink. I have a story to tell.” He sits down and talks, “the bastards of Silvery Moon abandoned Sundbar when the orc hordes attacked turning the city into a ruin. They didn’t raise a finger to help. And so we decided to leave the Lord’s Alliance which is not an alliance.” Seeing Orsik’s interest, he continues with a detailed and rambling discussion of the various battles that took place around Sundbar. And then he ends saying, “I am not surprised they are trafficking demons. That matches the blackness and depravity of their souls” 
Orsik waits to see what will happen next. The Captain stares at Orsik and then shouts at one of his guards, “go fetch me a letter so I can affix my mark on an introduction.” The guard groans and leaves. 
The dwarf is about to sit down and wait when Zoar shouts at Dral, “Bring foods and drink. And those little fishies that you make with the special sauce and the salad and the dressings.” 
When the guard returns, Zoar writes his letter Mark and asks, “ Your group’s name?” 
“3HPP,” mutters Thistle.
“You are the three half-pints and a pint?” 
Orsik stutters, “oh, no it doesn’t stand for anything.”
“Because I heard of the play. You are the producers of the play that has Bob Saget? It is an amazing production. I haven’t had a chance to see it, but friends have. And I do so look forward to it when it comes to town in a few weeks.” 
Orsik is delighted and is calculating the coin he will make, and then says, “you must see it several times!” 
“Rumor has it that you were silent backers?” 
“Yes. Although it involved quite a bit of work too. It was a little weird.” As he reflects on the events of the play. 
“Well it’s a smash performance every time. Although each time the play ends there is a different ending.” Then Zoar takes a sip of his wine, stretches back, and says, “And the lead actor, Bob Saget puts on a terrifying performance as a demon.”
Orsik worried that the demon may have killed folks, probes using an offhand question, “ and no deaths and murders!” 
“No, why would you ask?”
“The original plot had some murders, but we stopped that.”
“Is it family friendly? I was planning to take my children.” 
“Totally safe.” 
At this Captain Zoar is excited, “Given your previous encounters with the Silvery Moon constabulary, I was thinking of using a different name for your group. But now, given your fame, I think I should stick it to them. The idea that Everlund is allied with the producers of the smash production is going to make them eat some humble pie!”
As he finishes the paper work, Thistle asks, “have you considered running for me?” 
Orsik smiles, the smile of a wolf arriving on his prey, “you think about it and let us know how we can help you.” 
The Captain is taken aback, reflects on the offer and then says, “I am quite pleased with my role as High Captain. I fully support the current speaker, Vatrice Stormwright, and I don’t question her leadership and she’s up for re-election in a few years and many things can happen. Although if you were to be seen as defeating the Giant threat that would greatly increase my prestige.” 
With the business concluded, and High Captain Horix Zoar, finally returning to his duties, fed, a little bit inebriated and quite happy, Orsik turns to the group, “let’s go get ourselves an artifact.” 
Orsik, as a dwarf, and a cosmopolitan itinerant sale-sword, had learned much about the Nether Mountains. The first is that they looked dark and brooding. He never understood that. He saw the mountains as a rich repository of tangible riches like granite. That was not motivated his interest in following up on the quest from the Mary and the Fighters of Light. The Nether Mountains were said to have magical items that once belonged to the ancient Netheril. It was entirely possible that the Fighters of Light had lost such an artifact. Although why Kobolds would possess it was confusing. 
From the merchants that they bought supplies from, Orsik also learned that the mountains are also full of tribes of orcs, while the eastern peaks are home to several blue dragons chased out of the northern Anauroch by a dragon known as the Doom of Desert.
“I’ll have to ask Delsephine if she knows anything about that,” he mutters. 
## Vardis

Much to Vardis’ relief, Thistle found the trail and lead them directly to the entrance of the Kobold cave. As he stared at it he remarked to himself that Kobold warrens tend to look  the same. He wondered if it was some kind of instinctual behavioural pattern that drove that or something else. 
“Kobolds,” thinks Vardis. And watches as Unla Kee and Thistle walk into the warrens with no effort. 

He and Orsik are able to squeeze through the small entrance, despite their height and armor. 

Vardis is again annoyed to discover that the tunnels are barely tall enough for him to squeeze through, reaching 5-6 feet in height and sometimes 4-5 feet. He will not be able to move as quickly as he wants through these warrens. 
What he immediately notices is that there is a dim light in the tunnels, created by fire-light beatles. 
“No matter, that’s what our light sources are for.”
