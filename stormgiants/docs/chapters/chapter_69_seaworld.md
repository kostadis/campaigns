# Chapter 69 Seaworld 



Vardis, Thistle, and Unla Kee watch as Orsik advances on the crabs. They are wasting valuable time and resources for Orsik to get some gold.

Thistle looks for a mechanism to cause the portcullis to fall but realizes that the storm giants use brute strength. Raising the gates by hand is more straightforward as they can swim underwater for protracted periods and are very strong. 

Vardis notices that the Crabs have noticed Orsik. He grabs some sulfur and bat guano, points to the right between them, and a mote of magical fire rushes forward. It stops in midwater, and a burst of fire engulfs the two beasts. 

“Magic operates very differently than fire underwater.” 

Thistle signals to Thuul, and while the crabs are still fixated on Orsik and Unla Kee, they don’t see Thistle, who hits one of them with his arrows, and Thuul, who spits acid.

Vardis waves his hand, and a single chime of a dolorous bell fills the water. He points at the remaining crab. The creature writhes under the effect of Toll the Dead. 

Then, before it can react, two more arrow bolts strike him from Thistle's hand. 

Unla Kee, impressed with the power of Toll the Dead, casts the same spell, but this time, the Crustacean ignores the awful sound. 

There is one last badly injured monstrous beast, and Orsik tosses two hand axes. The first bounces off the crab’s armor harmlessly. The second kills the creature. 

As he digs through the treasure, he shouts, “Anyone who thought this was a bad idea doesn’t get any treasure. Except Vardis, who’s my friend and always has my back.” 

The haul is unimpressive at first, just some gold pieces and a goblet, until Orsik stumbles on two obvious magic items. The first he is to learn later is a Magma Warpick. 

The second item is Weaving Wasp Needles. These needles can be used to craft clothing or to restrain an enemy. 

Orsik is very pleased with himself. 

Thistle takes stock of their current situation. They are standing on the edge of a vortex that goes up 180 feet and is over 100 feet across. At the bottom of the vortex is a drain that would require a 50-foot plug. Going through the vortex would be hard but possible. Around the bottom of the vortex are shipwrecks. The crabs and the storm giants have probably picked them clean of any valuables. 

“Let’s continue counterclockwise,” he mutters. 

A bit further down is an underground tunnel that ends in a portcullis. Looking through the portcullis, it’s clear that there is air. Right in front of the portcullis is a killer whale. 

“I don’t think it is the right way,” he explains to his allies. 

But Vardis, Orsik, and Unla Kee need to be more convinced. 

Vardis points out that Thistle is the master of infiltration, and he could look. 

And so the fairy slips through the gate. Unlike the Fire Giants, the shark is more observant, notices Thistle, and swims straight at him. 

Thistle, wanting to avoid killing another beast of nature, casts “Speak with Animals.” The creature is confused, and Thistle shouts, “We are not your enemy.” 

The shark mutters, “Nym promised better tasty snacks. You are barely a mouthful. But you will do.” 

Thistle swims up, and as he does, the Shark snaps at him, wearing him down slightly. However, Thistle escapes into the air above the pool. There, he notices a stairwell going up and snacks.

He picks up a pile of snacks and throws them in the water. The shark devours the food in glee. 

Thistle coughs, “Ahem?”

“You are feeding me. You are not Nym. Why are you feeding me?” 

“Nym sent me.” 

The shark sticks its head above the water and tries to bring Thistle into focus, “You are much smaller than many of Nym’s friends. And I am so sorry for biting you. They don’t usually come that way. She comes from the shore.” 

Thistle asks, “Have you seen any dragons?”

The shark explodes - “ There's a dragon here? If there had been a dragon, I would have defended Nym. I would have killed it. Do you see any dragon bones? Of course, you don't. What are you saying about there's a dragon? Where's the dragon?”

Thistle tries to explain, “The dragon is a friend of Nym’s.” 

This confuses the shark, and it shouts, “If you have more fish, I’ll be happy to talk; if not, this conversation has become very boring.” 

Meanwhile, the rest of the 3HPP swim past the whale onto the shore. 

After devouring his snacks, the shark declares in a large, imperious voice, “I am Nym's Whale. I am her favorite pet. Nobody comes here other than her. I am the great whale. I protect Nym while she swims. “ 

At this point, the shark gets annoyed. “Are you sure you are a friend of Nym’s? I have never seen anyone but her here.” 

“I am usually invisible.” 

The shark snorts, “I don’t think you are her friend. But you fed me. I just want you to know that I must eat you if you get into the water. It won’t be personal.” 

Thistle watches as the creature submerges. 

He then looks around and declares, “The stairway going up probably goes to her quarters. I wonder what’s down the south hallway?” 

The hallway leads to an air-filled cave under 5 feet of water. A staircase in the south walls rises to another cavern. Thistle admires the cultivated coral garden. Coral growths sprout from walls and rock formations, all colorful and immense.

Thistle knows this coral is worth a fortune, but Adimond is here. Mining it would take time, resources, and risk exposure. He turns to the rest of the 3HPP and says, “This coral is worthless.” 

The ranger floats through the room past a stalagmite. A fleeting moment of horror blanks out all rational thought: the stalagmite he is passing has sprouted a single red, blinking eye above a slobbering, fanged mouth. Tendrils leap from its body, whipping out to snag the unwary for its next meal, the rocky hide proof against all but the strongest blows.

The tendrils reach out to every member of the 3HPP. Only Thistle and Unla Kee are grappled. 

It drags the ranger to its mouth and bites, but the fair dodges the teeth. 

Vardis reaches for the guano and sulfur, carefully measures the room, and arranges the fireball so that the fiery explosion reaches but does not touch Thistle. 

Orsik rushes in and strikes with his sword, but the tough hide stops his blows. 

Thistle tries to get free and fails. 

Unla Kee also prepares a fireball and uses Vardis’s previously carefully positioned fireball for his. And once again, the room explodes in fire, and not one of Thistle’s whiskers is singed. 

The creature flails at Vardis and Orsik to no effect. 

Vardis casts Toll the Dead, but the creature can see through the evil of the mournful sound of the bell and is unaffected. 

Unla Kee scoffs at Vardis and reaches for his bag of guano and sulfur. Once again, the room fills with a fiery explosion. The creature is now clearly hurt but still alive. 

Orsik then whacks the creature with his sword, and it dies. He is pleased with himself. He looks for treasure but finds nothing. He turns to Thistle and asks, " Are you sure this Coral is worthless?”

“Yes.” 

With that, the ranger heads towards the southern passageway. The passageway opens into another large room with a pool and a shark. The room also has several exits: a passageway to the southeast blocked by a portcullis, a spiral staircase going up, and a staircase going west. Around the room are cases of whale food. In the southeast corner is an anemone chest. 

Thistle throws some fish into the water. The shark starts chomping and then pokes its head up. It shouts, “Well, hello there. You are tiny.” 

“I am a friend.” 

“Yes, you are. Mirran usually feeds me. Why are you here? Is she okay?” 

“Phew. Mirran’s my pet.”

“Your pet?” 

“Yes. She needs this awkward space with no water to survive, so I bring her back whenever she goes out.” 

“Do you have any other pets?” 

“No, just the one. Having more pets is too much work. They start to squabble and demand things. I’m very much a one pet animal.” 

“Does she bring any pets?”

“Oh no. Although she spends much time hitting something outside the pool.” 

Thistle takes note of the sparring dummy down the west passageway. He asks the whale, “Where does she come from when she arrives?” 

The shark points his nose to the spiral staircase and continues, “Whenever she needs to rest and relax after a frustrating day, she comes here. And I take my pet out for a ride so it can relax.”

Thistle wonders if the shark has a mechanism to open the portcullis, and it says, “No. And if I did, I wouldn’t use it; otherwise, the pet would not feel in control. I am pretty progressive. By the way, have you met the other whale? It thinks it is the pet. A disgrace.” 

While Thistle was talking to the whale, Unla Kee explored the anemone. He recognized it as an engineered animal used to store valuables. As he looked at it some more, he noticed it was trapped. Opening it would require a delicate and careful application of touches. Unla Kee proceeded and was successful in opening the chest. 

He looks inside and finds 400 platinum pieces from various countries.

Thistle, done talking to the whale, floats down and turns to his team - “Do we wait here for Mirren to come down, or do we go up?” 

Unla Kee is already moving up the spiral staircase. 

Thistle groans, “Up it is…”

As they go up the spiral staircase, Thistle notices a secret door.
