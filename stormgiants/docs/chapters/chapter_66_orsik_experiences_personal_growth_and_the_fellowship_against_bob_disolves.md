# Chapter 66 Orsik experiences personal growth and the fellowship against Bob disolves. 

## Night of the 5th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleint 
### The battle with Bob. 

The battle is over. Bob is gone. 

It wasn’t that epic of a battle. Bob was ready with his zombie army. Despite the attempt at caution, the 3HPP and their allies were noticed. It looked grim. But Orsik had a sinister plan up his sleeve. He could make bags of holdings as an artificer, and he prepared two of them. So, while Bob taunted them, Unla Kee and Thuul approached the creature. As they got close enough, Unla Kee tossed the bag of holding towards the demon, and as the bag stood at the feet of the demon, Thuul rushed forward, stuffing a second bag in the first. 

Bob, the Red Wizard, and Thuul were caught in the blast. 

Lord Zymorven was momentarily outraged when he realized several citizens of Silverymoon who were still alive got caught up in the blast. 

But Bob is gone. 

With Bob gone, the zombie hordes were easy enough pickings for Vardis’ power over the undead, a few fireballs, and the sword and bow of Zymorven, Daratha, and Urgala. 

The Drow fighters who had been there to protect the Red Mage fled before being caught. Who knows what happened to them? 

Vardis finds the whole outcome very funny, the ease with which Bob was disposed of. 

“Touring the top food places of the Astral Plane,” laughs Vardis. 

The theater is full of dead zombies dressed like members of the 3HPP—a mass of decayed bodies dressed to look like Vardis, Orsik, Unla Kee, and Thistle. 

“Where are all the theatergoers?” asks Urgala.

“We don’t know. We entered the theater to see what they were all raving about and found these zombies. And since Bob was about to start the apocalypse today, we killed them. Honestly, I am disappointed I did not get to see what everyone was raving about.” 

Lord Zymorven tries to keep calm, “Does that mean we killed 200 of Silverymoon’s citizens?” 

Orsik shakes his head, “We’ll go find them.” 

Vardis adds, “And I am very motivated to go find them because I am really interested to see what this play was about. Like -why- was it so cool.” 

Darathra is furious, “We sent him to the Astral plane. We were supposed to kill him. He’ll be back.” 

“I had a foolproof idea,” protests Orsik.

“He can come back?” queries a confused and worried Vardis.

Darathra stares up at the theater's roof, pleading to whatever gods of Faerun made her ally with the 3HPP, and shouts, “Do you not understand how the multiverse works?” 

“I saved Silverymoon, right?” whines Orsik.

She pinches the bridge of her nose with her left hand, then rubs her temples with each hand, then takes a deep breath, “What you did was send 300 innocent people to a pocket dimension that.”

Orsik interjects, “Bob did that.” 

“We have no way to bring them back.” 

Orsik looks at him,r confused, “So we’ll get them back.”

And Darathra screams in exasperation, “THE ONE PERSON WHO KNOWS WHERE THEY ARE IS LOST IN THE ASTRAL PLANE!!!” 

“Well, when he comes back, we can ask him.”

Darathra, “YOU WANT TO BRING A DEMON BACK?” 

Urgala, who has finally recovered from the shock of the battle,e asks in a worried tone, “Is this my fault, too?” 

“We’re heroes!”

“We just sent the only evidence of this crime to the Astral Plane.” 

Orsik takes the tone of a parent reassuring their child, “What about the zombies?” 

“They are going to blame us for the zombies. See how they are dressed like you? They’ll assume this is something you did. And the powers that be will need to blame someone. THIS IS A DISASTER!” 

Vardis looks at her patiently and says, “Calm down. Flying off the handle won’t do anyone any good.” 

Orsik stares at the carrion and death and says, “We could burn it all?” 

Vardis turns his head, “Burn everything?”

Orsik warms up to the idea and shouts, “Yeah, we could burn everything. And if there is nothing here, there is no evidence. It was a tragic fire.” 
Urgala nods, “There is some promise in that idea.” 

Vardis shrugs, “The absence of evidence isn’t the evidence of absence of a crime.” 

Orsik dismisses his friend's concerns by tapping his headband, “My headband is tingling. This will work.” 

Vardis, who never liked the plan to save Silverymoon, mutters, “I think your headband is malfunctioning.” 

Lord Zymorven coughs, “Orsik, I admire your ruthlessness. You focused on what needed to be done. You chose to sacrifice those innocent victims, and that was a hard choice.”

Vardis nods, “Collateral damage is unavoidable in war.” 

“Yes, and as the commander on the field, Orsik and Orsik alone made that decision. I was not a party to that decision. And I would have made different decisions. But he was in charge, and it was his decision alone.” 

Vardis begins to understand that Lord Zymorven is making it clear who will bear any responsibility for what happened. 

“And so I declare that our debts between our families are cleared. And if anyone should inquire what happened, I will say that I obey my commander who made the decisions in the heat of the battle and did not consult me in any of his plans.” 

Orsik studies the windbag, and mutters, “Good Chat.” 

Lord Zymorven is annoyed. “I am not ready to be cut off. I have several more crucial points to make.” 

As Vardis wonders if he can turn Lord Zymorven, Urgala mutters, “There is a reason I dumped his ass.”

“Why did you date him in the first place?” 

She shrugs, “Two people adventuring, strange things happen.”
Vardis sighs, “Ah yes, the experimental years.” 

Urgala sighs, “You’re in the forest in the middle of the night.”

“I am too familiar.” 

“Exactly.”

And then Vardis looks around, “Look at the company I keep.”

Urgala smirks, “I wasn’t going say anything…” 

Orsik realizes what she thinks, “We’re just friends.” 

Vardis insists, “We go way back. Legacy friends.” 

Urgala has a smirk on her face when she says, “Of course you are.” 

Lord Zymorven has been droning on, and to change the conversation, the elf and the dwarf turn to listen to him: “Furthermore, I want to be clear: There is no evidence that Bob was a demon. No demonic actions were taken, and no definitive proof was provided. I could not testify in a court of law that Bob was a demon.” 

Orsik realizes that the Lord is still trying to cover his ass, “Well, if he comes back, that might be proof.” 

Lord Zymorven turns pale, “I have no opinion on that. I just want my position vis-a-vis the events here to be clearly understood.” 

Orsik is annoyed, “Well, if you are not proud of the events and what we accomplished.”

The old fighter becomes even paler. “No, I am very proud of what we accomplished and **your **valiant leadership.” 

Vardis coughs, “We? You’ve been shirking responsibility… ” 

Orsik silences him, “No, I’ll take we. He’s supposed to help with the aristocrats and spread the good news that we saved the town.” 

Urgala’s ex is frantic: “I have been trying to make the point that a bunch of theatergoers have disappeared.”

Vardis shrugs, “Only one theater’s worth.” 

“These were not random people. These are members of society. Over 200 disappeared.” 

Orsik looks around, “There are about 50 zombie bodies. So we burn the place down, and people can’t identify their loved ones. Body parts are all over the place. Some are destroyed. They died in the fire.” 

“And who will we blame on the fire,” shouts Zymorven. 

Orsik feels annoyed that Lord Zymorven doesn’t see the brilliance of his plan, “There was a threat in the theater. We rushed to neutralize it. Unfortunately, a fire broke out. We were unable to save the theatergoers.” 

“You intend to tell folks that you went into the theater and used fire spells????” he shrieks. 

Orsik sighs, “Vardis was turning the undead like it was going out of style. And the battle got boring, so he used fireball and flamestrike.” 

“Not a particularly effective spell,” mutters Vardis. 

Lord Zymorven shouts, “No. What happened was that we walked into the theater and saw what was going on. We tried valiantly to protect the innocent, but Bob, who was a demon, used fire spells in the theater. Unfortunately, the conflagration was intense. And although we defeated Bob, we could not save anyone else.” 

Vardis raises an eyebrow, “Don’t forget we saved the town.” 

“Yes, and we saved the town. I think that’s a fine story. Can we all agree to this?” 

Orsik smiles, “We’re getting our stories straight? Cool. I am down with that version.”

Realizing everyone must agree, he turns to Darathra, who thinks momentarily, “We’re all caught in a mess. If I told anyone I helped you, I’d get into so much trouble. My take is I was never here and never saw anything.” 

Vardis snarks, “No one takes accountability anymore.” 

Orsik nods, “Sounds Harper-like.” He pauses and puts his hand over his mouth in the shape of an o. “Did I say that out loud?” 

Darathra tries to kill him with her glare as she marches out. 

Orsik shouts, “You weren’t that big a help in the battle, lady.” 

She takes her sword and sheathes it. Then she shakes her head in frustration and marches out. 

“Need a lift,” asks Orsik. Seeing that she ignores him, he shouts, “Well, there was plenty of space. You should leave before it gets hot. And establish an alibi.”

Darathra turns around at him and spits each word, “I am out of here. We are done. Don’t ever come looking for me.” 

“So, life if I say hi, you won’t say hi?” 

“Of course, I’ll say hi. But no more calling in favors, no more special requests.” She stops at the door to the theater, turns inside, and shouts, “And no more plays.” 

Orsik starts to wonder how to burn the place down. 

Unla Kee takes it as his cue. He points one finger up, and a mote of fire heads up to the balcony. Then, it explodes in fire and the flammable materials there burn. He then points the other finger into the middle of the theater, and a bright, shiny star streaks into the middle. With a sudden explosive force, the room burns brightly. But the fire isn’t robust enough to sustain a burn. 

Thistle suddenly appears with all the rotting food and places it in a pile. He turns to Orsik and asks, “Can you please smash the benches?” 

“Because I am a dwarf?” 

“No, because you are strong.” 

“I am going to let that casual racism just drop.”

Vardis looks at him, “We’re in Silverymoon, and the racism just seeps into every conversation.”

“You're right. Two hundred hundred fewer today.”

Vardis laughs, “I am sure there were good people.” 

Lord Zymorven, hearing the discussion of race, decides it's his moment to leave the room quietly.

Urgala's laughter bursts into the room. “I haven’t had this much fun in years. Zymorven caught up in a mass murder spree? Is Darathra covering up a crime? I feel bad. I felt like I was doing you a favor. You have done me a favor. Do you have any idea for how long I have had to deal with those two self-righteous jerks because I had an affair? You knocked those two arrogant bastards seven pegs. If you need anything from me, I’m here for you.” 

Unla Kee observes that Bob’s victims were not the “goodly races.”:

“We could blame them? Here’s what we say - this was a demonic cult of the ungodly races that we uncovered. When they saw us arrive, they were convinced that their plot would be thwarted, and so slaughtered the theatergoers. The people of Silverymoon are going to believe this because it fits their preconceived racist stereotypes.” 

Orsik is troubled by this and says, “Look, we already have a story with Lord Zymorven. We can’t have two stories. So thank you, Unla Kee. It's a good initiative, but we’re sticking with the original plan.” 

Thistle drops his bow from the taught position. He was planning to pincushion the mage if Unla Kee didn’t get with the program. 

Vardis tries to settle everything: “This is just a normal part of the brainstorming process. There are no bad ideas. But we have chosen a plan, and it's time to execute.” 

Orsik sighs, “Okay, so can we start the fire?” 

Thistle nods and places the crushed beams on the pile of compost that he uses to ignite a bonfire. The 3HPP realizes that this bonfire will do the trick. 

Orsik wants to stay to be praised by the city for saving them, but Urgala encourages him to run. 

“There are spells that could reveal the truth. If Zymorven tells his story and nobody can find you guys, there will be less of a desire to ask hard questions, and it gives us time to find the missing people?” 

Orsik is  initially flustered that he didn’t come up with that, “Is his band failing him?” He then has a profound insight, “You know, the thing I am learning is that smart people surround themselves with other smart people and encourage them to come up with good ideas. My job is to identify the most brilliant idea.” 

Urgala looks at him and tries to keep the laugh out of her voice but can’t keep it out of her eyes, “I am leaving because I am going to lose my game face if I stay here much longer.” 

As they leave, Unla Kee feels a bit down. Orsik thinks it’s because Vardis’ ability to turn undead made it hard for him to shine and says, “You set up the Astral gate. Without you, we’re fighting Bob.” 

Unla Kee is startled. “Oh, I know that. I was just thinking about the chaos, civil strife, and evil we could have caused if we had told my story. The city would have erupted into violent race riots. It’s not that I am evil, but it’s one hell of a way to burn this evil place to the ground.”

And with that, they leave the burning building. 

The 3HPP arrives at the eastern city gate with the theater blazing in the background. There, a guard is delighted to see the real-life heroes made famous in the play, but when he sees the flames, he becomes agitated. He apologizes profusely and runs away, muttering something about someone he cares about who attended the play. 

To avoid meeting their friendly vampire and her spawn, the 3HPP board their airship in the morning.

## 6th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleint 

As they settle into the control tower, Orsik turns to Escarlotta, “This castle isn’t safe.” 

She’s surprised, “Safe for me.”

“But not for us.” 

The control gems form a confused expression.

Orsik mutters, “I didn’t capture the castle to have a room.” 

“You captured the castle,” she says in a disapproving giantess mom's voice. 

“Yes, we own it.” 

“You own it? Really? I am the flight command system. I am integrated with the whole castle. You are here because I find you amusing, and my goals line up with your goals. Think of this as a partnership, an agreement.”

Vardis asks, “Tenancy in common?” 

Orsik ignores her and turns to his friend, “If we get rid of the dracolich and the vampire, then we can have the whole castle.”

Then he stares at the control crystals and says out loud, “I am sure I can figure out how to use these crystals on my own.”

Escarlotta whispers gravely, “What?” 

Vardis tries to brush it off, “The headband has been defective.” 

Before this can escalate, Escarlotta says, “Look, you can’t get rid of me. I am the control system. But more importantly, why can’t we be friends?” 

Surprised, Orsik whines, “Because you keep telling us what to do and what not to do.”

She grumbles back, “I want to talk to people. You come in and give me orders, have no small talk, and don’t ask me how my day was.”

Orsik whines, “You have us traipsing around Faerun when we should save the world from giants.” 

Escarlotta is startled. “So why don’t you save the world from Giants? You have the conch. Why don’t you find out what’s up with King Hekaton?” 

Orsik is surprised. “That’s what you want us to do next?” Up until now, Escarlotta seemed more interested in revenge than saving the world, more furious at not being in the Ordning sweepstakes than ending the war. 

Her tone changes to a wistful reflective “Blagothkus and I had a long chat, and I realized that I misplaced a lot of my rage and anger at dying onto him. I came to understand his choices better. And we realized that we are good to each other, happy together, and willing to share the castle.” 

Orsik mumbles, “That’s a change.” 

“Yes, we saw how Thistle was willing to leave the woman he loved for a higher cause, and that made me appreciate how Blagothkus killing our son wasn’t his fault. The gods and, in particular, Amn were manipulating him for Amn’s agenda. We were caught up in events where our choices were circumscribed. If you can tell me how your day went, I’m happy to be your navigational system.”

Vardis tries this - small-talk - thing, “We saved silverymoon.” 

“You were willing to do the right thing. You were willing to clean up your own. You were willing to give up on the woman you loved to save a city of people who think that because of their race, your allies aren’t good. That is what heroes are. “

Orsik realizes he’s a hero and says in awe, “We did save them!” 

“That touched me. And it made it possible for Blagothkus and I to repair our relationship. And it made me want to talk about my feelings and needs.” 

Orsik is touched and then decides to treat Escarlotta as part of the team, “Vampire first?”

“I worry that the realms would be destroyed if you were to die. You are a critical player in the future of our corner of the world.” 

Orsik beams, “Could we get her to agree to join us and slaughter some enemies?”

Vardis nods, “Do the ends justify the means?” 

Blagothkus speaks for the first time, “That is precisely how we ended up in this emotional conflict between me and my wife.”

Escarlotta speaks, “Yes, the vampire must be destroyed. But the greater good matters more. It matters more than my petty hatred and frustrations. We must stop this mayhem that the giants are engaged in.” 

Orsik acknowledges her input with a thoughtful scratch of his chin, “What if we find good dragons and the Vampire and the Dracolich attack them?”

The gems bunch up into a ball as if in deep thought and then expand, “You want to make a deal with a vampire?” 

Vardis asks Orsik, “What do we have to offer her besides our blood?” 

Unla Kee chimes in that as she is the last member of the cult of the drag, there must be some cult artifact she wants that the 3HPP could fetch for her. 

Vardis turns to him, perplexed, “Memorabilia?” 

“Oh no, I am thinking something like an evil tome how to create a Dracolich from a dragon. Or an artifact that can be used to control Dracoliches.” 

“So, trade one problem for another?” 

Orsik observes that they do that a lot. Vardis nods and mutters, “That is our MO.” 

There’s a moment of silence, and then Orsik mutters, “Oka,y Unla Kee, blow the conch.” 

And he does. And —the world around them shimmers. 

## 6th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleint  - 
### Into the Maelstrom.

When the world stops shimmering, the 3HPP find themselves atop a glowing glyph carved into the floor of an alcove in the northeast corner of a rough-hewn chamber. 

The air is thick with the scent of the sea. The walls are slick with moisture and adorned with bioluminescent lichen, snails, starfish, and barnacles. Tiny crabs scuttle across the floor, avoiding a glowing glyph carved into the northeast corner. The chamber is filled with the haunting melody of a pipe organ and a powerful female voice. The music emanates from a spiral staircase that leads upwards. To the right, a circular pool plunges 40 feet deep. Two hallways stretched in opposite directions, each adorned with a life-sized statue of a storm giant. 

The dimensions of the room are most definitely storm giant-sized. It is roughly circular with a 100-foot diameter, and the ceilings are over fifty feet high. 

Orsik asks, “What do we need to do?” 

Vardis responds, “Talk to Queen Serissa because she might know where Hekaton is?” 

“I want to study these caverns a bit more… They are interesting.”  Orsik, examining the cavern masonry, is impressed despite himself. He mutters, "Storm Giants did this? Huh. Not bad. For giants, that is. The stonework's a bit rough and could use a dwarf's touch, but the scale of it... impressive, even for me." He runs a hand along the wall, noting the quality of the stone and the craftsmanship, grudgingly admitting, "Alright, maybe they know a thing or two about carving. Still, wouldn't want to live in it." He looks at one particular section, “Shoddy work, the kind of thing you would never see if a dwarf was making this place.” 

He then says, “Aha, I knew it. That’s why we couldn’t find this place. It’s deep underwater. With the dimensions of this place, it should have been obvious where it was, but not if it was hidden. And what better place than under the sea?” 

As Orsik examines the masonry, Vardis examines the pool and shouts, “Aha, this pool isn’t a pool but a passageway. It’s the physical entrance into this place.” 

Vardis turns to Unla Kee, “Will the conch take us back to the Sigil? Or let us escape?” 

Orsik mutters to himself, “C’mon, let’s keep going.” 

Vardis puts his foot down: “I would like to know how it works before we desperately look for an escape.” 

Just then, Orsik pattern matches to his insight from the previous day, “I don’t have to have the best plans, I just have to recognize them.” He says out loud, “Yes, that’s a great idea, Vardis.”

Vardis tries hard not to have his eyes roll. 

The conch is silent. The conch wielder can only blow on the device once per day. 

With that, the 3HPP explores the place they just landed in. The first place they look at is the geyser to the south. Vardis urges caution, and it’s a good thing because the geyser erupts, and scalding hot water that would have hurt fills the room. 

Thistle notes, “Right-hand rule?” 
And so they head to the north passage. At the end of the hallway, a statue depicts a male storm giant blowing a pink and blue conch shell.

On either side of the statue are guest rooms with beds fit for giants. Past the guest room to the west is a store room full of giant supplies. 

Orsik examines the wine barrels to see if there is any particularly good vintage, but there is not.
