# Chapter 84 - Thistle Decodes the Conspiracy

Thistle remembers Mary. Mary was an old lady in Everlund who warned passers-by of the Kraken. She had sent them on a goofball quest to get what she called an artifact but was a trinket. Her group was known as the “Fighters of the Light.” He remembers that part of the reason they went on the quest was that they didn’t have enough money to pay the admission fee, which was, at the time, *five* gold pieces.  

The artifact had been in the dark, brooding peaks of the Nether Mountains. Orsik and the team had been intrigued by the many legends of lost Netherese magic. Uncle Joon had noted that the mountains were also full of orcs, while the eastern peaks were home to several blue dragons chased out of the northern Anauroch desert by the Doom of the Desert.

A detail that Thistle had ignored at the time. Now he knew who the Doom was: Adimond Kroskas. 

“So what did you find,” asks Unla Kee.

“Nothing of value,” answers Thistle. 

Orsik is suspicious and asks about treasure, and Thistle quickly shares that. 

“That’s valuable.” remarks the mage. 

“No evidence of any written communications,” shrugs the fairy. “There’s always some written communication.”

“Were you expecting printed stationary?” 

Unla Kee wonders for a moment about the Kraken Society and whether they are using the name Kraken or whether there is some kind of Kraken manipulating events. Krakens were covered in the Nirany School of Magic, his alma mater, but more as legendary creatures, not as things that exist. 

As for Thistle, Uncle Joon never mentioned anything about society, but Krakens were famous in the Feywild. 

Unla Kee explains, “According to the sage of legendary creatures, they are Leviathans of Legend. At the beginning of time, Krakens served as fierce warriors of the gods. When the gods’ wars ended, the Krakens shrugged off their servitude, never again bound by other beings. According to Prof BigEyes, the Kraken is a primeval force that obliterates civilization's greatest achievements as if they were castles in the sand. I remember Prof BigEyes observing that when such creatures interact with the rest of the world, societies, and cults spring up to worship the creature. Such a thing may have happened with this Kraken.” 
Thistle then mutters, “But whether the Kraken Society is affiliated with a Kraken or just plays dress-up is unclear. Any evidence of telepathy?” 

Unla Kee scratches his head, trying to remember, “Oh, yeah. I  did ask how they talk and what languages they speak, and we were told telepathy over a limited range, like 100 feet.” 

Thistle thinks aloud - “Lord Khaspere Drylund and Lady Atalia are not the kind of people with the resources to steal a giant and hide him in a boat that can’t be tracked. What’s more likely that some mythical powerful creature would be involved in the mayoral elections of some non-descript town or dragons.” 

Everyone turns to him, confused. 

“Ilymrith, or Adminond Kroskas, wanted to continue his experiments on fairies.” He pauses for dramatic effect, “LOOK AT MY EYES!!!!” 

Pow is about to offer a compliment when he picks up the body language of the rest of the 3HPP and has the appropriate look of horror. 

“He needed a patron, and who better than the desperate Queen of the Storm Giants. Ilymrith is probably behind the dismantling of the Ordning itself. With Hekaton out of the way, he could work into Queen Serissa’s good graces and continue his work. The Kraken society is just a rebranded Cult of the Dragon.” 

Unla Kee looks at Thistle and tries hard not to point out the logical flaws but can’t help but wonder how a dragon could manipulate a lost oracle of giants. He then thinks that, more importantly, if such a dragon existed with such power, how would Unla Kee get that power? Given the opportunity for personal power, Unla Kee tries to poo-poo the idea, “A dragon with such power? I’m sure the dragon is a patsy. We shouldn’t try and kill him but talk.” 

“Dragons are evil. Did you see what he did to my eyes? DRAGON EYES!” 

Even the oblivious mage can pick up on the tone of anger from Unla Kee’s voice and is silent. 

Thistle then mutters, “I am not a freak.” 

As Thistle sits there stewing, Unla Kee wonders if there is another approach they could take to redirect Thistle from his murderous rage. 

“Could I join the Kraken society?”

“Do you want to work for a cult that has a remote brain-exploding device?” 

Unla Kee shudders. 
“What about the SEER? We could ask her?” 

Vardis splutters and Orsik shuts that idea down. 

At this point, Thistle has recovered from his rage, “Whenever there is trouble, it’s a dragon.” 

The 3HPP stares at him. 

He then rants, “The War of the Dragons, that thing that cost me my eyes.” He pauses, closes his eyes, and says, “I am not a freak.” After taking a deep breath, he continues, “The War of the Dragons was brought upon by Tiamat. She wanted to come into the world. So she tears up the North. And that’s how Iymrith gets the money to start capturing Fairies. Then, the First Faction defeats Tiamat, and Iymrith needs a new sponsor. The Dragon needs someone with resources and is desperate. Iymrith comes up with the plan of perverting the Oracle of Amman and starting a Giant war. She had hoped to use Eigeron, but his idiot father, Blagothkus, killed him. She’s about to give up on the plan and then comes up with a new plan. The war can still start. She just needs to find a new ally. She uses the Oracle to tell every giant lord that the ordning has been sundered. And the war starts. When the war started, she figured a giant would want her help. But they are all caught up in their master plan. Some are worried that if they work with a dragon, they will lose favor with their gods. None of them suspect the scam.  With the war raging, Iymrith is desperate, and she hatches a new plan involving the Storm Giants. She approaches Hekaton with an offer of help, but Hekaton says no. What to do? The Doom of the Desert decides to kill Nerissa. Iymrith assumes the King would go ballistic over the Queen’s death and wants her fairy-dragon hybrid to wage war on the little people. But Serissa gets in the way and convinces Hekaton to calm down, research, and figure it out. So, Iymrith now decides to get rid of the King. But this time, she wants him unreachable. So the dragon kidnaps Hekaton and hides him on a ship that no one can scry. Without Hekaton, Queen Serissa doesn’t have the credibility to hold the Giant world in order. Iymrith gets an introduction from the two sisters with a promise to deliver the throne to them. Queen Serissa is desperate for help and agrees to help the Dragon with his insane experiments. Now, no one must suspect that a Dragon is behind all this. I, of course, have a particular insight into their evil and saw through her plans. Lymrith needs a patsy and some muscle to kill Nerissa and kidnap Hekaton. What a better patsy than a bunch of dead-end cult of the dragon losers who are told that there is a new super powerful big bad that is a KRAKEN!” He pauses, sweat on his folder dripping, his eyes half-crazed, “It all fits.” 

Pow and the 3HPP stare at each other. Unla Kee breaks the silence, “Yep, that sounds about right.” 

“I’m guessing this means we are not going to Everlund?” asks Vardis

“Straight to the northern isles of the Trackless Sea.” 

Pow then turns to his new partners, “Will we have time to hire a crew to start the cleanup?” 

They look at him. 

“I need to hire a crew for basic cleanup and demolition work. I am thinking of ten people. It will cost about three sp per laborer, and we will have to double that since we are asking them to fly on a castle. And then there is the cost of supplies, so 1000 gold?” 

“If you stay here for a few days, I know some guys.” 

Thistle and Unla Kee want to purchase magic with their newfound wealth and agree. 

The next day, Pow has got his crew and says they will need one more day for supplies. The Shouan then starts to probe about the budget. 

He quickly explains that it will cost about 500,000 gold to turn the entire place into a grand casino. But for it to be a serviceable Casino, they will need 50,000 gold. As the place makes more money, they can reinvest and make it the jewel of the realms over time. 

Somehow, the conversation turns to theater. Pow asks, “You had this amazing theatre group headlined by that actor Bob, who was always in his demon costume! I went to several of the shows in Yartar. I heard he went to Silverymoon. The story I heard from a customer is that the place burned down, and he disappeared.” 

Orsik explains that the theater burned down, but Bob hasn’t disappeared. He has decided to take a leave of absence. 

Thistle explains that they still have the script of the play. 

Vardis reminds Thistle quietly that the script is a summoning ritual for a demon that they barely averted. 

Pow is pleased to hear that the script was saved, “Excellent! With Bob and his crew gone, we can use that script to bring in the crowds.” He then turns to the crew he has hired, “These are trusted members of the town. I have worked with them for many years, and they are all good people.” 

Thistle notes that they all appear to be from Shou and wonders to whom they are personally loyal. 

As they wait for the materials to be loaded, Thistle decides to talk to Escarlotta and see if they can do anything for her. 

She blushes when she hears that Thistle wants to do something for her. All control gems start to pulsate red, and Thistle assumes it’s blushing. 

“The grass on the tower and the tower itself is very dirty. It would be nice to have it clean!” 

“Done!” He shouts to Pow, “Our priority is to clean this tower.” 

“And some gold trim on the door with a nameplate …” 

“We will get to that!” And shouts to Pow - “put that on your list.” 

Finally, the day to leave arrives. As the ship takes off, Unla Kee asks Pow, “So if I gamble, can we set it up so I win?” 

Pow looks at Unla Kee as if to a small child to whom he must explain the simplest things, “Yes, but then we would lose money.” 

Unla Kee is unconvinced. 

Escarlotta sets the course for the Northern Isles of the Trackless Sea.
