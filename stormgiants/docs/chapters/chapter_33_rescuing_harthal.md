# Chapter 33  - Rescuing Harthal
After arriving in Yartar, the 3HPP met their contact in the Hand of Yartar at the Wink and the Kiss. Eltio was surprised to see them alive and even more impressed to see them with the Letter of Mark from Tamalin Zoar, so she gave them what they needed. 
“The boy is in prison, probably about to be executed. As for the sword, Brenner, a thug of a captain of the guard, has it.” 
Fortunately for the 3HPP, Eltio knew precisely where to find him in the central market of Yartar that was just below the Waterbarron’s residence. The market was a series of interconnected alleys that spread out in a thousand little directions like a bowl of spaghetti, where each intersection had a store or a stall, and each street had its own history. Everything that was sold in Yartar eventually found its way here. 
It was pretty late in the evening when Brenner finally showed, bagging at the various small stores demanding his cut of their profits for the protection he offered. 
The giant slayer sword hung from his best, and he had the smug satisfaction of a man with some power and loved to wield it over the unfortunate within his grasp. Surrounding him were several city guards, who looked bored, frustrated, and annoyed. 
At first, Orsik wants Unla Kee and Vardis to use fireball. After all, a group of enemies tightly packed in a small space cried out for that spell. Eltio, no shrinking violet when it came to violence, was shocked. 
“Are you mad? Create a 20’ fireball just outside the Waterbarron’s residence? We’ll be hunted down and killed.” She paused and stared at Orsik, “you’re Orsik, the guy who does the thing.”
The angry dwarf stared at the female rogue dressed in white and muttered, “fine, no fireball.”
And it was unnecessary. All but one of the guards died. All but one. 
Eltio turns to the heroes, “damn, we need to get your boy out of prison tonight. When Brenner’s death gets announced, they’ll think it was tied to the brat’s friends and kill him.” 
Thistle mutters, “this would be a good time for that convenient entrance into the prison.” 
The Hand of Yartar looks at him, “well, not so much an official backdoor, more of an unofficial secret passageway through an ancient temple of Bane.” 
At first, the 3HPP isn’t convinced that this is a good idea. Orsik insists on fighting through the Waterbarron’s house, “front door or bust.” 
When Eltio explains the problem of assaulting a center of government and living long enough to escape, even Orsik is convinced that perhaps, this time, the secret door through the temple of Bane to the prison is the right strategy.
The first part of their journey is into the sewers of Yartar, to a passageway that Eltio had heard of from her contacts, “I’m sure this will get you to where you want.” 
Orsik and Vardis are unsure but proceed. 
As advertised, the passageway leads to the abandoned temple of Bane. It’s a large area with a  pool of water in the middle. Thistle, Vardis, and Orsik hug the eastern wall near the entrance, then walk along the southern wall, trying to distance themselves and the pool as much as possible. 
Unla Kee rushes to take a dip. Fortunately for him, he notices the water weird and backs away. 
Also, luckily for Orsik, Thistle notices three markings, one on the south wall and one on the closet pillars to the wall, that form a triangle. 
“Stop, it’s a trap.”
 “Well, how do we get around this trap?”
“The other way.” 
And the 3HPP walk around the room and arrive at the secret door that Eltio promised them. They quietly open the door and see just beyond a room with many very bored and tired guards. 
“Can we use the fireballs now?” 
A few moments later, the guards were dead, charred, and blasted. As the last one died, he shouted, “you have no idea what you have done.” 
As Orsik and Thistle pick over the bodies for treasure, they find a lot of silver coins. Thistle mutters about a tradition involving the condemned’s families giving a silver bribe to see their loved ones one last time before they were executed. 
To the north of the guardroom is a room with chests, the possessions of the condemned. The containers are trapped, and although irritating, nothing fatal happens. More annoying is how little treasure is found. In one, there is an unused great sword, a spell book with no spells but doodles, and a note which refers to a condemned man whose family took all of his possessions, a potion of healing, and some coins and cheap gems
The other chest is magically trapped, and Unla Kee dispels its magic. Inside are more silver pieces and a glass decanter with a rich red fluid, a potion of greater healing. 
Thistle heads back into the guardroom and goes west into a hallway with a door to the north that leads to a room where the condemned’s family met their loved ones one last time. There is nothing of value there but the tears of sorrow and despair. The hallway leads to the Waterbarron’s castle, so he returns to the guardroom. 
From there, Orsik urges them to try the eastern doorway.
Beyond the door is a large room with bookshelves, several cadaverous creatures standing around moaning, a table with a skeleton, and a desk with a pale-faced woman sitting behind it. The room looks richly appointed and out of context for a prison. 
Across from where Thistle is standing on the eastern wall is a door, and another door is on the southern wall. The sounds of desperate people and chains can be heard from beyond those doors. 
The woman stares at Thistle, “what can I do for you?” who in turn asks to see Harthal Zymorven. 
The creature stares at her book and then says, “I suppose I can have you meet him.” She turns to one of the cadaverous moaning creatures that are clearly zombies and orders it. 
Thistle offers to go with the creature, but she says, “No, I can’t have you contaminate the experimental work that we are conducting on the prisoners. Every bit of air, food, water, and sentiment is tightly controlled.” 
The 3HPP shrug and wait. 
When Harthal appears, he shouts, “Father, you came for me.” 
Thistle is about to protest when he notices Orsik looks precisely like Lord Zymorven. 
 Harthal runs towards his father, but the woman violently holds him. 
“He can’t leave. We need him for our experiments. Experiments that have been sanctioned by the highest authorities in Yartar. Although if you were to provide us with fresh bodies, I could let him go.” 
Orsik-Zymorven smiles and returns with several of the bodies of the guards.
The creature smiles, and this time its tell-tale fangs are unmistakable, “yes, these guards will do nicely.” 
Thistle is surprised to not hear her refer to them as her guards. She notices and says, “the Waterbarron is under the mistaken impression that these guards can stop me from traveling among the citizens of his city.” The vampire laughs. 
As Harthal prepares to leave, the vampire whispers, “Harthal, my boy, you must leave tonight. If you were to be found in the city, I am afraid I would have to kill you the minute you came down here. Do you think, Lord Zymorven, you can find a way to get out of the city?”
Orsik-Zymorven remembers the portals and says, “yes.” 
An hour or so later, as the 3HPP and Harthal approach the Harper hangout, Harthal shouts, “wait, you’re Thistle Wendrod.” Then after a scream of frustration cries, “your girlfriend is why I ended up here.”
Thistle is stunned. Leila wanted to marry this guy? He wasn’t sure whether to despair about that or to be delighted she was alive. But he called her “Thistle’s girlfriend!” There was hope! 
Harthal then explains that Leila was the rogue who convinced him to steal the sword and head out on an adventure. Lord Zymorven’s heir mutters that he only wanted some experiences like dear old dad. He ends the story by explaining that after he killed someone who badly deserved it, Leila abandoned him, saying she needed to go find the absolute love of her life. 
“Why Yartar?” 
“She wanted to talk to the Hand of Yartar and join them. Thought they could help her find you. But they were too local of an operation, so she decided she needed to go find someone else. I mean, she kept talking about Thistle. And that she was looking for you. And I really felt used.” 
“You were,” mutters Orsik-Zymorven. 
Thistle adds, in as condescending a voice as possible, “and that’s why you should stay in your castle and be Lord Zymorven. That way, no one would dare use you in that way.”
Harthal stares at Orsik-Zymorven and says, “dad, I am so sorry! I just want to go straight home and become the next Lord and never follow some crazy Fairy that wants to use me to find the love of her life!” 
A few moments later, the group enters the Harper headquarters. As they enter, Thistle announces to Orsik-Zymorven, “Lord Zymorven, if I recall, you had some urgent business to attend to and would be staying behind. Your ally Orsik would be waiting for us at the other end of the portal?” 
Orsik-Zymorven stares, befuddled, and says, “yes, of course.” 
Thistle notices that the hallucinatory terrain in the shape of a garden with misappropriate fairy culture is gone and replaced with a more conventional modern Waterdeephavian look of straight lines and austere surfaces. 
As he examines the terrain, a man appears, “my name is Gauche the Young, and you must be the group known as the Three Halfpints and a Pint.” 
Thistle and Orsik stare at him, confused. Gauche is of average human size, with a heavy build from eating well, amber eyes, a broad nose, and gray hair, revealing he is not that young. 
“My predecessor, sadly, was killed. And you are the agents that Krowen Valharrow hired. I’ll have you note that Krowen is a follower of the school of Ezra. After the end of the age of sundering, the gods could no longer enter the world on their own. And we thought we were finally safe from their madness. But then Severin, of the cult of the Dragon, found a tome with the aid of Tiamat that contained a ritual that would let her back in. The Harpers were weak then, and most of our field agents were dead. And so Ezra decided to pilot a new program of hiring adventurers and using Harper funds to get them to do missions. The success of the First Faction and Ezra can not be denied. But I disagree with the policy. You are not Harpers. You are mercenaries. Normally, I wouldn’t let you use our teleportation circles, but the success of Ezra’s program and his promotion to the upper echelons of society require that I help you. But I don’t like it. Not one bit.” 
The 3HPP stare at the obviously disgruntled Gauche the Young. 
 “What happened to the other guy?”
“Oh, he was killed by a lightning bolt.”
“What?”
“A mighty one. And what’s interesting is that the room Kolbaz was in was destroyed as if something substantial materialized in that room, destroyed everything in it, and then disappeared. It also happened near the teleportation circle, almost as if whatever it was came from there.”
Thistle asks, “Did anyone else come through the teleportation circle?”
“Yes, of course, they did. But that would be Harper’s business and no business of yours.” 
“Well, I am sorry for your loss.” 
Gauche is surprised, “Yes, his death was a tragedy. But this is a plum position, and I have been looking forward to it, so it’s – how sad and congratulations, I suppose.” 
Thistle takes leave of Gauche, examines the area around the teleportation circle, and notices that something massive materialized and then dematerialized. It smashed the walls, the roof, and the ground. The marks on the stone make it evident that this was not the work of a giant. 
The ranger then turns to the remaining pieces of paper on the desk. It’s clear from the papers’ arrangement that the research into Adimond Kroskas was the proximate cause of Kolbaz’s death. Thistle is about to leave when he finds a fascinating piece of paper. Apparently, Adimond Kroskas created the ritual that the cult of Tiamat used to first create Drakes. 
“That sounds about right,” thinks the fairy.
And then he notices something that surprises him, Adimond Kroskas is an alias for someone else. That he is not who he says he is.

Thistle then turns to Gauche, who is dusting the room, and explains his suspicion that his predecessor Kolbaz was killed by Adimond Kroskas. Thistle tries to figure out if Gauche knows anything, but Gauche is remarkably disinterested. 
“I tell you, sirs, Harpers will solve this mystery with the appropriate urgency and priority.” 
Orsik-Zymorven persuades Gauche to work a little bit harder, hoping that he will look into Adimond, but Gauche doesn’t get to this plum position because he is a fool, “Yes, you are right. I will look into the circumstances of my predecessor’s death. Looking into Adimond Kroskas, however, is a distraction, and I will not look into him.”
He then pauses and starts taking measurements of the marks on the room. He mutters, “I have this theory that the thing that killed Kolbaz was demonic in nature or a wizard.”
Orsik-Zymorven mocks him – “or a wizard disguised as a demon?”
But Gauche doesn’t take the bait, “yes, I must look into wizards who masquerade as demons.”
Thistle and Orsik-Zymorven are frustrated and smile politely. Gauche will up the priority into every possible angle of the investigation except looking into who Adimond Kroskas is or where he went. 
A few moments later, the group emerges from the teleportation circle in Everlund into the Moongleam Tower, standing in front of Nespril Menk, a tall, coltish, gangly-built, green-eyed apprentice of Krowen Valhorrow. He snorts through his broad nose and ties his light brown hair into a knot.
And then, when he speaks, he speaks with the sophistication of a Chondathan for whom the common tongue is their mother tongue. 
“The 3HPP have arrived,” he says. 
Orsik-Zymorven, still disguised as Zymorven, is about to object when Thistle says, “Orsik, it’s so good to see you here. Lord Zymorven had to stay behind and would meet us in his hall.” 
Orsik remembers that Lord Zymorven never left his hall and quickly transforms back to himself before Harthal can see the deception.
“Right, Lord Zymorven had to stay behind.” 
Vardis stares at his friend and almost laughs. His friend has intelligence and the raw power of genius, but he doesn’t quite know how to use it effectively. “The sudden increase in intellect is like a suit of armor that you just bought and aren’t quite used to using,” he thinks. 
“You are Krowen’s latest project,” Nespril sighs. “Well, apparently, Krowen didn’t tell you about the exit through the sewers.”
They stare at him.
“He wanted you to go out from the front door. Krowen, doing the big picture stuff, never pays attention to the details. I will introduce you to our secret passageway. This way, the Zhentarim won’t spot you.”
Orsik can’t shut up. “So, can you tell me how you avoid the Zhentarim spies?”
“We use a special random system.”
“Is it described in a book?” 
Nespril stares at him, “Krowen Valharrow would be very upset if the book were to go missing or if he suspected that you were involved in the book’s disappearance.” 
“I am just saying you need independent contractors to ensure it’s secure.”
And then Nespril explodes, “I am not a believer in Krowen’s support for the Ezra model. Hiring anybody to do a job and saving the world if the check clears. I believe in the old ways where being a member of this group meant you believed.”
Thistle seeing that his companion will press the issue points out that they still need to collect the money from Lord Zymorven and that this could get in the way. 
Orsik sighs and declares, “Okay, first we go to Danivarr’s House and talk to Dral Thelev and give him the letter of Mark.”
A few minutes later, Orsik and Dral catch up in the well-appointed tavern about their mutual friend Tamalin. Orsik says, “she really helped me see how I can better monetize my play. Brilliant business lady.” 
Dral tells Orsik that the Zoars are exceptionally well connected and that the captain of the army, High Captain Horix Zoar is Tamalin’s brother. 
Orsik asks, “and what is their relationship to Silverymoon?”
Dral laughs, “The High Captain has never forgiven High Marshal Methrammar Aerasumé for abandoning his allies. As a result, Zoar broke the compact and dissolved the alliance. Although he would like to raise an army and raze the city to the ground, the High Captain also believes that you must let bygones be bygones. He could get you into the city as a diplomat of Everlund, making it impossible for the local police to arrest you.” 
And with that, the heroes head north on Rauvin Road. On their second day, north of Silverymoon, they are attacked by some Crag Cats.
