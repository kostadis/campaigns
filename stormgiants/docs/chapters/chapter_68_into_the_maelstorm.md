# Chapter 68 - Into the Maelstorm
# Vardis
Vardis stares, his mouth ajar, his sword hanging limp in his hand, and mutters, “The fairy’s creature can now breathe out death and destruction.” 
“Do it again,” he mutters. But the unconscious Thistle needs to be alive for Thuul to breathe again. 
Down the hallway, his wall of fire burned. It cast a bright light towards him and radiated only heat on the other side where his friend Orsic was fighting.
“He’s alone with two giants,” thinks the cleric of Corellon. “Ever since Orsik became smarter, he hasn’t liked his chosen career. It’s like he was too dumb to be anything but a sellsword, and now that he can be anything, he resents those choices. It’s almost as if he has a death wish.”
But the sound of the giant roaring beyond the wall of fire focuses his mind on the here and now. “Flamestrike should do the trick.” 
And then he remembers the endless debates in Waterdeep at the Temples before his heretical views on the true nature of the responsibilities of the Clerics of Corellon made him persona non-grata about how Flamestrike used to be more potent in the past. But after he ended the significant sundering era, Ao weakened that magic’s power. 
It was why he had followed the light aspect of Corellon to use Fireballs.
“Hold on.” 
And a fireball incinerates the Frost Giant and injures the Cloud Giant. 
Vardis mutters, “Orsik is still alive.” And then he cowers inside the doorway to the guestroom. 
And then he hears the crunch of Count Vaal’s morning star on Orsik and thinks, “Is Orsik still alive?”
Then he overhears the Dwarf taunting his opponent, “That hurt, but you did swing that morning star with grace and style. So I will give you that.” 
Vardis notices that Thuul is trying to figure out what to do, and he points to the downed fairy and urges the guard, Drake, to help Thistle. 
The creature mumbles and agrees.
Vardis wonders about the situation outside in the great hall. He overhears his companion’s shout. 
Orsik  - “This monster is still alive, and we must hurt it a lot.” 
Unla Kee – “Take that.” A shimmering glow surrounds his head, and then the cloud giant screams in agony and floats in the air, wobbling.
Vardis sees two hand axes impale themselves on the giant. 
And then he thinks, “Good thing I chose to follow the light, eh Corellon?” His hands move apart in a semicircle, from which, like spokes of a wheel, jet lances of pure flame toward the Count. 
He hears Thistle’s moans as the Healing Word’s effect wakes the ranger. 
And then Vardis smiles as one of the half pints unleashes their magical arrows on Count Vaal. The cloud giant, incapacitated by the other half-pint, barely notices the pain from the arrows as he grabs his head, still reeling from the psychic lance. 
“Shit, it’s Unla Kee’s turn,” he mutters. “A simple magic missile, and we’re done.” 
But that, as Vardis knows, would be too easy. 
“Say this about Unla Kee; say he won’t do the easy thing.” 
But this time, the self-centered wizard strikes the countdown with a few blasts of power. 
Vardis sighs in relief and blinks the wall of fire out of existence. 
He notices a bloodied Orsik still standing surrounded by the corpses of the giant lords. 
“Is he happy or sad? Hard to tell.” 
## Orsik
“Still alive,” he thinks. It’s said with the grim satisfaction of being alive and the bitter regret that he’s still a fighter. 
“After a well-fought battle, the one good thing is the plunder.” On Vaal, he finds a diamond-studded platinum earring and a vest of platinum embroidery. Tied to his belt is a teleportation conch and a potion of water breathing. On the Fire Giant, Duke Tartha finds a necklace of gilded Dragon’s teeth and another conch. Hellenhild finds a cloak of white dragon scales and another conch on the Frost Giant Jarl. And finally, on the Stone Giant Thane Braxow he rips out 15 gemstones embedded in his flesh. He looks at his friends. “12,000 gold pieces and some giant toenails.” 
Vardis shouts, “We need to move them from here.”
Orsik is about to get angry but then remembers that being the smartest person in the room isn’t always about having the right idea but about recognizing the right idea from other people and amplifying it. 
“The storeroom?” 
Orsik then starts the painful process of dragging the bloody and batter bodies north past the guestrooms for Giants and into the storeroom. 
And just in time, Mirran descends the staircase to see why no noise and no giant lords are coming up to celebrate. 
Orsik, hiding, hears her step into the Portal Chamber. She mutters, “They are all gone. Well, I suppose they killed those little people and decided to go back to their homelands and spread rumors about Serissa.”
He then barely can overhear Nin screaming something about loose ends at her sister. 
And with that he joins the rest of the 3HPP for a long rest in the guest chambers. 
A few hours later, as they prepare to head out, Orsik turns to his followers. 
“So our situation is the following – Serissa is the current Queen. The current Queen is being undermined by her two sisters, Mirran and Nym. Serissa believes her father is still alive and may have information that we need. Serissa’s mom was interested in an accommodation with the little people.”
He notices that Vardis is staring at him, “My plan was to make it appear that Serissa was also interested in the little people by claiming that I was a suitor she had found through a matchmaker in Waterdeep.” 

Orsik glares at his friend, who tries hard not to laugh. 
“What we need to do is get to the Queen. I recommend a full front assault.” 
He looks at Vardis, who is sad and concerned, Thistle, who just says no, and Unla Kee, who is already running towards the stairs to confront a castle full of storm giants and pauses. 
“Why?”
“Because we don’t all have a death wish, buddy.” 
## Thistle
After the foolish plan of a full frontal assault was discarded, Thistle cast pass without trace and sneaked alone upstairs to the Great Hall. 
Unlike the day before, there is no adoring crowd of Giant Lords watching the two sisters play their living organ and sing their frightful tunes, just a pair of bored guards. 
As he wonders how he will head past the two hill giants guarding the entrance to the throne room, Nym slams her fist into the living organ and shouts, “I must go speak to the Queen,” and heads to the door.”
“An opportunity!” 
He flies close to the ceiling, too small to be noticed and impossibly stealthy, and sneaks past the guards and into Maelstorm’s throne room. He flies through an archway and enters a large amphitheater. Stone bleachers overlook an oblong chamber with a 50-foot-deep seawater pool near its center. 
Facing the pool and the doors are three enormous thrones. The middle is carved from a solid chunk of obsidian, has four blue dragon skulls affixed to its base, and floats 1 foot above the floor.
“What did Joon say about that throne? Damn it, I can’t remember.” 
He then examines the people sitting on the thrones. In the middle is the Queen.
Serissa is in the rough equivalent of a Giant’s twenties. She exudes both regal authority and youthful vigor. She stands at an imposing height of over 20 feet, a statuesque and powerful figure. Her long, flowing hair is a vibrant shade of silver blue, cascading down her back like a waterfall of moonlight. Her eyes, the color of a stormy sea, have an intensity that speaks of both wisdom and the tempestuous nature of her giant lineage.
Her skin is a pale, almost luminescent blue, with a faint shimmer that reflects the lightning she commands. She wears a magnificent gown crafted from delicate, ethereal fabrics that shift and change like the clouds in a stormy sky. The gown is adorned with intricate patterns of gold and silver, reminiscent of lightning bolts and thunderclouds.
On her head rests a crown of enchanted silver encrusted with precious gems that glow with an inner light. The crown is both a symbol of her authority and a conduit for her elemental powers. Around her neck, she wears a necklace of pearls and sapphires, each stone holding a fragment of storm magic.
Her demeanor is a blend of youthful curiosity and the commanding presence of a ruler. She moves with the grace of a dancer and the strength of a warrior, every step echoing with the faint rumble of distant thunder. 
When she speaks, her voice is like the rolling thunder, commanding attention and respect from all who hear it. Yet, her tone has a softness, a hint of the gentle rain that follows a storm, revealing her compassionate side.
Serissa is a formidable and inspiring figure, embodying the raw power of the storm and the grace of a true leader. 
Thistle realizes that there is a reason why she and not her sisters are Queen. 
To the left is whom he comes to learn is Imperator Uthor, a middle-aged Storm Giant his presence exuding an air of authority and experience. Once a vibrant silver, his long hair has faded to a more subdued steel-gray, tied back neatly to reveal a face etched with the lines of age and wisdom.
His eyes, a piercing shade of stormy gray, hold a perpetual look of condescension as he gazes at Queen Serissa from her right. His skin, a deeper shade of blue than the Queen’s, is marked with faint scars and lines, testaments to a life of service and battle.
He wears a finely crafted deep blue and silver tunic adorned with intricate patterns that mimic the chaotic beauty of a thunderstorm. The tunic is paired with a belt made of enchanted leather, fastened with a large, ornate buckle that symbolizes his rank and authority within the court.
He wields a magnificent trident in his right hand, its three prongs crackling with latent electricity. The trident symbolizes his status and a weapon of immense power, its shaft made of a rare, sturdy metal that glows faintly with an inner light. The prongs are sharp and gleaming, ready to unleash the fury of a storm at a moment’s notice.
His rigid and formal stance reflects a lifetime of discipline and duty. Despite his age, his body is still muscular and strong, a testament to his enduring prowess. His left-hand rests casually on an armrest, fingers tapping lightly as he looks at Serissa. 
His voice, when he speaks, is deep and resonant, carrying the weight of authority and experience. There is a slight sneer in his tone, a hint of the condescension he feels towards the young Queen that matches his look. 
But the person on the left startles Thistle, a giant Adminond Kroskas. Thistle is tempted to strike but decides that the futility of the action makes this the wrong time and place to do anything. 
He flies closer to overhear the conversation. 
Nym – What new way will you disgrace us? Will you follow our mother to her death with your love of little people? At least she had the decency to care about them as a people, not to mate with them. You gave a dwarf a conch to come to here to go out on a date? Who helped you? A matchmaker for the rich and stupid of Waterdeep? Was it “Ok Ogre?” or was it “The short for the Tall?” 
Uthor – \*cough\* My Queen, far be it for me to suggest. 
Serissa snars – “They are liars.” 
Merissa soothingly – “We agreed. We felt they were assassins and had them killed.” 
Adminod – “Can you describe them?” 
Adimond freezes when he realizes this isn’t some random group but the 3HPP. 
Adimond – “Did you find the bodies?” 
Nym – “The Frost and Fire giants eat little people.” 
Serissa squirms. 
Nym – “I am sure they are well digested.” 
Imperator said in a barely disguised voice of irritation, “Mirran and Nym, why don’t you confirm these events? I wouldn’t want this to be another misunderstanding.” 
Adimond – “I agree Imperator.” 
He then turns to Serissa, “My Queen, the experiment to create a new dragon-fairy hybrid is proceeding. And I believe I will have a force loyal to you that will always guarantee the Storm Giant’s position in the Ordning.” 
Thistle mutters, “I like it when the bad guys tell us their plans.” 
Thistle knows that this blue dragon hasn’t survived this long without an exit strategy. The creature survived the collapse of the oracle, it must have an escape route. It’s then that Thistle remembers what Joon said about the Maelstorm. The Maelstrom has a series of interconnected tunnels. Adimond’s exit strategy is the pool of water in front of them. 
Serissa waves her hand at Adimond – “Yes, I know about your experiments. But if we found my father, we wouldn’t need them. I need someone I could trust to go look for him.”
The Imperator sighs – “We have sent so many, your highness. The clues you have provided have gotten us nowhere.” 
Thistle realizes that the Imperator likes having this Queen in power, a Queen he can manipulate. 
Thistle looks over the scene and realizes Adimond and the two sisters are allies. The blue dragon casually directs them to speak, supporting their arguments and indicating when to act. 
Adimond - my dearest, although I know your complaints and this shocking discovery of your sister’s decision to look for love in all the wrong places.
Mirran pouts - They weren’t suitors. They were cheaters 
She stamps her foot down. 
Mirran - They were assassins. We dealt with him. See, Imperator, we should be Queen, not Sarissa.

Serissa screams and attempts to get up from her throne, but the Imperator grabs her arm and forces her to reconsider. 
Adimond says – Perhaps a change in leadership might be appropriate if the Queen can not restrain her temper, Uthor?
Imperator – “Do not confuse your privilege with the right to speak about these matters. 
Adimond – “I am just an alchemist doing the Queen’s bidding.” 
## Unla Kee
Unla Kee enjoyed swimming, especially underwater, and he most definitely enjoyed all of that when he could breathe underwater.
After Thistle had informed them that the great enemy was in the throne room, the group debated their options.
Orsik, to Unla Kee’s surprise, wanted to charge. But his best friend Vardis and Thistle, once again, being the party poopers he knew they were, pushed back.
Instead, they agreed to sneak into the throne room through the waterways that linked the Maelstorm. 
In the portal chamber, there was a pool that led to an underwater portcullis. The portcullis was intended for much larger creatures than the Half-Pints. The poor pint had to squeeze through, barely avoiding getting cut on some barnacles. 
Through the portcullis, he arrives at what gave this palace its name. 
“The magnificent vortex—a true testament to the raw, untamed power that only I could hope to wield someday. I stand at the edge of this half-mile-wide whirlpool, watching with a smirk as it drags hapless ships and their pitiful crews to a watery grave. Imagine their terror, knowing they are powerless against such a force. Yet, with my unparalleled intellect and unmatched cunning, I see through to the heart of its mystery: a 100-foot-deep, 180-foot-wide sinkhole at the ocean’s bottom. “ 
He looks down and thinks, “The sinkhole is a marvel, a mere 100 feet deep and 180 feet wide. Child’s play for someone of my extraordinary capabilities. At its nadir lies a 50-foot-diameter drain, the linchpin of this aquatic spectacle. Plugging it would dissolve the vortex, but naturally, that’s far too simple for one as brilliant as me. The drain is covered by an iron grate, crusted with barnacles and 10-foot-wide gaps—perfect for any of my numerous plans.”
And then he cackles as he thinks - In these dark, oppressive depths, I am the master of my fate. The sinkhole’s secrets, its treasures, its power—all of it will belong to me. And when I emerge, the world will be forever changed by the sheer force of my genius.
Orsik, swimming to a tunnel on the floor, disrupted his reverie. Staring at Vardis, he realizes that the Dwarf has discovered that the treasure is no longer with the ships but was stolen by some crabs. Crabs Orsik intends to kill.
“I can’t have him have all of the fun…”
