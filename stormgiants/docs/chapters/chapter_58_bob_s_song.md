Thistle

As Thistle flies down on Thuul’s back, he sees a collection of dead members of the Cult of the Dragon before him. 
“Good riddance,” he thinks. But as he gets closer, he realizes they aren’t dead. 
“Zombies!” he shouts. 
The 3HPP stop their descent, stare, and realize that they can just land out of reach of the slow moving undead near the iceberg top that protrudes through the upper courtyard. 
“Let’s go to the tower on the port side of the castle,” he suggests.
As he flies down, he sees that Delsephine has moved the Dragon’s Breath to drop Orsik and Vardis nearby. 
Unla Kee follows Thistle. Thistle notes that the mage wanted to cast cast fireball. 
The tower is ancient and appears to be crumbling. Ice has sealed the windows shut and cracks have formed in the walls and rooftop—a balcony of sculpted ice hugs one side of the tower, 75 feet above the base. The door at the base of the building has been smashed open and then boarded shut. 
Thistle enters the tower, and notices exsanguinated corpses of orcs. He flies down and inspects the bodies when Orsik shouts, “Vampires.” 
Thistle looks at the spot on the neck Orisk points at. He nods. 
Thistle, Orsik, and Vardis ponder their next move. Unla Kee has already made it. He’s flown to the upstairs balcony and entered the room. 
The fairy groans and follows him but peeks in, trying to be discreet. 
Thistle can see a half-elven moon elf vampire smiling at Unla Kee, like a cat smiling at a mouse. 
And the blood-sucker screams, “You can’t be alive. You’re an abomination.” 
The fairy is stunned and points at himself with his mouth open, “Me? Look, I just escaped the cult of the dragon …” he begins. 
She screams, “Don’t call that abomination that Severin did to the cult of the dragon, the cult of the dragon. That was a twisted, devilish plot by Zariel to eliminate Tiamat from her plane of Avernus. She was an annoying office mate.” 
The 3HPP nods in understanding. 
Vardis finally makes it up the stairs, “a friendly vampire?” 
“I am no such thing. I am Sandesyl Morgia.
She ignores the insult and turns to Thistle once more. Thistle succeeds in convincing her that she knows what she is talking about. 
“Severin hired Adimond to create a fey hybrid dragon.” 
“So you know where he is?” 
“No. And yes, I understand that Severin perverted everything I believed in. But I believe in making clean breaks. They decided to go their way, and I decided my way.” 
Thistle tries to get the vampire to reveal anything but is met with ignorance and indifference. The vampire wants to talk about the experiments. 
“Severin was successful, but it almost drove that fairy mad. She would transform between the two creatures. It was more a form of lycanthropy than a fusing of the two essences.” 
She pauses, “I think she looked like you.” Thistle blushes and describes the love of his life. She sighs, “Yes, it was her. Can you turn into a dragon?” 
Thistle tries and fails. 
She then changes topics, “This castle is owned by a certain cloud giant Blagothkus. I want you to know that I don’t care who owns the castle as long as there is a fresh supply of bodies to exsanguinate. I am happy to be the castellan and manage the savoir-vivre.” 

## Orsik
The dwarf is intrigued. “So as long as we provide enough bodies, you will leave us alone?” 
“Have you heard of Silverymoon?” asks Thistle. 
Orsik is surprised at the dark turn the fairy has taken. And then he remembers that the fey can be very dangerous. 
“Yes, a city full of pompous yits. Tasty pompous yits.” Sandesyl explains, “I just want a steady supply of sentient humanoids I can exsanguinate at my leisure. I want to hear their screams as they suffer and are in pain.” 

Orsik looks at his allies and is unsure if what he sees is indifference, interest, or fear.
“I like the screaming to be private, so I use my magic to silence the screams outside the tower.”
“How considerate,” Orsik mutters. 
Thistle then shifts the conversation. “You said the master of the tower is still here?” 
“Yes, and Blagothkus has been moping. It’s been weeks.”
“What about?” 
“It’s not like we have dinner together regularly. I wouldn’t mind dining on him,” she laughs. “A bit of vampire humor…” 
Orsik then remembers the story of Blagothkus. 

Eigeron and his father, Blagothkus, came to the Eye of Annam seeking wisdom. The divine oracle told them that a great upheaval would upset the balance of power in the world, allowing all giants to win the respect of their gods and bring glory to their race. The oracle told Blagothkus outright that he could never impress the gods enough to earn their favor, then urged Eigeron to step out from beneath his father’s “dark shadow.” Blagothkus was overcome with despair and envy. A terrible fight between father and son ensued, in which Blagothkus slew Eigeron. Blagothkus then retired to his castle to mourn.
Eigeron asked the heroes to avenge his death by killing his father. 
Orsik shrugs. 
And asks, “How did you become a vampire? And, like how many spawn do you have? 
She smiles and says, “That would be telling.” 
“We are asking…” 
She smiles, that smile of the hunter to the prey, “I think it’s important for you to know that should you accidentally think – “Oh, she’s an old helpless vampire obsessed with her necromantic research,” at that moment, there would be a vengeance that would make you wish that you had never opened this door, and had ever spoken to me.” 
Orsik nods in approval at the threat. 
Vardis then asks about the Ogres. She explains that they were part of her necromantic experiments after they were no longer viable as a food supply. 
Orsik tries to get her to have the zombies perform in his next play, but she refuses.
She then explains that she has worked for Blagothkus for a long time. And then she rambles about Blagothkus. She shares, “He once tried to ally himself with the Cult of the Dragon against the little people, and unfortunately, that led to a group called the First Faction capturing control of the castle. The castle then crashed in the north. The First Faction captured the castle again. Then Blagothkus went to the Spine of the World to talk to his god, and when he came back, his son was dead. The First Faction had gotten rid of me before they left the north. It was an evil ploy. So I don’t know how Blagothkus got his castle back.”
As the 3HPP starts to leave the tower, she hands Orsik a book, “This is why I wanted to become a vampire.” He reads the title, “Dusk.” He sighs, as he remembers the young adult fantasy series about vampires and girls. 
He’s about to poke fun at her, and then he notices that Sandesyl is the kind of creature that goes from 1 to 50 level of anger in an instant. And he thinks the better of it.
