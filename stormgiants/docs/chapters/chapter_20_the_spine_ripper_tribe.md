# Chapter 20 - The Spine Ripper Tribe
## Orsik 4th day of the 1st Tenday of Mithul (3 days left)
Orsik stares at Chandra, hurt and confused. 
He then looks at her and tells her, “you don’t know these people. Bad Fuul can’t come here personally, but he could send someone here to kill you.” 
Sensing he’s got a winning argument, he continues, “he doesn’t have to kill you on his own. Why would he do that? He’s evil over Lord minion. He has minions to do his evil work for him, so he’s just gonna send someone here. Well, you’re all alone. These people are not gonna be able to protect you.” 
Seeing the winning argument right in front of Orsik, he shouts, “I’m the only one who can guarantee your safety.”
Chandra is shocked, intimidated by his plea’s forcefulness, and says, “yes.” 
Blasius turns to Thistle, “guell my boy! I am off.”
Orsik is about to follow him when Prince Thornacious shouts, “a fine dinner for our friends.”
## The group 5th day of the 1st Tenday of Mithul (2 days left)
The following day the heroes decide to see if they can find where Bad Fruul is. To maximize their effort,s they split up and promise to return later that day to Parnast to reveal what they have learned.
Thistle finds evidence of how Bad Fruul intends to invade Parnast by doing an aerial survey. Vardis later confirms the route by carefully investigating the forest trails near Parnast. Wallace, leveraging his local knowledge, further confirms the most likely direction. 
Thistle, Vardis, and Wallace confer and agree that the attack will come from the north. Then all three inspect the fortifications and are pleased to see the town center with the roads barricaded and pits dug in front of the barricades. 
Orsik finds some goblins that he deceives with information about Bad Fruul. The goblins reflect on what they have learned, and fewer join the hill giant’s banner. 
Unla Kee, for his part, recognizes the banner of Bad Fruul in the hands of some hoard members. From that, he extrapolates the size of the hoard. And from that, he infers that a tribe of Orcs has not yet joined the giant’s army. And from his knowledge of the area, he guesses the name, “The Spinerippers,” and a location for the orcs.

## The group – 6th day of the 1st Tenday of Mithul (2 days left)

In the morning, the companions head out to find the orcs’ den. Thistle shouts, “I got this!” and leads them straight to where the orcs are hidden. He then explains how Uncle Joon, whom he is sure is on the good guys’ side, told him all about every Orc tribe and the best places for Orcs to create a den. 
Orsik stares at him and mumbles, “or you can fly up 50 feet and see them.” 
Thistle grouses and flies at an altitude of 7 feet, leading the group deeper into the forest and then towards some hills. At some point, he gets quieter and indicates with his fingers towards the edge of a cliff wall. But it is too late; an Orog stands beside the cave entrance and runs inside. 
Seeing the Orog run into the cave, Thistle proposes that they wait outside, ready to attack anyone who exits the caves.
An ambush is prepared, and time passes. And no one exits. 
Thistle decides to enter the caves to see what’s up. 
Time passes, and nothing changes.  
And then all abyss breaks loose.

The orcs had set up a trap deeper in the cave. That trap was dealt with thanks to the use of fireballs.
“FINALLY” shouted Orsik when the first fireball exploded. 
Unfortunately, for the one known as Unla Kee, in the hallway from where he cast his spell, an orog and an acolyte of Grummsh erupted,  and he was promptly knocked unconscious. 
Fortunately for his allies, Orsik, Wallace, and Thistle were able to kill the enemy. 
The group explores the cave complex and the dead Orcs and Orog. One of the Orcs was an Eye of Grummsh, a shaman of Grummsh. The acolyte was clearly a follower of his. 
The room with the trap was, according to Vardis, a War Hearth. There were plentiful shrines to the orc gods and accommodations for many orcs.
The passageway that the orog used opened into a nursery full of orc children. Guarding the children was an orc claw of luthic. The companions at first considered killing them all until they realized the monstrosity of the crime. And the observation that they had leverage over the Orc Claw of Luthic as one of the whelps was theirs. 
Using that information, they threatened an attack, and the Orc Claw of Luthic grabbed her Whelp and moved away. 
The companions then noticed a pit full of human prisoners. They were two families. 

When they saw Orsik, they rejoiced, as they thought the militia had come to save them! 
They then quickly revealed that they were the families of Jarlor and Larrish. 
Orsik remembered the two men from his dealings as a town council member. 
“What did the orcs want with them?”
“They threatened to kill us if Jarlor and Larrish didn’t spy chief Kurglow.”

Seeing the murderous rage in Orsik’s eyes, one of the children shouts, “daddy didn’t want to do that, but the orc chief Kurglow said he would kill us all.”
