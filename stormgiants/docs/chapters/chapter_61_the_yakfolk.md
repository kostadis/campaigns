# Chapter 61 - The Yakfolk
Escarlotta, the sentient control center of Blagothkus’ Cloud Castle, “you need practice. I will not have you attack the Countess and lose. And I love a good love story; we will help Thistle find his love, Leala.” 
The other members of the 3HPP need clarification. They had hoped to go after Sansuri first, but Thistle had used his charming wit and sob story to sway the giantess. 
As the ship moved from the collapsing Eye of the All-Father, she shouted, “AT LEAST SOMEONE LOVES SOMEONE IN THIS SHIP!” 
Thistle smiles and watches through the viewport as the ship moves silently across the clouds. It’s daylight, so he steps outside, confident that the Vampire will not try and kill him. 
He tries to remember what he knows about Ancient Blue Dragons, but he only remembers blue fairies and blueberries. He doesn’t remember anything about blue dragons. 
Meanwhile, Escarlotta shouts inside the control center, " Does anyone have any ability to tell me exactly where I am supposed to go?”  
They share the information they have from Oracle. Three days later, they get closer. 

## 7th day of the of the first tenday of Eleint 1492

Escarlotta once again asks, “Anything more precise?” 

Orsik turns to Unla Kee, “Diviner?” 
The halfling shrugs. 
Vardis thinks, “Perhaps my god will help. And prays for divine intervention.” 
Correllon doesn’t listen, or maybe he does, and that’s why Vardis is somehow covered in bird shit. 
Vardis wipes himself clean, “not exactly endearing me to you right now.” 
But Thistle, still outside, spots a village on a mountain spire and shouts, “It’s there!” 
Up ahead, he spots the great dungeon forge of Ironslag. He remembers from Uncle Joon that the dwarves of Silvermarsh call it Mount Hamarhaast (“hammer of ashes”). Carved into the base of a 500-foot-high mountainside cliff are a pair of 50-foot-tall adamantine doors—that Uncle Joon says are airtight valves sealed by ancient magic. Atop the cliff is a village, the village of Yakfolk. 
As they approach, Escarlotta asks, “Where shall I stop?” 
Thistle tries to argue for the subtle approach and points out that there is a stairway they can use to climb.  But Orsik will have none of that and insists on what he calls a “show of force!” 
His enthusiasm convinces Escarlotta again. 
The ship glides over the village at the top of a mountain. 


The castle's front gate opens, and the heroes climb down and stand in the middle of the yurt nearest the iron gate. 
A young, frightened moon elf comes to greet them and says,  “Chief Kartha-Kaya would consider it beyond rude not to come and eat in her yurt!” 
Thistle stares at her and is convinced she is afraid for them, but he cannot say. 
At first, Thistle tries to avoid the meal, but the social graces he learned in the Fey court impose specific protocols on him. 
Orsik and Vardis are suspicious and so cast wards against poison and enter. 
Built on high ground in the middle of the Yakfolk village is the 60-foot-diameter hut with a beaded curtain entrance in the northeast wall, facing an outdoor campfire that is the Chief’s home. The hut's interior is one large chamber dominated by a shallow, 10-foot-diameter, circular pit. The pit contains a campfire that keeps the hut warm. Toward the back of the hut are piles of cushions, baskets laden with foodstuffs, and painted clay vessels filled with water, wine, and broth. Between the fire pit and the south wall is an ornately engraved bronze gong and a wooden mallet with a head padded with yak hide. The gong and the mallet hang from a wooden frame.
The food is spread across the hut and consists of fresh vegetables, cooked meats, and bread. 
Orsik starts to eat some, expecting it to be poisoned, but he is pleasantly surprised by how good and unpoisonous the food is. 
He nods to the group, and the rest of 3HPP dig in. The monotony of the cleric’s created food and water has begun to grate on everyone’s nerves. 
When the meal is completed, the Chief insists they must perform a cleansing ritual. 
Thistle and Vardis try to object, but the Chief is insistent and suggests that ill omens are likely if no food is eaten. 

Orsik will have none of it and stand outside. The Chief is disappointed and makes some snide remarks about dwarves. Then, he holds Thistle's left hand and Vardis's right hand. Imberu, his first hand, holds Thistle’s right hand and Unla Kee’s left. Nahala, his other wife, holds Unla Kee’s right hand and Vardis’ left, closing the circle. 
The chief begins to hum, and the heroes feel the cleansing. They also sense the Yakfolk minds trying to take them over. 
Orsik notices something amiss and warns his friends, who warned,  are now better able to repel the attempted possession. 
The failed attempt to control the minds of the 3HPP triggers a free-for-all melee.
