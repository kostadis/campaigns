# Chapter 29  - Circles within Circles
The fiendish half-human, half-demon spawn smiles at Unla Kee, tries to charm the halfling, and fails as the diviner shifts the fate in his favor. They hiss their frustration. 
Orsik turns to the mage, “We are going to exploit this town’s racism against undesirable races and run to the tavern we left Thistle and Vardis in and scream – ‘ungodly races’ and have the mob deal with them.” 
Unla Kee smiles, “that is an overly complicated plan. I love it. But we need to say something that will catch their attention….” 
“I got it. We were trying to take a walk. And these attacked us, these MONSTERS. I thought that this was a town of good races. And LOOK at what we found!” 
The diviner smiles – “and I’ll cast a lightning bolt into the tavern and blame it on the Cambians!” 
“Pin a robbery, start a lynch mob, and blame them for murders.” 
“Yep.” 
The two smile at each other. 
The Cambian, for his part, is pleased to see the dwarven fighter still there and tries to charm him but fails. 
Now Orsik and Unla Kee run. 
Orsik turns to Unla Kee – “remember, we want them to follow us, so don’t hide.” 
Unla Kee turns to Orsik – “I love this plan, but did you notice that the creatures can fly?”
“No.” 
The Cambian keeps up with the would-be thieves of House Margaster and strikes Unla Kee with his spells. 
A badly wounded Unla Kee, who has lost 2/3 of his hitpoints, turns to Orsik – “This plan is not that good anymore.”
“Yes. We’ll run into a house and try and get help.”
“I’m not sure a commoner will offer much resistance to the demons.”
“How remarkably unselfish of you!” 
“No, I am just thinking about how ineffective your plan is.” 
Orsik growls and barges into a house. The houses in Silverymoon are not locked. After all, only good races live here.
Inside the house is a sharply dressed halfling, wearing finely crafted jewelry, drinking some fine wine inside his drawing room, which is well-appointed with expensive furniture. The room is lit with candles. 
“DEMONS ARE CHASING US!” 
The gentleman drops his glass of wine and shouts, “DEMONS!” 
Unla Kee shouts, “Yes!” but is struck dead by the other infernal creature.
The first half-demon follows Orsik into the house – “We want our master’s treasure. Give it to us, and our master will forget this.” 
Orsik is determined to get his treasure, “we steal from you? YOU’RE DEMONS!” He turns to the halfling, “I thought this was a nice town!” 
The  Cambian that killed Unla Kee shouts, “Found it.” 
The one in the house says, “If you leave and don’t say anything and leave the city by tomorrow, your friend doesn’t need to die. He’s almost dead.”
Orsik is too stunned and stares. 
“Very well,” the creature nods to his friend, who kills the halfling. 
The Dwarf, who remembers that Unla Kee had said the world would end if he died, stares at the sky, “Huh, I guess the world isn’t coming to an end.” 
He then walks out of the house and looks at the body. “Maybe if he stays dead, the curse will be true.” So he picks him up and takes him to the Dancing Goat.
He shows up with the corpse over his body, and the tavern keeper shouts, “What happened?” 
“Impossible, such creatures do not exist in Silverymoon.” 
Orsik glares and heads up to the room upstairs. 
In the morning, Orsik attempts to carry Unla Kee’s corpse and is stopped. 
The tavern keeper, Nyalah Uuseff, dressed in formal silk clothes and her gold, says, “I have asked the guard to come and investigate these tales of ungoodly races being present!” 
“I agree, but we need my friend alive to provide testimony.” 
“The city guard will decide that!” 
The Silverwatch arrives, and the Captain announces, “We are here to take you to city courts.” 
Orsik growls, “Look, my friend who was killed can provide a reasonable testimony. Can we go get him raised?”
The Captain is stunned, “a murder by a demonic power?” He then pauses and looks straight at Orsik – “yes. We have many different temples; which one would he prefer?”
Thistle looks at Unla Kee and realizes he is “unclear.”
Orsik, however, remembers the kind of person his companion was and whispered to Thistle, “Cyric?” 
Thistle remembers that Uncle Joon had said that the city of Silverymoon specifically banned Cyric from their town and says, “No, no, we don’t.” 
“Well, he’s a halfling; the Goddess Meilikki loves them.”
And so they head out to Her grove, where the high priest is shocked to hear that a demon has killed Unla Kee. She takes his body to the inner sanctum and returns with the halfling no worse for wear and lighter one magical ring. 
She says, “Captain, the tale of the demon is true. You must look into this.” 
Orsik turns to him – “Wasn’t there this theory if you died, the world would end?”
Unla Kee sneers and says, “But I didn’t die, did I? I can see the future. I wasn’t scared. I knew I would be revived.” 
He pauses and says, “I was in no mood to race through the city, dodging flaming bolts, trying to raise a mob. I figured getting killed and having you raise me was a much better way.” 
The Captain turns to the group and announces, “You are expected in the Star Court.” 
Thistle groans, “Let me guess, Lord Margaster is the judge?”
Orsik and Vardis stare at each other, “THOSE MARGASTERS?” 
Before they know it, they have walked up the steps and are in a room with a judge and a beautiful woman wearing fine robes with two gorgeous men standing next to her. 
She asks in an imperious voice, “Are those the thieves?”
The men who flank her in a silky voice say, “Oh yes!”  
Sitting as far away from her as possible is the same halfling whose house Orsik had barged into. The Judge asks him, “Vinby Hearthgood, do you have anything to add?” 
“That dwarf is the person who smashed into my house.” 
Unla Kee is outraged, “Dude, I got killed, and you are worried about breaking and entering?” 
The Judge turns to Lady Margaster and asks, “Xamlyn, I understand you wish to arrive at an agreement.” 
“Yes, I want to talk to the Dwarf and his friends about a possible arrangement.” 
Orsik crosses his arms in front of his chest, squints, and says, “I am making no deals with a literal devil.” 
Vardis turns to Orsik, “These Margasters are rich and powerful, and we don’t want to make enemies of them.” 
The Judge seems to echo that when he declares that these accusations against a paramount citizen of Silverymoon are unprecedented and require the most robust evidence.
“How about some magic spells.”
“An excellent idea, bring the court magister.” 
Xamlyn realizes that this will be a disaster and declares, “No. I will not subject myself to this intrusion. There was no crime. This halfling claims someone killed him with a bolt of fire. This other halfling claims that there was a dwarf in the house. I claim that a drunken party went wrong, and they want to blame me.”
She takes her men and leaves. 
The Judge turns to Orsik, “I know you. You’re the one that did that THING! And now you are creating another mess for our city. The Lady Margaster is making sizeable investments in our city. And we need her investments and the investments of the City of Waterdeep. The War of the Dragons was a disaster; we are still reeling from that. I can’t have you here. The real estate market has collapsed. We need her money, and I order you to be gone by sunset.” 
Unla Kee asks – “can we buy some properties?”
“I don’t want anything to do with this town,” grumbles Orsik. 
The Judge turns to Thistle and says, “You are a credit to your kind and are demonstrating more goodliness than these folks are.” 
And with that, Thistle flitters out and says, “I’m done.” 
A little bit later, the group is leaving Silverymoon through the Sundbar gate and heading south on Rauvin Road to Everlund. The journey is mostly uneventful. 
As they leave, Thistle says, “Uncle Joon said that the Silverymoon betrayed the Silver Marches and their dwarf allies. So it’s cool that you hate them.” 
Orsik looks at him, “Look, I come from an upper-middle-class family, and I am not into these blood feuds of my ancestors that my parents keep talking about.”
Thistle stares, “It was five years ago.” 
“Yeah, you’re right; they are on my shit list.” 
Later in the day, before sunset, the heroes arrive after an uneventful journey to Everlund. The road to Rauvin Road from Silverymoon has the Nether Mountains to the east and the Silverwood forest to the west. The dark, brooding peaks of the Nether Mountains are the source of many legends about lost Netherese magic and a repository of tangible riches such as granite. The mountains are also full of tribes of orcs. At the same time, the eastern peaks are home to several blue dragons chased out of the northern Anauroch by a dragon known as the Doom of Desert. As for the Silverwood, it has grown atop the rugged, hilly terrain between the Evermoors and the Nether Mountains. The Silverwood was once part of the High Forest. Still, loggers working the woodlands on either side of the Evermoor Way have carved a great wound through the terrain over centuries. Bare hillsides littered with stumps line this gap.
They take in the sights as they arrive at the city of Everlund. The town is situated on the banks of the Rauvin River. It is one of the North’s most active mercantile communities. A thick stone wall encloses the city, pierced in five places by gates. Like the spokes of a wheel, broad, straight avenues lead from each entrance to the Bell Market at the city’s center. The streets are clean and wide enough to accommodate large caravan vehicles. Soldiers of the city’s army make a show of patrolling the walls to reassure citizens and visitors as well as to discourage attackers. The buildings of Everlund are stately and well-maintained, with steeply pitched rooftops and tall spires that sport colorful banners. Two bridges span the river, with parks and trees along its shores.
Orsik calls the gate guards and shouts, “Tell us something.” One of the guards, a younger human, squints at him and says, “Hey, are you Orisk?”
Orsik deceives him with his charm, and the guard apologizes. Unfortunately, the second, older dwarven guard says, “Orsik was a dumbass, you fool. This Dwarf is a knowledgeable and thoughtful person.” 
Orsik swallows his pride, “What do you think of Silverymoon?” 
The Dwarf starts to speak – “Until recently, Everlund was a member of the Lords’ Alliance. The five leaders who currently comprise the city’s Council of Elders voted three to two in favor of separation and, in a symbolic show of support for Sundabar, condemned the alliance for failing to come to Sundabar’s aid during the War of the Silver Marches. Silverymoon sat on its hands and did nothing.” 
“So you don’t like them?”
“Don’t like? I want to see the city ground and burned into ashes.”
Orsik smiles and continues to talk to the Dwarf. 
Thistle sighs and pulls Orsik away, reminding him of their mission to go to Danniver’s tavern, find a half-orc, and give him a platinum medallion. 
Orsik tells Thistle, “I’m thinking of signing up.” 
Vardis rolls his eyes. 
As they head down the main thoroughfare, the group notices an older woman talking in front of attentive young men about a monster that ravaged the city a long time ago. As Orsik passes by, she shouts, “One day, that monster, the Kraken, will return to destroy our city with its powerful tentacle and mind control! The Kraken will come with his allies; we must prepare because they are more powerful than anything we have seen!” 
“Are they giants?” asks Thistle.
“No. Much worse.” She pauses and looks straight at Orsik, “Do you want to join our group and fight against the ancient evil.”
“Yes!” 
She walks up to  him, grabs his arm to test his strength, smiles, and says, “Well, that will be five gold pieces, and you will get a certificate declaring you a fighter of the light!”
“We don’t have five gold pieces.” 
She looks sad. “Any money?”
“We’re broke. Spent our last gold piece and badger pelt to raise our friend from the dead.”
Thistle concurs, “Nothing in the kitty.”
“Can we do something?” asks Orsik. 
“Bus tables?” asks Vardis.
“No, that would not be appropriate. You must go out to the town, and there is a cave a few miles out that you must find, and in that cave, you will find some kobolds that have stolen an artifact, the star of light that belongs to our order! If you brought it to me, I would be willing to waive the fee.” 
“Is this L-I-G-H-T or L-I-T-E?” asks Unla Kee. 
She ignores him.
“So what does it do?” asks Orsik.
“Nothing, it’s just of symbolic value.” She dismisses them, “When you have the artifact, return it to me.”
“What’s your name?”
“Miery – not Mary. My mother worshipped the goddess Mielikki and wanted my name to reflect it.” 
“Okay, see you soon.” 
As they approach the Tavern, Orsik announces that they will not drink because they are broke. Unla Kee won’t accept that – “I could sell my spell books? Or my tower?” The group, seeing his earnestness, tells him no. 
As they walk in, they notice that it is an old and prominent place to eat that was once a noble’s mansion. As they walk, they realize this rambling inn is a favorite haunt for adventurers from the clientele. 
The manager, Dral Thelev, a half-orc who looks masculine, asks them, “What will it be?” 
Orsik presents the platinum brooch. Dral sees it and smiles – “Something must be amiss with my friend Darathra Shendrel. But before we discuss this,” he starts to pour wine, “a bottle of my finest Silverymoon wine.”
Orsik almost spits out the wine, “Do you have something that is not from Silverymoon? And you aren’t a goodly race.”
Dral smiles, “Yes, you are correct. I like to drink the finest bottle of wine and remind myself how much the citizens of the good city would be appalled.”
“How did you get it?”
“I have friends. The racist owner would be appalled to know I am drinking his wine.” And then he begins to talk about the city some more. He explains how the town is populated by people living on the reflected glory of having one of the Silver Sisters be their founder and that they are stuck in the past. And what’s worse, the younger sister is now the open Lord of Waterdeep and head of the Lord’s Alliance. 
“And that bridge is such a scam. You go to see the bridge, and you can’t.” 
Dral nods and holds up his cup. 

“Bottom’s up!”
And with that, the heroes find themselves teleported to a room. 
“Welcome,” says a Turami man dressed in the distinctive garb of that area.
“Where are we?”
“In Moongleam tower, of course.” 
Vardis says, “Yeah, I know that.”
“Of course you do. It’s the most famous building in the city.”
Vardis looks at the man more carefully and realizes he is a harper. 
“Who are you?”
“Krowen Valharrow.” 
“Why are we here?” 
“You told Dral something important. He teleported you here.”
“Why?”
“Because if you have a secret agent coming in from the outside world, having them show up at the door of the Moongleam tower is like waving a red flag at the Zhentarim and Lord’s Alliance and telling them – looky hear! But if an adventurer goes into a hangout for adventurers and then gets teleported here, no one is the wiser.” 
He pauses, “Each faction is busy monitoring every other faction, and no one is paying attention to the giants.” 
Vardis explains, “Well, the Ordning of the Giants has been sundered. Unclear as to why.”
Krowen clicks his lips, “Well if you are willing to find out and fix it or tell me how, I can help you quite a bit.” 
They stare at him, “Let me show you something.” He points to a teleportation circle on the floor and hands them a map. “These circles will provide instantaneous travel through the North. I am sure that would be of help.”

And, he says, “Drathara had some gifts for anyone who would take this quest on. More gifts to encourage you to take the quest. They are yours to keep.” 
He hands them a coffer, “it was a birthday present for exactly such a moment.” 
They open the coffer and find several spell scrolls.
