# Chapter 72 - Exploring the Lower Courtyard
Glazhael the Undying emphatically explained that her plan to defeat Sandesyl was a secret she wouldn’t share. 
And so the 3HPP had to rely on the word of an undying dracolich.
As they leave, Orsik turns to the creature, “Dragons can’t just say things simply.”
Vardis agrees, “Always got a be churching it up.” 
As they walk away, the words of the Lord’s Alliance ring true, “No alliance is surer than one where narrow interests align.” 
As they walk out of the Dragon’s lair, the 3HPP decide that killing any potential undead allies of Sandesyl is a good plan. And so the lower courtyard of Blagothkus’ castle is a good enough place to start. 
From within the glacier, they follow a corridor to an ice-sculpted stairwell that opens to the lower courtyard. 
Walls of solid ice enclose this courtyard, which is partially overhanging another courtyard. Standing in that courtyard is Blagothkus, who is watching. The courtyard is held aloft by sweeping arches of ice that soar to a height of over 100 feet. Doors of sculpted ice fitted with iron hinges lead to various outbuildings and the main keep on the far side of the courtyard from the gate towers.
As Orsik looks around, he notices a lot of skeletal remains. 
“Let’s get rid of them. Toss them over,” orders Orsik.
“Do we want skeletal remains to be falling onto the ground?” asks Vardis, whose appreciation for the rights of the dead as a cleric of Corellon worries him. Will the Dwarven gods seek vengeance? 
“Better there than as animation material for Sandesyl.” 

Vardis, confronted with the brutal logic, remembers that burning corpses is a Dwarven tradition. “Let’s burn them on the way down?” 
“Okay then,” and with that, Orsik opens a bag of holding, empties it of any valuable contents, and then proceeds to fit it with corpses. 
Thistle just stares at the peculiar site of skeletal remains being transported in a bag of holding. 
When the grizzly work of collecting all the remains is completed, Orsik takes the bag to the front of a covered gateway leading from the courtyard to an open portcullis, and a drawbridge.
 Thistle stares from above from the gateway. 
Orsik then proceeds to dump the remains overboard. 
Unla Kee stops drinking and shouts, “Target practice!” 
The dead bodies of the dwarves are tossed overboard. As they fall, Unla Kee and Vardis burn them with spells. 
And so the day of burning fire is noted in the Evermoors. The day the midday sky lit up with fire. 
 Blagothkus from the upper courtyard smiles and shouts, “I have been looking for someone to do this for almost a year! THANK YOU!!!!” 
Orsik acknowledges the thank you and asks, “We were told there were undead here? We only found dead?” 
The cloud giant tries hard not to sound like a condescending smart ass and says, “Undead like the dark. They are probably not out in the sunlight.” 
“That makes sense!” 
Vardis tries hard not to laugh. 
From the gateway and the drawbridge, Unla Kee leads the group to the doorway on the port side of the castle. 
He sighs as he sees that whatever was here is dead and still dead, and more importantly, whatever treasure was taken a while ago. The room is a barracks, and the place has all the signs of a group of adventurers who burned, slashed, and stolen. There is still some soot on the walls. 
“None for us?” asks Orsik.
“None,” replies a down Unla Kee.
But Thistle, whose eyes of True Seeing see all, finds some scattered coins among the linen and the cracks of the room. 
As he does that he goes, “Huh. That’s interesting.” 
“What?” asks Unla Kee. 
“Not all of the corpses were dwarves; some were dragonwings.” 
“Dragonwings?” 
“High-ranking members of the cult with the ability to fly.” 
It appears that the story of this place involved the castle belonging to the cult, and then belonging to someone else. 
After sifting through all the remains, the group heads to the starboard door to the stables. 
As Unla Kee enters, the stench of the place takes over, but what really draws his attention is the decaying and yet very animate Wyverns. 
Thistle shouts, “These are undead.” 
Unla Kee asks, “Can I use dominate?”
Vardis reminds him that the undead can not be dominated. 
“Fireball, 6th level, here we come!”  
Orsik rolls his eyes, “3rd, please. We need that sunbeam for later.” 
“FINE!” 
The red seed of light zips through the air faster than Unla Kee can blink. He sees its trace against his eyelids as he remembers to shut them against the fiery blast that roars. Opening his eyes, he still sees traces of the crimson bead’s path toward its deadly end.
One Wyvern took the full blast; the other managed to avoid some harm. 
Vardis then shouts to Orsik, “Okay, here’s the plan. I will use the divine power of Correllon to turn them; whichever one  doesn’t get turned, you attack.” 
Orsik nods. 
Vardis holds the holy symbol aloft and, with a flash of radiant light, sends a wave of divine energy hurtling outward. The light envelops the creatures who flee in the wake of the gods' celestial power. One head goes to the bow side of the stable and the other to the stern side. 
Orsik sighs as he dashes towards the stern, “Out of range again.”
Thistle’s arrow strikes the sternside Wyvern, but they are immune to his lightning damage and don’t die. 
Unla Kee decides that now is the time to use his magical rock, so he removes the artifact of wonder. 
The world shimmers, and then a pile of hay disappears.
The 3HPP just stares. 
“I believe the hay is in the ethereal plane?” 
Thistle nods. 
“Two more shots!” 
The cleric, the dwarf, and the fairy sigh. 
Vardis completes the incantation and hurls a lance of pure light toward the undead monstrosity foe. The creature is hit and begins to glow. 
The glow helps Orsik aim his glowing green blade to strike true, and soon enough, the creature is truly dead. With that, he runs towards the stern.
Thistle moves to get a better shot, but the stables provide enough cover for one of his arrows to miss. 
Unla Kee holds the stone artifact aloft and asks, “Wand of Wonder?” 
Vardis shouts, “Yes, we save spells for the real battle!” 
Orsik shouts, “No, magic missile! End the battle!” 
Unla Kee listens to their shouts and stops, ready to invoke magic. With four bass thrums, three missiles of crystalline energy appear above his head. They wobble and spin slowly before their points rigidly align with Unla Kee's gaze.  They fly toward the wyvern, swift as diving raptors.
“You made an excellent point, Orsik.” 
Vardis follows up with a sacred flame that leaves the creature just ever so charred. 
The Wyvern strikes and misses against Orsik and tries to flee towards the stern side.
Thistle strikes him with two arrows and thuul burns him with a burst of acid, but the creature still lives. 
Unla Kee reaches for the artifact of wonder, and once again, Orsik dissuades him. And then Unla Kee weaves strange, arcane gestures with his free hand as he recites alien words. The caster’s eyes become glassy and distant as the spell nears completion, then the deafening peal of a funerary bell sounds.
“They are undead! That’s necrotic damage!” screams Orsik.
The wyvern dies to Orsik and Vardis’ surprise. 
“They are resistant, not immune. I know what I am doing,” the magic smugly states. 
“No,” mutters Orsik.
“Debatable,” grouses Vardis. 
With the monsters dead, the take begins. 
Thistle finds a copper figurine of the goddess Yondala, the creator of the halflings. 
Unla Kee is delighted, “I know about this trinket. It lets you cast a ritual spell in half the time.” 
Orsik finds 120 gold pieces in saddle bags. As he empties the gold into his bag, he notes a bag of carefully wrapped sweets and a cork. 
The dwarf is about to discard the cork when Vardis thinks that this cork is special. “With our luck, this cork will be necessary to save the realms,” he declares and casts detect magic. 
Thistle examines it with the aid of his magical eye.
It is a cork, nothing more. 
Orsik declares, “We have a bottle of champagne de le stompe. If we need a cork, we can use that one.” 
As they leave the barracks and head into the open space of the lower courtyard, Unla Kee mutters, “That’s interesting.”
Seeing that the group is receptive, he says, “The key element of being a lich is the phylactery. As long as that is not destroyed, you can come back.” 
“You know a lot about being a lich?”
“Just idle intellectual curiosity.” 
“Are you saying these are liches?”
“Yes. Sandesyl is conducting broader experiments on the undead to create alternatives to Dracoliches. They will come back.” 
And so again, the 3HPP collects the remains and throws them overboard. Unla Kee and Vardis burn them with their spells as they fly overboard. 
“Where did you learn that?” 
“Magic School” 
“I love how you just say magic school. Like there’s no name, just – magic school.” 
But that pisses off Unla Kee – “ I went to Nirany School of the Arcane. That was not the most prestigious school, but the master wizards spent time mentoring their apprentices, so we had many more opportunities to learn. We also had more freedom to choose our classes than the typical magic school with its rigid adherence to specific classes and methodologies.” 
Orsik rolls his eyes. 
While this goes on, Thistle quickly examines the remaining rooms in the gatehouse and determines that they are empty and have no corpses. 
From the center of the lower courtyard, the group heads towards the stern and to a room on the port side.
It’s an abandoned kitchen. Unfortunately, Unla Kee’s brash entrance provokes undead. A chill emanates from everywhere at once. Rime creeps across surfaces and his breath plumes before him in the frigid air. A pained and distant moan rises with the wind, and in his peripheral vision, he sees several figures detach themselves from the shadows and glide toward the 3HPP—weightless, formless specters. He notes that two of the specters look very different. 
Orsik takes note of a misty raven. He remembers stories from his childhood about spectral ravens that were very dangerous, “The raven appears to be the real problem.”
The rest of the group is tempted to say, “It’s just a ghostly raven.” 
He strikes the raven before they can dissuade him but does not kill it. 
Two of the specters float toward Orsik and strike him with a wave of negative energy, draining the life force from him. 
Vardis uses a warning flare and thwarts one of the creatures with a flash of bright light. 
The raven then flies away from Orsik, incorporeally, to the other end of the room. From there, it caws, charming Orsik and Unla Kee. The effect of the charm leaves them stunned and prone. 
One of the specters attacks the stunned Unla Kee, critically wounding the mage.
