# Chapter 82 - Hekaton's location revealed at last

Orsik looks at the presumptive Waterbaron and smiles, “Getting information from him will be easy.” 

He shows Lord Drylund the golden goose that Queen Sansuri had given the 3HPP in the Maelstorm, a coin found on the body of one of the assailants who had murdered her mother, Queen Neri. A murder that would then lead to the unraveling of the Ordning and the disappearance of King Hekaton. 

Lord Drylund acts unimpressed, “Yes, that’s one of ours. I know it’s worth ten gold pieces, other than that …” 

Thistle is unimpressed with Orsik’s questioning. “Why is one coin different from another?” he muses. 

But Orsik was trying to throw Lord Drylund off his game, and he succeeded. The fool volunteers information - “On this ship, the coin is worth ten gold; off, it’s worthless. But you knew that. Why do you ask about it?” 

Orsik explains where the coin was found and why they are asking. 

Lord Drylund walks towards the aquarium, picks up his Octopus, and pets it. 

Thistle can’t figure it out Lord Drylund’s game. Unla Kee does and signals that Lord Drylund is lying and is worried. 

Orsik speaks reassuringly, “Why are you worried? We’re all friends.” 

Thistle flies up to him and says, “We are all friends.” 

Drylund mutters, “I am not nervous. I am not okay. But stories of murder and death of Queens and Kings of Storm Giants worry me. Imagine if they thought I was responsible.” 

Even Thistle can figure out he’s lying. 

Orsik reaches in with the verbal blade, “Look, we’re here to support you in this upcoming election. And we’re supposed to be meeting with your associates later today? And it would be great if we knew more by then.” 

Drylund splutters, “I will have nothing because I know nothing. We have many customers, and one must have left with a coin in their pocket.” 

“Then you won’t mind if we ask around?” 

“Ask!”
As they leave the room, Orsik asks, “So, where do we meet tomorrow?” 

“Ah yes, near the Waterbaron’s palace in the central market.” 

Thistle whispers to Orsik, “Isn’t that where they tried to kill us last time?” 

Orsik smiles and says, “Very well.” 

The night flies by. Unla Kee wanted to gamble, but the free booze got in the way. 

As they leave the boat in the early dawn hours at the Yartar Docks, they notice that Lady Atalia seems to be waiting for them. When they try to follow, she moves away discretely. But not so discretely that she cannot be followed. 

She stops and sits down at a tavern. The 3HPP sits down at her table. 
## 
Orsik.

“Mr. Orsik, please sit down,” she whispers to the dwarf. 

As he sits, she says, “This place has a drink that is perfect for getting rid of the effects of alcohol.” She stares at Unla Kee, “He will need five.” Then she turns to Orsik, “You wanted information, what kind?” 

He smiles, “You know.” 

“Yes, I do. You wish to know if your fake identity as your father is working out. You want to know if I have noticed how intelligent you are.”

Orsik is infuriated at the invasion of privacy and gruffly tells the group, “Someone else has to do the talking.” 

## Thistle 

“We want to know the connection between the Grand Dame and King Hexaton.”

Lady Atalia laughs. “That’s what your concern was? I completely misread you. I thought you were looking for Lord Artan’s kidnappers.” 

Thistle is confused, “Lord, who?” 

“A young lord of Yartar who was kidnapped. I thought you were looking for his kidnappers.” 

Orsik can’t be quiet and interrupts - “Look, that’s a very parochial concern. Yes, we know about Lord Drylund and his kidnappings and his assassinations. And we don’t care. We are interested in the connection between the Grand Dame and the disappearance of King Hexaton.” 

Thistle notices that she looks pretty anxious. As he probes deeper, he picks up on the fact that she, like Drylund, is trying hard to deflect the conversation away from King Hexaton. It’s as if they are afraid of talking bout it.

“I can’t help, but I know someone who could. An evil spell caster named Oosith.” 

## Vardis

“Evil is very binary. And it’s pretty judgemental of you. I mean, what if there is some underlying childhood trauma?”

Lady Atalia is taken aback. She didn’t expect this from a cleric of Corellon. 

“You are so right. And I am so glad you said that. Because now I feel I can unburden myself to you. Oosith has been running the operation to kidnap lordlings. I have been blackmailed to help them. How? Why was my ability at Suave noticed, and then Lord Drylund figured it out? He insisted that I help. I had no choice.”

Vardis looks at her and says, “Yes, it’s amazing how when our lives are on the line, we have no choice.” 

“Exactly.” 

Orsik then nods, “It’s like killing a Vampire by making a deal with Lich.” 

“Could we Stay on the Acolich Dray?” 

“It’s just an example. She doesn’t know what we are talking about, do you?” 

Sensing that there is more than she wants to know, Lady Atalia says, “No, I have no idea.” 

Vardis turns to her again, “Do you know anything about this person?”

“Not very much, other than he is in the sewers to avoid being caught by the Hand or the Shield of Yartar. Fish People protect him.”

Vardis turns to Thistle, who mutters, “Kuo-Toans.” 

Lady Atalia reaches out to Vardis and says, “Look, I know where he is. And you would do me a great favor in killing him. I am not an evil person. I was blackmailed. And I do so desperately want to go back to the light!” 

Vardis groans as he thinks of all those foolish people who believe in the “light” and “dark” motif of morality and goodness. He’s tempted to give her a full-throated sermon on the perversion of that motif in a world where people can see in the dark, but then he decides not to.

Instead, he turns to Orsik and says, “I think we learned everything we needed to. We have another meeting to go to.” 

Lady Atalia stares at him, “You won’t help?” 

And the 3HPP walked away. 

## Unla Kee

The wizard is sober. And he was able to get enough rest to memorize his spells. He’s pretty pleased with the drink the tavern produced and is determined to find out the recipe. 

But now, he’s in a market full of goods. 

Just then, Lord Drylund appears. 

“Let me introduce you to Tapouach, who sells the best apples. And Tapuz, whose oranges are impressive for their sweetness. And here’s Mitbach, where anyone buys goods for their kitchen.” 

Unla Kee notices Tapouach’s purse, which is close to his hand. He lifts it while Drylund talks to the poor merchant. 

When Unla Kee opens the bag, he finds 23 coppers. “This guy is so poor. That was a waste of my time.” So he decides to steal an apple as well. 

As Drylund speaks, he eats it. 

Tapouach notices, but realizing that Unla Kee is a friend of the Lord, he smiles. 

Orsik 

“So we are here to meet with someone, a Sorcer named Oosith perhaps, who specializes in kidnappings?” 

Lord Drylund, who speaks with the characteristic h’s and gargled r’s of the Calamanishan, is shocked. 

“We do not discuss this here. We use code. Oosith is my campaign manager in charge of special persuasion operations.” 

Orsik says, “And he’s in the sewers?” 

Drylund looks angered, “How did you know this?” 

Before Orsik can dazzle with his intelligence, Vardis answers, “Because we are equally connected.” 

Lord Drylund mutters, “This is an unacceptable security risk. I must know.” 

Orsik is still puzzled by the choice of language, “Campaign Manager?” 

Lord Drylund laughs, “It’s essential to appeal to what the voters value. They value their children, and I have them. If they wish to receive them, they will vote for me.” 

“You need a better campaign manager, and I think we can do a better job than this guy.” 

“Perhaps? Or you could work together? I will take you to him.” 

“You will.” 

And he does, leading them deep into the sewers of Yartar. The sewer tunnels are roughly 8 feet high, and there is a trench in the middle where the sewage flows. On either side of the trench is a pathway. 

At last, the sewers open into a large area, and a Kuo-toan appears. 

Thistle 

“Spend enough time with the Cult of the Dragon, and you will learn undercommon,” Thistle mutters. 

Thistle translates for the 3HPP. 

“The KuoToan is unhappy with Drylund because only the emissary is supposed to come here. Drylund then explains that they are friends. The KuoToan then mutters that they are not members of the society. Drylund then assures - him/her/them - that they will soon become members.”  

Just then, Thistle interrupts, “We are friends and have come to help.” 

The KuoToan glares, “This is a private society business.” 

The fish man turns to Drylund - “You should have never brought them here.” 

Drylund responds, “But they wanted to meet the boss.” 

At that, the Kuo Toan says, “The boss, yes, I think they should.” 

Drylund then turns to the 3HPP: " Unfortunately, I cannot join you. For operational security reasons and plausible deniability, I cannot see Oosith.” 

The Kuo Toan laughs, “The boss doesn’t want to see your ugly face!” 

Thistle latches onto that - “Lord Drylund, will you let them speak to you like that?” 

Drylund is demure, “They are doing their job.” 

Orsik then tries to use a deep, scary voice to intimidate Lord Drylund, but he slips, and his voice becomes squeaky. 

But Drylund caves. 

“I insist we go,” he says to the Kuo Toans. 

Thistle is happy and exclaims, “We are here to ensure that we clear up any misunderstandings between you and Mr Oosith.” 

And with that, three more Kuo Toans emerge from the darkness and march Drylund and the 3HPP through a great ladder and down a ladder to a much larger circular room. The room is filled with water except for a walkway heading east to a small room at the other end. Along the edge of the circular room is a walkway that heads north and south from the entrance that leads to a pipe and a valve. The room is 15 feet high. The water appears to reach a depth of 15 feet. 

Thistle hears a voice in their head as they step on the ground: “I apologize for the unpleasant local. Perhaps you could come to my office?” 

The rest of the 3HPP acknowledge that they also hear it. 

“Finally, a friendly voice,” acknowledged the ranger. 

Orsik nods and is pleased when he says, “At last, someone treating us with the respect and dignity we deserve!” 

Thistle piles on, “It so good to meet friends and allies.” 

“Lord Drylund tells me that you wish to help him get elected? Such a conversation will work best in my office at the end of the walkway. And Mr Kee, there is alcohol.” 

Thistle laughs when he hears Orsik say, “A siren song.” 
## 
Orsik

“I only trust Vardis. And I don’t trust you,” Orsik thinks. 

He grabs Drylund and shoves him forward. 

The voice says gently, “There is no need for hostages, Mr. Orsik.”

Orsik ignores the voice and thinks, “And that’s why I have one.” 

Drylund is shoved first into the room. Orsik expects a trap, but instead, he finds a room that looks like the offices of a very important person. 

The KuoToans follow the 3HPP to the door.  

Just then, the trap is sprung. The Kuo Toans slam the door shut and try to lock it. 

Unla Kee counters by casting telekinesis and shoving the door open. 

Drylund pleads with Orsik, “You have to save me. They’ll kill me.” 

Orsik pauses and stares at the man, “You have to tell me what you know about Hekaton.” 

“Is that your only price?” 

“Yes.” 

Drylund looks terrified, takes a deep breath, and whispers, “I’m a member of the Kraken Society. We’ve been keeping Hekaton prisoner on the Morkoth. It’s a special ship that can’t be detected. The Morkoth sails around the northern islands of the Trackless Sea.” 

“So how do we find it?” Orsiks asks. 

“You have a flying castle; fly there and use your eyes. Now, can you please…” 

Orsik sees his eyes bulge out in terror, and then they roll back into his eyesockets, and blood flows from his nose as he dies in Orsik’s hands. 
And then the mysterious voice calls out, “Well, that was annoying. Finding a new Waterbaron will be tricky. And we will have to kill you.” 

The creature tries to charm Orsik and fails. 
## 
Thistle 

“There's no point in letting this go to waste,” says the fairy as he quickly moves the treasure into Orsik’s bags of holding. 

Two items grab his attention. The first is a pendant with a capital R wrapped in robes that belonged to nobles. 

Vardis looks at it and mutters, “Another fetch quest.” 

The other is a peculiar device that causes some scattered golden goose coins to glow. Thistle picks one up and notices a number. 

Unla Kee burps, “Well, that’s how casinos prevent forgeries.”
