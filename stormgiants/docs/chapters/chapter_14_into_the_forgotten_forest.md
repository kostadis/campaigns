# Chapter 14 - Into the Forgotten Forest
## Azam 10-02 Taraskh
As the door slams behind Vardis, Azam stares at the remaining adventurers and thinks, “oh dear.” 
And when Thistle demands something more than the excellent reward his provider and leader have provided for these adventurers, Azam is left speechless. 
“The audacity!” 
But then Hsing comes to his rescue, raising his head from a sleepy position; he says in his quiet old voice, “I have information on your missing uncle.” 
Azam is shocked when Thistle seems ungrateful. 
“I would prefer to have the information related to my friend.” 
Azam is ready to leave, but Hsing gestures him to sit, and then the pseudo-dragon says, “Yes, I know. But I think you want your uncle’s location first.” 
“Where is he?”
“That is the reward,” explains Azam.
“How do I know you have the information?”
Azam is outraged. He stands up and shouts, “you insult Hsing? You question the honor of the Lord’s Alliance? You question the all-powerful SEER?” 
Hsing shouts, “AZAM!” Azam is frozen in place. Hsing turns to him, “These are adventurers, and they don’t know me. And they can’t tell the difference between a lizard and me. And the Zhentarim wanna be thought he can kill me. Now sit down and let them ask their questions.” 
Azam is stunned into silence, regains his composure, sits down, and Hsing yawns. 
“How do you know who my uncle is?” 
This time, Azam smiles as Hsing swats the insult away, “The SEER spoke. And if she, he or they, we are not going to reveal their gender, that they have the information, then it is valid.” 
Azam then adds, “we can also offer gold as well.”
Thistle and Unla Kee convene and agree to the quest. 
Azam turns to Hsing, “let me see if he wants to talk to you. He is fatigued.” Azam strokes the pseudo-dragon and brings his ear to his mouth. 
“I have been told to tell you what the SEER wants you to know.”
Thistle and Unla Kee’s eyes roll.
“You may have heard of the Ettin Gralm?” 
Thistle can not remember his uncle saying anything about this for the life of him. 
“Well, Gralm has a realm in the Lonely Moor and has a force of Hobgoblins and Goblins under his control. His realm is a tenday journey through the Forgotten Forest.” 
Azam pauses and stares at Thistle, “Our agents have said that Gralm has been hunting down Fairies for food and not just because of the food shortage.” 
Unla Kee snorts, “a likely story.” 
Azam glares but continues, “We have received word that Bad Fruul is trying to force him to join his army that Bad Fruul intends to use to destroy Parnast.” 
“So, what do you need?” 
“We have two magical contracts, either of which we would like Gralm to sign. The first says that Gralm will not align with Bad Fruul and our preferred contract, an alliance of Gralm and his forces with ours against Bad Fruul.”
Thistle stares, “Ettins and contracts?”
“Ettin law is poorly developed. So we rely on these to enforce magical penalties if the counterparty violates the terms.” 
Unla Kee looks unimpressed. 
Azam turns to him, whispers something, and then speaks loudly, “Mr. Kee, would this be enough?”
Unla Kee smiles. 
“And the amount I promised, Mr. Kee, is between you and your friends.” 
And with that, Hsing teleports away. 
Azam relaxes in the chair in the back room of the Golden Tankard and says, “While Hsing travels on his missions, I will be here, waiting for you.” 
## The Group

Orsik, Vardis, and Wallace are drinking in the main hall of The Golden Tankard. 
“I hate that lizard.” 
Thistle flits up to them,  tells them everything he has learned, and ends by saying, “He’s giving me some vital information about my family, so we gotta go kill this ettin.” 
Unla Kee stares and corrects him, “get Gralm to sign a contract.” 
“Contract, kill, big difference.” 
Orsik muses, “so we hike 10 days to go kill an Ettin.” 
“Sign a contract.” 
“What’s in it for me?” 
“100 Gold?”
“Each?”
“For all of us.” 
Sensing that Orsik is about to bail, Thistle, says, “Uncle Joon is an excellent source of intel. He travels everywhere. He learns all of the local stuff. He knows what’s going on.”
Unla Kee adds, “He’s well informed, and I can easily turn that 100 Gold into 300 Gold.” 
Vardis coughs, “or zero – we saw that.”
“Well, it was more like losing half.” 
Orsik is not interested. 
Wallace pulls him aside, “we gotta do this. I need to protect my village.” 
“Protect your village?” 
“Bad Fruul is going to attack the village. And if he’s got the Ettin, it will be worse.”
“Okay so let’s kill Bad Fruul. I have nothing against the Ettin. I barely know him.” 
Wallace groans as he realizes Orsik is confused about what was asked, “we’re not trying to kill the Ettin; we are trying to get him to sign a contract to help OR to sign a contract to not join the fight.” 
Just then, Azam coughs.
“What do you want?” 
“Well, it would appear that I misread my notes. I was told to offer you a potion of healing and a potion of greater healing if during the attack during the festival, none of you put personal glory over saving a villager of Parnast, and if you successfully defeated the attack. My apologies.” 
Wallace tries harder and reminds him that although he doesn’t love Elspeth, she is a friend, and his Dad is here, and he can’t really let them die. 
Orsik is annoyed and wants to kill something. “What if we first killed the Wyvern?” 
Wallace scratches his chin, “see, having the Wyvern is a cost-saving measure. If we didn’t have the Wyvern, we would have to have a prison, guards, and food. And we don’t really have the kind of people that can imprison some of the folks that come by here. It’s much simpler to hand them over to the Wyvern and not think about it. And frankly, the threat of imprisonment with the Wyvern acts as a deterrent.”
Orsik smugly turns and says, “why would we want to change a totalitarian system like that?
Wallace, who seems keen to do what Azam wants, jumps in, “hey Thistle, could your Uncle Joon help Orsick and Vardis with their map?” 
“Absolutely. He knows everything about the North.” 
Vardis seems more interested, and Orsik picks up on that. 
“Go on.” 
“He knows all kinds of the craziest things. He’s been up and down all of Faerun and the North. He’s the guy we have been looking for! He’s going to find your family treasure!” 
“He better.” Orsik thinks aloud, “This is the one town where a city guard wants me to work for them. And it’s where I have found my first lackey.”
And with that, Orsik nods, and the group heads off. 

## Unla Kee 1st day of the 3rd tenday of Taraskh
The first day of the journey to Gralm’s realm is through the grasslands that abut with the great desert. The weather is pleasant, and the trip is uneventful until midday when they see a clearing over a quarter mile wide. In that clearing, the land has no living creature. And the birds choose to fly around it. But the most curious part of the clearing is a 50’ rusty sword.
Unla Kee immediately recognizes the sword as a sign from one of the many prophecies of the end times. He knows what he must do. He must attempt to remove the sword. 
His traveling companions refuse to join him. 
“The burden of saving the world rests on my shoulders alone,” he thinks, “and that is why everyone must address me as Mr. Kee.” 
He enlarges himself and then proceeds to the sword. A slight electric shock zaps him when he tries to remove it. He is undeterred. He pulls hard, and the blade refuses to budge. 
As he stands next to the sword, heaving and thinking about what to do next, he notices the ground being disturbed as if something wants to come out. Soon a decayed hand pops out. 
“Mr. Kee is no fool,” he thinks, running to the perimeter. When he arrives at the edge of the dead land, he looks back, and the land is as undisturbed as it always was.
The next evening the group arrives at the edge of the Forgotten Forest. The group remembers the rocks of the Fey Portal that they had found. And they realize that perhaps that portal is not as inert as they thought. But that’s not the immediate problem; the electrical thunderstorm that is fast approaching is a much more significant concern. 
Mr. Kee rushes into the forest, and he overhears Orsik saying, “don’t be the tallest thing in a field when the storm god Thalos is throwing his bolts.” 
## Group 3rd day of the 3rd tenday of Taraskh

The rain continues as a slight drizzle through the night. The following day, as they march deeper into the forest, it turns into a raging thunderstorm.
Around midday, the storm abruptly stops, and the clouds break. The sun is blindingly hot, making it almost as host as the desert of Anauroch. 
And surrounding the group is a gang of goblins led by a hobgoblin named Mohm. 
Thistle, Orsik and Thuul kils the hobgoblin. The goblins freak out, shoot their arrows, flee into the forest, and hide behind some trees. 
Before the group can attack, the ground sprouts trees and vines that attempt to entangle everyone. Only Thistle can’t escape. 
Thuul rushes into the forest to attack and disappears in a pile of fine dust. 
Orsik stares in shock, “I guess this is why they called it the Forgotten Forest!” 
Thistle is in horror, “This is a Fey Crossing.” 
And then the group hears a voice, “Thistle, is that you?” 
The Goblins in the forest are in shock and start muttering about traitors. 
Mr. Kee kills two with a volley of magic missiles, injuring the other. 
Orsik throws a handaxe killing the last goblin. 
The group stands at the forest edge, worried about the Fey Crossings. Just then, Thuul appears and snaps at the space where the Goblins were. 
“Fey crossings have strange effects. This one sent him six seconds into the future.” 
The voice from the forest shouts, “are you The Thistle Wendrood, related to the Uncle Joon?” 
“What did he do?” 
“Yes, you are indeed his nephew. Only a family member would ask that.” 
Thistle remembers that this must be the sprite in Prince Thornacious’ court that is a spy for the Zhentarim whom Orsik is looking for. 
“Are you part of Prince Thornacious’ court?” 
“My dear boy, don’t you remember Sir Oblivillish? Well, I have heard such wonderful things about you!” He pauses, “from his highness, of course. I was a court member, but it was one of those unfortunate misunderstandings where I went hunting and killed a stag and upset the Prince. He felt that it was his stag. But I was so hungry, having not eaten in two days!” He pauses and looks into the distance, “It was so delicious, and I just wanted to eat it!” 
The group remembers how possessive Prince Thornacious was about his game from when they went hunting and were told to go elsewhere. 
“So what happened,” asks Orsik.
“I was cursed! I can’t turn invisible. I start invisible, then become visible, and then become invisible. A sprite that can not become invisible, the shame and the horror.” 
“Huh?” 
The sprite demonstrates, and it is clear, that the invisibility power no longer works. 
“Yeah, that does appear to malfunction.” 
“So I left the court. It was a lowly court, what with the exile. I know it was forced, even if Prince Thornacious says otherwise. The pompous fool pretends we don’t know Queen Tatiana sent him into exile. He acts as if it was his choice. Well, it wasn’t mine, but the Queen insisted all of the court follow the Prince. She could have sent him alone.” He sighs.
“What is a Prince without his court?” 
Orsik thinks, “my kind of backstabbing lackey.” And then continues, “so you left the court and betrayed your Prince by….” 
The sprite will have none of that, “I was sent into double exile! I was thrown out of the Royal Court and then out of Thornacious’ court. I was dismissed over a terrible misunderstanding.” 
Orsik is thinking, “why don’t I kill this guy.” 
Wallace picks up on Orsik’s intention and tells him to reread the notice from the Zhentarim. 
The dwarf reads it carefully and shouts, “OH, YOU ARE THE SPY THAT I AM SUPPOSED TO PROTECT!”
