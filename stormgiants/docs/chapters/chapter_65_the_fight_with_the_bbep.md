# Chapter 65 - The Fight with the BBEP
## (Big Bad at the end of the play)

## 7th day of the 1st tenday of Eleint of 1492 
### Thistle
As Blagothkus’ castle flies towards Silverymoon, Thistle remembers something. Laela’s near Ironslag. For all Thistle knows, she might be a prisoner of the Giant. 
He looks outside at Evermoors below the clouds and mutters to himself: 
“Laela, I know you’re hoping I’ll come to save you from whatever monstrous torment Adminond Kroskas, Ilymrith, or whatever has inflicted upon you, but I have to save Silverymoon. You’d never forgive me if I let the city burn to save you.” 
### Orsik 
“We need some allies who would be discrete,” says Orsik to the 3HPP.  
“There’s Darlathara who owes for her election,” mentions Thistle. 
“Lord Zymorven owes us one for saving his son,” observes Vardis. 
“Urgala for figuring out what happened to the love of her life?” notes Unla Kee. 
“Tamalin Zoar was someone who helped us once,” remembers Orsik. 
“And then there is her brother, High Captain Horix Zoar,” observes Vardis. 
“What about the Hand of Yartar?” asks Thistle. 
“And then there is the Zhentarim,” notes Vardis. 
“They like it when we do things for them, not for us,” observes Orsik. 
“What about those rich folks in Mirabar and Xanthalar’s Keep?” asks Unla Kee. 
Orsik observes, “We could fetch some guards from Parnast or get some guards from Silverymoon…” 
Then he sighed, remembering Wallace, and said, “Let the folks in Parnast be in peace.” 
And then he perks up: " We could always go to the Harpers and make the case that I am trying to redeem my family name from the damage that my son, Orsik, with a K, did.” 
They stare at him, and he laughs, “I don’t think that Krowen Valharrow would go for that…” 
He turns to Escarlotta and shouts, “To Zymorven Hall!” 
## 10th day of the 1st tenday of Eleint of 1492
### Lord Zymorven
The castle settles down a few miles from Zymorven Hall. Lord Zymorven is not surprised. His scrying magic tells him whenever anyone approaches his homestead. 
“I am the guard of the road!” he mutters, thinking about how he would like to have his giant killer sword right now.
“No matter!” 
He stands in his full armor, ready to defend his homestead. 
“I recognize Vardis, Unla Kee, and that charming fairy, but where is the dwarf?” 
There is a dwarf among them, but he looks a little bit older. 
The dwarf pulls out Lord Zymorven’s giant slayer and says, “I am Orsik’s father, Orsic, and I have come for help.” 
“I owe your son a debt for saving my son from a terrible set of choices and death.” 
“My son produced a play starring a famous actor who created a persona, Bob Saget, that was a demon. Unfortunately, a real demon, or doppelganger with magical powers, killed and replaced the real Bob. And now this creature plans to destroy Silverymoon with a zombie plague.” 
“We must tell the leaders of the city!” shouts Zymorven.
“We can’t. Bob will release his horde and destroy the city. So, we have decided to assemble a strike team to attack quietly. The watch will know if we fail and face a weakened evil.” 
“How noble of you! So different from your son!” 
### Orsik 
He puts on a masterful performance of dwarf acting and says, “Yes.”
As he marches towards the castle, he tells Zymorven, “Don’t stay anywhere other than the tower, and don’t go out at night and don’t go into the shadows.” 
“Why?”
“We have a vampire problem.” 
## 2nd day of the 2nd tenday of Eleint 1492
### Urgala Meltimer

“Lord Zymorven!” she says, smiling. 
“Ma’am,” says the Lord. 
She then notices Orsik’s father and the other members of the 3HPP, and her smile darkens. 
“What brings you here?” she asks, worried. 
Orsik explains the situation. 
She looks at him and then at Lord Zymorven and says, “I suppose we could have one last adventure.” 
“Saving Silverymoon is a thing of ours,” he says enigmatically. 
### Darthara Shendrel
The Lord Protector is not happy to see the 3HPP. She had assured Krowen Valharrow that these were the contractors he was looking for, and that had blown up in her face. 
She remembered Krowen’s tone. 
“They betrayed the circles. We lost many agents and a powerful set of magic that will be expensive and time-consuming to replace. I took a risk on these people, and they have betrayed us all.” 
She examines the dwarf, convinced he is Orsic, but the dwarf isn’t Orsik. 
He also explains that he is here to redeem his family name. 
“Your son did a terrible thing. I lent him my credibility and gave him the magic artifacts to help the heroes of the Ordning. What did he do? He unleashed hell up and down the cost.”
She notices how Orsik’s father seems particularly excited, “He did that? Tell me more!” 
Vardis snikers, “Some father you turned out to be.” 
Unla Kee shrugs, “Kids.” 
“He betrayed the Harper’s circles! Several agents were killed!” She then stares at the elder Orsik, “I am not sure if the apple falls far from the tree.” 
She stares at the Dwarf and realizes he’s just a father who wants to fix his son's mistakes and shouldn’t be blamed. 
“He did save the town. And more importantly, the information the 3HPP provided that we used against Cindrell did prevent the town from changing its allegiance to the Lord’s Alliance.” 
Then she turns to Vardis – “I am so pleased to see you have abandoned that boat anchor of a miscreant. As a Correlon cleric, your association harmed those who believed in your god.” 
She notes that the cleric is displeased and continues, “He seemed to know everything, always could figure everything out. There was something off about his intelligence.” 
### Vardis
He stares at Darathra and contemplates expressing himself freely, but then he remembers how much funnier it will be when she finds out she helped Orsik. 
So he turns to her and asks, “Will you join us?” 
She pauses, “Yes. I owe you that much for saving the town from Cindrell. But after this, all of our debts are cleared.” 
As they enter the castle, Urgala asks, “Why are we forced to stay in this part of the castle, and why are there zombies?” 
Unla Kee snickers, “It is an interactive experience.” 
Vardis is about to laugh hard when Orsik mutters, “There’s a vampire.”
Lord Zymorven shouts, “ A vampire? You brought this thing next to my house?” 
“We didn’t bring it that close. We were like a mile away, and it was sunlight,” mutters Vardis. 
Zymorven coughs, “The castle must be positioned away from Silverymoon. We can’t allow any undead vampiric infestation.” 
At that point, they hear a roar.
“What was that?” 
“A dragon,” explains Orsik.
“A DRAGON!” shrieks Zymorven. 
“Technically speaking, it’s a Dracolich,” explains Vardis.
“A DRACOLICH?” 
Unla Kee says, “I still think we should have just gone with the plan where we unleash…” 
Orsik interrupts him before Zymorven can hear, “We agreed that was a bad plan.” 
But Unla Kee isn’t dissuaded, “but was it?”
Vardis ignores him and turns to Orsik – “Anyone else?” 
“I am not begging for help from the Harpers, and I don’t think the Zoars will want to help. And the Hand of Triboar isn’t going to mix well with this group.” 
“Then I guess this is it?” 
## 4th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleint 1492 
### Unla Kee
Having spent years studying the fates, Unla Kee is sure that Bob is not the cause of the world's end. And for that reason alone, he is bored. 
The burden of knowing that it’s all irrelevant because he knows how to stop the end of the world. And only he knows. And he also knows the world doesn’t end. 
Unless he decides otherwise. 
And he also knows that if they don’t stop Bob tonight, Silverymoon will be the victim of a zombie apocalypse.
This brings him to a conflicted decision about Kaelen. Kaelen was a playwright who attempted a summoning ritual that was partly thwarted. 
Instead of summoning a fearsome force of destruction, he summoned Bob Saget. 
Unla Kee’s friends felt that Bob was a pet that could be exploited for profit. Unla Kee knew better. He knew that Bob was evil incarnate and that, in the end, destruction would follow. But, then again, as he would say, “Money?” 
He doesn’t understand Orsik’s reluctance to unleash the Dracolich and the vampire on Silverymoon. 
“The Destruction and Chaos would be – beautiful.” 
Unla Kee noodles – “Orsik doesn’t understand the beauty of the plan; with the Dracolich creating chaos, an attack on Bob would have the perfect cover. The destruction of the royal theater house would be one more tragedy in a day of tragedies that could be blamed on the Cult of the Dragon. As for why they would attack Silverymoon? Laeral Silverhand, as First Lord of Waterdeep, who created the coalition that defeated Severin and Sandesyl, is a perfect patsy.” 
Unla Kee takes a swig of his drink and forgets. 
He opens his eyes and remembers where he is, in Silverymoon, watching Thistle examining Kaelen’s house. 
“I could fireball the place?” 
But Thistle will have none of that.
So, his mind wanders to the discussion between Zymorven and Orsik in his castle. 
“Kill the young man? I think not!” shouts Lord Zymorven. “We should interrogate him.” 
Orsik sighs – “I get your desire for impartiality, but we don’t have time.” 
And the argument would have gone for hours if Urgala had not reminded everyone of how Kaelen’s original plan was the mass murder of the entire town of Triboar, and the 3HPP thwarted that plan, and that as far she is concerned, they should have killed Kaelen on the spot. 
Thistle breaks his reverie again. 
“So Kaelen is sitting there in his house tapping his fingers. The place looks like it’s covered in dust. The door to the house hasn’t been opened in a while. Something is very wrong with him.” 
“Fireball?” 
“No, we don’t need to attract attention. And we don’t know if Kaelen is somehow connected to Bob. The demon doesn’t know we are about to attack him.” 
Unla Kee sighs. 
### Vardis
Vardis has been standing watch for Thistle, which is a good thing. If the patrol saw a fairy casing a house, there’s no telling what would happen. 

The guard sees Vardis, recognizes him as a member of the 3HPP and a goodly race, and smiles. 
Vardis waves his hand and makes a noise, and the guard smiles back and tells him to be careful. 
The guard speaks to him for a few minutes and leaves. 
Just then, Orsik appears from the shadows, “Fucking racists.” 
“Hey, I benefit from Elven privilege.”
Thistle floats into view – “I think we leave Kaelen where he is.”
Vardis nods, “He may or may not be powering the demon, but if we kill him, Bob will know, and we will lose the advantage of surprise.”
Lord Zymorven coughs, “We will tell the Lord of Silverymoon before we attack?” 
“Do we want to put them in a state of panic?” 
Orsik whines – “but how are we going to get the credit if we don’t tell them?”
“That’s the concern here; we are only doing it for the accolades, and here I thought it was to remove the stain of your son’s and my best friend’s actions,” says Vardis with a  mischievous grin.”
Unla Kee pipes up – “We are going to make a big mess. They’ll know we did it. And then, Lord Aerasume won’t be too happy to hear that he let a demon summon a horde of zombies in his theater next to the royal palace. He might want to get rid of us.” 
Vardis almost laughs when he sees Orsik say in a flat, bored, and annoyed tone – “Good Orsik wouldn’t do it just for the accolades. He would do it because it’s the right thing to do.” 
The cleric is bemused and thinks that perhaps Orsik is finally seeing the wisdom of the “let it burn” strategy he was a fan of.
To his surprise, Lord Zymorven speaks, “My dear boy, I understand having to cover up your son’s failings.” He pauses and says, “We have that in common.” 
Vardies tries hard not to laugh when Orsik mutters, “Yes, we do have that in common.” 
“I know many in Silverymoon, and I will ensure they hear of your courageous, heroic sacrifice. I will take care of everything.” 
Vardis wonders if Orsik’s gritting teeth can grind adamantium steel when his friend says, “That’s so generous of you.” 
### Darthala 
“I owe the 3HPP many things. And I should tell the Harpers about this. But I made compromises and made choices. Not all of them wise,” are the thoughts going through Darthala’s head as she waits in the darkness.
“Explain to me what we are doing here?” 
“Bob Saget has created a portal at the entrance of the Theater. Theatergoers enter the portal to see a play. Meanwhile, in the real theater, he creates zombies with the help of a Red Wizard in league with the Drow,” explains Thistle. 
The theatergoers, dressed in their finest, approach the doors that appear to open to the theater. When they have all entered, the door slams shut. 
“But they are inside?” 
“No, they’re not. We thought so, too, but the portal is inside the theater.” 
With that, she watches as Unla Kee casts a dispel magic spell to remove the wards that protect the entrance. 
“Alarm spells.” 
And then she enters and sees the horror that Thistle described. The theater is a large rectangular building with a stage at one end, a series of benches at the orchestra level, and a balcony that can be reached from the back. There appear to be patrons sitting watching an empty stage. 
The stage itself is splattered with blood and the odd body part. 
Darthala looks at the patrons and realizes they all seem to be wearing one of three repeating costumes. She stares more intently and realizes that they are dressed like the 3HPP. 

She looks at the half-orc behind the concession stands and stifles a gasp. 

The head lolls oddly from side to side, disturbingly and unnaturally; its neck is broken. 
She looks at another in an Orsik costume and gulps – a Tabaxi whose neck is partially severed, with muscles and bones visible through the gore.
“They are all zombies.”
Thistle whispers, “That is what we said.”
