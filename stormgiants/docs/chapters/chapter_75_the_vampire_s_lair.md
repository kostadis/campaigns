# Chapter 75 - The Vampire's Lair
## Orsik
The Undead Ogres were still climbing up the tower, although they were slowed down for a minute because of the darkness Unla Kee had cast. 
“The wand of wonder accomplishing something other than amusing the wizard, surprising.” 
Blagothkus shouts, " The zombies can’t see us, and Glazahael has left. We can use this opportunity to run to Sandesyl’s tower.” 
The dwarf is not impressed with the giant's tactical and strategic awareness. The whole purpose of the maneuver was to kill off the ogres here, where they were primarily defenseless. 
Blagothkus, however, won’t shut up, “Orsik, I know we thought that the Zombie Ogres were intended to act as a reserve, but now we know that they are just a way to keep Sandesyl’s enemies trapped so that the dragon can kill them from above. We need to run.” 
The stout fighter shouts, “Then run. I have a mission, and I intend to complete it.” 
## Thistle 
The dragon fear is, well, quite something else. 
Of course, with his new dragon eyes, I should be terrifying the dragon, not the other way around. But that’s not how the world works if you are a fairy experimented on by a mad dragon. 
Wait, that’s odd. Glazhael has moved out of the eyeshot, almost like she wants me to approach Sandesyl’s tower. 
As Thistle flies to the tower, he sees Unla Kee fly with his giant Pegasus wings. Vaardis is running, and even Orsik seems to have abandoned the quest to kill all of the ogres. 
## Vardis. 
“Not that again,” thinks Vaardis.
“Last chance to summon Bob,” intones Orsik. 
“One enemy at a time,” whispers Thistle.
Vaardis sighs, “I liked the idea until I realized we were planning to give him a near-invulnerable emissary in our plane of existence.” 
“There were flaws, but we could work around them.” 
Vaardis pleads with his eyes to anyone who will help him. Surprisingly, Unla Kee does when he explains that Bob is currently trapped in the Astral Plane. 
“Trapped?” shouts an excited Orsik. 
“Yes, and as long as he is trapped in the Astral Plane, he doesn’t have access to all his power.” 
“So he’s impotent.” 
“I wouldn’t use that term, but yes.” 
“Let’s leave Bob where he is and go kill a vampire.” 
## Orsik
“The tower is empty,” he groans. 
Blagothkus sighs, “Yes, she’s carved out her own lair in the ice caves below.” 
“Why would she be in her lair?” 
“Because that’s where she is normally.” 
“But last time she was here.” 
“Yes, but last time you hadn’t killed her wyvernliches, gotten rid of her material for zombies, and started an attack on her Ogre Zombies. And most importantly, she invited you.” 
“But Glazhael killed us.” 
“Sandesyl isn’t the kind of person who is trusting of her minions. She’ll send several of her spawn to find your bodies, and when parts of them are brought to her, she might believe you are dead. At which point she may leave her lair.” 
Orsik is pleased. An enemy worthy of his tactical brilliance. 
Blagothkus
“So, are we going down?” 
The dwarf stares at him glumly, “how are we going to sneak with this guy?” 
Blagothkus is delighted, “Look, Escarlotta said I had to help you guys if you felt I was a help. How about I just stay here and guard the entrance?” 
Vaardis pats Blagothkus on his kneecap in a mixture of contempt and reassurance and says, “Oh, don’t be like that!” 
Before he can respond, the fairy squeals, “Staying here would be a hindrance.” 
And with that, Blagothkus sighs and squeezes himself into the tunnel below the tower. 
The tunnel under the tower opens up into a large cavernous area. 
 Vaardis
“Right hand rule,” shouts Thistle. 
Vaardis looks at him and remembers something the old guard of the cult of Correllon would say. If you keep doing something the same way, he will ensure the other way is easy. It’s why Vaardis always did things the other way. Although it wasn’t clear, things were simpler that way. 
Thistle ignores him, follows the ice tunnel north, and emerges in a large cavern with a statue of an enormous red dracolich with a terrifying blend of draconic might and infernal, demonic features. Its skeletal frame still crackles with arcane energy, and burning embers light its empty eye sockets. Blackened, jagged horns curve back over its skull, and leathery, tattered, and bat-like wings spread wide. Its claws are sharp and imbued with hellish fire, capable of searing through the toughest armor. Demonic runes are etched into their bone structure, pulsating with a malevolent glow. 
The cleric stares at the statue momentarily and then says, “Yep, I know this.” 
Everyone stares at him, and he answers, “Seriously? You don’t know who this is? Fine, I’ll explain. It’s Inferno, the Hellbound Dracolich, a once powerful red dragon who made a forbidden pact with a demon lord in its quest for immortality. The legends say that this was the first of the Dracoliches.” 
Orsik nods, “makes sense.” 
Meanwhile, Unla Kee notices something about the statues, “well, isn’t that interesting.”
Vaardis glares, “Will you share with the class?” 
“This is Sammaster, the original leader of the cult of the dragon holding the Tome of the Dragon - the definitive book on Dracoliches. How odd. It almost looks like Sammaster is learning the secrets of undeath from Inferno.” 
## Thistle 
Thistle - Can we please go?
Orsik - No, we need to get the treasure. 
Thistle - We can get it afterward. 
Orsik - But what if we need the treasure to defeat Sandesyl? 
The fairy sighs and scans with his new eyes, determining that other than some valuable gems, “There is nothing of value.”  
But it’s too late; Unla Kee is already flying up to the statue of Inferno and prying the gems loose from the demon’s eyes. 
The four statues surrounding the dracolich animate and advance on Unla Kee. 
Thistle flits up and down as if he is throwing a tantrum – “I TOLD YOU THIS WOULD HAPPEN!” 
The fairy stops and then turns to Blagothkus – “Go clean up his mess, the rest of us will move forward.” 
Orsik turns to him, “I don’t understand why you’re upset. We agreed not to waste resources, and we aren’t wasting any other than a giant we didn’t count upon.” 
“A giant we could have used to grapple the vampire?” 
Thistle’s frustration mounts as Orsik shrugs. 
Continuing to the right, Thistle sees a large cavern to the northwest with two vampire spawns. The same vampire spawns he met earlier in Sandesyl’s lair. Both are in their coffins gossiping.
Thistle sighs and looks south, “perhaps we can try that way.” 
Violating the right-hand rule, he goes towards the south along an ice-hewn passageway that opens into two caverns. To the east are orc zombies busy cleaning. To the west, he hears the sound of a Frost giant wheezing. 
He stops, sniffs, and concludes that Sandesyl’s lair is to the west – through two vampire spawns or a frost giant. 
“I thought we were going to fight a vampire, not an army of minions,” he mutters to Orsik.
Who is curiously smiling – “Good news, we have a minion. And there is Blagothkus, slightly worn down from defeating the armored horrors but still in fighting shape. 
“Did you find the Frost giant, Jarl?” the cloud giant asks. 
“The who?” 
Blagothkus rubs his temple, “Look, I lost the castle when it crashed and landed on the Miklos Glacier on the world's spine. Long story, but it's not important. Anyways, the Heroes of the War of the Dragon showed up, and they convinced the local frost giant jarl, Brunvild, that he should help them repair and steal the castle. When the castle left the Miklos glacier, Brunvild thought he was rid of Sandesyl. But he wasn’t. She was a stowaway. And when she discovered he planned to destroy the Cult of the Dragon, she was happy to let him try. Once he did, however, she turned on him. That’s why there were so many Frost Giant skeletons. Since she couldn’t run the castle without me, she came to me. Brunvild is kept alive, so she has something to snack on. I tried to starve her out, but she knew how to survive for a very long time. Esclarlota has a different version of the facts.” 
Orsik chirps, “Another ally?” 
“He’s too weak.” 
Thistle thinks, “A distraction?” 
And with that, Thistle flies off, carefully hiding in the shadows, past the orc zombies and the ghost that gives them particular instructions on keeping the caves clean. 
He stares at the exhausted and emaciated Frost Giant, sighs, and flies into his face – “Hello.” 
The giant opens his eyes and sees Thistle just a few feet away. 
“I’m here to free you, Harshnag sent me.” 
“Harshnag’s here?” 
“Well, no, but he sent us to free you.” 
The giant squints, “Aren’t you a little bit small to be allied to a giant?”  
Thistle’s eyes roll. “Yes, I am a fairy. I also have a mighty bow, access to some powerful magics, and am possessed by these Dragon Eyes.” 
“Is Blagothkus alive?” 
“Yes, but he’s seen the error of his ways. He’s here to help.”
“Help? He’s why I am trapped here. He’s why my men are dead. He’s why the dwarves trusted me are dead.” He pauses, “Have you seen the dwarves?” 
Thistle remembers the cadavers he tossed over the Evermoors and says, “They aren’t here. Maybe they escaped?” 
Brunvild sighs contentedly. “What about the Orcs? Sandesyl has transformed them into skeletons and put over them a ghost. It’s part of her sick experiments to see if she can create a cleaning service made of undead.”
“No overhead. Smart.” 
“Yes, but the ghost is very obsessed about the particular cave being clean and has not let the orcs move out of it for two years.” 
The Giant stares at him through tired eyes, “Sandesyl lives. If she lives, then I can’t escape.” 
“That’s the plan: you escape, draw some of her allies from her lair, and then we can kill her. We need your help to distract them.” 
Brunvild stares at the fairy, and Thistle recognizes that look. Is the look of a prisoner convinced that trying one more time to escape is better than staying here and living like a blood bank. Brunvild sees that same expression in Thistle, and it’s that moment that convinces Brunvild to try one more time. " Very well.” As Brunvild gets up, he turns to Thistle. “Flying creatures have had a weird impact on my life. First, there was a Kenku, and now you.” 
Thistle is momentarily stunned. The story of the flying Kenku who defeated the Cult of the Dragon, which terrorized the cultists, was a story the prisoners told themselves. He didn’t exist. Kenkus don’t fly. But here was Brunvild saying he knew him. 
The fairy wants to stop and ask him questions, but the sound of guard drakes moving towards the giant’s nest, following Brunvild, who is lumbering out through the southern tunnel, stops him. 
He flies back frantically to the rest of the group, “We need to attack now.” 
The vampire spawn is not surprised. However, the 3HPP are surprised when Orsik waits for Unla Kee to cast haste. Thistle turns to Orsik, “huh?” 
“Yeah, apparently, he has a haste spell.”
“Why hasn’t he been using it?”
Unla Kee shrugs, “it would be too easy that way.”
