# Chapter 21 - The Battle Begins

“Let’s find some more orcs to kill!” shouts Orsik. 
Vardis looks into the southwest cave where he finds a dead human beaten by orcs next to a rock.
“Vardis, I never understood why orcs just sit in their caves! I mean you’d think if they heard combat they would come rushing out and try to help one another? It’s like if you steal from me, that’s bad, but if you steal from them… well who cares.” 
Unla Kee thinks, “I understand their motivations.” 
And he says – “it might be because of the insulation?” 
They all stare at him. 
As Vardis walks in, two Orcs that were hidden attack, from the shadows but he is not surprised. He recognizes them as followers Yurtus, the god of death and disease.  
Orsik rushes in and demonstrates his unique battle tactics and kills one. Except the creature comes back to life. 
“No matter,” says the Dwarf who kills him again. 
Wallace then charges in and attacks the other orc. 
Unla Kee and Thistle were still in the cave with the whelps and the villagers when two more Orcs attacked. 
The first orc attacks with a spiritual weapon and hits Thistle. 
The second orc hits Unla Kee with his spiritual weapon and the spear. 
Thistle looks at his companion, “wow how did they hurt you so badly.”
One of the orcs can’t resist, “you have armor, he doesn’t!”
Unla Kee smirks, “not enough” and hits them with a lightning bolt that they can’t dodge for 29 points of damage. 
Orsik turns to Vardis, “go help the halfling.” 
Vardis approaches Unla Kee and says, “get ready for my light touch” and then heals him. 
Thistle strikes one of the orcs with his bow, but before the orc can laugh, Thuul acid’s damage leaves the orc dead. 
He then pierces the orc’s defenses for 11 HP of damage with an arrow. 
Wallace charges against one of the orcs and kills him. 
With all the orcs dead, Vardis explores the room to the south east of the prison room. As he enters he is hit by the smell of a foul smelling concoction that is brewing over a fire. As he looks around he notices bones and ash litter that is piled up as an altar to Yurtrus. To complete the disgusting spectacle, there is a wooden frame that holds a pale white section of elven skin. Then the smell of a dead corpse grabs his attention and he sees a dead flayed elf. 
Going back to the room he first entered, he finds several gems and gold trade bars. 
Wallace is pretty excited with the new feat he mastered, and charges into the last remaining area of the cave complex. 
As he runs in, he notices how the cave is unnaturally dark. He shouts, “guys, there’s someone here.” 
That’s the last thing he hears as an orc of the Red Fang kills him. The Orcs of the Red Fang are assassins for hire and followers of Shargaas. 
Unla Kee looks into the darkness, sees nothing, and then casts a fireball doing 29 points of damage. The two assassin orcs are unable to escape the blast. Nor is Wallace who is now clinging desperately to life.
Orsik screams, “Wallace!” and runs after him.
Unla Kee raises an eyebrow at Vardis, who says, “I know I have been replaced.” 
Orsik overhears that and says, “you’re BOTH my best friend!” 
Best friend or not, Orsik, is hit for 36 points of damage as he stands in the darkness over Wallace’s unconscious body. 
Thistle rushes into the darkness, stumbles around and finds Wallace’s mouth and forces a potion of healing down his throat healing him. 
Vardis sighs, and then channels the divine power of Larethien to bring daylight to the cave, banishing the magical darkness and causing 16 and 8 points of damage on the orcs. 
But the other Orc of Shargaas casts darkness again and disappears into the dark. 
“I just cleaned that up!” shouts Vardis. 
Unla Kee doesn’t hesitate, unable to see any target through the darkness, he runs into the darkness, under Orsik’s legs, jumps over Thistle, and past the outstretched arm of one of the orcs and then exits the darkness facing the other orc. 
Orsik uses his fighting spirit to gain an advantage over the Orc assassin, killing him. And then rushes to attack the last Orc that is outside the area of darkness and misses. 
Wallace who was knocked out, burned with a fireball, healed by a fairy, gets up from being prone on the ground, and then rushes at the last orc killing him. 
“You thought you were so clever!” 
With the death of the orc, the darkness is dispelled. 
Wallace takes a look around and points out a chest. Inside they find a miniature silver dragon skull and a small sack containing loose coins and gems. 
With the orcs dead, and the treasure secure, the group heads back to the main room with the whelps and villagers. 
“So how much time do we have before the battle?” asks Orsik.
“Two days.” 
“That’s plenty fo time for us to do what we need to do.
“Not if we want to bring all the children.” 
For a moment, Orsik thinks about it and then decides that he has to do the right thing and says, “We bring all of the children.”
Unla Kee raises an eyebrow. 
Along the way to Parnast, the group learns that one of the orc whelps is the son of the orc chieftain. The orc that guarded the whelps, thankful that her child wasn’t killed, tells them that Kurglow would be willing to abandon Bad Fruul to save his child. 
“He’s a terrible husband, but a good father.” 
Arriving in Parnast, Thistle suggests that they use Jarlor and Larrish to send a message to Kurglow through the spy channel. 
Jarlor isn’t interested until his wife explains, “if the orcs don’t attack we might survive so can you grow a spine and do what you need to do to protect your family?” 
He nods and listens as Orsik comes up with the worst threat he can – “if you attack, we send the whelp to bed without dessert and will not read a bed-time story!”
Jarlor stares at him, and wonders if he should correct the dwarf, but thinks, “never had kids and he’s the boss.” 
Thistle asks, plaintively, “Is there a Lionel Coster around?” 
Chandra looks at him queerly, and says, “my dear Thistle, this belonged to the Zhentarim who believed that power came from physical transport of goods. The transdimensional market that the Lionel Coster’s use is not available to folks here. Our poor local merchant Calder has been forced to use the Zhentarim physical transportation of goods.” 
Thistle sighs, and remembers what Uncle Joon had told him that the Lionel Coster was a devotee of KP Trade Equilibrium Theory. Apparently it| produced a more optimal general equilibrium because it considered all markets across all possible universes vs a single local physical market. Although optimal for consumers there had been violent resistance to them from merchants who preferred the profits of a more local equilibrium. The mathematics in the argument had involved a novel application of the Smalistin conjecture which asks if n-dimensional sphere’s basic symmetries are all the symmetries it has. But then again, for Thistle mathematics wasn’t his strong suit. 
The important point for Thistle, is that Calder didn’t have magic to sell. 
“Sorry about that!” the small halfling had told him. 
The next morning comes, and everyone in the militia arrives at their posts. All paths into the center of town where the well is are barricaded, windows have been boarded up, and pits have been dug to slow down the advance of any forces. 
The militia is terrified, the women and children are nervous, the refugees are worried, trapped in Parnast. 
As the sun begins to set, Orsik seeing how everyone is afraid steps forward and shouts – “my friends, they will not pass, they will not succeed, they will die! We will come through tonight alive, our town preserved!” 
The jingoistic speech is a rousing success and the militia cheer! 
As the moon begins to rise, Orsik and Thistle turn to Chandra, “our ace in the hole is wyvern.” 
She looks at them – “you do realize that the wyvern will kill everyone?”
“I thought you could control it?” 
“Um, no.” 
“Well I suppose someone could try to control it,” as Thistle tries to remember if he had learned how to control crazed hungry draconic creatures. Thuul whimpers.
Unla Kee scans through the book that SEER gave him and finds a relevant story about seven warriors who show up in a town and train the villagers to fight and together they all die in a glorious blood bath, their combined blood being the necessary part of some ritual that summons some extra-planar power. 
Unla Kee chooses to not share that, but instead shouts, “we must win!” 
Thistle from his elevated vantage point stares at the edge of the forest that he knows Bad Fruul must come.
And there he is. He is sitting on a mammoth, in bone-festooned plate armor, wearing a helm made from the skull of some unidentifiable beast. Thistle thinks, “I should know that.” Bad Fruul looks like a massive dark night, not like some hill giant. 

The giant dismounts, removes his helm, holds it under one arm and the eyes that gleam with intelligence scan the battlefield
“That’s the drow!” 
The giant shouts, “Little folk, give Fruul what he wants. The girl Chandra who hails from the folks of Evereska , the offspring of the horrid Luistarra and give us the vile statue of Agharradh. Comply with his demands and you will live. Failure to do so will mean the destruction  of this town after you have all been made slaves. ”
For a moment, the villagers ponder their choices, and then Ragnar and Gundalin shout, “NEVER! The Orsik’s Militia will never surrender one of our own! Go back before you die!” 
“Then death and destruction it is!” And at that the horde of golbins, orcs, ogres, and ettins begins howling and chanting. 
## Bad Fruul 
*They underestimate me! They think Bad Fruul is dumb! Bad Fruul will rule over all!*
He turns to a squad – “I want you to test their defenses. Go and kill them all!” 
Eight goblins, two wolfs and their goblin leaders march towards the town ready to attack the villagers. 
Bad Fruul watches as they attack along the main road – “they served their purpose.” 
The goblins fell into pits and died either on the pikes in the pits, or from the militia that attacked them from higher ground. 
The goblin bosses tried to rally their troops but were killed by the ever present archers of Orsik’s Militia. 
Bad Fruul shouts – “but I was told how badly their training went! My spies lied to me!”
The wolf tries to go around the pits and scale the wall. But the militia inspired by Orsik, and their successful defense stand their ground and hack it to bits. 
Bad Fruul thinks – “hmm… I must do something different, let me try this.” 
And now he sends ogres, half-ogres from Mama Mia and two cave bears. 
The ogres are able to walk around the pits full of dead goblins, and reach the walls. The half-ogres are again, killed by the Thistle Trained Archers. 
“You were saying, Vardis?” 
“Nothing.” 
“You were saying, Vardis?”
Although the ogres are able to navigate around the pits, the owl bears are not. 
Bad Fruul sees his ogres advance, and turns to his lieutenant, “Mama Mia’s ogres have breached the wall, send some more orgres with the owlbear.”
