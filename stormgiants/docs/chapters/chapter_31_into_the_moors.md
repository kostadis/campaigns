# Chapter 31 - Into the Moors
## 2nd day of the first 1st tenday of the month Flamerule
After a two-day journey, our heroes arrive at Calling Horns and head straight to the Inn. Calling Horns, as Thistle recounts, was nothing more than a trailside inn until a few years ago, when Tamalin Zoar bought the establishment and retired here. 
She attracted settlers to the region, giving rise to a small village whose citizens paid monthly fees for her protection. She’s still spry at 60 and is the law.
According to [Eltio](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/the-forgotten-realms-kostadis/a/eltio-person), she can provide the 3HPP a letter of mark that can be redeemed for the service they requested.
Broke, and desperate to get their sword, the heroes knock on the door of the Inn. Outside the Inn are a gruesome pile of bones from dead orcs who wandered too close and a large stable that houses several horses. 
Orsik turns to everyone – “she sounds like an amazing person.” 
They are let in and look around. The main room has several large tables and a kitchen in the back. The tavern is full of local villagers enjoying the food and company. 
As Tamalin enters the room, Orsik approaches her and declares – “I’m really interested in meeting you. Your business skills and vision lead to this investment. You created this thriving economy.” He pauses, “I’d like to learn from you.” 
Tamalin is surprised and flattered. She turns to the patrons at the main table and tells them to find other accommodations, shouts to a person in the kitchen to bring drinks and food, and begs the 3HPP to sit down so she can talk and share her experiences. 
It’s clear that as an adventurer at the end of her career, the idea of sharing her stories with others beginning their career is appealing. 
Thistle brings up the play in Triboar as an initial investment they made. Tamalin is impressed but mutters, “hits driven businesses are not very sustainable. But if you get a big hit, it’s worth more than a dragon’s hoard.” 
The conversation drags into the night, and eventually, Tamalin mutters about not getting older and says, “well, you brought me good company, and I have a soft spot for folks like you. Here have two rooms in the house for free.” 
Orsik and Vardis stay in one room, Unla Kee and Thistle in the other. As they all are about to get a good night’s sleep, Tamalin bangs at their doors, “trolls! I have a pair of trolls attacking my stables and several fine horses there.” 
Thistle flies out of the window and strikes one of the trolls outside the stables with his bow, directing Thuul to cover the troll in acid to stop its regeneration.
Unla Kee opens a window and then, from a distance, uses firebolts to kill the troll.
Orsik and Vardis grab their shields and weapons and, in their pajamas, rush out to face the 
The first troll dies outside of the stable. The second troll is killed inside the stables, but not before it slaughters two horses.
Seeing the 3HPP in action, Tamalin invites them to have a drink before they go back to sleep, and while they are drinking, she says, “I have a problem.”
“other than trolls?” asks Orsik.
“No, it’s trolls.” 
Orsik demures, “No, your problem is a lack of entertainment options for your patrons. You need a theater production.” 
She looks at him, thinks, and says, “I like your gumption, and I like your style, and I like all of that. But right now, I have got a more immediate problem.” She then turns to the group, “And you gotta know when to make the sales pitch. I think you are being too aggressive now.” 
Orsik is taken aback.
“You did ask for advice?”
“Not in front of everyone. I thought that was a 1x1 conversation.” 
“Oh, I wanted everybody else to benefit.” She looks confused, “oh, this was not a private lesson.” 
Orsik glares at her, “I don’t think this mentee thing is working out.” 
Tamalin realizes she made a  mistake and upsets the dwarf, so she turns to him and apologizes. Vardis is surprised to see his old friend accept the apology. 
Once everyone has settled down and drunk their first glass, Tamalin picks up the conversation again, “the trolls have started coming down into Calling Horns a lot more frequently of late. There are a lot of trolls in the Evermoors, or Trollmoors as we call them. It’s a region to the north that is a big swamp. I just need you to go there and find out what’s going on and why the Trolls are coming down. It would be great if you could solve my problem, but I just want to know what is happening. And if you can do that for me, I can provide you a Letter of Mark.” 
She pauses, “oh, you may not know this, but I am a member of the Zoar family.” She pauses, waiting for recognition. 
Thistle remembers something about the Zoar family and that a Zoar ran the city guard of Everlund. 
“Yes, my brother is the captain of the guard. If you go to Everlund and show Dral my note, he’ll get the captain to do whatever you need or want. Dral runs the tavern Danivarr’s House that the Zoar family owns. “ 
At this point, Unla Kee, who is no longer sober, reacts to what was just said. Now it turns out that Unla Kee had been alcohol-free for two weeks and that sobriety was starting to hurt his brain. The fresh alcohol soothed his nerves. 
He turns to Tamalin – “let me get this straight, we do this work for you, and we get a letter? We have better things to do.” 
Orsik pulls his hair out, “are you paying any attention? We get the letter, which gets Eltio to lead us to Harthal Zymorven, who gets us our sword, and when we drag him back to Zymorven Hall, a 1000 gold piece reward.” 
Unla Kee stares at Orsik and then says, “I guess we can do this.” 
As Orsik falls asleep, he thinks, “scoundrel. She’s a scoundrel. She went from thief and crook to scoundrel. I want that kind of arc.” 
## 3rd day of the first 1st tenday of the month Flamerule
The 3HPP head out into the Evermoors, looking for something to explain the trolls heading out of the moors to hunt for food. 
After a day of walking in mud, dealing with bugs, and sweating under the hot summer sun of Flamerule, a campsite is found near a stream. 
“Other than bugs,” Orsik grumbles, “we found nothing.” 
Thistle, on the other hand, is less convinced. There are signs, but he’s not sure what. He knows that all they have to do is go deeper into the marsh. 
“Got it,” he thinks. 

He points to Unla Kee and some people walking in the marsh. Thistle concentrates some more, “they are the same nomadic humans that attacked us when we left Triboar. Can you understand what they are saying?” 
Unla Kee casts comprehend languages, and he rushes off. Thistle stares, “damn it!” and follows him. 
And it’s a good thing. Unla Kee overhears them talk about Trolls moving out of their usual hunting grounds and encroaching on the nomad’s territory. Unfortunately, in trying to get into position, he didn’t pass with the grace of a ranger from the Feywild. 
At first, the warriors stare at him, and he stares at them and tries to be friendly. Except that one of the survivors of the last attack recognizes him as that annoying wizard and charges at Unla Kee and Thistle. 
Vardis, who saw Unla Kee’s noisy schlepping, notices the danger and hits the warriors near the shaman with a fireball lighting up the night sky. Soon the smell of charred flesh and burned trees fill the night air. 
Thistle strikes one with his bow. And then he strikes another.
Unla Kee looks at him, “wait, you can shoot two arrows that quickly?”
“Yep.”
“For how long?” 
“Ever since before we faced the hill giant in Parnast.”
“And you just did that now?”
“Yes.” 
“Why?” 
“Wanted the challenge.” 
“SERIOUSLY?”
The mage laughs and thinks, “I like this guy. I really like this guy.” 
He then pokes through the jungle to see where the remaining warriors are fleeing and finishes them with magic missiles. 
Orsik arrives on the scene ready to fight and sees nothing but dead bodies.
“But I didn’t get a chance to kill anyone!!!”
