# Chapter 06 - The tower
## Group 9th day of the 1st Tenday of Taraskh, Llorkh

Orsik wakes up and is curiously refreshed, “time to see what we can do here.”
Vardis groans, “seriously, can’t we just sleep more.”
The two leave their room, enter Nonnie’s rustic main dining hall, and sit with Thistle and Unla. 
“I would like to buy better armor,” Thistle declares between bites of food.
“I suppose we could sell our treasure at the local Lionel Coster,” mutters Orsik.
And it’s settled; that’s where they go.
Leo, as always, is spotless. Immaculately dressed, facial hair cut to perfection, his skin smooth as marble. They assumed Leo used magic of some kind, but as he would say, “this is all 100% natural, Leo.” 
Unla Kee stares, “to spend so much time on one’s appearance.” 
Orsik acts, “do you have a magnifying glass that could fit this?” 
Leo stares at it, “I could have such a thing in a few days. It will cost extra as it is not part of our standard inventory.”
Unla Kee snorts, “dd bay?” 
Leo is aghast, “we don’t work with THAT firm, sir! We have our inventory and exchange system that doesn’t have a grotesque surcharge!” 
Finally, he turns to Orsik, who is waiting to find out the fee, “well, 5 gold should cover the cost of shipping, etc.” 
Thistle is anxious, “I would like some studded leather armor.” 
The dwarf stares at him, “We got enough money, but why don’t we sell everything to Leo. Leo, we have some stuff you buying?” 
Leo looks at it, “yes, 10% discount.” 
With the group flush with cash, Leo stares at the floor and the dirt that the heroes dragged in. He looks at the door and then the doormat and notices that the doormat is spotless, and his floor is not. 
“My goodness, what a mess you dragged into my store.” Leo runs out and comes back with cleaning implements muttering, “what if an inspector came in?” 
With the store spotless, Leo notices that the group has some serious coin and begins to talk up his store, “I always carry a minimal inventory in a small town, such as this one. We do not have quite the range of products and services that Aurora Realms Stores have. They are famous for their magical inventory and boutiques of rare and exotic items. We try to offer lower price goods for the common sentient.”
Unla Kee reminds the group about rations, and Leo offers some. 
Finally, Thistle asks about magic. Leo ponders, “I do have a very rare item, which I think,” he pauses as he looks at their clothes and that they are counting coins, “out of your price range. But I think there might be something more in your price changes. There’s a potion of healing and a potion of climbing and a scroll with detect magic.” 
Unla Kee shrugs.
## The tower 9th day of the 1st Tenday of Taraskh, Llorkh
“Hmm, they have come again.”
It notices how the one called Vardis has used blessing to help his friends deflect the permanent antipathy field that surrounds the tower. 
“Surely, the mage and cleric will not continue without their warriors.” 
The cleric shouts, “Alright, Unla Kee, Let’s go check this place out. This is our shot.” The cleric then says, “this is what you get for swearing off that thing you used to do.”
The tower sighs, “oh, that thing.” 
The tower wonders if the mage and the cleric will continue or abandon their quest. It then turns to the scene in front of it. 
The cleric marches up to the dwarf and attempts to grapple the dwarf and bring him to the tower. The dwarf escapes and stars, cursing Vardis. At the same time, Unla Kee tries to grapple Thistle and fails. Thistle is more subdued in his reactions. 
The tower sighs, “I suppose I will have to see what can be done.” 
When the two adventures open the door, it says, “ah, this will do nicely.” 
## Unla Kee 9th day of the 1st Tenday of Taraskh, some interdimensional location 

Vardis turns to Unla Kee, “I’m game, let’s go do this.” 
Unla Kee, consumed with fear if he stops taking risks, agrees. 
The cleric observes, “huh, this isn’t the entrance to a tower. It’s a stairwell into a dungeon.” 
Unla Kee is increasingly unsure of the wisdom of exploring a dungeon without a fighter. 
A plaque is at the bottom of the stairs, “Acerak’s Construction Company.” 
Vardis stares and shouts, “It’s a generative dungeon!” 
The halfling stares, “what?”
“The dungeon generates every time you enter.”
“What?” 
Vardis shrugs at Unla Kee, who is very confused. 
Vardis turns to him and says, “lead on!” 
The mage is surprised but goes forward. The stairways lead to a T intersection, and from there, Unla Kee heads south. Then there is another intersection, and the halfling walks south again. Unla Kee follows the passageway until it ends in an archway that he enters. 
The place is very curious. It’s a large hexagonal room with a rope that ascends to a balcony. As he looks around, he notices a corroded key in the room’s southwest corner. 
“Should I pick up the key?” 
“Yes, mage. And be quiet while I prepare a ritual of detect magic.” 
A cranky bugbear arrives as the spell completes, and no magic is detected. 
Unla Kee stares at it. 
“What are you doing here?”
Vardis interjects before Unla Kee can say anything, “We’re not really sure.” 
“Did you go to some tower, open a door and enter?” 
“Yes, we did. Should we not have? I mean, I plead ignorance.” 
“The rules are you have the find a way to get out. It’s not clear what the way to get out is.” He stops and sniffs, “and there is not a lot of food growing over here. I smell rations.” 
“Really,” groans Vardis.
“Yes. Or I could eat the two of you.” 
As the bugbear munches down their food, Vardis again steps forward, “How do you get out of here anyway?” The bugbear ignores him. “Since we’re feeding you, we could have a friendly conversation.” 
The creature looks up, “It’s pretty easy. One group believes you have to find the secret passage out of here. There’s another group that believes there is no escape. And that all that happens is you eventually die of hunger and turn into a zombie.” Unla Kee looks pale but is relieved that he is with a  cleric. “And there’s a couple of those around here.”
The bugbear scratches its jaw, “And then there’s a third group that believes if you die, whatever traps you in here kicks you out as a sort of reminder to not mess around with a wizard’s tower.” 
To Unla Kee’s question of what he believes, he answers, “there are some people who go crazy and kill themselves and hope that the right thing happens. Some people give up and turn into zombies. I decided I’m going to tough it out and find the secret door.”
Vardis punches Unla Kee, and the halfling agrees, “Do you want to pair up with us? Because we could use the help. We could all look together.” The bugbear stares, “my mom always used to say that two walking meat bags is better than rations. I’ll come with you.” 
Now in a peculiar alliance, the bugbear draws the dungeon’s map. He explains that the dungeon has a tribe of goblins, a few ogre zombies, and himself. The goblins don’t believe that escape is possible and are just trying to stay alive. The zombies can be avoided if you move fast enough. To whether anyone has escaped, he smiles, “those goblins I killed escaped. They are no longer trapped in this infernal prison.” 
With the introductions completed, Vardis leads the group west. 
## Vardis 9th day of the 1st Tenday of Taraskh, some interdimensional location
The room is large. As his eyes scan, he notices a remarkably good wooden door to the north. A wall covered in tapestries of vile acts is right in front of him. Vardis thinks, “Nope, not what Orsik did.” Vardis’ eyes are drawn to the floor, where several candles are scattered on the floor along with spirals of white stone. 
Vardis walks up to the tapestry, looks behind it, and shouts, “the secret door!” He then turns smugly to the bugbear, “how long have you been looking for it?”
“A really long time.” 
“This is your lucky day! You found me, and I found the door!” The cleric opens the door, and an ice dart bounces off his armor. “Traps, there are traps.” 
The passageway behind the doorway leads to a door. Vardis realizing that the bugbear is the new Orsik, shouts, “Open the door!” 
It’s a mimic. 
The bugbear slashes at the creature, screaming, “you will not get in the way of my escape!” 
Vardis first rubs his silver circle and then casts a guiding bolt. A green flowing dart flies from his fingers and critically wounds the creature. “First the door, and now the bolt, I am on fire!” 
At this point, the bugbear notices that his morning star is stuck. He shouts, “that’s my morning star!” As the bugbear struggles, the mimic strikes him. 
Vardis turns to Unla Kee, “good thing we spellcasters are here, not the fighters.” Unla Kee agrees, but his cantrip is ineffective. 
Vardis thinks, “if our spells were effective, that is.” 
Vardis watches the grappled bugbear miss with its javelin. He is not much better with his last spell. Vardis thinks, “maybe I should have not been so quick to say I am on fire.” 
Unla Kee smiles, “sleep.” 
And the creature falls asleep. 
The bugbear smashes the creature with its morning star, and then Vardis kills it.
Inside the room, a pile of treasure lies in the center of the room, and a note says, “congratulations for making it this far!” 
The group picks up the treasure, goes through the northern door, and notices a staircase. Vardis, done with interdimensional prisons, says, “let’s get out of here.” 
A voice, quite unexpectedly, enters his head, “if you use the key, you forfeit the gold.” 
Unla Kee is confused, but Vardis is not. “The thing that runs this tower wants us to stick around. It’s tempting us with gold. Let’s get out of here.”
They shout, “come on, this is your chance.” 
But the bugbear is not swayed, “more treasure,” it growls and heads back into the dungeon. 
Vardis and Unla Kee are teleported in Llorkh, next to Orsik and Thistle. Vardis looks around and notices that it is late afternoon.
“To Aunt Nonnie’s?” 
On their way, they encounter Diego, the para-amour of Fiora. He is curious to know if they have entered the tower. Unla Kee manipulates fates and convinces him that not only did they escape the building, but it was essential that Diego and the Guard investigate the tower. 
As he leaves, Vardis explains the secret of the entrance. 
Aunt Nonnie is surprised to see them and asks if they plan to go back, but Vardis explains that this was a one-time thing. She looks at him, disappointed. 
His friend Orsik snickers and shouts, “are you talking smack about us?” Vardis is annoyed and sighs as he thinks, “at least it’s not the thing.” 
Aunt Nonnie is offended and increases the prices. Vardis glares at Orsik, who apologizes. 
## 1/2 Taraskh on the road to Anuaroch, some distance from Llorkh
Our heroes stumble on a group of cultists of the Dragon that are down on their luck, covered in dirt, with robes tattered, and are trying to be inconspicuous.
Thistle exacts a modest amount of revenge in killing several. He keeps one to ask questions. This cultist is obviously starving and desperate to stay alive. All of the arrogance of the Cult of the Dragon is gone. 
Thistle learns that his nemesis Adimond Kroskas abandoned the Cult just before the defeat at the Well of the Worlds. Adimond, apparently, left to work with a Giant. Thistle is surprised because the Giants loathe the Dragons and vice versa. Adimond always struck him as a true believer, but perhaps he was an opportunist. 
The cultist has no knowledge of where Thistle’s drake is, but it is in the desert if it is anywhere. 
Finally, to the question of what happened to his beloved, the cultist has terrible news. Apparently, the enslaved people either died from mistreatment or were eaten alive by a Dragon as a sacrifice to bring Tiamat into the world. The cultist tries to reassure Thistle that it would have been best if she were eaten alive because, as he puts it, “there can be no greater honor!”. 
Thistle disagrees and kills him.
