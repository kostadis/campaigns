# Chapter 09 - Ending a civil war
## Wallace 
“What the hell! Bugbears?” 
Wallace stares at the Bugbears on the cliffs firing javelins at the group below. There is no cover. The only way to get to the Bugbears is to climb the twenty or so feet to the top of the cliff. And while you are climbing, you can’t fight back, and it’s hard to defend yourself. 
Meanwhile, everyone cannot react to the ambush, and as he sees blood spurt, he wonders how he got here. 
![image]()

*Orsik had said that we had to hike to find treasure and glory. Apparently, some hobgoblins were going to investigate some magic-users tower in the Marsh of Chelimber. My dad thought it would be something Orsik and the gang would find valuable. I’m not sure he thought I would be going with them, but hey, I gotta make my own mark in the world. And Dad really understood how to survive. We are survivors. *
*As we walked out, the golden pseudo-dragon that belonged to Assam showed up. I think Vardis explained that the pseudo-dragon doesn’t belong to Assam. Assam belongs to the pseudo-dragon. This ancient creature, whose name is Hsing, said that he would like us to investigate the tower. *
*Dad was pleased. Dad figures that the defeat of the Cult of the Dragon and the chaos in the Zhentarim will encourage the Lord’s Alliance to expand and take control of the black road. He wants to make sure they do and wants them to claim this last outpost before Anauroch. I’m sure Dad will tell the Lord’s Alliance that Dad had already sent us on this quest! Or maybe, the Lord’s Alliance already told him to send us out, and Dad was pretending that he heard about it. And – really – how would he hear about it. *
*Orsik explained that this creature Hsing had been giving them jobs for a while, and since we had already planned to visit the tower, saying yes was very easy. For all this talk about how he’s going to be this kick-ass Zhentarim, he didn’t negotiate for any money. *
*
And so we hiked down to the gap between the Graypeak Mountains and the Forgotten Forest. Of course, Orsik never bothered to ask what I knew about the area. The opening is called Ambush Gap, and it’s a prized piece of real estate for gangs. The Zhentarim aren’t stupid. Taking the Black Road through Parnast is costly. And Ambush Gap would allow caravans to avoid paying the fees. The Zhentarim, ever since the War of the World, haven’t been able to reassert their control over the Black Road. So many caravans are trying other routes, and this is one so damn obvious. *
*But Orsik sees me as a young boy who knows nothing. You don’t survive in Parnast without knowing things, people, and what’s going on. *
*And I figured Orsik wasn’t a dumbass. But here we are, javelins from Bugbears, more or less where I knew they would hit us.*
Wallace watched as the party engaged the Bugbears and was happy that the Bugbears weren’t very lucky that day. And worse didn’t count on a flying ranger. 
Wallaces was underwhelmed with Orisk, who through his sword at the bugbears. 

But before long, the battle was over. The looting left them with very little money. 
*What the hell? These guys should have had more money. They are working for someone. I could talk, but Orsik looks like he’s going to get killed, and the rest look like they will also get killed. No need to speak; I just need to survive and find Orsik’s Zhentarim contact to offer the treasure’s location. *
## Feetor
Feetor, the mud mephit, hides in one of the many clumps of trees with hanging vines that stand together throughout the marsh. 
“Which side do I join? The half-ogres are powerful but have left. And the grabby water monster is powerful and will never leave. I just avoided getting killed in an ambush by halfsies today. I need a new plan.” 
Feetor hears a noise that breaks him from his reverie. “Wait, someone is fighting the grabby water monster. I need to go see it. It doesn’t sound like the Half-ogres.” 
He pauses a few seconds, “yep, definitely not them.” 
From his vantage point, he sees a water-filled channel that splits around a small muddy island strewn with a half-dozen dead hobgoblins with their weapons drawn, almost all sprawled halfway in the fetid water of the marsh. 
“Ah yes, the intruders. The Ogreshippers seem confident that the intruders will rid them of the grabby water monster.”
Soon he can hear screaming and shouting that blocks out the usual hum of bugs and insects. “Ah yes, it will be invisible and kill them all.” 
He realizes he needs to get closer as the bright light creates a haze that limits ground visibility to 100 feet. 
When he gets within visual range, he notices a weird glow surrounds the creature, making it visible to its enemies.
“They have the power to unhide the grabby water monster?”
The glowing outline of the monstrous creature allows Feetor to follow the battle carefully. No matter what the monster does, it cannot hide, and if it cannot hide, it can be defeated. 
“A new power! I must join this new power!” 
He steps out of his hiding place and sits on a vine-covered branch of a broad tree, clapping his flabby hands together eagerly and flinging stinky droplets of mud.
He thinks,  “I need an angle with these folks. I think the dumb swamp creature should do….” 
“You killed the evil grabby water monster that has kept me prisoner, all these years.”
“That’s good! You’re tough! The grabby water monster ain’t gonna stay dead—it reforms out of the marsh after a coupl’a day—butt here’s not many who can best it. Most’a we mud-folks serve whoever’s the toughest around; some reckon it’s the grabby water monster, and some think it’s Mama Manyknuckles, but my vote is that’s you lot. You’re best of all!”
He then pauses, “Oh, I forgot my manners in th’excitement! I’m Feetor.”
Feetor stands up, puffs out its chest, and gives an awkward pose. 
Feetor smiles, “it works!” 
At first, Feetor is worried he decided to work with some pretty unintelligent folks. The Dwarf asks him what killed the hobgoblins as if it wasn’t obvious. 
But then the Fairy asks the right questions, “where is the lair of the grabby water monster?” 
Sadly Feetor doesn’t know and suspects it doesn’t have one.
“Probably thinks the whole swamp is its lair. The monster is immortal and ancient; if destroyed, it always reforms in another part of the marsh within a few days. It mostly ignores us mud mephits that “serve” it.”
Feetor notices how the human whispers to the Dwarf, “there’s a reason we don’t announce that we are looting bodies before we loot them, Orsik.” 
## Orsik
Orsik picks through the bodies and finds a map, “Hey, here’s a map to the wizard’s tower.” 
As Orsik and Vardis stare at the map and compare it to that lost map of theirs, the mud mephit coughs. 
“You should ask me about Mama Manyknuckles. You have to defeat her, and then you are the King of the swamp, and I am your loyal follower!” 
Orsik thinks, “more followers.” 
“Can you leave the swamp? You’re bound to it?”
The creature nods its head. 
And then, unprompted, starts talking about Mama Manyknuckles. 
“She is the particularly ugly leader of a band of half-ogres. She doesn’t have nice smooth skin,” and he points to his big muddy belly, “like me. They live somewhere deeper in the marsh. I don’t know the way because I avoid her and her half-ogres as much as possible. They mock and attack me. They call me fat and muddy. Although I don’t understand why they think mud is an insult, I am appropriately sized.” 
Seeing Orsik unconvinced, he continues, “There is a big civil war between the mephits. Some support Mama Manyknucles, and some the grabby water monster. So you kill Mama Manyknuckles, and when you stay and keep killing the grabby water monster, or you leave. Then there is peace as everyone supports the grabby water monster.” 
Vardis asks, “where is the grabby water monster?” 
“It’s gone for a few days, so you are now the most powerful of all the creatures!” 
Vardis taps Orsik, “Three days as the King. Not so bad, eh?” 
Orsik smiles and shouts, “fall in.”
## Thistle

Thistle stares at the map and, thanks to the information that the mephit gives him, can quickly find Mama Manyknuckles’ hideout. 
As the group is walking, he stops. 
“Tsk, tsk.” 
Everyone stares at him.
“The map was wrong. Clearly, the island is this way. You can tell from the water flow and the bugbears. Anyone who followed this map was liable to get lost for hours.” 
Within three hours, they arrive at the very large kidney-shaped island. There are two different encampments, one to the North and the South. 
Feetor shouts, “there is where Mama Manyknuckles rules!”
Thistle watches as Orsik tries to probe Feetor’s suggestions on how to attack, but Feetor is very evasive. 
Finally, Orsik asks, “You being an expert on mud mephits,” 
“You tokenizing me? Not every mud mephit knows everything about every other mud mephit.” Feetor states indignantly. 
Thistle barely restrains a laugh.
“I was more trying to get the other Mud Mephits to align to us!” 
Thistle notices that Feetor has a big smile, “Yes! A token of your power! And I will say that all Mud Mephits must follow me as your number one Mud Mephit!” 
Before Thistle can intervene, Orsik and Vardis confer and Feetor a gold coin. And the mud mephit disappears into the swamp, which burbles and bubbles for a few minutes. 
“They agreed to wait and see who lives and who dies!” 
The group decides to wait until the sun sets before they attack. 
Thistle, who lost his drake to the water weird, conjures him back. 
While they wait for night to fall, Thistle decides to fly up and scout around. As they are near the island’s southern end, he first scouts the southern camp. The south end of the island contains four large, crudely built huts. One of the huts is sturdier than the others and appears to be cobbled together from overlapping pieces of rafts and flat-bottom swamp boats. A fire pit stands at the northwest side of the camp with a heap of logs next to it. Around the fire pit are a pair of ogres. 
Two of the half-ogres in the southern camp notice him as he is flying. The creatures start cursing, “damn bug!” Thistle realizes that they have him confused for some kind of swamp creature, zigs and zags, and doesn’t engage with them after they throw rocks and dirt in his general direction. 
The half-ogres laugh and then go back to doing whatever they are doing. 
Thistle continues to the middle of the island, where between the Razorvine lies a sinkhole. A wide stone foundation frames this sinkhole with a roughly square shape. The edges of the foundation are badly weathered but appear crenelated, like the walls of a battlement, making the stone frame seem less like a foundation and more like the top of a large keep or tower. 
“Found it,” he thinks. He knows this is the wizard tower. Looking a little bit closer, he notices that the floor of this sinkhole is ancient but well-laid flagstone covered with a thin sheen of swamp water. The northwest corner of the sinkhole contains a badly weathered statue. 
As he investigates further, Thistle notices something unexpected, “A large table with filthy furs and a bed?” Someone must live here, he muses. Then he sees two wide ramps made of logs and dirt that lead into the sinkhole. 
“Someone lives here,” he thinks.
