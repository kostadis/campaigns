# Chapter 12 - Securing the books, and freeing Elquen 
## Orsik 3rd day of the 3rd Tenday of Tarskh
*I want to kill that Pseudo-dragon. *
Wallace can see the look in his eyes and turns to him, “he’s a member of the Lord’s Alliance and works for the SEER.” 
“So?”
“Well, the SEER can teleport this creature within 5 feet of us. I suppose it can teleport something more dangerous?” 
“Yeah, but think about how the Zhentarim might reward us.”
“Orsik, I’ve lived in Parnast my whole life, and I know a thing or two about the Zhentarim. They don’t like wars. They don’t like freelancers. And they are just as likely to hand you over to the Lord’s Alliance as a reward. Heck, they might offer to torture us on behalf of the Lord’s Alliance in exchange for something the Zhentarim want and then kill us.” 
Orsik grumbles.
“I mean, why not ask him for something? Like about the map?”
“Be in his debt? Orsik is no creature’s debt!” 
Vardis rolls his eyes. 

Unla Kee
Unla Kee coughs. 
Orsik stares at him.
“If you are all done debating your plans, can we deal with the magmins in front of us?” 
The Wizard is about to cast a magic missile when Orsik shouts, “a magic missile for these puny creatures?” 
The dwarf watched in stunned amusement as Unla ponders the situation for a moment and then swings wildly with his shortsword. The magmin retaliates and knocks the gnome out. 
Orsik stares and sighs, “I guess maybe the magic missile would have been the better choice.” 
But Unla can manipulate the fates, and the killing blow is not a killing blow. 
The battle rages for a few more moments, and then the puny creatures die. 
Unla muses, “probably worked better when the wizard could show up and kill the trapped thieves.” 

Just then, Thistle notices a secret door. Everyone struggles to open it, and when they are about to give up hope, he casually walks over to a stone and presses it. The door swings open. 
## Vardis

“I’ll go in!” 
He marches into what was obviously a treasure room. The words “The Wizard of the Crag’s Treasure Room” are inscribed on the back wall quite ostentatiously. 
Vardis stares and sees nothing of value.
There is a ledger, and he picks it up. 
Turns out that the Wizard of the Crag made real estate investments that didn’t quite pan out, construction projects that had overruns, etc. 
He also discovers that there was apparently some duel with Taskor that was supposed to help with the debts. 

## The Wight 
Oh dear me, more people are arriving. And they will want powerful magical artifacts that I will have to deny. And then more people will die. 
The wight begins to reminisce. 
“Elquen, The Wizard of the Crag is dead!” shouted the guard.
“How?” 
“Taskor!” 
“What?”
“Taskor killed him!” 
“What?” 
His manservant rolled his eyes, “Elquen, it’s not that complicated. The Wizard of the Crag died in a duel with Taskor and no longer has the money to maintain the castle.” 
“The water elementals!” 
“What?” 
“If I don’t control them, they’ll destroy everything.” 
And then he remembers the wild magic surge that turned him into a wight and trapped the two guards and his manservants. 

He tried to help them, and they turned into Skeletons.
Returning from his reverie, he mumbles. “never again.”
*And of course, they want the books. *
“I am afraid the disastrous effects of magic mean there will be no removal of books from here, and more importantly, there will be no use of magic. It is my curse and burden to protect the world from this.” He waves his hand at the library, “magic.”
## Unla Kee 
“Orsik, are you sure?” 
“Yes, I am.” 
Unla Kee, who had not yet entered the library, makes his entrance, and Orsik declares, “But what if the Wizard of the Crag lives?” 
Elquen stares at Unla for a moment, and Unla manipulates the fates 
“Your arrogance,” he shouts.
“Yes?” 
“It’s so good to see you are alive!”
“Can I have my library?”
“Why yes!” 
Elquen then stares at the gnome, “there is the small matter of back pay.” 
“How much?”
“You never could hold onto money, so how about you give me 150 GP for walking-around money?”
Unla stares at his allies and nods, “here you go!” 
“And best of luck, sir!” And with that, Elquen walks out through the hallways to the laboratory. 
Vardis opines, “well, that’s interesting. We released a wight into the world.” 
“Probably won’t hurt anyone,” opines Orsik.
“Yep!” 
Group
The group collects all of the books from the shelves. Realizing that they don’t have enough space to carry them, they use one of the bookshelves as a sled and pile them on it. 
Once on the surface of the island, the group turns to Thistle.
*
My mad mapping skills are fantastic! *He thinks. 
Thistle successfully leads the group to the same point on the map where they found the dead hobgoblin within a few hundred feet of the end of the Marsh of Chelimber.
“We’re done!” 
Except they weren’t. Turns out that the Water Weird rematerialized, and this time it had a bunch of Mud Mephits as allies. 
The battle was difficult, but Vardis’ quick thinking to call in the group’s mephit allies turned the tide. The Mud Mephits once again began their civil war and fought each other to stand still. The only issue was that the heroes had to avoid the muddy explosion associated with a mud mephit dying for a few moments. 
All that remained was to head back to Parnast and claim their prize.
