# Chapter 46 - Find the relic, save the world
## 6th day of the 1st tenday of Eleasis
After securing the Shard of the Ise rune, the 3HPP head back into the Temple of Annam. 
### Thistle
“Excellent work, folks. I want to apologize for not really being present while you explored the warrens and the rooms.” 
They all turn to him, confused.
“I was spending time in my dreams with my dearest.” 
Harshnag groans, and Thistle chooses to ignore it.
“But now that I am fully here, we must be far more systematic in our exploration. For example, I noticed we didn’t explore the secret doors.”
He points to two secret doors on the west wall, one to the North and another to the south.
Thistle flies over and squeezes through the one to the North. 
Inside, he finds a featureless room except for a lever 12’ high off the ground. He tries to push it but realizes he can’t. 
“Harshnag?”
The Frost Giant looks at the lever, “If you could melt this ice, it would be easier.” 
Unla Kee starts to explore esoteric options, including Fireball and burning hands.
Thistle sighs and pulls out a torch, and lights it.
Vardis praises him, “Bless our ranger.” 
Harshang takes the torch, lets the ice melt, and then lifts the lever. 
A loud grinding sound is heard, and Thistle flies off to see what happened. 
“Yes, this opens the Portcullis!”
Then Thistle flies to the Southern door, and just as he is about to enter, Orsik notices a crack in the temple’s walls. 
“My goodness! Look at this crack! You can tell a lot from a break in a building. The stonemasonry is impressive!” Thistle then waits as he goes onto a monologue about stonework. He then proceeds to discuss the finer details of masonry, rock formations, cracks, and the use of magic, which he disapproves of because anyone can use magic, and it really dilutes the artisanal work, and that, as far as he is concerned magical stonework isn’t stonework. Orsik\] then concedes that a magical device can shape the stone. He concludes that using magic to make stonemasons more productive is fine, but using magic to shape stones is cheating and really about putting stonemasons out of a job.
The room to the south, like the room to the North, has a single lever that Harshnag once again raises. And once again, a portcullis opens. 
Thistle turns to everyone, “Well, it would appear we have to go through the southern doors.” 
### Unla Kee

The mage enters the door and sees a vast, brightly lit room. It is 240 feet wide by 440 feet deep, with a 200-foot-wide alcove in the center of the east wall. Six pillars, each 20 feet in diameter and 60 feet tall, support the vaulted ceiling. The walls are lined with niches and sconces identical to the ones in the temple. 
The room is warm, dry, and brightly lit. The source of the light and the heat is a raging fire within a bowl-shaped basin in the center of the chamber. 
Unla Kee marches over to this fascinating source of light. “40,000 years this has been lit.” 
Thistle thwarts Unla Kee’s attempt to conduct reckless experiments by rushing ahead and declaring, “How surprising. In the fey, we trap elementals or create a fire plane portal. But this fire is boringly natural. The fire has been kept alive by natural gas escaping through holes in the basin. It feeds the fire, sustaining it indefinitely.” 
Vardis nods. 
Unla Kee catches up to Thistle and takes in the tables around the fire pit. 
Arranged about the fire pit are six tables carved out of granite, matching benches, and two 9-foot-tall stone slabs stained with blood and grease. Atop the southernmost slab are neat stacks of plates and goblets made of beaten copper, all green and black with age. The piles of dishes and goblets on the northernmost slab have been knocked over, creating a mess.
And just then, out of the ground, a centipede-like creature erupts. Unla Kee gapes at what he sees. It’s a huge monstrosity whose body is blue, and extending out from the body are what appear to be hundreds of legs. But that is not what is terrifying. The head has winglike fans, and the mouth has thousands of sharp teeth. He also feels a kind of warmth emanating from the creature.
Harshnag bellows – “A remorhaz! I will take the ax of Thrym while the rest of you hold off the creature. Then we will run away!”  
### Orsik
“No, we will fight!” 
And just as he says that, the creature swallows Thuul whole. 
Thistle gasps. 
Vardis shouts, “Frosty doesn’t understand the power of Unla Kee!” 
Orsik nods to Thistle’s tactical acumen to fly above a table to give more space between him and any creature from beneath and is pleased that the arrows hit. 
“One arrow,” he mutters.
Orsik advances and hits the creature with a swipe from the left, then a slash to the right, then he pokes it with the point of the blade and slashes again. 

The creature is wounded. And Orsik can feel the heat of its body burn him. 
And just then, he feels a surge of psychic energy flow past him and hit the creature. He knows that spell; it’s Raulothim’s Psychic Lance. 
The creature is stunned in place. 
And then, a boulder lands on the ground missing the creature. 
Orsik mutters, “Right, now that you see we can fight it, you want to help, but not up close and personal, from afar.” 
And just then, Harshnag surprises Orsik when he charges at the monster. 
Vardis runs up to Orsik to heal him, but Orsik waves him off, so the cleric of the First of the Serdaline casts a sacred flame and summons a spiritual weapon in the shape of a long sword resembling Sahandrian. 
And the creature is now incapacitated because Unla Kee is a target for Thistle’s bow. One of the arrows hits, and the other misses.  
Orsik attacks again, braving the flames, and strikes twice. He then turns to Unla Kee and shouts, “Again!” 
“I can’t, but maybe this will work.” 
And he waves his hand, and little bits of ice stream from his hand and trap the creature in binding ice. 
Unla Kee smiles, and Orisk understands that the diviner has twisted the fates to get the desired outcome. 
“Attack my friends, you, a horror of the ice!” Orsik is delighted with the massive attacks of the giant on the immobile creature, seeing its blood splatter and its carapace crack. 
And then Vardis, his friend, finishes it with a swipe of his summoned sword. 
Harshnag shouts, “I knew you could do it.”
Orsik is about to retort when his friend responds, “Didn’t you just say we couldn’t?”
“That was me encouraging you!” 
“Ret-con.”
Harshnag stares at him. 
Orsik is equally confused and remembers how Vardis went through his temporal phase, convinced that time wasn’t linear and that it was possible to retroactively change facts that created a new reality, a process he said was ret-conning.
With the creature dead, and the sword in hand, Orsik and the 3HPP head back from the feast hall.
Orsik turns to Harshnag, “Alright, the safe thing for us to do is for you to go with the Ax of Thrym, and hit the rune while we wait here.”
Harshnag nods. 
Orsik stares aghast at Unla Kee, who is running after the Frost Giant. 
“Are you insane?” 
### Unla Kee
“To take no risks when you know that you will be there at the very end and if you aren’t, the universe will end is personally liberating.”
Beyond the thundering archway lies a hexagonal chamber whose walls rise 50 feet before tapering to a 90-foot-high apex. Standing in each corner of the room is a life-size statue of a giant holding up a heavy iron lantern with one raised arm. Each type of giant is represented: hill, stone, frost, fire, cloud, and storm. A cold, magical light radiates from each lantern, illuminating a giant corpse lying on the floor beneath a shroud of frost in the middle of the room. The corpse looks like it has been here for some time, preserved by the cold. Next to the body rests a giant, frost-covered Morningstar.
Curiosity piqued, Unla Kee advances on the corpse. The giant is wearing a gold breastplate. Underneath his body is a circle of runes. 

A ghost in the form of a cloud giant rises from the ground and bellows – “Why have you come here!” 
Unla Kee just sits there staring, incomprehensibly, because he doesn’t speak giant. 
Harshnag walks up to him, brings his mouth to Unla Kee’s ear, and whispers loudly – “Ask him how to use the oracle, ask him about the Ordning.” 
The ghost understands the question and says, “You are lucky.” 
Unla Kee nods, “Yes, I am!” 
The ghost stares at him, confused. 
“This is a true Oracle. You can ask six questions every day. Anyone who asks questions in the Oracle will receive a true answer.” 
The 3HPP immediately started proposing questions related to matters that are top of mine for them, like the treasure map’s location and a missing girlfriend. 
And just before they enter the circle, the ghost speaks again.
“I have a quest for you. The divine oracle told my father and me that a great upheaval would upset the balance of power in the world, allowing all giants to win the respect of their gods and bring glory to their race. The oracle told Blagothkus, my father, outright that he could never impress the gods enough to earn their favor, then urged me to step out from beneath my father’s “dark shadow.” My father was overcome with despair and envy. A terrible fight between us ensued, in which my father slew me. He has since retired to his castle to mourn.”
Unla Kee is delighted and worried, “so what do you want?” 
“For you to kill him!” 
“One giant cloud castle,” he thinks. 
“And where is he?” 
“Parnast. I don’t know if it is a real place.” 
Orsik laughs, “We know about that place.” 
Unla Kee then remembers the sight of the cloud giant’s castle that blew past them so many months ago, almost killing them. 
“Payback and a castle.” 
 Before this can continue much further, Thistle marches into the circle and asks, “Where is Laela!” 
One of the lights on the giants burns out, and the Oracle answers, “Duke Zalto has her.” 
Orsik jumps into the circle and asks, “What is this a map of?”. He shows his old family heirloom. 
Another light burns out, and the Oracle says, “Beyond the Silvery Marches, near the Ice Spires in the iron tower.” 
### Harshnag
Harshnag is aghast, “Now that you asked your trivial questions, perhaps we can ask how to stop the giants, or maybe you are comfortable with this war?” 
He stares at them and realizes that they just might be, and changes tack, “Look, there is a very powerful magic user who has chosen to help you and might decide that you were not deserving of his support and may decide that he should get rid of you…” 
Thistle looks at him, “Just confirming the accuracy of the Oracle.” 
Harshnag remembers that a sign was given and then counts slowly to 10. 
Unla Kee rushes in; Harshnag is horrified but relieved when the halfling asks, “What must be done to stop the giants?” 
“Find a magic conch of the storm giant king, Hekaton. Use it to visit Hekaton’s court. Root out the evil therein.”
Unla Kee walks out of the circle, turns to Harshnag, and says, “Yep, checks out, this is an authentic oracle.” 
Harshnag glares. 
Vardis asks Harhnag, “Do you know anything about Hekaton?” 
“Yes, but what is this evil?”
Harshnag realizes he’s in the circle, and the Oracle blows out another light and says, “The storm giant court is in the chaos following the disappearance of King Hekaton.” 
Harshnag quickly steps out of the circle. Vardis mutters, “Thanks.” 
“Where is the conch?” shouts Vardis, and turns to Harshnag, “most direct question.” 
“In the clutches of a giant lord.”
At this point, Orsik turns to Thistle, “and we know about Duke Zalto! Let’s ask about him!” 
Harshnag nods. 
And the Oracle sighs, and the last light goes out. And it shouts - “First, you must prove yourself! When human warriors came to these lands, they fought our kind and stole our relics, burying them in the ground. The humans built altars to Uthgar, their god-king, atop these relics and surrounded their altars with burial mounds. Go to these mounds, retrieve one or more of our lost relics, and bring them here as tribute. Do this, and your path will be made clear. There are many paths you can take. The more relics you deliver, the more paths you will have to choose from. Several evil giant lords stand ready to oppose you. You can ruin all of their plans by defeating just one of them. Which one that is remains to be seen.”
“What are we supposed to do now?” asks Thistle. 
Harshnag sighs, “I know what needs to be done.” He walks outside the Eye of Annam and sits on the main temple’s floor. 
“I know the names and locations of all nine spirit mounds.” 

He spends the next hour drawing a crude map that shows where the spirit mounds are located relative to key landmarks and terrain features. 
“I do not know what relics are buried at each mound or which giant lord is associated with each relic.”
Thistle then tells him, “We find a relic and come back, and the Oracle will tell us how to find a giant lord. But we don’t know which relic is where.” 
“That is correct.”
