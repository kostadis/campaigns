# Chapter 13 - Back to Parnast - 03-04-Taraksh

Did I mention how annoying it was to keep track of them? Seriously. All the agents in the area are tasked to track this group of annoying individuals. 
I know you will say they are the most promising group we have seen in these parts. And that the Parnast must not fall to the Zhentarim. And that there is a tumult brewing in the North. 
And everyone must do their part, and my part is to ensure Parnast is part of the Lord’s Alliance and that Parnast does not fall to the Giants. The strategic location as the last town before the Black Road is too important.

## The Free Agents and the Zhentarim Agent
Meanwhile, just past the Marsh of Chelimber, a group of adventurers carrying a full bookcase is trying to decide how best to return to their destination. 
“Wallace, what do you think?” shouts Orsik.
Wallace is flustered. He wasn’t expecting Orsik to seek his counsel. 
“Well, we have three ways we go to Parnast. We could go through the Grey Peak Mountains. We could go through the Forbidden Forest, or we can go through Ambush Gap.”
“That’s the choices you give us? Scale the tall mountains covered in snow, dare the dangerous forest, or go through a place known as Ambush Gap.” 
Everyone stares at Wallace, and he shouts, “Ambush Gap.” 
The journey to Ambush Gap is mostly uneventful except in the evening when some bugbears and goblins appear looking well-fed, armed, and ready to fight. 
The lead bugbear shouts, “This is Bad Fruul’s territory, and you must pay a fee to continue unmolested.” 
The second in command turns to the first, “I don’t think Bad Fruul said – unmolested.” 
“We are not illiterates.” 
Orsik smirks, “how much?”
“Half of what you have!”
Orsik tries to bluff the Bugbear – “Very well, we have 10 gold. We can give you five.” 
The bluff is not that well done, but Unla manipulates the fates, and the gang leader sighs and is about to say yes.
At that point, the goblins are outraged and start screaming at the Bugbear. The Bugbears raise their voices in return, but after a while, they seem defeated.
“The group decided that wasn’t enough. We noticed you have fancy armor. We want some of it.” 
“But we had a deal,” exclaims Thistle. 
“Yeah, and we thought you had more than 10 gold.”
Unla Kee jumps in, “let’s be honest, you know how little gold we had.” 
The two Bugbears argue, pointing at the Goblins and then back to the North. 
Finally, the second in command says, “we can’t show up with 5 gold. Can you throw us a bone, like a sword?” 
Unla Kee whispers to Orsik, “try to convince them the marked cards are worth a lot!” 
But Orsik’s smooth tongue fails him, and the Bugbears become angry. 
“All right, all right. We were just joking about the cards.”
The group huddles, and soon Unla Kee hands over the short sword he used on the Magmin and Orsik hands over the deck of cards. 
The Bugbear shouts, “two swords! One for the deal and another for tricking us!” 
Just then, the goblins ask for food, and Thistle offers them goodberries. The feeling of fullness makes the Goblins much less interested in this ambush. 
With the Goblins less interested, the Chief declares, “A sword, a deck of cards makes for a worthy haul. And you have paid appropriate tribute to Bad Fuul!” 
“Where is this Bad Fuul? We’d like to meet him!” 
Thistle jumps in, “I want to commend you to your boss on how well the way to the pass was guarded!” 
The Bugbear scratches its chin. “Look, we don’t actually meet Bad Fuul. Our Chief meets some other chief who then meets Bad Fuul.” 
## Ambush Gap 
Ambush Gap is a particular part of the badlands at the foothill of the Greypeak Mountains and to the North of the Forgotten Forest. From hundreds of feet above, the surface looks like that ground has been tiled. The areas between the tiles are 5-10’ wide winding trails formed from the rain event that is said to have created the Marsh of Chelimber. The trails criss-cross each other and have tall 20’ stone walls on either side. 
The tiles’ surfaces vary in shape and size, offering plenty of hiding places on them behind trees, bushes, and crannies. And on one of those tiles is Grik, the Goblin chief. 
The Goblin chief stares at his gang. They look hungry, but not too hungry. And they definitely look like they are ready to kill some foolish travelers. 
“Bad Fuul has given specific orders; we are to ambush anyone who comes through this pass! Bad Fuul has promised that anyone who comes up the pass will be weakened by releasing poison!” 
The goblins cheer at this announcement and then get back to waiting. 
Chief Grik can hear the adventurers coming up the pass, “they are coughing and wheezing!”
As they arrive, he shouts, “This pass is under the control of Bad Fuul!” 
A fey indignantly shouts, “we already paid to pass through here!” 
Chief Grik looks at him and thinks, “ugh, a fairy.” Then assuming a commanding position, shouts, “you will hand over all of your equipment and all of your treasure and leave naked, or your bodies will be stripped naked and left here to be eaten!” He takes a dramatic pause and shouts, “thus spake Bad Fuul!” 
The fairy turns to the dwarf, “definitely not the deal we got from the other goblins.” 
“So!”
Group. 
Thistle turns to Orsik and says, “I’m gonna get me some goblins.”
Three goblins standing on a tile look down into the trail and target Wallace. One hits, and the other accuses Wallace of being short. 
“Damn, they have good aim,” thinks the fighter. Is the target shout, “die little creature!” and miss. 
Wallace stares back and wonders, “who are you calling little?”

Wallace then charges up to the sheer cliffs and kills a goblin. 
 Thistle’s drake charges up the cliff where Chief Grik is standing and snaps the air. Thistle’s arrow also misses everything. A goblin standing before Chief Grik strikes the drake, but his sword does not penetrate the armor. 
Just then, some wolves charge. They were hiding a little bit further down one of the trails. They snap at the cleric and miss but penetrate Orsik’s armor. 
Vardis ignores them and climbs up to one of the tiles and uses burning hands, killing two of Bad Fuul’s gang, and badly wounding the other, who then flees. 
Unla Kee surveys the situation, compares the position of the sun to a piece of the updated prophecy that he was given, concludes that the end of the world isn’t happening yet, and unleashes binding ice, killing two wolves, binding one wolf, and the Chief and his henchman on the tile. Unfortunately, it also hits Thistle’s drake. 
Orsik seeing the frozen wolf, kills it and then charges up the walls to the tile top to attack the Chief. 
The two goblins on the tile that Wallace climbed up to flee. The first immediately, the second after seeing his scimitar’s ineffectiveness. 
Thistle and the Drake attack the Chief, but the chief grabs the last remaining goblin alive next to him and interposes the poor creature between the arrow and the acid damage. The goblin then attacks the drake and knicks it. 
Vardis uses his bow and kills a goblin who is on the surface of another tile. 
Chief Grik wisely avoids Unla Kee’s spells, only to be killed by Orsik. 
With all the goblins dead, the group searches for treasure. On chief Grik, they find a note and a gold stylus. They almost ignore the letter but read it and discover its orders from Bad Fruul – “Defend the pass with your lives! We will seize Parnast!” 
## Thistle 04-04 Taraskh 

“That’s odd, stones in a circle. That can’t be what I think it is.” 
Thistle begins to advance quietly. He advises Orsik, Vardis, and Wallace to hang back and then sighs when he sees Unla Kee follow him. 
The gnome whispers, “If I don’t go to danger, the world might end. And I am feeling lucky today.” 
“And it’s a good thing too,” thinks Thistle because the gnomish wizard finds an inscription in fay that he missed. 
Thistle stares and now understands what he is seeing. It’s a gathering place for the Fey. It’s a place where they would drink, eat and revel. And it was a portal that could be used to move between the Feywild and this place. But its magic has disappeared for some reason. 
Thistle glares, “this is how Adimond Kroskas brought so many troops into the Feywild.” He mutters, “Uncle Yorn used to spin tales of how the North was a playground for the Fey, and these portals were plentiful, and they were how we came in and out of our place.” 
He reflects some more, is pleased to see signs of his home plane, and sad to see the decayed remnant of a gentler, happier age. 
He flitters back to the group, and they all head on to Parnast. The two-day journey to Parnast is uneventful. 

## Orsik 06-04-Taraskh
Orsik heads to the Golden Tankard with the books in tow for a drink. But before he can get one, Hsing, the blind golden pseudo-dragon, appears. Orsik is furious. 
“Hello,” it says. 
“You again!” 
“The books. Ah yes, I can feel the magical power going through me as I read them!” 
“You might want to discuss the books with the Wizard of the Crag.” 
“The wizard is dead,” he says frantically. 
“Nope!” 
“But he died in the duel. Wait, did he become a Lich? Are we dealing with a Lich? I have to notify the SEER immediately.” 
He blinks out and then blinks back in. 
“Very well, I didn’t understand that you meant that Unla Kee was the new Wizard of the Crag.” He then whistles, and a covered wagon rolls up, and two members of the Lord’s Alliance start moving the book into the covered wagon. 
Orsik looks at them and glares. These are the kind of men who kicked him out of every guard job he ever had. Men dressed in too much armor that had not seen a blade in anger.
One of the men stands before him, “this is Lord’s Alliance official business.” 

Orsik clenches and unclenches his hand and is about to start a fight. 
But Wallace gets in front of him and says, “Hey, look, Parnast needs protection, and my dad has secured help from the Lord’s Alliance. I want to get out of here, and I don’t want to get married to Elspeth, but this is my hometown, so can we just avoid creating an incident?”
The enraged dwarf stares at Wallace and then stands down. 
He then watches as Thistle asks about the armor they found that clearly belonged to the Lord’s Alliance. The two armed men stare at it dismissively and offer no information.
He then shouts, “so is this an officer’s armor? Or are you all equals?”  
Again Wallace stops Orsik from going over the top by saying, “what part of the Lord’s alliance do you not understand? They are all rich bastards who wear blingy armor.” 
Orsik laughs, “oh, you are all equal!” 
At that point, Orsik notices how Wallace grabs Thistle from the scuff and hisses, “can you please stop waving that armor around? This armor might belong to some second cousin’s sister’s husband of these Lordies, and we can either be forced to turn it over or be accused of crimes. Or what if my dad sees it and demands we hand it over so he can make an offering to the Lordies?” 
Orsik is pleased. Wallace has begun to come out of his shell but can’t hesitate to troll him, “what if Elspeth finds out?” 
And Wallace takes the bait, “Elspeth? Shit. Why did we come here?”
“To get our reward!” 
And with that, Hsing hands over 150 gold pieces for their trouble. Orsik shouts, “I deserve more.” And Hsing sneers, “I have been making deals with adventurers for a long time, and you are not the kind to be able to convince me to give out more than the SEER commands.” The creature then promptly teleports away. 
Orsik’s ax swings in the air, and he shouts, “I intentionally missed!” 

## The next two weeks 
The local militia was so pleased with Orsik’s training and actions that they asked if he would join their guard and train them some more. They offer free room, board, and two beers daily as he is a dwarf. Orsik is delighted as this is the first time any guard has asked him to join. He almost cries when they announce that they want to be known as “Orsik’s Guards.” He turns red and says, “we’ll talk about it later.” 
While he is training the guards, a man in a dark cloak appears, “I spoke to Zuul Clovis, and he said that you wanted to join us and you had the kind of moral flexibility that we look for.” 
Orsik glares at him – “Who are you?”
“I am a Zhentarim!” 
“Prove it!”
The agent flashes his badge. Orsik handles it and says, “these can be faked, but let’s go on.”
“Your paranoia commends you, Orsik.”

“Yes, you would be. Oblivillish is our agent in Prince Thornacious’ court. Find him.” 
A day later, Orsik shares this information with his fellow adventurers. 
Thistle is surprised to hear about this agent in Prince Thornacious’ court. The Prince is in exile, forced. And Thistle’s good friend Blassios, the Satyr, is part of his court. But the idea that a Zhentarim agent would spy on a royal member of the Fey, even if a disgraced one, troubles him. He then reflects on the nature of Fey courts, where each Prince and lordling spy on each other, all in an attempt to gain favor with Titania by disgracing their rivals. He sighs. Prince Thornacious might even consider it a sign of prestige if the Zhentarim wants to spy on him.  

Unla Kee buys a new sword to replace the one he lost from Calder the Halfling. Calder’s runs a shop in Parnast that is unaffiliated with Lionel Coster. The halfling mutters as he sells the sword, “the Lionel Coster are not welcome in Parnast. They use their dimensional portals to move goods quickly and safely and put the little guy out of business.” 

He then spends the next week gambling at the Golden Tankard with travelers who come through the town. He’s had chiefly bad luck losing half of his initial stake but is curiously pleased. Every person that comes through has fun, as does he, except one day when a traveler accuses him of cheating. 
“But I lost!” observes Unla.
“You should have lost everything!” 
Unla is about to attack but wonders about this traveler. He could be a merchant, or he could be a member of the Zhentarim.
Ragnar intervenes, and the traveler leaves. He stares at Unla Kee, trying to commit Unla’s face to memory. 
Thistle, meanwhile, has taken to challenging travelers to an archery contest. In the first week, he wins more than he loses. He also acquires a bit of a fan club of the local children who have never seen a fairy and are delighted to see someone their size winning at what they call “bowery.” The following week, inspired by his fans, he wins against all comers. 
The children are so convinced of his prowess that they accost any traveler with a bow and shout, “I know someone who can beat you in a bowery contest!”
