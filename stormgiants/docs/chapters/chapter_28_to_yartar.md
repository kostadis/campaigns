## 3rd day of the 2nd tenday of Kythorn 
### Thistle 

“Well, Uncle Joon had visited this place,” he remarks. “At the intersection of the River Surbin, the Dessarin River, and the Evermoor Way, Yartar serves as a fortified crossroads waypoint for caravans and merchants. Yartar is also renowned for its expertise in river barge construction, their fine craftsmanship seen from Silverymoon to Waterdeep and along riverbanks between the two.”
Orsik smiles, “I knew it! We can take a river.” 
Thistle glares, waits for Orsik to realize that he was rudely interrupted, realizes that won’t happen, pauses, and then continues. 
“The city has seen an influx in population lately with more and more merchants choosing to stay and set up their wares at the Fishyard rather than continuing on their way.” 
Thistle then turns to Orsik, “this is where the information gets a little bit dodgy. According to my Uncle’s tales, along with an increased population, there has also been a steady rise in crime and shadowy business dealings.”
Orsik smiles, “my kind of town.” 
“And first, we have to get through the YSA.” 
Thistle worries that Orsik will make a fuss, as he tends to when the Dwarf insists on talking to the guards. 
“We’re just here to put up some posters for our play and hand out some playbills.” 
The guard looks at him, confused, and says, “okay, but remember, this is a law-abiding town.” 
And, what Thistle worried about happens; the guard recognized Orsik.
“Hey, you’re that guy who did that thing.” 
Thistle grabs his money a little bit tighter and takes a step back.
Orsik looks at the man, smiles, and says, “I’m not him.”
“No, you’re not. Orsik was a short dumb dwarf, and you are anything but. He would have pulled his sword and thrown himself out of Yatar.” 
Thistle smiles and hands the guard a playbill, “No harm, no foul! And enjoy our play!” 
The guard looks at the playbill and asks, “is this an expressionist, naturalist play, or is it a more classical production?” 
Thistle realizes that Kaelen hasn’t yet written it and steps forward confidently, “it’s an original production about the struggles of Uthgardtian tribespeople facing the onslaught of civilization that is taking their ancestral lands. Civilization is presented as a river boat from Yartar, the fastest, most unsinkable boat that will carry civilization everywhere. The boat gets destroyed as a result of the hubris and callousness of the captain. There is a romance angle between one of the officers and an Uthgardtian tribal leader.” 
Vardis smiles and says, “it’s spectacular. It’s both well crafted and has a timely message.” 
The guard seems impressed, excited, and wants to help. “You know, given what you said, this play has the potential to hit a broad cross-section of people. You should go to *The Three Rivers Festhall*. It’s at the crook of where the River Surbin and Dessarin River meet. It’s seen better days, but it’s still popular.” 
Thistle smiles and then asks, “sounds like a place sailors might frequent, so yes.” 
“Oh, in that case, I would go to the *Fishyard*. It’s an open-air market with dozens of vendors offering all sorts of imported goods, local catches, and delicacies. You can also find other traders with goods from Calishman, home remedy potions, luxurious fabrics, and more unique magical components there.” 
Thistle nods in approval. 
“Oh, and Yartar is home to Lionshield Costers.” 
Thistle really wants Orsik to stop, but he doesn’t. 
“We have one of those in Triboar; they get around?” 
The guard’s face darkens, “their use of pocket dimensions avoids the use of traditional trade routes. And that impacts local trade and local guards who need some money.” 
Thistle concurs but doesn’t tip the guard. 
He turns to Vardis, Unla Kee, and says – “to the Fishyard.” 
## Unla Kee

### **Rumors and Stories of Yartar**

The city has a dictatorial ruler called the Waterbarron. The current waterbarron Nestra Ruthiol. She has aligned herself with the Lord’s Alliance to keep her power. 

At the intersection of the River Surbin and the Dessarin River and the Evermoor Way, Yartar serves as a fortified crossroads waypoint for caravans and merchants of all sorts. Yartar is also renowned for its expertise in river barge construction, their fine craftsmanship seen from Silverymoon to Waterdeep and along riverbanks between the two.

The city has seen an influx in population lately with more and more merchants choosing to stay and setup their wares at the Fishyard rather than continuing on their way. With the increased population has come a steady rise in crime and shadowy business dealings as well. While the Waterbaron works alongside the Lord's Alliance to try and preserve the peace, the Hand of Yartar and other less well-intentioned factions continue to swell in membership and prominence as well.

With the brewing tensions, the city's prominence as a whole has always been diminished and tarnished by vicious infighting and subterfuge. Since the Waterbaron serves for life, those who desire power often find means of disenfranchising the position's influence within the city. With conflicts and schemes constantly undermining the city from speaking with one unified voice, the Waterbaron is forced to rely on outside connections with other Lord's Alliance members to implement any meaningful change. And with some much competition between merchants, business is often loss and won by rumors, sabotage, or other underhanded means of hindering the competition.

The Shields of Yartar, a coalition of 150 mounted guards work to keep the peace within the city and protect it from the threats of the wilderness and reside in Shield Tower, the massive keep to the west of Yartar.
