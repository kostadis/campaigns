# Chapter 70 Confronting Adimond and a lot of exposition


Thistle stands at the door and prepares to listen. 

Unla Kee tries to push past him to open the door, but Thistle blocks him. 

The fairy hears nothing but wind and water and carefully opens the door.

Inside is the armory containing weapons and armor sized for storm giants. Thistle quickly searches and finds four quarterstaffs carved from sunken ship masts, a large barrel holding six tridents, an open crate containing ten coral-encrusted rocks, and a storm giant-sized mannequin wearing a golden suit of scale mail. Hanging on the walls are three enormous shields made from dragon turtle shells and six massive greatswords with coral-inlaid hilts and sharpened whalebone blades lined with shark teeth. The shields and the weapons aren’t precious, especially given their weight.

Orsik and Thistle immediately get into a debate about whether they should steal the golden armor. Thistle argues about the sagacity of stealing from the Queen. Orsik takes the position they won’t notice. Finally, they agree that although it is a profitable heist, they should conserve resources and avoid accumulating enemies until after they deal with Adimond Kroskas. 

And with that, Thistle takes the door to the east. Three descending sets of stairs lead to submerged areas southeast, north, and northeast of the armory. 

Orsik turns left and arrives in a room full of submerged caves. Four fully submerged caves contain sharks. A passage heading northeast opens up into the vortex area. 

When Thistle refers to the Sharks as fish, Orisk corrects him, “These are mammals.” 

The fairy, a bit of a naturalist, looks at him aghast, “Sharks are fish.”

To which Orisk responds, “I have a headband of intellect, and I say that these are mammals, not fish.” 

Thistle is angry: “No, a shark, although having some attributes similar to a mammal's, is not a fish.” 

Somehow, cooler heads prevail, and they don’t come to blows. 

While this is going on, no one is paying attention to Unla Kee, who notices some giant clams and starts to poke and prod at them. 

At that point, Thistle notices Unla Kee struggling to open the clam and offers his sword. Inside the clam is a grapefruit-sized pearl worth 500 gold. 

As Unla Kee is about to reach for it, Thistle slams the clam shut, “After we deal with Adimond.” 
Orsik, seeing how Thistle is very protective of the Queen’s treasure, opens the other clam behind his back and finds a crystal ball. He stuffs it into his Bag of Holding. Later, he will act surprised that he found it. 

Thistle is not only distracted by Unla Kee but also by the passage to the Vortex. 

At this point, Unla Kee, demonstrating his uncanny ability to put things together, observes, “These pools are all connected to the Vortex. If we go through the Vortex, we can pop up in the throne room.” 

Thistle turns to the 3HPP, and they shout, “It’s your moment, man, let’s do this.” 

They swim through the Vortex, up through the pool, and arrive in the middle of the throne room. The imperator and Queen Serissa are stunned—Iymrith (or Adimond Kroskas) or whatever name she is using now glares. They were arguing.

Orsik charges and strikes Iymrith-Storm Giant. Both times, his sword strikes deep. Iymrith-Giantess is shocked. 

But not as shocked as the storm giants in the room are when she turns into a blue dragon and uses her wings to buffet everyone. 

Once again, Vardis reaches for the bat guano and sulfur, points his finger at an epicenter of the blast that will only affect the dragon, watches as a streak of lights flies out, and then basks in the warmth of the exploding blast. 

Iymrith sends out wave after wave of dragon fear. All but Orsik and Thistle succumb. 

The dragon heads to the pool, shoves Thistle aside and dives into the water. The last words are, " This is not the end of this.”

The Imperator and Queen Serissa recover from their shock and start demanding answers. Just then, Mirren and Nym charge in. 

Finally, Queen Serissa raises her voice with a regal bearing and demands silence. She then asks, “Could someone please explain to us what is going on?” 

Thistle screams - “He’s getting away!” 

Orsik shouts - “He’s afraid of us!” 

Vardis points out calmly - “You had a dragon here, did you know that?” 

Queen Serissa waves her hand again, demanding silence, “Could someone explain, very carefully and in simple language, what the hell is going on?” 

Thistle flies towards her, “That blue dragon is one of the leaders of the Cult of the Dragon. And we were tracking him down.” 

Queen Serissa turns to her sisters, “Did you know this creature was a dragon? Did you knowingly ally yourselves with a dragon?” 

Just then, more Storm Giants show up. 

Mirren and Nym vigorously shake their heads and mutter apologetics. 

Thistle shouts, “He’s getting away!!” 

Queen Serissa waves her hand dismissively, “That is of no concern to us. What is of concern is who the dragon is and who you are.” 

“Enemy of the dragon1” 

The Imperator squints at Thistle, trying to figure out how this tiny fairy could be a threat to an Ancient Blue Dragon. 

“I am its nemesis!” the fairy shouts. 

The imperator, who is over 30 feet tall, examines the 3-foot fairy and tries hard not to laugh. 

Serissa turns to Orsik, “Brave knight?” 

Orsik sighs, “The Dragon’s name is Adimond Kroskas. We’ve been tracking Adimond. He’s a blue dragon in disguise who has infiltrated your court. And we are here to save you.”

Silence.

Mirren breaks the silence, “They were trying to assassinate you.” 

The Imperator cuts her off, “I think we have heard enough from you. Guards take them to their quarters.” The queen’s sister protests but moves meekly away.

Orsik then plays a game: “Also, Serissa, I think we were going to go on a date. Did your sisters tell you about that?” 

The Queen’s eyes bulge.
“We can discuss it later.”

The Imperator rolls his eyes - “Nonsense.”

“I didn’t know,” he stammers.
