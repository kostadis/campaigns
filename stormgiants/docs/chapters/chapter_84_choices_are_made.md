# Chapter 84 - Choices are made.

King Hekaton rushes after Unla Kee, who enters the teleportation chamber behind the throne room. 

The halfling wizard can’t wait to return to his cloud castle and head off to kill the ancient blue dragon. 

“There is treasure to be had and excitement to be found,” he thinks. 

The rest of the 3HPP arrive a few moments later with Imperator Serissa. 

She looks at them, “I can’t convince you to take care of the problem that affects both our people?”

Orsik looks at her, realizing he could do the heroic thing. He thinks that he could save the Giant King, score the Giant Princess, and perhaps someday become the Giant King himself, but then, would that make him a hero?  

“No.” 

His personal honor does not permit it. He must fight for something that others may think is meaningless. 

He must kill Iymrith. He must either kill her or die in the attempt because heroes kill dragons and save girlfriends. That is heroic. 

The Imperator sighs and then attempts to teleport the heroes back to their cloud castle. 

Nothing happens. 

She looks at her King. 

He thinks. 

“Unfortunately, something is blocking the teleport spell,” he mutters. “I fear someone has cast forbiddance.” 

Unla Kee is unconvinced and demands to take control of the teleportation chamber. And for a moment, he ponders whether he should teleport into Waterdeep's treasury chambers rather than get his castle. 

He decides against it. 

After a moment, he says, “Yup, most of the castle is blocked. Someone or something has made it impossible for us to get to the castle except for some parts of it.” 
Thistle looks at Unla Kee - “That's impossible. They would need a very powerful cleric and to have the cleric on hand. We haven’t been that far away for that long.” 

Hekaton looks at Thistle and says, “Thistle, the Cult of the Kraken has access to mysterious powers and magic. Did they know about your castle?” 

Unla Kee thinks for a moment about how they brought their castle to Yartar and attacked a high-ranking member of the Kraken Society, who escaped. Every member of that society probably knew about the castle. 

“But how could they have sent a message from so far away?” he wonders. And then he remembers how Lord Drylund died as if something had triggered that. And he thinks that maybe the members of the Kraken are interlinked in some kind of hive mind. He then thinks this is information the King doesn’t need to know because the decision to show the castle to the world wasn’t his idea. And the King might think it’s his fault for not stopping Orsik, whose idea it was. 

He turns to the King, “No.” 

“Strange indeed how they knew to bring such a powerful spell caster.”

Serissas declares, “You were kept on a ship we could not find, despite the best of divination magic I could pay for. The Kraken Society knows how to hide things.” 

Hekaton nods his head.

“We will rest and head out in the morning,” declares Orsik. 

And indeed, they do. 

King Hekaton teleports them just outside the great doors of Ironslag. 

Orsik isn’t tempted to find his lost treasure because that’s the obvious thing to do. Nor is he interested in saving the King’s kingdom because that’s the thing anyone would do. 

From Ironslag, the 3HPP heads through the Silver Marches and directly to Fork Road. Their final destination is the town of Ascore at the end of Fork Road. During the Netherese Empire, Ascore was a port city of the dwarven nation of Delzoun on the northern shores of the Narrow Sea. However, by the 14th-century DR, it was a lonely, windswept ruin on the edge of the Anauroch, near the Frozen Sea, reputed to be haunted by ancient evil.

As Thistle explained, the desert Anauroch is the side effect of the mad king Krasus’ actions that brought down the Kingdom of Netheril. 

Vardis thinks it’s interesting that the 3HPP’s story began near Anauroch in Parnast and will now end one way or another in Anauroch. 

From Ascore, Iymrith’s lair is close by. 

Along the way, they encounter merchants from Citadel Adbar who tell them of rumors that the Uthgardtian Barbarians are raiding and that the Giants are on the prowl. 

The 3HPP expresses confusion and doubt about the veracity of such rumors. 

Thistle is horrified when the merchants say they are going to Silverymoon - “That’s a very bad place. They have cults to Asmodeus! They had a horde of Zombies that we saved them from! There is a demon running, or was running a play! Don’t go there.” 

The caravan driver looks at Thistle and then turns to Orsik, “He’s not a goodly race, eh?” 

The guard next to the driver looks at Orsik, sees the young, brash, arrogant, obnoxious dwarf, and asks, “Are you Orsic?” 

Orsik looks at him, “No it was my son.” \\

A few days later, the 3HPP are preparing to sleep the night when the SEER appears with her gold pseudo dragon, Hsing. 

She wants to make a deal.

Orsik growls at her, “How did you find us?” 

She explained that Hekaton was a friend of the Lord’s Alliance and willing to share the information she needed. When Orsik observed that it was impossible for her to just show up in the exact place she needed to, she sighs, “It’s called Divination.” 

The dwarf sits down, “What do you want?” 

“I have a deal to make. It seems that Hekaton trusts you. He sees you as - heroic. And he said he would be willing to consider a permanent alliance between the King of the Giants and the Lord’s Alliance if you dealt with his problem.” 

“Not interested,” mutters the dwarf. 

“I could make all of your problems go away? Instead of being known as the annoying prat, I could make you a hero!” 

He laughs, “A mass hypnosis?” 

“No, I am the SEER. I will claim that this was all part of an elaborate ruse I set up to get you infiltrated and close to King Hekaton. You will have statues and monuments all across Faerun.”

He stares at her, “Think about it!” 

She smiles, confident he will agree. 

“But I have other gifts for your group. Unla Kee, I could get your cloud castle back. And I could get it back without Escarlotta.” 

Before he can answer, she turns to Vardis, “And you, Cleric of Lorellon, I heard how much you worry about losing your friend. With my help, he’ll become a hero and live. All he has to do is say yes and get rid of these giant lords.”

She pauses, “I could also solve your Lorellon problem and get you into the temples again. I might even be able to engineer an apology.” 

Vardis is about to answer and turns to Thistle, “And for you, Thistle, we have arrows of dragon slaying and a powerful magical bow. If you only turn from Iymrith and deal with the Giants, these magical artifacts could be yours. Once you have dealt with the Giants, go deal with Iymrith. I don’t care.” 

She waits for them to say yes, but they don’t.

The SEER is confused. Her information was impeccable. Her agents had told her their weak points, and she had exploited all of them, but they weren’t saying yes. 

She is flustered, but she has been in more challenging negotiations.

Unla Kee mutters, “See, the castle is mine already. So you aren’t giving me anything I don’t already have.” 

The SEER wants to scream, “WHAT?” but catches herself. She knew this was possible: “I could guarantee a tax-free license for your casino and a permit for all cities that belonged to the Lord’s Alliance.” 

Unla Kee shrugs, “No.” 

Thistle is pained but agrees, “No.” 

Vardis looks at Orsik and says, “No.” 

The SEER is furious, “You don’t understand what is at stake here. We have an opportunity for the first time in 40,000 years to have peace between the Giants and the rest of the kingdoms of the North. Hekaton will agree to peace. But he will only do it if you four handle his problems. For some reason, he thinks this is a sign from the All-father.”

She then stops and composes herself, but the venom drips from her words, “I tried to offer him anyone else, but he said - no. He insisted that it had to be you. Why he thinks you four are so important is beyond me. But I need you to stop with your bizarre quest to go get yourselves killed and create a better world.” 

Orsik looks at her, “See, killing a dragon is heroic.” 

“No, no, it’s not. There are so many dragons in the realms. Each of them pretends that they are some kind of all-powerful force. They aren’t. They are self-absorbed, narcissistic, arrogant agents of chaos. Anyone can kill a dragon. Not anyone can save the realms.”

Orsik looks at her and says, “No, killing the dragon and saving his girl is heroic.” 

She stares at him in disbelief, “If you do this, I will guarantee that your name is cursed for a thousand generations.” 

Orsik screams, “Bad mouth me across the realms? No, I don’t think so. I am going to be a hero. Killing a dragon is heroic. Saving my friend’s girl is heroic. Having you create a fake story about my life while I do the bidding of your organization doesn’t make me a hero. It makes me a fake.” 

He attempts to strike her, but she is in a sanctuary, and he finds he can’t. 

Vardis casts a fireball, but she counters it. 

Unla Kee has heard enough, and he envisions a prison, crafting its floor, sides, and ceiling from the hardest thing he can think of. A diamond box, a kind of jeweled jail, takes form before his eyes, though only he can see it. With the last gesture, he pinches ruby dust in his pocket and turns it like you would a key. 

She is trapped in a forcecage. 

She smiles. 

And a few moments later, she escapes. She does leave a note behind. It says,  “Orsic, I won’t bad mouth you. I disagree with your choices. But if you defeat Iymrith, at least consider the possibility that you could do the realms a favor.” 

He wonders what happened and realizes she must have cast a time stop and teleported herself out after she wrote the note. 

There is a moment of silence. 

Thistle looks around, “Does anyone want a goodberry for dinner?” 

A few days later, the 3HPP is finally in Ascore. According to Uncle Joon - A millennia-old road ran from the west and into a great door set in a hill, flanked by two massive stone statues of crouching griffons with grim aspects. The door opened into a black yawning passage that descended into the earth. Beyond, the road exited the hill and came to the ruins about the base of the cliff above Ascore.

Two famous monuments are located within the city: the stone ships and the red pyramids. The stone ships were mighty vessels, designed for trade and warfare, and nigh indestructible, and they plied the Narrow Sea. Up to twenty stone ships could be docked in Ascore any time, with thirty more out at sea. In the ruined city, these were still visible, half-buried in the sand, with the stone docks standing proudly against the encroaching sea of sand and the empty hulks of weird stone ships emerging from the dunes to the east. 

As for the red pyramids, they stood in a circle in the very center of Ascore, carved out of a strange red stone. Uncle Joon told Thistle to avoid them. 

So the group navigates around the eroded city and its strange monuments until they come to the toppled stones of an ancient ruin that stood resilient against the arid wind and oppressive sun, the stone bleached bone-white after eons. Little remains of the original structure, though they imagine a reconstruction of the soaring pillars and central monolithic structure. 

And as they walk through it, the ground trembles. Two blue dragons emerge from the sand, one in front and one behind them. 

Orsik prepares to fight. Thistle notices something strange: the dragon’s bottom half resembles a purple worm.
