# Chapter 78 The first defeat of Sandesyl

The Vampire Spawn sees what’s coming and decides to warn the others. It’s not fleeing, it’s warning. “Perhaps,” the vampire spawn thinks, “Sandesyl will see it that way too.” 

Vardis sees it run, and from his outstretched hands, a raw blast of coruscating divine energy flies toward the spawn, which then burns when with the concentrated power of Correlon’s wrath. 

Thistle floats like a fairy and then, from his position, strikes at one of the other spawns. 

Seeing the battle, Blagothkus declares - “I will stand back and protect the magic user.” 

Orsik growls, “No, you will not defend the magic user. You will charge into battle.” 

The giant looks miffed. 

Orsik, the motivator, shouts, “Look, you defeated all those armored horrors by yourself. You are the key to this victory.” 

The giant sniffles, “Really?” And then charges into the fray, attacking and destroying one of the spawns of Sandesyl. 

Vaardis smiles, sensing the battle is almost over. But then, to the south, he sees another one of the Vampire’s minions with several guard drakes advancing on them. And to the east from another chamber is a giant skeletal dragon. Unla Kee matters - “A failed attempt at a dracolich, that’s why needed the book.” 

Thistle mutters, “I could have scouted, and we could have come up with a better plan, but Orsik wanted to charge.” 

At first, the dwarf is upset, but then he realizes that sometimes, when you are working with great people, they will come up with better plans and that, as a leader, it’s crucial to listen. And that he did what he was working so hard to do, not let other people provide input into the planning process. He sighs, “You’re right. My bad.” 

Vardis raises an eyebrow. 

“Do I attack the advancing hordes, or do I attack the Vampire? And where is the vampire?” He looks around and notices a coffin in a chamber whose entrance is in the western wall of the cavernous chamber. “I can see a coffin, but there is no vampire.” 

With Sandesyl nowhere to be found, he aims with his bow and strikes one of the drakes. Two more arrows strike the drake from the bow of Thistle. 

Orsik rushes in, kills the drake, and then looks around for the spawn directing these creatures. He strikes savagely at the undead creature. 
Then, seemingly out of nowhere, a fireball explodes, its heat and flames engulfing Vardis, Blagothkus, and Unla Kee. 

Blagothkus cannot avoid the heat, but Unla Kee does and, more importantly, doesn’t look his concentration. 

Blagothkus sees the Skeletal Dragon's jaw snap at his arms as the fire recedes. He howls in pain. 

The creature strikes at Thuul with its claws. Thuul avoids the left claw but lands on the right claw. The drake’s tough armor protects him, and he shakes his head, ready to fight. 

Meanwhile, Orsik is surrounded by guard drakes. His armor and his skill in battle leave him confident that, barring a lucky strike, these are not dangerous. And, of course, he gets cut by the jaws of a drake that got lucky. 

Unla Kee opens his eyes and sees Orsik surrounded. He does some quick math and realizes that a fireball will hurt five times more enemies than allies. A moment later, Orsik and the drakes are engulfed in a ball of fire. 

The drakes are angry and snarl and bite, but Orsik dodges every one of their attacks. 

Vardis shouts, “Another fireball?” 

Orsik screams, “I can handle the burn.” 

The fire bursts out lightning the entire cavern and then disappears. Vardis closes his eyes, hoping to see his friend standing, and opens them when he hears Orsik’s roar of approval. Whereas there were five drakes before, now there are two badly wounded creatures and one vampire spawn that is badly burned. 

Thistle aims at one and kills it. 

Orsik screams at Blagothkus, “Kill the dragon!” 

Blagothkus mutters, “I am injured. Can someone heal me?” 

The enraged dwarf shouts - “You don’t want to fight; I will kill you myself.” 

The cloud giant is taken aback and sputters, “What would escarlotta say?” 

“I don’t care.” 

Realizing that Orsik is not joking, the giant picks up a rock from the cavern floor and tosses it at the skeletal dragon. 

Orsik rushes past the last drake, which dies moments later from Thistle’s bow, and strikes the dragon first with a slice across its chest, then strike to its claw, followed by a crushing blow to one of its legs. 

The creature is in pain but still capable of fighting. If it weren’t undead, the beast would flee. 

Sandesyl, from her perch in her cave, is raging. Her minions have failed her. She is not worried about being killed. She has a secret place to escape, and nobody knows its location. A place where she can safely regenerate. “But first, I need to kill that wizard.” 

She invisibly moves through the crowd and grabs him with one of her claws, but the wizard avoids the attack. She then uses her other claw and has him in her grasp. Unla Kee smiles; he is ready for that and doesn’t lose his concentration. 

But Orsik’s haste ends when the Dragon strikes the Wizard. But the wizard doesn’t die. 

When she moves out of the way and hides behind the dragon, Unla Kee smiles. With words that mix prayer and an ancient magic language, his spell reaches out to the celestial sphere and opens a pathway to a foundational force of the universe, without which—quite literally—all the world would lie in darkness. He looks at the glow coming from between his fisted fingers and then throws out his open palm, directing the brilliant power of the Sun in a searing line of radiance.

The vampire tries to avoid the worst of the blast, but she can feel fate's twist against her. Unla Kee is smiling, and she screams, “A diviner!” 

Before she can recover, Vardis summons the power of the blazing sun of Olympus from Correlon’s domain and lets loose the tiniest fraction of its celestial brightness. The cavern is brightly lit, the skeletal dragon collapses, its’ magic force is spent, and Sandesyl is burned. 

Blagothkus and Thistle strike the last of the cult of the dragon, the last of Samaster’s disciples, with their ranged attacks. Sandesyl screams in rage but can’t get out of the range of the bright light that permeates the caverns from the spells of Thistle and Unla Kee. Her rage echoes throughout the cavern, and she turns into mist. 
	
Meanwhile, deep in the caverns, Thuul and the last drake snarl at each other. The drake strikes Thuul. But Thuul shrugs off the bite and then kills it with a vicious bite of his own. 

Orsik is burned, bloodied, and standing and annoyed, “WHERE IS THE DRACOLICH?” 

Thistle observes, “Well, we did decide to attack in her lair, and there is no way for the dracolich to enter.” 

“Glazhael said she would show up if we got the vampire deeply injured.” 

“Yes, and there was no way for her to come.” 

He stomps his foot down, like a petulant child, but realizes that Thistle is right, “Fine, we need to kill that vampire.”

He rushes to the coffin and tosses it open. It is empty. 

“Where is she?” 

Thistle stares at the ice and mutters, “Endless cracks go deep into the caves. I have no idea where she is.” 

“Okay, fine, we open the chests and then find this Dracolich.” 

Thistle is about to scream, “Wait!” but is too late. Orsik opens the chest, and a lightning bolt strikes him, Vardis, and Unla Kee.
