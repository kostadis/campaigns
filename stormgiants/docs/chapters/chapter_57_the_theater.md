# Chapter 57 - The Theater

## Eleasis 4th day, 2nd tenday

The 3HPP, hidden from sight using the invisibility potions that Orsik provided, stands in front of the double doors of the Great Theater.
Each door has a small, grilled viewport. A shutter blocks the view of what lies beyond. The door is decorated with magical runes made of many thousand small mosaic pieces. 
Vardis asks, “Where is everyone?” 
Orsik agrees that it is odd that there are no other patrons, “where did all those patrons that bought the tickets and mobbed us go? Are we too early?”
“No, we arrived 5 minutes before the play started.” 
Orsik moves boldly to the door and opens it. As he is about to enter, he notices a glyph that the 3HPP turns off. 
Unfortunately, he did not notice the alarm spell that had been cast. 
The shrill sound of the alarm forces the audience to turn towards the door. 
Orsik notices that one of the audience members is a suppurating, bloody mess; one of its eyes is missing. 
Vardis observes that another is clad in blood-soaked peasant clothes, having clearly suffered horrendous wounds before their death. 
Thistle stares at the gaping wound in their rib cage, exposing the desiccated organs. 
They all think, “Zombies.” 
The theater itself has two levels. The first level has a stage at the end with a red curtain that has been raised. Benches are arranged to give the zombies an excellent view of the play. And there is a staircase that is right next to the door. 
Standing on the stage, Bob Saget, dressed in a purple jacket, purple tie, and purple pants, shouts, “We have guests? Could it be the Three Half Pints and a Pint?” 
He waits and hears nothing. 
“Well, it doesn’t matter.” He clicks his fingers, and the doors behind the 3HPP close. 
“You can still enjoy the show; if you are here to disrupt it, you might want to think twice.” 
And just then, several drow knights and two cambions come from backstage and stand in front of Bob. They obstruct the view of the stage for the Zombie patrons. 
They don’t seem to mind. 
Bob Saget then continues. “The fool Kaesen’s summoning spell brought me here. And the 3HPP thought it would bring joy and light to this world, and of course, profit.” Then, with a dramatic hand gesture, he spits out, “But that was boring.” His tone turns to rage, “Who wants to bring joy and light? I am a demon. I cause chaos and mayhem!” Then it turns conspiratorial, “I’ve been casting my magic to twist the folks of Silverymoon against their fascist oppressors, the Spellguards. But I’ve been working on my own little spell. And it needs some victims. And rather than just kill someone, why not have four aspiring thespians pretend to be the 3HPP and die trying to kill me?” 
The drow disappears backstage and then shows up with four commoners dressed like the 3HPP, except one dressed in black and carrying a stick with a fairy attached to it. They are terrified and crying and screaming. 
He turns to the audience, breaking the fourth wall, and says, “I love the authenticity of their terror.” 
As the faux-Orsik screams, he tut-tuts, “Weren’t you looking to be members of this unbelievable show?’ 
And then again breaks the fourth wall, almost as if he is taunting his uninvited guests, “Do you want to take their parts?” 
The 3HPP are silent. 
“Look at them, a member of the – ungoodly race. They come into Silverymoon, and they get a foul of the Spellguard and nobody cares if they disappear. Why the folks of Silverymoon might say thank you.” 
He spins in a circle and claps his hands, “I am not killing people. I am solving a problem for the city. And after every performance, the cast joins the audience and passes judgment on their performance!” 
The 3HPP stares silently. 
The crying cast members are pushed on stage, and Bob says, “They don’t know how to fight or cast spells, but I think the authenticity of desperation makes it work.” 
A gnome dressed like Unla Kee waves his hands frantically as he tries to cast something, but the poor gnome is not a spell caster. Bob laughs and then sticks his claw through the poor person’s chest. 
At that, the Tabaxi, dressed like Orsik, charges and swings his sword. 
Bob shouts, “I’ve been injured. I am going to die.” The Tabaxi smiles, and then Bob kills the feline and says gleefully, “No, I am not,” as he kills them. 
The 3HPP can watch no more and close their eyes. When they open them, there are four corpses on the stage. 
At that, a Drow Mage stands in front of the corpses and animates them using some kind of device. The sad victims then join the audience. 
Bob laughs and leaves the stage. 
The 3HPP leaves the theater, horrified. 
As they stand outside, invisible, a blue shimmer appears, and then their fans march out. 
The fans are delighted, excited, and very alive. 
Orsik understands what is happening: “He’s using the Theater to conduct his bloodthirsty ritual, and he’s creating some kind of demi-plane to send the theatergoers to.” 
Then, each member of the 3HPP sits silently and wonders what to do. 
Unla Kee thinks, “I wanted to attack right then and there. Now, I don’t.” 
Orsik decides that the people of Silverymoon did nothing for him. And he remembers quite vividly how the Knights treated him. He, of course, forgets what he did to deserve that treatment. 
Vardis will do what his best friend wants. The city of Silverymoon is not an elven problem. 
Thistle thinks to himself, “This is awful! I must do something!” Then he pauses, “But I also have to save my sweetie!” And then remembers there is time, “We have 30 days!” And then remembers that all those innocent people are going to die, “Can I really make that tradeoff?” And then he realizes, “I am the only one who can save her, nobody else. I will save her and then deal with this.” 

Vardis mutters, “I don’t think we could take Bob. He’s too powerful. We need allies.” 
“We seem to have accumulated enemies, not allies.” 
Unla Kee quibbles, “Sure, the harpers hate us. The Zhentarim aren’t exactly trying to help us. The city of Silverymoon won’t look kindly on us when we abandon them. There is the city of Everlund. And then there is the Hand of Yartar. We have friends. And I have a fireball. And I can assure you that this is not how the world ends.” 
Orsik says, “We could tell the Lord of this city?” 
And then they all realize that if they did, it’s unclear if the Lord would think they were innocent of these crimes.
“What’s the vote?” 
They all agree that they will deal with this secretly, first dealing with the giants, then Bob. 
And with that, they turn to Unla Kee – “Let’s get you that tower.”
“A flying tower?”
“A flying castle!” 
The following day, the 3HPP are once again the celebrated heroes of Silverymoon. Bob Saget is, once again, the toast of the town, and his play continues its fantastic run! 
The 3HPP, however, are aware of the evil they have unleashed on the town of Silverymoon. 
## Eleasis 8th day, 2nd tenday

Reaching Parnast, Orsik, Thistle, and Unla Kee recognize the castle as the Dragon's Breath floats past. It’s the same castle that buzzed them so many months ago when they went on a quest on the black road. That quest set Orsik on his peculiar journey of redemption and led to the 3HPP’s uncompromising hate towards the Lord’s Alliance and its head of secret police, the SEER. 
But that was not the only beginning. The castle was the home of Blagothkus, who triggered the chaos of the Ordning. Blagothkus had allied himself with Tiamat to destroy the world of the small people. The cult had used his tower to transport treasure until a group known as the First Faction stopped him. The First Faction then secured the castle from Blagothkus and a Dracolich. Again, how Blagothkus ended up with his castle is a mystery. 
As they float near the Castle, Delsephine asks, “Where is Brezzik?” 
Orsik looks at her, “He decided to look for work elsewhere as their play wasn’t going to have that rewrite we were hoping for.” 
And with that, she drops them on the upper tower of the castle.
