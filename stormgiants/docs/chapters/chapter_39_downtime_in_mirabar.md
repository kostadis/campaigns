# Chapter 39 - Downtime in Mirabar
## Fred and George’s Report
Xaltivar’s attack on Xanthalar’s Keep was a ruse. The plan was to distract the main force of the guard while a detachment of bugbears jumped the walls and secured a prisoner named Worvil Forkbeard, who goes by the alias – “The Weevil.” 
We were able to thwart the main attack thanks to the support of a group known as the Three Halfpints and a Pint, or 3HPP. 
The 3HPP planned to leave Xanthalar’s Keep before the battle ended and demanded we open the gates. 
We stood our ground and insisted that they wait for the battle. 
The members of the 3HPP had a raucous debate. One of their members insisted that they leave right away because his lordship  Narbeck Horn would be duped by The Weevil and say that this was a case of mistaken identity.
We chose not to tell them that His Lordship Horn would know that this was The Weevil because of the distinctive braiding of his beard. A braiding that all shield dwarfs can recognize. 
Fortunately, the Lieutenant showed up in time and convinced the 3HPP to wait, and no further events occurred. 
## Orsik 
As the group headed to Mirabar on the Long Road North, Orsik kept staring at The Weevil. The dwarf wanted to tell him something.
Finally, unable to resist, he ungagged the prisoner. 
“I have treasure I am willing to exchange for my freedom.” 
“You are worth 5000 gold to me.” 
“Ah, but I know how to get a much bigger score, Orsik.” 
“You know me?”
“Who doesn’t.” 
“Well, at least I am not tied up and gagged.” 
Orsik notices Vardis staring at him and feels momentarily bad that his good friend, the elf, is a relative unknown while he’s infamous. 
“I know how we can isolate a caravan from the mines of Mirabar and rob it. There is a secret pattern that I happen to have figured out. All I need is a crew, and I promise we will make a fortune.”
The fighter stares at The Weevil and thinks that the other Orsik, the less intelligent one that had been a disgrace, might have taken that offer, but this Orsik, the Captain of the Guards of Parnast, Defender of Parnast, will not. 
He thinks how much he had changed from that day when the old dwarf called him a disgrace, stares at The Weevil, and gags him again. 
As Orsik silences him, he notices how everyone except for Unla Kee is pleased. 
## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is grouchy. For the last two days, he has glared at Orsik, who seems interested in going straight and making easy money instead of big.
To make matters worse, he can’t get close to The Weevil to learn his secret method of robbing the caravans from the mines. 
As he runs through his head the hundredth plan to extract The Weevil from the clutches of the suddenly very good Orsik, Harshnag does his -blah-blah-blah about some other important geographical location of the North.
“Mirabar,” he begins, “is the richest city of the North by far.”
At that point, Unla Kee perks up, and wealthy cities have rich people that like to gamble to pass away the hours. 
“It sits atop a knoll on the north side of the Mirar River like an unassailable fortress, enclosed on all sides by sloped outer walls as wide at the base as many city blocks in Waterdeep.”
The diviner smirks internally, and those stuck-up Waterdhavians never talk about this city. They like to brag about how their city is the most important and most prosperous and blah-blah-blah. 
And again, Frosty’s voice penetrates his reverie. 
“Defenders can fire arrows down from atop the walls or, in winter, pour water down them to make ice slides. There is no shortage of stone and weaponry. Even the docks have battlements and fortifications.”
Unla Kee can’t resist – “Can we go down the slides?”
“No, it’s not winter.” 
“Can’t you make snow, like a white dragon? I mean, you are a FROST giant.” 
Harshnag takes slams the bit of his battleaxe inches from Unla Kee. The eye of the handle is about the size of the halfling and says, “How dare you!” 
 Orsik sniggers, “That’s a bit of a racial slur.” 
Unla Kee shrugs, “No ice sledding?”
Harshnag the Grim stares at the halfling and solemnly explains, “I will say this once. The ancient enemy of the giant is the dragon. Between our two kinds, there can be no quarter. There can be no peace. Only ceasefires.”
The halfling can’t resist, “Isn’t that peace?”
“No. The dragons started this disaster between the giants and the little people. Tiamat tried to seize power and weakened the North. The Giant lords saw an opportunity in that vacuum and sundered the Ordning.” 
And this has thoroughly confused Unla Kee, “So your racial war is why we can’t have an ice slide?”
The Frost Giant glares and bellows and then continues his monologue, “ Visitors to Mirabar often wonder why they don’t see more dwarves, as humans make up most of the city’s surface dwellers. Another city lies just below the surface, and dwarves dominate that place. Underground, Mirabar is a city of lit residential caverns, superheated forges, foundries that operate day and night, and tunnels leading to the mines.” 
## In the city 
Upon entering the city, the 3HPP declare that they wish to hand over the Weevil for their reward.
The sergeant of the guard, a dwarf named Herald, is delighted and congratulates the group and their service to Mirabar. 
The guards of Mirabar are known as the Axe of Mirabar, who wore the crest of Mirabar (a deep-red double-bladed axe with a pointed haft and a flaring, flat base on a black field) on their shield or armor. The Axe army has almost 2000 well-trained soldiers that can protect the city from attack. 
The Weevil tries to convince Herald that he’s not whom they say he is, but Herald points to his braid and says, “Silence! You are the criminal.” His shouting draws some other members of the guard, and Herald smirks at The Weevil, “You wanted ore? Well, you will have as much ore as you want.” 
Herald turns to the 3HPP and says, “he will be working in the mines to the end of his days.”
Orsik informs Herald that apparently, The Weevil cracked some code. This intrigues Herald, who tells The Weevil – “we have ways of getting that information from you.” 
After handing over their prisoner and flush with 5000 gold pieces marked with the symbol of Mirabar, the group agrees to meet again at the entrance in five days. 
Thistle uses his time to learn about his lost love Laela. From the Axe, he learns that a fairy matching her description passed through Mirabar and headed east to the Lurkwood. From a merchant named Gale Wonderbrow, he learns more. Gale has an athletic, imposing frame, short hair, and a chin built for taking a punch. Gale promises information if Thistle helps him arrange an elaborate wedding. As Thistle is a fairy from the Seely court, he can’t resist, and the result is spectacular. Gale is so pleased with the result that he tells him of a tale involving a fairy matching Laela’s description, slaughtering some brigands and bandits. The story was that these brigands surrounded a caravan she was on, and then in a blinding flash of mayhem, everyone but her and a young man who was cowering in one of the wagons was dead. 
Unla Kee gambles. And he gambles his entire share at the tables of Mirabar. As a newcomer, he’s not invited to the tables with high stakes.  And it’s a good thing. Although his luck was good at first, over the next few days, in a desperate attempt to cover his losses, he made more and more outrageous bets and lost half of his money. He would still be gambling, but Thistle saw him bleary-eyed clutching his cash and ready to throw it all on one last bet. Thistle knew that look and dumped some cold water on Unla Kee as he muttered, “Worked for Uncle Joon.” 
As for Orsik, he decided to use his newfound skills in magical disguise to masquerade as a member of a minor noble family of Waterdeep, House Husteem, a house of landowners and mercenary company owners. They have a reputation as belligerent and forceful. It is generally agreed that they found their way into Waterdhavian nobility solely through the depth of their pockets and the military power they wield – it certainly isn’t through any inherent nobility on their part. The rough and tumble style of the House makes it possible for Orsik’s rough manners to be excused. |

As a member of House Husteem, he manages to get invited to parties thrown by the rich and powerful of Mirabar, including a dinner thrown by House Hammaver. They are a mercantile house of the first order and saw an opportunity to entertain a minor noble of House Husteem. Yip Hammaver, a 32-year-old bachelor of medium height and the strangest red eyes, befriends Orsik, whom he knows as John Husteem. 
Unfortunately for John-Orsik, he offended another minor noble, Tack Proudfoot Esquire, at one of these parties. That then leads to a duel at dawn. Yip told John-Orsik that Tack was a bit of a dandy and not a threat. According to Yip, Tack read one too many romance novels that talked about duels. His death would make things very awkward as Tack was betrothed to one of the leading houses of Mirabar, Clan Battlehammer. And Tack’s death would be avenged. 
The morning of the duel outside the city, Tack showed up dressed in the finest armor John-Orsik had ever seen, prompting his quip – “I thought you said he was a terrible fighter?”
Yip replied, “Yes, for a noble night of the elite guards.” 
Seeing no way to win, John-Orsik cast sanctuary on himself and refused to fight. When Percival realized that John-Orsik would not fight, he declared that honor was satisfied. He took off his helmet and embraced John-Orsik.
Yip, Percival, and their seconds were delighted with the outcome. Percival, so much so that he insisted that the single John-Orsik should be introduced to many eligible bachelors or bachelorettes of the various noble houses of Mirabar. 
John-Orsik smiled and left quickly.
As for Vardis, he finds a notice for a strong sword arm to deal with a problem in a building. Hork Gethorn, the owner of a meat pie shop whose face looks like a meat pie and whose size reflects his love of his product, needs help dealing with an infestation beneath his establishment. Vardis takes on the job and returns with a reward and some treasure. 

## Harshnag The Grim 7th day of the 3rd tenday of Flamerule

Harshnag sits under a tree some distance from the city of Mirabar, thinking. The war against the little people is getting worse. He has heard rumors of Jarl’s raiding settlements to the north in the Icewind Dales. If one of the refugees is to be believed, Bryn Shander was laid waste by a group of Frost Giants looking for some artifact. From the south, he heard that Hill giants attacked the city of Goldenfields. And then he learned that the stone giants had attacked settlements on the surface. That frightens him the most. The Stone Giants view the surface world as an abomination that must be destroyed. And finally, there is the story that the 3HPP said that the Fire Giants under Duke Zalto was scouring the lands for an artifact. 
“This is not good, not good at all.”
