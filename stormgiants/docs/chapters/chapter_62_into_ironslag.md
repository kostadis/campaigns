# Chapter 62 - Into Ironslag
The Yakfolk are not as easily killed as some of the enemies that the 3HPP have fought. There are many of them, and they are quite hardy. Worse, they have hostages making the use of the area of effect spell, the Fireball, dicey. 
And to make it worse, the Yalkfolk have priests that can summon elemental. And an earthen monster strikes, grabbing Thistle in his fist and tossing him against the ground like a rag doll, holding onto his body. Then he slams the puny fairy again. 
As that is going on, Unla Kee computes the space needed to hit the most number of enemies. “Four,” he shouts and the room explodes with a fireball. 
One of the Yakfolk screams in agony as it dies in the blast. 
Another seeing the blistering ball of fire, fires some arrows at Unla Kee, and hits with a glancing blow. The halfling smiles and says, “I am – after all – Unla Kee… For you.” 
Thistle manages to squirm from the Earth Elemental and uses his longbow kills one of the Yakfolk priests with an arrow to the throat. The other arrow wounds the other priest, but not enough to send her to whatever afterlife she may think she deserves. 
Thuul, squirms past the earth elemental to strike at the still living priest. The monstrous earthen extra-dimensional creature punches him, as he scoots by, and although there is a slight crunching sound, the drake survives and scalds the priestess with his acid.
She screams and dies. 
Unla Kee, who has recently learned the dark arts of Cloud Kill plots the trajectory of the spell. He mutters to himself, “you are getting in my way.” He sees two placements, one which gets the elemental and one which doesn’t but gets an extra Yakfokl. “What did my teachers say?” he mutters. “Right, immune to poison.” 
And soon a billowing poisonous cloud streams from his fingers and three of the half-human and half-Yak are hidden inside. Two thuds are heard, and some choking. 
Outside of the chief’s tent, Orsik is fighting the chief. Whom he kills. 
Thistle decides to try and kill the Earth Elemental who showed him so little respect. 
Slam! Slam! 
And the poor fairy is once again slammed against the ground. 
The cleric of Correlon seeing Thistle getting brutalized, uses his mace of terror. The elemental, is frightened, of what is unclear, and begins to burrow deep into the ground. 

And just then the last of the Yakfolk trapped in the billowing cloud of poison, dies with a thud. 
The tent is quiet. 
At this point one of the elves speaks, “I am Princess Halani Meliame, and this is Jevin and Vandar. We are from the Moonwood. The Yakfolk captured us. If you bring us to our homes, there is a great reward.”
Unla Kee turns to Orsik, “We made allies!” 
Thistle, who really doesn’t want to accumulate more enemies, interrogates the princess and learns that she was captured by the Yakfolk who had performed the ritual of possession on several of her guards. 
The halfling wizard seeing the profit says, “We’ve got a flying castle. Just climb the rope to the top level, and don’t go anywhere where there is no bright sunlight.”
“There are some vile creatures in our sky castle that we have not yet had a chance to get rid of,” offers Thistle. 
Before she climbs up the rope, Thistle asks her, “Any giants near by?”
She pauses, and explains – “Yes past the doorway to the north. There is an elevator that takes you down into the Giant’s home. Oh and another thing, the Yakfolk used their ritual to take over some dwarves from Citadel Adbar, and kill the dwarven king.” 
Thistle then wonders about the political situation in this part of the world and asks the Princess, “How do the Moon Elves feel about Silverymoon?”
The Princess expresses her bitter frustration that Silverymoon betrayed the Silver Marches in their hour of need. 
Thistle mutters, “I know!” 
And she smiles, and explains that the era of Silverymoon’s dominance has ended. Halani looks tired as she mutters, “They used to be a beacon of hope and light, with courageous and heroic leaders of society. But then they abandoned the Silvery March for their own selfish needs and exposed us to the raids of the hordes. Our people can no longer support or rely on the support of such fair weather allies. A new organization will protect the people of this world.”
“You wish to establish, A new world order?” the fairy asks. 
The Princess realizes she has said too much, “I have been in prison for some time, and I don’t know. And I am not sure where things are moving to.” She stares at the dead Yakfolk who had tormented her, and says angrily, “I would not be surprised if the agents of the Silverymoon put the Yakfolk up to their plots.” 

Vardis says convincingly, “This would not surprise us at all.” 
After talking a short rest, the group heads towards the door that leads down into the Fire Giant’s domain. 
The door is 20 foot tall, made of riveted iron plates and handles 9 feet above the floor. Even the Pint finds these doors too tall. 
After some discussion, Thistle flies to the handle, and the rest push the door open, which thanks to their combined might opens easily. 
Beyond the door is a portcullis, that Vardis lifts open. 
Unla Kee mocks him, “You should become a fighter.” 
Vardis stares at him, and remembers that the clerics of Corellon Latherian have to deal with a god that has trust issues. Ever since his first consort betrayed him, anything but true loyalty is a source of anxiety and affects the relationship. And for a person like Vardis who is already on the outs, a further demonstration of his lack of loyalty may provoke his god. 
Past the portcullis is a cold, dark and vast chamber with a 50’ high ceiling, and an 80’ diameter, 500’ deep shaft in the middle of the floor. An iron rig is bolted to the pit’s circular rim. Suspended from this rig by iron chains is a 60’ diameter circular platform made from oak beams held together by iron bands and rivets.
As the 3HPP stare, the notice that the platform ascends and descends the shaft constantly, powered by a wood water wheel located behind an opening in the southeast wall. 
Thistle hides behind some boxes and observes the platform’s movement. He notices that the  platform makes a series of 15 stops on its way up and down the shaft, each stop lasting 30 seconds. After 15 minutes, it is either all the way down, or all the way up. 
After some discussion, the group decides to look around for another way to go down. They find a staircase in an adjacent room. 
Thistle turns to the 3HPP and reminds them of something Uncle Joon talked about, “Ironslag is at the base of a 500 foot high mountainside cliff. The entrance is a pair of 50’ tall adamantine doors. The doors are airtight valves sealed by magic.’ 
Orsik turns to him, “so this is the only way in?” 
“Probably?” 
The descent into the mines is tedious and slow going. The first few levels are a maze of rough hewn tunnels having an occasional upward or downward slope. Orsik, whose experience in mines, comes in handy, guides the 3HPP deeper into the cave. 
The dwarven fighter thinks, “crappy construction, poor workmanship.” 
After descending some 500 feet, where nothing could be heard, Unla Kee hears the distinct dialect of rock gnomes. 
A strange hissing voice shouts, “don’t fool around! Keep working!” 
“Why aren’t the giants doing the work? Would be way more efficient,” Thistle wonders aloud. 
Thistle and Vardis misremember the little information they have about giants. 
“They suck,” mutters Vardis. 
“Not only do they suck but they suck in a very particular way – they are lazy!” adds Thistle. 
“That does make them relatable, though.”
Orsik turns to the group, “I can see two fire salamanders guarding dwarves and gnomes. We will sneak up and attack.” 
Thistle, hides behind Orsik who doesn’t notice him. Orsik is silent as a mouse. Unla Kee makes nary a sound.
Vardis, on the other hand, could wake up the dead. 
Orsik growls, “why did we bring you here?” 
And then casts Expeditious Retreat, catches up to one Salamander and strikes it with his sword. 
The heat of the Salamander’s body scorches the dwarf. 
Just then Thistle remembers something that Uncle Joon had said. It wasn’t about their fire damage, or their cold vulnerability but that they are really good smiths. 
Unla Kee steps forward and banishes the Fire Salamander furthest from the group. The creature doesn’t stand a chance, given Unla Kee’s manipulation of portents and fates.
