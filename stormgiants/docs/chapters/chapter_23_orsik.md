# Chapter 23 - Orsik
Thud. 
It was a satisfying thud, thought Chandra Stol. The death of a giant and a nemesis made her smile. And then she remembered that her pet wyvern was dead. 
Not of the body, mind you; Bad Fruul was killed while tied up. No, the sound of swords thudding on his body. 
That was satisfying. 
Raggnar shouts, “What have you done to this town?”
Gundalin is about to agree. 
Orsik, covered in blood, guts, and gore, shouts, “Yes, look what we did, saved your town!” 
Vardis, also covered in dirt, laughs, “and got rid of the wyvern, and got rid of some those architectural eye sores. You can modernize the look and feel of the town!” 
“That wyvern was a disappointment.” 
“Absorbed some blows?”
“Not a single attack landed; even Chandra caused more harm.” 
Meanwhile, the two sprites squeak, “We were more important than the wyvern! Our poisoned arrows saved the day! Bad Fuul was about to kill all of you when he finally succumbed!”
“Yes! We want to thank you for your work,” says a gracious Orsik. " Right, Vardis?” 
“We ever have to choose between a wyvern and sprites; we are so totally taking the sprites!” 
The Sprites are delighted and start tumbling, shouting, “Sprites FTW! Sprites FTW!” They high-five and start praising each other – “It was all you! No, you! You! You!” 
Blasius emerges at this point from his hideout, pulls out a flask, and shouts to Unla Kee – “my friend, my good friend, I have some alcohol. Some fine wine, would you like some?” 
“Yes.” 
Orsik stares – “You’re drinking anything today?”
“It’s a risk.” 
Blasius approaches Orsik, “My brother, my friend, my hero! This is a glorious day!” He waves his hand over the battlefield, and it is at this point that the cost of the battle is obvious. Most of Orsik’s militia is dead, half the town is devastated, the ground has seeped with blood, the air stinks with the smell of flesh, and the flies have started to come to feast. 
Orsik shouts, “Later.” 
Chandra, who was watching everything play out near the well, appears. 
“You were going to be the new head of the guard.” 
“But I never wanted the job, Orsik.” 
“Well, good, because after the performance of the wyvern, the job was no longer yours.”
“Win-win for everyone,” snarks Vardis. 
“My wyvern is dead,” Chandra sobs, “but, Orsik, he fought heroically?” 
“Nope.” 
“Yes, he did!” 
“You’re right; he did stand there and allow himself to be killed. 
Vardis blurts - He was a bold distraction
And Orsik completes - but I wouldn’t call that fighting.
“You’re a mean person,” the local cleric sobs.
“Hey, good outcome. You’re alive. We’re alive. The town is – saved.” 
At that point, the few remaining militia members, Raggnar, Gundalin, and the rest of the group that has internalized the scale of death and destruction stare at Orsik in disbelief.  
“We can rebuild the town. No problem.” 
And Vardis asks, “How many casualties did you suffer?”
“Half the militia, I think,” mumbles Ragnar.
“Well, civilian?” 
Chandra erupts in anger, “Mr. Vardis, I am not sure how things work in your big cities, and I don’t really care. But in this town, the distinction between civilians and militia is lost. These were our friends, our family, and our townspeople.” 
With that, Chandra, Ragnar, and Gundalin begin to argue about what will happen to the town and how to fix it. 
Everyone else decides to just sit down and breathe. Exhausted from fighting and just happy to be alive. 
Orsik takes that moment to sneak over to Bad Fruul. He pulls out a headband he had won in a game of cards while still a guard in the city. “I’ll swap this with that one on the giant’s head, and all will be well.” 
 After all, he’s never trusted the SEER. And he’s never trusted the pseudodragon. No one notices him as he approaches the giant’s head, so he smiles. He pulls the headband off Bad Fruul, stares at it, and thinks, “The SEER set up this complicated plan that almost killed me to find this band. And I am supposed to believe she isn’t going to use it for some evil purpose? No. I am going to swap it with this other band.” 
Unfortunately, Orsik spent too much time in his internal monologue and didn’t notice Thistle fly up and see him with the two bands. 
But Orsik may have gotten away with it if he hadn’t taken the other headband from between his legs and fallen on the ground. Orsik grumbles and stands up. He puts the cursed one in his mouth, picks the fake one from the ground, and tries to shove it on the giant’s head. 
Thistle sees the dwarf with two headbands and says, “Um.” 
“Look, Thistle, I don’t think we should give this headband to the SEER. That’s a bad plan.” 
“She said she would discuss it.”
“Why should we trust her?”
“She said we would get it back.”
“yeah?”
“Has she broken her promises? She’s led us into danger for no good reason without warning, but I don’t know if she’s broken her promises yet.” 
Orsik thinks momentarily, “Look, this artifact has an evil soul. How do we know she will not just do something with that soul? Like, what if she has plans?” 
At this point, Chandra explodes, “That soul has been tormenting my family for too long,” and waves her hand at the destruction, “Did this? Have the SEER get rid of it!” 
“You believe the SEER is going to do that?” 
And just then, Unla Kee says, “I want the soul in me.” 
Thistle turns – “you want what?” 
“To be a vessel? Is that what I am hearing?” 
“Yes, Vardis.” 
Vardis looks at him, confused.
“With the soul, I will have access to the magic of the drow and the lore of a 500-year-old wizard. Of course, I want that!” 
At this point, Vardis notices the other headband, “where did you get that?” 
“Not important.” 
At this point, things get very upsetting for Orsik. Everyone wants him to hand over the headband to the SEER, and no one wants to explain why they trust her. 
First, Chandra accuses him of putting her life in danger with his decision not to give it up, and when Orsik asks her why she trusts the SEER who put her life in danger, she sneers at him. 
Then, one of his guards claims that he’s putting the town at risk by not doing what the SEER wants. When Orsik asks why his guards trust the SEER more, the guard rolls his eyes as if it’s the dumbest question you could ask.  

Wallace is the next person to try to convince Orsik, and he points out that the SEER is an essential member of the Lord’s Alliance, that the town needs their support, and that he should be willing to give the headband to the SEER. Orsik is confused; Wallace wants to provide the SEER with the headband. The same SEER who put the town in danger, whose plots almost destroyed the city. So he asks why and once again is dismissed.  
At this point, Hsing shows up, and the gold pseudodragon demands that he hand over the headband. When the townspeople and his allies side with that creature, Orsik is fed up. They won’t listen to him, and what they want makes no sense. 
Orsik is confused and upset. No one answers his question, and they treat his concerns as if they don’t matter. Everyone acts as if he’s the bad guy for not trusting some stranger who put the town in acute danger. All his hard work to save the city is being ignored, and he’s being treated like another dumb sword. He had poured his heart and soul into this town and its people, who were willing to trust some strange woman over him. 
He had invested all of this time and energy in being redeemed for all the things he had done wrong, and at the very end, he was marginalized and distrusted. This was his redemption, his chance to turn it around, and still, nobody trusted him enough to answer his question. 
“I’m done.” He drops the magical object and marches out of the town.
Everyone stares at him as he marches off. 
Thistle flies up to him, “done with Parnast?”
“No, done with everyone. I am out.” 
“Wait, wait, what’s the problem?”
“I never liked the pseudo dragon. And I don’t know what this ring is; all we know is what this strange lady who put our lives and the town in danger tells us. And we plan to hand it over to her even though I don’t trust her. And nobody thinks the fact that I don’t trust her is important. And I can’t do anything to get anyone to trust me, so I am done!” 
Ragnar, racing after Orsik and overhearing him, says, “My dear friend. You make some excellent points.”
Racing after Orsik, Chandra mumbles, “What do we really know about the Lord’s Alliance?” 
“What good have they brought us? What I understand from Wallace and Orsik is that they were the ones whose machinations resulted in all this death and destruction to our town,” says a Gundalin, out of breath. 
Thistle says, “They did defeat the Cult of the Dragon?” 
Chandra shrugs, “so did the Zhentarim. They didn’t even see how they could profit. They figured they either help defeat Tiamat or get devoured by a five-headed dragon.” 
At this point, everyone is beginning to see Orsik’s point. 
Hsing is annoyed and shouts, “Do you doubt the SEER? She’s the top spymaster in the whole world.” Hsing pauses, “I guess you shouldn’t trust a spymaster. They do lie.” 
Orsik is smug; even Hsing sees the absurdity of trusting the SEER.
“So what are we going to do with this?” 
And just then, a door materializes, and the SEER steps through; my dear Thistle, give it to me!” 
“No, we won’t.”
“Why not step into my garden?” 
“Nope.” 
Orsik glares and points at her with the cursed ring, “What are you going to do with the evil soul?” 
The SEER laughs. 
“My dear boy, send it to where it was supposed to go.” She stares at Orsik and realizes that he’s changed profoundly. “My dear Orsik, you want to ensure I don’t do something with that soul and endanger this community! Why, then, I will perform the ritual in this town tomorrow morning!”
Orsik stares, confused, at the wall from which the SEER had just appeared.
Later that evening, the town throws a party, with the usual carousing, cheering, crying, drinking, and eating. 

An unexpected guest, Prince Thornacious, informs Thistle that he has mentioned him to Queen Tatiana of the Seelie court and that she was pleased. 
“You are being noticed!” 
Thistle is confused, “is that good?” 
“To be noticed by the Queen?” the Prince laughs, “that is all we should ever want.” 
“I just want my beloved.” 
A drunk Blasius and Unla Kee are still singing in the morning when the SEER arrives.
“Well, let’s get this done.” 
And a few moments later, Orsik is holding a headband of intellect.
As the sun sets, Wallace approaches his mentor and announces, “I’m going to call it quits. Elspeth and I decided to hook up, and the town needs a new guard captain. And…” 
Orsik grunts his approval and sheds not a single tear.
A few days later, Orsik and the other companions begin their long trip to Triboar to find Uncle Joon.
There is one slight difference: Orsik, who had the intelligence of an ox, now has a genius-level intellect. He was the only one who wanted the headband. 
As he marches forward, he thinks, “Well, my dear Orsik, it would appear this was an utterly unexpected outcome. Yes, I know you had this clever little plan to redeem yourself in the eyes of that old dwarf. To be a hero, not an outcast, you pulled that off without me. It was touch and go, but you did it. You completed a redemption arc. I am almost shocked that you could do that without me. I suppose redemption doesn’t need intellect, just a good heart. Yes, you probably have one. And then you had this insane plan with the headband, but this is better. A much better plan.” 
He remembers some old stories he had heard that had made no sense, and he smiles, “Yes, I am going to enjoy knowing more about the world and its history.”
He mumbles loudly, “I wonder if I should become a mage?” 
A few moments later, he hits himself on the forehead, “Oh, that’s why people kept getting upset at that thing.”
