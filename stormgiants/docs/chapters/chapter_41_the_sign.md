# Chapter 41 - The Sign
## 9th day of the 3rd 10 tenday of Flamerule 
### Harshnag the Grim
Harshnag stood at the edge of the small village on the route to the Oracle. He could see clearly the signs that giants had been here. The houses were destroyed with rocks, and bodies littered the grounds.
“We must go.” 
The one known as Thistle agreed, but Orsik would have none of that. There was a treasure to be found. 
Harshnag sat down and said, “I will not help you anymore.”
Orsik glared and charged in with the rest of the 3HPP. 
Although the ambush was well thought out, the heroes defeated. 
With the Crag Cats dead, Harshnag again shouted, “We must go.” 
And again, Orsik disagreed. And his friend Vardis stripped the pelts from the corpses of the monstrous cats. 
“Now!” thundered Harshnag. 
And again, Orsik objected - “We need to see if there are any survivors or treasure.” 
Harshnag sat down and began to pray. He had seen enough. 
Off in the distance, two frost giants were approaching. He would not fight to defend the 3HPP from their folly, they would either survive the combat, or he would bring their bodies to Krowen Valharrow and the mage would find new heroes. 
He overheard Thistle plead with the rest of them to flee. But Vardis would not leave his buddy. And the crazed mage was convinced that he had nothing to fear. He could not be killed because he alone knew how to save the world from the end. 
The fairy’s pleas fell on deaf ears. 
Harshnag’s pleas were scoffed at. 
So Harshnag left the village, and he said - “if you live, then you are the right hero. I cannot interfere.”  
Soon rocks flew. The Grim overheard Orsik scream for help. And the Frost Giant hero ignored the dwarf and prayed to the All-Father. 
As he closed his eyes and prayed, he could hear the sound of fireballs roaring into life. The ground shuddered from the sound of rocks falling. 
He sighed. He knew that his quest was coming to an end. 
And then he heard the most surprising of sounds, the body of a giant falling to the ground. 
“Can it be?” he wondered. And he prayed harder to the All-Father. 
He then heard the whooping of the 3HPP. 
A few minutes later, he opened his eyes and smiled, “They are the heroes.”
