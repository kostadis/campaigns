# Chapter 08 - Encounters on the Black Road
## Azam’s Journal 2/1 of Taraskh
The travel to Parnast is finally on its way. I had four wagons with three laden with goods. One carried the food, another the water and a shipment of medicinal herbs, another wagon had a shipment of weapons, and another wagon had the statue of Angharradgh. 
Travel in the desert is slow, hot and full of blistering sun. There are no clouds, no promise of water anywhere. 
It is crucial that we travel well before sunrise, leveraging the light of the moon that makes the dunes look like mountains of diamonds. 
We walk until sunrise where there is a gorgeous spill of pink and gold across the horizon. 
From sunrise until noon, we look for shade. 
At noon we must rest from the baking sun pounding on our skulls like a forge.
When the sun is setting we head out until it is time to eat. 
And then sleep. 
Such is the daily routine of the traveler.
The middleperson’s retainers are a curious bunch. A fairy who seems bent on revenge. Then there is the curious pair of the dwarf Orsik and the elf Vardis. Orsik, Hsing tells me, has done things that have made him unemployable. Vardis’ fellow clerics will not allow him in their temples. And then there is Unla Kee who is convinced that the fate of the world sits on his willingness to take risks. 
Hsing tells me that these are the heroes that he is looking for. 
I shrug. 
They arrived in Vuethel stinking of sweat, covered in sand, sunburned and exhausted. They looked every bit the raggedy adventurer who is down on their luck. I had hoped one of the factions might have sent an agent, but instead we get these independents. 
But last night, the faith Hsing had in them might be justified.
The Thistle, the fairy, proposed that he sleep during the day, and guard during the night. And his decision was wise indeed. 
I had gone to sleep leaving him and his friends to guard the caravan. The nighttime is bitterly cold and dry. Camel spiders, sand scorpions, snakes and large eared hopping mice come out at night to casually murder each other. Sometimes these creatures get into the tents. 
I was awakened by the shouts of the fairy, and the sounds of goblins charging. When I opened my eyes, I could see the moonlight glinting off the steel of the goblins’ weapons. 
A pack of goblins attacked us. They were after our goods and our water. But the one known as Thistle saw them, and before they were able to steal anything, two goblins were dead, and their boss and his foul steed. 
The other two fled from the battle. Thistle argued that they should pursue them to the ends of the earth, but the one known as Vardis objected. The rest of the night passed quietly and our journey continued. 
## Report from Fzoul Slovis 
Report on the state of the Black Road from 3/1 of Taraskh
Increasing reports had come in that the Goblin, Hobgoblin, and Bugbears of the area have been stirring up trouble along the Black Road up to the Greypeaks. 
On our journey from Parnast we saw a toll being set up on the edge of the desert. It is manned by hobgoblins that are decently armed and armored. They were too many for us to fight. I enclose their location and recommend that we deal with them. Our goal was to collect information, not clear the road of threats and so we paid the toll of 15 GP per camel. An expense report has been filed. 
We encountered a caravan near Vuerthyl that had been attacked by Goblins. These goblins lacked the sophistication of the attacks of the earlier groups. 
Note: I have said this many times, but this is further proof that the Zhentarim leadership must take a firmer stand on the Goblin threat to our trade on the black road. Things have gotten worse, and the threat cannot be ignored forever. We will not be taken seriously if Goblins threaten traders. I believe the time to take a more systematic approach of extermination of the Goblin menace must be considered. The piece meal attempts have emboldened them. 
We did find a potential new recruit, Orsik. He is a dwarf who has been accused of doing many things that makes him unemployable as a guard. The Zhentarim may find him useful. I intend to recruit him at the earliest opportunity.
