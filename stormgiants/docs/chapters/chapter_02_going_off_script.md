# Chapter 02 - Going off script
# Grrrk
We sat in silence, basking in our role as parts of this great pyramid. And we enjoyed the quiet and our serene existence.
One of the younger rocks, Prrrt was complaining about its feet. Why I remember when I was a young rock, how I had rock mites that tore into me, but I didn’t complain, I didn’t fidget. A Galeb Duhr of my generation knows how to do their job. 
As we sat there in the great pyramid, we noticed a fairy, a dwarf, a gnome and an elf. And we noticed how they fought the badgers that hid in the rocks. 
And then the fairy flew tickled my nose with its wings, and we talked. I told it about what I had seen – giants and kobolds in great numbers. 
Prrrt, of course, whined about his mites. But the fairy was happy to help, and Prrrt told them what the dead giant had said.
And they left to the top, I was sure that they would be dead, but Prrrt felt otherwise. He was young and optimistic. And he was right! They didn’t get zapped like the giant. I smiled! And then I heard them climb down into the pyramid. And then they disappeared. 
Enough excitement for this century, I think.
## The group

Vardis reads the Valdorra’s note - **. ***The source I questioned about this cache said that it was carefully hidden behind a secret entrance in the wall of a green-sided mountain. There is only one of those in the Greypeak range, according to my research. The source also said that the cultist who hid the treasure was an expert in magical curses*.
“So guys, why would we want to go get shrunk like this cute bear? What’s the thought process?” 
Thistle sighs, “he’s so cuute!” 
“Why are we going to this place?” asks Orsik.
“Because treasure!” pipes up Thistle. 
Unla Key muses, “what if we went off script? Why should we let this dead treasure hunter set our fate? What if we did something different?”
“Yeah, like get something that fights magical curses,” agrees Vardis
The group stares at its treasure to see whether they have enough money. Their kitty is almost bare: a ledger of the cult of the dragon, 100 gold coins, a necklace of copper charms and a scroll of comprehend language. 
Orsik pulls a map, and says, “Llorkh. It’s ruled by a bandit king. We had a bit of trouble of finding employment with the guards, but they didn’t kick us out. Parnast is the other town nearby, but it’s smaller.”
Thistle Wendrod whispers, “according to my uncle, Llorkh was at the western end of the black road that the Zhentarim controlled through the desert. It used to be the last outpost of that group.” 
Vardis adds, “I recall that treasure hunters met at the local tavern exchanging tall tales, and tribesmen from the great desert would show up with strange artifacts to sell.”
Orsik turns to the gnome, “where to?” 
Unla Key rolls a dice, and announces, “Llorkh.” 
After a short rest, the group heads out, and Grrrk, Prrrt shout out, “nice seeing you!” 
Vardis shouts back, “Nice to see you! Thanks for the treasure!” 
Prrrt shouts back, “Wasn’t ours! And thank you so much for dealing with the beetles!” 
Then the gruff voice of Grrrk is heard, “alright back to your positions.” And with that the Galeb Duhr are invisible again.
Before the group heads out, they look around and in unison shout, “that way!” 
The day is hot and sunny for the spring in the Greypeak mountains, but the whole atmosphere just feels so pleasant.
Until they sniff a pretty nasty wiff. Unla Key pauses, wondering what the smell is, but Vardis and Orsik plow on. “It’s a stench, why do we care?” 
Vardis and Orsik have been in smellier places, but the tender noses of Unla Key and Thistle are not comfortable with such a stench and start puking. Now it turns out that fairy’s when they vomit, release a staggering amount of vomit. In fact, the physics of how so much liquid escapes their body is damn near incomprehensible. 
As the two ruffians drag their fellow travelers out, four bandits surround the group. 
“Hand me your treasure or die!” 
“Good news there is none!” shouts Orsik. 
The bandits are unconvinced, “surely you and your friends must have something? You look like the type to be looking for treasure of the Cult of the Dragon that is hidden in these hills!” 
Unla Key turns to Orsik, “I don’t want to hand over anything.” 
Orsik agrees. Channeling every officer, sergeant that had ever screamed at him, in disgust he says, “back off! We don’t have any treasure! You’re easy to slaughter. Go away!” 
Unla Key whispers to him, “and hand us your treasure!” 
Orsik growls back softly, “don’t push it!” 
The bandit leader seeing this turn of events whispers to his friends, and shouts, “never let it be said that old-one-eye doesn’t have a soft spot for down on their luck mercenaries!” The bandits then melt away into the rocks. 
A few hours more of scrambling through rocks in the foothills of the Greyspeak mountains and the group ends up near the road to Llorkh. 
The sun is setting, and they breakout camp. Vardis and Orsik sleep as far way from everyone else while still be close enough to respond to an attack. In their minds, their not sure whether respond is flee, join in or defend.  Unla Key sleeps in his own corner.  Thistle cuddles his new pet bear friend and falls asleep feeling warm and loved. 
## Eden “Gleeful” Gleeson
“Oh, my goodness, I forgot a present for my beloved!” Eden has been traveling for several days to attend to his wedding, and he just remembered that he forgot to buy a wedding gift. Ever since he left Llorkh he despaired of finding any gift.
As he mumbles about his fate on the road east, he sees ahead a fairy, an elf, a dwarf and gnome. 
“Hello!”
“Not interested” and “goodbye” they shout back to him.
He is desperate and shouts back, “No, you don’t understand, I am going to my wedding and forgot to get my bride a wedding gift! Surely you must have something for sale?” 
Vardis shouts, “a copper necklace for 500?”
“No price is too high for my bride, but **that** is too high” 
“A magnifying glass with no glass?” asks Orsik.
“Perhaps something shiny, goldy?” 
“A polished gold piece we will sell for two?” Unla Key proposes. 
“A giant silver nose ring?” asks Vardis. 
“That will do just fine! I will turn it into a necklace!”
“What were you thinking?” 
And before he knows it, Vardis and Eden Gleeson are haggling. 
“200 GP!” 
“200? I would not be able to pay for my wedding”
“Sell one of your servants!” 
“They are in my employee; they are not my slaves! How dare you suggest I am part of the slave trade!” 
“150 seems fair,” shouts Vardis.
“You drive a hard bargain!” 
At that point his manservant Cisco steps forward. Cisco has an eyepatch and a small teddy bear that he cradles in his arms. “Eddie, you can’t make these impulsive purchases! You just ate into the whole desert budget!”

Later on,in the evening a few miles out of Llorkh, four wolves emerge from the trees on either side of the road and attack our adventurers. Unla Key is killed. Orsik kills a wolf. Thistle waits for another wolf to run out of the woods, and misses.  That wolf clamps down it’s teeth on Orsik and bites air. And then things take a turn for the worse, when Vardis is brought to death’s door by another wolf. Orsik stares at Unla Key and Vardis and decides to revive the mage. 
Orsik turns to Unla Key, “don’t make me regret my choice to heal you!” 
Unla Key casts a thunder wave and the wolves are blasted away. 
Unfortunately, the third wolf brings Thistle to death's door. 
Orsik kills a wolf. They are now down to 2 combatants on either side. 
The wolf over Vardis body, seeing that the battle is too evenly balanced, takes Vardis’ bow and runs into the forest. The other wolf over Thistle’s body takes his arrows and runs.
Orsik starts to scream, “Fucking wolves! We killed two of theirs, they killed two of ours. This was an embarrassing encounter. We lost a healing potion. We got no treasure! AND WE LOST A BOW!” 
The group takes a short rest under the trees where the wolves had staged their ambush. 
Feeling somewhat refreshed, the group heads out again and arrives towards sunset at Llorkh. 
Llorkh is a small town dominated by a keep on a hill. It has a single main road, and a few offshoots to either side. 
## The Guards of the Iron Circle -  Captain Jolanth

“Harumph, newcomers!” he mumbles as he sees an elf, a dwarf, a fairy and a gnome trudge up to town. 
Jolanth has been in Llorkh and managed to survive the Cult of the Dragon, and the decline in Zhentarim traffic after certain unfortunate events. He’s thought about leaving, but the pay is good, and he likes the weather. 
He stands in the middle of the road – “who goes there!” 
The gnome shouts back at him, “we are we!” 
“This one is funny,” he thinks.  “We are the Guards of the Iron Circle, and we protect this town. Those who disrupt the peace, are sent to the keep!” he shouts and points to the castle. “We managed to survive that nasty business with the Dragons, and we will not tolerate any disruption to the peace!” 
Orsik cuts him off, “we were almost killed off by a pack of wolves. You don’t have to worry about us.” 
Jolanth stares at Orsik and thinks, “doesn’t the fool know I know he is too weak to present a challenge to the guard! I don’t often get to lord it over the passers-by!” 
“This is a safe town, and I want to keep it that way!” 
Orsik smiles, “so that means if we are in danger, you will protect us? Is that what I heard?”
Jolanth is stunned, this was unexpected. He is flustered. “Well, if the mission to protect the town happens to require saving you,” 
But Vardis cuts him off, “free protection! I like it!” 
Jolanth stares in shock as the four walk past him. 

## The group 
“So, let’s see,” says Orsik. “This town, if I recall, has a tavern, a store and a temple.”
Vardis mumbles, “I can go to that one.” 
“You know what, let’s go to the tavern.” 
As they enter the only tavern in town, they notice that the bartender is a ghost.
It sees them and shouts, “hello!”
Vardis stares, “you can talk?”
“Yes, why?”
“Your condition?”
At that point Vardis notices that the tavern is full of local patrons who tense up with that question. 
“So, what will you have?” 
“What do you have?” 
“Ale, roasted turkey, a puff pastry and a pine nut salad!” 
“Is the pine nut salad vegan?”
“Yes!”
“Then I’ll have one of those.” 
Vardis stares at the ghost thinking how it is that the ghost cares about money. And that everyone is behaving as if this is totally normal. 
The rest order some food and drink and sit down. While waiting for it to be delivered, the group takes in the scene in the tavern. It's full of Guards of the Iron Circle. 
Vardis turns to Orsik, “you know they would protect us!” 
Orsik smiles, “hey ghost, how did you get to be ghost?” 
The bartender looks at him, “I am not a ghost.” 
Meanwhile everyone else in the bar starts to find a way to leave or brace themselves for what will come next. 
Orsik presses on, “No, you really ARE a ghost!” 
The ghostly bartender transforms from a kindly barkeep into a monstrous creature that terrifies Orsik. A few moments later he has aged 30 years. 
Orsik expects to see the guards come to his defense, but they have already fled the establishment. 
When he finally comes out, Diego Malfoy, a guard screams, “are you mad? We never tell him that he’s a ghost!” 
“Well, you could have told me when we came into town.” 
“Most people aren’t that stupid.” Diego pauses, “wait what is your name?” 
Orsik realizes that his reputation has preceded him. 
“No, I am not.” 
“You are Orsik! And that one is Vardis! You wanted a job, and you couldn’t even show up sober on your first day!” 
“Look, we’re nobodies.” 
Vardis interrupts the discussion, and asks, “where can we find a scroll of dispel magic?” 
“Oh, at the local store, The Lionel Coster outpost.” 
Just then a man appears, almost out of nowhere. He’s wearing boots, breeches, a sturdy belt with a steel buckle, a shirt and over that a brown cloak with a hood. 
“I saw your courage there and have an offer of employment.” 
Orsik rolls his eyes, “you saw a bunch of fools poke a ghost.” 
“Well, I don’t have a lot to people to pick from. And the clock is ticking.” The man sighs, “Do you want the job? It pays.”
Vardis smiles, “why didn’t you start with that?”
