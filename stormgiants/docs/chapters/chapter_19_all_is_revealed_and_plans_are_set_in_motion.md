# Chapter 19 - All is revealed and plans are set in motion
## Orsik 4th day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul 

The heroes arrive at the Golden Tankard, tired, bedraggled and exhausted. Ragnar, Gundalin, and Chandra are waiting for them inside. 
“So?” asks Chandra.
“No, mother,” grouses Orsik.
“Charred meat it is.” 
Orsik perks up and requests that the captain of the guard feed the Wyvern. Chandra stares at him, smiles, and, with a gesture of noblesse oblige, tells him to go ahead.
A few minutes later, Orsik stares as the Wyvern uses the thigh bone as a toothpick. The dwarf finds the sight quite relaxing. 
As they walk away, Orsik pats Wallace on the back and says, “someday, you’ll be feeding people to the Wyvern.” 
Wallace stares at him funny. 
As the group heads towards the Golden Tankard, a few local villagers come screaming that a horde of orcs is coming towards the town from the south.  
Then a corporal in the militia appears and shouts, “boss man, I think there is a group of orcs coming from the south. They may have followed you.” 
Orsik pulls his sword out and tells his men to follow him as he sneaks behind the Tavern from the east. He charges when they are all in position, screaming at the top of his lungs. Except there are no orcs. 
“Bossman, I promise you there were orcs!” 
Thistle carefully evaluates the situation and perceives no tell-tale signs of lying – “they were telling the truth about the orcs.” 
Ragnar and Gundalin appear outside, ready to fight the orcs, but they see nothing. As Orsik is about to laugh it all off, he sees an image of an ornate door in his mind with Shou’s writings. He turns to the rest of the group and can tell they see the same thing. 
The door then appears in the wall of the Golden Tankard. 
And then another image pops into his, an idea of him opening the door.
All of his companions are clearly caught up in the same mental image. While he rejects the suggestion, Unla Kee rushes, muttering, “when I stop taking risks, the word ends!” As he opens the ornate door, a small white bird flies out. 
Thistle stares at the bird, thinking it is crucial. The sound of hundreds of birds coming from the octagonal room behind the door convinces him otherwise. 
As Orsik stares at the door, Hsing, the golden blind pseudo dragon, beckons him to go in. 
Orsik refuses.
Hsing conjures up images of death in cities that the Lord’s Alliance protects as a warning to Orsik if he doesn’t enter the door. Orsik shrugs, “maybe if you had been nicer, I would listen to you.” 
Orsik continues to refuse. Vardis and Wallace stay with Orsik. 
Thistle shrugs and heads in. 
The villagers stare at Orsik, who is not entering. And the militia stares at him. Orsik realizes he’s close to doing something he won’t discuss. So, he calls on his militia to follow him. 
Once inside the door, he sees an octagonal room covered by shelves that hold scrolls, books, and birds. One of the militia stares slackjawed at the variety and beauty of the creatures that are calmly preening themselves or eating small dishes of bird seed. 
A large desk takes up a large portion of the room, and on it is a map of the Sword Coast that Unla Kee and Thistle are carefully examining. The map is held down with small marble statues of Shou gods. 
Thistle smiles at Orsik, “this map might help you find your map.” 
Vardis glares, “we have seen many maps. “
The double doors across the entrance open into what appears to be a garden. Standing at the door is a 50’ish-year-old Shou woman wearing a blue robe embroidered with birds. She is holding a silver staff topped with a crane’s head, whose eyes are made of crystal. 
She shouts, “you aren’t in Parnast anymore.” 
Unla Kee grabs a cookie on a silver tray and heads out through the doors. Thistle follows. Orsik stares glumly until she hears him say – “ be a dear, and then grab the tea cookies.” 
Orsik turns to one of his militiamen and tells them to carry the tray. 

## SEER 4th  day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul 

*Unla Kee came through the door! Perhaps the world’s end is not as close at hand as I feared. But the recent events are cause for alarm. The giants are engaged in a civil war to win favor with their gods. And these battles between the giant tribes will not end well for us all. *
She sighs. 
*Time to do that thing, where I demonstrate the reach of our agents and our information by sharing snippets of information from our files about their private lives. *
Your Uncle Joon is still in Triboar. As for your nemesis Adirond Kroskas, he’s disappeared into the North. And for your beloved Laela, we heard rumors that she lives. One of our agents suggested that some allies of Fire Giants had captured her. 
Thistle stares.
She turns to Orsik and whispers, “I know about the … ” The last word is too indistinct for anyone but Orsik. He turns to her and growls, “that’s not me anymore, and we don’t talk about it.” 
And then smiles at Unla Kee, “I am so happy to see you that you enjoyed the cookies. The world’s end may require that you take significant risks, Mr. Kee. I fear that the entire North may end up in chaos if you and others don’t.” 
Her eyes twinkle, “and I heard you own a tower.” 
Finally, she turns to Vardis, “there is a solution to the map. But part of the fun of a treasure map is trying to figure it out. Here’s what I know: your map’s location is that of an isle that will appear but has not yet.” 
Vardis stares at her, “is that the most useless clue of all time or what?”
*Well, I think that worked. They seem impressed.*
“I have manipulated events for some time, and now I need you to actively participate in my plans.” 
Orsik is about to storm out, but the door is gone. 
“Over the last 30 years, an objective of my organization has been to find Narcellia Varam’myr, a priestess of Lolth who has been trapped for several hundred years in a *headband of intellect. *A follower of Angharradh – the elven deity of spring - named Luistarra imprisoned Narcelia for an attack that destroyed Luistara’s village near Evereska. We had a good lead, but during the rise of the Cult of the Dragon, we lost her and have been unable to divine the location of the headband. We knew it was somewhere near the Greypeak mountains, but where? So we came up with a new plan; we would flush her out by encouraging Chandrfa Stol to seek a replacement for the statue of Angharradh. The statue in Parnast had been defaced by the last owner of the headband. We knew that if it was replaced, she would come back to destroy it. To make that happen, we provided the inspiration for an artist in the Anauoch Desert to create the statue and for Chandra to find him.”
She notices that Vardis wants to interrupt with questions and scowls, “I am not done with the opening remarks. Can you please wait until I am done?” 
She continues, “In parallel, we discovered that there was a way for us to scry out the owner’s location. The magical tomes you provided us from the Marsh of Chelimber provided a ritual that we were able to use it. 
And we discovered that the owner of the headband was Bad Fruul. Narcellia has been urging the hill giant to increase his wealth and girth. She taught him the basics of arcane magic. And with the collapse of the Ordning creating order among the Giants, Narcellia convinced Bad Fruul to step out of his comfort zone and draw monstrous humanoids to his banner. 
Unfortunately, our plots to flush out Narcellia have made Parnast a target. Kragota Threeteeth’s agents and farmers revealed the existence of the new statue. Bad Fruul, urged by Narcellia, has decided to destroy Parnast. 
Worse, we have learned that Chandra Stol has a particular pendant that was owned by her ancestor Luistarra. Narcellia has particularly gruesome plans for the cleric.”
Orsik is appalled. The militia turns to Orsik for what to do. 
Everyone else is wondering what the job is. 
“You have unwittingly helped us create a disaster, putting the village at risk and Chandra Stol. We need you to protect her at all costs, determine when Bad Fruul will attack and defend the city of Parnast, and finally bring me the headband.” 
Unla Kee stares at her dejectedly. 
“And to make it a little bit more worth your while, I offer 600 gold for trying, an extra 200 if no one dies, and an extra 100 if you can keep Chandra safe.” 
At this point, Vardis asks – “what is the Ordning?” 
“The Ordning is the social structure of the Giants that keeps them in check and preserves the peace. When it is dissolved, every Giant tribe and leader will attempt to grow their power at the expense of all other Giants. The goal is to be the new leader after the Ordning is re-established. We do not know who or what dissolved the Ordning. Just that it is dissolved.” 
She then pauses, “some more tea and cookies?” 
Unla Kee takes some nice. 
As the door rematerializes, she walks them to the outside. Just as Unla Kee is about to stop outside, she smiles and says, “here you go.” 

She smiles as he takes the book, “prophecies of the apocalypse.” 
## Vardis
Once through the doors, Vardis turns to his old friend. 
“So what are we going to do, Orsik?” 
“Obviously, protect Chandra, tell her about her pendant, reveal everything. I will make it my personal mission to protect her.”
“I would like to assassinate Bad Fruul.” Vardis stares at him, “I mean not on my own but as a group action.” 
Unla Kee smirks, “I am cool with an assassination.” 
Vardis nods. 
Wallaces, “acts what about protecting the village?” 
Orsik turns to the militia and shouts, “this is what I have trained you to do!” 
“Where?” 
Vardis snorts. 
“Where?” 
Vardis watches as Wallace explains that to the northeast of Paranast is old Paranast. It’s more defensible because it used to be a Zhentarim fortress. 
Vardis stares at his friend; this is the kind of decision he does not excel in.
He turns to his friend, and Vardis says, “well, the old city looks sturdier with walls that can be repaired. The problem is that the locals are convinced the old city is cursed. Don’t forget they felt the towers Ragnar set up burned down because they included logs from the old town.” 
Orsik looks predictably confused, “I need ideas, suggestions?” 
Thistle observes that the old city is a better defensive position.
But Orsik flips out, “tell them to defend the old Zhentarim stronghold and leave their homes?” 
Vardis watches as everyone stares at Orsik, talks to himself, and finally spits out, “we will make our defense here.” 
And then proceeds to give orders to dig trenches, build pickets, and fortify existing buildings. He remembers what happened as he fled one of his jobs and shouts, “build some traps, pits.” 
He turns to Vardis, “I know they will wield their weapons and wear their amour well. But I don’t think I was the right guy to teach them how to make weapons or strategies other than standing and fighting. And then the whole basic first aid was nothing I had to learn since we were friends. And I never had any morale that couldn’t be satisfied with some money. Damn, I forgot to tell them to stock up on food! But I know that my presence will give them a will of iron!”
He stops, “what have I done?”
Vardis says, “the best you could have?” 
Thistle says, “don’t worry, Orsik, I trained their archers well.” 
As they walk away, Orsik hears the sound of a bow breaking.
Vardis turns to him and says, “no, he didn’t.” 
## Chandra Stol 4th day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul 

Orsik marches right up to the Temple of Axes and calls out to Chandra. When she appears, her pleasure at seeing her fellow bloody thirsty councilman evaporates as Orsik tells her everything the SEER said. 
Chandra pauses and sighs. They* know about this. *
“It is true. Narcellia has plagued my family with her lust for vengeance for centuries. She was responsible for my mother’s death 30 years ago. After that death, I went into hiding and had not heard about the drow. My ancestor Luistara went mad after Narcelia destroyed her village and used hag magic to bound the drow into the headband.”
She pauses and then says, “perhaps there is something I know that might be useful. Narcellia was deathly afraid of the fey, sprites in particular. You are friends of Prince Thornacious, who might be of some use.”  
Chandra then becomes stoic and says, “You can do nothing for me.” 
All but Orsik shrug and walk away. 
Orsik is offended, “what if I’m not good enough for you?”
As Chandra ponders her action, she overlooks Thistle, who leaves the area, goes and gets some herbs from Syla’s garden, and rubs Orsik.  
Everyone stares at him, and he says, “Ms. Chandra Stol, I totally get that you can’t stand his smell, but see these herbs, and to be quite honest, I can’t. These herbs will help.” 
 Orsik glares. Chandra is impressed with his earnestness and Thistle’s willingness to go the extra mile and says yes, only after she asks Unla Kee if the plan makes sense. Unla Kee looks at her and says, “what plan?”
“To follow a group of adventurers into the forest, leaving my home and friends to go and kill my ancient family nemesis.” 
Unla Kee stares at her for a minute as if he is trying to peer into the hidden layers of truth and says, “if you do not come with us, it will mean the end of the world.” 
With that, she agrees. 
## The group

With Chandra in tow, the group ponders its next move. After some discussion, they decide to get help from Prince Thornacious. 
Unla Kee notes that the safest place for Chandra maybe with the fey, but Orsik considers that an insult to Wallace. 
As they are about to go, Sylas tells them of an antidote his wife used to make to cope with the blue mists of the Weathercote Forest. 
Thistle stares at the ingredients and is confused. Orsik just walks away. And Vardis wonders if the herbs can be smoked. 
Fortunately, Unla Kee stares at the ingredients and realizes how everything must be put together. A few minutes later, Unla Kee has made enough for four of them. 
Realizing that one of them will have to go unprotected, Orsik volunteers. 
Before they head out, the group scans the horizon, checks the weather, talks to some locals, ponders the impossible, and concludes that the most likely date to attack is 4 days hence or the 8th day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul. 

At the forest boundary, Thistle steps forward and says, “I know how to do this.” And he very quickly leads the group to Prince Thornacious’ camp. 
Thistle steps forward and makes his pitch for support. At first, things are going well, and Blasius is eager to help. But then Thistle asks Oblivillish as a hunting master to help him. Oblivillish is outraged and shouts, “We had clarified this point that I am not a hunter! The stags were killed by Unseelie, not by me. You killed those Unseelie to bring these outrageous claims forward again.” 
Thistle tries to clarify, “I didn’t say you killed the Stag, but you are a master hunter!” 
This doesn’t improve things when Prince Thornacious asks, “you are a hunter?” 
“No, sire!” And with that, Thistle is silent.
Prince Thornacious thinks, and Blasius stands and says, “Our highness has decided to help and is sending me and two sprites to fight this Bad Fruul!” 
Chandra Stol seeing that the group is about to leave begs Orsik to convince him to let her stay. Orsik is confused, “so I should stay as well because you need my protection.” She sighs, “no, I just need the fey.” 
Orsik’s pride is hurt, and he asks, “am I not good enough for you?”
