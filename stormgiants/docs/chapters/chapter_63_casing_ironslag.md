# Chapter 63 - Casing Ironslag
With the far Salamander banished, Orsik struck the one within reach. And kills it. 
Vardis rushes in, “DIE! SALAMANDERS!” 
Orsik shakes his head. 
Turning to the newly freed dwarves and gnomes, Orsik tries to get them to help. They all express their desire to flee. 
When Thistle points out they possess a cloud castle, the dwarves and gnomes shout, “Huzzah!” 
Orsik turns to him, “Really?” 
Thistle shrugs and then says – “Just be careful. Stay out of the dark places.” 
“And if someone asks to come in, just say no,” mutters Orsik. 
Finally, one of the Dwarves sees through the innuendo, “There are vampires in the castle?” 
“Only one Vampire and many spawn,” corrects Vardis. 
The dwarves and gnomes decide that going down the stairs is a safer bet and thank their saviors. 
One gnome, however, chooses to stay and introduces herself as Wiri Fleagol. “I have been here for quite some time.”
“Why? Did they take over your village or something? Or did you do something to deserve this?” asks Vardis. 
Wiri ignores the taunt, “Does anyone deserve to be locked up in chains mining for giants?” 
Turning to Thistle, she squints and says, “You are a fairy. I will help you. I know things about it down below. And I will do it for a price.” 
“Everyone wants a cut,” moans Orsik. 
“Money to buy a house in Silverymoon,” taunts Vardis. 
“I don’t want a house there. They are nothing but a bunch of bigoted, self-important, righteous bastards. Do you know what happened? I’ll tell you what happened: when the Orc tribes invaded, Silverymoon chose not to help the Silver Marches. The Dragons weakened them!” 
“Do you want vengeance?” 
The gnome recoils, “No, there are possibly innocent gnomes.” 
“Possibly living.” 
With that, Thistle explains the sordid story of Bob and the play in Silverymoon. However, he leaves out his involvement and, more importantly, makes it sound like House Margaster is to blame. 
But Wiri doesn’t believe a word he says. 
She then turns to Thistle and says, “I can tell you about what’s ahead.” 


“The rail tracks running through these tunnels end at a pair of wooden barriers in the first room of Ironslag proper. Three pillars of black basalt support the ceiling. The bucket chain passes through gaps in the western and eastern walls, its iron buckets dangling a few inches above the floor. A tunnel leading west connects to an iron gantry (18) that overlooks the foundry (34). It’s the job of the enslaved people to keep putting ore into the buckets. There are four dwarves with ankle chains. A fire salamander stands watch.” 
“From there, you go west to the Foundry. This area is scorching, and you will suffer extreme heat if you linger here. A rusted iron gantry clings to the north, west, and south walls 50 feet above the floor of the main foundry, which echoes with the sound of clanging metal, rattling chains, whooshing bellows, and bubbling molten iron. The ceiling looms 40 feet above the gantry. Cable towers support the bucket chain that runs near the path of the gantry.” 
“From there, you go through a southeast entrance to the Great Wheel (20). A fireplace in the middle of the north wall keeps this area warm and lit. An enormous wheel made of iron gears and wooden spokes dominates the room. The wheel is rigged to the bucket chain that travels throughout the forge. Turning the great wheel are ten shield dwarves and ten humans (all [commoners](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16829-commoner)) with iron manacles around their ankles. If you stop the wheel, everyone will come quickly to find out why.” 
The Giants' prisoners' sleeping quarters are south of that room. Iron portcullises seal off this long, foul-smelling hall. Three black basalt pillars support the ceiling, and thirteen 10-foot-tall iron cages line the walls. A padlock hangs from each cage door.
Thistle asks, “Can they fight?” 
“No, they are exhausted and dehydrated from the heat.” 
She then holds her hand, “That will be 20 gold.” 
Orsik shouts, “What a hard bargain you drive.” 
The gnome is puzzled, “Did I ask for too little.” 
“Nope, that was our maximum.” 
She glares at them, “I am beginning to sense that you are not the kind of guys I want to have a deep personal relationship with.” 
“Would you rather be on our good or bad side?” asks a mischievous Vardis. 
“Point taken.” 
She’s about to leave when she says, “I could help you?” 
Orsik convinces her that it will not end well. But Orsik can’t resist asking, “What will you do with the 20 gold? Buy a nice dinner?” 
She glares, “If I am going to tell you about what I am going to do with my money, you need to tell me about your hero.” 
Orsik declares, “My grandfather.” 
She smiles, “Well, I plan to buy a farm and never spend another day underground. Do you have any suggestions?” 
“Not Silverymoon,” says Thistle. 
“Yartar and Triboar are nice,” says Orsik genteelly. 
She pauses and thinks, “I think I’ll try them out. And if you are ever there, come find me.” 
Vardis gets a flash of divine inspiration and remembers that their objective is to get the Conch, not to kill the Giants. 
He asks Wiri, “Do you know where the conch is?” 
She looks at him, “I was a slave, but if I hazard a guess, it’s in his room, which is on the lower level. I never went there.” 
The gnome turns to Orisk. “So you’re Orsik, spelled with a K?” 
“No, a C. K is my derelict son.”
“And if I mention your name, will it be okay?” 
“Just mention the 3HPP. We helped the mayor in Triboar.” 
After she’s gone, the 3HPP thinks about what to do next. 
“We could case the place?” remarks Thistle. “Unla Kee makes me invisible, and then I sneak around until I find the bedroom.” 
“Then we can come up with a plan,” says Orsik. Orsik is pleased. In the past, he was the muscle; now, he is the brain. 
An invisible Thistle flies off and creates this map as he floats around the top part of Ironslag. 
Thistle
“I love sneaking around.” 
He begins his journey by floating through the eastern passage into the foundry, where the two tracks end. 
He scribbles down his observations - 

*A rusted iron gantry clings to the north, west, and south walls 50 feet above the floor of the main foundry. The foundry echoes with the sound of clanging metal, rattling chains, whooshing bellows, and bubbling molten iron. The ceiling looms 40 feet above the gantry. Cable towers support the bucket chain that runs near the gantry's path. *
He also notices four ogres and a bored-looking juvenile giant overseeing them. 
From his perch in the top western corner, he heads south and east through a large hallway to a large room. 
Again, Thistle takes notes. 

*Wide iron gantries extend a short distance into a vast chamber. Two lit braziers illuminate the western gantry, with iron stairs leading down to the floor. The steps are iron grills with holes large enough for a human fist to pass through. The steps cover three-quarters against any ranged attack that must pass through them to hit their target. The northern and eastern gantries are unlit. The ceiling is 40 feet above the gantries, and the floor is 50 feet below. The rattling of the bucket chain fills the room. Four cable towers support the bucket chain as it moves through this area.*
*The Vonindod's helmed head and upper torso hang in midair, held in place by chains bolted to the floor and ceiling. Cranes extending out from the western and eastern gantries have two other fragments of the colossus hanging from them: an enormous greatsword and a giant gauntlet-like hand and forearm. These fragments hang at the same level as the gantries. *
*An iron portcullis is set into the south wall at the top of the stairs, close to another lit brazier. Beyond the portcullis is a dark hallway leading to the prison.* 
Looking down into the room, Thistle observes Duke Zalto playing with his hellhounds, tossing a metal ball. The ball has a body in it.
Thistle examines the cranes and observes that a few well-placed reduced spells could cause havoc. 

From there, Thistle sneaks to the pressing mill. Again, he takes notes:
*Eight orcs work here under the watch of a fire giant. The bucket chain moves from north to south through this room, the iron buckets dangling a few feet above the floor. As buckets of iron ore pass by, two orcs with hooked poles tip the buckets and dump their contents onto the floor. Two more orcs place the ore atop stone slabs, and two more orcs pick out impurities. The last two orcs load the ore back into the buckets and shovel the impurities into piles. A fire burns in a hearth in the middle of the east wall.*
From there, Thistle heads down to the mustering room, where he takes more notes. 
*The walls of this room bear frescoes that depict fire giants forging armor and weapons, marching to war, and binding red dragons in chains. Four basalt pillars support the 40-foot-high ceiling, and iron chandeliers bereft of candles hang above a pair of basalt tables. A fireplace in the north wall provides only warmth and light.*
Three ogres are cheering two Fire Giants who are arm wrestling.
Thistle checks his internal clock and decides to return to Unla Kee and get another invocation of invisibility. 
He then finishes exploring the lower level of Ironslag 
From the mustering hall, he heads down into the kitchen, where he takes some notes 
*Standing between black basalt pillars are two 10-foot-tall basalt tables for food preparation. Underneath the tables are empty iron cauldrons weighing 100 pounds each, and above them are dozens of utensils hanging from iron hooks and chains. Brimskarda, the fire giant duchess, stands in front of a fireplace holding an iron spoon to her lips, sampling soup from a fat cauldron suspended above the flames. Five smoke mephits dance around the cauldron, cackling with glee. Two ogres wearing grease-stained leather aprons stand beside the tables, and twenty goblins scurry about, performing menial chores.*
*Brimskarda wears a gown from a young black dragon’s scaly hide and wings. She has wrapped her luxurious orange hair around a black dragon’s horn, giving her a hairstyle that looks like a curving pillar of flame. *
At some point, Brimskarda shouts to the goblins, who take food down the hallway. Thistle follows them through the door and ends up in the Dining Hall. 
When he enters the dining hall, he notices a room to the northwest and sneaks in. He realizes it’s the war room. 

*A basalt table, its base sculpted to look like a fountain of black fire, stands in the middle of this well-lit room. Bare marble shelves jut from the walls at 10 and 15 feet. Standing against the northwest wall is an iron golem with a rune-carved iron ingot embedded in its forehead*
“No treasure,” he thinks. 

From there, he goes back into the dining room. He remembers he has some poison. He sneaks towards Duke Zalto’s table and tries to pour poison into the liquids. Unfortunately, while trying to pour, he causes a disturbance. 

The nearby goblins look around, but Thistle has managed to fly away quietly. 
The goblins chatter and complain at each other. Thistle infers they must be convinced that one of them made the mess. 

Thistle then reaches an alcove against the East wall. There are two doors. 
“The bedrooms.” 
Thistle thinks about perhaps shrinking the door but then realizes that it will shrink, but it will be too big for him to hold in place and fall, making a considerable noise. Worse, he will stop being invisible. 
So he sighs, and heads back to Unla Kee with his map and notes.
