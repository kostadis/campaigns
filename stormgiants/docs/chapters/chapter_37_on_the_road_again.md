# Chapter 37 - On the Road Again
## Thistle Wendrod
Thistle Wendrod flitters about on the road to Xanthalar’s Keep. As he looks around for any information about his beloved, he reflects on the peculiar conversation his group had with the owner of The Triboar Travelers.
It all started when Orsik wanted to meet with the local Zhentarim agent. As Orsik was now a well-known agent, having acquired quite a bit of renown, Orsik wished to cash in on his popularity. 
The agent was Urlam Stockspool, of fairly conventional Illuskan stock, middle-aged, missing an eye, and caring his allegiance on the eye patch. The cloth had a red-emblazoned symbol of the Zhentarim. 
Urlam was quite happy to see Orsik and, after giving him a kiss on each cheek, agreed to go to the Talking Troll for drinks. 
At the Talking Troll, Urlam arrived with quite a crew. 
Thistle snickered as he remembered Orsik about to object about buying drinks for 10 people and then caving when he realized they were all members of the Zhentarim.
The conversation was going nowhere until Thistle asked about Adimond Kroskas. And then he replayed the conversation one more time. 
“Well, that is a name I haven’t heard in a very long time.”
Thistle fluttered closer, “we’re trying to track him down.”
Urlam looks at the fairy and has this uncomfortable look, “The black network is now all about making money and not trying to bring about the Apocalypse to enable Bane to take over all of creation. We’re a different kind of group. We are focused on profitability, gross margins, and endless growth. Adimond Kroskas used to work with that group.” 
Thistle is confused, “what would the Cult of the Dragon have to do with Bane?”  
The Illuskan trader scratches his head and continues, “Adminond Kroskas was not a member of the Cult. He was a free agent. He was one messed-up dude. People paid him to do things.”
At this point, Thistle is very interested and listens carefully to Urlam, “The stories that I have heard, and these are rumors and speculations, is that if you ever needed somebody to create a hybrid magical creature, he was your guy.” 
“Where can I find the people that knew him?”
“Oh, they are dead. And the people who may know are very senior in the Zhentarim hierarchy. The Zhentarim were involved in the events surrounding the fall of the Cult of the Dragon.” 
Thistle is frustrated, “where would I go to find any information?”
Urlam turns to Kaelen, “More wine.” 
After Kaelen returns with another bottle that Orsik has to pay for, Urlam says, “Silverymoon. They have schools of magic with the greatest libraries.” 
## Orsik
The dwarf’s ears perk up when Thistle asks about House Margaster. 
“Those devil-worshipping pieces of – bovine feces.” 
“We need to take them out.”  
Urlam swirls his glass, sniffs the wine, and says, “It’s not about taking them out. They came here and asked for a discount, and when I tried to object, I got these cultists to show who killed up and killed some of my guards. No made men, but still.”
“We really need to take them out.” 
“I wish the big bosses said they are untouchable.” Urlam pauses and continues,  “I know you talked to Orthovir, and I bet he told you about how to hurt House Margaster. Well, I know he has a relationship with those Margasters.”
Orsik is pissed off and very surprised when the normally taciturn, quiet cleric says, “He set us up.” 
And then Orsik is really pissed off when Thistle asks Urlam if he has any contraband he needs to dispose of. 
Urlam is furious, so Orsik intervenes, “We are having a nice conversation here, and you are bringing up this.” The owner of the Triboar Traveler is about to get testy, so Orsik shouts to Kaelen, “Another round.” 
Thistle backpedals, “We were thinking of using the contraband, that you don’t have to disgrace House Margaster.” 
“I just want them dead.” Urlam then pauses, “That’s not an official Zhentarim position.” 
Orsik smiles, “understood.” 
“I’ll pay you 200 gold?”
“I’d do it for free, but that is pretty low.” 
“Zhentarim friends and family discount?” 
“Sure, I mean, I was going to do it for free.” 
## Unla Kee

While Thistle and Orsik reflect on the barroom conversation while the 3HPP march to Xanthalar’s Keep, Unla Kee thinks about Hyuth. 
Hyuth is Unla Kee’s kind of guy. Hyuth is self-centered, obsessed with his importance, putting his needs above everyone else’s. 
“I like him.”
And then Unla Kee pauses, “I mean, I don’t – like – anyone….”
The halfling reflects silently, “I suppose if the world doesn’t end, I could retire like Hyuth. Big house, terrorizing people, being involved in various manic plots to destabilize the local community.” 
As he thinks about all the different ways he will arrange his house, Orsik, and Vardis declare that now they must rest. 
The fire in the clearing off the side of the road is warm. And Unla Kee is getting ready to sleep when out of the distance, a booming voice exclaims.
“It is I, Harshnag, the Grim of Force Grey.” 
Unla Kee readies his spells. 
Orsik shouts, “Harshnag, I know that guy. He’s a legendary good-aligned frost giant. In taverns throughout the North, he is known as a monster to some and a hero to others. In Waterdeep, he’s remembered as the largest member of Force Grey, a notoriously destructive adventuring company that came to the city’s defense on more than one occasion. Believed to be hundreds of years old, Harshnag wanders the wilderness with greataxe, seeking to lay his evil giant brethren low.” 
Unla Kee is impressed with Orsik’s erudite complete paragraph explanation. And is annoyed when Orsik notices that, and the fighter taps his headband.
The magic-user had much of his identity tied to the fact that he was the brains. 
Not that he was the most intelligent person, but that he was smarter than everyone else so that his evil plots would not be discovered. Orsik was going to make things – ever so enjoyable. 
Harshnag approaches. He looks like a typical frost giant except that he’s clad in plate armor and wears the skull of a white dragon as a helm. His jagged axe also looks quite formidable.
He sits by the fire and says, “I have been looking for people like you. The end of the world is near.” 
Unla Kee smiles. He’s the man with the plan. 
Harshnag continues, “The ordning has been broken, the storm giants no longer have the power to keep the smaller giants in check, and King Hekaton is missing.”
Unla Kee sighs and thinks, “Not the right end of the world.” 
Orsik is confused, and Harshnag explains that the giant world is kept in a strict hierarchical power structure that ensures that the giants do not meddle with the other races. 
“The power structure is often disrupted; when that happens, the giant kings wrestle for control. Their squabbles hurt the other people of the North. Such a thing hasn’t happened in a long time.” 
He then sighs, “I have been looking for you for some time. Krowen Valharrow said you had agreed to take on the quest to discover what was happening with the Giants. I have a proposal.”
Unla Kee stares at him carefully. 
“It might be time to consult with a higher authority. There is a mountain temple built by my ancient ancestors under the Spine of the World called the Eye of the All-Father. According to giant legend, the temple contains a divine oracle. I will guide you to the oracle so that you might learn what is going on.” 
Unla Kee is annoyed, “We were going to claim an award about a scumbag named Weevil who happens to be in Xanthalar’s Keep. Is this that urgent? Do we have to forgo that award?”
Harshnag stares at him, “Oh no. We can claim that bounty. But I will expect my share, a minor 100 gold.” 
The group stares and decides that the 100 gold is a small tax on their 5000 gold. A tax they can live with.
Orsik snickers, “Sure Frosty the Snow Giant!”
