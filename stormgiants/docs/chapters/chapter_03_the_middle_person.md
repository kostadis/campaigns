# Chapter 03 - The middle person

## Fiora Bendaega

“So, are you willing to take the job?” 
The fairy called Thistle stares at him, “would this have anything to do with the cult of the dragon?” 
“Would it help if I said yes?” 
Thistle grabs the note, and while he’s reading it, the middle person explains that he’s but a middle person looking for adventurers for honest tradespeople working the black road. 
Seeing an opening, the middle person tries empathy, “The cult of the dragon was a terrible tragedy. I noticed you were a victim. It was terrible for business.” 
That didn’t quite go according to plan as the fairy glares, “not exactly helping me. I want revenge.” 
The middle person sighs, “I’m a middle person. I connect people who need people with people who need jobs. You need jobs, and I need employees. And what happens is people pay me to give me these letters of introduction for quests that need to be completed. And I look for adventures which might fit the bill.” 
The dwarf cocks his head, “so how do I get your job? That looks like a pretty sweet gig; I hand out letters and get paid. How much does your job pay? Where do I sign up? I could take your job, and you can take the letter.” 
The middle person is shocked, “that is not how it works!” 
Seeing the dwarf approaching threateningly, Fiora signals to a guard, who advances, growling, “are these guys giving you problems?” 
Vardis mumbles, “just having a conversation.” 
Fiora smiles and whispers, “Diego is so fond of me. Diego, come here to meet these fine folks!” 
Fiora knows they will say yes, and when Orsik asks, “how long do they have?” the deal is closed. 
“Oh, the merchant will not be there for three weeks, and it’s a two-week journey. You have a month, I would say.” 
Although she does notice how Vardis seems dubious, “we are the best people for the job? I mean, you do notice that we poked the ghost?” 
She then notices Orsik, who sees Diego reaching for his sword, “heya, Diego.” 
Fiora winks to Diego, who backs down, “well then, if you ever need another job, you can find me at Nonna’s place. I don’t think you are ever going to be welcome here again.” 
## The group 2nd day of the first tenday of Taraskh

“Time to go to unload some of this stuff,” Thistle squeaks. 
*The town of Llorkh has a Lionel Coster franchise. The Lionel Coster trading house has storefronts everywhere, and even in the most obscure parts of the realm, the Lionel Coster is the place where you can find anything. They specialize in small towns and villages where their unparalleled inventory management allows them to sell goods that would typically be unavailable anywhere. Some claim that the trading firm is a front for the Harpers. Others argue that their destruction of small shopkeepers proves that they are a Zhentarim. Still, others claim they are a front of a cult, with some suggesting Mammon’s demonic power. Finally, others argue that it’s just a trading house committed to making money, but only the foolishly naïve believe that. *
The group walks into the franchise and is greeted by the immaculately dressed person, even in the most casual of clothes. He could wear a peasants tunic and wool pantaloons to a king’s ball and be the well-dressed person there. He’s got that clean, pressed, precise look that, if it wasn’t for the fact that magic is available and you could do illusions at the drop of a hat, would almost be unbelievable. But this guy achieves this look through careful and meticulous preparation.
His hair is perfectly combed. He’s got a beard, but it’s like perfectly cut. Even his teeth are freakishly white. 
Speaking with a gruff husky voice, he declares, “my name is Leo, and I am the proud owner of this franchise. I want you to know that I don’t do credit for first-time buyers.” 
Vardis sees an opening and asks, “so if we buy something, then we aren’t first-time buyers?” 
Leo glares at him. Vardis notices and walks out, “Diego wants to talk to me.” 
Leo then turns to Thistle, “what can I do for you?” 
“We are looking for a scroll of dispel magic.”
“Well, I have one of those, and it will be 300 gold.” 
Thistle looks at it and notices something about the scroll, “look at this, the tube has scuff marks, and the scroll is creased?” 
Leo is aghast and appalled. He is particularly proud of his store and that he has been nominated for the “Lionel Coster best presentation award!” “If the committee were to find out,” he thinks, “they would remove me from consideration.”
He turns to Thistle, “this is disgraceful. All my merchandise is perfectly kept. I can not sell this for full price; you can have it for 250 gold!” 
A few moments later, after buying a new bow and some new arrows, Thistle meets the group outside. 
“Where to next?” he asks. 
“There’s a temple. Let’s go check it out.” 
The group finds an old, abandoned temple to Bane. 
*Bane is vilified in many legends. Those who favor him have committed dark deeds in his name throughout history, but most people don’t worship Bane out of malice. Bane represents ambition and control, and those who have the former but lack the latter pray to him to give them strength. It is said that Bane favors those who exhibit drive and courage and that he aids those who seek to become conquerors, carving kingdoms from the wilderness and bringing order to the lawless.*
*At many times and in many places in Faerûn, the faithful of Bane have been seen as saviors for their efforts in slaughtering raiders, throwing down corrupt rulers, or saving armies on the brink of defeat. But in just as many other places, the worship of Bane had created or supported cruel dictatorships, aided mercantile monopolies, or brought about the practice of slavery where before it didn’t exist.*

The temple was built by the Zhentarim, who have since abandoned Llorkh. The two shrines are to two kinder Gods, Chauntea, The Great Mother or Grain Goddess, and Tempus the Foehammer, the Lord of Battles. 
*Chauntea’s faith is one of nurturing and growth. Agricultural aphorisms and farming parables dot her teachings. Growing and reaping, the eternal cycle, is a common theme in the faith. Destruction for its own sake, or leveling without rebuilding, is anathema to her.*

*Tempus’s favor might be randomly distributed, but over the centuries, his priests have made an effort to spread and enforce a standard code of warfare — to make war a thing of rules, respect for reputations, and professional behavior. This code, called Tempus’s Honor, has the purpose of making conflicts brief, decisive, and as safe as possible for those not directly involved. The rules in the code include the following: arm anyone who needs a weapon; disparage no foe; acquit oneself with bravery; train all for battle, and don’t engage in feuds. Those who poison wells, taint fields, kill noncombatants, or engage in torture in the name of war are all considered sinners.*
The shrines are not attended by any regular priests. 
Vardis sighs in relief, “won’t be banned here.” 
Orsik turns to the group, “Hey, there’s the tower of Green Flame?” 
Thistle and Unla Kee stare at him.
“Yeah, when we came through here, we thought it might be worth checking it out, but we did that thing, and then we had to leave.” 
More staring.
“Well, it’s rumored to be a residence of a mighty wizard that was destroyed during the Spellplague. Locals avoid it, fearing the arcane traps and wards still in place. One of the local kids told me that a dare is to try and go to it and touch the tower.” 
Thistle then argues, “not at night?” 
Vardis and Orsik nod. Orsik then remembers Old Keller and decides to go talk to him. 
Vardis warns Orsik, “I don’t think he likes us.” 
Orsik scoffs and says, “we dwarves stick together.” 
Arriving at the stonemason’s hut, Orsik shouts, “My kinsman!” 
“Don’t take a step closer,” shouts Keller. 
“Hey, I thought we dwarves stick together?” 
“We do until they do that thing in the dwarf’s place of work, disgracing all dwarves in the process.” 
“What thing?” 
“The thing in the keep!” 
Orsik groans, “I really need to do something, so that isn’t an issue.” He turns to Old Keller, “what can I do to redeem myself?”
“Stop doing that thing! And when you are less likely to be goblin food, come back to me, and I can tell you what you can do to redeem yourself in my eyes.” 
## Nonnie’s Place

Nonnie is a short ruddy-cheeked woman. She runs her small inn with its kitchen and common room for the few visitors to Llorkh that don’t guest with the Iron Circle. 
She is proud of her vegetarian-only menu and insists that everyone call her “Aunt Nonnie.” 
When the group enters her inn, she beckons them to eat. 
Vardis mumbles, “nice to be treated like a human being.” 
One of the clients lifts her head up from the table and says, “she treats everyone like a human being. Even refugees like me.” 
The woman notices that they are still listening and says, “I come from a town to the west, called Greenest. It was destroyed by the Cult of the Dragon. I fled east, looking to be as far away from the Dragons as I could. Now I am hearing rumors of giants nearby, so I am going back west to see if my town is safe and rebuild.” 
After the woman finishes her tale, Nonnie takes their order of four cheese pies. She heads off to the kitchen, whistling a whimsical tone. She comes back 20 minutes later with four warmed-up pies cooked to perfection, tasty beyond belief, and quite filling. 
After dinner, she asks, “you’ll need a place to stay. It will cost you. It’s not the fanciest rooms.”
As they head to bed, she thinks, “what a nice group of people.” 
## The Wizard Tower 3nd day of the 1st tenday of Taraskh

The entity in the tower sees the group advance upon its building. 
It starts talking to itself. 
“Oh dear, someone wishes to come to the tower. I suppose the usual wards will do.” 
It notices that half of the group is still advancing. 
“Well, how awkward, the dwarf and the elf are still approaching. And they have found the handle. And they are about to try it.” 
The elf touches the handle, and the entity is pleased to see that the dwarf and elf are hurt but not dead. 
It says to no one in particular, “They are still alive; that should be a good enough warning for now.” 
As the group walks away, it realizes with sorrow, “the door can now be opened.”  
## The group 3rd day of the 1st tenday of Taraskh 

The two-day hike to the third treasure trove that Veradda Store had pointed was remarkably uneventful, with only a druid collecting herbs interrupting the monotony. 
Her notes lead them a narrow path halfway up a mountain in the northern Greypeaks. And there they stood, looking down over a cliff to their right, with the sheer mountain to their left. 
“No way in,” shouted Orsik. 
“Must be a way in,” shouted Vardis
Thistle and Vardis work together to find a pair of secret doors hidden in the rocks. 
Vardis marches into the one to the south, Thistle follows. The door opens into an unlit corridor of unworked stone. Thistle flitters ahead until the natural tunnel splits in two. He waves to Vardis to come forward, “be careful of the pit trap.” 
Orsik shouts, “what about the other way?”
“Another trap!” shouts Thistle, then really gets annoyed at the dungeon makers -  “Really, they have no imagination. The same pit trap, everywhere. Like what do they expect invaders to do? OOOPS, I FELL INTO A TRAP! CAN’T POSSIBLY IMAGINE ANOTHER ONE!”
Thistle lands next to the trap and observes that the group can safely walk across the weight-triggered trap as long as he flies across. 
“Right-hand rule,” announces Thistle as he leads the group. The tunnels eventually end at a large chamber covered with ice. Thistle looks into and notices two large humanoid figures. “Frost giants!” he exclaims.  

The group heads over and notices that each giant clutches a bloody great ax in its hands, and each seems to have died from slashing wounds created by large bladed weapons. 
Vardis notices a diamond atop a three-foot-tall pedestal on the far side of the room. As he approaches the diamond, his mind sees that it gleams with an inner light that is definitely supernatural. That does not deter him, however. 
He shouts, “Unla Kee use the scroll we bought.” Unla Kee carefully walks to the bed, making sure to not slip on the ice. 
And just then, the diamond magically rises from the pedestal. Beams of dazzling green light erupt, and everyone and everything is bathed in a sickly green glow. 
Vardis shouts urgently, “NOW, Unla Kee.” 
A magical hand streaks from Unla Kee and slaps the diamond to no effect. 
The spell had no effect. 
Or maybe it did, but not the desired effect?” thinks the mage as he sees three masses of translucent green energy. These masses pulse in rhythm with the light of the diamond. He realizes that they are all connected by energy. The masses move towards the group, lashing out with barb-tipped tentacles. Unla Kee shouts, “every time they lash out, the synchronized pulsing of the creatures and the diamond grows brighter!” 

Vardis shouts, “destroy the diamond.” 
Thistle retorts, “that’s a pretty expensive diamond; how about not.” 
The two gang up on one of the creatures and bring it to near death, and when the killing blow should have killed the creature, the gem glows. 
Unla Kee thinks and shouts, “we have to destroy the diamond.” 
Thistle screams, “would a blanket work?” 
“No!” Unla Kee uses his magic missiles. He spreads them around, one on the diamond and one on the other creatures. The diamond is barely damaged. 
The creatures strike back. Pretty soon, only Orsik and Unla Kee are alive. But that is enough to destroy the diamond. With the diamond destroyed, the creatures are finally killed. 
As the last creature dissipates, they see the diamond dust swirl, creating the form of a green-tinted spirit that speaks in a provocative whisper, “Your companions want to kill you and take your possessions! Kill them before they kill you!” 
The group shrugs, pick up the treasure left behind by the giants,, and collects the diamond dust.
