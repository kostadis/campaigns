# Chapter 38- Harshnag the Grim of the Legendary Force Grey
## Harshnag the Grim
Harshnag sits and thinks to himself. 
“Orsik and his sidekick Vardis keep calling me Frosty. I am a Frost Giant. Being called Frosty is disrespectful. You would think having someone like me on their side and offering them help to get the secret lost temple of the Giant god would earn me some credibility, some respect, but no.” 
Harshnag grumbles some more, “And then there is the halfling, the one known as Unla Kee. I have never, ever met such a narcissistic, self-absorbed personality. And a complete disregard for personal safety.” 
“Krowen was insistent that they were his agents,” he mutters inaudibly. 
“Little people”!
“And, of course, they object,” he thinks.
“Very well, non-giants, the Harper Agent Krowen Valharrow has said that you have accepted being his agents for hire to learn about the events surrounding the end of the Ordning.” 
Harshnag stares at Vardis, who is confused about what the Ordning is. 
“The Ordning is hierarchical order that the Giant races fit into. It’s a caste system that the God of all giants, Amn, created. The storm giants are at the top of the order, the cloud giants are next, the fire and frost giants are peers, and the hill giants are at the bottom.” 
Vardis is delighted, “so your rigid caste system ended? That’s great! Freedom!” 
The Frost Giant glares – “You do not understand. It is the Ordning that has kept the peace for so many centuries. Without the Ordning, the Giants would attack and destroy each other and everyone in between.” 
Unla Kee is pleased, “chaos!”
Harshnag sighs and remembers what Krowen said, “You promised and were given access to the teleportation circles, and I believe there was a quid pro quo that you would help.” 
He watches them slump.
“Excellent. Now, I know that although the Ordning is a religious imperative that all pious Giants must observe, it is also true that Giants are not all pious. King Hekaton of the Storm Giants has disappeared. And without him, the powerful Giant leaders have started to wage war. I  now think it is time to consult with a higher authority. I know the location of a mountain temple, the Eye of the All-Father, built by my ancient ancestors under the Spine of the World. According to giant legend, the temple contains a divine oracle. I will lead you to the temple so you can consult this oracle and learn how best to end the giants’ threat to the North.”
## Unla Kee
Harshnag the Grim agreed to a 100 gold piece share of the 5,000 gold piece reward for the capture of the brigand Worvil Forkbeard, also known as the Weevil. 
Unla Kee was very disappointed to hear that Frosty didn’t know how to make a castle fly. 
“So you are saying that Cloud Giants are more powerful than you?” 
Frosty’s reaction was awkward, he brought his ax next to Unla Kee, and the blade was taller than the magic user. Unla Kee was not phased. He had faced death; if he died, the world would die with him, and the world would keep him alive. 
Unla Kee turns to him and asks – “so, are there any good cloud giants?” 
Frosty breaks out laughing. “Oh, my god. Are you one of those short-sighted, short-lived species who think biology determines morality? There are good cloud giants and evil cloud giants. To be sentient is to have the ability to make choices. What kind of bozos would believe that?”
“Silverymoon,” pipes Thistle.
“One of those places full of the bozos.” He then pauses, “But I wasn’t laughing about that. I laughed about a cloud giant helping you get a flying tower.” 
Orsik sees an opportunity, “anyone you might know?”
“Yes, let me think, Count Thullen, the younger brother of Countess Sansuri. Thullen is a good person. His sister, not so much.” 
After two days of travel, the edges of Longsaddle are visible. 
Harshnag tells them much about the town, “This sleepy little frontier village and member of the Lords’ Alliance.” 
Orsik interrupts, “Mr. Grim, what’s your take on the Lord’s Alliance?”

He spits on the ground, “What they want to do is create a federation of rich, powerful people who rule with an iron fist preventing the kind of freedom that adventure is and want to tax us.” 
Orsik nods. 
“Anyways, Longsaddle, as you can see, straddles the Long Road, with rows of homes and businesses on either side of the trade route. Noteworthy establishments include a rustic inn called the Gilded Horseshoe and a friendly fest hall called the Gambling Golem, where card games and a local marbles game known as scattershields are popular. “
He points to the distance beyond the town, “See!” And Unla Kee sees horses and herds of cattle on sprawling ranches.
Harshnag must have sensed what Unla Kee was thinking, seeing such a defenseless town.  “Monsters or brigands sometimes mistake Longsaddle for easy pickings — unaware that a family of powerful human wizards, the Harpells, lives nearby. Their grand house, Ivy Mansion, lies west of Longsaddle at the end of a long, winding path. The Harpells founded the village but took no part in its government, preferring to live quietly on their estate. Throughout Longsaddle and the Ivy Mansion, magical wards warn the Harpells when trouble’s afoot.”
Unla Kee is excited, “Gambling!” 
Harshnag begs the group to bring him a pot of the famous soup from the Gilded Horseshoe. 
Unla Kee heads straight for the Gambling Golem and places a small wager as they enter the town. A gamble that he influences with his ability to see the future and adjust the fates. 
After a long night, the group continues their journey to their final destination Xanthalar’s Keep. 
## Lord of Xantharl’s Keep Narbeck Horn
“How unbelievably annoying,” he thinks, “Orgazol has once again accepted a bribe from some adventurer who believes they must talk to me.” 
“Sir, I am pursuing a dangerous criminal called the Weevil.” 
“So?”
“He is in your town.” 
“So?”
“We wish to arrest him.” 
“Then do that, and make sure you don’t arrest the wrong person. The Ax of Mirabar shows no mercy to those who arrest the wrong man.”
“He’s a dwarf.” 
“My apologies. I don’t take care of these details; I take care of the bigger picture. My job is to set policy. Why don’t you talk to my lieutenant? That seems to like you.” 
Narbeck smiles evilly at his lieutenant, who realizes these bounty hunters may not be the only people arrested if this goes sideways. 
Just then, a horn blows, and Narbeck is shocked into action, “we are under attack.” 
Another officer whispers to him, and Narbeck smiles. “A foolish group of goblins, hobgoblins, and ogres are attacking! A Frost Giant leads them.” 
The dwarf’s friends seem worried. Narbeck thinks,  “I will have to look into that….” 
“From where?” Narbeck overhears. 
“The west.” And then he turns to shout to his guards. 
The fairy volunteers some help to Narbeck when another guard shouts, “Another Frost Giant is attacking the first one!” 

## Vardis

“If I wanted to be a city guard, I would have kept the job at Parnast. Not interested,” he thinks.
And as he looks around for a way to exit, he notices a group of bugbears coming in from the east. 
“It’s a diversion; the bugbears are coming from the east. The Falling Orc, where Larg, aka Weevil, is hiding, is there. They probably are looking to secure the reward.” 
Vardis starts to run. Orsik seeing the money about to be lost, follows. Thistle and Unla Kee join the pursuit.
The battle with the Bugbears is explosive. Unla Kee and Vardis let loose fireballs that wound poor weevil. And then Orsik and Thistle finish the thieves with good old-fashioned violence. 
## Weevil 

“Thank god you saved me!” Weevil shouts. “I was about to be captured by these evil creatures.” 
The fairy walks up to him, shows him the wanted poster he picked up in Longsaddle, and mutters, “I don’t think so.”
