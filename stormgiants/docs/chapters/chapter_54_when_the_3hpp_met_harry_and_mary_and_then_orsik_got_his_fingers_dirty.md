# Chapter 54 When the 3HPP Met Harry and Mary, and then Orsik got his fingers dirty.

## 5th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis 1492 at Flint Rock

Orsik grumbles, “It’s under the rock, it’s always under the rock. And the rock is always inconveniently placed.” 

He stares up ahead and sees Thistle and Unla Kee landing. 

Vardis sighs and starts marching. 

After a few minutes, sweat dripping from their foreheads, Orsik asks, “So where is it?” 

Thistle stares at Orsik, “Under the rock?” 

Vardis casts detect magic and confirms the location. 

Orsik looks at him, “No, that’s not how detect magic works. See, with 1 foot of stone, you shouldn’t be able to detect anything.”

Vardis stares at him, “You’re right. Something is wrong. We need to abandon this entire quest and start all over again.” 

Unla Kee sniggers, “This would have been undetectable in the past, but you’ll notice that the stone surface is weather-worn. So, although originally the stone was 1 foot, the stone now is just ever so slightly less than 1 foot. And as a result, the spell worked.” 

Orsik scratches his head. Vardis stops walking away. 

They head back to start digging when two giant elk approach. 

Thistle casts spells to speak with animals, flies within shouting range, and asks, “Have you seen any other creatures?” 

The male elk responds, “Well, there were the Joneses. They are the most irritating giant elks.” 

The other female elk intervenes, “Harry, how many times must I tell you to stop speaking ill of the Joneses.” 

“Harumph. Mary, they are the ones who like to strut around with their horns acting all superior to everyone.” 
Unla Kee laughs, “Keeping up with the Joneses…” 

Orsik, meanwhile, is furiously digging.

Then the female Elk squints into the sky and shouts, “Are you a fly?” 

“No, I am fae from the feywild.” 

“Fae from the feywild? Is that a particular river nearby? A watering hole? A grove of trees?” 

Harry, the male giant elk, says, “Oh no, it’s one of those creatures that think we worship some god with funny horns. I’ll have you note we are part of an atheistic club and do not worship the gods of the Fae. “ 

Mary groans, “I’m so sorry. My husband and I are trying to enjoy a quiet meal.” 

Thistle asks, “Anything dangerous?” 

Mary pauses, “No, not that I recall.” 

Thistle thanks them and flies off. 

He does hear Harry moan, “Another bipedal stealing our grass!” 

Orsik and Vardis are now trying to smash the rock. 

As they make more noise, Mary shouts, “Yoo-hoo! Pretentious flying creature!”

Harry mutters, “Calls himself a Fairy from the Feywild. Whoever heard of such nonsense. The Feywild is just a tool of the oppressor to keep the Giant Elk subservient to a doomed religion.” 

Mary ignores him, “We have noticed that other bipedal creatures think this is an important area for them. Are you sure you are allowed to do what you are doing?” 

Thistle manages to persuade them that they do. 

Harry says, “Well the sooner you get out of here, the sooner we get our peace and quiet.”

Mary and Harry approach the 3HPP and push the stone out of the way. 

A bit later, the 3HPP is on Delsephine’s ship, the Dragon’s Breath, with a mithral spear tip wearing 75 pounds. 

“Where can my ship take you next?” 
Vardis mutters, “Morgur’s Mound.” 

## 6th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis at Morgur’s Mound
As they approach Morgur’s Mound, Bressik is told to provide the information he has. He pulls out a map of the location that he found while at Candlekeep. 

“The spirit mound of the lost Thunderbeast tribe, Morgur’s Mound, stands in the Crags and is named after Uthgar’s brother. The Thunderbeast tribe disappeared. We're not sure why. It may have been tribal conflict, their ineptitude, or a dragon who swallowed them all. Nobody knows. 

The mound is shaped like a long-necked, wingless dragon — the Uthgardt impression of a thunderbeast. A ridge of dragon bones juts from the mound's head, neck, back, and tail. Enormous cairns encircle the mound out to a range of a quarter mile. Beneath them lie the bones of revered Thunderbeast tribal warriors.

The Thunderbeast tribe has not visited its spirit mound in years, leaving it ripe for plunderers. Several dig sites left by previous expeditions attest to the mound’s popularity among treasure hunters.”

Vardis mutters, “And they call us grave robbers.” 

Bressik coughs uncomfortably, “As I was, through no fault of my own, a member of a cult planning to end the world and bring about the age of dragons by bringing an evil goddess into our world and avoided the just punishment I deserve by serving an evil red dragon, I am not sure I am capable of casting aspersion on anyone’s character or their activities.” 

When they arrive at the mound, Vardis asks, “So where do you think it is?” 

Bressik answers, “No clue.” 

After casting detect magic, Vardis gets a hint that something might be below his feet, and says, “I am feeling something and it’s not the gas.” 

Orsik sniggers. 

Vardis asks, “Unla Kee, you’re up. You have mold earth?” 

The halfling shrugs, “No.” 

Orsik is furious but resigned and starts to dig. Vardis joins him. 

Thistle and Unla Kee stand watch. Unla Kee is too weak to dig, and Thistle is too, proud. 

Eight hours pass, and they find nothing. 

“Tomorrow?”

## 7th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis

Again, Orsik and Vardis are digging and the mage and the ranger just watch. 

Vardis gets annoyed, “Maybe you can dig?” 

Thistle says, “No.” 

Vardis gets annoyed, “Why don’t you take your pretty little eyes and check if you can see anything?” 

After a few more hours, the two friends find a magic item. Not the artifact they are looking for but an item. 

They identify it as an abjurer’s bangle, a device that absorbs damage from attacks. They hand it to Unla Kee. 

But still no artifact.

## 8th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis 

Orsik and Vardis move to the creature's head and dig some more. 

And once again, the find another magic item, a Helm of Comprehend Languages. 

Orsik curses, “Seriously?” 
Delsephine sighs, “Still no artifact.” 

Vardis gives her the stink eye. 

Thistle and Unla Kee shrug. 
## 9th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis

Nothing.
## 10th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis 

Orsik finds a giant tooth. It's a gold-plated fire giant’s tooth, an ancient giant relic of giantkind. The tooth is nonmagical and small enough to fit in the hand of a human.

Orsik waves at Thistle to come towards him. 

As Thistle approaches, four animated thunderbeast skeletons erupt from the mound around the hole Orsik is in. 

“The scouting was oh so useful.” 

Thistle sees what’s happening and flies as fast as he can, and no faster.

Orsik scrambles out of the hole. He sees four creatures and attacks the first, bringing it one tiny step close to oblivion. 

Just then, two arrows from Thistle strike another of the beasts that he identifies as ankylosaurus’. 

Thuul dashes closer. 

Three of the creatures attack. Orsik’s military skill and armor allow him to avoid the jaws of the monsters. Vardis casts a warding flair and distracts his enemy. Unla Kee is bitten.

Vardis casts a fireball. The burning blast destroys the one that Orsik had injured and wounds two more. 

Unla Kee pulls out his artifact, allowing him to produce effects similar to the wand of wonder. 

He points the rock at one of the creatures and performs the ritual, and a gust of wind blows out of the rock. Orsik laughs bitterly. 

But Unla Kee sees the opportunity and uses the wind to push one of the skeletons into the pit that Orsik and Vardis had dug. 

Orsik strikes with his sword twice at one of the monsters. 

Thistle moves in and impales another with two of his arrows. 

The creature in the pit tries to climb out, but the wind blowing from Unla Kee is too strong. 

Orsik sees what’s happening and mutters, “Excellent!” 

Vardis strikes one of the creatures down. 

Unla Kee uses a gust of wind to force one of the creatures away from the dwarf and his buddy. The skeletal monster loses its grip, and Orsik and Thule take the opportunity to miss striking at it. 

As the creature tumbles into the hole, Unla Kee ponders what to do. He considers using his artifact of wonder but casts a fireball in the hole instead. 

A sudden rush of heat explodes from the ground, and then silence. 

On the airship, Delsephine asks, “Should we take you somewhere where you can clean yourself, Orsik?” 

“No.” 

Orsik is annoyed at the cult leader. His body is covered in dirt from five days of digging, and all she cares about is her pretty little ship. 

He finds the cleanest spot on the ship and plops himself down.

Pulling out the tooth, he mutters, “Five days of digging in the dirt to find this.” 

Delsephine grimaces, and tells her crew to give the grumpy dwarf space. She then turns to Vardis, “The only places left are Raven’s Rock and the Great Wyrm Cavern.” 

“To Raven’s rock.”
