# Chapter 32  - Murder Hobos
The sun rises. The Evermoors stretch before our heroes’ eyes and ears like a plate of overcooked pasta. Humidity sticks to their skin, bugs crawl over their tents, and the stench of decaying plants and flesh overwhelms their packed food. 
“So, all we have to do is find the cause – a bit of recon, and we can return to collect our reward.”
Thistle acknowledges Orsik, straps his bow to his back, and buzzes off, flying around to find something. 
He comes back a while later. 
“I heard some snarling, whooping, and hollering, but I am unsure what it is.”
The suddenly intellectually enhanced fighter thinks momentarily, strains to hear, and declares, “That’s them.” 
Thistle stares, “huh?”
“Look, I don’t want you to feel bad, but I am **really** smart. There are two trolls and a fire giant.” 
The fairy stares, dumbfounded. 
Vardis shakes him, “My friend used to be so cool until he got this headband.” 
And then an argument breaks out. Thistle is determined to kill the Fire Giant and the trolls. Unla Kee has to be held back from charging at the monsters. Whereas Orsik is adamant that they don’t have to fight, they just have to ensure that the trolls are coming out because of the Fire Giant. 
They agree they should sneak up on the creatures to figure out what they are arguing about. 
So Thistle flutters off quietly. His boots of elvenkind silenced the air. And there, in an opening in the swamp, is a Fire Giant shouting at two trolls. The Fire Giant is waving a rod identical to the one the Fire Giant that acted Triboar used to find some peculiar artifact made of adamantium at the trolls. The creature looks hungry, frustrated, and annoyed. The trolls just stare at the Fire Giant uncomprehendingly. 

“They are working together! Like Cats and Dogs!” he thinks. 
And he flies off to the campsite, where Orsik is swatting away flies with his sword. 
“We should kill them!” 
“It was a bad idea last time, and we almost all died.” 
“There’s only one, this time.”
“Just a recon mission!” 
Thistle considers that Orsik was the only person who argued against the last attack, shrugs, and turns to Unla Kee: " Do you have a comprehend languages spell you can cast on me so I can figure out what they are saying?”
A few minutes later, Thistle once again arrives at the squabbling humanoids. This time, he overhears the giant shout, “I want something tasty. Not mud pies.” The trolls are meanwhile looking at the mudpies at her feet and are confused as to why she doesn’t find them tasty. She glares at them and their incomprehension and whines, “I can’t go back to Duke Zalto until I find a piece of the Vonindod, and this Rod isn’t helping!” 
Thistle returns to the 2HPP and explains the situation again. This time, the debate rages even longer. The fairy and mage are determined to kill the giant, the cleric is ambivalent, and Orsik wants to return and collect his reward. 
“The quest is completed,” he shouts.
“But treasure!” 
“We almost got killed.” 
“Details!”
Thistle is insistent, “they have treasure.” 
Unla Kee pipes up from the floor of his tent, where he is lying down bored. " Wait, we aren’t going to attack?” 
Everyone just glares at him. He glares back and then closes his eyes. 
Seeing the resistance to the idea, Unla Kee proposes that they entice the trolls with the bodies of the Uthgardt nomads. Then, he will sneak up to the giant and try to steal his rod. 
Orsik and Vardis discuss the situation amongst themselves. 
“I’ll do what the group wants,” muses the elf.
“I have grown attached to them,” grouses the fighter. 
“Alright, let’s do this. But I gotta admit, Thistle, I am amazed that you draw the line at stealing, but just going and killing creatures that have done nothing to us to harvest their organs and steal their magic items is okay.” 
He then mutters to Vardis, “Murder hobo.” Vardis nods. 
Thistle stares at Vardis and then thinks, “Well, it is a different Fire Giant that attacked Triboar, and I don’t have a vendetta against this one, so I suppose we could move on.” 
A few hours later, with the sun setting in the east, the heroes, covered in the stench of the Evermoors, arrive at the Calling Horns Inn. 
Tamalin hears what they say and says, “Well, that is very odd. Fire Giants are working with Trolls. Cats and Dogs, working together.” 
“That’s what I said,” says Thistle. 
Tamalin smiles at the young fairy and continues, “Learn anything else?”
“They are part of Duke Zalto’s tribe. And they are looking for something called the Vonindod.” 
Tamalin sighs, “Never heard of him. The funny thing about Fire Giants is that all their leaders are dukes. And since I am on the topic, hill giants are led by a chief, stone giants by thane, and all cloud giants by a count.” She then thinks for a moment, “Never heard of a Vonindod. Still, you could find something out about in a major city like Everlund and Silverymoon.” She stares at Thistle and then turns to the other three, “Well, the three of you might get something useful from the good folks of Silverymoon.” 
Orsik grumbles, “Yes, the good people of Silverymoon with their Cambion demons.” 
“Tell me about it.” 
As the heroes head out, she says, “Well, I have a brother who is the High Captain of Everlund, and that note I just gave you is good for a favor. He might be able to get what you need.” 
The following day, the heroes head out to Yartar on the Evermoor Road, leaving Calling Horns behind. 
The road is dusty and a lot less busy than they expected. They are unsure if it’s because of the weather, the trolls, the giants, or something else. As they trudge along, they see a hill giant with a sack picking up anything edible and eating it immediately or stuffing it in their bag for later. 
Thistle scouts out ahead and sees two bugbears and an ogre following the hill giant, looking obviously bored, uninterested, and unhappy. 
“Shall we kill this giant and do the travelers on this road a good deed?” 
“You’re bloodthirsty today?”
Thistle ignores Orsik’s comment. 
And so they set up an ambush. 
Thistle, Orsik, and Vardis hide in the bushes off the road while Unla Kee stands in the middle. 
The other three plead with Unla Kee to hide, but he shouts, “I am hiding.” 
They stare at him, stunned. In plain sight, he’s standing in the road with his hands over his eyes. 
And it works. 
The giant, seeing that Unla Kee can’t see him, decides that he can ignore the halfling. Instead, the humanoid focuses on an edible fungus on the side of the road. The bugbear and ogre, meanwhile, notice that their boss is about to be attacked and slow down their pace. 
Thistle fires two arrows from the trees and hits once. Orsik strikes three times, doing three times the damage that Thistle did while wearing a unique shield he crafted overnight in the Calling Horns Inn. 
Vardis uses his guiding bolt to help whoever strikes next. Unfortunately, despite his luck, he fails to hit. 
“It happens.” 
Unla Kee then opens his eyes and thinks, “See, I knew the giant would be surprised!” 
And then casts a lightning bolt, and the giant, already a slow-moving lumbering creature, has the fates twisted against him and bears the full brunt of the blast. 
In pain and down to 15 of its 105 hitpoints, the giant strikes angrily at the creatures in front of him. First, he tries to swing his club at Orsik, who tricks the beast into attacking Thuul, and just before he strikes Thuul, the ever-watchful Vardis uses his mastery of light to blind the giant just for a moment, so he swings wildly and misses. The giant burps a vile concoction and turns to swing at Orsik again. The dwarf, again, points at Thuul. The giant thinks for a moment and swings at the guard drake again, but misses. 
Thistl,e seeing the Giant strike at his friend, aims carefully, and the arrow goes through the giant’s eye and lodges itself in what passes for his brain. 
A loud thud is heard, and dust is tossed into the air as the giant falls. 
The bugbears and ogre see their dead boss and Vardis holding onto giant toenails, looking at each other, and running the other way. 
The next day our heroes meet a goblin caravan and wave them down. A short middle-aged goblin, wearing a red velvet shirt and blue pants, held up by green suspenders, steps forward.
“My name is Fritz, and this is my store. My clan and I are heading east because the giants have gotten crazy, strutting, fretting, and trying to prove that they are the biggest and badest. And then the nomads attack anything that moves. Our goods are priced to move.”
“So it’s a going-out-of-business sale?” 
“My mother told me to never be that desperate and never sell for anything less than a reasonable profit.
“Wise words.” 
Fritz’s goods are of no interest to the 3HPP as they aren’t trying to set up a kitchen in a house. But Fritz, who is always on the lookout for knickknacks and doo-dads, is more than happy to buy a bag of salt that belonged to Duke Zalto, “salt fit for a duke!” an empty chest, and drinking horn – “if a giant thought it was worth carrying then it must be valuable” and fairy leather armor. 
Thistle is about to explain to him that it’s a fairy’s leather armor, but Orsik stops him. 
The next day, on the 8th day of the 1st tenday of Flamerule, our heroes arrive in Yartar late in the evening and head to the Wink and the Kiss. 

Maia is happy to see them and asks if they need more work. Orsik shows her the Letter of Mark, and she tries to high-five him, but he leaves her hanging. 
Unhappy, Maia takes the group to the bar in the center of the maze that makes the interior of this tavern and sits them next to Eltio.
Eltio, a member of the Hand of Yartar, is dressed in white, wearing a hat and a white mask. The clothes have carefully stitched patterns in white on the cloth. 
When they present the Letter of Mark to her, she asks, “so what did you do?”
“Recon.”
“No murder? Thievery? Highway robbery? Damn, Tamalin has gone legit. She used to be a legendary figure in these parts. Parlayed the terror she induced on the folks around here to a bigger, more lucrative gig.” 
The 3HPP stare at Eltio as she reminisces. 
The Hand of Yartar swallows her drink, tells Maia to bring drinks for the 3HPP, and heads out. 
While she’s out, the group debates how they will convince Harthos Zymorven, the heir to Zymorven Hall, to come with them. Orsik proposes intimidation, and Thistle proposes that they fake that he will be arrested and offer him a way to escape to Daddy. Vardis suggests that they tell him that his dad is ill. Orsik looks at his friend and says, “I don’t think that will work.” He then pauses, “Thank you for your idea. There are no bad ideas during the brainstorming phase. All ideas are welcome. Keep them coming.” 
While the group is still mulling over options, Eltio returns. 
“So your boy is in prison for murder. The bloke will be executed on the 10th. We do that sort of thing – head cut off to make it harder to raise them from the dead. As for the sword, it’s in the possession of a man named Tholzar Brenner. He’s a Damaranian, a land from the east, who is not liked. Tholzar is a guard captain who, if he went missing, no one would be upset. I can take you to him if you want.” 
They look at her.
“I can take you to him where an ambush would be possible.” 
Later that evening, the group was in the market near the Waterbarron’s house. Eltio pointed out a small group of armed guards with a brutish boss as they swaggered through the small stores and stalls. 
“He’s there collecting his protection money.” 
Orsik stares – “They are so close together, we could fireball them all.” 
Eltio looks at him and thinks, “You are that guy Orsik who did that thing. I knew it.” She stops, thinks about letting him burn, and realizes that the blowback might affect her or, worse, the Hand of Yartar. So she turns to him, “Do you want to create a 20’ radius fireball that will explode in the main market of Yartar near the Waterbarron’s residence? Are you insane? Or is this part of your master plan to free Harthal?” She sighs, “I don’t think they will wait until the 10th to cut your head off….” 
Meanwhile, Thistle thinks, “And he called me a murder hobo. He’s willing to blow up a small town, and me wanting to kill two trolls and a fire giant was too much….”
