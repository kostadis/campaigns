# Sandesyl Morgia

## Identity
- **Role:** Castellan of Blagothkus' cloud castle; vampire lord; self-declared overlord of the flying castle; disciple of Sammaster and theological adherent to dracolich transformation as the path to draconic salvation
- **Race/Type:** Half-elf (moon elf), vampire
- **Faction:** Long-term servant of Blagothkus; formerly affiliated with the Cult of the Dragon (traditionalist faction; expelled by the First Faction); operates independently with her own necromantic agenda
- **First Appearance:** Upper balcony room of the crumbling port-side tower of Blagothkus' castle, smiling at Unla Kee "like a cat smiling at a mouse"; introduced to the party more broadly through her zombie spawns observing them from the castle tower exterior; first direct full appearance in the castle courtyard when the party announced Silverymoon as their destination

*Also known as: Sandesyl.*

---

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Acquire the magical tome from Xonathal's Keep; leave the castle and establish a permanent dark lair; re-establish rituals interrupted by Severin; convert Iymrith to dracolich immortality; advance the doctrine that all dragons should become dracoliches to rule Faerûn; maintain comfortable control of the castle; secure a steady supply of sentient humanoids to feed on; preserve her own power and autonomy
- **Drives:** Theological passion — she views Severin as a usurper and considers Tiamat's physical entry into the world a heresy compared to dracolich transcendence; expressed near-romantic devotion to Iymrith as a potential convert
- Predatory, theatrical, and unsettlingly chatty — she oscillates between sharing significant intelligence and issuing cold threats within the same breath.
- Calculating, cautious, and deeply distrustful — she required proof of enemy deaths before leaving her lair and abandoned her tower once she no longer trusted the party.
- Capable of negotiation and pragmatism, making deals when direct confrontation is disadvantageous.
- Takes visible pleasure in the suffering of her victims, using silence magic to muffle screams outside the tower.
- Volunteered the book *Dusk* to Orsik with an almost wistful sincerity, suggesting a sentimental streak beneath the cruelty.
- In unguarded moments, at least one of her own spawn visibly rolls his eyes at her grandiose declarations.

## History with the Party

**Pre-party arrival:** Sandesyl arrived at the castle, was unknowingly invited inside by ogres Worug and Tak, and massacred the castle's population. She turned the dead into undead servants, kept kobolds as a living blood bank, fed extensively on Frost Giant Brunvild, and negotiated with the castle's spirit Escarlotta to maintain her presence. After the War of the Dragons, she had similarly remained hidden, transforming the castle's Frost Giant Jarl and new occupants into undead. She arranged with Escarlotta to locate Blagothkus in exchange for continued castle access. She used kobold quarters as a blood farm and carved a lair into the glacier ice outside Glazhael's caves. She holds Glazhael's phylactery as leverage.

**First Contact (tower balcony):** Immediately called Unla Kee an abomination upon seeing him, then launched into an extensive, unsolicited monologue about the true nature of the Cult of the Dragon's corruption.
2. **Cult Intelligence:** Revealed that Severin's actions were part of a plot by **Zariel** to eliminate Tiamat from Avernus, describing Tiamat as "an annoying office mate."
3. **Adimond / Thistle's Connection:** Disclosed that Severin hired **Adimond** to create a fey hybrid dragon. The experiment succeeded but nearly drove the subject mad, causing uncontrolled transformation between two creature forms — more like lycanthropy than true fusion. Confirmed the subject resembles Thistle and is the person Thistle identifies as the love of his life. Asked Thistle if he could transform into a dragon; he cannot.
4. **Castle History:** Recounted that Blagothkus allied with the Cult of the Dragon against smaller peoples; the First Faction captured the castle; it crashed in the north; the First Faction recaptured it; the First Faction previously expelled Sandesyl; Blagothkus traveled to the Spine of the World to commune with his god and returned to find his son dead. She does not know how Blagothkus retook the castle.
5. **Blagothkus:** Noted he has been moping for weeks and offhandedly remarked she would not mind feeding on him.
6. **Orsik's Request:** Refused to lend her zombies for Orsik's play.
7. **Parting Gift:** Before the party departed, handed Orsik a book titled ***Dusk***, explaining it was the reason she wanted to become a vampire.

**Courtyard appearance:** Communicated with the party initially through a zombie spawn under an umbrella. When Thistle revealed a vendetta against House Margastar, she appeared personally in the courtyard surrounded by undead. She attempted to lure Thistle outside (refused), expressed disappointment at his distrust, and stated she would not move against House Margastar because Asmodeus protects them. Negotiated a feeding agreement: the Uthgardt barbarians of Flint Rock.

**Eleasis 1st–2nd (Uthgardt attack):** Her vampire spawn captured a group of Uthgardt nomads mourning a despoiled artifact, brought them to the castle, and fed. They planted manufactured evidence implicating House Margastar and Silverymoon, and left one survivor to spread the false account. Screaming from victims echoed through the castle over subsequent days.

**Tower encounter (midday):** Met the party in her tower, concealed within magical darkness. Offered Thistle transformation into a fey vampire spawn (refused). Proposed a quest to retrieve a magical tome from Xonathal's Keep — lost during Severin's failed Tiamat ritual when Heroes of the Dragon War raided the tower after Iskander's betrayal of the inner circle. The heroes took the Blue Dragon mask but missed the tome. Sandesyl wants it for herself to study and use in her dracolich agenda, particularly to persuade Iymrith. Acknowledged she cannot control Escarlotta directly, forcing her into arrangements with others.

**Progressive deterioration of position:** The party destroyed her wyvernliches, eliminated zombie material, and attacked her Ogre Zombies. She abandoned her tower for the ice cave lair, no longer trusting the party. Blagothkus reported she was conducting experiments using orc zombies and a ghost (two-year-long obsessive cleaning operation in one cave), and had come to him after turning on Brunvild, needing him to operate the castle.

**Lair battle:** Directed minions — vampire spawn, guard drakes, and a failed dracolich skeleton — against the party while remaining hidden and invisible. Targeted Unla Kee as the greatest threat. Clawed him (one hit), then retreated behind the skeletal dragon as cover, which gave Unla Kee an opening for Sunbeam. She screamed "A diviner!" upon realizing he had foreseen the strike. Further burned by Vardis's radiant attack. Overwhelmed by permeating bright light with minions destroyed, she turned to mist. Her visible coffin was found empty; her true secret regeneration location remained unknown to the party.

**Final confrontation (coffin):** Glazhael shattered the glacier ice wall to reveal Sandesyl's hidden coffin. Found inside: her near-death but reforming corpse, and a locked gold-inlaid box containing Glazhael's phylactery. Unla Kee briefly considered using telekinesis to make her a vampire thrall. Orsik pushed him aside and staked her in her coffin, killing her permanently. Her corpse remained in the coffin.

## Current Status
- **Status: DEAD** — staked in her coffin by Orsik; confirmed permanent kill
- **Last known location:** Glacier lair adjacent to Glazhael's cave, within the flying castle complex
- **Glazhael's phylactery** was retrieved from her coffin by the party
- The party later noted parallels between her necromantic experiments and Iymrith's hybrid experiments (referenced by Orsik)

**What the party knows:**
- She is the castellan of the castle
- She has vampire spawn / zombies under her control (number unknown)
- She has long history with Blagothkus and the Cult
- She knows about Adimond's experiment and the fey hybrid dragon subject
- She does not know Adimond's current whereabouts

**What remains hidden:**
- How she became a vampire
- How many spawn she controls
- The full extent of her power (she explicitly warned the party not to underestimate her)

## Relationships
- **Blagothkus:** Long-term employer/liege and transactional partner — she permitted his plots while he supplied victims; when victims dried up, she consumed his minions; later needed him to operate the castle after exhausting other options. Mused about feeding on him in his depressed state.
- **Escarlotta (castle spirit):** Negotiated coexistence; Escarlotta is immune to Sandesyl's charms, which limited Sandesyl's control of the castle.
- **Glazhael the Undying (dracolich):** Held Glazhael's phylactery as leverage; theoretically sought to use recovered lore to further dracolich theology; Glazhael ultimately led the party to Sandesyl's coffin.
- **Iymrith:** Object of Sandesyl's near-romantic devotion; Sandesyl hoped to convert her to dracolich immortality using the Xonathal tome.
- **Worug and Tak (ogres):** Inadvertently invited her in; survived by refusing further invitation into their tower, which she was magically bound to respect.
- **Brunvild (Frost Giant):** Kept alive as a long-term blood source; Sandesyl fed on him extensively.
- **Severin / Cult of the Dragon (First Faction):** Hostile — they previously expelled her from the castle.
- **Adimond / Adimond Kroskas:** Aware of his work creating fey hybrid dragons; does not know his current location.
- **House Margastar / Asmodeus:** Refused to move against House Margastar, citing Asmodeus's protection and unwillingness to disrupt the archfiend's plans.
- **Thistle:** Possesses information directly relevant to his lost love (Laela); tone toward him was pointed and probing (asked if he could transform). Primary negotiation target; she attempted to recruit him as a fey vampire spawn and negotiated the Uthgardt feeding deal with him directly.
- **Unla Kee:** Opened by calling him an abomination; identified as the greatest combat threat; targeted for elimination during the lair battle; Unla Kee briefly considered turning her into a thrall before Orsik intervened.
- **Orsik:** Declined his request but gave him *Dusk* — the most personally revealing gesture she made with anyone in the party. Delivered her permanent death blow.
