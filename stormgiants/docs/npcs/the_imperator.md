# The Imperator

## Identity
- **Role:** Senior advisor / court official to Queen Serissa of the Storm Giant court
- **Faction:** Storm Giant nobility; aligned with the court of Maelstrom
- **First Appearance:** Throne room of Maelstrom; the party encountered him mid-argument with Iymrith (disguised as Adimond Kroskas) upon their arrival

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintaining storm giant supremacy and the stability of the giant court; advising Serissa, however poorly received that advice may be
- Deeply traditionalist and condescending toward lesser giant races, small folk, and anyone outside storm giant nobility — he barely suppressed laughter at the idea of a three-foot fairy threatening an Ancient Blue Dragon
- Politically cautious to a fault; his support for Adimond Kroskas as a "stopgap strategy" reflects pragmatism overriding discernment
- Proud and easily embarrassed; he deflects blame smoothly but crumbles when contradicted publicly

## History with the Party
- **Throne Room Arrival:** Was present arguing with Iymrith/Adimond Kroskas when the party appeared. Stunned by both the party's entrance and Iymrith's dragon reveal.
- **Historical Briefing:** After the chaos settled, provided the party with an extensive account of the Ordning, King Hekaton's reign, Queen Neri's death at the hands of small folk, Hekaton's grief and rage, Princess Serissa's plea for restraint, and Hekaton's decision to name Serissa heir apparent over her elder sisters.
- **Public Humiliation:** Queen Serissa rebuked him directly in front of the party, questioning whether it was his job to know Adimond was a dragon. He attempted to deflect by blaming Adimond's magic — the party immediately and loudly contradicted him, worsening his position.
- **Payment Dispute:** Laughed dismissively at Unla Kee's request for 50,000 gold, calling it preposterous. Was visibly objecting when Serissa authorized the golden suit of scale mail as payment.
- **Tension with Party:** His condescension toward non-storm giants drew a pointed remark from Orsik about his arrogance.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Maelstrom throne room / court
- **Political Standing:** Chastened and weakened following Serissa's public rebuke over the Adimond failure; still present and functional as a court official but with diminished authority
- **Active Operations:** None confirmed; likely working to rebuild standing within the court
- **What the Party Knows:** He backed Adimond Kroskas, was deceived by him, and was publicly dressed down for it. He is dismissive of small folk and lesser giants.
- **What Remains Hidden:** His private views on Serissa's leadership; whether he has any independent agenda or merely serves the court

## Relationships
- **Queen Serissa:** Nominally serves her; relationship strained by her public humiliation of him. He supported a policy she has since repudiated.
- **Iymrith/Adimond Kroskas:** Was deceived by the disguised dragon and actively advocated for an alliance with it — a severe political liability now exposed
- **The Party (general):** Dismissive and condescending; views small folk as beneath concern. The party has directly contradicted and embarrassed him in open court.
- **Orsik (specifically):** Orsik called out his arrogance explicitly over his treatment of lesser giants.
- **Unla Kee (specifically):** Openly mocked her payment demand and her ability to threaten a dragon

## Arc Score Events
- **Decrease — Public Contradiction:** The party immediately and loudly refuted his claim that Adimond's magic deceived everyone, undercutting his deflection in front of Serissa and the court.
- **Decrease — Serissa's Rebuke:** Queen Serissa's pointed public questioning of his competence constitutes a significant loss of standing.
