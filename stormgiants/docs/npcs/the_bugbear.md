# The Bugbear (Unnamed)

## Identity
- Role: Lone survivor / dungeon delver; no faction affiliation
- First appearance: Encountered inside the tower's interdimensional dungeon during the party's exploration; introduced himself to Vardis and Unla Kee as a long-term inhabitant

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goal:** Survive and find the secret door out — though ultimately, the lure of treasure proved stronger than escape
- Pragmatic and darkly humorous; casually describes eating trapped goblins and quotes his mother's proverbs about "walking meat bags"
- Self-reliant and stubborn — he has rejected both the zombie-acceptance and death-expulsion theories of escape, trusting only his own search
- Loyalty is transactional; he joined the party for food and departed the moment treasure became more appealing than freedom

## History with the Party
1. **First meeting:** Found alive inside the dungeon, having survived by eating other trapped creatures (goblins). Explained the three escape theories held by dungeon inhabitants.
2. **Alliance formed:** Joined Vardis and Unla Kee after they offered food. Provided a hand-drawn map of the dungeon and briefed the party on its inhabitants: a goblin tribe, ogre zombies, and himself.
3. **Mimic fight:** His morning star became lodged during the encounter. Continued fighting and helped finish the mimic after Unla Kee put it to sleep.
4. **Departure:** When the party located the staircase exit, the bugbear refused to leave. He turned back into the dungeon, apparently choosing to pursue more treasure over escape.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Deeper inside the tower's interdimensional dungeon
- **Active:** Presumably continuing to search for treasure and/or the secret door
- **Party knows:** He voluntarily remained; his fate after that point is unknown
- **Hidden:** Whether he survives, finds what he's looking for, or eventually escapes is unresolved

## Relationships
- **Vardis & Unla Kee:** Brief, functional alliance — he worked with them, contributed meaningfully in combat, and parted on neutral terms; no lasting bond established
- **Dungeon inhabitants (goblins, ogre zombies):** Hostile or predatory relationship; he has been eating the goblins
