# Imber Chen

## Identity
- **Role:** Gnome merchant and fabric trader; stall operator at the Fishyard, Yartar; dockside intelligence source
- **Faction:** No known affiliation; aware of the Harpers, Zhentarim, and Lord's Alliance but expresses pointed opinions about each
- **First Appearance:** Party approached his Fishyard stall seeking intelligence on Silverymoon (also encountered by the 3HPP on the docks in an earlier session)

*Also known as: Imber.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Running a successful merchant operation; trading in luxury dyed fabrics; maintaining his commercial relationships and reputation on the river trade routes
- Imber is knowledgeable, commercially minded, and quick to turn any social situation to his advantage — he converted a tense cultural exchange into a paying advertising deal.
- He holds conventional, exclusionary views about race and personhood (excluding half-orcs from his vision of Silverymoon's "peaceful" multi-racial society) and about fairy biology, and he does not yield these views gracefully under pressure.
- He is enthusiastic about culture and theatre, at least when he believes it aligns with his values.

## History with the Party
1. **Earlier dockside intelligence (pre-current session):** Imber met with the 3HPP on the docks and provided key intelligence — that the Grand Dame was owned by Lord Khaspere Drylund, and that Drylund was manipulating votes to become the next Waterbaron. This information directly shaped the party's decision to approach the Grand Dame as a site connected to the disappearance of the Storm Giant King.
2. **Initial Contact (Fishyard stall):** Party approached Imber for information about Silverymoon. He was startled by Unla Kee's presence and made a dismissive comment about fairy reproduction, citing a great sage's work.
3. **Tense Exchange:** Orsik challenged his heteronormative assumptions. Imber was drawn into the play promotion when Orsik leveraged the moment; Imber expressed genuine enthusiasm, believing it to be a progressive theatrical production. He noted Silverymoon was a two-day journey and asked about accommodations.
4. **Intelligence Provided:** Gave the party a substantive briefing on Silverymoon — its beauty, Lord's Alliance influence, the Waterbarron's alliance with the city, and his characterizations of the Harpers (anarchist chaos) and Zhentarim (exploiters). When pressed on Silverymoon's racial inclusivity, he clarified he meant only the "standard" civilized races, pointedly excluding half-orcs and declining Orsik's neutral framing.
5. **Cap'n Squint Disclosure:** Volunteered that Cap'n Squint, a halfling river captain and former friend, would be gambling that evening aboard the Grand Dame (Lord Khaspere Drylund's luxury boat) and departing the next morning.
6. **Commercial Deal Concluded:** Stopped the party from leaving without a transaction. Negotiated 43 days of banner advertising at his stall for a half gold piece. Rejected Orsik's affiliate coupon-code commission model (insufficient data, no tracking mechanism). Offered an introduction to the Hand of Yartar for a stall — rendered unnecessary when Thistle suggested Imber host the banner himself. Deal closed with a handshake.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** His stall at the Fishyard, Yartar
- **Active:** Displaying the party's promotional banner for 43 days
- **What the Party Knows:** His stall location, his knowledge of river routes and Silverymoon politics, his connection to Cap'n Squint, his contact in the Hand of Yartar
- **What Remains Hidden:** The nature and depth of his Hand of Yartar connection; the full story of his falling out with Cap'n Squint

## Relationships
- **Cap'n Squint:** Former friend; drifted apart. Imber still knows Squint's movements well enough to report them in detail.
- **Hand of Yartar:** Has at least one contact able to secure merchant stalls — degree of involvement unknown
- **Lord Khaspere Drylund:** Aware of him as owner of the Grand Dame; no stated relationship
- **Orsik:** Strained. Imber lost two cultural arguments and ceded commercial ground; unlikely to be warm.
- **Unla Kee:** Made an inadvertently offensive first impression.
- **Thistle:** Neutral to positive; Thistle's practical suggestion resolved the stall-introduction question cleanly.
