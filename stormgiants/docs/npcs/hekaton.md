# King Hekaton

## Identity
- **Role:** Storm Giant King; ruler of the Maelstrom and sovereign of the Ordning; lord of the giant court
- **Faction:** Storm Giants / the Maelstrom court; aligned with the Lord's Alliance
- **First Appearance:** Discussed in an earlier session via oracle consultation; physically encountered when the party rescued him from a Kraken Society prison ship at sea

*Also known as: King Hekaton.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Restore the Ordning by compelling all five giant faction leaders to kneel before him; ultimately destroy Iymrith, the blue dragon behind the plot against his kingdom; avenge Queen Neri's death.
- **Secondary drives:** Preserve the age of giants, though he has privately concluded it may be over; seek signs from Annam the Allfather as validation; dynastic duty and political order.
- Hekaton is decisive and authoritative — within moments of regaining his footing, he was restructuring his court and stripping titles.
- He is also proud, domineering, and emotionally volatile — he insists on speaking first, drowns out urgent news, and responds to challenge with fury before guilt catches up with him. He is not cruel, but he is not wise.
- His bad political decisions (naming Serissa heir prematurely, abandoning his court on a vengeance quest) haunt him visibly. He seeks face-saving exits rather than open acknowledgment of error.
- He does not dwell on his captivity; he pivots immediately to governance and threat response. He expresses visible frustration at being politically obligated before he can pursue Iymrith personally, and is willing to deal with the party pragmatically to accelerate that outcome.

## History with the Party

**Before the party met him:**
Queen Neri was found dead on a coastal island, killed by small folk. Hekaton was devastated and nearly retaliated against coastal territories before Serissa persuaded him to seek the truth first. He then disappeared while investigating her death.

**During the investigation (sessions prior to his rescue):**
The oracle identified Hekaton as the storm giant king, directed the party to use his magic conch to reach his court, and revealed that his disappearance had thrown the storm giant realm into chaos. The party learned via Unla Kee that a gambling token found near Neri's body traced to Lord Drylund and the Golden Goose in Yartar. Under duress, Drylund revealed that the Kraken Society had been holding Hekaton prisoner aboard a specially undetectable ship, the *Morkoth*, sailing the northern Trackless Sea. Iymrith had engineered the kidnapping to remove Hekaton from power and leave Serissa without the authority to maintain order.

**Rescue from the Kraken Society:** The party — led by Orsik and Vardis — broke Hekaton's chains aboard a Kraken Society ship under assault from an archmage and the ship's first mate. Thistle revived Orsik mid-fight with a goodberry. The group used the Conch of Teleportation to escape with Hekaton to the Maelstrom grotto.

**Arrival at the Maelstrom:** Hekaton was briefly disoriented ("Where am I? Who are you?"). Once the party explained Serissa had sent them, he demanded to go to the throne room immediately.

**Court reckoning:** In the throne room, Hekaton reunited with Serissa, Uthor, Nym, and Mirran. He questioned Uthor directly about the search for him; on learning Serissa had borne the burden of hope alone, he acted: stripped Uthor of the Imperatorship, appointed Serissa in his place, and had Nym and Mirran confined to their chambers for betraying him, Serissa, and the realm by allying with a dragon.

**Deal offered to the party (post-restoration):** Hekaton laid out his plan to restore the Ordning by compelling Chief Guh, Thane Kayalithca, Jarl Storvald, Countess Sansuri, and Duke Zalto to kneel before him. He offered the party a bargain: defeat or reform those faction leaders, and he will join them for the final battle against Iymrith. **Orsik declined on behalf of the group**; the rest of the party was less certain.

**First physical appearance to the party at the Maelstrom (post-rescue):**
Hekaton observed that Forbiddance was blocking a teleportation attempt, attributing it to the Cult of the Kraken. He teleported the party to just outside Ironslag the following morning as a parting gesture of hospitality.

He also communicated with the SEER, sharing the party's whereabouts and vouching for them as allies. He indicated he would consider a permanent alliance between the Giant King and the Lord's Alliance if the party handled his rogue giant lord problem — and insisted it had to be specifically these four adventurers, citing it as a sign from the Allfather. The SEER confirmed he refused alternative agents.

**Return to the Maelstrom (most recent session):**
Hekaton was initially dismissive and domineering when the party returned with intelligence about traitors gathering at Iymrith's lair. He claimed his hands were tied, drawing a blunt rebuke from Orsik. When Serissa erupted at him, he responded with fury before being visibly stalled by his own guilt. He used Unla Kee's divination as a face-saving mechanism to agree to the full assault on Iymrith's lair. He accepted Orsik's proposed five-day delay for preparation. The battle is now set.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** The Maelstrom, his storm giant domain (seated on the Wyrmskull Throne, obsidian crown on, Serissa installed as Imperator)
- **Active plans:** Full assault on Iymrith's lair, delayed five days by agreement with the party. Reconsolidating giant power under the restored Ordning; intends to personally confront Iymrith once political order is secured.
- **What the party knows:** His full situation — the Kraken Society capture, the court betrayal, Iymrith's role, his restoration plan, his emotional volatility and guilt, his alliance intentions with the Lord's Alliance, and his reverence for the party as instruments of the Allfather's will.
- **What remains hidden:** His decision on the fates of Nym and Mirran; his precise timeline and methods for compelling the faction leaders; whether he will act against Iymrith independently if the party does not take his deal. Only Hekaton knows Iymrith's true lair location — this information is now operationally relevant to the coming assault.

## Relationships
- **Serissa (daughter):** Deep trust; appointed her Imperator. She bore the weight of the kingdom in his absence and he recognizes it. Their relationship is also strained — he named her heir apparent years ago, a decision he may now recognize as politically damaging. She confronted him directly in the throne room; he responded with fury but was shaken.
- **Mirren & Nym (daughters):** Betrayed him and Serissa by allying with Iymrith. Currently confined; their fates are undecided. His premature naming of Serissa drove them into alliance with Iymrith. He has not addressed this openly.
- **Queen Neri (wife, deceased):** His grief over her death triggered his disappearance and the entire crisis. The vengeance quest and the chaos it caused remain a source of unresolved guilt.
- **Uthor (former Imperator, brother):** Stripped of rank for failing to support Serissa during the crisis. Relationship is cold at best.
- **Iymrith ("Doom of the Desert"):** Recognized the name. Holds her responsible for the plot against his kingdom; she engineered his kidnapping and exploited his grief. He possesses unique intelligence on her lair location; frustrated he cannot pursue her immediately.
- **The SEER:** Correspondent and intermediary. He shared the party's location with her and used her as a channel to communicate alliance intentions.
- **Unla Kee:** Recognized as a diviner; used Unla Kee's recommendation as justification to greenlight the assault — a face-saving move, but a genuine one.
- **Orsik:** Orsik attempted to reach him through his guilt; Hekaton was too overwhelmed for the words to fully land, but the interaction was not hostile. Hekaton respects Orsik's directness even when it stings. Orsik declined his initial deal outright — Hekaton is aware the refusal came from him.
- **The Party (3HPP):** Owes them his freedom. Considers them heroes and instruments of the Allfather's will. Has vouched for them to the Lord's Alliance and refuses to work with any other agents.
