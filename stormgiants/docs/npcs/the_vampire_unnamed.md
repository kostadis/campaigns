# The Vampire (Unnamed)

## Identity
- **Role:** Researcher / jailer operating within the prison beneath Yartar
- **Faction:** Operates under sanction from Yartar's highest authorities; tolerated autonomy rather than true loyalty to the Waterbarron
- **First Appearance:** Encountered in an incongruously well-appointed room inside the prison — bookshelves, a desk, a table with a skeleton, and several zombies under her command. The party approached her seeking the release of Harthal.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goal:** Conducting controlled experiments on prisoners, tightly regulating air, food, water, and emotional stimulation. Research appears to be her primary drive.
- Calm, businesslike, and precise — she consulted her records before agreeing to any action and cited experimental contamination as grounds to restrict Thistle's movements.
- Contemptuous of the Waterbarron's ability to control her; laughed openly at the notion that his guards could monitor or contain her. She does not consider herself subordinate in any meaningful sense.
- Pragmatic in negotiation — accepted the guards' corpses as a fair trade without hesitation, visibly pleased.

## History with the Party
1. **Thistle** approached her requesting Harthal's release. She was cooperative but firm, refusing to allow Thistle past her threshold to avoid contaminating experimental conditions.
2. **Orsik (disguised as Zymorven)** offered the bodies of slain guards as payment. She accepted, smiled wide enough to reveal her fangs, and released Harthal.
3. Before Harthal left, she whispered a warning to him — if he returned to the city and was found, she would be compelled to kill him the moment he came back down. The warning was implicitly directed at Orsik-as-Zymorven as well, making clear they needed to leave Yartar that night.

## Current Status
- **Location:** Prison complex beneath Yartar
- **Active:** Ongoing prisoner experiments, sanctioned by Yartar's highest authorities
- **What the party knows:** She is a vampire, a researcher, holds Harthal's life in her hands if he returns, and has no real loyalty to the Waterbarron despite working under his sanction
- **Hidden from the party:** Her name, the full scope or purpose of her experiments, the nature of her arrangement with Yartar's authorities, and the extent of her movements throughout the city

## Relationships
- **The Waterbarron:** A relationship of tolerated autonomy. She has official sanction but openly mocks his belief that he can constrain her. She is not his asset — she merely operates within his structure when convenient.
- **Harthal:** Held him as an experimental subject; released him under negotiated terms. Issued him a death warning on return.
- **Thistle:** Interacted with directly; was cooperative but kept at arm's length.
- **Orsik (as Zymorven):** Negotiated with directly; she accepted his terms. May believe she dealt with Zymorven rather than Orsik.
