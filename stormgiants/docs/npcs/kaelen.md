# Kaelen Sarssir

## Identity
- **Role:** Proprietor and barkeep of the Talking Troll / Troll Tavern (Triboar) — a run-down dive tavern with a partially burned, unrepaired roof where birds nest; playwright, actor, and producer with theatrical ambitions
- **Faction:** No confirmed affiliation; independent operator; primary suspect in the Lionshield Coster theft (Triboar)
- **First appearance:** The party visited the Talking Troll while tracking down Uncle Joon; Kaelen served as their first point of contact regarding Joon's tab and situation. Later named by Darz Helgar as the individual who coerced him into stealing interplanar supply chain keys from the Lion's Share vault.

*Also known as: Kaelen.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Recognition as a creative genius; revenge against a theater-going public he felt had never appreciated him — originally pursued through mass murder, now apparently supplanted by the sudden taste of fame. Earlier ambition: transform the Talking Troll into a legitimate theater capable of drawing wealthy patrons from Waterdeep, inspired by seeing *The Inn of Shadows* off the High Road.
- **Secondary Goals:** Fix the inn's roof, hire a magic special effects crew, stage an original production, make a name for himself.
- Theatrical, flamboyant, and self-described as a method actor collecting material for his art — but beneath the dreamy persona is a cold, calculating opportunist willing to blackmail a criminal to get what he wants.
- His poor acting makes him a transparent liar under direct pressure, which undercuts his dramatic self-image.
- Emotionally fragile and collapses quickly under criticism, but becomes defensive and evasive when cornered about things that matter to him — particularly the endings of his work.
- Vain enough that genuine acclaim derailed a premeditated mass murder plot on the spot.
- His relationship to Bob Saget has since evolved into active collaboration, suggesting his goals and loyalties may have shifted in ways the party does not fully understand.

## History with the Party

**First Contact (tracking Uncle Joon):**
The party met Kaelen at the Talking Troll while looking into Uncle Joon. Kaelen managed Joon's tab (30 gold, inclusive of surcharges) and tolerated Joon's moaning, framing it as method acting research. Vardis asked if he was an actor; Kaelen confirmed enthusiastically.

**Wine Cellar Boast:**
Kaelen showed off his cellar, including a sparkling wine called *Champagne du le Stomp*, which he claimed came from a hidden dimension ruled by a vampire and was sold to him by an elf as the last of its kind. Uncle Joon dismissed this as fiction, suggesting Kaelen simply inherited the cellar full.

**Blackmail Revealed:**
Darz Helgar confessed to the party that Kaelen had discovered his criminal past and threatened to expose him to the Lord Protector unless Darz stole the Lionshield Coster's gold and keys. Orsik promised Darz the party would deal with Kaelen.

**Confrontation & Deal:**
The party confronted Kaelen at the Talking Troll. He first invited them to act out scenes from a script in his cellar. When Orsik directly accused him regarding the Lionshield Coster key, Kaelen turned pale, denied knowledge, and immediately gave himself away through transparently bad acting. He admitted the scheme and his motivations.

**The Agreement:**
Orsik negotiated a 70/30 split of the Talking Troll's gross revenue in exchange for the party returning the key and keeping quiet about Kaelen's role. Kaelen accepted. He surrendered both the stolen key and a bottle of *Champagne du le Stomp* to Orsik as part of the arrangement.

**Early dealings (pre-Triboar production):**
Orsik made a deal with Kaelen to produce a play. Kaelen borrowed 5,000 gold pieces in the party's name, then used the funds to purchase the theater property outright rather than renting it. He had not yet written the play when the party departed for Yartar; Thistle had to improvise a description of it to a gate guard. A production deadline of the 5th day of the 3rd tenday of Mirtul was extracted only after Orsik threatened physical harm.

**The Triboar production:**
When the party arrived at the Talking Troll, Kaelen's play was critically deficient in every dimension. Confronted by Unla Kee, he broke down immediately, retreated, and rewrote the script multiple times — first in archaic common tongue, then in a cleaner form incorporating a "play within a play" framing device suggested by Unla Kee. Throughout the revision process, he was unusually protective of the play's ending and resisted any changes to it; Unla Kee required extraordinary measures to influence that section at all.

**Opening night and the revelation:**
The play performed successfully. The finale triggered the summoning ritual embedded in the original script — but because the party had improved the production, the ritual misfired. Instead of a destructive demon consuming the audience, Bob Saget was summoned. Kaelen's revenge plot collapsed entirely when the crowd responded with acclaim. He abandoned his murderous intentions on the spot and declared he was going to be famous.

**Post-opening:**
Kaelen served wine during the party's meeting with Urlam Stockspool at the Talking Troll, playing a minor hosting role with no notable incidents.

**Current deteriorated state:**
Thistle observed Kaelen sitting inside his house in Silverymoon. The door had not been opened in some time, dust covered the residence, and something appeared visibly wrong with him. The party debated killing him but decided against it, reasoning he may be actively powering Bob Saget's presence and that killing him would alert Bob and eliminate their tactical advantage.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** His house in Silverymoon — door sealed, dust-covered, apparently in a deteriorated or entranced condition
- **Active involvement:** Per Bob Saget's own account, Kaelen and Bob are actively collaborating to improve the play; the party was unaware of this ongoing relationship until Bob disclosed it
- **What the party knows:** Kaelen wrote a summoning ritual disguised as a play; his original plan was the mass murder of Triboar; he is now in an alarming physical state
- **What remains hidden:** The nature and full extent of his collaboration with Bob Saget; whether his current condition is voluntary, coerced, or the result of the ritual; his connection to the Lionshield Coster theft and any criminal organization behind it

## Relationships
- **Bob Saget:** Active collaborator post-summoning; Bob credits Kaelen's play with saving him from "an eternity of bad dad jokes." The power dynamic and terms of this arrangement are unknown.
- **Orsik:** Primary negotiating counterpart and business partner under duress. Orsik holds financial leverage over Kaelen via the 70/30 deal and knowledge of his crimes; Kaelen responds to Orsik's threats and has financial obligations to him.
- **Unla Kee:** Served as creative editor during rewrites; Unla Kee recognizes Bob as evil incarnate and by extension views Kaelen's work with deep suspicion.
- **Thistle:** Conducted the observation of Kaelen's Silverymoon residence; noted his deteriorated state firsthand.
- **Darz Helgar:** Blackmail victim. Kaelen exploited knowledge of Darz's criminal past to coerce him into theft. Relationship is now exposed.
- **Uncle Joon:** Joon is a long-suffering regular whose tab Kaelen inflates. Kaelen tolerates him as "material."
- **Ha-Ha Pinkerton:** Previous owner of the Talking Troll. Sold cheaply and disappeared — connection unclear.
- **Urlam Stockspool:** Interacted briefly during a Talking Troll meeting; no deeper relationship established.
- **The Party (general):** Reluctant business partners and de facto loan sharks. Kaelen accepted the arrangement, suggesting he sees it as the least bad option.

## Arc Score Events
- **Decrease:** Revealed as the blackmailer behind Darz Helgar's theft of the Lionshield Coster key.
- **Increase (partial):** Cooperated fully once cornered, admitted the scheme honestly, and honored the negotiated 70/30 deal without further deception.
- **Decrease:** Revealed as the architect of a planned mass murder via demonic summoning ritual disguised as community theater.
- **Increase (partial):** Abandoned the murder plot when overcome by genuine public acclaim — suggesting vanity as a stabilizing force, however unreliable.
- **Ambiguous:** Ongoing collaboration with Bob Saget following the ritual; the party is uncertain whether this represents continued danger or a changed man.
