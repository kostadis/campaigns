# Everard Barney

## Identity
- **Role:** Theater director of Kaelen's play at the Talking Troll, Triboar
- **Faction:** None; formerly a bookkeeper from Waterdeep, now attempting a career in theater
- **First Appearance:** Met Thistle at the Talking Troll during play rehearsals; introduced himself as fully committed to Kaelen's vision before immediately breaking down

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** Make the play succeed so he never has to return to Waterdeep to work as an accountant; his entire family are bookkeepers and theater is his escape from that life
- Emotionally volatile and quick to sob, but genuinely receptive to useful guidance once he calms down
- Lacks confidence in managing people — he has never thought to ask his actors what they want and resorts to shouting as his primary directorial tool
- Eager and enthusiastic when given a framework he can understand; he latches on to reframings quickly and makes them his own

## History with the Party
- **Rehearsal at the Talking Troll:** When Thistle arrived, Everard broke down sobbing almost immediately, confessing his fear of failure, his discomfort around Kaelen, and his inability to control his cast. Thistle coached him through two specific problems: (1) staging — Everard had planned to place lead actor Amy atop a dung heap for her iconic soliloquy as a thematic contrast; Thistle redirected him toward a hill with the dung below, letting Amy appear to elevate the audience above hardship rather than sit in it; Everard accepted this enthusiastically. (2) Drunken actors — Everard was tempted to incorporate their drunkenness into the performance but ultimately chose to scream at them in the morning instead, bonding with Thistle over competing methods of managing drunk people (his uncle's screaming vs. Thistle's bucket of water). At the end of rehearsal, he sheepishly asked Thistle about the finale and accepted her verdict to change nothing.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The Talking Troll theater, Triboar
- **Active Operations:** Preparing Kaelen's play for performance; has revised his staging approach following Thistle's coaching
- **What the Party Knows:** His family background, his fear of returning to Waterdeep, his discomfort with Kaelen, his general incompetence as a director
- **What Remains Hidden:** Nothing significant revealed as concealed — his anxieties are entirely on the surface

## Relationships
- **Kaelen:** His playwright; Everard is professionally committed to Kaelen's vision but personally creeped out by them
- **Amy (lead actor):** Struggles to direct her; now has a workable framework for handling her thanks to Thistle
- **Drunken Actors:** Cannot control them; plans to address via morning screaming
- **Thistle:** Received meaningful help from her and responded warmly; views her as a competent, trustworthy advisor; shared a genuine friendly moment with her
