# Serissa

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Youngest daughter of King Hekaton and Queen Neri; Princess of the Maelstrom; current reigning queen of the storm giant court (heir apparent named by Hekaton himself); Imperator of the Maelstrom (appointed by Hekaton on his return, replacing Uthor)
- **Faction:** Storm giant court at the Maelstrom (underwater citadel)
- **First Appearance:** Throne-room scene observed by Thistle during reconnaissance; first met directly when the party emerged through the Vortex pool into the throne room during her confrontation with Adimond Kroskas (Iymrith in disguise)

*Also known as: Queen Serissa, Imperator Serissa, Princess Serissa.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Find her missing father King Hekaton (later: hold him accountable); stabilize her rule; rebuild the storm giant court's power; form alliances with small folk in the tradition of her late mother Queen Neri; resist the influence of her treacherous sisters; defeat threats like the Kraken Society and Iymrith.
- Composed and commanding under pressure — she took control of the throne room immediately after Iymrith fled, repeatedly calling for silence and demanding clear answers.
- Politically shrewd, visibly aware of compromises she has made out of desperation, and willing to be diplomatically accommodating when it serves her interests.
- Carries guilt or at minimum self-awareness about her cooperation with Iymrith; she frames it as a stopgap born of necessity rather than good judgment.
- Projects compassion beneath the power of her voice and bearing — Thistle concluded there is a reason she and not her sisters is Queen.
- Capable of fierce anger (snarling, nearly rising from her throne) but exercises restraint when compelled to. Capable of fierce, articulate anger when pushed past her limits.
- Carries long-suppressed frustration toward her father that finally broke open on his return; frames her actions in terms of honor and contrast with those she sees as cowards or abdicators.
- Duty-bound and pragmatic — she paid for divination magic out of her own resources to locate Hekaton's prison ship and persisted when no one believed her.
- When her moment of vindication arrives, she is noted as enjoying it perhaps a little too much.

## History with the Party

**Throne Room (initial reconnaissance — Thistle):**
Defended the party when her sisters Mirran and Nym accused them of being assassins, calling the sisters liars. She nearly rose from her throne in anger when Mirran argued she and Nym should be Queen, but was physically restrained by Imperator Uthor. She dismissed Adminond's report on the dragon-fairy hybrid experiments, stating she already knew about them and that finding her father would render them unnecessary. Expressed a need for trustworthy agents to search for Hekaton.

**First Meeting (Throne Room):**
The party arrived through the Vortex pool mid-argument between Serissa and Iymrith (disguised as Adimond Kroskas). Serissa witnessed Iymrith's true form revealed and her flight. She recovered quickly, took command of the room, and interrogated the party. She produced a gambling chit found on the bodies of small folk responsible for Queen Neri's death, presenting it as a lead to Hekaton's location. When Unla Kee identified the chit as coming from the Golden Goose boat in Yartar (run by Lord Drylund), she accepted his offer to investigate, authorized payment via a golden suit of scale mail from the armory — with a transparent fiction that it was for "research" — and winked to signal she knew exactly what she was doing. She issued a clear warning: if Unla Kee took the money without producing results, there would be consequences. She also ordered her sisters Mirren and Nym escorted to their quarters when they disrupted proceedings.

**Second Referenced Appearance (Maelstrom):**
Serissa appeared at the Maelstrom and observed Thistle's dragon eyes with dry amusement, redirecting the group back to the priority of finding Hekaton. She held up the wooden coin bearing the golden goose, referencing intelligence from a mage. Her past cooperation with Iymrith was later confirmed by Sandesyl and criticized by Blagothkus as foolish.

**Teleportation Chamber (Early Encounter):**
Serissa made a direct, personal appeal to the 3HPP to cooperate on the shared threat facing giants and smaller folk alike. When Orsik refused, she accepted the outcome without escalating and attempted — unsuccessfully — to teleport the party home (blocked by Forbiddance). She explained the Kraken Society's concealment capabilities and acknowledged the limits of her own intelligence-gathering efforts.

**Reunion and Promotion:**
When Hekaton was returned, Serissa was described as delighted. Hekaton asked her directly to explain the situation, and her account exposed Mirran and Nym's failures and betrayals fully. In recognition of her loyalty and diligence, Hekaton appointed Serissa as the new Imperator of the Maelstrom, granting her Uthor's insignia of office. She immediately ordered guards to confine Nym and Mirran when they protested.

**Maelstrom Throne Room (later session — confrontation with Hekaton):**
Upon the 3HPP's return, Serissa was visibly tense before the confrontation began — both Vardis and Orsik noted her displeasure; Hekaton did not. When her father claimed his hands were tied and Orsik challenged him, Serissa broke. She publicly catalogued her grievances against Hekaton:
- He declared her two sisters unqualified as Queen, inadvertently pushing them toward seizing the position by other means.
- He abandoned the kingdom on a personal vengeance quest when the Ordning shattered, rather than ruling.
- No one believed her when she insisted he was still alive.
- He is not the giant she remembers.

She also invoked Thistle's sacrifice — setting aside his personal search to save the world — as a direct rebuke to her father's abandonment. When Thistle revealed Iymrith would attend the coming gathering, Serissa immediately declared she would lead the palace guard to defend their honor, framing Hekaton as a coward by contrast. This triggered open screaming between father and daughter.

**Referenced in Party Discussions:**
Thistle's conspiracy account establishes that Serissa convinced Hekaton to restrain himself and investigate rather than go to war after Queen Neri's death — a decision that left her without credibility when Hekaton was subsequently kidnapped. Desperate for support, she was then approached by Hekaton's elder sisters, who introduced her to Iymrith and promised her the throne. Serissa agreed to assist Iymrith's fairy hybridization experiments in exchange for backing.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The Maelstrom, Hekaton's stronghold
- **Active Operations:** Intends to mobilize the palace guard to confront Iymrith at the coming gathering; managing fallout from Iymrith's infiltration and her sisters' treachery; managing the aftermath of Hekaton's return
- **What the Party Knows:** Serissa cooperated with Iymrith out of desperation; she is aware the cooperation was a mistake; she has been largely transparent about her current emotional state and grievances against Hekaton; she has funded the party's investigation; she now holds significant authority as Imperator
- **What Remains Hidden:** Full extent of what she agreed to in her arrangement with Iymrith; whether her sisters' promise of "the throne" implies ongoing contact with conspirators; the details of the broader conspiracy potentially implicating one of Hekaton's closest advisors; her longer-term intentions as Imperator

## Relationships
- **King Hekaton (father):** Was missing for the first arc; she ruled in his absence without his full authority or resources, and fought to prove he was alive. His return has not repaired the damage — she sees him as diminished, avoidant, and culpable for the chaos that followed his disappearance. Deeply strained but rewarded with the Imperatorship.
- **Queen Neri (mother, deceased):** Serissa explicitly echoes Neri's philosophy of allying with small folk; Neri's death is the event that set the current crisis in motion.
- **Mirren & Nym (elder sisters / aunts of the line):** Hostile; named unfit to rule by Hekaton; resent Serissa's appointment; were involved in introducing Iymrith to Serissa; had to be physically removed from the throne room by guards. Serissa now holds authority over their confinement. She believes Hekaton's judgment of the elders as unqualified inadvertently drove their ambitions and caused downstream harm.
- **Hekaton's Sisters:** Believes Hekaton's judgment of them as unqualified inadvertently drove their ambitions.
- **Iymrith (ancient blue dragon):** Former de facto ally out of necessity; Serissa had doubted the Adimond alliance but lacked the power to refuse; Iymrith has now fled.
- **The Imperator (Uthor, formerly):** Serissa held him accountable for failing to detect Iymrith's deception, pointedly embarrassing him in front of the court; he physically restrained her during the first throne room scene; his failure led to his replacement by Serissa.
- **Adminond Kroskas:** Received his report on the dragon-fairy hybrid experiments; she was dismissive, calling the experiments unnecessary if Hekaton were found.
- **Unla Kee:** Commissioned as primary investigator into Hekaton's disappearance; warned of consequences for failure.
- **Blagothkus:** Views her cooperation with Iymrith as foolish; not an ally of Serissa's choices.
- **Thistle (party member):** Specifically invoked his personal sacrifice as a moral example in her condemnation of her father — she sees his choices as the standard Hekaton failed to meet.
- **Orsik (party):** Noticed Serissa and attempted to look dashing; acknowledges internally that she will someday be Queen. She accepted his refusal of her early appeal with dignity.
- **The 3HPP (Party):** Cautiously allied; cooperative but not close; Serissa sees them as useful small folk agents operating in a world her giants cannot easily reach; she extended unusual trust and resources to them and shares common enemies and common ground.

## Arc Score Events
- **Positive:** Welcomed party as allies; corrected "little people" slip diplomatically when Orsik took offense; authorized payment and resources for the investigation; expressed genuine desire to honor Queen Neri's philosophy of small folk alliance.
- **Negative (toward party trust):** Issued explicit warning to Unla Kee about consequences for taking payment without results.
- **Increase:** Appointment as Imperator of the Maelstrom following Hekaton's return, in recognition of loyalty and diligence (Uthor's simultaneous failure contributed).
- **Significant character moment:** Throne room confrontation — Serissa publicly breaks with Hekaton (relationship with Hekaton: sharp decrease).
- **Implicit positive regard for Thistle:** Invokes his sacrifice as moral contrast.
