# Darathra Shendrel

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Lord Protector of Triboar; covert/Harper Agent
- **Faction:** Harpers (affiliated; vouched for the party through Krowen Valharrow)
- **Origin:** Originally from Waterdeep — elected to Lord Protector of Triboar with expectations of "Waterdeep magic" she may not always have delivered
- **First Appearance:** Not present in person at first mention — introduced by reference when Dral Thelev recognized the platinum brooch the party carried as hers, noting "something must be amiss." First met in person on the battlefield during the fire giant attack on Triboar.

*Also known as: Darathra, Darthara, Darthala, Darlathara, Darthara Shendrel, The Lord Protector.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintain her political authority as Lord Protector; protect Triboar and its citizens; gather intelligence on the giant incursion; coordinate Harper interests in the region; neutralize rivals (particularly Cindrell); stabilize Triboar under Harper-aligned governance; aim to win the next election by a landslide once the current political situation resolves; maintain her professional standing and Harper relationships; clear debts cleanly before moving on.
- Politically shrewd and calculating — her reaction to Cindrell's contract was described as "a cat about to pounce on a rat."
- Cautious and pragmatic under pressure — her instinct is to monitor threats, minimize casualties, and document rather than escalate.
- Exercises cautious, pragmatic authority — willing to act on evidence but asserts control over the party's movements.
- Prefers leverage and institutional pressure over direct confrontation, as seen in her approach to both Cindrell and Hyuth.
- Warm toward useful allies — having already praised the party publicly before they arrived to her tower meeting.
- Pragmatic and blunt — names grievances directly but is willing to set them aside when the stakes demand it.
- Holds people accountable (challenged the elder dwarf on his parenting; called out Vardis on his association with Orsik) but is not vindictive. Honors her commitments: once she agrees to help, she declares all debts cleared afterward.
- Has a short fuse when she perceives incompetence, and does not forgive public disrespect.
- Prioritizes outcomes and consequences over credit; defaults to Harper-style plausible deniability when things go wrong.
- Can be blunt and accusatory when she feels others have acted recklessly or unfairly, as shown in her confrontation with Thistle.
- Despite her fear during the fire giant battle, she maintains authority and keeps focus on recovery and order once the danger passes.

## History with the Party

**Before the Session:** The platinum brooch the party carried was identified by Dral Thelev as Darathra's; its presence in their hands led him to suspect something was wrong with her. Krowen Valharrow separately noted she had prepared a coffer of spell scrolls as a "birthday gift" — apparently pre-positioned for exactly this kind of quest recruitment.

**Mirak/Tolmara Affair:** Accepted Mirak's remains as evidence and agreed to imprison Tolmara until a cleric capable of *Speak with Dead* could be located. Ordered the party to remain in town for the following day due to the commotion they caused.

**Fire Giant Attack:** Darathra was present on the battlefield, visibly frightened, and retreated into the tower after Orsik shouted a warning. She warned those inside to stay away from the giants. She was killed during the battle and subsequently revived by Thistle's spell.

**Post-Battle Confrontation:** She confronted Thistle after the fight, accusing her of coming through unscathed while others nearly died. She physically stopped Thistle from firing on the second fire giant and the orc, insisting the town sit quietly and pray the giant left without further destruction. She advocated for monitoring and "copious notes."

**Recovery Efforts:** Went to the market square to find Urgala, Narth Tezerin, and Othovir and began organizing the cleanup.

**Meeting at the Talking Troll:** Later visited the party at the Talking Troll with two guards. Confirmed she had heard locals were offering treasure information as rewards. Revealed her Harper affiliation via the hand gesture. Expressed alarm that fire giants had reached the Derrien Valley undetected. Gave the party a platinum badge bearing the Triboar insignia and directed them to **Danivarr's House in Everlund** — east along the Evermoor Way — to deliver it to **Dral Thelev**, the one-eyed half-orc proprietor.

**Central Tower Meeting (Cindrell contract):** When the party delivered Cindrell's signed contract to her at the central tower, Darathra received them warmly. She expressed satisfaction with the contract and outlined a two-front plan: use it to politically bury Cindrell, and leverage it against Hyuth as a warning that future assassination attempts might not reach her desk. She delegated the Hyuth conversation to the party, instructing them to make clear the intervention came from her as Lord Protector, and asked them to return the contract afterward for safekeeping.

**Bob Saget recruitment / theater infiltration:** Reluctantly agreed to join the strike team against Bob Saget. Acknowledged that the 3HPP's information helped prevent Triboar from defecting to the Lord's Alliance via the Cindrell affair — this tipped the scales toward cooperation. Declared all debts cleared once the mission would be complete. During the recruitment meeting, examined the elder dwarf and briefly believed him to be Orsik; accepted his claim of being Orsic, Orsik's father. Challenged his parental character when he seemed pleased by his son's misdeeds. Separately expressed relief to Vardis that he had distanced himself from Orsik, noting that Orsik's association with a Corellon cleric had harmed believers, and remarked that Orsik always seemed "unnaturally perceptive — something off about his intelligence." During theater infiltration, questioned Thistle about the portal deception; observed Unla Kee dispel the alarm wards. Witnessed the staged horror inside the theater — zombie audience members dressed to mimic the 3HPP, including a half-orc with a broken neck behind the concession stand and a Tabaxi in an Orsik costume with a partially severed neck.

**The Theater Incident (post-Bob):** Present at the battle and its aftermath. Furious that Orsik sent Bob to the Astral Plane rather than killing him — specifically noting that Bob can return, that 300 missing theatergoers are now trapped in a pocket dimension with no known retrieval method, and that the primary evidence of the crime left with Bob. After a cover story was negotiated, she declared she was never present and never saw anything (consistent with Harper operational secrecy). When Orsik called this out as "Harper-like," she responded with a lethal glare. After Orsik insulted her battlefield contribution, she marched out, delivering a final ultimatum before leaving through the theater door.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Departing the theater (after the post-Bob incident); previously seen at the central tower in Triboar
- **Active Plans:** Using Cindrell's contract as political leverage against both Cindrell and Hyuth; positioning herself for a landslide victory in the next Lord Protector election; pursuing Harper intelligence on the giant movements; coordinating cleanup and civic response
- **Active Stance (post-theater):** Has formally severed ties with the party — declared "We are done. Don't ever come looking for me" and explicitly forbade any future favors or special requests
- **What the Party Knows:** She is a Harper Agent and Lord Protector; has a prior connection to the platinum brooch; prepared spell scroll gifts for the party in advance; leveraged the Cindrell contract aggressively; is now actively hostile and closed to future contact (post-theater)
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent of her Harper operations and network; her current whereabouts relative to the brooch situation (Dral's concern about "something amiss" has not been resolved or explained); her specific intelligence on the giants; what Dral Thelev is meant to do with the badge; her personal read on Orsik ("something off about his intelligence") has not been fully explained — it is unclear how much she knows or suspects about his nature; what, if anything, she does with the information about Bob's escape and the 300 missing theatergoers

## Relationships
- **Cindrell / Cindell Trueshot:** Political rival. Darathra defeated her in a prior election. Cindrell resents her Harper affiliation and considers her a "nobody." Darathra intends to use the signed contract to politically bury her.
- **Hyuth Kolstaag:** Adversarial but transactional. She views him as manageable — willing to offer him protection from the 12 in exchange for becoming a "productive citizen," using the contract as implicit threat.
- **Krowen Valharrow:** Superior/colleague through Harper networks; Krowen was aware of her prepared gifts for the party. Her vouching for the 3HPP damaged her standing with him; Krowen issued the formal condemnation of the party.
- **Dral Thelev:** Recognizes her by the platinum brooch; concerned about her wellbeing when it appeared in the party's hands. Harper contact at Danivarr's House; Darathra's conduit eastward.
- **Orsic (elder dwarf, Orsik's father):** Skeptical of his character; briefly mistook him for Orsik. Challenged him over his apparent delight in his son's misdeeds.
- **Orsik (party):** Distrustful; noted something abnormal about his perceptiveness. Relationship destroyed at the theater incident — he bore the brunt of her fury and directly provoked her final exit with an insult to her battlefield contribution.
- **Vardis (party):** Expressed specific concern about his association with Orsik as a Corellon cleric; seemed relieved by their separation. Relationship is cautiously positive.
- **Thistle (party):** Tense — direct accusation after the fire giant battle, physically intervened to stop her from shooting; later questioned directly during theater infiltration; functional working relationship.
- **Unla Kee (party):** Observed her dispelling the alarm wards; no noted tension.
- **The Party (3HPP):** Formerly a warm ally; now under a clean-slate agreement contingent on Bob mission success — and ultimately actively hostile and closed to future contact after the post-theater incident.
- **Bob:** Views his escape to the Astral Plane as a serious ongoing threat; has confirmed Bob can return.
- **Tolmara:** Held her in custody on the party's evidence.
- **Urgala, Narth Tezerin, Othovir:** Local leaders she turned to immediately for recovery coordination.
- **Locals of Triboar:** She is an outsider — originally from Waterdeep — and was elected with expectations of "Waterdeep magic" she may not be delivering.
