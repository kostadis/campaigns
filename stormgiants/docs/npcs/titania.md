# Titania

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Queen / Goddess of the Fey; Ruler of the Seelie Court (Fey Court); sovereign of the domain in which the dryads' exile originated
- **Faction:** Seelie Court
- **First Appearance:** Never appeared in person; first referenced when the dryads Halisa and Helia explained the circumstances of their exile, then introduced via a Sprite messenger who delivered her judgment to the party

*Also known as: Queen Titania, Queen Tatiana.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintaining order and the integrity of her judgments; punishing interference in Seelie Court affairs; enforcing her authority over both Seelie and Unseelie matters.
- Capricious, vain, and manipulative — she exiled Halisa for an honest opinion about her dress, and Helia simply for accompanying Halisa.
- Described by Thistle as "vengeful, spiteful, cruel, and heartless" and explicitly not the forgiving type. Despite this, she demonstrated calculated restraint with the party — not out of mercy, but because trickery was employed and results were to her liking. She is transactional rather than magnanimous: approval is earned, not given.
- Willing to use emotional leverage and false ultimatums to control those around her; her threat that "if one dryad left, the other would die" was later revealed to be a lie (or a misunderstanding the dryads imposed on themselves).
- Generosity is transactional — Thistle explicitly warns that her gifts always come with strings attached.

## History with the Party
- Titania had previously punished two Dryads, Helia and Halisa, for unknown reasons (later revealed: petty grounds — an honest opinion about her dress).
- The party freed the Dryads, ostensibly for personal/selfish reasons, but used trickery in doing so — which earned Titania's specific approval.
- Rather than issuing a severe punishment (which others in the court had advocated for), Titania assigned the party a task: investigate and deal with a Fomorian of the Unseelie Court who has taken refuge in the upper lands.
- Thistle's handling of his Uncle Joon was noted favorably and factored into her leniency.
- Titania used Orsik-Thornacious' involvement as justification to bar him from the Feywild entirely.
- After the defeat of Maergun, sent a colorful sprite to deliver a Blessed Horn as a gift and opened the portal back from the Feywild on the party's behalf.
- Orsik accepted the gift over Thistle's visible objection; Thistle ultimately took possession of the Blessed Horn himself.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Off-screen; operates exclusively through her Sprite emissary
- **Active Operations:** Has tasked the party with dealing with an Unseelie Fomorian in the upper lands; the Blessed Horn has been given but its strings, if any, have not yet materialized
- **What the Party Knows:** She exiled the dryads on petty grounds, she lies or manipulates with ultimatums, her gifts carry implied obligations, her judgment, her reputation, and her current task for them.
- **What Remains Hidden:** Her deeper motivations; what consequences follow if the task is failed or refused; the full nature of the Fomorian threat; the nature of any conditions attached to the Blessed Horn; her actual intentions toward the party.

## Relationships
- **Halisa & Helia (dryads):** Exiled both under capricious and manipulative circumstances; ongoing resentment from the dryads' side; was their punisher; the party's interference in that judgment was the inciting issue.
- **Thistle:** Viewed with some favor; his actions against Uncle Joon pleased her; familiar with her behavior, describing it as a pattern ("would pull this kind of stuff all of the time"); deeply skeptical of her generosity.
- **Orsik / Orsik-Thornacious:** Explicitly banned from the Feywild; used as an example of her continued authority. Accepted her gift despite warnings; now in possession of a Titania-gifted item (via Thistle).
- **Unla Kee:** Regards Titania as one of the party's cultivated allies, suggesting a perceived political relationship.
- **The Party (general):** Conditionally tolerant — she could have punished them harshly and chose not to; they are now bound to her task.
- **Sprite Emissary:** Her chosen proxy for all communication with the upper lands.
- **Prince Thornacious:** Exiled him from the Seelie Court (he frames his exile as voluntary).
