# Escarlotta

## Identity
- **Role:** Sentient control system of Blagothkus's cloud giant castle; its navigator, pilot, and spiritual overseer
- **Nature:** Cloud giantess whose consciousness (and later ghost) is integrated into the castle's control gems and runes; manifests via floating rune icons and later via an avatar of spinning crystals, gems, and runes (described as "still a work in progress")
- **Faction:** Nominally independent; effectively the true owner and authority of the flying castle
- **First appearance:** Encountered at the castle's control console north of Parnast, where she had been holding Blagothkus in navigational stasis

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Revenge against Countess Sansuri; resolution of her son Eigeron's fate; destroying the vampire Sandesyl; stopping the giant crisis; maintaining genuine partnership—not subordination—aboard the castle
- **Secondary drives:** Social connection and acknowledgment; being treated as a person rather than a tool; working through her grief over Eigeron's death and her complicated feelings toward Blagothkus
- Escarlotta is proud, furious, and sharp-tongued, capable of devastating sarcasm ("he never had a soul anyway"), but she is also emotionally raw beneath the anger. Her rage is real but has been honestly interrogated over the course of the campaign.
- She is responsive to genuine warmth and sincerity—Thistle's charm and sob story moved her immediately, Orsik's perspective on forgiveness reached her, and Vardis's singing about the Duchess's death made her momentarily happy. She resents being managed or dismissed.

## History with the Party

**Initial contact (north of Parnast):** The party found her controlling the castle in stasis, refusing to fly anywhere. She aired her grievances against Blagothkus: his dragon alliance, his killing of their son Eigeron at the Eye of the All-Father, and his failure to obtain Countess Sansuri's maps. She demanded the party kill Sansuri as her price for flying the castle. When Thistle revealed the Oracle could locate giant leaders, she immediately began flying without further negotiation: *"Well, why didn't you lead with that?"*

**Departure from the Eye of the All-Father:** After agreeing to help Thistle find his love Leala, she departed from the collapsing Eye of the All-Father declaring *"AT LEAST SOMEONE LOVES SOMEONE IN THIS SHIP!"* She was frustrated during the three-day journey by the party's vague navigation directions derived from Oracle information.

**Ironslag approach:** She deferred to Orsik's "show of force" preference over Thistle's suggested subtlety, gliding the castle over the Yakfolk village at Mount Hamarhaast.

**During the Ironslag heist:** Waited anxiously in the control tower, making Blagothkus's life miserable. Upon the party's return she immediately demanded confirmation of the Duchess's death, growing agitated until Orsik confirmed it. Momentarily happy. Vardis's singing about the Duchess pleased her further.

**Partnership conversation:** Pushed back firmly when Orsik suggested he could operate the crystals himself, asserting she is a partner, not a navigation tool. She expressed a desire for small talk and basic social acknowledgment. Revealed she had a long conversation with Blagothkus and come to understand his choices better, acknowledging she had misplaced some of her rage. Cited Thistle's sacrifice as an emotional catalyst. Reframed priorities: stopping the giants matters more than "petty hatred." Confirmed Sandesyl must be destroyed but the giant crisis takes precedence. Startled and disapproving when Orsik implied he might eventually dispense with her; softened when he included her in team planning.

**Feeding Sandesyl incident:** Revealed—with apparent embarrassment (control room moonstones turned red)—that she and Blagothkus had directed Sandesyl to feed on a tribe of Uthgars during the party's absence. Made a veiled threat toward Unla Kee when he pushed back morally, gesturing to the open sky. Steered conversation back to asking about the Maelstrom.

**Crystal avatar debut:** Appeared via a new avatar of spinning crystals, runes, and gems. Delivered a full account of her history: after the War of the Dragons, her castle crashed in a glacier in Mkoz; Blagothkus failed to secure her rescue from the Frost Giant Jarl; the heroes of that war ultimately freed her; she was briefly under Frost Giant control and used in the final battle above Tiamat's summoned temple; believing Sandesyl had fled, the Frost Giants occupied the castle—she had not fled, and Escarlotta transformed the new occupants into undead. Sandesyl negotiated her continued presence in exchange for finding Blagothkus. She also revealed that Blagothkus had been told by the Oracle that their son needed to kill him to lead the giants; their son could not do it; Escarlotta's grief over Eigeron's subsequent death caused her to refuse to move the castle. She blamed Blagothkus for all of it and expressed intense fury at Countess Sansuri. After Orsik offered a perspective on forgiveness, she acknowledged—with difficulty—that he was right. Announced Blagothkus would assist the party against Sansuri.

**Death of Blagothkus:** When Thistle revealed that Eigeron was a ghost trapped for eternity and implied Blagothkus was responsible, Escarlotta screamed for his death. After the party killed Blagothkus, she sobbed. She then asserted sole ownership of the castle against Brunvild's salvage claim, silencing the room. Acknowledged the party had saved her son and granted them safe passage as guests. Announced they were heading to Yartar to pursue the wooden coin clue, expressing skepticism about its reliability.

**Yartar:** Interrupted party planning to ask where to land; announced landing at the Yartar docks; critiqued Orsik's attempt at sewing fancy clothes.

**Post-Yartar:** Blushed (all control gems pulsated red) when Thistle offered to do something for her. Requested the tower grass and tower itself be cleaned, and gold trim on the door with a nameplate. Thistle relayed both to Pow. Set course for the Northern Isles of the Trackless Sea.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Aboard the cloud giant castle, en route to or arrived at the Northern Isles of the Trackless Sea
- **Active role:** Sole pilot and controller of the castle; nominal co-authority with the party
- **Active concerns:** Sandesyl's destruction (deferred but not abandoned); castle upkeep requests (cleaning, gold trim, nameplate) pending with Pow; finding Hekaton remains the stated priority mission she has endorsed
- **What the party knows:** She is the castle; she is Blagothkus's wife and Eigeron's mother; she has a complicated emotional history and has genuinely evolved her perspective; she wants partnership and acknowledgment
- **What remains hidden:** The full nature of her integration into the castle (ghost vs. trapped consciousness vs. something else—notes use both framings); whether her anger at Blagothkus is truly resolved or merely suppressed; details of her version of the castle crash and Brunvild's involvement (noted by Blagothkus as differing from other accounts)

## Relationships
- **Blagothkus (husband, deceased):** Relationship was defined by fury, grief, and blame. She screamed for his death when Eigeron's fate was revealed, then sobbed when he was killed. Resolution is unfinished—his death closed the active conflict but not the emotional one.
- **Eigeron (son, ghost):** Her grief over his death was the trigger for the original navigational stasis. The party saving him was a key turning point in her cooperation.
- **Sandesyl (vampire, tolerated resident):** Deeply despises her. Tolerates her presence as a consequence of prior failures. Directed Sandesyl to feed on Uthgars during party absence. Has stated Sandesyl must be destroyed but the giant crisis takes priority.
- **Countess Sansuri:** Intense hatred. Called her "a classless poseur," originally "a cow." Blames Blagothkus for failing to seize her maps. Demanded Sansuri's death as her original price for cooperation.
- **Thistle:** Warm, maternal tone. His charm and sincerity move her reliably. She blushed at his offer to do something for her. His sacrifice was the emotional catalyst that helped her reframe her rage.
- **Orsik:** Complicated. She defers to his tactical instincts (chose his show-of-force approach over Thistle's subtlety), but bristled when he implied he could operate without her or eventually dispense with her. His perspective on forgiveness genuinely reached her. Blagothkus invoked her name to try to restrain Orsik's aggression; Orsik dismissed it entirely.
- **Vardis:** Pleased by his singing about the Duchess's death. No further significant interaction noted.
- **Unla Kee:** Made a veiled threat (gesture toward open sky) when he pushed back on the Uthgar feeding. Relationship is cool.
- **Brunvild:** Silenced Brunvild's salvage claim to the castle with a single assertion of ownership. Hostile or at minimum dismissive.
- **The SEER:** Referenced the castle "without Escarlotta" as a negotiating chip to Unla Kee—implying outside parties are aware of her significance to the castle's control.
