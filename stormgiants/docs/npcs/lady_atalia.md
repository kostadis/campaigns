# Lady Atalia

## Identity
- **Role:** Socialite, telepath, operative within Yartar's criminal/political underworld; coerced asset of Lord Drylund
- **Faction:** Loosely connected to the Kraken Society (exact allegiance ambiguous); protected by a group of female rogues called "the Hands"
- **First Appearance:** Aboard the Grand Dame gambling hall, playing Sava against nobles including Lord Hubbard

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Self-preservation, escape from Drylund's blackmail, and a stated desire to "return to a moral life" — though her trustworthiness is genuinely unclear
- Sophisticated and dangerous beneath a cultivated veneer of vulnerability; she deploys social performance as a weapon
- Capable of running long confidence tricks — she may have faked telepathic ability entirely, exploiting the party's assumption against them
- Fears the Kraken Society's retaliation and becomes visibly anxious when King Hekaton is raised as a subject

## History with the Party

**Aboard the Grand Dame**
Orsik played Sava against her and suspected she was reading his mind when she countered his moves precisely. When he quietly accused her, she denied it but reached out via apparent telepathy to arrange a private meeting: *"We need to talk. Not here. Follow me when the boat returns to the docks."*

**Yartar Docks — Early Dawn**
Met the party discreetly at a tavern. Demonstrated knowledge of Orsik's fake identity, infuriating him. When Thistle redirected toward King Hekaton, she was visibly surprised — she had assumed the party was investigating the kidnapping of a young lord named Lord Artan. Revealed she had been blackmailed by Drylund into using her social skills to assist his operation. Named **Oosith**, a spellcaster hiding in the sewers with Kuo-Toan protection, as the man running the lordling kidnappings. Offered Oosith's exact location in exchange for his death. Appealed directly to Vardis. The party walked away without agreeing.

**Fashionable Cafe — Post-Grand Dame**
Lady Atalia had apparently since engaged the party to kill Oosith (off-screen agreement). When they arrived without having killed him, her composure broke. She stood, straightened her clothes, and left in fury. Her protective rogues ("the Hands") briefly blocked the party until they explained Oosith had been neutralized.

**Outside Her House**
Reverted to a raw dock accent — social mask fully dropped — and told the party they were done. Thistle provoked her by suggesting she'd never become Waterbarron with that attitude; she let slip a warning: *"The Kraken doesn't like it when anyone disrupts its plans. Drylund's going to come back, and he's going to be…"* before stopping herself. When Orsik doubted Drylund's return, she pivoted and revealed she may have been faking telepathy the entire time: *"It's amazing how easy it is for people to read if they think you can read their minds."* She then closed the door. The Hands subsequently approached Orsik to negotiate a 95% cut of the Grand Dame plus fees; the party declined and left.

**Later Reference (No Appearance)**
Thistle dismisses Lady Atalia and Drylund as lacking the resources to orchestrate a giant's kidnapping and a ship that cannot be scried, pointing toward a more powerful hidden actor.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Her home in Yartar, protected by the Hands
- **Active Concerns:** Fears Kraken Society retaliation now that Oosith's operation has been disrupted; Drylund's possible return is a source of visible anxiety
- **What the Party Knows:** She was blackmailed by Drylund; she wanted Oosith dead; she fears the Kraken; she may have fabricated her telepathic ability entirely
- **What Remains Hidden:** Her true allegiance to or standing within the Kraken Society; whether her "desire to return to a moral life" is genuine; the full extent of what she knows about Drylund and Hekaton; what she was about to say before catching herself about Drylund's return

## Relationships
- **Lord Drylund:** Blackmailed her into assisting his lordling-kidnapping operation; she clearly resents him and fears his return
- **Oosith:** The operative she wanted dead; appears to have been a threat or liability to her personally
- **The Hands:** Female rogue retinue that protects her; they operate independently enough to shake down the party on her behalf
- **The Kraken Society:** Connected, but the nature of the relationship is unresolved — she may be a coerced asset, a lower-tier member, or playing multiple sides
- **Orsik:** Tense; she exploited his assumption about her telepathy and knows his fake identity
- **Thistle:** Antagonistic at last meeting; he pushed her buttons effectively but burned the relationship further
- **Vardis:** She singled him out in her appeal for help — possible opening if re-engaged
