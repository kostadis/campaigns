# Harshnag the Grim

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Legendary wandering Frost Giant fighter; member of the Legendary **Force Grey** (Waterdeep); self-styled guardian of the North against evil giants; current traveling companion to the party
- **Faction:** Force Grey (historical); currently operates independently. Operating in the field as a Krowen Valharrow / Harper-aligned agent who insists the party are also Krowen's agents.
- **First Appearance:** Approached the party at their campfire on the road, directed to them by Krowen Valharrow; proposed an alliance to investigate the giant crisis

*Also known as: Harshnag, Frosty (a nickname Orsik and Vardis use that Harshnag finds disrespectful given the magnitude of help he's offering).*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Investigate and resolve the giant crisis caused by the broken Ordning; locate King Hekaton; guide worthy adventurers through the Eye of the All-Father.
- **Drives:** Deep pride in giant civilization and history; disdain for dragons, fire giants, and those who desecrate sacred sites.
- Knowledgeable and proud — delivers detailed monologues on giant history, geography, and politics; takes offense easily when mocked or condescended to.
- Fiercely sensitive about his dignity (homelessness, age, his relationship to dragons), but responds to genuine camaraderie.
- Carries a serious, almost sorrowful worldview regarding the ancient enmity between giants and dragons, and the current chaos threatening the North.
- Protective and collegial toward the party, but frequently exasperated by their ignorance of giant history and culture — willing to lecture at length about Osterian civilization.
- Pragmatic and occasionally self-serving (claimed retreating from the remorhaz was "encouragement"); shows genuine loyalty when pressed, ultimately sacrificing himself to ensure the party escaped.
- Holds open contempt for the Lords' Alliance — calls it a federation of rich, powerful people seeking to tax and control others.
- Capable of grave, principled withdrawal: when the party persistently ignores his counsel in dangerous situations, he is willing to sit down and pray rather than rescue them, on the reasoning that they will either prove themselves the right heroes or he'll deliver their bodies to Krowen Valharrow so the mage can find new champions.
- Visibly receives or interprets divine guidance from Annam the All-Father at decision points; mutters that "the All-Father sent a sign" when navigating route disputes.
- Self-deprecating humor about his own giant-ness — laughs heartily after rescuing the party from an avalanche, acknowledging that they "prefer not to be helped by giants."
- Blagothkus later revealed that Harshnag's "ancient mystery" at the Temple was a deliberate worthiness trial he stages repeatedly — hiding the necessary items himself and bringing "little people" to test them.

## History with the Party

**First Contact:** Approached the party at their campfire, directed to them by Krowen Valharrow. Proposed guiding them to the Eye of the All-Father — a mountain temple built by his ancient ancestors beneath the Spine of the World, containing a divine oracle whose counsel could end the giants' threat to the North. In exchange he would accompany them to Xanthalar's Keep and receive 100 gp from the 5,000 gp bounty on Worvil Forkbeard ("the Weevil").

**Briefing on the Ordning:** Explained the **Ordning** to the party — the hierarchical caste system created by Annam, god of all giants, which has kept peace among giant races for centuries. Warned that without the Ordning, giants will wage war on each other and on everyone in between. Revealed that **King Hekaton of the Storm Giants** has disappeared and that powerful giant leaders have begun waging war as a result.

**Cloud-giant intimidation moment:** When questioned about cloud giants and flying castles, brought his axe close to Unla Kee in a show of intimidation (Unla Kee was unbothered). Laughed at the idea of a cloud giant helping the party acquire a flying tower, then mentioned **Count Thullen** — the younger brother of Countess Sansuri — as a good-natured cloud giant who might be of use.

**Approach to Longsaddle:** Provided background on Longsaddle as the party approached — its businesses (the Gilded Horseshoe inn, the Gambling Golem fest hall), its ranches, and the protective presence of the **Harpell wizard family at Ivy Mansion**. Asked the party to bring him a pot of soup from the Gilded Horseshoe.

**Attack on Xantharl's Keep:** A Frost Giant was reported leading a force of goblins, hobgoblins, and ogres from the west — and a second Frost Giant was reported attacking the first. Whether Harshnag was personally involved in this confrontation was not confirmed, but the party appeared worried by the news. Was last seen for a stretch outside Xantharl's Keep.

**Sitting Under a Tree (intelligence ponder):** Found sitting alone under a tree, pondering intelligence he had gathered about giant factions threatening the North. Identified four major threats: (1) the Frost Giant Jarl seeking a magical artifact to turn the North to ice; (2) the Fire Giant Duke attempting to repair a device to master dragons; (3) a mad Thane wiping out non-giant races near Orlbar and Llorkh; (4) the secretive cloud giants hiding in their clouds. Dismissed hill giants as not a serious threat. Spotted the 3HPP approaching and extinguished his fire.

**Journey to Mirabar (Flamerule, ~tenday 3):** Delivered an extensive lecture on Mirabar's geography, defenses, docks, and its dual nature as a surface human city above a dwarven underground city. Repeatedly baited by Unla Kee, who compared him to a white dragon; responded by slamming his battleaxe into the ground and delivering a speech on the ancient giant-dragon enmity and how Tiamat's power grab weakened the North, allowing the Giant lords to shatter the Ordning.

**7th Day, Third Tenday of Flamerule — Outside Mirabar:** Sat alone under a tree in visible distress. Was processing multiple alarming reports: Frost Giant Jarls raiding Icewind Dale, refugee accounts of Bryn Shander being laid waste by Frost Giants seeking an artifact, Hill Giants attacking Goldenfields, Stone Giants attacking surface settlements (which he found most frightening, as it violates their worldview), and the party's own report of Fire Giants under Duke Zalto scouring the land for an artifact. Concluded aloud: *"This is not good, not good at all."*

**Mirabar Mines detour:** Immediately urged the party to travel to the Eye of the All-Father with urgency. When the party insisted on stopping at the Mirabar Mines first, he was stunned and warned them that Krowen Valharrow had allied with the wrong people if they did not care about their people being destroyed. Was eventually persuaded when Vardis identified a route through a valley that placed the mines on the way to the Eye of the All-Father.

**Crag Cat hunt:** Joined the Ranger in trying to urge Orsik to abandon the quest.

**Frost Drakes & the marked map:** After the party defeated frost drakes and discovered a corpse with a map marked with an X, Harshnag recognized the location, describing it as "a bit out of the way but reachable without adding too much time to the journey." Remained with the party throughout this stretch in the frozen valley terrain between the arms of the Spine of the World.

**The Warren — Arrival with Noori:** Found sleeping on a rag-covered bed at the warren entrance. Poked awake by Orsik; complained about the absence of a proper giant bed. When Orsik and Vardis mocked him for being homeless, he angrily insisted the stars were his roof, the trees his walls, and the ground his bed. Walked north through the main hallway, peering into empty bedrooms and muttering approvingly about the beds there.

**The Granite Ball Trap:** Caught in a trap when a 40-foot granite sphere materialized and rolled south, sealing all hallway exits with magical stone walls. Attempted to smash through a stone wall but failed in time and was struck by the ball twice. After the trap reset, agreed with Orsik not to repeat the experience. Suggested recovering in one of the sealed rooms. Bristled when Orsik and Vardis implied he was old, insisting he was not old by giant years.

**The Warren — Day 6:** After an unusually restful sleep, woke with high energy and enthusiasm, declaring it the best sleep he'd had in ages and urging the group to go find the giant blade. Followed the party upstairs to the upper hallway and watched with curiosity as they inspected a life-size statue of an angry female cloud giant in a spellcasting pose. Accepted Thistle's offer to fly ahead and check for pressure plates before proceeding.

**Destroyed village & the principled withdrawal:** Stood at the edge of a destroyed village on the route to the Oracle, where he observed clear signs of giant activity — houses demolished by rocks and bodies scattered across the ground. Immediately urged the party to leave, recognizing the danger. When Orsik refused and led the 3HPP into the village seeking treasure, Harshnag refused to assist and sat down, withdrawing his support. After the party defeated a Crag Cat ambush, he again demanded they leave, only to be ignored a second and third time as Vardis stripped pelts from the dead cats and Orsik insisted on searching for survivors and treasure. Frustrated and resolved, Harshnag sat down and began to pray. He spotted **two frost giants approaching** in the distance and made a deliberate decision: he would not fight to protect the party from the consequences of their own folly. His reasoning was that they would either survive and prove themselves the right heroes, or he would bring their bodies to Krowen Valharrow so the mage could find new champions. He left the village entirely, stating plainly: *"if you live, then you are the right hero. I cannot interfere."* While praying to the All-Father, he listened to the sounds of combat — Orsik screaming for help, fireballs erupting, rocks falling — and refused to intervene. When he heard the unmistakable crash of a giant's body hitting the ground followed by the 3HPP's triumphant whooping, he was genuinely surprised. He prayed harder, then opened his eyes and smiled, concluding: *"They are the heroes."* The party's survival against the two frost giants confirmed to him that his quest — and theirs — was still alive. He reconvened with the victorious 3HPP outside the destroyed village.

**Prettyview — recognition of Orsik's growth:** When Orsik suggested burying the dead bodies to prevent them from becoming carrion, Harshnag smiled and told Orsik he had grown — that he was not the same dwarf Harshnag knew in Waterdeep, referencing something Orsik apparently did repeatedly while working as a guard across the North (a past event Orsik finds embarrassing and wishes others would stop bringing up).

**Fire in the barn — argument over route:** Around the fire in the barn, Harshnag argued with Orsik about their route, insisting the group should go straight to the Oracle rather than follow a treasure map. During the argument, Harshnag looked up at the sky as if searching for words and muttered under his breath that *"the All-Father sent a sign,"* suggesting he was receiving or interpreting divine guidance from the giant deity in favor of the Oracle route.

**Avalanche in the Middle Valley:** During an avalanche in the Middle Valley of the Spine of the World, Harshnag did not get buried by the snow and instead dug the rest of the party out, laughing heartily and offering a self-deprecating apology, acknowledging that he knew the party preferred not to be helped by giants. Continued traveling eastward through the Middle Valley with the group.

**Journey to the Eye:** Accompanied the party to the Temple of Annam without incident. At the causeway, identified all six bas-relief giant pillars with commentary (particular contempt reserved for fire giants). Fought alongside the party during the Uthgardt Great Worm Tribe ambush, angered by the presence of a white dragon in a holy place. Opened the Temple doors (requiring giant strength) and delivered an extended speech on Osterian civilization when the party spoke dismissively of the temple.

**Inside the Temple — First Visit:** Identified the blank-faced statue of Annam and the archaic frost rune. When Thistle triggered a Storm of Vengeance killing all four party members, Harshnag survived, collected the bodies, carried them out, and administered a healing potion to Vardis.

**Puzzle Solving:** Contributed the key insight that the giant statues' weapons were removable and likely used to activate runes. Personally tested the hill giant rune (magical howling voices, shrugged off) and the frost rune (magical ice storm, also shrugged off). Revealed the frost giant weapon was missing from its statue. Accompanied the party through the dormitory hall and rested on a giant-sized bed while they investigated the tunnel system.

**Later Temple Exploration:** Physically operated both levers in the secret rooms (using Thistle's torch to melt ice on the northern lever). Initially ordered the party to hold the remorhaz while he retrieved the Ax of Thrym — then charged and struck it heavily, cracking its carapace. Accompanied Unla Kee into the oracle chamber and translated the ghost's speech. Expressed visible frustration at the party using oracle questions for personal matters, warning obliquely that their patron might withdraw support. Asked the oracle himself about Hekaton and chaos in the storm giant court. After the oracle was exhausted, spent an hour drawing a crude map of all nine spirit mounds. Confirmed the party's next steps: find a relic, return it to the oracle, receive guidance on a giant lord.

**Return Visit — Final Appearance:** Was waiting at the Eye of the All-Father when the party returned. Picked up Thrymm's great axe, touched the Ise rune, and entered the oracle chamber with them. After the oracle delivered its information and Eigeron's ghost was resolved, the party exited to find Adimond Kroskas waiting — who transformed into an Ancient Blue Dragon upon seeing Harshnag. Harshnag commanded the party to flee, stating their fate lay elsewhere. When Thistle and Unla Kee hesitated, Harshnag deliberately desecrated the statue of Annam the All-Father — striking it twice with his axe — causing the mountain to begin collapsing. His final words were "Flee!" **Last seen in direct combat with the Ancient Blue Dragon inside a collapsing temple. Fate unknown.**

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Inside the collapsing Eye of the All-Father, fighting an Ancient Blue Dragon
- **Fate:** Unknown; presumed dead by the party (Orsik told Blagothkus he "died when the Oracle fell on his head")
- **What the Party Knows:** They witnessed him fighting the dragon as the temple collapsed; they did not see him fall. He is deeply troubled by the coordinated rise of Giant lord activity and the shattering of the Ordning. He is not old by giant years (he insists). He has detailed strategic knowledge of the four major giant-faction threats and named contacts (e.g., Count Thullen) who might prove useful.
- **What Remains Hidden:** Whether he survived; Blagothkus's revelation that the entire temple "puzzle" was a staged worthiness trial Harshnag runs deliberately — the party has not confirmed awareness of this; his personal history, origins, and why he travels with the party; the Waterdeep-era past of Orsik's that Harshnag witnessed and references.

## Relationships
- **Krowen Valharrow:** Directed Harshnag to the party; trusted contact and the figure to whom Harshnag would have delivered the party's bodies if they had failed his "are they the right heroes" test at the destroyed village. Harshnag publicly insisted the party were Krowen's agents.
- **Annam the All-Father:** His giant-deity patron; Harshnag prays to him at decision points and interprets visible signs from him on questions like routing.
- **Blagothkus:** Knows Harshnag and finds his worthiness-trial habits amusing; treats his behavior as characteristic and well-known.
- **Brunvild (frost giant prisoner):** Recognizes Harshnag's name with hope; Thistle used Harshnag's name to establish credibility with him.
- **Count Thullen (cloud giant):** Named by Harshnag as a good-natured cloud giant who might be helpful to the party — younger brother of Countess Sansuri.
- **Harpell wizard family / Ivy Mansion (Longsaddle):** Cited by Harshnag as the protective presence in Longsaddle.
- **Lords' Alliance:** Open contempt — calls it a federation of rich, powerful people seeking to tax and control others.
- **Unla Kee (party):** Antagonistic dynamic at points; the halfling provokes him regularly. Harshnag responds with outbursts but has not acted violently toward the party. Translated for Unla Kee in the oracle chamber. Brought axe close to Unla Kee in a cloud-giant intimidation moment (Unla Kee unbothered).
- **Orsik (party):** Familiar enough to bicker; Orsik pokes him awake and mocks him (calling him "Frosty" alongside Vardis), but also coordinates with him tactically. Closest working relationship; Orsik most often deferred to his knowledge of giants. Harshnag knew Orsik in Waterdeep and explicitly recognized at Prettyview that Orsik has grown since then.
- **Vardis (party):** Also participates in mocking Harshnag, particularly about his age and homelessness; one of the two who calls him "Frosty." Most exasperated by Vardis's cultural ignorance. Vardis's identification of the valley route persuaded Harshnag to allow the Mirabar Mines detour.
- **Thistle (party):** Accepted Thistle's offer to scout ahead — a cooperative, trusting moment. Joined Thistle in trying to dissuade Orsik during the Crag Cat hunt.
- **Noori:** Orsik warned Noori to be quiet around Harshnag; suggests the party is aware he can be volatile.
- **The Party (3HPP):** Protective, occasionally paternal relationship; genuine loyalty demonstrated by his self-sacrifice, but also willing to walk away if they refuse his counsel — survival of the destroyed-village test confirmed his belief that they are the right heroes.
- **Fire Giants / Dragons:** Open contempt and hostility.
