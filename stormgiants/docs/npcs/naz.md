# Naz

## Identity
- **Role:** Caregiver to orc whelps held in Parnast; currently active combatant in the defense of Parnast
- **Faction:** Unclear — not aligned with Kurglow's faction; defending Parnast against the attacking giant and his orc followers
- **First Appearance:** Encountered in Parnast, where she was caring for orc children, including Kurglow's son

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protecting the whelps in her care; now driven by revenge against Kurglow following his public humiliation and rejection of her
- Naz is maternal and desperate — she begged Kurglow to take her with him rather than be left behind. She accepts mistreatment rather than sever the connection entirely, suggesting deep personal attachment or dependence. However, once abandoned, her grief rapidly transforms into cold resolve and anger.

## History with the Party
- Served as caregiver to orc whelps, including Kurglow's child, during their captivity in Parnast
- When Kurglow departed with his son, Naz collapsed by the town well and sobbed; she begged him to take her along
- Kurglow accused her of being willing to sacrifice his child to save her own, delivered a parting insult, and left her behind
- During the final assault on Parnast, Naz rose from the well, declared her intent to take revenge on Kurglow after surviving the battle, then entered the fight — striking the mammoth with a guiding bolt

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Active in the battle for Parnast
- **Active Plans:** Survive the assault; pursue revenge against Kurglow afterward
- **What the Party Knows:** She was a caregiver to the whelps, was publicly rejected and humiliated by Kurglow, and is now fighting in Parnast's defense
- **What Remains Hidden:** The fate of her own child; the full nature of her relationship with Kurglow; her capabilities beyond the use of guiding bolt

## Relationships
- **Kurglow:** Former close associate, possibly more. He publicly accused her of betrayal and abandoned her. She now harbors explicit revenge intentions toward him.
- **The Whelps:** Responsible for their care; the safety of her own child appears to have been a source of conflict with Kurglow
- **The Party:** No direct significant interaction noted
