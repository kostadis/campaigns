# Uncle Joon

*Also known as: Joon, Uncle Yorn.*

> **Note:** "Uncle Yorn" was an early in-session rendering of the same NPC (a Thistle family elder/storyteller about Fey lore and the North as a Fey playground). Per user merge confirmation, treat as the same character.

## Identity
- **Role/Title:** Fairy (fae) relative and mentor of Thistle; former member of Queen Titania's Court (expelled for drunkenness)
- **Faction:** None current; formerly Titania's Court. Has had dealings with Adimond Kroskas and knowledge of the Kraken Society
- **First Appearance:** Referenced from Session 1 as Thistle's source of religious/planar knowledge; physically encountered at the Talking Troll tavern in Triboar

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** Unclear and likely self-serving; claims altruistic motivations (e.g., rescuing Thistle's girlfriend Laela) but Thistle privately believes he is lying
- **Personality:** Charming, deflective, and manipulative when cornered — immediately attempts to dodge blame, scheme with strangers, and extract drinks from family. Chronically drunk, financially irresponsible, and dismissive of others' ambitions. His pattern of leaving debts — monetary and otherwise — trails him across multiple cities and even into the Feywild.

## History with the Party
- **Pre-campaign:** Uncle Joon educated Thistle extensively — religion and the planes, orc tribes and den-making, economic theory (KP Trade Equilibrium Theory, Lionel Coster merchants), geography (Ironslag, the Nether Mountains, Ascore), and political history (Silverymoon's betrayal of the Silver Marches, Silverymoon's ban on Cyric). He is effectively the source of much of Thistle's worldly knowledge.
- **Ambush Gap (portal site):** Thistle invoked Joon's memory (rendered as "Uncle Yorn" in the moment) while examining a decayed Fey portal. Joon reportedly told tales of the North as a former Fey playground, with portals once used freely between the Feywild and the material plane. Thistle's recollection was tinged with mourning for a lost age.
- **Mission briefing reward:** Joon was introduced as the reward, not a participant. Hsing and Azam claimed the SEER held information on Joon's location and offered to reveal it upon mission completion. Thistle confirmed the family connection and vouched for Joon's value as an intelligence contact. Unla Kee corroborated this characterization. Thistle noted Joon had never mentioned the Ettin Gralm.
- **Triboar (first meeting at table):** Found drunk and covered in bird droppings at the Talking Troll, having declined from the upscale Everwyvern House. Owes 15 gold in bar tab (inflated to 30 by Kaelen's surcharges). Thistle wakes him with a bucket of water — apparently a prepared and practiced habit.
- **Triboar interrogation:** When pressed about Adimond Kroskas, Joon reacts visibly before composing himself. Under pressure from Orsik, he reveals: Kroskas was an alchemist who created drakes from dragon eggs and planned to combine faeries and dragon eggs for more powerful results; Kroskas was rejected by the Cult of the Dragon for going too far and found new sponsors in the **Kraken Society**, who are allied with ancient dragons. Joon claims he was dealing with Kroskas to rescue Thistle's girlfriend Laela. Thistle does not believe him.
- **Joon refused to give the location of the ancient dragons' lairs**, calling engagement with them madness. Confirmed Laela was still held by Kroskas at last knowledge.
- **Triboar (second session):** Still at the Talking Troll, still drunk, deeper in debt. Thistle declines to pay and suggests debtor's prison. Party departs without him; Vardis explicitly refuses to bring him along.
- **Rebecca Deakin revelation:** Joon separately approached Rebecca Deakin and provided her information connecting Adimond Kroskas to the Fae — specifically that Kroskas traveled into the Feywild to obtain the First Flower of Spring for his spell research, and that fairies played a role in that research. Upon learning this, Thistle erupted in rage and declared intent to kill Joon, implying Joon withheld critical information about Laela's situation and the fairy connection.
- **Dryad encounter:** An elderly dryad recognizes Joon's name instantly. Thistle's immediate response — "How much does he owe you?" — suggests this is routine. Joon owes her a good joke.
- **Sky Pony tribe:** Joon had a disagreement with them over honor; they insulted him in terms described as "beyond the pale for a member of Queen Titania's Court," though Thistle notes the irony given Joon was expelled from the Court for drunkenness.
- **Seer's intelligence:** The Seer reported to Unla Kee that Joon was still in Triboar. Unla Kee later names Joon as a likely ally — which causes Thistle to visibly blanch.
- **Maelstrom intelligence:** Prior to the Maelstrom session, Joon provided the party with detailed intelligence about the Maelstrom's internal structure, including a system of interconnected tunnels. During the infiltration, Joon was not present but his intelligence proved directly actionable: Thistle used Joon's tunnel information to deduce that a pool of water in the throne room was Adminond's escape route. Thistle also attempted to recall something Joon had said about the obsidian floating throne with dragon skulls but could not retrieve the information.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Triboar, at the Talking Troll tavern
- **Active:** No known active plans. The Seer has eyes on him.
- **What the party knows:** He has knowledge of Kroskas, the Kraken Society, and ancient dragons. He withheld information connecting fairies to Kroskas's experiments. He is unreliable, possibly manipulative, and deeply in debt everywhere he goes.
- **What remains hidden:** His true motivation for dealing with Kroskas. Why he told Rebecca Deakin about the Feywild connection but not Thistle directly. The full extent of what he knows about Laela's situation. Whether his expulsion from Titania's Court is relevant to current events.

## Relationships
- **Thistle (nephew):** The central relationship. Joon is Thistle's primary source of knowledge and education, yet Thistle distrusts his stated motivations, was enraged by the Rebecca Deakin revelation, and has floated letting him rot in debtor's prison. The relationship is affectionate in structure and corrosive in practice.
- **Orsik:** Joon attempted to charm Orsik into splitting a fake tab arrangement upon first meeting. Orsik's stare-down was what broke Joon's composure during the Kroskas interrogation.
- **Kaelen Sarssir:** Kaelen added surcharges to Joon's tab for "emotional labor." Joon was dismissive of Kaelen's acting ambitions.
- **Vardis:** Explicitly voted against bringing Joon along when the party left Triboar.
- **Unla Kee:** Named by Unla Kee as a potential ally; Thistle's reaction suggests this prospect is unwelcome.
- **The Seer:** Has knowledge of Joon's location; likely has him under some degree of observation.
- **Elderly dryad:** Joon owes her a joke.
- **Sky Pony tribe:** Active bad blood over a matter of honor.
- **Adimond Kroskas:** Had direct dealings with him, claimed in service of rescuing Laela. Thistle does not believe this.
- **Kraken Society / ancient dragons:** Aware of their existence and operations; refuses to engage or share location information.
