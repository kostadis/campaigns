# Adimond Kroskas

> **Cross-reference:** This dossier covers Adimond Kroskas as the disguise / political identity. His true form is the ancient blue dragon **Iymrith** (see `iymrith.md` for the dragon-form dossier). Both dossiers are maintained in parallel because in-world the giant court still treats "Adimond Kroskas" as a distinct courtier — and party intelligence about him often surfaces under that name.

## Identity
- **Role:** Rogue spellcaster and free agent; not a formal Cult member but contracted by the Cult of Tiamat; self-described alchemist; de facto political advisor/manipulator in the Storm Giant court (allied with Mirran and Nym against Queen Serissa)
- **True Nature:** Almost certainly an Ancient Blue Dragon. Cult of the Dragon documents referred to him as both "a dragon" and "a dragon's pet." Vardis identified him as likely a Blue Dragon based on unusual damage in Kolbaz's chambers. This was confirmed when he transformed into an Ancient Blue Dragon upon seeing Harshnag at the Eye of the All-Father.
- **"Adimond Kroskas" is an alias** — also known as **Iymrith** and **"the Doom of the Desert."**
- **First encountered:** Directly at the Eye of the All-Father (Temple of Annam), after the party exited the Oracle's portal.

*Also known as: Adminond Kroskas, Adimond, Adirond Kroskas, Iymrith, Doom of the Desert.*

---

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core drive:** Unclear — he operates as a powerful, independent magical contractor, suggesting ambition and self-interest over ideology. He abandoned the Cult before its defeat, suggesting pragmatism over loyalty.
- Delsephine noted that Severin trusted him to deliver powerful magics but that he failed to do so, implying he overpromised or deliberately withheld.
- Characterized by Urlam Stockspool as "one messed-up dude" who specializes in creating hybrid magical creatures. All his known associates are dead.
- He conducted magical experimentation on Thistle, giving him dragon eyes and True Seeing — suggesting he views living creatures as materials for his research.

## History with the Party

**Pre-campaign (Thistle's backstory):**
Kroskas experimented on Thistle, giving him dragon eyes and the True Seeing ability. Thistle considers him his arch nemesis and is emotionally destabilized by evidence of his continued interference.

**Road to Anauroch:**
A captured cultist revealed Kroskas abandoned the Cult just before its defeat at the Well of the Worlds and departed to work with a Giant — surprising given the known enmity between Giants and Dragons. Thistle revised his view of Kroskas from true believer to opportunist.

**Ruined Fey Portal Site:**
Thistle identified a portal site as the type Kroskas used to move large numbers of troops into the Feywild.

**Yartar Harper HQ (Kolbaz's papers):**
Intelligence revealed two facts: Kroskas created the ritual the Cult of Tiamat used to first produce Drakes; and "Adimond Kroskas" is an alias. Thistle connected him to Kolbaz's death. Gauche refused to pursue the lead.

**Thistle's ongoing theory:**
Kroskas is responsible for the Cult targeting Thistle — because Thistle is searching for him, and because Kroskas's use of the Spring's First Blossom represents a mistake the Cult wants erased. Kolbaz disputed that the Spring's First Blossom was used in the ritual at all.

**Nether Mountains (retroactive recognition):**
Thistle later identified Kroskas as the "Doom of the Desert" — a figure who had chased Blue Dragons out of the northern Anauroch desert. This detail had been noted and ignored during an earlier quest.

**Oracle of Annam:**
The Oracle revealed Kroskas's location: the Maelstrom, court of Storm Giant King Hekaton. Queen Serissa framed finding Hekaton and taking vengeance on Kroskas as linked goals.

**Eye of the All-Father (first direct encounter):**
Kroskas appeared after the party exited the Oracle's portal. Thistle immediately moved to attack. Before he could act, Kroskas spotted Harshnag and transformed into an Ancient Blue Dragon. Harshnag chose to sacrifice himself fighting Kroskas as the temple collapsed around them.

**Maelstrom throne room (Thistle's reconnaissance):**
Adimond was present when the party appeared before Serissa, seated to her left. He froze upon realizing the visitors were the 3HPP, and immediately asked whether "the bodies" had been found — revealing guilty knowledge of something concealed. He did not press the point when Nym deflected. He reported to Serissa that his dragon-fairy hybrid experiment was progressing, framing it as a force to guarantee Storm Giant dominance in the Ordning. When tensions escalated, he subtly suggested to Uthor that a leadership change might be warranted if Serissa could not restrain herself, then retreated to the position of obedient alchemist when Uthor rebuked him. Thistle observed Adminond covertly directing Mirran and Nym during the audience — signaling when to act in their shared arguments against Serissa. Thistle believes the pool of water in the throne room connects to the Maelstrom's tunnel network and serves as Adminond's exit strategy — consistent with Joon's information about the interconnected tunnels.

**Sandesyl's account:**
Sandesyl Morgia revealed that Severin contracted Adimond to create a fey hybrid dragon. The experiment succeeded but nearly drove the subject mad, causing uncontrolled transformation between two creature forms — more like lycanthropy than true fusion. Sandesyl confirmed the subject resembles Thistle and is the person Thistle identifies as the love of his life (Laela). Sandesyl claimed she did not know Adimond's current whereabouts.

**Seer reference:**
The Seer described Adirond Kroskas to Unla Kee as Unla Kee's nemesis, last known to have "disappeared into the North."

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** The Eye of the All-Father, in combat with Harshnag inside the collapsing temple. **Status unknown** following the collapse.
- **Active operations:** Previously confirmed to be operating at the Maelstrom alongside Giant leadership — the degree to which this is still true post-collapse is unknown.
- **What the party knows:** He is an Ancient Blue Dragon, operating under an alias, who created Drake-hybrid magic for the Cult, experimented on Thistle, and was last seen fighting Harshnag in a collapsing temple.
- **What remains hidden:** His true name and identity beyond "Iymrith," his current survival status, his full agenda and relationship with Giant leadership, and the precise nature of his earlier arrangement with the Cult.

---

## Relationships
- **Thistle Wendrod:** Arch nemesis. Kroskas experimented on Thistle directly. Thistle's revenge against him is a primary personal quest driver. Thistle is emotionally volatile when confronted with evidence of Kroskas's influence.
- **Harshnag:** Harshnag sacrificed himself to fight Kroskas when he transformed — outcome unknown.
- **Cult of Tiamat / Severin:** Contracted to provide powerful magic; reportedly failed to deliver. Abandoned the Cult before its defeat.
- **The Cult of the Dragon (general):** Not a member. A hired external contractor.
- **Kolbaz:** Thistle believes Kroskas (or agents acting to protect his secrets) is responsible for Kolbaz's death.
- **Giants / Storm Giant court:** Operating alongside Giant leadership at the Maelstrom. His presence among Giants — given known Dragon-Giant enmity — remains unexplained from the in-world perspective. He publicly poses as Serissa's loyal advisor while privately allying with Mirran and Nym to destabilize her.
- **Mirran & Nym:** Covert allies. Adminond directs their actions and timing in court disputes.
- **Serissa:** Publicly loyal; privately working to destabilize or replace her, using her sisters as proxies.
- **Uthor:** Uthor rebuked Adminond when he overstepped, suggesting Uthor is not part of the faction or at least will not tolerate open challenges to Serissa.
- **Sandesyl Morgia:** Aware of his hybrid-dragon work; claims no knowledge of his current whereabouts.
- **Laela:** Thistle's love; Adimond/Kroskas conducted the hybridization experiment on her, producing the fairy-dragon hybrid resembling Thistle. He may also have been involved in her torment near Ironslag.
- **Unla Kee:** Identified by the Seer as Unla Kee's nemesis ("Adirond Kroskas, disappeared into the North").
- **Zhentarim:** Senior Zhentarim figures may have information about him from the period surrounding the Cult's fall.
- **Blagothkus & Escarlotta:** Described as horrified upon hearing Thistle recount Kroskas's involvement.
