# Bob Saget

## Identity
- **Role:** Demon of dad jokes; demon lord (self-described MPE — Massively Powerful Entity); self-described "tormentor of kings who killed their jesters"; former headlining actor and theater impresario; holder of a portfolio covering demonic powers of jokes, with traditional punishments as backup for resistant souls
- **Faction:** The Abyss; loosely aligned with the 3HPP (initially); works alongside Kaelen; signatory-adjacent to the 1489 Treaty on Export Regulation of Interplanar Storage Devices (he failed to verify Toril's signatory status); commands drow knights, two cambions, and a Drow Mage capable of animating corpses
- **First Appearance:** End of opening night at the Talking Troll theater in Triboar, manifested during the play's finale through a summoning ritual embedded in the script

*Also known as: Bob, Bob Bob Saget, MPE, Massively Powerful Entity.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Escape boredom; perform comedy for new audiences; twist public opinion against magic and the Spellguards of Silverymoon; execute a ritual requiring murdered victims to build a zombie army; restore his reputation in the Abyss; avoid capture by the three Astral empires; prove he is not a joke to his peers despite his joke-based portfolio.
- Presents as jovial, theatrical, and genuinely delighted to be free of the Abyss, but this charm masks deliberate cruelty — he murders terrified commoners on stage while treating their deaths as punchlines.
- Manipulative and evasive when pressed, deflecting with humor.
- Demonstrates a flair for theatrics, public manipulation, and large-scale schemes — the play and zombie army suggest he enjoys spectacle alongside his malice.
- Confident and taunting, willing to arrive to confrontations with an army before engaging.
- Normally arrogant, but arrived genuinely frightened post-banishment — his ego is tied closely to how others perceive him, and his new portfolio has made him a laughingstock among Abyssal powers.
- Responds strongly to offers that combine personal glory with public humiliation of a rival; the prospect of performing at Iymrith's grand reveal motivated him far more than gold.
- Quick to bluster and threaten (warns he will hunt and burn the party if the performance doesn't happen), but visibly flustered when negotiation goes better than expected.
- Has no apparent loyalty to anyone beyond his own amusement and schemes, framing even his most predatory behavior as a service to others.

## History with the Party

**Triboar — Opening Night, Talking Troll Theater:**
Summoned accidentally during the play's finale as a result of the party's improvements to the original ritual script. Manifested in full demonic regalia: purple skin, skull face, giant purple eyes, animated tassels, moving embroidery. Introduced himself to Unla Kee, stated his pronouns (he/him), and explained his nature as a tormentor of regicidal kings. Declined Orsik's implicit offer of a fight, expressing genuine gratitude to the party for freeing him from a boring Abyss. Became an immediate sensation and took up residency as the theater's star attraction. Revealed the 3HPP had arranged his escape and credited them with his summoning.

**Westpharl Ranch:**
Appeared as a performer at Cindrell's party, cracking jokes for assembled guests. No further context or allegiances disclosed.

**Prior History (Yartar — pre-villain reveal):**
Pow attended Bob's shows multiple times in Yartar, knowing him as a famous actor who always performed in his "demon costume." The theater later burned down; Bob's absence was publicly framed as a leave of absence, not a disappearance.

**Meeting with the 3HPP (Silverymoon):**
Met the party privately. Reported the theater was full every night. Described the play as "a successful perversion of society" that would achieve the party's anti-magic aims within 30 days. Refused to give the party seats, insisting their presence would distract. Disclosed his ongoing creative partnership with Kaelen, who wrote the original summoning play and whom Bob credits with saving him from eternity. Explained that amateur performers were cast as 3HPP stand-ins because their genuine terror reads as authentic on stage. Orsik purchased invisibility potions to attend anyway.

**Great Theater, Silverymoon — The Performance:**
The party attended invisibly. Bob took the stage in purple jacket, tie, and pants; recognized the party's presence when an alarm spell triggered, calling them out by name. Revealed his true scheme openly: luring commoners — particularly those targeted by the Spellguard — with promises of theatrical roles, forcing them to perform as terrified 3HPP stand-ins, killing them on stage, and having a Drow Mage animate the corpses for a zombie audience. During the witnessed performance, he murdered all four commoner performers theatrically (including a gnome stand-in for Unla Kee and a Tabaxi stand-in for Orsik), broke the fourth wall repeatedly, and taunted his invisible guests. He routes the real theater audiences to a personal demi-plane, keeping them alive while the ritual proceeds with substitute victims. Departed the stage laughing. The 3HPP chose not to confront him, prioritizing the giant threat and planning to return covertly.

**Silverymoon — Subsequent Intelligence:**
Later notes describe Bob as having killed and replaced a "real Bob Saget" (a famous actor performing an Orsik-originated persona), suggesting his current public identity is a fabrication. The theater's entrance contains a magical portal intercepting theatergoers. The interior hosts a staged zombie audience dressed as the 3HPP.

**Silverymoon Theater Confrontation:**
Bob arrived with a zombie army and taunted the 3HPP before battle. He was defeated when Unla Kee threw a bag of holding near him and Thuul triggered a planar explosion by stuffing a second bag into the first, banishing Bob to the Astral Plane.

**Sent to the Astral Plane:**
Approximately 200–300 Silverymoon theatergoers were imprisoned in a pocket dimension prior to the fight. With Bob lost in the Astral Plane, no known method initially existed to retrieve them. Lord Zymorven noted that Bob committed no provable demonic actions under the law, leaving him legally untouchable even if he returned. Vardis privately warned Thistle that the recovered script is a demonic summoning ritual — the same one the party barely stopped. Pow, unaware or unconcerned, expressed enthusiasm about using it to draw crowds to the casino. Orsik had Unla Kee banish him via the bag-of-holding explosion, accompanied by one drow ally and four stooges. A second drow was allowed to escape. Bob is noted to be plotting revenge.

**Summoned at Ascore (later):**
Arrived via Vardis's casting of Planar Ally, shaken from being trapped near Toril in the Astral Plane and at risk of capture by the Githyanki, Githizeri, or Mindflayer empires. Immediately focused on the geopolitical fallout from ISD misuse rather than vengeance. Explained the 1489 Treaty, confirmed his MPE status, and acknowledged being tricked by what he called the oldest trick in the book.

**Deal Struck — Iymrith Performance:**
The party proposed having Bob perform at Iymrith's grand reveal disguised as one of her dragon creations. The opportunity to publicly humiliate Iymrith before the most powerful creatures in the region — and to rehabilitate his reputation in the Abyss — appealed enormously. He demanded many speaking lines and a villain speech before attacking. The party offered two zombie blue chromatic dragons hybridized with purple worms as payment; Bob accepted immediately and asked if the party had an invitation.

**Deal Struck — Prisoner Release:**
Bob agreed to release 200 prisoners from a pocket dimension for 20,000 gold. He was visibly flustered when the party accepted without haggling, suggesting he underestimated his leverage. He threatened to hunt the party down if the performance did not occur. He also asked Orsik about a DNRRR (nature unclear). He reserved the two living hybrid dragons for himself, refusing to share the stage with other demons.

**Prisoner Release Fulfilled (Chapter 87):**
Bob was not physically present, but the arrangement was honored — 200 guests emerged from a magical door at the former theater site in Silverymoon when Thistle spoke the words *"Ictum. Antium. Merthium."*

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Operating between the Astral Plane and the Material Plane via summoning; deal fulfilled on the prisoner release; the Iymrith performance deal is still pending
- **Active Plans:** Preparing to perform at Iymrith's grand reveal disguised as one of her dragon creations; expects the two zombie hybrid dragons as payment upon completion. Also (per earlier intelligence) plotting revenge against the party.
- **What the Party Knows:** Bob is a demon, was running a murder-ritual theater operation in Silverymoon, works with Kaelen, commands drow and cambion forces, was banished but is not dead; he is an MPE at risk of Astral capture; he holds a joke-based demonic portfolio; he wants to restore his reputation; he will hunt the party if the performance doesn't happen; Darathra confirmed his banishment was not permanent.
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full extent of his partnership with the 3HPP; the depth of his connection to Kaelen and whether Kaelen powers or sustains him; what resources or allies he retains on the Material Plane; the nature of what he asked Orsik about regarding the DNRRR; the full extent of his vulnerability while near Toril; his actual power level now vs. his claimed capabilities.

## Relationships
- **Kaelen:** Creative partner and the original author of the summoning play. Bob credits Kaelen with saving him from the Abyss. Their collaboration is ongoing and appears to be a genuine working relationship, not mere exploitation.
- **The 3HPP / The Party:** Initially credited with arranging his escape; relationship has soured significantly following his exposure and banishment. Now bound by two deals — one fulfilled, one pending. Relationship is transactional but with real stakes; he has threatened lethal consequences for non-delivery. Targeted them specifically, using zombie replicas of the party as theatrical performers; considers them notable enough to antagonize directly.
- **Drow Forces / Cambions:** Commands two cambions and drow knights; works with a Red Wizard-allied Drow Mage capable of corpse animation.
- **Orsik:** Primary point of contact within the party; Orsik both confronted him and proposed the most aggressive plan to leverage him (the phylactery deal). Bob is aware of Orsik by name. Asked Orsik directly about a DNRRR — suggests some prior knowledge of or interest in Orsik specifically.
- **Unla Kee:** First party member Bob formally introduced himself to; Unla Kee executed the banishment via the bag-of-holding explosion.
- **Pow:** A genuine fan of Bob's pre-villain theater work; attended multiple shows in Yartar; does not fully grasp (or care about) the danger Bob represents.
- **Lord Zymorven:** Assessed the legal situation post-battle; his framing protects Bob from prosecution if he returns.
- **Darathra:** Issued the warning that Bob's banishment is temporary.
- **Vardis / Thistle:** Vardis privately flagged the recovered script as a summoning ritual, keeping this from the wider group. Vardis later summoned him via Planar Ally.
- **Iymrith:** Target of the planned public humiliation; no prior personal relationship noted.
- **Githyanki / Githizeri / Mindflayers:** Active threat — all three empires are aware Toril violated the 1489 Treaty and are mobilizing toward the planet; Bob was nearly captured by them.
- **The Abyss (peers):** Regarded as a joke since receiving his new portfolio; the Iymrith performance is partly motivated by a need to prove himself to them.
- **The Trading House / Chromax:** Co-signatories to the 1489 Treaty; relationship and current stance toward Bob or Toril not established.

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase (implied):** Manifested with crowd ecstasy; became the toast of Silverymoon; theater full every night — Bob achieves genuine cultural influence.
- **Decrease:** Exposed before the party during the Silverymoon performance; his ritual and methods fully revealed; subsequently banished to the Astral Plane via Unla Kee/Thuul's bag-of-holding explosion — a significant defeat, but not a permanent one.
