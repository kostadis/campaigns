# Tack Proudfoot Esquire

## Identity
- Minor noble of Mirabar; revealed to be a knight of the elite guard
- Betrothed to a member of Clan Battlehammer, one of Mirabar's leading houses
- First appeared when he challenged Orsik (disguised as John Husteem) to a duel at dawn outside the city, following an offense at a dinner party

## Personality & Motivations
- Presents as a romantic idealist steeped in chivalric dueling culture — initially dismissed by Yip Hammaver as a dandy who read too many romance novels
- Beneath the affectation lies a genuine nobleman's code of honor: when Orsik refused to fight, Tack accepted the gesture as honorable and bore no ill will
- Warm, socially enthusiastic, and quick to shift from adversary to ally; immediately pivoted to trying to introduce Orsik to eligible noble matches

## History with the Party
1. Tack was offended by Orsik (as John Husteem) at a Mirabar dinner party and issued a formal dueling challenge for dawn outside the city.
2. Yip Hammaver downplayed the threat, calling Tack a non-serious dandy.
3. At the duel, Tack arrived in the finest armor Orsik had ever seen and was recognized as a knight of the elite guard — far more dangerous than expected. His political connections (the Battlehammer betrothal) made his death potentially catastrophic.
4. Orsik cast sanctuary and refused to fight. Tack declared honor satisfied, embraced Orsik warmly, and immediately began pushing to introduce him to noble marriage prospects in Mirabar.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Mirabar
- Betrothed to a member of Clan Battlehammer; social life appears active
- No known hostile operations; currently an unlikely ally and social contact
- **What the party knows:** His identity, political connections, and friendly disposition post-duel
- **What remains hidden:** Nothing flagged in notes — his cards appear to be on the table

## Relationships
- **Orsik (alias John Husteem):** Challenged him, then embraced him as a friend after the duel resolved peacefully; actively trying to integrate him into Mirabar noble society
- **Clan Battlehammer:** Betrothed to a member; killing him would have triggered serious political consequences in Mirabar
- **Yip Hammaver:** Yip underestimated him and was wrong — potential awkwardness if this surfaces
