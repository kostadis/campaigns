# Methrammar Aerasumé

## Identity
- **Role:** High Lord of the Silver Marches; ruler of Silverymoon; High Marshal of the Argent Legion
- **Faction:** Silverymoon political establishment
- **Parentage (per in-campaign reference):** Son of Lady Laeral Silverhand; SKT canon names his mother as Alustriel Silverhand. The campaign has used both at different points; treat as recorded.
- **First Appearance:** First referenced by Dral Thelev in Everlund as the cause of the political rupture between Silverymoon and Everlund; first met in person when he summoned the 3HPP to his audience chamber via a Silverwatch captain, framing it as an obligation to present diplomatic credentials rather than a request

*Also known as: Lord Methrammar Aerasumé, Lord Methrammar Aersume.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Maintaining Silverymoon's political sovereignty, reputation, and pro-magic cultural identity; protecting guests under his city's hospitality; advancing the city's cultural prestige; eliminating threats (e.g., Iymrith) that benefit his enemies; stabilizing his politically weakened rule
- **Drives:** Legacy and civic pride — deeply invested in the narrative that magic serves and defines Silverymoon
- Enthusiastic and somewhat impulsive — leaped to offer 50,000 gold for a theater run before his High Treasurer reined him in. Enthusiastic about art and spectacle (described the play's battle scene with evident excitement), but keeps firm control over how the city and its symbols are presented.
- Cordial on the surface, but carries a clear undercurrent of authority; he rewards loyalty and success generously but responds to perceived political probing with swift, veiled warnings.
- Pragmatic and politically calculating — he will accept a convenient narrative if the outcome serves Silverymoon, regardless of what he privately suspects.
- Haggard and under pressure but not ineffective; he knows how to read a room and respond to public commitments once made.
- Not naïve: he suspects the "dragon explanation" for the theater incident is a simplification but is "wise enough not to care."
- His hospitality ethic appears to be a genuine governing principle, not merely a pretext; he refused extradition of Xamlyn Margaster on those grounds without apparent hesitation.

## History with the Party

1. **Pre-meeting (Everlund):** Referenced by Dral Thelev as the root cause of the Silverymoon–Everlund political split. High Captain Horix Zoar holds a personal grievance against him for abandoning allies, which led Zoar to dissolve the alliance and break the compact.

2. **Audience chamber, Silverymoon:** Summoned the 3HPP and framed the meeting as a diplomatic formality — they were to present credentials as Everlund envoys. Revealed he had seen the traveling play twice and was openly delighted by it, particularly the "Mordekainens' Best Home Illusions" segment and the rotating endings. Offered an extended run at the Royal Theater; initial offer of 50,000 gp was walked back by his High Treasurer to 1,000 gp plus free use of the theater. Refused outright to extradite Xamlyn Margaster, citing her status as a city guest and member of a respectable Waterdhavian noble house. Acknowledged that the 3HPP's prior removal from Silverymoon was a "terrible misunderstanding" corrected by High Captain Horix Zoar.

3. **High Palace Meeting (post-play):** Delivered 12,000 gp as the party's share of profits from *Bob Saget's* play, which had sold out for two months. Explained their cut would cover the 6,000 gp owed to the red dragon. Praised the play warmly, describing the depictions of Orsik, Vardis, and Unla Kee with enthusiasm. Noted that Thistle's role is performed by a puppet on a stick — no actor small enough was found. Characterized the play as a "spiritual investigation" into individual accountability and the dangers of unchecked magic, not a political piece.

4. **Tension with Orsik:** Flatly rejected a proposal for a new play. When Orsik pressed him on whether magic was dangerous, Aersume responded with a direct veiled threat: *"There is a danger in your questions."* Declared that anyone claiming magic cannot serve Silverymoon is a traitor to the city. Invoked his mother, Lady Laeral Silverhand (favored of Mystara), as proof that magic has served Silverymoon across generations.

5. **Pre-meeting rumors (later):** Aerasumé was already under political attack for patronizing Bob's play; the 3HPP's association with the cloud castle civil war and regional economic decline compounded his vulnerability.

6. **Palace confrontation (Iymrith arc):** Met the party over breakfast, visibly worn down. Listed formal grievances: ~200 notables missing after the theater fire, no witness memory of the play (suggesting infernal magic), and damage to Silverymoon's reputation.

7. **Negotiation:** Became cautiously engaged when Thistle offered to return the missing citizens and implicated Iymrith. Agreed to attend the rescue personally after a courtier noted he'd lose political credit otherwise; deferred to Taern Hornblade on security matters.

8. **Public commitment:** Was caught off guard when Thistle publicly pledged his support against Iymrith in front of the theater crowd, but accepted the fait accompli.

9. **Resource commitment:** Agreed to provide five battle mages (overriding Taern's suggestion of four).

10. **Private conversation with Thistle:** Revealed plans to formally recognize fairies as a goodly race of Everlund within a few months, contingent on Iymrith's defeat. Confirmed he suspects the dragon explanation is politically convenient but intends to use it regardless.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Silverymoon; returned with the party to the rotunda following the theater rescue
- **Active Plans:** Coordinating five battle mages to support the mission against Iymrith; preparing (privately) to formally recognize fairies as a goodly race pending Iymrith's death; hosting Xamlyn Margaster as a protected guest; extended run of the 3HPP's play at the Royal Theater
- **What the Party Knows:** He controls Silverymoon absolutely, will not extradite Margaster, and is personally charmed by the party's theatrical work. He considers the prior expulsion from the city resolved. He is a willing, if reluctant, political ally; he is privately skeptical of the dragon narrative but supportive of the outcome; he has made a public commitment to the 3HPP's mission. He is generous with profit-sharing, protective of Silverymoon's pro-magic identity, and not someone to push politically.
- **What Remains Hidden:** His full reasoning for the original abandonment of Everlund's allies; his deeper political calculus regarding Margaster's presence in the city; the full extent of his political weakness and what contingencies he may have if Iymrith is not defeated; the precise timeline and conditions for fairy recognition; his deeper agenda around the play's "spiritual investigation" framing.

## Relationships
- **Lady Laeral Silverhand (mother, per campaign reference):** Invoked with evident pride and reverence; uses her legacy as civic and personal authority. Her legacy shapes the standard Silverymoon is held to.
- **Horix Zoar (High Captain, Everlund):** Deep personal animosity. Zoar has never forgiven Methrammar for abandoning allied forces, leading to the dissolution of the Silver Marches compact.
- **Xamlyn Margaster:** Extends her full diplomatic hospitality as a guest of Silverymoon and member of a Waterdhavian noble house; shielded her from extradition.
- **Taern Hornblade:** Trusted security advisor; Aerasumé generally defers to him on military/security matters, though he overrode him on the mage count.
- **Thistle (party member):** Primary point of contact; Thistle has twice maneuvered him publicly, but Aerasumé has accepted both moves and engaged in candid private dialogue with her.
- **Orsik (party member):** Cordial business relationship undercut by friction; directed his warning and political rebuke at Orsik after his probing questions.
- **The 3HPP (party):** Reluctant allies — he holds real grievances against them but recognizes their utility against Iymrith. Cordial and enthusiastic on the diplomatic surface; treats them as envoys from Everlund and as artists whose work he personally admires.
- **His High Treasurer:** Unnamed; actively moderates Methrammar's more impulsive financial decisions.
- **Iymrith:** Identified enemy; her death is explicitly in Silverymoon's interest regardless of the details surrounding the theater.
