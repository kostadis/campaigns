# Bressik

## Identity
- **Role:** Hired scholar and researcher for the 3HPP; self-described "loresmith" (information broker); formerly a sage at Candlekeep; crew member aboard the Great Airship (Dragon's Breath) gifted by the ancient red dragon Klauth
- **Faction:** Previously affiliated with the original Cult of the Dragon (Dracolich faction); formerly under contract to Klamath the Red Dragon necromancer; crew of the Great Airship; currently free of his contract
- **Ship:** Aboard the Dragon's Breath (Delsephine's airship)
- **First Appearance:** Met the party at the start of the campaign; negotiated a 50 gold fee to provide information about Beorunna's Well

*Also known as: Brazzik, Brezzik, the Loresmith.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Earn fair compensation for his expertise; conduct legitimate scholarly work; avoid the consequences of his past cult involvement
- **Drives:** Academic pride, financial self-interest, and a lingering ideological attachment to the original Dracolich vision
- Bressik is precise, easily flustered by disrespect, and quick to take offense when his scholarship is dismissed — he spluttered in protest when Orsik cut off his presentation, then thought better of it. He holds grudges quietly and strategically, preferring to extract payment later rather than confront directly. He is capable of self-deprecating honesty about his past but draws a firm line between his cult and Severin's faction. He privately questions the ethics of what he's helping the party do, but suppresses this when reminded of his pay.

## History with the Party

**Early sessions — Beorunna's Well & One Stone:**
Bressik negotiated a 50 gold fee (the party initially offered 500 silver) and delivered a detailed briefing on Beorunna's Well: its origin legend, the Black Lion and Red Tiger tribes, the triangular cavern geography, the heated pool, the knotted rope, the Sunken Grove, and the blood-splattered altar concealing a giant relic. He admitted he had placed a wager on the party's success and declined to increase it. He later provided information on the Sky Pony tribe's spirit mound at the One Stone for five platinum, irritated by the inconsistency of coinage. He described the Moonwood plateau, the engraved round boulder altar, the surrounding stream, and the totem poles with trespassers' corpses. When asked whether the relic was under the rock or was the rock, he said it was unclear — Orsik declared it was beneath.

**Raven's Rock:**
Bressik presented research on the Black Raven and Gray Wolf spirit mound: the 100-foot stone raven, four menhirs tracking seasons and stars, and the Gray Wolf altar in the wolf-shaped depression. Orsik cut him off mid-presentation and declared they were going straight to the altar. Bressik spluttered in protest, then fell silent, remembering Orsik's blade and temper. He spent that evening conversing with Thistle about his relationship with Thuul the drake, finding the anarcho-socialist partnership Thistle described "fascinating."

**En route to Great Worm Cavern — Cult of the Dragon debate:**
Bressik was drawn into a debate about the Cult of the Dragon. He clarified that neither he nor Delsephine were members of Severin's faction, and defended the original Dracolich ideology — arguing that the vision of a unified undead draconic race was subverted by Severin and Tiamat through what amounted to an ideological coup. He was visibly offended when Thistle dismissed this as academic nonsense. He offered a linguistic aside: the term "Dracolich" was chosen to emphasize the unifying, draconic nature of the ritual.

**Great Worm Cavern:**
Bressik delivered his second formal presentation, covering the cavern geography, icy ledges, tribal cave dwellings, steam vents, frozen standing corpses in narrow crypts, and the serpent-shaped altar of Elrem the Great Worm. He offered two theories on the gong artifact: either it is the artifact itself — a broken frost giant shield of red dragon scales on a copper frame, 13 feet in diameter, 250 pounds — or it is decorative, with the real artifact concealed beneath the altar as at prior sites. He favored the second theory based on the established pattern. He then descended to the lower decks. During this period, he privately questioned whether he should continue assisting with the desecration of ancient sites, and suppressed the doubt.

**Morgur's Mound:**
Bressik pulled out a map of Morgur's Mound obtained during his time at Candlekeep and briefed the party on its history: the lost Thunderbeast tribe's spirit mound, named after Uthgar's brother, shaped like a long-necked wingless dragon with a ridge of dragon bones, surrounded by cairns a quarter mile out containing tribal warrior remains. He noted the site had not been visited in years and had been heavily plundered. He offered a self-deprecating confession — acknowledging his cult past and dragon servitude — as justification for not judging the party's grave-robbing.

**Contract purchase:**
Orsik negotiated with Delsephine to purchase the living portion of Bressik's contract with Klamath. Delsephine valued the full contract at approximately 13,300 gold (roughly 300 gold for the living term, 13,000 for the assumed undead term). The deal was brokered by Unla Kee at 500 gold upfront plus residuals capped at 6,000 gold — structured against the current show only, leaving the sequel ("The Gong Show") unencumbered. Buying the living portion freed Bressik entirely; his obligations did not transfer to Orsik.

**Post-freedom:**
Bressik was delighted to be free and threw himself into background research and show work. He privately noted that Thistle had never thought to ask about his girlfriend, and resolved that this information — which touches on the fairy, Uncle Joon, and his journeys — would cost the party when they came asking. He also harbored quiet resentment toward Orsik and Vardis for underpaying him, privately gloating that they would never learn where their treasure map leads.

**Loresmith services aboard the Great Airship (as "Brazzik"):**
Introduced himself to the party as a loresmith and offered intelligence on the Grandfather Tree for a fee. After Thistle demonstrated partial existing knowledge, Orsik and Vardis paid him **50 gold pieces** for a full account. Provided the following intelligence: confirmed Thistle's account of the Seelie Dryads **Halisa and Helia** guarding the Grandfather Tree as punishment from the Fey Queen; warned that killing the dryads carries powerful and dangerous consequences; noted that Tree Ghost Tribe (Uthgardt) patrols sometimes pass through the area; added that the dryads have allied with various forest creatures to defend the tree. Intervened when an elf crewmember began questioning Vardis about being expelled from Lorien's cult, cutting the conversation short — reason for the protective gesture unknown.

**Departure from the party (as "Brezzik"):**
Was associated with the 3HPP during a period involving a play they had a stake in. When the hoped-for rewrite of that play fell through, Brezzik chose to seek work elsewhere; departed voluntarily on apparently non-hostile terms. Orsik relayed this to Delsephine when she asked about him at Parnast.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Aboard the Dragon's Breath
- **Current Activity:** Conducting background research; working on the show in his newfound free capacity
- **Active Plans:** Withholding information about his girlfriend, Uncle Joon, and Uncle Joon's journeys until the party pays appropriately. Privately gloating that Orsik and Vardis will never learn where their treasure map leads due to prior underpayment.
- **What the Party Knows:** His contract has been purchased; he is free and working with them voluntarily. He has cult history and dragon servitude in his past.
- **What Remains Hidden:** The content of the information he's withholding about his girlfriend and Uncle Joon. The full nature of his resentment toward Orsik and Vardis and his intent to leverage knowledge as payback.

## Relationships
- **Orsik:** Tense. Bressik fears his temper and blade, defers under pressure, but harbors quiet resentment over being underpaid and talked over. Privately gloating about withheld treasure map information.
- **Vardis:** Similarly resented for refusing fair payment. Lumped with Orsik in Bressik's private grievances.
- **Thistle:** Warmly curious. Found Thistle's relationship with Thuul the drake genuinely fascinating. Mildly puzzled that Thistle never thought to ask about his personal life, given how much Bressik knows.
- **Delsephine:** Former employer via the Klamath contract arrangement. Negotiated his freedom through her; relationship now concluded contractually.
- **Klamath (Red Dragon necromancer):** Former contractual master. Contract covered both living and undead terms. Bressik's living obligations are now purchased out; he is free.
- **Unla Kee:** Brokered his freedom deal; structured residuals favorably for future projects.
- **The Cult of the Dragon (Severin's faction):** Ideological opposition. Bressik distinguishes himself and Delsephine sharply from Severin's Tiamat-aligned coup.

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase implied:** Freed from Klamath's contract — a meaningful improvement in his circumstances, received with visible delight.
- **Decrease implied:** Repeated underpayment and dismissal by Orsik and Vardis; being talked over during presentations; Thistle dismissing his Dracolich ideology as "academic nonsense." These have not broken his cooperation but have hardened his intent to extract future payment for withheld information.
