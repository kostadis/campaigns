# The SEER

## Identity
- **Role:** Top Spymaster and intelligence chief of the Lord's Alliance; runs its assassination, covert operations, and dirty-work wing. Described by Hsing as "the top spymaster in the world."
- **Faction:** Lord's Alliance (senior architect, not public-facing)
- **First Appearance:** Never appears in early sessions but is a subject of discussion from the party's time in the Marsh of Chelimber onward. First physical appearance: materializes through a door in Parnast.
- **Associates:** Hsing (gold pseudodragon, direct messenger and payment enforcer); an unidentified agent tracking the party

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Protect Lord's Alliance interests in the region (keep Parnast aligned, counter Zhentarim and giant influence); acquire ancient magical manuscripts; broker a historic peace between Giants and the northern kingdoms by leveraging King Hekaton's goodwill; monitor and utilize the party as free agents.
- **Traits:** Strategically patient and meticulous — she arrives to negotiations having already profiled each party member's individual desires. She is capable of warmth and pragmatism (publicly performing the headband ritual to address Orsik's concerns), but becomes visibly flustered and then furious when her authority and framing are rejected. She is not above threats, but also not above backing down with grace — her parting note to "Orsic" was measured rather than vindictive.

## History with the Party

1. **Marsh of Chelimber (early sessions):** The SEER directs Hsing to locate the party in the swamp and open negotiations for manuscripts from the Wizard of the Crag's tower. Wallace warns the party not to antagonize her. Unla Kee privately resolves not to hand over recovered spellbooks.

2. **Intelligence report (mid sessions):** A letter from an unidentified agent to the SEER reveals she is tracking the party closely, prioritizing Parnast's political alignment, and had hoped to recruit Orsik to the Lord's Alliance before learning he was Zhentarim.

3. **Parnast — Headband Ritual:** The SEER steps through a materializing door into Parnast. Orsik confronts her at wandpoint over the evil soul in the cursed headband. She explains she intends to send the soul to its proper destination, offers to perform the ritual publicly to satisfy his concerns, and follows through the next morning. The headband becomes a headband of intellect, which Orsik keeps.

4. **Post-Parnast (offhand reference):** Orsik mentions the party "tried to kill her." Unla Kee reasons that since it failed, she should be counted a friend.

5. **Campsite en route to Ascore:** The SEER arrives via divination magic (aided by intelligence from King Hekaton) with a fully prepared negotiation. She offers each party member individually tailored rewards in exchange for abandoning the hunt for Iymrith and instead resolving the rogue giant lord crisis. All four party members refuse. She escalates to threats, Orsik attempts to strike her (blocked by sanctuary), Vardis fireballs her (countered), and Unla Kee traps her in a forcecage using ruby dust. She smiles and escapes — apparently via time stop and teleportation. She leaves a written note asking Orsik to consider helping the realms after defeating Iymrith, and declining to publicly malign him.

6. **Referenced by Iymrith:** Iymrith denies any alliance with the SEER, calls her the true power behind the Lord's Alliance, describes her as committed to a long-term plan to end the freedom of creatures like Iymrith, and calls her evil. Iymrith affirms Orsik is right to distrust her.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** Unknown — escaped the party's forcecage via time stop and teleportation en route to Ascore.
- **Active Plans:** Brokering a Giant–Alliance peace through Hekaton; continued monitoring of the party; broader long-term agenda (nature unclear) that Iymrith characterizes as threatening to creatures of her kind.
- **What the Party Knows:** She is the Lord's Alliance's spymaster; she has demonstrated teleportation and surveillance capability; she wants the giant lord crisis resolved before Iymrith is dealt with; she has shown both good faith (headband ritual) and willingness to threaten and manipulate.
- **What Remains Hidden:** The full scope of her "long-term plan" that Iymrith references; the identity of the agent tracking the party; what she would actually have done with the evil soul if Orsik hadn't been watching.

## Relationships
- **Hsing (pseudodragon):** Direct agent, messenger, and payment enforcer. Loyal to the SEER's commands explicitly.
- **King Hekaton:** Ally — he provided the SEER with the party's location and is a friend of the Lord's Alliance, though he refused all of her other agents and insisted on this specific group.
- **Wallace:** Vouches for her power and counsels the party not to antagonize her; appears to respect or fear her authority.
- **Unla Kee:** Suspicious of her from the start (withheld spellbooks); pragmatically categorizes her as an ally since the party survived the confrontation.
- **Orsik:** Deep mutual distrust. He challenged her twice (headband confrontation; attempted strike at the campsite). She recognizes he has "changed profoundly" and ultimately declined to curse his reputation despite threatening to.
- **Vardis:** She offered him the most personally costly boon (Lorellon problem resolved, Orsik's safety guaranteed); he responded with a fireball.
- **Thistle:** Offered arrows of dragon slaying and a powerful magical bow; also refused.
- **Iymrith:** Mutual enemies. Iymrith frames the SEER as a systemic threat to creatures like herself and explicitly denies cooperation.
- **Zhentarim:** Adversaries — her intelligence operations exist partly to counter Zhentarim influence in the region.

## Arc Score Events
- **Increase:** Performed the headband ritual publicly and transparently in Parnast, following through on her stated intention. (+trust)
- **Decrease:** Arrived at the campsite with individually profiled manipulation tactics, escalated to threats when refused, and had to be forcecaged before escaping. (-trust, confirmed willingness to coerce)
- **Ambiguous:** Left a measured, non-vindictive note after her escape — consistent with pragmatism rather than genuine goodwill.
