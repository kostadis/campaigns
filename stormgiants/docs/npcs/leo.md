# Leo

## Identity
- **Role:** Proprietor and franchise operator of the Lionel Coster store in Llorkh
- **Faction:** Lionel Coster franchise network (corporate retailer chain)
- **First Appearance:** The party entered his store in Llorkh; he greeted them and immediately established his no-credit policy with new customers

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** Maintaining the presentation, reputation, and standing of his store within the Lionel Coster franchise system — including winning the "best presentation award"
- **Secondary Drive:** Competitive pride; actively disdains rival firms (notably "dd bay," which he refuses to work with due to its "grotesque surcharge")
- Leo is fastidiously groomed at all times — immaculate dress, perfectly trimmed beard, unnaturally white teeth — and the party suspects he uses magic to maintain his appearance, though he insists it is "100% natural, Leo." He is easily rattled by anything that threatens his store's presentation: damaged merchandise, tracked-in dirt, or unexpected inspections send him into visible anxiety. He is gruff and transactional but not unfair — when cornered on a legitimate flaw, he adjusts his price without prolonged argument.

## History with the Party

**First Visit — Scroll Purchase**
Leo greeted the party and stated upfront he does not extend credit to first-time buyers. When Vardis pushed back and invoked Diego's name to leverage a sale, Leo pivoted to Thistle instead. He quoted 300 gold for a scroll of dispel magic. Thistle identified scuff marks on the tube and a crease in the scroll; Leo reacted with genuine horror over the damaged merchandise threatening his "best presentation award" nomination and immediately dropped the price to 250 gold. The party also purchased a new bow and arrows.

**Second Visit — Selling and Shopping**
The party returned to sell treasure and purchase equipment. Leo offered a 10% discount on items he purchased from them. When Unla Kee mentioned "dd bay," Leo reacted with offense and disgust, clarifying he does not work with that firm. Orsik asked about a magnifying glass sized to a specific item; Leo said he could source one in a few days for an additional 5 gold, as it is not standard stock. He acknowledged his Llorkh location carries minimal inventory compared to Aurora Realms Stores, which specialize in magical and exotic items. He offered the party a potion of healing, a potion of climbing, and a scroll of detect magic, noting rarer items are likely beyond their price range. When the party tracked dirt into the store, Leo rushed out and returned with cleaning supplies, visibly anxious about a potential inspection.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** His Lionel Coster store in Llorkh
- **Active Concerns:** Pending "Lionel Coster best presentation award" nomination; possible upcoming store inspection
- **What the Party Knows:** He is a franchise retailer with limited magical inventory, competitive pricing, and strong franchise loyalty. He can special-order non-standard items for a small fee.
- **What Remains Hidden:** The nature of his relationship with Diego (Vardis's name-drop redirected Leo's attention but was never followed up). Whether he actually uses magic to maintain his appearance.

## Relationships
- **Lionel Coster Franchise:** Deep professional loyalty; highly motivated by franchise recognition and awards
- **"dd bay":** Active competitor he refuses to do business with; considers their surcharge practices grotesque
- **Aurora Realms Stores:** Acknowledges them as the superior option for magical/exotic inventory; not hostile, simply pragmatic
- **Diego:** Name carries enough weight that dropping it changed Leo's behavior during the first visit — relationship unclear
- **The Party:** Repeat customers; transactional but functional rapport. Leo is not warm but is consistent and reliable.
