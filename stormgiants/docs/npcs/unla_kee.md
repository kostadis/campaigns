# Unla Kee

## Identity
- **Role:** Gnome/halfling wizard (diviner); member of the party (3HPP)
- **Faction:** Loosely affiliated with the SEER; alumni of the Nirany School of the Arcane; named co-owner of the *Kee and Wing Casino*
- **First Appearance:** Day 9 of the campaign; accompanied Vardis into the tower outside Llorkh

*Note on race: Session notes inconsistently describe Unla Kee as a gnome, halfling, and halfling. Treat as halfling unless GM has reconciled this; he is approximately 3 feet tall and 1 foot wide, and uses oversized pegasus wings (30-foot span) to fly.*

---

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core drive:** A consuming belief that his willingness to take risks is the sole thing preventing the end of the world — he is the universe's unwilling insurance policy against apocalypse, and he knows it.
- **Secondary drives:** Acquisition of arcane power and forbidden lore; gambling; personal glory; chaotic self-interest barely contained by genuine loyalty to the party.
- Unla Kee is impulsive to the point of self-destruction — he charges ahead of the group, grabs dangerous objects, opens trapped chests, pries gems from animated statues, and volunteers to become a vessel for an evil soul. He frames every reckless act as a principled stance.
- He is also genuinely clever: a skilled negotiator, a tactically inventive caster, and perceptive enough to read situations others miss — he simply refuses to slow down long enough to let caution catch up to competence.
- His moral compass is erratic: he proposed blaming a demon attack on "ungodly races" to ignite riots, then felt genuine remorse about it. He calls himself not evil but is drawn to destructive potential.

## History with the Party

**Early Llorkh & the Tower (Day 9)**
Met Vardis and entered the tower. Led the group south at T-intersections, discovered a hexagonal room and a corroded key. During combat with a mimic, his cantrip failed but *Sleep* turned the tide. Confused by the voice warning against using the key; followed Vardis's lead to leave. On return to Llorkh, used fate-manipulation to convince Diego and the Guard to investigate the tower.

**Road to Anauroch / Azam's Caravan**
Traveled with the group toward Parnast in Azam's caravan. Azam described him as "convinced that the fate of the world sits on his willingness to take risks."

**The Mephit Island**
Noticed the marsh mephits taking bets on a new king. Had privately observed portents suggesting a daytime attack would have been better. Approached by Feetor, who offered to front special mud for the betting pool; initially crestfallen at the condition, cheered when Feetor offered to front the stake.

**The Hexagonal Temple (Shrine Crawl)**
Rotated the gargoyle's arm out of confidence in his luck. Declined to steal from Mystara's offering bowl ("I'm a gambler, not a thief"). Made a water offering at Istishia's altar and received immunity to water-based harm. Used *Comprehend Languages* to translate gargoyle speech. Wrote an elaborate personal-cipher entry in Oghma's tome claiming to know how the world ends; Oghma's voice publicly corrected him, then whispered a private correction to Unla alone. Claimed a wand of webs (disputed) and jeweler's tools. Grabbed three spellbooks from the laboratory, triggering a glyph that locked the door and summoned two magmin. Privately resolved not to give the spellbooks to the SEER.

**The Magmin Fight & Elquen the Wight**
Chose to swing a shortsword at a magmin instead of casting *Magic Missile*; was knocked out by the retaliation. Survived via fate manipulation. Later used fate manipulation to cause the wight Elquen to mistake him for a past associate ("The Wizard of the Crag"). Elquen greeted him warmly, relinquished the library, and requested 150 GP in back pay; Unla paid without hesitation, resolving the encounter peacefully.

**Meeting the SEER**
Rushed to open the ornate door ("when I stop taking risks, the world ends!"), grabbed a cookie immediately. Examined the Sword Coast map with Thistle. Received personal intelligence: Uncle Joon is in Triboar; nemesis Adirond Kroskas has vanished into the North; beloved Laela may be alive — possibly captured by Fire Giant allies. Stared at this information silently. Figured out how to combine Sylas's antidote ingredients when no one else could. Told Chandra Stol that not joining would mean the end of the world. Received the book *Prophecies of the Apocalypse* from the SEER on departure.

**Road to Prince Thornacious / Days 5–6**
Identified Bad Fruul's banner among hoard members; extrapolated hoard size, inferred an orc tribe (the Spinerippers) had not yet joined, and deduced their likely location. During the cave battle, cast fireballs to deal with a trap, was then knocked unconscious by an orog and acolyte of Grummsh erupting from that direction; saved by Orsik, Wallace, and Thistle.

**Battle for Parnast**
Cut down by an orc screaming "Die Unla Kee!" — internally thought "The prophecy says otherwise." Healed by Vardis and returned to the fight. Used *Mind Sliver* twice on chieftain Kurglow, penetrating his defenses on the second attempt. Deployed the wand of web to neutralize the mammoth. Positioned himself directly in front of Bad Fruul. Greeted warmly by Blassios as "my drinking buddy."

**Post-Parnast / Triboar**
Accepted Blasius's wine as "a risk." Declared desire to become a vessel for the evil soul in the magical headband, wanting access to drow magic and five-hundred-year-old lore. Drank and sang with Blasius into the following morning. Cast *Thunderwave* to shatter ice on a fire giant; cast *Magic Missiles* for the killing blow on the second wounded giant. Cast *Speak with Dead* on a fire giant corpse and translated the results (the giant revealed Duke Zalto's ambitions and condemned storm giants before refusing further answers). Helped Orsik estimate the weight of a massive metal band (conclusion: between 1 and 10,000 pounds).

**Longsaddle / Xantharl's Keep**
Went straight to the Gambling Golem in Longsaddle; used future-sight to influence a wager. During the battle at Xantharl's Keep, unleashed fireballs that wounded Weevil and his bugbears. Confronted the captured Weevil with a wanted poster and flatly called him a liar.

**Harshnag Encounter**
Described by Harshnag as the most narcissistic, self-absorbed personality he has ever encountered, with complete disregard for personal safety. Expressed disappointment Harshnag couldn't make a castle fly. Was entirely unbothered when Harshnag held his axe blade next to him. Called the end of the Ordning "chaos!" with enthusiasm.

**Prettyview / Spine of the World**
Eagerly drank Yazmeen's magical tea. Stayed alert at night, noticed the Slaad preparing a fireball, and countered it. Used the wand of web to trap the Slaad at the window, maintained concentration through fire attacks, held until the creature was killed. After the fight, asked about checking on Yazmeen while Vardis evacuated. Cast *Water Breathing* on the group during the avalanche to prevent asphyxiation.

**The Temple of the All-Father**
Dashed past massive stone blocks despite Thistle's trap warnings (did not trigger them). Rushed to examine the giant portal archway and ran through the glowing mist — hit the wall, reported nothing to see. When asked for a divination spell, revealed he had none memorized ("I know the future; what else is there to divine?"). Knocked unconscious by the ice storm; killed by the subsequent Storm of Vengeance. Carried out by Harshnag. The next morning, expressed enthusiasm about repeating the experience.

**The Remorhaz & Oracle Chamber**
Was swallowed whole by the remorhaz early in combat. Cast *Raulothim's Psychic Lance* to stun it, then *Binding Ice* to immobilize it. Voluntarily followed Harshnag into the oracle chamber over Orsik's objections. Used one of the six oracle questions to ask how to stop the giants; received the answer about the magic conch and Hekaton's court. Informed Harshnag the oracle "checks out." Privately dismissed the oracle's revelations as having nothing to teach him.

**Dragon's Breath / Cloud Castle Approach**
Recited a comprehensive list of party allies unprompted. Flew to the crumbling tower's balcony on his own and was immediately confronted by Sandesyl Morgia. Referenced wanting to cast fireball during the zombie encounter.

**Blagothkus's Castle — Lower Courtyard**
Called out "Target practice!" while burning skeletal remains. Disappointed to find the barracks already looted. Attempted repeatedly to use the Wand of Wonder over party objections. Identified the copper figurine of Yondalla and explained its properties. Revealed knowledge of lichdom and phylacteries (from Nirany School training). Triggered the kitchen undead by entering brashly; critically wounded and left stunned and prone by a specter.

**The Specter Aftermath**
Had his life force drained while stunned. Identified the specter-creation process (souls ripped while still living), implicating Sandesyl. Pocketed a gem of elemental summoning. Firmly refused to confront Sandesyl, calling it "bright red" and referencing a larger "END GAME" he declined to explain. Proposed the theory that Sandesyl had carved her lair into the glacier's ice outside the Dracolich's caves.

**Statue Cavern / Approach to Sandesyl**
Identified the second statue as Sammaster holding the Tome of the Dragon. Despite Thistle's warning, pried gems from Inferno's eye sockets, animating four statues. Was dealt with by Blagothkus. Revealed a *Haste* spell he had been withholding ("it would be too easy"). Cast it before the assault on the vampire spawn.

**Sandesyl's Lair**
Identified the skeletal dragon immediately as "a failed attempt at a dracolich," explaining Sandesyl's need for the book. Survived a fireball (saved, maintained concentration). Calculated and cast *Fireball* into the cluster around Orsik, devastating enemies while Orsik endured the blast. Held concentration when Sandesyl seized him with her claws. Cast *Sunbeam* as she retreated behind the dragon — a searing strike that burned her severely and caused her to scream "A diviner!" revealing his predictions had tracked her movement. Struck by the lightning bolt trap when Orsik opened the chest.

**The Heist (Zalto's Stronghold)**
Repeatedly suggested fireball, cloudkill, and dominate person — all dismissed. Used a psychic lance to stun a fire salamander, enabling a quiet kill. Cast *Knock* on an iron chest, triggering an incendiary blast that killed him (subsequently revived). Expressed desire for the riskiest plan and interest in "letting chaos reign in Silverymoon." Was stopped before using the rock of wonder by Orsik.

**Bob / Silver Sword of Gith**
Executed the key move: tossed the first bag of holding near the demon to set up Thuul's kill. Afterward, reflected with genuine discomfort on the race-riot cover story he had briefly proposed. Blew the conch, transporting the group to the Maelstrom. Suggested offering the vampire a tome on creating Dracoliches or a Dracolich-controlling artifact as a bargaining chip.

**The Maelstrom / Count Vaal**
Struck the killing blow on Count Vaal using multiple power blasts. Used a psychic lance earlier in the fight. Was already running toward the stairs to fight storm giants before the debate concluded. Squeezed through the portcullis in the portal chamber. Internally monologued with grandiose satisfaction about owning the Maelstrom's sinkhole. Swam toward treasure guarded by crabs, unwilling to let Orsik have it alone.

**Underwater Maelstrom Exploration**
Cast *Toll the Dead* against a crab (resisted). Was grappled by the roper. Cast two fireballs in the roper fight. Independently identified an engineered anemone in the shark room as a trapped valuables container, opened it safely, and retrieved 400 platinum pieces. Was ascending the spiral staircase before Thistle finished deliberating.

**Oosith / The Aboleth**
Used *Telekinesis* to open a drainage valve, slowing the Merrow's flood. Was dominated by the aboleth Oosith and ordered to "Destroy them!" — used the wand of wonder and turned invisible instead. The aboleth was furious; Orsik and Vardis noted this was characteristic. Secured naming rights to the casino: the establishment is now the *Kee and Wing Casino*.

**Kraken Society Investigation**
Drew on Nirany School education: Krakens are Leviathans of Legend, primeval forces, communicate via telepathy at ~100-foot range. Initially tried to undermine Thistle's dragon-conspiracy theory to advocate for talking to (and obtaining power from) the dragon. Ultimately conceded the theory. Asked whether he could join the Kraken Society; recoiled at the remote brain-exploding device.

**Ship Battle / Hekaton's Court**
Flew using pegasus wings; frustrated by the sleet storm obscuring targets. Forgot and then remembered *Haste* mid-combat; lost flight inside the storm and landed prone on the deck. Downed by a poisoned weapon from the first mate; countered the wizard's *Cone of Cold* before going down. Survived. During the throne room scene, muttered "No, you aren't" when Nym and Mirran greeted Hekaton — signaling awareness of the daughters' treachery. After Hekaton's offer, immediately ran past the King toward the teleportation chamber, intent on returning to the cloud castle to kill Iymrith alone.

## Current Status
- **Last known location:** Running toward the Maelstrom's teleportation chamber, bypassing Hekaton's diplomatic proceedings.
- **Active intent:** Return to the cloud castle and kill the ancient blue dragon Iymrith — apparently on his own initiative, without waiting for the party.
- **What the party knows:** Unla Kee is a diviner; he has been withholding *Haste* until he deems it necessary; he has a "END GAME" plan he has not disclosed; he received a private whispered correction from Oghma's voice that no one else heard; he intends to keep the spellbooks he took rather than deliver them to the SEER.
- **Hidden from the party:** The exact content of Oghma's private whisper; the full scope of his "END GAME"; his private resolution not to give the spellbooks to the SEER; the fact that the residuals deal with Delsephine applies only to the current play (a sequel would not be encumbered).

## Relationships

**Vardis** — Traveling companion and de facto authority figure Unla Kee follows when he follows anyone. Vardis healed him at Parnast and frequently witnesses his worst decisions. Genuine mutual respect beneath the chaos.

**Orsik** — Frequent foil. Orsik watches Unla Kee's recklessness with stunned amusement, shuts down his worst plans (rock of wonder, race-riot cover story), but also cheers his successes. They operate as chaotic-practical partners.

**Thistle** — Unla Kee's relationship with Thistle is barbed; he revealed her romantic interest to the group unprompted, provoking her ire. He respects her tactical instincts but competes with her for the role of person who knows how things end.

**Harshnag** — Harshnag described Unla Kee as the most narcissistic, self-absorbed personality he has ever encountered. Unla Kee treated Harshnag's axe blade next to his face as a conversation prompt. They have a functional working relationship.

**Blassios / Blasius** — Prior friendship; greeted as "my drinking buddy." Drank and sang with him into the morning after Parnast.

**Elquen (the Wight)** — Elquen mistook him for a past associate connected to Elquen's prior life. Unla paid him 150 GP in back pay and they parted peacefully.

**The SEER** — Receives intelligence and resources from the SEER; possesses the *Prophecies of the Apocalypse* from her. Privately plans to withhold spellbooks from her. The SEER has identified Unla Kee's risk-taking as potentially essential to preventing the world's end.

**Delsephine** — Negotiated her down to 500 GP upfront plus capped residuals; privately structured the deal so a sequel production would not be encumbered.

**Pow** — Named the casino *Kee and Wing Casino* with a handshake. Asked Pow to rig it in his favor; unconvinced by the explanation that this would cost the house money.

**Uncle Joon** — Still alive in Triboar. Unla Kee is aware of his location.

**Laela** — Described as Unla Kee's "beloved." Rumored to be alive, possibly captured by Fire Giant allies. Unla Kee received this information silently.

**Adirond Kroskas** — Described as Unla Kee's nemesis. Has disappeared into the North.

**Oghma** — Publicly corrected Unla Kee's apocalypse claim in the tome, then whispered a private correction to him alone. Content of the whisper unknown.
