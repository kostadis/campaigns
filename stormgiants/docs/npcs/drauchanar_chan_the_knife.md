# Drauchanar ("Chan" / "the Knife")

## Identity
- **Role:** Elven hunter of rare and dangerous game; operates independently in the Feywild
- **First appearance:** Encountered by the party shortly after they arrived through the Feywild portal; offered himself as a guide and ally

*Also known as: Drauchanar, Chan, Chan the Knife, The Knife.*

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core goals:** To hunt and claim the head of the Fomorian (Maergun) as a trophy — a matter of personal pride and apparent professional reputation. Beyond that, motivated by self-aggrandizement and aligning himself with perceived power or royalty.
- Pragmatic and socially savvy; quickly negotiated the awkward situation of a Fey Prince's presence to everyone's satisfaction.
- Has a deep cultural and emotional connection to elven tradition — his campfire song about Corellon and Araushnee was clearly meaningful to him, not performative.
- Goes by familiar nicknames (Chan, the Knife), suggesting a relaxed confidence in his own identity and standing. Takes his nom-de-guerre seriously and corrects those who misuse it ("I am not a knife, I am THE Knife").
- Boastful and theatrical post-rescue; reframes captivity as a deliberate strategic sacrifice and claims credit for the giant's death through "spectacular leadership" despite having been chained and helpless during the battle.
- Quick to compliment those he perceives as important (especially "Prince Thornacious") and to flatter the party in ways that center his own genius.
- Sycophantic and status-conscious — defers to who he believes is Prince Thornacious and actively suppresses Thistle's questions on Orsik's behalf.
- Maintains confident, upbeat affect despite obvious exhaustion.

## History with the Party

1. **First meeting (Feywild forest):** Appeared in the Feywild after the party's arrival; introduced himself as a specialist hunter with the Fomorian as his current quarry and offered to guide them.

2. **Negotiation over Orsik:** Raised that the presence of Fey Prince Orsik-Thornacious would cost him his trophy rights; accepted Thistle's suggestion that Orsik disguise himself as a dwarf, framing it enthusiastically as preserving social forms while keeping the hunt intact.

3. **Camp:** Led the party into the forest at night; sang an old elvish song about Corellon and Araushnee before the sundering — the song visibly affected Vardis despite his cynicism.

4. **Abduction:** During the night, was ambushed and dragged off by a giant-like creature and a four-legged beast; his disappearance became the immediate catalyst driving the party toward the Fomorian's cave.

5. **Captive in Maergun's throne room:** Found hanging from a chain in Maergun's throne room when the party reached it. Once freed by Vardis, he immediately declared victory, claimed his capture was intentional, and credited himself with leading the party to the location. Complimented the group on navigating the waterfall entrance and praised Vardis for seeing through an illusion. Continued to believe Orsik was Prince Thornacious. Last seen with the party as they entered Maergun's treasure vault.

6. **Outside the cave (post-defeat) — "The Knife":** Appeared outside the cave after the party defeated Maergun and banished Aeglossil. Was introduced again by Thistle ("Here's The Knife"). Continued to believe Orsik was Prince Thornacious and ingratiated himself accordingly. Shouted Thistle down when he asked uncomfortable questions about Aeglossil, defending "Prince Thornacious's" right to end a conversation. Led or accompanied the party to the portal back from the Feywild.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The Feywild, having escorted the party to the portal
- **Active Plans:** Unknown
- **What the party knows:** He was their hunting-guide-turned-captive-turned-rescued-companion; aggressively loyal to whoever he believes Orsik to be; has the Fomorian's head as his trophy claim
- **Hidden from the party:** No indication he suspects Orsik is not Prince Thornacious — this is an active misconception that could become a complication
- **What remains hidden from the party:** His true identity beyond the nicknames; his actual role or faction; his relationship to Prince Thornacious; why he was apparently waiting for them outside the cave

## Relationships
- **The party (general):** Short but positive rapport; served as guide and cultural touchstone in an unfamiliar plane; positions himself as their de facto leader despite having just met them.
- **Vardis:** Views favorably; credited Vardis with seeing through the illusion and implicitly with leading the rescue. Chan's campfire song had a notable emotional effect on Vardis, creating an unexpected personal connection despite Vardis's cynicism.
- **Thistle:** Solved his political problem with Orsik; Chan responded with genuine enthusiasm — likely views her favorably. Later dismissive and hostile when Thistle asked questions Chan found impertinent.
- **Orsik / Orsik-Thornacious:** Believes him to be Prince Thornacious; openly deferential and flattering; relationship built on mistaken identity. The underlying tension around Fey social law (trophy rights) remains.
- **Prince Thornacious:** Has some prior knowledge of or connection to the Prince — enough to expect him and advocate on his behalf.
- **The Fomorian (Maergun):** Chan's designated quarry; the nature of their prior history (if any) is unknown.
