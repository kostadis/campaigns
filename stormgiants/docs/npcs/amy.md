# Amy

## Identity
- **Role:** Lead actor in Kaelen's play at the Talking Troll theater, Triboar
- **Faction:** None known
- **First Appearance:** The Talking Troll theater; Thistle observed her during rehearsals, and Orsik approached her in disguise as acting coach "Geoff Mamet"

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goals:** Status and social approval; she is fundamentally concerned with how others perceive her
- **Drives:** Craves validation and defers heavily to authority figures and perceived experts
- Amy presents as cheerful and lighthearted but avoids all definitive statements or commitments, always deflecting with vague uncertainty — behavior Thistle read as concealing a guarded or mysterious past. She has creative opinions about her lead role but lacks the spine to advocate for them against pushback. Under pressure from an authority figure, she folds immediately and agrees to comply rather than argue.

## History with the Party
- **Rehearsal Period:** Thistle observed Amy's behavior and body language, reading her as status-driven and evasive beneath a bubbly exterior. Amy had ongoing creative disagreements with director Kaelen about how to play the lead role, but was too timid to hold her ground.
- **Coaching Session (Orsik as "Geoff Mamet"):** Orsik approached her in the disguise of an acting coach. Amy questioned whether Kaelen had approved the hire and whether it was in the budget, then deflated under Orsik's commanding tone. She complained about confusing lines and the over-the-top gestures required at the play's ending. Orsik ordered her to deliver the lines with energy and stop trying to rewrite or redirect the material. The attempt to build her confidence by shouting at her to grow up failed; she simply sighed and agreed to do whatever Kaelen wanted.
- **Play's Success:** Amy performed well enough under the coaching that the play succeeded.
- **Post-Show:** When Orsik reappeared out of disguise, Amy warmly thanked him — believing he had *paid* for Geoff Mamet's coaching — and credited the coach with making all the difference. She offered to show Orsik the improved performance.

## Current Status
- **Last Known Location:** The Talking Troll theater, Triboar
- **Current Activity:** The play has concluded successfully; no further plans are known
- **What the Party Knows:** Amy is a capable actor when pushed; she is deferential, conflict-averse, and status-conscious; she has unresolved creative frustrations with Kaelen
- **What Remains Hidden:** The nature of her guarded or mysterious past; what specifically she wanted to do differently with the lead role

## Relationships
- **Kaelen (Director):** A source of creative friction. Kaelen has pushed back on Amy's ideas about the role; Amy has been too timid to push back in return.
- **Orsik:** Amy believes Orsik is a patron who generously funded Geoff Mamet's coaching. She is warm toward him and grateful.
- **"Geoff Mamet" (Orsik's disguise):** Amy deferred to him once his authority was established and credits him with the play's success. She does not know Geoff and Orsik are the same person.
- **Thistle:** Observed by Thistle, who successfully read her personality and underlying motivations.
