# Blagothkus

## Identity
- **Role:** Cloud Giant lord; owner and operator of the flying castle at Parnast
- **Faction:** Former ally of the Cult of the Dragon / Tiamat's forces; later conscripted ally of the 3HPP
- **First Appearance:** Discussed before first appearing in person; the party first encountered him locked inside the castle's control room at Parnast, communicating through a mirror periscope to verify visitors were not vampires

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core drives:** Restoration of giant prestige and his own standing with the gods; driven by ambition that consistently outran his judgment and led to catastrophic personal loss
- Emotionally volatile and prone to deflection — his first instinct in any confrontation was to declare the situation was not his fault
- Capable of warmth and dry humor, particularly with Escarlotta when relations were repaired, and in his genuine gratitude when the party cleared skeletal remains from his courtyard
- Fundamentally reactive rather than decisive; he required external pressure — from Escarlotta, Orsik, or circumstance — to act

## History with the Party

**Before first contact:** The cloud castle passed the party months earlier, nearly killing them. The ghost of Eigeron asked the party to kill Blagothkus. Unla Kee flagged both the vengeance angle and the value of the castle itself.

**Parnast — first meeting:** Found barricaded in the control room, emotionally shattered, having lost control of most of the castle to Sandesyl Morgia. He clarified that his obstacle was not the dracolich Glazhael but his wife. He revealed that Harshnag's "unopened for a thousand years" oracle claim is a running joke among giants.

**Control room confrontation with Escarlotta:** Escarlotta's avatar emerged and accused Blagothkus of engineering the Tiamat alliance as a cynical ploy — helping dragons fail so giants could rule the weakened survivors — and of killing Eigeron at the Oracle rather than allowing Eigeron to kill him. He offered little defense. Escarlotta conscripted him into helping the party defeat Sandesyl.

**Castle operations and Sandesyl campaign:** Participated reluctantly but increasingly as an ally. Dropped boulders on zombies, correctly deduced the zombies were a trap to hold the party for Glazhael, and explained Sandesyl's likely retreat to her ice cave. Compressed himself into the tunnels despite objections. Fought animated statues. Provided the backstory of Brunvild's imprisonment and Sandesyl's infiltration of the castle. In the final assault on Sandesyl's lair, he charged after Orsik's praise moved him to tears, took heavy damage from a fireball and the skeletal dragon, demanded healing under threat from Orsik, and ultimately helped finish off the last of the Cult of the Dragon disciples.

**Relationship repair with Escarlotta:** After a long private conversation, Blagothkus and Escarlotta reconciled. He warned the party that allowing ends to justify means was precisely what had destroyed his own life.

**Death:** In the control room, Escarlotta learned from Brunvild the full truth of how Blagothkus had reacquired the castle — through deals with dragons and Sandesyl. Thistle revealed that Eigeron was trapped as a ghost and implied Blagothkus bore responsibility. He could only whimper that it was "an accident." At Escarlotta's command, the party executed him: Vardis (guiding bolt), Orsik (sword), Thistle (critical arteries), Unla Kee (lightning), Brunvild (Blagothkus's own hammer). **Blagothkus is dead.**

## Current Status
- **Dead.** Killed in the castle control room by the 3HPP at Escarlotta's direction
- His death was the stated condition for releasing Eigeron's ghost
- The castle remains; Escarlotta controls its systems
- Brunvild holds Blagothkus directly responsible for his imprisonment and the deaths of his men and the dwarves — a grievance he voiced before the killing blow

## Relationships
- **Escarlotta (wife):** Spectral entity bound to the castle's control console. Their relationship cycled from open conflict to fragile reconciliation to her ordering his execution. She controlled him more effectively than anyone else
- **Eigeron (son, deceased/ghost):** Killed by Blagothkus at the Eye of the All-Father, circumstances disputed — Escarlotta frames it as deliberate murder; Blagothkus called it an accident. Eigeron's ghost asked the party to avenge him
- **Sandesyl Morgia:** His castellan, who he had made a prior deal with to be located on the Mkoz Glacier. She subsequently infiltrated the castle, massacred his retinue, and effectively imprisoned him in the control room
- **Brunvild:** The frost giant jarl whose men died partly due to Blagothkus's deals. Brunvild delivered the damning account to Escarlotta and struck the killing blow
- **Harshnag:** Known to Blagothkus; the giant's "oracle worthiness test" routine is common knowledge in their social circle
- **Orsik:** The party member who most consistently pushed Blagothkus to act — through praise, threats, and sheer stubbornness. Blagothkus responded to both
- **Thistle:** Delivered the revelation about Eigeron's ghost that sealed Blagothkus's fate. Blagothkus called Thistle's dragon eyes "so cool," which was not appreciated
- **Vardis / Vaardis:** Defended Blagothkus against Escarlotta's insults during early negotiations; Blagothkus found brief solidarity with him
- **The 3HPP broadly:** Referenced post-death as having inadvertently disrupted Iymrith's plans by killing Blagothkus, who had himself disrupted them first by killing Eigeron

## Arc Score Events

- **Decrease:** Allied with Tiamat's cult and Sandesyl as part of a cynical scheme against "small peoples"
- **Increase (partial):** Reconciled with Escarlotta; warned the party against his own mistakes
- **Increase (partial):** Fought alongside the party against Sandesyl and the dracolich
