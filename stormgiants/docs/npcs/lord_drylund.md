# Lord Drylund

## Identity
- **Role:** Presumptive Waterbaron of Yartar; secret member of the Kraken Society
- **Faction:** Kraken Society (covert); Yartar electoral politics (public)
- **First Appearance:** Identified remotely via a gambling chit by Unla Kee when the party examined a token found on Queen Neri's assassin. Later encountered in person aboard the Grand Dame.

## Personality & Motivations
- **Core Goal:** To become the elected Waterbaron of Yartar, by any means necessary — including kidnapping the children of voters to coerce their support.
- Drylund was visibly anxious under pressure, prone to deflection and feigned ignorance, but cracked quickly when cornered. He was self-serving and cowardly, ultimately prioritizing his own survival over loyalty to the Kraken Society.

## History with the Party
1. **Pre-Contact (Session 1 reference):** A gambling chit from Drylund's vessel, the Golden Goose, was found on a small folk assassin connected to Queen Neri's death. Unla Kee identified it, flagging Drylund as a lead. Unla Kee was funded by Queen Serissa to investigate.
2. **Aboard the Grand Dame:** Orsik confronted Drylund with the coin. He feigned ignorance, claiming it was worth ten gold aboard the ship, but his anxiety was read clearly by Unla Kee and Thistle. He inadvertently confirmed the coin's significance by asking why they were asking about it.
3. **Central Market, the following morning:** Drylund introduced the party to local merchants (Tapouach, Tapuz, Mitbach). When Orsik revealed knowledge of Oosith and the sewers, Drylund was alarmed but continued to cooperate. He openly described his electoral strategy — kidnapping voters' children — and referred to Oosith as his "campaign manager in charge of special persuasion operations." He led the party into the sewers despite a Kuo-Toan guard's objections, citing pressure from the 3HPP.
4. **In the Sewers:** When the Kuo-Toans attempted to lock the party in Oosith's office, Drylund panicked and pleaded with Orsik for protection. Under extreme duress, he revealed the Kraken Society's most critical secret: **King Hekaton is held prisoner aboard a ship called the Morkoth**, an undetectable vessel sailing the northern waters of the Trackless Sea. He suggested the party use their flying castle to locate it visually.
5. **Death:** Immediately after disclosing this information, Drylund died in Orsik's hands — eyes bulging, blood from his nose — apparently killed remotely by Oosith as punishment for the betrayal. A mysterious voice remarked that finding a new Waterbaron would now be difficult.

## Current Status
- **Deceased.** Killed remotely, likely by Oosith, moments after revealing King Hekaton's location to the party.
- His death is being analyzed by Unla Kee, who theorizes it may indicate a Kraken Society hive mind — explaining both the kill trigger and how word of the party's cloud castle spread so rapidly through the organization.

## Relationships
- **Oosith:** Drylund's handler within the Kraken Society, described publicly as his "campaign manager." Oosith executed him remotely upon detecting his betrayal.
- **Queen Neri's assassin:** Linked to Drylund through the Golden Goose chit found on the assassin's body.
- **Queen Serissa:** Indirectly — her funding of the investigation led the party to Drylund.
- **Orsik:** Primary interrogator. Drylund died in his hands after breaking under Orsik's pressure.
- **Unla Kee & Thistle:** Both read through Drylund's deceptions aboard the Grand Dame.
- **3HPP:** Referenced as a source of pressure that compelled Drylund to cooperate with the party's sewer visit despite Kuo-Toan objections. Nature of this relationship not elaborated.
