
The Feywild is a land of unbridled nature. Lands teeming with vibrant viridescent vitality, inhabited by magical beings, and ruled by the Seelie Court. Titania, the Summer Queen, rules fairly, justly, and with a gleaming magical twinkle in her eye.

Scattered through the wilds are the towns and cities of the Faerie, and in the west the land of Autumn once held a small village, the village of Auburn. It was not a special town, but the Faerie who lived there celebrated the turns of the seasons, the festivals of the moon, and the passing of the Winter’s star.

In Auburn, 60 years ago was born an eccentric and irascible faerie by the name of Jun. He did not follow the ways of his town, and after tumultuous teenage years, he went off to find his way in the wide world. 20 years later, he returned, telling tales of his adventures throughout the lands of Faerun, having decided that the lands of the Faerie are better suited to settle down.

Ten days past the next Winter Solstice, in a rare convergence of the Wandering Star and the Constellation of the Dragon of Dawn, was born a young faerie boy named Thistle. This convergence forewarned a fate of tragedy, far-striding, and a curious association with dragons. He was born as nephew to Jun, and during his childhood he heard all the grand and fanciful tales of his favorite Uncle’s travels. [[  Advantage on Nature checks (about cartography) from his Uncle’s tales. ]]

Laela was a young faerie lass of the village. Thistle and Laela grew up together blooming as young friends, and as they grew a special bond formed between them. Those who saw them together knew they would marry once they celebrated their citizen’s ritual, in their 20th springtime.

Each year after the winter’s chill abated, the Springtime festival was celebrated with dancing, music, much merry making, the citizen’s ritual, and dedication to Queen Titania. The festival is marked by the blooming of Spring’s First Flower, which is plucked by a lucky villager and brought with much pomp and circumstance to the village. The flower is imbued with the power of springtime, and is hung in the village square to bring the blessing of Titania to the harvest of the year.

That year, Thistle and Laela went out into the wilds to search for the Spring’s First Flower, and on finding it, returned to the village to celebrate. But they returned not to the sounds of celebration, but to the sounds of screaming and flames. A band of black robed humans were in the village, burning homes and slaying any faerie they could see. They were led by a foul looking man named Adimond Kroskas.

The Cult of the Dragon invaded the Feywild, to steal the Spring’s First Flower as part of their treasure horde for the ritual of Tiamat, and as a side thought slayed or captured many of the villagers to sell into slavery. For the next year, Thistle led his life as a captive pet of Adimond Kroskas, together with other captives.

While imprisoned he befriended a copper (acid) drake named Thuul, who was planned to be a blood sacrifice as part of the ritual. But as Valindra Shadowmantle was defeated, the cult’s cell scattered to the winds. Adimond escaped with Laela in a cage on the back of a cart before Thistle could unjam the locks and free himself and Thuul.

Thistle now wanders the landscape searching for traces of the Cult of the Dragon, vowing to catch up to Adimond and rescue Laela. Thuul parted ways to return to his kin in the Greypeak range, but offered to return to help Thistle with his search at first opportunity.
