
The Career Soldier

He was originally from Waterdeep where he met Orsik and became a very good friend. 

Vardis is established as a professional mercenary or soldier of fortune. Along with Orsik, he spent years operating within the established military structures of the North. He has served in:

Garrisons and City Guards: Providing local security in various settlements.

Faction Armies: Taking contracts for the major powers like the Lord’s Alliance or the Zhentarim.

The War of the Cult: He likely saw active duty during the rise of Tiamat, as his current unemployment is a direct consequence of the peace that followed that conflict.

A Master of the "Black Road"
The text highlights Vardis’ familiarity with the geography of the Greypeak Mountains and the Delimbiyr Valley. He has clearly spent years patrolling or guarding caravans between towns like Secomber, Loudwater, and Llorkh. This suggests he isn't just a fighter, but someone with survival skills and regional knowledge of the harsh frontiers.

The Post-War Slump
Vardis' recent history is defined by a lack of opportunity. The document notes that he has essentially "run out" of reputable employers. As the world entered a period of rebuilding, his specific skillset—likely high-intensity combat—became less valuable to local lords, leading to:

Financial Desperation: He is down on his luck and searching for "Plan B" treasure hoards left behind by Cultists.

A Shift to Adventuring: He has been forced out of the structured life of a soldier and into the unpredictable life of a treasure hunter.

Relationship with Orsik
The content suggests a long-term partnership built on mutual survival. They aren't just allies of convenience; they are a duo that has exhausted every other option together. Vardis appears to be the more pragmatic or perhaps cynical of the two, willing to follow rumors of "magic-trap-filled caves" because the alternative is starvation in a peaceful world.

The Disillusioned Disciple
Vardis belongs to a tradition that appears more pragmatic than mystical. The text mentions that after the war with the Cult of the Dragon, many institutions—including religious ones—faced internal divisions. Vardis' backstory suggests he is a man who saw the "ugly heads" of factionalism within the various orders (like the Order of the Gauntlet). His current status as a "down-on-his-luck soldier" implies that his clerical order may have either fractured or stopped providing him with the support and purpose it once did.
