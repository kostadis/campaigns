## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul is the primary antagonist commanding the attacking horde outside Parnast. He arrives riding a mammoth, wearing bone-festooned plate armor and a helm made from the skull of an unidentifiable beast. When he removes the helm, Thistle recognizes him as a drow — an unexpected revelation given his giant-like appearance. His eyes gleam with intelligence, marking him as far more cunning than a typical hill giant.

Bad Fruul issues demands to the town before the battle begins, calling out by name for Chandra (identified as hailing from Evereska and as the offspring of someone named Luistarra), the statue of Agharradh, and threatening destruction and enslavement if his demands are not met. His demands suggest he has specific intelligence about Parnast's defenders and their assets.

During the battle, Bad Fruul demonstrates tactical thinking. He first sends a test wave of eight goblins, two wolves, and goblin leaders to probe Parnast's defenses, explicitly treating them as expendable — "they served their purpose." When the goblins are slaughtered by pits and militia archers, he reacts with surprise and anger, shouting that his spies had told him the militia's training had gone badly, indicating he had informants inside or near Parnast who misled him. He then adapts, sending ogres, half-ogres from Mama Mia, and cave bears as a second wave. When his ogres breach the wall, he orders additional ogres sent in alongside an owlbear.

Bad Fruul's internal monologue reveals he is aware others underestimate his intelligence and is motivated by a desire to rule. His last known position is commanding his horde from outside Parnast at the edge of the forest, actively directing the assault.

## Chandra

Chandra is present in Parnast and is one of Bad Fruul's named targets — he demands she be handed over along with the statue of Agharradh. She is identified as hailing from Evereska and as the offspring of someone named Luistarra, whom Bad Fruul calls "horrid." The specific demand for her person suggests she has significance beyond being a simple villager.

Earlier in the session, Chandra explains to Thistle why a Lionel Coster transdimensional market is unavailable in Parnast, noting that the local trade network was established by the Zhentarim and relies on physical transportation of goods. She references the local merchant Calder in this context. When Orsik and Thistle suggest using the wyvern as a weapon, Chandra corrects their assumption that she can control it, making clear she cannot.

Chandra's last known location is Parnast, where she is present as the battle begins.

## Jarlor

Jarlor is a resident of Parnast who is initially reluctant to participate in sending a covert message to Orc chieftain Kurglow through the spy channel. He is persuaded to act only after his wife challenges him to protect his family. He listens to Orsik's proposed message — a comically non-threatening warning about denying the orc whelp dessert and a bedtime story — and internally questions whether to correct the dwarf but defers to him as the authority figure. His last known location is Parnast.

## Larrish

Larrish is mentioned alongside Jarlor as one of the two individuals Thistle suggests using to send a message to Kurglow through the spy channel. No further details about Larrish are provided beyond this reference. Physical presence in the scene is implied but not confirmed independently.

## Kurglow

Kurglow does not appear directly in this session but is meaningfully discussed. He is identified as the orc chieftain and the father of the orc whelp captured by the party. The orc woman who guarded the whelps — described as thankful her child was not killed — informs the party that Kurglow would be willing to abandon Bad Fruul's alliance in order to save his child. She characterizes him as "a terrible husband, but a good father." The party attempts to exploit this by sending a message through the spy channel via Jarlor and Larrish, with Orsik crafting an absurd threat about withholding dessert and bedtime stories from the whelp. Kurglow's current status and response to the message are not yet known.

## Mama Mia

Mama Mia does not appear directly but is referenced during the battle. Bad Fruul references "Mama Mia's ogres" when her half-ogres and ogres are sent as part of the second assault wave against Parnast's walls. The half-ogres attributed to her are killed by Thistle's trained archers, while her ogres succeed in breaching the wall. This indicates Mama Mia is a named subordinate commander or supplier of troops within Bad Fruul's horde.

## Calder

Calder does not appear directly but is referenced by Chandra in her explanation to Thistle about the absence of a Lionel Coster market in Parnast. Calder is described as Parnast's local merchant, who has been forced to rely on Zhentarim physical transportation of goods rather than transdimensional trade. Thistle notes this means Calder has no magic items to sell. Calder's current whereabouts are within Parnast.

## Ragnar

Ragnar is a Parnast resident present during Bad Fruul's pre-battle demands. When Bad Fruul calls for surrender, Ragnar shouts defiance alongside Gundalin — "NEVER! The Orsik's Militia will never surrender one of our own! Go back before you die!" — helping to steel the town's resolve and trigger the battle. His last known location is Parnast's defensive lines.

## Gundalin

Gundalin is a Parnast resident present during Bad Fruul's pre-battle demands. He shouts defiance alongside Ragnar in response to Bad Fruul's ultimatum, declaring that the militia will never surrender one of their own. His last known location is Parnast's defensive lines.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session but is referenced in Thistle's internal thoughts. Thistle recalls that Uncle Joon had explained to him that the Lionel Coster operates according to KP Trade Equilibrium Theory, which considers markets across all possible universes and produces a more optimal general equilibrium than local physical markets. Uncle Joon's explanation touched on the Smalistin conjecture and noted that Lionel Coster merchants have faced violent resistance from local merchants who prefer the profits of a more localized market equilibrium. Uncle Joon's current whereabouts and relationship to Thistle beyond being his uncle are not elaborated upon in this chunk.
