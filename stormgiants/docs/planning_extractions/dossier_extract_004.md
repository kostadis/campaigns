## Agra

Agra is a kobold with red scales scarred with burn marks, whose clothes appear noticeably filthy even by kobold standards. He is first encountered hiding inside the small stone building at the center of the abandoned dwarven outpost, alongside his companion Gen. He remained hidden during the initial confrontation between Nug and the adventuring party, counseling Gen to stay quiet and wait, hoping to use the trap to their advantage. When the trap failed and the adventurers defeated the other kobolds, Agra made the decision to surrender, stepping outside waving something white.

During the surrender, Thistle confronted Agra about his past connection to the Cult of the Dragon. Agra recognized the marks of Cult torture on Thistle and appeared genuinely disturbed, admitting he had served under orders and insisting he "didn't mean any of it." He claimed not to know the whereabouts of Thistle's girlfriend, but disclosed that after the Red Dragon their group served departed, the kobolds' boss made a deal with a cloud giant, at which point Agra and his companions fled.

Agra provided significant intelligence to the group: kobold tribes have been reduced to working for giants following the defeat of the dragons; his group was hired by a fire giant; other kobold groups were hired by hill and cloud giants; there appears to be unusual competition and tension brewing between the giant types, which he described as odd because they do not normally behave that way. He speculated the giants are competing for something, though he did not know what. He noted that when the cloud giant's castle arrived, his group ran. The fire giants sent his group to this outpost to find cultist treasure, claiming the space was too small for them, though a kobold named Gun had heard the place was cursed.

Agra relayed Nug's report of a treasure in the southern room's corner, a glowing magical fire symbol, and a river of lava, and confirmed that when kobolds entered the southern room, creatures emerged, attacked, and exploded upon death.

He attempted to negotiate with the party, offering to serve as "employees" rather than slaves, citing fluid loyalties. When the group declined due to lack of funds, he offered information instead. Gen suggested offering treasure, which the group accepted. After the treasure was handed over and the dwarf Orsik gave permission to leave, Agra and Gen were allowed to depart. Before leaving, when Vardis raised concern about Agra informing the fire giants of the party's presence, Agra explained that the fire giants expected them to return with treasure or not at all, and had made clear that failure would mean termination — implying the kobolds had no intention of returning to the fire giants. He punctuated this by picking up dirt, squeezing it in his hand, and swallowing it.

Last known status: departed the abandoned dwarven outpost alive, along with Gen, after being released by the party.

## Gen

Gen is a kobold companion to Agra, also hiding in the stone building during the party's encounter at the abandoned dwarven outpost. His red scales are scarred with burn marks and his clothes are described as stinky even by kobold standards. During the tense standoff, Agra physically restrained Gen — clamping a hand over his mouth — to prevent him from doing something that might have provoked the adventurers. Gen deferred to Agra's judgment and remained quiet.

When the situation became dire after the trap failed, Gen turned to Agra and asked, "what will we ever do?" During the subsequent negotiation, Gen suggested that offering some treasure might help secure the party's goodwill, a suggestion that proved successful when the group accepted the treasure and allowed the two kobolds to leave.

Last known status: departed the abandoned dwarven outpost alive alongside Agra after the party released them.

## Nug

Nug does not appear directly in the events as a survivor but is referenced during the encounter. Nug was the kobold leader who initiated the confrontation with the adventuring party, shouting "begone foul creatures!" Agra privately characterized him as "that damned fool" for choosing to fight heavily armed adventurers. Nug argued with Thistle over whether the fairy was or was not a filthy creature, which Agra privately hoped would lure the party into the trap.

Nug also reported to the group that there was a treasure in the corner of the southern room, a glowing magical fire symbol, and a river of lava — information Agra later relayed to the party during surrender negotiations.

Last known status: deceased. Nug is among the kobolds referred to by Agra as "dead buffoons" after the party defeated the kobolds at the barricade, apparently killed during the failed ambush.

## Old Keller

Old Keller does not appear in this session. He is referenced by Orsik during an emotional outburst in the southern room of the abandoned dwarven outpost. Orsik mentions that Old Keller "didn't even want to talk to me," which Orsik describes as deeply affecting him, using it as an example of being looked down upon and treated as unworthy. The reference is made in the context of Orsik's frustration with his exclusion from Clan Licehair. No further details about Old Keller's identity or relationship to Orsik are provided.
