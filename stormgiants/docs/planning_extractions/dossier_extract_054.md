## Harry

Harry is a male giant elk encountered at Flint Rock on the 5th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis. He and his mate Mary were grazing in the area when Thistle approached them using speak with animals. Harry is vocally atheistic, dismissing the Feywild as "a tool of the oppressor to keep the Giant Elk subservient to a doomed religion" and expressing contempt for any suggestion that giant elks worship fae gods with "funny horns." He is irritable and opinionated, complaining about neighboring giant elks called the Joneses who he accuses of strutting around acting superior, and grumbling about bipedal creatures stealing their grass. Despite his gruff demeanor, Harry and Mary ultimately cooperated with the party — after Thistle persuaded them the group was allowed to be there — and physically pushed the stone out of the way to assist with the excavation. His last known action was expressing relief at the party leaving so he and Mary could have peace and quiet.

## Mary

Mary is a female giant elk encountered at Flint Rock alongside her mate Harry on the 5th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis. She is more measured and socially gracious than Harry, frequently trying to moderate his outbursts and apologizing to Thistle for her husband's behavior. She is the one who noticed the party's digging activity and called out to Thistle to ask whether they were permitted to be there. After Thistle persuaded her, she and Harry cooperated by pushing the large stone aside to assist the excavation. She appears to be the more practical and diplomatically inclined of the two, though she shares Harry's desire to be left in peace. Her last known action was helping move the stone at Flint Rock.

## Thistle

Thistle is a member of the 3HPP and is identified as fae from the Feywild. At Flint Rock on the 5th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis, he cast speak with animals and flew within range to question the giant elks Harry and Mary about any dangers in the area, learning there were none. He refused to assist with the digging at both Flint Rock and Morgur's Mound, citing pride as his reason when pressed by Vardis. During the thunderbeast skeleton ambush on the 10th day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis, he flew toward the combat and struck one skeleton with two arrows, identified the creatures as ankylosauruses, and subsequently impaled another skeleton with two more arrows. He was criticized by his companions for his ineffective scouting that failed to detect the ambush. His last known location is aboard Delsephine's ship, the Dragon's Breath.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a halfling member of the 3HPP. He provided the explanation at Flint Rock for why Vardis's detect magic spell was able to penetrate the stone — the weather-worn surface had reduced the stone to just under one foot, making the magic detectable. He was noted as too weak to assist with digging at Morgur's Mound. During the skeleton ambush, he was bitten by one of the creatures. He possesses an artifact that can produce effects similar to a wand of wonder; he used it to generate a gust of wind, which he cleverly redirected to push a skeleton into the pit Orsik and Vardis had dug, then used the sustained wind to prevent the creature from climbing out. He later cast a fireball into the pit to destroy the trapped skeleton. He was given the abjurer's bangle, a damage-absorbing magic item found during excavation. His last known location is aboard the Dragon's Breath.

## Vardis

Vardis is a member of the 3HPP. At Flint Rock, he cast detect magic to confirm the location of the buried object and briefly feigned walking away from the quest when Orsik challenged the mechanics of his spell, before Unla Kee resolved the dispute. He marched with the group and participated in smashing the rock. At Morgur's Mound over multiple days of excavation, he dug alongside Orsik and used detect magic to identify possible locations of the artifact. He became increasingly frustrated with Thistle and Unla Kee's refusal to dig, pointedly asking Thistle to at least use his eyes to help. During the skeleton ambush, he cast a warding flair to distract one creature, then destroyed two of the skeletons with a fireball. He gave Delsephine a "stink eye" when she lamented finding no artifact. After the battle, he received Delsephine's update on remaining locations and directed the ship to Raven's Rock. His last known location is aboard the Dragon's Breath.

## Bressik

Bressik is a member of the group traveling with the 3HPP who provides research and logistical information. He pulled out a map of Morgur's Mound that he obtained during time at Candlekeep and delivered a detailed briefing on the mound's history: it is the spirit mound of the lost Thunderbeast tribe, named after Uthgar's brother, shaped like a long-necked wingless dragon with a ridge of dragon bones, surrounded by cairns a quarter mile out containing the bones of tribal warriors. He noted the tribe has not visited in years and that the site has been heavily plundered by treasure hunters. He offered a self-deprecating confession about his own past — having been a member of a cult planning to end the world by bringing an evil goddess into the world and serving an evil red dragon to avoid punishment — as context for why he could not judge the party's grave-robbing activities. His last known location is with the group traveling aboard the Dragon's Breath.

## Delsephine

Delsephine is the captain of the airship Dragon's Breath and is identified as having past involvement as a cult leader. She transported the 3HPP from Flint Rock to Morgur's Mound and remained aboard the ship during the excavation. She sighed when days of digging yielded only miscellaneous magic items rather than the sought artifact. After the battle with the thunderbeast skeletons, she offered to take Orsik somewhere to clean up, which he refused, and she instructed her crew to give the grumpy dwarf space. She then briefed Vardis that the only remaining locations to search are Raven's Rock and the Great Wyrm Cavern, after which Vardis directed her to Raven's Rock. Her current status is captain of the Dragon's Breath, en route to Raven's Rock.
