## Lord Drylund

Lord Drylund is the presumptive Waterbaron of Yartar and a secret member of the Kraken Society. He appeared first aboard the Grand Dame, where Orsik interrogated him using the golden goose coin found on Queen Neri's assassin. Drylund initially feigned ignorance, claiming the coin was merely worth ten gold on the ship, but his anxiety was visible to Unla Kee and Thistle, who recognized he was lying and deflecting. Under Orsik's pressure, he inadvertently confirmed the coin's significance by asking why they were inquiring about it.

Drylund met the party again the following morning at the central market, where he introduced them to local merchants including Tapouach, Tapuz, and Mitbach. When Orsik revealed knowledge of Oosith and the sewers, Drylund was alarmed at the security breach but continued to cooperate, referring to Oosith as his "campaign manager in charge of special persuasion operations." He openly described his electoral strategy: kidnapping the children of voters to coerce their support. He led the party into the sewers to meet Oosith despite the Kuo-Toan guard's objections, citing pressure from the 3HPP.

When the Kuo-Toans attempted to lock the party in Oosith's office, Drylund pleaded with Orsik for protection. Under duress and in terror for his life, he revealed the Kraken Society's most critical secret: King Hekaton is being held prisoner aboard a ship called the Morkoth, a vessel that cannot be detected, which sails around the northern islands of the Trackless Sea. He suggested the party use their flying castle to locate it visually.

Immediately after delivering this information, Drylund died in Orsik's hands — his eyes bulging and rolling back, blood flowing from his nose — apparently killed remotely by Oosith. The mysterious voice subsequently noted that finding a new Waterbaron would be difficult.

## Queen Neri

Queen Neri does not appear in this session but is referenced in the context of the golden goose coin. The coin was found on the body of one of her assassins. Her murder is described as the event that began the unraveling of the Ordning and led to the disappearance of King Hekaton. Orsik explains this backstory to Lord Drylund during the interrogation aboard the Grand Dame.

## Queen Sansuri

Queen Sansuri does not appear in this session but is referenced as the giant who gave the golden goose coin to the 3HPP during events at the Maelstrom. The coin had been recovered from the body of one of Queen Neri's assassins and was passed to the party as evidence of the conspiracy.

## King Hekaton

King Hekaton does not appear in this session but is the central subject of the party's investigation. Both Lord Drylund and Lady Atalia are observed deflecting conversation away from him with visible anxiety, as though afraid to discuss his disappearance. Drylund ultimately reveals under duress that Hekaton has been held prisoner by the Kraken Society aboard a specially undetectable ship called the Morkoth, which sails the northern islands of the Trackless Sea. This is the first direct confirmation of Hekaton's location.

## Lady Atalia

Lady Atalia appeared waiting for the party at the Yartar Docks in the early dawn hours as they disembarked from the Grand Dame. She moved discreetly but allowed herself to be followed, leading the party to a tavern where she sat with them. She demonstrated knowledge of Orsik's fake identity as his father and correctly read his intelligence, which infuriated him.

When Thistle redirected the conversation to the connection between the Grand Dame and King Hekaton, Lady Atalia was visibly surprised, having assumed the party was investigating the kidnapping of a young lord named Lord Artan. Like Drylund, she displayed anxiety when the subject of Hekaton arose and attempted to deflect the conversation away from him.

She revealed that a spellcaster named Oosith has been running the lordling kidnapping operation and that she had been blackmailed by Lord Drylund into using her social skills to assist them. She described Oosith as hiding in the sewers to avoid the Hand or the Shield of Yartar, protected by Fish People (Kuo-Toans). She offered to reveal Oosith's exact location in exchange for the party killing him, claiming she wished to return to a moral life. She appealed particularly to Vardis, reaching out to him directly.

The party walked away without agreeing to help her, leaving her staring after them.

## Lord Artan

Lord Artan does not appear in this session but is referenced by Lady Atalia as a young lord of Yartar who was kidnapped. Lady Atalia initially assumed the party had come to Yartar to find his kidnappers. This reference confirms that the lordling kidnapping operation attributed to Oosith and the Kraken Society has claimed at least this named victim.

## Oosith

Oosith does not appear physically in this session but is a significant referenced and partially encountered figure. Lady Atalia describes him as an evil spellcaster hiding in the sewers of Yartar, protected by Kuo-Toans, to avoid capture by the Hand or the Shield of Yartar. Lord Drylund refers to him as his "campaign manager in charge of special persuasion operations," the architect of the child-kidnapping voter coercion scheme.

A telepathic voice — strongly implied to be Oosith — communicates with the party as they enter the sewer chamber, speaking cordially and inviting them to his office, mentioning alcohol to lure Unla Kee. This voice demonstrated awareness of the party members' names and characteristics. When the Kuo-Toans attempted to trap the party in the office, the voice revealed its hostility: it noted that Drylund's death was "annoying" and declared it would have to kill the party. It then attempted to charm Orsik and failed. Oosith's office contained treasure, a pendant bearing a capital R wrapped in robes associated with nobles, and a device that caused golden goose coins to glow with identifying numbers.

## Tapouach

Tapouach is a market merchant in Yartar who sells apples, introduced by Lord Drylund during the morning market meeting. Unla Kee pickpocketed his purse, finding only 23 coppers, and then stole an apple. Tapouach noticed the theft but, recognizing Unla Kee as a companion of Lord Drylund, smiled and said nothing.

## Tapuz

Tapuz is a market merchant in Yartar introduced by Lord Drylund during the morning market meeting. Drylund praised Tapuz's oranges for their sweetness. No further interaction is recorded.

## Mitbach

Mitbach is a market merchant in Yartar introduced by Lord Drylund during the morning market meeting, described as a place where people buy kitchen goods. No further interaction is recorded.
