## Darz Helgar

Darz Helgar is an ex-convict who appears at the start of the session, compelled by Orsik's stare to reveal information. He was formerly a member of the Coffin Nail thieves guild in Waterdeep, a ruthless and highly organized criminal organization, until he was caught ten years ago and lost contact with the guild. He is uncertain whether the Coffin Nail still exists, noting that guilds rarely survive long given the number of enemies they accumulate. When Orsik asks where to find traces of the guild, Darz suggests going to Skullport. During the conversation, Darz makes a hand signal identifying himself as a member of the Zhentarim, which Orsik reciprocates. He goes on to reveal that the Black Network in Waterdeep is split into two factions, one of which is led by Manshoon — whom he claims is alive and well, despite stories that all of Manshoon's clones were killed. Darz was the individual blackmailed by Kaelen Sarssir into stealing from the Lionshield Coster, and he cooperated with the party because he wants revenge on Kaelen. He expressed indifference to being reported, suggesting he had little left to lose. His last known whereabouts are at the location of the initial interrogation by Orsik.

## Kaelen Sarssir

Kaelen Sarssir is the bartender and owner of the Talking Troll inn. He is revealed to be a thespian with dreams of transforming the inn into a theater that would attract wealthy patrons from Waterdeep. His ambitions were inspired by seeing the play *The Inn of Shadows* off Highroad. To fund this dream, he blackmailed Darz Helgar into stealing keys from the Lionshield Coster, intending to sell them to the right buyer for over 5,000 gold. When the party arrives, Kaelen immediately involves them in his theatrical schemes, inviting them to act out scenes from a script — one involving a rogue and a vampire — in his cellar. When Orsik directly confronts him about the Lionshield Coster key, Kaelen turns pale and denies knowledge, though his poor acting makes the lie immediately transparent. He admits his scheme and reveals his motivation: fixing the inn's roof, hiring a magic special effects crew, and staging a production to make a name for himself. Orsik negotiates an arrangement in which the party acts as investors — framed more as loan-sharking — receiving a 70/30 split of the gross in exchange for returning the key and keeping quiet about Kaelen's role in the theft. Kaelen accepts, revealing a briefly glimpsed cold and calculating side beneath his dreamy theatrical persona, which shows the party how he was capable of blackmailing a criminal. He currently holds possession of the Talking Troll and its well-stocked but neglected cellar, and surrenders both the key and a prized bottle of Champagne du le Stomp to Orsik as part of the deal. He mentions the cellar's previous owner was a man named Ha-Ha Pinkerton, who sold the inn cheaply and then disappeared.

## Ha-Ha Pinkerton

Ha-Ha Pinkerton does not appear in this session but is mentioned by Kaelen Sarssir during the cellar scene. He was the former owner of the Talking Troll, described as a short man with bright, cheery cheeks who was always smiling. He sold the inn to Kaelen cheaply, apparently out of desperation, and then disappeared afterward. Kaelen claims to have known little about him and was surprised to find how well-stocked the cellar was. His fate and the circumstances of his disappearance are unknown.

## Manshoon

Manshoon does not appear in this session but is meaningfully discussed by Darz Helgar during his interrogation. Darz reveals that the Zhentarim's Black Network in Waterdeep is split into two factions, one of which is led by Manshoon. According to Darz, Manshoon was killed by Fzoul but had cloned himself, and while stories hold that all clones were subsequently eliminated, Darz claims to believe Manshoon is alive and well. Orsik, whose intelligence was recently augmented, recalls a story about a spellcaster who created copies of himself stored around as a form of immortality and realizes this story was about cloning and likely referred to Manshoon.

## Fzoul

Fzoul does not appear in this session but is referenced briefly by Darz Helgar, who states that Fzoul killed Manshoon before Manshoon's clones came into play. No further detail is provided about Fzoul.

## Alaestra

Alaestra is one of the owners of the Lionshield Coster, referred to as Lion's Share. She appears when the party returns the stolen key and is described as looking particularly relieved at its recovery. When Orsik attempts to renegotiate their deal, she and her partner Narth do not budge, and Orsik gets the sense that whoever employs them is even more intimidating than he is. She is last seen at the Lionshield Coster, where the party uses their 15% discount to purchase healing potions, new weapons, and Thistle's boots of elvenkind.

## Narth

Narth is the other owner of the Lionshield Coster, appearing alongside Alaestra when the party returns the stolen key. He is delighted at the key's return and, together with Alaestra, holds firm against Orsik's attempt to renegotiate terms. He conveys the same implicit threat as Alaestra — that their employers are not to be trifled with. He is last seen at the Lionshield Coster.

## Urgala Meltimer

Urgala Meltimer is the proprietor of the Northshield House Inn, where the companions head at sunset. She is revealed to be curiously well-informed about what occurred in the Sheriff's office, explaining that rooms in a small town are not as private as people assume. She reacts with emotion — briefly crying — to the news, suggesting a personal connection to the affair referenced, though she stops herself and remarks that it was never going to end well. In gratitude for the peace the party has brought her, she offers them one free night's stay with breakfast. When Thistle pushes for more, she briefly reveals the formidable temperament of a seasoned adventurer, firmly shutting down his charm and making clear the offer is final. She is a former adventurer who is experienced with fast-talking individuals. She is last seen at the Northshield House Inn.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon is Thistle's uncle, present at the Talking Troll in a state of drunkenness. Kaelen Sarssir announces his arrival by declaring that Joon has already drunk through money given to him and now owes even more. Thistle responds by dumping a bucket of water on him — apparently a recurring habit, as Thistle had prepared the bucket before entering. When Joon's debt comes up again, Thistle declines to cover it and suggests that debtor's prison might do his uncle some good. Uncle Joon does not speak or act meaningfully during the session beyond his drunken presence. He is last seen wet and in debt at the Talking Troll.
