## Bob

Bob was a demon who had been staging a play in Silverymoon's theater, using zombie replicas of the 3HPP as performers. He arrived to the confrontation with a zombie army and taunted the party before the battle began. Bob was defeated when Unla Kee tossed a bag of holding near him and Thuul stuffed a second bag into the first, causing an explosion that sent Bob to the Astral Plane. Bob had taken approximately 200-300 Silverymoon theatergoers and placed them in a pocket dimension; with Bob lost in the Astral Plane, there is no known way to retrieve them. Darathra pointed out that Bob can return from the Astral Plane, meaning the threat is not permanently eliminated. Bob had no known demonic actions that could be proven in a court of law, according to Lord Zymorven's careful post-battle framing. Last known status: lost in the Astral Plane.

## Thuul

Thuul was present at the battle with Bob and played a critical role in the demon's defeat. He rushed forward during the battle and stuffed the second bag of holding into the first bag that Unla Kee had tossed near Bob, triggering the extradimensional explosion. Thuul was caught in the resulting blast along with Bob and the Red Wizard, and his fate after the explosion is not addressed in this session summary.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee participated in the battle with Bob and executed the key tactical move: tossing the first bag of holding near the demon, setting up Thuul's finishing action. After the battle, he felt deflated, which Orsik initially attributed to Vardis' undead-turning overshadowing his contributions. Unla Kee corrected this, revealing he was actually reflecting on the chaos and racial violence that could have been unleashed had they blamed the attack on "ungodly races" — a cover story he briefly proposed before Orsik shut it down. He acknowledged this plan would have ignited race riots, and described it as "one hell of a way to burn this evil place to the ground," clarifying he is not evil but was drawn to the destructive potential. He also blew the conch shell when Orsik instructed him to, transporting the group to the Maelstrom. In the Maelstrom, Unla Kee suggested that the vampire might want a cult artifact — such as a tome on creating Dracoliches or an artifact to control them — as a bargaining chip. He also confirmed that the conch can only be blown once per day.

## Vardis

Vardis was present throughout the aftermath of the Bob battle and the subsequent discussions. He found the ease of Bob's defeat genuinely amusing, joking about Bob "touring the top food places of the Astral Plane." He pushed back on the cover-up plan, noting that "the absence of evidence isn't the evidence of absence of a crime" and that Orsik's headband may be malfunctioning. He tried to mediate between Orsik and Darathra during her furious outburst. He observed that Unla Kee's racist cover story was part of a "normal brainstorming process" before firmly redirecting to the chosen plan. He engaged in banter with Urgala about her past relationship with Lord Zymorven. Aboard the airship, he asked Escarlotta about "tenancy in common" regarding castle ownership and later pressed to understand how the conch worked before using it as an escape route — a moment of caution that Orsik publicly praised as his own good-idea-recognition. In the Maelstrom, Vardis correctly identified the pool as a passageway rather than a pool. His caution about the geyser prevented the party from being scalded.

## Darathra

Darathra was present at the battle and its aftermath. She was furious about the outcome, repeatedly screaming at Orsik for sending Bob to the Astral Plane rather than killing him, pointing out that Bob can return and that the 300 missing theatergoers are now in a pocket dimension with no known way to retrieve them. She also emphasized that the evidence of the crime had been removed along with Bob. After the cover story was negotiated, she declared she was never present and never saw anything, consistent with Harper operational secrecy. Orsik called this out as "Harper-like," earning a lethal glare. She marched out after Orsik insulted her battlefield contribution, delivering a final declaration: "We are done. Don't ever come looking for me," and specifically forbidding any future favors or special requests. Her last words in the session were "And no more plays," shouted from the theater door.

## Lord Zymorven

Lord Zymorven was present at the battle and its aftermath. He was momentarily outraged when Silverymoon citizens caught in the bag-of-holding explosion were killed. After the battle, he carefully and deliberately worked to shift all responsibility for the operation onto Orsik, stating that Orsik made decisions alone without consulting him and that he would have made different choices. He declared debts between their families cleared. He constructed a formal cover story: Bob used fire spells in the theater, caused the conflagration, the party fought valiantly but could not save the theatergoers, and Bob was a demon who has been defeated. He insisted everyone agree to this version. He expressed alarm at the possibility that 200-plus prominent Silverymoon citizens had disappeared, noting these were members of society. He also carefully stated that there is no evidence Bob was a demon that could hold up in a court of law, further insulating himself legally. When the conversation turned to racism and the cover story briefly touched on blaming non-human races, he quietly slipped out of the room. Urgala revealed she has a long-standing negative opinion of him as self-righteous. Last known status: departed the theater before it was set ablaze.

## Urgala

Urgala was present at the battle and its aftermath. She initially worried she bore some responsibility for the situation. She offered a key strategic suggestion: the party should flee before spells could reveal the truth, allowing Lord Zymorven's cover story to fill the vacuum and buying time to find the missing people. Orsik credited this as brilliant insight. She is Lord Zymorven's ex-partner, their relationship having started during a shared adventuring period. She expressed long-suppressed satisfaction at seeing Zymorven and Darathra — whom she described as self-righteous — knocked down several pegs, and pledged future assistance to the 3HPP. She had difficulty maintaining her composure due to her amusement and excused herself before losing her "game face." She also engaged in brief suggestive banter with Vardis and Orsik about the nature of Orsik and Vardis's friendship.

## Thistle

Thistle was present throughout the aftermath in the theater. He drew his bow and had it aimed at Unla Kee during the racist cover story proposal, ready to shoot if Unla Kee did not drop the idea. He collected rotting food and crushed beams to build a sustainable fire, demonstrating practical knowledge of arson. He asked Orsik to smash benches for fuel, triggering a brief exchange about casual racism. Escarlotta later referenced Thistle's willingness to leave the woman he loved for a higher cause as a moment that moved her and helped repair her relationship with Blagothkus. In the Maelstrom, Thistle suggested using the "right-hand rule" for navigation.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta does not appear physically but communicates through the control gems aboard the airship. She pushed back firmly when Orsik suggested he could figure out the control crystals on his own, revealing she is not merely a navigation tool but an integrated part of the castle and considers the arrangement a partnership. She expressed a desire for social connection — small talk, being asked how her day went — and described feeling treated as a subordinate rather than a partner. She revealed that she and Blagothkus had a long conversation and that she had come to understand his choices better, acknowledging she had misplaced rage onto him. She cited Thistle's sacrifice as an emotional catalyst that helped her appreciate Blagothkus's situation. She reframed her perspective, stating that the greater good — stopping the giants — matters more than her "petty hatred." She confirmed that the vampire must be destroyed but that the giant crisis takes priority. She was startled and disapproving when Orsik implied he might eventually dispense with her, but softened when he included her in team planning. She expressed that the 3HPP are critical players in the future of their corner of the world. Last known status: active flight command system aboard the airship/castle.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is referenced as having had a long conversation with Escarlotta that helped repair their relationship. He spoke one line during the airship conversation, warning that allowing ends to justify means is precisely what led to his emotional conflict with Escarlotta. He is now described as being in a good place with her — happy together and willing to share the castle. His killing of their son is acknowledged as having been the result of manipulation by the gods and Amn rather than free choice. Last known status: present aboard the airship/castle.

## The Red Wizard

The Red Wizard does not appear directly but is referenced as having been caught in the bag-of-holding explosion along with Bob and Thuul. The Red Wizard was apparently present at the theater in Bob's service. Fate after the explosion is not addressed. Drow fighters who had been present to protect the Red Wizard fled before being captured; their whereabouts are unknown.
