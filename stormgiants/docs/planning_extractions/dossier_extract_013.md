## Hsing

Hsing is a blind golden pseudo-dragon and messenger of the SEER. Hsing appears in Parnast at the Golden Tankard when Orsik arrives with the books, apparently sensing the magical power of the books. Hsing expresses surprise that the Wizard of the Crag is still alive, initially panicking that he had become a Lich before being corrected that Unla Kee is the new Wizard of the Crag. Hsing then whistles up a covered wagon with two Lord's Alliance agents to collect the books as official Lord's Alliance business.

Hsing pays the party 150 gold pieces as reward for their trouble on behalf of the SEER. When Orsik protests the sum is too low, Hsing dismisses him coldly, stating he has been making deals with adventurers for a long time and Orsik is not the type who could convince him to exceed the SEER's commanded payment. Hsing then teleports away, provoking Orsik to swing his axe at empty air.

Hsing returns approximately ten days later, appearing in the back room of the Golden Tankard alongside Azam, summoned there by Ragnar. Orsik nearly attacks him again but is restrained by Wallace. Thistle confronts Hsing directly, telling him he needs to improve his game as the quests are not worth the effort given the rewards offered. Hsing's last known whereabouts are this back room meeting in the Golden Tankard in Parnast.

## The SEER

The SEER does not appear directly in this chunk but is discussed at length and communicates through agents. The chapter opens with a note written to the SEER from an unidentified agent tasked with tracking the party. The note reveals the SEER's strategic priorities: ensuring Parnast remains part of the Lord's Alliance, preventing Parnast from falling to the Zhentarim or to Giants, and monitoring the party as the most promising group of free agents in the region. The agent also informs the SEER that Orsik has been confirmed as a Zhentarim member, noting the SEER had apparently hoped to recruit him for the Lord's Alliance.

Wallace explains to Orsik that the SEER runs the intelligence, assassination, and covert operations wing of the Lord's Alliance, doing the dirty work that keeps the Lordly public face clean. Wallace warns that making an enemy of the SEER is more dangerous than making an enemy of the Zhentarim, citing the SEER's demonstrated ability to teleport Hsing to the middle of the Marsh of Chelimber as evidence of far-reaching surveillance capability. Hsing acts as the SEER's direct messenger and enforcer of payment terms, stating explicitly that he does not pay beyond what the SEER commands.

## Azam

Azam appears in the back room of the Golden Tankard in Parnast alongside Hsing during the second meeting with the party. No further details about Azam's role, speech, or actions are provided in this chunk beyond his presence at this meeting. His relationship to the SEER or the Lord's Alliance is implied by his company but not explicitly stated here.

## Ragnar

Ragnar is the proprietor or a figure of authority at the Golden Tankard in Parnast. Ragnar intervenes when a traveler accuses Unla Kee of cheating at gambling, preventing a potential fight. The traveler leaves after Ragnar's intervention but stares at Unla Kee as if memorizing his face. Later, Ragnar gathers the party and leads them to a previously unmentioned back room of the Golden Tankard, revealing he has "important friends" — specifically Azam and Hsing. Ragnar's willingness to host Lord's Alliance agents in a private back room suggests a working relationship with the SEER's network.

## Grik

Grik is the Goblin chief commanding Bad Fruul's forces at Ambush Gap. He is positioned on a raised tile surface above the trail with his gang when the party arrives. Grik announces the pass is under Bad Fruul's control and demands the party surrender all equipment and treasure or be stripped naked and killed, invoking Bad Fruul's name as his authority. He claims Bad Fruul promised that any travelers coming up the pass would be weakened by poison released ahead of the ambush.

During the fight, Grik uses a goblin subordinate as a living shield to block Thistle's arrow and the drake's acid damage. Despite avoiding Unla Kee's binding ice, Grik is ultimately killed by Orsik on top of the tile. On his body the party finds a gold stylus and a letter from Bad Fruul ordering the defense of the pass and announcing Bad Fruul's intention to seize Parnast.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced throughout the Ambush Gap section. Bad Fruul is the regional warlord controlling territory around the Ambush Gap and surrounding badlands, commanding bugbears and goblins through a chain of intermediary chiefs. The bugbear lieutenant explains that Bad Fruul operates through layers of command — their chief meets another chief, who then meets Bad Fruul directly.

A letter found on Chief Grik's body, written by Bad Fruul, orders his forces to defend the pass with their lives and declares an intention to seize Parnast. Bad Fruul also apparently arranged for poison to be released into the pass to weaken travelers before ambushes. Bad Fruul's last known status is absent from the combat — only subordinates were present at the Ambush Gap encounter.

## Zuul Clovis

Zuul Clovis does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced by the Zhentarim agent who approaches Orsik. The agent states that Zuul Clovis told him Orsik wanted to join the Zhentarim and had the moral flexibility they look for. Zuul Clovis apparently served as Orsik's Zhentarim referral, vouching for his character to the organization.

## Oblivillish

Oblivillish does not appear directly in this chunk but is the subject of the Zhentarim mission delivered to Orsik. Oblivillish is a Zhentarim agent embedded in the court of Prince Thornacious, a Royal Fairy Prince in exile. The Zhentarim have lost contact with Oblivillish and fear either that Prince Thornacious has discovered his true purpose or that he has been captured by local goblinoids. The Zhentarim agent instructs Orsik to find Oblivillish and, if located, ensure he returns to service in Thornacious' court. Oblivillish's current whereabouts and status are unknown.

## Thornacious

Thornacious does not appear directly in this chunk but is discussed when Orsik shares the Zhentarim mission with the party. Thornacious is identified as a Royal Fairy Prince currently in exile. Oblivillish, the missing Zhentarim agent, was embedded as a spy in Thornacious' court. Thistle reflects that Thornacious' exile was forced, that Thistle's good friend Blassios the Satyr is part of his court, and that the Zhentarim spying on a royal Fey — even a disgraced one — is troubling. Thistle further reflects that Fey courts routinely spy on each other to gain favor with Titania by disgracing rivals, and that Thornacious might even view Zhentarim interest as a mark of prestige.

## Blassios

Blassios does not appear directly in this chunk but is mentioned by Thistle when the Zhentarim mission regarding Prince Thornacious is revealed. Blassios is a Satyr and a good friend of Thistle's who is part of Prince Thornacious' court. Thistle's concern about the Zhentarim spy situation is heightened by Blassios' presence in that court.

## Uncle Yorn

Uncle Yorn does not appear directly in this chunk but is mentioned by Thistle while examining the ruined Fey portal site near Ambush Gap. Thistle recalls that Uncle Yorn used to tell tales of how the North was once a playground for the Fey, with portals plentiful and used freely to travel between the Feywild and the material plane. The memory is invoked as Thistle mourns the decayed remnant of what he sees as a gentler, happier age.

## Adimond Kroskas

Adimond Kroskas does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced by Thistle upon discovering the ruined Fey portal site. Thistle identifies the portal as the kind of structure Adimond Kroskas used to move large numbers of troops into the Feywild. No further context about Kroskas' identity or current status is provided in this chunk.

## Calder

Calder is a halfling shopkeeper in Parnast running a shop unaffiliated with Lionel Coster. Unla Kee purchases a replacement sword from Calder during the two-week stay. While completing the sale, Calder complains bitterly that the Lionel Coster operation is unwelcome in Parnast because their dimensional portals allow them to move goods quickly and safely, undercutting independent small traders like himself. Calder's shop is his last known location.
