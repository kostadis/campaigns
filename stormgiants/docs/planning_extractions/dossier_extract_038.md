## Harshnag the Grim

Harshnag is a Frost Giant and member of the Legendary Force Grey. He was referred to and assigned by the Harper Agent Krowen Valharrow, who insisted the party were his agents. Harshnag is frustrated by the party's lack of respect — particularly Orsik and Vardis calling him "Frosty," which he finds disrespectful given the magnitude of help he is offering.

Harshnag explains the Ordning to the party: the hierarchical caste system created by Anm, the god of all giants, which has kept peace among giant races for centuries. He warns that without the Ordning, giants will wage war on each other and everyone in between. He reveals that King Hekaton of the Storm Giants has disappeared, and that powerful giant leaders have begun waging war as a result.

Harshnag proposes leading the party to the Eye of the All-Father, a mountain temple built by his ancient ancestors beneath the Spine of the World, which contains a divine oracle. His plan is to consult the oracle to learn how to end the giants' threat to the North.

He negotiated and accepted a 100 gold piece share of the 5,000 gold piece reward for the capture of Worvil Forkbeard, also known as the Weevil. When questioned about cloud giants and flying castles, he brought his axe close to Unla Kee in a show of intimidation, though Unla Kee was unbothered. He laughed at the idea of a cloud giant helping the party acquire a flying tower, then mentioned Count Thullen — the younger brother of Countess Sansuri — as a good-natured cloud giant who might be of use.

Harshnag provided background on Longsaddle as the party approached, describing its businesses (the Gilded Horseshoe inn, the Gambling Golem fest hall), its ranches, and the protective presence of the Harpell wizard family at Ivy Mansion. He expressed open contempt for the Lords' Alliance, calling it a federation of rich, powerful people seeking to tax and control others. He asked the party to bring him a pot of soup from the Gilded Horseshoe.

During the attack on Xantharl's Keep, a Frost Giant was reported leading a force of goblins, hobgoblins, and ogres from the west — and a second Frost Giant was reported attacking the first. Whether Harshnag is involved in this confrontation is not confirmed, but the party appeared worried by the news. His last known position is outside Xantharl's Keep.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a magic user traveling with the party, described by Harshnag as the most narcissistic, self-absorbed personality he has ever encountered, with a complete disregard for personal safety. Unla Kee holds a belief that the world will keep him alive because if he dies, the world dies with him.

He expressed disappointment that Harshnag did not know how to make a castle fly, and used this to probe whether cloud giants were more powerful than frost giants. When Harshnag brought his axe blade next to Unla Kee — taller than Unla Kee himself — he was entirely unbothered. He then asked whether there were any good cloud giants, prompting Harshnag's laughter and the introduction of Count Thullen.

Unla Kee reacted with enthusiasm to the end of the Ordning, calling it "chaos!" He was similarly excited upon seeing Longsaddle's gambling establishments and headed straight for the Gambling Golem, where he used his ability to see the future and adjust fates to influence a small wager. He spent a long night there before the group continued to Xantharl's Keep.

During the battle at Xantharl's Keep, Unla Kee let loose fireballs that wounded Weevil and the bugbears. He later approached the captured Weevil, showed him the wanted poster acquired in Longsaddle, and flatly contradicted Weevil's claim of innocence.

## Krowen Valharrow

Krowen Valharrow does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced multiple times by Harshnag. He is identified as a Harper Agent who arranged for the party to work with Harshnag. Krowen was insistent that the party were his agents, and Harshnag reminded the group that they had promised to help and were given access to teleportation circles as part of a quid pro quo arrangement. Harshnag quotes Krowen directly in recalling this agreement.

## Lord Narbeck Horn

Narbeck Horn is the Lord of Xantharl's Keep. He is irritated that his aide Orgazol accepted a bribe to allow the party an audience with him. He is dismissive and bureaucratic, deflecting the party's report about the Weevil by directing them to his lieutenant and reminding them that the Ax of Mirabar shows no mercy to those who arrest the wrong person.

When alerted to the attack on the keep — a force of goblins, hobgoblins, and ogres led by a Frost Giant approaching from the west — he shifts into action and appears energized by the threat. He smiles evilly at his lieutenant, implying consequences if the situation goes badly. When a second Frost Giant is reported attacking the first, he is surprised and notes he will need to look into it. Thistle (a fairy in the party) volunteers help to Narbeck during the attack.

## Orgazol

Orgazol is Narbeck Horn's aide or door-keeper at Xantharl's Keep. He is not a named NPC with a speaking role but is referenced by Narbeck as having accepted a bribe from the party to arrange their audience with the Lord. Narbeck finds this annoying.

## Vardis

Vardis is a party member but is included here because Harshnag singles him out by name. Harshnag stares at Vardis when explaining the Ordning after Vardis expresses confusion about what it is. Vardis is delighted at learning the rigid caste system has ended, interpreting it as freedom — an interpretation Harshnag firmly rejects.

During the attack on Xantharl's Keep, Vardis notices bugbears approaching from the east while looking for a way to exit the lord's hall. He identifies the giant attack from the west as a diversion and deduces that the bugbears are heading for the Falling Orc, where the Weevil is hiding, likely to claim the reward. He immediately runs toward the Falling Orc, with Orsik following. During the battle with the bugbears, Vardis fires fireballs alongside Unla Kee, wounding the Weevil.

## Count Thullen

Count Thullen does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Harshnag. He is a cloud giant, the younger brother of Countess Sansuri. Harshnag describes Thullen as a good person, in contrast to his sister, whom he says is "not so much." Harshnag mentions Thullen as someone who might potentially help the party in relation to a flying tower, though he finds the idea amusing. Thullen's current whereabouts are not specified.

## Countess Sansuri

Countess Sansuri does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Harshnag as the sister of Count Thullen. Harshnag describes her as not a good person, implying she is dangerous or villainous. No further details are given.

## Worvil Forkbeard (The Weevil / Larg)

Worvil Forkbeard, also known as the Weevil and apparently also going by the alias Larg, is the fugitive dwarf the party has been pursuing for the 5,000 gold piece reward. He was hiding at the Falling Orc tavern in Xantharl's Keep. During the battle triggered by the giant attack diversion, the bugbears — also seeking the reward — closed in on his location. He was wounded by fireballs from Unla Kee and Vardis, then finished off (or subdued) by Orsik and Thistle.

Upon capture, Weevil proclaimed that the party had saved him from evil creatures. Unla Kee immediately produced the wanted poster and dismissed his claim. His current status is captured. Harshnag had already accepted a 100 gold piece share of the reward for his capture.

## Thistle

Thistle is a fairy traveling with the party. Referenced by Harshnag as one of the "non-giants" assigned by Krowen. During the discussion about whether biology determines morality, Thistle remarks "Silverymoon" when Harshnag asks what kind of people would believe that — implying Silverymoon is home to such thinking. Thistle volunteers help to Narbeck Horn during the attack on the keep. Thistle participates in finishing the bugbears with "good old-fashioned violence" alongside Orsik.
