## Azam

Azam is the leader of a caravan traveling from Vuethel to Parnast along the Black Road. He organized four wagons carrying food, water, medicinal herbs, weapons, and a statue of Angharradgh. He keeps a personal journal documenting the hardships of desert travel and his observations of the party hired as caravan guards.

Azam was asleep when the goblin attack occurred but was awakened by Thistle's shouts and witnessed the combat from his tent, observing the moonlight glinting off goblin steel. He assessed the party with measured skepticism upon their arrival, noting they appeared sunburned, exhausted, and "every bit the raggedy adventurer who is down on their luck," and had hoped a faction agent would be sent instead. However, the successful repulsion of the goblin raid led him to acknowledge that Hsing's faith in the group may be justified.

Azam works with a middleperson contact named Hsing, who vouched for and selected the party members. His current status is traveling the Black Road toward Parnast with the caravan intact.

## Hsing

Hsing does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced by Azam as the middleperson who selected and vouched for the party. Hsing provided Azam with background information on several party members, specifically noting that Orsik "has done things that have made him unemployable" and that Vardis' fellow clerics will not allow him in their temples. Hsing explicitly told Azam that these individuals are "the heroes that he is looking for." Hsing's confidence in the group appears to have been at least partially validated by the goblin attack outcome, per Azam's journal entry.

---

## Thistle

Thistle is a fairy member of the party serving as a caravan guard. He is described by Azam as appearing "bent on revenge," though the target or cause of this revenge is not elaborated upon.

During the nighttime goblin raid on the caravan, Thistle took the initiative to stand watch through the night, having proposed during the day that he sleep then and guard after dark. His vigilance proved critical — he spotted the approaching goblins and raised the alarm before they could steal any goods. In the ensuing fight, Thistle personally killed two goblins and the goblin boss along with its mount. After the battle, Thistle argued that the party should pursue the two fleeing goblins, though Vardis objected and the pursuit did not occur. The rest of the night passed without incident.

Last known status: traveling with Azam's caravan toward Parnast.

---

## Vardis

Vardis is an elf member of the party, described by Azam as one of a "curious pair" alongside the dwarf Orsik. According to Hsing, Vardis' fellow clerics will not allow him into their temples, suggesting a falling out or disgrace within his religious order.

During the aftermath of the goblin raid, Vardis objected to Thistle's proposal to pursue the two fleeing goblins, and his position prevailed — the party did not give chase.

Last known status: traveling with Azam's caravan toward Parnast.

---

## Orsik

Orsik is a dwarf member of the party, described by Azam as part of a "curious pair" with the elf Vardis. According to Hsing, Orsik has done things that have made him unemployable as a guard, though the specific acts are not named.

Orsik is also noted in Fzoul Slovis's report as a potential Zhentarim recruit. Slovis encountered him (or learned of him) during the journey from Parnast, identified him by name, and stated an intention to recruit him at the earliest opportunity, believing the Zhentarim may find use for someone with his background despite — or because of — the reasons he cannot find conventional guard work.

Last known status: traveling with Azam's caravan toward Parnast.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a member of the party described by Azam as being "convinced that the fate of the world sits on his willingness to take risks." No further details about his actions during the goblin attack or his background are provided in this chunk.

Last known status: traveling with Azam's caravan toward Parnast.

## Fzoul Slovis

Fzoul Slovis is a Zhentarim operative who authored an intelligence report dated 3/1 of Taraskh regarding the state of the Black Road. He was traveling from Parnast toward Vuerthyl on a reconnaissance mission, with the explicit objective of collecting information rather than clearing threats.

During his journey, Slovis encountered a hobgoblin toll post on the edge of the desert, which he assessed as too well-manned to fight. He paid the toll of 15 gold pieces per camel and filed an expense report, while also noting the post's location and recommending the Zhentarim deal with it. He further encountered a caravan near Vuerthyl that had been attacked by goblins, which he characterized as lacking the sophistication of earlier goblin raids.

Slovis used his report to repeat a longstanding recommendation — one he notes he has made "many times" — that Zhentarim leadership take a firmer, more systematic approach to eliminating the goblin threat along the Black Road. He argued that piecemeal attempts have emboldened goblins and that the organization's credibility as a trade power is at stake.

He identified Orsik as a potential Zhentarim recruit and stated his intention to approach him at the earliest opportunity.

Last known status: returning from a scouting run along the Black Road; based in or reporting to Zhentarim leadership.
