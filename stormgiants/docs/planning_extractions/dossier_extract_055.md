## Bressik

Bressik serves as the hired scholar for the 3HPP (Three-Headed Pirate Party or similar group), accompanying them aboard Delsephine's airship the Dragon's Breath. He is approximately 30 years old. He has a contract with Klamath, a Red Dragon necromancer, that covers both his living and undead life — valued at roughly 13,300 gold total by Delsephine's calculation. The contract includes a payout clause.

At Raven's Rock, Bressik presents his research on the spirit mound of the Black Raven and Gray Wolf tribes, describing the 100-foot stone raven, the four menhirs, and the Gray Wolf altar mound in the wolf-shaped depression. He is irritated and briefly splutters when Orsik overrules him and dismisses exploration of Raven's Rock itself in favor of going directly to the altar. He finds the relationship between Thistle and his drake Thuul fascinating and spent an evening conversing with Thistle about it, learning that Thistle describes the partnership as "anarcho-socialist."

Aboard the Dragon's Breath en route to Great Worm Cavern, Bressik becomes drawn into a debate about the Cult of the Dragon. He clarifies that he and Delsephine were not members of Severin's faction. He argues that the original Dracolich vision — creating a new undead race of dragons to transcend draconic conflict — was subverted by Severin and Tiamat, who manipulated Severin into creating a summoning ritual for Tiamat instead. He presents this as a philosophical and ideological distinction, not merely a power struggle, and is annoyed when Thistle dismisses it as "academic nonsense." He also offers a brief linguistic note that the term "Dracolich" was chosen to emphasize the draconic and unifying nature of the undead ritual.

At Great Worm Cavern, Bressik delivers his second formal presentation, describing the cavern's geography, the icy ledges, totem poles, tribal crypts, and the altar of Elrem the Great Worm. He offers two theories on the gong artifact: that the gong itself (a broken frost giant champion's shield of red dragon scales, 13 feet in diameter, 250 pounds) is the artifact, or that it is merely decorative and the real artifact lies beneath the altar as in prior sites. He favors the second theory based on the pattern established at previous mounds.

During the journey, Bressik privately questions whether he should continue assisting with the desecration of ancient tribal sites and artifacts. He suppresses this doubt when he recalls his pay and audience.

Orsik approaches Delsephine to negotiate purchasing Bressik's contract with the intent of recruiting him into their show. Delsephine informs Orsik that buying the living portion of the contract would free Bressik entirely — his obligations would not transfer to the buyer. No final deal on the contract itself is struck during this session.

Bressik descends to the lower decks of the Dragon's Breath after his presentation. His last known whereabouts are aboard the Dragon's Breath as the party enters Great Worm Cavern.

## Delsephine

Delsephine is the commander of the airship Dragon's Breath and acts as an agent for Klamath, a Red Dragon necromancer. She wears a dark mask over her face. She is affiliated with a faction of the Cult of the Dragon distinct from Severin's Tiamat-focused group, aligned with the original Dracolich vision.

At Raven's Rock, Delsephine observes the comical sight of Unla Kee flying with pegasus wings and finds it consistently amusing. When Thistle and Unla Kee inform her that the ravens may not view her as an innocent bystander, she agrees to move the Dragon's Breath away, steering it toward Great Worm Cavern.

During the transit to Great Worm Cavern, she interjects into Bressik's presentation to advertise climbing equipment for sale, and pockets 40 gold from Orsik for two sets of crampons and gear.

Delsephine negotiates the terms of Bressik's contract on behalf of Klamath when Orsik inquires. She explains the dual-life structure of the contract — 300 gold for the living portion (10 gold per year, 30 years remaining) and 13,000 gold for the undead portion (100 years at 10 gold per year) — noting that Klamath is a necromancer and the undead clause has real value. She states that if Bressik were killed and his body desecrated, the parts could be used in necromantic constructs. She confirms Klamath is willing to negotiate and that she can act on his behalf.

When Unla Kee proposes a residuals structure instead of a straight cash deal for Bressik's contract, Delsephine agrees to terms of 500 gold upfront with residuals capped at 6,000 gold tied to the party's play. She does not know that Unla Kee has already signed a deal for the current play and that her residuals will not burden a sequel production.

Delsephine refuses to reveal Klamath's location to Orsik but promises that Klamath will speak with and reward the party once they have dealt with the giants.

Her last known position is aboard the Dragon's Breath at Great Worm Cavern.

## Klamath

Klamath does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. He is a Red Dragon necromancer who holds Bressik's contract in his hoard. The contract is structured to cover both Bressik's living life (valued at approximately 300 gold) and his potential undead life (valued at approximately 13,000 gold over an assumed 100 years). Delsephine acts as his agent and negotiator.

Klamath is described as "very supportive" of the party's plans to deal with the giants. Delsephine explains that dragons view giants as an obstacle to their eventual domination of the North, as giants are uniquely capable of surviving in similar terrains and territories as dragons.

Klamath is willing to negotiate the payout clause on Bressik's contract. He is said to be a necromancer, which explains the undead clause in the contract and his interest in Bressik's potential corpse and parts for necromantic constructs if the body were desecrated.

Klamath's location is not disclosed — Delsephine explicitly refuses to reveal it to Orsik. He is said to be aligned with the original Dracolich faction of the Cult of the Dragon, not Severin's Tiamat-focused splinter group. Delsephine promises he will reward and speak with the party after the giant threat is resolved.

## Orsik

Orsik is a dwarf member of the party and a core decision-maker for the group. At Raven's Rock, he is irritated by the extensive digging at Morgur's Mound and immediately directs the group to the Gray Wolf altar, cutting off Bressik's attempt to advocate for exploring Raven's Rock proper. He has sufficient presence and a reputation for blade and temper that Bressik falls silent rather than argue.

Orsik and Vardis physically carry the excavated thigh bone artifact on their shoulders through the snow back to the Dragon's Breath after finding it beneath the altar. He is wet, snow-covered, and steaming when he announces the find.

He pokes Thistle to ask if he is comfortable working with dragons, seemingly amused by the irony given Thistle's prior stance on the Cult of the Dragon. He listens to Bressik's explanation of the Dracolich faction split without vovert comment.

En route to Great Worm Cavern, Orsik immediately decides to retrieve the gong artifact and notes it would pair well with an existing "bong" they possess. He expresses an interest in giving the gong to Harshnag if it proves to be a shield. He jokes about naming a future production "the Gong Show." He also agrees with Bressik's second theory — that the real artifact lies beneath the altar rather than being the gong itself.

Orsik approaches Delsephine to negotiate purchasing Bressik's living contract with the intent of recruiting him into their show. He is surprised to learn that the purchase would free Bressik entirely rather than transferring his obligations. He expresses a strong desire to meet Klamath in person. He agrees to the overall deal Unla Kee structures with Delsephine. He is impressed by Unla Kee's legal maneuvering around the residuals clause.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a halfling member of the party, approximately 3 feet tall and 1 foot wide, who uses wings of a pegasus to fly — a sight Delsephine finds consistently comical given the 30-foot wingspan on so small a frame. Unla Kee flies to the Gray Wolf altar ahead of the group while Orsik and Vardis trudge through the snow.

At Great Worm Cavern, Unla Kee and Vardis exchange a glance and jointly incinerate all members of the Great Worm tribe who are preparing to defend their totems, using fireballs. They react with smug satisfaction at the result.

During the negotiations over Bressik's contract, Unla Kee proposes restructuring the deal as a residuals arrangement rather than a straight cash payment — a residual being a percentage of future earnings, which can be bounded or unbounded. He successfully negotiates Delsephine down to 500 gold upfront with residuals capped at 6,000 gold, and immediately agrees without further haggling.

Crucially, Unla Kee privately reveals to Orsik afterward that the residuals deal applies only to the current play, for which he has already signed a separate deal. A sequel production would not be burdened by Delsephine's residuals clause. He describes this tactic as "showbusiness." He also raises the concern of whether structuring a deceptive deal is wise given Klamath is a Red Dragon.

## Thistle

Thistle is a fairy Ranger and member of the party who scouts Raven's Rock with his drake Thuul. Bressik finds the relationship between Thistle and Thuul fascinating; after an evening's conversation, he learned that Thistle describes their partnership as "anarcho-socialist," with the two taking turns being in charge.

At Raven's Rock, Thistle approaches a murder of giant ravens and attempts to ask whether they have seen any Uthgardian barbarians. The ravens immediately challenge his use of the term "barbarians," pointing out the irony of the party digging up a religious site to steal an artifact while calling the tribes barbaric. Thistle stumbles through an attempted retraction and correction, acknowledging he put his "wingtip in his mouth." He then asks the ravens not to tell the Uthgardt who took the artifact. The lead raven responds "Nevermore," which alarms Thistle — he cannot recall what his Uncle Joon said about the word.

Thistle immediately flies to Orsik and Vardis to urge haste, warning of potential incoming Uthgardt.

Later, Thistle speaks with Delsephine alongside Unla Kee to request she move the Dragon's Breath, concerned the ravens may implicate her as well.

In the debate about the Cult of the Dragon, Thistle states he is not opposed to all dragons, only to the Cult of the Dragon. When Bressik argues the Dracolich vision represents a legitimate philosophical position about the natural order, Thistle dismisses the entire debate as "about power-hungry individuals," visibly annoyed.

At Great Worm Cavern, Thistle raises the concern of whether it is wise to risk angering a Red Dragon by structuring the residuals deal deceptively.

## Vardis

Vardis is a member of the party who participates in the physical excavation at Raven's Rock alongside Orsik, helping hoist the recovered thigh bone artifact onto their shoulders to carry it back to the Dragon's Breath.

At Great Worm Cavern, Vardis exchanges a glance with Unla Kee and the two jointly launch fireballs to incinerate the Great Worm tribe defenders, reacting with smug satisfaction at eliminating the resistance.

## Elrem

Elrem does not appear in this chunk as an active presence but is described by Bressik as "the Great Worm" — the entity worshipped by the Great Worm tribe at their altar in the Great Wyrm Cavern. The altar is carved in the shape of a coiled serpent with great wings representing Elrem. The tribe makes sacrifices at the altar to honor both Elrem and Uthgar, believing that doing so prevents Elrem from returning to devour them. The creature that bursts from the snowbank at the end of the session — described as a mingling of worms, centipedes, and dragons with dozens of legs and wing-like membranes — may be connected to or be Elrem, though this is not explicitly confirmed in the text.

## Severin

Severin does not appear in this chunk but is discussed in the debate about the Cult of the Dragon. Bressik identifies Severin as the leader of a faction that "misread Samaster's text on Dracoliches."
