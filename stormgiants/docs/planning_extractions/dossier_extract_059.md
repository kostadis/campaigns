## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is encountered in the control room of his flying castle, sitting cross-legged, appearing harried, tired, unshaven, and disheveled. He is in a state of domestic conflict with his wife Escarlotta, who is trapped within the castle's control console and has been preventing the castle from moving. He screams at the runes she controls, demonstrating his frustration and powerlessness in the situation.

His backstory is revealed through Escarlotta's accusations: Blagothkus had a plan to help the Dragons bring Tiamat into the world, calculating that when the dragons failed, the giants could rule over the weakened "little people." To further this scheme, he traveled to the Eye of the All-Father and killed his own son Eigeron, rather than allowing Eigeron to kill him — an act that caused Amn to declare him unworthy. Escarlotta condemns him for pursuing these grand schemes while ignoring a vampire, Sandesyl, who had taken over his castle and consumed all his minions.

During the 3HPP's negotiations, Blagothkus briefly rouses himself as if to fight when Thistle's hypothetical about killing him is raised, but stands down when Vardis intervenes. He finds momentary solidarity with Vardis when the latter defends him against Escarlotta's insults. He and Escarlotta both laugh at the notion of Fire Giant Duke Zalto making a play for rulership, sharing jokes about Fire Giants that don't translate into Common.

Blagothkus is present when the castle begins moving at Escarlotta's direction, bound for the Eye of the All-Father. When the 3HPP arrives at the Eye, they are non-committal to Harshnag about Blagothkus's status. His current whereabouts are the castle, which has landed near the Eye of the All-Father.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta is Blagothkus's wife, a cloud giantess whose voice booms from the castle's control console, in which she is trapped. She controls the castle's navigation and has been refusing to allow Blagothkus to fly it anywhere, effectively imprisoning him in stasis north of Parnast. Her control of the console is demonstrated when floating rune icons arrange themselves into an arrow pointing at Blagothkus at her direction.

Her fury at Blagothkus is multifaceted: she condemns his alliance with dragons to bring Tiamat into the world, his killing of their son Eigeron at the Eye of the All-Father, and his failure to seize maps from Countess Sansuri that might have enabled their family to make a play for rulership among the giants. She bitterly notes that their family was not among those making a serious bid for rulership, and blames Blagothkus's poor choices for this outcome. She goes so far as to say she might prefer Blagothkus as a vampire, claiming he never had a soul anyway.

Escarlotta also despises Sandesyl the vampire, though she tolerates the vampire's continued presence in the castle as a consequence of Blagothkus's failure to deal with her. She had previously warned Blagothkus to remove Sandesyl, and his inaction is another grievance she holds against him.

Her terms for flying the castle to the Eye of the All-Father are that the 3HPP must kill Countess Sansuri, whom she hates intensely — initially calling her "a cow" before walking back the physical insult and replacing it with condemnations of Sansuri's arrogance and selfishness. When Thistle reveals the party can find the giant leaders' locations at the Oracle, Escarlotta immediately begins flying the castle without further negotiation, saying, "Well, why didn't you lead with that?"

She does not know the locations of the fire giant leaders at the time of negotiation. Her current status is within the castle's control console, which is parked near the Eye of the All-Father.

## Sandesyl

Sandesyl is a vampire who has been treating Blagothkus's flying castle as her personal domain. The arrangement described is that she allowed Blagothkus to pursue his various plots so long as he supplied her with a steady stream of victims. When Blagothkus parked the castle north of Parnast and fell into a funk, the supply of victims dried up, and Sandesyl responded by consuming all of his minions over the intervening months. By the time the 3HPP is present, the only living beings available as prey are the party, Blagothkus, and two ogres named Worug and Tax.

Sandesyl makes herself known through her zombie spawns, whose eyes she uses to observe the party from the tower exterior. She is dressed — and her spawn are dressed — in the purple of the cult. When the castle begins to move, a spawn under an umbrella (to avoid the sun) knocks on the door and, speaking on her behalf, asks where the castle will stop for a meal. When Thistle announces Silverymoon and reveals a vendetta against House Margastar, the spawn is stunned and Sandesyl herself later appears in the courtyard to address the party directly.

In the courtyard, surrounded by zombies and spawns, Sandesyl attempts to lure Thistle outside, which he refuses. She expresses apparent disappointment at the lack of trust. She reveals that she has no interest in warring with House Margastar because Asmodeus protects them, and she will not risk upsetting the archfiend's plans. She negotiates with Thistle over a feeding ground, eventually agreeing on the Uthgardt barbarians of Flint Rock.

On the night of Eleasis 1st to morning of the 2nd day of the 3rd tenday, Sandesyl's vampires locate a group of Uthgardt nomads mourning a despoiled artifact, capture them, bring them to the castle, and feed. They leave manufactured evidence implicating House Margastar and Silverymoon, and leave a survivor to spread this false tale. Over the following days through the party's arrival at the Eye, the screams of Sandesyl's victims echo through the castle.

Sandesyl's last known whereabouts are the castle, which is parked near the Eye of the All-Father.

## Countess Sansuri

Countess Sansuri does not appear in this chunk but is discussed at length by Escarlotta. She is a cloud giantess who lives in Lyn Armaal, her flying castle. Escarlotta describes her as vain, glorious, greedy, arrogant, selfish, and "untethered from reality," surrounded by enemies who wish to topple her. Her family held magical artifacts when Ostoria fell, and she is pursuing them with the goal of establishing rulership over the giants.

Escarlotta is intensely hostile toward Sansuri and names killing her as her price for flying the castle to the Eye of the All-Father. She insists Blagothkus should have seized maps from Sansuri rather than pursuing his dragon schemes. The Oracle later confirms the Cloud Giant Countess is located in Lyn Armaal, which can be found by traveling southeast to the untamed moors and looking to the sky. The conch the party needs is in a secret chest in the highest spire of Lyn Armaal.

## Duke Zalto

Duke Zalto does not appear in this chunk but is discussed. Thistle mentions the Fire Giant Duke's ambitions and his collection of artifacts to Blagothkus and Escarlotta as part of his effort to build rapport and interest them in the party's mission. Both Blagothkus and Escarlotta find the idea of a Fire Giant making a play for rulership of all giants absurd and laugh at his expense, trading jokes about Fire Giants that don't translate into Common.

The Oracle later reveals that Duke Zalto, referred to as "the fire giant duke," operates out of Ironslag, a forge located east over mountains and snow, near a village of yakfolk. The conch the party needs is in his quarters.

## Harshnag

Harshnag is present at the Eye of the All-Father, where he has apparently been waiting for the 3HPP. He meets the party in the main temple of Annam. When told the party has a castle and asked about Blagothkus, the party is non-committal. Harshnag picks up Thrymm's great axe, touches the Ise rune on the portal, and walks into the Eye with the party.

After the Oracle delivers its information and the ghost of Eigeron is dealt with, the party exits the portal into the main temple, where Adimond Kroskas is waiting. When Kroskas sees Harshnag, he transforms into an Ancient Blue Dragon. Harshnag commands the party to flee, telling them their fate lies elsewhere and that he will distract the dragon. When the party hesitates — Thistle wanting revenge and Unla Kee wanting to fight — Harshnag takes drastic action: he swings his greataxe at the statue of Annam the All-Father, chipping it and causing the temple to shudder. He dodges the dragon and strikes the statue again, breaking off a large chunk. The desecration causes the ceiling to crack and the mountain to begin collapsing. He shouts "Flee!" as a final warning.

The heroes run. The last the party sees, Harshnag is fighting the Ancient Blue Dragon in a collapsing temple. The hallway and temple collapse behind the fleeing heroes. Harshnag's fate is unknown; he is last seen in combat with the dragon amid the ruins of the Eye of the All-Father.

## Eigeron

Eigeron is the son of Blagothkus, killed by his own father at the Eye of the All-Father as part of Blagothkus's scheme — rather than allowing Eigeron to kill him, as the ordeal apparently required. Amn declared Blagothkus unworthy as a result, according to Escarlotta.

Eigeron appears within the Eye of the All-Father as a ghost or spirit. He asks the party about his father and, upon learning Blagothkus is not dead but that his castle is present, goes mad and attempts to possess one of the heroes. Vardis invokes the power of Corellon and turns the monster. The heroes, sensing time is short, deposit the artifacts and receive the Oracle's information before Eigeron's turned state ends. They flee through the portal back to the main temple, leaving Eigeron howling for revenge behind them.

## Adimond Kroskas

Adimond Kroskas appears in the main temple of Annam at the Eye of the All-Father after the party exits the Oracle's portal. Thistle's first instinct upon seeing him is to attack — suggesting prior hostile history between them. Before Thistle can act, Kroskas spots Harshnag and transforms into an Ancient Blue Dragon. He is the dragon Harshnag chooses to sacrifice himself to fight, attacking Harshnag as the temple collapses around them. His current status is unknown following the collapse of the Eye of the All-Father; he was last seen in combat with Harshnag inside the collapsing temple.

## Worug

Worug is an ogre named as one of the last surviving living beings aboard Blagothkus's castle at the time of the events, alongside the 3HPP, Blagothkus, and another ogre named Tax. Worug is mentioned only as one of the potential remaining prey items that Sandesyl might feed upon. No further detail is provided.
