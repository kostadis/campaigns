## Xaltivar

Xaltivar does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced in Fred and George's report. Xaltivar organized an attack on Xanthalar's Keep that was deliberately a ruse — a distraction to draw the main force of the guard away while a detachment of bugbears jumped the walls and attempted to secure the prisoner Worvil Forkbeard, known as The Weevil. The true objective of the attack was the capture of this specific individual, not the Keep itself. Xaltivar's plan was ultimately thwarted when the party and the Three Halfpints and a Pint held their ground and repelled both elements of the assault.

## Worvil Forkbeard (The Weevil)

The Weevil is the prisoner transported by the party from Xanthalar's Keep to Mirabar, worth 5,000 gold pieces as a bounty. He was the true target of Xaltivar's raid on the Keep. During the journey north along the Long Road, he was gagged but managed to speak when Orsik removed his gag. He attempted to bribe Orsik with the promise of a much larger score — specifically, he claimed to have cracked a secret pattern governing the movements of ore caravans from the mines of Mirabar, and offered to share this information in exchange for his freedom. He knew Orsik by name and reputation. Orsik rejected the offer and re-gagged him. Upon arrival in Mirabar, The Weevil attempted to convince the guard sergeant Herald that he was not who the party claimed, but Herald identified him by the distinctive braiding of his beard and silenced him. Herald declared that The Weevil would spend the rest of his days working in the mines, and also indicated that Mirabar had ways of extracting The Weevil's knowledge of the caravan pattern. His current status is prisoner of Mirabar, sentenced to a life of labor in the mines.

## Narbeck Horn

Narbeck Horn does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced during the debate among the Three Halfpints and a Pint at Xanthalar's Keep. One member of the 3HPP argued that the group should leave immediately because His Lordship Narbeck Horn would be deceived by The Weevil's claim of mistaken identity. The party chose not to reveal to the 3HPP that Lord Horn would in fact be able to identify The Weevil by the distinctive braiding of his beard — a detail recognizable to all shield dwarfs. His relationship to The Weevil's capture and to the Keep's affairs is not further elaborated, but he appears to be a lord with authority over the matter of the prisoner.

## Herald

Herald is the sergeant of the guard of Mirabar's Axe of Mirabar who received the prisoner from the party upon their arrival in the city. He was delighted by the handover and congratulated the group on their service to Mirabar. When The Weevil attempted to deny his identity, Herald immediately identified him by his distinctive beard braid and silenced him, drawing other guards in the process. He declared that The Weevil would work in the mines for the rest of his days and, upon learning from Orsik that The Weevil had cracked the caravan scheduling code, told The Weevil directly that Mirabar had ways of extracting that information. He paid out the 5,000 gold piece bounty in coins marked with the symbol of Mirabar. He is last seen in Mirabar processing the prisoner.

## Laela

Laela does not appear directly in this chunk but is the subject of Thistle's inquiries during the downtime in Mirabar. She is described as Thistle's lost love, a fairy whose description was recognized by members of the Axe of Mirabar, who confirmed she passed through the city and headed east toward the Lurkwood. Additional information was obtained from the merchant Gale Wonderbrow, who relayed a tale of a fairy matching Laela's description single-handedly slaughtering a group of brigands and bandits who had surrounded a caravan. The only survivors were Laela and a young man who had been cowering in one of the wagons. Her current whereabouts are somewhere east of Mirabar, in or near the Lurkwood.

## Gale Wonderbrow

Gale Wonderbrow is a merchant in Mirabar who had information about Laela. He is described as having an athletic and imposing frame, short hair, and a chin built for taking a punch. He agreed to share his information with Thistle in exchange for help arranging an elaborate wedding. Thistle, being a fairy of the Seelie Court, obliged and produced a spectacular result. Pleased with the outcome, Gale shared the tale of a fairy matching Laela's description massacring brigands surrounding a caravan, leaving only Laela and one cowering young man alive. His last known location is Mirabar.

## Yip Hammaver

Yip Hammaver is a 32-year-old bachelor of the mercantile House Hammaver of Mirabar, notable for his medium height and unusually red eyes. His house arranged a dinner to entertain Orsik, who was disguised as John Husteem of House Husteem of Waterdeep. Yip befriended Orsik-as-John and acted as his social guide during the Mirabar nobility events. When the duel with Tack Proudfoot arose, Yip advised John-Orsik that Tack was a dandy who had read too many romance novels and was not a real threat — an assessment that proved dramatically incorrect when Tack appeared in elite guard armor. After Orsik resolved the duel without violence by casting sanctuary, Yip was delighted with the outcome. He then offered to introduce the apparently single John-Orsik to eligible bachelors and bachelorettes from the noble houses of Mirabar. His last known location is Mirabar.

## Tack Proudfoot Esquire

Tack Proudfoot Esquire is a minor noble of Mirabar who was offended by Orsik (disguised as John Husteem) at one of the dinner parties and challenged him to a duel at dawn outside the city. He is described by Yip Hammaver as a dandy who read too many romance novels about dueling, leading Yip to dismiss him as not a real threat. However, Tack arrived to the duel wearing the finest armor Orsik had ever seen and was identified as a noble knight of the elite guards — a far more dangerous opponent than anticipated. He was also betrothed to a member of Clan Battlehammer, one of the leading houses of Mirabar, meaning his death would have had serious political consequences. When Orsik cast sanctuary and refused to fight, Tack declared that honor was satisfied, removed his helmet, and embraced Orsik warmly. He then enthusiastically insisted that Orsik be introduced to eligible noble matches in Mirabar. His last known location is Mirabar, where he remains betrothed to a member of Clan Battlehammer.

## Hork Gethorn

Hork Gethorn is the owner of a meat pie shop in Mirabar, described as a man whose face resembles a meat pie and whose size reflects his deep love of his own product. He posted a notice seeking a strong sword arm to deal with an infestation beneath his establishment. Vardis answered the notice, completed the job, and was rewarded with payment and recovered treasure. Hork's last known location is his meat pie shop in Mirabar.

## Harshnag The Grim

Harshnag is the frost giant traveling with the party. During the journey to Mirabar, he delivered an extended monologue about the city — its geography, its defenses including sloped outer walls wide enough to serve as ice slides in winter, the fortified docks, the surface city of humans, and the underground dwarven city of forges, foundries, and mines. His lecture was frequently interrupted by Unla Kee's provocations, most notably the halfling's suggestion that Harshnag could make ice slides like a white dragon. Harshnag responded by slamming his battleaxe into the ground near Unla Kee and delivering a solemn speech about the ancient enmity between giants and dragons, explaining that Tiamat's attempt to seize power weakened the North and allowed the Giant lords to exploit the resulting vacuum and shatter the Ordning. On the seventh day of the third tenday of Flamerule, Harshnag sat alone under a tree outside Mirabar, deeply troubled. He was processing a series of alarming reports: Frost Giant Jarls raiding settlements in Icewind Dale, a refugee account suggesting Bryn Shander was laid waste by Frost Giants seeking an artifact, Hill Giants attacking Goldenfields to the south, Stone Giants attacking surface settlements (which he found most frightening, as Stone Giants view the surface world as an abomination), and the party's earlier report that Fire Giants under Duke Zalto were scouring the lands for an artifact. He concluded aloud, "This is not good, not good at all." His last known location is outside the walls of Mirabar.

## Duke Zalto

Duke Zalto does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced by Harshnag during his solitary reflection outside Mirabar. Harshnag recalls that the party previously reported that Fire Giants under Duke Zalto's command were scouring the lands in search of an artifact. This information is one of several alarming giant-related developments that Harshnag is weighing as evidence that the war against the little people is worsening across multiple giant factions simultaneously.
