## Kurglow

Kurglow is the orc chieftain of the Spinripper Clan, identifiable by his green sash. He appears as a major combatant and antagonist in the battle for Parnast, leading forces alongside the blue-sashed orc chieftain.

During the battle, Kurglow is blinded by Vardis's invocation of Correllon but still lands a blow for 15 hit points. He becomes enraged at Orsik specifically because Orsik's threats implied that Kurglow's whelp was soft, overly civilized, pampered, and spoiled — an insult to Kurglow's identity as a wild and dangerous orc. He charges at Orsik, taking opportunity attacks from Chandra, Vardis, Wallace, and Unla Kee along the way. Unla Kee's mind spike penetrates his mental defenses on the second attempt. After the green-sashed chieftain is killed by Orsik, Kurglow demands the return of his son, threatening to kill the party otherwise, and agrees to leave if his child is returned.

When his son is brought forward, Kurglow confronts Naz, accusing her of caring only about her own child and being willing to sacrifice his whelp to save hers. He dismisses her denial, refuses her plea to take her with him, and delivers a final cutting remark — "Unlike you, I can take my whelp and leave you to die" — before departing with his son. His departure triggers Bad Fruul's rage at what Fruul perceives as betrayal.

Kurglow's last known whereabouts: departed Parnast alive with his whelp after the negotiated exchange.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a gnome spellcaster traveling with the party. He is cut down early in the session by an orc's axe — the orc screams "Die Unla Kee!" as his axe slices through Unla Kee's robes, and Unla Kee falls. His internal thought at the moment of falling is "The prophecy says otherwise," suggesting he believes himself protected by some foretold fate. Vardis subsequently heals him, returning him to the fight.

Once recovered, Unla Kee attempts a mind sliver on Kurglow (the green-sashed chieftain) with no initial effect, then tries again and successfully penetrates the chieftain's mental defenses. He retrieves his wand of web and uses it to catch the mammoth in a web, neutralizing that threat. He then moves directly in front of Bad Fruul, positioning himself aggressively before the giant.

Blassios greets him warmly upon arriving, calling him "my drinking buddy Unla Kee," indicating a prior friendship between the two.

Current status: active in the battle for Parnast, standing in front of Bad Fruul.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul is a giant — described as riding a mammoth and commanding a force of ettins and orcs — who serves as the primary antagonist of the assault on Parnast. He is guided in his assault by a figure described as Chandra's family's nemesis, who is pressing the attack now to avoid losing the chance for vengeance.

Bad Fruul howls in anger at Kurglow's betrayal when Kurglow withdraws from the battle after retrieving his whelp. He then mounts his mammoth and leads the final assault from the east, with ettins and a horde of orcs attacking from north and south simultaneously.

During the battle, Bad Fruul smashes through the houses of Parnast and casts hold person, targeting Thistle specifically — shouting "I hate fairies that fly!" The spell fails to hold Thistle. He bangs his head in frustration when the spell does not work. His mammoth is slowed when Unla Kee catches it in a web and the sprites poison it, and Naz strikes it with a guiding bolt. The freed wyvern then attacks Bad Fruul directly. One of the ettins is heard screaming — "I thought you said they would not free the Wyvern!" — indicating surprise at the tactic.

Current status: engaged in combat with the wyvern outside the stables of Parnast.

## Chandra

Chandra is a member of the party with a personal connection to Bad Fruul's assault — she identifies the force guiding Bad Fruul's actions as her family's nemesis, who is driving the timing of the attack to seek vengeance. Chandra knows the wyvern's situation in detail: it is chained to a post in the former stables, and she advises Orsik to throw dead orc bodies into a corner to distract it with fresh meat, then rush to remove the chain from the hoop. She warns that once freed, the wyvern will charge the nearest living thing and that Orsik must ensure that is the giant.

She expresses strong skepticism about freeing the wyvern, shouting "are you mad? He will kill us all!" She casts bless on Orsik, Thistle, and Wallace during the battle — choosing these three deliberately: Orsik because he promised to protect her, Thistle because Bad Fruul has targeted him and a target that is not her is worth preserving, and Wallace because she has known him since birth and loves him like a son.

Current status: active in the battle for Parnast.

## Naz

Naz is an orc female who served as caregiver to the orc whelps — including Kurglow's child — while they were held in Parnast. After Kurglow departs with his son, she collapses by the well in the center of town and sobs. When Kurglow confronts her, he accuses her of prioritizing her own child over his, saying she would have sacrificed his whelp to save hers. She denies this and begs him to take her with him. He refuses and delivers a parting insult before leaving.

She sits sobbing by the well until, during the final assault, she rises and declares: "I will get my revenge on Kurglow after we kill this idiot of a giant and the orcs that follow him to their doom." She then enters the battle and strikes the mammoth with a guiding bolt.

Current status: active combatant in the defense of Parnast.

## Blassios

Blassios is a Seelie fairy — he describes himself internally as "the bravest of all Seelies" — who arrives at Parnast with two sprites to aid the party. He greets the group enthusiastically, calling them his great friends and singling out Unla Kee as his drinking buddy. Thistle is delighted by his arrival.

His internal monologue reveals ambivalence: he questions why he is present, attributes his involvement to the revenge of someone named Oblivilish punishing him for an unspecified indiscretion, and is uncomfortable fighting at close quarters with his sword. He takes cover and fires an arrow at the orc who struck him with a javelin. He directs the two sprites to attack the mammoth; they poison it but do not put it to sleep.

He is struck by a javelin from an orc during the fighting, which he complains about as rudeness.

Current status: active in the battle for Parnast, fighting from cover.
