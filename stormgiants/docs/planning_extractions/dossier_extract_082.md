## Mary

Mary does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Thistle's memories. She was an old lady living in Everlund who warned passers-by about the Kraken. She led a group called the "Fighters of the Light" and sent Thistle's group on a quest to retrieve what she called an artifact but was in reality a trinket. The quest was located in the dark, brooding peaks of the Nether Mountains. Thistle recalls that part of the reason his group agreed to go was that they lacked the five gold pieces required as an admission fee to her group.

## Adimond Kroskas

Adimond Kroskas does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Thistle's theorizing. He is identified as the "Doom of the Desert," a figure whose identity Thistle had not known during the earlier Nether Mountains quest but now recognizes. Blue dragons had been chased out of the northern Anauroch desert by the Doom of the Desert, a detail Thistle had ignored at the time. Thistle also refers to Kroskas by the name Iymrith and uses the names interchangeably throughout his conspiracy theory.

## Iymrith

Iymrith does not appear in this chunk but is the central subject of Thistle's extended conspiracy theory. Thistle identifies Iymrith as the "Doom of the Desert" and as the same entity as Adimond Kroskas. Thistle theorizes that Iymrith is a dragon who conducted experiments on fairies, which Thistle holds personally responsible for his dragon eyes. According to Thistle's theory, Iymrith's activities unfolded in the following sequence: she initially received funding from Tiamat's efforts to enter the world during the War of the Dragons and used those resources to begin capturing fairies. After Tiamat was defeated by the First Faction, Iymrith needed a new sponsor. She then perverted the Oracle of Amman and engineered a Giant war, originally planning to use Eigeron but pivoting when Eigeron was killed by his father Blagothkus. She then used the Oracle to tell every giant lord that the Ordning had been sundered, starting the war. When no giant lord would ally with a dragon, she approached King Hekaton directly with an offer of help, which Hekaton refused. She then killed Queen Nerissa, hoping Hekaton would go to war against the smaller peoples, but Queen Serissa intervened and convinced Hekaton to investigate instead. Iymrith then had Hekaton kidnapped and hidden on a ship that cannot be scried, removing him from power. She secured an introduction to Queen Serissa through Hekaton's two sisters, promising them the throne, and Serissa, desperate for help, agreed to assist Iymrith's experiments. To conceal her involvement, Iymrith rebranded the Cult of the Dragon remnants as the Kraken Society, giving them the fiction of a new powerful patron — a Kraken — to serve as patsy and muscle. Thistle concludes that the Kraken Society is therefore a rebranded Cult of the Dragon operating under Iymrith's direction. Unla Kee ultimately concedes the theory sounds about right, though he had initially tried to dismiss it out of self-interest in acquiring such a dragon's power. The party decides to sail straight to the northern isles of the Trackless Sea in response.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee participates actively in the conspiracy discussion. He studied at the Nirany School of Magic, where Krakens were covered as legendary creatures rather than real ones. He recalls learning from a professor called Prof BigEyes that Krakens are Leviathans of Legend who served as warriors of the gods at the beginning of time, shrugged off their servitude after the gods' wars ended, and now act as primeval forces obliterating civilization. Prof BigEyes had also noted that when such creatures interact with the world, societies and cults spring up to worship them. Unla Kee contributes the detail that Krakens communicate via telepathy over a limited range of about 100 feet. When Thistle proposes that a dragon is behind everything, Unla Kee initially tries to undermine the theory and advocate for talking to the dragon rather than killing it, motivated by a desire to obtain the dragon's power for himself. He suggests the party could ask the Seer for guidance, which Vardis and Orsik shut down. He also asks whether he could join the Kraken Society, then recoils when reminded of their remote brain-exploding device. He ultimately concedes Thistle's theory sounds correct. Before departure, he asks Pow whether the casino can be rigged so he wins, and remains unconvinced by Pow's explanation that this would cause the house to lose money.

## Lord Khaspere Drylund

Lord Khaspere Drylund does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Thistle in his analysis. Thistle asserts that Drylund and Lady Atalia are not the kind of people with the resources to steal a giant and hide him on an untrackable ship, using this reasoning to argue that a more powerful entity — a mythical creature or a dragon — must be behind the operation.

## Lady Atalia

Lady Atalia does not appear in this chunk but is referenced alongside Lord Khaspere Drylund. Thistle dismisses both as lacking the resources to have orchestrated the kidnapping of a giant and concealment of a ship that cannot be scried, supporting his argument for a more powerful hidden actor.

## Prof BigEyes

Prof BigEyes does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Unla Kee as a professor at the Nirany School of Magic. Prof BigEyes taught on the subject of legendary creatures and is quoted as describing Krakens as primeval forces that obliterate civilization's greatest achievements as if they were castles in the sand, and as having observed that when such creatures interact with the world, societies and cults spring up to worship them.

## Queen Serissa

Queen Serissa does not appear in this chunk but is referenced extensively in Thistle's conspiracy theory. According to Thistle's account, Serissa intervened after the death of Queen Nerissa to convince King Hekaton to calm down, research the situation, and not go to war. After Hekaton was subsequently kidnapped, Serissa lacked the credibility to hold the Giant world in order without him. Iymrith then gained an introduction to Serissa through Hekaton's two sisters, who promised Serissa the throne, and Serissa — desperate for support — agreed to assist Iymrith's experiments on fairies.

## King Hekaton

King Hekaton does not appear in this chunk but is central to Thistle's conspiracy theory. According to Thistle, Iymrith approached Hekaton with an offer of help during the Giant war, which Hekaton refused. After Queen Nerissa was killed, Hekaton was persuaded by Serissa not to retaliate immediately. Iymrith then arranged for Hekaton to be kidnapped and hidden on a ship that cannot be scried, removing him from power so that Serissa would be left without credibility to maintain order in the Giant world.

## Queen Nerissa

Queen Nerissa does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Thistle's conspiracy theory as a victim. Thistle theorizes that Iymrith arranged for Nerissa's assassination, hoping that Hekaton would respond by going to war against smaller peoples and thus provide Iymrith with a useful conflict to exploit. The plan failed because Serissa convinced Hekaton to restrain himself.

## Eigeron

Eigeron does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Thistle's conspiracy theory. Thistle states that Iymrith had originally hoped to use Eigeron as part of her plan to start a Giant war, but that Eigeron was killed by his own father, Blagothkus, derailing that approach and forcing Iymrith to pivot to a new strategy.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Thistle's conspiracy theory as Eigeron's father. Thistle describes him as an "idiot father" who killed his own son Eigeron, inadvertently disrupting Iymrith's original plan.

## Tiamat

Tiamat does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Thistle's conspiracy theory. Thistle identifies Tiamat as the instigator of the War of the Dragons, motivated by a desire to enter the world. The war tore up the North and, according to Thistle, provided Iymrith with the financial resources to begin capturing fairies. After the First Faction defeated Tiamat, Iymrith lost that sponsorship and was forced to seek a new patron.

## Bob

Bob does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in conversation between Pow and Orsik. Pow describes Bob as a famous actor who headlined a theater group, always performing in his demon costume, whose shows Pow attended multiple times in Yartar. Pow had heard a rumor that the theater burned down and Bob disappeared, but Orsik corrects this: the theater did burn down, but Bob has not disappeared and has instead chosen to take a leave of absence. Pow expresses enthusiasm about using the saved script from Bob's play to draw crowds to the casino, unaware — or unconcerned — that Vardis quietly reminds Thistle the script is a summoning ritual for a demon that the party barely averted.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta is present on the flying castle. When Thistle offers to do something for her, she blushes, causing all control gems to pulsate red. She requests that the grass on the tower and the tower itself be cleaned, and also asks for gold trim on the door with a nameplate. Thistle relays both requests to Pow to add to his work list. At the end of the session, Escarlotta sets the course for the Northern Isles of the Trackless Sea as the party departs.

## Pow

Pow is present throughout and takes an active role in planning the castle's renovation. He has hired a crew of ten laborers, all appearing to be of Shou origin, paying double the standard rate of three silver pieces per laborer due to the unusual nature of working on a flying castle, with total startup costs estimated around 1,000 gold. He explains that making the castle a serviceable casino would require 50,000 gold, while a full grand casino would cost approximately 500,000 gold, with the intention of reinvesting profits over time. He reacts with appropriate horror when he realizes the play script Thistle mentions is a demon summoning ritual. He is also the one who informs the group that he knows some people who could supply Unla Kee and Thistle time to purchase magic items, allowing a few days' delay before departure. When Unla Kee asks whether the casino can be rigged so he wins, Pow patiently explains that this would cause the house to lose money.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this chunk but is referenced twice in Thistle's memories. During the earlier Nether Mountains quest, Uncle Joon noted that the mountains were full of orcs and that the eastern peaks were home to blue dragons chased out of the northern Anauroch desert. Separately, Thistle recalls that Uncle Joon never mentioned anything about the Kraken Society, though Krakens were famous in the Feywild.

## Orsik

Orsik is present during the conspiracy discussion and reacts to events. He is described as having been on the earlier Nether Mountains quest with Thistle and was intrigued at the time by legends of lost Netherese magic. In the current scene, he is suspicious of Thistle's claim that nothing of value was found and asks about treasure. He also shuts down Unla Kee's suggestion to ask the Seer for guidance. He clarifies to Pow that Bob has not disappeared but has taken a leave of absence.

## Vardis

Vardis is present and participates minimally but meaningfully. He asks whether the group is going to Everlund, which Thistle negates in favor of heading to the Northern Isles of the Trackless Sea. He splutters when the Seer is mentioned as a potential resource. He quietly reminds Thistle that the play script is a demon summoning ritual when Pow expresses enthusiasm about using it for the casino.
