## Azam 10-02 Taraskh

Azam appears in a back room of the Golden Tankard alongside the pseudo-dragon Hsing, acting as intermediary and representative of the Lord's Alliance and the SEER. When Thistle demands more than the offered reward, Azam is left speechless by the audacity. He is repeatedly rescued from difficult conversational moments by Hsing, who intervenes when Azam loses his temper and shouts at Thistle for questioning Hsing's honor and the SEER's credibility, prompting Hsing to freeze him in place and order him to sit down.

Once composure is restored, Azam takes an active role in explaining the mission: the party must travel a tenday through the Forgotten Forest to reach the Ettin Gralm's realm in the Lonely Moor. Gralm has been hunting Fairies and is being pressured by Bad Fruul to join an army intended to destroy Parnast. The Lord's Alliance wants the party to deliver two magical contracts — one requiring Gralm not to align with Bad Fruul, and a preferred contract establishing a full alliance against Bad Fruul. Azam explains that ettin law is poorly developed and that the contracts carry magical penalties for violations.

Azam negotiates separately with Unla Kee, whispering a specific sum to him that is kept private from the rest of the group. He also belatedly admits he misread his notes, apologizing and offering a potion of healing and a potion of greater healing as a reward owed for the party's conduct during the festival attack — specifically for not putting personal glory over saving villagers and for successfully defeating the attack.

After Hsing teleports away, Azam remains behind in the back room of the Golden Tankard, stating he will wait there while Hsing travels on other missions. His current status is waiting at the Golden Tankard for the party's return.

## Hsing

Hsing is a pseudo-dragon and the apparent agent or voice of the SEER, appearing alongside Azam in the back room of the Golden Tankard. He initially appears to be dozing but raises his head to intervene at a critical moment, offering information on Thistle's missing uncle as the reward for the mission. When Azam loses his temper and shouts at Thistle, Hsing freezes him in place, firmly reprimands him, and orders him to sit down, noting that the adventurers can't distinguish a lizard from a pseudo-dragon and that the Zhentarim associate thought he could be killed — framing this as a reason for patience rather than outrage.

Hsing then speaks on behalf of the SEER, relaying the intelligence about Gralm's realm, his goblin and hobgoblin forces, his fairy hunting, and Bad Fruul's recruitment efforts. He deflects Thistle's skepticism about the SEER's knowledge by asserting that if the SEER has stated the information is valid, it is valid, and by pointedly declining to reveal the SEER's gender.

After the mission briefing is complete, Hsing teleports away. His current whereabouts are unknown; Azam states he is traveling on other missions.

## Gralm

Gralm does not appear directly in this session but is the central subject of the mission briefing delivered by Azam and Hsing. He is an Ettin with a realm in the Lonely Moor, reachable by a tenday journey through the Forgotten Forest. He commands a force of Hobgoblins and Goblins. According to Lord's Alliance intelligence, he has been hunting Fairies for food, and the implication is that this extends beyond mere food shortage. Bad Fruul is actively pressuring him to join an army being assembled to destroy Parnast. The Lord's Alliance wants the party to present him with two magical contracts — preferring the one that would ally Gralm's forces against Bad Fruul — and relies on magical enforcement rather than ettin law, which Azam describes as poorly developed. Gralm's current location is his realm in the Lonely Moor. His decision regarding Bad Fruul's offer is unknown.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul does not appear directly in this session but is referenced repeatedly as the primary antagonist driving the mission. He is assembling an army with the stated intention of destroying Parnast. He is actively recruiting Gralm and his goblinoid forces to this end. Wallace invokes the threat Bad Fruul poses to Parnast and its villagers as the reason Orsik should participate in the mission. The Lord's Alliance's preferred outcome — a full alliance with Gralm — is explicitly framed as opposition to Bad Fruul. Bad Fruul's current location and the scale of his forces beyond Gralm's potential contribution are not discussed.

## Joon (Uncle Joon)

Uncle Joon does not appear directly in this session but is the stated reward for completing the mission: Hsing and Azam claim the SEER has information on Thistle's missing uncle's location, and revealing that location is the compensation offered. Thistle confirms Joon is his uncle. Thistle describes him to the group as an excellent source of intelligence who travels extensively across Faerûn and the North, knows local information wherever he goes, and is the kind of well-informed contact who could help Orsik and Vardis locate their family treasure. Unla Kee seconds this characterization. Thistle cannot recall his uncle ever mentioning the Ettin Gralm. Joon's current whereabouts are unknown to the party but allegedly known to the SEER.

## Mohm

Mohm is a hobgoblin encountered in the Forgotten Forest on the third day of the third tenday of Taraskh. He leads a gang of goblins that surrounds the party when the thunderstorm abruptly stops. He is killed in combat by Thistle, Orsik, and Thuul. His death causes the goblins under his command to panic — they shoot arrows and flee into the forest. No further information about his allegiances, purpose, or background is given. He is deceased.

## Sir Oblivillish

Sir Oblivillish is a sprite encountered at the edge of the Forgotten Forest after the goblin skirmish. He identifies himself by calling out to Thistle, recognizing him as the nephew of Uncle Joon. He was formerly a member of Prince Thornacious' court but was cursed and expelled after he killed a stag that the Prince considered his own, having gone two days without food. The curse stripped him of his natural invisibility: instead of being able to turn invisible at will, he now involuntarily cycles between visible and invisible states in a broken, non-functional manner, which he demonstrates for the group. He describes this as a source of deep shame for a sprite.

He subsequently left Thornacious' court, though he frames this as being sent into "double exile" — first from the Royal Court (Queen Tatiana having exiled Thornacious, with his court compelled to follow), and then from Thornacious' court itself over what he calls a terrible misunderstanding. He is contemptuous of Thornacious, whom he calls a pompous fool for pretending his exile from the Royal Court was voluntary.

Thistle identifies him as a sprite from Prince Thornacious' court who serves as a spy for the Zhentarim. Orsik, upon rereading a Zhentarim notice, realizes Sir Oblivillish is the spy he is supposed to protect. Orsik had been considering killing him before Wallace prompted him to reread the notice. Sir Oblivillish's current status is present at the forest edge, newly recognized by the party as their charge.

---

## Prince Thornacious

Prince Thornacious does not appear directly in this session but is referenced by Sir Oblivillish. He is described as a pompous fool who was sent into exile by Queen Tatiana and pretends it was his own choice. He cursed Sir Oblivillish for killing a stag he considered his own game. He compelled his entire court to follow him into exile, though Sir Oblivillish notes the Queen could have sent him alone. The party also recalls from a prior session that Thornacious was possessive about his hunting grounds and directed them to go elsewhere. His current location is his exiled court, somewhere in or near the Forgotten Forest.

---

## Queen Tatiana

Queen Tatiana does not appear in this session and is only briefly referenced by Sir Oblivillish. She is identified as the authority who sent Prince Thornacious into exile and required his court to follow. No further details about her, her realm, or her motivations are provided.
