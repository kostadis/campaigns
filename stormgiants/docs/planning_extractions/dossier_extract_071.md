## Adimond Kroskas

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced repeatedly. He is identified as Thistle's arch nemesis and the one responsible for giving Thistle dragon eyes and the True Seeing ability through magical manipulation. Thistle emotionally declares "Adimond Kroskas is messing with me again" and sobs that he is "not one of his freakish experiments." Queen Serissa references him as the target of Thistle's revenge, framing the recovery of King Hekaton and vengeance against Kroskas as linked goals. The cloud giants Blagothkus and Escarlotta are described as horrified when Thistle recounts his involvement. No further details about Kroskas' current location or plans are revealed in this chunk.

## Queen Serissa

Appears at the opening of the session in what appears to be the Maelstrom. She observes Thistle's distress over his dragon eyes with bemusement, squinting to see the tiny eyeballs and telling him they are "quite fetching." She attempts to redirect the group to matters of importance — specifically saving her father King Hekaton — and holds up a wooden coin bearing a golden goose, which she identifies as evidence from a mage's information. She references working with Ilymrith, the ancient blue dragon known as the Bane or Doom of the Desert, which shocks Blagothkus and Escarlotta when Thistle recounts it later. Sandesyl later confirms Ilymrith has been undermining the giant world to gain access to the Storm Giant's castle for her hybridization experiments. Serissa's cooperation with Ilymrith is characterized by Blagothkus as foolish.

## Unla Kee

Present throughout the session. Identifies the wooden coin with the golden goose as a gambling chip from a booze cruiser called The Golden Goose in Yartar, and claims he was denied entry — attributing this to racism rather than lack of funds. Advocates strongly for removing Sandesyl from the castle, leading a chant of "What do we want? It's a vampire-free zone. When do we want it? Now!" Expresses moral discomfort with the group's approach, framing their enemies not as evil creatures per se but as racists and bullies, and questioning whether killing is the right solution. When confronting Glazhael, he corrects Orsik's failed greeting attempt and takes over with more flattering language. He explains to the group that a Dracolich's phylactery is the key to controlling it. He ultimately counsels against charging Sandesyl directly, arguing a clever ploy has better odds.

## Blagothkus

Appears in the castle control room. He is physically unable to lower himself far enough to examine Thistle closely, so he offers his hand to bring the fairy to eye level. He declares Thistle's dragon eyes "so cool," which Thistle does not appreciate. He engages in an awkward philosophical defense of segregation between dragons and giants, which Orsik and Unla Kee both characterize as racism. He reveals that the ancient blue dragon Thistle described — Ilymrith — is known as the Bane of the Desert, and is horrified that Serissa was working with her. He urges the group to find King Hekaton to have the strength needed to defeat Ilymrith. He suggests the Vampire situation is not the priority given the urgency of saving the realms, though he is overruled. His relationship with Escarlotta is noted as repaired but still fragile.

## Escarlotta

Present in the castle control room. She speaks to Thistle in a warm, maternal tone about his new powers. She reveals a troubling decision made during the party's absence: with the Vampire needing to be fed, she and Blagothkus directed Sandesyl to feed on a tribe of Uthgars, reasoning the party disliked them. The control room's moonstones turn red when this is revealed, suggesting embarrassment or magical resonance. She implies a veiled threat to Unla Kee when he pushes back on the moral ambiguity, gesturing to the open sky visible through the control room. She steers the conversation back to asking what happened at the Maelstrom.

## Sandesyl

Encountered in her tower in the middle of the day, hidden within a magical shroud of darkness. She offers Thistle the chance to become a fey vampire spawn, which he rejects. She proposes a quest: retrieve a magical tome from Xonathal's Keep that was lost during Severin's failed ritual to bring Tiamat into the world. She explains the tome's history — Severin offered a Blue Dragon mask to a traditionalist faction member to investigate it, needed the book for that investigation, but the tower was raided by the Heroes of the War of the Dragon after inner circle member Iskander betrayed the cult to the Lord's Alliance. The heroes took the mask but missed the tome. Sandesyl wants the tome for herself. She reveals her long-term plan: to leave the castle, establish a dark lair, study cult lore, and re-establish rituals interrupted by Severin, whom she considers a usurper. Her theological position is that salvation comes not from Tiamat entering the world but from all dragons being transformed into dracoliches to rule Faerûn. She expresses passionate, almost romantic devotion to Ilymrith and hopes to use the tome to convince Ilymrith to embrace dracolich immortality. She acknowledges she cannot control the castle because Escarlotta is immune to her charms, forcing her into arrangements with others. She holds Glazhael's phylactery and uses it as leverage. One of her spawns is present, hidden in the darkness, and rolls his eyes at her — an expression only Thistle can see with his True Seeing.

## Iskander

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Sandesyl's account of the tome's history. He is described as an inner circle member of the Cult of the Dragon who betrayed Severin and the cult to the Lord's Alliance, enabling the Heroes of the War of the Dragon to raid Xonathal's tower. The heroes secured the Blue Dragon mask but missed the tome Sandesyl seeks.

## Severin

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced throughout. He is described as the leader of the Cult of the Dragon who attempted to bring Tiamat into the world. Sandesyl regards him as a usurper whose theology was wrong. He declared Talis the new White Wyrmspeaker after the previous one was captured. He attempted to rally a fractured cult by offering the Blue Dragon mask to a traditionalist faction for investigation. His ritual ultimately failed. Glazhael references him as the one who declared that a Dracolich could not serve the cult, which hurt and alienated Glazhael.

## Ilymrith

Does not appear in this chunk but is discussed extensively. She is an ancient blue dragon known as the Bane of the Desert and the Doom of the Desert. Blagothkus and Escarlotta are horrified to hear Serissa was working with her. Sandesyl speaks of her with intense passion and romantic obsession, describing her as "one of the greatest of dragon kind." Sandesyl's stated long-term goal is to persuade Ilymrith to become a dracolich. Sandesyl reveals that Ilymrith has been working to undermine the giant world not out of care for giants but to gain access to the Storm Giant's castle to continue her hybridization experiments — on both dragons and fey. This strongly implies Ilymrith is behind what was done to Thistle through Adimond Kroskas.

## Talis

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Glazhael's account. She was Glazhael's partner and the White Wyrmspeaker before being replaced by Severin's appointee. After Glazhael was killed by the Heroes of the War of the Dragon at the Tomb of Diderius and resurrected as a Dracolich by Jorgen, Glazhael returned to Talis rather than serve Jorgen. Talis sent Glazhael to spy on a settlement in the north and later was involved in a plot to murder the First Faction. She is described as "no more" following the destruction of the cult and the grand alliance's victory.

## Jorgen

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Glazhael's account. He performed the arcane ritual in Xonathal's tower that transformed the recently dead Glazhael into a Dracolich, using the power of the Blue Dragon mask in an unconventional application. Normally the ritual requires a living dragon; Jorgen found a way around this. He was an enemy of Talis, which meant Glazhael — loyal to Talis — turned against him after the resurrection.

## Glazhael the Undying

Encountered in the glacier vault beneath Blagothkus' castle, clinging to the ceiling above a massive treasure hoard as a vast white dracolich. She recounts her history: she was formerly Glazhael the Cloudchaser, partner to Talis, and made a non-aggression pact with the Heroes of the War of the Dragon at their first encounter. When called back to service after the White Wyrmspeaker's capture, she was killed at the Tomb of Diderius by the dwarf Stroud using a bow. She was subsequently resurrected as a Dracolich by Jorgen in Xonathal's tower using the Blue Dragon mask. She returned to Talis, was rejected from the cult by Severin who declared dracoliches could not serve, and was sent north to spy. She encountered the First Faction again at Mkoz glacier and was deceived by them with false information about the Blue Dragon mask's location. To clear her dishonor she joined a plot to kill the First Faction and was defeated again. She is now bound to Sandesyl, who holds her phylactery. She is furious about this subjugation and agrees to a deal with the party: she will join the fight against Sandesyl, but only once the party has nearly won, striking at the moment of weakness. She claims to know how to kill the vampire but refuses to share this secret, dismissing the group with "Now go."

## Stroud

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Glazhael's account as the dwarf member of the First Faction (Heroes of the War of the Dragon) who struck Glazhael down with a bow at the Tomb of Diderius, killing her for the first time.
