## Vardis

Vardis appears throughout the session as a cleric of Corellon, beginning in the aftermath of battle in the hallway near the guestrooms. He witnesses Thuul breathe destruction and mutters about the fairy's creature, urging Thistle to be kept alive so Thuul can act again. He is hiding in a doorway to the guestroom while his wall of fire burns down the hallway, separating him from Orsik who is fighting two giants alone.

He reflects internally on Orsik's changed demeanor since gaining intelligence — that Orsik now resents his career as a fighter and may have a death wish. He uses a Fireball to incinerate the Frost Giant and injure the Cloud Giant, choosing this spell deliberately based on his theological history: in Waterdeep, he had heretical views at the Temples of Corellon about clerical responsibilities, which made him persona non-grata. He had debated how Flamestrike was weakened after the sundering era when Ao reduced its power, which led him to follow the light aspect of Corellon and use Fireballs instead.

He directs Thuul's attention and urges the guard Drake to help the downed Thistle. He uses Healing Word to wake Thistle. He casts Flame Strike (described as lances of pure flame) against Count Vaal. He blinks the wall of fire out of existence once the cloud giant is defeated and observes a bloodied Orsik standing among the corpses of the giant lords, noting it is hard to tell if Orsik is happy or sad.

After the battle, Vardis urges the party to move the giant bodies out of sight, which Orsik initially bristles at before recognizing it as the right call. Vardis stares at Orsik with barely suppressed laughter when Orsik explains the suitor-matchmaker cover story. He is described as sad and concerned when Orsik proposes a full frontal assault, helping to convince him to abandon that plan. He joins the long rest in the guest chambers.

In the underwater approach to the throne room, Vardis is noted by Unla Kee to be present in the portal chamber pool, and Orsik stares at Vardis after discovering the treasure has been taken by crabs. His last known position is underwater in the sinkhole area with the rest of the party.

## Orsik

Orsik survives the battle against the giant lords with grim satisfaction mixed with bitter regret at still being a fighter. He loots the bodies systematically: from Count Vaal he takes a diamond-studded platinum earring, a vest of platinum embroidery, a teleportation conch, and a potion of water breathing; from Duke Tartha (the Fire Giant) a necklace of gilded dragon's teeth and another conch; from Frost Giant Jarl Hellenhild a cloak of white dragon scales and another conch; from Stone Giant Thane Braxow 15 gemstones embedded in his flesh — totaling approximately 12,000 gold pieces.

He leads the effort to drag the bodies north past the guestrooms into the storeroom just in time, as Mirran descends the staircase to investigate the silence. He hides and overhears Mirran conclude the giant lords simply returned to their homelands, and overhears Nin shouting at Mirran about loose ends.

During the long rest planning session, Orsik demonstrates his increased intelligence by summarizing the political situation clearly: Serissa is the current Queen, undermined by sisters Mirran and Nym; Serissa believes her father is alive and may have useful information; Serissa's mother had sought accommodation with small folk. His plan had been to pose as a suitor Serissa found through a Waterdeep matchmaker. He proposes a full frontal assault, which Vardis, Thistle, and Unla Kee all reject, and he backs down after Vardis points out not everyone has a death wish.

Underwater in the sinkhole area, Orsik swims to a tunnel on the floor and discovers that treasure from the ships has been stolen by crabs, which he intends to kill. His last known position is underwater in the sinkhole tunnels.

## Thistle

Thistle begins the session unconscious, having been knocked out during the fight with the giant lords. Thuul's breath weapon was contingent on Thistle being alive, making Thistle's revival urgent. Drake the guard is directed to help Thistle. Vardis uses Healing Word to wake Thistle. Once conscious, Thistle moans and then participates in the subsequent combat, with magical arrows striking Count Vaal.

During planning, Thistle flatly says no to Orsik's full frontal assault proposal. Thistle then takes the initiative by casting Pass Without Trace and sneaking alone upstairs to the Great Hall to scout.

In the Great Hall, Thistle finds only two bored hill giant guards instead of the crowd of giant lords from the previous day. He seizes an opportunity when Nym storms off toward the throne room, flying close to the ceiling and slipping past the guards invisibly. He enters the throne room amphitheater, observing the 50-foot-deep seawater pool at its center and the three great thrones, including the obsidian throne floating above the floor. He tries to recall what Joon said about the throne but cannot remember.

He observes and listens to the full throne room confrontation between Serissa, Nym, Mirran, Imperator Uthor, and Adminond Kroskas. He notices Adminond Kroskas and is tempted to strike but restrains himself, recognizing this as the wrong time. He observes that Adminond casually directs the sisters in their arguments and signals when to act, identifying Adminond and the two sisters as allies. He overhears Adminond reveal an ongoing experiment to create a dragon-fairy hybrid force loyal to Serissa. He mutters, "I like it when the bad guys tell us their plans."

Thistle connects Adminond's presence to an escape route: he recalls Joon's information that the Maelstrom has interconnected tunnels and concludes that Adminond's exit strategy is the pool of water in the throne room. He reports his intelligence back to the group, informing them that the great enemy is in the throne room, which triggers the party's debate about approach. He again opposes a frontal assault. His last known position is with the party approaching through the underwater waterways.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is described as enjoying swimming, particularly when he can breathe underwater. He strikes the killing blow on Count Vaal during the battle, using multiple blasts of power — noted as not the easy option (a single magic missile) that Vardis anticipated, but effective nonetheless. He also uses a psychic lance earlier in the fight, surrounding the cloud giant's head with shimmering energy and causing it to scream in agony and float wobbling in the air.

During the planning debate, to Orsik's surprise Unla Kee does not wait for the debate to conclude — he is described as already running toward the stairs to confront the castle full of storm giants before being stopped. Despite this impulsiveness, the group agrees on the underwater approach through the waterways.

In the portal chamber, the party enters a pool leading to an underwater portcullis intended for much larger creatures. Unla Kee squeezes through the portcullis, barely avoiding barnacle cuts. He arrives at the Maelstrom itself — a half-mile-wide whirlpool — and internally monologues with grandiose self-satisfaction about its power, describing a 100-foot-deep, 180-foot-wide sinkhole at the ocean's bottom with a 50-foot-diameter drain covered by an iron grate with 10-foot-wide gaps crusted in barnacles. He fantasizes about mastering and owning the sinkhole's power.

His reverie is disrupted by Orsik swimming toward a tunnel on the floor. Unla Kee decides he cannot let Orsik have all the fun with the crab-guarded treasure. His last known position is in the sinkhole area underwater.

## Count Vaal

Count Vaal does not appear alive in this chunk; he is present as a combatant and then a corpse. During the battle, he strikes Orsik with his morning star, causing a crunch that Vardis overhears and fears for Orsik's life. Orsik taunts him after the blow, complimenting his style. Vardis uses Flame Strike against Count Vaal. Unla Kee delivers the killing blows with blasts of power.

His body is looted by Orsik: a diamond-studded platinum earring, a vest of platinum embroidery, a teleportation conch, and a potion of water breathing are taken from him. His body is then dragged by Orsik to the storeroom to conceal the evidence of the battle.

## Thane Braxow

Thane Braxow does not appear alive in this chunk. He is identified as the Stone Giant Thane and is present only as a corpse. Orsik extracts 15 gemstones that were embedded in his flesh. His body is dragged to the storeroom.

## Mirran

Mirran descends the staircase to the great hall area to investigate why no noise is coming from below and no giant lords are arriving to celebrate. She enters the Portal Chamber and, finding it empty, concludes aloud that the giants killed the small folk and returned to their homelands to spread rumors about Serissa. She does not discover the hidden party.

Later in the throne room confrontation observed by Thistle, Mirran speaks soothingly, noting that she and Nym agreed the visitors were assassins and had them dealt with. She pouts and stamps her foot, insisting they were not suitors but cheaters and assassins, and argues to the Imperator that she and Nym should be Queen rather than Serissa. She is described as an ally of Adminond, with the blue dragon directing her arguments. Her last known position is in or near the throne room.

## Nym

Nym is referenced in two contexts. First, Orsik overhears Nin (Nym) screaming at Mirran about loose ends after Mirran concludes the party must be dead. Second, in the throne room observed by Thistle, Nym storms away from the living organ, slams her fist into it, and declares she must speak to the Queen — providing Thistle his opportunity to slip through. In the throne room, Nym confronts Serissa aggressively: she mocks Serissa for supposedly seeking a dwarf suitor through a Waterdeep matchmaker, comparing it unfavorably even to their mother's interest in small folk, and references matchmaking services "Ok Ogre" and "The Short for the Tall." She also states bluntly that the Frost and Fire giants eat small folk and that the visitors are "well digested," causing Serissa to squirm. She is described as an ally of Adminond. Her last known position is the throne room.

## Serissa

Serissa does not appear in person until the throne room scene observed by Thistle. She is described in extensive detail: a storm giant in her equivalent twenties, over 20 feet tall, with silver-blue hair, stormy-sea-colored eyes, pale luminescent blue skin, wearing a gown of shifting cloud-like fabrics adorned with gold and silver lightning patterns, a crown of enchanted silver with glowing gems, and a necklace of pearls and sapphires holding storm magic. Her voice is like rolling thunder but with a softness suggesting compassion.

Thistle concludes there is a reason she and not her sisters is Queen. In the throne room, she snarls that the party members are liars when her sisters claim they were assassins. She attempts to rise from her throne in anger — apparently in response to Mirran arguing she and Nym should be Queen — but Imperator Uthor grabs her arm and forces her to restrain herself. She waves her hand at Adminond's report on the dragon-fairy hybrid experiment and states she knows about the experiments but that finding her father would make them unnecessary; she says she needs someone she can trust to search for him. Orsik's earlier briefing to the party notes that Serissa believes her father is still alive and may have information the party needs, and that her mother was interested in accommodation with small folk. Her last known position is the throne room, seated on the obsidian floating throne.

## Imperator Uthor

Imperator Uthor is a middle-aged Storm Giant observed by Thistle in the throne room, seated to Serissa's right. He has steel-gray hair tied back, piercing stormy gray eyes with a perpetual look of condescension toward Serissa, deeper blue skin marked with scars, and wears a deep blue and silver tunic with a belt bearing an ornate rank buckle. He wields a trident crackling with latent electricity. His voice is deep and resonant with authority and barely disguised irritation.

In the throne room scene, he coughs and begins to interject when Nym is attacking Serissa, then physically grabs Serissa's arm to force her to restrain her temper when she attempts to rise. He sighs about the searches for Serissa's father, saying many have been sent and the clues provided have gotten nowhere. He tells Adminond in a voice of barely disguised irritation to direct Mirran and Nym to confirm the events regarding the supposed assassins. When Adminond suggests a change in leadership might be appropriate if the Queen cannot restrain her temper, Uthor sharply rebukes him: "Do not confuse your privilege with the right to speak about these matters."

Thistle observes that the Imperator prefers having Serissa as Queen precisely because she is a young ruler he can manipulate. His last known position is the throne room.

---

## Adminond Kroskas

Adminond Kroskas — identified as a giant blue dragon — is observed by Thistle in the throne room seated to Serissa's left. Thistle is tempted to strike upon seeing him but restrains himself. Thistle recognizes him as a great enemy and notes that this blue dragon has survived long enough to always have an exit strategy; Thistle concludes the pool of water in the throne room is Adminond's escape route, connecting this to Joon's information about the Maelstorm's interconnected tunnels. Thistle also notes that Adminond survived the collapse of the oracle.

In the throne room, Adminond freezes when he pieces together that the visitors were not a random group but the 3HPP, and immediately asks whether the bodies were found. When Nym dismisses this by saying the Frost and Fire giants eat small folk, Adminond does not press further publicly. He reports to Serissa that his experiment to create a dragon-fairy hybrid — a force loyal to her that would guarantee the Storm Giants' position in the Ordning — is proceeding. He addresses Mirran and Nym soothingly when tensions escalate, calling them "my dearest." When Serissa's temper flares, Adminond suggests to Uthor that a change in leadership might be appropriate if the Queen cannot restrain herself, before retreating to the position of "just an alchemist doing the Queen's bidding" when Uthor rebukes him.

Thistle observes that Adminond casually directs the two sisters in their arguments and signals when to act, confirming that Adminond and the sisters are allied against Serissa. His last known position is the throne room.

## Drake

Drake is a guard present in or near the guestroom during the battle's aftermath. Vardis notices Thuul trying to figure out what to do and points to the downed Thistle, urging Drake to help. Drake mumbles and agrees. No further action from Drake is recorded in this chunk.

## Thuul

Thuul is described as "the fairy's creature" — apparently bound in some way to Thistle. During the battle, Thuul breathes out "death and destruction," astonishing Vardis who mutters about the fairy's creature. Vardis wants Thuul to do it again but recognizes that Thistle must be conscious for Thuul to act. After Thistle falls unconscious, Thuul is observed by Vardis trying to figure out what to do. Vardis directs Thuul toward Thistle and urges Drake to assist. No further action from Thuul is recorded in this chunk.

## Joon

Joon does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully referenced twice by Thistle. First, upon seeing the obsidian floating throne with dragon skulls, Thistle tries to recall what Joon said about it but cannot remember. Second, Thistle recalls Joon's information that the Maelstrom has a series of interconnected tunnels — this recollection is what leads Thistle to deduce that Adminond's escape route is the pool of water in the throne room. Joon's prior intelligence about the Maelstorm's structure proves directly useful to the party's tactical planning.
