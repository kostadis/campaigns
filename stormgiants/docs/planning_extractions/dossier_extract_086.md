## Iymrith

Iymrith does not appear directly in these chapters but is extensively referenced as the primary antagonist whose lair serves as the location for the upcoming battle.

Thistle informs King Hekaton that traitors among giants are gathering at a known location, and separately tells Imperator Serissa that Iymrith will be present at that gathering. The revelation that Iymrith will be there is what motivates Serissa to commit the palace guard to the fight.

When the 3HPP negotiates with Bob, it is revealed that Iymrith has created two zombie blue chromatic dragons hybridized with purple worms, described as unique creations no other planet has. Bob becomes excited at the prospect of attending what appears to be Iymrith's grand reveal of these creations before her patrons — a gathering of powerful, deadly creatures. The plan takes shape around using Bob, polymorphed into a dragon, to infiltrate this event and publicly humiliate and disgrace Iymrith before her allies when the fraud is discovered.

In Silverymoon, Thistle attributes the disappearance of the 200 notable citizens and the broader giant civil war chaos to Iymrith, presenting a feather as a relic from her lair. Rebecca Deakin, the sage from Wyrmworks, refers to Iymrith as "a legendary dragon of unsurpassed power." Lord Aerasumé conditionally agrees to provide aid if Iymrith is confirmed as the power behind the disruptions to his kingdom, and privately acknowledges that the death of Iymrith would be a good thing for Silverymoon.

The House Margaster is implied to have allied with Iymrith, though Thistle's delivery of this information is unconvincing to Lord Aerasumé.

Iymrith's lair is established as the location for the final battle, set to occur in five days, with King Hekaton committing his forces and Serissa pledging the palace guard.

## King Hekaton

Hekaton appears in the throne room of the Maelstrom when the 3HPP return via the conch. He is initially dismissive and domineering, insisting on speaking first and drowning out Thistle's urgent news. He expresses pleasure that the party survived, remarks on Orsik's apparent desire for a heroic death, and only after significant disruption does he engage with the intelligence about traitors gathering at Iymrith's lair.

His emotional state is volatile throughout the scene. He initially claims his hands are tied regarding the traitors, which provokes Orsik's blunt rebuke. When Serissa erupts, Hekaton responds with fury at being challenged, asserting his authority as king. He is ultimately brought up short by his own guilt — Orsik attempts to soothe him, observing that Hekaton feels guilty for his vengeance quest and the chaos it caused. However, the guilt and rage are described as too overwhelming for Orsik's words to fully reach him.

Hekaton finds a face-saving exit by asking Unla Kee, recognizing him as a diviner, to determine whether to strike the lair now or continue his existing plan of hiring agents to kill enemies one by one. When Unla Kee recommends the full assault, Hekaton agrees immediately. He asks if they attack now, and accepts Orsik's proposal of a five-day delay to allow further preparations. The battle is set to take place at Iymrith's lair.

Hekaton is portrayed as a quasi-deity in scale and power, whose anger in the moment is directed not at the party or Serissa but at his own bad choices.

## Imperator Serissa

Serissa is present in the throne room of the Maelstrom alongside her father when the 3HPP returns. She is visibly annoyed before the confrontation begins — both Vardis and Orsik notice her displeasure, and Orsik speculates it resembles unresolved feelings about her father. Hekaton does not notice her discomfort.

When Hekaton claims his hands are tied and Orsik challenges him, Serissa unleashes accumulated anger. She articulates specific grievances: that Hekaton declared his two sisters unqualified to be Queen, inadvertently driving them to seek the position through other means; that he abandoned his post and went on a rage-filled vengeance quest when the Ordning shattered rather than remaining as king; that no one believed her when she said her father was alive; and that her father is not the giant she remembers. She also invokes Thistle's sacrifice of setting aside his search for his love to save the world, contrasting it with Hekaton's abandonment.

When Thistle reveals that Iymrith will be present at the gathering, Serissa immediately declares she will take the palace guard and defend their honor, framing Hekaton as a coward by contrast. This triggers another round of screaming between father and daughter.

Serissa's arc in this scene is one of long-suppressed frustration finally expressed, with her father's return having clearly not resolved the damage done by his disappearance.

## Bob (Bob Saget)

Bob appears in Ascore when summoned by Vardis's casting of Planar Ally. He arrives visibly shaken and afraid, his usual arrogance diminished. His first concern is not vengeance against the party but alarm about the geopolitical consequences of their actions.

He explains that the artificer's use of ISDs (Interplanar Storage Devices — defined as non-transient portable magical devices establishing cross-planar connections, such as bags of holding and portable holes) violated the 1489 Treaty on Export Regulation of Interplanar Storage Devices, signed in Sigil between representatives of the Trading House, Chromax, and the Githyanki, Githizeri, and Mindflayers. The treaty exists to regulate ISDs as weapons, as an ISD placed inside another ISD teleports nearby entities — specifically massively powerful entities (MPEs) — to the Astral Plane, where the three empires seek to capture them for their armies. Bob confirms he is an MPE. He warns that the three empires have discovered Toril is in violation and are planning to come to the planet, and that he was trapped near Toril in the Astral Plane and at risk of being captured and turned into a vassal.

Bob is angry at being tricked by what he calls the oldest trick in the book, and annoyed that he failed to check whether Toril was a signatory to the treaty or had prohibitions against ISD use. He is resigned to serving the party.

His demeanor shifts when the party proposes having him perform at Iymrith's grand reveal. The prospect of publicly humiliating Iymrith before the most powerful creatures in the region, while disguised as one of her dragon creations, appeals enormously to him — both as the ultimate joke and as a way to prove to the rest of the Abyss that he is not a joke himself. He reveals that since receiving his new portfolio (demonic powers of jokes, with traditional punishments as backup for resistant souls), the other powers of the Abyss have regarded him as something of a joke. He insists he wants many speaking lines and demands a villain speech before attacking.

When offered two zombie blue chromatic dragons hybridized with purple worms as payment, Bob is immediately excited, asking if the party has an invitation to the event. He accepts the deal.

Bob agrees to free the 200 prisoners from the pocket dimension for 20,000 gold, and is flustered when the party accepts immediately, suggesting the success of his play exceeded his expectations. He threatens to hunt the party down and burn them if the performance does not happen. He asks Orsik about a DNRRR (the specific nature unclear). He summons demons to deal with the two living hybrid dragons himself, refusing to share the stage with other demons for the main battle.

Bob sets the prisoner release to occur the following morning at 9 am at the door of the theater in Silverymoon, at Orsik's request.

Bob is not physically present in Chapter 87 but his arrangement with the party is fulfilled — the 200 guests walk out of a magical door at the former theater site when Thistle speaks the words "Ictum. Antium. Merthium."

## Lord Methrammar Aerasumé

Lord Aerasumé is the current ruler of Silverymoon and son of Lady Alustriel Silverhand. He is introduced through rumors before his appearance — he is described as being under political attack because he was the patron of Bob's play, and his leadership is further undermined by the 3HPP's association with a giant cloud castle during the civil war, the economic deterioration of the region, and the failures of his alliance network.

When the 3HPP arrives at his palace, he is described as haggard and eating breakfast. He immediately confronts them about the disasters they have caused and lists his grievances: 200 notables missing after the theater fire, no memory of the play among witnesses (suggesting magical infernal powers), and the damage to Silverymoon's reputation as a bastion of goodly races.

He becomes cautiously engaged when Thistle offers to return the missing citizens and implicates Iymrith as the power behind both the theater incident and the giant civil war. He is skeptical but politically aware — a courtier's argument that he must be present for the rescue or lose credit for it convinces him to attend. He defers to Taern Hornblade's security concerns.

He is taken aback when Thistle publicly commits him to supporting the 3HPP's mission against Iymrith in front of the crowd outside the theater, but accepts it. He returns with them to the rotunda and ultimately agrees to provide five battle mages (overriding Taern's suggestion of four, whether out of pique or genuine generosity).

In a private conversation with Thistle, he reveals he intends to formally recognize fairies as a goodly race of Everlund in a few months, contingent on the defeat of Iymrith. He admits he is not naïve about what actually happened at the theater — he suspects a dragon was a convenient explanation — but states he is wise enough not to care, as Iymrith's death would benefit Silverymoon regardless.

## Taern Hornblade

Taern Hornblade is identified as a High Mage and hero of the War of the Dragons, and is now present as an advisor to Lord Aerasumé. Thistle recognizes him as someone who was present at the final battle when Tiamat's temple was destroyed, and recalls stories of him as a pompous blowhard.

Taern is openly skeptical of the 3HPP throughout the meeting. He calls their story very suspicious, advocates for cordoning off the theater area before the prisoner release, and suggests himself as an observer to ensure no trap is set. When the party asks for Silverymoon's leading wizards, Taern quotes the saying "Do not meddle in the affairs of wizards, for they are subtle and quick to anger" in a pointed manner. He recommends four battle mages rather than five when Lord Aerasumé offers aid, and is overruled. He states he never liked House Margaster and calls for an investigation into them.

After the successful prisoner release, Taern whispers to Thistle, "Well played fairy," suggesting a grudging respect.

## Rebecca Deakin

Rebecca Deakin is described as a sage from Wyrmworks whom Thistle previously asked to research Iymrith. She is now serving as an advisor to the Lords of Silverymoon. She is present in the rotunda meeting and challenges the party's credibility, calling it "almost absurd" that four adventurers could have discovered anything about a legendary dragon of unsurpassed power, and goes so far as to suggest they themselves might be behind the events. She is not convinced by Orsik's diplomatic counter-argument invoking Everlund but does not press the accusation further after the political dynamics shift.
