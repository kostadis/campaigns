## Grrrk

Grrrk is an elder Galeb Duhr who was part of the great pyramid formation in the Greypeak mountains. He narrates the session from his perspective, noting the party's arrival and their battle with badgers. He spoke with the fairy (Thistle) when she flew up and tickled his nose, sharing information about what he had observed — giants and kobolds in great numbers in the area. He expressed skepticism that the party would survive going to the top of the pyramid, expecting them to be "zapped like the giant." When they successfully descended into the pyramid and disappeared, he expressed quiet approval. When the party departed, Grrrk called out a farewell and then ordered the other Galeb Duhr back to their positions, at which point all of them became invisible again. Grrrk is characterized as gruff, old-fashioned, and dismissive of younger rocks' complaints, contrasting sharply with the more optimistic Prrrt. Last known location: the great pyramid in the Greypeak mountains.

## Prrrt

Prrrt is a younger Galeb Duhr also stationed in the great pyramid formation. Grrrk notes that Prrrt complained about his feet (rock mites), which Grrrk found undignified. When the party spoke with the Galeb Duhr, Prrrt relayed information to them about what a dead giant had said, after the fairy (Thistle) helped treat his mites. Prrrt was optimistic that the party would survive the pyramid, and was proven right. He called out a friendly farewell to the party as they departed, thanking them for dealing with the beetles, and clarified that the treasure they found was not the Galeb Duhr's to claim. Prrrt is characterized as younger, more hopeful, and more sociable than Grrrk. Last known location: the great pyramid in the Greypeak mountains.

## Eden "Gleeful" Gleeson

Eden Gleeson is a man encountered traveling west on the road from the Greypeak mountains toward Llorkh. He had been traveling for several days to attend his own wedding and realized en route that he had forgotten to purchase a wedding gift for his bride. He desperately hailed the party and explained his situation, seeking something to buy. After the party offered several items — a copper necklace, a magnifying glass with no glass, a polished gold piece — Eden settled on a giant silver nose ring, intending to have it fashioned into a necklace. He and Vardis engaged in a haggling exchange, with Vardis opening at 200 GP and eventually agreeing to 150 GP. Eden accepted, calling it a hard bargain. His manservant Cisco intervened to scold him for the impulsive purchase, noting it ate into the entire dessert budget for the wedding. Eden is characterized as sentimental, impulsive, and genuinely devoted to his bride. He is traveling west toward his wedding. Last known location: the road west toward Llorkh.

## Cisco

Cisco is the manservant of Eden Gleeson, traveling with him to the wedding. He is notable for wearing an eyepatch and carrying a small teddy bear in his arms. He stepped forward during Eden's haggling with Vardis to rebuke his employer, warning him that the impulsive purchase of the silver nose ring had consumed the entire dessert budget for the wedding. Cisco appears protective of Eden's finances and perhaps of Eden himself. He is not described as aggressive or hostile, merely exasperated. Last known location: the road west toward Llorkh, traveling with Eden Gleeson.

## Captain Jolanth

Jolanth is a captain of the Guards of the Iron Circle, stationed at the entrance to Llorkh. He has been in Llorkh long enough to have survived the Cult of the Dragon period and the decline of Zhentarim traffic through the town. He considered leaving but stayed due to good pay and favorable weather. He stopped the party at the entrance to town, announced his role as protector of Llorkh's peace, and warned that those who disrupted the peace would be sent to the keep. He attempted to project authority but was outmaneuvered by Orsik, who reframed Jolanth's proclamation as a promise of free protection for the party. Jolanth was flustered and unable to recover before the party walked past him. He is characterized as officious and fond of lording authority over weaker-seeming visitors, but easily wrong-footed. Last known location: the entrance road to Llorkh.

## Diego Malfoy

Diego Malfoy is a guard of the Iron Circle in Llorkh. He did not appear during the party's entry into town but emerged after the incident with the ghostly bartender. He was among the guards who fled the tavern when the bartender's true nature was provoked. Once Orsik came out, Diego confronted him in a panic, screaming that no one ever tells the bartender he is a ghost. He then recognized Orsik and Vardis by name and reputation, calling out that they had previously sought employment with the guards and failed to show up sober on their first day. He directed Vardis to the local store — the Lionel Coster outpost — when asked where to find a scroll of dispel magic. Diego is characterized as exasperated and familiar with the party's prior incompetence. Last known location: outside the tavern in Llorkh.
