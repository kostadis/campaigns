## Wallace

Wallace appears in this session as a young man accompanying Orsik's group into the Marsh of Chelimber. He is the son of a figure in Parnast who understands survival and has connections to the Lord's Alliance. His internal narration reveals that his father anticipated the Lord's Alliance expanding to take control of the Black Road following the defeat of the Cult of the Dragon and chaos in the Zhentarim, and either sent the group on this quest at the Alliance's direction or planned to claim credit for it.

Wallace knew about Ambush Gap before the group entered it — he notes it is a prized piece of real estate for gangs, used by caravans trying to avoid paying Zhentarim fees on the Black Road through Parnast — but Orsik dismissed him as a young boy who knows nothing and never asked for his knowledge. Wallace privately resents this. When the Bugbear ambush occurs exactly where he expected, he feels vindicated but says nothing.

During the battle, Wallace observes that Orsik threw his sword at the Bugbears and considers him underwhelming. After the battle, he notes that the Bugbears had very little money on them despite clearly working for someone. He considers speaking up but decides to stay quiet, survive, and find Orsik's Zhentarim contact independently to offer the location of the treasure himself. He also observes a more experienced party member whispering to Orsik about not announcing looting before actually looting bodies.

## Feetor

Feetor is a mud mephit encountered in the Marsh of Chelimber. He is hiding in a clump of vine-covered trees when the party defeats the water weird (the "grabby water monster"). He had been weighing his allegiances — the half-ogres under Mama Manyknuckles had left, and the water weird was powerful but immortal, reforming within a few days of destruction anywhere in the marsh. After watching the party defeat the water weird using a power that made it visible and unable to hide, Feetor decides to align with them as the new strongest power in the marsh.

He steps out of hiding, claps his hands, flings mud, and introduces himself. He delivers a pre-planned speech claiming the party freed him from years of captivity by the grabby water monster, then declares that mud mephits serve whoever is toughest and that his vote is the party. He privately notes the speech worked.

Feetor provides the party with information about the marsh's power structure: the water weird is immortal and ancient, reforms in a few days, and likely considers the whole swamp its lair. Mama Manyknuckles is the leader of a band of half-ogres living deeper in the marsh. He describes her as particularly ugly. He explains there is a civil war among the mephits, with some supporting Mama Manyknuckles and some the water weird. He argues that if the party kills Mama Manyknuckles, peace will follow when all mephits consolidate behind the water weird.

Feetor does not know the location of the water weird's lair. He is evasive when Orsik tries to get tactical advice about attacking. When Orsik attempts to ask him about mud mephits generally, Feetor pushes back indignantly, accusing him of tokenizing. However, when Orsik explains he wanted the other mephits to align with the party, Feetor pivots immediately and enthusiastically, demanding a gold coin as a token of their power so he can position himself as the party's number-one mud mephit. He receives the coin from Orsik and Vardis and disappears into the swamp. He returns reporting that the other mephits have agreed to wait and see who lives and who dies.

Later, Feetor approaches Unla Kee and reveals he has figured the Halfling out. He offers Unla Kee a chance to get in on mephit betting action, requiring special mud, and offers to front some to him.

## Orsik

Orsik is the de facto leader of the party and is treated by Wallace as overconfident and dismissive of local knowledge. He had previously told Wallace the group needed to hike to find treasure and glory, and had arranged to investigate a wizard's tower in the Marsh of Chelimber after being asked by the pseudo-dragon Hsing. According to Wallace, Orsik did not negotiate for any payment when agreeing to Hsing's request.

During the Bugbear ambush at Ambush Gap, Orsik throws his sword at the Bugbears, which Wallace finds underwhelming. After the battle, Orsik picks through the bodies and finds a map to the wizard's tower, announcing it aloud before looting — drawing a quiet rebuke from a human party member who notes you don't announce looting before doing it.

Orsik compares the found map to their existing lost map alongside Vardis. He questions Feetor about whether the mephit can leave the swamp and is told yes. He attempts to probe Feetor for tactical advice on attacking Mama Manyknuckles' camp, but Feetor is evasive. He confers with Vardis and agrees to give Feetor a gold coin as a token of power. After Feetor reports that the mephits will wait and see, Orsik smiles, listens to Vardis joke about being King for three days, and orders the group to fall in. He is described as thinking "more followers" when Feetor first proposes loyalty.

## Hsing

Hsing does not physically appear in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. Hsing is described as an ancient golden pseudo-dragon who belongs to Assam — or more precisely, as Vardis corrects, Assam belongs to Hsing. Hsing had previously been giving the party jobs for some time. When the group was already planning to visit the wizard's tower, Hsing appeared and asked them to investigate it, making the agreement easy. Orsik recounts this history to Wallace.

## Vardis

Vardis appears as a party member alongside Orsik. He corrects Wallace's understanding of the pseudo-dragon Hsing, explaining that Assam belongs to Hsing rather than the reverse. He examines the found map alongside Orsik after the Bugbear ambush. He quietly reminds Orsik not to announce looting before actually looting the bodies. When Feetor proposes the party could be King of the swamp for the few days the water weird is reformed, Vardis jokes to Orsik, "Three days as the King. Not so bad, eh?" He confers with Orsik on giving Feetor the gold coin and participates in that decision.

## Mama Manyknuckles

Mama Manyknuckles does not appear directly in this chunk but is extensively discussed. She is described by Feetor as the particularly ugly leader of a band of half-ogres living somewhere deeper in the marsh. Feetor avoids her and her half-ogres because they mock and attack him, calling him fat and muddy. Feetor considers the insult about mud baffling given his nature.

She is one of the two major powers in the marsh's civil war, with some mephits supporting her and others supporting the water weird. Feetor advocates that the party kill her to end the civil war and consolidate mephit loyalty. Her hideout is located by Thistle within three hours using the mephit's information — a large kidney-shaped island with encampments to the North and South. She is not yet encountered at the end of this session.

## Thistle

Thistle is a party member, described as a fairy with a drake companion. During the hike, he examines the found map and, using Feetor's information, quickly identifies the correct route to Mama Manyknuckles' hideout. He corrects the rest of the group's navigation, noting that the map was wrong and that the island is in a different direction based on water flow and the bugbears' positions. He leads them to the kidney-shaped island within three hours.

He barely restrains laughter when Feetor accuses Orsik of tokenizing. He attempts to intervene before the gold coin is handed to Feetor but is too slow as Orsik and Vardis confer and act.

Thistle had previously lost his drake to the water weird and conjures the drake back while the group waits for nightfall. He then flies up to scout both the southern camp and the middle of the island. In the southern camp he finds four large crudely built huts, one sturdier hut cobbled from rafts and flat-bottom swamp boats, a fire pit with logs, and two ogres around the fire. Two half-ogres in the southern camp notice him but mistake him for a swamp creature and throw rocks and dirt without engaging seriously. Thistle zigs and zags and avoids confrontation.

In the middle of the island he discovers the sunken wizard's tower — a sinkhole framed by a weathered stone foundation with crenelated edges, flagstone floor covered in a thin sheen of swamp water, a weathered statue in the northwest corner, a large table with filthy furs and a bed, and two wide log-and-dirt ramps leading into the sinkhole. He concludes someone is living there.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a Halfling party member. During the approach to the island at night, they notice the marsh burbling with mephit chatter and infer the mephits are taking bets on who will be the new king. Unla Kee had seen portents and signs suggesting a daytime attack would have been the better choice and privately notes this. They are approached by Feetor, who has figured them out, and offered a chance to participate in the mephit betting if they have special mud — with Feetor offering to front some. Unla Kee is initially crestfallen at the condition before Feetor's offer of fronting the mud cheers them up.
