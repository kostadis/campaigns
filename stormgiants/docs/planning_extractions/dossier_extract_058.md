## Bob Saget

Bob Saget does not appear physically in this chunk but is referenced through a song recalled by Vardis as he climbs down the rope ladder to Blagothkus' castle. The song, titled/styled after "Bohemian Rhapsody," is a comedic curse directed at a king who killed his jester. The lyrics describe an eternity of punishment through bad dad jokes and puns, with themes of a jester's ghost watching in silent despair as the king is condemned to butcher punchlines forever. The song is described as "eerie" in Vardis' recollection.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is present on the Dragon's Breath deck at the start of the session and surprises the group by reciting a detailed list of allies the party has cultivated, including the new mayor of Triboar, Lord Zymorven, Tamalin Zoar, Hirax Zoar, the SEER, Wallace and his dad, Queen Titania, Prince Thornacious, Uncle Joon, and the possibility of Thistle's fairy dragon hybrid girlfriend. He also refers to Thistle's romantic interest as "this fairy dragon hybrid that happens to be Thistle's girlfriend," provoking Thistle's ire. He flies down to the castle using his 12-foot wings. Upon reaching the crumbling tower, rather than waiting with the group, he immediately flies up to the balcony and enters the room on his own, where he is confronted by Sandesyl Morgia. He was noted to have wanted to cast fireball during the zombie encounter.

## Sandesyl Morgia

Sandesyl Morgia is a half-elven moon elf vampire encountered in the upper balcony room of the crumbling port-side tower of Blagothkus' castle. She is first seen smiling at Unla Kee "like a cat smiling at a mouse" and immediately screams at him that he is an abomination. She then launches into a tirade clarifying that what Severin did to the Cult of the Dragon was not truly the Cult of the Dragon, but rather "a twisted, devilish plot by Zariel to eliminate Tiamat from her plane of Avernus," describing Tiamat as "an annoying office mate." She reveals knowledge of Severin's experiments: he hired Adimond to create a fey hybrid dragon, and the experiment was successful but nearly drove the subject mad, causing her to transform between two creature forms in a manner more akin to lycanthropy than a true fusion. She identifies the subject as resembling Thistle and confirms it was the person Thistle describes as the love of his life. She asks if Thistle can transform into a dragon; he cannot. She describes herself as the castellan of the castle, content to manage it as long as she has a fresh supply of sentient humanoids to exsanguinate, explicitly stating she enjoys their screams and uses magic to silence them outside the tower. She states she does not know where Adimond currently is. She declines to reveal how she became a vampire or how many spawn she controls, and issues a chilling threat to the group against underestimating her. She confirms she worked for Blagothkus for a long time and recounts the history of the castle: Blagothkus allied with the Cult of the Dragon against smaller peoples, the First Faction captured control of the castle, the castle crashed in the north, the First Faction captured it again, Blagothkus went to the Spine of the World to speak to his god, returned to find his son dead, and the First Faction had previously expelled her. She does not know how Blagothkus reclaimed the castle. She notes Blagothkus has been moping for weeks and jokes that she would not mind dining on him. She refuses Orsik's request to use her zombies in his play. Before the group leaves, she hands Orsik a book titled "Dusk," explaining it as the reason she wanted to become a vampire. Her last known location is the upper tower room on the port side of Blagothkus' castle.

## Severin

## Adimond

Adimond does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Sandesyl Morgia. She states that Severin hired Adimond to create a fey hybrid dragon, the subject of which appears to be Thistle's romantic interest. Sandesyl claims she does not know where Adimond currently is.

## Zariel

Zariel does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Sandesyl Morgia. She claims that Zariel orchestrated Severin's manipulation of the Cult of the Dragon as a plot to eliminate Tiamat from Avernus, where Tiamat was apparently Zariel's "annoying office mate."

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus does not appear directly in this chunk but is discussed at length. Sandesyl Morgia notes he has been moping for weeks and that she serves as his castellan. Orsik recalls the backstory of Blagothkus: he and his son Eigeron visited the Eye of Annam seeking wisdom. The divine oracle told Blagothkus he could never impress the gods enough to earn their favor and urged Eigeron to step out from his father's "dark shadow." Overcome with despair and envy, Blagothkus killed Eigeron in a terrible fight and then retired to his castle to mourn. Eigeron subsequently asked the heroes to avenge his death by killing Blagothkus. Sandesyl also recounts that Blagothkus once allied with the Cult of the Dragon against smaller peoples, his castle was twice captured by the First Faction, he traveled to the Spine of the World to speak to his god, and returned to find his son dead. His current location is his tower chambers at the stern of the castle, which are found in a state of shambles.

## Eigeron

Eigeron does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Orsik's recollection. He was the son of Blagothkus. Both visited the Eye of Annam together. The oracle urged Eigeron to step out from his father's dark shadow, leading to a confrontation in which Blagothkus slew him. After his death, Eigeron asked the heroes to avenge his death by killing Blagothkus. He was subsequently killed by the First Faction while Blagothkus was away in the Spine of the World.
