## Veradaa Storr

Veradaa Storr does not appear alive in this chunk. She is found dead by the party in a clearing along the Black Road near Parnast. Orsik, Vardis, Thistle, and Unla Key had each independently sought her out, intending to offer their services as guards or assistants in exchange for a share of the Cult treasure she had mapped. Her death precedes the party's arrival and is not explained in this session. Her notes, discovered by Thistle among her belongings, detail five treasure caches hidden by the Cult of the Dragon in the Greypeak mountains. These notes become the primary driving document for the party's subsequent actions.

## Sergeant Gronk

Sergeant Gronk is the goblin commander holding defensive position inside the cave at the base of the southernmost Greypeak mountain, the site of the first Cult treasure cache. He is managing the aftermath of a magical curse that has shrunk some of his soldiers to toy size and killed one of his two giants in a cave-in. He laments the disaster the mission has become and organizes his remaining troops into a defensive formation using partial cover inside a cavern, with a southern tunnel as a fallback escape route. He notes the dead giant buried in the eastern tunnel as the source of the dust in the air. When the party approaches, he sets an ambush and initially succeeds, killing or dropping two party members including Orsik. He is killed when Unla Key casts Thunderwave, which also kills two of his goblins. His last thought is wondering whether the buried giant will recover. He dies in the cavern.

## Slayermighty

Slayermighty is a giant who has been magically shrunk to a tiny size, apparently as a result of whatever curse was triggered in the cave. He is found in a deeper cavern standing in front of a stone chest, dressed in ratty furs and wielding a club. He is accompanied by his bear, Blooddrinker. When Thistle enters the cavern and alerts him, Slayermighty shouts that he will tear the party apart and feed them to Blooddrinker, and declares he will soon be ruling all of Giant-kind. Despite his threats, he is unable to land a blow on the party and is killed before he can swing his club. Orsik and Vardis find his diminutive state difficult to take seriously. He had in his possession two vials of alchemist fire, a potion of healing, and a necklace of twenty copper charms.

## Blooddrinker

Blooddrinker is Slayermighty's bear companion, also reduced to the size of a cat by the cave's magical curse. After Slayermighty is killed, Blooddrinker rolls onto his belly expecting to be petted rather than behaving ferociously. When Thistle opens the stone chest with Orsik and Vardis, Blooddrinker begins convulsing, and Thistle saves the creature through quick thinking. Blooddrinker bonds immediately to Thistle afterward. His current status is alive, cat-sized, and in Thistle's care.

## Clan True-Nimbus

Clan True-Nimbus does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced through a note left inside the stone chest at the first cache location. The note, addressed mockingly to hill giants, claims that Clan True-Nimbus of the Clouds has already taken the treasure, calling the hill giants slow, dim-witted, and small. The note includes a condescending definition of the word "prevail." Their identity as cloud giants and their prior arrival at the cache site establishes them as rivals or antagonists in the larger treasure hunt. The cache itself is confirmed empty as a result of their intervention.
