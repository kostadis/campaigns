## Hekaton

King Hekaton is the primary NPC rescued in this session. He had been captured by the Kraken Society and held in chains aboard a ship at sea. The party — particularly Orsik and Vardis — broke his chains using magical weapons while under sustained assault from an archmage and the ship's first mate. Thistle revived Orsik with a goodberry, allowing the group to use the Conch of Teleportation to escape with Hekaton to the Maelstrom grotto.

Upon arrival, Hekaton is described in extensive physical detail: enormous stature nearly scraping the vaulted geode ceiling, deep purple skin laced with lightning-scar lines, winter-moonlight hair and beard, storm-churned sea eyes shot with amethyst light, legendary scale armor, and a belt of woven lightning hung with bone and gemstone talismans. His presence causes the air to crackle with ozone and the stones to tremble.

His first words upon standing are disoriented — "Where am I? Who are you?" — and the party explains they were sent by his daughter Serissa to rescue him from the Kraken Society. He immediately demands to go to the throne room.

In the throne room, Hekaton receives a joyous family reunion from Serissa, the Imperator Uthor, and his daughters Nym and Mirran. He quickly pivots to business, acknowledging the realm is in danger. When told Iymrith — the blue dragon called "the Doom of the Desert" — is responsible for the plot against his kingdom, he recognizes the name.

He then conducts a reckoning: he asks Uthor directly whether he searched for him, and when the party clarifies that Serissa bore the burden of hope while Uthor offered little support, Hekaton acts decisively. He ascends the Wyrmskull Throne, strips Uthor of his position as Imperator and appoints Serissa in his place, then confronts Nym and Mirran for betraying him, betraying Serissa as their Queen, and allying with a dragon. He orders them confined to their chambers pending a decision on their fates, and dons his obsidian crown.

Hekaton tells the party he intends to restore the Ordning — which the All-Father broke with "visions of grandeur that were foolish" — by compelling the giant faction leaders to kneel before him: Chief Guh of the Hill Giants, Thane Kayalithca of the Stone Giants, Jarl Storvald of the Frost Giants, Countess Sansuri of the Cloud Giants, and Duke Zalto of the Fire Giants. He expresses frustration that Iymrith will live while he handles this political restoration, and offers the party a deal: if they defeat or reform the giant faction leaders, he will join them for a final battle against Iymrith. Orsik declines on behalf of the group, though the rest of the party is less certain.

Current status: Seated on the Wyrmskull Throne in the Maelstrom, restored as King, wearing the obsidian crown, and beginning the work of reconsolidating giant power under the restored Ordning.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee participates in the ship battle throughout the session. He uses pegasi wings to fly, attempts to use fire offensively but is frustrated by the sleet storm obscuring his targets. He remembers he can cast haste mid-combat, flies into the storm, loses the ability to fly within it, and lands prone on the deck. He is struck down by the first mate using a poisoned weapon and brought to death's door. Despite this, he counters the wizard's cone of cold before being downed. He survives but is barely clinging to life through much of the later battle.

During the throne room scene, when Nym and Mirran greet their father with apparent joy, Unla Kee mutters, "No, you aren't," signaling his awareness of their treachery.

After Hekaton makes his offer to the party, Unla Kee does not deliberate — he immediately runs past the King toward the teleportation chamber, evidently intent on returning to his cloud castle to kill the ancient blue dragon Iymrith on his own.

Current status: Last seen running toward the Maelstrom's teleportation chamber, bypassing all diplomatic proceedings, with stated intent to kill Iymrith.

## Thistle

Thistle participates in the ship battle using a bow and his Eyes of True Seeing. He scouts the fore section of the ship for the invisible wizard and finds nothing. He is knocked prone by a chunk of sleet when trying to reach Orsik. He hits the first mate with an arrow, staggering him, which allows Thuul to rush in. After the wizard's final cone of cold leaves the party in ruins, Thistle — barely alive — announces he has a plan, and over Vardis's skeptical objection, executes it: feeding Orsik a goodberry to revive him, enabling the escape via the Conch.

In the throne room, Thistle flies up to Hekaton to explain the rescue. When the King raises the threat to the realms, Thistle immediately redirects: "Yes, we have to rescue my girlfriend." He also notes that his love interest is located somewhere near Duke Zalto's kingdom, which is a factor in the party's hesitation to decline Hekaton's offer outright. He further knows that Countess Sansuri's defeat matters to Escarlotta, creating additional pressure.

Current status: Present in the Maelstrom throne room, motivated primarily by rescuing his girlfriend and aware of the strategic importance of Zalto's and Sansuri's territories to personal goals.

---

## Vardis

Vardis fights in the ship battle, descending into the sleet storm to help break Hekaton's chains. He is wounded by a ballista shot from a cultist before entering the storm. He casts a spiritual weapon to attack the chains but initially finds it ineffective. After Orsik is frozen by the prismatic spray, Vardis continues the assault — his spiritual weapon ultimately breaks two chains, and his magical mace destroys the damaged third. He survives two cone of cold attacks from the wizard and is barely alive by the time the escape happens.

During the chaos, he shouts at Thistle to stop strategizing and simply heal Orsik. He heals Orsik in the grotto after arrival.

In the throne room, Vardis is aware that a treasure map is located near Duke Zalto's stronghold, which makes him less willing than Orsik to simply dismiss Hekaton's offer. This creates a quiet tension with Orsik's confident refusal.

Current status: Present in the Maelstrom throne room, motivated by the treasure map near Zalto's territory.

---

## Orsik

Orsik drives much of the tactical decision-making during the ship battle. He throws two axes at the wizard at the outset, hitting once. He commands Vardis to "light them up" into the sleet storm. He directs the party to free Hekaton and use the Conch. He is the one who realizes mid-combat that only magical weapons can break the chains. He is frozen by the wizard's prismatic spray but maintains concentration on the sustaining spell. He breaks one chain himself before being knocked unconscious by the second cone of cold. He is revived by Thistle's goodberry and uses the Conch to teleport the group out.

In the grotto and throne room, Orsik runs after the King, tries to look dashing for Serissa (aware she will someday be Queen), and adds commentary during the party's introduction to Hekaton. When Hekaton offers his deal, Orsik confidently declines on behalf of the group without consulting them, prompting silent disagreement from his companions.

He also internally reflects on the irony that his DNRRR (Do Not Reanimate clause is absent), worrying the wizard might be a necromancer.

Current status: Present in the Maelstrom throne room, healed, and having unilaterally declined Hekaton's alliance offer.

---

## Serissa

Serissa does not appear until the throne room scene. She is described as delighted to see her father again. Hekaton turns to her directly and asks her to explain the situation, and she does — in doing so, Mirran and Nym's failures and betrayals are laid bare.

As a result of her loyalty, diligence in maintaining hope, and Uthor's failure, Hekaton appoints Serissa as the new Imperator of the Maelstrom. She receives Uthor's insignia of office. She then orders the guards to take Nym and Mirran to their chambers when they attempt to protest, and is described as "enjoying this moment too much" as she follows the guards.

Orsik notices her and attempts to look dashing, acknowledging to himself that she will someday be Queen.

Current status: Newly appointed Imperator of the Maelstrom, overseeing the confinement of Nym and Mirran.

---

## Uthor

Uthor is the Imperator at the time of Hekaton's return, identified as the King's brother. He greets Hekaton with an exclamation of joy at seeing him alive. However, when Hekaton asks whether Uthor was the one who left no stone unturned to find him, Uthor attempts to deceive — and the party contradicts him, clarifying that Serissa bore that burden while Uthor offered little support.

Hekaton strips Uthor of the Imperatorship and appoints Serissa in his place. He addresses Uthor as "Brother, Uthor" and tells him he is dismissed. Uthor's response is notably composed and cryptic — he says it is "time I took care of other business," smiles, hands his insignia of office to Serissa, and marches out of the throne room. His exit produces a stunned silence among those present.

Current status: Dismissed as Imperator, whereabouts unknown after leaving the throne room. His calm, knowing smile and mention of "other business" suggest a potential ulterior agenda.

---

## Nym

Nym is one of Hekaton's daughters. She greets her father with apparent joy upon his return, shouting "Daddy, we're so happy to see you!" Unla Kee audibly doubts her sincerity. When Serissa's account of events is given to Hekaton, Nym and Mirran's betrayals — including allying with the dragon Iymrith — are revealed. Hekaton condemns them for betraying him, betraying Serissa as their Queen, and allying with a dragon. He orders them to their chambers pending a decision on their fates. Nym attempts to protest but is removed by guards on Serissa's order.

Current status: Confined to her chambers in the Maelstrom, fate to be decided by Hekaton.

---

## Mirran

Mirran is Hekaton's other daughter present in the throne room. Her behavior mirrors Nym's — apparent joyous greeting to her father, exposure during Serissa's account, condemnation by Hekaton for the same betrayals, and attempted protest before being removed by guards on Serissa's order.

Current status: Confined to her chambers in the Maelstrom, fate to be decided by Hekaton.

## Iymrith

Iymrith does not appear in this session but is extensively discussed. The party identifies her to Hekaton as "the blue dragon who tried to steal your kingdom." Hekaton recognizes her by the title "the Doom of the Desert." Hekaton expresses that it "galls him" that Iymrith will remain alive while he works to restore the Ordning by forcing the giant faction leaders to kneel. He offers the party a deal predicated on their defeating or reforming those faction leaders, after which he would join a "final battle against the Doom of the Desert."

Unla Kee's immediate reaction upon hearing this offer is to run toward the teleportation chamber, apparently intent on attacking Iymrith independently at his cloud castle.

Current status: Not present. At large. Identified as the primary antagonist behind the plot against Hekaton's kingdom and the destabilization of the Ordning.
