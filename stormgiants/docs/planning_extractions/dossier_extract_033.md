## Eltio

Eltio is the 3HPP's contact in the Hand of Yartar, met at the Wink and the Kiss tavern in Yartar. She was surprised to see the party alive and further impressed by their Letter of Mark from Tamalin Zoar, which satisfied her enough to provide the intelligence they needed. She informed them that Harthal was in prison and likely facing execution, and that the Giant Slayer sword was in the possession of Brenner, a captain of the guard, who could be found in the central market below the Waterbarron's residence.

When Orsik suggested using a fireball in the crowded market just outside the Waterbarron's residence, Eltio was visibly shocked and firmly objected, warning it would get them all hunted down and killed. She recognized Orsik by reputation — "you're Orsik, the guy who does the thing" — indicating his notoriety has spread through criminal or adventuring circles. She also pushed the group to act quickly after Brenner's death, warning that authorities would connect his killing to Harthal's associates and execute the boy.

Eltio guided the party to an unofficial secret passageway through an abandoned temple of Bane in the sewers, which she had learned of through her contacts. She was candid that it was not an official backdoor and that she was not entirely certain of its layout. She is described as no shrinking violet when it comes to violence, suggesting she is experienced and pragmatic. Her last known role in these events was directing the party into the sewers toward the temple passage.

## Brenner

Brenner is a captain of the city guard in Yartar who had come into possession of the Giant Slayer sword, which he wore on his belt. He operated a protection racket in the central market, moving through the interconnected alleys in the evening to collect cuts from small store owners and stalls. He was described as thug-like, smug, and prone to wielding whatever power he had over those beneath him. His guards appeared bored and dissatisfied.

Brenner was killed during the 3HPP's ambush in the market. Following his death, Eltio warned that once the news spread, authorities would assume the killing was connected to Harthal's allies and move to execute the boy, creating urgency for the prison rescue. Brenner does not appear to have been affiliated with the vampire or the Waterbarron's experimental program.

## Harthal Zymorven

Harthal Zymorven is the son and heir of Lord Zymorven, imprisoned in Yartar and awaiting execution at the time of the rescue. He was brought before the party by one of the vampire's zombie servants after Thistle requested to see him. Upon appearing, he immediately mistook the disguised Orsik for his father Lord Zymorven and cried out "Father, you came for me," revealing both his emotional state and Orsik's convincing disguise. When he attempted to run to "his father," the vampire violently restrained him.

After being released in exchange for the guards' corpses, Harthal accompanied the party. Upon reaching the Harper hangout, he recognized Thistle Wendrod and expressed frustrated anger, blaming Thistle's girlfriend — Leila — for his situation. He explained that Leila was the rogue who had convinced him to steal the Giant Slayer sword and pursue an adventure, telling him she wanted experiences like his father. After Harthal killed someone who "badly deserved it," Leila abandoned him, saying she needed to find "the absolute love of her life." She had come to Yartar intending to join the Hand of Yartar to help locate Thistle, but found them too locally focused and moved on. Harthal felt used by Leila and resented the entire episode.

By the end of the session, Harthal was deeply remorseful, apologized to Orsik-disguised-as-his-father, and declared he wanted to go straight home and become the next Lord Zymorven. The deception was maintained until Orsik reverted to his true form at the Moongleam Tower in Everlund before Harthal could witness the transformation. Harthal's current status is traveling with or near the party, headed toward Zymorven Hall.

## The Vampire (Unnamed)

The vampire is an unnamed pale-faced woman encountered in a richly appointed room within the prison beneath Yartar, sitting behind a desk surrounded by bookshelves, a table with a skeleton, and several zombies under her command. The room was out of place for a prison setting. She appeared to be conducting controlled experiments on the prisoners — tightly regulating every element of their environment including air, food, water, and emotional stimulation — and had received sanction for these experiments from the highest authorities in Yartar.

She was calm and businesslike when Thistle approached her, consulting a book before agreeing to summon Harthal. She refused to let Thistle accompany the zombie to retrieve Harthal, citing contamination of her experimental conditions. When Orsik-disguised-as-Zymorven offered the corpses of the slain guards in exchange for Harthal's release, she accepted, visibly pleased and revealing her fangs when she smiled. Notably, she referred to the guards as belonging to the Waterbarron rather than herself, and made clear she considered the Waterbarron's belief that those guards could contain her movements throughout the city to be a delusion — she laughed at the notion.

Before Harthal left, she whispered a warning to him: if he were found in the city after this, she would be compelled to kill him the moment he came back down. She directed this warning at Harthal but implicitly asked Orsik-Zymorven to ensure they escaped the city that night. Her motivations appear to be research-oriented rather than political, and her relationship with the Waterbarron's administration is one of tolerated autonomy rather than loyalty. Her current status is in the prison complex beneath Yartar.

## Gauche the Young

Gauche the Young is the new Harper leader of the Yartar Harper headquarters, introduced to the party when they arrived via the teleportation circle. He is of average human size with a heavy build, amber eyes, a broad nose, and gray hair — belying his surname "the Young." His predecessor, Kolbaz, had been killed, and Gauche described the event: Kolbaz was struck by a powerful lightning bolt in a room near the teleportation circle, and the physical destruction of the room suggested something massive had materialized there and then dematerialized — damage inconsistent with the work of a giant.

Gauche was openly hostile to the practice of hiring outside adventurers, describing the 3HPP as mercenaries rather than true Harpers and explicitly stating he disagreed with the Ezra model championed by Krowen Valharrow. He made clear he was cooperating only because Ezra's program had been successful and Ezra had been promoted. Despite this hostility, he acknowledged the party's condolences about Kolbaz's death — with the awkward admission that the position was a plum assignment and he had been looking forward to it.

When Thistle identified research into Adimond Kroskas as the likely cause of Kolbaz's death, Gauche acknowledged he would investigate the circumstances of his predecessor's death but explicitly refused to investigate Adimond Kroskas, calling it a distraction. Even when Orsik-Zymorven attempted to persuade him otherwise, Gauche remained firm — he would look into wizards who masquerade as demons, and every possible angle of the investigation except Adimond Kroskas's identity or whereabouts. He was taking measurements of the room's destruction marks and had a theory the killer was demonic in nature or a wizard. His current status is at the Yartar Harper headquarters, conducting his investigation on his own narrow terms.

## Kolbaz

Kolbaz does not appear in these events — he is deceased prior to the party's arrival. He was the previous Harper leader of the Yartar headquarters and was killed by a lightning bolt in a room near the teleportation circle. The physical evidence suggested something massive had materialized in the room, destroyed everything, and then dematerialized. Gauche confirmed that other individuals had come through the teleportation circle around the time of his death but declined to share that information with the party. Thistle's examination of Kolbaz's papers revealed that his research into Adimond Kroskas appears to have been the proximate cause of his death. The papers also contained the revelation that Adimond Kroskas is an alias — he is not who he claims to be. Kolbaz's body is not described in detail; his significance is primarily as the victim whose research led to a critical discovery about Adimond Kroskas.

## Adimond Kroskas

Adimond Kroskas does not appear in person in these events but is a central subject of discovered intelligence. Kolbaz's papers at the Yartar Harper headquarters revealed two significant facts: first, that Adimond Kroskas created the ritual that the Cult of Tiamat used to first create Drakes; and second, that Adimond Kroskas is an alias — he is not who he claims to be. Thistle connected Kroskas to Kolbaz's death, believing the Harper was killed for getting too close to this information. Gauche explicitly refused to investigate Kroskas despite Thistle's reasoning. Kroskas's true identity and current whereabouts are unknown.

## Krowen Valharrow

Krowen Valharrow does not appear in person in these events but is discussed by both Gauche the Young and Nespril Menk. He is the Harper figure who originally hired the 3HPP and is described as a follower of the school of Ezra — a philosophical approach to Harper operations that involves hiring outside adventurers and using Harper funds for missions. Gauche described Krowen as a big-picture thinker who misses details — specifically, he had intended the party to exit through the front door of the Moongleam Tower rather than the secret sewer passage. His program's documented success led to his promotion to the upper echelons of Harper society. Nespril also expressed frustration with Krowen's model, characterizing it as hiring mercenaries who would "save the world if the check clears" rather than true believers. Krowen's current whereabouts are not stated, but he is described as having moved to a higher position within Harper leadership.

## Nespril Menk

Nespril Menk is a tall, coltish, gangly apprentice of Krowen Valharrow, stationed at the Moongleam Tower in Everlund. He has green eyes, a broad nose, and light brown hair tied in a knot, and speaks with the sophistication of a native Chondathan speaker of Common. He greeted the 3HPP by name upon their arrival through the teleportation circle and immediately began managing their exit from the tower, pointing out that Krowen had neglected to brief them on the secret sewer exit used to avoid Zhentarim surveillance.

Nespril revealed that the tower uses a special randomized system to evade Zhentarim spies. When Orsik pressed him about the system and whether it was documented in a book, Nespril warned that Krowen would be very upset if that book went missing or if Orsik were implicated in its disappearance. When Orsik continued to push, suggesting the system needed independent security contractors, Nespril erupted — declaring that he did not believe in Krowen's Ezra model and that being a Harper should mean genuine belief, not mercenary contract work. He shares Gauche's philosophical opposition to the outsourcing approach but is more openly emotional about it. His current position is at the Moongleam Tower in Everlund.

## Dral Thelev

Dral Thelev is a contact of Orsik's at Danivarr's House in Everlund, connected to Tamalin Zoar. He met with Orsik and caught up over their mutual acquaintance with Tamalin, whom Orsik praised as a brilliant businesswoman. Dral provided significant political intelligence: Tamalin's brother is High Captain Horix Zoar, commander of the army, and the High Captain has never forgiven High Marshal Methrammar Aerasumé of Silverymoon for abandoning his allies — a grievance that caused Zoar to break the compact and dissolve the alliance between Everlund and Silverymoon. While Horix Zoar privately would like to raze Silverymoon, he also believes in letting bygones be bygones in practice. Dral indicated that Horix Zoar could get the party into Silverymoon as diplomatic representatives of Everlund, granting them protection from local law enforcement. Dral's current status is at Danivarr's House in Everlund.

## Tamalin Zoar

Tamalin Zoar does not appear in person in this chapter but is referenced in two contexts. First, her Letter of Mark was sufficient to impress Eltio and secure the party's cooperation from the Hand of Yartar. Second, Dral Thelev confirmed her family connection: her brother is High Captain Horix Zoar of Everlund's army, making her significantly more politically connected than may have previously been apparent. Orsik praised her business acumen to Dral. Her current whereabouts are not stated in this chapter.

## Horix Zoar

Horix Zoar does not appear in person in these events. He is Tamalin Zoar's brother and the High Captain — commander of Everlund's army. Dral Thelev described him as bearing a lasting grudge against High Marshal Methrammar Aerasumé of Silverymoon for abandoning his allies, a betrayal that caused Horix to break the compact and dissolve the Everlund-Silverymoon alliance. Despite privately wanting to raze Silverymoon, he holds to a policy of letting bygones be bygones. He has the political authority to credential the 3HPP as Everlund diplomats, which would shield them from arrest by Silverymoon's local authorities.

---

## Methrammar Aerasumé

Methrammar Aerasumé does not appear in person in these events. He is the High Marshal of Silverymoon and is referenced by Dral Thelev as the cause of the political rupture between Silverymoon and Everlund — specifically, High Captain Horix Zoar has never forgiven him for abandoning his allies, which led Zoar to dissolve the alliance and break the compact. His current role and relationship to the party's upcoming visit to Silverymoon are not elaborated upon beyond this political context.

## Leila

Leila does not appear in person in these events but is extensively discussed by Harthal Zymorven. She is described as a rogue — and as Thistle's girlfriend, or at least someone who considers Thistle the absolute love of her life. Harthal revealed that Leila was the one who convinced him to steal the Giant Slayer sword and go adventuring, leading directly to his imprisonment. After Harthal killed someone during their travels, Leila left him, saying she needed to go find Thistle. She came to Yartar with the intention of joining the Hand of Yartar to help locate him, but found the organization too locally focused and moved on to seek other means of finding him. Harthal felt used by her. Thistle, upon learning she called him "the absolute love of her life," experienced hope. Leila's current whereabouts are unknown — she departed Yartar before the party's arrival.
