## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a gnome wizard traveling with the party. During the encounter with the magmins, he hesitated to cast magic missile, then chose to swing wildly with his shortsword instead — a decision that resulted in him being knocked out by the magmin's retaliation. Orsik observed this with stunned amusement and conceded the magic missile would have been the better choice. Unla survived due to his ability to manipulate the fates, turning what would have been a killing blow into a non-lethal one. After the fight, he mused that the trap room probably worked better when a wizard could show up to kill trapped thieves.

When the party entered the library and encountered Elquen the Wight, Unla had not yet entered. Orsik prompted him to make his entrance, and Unla used his fate manipulation ability at a key moment — causing Elquen to mistake him for someone he recognized, apparently a past associate ("The Wizard of the Crag" or someone connected to Elquen's prior life). Elquen greeted him warmly, called out his arrogance affectionately, and offered to relinquish the library. Elquen then requested 150 GP in back pay as walking-around money, stating that Unla "never could hold onto money." Unla agreed, paid the 150 GP, and Elquen departed peacefully. Unla's intervention was the key to resolving the Elquen situation without violence.

## Vardis

Vardis entered the secret treasure room of the Wizard of the Crag, which bore the ostentatious inscription "The Wizard of the Crag's Treasure Room" on the back wall. He found nothing of monetary value, but discovered a ledger detailing the Wizard's failed financial ventures — real estate investments that did not pan out, construction projects with cost overruns, and a reference to a duel with a figure named Taskor that was apparently connected to the wizard's debts. After Elquen was freed and departed, Vardis remarked, "well, that's interesting. We released a wight into the world." He also demonstrated quick thinking during the later battle against the Water Weird and Mud Mephits by calling in the group's own mephit allies, which triggered a Mud Mephit civil war and turned the tide of the fight.

## The Wight (Elquen)

Elquen is a wight who was formerly a servant or associate in the Wizard of the Crag's castle. He is encountered in the library of the castle, where he has taken on a self-appointed role as guardian against the removal or use of magical books, believing this to be his burden and curse — a consequence of a wild magic surge that transformed him into a wight and turned his companions (two guards and manservants he tried to help) into skeletons.

His backstory is revealed through a reminiscence: when the Wizard of the Crag was killed in a duel with a figure named Taskor, Elquen became concerned about the water elementals the wizard had been controlling, fearing they would destroy everything without proper oversight. The wild magic surge that transformed him occurred in this context.

When the party arrived, Elquen initially refused to allow the books to be removed, citing the dangers of magic and his role as protector. He declared: "I am afraid the disastrous effects of magic mean there will be no removal of books from here, and more importantly, there will be no use of magic. It is my curse and burden to protect the world from this."

However, when Unla Kee entered and manipulated the fates, Elquen recognized him as someone from his past and warmly embraced his presence. He agreed to relinquish the library and requested 150 GP in back pay — referencing Unla's historical inability to hold onto money — before departing through the hallways toward the laboratory. His current whereabouts are unknown; he is loose in the world as of this session.

## Taskor

Taskor does not physically appear in this session but is referenced in two separate sources. First, Vardis discovers a ledger in the Wizard of the Crag's treasure room that references a duel with Taskor, apparently connected to the wizard's mounting debts. Second, Elquen's reminiscence reveals that Taskor killed the Wizard of the Crag in this duel, an event that set off the chain of circumstances — loss of funding, loss of control over water elementals, and the wild magic surge — that transformed Elquen into a wight. Taskor's current whereabouts and role in the broader story are not addressed in this session.
