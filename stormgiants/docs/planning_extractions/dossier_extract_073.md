## Thistle

Thistle appeared throughout the session in the combat and exploration of the castle. During the fight with the undead, he killed a specter with his arrows while other party members were stunned. He also successfully struck down the spectral Raven despite being affected by its soul-chilling cry of despair, which triggered intense personal emotion — reminding him of his lost love and the years he has spent searching for her. Although ordinarily resistant to such evil, he was not immune to the Raven's despair effect. After killing the Raven, the fear vanished, and Thistle redoubled his internal resolve to find his lost love.

During exploration, Thistle found a note in the kitchen that he initially mistook for a recipe. Reading it, he identified it as a rant accusing Sandesyl of betrayal and treachery. This prompted him to share knowledge passed on by his Uncle Joon: Sandesyl is a disciple of Sammaster, the chosen of Mystara who originally devised the process for creating Dracoliches. Thistle explained that some members of the Cult of the Dragon believed dragons were the perfect form of undead, and that certain cult members pursued other necromantic research beyond Dracoliches.

Thistle also recalled stories about Rezmir, identifying the half-dragon remains wearing purple Wyrmspeaker garb as the Black Dragon Wyrmspeaker. He recounted rumors that Rezmir was responsible for the castle and for moving the hoard as part of the ritual, and that she was either killed by the heroes of the War of the Dragons or fell victim to internal politics.

During the conversation with Escarlotta, Thistle felt awkward at the discussion of deals with devils and remarked that it's not ideal to have a devil running a play. He also noted that people sometimes audition for a part without knowing what they're getting into. He was tempted to ask Escarlotta why she was obsessed with destroying the Cloud Giant Countess Sansuri, but did not follow through. His mention of Uncle Joon establishes a familial or mentor-like relationship with someone who had knowledge of the Cult of the Dragon's inner history.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee was stunned at the start of the session alongside Orsik, leaving him vulnerable. A shadowy undead figure glided toward him and drained his life force by flowing through him. Despite being stunned, he passively observed that Orsik was also being targeted. He survived the encounter.

During post-combat exploration, Unla Kee provided arcane expertise, casually explaining that a specter is created when a soul is ripped from a creature while it still lives, implying Sandesyl's direct involvement in the creation of the undead infestation. He also identified a magical gem found by Vaardis as a gem of elemental summoning, which he promptly pocketed.

When the group discussed confronting Sandesyl, Unla Kee firmly refused, citing an intuition about bad plans and stating the situation was "screaming bright red." He also dismissed Sandesyl as "not part of the end game," referencing a larger, unspecified "end game" that he declined to elaborate on beyond calling it "THE END GAME."

Unla Kee briefly left the kitchen and rushed into the unlit barracks, then returned sheepishly upon realizing he had already explored them. He also contributed to the group's discussion about resurrection, suggesting they could use "not good" clerics if needed, raising the question of whether such clerics would raise or animate the dead.

Later, Unla Kee proposed a tactical theory: that Sandesyl had carved her lair into the glacier's ice outside the Dracolich's caves, explaining how she could control the castle and appear anywhere within it. He advocated for resting before engaging her and warned that eliminating the Ogre Zombies would provoke her further.

## Vaardis

Vaardis appeared during exploration and combat. He found a magical gem, which he handed to Unla Kee without apparent hesitation. He asked whether the note Thistle found was a recipe. He also proposed confronting Sandesyl directly, asking "So we going to take this Sandesyl character down?" — a suggestion Unla Kee immediately rejected. Vaardis also reflected on the practicality of raise dead spells as a safety net, noting that money could substitute for access to temples — except for clerics who have been banned from temples, though he noted that money could solve even that problem by hiring non-orthodox clerics.

## Orsik

Orsik opened the session in a stunned state, reflecting at length on the nature of being stunned and the limitations of his fighter profession now that he possesses enhanced intelligence. He philosophized about the phrase "drag them down to your level and beat them with experience" and internally mourned the simpler satisfaction he once derived from being the most dangerous presence in any room. He was targeted by the shadowy undead figure along with Unla Kee. A fireball — presumably cast by a party member — broke him free of his stunned condition, but by that point all the undead were already dead.

During exploration, Orsik found a bottle of Blackstaff wine and delivered an informed explanation of its origins — a Waterdhavian wine with a big, unbalanced flavor exported to the Western Heartlands and Baldur's Gate, which explained its presence in the castle. He found a note signed by or attributed to Rezmir protesting an audit of her operations and arguing against internal mistrust within the Cult of the Dragon, using the metaphor of Tiamat's five heads not attacking one another.

Orsik searched Rezmir's room for treasure and found nothing. He intervened in the standoff between Escarlotta and Blagothkus, soothingly arguing that people must be forgiven for making deals with devils they did not fully understand, and that direct action is sometimes necessary to address the mistakes of the past. His intervention was effective: Escarlotta agreed and announced that Blagothkus would help the group defeat Sandesyl.

## Sandesyl

Sandesyl does not appear physically in this session but is extensively discussed. She is identified by Thistle as a disciple of Sammaster, placing her within a necromantic offshoot of the Cult of the Dragon. Unla Kee confirmed that the specters encountered in the kitchen were created through necromancy — souls ripped from living creatures — which the party attributed to Sandesyl's experiments.

A note found in the kitchen confirmed that Sandesyl was accused of betrayal and treachery by unknown parties. Escarlotta's account further revealed that after the War of the Dragons, Sandesyl had not fled the castle as the victorious Frost Giants believed. Instead, she remained hidden and transformed the new occupants — including the Frost Giant Jarl — into undead over time. She later negotiated with Escarlotta, agreeing to locate Blagothkus on the Mkoz glacier in exchange for continued access to or coexistence within the castle. This deal allowed her to maintain her presence and continue her necromantic experiments.

Unla Kee noted that Sandesyl cannot control the castle or Escarlotta directly. He theorized that she has carved her lair into the glacier's ice outside the Dracolich's caves, explaining her ability to appear throughout the castle at will. The group agreed not to confront her immediately but acknowledged that eliminating the Ogre Zombies would provoke a serious response from her. She is considered a significant threat.

## Rezmir

Rezmir does not appear in this session but is identified through physical remains and documents. The party discovered the partial remains of a half-dragon wearing the distinctive purple garb of a Wyrmspeaker — specifically identified by Thistle as the Black Dragon Wyrmspeaker. The body was located in a room north of the barracks.

Thistle recalled rumors about Rezmir: she was reportedly in charge of the castle and responsible for moving the hoard as part of the draconic ritual. Competing accounts suggest she was killed by the heroes of the War of the Dragons or that she fell victim to internal Cult of the Dragon politics.

A note found by Orsik in the same room supports the internal politics theory. The note, written from Rezmir's perspective, protests an audit of her operations, dismisses the auditors as spies who infiltrated the Black Dragon Hatchery, and invokes the unity of Tiamat's five heads as an argument against internal distrust. She insisted that any future auditing must involve all Wyrmspeakers collectively or be considered enemy sabotage. Her current status is deceased, with her remains present in the castle.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta appeared via a new avatar — a female cloud giant's head formed from spinning crystals, runes, and gems in the castle's control tower. She noted the avatar was still a work in progress. She immediately questioned the party about whether they had removed the undead infestation and volunteered that the undead were products of Sandesyl's necromantic experiments before they could answer.

She then delivered an extended personal account of her history. She explained that after the War of the Dragons, she was trapped in a glacier in Mkoz when her castle crashed. Her husband Blagothkus failed to secure the help of the Frost Giant Jarl to free her. The heroes of the War of the Dragons ultimately freed her. For a time she was under the control of the Frost Giants, who used her to help defeat the dragons at the final battle above the summoned temple of Tiamat. The Frost Giants mistakenly believed Sandesyl had fled and that the castle was theirs — she had not, and she transformed the new occupants into undead after the cult's destruction.

Escarlotta revealed that Sandesyl negotiated a deal with her, agreeing to find Blagothkus in exchange for her continued presence. Blagothkus, having visited the Oracle of the All-Father and been told that their son needed to kill him to lead the giants, was failed by their son — who could not bring himself to do it. In her grief over her son's death, Escarlotta refused to move the castle, compounding their stranded situation.

She expressed intense anger at Blagothkus throughout, blaming him for all of it. She also expressed fury at the Cloud Giant Countess Sansuri, calling her a "classless poseur" making a play for the Ordning, and insisted Blagothkus was responsible for this situation as well. When Orsik offered a soothing perspective on forgiveness and direct action, Escarlotta acknowledged — with difficulty — that forgiveness was hard but that he was right. She then announced that Blagothkus would assist the party in defeating Sandesyl.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus appeared in the control tower alongside Escarlotta's avatar. His first action was to preemptively declare that the situation was not his fault, which immediately drew a rebuke from Escarlotta about accountability. He remained largely reactive throughout the conversation, saying little beyond a single-word response ("Stand?") when Escarlotta demanded he do something other than sit. He and Escarlotta appeared to be in a standoff until Orsik intervened.

Blagothkus's backstory was revealed primarily through Escarlotta's account: after the War of the Dragons, he became obsessed with the role of giants and visited the Oracle of the All-Father, who told him he was a failure and that their son was the true giant leader — but only if the son killed the father. Their son failed to do so, and this outcome contributed to the family's grief and stagnation. Blagothkus had previously made a deal with Sandesyl to have her locate him on the Mkoz glacier. By the session's end, Escarlotta declared that Blagothkus would help the party defeat Sandesyl, implying he has been conscripted into this effort whether or not he fully agreed.

## Sammaster

Sammaster does not appear in this session but is referenced by Thistle as background context for Sandesyl's identity. Sammaster was a chosen of Mystara and the individual who originally devised the process for creating Dracoliches. Thistle's Uncle Joon had told him about how certain members of the Cult of the Dragon believed dragons were the perfect form of undead and pursued other magical processes for creating undead beyond the Dracolich formula — research that Sandesyl appears to have continued as a disciple of Sammaster's tradition.

## Severin

Severin does not appear in this session but is referenced briefly during the discussion of deals with devils. Unla Kee and Escarlotta clarified the distinction between Sandesyl (a vampire, not a devil) and the actual devil in the situation: Tiamat. Severin was identified as the one who made the actual deal with Tiamat. No further detail was provided about Severin's current status or whereabouts.

## Countess Sansuri

Countess Sansuri does not appear in this session but is mentioned by Escarlotta with considerable hostility. Escarlotta described her as a "classless poseur of a magic-wielding cloud giant" who was making a play for the Ordning — the giant social hierarchy — and characterized this as an outrage. Thistle briefly considered asking Escarlotta why she was so obsessed with destroying Sansuri but did not do so. No further detail about Sansuri's plans or current whereabouts was provided.
