## Mary

Mary does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Vardis's recollection of how the party came to be in the Netherese Mountain Range. She is described as an old lady encountered when the party first arrived in Everlund, who was shouting warnings about the Kraken returning to destroy the city using powerful tentacles and mind control, and about the Kraken's allies being more powerful than anything the city had faced before. She is the head of an organization called the "Fighters of the Light," which charges a 5 GP membership fee. The party joined because they were broke at the time, and Vardis reflects with some disbelief that the chain of events stemming from that membership has led him to fight a blue dragon to protect a 5 Gold investment.

## Thistle

Thistle is present throughout the battle in the cavern of the Netherese Mountain Range. He is the one who precipitates the fight by shouting "Pro or anti-Tiamat?" at the dragon, and when the dragon declares "Tiamat for-ever!" Thistle immediately reaches for his bow and announces the dragon is dead. He opens the battle by flying off, ordering his companion Thuul to advance on the dragon, and firing two arrows that hit, drawing the dragon's first roar of pain. During the extended chase through the enormous cavern, Thistle flies toward the dragon when it retreats, fails to locate it, and instead finds a nook to hide in and prepare an ambush shot. He successfully uses his experience against dragons to avoid the brunt of the dragon's lightning breath. In the final confrontation in the steep cave, Thistle attempts two shots at the critically weakened dragon and misses both, drawing glares from Orsik and commentary from Vardis. After Orsik kills the dragon, Thistle fires additional arrows into the corpse. He and Orsik travel to the dragon's hoard, approximately 300 feet away, where they recover 2,000 gp, fourteen gems worth 250 gp each, a potion of mind reading, a bag of beans, a potion of superior healing, and a peculiar star-shaped gem. The dragon teeth harvested by Unla Kee are noted as being intended for Thistle, described as "our favorite fey," to be crafted into dragon arrows.

## Orsik

Orsik is present throughout the session and serves as one of the primary combatants against the blue dragon. In the opening sequence, he leads the party down a twisty passage, repeatedly commenting on the shoddy non-Dwarven workmanship, and proposes following the right-hand rule at the intersection, which leads the party in a circle back to where they started. He is the first to identify the creature they are hearing, having previously mentioned that the Netherese Mountain Range is home to blue dragons. When Vardis questions why they are here, Orsik articulates the three things of value in the mountains: granite, dragon hoards, and Netherese Artifacts, with the last being potentially the most powerful magic items in the realms. He helps sketch a battle plan before Thistle launches the fight early. In combat, Orsik dashes to the dragon and slashes it four times, causing it to bleed and sheathing it in booming energy. He also calls out to Unla Kee that all his third and fourth level spells have already been used. When the dragon flies 80 feet away, Orsik throws hand axes and hits once. He takes a lightning bolt from the dragon that halves his strength, leaving him at 8 HP by the final confrontation. He spots a corridor the group had ignored and uses it to flank the dragon, emerging from the tunnel and hitting the dragon with a Firebolt rather than using his bow, to Thistle's visible disbelief. When the dragon asks to reach an accommodation, Orsik flatly refuses. In the final stand in the steep cave, Orsik charges up a 60-foot climb, positions himself five feet from the top, takes another lightning bolt (dodging the bulk of the damage), and ultimately rushes the last five feet and kills the dragon with his sword. After the battle, with the spellcasters out of spells and the party bloodied, Orsik declares they have what they came for and calls off further exploration.

## Vardis

Vardis is present throughout the session. His plan to obliterate a large kobold force with a powerful area attack is foiled when only a single kobold is in the chamber, and his arrogance causes him to fall into a pit in the process. After clambering out, he examines the charred kobold remains and takes interest in a shiny idol of Tiamat. During the approach through the passage, he struggles to contain his boredom at Orsik's Dwarven craftsmanship commentary. When the dragon is identified, Vardis reflects on how the party came to be there, tracing it back to joining Mary's Fighters of the Light for a 5 GP membership fee when they were broke in Everlund, and notes with irony that he is about to fight a blue dragon to protect that 5 Gold. During the battle, Vardis is swarmed by kobolds alongside Unla Kee. He fires a fireball at the dragon that scores only a glancing blow as the dragon dodges most of the damage. He misses a kobold with his sword at one point, and later kills a kobold with his bow just as it was about to strike Unla Kee. He remarks on the dragon's apparent aggression, though Unla Kee corrects him that it was Thistle's Tiamat question that started the fight. Vardis monitors the final dragon confrontation from a distance and comments on Thistle's two misses.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is present throughout the session and handles the spellcasting and post-battle harvesting roles. During the battle, he reaches for Fireball components only to be reminded by Orsik that all his third and fourth level spells are already spent. He visibly pouts and instead casts Sleep. He later shouts that he is running out of second-level spells, prompting Vardis to suggest Magic Missile, after which three kobolds are killed. He uses Rime's Binding Ice to kill four materialized kobolds at once. As the dragon makes its final stand, Unla Kee decides to abandon the kobold fight and run toward the dragon, though he arrives only after Orsik has already killed it. At the dragon's corpse, Unla Kee performs the harvesting, extracting dragon meat, dragon blood for potions of dragon strength, dragon teeth to be crafted into dragon arrows for Thistle, dragon bone to be crafted into dragon weapons, and a dragon horn to craft a dragon wand. He then calls for the party to continue onward.

## The Blue Dragon

The blue dragon is encountered in the enormous cavern of the Netherese Mountain Range. It is identified as a follower of Tiamat, declaring "Tiamat for-ever!" when questioned by Thistle, which immediately provokes the fight. In the opening exchange, it is struck by two arrows from Thistle, stung by acid from the guard drake, slashed four times by Orsik, and sheathed in booming energy. It disengages and flies 80 feet to the middle of the cavern. It later uses its lightning breath on Orsik, halving his strength. It retreats west, out of view, then investigates its surroundings and spots Thistle, attempting to maneuver and breathe lightning on him; Thistle avoids the worst of it. When bloodied and cornered in a steep southern cave with a 60-foot climb, the dragon attempts negotiation, asking if an accommodation could be reached and noting it felt it should ask, described as a "polite dragon" moment. It fires one more lightning bolt at Orsik, who dodges most of the damage, but with only 6 HP remaining the dragon is killed by Orsik's sword strike. Its hoard contains 2,000 gp, fourteen gems worth 250 gp each, a potion of mind reading, a bag of beans, a potion of superior healing, and a peculiar star-shaped gem. Its body is harvested by Unla Kee for meat, blood, teeth, bone, and horn.

## Thuul

Thuul is a guard drake companion to Thistle, present during the battle. At Thistle's order, Thuul advances on the dragon in the opening moments of the fight and stings it with a squirt of acid. Thistle orders Thuul forward again in a subsequent turn. Thuul's subsequent actions in the battle are not detailed beyond these initial commands.
