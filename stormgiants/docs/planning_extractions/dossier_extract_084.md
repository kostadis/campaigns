## King Hekaton

King Hekaton appears in the throne room area when the teleportation attempt fails. He observes that something is blocking the teleport spell and suspects that Forbiddance has been cast, likely by the Cult of the Kraken. He acknowledges the Kraken Society's ability to hide things and their access to mysterious powers and magic. He teleports the 3HPP to just outside the great doors of Ironslag the following morning, fulfilling his role as host before parting ways with the party.

Hekaton is also referenced as the source of information the SEER used to locate the party — he shared the party's whereabouts with her because he is a friend of the Lord's Alliance. According to the SEER, Hekaton trusts the 3HPP and sees them as heroic. He reportedly told her he would consider a permanent alliance between the Giant King and the Lord's Alliance if the party dealt with his problem — the rogue giant lords — and that he insisted it had to be specifically these four adventurers, citing a belief that this is a sign from the All-father. The SEER notes she tried to offer him other agents but he refused. His current whereabouts are his own domain following the party's departure.

## Imperator Serissa

Serissa appears in the teleportation chamber alongside the party and her father, King Hekaton. She makes one final attempt to persuade the 3HPP to deal with the problem affecting both their peoples and the giants, asking directly if she can't convince them. When Orsik refuses, she sighs and attempts to teleport the heroes back to their cloud castle, but the spell fails due to the Forbiddance effect. She explains to the group that the Kraken Society knows how to hide things and that despite her best efforts — including paying for divination magic — she could not locate the ship Hekaton was held on. Her last known location is the Giant King's stronghold. She does not accompany the party further.

## The SEER

The SEER appears several days into the party's journey toward Ascore, arriving at their campsite accompanied by her gold pseudodragon, Hsing. She used Divination magic to find the party's exact location, and acknowledged that King Hekaton — a friend of the Lord's Alliance — provided her with information on their whereabouts.

She arrives with a prepared negotiation, having gathered intelligence on each party member's individual weaknesses and desires. Her offers are as follows: to Orsik, she offers fame, statues, and monuments across Faerûn by publicly claiming his infiltration of Hekaton's court was an elaborate ruse she orchestrated, rebranding him as a hero rather than an annoying prat; to Unla Kee, she offers return of the cloud castle without Escarlotta and a tax-free casino license with permits across all Lord's Alliance cities; to Vardis, she offers to solve his Lorellon problem, restore his access to the temples, potentially engineer an apology, and guarantee Orsik's survival and heroic reputation; to Thistle, she offers arrows of dragon slaying and a powerful magical bow.

Her condition: the party must abandon their quest to kill Iymrith and instead deal with the rogue giant lords. She frames this as a historic opportunity — the first chance in 40,000 years for peace between Giants and the northern kingdoms — and argues that Hekaton will agree to a permanent alliance with the Lord's Alliance only if the 3HPP handle his problem. She is visibly frustrated that Hekaton refused all other agents and insisted specifically on this group.

All four party members refuse. The SEER becomes flustered and then furious, denigrating the significance of killing dragons and arguing that saving the realms is far more important. When Orsik rejects her framing of heroism and she threatens to curse his name for a thousand generations, he attempts to strike her. She is protected by sanctuary. Vardis casts a fireball, which she counters. Unla Kee traps her in a forcecage made of diamond-hard material using ruby dust. She smiles — and escapes, apparently via time stop followed by teleportation.

She leaves a written note addressed to "Orsic" stating she will not bad-mouth him, that she disagrees with his choices, but that if he defeats Iymrith, she asks him to at least consider doing the realms a favor afterward. Her current whereabouts are unknown following her escape.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this chunk but is referenced as a source of prior information about the ruins of Ascore. Thistle recalls or references Uncle Joon's descriptions of the city: the millennia-old road, the great door set in a hill flanked by crouching stone griffon statues, the stone ships half-buried in sand, the red pyramids at the center of the city, and a specific warning to avoid the red pyramids. Uncle Joon's information proves accurate and guides the party's navigation through the ruined city.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta does not appear in this chunk but is referenced during the SEER's negotiation. The SEER offers Unla Kee the return of his cloud castle "without Escarlotta," implying Escarlotta is currently associated with or in control of the castle in some way that Unla Kee would find undesirable. Unla Kee dismisses the offer, asserting the castle is already his. No further details about Escarlotta's current status or location are provided.

## Lord Drylund

Lord Drylund does not appear in this chunk but is referenced briefly by Unla Kee during his internal reasoning about the Kraken Society's apparent awareness of the cloud castle. Unla Kee recalls how Lord Drylund died as though something had triggered it, and uses this memory to speculate that members of the Kraken Society may be interlinked in a hive mind, which would explain how word of the castle spread so rapidly. Drylund is deceased; his death is being used as evidence in Unla Kee's private theorizing.

## Iymrith

Iymrith does not appear in this chunk but is the central subject of the party's current quest and is referenced repeatedly. The 3HPP is traveling to her lair near the ruins of Ascore at the edge of the Anauroch desert. Orsik frames killing Iymrith as the defining heroic act — killing her or dying in the attempt. The SEER argues against this prioritization, calling dragons self-absorbed, narcissistic, and arrogant agents of chaos and asserting that anyone can kill a dragon. Thistle is described as pained by the choice to continue toward Iymrith rather than accept the SEER's offer, but agrees with the party's decision. Her lair is described as close to Ascore. At the end of the session, two blue dragons with purple worm lower halves emerge from the sand near the party — possibly guardians or agents associated with Iymrith, though this is not confirmed in the text.

## Krasus

Krasus does not appear in this chunk but is referenced by Thistle during travel through the Silver Marches. Thistle explains that the desert of Anauroch is a side effect of the mad king Krasus's actions, which brought down the Kingdom of Netheril. He is described as a mad king responsible for a catastrophic historical event that reshaped the geography of the region.
