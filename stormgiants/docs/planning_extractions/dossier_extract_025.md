## Jacob Hysstryn

Jacob Hysstryn, along with his brother Persson, approached Thistle and convinced her to investigate the mysterious death of their brother Mirak. Jacob's motivations appear to be seeking justice or answers regarding his brother's death. He is directly related to Mirak Hysstryn, whose body was discovered in the attic of the Six Windows Inn.

## Persson Hysstryn

Persson Hysstryn, alongside his brother Jacob, convinced Thistle to dig into the mysterious death of their brother Mirak. Like Jacob, Persson's motivations center on uncovering the truth behind Mirak's death. He is directly related to Mirak Hysstryn, whose remains were found in the attic of the Six Windows Inn.

## Mirak Hysstryn

Mirak Hysstryn does not appear alive in these events but is central to the chapter's murder plot. His remains were discovered in the attic of the Six Windows Inn after a tip from a drunk priest of Helm found passed out in the Talking Troll. Mirak had an affair with Urgala Meltimer, the ex-adventurer who owns the Northshield House Inn. His death is being investigated at the request of his brothers Jacob and Persson. Using his remains as evidence, the group convinced Lord Protector Darathra to imprison a person named Tolmara until a cleric capable of casting Speak with the Dead could be found. Mirak's cause of death remains officially unconfirmed pending that spell.

## Urgala Meltimer

Urgala Meltimer is an ex-adventurer who owns the Northshield House Inn. She had an affair with the deceased Mirak Hysstryn. She shared personal history with the party, including the death of her first wife in the Underdark. She provided information about the Dessarin Hills, describing various locations suitable for wildcat mining — areas with enough treasure to scrounge but not enough to be worth controlling by larger interests, and inhabited by bandits and uncivilized types. She provided the party with a map of one area she considered of extreme interest. Her relationship with the party appears cooperative and forthcoming.

## Darathra

Darathra is the Lord Protector of the town. When presented with Mirak's remains by the party, she agreed to imprison Tolmara until a cleric capable of casting Speak with the Dead could be located. She also demanded that the heroes remain in town until the next day due to the commotion they caused. Her stance appears to be one of cautious authority — willing to act on the evidence presented but also asserting her jurisdiction over the party's movements.

## Tolmara

Tolmara does not appear directly in these events but is implicated in the death of Mirak Hysstryn. On the strength of the remains recovered from the Six Windows Inn, the Lord Protector Darathra ordered Tolmara imprisoned pending a Speak with the Dead casting. No further details about Tolmara's identity, background, or motive are revealed in this summary.

## Alaestra

Alaestra is one of the owners of the Lionshield Coster outpost known as Lion's Share in town. She and her co-owner Narth were in disarray when Orsik and Vardis arrived to shop, because their vault had been breached. The vault contained special keys integral to the Lionshield Coster's interplanar supply chain. Alaestra was relieved to find outsiders willing to help investigate the theft, suggesting she may distrust or feel alienated from the local population, likely due to the social disruption the Lionshield Coster's planerized trade model has caused among local merchants.

## Narth

Narth is co-owner of the Lionshield Coster outpost Lion's Share alongside Alaestra. He was present when Orsik and Vardis arrived and was similarly in disarray over the vault breach. The stolen vault contents included special keys for the company's interplanar supply chain. No further details about Narth's individual actions or statements are recorded beyond his shared distress over the theft.

## Orthovir

Orthovir is the master harness maker in town. When presented with the leather harness found at the scene of the Lion's Share vault breach, he admitted he did not make it. He identified it as the work of someone in Waterdeep, and noted it was sized to fit a thin person of approximately 150 pounds.

## Harriet Uldinath

Harriet Uldinath operates a shop identified as T17. When shown the masterfully crafted grappling hook found at the Lion's Share vault breach, she did not recognize it and insisted it was not from her shop. She acknowledged it was superior to anything she could produce and suggested it might be the work of her competitor, Ghelryn Foehammer.

## Ghelryn Foehammer

Ghelryn Foehammer is a dwarf craftsman operating out of T18 who values privacy strongly. When approached by the party regarding the grappling hook, he stated that his belief in privacy does not extend to protecting burglars. He confirmed the grappling hook was his work and revealed that he sold it to Darz Helgar.

## Darz Helgar

Darz Helgar is the campground overseer and an ex-criminal from Waterdeep. When the party presented him with the leather harness, he immediately gave up information, naming Kaelen — the operator of the Troll Tavern — as the person who forced him into the vault burglary. Darz spent time in Waterdeep's dungeons over ten years ago. Orsik offered to keep Darz's criminal past secret in exchange for information about the thieves guild, to which Darz agreed, saying "It was ten years ago, but I will."

## Kaelen

Kaelen runs the Troll Tavern (referenced elsewhere as the Talking Troll). He does not appear directly in these events but was named by Darz Helgar as the person who coerced him into breaking into the Lion's Share vault and stealing the interplanar supply chain keys. Kaelen is currently the primary suspect in the Lionshield Coster theft. His motivations and connections to any larger criminal organization are not yet revealed.

## KP

KP does not appear in these events but is referenced in the context of the Lionshield Coster's business model. Described as a famous gnomish sage, KP was the first to formally analyze the economic impact of interplanar trade routes across the entirety of existence. KP's research has been controversial: while it demonstrates that achieving a multi-planar optimum for economic efficiency is theoretically possible, critics argue the equations disregard the impact on local social cohesion. The social disruption experienced by Alaestra and Narth at Lion's Share — locals viewing the outpost with caution and disgust due to undercutting of local merchants — is framed as exactly the kind of consequence KP's framework dismissed.
