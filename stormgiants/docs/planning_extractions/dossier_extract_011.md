## Mama Mia

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced through her belongings and living space. The group is standing in what was her bedroom, which was located on the roof of a sunken wizard's tower. Her stone table (disguised as a bed) conceals a staircase leading down into the tower. Her pendant serves as the key to open the stone table mechanism. No further details about Mama Mia are discussed.

## Wallace

Wallace is a young lad from Parnast who accompanies the party into the tower. He cleans the gargoyle statue on Orsik's direction, discovering its arm can be rotated. He is excited about entering "his first tower" and runs down the stairs ahead of the group. He is the one who walks into what he believes is a secret door in the gargoyle room's southeast wall, which turns out not to be truly secret given the visible wear marks. In the shrine of Oghma, Wallace writes "My name is Wallace" in the offered tome. Wallace finds an inscription in the elemental wall section that contains the key information about allied and enemy elemental gods, which he whispers to Orsik. In the laboratory, Wallace discovers several gems and coins totaling 200 GP. Orsik is notably protective of him, warning him to back away from potentially dangerous situations such as the dirt offering.

## Thistle

Thistle is a Fey ranger and spellcaster who serves as the group's scout and trap-checker throughout the exploration. He examines the puzzle of symbols at the landing, quickly helping determine they represent gods in ascending order. He volunteers to guess the god order a second time after the boulder trap, reasoning he can fly away from harm, and succeeds in opening the door by correctly identifying Mystara. He identifies Grumbar from a story told by his uncle, explaining the god's role as boss of elementals, guardian of secret treasures, and preference for sardonyx, jasper, and ruby-eyed statues. He makes an offering of dirt to Grumbar's altar and receives a blessing granting resistance to earth-based harm. He notes the absence of insects and spiders in the tower as suspicious. He identifies the statue in the north passage as Azuth and recognizes Deneir from the earlier symbol. He leads the group through the elemental wall section, singing the water goddess Istishia's inscription for the air wall and getting zapped, then correctly deducing the praise-song system for the remaining walls after Vardis explains the elemental alliances. He checks the laboratory door for traps and finds none, grumbling that the wizard likely expected intruders to be dead by that point. He disputes possession of the wand of webs, noting he is also a spellcaster. His uncle is repeatedly referenced as the source of his extensive knowledge of Faerûnian gods.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is a gambler and apparent cosmic egotist who believes he is the reason the world continues to exist. He steps forward to rotate the gargoyle's arm out of confidence in his luck. He declines to take the tourmaline from Mystara's offering bowl, explaining he is a gambler, not a thief. He makes a water offering at Istishia's altar and receives a blessing granting him immunity to water-based harm. He considers setting himself on fire as an offering to Kossuth but decides against it. He uses comprehend languages to translate the grinding speech of the gargoyle statues. He writes an extended entry in Oghma's tome claiming to know how the world will end, written in a personal cipher he calls "Bad Hand Writing." The divine voice of Oghma corrects him, saying the world does not end that way, then whispers a private correction to Unla alone. He discovers a wand of webs in the moldy room and claims it, though Vardis insists the group will decide later who gets it. He also pockets a set of jeweler's tools from the laboratory. He grabs three spellbooks from the laboratory table, triggering a glyph trap that locks the door and summons two magmin. His final thoughts reveal he is wondering who "Bad Fuul" is, why the SEER believed books would be in this tower, and that he has no intention of giving the spellbooks to the SEER.

## Vardis

Vardis is a cleric traveling with the group, notable for having been exiled — apparently for his willingness to make offerings to gods outside his own faith, which Orsik mockingly calls him out on. He suggests using their mud mephit as the dirt offering for Grumbar. He burns incense at Akadi's air altar and receives a blessing as a result. He repeatedly insists the group should not loot places of worship, refusing to take the topaz-capped staff from Azuth's shrine and discouraging general theft. He is the one who correctly deduces the elemental alliance system — fire and air are allies, earth and water are allies, while fire and water are enemies, and earth and air are enemies — enabling the group to navigate the elemental walls safely. He receives a scroll of lesser restoration that materializes from Oghma's shrine. He initially fails to recognize the symbol of Mystara, contributing to the boulder trap being triggered. He does not recognize the star of Mystara when it first appears. He offers a lit candle and torch to Kossuth's altar on Orsik's behalf, but the offerings have no effect.

## Orsik

Orsik is a dwarf who serves as an authority figure and guide to Wallace throughout the exploration. He directs Wallace to clean the gargoyle statue and warns him to stand back from potentially dangerous situations. He waves off the elemental god altars with practical interest — he wants the resistances — but fails to get Kossuth's blessing despite multiple attempts with fire sources including rope, a candle, a torch, and produce flame. He recognizes Azuth and expresses dissatisfaction with the god's unwillingness to grant blessings. He is irritated at the suggestion of using thieves' tools, insisting he would not have them. He communicates with the gargoyle statues through hand gestures, learns from the largest gargoyle that a wizard's arrogance bound them to protect the tower, but cannot maintain the conversation beyond basic exchanges. He is dismissive of book-keeping and Oghma's shrine, writing "Dwarves are awesome" in the tome and questioning whether it truly counts as knowledge. He shouts "Look what I found!" after Wallace actually discovers the elemental alliance inscription. He is consistently skeptical of and occasionally condescending toward the other characters' abilities, smirking when Thistle is burned by the fire wall. He tries and fails to open the southern locked door in the gargoyle room.

## The SEER

Does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Unla Kee's final thoughts. The SEER apparently directed the group toward this tower with the belief that relevant books might be found there. Unla Kee reflects on this with suspicion and has decided he will not be handing the recovered spellbooks over to the SEER. No further details about the SEER's identity or plans are given in this chunk.

## Bad Fuul

Does not appear in this chunk and is not discussed in dialogue — the name appears only in Unla Kee's final private thoughts. Unla wonders who Bad Fuul is, suggesting the name may be connected to the tower or its owner. No other information is provided.
