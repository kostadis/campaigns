## The Cambian (First)

The first Cambian is a fiendish half-human, half-demon spawn who appears in Silverymoon pursuing Orsik and Unla Kee on behalf of their master. They attempt to charm Unla Kee and fail when the diviner shifts fate in his favor, then attempt to charm Orsik and fail as well. The creature gives chase when the two flee, keeping pace due to its ability to fly — a capability Orsik had not accounted for in his plan. It strikes Unla Kee with spells during the pursuit, badly wounding him (reducing him to roughly 1/3 of his hit points). After following Orsik into Vinby Hearthgood's house, this Cambian demands the return of their master's treasure and offers Orsik a deal: leave the city by tomorrow and say nothing, and their friend will be spared. When Orsik does not immediately agree, the creature nods to its companion, who kills the halfling. Last known status: departed the scene after the killing; not captured or destroyed.

## The Cambian (Second)

The second Cambian pursues Orsik and Unla Kee alongside the first, also flying to keep pace with the fleeing pair. This creature delivers the killing blow to Unla Kee during the chase, striking him dead. It is also this Cambian who locates what the pair were carrying, announcing "Found it." It later kills Vinby Hearthgood's halfling companion inside the house on the nod of the first Cambian after Orsik fails to immediately comply with their ultimatum. Last known status: departed the scene after the killing; not captured or destroyed.

## Nyalah Uuseff

Nyalah Uuseff is the tavern keeper of the Dancing Goat in Silverymoon. She is described as dressed in formal silk clothes and gold. When Orsik arrives carrying Unla Kee's corpse, she shouts asking what happened and states that such creatures — demons — do not exist in Silverymoon. The following morning, she has summoned the city guard to investigate the reports of ungodly races being present in the city, refusing to allow Orsik to remove Unla Kee's body before the Silverwatch arrives. Her last known location is the Dancing Goat.

---

## Vinby Hearthgood

Vinby Hearthgood is a sharply dressed halfling resident of Silverymoon, found drinking fine wine in a well-appointed drawing room with expensive furniture when Orsik and Unla Kee barge through his unlocked door fleeing the Cambians. His companion — an unnamed halfling present in the room — is killed by the second Cambian during the confrontation. Vinby himself survives. He later appears in the Star Court, seated as far from Lady Margaster as possible, and when the Judge asks him to testify, he states only that Orsik is the person who smashed into his house. Unla Kee is outraged by Vinby's focus on breaking and entering when he himself was killed. Vinby's last known location is the Star Court in Silverymoon.

---

## Xamlyn Margaster

Xamlyn Margaster is the Lady Margaster, a beautiful woman wearing fine robes who appears in the Star Court flanked by two gorgeous men. She is identified as associated with House Margaster and is making sizeable investments in Silverymoon, which the Judge notes the city depends upon, along with Waterdeep investments, as the city recovers from the War of the Dragons. She asks imperiously whether the party are the thieves, and her companions confirm it. She requests the opportunity to arrive at an agreement with Orsik and his friends — specifically wanting to speak to the Dwarf and his companions about a possible arrangement. When the court magister is proposed to use magic spells to verify testimony, Xamlyn refuses to submit to the intrusion, reframes events as a drunken party gone wrong, and departs with her men before the examination can occur. Orsik refuses to deal with her, calling her "a literal devil." She is connected to the Cambians who attacked the party, as the creatures referred to acting on behalf of their master and seeking their master's treasure. Her current status is that she left the Star Court and remains at large in Silverymoon.

## The Judge (Star Court)

The Judge of the Star Court in Silverymoon presides over the hearing involving the party, Lady Margaster, and Vinby Hearthgood. He asks Vinby for his testimony, addresses Xamlyn as a "paramount citizen of Silverymoon," and declares that accusations against her require the most robust evidence. When the court magister is suggested, he agrees it is an excellent idea before Xamlyn refuses and departs. He then turns on Orsik, recognizing him from a previous incident ("You're the one that did that THING!"), and accuses him of creating another mess for the city. He explicitly prioritizes Lady Margaster's investment in Silverymoon's post-War of the Dragons economic recovery over the party's grievances, and orders the group to be gone by sunset. He singles out Thistle as "a credit to your kind." His last known location is the Star Court in Silverymoon.

## The High Priest of Mielikki

The high priest of Mielikki's grove in Silverymoon is a woman who is shocked to hear that a demon has killed Unla Kee. She takes his body to the inner sanctum and returns with Unla Kee revived, the cost being one of his magical rings. She then confirms to the Captain of the Silverwatch that the tale of the demon is true and urges him to look into it. Her identity is not named beyond her role. Last known location is Mielikki's grove in Silverymoon.

---

## The Captain (Silverwatch)

The Captain of the Silverwatch arrives at the Dancing Goat to take the party to city courts following Nyalah Uuseff's report. He is stunned when Orsik requests that Unla Kee be raised to provide testimony — specifically at the revelation of a murder by a demonic power. He agrees to the detour to the temple and asks which temple Unla Kee would prefer. After the high priest of Mielikki corroborates the party's account of the demon, he escorts the group to the Star Court and subsequently announces they are expected there. He is not named beyond his title. Last known location is Silverymoon.

## Dral Thelev

Dral Thelev is the half-orc manager of Danniver's tavern in Everlund, described as masculine-looking. He is a contact for Darathra Shendrel and recognizes the platinum brooch the party presents, remarking that something must be amiss with his friend Darathra. He pours a bottle of his finest Silverymoon wine as a deliberate act of personal satisfaction, explaining he enjoys drinking fine wine from Silverymoon to remind himself how much its citizens would be appalled by a "non-goodly race" drinking it. He then discusses Silverymoon's culture at length, describing its people as living on reflected glory from the Silver Sisters and stuck in the past, and notes that the younger Silver Sister is now the Open Lord of Waterdeep and head of the Lords' Alliance. After the conversation, he teleports the party via the wine to Moongleam Tower, a security measure to avoid drawing factional attention to the Harpers' operations. His last known location is his tavern in Everlund.

## Darathra Shendrel

Darathra Shendrel does not appear in this session. She is mentioned by Dral Thelev upon seeing the platinum brooch the party carries, which he identifies as hers. He remarks that "something must be amiss" with her, suggesting the brooch being in the party's hands signals trouble. She is also referenced by Krowen Valharrow as having prepared gifts — a coffer of spell scrolls — as a birthday present intended for exactly this kind of quest recruitment moment. Her current whereabouts are not specified in this session.

## Krowen Valharrow

Krowen Valharrow is a Turami man dressed in the distinctive garb of that region, identified by Vardis as a Harper. He greets the party in Moongleam Tower in Everlund after Dral Thelev teleports them there. He explains the security rationale for the indirect teleportation method: having a known agent appear at the door of Moongleam Tower would alert the Zhentarim and Lords' Alliance, whereas adventurers teleporting from a popular adventurers' haunt go unnoticed. He notes that the factions are too busy monitoring each other to pay attention to the giants. He engages the party on the matter of the sundered Ordning of the Giants, offering the Harpers' teleportation network — a series of teleportation circles spanning the North — as an incentive for the party to investigate and attempt to fix or report on why the Ordning was broken. He presents the party with a coffer of spell scrolls on Darathra Shendrel's behalf. His last known location is Moongleam Tower, Everlund.

## Miery

Miery is an older woman encountered on the main thoroughfare of Everlund, speaking to attentive young men about a Kraken that ravaged the city long ago and warning that it will return with powerful allies to destroy the city using tentacles and mind control. When Orsik passes, she addresses him directly and invites him to join her order to fight this ancient evil. She tests his strength by grabbing his arm and smiles approvingly, then asks for five gold pieces in exchange for membership and a certificate declaring him a fighter of the light. When the party reveals they are broke, she offers an alternative: retrieve an artifact called the Star of Light from a cave a few miles out of town, stolen by kobolds, and she will waive the fee. She clarifies the artifact is of symbolic rather than functional value. She explains her name is Miery — not Mary — as her mother worshipped Mielikki and wanted the name to reflect that devotion. Her last known location is the streets of Everlund near the Bell Market area.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session but is referenced twice by Thistle. First, Thistle recalls that Uncle Joon told him Silverymoon specifically banned Cyric from their city, which informs the decision not to raise Unla Kee at a Cyric temple. Second, as the party leaves Silverymoon, Thistle mentions that Uncle Joon said Silverymoon betrayed the Silver Marches and their dwarf allies, framing this as context for why Orsik's animosity toward the city is understandable.
