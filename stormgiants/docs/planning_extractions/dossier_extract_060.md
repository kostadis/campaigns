## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is a Cloud Giant who controls the cloud castle and is holed up in the control room at the aft of the castle. He refuses to open the door to anyone, having barricaded himself inside after a traumatic series of events. He uses a periscope with mirrors to verify that visitors are not vampires before communicating with them. When the party knocks and identifies themselves as non-vampires, he shifts from aggressive to calmer in tone and begins to speak with them.

Blagothkus is in a state of emotional distress. The ogres Worug and Tak reported that he was heard crying and saying that he had killed his son, though the details of this are not elaborated upon in the session. He has been unwilling to move the castle since it arrived at Parnast, remaining locked in the control room.

He reveals that his current predicament began when he spoke to the Oracle, suggesting he had prior contact with Harshnag and the Temple of the All-Father. He laughs when the party mentions the Oracle chamber, exposing that Harshnag's claim that the chamber had not been opened in a thousand years is a running joke — Harshnag habitually tells visiting "little people" this, hides items needed to access the temple throughout the dungeon, and uses the experience as a worthiness test. Blagothkus treats this as common knowledge among those in Harshnag's circle.

When Orsik asks why Blagothkus does not simply go to the Oracle now, Blagothkus clarifies that the obstacle keeping him trapped is not the dracolich Glazhael but his wife. The session ends on this revelation without further detail about the wife's identity or nature.

His relationship to the vampire Sandesyl is one of victimization: Sandesyl infiltrated the castle, massacred his retinue, and turned the dead into undead. Blagothkus has lost control of most of his castle and is effectively imprisoned in the control room.

Last known whereabouts: locked inside the control room tower at the aft of the cloud castle.

## Sandesyl

Sandesyl does not appear directly in this session but is extensively discussed by the ogres Worug and Tak and inferred from physical evidence throughout the castle.

Sandesyl is described as a "purple lady" and "crazy vampire lady" by Worug. She arrived and declared herself "big boss lady," then was invited in by the ogres who did not know the rules of vampire entry. Once inside, she conducted a massacre of the castle's inhabitants. Those who refused to invite her — specifically Worug and Tak — were safe from her entry into their tower, confirming she is bound by the traditional vampire rule requiring invitation.

After killing the castle's population, Sandesyl turned the dead into undead, creating the army of zombie and skeletal creatures the party has been fighting throughout the castle. She also used the kobold quarters as a living blood bank, keeping kobolds tied to their beds and feeding on them slowly until they died — all of whom are now dead.

The party speculates that Sandesyl may have been the actual proprietor of the castle all along, suggesting she sought a sky-based location for necromantic experiments. The nature of what is inside the glacier is also raised in connection with her possible plans.

Sandesyl and her vampire spawn do not go out during the day, which allowed Worug and Tak to forage for food and which gives the party a window of relative safety.

Unla Kee notes that Sandesyl might conclude she only needs three heroes rather than four, suggesting she may attempt to turn or kill one party member if she decides to cooperate with the rest.

Current status: present on the castle but not encountered this session; active during nighttime hours.

## Worug

Worug is an ogre survivor on the cloud castle, identified by a scar on his face. He and his companion Tak barricaded themselves in a side tower and refused to invite the vampire Sandesyl inside, which saved their lives while the rest of the castle's ogre population was slaughtered.

When the party opens their barred door using Orsik's knock spell, both Worug and Tak scream in terror. Once calmed with goodberries provided by Thistle, Worug becomes communicative. He explains the events of the past few months: Sandesyl's arrival, her massacre of the castle's population, Blagothkus' emotional breakdown over his son, and the daily routine of sneaking out for food while the vampires are inactive.

Worug attempts to hug Thistle out of gratitude and is rebuffed.

In exchange for a promise of evacuation from the castle, Worug shares the secret that a dracolich named Glazhael resides inside the glacier the castle is built around — originally the steed of Talis the White, killed by the First Faction, now returned as a dracolich that attempted to claim the castle and was lured away on a false quest for an artifact before Blagothkus retook control.

Worug's emotional dynamic with Tak is hinted at — Tak's over-eager denial of romantic feelings and Worug's confused but warm response suggest an unspoken attachment between them.

Worug and Tak declare their tower a safe refuge for the party and state they will not leave it. They invite the party to return there for rest and safety from the vampire.

Last known whereabouts: barricaded in their tower on the cloud castle.

## Tak

Tak is an ogre survivor on the cloud castle, identified by a bandage on his right hand. He survived alongside Worug by refusing to invite the vampire Sandesyl into their tower.

Tak is hungry and visibly weakened when the party finds them, and he consumes goodberries provided by Thistle. He is slightly more reticent than Worug but joins in negotiating with the party.

When Vardis jokingly suggests the ogres' secret might be that one of them likes the other, Tak coughs "a little too eagerly" and insists it is ridiculous — then immediately turns to Worug and asks why he never said anything, inadvertently confirming the implication. This exchange is left unresolved in the session.

Tak formally promises the party evacuation from the castle in exchange for the information about Glazhael, which Worug then delivers. Tak puts his arm around Worug as they leave the party's company.

Last known whereabouts: barricaded in their tower on the cloud castle alongside Worug.

## Glazhael

Glazhael does not appear directly in this session but is revealed through Worug's account.

Glazhael was originally a white dragon and the steed of Talis the White. The group known as the First Faction killed Glazhael. The dragon subsequently became a dracolich and attempted to seize the cloud castle for itself. Blagothkus lured Glazhael away on a false quest to find some unspecified artifact. When the return of Tiamat was thwarted, Blagothkus retook control of the castle and Glazhael returned — and now resides within the glacier that the cloud castle is built around.

Orsik's response upon learning of Glazhael's presence — "A vampire, a dracolich, and an army of undead. How badly do we want this castle?" — indicates the party views Glazhael as a significant and dangerous threat.

Current status: a dracolich residing inside the glacier beneath the cloud castle.

## Talis the White

Talis the White does not appear in this session but is referenced by Worug in his account of Glazhael's history.

Talis the White was the original rider and companion of the white dragon Glazhael. No further detail about Talis is provided in this session beyond this relationship.

## Harshnag

Harshnag does not appear in this session but is discussed at some length by Blagothkus during their conversation through the control room door.

Blagothkus reveals that Harshnag's claim — that the Oracle chamber had not been opened in a thousand years — is a well-known joke among those familiar with Harshnag's habits. Harshnag deliberately hides the items required to access the Temple of the All-Father throughout the dungeon and brings "little people" to the temple as a worthiness test: those who can solve the puzzle and survive are deemed worthy; those who cannot are deemed unworthy. Blagothkus treats this as characteristic behavior that he finds amusing.

This revelation reframes the party's earlier experience at the Oracle as a deliberately staged trial engineered by Harshnag rather than a genuine ancient mystery.
