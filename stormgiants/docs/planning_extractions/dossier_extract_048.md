## Methrammar Aerasumé

High Lord of the Silver Marches, ruler of Silverymoon, and High Marshal of the Argent Legion. He summoned the 3HPP through a captain of the Silverwatch, framing the encounter as an obligation for them to present diplomatic credentials as envoys from Everlund rather than a request. In the audience chamber, he expressed enthusiastic familiarity with the traveling play, having seen it twice already, and showed particular delight in the segment "Mordekainens' Best Home Illusions" and the rotating endings. He eagerly ordered an extended run in the royal theater and initially offered 50,000 gold pieces before being restrained by his High Treasurer, ultimately settling on 1,000 gold pieces and free use of the Royal Theater. He refused outright to extradite Xamlyn Margaster, citing her status as a guest of the city and member of a respectable Waterdhavian noble house. He also acknowledged that the 3HPP's previous removal from the city was a "terrible misunderstanding" corrected by High Captain Horix Zoar. Last seen in his audience chamber in Silverymoon.

## Taern "Thunderspell" Hornblade

High Mage of Silverymoon. Present in the audience chamber during the 3HPP's meeting with High Lord Aerasumé. He examined the letters of introduction presented by the party and declared them satisfactory. Described as the former ruler of Silverymoon. Distantly related to Uzorel "The Viper" Hornblade. No further action or dialogue attributed to him beyond authenticating the party's credentials.

## Xamlyn Margaster

Does not appear directly in this chunk but is a central subject of discussion. She is the target the 3HPP came to Silverymoon to pursue, having committed what Thistle describes as "unpardonable sins" in Everlund. High Lord Aerasumé refused to extradite her, identifying her as a member of the noble house of Margaster, a respectable Waterdhavian noble house, and a guest of the city. Senneth confirmed that the Cambions who appeared at the trial are the men who follow Lady Margaster. Thistle concluded that killing her is too easy and that they need to disgrace her by exposing her devils. Her current whereabouts are within Silverymoon as a protected guest of the High Lord.

## Senneth

Cleric of Mielikki who previously raised Thistle from the dead. She was visited by the 3HPP at Mielikki's Glade to discuss the presence of devils in Silverymoon. She was not surprised by the idea of a devil existing in a city of 40,000 but dismissed the likelihood of active cults, noting that after the defeat of the Cult of the Dragon there was a general crackdown and the "smart ones packed their bags." When Thistle described the appearance of the Cambions from the trial, she was shocked and identified them as the men who follow Lady Margaster. She began drawing connections between the 3HPP, the Margasters, and the play involving a devil and a demon before being redirected by Vardis's question about the Lady's College. Last seen at Mielikki's Glade in Silverymoon.

## Sage Vusk

A traveling scholar giving a lecture at the Lady's College on the topic of Cambions. His lecture hall was largely empty. He presented updated scholarly views on Cambion origins — that they are the progeny of any union between a demon and a non-demon, not merely male demons and female humans — while personally holding the traditionalist view that Cambions cannot possess free will due to their infernal nature. He noted Cambions serve as foot soldiers in the Blood War and are disposable to their masters. He provided tactical information: that a Cambion's Fiendish Charm requires line of sight to the target, and that magical darkness, blindness, or invisibility might provide protection. He confirmed that Dispel Magic works against a Cambion's disguise but warned that using it socially is considered a grave breach of etiquette and can provoke duels. He laughed at the suggestion of banning magic in Silverymoon. Last seen at his lecture podium in the Lady's College.

## Anakis

A young tiefling student at the Lady's College wearing an "Ask Me" sign. She enthusiastically greeted the 3HPP and directed them to Sage Vusk's lecture on Cambions, describing it as her favorite topic and noting that nobody in Silverymoon attends his lectures because locals assume they will never encounter an infernal creature. She appeared briefly self-conscious when Orsik stared at her, preemptively defending the Lady's College's open-mindedness toward non-goodly races. Last seen in the hallway of the Lady's College.

## Krowen Valharrow

Does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced through a Harper agent who speaks on his behalf. Krowen sent the agent to pressure the 3HPP about their inaction on the Giant threat, noting that the party had received information from the Oracle but had not acted on it. He offered treasure maps as an incentive and warned that access to magical pathways would be lost if the party did not begin moving immediately. Following Orsik's refusal and termination of the relationship, it is implied Krowen's offer was rejected entirely. His last known position is as a Harper figure with authority over the party's contract, now severed.

## The Harper Agent

An unnamed elf wearing a purple robe and openly displaying a Harper symbol. He approached the 3HPP in the evening in Silverymoon on behalf of Krowen Valharrow to deliver a warning about the party's inaction on the Giant crisis. He cited the near-sacking of Bryn Shander by Frost Giants and the Hill Giant attack on Goldenfields, and warned that magical pathway access would lapse if the party did not move immediately. He accused the party of allying with cultists. When Orsik refused his terms and ended the contract, the agent took the treasure maps back, made a parting remark criticizing the use of mercenaries over full Harper agents, and referenced someone named Ezra as the origin of that policy. He left after Orsik dismissed him. Last seen departing from Silverymoon.

## Uzorel "The Viper" Hornblade

Silverymoon's local Zhentarim agent and a distant relative of Taern Hornblade, the former ruler of Silverymoon. He was initially reluctant to meet Orsik but agreed upon learning his reputation from Parnast and Triboar. Described as diminutive with lanky limbs, pale skin, and a sharp angular goatee, compensating for his lack of physical presence with tact and charm. He was visibly suspicious and paranoid when Orsik offered information without asking for payment, reaching for his sword at the word "friends" before being reassured that Orsik's motivation was revenge against a Harper. He accepted Orsik's betrayal of the locations of the teleportation circles in Everlund and Yartar without payment and promised to ensure that the Zhentarim leadership would know of Orsik's gesture. Last seen in Silverymoon.

## Rebecca Deakin

An archivist employed by Wyrmworks in their cataloging department. Thistle found her as a scholar to research Adminod Kroskas. She is described as possessing unparalleled intellect and mastery of all dragon-related topics. She agreed to research the question for a fee of 1,800 gold pieces and instructed the party to return in a week. Last seen in Silverymoon.

## Brazzik

A crew member aboard the Great Airship gifted by the ancient red dragon Klauth. He self-described as a "loresmith" and offered information about the Grandfather Tree for a fee. After Thistle demonstrated partial knowledge, Orsik and Vardis paid him 50 gold pieces for more complete information. He confirmed Thistle's account of the Seelie Dryads Halisa and Helia protecting the tree as a punishment from the Fey Queen, warned that killing the dryads carries powerful and dangerous consequences, noted that patrols from the Tree Ghost Tribe of the Uthgardt sometimes pass through the area, and added that the dryads have allied with various creatures in the surrounding forest to protect the tree. He also interrupted an elf crewmember who was questioning Vardis about being kicked out of Lorien's cult. Last seen aboard the Great Airship flying over the Evermoors.

## Halisa

Does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced during the discussion about the Grandfather Tree. Described as one of a pair of Seelie Dryads — alongside Helia — who misspoke at a ball, offended the Fey Queen, and were punished by being bound to protect the Grandfather Tree for an indeterminate period. Brazzik warned that killing either dryad carries powerful and dangerous consequences. Their current status is as guardians of the Grandfather Tree.

## Helia

Does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced alongside Halisa as the second of the pair of Seelie Dryads punished by the Fey Queen to protect the Grandfather Tree. Subject to the same warnings about dangerous consequences if harmed. Current status is as a guardian of the Grandfather Tree.
