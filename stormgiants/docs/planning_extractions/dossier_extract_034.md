## Harthal Zymorven

Harthal Zymorven traveled with the 3HPP on the Rauvin Road as they escorted him home to Zymorven Hall. During the crag cat ambush, he hid behind Vardis rather than fighting. When Unla Kee appeared distracted during the battle, Harthal screamed at her demanding she act. He was relieved when Orsik suggested he had been "scouting," particularly given reference to a previous incident. Upon arriving at Zymorven Hall, he was reunited with his father and declared it "a great bit of adventuring." He is last seen happily returned home at Zymorven Hall.

## Lord Zymorven

Lord Zymorven was surprised to see Thistle arrive at Zymorven Hall without his son. He feared his son was dead, then expressed escalating shock as Thistle explained the full story — the murder charge, death row, the prison break, the ally disguised as him, and the vampire jailer. He accepted the news with good humor, noting he would need to avoid the vampire rather than Yartar entirely, and laughed. When Harthal arrived with the rest of the group, Lord Zymorven was delighted. He paid the party their reward, described the prison break as "a good old fashioned prison break" of a kind he'd never done, and winked. He remarked that his own adventuring days were over, and upon noticing Orsik carrying the sword, expressed hope that it would bring Orsik better luck than it brought his son. He is last seen at Zymorven Hall.

## Urgala

Urgala appeared at the Talking Troll in Triboar and invited the 3HPP to visit her establishment, the Northshield House. There she delivered the bad news that Kaelen had borrowed 5,000 gold pieces in the party's name, leveraging their reputation and the fame of their exploits in Parnast to secure the loans from multiple creditors including the Hand of Yartar, the Zhentarim, and local criminals. She warned that failure to repay would result in violence against Kaelen and then the party. She told them they would need a full month of sold-out performances to raise the necessary funds. She also provided intelligence on Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation — a magical device held by a tribe of Kobolds south of Yartar in the Dessarin Hills — noting the Kobolds had stolen it from one of Geoffrey's messengers and that Geoffrey had been searching for it. When asked how she knew, she said she wasn't always an innkeeper. She expressed confidence in the party despite the situation. On the night of the successful performance, she informed Vardis that they had a hit and that audiences wanted to return the following night. She is last seen at the Northshield House in Triboar.

## Kaelen

Kaelen does not physically appear until the party arrives at the Talking Troll in Triboar, though his actions set the plot in motion. He had borrowed 5,000 gold pieces in the party's name, purchased the theater property outright rather than renting it, and produced a play that was critically deficient in script, direction, acting, and set design. When confronted by Unla Kee, he was initially confident about the script before breaking down. He revised the script multiple times under Unla Kee's direction, first producing an archaic dialect version and then, after guidance, a cleaned-up version incorporating the "play within a play" framing device. He was protective and oddly touchy about the play's bizarre ending — which was later revealed to be intentional. Kaelen had designed the entire play as a summoning ritual, intending the theatergoers as sacrificial fodder for a demon that would destroy the town, as revenge on a theater-going public that had never appreciated his creative genius. When the performance instead succeeded and the demon Bob Saget proved to be a comic rather than a destroyer, Kaelen's plans were completely subverted — and he reacted with ecstatic declarations that he would be famous. He is last seen at the Talking Troll theater in Triboar.

## Everard Barney

Everard Barney is the director of Kaelen's play at the Talking Troll in Triboar. He introduced himself to Thistle as heavyset, wearing a multi-colored shirt and a purple scarf over a buzz cut, and identified himself as completely committed to Kaelen's vision. He immediately broke down sobbing when Thistle arrived, confessing that Kaelen crept him out, that his actors ignored his direction, and that his entire family were bookkeepers — he had branched out into theater and feared being forced to return to Waterdeep to work as an accountant if the play failed. He lacked understanding of his actors' motivations and had made poor staging decisions, including placing the lead actor on a pile of dung for a soliloquy. With Thistle's guidance he reconsidered that staging, moving toward a hill with the dung below. He initially resisted the suggestion to incorporate actor drunkenness into the play but resolved to yell at the actors louder in the morning, agreeing with Thistle's comparison to pouring water on a drunk uncle. He was genuinely helped by Thistle's coaching. At the end of rehearsal he sheepishly asked about the finale, and Thistle advised him to change nothing. He is last seen at the Talking Troll theater in Triboar.

## Amy

Amy is the lead actor in Kaelen's play at the Talking Troll. Thistle observed that Amy presents as cheerful and lighthearted but is fundamentally status-driven and evasive, never committing to definitive statements — suggesting a mysterious or guarded past. Amy had ideas about the lead role that Kaelen had been pushing back on, but was too timid and milquetoast to advocate for herself. When approached by Orsik in the disguise of acting coach "Geoff Mamet," Amy was deferential despite initially questioning Geoff's credentials and whether Kaelen had approved the hire. She complained about confusing lines and over-the-top gestures required at the play's end. Orsik's coaching focused on energy and obedience to direction, which proved effective. After the session, Amy mistakenly thanked Orsik for hiring Geoff Mamet and offered to show him the improved performance. She is last seen at the Talking Troll theater in Triboar.

## Bob Saget

Bob Saget is a demon from the Abyss who appeared at the end of the play's first performance, apparently summoned through what was originally designed as a ritual embedded in the play's script and staging. He manifested with a theatrical appearance: purple skin stretched over a skull-like face, giant purple-irised eyes, jagged teeth, a purple top hat and formal jacket with animated tassels, embroidered pants, and white gloves. Despite his terrifying appearance, he performed stand-up comedy to an ecstatic crowd. He introduced himself to Unla Kee as "the tormentor of the kings who killed their jesters," explaining he had spent eternity tormenting kings with dad jokes. He stated his pronouns as he/him. He expressed delight at being summoned, calling the Abyss boring and welcoming the new audience for his material. He recognized the party by name, addressing Orsik directly when asked if they should fight. He credited the 3HPP with arranging his escape from the Abyss and noted they were the first to summon him in eons. He is last seen performing on stage at the Talking Troll theater in Triboar, having become the play's unexpected star.
