## Orsik

Orsik authored a lengthy letter sealed with his logo, delivered to the 3HPP, outlining a plan to use the Planar Ally spell to summon Bob Saget as an alternative to freeing the dracolich. In the letter, he revealed that he had previously arranged for Bob to be sent to the Astral Plane rather than killed, having convinced Unlakee to do so, and that he had also allowed Bob's drow allies to escape safely. He framed this as deliberate and heroic rather than a failure.

During the planning discussion, Orsik engaged in written correspondence with Thistle debating the merits of the Bob plan. When the plan was challenged by Vardis and others on the grounds that Bob could use the dracolich for his own ends and that giving a demon a dracolich was not heroic, Orsik internally acknowledged the valid points while tabling the plan, convinced he would revisit it when the team was ready. He also floated the idea of double-crossing Bob by giving him the phylactery and then sending the dracolich to retrieve it.

During the zombie battle atop the control tower, Orsik asked about using Mold Earth on stone, admonished the group to conserve spells, expressed frustration at Unla Kee's use of Stinking Cloud against poison-immune zombies, ordered Unla Kee to dismiss the spell, and used his decanter of endless liquids to dump two gallons of mayonnaise on an approaching zombie (causing it to slip but not die). He also goaded Blagothkus by informing him that his son's ghost had said he wasn't good enough for the All-Father.

When Glazhael advanced and used dragon fear, Orsik was unaffected and retreated to cover. As Glazhael flew away after icing the tower, Orsik shouted that Bob was still an option.

## Thistle

Thistle received and read Orsik's letter, was impressed by the plan, and penned a reply raising three concerns: that Bob might betray them to see them fail, that Bob might betray them to obtain the dracolich for free, and that Bob controlling a dracolich might be worse than a free dracolich. Orsik criticized Thistle's grammar, capitalization, and prose quality.

During the tower battle, Thistle fired on the Ogre Zombies alongside the others. He flew off to the side to scout for incoming threats and spotted Glazhael the Undying sneaking toward the tower, shouting a warning to the group. Orsik, distrustful of Thistle's recent perception, initially dismissed the warning. Thistle was affected by Glazhael's dragon fear along with Thuul. Heeding Blagothkus's warning, Thistle moved away from the tower and shot two more zombies. He observed that Glazhael's fear breath lacked conviction, as if her heart wasn't in it.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee participated in the morning planning discussion, asking that the group examine the exact rules for Planar Ally. He shook his head and refused when Orsik suggested giving Bob a wand of wonder as payment. During the debate, he noted his background at a school of magic where a forbidden lore teacher focused on the dangers of Ravenloft, and he digressed into an extended account of Slerdax, a minor dark lord trapped in the mists of Ravenloft who runs a cursed restaurant. He acknowledged having taken the advanced course on minor lords with small domains.

During the zombie battle, Unla Kee used Toll the Dead on a zombie, irritating Vardis. He then used the small boulder (boulder of wonder) from the one stone, producing a Stinking Cloud — useless against the poison-immune zombies and blocking line of sight. When ordered by Orsik to dismiss the spell, he complied but muttered that living meant taking risks and going outside the box. Later, he successfully used the giant artifact to envelop the zombies in darkness, which Orsik cheered but Vardis noted prevented use of Toll the Dead. Unla Kee was subsequently overcome by Glazhael's dragon fear. While retreating into the building, he conjured the artifact's wild magic again, causing the tower to shimmer green and become covered in grass, reasoning that the darkness slowed zombies but the grass would speed them outside — a tradeoff he considered acceptable.

## Vardis

Vardis participated in the morning planning session, asking what prevented Bob from sending the dracolich to attack them and pointing out that under Planar Ally, Bob returns to the Abyss with an emissary on the prime material plane whose phylactery would be in the Abyss, making it invulnerable. He noted that giving a demon a dracolich did not seem heroic. When Orsik suggested a double-cross, Vardis observed that it would be a "stab" rather than a backstab since Bob was never on their side.

During the tower battle, Vardis fired on the Ogre Zombies, used Toll the Dead on the same zombie Unla Kee had weakened, noting it was the best cantrip against injured targets, and criticized Unla Kee's choice of Stinking Cloud. He observed that Glazhael was "shadowboxing" the group — advancing but not fully committing. He was unaffected by Glazhael's dragon fear, shot an arrow into the darkness to kill a zombie, and deliberately left a weakened zombie for Unla Kee.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is the cloud giant accompanying the 3HPP, new to the group. During the zombie battle atop the control tower, he dropped a boulder on an oncoming zombie when prompted by Orsik, and sardonically confirmed he had more boulders and only the very best. Upon hearing Unla Kee recount his credentials in arcane lore, Blagothkus stated he had met and knew better magic users and had "a whole binder full" of them.

When Glazhael advanced with dragon fear, Blagothkus announced he was going downstairs rather than standing to be killed, citing that he would return to fight once the dragon acted. When Orsik challenged this by invoking his wife's instructions that he participate in the battle, Blagothkus confirmed he would return with more rocks. He also explained to the group that he had deduced the zombies' tactical purpose: to occupy the party long enough to give Glazhael time to kill them. He tossed another boulder on a zombie before departing.

## Glazhael the Undying

Glazhael the Undying is the dracolich present in the castle who had previously agreed to help the party kill a vampire in exchange for having her phylactery located and returned. During the tower battle, she was spotted by Thistle sneaking toward the tower. She used dragon fear on the 3HPP from a safe distance, affecting Thistle, Thuul, and Unla Kee. Vardis observed she appeared to be shadowboxing — not fully committing to the attack. Thistle noted that if she had a heart, it wouldn't have been in her use of the fear ability.

Glazhael then advanced and breathed ice on the tower, encasing it. As she flew away, she screamed "THEY ARE ALL DEAD!" Last seen flying away from the tower after the ice breath attack.

## Bob Saget

Bob Saget does not physically appear in this session but is extensively discussed. He is a demon (described as "the demon of dad jokes") who previously commanded a zombie army and planned to attack Silverymoon. Rather than killing him, Orsik had Unlakee send Bob to the Astral Plane, accompanied by one of his drow allies and four stooges, while the other drow was allowed to escape. Orsik proposed using Planar Ally to summon Bob and offer him the dracolich's phylactery in exchange for fighting alongside the party against the vampire, thus removing Bob as an adversary and solving the dracolich problem simultaneously.

The plan was debated but ultimately tabled due to concerns that Bob might betray the party, use the dracolich for his own purposes, or end up as an even greater threat controlling the dracolich from the Abyss. Orsik also floated double-crossing Bob after the battle. Bob is noted to be currently plotting revenge against the party. At one point in the session, a narrative aside states that somewhere in the Astral Plane, Bob hears the faint sound of his name.

## Harshnag

Harshnag does not appear in this session but is referenced by Orsik during the tower battle. Orsik told Blagothkus that Harshnag was a previous giant ally of the party who died when the Oracle fell on his head, and that the party learned of Blagothkus's location from his son's ghost.

## Slerdax

Slerdax does not appear in this session but is described at length by Unla Kee. He is a dark lord trapped in the mists of Ravenloft who operates a restaurant that periodically opens its doors in various cities to trap the unwary. His curse is that he desires to be praised for his cooking and service but is compelled to believe all praise is a lie, causing him to attempt to kill those who compliment him, first subtly and then with open violence. To escape his domain, one must either kill him (difficult since guests do not typically arrive armed) or convince him that one's praise is genuine. Unla Kee learned of Slerdax in the advanced course on minor Ravenloft lords at his school of magic.

## Thuul

Thuul is referenced only briefly as one of the 3HPP members affected by Glazhael's dragon fear during the tower battle, alongside Thistle. No further details about Thuul's actions are given in this session.
