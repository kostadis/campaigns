## Orsik

Orsik is the central figure of this session, and the events follow him from the aftermath of the battle against Bad Fruul through a confrontation over the magical headband, to his departure from Parnast toward Triboar.

In the immediate aftermath of the battle, Orsik is covered in blood, guts, and gore. He shouts back at Raggnar's accusation, reframing the devastation as a victory — the town was saved. He dismisses the wyvern's combat performance entirely, calling it a failure, and refuses to credit it with any meaningful contribution despite Chandra's protests. He graciously acknowledges the sprites' contribution and endorses Vardis's declaration that sprites would always be preferred over wyverns.

Orsik attempts to deceive the group by swapping the magical headband recovered from Bad Fruul's corpse with a fake headband he had won in a card game during his time as a city guard. His internal reasoning is explicit: he does not trust the SEER and believes she intends to use the evil soul within the headband for a malicious purpose. He rejects the idea that her past promises constitute sufficient trustworthiness, especially given that her machinations nearly destroyed Parnast and killed most of his militia. He is caught in the act by Thistle, who observes him holding two headbands.

A long and escalating confrontation follows. Orsik is pressured by Chandra, one of his own guards, Wallace, Thistle, and the pseudodragon Hsing to surrender the headband to the SEER. Each person dismisses his concerns without answering his central question — why the SEER should be trusted. Orsik experiences this as a profound betrayal. He has sacrificed deeply to save Parnast, has invested his redemption in this community, and finds that no one trusts him enough to engage seriously with his objections. He feels marginalized and treated as a blunt instrument rather than a person of conscience.

He drops the magical object, declares he is done with everyone, and begins to march out of town. Thistle pursues him, and Orsik articulates his grievances clearly: he distrusts the pseudodragon, he distrusts the SEER, and he cannot accept that no one thinks his distrust matters. Raggnar and Chandra, overhearing, begin to come around to his perspective. Even Hsing inadvertently concedes the point, noting that one probably shouldn't trust a spymaster since they lie.

The SEER then arrives through a materialized door. Orsik directly confronts her, pointing the cursed headband at her and demanding to know what she will do with the evil soul. The SEER, recognizing how much Orsik has changed, offers to perform the ritual publicly in Parnast the following morning so that the community can witness it. This satisfies Orsik.

After the ritual is performed the next morning, Orsik ends up in possession of the headband of intellect — he was the only one who wanted it. His genius-level intellect replaces what had previously been described as the intelligence of an ox. His internal monologue reflects on his completed redemption arc, acknowledging that he managed it through good character rather than cleverness, and anticipates using his new intellect to explore history and lore. He briefly considers becoming a mage and then has a belated realization about why people had been reacting negatively to the artifact.

Orsik approves without sentiment when Wallace announces he is leaving to be with Elspeth and take on the role of town guard captain.

He departs Parnast with his companions several days later, heading toward Triboar to find Uncle Joon. His redemption arc is described as complete.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul does not appear as a living combatant in this session; his death has already occurred prior to the opening scene. His corpse is present on the battlefield and is the focus of Orsik's deception attempt. Orsik approaches the giant's severed head to retrieve the magical headband from it, intending to replace it with a fake. The body is described as having been killed while tied up, and Orsik's attempt to swap the headbands on the giant's head is what draws Thistle's attention. Bad Fruul's combat performance is retroactively discussed: Orsik flatly states he landed not a single attack, standing there until he was killed. Vardis charitably calls him "a bold distraction." The wyvern associated with Bad Fruul is confirmed dead and mourned by Chandra.

## Chandra Stol

Chandra is present throughout the post-battle scene. She is described as watching events from near the well and is identified as having been intended to become the new head of the guard — a position she never wanted and which Orsik withdraws following the wyvern's failure. She mourns the death of her pet wyvern and insists it fought heroically, a claim Orsik sardonically rejects.

Chandra erupts in anger at Vardis's civilian/militia distinction, asserting that in Parnast such categories are meaningless — the dead were friends, family, and townspeople. She joins Raggnar and Gundalin in arguing about how to rebuild the town.

When Orsik attempts to withhold the headband from the SEER, Chandra accuses him of endangering her life. However, when Orsik asks why she trusts the SEER — who also put her life in danger — she only sneers at him. Later, overhearing Orsik's explanation as he marches out, she begins to reconsider, mumbling "What do we really know about the Lord's Alliance?" and questioning what good the Alliance has actually brought Parnast. She notes that the machinations of the Alliance led to the town's death and destruction. She also draws a comparison to the Zhentarim's pragmatic assistance against the Cult of the Dragon, suggesting the Alliance deserves no special trust.

Chandra is the local cleric of Parnast.

## Raggnar

Raggnar is present in the aftermath of the battle. He shouts at the party — "What have you done to this town?" — expressing shock at the scale of destruction. He later reports that approximately half the militia was killed. He is among those who join in arguing about rebuilding after the fighting ends.

When Orsik marches out of town, Raggnar races after him and, having overheard his explanation, tells him he makes excellent points. Raggnar is thus among the first to openly validate Orsik's concerns about the SEER and the Lord's Alliance.

## Gundalin

Gundalin is present in the post-battle scene, initially about to agree with Raggnar's accusation against the party. He is among those who argue about rebuilding the town after the battle. Later, out of breath while pursuing Orsik, he articulates a position sympathetic to Orsik's distrust: that the SEER and the Lord's Alliance were responsible for the machinations that resulted in death and destruction in Parnast, based on what he understands from Wallace and Orsik.

## Vardis

Vardis is present throughout the post-battle scene, covered in dirt and laughing. He jokes about modernizing the town's architecture given the destruction. He enthusiastically endorses the sprites over the wyvern, declaring they would always choose sprites. He calls the wyvern "a bold distraction." He notices the second headband in Orsik's possession during the deception attempt and presses Orsik on its origin, though Orsik deflects. He expresses confusion at Unla Kee's desire to become a vessel for the evil soul. He is described as from a big city, a distinction Chandra pointedly rejects.

## Blasius

Blasius emerges from a hideout after the battle concludes. He pulls out a flask and offers fine wine to Unla Kee. He enthusiastically celebrates Orsik, calling him "my brother, my friend, my hero" and gesturing over the battlefield as if it is a glorious day. He is still drunk and singing with Unla Kee the following morning when the SEER arrives to perform the ritual.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee accepts Blasius's offer of wine, framing it as "a risk." When the magical headband and the evil soul within it are being discussed, Unla Kee declares a desire to become a vessel for the soul, wanting access to the drow magic and the five-hundred-year-old wizard's lore contained within it. This startles Thistle and confuses Vardis. Unla Kee is still drinking and singing with Blasius into the following morning.

## Thistle

Thistle is a sprite (implied to be of some significance given the Seelie Court connection) who catches Orsik in the act of holding two headbands. Thistle challenges Orsik's distrust of the SEER, pointing out that the SEER has not yet broken her promises, even if she has led the group into danger. Thistle argues that the headband should be handed over and that the SEER said they would get it back. When Orsik marches out, Thistle flies after him and draws out a fuller articulation of his grievances. When the SEER arrives and asks Thistle to hand over the headband, Thistle refuses on behalf of the group. Thistle is separately told by Prince Thornacious that Queen Tatiana of the Seelie Court has been informed of Thistle and is pleased — Thistle is being noticed. Thistle's response is that they only want their beloved.

## Hsing

Hsing is the gold pseudodragon associated with the SEER. Hsing arrives and demands that Orsik hand over the headband. The townspeople and Orsik's allies side with Hsing, which pushes Orsik past his limit. Hsing then inadvertently undermines their own argument by saying one probably shouldn't trust a spymaster since they lie — a concession that Orsik takes as validation. Orsik has never trusted the pseudodragon and names this distrust explicitly when articulating why he is leaving.

## Wallace

Wallace attempts to convince Orsik to surrender the headband to the SEER. His argument is institutional: the SEER is an essential member of the Lord's Alliance, the town needs Alliance support, and therefore Orsik should comply. Orsik finds this unpersuasive given that the SEER's plans nearly destroyed the town. Orsik asks why Wallace trusts the SEER and is dismissed. Later in the session, Wallace approaches Orsik to announce he is retiring — he and Elspeth have decided to be together, and the town needs a new guard captain. Orsik grunts approval without sentiment.

## The SEER

The SEER does not appear physically until late in the session, when a door materializes and she steps through it. Prior to her arrival, she is the central subject of debate: Orsik refuses to hand over the headband because he believes she intends to misuse the evil soul it contains. The townspeople and party members largely trust her, but no one can satisfactorily explain why to Orsik.

When she arrives, Orsik directly confronts her — pointing the cursed headband at her and demanding to know what she will do with the evil soul. She laughs and explains she intends to send the soul where it was supposed to go. Recognizing that Orsik has changed profoundly, she offers to perform the ritual publicly in Parnast the following morning to address his concerns about community safety. The next morning she arrives and performs the ritual. The result is that the headband becomes a headband of intellect, which Orsik keeps.

The SEER is identified as the top spymaster in the world (per Hsing), a senior member of the Lord's Alliance, and the architect of the plans that led the group to Parnast. Her motivations regarding the evil soul in the headband are stated to be disposal rather than exploitation, and she follows through on this claim.

## Prince Thornacious

Prince Thornacious appears at the post-battle party as an unexpected guest. He informs Thistle that he has mentioned them to Queen Tatiana of the Seelie Court and that she was pleased. He frames being noticed by the Queen as the highest possible honor — "that is all we should ever want." His current status is present at the celebration; he appears briefly and then is not mentioned further.

## Queen Tatiana

Queen Tatiana does not appear in this session. She is referenced by Prince Thornacious, who reports that she has been informed of Thistle and is pleased by what she has heard. She is identified as the Queen of the Seelie Court.

## Elspeth

Elspeth does not appear in this session. She is referenced by Wallace when he announces his retirement: he and Elspeth have decided to be together, which is part of his reason for stepping back from adventuring and taking up the role of town guard captain in Parnast.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session. He is referenced at the close of the chapter as the destination of Orsik and his companions' journey: they depart Parnast for Triboar several days after the battle in order to find him.
