## Maergun

Maergun is the Fomorian whose throne room serves as the final battleground. The room is newly finished with smooth walls, a massive throne, and features Drauchanar hanging from a chain. Maergun commands a cyclops and a displacer beast as guardians. When the displacer beast is killed by Vardis' Fireball, Maergun shouts, "You killed my puppy!" and reveals the creature's name was "Haerbega" after Unla Kee's taunting suggestions of "Fluffy" and "Spiky." Maergun attempts an evil eye on Vardis, who avoids the worst effects but is struck by Maergun's club. The Fomorian is ultimately killed through the combined efforts of a cantrip, a goblin arrow, and devastating attacks from Orsik using the Giant Sword of Lord Zymorven, with Vardis delivering the killing blow via a cantrip. His body is searched by Orsik, who finds an iron key that unlocks a secret staircase to the treasure vault beneath the throne. His hoard contains turquoise figurines, a brass crown, an ivory statuette, a tapestry depicting elves feasting, a mass of coins, a potion of greater healing, and a potion of water breathing. Maergun's death pleases Queen Titania, who sends a sprite to reward the party.

## Tlega

Tlega is one of the goblin leaders accompanying the 3HPP into Maergun's throne room. When combat begins against the cyclops and displacer beast, Tlega positions himself in the corridor leading to the chamber and shouts to his troops, "Move ahead. I'm right behind you," while making no move to enter the fight himself. Orsik glares at him upon realizing that shouting encouragement is the full extent of Tlega's personal combat contribution. Tlega shouts "ATTACK!" after the Cyclops falls, prompting Orsik to ask, "Don't you fight?" to which Tlega replies, "We lead!" Orsik mutters, "From the rear!" in response. Tlega's role throughout the battle is entirely one of rear leadership and verbal direction.

## Mirk

Mirk is the other goblin leader accompanying the 3HPP in Maergun's throne room. During the battle, Mirk's decision to do nothing but heap praise on the goblin archers creates dissension in the ranks, with some goblins feeling he is not engaged in the mission and is slacking. The dissent threatens to become disruptive, at which point Mirk throws a tantrum, grabs his javelin, hurls it at Maergun the Fomorian, and hits, shouting "Satisfied!" to the cheers of the assembled goblins. After the Fomorian is killed, Mirk shouts "Well done!" as the goblins break into their victory chant.

## Drauchanar

Drauchanar is found hanging from a chain in Maergun's throne room when the party enters. After the battle concludes and Vardis frees him, Drauchanar declares, "I am free, exhausted, tired, and free!" and immediately claims that the giant is dead "thanks to my spectacular leadership," asserting that he deliberately allowed himself to be captured so the party could follow him there. He compliments the party for coming through the waterfall and credits Vardis — whom he believes to be accompanied by Prince Thornacious — for seeing through the illusion. He appears unaware that Orsik is not actually Prince Thornacious. His last known status is freed and present with the group as they explore the treasure vault.

## Aeglossil

Aeglossil does not appear until the party discovers a secret door southeast of the throne room, leading to a tomb decorated with delicate bas-reliefs of elven nature designs. She lies on a raised platform as what appears to be a white stone statue of a sleeping elven woman. Two duergars are cleaning the tomb reverentially when the party enters. After Thistle — acting under what is later understood to be charm — kisses the statue, it shimmers with bright light and Aeglossil appears in place of the figure, a female elf who is disoriented. Vardis immediately identifies her as a vampire. She grapples Orsik, extends her fangs, and bites him, feeding for the first time in what appears to be eons, causing some color to return to her pallor. She snarls in fear when Vardis presents the symbol of Corellon. Unla Kee then casts Banishment, and she is expelled into another realm with a great bang and a ripple through reality. The following day, Thistle acknowledges that he had been charmed by her and that his actions in kissing the statue were not his own. The derro remaining in the caves wait for their vampire mistress to return. Aeglossil's current status is banished to another realm.

## The Knife

The Knife appears outside the cave after the defeat of Maergun and the banishment of Aeglossil, when the party emerges into the sunlight of the Feywild. Thistle introduces him by shouting "Here's The Knife," to which Orsik replies "That's no knife," and The Knife clarifies, "I am not a knife, I am THE Knife. It's a nom-de-guerre." The Knife is under the illusion that Orsik is Prince Thornacious and attempts to ingratiate himself, shouting down Thistle when he asks uncomfortable questions about Aeglossil — "How dare you speak so to Prince Thornacious! He gave you an answer. You should accept it." — and complimenting Orsik's patience. The Knife leads or accompanies the party to the portal back from the Feywild. His last known whereabouts are the Feywild.

## Queen Titania

Queen Titania does not appear in person but is meaningfully referenced. A colorful sprite arrives after the defeat of Maergun to announce that Queen Titania is pleased and wishes to give the party a gift: a Blessed Horn, described as a magical item of raw and incredible powers. Thistle attempts to signal Orsik that accepting gifts from Queen Titania always comes with strings attached, but Orsik's greed overrides this warning and he declares the party willing to accept the gift. Thistle, caring nothing for the political implications, takes the Blessed Horn himself. Queen Titania's gift also opens the portal back from the Feywild. She does not appear in person in this chapter.

## Delsephine

Delsephine is waiting on the other side of the portal when the 3HPP return from the Feywild. From her perspective, only moments have passed. She stares at the aged Orsik and asks, "Who is this?" Orsik tells her he is "Orsik's father," adding the alias "Orsic, with a c," and claims he gave his son a whupping for disgracing the family name. Delsephine stares at him intently and smiles beneath her mask, and Orsik notes she will keep this secret because she understands something about second chances. Later, when the party prepares to investigate the One Stone, she shouts asking whether they want to leave now or in the morning, and when they choose morning, she accommodates without argument. When Orsik suggests she might join them next time on an expedition, she refuses with a definitive "no." Her last known location is her airship.

## Bressik

Bressik is described as the sage from Candlekeep, now reduced to working on an airship for a dragon. He negotiates a fee of 50 gold from the 3HPP — who offer 500 silver — and provides detailed information about Beorunna's Well: its origin legend involving the Uthgardt hero Beorunna and the half-ogre bones, the two tribes (Black Lion and Red Tiger) and their totem poles, the triangular cavern's geography including the heated pool, the knotted rope, the Sunken Grove, and the blood-splattered altar concealing a giant relic. He notes confidence in their success by mentioning he has placed a wager on them returning with the artifact, though he refuses to increase the wager despite nudging. He later provides information about the One Stone and the Sky Pony tribe for five platinum, noting irritation at the inconsistency of coinage. He describes the Sky Pony spirit mound in the Moonwood, the two-tiered plateau, the round boulder covered in line engravings used as an altar, the surrounding stream, the totem poles, and the trespassers' corpses lashed to them. When Vardis claims the relic is under the rock, Bressik says it is unclear whether it is under or is the rock, while Orsik confidently declares it is beneath. His last known location is Delsephine's airship.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this chapter but is referenced by Thistle when Vardis asks about the Sky Pony tribe. Thistle notes that the Sky Pony tribe and his Uncle Joon had a disagreement over some matter of honor, and that they referred to him in a way that was "beyond the pale for a member of Queen Titania's court." Thistle then undercuts this by observing that Uncle Joon was kicked out of the Court for being a drunk, casting doubt on what exactly could be beneath his dignity. Uncle Joon is not present and his current whereabouts are unknown.
