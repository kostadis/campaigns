## Harshnag the Grim

Harshnag stood at the edge of a destroyed village on the route to the Oracle, where he observed clear signs of giant activity — houses demolished by rocks and bodies scattered across the ground. He immediately urged the party to leave, recognizing the danger.

When Orsik refused and led the 3HPP into the village seeking treasure, Harshnag refused to assist and sat down, withdrawing his support. After the party defeated the Crag Cat ambush, he again demanded they leave, only to be ignored a second and third time as Vardis stripped pelts from the dead cats and Orsik insisted on searching for survivors and treasure.

Frustrated and resolved, Harshnag sat down and began to pray. He spotted two frost giants approaching in the distance and made a deliberate decision: he would not fight to protect the party from the consequences of their own folly. His reasoning was that they would either survive and prove themselves the right heroes, or he would bring their bodies to Krowen Valharrow so the mage could find new champions.

He left the village entirely, stating plainly, "if you live, then you are the right hero. I cannot interfere." While praying to the All-Father, he listened to the sounds of combat — Orsik screaming for help, fireballs erupting, rocks falling — and refused to intervene.

When he heard the unmistakable crash of a giant's body hitting the ground followed by the 3HPP's triumphant whooping, he was genuinely surprised. He prayed harder, then opened his eyes and smiled, concluding: "They are the heroes." The party's survival against the two frost giants confirmed to him that his quest — and theirs — was still alive.

His last known status is outside the destroyed village, having reconvened with the victorious 3HPP.
