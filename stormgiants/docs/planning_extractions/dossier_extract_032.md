## Tamalin

Tamalin appears at the Calling Horns Inn when the heroes return from their recon mission in the Evermoors. She listens to their report and reacts with surprise that Fire Giants are working with Trolls, echoing Thistle's earlier observation that it was like "Cats and Dogs, working together." She asks follow-up questions and, upon hearing about Duke Zalto and the Vonindod, shares her knowledge of giant social hierarchy — that Fire Giant leaders are all called dukes, Hill Giant leaders are chiefs, Stone Giant leaders are thanes, and Cloud Giant leaders are counts. She admits she has never heard of the Vonindod and suggests the heroes might learn more in Everlund or Silverymoon, though she notably implies that Thistle specifically might not get much from the people of Silverymoon, directing her comment about useful contacts to the other three members of the party.

As the heroes depart, Tamalin reveals that her brother is the High Captain of Everlund and gives the party a note that can be used to call in a favor from him, suggesting he may be able to help them learn what they need.

Tamalin is also revealed through Eltio's reminiscing to have a notable criminal past — she was a legendary and feared figure in the Yartar region who used the terror she induced to parlay her reputation into a bigger and more lucrative operation, which is apparently what the Calling Horns Inn represents. Orsik's earlier comment about "the good people of Silverymoon with their Cambion demons" draws a sympathetic response from her, suggesting she has her own complicated history with that city.

Tamalin does not travel with the party; her last known location is the Calling Horns Inn.

## Duke Zalto

Duke Zalto does not appear in this session but is meaningfully discussed. The Fire Giant encountered in the Evermoors is revealed to be working under Duke Zalto's orders. The giant explicitly states she cannot return to Duke Zalto until she finds a piece of the Vonindod. This establishes Zalto as a commanding figure directing Fire Giants on search missions across the region. Tamalin notes that all Fire Giant leaders hold the title of duke, contextualizing Zalto's rank within Fire Giant society. A bag of salt described as belonging to Duke Zalto — "salt fit for a duke" — is later sold to the goblin merchant Fritz, suggesting the party had previously looted goods associated with him.

## The Fire Giant (Evermoors)

An unnamed female Fire Giant is encountered in an opening in the Evermoors, shouting at two trolls. She is hungry, frustrated, and annoyed. She carries a rod identical to the one used by the Fire Giant that attacked Triboar, which she is using to search for an artifact. She complains that the trolls keep offering her mud pies instead of proper food, and she is unable to return to Duke Zalto until she locates a piece of the Vonindod. The trolls appear to not understand her at all.

Thistle, using a Comprehend Languages spell provided by Unla Kee, eavesdrops on the confrontation and gathers this intelligence. The party ultimately decides not to attack her — partly due to Orsik's insistence that the recon mission is complete, and partly because Thistle concludes she is a different Fire Giant from the one that attacked Triboar and he has no personal vendetta against her.

Her last known location is the Evermoors. She remains alive.

## Fritz

Fritz is a short, middle-aged goblin merchant encountered on the Evermoor Road heading east. He is dressed distinctively in a red velvet shirt, blue pants, and green suspenders. He introduces himself and explains that his clan is relocating eastward because giants have become aggressive and erratic, and Uthgardt nomads are attacking anything that moves. He describes his current situation as a sale driven by necessity, though he refuses to call it a going-out-of-business sale, citing his mother's advice never to sell for less than a reasonable profit.

Fritz purchases several items from the party: a bag of salt that belonged to Duke Zalto (which he is delighted by, reasoning it must be quality salt if it was fit for a duke), an empty chest, a drinking horn (reasoning that if a giant thought it worth carrying it must be valuable), and fairy leather armor. Orsik prevents Thistle from clarifying to Fritz that the armor is a fairy's leather armor rather than armor made of fairy leather.

Fritz's last known location is the Evermoor Road, heading east.

## Maia

Maia is encountered at the Wink and the Kiss tavern in Yartar when the party arrives on the 8th day of the 1st tenday of Flamerule. She is happy to see the heroes and asks if they need more work. When Orsik presents the Letter of Mark, she attempts to high-five him but is left hanging. Unhappy with this reception, she escorts the group through the interior of the tavern — described as a maze — to the central bar, where she seats them next to Eltio. She is later instructed by Eltio to bring drinks for the party.

## Eltio

Eltio is a member of the Hand of Yartar, encountered at the Wink and the Kiss in Yartar. She is dressed entirely in white — white hat, white mask, and white clothes with carefully stitched white-on-white patterns. When the party presents the Letter of Mark, she questions what they did to earn it and expresses mild disappointment that it involved only recon and no murder, thievery, or highway robbery. She uses this as an opportunity to reminisce about Tamalin's past as a legendary criminal figure in the region, noting that Tamalin parlayed the terror she inspired into a more lucrative operation.

Eltio then leaves briefly and returns with intelligence on the party's targets in Yartar. She informs them that Harthos Zymorven is currently imprisoned for murder and scheduled to be executed on the 10th — beheaded specifically to prevent resurrection. She also reveals that the sword the party is seeking is in the possession of a man named Tholzar Brenner.

Eltio then escorts the party to the market near the Waterbarron's residence to point out Tholzar Brenner, offering to take them somewhere an ambush would be possible. When Orsik suggests fireballing Tholzar and his guards in the crowded market, Eltio reacts with alarm and disbelief, pointing out the proximity to the Waterbarron's house and warning that such an act would likely result in the party being executed before the 10th. She appears to recognize Orsik specifically — "You are that guy Orsik who did that thing. I knew it" — suggesting his reputation has preceded him.

Her last known location is the market district of Yartar.

## Harthos Zymorven

Harthos Zymorven does not appear in this session but is a central subject of the party's planning. He is revealed by Eltio to be currently imprisoned in Yartar for murder and is scheduled to be executed on the 10th of the month by decapitation — a method chosen specifically to prevent magical resurrection. The party debates how to handle his situation before receiving this information, with proposals including intimidation, faking an arrest and offering escape, and claiming his father is ill. Harthos is apparently the heir to Zymorven Hall, and the party's interest in him is connected to reuniting him with his father.

## Tholzar Brenner

Tholzar Brenner is revealed by Eltio to be in possession of the sword the party is seeking. He is described as a Damaranian — a man from a land to the east — and is noted to be disliked, with Eltio observing that no one would be upset if he went missing. He holds the position of guard captain in Yartar. The party observes him in the market near the Waterbarron's residence, where he is seen with a small group of armed guards collecting protection money and described as brutish and swaggering. Eltio offers to lead the party somewhere an ambush would be viable.

His last known location is the market district of Yartar. He remains alive and unaware of the party's interest in him.
