## Thistle

Thistle appeared throughout the Temple of Annam exploration. He apologized to the party for being absent during the warren exploration, explaining he had been spending time in his dreams with "his dearest." He took the lead in systematically exploring the temple, directing attention to two secret doors on the west wall. He squeezed through the northern secret door and discovered a lever 12 feet high, calling on Harshnag for help. When Unla Kee proposed reckless experiments with the fire pit's natural gas flames, Thistle rushed ahead to preempt him, explaining the fire was naturally sustained by gas escaping through holes in the basin rather than elemental or planar magic. During the remorhaz fight, Thistle flew above a table for tactical positioning and fired arrows at the creature, contributing to its incapacitation. In the Eye of Annam, he entered the oracle circle and used one of the six daily questions to ask the location of Laela, receiving the answer that Duke Zalto has her. When asked what to do next after the Oracle's lights were exhausted, Thistle expressed uncertainty. He also sarcastically justified wasting an oracle question as "confirming the accuracy of the Oracle" when challenged by Harshnag.

## Harshnag

Harshnag appeared throughout the Temple of Annam exploration as a key combat and knowledge resource. He groaned when Thistle mentioned spending time with his beloved. He physically assisted with both levers in the secret rooms, using a torch provided by Thistle to melt ice before lifting the northern lever, and lifting the southern lever as well. When the remorhaz appeared, he initially ordered the party to hold it off while he retrieved the Ax of Thrym, intending to flee — but later charged the monster directly, striking it heavily and cracking its carapace. He claimed his earlier hesitation was "encouraging" the party, which Orsik and Vardis disputed as a ret-con. After the fight, he accompanied Unla Kee into the hexagonal oracle chamber and translated the ghost's giant speech into Unla Kee's ear. He was visibly frustrated by the party's use of oracle questions for personal matters such as Laela's location and the treasure map, threatening (obliquely) that the powerful magic user supporting them might withdraw support or act against them if they proved unworthy. He stepped into the oracle circle to ask about Hekaton and the nature of the evil in the storm giant court, receiving the answer that the court was in chaos following Hekaton's disappearance, and quickly stepped out. After the oracle's lights were exhausted, Harshnag led the party outside the Eye of Annam and spent an hour drawing a crude map of all nine spirit mounds with locations relative to landmarks and terrain features. He stated he did not know which relics were buried at each mound or which giant lord was associated with each relic. He confirmed Thistle's summary of the party's next steps: find a relic, return it to the oracle, and receive guidance on a giant lord.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee appeared throughout the session as the party's mage. He explored esoteric options for melting the ice on the northern lever, including Fireball and Burning Hands, before Thistle simply used a torch. He was the first to enter the feast hall, observing the vast chamber and its fire pit. He attempted to conduct experiments on the 40,000-year-old fire until Thistle intervened. During the remorhaz fight, he was swallowed whole by the creature early in the combat. He later cast Raulothim's Psychic Lance, stunning the remorhaz, and followed up with a binding ice spell that immobilized it, twisting fate through his diviner abilities to achieve the desired outcome. He voluntarily followed Harshnag into the oracle chamber despite Orsik's protests, justifying this by reasoning that his certain presence at the end of the universe made personal risk personally liberating. In the oracle chamber, he communicated with the ghost through Harshnag's whispered translation, learned about the oracle's function (six true-answer questions per day), and heard the ghost's quest to kill Blagothkus at Parnast. He recalled the cloud giant castle that had nearly killed the party months ago and framed the quest as "payback and a castle." He used one of the six oracle questions to ask what must be done to stop the giants, receiving the answer about finding the magic conch and visiting Hekaton's court, then walked out and told Harshnag the oracle "checks out." He was last seen processing the oracle's final directive about retrieving giant relics from spirit mounds.

## Orsik

Orsik appeared throughout the session. He noticed a crack in the temple wall and launched into an extended monologue about stonemasonry, rock formations, magical versus artisanal stonework, and his philosophical objection to using magic to shape stone — conceding only that magic which makes stonemasons more productive is acceptable. During the remorhaz fight, he called for the party to stand and fight rather than flee, struck the creature multiple times with his blade (braving the heat damage from its body), and coordinated attacks by shouting to Unla Kee to cast again. He noted Harshnag's hesitation before the giant's charge and was bemused by the ret-con claim. After the fight, he advised Harshnag to go retrieve the Ax of Thrym alone while the party waited safely. He was aghast when Unla Kee followed Harshnag into the oracle chamber. In the oracle chamber, he used one of the six questions to ask about his family heirloom map, receiving the answer that what it depicted was located beyond the Silvery Marches near the Ice Spires in an iron tower. He later suggested asking the oracle about Duke Zalto after the topic of Zalto holding Laela arose, but the oracle's lights ran out before this question could be asked.

## Vardis

Vardis appeared throughout the session. He praised Thistle for using a practical torch rather than magic to melt the ice. During the remorhaz fight, he attempted to heal Orsik, who waved him off, so he instead cast Sacred Flame and summoned a spiritual weapon in the shape of a longsword resembling Sahandrian. He delivered the killing blow to the remorhaz with the summoned sword. He explained the concept of "ret-conning" to a confused Orsik, referencing his earlier "temporal phase" in which he believed time was non-linear and facts could be retroactively changed. In the oracle chamber, he asked Harshnag about Hekaton, prompting Harshnag's accidental oracle question. He then used a question himself to ask where the conch was, receiving the answer that it was in the clutches of a giant lord, and described this as "the most direct question."

## Ghost (Cloud Giant)

The ghost of a cloud giant rose from the floor of the hexagonal oracle chamber within the Temple of Annam. He was the son of Blagothkus and had been slain by his father in a fight that erupted after the oracle told Blagothkus he could never impress the gods enough to earn their favor, while urging the son to step out from beneath his father's "dark shadow." The father was overcome with despair and envy. The ghost explained the oracle's function: six questions per day, each yielding a true answer, with each question consuming one of the lantern lights held by the six giant statues in the chamber. He offered the party a quest: kill his father Blagothkus, who had since retired to his castle in Parnast to mourn. He acknowledged uncertainty about whether Parnast was a real place. His last known status is as a ghost haunting the oracle chamber, seeking vengeance against his father.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus does not appear in this session but is meaningfully discussed. He is the father of the ghost cloud giant in the oracle chamber. The divine oracle previously told him outright that he could never impress the gods enough to earn their favor. Overcome with despair and envy, he fought and killed his own son. He has since retired to his cloud giant castle at Parnast to mourn. The ghost has asked the party to kill him. Unla Kee recalled that a cloud giant castle had blown past the party months ago, nearly killing them, and framed the Blagothkus quest as an opportunity for payback and acquisition of the castle.

## Laela

Laela does not appear in this session but is meaningfully discussed. Thistle used one of the oracle's six daily questions to ask her location. The oracle revealed that Duke Zalto has her. She is Thistle's "dearest," the subject of his dream-time during the party's earlier exploration.

## Duke Zalto

Duke Zalto does not appear in this session but is meaningfully referenced. The oracle revealed he is holding Laela. Orsik suggested asking the oracle about him specifically, connecting Zalto to a conch that may be in a giant lord's clutches, but the oracle's lights were exhausted before this question could be asked. He is identified as one of the evil giant lords the party may need to confront.

## Hekaton

Hekaton does not appear in this session but is meaningfully discussed. The oracle identified him as the storm giant king. The oracle directed the party to find his magic conch, use it to visit his court, and root out the evil therein. A follow-up oracle question (asked accidentally by Harshnag) revealed that the storm giant court is in chaos following Hekaton's disappearance. His current whereabouts are unknown.
