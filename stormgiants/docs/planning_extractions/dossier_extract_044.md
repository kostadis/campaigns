## Harshnag

Harshnag accompanied the 3HPP on the two-day journey from the Golden Dragon's cave to the Temple of Annam without incident. At the causeway, he identified each of the six bas-relief pillars for the party, describing them from North to South: a hill giant lifting a rock (identified by its small size and large belly), a proud Frost Giant cutting down a pine, Cloud Giants flying with birds, a Storm Giant tossing lightning at a ship, a Stone Giant climbing down a mountain, and — with visible contempt — Fire Giants depicted as slavers with dwarves chained to a wall, which he called "a plague on all of us."

During the ambush by the Uthgardtian barbarians and their white dragon, Harshnag entered the battle in a fury at the presence of a dragon in the holy place, helping turn the tide. Afterward, he stood over the corpses and identified them to Orsik as members of the Great Worm Tribe, declaring they had come to plunder the temple and were foolish. He pushed open the doors to the Temple, stating only a giant could open them, then delivered a 10-to-15-minute speech about the tremendous giant civilization of Osteria and its 40,000-year history when the party referred to the space as an antechamber and implied it had been penetrated. When Vardis challenged this by claiming Toril is only 5,000 years old, Harshnag made the Giant gesture for "loon" and called him a creationist.

Inside the Temple of Annam, Harshnag noted that the face of the All-Father's statue was blank and explained the sculptors chose not to offend by depicting something they did not know. He recognized the frost rune on the archway, noting it was an older version than the current one. When Thistle touched the Storm rune and triggered a Storm of Vengeance — killing all four members of the 3HPP — Harshnag survived, gathered the bodies of Orsik, Thistle, Unla Kee, and Vardis into his satchel, and carried them out of the Temple. He rummaged through their belongings, found a healing potion, and administered it to Vardis. His internal thought was relief that "it's three tiny people and an elf."

The following day, Harshnag contributed the key insight that the weapons on the giant statues were removable, which led the party to theorize that the correct weapon must be used to activate the corresponding rune. He suggested starting with the hill giant rune rather than the storm giant rune given its lethality. He retrieved the hill giant's 500-pound bone club and touched the hill rune, which triggered a confusion-like spell of howling magical voices — an effect he appeared to shrug off before returning to report it hadn't worked. He then attempted to touch the Frost rune directly, reasoning on Orsik's suggestion that he was immune to frost damage; this triggered a magical ice storm that struck him, and again he reported failure. When the party discussed using the Frost Giant weapon, Harshnag revealed it was missing from the statue, prompting speculation that it might be somewhere else in the temple. He accompanied the party through the double doors north of the Temple into the dormitory hall, where he took the tattered furs from one of the giant-sized beds and lay down contentedly while the 3HPP investigated the crack in the wall.

Harshnag's last known position is resting on a giant stone bed in the dormitory hall while the party entered the tunnel system. His relationship with the party is protective and collegial, though marked by exasperation at their ignorance of giant history and culture.

## Orsik

Orsik declared upon arrival at the Temple dome that there was no danger of collapse even if the pillars were destroyed, prompting a brief exchange with Vardis. He tried to urge caution when Unla Kee rushed ahead past the stone blocks. During the Uthgardtian ambush, he participated in the battle. After the fight, he pointed to the barbarian corpses to undercut Harshnag's assertion about the temple being impenetrable. Inside the Temple, he examined the six runes on the archway and identified them left to right as hill, cloud, fire, storm, stone, and frost, noting the frost rune as an old variant. He cut off Harshnag when the giant began to share his own theory, wanting the party to work it out themselves.

Orsik suggested touching the Storm rune first because it matched the top of the ordning hierarchy, and Thistle acted on this. After the resulting Storm of Vengeance killed the party, Orsik was scooped up by Harshnag and revived the following day. He took the lead in devising a new plan, suggesting that the key was who — or what — touched the rune. When Harshnag muttered about the weapons being removable, Orsik (along with Vardis) immediately recognized the implication and shouted that a weapon must be used to touch its corresponding rune. He suggested Harshnag try the Frost rune directly on the grounds that the giant was immune to frost damage. After both attempts failed, he proposed searching the rest of the temple for the missing Frost Giant weapon and led the decision to head through the double doors north of the Temple. He called out to explore the northern hallway. Last seen entering the spider-filled tunnel system with Thistle and Vardis.

## Thistle

Thistle noticed fresh human tracks on the approach to the Temple and questioned Harshnag about them. He tried to warn Unla Kee about the possibility of traps when he spotted the massive stone blocks held by a contraption above the hallway, but was too slow to stop the halfling from running past them. He participated in the battle against the Uthgardtian barbarians. Inside the Temple, he suggested starting with the Storm Giant rune because it matched the ordning hierarchy, then flew up and touched it, triggering a Storm of Vengeance. After the thunder and acid rain began, he touched it again at the party's suggestion, but nothing changed. He was killed along with the rest of the party by the lightning bolts and was carried out by Harshnag.

The following day, Thistle led the group down the snaking tunnel beyond the crack in the wall in the dormitory hall. His first thought upon being ambushed by spiders was that he hated them. Last seen in the tunnel fighting spiders alongside Orsik and Vardis.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee was in a rush upon approaching the Temple and dashed ahead despite Thistle's warnings about traps, running past the massive stone blocks into the main hallway without triggering them. During the Uthgardtian ambush, he participated in the battle. Inside the Temple, he rushed to the far eastern wall to examine the giant portal archway and ran through the glowing mist, colliding with the wall behind it and reporting there was nothing to see. When Orsik turned to him for a divination spell to help with the runes, Unla Kee's blank expression revealed he had none memorized and none in his spellbook. His explanation was: "I know the future; what else is there to divine?" He was knocked unconscious by the ice storm triggered by Orsik touching the Frost rune, then killed by the lightning from the Storm of Vengeance. He was carried out by Harshnag.

The following morning, Unla Kee expressed enthusiasm about repeating the experience that had just killed the entire party ("That was fun! Let's go try that again!"), drawing stares from everyone. He does not appear to have contributed meaningfully to the subsequent puzzle-solving discussion. Last known position: with the group, presumably entering the northern section of the temple.

## Vardis

Vardis was present at the causeway and reacted with a stare to Orsik's structural analysis. Inside the Temple, he called the initial chamber an antechamber, which triggered Harshnag's extended lecture. He challenged Harshnag's claim about the temple's age by stating Toril is only 5,000 years old, earning the Giant gesture for "loon." Before entering the Temple proper, he picked up an amulet from the dead Uthgardtian chieftain and hung it around his neck under his armor. Inside, he stared at the blank face of Annam's statue. After the Storm of Vengeance killed the party, he was revived by Harshnag with a healing potion.

The following day, Vardis mused that the problem might be who touched the rune rather than which rune was touched, contributing to the group's breakthrough alongside Orsik when Harshnag mentioned the removable weapons. He later suggested trying the Frost Giant weapon specifically, at which point Harshnag revealed it was missing. Vardis nodded and suggested it might be elsewhere in the temple, supporting the decision to search further. Last seen entering the spider-filled tunnel system.

## The Shaman (Great Worm Tribe)

The Shaman was among the Uthgardtian barbarians who ambushed the 3HPP just before the stairway inside the Temple. When the battle turned against the attackers, the Shaman fled deeper into the Temple rather than fighting to the end. Current status: last seen fleeing into the Temple of Annam. Whereabouts unknown; presumed still somewhere inside the temple.
