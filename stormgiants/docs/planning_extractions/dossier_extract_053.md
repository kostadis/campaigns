## Bressik

Bressik serves as the hired scholar for the 3HPP, a role he clearly prefers over his previous work as a deckhand. He watches the Dragon's Breath approach Raven's Rock at the start of events and delivers two formal presentations to the party over the course of the two days.

At Raven's Rock, he presents his research on the spirit mound of the Black Raven and Gray Wolf tribes, describing the 100-foot stone raven, the four surrounding menhirs used by Uthgardt shamans to track seasons and stars, and the Gray Wolf altar in the wolf-shaped depression to the southwest. He is cut off mid-presentation by Orsik, who declares they are going directly to the altar. Bressik splutters in protest but stays silent when he remembers Orsik's blade and temper.

During downtime, Bressik spends the evening chatting with Thistle about his relationship with Thuul the drake. He finds the anarcho-socialist partnership Thistle describes "fascinating."

At Great Worm Cavern, Bressik delivers his second and final presentation, describing the cavern's geography, the icy ledges, the tribal cave dwellings, the steam vents, the narrow crypts containing frozen standing corpses, and the triangular promontory bearing the serpent-shaped altar of Elrem the Great Worm. He offers two theories about the gong artifact: either it is the artifact itself — a 250-pound broken frost giant shield made of red dragon scales on a copper frame — or it is purely decorative. He favors the second theory based on the pattern of artifacts being found under altars at previous sites. He then descends to the lower decks after his presentation concludes.

Bressik's contract with Klamath the Red Dragon becomes a significant topic of negotiation. It is revealed that he signed a contract with Klamath, a necromancer, covering both a living and an undead term of service — valued at approximately 300 gold for his living years and 13,000 gold for the assumed undead portion. During the cult-and-dragon discussion, Bressik defends the original Cult of the Dragon's ideology against Thistle's criticism, arguing that the Dracolich vision was about creating a unifying undead draconic race, and that Severin and Tiamat subverted this original plan through a coup. He is offended when Thistle dismisses this as academic nonsense.

Orsik negotiates with Delsephine to purchase the living portion of Bressik's contract, which would free Bressik entirely from his obligations to Klamath — the obligations do not transfer to Orsik. The deal is ultimately struck as a residuals arrangement brokered by Unla Kee for 500 gold upfront and residuals capped at 6,000 gold. Unla Kee privately notes that the residuals are structured against the current play only, meaning the sequel — tentatively titled "The Gong Show" — would not be encumbered by Delsephine's cut.

Bressik's current status is aboard the Dragon's Breath at the time the Great Worm tribe defenders are killed and the worm creature erupts from the snowbank. His contract's living term has been purchased, making him technically free.

## Delsephine

Delsephine is the commander of the Dragon's Breath, a cult airship, and serves as the party's transport and a recurring commercial presence throughout these events. She is described as wearing a dark mask and having blazing eyes.

At Raven's Rock, Delsephine watches Unla Kee fly to the altar with pegasus wings, finding the comical visual of the 30-foot wingspan on a 3-foot halfling endlessly amusing. When Thistle and Unla Kee approach her about moving the ship, she agrees to bring the Dragon's Breath closer to the group's position, noting she fears the ravens may not view her as an innocent bystander. She then directs the ship toward Great Worm Cavern.

At Great Worm Cavern, she interrupts Bressik's presentation to note that her ship carries climbing equipment for sale. She pockets 40 gold from Orsik for two sets of crampons and related gear.

Delsephine negotiates Bressik's contract buyout on behalf of Klamath the Red Dragon. She explains the contract's dual-life structure — living and undead terms — and the valuation methodology. She clarifies that purchasing the living portion frees Bressik entirely, with no transfer of obligation to the buyer. When Orsik asks where Klamath is, she refuses to reveal his location but promises that Klamath will speak with and reward the party once they have dealt with the giants.

She ultimately accepts Unla Kee's residuals proposal: 500 gold upfront and residuals capped at 6,000 gold. She does not know, as Unla Kee privately notes, that the residuals are structured only against the current play and will not encumber the sequel.

Delsephine's last known position is aboard the Dragon's Breath as it arrives at Great Worm Cavern.

## Klamath

Klamath does not appear directly in these events but is discussed at length during negotiations over Bressik's contract.

Klamath is identified as a Red Dragon and a necromancer. He holds Bressik's contract in his hoard, and Delsephine acts as his negotiating agent. The contract is structured to cover two life terms — a living term valued at approximately 300 gold (10 gold per year over an assumed 30 remaining years) and an undead term valued at approximately 13,000 gold over an assumed 100 years of undead service. Delsephine confirms that Klamath is willing to negotiate terms and that he is "very supportive" of the party's plans against the giants, viewing giants as an obstacle to draconic domination of the North.

Bressik references Klamath's faction in contrast to Severin's, aligning Klamath with the original Dracolich ideology — creating a unified undead draconic race — rather than the Tiamat-summoning path that Severin pursued.

Orsik expresses genuine interest in meeting Klamath in person. Delsephine refuses to reveal his location but promises he will make contact once the giant threat is resolved.

Klamath's current whereabouts are unknown and deliberately withheld by Delsephine.

## Harshnag

Harshnag does not appear directly in these events but is referenced during the party's discussion of the gong artifact at Great Worm Cavern.

Orsik suggests that the gong — identified as a broken frost giant shield made of red dragon scales on a copper frame — could be given to Harshnag if it is indeed a shield, implying an existing relationship between the party and Harshnag and that Harshnag is of a size and nature that would make use of such an object.

## Severin

Severin does not appear directly in these events but is referenced during Bressik's defense of Cult of the Dragon ideology.

Bressik identifies Severin as the leader of the faction that misread Sammaster's texts and pursued the Tiamat summoning ritual rather than the original Dracolich vision. Bressik characterizes Severin's path as a coup against the original cult ideology, enabled by manipulation from Tiamat or another devil, and frames the summoning of Tiamat as something that would have prolonged the eternal conflict between dragons rather than resolved it.

## Elrem

Elrem does not appear directly but is referenced in Bressik's presentation on Great Worm Cavern.

Elrem is identified as the Great Worm, a legendary creature revered by the Great Worm tribe. The tribe's altar atop the triangular promontory is carved in the shape of a coiled serpent with great wings and represents Elrem. Tribal warriors make sacrifices at this altar to honor Uthgar and to ensure that Elrem does not return to devour them. Elrem's current status is ambiguous — the creature that bursts from the snowbank at the chapter's end, described as a mingling of worms, centipedes, and dragons with wing-like membranes and dozens of legs, may represent Elrem's actual appearance, though this is not confirmed in the text.
