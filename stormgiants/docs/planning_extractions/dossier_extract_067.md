## Mirran

Mirran is the eldest daughter of King Hekaton and is present in the great hall of the underwater stronghold performing alongside her sister Nym. She is tall and statuesque, and her voice soars with the power of a storm during the musical performance that the 3HPP interrupts upon their arrival.

When Orsik enters and claims to be there for a matchmaking appointment with Serissa, Mirran laughs and deflects him, telling him Queen Serissa is not ready to meet anyone and has important activities of state to manage. She coordinates with Nym on timing, suggesting Serissa might be free "in a few hours," buying time while setting up a trap.

Mirran later arrives at the guest rooms and uses a facade of warmth and welcome to lure the party toward the large cavernous room with the pool and teleportation circle. Once past the party, she drops the pretense and orders the four assembled giants — Duchess Tartha, Count Vaal, Jarl Hellenhild, and Thane Braxow — to kill them, calling the party "assassins" and framing them as threats to the queen. She shouts this accusation in Giant so the assembled lords will act on it.

Thistle recognizes the political trap: if the party kills Mirran, Serissa will believe her sister's framing that they are assassins. He warns the party not to harm her.

As the fight breaks out, Mirran takes a swipe at Orsik as he runs past her but misses. She also hurls a lightning bolt that strikes both Vardis and Unla Kee before departing the scene.

Mirran's motivations appear to center on preventing Serissa from forming any alliance or relationship with the party, and she is willing to orchestrate their deaths to accomplish this. Her disgust at the idea of a storm giant mating with a dwarf is explicit. Her last known position is having departed the combat area after throwing the lightning bolt.

## Nym

Nym is the younger sister of Mirran and Serissa, and a daughter of King Hekaton. She is present in the great hall performing alongside Mirran, playing a rhythmic melody on the shell of a hulking crab using whalebone mallets, moving with practiced grace.

When Mirran asks when Serissa might be free to meet Orsik, Nym suggests "in a few hours," participating in the deception alongside her sister. She also makes a pointed remark that Serissa was like their mother, always interested in "the little people," describing it as a fascination. When Orsik calls this casual racism, Vardis corrects him as fetishism, and Nym firmly counters that it is not a fetish but racism, asserting that giants are superior to little people.

Nym is present in the great hall during the initial confrontation but does not appear to follow the party to the combat area. Her motivations align with Mirran's in opposing any connection between Serissa and the party, and she shares the same contempt for smaller races. Her last known location is the great hall.

## Duchess Tartha

Duchess Tartha is a fire giant and one of four giant lords assembled by Mirran to kill the 3HPP. She is present in the cavernous room with the pool and teleportation circle when Mirran springs the trap.

She introduces herself and her companions once combat begins, naming herself, Count Vaal, Jarl Hellenhild, and Thane Braxow, and declaring that the party has come to kill the queen and that the lords will do their duty. She expresses a backhanded respect for Orsik after he kills Thane Braxow quickly, saying the queen may have seen something in him before promising he will not see her alive.

As a fire giant, Tartha is immune to fire effects and walks through Vardis's wall of fire unaffected, striking Thistle with her giant sword after passing through it. She also passes by Orsik, who gets an attack of opportunity against her. She is struck by Thistle's magically charged arrows and later knocked prone by Orsik's second strike, after which Orsik moves away from her. Unla Kee's fireball-like explosion invigorates rather than harms her due to her fire immunity.

Tartha also taunts Jarl Hellenhild for missing Thistle with a thrown boulder.

At the end of the recorded events, Tartha has been knocked prone but is not confirmed dead. She remains in the combat area.

## Count Vaal

Count Vaal is a cloud giant and one of the four giant lords assembled by Mirran. He is present in the cavernous room when the trap is sprung.

He introduces himself as part of the group of lords when Tartha makes the formal declarations. During combat, he activates a flight ability, rising into the air unbound by gravity. When Orsik expresses surprise that cloud giants can fly, Vaal mocks him, confirming the fact and rubbing it in. He is struck by Unla Kee's explosive fire mote.

Unla Kee also notes — apparently mid-combat — that cloud giants can not only fly but also cast Misty Step, which causes Vardis significant alarm. Vaal's last known position is airborne in the combat area, still active.

---

## Jarl Hellenhild

Jarl Hellenhild is a frost giant and one of the four giant lords assembled by Mirran. She is introduced alongside the other lords during Tartha's declaration at the start of combat.

During the fight she is unable to pass through Vardis's wall of fire and is frustrated that Duchess Tartha will claim the glory while she is blocked. She spots Thistle flying above the wall and throws a boulder at him, missing badly. When Count Vaal stares at her in disbelief and Tartha taunts her, Hellenhild claims she was trying new powder for a better grip.

She is struck by Unla Kee's fire explosion and is described as beginning to look worse for it. Her last known position is on the far side of the wall of fire, still active but significantly damaged.

---

## Thane Braxow

Thane Braxow is a stone giant and one of the four giant lords assembled by Mirran. He is introduced as part of the group alongside Tartha, Vaal, and Hellenhild. He is described as "the quiet one."

Orsik targets him first using Expeditious Retreat to close the distance quickly. He strikes Braxow twice, and although Braxow initially resists the giant slayer sword's magic, continued strikes cause him to bleed and eventually fall prone. Orsik strikes him a final time while he is prone and kills him.

Thane Braxow is the first giant casualty of the encounter. He is dead.
