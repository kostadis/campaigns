## Krowen Valharrow

Krowen Valharrow appears in his tower and inner sanctum in Everlund, where he meets with the adventuring party and recruits them as Harper contractors. He reflects internally that he is an old-timer who believed in the old ways, but acknowledges these are desperate times and that the group was sent by Dalthara herself. He privately wonders if they are the tool he needs to unlock the secrets behind the fall of the Ordning.

Krowen had previously arranged for the party to arrive via a special teleport spell involving alcohol to obscure their identities — a ruse that Vardis sees through, recognizing it as an attempt to burn Orsik. Krowen internally acknowledges the elf saw through him and puts on his best confused old mage act to cover it. He also nearly directs the party out the front door before being called out on the contradiction.

When asked about payment, Krowen tells the party there is none — only their loyalty. He is internally uncertain about Orsik due to reports of the dwarf's closeness to the Zhentarim.

When Vardis raises the possibility of obtaining a flying tower orb from Cloud Giants, Krowen is alarmed and works to dissuade them, warning that cloud giants are superior to fire giants in every respect and armed with magical weapons and defenses. He is reassured when the elf's expression suggests the point has landed.

Krowen directs the party to travel to Yartar to find a sword, and teleports them to his agent there. He has a dismissive reputation among older Harper agents — his agent Kolbaz refers to him as a "pompous two-bit self-aggrandizing" figure who has degraded what it means to be a Harper by using non-sworn contractors.

Krowen references Ezra as the person who originated the strategy of using mercenaries to derisk core Harper agents and provide plausible deniability, a strategy Krowen has adopted despite his reservations.

## Thistle

Thistle does not appear as a player character but is treated as an NPC in portions of this session involving his personal history and assassination attempts. Upon arrival in Yartar via teleport, Thistle lands in a garden meant to evoke the Seelie court, sees through the illusion immediately, and reacts with visible disgust at the cultural inaccuracies — identifying specific errors such as a dryad frolicking with Alciabes, whom he notes she hated. He confronts Kolbaz about using his people as props for a "magic fantasy land."

Thistle is revealed to be an assassination target of the Hand of Yartar, though he insists he did nothing to provoke them. He also reveals that the Cult of the Dragon has been targeting him because he is searching for Adimond Kroskas and because the Spring's First Blossom — a key ingredient Kroskas collected — is tied to the ritual investigation. Kolbaz disputes that the Spring's First Blossom was used in the ritual, claiming to have been involved in the research himself.

Thistle is reluctant to visit the Wink and Kiss due to the assassination attempt and must be persuaded by his companions. He names the group "Thistle Wendrod and Companions," provoking conflict with Orsik. He is visibly moved by his companions' declarations of loyalty and protection.

## Kolbaz

Kolbaz is Krowen's Harper agent in Yartar and the party's contact upon their arrival. He greets the party with suspicion, demanding to know who they are, and quickly expresses deep displeasure with Krowen's policy of using non-sworn contractors, saying that being a Harper used to mean something and was not just a job.

Kolbaz is an old and proud Harper operative. He identifies himself as the party's host and welcomes them to Yartar. He fought the Cult of the Dragon at the Well of the Dragons and reacts with intense indignation when Thistle asks if he knows Adimond Kroskas, shouting that he fought at the Well and insisting the cult was destroyed. He considers the idea that the Cult would target a fairy preposterous.

Kolbaz claims to have been personally involved in the research surrounding the ritual and is certain the Spring's First Blossom was not used in it, though he acknowledges the situation is "very odd" when pressed.

He has an interest in Seelie court aesthetics, having constructed an elaborate (if inaccurate) illusory garden as a safe house arrival point. When Thistle points out its cultural errors, Kolbaz invites him to discuss improvements over tea before being rebuffed. He takes professional pride in his illusion work and is noticeably stung by Thistle's critique.

Kolbaz directs the party to the Wink and Kiss tavern as the Hand of Yartar's known gathering place and confirms that rogues are pervasive throughout Yartar.

Kolbaz's fate: On the 2nd day of the 1st tenday of Flamerule, Kolbaz is struck by a bolt of lightning and collapses. A small, unidentified figure stands over him and delivers the threatening words "you meddling little fool" in an ominously large voice. His current status is unknown — likely gravely injured or dead.

## Maia (Qumaia)

Maia is a tiefling barhop at the Wink and Kiss tavern in Yartar. She recognizes the party as the group that defeated the giant in Triboar and has been advertising their play, referring to them as "the three half-pints and a pint." She is forthright and businesslike, and when she sees the party is broke, she mentions she knows someone who can give them work.

Maia connects the party with a bounty hunting opportunity and later, for five gold, directs them to Eltio at the bar as the person who can provide information on Harthal. She is later reprimanded by Eltio for vouching for the party, as Eltio is unhappy that they do not know who Tamlin Zoar is. Maia attempts to defend the introduction before being sent off with a warning.

## Elicia "Kal" Kallaphan

Elicia "Kal" Kallaphan is a severe half-elf operative of the Hand of Yartar who handles bounty payouts. She has blond hair, wears a cloak at all times, and carries a finely crafted rapier. She is described as seeing through the party's attempts at humor without amusement. She pays out 200 gold for the two bounties — 50 for herself and 150 for the party.

## Dashing Dallon Flek

Dashing Dallon Flek does not speak or act in this session. He is a bounty target captured by Unla Kee over the five-day period of bounty hunting. He is described as a handsome redhead with bright eyes and roguish good looks. He is turned in to the Hand of Yartar for a payout.

## Tink Urbelle

Tink Urbelle does not speak or act in this session. He is a bounty target captured by the rest of the party during the five-day bounty hunting period. He is described as a grubby gnome with curly hair and animated facial expressions. He is turned in to the Hand of Yartar for a payout.

## Eltio

Eltio is a woman dressed in white, encountered at the bar of the Wink and Kiss. Vardis sizes her up as sullen, distant, and dangerous. She is a Hand of Yartar operative who controls access to information about Harthal's whereabouts. She demands either 500 gold or a letter of recommendation from Tamlin Zoar in exchange for the information.

Eltio describes Tamlin Zoar to the party when they do not recognize the name, identifying her as a legendary member of the Hand of Yartar who built her own town on the Evermoor Road toward Everlund. She is dismissive of Maia for vouching for people who do not know Tamlin, and reprimands Maia before sending her off. She is noted as a patron of the arts, which Vardis exploits to negotiate — ultimately, Orsik secures her agreement to bring 50 friends to their play in exchange for waiving the fee.

## Tamlin Zoar

Tamlin Zoar does not appear in this session. She is discussed by Eltio as a legendary member of the Hand of Yartar who built her own town on the Evermoor Road between Yartar and Everlund. A letter of recommendation or personal endorsement from Tamlin would be sufficient to waive the 500 gold fee for information on Harthal. The party's next destination at the end of the session — Calling Horn — is presumably connected to finding her.

## Adimond Kroskas

Adimond Kroskas does not appear in this session. He is referenced by Thistle as a key Cult of the Dragon cultist involved in collecting the Spring's First Blossom — a powerful artifact — as an ingredient for an unspecified ritual. Thistle believes the Cult is targeting him because he is searching for Kroskas and because Kroskas's use of the Spring's First Blossom represents a mistake the Cult wants to clean up. Kolbaz claims never to have heard of Kroskas, and disputes that the Spring's First Blossom was used in the ritual at all, having been involved in the research himself.

## Dalthara

Dalthara does not appear in this session. She is referenced by Krowen as the person who sent the party to him, which is what persuaded him to work with non-sworn Harper contractors despite his reservations.

## Ezra

Ezra does not appear in this session. Krowen references Ezra as the Harper operative who pioneered the strategy of using mercenaries and contractors to provide plausible deniability and derisk core Harper agents — a strategy Krowen has now adopted. Krowen privately wonders how Ezra managed to pull it off with the First Faction.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is referenced throughout the session as an active member of the party. He is noted as still recovering from a prior ordeal at the start of the session. He is at low hit points (6) but declares his willingness to defend Thistle at the Wink and Kiss. He later goes on a lengthy drinking binge during the bounty hunting period and subsequently achieves two weeks of sobriety — described as his longest run — with noticeably sharper eyes. He uses his fate-altering abilities to help capture Dashing Dallon Flek. He advises the group to gamble as a way to earn money, noting he can change fates, but is warned against risking his life given his importance to preventing the end of the world.
