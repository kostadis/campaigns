## Delsephine

Delsephine appeared at the entrance of the Eye of the World, captaining a sky vessel — a small ship with sleigh runners held aloft by a giant red balloon — sent by Klauth. She introduced herself and her crew (Nyzroth, Brassik, Laz, Oriskus, Perella, Tralt, and Zalthia), all clad in black leather with black leather masks. She delivered Klauth's message: that the party should forge ahead, slay the giant leaders, and come to Klauth's hidden vale for a reward if they succeed in quelling the giant threat.

She agreed to transport the party to Everlund, though she expressed quiet skepticism about how that served the mission. She obeyed Orsik's direction despite his refusal to explain operational details.

When Thistle asked about the Cult of the Dragon, Delsephine revealed she had been a former member. She expressed bitter contempt for Severin, whom she called a fool for abandoning the cult's true divine purpose — creating Dracolichess to end the war between Tiamat and Bahamut — in favor of the insane plot to bring Tiamat into the world. She noted that Tiamat herself was played for a fool by Zariel in Avernus. Delsephine left the cult when she was ordered to halt all Dracolich research in favor of securing loot for the ritual; Klauth then offered her an opportunity, which she took.

She displayed a barely concealed deep sexual attraction toward Klauth, which unsettled Orsik.

When Thistle asked about Adimond Kroskas, Delsephine spat in contempt. She revealed that Severin had trusted Kroskas to provide powerful magics, and that Kroskas — whose gender she was uncertain of — had failed to deliver. This established Kroskas as someone operating at the highest levels of the Cult of the Dragon during Severin's leadership.

Last known status: captaining Klauth's sky vessel, transporting the party.

## Klauth

Does not appear in this chunk but is extensively discussed. Klauth is described as the Great Dragon of the North who sent Delsephine and her crew to the party as a gift, offering the sky vessel and crew services so the party can cross his vast dominion while avoiding the perils below. His message, delivered through Delsephine, urged the party to forge ahead, slay the giant leaders, and visit his hidden vale for a reward if they succeed. Orsik interpreted Klauth's motivation as self-interest: the giants and dragons are rival ethno-religious groups, Klauth's kind was badly wounded at the Well of the Dragon, and Klauth does not want the Lord's Alliance investigating the Spine of the World. Delsephine also revealed a deep personal devotion and sexual attraction to Klauth, having left the Cult of the Dragon to serve him.

## Adimond Kroskas

Does not appear in this chunk but is discussed multiple times. The Oracle of Annam revealed that Kroskas is currently located at the Maelstorm, the Storm Giant King's court. Thistle also learned that King Hekaton's conch of teleportation will take him to the Maelstorm. Delsephine, when asked about Kroskas, spat in contempt and revealed that during Severin's leadership of the Cult of the Dragon, Kroskas had been trusted to provide powerful magics but failed to deliver. Delsephine was uncertain of Kroskas's gender. Orsik noted that Kroskas was evidently important enough to be operating alongside the most powerful leader of the Cult of the Dragon. Kroskas is established as Thistle's primary nemesis and a major target of the party's larger quest.

## Annam (the Oracle)

The Oracle of Annam appeared in the Eye of the World and answered questions put to it by the party over two days. On the first day, it responded to a question about favorite books with "the collected sayings of Annam," extinguishing the last torch. The following morning it answered: that Adimond Kroskas is at the Maelstorm; that King Hekaton's conch will take Thistle there and the path they choose will lead to it; that the act that will most impress the Zhentarim is ending the Giant War, restoring trade routes, and securing preferential treatment from the Storm Giant King for Zhentarim merchants. When Thistle attempted a trick question about burial grounds and Duke Zalto, the Oracle laughed deeply and said "I have been doing this for a very long time," extinguishing a torch. It also answered that evil giants seek power, glory, and divine favor so they are elevated when the ordning is remade. It revealed that Urgala's wife fell to her death in a well that led to the Underdark. The Oracle is limited to six questions per day.

## Dral Thelev

Dral Thelev appeared at Danivarr's House in Everlund when the party arrived after several days of airborne travel. He greeted Orsik and the party with mild ribbing about whether they had solved the mystery of the ordning and brought peace to the world. He expressed brief concern, muttering that he was sure Krowen Valharrow had chosen them for a reason. He arranged for a tavern boy to fetch Captain Zoar, and later at Zoar's request brought food, drink, and a specialty dish of fishies with special sauce, salad, and dressings. He facilitated the meeting between the party and the Captain.

## Horix Zoar (Captain Zoar)

Captain Horix Zoar, High Captain of Everlund, appeared at Danivarr's House after being summoned by Dral Thelev. He arrived with three guards and was initially dismissive of the party ("smelly adventurers"). His attitude changed when Thistle presented a letter of introduction from his sister Tamalin Zoar, the mayor of Calling Horns. He spoke warmly and proudly of his sister's transformation from cutthroat and brigand to person of character and substance.

Zoar agreed to write a letter of mark establishing the party as his agents, granting them assistance and grace from Silvery Moon authorities. He was enthusiastic about the request once Thistle mentioned a criminal organization trafficking demons in Silvery Moon, launching into a detailed and bitter account of Silvery Moon's abandonment of Sundbar during the orc horde attacks and Everlund's subsequent departure from the Lord's Alliance. He expressed that Silvery Moon trafficking demons matched the "blackness and depravity of their souls."

Zoar was also delighted to discover the party were the silent backers of the smash theatrical production featuring Bob Saget. He planned to take his children to see it and relished the idea that Everlund being allied with the producers would embarrass Silvery Moon. He decided to keep the party's name as 3HPP in the letter for that reason.

When Thistle suggested Zoar consider running for a political office, Zoar demurred, saying he was pleased as High Captain, fully supported Speaker Vatrice Stormwright, and noted her re-election was years away with much that could happen. He acknowledged that if the party were seen defeating the giant threat, it would greatly increase his own prestige.

Last known status: returned to his duties, fed, slightly inebriated, and happy, having issued the party a letter of mark.

## Tamalin Zoar

Does not appear in this chunk but is discussed. Tamalin Zoar is the mayor of Calling Horns and the sister of High Captain Horix Zoar. She had previously given the party a letter of introduction. Her brother described her history: she began as a cutthroat, brigand, and cutpurse, robbing people up and down the coast, then became a member of a gang of women thieves in Triboar, before eventually buying a house on a road, building a town, and becoming mayor. Captain Zoar expressed great pride in her transformation into a person of character and substance. Her letter of introduction was the key that opened Zoar's cooperation with the party.

## Severin

Does not appear in this chunk but is discussed by Delsephine. Severin is described as the former leader of the Cult of the Dragon whom Delsephine condemns as a fool. He redirected the cult away from its divine purpose of creating Dracoliches to end the Tiamat-Bahamut war, instead pursuing the insane plot to bring Tiamat into the world. He trusted Adimond Kroskas to provide powerful magics, which Kroskas failed to deliver. The implication is that Severin is no longer in power, likely dead or deposed following Tiamat's failed manifestation.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee appeared briefly in the scene at the Eye of the World. When Thistle turned to her hoping for a better reaction after the Oracle's answer about the Zhentarim, she stared smugly back and whispered, "I know how the world ends, this Oracle has nothing to teach me." She is established as a halfling and appears to hold knowledge or beliefs that place her beyond the Oracle's revelations in her own estimation.

## Zariel

Does not appear in this chunk but is mentioned by Delsephine. Zariel is identified as Tiamat's hostess in the planes of Avernus in the Nine Hells. Delsephine revealed that Tiamat believed the plan to enter the world was her own, but she was in fact played for a fool by Zariel. The implication is that Zariel manipulated Tiamat's attempted manifestation for her own ends.
