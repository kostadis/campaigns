## Orsik

Orsik appears throughout this chapter as an active participant in the group's efforts against Bad Fruul and the Spine Ripper tribe.

On the 4th day, Orsik confronts Chandra, arguing forcefully and with evident hurt that Bad Fruul could send minions to kill her and that he alone can guarantee her safety. His argument is persuasive enough that Chandra agrees. He is about to follow Blasius when Prince Thornacious announces a dinner for their friends.

On the 5th day, Orsik splits from the group and finds goblins in the field, deceiving them with false information about Bad Fruul. His deception is effective enough that fewer goblins join the hill giant's banner.

On the 6th day, Orsik enters the cave complex with the group. He shouts "FINALLY" when the first fireball explodes during the cave battle. He is among those who kill the orog and acolyte that knocked Unla Kee unconscious, helping to turn the tide. During the exploration of the nursery, he participates in the decision to spare the orc children and the Orc Claw of Luthic.

When the human prisoners are discovered, they initially mistake Orsik for the militia coming to their rescue. Orsik recognizes the families as belonging to Jarlor and Larrish, men he knows from his time as a town council member. Upon learning the orcs were using the families as leverage to force Jarlor and Larrish to spy on Chief Kurglow, Orsik reacts with visible murderous rage.

## Chandra

Chandra does not physically appear beyond the opening scene. On the 4th day, she is confronted by Orsik, who argues that Bad Fruul could send minions to kill her and that she is not safe without him. She is shocked and intimidated by the forcefulness of his plea and agrees to his terms. Her last known status is having consented to Orsik's offer of protection.

## Blasius

Blasius appears briefly on the 4th day. After Orsik succeeds in convincing Chandra, Blasius turns to Thistle and says, "Guell my boy! I am off," indicating he is departing. His destination and purpose are not specified. He does not appear in subsequent events.

## Prince Thornacious

Prince Thornacious appears briefly on the 4th day. As Orsik is about to follow Blasius out, the Prince shouts an announcement ordering a fine dinner for their friends. This is his only action in this chapter. His last known location is the gathering where Chandra, Orsik, and Blasius were present.

## Thistle

Thistle is an active participant throughout the chapter.

On the 4th day, Blasius bids farewell to him directly before departing.

On the 5th day, Thistle conducts an aerial survey and finds evidence of Bad Fruul's intended invasion route, which he confirms with Vardis and Wallace as coming from the north. He joins the group in inspecting the fortifications and is pleased with what he finds.

On the 6th day, Thistle confidently shouts "I got this!" and leads the group to the orc den, crediting his knowledge to Uncle Joon, whom he believes is on the side of good and taught him about every orc tribe and den location. After Orsik's dismissive comment about flying up 50 feet, Thistle flies at a mere 7 feet altitude, leading the group toward some hills until an orog spots them and retreats into the cave. Thistle then proposes waiting outside in ambush. When no one exits after some time, he enters the caves alone to investigate. He is among those who ultimately kill the orog and acolyte that felled Unla Kee. He also participates in the decision regarding the nursery and the Orc Claw of Luthic.

## Vardis

Vardis participates in the reconnaissance on the 5th day, carefully investigating forest trails near Parnast to confirm the invasion route from the north. He confers with Thistle and Wallace and agrees the attack will come from the north. He also joins the inspection of the town's fortifications.

On the 6th day, Vardis provides crucial context during the cave exploration, identifying the room with the trap as a War Hearth and noting its plentiful shrines to orc gods and capacity for many orcs.

## Wallace

Wallace participates in the 5th day reconnaissance, leveraging his local knowledge to further confirm the northern invasion route. He confers with Thistle and Vardis and joins the inspection of the town's fortifications.

On the 6th day, Wallace is among those who kill the orog and acolyte that knocked Unla Kee unconscious during the cave battle.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is active on the 5th day, recognizing the banner of Bad Fruul among hoard members. From the banner he extrapolates the size of the hoard, infers that a tribe of orcs has not yet joined the giant's army, and uses his knowledge of the area to identify the tribe as "The Spinerippers" and deduce their likely location.

On the 6th day, during the cave battle, Unla Kee casts fireballs to deal with a trap deeper in the cave. However, while standing in the hallway after casting, an orog and an acolyte of Grummsh erupt from that direction and promptly knock him unconscious. He is saved when Orsik, Wallace, and Thistle kill the attackers. His status after the battle is not specified beyond having been knocked unconscious and his allies surviving to continue exploring.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this chapter but is referenced by Thistle on the 6th day. Thistle invokes Uncle Joon's name as the source of his knowledge about every orc tribe and the best places for orcs to create a den, and expresses confidence that Uncle Joon is on the side of good. Uncle Joon's actual identity, relationship to Thistle, and current whereabouts are not detailed in this chapter.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul does not appear in this chapter but is central to the events throughout. On the 4th day, Orsik warns Chandra that Bad Fruul could send minions to kill her rather than acting personally, characterizing him as an evil overlord who uses subordinates for dirty work.

On the 5th day, the heroes' entire reconnaissance effort is directed at learning where Bad Fruul is and how he intends to invade Parnast. Thistle's aerial survey, Vardis's trail investigation, and Wallace's local knowledge all converge on confirming the northern route of attack. Orsik deceives goblins with false information about Bad Fruul, reducing the number who join his banner. Unla Kee identifies Bad Fruul's banner among hoard members and uses it to infer the size of the hoard and the absence of the Spine Ripper orcs.

His army is described as a hoard with a banner and includes goblins and various humanoid followers. The Spine Ripper orcs had not yet joined his army at the time of the events.

## Chief Kurglow

Chief Kurglow does not appear in person but is revealed as a key figure in the orc tribe's operations. The orc prisoners' families reveal that Kurglow threatened to kill them unless Jarlor and Larrish spied on him — the clear implication being that Kurglow is himself a target of surveillance, not the one ordering it. One of the children clarifies that their father did not want to spy but was coerced by Kurglow's death threats. Kurglow's relationship to the Spine Ripper tribe and his current whereabouts are not specified beyond his role as the chief who coerced the two men.

## Jarlor

Jarlor does not appear in person but is revealed through the testimony of his family, who are among the human prisoners freed from the orc cave. His family was held hostage by Chief Kurglow to coerce Jarlor into acting as a spy. One of his children defends him, saying he did not want to spy but was forced to by Kurglow's threats. Orsik recognizes Jarlor from his time on the town council. Jarlor's current whereabouts are unknown.

## Larrish

Larrish does not appear in person but is revealed through the testimony of his family, who are among the human prisoners freed from the orc cave alongside Jarlor's family. Like Jarlor, Larrish was coerced into spying by Chief Kurglow under threat of his family's death. Orsik recognizes Larrish from his time on the town council. Larrish's current whereabouts are unknown.

## Orc Claw of Luthic

The Orc Claw of Luthic is encountered in the nursery within the cave complex, where she is guarding orc children. The companions initially consider killing all the orcs in the nursery before recognizing this as monstrous. They gain leverage over her when they realize one of the whelps is theirs — the exact meaning of this is not elaborated, but it implies some ownership or prior claim. Threatened with attack and confronted with this leverage, the Orc Claw of Luthic grabs her whelp and moves away. She is not killed and her final status is withdrawing from the confrontation.

## Eye of Grummsh

The Eye of Grummsh is discovered among the dead orcs during the exploration of the cave complex following the battle. He is identified as a shaman of Grummsh and one of the orcs killed during the fighting. The acolyte of Grummsh who attacked Unla Kee is described as a clear follower of his. He is deceased.
