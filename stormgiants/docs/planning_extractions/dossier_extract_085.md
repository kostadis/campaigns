## Iymrith

Iymrith is an ancient, gargantuan blue dragon who appears through a portal at the ruined courtyard just as the 3HPP is on the verge of being wiped out by two blue dragon–purple worm hybrids. Thistle recognizes her from the Maelstrom. She arrives with cultists on her back who heal Orsik, Unla Kee, and Vardis. Rather than attacking the party, she begins clapping, indicating she has been watching and is pleased.

Iymrith reveals that the two hybrid creatures guarding the desert are her creations — deadly but mindless sentinels she uses to keep out unwanted guests. Her true ambition is a more sophisticated experiment: a hybrid that combines the innocence of a fairy with draconic destructive power, capable of simultaneously existing in the Feywild and the Prime Material Plane through an intrinsic, non-magical physical swap between forms. Magical detection would not work on such a creature. This creature is Laela, whom she captured after finding Laela torching a caravan in dragon form.

Iymrith needs the party's help because Laela refuses to shift out of her fey form, practicing what Iymrith calls "fey-fullness" — a powerful fey meditation. Without a demonstration, Iymrith cannot prove to her investors that her experiment is a success. She calls periodic meetings of the five chromatic dragon clans and wants the party to fight Laela at a coliseum event, not to kill her, but to demonstrate that she is a lethal Fey Dragon hybrid capable of transforming into dragon form.

Her investors are revealed under pressure: various giant lords who believed she was a giant and now know she is a dragon (creating political danger for her), a delegation of Red Wizards (whose involvement stems from a deal she made with Szass Tam), and representatives of both the Unseelie and Seelie courts. Thistle's revelation that the Seelie Court — Queen Titania — knew of and approved the experiments is implied to be why Thistle was expelled from the Seelie Court.

Iymrith explicitly distances herself from the SEER, calling the SEER the real power behind the Lord's Alliance and describing her as committed to a long-term plan to end the freedom of creatures like Iymrith. She says the SEER is evil and that Orsik is right to distrust her. She states she does not work with the SEER.

She draws a precise, detailed map of the coliseum in the sand, showing where Laela would emerge and how the event would unfold. She agrees — somewhat desperately — to allow the party to arrive days in advance to "rehearse," which the party uses as a pretext to gain access to her lair and time with Laela. She is manipulated into this arrangement by Orsik's theatrical framing, agreeing that a staged performance (including scripted dialogue and unitards) was something she had already planned.

Iymrith cannot teleport the party to her lair because the coliseum has been magically warded against scrying and teleportation. She refuses to carry them herself, stating only those who worship her greatness may ride her. She offers the hybrid dragons as transport instead. Her lair is a three-day journey away.

She describes herself not as a mere powerful dragon but as an artiste and creator, frustrated that her reputation rests on what she considers lucky results with drakes. She wants validation of her scientific and artistic credentials. When Orsik asks why she didn't self-fund the experiment from her hoard, she reacts with volcanic horror at the suggestion of spending her own treasure.

Iymrith provides Thistle with a sending stone to speak with Laela as proof of life, then takes it back. She acknowledges frustration that even an entity of her power can become trapped by too many deals. Despite her immense strength — described as a legendary force that dwarfs the two hybrids, whose movements shake the earth and whose voice makes mountains tremble — she is clearly under pressure from her investors and in genuine need of the party's cooperation.

Her current status: present at the ruined desert courtyard, having negotiated a deal with the party. She will transport them via her hybrid creatures to her lair over three days, where the coliseum demonstration is to take place.

## Laela

Laela does not appear in person in this session but is contacted via a sending stone provided by Iymrith as proof of life.

During the brief communication, Laela immediately recognizes Thistle's voice and expresses relief that he is alive while simultaneously scolding him for his long absence, claiming she has been looking all over for him. She reacts with hostility to Vardis's presence, objecting to Thistle working with an elf, and states they had agreed they don't like elves. The conversation is cut off by Iymrith before it continues further.

Iymrith reveals the full circumstances of Laela's situation: she was found torching a caravan while in dragon form. Iymrith captured her. Since then, Laela has refused to shift back into her dragon form, instead maintaining a state of fey meditation Iymrith calls "fey-fullness." This refusal is the central problem preventing Iymrith from demonstrating her experiment's success. Iymrith cannot compel her to transform. Orsik deduces that Laela's fey nature means she is always angry, which he suggests is the secret to her power.

Vardis notes that Laela's claim to have been looking for Thistle is inconsistent with Iymrith's framing of her as a captive subject, implying Laela has more agency in her situation than Iymrith presents.

Iymrith describes Laela as still very much a fairy in personality — she has a squeaky voice, complains about her wingtips needing wingmuffs, and has a fully fey temperament despite her draconic capabilities. She is currently held in a cage in Iymrith's lair.

## Szass Tam

Szass Tam does not appear in this session but is referenced by Iymrith when she explains her investor relationships. Iymrith states she made a deal with Szass Tam, with the Red Wizards of Thay serving as his minions and investors in her hybrid experiments. Iymrith acknowledges it was a mistake but justified it at the time by her need for funding. When Thistle asks why she doesn't simply eat the Red Wizards, she explains she cannot because of her contractual obligations to Szass Tam.
