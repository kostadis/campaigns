## Kagrota Threeteeth (Kagrota)

Kagrota Threeteeth, referred to as "Mother of Bones," is an Ogre who serves as the primary antagonist in the attack on the Brandimen cabin. She is in command of a force of orcs and goblins who have set up camp at the cabin, holding prisoners from a nearby commune in a cage in the clearing.

Kagrota had received orders from Bad Fruul to destroy the commune, which resulted in several commune members being killed. She and her forces raided the commune and took survivors as prisoners, one of whom had a finger severed — a detail relevant to the party's investigation into Tove Brandimen's missing mother.

One of the prisoners reveals that Tove Brandimen was "thick as thieves" with Kagrota, suggesting Tove's claim about his mother being held hostage was a deception and that he was working in collusion with her.

During the battle, Kagrota shouted orders at her troops, expressing frustration at their poor performance ("Do I have to do everything?"). She threw a javelin that slipped from her fingers and missed. She demonstrated spellcasting ability by summoning a spiritual weapon in the form of a hammer, striking Unla Kee with it. She also attacked Wallace with the spiritual weapon and struck at Orsik with her hammer during the fight.

Thistle reduced Kagrota to 1 HP, at which point she screamed, "Fairies! We had a deal with Prince Thornacious!" — revealing an existing agreement or alliance with a figure named Prince Thornacious. Wallace subsequently delivered the killing blow, punching Kagrota to death.

Beneath the cabin, a hidden underground chamber contained a crude altar bearing the painted image of a black sunburst, two black candles, an elven woman's skull, and the shattered remains of a jade statuette — all symbols associated with the god Bane. This suggests Kagrota was a worshiper of Bane. The chamber also contained several chests and bags of treasure.

Kagrota is deceased, killed during the combat at the cabin.

## Tove Brandimen

Tove Brandimen does not appear in this session but is meaningfully discussed. He had previously provided the party with a note supposedly from Kagrota Threeteeth and a severed finger as evidence that his mother was being held hostage at the cabin, in order to send the adventurers there.

Vardis and Orsik had already harbored suspicions about the hostage story, with Vardis whispering to Orsik that it reminded them of a "hostage scam" they had previously pulled themselves.

Their suspicions are confirmed when one of the rescued prisoners states that Tove was "thick as thieves" with Kagrota, indicating he was complicit with the ogre rather than a victim of her. The severed finger presented as evidence of his mother's captivity is revealed to belong to a member of the commune that Kagrota raided — causing one of the surviving women to cry hysterically upon seeing it.

Orsik's reaction upon learning the truth is to remark, "I told you we should have fed him to the Wyvern." Tove's current whereabouts are unknown.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul does not appear in this session but is referenced by the rescued prisoners. One of the prisoners explains that Bad Fruul had sent Kagrota to destroy their commune, resulting in the deaths of several commune members and the imprisonment of the survivors.

When Orsik asks the prisoners if they know anything about Bad Fruul, they respond with "Bad who?" indicating they have no further information about him beyond his role in ordering the attack. Bad Fruul's identity, location, and broader motivations remain unclear to the party.

## Prince Thornacious

Prince Thornacious does not appear in this session but is referenced in a single moment of dialogue. As Thistle reduced Kagrota to 1 HP, Kagrota screamed, "Fairies! We had a deal with Prince Thornacious!" — suggesting that Kagrota or her forces had some prior arrangement or alliance with a figure named Prince Thornacious, and that she believed the presence of Thistle (a fairy) was connected to or a betrayal of that deal. No further details about Prince Thornacious, the nature of the deal, or his identity are provided.

## Chandra Stol

Chandra Stol does not appear in this session but is referenced at the very end. As the party heads back to Parnast, the narrator notes that foremost on their minds is that Chandra Stol will be upset that she has to feed the Wyvern cooked meat instead of living prey. This implies Chandra Stol has responsibility for feeding the wyvern in Parnast and has preferences or standards regarding how it is fed. Her current whereabouts are Parnast.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session but is referenced in the context of Thistle's knowledge. When the group encounters the altar of Bane beneath the cabin, Thistle explains the nature and history of Bane to the group, with the narration noting that "Thistle, whose Uncle Joon explained everything to him," is the source of this knowledge. Uncle Joon is thus established as a mentor or relative who educated Thistle in matters of religion and the planes. No other details about Uncle Joon are provided.
