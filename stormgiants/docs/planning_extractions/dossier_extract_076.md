## Sandesyl

Sandesyl is a vampire (not merely a spawn) who serves as the primary antagonist of this session, operating from within her lair deep in a cavernous complex. She is never directly seen during the main battle, remaining hidden while directing her minions — vampire spawn, guard drakes, and a failed dracolich skeleton — against the party.

From her perch in her cave, Sandesyl watches her minions fall with rage but without panic. She has a secret escape location unknown to anyone else, where she can safely regenerate, and this knowledge gives her confidence she cannot be permanently killed in this encounter. Her goal during the battle is to eliminate the wizard Unla Kee, whom she identifies as the greatest threat.

Acting invisibly, she moves through the chaotic melee and attempts to grab Unla Kee with her claws. Her first claw attack misses, but the second grasps him. Despite this, Unla Kee maintains his concentration. She then retreats behind the skeletal dragon to use it as cover, which proves to be a fatal tactical error — it gives Unla Kee a clear opportunity to unleash his Sunbeam spell. Sandesyl attempts to avoid the worst of the blast but fails, with Unla Kee noting that fate twisted against her. She screams "A diviner!" upon realizing Unla Kee had foreseen the attack. Vardis follows up with his own radiant attack, burning her further.

Unable to escape the permeating bright light from Thistle and Unla Kee's spells, and with her minions destroyed, Sandesyl screams in rage and turns to mist. Her coffin, found in a chamber accessible from the western wall of the main cavern, is discovered to be empty. Her actual refuge — her secret regeneration location — lies somewhere in the endless deep cracks in the ice caves, location unknown to the party.

Her defeat is described as the "first defeat," implying she will regenerate and return. The party's objective of luring the Dracolich Glazhael to the battle by severely injuring Sandesyl technically succeeded in injuring her, but Glazhael could not physically enter the lair, making that part of the plan a failure.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is a cloud giant accompanying the party in the assault on Sandesyl's lair. At the opening of the battle, he announces his intention to hang back and protect the magic user, which Orsik forcefully overrides, ordering him to charge into battle. Orsik motivates him by praising his earlier solo defeat of armored horrors, calling him "the key to this victory." The giant responds emotionally — sniffling at the praise — and then charges in, destroying one of Sandesyl's vampire spawn.

When the fireball explodes (origin unclear, possibly from the skeletal dragon), Blagothkus is caught in the blast and cannot avoid the heat, taking significant damage. Immediately after, the skeletal dragon's jaw snaps at his arms, and he howls in pain. He subsequently asks for healing before continuing to fight the dragon, which provokes Orsik into threatening to kill him. Taken aback and apparently using the name "Escarlotta" as a social lever — asking what she would say — Blagothkus is unmoved by Orsik's dismissal of the reference. Realizing Orsik is not joking, he picks up a rock from the cavern floor and hurls it at the skeletal dragon. He later participates in the ranged attacks that finish off the last of the Cult of the Dragon disciples alongside Thistle.

His current status is injured but standing at the end of the session.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta does not appear in this session. She is referenced by Blagothkus during a confrontation with Orsik, when Blagothkus invokes her name — "What would Escarlotta say?" — apparently as a social or moral appeal intended to restrain Orsik's aggression. Orsik dismisses the appeal entirely. The reference implies Escarlotta holds some authority or moral weight over Blagothkus, or that she has a known relationship with Orsik as well.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is the party's wizard (referred to as "the magic user" by Blagothkus), present throughout the assault on Sandesyl's lair. Upon seeing the skeletal dragon emerge from the eastern chamber, he identifies it immediately as "a failed attempt at a dracolich" and explains that this is why Sandesyl needed the book — providing critical intelligence about her plans mid-battle.

When engulfed by the fireball, Unla Kee succeeds in his saving throw and, crucially, maintains his concentration on an active spell. He surveys the battlefield and calculates that a fireball will damage five times more enemies than allies, then casts it into the cluster surrounding Orsik, devastating the guard drakes and the vampire spawn while Orsik endures the blast.

Sandesyl targets him specifically, moving invisibly to seize him with her claws. Her second claw grasps him, but he is prepared — he does not lose concentration. When Sandesyl retreats behind the skeletal dragon, Unla Kee takes the opportunity to cast Sunbeam, combining prayer with ancient magical language and directing a searing line of radiance that burns Sandesyl severely and causes her to scream "A diviner!" — revealing that Unla Kee's divination abilities had predicted her movement and allowed him to counter her. The dragon's strike on him ends Orsik's Haste effect, but Unla Kee survives.

He is struck by the lightning bolt trap when Orsik opens the chest at the session's end.

## Glazhael

Glazhael does not appear in this session. She is referenced in dialogue as the dracolich the party had hoped to lure into the fight. According to Orsik, Glazhael had agreed to show up if the party got Sandesyl deeply injured. Thistle points out that Glazhael had no way to physically enter Sandesyl's lair, making the plan unworkable regardless of how badly Sandesyl was hurt. Glazhael's non-appearance is a source of frustration for Orsik at the session's end.

## Thuul

Thuul is a party member (described with drake-armor, suggesting he may be a dragonborn or similar) who fights a guard drake in a separate part of the caverns during the main battle. The drake strikes Thuul, but he shrugs off the bite and kills the creature with a vicious bite of his own. He is last noted as having survived his engagement, standing in the deep caverns after killing the final drake.
