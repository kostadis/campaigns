## Coatl

The Coatl emerged from the totem as Orsik passed it, demanding that the 3HPP destroy Wormblod, who it stated had perverted the tribe. It offered a boon in exchange for this act. When Thistle immediately claimed they had already killed Wormblod, the Coatl granted all members of the party a Charm of Restoration. The creature was visibly displeased by the group's lukewarm gratitude, its eyes bulging as it complained, "Usually, they appreciate me more," and muttered its displeasure while slithering its tongue in and out of its mouth.

## Bressik

Bressik was aboard the Dragon's Breath following his newfound freedom, delighted at the opportunity to work on background research and the play. ## Borin Greycastle

Borin Greycastle does not appear in this chapter but is referenced by the starstruck gate guard, who asked Unla Kee to sign an autograph to be sent care of Borin Greycastle. No further details about this individual are provided.

## Rebecca Deakin

Rebecca Deakin was found at Wyrmworks in Silverymoon, where Thistle had previously paid her to conduct research on Adimond Kroskas. She expressed surprise that the party was still alive, having assumed anyone investigating Adimond would have been killed. Her research had apparently radicalized her further against magic users and the Spellguard, whom she called a "crypto-fascist tool of the mages and a force of control." She articulated a belief that non-magic users are treated as lesser people by wizards and that the Spellguard's hands are never truly dirty. She caught herself speaking too freely and paused before continuing.

Regarding her research findings on Adimond Kroskas: she revealed he is a powerful spellcaster who knows a spell to transform Drakes from Dragon eggs. She clarified the distinction between pure Drakes (created from Dragon eggs) and lesser Drakes (their offspring). She reported that Adimond was hired by the Cult of Tiamat to work on a fay-dragon drake hybrid. She further relayed information from a man who claimed to be Thistle's Uncle Joon, who told her that Adimond had gone into the Fae to obtain the First Flower of Spring as a critical component of his spell research, and that fairies were also involved — possibly as test subjects, components, or "treats." She concluded with her suspicion that Adimond Kroskas himself may be a dragon, noting that the Cult of the Dragon referred to him alternately as a dragon and as a dragon's pet.

## Adimond Kroskas

## Lord Methrammar Aersume

Lord Methrammar Aersume met with the 3HPP at the High Palace. He reported warmly that Bob Saget's play had been a smashing success, sold out for the next two months, and handed the party 12,000 gold pieces as their share of profits — noting they could cover the 6,000 gold owed to the red dragon from their own cut. He described the play's final battle scene with evident enthusiasm, detailing how Orsik's battle mastery, Vardis's divine powers, and Unla Kee's spells are depicted, along with impressive lighting and explosive effects. He noted that Thistle's role is played by a puppet on a stick due to inability to find a small enough actor.

He characterized the play as a "spiritual investigation" about individual accountability and the dangers of unchecked magic rather than a political piece, and firmly rejected Orsik's suggestion of a new play. When Orsik attempted to probe whether he agreed that magic was dangerous, Aersume responded with a veiled threat — "There is a danger in your questions" — and declared that those who claim magic cannot serve Silverymoon are traitors to the city. He invoked his mother, Lady Laeral Silverhand, favored of Mystara, as evidence that magic has served Silverymoon for generations and will continue to do so. His relationship with the party appears cordial but carries an undercurrent of authority and warning.

## Lady Laeral Silverhand

Lady Laeral Silverhand does not appear in this chapter but is referenced by Lord Methrammar Aersume as his mother, one of the seven anointed of Mystara and a former ruler of Silverymoon. Aersume cited her favorable view of magic as justification for his own position that magic can and should continue to serve the city, using her legacy to shut down Orsik's line of questioning.

## Bob Saget

Bob Saget met with the 3HPP that evening, appearing in a noticeably different appearance than before. He was delighted to see the party and reported that the theater is full every night. He described his play as "a successful perversion of society" that will achieve the party's stated aims against magic within 30 days, framing the work as serving their goals rather than his own. He was evasive and dismissive about the identities of the actors portraying the 3HPP, calling them "nobodies" and explaining that amateurs work better in the climactic scene because their genuine terror reads as authentic on stage.

Bob refused to provide the heroes with seats to watch the performance, insisting their presence would be a distraction. He revealed that Kaelen wrote the original play that summoned him, crediting Kaelen with saving him from "an eternity of bad dad jokes," and stated that he and Kaelen now work together to improve the play. This disclosure suggests an ongoing partnership between Bob and Kaelen that the party had not previously been aware of. Orsik, hearing the refusal to grant access, purchased invisibility potions to attend regardless.

## Kaelen

Kaelen does not appear directly in this chapter but is referenced by Bob Saget, who revealed that Kaelen wrote the original play that summoned him and saved him from "an eternity of bad dad jokes."
