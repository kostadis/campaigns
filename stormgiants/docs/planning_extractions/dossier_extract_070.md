## Queen Serissa

Queen Serissa is the youngest daughter of King Hekaton and Queen Neri, and the current ruler of the storm giant court at the underwater citadel. She was named heir apparent by her father, who judged her elder sisters Mirren and Nym unfit to rule — Mirren for being tempestuous and prone to emotional outbursts, Nym for being cold and unloving. This decision was made known to the elder sisters years before Hekaton's disappearance, sowing resentment.

Serissa was present in the throne room arguing with Adimond Kroskas (Iymrith in disguise) when the party emerged through the Vortex pool. She was stunned by the party's sudden appearance and further shocked when Iymrith revealed her true form as a blue dragon and fled. After recovering, Serissa took command of the situation, repeatedly calling for silence and demanding a clear explanation of events.

She was already aware, or quickly became aware, that Adimond Kroskas was a dragon — she pointedly questioned the Imperator about whether it was his job to have known this, twisting the knife when he admitted he had been deceived. She revealed that she had disagreed with the Imperator's decision to ally with Adimond but had no choice at the time, as Adimond claimed his fairy/dragon hybridization experiments would produce a new breed of dragons to use as weapons against rival giant factions. She describes this as a stopgap measure while she searched for her missing father.

Serissa articulated three major problems facing the court: finding her father King Hekaton, dealing with the fallout from Adimond's infiltration, and managing her sisters' treachery. She produced a token found on the bodies of the small folk who were discovered dead alongside Queen Neri, presenting it as a potential clue to Hekaton's whereabouts. When Unla Kee identified it as a gambling chit from the Golden Goose boat in Yartar run by Lord Drylund, she accepted his offer to research the lead and agreed to provide funding, authorizing the use of the golden suit of scale mail from the armory — with the fiction that it was given to Unla Kee for research rather than simply handed over. She winked at the party, making clear she was fully aware they had already been eyeing the armor and that she was being deliberately accommodating.

She issued a warning to Unla Kee: she remains queen of the storm giants, and if he took the money without producing results, there would be consequences.

Serissa expressed a desire to form alliances with the small folk, echoing her late mother Queen Neri's philosophy. She used the phrase "little people" to describe the party, corrected it diplomatically when Orsik took mild offense, and formally welcomed Dwarves, Elves, Fairies, and Halflings as potential allies. She acknowledged Orsik's point that there is more to ruling than subjugating other giant races.

She ordered her sisters Mirren and Nym to be escorted to their quarters by guards after the Imperator cut off their protests.

Her father King Hekaton is missing. Her mother Queen Neri is dead, killed by small folk under circumstances not yet fully explained, with a gambling token found on the bodies of those responsible. Serissa is currently the reigning queen, operating without her father's authority or resources, and is actively seeking allies to stabilize her position and find Hekaton.

## The Imperator

The Imperator is a storm giant of enormous size — over 30 feet tall — serving as a senior advisor or court official to Queen Serissa. He was present in the throne room arguing with Iymrith/Adimond Kroskas when the party arrived. He was stunned by the party's appearance and by Iymrith's dragon reveal.

After the chaos subsided, the Imperator provided the party with an extended historical account of the Ordning, King Hekaton, and the events leading to the current crisis. He explained that Hekaton had presided over a court including representatives of all giant races, using the power of the throne to keep lesser giants in line. He described the shattering of the Ordning as a profound blow to Hekaton, who had come to believe giants were no longer the rightful masters of the world. He recounted Queen Neri's affection for the small folk, her death at the hands of small folk on the Sword Coast, Hekaton's grief and rage, and Princess Serissa's successful plea for her father to seek the truth before retaliating. He also explained Hekaton's decision to name Serissa heir apparent over her elder sisters.

The Imperator admitted he was deceived by Adimond Kroskas and did not know the creature was a dragon. Queen Serissa publicly humiliated him over this failure, asking pointedly whether it was his job to know such things. He stammered that Adimond's magic confused everyone, to which the party immediately and loudly contradicted him. He had supported allying with Adimond as a stopgap strategy, a decision Serissa overruled him on in hindsight but could not prevent at the time.

He laughed dismissively at Unla Kee's request for 50,000 gold, calling it preposterous. He was also speechless and objecting when Serissa authorized giving the golden suit of scale mail as payment.

He described the lesser giant races with condescension throughout, looking down on the party and barely suppressing laughter at the idea that a three-foot fairy could be a threat to an Ancient Blue Dragon. He referred to non-storm giants in dismissive terms, prompting Orsik to note the arrogance involved.

His current status is that of a chastened but still-present court official, politically weakened by Serissa's public rebuke over the Adimond failure.

## Iymrith

Iymrith, also known as Adimond Kroskas and the Doom of the Desert, is an Ancient Blue Dragon who had infiltrated the storm giant court disguised as a storm giant under the name Adimond Kroskas. She was present in the throne room arguing with Queen Serissa when the party emerged through the Vortex pool.

When Orsik charged and struck her twice with deep sword blows, she abandoned her storm giant disguise and revealed her true dragon form, using her wings to buffet everyone in the room. She unleashed waves of dragon fear, incapacitating all but Orsik and Thistle. Vardis struck her with a fireball-style spell before she could act further. She then shoved Thistle aside, dove into the pool, and fled through the Vortex. Her parting words were: "This is not the end of this."

The Imperator's account revealed that Iymrith has been conducting hybridization experiments for an extremely long time — "since forever" in his words. She is rumored to have created Drakes. Her fairy/dragon hybridization experiments were originally part of a deal made with Tiamat during a war of the dragons. When Tiamat's plans were thwarted, Iymrith approached the storm giant court with a new offer: create a hybrid dragon weapon to help the storm giants retain dominance over other giant races during the crisis caused by the Ordning's collapse. She presented herself as Adimond Kroskas and was accepted, with Mirren and Nym allying with her directly. The court, including the Imperator, was deceived about her true nature.

She also represented herself as one of the leaders of the Cult of the Dragon, as identified by Thistle to Queen Serissa.

Thistle believes that Iymrith abducted Leela and other captives — including people taken when Thistle and Thuul escaped as the cult disintegrated — and is continuing hybridization experiments somewhere. Queen Serissa noted that only King Hekaton knows where Iymrith actually lives.

Her current whereabouts are unknown. She escaped through the pool into open water. She is at large.

## Mirren

Mirren is one of Queen Serissa's two elder sisters, described by the Imperator as tempestuous and prone to emotional outbursts — the reason King Hekaton passed her over for succession in favor of Serissa. She was present in the throne room and charged in alongside Nym after the initial confrontation with Iymrith.

When Serissa asked her sisters whether they had knowingly allied with a dragon, Mirren vigorously shook her head and offered apologetics. She then attempted to accuse the party of trying to assassinate the queen, but the Imperator cut her off before she could make the accusation fully. She protested being dismissed but was escorted to her quarters by guards on the Imperator's order.

The Imperator revealed that Mirren and Nym had allied directly with Adimond Kroskas after Serissa took the throne, motivated by fury at being passed over. What they promised or offered Iymrith in exchange for her magical weapons is unknown. Serissa stated she thought her sisters were willing to live with their father's decision, but their alliance with Adimond proved otherwise.

Her current status is confined to her quarters under guard.

## Nym

Nym is the other of Queen Serissa's two elder sisters, described by the Imperator as cold and unloving — the other reason Hekaton passed her over for the succession. She was present in the throne room and charged in alongside Mirren after the initial confrontation.

Like Mirren, she vigorously shook her head when Serissa asked whether they had knowingly allied with a dragon, and offered apologetics. She was also escorted to her quarters by guards alongside Mirren.

The Imperator identified her as having allied with Adimond Kroskas together with Mirren following Serissa's ascension. The nature and terms of that alliance remain unclear.

Her current status is confined to her quarters under guard.

## King Hekaton

King Hekaton does not appear in this session. He is the missing king of the storm giants, father of Serissa, Mirren, and Nym, and husband of the late Queen Neri. His disappearance is identified by Queen Serissa as the first and most pressing of the three problems facing the court.

The Imperator provided extensive background on him. Hekaton was described as arguably the most powerful of all storm giants, presiding over a court that included representatives of every giant race. He used the throne's power to keep lesser giants in line. In recent years he became convinced the age of giants was past, as evidenced by Annam the Allfather's silence and the constant warring of divine offspring on the outer planes. The shattering of the Ordning confirmed his fears.

When Queen Neri was found dead on a coastal island, killed by small folk, Hekaton was devastated and nearly unleashed vengeance on coastal territories. Princess Serissa persuaded him to seek the truth before retaliating. He then set out to learn how his wife died and subsequently disappeared.

The Imperator noted that Hekaton was not as wise as he once was by the time of the Ordning's collapse. His decision to publicly name Serissa heir apparent years before his disappearance — bypassing Mirren and Nym — is identified as a politically foolish move that created the conditions for his elder daughters' alliance with Iymrith.

Only Hekaton knows where Iymrith actually lives. Finding him is essential both for recovering that information and for stabilizing the storm giant court.

A token found on the bodies of the small folk who were discovered dead alongside Queen Neri has been identified by Unla Kee as a gambling chit from the Golden Goose, a vessel in Yartar operated by Lord Drylund. This is the primary lead on Hekaton's disappearance.

His current location is unknown.

## Queen Neri

Queen Neri does not appear in this session; she is deceased. She was King Hekaton's wife and the mother of Serissa, Mirren, and Nym. The Imperator described her as particularly fond of the small folk, visiting them often before the Ordning was sundered and rising out of the sea to meet them on shore. She continually urged Hekaton to respect the small folk and leave them alone if he could not form alliances with them.

She failed to return from one of her trips to the Sword Coast to meet with humans. Her corpse was found on a small island where she was known to meet humans. It was clear she had been killed by small folk. A token — later identified as a Golden Goose gambling chit from Yartar — was found on the bodies of the small folk found dead alongside her.

Her death triggered Hekaton's grief, his near-declaration of war against coastal territories, and ultimately his disappearance while investigating her fate. Her philosophy of alliance with the small folk is now being taken up by Serissa.

## Lord Drylund

Lord Drylund does not appear in this session but is identified by Unla Kee when examining the token presented by Queen Serissa. Unla Kee recognized it as a gambling chit from the Golden Goose, a boat in Yartar operated by Drylund. The Golden Goose was a high-stakes gambling establishment accessible only to high rollers. Drylund was described as seeking to become the new Waterbarron of Yartar, a position that is elected and currently open following the death of the previous Waterbarron.

The token connects Lord Drylund's operation to the small folk found dead alongside Queen Neri, making him a significant lead in the investigation into King Hekaton's disappearance. Unla Kee is now funded by Queen Serissa to pursue this lead.

His current status and whereabouts are unknown, but he is connected to Yartar.

## Annam the Allfather

Annam the Allfather does not appear in this session and is not directly addressed but is referenced by the Imperator in his historical account. He is the giant god whose silence and absence contributed to King Hekaton's belief that the age of giants was past. The Imperator noted that Annam did not answer prayers and that his divine offspring were constantly waging war against one another on the outer planes, leaving the giant races without divine guidance or support. This spiritual abandonment was a contributing factor to the shattering of the Ordning.
