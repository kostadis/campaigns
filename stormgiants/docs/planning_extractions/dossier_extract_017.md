## Ragnar Redtooth

Ragnar Redtooth, a brown-haired man with a large belly and prominent muttonchops, is found at the Golden Tankard bidding farewell to two militia members and a short half-elf when the party arrives. He greets the party warmly and has Elspeth bring food and ale.

Ragnar explains that in Orsik's 20-day absence, he has tried to organize the town's defenses but has faced resistance and ill fortune at every turn. He suspects foul play and names Gundalin Cartwright as the likely saboteur. He offers 50 gold each for information — clarifying explicitly that he wants evidence, not Gundalin's death.

He describes three incidents of suspected sabotage: tools disappearing daily from the central toolshed, the collapse of the newly completed southern watchtower (which killed a militiaman named Donnal, with a witness reporting a light near the base beforehand), and a cart breaking its second axle in a tenday — wheels and axle having been replaced by Gundalin. He notes that the tower collapse caused the men to believe the scavenged materials from Old Parnast were cursed by the Cult of the Dragon, undermining morale.

Ragnar reveals that the villagers still hold him responsible for involvement with the Cult of the Dragon, despite his claim that he joined to work from the inside and save the town. Thistle is openly skeptical of this explanation. Ragnar notes that Gundalin and his craftsmen are essential to the defense effort and that he needs them working with him, not against him.

He quickly pivots away from Orsik — whom he views as a good soldier but not the right person for this problem — and directs his appeal to Vardis, whom he sees as having the brains and judgment the situation requires.

Ragnar also shares intelligence brought by the young half-elf Tove: monsters are on the move, constantly destroying what they find, and Tove could not locate their main camp. Ragnar states his belief that an attack by Bad Fruul's army is imminent and warns that if the villagers cannot be united, they will flee into the hills and be slaughtered.

Last known location: the Golden Tankard in Parnast.

## Azam

Azam runs after the party as they leave the Golden Tankard to deliver a message he had forgotten. He informs Thistle that Uncle Joon is in the town of Triboar, approximately 400 miles away. He reports that Joon did not join the Cult of the Dragon, but has joined a secretive organization that even the SEER does not know about.

Sensing that Thistle, Orsik, and Vardis may leave Parnast to pursue Joon, Azam becomes desperate and conveys a message from the SEER: the protection of Parnast from Bad Fruul is of paramount importance to the Lord's Alliance, and successfully defending the town would earn the party the admiration and support of a powerful personage. He reassures the group that Joon is not going anywhere and that the Lord's Alliance will keep track of him if he moves.

His intervention is effective — the party chooses to remain in Parnast.

Last known location: outside the Golden Tankard in Parnast.

## Tove

Tove does not appear directly in this session but is referenced by Ragnar Redtooth during his briefing. Ragnar describes Tove as a young half-elf clad in worn furs who brought him military intelligence. Tove reported that there are monsters on the move, that the forces are constantly moving and destroying what they find, and that he was unable to locate their main camp. Tove was one of the three men Ragnar was bidding farewell to when the party arrived at the Golden Tankard.

## Donnal

Donnal does not appear in this session but is referenced as a named casualty. Ragnar Redtooth mentions that the newly completed southern watchtower collapsed the day after it was finished, and that Donnal — a militiaman — was killed in the collapse. His death is part of the suspected sabotage pattern Ragnar is investigating.

## Gundalin Cartwright

Gundalin Cartwright does not appear directly in this session but is the central subject of Ragnar's investigation. Ragnar names him as the primary suspect behind the sabotage of Parnast's defenses: the disappearing tools, the watchtower collapse, and the broken cart axle — the last of which Ragnar specifically notes had its wheels and axle replaced by Gundalin. Ragnar emphasizes that Gundalin and his cronies question his decisions daily and that they blame him for the Cult of the Dragon involvement.

Wallace, Gundalin's son, reacts with disbelief when told his father is suspected, saying, "Right, my father is doing his best to betray the town so that Orcs will take over? Really?"

At the end of the session, Thistle follows a trail of footprints from the trampled bushes in Silas Carras' garden and discovers that the tracks lead directly to the front door of Gundalin Cartwright's house.

Current status: not yet confronted; primary suspect in the sabotage investigation. Last known location: his house in Parnast, where a trail of footprints leads.

## Elspeth

Elspeth works at the Golden Tankard under Ragnar. She brings food — cheese, fresh bread, oil, ale, and mugs — at Ragnar's request when the party arrives. She is seen speaking with Wallace outside the tavern during Ragnar's briefing; Orsik notices and smiles. Wallace kisses Elspeth when the group departs and promises to talk to her later.

Last known location: the Golden Tankard in Parnast.

## Chandra Stol

Chandra Stol, the local cleric of Parnast, arrives at the scene of the afflicted horses and is initially silent, simply staring. She expresses recognition and warmth toward the party, crediting them with bringing her temple and saving the village from raiders. She suggests that the late wife of Silas Carras was a herbalist who may have something to help the poisoned horses. She also quietly appeals to Thistle to resolve the escalating tension between refugees and residents before it becomes a riot.

Last known location: the scene of the horse poisoning in the center of Parnast.

---

## Silas Carras

Silas Carras greets the party at his home, recognizing them as the Defenders of Parnast and joking about "wakeup sauce" for Unla Kee. He clarifies for what he describes as the n-th time that he is not the herbalist — his deceased wife was — and is uncertain whether any antitoxin remains, but invites the party to search the backyard garden.

He explains that his wife enjoyed puzzles and built a system of nested enigmas into the garden layout to hide herbs from those who wouldn't pay. He expects the search to take a very long time but is shocked when Unla Kee quickly produces the antidote.

He then raises a new concern: someone has recently trampled some of his wife's bushes in the garden. This observation prompts Thistle to examine the trampled plants and follow a trail leading out of the garden — ultimately to Gundalin Cartwright's front door.

Last known location: his home and garden in Parnast.

Last known location: the scene of the horse poisoning in the center of Parnast.
