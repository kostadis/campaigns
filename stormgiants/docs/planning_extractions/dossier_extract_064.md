## Thistle

Thistle appeared throughout the Ironslag heist as the primary planner and tactical guide for the group. He presented the heist plan to the party, explaining the two main problems: the large number of Fire Giants and the need for a quiet approach. His plan involved the party riding the bucket carts around the upper level, jumping off near the pressing mill, and descending the elevator shaft while he created a distraction by magically destroying the wheel mechanism to draw the giants away. He pointed out the fire salamander at the end of the tracks as a problem requiring a silence spell, which Unla Kee did not have.

Thistle scouted the wheel room and observed ten shield dwarf prisoners in iron manacles working under the supervision of two spiked-armor ogres. He destroyed the wheel prematurely, before the party had cleared the bucket chain, stranding the group mid-route. He flew to assist his stranded allies and offered an apologetic but weak explanation about the confusion between multiple plans.

In the Duke's bedchamber, Thistle was close to the iron chest when Unla Kee cast knock and triggered a blast of incendiary fire, injuring him. He discovered the conch inside the chest and began attempting to load additional treasures into the bag of holding, including a 750-pound hairbrush, over Orsik's objections. He called for a vote on staying to collect more treasure before ultimately relenting when the group departed.

During the post-heist discussion about Bob Saget and Silverymoon, Thistle reminded Orsik of the original plan to frame House Margaster and noted that Bob would find a way to pin the blame on the party unless they explicitly fought him. He then proposed the cover story that Bob Saget had been killed by a demon and that the party had come to Silverymoon to deal with the demon threat, leveraging their status as emissaries of Everlund to avoid magical interrogation.

Thistle suggested that the group could return to Ironslag later to kill the giants, or use Bob Saget's zombie army or the cloud castle itself as weapons against them.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee participated in the heist planning session, repeatedly suggesting spells — fireball, cloudkill, dominate person — that Thistle dismissed as ineffective or impractical against the assembled Fire Giants. He expressed frustration at the limited utility of his spell repertoire in this situation and wondered aloud whether there was any point in knowing the spells.

During the encounter with the fire salamander, Unla Kee launched a shimmering psychic lance from his mind that stunned the creature, enabling the group to kill it quietly. He cast toll the dead during the fight, though the creature avoided the effect. He directed the dwarven prisoners to continue filling the carts and suggested filling one cart with salamander parts.

In the dining hall, Unla Kee proposed casting dominate person on the goblins, which Orsik dismissed by pointing out it would only control one. He walked through the dining hall acting as though he were in charge, and the goblins ignored the party.

In the Duke's bedchamber, Unla Kee cast knock on the iron chest, triggering a blast of incendiary fire that killed him. He was subsequently revived or otherwise present for the departure, as he participated in the post-heist debate. During that debate he stated he wanted the riskiest plan and expressed that letting chaos reign in Silverymoon sounded appealing, before pivoting to support killing Bob Saget. He reached for his rock of wonder before Orsik cut him off. He also mentioned Hsing of the Lord's Alliance as a potential ally.

## Vardis

Vardis appeared throughout the heist. He expressed discomfort with heights when Orsik proposed jumping from bucket to bucket. He was sprawled on the ground after the chest explosion, having tripped trying to escape the incendiary blast. He participated in the discussion about how to handle the goblins in the dining hall, suggesting intimidation or bluffing as options, and observed that the goblins likely saw many strange people and therefore ignored the party.

On entering the Duke's bedchamber, Vardis commented "No kink shaming" upon observing the chains, manacles, and sword-blade bed frame. He declined Thistle's offer to take additional treasure from the basalt table, saying nothing piqued his interest.

Vardis received a ring of jumping from the chest, which Orsik suggested given Vardis's self-described clumsiness.

During the post-heist debate, Vardis sang "Ding dong, the Duchess is dead" and urged action against Sansuri. When the group debated Bob Saget and Silverymoon, Vardis — described as a fallen cleric of Corellon banned from every temple of his god — argued for walking away, noting there was no guarantee the party would be blamed for the chaos, that Silverymoon might not survive regardless, and that the party's track record of successful scheming was weaker than Orsik believed. He consistently favored disengagement as the safer option.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta had been waiting anxiously in the control tower for the party's return during the Ironslag heist. During their absence she had made her husband's life miserable, complaining that she should not have to rely on small people to obtain her revenge. When the party returned, she immediately demanded to know whether the Duchess was dead, growing agitated when Orsik only mentioned the conch. She accepted Orsik's confirmation of the Duchess's death and imagined a suitably gruesome end, becoming momentarily happy. Vardis's singing about the Duchess's death pleased her further.

Escarlotta's last known location is the control tower of the cloud castle. Her relationship with her husband is strained due to her frustration during the waiting period. Her primary motivation was revenge against the Duchess of Ironslag.

## Duke Zalto

Duke Zalto does not appear directly as an active participant but is referenced during the heist. Thistle heard Zalto rushing up the stairs toward the wheel room after the wheel was destroyed, which prompted Thistle to immediately fly to rejoin the party. The iron chest in his bedchamber was trapped with an incendiary fire blast. The bedchamber contained the adamantine crown, thousands of gold pieces, the conch, and several other magic items, as well as furnishings including chains and manacles above the bed, a black dragon scale shield, and two fire-scorched wardrobes.
