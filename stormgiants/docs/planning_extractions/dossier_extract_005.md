## Kraddock

Kraddock is a storm giant who appears at the summit shrine in the Greypeak mountains after the party defeats the giant owls and enters the ancient holy site. He speaks through an open-air pipe with a booming voice. Thistle privately notes to Orsik that he believes Kraddock could kill them all without breaking a sweat.

Kraddock reveals he was told the shrine had been desecrated several days prior and traveled there to assess the damage. He is a shaman whose role is to maintain and repair the sacred site — he lists his tasks as fixing tattered drapes, patching the roof, restocking the braziers with charcoal, restoring the frescoes, polishing the altars, and removing the dead cultists.

He confronts Unla Kee over the stolen gold plates, staring at the halfling while the halfling attempts several increasingly transparent lies. Kraddock does not threaten violence but states he would have invoked a curse of the All-Father rather than kill the party. When Unla Kee returns the plates, Kraddock polishes them carefully.

Kraddock delivers significant intelligence about the current state of the world. He explains that Tiamat failed in her plots to conquer the world, but her failure inspired the giants, who are now at war with the smaller races. He describes the ancient giant kingdom of Ostoria as having once stretched from the Spine of the World to the great desert in the east, the Sword Coast to the west, and the kingdom of Amn to the south, and states the giants now seek to reclaim this territory. He warns the party to steer clear of locations where giants live or were rumored to live, acknowledging that this encompasses most of the North where the party resides.

Kraddock mentions King Hekaton and his missing wife, noting the king had spoken of more inclusive ways of speaking about smaller peoples, and that his wife had much to say on the matter. He expresses personal sadness that the age of tolerance may be ending alongside the age of dragons. He disagrees with the All-Father's decisions but serves him nonetheless.

When pressed by Vardis about what he will personally do to stop the coming devastation, Kraddock states he can only pray to the All-Father and offers the party his thoughts and prayers. He acknowledges this is essentially nothing of practical value but gives the party several potions as they depart, framing this as "perhaps more than just thoughts and prayers."

He declines Unla Kee's invitation to accompany the party. As they leave, he sighs and expresses a private hope that the All-Father will come to understand that the war will bring glory to no one.

Kraddock's last known whereabouts are the summit shrine in the Greypeak mountains, where he remains to restore the desecrated holy site.

## Aunt Nonnie

Aunt Nonnie does not appear in the summit events but is referenced as the destination the party races toward on their return journey, with the group described as nearly tasting her food. She appears in person when the group arrives at her establishment in Llorkh on the 8th day of the 1st tenday of Taraksh.

She is described as friendly and welcoming as always, greeting the party and providing them rooms and vegetarian entrees. She is referred to as "Aunt Nonnie" as she prefers to be called.

She reports to the party that there have been many recent sightings of kobolds in the area, and that the town guards have been unwilling to act, preferring to remain in their tower. When Orsik mumbles that he would do the same, she silences him by referencing something from his past — "because of that THING you did" — suggesting she has prior knowledge of Orsik's history and some degree of authority or familiarity over him.

Vardis speculates, apparently in jest, that her friendliness may indicate she is affiliated with an evil cult.

Her last known whereabouts are her establishment in Llorkh.
