## Escarlotta

Escarlotta is the sentient control center of Blagothkus' Cloud Castle. She agreed to help Thistle find his love Leala, swayed by his charm and sob story, expressing this with the declaration "AT LEAST SOMEONE LOVES SOMEONE IN THIS SHIP!" as the castle departed from the collapsing Eye of the All-Father. She repeatedly asked for more precise navigation information during the three-day journey, frustrated by the party's vague directions derived from Oracle's information. When the party spotted Ironslag and the Yakfolk village, she deferred to Orsik's enthusiasm for a "show of force" approach over Thistle's suggestion of subtlety, gliding the castle over the village. She asked where to stop as they approached. Last known status: piloting the cloud castle, which remains stationed above the Yakfolk village at Mount Hamarhaast.

## Kartha-Kaya

Kartha-Kaya is the chief of the Yakfolk village atop Mount Hamarhaast. She insisted the party come to eat in her yurt, framing refusal as beyond rude. Her hut is a 60-foot-diameter structure built on high ground in the middle of the village, featuring a central fire pit, cushions, foodstuffs, clay vessels, and an ornately engraved bronze gong. She provided a feast of fresh vegetables, cooked meats, and bread — which proved to be genuinely uncontaminated, disarming the party's suspicions. After the meal, she insisted on a cleansing ritual, dismissing Thistle and Vardis's objections and suggesting ill omens would follow if the ritual was skipped. She made snide remarks about dwarves when Orsik refused to participate. During the ritual she held Thistle's left hand and Vardis's right, leading the humming that masked an attempted psychic possession of the party. The failed possession triggered open combat. Current status: engaged in melee with the party.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session but is referenced by Thistle. He is the source of Thistle's knowledge about Ironslag and Mount Hamarhaast, which the dwarves of Silvermarsh call "hammer of ashes." Uncle Joon described the great dungeon forge, including the pair of 50-foot-tall adamantine doors carved into the base of a 500-foot-high mountainside cliff, noting that they are airtight valves sealed by ancient magic. His relationship to Thistle appears to be familial, as Thistle recalls this information as knowledge passed down from him.
