## Laela

Laela does not appear in this chunk. Thistle references her as potentially being a prisoner near Ironslag. He mutters to himself that she is likely hoping he will come to save her from whatever torment Adminond Kroskas, Ilymrith, or some other threat has inflicted upon her, but that he must prioritize saving Silverymoon. He believes she would not forgive him if he let the city burn to rescue her instead.

## Darlathara (Darathra Shendrel / Darthara Shendrel / Darthala)

Darathra Shendrel, Lord Protector, is approached by the 3HPP and Orsik's father as part of the effort to assemble a discreet strike team against Bob Saget. She is initially unhappy to see them, having previously vouched for them to Krowen Valharrow and suffered professional damage as a result. She recounts Krowen's condemnation: that the party betrayed the Harper circles, got agents killed, and destroyed expensive magical assets. She examines the elder dwarf and is briefly convinced he is Orsik before he claims to be Orsic, Orsik's father, seeking to redeem the family name. She challenges the elder dwarf's parental character when he seems delighted to hear of his son's misdeeds. She also singles out Vardis, expressing relief that he has separated from Orsik, noting that his association with Orsik as a Corellon cleric had harmed believers, and remarking that Orsik always seemed unnaturally perceptive — "something off about his intelligence." Despite her grievances, she acknowledges that the 3HPP's information helped prevent Triboar from defecting to the Lord's Alliance via the Cindrell affair, and agrees to join the mission, declaring all debts cleared afterward. During the infiltration of the theater, she questions Thistle about the portal deception and watches Unla Kee dispel the alarm wards. She enters the theater and observes the staged horror: zombie audience members dressed in repeating costumes mimicking the 3HPP's appearance, including a half-orc with a broken neck behind the concession stand and a Tabaxi in an Orsik costume with a partially severed neck. Her last known status is inside the theater at the start of the assault.

## Lord Zymorven

Lord Zymorven is alerted to the approaching castle by his scrying magic and meets the party in full armor, ready to defend his homestead. He recognizes Vardis, Unla Kee, and Thistle but is puzzled by the unfamiliar dwarf until Orsic presents his giant slayer sword and identifies himself as Orsik's father. Upon hearing the explanation about Bob Saget and the zombie plague threatening Silverymoon, he immediately demands they warn the city's leaders, but accepts the reasoning that a quiet strike team is necessary to prevent Bob from releasing the horde prematurely. He comments favorably on how different Orsic seems from his son. He is warned by Orsik to stay in the tower, avoid shadows, and not go out at night — and learns there is a vampire problem. He is visibly alarmed by the successive revelations of vampires and a Dracolich in proximity to his estate and insists the castle be repositioned away from Silverymoon to prevent vampiric infestation. During planning discussions, he argues against killing Kaelen, preferring interrogation, and is overruled in part by Urgala's reminder of Kaelen's original mass-murder plot. He reassures Orsik that he knows people in Silverymoon and will ensure the party receives credit for their heroic sacrifice, offering to handle all public messaging. His last known location is in the field with the strike team as they approach the theater.

## Urgala Meltimer

Urgala Meltimer is recruited at what appears to be an inn or establishment she runs, encountered on the 2nd day of the 2nd tenday of Eleint. She greets Lord Zymorven warmly, then notices Orsik's father and the 3HPP and becomes worried. After hearing the explanation of the threat to Silverymoon, she agrees to join, framing it as "one last adventure" and referencing saving Silverymoon as something of a shared tradition. During planning debates in the castle, she intervenes to cut off the argument about Kaelen's fate by reminding everyone that Kaelen's original scheme was the mass murder of all of Triboar, which the 3HPP thwarted, and stating flatly that in her view they should have killed him on the spot. Her last known status is with the strike team preparing to assault the theater.

## Krowen Valharrow

Krowen Valharrow does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. Darathra recounts his direct condemnation of the 3HPP: that they betrayed the Harper circles, caused the deaths of several agents, and destroyed powerful and expensive magical assets. His tone, as Darathra recalls it, was one of cold accountability. Orsik briefly considers approaching the Harpers by claiming to redeem his family name from his son's actions, then dismisses it, saying he does not think Krowen would accept that framing. Krowen's current whereabouts and status are not specified.

## Bob Saget

Bob Saget is the primary antagonist of this session. He is described as a demon or supernaturally powered doppelganger who killed and replaced the real Bob Saget — a famous actor who had been performing a persona created by Orsik's son's play. The real Bob was killed and replaced by this creature, which now operates out of Silverymoon's royal theater. His scheme involves a magical portal at the theater's entrance that intercepts theatergoers, while inside the actual theater he manufactures zombies with the assistance of a Red Wizard allied with the Drow. The theater interior contains a staged zombie audience dressed in costumes mimicking the 3HPP. The party believes Bob does not yet know they are coming, giving them the advantage of surprise — an advantage that would be lost if Kaelen were killed, as Kaelen may be powering or linked to the demon. Bob's last known location is inside the royal theater in Silverymoon.

## Kaelen

Kaelen is a playwright referenced throughout the session as the person whose summoning ritual originally brought Bob Saget into the world. His original plan, as Urgala reminds the group, was the mass murder of the entire town of Triboar, which the 3HPP thwarted. His ritual was only partly successful — instead of summoning a powerful destructive force, he summoned Bob Saget. Unla Kee reflects that the 3HPP initially treated Bob as an exploitable asset for profit, while Unla Kee himself recognized Bob as evil incarnate. At the time of this session, Kaelen is observed by Thistle sitting in his house, which is covered in dust; the door has not been opened in some time and something appears very wrong with him. The party debates whether to kill him but decides against it, reasoning that he may be powering the demon and that killing him would alert Bob and eliminate their element of surprise. Kaelen's current status is alive and confined to his house in Silverymoon, apparently in a deteriorated or entranced condition.

## Laeral Silverhand

Laeral Silverhand does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in Unla Kee's strategic musing. He considers her a potential "patsy" for the chaos a Dracolich attack on Silverymoon could cause: as First Lord of Waterdeep and leader of the coalition that defeated Severin and Sandesyl, she would be a plausible target for Cult of the Dragon blame narratives. This is part of Unla Kee's internal argument for the "let it burn" plan that Orsik rejected.
