## Lord Khaspere Drylund

Lord Khaspere Drylund does not appear alive in this session; his body is present as a corpse throughout the events. He was killed prior to this chapter, and the party discovers during this session that the darkest secret of the War of the Giants was tied to his operations: King Hekaton is alive, and Orsik knows where he is as a result of intelligence gathered around Drylund's death.

The party debates raising him from the dead. Orsik asks Vardis if they can cast raise dead on the body, but Vardis examines the corpse and declares it impossible — "Like all Politicians, that's impossible. His brain is gone." Thistle notes that True Resurrection would work but would cost 50,000 gold pieces, making it effectively off the table.

Thistle searches the body and recovers a ring with keys, 120 gold pieces, and three platinum pieces. Orsik demands the return of his "Champagne Du Stomp." The party debates what to do with the body: Thistle considers the problem, Thuul picks it up in his jaws and drops it in a room, Thistle covers it and locks the door. Orsik's final word on the matter: "Take the gold, leave the body."

Lady Atalia later references Drylund directly, warning that "Drylund's going to come back," suggesting the Kraken Society has means to resurrect or replace him. Orsik expresses doubt that he is coming back given the state of the body.

Thistle later searches Drylund's room aboard the Grand Dame and finds no incriminating paperwork or correspondence, which surprises him given that any organization requires communication infrastructure. He does recover an ornately decorated weapon with an octopus pommel among the room's treasure.

Drylund's death has created a power vacuum in Yartar that the party explicitly discusses filling, and his association with the Kraken Society and the Aboleth Oosith is now confirmed.

## Oosith

Oosith is revealed in this session to be an aboleth, having previously concealed his true nature. He reveals himself during the combat in the circular sewer chamber and immediately attempts to dominate party members.

His first action upon revealing himself is to dominate Unla Kee. He then attempts an attack on Thistle, which fails when Vardis uses his powers as a light cleric to blind the creature. He commands the dominated Unla Kee to "Destroy them!" Unla Kee responds by using the wand of wonder and becoming invisible rather than attacking the party. Oosith shrieks into everyone's minds, "What the hell did you just do?!" — breaking the dramatic tension of domination through Unla Kee's chaotic action.

When the tide of battle turns decisively against his forces — the Kuo-Toans are routed or dead, the Merrow's domination is broken by damage, and the water level is falling — Oosith chooses to flee rather than press the fight.

His final words before fleeing are: "The Kraken will have its revenge!" This confirms his direct association with the Kraken Society.

Lady Atalia, when confronted by the party afterward, is furious that Oosith escaped. She explicitly describes him to the party as "a sorcerer named Oosith" when the party relays this name to her protective rogues. His survival is treated as a significant threat — Lady Atalia fears the Kraken's retaliation precisely because Oosith is still alive and will report the disruption of their plans.

Last known status: fled the sewer chamber, alive, still connected to the Kraken Society.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is an active participant in the sewer combat. He uses telekinesis to open one of the drainage valves early in the fight, slowing the water flow that the Merrow are using to flood the chamber.

He is then targeted and successfully dominated by the aboleth Oosith. Under domination, Oosith commands him to "Destroy them!" Unla Kee considers his options — briefly entertaining fireball and dominate person — then pulls out his wand of wonder, his "rock," and casts from it. The result is that he turns invisible. The Aboleth shrieks in everyone's minds, "What the hell did you just do?!" Orsik and Vardis comment in unison that this is characteristic Unla Kee behavior. It is unclear from the text whether the domination is broken by this act or whether Unla Kee simply fulfilled the command in his own chaotic way.

After combat, Unla Kee participates in the group's planning discussions, though largely passively. When the casino plan crystallizes, he inserts one firm condition: "I am only going along with this if I get naming rights. I want this place to be called *Kee and Wing Casino*." Pow accepts this, shaking hands with the halfling. The casino is named accordingly.

## Thule

Thule is present in the sewer combat. During the early stages of the fight, he rushes out the door past a Kuo-Toan that was trying to hold the door shut, creating an opportunity. The Kuo-Toan attempts to strike him as he passes and misses. Thule retaliates with his breath weapon. Later, Thuul (same character, variant spelling used in the text) picks up Lord Drylund's body in his jaws at Thistle's direction and drops it in a side room so the body can be concealed and the door locked.

## Vardis

Vardis is active throughout the sewer combat and subsequent planning. In combat, he uses his mace of terror to create a field of powerful energy that affects three of the four Kuo-Toans in the room, causing them to jump into the water and flee to the far edges of the chamber. The fourth Kuo-Toan Whip is unaffected. When Oosith attempts to attack Thistle, Vardis uses his powers as a light cleric to blind the aboleth, causing the attack to fail — a pivotal moment in the combat. He also opens a drainage valve to lower the water level.

When Orsik asks if they can raise Lord Drylund from the dead, Vardis initially responds "I am a light cleric, not a necromancer," then remembers he can cast raise dead. He examines the body and declares it impossible because Drylund's brain is gone.

In the planning discussions, Vardis supports the flying casino concept and pushes back on Thistle's concerns about repeat business by comparing it to a cruise ship: "It's like a cruise ship. When we land, they can come on and off. We can even make it so they can visit any place before we go and pillage and mess it up." He also advocates for simply lying to Lady Atalia about the aboleth's fate, saying "We'll lie?" when Orsik resists the idea.

## Lady Atalia

Lady Atalia is first mentioned as the party's contact who had tasked them with killing the aboleth Oosith. She is sitting at a fashionable cafe when the party finds her, and she smiles upon seeing the 3HPP — until she learns that they did not kill the aboleth. Her smile evaporates and she becomes furious, standing up, straightening her clothes, and marching out of the cafe.

When the party attempts to catch up, a small group of female rogues stops them, announcing that Lady Atalia is under their protection. The party explains they eliminated a threat to her in the form of "a sorcerer named Oosith," and the rogues stand down and follow them instead.

Outside her house, Lady Atalia is standing and visibly furious. When Thistle tries to address her, she reverts to a very plebian dock accent — a notable class/social tell — and snarls at him: "You were supposed to kill him. I don't want anything to do with you. We're done." Thistle pushes back by saying she won't become the new Waterbarron with that attitude, which prompts her to reveal more: "The Kraken doesn't like it when anyone disrupts its plans. Drylund's going to come back, and he's going to be…"

When Orsik expresses doubt that Drylund is coming back, she pivots unexpectedly and reveals that she had been using the party's belief that she could read minds — specifically noting it is "amazing how easy it is for people to read if they think you can read their minds" — implying she had been performing a confidence trick on the party regarding telepathic or mind-reading abilities, and that she had information about potions she would have needed to drink. She then closes the door.

Her protective rogues — referred to as "the Hands" — immediately afterward approach Orsik and offer to take an "equitable share" of the Grand Dame, plus an operating fee and a security fee, totaling 95% of turnover plus an annual security fee. The 3HPP nods politely and leaves.

Lady Atalia's motivations and true allegiances remain somewhat opaque: she is clearly connected to Yartar's criminal/political underworld, fears the Kraken Society's retaliation, and is angry the aboleth survived — but her relationship to the Kraken Society itself is ambiguous. She may have been playing multiple sides.

## Pow

Pow is the COO of the Grand Dame and is identified by the party as a potential partner in repurposing the vessel. He is met at the docks once the party arrives at the ship.

Orsik explains the flying casino plan to him. Pow responds with enthusiasm, speaking with the accent of a citizen of Shou: "There is a tremendous opportunity here that I had not considered. We land in a city, open up the rooms for gambling, and then fly to a different location for more gambling. The nature of our flying ship makes it possible for us to avoid any taxation and protection fees. We also have a lot more space and rooms."

He accepts Unla Kee's condition of naming rights — the casino will be called the *Kee and Wing Casino* — sealing the deal with a handshake with the halfling.

Pow is presented as a pragmatic and entrepreneurially minded figure who recognizes the value of the proposal immediately and engages with it constructively. His prior role as COO of the Grand Dame (a boat docked in Yartar) positions him as someone with existing operational knowledge of running a gambling operation, now with a dramatically expanded scope.
