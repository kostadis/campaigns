## Titania

Titania does not appear directly in this chunk but is referenced and discussed throughout. She is identified as the Goddess of the Fey and ruler of the Fey Court (specifically the Seelie Court). The Sprite messenger reveals that Titania had previously imposed a punishment on two Dryads named Helia and Halisa, and that the 3HPP's actions in freeing them — though in service of a personal and selfish goal — earned her approval specifically because trickery was employed. Titania is described by Thistle as "vengeful, spiteful, cruel, and heartless" and explicitly not the forgiving type. Rather than issuing a severe punishment for the party's interference in her judgment, Titania instead assigned them a task: investigate and deal with a Fomorian of the Unseelie Court that has taken refuge in the upper lands. The Sprite notes that others argued for more drastic punishments but that Titania exercised restraint. She was also pleased by how Thistle dealt with his Uncle Joon, which factored into her leniency. Additionally, Titania used Orsik-Thornacious' involvement in the affair as further justification to keep him out of the Feywild entirely. Her current status is off-screen; she acts entirely through her Sprite emissary.

## The Sprite (Seelie Court Emissary)

The Sprite appears in the sky before Thistle, described as wearing pink pants and an orange camisole with a high-pitched squeaky voice. It announces itself as appearing at the request of the Seelie Court. Speaking first in Common, it relays Titania's acknowledgment of the party's interference with the punishment of Helia and Halisa, framing Titania's pleasure as stemming from the use of trickery and the selfish motivation behind the act. It then switches to Fey to speak candidly to Thistle alone, warning that it will not be fooled as the Dryads were, and revealing Titania's intent to use Thornacious' involvement as a reason to bar him from the Feywild. Returning to Common, it delivers Titania's assigned task — dealing with a Fomorian of the Unseelie Court — and notes that others pushed for harsher punishment. The Sprite also references Titania's appreciation for how Thistle handled his Uncle Joon. It then opens a portal into the Feywild, gleefully sending the party through. Orsik briefly considers killing the Sprite without anyone noticing; Thistle talks him down. The Sprite disappears after the party enters the portal.

## Drauchanar (Chan / the Knife)

Drauchanar appears in the Feywild shortly after the party arrives through the portal. He identifies himself as an elven hunter who goes by Chan or "the Knife," and clarifies that he is no ordinary hunter — he hunts rare game, specifically citing the Fomorian as his quarry. He quickly offers to guide the party. Upon learning that Orsik-Thornacious (a Fey Prince) is among the group, he raises a complication: if the Prince accompanies them, Drauchanar will not be permitted to keep the Fomorian's head as a prize. Thistle resolves this by suggesting Orsik disguise himself as a dwarf, which Drauchanar accepts with great enthusiasm, framing it as a way for the Prince to guide the hunt while preserving social forms and allowing the Knife to keep his trophy. Drauchanar leads the party into the forest at night and sings an old elvish song around camp — the song concerns the love of Corellon and Araushnee before the sundering and visibly affects Vardis despite his cynicism. During the night, Drauchanar is ambushed and dragged off by a giant-like creature and a four-legged beast. His current status is unknown — he was taken, not confirmed dead — and his disappearance is what leads the party to the Fomorian's cave.

## Tlega

Tlega is a larger goblin who emerges from behind the waterfall tunnel after a smaller goblin (Goblin #1) reports the party's presence. Tlega speaks with a very nasal, squeaky voice and initiates contact with the 3HPP. Upon learning they intend to kill the Fomorian Maegurn, Tlega is immediately receptive, explaining that the traditional Bully/Bullied relationship between goblins and the Fomorian has broken down because Maegurn has become outright abusive rather than merely a bully. Tlega declares "it's time for him to go" and guides the party deeper into the caves, showing genuine enthusiasm for the alliance. Tlega is touched by Thistle's flawless pronunciation of the goblin's name. When Orsik suggests enlisting the goblins' help in the attack, Tlega is delighted and calls for all the goblins to assemble — at which point the rival goblin boss Mirk appears and challenges the decision. Tlega and Mirk argue, and Tlega becomes deflated as the dispute stalls momentum. Ultimately, after Unla Kee uses Jim's Glowing Coin, both Tlega and Mirk agree to proceed with dispatching Maegurn.

## Mirk

Mirk is a goblin boss who appears in opposition to Tlega's decision to ally with the 3HPP. Mirk is visibly unhappy with Tlega's choice and argues on ideological grounds: the Bully/Bullied relationship is the core of goblin society, and the boundary that has been crossed by Maegurn is, in Mirk's view, just an imaginary line. Mirk's objection deflates Tlega and the other goblins. Orsik considers using intimidation on Mirk, but Unla Kee intervenes with Jim's Glowing Coin, which distracts both Mirk and Tlega long enough for them to agree — apparently sincerely, if briefly — that dispatching Maegurn is the right next step for the tribe.

## Maegurn

Maegurn does not appear directly in this chunk but is the primary target of the party's Feywild mission and is discussed by multiple characters. Maegurn is a Fomorian of the Unseelie Court who has taken refuge in the upper lands (the material plane or a border region). Titania, through her Sprite emissary, assigns the 3HPP to investigate and deal with Maegurn. Within the Feywild cave complex, Tlega explains that Maegurn has broken the traditional Bully/Bullied dynamic with the goblins by becoming genuinely abusive rather than just a bully — prompting even goblin factions to want the Fomorian gone. A Cyclops is observed standing guard at the cave entrance, suggesting Maegurn commands at least some followers. Maegurn's precise current location is the interior of the cave complex. The party has not yet confronted Maegurn directly as of the end of this chapter.
