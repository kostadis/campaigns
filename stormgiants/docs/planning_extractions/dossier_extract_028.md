## Ghelryn Foehammer

Ghelryn Foehammer, the smithy (dwarf), appeared at the Troll Tavern during the group's morning debate about where to travel next. He coughed to get Vardis's attention, then presented a sealed letter of mark. He explained that the party had killed a fire giant at great personal danger, and that while it would have been tactically safer to defeat the giant's allies who were attacking the town rather than engaging the giant directly, their refusal to back down from a hated enemy was an act of bravery worthy of recognition. He instructed the party to take the letter to King Morinn or Queen Tithmel at Citadel Felbar, where they would be rewarded for their heroism.

## Imber Chen

Imber Chen is a lavender-haired gnome merchant with violet eyes and a clear Calishmanian accent who operates a stall at the Fishyard in Yartar. His clothes match his violet eyes. He has plied the rivers for many years and trades in luxurious fabrics dyed in rich hues.

When the party approached him seeking information about Silverymoon, he was startled to see a fairy (Unla Kee) and made an inadvertently dismissive comment about fairies reproducing asexually in egg creches, citing a great sage's work — which led to a tense exchange with Orsik about heteronormative assumptions. He was drawn into the party's play promotion when Orsik leveraged the moment, and he expressed genuine enthusiasm for what he understood to be a progressive theatrical production, noting it was a two-day journey from Yartar and asking about accommodations.

On Silverymoon, Imber provided the following intelligence: it is a spectacularly beautiful and influential city, a powerful Lord's Alliance member, where the Yartar Waterbarron has allied herself to maintain power. He characterized the Harpers as promoting anarchist chaos and the Zhentarim as exploiters. He described Silverymoon as a peaceful realm of many races living together — but when pressed, clarified he meant humans, dwarves, gnomes, elves, halflings, and half-elves — pointedly excluding half-orcs and declining to use Orsik's more neutral framing.

Imber also introduced the party to the existence of Cap'n Squint, a halfling river captain he described as a former friend from whom he had drifted apart. He disclosed that Squint would be gambling that evening on the Grand Dame, Lord Khaspere Drylund's luxury boat, and departing the next morning.

When the party attempted to leave without purchasing anything, Imber stopped them and negotiated a commercial arrangement: a half gold piece for 43 days of banner advertising at his stall. He rejected Orsik's proposed affiliate coupon-code commission model due to insufficient data and lack of a tracking mechanism. He also offered to make an introduction to a member of the Hand of Yartar who could secure the party a stall, though Thistle's suggestion that Imber himself could host the banner made the introduction unnecessary. The deal was concluded with a handshake.

## Cap'n Squint

Cap'n Squint does not appear in person during this session but is introduced through Imber Chen's description. He is a small halfling who wears a tricorne hat, barks orders at men twice his size, and stomps the planks as if he were ten feet tall. He previously worked on Imber's boat before making his fortune and acquiring his own barge. Imber described him as a former friend from whom he had simply drifted apart.

His current status: he was spending the evening gambling aboard the Grand Dame, Lord Khaspere Drylund's luxury vessel, and was scheduled to depart the following morning on a return trip — presumably north on the River Surbin toward Silverymoon. The party intended to seek passage with him. He departed with the group the following morning, with Thistle arriving just in time to board.

## Lord Khaspere Drylund

Lord Khaspere Drylund does not appear in person but is referenced by Imber Chen during his introduction of Cap'n Squint. He is the owner of the Grand Dame, described as the most famous and luxurious boat in Yartar. Imber disclosed that Drylund has political ambitions — specifically, he wants to become the new Waterbarron when the current one dies, and has been actively working the voters toward that end. The Grand Dame serves as a venue for gambling, which Drylund apparently uses as part of his social and political networking.

## Nestra Ruthiol

Nestra Ruthiol does not appear in person but is referenced in the rumors and context provided about Yartar. She is the current Waterbarron of Yartar, a position held for life. She has aligned herself with the Lord's Alliance to maintain her power. The city's governance is described as plagued by vicious infighting and subterfuge, with factions constantly undermining her position's influence, forcing her to rely on outside Lord's Alliance connections to implement meaningful change. Her rule is characterized as dictatorial by Yartar's populace, who are noted to intensely dislike dictatorial and hereditary governments — a sentiment Thistle observed while researching the local audience for the play.
