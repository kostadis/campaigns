## Zephyros

Zephyros is the cloud giant whose flying castle is transporting the party to Triboar. He appears throughout the journey, shouting down commentary and observations from his castle above. He points out landmarks including the High Forest (describing its enormous animals), the Star Mounts to the northeast (noting the powerful winds that prevent approach), the Iceshield lands, and the Dessarin Hills, where he recalls fondly spending time with dwarves who mined there, though he is uncertain if they remain.

When the last cultists fly off after an earlier encounter, Zephyros asks the party whether they still think their decision was the right one, suggesting some residual uncertainty about the group's allegiances or choices. He takes pity on the party when their rations run low and sends food down to them.

Zephyros is not present for the confrontation with Clarion and Diana's strike team on the castle platform, but sounds of combat — swords clashing, furniture breaking, spells being cast — emerge from his room during the encounter, indicating he was fighting the dwarven infiltrators who had already breached the interior. The confrontation ends when Diana's team stands down after learning the party works for the SEER.

His castle lands near Triboar at the conclusion of the journey. His current status following the combat in his quarters is not explicitly confirmed.

## Clarion

Clarion is a silver dragon operating under the command of Diana as part of a Lord's Alliance strike team. Clarion arrives at Zephyros's cloud castle carrying several shield dwarves via a harness, landing on the castle platform before the party. The dragon is startled to find non-giants aboard and initially threatens to fight and eat the party, declaring them enemies. After Thistle clarifies the party are merely passengers, Clarion shifts tone and informs them to stand aside while the strike team destroys the orb that powers the castle's flight, citing intelligence that a cloud giant has been dropping rocks on northern settlements.

Clarion corrects any assumption that the dragon is in command, noting it is a "common misconception" when Vardis implies otherwise. After the party invokes the SEER, Clarion pauses and defers to Diana, saying the commander will want to speak with them. When Clarion examines Orsik's map, the dragon recalls seeing something similar, initially saying northwest before correcting to northeast. Clarion had not personally heard of Adimond Kroskas, noting limited involvement in efforts against the cult.

Clarion and Diana's team depart after Diana concludes they targeted the wrong castle.

---

## Diana

Diana is a female shield dwarf and the commanding officer of the Lord's Alliance strike team sent to destroy Zephyros's orb. She removes her helm to identify herself after Clarion relays that the party claims to work for the SEER. Diana is practical and mission-focused, immediately acknowledging upon hearing the SEER connection that the team has interfered with the wrong castle. She accepts Orsik's testimony that the castle has not been dropping rocks and that Zephyros has not made threatening speeches about cloud giant dominion, and uses these as her verification that Zephyros may be one of the good ones.

When told about the cultists who earlier visited the castle seeking Zephyros as an ally, Diana laughs and says the party should have led with that information. She confirms her team fought the Cult of the Dragon to a standstill when Thistle asks about Adimond Kroskas, but has no personal knowledge of Kroskas by name. Diana departs with her team after concluding the mission was a failure.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon is Thistle's fairy uncle, found drunk and covered in bird droppings at the Talking Troll tavern in Triboar. According to a member of the local militia, Uncle Joon initially arrived in Triboar in good spirits, drinking at the upscale Everwyvern House, but has progressively declined and now drinks at the Talking Troll, a local dive. He has run up a tab of 15 gold, with Kaelen adding surcharges for taxes, late charges, and emotional labor, bringing the total to 30 gold.

Thistle wakes Uncle Joon by dumping a bucket of water on him. Joon immediately attempts to deflect blame, charm Orsik into splitting a fabricated tab arrangement, and get Thistle to buy him a drink. He is dismissive of Kaelen's acting ambitions.

When Thistle says the name Adimond Kroskas, Joon visibly reacts before composing himself. After Orsik stares him down, Joon reveals the following: Adimond Kroskas was an alchemist who discovered how to make drakes from dragon eggs, and his next plan was to combine faeries and dragon eggs to create a more powerful and deadly drake. Joon claims he was attempting to make a deal with Kroskas in order to rescue Thistle's girlfriend, who Kroskas had captured. Thistle privately concludes Joon is lying about this motivation but does not challenge him.

Joon then reveals that Kroskas's experiments were too extreme even for the Cult of the Dragon, and that Kroskas found other sponsors — specifically the Kraken Society — who were working with ancient dragons. Joon believes Kroskas was either working for these sponsors all along or found them after being rejected by the cult. He states the ancient dragons allied with or were allied by the Kraken Society, and warns that their lairs are impregnable and that engaging them would be madness. He refuses to share the location even if he knew it, citing the danger.

When asked about Laela, Joon states that as far as he last knew, Adimond still has her. The party leaves Joon at the Talking Troll when they depart to investigate Mirak's disappearance. Kaelen asks if they are taking Joon; Vardis declines on behalf of the group.

## Kaelen Sarssir

Kaelen Sarssir is the proprietor and barkeep of the Talking Troll, a dim, smelly dive tavern in Triboar with a partially burned and unrepaired roof where birds nest. Kaelen has dramatic flair, prompting Vardis to ask if he is an actor — which he confirms enthusiastically. He describes himself as a method actor collecting material for an "off-High Road production," which is why he tolerates Uncle Joon's moaning rather than simply charging more.

Kaelen holds Uncle Joon's tab at 30 gold (15 in actual debt plus surcharges) and informs the party that it would go up to 30 the moment Thistle mentions Adimond Kroskas in Joon's presence. He boasts an extraordinary wine cellar, including a rare sparkling wine called Champagne du le Stomp, supposedly from a hidden dimension ruled by a vampire, sold to him by an elf who claimed it was the last of its kind. Uncle Joon dismisses this provenance as fiction, claiming Kaelen found the cellar full and that it is just a random bottle from a random vintner.

Kaelen is later revealed to be a blackmailer. Darz Helgar confesses that Kaelen discovered Darz's criminal past and threatened to expose him to the Lord Protector unless Darz stole the Lionshield Coaster's gold and key. Orsik promises Darz that the party will deal with Kaelen.

## Daratha Shendrel / Delratha

Daratha Shendrel (also referred to as Delratha by the brothers and in the session's later passages) is the current Lord Protector of Triboar, originally from Waterdeep. The local militia member describes her as fair, clearheaded, and widely regarded as a good leader. Jacoby mimics her voice and manner in the Talking Troll to entertain the patrons, recounting her dismissal of Mirak's disappearance: she told the brothers that Mirak had run off with a lady friend to the embarrassment of his wife and threatened them with a fortnight in jail if they continued bothering her about it.

When the commotion from Tolmarra's confrontation with Thistle draws a crowd to the tower, Delratha emerges and commands silence before summoning the party, the brothers, and Tolmarra into her office. She is initially furious at the adventurers for what she perceives as enabling another of the brothers' wild goose chases, but is stopped mid-rant when Thistle informs her they found a murdered body. She accepts this development and arranges for a cleric to cast speak with the dead the following day. She dismisses everyone from her office except Tolmarra, who does not emerge that evening — to the amusement and wagering of the Triboar marketplace vendors.

---

## Jacoby Hysstryn

Jacoby is one of two brothers searching for their missing sibling Mirak Hysstryn. He appears at the Talking Troll with his brother Persson, visibly upset that no official investigation into Mirak's disappearance has been conducted. Jacoby performs an impression of the Lord Protector for the tavern crowd, dramatizing her dismissal of their concerns. He engages Thistle in conversation and offers a magical bow in exchange for help finding Mirak, noting he wants actual proof — not just a pile of dust being passed off as the remains of his brother.

When the party presents their findings at the Lord Protector's office, Jacoby and Persson explode in frustration at Delratha, arguing that their family's lack of wealth is why they were ignored for a decade and that noble families would have been treated differently.

---

## Persson Hysstryn

Persson is Jacoby's brother and shares his mission to find Mirak. He contributes the detail that the enduring rumor names Urgala Meltimer, a local innkeeper, as Mirak's lady friend, and that Urgala has always denied this. Persson also mentions that they paid a drunken priest to ask his god about Mirak, and that Helm's response was that Mirak was still at home and never left — a clue Persson found significant enough to share, though the priest withheld part of the answer.

Like Jacoby, Persson erupts in the Lord Protector's office when the body is found, expressing years of frustration at being dismissed.

---

## Sivlarren Loomshank (Loomy)

Sivlarren Loomshank, called Loomy, is a drunken priest sitting in the Talking Troll. He is identified as the cleric the brothers paid to divine Mirak's location through his god. Loomy explains he formerly lived in Neverwinter but got into trouble with the clerical hierarchy and was banished to the Allfaiths Shrine in Red Larch, insisting it was not his fault. He is trapped in Triboar by debts to the tavern, which the party pays off.

In exchange, Loomy clarifies his divine reading: Helm told him not only that Mirak was still at home and had never left, but also something about not being buried in the ground — being above the ground. He withheld the second part from the brothers because he feared it would anger them. His full account gives the party the key clues that lead them to search the attic of the Six Windows Inn. Once his tab is cleared, Loomy departs.

---

## Urgala Meltimer

Urgala Meltimer is a large, powerfully built black woman with greying hair who runs the Northshield House, a stately and clean local inn. She is a retired adventurer who operated primarily in the Dessarin Hills, which she describes as full of abandoned mines, fortresses, and tombs. She retired after a traumatic incident in one mine more than ten years ago, when her wife fell into the Underdark. Urgala expresses no regrets about retirement in general, though that mine lingers in her memory.

Urgala is politically libertarian, rejecting the Lord's Alliance as antithetical to freedom and describing herself as a believer in small local governments. She provides Thistle with a hand-drawn map and notes about landmarks leading to the mine that connects to the Underdark, and explains the broader pattern of wildcat mining in the Dessarin Hills — miners strike it rich, leave, and monsters or others repurpose the abandoned mines.

When pressed about Mirak, Urgala admits they were involved for a period after her wife disappeared, while she was grieving. She insists she had nothing to do with his disappearance and that she never believed the rumors of other women — she says Mirak was devoted only to her. She then implies, before catching herself, that she suspects Mirak's wife Tolmarra upon discovering the affair. She asks Thistle not to repeat this to avoid trouble.

## Tolmarra

Tolmarra is Mirak Hysstryn's wife and the proprietor of the Six Windows Inn, described as a low-rent, chilly, and decrepit two-story building. She discovers Thistle attempting to sneak in through a window and rushes outside screaming accusations of trespassing and rent-evasion, attacking him with a rolling pin and throwing potatoes while he hangs from a rope. She is briefly disarmed when Thistle mentions a giant bumblebee, which for unknown personal reasons resonates with her, causing her to soften and offer him a salve.

While Tolmarra is distracted outside, Orsik, Vardis, and Unla Kee enter and discover bones in the attic — identified as Mirak's remains. When the party emerges and Orsik states they will be calling the Twelve, the inn's residents do not support Tolmarra and one mutters "good riddance on that peeping tom," suggesting she was an unpopular landlord.

Tolmarra is brought to the Lord Protector's office along with the party and the brothers. She attempts to leave with everyone else after Delratha dismisses the group, but Delratha stops her. Tolmarra does not emerge from the tower that evening, to the amusement of wagering marketplace vendors who had apparently bet on whether someone would spend the night in the tower.

---

## Mirak Hysstryn

Mirak Hysstryn does not appear in this session, having been dead for approximately ten years. He is the subject of a missing persons investigation brought to the party by his brothers Jacoby and Persson. The investigation establishes that Mirak had an affair with Urgala Meltimer after her wife disappeared, that his own wife Tolmarra claimed he ran off with a lady friend, and that Helm divined he never left home and was not buried in the ground but above it.

The party locates bones in the sealed attic of the Six Windows Inn matching Mirak's description. The skeleton animates briefly when Unla Kee touches it and is destroyed by Orsik in a single blow. No cause of death is determined from the bones alone. Delratha arranges a speak with the dead the following morning to gather further information.

Urgala Meltimer describes Mirak as having been madly in love with her, and implies she suspects Tolmarra killed him upon discovering the affair.

---

## Alaestra

Alaestra is one of two owners of the Lionshield Coaster outpost in Triboar, found in a state of distress when Orsik arrives. She immediately offers a 10% discount in exchange for help recovering stolen goods. She inadvertently reveals that the theft included not just gold but a key — a special key used to order goods from the Lionshield Coaster's mysterious interdimensional supplier. Without it, the company will be displeased. Her partner Narth tries to stop her from disclosing this detail. She negotiates with Orsik up to a 20% discount and 50 gold before Orsik accepts.

---

## Narth

Narth is Alaestra's partner and co-owner of the Lionshield Coaster outpost. He attempts to prevent Alaestra from revealing the existence and significance of the key to Orsik, recognizing it gives the dwarf leverage. He participates in the negotiation and agrees to Orsik's terms along with Alaestra.

## Othovir

Othovir is the local harness maker. When shown the harness left behind by the thief, he confirms he did not make it but assesses it as impressive work — likely made in Waterdeep, approximately a decade old, and sized for a person of around 150 pounds. He directs the party to speak with Harriet about the grappling hook.

---

## Harriet

Harriet runs Uldinath's Arms, a smithy in Triboar and a rival of Forehammer's Forge. When shown the grappling hook, she confirms she did not make it and identifies the maker as Gherlyn, adding affectionately that she has known him since childhood, considers him a good man, and is teasing when she calls him a lousy smith. She directs the party to Forehammer's Forge.

---

## Gherlyn

Gherlyn is an elderly dwarf who runs Forehammer's Forge in Triboar. He is too old to have been the cat burglar himself. He is initially coy about who he sold the grappling hook to, but responds to Orsik's account of killing a hill giant and crushing orcs, expressing pride and enthusiasm. He then confirms he sold the hook to Darz Helgar, the caretaker of the northern campgrounds, who lives in a cottage on site.

---

## Darz Helgar

Darz Helgar is the caretaker of Triboar's northern campgrounds, living in a cottage on site. When Orsik presents the harness and grappling hook and obliquely asks what Darz knows, Darz immediately confesses. He admits to stealing the gold and bags from the Lionshield Coaster but explains he had no choice: Kaelen, the Talking Troll proprietor, discovered Darz's criminal past and threatened to inform the Lord Protector unless Darz committed the theft. Darz served ten hard years in Waterdeep's dungeon and is tired of running and hiding; he expresses relief that the game is up.

Darz asks the party to make sure Kaelen faces consequences. Orsik negotiates with him, offering to keep quiet about Darz's involvement in exchange for whatever contacts Darz has with the Thieves Guild, and promises that the party will deal with Kaelen. Darz's current status is that Orsik has made this arrangement, with Darz presumably remaining at the campgrounds.

---

## Syreen Widdens

Syreen Widdens is the owner of The Cart and Coin, a business in Triboar that sells and swaps horses and draft animals. Thistle stops in and politely asks to borrow a bucket, which Syreen is happy to lend. No further interaction is recorded.
