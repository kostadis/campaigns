## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is a cloud giant and the former master of the flying castle, who previously allied with the Cult of the Dragon. He is described as a pivotal figure in the castle's control structure, driven by guilt and a desire to restore his family's honor. His fate is directly tied to his son, who remains trapped as a ghost until Blagothkus is slain — meaning his death is presented as a condition for his son's spiritual release. Depending on the adventurers' choices, he may either aid the party in establishing control over the castle or become a hindrance to their efforts. His relationship with Escarlotta, his spectral wife, remains a central emotional thread, as she controls the castle's systems and her own decisions may be shaped by what happens to Blagothkus and their son. His current status and last known whereabouts place him within or connected to the flying castle.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta is the ghost of Blagothkus' wife and serves as the central controller of the flying castle's systems. She is described as a complex entity capable of being either helpful or obstructive to the party depending on their actions and how they choose to deal with her family's situation. She holds her own desires regarding the fate of the castle and her family — most notably, she may be persuaded to side with the party if they can convince her they intend to save her son, who is trapped as a ghost. Her cooperation is framed as a key factor in the adventurers establishing true control over the flying fortress. She is tied emotionally and spiritually to both Blagothkus and their ghostly son, making her motivations personal as much as they are strategic. Her last known location is within the flying castle, where she operates as its spectral overseer.

## Glazeal

Glazeal is a Dracolich — a cunning undead dragon — whose lair is located deep within a frozen ice cavern inside the flying castle. Glazeal struck a deal with the adventuring party to retrieve its phylactery, which is hidden within that same cavern. Despite this arrangement, Glazeal is explicitly noted as not being entirely trustworthy and may turn on the party if circumstances make doing so beneficial. The phylactery is described as holding great power, and the party faces a choice regarding it: either use it to solidify the alliance with Glazeal, or destroy it to free the castle from the Dracolich's influence. The cavern guarding the phylactery is filled with ancient magic, treasure, undead enemies, and hidden dangers. Glazeal's current status is alive and present within the flying castle's ice cavern, operating under a conditional and fragile truce with the party.

## Lord Khasperi Dryland

Lord Khasperi Dryland is the charismatic and secretive owner of The Grand Dame, a luxurious floating casino and gambling boat moored in Yartar. He is connected to the plot surrounding the disappearance of the storm giant king, Hekaton, though the nature of his involvement is not yet fully revealed. Extracting information from him is described as difficult, suggesting he is guarded, cagey, or politically protected. The adventurers must infiltrate The Grand Dame — either through subtle means such as disguise and gambling, or through direct confrontation — in order to access Dryland's private quarters and uncover his darkest secrets. Winning big at high-stakes gambling aboard his ship is noted as one potential avenue for gaining access to those secrets. His relationship to the broader conspiracy around Hekaton's capture places him within a network involving human factions and ancient powers. His last known location is aboard The Grand Dame in Yartar.

## Queen Serissa

Queen Serissa does not physically appear in the events of this chapter but is referenced as a key motivating figure. She is the acting head of the storm giant court and the daughter of the missing storm king, Hekaton. She has directly tasked the adventuring party with finding her father, whose disappearance threatens the balance of power across the realm. She is described as desperate, understanding the existential stakes of Hekaton's absence for the storm giant kingdom. She provides guidance to the party but is not present at the locations they are currently operating in. Her arc is tied to the broader conspiracy involving human factions and potential betrayals from within the storm giant court, including possibly one of Hekaton's closest advisors. Her last known whereabouts are the storm giant court.
