## Chandra Stol

Chandra is waiting inside the Golden Tankard when the heroes arrive. When Orsik returns without the desired outcome, she responds with dry humor, offering "charred meat" instead. She grants Orsik permission, with a gesture of noblesse oblige, to have the captain of the guard feed the Wyvern.

Later, when Orsik marches to the Temple of Axes and tells her everything the Seer revealed, Chandra's initial pleasure at seeing him evaporates. She confirms the Seer's account, revealing that Narcellia has plagued her family for centuries and was responsible for her mother's death 30 years ago. After that death, Chandra went into hiding and had not heard of the drow since. She explains that her ancestor Luistara went mad after Narcellia destroyed her village and used hag magic to bind the drow into the headband.

Chandra offers a potentially useful piece of intelligence: Narcellia was deathly afraid of the fey, sprites in particular, and suggests that Prince Thornacious might be of use. She then becomes stoic and tells the group there is nothing they can do for her personally, prompting most of the group to shrug and walk away.

She is won over by Thistle's earnestness in gathering herbs from Syla's garden and rubbing them on Orsik, and she agrees to join the group — but only after consulting Unla Kee about whether the plan makes sense. When Unla Kee tells her that not coming will mean the end of the world, she agrees.

At the forest boundary, after Thistle's pitch to Prince Thornacious secures fey assistance, Chandra begs Orsik to convince Thornacious to let her stay with the fey rather than continue with the group. When Orsik is confused, she clarifies she doesn't need his protection — she just needs the fey. This wounds Orsik's pride. Her current status is at or near Prince Thornacious' camp, seeking to remain under fey protection.

## Seer

The Seer is a 50-something Shou woman wearing a blue robes embroidered with birds. She carries a silver staff topped with a crane's head with crystal eyes. She is encountered when an ornate door appears in the wall of the Golden Tankard — a door covered in Shou writings that projects mental images into the minds of the heroes. Her octagonal room is lined with shelves holding scrolls, books, and a remarkable variety of birds. A large desk holds a map of the Sword Coast weighted down with small marble statues of Shou gods. Beyond the octagonal room is a garden.

She greets the group by announcing, "you aren't in Parnast anymore." She uses her intelligence network to demonstrate her reach by sharing private information: she tells Unla Kee that his Uncle Joon is still in Triboar, that his nemesis Adirond Kroskas has disappeared into the North, and that his beloved Laela may still live — rumors suggest allies of Fire Giants have captured her. She whispers something private to Orsik about his past that he refuses to discuss and growls is "not me anymore." She expresses happiness at seeing Unla Kee eating the cookies and notes the world's end may require that he take significant risks.

She reveals her organization's thirty-year objective: to find Narcellia Varam'myr, a priestess of Lolth trapped in a headband of intellect by an elven follower of Angharradh named Luistarra, as punishment for Narcellia's destruction of Luistarra's village near Evereska. The Seer's organization lost track of the headband during the rise of the Cult of the Dragon and knew only that it was somewhere near the Greypeak Mountains.

She explains that she devised a plan to flush Narcellia out: she arranged for an artist in the Anauoch Desert to create a replacement statue of Angharradh for Parnast, and engineered Chandra's discovery of that artist, knowing Narcellia would return to destroy any new statue. In parallel, magical tomes retrieved from the Marsh of Chelimber provided a ritual that allowed her organization to scry out the owner of the headband — revealing it to be Bad Fruul. Narcellia has been urging the hill giant to increase his wealth and power, has taught him the basics of arcane magic, and used the collapse of the Ordning to convince him to draw monstrous humanoids to his banner.

She acknowledges that her plots have inadvertently made Parnast a target: Kragota Threeteeth's agents and farmers revealed the existence of the new statue, and Bad Fruul, urged by Narcellia, has decided to destroy Parnast. She further warns that Chandra Stol possesses a pendant owned by her ancestor Luistarra, and that Narcellia has particularly gruesome plans for the cleric.

She explains the Ordning when asked by Vardis: it is the social structure of the Giants that keeps them in check, and its dissolution has caused every Giant tribe and leader to attempt to grow their power. She states her organization does not know who or what dissolved the Ordning.

Her ask of the group: protect Chandra at all costs, determine when Bad Fruul will attack and defend Parnast, and bring her the headband. She offers 600 gold for trying, 200 extra if no one dies, and 100 extra if Chandra is kept safe. She also mentions she heard Orsik owns a tower. Before the group departs, she gives Unla Kee a book titled "Prophecies of the Apocalypse." She walks them to the outside as the door rematerializes. Her last known location is her extradimensional octagonal room/garden, accessible via a door she can project into physical locations.

When Orsik refuses, Hsing conjures up mental images of death in cities protected by the Lord's Alliance as a warning of what will happen if Orsik does not enter. Orsik is unimpressed and refuses regardless, commenting that Hsing might have been more persuasive by being nicer. Hsing's further whereabouts or role in the session are not recorded.

## Wallace

Wallace is present when Orsik pats him on the back outside the Golden Tankard and tells him that "someday, you'll be feeding people to the Wyvern." Wallace stares at him funny. He stays with Orsik and Vardis when the group is initially reluctant to enter the Seer's door.

During the planning discussion after the Seer's meeting, Wallace raises the practical concern about protecting the village while Orsik talks of assassination. He explains to the group that to the northeast of Parnast is Old Parnast, which used to be a Zhentarim fortress and is more defensible, though locals believe it to be cursed (connected to a belief that towers Ragnar built burned down because they used logs from the old town). Orsik finds Wallace's strategic thinking beyond his own comfort zone and ultimately decides to make their defense at the current Parnast location rather than the old fortress. Wallace's current status is with the group as it heads toward Prince Thornacious' camp.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is present when the group arrives at the Golden Tankard and when the ornate door appears. He rushes to open the door, muttering "when I stop taking risks, the world ends!" He grabs a cookie from a silver tray immediately upon entering the Seer's room and heads out through the doors to the garden.

He carefully examines the map of the Sword Coast on the Seer's desk alongside Thistle. He receives personal intelligence from the Seer: his Uncle Joon is still in Triboar, his nemesis Adirond Kroskas has disappeared into the North, and his beloved Laela is rumored to still be alive — possibly captured by allies of Fire Giants. He stares at this information without recorded verbal response.

He is noted by the Seer as someone whose risk-taking may be essential to preventing the world's end. He smirks when Orsik says he would like to assassinate Bad Fruul and states he is "cool with an assassination."

When the group cannot figure out how to combine Sylas's antidote ingredients, Unla Kee studies them and figures out how to put everything together, producing enough antidote for four of the group. He dejectedly reacts to the Seer's reward offer. Chandra Stol consults him before agreeing to join the group, and he tells her that if she does not come, it will mean the end of the world. Just before leaving the Seer's space, he receives the book "Prophecies of the Apocalypse" from her. His current status is with the group heading toward Prince Thornacious' camp, carrying the apocalypse prophecy book.

## Thistle

Thistle is present throughout the session. When the villagers claim orcs are coming, he carefully evaluates the situation and perceives no signs of lying, confirming the villagers were telling the truth. He is among those who receive the mental image of the ornate door and follows Unla Kee inside. He examines the map of the Sword Coast and tells Orsik it might help find his map. He briefly believes the white bird that flies out of the door is crucial, until the sound of hundreds of birds from the octagonal room changes his mind.

During planning, Thistle observes that Old Parnast is a better defensive position. He reassures Orsik, noting "don't worry, Orsik, I trained their archers well" — though this is immediately undermined by the sound of a bow breaking as they walk away.

When the group meets Chandra and she becomes stoic and refuses help, Thistle quietly leaves, gathers herbs from Syla's garden, and rubs them on Orsik to address his smell problem in an earnest attempt to win Chandra's cooperation. He explains his actions openly to the group, impressing Chandra with his willingness to go the extra mile and contributing to her agreeing to join them.

At Prince Thornacious' camp, Thistle leads the group quickly to the prince's location and makes the pitch for support. Things go well initially, but he inadvertently offends Oblivillish by referring to him as a "hunting master," causing an outburst from Oblivillish who insists he is not a hunter and that the stag deaths were caused by Unseelie. Thistle attempts to clarify but makes things worse when Thornacious asks Oblivillish directly if he is a hunter. After Oblivillish denies it, Thistle goes silent. Despite this setback, the pitch ultimately succeeds. Thistle's current status is with the group at or near Prince Thornacious' camp.

## Narcellia Varam'myr

Narcellia Varam'myr does not appear in person in this session but is extensively discussed by the Seer and Chandra Stol. She is a priestess of Lolth who has been trapped for several hundred years inside a headband of intellect. She was imprisoned by Luistarra, a follower of Angharradh, as punishment for destroying Luistarra's village near Evereska. Luistarra used hag magic to bind Narcellia into the headband after going mad with grief.

The Seer's organization spent thirty years trying to locate her. They lost track of her during the rise of the Cult of the Dragon and knew only that the headband was somewhere near the Greypeak Mountains. Magical tomes from the Marsh of Chelimber provided a ritual that allowed the Seer to scry out the headband's current owner: the hill giant Bad Fruul.

While bound in the headband, Narcellia has been actively influencing Bad Fruul: urging him to increase his wealth and girth, teaching him the basics of arcane magic, and exploiting the collapse of the Ordning to convince him to draw monstrous humanoids to his banner and step out of his comfort zone. She also had Bad Fruul's agents — Kragota Threeteeth's farmers — reveal the existence of the new statue of Angharradh in Parnast, making the village a target for destruction.

Narcellia has a centuries-long vendetta against Chandra Stol's family. She was responsible for the death of Chandra's mother 30 years ago, which drove Chandra into hiding. She is aware that Chandra possesses a pendant owned by her ancestor Luistarra and has particularly gruesome plans for the cleric.

Chandra adds that Narcellia was deathly afraid of the fey, specifically sprites, which is why Prince Thornacious may be useful against her. Narcellia's current location is unknown, though she is presumed to be with or influencing Bad Fruul somewhere to the south.

## Bad Fruul

Bad Fruul does not appear in person in this session but is discussed at length by the Seer. He is a hill giant who currently possesses the headband of intellect containing Narcellia Varam'myr. Under Narcellia's influence, he has increased his wealth and girth, learned the basics of arcane magic, drawn monstrous humanoids to his banner, and — following the collapse of the Ordning — stepped beyond his usual comfort zone to pursue greater power. His agents, working through Kragota Threeteeth's network, revealed the existence of Parnast's new statue of Angharradh, and he has decided to destroy Parnast. The group estimates the most likely date of his attack as the 8th day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul — four days hence. The group plans to assassinate him as a group action. His current whereabouts are not specified beyond being a threat approaching from the south.

## Oblivillish

Oblivillish is present at Prince Thornacious' camp. When Thistle refers to him as a hunting master and asks for his help, Oblivillish is outraged. He shouts that this point had already been clarified — he is not a hunter, and the stags were killed by Unseelie, not by him.
