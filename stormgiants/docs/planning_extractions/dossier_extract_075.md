## Blagothkus

Blagothkus appears throughout the session as an unwilling but present ally. He urges the party to run to Sandesyl's tower rather than finish killing the Zombie Ogres, arguing that the zombies are not a reserve force but a trap to hold enemies in place for Glazhael to kill from above. Orsik dismisses his tactical advice. When the party reaches Sandesyl's empty tower, Blagothkus explains that Sandesyl has retreated to her ice cave lair because the party has depleted her resources and she was not expecting them this time — unlike their previous invited visit. He describes her likely behavior: sending spawn to collect pieces of the party's bodies before she'll believe they're dead. He volunteers to stay behind and guard the tower entrance rather than squeeze through the tunnels, citing Escarlotta's instruction that he only help if the party finds him helpful. After Vaardis and Thistle reject this, he sighs and compresses himself into the tunnel. He is then sent by Thistle to deal with the animated statues that Unla Kee triggered, and returns afterward slightly worn down but still functional. He provides the backstory on Brunvild: that the frost giant jarl helped the Heroes of the War of the Dragon repair and steal the castle after it crashed on the Miklos Glacier, believing Sandesyl would be left behind, but she stowed away. When Brunvild planned to destroy the Cult of the Dragon, Sandesyl allowed it, then turned on him after. She kept Brunvild alive as a food source and came to Blagothkus when she needed the castle operated. Blagothkus notes that Escarlotta has a different version of these events. Brunvild blames Blagothkus directly for his imprisonment and the deaths of his men and the dwarves. Last known location: with the party, preparing to attack Sandesyl's lair.

## Glazhael

Glazhael does not appear directly in this session but is referenced. Blagothkus mentions she has left the area during the darkness, presenting what he frames as an opportunity to run. Thistle observes that Glazhael moved out of eyeshot in a way that seems deliberate — almost as if she wants Thistle to approach Sandesyl's tower. This raises suspicion that Glazhael may be maneuvering the party rather than retreating. Last known status: absent from the immediate area, whereabouts and intentions unclear.

## Sandesyl

Sandesyl does not appear in person during this session but is the central subject of the party's planning. Blagothkus explains that she abandoned the tower for her ice cave lair because the party destroyed her wyvernliches, eliminated her zombie material, attacked her Ogre Zombies, and because she no longer trusts them since they arrived without an invitation. He describes her as deeply distrustful of her own minions and cautious enough to require proof of enemy deaths before leaving her lair. Her lair is accessible either through two vampire spawn or through a frost giant's chamber. She has been conducting experiments using orc zombies and a ghost to create an undead cleaning service, keeping the ghost obsessively maintaining one cave for at least two years. She has been feeding on Brunvild for an extended period, keeping him alive as a blood source. She came to Blagothkus after turning on Brunvild, needing him to operate the castle. Last known location: her lair to the west, accessible through the ice cave tunnels.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee appears throughout the session. He casts darkness using the wand of wonder, slowing the Undead Ogres. He flies using giant Pegasus wings. He informs the party that Bob is currently trapped in the Astral Plane and lacks access to his full power, which Orsik uses to justify leaving Bob where he is. In the statue cavern, Unla Kee notices that the second statue is of Sammaster, original leader of the Cult of the Dragon, holding the Tome of the Dragon, and that the scene appears to depict Sammaster learning the secrets of undeath from Inferno. Despite Thistle's warning, Unla Kee flies up to the statue of Inferno and pries gems from its eye sockets, animating the four surrounding statues. He is dealt with by Blagothkus while the party moves forward. He is revealed to possess a haste spell that he has been withholding, which he casts before the attack on the vampire spawn. When asked why he hasn't been using it, he says it would be too easy. Last known location: with the party, hasted, preparing to engage vampire spawn outside Sandesyl's lair.

## Inferno

Inferno does not appear in person but is present as a large statue in the ice cave cavern. Vaardis identifies the statue as Inferno, the Hellbound Dracolich — described as a once-powerful red dragon who made a forbidden pact with a demon lord to achieve immortality and is said to be the first of the Dracoliches. The statue depicts a skeletal red dragon with burning embers in its eye sockets, blackened jagged horns, tattered bat-like wings, claws imbued with hellish fire, and demonic runes etched into the bone structure. Unla Kee identifies the scene as depicting Sammaster learning the secrets of undeath from Inferno. Valuable gems were set into the statue's eyes and were removed by Unla Kee, triggering the animated statues.

## Sammaster

Sammaster does not appear in person but is present as a statue in the ice cave cavern. Unla Kee identifies it as a depiction of Sammaster, the original leader of the Cult of the Dragon, holding the Tome of the Dragon — described as the definitive book on Dracoliches. The tableau appears to show Sammaster learning the secrets of undeath from the dracolich Inferno. Unla Kee notes this as odd and significant. No further information about Sammaster's current status or whereabouts is provided.

## Brunvild

Brunvild is a frost giant jarl found imprisoned and emaciated in a cave within Sandesyl's lair, kept alive as a food source. Thistle approaches him directly and claims Harshnag sent them to free him. Brunvild is initially skeptical — questioning Thistle's small size and doubting Blagothkus's motives given that he holds Blagothkus responsible for his imprisonment, his men's deaths, and the deaths of dwarves who trusted him. He asks about the dwarves and appears relieved when Thistle implies they may have escaped. He explains that Sandesyl placed a ghost over a group of orc zombies as part of an experiment in creating an undead cleaning service, and the ghost has kept the orcs confined to one cave for two years. He agrees to serve as a distraction — escaping through the southern tunnel to draw guard drakes and other minions away from Sandesyl's lair — after Thistle convinces him that one more attempt at freedom is worth the risk. Before leaving, he mentions to Thistle that flying creatures have had a strange influence on his life: first a Kenku, then Thistle. This shocks Thistle, as the story of a flying Kenku who defeated the Cult of the Dragon is a legend told among prisoners — Kenkus do not fly — yet Brunvild speaks of this Kenku as someone he personally encountered. Brunvild then lumbers out through the southern tunnel, drawing the guard drakes. Last known location: moving through the southern tunnel as a distraction, freeing himself from Sandesyl's imprisonment.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta does not appear in this session but is referenced twice. Blagothkus cites her instruction that he only needs to help the party if they feel he is helpful, using this as justification for volunteering to stay behind at the tower entrance. Blagothkus also notes that Escarlotta has a different version of the events surrounding the castle's crash, Brunvild's involvement, and Sandesyl's takeover. No further details about Escarlotta's current location or activities are provided.

## Harshnag

Harshnag does not appear in this session but is referenced by Thistle when speaking to Brunvild. Thistle uses Harshnag's name to establish credibility with the imprisoned frost giant, claiming Harshnag sent them to free him. Brunvild recognizes the name and responds with hope, asking if Harshnag is present. Thistle confirms Harshnag did not come personally. No other information about Harshnag's current location or status is provided in this session.
