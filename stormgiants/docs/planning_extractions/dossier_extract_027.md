## Urgala

Urgala appeared on the battlefield during the fire giant attack on Triboar. She was observed by Thistle from atop a two-story tower in the market. She attempted to fight, firing her short bow at a fire giant but missing. She was also attacked by an orc mounted on an axe beak, which she appeared to ignore. She had visible burn marks, which Thistle attributed to her having fought off magmins. Her home and inn, the Northshield House, was burning to the northeast during the battle.

After the battle, Urgala was found in the market square alongside Narth Tezerin and Othovir, discussing the destruction. When Narth shouted about her burned house, she responded calmly: "Houses burn, but they can be rebuilt, especially if I am in a strong community of friends." The group shared a hug.

Later, Urgala entered the Talking Troll tavern and sat with the adventurers. She offered mild criticism of their choices during the battle: "Good on killing the giant, but you could have helped us deal with the magmins. The giants were just looking for something." She also made a comment about the fairy's uncle, who Thistle said was fine.

Urgala then offered the party a lead as a reward for their help, including saving someone named Miraz and aiding the town. She told them that a former adventuring companion, a wealthy knight named Harthos Zymorven, possessed a giant-slaying longsword and was living at Zymorven Hall near his ancestral keep on the Rauvin Road northwest of Silverymoon. She indicated he would part with the sword if the party dropped her name and stated their intention to defeat giants and save the realms, noting "we go way back."

She left, then returned moments later to add that she had saved a map to a mine she had never explored, near where her wife had died. Last known location: the Talking Troll tavern in Triboar.

## Darz

Darz, described as a former thief from Waterdeep, was found dead near the entrance of the campground by Thistle during the battle. Orsik felt strongly about raising him and committed to doing so.

After Cindell performed the raise dead spell at a discount, Darz was revived. His first words were "I'm alive." He observed the various parties — Orsik, Cindell, and Darthara — jockeying to receive credit for his revival, and privately assessed each of their motivations cynically. He also stared at the hole left in the campground where the fire giant had been digging and privately noted that had he known about the artifact buried there, he would have dug it up and sold it himself.

The following day, Darz went to the Talking Troll to speak with his benefactors. He sat down with them and acknowledged: "I've never been dead before." He then shared information he had been keeping for himself as a retirement job, out of gratitude for being raised: an old friend, a merchant from Mirabar, had spotted a dwarf named Weevil cleaning stables in Xantharl's Keep, a fortified village on the Long Road. Weevil had been recognized from a wanted poster seen while traveling south through Longsaddle, and was worth 5,000 gold to the Axe of Mirabar. After sharing this information, Darz finished his drink and left.

Last known status: alive, departed the Talking Troll after sharing the Weevil tip.

## Orsik

Orsik was wounded during the battle against the fire giants. He watched Urgala's arrow miss the fire giant and calculated that it would have barely pierced the giant's armor even if it had connected. He also called out during the battle for Darathra to run. He was subsequently crushed by the giant's sword and died, with his last thoughts being: "I told them that we shouldn't fight these giants. Frankly, I don't think these giants are important."

Orsik was pulled into the tower by Vardis. He was then revived, apparently through Thistle's spell that revived the Lord Protector, or through other means — the text indicates Thistle cast a revival spell during battle. After recovering, Orsik expressed regret about the battle: "pointless battle. I told you guys we didn't need to fight the giants." He commented on Thistle's performance: "not your best work." When Vardis told him to go back out and fight, Orsik flatly refused: "Nope, I'm out."

After the battle, Orsik prioritized raising Darz from the dead and suggested casting Speak with Dead on the fire giant's corpse and looting its body before others arrived. He also proposed meeting the powerful cleric who had appeared in town, though he sneered at Thistle's more commercial suggestions for how to leverage her presence.

Orsik interacted with Cindell Trueshot, initially dismissing her before realizing she was the cleric. He negotiated with her for the raise dead and Speak with Dead spells, attempting to pass off the ruby found on the giant's body as being worth more than it was. He also apparently invited her to dinner, which she initially misread as flirtation before understanding he meant a group outing to the Talking Troll, noting he owned the place and would benefit from her attendance drawing other ranch owners.

Orsik asked the dead fire giant: "Why did you come to Triboar?" and "Where can we find Duke Zalto?" The giant answered the first with "To rule over the giants! Duke Zalto will soon rule over all giants!" and the second with "In his throne room!" before refusing further questions.

Last known status: alive and recovering in Triboar, at the Talking Troll.

## Thistle

Thistle observed the battle from the top of a two-story tower in the middle of the market. He saw the market in shambles, spotted a second fire giant digging to the north with a longsword using what appeared to be a divining rod (later tossed aside), observed Darz's body near the campground entrance, and noted Urgala's burn marks and the orc mounted on an axe beak attacking her.

During the battle, Thistle cast a reviving spell on the Lord Protector, then fired his bow twice and hit both times, privately noting that "Urgala needs to attend my classes." After the battle ended, he wanted to fire at the orc and then at the second fire giant, but was stopped by Darthara both times. He accepted Orsik's criticism — "not your best work" — and reflected, "It's like I was possessed by someone else."

Thistle flew discreetly toward the second fire giant to monitor it, observing it drag out a large 11-foot metal band. He flew closer to get a better angle but could not determine what the band was made of. The giant did not notice him. On his return, he surveyed the damage: Urgala's Northshield House, the Apothecary, and several cottages and farms had burned.

During the Speak with Dead session, Thistle asked the dead giant what the band that was carried out was, receiving the answer: "A piece of the Vonidod." Thistle identified the Vonidod as an artifact related to killing dragons, noted as being powerful and sought after across the realms. He credited his uncle Joon's travels as a source of this knowledge (referenced by Unla Kee's narration). Thistle also suggested selling tickets to see Cindell and having a play written about her god, proposals that annoyed Orsik.

He removed toenails from the dead fire giant to make potions of giant strength, then lamented that the Apothecary had been destroyed. He overheard the smithy Ghelryn mention that the fire giant's heart could be turned into a living lamp and noted it would be useful for the theater.

Last known status: alive in Triboar, present at the Talking Troll.

## Vardis

Vardis was present on the battlefield during the fire giant attack. He had his sword extended and ready to attack the frozen giant alongside Orsik, but neither was able to kill it. He fired an arrow at the giant that missed but retreated fast enough to reach the tower safely. From inside the tower, he witnessed Orsik being crushed by the giant's sword.

After the battle, Vardis ran out of the tower and pulled Orsik's body inside. He also patched Orsik up and sarcastically told him to go back out and fight: "hey, I patched you up. Go out there and fight, soldier." He found a bag of salt on the dead fire giant's body and quipped that the giant was "probably a gourmet who liked his food seasoned." He attempted to remove the fire giant's heart after Ghelryn explained it could be turned into a living lamp, but failed.

During the group's interactions with Cindell, Vardis internally assessed her as "one of those clerics who doesn't want to be a cleric." He watched Orsik's negotiation attempts with her and later exchanged banter about her residence, calling it a "bungalow." During the Speak with Dead session, Vardis grumbled "a question with a question" when the giant responded to a question with another question.

Last known status: alive in Triboar, present at the Talking Troll.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee was present during the battle and was described as barely alive at one point. He cast Thunderwave at the ice-frozen fire giant, shattering the ice and knocking the giant back. He then hid in the tower with the others and asked "are we safe?" His magic missiles delivered the killing blow to the wounded giant that Thistle had brought down.

After the battle, Unla Kee helped Orsik estimate the weight of the 11-foot metal band dragged out by the second fire giant, though their combined conclusion was only that it weighed between 1 and 10,000 pounds. He also administered the Speak with Dead spell on the fire giant corpse. He framed his motivation as wanting to learn the giant's secrets for personal advantage.

During the Speak with Dead session, Unla Kee translated the giant's words for the group. He asked the giant what the other fire giant had been digging from the ground, to which the giant responded with a counter-question. He also asked where the giant's throne room was located, but the giant became angry and refused to answer further, instead cursing the party and declaring that Duke Zalto would rule over all, particularly condemning the storm giants.

Unla Kee exchanged banter with Cindell about her residence, smirking: "because why leave your palatial residence?"

Last known status: alive in Triboar.

## Darathra (The Lord Protector)

Darathra was present on the battlefield and was described as staring at the immobilized giant with fear and uncertainty, weighing whether to stay and fight or flee. After Orsik shouted "run," she ran into the tower and warned the others to "stay away from the giants." Inside the tower, when Unla Kee asked if they were safe, she responded: "as long as the giants don't want to come inside."

She was killed during the battle — the text notes "The Lord Protector is dead" — and was subsequently revived by Thistle's spell during the fight. After the battle, she confronted Thistle: "the rest of us almost died while you were unscathed." She stopped Thistle from firing at the second fire giant and at the orc, insisting: "you will not fire an arrow at that giant. We will sit here and pray that it leaves without killing anyone or destroying anything else." She advocated monitoring the situation and taking "copious notes."

After the danger passed, Darathra went to the market square where she found Urgala, Narth Tezerin, and Othovir and asked "how are we going to clean up this place."

Later, Darathra visited the Talking Troll with two members of her guard and sat with the adventurers. She confirmed she had heard that locals had been sharing information about treasures as rewards. She revealed her membership in the Harpers, making the Harper hand gesture. She expressed concern that fire giants had penetrated so far into the Derrien Valley without anyone's knowledge. She provided the party with a platinum badge bearing the Triboar insignia and instructed them to visit Danivarr's House in Everlund — east along the Evermoor Way — and give the badge to Dral Thelev, the one-eyed half-orc proprietor.

A farmer standing near Orsik privately noted that Darthara was not originally from Triboar but from Waterdeep, and that locals had voted her in expecting "Waterdeep magic."

Last known status: alive, Lord Protector of Triboar, affiliated with the Harpers.

## Cindell Trueshot

Cindell Trueshot does not appear until after the battle. She arrived at the market area where the party had gathered, sitting down at a right angle to the group. She is described as a tall, striking woman with a smirking green grin, long ash blond hair tucked under a large-brimmed hat exposing an elven ear, and leather armor dyed with accents of blue, red, and yellow. Her bearing was described as formal, as if accustomed to being in charge.

She opened by saying dismissively: "What a fine mess you have gotten yourselves into." She taunted Darthara about the destruction, suggesting it would not make a good re-election achievement and that she would personally remind voters of it. She identified herself as a candidate for mayor of Triboar: "Me, Cindell Trueshot." Darthara called her a "carpetbagger," which Cindell ignored. Orsik called both women carpetbaggers, which made Cindell smile.

Cindell described herself as having been at her vacation home — a six-room house with a couple of acres — when she saw the giants arrive. She stated she was not dressed to fight and knew Darthara would not leave the city unprotected, and that it took her time to get ready. She clarified she had not brought the skull used for Speak with Dead, but rather that it had been brought to her, and that she does not typically come into town — "I have people for that."

Cindell is a cleric, though she resisted being defined by her profession: "I don't like to be defined by my profession." She was identified by Orsik as the powerful cleric referenced earlier. Vardis internally categorized her as "one of those clerics who doesn't want to be a cleric."

She quoted prices of 90 gold for Speak with Dead and 1,250 gold to raise someone from the dead, then pivoted to taunting Darthara by offering to heal her first. When Thistle offered theater tickets as payment, she declined, saying she was not a fan of theater or Kaelen's work. She adjudicated that answering the dead giant's counter-question would count as one of the questions in the Speak with Dead session.

Cindell ultimately performed the raise dead spell on Darz at a significant discount, framing it as community solidarity while privately noting her own interest in making Darthara look bad and cultivating allies among the ranch-owning class. She apparently accepted an invitation to dinner at the Talking Troll, though she initially misread Orsik's invitation as flirtation.

Her narrated internal perspective (labeled "Cindell" in the text) reveals she is politically calculating, skeptical of Orsik's attempts to overvalue the ruby, aware that Orsik owns the Talking Troll and benefits from her attendance, and privately cynical about Orsik's, Darthara's, and her own motivations in the Darz revival.

Last known status: alive in Triboar; political rival to Darthara for the position of mayor; cleric of unstated deity; owns a vacation property near Triboar with a ranch.

And your house, it burned to the ground." He participated in the group hug that followed Urgala's response. No further actions or dialogue attributed to him.

## Othovir

Othovir, the harness store owner, was present in the market square after the battle alongside Urgala and Narth Tezerin. He later approached the adventurers at the Talking Troll, looking around nervously before speaking. He told them he had no money but would share information about where to find some, as a favor for their help.

He revealed a strong personal hatred for the Margasters: "I hate the Margasters. We have a history. And that's all I will share." He then disclosed the location of the Margaster family's emergency magic stash: a three-story tower in Silverymoon with a detached carriage house on the property. He described the carriage house as a 30-foot square room with two floors. The door is protected by an arcane lock, two human guards are stationed upstairs, and the carriage itself is protected by an alarm spell. The magic items are hidden in a compartment under the passenger seat. After sharing this, Othovir got up and left without further conversation.

Last known status: alive in Triboar; has undisclosed personal history of conflict with the Margaster family.
