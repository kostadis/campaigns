## Halisa

Halisa is a dryad exiled from the Fey after she offered her honest opinion about Queen Titania's dress. She is bound to the southernmost tree under the Grandfather Tree's canopy as punishment under "Tree Protection Schedule D," the terms of which forbid her from touching the tree. She introduces herself to Thistle when he approaches, explains her exile, and rebuffs his request to search for the artifact, citing the terms of her punishment. She is terrified when she believes Prince Thornacious has arrived, demanding the party leave immediately.

When Orsik-Thornacious visits her again and declares she is free to leave, her reaction reveals the emotional truth of her situation: rather than relief, she expresses bitterness toward Helia, noting that he had dumped her publicly at the precise moment Queen Titania was about to exile her, and that they could have been happily in love instead. She declares her intention to return every 100 years to taunt Helia, and departs without reconciliation.

Her departure, along with Helia's, breaks the apparent stalemate at the Grandfather Tree. Sleth later reveals there was never a true curse — Halisa and Helia had cursed each other through their mutual refusal to sacrifice themselves.

## Helia

Helia is a dryad bound to the easternmost tree under the Grandfather Tree's canopy as punishment. He was Halisa's plus-one at a Fey ball. When Halisa spoke freely and angered Queen Titania, the Queen demanded the plus-one present itself. Helia attempted to clarify he was not Halisa's date and dumped her on the spot, but Queen Titania exiled him anyway. He has spent his exile bickering with Halisa, contributing to the cancellation of the moon festival and the misery of the other dryads.

When Orsik-Thornacious arrives, Helia is delighted at the apparent presence of royalty and unleashes a stream of insults at the disguised sprite, hoping to provoke a reaction, but gives up when Orsik-Thornacious understands nothing and misinterprets his gestures. Helia offers Thistle a conditional deal: he will grant permission to search the tree if Thistle can convince Halisa to apologize.

When Orsik-Thornacious tells him he too is free to leave — presenting it as a royal pardon — Helia hesitates only a moment before flying off, apparently unsettled that Halisa is leaving. His reaction suggests complicated feelings toward Halisa despite his professed hatred of her. Sleth later notes there was no actual curse; Helia and Halisa had imprisoned each other through their mutual stubbornness.

## Sleth

Sleth is a dryad assigned to the westernmost tree under the Grandfather Tree's canopy. Unlike the other dryads, she is not trapped — she is a willing caretaker on what she considers a prestigious and wonderful assignment, describing the Grandfather Tree as "the greatest tree in the Universe." She is frustrated that Helia and Halisa have been neglecting their trees; she points out a branch growing in the wrong direction that will cause a terrible imbalance in thirty years, though Thistle cannot see the problem.

Sleth reveals the truth of the situation to Thistle: Queen Titania did not actually curse Helia and Halisa. There was no prison. The two dryads cursed each other through their refusal to sacrifice themselves, and their mutual hatred kept them trapped. She expresses relief when both dryads finally depart.

At the end of the session, Sleth is seen speaking with the son of Great Chief Boorvald Orcbane when he arrives furious at the theft of his tribe's artifact. She is present when the barbarian leader learns the torc has been taken. As the party sneaks away, Sleth remarks that "Prince Thornacious was so gracious and helpful," apparently unaware of the deception.

## Elderly Dryad (northern tree)

This unnamed elderly dryad with a grumpy, breathy voice is bound to the tree at the northern cardinal point. He knows Thistle's Uncle Joon and claims Joon owes him a good joke. When Thistle lands a fey joke about a dryad and the charm interaction with tree movement — one he used to share with his beloved — the elderly dryad laughs heartily.

Despite his amusement, he refuses Thistle's request for permission to search the Grandfather Tree unless Thistle can get Helia and Halisa to stop bickering, explaining that their constant fighting has caused the cancellation of the moon festival. He is uncertain of the precise mechanics of Queen Titania's supposed curse but relays what he understood: that if one dryad left, the other would be safe but the departing one would die. He concludes that neither loved the other enough to sacrifice themselves.

When both Helia and Halisa finally depart, the elderly dryad appears beside Sleth near the party and exclaims with relief, groaning "FINALLY LEFT!"

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session. He is referenced by Thistle when introducing himself to the elderly dryad at the northern tree. The elderly dryad recognizes Joon's name immediately, and Thistle's resigned response — "How much does he owe you?" — implies a pattern of Joon leaving debts behind. In this case, Joon owes the elderly dryad a good joke.

## Boorvald Orcbane's Son

The son of Great Chief Boorvald Orcbane is a young man leading a group of Tree Ghost Tribe barbarians who arrive at the Grandfather Tree just as the party is returning to the airship. He is speaking with Sleth when the party sneaks past. He becomes furious upon learning that the electrum torc — a precious artifact of his tribe — has been stolen from the tree's roots, and he promises vengeance. His name is not given. The party escapes before he can identify them as the thieves.

## Queen Titania

Queen Titania does not appear in this session. She is extensively referenced as the cause of the dryads' exile. She exiled Halisa for offering an honest opinion about her dress, and exiled Helia when he presented himself as Halisa's plus-one, despite his protests. She is also said to have told both dryads that if one left, the other would be safe but the departing one would die — a claim Sleth reveals was a lie, or at minimum a misunderstanding that the dryads imposed on themselves. Thistle reflects that "Queen Titania would pull this kind of stuff all of the time," suggesting familiarity with her manipulative behavior.
