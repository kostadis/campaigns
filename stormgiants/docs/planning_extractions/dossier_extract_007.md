## Chandra Stol

Chandra Stol is an elderly elven woman living in the abandoned village of Parnast who approaches the party when they begin knocking on the sealed stable doors housing the Wyvern. She initially tries to dissuade the group from disturbing the Wyvern, explaining that the last person to enter was killed over the course of days while the villagers were forced to listen. She reveals that the Cult of the Dragon left the Wyvern behind after their defeat, and that the villagers have come to rely on its presence as a deterrent — feeding it to keep it docile and using the threat of it to maintain local order.

When the party presses her for information about the Cult of the Dragon and specifically about Adimond Kruskas, she refuses to share anything until they retrieve her idol. She offers the party a job: a merchant named Azam is stranded in Vuerthyl with a religious artifact — an idol belonging to her shrine — that was sent into the desert during the war. She explains that a middleperson named Fiora was supposed to find her mercenaries for the job.

Thistle perceives that she is withholding information. She confirms this explicitly, stating that any information she has will only be exchanged once she has her idol back. Under continued pressure from Thistle, she volunteers one additional piece of information: the Cult of the Dragon brought a large flying castle over Parnast to move treasure, which Orsik recognizes as the castle that crash-landed near Waterdeep and helped trigger the alliance that defeated the Cult at the Well of the Worlds.

Chandra Stol is last seen in Parnast. She is the cleric of the Shrine of Axes, which currently lacks its idol — a statue of Angharradh. The barkeep Ragnar Redtooth refers to her by name and confirms she has been seeking someone to retrieve the idol for weeks.

## Ragnar Redtooth

Ragnar Redtooth is the heavyset barkeep and proprietor of The Golden Tankard tavern in Parnast, identifiable by his enormous muttonchop sideburns. He greets the party when they enter, noting that they have been speaking with him for two minutes without buying anything. Once the party purchases drinks, he becomes more talkative.

He confirms knowledge of Chandra Stol's quest for the idol, dismissively calling the mission foolish and noting that the merchant Azam refuses to come to Parnast due to dangers on the road. He also observes that while eastbound traffic has been very slow, significantly more people have been traveling east than west.

When shown the party's treasure map, Ragnar offers unhelpful speculation, suggesting the location could be the Silver Marches, the Spine of the World, the Icespires, or the Nethermountains — summarized by the party as "somewhere in the North." He accepts a gold piece for this information and, in gratitude, provides his finest sausage and cabbage. He then charges the party five gold for a dingy room and another five for use of sheets, defending the pricing by saying that when a poor man sees someone throwing coins around, he'd be a fool not to try to get some. Ragnar is last seen in Parnast at The Golden Tankard.

## Azam

Azam is a lean half-elven man with a long, craggy face, dark skin and hair reflecting Rashemi ancestry, and an impressive mustache noted by Vardis. He is a merchant currently stranded at the oasis settlement of Vuerthyl, having been hired to transport a large religious statue to Parnast. He is the patron-agent of the golden-scaled pseudodragon Hsing, whom he defers to for decisions and whose approval he seeks before showing the party the statue.

He meets the party at the tent tavern in Vuerthyl, having been directed to their table by a Dragonborn waiter. He explains that he has been contracted to deliver a statue to Parnast securely and that he does not know or particularly care whether the statue is magical — only that the job be completed. He is visibly frustrated by the statue's size, describing it as five feet tall, four feet wide, and very heavy.

He takes the party to a wagon where a stout half-orc woman is wrapping the statue in canvas, allowing them to examine it. He offers the party additional work from Hsing if they successfully complete this delivery mission. When Thistle asks about a Drake named Thule, Azam is visibly taken aback, leaving the response to Hsing.

Azam is last seen at Vuerthyl at the start of the party's engagement with the statue delivery mission.

## Hsing

Hsing is a golden-scaled pseudodragon who serves as Azam's patron. He lies draped across Azam's shoulders when the party first meets them, initially appearing asleep. He stirs, blinks with milky irises, sniffs the air, gives Azam a nod of approval when Azam asks whether the party can see the statue, and then returns to sleep.

Despite his sleepy demeanor, Hsing speaks directly to the party at least once, delivering what Azam treats as wisdom: "When there is a seed, it must be let to grow before it is killed," in reference to the consequence of the party failing the mission. When Thistle asks about the Drake named Thule, Hsing smiles and answers directly: "Where there is the yellow sky, there is the drake you are looking for." This is the only concrete lead on Thule's whereabouts provided in the session.

Hsing is last seen at Vuerthyl alongside Azam.

## Fiora

Fiora does not appear in this session. She is mentioned by Chandra Stol as the middleperson who was supposed to find mercenaries to retrieve the idol from Azam in Vuerthyl. No further details about Fiora's identity, location, or current status are provided.
