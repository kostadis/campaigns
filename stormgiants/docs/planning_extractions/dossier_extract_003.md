## Fiora Bendaega

Fiora Bendaega is a middle person — a broker who connects people needing jobs with people needing adventurers. She appeared at a location (implied to be a tavern or public meeting place) to recruit the party for a job involving honest tradespeople working the black road. She presented a letter of introduction for a quest, explaining that merchants pay her to distribute such letters to suitable adventurers.

During negotiations, she attempted to use empathy with Thistle by acknowledging the Cult of the Dragon as a tragedy and noting it was "terrible for business," which backfired when Thistle expressed a desire for revenge rather than sympathy. When the dwarf Orsik approached threateningly and asked how to take her job, she signaled her guard Diego to intervene. She used Diego's presence strategically, calling him over warmly and introducing him to the group to defuse tension while reasserting control of the situation.

She confirmed the job's timeline: the merchant would not be at the destination for three weeks, the journey takes two weeks, giving the party roughly a month. She knew the group would accept the job before they officially agreed.

After the encounter, she informed the group they would no longer be welcome at her current location and directed them to find her at Nonna's place for future work. Her relationship with Diego appears affectionate and loyal — she describes him as "so fond of me."

Her motivations are mercantile and professional. She shows no personal stake in the quest itself, only in placing adventurers and collecting her fee.

## Diego

Diego is Fiora Bendaega's personal guard. He appeared at her side during the recruitment meeting when Orsik became threatening, advancing on the group and growling, "are these guys giving you problems?" He backed down when Fiora signaled him with a wink after Orsik greeted him. Fiora described him as being "so fond of" her, suggesting personal loyalty beyond professional duty. His last known location is with Fiora at her recruitment venue.

## Leo

Leo is the proud owner and operator of the Lionel Coster franchise in Llorkh. He is described as immaculately dressed regardless of circumstance, with perfectly combed hair, a precisely trimmed beard, and unnaturally white teeth. He speaks in a gruff, husky voice.

He greeted the party upon entering his store and immediately stated he does not extend credit to first-time buyers. When Vardis pressed the point and then made to leave citing Diego, Leo redirected his attention to Thistle. He quoted 300 gold for a scroll of dispel magic. When Thistle pointed out scuff marks on the tube and a crease in the scroll, Leo reacted with genuine horror — he has been nominated for the "Lionel Coster best presentation award" and feared the committee would remove him from consideration if they learned of damaged merchandise. He reduced the price to 250 gold on the spot. The party also purchased a new bow and arrows from his store.

Leo's primary motivation appears to be professional pride in his store's presentation and standing within the Lionel Coster franchise system.

## Old Keller

Old Keller is a dwarf stonemason living in Llorkh. He did not appear in the previous session's events but is referenced as someone the party had a prior negative encounter with, specifically relating to "that thing in the keep" — an incident Keller describes as a disgrace to all dwarves, committed in a dwarf's place of work.

When Orsik arrived at his stonemason's hut calling him "kinsman" and appealing to dwarf solidarity, Keller shouted for him to stop and not take another step closer. He acknowledged dwarf solidarity in principle but stated it ended when someone committed such a disgrace. When Orsik asked how to redeem himself, Keller told him to stop doing "that thing" and to return when he was less likely to be goblin food, at which point Keller would tell him what redemption looks like in his eyes. His current status is at his stonemason's hut in Llorkh, unwilling to engage further with the party until the matter is addressed.

## Nonnie

Nonnie (also referred to as "Aunt Nonnie") is a short, ruddy-cheeked woman who runs a small inn in Llorkh with a kitchen and common room. She serves visitors who do not stay with the Iron Circle. She insists everyone call her "Aunt Nonnie" and is proud of her exclusively vegetarian menu.

She welcomed the party warmly when they entered, beckoning them to eat. She took their order of four cheese pies and returned twenty minutes later with pies described as perfectly cooked, tasty beyond belief, and filling. After dinner, she offered them rooms for the night at a cost, noting they were not fancy. She privately reflected that the group seemed like "a nice group of people."

Fiora Bendaega uses Nonnie's place as her base of operations and directed the party to find her there for future work. Nonnie's inn is also where a refugee woman fleeing Greenest was sheltering. Nonnie's relationship with her guests is described as treating everyone — including refugees — like human beings.

## The Refugee Woman from Greenest

This NPC is not named in this chunk. She appeared at Nonnie's inn, head down on the table, and overheard Vardis's comment about being treated like a human being. She lifted her head and noted that Nonnie treats everyone that way, including refugees like herself. She explained that she came from a town to the west called Greenest, which was destroyed by the Cult of the Dragon, and that she had fled east to put distance between herself and the dragons. Upon hearing rumors of giants in the area, she decided to reverse course and head back west to determine whether Greenest was safe and to help rebuild. Her current status is at Nonnie's inn, preparing to travel west.

## Veradda Store

Veradda Store does not appear in this chunk but is referenced as the source of notes that led the party to the treasure trove location in the northern Greypeaks. Her notes directed the group to a narrow path halfway up a mountain, which contained the secret doors, tunnel complex, and ice-covered chamber with frost giants and the cursed diamond. No other details about her are discussed in this chunk.

## The Entity in the Tower

The entity residing in the Tower of Green Flame observed the party's approach from within the tower. It spoke to itself throughout, revealing a composed but wary disposition. When the group approached, it noted their arrival and decided the "usual wards" would suffice as deterrent. When Thistle and Orsik continued advancing and found the door handle, it watched with interest. When Thistle touched the handle and both were hurt but not killed, the entity was pleased, treating the outcome as an adequate warning.

Its final noted reaction was sorrow upon realizing that the activation of the trap had paradoxically left the door able to be opened — an unintended consequence it had not anticipated. Its nature, identity, and motivations are not revealed. It resides in the Tower of Green Flame, a structure rumored to be the destroyed residence of a mighty wizard, damaged during the Spellplague. Locals avoid the tower due to fear of remaining arcane traps and wards. The tower is located near Llorkh.
