## Thistle Wendrod

Thistle Wendrod is traveling on the road to Xanthalar's Keep with the rest of the 3HPP. While walking, he reflects on a recent conversation at the Talking Troll tavern in Triboar. He had initiated the conversation with Urlam Stockspool about Adimond Kroskas, learning that Kroskas was a free agent (not a Cult of the Dragon member) who specialized in creating hybrid magical creatures, and that people who knew him are dead. Thistle was directed to Silverymoon and its magical libraries as the best source of further information. Thistle also asked Urlam about House Margaster and then, awkwardly, whether Urlam had any contraband the party could use to disgrace House Margaster — a question that nearly derailed the meeting and required Orsik's intervention. Thistle backpedaled by framing the contraband inquiry as a means to disgrace House Margaster rather than a business proposition.

## Urlam Stockspool

Urlam Stockspool is the local Zhentarim agent and owner of The Triboar Travelers inn, described as middle-aged, of conventional Illuskan stock, missing one eye, and wearing an eye patch bearing a red-emblazoned Zhentarim symbol. He met with Orsik at Orsik's request, greeting him warmly with a kiss on each cheek, and agreed to drinks at the Talking Troll — arriving with a crew of ten Zhentarim members whose tab Orsik ended up covering. When asked about Adimond Kroskas, Urlam confirmed Kroskas was a free agent unaffiliated with the Cult of the Dragon, described as someone hired to create hybrid magical creatures, and noted that those who knew him are dead. He suggested Silverymoon's magical libraries as the best place to find information. Regarding House Margaster, Urlam revealed they had coerced him into giving discounts and had cultists kill some of his guards when he objected. He stated that Zhentarim leadership considers House Margaster untouchable officially, but personally declared he wants them dead — carefully noting this is not an official Zhentarim position. He also revealed that Orthovir has a relationship with the Margasters, implying Orthovir may have set the party up. Urlam offered 200 gold to the party to deal with House Margaster, which he framed as a "friends and family discount." He became briefly furious when Thistle asked about contraband, and required Orsik's intervention and another round of drinks to smooth things over. His last known location is the Talking Troll tavern in Triboar.

## Adimond Kroskas

Adimond Kroskas does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. He was described by Urlam Stockspool as a free agent — not a member of the Cult of the Dragon — who could be hired to create hybrid magical creatures. He was characterized as "one messed-up dude." His associates and those who knew him are reportedly all dead. Urlam noted that the Zhentarim were involved in events surrounding the fall of the Cult of the Dragon, suggesting that senior Zhentarim figures might have information about Kroskas. Thistle Wendrod is actively trying to track him down. Silverymoon's magical libraries were recommended as the best avenue for further research.

## Orthovir

Orthovir does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. Urlam Stockspool reveals that Orthovir has a relationship with House Margaster, and implies — in reaction to Orsik's surprise — that Orthovir may have set the party up. Urlam noted that Orthovir likely told the party how to hurt House Margaster, but framed this with suspicion given his apparent ties to the Margasters.

## Kaelen

Kaelen is present at the Talking Troll during the meeting with Urlam Stockspool, serving wine to the group. Kaelen is called upon twice by Urlam — once to bring more wine and once for another round — both times at Orsik's expense. No other details about Kaelen are provided.

## Hyuth

Hyuth does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. Unla Kee reflects on Hyuth favorably, describing him as self-centered, obsessed with his own importance, and placing his needs above everyone else's — traits Unla Kee finds admirable or at least relatable. Unla Kee imagines that if the world doesn't end, he could retire similarly to Hyuth: living in a big house, terrorizing people, and involving himself in manic plots to destabilize the local community.

## Harshnag

Harshnag is a legendary, good-aligned frost giant who appears at the party's campfire on the road, introducing himself as "Harshnag, the Grim of Force Grey." He is described as looking like a typical frost giant but wearing plate armor and the skull of a white dragon as a helm, and carrying a formidable jagged axe. Orsik identifies him as hundreds of years old, once the largest member of Force Grey in Waterdeep, known both as a monster and a hero across the North, who wanders the wilderness fighting evil giants. Harshnag reveals that he has been searching for the party specifically, having been directed to them by Krowen Valharrow, who confirmed the party had agreed to investigate the giant crisis. He informs the group that the ordning has been broken, the storm giants can no longer keep lesser giants in check, and King Hekaton is missing — causing destabilizing giant activity across the North. He proposes guiding the party to the Eye of the All-Father, an ancient mountain temple under the Spine of the World built by his ancestors, which contains a divine oracle that may explain the giant crisis. He agreed to first accompany the party to Xanthalar's Keep to collect the bounty on Weevil, requesting a share of 100 gold from the expected 5,000 gold reward — a condition the party accepted. His current status is traveling with the 3HPP toward Xanthalar's Keep.

## Krowen Valharrow

Krowen Valharrow does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully referenced. Harshnag states that Krowen Valharrow told him the party had agreed to take on the quest to discover what was happening with the giants, which is why Harshnag sought them out.

## Weevil

Weevil does not appear in this chunk but is meaningfully referenced. Unla Kee refers to Weevil as "a scumbag" who is currently located in Xanthalar's Keep and is the subject of a bounty worth 5,000 gold that the party intends to collect. Harshnag agreed to accompany the party to claim this bounty before proceeding to the Eye of the All-Father.
