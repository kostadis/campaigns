Estrella does not appear in person beyond a single line but is present as the one piloting or navigating the cloud castle. She shouts out "We are approaching Yartar" as the 3HPP nears their destination, indicating she is serving as navigator or lookout aboard the castle during the voyage. Her last known position is aboard the cloud giant castle near the docks of Yartar.

## Imber

Imber does not appear in this chunk but is referenced in the opening summary as a merchant on the docks who provided the 3HPP with key intelligence prior to this session. He informed them that the Grand Dame was owned by Lord Khaspere Drylund and that Drylund was manipulating votes to become the next Waterbaron. His information directly motivated the party's approach to the Grand Dame as a source of intelligence connected to the disappearance of the Storm Giant King.

## Escarlotta

Escarlotta is present aboard the cloud giant castle as the party approaches Yartar and is responsible for piloting or landing the castle. She interrupts the party's planning to ask "Where do you want me to land the castle?" and later shouts "We have landed" once the castle is set down near the docks. She also critiques Orsik's attempt at sewing fancy clothes, tsking his efforts. Her last known position is aboard or near the castle at the Yartar docks.

## Lord Khaspere Drylund

Lord Khaspere Drylund is the owner of the Grand Dame gambling boat and a political figure in Yartar seeking to become the next Waterbaron. He appears in person in two key scenes in this chapter.

First, he arrives at the city gates with his retinue after the 3HPP lands the cloud castle near the docks. He intervenes to prevent the captain of the guard from taking hostile action, reasoning that if the party wanted to cause harm they would have done so already. He accepts the bottle of Champagne de Stomp from Orsik without recognizing its rarity and invites the party aboard the Grand Dame, promising comped food and alcohol. His primary motivation in this moment is clearly to extract as much gambling money from the party as possible.

Second, he hosts the 3HPP in his private office aboard the Grand Dame. The office is described as decorated in nouveau riche style — purple velvet curtains, scented candles in gaudy candelabras, an aquarium containing a pet octopus on a coral reef, a silk hammock, a writing desk, and a silver wine decanter. He picks up the bottle of Champagne de Stomp and nearly opens it before Orsik dissuades him, deciding to save it for when he becomes Waterbaron.

In conversation, Drylund reveals several things: he is actively campaigning to become Waterbaron of Yartar and views the party's arrival as a political endorsement; the current Waterbaron election is contingent on the current Waterbaron's death; he is aware of a rash of nobles disappearing and mentions that Lord Zymorven's son was freed by adventurers and magically returned to his father, which he calls a blow to the current Waterbaron; and he outlines three methods of winning Yartar elections — persuasion, bribery through promises, and holding loved ones hostage. He rejects Orsik's offer to kill his rivals as too dangerous due to guild and assassin reprisals.

He is receptive to the 3HPP's offer of informal alliance, promising that if he becomes Waterbaron, Yartar will be a friend to the 3HPP, and framing the party as having committed to protecting Yartar from cloud giants. He then suggests continuing the conversation in a different private location and says he needs the party to meet some associates, ushering them out at the chapter's end. His relationship with the Kraken Society or any deeper conspiracy is implied but not yet confirmed in this chunk.

## Pow Ming

Pow Ming is a Shou human who serves as the Grand Dame's majordomo or chief of operations, handling everything so that Lord Drylund can focus on his political role. He intercepts the 3HPP immediately upon boarding and asks how many chips they will be buying. When Orsik asks about the high-rollers table, Pow Ming directs them toward the Sava tables. He later overhears Vardis expressing a desire to meet Lord Drylund and arranges a private meeting in Drylund's office. He is polite but forceful in manner and appears to be the operational backbone of the Grand Dame.

## Lady Atalia

Lady Atalia is a beautiful woman in a gorgeous purple dress with black hair cropped short on the sides and a stunning necklace set with black stones. She is present in the Grand Dame's gambling hall playing Sava, the drow-derived chess variant. She is first observed defeating a white-haired noble, Lord Hubbard, who praises her skill and kisses her hand before departing.

She is a skilled telepath and uses telepathy during gameplay to read her opponents' minds, giving her a significant and hidden advantage at the Sava table. Orsik detects her mind-reading during his game against her; she wins despite appearing to be losing at first, countering Orsik's moves precisely when he moves in for the kill. When Orsik quietly accuses her of reading his mind, she denies it in an equally dangerous tone.

She attempts to manage the situation by projecting false vulnerability — describing herself as "a poor lady playing games of skill, chance, and treachery" — while clearly being a sophisticated and dangerous operative. When Orsik suggests a private conversation and mentions gold, she responds directly through telepathy: "We need to talk. Not here. Follow me when the boat returns to the docks. Nod if you understand me." This indicates she is willing to trade information but requires discretion and privacy.

Her motivations and affiliations are not yet revealed in this chunk. Thistle notes that she likely holds significant information worth trading, and the party plans to follow her when the boat docks. Her last known whereabouts are aboard the Grand Dame.

## Lord Hubbard

Lord Hubbard is a white-haired noble present on the Grand Dame who is playing Sava against Lady Atalia when the 3HPP first observe her. He loses the game, pushing a stack of golden geese across the table, and departs graciously, complimenting Lady Atalia's apparent precognition of his every move and kissing her extended hand before going to find another table. He serves primarily to establish Lady Atalia's reputation and ability at the Sava table. His current whereabouts are elsewhere aboard the Grand Dame.

## Lord Zymorven

Lord Zymorven does not appear in this chunk but is referenced meaningfully in conversation. Lord Drylund mentions that Lord Zymorven's son had been among nobles who disappeared, and that a group of adventurers freed him and magically returned him to his father — an event that was apparently reported in the papers and described by Drylund as "a terrible blow for the Waterbaron." This reference suggests that noble kidnappings are connected to political maneuvering around the Waterbaron election. Lord Zymorven himself is not described further.
