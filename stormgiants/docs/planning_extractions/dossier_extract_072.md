## Glazhael the Undying

Glazhael the Undying emphatically explained that her plan to defeat Sandesyl was a secret she would not share. The 3HPP had to rely solely on her word as an undying dracolich. Her appearance in this chapter is limited to the opening moments as the party departs her lair. Her motivations remain opaque, though the party reasons that narrow aligned interests make her a tentative ally. Last known location: her lair within the glacier of Blagothkus' castle.

## Sandesyl

Sandesyl does not appear directly in this chunk but is meaningfully discussed. Unla Kee reveals that Sandesyl is conducting broader experiments on the undead to create alternatives to Dracoliches. Because Sandesyl's undead creations are effectively liches — possessing phylacteries — they will return unless their remains are destroyed. This revelation drives the party's decision to burn all undead corpses they defeat. Sandesyl is also referenced as a potential threat who could animate skeletal remains found in the lower courtyard. Current whereabouts unknown.

## Blagothkus

Blagothkus is observed watching the party from the upper courtyard as they work in the lower courtyard. When Orsik dumps the skeletal remains overboard and the party burns them, Blagothkus shouts with genuine gratitude that he had been looking for someone to do exactly this for almost a year. When Orsik asks about undead in the courtyard, Blagothkus tries not to be condescending as he explains that undead prefer the dark and would not be found in direct sunlight. Last known location: the upper courtyard of his castle, observing the party.

## Unla Kee

Unla Kee is an active participant throughout the lower courtyard exploration. He calls out "Target practice!" and uses spells to burn the skeletal remains as they fall overboard. He leads the group to the port-side doorway and is visibly disappointed to find the barracks already looted. In the stables, he asks about dominating the undead wyverns, fires a 3rd-level Fireball at them, and repeatedly attempts to use the Wand of Wonder artifact over the objections of Orsik and Vardis. He successfully uses Magic Missile to damage one wyvern and kills the second with a necrotic-damage spell, smugly noting it is resistant not immune. He identifies the copper figurine of Yondalla and explains its ritual-casting property. He reveals knowledge of lichdom and phylacteries, explaining that Sandesyl's undead wyverns will return unless their remains are destroyed — crediting this knowledge to his time at the Nirany School of the Arcane. He defends the school passionately when mocked, explaining it offered strong mentorship and academic freedom despite not being the most prestigious institution. In the kitchen, his brash entrance provokes the undead. He is critically wounded by a specter after being charmed and left stunned and prone by the spectral raven's caw. Current status: critically wounded, stunned and prone in the abandoned kitchen.

## Vardis

Vardis participates throughout the chapter. As a cleric of Corellon, he initially expresses concern about the rights of the dead when Orsik orders the skeletal remains tossed overboard, but accepts the logic when reminded of the Dwarven tradition of burning corpses. He helps burn the falling remains with spells. In the wyvern fight, he coordinates tactics with Orsik — using Turn Undead to drive the wyverns apart and following up with Sacred Flame. He casts Detect Magic on the cork found in the saddlebags and humorously declares it may yet save the realms. He burns the undead wyvern remains overboard alongside Unla Kee. In the kitchen encounter, he uses a warning flare to thwart one specter. He is present but not noted as stunned or critically wounded at the end of the chapter. Current status: active, in the abandoned kitchen facing specters and the spectral raven.

## Orsik

Orsik takes a leadership role throughout the chapter, ordering the collection and disposal of skeletal remains and overruling Unla Kee's use of the Wand of Wonder in favor of more efficient spell choices. He empties a bag of holding to transport the corpses and dumps them overboard from the drawbridge gateway. He acknowledges Blagothkus' thanks and asks about the undead's whereabouts, accepting the cloud giant's answer about undead preferring darkness. In the wyvern fight, he strikes one with his glowing green blade after Vardis' Sacred Flame illuminates it, then dashes toward the stern wyvern. He dissuades Unla Kee from using the Wand of Wonder twice and is surprised when Unla Kee's necrotic spell kills the second wyvern. He finds 120 gold pieces and a bag of sweets in saddlebags, and dismisses the cork as useless. He correctly identifies the spectral raven as the real threat from childhood stories, strikes it first but fails to kill it, and is subsequently struck by two specters draining his life force. He is then charmed by the raven's caw and left stunned and prone. Current status: charmed, stunned and prone in the abandoned kitchen, having suffered life drain from specters.

## Thistle

Thistle observes the skeletal remains being packed into the bag of holding with quiet bewilderment. Using True Seeing, he identifies that not all the corpses in the barracks were dwarves — some were Dragonwings, high-ranking cult members with the ability to fly, suggesting the castle changed hands from the cult to its current occupants. He correctly identifies the wyverns in the stables as undead. In combat, one arrow hits the stern wyvern but deals lightning damage, to which it is immune. He moves for a better shot but is blocked by stable cover. He strikes the fleeing wyvern with two arrows and his companion Thuul burns it with acid. He quickly examines the remaining gatehouse rooms and finds them empty. He discovers a copper figurine of the goddess Yondalla in the stables. In the kitchen, he is present when the specters and the spectral raven attack. Current status: active, in the abandoned kitchen facing the undead.
