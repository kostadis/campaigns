## Coniferous Dryad

An Unseelie dryad whose leaves and branches resemble a conifer. During the battle, he opened by casting Entangle on the party, trapping Thistle, Vardis, and Unla Kee. He used tree-step teleportation repeatedly throughout the fight, reappearing 50 feet away after being hit by Unla Kee's magic missile, then teleporting again to appear next to Unla Kee and charm him into sitting down and drinking. He later teleported to the other side of the grove, some 40 feet away. He was ultimately killed by Orsik, who knocked him prone and cut him in half. His death ended the charm effect on Unla Kee.

## Deciduous Dryad

An Unseelie dryad with a deciduous appearance. During the battle, she attempted to charm Orsik by calling out that the fight was not his and that the Unseelie were his friends — successfully causing him to sit down. She was struck by Thistle's arrow and covered in acid by Thuul, which broke her concentration and freed Orsik. She was finished off by Unla Kee's magic missiles. Her death did not break the charm on Unla Kee, which was maintained by the Coniferous Dryad.

## Unseelie Halfling

A halfling mage in a dirty, unseemly robe who was part of the Unseelie group playing cards. He cast magic missile at Orsik, and later at Wallace. He was highlighted by Vardis's Faerie Fire, covered in an eerie pink glow. He was cut deeply by Wallace's sword and finished off by Wallace during the battle.

## Oblivillish

An Unseelie-adjacent figure who was present in the clearing during the battle. He claimed during the fight that the lion and bear had eaten the stag, and that he had been framed for it — a claim Vardis accepted. After the entanglement spell ended, he celebrated his freedom by flitting about. He kissed Blasius and attempted to hug Orsik, Vardis, Thistle, and Wallace, all of whom rejected his advances. When the group returned to Prince Thornacious, Blasius credited Oblivillish with commanding the heroes against the Unseelie agents and leading their defeat, which was confirmed by the party. As a result, Prince Thornacious reinstated Oblivillish as his Chief of Staff.

## Blasius

A satyr who was in a drunken stupor during the battle and woke up afterward to offer drinks, celebrating the Dryads' deaths. He announced happily that he was no longer Chief of Staff to Prince Thornacious. He gave Unla Kee a ring of jumping, calling him his "true honored drinking friend," then fell asleep. Later, he opened a Fey portal to bring the group before Prince Thornacious, citing his Chief of Staff status as a perk that allowed bypassing trickster fey. Before the Prince, he recanted his accusations against Oblivillish, claiming he had been drunk and possibly joking, and attributed the stag's death to the bear and lion. He credited Oblivillish with leading the heroes against the Unseelie threat. Following the Prince's decree, Blasius was reinstated as the Prince's Satyr. He then organized a vision quest involving drinking and fey communion, through which the heroes learned to neutralize poison once per quest. He noted the ability "only works once per vision quest."

## Gralm

An ettin encountered after the battle. Orsik's persuasion, their victory in drinking games, and a magnim sound-canceling headphone were sufficient to convince Gralm to sign a contract agreeing to deal with Bad Fruul. He signed the contract in Grolm's precise script. Afterward, he invited the group to stay for a small celebration and offered them drinks from fine crystals filled with various brews he had manufactured, calling Unla Kee "a civilized adventurer" when the gnome requested a drink. Last known location: his own territory, where the contract was signed.

## Prince Thornacious

Does not appear on-screen in the battle scene but is central to the Return of the Spy sequence. When the group arrived before him via Fey portal, he initially called Blasius "traitorous scum." After Blasius recanted his accusations, credited Oblivillish, and the party confirmed the account, the Prince reinstated Blasius as his Satyr and Oblivillish as his Chief of Staff. He scoffed at the explanation about the bear and lion but accepted the party's confirmation. He asked Thistle directly whether Blasius's account was accurate.

## Azam

A member of the Lord's Alliance and agent of the SEER. He had been waiting in Parnast for two weeks since the party left, spending his time at the Golden Tankar tavern, attended by Ragnar and Elspeth. He had grown convinced the party had failed and that the SEER would be disappointed. His reverie was broken by children shouting that Thistle was coming. He met the group in the main square of Old Parnast during the Festival of Greengrass, congratulated them on securing their rewards, and gave a magical potion and a pouch of gold to Unla Kee. When Unla Kee honestly reported a deal for 200 GP rather than keeping the difference, Azam was impressed and approached him with a recruitment pitch for the Lord's Alliance. He described the Alliance as the only faction in the North devoted to order, centralization, and safety, dismissing the Harpers as anarchists, the Zhentarim as parasitic trade monopolists, the Order of the Gauntlet as paranoid, and the Emerald Enclave as anti-development. Unla Kee refused the offer with a burp. Azam's last known location is Parnast.

## Ragnar

Does not appear directly but is referenced as someone Azam left coin for at the Golden Tankar before walking out to meet the returning party. No other details provided.

## Elspeth

Does not appear directly but is referenced alongside Ragnar as staff at the Golden Tankar, for whom Azam left coin before going to meet the party. No other details provided.
