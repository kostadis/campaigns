## Princess Halani Meliame

Princess Halani Meliame is an elf from the Moonwood who was found captive among the Yakfolk in their tent, along with her companions Jevin and Vandar. She identifies herself to the party after the Yakfolk are defeated, offering a great reward if the 3HPP return her and her companions to the Moonwood.

She speaks with Thistle during an interrogation, explaining that the Yakfolk had used their ritual of possession on several of her guards to capture her. She is escorted up a rope to the party's flying castle, with warnings to stay in sunlit areas.

Before departing, she provides the party with useful intelligence: there is a doorway to the north containing an elevator leading down into the giants' home, which corresponds to Ironslag. She also reveals that the Yakfolk used their possession ritual to take over some dwarves from Citadel Adbar and kill the dwarven king.

When Thistle asks about the political situation regarding Silverymoon, Halani expresses bitter frustration at Silverymoon's betrayal of the Silver Marches in their hour of need. She states that Silverymoon abandoned the region for selfish reasons, leaving it exposed to raids, and declares that its era of dominance is over. She indicates that a new organization will rise to protect the people of the world, though she catches herself and walks back the statement, claiming she has been imprisoned and does not know where things are moving. She speculates, seemingly in anger rather than with certainty, that agents of Silverymoon may have been behind the Yakfolk's plots.

Her last known whereabouts are the 3HPP's flying castle, instructed to remain in brightly lit areas.

## Uncle Joon

Uncle Joon does not appear in this session but is referenced twice by Thistle. First, Thistle recalls that Uncle Joon had described Ironslag as being situated at the base of a 500-foot-high mountainside cliff, with a pair of 50-foot tall adamantine doors sealed by airtight magic valves as its entrance. Second, while preparing to fight the fire salamanders, Thistle recalls something Uncle Joon had said — not about the creatures' fire damage or cold vulnerability, but that fire salamanders are exceptionally skilled smiths.
