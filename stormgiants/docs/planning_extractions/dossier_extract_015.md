## Oblivillish

Oblivillish the Royal Pixie is a central NPC throughout this chapter, accompanying the party through multiple scenes and driving several plot threads.

He is in exile, having been banished from Prince Thornacious's court over a stag he killed and ate, not knowing the stag belonged to the Prince. He is aware that Thornacious may not be pleased to have him back, and privately hopes to find a way out of his exile. When Thistle suggests groveling as a solution, Oblivillish is initially appalled — "Me grovel?" — as he considers himself noble and above such acts. After some prodding, he attempts to practice groveling on his knees, awkwardly, while the rest of the group suppresses laughter.

He agrees to Thistle's cover story: that he is the last survivor of his group, wiped out by Bad Fruul's goons, and that the party saved him and seeks to offer their swords to Gralm. He quickly embraces the deception plan and leads the group past the guards into Gralm's encampment, having donned goblin attire. He is described as a spy, and successfully gets the group inside.

Inside Gralm's hut, Oblivillish whispers to Thistle that Prince Thornacious has been foolishly expanding his sphere of control into territory Gralm considers his own. He warns that if the music problem is not resolved, Gralm will not join the alliance against Bad Fruul, and a hill giant will kill the Fey. This is a moment of practical political insight from him, despite his pomposity.

When the party encounters Blasius near the pond, Oblivillish reacts with hostility — a Sprite voice (Oblivillish's) shouts that Blasius is why he was banished and demands an attack. He is then put to sleep by Unla Kee. While he is sleeping, Blasius reveals that Oblivillish's exile was Blasius's fault: drunk, Blasius falsely told Thornacious that Oblivillish killed the stag.

Once Oblivillish wakes, he and Blasius have an emotional reunion, calling each other "dear friend" and "brother" and embracing, to the eye-rolling of the rest of the group. He acknowledges he wanted Blasius dead but says seeing his dear friend die would make him sad. He then pivots to authority, commanding Thistle as a royal Fey to kill the Dryads, arguing that they are Unseelie because anyone can swear fealty to the Gloaming Court. He explains that Unseelie loyal to the Queen of Air and Darkness are enemies of the Seelie, and that the Unseelie stench has been turning Ettins against the Fey. He frames the killing of the Unseelie as both a duty to Queen Tatiana and the path to restoring his position at court.

His current motivation is to return to Prince Thornacious's service with glory. His plan depends on Blasius blaming the dead stag on the Unseelie, and on the party eliminating the Unseelie threat as a heroic act credited to him.

## Pandi

Pandi is a small dog with a vibrantly colored mane who appears inside a puzzle room accessed through a glowing blue door in the Forgotten Forest. Pandi communicates telepathically — specifically, Thistle hears yipping and Pandi's voice in his head. Pandi is enthusiastic, lonely, and obsessed with word games and art.

Pandi greets the travelers excitedly, declaring they were expected and that they must play a word game before being allowed through. The game requires the party to name what they seek at that very moment. Thistle guesses "a grail" and then "a hallway," earning dismissal: "They don't teach fairies anything these days in fairy school." Vardis guesses "keep walking through an exit." Pandi grows frustrated and tries to help. Unla Kee eventually answers correctly with "a passage," delighting Pandi, who leaps around the room in joy.

The second challenge Pandi presents is a Tangram puzzle. Pandi explains that their paws are not good for picking up tiny objects and asks the party to assemble the pieces into a picture for the room's walls. The party solves it, the picture attaches to the wall, and Pandi howls with joy, declaring that the gift of passage is given in return.

Pandi is associated with the Forgotten Forest, which appears to have sent the glowing door as a shortcut or aid to the party. Pandi's room is a cold, fifteen-foot square space with blue marble walls and plush velvet cushion floors, filled with disembodied giggles and guffaws.

Last known location: inside the puzzle room beyond the Forgotten Forest door.

## Gralm

Gralm is an ettin — a two-headed giant — who leads a large encampment in or near the Forgotten Forest. Gralm is described as slightly short for an ettin at only nine feet, wearing a simple loincloth and deerskin boots, and seated on a ragged wooden throne in a hut over thirty feet tall with a floor made entirely of skulls.

Gralm has two distinct heads with separate personalities. The blonde-haired head is patient, articulate, and politically minded, conducting most of the negotiation. The bald head is aggressive, sleep-deprived, easily irritated by noise, and primarily interested in eating and quiet. The bald head repeatedly interrupts with shouts like "No. Less talk. More eat!" and "YOU LOUD! MUSIC EVERYWHERE! QUIET!"

Gralm knew the party was coming before they arrived. The blonde-haired head conducts the meeting with interest, hearing out the alliance proposal against Bad Fruul. Gralm acknowledges Bad Fruul is an enemy and that his power wanes while Gralm's waxes, agreeing the time to strike is now. However, Gralm requires proof of worth through feats of strength and drinking before formalizing any alliance.

The drinking contests Gralm hosts are: Pixie Sangria, The Everyday Ettin (dark ale with orange hints), The Ettin Grinder (crème liqueur with sour juice), and the Harpy's Dream (a violent frothing substance). The party passes all of them without obvious effect, with Thistle developing hiccups after the Ettin Grinder. The physical contests are: a deadlift of a boar, knocking down a brick wall with their feet, and a boar dog eating contest. Unla Kee wins all three through clever use of magic (Reduce on the boar, Thunderwave for the wall) and sheer appetite (24 boar dogs).

Gralm is delighted by Unla Kee's victories and grants provisional alliance trust. His condition for full alliance against Bad Fruul is that the party either bring him four heads for his collection or convince the nearby Fey to stop playing music near his tent. The bald head's sleep deprivation, caused by Fey music from Thornacious's expanding territory, is resolved temporarily when Unla Kee crafts ettin-sized earmuffs from magmin shells. The bald head immediately falls asleep wearing them; the blonde-haired head puts his on briefly, then removes them to continue the conversation.

The encampment is massive, heard from a mile away, with bonfires, ramshackle buildings, countless guards, goblins, hobgoblins, and at least one hill giant spotted under construction. Gralm's champion is a nearly thirteen-foot ogre named Grindle.

Last known location: his hut at the center of his encampment.

## Grindle

Grindle is a massive ogre, nearly thirteen feet tall, who serves as Gralm's champion in contests of strength. Grindle enters the tent at Gralm's command to compete against the party. Upon noticing Thistle, Grindle approaches and asks "toy for me?" Gralm immediately orders Grindle to stand down, saying they are not a toy. Grindle pouts.

Grindle competes in the physical contests against the party and loses all three to Unla Kee. After the boar lifting contest, Grindle howls but Gralm rules in Unla Kee's favor. After the wall-smashing contest, Grindle shouts "unfair! Unfair!" Grindle pouts and screams repeatedly throughout.

Last known location: Gralm's hut in the encampment.

## Blasius

Blasius is a Satyr encountered near a small pond in the Forgotten Forest, drinking from a hip flask. He is accompanied by a giant eagle with a majestic brown and golden plumage and an ornamental leaf necklace. Blasius recognizes both Thistle and Oblivillish when he hears the approaching group.

Blasius is the direct cause of Oblivillish's exile. He confesses that while drunk, he told Prince Thornacious that Oblivillish killed the stag. He is guilt-ridden about this and has been miserable ever since, forced to serve as a de facto chief of staff under Thornacious — attending meetings, tracking decisions, following up on action items, and being screamed at when he forgot things, all without being able to drink. His private thought is "I'm going to die. Better dead than a chief of staff."

When Oblivillish attacks him, the rest of the party intervenes. Oblivillish is put to sleep by Unla Kee. Once the immediate crisis passes, Blasius confesses his guilt openly. He proposes a plan: if the party kills the Unseelie who have been plaguing the region, he can blame the dead stag on them, claim he was drunk and confused, and Thornacious will take Oblivillish back — restoring Blasius to his preferred role as a carefree Satyr.

Blasius reveals that Unseelie have been in the area, accompanied by carnivorous creatures, and that they sing and play music at all hours, which he connects to the Fey music disturbing Gralm. He leads the party to a large clearing where a group of Dryads and seedy halflings are playing cards around a table surrounded by rotted stuffed animals, with a broken harp lying nearby. He identifies the Dryads as Unseelie and insists they must be dealt with.

Blasius is warm toward Unla Kee, offering him alcohol and hugging him enthusiastically. He is dismissive of court protocol interruptions from Thistle, insisting a Satyr telling a story of "tragedy, betrayal, and woe" must not be interrupted.

Last known location: the clearing with the Unseelie card players, having led the party there.

## Prince Thornacious

Prince Thornacious does not appear in this chapter but is frequently discussed and drives much of the plot.

He is the Fey prince whose stag Oblivillish unknowingly killed, leading to Oblivillish's exile. He considers all stags to belong to him and made this known explicitly, though Oblivillish claims to have missed the royal memorandum. Thornacious forced Blasius into a chief of staff role in Oblivillish's absence, which Blasius found miserable.

According to Blasius, Thornacious has ambitions to build a great Fey empire and destroyed a Fey circle in service of that goal, declaring they would "build an empire or die trying." He has been expanding his sphere of control into territory overlapping with Gralm's encampment, which is the direct cause of Fey music disturbing Gralm and destabilizing the region.

Thistle attempts to pitch Thornacious's ambitions being redirected to the High Forest with Star Mountain at its center as a grander, more fitting realm for the Prince. Blasius is skeptical but acknowledges a good gift and a credible story — such as blaming the Unseelie for the stag's death — could win Thornacious's favor and restore Oblivillish to court.

Thornacious is not described as being physically present in the Forgotten Forest. Last known association: his Fey court, location unspecified.
