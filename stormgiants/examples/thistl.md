## From Scene 01 (Arrival at Iymrith's Lair): the dragon greeting, performer-light

The desert had changed. Or maybe I had.

The last time I'd looked out at that horizon — flat, bleached, ancient — I'd been running *toward* something. Now I was arriving. There's a difference, and I felt it in my chest like a second heartbeat: Laela was in there. Somewhere past those walls, past whatever Iymrith had built to impress people who could level mountains, Laela was waiting.

I needed to be very careful. I needed to be very calm.

"So, you've arrived."

The blue dragon was magnificent in the way that things engineered to destroy you are magnificent. She filled the space she inhabited — not just physically, though she was vast, but in the particular way of creatures who have had millennia to perfect the impression of absolute authority. I made myself breathe normally.

"Why, yes," I said, and I kept my voice easy, performer-light. "We're here to fulfill our bargain."

She swept a claw in something between a gesture and a warning, and the scale of what she'd assembled pressed down on all of us at once. A great many powerful guests would attend. It was really important that this go off without a hitch. The subtext was obvious: she didn't need to explain what happened to performers who disappointed audiences this dangerous.

And then she said it plainly, because Iymrith is the kind of creature who prefers her threats unwrapped.

"I don't mean to be rude or anything, but I do need this to happen tomorrow, and if it doesn't happen, well, you know, someone's gonna have to be burned to a crisp."

Orsik asked if he could be the one burned.

I should not have found that as funny as I did. But there's something about a dwarf in full plate cheerfully volunteering for incineration that works on me every time, regardless of context. Iymrith, to her credit, understood exactly what she was looking at and refused him the satisfaction.

## From Scene 01 (Arrival at Iymrith's Lair): the bones, the precision instinct

"Every time I want to think about what a success I am," she said at last, "I just fantasize about that meal. Is there something else I should have done?"

"Eh, it's alright."

"Because you don't have the bones," I said.

I'm not sure why I said it. Some instinct toward precision, maybe. Some reflexive need to observe the gap in the argument. Iymrith paused, and for a moment I thought I'd miscalculated.

"No. No, I do not have the bones. Aw." A brief, strange almost-silence. Then: "Of course it wasn't quite high school, it was more — you know — a thing."

She led us into the lair after that, moving through corridors designed to impress. Everything here was scaled for something larger than human, larger than giant. Built to remind you of your smallness and be enjoyed for it. I catalogued details the way I always do in places I might need to escape quickly: the angles, the passages, the light.

## From Scene 01 (Arrival at Iymrith's Lair): Laela in the contraption

And then there she was.

Laela. In a contraption I couldn't immediately parse — magical, clearly; constructed specifically to hold her; inescapable, from the look of it. She looked up and saw me and —

The sappiness. I know. I know Vardis had something to say about it, and I know certain other party members made certain other faces. I caught Orsik, through my peripheral vision, performing some form of tolerance.

"We're happy for you, man," he said.

What I will say is only that she was there, and she was herself, and that the particular quality of relief that moved through me then was different from every other fear I'd been carrying. Sharper. Warmer. The kind of feeling that doesn't announce itself — it just arrives, and you realize you'd been bracing for it for longer than you knew.

I had to think. I had to be useful.

Iymrith let us have a moment. Then she started tapping her claws, and the tapping was the kind of sound made by creatures who have had millennia to develop implications.

## From Scene 06 (Iymrith's Grand Opening): the smile that costs

The lightning bolt came first.

Iymrith threw it straight up into the sky — not at anyone, not yet — just to open the evening. A declaration. The crowd understood it perfectly. Around the amphitheatre, the other dragons answered her: gouts of fire, arcs of acid, the whole assembly exhaling violence in appreciation. The giants pounded their fists against the stone until I could feel it in my sternum. Every faction arrayed in their proper place, the Mind Flayers hovering in absolute silence at the periphery, untouched by the spectacle, untouchable — no one went near them, and they seemed to prefer it that way.

I was smiling. I had been smiling since we arrived. The smile cost me something I haven't named yet.

Before the main event, Iymrith paused, turned to address the audience with what I can only describe as the air of a host managing a particularly carnivorous dinner party, and said: "I know you are all here to see my greatest creation. I just want to get some minor bureaucratic things out the way. There will be no sampling of any of my creations."

Orsik, bless him, said: "No what?"

"Yes," Iymrith said.

Vardis, apparently under the impression he'd wandered into a music venue, offered: "Like remixing?"

"No," she said. "Chewing."

She turned — and I noticed her turn specifically, her eyes moving to Flume with the particular weight of a warning that had been issued before — and said: "Last time I did one of my exposés, there was hearts everywhere. It was a mess." Then, directly to Flume: "I especially be new."

Flume waved her claw in response. The gesture said *yes, yes, I remember, don't fuss* in a way that communicated she absolutely did not intend to comply, and Iymrith, apparently deciding this was acceptable, moved on.

I kept smiling.

Somewhere in this lair, behind some door or wall or enchantment I hadn't found yet, Laela was in a cage. I knew it the way I knew the shape of her voice — as a fact that lives in the body, not the mind. She was here. And I was here. And between those two truths was everything I had to perform my way through.

## From Scene 06 (Iymrith's Grand Opening): the speech, the closing breath

She closed without hesitation: "You invested not in my vision, but in your own fears. You placed your trust in the laws of the world that I was meant to rewrite. You failed to see what I have always known. That impossible is a word spoken only by those too weak to claim what is theirs."

The assembled crowd — dragons, giants, aberrations, factions I didn't have names for — responded. They always respond when someone tells them they were right about everything.

The pause that followed was theatrical and deliberate. Iymrith let it breathe. Then:

"And now, I present to you — the perfect assassin. The fairy dragon."

Orsik said something under his breath. I didn't catch it. I was looking at the stage, at the space where Laela was about to appear, and everything I felt was very far below my face, nowhere near the surface, nowhere near the smile.

I kept smiling.
