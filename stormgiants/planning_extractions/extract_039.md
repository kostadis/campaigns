## NPC Activity

**The Weevil (Worvil Forkbeard)**
- Was the target of Xaltivar's diversionary attack on Xanthalar's Keep; bugbears attempted to extract him as a prisoner.
- Attempted to bribe Orsik during transit with an offer to share a method for robbing Mirabar mining caravans, claiming knowledge of a secret caravan pattern.
- Recognized Orsik by reputation and used his name directly.
- Upon arrival in Mirabar, attempted to deny his identity to Herald but was identified by his distinctive shield dwarf beard braiding.
- Sentenced by Herald to labor in the mines for life.
- Revealed under interrogation threat that he had cracked a code relating to caravan movements; Herald indicated the city would extract this information by force.

**Herald (Sergeant of the Guard, Axe of Mirabar)**
- Received the Weevil from the party at the city gates.
- Identified the Weevil by his beard braiding despite the prisoner's denials.
- Paid out 5,000 gold pieces marked with the symbol of Mirabar to the party.
- Expressed intent to extract the Weevil's caravan code information through coercive interrogation.
- Sentenced the Weevil to permanent mine labor.

**Harshnag the Grim**
- Escorted the party along the Long Road North to Mirabar, providing extensive geographical and historical briefings on the city.
- Provided an account of the ancient enmity between giants and dragons, attributing the sundering of the Ordning to the power vacuum created by Tiamat's attempt to seize power.
- Camped outside Mirabar during the party's downtime, prohibited from entering the city.
- Received and reflected on multiple intelligence reports regarding giant activity across the North: Frost Giants raiding Icewind Dale and reportedly laying waste to Bryn Shander in search of an artifact; Hill Giants attacking Goldenfields; Stone Giants attacking surface settlements; Fire Giants under Duke Zalto scouring the land for an artifact.
- Expressed serious alarm, particularly regarding Stone Giant surface attacks.

**Gale Wonderbrow (Merchant)**
- Described as athletic with an imposing frame, short hair, and a strong jaw.
- Traded information about Laela in exchange for Thistle organizing an elaborate wedding, which Thistle executed to Gale's satisfaction.
- Provided a report of a fairy matching Laela's description who massacred a group of brigands surrounding a caravan, leaving only herself and a young man cowering in a wagon alive.

**Yip Hammaver**
- 32-year-old bachelor, medium height, notable for strange red eyes.
- Member of House Hammaver, a major mercantile house of Mirabar.
- Befriended Orsik while he was disguised as "John Husteem" of House Husteem.
- Downplayed Tack Proudfoot as a threat prior to the duel, failing to disclose that Tack was a knight of the elite guards.
- Was present at the duel and expressed delight at the non-violent resolution.

**Tack Proudfoot Esquire (also referred to as Percival)**
- Minor noble involved in a duel with "John Husteem" (Orsik in disguise).
- Arrived at the duel in exceptionally fine armor; revealed to be a trained knight of the elite guards, contrary to Yip's characterization.
- Betrothed to a member of Clan Battlehammer, one of Mirabar's leading houses.
- Declared honor satisfied when Orsik refused to fight under sanctuary, then embraced him and proposed introducing the "single John Husteem" to eligible nobles of Mirabar.

**Hork Gethorn (Meat Pie Shop Owner)**
- Posted a notice seeking a fighter to deal with an infestation beneath his shop.
- Described as heavyset with a face resembling a meat pie.
- Hired Vardis, who resolved the infestation; paid Vardis a reward and treasure.

**Laela (Referenced, Absent)**
- A fairy; Thistle's lost love.
- Confirmed by the Axe of Mirabar to have passed through the city and headed east toward the Lurkwood.
- Confirmed by Gale Wonderbrow to have single-handedly slaughtered brigands who surrounded a caravan, leaving only herself and one surviving young man.

**Fred and George (Referenced, Absent — Reporting to Party)**
- Guards who held Xanthalar's Keep during Xaltivar's attack; submitted a report on events there.
- Withheld from the 3HPP the fact that Lord Narbeck Horn would identify the Weevil by beard braiding, preserving the capture.
- Were instrumental in convincing the 3HPP to wait until the Lieutenant arrived.

**Narbeck Horn (Referenced, Absent)**
- A lord referenced by the 3HPP who was reportedly at risk of being deceived by the Weevil's mistaken-identity claim.
- Confirmed (via Fred and George's knowledge) to be capable of identifying the Weevil through shield dwarf beard braiding.

## Revealed Information

- **The Weevil's value to Xaltivar**: Xaltivar's attack on Xanthalar's Keep was entirely a ruse to extract the Weevil — suggesting Xaltivar has a specific use for him, possibly related to the caravan code the Weevil claims to possess.
- **The Weevil's caravan code**: The Weevil claims to have independently deciphered a secret pattern governing Mirabar's mining caravans, sufficient to reliably rob them. The Axe of Mirabar now knows this and intends to extract the method.
- **Laela's trajectory**: Confirmed to have passed through Mirabar heading east into the Lurkwood. She demonstrated lethal combat ability, massacring bandits threatening a caravan single-handedly.
- **Giant Ordning collapse causation (per Harshnag)**: Tiamat's bid for power weakened the North; the Giant lords exploited the resulting vacuum to shatter the Ordning. This is Harshnag's framing of the current giant crisis.
- **Stone Giant ideology**: Harshnag explicitly characterizes Stone Giants as viewing the surface world as an abomination requiring destruction — distinct from other giant types who are raiding for material or artifact-related goals.
- **House Husteem reputation**: Belligerent, forceful, mercenary-backed Waterdhavian house whose rough culture made Orsik's disguise plausible among Mirabar's elite.
