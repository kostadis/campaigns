## NPC Activity

**Lord Zymorven**
Appeared at Zymorven Hall. Received the party and learned the full account of his son Harthal's imprisonment, the fake-disguise gambit, and the vampire jailer. Expressed relief at Harthal's return, paid the party their promised reward, and acknowledged he should avoid the vampire in Yartar. Noted that his own adventuring days are over. Observed Orsik carrying the Zymorven family sword and wished him better luck with it than his son had.

**Harthal Zymorven**
Traveled with the party as an escort/ward. Hid behind Vardis during the crag cat ambush. Arrived at Zymorven Hall with the party and was reunited with his father. Expressed genuine enthusiasm for the adventure he had experienced.

**Matronly Orc (unnamed)**
Encountered on the Rauvin Road between Everlund and Silverymoon. Revealed that her tribe had been displaced from their homestead by giant activity and was seeking a new settlement location. Challenged Thistle's assumptions about orcs. Offered Thistle berry jam as consolation after his archery failure.

**Teenage Orc (unnamed)**
Member of the displaced orc tribe. Challenged Thistle to an archery contest, using a thrown rock as a target. Won the contest and gained local renown among the tribes as a result.

**Kaelen**
Present at the Talking Troll in Triboar managing the play production. Revealed to have borrowed 5,000 gold pieces in the party's name, purchased the theater property outright rather than renting, and written a script that was structurally incoherent. Accepted Unla Kee's script revisions, including the "play within a play" framing device. Was ecstatic at the play's success. Revealed post-performance that the original play was intentionally designed as a summoning ritual and revenge fantasy: he planned to summon a demon to slaughter the audience as vengeance against a theater-going public he felt had never appreciated his creative genius. Reversed position entirely upon discovering he is now famous.

**Urgala**
Appeared at the Northshield House in Triboar. Informed the party of Kaelen's 5,000 gold debt taken in their names, owed to the Hand of Yartar, the Zhentarim, and local criminals. Provided intelligence on Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation — its nature, that a kobold tribe had stolen it from one of Geoffrey's messengers, and its location in the Dessarin Hills south of Yartar. Implied a non-innkeeper past when pressed on how she knew this. Confirmed the play was a hit and that repeat performances were immediately in demand.

**Everard Barney**
Director of the play at the Talking Troll. Heavy-set man in a multicolored shirt and purple scarf. Introduced himself to Thistle as fully committed to Kaelen's vision. Revealed he is from a family of bookkeepers in Waterdeep and fears having to return to accounting if the play fails. Accepted Thistle's directorial guidance, particularly regarding lead actor Amy's staging and the handling of drunk actors.

**Amy (lead actor)**
Lead actor in Kaelen's play. Status-driven and evasive about making firm commitments. Resisted direction from Kaelen but capitulated to the false authority of "Geoff Mamet" (Orsik in disguise). Delivered the improved final performance successfully. Was unaware that Geoff Mamet was Orsik.

**Bob Saget**
Demon summoned at the conclusion of the play. Appeared on stage as a purple-clad, jagged-toothed demonic figure with animated embroidery and a top hat. Introduced himself as a tormentor of kings who killed their jesters, specializing in "dad jokes." States his pronouns as he/him. Expressed delight at being summoned from the Abyss and welcomed the opportunity to perform for new audiences. Confirmed the party summoned him and that he has not been summoned in eons. Declined combat with Orsik and expressed enthusiastic interest in continuing as the play's star attraction.

**Chancellor Geoffrey (referenced, absent)**
Referenced by Urgala as the original owner of Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation. Has been actively searching for the device since it was stolen from one of his messengers by the kobold tribe.

## Faction Movements

**Hand of Yartar**
Named as one of Kaelen's creditors, owed a portion of the 5,000 gold borrowed in the party's name. Sent a note with a hand stencil to Unla Kee at the Talking Troll requesting a meeting outside. Active in Triboar. Previously attempted (or were framed for attempting) to assassinate Thistle.

**Zhentarim**
Named as one of Kaelen's creditors alongside the Hand of Yartar. Owed money through Kaelen's borrowing. Urgala warns they will pursue the party if Kaelen cannot repay.

**Kobold Tribe (Dessarin Hills)**
Hold Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation, taken from one of Geoffrey's messengers. Maintain a warren in the Dessarin Hills south of Yartar with tripwire traps, fire beetle lighting, kobold guards, at least one ogre guardian, and what appear to be guard dogs. Worship an altar depicting Gruumsh strangling Tiamat, with Gruumsh identified as a recent addition to an older monolith. Lost the Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation when Vardis infiltrated and extracted it. Vardis killed a number of kobolds and centipedes with a fireball during his extraction.

**Displaced Orc Tribe (Rauvin Road)**
A family/tribe of orcs displaced from their homestead by giant activity. Traveling the Rauvin Road seeking a new settlement location. Non-hostile; shown as a domestic group with children. No known affiliation with hostile factions.

## Threat Arc Events

**Planar Distortion / Demonic Incursion**
- Kaelen's play was a functional summoning ritual embedded in theatrical form, using the audience as potential sacrificial draw to open a gate from the Abyss. The ritual succeeded in a modified form despite significant alteration of the script and staging.
- Bob Saget, a demon, successfully crossed from the Abyss into the Material Plane through a public theatrical performance in a crowded inn in Triboar. This is the first successful summoning of this entity in eons by his own account.
- The summoning was unintentionally enabled by the party's improvements to the play, which apparently preserved enough ritual structure (particularly the unchanged finale) to complete the gate.

## Revealed Information

- **Kaelen's true motive:** The play was designed as a summoning ritual to kill the Triboar theater audience in hellfire as revenge for their failure to appreciate his creative work. He borrowed 5,000 gold in the party's name to fund this scheme.
- **Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation:** A magical device that projects animated, musical illusions lasting longer than a standard illusion spell and requiring no mage to operate. It was stolen from one of Chancellor Geoffrey's messengers by a kobold tribe in the Dessarin Hills. Urgala knew its location from her pre-innkeeper life.
- **Bob Saget's nature and purpose:** He is a demon whose specific domain is tormenting kings who killed their jesters, using humor as his instrument. He has been confined to the Abyss for eons and is genuinely pleased to have been summoned.
- **The kobold altar:** The monolith in the kobold warren originally predates any Gruumsh iconography; Gruumsh was added recently, suggesting a recent religious or factional shift within the tribe.
- **Urgala's past:** She implied she has intelligence or field experience beyond innkeeping, demonstrated by knowing the location of the stolen Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation and the kobold tribe that holds it.
