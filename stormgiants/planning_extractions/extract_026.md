## NPC Activity

**Darz Helgar**
- Revealed the name of his former guild: the **Coffin Nail**, a thieves' guild in Waterdeep, which he was part of until his arrest ten years ago. He is uncertain whether it still exists.
- Directed the party toward **Skullport** as the best place to find traces of the Coffin Nail.
- Made a hand signal identifying himself as a member of the **Zhentarim**.
- Disclosed that the Zhentarim in Waterdeep is currently split into two factions — one considered the "true" Black Network and one led by **Manshoon**.
- Claimed personal knowledge that Manshoon is alive, despite widespread belief that all his clones were destroyed.
- Was previously blackmailed by Kaelen Sarssir into stealing from the Lionshield Coster; sought the party's help in retaliation.

**Manshoon** (referenced, not present)
- Named by Darz Helgar as the leader of a splinter Zhentarim faction in Waterdeep.
- Described as having been killed by **Fzoul**, but having cloned himself as a form of immortality. All clones were believed destroyed, but Darz insists Manshoon is alive.
- Not confirmed present or active in current events; information is Darz's personal belief and is ten years old.

**Fzoul** (referenced, not present)
- Named only as the individual who killed Manshoon prior to the cloning events. No current activity.

**Kaelen Sarssir**
- Operates the **Talking Troll** tavern in Red Larch.
- Was blackmailing Darz Helgar to steal keys from the Lionshield Coster. Motivation: to fund his theatrical ambitions.
- Revealed dreams of converting his tavern into a theater venue, inspired by *The Inn of Shadows off Highroad*, a Waterdeep production.
- Acquired the Talking Troll from a previous owner named **Ha-Ha Pinkerton**, who sold it cheaply and then disappeared. Kaelen was unaware of the well-stocked wine cellar's value at the time of purchase.
- Denied knowledge of the stolen key when confronted, though his denial was transparently false.
- Agreed under pressure to hand over the stolen Lionshield Coster key and a prized bottle of Champagne du le Stomp.
- Entered into an investment/partnership arrangement with the party: received a 500 gold initial investment in exchange for listing them in his "Founder's Circle," with a 70/30 gross revenue split (70% to the party). Party agreed to conceal his role in the theft.
- Revealed a coldly calculating side beneath his theatrical demeanor when pressed on profit-sharing terms.

**Ha-Ha Pinkerton** (referenced, not present)
- Former owner of the Talking Troll tavern. Described as a short man with bright, cheery cheeks who was always smiling.
- Sold the tavern cheaply to Kaelen, claiming desperation, then disappeared. Circumstances of his disappearance are unknown.
- Left behind a well-stocked, neglected wine cellar.

**Joon** (Thistle's uncle)
- Found drunk at the Talking Troll, having spent money and accumulated debt with Kaelen.
- Doused with a bucket of water by Thistle.
- No further information revealed about his current situation; Thistle declined to cover his debt.

**Unnamed Priest/Cleric of Helm**
- Located at the Talking Troll, intoxicated.
- Claimed Helm spoke to him truly regarding the dead body previously discovered by the party.
- Revealed he was formerly stationed in Neverwinter, where he had an active social and professional life. Was banished to the Allfaiths Shrine in Red Larch following a theological dispute with the high priest — described by him as "temple politics."
- Refused to cast *Speak with Dead*, stating he does not like talking to the dead.
- Currently stationed at the **Allfaiths Shrine**, Red Larch.

**Alaestra** (co-owner, Lionshield Coster)
- Present when the party returned the stolen key. Appeared visibly relieved.
- Declined to renegotiate the previously agreed 15% discount despite Orsik's attempt. She and Narth gave the impression their employers are more intimidating than the party.

**Narth** (co-owner, Lionshield Coster)
- Present alongside Alaestra for the key's return. Held firm on original deal terms alongside Alaestra.

**Urgala Meltimer**
- Proprietor of the **Northshield House Inn**, Red Larch.
- Was already informed of events in the Sheriff's office, attributing her knowledge to thin walls in a small town.
- Made a brief emotional comment — "that affair was never going to end well" — suggesting personal knowledge of or connection to the deceased and the circumstances of the death. Declined to elaborate.
- Offered the party one free night with dinner and breakfast in gratitude for bringing her "some measure of peace."
- Demonstrated a sharp, experienced temperament when Thistle pushed for more, revealing a background as a long-time adventurer.

## Revealed Information

- **Manshoon** is believed by Darz Helgar to be alive, contradicting common knowledge that all his clones were eliminated after Fzoul killed him. The source is Darz's personal knowledge, now a decade old.
- The **Zhentarim in Waterdeep** is factionally divided, with the legitimacy of each side contested.
- The **Coffin Nail** was a major, ruthlessly organized thieves' guild in Waterdeep with informants inside the city guard and local borough connections. Its current status is unknown; Skullport is the recommended starting point for investigation.
- **Ha-Ha Pinkerton** sold the Talking Troll suspiciously cheaply and vanished. The well-stocked wine cellar he left behind suggests either ignorance of its value or an abrupt, unplanned departure.
- **Urgala Meltimer** has some personal connection to the victim or the affair referenced in the murder case. She reacted emotionally and made a knowing comment before stopping herself.
- The **Allfaiths Shrine cleric** was politically exiled from Neverwinter and resents his posting in Red Larch. He is technically capable of *Speak with Dead* but refuses to use it.
- The Lionshield Coster owners implied they answer to employers significantly more powerful or threatening than themselves.
