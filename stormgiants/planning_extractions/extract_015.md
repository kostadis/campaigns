## NPC Activity

**Oblivillish (Royal Pixie, exiled)**
Revealed the cause of his exile: he killed a stag belonging to Prince Thornacious, claiming ignorance of the ownership. Confirmed he had been employed by the ettin Gralm prior to the session's events. Participated in cover story construction with Thistle to gain entry to Gralm's camp. Attempted to learn to grovel at Thistle's instruction. Successfully bluffed Gralm's guards using goblin attire. Whispered strategic intelligence to the party: Prince Thornacious has been expanding his sphere of control into Gralm's territory, and the Fey music keeping the ettin awake is a result of this expansion. After Blasius was subdued, identified him and was visibly distressed. Endorsed killing the Unseelie Dryads as necessary to restore both himself and Blasius to Thornacious's favor. Invoked Thistle's fealty to Queen Tatiana as justification for attacking the Dryads.

**Gralm (Ettin, two-headed)**
Presides over a large encampment of goblins, hobgoblins, and at least one hill giant (observed under construction). Received the party in a 30-foot hut with a floor of skulls and a ragged wooden throne. Identified as an enemy of Bad Fruul. Accepted the cover story and hosted a series of drinking and strength contests as a trial of worth. Blonde-haired head is the articulate, patient personality; bald head is aggressive, sleep-deprived, and fixated on noise. The bald head's sleep deprivation was confirmed by Unla Kee to be caused by nearby Fey music. The bald head fell asleep after receiving magmin shell earmuffs. The blonde-haired head conditionally agreed to an alliance against Bad Fruul contingent on the party either bringing four heads of Fey or convincing the Fey to stop playing music near his tent.

**Grindle (Ogre, approximately 13 feet tall)**
Serves as Gralm's champion and camp enforcer. Presented as the named contestant in the strength trials. Was defeated in all contests by Unla Kee. Reacted with pouts and accusations of unfairness. Briefly showed interest in Thistle as a "toy," was verbally corrected by Gralm.

**Pandi (named entity, dog-like with colorful mane, associated with the Forgotten Forest gate)**
Manifested within a puzzle room triggered by a blue-flame door in the Forgotten Forest. Communicates telepathically, at least with Thistle. Described itself as bored and enthusiastic. Required the party to guess a word ("passage") and complete a tangram puzzle as toll for passage. Rewarded success with accelerated travel through the forest. Referenced knowing the travelers would come. Identity and nature unexplained.

**Blasius (Satyr)**
Found near a small pond in the Forgotten Forest, drinking from a hip flask, accompanied by a giant eagle wearing an ornamental leaf necklace. Recognized Thistle and Oblivillish. Confessed to fabricating the accusation against Oblivillish: while drunk, he told Prince Thornacious that Oblivillish killed the stag. Revealed that since Oblivillish's exile, he has been forced into an administrative role as Thornacious's de facto chief of staff, which he despises. Proposed that if the party kills the Unseelie in the nearby clearing, he will blame the stag's death on them, claim his earlier accusation was drunken confusion, and use the act to restore Oblivillish to favor. Identified nearby Dryads as Unseelie and led the party to their location. Gave alcohol to Unla Kee multiple times and expressed warm regard for him.

**Giant Eagle (unnamed, companion of Blasius)**
Present at Blasius's location near the pond. Wears an ornamental leaf necklace. Behavior described as watchful, head constantly scanning the sky. No further interaction recorded.

**Unseelie Dryads and halflings (unnamed, multiple)**
Located in a large clearing near Blasius's position. Playing cards around a table with a large coin pile. Overheard cursing Queen Tatiana ("May the false Queen Tatiana fart on you"), confirming Unseelie allegiance to the Gloaming Court. A broken harp lies in pieces beside one of the Dryads. Large rotted stuffed animals (lions, tigers, bears) surround the clearing. Blasius identified their music as the source of the noise disturbing Gralm. Not yet engaged in combat at session's end.

## Faction Movements

**Gralm's Forces**
Operating a large, well-populated encampment in or near the Forgotten Forest. Identified as enemies of Bad Fruul. A hill giant was observed within the camp's construction activity. Alliance with the party is conditionally available but contingent on resolution of the Fey music problem.

**The Seelie Court / Prince Thornacious's Fey**
Thornacious has been actively expanding his territorial sphere into the Forgotten Forest, including into areas overlapping with Gralm's territory. This expansion brought Fey music into proximity of Gralm's camp, creating the inter-faction tension driving much of the session's plot. Oblivillish remains in exile. Blasius remains in an unwanted administrative role. The Fey circle was previously destroyed by Thornacious, who declared the group would "build an empire or die trying."

**The Gloaming Court / Unseelie**
Active presence confirmed in the Forgotten Forest. A group of Unseelie-aligned Dryads and halflings has established itself in a clearing. Their music has been the proximate cause of Gralm's sleep deprivation and his hostility toward the Fey broadly. Their presence creates simultaneous problems for Gralm, the Seelie court's operatives, and the party's alliance-building goals. They are the target of the session-ending planned attack.

**Bad Fruul's Forces**
Referenced but not present. Previously killed a group that included goblins formerly employed by Gralm. Gralm acknowledges Bad Fruul as an enemy. His power is described by Gralm as currently waning.

## Revealed Information

- Blasius fabricated the accusation that got Oblivillish exiled. He told Prince Thornacious that Oblivillish killed the stag while drunk, and it was a lie by omission or confusion. He now seeks to undo this by blaming the dead stag on the Unseelie.
- The Unseelie Dryads and halflings in the Forgotten Forest are aligned with the Gloaming Court, confirmed by their stated opposition to "false Queen Tatiana."
- Gralm's bald head's erratic behavior and hostility toward Fey is explained by chronic sleep deprivation, not innate aggression—caused directly by Unseelie music near his camp.
- Prince Thornacious destroyed the Fey circle in the Forgotten Forest and has declared an imperial expansion into the region, bringing his forces into Gralm's territory without apparent negotiation.
- The Fey music creating conflict between Gralm and the local Fey is Unseelie in origin, meaning the Seelie Fey are being blamed for Unseelie provocation.
- Gralm is aware Bad Fruul is weakening and considers the current moment favorable for offensive action.
- Oblivillish confirms that Unseelie status is not species-locked—any creature may swear fealty to the Gloaming Court.
