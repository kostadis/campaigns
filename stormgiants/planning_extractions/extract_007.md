## NPC Activity

**Chandra Stol** (elderly elven woman, Parnast)
- Approached the party when they knocked on the wyvern's stable, warning them not to provoke it.
- Explained the wyvern was left behind by the Cult of the Dragon after their defeat.
- Revealed the wyvern is kept fed and alive by villagers as a deterrent/execution method.
- Offered the party a job: retrieve a religious idol from a merchant named Azam, currently stuck in Vuerthyl.
- Withheld information about the Cult of the Dragon and Adimond Kruskas until the idol is returned.
- Under pressure, voluntarily revealed that the Cult of the Dragon moved treasure via a large flying castle over Parnast — later confirmed to be the castle that crash-landed near Waterdeep and triggered the alliance against the Cult.
- Named Fiora as the intermediary who was supposed to find mercenaries for the job.

**Ragnar Redtooth** (barkeep, The Golden Tankard, Parnast)
- Confirmed Chandra Stol has been seeking someone to retrieve her idol for weeks.
- Stated Azam refuses to travel due to dangers on the road.
- Reported that traffic has been slow from the east but many travelers are heading east.
- Examined the party's treasure map but could only vaguely place it somewhere in the North (speculated Silver Marches, Spine of the World, Icespires, or Nethermountains).
- Charged the party exploitative rates for lodging and sheets.

**Azam** (lean half-elven merchant of Rashemi ancestry, Vuerthyl)
- Met the party in the oasis tavern at Vuerthyl.
- Explained he has been hired to transport a large wooden statue (the Triune Queen / Angharradh) from Vuerthyl to Parnast.
- Showed the party the statue, currently in a wagon being wrapped by a half-orc woman.
- Offered the party additional work from his patron Hsing upon successful completion of the delivery.
- Appeared startled when Thistle asked about a Drake named Thule, deferring immediately to Hsing.

**Hsing** (golden-scaled pseudodragon, patron/companion of Azam)
- Travels draped around Azam's shoulders; has milky irises.
- Acts as Azam's patron and decision-maker — Azam defers to Hsing on business matters, including whether to show the party the statue.
- Gave a cryptic answer about Drake Thule: *"Where there is the yellow sky, there is the drake you are looking for."*
- Indicated he is capable of mercy ("Hsing has been known to forgive… when there is a seed, it must be let to grow before it is killed").

**Fiora** (referenced, not present)
- Named by Chandra Stol as the intermediary tasked with finding mercenaries to retrieve the idol. No further details provided.

**Adimond Kruskas** (referenced, not present)
- Named by Thistle as a target of inquiry; Chandra Stol refused to provide any information until the idol is returned.

**Drake Thule** (referenced, not present)
- Named by Thistle as someone he is searching for.
- Hsing responded cryptically: "Where there is the yellow sky, there is the drake you are looking for."

## Faction Movements

**Cult of the Dragon**
- Formerly controlled the Black Road through Anauroch and the town of Parnast.
- Abandoned the route after Tiamat's entry into the North was blocked at the Battle at the Well of the Worlds.
- Left a wyvern chained in Parnast's stables upon their departure; the animal remains there.
- Confirmed to have used a large flying castle to move treasure over Parnast — this castle later crash-landed near Waterdeep, prompting the broader alliance against the Cult.

**Zhentarim**
- Previously controlled the Black Road through Anauroch before the Cult of the Dragon took over.
- Attempting to reassert control over the Black Road following the Cult's departure, but their hold remains tenuous.

## Revealed Information

- **Chandra Stol is withholding intelligence** about the Cult of the Dragon and the named individual Adimond Kruskas. She has explicitly made this information conditional on the party returning the idol.
- **The wyvern in Parnast's stables** was deliberately left behind by the Cult of the Dragon. It is chained but capable of breaking the chain; it remains passive only because villagers continue to feed it.
- **The flying castle** that crashed near Waterdeep and catalyzed the alliance against the Cult originated from or passed through Parnast — confirmed by Chandra Stol.
- **The statue** being transported is the Triune Queen (Angharradh), a threefold elven deity. Its inscription warns against "She Who Was Banished" and calls for unity among elves against evil.
- **Drake Thule's location** is described cryptically by Hsing as being "where there is the yellow sky."
- **Hsing functions as Azam's patron**, not merely a companion — Azam defers all significant decisions to the pseudodragon.
- **Fiora** acted as intermediary between Chandra Stol and mercenary hiring, but is not currently present.
