Participated in the card game confrontation. Cast Entangle on the party. Used tree-stepping teleportation to reposition multiple times during battle. Attempted to charm Unla Kee by suggesting he sit and drink. Was knocked prone and killed by Orsik, who cut it in half.

**Deciduous Dryad (Unseelie)**
Participated in the card game confrontation. Attempted to charm Orsik into standing down ("This isn't a battle you want to fight in, and we are your friends"), successfully charming him briefly. Was struck by Thistle's arrow and covered in acid, breaking concentration on Orsik. Killed by Unla Kee's magic missiles.

**Unseelie Halfling Mage**
Participated in the card game confrontation. Cast Magic Missile twice — once at Orsik and once at Wallace. Was covered in faerie fire (pink glow) by Vardis. Was wounded by Wallace and killed by Wallace's sword strike.

**Blasius (Satyr)**
Found in the clearing, still drunk, alongside Oblivillish when the battle concluded. Gave Unla Kee a Ring of Jumping as a gift, naming him his "true honored drinking friend." Declared his happiness at no longer being chief of staff to Prince Thornacious. Vouched for Oblivillish and the party before Prince Thornacious, retracting his earlier accusations and attributing them to drunkenness. Credited Oblivillish with commanding the heroes against the Unseelie incursion. Was restored to his position as the Prince's Satyr. Led the group through a Fey portal directly to Prince Thornacious (a perk of his former chief-of-staff status). Organized a Vision Quest involving ritual drinking that lasted six days, during which participants gained the ability to neutralize poison once per vision quest.

**Oblivillish**
Present in the clearing with Blasius after the battle. Shouted that the bear and lion ate the stag and claimed he was framed for the stag's death. Was confirmed by Vardis as having been framed. Was restored to the position of Chief of Staff to Prince Thornacious following Blasius's retraction before the Prince. Was credited by Blasius with commanding the heroes against the Unseelie.

**Prince Thornacious**
Received the party via Fey portal. Initially hostile, referring to Blasius as "traitorous scum." Accepted Blasius's revised account after confirmation from Thistle and the group. Restored Blasius to his Satyr role and Oblivillish to the position of Chief of Staff.

**Gralm (Ettin)**
Agreed to deal with Bad Fruul after being persuaded by Orsik's persuasion, the drinking game victory, and the magnim sound-canceling headphones. Signed the contract in Grolm's precise script. Hosted a small celebration afterward, offering flights (a fine brew) in crystal glasses. Has a goblin butler on staff.

**Azam**
Had been waiting in Parnast for two weeks, drinking at the Golden Tankar and assuming the party had failed. Was present in the village of Old Parnast during the Festival of Greengrass. Upon the party's return, congratulated them and delivered a magical potion and a pouch of gold to Unla Kee as the promised reward. Observed Unla Kee's honesty in reporting only 200 GP to his companions and concluded the gnome might be a suitable SEER recruit. Pitched the Lord's Alliance to Unla Kee, detailing its ideology and contrasting it with the Harpers, Zhentarim, Order of the Gauntlet, and Emerald Enclave. Was rejected.

**Ragnar and Elspeth**
Staff at the Golden Tankar in Parnast. Azam left coin for them when he departed to meet the returning party. No further detail provided.

**Unseelie Agents in the Grove:** The Dryads and the Halfling Mage were identified as Unseelie agents responsible for the stag's death, clearing Oblivillish of the accusation Blasius had made while drunk.

Azam characterized the Harpers as anarchists, the Zhentarim as parasitic trade monopolists, the Order of the Gauntlet as overzealous monster-hunters, and the Emerald Enclave as opposed to resource extraction. He is actively recruiting for an entity called "the SEER."

**Azam Works for the SEER:** Azam referenced the SEER as a superior or patron whose disappointment he feared while waiting in Parnast. He assessed Unla Kee's trustworthiness based on the gnome's honesty with his companions.

**Gralm's Contract:** Gralm (the Ettin) has formally agreed to deal with Bad Fruul, sealed by a signed contract written in Grolm's script.
