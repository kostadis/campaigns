## NPC Activity

**Bad Fruul**
- Appeared at the edge of the forest outside Parnast, mounted on a mammoth, wearing bone-festooned plate armor and a beast-skull helm.
- Dismounted and personally addressed the town, demanding Chandra (identified as hailing from Evereska, offspring of Luistarra), a statue called Agharradh, and threatening slavery and destruction if refused.
- Revealed to be a drow disguised as or inhabiting the appearance of a giant; Thistle identified him as "the drow" upon seeing his eyes gleam with intelligence.
- Commanded a three-wave assault: first sending eight goblins, two wolves, and goblin leaders as a probe; then deploying ogres, half-ogres from Mama Mia, and two cave bears after the first wave failed.
- Reacted with frustration when his spy intelligence proved wrong about the militia's poor training.
- Ordered a third wave including more ogres and an owlbear after his ogres breached the wall.

**Mama Mia**
- Referenced as the source of half-ogres deployed in Bad Fruul's second assault wave.
- Her ogres were noted by Bad Fruul as having breached the wall.

**Goblin Bosses (unnamed)**
- Attempted to rally goblin troops during the first assault wave; killed by militia archers.

**Orc Whelps' Guard (unnamed orc female)**
- Revealed that the orc chieftain Kurglow would abandon Bad Fruul to save his captured son.
- Described Kurglow as "a terrible husband, but a good father."

**Kurglow (orc chieftain, absent)**
- Revealed to have a son among the captured whelps.
- Identified as potentially willing to defect from Bad Fruul's alliance in exchange for his child's safety.
- Was sent a message through the Jarlor/Larrish spy channel containing Orsik's negotiation terms.

**Jarlor**
- Reluctantly agreed to pass a message to Kurglow through the spy channel after his wife pressured him.
- Operates as a known node in a spy communication channel connected to the orc forces.

**Larrish**
- Named alongside Jarlor as part of the spy channel used to contact Kurglow.

**Chandra**
- Confirmed as a person of significance — named as one of Bad Fruul's explicit demands.
- Identified as hailing from Evereska and as the offspring of someone named Luistarra.
- Clarified to the party that she cannot control the wyvern.
- Explained that the local merchant Calder uses Zhentarim physical transportation, not the transdimensional market system used by Lionel Coster merchants.

**Calder**
- Local merchant in Parnast.
- Uses Zhentarim physical transportation of goods; has no magic items to sell.

**Ragnar and Gundalin**
- Militia members who vocally refused Bad Fruul's demands on behalf of the town.

**Yurtrus (deity, referenced)**
- Two orcs in the cave were identified as followers of Yurtrus, god of death and disease.
- An altar of bones and ash dedicated to Yurtrus was found, along with a flayed elf corpse and a stretched section of elven skin on a wooden frame.

**Shargaas (deity, referenced)**
- The Red Fang orc assassins were identified as followers of Shargaas.
- Their magical darkness ability was consistent with Shargaas worship.

**Luistarra (referenced, absent)**
- Named by Bad Fruul as Chandra's parent, described as "horrid."
- No further details provided.

## Faction Movements

**Bad Fruul's Horde**
- Launched a coordinated assault on Parnast with multiple waves: goblins and wolves (probing force, wiped out), then ogres, half-ogres, and cave bears (partial success — ogres breached the wall, half-ogres killed).
- Suffered significant losses to militia pit traps, archers, and wall defenders in the first two waves.
- Bad Fruul's intelligence from spies was wrong about the militia's training level; he acknowledged his spies lied to him.
- Third wave initiated by chapter's end: additional ogres plus an owlbear dispatched.

**Orsik's Militia (Parnast defenders)**
- Successfully repelled first two assault waves using pre-dug pit traps with pikes, elevated archer positions, and boarded fortifications.
- Militia morale significantly boosted by Orsik's pre-battle speech.
- Thistle-trained archers specifically noted as effective against half-ogres.

**Zhentarim (referenced)**
- Identified as the faction responsible for physical goods transportation in the Parnast region.
- Calder operates under their logistics network.

**Lionel Coster / KP Trade (referenced, absent)**
- A transdimensional merchant network not available in Parnast.
- Referenced as ideologically opposed to local-market merchants; has faced violent resistance from traditional merchants.

**Red Fang Orcs (Shargaas assassins)**
- Two were present in the cave complex acting as ambushers.
- Both killed during the cave battle.

**Yurtrus Orc Followers**
- Small group present in the cave; maintained a ritual space with a Yurtrus altar, a flayed elf, and an alchemical/disease concoction.
- All killed during the cave battle.

## Revealed Information

- **Bad Fruul is a drow**, not a true giant or hill giant as his appearance suggests. His intelligent gleaming eyes gave him away to Thistle.
- **Bad Fruul's demands** are: (1) Chandra of Evereska, (2) the "offspring of Luistarra," and (3) the statue of Agharradh.
- **Chandra is the offspring of Luistarra**, a figure Bad Fruul described as "horrid" — suggesting Luistarra is known and significant to him.
- **Kurglow has a son among the captured orc whelps** and may be turned against Bad Fruul through that leverage.
- **Bad Fruul has spies in or around Parnast** who reported (falsely) that militia training was poor.
- **The Jarlor/Larrish channel** is a functional spy communication route that can reach Kurglow's forces.
- **The Yurtrus orc cell** in the cave was conducting ritual flaying of elves and brewing foul concoctions — suggesting active religious practice, not just opportunistic violence.
- **The miniature silver dragon skull** found in the Red Fang orcs' chest may be a significant object (nature unknown).
- **The statue referenced is called Agharradh** — first time its name has been confirmed as a specific demand of Bad Fruul.
