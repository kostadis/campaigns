## NPC Activity

**Wallace**
Participated in the ambush battle at Ambush Gap. Internally noted that the Bugbears were working for someone and should have had more money. Withheld this assessment from the group. Internally resolved to find Orsik's Zhentarim contact independently to offer the treasure's location. Observed and was critical of Orsik's combat decisions (throwing his sword at the Bugbears). Reflected on his father's political goals regarding the Lord's Alliance and the Black Road.

**Feetor (Mud Mephit)**
Hiding in the marsh when the party defeated the water weird (the "grabby water monster"). Observed the battle from concealment and was struck by the party's ability to make the water weird visible. Emerged and introduced himself, pledging loyalty to the party as the new strongest force in the marsh. Provided intelligence on the water weird (it reforms in 2–3 days, has no fixed lair, treats the whole swamp as its territory). Provided intelligence on Mama Manyknuckles (half-ogre leader, somewhere deeper in the marsh, hostile to mephits). Explained the mephit civil war: factions split between supporting Mama Manyknuckles and the water weird. Accepted a gold coin from Orsik and Vardis, disappeared into the swamp, and returned claiming the other mud mephits agreed to wait and observe the outcome. Approached Unla Kee separately, identified him as perceptive, and offered to front him "special mud" to participate in mephit betting on the marsh's new ruler.

**Mama Manyknuckles**
Not present but described by Feetor. Leader of a band of half-ogres. Described as particularly ugly. Controls one faction of the marsh civil war. Half-ogres mock and attack mud mephits. Location unknown to Feetor but Thistle identified her encampment via scouting. Last known location: kidney-shaped island, northern encampment.

**Hsing (Ancient Pseudo-Dragon)**
Referenced but not present. Described by Orsik as having given the party jobs for a while. Appeared before the current journey to request the party investigate the wizard's tower in the Marsh of Chelimber. Clarified by Vardis as the owner of Assam, not the other way around — Assam belongs to Hsing.

**Orsik**
Found a map to the wizard's tower on the hobgoblin bodies. Compared it with the party's existing map. Led the group's tactical discussion about attacking Mama Manyknuckles' camp. Authorized payment of a gold coin to Feetor to negotiate mephit neutrality. Was noted internally by Wallace as failing to negotiate payment from Hsing and failing to anticipate the ambush at Ambush Gap.

**Thistle**
Identified the map's error and corrected the party's route, arriving at the kidney-shaped island within three hours. Scouted both the southern camp and the island's center via aerial reconnaissance. Observed the southern camp: four crude huts, one sturdier hut built from raft and boat materials, a fire pit with a log heap, and two ogres around the fire. Two half-ogres noticed him but mistook him for a swamp creature; he evaded without engaging. Discovered the sinkhole at the island's center: built on a wide stone foundation with crenelated edges suggesting a sunken keep or tower, flagstone floor with a thin layer of swamp water, a weathered statue in the northwest corner, a large table with filthy furs and a bed, and two log-and-dirt ramps leading into the sinkhole. Concluded someone currently lives in the sunken tower. Conjured his drake back after losing it to the water weird.

## Faction Movements

**Mud Mephits**
Split into two factions: one aligned with Mama Manyknuckles, one with the water weird. Following Feetor's negotiation (funded by the party's gold coin), the mephit community agreed to a wait-and-see stance, observing the coming conflict between the party and Mama Manyknuckles rather than intervening. Feetor positioned himself as the party's "number one mud mephit" and intermediary.

**Half-Ogres (Mama Manyknuckles' Band)**
Encamped on the kidney-shaped island, split across northern and southern camps. Southern camp observed: approximately two ogres and at least two half-ogres active around the fire pit. Hostile to mud mephits. Described as having previously been active in the marsh but having "left" in some capacity (per Feetor's earlier assessment). Unaware the party is staging a night attack.

**Lord's Alliance**
Referenced but not present. Wallace's father is working to encourage the Lord's Alliance to expand control over the Black Road and claim the last outpost before Anauroch. Wallace notes his father may be operating with Lord's Alliance backing or may be maneuvering to claim credit retroactively.

**Zhentarim**
Referenced. Described as weakened since the War of the World, unable to reassert control over the Black Road. Orsik is pursuing Zhentarim ambitions but has not yet made contact with a local Zhentarim operative. Wallace is independently planning to contact Orsik's Zhentarim contact to sell information about the treasure's location.

**Hobgoblins (unnamed group)**
Previously encountered; now dead. Six bodies found on the muddy island in the marsh, sprawled in the water, weapons drawn — killed by the water weird. They had been sent to investigate the wizard's tower. Orsik looted a map to the tower from their bodies.

## Revealed Information

- The water weird is immortal, reforming 2–3 days after destruction in a different part of the marsh. It treats the entire marsh as its territory and has no fixed lair.
- The mud mephit civil war is between factions loyal to Mama Manyknuckles and those loyal to the water weird. Defeating both would unify the mephits.
- Feetor believes that whoever is strongest rules the marsh by default, and mud mephits follow power.
- The wizard's tower has sunk into a sinkhole at the center of the kidney-shaped island and is currently occupied — someone has set up living quarters (table, furs, bed) inside the sunken ruin.
- The stone foundation of the sinkhole resembles the top of a large keep or tower, with crenelated, weathered stonework, suggesting a significant structure lies submerged below.
- The map found on the hobgoblins was incorrect; Thistle identified the error and rerouted successfully.
- Hsing (Assam's pseudo-dragon) initiated this quest, independently aligning with the party's existing plan to visit the tower.
- Wallace's father has political motives tied to Lord's Alliance expansion along the Black Road and may be operating as an Alliance agent or freelance power broker.
