## NPC Activity

**Sandesyl Morgia** (half-elven moon elf vampire)
- Encountered in the upper tower of Blagothkus' castle on the port side, having taken up residence there as self-appointed castellan.
- Reacted with hostility to Unla Kee, calling him an "abomination."
- Revealed that Severin's version of the Cult of the Dragon was a scheme by Zariel to eliminate Tiamat from Avernus, calling it a perversion of the true Cult.
- Confirmed she severed ties with Severin's faction and operates independently.
- Revealed that Severin hired Adimond to create a fey-dragon hybrid, and that the experiment was successful but caused the subject to shift uncontrollably between forms — more akin to lycanthropy than true fusion.
- Confirmed the hybrid subject matches Thistle's description of his love interest.
- Currently exsanguinating orcs (and previously ogres) in the tower; uses silence magic to contain screaming.
- Converted ogres into zombies as part of necromantic experimentation after they were no longer viable as a food supply.
- Has worked for Blagothkus for an extended period.
- Was removed from the castle by the First Faction before they departed the north — described this as "an evil ploy."
- Does not know how Blagothkus reclaimed the castle.
- Threatened the party against underestimating her.
- Gave Orsik a book titled *Dusk* (a young adult vampire romance) as the reason she sought vampirehood.
- Declined to reveal how she became a vampire or how many spawn she controls.
- Declined to loan her zombies to Orsik for theatrical purposes.
- Expressed predatory interest in the population of Silverymoon ("tasty pompous yits").

**Blagothkus** (cloud giant)
- Currently residing in his castle; reportedly has been "moping" for weeks.
- Background recalled: slew his own son Eigeron during a fight following a prophecy from the Eye of Annam, in which the oracle told Blagothkus he could never earn divine favor and urged Eigeron to escape his father's "dark shadow."
- Previously allied with the Cult of the Dragon against smaller races, which led to the First Faction seizing control of his castle.
- Traveled to the Spine of the World to commune with his god; returned to find Eigeron dead and the castle in the First Faction's possession.
- Last known location: his tower chambers in the stern of the castle, which the party found in a state of shambles.

**Eigeron** (cloud giant, deceased)
- Referenced in Orsik's recollection; killed by his father Blagothkus following the Eye of Annam incident.
- Previously asked the heroes to avenge his death by killing Blagothkus.

**Bob Saget** (referenced by name only)
- Recalled through Vardis' memory during the descent; sang a parodic song ("Bohemian Rhapsody" variant) apparently directed at a king who killed his jester, cursing him to an eternity of bad jokes.
- Physical presence not established in this session.

## Faction Movements

**Cult of the Dragon (Severin's faction)**
- Revealed to have been a corruption of the original Cult, orchestrated by Zariel to remove Tiamat from Avernus.
- Confirmed to have funded the Adimond fey-dragon hybrid experiment.
- Dead members (not zombies) were found in the upper courtyard; Sandesyl confirmed the castle's history of Cult involvement.

**The First Faction**
- Recap via Sandesyl: previously seized control of Blagothkus' castle twice — once when Blagothkus allied with the Cult, and again while he was away at the Spine of the World.
- Deliberately expelled Sandesyl from the castle before departing the north.
- No current confirmed location or activity.

## Revealed Information

- **Zariel orchestrated Severin's Cult of the Dragon** as a scheme to remove Tiamat from Avernus, where Tiamat was described as "an annoying office mate."
- **The fey-dragon hybrid experiment** was commissioned by Severin, conducted by someone named **Adimond**, and successfully produced a subject — likely Thistle's love interest — who shifts involuntarily between fey and dragon forms, resembling lycanthropy rather than a stable fusion.
- **Sandesyl has an unknown number of vampire spawn** and an unknown origin story for her own vampirehood; she deliberately withheld both.
- **The ogre zombies in Blagothkus' tower** were not under Sandesyl's control, evidenced by their distinctive plumed helmets — suggesting a separate controlling force or residual animation.
- **Blagothkus does not know how he reclaimed his castle**; Sandesyl likewise does not know.
