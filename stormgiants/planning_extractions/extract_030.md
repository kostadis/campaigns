## NPC Activity

**Krowen Valharrow**
- Appeared in his tower/inner sanctum in Everlund, recruiting the party as Harper contractors.
- Revealed internal Harper strategy: using mercenaries provides plausible deniability and derisks core Harper agents — a method pioneered by someone named Ezra, used previously with a group called the "First Faction."
- Internally suspicious of Orsik due to reports of his Zhentarim connections; attempted (and failed) to manipulate Orsik into leaving through the front door as a ruse to expose him.
- Explained that a flying castle orb can only be obtained from Cloud Giants, either by persuasion or violence, and actively discouraged the party from pursuing that path.
- Sent the party via teleport to his agent Kolbaz in Yartar, with the mission to find a sword.

**Kolbaz**
- Harper agent stationed in Yartar; received the party in an enclosed garden patio with a faulty illusion of a Seelie court scene.
- Expressed strong discontent with the Harper practice of using non-sworn contractors, viewing it as a degradation of what being a Harper meant.
- Expressed strong anti-Lord's Alliance sentiment, characterizing them as serving only the powerful and pursuing a dangerous supernational consolidation.
- Confirmed he fought the Cult of the Dragon at the Well of the Dragons and was involved in research related to the ritual.
- Stated definitively that the Spring's First Blossom was *not* used in the Cult of the Dragon's ritual, despite Thistle's claims, and found this discrepancy genuinely puzzling.
- Had no knowledge of Adimond Kroskas despite his involvement in Cult research.
- Had no direct links to the Hand of Yartar but directed the party to the Wink and Kiss tavern as the rogues' known hangout.
- Found dead or incapacitated at the end of the session: a flash of lightning struck him; he crumpled to the ground. A small, unidentified figure stood over him and said, "you meddling little fool."

**Maia (Qumaia)**
- Tiefling barhop at the Wink and Kiss tavern in Yartar.
- Recognized the party as local celebrities from the Triboar giant incident and the play.
- Acted as an information broker, selling the party a lead on the Hand of Yartar contact (Eltio) for five gold.
- Introduced the party to Eltio; was later reprimanded by Eltio for the introduction and warned off.

**Elicia "Kal" Kallaphan**
- Severe half-elf with blond hair, always wears a cloak, carries a finely crafted rapier.
- Operates as the Hand of Yartar's bounty payout handler.
- Paid the party 200 gold for bounties (keeping 50 for herself), yielding 150 gold to the party.

**Eltio**
- Woman dressed in white, found at the bar of the Wink and Kiss; described as sullen, distant, and dangerous.
- Hand of Yartar operative who controls access to information about Harthal.
- Quoted a price of 500 gold for Harthal's whereabouts, or alternatively offered to waive the fee if the party obtains a letter of recommendation from Tamlin Zoar.
- Described herself as a patron of the arts but declined the party's offer of play tickets and sponsorship before accepting Orsik's counter-offer of bringing 50 friends to the production.

**Tamlin Zoar**
- Referenced but not present. Described as a legendary member of the Hand of Yartar.
- Built her own town on the Evermoor Road between Yartar and Everlund.
- Her endorsement can waive the 500 gold fee for information on Harthal.

**Harthal**
- Referenced but not present. The party's primary target — they are searching for him and a sword connected to him.
- Kolbaz and the Harper network do not appear to have his current location on file.
- Was reportedly in a relationship with a rogue, suggesting Hand of Yartar connections.

**"Dashing" Dallon Flek**
- Bounty target captured by Unla Kee during the party's five-day bounty hunting stint.
- Described as a handsome redhead with bright eyes and roguish good looks.
- Turned over to the Hand of Yartar for payment.

**Tink Urbelle**
- Bounty target captured by the rest of the party during the same period.
- Described as a grubby gnome with curly hair and animated facial expressions.
- Turned over to the Hand of Yartar for payment.

**Adimond Kroskas (also rendered "Adminod")**
- Referenced but not present. Described as a lead cultist of the Cult of the Dragon.
- Allegedly collected the Spring's First Blossom as a key ingredient for a ritual.
- Kolbaz claims to have no knowledge of him despite his own involvement in Cult research.
- Thistle believes the Cult is trying to assassinate him to cover up Kroskas's actions.

**Ezra**
- Referenced but not present. Named as the Harper operative who pioneered the use of mercenary contractors and non-sworn agents, previously deploying this model with a group called the "First Faction."

## Faction Movements

**The Harpers**
- Actively using non-sworn mercenary contractors as a deliberate operational strategy, attributed to Ezra's innovation.
- Krowen Valharrow personally recruited the party under this model, deploying them toward Yartar to locate a sword.
- Harper agent Kolbaz appears to have been attacked and neutralized at his Yartar safehouse on the 2nd of Flamerule by an unknown small assailant — a potential blow to Harper intelligence operations in Yartar.

**The Hand of Yartar**
- Active presence throughout Yartar; described as having rogues all over the town.
- Control information brokerage around local figures including Harthal.
- Elicia "Kal" Kallaphan manages bounty operations; Eltio manages sensitive information access.
- Tamlin Zoar is a senior/legendary figure operating from her own town on the Evermoor Road.
- Previously made an attempt on Thistle's life (referenced as an assassination attempt), though Kolbaz found this surprising and out of character for the Hand.

**The Cult of the Dragon**
- Referenced as largely destroyed following the War of the Dragon, in which the Harpers played a leading role.
- Thistle alleges surviving elements are actively hunting him to cover up Kroskas's use of the Spring's First Blossom.
- Kolbaz disputes this, stating the Spring's First Blossom was not part of the ritual — leaving the true purpose of Kroskas's collection of the artifact unknown.

**The Lord's Alliance**
- Referenced but not present. Kolbaz expressed strong opposition, characterizing them as serving only the powerful and pursuing illegitimate supernational unification.
