## NPC Activity

**Ragnar Redtooth** — Tavern owner and acting town defense coordinator in Orsik's absence. Appeared at the Golden Tankard, briefed the party on the sabotage situation, and requested 50 gold each for information on Gundalin Cartwright. Revealed three incidents of suspected sabotage: daily tool thefts from the central toolshed, collapse of the southern watchtower (killing militiaman Donnal), and a broken cart axle (second in a tenday, on wheels replaced by Gundalin). Acknowledged his past involvement with the Cult of the Dragon, claiming he worked from the inside to save the town. Directed the party toward Gundalin as the primary suspect. Separately noted intelligence brought by a half-elf named Tove about monster movements.

**Tove** — Short half-elf in worn furs, present at the Golden Tankard when the party arrived. Provided Ragnar with military intelligence: monsters are on the move, forces are constantly moving and destroying what they find, no main camp could be located. Not part of the party's direct conversation.

**Azam** — SEER operative. Intercepted the party outside the Golden Tankard to deliver intelligence about Thistle's Uncle Joon: Joon is located in Triboar (~400 miles away), did not join the Cult of the Dragon, but has joined a secretive organization unknown even to the SEER. Invoked the SEER's authority to urge the party to stay and defend Parnast, citing the Lord's Alliance's interest and promising the admiration of a powerful personage as reward.

**Joon Underwood (Uncle Joon)** — Referenced but absent. Located in Triboar. Has joined an unnamed secretive organization the SEER cannot identify. Has "set up shop" and is not believed to be leaving; the Lord's Alliance is monitoring him.

**Elspeth** — Present at the Golden Tankard. Brought food and drink to the party at Ragnar's request. Had a private conversation with Wallace outside the tavern during the meeting; Wallace kissed her and promised to speak again later.

**Wallace** — Reacted with skepticism to Ragnar's accusation against his father (Gundalin Cartwright). At the horse scene, examined the afflicted animals and identified poisoning, though this was already apparent to the owners.

**Gundalin Cartwright** — Referenced but not present. Named by Ragnar as the primary sabotage suspect. Described as a skilled craftsman whose cooperation is necessary for the town's defense. Ragnar noted that Gundalin and his cronies regularly undermine Ragnar's decisions, and that they blame Ragnar for the Cult of the Dragon involvement. The cart axles that broke were replaced by Gundalin. Footprints from the trampled section of Silas' wife's herb garden were tracked by Thistle directly to Gundalin's front door.

**Chandra Stol** — Local cleric. Arrived at the horse incident scene and was initially silent and overwhelmed. Recognized the party warmly as those who brought her the temple and saved the village from raiders. Urged the party to resolve the crowd dispute before it became a riot. Directed the party to the late wife of Silas Carras as a source of herbal antitoxin.

**Silas Carras** — Local resident and former herbalist's husband. Appeared at his home when the party visited. Clarified that the herbalist knowledge belonged to his deceased wife, not himself. Noted that bushes in his wife's garden had been recently trampled, which he flagged as suspicious. Expressed surprise at how quickly Unla Kee located the antidote within the puzzle-structured garden layout.

**Silas' Wife (deceased)** — Referenced but absent (deceased). Former herbalist whose garden contained antitoxin for yew poisoning, hidden behind a series of nested puzzle-based concealment schemes. Her garden was recently trampled by an unknown party; the trail from the trampled area leads to Gundalin Cartwright's front door.

**Donnal** — Referenced but absent (deceased). Militiaman killed when the southern watchtower collapsed the day after its completion.

**Eryn** — Villager, horse owner, childhood friend of Wallace. Present at the horse incident. Eye-rolled at Wallace's dramatic pronouncements.

**Wald** — Villager, horse owner. Present at the horse incident alongside Eryn.

**Bad Fruul's Army** — Referenced through Ragnar and Tove's intelligence. Forces are actively on the move, sacking farms (at least one confirmed), driving refugees into Parnast. No fixed main camp identified. An attack on Parnast is described by Ragnar as imminent.

**Cult of the Dragon (Parnast remnant)** — Referenced as defunct locally. Ragnar asserts there is no remaining Cult activity in Parnast. However, lingering suspicion about cursed materials scavenged from Old Parnast is being used by local craftsmen to undermine morale around the defense effort.

**Unknown Secretive Organization** — Joined by Joon Underwood in Triboar. Identity, goals, and resources entirely unknown. The SEER has no information about it.

## Revealed Information

- Ragnar Redtooth was a Cult of the Dragon member but claims he was working from the inside; local craftsmen including Gundalin Cartwright do not accept this explanation and are withholding cooperation from Ragnar's defense efforts.
- The sabotage pattern (tool theft, watchtower collapse, cart axle failure) appears deliberate and ongoing, designed to slow rather than halt construction.
- A witness saw light near the base of the southern watchtower just before its collapse.
- The yew branches found in the horses' hay are not native to the region, suggesting deliberate introduction.
- Footprints from Silas' wife's trampled garden lead to Gundalin Cartwright's front door, suggesting a connection between Gundalin and the yew poisoning.
- Uncle Joon is alive in Triboar, has not joined the Cult of the Dragon, but has joined an organization completely unknown to the SEER.
- The SEER and the Lord's Alliance consider the defense of Parnast against Bad Fruul a matter of paramount strategic importance.
