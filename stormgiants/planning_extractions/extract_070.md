## NPC Activity

**Iymrith (also known as Adimond Kroskas, "Doom of the Desert")**
- Was present in the throne room of Maelstrom in disguise as a storm giant, apparently in an ongoing argument with Queen Serissa and the Imperator when the party arrived.
- Revealed her true form as an Ancient Blue Dragon when Orsik attacked her.
- Used her wings to buffet everyone in the room and unleashed dragon fear, incapacitating most of the party and all giants present.
- Dove into the vortex pool and escaped, delivering the parting words "This is not the end of this."
- Confirmed as a leader of the Cult of the Dragon.
- Revealed (by the Imperator) to have been conducting hybridization experiments since ancient times, rumored to have created Drakes, and to have pursued fairy-dragon hybridization as part of a deal with Tiamat during the war of the dragons.
- After Tiamat's plans failed, approached the Storm Giant court with an offer of hybridization-based weapons; concealed her dragon nature from the court.
- Drained the Storm Giant treasury of significant resources in exchange for promised weapons.
- Escaped through the underwater vortex system; last known direction is through the pools beneath Maelstrom.

**Queen Serissa**
- Present in throne room, arguing with the Imperator and Iymrith-in-disguise when the party arrived.
- Initially demanded silence and explanation from the party after Iymrith's escape.
- Turned on the Imperator publicly, noting it was his job to identify that Adimond was a dragon and that he had advocated for accepting Adimond's support.
- Declared a need to ally with non-giant peoples ("small folk") as a strategic conclusion drawn from events.
- Revealed she disagreed with using Adimond's offer but had no choice given circumstances.
- Provided a golden token found on the bodies of small folk near Queen Neri's corpse, asking the party for help identifying it.
- Offered the golden suit of scale mail armor from the armory as payment for Unla Kee's "research," framing it as melted down for the king's benefit.
- Demonstrated political shrewdness: winked at the party, indicating she knows about their pilfering but is choosing to use them strategically.
- Warned Unla Kee of consequences if the money is taken without results.
- Confirmed only her father King Hekaton knows where Iymrith truly lives.

**The Imperator**
- Present in the throne room, arguing with Serissa and Iymrith when the party arrived.
- Dismissed the party's claims initially and attempted to shut down discussion.
- Publicly blamed Adimond's powerful magic for deceiving everyone, including himself.
- Provided extensive historical exposition on King Hekaton, Queen Neri, the Ordning, and the succession crisis.
- Visibly humiliated by Serissa's pointed questioning about his failure to identify Adimond as a dragon.
- Objected strenuously to giving away the golden armor; overruled by Serissa.
- Showed condescension toward non-giants throughout.

**Mirren (elder princess)**
- Entered the throne room during the aftermath with Nym.
- Attempted to accuse the party of assassination; was cut off by the Imperator.
- Confirmed to have allied with Adimond Kroskas alongside Nym, motivated by fury at being passed over for succession.
- Escorted away to her quarters by guards, protested meekly.

**Nym (elder princess)**
- Entered the throne room alongside Mirren.
- Described historically as cold and unloving, passed over for succession.
- Confirmed to have allied with Adimond alongside Mirren.
- Escorted away with Mirren, made no significant statement.

**King Hekaton**
- Referenced extensively but absent; currently missing.
- Established as the most powerful of all Storm Giants, ruled from Maelstrom over a court of all giant races.
- Was shaken by the shattering of the Ordning, came to believe giants were no longer rightful masters of the world.
- Named Serissa heir apparent over Mirren and Nym years before his disappearance, creating the succession conflict.
- Set out to investigate the death of Queen Neri after his rage was calmed by Serissa; disappeared during or after this investigation.
- Described as no longer as wise as he once was.
- Only King Hekaton knows where Iymrith truly lives.

**Queen Neri (deceased)**
- Referenced but absent; confirmed dead.
- Was fond of small folk, visited them regularly on the shores of the Sword Coast before the Ordning was shattered.
- Continually urged Hekaton to respect and potentially ally with small folk.
- Failed to return from a visit to the Sword Coast; her corpse was found on a small island where she was known to meet humans.
- Death was apparently caused by small folk, triggering Hekaton's rage and his eventual investigation.
- A golden token (a gambling chit from the Grand Dame riverboat in Yartar) was found on the bodies of small folk near her corpse; this token is now in the party's possession.

**Lord Drylund**
- Referenced but absent.
- Identified by Unla Kee as the operator of a high-stakes gambling boat called the Grand Dame, docked in Yartar.
- Was attempting to become the new Waterbarron of Yartar, a position that became vacant after the death of the previous Waterbarron.
- The golden token found near Queen Neri's corpse is a gambling chit from his establishment, worth ten gold.

**Leela**
- Referenced but absent; Thistle's girlfriend.
- Was captured alongside Thistle and Thuul by the Cult of the Dragon.
- Thuul and Thistle escaped when the cult disintegrated; Leela did not.
- Iymrith escaped and took captives including Leela with him.
- Suspected by Orsik (disputed by Thistle) of being a product of Iymrith's fairy-dragon hybridization experiments.

**Thuul**
- Referenced but absent.
- Was captured alongside Thistle and Leela by the Cult of the Dragon.
- Escaped alongside Thistle when the cult disintegrated.

- The court's treasury was significantly drained by Adimond's demands.
- Serissa has authorized transfer of the golden scale mail armor (appraised at significant value) to Unla Kee as payment for locating King Hekaton, framed internally as melted down for research.

## Revealed Information

- **Iymrith's true identity**: Adimond Kroskas = Iymrith = "Doom of the Desert," an Ancient Blue Dragon and Cult of the Dragon leader, confirmed by her transformation in the throne room.
- **Iymrith's hybridization program**: She has been conducting creature hybridization experiments for an extremely long time; she is rumored to have created Drakes; the fairy-dragon hybridization program was originally part of a deal with Tiamat and was repurposed as a weapon offer to the Storm Giants.
- **Iymrith's lair**: Location is unknown to everyone present, including the Storm Giant court. Only King Hekaton knows where Iymrith lives.
- **The golden token**: Found on the bodies of small folk near Queen Neri's corpse. Identified by Unla Kee as a gambling chit from the Grand Dame, a high-stakes riverboat in Yartar operated by Lord Drylund, who was seeking the position of Waterbarron.
- **Queen Neri's death**: She was killed by small folk on a coastal island. Her death triggered King Hekaton's near-rampage and his subsequent investigation, which preceded his disappearance.
- **Mirren and Nym's alliance with Adimond**: Confirmed; motivated by resentment over being passed over for succession. The nature of what they promised or offered Adimond is unknown.
- **The Ordning's shattering**: Confirmed to have destabilized giant society and shaken King Hekaton's confidence in giants' divine mandate; Annam the Allfather is not answering prayers.
- **Succession history**: Hekaton declared Serissa heir apparent over Mirren and Nym years ago, characterizing Mirren as tempestuous and Nym as cold. This decision was made known to the sisters well before his disappearance.
