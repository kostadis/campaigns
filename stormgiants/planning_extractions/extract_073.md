## NPC Activity

**The Spectral Raven**
- Appeared in the kitchen area of the castle and emitted a soul-chilling cry inducing despair in the party, specifically targeting Thistle's emotional vulnerability around his lost love.
- Was ultimately struck down by Thistle using his bow, after which the despair effect dissipated.

**Sandesyl**
- Not physically present but heavily discussed. Revealed to be a disciple of Sammaster and practitioner of necromantic experimentation, having created specters by ripping souls from living creatures.
- Identified as the author of a written rant (found as a note) accusing unnamed parties of betraying her and accusing investigators of being spies.
- Described as having remained hidden within ice she carved into the glacier rather than fleeing the castle after the War of the Dragons, and used this position to gradually transform new castle occupants (including the Frost Giant Jarl and his forces) into undead.
- Made a deal with Blagothkus to locate Escarlotta in exchange for some arrangement; used this to extend her influence.
- Assessed by Unla Kee as a dangerous target whose lair assault would provoke an immediate and severe response.

**Rezmir (deceased)**
- Half of her dragon remains were found in a room across from the pantry, still wearing the distinctive purple garb of a Wyrmspeaker — specifically identified as the Black Dragon Wyrmspeaker.
- Her remains are in play; the party examined them and found a written note among them.
- The note attributed to her argues against internal audits of her operations, defends her loyalty to the Cult of the Dragon, and warns against factionalism within the Cult, invoking the unity of Tiamat's five heads.
- Thistle recounted rumors that she was in charge of the castle and responsible for moving the hoard for the Tiamat ritual, and that she was either killed by the heroes of the War of the Dragons or was a victim of internal Cult politics.

**Escarlotta**
- A cloud giant, wife of Blagothkus, currently manifesting through the castle's control tower via a constructed rune-and-gem avatar of a female cloud giant's head (which she noted is still a work in progress).
- Revealed her history: she was crashed into the Mkoz glacier and freed by the heroes of the War of the Dragons, then briefly controlled by Frost Giants who used her in the final battle above the summoned temple of Tiamat.
- Confirmed that the Frost Giants (led by the Jarl, whom the party had already destroyed as a skeletal giant) believed Sandesyl had fled, only to be transformed into undead by her once the Cult was destroyed.
- Stated that Sandesyl could not control the castle or Escarlotta herself, and so made the deal to locate Blagothkus instead.
- Revealed that Blagothkus visited an Oracle (the All-Father) after the War of the Dragons and was told he was a failure and that their son was the true giant leader — but only if the son killed his father. The son failed and is now dead.
- Expressed intense animosity toward Countess Sansuri, whom she regards as a "classless poseur" making an illegitimate play for leadership of the Ordning.
- After Orsik's intervention, agreed to have Blagothkus assist the party in defeating Sandesyl.

**Blagothkus**
- Present in the control tower during Escarlotta's address. Deflected responsibility for the undead infestation and the castle's situation.
- Confirmed (via Escarlotta's account) that he made a deal with Sandesyl to locate Escarlotta, and that he subsequently became obsessed with giants' role in a perceived renaissance following the War of the Dragons.
- After Orsik's diplomatic intervention, was assigned by Escarlotta to assist the party against Sandesyl.

**The Frost Giant Jarl (deceased)**
- Confirmed to be the skeletal giant previously destroyed by the party. Had occupied the castle believing Sandesyl was gone, and was transformed into undead by her.

**Sammaster (referenced)**
- Named as the original disciple of Mystara who developed the process for creating Dracoliches and inspired factions within the Cult of the Dragon to pursue broader undead-creation research. Identified by Thistle as Sandesyl's ideological mentor. Not present.

**Severin (referenced)**
- Briefly referenced by Escarlotta as the individual who made the actual deal with Tiamat (described metaphorically as "the devil"). Not present.

**Countess Sansuri (referenced)**
- Named by Escarlotta as a cloud giant making a play for leadership of the Ordning. Described with hostility as a "classless poseur." Not present.

**Uncle Joon (referenced)**
- Named by Thistle as the source of his knowledge about Sammaster and Cult of the Dragon factions pursuing non-Dracolich undead research. Not present; referenced in dialogue only.

- Rezmir's written note reveals internal tensions within the Cult: she was subjected to an unauthorized audit of her operations at the Black Dragon Hatchery and accused infiltrators of being enemy spies. The note argues for collective oversight or none at all, framing unilateral audits as potential enemy manipulation.
- Rezmir's remains and the note together suggest that internal political disputes may have contributed to her death, corroborating speculation that she was not killed solely by external heroes.

**Sandesyl's Necromantic Operation (informal faction)**
- Confirmed to have successfully converted the castle's post-war Frost Giant occupants into undead, establishing undead control over castle spaces.
- Maintains a hidden lair carved into glacier ice, giving her freedom of movement within and around the castle without requiring control of the castle itself.
- Has been assessed as having advance warning if the party eliminates the Ogre Zombies, meaning the lair assault will provoke an immediate and escalated response.

## Revealed Information

- Sandesyl is a disciple of Sammaster and conducts necromantic experiments in the creation of specters through soul extraction from living victims.
- The specters and undead infesting the castle were the products of Sandesyl's experiments, not a naturally occurring phenomenon or Blagothkus's doing.
- Sandesyl has a hidden lair carved into the glacier's ice, separate from the caves used by the Dracolich, which explains her ability to appear throughout the castle at will.
- Rezmir's note reveals that the Cult of the Dragon suffered internal political disputes, with at least one faction conducting unauthorized audits of other members' operations, which Rezmir characterized as infiltration by enemy spies.
- The Frost Giant Jarl who previously occupied the castle was transformed into undead by Sandesyl and was the skeletal giant the party already destroyed.
- Escarlotta cannot be controlled by Sandesyl, which was a limiting factor that drove Sandesyl to deal with Blagothkus instead of simply taking over the castle.
- The All-Father Oracle told Blagothkus he was a failure and that his son was the destined giant leader — contingent on the son killing his father. The son failed this task and is dead.
- Countess Sansuri is making a political play for leadership of the Ordning, which Escarlotta views as an existential affront.
