## NPC Activity

**Adimond Kroskas**
- Referenced but absent. His most recent manipulation of Thistle was revealed at session open: he covered Thistle in a green gooey substance that evaporated, leaving Thistle with dragon eyes and the True Seeing ability. This is described as another in a series of unwanted experiments/transformations performed on Thistle.

**Queen Serissa**
- Present at the Maelstrom at session open alongside the Imperator and storm giant guards. Attempted to calm Thistle and redirect the group toward saving her father, Hekaton. Held up a wooden coin bearing a golden goose — a gambling chip — and presented Unla Kee's identification of it as evidence pointing toward Yartar and a vessel called *The Golden Goose*.

**The Imperator (Storm Giant)**
- Present at the Maelstrom. Attempted to de-escalate Thistle's distress and noted that the dragon eyes come with the blessing of True Seeing. Reminded the group of the Maelstrom's teleportation device and asked where they wished to travel.

**Unla Kee**
- Identified the wooden coin with a golden goose as a gambling chip from a "booze cruiser" in Yartar called *The Golden Goose*. Claims he was denied entry to the vessel — attributes this to racism against diviners. Teleported with the group to Blagothkus' castle. Advocated strongly for removing Sandesyl from the castle. Engaged Glazhael with a more diplomatic opening after Orsik's initial attempt failed. Raised ethical concerns about racism and moral relativism throughout the session.

**Blagothkus (Cloud Giant)**
- Present in the castle control room. Received Thistle's report on events at the Maelstrom. Expressed horror upon hearing about Adimond Kroskas. Identified the blue dragon described by Thistle as "the Bane of the Desert" (Ilymrith), an ancient and powerful blue dragon. Stated urgency to find Hekaton, arguing his strength is needed to defeat Ilymrith. Noted a reformed but still fragile relationship with Escarlotta. Has a changed attitude toward dragons — no longer wishes to consume them but maintains a "live and let live at a distance" philosophy, which other characters characterize as segregationist.

**Escarlotta (Cloud Giant)**
- Present in the castle control room. Informed the group that in their absence, the castle's vampire had to be fed; she and Blagothkus directed Sandesyl's spawn to feed on a tribe of Uthgards spotted below. Reacted with apparent embarrassment (the moonstones turned red) when pressed. Implied a veiled threat to Unla Kee when discussing reform vs. killing. Redirected the group to debrief on the Maelstrom.

**Sandesyl (Vampire)**
- Occupies a tower within Blagothkus' castle, concealed in magical darkness. Fed her spawn using Uthgard tribespeople arranged by Escarlotta during the party's absence. Met with the party in her darkened tower. Offered to vacate the castle in exchange for a quest: retrieve a specific arcane tome from Xonathal's Keep. Revealed details about the tome's history and her long-term plans. Expressed intense, near-romantic obsession with Ilymrith, the ancient blue dragon, and desires to use the tome to convince Ilymrith to become a Dracolich. Disclosed that her true goal is to reanimate elements of the Cult of the Dragon — specifically the traditionalist faction that seeks to turn all dragons into Dracoliches rather than summon Tiamat. Cannot retrieve the tome herself because Xonathal's Keep involves an interdimensional puzzle constantly bathed in bright sunlight. Holds Glazhael's phylactery and uses it to control the Dracolich.

**Sandesyl's Spawn (unnamed)**
- Briefly visible to Thistle (via True Seeing through the magical darkness). Rolled his eyes at Sandesyl when she denied the tome was the final book in a romance series. No further details.

**Glazhael the Undying (White Dracolich, formerly Glazhael the Cloudchaser)**
- Located in the glacier vault beneath Blagothkus' castle, clinging to the ceiling above a treasure hoard. Provided a full account of her history when engaged diplomatically by Unla Kee. Expressed deep resentment at being controlled by Sandesyl via her phylactery. Agreed to a covert arrangement with the party: she will intervene against Sandesyl at the critical moment — when the party has nearly won — and claims to know a secret method to kill the vampire. Refused to act openly before that moment, fearing Sandesyl would destroy the phylactery. Her phylactery's current location is in Sandesyl's possession.

**Ilymrith ("The Bane of the Desert" / "The Doom of the Desert")**
- Referenced but absent. Identified by Blagothkus as an ancient blue dragon. Described by Sandesyl with near-reverential obsession. Has been actively undermining the Giant world, not out of care for giants, but to gain access to the Storm Giant's castle infrastructure for her own hybridization experiments — specifically experiments crossing dragons with fey. Revealed (via Sandesyl's slip) to be the dragon behind Adimond Kroskas' fey-dragon hybrid experiments on Thistle. Sandesyl hopes to convert her into a Dracolich using the tome from Xonathal's Keep.

**Talis the White (Wyrmspeaker, deceased/absent)**
- Referenced in Glazhael's account. Was Glazhael's partner and the original White Wyrmspeaker before being displaced by Severin's declaration of a new Wyrmspeaker. Directed Glazhael to spy on a settlement near the Mkoz glacier. Described as "no more" following the destruction of the Cult of the Dragon.

**Severin (Cult of the Dragon leader, deceased/absent)**
- Referenced in Sandesyl's and Glazhael's accounts. Led the Cult of the Dragon's effort to bring Tiamat into the world. Created an inner-circle split between apocalypse-seekers (summon Tiamat) and traditionalists (convert dragons to Dracoliches). Offered the Blue Dragon Mask to a traditionalist to investigate it, which required a tome now held in Xonathal's Keep. Declared that a Dracolich could not serve the cult, which led to Glazhael's estrangement.

**Jorgen (Cult mage, absent)**

**Iskander (Cult inner circle member, absent)**
- Referenced in Sandesyl's account. Betrayed the Cult of the Dragon to the Lord's Alliance, which sent the Heroes of the War of the Dragon to Xonathal's Tower. The heroes secured the Blue Dragon Mask but failed to retrieve the tome Sandesyl now seeks.

**Stroud (Dwarf, "Heroes of the War of the Dragon," absent)**

## Faction Movements

**Cult of the Dragon (Traditionalist Remnant)**
- Sandesyl is actively working to revive traditionalist cult operations — specifically the faction that seeks to convert dragons into Dracoliches rather than summon Tiamat. Her acquisition of the tome from Xonathal's Keep is the first step in this effort. She intends to use the tome to recruit Ilymrith as a Dracolich convert. The cult's formal structure was destroyed following the War of the Dragon, but Sandesyl views herself as a continuity agent for the traditionalist wing.

**The Kraken Society / Ilymrith's Network (implied)**
- Ilymrith has been systematically undermining the Giant social order (the Ordning) not for political reasons but to gain access to Storm Giant infrastructure for hybridization experiments. These experiments now confirmed to include fey-dragon hybridization — directly responsible for what has been done to Thistle. Adimond Kroskas appears to be operating as an agent or collaborator of Ilymrith's experimental program.

**Storm Giant Court (Maelstrom)**
- Queen Serissa presented actionable intelligence (the Golden Goose chip) and offered use of the Maelstrom's teleportation device. The court's immediate goal remains finding and rescuing King Hekaton. Serissa was previously revealed (this session, from Blagothkus' horrified reaction) to have been cooperating with Ilymrith — described as "that fool Serissa was working with her."
