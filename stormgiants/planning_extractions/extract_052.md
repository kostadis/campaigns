## NPC Activity

**Maergun (Fomorian)**
- Appeared in his throne room with a cyclops, displacer beast, and the captive Drauchanar chained overhead.
- Named his displacer beast "Haerbega." When the party mocked the name, he engaged in brief banter before attempting his Evil Eye on Vardis.
- Struck Vardis with his club after the Evil Eye failed to fully take hold.
- Killed by Vardis via cantrip after being severely wounded by combined party and goblin attacks.
- His throne concealed an iron key that opened a secret staircase to a treasure vault.

**Drauchanar**
- Found chained from the ceiling of Maergun's throne room, alive but exhausted.
- Freed by Vardis after the Fomorian's death.
- Immediately claimed credit for the party's success, asserting he had allowed himself to be captured as a leadership strategy and that he knew Vardis would see through the waterfall illusion.
- Last seen in the throne room area following his release.

**Tlega (Goblin Leader)**
- Present in the corridor leading to the Fomorian's chamber.
- Repeatedly ordered troops forward while remaining in the rear, refusing to engage in personal combat.
- When confronted by Orsik about his lack of fighting, declared "We lead!" and refused to elaborate.
- Eventually threw a tantrum at dissenting goblins and threw a javelin at the Fomorian to quiet them, succeeding in maintaining troop morale.

**Mirk (Goblin Leader)**
- Present during the Fomorian battle.
- Initially did nothing but praise goblin archers.
- Faced dissension among goblin ranks who felt he was slacking.
- Threw a tantrum and hurled a javelin at the Fomorian to demonstrate engagement; successfully restored goblin morale and enthusiasm.

**Aeglossil (Vampire)**
- Found in a secret tomb southeast of Maergun's vault, lying on a raised platform in the form of a white stone statue of a sleeping elven woman.
- The tomb was decorated with delicate bas-relief elven nature designs and was being cleaned reverentially by two duergar.
- Awakened when Thistle kissed her while under her charm influence.
- Immediately grappled and bit Orsik upon waking, feeding for the first time in what appeared to be an extremely long period; her pallor receded slightly after feeding.
- Snarled in fear when Vardis presented the symbol of Corellon.
- Banished to another plane by Unla Kee using the Banishment spell.
- The duergar in her tomb remained afterward, waiting for her return.
- Referenced as having charmed Thistle during the encounter; the charm broke when she was banished.

**The Knife**
- Met the party outside after they exited the Feywild.
- Operating under the belief that Orsik is Prince Thornacious; actively sucked up to "Thornacious" and attempted to defend his authority when Thistle questioned Vardis.
- Offered to lead the party to a portal but was pre-empted by Thistle.
- Last seen in the Feywild/transition area following the party's exit.

**Colorful Sprite (Queen Titania's messenger)**
- Reappeared after the Fomorian's death to relay Queen Titania's pleasure.
- Delivered a Blessed Horn as Titania's gift to the party.
- Opened a portal for the party following the gift exchange.

**Queen Titania**
- Not physically present; communicated through the sprite.
- Expressed pleasure at the Fomorian's death.
- Gifted the party a Blessed Horn described as "a magical item of raw and incredible powers."
- Referenced as being powerful, vindictive, and known for attaching strings to gifts.

**Delsephine**
- Waiting at the portal exit when the party returned; only moments had passed for her.
- Reacted to Orsik's aged appearance with confusion; accepted his cover story ("I am Orsik's father, Orsic with a c") with a knowing smile, apparently understanding the situation but choosing not to expose it.
- Agreed to transport the party to Beorunna's Well and later to the Sky Pony spirit mound.
- Declined Orsik's invitation to join them in the field with a definitive "no."
- Last seen aboard her airship.

**Bressik (Sage of Candlekeep)**
- Provided paid briefings on both Beorunna's Well and the Sky Pony spirit mound's One Stone.
- Charged 50 gold per briefing (party paid in mixed coinage; he requested standardization).
- Placed a personal wager on the party's success in retrieving the artifact from Beorunna's Well; declined to increase the wager despite prompting.
- Described as a sage "reduced to working on an airship for a dragon."
- Assessed that the relic at Beorunna's Well was beneath the altar; assessed the One Stone itself (not beneath it) was the artifact at the Sky Pony mound, though Orsik overruled this assessment.
- Last seen aboard Delsephine's airship.

**Beorunna's Well Shaman (Uthgardt, unnamed)**
- Heard the party descend and roused the barbarian defenders and the manticore.
- Attempted to cast spells during combat; was placed under Unla Kee's Enemies Abound, causing him to strike his own tribesmen.
- Recovered from the spell when a tribesman physically punched him; resumed directing defenders to protect the relics.
- Attempted a ritual spell during the battle that created a protective effect around the rope exit (animated rocks attacking climbers).
- Appeared to die from a glancing arrow blow from Thistle late in the battle.
- Revealed to have faked death; regained consciousness after the party left.
- Upon waking and seeing the staged scene implicating the Red Lion tribe, concluded it was better to pursue the thieves than allow a tribal civil war.
- Last known status: alive, alone in Beorunna's Well, having decided to pursue the 3HPP.

**Manticore (Beorunna's Well, unnamed)**
- Served as the manticore guardian of Beorunna's Well cave, roused by the barbarian shaman.
- Attacked Thistle and Unla Kee with spike volleys; first volley missed, second was more effective against Thistle.
- Retreated to hide behind stalactites after being wounded by Thistle's arrows.
- Killed by Thistle's Fireball.
- Lair explored by Thistle post-battle; found to contain nothing of value beyond bones.

**Cult of the Black Earth Riders (two unnamed humans)**
- Arrived at the Sky Pony spirit mound on Bulettes, emerging from the ground.
- One attempted to cast Hold Person on Orsik and Vardis; the spell was countered by Unla Kee.
- The other used a commanding magical voice to order the party to flee; party resisted.
- One declared "You shall not seize the stone from the cult of the black earth!" before dying.
- The surviving rider attempted to flee but was cut down by Orsik.
- Both killed; their stone breastplates (described as a strange, previously unknown construction) and other equipment were looted.

**Sky Pony Shaman (unnamed)**
- Emerged from the tree line after the battle with the Cult of the Black Earth riders concluded.
- Accepted Vardis' framing that the party had defended the stone from thieves.
- Upon seeing Unla Kee's pegasus wings, declared him the "Sky Pony Avatar."
- Proposed a massive festival in Unla Kee's honor and offered to bring 1,000 warriors to guard the stone.
- Accepted Unla Kee's command to send the tribe on a seven-day vision quest (three days out, one day fasting, three days back) as divine penance for failing to protect the stone.
- Led the tribe away, with the party left alone at the boulder for seven days.
- Last seen departing the spirit mound with the tribe.

**Thistle's Uncle Joon (referenced, absent)**
- Mentioned in conversation; had a dispute with the Sky Pony tribe over an unspecified matter of honor.
- Described by Thistle as having been "kicked out of the Court for being a drunk," suggesting his characterization of the Sky Ponies' insult as being "beyond the pale for a member of Queen Titania's court" may be self-serving.

- The party left a stone carving of Unla Kee and a magical water pool (from an enchanted egg and bean) as gifts, with the hope the tribe would find them meaningful.
- Will return in approximately seven days to find the One Stone missing.

## Revealed Information

- **Aeglossil** is a vampire of apparent extreme age, having been encased in stone for long enough that she was described as recovering from starvation upon feeding. She had an active duergar servant presence maintaining her tomb.
- **The Cult of the Black Earth** is actively pursuing Giant relics (specifically the One Stone at the Sky Pony mound) and fields operatives mounted on Bulettes. Their stone breastplate armor is an unusual and previously unidentified construction.
- **Queen Titania** confirmed as pleased by the Fomorian's death and willing to gift the party; Thistle warns that her gifts always come with strings attached.
- **Beorunna's Well relic**: Located beneath a multi-stone blood-splattered altar; successfully retrieved by the 3HPP by smashing the altar.
- **The One Stone**: The artifact is the boulder itself, not something beneath it (confirmed by Unla Kee's examination). It can be shrunken and enlarged three times daily and can produce an effect similar to a Wand of Wonder. Activation requires tracing lines in the correct order (causes blistering damage to the tracer).
- **The Sky Pony tribe's shaman** believes Unla Kee is a divine avatar of Uthgar; this belief was manufactured but accepted fully.
- **Orsik's disguise as Prince Thornacious** is maintained; Delsephine appears to have deduced the truth but is keeping it secret. The Knife remains fully deceived.
