## NPC Activity

**Cambions (unnamed, plural)**
- Appeared in Silverymoon pursuing the party over "their master's treasure"
- Attempted to charm Unla Kee and Orsik; both failed
- Pursued the party through the streets using flight and offensive spells, striking Unla Kee repeatedly
- One killed Unla Kee with a spell during the chase
- A second entered the house of Vinby Hearthgood; delivered ultimatum to Orsik demanding silence and departure from the city by the next day
- Killed Vinby Hearthgood's unnamed halfling companion (or household member) when Orsik did not immediately comply
- Referenced as servants of an unnamed master whose treasure was stolen
- Last known status: departed after delivering ultimatum; not apprehended

**Nyalah Uuseff**
- Tavern keeper of the Dancing Goat in Silverymoon
- Dressed in formal silk and gold
- Responded with skepticism to Orsik's report of demonic creatures ("impossible, such creatures do not exist in Silverymoon")
- Summoned the Silverwatch (city guard) to investigate

**Silverwatch Captain (unnamed)**
- Arrived at the Dancing Goat in response to Nyalah's summons
- Escorted the party to the Star Court
- Facilitated the detour to Mielikki's grove for Unla Kee's resurrection
- Received confirmation from the high priest that the demonic attack was genuine; tasked with investigating

**High Priest of Mielikki (unnamed)**
- Presided over Mielikki's grove in Silverymoon
- Expressed shock at the report of a demon killing Unla Kee
- Performed resurrection of Unla Kee; payment was one magical ring (taken from Unla Kee)
- Confirmed to the Silverwatch Captain that the demonic attack was true

**Vinby Hearthgood**
- Halfling resident of Silverymoon; well-dressed, wealthy, living in a finely appointed home
- Was present when Orsik burst into his house fleeing the Cambions
- Appeared as a witness in Star Court against Orsik for breaking and entering
- Testified that Orsik smashed into his house; made no mention of the demonic attack

**Xamlyn Margaster (Lady Margaster)**
- Beautiful woman in fine robes, attended by two men described as gorgeous
- Appeared in Star Court as the accusing party, identifying the group as thieves
- Sought an "arrangement" with the party rather than full legal proceedings
- When the Judge proposed using magic spells (court magister) to verify claims, she refused, denied all wrongdoing, reframed events as a drunken party gone wrong, and departed
- Identified as a powerful and wealthy noble making sizeable investments in Silverymoon and connected to Waterdeep
- The Cambions are implied to be agents working for her or her house ("our master's treasure")
- Last known status: departed Star Court; still present in Silverymoon making investments

**Xamlyn's Two Men (unnamed)**
- Flanked Lady Margaster in Star Court
- Confirmed the party's identity as thieves when asked; described in a "silky voice"
- Likely Cambions or fiendish associates given context, though not explicitly stated in court

**The Judge (unnamed)**

**Dwarven Gate Guard of Everlund (unnamed)**
- Older dwarven guard at Everlund's gate
- Expressed intense hatred of Silverymoon, citing the city's inaction during the War of the Silver Marches and Sundabar's destruction
- Noted Everlund had left the Lords' Alliance in protest, voting three to two among the Council of Elders
- Last known location: Everlund gate

**Miery**
- Older woman in Everlund, speaking publicly to young men about the threat of the Kraken
- Runs what appears to be an anti-Kraken order or militia group
- Charges five gold pieces for membership with a certificate
- Tasked the party (in lieu of payment) with retrieving a stolen artifact — "the Star of Light" — from kobolds in a cave a few miles from Everlund
- Described the artifact as symbolic with no magical function
- Her name is a deliberate reference to the goddess Mielikki (her mother's choice)
- Last known location: Everlund streets near main thoroughfare

**Dral Thelev**

**Krowen Valharrow**

- Offered Harper resources — specifically access to a network of teleportation circles across the North — in exchange for the party investigating and resolving the sundering of the Ordning
- Delivered a coffer of spell scrolls sent by Darathra Shendrel as incentive gifts
- Last known location: Moongleam Tower, Everlund

**Darathra Shendrel**
- Not physically present; referenced by Dral Thelev as the sender of the platinum brooch
- Had prepared a coffer of spell scrolls as gifts for agents undertaking the giant quest ("a birthday present for exactly such a moment")

## Faction Movements

**House Margaster**
- Lady Xamlyn Margaster is actively investing money in Silverymoon's post-war economic recovery, giving her significant political leverage over the city's judiciary
- The Cambions working for "their master" (implied: Margaster) were operating covertly in Silverymoon pursuing stolen treasure
- Xamlyn avoided magical interrogation in Star Court and departed without legal consequence, demonstrating her protected status in the city
- The party has confirmed House Margaster's use of fiendish agents (Cambions) as enforcers

**The Harpers**
- Operating a covert insertion network through Everlund: Dral Thelev at Danniver's Tavern acts as a cut-out, receiving Harper contacts via recognition tokens (platinum brooch) and teleporting them to Moongleam Tower
- Krowen Valharrow is actively recruiting agents to investigate the sundering of the Ordning, treating the giant crisis as an under-watched threat
- Possess a network of teleportation circles covering the North, offered as an operational resource to the party
- Darathra Shendrel had pre-positioned gifts (spell scrolls) for this recruitment moment

**Lords' Alliance**
- Referenced as monitoring other factions in Everlund, to the point of missing the giant threat entirely (per Krowen)
- Everlund has formally separated from the Lords' Alliance, citing the alliance's failure to aid Sundabar during the War of the Silver Marches

**Zhentarim**
- Referenced as actively surveilling other factions in Everlund (per Krowen); noted by Orsik as lacking teleportation circle infrastructure comparable to the Harpers'

**Anti-Kraken Order (Miery's group, unnamed)**
- Small, locally operating group in Everlund warning of the Kraken's return
- Currently missing an artifact ("the Star of Light") stolen by kobolds in a nearby cave
- Membership-based organization; financially strained enough to barter artifact retrieval for initiation fees

## Threat Arc Events

**Kraken Society Echoes**
- Miery publicly warned in Everlund that the Kraken will return with powerful allies to destroy the city; specifically mentioned mind control as one of the Kraken's weapons
- An organized (if small) order exists in Everlund dedicated to monitoring and preparing for the Kraken's return, suggesting the threat has local historical precedent

**House Margaster / Fiendish Infiltration**
- Cambions operating openly (if covertly) within Silverymoon as agents of House Margaster, using charm spells and lethal force against civilians (killed Vinby Hearthgood's associate) with no legal accountability
- Lady Margaster successfully leveraged economic influence to shut down a legitimate court inquiry, including blocking magical verification of testimony

**Giant Threat / Ordning Sundering**
- Krowen Valharrow confirmed the Harpers are aware that the Ordning of the Giants has been broken and are treating it as a serious, under-monitored threat
- Noted that all major factions (Zhentarim, Lords' Alliance) are preoccupied watching each other, leaving the giant crisis unaddressed

## Revealed Information

- **Lady Xamlyn Margaster employs Cambions as agents** and is connected to the theft accusation against the party; the Cambions explicitly named "their master's treasure" as the object of pursuit, linking fiendish muscle directly to House Margaster's interests
- **Silverymoon's judiciary is compromised by Margaster money**: the Judge openly admitted prioritizing her investment over legal process
- **The Harper covert network in Everlund** routes contacts through Danniver's Tavern (Dral Thelev) to Moongleam Tower to avoid faction surveillance — the platinum brooch is a recognition token for this network
- **Darathra Shendrel is Dral Thelev's Harper contact** and is the origin of the party's platinum brooch; Dral's concern that "something must be amiss" implies she should have come personally
- **The Ordning's sundering is known to the Harpers** but the cause remains unknown; Krowen offered resources in exchange for the party finding out and either fixing it or reporting back
- **Everlund left the Lords' Alliance** three-to-two Council vote, in protest of the alliance's inaction during the War of the Silver Marches, specifically the abandonment of Sundabar
- **Thistle confirmed** (via Uncle Joon) that Silverymoon "betrayed the Silver Marches and their dwarf allies" — corroborating the dwarven guard's account
- **Cyric is banned from Silverymoon** (established by Uncle Joon's prior instruction to Thistle)
