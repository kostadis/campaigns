## NPC Activity

**Sandesyl** (vampire): Confirmed dead (staked in her coffin by Orsik). Her perfectly preserved corpse was found inside the coffin alongside Glazhael's phylactery. Unla Kee briefly considered using telekinesis to manipulate her and contemplated the power of becoming a vampire before Orsik intervened and killed her in her coffin.

**Glazhael the Undying** (dracolich): Located in her lair at the glacier's center. Was surprised the party survived their encounter with Sandesyl. Directed the party to Sandesyl's coffin (concealed behind an ice wall she shattered with her tail). Received her phylactery box from Unla Kee. Claimed ignorance about how to kill a vampire. Departed the castle after receiving her phylactery, fulfilling her end of the deal.

**Blagothkus** (cloud giant): Confronted by his wife Escarlotta over his deals with dragons and a vampire to reacquire the castle. When Thistle revealed that their son Eigeron is a ghost and implied Blagothkus was responsible, Blagothkus admitted only that "it was an accident." Was killed by the party and Brunvild before he could react.

**Escarlotta** (cloud giant, castle's golem-form consciousness): Furious at Blagothkus upon learning the true circumstances of how he reacquired the castle. Devastated to learn her son Eigeron is trapped as a ghost. Ordered Blagothkus killed to free Eigeron. Asserted full ownership of the castle, granted the party safe passage as guests in recognition of saving her son. Redirected the party toward their primary mission — finding Hekaton — and is steering the castle toward Yartar. Possesses awareness of the party's multiple mission threads.

**Brunvild** (frost giant or similar): Informed Escarlotta of the real story behind Blagothkus's deal. Attempted to claim salvage rights to the castle after Blagothkus's death. Backed down when Escarlotta asserted ownership. Delivered the killing blow to Blagothkus using one of Blagothkus's own hammers. Retreated to find sleeping quarters aboard the castle.

**Eigeron** (ghost, son of Blagothkus and Escarlotta): Referenced but not present. Previously encountered at the Eye of the All-Father. Implied to have been turned into a ghost by Blagothkus (described as "an accident" by Blagothkus). His unresolved state motivated Escarlotta to order Blagothkus's death.

**Lord Khaspere Drylund** (human, owner of the Grand Dame gambling vessel): Not present. Identified as the owner of the Grand Dame, currently docked at Yartar. Previously refused the party entry to his vessel. Named as the party's primary intelligence target — they plan to use their wealth to gain access and compel him to talk.

- **Hekaton clue**: A wooden gambling chip from a casino, traced to Yartar and the Grand Dame, a gambling vessel owned by Lord Khaspere Drylund. Queen Serissa provided this clue; its reliability is uncertain.
- **Lord Drylund**: Previously denied the party access to the Grand Dame. The party intends to use their newfound wealth to gain entry and pressure him for information.
