## NPC Activity

**Kaelen** (actor/producer/playwright): Presented an initial script that was a creative disaster. When confronted by Unla Kee, his confidence collapsed immediately. Rewrote the script in archaic common before being redirected. Became ecstatic when Unla Kee devised the "play within a play" framing. Revealed to be unusually attached to the bizarre, incomprehensible ending. Post-performance, it emerged that the entire play was intentionally designed as a summoning ritual — the confusing ending was the ritual's mechanism, and the theatergoers were intended as sacrifices to tempt a demon through a gate. Kaelen had planned the play as revenge on the theater-going public that had never recognized his genius, intending the demon to lay waste to the town. Upon witnessing the play's success instead, he abandoned his grievance and declared he would be famous.

**Everard Barney** (director): Heavy-set man in a multi-colored shirt and purple scarf, described as unimposing. From a family of bookkeepers; became a director to escape accounting and fears being forced to return to Waterdeep if the production fails. Has no understanding of actor motivations. Unable to manage the lead actor or the drunk supporting cast. Was coached by Thistle through the production. Accepted guidance on staging and on the incomprehensible finale, which he was told to leave unchanged.

**Amy** (lead actor): Status-driven and image-conscious despite projecting a pleasant, noncommittal demeanor. Avoids definitive statements and deflects confrontation, suggesting a hidden past. Was timid in pushing her own creative ideas against Kaelen's direction. Coached by Orsik in disguise as "Geoff Mamet," who redirected her energy and compliance. Post-coaching, her performance improved noticeably. Did not recognize Orsik as her coach, believing Orsik had hired the fictional Geoff Mamet on her behalf.

**Urgala** (innkeeper, former operative): Briefed the party on the production crisis and the financial stakes (Zhentarim and Hand of Yartar will collect if the play fails to run a second night). Knew the location of Chancellor Geoffrey's CGI device and the kobold tribe that had stolen it, knowledge she attributes to her pre-innkeeper life. Confirmed the play's success post-performance and reported audience demand for a second night.

**Bob Saget** (demon): Appeared at the end of the play, summoned through the ritual embedded in the script. Described as demon-like in appearance — purple skin stretched over a skull, jagged teeth, enormous purple-irised eyes, purple top hat and formal jacket with animate tassels, white gloves. Introduces himself with pronouns he/him. Self-described as "the tormentor of kings who killed their jesters," specializing in tormenting rulers with "dad jokes" for eternity. Claims to have been tormenting kings since ancient times and had not been summoned in eons. Expressed delight at escaping the Abyss and welcomed the new audience. Declined to fight. Became the star attraction of the production.

**Chancellor Geoffrey** (referenced, absent): A chancellor who uses the CGI mirror-ball device to create elaborate animated invitations. Has been searching for the device since kobolds stole it from one of his messengers. Not present in the session.

Warren includes guard posts, tripwire traps, fire beetle lighting, an ogre guardian, an altar to Grumsh (a recent addition over an existing monolith), and an illusory outdoor chamber projected by the mirror ball. Vardis infiltrated, triggered a tripwire, was released by a guard, and escaped with the device after incapacitating the ogre with a warding flare and using a fireball against pursuing kobolds and centipede swarms.

**Zhentarim / Hand of Yartar**: Referenced as creditors who will extract payment if the play does not succeed into a second night. No direct appearance or action this session; their threat provided the financial stakes driving the party's efforts.

## Threat Arc Events

**Planar Distortion / Demonic Incursion**: A demon (Bob Saget) was successfully summoned through a ritual concealed within a theatrical script. The ritual used an audience of theatergoers as intended sacrifices to open a gate from the Abyss. The summoning succeeded despite the party's extensive modifications to the script and staging, suggesting the core ritual structure survived their edits. This represents a completed planar breach — a demon has crossed from the Abyss into the Material Plane in Yartar.

## Revealed Information

- The play Kaelen wrote was not a creative project but a deliberately engineered summoning ritual, with the theatergoers as intended sacrificial fuel.
- Kaelen's motive was revenge against the theater-going public for ignoring his genius; he intended the demon to destroy the audience and the town.
- The bizarre, incomprehensible ending Kaelen was so protective of was the functional core of the summoning ritual.
- Bob Saget has not been summoned in eons, suggesting this class of ritual or this demon's name has been lost or suppressed for a very long time.
- Urgala has intelligence connections and operational knowledge beyond innkeeping — she knew the precise location of a stolen magical device and the tribe that held it without explanation.
- The CGI mirror-ball device belongs to Chancellor Geoffrey and was stolen by kobolds from his messenger.
