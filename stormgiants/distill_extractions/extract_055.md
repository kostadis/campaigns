## Locations

**Raven's Rock**
- What it is: Spirit mound of the Black Raven and Gray Wolf Uthgardt tribes; located on the icy slopes of the Spine of the World; a geographical anomaly perpetually covered in snow regardless of season; cause unknown
- Key features: 100-foot-tall stone carved as a perching raven at the center of the highest plateau, facing west; four 50-foot-tall menhirs used by shamans to track seasons and stars; a wolf-shaped depression to the southwest containing a Gray Wolf altar (rectangular stone slab with moon phases carved on its sides); timber roosts for giant ravens
- What happened there: The 3HPP excavated the Gray Wolf altar mound and retrieved a thigh bone artifact; Thistle spoke with the murder of giant ravens; the group departed rapidly after the ravens gave an ambiguous warning
- Current state: Artifact removed; site disturbed; giant ravens aware of who took the artifact; Uthgardt arrival potentially imminent

**Great Worm Cavern**
- What it is: Spirit mound of the Great Worm Uthgardt tribe; located inside a cavern at the northeast end of the Valley of Khedrun, deep in the Spine of the World
- Key features: Thick ice at the pillared entrance; interior walls covered in icy glaze and massive icicles (too slippery to climb without gear or magic); cavern floor is a sheet of slippery ice; 70-foot-high flanking ledges with two totem poles of chiseled ice carved as winged snakes; caves in ledge walls where tribe members live (warmed by natural steam vents); narrow crypts above the ledges where honored dead stand frozen; a triangular promontory of ice-covered rock 120 feet high at its peak; an altar of frost-covered stone carved as a coiled winged serpent (representing Elrem the Great Worm)
- The gong: A 13-foot-diameter circular disk, once a frost giant champion's shield, broken in combat with a large piece missing; weighs 250 pounds; made of red dragon scales bolted to a beaten copper frame; leather arm straps gone; suspended by ropes that could be cut with two sword swings
- What happened there: Great Worm tribe defenders were killed by fireballs from Unla Kee and Vardis; a large worm-like creature burst from a snowbank and rose up aggressively
- Current state: Defenders dead; creature emerging and apparently attacking; altar not yet reached

**Valley of Khedrun**
- What it is: A valley in the Spine of the World; northeast end contains the entrance to Great Worm Cavern
- Current state: Traversed; no other details given

**Spine of the World**
- What it is: A mountain range; location of both Raven's Rock and Great Worm Cavern; flown over by the Dragon's Breath
- Current state: No change noted

**Morgur's Mound**
- What it is: Referenced by Orsik as a previous site of digging; no further details given
- Current state: Previously excavated; Orsik was irritated by the digging there

**Dragon's Breath**
- What it is: Airship commanded by Delsephine; used as transport and base of operations for the 3HPP's journey
- What happened there: Multiple research presentations by Bressik; contract negotiations; sale of climbing equipment; repositioned from Raven's Rock to Great Worm Cavern
- Current state: Parked at or near Great Worm Cavern

## Threads & Mysteries

- **The ravens' warning ("Nevermore"):** The lead giant raven said "Nevermore" to Thistle; Thistle does not know what this means and could not remember what Uncle Joon said about it. It is unclear whether this means the ravens will tell the Uthgardt, will never tell, or something else entirely.
- **Uthgardt arrival at Raven's Rock:** The ravens said the Uthgardt could arrive "today, tomorrow, or five from now." It is unresolved whether Uthgardt warriors will pursue the 3HPP.
- **The thigh bone artifact:** Retrieved from the Gray Wolf altar at Raven's Rock; its nature, purpose, and how it fits into the larger artifact collection is unresolved.
- **The gong at Great Worm Cavern:** Bressik presented two theories — it is the artifact, or it is merely decorative. Orsik wants to acquire it regardless; the party has not yet reached it. Orsik suggested gifting it to Harshnag if it functions as a shield.
- **The worm creature:** A large creature resembling a worm-centipede-dragon hybrid with many legs, wing-like membranes, and mandibles has burst from a snowbank inside Great Worm Cavern. It is unclear if this is Elrem the Great Worm itself or another creature; combat appears imminent.
- **Elrem the Great Worm:** The Great Worm tribe makes sacrifices at the altar to prevent Elrem from returning to devour them. It is unresolved whether the creature that emerged is Elrem.
- **Klamath the Red Dragon:** Location is unknown; only Delsephine knows. He promises to meet and reward the party once the giants are dealt with. His necromantic nature and the undead clause in Bressik's contract are unresolved plot elements.
- **Bressik's future:** His living contract was purchased by the 3HPP, freeing him from Klamath. He is now free to do as he wishes; it is unresolved whether he will join the party formally or independently.
- **The undead clause of Bressik's contract:** The undead portion of the contract (worth ~13,000 gold) was not purchased; Klamath still holds a claim on Bressik's undead existence. Delsephine noted Bressik's remains could be used in necromantic constructs if his body is desecrated.
- **The "Gong Show" sequel play:** Orsik and Unla Kee plan a new theatrical production; Bob needs to be told to wind down the current play. The residuals deal with Delsephine will not apply to this sequel due to how Unla Kee structured the contract. Whether Delsephine will discover and react to this is unresolved.
- **The perpetual snow at Raven's Rock:** The geographical anomaly of permanent snow coverage has no known cause; Bressik's research revealed nothing. Left as an open mystery.
- **Harshnag:** Referenced as a potential recipient of the gong; his current location and relationship to the party are not elaborated upon in this session.
- **Uncle Joon:** Referenced by Thistle as someone who apparently said something relevant about "Nevermore"; no further details given.
- **The giants:** Referenced as the broader threat the party is working against; no direct encounter in this session.
