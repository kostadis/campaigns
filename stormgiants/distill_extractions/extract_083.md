- **The escaped Wizard**: The Kraken Society archmage escaped via contingency teleport and was not found by Thistle's True Seeing. His identity, allegiances beyond the Kraken Society, and current location are unknown. He may return.
- **The First Mate's fate**: Last seen staggering inside the cabin after being shot; his survival or death is unconfirmed.
- **Uthor's "other business"**: Uthor accepted dismissal with a smile and stated he had other business to attend to. His intentions and destination are entirely unknown. His deception of Hekaton and apparently unconcerned departure suggest a hidden agenda.
- **Nym and Mirran's fates**: Hekaton stated he would decide their fates later. No judgment has been rendered. Their alliance with Iymrith and extent of their betrayal may be explored further.
- **Thistle's girlfriend**: Located somewhere near Duke Zalto's domain; not yet rescued; identity not given in this chapter.
- **The map to treasure**: In Vardis's awareness; located near Duke Zalto's abode; not yet retrieved.
- **Escarlotta's demands**: Thistle knows Escarlotta will be displeased if Countess Sansuri is not defeated; this creates a thread requiring engagement with the Cloud Giants.
- **The five rogue giant lords**: Hekaton's offer to join the final fight against Iymrith is conditional on the party dealing with Chief Guh (Hill Giants), Thane Kayalithca (Stone Giants), Jarl Storvald (Frost Giants), Countess Sansuri (Cloud Giants), and Duke Zalto (Fire Giants). None have been dealt with yet in this chapter.
- **Iymrith ("the Doom of the Desert")**: Ancient blue dragon; at large; sought by Unla Kee immediately; Hekaton wants to join a final battle against her. Her current location and immediate plans are unknown.
- **The Ordning**: Hekaton stated he can now restore the Ordning as it was, following his freedom. The All-Father's breaking of it is referenced as a past mistake. Restoration process not detailed.
- **Unla Kee acting alone**: Unla Kee has broken from the group to pursue Iymrith independently via the teleportation chamber. Whether the party reunites before confronting her is unresolved.
- **Orsik's DNRRR**: Orsik noted internally that his "Do Not Reanimate" clause is absent from his DNRRR document, and worried the wizard might be a necromancer. The wizard's school of magic and whether this is a real threat is unresolved.
