## World Events

- The party, carrying a full bookcase, deliberated on three routes back to Parnast: through the Grey Peak Mountains, through the Forbidden Forest, or through Ambush Gap; Wallace chose Ambush Gap.
- Travelling toward Ambush Gap, the party was intercepted by Bad Fruul's bugbears and goblins demanding half of the party's possessions as tribute.
- Orsik bluffed that the party only had 10 gold; Unla Kee manipulated fate to make the bluff partially succeed.
- Goblins objected to the 5 gold offer; the bugbears renegotiated, demanding a sword; Orsik and Unla Kee's attempt to pass off marked cards failed; the party surrendered Unla Kee's short sword (used on the Magmin) and the deck of marked cards.
- Thistle offered goodberries to the goblins, reducing their interest in the confrontation; the bugbear chief accepted the tribute and declared it worthy of Bad Fruul.
- The bugbears revealed they do not meet Bad Fruul directly; their chief meets another chief who meets Bad Fruul.
- The party entered Ambush Gap; poison had been released in the pass, causing coughing and wheezing.
- Chief Grik ambushed the party in Ambush Gap, demanding all equipment and treasure or death.
- Combat began: three goblins targeted Wallace (one hit); Wallace charged up the cliff and killed a goblin; Thistle's drake charged Chief Grik's tile; a goblin struck the drake without penetrating armour.
- Wolves emerged from a trail, missed the cleric, and penetrated Orsik's armour.
- Vardis climbed to a tile and used burning hands, killing two goblins and wounding a third who fled.
- Unla Kee cast binding ice, killing two wolves, binding one wolf, and immobilising Chief Grik and his henchman; Thistle's drake was also hit.
- Orsik killed the bound wolf and climbed to Chief Grik's tile.
- Two goblins on Wallace's tile fled; Chief Grik used a subordinate goblin as a shield against Thistle and the drake's attack; the goblin then nicked the drake.
- Vardis killed a goblin on a separate tile with his bow.
- Chief Grik was killed by Orsik.
- The party looted Chief Grik's body, finding a note and a gold stylus; the note was orders from Bad Fruul to defend the pass and a declaration of intent to seize Parnast.
- Thistle and Unla Kee discovered a decayed Fey portal (stones in a circle with a Fey inscription) near Ambush Gap; Thistle identified it as a former Feywild gathering and transit portal, now magically inert.
- Thistle connected the portal to Adimond Kroskas' troop movements into the Feywild and to his Uncle Yorn's tales of the North.
- The party arrived in Parnast on 06-04-Taraskh after a two-day uneventful journey.
- Hsing appeared at the Golden Tankard, confirmed Unla Kee as the new Wizard of the Crag (having initially feared the old wizard had become a Lich), and arranged for Lord's Alliance agents to collect the books via covered wagon.
- The Lord's Alliance agents dismissed inquiry about armour found by the party; Orsik mocked them; Wallace repeatedly de-escalated.
- Hsing paid the party 150 gold pieces; Orsik protested the amount; Hsing refused to increase it and teleported away; Orsik swung his axe at the space where Hsing had been.
- Parnast's local militia asked Orsik to join their guard, train them, and offered room, board, and two beers daily; the militia proposed naming themselves "Orsik's Guards."
- A Zhentarim agent approached Orsik during guard training, citing Zuul Clovis as a referrer, and delivered a written missive requesting Orsik locate the missing agent Oblivillish in Prince Thornacious' court.
- Orsik shared the Zhentarim missive with his fellow adventurers one day later.
- Unla Kee purchased a new sword from Calder the Halfling; Calder complained about Lionel Coster's dimensional portals undercutting independent merchants.
- Unla Kee gambled at the Golden Tankard over approximately two weeks, losing half his stake; a traveller accused him of cheating despite his losses; Ragnar intervened; the traveller departed after memorising Unla Kee's face.
- Thistle won archery contests against travellers across two weeks and acquired a fan club of local children who began accosting travellers on his behalf.
- On approximately 10-02-Taraskh (date appears anomalous relative to earlier entries), Hsing returned to Parnast with Azam; Ragnar escorted the party to the Golden Tankard's back room for a meeting.
- Orsik nearly attacked Azam; Wallace intervened and explained the SEER's power and the importance of the alliance to his father.
- Orsik refused to participate in the meeting and waited outside with Wallace; Vardis' decision was solicited but not recorded.
- Thistle challenged Hsing to offer better rewards for quests.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Bad Fruul's campaign against Parnast**: Bad Fruul's written orders to Chief Grik explicitly state an intent to seize Parnast. The nature, scale, and timeline of this threat are unresolved. Bad Fruul has not been encountered directly.
- **Bad Fruul's poison in Ambush Gap**: Bad Fruul promised that travellers would be weakened by released poison before the goblin ambush. The mechanism, source, and whether it remains active are unknown.
- **Bad Fruul's chain of command**: The bugbears revealed Bad Fruul communicates through at least two layers of intermediary chiefs. The upper levels of this hierarchy have not been encountered.
- **The missing Zhentarim agent Oblivillish**: Oblivillish was embedded in Prince Thornacious' exiled court; contact has been lost. Whether he was discovered, captured by goblinoids, or killed is unknown. The party (specifically Orsik) has been tasked by the Zhentarim to locate him.
- **Prince Thornacious' exiled court**: The court is in exile in or near the Forgotten Forest. The circumstances of the exile and the court's current situation are unresolved.
- **Zhentarim surveillance of Prince Thornacious**: Why the Zhentarim wants intelligence on an exiled Fey prince is unexplained.
- **The decayed Fey portal**: The portal's magic has disappeared. Whether this was deliberate, connected to Adimond Kroskas' actions, or part of a broader decline of Fey presence in the North is unresolved.
- **Adimond Kroskas and the Feywild incursion**: Thistle believes Kroskas used Fey portals to move troops into the Feywild. Kroskas does not appear in this chunk; his current status and the outcome of that incursion are not addressed here.
- **Lord's Alliance armour found by the party**: The party possesses armour that appears to belong to the Lord's Alliance. The Lord's Alliance agents dismissed inquiry about it. Whose armour it was and how it came to be in the party's possession (or found in the field) is unresolved.
- **The old Wizard of the Crag**: Hsing initially believed the Wizard of the Crag was dead from a duel and feared he had become a Lich. The prior wizard's fate and the circumstances of the duel are referenced but not resolved in this chunk. Unla Kee is now recognised as the new Wizard of the Crag.
- **The unnamed traveller who accused Unla Kee of cheating**: The traveller memorised Unla Kee's face before leaving. Whether he is a merchant, a Zhentarim agent, or another faction's operative is unknown. His intentions are unresolved.
- **Hsing's return visit with Azam**: The purpose of Hsing and Azam's return to Parnast is not yet disclosed. The meeting in the back room was beginning as the chapter ended. Whatever quest or information Hsing intends to offer — and whether the reward will be adequate — is unresolved.
- **Wallace's marriage to Elspeth**: Wallace is resistant to marrying Elspeth but has returned to Parnast. Whether this situation will be forced into play is unresolved.
- **Wallace's father and the Cult of the Dragon**: Wallace's father has a past association with the Cult of the Dragon and is using the Lord's Alliance connection to rehabilitate his reputation. The extent and nature of that involvement are not detailed in this chunk.
- **Giants as a threat to Parnast**: The framing memo references a tumult brewing in the North and the risk of Parnast falling to Giants. No Giants have appeared yet; this is flagged as a coming threat.
- **The Lord's Alliance field agent authoring the memo**: The identity of the Lord's Alliance agent tracking the party and writing reports to the SEER is not revealed.
- **Unla Kee's prophecy fragment**: Unla Kee consulted an "updated piece of prophecy" during the Ambush Gap battle to determine whether the world was ending. The content, source, and full scope of this prophecy are unresolved.
