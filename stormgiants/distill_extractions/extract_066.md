## World Events

- Orsik deployed two bags of holding against Bob: Unla Kee tossed the first toward Bob, and Thuul stuffed the second into the first, triggering a dimensional explosion that sent Bob, the Red Wizard, and Thuul to the Astral Plane.
- Approximately 300 theatergoers had previously been sent to an unknown pocket dimension by Bob; they remain stranded with no known means of retrieval.
- The zombie hordes (dressed as 3HPP members) were destroyed by Vardis's power over undead, fireballs, flamestrike, and the weapons of Zymorven, Daratha, and Urgala.
- The Drow fighters who were protecting the Red Wizard fled the theater and escaped.
- Lord Zymorven formally cleared the debt between his family and Orsik's, publicly placing sole responsibility for battlefield decisions on Orsik.
- Lord Zymorven, the 3HPP, and (reluctantly) Darathra agreed on a cover story: Bob (a demon) used fire spells in the theater, causing a conflagration that killed the theatergoers, while the party defeated Bob and saved Silverymoon.
- Darathra severed her working relationship with the 3HPP and left, declaring she was never present and would extend no further favors.
- Unla Kee proposed blaming the deaths on a demonic cult of "ungodly races," which would have incited violent race riots in Silverymoon; the idea was rejected by Orsik, and Thistle had an arrow drawn in case Unla Kee did not comply.
- Thistle built a bonfire of rotting food and smashed timber; Unla Kee ignited it with fire spells, and the theater was set ablaze.
- The 3HPP departed Silverymoon through the eastern city gate as the theater burned; a guard who recognized them became agitated upon seeing the fire and ran off, mentioning someone he cared about who had attended the play.
- To avoid the vampire and her spawn aboard the castle, the 3HPP boarded the airship in the morning.
- Orsik attempted to assert ownership of the airship castle and threatened to learn to use the control crystals without Escarlotta; Escarlotta pushed back, reframing the arrangement as a partnership.
- Escarlotta revealed she and Blagothkus had reconciled following a long conversation, partly inspired by Thistle's sacrifice of his romantic relationship for a higher cause.
- Escarlotta redirected the party's mission toward finding King Hekaton and stopping the giants' campaign.
- Unla Kee blew the conch, transporting the 3HPP to the Maelstrom.
- The 3HPP arrived in a large underwater storm giant stronghold; the world shimmered and they materialized atop a glowing glyph in the northeast corner of a rough-hewn chamber.
- The party narrowly avoided being scalded by a geyser to the south of the arrival chamber, thanks to Vardis's caution.
- The party explored north, finding a statue of a male storm giant blowing a pink and blue conch shell, two giant-sized guest rooms, and a storeroom of giant supplies; Orsik examined the wine barrels and found no notable vintage.

- What it is: A major city, apparently a hub of culture and "goodly races"; politically sensitive, with an aristocracy and governance structure
- What happened there: Bob's plot to start an apocalypse was thwarted; approximately 200–300 theatergoers remain missing in a pocket dimension; cover story agreed upon to protect all parties; city guard observed the fire as the party left

- What it is: A flying castle controlled by Escarlotta (the flight command system, integrated with the castle); also inhabited by Blagothkus and a vampire with spawn

- What it is: A vast underwater stronghold built to storm giant scale; roughly circular chamber approximately 100 feet in diameter with ceilings over 50 feet high; walls are rough-hewn, slick with moisture, adorned with bioluminescent lichen, snails, starfish, barnacles, and tiny crabs

**The Astral Plane**
- What it is: A plane of existence; referenced as a destination from which entities can potentially return

## Threads & Mysteries

- **The 300 missing theatergoers**: Sent to an unknown pocket dimension by Bob; the only person who knows where they are is Bob, who is now lost in the Astral Plane. No known means of retrieval currently exists.
- **Bob's potential return**: Darathra explicitly warned that sending Bob to the Astral Plane does not destroy him and that he can return. The party has no current plan to prevent or prepare for this.
- **The cover story's durability**: The agreed story (Bob caused the fire) may unravel under magical investigation (divination spells, truth magic). Urgala noted that fleeing quickly reduces the impetus for hard questions, but the risk remains.
- **The missing guard's loved one**: A city guard at the eastern gate recognized the 3HPP, saw the theater fire, and ran off mentioning someone he cared about who had attended the play. This person is presumably among the missing 300.
- **The vampire and dracolich aboard the castle**: Both remain unresolved threats. The party discussed the possibility of bribing the vampire with a cult artifact; Unla Kee suggested a tome for creating Dracoliches or an artifact to control them.
- **King Hekaton's whereabouts**: The storm giant king is missing. Queen Serissa is the party's intended contact for information. The nature and cause of his disappearance are unknown.
- **The giants' campaign of mayhem**: Escarlotta states the giants are engaged in destructive activity across the realms. The scope, leadership, and goals of this campaign are not yet detailed.
- **The Drow fighters**: Fled the theater before being caught. Their employer (beyond protecting the Red Wizard), their current location, and whether they will resurface are unknown.
- **Queen Serissa**: Heard but not yet encountered; pipe organ music and a powerful female voice emanate from above the spiral staircase. Her disposition toward the party is unknown.
- **The pink and blue conch statue**: A statue of a storm giant blowing a pink and blue conch shell stands at the end of the north hallway. Its significance (decorative, functional, symbolic) has not been established.
- **Thistle's sacrificed relationship**: Referenced by Escarlotta as an inspiration; the identity of the woman Thistle left and the full circumstances of that sacrifice have not been detailed in this session.
- **Amn's manipulation of Blagothkus**: Amn and unnamed gods are stated to have manipulated Blagothkus into killing his and Escarlotta's son. The purpose of this manipulation and whether Amn remains a threat are unresolved.
