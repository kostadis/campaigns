## World Events

- Orsik and companions returned to Parnast after approximately 20 days away, on the 1st day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul.
- During the group's absence, Bad Fruul's army sacked at least one farm, causing refugees and their animals to flee into Parnast, creating overcrowded, makeshift conditions.
- Ragnar Redtooth organized Parnast's defenses in Orsik's absence, encountering resistance and ill fortune he suspects is deliberate sabotage.
- The completed southern watchtower collapsed, killing a militiaman named Donnal; a witness reportedly saw a light near the tower's base just before collapse.
- Tools have been disappearing from the central toolshed, a few each day, slowing construction without constituting an obvious robbery.
- The cart used to haul materials from Old Parnast broke its axle for the second time in a tenday; the wheels and axle had been replaced by Gundalin Cartwright.
- Tove, a half-elf scout, brought Ragnar intelligence that monsters are constantly on the move and destroying what they find, and that he could not locate Bad Fruul's main camp.
- Azam intercepted the group outside the Golden Tankard and informed Thistle that Uncle Joon is in Triboar and has joined a secretive organization unknown to the SEER.
- Azam conveyed the SEER's message that defending Parnast is a Lord's Alliance priority and promised powerful patronage for success.
- Three horses were found afflicted in Parnast's town center: one dead, one trembling violently, one staggering; the horses had eaten hay contaminated with Yew (a toxic evergreen).
- Vardis cast lesser restoration on the near-dead horse, saving it.
- Thistle cast *speak with animals* and learned from the horses that they ate hay with a funny taste and kept eating despite feeling ill.
- The poisoned horses triggered a community debate that escalated tension between Parnast residents and refugees; Orsik's inflammatory comment about "poison rights" further inflamed the crowd.
- Unla Kee identified the toxic plant as Yew and located the antidote in Silas Carras' late wife's garden.
- Silas Carras informed the group that some of his late wife's bushes had been recently trampled.
- Thistle followed the trail from Silas' trampled garden and found it leads directly to Gundalin Cartwright's front door.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Who is sabotaging Parnast's defenses?** Tool theft, watchtower collapse (with witness reporting a light at the base beforehand), and the cart's broken axle are all suspected sabotage. Gundalin Cartwright is Ragnar's prime suspect, and footprints from Silas' trampled garden lead to Gundalin's door, but no proof has been found yet.
- **Who poisoned the hay with Yew, and why?** The source of the Yew in the horses' hay is unknown. The footprints from Silas' garden (where the Yew/antidote connection was found) lead to Gundalin's house, but the link between these events is unconfirmed.
- **Is Gundalin Cartwright actually behind the sabotage?** Wallace is skeptical. Ragnar believes so. Physical evidence (footprints, broken axle) is circumstantial. His motivations (grudge against Ragnar over the Cult of the Dragon) are established but unproven as cause for sabotage.
- **What is the secretive organization Uncle Joon joined in Triboar?** The SEER does not know what it is. Thistle is aware and will presumably investigate after the Parnast crisis.
- **What is the nature and location of Bad Fruul's main camp?** Tove could not find it. The army's movements are unpredictable. An attack on Parnast is considered imminent.
- **What ettins might be coming?** The fairy (Thistle) asked the militia guard about ettins showing up, turning the guard pale. No explanation was given for why ettins would be associated with Bad Fruul's forces or the current threat.
- **Who is the "most powerful personage" the SEER referenced?** Azam promised admiration and support from this unnamed personage if Parnast is successfully defended. Identity unknown.
- **Was the southern watchtower's collapse definitely sabotage?** A witness saw a light near the base just before collapse; villagers attributed it to cursed materials from Old Parnast. Not yet confirmed as deliberate.
- **What is the nature of Ragnar's claimed inside-operative role in the Cult of the Dragon?** He asserts he joined to work from the inside and saved the town. Thistle is openly skeptical and has indicated he intends to confront Ragnar about this.
- **What map does the group possess, and how does it relate to the SEER's plan?** Referenced briefly; Orsik and Vardis want to learn more about a map, and Azam notes this would affect the SEER's plan. No further detail given.
- **What is Hsing's reward connected to, and where is Hsing now?** Orsik received reward money from Hsing just before the session begins; Hsing does not appear and is not further discussed.
