## World Events

- The adventuring party departed Parnast's Tavern of the Golden Tankard on the morning of the 2nd day of the 1st Tenday of Mirtul to investigate the Brandimen cabin.
- Orsik and Vardis privately expressed suspicion that Tove Brandimen's hostage story was a scam, referencing their own past use of such a scheme.
- The party crested a hill and spotted the Brandimen cabin amid boulders and fir trees, hearing human screaming.
- Thistle identified the screaming as human voices using local slang, then identified the content as obscene.
- Orsik and Vardis charged the clearing; the rest of the party followed without scouting.
- The party discovered a cage of human prisoners, a firepit, and the Brandimen cabin; a goblin was torturing prisoners with a hot poker.
- Orcs and goblins had taken ambush positions in response to the charging party.
- The Green Jacket Goblin fired on Unla Kee from behind a shed east of the firepit.
- An orc threw a javelin from the cabin over the prisoners at Orsik and missed.
- Vardis shot the skull-necklace orc for 9 HP; Wallace killed him.
- Thistle and Thuul engaged two goblins; one fled, one fired and missed.
- An orc threw a javelin at Unla Kee and missed; Unla Kee taunted him.
- The Green Jacket Goblin fired at Orsik and missed.
- The skull-necklace orc charged Orsik; Kagrota was revealed, shouting orders and throwing a javelin that slipped and missed.
- Vardis cast scorching ray at two fleeing goblins, hitting one severely.
- Wallace missed the ogre (Kagrota).
- Unla Kee cast Rime's Binding Ice, trapping the ogre; the nearby orc was unaffected.
- Thistle killed the goblin that had attacked him with a scimitar.
- The goblin archer hit Unla Kee and celebrated.
- An orc advanced on Wallace and Orsik and missed both.
- Orsik killed the orc that advanced on him.
- The Green Jacket Goblin stepped from cover and hit Unla Kee for 3 HP.
- Kagrota cast a spiritual weapon (hammer form) and struck Unla Kee.
- Vardis cast faerie fire; only one orc was affected.
- Unla Kee twisted fate to give Wallace a critical hit on the ogre, wounding her.
- Unla Kee cast Aganazzar's Scorcher: the ogre avoided most damage, an orc was badly hurt, and a goblin was killed.
- Thistle flew up and attacked Kagrota, reducing her to 1 HP.
- Kagrota, at 1 HP, shouted that she had a deal with Prince Thornacious.
- An orc next to Unla Kee (unarmoured) missed him.
- Orsik killed that orc and advanced on the ogre.
- The Green Jacket Goblin fired at Unla Kee, knocking him unconscious.
- Kagrota's spiritual weapon attacked Wallace; she struck the flanked Orsik with her hammer and missed.
- Vardis rushed to Unla Kee and healed him.
- Wallace punched Kagrota to death.
- Unla Kee, now conscious, rushed up to an orc next to Orsik and cast Aganazzar's Scorcher again, killing the Green Jacket Goblin and wounding the orc.
- Orsik killed the remaining orc.
- The party looted the dead bodies, finding 50 gold in scattered coins and six sapphires worth 100 gold total.
- The party freed the prisoners from the cage.
- Prisoners revealed that Bad Fruul had sent Kagrota to destroy their commune; several commune members died in the raid.
- The party checked the prisoners' hands for missing fingers; none were missing.
- One prisoner woman began crying hysterically upon seeing the severed finger; her companions explained it belonged to a commune member.
- A prisoner confirmed that Tove Brandimen was "thick as thieves" with Kagrota, contradicting his hostage story.
- Prisoners stated they had never heard of Bad Fruul by name.
- The party entered the Brandimen cabin and found it in a state of filth and disorder.
- Thistle discovered a trapdoor under a rug in the cabin floor.
- Thistle descended into an earthen chamber below the cabin, finding ropes of braided human hair strung with bells hanging from the ceiling.
- Orsik, Vardis, and Thistle identified a crude altar bearing a black sunburst symbol, two black candles, an elven woman's skull, shattered jade statuette remains, and several chests and bags of treasure.
- Orsik and Vardis identified the altar as belonging to Bane.
- Thistle recounted lore about Bane for the group, referencing his uncle Joon as the source.
- The party began returning to Parnast.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Tove Brandimen's deception**: Prisoners confirmed Tove was "thick as thieves" with Kagrota, directly contradicting his claim that his mother was her hostage. The severed finger he presented belonged to a commune member, not his mother. His true motives and whereabouts are unknown. Orsik suggested feeding him to the wyvern.
- **Tove's mother**: No evidence found that Tove's mother was ever held by Kagrota. Her actual fate and location remain unknown.
- **Bad Fruul**: Referenced as the power who sent Kagrota to destroy the commune. Prisoners had never heard of him by name. His identity, location, and broader plans are unknown.
- **Prince Thornacious**: Kagrota's dying words revealed she had a deal with someone called Prince Thornacious. The nature, terms, and current status of this deal are unknown. It is unclear whether the deal involved the adventurers, Thistle (a fairy), or something else.
- **The Bane altar**: A hidden Bane worship site was operating beneath the Brandimen cabin. It featured an elven woman's skull (identity unknown) and a shattered jade statuette (significance unknown). The extent of Kagrota's religious activities and any broader Bane network in the region are unknown.
- **Treasure in the underground chamber**: Chests and bags of treasure found beside the altar; contents not fully enumerated in this session.
- **Chandra Stol and the wyvern**: The wyvern at Parnast is noted to require feeding; Chandra Stol manages this. The party notes she will be displeased about feeding it cooked rather than live prey — the significance of this detail and the wyvern's role are unresolved.
- **The commune**: Several members died in Kagrota's raid. Surviving members were freed. Their plans and whether they will interact further with the party are unresolved.
