## World Events

- Kaelen purchased the theater property outright rather than renting it, creating a financial and logistical problem for the 3HPP's production.
- Orsik, Thistle, Unla Kee, and Vardis divided responsibilities to salvage the production on the day of the opening performance.
- Unla Kee confronted Kaelen and forced multiple script rewrites, eventually devising a "play within a play about a bad play" framing device to salvage the incoherent material.
- Thistle advised director Everard Barney on actor motivation and staging, including repositioning Amy's soliloquy from a dung pile to a hill.
- Orsik disguised himself as "Geoff Mamet" and coached lead actor Amy, successfully improving the energy and commitment of her performance.
- Vardis visited Urgala and learned of a CGI mirror ball device held by Kobolds south of Yartar.
- Vardis infiltrated the Kobold Warrens disguised as a Kobold, triggered a tripwire, successfully bluffed Kobold guards, and located the mirror ball in a chamber guarded by an Ogre.
- Vardis seized the mirror ball, stunned the Ogre with warding flare, and fled the warren.
- During his escape, Vardis took a wrong turn into a chamber with centipedes and Kobolds and cast Fireball, killing those enemies.
- Vardis escaped the Kobold Warrens with the mirror ball and delivered it in time for the performance.
- On the 6th day, the play was performed to a live audience and was a smashing success.
- At the end of the play, a demon calling itself Bob Saget manifested on stage, amplified by the mirror ball's effects, and performed comedy for the audience, who responded with delight rather than terror.
- Unla Kee approached Bob Saget after the performance; Bob Saget confirmed he had been summoned by the play and expressed enthusiasm for his new situation.
- Urgala confirmed the play is a hit and that audiences wish to return for a second night.
- Kaelen revealed to himself (and the reader) that the play was designed as a revenge summoning ritual intended to destroy the audience; he is now unexpectedly pleased at becoming famous instead.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **The summoning ritual**: Kaelen deliberately embedded a demon-summoning ritual into the play's script, staging, and finale. The party's improvements altered but did not prevent the summoning. It is unresolved whether the changes to the play altered the terms of Bob Saget's summoning in any meaningful way, and whether Kaelen will attempt another ritual or take further hostile action.
- **Bob Saget's nature and agenda**: Bob Saget is a demon who claims to torment kings who killed their jesters. His stated benign intent (performing comedy) has not been verified. His actual demonic powers, allegiances, and any conditions of his summoning are unknown. It is unresolved whether his presence in Yartar will have consequences.
- **The oddly elusive story**: Unla Kee noted that even after fixing the script, the story itself remained "oddly elusive." The nature and meaning of this observation is unexplained.
- **Amy's mysterious past**: Amy is described as seemingly hiding some mysterious past. This has not been elaborated upon.
- **Urgala's past**: Urgala stated she "wasn't always an innkeeper" and demonstrated knowledge of Chancellor Geoffrey's device and its location. Her prior identity, faction affiliations, and intelligence sources are unresolved.
- **Chancellor Geoffrey's missing device**: The mirror ball has been taken from the Kobolds and used in the play. Chancellor Geoffrey has been searching for it. It is unresolved whether he will learn of its recovery and use, and what his response will be.
- **Zhentarim and Hand of Yartar debts**: The party needs the play to continue succeeding to repay these debts. The specific amounts and terms remain unstated; whether a single successful run is sufficient is unconfirmed.
- **Kaelen's legal and financial position**: Kaelen purchased the theater property, creating an unknown financial liability. The implications of this for the production's profits have not been resolved.
