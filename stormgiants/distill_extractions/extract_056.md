## World Events

- On the 2nd day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis, a Coatl emerged from the totem as Orsik passed it and demanded the destruction of Wormblod.
- Thistle informed the Coatl that Wormblod had already been killed in the Eye of the All-Father; the Coatl granted the 3HPP Charms of Restoration.
- The 3HPP looted the Remorhaz's lair, taking gold, gemstones, and minor magical artifacts.
- Vardis noted that the Remorhaz fight with fireballs was unnecessary and the gong could have been retrieved without combat.
- Bressik, now freed, began background research and work on a play aboard the Dragon's Breath, internally deciding to withhold information about Uncle Joon and the treasure map from the party until paid.
- On the 3rd day of the 2nd tenday of Eleasis, the 3HPP and Bressik arrived at Silverymoon's Mulgate gate.
- The 3HPP gained entry by identifying themselves as business partners of Lord Aersume, confirmed by Sir Cedric Hollowcrest.
- The 3HPP was recognized as minor celebrities due to Bob Saget's play; the unnamed guard became an ardent fan of Unla Kee and requested an autograph.
- Thistle took the group to Wyrmworks to receive research findings from Rebecca Deakin.
- Rebecca revealed that Adimond Kroskas was hired by the Cult of Tiamat to develop a fay-dragon drake hybrid and entered the Fae to obtain the First Flower of Spring.
- Rebecca reported that Uncle Joon had personally met with her and provided information about Adimond's activities.
- Rebecca stated that the Cult of the Dragon referred to Adimond as both a dragon and a dragon's pet; she concluded he is likely a dragon himself.
- Thistle reacted with fury upon hearing Uncle Joon had been in contact with Rebecca and had withheld this information.
- Vardis connected the damage in Kolbaz's chambers to a Blue Dragon, inferring Adimond may be a Blue Dragon.
- Thistle realized with horror that his girlfriend's violent behavior may have a more sinister explanation in light of this information.
- The 3HPP found the High Palace advertising a sold-out play for two months and were mobbed by the public outside.
- The 3HPP met with Lord Methrammar Aersume, who paid them 12,000 gold pieces from play profits.
- Lord Aersume revealed the play's plot involves the 3HPP defeating Bob Saget in a real battle (Orsik via battle mastery, Vardis via divine power, Unla Kee via spells); Thistle is represented by a puppet on a stick.
- Orsik attempted to use the threat of Delsephine (a red dragon) to extract 6,000 gold from Lord Aersume; Aersume instead paid 12,000 gold and told Orsik to cover the 6,000 from his share.
- Orsik proposed a new play; Lord Aersume refused, framing the current play as a spiritual and cultural phenomenon.
- Lord Aersume declared anti-magic advocates to be traitors to Silverymoon.
- That evening, the 3HPP met with Bob Saget, who stated the play's anti-magic perversion of society would achieve its goals within 30 days.
- Bob Saget revealed he is collaborating with Kaelen Sarssir, crediting Kaelen with saving him and improving the play together.
- Bob refused to give the 3HPP seats at the play and refused to identify the actors portraying them.
- Orsik decided to purchase invisibility potions to attend the play covertly.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Bressik's withheld information**: Bressik knows where the treasure map leads, has knowledge of Uncle Joon's journeys, and has information about the fairy. He intends to withhold this until properly paid. The 3HPP does not yet know he has this information.
- **Adimond Kroskas's true nature**: Rebecca and Vardis believe Adimond may be a Blue Dragon. The Cult of the Dragon referred to him as both a dragon and a dragon's pet. His exact identity and current location are unknown.
- **The fay-dragon drake hybrid**: Adimond was hired by the Cult of Tiamat to create one. Its current status — whether it exists, is in progress, or has been completed — is unknown.
- **The First Flower of Spring**: Adimond entered the Fae to obtain it as a component for his spell research. Whether he succeeded is unknown.
- **Thistle's girlfriend's violent behavior**: Thistle now suspects her behavior may be connected to Adimond's experiments or the fay-dragon drake hybrid project. The nature and extent of this connection are unresolved.
- **Uncle Joon's role and motivations**: Joon met with Rebecca Deakin and provided her with information about Adimond, apparently without telling Thistle. Thistle is furious. Joon's motivations and current location are unknown. Bressik also has knowledge of Joon's journeys.
- **Kolbaz's death**: Kolbaz was the last person to research Adimond before dying under circumstances involving unusual damage to his chambers. Vardis suspects a Blue Dragon. The full circumstances of his death are unresolved.
- **Bob Saget's 30-day goal**: Bob stated the anti-magic "perversion of society" will achieve its aims in 30 days. What those aims are in concrete terms, and what happens at the 30-day mark, is unresolved.
- **Kaelen Sarssir's involvement**: Kaelen wrote the original demon-summoning play and is now collaborating with Bob Saget on the current play. His current goals and the nature of his collaboration with Bob are unclear.
- **The actors in Bob Saget's play**: Bob refused to identify them and was evasive. He noted that their genuine terror in the combat scene is what makes it work, and that they will be replaced. The identity of the actors and the nature of their fear are unresolved.
- **Orsik's plan to attend the play covertly**: Orsik purchased invisibility potions to attend despite Bob's refusal. Outcome unknown.
- **Delsephine (red dragon) and the play**: Delsephine has financial or proprietary interest in the play. The 3HPP covered 6,000 gold from their share, but whether this resolves the matter with Delsephine is unclear.
- **The treasure map**: Its destination is known to Bressik but not to the 3HPP. Its contents and significance remain a mystery.
- **Blagothkus's flying castle**: Mentioned as a potential asset; no resolution or plan established.
