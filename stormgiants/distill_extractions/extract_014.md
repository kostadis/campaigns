## World Events

- Vardis storms out before the meeting with Hsing and Azam begins.
- Thistle demands additional reward beyond what Azam and Hsing have offered; Azam is outraged.
- Hsing intervenes, freezes Azam with a command, and takes control of the negotiation.
- Hsing conveys the SEER's intelligence: Gralm is an Ettin ruling a realm in the Lonely Moor with hobgoblin and goblin forces, has been hunting Fairies beyond food necessity, and is being pressured by Bad Fruul to join an army aimed at destroying Parnast.
- Thistle and Unla Kee agree to the quest; Azam privately offers Unla Kee an undisclosed additional sum.
- Hsing teleports away to other missions; Azam remains at the Golden Tankard.
- Thistle briefs the rest of the party in the main hall.
- Wallace persuades Orsik to join the quest by invoking the threat to Parnast.
- Azam reveals he previously misread his notes and offers the party a potion of healing and a potion of greater healing retroactively for their performance during the festival attack (conditional rewards).
- Wallace explains and implicitly defends Parnast's Wyvern-as-prison system to Orsik.
- The party departs on a tenday journey toward Gralm's realm through the Forgotten Forest.
- On the first day, the party encounters a dead clearing over a quarter mile wide containing a 50-foot rusty sword; no living creatures inhabit it and birds fly around it.
- Unla Kee enlarges himself and attempts to pull the sword; receives an electric shock; the sword does not move.
- The ground in the clearing begins to be disturbed and a decayed hand emerges; Unla Kee flees to the perimeter; when he reaches the edge the clearing appears undisturbed again.
- The party arrives at the edge of the Forgotten Forest that evening; they recall the Fey Portal rocks they previously found and note the portal may not be inert.
- An electrical thunderstorm approaches; Unla Kee rushes into the forest; Orsik warns against being the tallest thing in a field during Thalos's storm.
- The rain continues as a drizzle overnight and becomes a raging thunderstorm the following day.
- On the third day of the third tenday of Taraskh, midday, the thunderstorm abruptly stops and the sun becomes blindingly hot, as hot as the Anauroch desert.
- The party is surrounded by a gang of goblins led by the hobgoblin Mohm.
- Thistle, Orsik, and Thuul kill Mohm; the remaining goblins panic, fire arrows, and flee into the forest.
- The ground sprouts trees and vines attempting to entangle the party; only Thistle is caught.
- Thuul charges into the forest and is displaced by a Fey Crossing, vanishing in a pile of fine dust.
- Unla Kee kills two goblins with magic missiles and injures a third; Orsik kills the last goblin with a thrown handaxe.
- Thuul reappears six seconds later (displaced into the future by the Fey Crossing) and snaps at empty space where the goblins had been.
- A voice from the forest calls out to Thistle by name and by family connection to Uncle Joon.
- The voice reveals itself as Sir Oblivillish, a former member of Prince Thornacious' court now living under a curse and in double exile; the goblins react with accusations of "traitors."
- Sir Oblivillish explains his curse (reversed/malfunctioning invisibility) and his expulsion from both Queen Tatiana's Royal Court and subsequently from Thornacious' own court.
- Wallace stops Orsik from harming Sir Oblivillish by directing him to reread the Zhentarim notice.
- Orsik reads the notice and realizes Sir Oblivillish is the Zhentarim spy he is assigned to protect.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Uncle Joon's location**: The core promised reward from Hsing and the SEER; his exact location has not yet been revealed; the party must complete the Gralm mission first.
- **The 50-foot rusty sword in the dead clearing**: Unla Kee recognizes it as a sign from prophecies of the end times; he could not remove it; the electric shock upon contact, the dead land, and the undead hand emerging from the ground are unexplained; the clearing appeared undisturbed when viewed from the perimeter after the hand appeared — nature of this phenomenon unknown.
- **The undead hand in the clearing**: Something beneath the dead clearing attempted to emerge when Unla Kee was near the sword; its nature and intentions are unknown.
- **The Fey Portal rocks**: Previously found by the party; they now believe the portal may not be inert; not yet investigated further.
- **The Fey Crossings in the Forgotten Forest**: Active and unpredictable; Thuul was displaced six seconds into the future; extent of crossings and their effects unknown.
- **Sir Oblivillish and the Zhentarim**: Orsik holds a Zhentarim notice assigning him to protect Sir Oblivillish; the nature of Sir Oblivillish's mission or value to the Zhentarim is not yet explained; his relationship to the party's broader quest is unclear.
- **Sir Oblivillish's curse**: Cannot control his invisibility (starts invisible, becomes visible, then invisible again); the mechanism for breaking or living with the curse is unaddressed.
- **Prince Thornacious' exile**: Queen Tatiana sent him into exile; he pretends it was his own choice; his court (including Sir Oblivillish) was compelled to follow; the reasons for the exile are not given.
- **Gralm and Bad Fruul's recruitment pressure**: Whether Gralm can be persuaded to sign either contract (alliance or non-aggression) is unresolved; the magical enforcement mechanisms of the contracts have not been tested.
- **Gralm hunting Fairies beyond food necessity**: Lord's Alliance agents flagged this as significant; reasons for Gralm's fairy hunting beyond hunger are not explained.
- **Orsik and Vardis's map and family treasure**: Uncle Joon is identified as potentially key to locating a family treasure; details of the treasure and map remain undeveloped in this chunk.
- **The potion rewards from Azam**: Conditional on the party having placed no personal glory over saving villagers during the festival attack and having successfully defeated it; whether these conditions are deemed met has not been formally confirmed, only that Azam offered them as an apology for misreading his notes.
- **Unla Kee's private financial arrangement with Azam**: The specific amount offered to Unla Kee was kept secret from the rest of the party; the implications for group trust and future dealings are unresolved.
- **Bad Fruul's planned attack on Parnast**: The timing and scale of the attack are not yet known; the Wyvern prison system is referenced as a factor in village defense.
