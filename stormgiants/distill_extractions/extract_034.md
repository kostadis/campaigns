## World Events

- The 3HPP were ambushed by four crag cats on the Rauvin Road north of Silverymoon; all four cats were killed; Thistle harvested a pelt for value

- On the Evermoor Way south of Silverymoon, the 3HPP nearly encountered Uthgardtian barbarians but Unla Kee tricked them and the group avoided detection
- The 3HPP arrived in Triboar on the 5th day of the 2nd Tenday of Flamerule; they found the town overcrowded with visitors
- The 3HPP entered The Talking Troll and discovered the play production was in serious trouble (sets unfinished, actors drunk, script incoherent)
- Unla Kee received a note with a hand stencil from the Hand of Yartar requesting a meeting outside
- The 3HPP visited Urgala at Northshield House; she revealed Kaelen had borrowed 5,000 gp in the group's name from the Hand of Yartar, Zhentarim, and local criminals
- Urgala informed Vardis that Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation was held by a kobold tribe in the Dessarin Hills south of Yartar
- Unla Kee revised the play's script, devising a "play within a play" framing device and resolving motivational inconsistencies
- Thistle coached director Everard Barney on actor management and revised staging for Amy's key soliloquy scene
- Orsik disguised himself as acting coach "Geoff Mamet" and coached lead actor Amy, improving her energy and compliance
- Vardis infiltrated the kobold warren disguised as a kobold; navigated tunnels, traps, and an ogre guardian; retrieved Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation (a mirrored ball); cast a fireball killing kobolds and centipedes; escaped the warren
- On the 6th day of the 2nd Tenday of Flamerule, the play opened to a sold-out audience and was a smashing success
- At the play's conclusion, the summoning ritual embedded in the original script manifested; the demon Bob Saget appeared on stage and performed comedy rather than attacking the audience
- Bob Saget declared himself content to remain in the material plane performing, having escaped the Abyss
- Urgala confirmed repeat audiences are already demanding; the production is a financial hit
- Kaelen, having intended the play as a mass murder/summoning event, abandoned that intent upon discovering he is now famous

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Hand of Yartar note**: Unla Kee received a note with a hand stencil reading "Meet me outside." The group has not yet met with the Hand of Yartar contact. The Hand is also a creditor of Kaelen. Their full intentions are unknown. Thistle believes they previously tried to assassinate him; Orsik believes they were framed.
- **Kaelen's original intent**: Kaelen deliberately designed the play as a mass-murder summoning ritual to kill the theater-going public out of revenge. He has now abandoned this and embraced fame, but his culpability and whether others learn of his original plan is unresolved.
- **Bob Saget's presence**: A demon has been summoned into the material plane and is now performing at The Talking Troll. His long-term presence, any infernal obligations, and whether his summoning has consequences (planar, legal, or otherwise) are unresolved.
- **The 5,000 gp debt**: The 3HPP needs a full month of sold-out performances to repay Kaelen's debt to the Hand of Yartar, Zhentarim, and local criminals. Only the first night has occurred. Whether the run continues successfully is unresolved.
- **Chancellor Geoffrey's Invitation**: Vardis retrieved the device; it was used for the production. Chancellor Geoffrey has been searching for it. Whether he learns of its use or seeks restitution is unresolved.
- **The play's strange ending**: Thistle identified that the original ending was a summoning ritual. The improved play nonetheless triggered the summoning. The full mechanics and implications of what was summoned — and whether anything else was initiated — remain unclear to the party.
- **Orsik's old acquaintances in Triboar**: Orsik considered finding old friends in Triboar but decided it was a bad idea and magically disguised himself. Who these people are and why reconnecting would be dangerous is unresolved.
- **The vampire jailer of Yartar**: Lord Zymorven was advised to avoid the vampire in Yartar rather than the city entirely. The vampire conducted experiments on prisoners including Harthal. Whether the vampire takes action against any involved parties is unresolved.
- **Giant displacement of orc tribes**: The orc family encountered on the road was displaced from their home by giant activity and seeking a homestead. This references broader giant movements in the region; significance is unresolved.
- **Thistle's archery legend**: Thistle has become a minor legend among orc tribes as "the fairy that couldn't fire." Future consequences or encounters arising from this reputation are unresolved.
- **Amy's mysterious past**: Lead actor Amy presents as cheerful but is described as status-driven and hiding a mysterious past, avoiding commitment or definitive statements. Her background is unresolved.
- **The iron monolith in the kobold warren**: The altar shows Grumsh strangling Tiamat, with Grumsh described as a new addition. The significance of this religious iconography change and who made it is unresolved.
