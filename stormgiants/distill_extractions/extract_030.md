## Factions

**The Harpers**
- Current goals: Investigate the fall of the Ordning; complete missions that were previously impossible; maintain plausible deniability through use of mercenary contractors
- Recent actions: Recruited the party as contractors via Krowen; deployed them to Yartar to find a sword; maintain safe house agents (e.g., Kolbaz) in cities
- Relationships: Tolerant but internally divided over the contractor model (Krowen initially reluctant, Kolbaz openly resentful); separate from but coordinating with the party
- Key members: Krowen Valharrow, Kolbaz, Ezra (referenced), Dalthara (referenced)
- Notes: The contractor/mercenary model was pioneered by Ezra and is credited with allowing missions that sworn agents could not complete; Kolbaz represents old-guard Harper values resistant to this change

**The Hand of Yartar**
- Current goals: Operating as a rogues guild and information brokerage in Yartar; issuing bounties; controlling information flow for a price
- Recent actions: Previously attempted to assassinate Thistle (blades pointed at his neck); paid out 200 gold in bounties to the party (minus Kal's 50-gold cut); offered to sell information on Harthal for 500 gold or a Tamlin Zoar letter; agreed to waive fee for 50 play attendees
- Relationships: Loosely known to Kolbaz but no direct links; Maia is an informal associate or contact
- Key members: Elicia "Kal" Kallaphan (bounty handler), Eltio (information broker), Tamlin Zoar (legendary member, currently absent), Maia (informal associate)
- Notes: Rogues are described as being "all over" Yartar; Wink and Kiss is their primary hangout; Kolbaz notes they do not typically do "wet work" (assassination), making their attack on Thistle anomalous

**The Cult of the Dragon**
- Current goals: Apparently still active in some capacity despite being described as destroyed; allegedly assassinating people to cover up activities connected to the Well of Dragons ritual
- Recent actions: Allegedly attempting to assassinate Thistle to prevent him from finding Adimond Kroskas; used (per Thistle) the Spring's First Blossom as a ritual ingredient — disputed by Kolbaz
- Relationships: Opposed by the Harpers (Kolbaz fought them at the Well of Dragons); Adimond Kroskas is a known lead cultist
- Key members: Adimond Kroskas (lead cultist, location unknown)
- Notes: Kolbaz insists the Spring's First Blossom was NOT part of the ritual; the discrepancy between Thistle's claim and Kolbaz's research is unresolved

**The Lord's Alliance**
- Current goals: Attempting to unite various city-states into a supranational state
- Recent actions: None directly depicted
- Relationships: Opposed ideologically by the Harpers (at least by Kolbaz, who sees them as serving only the powerful)
- Key members: None named
- Notes: Kolbaz describes them as serving wealthy lords and powerful families at the expense of ordinary people; doubts they will succeed due to opposition from those who value freedom

**The Zhentarim**
- Current goals: Not stated

## Threads & Mysteries

- **The sword in Yartar**: The party has been sent by Krowen to find a sword connected to Harthal; the nature, identity, and location of the sword remain unknown.
- **Harthal's location**: The party is seeking Harthal; his current whereabouts are unknown; Eltio has agreed to provide information in exchange for 50 play attendees, pending the deal being fulfilled.
- **Tamlin Zoar**: The party must travel to Tamlin Zoar's town on the Evermoor Road to obtain her endorsement (or a letter) to unlock Eltio's information; this visit has not yet occurred.
- **The attack on Kolbaz**: Kolbaz was struck by lightning and collapsed; a small person with an impossibly deep voice called him a "meddling little fool" — the identity of the attacker, whether Kolbaz survived, and the motive for the attack are all unknown.
- **The Spring's First Blossom contradiction**: Thistle believes the Spring's First Blossom was a key ritual ingredient collected by Adimond Kroskas; Kolbaz, who was involved in the research, flatly denies it was used — the discrepancy is unresolved and noted as "very odd" by Kolbaz.
- **Adimond Kroskas**: A lead Cult of the Dragon cultist being sought by Thistle; Kolbaz claims never to have heard of him despite involvement in Cult research — either Kolbaz is ignorant, mistaken, or concealing something.
- **The Cult of the Dragon's continued activity**: Described as destroyed, yet allegedly still assassinating targets; the extent of their survival and ongoing operations is unclear.
- **The Hand of Yartar's assassination attempt on Thistle**: The Hand of Yartar does not typically conduct wet work (assassination); Kolbaz was baffled by it; the reason they targeted Thistle specifically is unresolved.
- **The fall of the Ordning**: Krowen's primary interest; he hopes the party can help him unlock the secrets behind it; no further details revealed this session.
- **Orsik's Zhentarim ties**: Krowen is aware of Orsik's close connections to the Zhentarim; the implications for trust and the party's Harper work have not been addressed.
- **The party's unnamed status**: The party lacks an official name ("the three half-pints and a pint" is informal); "Thistle Wendrod and Companions" was proposed by Thistle and contested by Orsik.
- **The cloud giant orb**: Krowen explained that a flying tower orb can only be obtained from Cloud Giants by persuasion or violence; the party's interest in obtaining one is flagged as a potential future goal.
- **Asklar and Beldorm**: Vardis believes he knew the former owners of the Wink and Kiss but cannot recall how; this connection is unexplored.
- **Unla Kee's cosmic significance**: Unla Kee believes his survival is necessary to prevent the end of the world; the nature of this threat is not elaborated upon in this session.
