## World Events

- The 3HPP arrived at the Great Theater in Silverymoon on Eleasis 4th, invisible due to potions supplied by Orsik, five minutes before the advertised start of the play.
- Orsik opened the theater doors; he disabled a glyph but failed to notice an alarm spell, which triggered and alerted the zombie audience.
- The 3HPP observed that the theater audience was composed entirely of zombies in various states of decomposition.
- Bob Saget appeared on stage, dressed entirely in purple, and acknowledged that uninvited guests may have arrived.
- Bob Saget closed the theater doors behind the 3HPP with a finger-click.
- Bob Saget revealed that he was summoned by Kaesen's spell, that the 3HPP's original purpose had been "boring," and that he has been magically manipulating Silverymoon's population against the Spellguards.
- Bob Saget revealed that he is conducting a blood ritual requiring victims, structured as theatrical performances in which proxies of the 3HPP are killed on stage.
- Drow knights brought four terrified commoners costumed as the 3HPP onto the stage.
- Bob Saget killed the gnome commoner (dressed as Unla Kee) by thrusting a claw through their chest.
- The Tabaxi commoner (dressed as Orsik) charged and struck Bob Saget before being killed by him.
- All four commoners were killed on stage by Bob Saget.
- A Drow Mage animated the four corpses using a device; they joined the zombie audience.
- Bob Saget left the stage.
- The 3HPP exited the theater, invisible and horrified.
- Outside the theater, a blue shimmer appeared and the legitimate ticketed audience members marched out alive and delighted — confirming Orsik's theory that Bob Saget is shunting real patrons to a demi-plane.
- The 3HPP debated whether to report Bob Saget to the Lord of Silverymoon and decided against it, fearing they would be blamed.
- The 3HPP voted unanimously to deal with Bob Saget secretly, after first handling the giant threat.
- The following day (Eleasis 5th, implied), the 3HPP resumed their public status as celebrated heroes of Silverymoon; Bob Saget's play continued its successful public run.
- On Eleasis 8th, the 3HPP (Orsik, Thistle, and Unla Kee) reached Parnast aboard the Dragon's Breath with Delsephine.
- The group recognized the castle at Parnast as the same castle that had previously flown over them during the Black Road quest.
- Delsephine inquired about Brezzik; Orsik explained he had departed to seek work elsewhere.
- Delsephine dropped the 3HPP on the upper tower of Blagothkus's castle.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Bob Saget's blood ritual**: The nature and ultimate purpose of the ritual being conducted at the Great Theater is unknown. What does it accomplish when complete? How many more performances are planned?
- **The demi-plane**: Bob Saget has created a demi-plane to which legitimate theatergoers are sent during performances. Its nature, contents, and whether patrons can be retrieved are unknown.
- **The 3HPP's plan to stop Bob Saget**: The group has resolved to deal with Bob secretly after handling the giant threat, but have no specific plan. Bob was judged too powerful to confront directly without allies.
- **Thistle's "sweetie"**: Thistle has someone he needs to save within a 30-day window. The identity of this person, the nature of the threat to them, and how Thistle plans to save them are not established in this chunk.
- **The giant threat**: Referenced as the priority mission before dealing with Bob Saget; details not provided in this chunk.
- **Unla Kee's flying castle**: The group is pursuing acquisition of a flying castle for Unla Kee; Blagothkus's castle at Parnast appears to be the target. How they plan to obtain it is unresolved.
- **Blagothkus's return**: How Blagothkus reacquired his castle after the First Faction secured it is explicitly noted as a mystery.
- **The Ordning chaos**: Blagothkus is credited with triggering the chaos of the Ordning; the current state of this event and its consequences are not addressed in this chunk.
- **Kaesen's summoning spell**: Bob Saget claims Kaesen's summoning spell brought him to this world. Kaesen's intent, awareness of the outcome, and current status are unknown.
- **The 3HPP's relationship with Silverymoon**: By choosing to stay silent about Bob Saget's atrocities, the 3HPP risk culpability; the Lord of Silverymoon might blame them if the truth emerges. This is an open political and moral thread.
- **Brezzik's departure**: Brezzik has left the group; the true reason and his current whereabouts are unstated beyond the vague explanation Orsik gave Delsephine.
- **The SEER and the Lord's Alliance**: The source of the 3HPP's hatred toward the SEER and the Lord's Alliance (rooted in the Black Road quest) is referenced but not elaborated upon in this chunk; whether this conflict has ongoing consequences is unresolved.
