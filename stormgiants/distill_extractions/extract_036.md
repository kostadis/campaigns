## World Events

- The 3HPP attended a party at Westpharl Ranch hosted by Cindrell.
- Cindrell invited the 3HPP to a private audience in her study and asked them to arrange the death of Hyuth Kolstaag, framing it as being "whatever you think is appropriate."
- Orsik declined to commit to the assassination; Thistle obtained a signed written contract from Cindrell specifying the nature of the deal before the group departed.
- Several weeks prior to this session: Two assassins entered Triboar and attempted to kill Hyuth Kolstaag; the attempt failed amid thunder and lightning; the assassins subsequently stole a horse from the Cart and Coin and murdered the stable hand there.
- Following the assassination attempt, Hyuth Kolstaag summoned gargoyles to protect his mansion and refused to pay any restitution or send condolences to the stable hand's family.
- Cindrell organized a luncheon to support the stable hand's family; Hyuth refused to buy a table.
- Darathra Shendrel responded to the incident with only warnings and stern notices to Hyuth.
- The morning after the party, Thistle visited Urgala at the Northshield House, revealed Cindrell's scheme without full detail, then presented the contract; Urgala expressed surprise at Cindrell's cold-bloodedness and provided background on Hyuth.
- Thistle rejoined the 3HPP at the Talking Troll.
- The 3HPP visited Darathra at the central tower; Thistle presented her with the contract; Darathra expressed intent to use it against Cindrell politically and as leverage over Hyuth.
- Darathra asked the 3HPP to deliver a message to Hyuth: that she, the Lord Protector, intervened to stop this assassination attempt; that she may not be able to intervene in the future; and that he should reconsider becoming a productive citizen to remain under the protection of the 12.
- The 3HPP approached Hyuth's mansion, observed the destroyed roof and scorch marks, and gained entry using a false pretense of free roof repairs sent by the Lord Protector.
- Hyuth admitted the 3HPP and led them to his destroyed second-floor study; Thistle was uncertain of the next move.
