## Threads & Mysteries

- **Who hired the bugbears at Ambush Gap?** Wallace suspects they were working for someone and planned to find their employer and offer information about the treasure's location independently of Orsik
- **Wallace's independent agenda:** Wallace intends to contact Orsik's Zhentarim contact himself; his loyalty to the group is pragmatic and self-interested
- **Wallace's father and the Lord's Alliance:** Whether Wallace's father was instructed by the Lord's Alliance to send the group, or is claiming independent credit for doing so, is unresolved; the nature of his relationship with the Alliance is ambiguous
- **The wizard's tower:** Who built it, why it sank, and what is inside remains unknown; someone is currently living in the sinkhole — identity unknown
- **Hsing's true motives:** Why does an ancient pseudo-dragon want this tower investigated? What is his interest in the marsh?
- **The grabby water monster's immortality:** It will reform within a few days somewhere else in the marsh; the party has no permanent solution
- **Mama Manyknuckles:** Not yet encountered; her full strength, location within the island, and capabilities are unknown
- **The mephit civil war resolution:** Feetor claims killing Mama Manyknuckles will end the civil war with all mephits defaulting to the grabby water monster faction; but the water weird will also reform — the long-term stability of any "peace" is unresolved
- **Feetor's evasiveness on tactics:** Feetor deliberately avoided giving tactical advice on attacking the island; his reasons are unexplained
- **The Ogreshippers:** Mentioned by Feetor as expecting the party to kill the grabby water monster; their identity, location, and relationship to other factions is unexplained
- **The northern camp:** Thistle scouted the southern camp and the central sinkhole but the northern camp was not described; its occupants and layout are unknown
- **Unla Kee's portents:** Unla Kee saw signs that a daytime attack would have been better; the significance of attacking at night (and whether this will prove costly) is unresolved
- **What "special mud" is:** Feetor offered Unla Kee special mud to participate in the mephit betting pool; the nature and properties of this mud are unexplained
- **The statue in the sinkhole's northwest corner:** A badly weathered statue noted by Thistle; its identity and significance are unknown
- **The War of the World:** Referenced as the event after which the Zhentarim lost control of the Black Road; details not provided
