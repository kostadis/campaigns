## World Events

- Thistle scouted the dark tunnels ahead of the group and discovered a large cavern filled with spider webs and three giant cocoons.
- Orsik burned through the webs and opened the first cocoon, finding a dead goat bled dry; slashed its belly open and found nothing of value.
- Orsik opened the second cocoon and found a dead dwarf with 17 copper pieces, a deck of cards, and a set of dice.
- Thistle spotted movement in the third cocoon and flew up to confirm the person inside was alive; flew down and gave her berries.
- Orsik introduced himself to the survivor, identified as Noori, a female human in Uthgardt tribal garb belonging to the same tribe as the chieftain the group had previously killed.
- Orsik deceived Noori, telling her the tribe was found dead at the entrance; she did not see through the deception.
- The group escorted Noori to the entrance of the warrens, where Harshnag the Grim was found sleeping on a bed covered in rags.
- Orsik told Noori to be quiet around the giant; she smiled and ran off.
- The group re-entered the crack in the wall and continued exploring the natural cave tunnels following the right-hand rule.
- The tunnel system emerged at an exterior opening on the mountainside, approximately 400 feet above the valley below, with a steep and treacherous descent; the group turned back.
- Orsik deduced that the cave tunnels were not part of the original temple construction but natural formations caused by the mountain cracking and moving, subsequently used by creatures as lairs.
- The group passed several intersections and arrived at another chamber with a central column supporting a domed ceiling and webs stretching from walls to a giant spider.
- Unla Kee rushed into the spider chamber before the group could assess the situation.
- Orsik struck the giant spider twice with his green blade magic; the spider bit Orsik but he resisted the poison via dwarven resistance.
- Vardis killed the spider with his bow.
- A pile of spider eggs hatched and swarmed beneath the dead spider.
- Unla Kee struck the hatchlings with a thundering clap; Orsik finished the remaining creatures.
- Vardis found a frozen potion of climbing and an old axe with a spectacularly beautiful sapphire on the dead spider.
- The group returned to the room where Harshnag the Grim was sleeping; Orsik poked him awake.
- Harshnag walked north down the main hallway, triggering a trap: all bedroom entrances were sealed with magical walls of stone, and a 40-foot-diameter sphere of solid granite materialized and began rolling south.
- Harshnag attempted to smash through a sealed stone wall but could not break through in time.
- Thistle flew above the rolling ball, avoiding it.
- The rest of the group and Harshnag were struck by the rolling ball; Harshnag was struck twice.
- When the ball reached the end of the hallway it reversed direction, moving back north more slowly.
- Orsik instructed everyone to press against the walls; they did so and avoided being crushed.
- Orsik identified the source of the ball's movement: the stone giant statue from the hallway's south entrance was pushing it.
- The granite ball disappeared into the northern wall; the stone walls vanished; the stone giant walked south, returned to its pedestal, and became immobile.
- Harshnag chose to sleep in one of the giant-sized bedrooms to recover; the group rested overnight.
- On the 6th day of the 1st Tenday of Eleasis, Harshnag woke from a deep, comfortable sleep and declared they should find the giant blade.
- The group moved south past the immobile stone giant statue and up stairs located north of the doors to the temple of Annam.
- The upper hallway was found to contain more giant-sized rooms with two beds each; the northern end of the hallway had collapsed.
- At the entrance to the upper hallway stood a life-size statue of a female cloud giant wearing an angry mask, arms positioned as if casting a spell.
- The group inspected the statue and determined it posed no immediate threat.
- Thistle offered to fly ahead and scout for pressure plates; Harshnag agreed.
- Thistle discovered a giant bag in one of the upper rooms containing a bong, a gong, and a waterskin.
- Thistle found a long, slender shard of ice roughly the size of a dagger, within which a rune symbol glowed.
