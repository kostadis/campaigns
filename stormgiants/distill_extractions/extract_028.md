## World Events

- On the 1st day of the 2nd tenday of Kythorn, Ghelryn Foehammer presented the party with a letter of mark at the Troll Tavern, rewarding them for killing a fire giant and directing them to Citadel Felbar for further reward from King Morinn and Queen Tithmel.
- Orsik proposed a travel route: east to Yartar, then north by boat on the River Surbin to Zymorven Hall, then to Silverymoon to deal with the Harpers.
- Kaelen was threatened by Orsik and agreed to have a play production ready in 45 days, with the target performance date of the 5th day of the 3rd tenday of Mirtul.
- Orsik failed to obtain horses from Narth, so the party walked to Yartar.
- An Elk totem Uthgardtian raiding party ambushed the party on the road to Yartar; Orsik nearly talked them down but Unla Kee's use of magic enraged them; the raiders were killed; their corpses were examined and found to bear elk totems and have nothing of value.
- On the 3rd day of the 2nd tenday of Kythorn, the party arrived at Yartar.
- The party entered Yartar through a gate guarded by a member of the Yartar Shields' Administration (YSA); Orsik was recognized but the guard concluded he was not the same Orsik; Thistle presented a cover story about their play.
- Thistle invented a detailed premise for the party's upcoming play on the spot while speaking to the gate guard.
- The party proceeded to the Fishyard and encountered merchants including Talni Barcoba, Imber Chen, and Baash'a Kweelani.
- Imber Chen provided information about Silverymoon and Cap'n Squint; warned the party that Squint would be gambling on the Grand Dame that night and leaving the next morning.
- Imber Chen shared that Lord Khaspere Drylund, owner of the Grand Dame, is seeking to become the next Waterbarron.
- Imber Chen agreed to display the party's play banner for 43 days in exchange for half a gold piece; a performance coupon commission scheme proposed by Orsik was rejected.
- Orsik and Vardis used Thistle's market research to advertise the play in Yartar markets.
- Unla Kee attempted to run a ticket pump-and-dump scheme; Thistle followed him.
- Members of the Hand of Yartar surrounded Thistle and Unla Kee; their intent was to kill Thistle and reprimand Unla Kee for running scams without paying a cut.
- Thistle fled and successfully hid; the Hand's magic user and two of three thugs could not locate him.
- Unla Kee negotiated a deal with one Hand thug: a cut of VIP tickets at a reduced resale price in exchange for the Hand standing down.
- Thistle arrived the following morning just as Cap'n Squint was preparing to depart, and the party boarded for the trip north on the River Surbin.

The party has not yet gone there. Whether this fits into their current itinerary is unresolved.

- **The play**: Thistle invented an entire premise on the spot for the play (Uthgardtian peoples vs. encroaching civilization, represented by a river boat from Yartar; the boat is destroyed through the captain's hubris; a romance subplot). Kaelen has not yet written it. The performance is scheduled for the 5th day of the 3rd tenday of Mirtul at the Talking Troll. Whether Kaelen can deliver is unresolved.

- **Hand of Yartar**: Their contract to kill Thistle remains unexplained — who hired them, and why Thistle specifically was targeted (described as "not personal, just business") is unknown. The deal Unla Kee struck (VIP ticket cut) may or may not satisfy them long-term.

- **Silverymoon's racial exclusivity**: Yartar explicitly excludes Half-Orcs and non-"goodly" races. Orsik's reaction to this was noted. Whether the party will face difficulties entering or operating in Silverymoon due to their composition is unresolved.
- **Zymorven Hall**: Named as a stop on the river journey north; no further information provided. Its significance is unresolved.
- **Unla Kee's tower / flying tower concept**: Vardis mused about making his tower into a flying tower. Whether this is a serious plan and how it might be achieved is unresolved.
- **Cindell**: Kaelen suggested Cindell's attendance at dinner would boost ticket sales among Waterdhavians. Orsik reacted negatively. Cindell's identity, relationship to Orsik, and relevance to the party remain unexplored.
- **Orsik's past in Yartar**: The gate guard recognized Orsik by reputation as "a short dumb dwarf" who would have pulled his sword and thrown himself out of Yartar. The specific incident referenced is unnamed and unresolved.
- **The Drunk Priest's new assignment**: The unnamed drunk priest at the Troll Tavern was moaning about a new assignment. Its nature is unrevealed.
- **Baash'a Kweelani**: A magical goods merchant noted at the Fishyard but not interacted with; her wares and potential usefulness to the party (particularly Unla Kee) are unexplored.
- **Talni Barcoba's potions**: Her home remedy potions are advertised as curing any ailment. Their actual efficacy is unverified and uninvestigated.
- **Elk totem Uthgardtian tribe**: The raiding party was described as desperate and having nothing of value, suggesting broader tribal collapse or hardship. The state of the wider tribe and their conflict with advancing civilization is unresolved background lore.
