## Threads & Mysteries

- **The alliance with Gralm** is conditional: the party must either bring Gralm four Fey heads or convince the Fey to stop playing music near his tent. The party intends to solve this by defeating the Unseelie.
- **The Unseelie in the Forgotten Forest**: Identity and full purpose of the Unseelie Dryads and halflings is unknown. Their broken harp and rotted stuffed animals are unexplained. Their relationship to the Gloaming Court and Queen of Air and Darkness is asserted but not detailed.
- **Oblivillish's return to court**: Depends on the party killing the Unseelie so Blasius can blame the stag's death on them and present it to Prince Thornacious. The plan requires Thornacious to accept the story.
- **Prince Thornacious's imperial ambitions**: He destroyed a Fey circle and is expanding into the Forgotten Forest. The party (via Thistle) has floated the idea of redirecting his ambitions to the High Forest with Star Mountain. Thornacious has not responded to this proposal.
- **The High Forest and Star Mountain**: Referenced as a potentially grander location for Thornacious's empire; not yet explored or confirmed as viable.
- **The hill giant under construction** in Gralm's encampment: Unla Kee noticed this and thought he was mistaken. Its presence and purpose are unexplained.
- **The SEER's contract**: Introduced as a document the party brought to Gralm to formalize an alliance. Its full terms, signatories, and the SEER organization itself are not detailed in this session.
- **Bad Fruul's waning power**: Both Gralm and Oblivillish note that Bad Fruul's power is weakening. The cause and implications are not explained.
- **Pandi's nature and purpose**: Pandi is an entity living in a room accessible through a magically-created door in the Forgotten Forest. Her origin, her relationship to the Forest, and why she told the party "I knew YOU would come" are unexplained.
- **The giant eagle with the ornamental leaf necklace**: Sitting with Blasius at the pond; its significance, ownership, and the meaning of the leaf necklace are unexplained.
- **Unla Kee's fate manipulation**: The narrator notes he "manipulated the fates twice today." The nature and mechanics of this ability, and its consequences, are not elaborated upon.
- **Blasius's plan's viability**: Whether Prince Thornacious will accept the story that the Unseelie killed the stag, and whether he will reinstate Oblivillish, remains unresolved.
- **Gralm's hill giant**: Whether the hill giant spotted under construction is affiliated with Bad Fruul or Gralm is unclear and may be significant.
