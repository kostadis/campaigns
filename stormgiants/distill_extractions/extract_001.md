## World Events

- The Cult of the Dragon was defeated at the Well of the World; Tiamat was prevented from entering the realm.
- Post-war, the five factions (Harpers, Zhentarim, Lord's Alliance, Order of the Gauntlet, Emerald Enclave) fractured and ceased cooperating.
- Cult of the Dragon leadership had hidden multiple treasure caches as a contingency plan before or during their operations.
- Rumors spread across the North of hidden Cult treasure, leading to a proliferation of fake maps and cons, as well as some genuine leads.
- Orsik and Vardis traveled through Secomber, Zelbross, Loudwater, Llorkh, and Parnast and were unable to find military or faction employment anywhere.
- Rumors of Giant activity—rune-covered boulders, huge footprints, missing livestock—were heard at or near Parnast by Vardis.
- Veradaa Storr was found dead in a clearing along the Black Road near Parnast; her cache notes were recovered from her body by Thistle.
- Orsik, Vardis, Thistle, and Unla Key met over Veradaa's body and agreed to work together to find the first cache.
- The group traveled to the first cache site: an abandoned cave at the base of the southernmost Greypeak mountain.
- A goblin unit under Sergeant Gronk, operating alongside shrunken giants (victims of a magical curse triggered in the cave), had taken up a defensive position in the cave's entrance cavern.
- The party was ambushed by Sergeant Gronk's goblins; early fighting resulted in significant party casualties (at minimum two members badly wounded or briefly incapacitated).
- A magic-user in the party cast Thunderwave, killing two goblins and turning the battle.
- Sergeant Gronk was killed.
- Thistle advanced down the southern hallway and encountered a swarm of goblin-sized mice armed with tiny scimitars and shields.
- The goblin-mice were killed; their bodies reverted to normal goblin size upon death, nearly blocking the southern cave entrance with corpses.
- The party deduced from the reversion that the curse was not permanent and pressed forward.
- Orsik and Vardis followed Thistle into an inner cavern where Slayermighty the (shrunken) giant was guarding a stone chest.
- Slayermighty threatened the party and was killed without landing a single blow.
- Blooddrinker, Slayermighty's bear (also shrunken), turned docile after his master's death.
- Blooddrinker convulsed; Thistle saved it through quick thinking.
- Orsik and Vardis opened the stone chest and found it empty except for a note from Clan True-Nimbus of the Clouds.
- Clan True-Nimbus's note revealed they had already taken the treasure from the first cache and mocked the hill giants.
- Slayermighty's remaining possessions were looted: two vials of alchemist fire, one potion of healing, one necklace of 20 copper charms.
- Blooddrinker bonded to Thistle and became his companion.

## Threads & Mysteries

- **Veradaa Storr's death**: She was found dead before the party arrived. Who killed her, and why? Was it connected to the treasure caches or to someone else pursuing the same leads?
- **Orsik and Vardis's separate treasure map**: The two possess an independent map of unknown content or destination. Veradaa was seen as a potential source of information about it. What does their map lead to, and is it related to the Cult caches?
- **Thistle's unnamed friend**: Thistle is searching for a specific person. Who is this friend, where are they, and what is their connection to the Cult of the Dragon or the treasure caches?
- **Thistle's imprisonment by the Cult**: Thistle was imprisoned by the Cult for a long time. Under what circumstances? What did the Cult want with a fairy?
- **Clan True-Nimbus of the Clouds**: Cloud giants have already looted the first cache and left a mocking note. Are they pursuing all five caches? How did they learn of the caches? What is their broader agenda?
- **Giant activity in the North**: Rune-covered boulders, huge footprints, and missing livestock were reported near Parnast. Is this connected to Clan True-Nimbus, Slayermighty's group, or a broader giant movement?
- **The magical curse in the first cache cave**: Something in the cave triggered a curse shrinking all creatures within it. The curse reverts on death but appeared active while living. Was this a Cult trap (contradicting Veradaa's notes), something the giants triggered, or something else? What was its source?
- **The "terrible and immortal" entity in the fourth cache's dwarven outpost**: Something drove the dwarves out centuries ago. What is it, and is it still there?
- **The loud bell at the fifth cache shrine**: An unexplained bell sound heard from the giant shrine under certain wind conditions. What is its source?
- **The origin of the stone pyramid at the second cache**: Veradaa's sources could not identify who or what built the boulder pyramid. Its builder is unknown.
- **Blooddrinker's curse**: The bear was also shrunken. Will the curse reverse on Blooddrinker as it did on the dead goblins? Or is its bond to Thistle a factor in whether it remains small?
- **The five factions' post-war fracture**: The Harpers, Zhentarim, Lord's Alliance, Order of the Gauntlet, and Emerald Enclave have resumed their divisions. How will this affect the party's ability to find support or allies?
- **The remaining four caches**: Caches two through five remain unvisited and unlooted (by the party). Have other giants, factions, or parties already moved on them as well?
