## Threads & Mysteries

- **Troll displacement cause unknown**: Trolls are moving out of their usual hunting grounds in the Evermoors in increased numbers, encroaching on both nomad territory and Calling Horns. The cause has not been identified; the 3HPP have been tasked with finding out why.
- **Letter of Mark chain incomplete**: The 3HPP must complete Tamalin's investigation task to receive the Letter of Mark, then travel to Everlund, present the note to Dral at Danivarr's House, and use the captain of the guard's assistance — the full chain is established but none of these steps have been completed.
- **Harthal Zymorven's whereabouts**: Harthal Zymorven's location remains unknown; Eltio is the key to finding him, but Eltio has not yet been contacted this session.
- **The sword**: The 3HPP seek a specific sword connected to Zymorven Hall; its nature, current holder, and significance have not been detailed in this session.
- **Eltio's role and location**: Eltio is apparently positioned to provide guidance to Harthal Zymorven once the Letter of Mark is in hand; Eltio does not appear in this session and their current location is not stated.
- **Nomadic tribe remnants**: Some nomads may have survived the encounter; their shaman's fate is unconfirmed. The tribe's wider presence in the Evermoors and their knowledge of troll movements may be relevant to the investigation.
- **Thistle's deliberately withheld capabilities**: Thistle concealed his rapid multi-shot ability from his companions since before the Parnast hill giant encounter, choosing the challenge of fighting without it. Whether other capabilities are similarly withheld is an open question.
- **Orsik's admiration for Tamalin's "arc"**: Orsik internally frames Tamalin as having gone from "thief and crook to scoundrel" and wants a similar arc for himself; the nature of Tamalin's past criminal or adventuring history is not elaborated upon but is implied to be significant.
- **Unla Kee's sobriety and relapse**: Unla Kee had been alcohol-free for two weeks, and the abstinence was described as damaging his cognitive state; his return to drinking is framed as medically necessary in his own perception. The underlying reason for his prior sobriety and its effects remain unexplained.
