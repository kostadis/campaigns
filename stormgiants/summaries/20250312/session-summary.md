# Session 2025-03-12

Date: Mar 12th, 2025

## Summary
The night before the great confrontation, the party gathered to share a magical feast, a grand banquet conjured to steel their bodies and spirits for the battle ahead. The meal fortified each of them, bolstering their endurance and granting them a measure of protection against fear and poison. As they prepared, the weight of what lay ahead settled over them — an ancient blue dragon named Iymrith, a creature of terrifying power who had survived long enough to become a legend, guarded by devoted priests and surrounded by minions yet unseen.

Deep within the lair, Thistle faced a painful choice. He had come so far to rescue the woman he cared for, and now, with escape within reach, she urged him to flee with her through a hidden side exit. Thistle, however, had other plans — he would charge upward into the heart of the dragon battle as a distraction, drawing attention away from her escape. She was unconvinced and frightened, but she came to understand the grim logic of his plan: his presence in the chaos above would make their enemies believe the whole affair had been a prank by the demon Bob, not a rescue. With a tearful farewell, she slipped away through the side passage while Thistle flew upward to join his companions in the storm of battle.

The battle itself erupted with terrifying force when Bob, the great Balor demon, hurled himself at Iymrith in a fury of fire and rage. The storm giants — Serissa and her kin — thundered across the vast cavern floor, hurling massive boulders and closing the distance with earth-shaking strides. Gargoyles manning a trebuchet launched a crushing projectile that slammed into Bob, while Hekaton, the Storm Giant King, unleashed a blast of lightning that vaporized several of the gargoyles and silenced one of the siege engines. Dragon priests cowered at the edges of the chamber, channeling healing energy into Iymrith to keep her standing against the onslaught, while Iymrith herself turned her wrath upon Bob, rending him with her claws and tail before vanishing into invisibility to reposition.

Despite the chaos, the party fought with desperate coordination. Unla Key, soaring through the air on a Pegasus, repositioned above the fray and hurled a roaring sphere of flame at the dragon, forcing her to draw upon her legendary resilience to weather the blast. Vardis summoned a powerful Djinn to the battlefield to press the dragon from another angle, while Thistle marked Iymrith as his quarry and loosed arrows at her from a distance. Then came the decisive volley — five mages, moving in unison, unleashed a simultaneous storm of fire upon the ancient dragon, burning through every last reserve of her legendary defenses in a single, thunderous barrage. Serissa punctuated the assault with a critical boulder throw that struck Iymrith with bone-crushing force, and for a moment, it seemed as though the tide had turned.

But Iymrith was not finished. She turned her fury upon Bob one final time, striking him down with a legendary blow that sent the Balor vanishing in a puff of smoke — his brief, violent return to the world ending after barely a minute of combat. Then she unleashed her lightning breath in a devastating arc that tore through the summoned air elemental, destroying it instantly, and left Orsik grievously wounded and struggling to maintain his focus. As the dust settled on the first brutal exchange, Iymrith turned invisible once more, preparing to make herself an elusive and deadly target for the giants' next volley of stones, while the battered party braced themselves for what was still to come.

## Scenes
### Pre-Battle Preparations and Banter
#### The party gathers and prepares for a massive confrontation with an ancient blue dragon, discussing tactics and magical preparations.
- The party discusses the upcoming battle against Iymrith, an ancient blue dragon the DM describes as one who "didn't survive this long by being your average dragon" — the reason "everybody's going for the treasure trove."
- The party debates the action economy and the critical importance of not wasting actions on healing mid-fight. Orsik warns Vardis directly: "If you burn your actions trying to heal me, we will both end up dead."
- Vardis counters: "But by definition of healing you, you wouldn't be dead," to which the DM remarks, "That's really dark, I think."
- Orsik clarifies he is "just making a comment about the action economy."
- The party considers using Hero's Feast to bolster their health before the encounter, with Orsik suggesting it would have been prepared "the night before" or "in the morning."
- Vardis realizes she does not have Hero's Feast learned and swaps out a spell to acquire it, dropping Blade Barrier — which she has "never used" and "never will" — while keeping Sunbeam, which she has used.
- The roll for Hero's Feast yields a 16 increase to maximum hit points for all who partake — described as better than temporary hit points because it raises the actual maximum for 24 hours.
- The DM reveals that the dragon is supported by a group of clerics who will heal her during battle, and warns the party that "there are more minions that you haven't yet spotted."
- The group jokes about the possibility of a Tarrasque appearing: Orsik asks "Is the Tarrasque coming? Because that's what I really want," and the DM flatly replies "No."
- The discussion devolves into a brief tangent on how the Tarrasque could formerly be killed by a first-level Aarakocra due to lacking ranged attacks in the 2014 edition, and how the 2024 edition cleaned up those exploits.
- Orsik considers which elemental to summon via his Cube of Summoning, with the party debating air versus earth. They settle on Air Elemental because it can fly — though Orsik notes the Blue Dragon's immunity to lightning and confirms the Air Elemental deals thunder damage instead, making it a viable choice.
- The party attunes to items and discusses the Conch of Teleportation, with Orsik suggesting someone else carry it since he does not intend to use it.
- The DM notes that Bob the Balor — who the party previously declined to fight when the DM "had him as the hero of the play" and the party "did that really lame ass thing" — will finally see combat: "Well done. You finally get to use him in combat after all these years."

### The Escape Plan and the Call to Battle
#### Thistle and his companion discuss their escape from the dragon's lair while a massive battle erupts above.
- Thistle and his companion are still below when the battle begins above. The companion hears the commotion and proposes using the secret side exit rather than going up through the main entrance into the chaos.
- Thistle attempts to persuade his companion to follow him upward through the top entrance and into the heat of battle, rolling a persuasion of only 3. She is described as "kind of giving him the, you know, I don't trust you" look, and screams: "We're gonna go up there where the big battle is going to occur? Is that your plan?"
- Thistle retorts: "One-dimensional chess," and the DM corrects him — "Chess is on a two-dimensional board. It's one-dimensional chess."
- Unconvinced, the companion announces her counter-plan: "You're gonna go up that way and distract them while I go out the other side and we'll meet at the exits."
- Thistle relents: "Alright, we'll do it your way."
- Thistle rolls an insight check of 26 to read her sincerity. The DM confirms she genuinely believes her plan is best — she would prefer both of them to flee, but if Thistle is truly committed to fighting, she will not follow him into danger.
- The companion reasons that Thistle fighting above will convince the dragon's forces the whole affair was a prank by Bob: "If you don't show up, they might suspect something."
- She then offers her final appeal: "You've come so far to rescue me. Why don't you go one step further?" — and bids him a tearful farewell before slipping out through the side exit.
- Thistle flies upward into the main battle, and the DM confirms this sells the deception perfectly: the enemies become "all convinced that you went down there to see whether your love of your life was there and she wasn't. And so clearly it was a prank by Bob."
- The DM notes with grim humor that the fate of Thistle's lineage — something the late Uncle Joon was about to reveal — will never be known: "Unfortunately, Uncle Joon is dead, so we'll never know exactly what he was about to tell you."
- The Balor Bob initiates combat by rushing the dragon: "Alright, Bob rushes the dragon. And combat begins."

### The Battle of the Ancient Dragons Begins
#### The party and their giant allies engage the ancient blue dragon Iymrith and her minions in a massive cavern.
- The initiative order is established; Iymrith has a +16 initiative bonus — the DM notes she "didn't survive this long by being your average dragon." She rolls high and acts early.
- Iymrith opens by moving across the vast battlefield. She attempts to use her lightning breath but cannot line up a hit on Hekaton, so instead opts to rend the Balor physically — landing three hits for a combined 87 points of slashing and lightning damage (later revised upward with an additional ability for a total near 94 points). She then turns invisible.
- The DM clarifies the damage breakdown: 18 points from slashing, plus 11 from lightning, plus 25 from an additional ability.
- Serissa the Storm Giant charges toward the battle; she and her kin (Storm Giants 1 and 5) begin moving across the chamber.
- Orsik summons an Air Elemental via his Cube of Summoning (rolling a 5, selecting "dealer's choice," landing on Air Elemental after party discussion). The elemental is positioned near Iymrith.
- Gargoyles manning a trebuchet fire upon the Balor, hitting for 44 points of damage — but Bob takes only half due to his resistances, for 22 points of damage.
- Hekaton advances and destroys two gargoyles with a lightning blast, incapacitating one trebuchet, then moves 40 feet toward the fight.
- Dragon priests channel healing magic into Iymrith — one Archpriest among them heals her for 10 hit points.
- Storm giants continue to clear gargoyles from the battlefield, eliminating the remaining ones over the course of the round. The DM eulogizes: "The gargoyles are all dead. We barely knew you."
- The five red mages are added to the initiative order (rolling a 17 with a +2 bonus) after being left off the tracker initially. They begin moving up behind the party.
- Iymrith attacks the Balor with her main action: she hits Bob twice for a combined 63 points of damage.
- Orsik advances 100 feet (using Dash as a bonus action) to close the enormous distance across the cavern. He directs the Air Elemental to move 90 feet into flanking position.
- Hekaton hurls a boulder (the DM notes his stat block oddly lists a "Ballista" attack, describing it as "a weird set of attacks for him"), dealing 35 points of damage to Iymrith.
- Dragon priests channel a combined healing of 33 hit points into Iymrith.
- Giants 2 and 3 advance and throw boulders — one hits for 33 points of damage, one misses.
- Unla Key, always in Pegasus form, has a fly speed of 90 feet and maneuvers out of the central danger zone, repositioning to find a vantage point above the fray.
- Orsik's Air Elemental attacks Iymrith but misses. Iymrith takes 30 points of passive fire damage from Bob's aura.
- The DM notes that the other chromatic dragons present in the chamber are advancing to attack each other and do not consider the party important enough to target — "They don't think you're important enough to kill" — buying the party a temporary reprieve.
- Vardis books it 70 feet across the chamber, moving out of the central danger zone and toward the fight.
- Thistle moves 70 feet upward from the lower passage and into the main battle area. The DM moves the mages 60 feet forward simultaneously.

### The Siege of Iymrith
#### The party and their diverse allies launch a coordinated assault on the ancient blue dragon Iymrith, depleting her legendary defenses.
- Unla Key, flying on the Pegasus, repositions to approximately 80 feet from Iymrith — just within Fireball range — and launches a Fireball at DC 21. Iymrith has a +5 Dexterity saving throw and fails, burning one of her legendary resistances to halve the 20 points of fire damage.
- The party briefly debates other options: Chain Lightning is rejected because Iymrith is immune to lightning damage ("Maybe don't chain lightning the lightning immune dragon — just, you know, minor suggestion"); Disintegrate is considered but Unla Key is too far away at 60-foot range. Fireball is chosen as the best available option.
- The DM counsels saving high-level spells and save-or-suck effects for after Iymrith's legendary resistances are burned: "Spells that have save or suck are not very useful against ancient dragons until after they've burned through their Legendary Resistances."
- Iymrith focuses her counterattack on Bob the Balor, striking him repeatedly with claws and tail for a combined 63 points of damage over the course of the round. Bob retaliates, landing two hits for a combined 36 points of damage against Iymrith.
- Serissa hurls a massive boulder at Iymrith, scoring a natural 20 for 80 points of damage — a critical hit that represents the most damage dealt to the dragon in a single blow thus far.
- Iymrith briefly turns invisible to reposition and gains advantage on defensive maneuvers, causing attackers (including the Djinn) to act at disadvantage. She takes 30 additional points of passive fire damage from Bob's aura during this time.
- Orsik advances further into the heart of the conflict, using both his action and Dash bonus action to move 100 feet. He directs the Air Elemental 90 feet into flanking position near the dragon.
- Orsik shouts "KILL HER! KILL HER!" at Bob the Balor, rolling a 13 on his persuasion check. The DM rules that the enraged demon — "insanely enraged beyond all human recognition" — focuses his fury on Iymrith regardless.
- Vardis advances 35 feet and spends her action to summon the Djinn, who appears within 120 feet of her on the opposite side of the dragon to provide flanking. The Djinn attacks three times with melee strikes but misses all three — "Good job, Jin, but at least you're flanking."
- Thistle activates Hunter's Mark on Iymrith and fires two arrows, both of which miss (rolling a 20 total, against Iymrith's AC of 22).
- Hekaton continues his assault, hurling a boulder for 35 points of damage.
- The five NPC mages move 30 feet closer to Iymrith and unleash five simultaneous Fireballs (spell DC 14). Iymrith saves against two of them but burns her remaining three legendary resistances to survive the other three. Total fire damage from the barrage is approximately 71 points, dropping Iymrith below 200 hit points.
- Orsik counts down Iymrith's legendary resistances as the mages fire: "She can burn all her legendary resistances. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 Fireballs."
- Unla Key contributes one final low-level Fireball, forcing Iymrith to burn her last remaining legendary resistance.
- With all legendary resistances exhausted, Iymrith uses her final legendary action to strike down Bob the Balor — dealing 21 points of damage to finish him off. Bob vanishes in a puff of smoke, returning to his home plane. The DM marks the moment: "Bob absorbed quite a bit of attacks."
- Orsik notes philosophically that while Bob is not truly dead — merely returned to his native plane — "he's not really dead, right? He's just at home. This was his, like, bus ride home."
- Iymrith unleashes her lightning breath in a devastating line attack, dealing 103 points of damage. Orsik faces a DC 23 Dexterity saving throw — his worst save — and requires a natural 20 to succeed. He does not roll one, taking the full damage. His concentration is broken and the Air Elemental, also caught in the blast, is instantly destroyed. "Aw, well that was a waste."
- Iymrith prepares to turn invisible at the end of the round, timing it so that the giants' subsequent boulder throws will be made at disadvantage. The DM explains: "She's going to wait for one action and then she's going to use her legendary action to become invisible, so that when she gets the Attacks of Opportunity, they are taken at a disadvantage."
- The session ends at the half-hour mark with Iymrith below 200 hit points, all legendary resistances burned, but the party battered — Orsik severely wounded, the Air Elemental destroyed, and Bob gone — with the dragon now invisible and preparing for the next brutal exchange.

## Locations
### Iymrith's Lair
A vast cavernous area featuring multiple exits, including a secret back door and a main entrance leading to the battlefield above. Large enough to host a battle between multiple ancient dragons, storm giants, and their allies, with gargoyle-manned trebuchets positioned throughout. The teleportation-blocking magic in the area prevents use of the Conch of Teleportation until the party escapes its influence. The DM notes the map is so large it would take roughly three rounds just to get into Fireball range from the starting position, and positions the party closer to the action to compensate.

### Hekaton's Domain
An underwater palace mentioned as a potential destination for escape, serving as a long-term goal for the party. The companion is directed toward it as her refuge after fleeing through the side exit.

## Items
### Conch of Teleportation
A magical item capable of transporting users across great distances. The party discusses giving it to Thistle's companion for her escape, but notes that its use is currently blocked by the anti-teleportation magic blanketing the lair. Orsik suggests giving it to the companion so she can use it once outside the warded area: "Give her the conch and then come join us in battle."

### Cube of Summoning
A magical artifact used by Orsik to call forth an Air Elemental to fight alongside the party on the battlefield. Orsik notes with enthusiasm upon finding it in his equipment: "Ah, yes, the Cube of Summoning. I definitely want to use that." The Air Elemental it produces is ultimately destroyed by Iymrith's lightning breath.

### Trebuchet
A large siege engine manned by gargoyles that successfully struck the Balor with a massive projectile during the battle, dealing 44 points of damage (22 after Bob's resistances). Hekaton subsequently destroys the gargoyles operating one trebuchet with a lightning blast, incapacitating that engine. The DM warns the party that approaching the other remaining trebuchet could become relevant.

### Wand of Wonder
A chaotic magical wand with three charges discussed by the party as a potential last-resort weapon. Unla Key confirms it is in his inventory as a special magic item granted by the DM. The group agrees it should be saved for a finishing blow — the DM concurs: "The Wand of Wonder should be reserved as a last-ditch thing." Unla Key adds: "I feel like that needs to be like the finishing blow."

## Spells
### Hero's Feast
A sixth-level conjuration that creates a grand magical banquet. Vardis swaps Blade Barrier to learn it, having never used Blade Barrier and determining it more valuable for this fight. The party consumed it the night before (or morning of) the battle. The roll of 2d10 yielded 16, increasing everyone's maximum hit points by 16 — not temporary hit points, but a true increase to maximum — and granting immunity to the frightened and poisoned conditions, as well as resistance to poison damage for twenty-four hours. Orsik confirms: "Extra 16 hit points, I'll take them," and notes the party can still stack temporary hit points on top.

### Fireball
A sphere of roaring flame used by Unla Key (at a spell DC of 21) and then by a coordinated group of five NPC mages (at spell DC 14) to bombard Iymrith. Unla Key's cast forced Iymrith to burn one legendary resistance; the five-mage barrage burned three more; a final cast from Unla Key consumed the last. The choice of Fireball over Chain Lightning is deliberate — Iymrith is immune to lightning damage, and fire bypasses that immunity.

### Lightning Breath
Iymrith's signature draconic attack, a massive line of electrical energy at DC 23 Dexterity save. It dealt 103 points of damage, destroyed the summoned Air Elemental, broke Orsik's concentration, and left him severely wounded. Orsik required a natural 20 on his worst saving throw to halve the damage — and did not get one.

### Hunter's Mark
A tracking spell used by Thistle to focus his attacks on Iymrith, marking her as his quarry to increase the effectiveness of his arrows. Both arrow attacks missed despite the mark (rolling a combined 20 against AC 22).

### Invisibility
A magical ability used by Iymrith on multiple occasions during the battle to vanish from sight. She times her use of legendary-action invisibility strategically so that subsequent attacks of opportunity — and the giants' boulder throws — are made at disadvantage. The DM notes Bob lacks See Invisibility, which hampers his ability to retaliate effectively.

### Chain Lightning
A powerful electrical spell considered by Unla Key as an option against multiple targets, but rejected due to Iymrith's complete immunity to lightning damage. The party agrees immediately: "Maybe don't chain lightning the lightning immune dragon."

### Expeditious Retreat
A speed-enhancing spell considered by Orsik to increase his movement across the vast battlefield, though he chooses not to expend a spell slot on it: "I don't want to burn a spell slot on that."

## NPCs
### Iymrith
An ancient blue dragon of immense power and legendary resilience, immune to lightning damage, with an Armor Class of 22 and a +16 initiative bonus. She opens the battle by rending Bob three times for approximately 94 combined points of slashing and lightning damage, then turns invisible to reposition. She absorbs the party's full assault — including five simultaneous Fireballs — by burning through all five of her legendary resistances, then retaliates by striking down Bob with a legendary action and unleashing her lightning breath to destroy Orsik's Air Elemental and leave him grievously wounded. She ends the session invisible, preparing to make herself an elusive target for the giants. The DM notes she "didn't survive this long by being your average dragon."

### Bob (Balor)
A powerful Balor demon who served as a temporary and unpredictable ally to the party — one the party previously declined to fight when the DM had introduced him as a would-be major encounter. He charged headlong into battle against Iymrith, absorbing three hits for ~94 points of damage, a trebuchet shot for 22 points (after resistances), Iymrith's repeated claw and tail attacks across multiple rounds, and passive area-of-effect fire damage — before being struck down by Iymrith's legendary action and vanishing in a puff of smoke after fewer than three rounds of combat. Orsik laments: "All that time on the astral plane, and he's just — not gonna make it to a third round. It gets 12 seconds of combat." The party notes he is not truly dead, merely returned to his home plane.

### Hekaton
A Storm Giant King who leads the assault against Iymrith. He uses lightning blasts to eliminate gargoyles and disable a trebuchet, then hurls massive boulders at the dragon for 35 points of damage. His stat block peculiarly lists a "Ballista" attack, which the DM describes as "a weird set of attacks for him."

### Serissa
A storm giant ally who charges into battle against Iymrith alongside her kin. She scores a critical hit (natural 20) with a massive boulder dealing 80 points of damage — the single largest hit dealt to the dragon in the session. Earlier in the round she attempts a lightning-based attack but the DM confirms Iymrith is immune.

### Dragon Priests
Devoted followers of Iymrith who focus entirely on channeling healing magic into the dragon to sustain her during the onslaught. They heal her for 10 hit points in the first round (via an Archpriest among them rolling 2d4) and a combined 33 hit points in a subsequent round, partially offsetting the party's damage output.

### Red Wizards (Mages)
A group of five mages from Silverymoon who have joined the fray alongside the party. They rolled a 17 on initiative (+2 bonus) and are added to the tracker mid-session. On their decisive turn, all five advance 30 feet and unleash simultaneous Fireballs at Iymrith (spell DC 14), dealing a combined approximately 71 points of fire damage and forcing Iymrith to burn her last three legendary resistances. Orsik directs them: "They all cast Fireball. Every single one of them." Vardis jokes about their "backward outlook on Silverymoon" while affirming they are firmly on the party's side today.

### Thistle's Companion
A woman Thistle rescued from deep within Iymrith's lair who is sharp, pragmatic, and frightened. She proposes the side-exit escape plan herself when she hears the battle above: "You're gonna go up that way and distract them while I go out the other side and we'll meet at the exits." She sees through Thistle's poor persuasion roll (a 3) but ultimately accepts the grim logic that his presence in the main battle will serve as cover for her escape, reasoning: "If you don't show up, they might suspect something." Thistle's insight check of 26 confirms she genuinely believes this is the best available outcome. She bids him a tearful farewell and flees through the side passage. The DM notes the party will never learn what the late Uncle Joon was going to reveal about her — or Thistle's — nature.

### Djinn
A powerful elemental being summoned by Vardis using the Cube of Summoning to provide flanking support from the opposite side of Iymrith. He has 161 hit points and a fly speed, and is positioned on the far side of the dragon. Despite attempting three melee strikes on his first turn, the Djinn misses all three — prompting Vardis to remark "Good job, Jin, but at least you're flanking." His Storm Bolt ranged attacks are noted as starting at disadvantage in melee range, which is why Vardis opts for melee strikes instead.
