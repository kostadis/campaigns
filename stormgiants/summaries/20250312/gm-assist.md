# Session 2025-03-12

Date: Mar 12th, 2025

## Summary
The night before the great confrontation, the party gathered to share a magical feast, a grand banquet conjured to steel their bodies and spirits for the battle ahead. The meal fortified each of them, bolstering their endurance and granting them a measure of protection against fear and poison. As they prepared, the weight of what lay ahead settled over them — an ancient blue dragon named Iymrith, a creature of terrifying power who had survived long enough to become a legend, guarded by devoted priests and surrounded by minions yet unseen.

Deep within the lair, Thistle faced a painful choice. He had come so far to rescue the woman he cared for, and now, with escape within reach, she urged him to flee with her through a hidden side exit. Thistle, however, had other plans — he would charge upward into the heart of the dragon battle as a distraction, drawing attention away from her escape. She was unconvinced and frightened, but she came to understand the grim logic of his plan: his presence in the chaos above would make their enemies believe the whole affair had been a prank by the demon Bob, not a rescue. With a tearful farewell, she slipped away through the side passage while Thistle flew upward to join his companions in the storm of battle.

The battle itself erupted with terrifying force when Bob, the great Balor demon, hurled himself at Iymrith in a fury of fire and rage. The storm giants — Sarissa and her kin — thundered across the vast cavern floor, hurling massive boulders and closing the distance with earth-shaking strides. Gargoyles manning a trebuchet launched a crushing projectile that slammed into Bob, while Hekaton, the Storm Giant King, unleashed a blast of lightning that vaporized several of the gargoyles and silenced one of the siege engines. Dragon priests cowered at the edges of the chamber, channeling healing energy into Iymrith to keep her standing against the onslaught, while Iymrith herself turned her wrath upon Bob, rending him with her claws and tail before vanishing into invisibility to reposition.

Despite the chaos, the party fought with desperate coordination. Unlucky, soaring through the air on a Pegasus, repositioned above the fray and hurled a roaring sphere of flame at the dragon, forcing her to draw upon her legendary resilience to weather the blast. Vartis summoned a powerful Djinn to the battlefield to press the dragon from another angle, while Bist marked Iymrith as his quarry and loosed arrows at her from a distance. Then came the decisive volley — five mages, moving in unison, unleashed a simultaneous storm of fire upon the ancient dragon, burning through every last reserve of her legendary defenses in a single, thunderous barrage. Sarissa punctuated the assault with a critical boulder throw that struck Iymrith with bone-crushing force, and for a moment, it seemed as though the tide had turned.

But Iymrith was not finished. She turned her fury upon Bob one final time, striking him down with a legendary blow that sent the Balor vanishing in a puff of smoke — his brief, violent return to the world ending after barely a minute of combat. Then she unleashed her lightning breath in a devastating arc that tore through the summoned air elemental, destroying it instantly, and left Orsik grievously wounded and struggling to maintain his focus. As the dust settled on the first brutal exchange, Iymrith turned invisible once more, preparing to make herself an elusive and deadly target for the giants' next volley of stones, while the battered party braced themselves for what was still to come.

## Locations
### Iymrith's Lair
A vast cavernous area featuring multiple exits, including a secret back door and a main entrance leading to the battlefield above. Large enough to host a battle between multiple ancient dragons, storm giants, and their allies, with gargoyle-manned trebuchets positioned throughout.

### Hekaton's Domain
An underwater palace mentioned as a potential destination for escape via the mortal coil, serving as a long-term goal for the party.

## Items
### Conch of Teleportation
A magical item capable of transporting users across great distances. The party considers giving it to Thistle's companion for her escape, though its use is currently restricted by local magical bindings preventing teleportation in the area.

### Cube of Summoning
A magical artifact used by Vartis to call forth a Djinn to fight alongside the party on the battlefield.

### Trebuchet
A large siege engine manned by gargoyles that successfully struck the Balor with a massive projectile during the battle, dealing significant damage.

### Wand of Wonder
A chaotic magical wand with three charges discussed by the party as a potential last-resort weapon to use against Iymrith, with the group agreeing it should be saved for a finishing blow.

## Spells
### Hero's Feast
A high-level conjuration that creates a grand magical banquet. The party consumed it the night before the battle, increasing everyone's maximum hit points by 16 and granting immunity to the frightened and poisoned conditions, as well as resistance to poison damage for twenty-four hours.

### Fireball
A sphere of roaring flame used by Unlucky and then by a coordinated group of five mages to bombard Iymrith. The mage barrage successfully forced the dragon to expend all of her legendary resistances to avoid the full brunt of the magical fire.

### Lightning Breath
Iymrith's signature draconic attack, a massive line of electrical energy that decimated the summoned air elemental and severely wounded Orsik in a single devastating blast.

### Hunter's Mark
A tracking spell used by Bist to focus his attacks on Iymrith, marking her as his quarry to increase the effectiveness of his arrows.

### Invisibility
A magical ability used by Iymrith on multiple occasions during the battle to vanish from sight, making her a much harder target for the giants' boulder strikes and forcing attackers to act at a disadvantage.

### Chain Lightning
A powerful electrical spell considered by Unlucky as an option against multiple targets, but ultimately rejected due to Iymrith's complete immunity to lightning damage.

### Expeditious Retreat
A speed-enhancing spell considered by Orsik to increase his movement across the vast battlefield, though he chose not to expend a spell slot on it.

## NPCs
### Iymrith
An ancient blue dragon of immense power and legendary resilience, immune to lightning damage. She defends her hoard with lethal lightning breath, physical rending attacks, and cunning use of invisibility to outmaneuver her many foes. She is supported by devoted dragon priests who heal her during battle.

### Bob (Balor)
A powerful Balor demon who served as a temporary and unpredictable ally to the party. He charged headlong into battle against Iymrith, absorbing massive amounts of damage from her claws, tail, and a gargoyle-fired trebuchet before being struck down and vanishing in a puff of smoke.

### Hekaton
A Storm Giant King who leads the assault against Iymrith. He uses lightning blasts to eliminate gargoyles and their siege engines, and hurls massive boulders at the dragon.

### Sarissa
A storm giant ally who charges into battle against Iymrith, hurling massive boulders with incredible force, including a critical hit that dealt enormous damage to the dragon.

### Dragon Priests
Devoted followers of Iymrith who focus on channeling healing magic into the dragon to keep her in the fight during the massive battle.

### Red Wizards (Mages)
A group of mages who have joined the fray alongside the party, positioned near them to provide magical support. They unleash a coordinated barrage of Fireballs at Iymrith.

### Thistle's Companion
A woman Thistle rescued from the lair who is skeptical of his combat plans. She chooses to escape through a side exit rather than follow Thistle into the dragon battle above, bidding him a tearful farewell.

### Djinn
A powerful elemental being summoned by Vartis to assist in the melee, capable of striking with thunderous force, though his initial attacks failed to connect.
