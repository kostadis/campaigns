**Suggested Fix:** Replace "soaring on a Pegasus" with "flying on his Pegasus wings" (or simply "flying"). Remove the characterization "always in Pegasus form" — he has no such form. Confirm his fly speed against the wings item stats.

## ISSUE 8: Iymrith's Legendary Resistance Count — Internal Inconsistency

**Location:** Scenes ("The Siege of Iymrith") / Spells (Fireball) / NPCs (Iymrith)

**Issue:** The recap describes the expenditure of legendary resistances as follows:
- Unla Key's first Fireball: burns **1** resistance
- Five-mage barrage: Iymrith saves against two Fireballs, burns **3** resistances on the other three
- "A final cast from Unla Key consumed the last."

This implies a total of **1 + 3 + 1 = 5** legendary resistances. However, the Scenes section states Orsik counts down "1, 2, 3, 4, **5** Fireballs" from the mages — but only five mages fire, two of which Iymrith saves against normally. If she burned 3 on the mage barrage plus 1 from Unla Key's first cast, that is 4 — and then "a final cast from Unla Key consumed the last" would make 5 total. This arithmetic is internally consistent **if** Iymrith has exactly 5 legendary resistances, which is correct for an ancient dragon in 5e.

However, the Scenes section also says Unla Key launches "one final low-level Fireball, forcing Iymrith to burn her **last remaining** legendary resistance" — which would mean at that point she had exactly 1 left, consistent with the count. **No contradiction here mathematically,** but Orsik's quoted line "She can burn all her legendary resistances. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 Fireballs" is potentially misleading, since she saved against 2 of the 5 mage Fireballs without spending a resistance. The quote implies all 5 Fireballs burned a resistance, which is not what happened.

**Evidence:** Internal to the recap. Ancient blue dragon (5e): 5 legendary resistances per day.

**Suggested Fix:** Clarify in the Memorable Moments or Scenes note that Orsik's "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" countdown refers to the five Fireballs cast, not five resistances burned from that barrage alone. A brief editorial note — e.g., "(she saved against two normally, burning three resistances on the others)" — prevents future confusion about when resistances were exhausted.
