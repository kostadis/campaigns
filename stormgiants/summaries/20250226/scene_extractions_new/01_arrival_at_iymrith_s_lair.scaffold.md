[Scene 1] Arrival at Iymrith's Lair
Narrator: 
Focus: 

- The party arrives at Iymrith's castle and is greeted by the blue dragon, who emphasizes the importance of the upcoming event and the dire consequences of failure.

- Vardis attempts to joke with Iymrith about her high school bully; Iymrith responds with disturbing sincerity: "Every time I want to think about what a success I am, I just fantasize about that meal."

- Thistle notes that Iymrith "doesn't have the bones" — to which Iymrith mournfully agrees before collecting herself.

- Iymrith threatens the party: failure means someone is burned to a crisp. When Vardis jokes "Can it be me?", Iymrith responds that she has heard about his goals in life and promises specifically that he will not be allowed to die — she will protect him from dragons herself if she has to. "The real punishment."

- Iymrith states she needs the performance to happen the next day: "If it doesn't happen, well, you know, someone's gonna have to be burned to a crisp."

- Thistle tells Iymrith "This show's improv" to explain why they only arrived the day before; Iymrith is visibly uncomfortable with this answer.

- Vardis employs "method acting" as a cover for pressing Iymrith for information, insisting he needs to understand her theology in order to be "in character" for his role; Iymrith refuses to engage: "I don't have time to explain this to you."

- Iymrith leads the party through the lair, passing a group of Yuan-ti worshiping a statue in her image. She dismisses them as "just part of my group that worships me" — apparently unaware of or unbothered by the theological inconsistency Vardis attempts to point out.

- The party is taken to the lower level where Laela is held in a magical contraption she cannot escape.

- Iymrith demands a monologue for the performance, expressing a desire to silence all who doubted her genius and prove herself to her investors: "I want to show all of those who've doubted me and all of my investors that I have achieved the impossible."

- Thistle suggests Iymrith go practice her monologue with Orsik as director while he works with Laela; Iymrith designates Orsik as director on the spot: "Are you the director? Yes. Orsik is the director."

- Orsik achieves a natural 20 on deception to convince Iymrith that Laela cannot rehearse inside the magical contraption — arguing that his "artistic vision is being compromised" — successfully getting Laela released for the dress rehearsal.

- The party begins a "dress rehearsal," with Thistle insisting they need to be in costume: "I can't rehearse without them. This is a dress rehearsal."
