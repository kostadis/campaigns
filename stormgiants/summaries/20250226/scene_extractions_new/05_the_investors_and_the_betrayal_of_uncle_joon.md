# The Investors and the Betrayal of Uncle Joon

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### As the grand performance nears, the party encounters mysterious mind flayer investors and deals with the treachery of Thistle's uncle.
- A group of mind flayers arrives, with Iymrith identifying them only as "investors" and dismissing further questions: "None of your business. Investors."
- The party presses: Vardis wonders whether these are the mind flayers connected to the Kraken Society; Iymrith refuses to confirm or deny but is visibly nervous around them. The party notices this unease.
- The party observes that no other faction — dragons, giants, or otherwise — approaches the mind flayers. As Unla Key notes: "That's just good." Self-preservation instinct.
- Unla Key considers going to fight the mind flayers; the party instead discusses charming them or getting them on side. The question goes unresolved as the evening winds down.
- Uncle Joon appears in the shadows of the lair, greeting Thistle with casual familiarity: "I've been waiting for you to show up here."
- Uncle Joon admits without much preamble that he staged the entire situation — he needed the party to either distract Iymrith or kill her so he could slip into the hoard and extract the item he needed to pay off his debts: "Maybe it wasn't Titania. I don't want you to think that it was Titania. But it was somebody with that kind of importance level, you know."
- Uncle Joon admits he knew where Laela was the entire time and has been waiting in the lair for weeks, confused about why the party showed up for the conference instead of arriving earlier: "You weren't supposed to show up at this big conference — you were supposed to show up when you could."
- Vardis notes privately: "This is like the ultimate betrayal. Game of Thrones level."
- The party moves immediately: Orsik grapples Joon (noting a +10, making it a foregone conclusion), and the group gags and binds him. Joon pleads "Guys, I can help" but is left tied up in the lair.
- The party arranges to retrieve the item Joon had wanted and place it in his bound hands after the post-reveal chaos — a final, pointed flourish described as leaving "the item he had sold out his nephew to obtain" in his helpless grip.
- Orsik comments: "Next thing you know, we're gonna wake up and it was all just a dream. We were all just coping with death all along." (paraphrase)
