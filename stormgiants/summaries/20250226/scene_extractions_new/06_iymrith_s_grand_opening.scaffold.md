[Scene 6] Iymrith's Grand Opening
Narrator: 
Focus: 

- Iymrith opens the show by roaring and firing a lightning bolt into the sky; the assembled chromatic dragons respond with breath weapons (fire and acid), and the giants pound their fists on the stone. The effect is described as "this whole spectacle of things."

- The mind flayers hover in total silence at the edges of the gathering, avoided by every other faction — giving the opening a genuinely unsettling texture.

- Iymrith's first words to the assembled crowd address her "minor bureaucratic" housekeeping: "There will be no sampling of any of my creations" — singling out the red dragon Flume specifically.

- Flume waves a dismissive claw; Iymrith moves on.

- Iymrith delivers her practiced monologue, with Unla Key feeding her the lines when she falters mid-speech. The monologue mocks all who doubted her ambition, boasts of her mastery over magic, and culminates in pointing directly at a storm giant: "Where is your storm now?"

- Full monologue delivered on stage: "I remember the whispers, the doubts, the laughter, the shaking hands in candlelit halls, the murmurs and shadowed corners. Iymrith is too ambitious, you said. No dragon can bend the laws of magic itself. No creature, scaled, mortal, or divine, can wield such power without being consumed by it. And yet here I stand."

- Iymrith concludes by presenting Laela as her "perfect assassin, the fairy dragon" to the gathered audience, spreading her wings as "the air crackling as reality seems to bend itself around her presence."
