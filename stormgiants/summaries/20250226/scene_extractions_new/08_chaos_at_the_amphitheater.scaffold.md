[Scene 8] Chaos At The Amphitheater
Narrator: Orsik
Focus: Orsik has wanted a fight worthy of the death he's been promising himself, and the world has finally obliged.

- The chromatic dragons immediately recognize the entrance to Iymrith's legendary hoard — described as a hole leading below — and begin racing toward it; the red and black dragons snap and snarl at each other over who gets there first.

- The DM makes clear that the hoard's legendary contents — gold and magical artifacts — are the primary motivator: "Remember, these are dragons. They care about their money. Especially red dragons care about the money."

- The storm giants arrive on the battlefield as allied reinforcements, their presence immediately drawing the attention of every other giant faction: fire giants, cloud giants, and others who had come with old grudges.

- The Red Wizards of Thay withdraw quietly, writing off their investment rather than risk dying in a melee: "They'll come back and they'll find another way to get their money back."

- The mind flayers step back to observe, avoided by everyone. The DM notes pointedly: "Everybody's got somebody they want to go fight except the mind flayers."

- Unla Key briefly announces he will go fight the mind flayers. The party advises against this.

- Orsik delivers a rousing speech to the storm giants — aided by Unla Key's portent die roll of 19, bringing persuasion to a 26. He argues that Iymrith is to blame for the chaos and that her death is both just and profitable.

- The storm giant king is persuaded; the DM clarifies the chromatic dragons are not interested in fighting Iymrith — they want the gold — but the storm giants now commit to a direct assault.

- The other giant factions mostly gravitate toward the melee or the hoard entrance; the cloud giants hang back, watching with calculating neutrality.

- Iymrith is left preoccupied: Bob's relentless taunting keeps her occupied on one flank while the storm giants press in from the other.

- The party discusses strategic options: Thistle suggests using Wall of Force to seal the hoard entrance and trap the looting dragons underground; both Vardis and Unla Key note they have Mold Earth. These options remain on the table as the battle begins.

- The DM establishes the battlefield as a red zone of stray dragon breath, thundering footsteps, and random hazard: "Saving throws will have to be made against dragon breath and potentially getting attacked by dragons. If you choose to engage with anybody, you become a target."

- The party reaches a grim strategic clarity: defeating Iymrith will make every greedy, ruthless faction present owe them something. As the DM explains: "In the same way Tony Soprano is grateful. Or Michael Corleone was grateful." The party accepts this framing.

- The session ends on the cusp of the final battle: Laela is free, Uncle Joon is bound in the dark holding the very item he sold out his nephew to obtain, Bob is still making jokes, and Iymrith — the doom of the desert — is finally cornered.
