# Schmoozing with the Chromatic Dragons

## Scene summary (from gm-assist, verbatim)

#### Orsik and Vardis navigate the egos of ancient dragons while preparing for Iymrith's grand performance.
- Orsik approaches the ancient red dragon Flume's entourage; a young dragon intercepts him and warns he is not welcome, relaying Flume's initial contempt: "I was promised something special and all I smell is a tiny, sniveling dwarf."
- Orsik rolls successfully on persuasion to gain an audience; he offers Flume a private preview that no other guest will receive. Flume is intrigued and allows him forward.
- Flume is described as a gargantuan ancient red dragon seated on a lowered chair, giving her presence a throne-like quality. She explains her philosophy on naming: "When you're me, you need something simple, something so that people can remember it." She dislikes the long, theatrical names other red dragons use.
- Flume warns that there had better be "hearts everywhere" at the last exposé — referencing apparently the mess left at some prior event. Iymrith specifically warns Flume against "sampling" during the opening remarks.
- Vardis interacts with the ancient green dragon, who is described as deeply skeptical — she suspects the fairy dragon might be an illusion, or that the transformation will be faked with a spell cast when no one is looking. She warns: "If she does not pay out this time, there will be hell to pay," citing a long and costly investment in Iymrith's experiments.
- Vardis leans into the green dragon's frustration and encourages her expectations: "Your money is safe. No rug pulls here." The party privately notes this dragon is already fuming against Iymrith and will not need much more persuasion.
- The party — led by Unla Key — attempts to provoke the ancient black dragon Obsidian by relaying that the red and green dragons had been calling him the weakest. Obsidian's response is to boast: "I have spent centuries outmaneuvering them, and now my clan controls most of the trade."
- The party pushes further, questioning whether Obsidian is essentially just a Zhentarim capo. Obsidian responds: "I don't want to talk to you" and flies off. The party acknowledges he may need further persuasion later.
