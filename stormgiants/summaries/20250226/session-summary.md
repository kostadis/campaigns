# Session 2025-03-12

Date: Mar 12th, 2025

## Summary
The night before the great confrontation, the party gathered to share a magical feast, a grand banquet conjured to steel their bodies and spirits for the battle ahead. The meal fortified each of them, bolstering their endurance and granting them a measure of protection against fear and poison. As they prepared, the weight of what lay ahead settled over them — an ancient blue dragon named Iymrith, a creature of terrifying power who had survived long enough to become a legend, guarded by devoted priests and surrounded by minions yet unseen. Vardis dropped Blade Barrier from her prepared spells, a power she had never found occasion to use, and replaced it with Hero's Feast.

Deep within the lair, Thistle faced a painful choice. He had come so far to rescue the woman he cared for, and now, with escape within reach, she urged him to flee with her through a hidden side exit. Thistle, however, had other plans — he would charge upward into the heart of the dragon battle as a distraction, drawing attention away from her escape. A persuasion roll of only 3 failed to bring her along willingly, but a subsequent insight check of 26 confirmed she was acting in genuine good faith. She was unconvinced and frightened, but she came to understand the grim logic of his plan: his presence in the chaos above would make their enemies believe the whole affair had been a prank by Bob, not a rescue. With a tearful farewell, she slipped away through the side passage while Thistle flew upward to join his companions in the storm of battle.

The battle itself erupted with terrifying force when Bob, the great Balor demon, hurled himself at Iymrith in a fury of fire and rage. The storm giants — Serissa and her kin — thundered across the vast cavern floor, hurling massive boulders and closing the distance with earth-shaking strides. Gargoyles manning a trebuchet launched a crushing projectile that slammed into Bob for 22 points of damage, while Hekaton, the Storm Giant King, unleashed a blast of lightning that vaporized several of the gargoyles and silenced one of the siege engines. Dragon priests cowered at the edges of the chamber, channeling healing energy into Iymrith to keep her standing against the onslaught, while Iymrith herself turned her wrath upon Bob, rending him with her claws and tail before vanishing into invisibility to reposition.

Despite the chaos, the party fought with desperate coordination. Unla Key, soaring through the air on a Pegasus, repositioned above the fray and hurled a roaring sphere of flame at the dragon with a DC 21, forcing her to draw upon her legendary resilience to weather the blast. Vardis summoned a powerful Djinn to the battlefield to press the dragon from another angle, while the Djinn's three initial melee swings all missed. Thistle marked Iymrith with Hunter's Mark and loosed arrows at her, also failing to connect through her formidable armor. Then came the decisive volley — five mages, moving in unison, unleashed a simultaneous storm of fire upon the ancient dragon with a spell DC of 14, burning through every last reserve of her legendary defenses in a single, thunderous barrage and dealing a combined 71 points of damage. Serissa punctuated the assault with a critical boulder throw that struck Iymrith with bone-crushing force for 80 points of damage, and for a moment, it seemed as though the tide had turned.

But Iymrith was not finished. She turned her fury upon Bob one final time, striking him down with a legendary blow that sent the Balor vanishing in a puff of smoke — his brief, violent return to the world ending after barely a minute of combat. Then she unleashed her lightning breath in a devastating arc at DC 23, dealing 103 points of damage, that tore through the summoned air elemental, destroying it instantly, and left Orsik grievously wounded and unable to maintain his concentration. As the dust settled on the first brutal exchange, Iymrith turned invisible once more, preparing to make herself an elusive and deadly target for the giants' next volley of stones, while the battered party braced themselves for what was still to come.

## Scenes
### Pre-Battle Preparations and Banter
#### The party gathers and prepares for a massive confrontation with an ancient blue dragon, discussing tactics and magical preparations.
- The party discusses the upcoming battle against an ancient blue dragon and her horde, with the DM noting she "did not survive this long by being your average dragon" and warning of unseen minions beyond those already spotted.
- The party debates the action economy and the importance of healing during the fight. Orsik explicitly warns Vardis not to burn actions healing him, arguing they would both end up dead; Vardis counters that by definition, healing him would prevent his death.
- The party considers using Hero's Feast to bolster their health before the encounter. Orsik notes it is a 6th-level spell and remarks it would be "amazing."
- Vardis swaps their prepared spells, dropping Blade Barrier ("I've never used that, never will") in favor of Hero's Feast. Sunbeam was kept on the grounds that it had seen prior use.
- The DM reveals the dragon is supported by a group of clerics who will heal her during combat, and warns there are more minions the party has not yet spotted.
- The group jokes about the possibility of a Tarrasque appearing in the battle; Orsik asks directly, "Is the Tarrasque coming? Because that's what I really want." The DM firmly declines. The group discusses old cheese strategies against the Tarrasque, including a first-level Aarakocra killing it due to its lack of ranged attacks in the 2014 edition, and an elaborate scheme involving reducing cattle, feeding them to the Tarrasque, and having them re-enlarge inside its gut.
- Hero's Feast is determined to have been consumed the night before (or the morning of) the battle, granting all party members 16 additional maximum hit points (rolled as 2d10), immunity to the frightened and poisoned conditions, and resistance to poison damage for 24 hours.
- Orsik notes that the extra 16 hit points stack on top of any temporary hit points he might generate, bringing his maximum to 162.
- The DM notes that the Aarakocra first-level cheese and the "peasant gun" rail strategy were cleaned up in the 2024 edition.
- The party reviews the initiative tracker and discusses which cube result Orsik rolled — a 5 — settling on summoning an Air Elemental for its ability to fly, and confirming that Blue Dragons are immune only to lightning, while the Air Elemental deals thunder damage.

### The Escape Plan and the Call to Battle
#### Thistle and his companion discuss their escape from the dragon's lair while a massive battle erupts above.
- Thistle's companion hears the dragon battle erupting above and knows of two exits: the main entrance leading into the battle and a secret side exit. She favors the side exit for her own safety.
- The DM clarifies the geography: Thistle and his companion are far from the side exit, making it a long path to safety, while the main entrance leads directly into the chaos above.
- Thistle attempts to persuade his companion to follow him through the top entrance into the heat of battle as a distraction, rolling only a 3 on persuasion. She is not convinced.
- Orsik suggests giving the companion the Conch of Teleportation and having her teleport out; the DM notes the area is blocked against teleportation, making this impossible.
- Gary jokes that perhaps the companion could turn into a dragon, prompting the DM to darkly note that Uncle Joon is dead and "we'll never know exactly what he was about to tell you."
- The companion, unconvinced by Thistle's plan, decides to flee through the side exit, but wishes Thistle well.
- Thistle rolls a 26 insight check, confirming she is genuinely sincere — she truly believes escaping separately is the best plan for both of them.
- The companion acknowledges the logic: Thistle appearing above in the battle will convince their enemies the whole situation was a prank by Bob, not a genuine rescue attempt. As the DM puts it, "you going up and fighting them will probably guarantee my escape."
- The companion pleads with Thistle and bids him a tearful farewell before slipping out the side passage.
- Thistle flies upward into the chaos, his arrival in the main battle serving as perfect cover for the companion's escape.
- The DM grants the party one free round of chaos during which, amid the pandemonium of Bob's arrival, nobody notices Thistle emerging from below.
- The Balor, Bob, initiates the combat by rushing toward Iymrith, and the battle begins in earnest.

### The Battle of the Ancient Dragons Begins
#### The party and their giant allies engage the ancient blue dragon Iymrith and her minions in a massive cavern.
- The DM sets the scene: this is an enormous map, with the party noting it would take multiple rounds just to reach fireball range. The DM distinguishes between the "inner circle" where the worst mayhem will occur and the "outer circle" where the party might get hit.
- Iymrith moves across the battlefield and attacks the Balor known as Bob, rending him with three strikes for a combined 94 points of damage (18 slashing, 11 lightning, plus an additional 25 from a special ability), then turns invisible.
- Serissa charges toward the melee, her movement rate of 100 feet allowing her to close the distance rapidly. Other storm giants (numbers 1 and 5) do likewise.
- Orsik rolls a 5 on his Cube of Summoning and, after group deliberation, chooses to summon an Air Elemental for its flight capability and thunder damage — both relevant against a lightning-immune blue dragon. The elemental is placed adjacent to the dragon. Its attacks miss (Iymrith's AC is 22).
- Iymrith takes 30 points of fire damage from Bob's aura at the end of her turn near him.
- The chromatic dragon (Iymrith) takes 40 points of fire damage from Bob's fire aura as well, bringing her total down.
- Gargoyles manning a trebuchet fire upon Bob, striking him for 22 points of damage (the projectile hit for 44 but he takes half).
- Hekaton vaporizes two gargoyles with a lightning blast, disabling one of the trebuchets, then advances 40 feet.
- Dragon priests do not cower — they channel healing magic into Iymrith, restoring 10 hit points.
- Storm giants continue to clear the remaining gargoyles from the battlefield; all gargoyles are eventually eliminated, with the DM remarking "we barely knew you."
- Orsik moves 100 feet using his bonus action to Dash, closing toward the battle while directing the Air Elemental 90 feet into position.
- Unla Key, always in Pegasus form (90-foot fly speed), maneuvers through the air to find a vantage point away from the "zone of death" and prepares to launch a magical assault. The group debates spell choice — Chain Lightning is rejected due to Iymrith's lightning immunity; Disintegrate is considered but the range is too short at that distance; Fireball is chosen as most practical.
- The DM color-codes the battle map, marking enemies in pink and Iymrith in blue so the party can distinguish friend from foe. The DM notes Thuul is technically an enemy but is not a priority target.
- Bob the Balor is tracked on the initiative sheet; his hit point total is noted and adjusted throughout the round.

### The Siege of Iymrith
#### The party and their diverse allies launch a coordinated assault on the ancient blue dragon Iymrith, depleting her legendary defenses.
- Unla Key, flying on a Pegasus, repositions to avoid the central danger zone and launches a powerful Fireball at Iymrith at DC 21. Iymrith fails her save (she has only a +5 dexterity modifier against DC 21) and burns one of her five legendary resistances, taking 20 points of fire damage.
- The group debates saving high-level spells for later, with the DM noting that "save or suck" spells are ineffective until Iymrith's legendary resistances are depleted.
- Iymrith focuses her counterattack on Bob the Balor, striking him twice for a combined 63 points of damage.
- Serissa hurls a massive boulder at Iymrith with a natural 20, scoring a critical hit for 80 points of damage.
- Iymrith briefly turns invisible and repositions — using her 80-foot movement — then reappears near Bob and strikes him with her tail for 21 additional points of damage. Her invisibility causes Bob's retaliatory attack to be made at disadvantage (he lacks see invisibility), though she still takes 30 points of fire damage from his aura.
- Orsik advances 100 feet (using Dash as a bonus action) and commands the Air Elemental 90 feet into position near the dragon.
- Orsik screams at Bob to "KILL HER! KILL HER!" and rolls a 13 on persuasion. The enraged Balor, deciding to blame the dragon rather than the party, strikes Iymrith twice for 36 combined points of damage, bringing her to approximately 270 hit points.
- Giant number 1 throws a rock but misses (rolling 16, below her AC). Giant number 5 connects, dealing 35 points of damage. Iymrith uses a legendary action to strike Bob again for 21 more points of damage.
- The DM notes Iymrith has used 4 of her 5 legendary actions and uses the last to deal 21 more damage to Bob, bringing him to 15 hit points remaining. Bob survives by 15 hit points.
- Dave (Orsik) orders the five NPC mages to each cast Fireball on Iymrith. They move 30 feet closer first. Their spell DC is 14. Iymrith fails two saves but burns her three remaining legendary resistances on the other three, taking a combined 71 points of fire damage. Iymrith drops below 200 hit points.
- Iymrith uses her final legendary action to strike Bob the Balor, dealing enough damage to reduce him to zero hit points. Bob vanishes in a puff of smoke, returning to his home plane. Orsik notes philosophically that Bob is not truly dead — just home.
- Hekaton strikes Iymrith with a boulder (described as a "Ballista" in his stat block, which the DM finds odd) for 35 points of damage.
- Dragon priests heal Iymrith for 33 hit points, bringing her back up to 197 hit points.
- Two additional storm giants throw boulders: one hits for 33 points of damage, one misses.
- Unla Key casts one more Fireball (the last legendary resistance has already been burned). Iymrith now has no resistances left. She takes full damage: 18 points. (Her dexterity save of +7 against DC 21 fails.)
- Iymrith retaliates at the top of the next round with her lightning breath in a line, targeting Orsik at DC 23 for 103 points of damage. Orsik's dexterity save is his worst save and he cannot make DC 23 without a natural 20; he fails, taking full damage. He loses concentration on whatever he was maintaining.
- The Air Elemental is caught in the lightning breath and instantly destroyed — the DM confirms it is dead, not merely knocked aside.
- Vardis summons a Djinn to the battlefield (the Cube of Summoning produces result 5; Orsik selects Air Elemental for the first summon; Vardis later uses the Djinn via a separate item — the Cube of Summoning). The Djinn is placed on the opposite side of Iymrith from Vardis to create flanking. His three melee attacks all miss.
- Thistle activates Hunter's Mark on Iymrith, moves 70 feet into position, and fires two arrows. Both miss Iymrith's AC of 22.
- Iymrith prepares to turn invisible once more at the end of the round, ensuring that the giants' boulder throws — which require sight — will be made at disadvantage, as the session ends.

## Locations
### Iymrith's Lair
A vast cavernous area large enough to host a battle between multiple ancient dragons, storm giants, and their allies. Features multiple exits: a main entrance leading into the central battle area, and a secret side exit used by Thistle's companion for her escape. The DM distinguishes between an "inner circle" of maximum danger and an "outer circle" where the party risks incidental hits. Gargoyle-manned trebuchets are positioned throughout the space. The sheer scale of the map means the party would need several rounds just to reach the central melee from their starting positions.

### Hekaton's Domain
An underwater palace mentioned as a potential destination for escape via the Conch of Teleportation and the portal the party has learned to use, serving as a long-term rally point and goal for the party.

## Items
### Conch of Teleportation
A magical item capable of transporting users across great distances to Hekaton's domain. The party considers giving it to Thistle's companion for her escape, but the DM notes the area around the lair is blocked against teleportation, making this impossible at the moment. Orsik suggests giving it to Thistle or another party member; it is discussed as a potential exit strategy for after the battle, once the magical binding is lifted.

### Cube of Summoning
A magical artifact used by Orsik to call forth an elemental to fight alongside the party. Orsik rolled a 5, and after group debate chose an Air Elemental for its flight speed and thunder damage (relevant against a lightning-immune dragon). The elemental was later destroyed by Iymrith's lightning breath. Vardis separately used this (or a similar item) to summon a Djinn.

### Trebuchet
A large siege engine manned by gargoyles. It successfully struck Bob the Balor with a massive projectile during the battle, dealing 22 points of damage (44 halved). Hekaton later vaporized the gargoyle crew with a lightning blast, disabling the siege engine.

### Wand of Wonder
A chaotic magical wand with three charges, already attuned to Unla Key. Discussed by the party as a potential last-resort weapon against Iymrith — the DM and players agree it "should be reserved as a last ditch thing" and "needs to be the finishing blow." Not yet used.

## Spells
### Hero's Feast
A 6th-level conjuration that creates a grand magical banquet. Vardis dropped Blade Barrier to prepare it. The party consumed it the night before (or the morning of) the battle. It increased everyone's maximum hit points by 16 (rolled as 2d10, result 16), granted immunity to the frightened and poisoned conditions, and resistance to poison damage for twenty-four hours. Orsik notes his maximum rises to 162 and that the increased maximum stacks with temporary hit points on top.

### Fireball
A sphere of roaring flame used by Unla Key (DC 21) and then by a coordinated group of five NPC mages (DC 14) to bombard Iymrith. Unla Key's initial cast forced Iymrith to expend one legendary resistance for 20 points of damage. The mage barrage of five simultaneous Fireballs burned through all of Iymrith's remaining legendary resistances for a combined 71 points of damage, dropping her below 200 hit points. A final Fireball from Unla Key after resistances were exhausted dealt an additional 18 points.

### Lightning Breath
Iymrith's signature draconic attack, a massive line of electrical energy at DC 23. It decimated the summoned Air Elemental (instantly destroying it) and dealt 103 points of damage to Orsik, who could not make the save without a natural 20 and failed, losing concentration. The attack proved she remains lethally dangerous even after her legendary resistances were stripped.

### Hunter's Mark
A concentration tracking spell used by Thistle to focus his attacks on Iymrith, marking her as his quarry to increase the damage of his arrows. His two arrow attacks both missed Iymrith's AC of 22.

### Invisibility
A magical ability used by Iymrith on multiple occasions during the battle to vanish from sight. She turns invisible after her opening assault on Bob, repositions, and becomes visible again briefly before turning invisible once more at the session's end to deny the giants' line of sight for their boulder throws, forcing disadvantage on any attack requiring vision.

### Chain Lightning
A powerful electrical spell considered by Unla Key as an option to hit multiple targets. Rejected because Iymrith is completely immune to lightning damage, making it ineffective against the primary target.

### Expeditious Retreat
A speed-enhancing spell considered by Orsik to increase his movement across the vast battlefield, though he chose not to expend a spell slot on it, instead using his Dash bonus action to cover 100 feet.

### Disintegrate
A high-level single-target spell briefly considered by Unla Key against Iymrith. Rejected at that moment because the range (60 feet) was shorter than Unla Key's distance from the dragon at the time, making it impossible to cast safely.

## NPCs
### Iymrith
An ancient blue dragon of immense power and legendary resilience, immune to lightning damage and possessed of five legendary resistances. The DM notes "she did not survive this long by being your average dragon" and she has a +16 initiative bonus. She defends her hoard with lethal lightning breath (DC 23, 103 damage), physical rending attacks (three strikes for a combined 94 points in one round), and cunning use of invisibility to outmaneuver her many foes. She is supported by devoted dragon priests who heal her during battle. Her AC is 22. At session's end she has approximately 164 hit points remaining and is turning invisible to deny the giants' sight lines.

### Bob (Balor)
A powerful Balor demon who served as a temporary and unpredictable ally to the party — though one the DM notes was originally introduced as a "hero of the play" the party refused to fight, doing "that really lame ass thing" with a string of natural 20s. He charged headlong into battle against Iymrith and absorbed massive punishment: 94 points from Iymrith's opening rend, 22 from the trebuchet, multiple further strikes from Iymrith's legendary actions totaling dozens more points of damage, before being struck down and vanishing in a puff of smoke. Orsik and Vardis both note that Bob is not truly dead — he has merely returned to his home plane. The DM confirms "he'll be back."

### Hekaton
A Storm Giant King who leads the assault against Iymrith. He uses a lightning blast to eliminate gargoyles and disable a trebuchet, then hurls boulders at the dragon (his stat block lists a "Ballista" attack, which puzzles the DM). He deals 35 points of damage in the session's main combat exchange.

### Serissa
A storm giant ally who charges into battle against Iymrith with a movement rate of 100 feet, hurling massive boulders with incredible force. She scores a critical hit (natural 20) that deals 80 points of damage to Iymrith — the single largest damage roll of the session.

### Dragon Priests
Devoted followers of Iymrith who focus on channeling healing magic into the dragon to keep her in the fight. Three priests are present, including one Archpriest. They restore 10 hit points in the first round and 33 hit points in the second, partially offsetting the party's damage output.

### Red Wizards (Mages)
A group of five mages from Silverymoon who have joined the fray alongside the party. They roll initiative as a group (rolling 17 with a +2 bonus for a total of 19), have an AC of 15, and a spell DC of 14. They unleash a coordinated barrage of five simultaneous Fireballs at Iymrith, collectively burning through all of her remaining legendary resistances and dealing 71 combined points of fire damage. The party is reminded not to Fireball or Chain Lightning them.

### Thistle's Companion
A woman Thistle rescued from the lair who is skeptical of his combat plans. When Thistle rolls only a 3 on persuasion to bring her along into the battle above, she refuses. Thistle's follow-up insight check of 26 confirms she is sincere — she truly believes escaping separately is the safest plan. She acknowledges that Thistle fighting above will convince their enemies the whole rescue was merely a prank by Bob, and that this will likely guarantee her own escape. She bids Thistle a tearful farewell and slips out through the side exit. Her ultimate destination is implied to be safety outside the lair; the party discusses reuniting later via the portal to Hekaton's domain. The DM darkly notes that Uncle Joon is dead, so the question of whether she might "turn into a dragon" will never be answered.

### Djinn
A powerful elemental being summoned by Vardis to assist in the melee. He has 161 hit points, a fly speed, and can attack with Storm Bolt (ranged, thunder damage) or melee strikes. Placed on the far side of Iymrith to create flanking, his three initial melee attacks all miss. The DM notes that ranged attacks in melee would be at disadvantage, so melee was chosen — but the dice were unkind.
