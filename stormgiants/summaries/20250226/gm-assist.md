# 2025-02-26

Date: Feb 26th, 2025

## Summary
The party arrived at Iymrith's vast and imposing lair, greeted by the ancient blue dragon herself, who made her expectations unmistakably clear. She reminded them that a great many powerful guests would be arriving for the grand performance, and that failure was not an option — someone would be burned to a crisp if things went wrong. With a particular cruelty reserved for Thistle, she promised that he specifically would not be allowed the mercy of death, no matter what happened. She then led the party down to the lower levels of her lair, where Laela was held captive in a magical contraption she could not escape — and where, in the same dungeon chamber, a group of Yuan-ti devotees were worshiping a stone idol carved in her likeness.

With Iymrith momentarily distracted by her own theatrical ambitions, Thistle slipped away to speak privately with Laela. He explained the plan: a demon ally named Bob Saget had been disguised — via a Polymorph scroll the party had secured before reaching the lair — to look exactly like her, and when the moment was right, the two would swap places while the crowd's attention was fixed on the stage. Laela's reaction was somewhere between disbelief and desperate acceptance, especially once she laid out the full scope of who would be in attendance — the five heads of the chromatic dragon families, representatives of the frost, fire, stone, and storm giants, the Red Wizards of Thay, and Prince Thornatius among others. She also warned that the guests would be inspecting her for magical properties before the show began, meaning the swap would have to happen at precisely the right moment, after the inspections and just as the performance was getting underway.

Meanwhile, Orsik had taken on the role of director, coaching Iymrith through the grand monologue she had demanded for her moment of triumph. He pushed her to deliver her lines with more speed and natural confidence, and she responded with surprising enthusiasm, genuinely moved by the quality of the speech that had been prepared for her. She declared that Orsik was a genius and that the performance was going to be extraordinary. Orsik, for his part, kept his expression neutral and his thoughts to himself.

As the guests began to arrive the evening before the show, the party moved through the crowd to make introductions. Orsik approached the ancient red dragon Flume, flattering her with the offer of a private preview that no other guest would receive. Flume, initially contemptuous, was won over by the attention and the promise of something exclusive. Vardis then spoke with the ancient green dragon, who made no secret of her financial frustration, warning that if Iymrith's demonstration failed to deliver after years of costly investment, there would be hell to pay. A brief and ill-fated attempt to taunt the ancient black dragon Obsidian — by relaying that the other dragons had called him the weakest — ended with Obsidian dismissing the party entirely and flying off in contempt.

The party's most chaotic diplomatic effort came when they approached Duke Zalto, the fire giant lord, under the guise of being Iymrith's personal assistants. They presented him with items that had been quietly lifted from his own private quarters during an earlier infiltration of the fire giants' lair, claiming that Iymrith had been preserving them especially for him. Then, with careful and deliberate deception, they implied that Iymrith had been gossiping about his private tastes to the other giants — suggesting she was ashamed of him. Duke Zalto's face darkened with fury, and his guards had to physically hold him back as he began cursing Iymrith's name. The party retreated quickly, satisfied that at least one more crack had been driven into Iymrith's coalition.

As the evening wound down and the party prepared for the following day, a familiar and unwelcome figure appeared in the shadows of the lair: Uncle Joon, Thistle's uncle. He greeted the party with the casual ease of someone who had been waiting patiently, and then, without much preamble, admitted the truth. He had known where Laela was the entire time. He had orchestrated the party's journey toward this confrontation deliberately, using them as a distraction so that he could slip into Iymrith's legendary hoard and make off with what he needed to pay off his own debts. The party's shock curdled quickly into cold fury, and before Uncle Joon could finish explaining himself, they had already moved — grappling him, gagging him, and tying him up to be left bound in the lair. The item itself was retrieved the following day, after the post-reveal chaos gave one of the party cover to slip down into Iymrith's hoard and bring it back to place in his bound hands as a final flourish.

The morning of the performance arrived with a crackling energy. Iymrith opened the spectacle with a roar and a bolt of lightning fired into the sky, while the assembled dragons breathed fire and acid and the giants pounded their fists against the stone. The mind flayers hovered in absolute silence at the edges of the gathering, avoided by every other faction out of pure self-preservation instinct. Then Iymrith delivered her monologue — the one Orsik had helped her craft and rehearse — boasting of her mastery over magic, mocking those who had doubted her, and finally presenting her greatest creation: the fairy dragon Laela, her so-called perfect assassin.

What emerged from below, however, was not quite what anyone expected. The figure that appeared before the crowd began telling terrible jokes instead of transforming into a dragon, leaving the audience in baffled silence and Iymrith in barely contained fury. Then, with a flourish, the disguise dropped entirely — and Bob the demon stood before the assembled chromatic dragons, giants, and investors, loudly announcing that the joke was on Iymrith and that she had never had Laela at all. The crowd erupted. The party joined in the booing, egging on the audience with gleeful abandon, while Iymrith sputtered and raged, completely blindsided by the humiliation unfolding in front of her most powerful allies.

The chaos that followed was immediate and total. The chromatic dragons, realizing the performance was a sham and their investments were lost, turned their attention to the one thing that still made the trip worthwhile: Iymrith's legendary hoard. The red and black dragons began racing each other toward the entrance to the treasure vault, snapping and snarling over who would get there first. The Red Wizards of Thay and the mind flayers quietly withdrew, cutting their losses with the cold pragmatism of those who always had other plans. The various giant factions, meanwhile, began gravitating toward the melee, some drawn by the gold and others by old grudges.

In the midst of the swirling madness, Orsik seized his moment. He stood before the storm giants and delivered a rousing speech, arguing that Iymrith was the source of all of this chaos and that her defeat was the only path to justice — and to the hoard. The storm giant king, moved by the argument and the arrival of his own kin on the battlefield, agreed to lead his people directly against the blue dragon. With the storm giants now committed to the fight and Iymrith already preoccupied by Bob's relentless taunting, the battlefield had shifted in the party's favor in the most improbable way imaginable.

The amphitheater had become a war zone of stray dragon breath, thundering giant footsteps, and the distant crash of treasure being fought over in the depths below. Iymrith, legendary and fearsome as she was, found herself surrounded — the storm giants pressing in from one side, Bob harassing her from another, and the party positioning themselves to strike. The cloud giants hung back at the edges, watching with calculating eyes to see which way the wind would blow. The party understood, with a grim clarity, that if they could bring Iymrith down, every greedy, ruthless faction in that amphitheater would owe them something — not out of gratitude exactly, but out of the cold acknowledgment that the party had made their windfall possible.

What had begun as a one-dimensional plan — a body double, a distraction, and a quiet escape — had somehow spiraled into the most consequential battle of their lives, with ancient dragons, giant lords, and demonic allies all converging on a single point of chaos that the party had set in motion. Laela was free. Uncle Joon was tied up in the dark. Bob was still making jokes. And Iymrith, the doom of the desert, was finally cornered.

## Locations
### Iymrith's Lair / Amphitheater
A grand and dangerous fortress and open amphitheater where Iymrith resides, keeps her prisoners, and hosts her grand spectacle for powerful investors.

### The Dungeon / Lower Level
A lower level of Iymrith's lair containing a magical contraption used to imprison Laela, and a group of Yuan-ti worshiping a statue in Iymrith's image.

### Iymrith's Treasure Horde Entrance
A hole leading into Iymrith's vast legendary hoard, which becomes the primary target for the greedy chromatic dragons and giants during the chaos of the failed performance.

## Items
### Magical Contraption
A complex device constructed by Iymrith to imprison Laela within the lower levels of her lair, preventing the fairy dragon from escaping.

### Performance Costumes
Outfits brought by the party for the dress rehearsal to ensure the performance looked authentic to Iymrith and her guests.

### Items from the Fire Giants' Private Quarters
Objects previously taken from Duke Zalto's private bed chambers during an earlier infiltration of the fire giants' lair. The party presented these to Duke Zalto, claiming Iymrith had been 'preserving' them for him, in order to sow discord and embarrass the dragon.

### Iymrith's Treasure Hoard
A legendary collection of gold and magical artifacts stored beneath Iymrith's lair. It becomes the primary target for the chromatic dragons and giants once the performance collapses, and was the true prize Uncle Joon had been trying to access all along.

## Spells
### Polymorph
Used to disguise Bob the demon as Laela the fairy dragon, enabling the party's infiltration and the switcheroo at the heart of their plan to humiliate Iymrith.

### Detect Magic
Anticipated to be used by the Red Wizards of Thay to inspect Laela's magical nature before the show began, which factored into the party's timing for the swap.

### Prestidigitation
Suggested by a party member as a way to add minor magical flair to Bob's performance to make it even more ridiculous during the switcheroo.

### Wall of Force
Proposed by the party as a strategic means to seal the entrance to Iymrith's hoard and trap the looting dragons inside during the chaos.

### Mold Earth
Considered by multiple party members as a means to collapse the entrance to the hoard and trap the greedy dragons and giants underground.

## NPCs
### Iymrith
An ancient blue dragon with a flair for the dramatic and a cruel streak. She is obsessed with proving her magical genius to her investors and demands a grand monologue for her performance. She is left vulnerable and preoccupied during the final battle.

### Laela
A fairy dragon held captive in a magical contraption within Iymrith's lair. She is Thistle's companion and is skeptical of the party's rescue plan, but desperate enough to go along with it. She is successfully rescued during the distraction.

### Bob
A demon ally brought by the party to act as a body double for Laela. He was disguised via Polymorph and, rather than transforming into a dragon, revealed himself and publicly humiliated Iymrith in front of all her guests, continuing to preoccupy her during the battle.

### Yuan-ti Worshipers
A group of snake-like creatures found in the lower levels of Iymrith's lair, worshiping a statue idol in Iymrith's image rather than Iymrith herself.

### Flume
A gargantuan ancient red dragon with an aura of contempt. She prefers a simple name to reflect her immense power and is one of the primary investors in Iymrith's project. She is warned by Iymrith not to eat the performers, and quickly turns her attention to looting the hoard once the performance fails.

### Green Dragon
An ancient dragon investor who is deeply skeptical of Iymrith's claims and warns that there will be 'hell to pay' if the demonstration is a failure, citing a long and costly investment.

### Obsidian
An ancient black dragon who prides himself on his clan's control over trade and the Zhentarim. He dismisses the party after they taunt him by claiming the other dragons called him the weakest.

### Duke Zalto
A powerful fire giant Duke attending the summit with four henchmen. He is easily agitated and becomes furious when the party implies Iymrith has been mocking his private life to other giants.

### Uncle Joon
Thistle's uncle, revealed to have known about Laela's location all along and to have manipulated the party's entire journey as a distraction so he could loot Iymrith's hoard to pay off his own debts. He is subdued, gagged, and tied up by the party.

### Storm Giant King
The leader of the storm giants who arrives at the amphitheater. His presence draws the attention of the other giant factions. He is eventually persuaded by Orsik to lead his kin in a direct assault against Iymrith.

### Mind Flayers
A group of eerie investors in Iymrith's project who arrive silently and hover during the spectacle. All other factions avoid them. They step back to observe once the chaos begins, avoided by everyone due to their fearsome reputation.

### Red Wizards of Thay
Calculating magic users who attended as guests and were expected to inspect Laela for magical properties. They retreat once the performance collapses, writing off their investment rather than engaging in a suicidal melee.
